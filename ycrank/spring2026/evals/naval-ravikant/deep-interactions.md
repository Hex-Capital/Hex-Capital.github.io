# Deep Interactions -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Deep Interactions is a research-credentialed founder running a services-heavy B2B wedge in a category whose incumbents are scaling on permissionless PLG -- Sruthi Viswanathan's Oxford DPhil in Human-AI Interaction is the highest signal in the dossier, and the Calendly-plus-Typeform sales funnel for "we ship a working AI product in the same afternoon" is the lowest. That pairing is the entire story: real specific knowledge, wrong leverage architecture.

I read leverage first, and on that axis Deep Interactions sits closer to **Anchorage Digital** than to **Twitter**. **Twitter (2007)** was media-as-protocol with zero marginal cost of replication; **Notion (2013)** sat one rung below as code-leverage with freemium PLG that compounded without gatekeepers; **Anchorage** required a Treasury OCC charter and only earned the override because category-timing in crypto was load-bearing. Deep Interactions runs on labor leverage: a four-person team delivering bespoke AI apps to ten design partners (Jack Fertility, Canopie, FitMatrix) with the inbound funnel literally being calendly.com/sruthi-deepinteractions/30min. The YC description -- "you give us a problem and we ship a working AI product in the same afternoon" -- is the inverse of the **Stack Overflow (2008)** developer-pull pattern. No GitHub presence, no Product Hunt launch, no open-source artifact, no organic X following surfaced for either Sruthi or Beni. Labor and capital require permission; that is what this distribution stack is. Closer to LOW than MID rung.

Specific knowledge is where Deep Interactions earns its only above-default placement. **Stack Overflow** -- Atwood and Spolsky as practicing developers writing for developers -- is the HIGH rung; **Perplexity (2022)** -- Aravind Srinivas's Berkeley retrieval PhD -- is the MID rung; the LOW rung is the "ex-McKinsey PM identified a market opportunity" profile I pass on reflexively. Sruthi's DPhil thesis topic is literally Human-AI Interaction at Oxford, with publications on Google Scholar and OpenReview, and her Limbic AI tenure (2022-2024) is direct lived exposure to building mental-health AI products -- the same vertical where Canopie now sits as a named customer. That is not pedigree-as-thesis; it is the founder having reverse-engineered the problem from inside the research literature and a prior operating role. Between MID and HIGH, closer to HIGH.

Marketplace and network mechanics is where Deep Interactions falls back into Perplexity-shaped territory without Perplexity's overrides. **Uber (2010)** is the HIGH rung where supply made demand more valuable; **Stack Overflow** is the MID rung where developer answers compounded developer arrivals; **Perplexity** is the LOW rung I funded only because three other gates cleared at top quartile. Deep Interactions is single-sided B2B SaaS at ten customers with "10,000+ usage instances" of undefined metric -- each customer is economically independent of the next, and the workflow integrations across Slack, Gmail, Figma, GitHub, Vercel, Supabase do not compound across the customer base. Below midpoint, near the LOW rung. The Perplexity override does not apply because criterion #1 is not clearing at top quartile here.

Market size and pivot-room places Deep Interactions between **Notion (2013)** (productivity, platform-shaped) and the vertical-SaaS LOW rung I avoid. The no-code AI platform TAM at $8.6B in 2026 expanding to $75B by 2034 (Grand View Research) is real platform-shaped territory, and adjacent pivot vectors into vibe-coding-for-PMs or internal-tools-for-ops exist. Lovable at $400M ARR (Sacra) proves the market absorbs scale -- which is the problem, not the comfort. Founder trio reads clean against the **Notion**/**Stack Overflow** mid rungs and nothing like the **Clearview** integrity exception: Sruthi was named to 100 Women in AI, Beni founded WEKA and survived Barclays IB, both Oxford -- no contested exits, no honesty-volunteering, but no decade-long track record either. Mid-rung placement on both.

Thirty-six months out, Deep Interactions is acquihired by Lovable, Replit, or v0 for sub-$50M for the Oxford Human-AI Interaction research talent, or it pivots into a vertical AI consulting boutique generating $2-5M of services revenue without crossing into venture-scale ARR. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: Sruthi's specific knowledge is exactly what a vibe-coding incumbent would buy, and the services-plus-Calendly distribution stack cannot outrun a competitor with $330M of Series B capital (Lovable, December 2025) attacking the team-collaboration surface. Capital asymmetry compounds; research credentials get acquihired.

The strongest bull case is that the "context layer" wedge is structurally distinct from single-user vibe-coding -- Lovable's $400M ARR was built one prompter at a time, and B2B teams genuinely lose context between stakeholders and builders, which is the exact problem Sruthi's DPhil studies. If Deep Interactions converts the human-in-loop methodology into proprietary workflow IP and the integrations across nine team tools accrete switching costs, the wedge could become the Notion-of-internal-AI-apps. The bear-case dismantle: my Unstated Pattern is that I fund products that distribute via permissionless code or media, and Deep Interactions distributes via Calendly. **Stack Overflow** had developer-pull before equity; **Replit** had a self-taught code-environment shipped publicly since 2011; **Notion** had Ivan Zhao's adolescent-onward specific knowledge expressed in shipped product. Deep Interactions has zero open-source surface, zero Product Hunt, zero press, and "Stealth Startup" on the founder's LinkedIn. One verifiable condition flips this: if Deep Interactions ships an open-source core SDK or browser extension that gets organic GitHub stars and unprompted developer adoption inside six months, the architecture flips from labor to code leverage and I re-underwrite. Until then, pass.
