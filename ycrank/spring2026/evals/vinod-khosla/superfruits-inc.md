# Superfruits Inc. -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Superfruits Inc. is the rarest shape in my Pass pile: a team with a learning-rate signature strong enough to recall the **Slash** (2024) exception paired with a consequence-magnitude that lives squarely inside the "GPT-4 summarizes sales calls" example from my own Pass framework. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Román Czerny sold CoCo AI in 2025, Dylan Teixeira sold Edusign in 2025, and the three of them pivoted Gojiberry from services to product inside twelve months on the way to $2.5M ARR. That is real founder telemetry. It is also pointed at "AI GTM agents for B2B teams" -- an SDR optimization play priced at $99/month against Clay's $3.1B valuation and $100M ARR. The signature is "serial pivoters compounding inside a category I would not fund on consequence."

**OpenAI** (2019) is the consequence-anchor I keep returning to, and Superfruits Inc. is the inverse of why I wrote that $50M check. The OpenAI mechanism that mattered was the success-state collapsing the cost curve of cognitive labor itself -- $15 trillion of U.S. GDP that is labor, per my Fortune (2026-03-06) framing. Superfruits Inc. names a much narrower success-state: 2,000+ customers paying $99/month for LinkedIn outreach that yields "2-5x more positive replies than traditional outbound" (YC description, 2026). The incumbent regime here is not labor itself -- it is the SDR seat, a sub-category that Clay, 11x, Artisan, and Regie.ai are already reshaping with $387M of combined disclosed capital. Even at maximum ambition, Superfruits Inc.'s success-state is "the SDR job function compresses 70%," not "outbound as an economic mechanism disappears." That is the McKinsey-1986-cellphone error in reverse: extrapolating a known seat into a slightly cheaper version of the same seat.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) failed because the capital plan understated what the science actually required -- that is the LOW rung on technical risk, and Superfruits Inc. sits on the opposite failure axis: there is no science to underwrite at all. The Gojiberry stack is LinkedIn sender accounts plus an LLM plus a 30+ signal aggregator (job changes, funding rounds, competitor engagement), all of which are sourced from commodity third-party data providers per the dossier. Any Tier-1 generalist can fund this. Worse, the central mechanism -- automated LinkedIn outreach via metered "sender" accounts (prospeo.io, 2026) -- depends on a counterparty that banned Artisan's Ava in 2025 (completeaitraining.com, 2025). The thing that repels consensus VCs from this category is not technical risk but platform-dependency risk, which is the wrong kind of risk: it is not a moat I get paid for.

Founder-defiance-of-experts on Superfruits Inc. compounds the wrong direction over 18 months. Lallemant, Czerny, and Teixeira have built and sold SaaS-shaped companies before -- CoCo AI was WhatsApp marketing for Shopify, Edusign was paperless attendance -- which is exactly the "career SaaS operator continuing the same playbook with AI" profile I flagged in my TED Talk about UC Berkeley's 28,000-prediction expert study. The dossier contains zero evidence that any of the three founders has publicly contradicted their own field's orthodoxy on outbound, SDR labor economics, or sales tooling. The compounding failure mode is that as Clay (Series C 2025) extends downward into agentic execution and 11x extends sideways into intent signals, Superfruits Inc.'s position is defended by playbook competence, not by a defied-consensus thesis. That defense erodes at the speed Clay ships.

Counterfactually, Superfruits Inc.'s learning-rate score would have to flip Slash-shaped for the deal to clear my bar -- and it almost does. The **Slash** (2024) override was a documented pivot driven by a public moment of founder thesis-update, which justified a $100M Series C despite the consumer-banking consequence ceiling. Superfruits Inc. has three structurally similar signals: a services-to-product pivot inside twelve months ("$33K Revenue in 4 Months — The Pivot That Worked," superframeworks.com, 2025); two prior exits by Lallemant and Czerny on CoCo AI; one prior exit by Teixeira on Edusign. Czerny's @romanbuildsaas reach (2M+ view threads) and the Product Hunt #1 launch are distribution-as-learning-rate, not noise. If the dossier had even one founder quote stating "we were wrong about $incumbent_category for $reason," I would move Superfruits Inc. up a tier on this axis alone.

Single-anchor on capital fit: **Cognition** (Series B April 2024, $175M round) is what concentrated capital looks like deployed against AI labor displacement -- the roadmap eats the money because foundation-model training, fleet, plant, or drug pipeline absorb it. Superfruits Inc. is profitable at $2.5M ARR with the YC standard deal as the only disclosed institutional capital (Crunchbase, 2026). I am over-resourced for this deal by an order of magnitude, and there is no commercial milestone in the dossier that requires a $50M+ check. The capital-fit failure here is not "they will run out of money" -- it is "they do not need me, and the deal does not deploy at a scale that produces a fund-returning outcome even on the upside."

Thirty-six month forecast: Superfruits Inc. lands at $10-25M ARR and is acquired by Clay, HubSpot, or Salesforce for $80-180M between mid-2027 and late-2028, as Clay's Series C product roadmap (clay.com 2025) extends into end-to-end agentic execution and Gojiberry's $99/month SMB base churns under Clay's data marketplace advantage. The mechanism: 35% MoM at $2.5M ARR decelerates inside twelve months as Clay's distribution and 11x's persona-SDR product converge on the same buyer; the founders, who have sold twice before, take the strategic acquisition rather than raise a Series B into a contested category. Not dead, not a unicorn -- a competent exit consistent with the dimensional signature.

**Slash** (2024) is the strongest opposing case and it does sting. Three working SaaS operators with documented exits, a public pivot, viral founder-led distribution, YC pedigree -- Slash's $100M Series C cleared on weaker consequence-magnitude than this. Why not write the same override? Because Slash's mechanism was consumer business banking with network-economic deposit gravity, and Superfruits Inc. is sandwiched inside Clay's $210M-backed expansion radius (TechCrunch Aug 2025) on a category where LinkedIn ToS enforcement is an exogenous kill-switch (the Artisan/Ava ban event, 2025). The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Superfruits Inc. ships a proprietary intent-signal source -- a first-party dataset not buyable from a third-party provider -- that produces a reply-rate uplift validated by an independent customer cohort, not the self-reported "2-5x" figure. Until then, Superfruits Inc. is the Pass-Example template with better founders. Pass.
