# HEVN, inc -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

HEVN, inc is a competent operator team running a literal "X for Y" pitch -- "Brex for international companies" per the YC page itself -- into a category where Airwallex is already at $1.2B ARR and a $12B valuation. That's the signature: founders who shipped HOT Wallet to 30M+ users (Chainwire, Nov 2024) attacking the most-pitched fintech wedge of the cycle with the most consensus framing imaginable. Sane people. Sane idea. That's not the ratio I'm hunting for.

The mispricing test is where HEVN, inc dies for me. My HIGH rung here is Solana at four cents -- Ethereum had "won," every smart crypto person told me to pass, and the market had priced the asset at zero. My LOW rung is the YC AI copilot wrapper: hot category, ten VCs bidding, no asymmetry left. HEVN, inc sits closer to the LOW rung than the middle. The dossier literally calls the company "Brex for international companies" and lists three well-funded incumbents -- Brex (Capital One acquiring at $5.15B), Mercury ($650M ARR, OCC charter applied Dec 2025), Airwallex ($1.2B ARR March 2026) -- already serving the cross-border SMB. The "no-EIN FDIC USD account" is the only contrarian hook, and the dossier marks it "soon," not live. There's no category discomfort here. No mainstream VC is rejecting HEVN, inc because they think non-US USD banking is icky -- they're rejecting it because Airwallex already exists. That's not mispricing. That's pricing.

The primitive test is even worse for HEVN, inc. My HIGH rung is Venmo -- P2P mobile payments became a verb, the behavior didn't exist before. My MID rung is Patreon, which made direct-creator-monetization a behavioral standard. My LOW rung is "better CRM." HEVN, inc is the textbook "X for Y" framing -- it doesn't even pretend to create a primitive. Multi-currency wallets exist (Wise, Payoneer, Airwallex's 20+ local-currency accounts). USD virtual cards with cashback exist. Stablecoin payment rails exist. HEVN, inc is recombining known primitives for a specific buyer -- the non-US founder running Google ads. That's a feature-bundle product, not a primitive. Nobody will say "I HEVN'd you the money." Solidly LOW rung.

On sanity-insanity, HEVN, inc actually scores its best -- and it's still mediocre. My HIGH rung is Raj on Solana, watched across three companies, calm engineer pitching an "insane" L1. HEVN, inc has the sane half: Volnov, Briuzgin, and Zhevlakov co-founded HOT Labs and shipped HERE Wallet to 600K+ active users together (Cryptoadventure, 2024). Multi-year deep relationship -- not networking-event co-founders. That hits one of my Unstated Patterns. But the idea side is the inverse of insane -- it's the most consensus pitch in the YC fintech track. Sane person, sane idea is boring. And the pivot from TON-ecosystem crypto wallets to US-regulated banking partners is a domain transition with no public evidence of compliance staffing -- a real bandwidth question given HOT Labs is "still operating as of 2024-2025 press."

The AI kill test is the one place HEVN, inc actually defends itself. My HIGH rung is Metropolis -- physical parking infrastructure that AI cannot commoditize. My LOW rung is any GPT-4 wrapper. HEVN, inc isn't AI-commoditizable -- regulated bank-partner stacks, KYB pipelines for non-US entities, FDIC sponsor relationships are a real (if not unique) compliance moat. But it's a weaker version of the same moat Airwallex has at 1000x scale. And the Capital Discipline dimension -- HIGH rung Gusto, LOW rung Allbirds -- has no public data either way for HEVN, inc; this category structurally requires multi-round capital deployment to compete with Mercury's $346M raised and Airwallex's $1.58B. The dossier shows no public pricing, no revenue, no LOIs. HEVN, inc is going to need the factory farm I keep saying is dead.

My 36-month forecast: HEVN, inc reaches $2-5M ARR serving a niche slice of non-US SaaS founders running USD ad spend, fails to deliver the "no-EIN FDIC" product as a defensible primitive before Airwallex or Mercury extend down-market, and gets acqui-hired by Payoneer or a mid-tier cross-border player for low-tens-of-millions -- or pivots back to stablecoin-rail tooling for emerging-market founders, leveraging the HOT Wallet user base. Mechanism: the wedge is too narrow to outrun a $1.2B ARR incumbent already advertising "Brex alternatives" content, and the founders' real distribution edge is crypto-native, not regulated-banking-native.

The strongest bull case I can build -- and it does sting -- is that Mercury's December 2025 OCC charter application and Capital One's Brex acquisition both signal US incumbents pulling further upmarket and away from non-US KYB. That leaves a real underserved segment: Indian, SEA, and LatAm SaaS founders who can't get a Mercury account and pay 2-3% FX on $50K/month of Google ads. If HEVN, inc lands the no-EIN FDIC product as the only player and channels HOT Wallet's 30M-user audience into onboarding, this could be the Brex of emerging markets -- a $200M+ ARR business in five years. That's not crazy. But the dossier evidence wins: Airwallex's "20+ local-currency accounts globally" plus its $1.58B war chest means any wedge HEVN, inc opens gets closed in 18 months with a press release. Verifiable flip condition: if HEVN, inc shows $5M+ ARR within 12 months concentrated among non-US founders with a live (not "soon") FDIC USD account that doesn't require an EIN, I'd reopen the file. Until then, pass.
