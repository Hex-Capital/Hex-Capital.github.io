# Care GP -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Melvin Chen of Care GP is an Oxford valedictorian and Forbes 30 Under 30 financier running an AI-admin layer on top of Best Practice Software while the platform itself ships a native admin agent. That is the analytical signature: a credentialed-outsider founder ? the highest-scoring dimension in the Care GP dossier ? executing inside a category whose contrarian secret has already been named publicly by Heidi Health and Lyrebird and is being absorbed by the incumbent PMS. Care GP is a disciplined operator bet, not a monopoly bet, and the gap between those two is the entire Zero to One distinction.

**Stripe** (2010) is the load-bearing analogy for the contrarian-secret dimension, because Patrick Collison named a specific incumbent failure mode ? PayPal's developer-experience indifference inside a category PayPal had "won." Care GP's stated secret is that AI scribes (Heidi, Lyrebird) are solving the wrong workflow and that back-office admin ? Samantha for documents, Veronica for phones, Bill for billing ? is the unaddressed surface. That is a segmentation observation, not a secret; the Medical Republic 2026 piece confirms Heidi and Lyrebird are already extending from scribing into admin agents, and Best Practice Software has shipped a native admin agent. Care GP names no architectural reason Heidi's $138M war chest cannot replicate Samantha's document classifier in nine months. The "secret" terminates at a market cut, not a structural blind spot ? the same shape as the pass example, with better packaging.

The **cleantech 1.0** failure mode is the relevant low rung for monopoly trajectory: a real category, real customers, real revenue, and zero pricing-power durability once incumbents woke up. Care GP is the primary-care-admin version of that shape. The named monopoly hypothesis in the Care GP dossier is ISO27001 certification plus Best Practice partner listing, but Best Practice itself is the channel and the competitor ? bestpracticesoftware.com lists Care GP as a partner while the platform ships its own scribe and admin agent natively. That is not a moat; that is rented distribution with a six-month notice period. Pricing power five years out is invisible in the Care GP dossier because the substitution path runs through Care GP's own integration partner. Heidi's claimed 50% GP share is the consensus consolidation Thiel writes against ? Care GP is not the duopoly survivor in the Lyft sense, it is the third entrant in a category where the platform owns the rail.

Founder lineage is where Care GP scores highest, and the right way to handle it is a single-anchor parallel to **Asana** (2010) ? the canonical Known Exception where operator pedigree overrode missing structural moat. Moskovitz had shipped Facebook to a billion users; that loaded the exception. Melvin Chen's pedigree is real ? Oxford Engineering triple major valedictorian, Avigo and StarLake co-founder, PE at Terra Firma and Goldman Sachs, Forbes 30 Under 30 ? but it is finance-and-allocation pedigree, not shipped-to-scale operator pedigree. The Asana override does not load for Care GP because Chen has not shipped a software product to consumer-scale, and Care GP lists no co-founder on the YC page. Credentialed outsider, yes; PayPal Mafia re-up shape, no.

Definite-plan analysis is mechanism over 18 months: the Care GP dossier names "+600 new customers by year end" and "7,000 GP clinics nationally" ? a sales target, not the SpaceX-style technical commitment the dimension is designed to reward. Samantha's 175% MoM usage growth since May 2025 and the Yarrawonga "six hours in thirty minutes" testimonial are real PMF signals, but the roadmap compounds as land-and-expand inside Australia, which is the indefinite-optimism shape ? "we'll add more clinics and more agents." There is no named architectural commitment Chen will not pivot from. Eighteen months out, the Care GP plan converges on whichever workflow customers ask for next, which is exactly the iteration-mode Founders Fund's 2011 manifesto attacked.

Counterfactual for the regulated-sector dimension: Care GP would score in the upper half of the atoms-heavy/regulated rubric if the customer were a Primary Health Network or the Australian Department of Health rather than a private GP clinic with no switching cost beyond filing taxonomy. ISO27001 and onshore encryption are table-stakes B2B SaaS controls, not Palantir-grade certification depth. Care GP's regulatory anchor would have to become a government primary-care procurement vehicle ? PHN-level integration, MBS billing-adjacent compliance ? for the dimension to flip a tier. The Australian-geography concentration cited in the Globe and Mail October 2025 release cuts the opposite way: regulatory work is per-country, and the moat does not travel.

The 36-month forecast is that Care GP reaches AUD $5?15M ARR confined to Australia and is acquired by Best Practice Software or MedicalDirector for sub-$100M as the PMS layer absorbs admin-agent functionality natively, or alternatively that Heidi Health bundles equivalent admin agents into its existing ~50% GP footprint and Care GP plateaus as a profitable sub-scale niche. The mechanism is the channel-conflict logic ? Care GP rents distribution from the entity most structurally incentivized to absorb the product, and "profitable, zero churn" at 150 clinics is the exact financial signature of a tuck-in acquisition target, not a fund-returner.

The strongest opposing case is the Asana exception: Care GP is genuinely profitable at pre-seed, has 140% MoM usage growth, customer testimonials with named clinics (Sonya at Yarrawonga, Josh Casey at Green Square Health), zero churn, and a credentialed founder ? a real PMF signal, and the Known Exceptions list explicitly permits operator-pedigree overrides where the moat is thin. Moskovitz had shipped Facebook before Asana; Chen has shipped Avigo and StarLake but neither is a scaled software outcome, and PE pedigree is not the operator-pedigree the Asana exception was written to honor. The bull case stings because the traction is real, but the override criteria do not load. The single condition that would flip the verdict: Care GP wins a structural integration exclusive with Best Practice Software, or signs a Primary Health Network as a national admin-automation contractor ? either converts rented distribution into a regulated-customer moat in the Palantir shape. Absent that, this is a competent indefinite-optimism bet inside a category whose monopoly slot is already being filled by a $700M competitor.
