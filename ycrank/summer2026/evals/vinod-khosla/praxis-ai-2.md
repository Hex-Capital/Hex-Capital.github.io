# Praxis AI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Praxis AI is an arms dealer to a gold rush whose founders are the exact expert-consensus profile my 2013 TechCrunch op-ed warned against — highest on consequence adjacency, lowest on defied-expert conviction, and that pairing is the whole diagnosis. Rohan Seelamsetty, Dev Karpe, and Tommy Li are three University of Pennsylvania credentials — Wharton Huntsman, ex-Morgan Stanley PE, ex-a16z industrial-tech VC — pointing a data-procurement business at the humanoid training bottleneck. The tagline "turns every company into a data vendor" is a coordination thesis, not a science thesis, and Praxis AI's dossier confirms it: 60k workers, 150+ environment types, no physics, no capital wall, no defied orthodoxy.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the load-bearing anchor for consequence, and Praxis AI is nowhere near it — but the fair comparison is not to OpenAI itself, it is to being the training-data supplier to OpenAI. The mechanism that could load Praxis AI onto my consequence ladder is the $15T labor-displacement thesis I laid out in Fortune (2026-03-06): if humanoid VLA models eat physical labor, whoever supplies the egocentric capture at scale sits inside that redirection. Praxis AI's dossier cites robotics vendors spending >$100M/year each on real-world data (Forbes, Dec 2025) and Scale AI's >100,000 humanoid training hours. That is a real number. But Praxis AI is a supplier to the collapse, not the collapse itself — and suppliers get commoditized when the buyer consolidates. **Impossible Foods** (2011) *was* the collapse of animal protein; Praxis AI is a subcontractor to whichever humanoid company becomes the Impossible Foods of labor. Consequence-derivative, not consequence-primary.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) failed because the capital plan understated the burn required for the science to work — that is the low-rung failure mechanism on technical risk, and Praxis AI has the inverse problem. Praxis AI has *no* science risk to underprice. The dossier describes a workflow: embed inside conglomerates, hand workers capture kits, resell the data. Micro1 already ships Ray-Ban glasses to gig workers (Forbes, Dec 2025); Scale AI runs its own SF R&D lab; Encord raised $60M Series C from Wellington (Feb 2026). Praxis AI's differentiator — captive conglomerate worker fleets instead of gig recruits — is a procurement structure, not a physics claim. There is no thermodynamic wall the way KiOR had catalyst yield, no ten-year plant like QuantumScape. A Tier-1 generalist can fund this. That is the definitional failure of my second criterion.

On defied-expert consensus, the dossier reads like a case study in what my TED Talk called extrapolating the past. Tommy Li was an industrial-tech VC at a16z — the exact seat that generates the "AI needs more data" consensus he is now selling into. Dev Karpe came out of a Fortune 500 CEO's office in supply-chain operations. Seelamsetty is a Penn debater and Oxford Apollo Fellow. Not one of these three has publicly contradicted their field, sold a prior company, or taken a "too important not to do" position. Compare to **Physical Intelligence** (2024), where Sergey Levine had years of public robotics-learning positions that most of the field rejected before they were vindicated. Praxis AI has credentialed conformity where I look for instigators.

Founder learning rate is where the dossier goes silent, and I apply my default rather than penalize aggressively — but the silence rhymes with the consensus-credential problem. There is no documented pivot, no prior thesis update, no "we were wrong about X" moment for Praxis AI. A three-person team formed at Penn shipping into the current physical-AI hype cycle looks like a first linear plan. This compounds over 18 months in a specific way: if the initial conglomerate embed does not scale past 2-3 partners, does Praxis AI pivot to the annotation-tooling adjacency Encord already owns, or double down and burn out competing with Micro1's $100M ARR machine? I have no evidence either way.

Required-capital profile is where Praxis AI most cleanly fails my check-size test. The **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) $115M Series A worked because the roadmap *required* capital depth no consensus VC would deploy. Praxis AI's roadmap requires laptops, capture kits, and BD headcount. My $50M **OpenAI** (2019) check bought a foundation-model training run; a $50M check into Praxis AI buys a lot of Ray-Ban glasses and business development travel. Praxis AI is over-resourced by Khosla Ventures — this is a $2-5M seed round for a Tier-2 generalist, not a Khosla concentration bet.

My 36-month forecast for Praxis AI: acqui-hired by Scale AI or Micro1 at sub-$100M for the conglomerate-partnership rolodex, or plateaus at $2-5M ARR as one or two partner embeds fail to generalize past initial pilots. Not dead, not a fund-returner — the "efficient exit" outcome that adds negative expected value to my portfolio because it consumes attention I owe to the **Genesis AI** (2025) and **Physical Intelligence** (2024) end of the stack.

The bull case that would sting: arms dealers in real gold rushes make real money, and Micro1 went from zero to $100M ARR in eight months in the exact same category (Forbes, Dec 2025). If Tommy Li's a16z + NASA JPL + PerceptIn network is a genuine buy-side wedge into Physical Intelligence and Figure procurement teams, and if even one conglomerate signs a multi-year exclusive, Praxis AI could be at $30-50M ARR by 2028 — and the **Slash** (2024) Known Exception says I have written $100M checks into YC-backed businesses without civilizational consequence when the founder learning-rate signal was strong. The problem: the Slash exception required a documented Kanye-pivot moment, and Praxis AI's founders have no such moment on record. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a named, exclusive, multi-year contract with a Physical Intelligence or Figure — not the 60k-worker claim, which is a supply-side count that any well-capitalized incumbent can replicate. Absent that, Praxis AI is a pass I make in about 30 seconds, the way I described in the pass example.
