# HeyClicky -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

HeyClicky is a creator-asset force-fit into a standard YC consumer-AI cap table -- Farza Majeed's 89.3K X followers and 5.8k GitHub stars on `farzaa/clicky` are the real asset on offer, and the round is asking me to underwrite a commoditized Mac overlay where OpenAI's "Work with Apps" and Anthropic's "computer use" already ship the same primitive natively. That signature -- HIGH on founder distribution, LOW on financing structure -- is the analytical core of HeyClicky, and it pulls me in opposite directions from the same dossier.

On founder-as-brand, the ladder runs from **John Fish / Bookshelved** at the top (YouTuber-built literary hub where owned distribution was the entire underwriting case), through **Marina Mogilko / LinguaTrip** in the middle (millions of subs plus a profitable business, where I structured 5% of 30-year earnings rather than equity), down to the no-community paid-acquisition YC seed at the bottom. HeyClicky sits between Bookshelved and Mogilko -- Farza's 89.3K X following, the 2.9M-view launch demo, 5.8k GitHub stars, a Product Hunt 4.9 across 23 reviews, and an XDA Developers feature constitute owned distribution that pre-existed the product. This is exactly the profile the $60M Slow Creator Fund (Axios, February 12 2025) was built to write into, and it's the strongest single signal in the HeyClicky dossier.

On capital structure, the ladder runs from **Teamshares** (employee-ownership rollup, a structure no other fund could underwrite) at the top, through **Ro** (capital-intensive but a real "venture moment" because 50-state prescription rails required VC dollars to clear) in the middle, down to the generic SaaS cap table needing five rounds to reach default-alive at the bottom. HeyClicky sits at the bottom rung: free, open-source per the README, no pricing page, per-session COGS stack of Claude + AssemblyAI + ElevenLabs, and clone repos (`emreyilmaz46/clicky_windows`, `jasonkneen/openclicky`) already shipping. Farza himself open-sourced the code in April 2026 -- the paid tier must out-deliver the founder's own free build. There is no "venture moment" here, just a Mac overlay app that costs money to run with no monetization disclosed.

On the round itself, the ladder is **Solana** at $0.05/coin (everyone had quit crypto post-ICO crash) at the top, **Sublime Security** in the middle (enterprise security seed while consensus stayed in late-stage SOC SaaS), and the canonical 2026 YC pitch -- "AI agent for [vertical], four tier-1 VCs already in" -- at the bottom. HeyClicky sits one rung above the bottom: **Cluely** just raised $15M from a16z (TechCrunch, June 2025) at ~$5.2M ARR for the same architectural primitive, **Granola** is at $1.5B (TechCrunch, March 2026), and HeyClicky's 2.9M-view demo plus second-time-YC-founder-with-a16z-history will draw a packed party round at consensus pricing. This is exactly the "deck screams hot deal" pattern I de-rate.

On founder conviction, the ladder pins HeyClicky between **Pinterest / Ben Silbermann** (missionary temperament, operator-friend sourcing) and the sanitized YC-consulting-template founder at the bottom. Farza ran buildspace 2019-2024, raised $10M from a16z at $100M, wound it down in August 2024, and frames HeyClicky as "the future of education and the future of AI interfaces" (x.com/FarzaTV/status/2041691382008705321). That's five years of public obsession with the education-tools-for-builders thesis -- missionary, not mercenary. But the wind-down is a real data point about the "weather the downs without getting too manic on the ups" filter (Antoine Buteau, ~2023); a $10M raise that ended in shutdown is exactly the manic-up-then-down arc the criterion warns against.

On category sleeve, HeyClicky sits below the mid rung. The ladder's top is pre-consensus crypto (**Solana, Aleo**) and DTC consumer (**Allbirds**); the middle is healthcare-DTC like **Ro** that required a capital-efficiency override; the bottom is generic AI agent shells. HeyClicky is a consumer AI Mac overlay -- "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024) is the precise re-weighting that pushes this out of my sleeve. The education adjacency via Farza's buildspace audience nudges it off the bottom rung, but not far.

My 36-month forecast: HeyClicky either gets quietly wound down or folded into Farza's next education-shaped venture, with the open-source `farzaa/clicky` repo persisting as a brand-building flywheel for Farza personally -- the more likely outcome is wind-down by 2029 at sub-$2M ARR, given that OpenAI and Anthropic absorb the Mac-overlay primitive natively and the open-source clones (`openclicky`, `clicky_windows`) commoditize the paid tier before pricing ships. The mechanism is the same one that ended buildspace: a charismatic founder-built audience product that never resolves the COGS-versus-monetization gap before the next pivot.

The strongest case against me: this is **Marina Mogilko** miscategorized. Farza IS the asset; HeyClicky is the current expression; the right move is to ignore the equity round entirely and structure a Slow-Creator-Fund instrument on Farza's next 15-20 years of educational IP at 5% of cross-property earnings, exactly the play the dossier on the Mogilko deal documents. The bear-case dismantle: Farza already monetized himself once at $10M from a16z and that ended in August 2024 -- a creator-instrument on him still carries the wind-down risk, and the round on offer is standard YC equity, not the instrument I'd want. The flip condition is concrete: if Slow could re-paper HeyClicky as a creator-equity instrument on Farza's long-arc educational output rather than HeyClicky-the-company equity, I'd write. As shown, I pass.
