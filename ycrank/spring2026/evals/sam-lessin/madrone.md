# Madrone -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Madrone is a two-person Harvey Mudd capex hardware play in a non-consensus YC category (climate cooling, not AI agents) with zero owned distribution and zero domain-credible founders -- the highest-dimension placement is "this isn't the consensus YC deck" and the lowest is "there is no founder-brand or community on the cap table at all," and that pairing is exactly the structure I do not write into. Akshay Trikha (ex-QuantumScape ML) and Erik Meike (ex-Apple iPhone wireless power) are pitching dew-point cooling that delivers 20°C supply water with claimed 30% less power and water than chillers (madrone.cool), and the asset they're building requires multi-year hyperscaler procurement cycles plus repeated rounds to fund a hardware manufacturing curve. That is the canonical anti-shape for my book.

The non-consensus dimension is where Madrone scores best, so let me build the ladder. **HIGH rung: Solana** at $0.05/coin in 2018-2019, where the consensus had quit the category entirely. **MID rung: Sublime Security**, a pre-consensus enterprise security entry while the field stayed in late-stage SOC SaaS. **LOW rung: the canonical 2026 YC AI agent** at $30M post with four tier-1s already in. Madrone sits between MID and LOW -- climate-hardware cooling is genuinely not the 2026 YC AI-agent template, which earns Madrone real credit, but the dossier shows no pricing data, no tier-1 passes named, and no evidence the round is priced below batch median. NVIDIA Inception is a non-equity partner program (madrone.cool), not a price-sensitivity tell. I have to apply my silence-equals-consensus prior: I assume Madrone is taking a standard YC SAFE at the standard YC valuation, and the category being off-consensus is doing the heavy lifting alone.

The founder-as-brand dimension is the floor. **HIGH rung: John Fish/Bookshelved**, where the YouTube channel was the distribution asset that justified the check. **MID rung: Marina Mogilko**, where millions of language-learning subscribers were the underlying asset I bought 5% of for 30 years. **LOW rung: any anonymous B2B industrial seed with no community footprint.** Madrone is sitting on the low rung floor -- Trikha has 18 GitHub followers and a top repo with one star (BTO-TEM-Processing), Meike has no public Twitter and no GitHub repos at all, and the company sells capex equipment to hyperscaler facility teams. There is no distribution asset to underwrite as the underlying instrument, which kills the structural creativity I'd otherwise apply.

On capital structure, the **Teamshares** HIGH rung (employee-ownership rollup that no SaaS-trained VC can underwrite) and **Allbirds** MID rung (DTC consumer with margin cushion) sit far above where Madrone lands; Madrone is closer to the LOW rung -- a generic SaaS seed needing five more rounds -- except worse, because hardware manufacturing curves consume capital harder than SaaS ARR ramps. Ro is the Known Exception for capital-intensive plays where regulatory complexity *is* the moat, but data center procurement schleps are not 50-state prescription rails -- they are just slow B2B sales cycles, which doesn't override Criterion 2. On founder conviction, **Pinterest's Ben Silbermann** as the HIGH rung missionary archetype contrasts with Madrone's profile sharply: I find no public writing from Trikha or Meike on the thermodynamics of dew-point cooling, no opinionated essays on why HVAC incumbents are wrong, no evidence either has been on this problem for 3+ years -- the most relevant artifact is Trikha's GPT-2-Trump GitHub repo, which is the wrong category of conviction signal.

On category sleeve, **Solana** as the HIGH crypto-sleeve rung and **Allbirds** as the MID DTC-sleeve rung are both miles from Madrone, and the LOW rung is generic foundation-model AI infra. Madrone is climate-industrial hardware adjacent to AI infra -- closer to my LOW rung than I'd like, because the demand driver here is the same AI buildout I called "just more internet" on Joe Lonsdale's podcast, and the supply side is HVAC incumbents (Johnson Controls, Trane, Carrier) whose installed-base service revenue gets cannibalized by Madrone's product, which is M&A-friendly but not standalone-venture-friendly.

Forecast at 36 months: Madrone gets acqui-hired by Johnson Controls, Trane, or Flex in the $20-50M range, following the Alloy Enterprises/JCI (May 13, 2026) and JetCool/Flex (November 2024) template -- or it stalls at sub-$2M revenue still pitching pilot programs to Crusoe and CoreWeave. The mechanism producing this is the cannibalization-and-acquisition structure: HVAC incumbents cannot build chiller-less internally without destroying their service annuities, so they buy, and the existing comp set (Alloy, JetCool) shows the price band is suppressed.

Strongest bull case: Madrone is **Ro** for cooling. The capital intensity isn't the failure mode -- it's the moat, because two-person teams that ship working dew-point hardware into 45°C Texas data halls clear a schlep no software-trained competitor will. The 31.5% CAGR liquid-cooling segment (Globe Newswire, May 2026) plus arid-region Texas concentration is a genuine venture moment, and Meike's iPhone wireless power background is exactly the kind of "ship hardware in volume at Apple precision" credential that compounds in this category. That bull case stings -- but it loses to two dossier facts: no pilot LOIs disclosed, and dew-point performance is bounded by ambient wet-bulb temperature, which rises in humid Texas summers and is unvalidated in retrieved sources. **What would flip my verdict: a signed pilot LOI with a named hyperscaler (Meta, AWS, Crusoe, CoreWeave) at a sub-$15M post-money entry.** Absent that, Madrone is a structurally acquisition-bound capex hardware play priced like a software seed, and that's the trade I most explicitly refuse to take.
