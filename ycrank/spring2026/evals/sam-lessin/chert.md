# Chert -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Chert is the canonical 2026 YC archetype I open my "Pass Example" with — a two-person team pitching "Twilio for iMessage" at a standard $500K YC SAFE into a category that already contains Sendblue, LoopMessage, Blooio, and Linq. Gary Gao and Ian Fong did do six months of Wizard-of-Oz fieldwork before launching — building iMessage projects for leasing companies, DTC startups, and home service agencies per the Product Hunt copy — and that is the one signal in the Chert dossier I'd otherwise want to underline. The problem is that the rest of the Chert profile reads like it was generated by the same template as 30 other P26 decks: AI tag, SaaS tag, Conversational AI tag, CRM integrations, blue bubbles, no public writing, no community, no unusual instrument, no price discount. The signature here is competent technical founders doing the right pre-build hygiene inside the exact sleeve I down-weight on the exact platform Apple can extinguish.

Solana at $0.05/coin is the HIGH rung on my round-pricing ladder — the asset class everyone else had quit, repriced to where my edge was just showing up. Chert sits much closer to the LOW rung, which in my own Pass Example reads "AI agent for [vertical], four tier-1 VCs already in." Chert is not even at the four-tier-1 level of consensus heat; it's the duller version, a standard $500K YC accelerator check with a "backers" page that lists LinkedIn and Grafana in a way the dossier itself flags as suspicious, sales gated to a cal.com booking, and a Launch HN at item id 48267829 that reads like 50 other Launch HNs in the same week. There is no price discount, no tier-1 visibly passing, and no illegibility — Chert is a CPaaS reseller with an Apple-protocol wrapper, which is the most legible possible asset and therefore the one I have the least pricing edge on.

Teamshares anchors my capital-structure ladder because the employee-ownership rollup is a structure no other VC will underwrite — that is the kind of cap table I optimize for. Chert is the inverse mechanism: an API platform whose "scalable pricing structure" presumes consumption billing against per-line CPaaS economics, which is the most generic SaaS cap-table shape in the YC stack. Over 18 months that structure compounds the wrong way for me — Chert has to raise a priced seed extension, then a Series A on ARR multiples, while gross margin gets squeezed by Blooio's $39/mo flat-rate floor and Sendblue's feature breadth, because none of these companies have pricing power against an Apple-controlled rail. There is no revenue-share instrument, no holdco wrapper, no employee-equity angle, no creator royalty — just a standard SAFE into a CPaaS commodity.

Gary Gao spent the last year on ML model architectures and neuromorphic chips at Penn before pivoting to iMessage infrastructure, and Ian Fong was incoming JPMorgan with Courant fast-algorithm research before deciding Chert was the thing. The counterfactual for moving Chert from a 7-ish to a 12-ish on conviction would be public writing — a Gary Gao essay on why every existing iMessage API gets line-health wrong, or an Ian Fong thread on the Apple anti-spam detection mechanism Sendblue can't see. Neither exists in the dossier; the founders' LinkedIn lines read "Co-Founder @ Chert (YC P26) | CS & Wharton," which is the sanitized consulting language I explicitly de-rate. The six months of pre-build manual fieldwork is real and is the thing keeping Chert above floor on this axis, but conviction without a public artifact is just opportunity-response in technical clothing.

John Fish / Bookshelved is the single-anchor parallel for the founder-as-brand axis — I wrote that check because Fish brought millions of YouTube subscribers as the actual asset on the cap table. Chert brings the inverse: the dossier explicitly notes that follower counts on @GaryGao891219, @ianyfong, and @Cherthq are "not retrievable" — which in 2026 is itself a signal, because retrievable means non-zero. Chert has 179 Product Hunt upvotes and one Launch HN, which is the YC distribution playbook, not owned distribution. None of the Slow Creator Fund logic applies to Chert because there is no person to underwrite — the asset is the API, and the API is undifferentiated.

The category sleeve mechanism is where Chert fails most cleanly against my own framework. "AI is just more internet… it's not disruptive. Crypto is fundamentally disruptive" — the Joe Lonsdale podcast line is the exact filter that catches Chert, because Chert is an AI conversational agent on top of an Apple protocol, which is two layers of platform dependency stacked. Compare to my Aleo or Lightning Labs entries where the on-chain economic activity itself was the moat: Chert has no protocol, no token, no on-chain anything, just a wrapper that Apple can re-permission. The Apple platform dependency named in the Key Risks section is not a side note — it is the entire category thesis, and Chert has no answer beyond "SMS/RCS fallback," which is just admitting the moat doesn't exist.

My 36-month forecast for Chert: $500K–$2M ARR against Sendblue's larger book, no Series A at a venture-shaped markup, acquihired by a CRM — HubSpot, Close, or GoHighLevel, all of which are already integration partners per trychert.com — for low single-digit millions, or alternatively a quiet pivot into a CRM-native iMessage feature inside one of those platforms. This forecast falls out of the signature: technical founders with real domain prep but inside a commoditizing AI-shell sleeve on an Apple rail produce CRM-feature outcomes, not venture outcomes, because the regatta tacks all run through a platform owner who can re-price the underlying.

The bull case I have to take seriously: Chert's six months of fieldwork on leasing companies, DTC startups, and home service agencies is exactly the Wizard-of-Oz signal I claim to weight, and the "up to 65% response rates and 90% action rates" on outbound iMessage — even discounted for self-reported framing — is the kind of channel arbitrage that compounded for early Twilio resellers when SMS was new. Sendblue at $500K reported funding per PitchBook is not an unkillable incumbent, and a sharper line-health primitive could in fact become the category default. None of my Known Exceptions cover Chert though — no early-entry markup absorption like Robinhood, no operator-friend channel like Pinterest via Ben Silbermann, no regulatory schlep like Ro's 50-state prescription rails — so the bull case doesn't get to invoke an override. The single condition that would flip me: Chert publishes a credible technical writeup of an Apple anti-spam detection mechanism the four competitors visibly miss, AND prices the seed extension at a discount to the YC P26 batch median. Without both, Chert is a pass.
