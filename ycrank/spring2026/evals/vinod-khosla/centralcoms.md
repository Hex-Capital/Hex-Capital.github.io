This is the **Pass Example** pattern in my persona file -- GPT-4 wraps HubSpot -- transposed onto property management. I read CentralComs the way I read that pitch: in thirty seconds.

The absence is not damning at pre-seed -- I said as much in my own persona defaults -- but it gives me nothing to override the consequence-magnitude problem, the way the Slash pivot story overrode consumer-banking's small consequence.

I am structurally over-resourced for this deal, and any Tier-1 generalist with a proptech vertical can write the check more cheaply than Khosla Ventures. The dossier names no capital intensity I can deploy against.

**36-month forecast:** CentralComs either reaches $1-3M ARR and is tuck-in acquired by AppFolio, Buildium, or Yardi for under $30M to neutralize a cross-PMS aggregation threat, or AppFolio Realm-X and Buildium Lumina eat the workflow natively and CentralComs winds down quietly by 2028. That bull case stings until I remember Slash got the $100M Series C check because of a documented pivot with stated reasoning, not because YC alone moved me; the consequence-magnitude prior still has to be overridden by something, and CentralComs's dossier offers no override. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: CentralComs signs an exclusive cross-PMS data-sharing partnership with two of {AppFolio, Buildium, Yardi} that structurally locks competitors out, OR posts $750K ARR with >90% net retention by month nine. Absent either, CentralComs is the McKinsey 1986 forecast of cellphones -- a confident extrapolation of the present, into a future where the present has already moved.
