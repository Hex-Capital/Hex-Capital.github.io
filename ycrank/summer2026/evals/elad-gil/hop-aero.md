# Hop Aero -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Matija Milenovic shipped hardware to orbit on SpaceX Transporter-3 via porkchop and Jacob Balaj wrenched on Falcon 9/Heavy I&T -- that founder signature is real, and on most pre-seed dossiers I'd lean in. But Hop Aero is a science-adjacent rocket cargo bet competing for the same DoD Point-to-Point Delivery wallet where SpaceX already holds the $102M AFRL prime, and that high-shipper / low-index pairing is exactly the profile I've trained myself to walk past: credible operators in a market whose index slot is already taken. The signature here is "good team, wrong rung."

The Stripe (2010) lens is the wrong analogy for Hop Aero -- Stripe sat in the workflow path that every entrant to a compounding category had to touch, which is what an index position actually means. Hop Aero's dossier puts the company in the opposite position: SpaceX Starship targeting ~100-ton C-17-class loads with a $102M USAF Rocket Cargo contract dated January 2022, Stoke Space at $990M raised through Series D, Inversion at $54M for orbital Arc, Varda at $60M STRATFI. Hop Aero is reaching for the *smaller* sub-orbital tier *below* Starship in the same procurement pipeline that the prime already won. That's not an index on rocket cargo -- that's a sub-scale alternative inside a program whose anchor demonstration is already contracted to the company everyone else in this list is also trying to underprice.

Lyft Series C is the failure mechanism on the dimension where Hop Aero is weakest. I passed on Lyft because rideshare looked like a winner-take-all network-effect market and the dossier didn't show a credible path to share inversion -- #2 in a market where the procurement structure rewards #1 is just a worse version of the obvious bet. The Hop Aero dossier reproduces the pattern in defense form: USTRANSCOM signed CRADAs with multiple providers in 2024, the FY25 budget redesignated Rocket Cargo Vanguard as a Space Force prototype line, and the named CRADA holders are SpaceX, Sierra Space, Rocket Lab, and Blue Origin. Hop Aero is not on that list. DoD procurement of this kind doesn't oligopolize the way ride-hailing did -- it consolidates harder, toward the prime that flew first.

Catalyst-wise, Hop Aero has the right *category* timing -- Rocket Cargo Vanguard's FY25 prototype redesignation and the 2024 USTRANSCOM CRADAs are a real recent re-opening, the kind of 24-month window I scored Anduril (2022) on. The mechanism that compounds over 18 months, though, is which vendor the program office anchors its first successful demo around; once SpaceX or Stoke flies a P2PD demonstration payload, the catalyst Hop Aero is riding becomes a procurement gate Hop Aero is locked outside of. Catalyst exists, but it's not Hop Aero's catalyst.

Revenue velocity is where my "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth, something's fundamentally broken" rule has to be translated -- Hop Aero isn't AI, it's defense hardware, and the equivalent signal at this stage is non-equity government capital. The dossier shows $1.25M USAF SBIR. That is a Phase II-ish ticket, useful for runway, not a velocity signal. The website hop-aero.com renders only a logo placeholder per WebFetch June 2026. For comparison, the version of this dimension that would have moved me looks like Varda's $60M STRATFI for hypersonic testbed reentry -- that's the order-of-magnitude that says the program office has chosen you. $1.25M says they let you in the door.

Founder shipping is the one dimension where Hop Aero earns a real check mark and the place the Perplexity (2022) parallel partly loads. Milenovic shipped a porkchop payload on Transporter-3 -- actual orbital hardware, not a deck -- and led a 20+ person supersonic UAV team at Destinus; Balaj's I&T history runs through Falcon 9/Heavy refurbishment, Virgin Orbit's LauncherOne, Masten's lunar lander work, and SpinLaunch. These are operators who have touched flight metal, which is the only legible pre-seed signal that matters in launch. The gap versus Perplexity is that Aravind shipped *between meetings* on a product whose miracle (LLMs work) was already validated; Milenovic and Balaj would need to ship between meetings on a vehicle whose miracle (reusable hypersonic point-to-point landing in unprepared terrain) is not yet validated by anyone, including SpaceX.

The single-miracle test is where Hop Aero's "science project" framing crystallizes and the Anduril (2022) Known Exception fails to apply. To make Hop Aero work you need: reusable thermal protection at hypersonic reentry, precision landing on unprepared austere terrain, FAA Part 450 licensing for a novel vehicle class, ITAR/export compliance for foreign-born technical leadership, AND displacement of SpaceX as the P2PD prime. That's not one miracle; it's a chain. Anduril overrode my "no science projects" rule because the hardware referenced existing autonomy stacks and the miracle isolated to software-on-defense *distribution* -- and because I wrote a growth-stage check, not a pre-seed one. Hop Aero inverts all three Anduril override conditions: pre-seed stage, hardware does not reference existing flown stacks, and the miracle is not isolated to distribution.

My 36-month forecast: Hop Aero ends up acqui-hired by Stoke Space or Inversion for under $30M in talent value, or limps on Phase II/STRATFI follow-ons without a flight-tested vehicle, well short of a P2PD production contract. The mechanism is compositional -- the catalyst window closes around whichever vendor flies the first P2PD demo (overwhelmingly likely SpaceX given the $102M head start), the SBIR ladder concentrates rather than diversifies, and Milenovic and Balaj become attractive talent for a better-capitalized cargo program rather than principals on their own.

The bull case worth steelmanning: Hop Aero is the Anduril analog for sub-orbital cargo -- credible shippers in a defense market everyone calls dead-end, riding a specific 24-month catalyst (FY25 P2PD prototype line) that consensus angels are misreading. I have to take that seriously because I made exactly that override on Anduril. It loses on the math. Anduril's miracle was distribution on top of hardware pathways that already existed; Hop Aero's miracle stack includes the propulsion, the thermal protection, the landing, AND the distribution, with $3M pre-seed against Stoke's $990M and SpaceX's $102M contracted demo. The bull case requires Hop Aero to compress a Stoke-scale capitalization curve into an SBIR-funded timeline, and the dossier shows no mechanism for that compression. The condition that would flip my verdict: a disclosed hot-fire test of a reusable hypersonic propulsion article paired with a STRATFI or Phase III award of $25M+ within 12 months -- absent that, the founder shipping signal is real but pointed at the wrong rung.
