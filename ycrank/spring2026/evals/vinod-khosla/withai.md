# WithAI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

WithAI is the Bridgewater-pedigree expression of my Pass Example -- two Princeton '23 founders, Ian McInnis and Benjamin Finch, selling a "Multiplier" workflow tool into fundamental equity hedge funds at >$100K ARR three months in, where the highest-loading dimension is founder learning rate by default and the lowest is required-capital profile, because a 3-person team with zero open jobs at WithAI is not a deployment that absorbs the kind of check I write. The success-state at maximum ambition is that fundamental equity PMs flip from "80% gathering, 20% acting" to the reverse -- Mercator Partners' own testimonial on withai.co. That is a productivity gain inside one persona at one buyer type. No incumbent regime collapses.

The HIGH rung on consequence magnitude is **OpenAI** (2019), where I wrote $50M into a no-revenue company because the success-state was civilizational redirection of cognition itself. WithAI shares the surface vocabulary -- agents, foundation models, knowledge work -- but the mechanism that made the OpenAI check load-bearing was that the world-state at full success had no recognizable analog to the world before it. WithAI's full-success world-state is that ~100,000 PE/hedge-fund professionals (Sacra's Rogo-adjacent number) get faster at synthesizing Bloomberg, Refinitiv, FactSet, Tegus, and AlphaSense feeds inside their own AWS tenant. That is the **DoorDash** (2013) shape at best -- consensus capture of a workflow -- without DoorDash's consumer-commerce surface area. My Fortune (2026-03-06) frame is "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor"; WithAI is going after the slice where the labor is already among the highest-paid and lowest-headcount in the economy. The arithmetic of consequence does not arrive.

The Pass Example failure mechanism -- "we use GPT-4 to summarize sales calls" -- is domain-expert continuity wrapping a foundation model in a workflow. Ian McInnis spent his post-Princeton career as a Bridgewater Investment Associate running "how can we use LLMs to invest?", and Ben Finch came out of Sentient Labs on prompt security and multi-agent coordination. That is the Salesforce-veteran pattern translated to Westport, not the Sergey Levine pattern at **Physical Intelligence** (2024). The dossier itself flags that WithAI's founder thesis concedes agents are "powerful but insufficient" for investing because outputs are non-verifiable -- a candid admission that the central technical miracle has no ground-truth evaluator. That is not defying expert consensus. That is restating it with a client-cloud deployment diagram.

On technical and capital risk that repels consensus VCs, WithAI's client-side deployment across AWS, Azure, and GCP plus 60+ integrations is enterprise plumbing, not the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) $115M Series A pattern where I led because the science was investor-toxic. Any Tier-1 generalist -- Sequoia already wrote the $75M Series C into Rogo at $750M post-money in Jan 2026 -- can underwrite this. The compounding mechanism over 18 months is that fund-specific ontology accumulates per client, but switching costs in hedge-fund tech historically erode against incumbents with broader data partnerships; Hebbia at ~30% of asset managers and Rogo's OpenAI collaboration are the actual gravitational wells. WithAI's moat compounds in months while competitors' moats compound in capital.

For founder learning rate, the counterfactual that would flip the score a tier is a documented pivot in WithAI's first 18 months -- McInnis and Finch publicly retracting the "client-cloud single-tenant" thesis and shipping a different shape because the sales cycle math broke. The dossier shows zero such evidence; WithAI is six months old on a linear plan, which is why I apply my 7/15 absent-evidence default rather than penalizing. The **Slash** (2024) Kanye pivot worked as an override signal because the founder had already taken a public thesis loss and updated. WithAI's founders have not yet had that moment, which is stage-appropriate but not score-elevating.

WithAI's required-capital profile is the single dimension where I am most clearly the wrong investor, parallel to where I would have been wrong for any pre-Series-B SaaS optimization play: a 3-person team with 0 open jobs on the YC page and an enterprise sales motion that hedge funds will pay six-to-seven figures for does not need my $50M check. It needs a $3-5M seed and a sales hire. My $105M co-led seed in **Genesis AI** (2025) deployed against robot foundation model training -- a roadmap where capital is the constraint. WithAI's roadmap is constrained by enterprise procurement cycles and headcount, neither of which my check unlocks.

My 36-month forecast on WithAI: acquired by Hebbia or AlphaSense for $40-120M as an acqui-hire-plus-client-book around late 2027 or 2028, after Rogo's Sequoia-funded hedge-fund expansion via the OpenAI collaboration compresses WithAI's lane to a handful of mid-tier fundamental funds and the Bridgewater co-CIO angel intros run dry. The mechanism is straightforward: Hebbia and Rogo are the consolidators in finance-vertical AI, WithAI's client-cloud single-tenant deployment is acquirable IP, and Ian McInnis plus Ben Finch are talent the acquirers will pay to keep for 24 months.

The bull case against my pass is the **DoorDash** (2013) override -- consensus consumer-logistics, capital-light, no science miracle, $72B IPO 2020. WithAI's analog is that Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour, the co-CIOs of Bridgewater, wrote angel checks; that >$100K ARR landed in three months from a cold start; and that hedge-fund regulatory and competitive constraints genuinely do block multi-tenant SaaS, which could harden into the structural wedge Hebbia cannot follow without rebuilding its product. That stings because it is the exact shape of an Unstated Pattern in my book -- YC-pedigreed, founder-led enterprise sale, named angels in the buyer's exact org chart. But the wedge has to clear Rogo's $143M war chest and Hebbia's installed base inside the same 18 months WithAI is hiring its first non-founder. One condition flips my verdict: WithAI reaching $5M ARR across 10+ named tier-1 hedge funds by Spring 2027 -- that would prove the client-cloud regulatory moat is real and not a feature Hebbia ships in a quarter.
