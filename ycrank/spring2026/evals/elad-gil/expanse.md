# Expanse -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Expanse is a thesis-research spinout sitting in a real structural seam -- the post-Run:ai HPC/Slurm gap that nobody is consolidating -- staffed by four first-time founders whose CEO Ismaeel Bashir just finished his MSc at Edinburgh's EPCC. That high/low pairing -- credible nonobvious market timing yoked to an organizationally underweight team -- is the entire diagnostic for Expanse. The single most important fact in the dossier is that NVIDIA closed Run:ai in December 2024 and is open-sourcing it on a Kubernetes-native track, which leaves Slurm-based national HPC and quant batch environments underserved. The single most concerning fact is that the CEO is 25 years old at most, the CTO Nikodem Bieniek has zero retrievable public footprint, and the company already has four co-founders before it has a logo on the homepage.

On Nonobvious Market Timing, my ladder runs Coinbase (read the digital-asset infrastructure shift in 2012 before consensus) at the top, Gusto (boring payroll category with steady regulatory tailwind but no capability-curve break) in the middle, and the consumer social apps I pass on every batch (no exogenous shift, stable platform layer) at the bottom. Expanse sits between Gusto and Coinbase, closer to Gusto. The Run:ai consolidation plus the absolute-dollar pain of 30% over-allocation on H100/B200 cycles is a genuine structural opening -- hyperscalers have negative incentive to optimize away their own billable GPU hours, and SchedMD has not productized prediction. This is the right kind of "stuck market cracking open" pattern, but GPU-optimization broadly is not invisible to consensus the way crypto-rails was in 2012; the nonobviousness is specifically the HPC/Slurm-not-Kubernetes wedge, which is narrower.

On Operator-Scaler Founder Profile, the ladder runs Parker Conrad rebuilding Rippling after scaling Zenefits at the top, Henrique Dubugras at Brex (operator-mindset but first US scaling) in the middle, and the equal-co-founder undergraduate teams I routinely pass on at the bottom. Expanse sits squarely on the bottom rung. Bashir, Yafet Melake, and Eren Mendi are all final-year or just-graduated Edinburgh students; Melake and Mendi list G-Research as SWE interns. None of the four has scaled an organization from 10 to 1,000. The four-founder structure with no clear decision-maker hits my equal-co-founder red flag from the NFX framework directly -- somebody has to make the call when the deal slips and the runway tightens, and four equal co-founders with overlapping Edinburgh ties don't have that command structure proven yet.

On Infrastructure-Layer Positioning, the ladder runs Stripe (payments API embedded in thousands of businesses) at the top, Checkr (narrower API but still infrastructure) in the middle, and Wish (no platform layer, no API, consumer marketplace) at the bottom. Expanse sits between Checkr and Wish: the passive-telemetry agent is sidecar infrastructure that optimizes existing Slurm scheduling, but Slurm itself is the workflow layer -- Expanse rides on top of it. That is closer to the application layer Gil typically discounts, and it gets weaker as Run:ai's open-source extends. On Single-Miracle Viability, my ladder runs Harvey (one exogenous miracle: GPT-4 capability) at the top, Gusto (no breakthroughs needed, just execution) in the middle, and Anduril (three simultaneous miracles, only justified by overwhelming market timing) at the bottom; Expanse sits between Gusto and Anduril -- the predictor must actually generalize beyond Bashir's MSc cluster, the sales cycle into national HPC centres is multi-quarter government procurement, and Run:ai open-sourcing into Slurm could commoditize the tech, which compounds to roughly two miracles.

On Margin Architecture, the ladder runs Abridge (per-encounter, scales with value) at the top, Gusto (per-employee SaaS, sticky negative-churn) in the middle, and Wish (commodity-margin marketplace) at the bottom. Expanse has no pricing page on expanse.sh, so I'm applying the absent-evidence default. The right pricing here is share-of-savings or per-cluster outcome-based -- if Expanse prices on percentage of GPU hours saved, this fits the "selling units of cognition" thesis cleanly; if they default to per-seat or flat per-cluster SaaS, they leave value on the table. Insufficient evidence to credit them yet.

In 36 months my forecast for Expanse is acqui-hire by NVIDIA, SchedMD, or CoreWeave for under $40M as a feature inside a larger orchestration play, or stalled at sub-$2M ARR with a handful of quant-fund pilots while Run:ai's open-source extends to Slurm. The mechanism producing that outcome is exactly the high/low signature: Bashir's research IP is real enough to validate the wedge but the four-founder undergraduate team lacks the operator-scaler firepower to convert a multi-quarter HPC procurement cycle into category-defining ARR before the incumbent open-source release closes the productization gap.

The strongest opposing case is the boring-but-critical pattern. HPC GPU optimization is precisely the kind of unglamorous infrastructure that prestige-seeking founders avoid -- it looks like Gusto in payroll or Checkr in background checks, where the absence of competition is the moat. Bashir's EPCC thesis is a real research artifact, not LinkedIn theatre, and Melake and Mendi's G-Research origin gives Expanse direct quant-fund GTM access, which is a fast-buying logo set. If Bashir's predictor truly beats published baselines, and Expanse prices on percentage-of-savings against $30K H100 hours, the unit economics at one quant fund pay for the company. That case stings -- it's the exact pattern I backed Gusto and Checkr on. What dismantles it is the unverified "beat every published baseline" claim with no peer-reviewed citation, the four-equal-co-founder structure, and the domain fragmentation across expanse.sh, expanse.org.uk, and getexpanse.io that signals unresolved naming basics. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a signed paid pilot with a named tier-1 quant fund (Citadel, Jane Street, G-Research itself) or a national supercomputing centre at >$250K ACV within nine months -- not a free two-week eval, a paid contract. Until then, neutral.
