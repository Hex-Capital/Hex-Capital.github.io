# Napkin Math -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Napkin Math is a tactile consumer iOS app — "Napkin Math: Gamify Nutrition," App Store ID 6758642182 — shipping into the most consensus AI category of 2026, eight weeks after MyFitnessPal acquired Cal AI (Athletech News, March 2026) and absorbed the exact AI-photo-logging mechanic that would have been Napkin Math's differentiator. The signature is plain: a downloadable artifact I can install in 90 seconds, dropped into a lane where the incumbent at 220M registered users and $310M revenue (Business of Apps, 2026) just bought the disruption. The physical verifiability is real. Everything around it is consensus.

HQ Trivia (2017) is the rung I keep reaching for here, because it is the one company in my portfolio where I overrode every governance objection on the strength of tactile virality — I could play it and feel the pull before any metric existed. Napkin Math earns the analogy halfway: I can open the App Store, hit TestFlight, see Jynnie Tang's gaming-UX fingerprints (1st place MIT Web Programming Competition 2018, One More Multiverse), and feel for the "share your table with friends" hook. But where HQ Trivia was a screaming siren of viral pull at week one, the Napkin Math App Store listing reads "not enough ratings or reviews to display" (App Store, 2026). The artifact exists; the pull does not yet. That is a MID-rung placement, not a HIGH one — I am not feeling the room go quiet at dinner because everyone is opening the app.

Crusoe (~2018) is the rung that exposes how unweird Napkin Math is. The Crusoe pitch was three uninvestable things stapled together and credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds — that was the tell. Napkin Math is the inverse: a YC Spring 2026 consumer AI app in a category where Cal AI hit $34M ARR in eighteen months (Latka), MacroFactor sits at ~$500K MRR (Prospeo), and the 2026 roundups list Vora, Cronometer, FatSecret, Fitia, Welling.ai, SnapCalorie, and Lose It as live alternatives (Nutrola, 2026). Pete Koomen is the partner, the deck likely opens with "Strava for food," and nothing in the dossier suggests anyone has passed on this for moral, regulatory, or capital-intensity reasons. The category is not laughed at. It is over-served. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus, and Napkin Math arrives after.

Manuel Castro at Sonos and Verkada, Claire Nord at Apple and Samsara, Jynnie Tang at Retool and One More Multiverse — three MIT alumni who met "roughly a decade ago" per the YC page — gives me a credentialed team with no autobiographical contact with the food problem I can detect. Mechanism analysis on this dimension over 18 months: founders who picked the problem from a product-market scan rather than from lived hunger, disordered eating, athletic obsession, or a parent's diabetes diagnosis tend to pivot under category pressure, because the conviction is not load-bearing when the metrics flatten. The dossier contains no founder story about food — no "I tracked my own meals through MIT and hated calorie targets," no mom-was-a-dietitian, no recovery arc. The Stanford/Goldman pattern-match I avoid is here in the MIT/Apple/Samsara/Retool form: a four-logo team page where a polymath generalist could rebuild the product in a quarter.

Counterfactual on the CEO axis: for the governance score to flip a tier, the dossier would have to name one CEO with majority operating authority among Castro, Nord, and Tang. It names none — all three are labeled "Founder" on the YC page. This is the GameCrush (~2011) and HQ Trivia (2017) configuration I have explicitly flagged as a near-term blow-up risk — "who's the tiebreaker?" — and it lives at the front of a consumer-social product where the first hard disagreement will be about whether to chase virality or retention. Three co-founders with no declared CEO is the structural inverse of the Anduril (2017) template where Palmer Luckey's authority was visible from day one.

Single-anchor parallel on capital intensity: compare Napkin Math to Anduril (2017), where I tattooed my conviction onto an unsexy defense category because the moat compounded in a domain other investors refused to enter. Napkin Math is consumer iOS freemium ([Inferred] pricing not disclosed) with no hardware, no regulatory perimeter, no energy arbitrage, no defense posture. The legal entity is Paper Star, Corp.; the moat candidates listed in the dossier are "social graph" and "longitudinal personal food data," both [Inferred]. AI photo-to-food is now commodity — Cal AI was built by two teenagers (TechCrunch, March 2025). The category where the moat compounds with time is not this one.

Thirty-six month forecast: Napkin Math gets acqui-hired by MyFitnessPal, Strava, or Whoop for under $15M with negligible ARR, or shuts down quietly, by Spring 2029 — driven by the dimensional signature of a tactile-but-undifferentiated consumer app in a post-Cal-AI-acquisition lane where the incumbent will ship a "no-calorie-counting" toggle as a settings change inside its 220M-user base before Napkin Math reaches 100K DAU. The mechanism is incumbent absorption of the only positioning differentiator, compounded by three-founder governance ambiguity under category pressure.

The strongest bull case is the HQ Trivia (2017) known exception — explosive product-led traction in a tactile consumer artifact can override co-founder governance, full stop. Tang's gaming background at One More Multiverse and her 2018 MIT Web Programming Competition win is exactly the Yusupov-shaped consumer-virality skill I want at the top of a social food app, and the "share your table with friends" mechanic is Strava-coded in a way Cal AI's calorie scanner is not. If Napkin Math hits viral coefficient north of 1.2 inside six months on TestFlight, the governance and consensus-category objections collapse the way they did for HQ Trivia. The dossier evidence that wins against this case: the App Store listing has insufficient ratings to display, no Product Hunt launch surfaced, no press, no follower counts, no funding announcement located — six months in, the artifact exists but the pull has not materialized, and HQ Trivia did not have to wait for it. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a public 100K+ DAU number with a 7-day retention curve above 30% by Q3 2026, sourced from anywhere other than the founders themselves.
