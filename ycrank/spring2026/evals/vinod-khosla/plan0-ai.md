# PLAN0 AI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

PLAN0 AI is an optimization play wrapped around a paradigmatic domain-expert founder -- the exact shape my Pass Example was written to describe. Arash Barati spent eight years in CRE private equity, three in lending, three in development, totaling $7B of deals, and is now applying computer vision to compress a 2-week estimating workflow into 30 minutes. That is the success-state at maximum ambition: pre-construction underwriting gets faster. No incumbent regime collapses; no civilizational variable moves; the $15T labor-displacement frame from Fortune 2026-03-06 does not touch this. PLAN0 AI is **the HubSpot-GPT-summarizer of construction**, with better founders.

The consequence ladder anchors at **OpenAI** (2019) -- a success-state that redraws the cost of cognition itself, which is why I wrote the $50M check before the moat was legible. PLAN0 AI's success-state is that a developer's pro-forma underwriting cycle goes from 14 days to half a day on a $20B project pipeline (PLAN0 launch claim). The economic mechanism that "breaks if this works" -- my inverted question -- is the estimating-consultant line item on a development budget, which is a rounding error against the $15T labor frame. Togal, Kreo, STACK, Beam, InEight, and at least two more AI estimators benchmarked by Robotics & Automation News in Feb 2026 are all chasing the same workflow; the success-state for PLAN0 AI is being one of three winners in a category that none of those names will reshape civilizationally.

The tech-risk ladder bottoms out at **DoorDash** (2013) -- capital-light, software-only, no scientific miracle, fundable by any Tier-1 generalist. DoorDash got the exception because consumer-logistics reshape carried real consequence magnitude and Tony Xu's ambition framing was unmistakable. PLAN0 AI inherits the DoorDash anti-pattern -- $4M seed, commodity computer vision against six benchmarked competitors, no named technical breakthrough -- without inheriting DoorDash's consequence-magnitude offset. The "proprietary localized cost database" is the claimed moat, but RSMeans and Gordian license equivalent feeds to any well-capitalized competitor, and the partnerships feeding PLAN0 AI's database are unnamed in every public source. This is software risk a generalist would underwrite at $4M seed; my edge is not here.

Founder-defies-expert-consensus is where PLAN0 AI registers most explicitly as anti-pattern. Arash Barati's profile is Columbia MSRED, adjunct professor, $7B of underwriting at brand-name CRE shops -- the "20 years at incumbent, now applying same playbook with AI" silhouette my TED Talk warned against, citing UC Berkeley's 28,000-prediction study where experts beat chance "only slightly." There is no public moment where Arash Barati contradicted CRE underwriting orthodoxy; the founding story is continuity, not defiance. The **Glean** exception applies only when capital scale and consequence magnitude override the consensus-credential profile; PLAN0 AI carries neither. Shervin Barati's Apple AirPods background and Dimitris Pagonakis's Citadel/MIT pedigree compound the same pattern -- credentialed, conformist, competent.

Learning-rate evidence is thin in both directions across PLAN0 AI's dossier -- no documented pivot, no public thesis update, no "we were wrong about X" moment for any of the three founders. I apply my absent-evidence default rather than penalize, because 18 months of company history is short runway for the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot shape to emerge. Eighteen months from now the falsifiable test is whether PLAN0 AI pivots from developer/lender underwriting -- where Barati's $7B network is the wedge -- into GC-side takeoffs where Togal's $17.2M raise and 98% accuracy benchmark dominate, or vice versa. A documented pivot would move this dimension; its absence today does not.

Required-capital profile makes PLAN0 AI structurally wrong for my check. The roadmap is per-seat SaaS to developers and lenders with $4M already raised at seed -- this is $5-10M to $1M ARR economics, not the $115M **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) Series A profile or the $105M **Genesis AI** (2025) seed where capital depth is the moat. I am over-resourced for PLAN0 AI by an order of magnitude, and any Tier-1 generalist -- Bessemer, a16z growth, Insight -- can fund the Series A on metrics alone. Capital intensity is not the moat here; relationships are, and relationships do not absorb $50M checks.

PLAN0 AI in 36 months: $3-8M ARR serving 40-120 mid-market developers and regional lenders, acquired by **Procore** or **Autodesk** for $50-120M as a feature inside their existing pre-construction suites, or absorbed into Togal's roadmap if Togal raises a real Series B against its $17.2M base. The mechanism is straightforward -- Arash Barati's CRE network produces a respectable enterprise sales motion in a relationship-driven category, computer-vision parity with six competitors prevents premium pricing, and the cost-database "moat" gets neutralized by an incumbent licensing Gordian. This is a fine outcome for a generalist seed fund and an irrelevant outcome for Khosla Ventures.

The strongest bull case I owe PLAN0 AI is the **Glean** exception: Arash Barati's $7B CRE relationships are the construction-industry equivalent of Arvind Jain's Google search credibility, and the "$20B of projects already running through the platform" claim, if true and converting, is a data-accumulation flywheel that compounds faster in construction than in horizontal SaaS because cost data is hyper-local and partnership-gated. If PLAN0 AI lands three named top-20 ENR contractor partnerships and discloses a 5%+ miss rate against InEight's 1.8% benchmark within 12 months, the cost database stops being a claim and starts being a moat. That case stings -- but Glean got the override at hundreds-of-millions in capital scale serving Fortune-500 information asymmetry, and PLAN0 AI is at $4M serving a labor line that does not move my $15T frame. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: PLAN0 AI publishing a public accuracy benchmark below 3% on heterogeneous commercial plans paired with two named institutional lender deployments within 18 months. Absent that, I pass -- not because PLAN0 AI is a bad company, but because make a great company and the money takes care of itself, and this great company's money will be taken care of by someone other than me.
