# Hub.xyz -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

The first thing that jumps out is the co-founder situation. Léonie Nguyen, listed as co-founder and CBO, was appointed CEO of Meey Group — a Vietnamese proptech company — in October 2025. She's not on the YC page. When a co-founder takes the CEO title at an unrelated company while your startup is entering YC, the signal is loud. Signals are what people do, despite what they say. A principal doesn't split themselves across two companies. This alone tells me the founding team's commitment structure has fractures I can't ignore.

Now, specific knowledge. I always ask: if these founders disappeared, would the company's core insight survive? Armin has an industrial engineering degree, LP positions at two venture funds, and "early crypto experience." Tim has business school credentials and financial data work at Tiffany & Co. in Japan. Neither founder has public GitHub repos. They're hiring a "Founding Full Stack AI Engineer" — meaning the person who builds the core product hasn't joined yet. The team identified "AI training data via DePIN" as a market opportunity. That's trend analysis, not specific knowledge. Compare this to Aravind at Perplexity, who spent years at Google Brain building the exact systems his company productizes. Here, the founders' knowledge stack is interchangeable — you could swap them with any competent operators from the crypto-AI intersection and the company would be substantively identical. That's the definition of replaceable.

The leverage audit reveals an architecture that looks permissionless but is actually borrowed. Yes, the DePIN model scales — 500,000 users contributing bandwidth is a real number. Code on Ethereum, P2P mesh networking, zero marginal cost per additional node. But this leverage belongs to Ethereum and to the users' ISPs, not to Hub.xyz. Strip away the token incentives and ask: do those 500,000 users stay? They're earning IQ Points redeemable for $HUB tokens through quizzes and Discord engagement. This isn't organic pull — it's purchased distribution wearing the mask of a network effect. When Notion grew to millions, users stayed because the product was indispensable. When token prices collapse or regulatory pressure hits the TGE, the incentive evaporates. A network held together by token speculation is structurally fragile — it's the OpenSea problem I've seen before.

The strongest bull case: AI training data is a genuine bottleneck that's getting worse. Regulatory friction around web scraping — the lawsuits against AI companies in 2024-2025 — creates a structural opening for user-consented, distributed data collection. If Hub.xyz builds the best structured API layer on top of raw bandwidth contribution, they could become the default interface between AI labs and real-world data. The SwissBorg partnership gives them 1M+ KYC-verified users as distribution. And the DePIN sector has proven the model works — Grass at roughly $1B validates the category. For this to be a great investment, Hub.xyz would need to demonstrate that their data structuring and multimodal processing is meaningfully superior to Grass and Nodepay, that enterprise customers care about the API quality layer more than raw network scale, and that the founding team can execute a pivot from crypto-community growth hacking to enterprise API sales. That's three simultaneous jumps, each unproven.

But here's what I can't get past: Grass has $17.5M in funding and an estimated $1B valuation. Nodepay has $7M and 1.5M active users. Hub.xyz has $1.7M and founders with no demonstrated technical depth in the core problem domain. In a race where the DePIN bandwidth model is fully replicable — and already replicated — your only moat is execution speed or unique technical insight. I see evidence of neither. The "API for real-world training data" positioning is a tagline, not a technical moat. The underlying data is public internet content collected through user bandwidth — it's not proprietary to any single network. This is a commodity business pretending to be a platform play. Innovation without a moat is consumer surplus — all the value will flow to the AI labs buying the data, and eventually to whichever DePIN network achieves the most scale, which is currently not Hub.xyz.

Ten people, no public technical output from founders, a co-founder who left, token-incentivized growth in a crowded DePIN sector, better-funded competitors with head starts, and no evidence of the obsessive founder-problem fusion that produces outlier outcomes. I pass.
