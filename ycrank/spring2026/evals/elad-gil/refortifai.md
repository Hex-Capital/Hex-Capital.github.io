# Refortifai -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Microsoft released the Agent Governance Toolkit as MIT-licensed open source on April 2, 2026 -- covering all ten OWASP agentic AI risks with sub-0.1ms p99 latency across Python, TypeScript, Rust, Go, and .NET, integrating directly with LangChain, CrewAI, and Google ADK. That's the single most important fact in Refortifai's dossier. When the dominant platform player commoditizes the exact layer you're trying to sell, your pricing power evaporates before you've written your first invoice. Refortifai's YC page describes "intercepting tool calls, enforcing governance policies, and blocking attacks in real time" -- which is precisely what Microsoft is giving away for free. I've backed companies into headwinds before, but only when the startup occupied a layer the incumbent structurally couldn't reach. Here, the incumbent built the agent frameworks themselves and released the governance toolkit to commoditize the complement. Refortifai isn't competing with Microsoft's product -- it's competing with Microsoft's strategy.

Refortifai enters a market where the structural shift is real but the timing is fully consensus. The move from LLMs-as-chatbots to LLMs-as-autonomous-agents with tool-use capabilities does create a genuinely new attack surface. I don't dispute that. But when Noma Security has raised $132M with reported 1,300% ARR growth, Capsule Security closed a $7M seed in the same month as Refortifai's YC batch, and PromptArmor (YC W24) already has $3M with Accel and Lightspeed behind it, the window isn't nonobvious -- it's a land rush. My framework asks whether consensus underestimates the opportunity. Here, consensus has already formed and is writing nine-figure checks. Compare this to when I invested in Coinbase in 2012: crypto infrastructure was dismissed by mainstream investors, not pursued by them. Refortifai is entering a market where every major security vendor is already positioning.

Refortifai's website describes something entirely different from its YC page -- "anti-cheat for AI models" with weight obfuscation technology that maintains inference speed, versus runtime agent security through tool-call interception. These are fundamentally different products targeting different buyers with different threat models. At pre-seed, focus is the only thing that matters. A team of three cannot simultaneously build a model IP protection platform and an agent runtime governance system. The weight obfuscation product is actually more technically interesting -- Atman Kar's hardware engineering background at Texas Instruments building automotive RADAR accelerators and his time at Tenstorrent map directly to the low-level runtime instrumentation that weight obfuscation requires. But Refortifai appears to have pivoted toward agent security for YC, which moves them away from their CTO's deepest technical strength and into a domain where none of the three founders has confirmed security expertise.

The bull case deserves serious consideration. Every security category that eventually produces a billion-dollar winner looks crowded in year one -- CrowdStrike launched into a market with dozens of endpoint vendors, and Palo Alto entered an already-established firewall market. Refortifai could argue that Noma's full-lifecycle platform is too broad, Microsoft's open-source toolkit requires self-hosting expertise enterprises lack, and there's room for a focused managed service at the tool-call interception layer. The agentic AI market is projected to grow from $7.29B in 2025 to $139.19B by 2034 at 40.5% CAGR. If Refortifai captures even a thin slice of that security spend, the TAM supports venture returns. I've seen this pattern before where the crowd is right about the market but wrong about which company wins. But the CrowdStrike analogy breaks on founder-market fit: George Kurtz was McAfee's CTO with decades of security operations experience. Sayan Mitra's background is unconfirmed beyond the YC listing, Rithik Jain transitioned from economics to CS at UW-Madison, and Atman Kar is a hardware engineer. Refortifai is a security company without visible security domain expertise, entering a market where the dominant platform vendor is giving away the core product. None of my Known Exceptions apply -- Anduril's override required a generational structural opening with no incumbent response, and here the incumbent (Microsoft) responded in the same month.

Refortifai's single-miracle test reveals compounding challenges. The technology miracle is happening exogenously -- agents with tool-use are proliferating -- but the tool-call interception pattern is, as the dossier notes, "architecturally straightforward." That means Refortifai's technology isn't the miracle; their miracle is distribution and differentiation against free open-source alternatives and $132M-funded competitors simultaneously. Add the product direction ambiguity as a strategic clarity miracle, and you have at least two independent breakthroughs required. On infrastructure positioning, Refortifai describes itself as "sitting on top of existing agent setups" -- a monitoring overlay, not a dependency layer. Stripe became indispensable because ripping out payment infrastructure breaks your revenue stream. Ripping out an agent security monitor and replacing it with Microsoft's free toolkit breaks nothing. The switching costs are minimal by design ("minimal integration"), which is a feature for adoption but a liability for retention.

**Pass.** Refortifai sits at the intersection of three patterns I avoid: an obvious and crowded market where consensus has already formed and is writing large checks (Noma's $132M, Capsule's $7M same-month seed), a startup building on a contested infrastructure layer where the platform owner is actively commoditizing the complement (Microsoft's open-source toolkit), and a team without confirmed domain expertise in their target category. The decisive dimensions are Nonobvious Market Timing -- where every data point confirms this is consensus, not contrarian -- and Single-Miracle Viability, where Refortifai needs both distribution differentiation and strategic focus simultaneously. The one condition that would flip me to Invest: evidence that Refortifai's weight obfuscation technology delivers measurably superior model IP protection that enterprises will pay for at scale -- that's a more defensible, less crowded wedge where Atman Kar's hardware background creates genuine technical moat, and if they showed $50K+ in LOIs from AI model distributors specifically for weight protection, I'd revisit the entire evaluation.
