# HyperProbe -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

HyperProbe is a domain-competent dev-tools operator running the exact 2026 consensus AI-agent play into a category where Lightrun just took $70M in April 2025 and Dynatrace already owns the Rookout runtime-debugging IP from July 2023 — the founder is the strongest thing on the page, and the category sleeve is the weakest, which is a signature I have priced out of my portfolio at seed. Shailendra Singh spent a decade at HyperTest ($2.13M raised, Better Capital-led seed December 2022) shipping API-testing to Urbancompany, Nykaa, Groww — that's real, and it's still not enough to buy exposure to a YC S26 tagline that reads "Your coding agent writes code. Now let it fix prod too."

On the price-and-consensus axis, HyperProbe is the Pass Example in my own persona file made flesh — a canonical 2026 YC seed for an AI agent on top of a crowded category, where the "probe-from-agent" primitive is replicable by any of the four named competitors in the dossier. Sublime Security is the anchor I want to compare HyperProbe against because Sublime was an enterprise security wedge where tier-1 security funds were still parked in late-stage SOC SaaS — the seed was legitimately un-crowded. HyperProbe fails the same test in the opposite direction: Lightrun raised its Series B thirteen months before this pitch, Dynatrace-Rookout has been shipping since 2023, and Sentry Seer plus Datadog Bits AI SRE cover the AI-triage adjacency — this is a "hot YC deck" priced into a category two tier-1 vendors already occupy, which is the exact deal I under-write against by policy.

The canonical 2026 YC AI-agent seed fails on category sleeve because "AI is just more internet" and the whole premise assumes teams keep adopting Cursor, Claude Code and Codex fast enough that HyperProbe's wedge doesn't get eaten by those same platforms shipping native runtime probes — that failure mode is directly present in HyperProbe's dossier under "Distribution dependency on coding-agent ecosystems" and it is not mitigated. HyperProbe is not pre-consensus crypto, not DTC-with-margin, not a creator-holdco, not employee-equity — it is undifferentiated SaaS-on-top-of-LLM in the exact sleeve I've publicly re-weighted away from since the Lonsdale podcast, and the dossier gives me no reason to make it an exception.

Founder conviction is where HyperProbe actually earns points, and the mechanism analysis matters: Shailendra Singh has spent from 2015 to 2026 inside developer-testing tooling — HyperTest paying B2B logos, the pivot into HyperProbe on YC admission — which is the "3+ years working on the problem before the round" filter, extended to eleven. What compounds over 18 months from that base is domain speed of iteration on runtime probes across Node/TS/Java/Scala/Kotlin — what does not compound is missionary temperament visible to me pre-pitch, because I cannot find a single essay, thread, or opinionated take from Shailendra on why agent-driven debugging is non-obvious. Domain persistence without public conviction reads to me as "responding to opportunity" — HyperTest rebranded into HyperProbe on YC admission, which is the tell.

Counterfactually, HyperProbe's capital-structure score flips a tier only if the pitch is a holdco wrapper around HyperTest's existing API-testing revenue plus the new probe product — a two-product cash-flow entity where the API-testing book underwrites the probe R&D and neither line individually needs venture capital. That's the Teamshares-shaped instrument I would actually pay for at this stage. What HyperProbe is instead presenting, per the dossier, is a standard usage/seat-based SaaS with a demo-gated Calendly GTM funneled through calendly.com/shailendra-hyperprobe/30min — a generic cap table that will need five more rounds to reach default-alive against Lightrun's $110M war chest, which is the failure mode the Slow letter's capital-efficiency language directly inverts.

HyperProbe's founder-as-brand score has a single-anchor parallel worth naming: John Fish / Bookshelved (January 2024) was underwritable because Fish's owned distribution existed 2+ years before the check and was measurable in subscribers. HyperProbe has a LinkedIn showcase page with unretrievable follower count, an @hyperprobe_eu Twitter handle that belongs to an unrelated EU medical device project, no GitHub presence, and two customer testimonials from CheQ Digital and Housing.com quoted on the marketing site — that is the absence of the very asset the Slow Creator Fund was built to price. HyperProbe is a founder-led enterprise sale, not a founder-as-distribution asset, and I score it accordingly rather than pretend two testimonials substitute for owned reach.

My 36-month forecast on HyperProbe: acqui-hired by Datadog or Dynatrace for under $25M, or dead, with ARR stalling under $2M as Lightrun ships an IDE-native agent workflow and Dynatrace bolts probe-from-agent onto its post-Rookout stack — Rookout itself is the comp, $33.9M after seven years and roughly $28M raised, which is a losing outcome for venture math and the ceiling I see for HyperProbe absent a category unlock the dossier does not disclose. The mechanism is straightforward: HyperProbe's wedge is speed-to-agent-native, not a durable primitive, and both incumbents have distribution HyperProbe cannot outrun on $500K of seed capital.

The strongest opposing case: Shailendra is a founder-market-fit machine — IIT Bombay 2009, VP Products at OYO Rooms unicorn, HyperTest paying customers at Urbancompany and Nykaa — and he is running the Wizard-of-Oz play in a wide-open moment where AI-authored backend code has genuinely outpaced AI-authored debugging, so speed and taste beat capitalization; the self-reported MTTR from 3-4 hours to nine minutes with sub-1% CPU overhead is a real product claim if it survives third-party benchmarking, and Rookout's $33.9M outcome proves the acquirer set exists. That bull case stings — but it collapses on the same fact that anchors the bear case: Lightrun already raised $70M in April 2025 for "AI-SRE dynamic instrumentation," which is HyperProbe's product one linguistic layer up, and Dynatrace has been integrating Rookout for three years. HyperProbe is bringing a seed check to a Series-B knife fight in a sleeve I don't buy. My verdict flips if HyperProbe closes a named Fortune-500 enterprise LOI at ≥$200K ACV within six months at a post below $12M — evidence the wedge is real and the entry price is not consensus. Absent that, this is a pass, and I would rather see Shailendra structure the HyperTest+HyperProbe book as a two-product holdco and come back with an instrument I can actually underwrite.
