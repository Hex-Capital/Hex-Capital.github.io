# Savant -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Savant is a 2-person team building an enterprise memory layer that integrates with Google Workspace, Office 365, Salesforce, Slack, Zendesk, and Intercom — and the moment I read that integration list I have already scored it. Anthony Kim (Yale EECS) and Kevin Yu (Cornell CS) are pitching a knowledge-layer for AI-native enterprises against Glean's $300M ARR and Dust's $60M war chest, and the distribution architecture this requires is enterprise sales — labor and capital leverage that need permission from procurement, from IT, from every CISO who has to greenlight a new connector into Salesforce. That is the structural opposite of the Twitter/Stack Overflow/Notion bet I write.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung on leverage architecture — anyone can post, the platform charges nothing, marginal cost of replication is zero — and Savant sits two rungs below it. The shared mechanism I look for is whether a single user, with zero gatekeeper permission, can pull the product into existence on their own desk. Savant cannot be pulled in by an individual; the dossier names Salesforce, Office 365, and Zendesk as integration surfaces, which means the buyer is a VP and the sale is gated by SSO provisioning, security review, and seat counts. There is no open-source repo named in the founder section, no GitHub presence for either Anthony Kim or Kevin Yu, no Product Hunt listing, no Twitter following — none of the permissionless-distribution artifacts that flagged Replit or Stack Overflow before the priced round arrived.

Perplexity (~2022) is the relevant low-rung reference on networks — I wrote that check despite single-sided architecture because Aravind Srinivas brought a Berkeley retrieval PhD and the AI-cycle timing was pre-consensus. Savant is single-sided in the same shape — each customer's corpus does not compound the next customer's value — but the override conditions that saved Perplexity are missing. The dossier surfaces no specific-knowledge gate on Anthony Kim or Kevin Yu, no prior enterprise-memory shipped artifact, no retrieval research, and the "AI-cycle timing" argument is fatally weakened by Glean's ARR tripling from $100M to $300M in 15 months and Dust deploying 300K+ agents — this is not pre-consensus, this is mid-narrative, and the price of admission has already moved.

Specific knowledge is where Savant fails my filter most loudly. Yale EECS and Cornell CS are credentials, not evidence — "Specific knowledge is knowledge you cannot be trained for. If society can train you, it can train someone else and replace you" (Almanack, 2020). The dossier explicitly notes no prior companies, no exits, no domain employers, no GitHub repos, no Twitter accounts for either Anthony Kim or Kevin Yu, and no public writing on enterprise knowledge management. Notion's Ivan Zhao had been rebuilding personal productivity tools since adolescence; Stack Overflow's Atwood and Spolsky were practicing developers writing for developers — the Savant founders' relationship to undocumented operational procedure capture is undisclosed and undemonstrated.

The market itself has genuine pivot room — $5B AI-in-KM TAM with double-digit CAGR per market.us, and adjacent vectors into agent-memory infrastructure and developer SDK layers exist. Over 18 months the dimension that decides Savant's fate is not market size but whether the founders can compound a specific-knowledge edge faster than Microsoft 365 Copilot Agents, Atlassian Rovo, and Notion Custom Agents bundle the same functionality into the suites Savant integrates with. The mechanism is unforgiving: every quarter Glean and Dust ship, Savant's differentiation on "undocumented procedure capture" gets quietly absorbed by an incumbent shipping the same feature at zero marginal CAC because their distribution is already paid for.

On the founder trio, the dossier gives me nothing to fail and nothing to underwrite — no contested prior exits, no partner litigation, no self-promotion about ethics, and equally no multi-year public writing or shipped artifacts that would let me run the 10-year-partner test. I apply the absent-evidence default at neutral and move on. The counterfactual that would flip this dimension is a documented prior operator role at a company like Glean, Notion, or Anthropic where Anthony Kim or Kevin Yu owned the memory-layer surface — that single fact would reset the specific-knowledge score and pull this whole evaluation up a tier.

My 36-month forecast for Savant: acqui-hired by a mid-tier enterprise SaaS vendor (Atlassian, ServiceNow, or Zendesk itself) for under $30M between 2027 and 2029, or quietly wound down after failing to reach $2M ARR against Glean's now-$600M+ ARR commercial machine. The producing mechanism is straightforward — a 2-person team running an enterprise sales motion against incumbents with three orders of magnitude more revenue, distribution, and integration surface cannot win the connector arms race, and the "capture undocumented procedures" wedge is a feature, not a category.

The strongest bull case against my forecast invokes the Perplexity exception — agent-memory is a category in formation, Savant could be the Pinecone of operational context, and pre-consensus AI-infrastructure bets are exactly where my ledger overperforms. The exception requires three gates to clear emphatically: permissionless API distribution, deep specific knowledge, and pre-consensus timing. Savant clears none — the integration surface is procurement-gated not API-gated, Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu have no surfaced retrieval or memory-systems track record, and Glean's $7.2B valuation and Dust's Sequoia-led Series B mean the consensus already arrived. The condition that flips my verdict: Savant ships an open-source agent-memory SDK with organic GitHub adoption (>1K stars, unpaid contributors) before the next priced round — that single artifact would re-architect this from a permissioned enterprise sale into a permissionless developer-tool bet, and I would revisit.
