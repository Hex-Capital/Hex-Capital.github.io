# Ekpa -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Ekpa is two MIT students -- Yuga Patel and Anant Asthana -- building a read-only portfolio dashboard that layers LLM-generated per-holding "share count recommendations" on top of brokerage-aggregation APIs, and the dimensional signature that stops me is this: the least-weak axis is a thematic founder-market interest (Asthana's own blog post on autonomous trading agents), and the most-weak axis is Ekpa's structural distribution position -- read-only aggregation on top of Plaid/SnapTrade, sold to retail account holders whose accounts already live inside Robinhood, Public, Fidelity, and Schwab. That is a feature waiting to be shipped natively, not a company.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for market re-opening because Ukraine gave defense software a specific 24-month catalyst peers were ignoring; Ekpa's "why now" is that "LLM cost/quality thresholds crossed in 2024-2025" -- which is not a catalyst, it is the ambient weather every YC S26 fintech company is invoking. The Ekpa dossier does not name a regulatory shift, an incumbent failure mode, or a distributional rupture in retail brokerage inside the 24-month window. Robinhood and Public shipped in-app AI research in 2024-2026 without waiting for anyone. If the catalyst is "LLMs got cheaper," the incumbents got the same memo, and Ekpa's window is their roadmap.

**Lyft** Series C is the pattern I keep coming back to on Ekpa's index position -- I passed because #2 in a network-effect market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and the dossier didn't show a share-inversion path (20VC). Ekpa's version of that failure mode is subtler: Ekpa is not #2, it is a thin research overlay sitting on brokerage rails owned by Fidelity/Schwab/Robinhood, and read-only aggregation itself is a commodity API (Plaid Investments, SnapTrade). **Stripe** (2010) was an index on e-commerce because every merchant had to touch the rails; Ekpa touches nothing every retail investor must touch -- retail investors touch their broker, and their broker will ship a per-position AI summary before Ekpa has a pricing page.

Ekpa's revenue velocity dimension is the cleanest cut in the dossier: pricing is "forthcoming," product is in pre-launch read-only mode, no waitlist size disclosed, no Product Hunt, no app store, no Discord, no press. Mechanism analysis on 18 months: in the AI cycle, silence on growth compounds into founder-narrative dependency because real growth is easy to disclose -- I said this on Tim Ferriss #863 and I mean it literally. **Harvey** (2023) at $3B ? $5B ? $8B in months is the shape of "just working"; Ekpa at a pre-launch waitlist is the shape of "we'll find out." The absent-evidence default applies and I am not going to smuggle in optimism the dossier does not earn.

Ekpa's founder-shipping dimension fails counterfactually: what would have to be true to move Ekpa a tier here is a named prior product Patel or Asthana shipped independently with a user-feedback loop -- **Perplexity** (2022) came in because Aravind pinged me and showed up a week later with the thing built. Ekpa's dossier names Asthana's blog post on autonomous AI trading agents and Patel's CS/AI + Econ coursework at MIT. No GitHub, no shipped prior product, no Twitter presence to observe the shipping rhythm on. This is a pedigree dossier in a stage where pedigree is the weakest legible signal.

Ekpa's single-miracle test is where I stop equivocating -- a single-anchor parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015) makes this clean. Color was structured as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing so the miracle isolated to workflow, not chemistry. Ekpa is structured to require at least three independent miracles: (1) LLM accuracy on personalized share-count recommendations sufficient for retail trust, where hallucination is a documented open problem; (2) a regulatory posture where "share count recommendations" tied to specific holdings do not trigger investment-adviser registration despite the disclaimer on goekpa.com; (3) consumer CAC economics that beat Robinhood's zero-cost in-app research. Compounding those three probabilities is the exact NFX failure math I use to pass on science-adjacent seeds -- except here the miracles are LLM+regulatory+consumer-distribution rather than physics.

Ekpa 36 months out: dead or pivoted into a B2B filing-summarization API sold to a mid-tier RIA platform or a second-tier broker (something in the M1/SoFi weight class), because the consumer wedge cannot survive incumbent native features and the "share count recommendation" framing invites SEC exposure that a two-person team cannot fund through. I do not see the acquirer, because the acquirer wants distribution or data, and Ekpa builds neither at this scope.

The strongest bull case is that Ekpa is early **Betterment** -- retail investors genuinely want personalized guidance, brokerages are conflicted from offering fiduciary-flavored recommendations, and there is real wedge here for an outsider who is willing to register. That case stings for a second, then breaks: Betterment raised nine figures specifically to fund the RIA/compliance moat and treated registration as the product; Ekpa's dossier explicitly disclaims broker-dealer and adviser status *while offering share-count recommendations tied to holdings*, which is a regulatory contradiction rather than a moat. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is Ekpa disclosing a signed distribution agreement with a named brokerage or RIA platform, with pilot data showing >20% MoM engagement growth on the LLM recommendation surface -- absent that, this is a Robinhood feature request, not a check I write from my own money.
