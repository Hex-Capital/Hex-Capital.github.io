# Korso -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Epicor launched Prism in 2026 — an AI agent for RFQ workflows built inside their own ERP. That's the structural tell. Epicor's revenue depends on seat-based licensing for human operators performing manual workflows. Every automated workflow their AI handles is a seat they can no longer justify charging for. Korso is positioned on the correct side of this principal-agent trap: an ERP-agnostic agent layer that eliminates manual work rather than upselling it. The structural parallel to Perplexity facing off against Google's ad-revenue-encumbered search is real — incumbents become agents of their own P&L, structurally unable to build the thing that replaces their core business. But Perplexity had Aravind Srinivas with years at Google Brain and OpenAI, specific knowledge so deep a competitor would need half a decade to replicate it. Korso has Martin Pan's time at General Motors.

Martin Pan's GM experience gives Korso direct exposure to how automotive manufacturing operations actually break — where the email-and-spreadsheet bottlenecks live, why institutional knowledge vanishes when employees leave. Daichi Hiraoka's electrical engineering work at Penn Electric Racing and ML research that reduced radiotherapy dose prediction discrepancies by 97% adds genuine technical execution capability. Alex Liu contributes research depth from Notre Dame and systems experience from Gatekeeper Systems. But the specific knowledge test asks something sharper: is the founder's non-trainable expertise the company's core engine, or merely adjacent to it? Korso's founders identified their thesis — "the great inefficiency of modern manufacturing is not on the shop floor, it's in the office" — through traveling across Asia talking to clients. That's diligence, not obsession. If Korso's three founders vanished, any competent AI team with manufacturing domain consultants could reconstruct the identical thesis from first principles. The knowledge here is relevant but replicable.

Korso's leverage architecture is where the evaluation gets interesting and where it also collapses. AI agents autonomously triaging RFQs, generating quotes, and chasing delayed orders across multi-week manufacturing cycles with checkpointing — that's architecturally harder than what Turian (17 employees, cross-industry procurement) or Leverage AI ($14.9M raised, narrower PO scope) are building. Long-running stateful workflows with audit trails require purpose-built infrastructure, not an API wrapper. In principle, this is pure code leverage — zero marginal cost per workflow executed. In practice, Korso itself acknowledges the constraint: "Most AI automation breaks down at the ERP integration layer." SAP, Epicor, Oracle, JobBOSS — heterogeneous APIs, bespoke customizations, decades of accumulated configuration debt. Each manufacturer deployment risks becoming a custom integration project requiring weeks of engineering effort. Custom integration is labor leverage wearing software's clothing. The question that determines whether Korso is a platform or a services business — whether they can abstract ERP integration into a generalizable layer — has no answer in the dossier.

The advocate's case for Korso is genuine. Three founders with pre-existing relationships — Hiraoka and Pan through Penn Electric Racing, Liu and Pan through UC Irvine — not a co-founder matching event. Manufacturing AI is not a status play; recent graduates choosing this over consumer AI or fintech signals attraction to the problem rather than the funding environment. SOC 2 Type 1 and ISO 27001 certifications in progress at the pre-seed stage suggest enterprise seriousness beyond their cohort. Per-account data accumulation — pricing history, supplier patterns, customer preferences — creates switching costs that compound, converting tribal knowledge into a proprietary data layer. The $34B market growing at 35% CAGR provides runway. And the principal-agent dynamic with ERP vendors is structural, not cyclical — it persists as long as incumbents depend on seat revenue that automation eliminates.

Korso's core capability, though, runs on generally available infrastructure — LLM document parsing, email triage, API integration. Paperless Parts has $45-51M in funding and has already shipped AI-supported quoting. Leverage AI has $14.9M targeting the same manufacturer buyer. Tacton has built CPQ since 1998 and recently took strategic PE capital from Rubicon Technology Partners. Korso claims differentiation through autonomous agents across the full quote-to-order cycle versus single-step tools, but that claim sits on zero public validation: no named customers, no revenue signals, no LOIs, no user metrics, no press. At pre-seed I don't penalize absent traction as a stage default. But Korso's founders claim to have spent time traveling across Asia talking directly to clients — and the dossier surfaces not a single named pilot or signed letter of intent from those conversations. The compound interest test requires something to compound from. The data flywheel needs its first revolution.

One verifiable condition would flip me: evidence that Korso has deployed agents against two or more distinct ERP systems and completed a full autonomous quote-to-order cycle for a paying manufacturer. That would prove the ERP abstraction layer converts this from custom integration (labor) to platform deployment (code leverage). Until then, Korso occupies a structurally attractive position — principals versus incumbent agents, AI leverage versus manual workflows — without the founder-specific-knowledge density required to exploit it. Structural positioning without non-replicable expertise is a bet on execution speed in a competitive market. Execution speed is labor leverage. The two dimensions that lock Korso into Neutral territory: specific knowledge that is relevant but replaceable (Pan's GM stint does not create the years-deep, non-trainable expertise my highest-conviction bets require), and a leverage architecture whose permissionless scaling depends on solving ERP integration abstraction — a problem the dossier acknowledges but does not demonstrate solving.
