# WithAI -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Ian McInnis ran an equity book at Bridgewater and led the "how can we use LLMs to invest?" initiative there before starting WithAI, and that biographical fact is the most load-bearing signal in the entire dossier — more than the >$100K ARR, more than the 60+ integrations, more than the Mercator testimonial. WithAI is a definite-architectural-commitment bet (client-cloud single-tenant, fund-specific ontology) wrapped around a thin contrarian secret, sold into a sector where Hebbia at $700M and Rogo at $750M post-money already have the consensus-shaped product. The signature is highest on roadmap definiteness and lowest on atoms-heavy structural moat — that pairing tells you what kind of company WithAI is: a credentialed-insider wedge into a crowded category, betting the architectural posture (deploy inside the client's cloud) is the durable thing.

**Stripe** (2010) is the high rung on the contrarian-secret ladder because Patrick Collison named a specific incumbent failure mode — PayPal's seven-click checkout and the merchant-of-record bureaucracy — that the consensus said was solved. WithAI's stated secret is structurally similar in shape: Hebbia's multi-tenant architecture cannot ingest a fundamental hedge fund's proprietary positions and research without violating the fund's competitive and regulatory posture, so the "winner" of enterprise-search-for-finance has a load-bearing architectural blind spot. Where WithAI diverges from Stripe is that the blind spot is a deployment-mode configuration, not a re-architected primitive — Hebbia can ship a single-tenant VPC variant in two quarters if enough Mercator-class buyers demand it, and Rogo's Sequoia-funded sales team will price-cut to defend the hedge-fund expansion the OpenAI collaboration signals is already underway. The secret is real but shallow; it terminates at a deployment SKU, not at an institutional impossibility the way Lockheed's cost-plus structure does for Anduril.

The canonical pass pattern from my own pass example — "AI-powered platform for [vertical X]" with no atoms, no regulatory anchor, no hardware capex — is the low rung on the structural-moat ladder, and WithAI's dossier sits closer to that rung than to anywhere I want to be. "Custom command centers for hedge funds" is the 2026 reformulation of the 2024-26 YC archetype; the client-cloud deployment is real product work, but it is not the regulated-certification depth of Palantir's IL5/IL6 accreditation or the capex barrier of SpaceX's reusable first stage. Fundamental equity hedge funds are demanding customers, not regulated ones in the FedRAMP sense — the switching costs after integration with proprietary firm data are real but rebuildable, and the Bridgewater co-CIO angels are a sales channel rather than a moat. WithAI has no atoms.

The definite-plan dimension is where WithAI quietly compounds beyond a typical YC mean: 60+ named integrations (Bloomberg, Refinitiv, FactSet, Tegus, AlphaSense, MSCI, Snowflake, Databricks, JP Morgan, Goldman) is a multi-quarter architectural commitment, not iteration-mode optionality, and Ian's YC-page thesis that agents are "powerful but insufficient" for non-verifiable investing work is the kind of named technical commitment that compounds rather than pivots. Over 18 months this either becomes a defensible workflow-ontology asset that Hebbia would have to rebuild from scratch, or it becomes a configuration list that any competitor matches with engineering quarters and a partnerships hire. The mechanism only works in WithAI's favor if the fund-specific ontology proves stickier than per-customer prompt engineering, which is unproven and which the dossier does not yet evidence beyond one Mercator testimonial.

For the monopoly-trajectory score to flip from "no" to "yes," WithAI would have to demonstrate within 12 months that integration depth at a single hedge fund produces measurable second-derivative switching costs — that Mercator's "80% gathering to 20% acting" inversion requires WithAI specifically, not just a comparable client-cloud agent platform. The dossier does not show that yet; one named client and ">$100K ARR with multiple clients" three months in is consistent with both the early-Palantir trajectory and the early-Hebbia-competitor trajectory, and the priors do not separate. Pricing-power five years out runs into Rogo's Sequoia capital and OpenAI partnership, which is the kind of well-capitalized competitive set the Founders Fund manifesto would have called the consensus indefinite-optimism wave of 2026.

**Affirm** (2014, Levchin) is the relevant single-anchor parallel on founder lineage, and the comparison cuts both ways for WithAI. Ian McInnis is not Max Levchin — he is a Princeton '23 with two years at Bridgewater on the equity book and an AI-for-investing mandate, which is the buyer-side analog of operator pedigree but not the shipped-to-scale-at-PayPal kind. Benjamin Finch's Sentient Labs founding-researcher role and co-authorship on "OML: Open, Monetizable, and Loyal AI" is real technical signal at the unusual-intellectual-identity bar I weight. Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour writing angel checks is revealed-preference signal from the apex buyer in the category — the Bridgewater co-CIOs do not write angel checks into AI-for-hedge-funds reflexively, and that single data point is the strongest thing in the dossier after Ian's biography.

My 36-month forecast: WithAI lands between $3M and $12M ARR by mid-2029, gets acquired by Bloomberg, FactSet, or S&P for between $40M and $180M as a fund-workflow team-and-tech tuck-in, with the Bridgewater angels brokering the introduction. The mechanism is the lowest-dimension pulling the highest: the definite client-cloud architecture is real engineering and the Bridgewater channel produces 5-15 named hedge-fund logos, but the absence of an atoms-heavy or regulated-certification moat means Hebbia and Rogo will compress pricing as soon as the category proves out, and a strategic data vendor will buy the customer relationships before the squeeze finishes. This is not a fund-returner; it is a respectable outcome in a category I am structurally underweight.

The strongest bull case against the acqui-hire forecast is that fund-specific ontology is not a configuration list — it is a network effect inside each client, where every analyst interaction trains a per-fund knowledge graph that no horizontal competitor can replicate without rebuilding the relationship from zero, and the Bridgewater co-CIO angels are the leading indicator that Bridgewater itself becomes a customer, which would make WithAI the **Palantir** of fundamental equity in the way Palantir became the Palantir of intelligence after the first three-letter-agency contract. The case stings because the analog is real: Palantir's moat was integration depth into customer workflow, not algorithm superiority. The dossier evidence that wins against the bull case is the competitive set's velocity — Rogo's $75M Series C in January 2026 from Sequoia, explicitly to expand into hedge funds via OpenAI collaboration, is a 24-month parallel buildout against a 3-person team with zero open job postings on the YC page. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict to invest: Bridgewater itself becomes a paying production customer of WithAI within 12 months, not just a source of angel capital — that single data point would re-rate the monopoly trajectory from "crowded" to "Palantir-shaped" and override the missing atoms-heavy criterion the way Moskovitz's Facebook pedigree overrode it for Asana.
