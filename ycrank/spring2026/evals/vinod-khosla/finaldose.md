# FinalDose -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

FinalDose is the **Impossible Foods** (2011) shape with the **Physical Intelligence** (2024) gap -- a maximally consequential success-state ("destroying all cancers, unlocking 80% of targets" including MYC, TP53, APC per the YC Launch page) carried by three Oxford PhDs who are extending their CRISPR-screening careers rather than defying their field. The dimensional signature is high-consequence, orthodox-founder -- which is exactly the profile I underwrote at **QuantumScape** before the 2020 IPO and exactly the profile I lost on at **KiOR** (~2008). I do not pass on this signature; I price it.

On consequence magnitude, FinalDose sits near the **OpenAI** (2019) rung, not the **DoorDash** (2013) rung. The success-state names the incumbent regime it collapses: ~85% of disease drivers that lack a druggable protein, and the specific "undruggable" oncology targets -- MYC, TP53, APC -- that small molecules have failed at for forty years. That is a civilizational variable, not a workflow improvement. The McKinsey 1986 forecast of <1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 (actual: 109M) is the right reference point for "undruggable" -- the word means "we have not yet found the modality," not "thermodynamically impossible." FinalDose diverges from the **OpenAI** rung only in that the consequence is bounded to oncology rather than to cognition itself; that is a 0.5x adjustment, not a tier drop.

The **KiOR** (~2008) failure mechanism was not the catalyst miracle -- that one tracked. It was the *compounding* of three miracles where the refinery-integration leg broke. FinalDose has the identical topology: miracle one is the DNA-sensing protein's structural-change-as-kill-switch (publishable, demonstrable in a dish); miracle two is in vivo specificity at single-base resolution against ~3 billion bases without triggering apoptosis in healthy cells; miracle three is delivery -- the dossier explicitly states "the company has not publicly disclosed a delivery modality (LNP, AAV, etc.)." That is not a minor disclosure gap. That is the leg that has killed every programmable-oncology platform from antisense oligos through RNA-targeted therapeutics. The pattern shows up cleanly in the dossier; I am pricing it, not pretending it is absent.

On technical and capital risk that repels consensus VCs, FinalDose is squarely in **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) territory by structure -- oncology platforms require $100M+ to clinic, IND timelines of 3-5 years that exceed standard fund clocks, and a regulatory pathway that no generalist Tier-1 will underwrite at pre-seed. The Crunchbase search returning no Series A is the *evidence* of capital-hostility, not an absence of evidence. Compounding mechanism over 18 months: every preclinical data point either tightens the specificity miracle (raises the company's IND probability) or widens off-target activation (collapses the platform claim). There is no middle path where FinalDose grinds out incremental ARR -- that is the property I want.

Founder-defies-expert-consensus is where FinalDose scores lowest, and I have to be honest about it. Jeff Liu, Li-Yao Huang, and Steven Lin are three Oxford PhDs whose CV is *continuity* with the CRISPR-screening establishment -- Liu's 5-year postdoc at MRC Weatherall, Huang's Cas9-based target-validation framework, Lin's Wellcome-funded computational biology. The UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study I cite in my TED Talk is about exactly this profile: domain experts extrapolating their field's trajectory. The counterfactual that would flip this dimension a tier: a publicly defended position by Liu or Huang that the gene-editing consensus (Beam, Intellia, CRISPR Therapeutics) is solving the wrong problem -- "edit, do not kill" versus "kill, do not edit." That position is implicit in FinalDose's mechanism but not articulated in the dossier. The **Glean** exception applies in spirit -- Arvind Jain was ex-Google search, paradigmatic domain-expert, and I overrode on consequence magnitude. Same override logic here.

Founder learning rate sits at the **Slash** (2024) mid-rung by one specific datum: Jeff Liu previously co-founded Vivid Dx, a sepsis diagnostics company that raised $15M, and is now pivoting domain from sepsis diagnostics into oncology therapeutics. That is a real pivot -- not a Kanye-pivot in narrative drama, but a documented modality change with prior venture-grade execution. The learning-rate evidence is one founder deep rather than three founders deep, which is why this is mid-rung not high-rung.

Required-capital profile is a clean match. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) needed $115M to first commercial milestone; FinalDose will need a comparable order of magnitude to IND plus first-in-human, and the dossier's silence on Series A is the tell that consensus biotech firms have not yet led. That is precisely the asymmetry I exploit -- write the conviction check early, syndicate the capital intensity later.

**Forecast (36 months):** FinalDose is alive but pre-clinical, having raised a $40-80M Series A led by a biotech specialist (ARCH, RA Capital, or Flagship) on the back of mouse efficacy data in one MYC-driven tumor model, with no IND filing yet and the delivery-modality miracle still unsolved -- the standard platform-biotech holding pattern that takes the **QuantumScape** decade-to-validation shape, not the **OpenAI** two-year takeoff. The signature drives the forecast: high-consequence orthodox-founder biology platforms do not die in 36 months and they do not break out in 36 months; they accumulate preclinical signal and capital.

**Strongest opposing case (bear):** the specificity problem is not a miracle that "might" fail -- it is the same wall that killed every previous programmable cell-killing modality, and the dossier's admission of "no specificity or off-target benchmarks are public" combined with "delivery modality undisclosed" means FinalDose is selling two unfunded miracles under the language of one platform. Beam and Intellia have spent ~$1B+ combined and still publish off-target rates that would be unacceptable in a kill-switch context, because *editing* tolerates some off-target activity while *killing* does not. That objection stings. My dismantling: it is exactly the **KiOR** logic in reverse -- the consequence of curing the 80% of undruggable oncology targets justifies a 70-80% failure prior, and that is the framework, not its indictment. The single verifiable condition that would flip me to pass: published in vivo data showing >100x activation specificity in tumor versus healthy tissue at clinically relevant doses. Absent that data point in 18 months, I do not write the follow-on.
