# Alloovium -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Alloovium is a workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-a-hard-thing bet — Zander Schweitzer grew up on Port of Brisbane sites and Cielo Nicolosi is running the citation-grounded retrieval layer over Procore, Aconex, SharePoint, and Bluebeam — but Alloovium has no disclosed ARR, only a founder-stated $1M target for year-end 2026, which means the structural shape I like is unaccompanied by the "just working" curve I require. That pairing — clean single-miracle architecture, silent revenue — is the analytical signature. It's the *Color Genomics* (2015) template without the *Harvey* (2023) velocity.

Anduril (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on catalyst — Ukraine revealed Pentagon software inadequacy inside a 24-month window, and Trae Stephens shipped into the opening. Alloovium's dossier does name a comparable technical step-function: citation-grounded retrieval crossing the audit-defensibility threshold in 2024–2025, which Trunk Tools' Series B thesis cites in the same language. But construction admin was never a "dead market" the way defense was pre-Anduril or payments were pre-**Stripe** (2010) — McKinsey has been writing about the 15–20% overrun problem for a decade, and the catalyst here is a *tooling* catalyst rather than a market re-opening. Alloovium is riding an enablement wave, not a re-priced category, and that distinction is why this dimension pins closer to a mid-rung Brex-style entry than to my Anduril template.

The failure mode on revenue velocity — "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" — is the exact silence I look for in every AI dossier, and Alloovium is silent. The $400M in "active construction projects deployed" is a project-footprint number, not a revenue number, and I've seen enough construction pilots to know footprint-language usually paper over unpaid or heavily-discounted deployments. Alloovium discloses no ARR, no MoM growth, no per-customer expansion, no signed enterprise logos in the last 90 days — the founder-stated $1M ARR / 75-partner goal for EOY 2026 is a *target*, and targets don't get the *Harvey* $3B → $5B → $8B treatment. Real growth is easy to disclose; the absence is itself the signal.

On founders, Alloovium runs the mechanism I care about — domain-authenticity compounding into workflow instincts over 18 months. Schweitzer's Brady Marine & Civil tenure and Brisbane construction upbringing is the real founder-market fit, and Nicolosi's coding-since-8 / claimed-exit-at-15 profile is the kind of thing I'd want to verify in a founder meeting but is directionally the Perplexity-style shipping-obsessed archetype. Alloovium's dossier names no GitHub, no independent shipped artifacts, no side projects — which is the piece missing from making this a **Perplexity** (2022) cold-inbound pattern rather than a résumé.

On distribution, the counterfactual for a tier flip is whether Alloovium becomes the citation layer *every* construction firm's document stack touches — the way **Stripe** (2010) sits in every e-commerce checkout. Alloovium reads across SharePoint, Procore, Aconex, Outlook, Teams, Google Drive, and Bluebeam, which is genuinely the workflow-path position I look for. But Trunk Tools has raised $70M against the identical position with a US GC head start, and Procore shipping native Copilot substitutes the "Ask" workflow outright. For distribution to flip from mid-rung to Stripe-adjacent, Alloovium would need either a Procore/Aconex integration partnership Trunk Tools cannot get, or an ANZ-regulatory moat (IMS/ITP clause-level compliance) that doesn't port to AIA contracts in the US.

Compared to **Color Genomics** (2015) on single-miracle architecture, Alloovium is unusually clean. The isolated miracle is "LLM citation-grounded retrieval, faithful to source sentence/page/revision, on construction documents." Everything else — SharePoint integration, phone-call voice capture, Routines, human sign-off — is GTM and product execution, not new science. Alloovium doesn't require new physics, new regulatory approval, or new consumer behavior. This is the dimension where Alloovium scores well and it's why I don't dismiss it outright.

My 36-month forecast: Alloovium lands at $6–12M ARR by mid-2029 as an ANZ-anchored document-intelligence layer with a modest US toehold, then gets acqui-hired by Procore or Oracle Aconex once their native Copilot roadmap forces consolidation of the citation layer — not a fund-returner from angel entry, but not zero either. The mechanism is the pincer: Trunk Tools' $70M consumes the US GC oxygen while Procore's platform-native Copilot cannibalizes the cross-system read position Alloovium currently monetizes.

The strongest bear case against my forecast is actually the bull case that Alloovium *is* the **Color Genomics** template exactly — a workflow-infrastructure layer where the founder's domain authenticity (Schweitzer's site-engineering years) compounds faster than a well-funded generalist competitor's capital advantage, and construction is fragmented enough (unlike legal, where Harvey has declared winner status per my *AI Market Clarity* piece) that a #2 can still build a $500M outcome. That case stings — because construction genuinely is fragmented and Trunk Tools' $70M raised doesn't equal Trunk Tools' $70M deployed into ANZ. What defeats it in the dossier is the silence: no disclosed paying-customer count, no MoM, no per-seat pricing, no US logo. In an AI cycle where **Harvey** (2023) showed you what real velocity looks like, Alloovium's founder framing "$10B in lost value annually" is TAM-math where I'd want revenue-math. One verifiable condition flips the verdict: Alloovium discloses $2M+ ARR growing >20% MoM with three named US general contractor logos inside the next six months, and I'd bridge to a Series A check.
