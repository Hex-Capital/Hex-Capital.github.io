# HEVN, inc -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Peter Volnov shipped HOT Labs to 15M users and $100M TVL before pivoting into HEVN, inc, and that lived-operator residue is the only thing in this dossier that makes me sit up. HEVN, inc is, on the signature pairing, a real operator team walking into the most consensus fintech lane of the 2026 cycle -- Mercury at $5.2B with $650M ARR, Brex acquired by Capital One for $5.15B in April 2026, Fasset's $51M stablecoin-neobank round in May 2026 all stacked into the same room. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game HEVN, inc seems to be playing is "Mercury for non-US entities," which is exactly the game six other funds are also playing.

Anduril-Luckey is the HIGH rung on founder lived experience -- Palmer shipped a comparable artifact to scale in an adjacent domain before pivoting into the problem he had personally felt. HEVN, inc gets partial credit on that mechanism: Volnov, Briuzgin, Zhevlakov, and Mitsevich ran HOT Labs together for four years, scaled a stablecoin-adjacent wallet to 15M users, and operated their own entities across SF, UAE, BVI, and HK -- the dossier says they "felt the pain of global payments automation" running those companies. That is real. Where HEVN, inc diverges from the Luckey template is that the adjacency is consumer crypto wallet, not B2B treasury -- they shipped retail UX, and the new product is API-first contractor and vendor payout infrastructure for CFOs. The pain was felt, but the prior shipped artifact is not the same artifact. That puts HEVN, inc on the mid-to-high stretch of the ladder, not the top.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling is the LOW rung on "magically weird," and the failure mechanism there is simple -- when six tier-one VCs are chasing the same round, the price prices in the outcome and you cannot find them before consensus. HEVN, inc walks straight into that pattern. The dossier names Mercury ($5.2B), Brex (acquired $5.15B), Wise, Deel, BVNK, and Fasset ($51M) all stacked in the same competitive ring; TechCrunch reports YC itself began funding the Spring 2026 batch in USDC; BVNK already powers Deel, Worldpay, and Flywire. This is not Crusoe burning gas flares in North Dakota where two credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds. HEVN, inc is the opposite -- it is the aesthetic. Stablecoin neobank for global internet companies is the 2026 cycle's center of gravity.

Single CEO authority -- the mechanism that compounds over 18 months is this: when a four-cofounder team hits its first real disagreement, the named CEO either has tiebreaker authority on paper or the team grinds. HEVN, inc has a named CEO in Volnov, and the four-cofounder team has co-built for four years at HOT Labs, which mitigates the HQ Trivia / GameCrush failure mode where co-founders learned each other inside the crucible. What the dossier does not give me is equity split, vesting structure, or who breaks the tie when Volnov and Zhevlakov disagree on whether to pursue an EU charter versus an MTL stack. I default cautiously here.

Capital-intensive or unsexy-regulated -- counterfactual: for HEVN, inc to flip from mid to high on this dimension, the dossier would need to show HEVN, inc holding an EMI license in the EU, a state MTL stack in the US, or a VARA license in UAE rather than depending on local bank partners across 82 countries. The Traders Union (2026) piece says the bank accounts are on a waitlist; the gethevn.com page says the rails go through partners. That is the BVNK-Deel infra dependency, not the regulated-charter moat. The regulation is real -- stablecoin treasury for non-US companies crosses MTL, banking, and securities regimes -- but HEVN, inc is renting the regulatory surface, not building it.

Niantic is the single-anchor parallel for physical-world verifiability -- I played Ingress obsessively from 2012 before John Hanke ever pitched me. HEVN, inc fails that test in its current state: the product is a waitlist for EU/US/Swiss/UAE accounts, the X account has 1,056 followers, there is no pricing page on gethevn.com as of May 2026, and the only thing I could ostensibly touch is a USD card advertising "<0.5% FX fees." A normal human cannot install it, send a contractor payout through it, or hand it to a friend within 48 hours. The 463.2K-follower @p_volnov account is distribution potential, not a tactile artifact.

Forecast, 36 months out: HEVN, inc reaches $3-8M ARR by mid-2029, gets squeezed between Mercury extending coverage to non-US entities under its new charter capital and BVNK-style infra commoditizing the rails it rents, and either acqui-hires into Deel or Rippling at $30-80M or pivots specifically into AI-agent treasury rails -- the "built for humans and AI agents" line on the YC page is the pivot vector I'd watch. Volnov's 463.2K-follower distribution lane prevents the zero outcome; the commoditization layer prevents the venture-scale outcome.

The bull case stings, and it stings on my own anti-pedigree thesis: Volnov is exactly the outsider-trajectory founder I usually love -- ITMO-adjacent team, first SWE role at fifteen for Zhevlakov, a $250K Stellar Foundation grant, a prior product that hit #1 on DappRadar for eight months without a Stanford or Goldman line on anyone's resume. The bear answer is that outsider trajectory only loads when paired with a non-consensus category, and HEVN, inc is in the most consensus fintech category of the cycle -- the same dossier that gives me the underdog founder profile gives me Mercury at $5.2B and Brex at $5.15B in the same paragraph. The condition that flips my verdict: HEVN, inc shows a signed treasury or payout contract with a named non-US YC company (Spring 2026 batchmate, ideally), at a contract value over $50K ARR, before Demo Day -- a tactile artifact and a paying customer in the lane they claim, sourced from a network the incumbents structurally cannot reach. Until then, this is a rebuttal, not a hard no.
