# Chert -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Chert's entire wedge rests on a single channel-arbitrage observation: iMessage blue bubbles get higher reply rates than email because B2B inboxes are saturated. That is not a secret. That is a consensus complaint repackaged as a startup. Every founder, every SDR leader, every growth blog in 2025 noticed that email reply rates collapsed; Chert's response is to migrate the same activity into a channel that users have not yet learned to ignore. The half-life of that arbitrage is measured in quarters, not decades, and the Sendblue and Project Blue API layers cited in the dossier already commoditize the underlying plumbing. Chert is not crossing the chasm from zero to one ? it is sliding sideways from one saturated channel to one that will be saturated next.

Apply the secret test to Chert and nothing comes back. The contrarian claim implicit in Chert is that buyers will tolerate cold sales pitches arriving on what trychert.com itself calls a "trusted, personal channel." That is not a secret hidden from consensus ? it is a bet that users have not yet revolted, and the revolt is the reversion. The 11x.ai precedent in the dossier is the tell: $74M raised, ~$10M ARR, then 70-80% customer churn within months per TechCrunch. The AI-SDR category has already run the experiment Chert is rerunning with a different transport protocol. When the canonical comparable in your category lost three-quarters of its book in months, the burden of proof on a follow-on entrant is to articulate why it escapes the same pattern. Chert articulates nothing.

Founders Ian Fong and Gary Gao are an NYU sophomore and a Penn/Wharton sophomore with a JPMorgan summer internship pending and a circuits-lab research credential, respectively. Paul Graham's lens treats founder earnestness as the dominant signal; mine does not. Even granting full credit for the eight hackathon wins and Penn CURF research, the dossier shows zero shared prehistory between Fong and Gao, no prior B2B sales-tooling employment, and one co-founder still planning a JPMC rotation. *Zero to One* Chapter 9 is explicit: founders without shared prehistory are rolling dice. Chert's team profile reads as two strong individual undergraduates assembled through the YC/Z Fellows/hackathon mesh, not as a definite-optimist pair who built the autonomous-iMessage thesis together over years.

The strongest bull case for Chert ? and I want to construct it honestly ? runs through the Lyft exception. Lyft violated "competition is for losers" because the ride-share category was generating so much aggregate value that a #2 player still produced fund-relevant outcomes. Could Chert be the Lyft of AI SDR ? a #3 or #4 in a category that compounds so fast the laggards still print money? The MarketsandMarkets number says AI SDR goes from $4.12B to $15.01B by 2030 at 29.5% CAGR. If Chert captures even 1% of that, it is a profitable services business. But that is precisely the trap. The Lyft override applies when category economics produce $20B #2 outcomes; AI-SDR economics so far produce $10M ARR companies that lose 80% of customers. The category is not Lyft-shaped ? it is more like the cleantech pattern, founders swept up by a social trend rather than by hard analysis, and the 11x churn data is the cleantech crash arriving early.

Run Chert against my unstated patterns and the results compound the pass. Atoms over bits: Chert is pure software riding on someone else's platform ? Apple's, and Apple has no business iMessage API, which means Chert exists at Tim Cook's discretion. Government as monopoly customer: not applicable. Network-mediated deal flow into my portfolio: no Founders Fund partner is incubating this, no PayPal mafia member is on the cap table, the named backers are YC, Z Fellows, and Betafund. Portfolio cross-pollination: Ramp and Anduril are not buying iMessage outbound from sophomores. The General Matter parallel does not apply, the Palantir parallel does not apply, the Anduril parallel does not apply. The closest portfolio anchor is Wish ? a no-moat layer competing on tactical advantage in a market with multiple funded entrants and one dominant platform owner who can change terms at will. Wish was the portfolio's most expensive lesson in violating the monopoly criterion; Chert is the seed-stage version of that lesson.

The TCPA dimension and the Apple ToS dimension are the two physical-world realities that make Chert structurally fragile. iMessage outbound at scale is one Apple policy update away from disabling the product, and one FTC or state-AG action away from being treated as cold SMS under TCPA. Chert's dossier reports no compliance posture and no platform-relationship disclosure. A real monopoly requires a moat that Apple cannot revoke; Chert has chosen a moat that Apple defines. Customer logos like Whop and testimonials referencing Databricks/Ramp leaders are encouraging pre-seed signals, but they are validation that managed iMessage outreach books meetings this quarter, not that Chert can hold a category for a decade.

The single condition that would flip me to Invest is evidence that Chert has built proprietary lead-sourcing infrastructure ? specifically, a verified-mobile-number dataset of B2B decision-makers at meaningful scale that no competitor can replicate, analogous to Clearview's scraped image corpus that overrode my stated objections to that investment. The dossier hints at "verified phone numbers matching customer ICP" but provides zero scale data, no proprietary acquisition mechanism, and no defensibility around it. If Fong and Gao came back with a million verified mobile numbers cross-referenced to title and company, sourced through a method incumbents structurally cannot replicate, that would be a Clearview-shaped secret and the channel question would become secondary. Without that, Chert is a managed service in a saturated category gated by a platform owner who will eventually notice. Pass.

The decisive dimensions are Monopoly Secret and Physical-World Moat. The secret score is locked by the absence of any contrarian thesis beyond channel migration ? the consensus AI-SDR pattern with a bluer bubble. The moat score is locked by Chert being a software layer atop Apple's consumer messaging platform with no proprietary atoms, no government embedding, and no infrastructure that survives a Cupertino policy memo.
