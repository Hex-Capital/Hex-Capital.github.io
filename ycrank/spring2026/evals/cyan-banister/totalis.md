# Totalis -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Polymarket launched perpetual futures on April 22, 2026 — the same week Totalis is opening early access for its derivatives layer built on top of Polymarket. Kalshi announced perps simultaneously. Fanatics Markets shipped "Combos" for sports parlays. DraftKings acquired Railbird, a CFTC-licensed derivatives exchange, in December 2025. Totalis is pre-launch with two GitHub repos totaling one star, positioning itself as a derivatives layer on top of primary venues that are actively building the exact same derivative products with billions in capital and millions of existing users. I've made this mistake before — backing a product that sits one layer above an infrastructure owner who can vertically integrate your feature set in a quarter. When I look at Totalis's cross-venue parlay aggregation, I see something that Polymarket (seeking $400M at $15B) or Kalshi ($22B valuation) could ship as a feature, not something they'd need to acquire.

Eric Liu and Pravesh Mansharamani are clearly sharp builders — Pravesh alone has won 11+ hackathons and earned roughly $55,000 in prizes, including $16,000 at ETHDenver 2025 for EigenBets, a prediction market with AI-agent oracle verification. Eric and Pravesh built goddid.money together at ETHGlobal Buenos Aires, converting Polymarket trading history into on-chain credit scores. Pravesh has five DeFi engineering internships across EigenLabs, SquidRouter, Nethermind, and others. These are technically capable people who know the prediction market stack from the inside. But when I run my first question — tell me your story, not the business — what I hear is: two University of Waterloo math students who were active in the Blockchain club, won a bunch of hackathons in the DeFi space, and spotted a structural gap in prediction market plumbing. That's a market opportunity identified through technical proximity, not a biographical crucible that made these founders incapable of working on anything else. Totalis emerged from hackathon projects, not from a life experience that burned this problem into someone's nervous system. I don't doubt their competence, but competence and forging are different things entirely.

Prediction markets in 2026 are the opposite of a space that makes sophisticated investors uncomfortable. Bernstein projects $1 trillion in annual trading volume by 2030 at 80% CAGR. Every major financial player — Robinhood, ICE/NYSE, DraftKings — is racing to enter. Kalshi won its CFTC battle and is valued at $22 billion. Polymarket just raised at $15 billion. This is consensus capital flooding into a consensus thesis. When I backed Anduril, defense tech was politically toxic in my world. When I wrote my SpaceX check, rockets were blowing up and everyone thought Elon was burning cash into the atmosphere. Totalis is building financial plumbing in the hottest fintech category of the year. Nobody shifts uncomfortably in their chair when you describe prediction market derivatives — they lean forward and ask how to get allocation. That's exactly the signal that tells me this isn't where my edge lives.

The invisible system test is where Totalis falls furthest from my framework. Prediction markets are, almost by definition, machines for making hidden information visible — they surface collective beliefs about future outcomes through transparent price discovery. Totalis isn't exposing an opaque structure; it's adding financial engineering on top of one of the most transparent market mechanisms ever created. Multi-leg parlays combining geopolitics with crypto prices serve sophisticated traders who already have both capital and information. When I backed Affirm, Max Levchin was attacking credit card opacity — a system that extracted billions from consumers who couldn't understand their own interest rates. When I backed Ownwell, the founders were attacking property tax assessments that overcharged homeowners who didn't know they could appeal. Totalis is building structured products for crypto-native speculators. The users aren't being exploited by information asymmetry; they're the most information-rich participants in the market. There's no hidden tax being revealed.

The strongest bull case for Totalis centers on infrastructure timing: derivatives markets in traditional finance are typically 10-100x the size of their underlying spot markets, and if prediction markets truly reach $1 trillion, a well-positioned derivatives layer could capture enormous value. The cross-venue aggregation thesis — unifying liquidity across Polymarket, Kalshi, and future venues — has genuine structural merit, since no single venue will win everything and traders want unified positions. YC's first all-USDC investment on Solana signals institutional legitimacy for crypto-native financial infrastructure. And the founders' specific technical background — building EigenBets, goddid.money, and working at EigenLabs and SquidRouter — gives them authentic domain fluency that most pre-seed teams lack. I checked my known exceptions: RTFKT was justified by a narrow cultural timing window where the acquirer landscape was identifiable. But prediction market derivatives don't have a narrow window — the market is expanding over years, giving well-capitalized incumbents plenty of time to build competing products, which they are already doing this week. The condition that would flip me: if Totalis secured exclusive or privileged API access to a major venue — a structural integration that couldn't be replicated — or if Pravesh or Eric had a biographical connection to prediction markets that went beyond hackathons (a family financial loss from opaque betting markets, a personal experience with information asymmetry in emerging markets), I'd reassess. Without either, Totalis is technically competent founders building a feature, not an infrastructure company, in a consensus market with no invisible system to disrupt.

Totalis scores lowest on the two dimensions I weight most heavily: founder biographical forge and contrarian conviction. The absence of a personal crucible driving these founders toward prediction market derivatives — combined with the most consensus-attractive market timing I've seen in fintech this year — means this doesn't activate my framework where it matters most. I wish Eric and Pravesh well; they're talented builders in a real market. But this isn't a Long Journey bet.
