# Hyper -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Hyper is two ex-Matic Robots engineers — Shalin Shah and Kanyes Thaker, UC Berkeley '21 — running tight against zero cash burn while building exactly the consensus-hot AI memory wedge that Glean is already converting to $200M ARR. That's the signature: high capital discipline, near-zero consensus mispricing. A disciplined team executing on a priced-in category is not the asymmetry I'm paid to find.

The mispricing ladder runs from Solana at $0.04 — where every smart crypto person told me Ethereum had won and the market priced the bet at zero — through Slack, where I paid consensus price because the primitive was real, down to the AI-copilot YC wrapper where ten VCs are bidding at the demo and the upside is already in the price. Hyper sits one rung above the floor. The dossier names the wedge plainly: "shared memory for humans and AI," ingesting Notion, Cursor, Claude Code, email, LinkedIn DMs. Glean just raised $150M at $7.2B in June 2025 and went $100M to $200M ARR in nine months. Notion AI is shipping connectors. Dust is at $7.3M ARR with $21.5M raised. There is no version of Hyper's pitch where every smart investor in the room hasn't already heard the same deck three times this week. The risk is not in the price.

The structural durability ladder runs from Metropolis — AI-powered parking where the physical infrastructure cannot be replicated by an LLM and the seed is now valued near $5B — through Solana's protocol-level consensus moat, down to the generic AI copilot that Adobe replicates by Tuesday. Hyper sits at the bottom rung. The technical primitives Hyper relies on — webhook ingestion, embeddings, retrieval injection at chat turn — are commodity. The dossier itself flags that Cursor, Claude Code, ChatGPT, and Notion are each shipping native memory. Every time I get pitched "shared memory across all your AI tools," I think: this is perfect for OpenAI, this is perfect for Notion, this is perfect for Glean. The value accrues to the platforms, not the middleware.

The primitive ladder runs from Venmo — a verb, a behavior that did not exist before — through Patreon's creator monetization, down to "better CRM." Hyper lives in the lower-middle: "shared memory for humans and AI" is conceptually adjacent to a primitive, but the dossier positions Hyper as a write-layer that injects context into other AI tools per chat turn. That's middleware, not a new verb. Nobody is going to say "Hyper me the context." On the sane/insane ladder — Raj at Solana (engineer through three companies, calm, doing something the market called impossible) at the top, down to combative founders pitching reasonable ideas — Shah and Thaker read sane. Matic Robots autonomy and perception is real signal-fusion work, conceptually relevant. But the idea isn't insane. It's the consensus 2026 idea. Sane person, sane idea is boring; that's the whole point. Capital discipline is the bright spot: two people, not hiring, prior product self-funded per the Product Hunt notes. That's the Gusto-side of the ladder, not the Allbirds-side. They could survive a long winter. The question is whether the destination is worth surviving toward.

Thirty-six month forecast: Hyper does not reach $5M ARR independently and gets acqui-hired into Notion, Glean, or a model lab for the team in the $5–20M range, or quietly winds down. The mechanism is the AI kill test running in real time — Notion AI's connector roadmap and per-tool native memory compress Hyper's wedge from both sides while Glean owns the enterprise budget line. Two engineers cannot out-distribute a $7.2B incumbent into the same buyer.

The strongest objection is the Slack exception. Butterfield was a hot deal, Lessin paid consensus price, and it returned billions because real-time workplace messaging was a genuine new primitive. What if Hyper is the same — what if "shared cross-tool team memory injected per chat turn" is a real new behavior I'm dismissing because it sounds like middleware? It stings because the Matic Robots multi-source perception background is exactly the kind of weird-adjacent founder background that sometimes births a primitive in a non-obvious wrapper. But Butterfield had a Flickr exit before Slack — observed sanity across cycles. Shah and Thaker are first-time founders I have not watched through three companies. And the March 2026 Product Hunt launch as an iOS voice note-taker followed by a May 2026 reframe as a "company brain" is the positioning ambiguity that betrays a team still searching, not a team executing on a primitive they see clearly. The flip condition is concrete: five named enterprise design partners with measurable retention against Notion AI's native memory, by month twelve. Until then, pass.
