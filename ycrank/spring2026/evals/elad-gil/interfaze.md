# Interfaze -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Interfaze is the dimensional inverse of my **Perplexity** (2022) template -- a 2x-founder with shipping history (Yoeven Khemlani took a prior travel startup to $3M ARR before acquisition and raised $1.5M pre-seed for JigsawStack from Antler and Ada Ventures) attacking a market with no disclosed revenue curve. That high/low pairing is the whole analytical signature: competent repeat operator, an academic co-founder with an IEEE CAI 2026 paper, $1.50/$3.50 per-million-token pricing live -- and a dossier that names zero customers, zero MRR, and zero MoM growth. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose.

On revenue velocity, the **Harvey** (2023) rung is "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" against a backdrop of named AmLaw logos -- that's what "just working" looks like in dollars (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). Interfaze sits nowhere near that rung. The dossier discloses a price sheet, "up to 50 requests per second" capacity, and a founder co-hosting a 100-person SF meetup on May 28, 2026 -- none of which is buying behavior. The mechanism that matters here is the false-signal-versus-just-working distinction: developer-API startups generate pilot tourism easily, and the absence of a single named enterprise integration in the Interfaze dossier means I can't tell whether the OpenAI-compatible Chat Completions surface is converting traffic or collecting curiosity hits. If Yoeven had $50K MRR compounding 30% MoM, that line would be in the dossier; its absence forces me to apply the absent-evidence default.

The market re-opening question is where Interfaze gets partial credit. Reducto's March 2025 RD-FormsBench documented Mistral OCR at ~45% and Gemini 2.0 Flash at ~80% on dense tables -- that's a measured failure mode of frontier models on deterministic tasks, published in the last 24-month window, which is structurally the kind of catalyst I look for. The mechanism: once a failure mode is legible to procurement, specialist-routing has a wedge. But it's a thin catalyst compared to defense-post-Ukraine for **Anduril** (2022) or crypto-post-skepticism for **Coinbase** (2013), because the same benchmarks Interfaze would cite as a tailwind also show frontier models closing the gap quarter-over-quarter. Eighteen months from now, either GPT-5-class structured-output guarantees neutralize the catalyst, or Reducto-class specialists own the enterprise channel -- the Interfaze window is narrower than the founder narrative implies.

Yoeven Khemlani clears the founder-ships bar that **Perplexity** (2022) set, but he clears it sideways. He's shipped a property/travel company to $3M ARR plus a funded AI-API company (JigsawStack), and Harsha Vardhan Khurdula brings computer-vision research credentials with peer-reviewed work. That's a real shipping rhythm. The friction is the JigsawStack overlap -- Yoeven is still publicly identified as JigsawStack's founder on LinkedIn, and JigsawStack's "unified AI API suite" positioning is structurally adjacent to Interfaze's "deterministic developer tasks" thesis. Counterfactually, for this dimension to vault into my **Perplexity** rung, the Interfaze dossier would need to name the JigsawStack wind-down or merger, plus a shipped artifact between YC interview and demo day -- a new model card, a new tool, a published latency curve. The dossier names none of those, which keeps Interfaze pinned just above the Brex-archetype mid-rung rather than into ship-between-meetings territory.

Interfaze's distribution position is not index-like in the **Stripe** (2010) sense. **Stripe** sits in the workflow path of every e-commerce entrant; Interfaze sits in a route that competes against Reducto's 12-model orchestration, LlamaParse inside the LlamaIndex framework, Unstructured.io's $65M Series B distribution, Mistral OCR from a frontier lab, and -- structurally most dangerous -- the OpenAI and Anthropic endpoints that Interfaze's own "OpenAI-compatible" API explicitly mimics. The single-anchor parallel here is **Decagon** (2023), which I funded because customer support was an open AI sub-market per my *AI Market Clarity* piece; deterministic developer extraction is not on that "wide open" list, it's a contested specialist lane where multiple funded entrants are visible. Interfaze has to win share, not ride a category.

On the single-miracle test, Interfaze is cleaner than the dossier first suggests. The miracle is the claim of "98%-99% structured output accuracy" via task-routed small models plus LLMs -- one technical bet, validated indirectly by an IEEE CAI 2026 paper acceptance (arxiv.org/abs/2602.04101, which I'd want to independently verify given the unusual identifier). No new physics, no regulatory miracle, no consumer-behavior miracle. This is **Color Genomics** (2015) -shaped on the architecture axis: workflow infrastructure on top of commodity model capability, not a chemistry bet. The single-miracle posture is the strongest thing in the dossier -- which is why Interfaze doesn't fail outright; it just fails to cross the velocity threshold that would make the miracle bankable.

My 36-month forecast: Interfaze reaches $1-3M ARR servicing indie developers and a handful of mid-market data-extraction customers, then gets acqui-hired by Unstructured.io or folded back into JigsawStack as a "deterministic models" sub-product -- unable to break out because frontier-model structured-output guarantees compress the wedge and Reducto owns the enterprise procurement channel that Interfaze's self-serve API was never designed to penetrate. The mechanism is the velocity/index pairing: no compounding curve plus no index position equals share-fight outcome, which in specialist-API markets historically caps at small acquisition.

The bear case I would have to dismantle on the bull side is that "deterministic developer AI" is a Decagon-shaped sub-market hiding in plain sight, that Reducto's 2025 benchmarks are the catalyst I should be weighting harder, and that Yoeven's prior $3M ARR exit is exactly the operationally-disciplined-CEO archetype that *High Growth Handbook* (2018) is implicitly a profile of. That case stings -- I funded **Decagon** (2023) on weaker founder evidence than Interfaze offers. What dismantles it is the JigsawStack overlap (the same founder pitching adjacent theses is a split-focus tell, not a serial-founder flex) plus the dossier's silence on a single named enterprise logo, which in an AI-API market in 2026 is dispositive. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: $500K MRR with one named frontier-adjacent customer (Anthropic, Vercel, Cursor, Linear) using Interfaze in production within six months, compounding above 20% MoM. Absent that, this is a pass.
