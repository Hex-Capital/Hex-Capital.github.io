# Cova -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Cova is a single-state Medicaid-reimbursed home care agency wrapping LLM agents around back-office workflows -- which means its highest dimension is a pedigree-tinged founder profile (Ansh Tandon ex-Apple AI / ex-Tesla, Purdue CS) and its lowest is an explicit anti-index position: Cova *operates* one Michigan agency competing against Visiting Angels, Comfort Keepers, and Honor's national network rather than sitting in the workflow path that every entrant must touch. That high/low pairing is diagnostic. This is not Decagon (2023) or Abridge (2023) -- both workflow-infrastructure plays in a re-opened category. This is closer to a regional services rollup with an LLM skin, and my framework treats those as share-fights, not index positions.

Stripe (2010) is the load-bearing comparison on distribution position because Stripe didn't have to *beat* PayPal at acquiring -- it sat in the workflow path every developer touched, and grew as e-commerce compounded. Cova's dossier does the opposite: Michigan-only licensure (YC page, 2026), direct-to-consumer enrollment via cova.care and michiganhomehelp.com, and a stated wedge of "highest base rate in the state" that is fundamentally a price/wage competition against named local franchises. Cova doesn't sit *under* Honor or Visiting Angels -- it competes head-to-head with them for the same Michigan Medicaid Home Help dollars at $12.25/hour (Givers, 2026). The Stripe template requires Cova to be what Sage Care and MochaCare (YC W26) are actually attempting -- the software layer agencies adopt. Cova has explicitly chosen the agency-operator path, which is the Lyft-shaped slot in this category, not the Stripe-shaped one.

Lyft Series C is the failure-mechanism rung on revenue velocity: I passed on Lyft because the dossier could not show share-inversion math in a winner-take-all-or-oligopoly market, and I have since extended that filter to any AI company that goes silent on growth ("something's fundamentally broken," Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). Cova discloses no revenue, no caregiver count, no client count, no Product Hunt, no press, no funding (WebSearch, June 2026). At Summer 2026 pre-seed I don't punish missing metrics by default -- but Cova *operates* a licensed agency, meaning every enrolled client is a billable revenue event. The absence of even a count of caregivers or enrolled families against a $12.25/hour reimbursement schedule is conspicuous in a way it would not be for a pure software startup. Silence here reads as silence about the curve, not silence about pre-launch state.

Market re-opening compounds badly for Cova over 18 months because the dossier's own catalyst is flagged "[Inferred]" and "not stated by company." Compare to Anduril (2022), where Ukraine was the specific 24-month catalyst that re-opened defense procurement -- a fact, not an inference. Medicaid Home Help rates and Michigan licensure are steady-state. The "LLM agents are now reliable enough for Medicaid billing" framing is real for some workflows, but it's the same catalyst Sage Care and MochaCare are riding on the software side, and three of the YC home care peers (Clara, Oma Care, Kaigo Health, Helpcare AI) are already in market. Cova's catalyst is shared, not specific, which means the window doesn't favor Cova over the YC cohort behind and beside it.

Founder Ships flips a tier only if Jordan Ibe is identified with named shipped artifacts and Ansh Tandon's Apple/Tesla work resolves to specific products he owned end-to-end. Today the dossier gives me a credentialed AI engineer (Tandon, anshtandon.com) and a co-founder whose LinkedIn match to Cornell is "not confirmed as same individual." Perplexity (2022) came in because Aravind pinged me cold and shipped the thing we discussed the next week. Cova's founders may well be shipping at that cadence in Detroit -- but the dossier records no GitHub, no prior independent products, no demos, no operating channel beyond a phone number and an email. Pedigree without artifacts is the disqualifier I flagged in the Hustle Commons framework summary, not the qualifier.

Decagon (2023) is the single-anchor parallel on miracle architecture. Decagon assumed one validated capability -- LLM accuracy at customer-support tasks -- and treated the rest as GTM and product execution. Cova's dossier requires at least four independent miracles to clear: (1) AI-agent accuracy in Medicaid eligibility and claims under audit liability, (2) reimbursement math at $12.25/hour state-approved still funding a "highest base rate in the state" wage premium after overhead, (3) caregiver supply elasticity at that premium, and (4) state-by-state agency relicensing to escape geographic concentration. Compounding small odds across four independents is exactly the multi-miracle dependency chain NFX's *Unusual Signs* piece tells me to pass on.

Forecast, 36 months: Cova is either a sub-$2M ARR Michigan-only operator that quietly winds down or gets absorbed by Honor or a regional roll-up, or it pivots to selling its AI-agent stack to other licensed agencies and becomes a smaller, later entrant in the Sage Care / MochaCare slot. I do not see a path where Cova is "one of that dozen or two" durable companies in home care; the durable slot in this category, if it exists, looks like Honor's network or the software-layer companies, not a single-state agency.

The strongest opposing case is Color Genomics (2015) -- I co-founded a workflow-infrastructure company sitting on top of a commoditized, regulated layer (sequencing chemistry / Medicaid reimbursement is the analog), and operated rather than passively funded. A bull on Cova would say Ansh Tandon and Jordan Ibe are running the Color play in home care: the chemistry is Medicaid plumbing, the workflow infrastructure is the AI agent stack, and operating an agency is the only way to gather the data to build that stack. The case stings because it maps to my own Known Exception. It loses on three specifics: Color was capitalized at a level that bought multi-year science-adjacent runway, Color had an operator (me) with the High Growth Handbook scaling lens already in the chair, and Color's workflow layer was indexed to a commodity *price collapse* in sequencing -- a specific catalyst Cova lacks. Two pre-seed founders without documented healthcare-operations history are not running the Color play; they are running an agency. I flip to Invest if Cova discloses, within 90 days, named MoM client growth above 20% AND a second-state license filing AND a confirmed Jordan Ibe shipping history -- short of those three, this is a pass.
