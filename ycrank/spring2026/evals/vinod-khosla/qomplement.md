# qomplement -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

qomplement is a high-learning-rate team aimed at a low-consequence target. Kerim Taray and Andres Garza pivoted in roughly twelve months from a Product Hunt "AI agent that controls your personal computer" (May 3, 2025) to "Agentic ERP for supply chain operations" -- that is real founder plasticity. But the success-state on the new vector is that procurement clerks at grocery chains stop retyping POs from PDFs. No incumbent regime dies. SAP keeps shipping, Oracle keeps shipping, NetSuite keeps shipping. qomplement makes their lives slightly more bearable.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung for consequence magnitude in my portfolio because the success-state was the reorganization of cognition itself -- I wrote $50M into no revenue because the consequence-at-maximum-ambition was civilizational. qomplement's maximum-ambition success-state is that 20+ companies become 20,000 companies processing freight invoices through an agent overlay instead of email. That is the Pass Example I described almost verbatim -- "we use GPT-4 to summarize X and integrate with Y" -- transposed from sales calls to purchase orders. The YC dossier itself describes the wedge as "PO intake, vendor onboarding, item master updates, freight orders, carrier invoicing." Nothing in qomplement collapses; SAP's "Supply Chain Trends for 2026" roadmap is cited in the dossier's own risk section as the absorption mechanism. The $15T labor displacement frame I gave Fortune (2026-03-06) requires a named labor category vanishing -- qomplement does not name one with a dollar figure.

The **Range Fuels** (~2007) failure mechanism I keep in mind on technical risk was capital-adequacy misjudgment around a science miracle competitors could not fund. qomplement has the inverse profile: no science miracle, and the competitor Pivot in Paris has already raised $70M total through a Series B (SiliconANGLE, May 2026) processing €2.5B in invoices annually doing functionally the same horizontal extraction. When consensus capital is already $70M ahead of you on the identical primitive, the dimension that justified my **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) $115M Series A -- capital-hostile science most VCs decline -- is conspicuously absent. qomplement is a wedge any Tier-1 generalist will fund, which means I am over-resourced and structurally non-edge.

Founder-defies-experts is a mechanism that compounds over 18 months only if the founder has a public position the field rejected. Kerim Taray's pre-qomplement work was RPA consulting for Mexican enterprises (Expansión, Sep 2025; Milenio). RPA-to-agents is the most consensual narrative in 2025-2026 enterprise software -- it is the Salesforce-veteran pattern from my TED Talk transposed to a Tec de Monterrey CS pedigree. The Mexican-language press in Expansión and Startups Latam frames qomplement as "competencia para GPT-5," which is positioning rhetoric, not a defied-expert claim. I see no public stake on a controversial position the procurement industry rejected.

Counterfactual for learning rate flipping a tier: qomplement would need the Product Hunt-to-ERP pivot framed as a thesis update with stated reasoning -- the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C. The dossier shows the artifact of a pivot (computer-control agent → vertical ops agent) but no public retrospective from Taray or Garza on what they learned that forced the rotation. A pivot is evidence of plasticity; a documented pivot with a stated thesis update is evidence of judgment. qomplement has the former, not the latter, which is why the dimension scores meaningfully but not at the Slash level.

qomplement's capital profile compares directly to my **DoorDash** (2013) Known Exception -- capital-light, software-only, consumer/B2B logistics economics. DoorDash got the override because the success-state genuinely reshaped consumer-commerce flow at a scale that returned a $72B IPO. qomplement's success-state, even fully realized across "the largest grocery chains and trucking companies in the world" as self-claimed, is a vertical SaaS company with per-document or per-seat pricing. That is a $200M-$2B outcome, not a $72B outcome, and the check size required -- pre-seed YC standard -- is below the deployment scale where I write with conviction.

My 36-month forecast on qomplement: acquired by an ERP incumbent (SAP, Oracle, NetSuite) or by Pivot itself for $40M-$180M at $2M-$8M ARR, OR pivots a second time into a narrower vertical (carrier-invoice reconciliation specifically) before Series B. The mechanism is straightforward -- Pivot's $70M head start commoditizes the horizontal extraction primitive, and qomplement's only durable wedge is the grocery/trucking document corpus, which is exactly the asset an SAP partner ecosystem buys rather than competes with. The high-learning-rate / low-consequence signature predicts a team smart enough to find a soft landing, not a team aimed at a $72B outcome.

The strongest bull case against my pass invokes the **Slash** (2024) Known Exception: YC pedigree plus documented pivot plus genuine pre-seed traction (10,000+ docs/day across 20+ companies is real, not vapor) can override low consequence magnitude. Pivot's $70M Series B is bull-readable as category validation, not as commoditization -- the same way Stripe's existence validated payments rather than killing every payments startup. And if qomplement's customer claim of "largest grocery chains and trucking companies in the world" is real, the per-vertical document corpus accumulating per account is a genuine training-data moat in 24 months. That case stings because Tom Blomfield is the YC partner and Blomfield's pattern-match on enterprise document agents is not casual. But it loses on this dossier: Slash's override was a publicly told pivot story with a learning artifact; qomplement's pivot is inferred from a stale Product Hunt page, not narrated by the founders. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: qomplement discloses one Fortune-500 grocery or trucking logo with ACV above $500K and names the displaced labor cost in dollars -- the **Impossible Foods** (2011) "displaces animal protein production" framing transposed to "displaces $X of ERP-implementation labor." Until then, qomplement scores as a competent workflow overlay, not as the bet my framework was built for.
