# Loops AI -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Hakan Baş co-founded Peak Games and exited to Zynga for $1.8 billion. That's a real credential — one of the larger Turkish tech exits in history. But Peak Games was a casual gaming company. The specific knowledge that builds a gaming empire — viral loop mechanics, ad monetization, player psychology — is almost entirely non-transferable to building AI commerce agents. Baş also co-founded Lidyana.com, a fashion marketplace, which is closer to the target. Yet his role at Loops AI is listed as "Co-Founder" with no functional title, and the dossier finds no documented connection to the other three founders. When a $1.8B-exit founder joins a pre-seed company without taking the CEO seat, I want to understand the structure. Is he a principal whose identity is fused with Loops AI, or is he lending credibility to a team that needs it? The dossier can't answer that, and the distinction matters enormously.

The specific knowledge question is where Loops AI struggles most under my framework. Ari Nazir's background spans HSBC, the White House NEC, and crypto fund management — impressive trajectory, zero overlap with building AI systems for e-commerce. İlker Zorluoğlu has marketing strategy and a stint at GoArt Metaverse. Yusuf Bahadır, the CTO, has no visible public repos and no documented AI/ML expertise beyond an Istanbul Technical University degree. The company's origin story — Ari and İlker meeting in Bali and sharing frustration with e-commerce support — is market observation, not the kind of obsessive domain immersion I look for. Compare this to Aravind Srinivas at Perplexity, who spent years at Google Brain and OpenAI building the exact systems he then productized. At Loops AI, nobody on the team appears to have spent years building the systems that constitute the product. If Ari disappeared, could a competent operator continue? Almost certainly yes. That's the test, and Loops AI fails it.

The leverage audit raises the deepest concern. Loops AI's core capabilities — LLM-powered chatbots, semantic search, virtual try-on — are built on rented infrastructure. OpenAI launched native agentic checkout in ChatGPT. Google launched agentic commerce in Search and Gemini. Both happened in 2026, which means the platform providers are moving into Loops AI's exact value layer. This is the AI wrapper pattern I consistently pass on: the company creates integration value today, but the moment the foundation model provider ships the feature natively, the wrapper evaporates. The $500K ARR is real revenue, but Gorgias at $80M+ ARR with $114M raised and AI Agent 2.0 already in market has a hundred-fold head start on the integration and data layer. Loops AI's multi-modal product breadth — combining search, try-on, and sales agents — creates surface area, but breadth without depth is a liability when each vertical feature is independently reproducible.

The bull case deserves honest examination. $500K ARR with claimed 2x growth in 45 days is genuine traction — not the kind you manufacture. Named customers like P&G Fairy, Radisson Collection, and Mövenpick are recognizable brands. The Turkish/MENA market entry is a smart geographic wedge where Gorgias and Rep AI have minimal presence, and Baş's network likely greased those initial deals. If Loops AI can compound its data flywheel across 50+ brand integrations into a proprietary semantic intelligence layer — product catalogs, customer interaction patterns, conversion data — that's a defensibility path. For this to work, the data advantage would need to accumulate faster than OpenAI and Google can build their own commerce data layers, which is a bet on speed against organizations with orders-of-magnitude more resources. The a16z Speedrun selection is a co-investor signal, though weaker than a Founders Fund or Jeff Dean co-sign would be.

Twelve people generating $500K ARR yields roughly $42K per person — leagues away from the Cursor benchmark of $10M+ ARR per employee that defines extreme team-to-value leverage. The founding team includes two non-technical co-founders (Nazir and Zorluoğlu) and a CTO with no visible open-source or AI research footprint. Enterprise sales expansion into the U.S. market will almost certainly require proportional headcount growth — account executives, integration engineers, customer success — the exact labor leverage pattern I penalize. Loops AI reads as a competent enterprise SaaS operation, not an AI-native team that could reach $10M ARR with fifteen people.

My Wish investment taught me that marketplace leverage without specific knowledge is structurally fragile — Wish collapsed 95% from its IPO because the leverage criterion alone, without founder-specific-knowledge moat, was insufficient. Loops AI rhymes with that pattern: real traction in a real market, reasonable leverage through software, but no knowledge so specific that a well-funded competitor couldn't replicate the product. The access democratization angle is modest — Loops AI gives mid-market brands better AI tools, but it's not unlocking something previously gatekept from an entire population the way AngelList or CoinList did. The two dimensions that lock my verdict are specific knowledge productization — thin across all four founders — and permissionless leverage architecture, where the core value layer is already being absorbed by platform providers. Pass.
