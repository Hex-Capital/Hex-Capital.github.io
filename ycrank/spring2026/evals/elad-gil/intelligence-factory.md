# Intelligence Factory -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Intelligence Factory is an infrastructure-layer claim ("works on any robot," generalized manipulation) sitting on top of a single-miracle ledger that demands at least four miracles simultaneously -- and the highest dimension here is exactly the one where NVIDIA's open-source GR00T N1 sets the commoditization floor. That pairing is the diagnostic: Intelligence Factory is making the right *kind* of bet (the policy layer, not a humanoid OEM) at exactly the moment the policy layer is being contested by Skild ($14B valuation, $1.4B raised), Physical Intelligence ($1B+ cumulative), and a free NVIDIA foundation model. The dossier gives me a five-person team in SF with no disclosed funding beyond YC and an unconfirmed identity for one of the two co-founders. That is the entry point: structural timing is real, but consensus is not just formed -- it is overheated.

Infrastructure-Layer Positioning is where Intelligence Factory ranks highest, so I'll start there. **Stripe** is my high rung -- a payments API that became the default substrate, compounding switching costs across an entire generation of internet companies. **Harvey** is my mid rung -- a platform for law firms that embeds in workflows but doesn't expose an API the way Stripe does. **Wish** is my low rung -- a consumer marketplace I backed that had no platform layer and collapsed 99% post-IPO when growth slowed. Intelligence Factory's stated wedge -- a hardware-agnostic generalist policy licensed to OEMs -- is structurally above Harvey and below Stripe in ambition. The dossier evidence pinning the placement is the headline "Generalized Robotics Manipulation" (intelligence-factory.com) combined with the claim of cross-embodiment generalization. That is genuinely the right layer. What pulls Intelligence Factory off Stripe and toward Harvey-or-below is NVIDIA Isaac GR00T N1 being "open and fully customizable" -- the platform layer Intelligence Factory is trying to own is being given away by the GPU vendor every robot will run on.

Single-Miracle Viability is where Intelligence Factory ranks lowest, and this is where I lose the deal. **Harvey** is my high rung -- one exogenous miracle (foundation model capability), proven buyers (law firms already buy associate-time-saving software), execution from there. **Anduril** is my mid rung and a Known Exception -- I overrode the multi-miracle test because bipartisan defense procurement reform was a generational structural opening that justified the override. **Consumer social apps** (my standing pass example) are the low rung -- distribution miracle plus monetization miracle plus retention miracle, compounding small odds. Intelligence Factory sits at the low rung and arguably below it. The miracles required: (1) match Skild/Physical Intelligence's policy quality with $0 disclosed external capital vs. their $1B+, (2) build a proprietary tactile-and-teleop dataset that doesn't exist yet, (3) win OEM design wins against NVIDIA's free, bundled alternative, (4) survive the "Meta acquires Assured Robot Intelligence" pattern (TechCrunch May 2026) where hyperscalers buy the larger players and leave subscale generalist policy startups stranded. Anduril got the override because Palmer Luckey was Palmer Luckey and defense reform was a once-in-a-generation opening. Intelligence Factory has neither the founder firepower nor the comparably nonobvious structural opening to earn that override.

My 36-month forecast: Intelligence Factory is acqui-hired by a robot OEM or hyperscaler (NVIDIA, Meta, or a Tier-2 humanoid OEM) for under $50M within 24-36 months, or quietly winds down. The signature -- "right layer, wrong moment, wrong capital base" -- produces an acqui-hire, not a zero and not a winner.

The strongest bull case invokes my **Anduril** override. Robotics in 2026 is plausibly a generational structural shift on the scale of defense modernization in 2017, and when that magnitude of shift is happening I have explicitly accepted multi-miracle risk. If the VLA-on-any-robot thesis is correct, the eventual winner captures a category that the dossier itself sizes at ~$5T at maturity, and being subscale at pre-seed is irrelevant if Intelligence Factory's policy outperforms on a benchmark that matters to OEMs. I dismantle this with the dossier's own evidence: Anduril's override required a market that mainstream investors had *dismissed* (defense was uninvestable on Sand Hill in 2017). Robotics policy in May 2026 is the opposite -- it is the most obvious, most-funded, most-hyped AI category, with $1B+ peers already shipping demos and revenue (Skild's "$30 million in revenue in just a few months"). Anduril override applies when consensus is wrong; here consensus is right *and* already priced. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a published benchmark or named OEM design win where Intelligence Factory's policy beats Physical Intelligence or Skild on a manipulation task that matters commercially -- with that I would re-underwrite, because at five people with no funding that would be a genuine technology-asymmetry signal worth following.
