# Auxos -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Auxos is the canonical software wrapper on a commodity capability — LLM-based persona simulation — sold into a crowded category where Aaru already carries a $1B Series A headline (TechCrunch, Dec 2025) and Evidenza is bootstrapped-profitable with Salesforce and GE among 100+ customers. The dimensional signature is unambiguous: Auxos's consequence-of-success and its technical-risk scores are both low, and the pairing tells me what kind of company this is — a credentialed YC team competently building what PyMC Labs already publishes as a free recipe and what Weavely ships as a ChatGPT prompt. That is not my deal.

On consequence magnitude, the HIGH rung is **OpenAI** (2019) where the success-state redirected the global trajectory of cognition; the MID rung is **DoorDash** (2013) where the success-state restructured consumer commerce flow; the LOW rung is the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer I pass on in thirty seconds because it improves one workflow by 20-30%. Auxos sits just above the LOW rung: Ashton Daniel's framing — "Fortune 500 clients spend a fortune for insights" — is a cost-compression pitch against 2-6 week panels with 50-100 respondents, not a thesis that collapses an incumbent regime. No category disappears if Auxos wins. Marketing teams run 10 variants instead of 2 and pay less. That is a feature, not a civilization.

On technical and capital risk that repels consensus VCs, the HIGH rung is **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where I led the $115M Series A because fusion required physics miracles no generalist would underwrite; the MID rung is **Cognition** (2024) where the stack-depth around AI software engineering at least demanded real model and tooling work; the LOW rung is any sales-summarizer wrapper. Auxos lands at the LOW rung — Kerry Lu's Amazon Ads simulation infrastructure and Jerry Wu's Meta ranking experience are real, but the dossier itself flags that PyMC Labs reports 85-95% aggregate-level accuracy and Nielsen Norman Group caps synthetic users at "hypothesis generation and early-stage testing, not final design decisions." Any Tier-1 generalist with a $1M API budget can fund this. I am over-resourced for the deal.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus, the HIGH rung is **Impossible Foods** (2011) where Pat Brown attacked animal protein production at the molecular level against a century of food-industry orthodoxy; the MID rung is **Glean**, my known exception where Arvind Jain's ex-Google-search profile was pure expert continuity but consequence magnitude bought the override; the LOW rung is the "8 years at Salesforce now applying GPT" archetype my TED Talk warned against. Auxos sits at LOW-to-MID — Daniel went from BCG consulting on customer research to selling customer research; Lu went from Amazon Ads simulation to ad simulation; Wu went from Meta ranking to ranking-style modeling. Three Team USA fencers from Columbia, Duke, and UPenn is an interesting acquaintance pattern, not a defied-expert moment. The Glean override does not apply because Auxos has neither the consequence magnitude nor the Fortune-500-information-asymmetry surface area.

On founder learning rate, the HIGH rung is **Slash** (2024) where the publicly told Kanye-pivot earned my $100M Series C; the MID rung is **Physical Intelligence** (2024), Chelsea Finn and Sergey Levine pivoting from academic robotics into a foundation-model company; the LOW rung is any first-time team executing one linear plan. Auxos has no documented pivot in the dossier — short company history, so I apply the absent-evidence default rather than penalize aggressively. On required-capital profile, the HIGH rung is **Commonwealth Fusion**'s $115M Series A, the MID rung is **Cognition**'s $175M software round, the LOW rung is commodity SaaS reaching $1M ARR on $5-10M. Auxos sits at the LOW rung — SaaS subscription, no hardware, no plant, no foundation-model training run. I write $50M checks and $115M Series A leads. Auxos needs $2M and a YC partner.

My 36-month forecast: Auxos is either acqui-hired by a marketing-research incumbent — Qualtrics, SurveyMonkey/Momentive, or User Interviews — at sub-$30M for the three fencers, or it pivots into a narrower wedge like enterprise pricing optimization and reaches $1-3M ARR while Aaru and Evidenza compound their distribution advantages. The mechanism is commoditization: the underlying capability is publicly documented, the customer-validation ceiling is set by NN/g's "hypothesis generation only" guidance, and six funded competitors are already in front of the same enterprise marketing buyer Daniel knows from BCG.

The strongest bull case: this is a Slash-pattern exception waiting to be made — YC pedigree, unusually credentialed founders (ex-Amazon Ads infrastructure modeling 40% of US e-commerce traffic is not nothing), and a category HBR validated in November 2025. The bear refutation that wins: Slash earned the exception via a documented public pivot demonstrating learning rate strong enough to override the consequence-magnitude prior, and Auxos has no equivalent thesis-update moment. Three first-jobs founders applying their first employer's playbook is the inverse of the Slash pattern. The flip condition is concrete: signed six-figure ACV LOIs from three or more Fortune 50 marketing departments before YC Demo Day, or a proprietary calibration dataset that competitors provably cannot reproduce — either would force me to reconsider. Absent that, this scores roughly where my pass-example sits.
