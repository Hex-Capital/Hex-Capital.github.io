# Klaimee -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Klaimee is what happens when an MGA operator who actually shipped a carrier license walks into a category seven other startups already share with Swiss Re paper -- a regulated-sleeve fit with consensus-category drag. Ines Boutemadja built SafetyWing's Nomad Insurance from $5M to $60M and structured a Tokio Marine MGA into its own Puerto Rico carrier, which is the kind of biography I respect on the *insurance* axis. But Klaimee is selling against Armilla AI (Swiss Re/Chaucer backing), Testudo (Lloyd's, $9.25M limits, Apollo/Atrium/QBE), Munich Re aiSure, Vouch, Embroker, Corgi, and Mayflower Specialty simultaneously, and I am not playing the game the deck wants me to play. The signature here is "ex-MGA operator running into a crowd," not "magically weird outsider before consensus."

Vigil Labs (August 2025) is the load-bearing analogue on the dimension where Klaimee scores highest -- the unsexy-regulated swing where the moat compounds because nobody else wants to do the carrier paperwork. The mechanism in Vigil and in Ines's prior SafetyWing buildout is identical: years of state-by-state regulatory grind, MGA structuring, carrier licensure -- work that does not compress with funding. Klaimee genuinely sits in that lane; AB 316 in California and the EU AI Act enforcing August 2026 are real catalysts, and the carrier retreat (>80% of state requests to exclude AI damages, per PYMNTS 2026) is exactly the kind of incumbent business-model conflict I look for. Where Klaimee diverges from Vigil is that Vigil entered a category nobody was running into; Klaimee's dossier names seven competitors already underwriting AI risk with $2M–$50M limits in 2026, and that is the divergence the rest of the evaluation has to absorb.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on -- six top funds chasing, ex-FAANG PMs, discovered the problem in a memo -- failed because the round itself was the deselection signal. That same pattern shows up in Klaimee's market, not in Klaimee's round: the *category* is consensus even if Klaimee's seed is not. Armilla raised $6.37M and disclosed Swiss Re/Chaucer capacity; Testudo disclosed Apollo/Atrium/QBE and a Lloyd's syndicate; Klaimee's dossier says "no capacity provider publicly disclosed." Procurement teams at AI deployers will read the disclosed-capacity gap as the only signal that matters, and "find them before it becomes consensus" (Yahoo Finance, March 2025) is precisely what Klaimee cannot claim when Munich Re launched aiSure in 2018 and Vouch designated an AI program in February 2024.

Founder lived-experience on Klaimee is the dimension I keep rereading. Ines built MGAs before -- that is real. Mechanism over 18 months: the regulatory infrastructure she carries from SafetyWing compounds (carrier relationships, treaty templates, state-filings muscle), and that compounding is *real* and not replicable inside a quarter by a polymath generalist. But the AI-agent-risk part -- the 8-dimension scoring across scope, exfiltration, drift, adversarial manipulation -- that part of Klaimee's biography is analytical, not autobiographical. Neither founder shipped an autonomous-agent product to scale; the AI side was assembled from market reading, not lived adjacency. The Anduril-Luckey test is half-passed by Ines on insurance and unpassed by either co-founder on agents.

Married co-founders is the governance fact that does not fit my normal "multiple founders, always a recipe for disaster" filter cleanly -- counterfactual: if Ines and Julien were *not* married, the 50/50 structural risk would push Klaimee's CEO dimension into HQ Trivia territory. Because they're married with clear CEO/CTO title delineation (Ines CEO, Julien CTO per LinkedIn and YC page), the tiebreaker is implicit in the marriage rather than the cap table, and that holds until the marriage doesn't -- which is not a risk I can underwrite from a dossier. The score would flip a tier upward if Klaimee disclosed a documented founder operating agreement with an explicit tiebreaker; absent that, I'm at the cautious end of the mid-range.

Physical-world verifiability on Klaimee parallels the Flock Safety axis inversely: Flock I sourced because I read the founder's Wi-Fi network at a Four Seasons -- I could see the cameras on poles, photograph them, deploy one. Klaimee is a "Klaimee Certified" badge, a procurement-readiness PDF, and a Calendly link to the CTO. There is no artifact a normal human installs, rides, scans, or hands to a friend. This is the dimension where Klaimee scores closest to the pass example, and the absence of a tactile artifact is itself information -- if the dossier cannot describe one, nothing tactile has shipped yet.

Forecast: 36 months out, Klaimee is acqui-hired by an established reinsurer or carrier (Munich Re, Swiss Re, or a Lloyd's syndicate) for $15M–$40M on the strength of Ines's MGA-buildout chops, after failing to win enterprise procurement against Armilla and Testudo's disclosed capacity stacks; the secondary path is a $2–5M ARR niche MGA serving mid-market AI deployers EU-side. The mechanism is straightforward: regulatory expertise is a hireable asset, AI-liability underwriting capability is a strategic asset reinsurers want in-house, and Klaimee's smallest tradeable unit is Ines's regulatory rolodex.

The bull case stings: Ines is the HandUp-style outsider-trajectory founder (first Algerian female YC founder, fwdstart.me) layered onto Vigil Labs-style regulated-category fit, and she has actually shipped the insurance-infrastructure stack the competitors are still building -- Armilla at $6.37M raised has not disclosed a Puerto Rico carrier license, and Ines has. If the Anduril analogue holds on the *insurance* axis rather than the *AI* axis, Klaimee compounds for years while the better-funded competitors burn cycles on regulatory grind. I dismantle this with one fact: Klaimee's own dossier does not name a capacity provider, and in this category capacity is the product. The verdict flips to Invest the day Klaimee names a tier-1 reinsurer on the website *and* a named enterprise design partner using the certification in production -- until then, Pass.
