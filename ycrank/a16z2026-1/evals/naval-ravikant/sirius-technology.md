# Sirius Technology -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

The crowded competitive landscape tells me almost everything I need to know about where Sirius Technology sits on the specific knowledge spectrum. Five funded competitors — LiveX AI, Subsets, Churned.io, QuadSci — plus two entrenched players in ChurnZero and Gainsight, all converging on AI-powered churn reduction. When I see that density, I ask: what does this team know that those seven organizations don't? "Escape competition through authenticity" — but the core concept of intercepting cancellation flows with AI is reproducible by any competent ML team with Stripe API access. The dossier describes Sirius Technology as having a "proprietary AI model purpose-built for retention," but every competitor in that table could write the same sentence. Proprietary models are only as durable as the specific knowledge embedded in them.

Benazir Toleubekova's claimed background is the one signal that could break through this competitive noise. Architecting Alibaba's recommendation engine serving 500M daily users — if true — represents specific knowledge in large-scale personalization systems that would take a competitor years to accumulate. That's the kind of knowledge that feels like play to you but looks like work to others. Her Momenta experience processing 1M+ sensor frames per day for autonomous driving confirms she operates at scale. But the Alibaba claim couldn't be independently verified, and I'm writing checks from my own account. LinkedIn confirms Taobao and Tencent experience, not the specific "architected the core recommendation engine" narrative. The distance between "worked at Alibaba" and "architected the recommendation engine serving 500M users" is enormous — one is a resume line, the other is specific knowledge so dense it becomes the company. I can't distinguish which is true from public evidence alone.

The leverage audit reveals a split architecture at Sirius Technology. The product side is genuinely leveraged — AI autonomously retaining 20% of churning subscribers without human intervention, processing millions of interactions daily. That's code leverage, compounding through a data flywheel. Every retained subscriber trains the model to retain the next one. But the distribution side runs on labor leverage: fifteen employees, five open job postings including an Account Executive and Customer Success Manager, enterprise sales to consumer subscription brands one deal at a time. Azamat Khamidov's team is building a leveraged product and selling it through an unleveraged motion. Compare this to Notion — which scaled to millions through word-of-mouth template sharing, no sales team required. Sirius Technology needs a human to sell each integration, and each integration depends on Stripe or Chargebee continuing to permit deep API access. That's doubly permissioned leverage — gated by both your own sales team and by platform partners who could build this natively.

The principal-agent diagnostic gives me pause. Azamat and Benazir co-founded PointAI together — an AI e-commerce support chatbot — then pivoted to subscription retention in 2025. Pre-existing co-founder relationships are a strong positive signal. But the pivot itself reads as opportunity-driven rather than obsession-driven. They didn't spend years thinking about why subscribers cancel; they built a chatbot, saw that customer interaction AI had broader applications, and identified retention as a higher-value target. That's good product sense, not principal-level fusion with the problem. A true principal in this space would have been a Head of Retention at a subscription company who couldn't sleep because the tools were so bad, then quit to build the platform themselves. Sirius Technology's founders are talented AI builders who identified a market, which is a different signal — competent agents, not possessed principals.

The bull case deserves scrutiny: $3M ARR with 200% NRR and 75% month-over-month growth is, if real, extraordinary traction that renders most of my objections academic. Growth at that rate means something is working that competitors haven't figured out. "Unicorn-status consumer companies" as customers would validate the specific knowledge hypothesis — maybe the model really is better. And the timing is right: RevenueCat's data showing AI-powered apps churn 30% faster than traditional apps means the pain is acute and growing. For Sirius Technology to justify an invest from me, I'd need to verify those numbers directly with Azamat, talk to two customers, and understand what specifically in Benazir's recommendation systems work makes their retention model outperform LiveX AI's claimed 35% improvement rate. The data flywheel could compound into something durable if the initial model advantage is real.

But I keep returning to platform dependency — Stripe could build a native "smart cancellation flow" feature tomorrow and bundle it into their billing product. Sirius Technology's entire value chain routes through billing APIs it doesn't control. This is the structural fragility that separates tools built on someone else's infrastructure from platforms that create their own leverage. The Wish analogy nags at me: marketplace leverage without underlying structural advantage evaporates when the platform moves. Sirius Technology's 15-person team at this stage, with sales and CS hires already in motion, signals a trajectory toward 50+ employees within two years — the opposite of the shrinking company thesis. Cursor hit $500M ARR with under 50 people. Sirius Technology is at $3M ARR (claimed) with 15, growing headcount alongside revenue rather than leveraging AI to keep the denominator fixed.

I pass. The specific knowledge question is unresolved — Benazir's background could be the real thing, but I can't verify the critical Alibaba claim. The leverage architecture is split between a leveraged product and unleveraged distribution. The competitive density suggests this is becoming consensus, not contrarian — and you don't get paid for being right at the same time as everyone else. The two dimensions that lock this verdict are Specific Knowledge Productization, where the most important claim is unverifiable, and Team-to-Value Leverage, where 15 employees with sales hires already in motion contradicts the extreme output-per-person profile I invest behind.
