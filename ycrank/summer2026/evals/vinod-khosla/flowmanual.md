# FlowManual -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

**FlowManual** is the platonic shape of a deal I pass on in thirty seconds: two Harvard dropouts pointing a frontier LLM at construction PDFs to flag bid-versus-contract deltas, with Cedar Ridge Mechanical as the lone named customer and a $10.62B-$17.35B construction management software TAM that already contains Trunk Tools ($70M raised) and Document Crunch ($30.5M raised) doing variants of the same thing. The dimensional signature is a company whose highest dimension still sits in the lower-mid range -- founder-defies-consensus, only because Harvard-dropout-before-matriculation is mildly anti-credential -- paired with a capital profile that is structurally wrong for me. **FlowManual** is an optimization play dressed as an AI play. The Outlook Business test -- "too important not to do" -- fails on contact.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence: the success-state was a civilizational redirection of cognitive labor, which is why I wrote $50M into a no-revenue, unconventional-structure entity. **FlowManual**'s success-state, fully maximized, is that mid-market GCs and specialty contractors spend fewer hours reconciling RFIs and submittals against contracts -- a margin improvement inside one back-office workflow at one persona. No incumbent regime collapses; Procore does not die; the $15T labor displacement frame I outlined in Fortune (2026-03-06) is not engaged because back-office document review at U.S. contractors is, generously, a low-tens-of-billions labor pool and **FlowManual** captures only the discrepancy-detection slice of it. The "color-coded margin labels, clause references, and line-item links back to exact page locations" language on flowmanual.com is the language of a feature, not a category collapse -- it is exactly the **Impossible Foods** (2011) inverse, where the consequence was displacing animal protein production, not improving alt-protein options.

The "GPT-4 wrapper on sales calls" pattern from my own pass example is the LOW rung on technical risk, and **FlowManual** sits one notch above it only because air-gapped containerized deployment with AES-256-GCM and SHA-256 audit digests requires marginally more engineering than a Chrome extension. The failure mechanism on that LOW rung is that frontier-model commoditization removes any technical moat within 12-18 months -- and the **FlowManual** dossier admits the mechanism explicitly: Document Crunch already runs on OpenAI plus Anthropic, the same model pool **FlowManual** must use. There is no specific physics, no biology, no fleet, no plant. Compare this to **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where the $115M Series A I led was justified precisely because consensus VCs could not write it -- here, any Tier-1 generalist can fund **FlowManual** with a $2M seed, which is the definition of me being over-resourced.

David Shijoon Bae and Michael Lin defying expert consensus is the strongest reading I can give **FlowManual** on dimension three, and even that reading is thin: dropping out of Harvard before the Class of 2029 matriculates is a credential-disregard signal, but the dossier shows no public contrarian position either founder has staked in construction, in AI, or in document workflows. My TED Talk skepticism is aimed at career domain experts extrapolating the past -- **FlowManual**'s founders avoid that trap by having zero construction-industry employment history, but the inverse problem applies: no domain credibility against the slow-cycle GC buyer either. This is neither the **Physical Intelligence** (2024) Levine/Finn pattern of named research PIs defying robotics orthodoxy nor the **Slash** (2024) pattern of documented public pivot.

Learning rate is the dimension where I would normally apply the absent-evidence default of 7/15, but **FlowManual** has executed one linear plan since YC S26 admission with no documented pivot, no public thesis update, and no prior company sale for either David Bae or Michael Lin to point at. The counterfactual for a tier flip is concrete: if the dossier surfaced a documented pre-YC pivot -- "we started building X for general contractors, hit a wall on Y, and rebuilt around bid-versus-contract deltas after Z" -- I would score this closer to **Slash**'s Kanye-pivot tier and override some of the consequence-magnitude deficit, the way I did when I wrote the $100M Series C into Slash despite consumer business banking failing the civilizational test. That evidence does not exist in the **FlowManual** dossier.

**QuantumScape**'s capital profile -- early-stage solid-state battery science requiring deployment depth no generalist VC would underwrite, returning >$1B over ~10 years -- is the single-anchor comparison that makes the **FlowManual** capital mismatch obvious. **FlowManual** is a two-person team running self-serve free trials with no-credit-card sign-up; the entire roadmap to $1M ARR probably fits inside $3-5M, which means I am structurally over-resourced and any seed fund can lead this. The air-gapped/on-prem distribution is interesting from a buyer-trust angle but, as the dossier notes, conflicts with a 2-person team scaling self-serve -- so the capital intensity that would justify a Khosla check (high-touch enterprise deployment, services revenue, multi-year ramp) is exactly the unit-economics conflict the founders have not resolved.

My 36-month forecast for **FlowManual**: under $1.5M ARR by mid-2029, acqui-hired into Trunk Tools or Document Crunch for talent at sub-$15M, or shut down entirely as the cross-document delta feature is shipped as a checkbox inside Procore's AI suite or Trunk Tools' next release. The mechanism producing this forecast is the one Trunk Tools' "scaled revenue 5x over past six months" (CNBC August 2025) already demonstrates -- the AI-construction-document category has a well-funded leader with a multi-year head start on data, integrations, and ENR-top-400 references, and **FlowManual** has Cedar Ridge Mechanical.

The strongest bull case against this forecast routes through my **DoorDash** (2013) known exception: a capital-light consumer/SMB business with no science risk that I funded anyway because founder ambition and category-reshaping consequence-magnitude overrode the technical-risk dimension, and which returned a $72B IPO in 2020. The **FlowManual** bull reads: GCs are a notoriously under-digitized $15B+ software market, two Harvard dropouts can move faster than Trunk Tools' Series B bureaucracy, and air-gapped deployment is a real buyer-constraint wedge against cloud-native incumbents. The bear case wins on the dossier: **DoorDash** had a stated ambition to reshape consumer commerce flows and Tony Xu had assembled the operational team to do it -- David Bae's public profile is CASC National President 2024-25 and Michael Lin's is HCS Tech for Social Good, which is student leadership, not operational compounding against Insight Partners-backed competition. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict on **FlowManual**: signed annual contracts at >$250K ACV with three named ENR-top-200 general contractors within twelve months, proving the air-gapped wedge is a durable distribution moat rather than a deployment tax.
