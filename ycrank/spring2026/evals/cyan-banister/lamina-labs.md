# Lamina Labs -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Lamina Labs is the inverse of the bet I take: I can touch the artifact at app.laminalabs.ai/simi and watch it spit out an 11-second whiteboard video, but the founders -- Kartikesh Mishra (MIT EECS BS'24/MEng'25, CSAIL drone research) and Sudip Rokaya (MIT CS, on leave) -- arrived at "explainer video for educators" the way a PM arrives at a roadmap, not the way Palmer Luckey arrived at headsets. The signature is tactile-on-top, consensus-underneath -- exactly the pattern I trained myself to walk past at YC demo days.

Anduril's Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of a parents' garage before he ever touched a defense contract -- that is the load-bearing analogy on founder-lived-experience, because the biography put him in front of the problem before the company existed. Lamina Labs's dossier gives me Mishra's MIT CSAIL paper on training drones to follow natural-language commands -- legitimately impressive ML chops -- and Rokaya's "Leaving MIT to build the next generation" LinkedIn post (activity 7438096805084495872), which is a builder's announcement, not a teacher's confession. Nothing in the dossier tells me either founder tutored kids, ran a course, sweated through Manim at 2am for a calculus class, or felt the specific pain that course creators feel. The polymath exception that rescued DeepMind's pedigreed team doesn't apply here yet -- Hassabis was a chess prodigy *and* neuroscientist *and* game designer compounding; Mishra is one researcher with a 14-star GitHub repo. The absence of a "moment of contact" with the educator pain is itself the signal.

Crusoe at the bottom of the magical-weirdness ladder is the failure mode in reverse -- I funded it because nobody else would touch flared gas plus Bitcoin plus North Dakota roughnecks, and that aesthetic revulsion was the underpricing. Lamina Labs sits at the opposite pole: Golpo AI just closed $4.1M in October 2025 from BNVT Capital, Emergence, YC itself, and Afore Capital, all targeting "education, training, and technical content" -- the identical buyer set. Add Synthesia, Pictory, Samvity AI, Mootion, and the open-source Manim ecosystem, and Lamina Labs is the sixth or seventh logo in a category that YC partners are actively syndicating. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing -- and the game everyone seems to be playing right now is AI explainer video, which means whatever game is actually worth playing is somewhere else.

Co-founder governance at Lamina Labs is the HQ Trivia question I always have to ask before signing: when Mishra and Rokaya disagree on whether to be "visual infrastructure for EdTech AI applications" (YC tagline) or "Simi, the consumer explainer video tool" (the live product), who wins? The dossier names no CEO. Two MIT classmates, both technical, no documented tiebreaker, and a scope split between API/infra and end-user app that is already visible in the public positioning. I default to 6/16 here not because they will fail this test, but because they haven't had the conversation yet, and the scope-split shows up in the surface area.

Capital-intensity is where the 18-month mechanism turns against Lamina Labs: a pure-software AI video wrapper with a "deterministic rendering" claim compounds nothing structural over time -- no regulatory moat like Vigil Labs, no hardware moat like Anduril, no energy-arbitrage moat like Crusoe, no fertility/health licensure like Superpower. The "every frame is mathematically precise" marketing line is a quality claim, not a moat. When Golpo 2.0 closes the 8-11 second latency gap -- and well-funded competitors close latency gaps -- the wedge becomes brand and distribution, neither of which a two-person team in a six-logo category can outspend.

Physical-world verifiability is the one rung where Lamina Labs almost works. I can go to app.laminalabs.ai/simi right now, paste a PDF, and watch a whiteboard video render -- that beats the dev-tools-abstraction pass example I wrote at the YC darling, and Aaron Siim's X post about "watching Lamina deliver in 11 seconds while others are still generating 15 minutes later" (status 2045493855496323248) is exactly the unsolicited third-party observation I weight. The counterfactual that would flip this dimension to a top-tier score: 100K creators sharing Simi outputs on TikTok the way HQ Trivia made me feel viral pull before any metric existed. The dossier shows one third-party tweet and two founder LinkedIn posts -- tactile, not yet viral.

Lamina Labs in 36 months: acqui-hired into Golpo AI or a Series A education incumbent like Coursera/Outschool at a modest multiple on the seed, or quietly wound down with Mishra returning to a CSAIL-adjacent ML role and Rokaya finishing MIT. The mechanism is straightforward -- the better-funded direct competitor (Golpo's $4.1M, October 2025) compounds buyer mindshare while Lamina Labs's two-person team is forced to choose between the "visual infrastructure" API thesis and the "Simi" consumer app, and either choice forfeits the other half of the market.

The HQ Trivia exception is the bull case worth steel-manning: I overrode unresolved co-founder governance (Yusupov/Kroll later feuded the company into the ground) because the tactile virality of the product was so loud I could feel it before any metric. Could Lamina Labs's 11-second whiteboard generation be that artifact? Possibly -- speed advantages in consumer creative tooling have triggered viral loops before. The dossier kills the analogy: HQ Trivia had millions of concurrent players I could literally play with by week six; Lamina Labs has one third-party testimonial (Siim) and zero retrievable follower counts on @laminalabs. Without observable viral pull, the HQ Trivia override doesn't trigger. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Lamina Labs's deterministic engine integrated as the rendering layer behind two or more named EdTech AI products with public attribution within 12 months -- that would convert the "visual infrastructure" tagline from inferred to real, and infrastructure adoption is the one path where the consensus-category objection inverts into a wedge.
