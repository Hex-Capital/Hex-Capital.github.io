# Thomas -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Human Thomas built game bots at 13 and presented at NeurIPS at 18 -- which means Thomas has a real shipper at the helm attacking a category with zero index position and a three-miracle dependency chain. That high/low pairing tells me what this is: a capable solo founder's meme-positioned demonstration project, not a workflow-infrastructure bet. Thomas is what happens when "the founder ships" runs ahead of "the market has a position to occupy."

The closest thing Thomas has to a load-bearing analog on shipping rhythm is Aravind at **Perplexity** (2022), where the mechanism was "ping on LinkedIn, show up a week later with the thing we discussed built" -- the founder's shipping cadence was itself the diligence. Thomas's dossier says Human Thomas scaled freelance work to $40k/month and then automated himself out of that role, which is a shipped artifact in the Perplexity spirit: a working autonomous-income system the founder used on themselves. The divergence from Perplexity, though, is that Aravind shipped *into a category I could index on* (search/answers in a transformer step-function), whereas Thomas's "automated my own freelance" output has no public artifact, no GitHub, no demo on madebythomas.ai per the WebFetch -- the page shows only the headline. Shipping-without-evidence is a weaker signal than Perplexity's pattern, and the persona's legal name is not even disclosed on the YC page. I'll give Thomas credit for the bio, but not Perplexity-credit.

The way I lost on this dimension before was passing on **Lyft** Series C because #2 in a winner-take-all market is just a worse version of the obvious bet -- and "no index position" is the same failure mode in different clothing. Thomas doesn't sit in the workflow path of anything; Thomas *is* one persona competing for attention against Synthesia ($333M raised, ~$100M ARR), Adept (~$415M pre-Amazon), and Manus (~$75M) plus every X account running a Claude computer-use loop. There is no rail every entrant has to touch -- if "AI founder personas" becomes a category, OpenAI or Anthropic spin up a parallel persona by Tuesday, exactly as the dossier flags. That's the Lyft pattern: no structural reason Thomas captures the upside even if the meme works.

Mechanism over 18 months on the catalyst dimension: Anthropic's computer-use (Oct 2024) and OpenAI's Operator (Jan 2025) did open a real window for sustained autonomous web work -- that's a credible 24-month catalyst in the **Stripe** 2010 / **Anduril** 2022 sense. But catalysts compound for whoever sits at the *workflow choke point* of the new capability, not whoever performs the most legible demonstration of it. Thomas converts the catalyst into a persona-and-narrative play; the dossier names no API, no SDK, no agent-orchestration layer that other builders would route through. Eighteen months from now the catalyst will have minted infrastructure companies (the Decagons and Abridges of computer-use), and Thomas will have either monetized as a media property or run out of runway.

Counterfactual for revenue velocity to flip a tier: the dossier shows "No public revenue or user counts found" and a follower count not even retrievable from @madebythomasai. My own rule from Tim Ferriss #863 is that silence on growth in AI is itself a negative signal -- real growth is easy to disclose. To flip this from a 5/25 to even a 15/25, Thomas would need to show one operated company crossing ~$50K MRR with verifiable autonomous attribution, *or* a persona follower curve compounding >30% month-over-month with engagement to match. Neither is in the dossier. Pre-seed gets a default of 8/25 from me; the AI-specific silence pushes Thomas below default.

Single-miracle parallel: **Color Genomics** (2015) worked as a bet because I structured it as workflow on commodity sequencing -- one miracle (operational throughput on existing chemistry) rather than a stack of three. Thomas requires (1) end-to-end agent reliability at sustained company operation, which the dossier itself concedes has "no public proof point at production scale as of May 2026"; (2) brand/audience capture for the persona in a market that will be saturated with AI-founder personas within twelve months; (3) corporate-signatory and liability accommodation for an AI as named operator, which U.S. and state law does not currently provide per the dossier's regulatory flag. Three independent miracles, compounded. That is exactly the math I use to pass on science projects, in a different costume.

**Forecast (36 months):** Thomas is either dead or pivoted into a narrow autonomous-agent product for a specific vertical -- most likely autonomous social-media operations for SMBs or solo creators -- running under $1M ARR with the "AI founder" framing quietly retired from the marketing. Not acquired; the persona is the brand and doesn't transfer to an acquirer's roadmap. The mechanism producing this is the three-miracle compound: even if Human Thomas ships hard, no amount of shipping resolves regulatory personhood or generates an index position that wasn't there at founding.

**Strongest objection:** the bull case is that this is shaped exactly like Perplexity's cold-inbound -- a young, technically credentialed solo founder (NeurIPS at 18, prior autonomous income system), entering on a real transformer-adjacent catalyst, with a media-property monetization path that could compound the way Mr. Beast's did, ending in an Anthropic or OpenAI acquihire at $50-150M. It stings because I *did* back Aravind on thinner evidence than this. The reason it doesn't carry: Perplexity built into a category my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy could name (search/answers), with an index position on every query a user might run. Thomas's category -- "AI founder personas" -- does not appear in *AI Market Clarity* because there isn't one, and my Known Exceptions (Anduril, Color, Mistral) all overrode my rules by being *workflow infrastructure on a hard thing*, not personas on top of a hard thing. The condition that flips my verdict: within six months, Thomas shows one autonomously-operated company crossing $50K MRR with third-party verification of autonomous operation -- that collapses the three miracles into one and I'll re-underwrite.
