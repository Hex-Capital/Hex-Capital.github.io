# PerfectBit -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

PerfectBit is a picks-and-shovels services business dressed in a verifier-grounded data thesis, built by two career Meta foundation-model leads -- Peter Vajda (11 years, Director of Media Generation, Emu/Movie Gen) and Seiji Yamamoto (9 years, Core Llama / AGI Foundations) -- selling into the ~10-20 frontier labs that are simultaneously their most capable competitors. The dimensional signature here is moderate technical depth paired with near-zero expert-defiance: PerfectBit is exactly the kind of "ex-incumbent applying the incumbent's playbook" profile my TED Talk warned about, and the consequence ceiling is enabling, not collapsing.

PerfectBit's highest-scoring dimension is Technical/Capital Risk, but the placement is modest. The HIGH rung on this ladder is **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where I led the $115M Series A into deuterium-tritium magnetic confinement physics no consensus VC would touch. The LOW rung is the sales-call summarizer from my Pass Example -- pure GPT-4 wrapper, $5M to ARR, any Tier-1 generalist could fund it. PerfectBit sits between the LOW rung and **Cognition** (Series B April 2024, $175M) on the MID rung: assembling verifier stacks across physics simulators, formal proof systems, and oracle scientific databases is real software engineering depth, but it is not a science miracle and it is not capital-hostile. A 2-person team in SF, no plant, no fab, no drug pipeline -- this is software services with domain-specialist hiring as the gating constraint, not the **QuantumScape** (IPO 2020) profile of decade-long materials patience.

PerfectBit's lowest-scoring dimension is Founder Defies Documented Expert Consensus. The HIGH rung is **Impossible Foods** (2011), where Pat Brown abandoned a tenured Stanford biochemistry career to claim the entire animal-protein production system was obsolete -- a position the food-science establishment dismissed. The LOW rung is again the sales-summarizer founder, "eight years at Salesforce, now with AI." PerfectBit sits one notch above LOW, near my **Glean** Known Exception where Arvind Jain was paradigmatic ex-Google search. But the Glean override required civilizational consequence and platform scale that PerfectBit's bespoke-dataset model does not have. Vajda and Yamamoto are not defying frontier-lab orthodoxy on training data -- they are *the* frontier-lab orthodoxy on training data, now selling it back to peers. There is no "too important not to do" framing in the dossier, no controversial public position, no instigator pattern.

Consequence Magnitude is where PerfectBit fails my entry-point test. The HIGH rung is **OpenAI** (2019) -- $50M first VC check because the success-state was civilizational. PerfectBit's success-state is "frontier labs train slightly better LLMs because their RLVR data is verifier-grounded instead of human-labeled." Even at maximum ambition, PerfectBit captures spend inside an existing $1.865B training-data market growing at 23.5% CAGR (Cognitive Market Research) -- it does not collapse Scale AI's $14B human-labeling regime, it competes with it inside the same buyer wallet. No incumbent system dies if PerfectBit wins; **Mercor** at $450M+ ARR continues, **Surge AI** at ~$1.2B 2024 revenue continues. This is consequence-capture, not consequence-creation.

Founder Learning Rate sits near the LOW rung of my **Slash** (2024) ladder, where the documented Kanye West pivot justified the $100M Series C into a YC-pedigreed fintech I would normally pass on. PerfectBit's dossier shows no prior pivot, no public thesis update, no "we were wrong about X" moment -- just two linear Meta tenures of 9 and 11 years. Required-Capital Profile sits below the MID rung anchored by **DoorDash** (2013): PerfectBit's bespoke high-touch enterprise contracts with a handful of frontier labs do not require the $50M-to-first-milestone capital depth that **Genesis AI** (July 2025, $105M seed co-led) demanded for robot foundation model training. I am over-resourced for this check.

My 36-month forecast: PerfectBit reaches $4-12M of NDA-bound revenue across 2-4 frontier-lab contracts, then gets acqui-hired by Meta, Anthropic, or Scale AI for $40-90M by mid-2029, with Vajda and Yamamoto returning to internal foundation-model roles. The mechanism is straightforward -- buyer concentration in 10-20 labs combined with those same labs having the in-house verifier-engineering capability means the math points to talent-acquisition outcomes, not category-defining ones. The 1986 McKinsey forecast of <1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 reminds me to be humble about ceilings, but McKinsey was wrong because they extrapolated a known curve; here the curve is open research (arXiv 2601.22607, opendilab/awesome-RLVR), which means the ceiling is real.

The strongest bull case borrows my **Glean** Known Exception: Vajda and Yamamoto have direct, recent buyer relationships at OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, and xAI through their Meta tenure on Llama and Emu/Movie Gen, RLVR is the dominant 2026 post-training paradigm (llm-stats.com 2026), and if PerfectBit lands two $20M multi-year contracts with frontier labs they are at $40M+ ARR before any traction is publicly visible -- the dossier silence on revenue is consistent with NDA-bound frontier-lab procurement, not absence. The case stings because I have repeatedly backed YC-pedigreed companies (the **Slash** pattern) despite my TechCrunch Disrupt 2013 rhetoric. The dossier evidence that wins against this case is the buyer-equals-competitor problem: Glean sold to Fortune 500 information workers who could not build search internally; PerfectBit sells to the exact ten companies on Earth most capable of insourcing verifier pipelines. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a publicly disclosed, multi-year, eight-figure contract with OpenAI or Anthropic naming PerfectBit as an exclusive verifier-data partner in 2+ domains -- absent that, this is a pass.
