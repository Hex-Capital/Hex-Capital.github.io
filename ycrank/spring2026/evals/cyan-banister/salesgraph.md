# Salesgraph -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Ricardo Nunez and Ruhan Ponnada built the v0 of Salesgraph as an internal business-case generator inside Mintlify when they were two of the first ten employees there, and that is the only sentence in this dossier I find load-bearing. Salesgraph is the rare pre-seed where the founders did live the problem before they pitched it -- but they lived it inside a YC-backed dev-tools company selling to enterprise revenue teams, not adjacent to flare-gas operators or VR garages, and the category they have walked into is the most-crowded AI-agent lane in the Spring 2026 batch.

The Anduril-Luckey template is what I am testing Salesgraph against on lived-experience: Palmer shipped Oculus to scale before he ever touched defense, and the prior artifact is what bought him the social license to recruit and to sell. Salesgraph's founders shipped a working internal business-case generator inside Mintlify that combined call recordings, news triggers, ROI calculator, and case studies, which is the same product they are now externalizing -- this is not a memo-discovered problem, this is a "we built it for ourselves, then we left to sell it" arc. Where Salesgraph diverges from the Anduril rung is consequence magnitude and the absence of a public artifact at scale; the predecessor was an internal tool, not a shipped product with users I can point to. That gets Salesgraph above the FAANG-PM pass-example floor but not into the Luckey-class rung.

HQ Trivia's Yusupov-Kroll collapse is the failure mechanism I read first on co-founder governance: two ex-Vine peers, no designated tiebreaker, productive virality masking an unresolvable power split that eventually ate the company. Salesgraph is a two-person team of Mintlify peers with no CEO named anywhere in the dossier, no operating-authority disclosure, equity split not disclosed -- the structural shape of the HQ Trivia setup minus the explosive product-led traction that gave me the override to write that check anyway. There is no Slack-native sales-context product in the wild today addictive enough to override the silence on tiebreaker authority.

Magically weird, this is not. The Sales Enablement Platform market is projected from $7.20B in 2026 to $25.65B by 2034 at 17.20% CAGR per Fortune Business Insights, Clari closed the Salesloft acquisition in December 2025 at ~$450M combined ARR, and Gong sits at $300M+ ARR with announced AI-agent expansion -- this is the opposite of the Crusoe profile where two credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds. Salesgraph is the consensus lane: Momentum.io, Day.ai, and the Clari+Salesloft+Gong axis are all converging on the same orchestration-layer thesis. I am not looking for the best execution in a hot category; I am looking for the founder nobody else has called yet.

Capital intensity and frontier-hardware is where Salesgraph sits closest to my pass-example floor. The product is LLM orchestration over Gong, Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, Salesloft, Attio, Grain, Granola, Circleback, Artisan, and Parallel.ai APIs -- twelve integrations is a feature list, not a moat, and the dossier's own risk section concedes Salesforce Agentforce pricing changes could compress unit economics. If AI is the age of the polymath, then the defensibility here lives in the model and the API surface area, not in Ricardo or Ruhan, and a polymath generalist could rebuild this in a quarter. Counterfactual: for Salesgraph to flip a tier on this axis, the founders would need to be writing into a regulated or aesthetically transgressive lane -- compliance-graded revenue intelligence for defense procurement, or something in the Vigil Labs class -- not a Slack overlay on the existing sales stack.

Physical-world verifiability fails the Niantic-Ingress test cleanly. I played Ingress for three years before I sat outside John Hanke's door; I rode in an Uber before I wrote the check; I scanned Flock Safety's network name at a Four Seasons before I knew the company existed. Salesgraph is an enterprise B2B Slack agent gated behind Gong and Salesforce integrations -- I cannot install it, I cannot photograph it, I cannot hand it to a friend, and no Product Hunt, Chrome Web Store, or app store presence was found. The value of Salesgraph collapses to founder testimony and a customer logo wall that does not yet exist.

My 36-month forecast for Salesgraph: acqui-hired by HubSpot, Attio, or the Clari-Salesloft entity for under $40M after the orchestration-layer whitespace closes from above, or pivoted into a Mintlify-adjacent dev-tools-specific revenue motion where Ricardo and Ruhan's actual lived domain (selling docs infrastructure into enterprise) is the moat rather than a generic sales overlay. The mechanism is straightforward -- Gong and Clari own the underlying data, Salesgraph rents it via API, and the rent goes up the moment the incumbents ship their own agent layer, which the Forrester piece already telegraphs.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Ricardo and Ruhan are the Affirm-Levchin shape if you squint -- repeat builders, real domain context from Mintlify, polymath-adjacent engineers (Bun queue benchmarks, server components) walking into a market they actually ran the back-office of. The Known Exception that almost applies is the polymath override from DeepMind -- if these two are cross-disciplinary compounders rather than category specialists, the lived-Mintlify-sales arc plus YC plus Andrew Miklas as partner is a real combination. What dismantles it: there is no evidence in the dossier of polymath range outside engineering, no GitHub repo above 100 stars, no third dimension beyond "they built it internally," and the category itself is the precise pass-example shape -- two technical co-founders, consensus AI-agent lane, no observable artifact, defensibility in the model rather than the founder. The single condition that flips my verdict: Ricardo or Ruhan ships a public artifact -- Chrome extension, free Slack bot, anything I can install and feel -- that demonstrates the context layer working in the wild within 90 days of YC Demo Day, and one of them publicly takes the CEO title with a documented founder-vesting agreement. Until then, this is a rebuttal-worthy pass, not a hard no.
