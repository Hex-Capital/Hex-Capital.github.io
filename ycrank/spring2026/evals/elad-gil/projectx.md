# ProjectX -- Elad Gil Evaluation

ProjectX is what happens when a real catalyst (computer-use agents needing sandboxed multi-VM compute) meets a dossier with zero curve to prove anyone is buying it -- $704K total raised over two rounds since Dec 2023, 14 Twitter followers on @ProjectXcloud, no revenue signal disclosed, and a "[Inferred]" tag on the business model itself. The signature pairing is *catalyst-plausible, curve-absent* -- the exact profile I learned to distrust in the 2023-24 AI cohort, where founders correctly identified that something had changed but couldn't show that anyone was paying them for it yet.

**Decagon** (2023) is the ladder rung this lives near on market catalyst -- a workflow-infrastructure layer in a category re-opened by an LLM step-function, which is precisely how ProjectX frames "when half the users of a computer are not people" (Inc42 via dossier). The shared mechanism is real: Anthropic's computer-use and OpenAI's Operator created actual demand for sandboxed, parallelizable desktop environments that hyperscalers haven't packaged as developer primitives, and ProjectX's per-app GPU isolation is at least pointed at that primitive. But Decagon entered with named enterprise design-partners closing inside 90 days; ProjectX's dossier names zero AI-lab counterparties, zero agent-framework integrations, and the only "catalyst" evidence is my own inference because the company itself frames the problem as conventional-OS-constraints rather than naming the 2024-2026 agent-runtime gap explicitly. That gap between catalyst-as-inferred and catalyst-as-named is the difference between a Decagon entry and a pass.

The "fundamentally broken" pattern from Tim Ferriss #863 -- AI companies that don't show explosive growth quickly -- is the failure mode I have to test ProjectX against on revenue velocity, and the dossier delivers it cleanly: "No public data found" on revenue, $704K cumulative across two and a half years, and a Twitter account that has gained roughly five followers per year since Oct 2022. ProjectX has had thirty months to put up a number, and the dossier instead surfaces a WTC Innovation Award at IIT Bombay Eureka! and an IIM Bangalore Launchpad affiliation -- the student-competition trail, not the buying-behavior trail. In the AI cycle, silence on growth *is* the signal because real growth is trivially easy to disclose; ProjectX's silence is doing work.

On founder shipping rhythm, the mechanism over the next 18 months is what determines whether ProjectX clears the **Perplexity** bar or sits below it. Rounak Adhikary self-funded ProjectX through Woldix consulting, which proves resourcefulness but is a services artifact, not a product artifact -- and the dossier finds *no public GitHub repos for any of the three co-founders*, which in an infrastructure company claiming per-app GPU virtualization with seconds-cold-start is the conspicuous absence. Bishal Karmakar's "systems, streaming, cloud infra" focus is the right shape on paper, but Aravind at Perplexity was sending me a built thing a week after each conversation; ProjectX's public surface area shows no equivalent shipping cadence, and the co-founder turnover between the 2022 incorporation (Adhikary, Banerjee, Das) and the YC P26 team (Adhikary, Karmakar, Majumder) compounds the read that the shipping muscle is still being assembled.

The counterfactual on index-like position is the only place ProjectX could surprise me: for the score to flip a tier on this dimension, ProjectX would need to be the runtime that *every* agent framework (LangChain, CrewAI, Anthropic SDK consumers, Operator-style apps) imports as their sandbox primitive -- the way Stripe became the rail every e-commerce SKU touched. The dossier gives me the opposite shape. ProjectX is positioned as a browser-delivered end-user OS competing in DaaS ($10.5B Gartner 2026) against Amazon WorkSpaces, Azure Virtual Desktop, Neverinstall, Kasm, and Shadow.tech -- a fight-for-share posture, not a workflow-path posture. And the agent-runtime category that *would* be the index position is already occupied by E2B and Modal, neither of whom appear in the dossier's competitive map, which tells me ProjectX may not even know which fight it's actually in.

**Color Genomics** (2015) is the single-anchor parallel for the miracle test, because Color worked by isolating one miracle (workflow on commodity sequencing) and explicitly refused the chemistry bet. ProjectX is the inverse architecture: the dossier requires per-app GPU virtualization at unit economics below incumbent VDI curves *and* agent-developer adoption *and* survival against hyperscalers who own the rails *and* a brand-disambiguation fight against multiple unrelated "ProjectX" entities on X and propfirmapp.com. That's three to four independent miracles compounding, and none of them is the "single validated capability + GTM execution" shape I funded in Decagon or Abridge.

ProjectX over 36 months: dies or pivots to a narrow agent-sandbox SDK acquired sub-$25M by a mid-tier infra player (DigitalOcean, Vercel, or a hyperscaler GPU-pool buyer following the Paperspace-$111M-cash template from Jul 2023), with the surviving asset being the per-app GPU orchestration code rather than the consumer-OS framing. The mechanism: $704K of capital plus an undisclosed YC seed doesn't fund a hyperscaler fight, the agent-runtime category will be claimed by whoever ships an SDK with public GitHub stars in 2026, and ProjectX's dossier shows no SDK, no public benchmarks, no design partners, and a competitive map that misses the actual category.

The strongest bull case is the Anduril-style override: ProjectX could be the software layer on a category re-opening (agent compute) that consensus is misreading as a DaaS commodity, and Bishal Karmakar's systems background is the Trae-Stephens-shaped insertion point for "software on top of hard infrastructure." That objection stings because the catalyst is genuinely real -- Anthropic's computer-use launch did create demand for the exact primitive ProjectX describes. But Anduril came with Palmer Luckey having shipped Oculus and Stephens having shipped Palantir's forward-deployed muscle; ProjectX's founders have shipped Woldix consulting and a ~120K MRR IT services team, which is not the shipping-artifact ledger that justifies the override. **My verdict flips to invest if ProjectX shows, in the next six months, a public agent-runtime SDK with >500 active developers and named lab customers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Mistral, or a top-five YC AI batch company) building on it** -- without that, this is the "fundamentally broken" pattern dressed in a real catalyst.
