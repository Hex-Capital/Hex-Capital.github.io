# Cerenovus -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Three Harvard sophomores — Jonathan Waldorf, Lucas Baur, and Oliver Moreland — are pitching an executive-decision knowledge graph in a category where Glean is already at $300M ARR (TechCrunch, May 2026), and the dimensional signature of Cerenovus is that its single least-bad axis is its governance default while its worst is the pure-software-wrapper posture in a consensus lane. That pairing reads, to me, as a credentialed pitch-deck company in disguise: nobody at the table has touched the problem they claim to solve, and the moat is whatever the next OpenAI model release decides it is.

The Anduril (2017) test for lived experience is Palmer Luckey shipping Oculus to scale from his parents' garage before pivoting to defense — the founder was *of* the domain. Cerenovus's founders are Harvard physics/EE undergraduates whose deepest professional artifact is a QuEra Computing internship building neutral-atom visualization tools and a Harvard Financial Analysts Club analyst seat. None of that puts Waldorf, Baur, or Moreland in front of an executive's broken vendor handoff or a stalled team restructuring; they discovered the problem the way the FAANG-PM pass-example founders did — in a memo, not in a job. The "Harvard Dropout | Cerenovus" LinkedIn headline is the tell. That is exactly the "Stanford or Harvard, Goldman Sachs or McKinsey" pattern I avoid (Hustle Fund profile, 2024), and Cerenovus offers no offsetting autobiographical contact with the C-suite buyer.

Cerenovus competes head-on with Glean ($765M raised, $300M ARR), Notion AI, Guru's MCP launch (March 2026), Microsoft Copilot, and Slack AI — the failure mechanism of the consensus YC AI-agent darling in my pass example was that six top funds were already chasing and the moat collapsed to the model itself. Cerenovus walks straight into that mechanism: the YC page differentiation is a "markdown knowledge graph with native AI-agent integration," which is precisely what a polymath generalist could rebuild in a quarter once Anthropic or OpenAI ships the next agent primitive. Cerenovus is not magically weird; it is the most-pattern-matched enterprise AI category of S26.

Three co-founders, no named CEO in the YC dossier, no documented tiebreaker — Cerenovus inherits the structural risk profile the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll feud later validated. The dossier offers no operating agreement, no founder-vesting protocol, no equity split. With three Harvard classmates of identical career stage and identical pedigree, the "who is the tiebreaker" question has not been asked because nobody has had the conversation yet. I'm applying the absent-evidence default of 6/16 and shading down because three undifferentiated peers is harder than two.

Cerenovus's "ingest documents, PDFs, emails, Slack messages, spreadsheets, meeting notes" is the textbook software-only swing in my Pass Example — over 18 months, this dimension compounds adversely as the LLM substrate commoditizes the ingestion layer and Glean's $300M ARR distribution chokes off enterprise procurement budgets. There is no hardware, no regulatory perimeter, no fertility/defense/energy edge where time builds moat. The J&J MedTech "Cerenovus" neurovascular brand collision is the most frontier-adjacent thing in this dossier, and that is a trademark headache, not a moat.

For Cerenovus to clear physical-world verifiability, a normal human would need to install, photograph, ride, or scan something inside 48 hours — the Niantic-Ingress test where I played the product in 2012 before the Pokémon Go check landed. Cerenovus's product is an enterprise knowledge graph evaluated by an executive over a pilot cycle; the YC page snippet says "pilot customers are live" but names none, ships no Product Hunt artifact, no public Twitter, no observable thing I can touch from a Four Seasons café. Counterfactual: one signed LOI from a named Fortune 500 buyer would move this score up a tier, but the dossier provides zero such anchor.

My 36-month forecast on Cerenovus is dead or quiet-acqui-hired into a larger enterprise-search incumbent (Notion, Guru, or a horizontal LLM vendor) below $25M outcome, with the three Harvard founders dispersing back to graduate programs or other startups by 2029. The mechanism is straightforward: Glean's $300M ARR distribution already won the C-suite procurement motion, the moat reduces to the model layer, and three undergraduate co-founders without a CEO tiebreaker cannot out-execute a category leader with $765M in capital.

The DeepMind exception is the strongest bull case against my pass on Cerenovus — Hassabis, Suleyman, and Legg were elite-pedigreed and yet won because polymath cross-domain compounding overrode my anti-credential filter. Could Waldorf's quantum-algorithms work plus Baur's finance background plus a third polymath profile be the same compounding bet? No. Hassabis was a chess prodigy, neuroscientist, and game designer with two decades of cross-domain mastery before DeepMind; Cerenovus's founders are sophomores whose deepest non-academic credential is an internship. The polymath exception requires *compounded* range, not *advertised* range. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed enterprise LOI from a named Fortune 500 CFO or COO, plus one founder publicly demonstrating five-plus years of lived operations experience inside the executive-decision workflow Cerenovus claims to model.
