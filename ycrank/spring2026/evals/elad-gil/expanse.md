# Expanse -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Expanse is a research-pedigree pre-seed with one shipped artifact -- Ismaeel Bashir's EPCC multimodal HPC predictor that "beat every published baseline" -- pointed at a customer segment (national supercomputing centres and quant funds) where revenue velocity will be structurally invisible for 18 months whether the product works or not. That's the signature: highest on founder shipping, lowest on the revenue curve, and the gap between those two is the whole investment question.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for Expanse's catalyst test, and it's the one Expanse partially earns. Defense pre-Ukraine was a market peers misread as dead until a specific 24-month window cracked it open; Expanse's equivalent is the Dell'Oro "from scale to optimization" framing at GTC 2026 layered on $320B of FY2025 hyperscaler capex (evolvancemarketresearch.com) -- enterprises and labs being forced to harvest utilization from existing GPU fleets rather than buying more. That is a real recent catalyst, not TAM math. Where Expanse diverges from the Anduril template: Anduril's catalyst hit a $700B procurement budget where one signed contract is nine figures, whereas Expanse's catalyst hits a 37%-utilization 32-GPU research cluster -- the same physics, two orders of magnitude less budget per logo.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure pattern I'm scanning Expanse against on revenue velocity, and the absence stings: I passed Lyft because the dossier could not show share inversion, and Expanse's dossier shows zero disclosed revenue, no pricing page on expanse.sh, no customer logos, and a single illustrative 32-GPU example that may or may not be a paying account. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863) -- and while Expanse can fairly claim its buyers (EPCC-class HPC centres, G-Research-class quant funds) procure in 9-18 month cycles, that defense is itself the problem. Slow-procurement customers don't give you the Harvey-style $3B?$5B?$8B curve that distinguishes "just working" from "false signal," they give you 18 months of pilot tourism indistinguishable from traction.

Bashir's EPCC predictor is the closest Expanse comes to the **Perplexity** (2022) template -- not the Aravind cold-LinkedIn-ping cadence, but the analogous artifact-before-pitch signal: a research-grade multimodal predictor that exists, with a free adjacent tool (the OS Wastage Scanner at wastage.expanse.sh) deployed as a wedge. Mechanism over 18 months: if Bashir keeps shipping at EPCC-research velocity, the OS Wastage Scanner becomes a top-of-funnel that converts to paid clusters and the data network effect compounds; if shipping decelerates because three of four founders (Bieniek, Melake, Mendi) have thin public artifact footprints and the load sits entirely on Bashir, the moat narrows to one person's research output. The dossier cannot tell me which yet, and that's a genuine information gap, not a default.

Expanse's index-position claim is where the **Stripe** (2010) "index on e-commerce" parallel breaks down on a specific dossier line: Expanse sits on top of Slurm and Kubernetes, which is the right architectural posture -- workflow infrastructure on top of a hard thing, the Color/Applied-Intuition pattern from my Unstated Patterns -- but the underlying category (on-prem HPC clusters at national labs and quant funds) is not Stripe's compounding e-commerce; it's a finite installed base where NVIDIA Run:ai post-acquisition is bundled into the GPU stack itself. To flip this dimension a tier, Expanse would need to show the OS Wastage Scanner converting cloud GPU buyers, not just on-prem -- because cloud GPU spend is the curve that compounds, on-prem HPC is the curve that's already mostly built.

Single-miracle architecture is where I give Expanse the benefit of the doubt by the **Color Genomics** (2015) precedent: the miracle is "multimodal ML predicts HPC job resource fit at submission" and the EPCC research suggests that capability is validated, leaving GTM and product execution as the remaining work. That's a defensible one-miracle frame. The wrinkle: Expanse also needs the predictor to generalize across heterogeneous Slurm/K8s fleets in production -- which is closer to 1.3 miracles than 1.0 -- and needs to beat Run:ai's distribution before NVIDIA extends Run:ai into pre-submission prediction, which is a market-timing miracle stacked on top. Not multi-miracle in the fusion-or-fission sense, but not as clean as Color's commodity-sequencing-plus-workflow either.

36-month forecast: Expanse reaches $1.5-4M ARR across 6-12 HPC and quant logos, then gets acqui-hired by NVIDIA (rolled into Run:ai) or Rafay at $40-90M as a feature/team tuck-in -- the slow-procurement segment caps breakout velocity, and the predictor IP is exactly the kind of feature an incumbent GPU orchestration platform absorbs rather than competes against. The mechanism: Bashir's research artifact is real enough to acquire but the buyer set is too narrow and slow to compound into an independent durable company before Run:ai's bundled distribution closes the window.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the Anduril Known Exception applied to GPU infrastructure: Bashir + Mendi (Imperial College London) + Melake (G-Research) + Bieniek look structurally like Stephens + Luckey in miniature -- domain operators who shipped at the institutions Expanse now sells into, attacking a market peers are mispricing as a feature inside Run:ai. The Dell'Oro "scale to optimization" framing is a real 24-month catalyst, the workflow-infrastructure-on-hard-thing pattern is one of my Unstated Patterns, and "wide open" GPU optimization is exactly the kind of sub-market my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy flags as not-yet-won. The dossier evidence that wins against this case is the silence: no Product Hunt, no press, no disclosed customer logos, no revenue figure, no GitHub footprint for three of four founders -- "in the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose." One verifiable condition flips my verdict: three signed enterprise logos at $200K+ ACV within the next 6 months, with at least one outside the EPCC alumni network. Until then, Expanse is a watch-list company, not a check.
