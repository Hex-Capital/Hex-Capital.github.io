**Lyft** Series C is the rung I pin Bloom against on revenue velocity, because the failure mechanism there was a dossier full of operational signal with no math that resolved winner-take-all share dynamics — and I passed, correctly per framework, on absence of curve. Bloom's dossier shows the same pattern in miniature: 1.4M views and 50K images generated in launch week is a top-of-funnel artifact, not a paid-conversion artifact, and the $90/month Scale plan is priced but not reported as booked ARR anywhere in the dossier. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863) — Bloom may well be growing, but the dossier's silence on paying-customer count, on retention, on whether the 14K users converted at all, is itself the negative signal in this cycle.

Market re-opening — mechanism analysis. MCP rollout across Anthropic, OpenAI, Cursor, and Codex through 2025 is a *real* 24-month catalyst, not invented; Bloom's 8-platform MCP integration footprint (Claude Desktop, ChatGPT, Claude Code, OpenCode, Cursor, VS Code, OpenAI Codex, Manus) is the index-position consequence of that catalyst. Where this compounds over 18 months: every new agent surface that ships MCP support increases Bloom's callable distribution without Bloom shipping code. Where it fails: Canva AI 2.0 Brand Intelligence (launched April 2026, per Flatline Agency) is the platform-substitution clock — Canva already has the brand assets sitting in user accounts, so if Canva ships an MCP endpoint against its own corpus, Bloom's ingestion step is bypassed entirely.

Index position — counterfactual. For Bloom's score on this axis to flip from a "workflow path" 10 to a Stripe-grade 13+, the dossier would need to show that an agent calling Bloom is the *default* path rather than a *competing* path — i.e., a named partnership with Anthropic or OpenAI surfacing Bloom in Claude's first-party tool catalog, or a Vercel-distribution wedge (Rauch is on the cap table) where every v0-generated site pings Bloom for brand context. Absent that, Bloom is one of several callable brand endpoints and must win share against Canva's incumbent asset gravity.

Single-miracle architecture — single-anchor parallel to **Decagon** (2023). Decagon's miracle was one validated capability (LLM accuracy at customer-support resolution) plus GTM execution, which is why I funded it as workflow infrastructure in an open AI sub-market (*AI Market Clarity*, Nov 2025). Bloom's miracle reduces identically: assume agents will increasingly need callable brand context as MCP usage compounds, and the remaining work is product + distribution. That is a clean single-miracle structure — not fusion, not new chemistry — and it's the dimension where Bloom most clearly clears my pass-bar.

Forecast over 36 months: Bloom is acqui-hired by Canva, Adobe, or Figma for $30-80M as their MCP brand endpoint, or it grinds to sub-$5M ARR as a prosumer tool and the team rolls to a more defensible adjacency in agent-callable creative ops — the dominant outcome is platform absorption, not independent breakout. The mechanism is straightforward: ingestion is commoditized (Ad Legends offers it free), the runtime moat depends on retention data Bloom hasn't disclosed, and the platforms with the brand assets already in their database (Canva, Adobe, Figma) will close the MCP loop themselves rather than route through an independent layer.

The opposing case worth steelmanning: MCP becomes the protocol for agent context the way HTTP became the protocol for web content, and Bloom — first-mover on the brand-context vertical, integrated across 8 surfaces, backed by Rauch who owns the Vercel/v0 generative-frontend distribution — becomes the Stripe of brand-callable context, an index on every agent-generated creative output as the category compounds. The bear-side dismantle: Canva AI 2.0 (April 2026) is the platform-substitution proof that incumbents will close the brand-asset-to-agent loop themselves, and Bloom's principal moat is integration distribution rather than proprietary data — the assets ingested into Bloom are the same assets sitting in Canva, Figma, and Google Drive accounts, so the switching cost is one MCP endpoint deep, not data-graph deep. Flip condition I'd actually act on: Bloom shows $500K+ ARR with at least three named enterprise logos (mid-market brand teams, not prosumer designers) within 6 months, and an exclusive MCP-surfacing partnership with Anthropic or Vercel — at that point this is a Decagon-template check, not a Neutral.
