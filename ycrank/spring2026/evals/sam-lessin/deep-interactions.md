# Deep Interactions -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Deep Interactions is a credentialed Oxford DPhil founder doing genuine Human-AI Interaction research who has force-fit her actual asset into the single most consensus-priced category in the YC Spring 2026 batch — the vibe-coding builder wedge that Lovable just raised $330M into at a $6.6B valuation in December 2025. Sruthi Viswanathan is the most interesting thing in this dossier, and the company she's built around her is the least interesting thing in this dossier, and that gap is the entire evaluation.

Deep Interactions on the price-discipline ladder sits one rung above the canonical Pass Example I keep in my head. Solana at $0.05 in 2018 is the HIGH rung — everyone had quit crypto, the round priced like nobody wanted it, and that was the entire alpha. Sublime Security sits at MID — pre-consensus enterprise security at a moment when other security investors were still chasing late-stage SOC SaaS. The LOW rung is the canonical 2026 YC AI-agent pitch I described in my Pass Example: "AI agent for [vertical], $30M post, four tier-1s in." Deep Interactions sits between MID and LOW, leaning LOW: the company is positioning explicitly against Lovable ($400M ARR by Feb 2026 per Sacra), Bolt.new, v0.app (rebranded Jan 2026), and Replit Agent — the four most-watched names in the hottest software category of the batch. Nothing in the dossier tells me the round is below batch median or that a tier-1 visibly passed. Silence on round dynamics in a category this hot reads as consensus.

Category sleeve fit is where Deep Interactions sits lowest, and this is the one that decides the check. The HIGH rung is Solana / Aleo — pre-consensus crypto with real on-chain economic activity, exactly the sleeve I've explicitly said is "fundamentally disruptive" while AI is "just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024). The MID rung is Sublime Security in the enterprise-security sleeve — recent, not yet consensus, defensible category logic. The LOW rung is the generic AI-agent-shell wrapper on top of a foundation model with no category sleeve fit. Deep Interactions sits squarely at LOW: an "AI builder" wedge against Lovable / Bolt / v0 / Replit Agent is the most-internet'd corner of the most-internet'd sleeve, and the differentiator the dossier offers — "context layer that operates within team tools rather than as a standalone code generator" — is exactly the kind of feature surface a $6.6B-valued Lovable can ship as a quarterly roadmap item.

Founder conviction is where Deep Interactions actually punches above. Drop.io is my HIGH rung — I bootstrapped a file-sharing primitive against free incumbents because I had a non-obvious belief about consumer file workflows. Sublime Security's founders are the MID — credible domain operators with category opinions. The LOW rung is the sanitized strategic-consulting deck with no public writing. Sruthi Viswanathan is between MID and HIGH: a published DPhil at Oxford specifically in Human-AI Interaction with a Google Scholar profile, prior research at Naver Labs Europe and Limbic AI, and a UX research career that maps directly onto "humans-in-the-loop" as a thesis rather than a marketing word. That's real public artifact of conviction — academic publications are writing, and writing is thinking. The problem is she's pointed that conviction at a category where the academic insight cannot escape the capital asymmetry of Lovable's $352.5M war chest.

Capital structure for Deep Interactions reads as Teamshares' opposite. Teamshares is the HIGH rung — an unusual employee-ownership rollup other VCs cannot underwrite. Ro is MID — high-burn DTC pharma where regulatory complexity made the capital intensity the moat (a Known Exception I've made before). The LOW rung is the generic SaaS cap table needing five more rounds to reach default-alive. Deep Interactions sits between LOW and MID: the YC description ("you give us a problem and we ship a working AI product in the same afternoon") with a team size of 4 and 10 design partners reads as a services-weighted delivery model wearing a SaaS hat. That could be a feature — a manual Wizard-of-Oz operation before automating, which is a signal I look for. But there's no instrument innovation here, no revenue-share, no holdco — just a presumed standard YC SAFE into a category dominated by an incumbent with 1000x the capital.

Founder-as-brand is the cleanest LOW on the dossier. John Fish / Bookshelved (January 2024) is the HIGH rung — a creator with owned literary distribution before the check. Marina Mogilko sat at HIGH on a different mechanism — millions of YouTube subscribers feeding LinguaTrip. The LOW rung is paid-acquisition-dependent with no community footprint. Deep Interactions is LOW: LinkedIn headline reads "Stealth Startup," no Product Hunt launch, no press, follower counts not even retrievable, sole inbound funnel is a Calendly link and a Typeform waitlist. The "100 Women in AI" listing is a credential, not distribution.

Deep Interactions in 36 months either (a) pivots to a HAI-methodology consulting / services business around Sruthi landing at $2–5M services revenue and quietly off the venture path, or (b) gets acquihired by a Vercel / Replit / Lovable for the team at sub-$25M, before any meaningful Series A is achievable against the capital asymmetry. The mechanism is straightforward: the differentiated work in Deep Interactions is the workflow-design methodology, which is observable to competitors with 1000x the engineering capacity, and the customer concentration (Jack Fertility, Canopie, FitMatrix — all early-stage consumer-health startups) is too narrow to compound into the enterprise GTM that would justify Series A pricing against Lovable comps.

The strongest objection: Lovable, Bolt, v0, and Replit are all single-user self-serve vibe-coders, and the team-collaboration enterprise wedge is genuinely uncovered surface. Sruthi's actual Oxford HAI research could produce a real interaction-design advantage that the capital-rich incumbents — staffed with frontier-model engineers, not HCI researchers — structurally cannot replicate; 10 customers and "10,000+ usage instances" at pre-seed is not nothing. That bull case stings. It loses to two facts in the dossier: first, the "context layer" framing is exactly the kind of feature surface Lovable can roadmap once enterprise demand materializes, and second, neither Sruthi nor Beni has any disclosed enterprise GTM background, which is the actual missing input — the HAI moat doesn't reach customers without it. One verifiable condition flips me: if Deep Interactions is raising on a non-standard instrument (services-equity, revenue share against delivery contracts) at a sub-batch-median cap, OR if Sruthi publishes an opinionated long-form piece arguing why single-user vibe-coding structurally fails inside teams with named-customer retention data attached, I'll take a meeting. Without either, Deep Interactions is the Pass Example, and I wrote that example before I read this dossier.
