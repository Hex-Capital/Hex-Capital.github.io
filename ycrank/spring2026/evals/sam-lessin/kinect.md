# Kinect -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Alhena AI raised $4.9M in its seed round led by Slow Ventures. That's my fund. We already placed our bet in the AI e-commerce conversation layer, and we placed it on a team with enterprise logos like Tatcha and Unilever and a hallucination-free guarantee that matters when you're handling purchase decisions. Kinect is entering a category where I've already seen the competitive dynamics up close and picked a horse. That's not automatically disqualifying — sometimes the second entrant wins — but it means I can't pretend this is some undiscovered frontier. AI shopping assistants are consensus. OpenAI launched one. Perplexity launched one. Rep AI has $8.2M+ and 500 merchants. Tolstoy has 2,000+ brand partners. This is the exact opposite of a mispriced contrarian bet — every sophisticated investor in e-commerce infrastructure is already staring at this category and writing checks. The risk is not "in the price" here. The hype is in the price.

Kinect's pitch is that combining conversational AI with adaptive page rendering — dynamically changing hero images, copy, and recommendations based on visitor context — creates something categorically different from chat-only competitors like Rep AI or Alhena. I'll grant that's a real product idea. But it is not a new primitive. Behavioral targeting and dynamic landing pages have existed since Optimizely in 2010. LLMs make the conversation layer better, but Kinect is not creating a new behavior that didn't exist before — nobody is going to "Kinect me" the way they "Venmo me." When I backed Venmo, peer-to-peer mobile payments literally did not exist as a consumer behavior. Kinect is a better mousetrap in a crowded category of mousetraps. The dossier itself states it plainly: "The core components — LLM-powered chat, behavioral targeting, and dynamic page rendering — are individually commoditized. An experienced team could replicate the feature set in months."

Kratik Agrawal and Varun Kandula are competent engineers — Google Commerce, Anduril, Verkada, MongoDB, and notably both co-led conversational intelligence at Reevo, which raised $80M. Varun's consulting work sourcing $200K+ contracted ARR across brands like Sephora and Ralph Lauren shows he understands the buyer. These are capable people who know this domain. But capable people building a sensible product in a validated market is precisely the combination that doesn't excite me. The sane-insane ratio is inverted: sane founders, sane idea. Nothing about "AI-powered personalized storefronts for Shopify merchants" makes anyone say "that's fucking crazy." It makes them say "yeah, that makes sense," which is the death knell for asymmetric returns. The idea needs to be insane. Kinect's idea is obvious.

Kinect fails the AI kill test more completely than almost any company I've looked at this batch. Shopify — Kinect's primary platform dependency — already runs ChatGPT-powered shopping assistance through Shop.app. The dossier flags this directly: "Shopify could integrate similar functionality natively if the category proves valuable." And Shopify will, because this is exactly the kind of feature that accrues to the platform. Every time you get pitched a Shopify plugin that makes the merchant's page smarter, you're like, this is perfect for Shopify to build themselves. Pure software, no physical assets, no protocol-level infrastructure, no network effects that strengthen with scale. Metropolis works as an investment because you can't replicate parking garages with an API call. Kinect's entire product can be replicated by Shopify's product team in a quarter.

The bull case I'd construct for an advocate: Kinect's "adaptive storefronts" angle — modifying the actual page, not just running a chatbot — is genuinely underexplored. The intelligence dashboard surfacing aggregated shopper questions and comparison patterns could become a data product that's more valuable than the conversion tool itself. The founders' shared Reevo history means this isn't a synthetic co-founder match — they've built conversational AI together before. And the timing is arguably perfect: LLM inference costs collapsed, consumer AI shopping behavior is being trained by OpenAI and Perplexity, and the tool-of-record for Shopify merchants in this category hasn't been decided yet. If Kinect could prove 200+ basis points of conversion lift with verified case studies and lock in 1,000+ merchants before Shopify builds natively, there's a real business here. But that conditional — "before Shopify builds natively" — is a race against a platform that has every incentive and capability to run it. I'd flip to Invest if Kinect showed me $50K+ MRR with verified, named-merchant case studies demonstrating conversion lift that competitors can't match, and a distribution channel that doesn't depend entirely on Shopify's App Store. Until then, this is a feature looking for permission to exist as a company in a category my own fund has already priced.
