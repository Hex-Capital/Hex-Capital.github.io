# Bylaw -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Bylaw is a cleanly-isolated single-miracle architecture wrapped around three Waterloo undergrads with zero shipped artifacts -- a thesis-shaped product in search of a founder who can build it. The architectural call (deterministic policy engine, LLM compiles but doesn't decide, signed audit logs) is the kind of restraint I respect; the team page (Mazin Al-Ani, Farhan Ur Rehman, Gurshabd Singh Varaich -- internships at Optiver, Meta, BitGo, theScore, Ford) is a résumé, not a track record. That's the signature: high on miracle-isolation, low on shipping evidence, and that pairing almost never resolves into a defining company.

On founder execution rhythm, my template is **Perplexity** (2022) -- Aravind would ping me on LinkedIn and "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). The dossier on Bylaw inverts that template line-by-line: no GitHub repos definitively attributed to Mazin Al-Ani, no public account for Farhan Ur Rehman, Gurshabd Singh Varaich's @gurshabdv has "no notable starred repo identified." Three CS undergrads who met in first year at Waterloo with internships at trading firms and FAANG is a pedigree dossier, and the Hustle Commons summary of my framework is explicit -- "has this founder actually shipped products before, not just at a big company, but independently?" Bylaw's founder section answers that with silence, and silence on shipping at pre-seed is the only legible negative signal I get.

The market re-opening question is where Bylaw is most overrated by the dossier itself. EU AI Act high-risk obligations applying Aug 2, 2026 and Galileo's Agent Control OSS launch in March 2026 are real, but they're not the **Anduril** (2022) post-Ukraine moment -- they're a slowly arriving compliance tailwind that 200 other YC companies are also riding. Compounded over 18 months, this dimension fails the way **Lyft** Series C failed for me: the catalyst exists, but it's catalyzing a *crowded* market with named, funded incumbents (Galileo at $68M raised with 834% YoY growth and six Fortune 50 customers, Lakera acquired by Check Point for ~$300M in Nov 2025, Prompt Security at $23M raised) rather than re-opening a dead one. Bylaw needs the catalyst to *expand the pie faster than incumbents absorb the primitive* -- the dossier provides no evidence that gap exists.

Revenue velocity is straightforward to score: there isn't any disclosed. No customers, no logos, no pilots, no pricing page on usebylaw.com, no waitlist (WebFetch, Jun 2026), Hiring: False on the YC page. For score to flip a tier here, Bylaw would need to publish even one named design partner -- a Braintrust customer running their SDK on production write-actions with a disclosed false-block rate. The counterfactual is generous: I would take *any* deployed-customer signal and re-rate, because the architecture is at least coherent. But "AI company with no growth curve disclosed" is verbatim from my own red-flag list, and "something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss #863, April 2026) was my own phrasing -- I don't get to suspend it for Bylaw.

Index position is where Bylaw's pitch wants to feel like **Stripe** (2010) -- a layer every agent must touch before writing. The structure doesn't hold. Stripe sat between merchants and card networks where there was no credible substitute primitive. Bylaw sits between agent runtimes and LangSmith/Langfuse/OpenAI/Braintrust -- and those integration partners are *exactly* the platforms most likely to ship the evidence-verification primitive natively. Galileo already did, in March 2026, Apache 2.0, with AWS, CrewAI and Glean as launch partners (VentureBeat, Mar 2026). Bylaw isn't an index on agent-runtime safety; it's a thin SDK competing with an OSS control plane backed by $68M and Fortune 50 distribution, which is the structural shape of #2 in a category where #1 is already shipping for free.

Single-miracle architecture is genuinely where Bylaw earns its highest marks. The "deterministic policy engine, LLM assists compilation only" framing isolates the bet to one technical claim: can you reconstruct an evidence manifest across five sources at sub-write-action latency without unacceptable false-block rates? That's one miracle, not three -- the **Color Genomics** (2015) template of workflow on top of a commodity capability, not a new chemistry bet. No benchmark, latency budget, or false-block rate is published, which is the gap, but the architectural restraint is real and I credit it.

Forecast, 36 months: Bylaw is acqui-hired sub-$10M by Galileo, Braintrust, or LangChain by Q3 2028, or it's dead, with runtime evidence gates shipped as a default feature inside Galileo Agent Control before Bylaw clears $1M ARR. The mechanism is straightforward -- the highest-rated dimension (clean single-miracle architecture) and the lowest-rated dimension (no shipping evidence from the founders) compound into a company that has *correctly identified* the right primitive but loses the race to ship it against a vendor with distribution, capital, and an OSS wedge already in market.

The bull case that stings: Bylaw is **Stripe** vs. Braintree -- the focused new entrant with the cleaner SDK beats the better-funded incumbent because three hungry Waterloo CS undergrads ship faster than a $68M-raised committee chasing Fortune 50 logos, and the EU AI Act Aug 2026 deadline forces every enterprise into a verification layer that Galileo's open-source Agent Control is too generic to fill. I take this case seriously because it's a real pattern in my portfolio. It loses on one fact: Patrick Collison had built and sold Auctomatic before Stripe, and Aravind had shipped at OpenAI before Perplexity -- the Stripe-vs-Braintree template requires founders with prior independent shipping, and Mazin Al-Ani, Farhan Ur Rehman, and Gurshabd Singh Varaich have internships, not artifacts. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Bylaw publishes a named production design partner (e.g., a public Decagon, Sierra, or Cresta integration) with disclosed sub-100ms latency and a real false-block rate within 90 days -- that's the *only* evidence that would override the founder-shipping gap. Until then I pass, and I'd revisit at the A if Galileo hasn't already eaten the layer.
