# Wealor -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Wealor sits at an awkward intersection: a market that genuinely re-opened in Q1 2026 (Anthropic's RIA plug-ins, Orion's Denali AI in February, Addepar's Addison in March -- all named in the Wealthmanagement.com April 2026 piece) paired with a dossier that contains zero disclosed customers, zero revenue, and a "Team Size: 0" entry on the YC page. The signature of Wealor is *real catalyst, no evidence of traction into the catalyst's window* -- exactly the profile that gets harvested by incumbents who already own the desk.

On catalyst, the load-bearing analogy is **Anduril** (Series D, 2022). Anduril worked because Ukraine made Pentagon software inadequacy a 24-month re-opening, and Trae Stephens had already built Palantir's forward-deployed muscle to convert the window. Wealor's catalyst is structurally real -- Anthropic RIA plug-ins plus two incumbent native-AI launches inside ninety days is a legitimate step-function, not a TAM-deck fantasy -- and Wealor's "forward deployed engineers for RIAs and family offices" positioning is consciously cribbed from the Palantir/Anduril playbook. Where Wealor diverges from the Anduril rung is the *direction* of the catalyst: Ukraine forced procurement *outside* Lockheed, whereas Orion ($5.8T AUM, 17 of top 20 Barron's RIAs) and Addepar ($9T across 1,400+ firms) shipping native AI *consolidates* the workflow inside the platforms RIAs already pay for. That's a catalyst that benefits the incumbents Wealor must displace, not an opening that runs around them.

On revenue velocity, the failure mechanism comes from **Lyft** Series C and every "Harvey-clone at consensus price" pitch I've passed on in the last eighteen months: when the dossier cannot show me dollars or pilots, the default assumption in an AI cycle is that there are none, because real growth is trivially easy to disclose. Wealor's dossier states "No public revenue, customer count, or AUM data found as of May 2026," no Product Hunt launch, no named pilot RIA, no LOI. Pre-seed calibration buys some grace here, but Jump just closed an $80M Series B on revenue Insight Partners was willing to underwrite and Zocks closed $45M from Lightspeed/QED -- the bar for "just working" in advisor AI is already being set inside the same six-month window Wealor is being raised in, and Wealor is on the wrong side of that bar.

Karim Bouri and Pierre Depuydt have the credential stack -- ENSAE, Polytechnique, HEC, Berkeley, CANAL+ ML, BCG, Tiffany finance -- but the dossier names zero shipped products from either of them. Over 18 months, the founder-shipping dimension compounds asymmetrically: a Karim who pushes weekly artifacts to design-partner RIAs builds the integration depth that becomes Wealor's only durable moat, while a Karim who leans on the consulting-and-credentials archetype loses the design-partner race to Jump and Zocks teams that have already shipped note-takers used by thousands of advisors. The dossier gives me no GitHub, no prior independent product, no "shipped a thing between our calls" pattern -- which is the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind-pinged-me-on-LinkedIn signature I actively hunt for. Karim's direct buyer-side experience selling financial products to wealth managers is real founder-market fit on the *sales* axis; it is not evidence of execution rhythm on the product axis.

The counterfactual for Wealor's index position is sharp: for the score on dimension 4 to flip a tier, Wealor would need to be the integration layer that Orion and Addepar's customers reach for *across* both platforms -- the way Stripe became the layer every e-commerce platform integrated against. The dossier's "embeds with wealth management firms to fix workflow bottlenecks between their tools" positioning is the right shape for that bet, and a horizontal layer across a fragmented RIA stack is genuinely index-like if it lands. But Altruist's Hazel at $60/seat/month (built on the YC-backed Thyme acquisition from September 2025) is custodian-bundled, which means the distribution path Wealor needs to win is being *priced out from underneath* by a custodian giving AI away as a feature. Index positions don't survive incumbents pricing the layer at zero.

Compared to **Color Genomics** (co-founded 2015) on the single-miracle axis -- which I architected explicitly as workflow on top of commodity sequencing so the miracle was data/workflow rather than chemistry -- Wealor's miracle stack is uncomfortably long: (1) LLMs are accurate enough on advisor workflows, (2) RIAs will buy a horizontal layer rather than accept Orion/Addepar native AI, (3) Wealor outships Jump's $105M and Zocks' $65M war chests on a pre-seed budget, (4) SEC fiduciary/recordkeeping posture under the Investment Advisers Act doesn't blow up the GTM. Miracle (1) is done. Miracles (2)-(4) are independent, and compounding three independent probabilities is the pattern I pass on.

My 36-month forecast on Wealor: acqui-hired by Altruist, Orion, or a mid-tier RIA tech vendor for under $30M with sub-$2M ARR, or dead. The mechanism is straightforward -- Wealor's catalyst is real but the catalyst *favors* the platforms Wealor is trying to sit on top of, and the founder profile lacks the shipping-rhythm signal that would let me underwrite the Jump/Zocks capital deficit.

The strongest bull case on Wealor invokes the Anduril Known Exception directly: forward-deployed engineering into a recently-catalyzed vertical with long sales cycles is exactly the template that looked uninvestable until it wasn't, and Karim's direct experience selling to wealth managers is closer to founder-market fit than most YC fintech teams ever achieve. The case stings because the Anduril override was real and I almost talked myself out of that round too. What dismantles it is the specific clause of the Anduril override I wrote into my own framework: Trae Stephens had *already shipped Palantir's forward-deployed muscle at scale* before Anduril, and Palmer Luckey had shipped Oculus. Wealor's dossier names no comparable prior shipping artifact from Karim or Pierre, and the override does not extend to credentialed-but-unshipped teams attempting the same template. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict on Wealor: two signed paid pilots with named RIAs above $1B AUM each, disclosed within six months, at which point the revenue-velocity dimension reopens and the catalyst math starts to pencil.
