# OpenProse -- Elad Gil Evaluation

OpenProse is a shipping-rhythm-rich, revenue-silent OSS dev-tool whose dimensional signature -- high on Founder-Ships (14 releases, v0.14.0 on May 19 2026, 13 internally-dogfooded "responsibilities") and low on Revenue Velocity (zero paying customers disclosed, no pricing page) -- diagnoses it as a classic GitHub-stars-without-buying-behavior pattern, which in my book usually means the founder is real but the wedge is wrong. Dan Barrett ships like Aravind shipped at Perplexity (2022); the difference is that Aravind was shipping an end-user product whose usage curve was legible from day one, and Dan is shipping a framework whose only legible metric is 7,000 installs that have not yet converted to a dollar.

**Perplexity (2022)** is the load-bearing rung on the dimension OpenProse scores highest. The mechanism that made Perplexity load-bearing for me was not the pedigree -- it was that Aravind would ping me on LinkedIn and a week later the thing we discussed was shipped, which is the same artifact-cadence I see in OpenProse's 14 releases since launch, the `npx skills add openprose/prose` install path, and Dan Barrett dogfooding 13 of his own responsibilities to run OpenProse's GTM. Where OpenProse diverges from the Perplexity template is the *substrate of what gets shipped*: Aravind was shipping consumer-search behavior I could measure in DAU within months, while Dan Barrett is shipping a contract spec whose adoption I can only measure in stars and forks -- 1.3k and 104 respectively -- both of which are notoriously false signals in the dev-tools category until enterprise pulls.

**Lyft Series C** is the failure mechanism I keep returning to for OpenProse's weakest dimension, which is index-like distribution. The Lyft pass turned on a structural read that #2 in a winner-take-all network-effect market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and the same shape shows up in OpenProse's competitive map: LangChain at a $1.25B valuation post Oct 2025 Series B with ~$16M ARR per Latka, CrewAI at ~$3.2M ARR, and Claude Code's own "skills" primitive sitting natively inside Anthropic's harness. OpenProse's `npx skills add openprose/prose` install path is the tell -- the very command shape concedes that Claude Code's skills mechanism is the substrate, which means OpenProse is renting distribution from the entity most likely to ship a first-party Markdown-contract format and subsume the runtime layer outright.

Mechanism analysis on Market Re-Opening: the catalyst OpenProse implicitly invokes is the 2025-26 emergence of harness-level skills/sub-agent primitives in Claude Code and OpenCode, which is a real 24-month step-function -- but the catalyst predominantly accrues to the harnesses themselves and to whoever sets the contract format. Over 18 months this compounds against OpenProse rather than for it: every Anthropic release of a native skills spec narrows the surface where `*.prose.md` is differentiated, the same dynamic that compressed third-party LangChain-wrappers when LangGraph shipped natively.

Counterfactual on Single-Miracle Architecture: OpenProse would flip from a 7-9 range to a 10-12 range on this axis if the dossier showed Anthropic or OpenAI publicly endorsing `*.prose.md` as a reference contract format, because then the miracle collapses to "ship the registry of reusable responsibilities" rather than "win a standards war against the harness vendors themselves." Today the dossier shows neither endorsement nor any defensive proprietary surface -- MIT licensing on openprose/prose means LangChain ($260M raised) or Anthropic can copy the Markdown-contract pattern wholesale without legal friction. That's not a single miracle; that's a standards-war miracle stacked on a monetization miracle.

Single-anchor parallel on Revenue Velocity: **Harvey (2023)** is the template I use for what "just working" looks like in this cycle -- $3B → $5B → $8B in months because revenue was compounding against named AmLaw logos, not because Twitter was excited. OpenProse's traction section names zero paying customers, zero LOIs, no pricing page, and self-reports 7,000+ installs -- a metric that in 2026 OSS dev-tools is downstream of a single viral X post by Dan Barrett's @irl_danB account rather than upstream of enterprise pulls. If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive *paid* growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken; OpenProse hasn't disclosed any.

36-month forecast: OpenProse gets acqui-hired by Anthropic or absorbed into Claude Code's native skills format for under $20M within 24 months, OR shuts the commercial entity and continues as a maintained-but-unmonetized OSS project -- the *.prose.md syntax pattern survives as convention; the company does not. The mechanism is the platform-dependency risk the dossier itself names: Claude Code's existing skills primitive is the path of least resistance for Anthropic to subsume the contract layer, and Dan Barrett's distribution today runs entirely through `npx skills add openprose/prose`, which is rented surface.

The strongest objection is that **LangChain (2022-2025)** is the existence proof that an MIT OSS framework can compound from GitHub stars to a $1.25B valuation in roughly three years, and OpenProse at v0.14.0 with 1.3k stars and 7K installs is plausibly at LangChain's 2022 stage on the same arc. The objection stings because LangChain genuinely did walk that path -- and I'd take it more seriously except that LangChain caught the immediate post-ChatGPT window when *no* framework existed, while OpenProse is entering after LangChain, CrewAI, Claude Code skills, and the OpenAI Agents SDK have already partitioned the category, which puts OpenProse in the #3+ slot in what my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy would call a "winners forming" sub-market rather than a wide-open one. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: OpenProse discloses two named enterprise contracts above $250K ARR within six months, or Anthropic publicly designates `*.prose.md` as a reference format inside Claude Code -- absent either, OpenProse is the dev-tool I admire from a distance and don't write a check into.
