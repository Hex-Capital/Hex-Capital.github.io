# Prescience, Inc. -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Rishab and Aditya Jain are building an AI-driven employer health plan in a regulated category I genuinely like, but Prescience, Inc. arrives at my door as a Harvard-credentialed research duo selling against Sidecar Health's $328M war chest -- the dimensional signature is "regulated swing without a tactile artifact," and that pairing pattern-matches closer to my pass file than my Anduril file. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game here is whether two brothers from the Broad Institute can out-execute a $1.2B unicorn on actuarial ground they didn't grow up walking.

Anduril (2017) is the high rung on capital-intensive-unsexy-regulated, and Prescience, Inc. earns real altitude on that ladder because operating an employer health plan -- not enabling ICHRA choice like Thatch's $84.5M Index-backed bet -- requires state insurance licensure or a carrier partner, and that is exactly the kind of compounding regulatory moat I write checks into. Where Prescience, Inc. diverges from the Anduril rung is that Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus before he ever touched defense; the Jains shipped ML papers and ICOR Therapeutics before they touched a PEO contract, and the dossier does not name the licensed carrier or disclose the licensure path the way a Luckey-class operator would have. The moat is there in theory. The walk-on-the-well-pad credibility is not yet on the page.

HQ Trivia (2017) is the low rung on tactile verifiability for an instructive reason -- I could literally play the app and feel the viral pull before any metric existed, which is what overrode the Yusupov/Kroll co-founder governance risk for me. Prescience, Inc. inverts that override: it is a B2B insurance product I cannot ride, scan, install, or hand to a friend at the Four Seasons. The "AI Care Companion with 24/7 MD support" lives behind an employer enrollment wall, the "Prescience Time Machine" is described as "AlphaGo-like" without a benchmark, and the cal.com/prescience/30min demo link is the only artifact a normal human can touch. There is no Ingress to play obsessively from 2012 onward here, and the absence of a tactile artifact is the exact reason I cannot source this deal the way I source the ones I love.

On founder-lived-experience, the mechanism that worries me over the next eighteen months is that Rishab Jain's biography -- Regeneron $50K at 13, TIME 25 Most Influential Teens, Harvard CS/neuro, ICOR Therapeutics acquired -- is a research-trajectory biography, not a sat-in-a-PEO-broker-meeting biography, and AI-driven employer health benefits is a category where the founder needs to have been screamed at by an HR director about a $16,800 per-employee premium at least once. Aditya Jain's HMS M.D. and Broad Institute AI residency credentialize the clinical side beautifully, but neither brother appears to have operated a benefits plan, negotiated with United, or written a stop-loss policy -- the lived contact with the problem is downstream (the patient, the claim) rather than upstream (the buyer, the actuary), and at pre-seed that gap typically widens, not closes.

The counterfactual on magically-weird is what would need to be true to flip Prescience, Inc. from consensus to non-consensus: I would need to see two named tier-one funds visibly pass on aesthetic or moral grounds, the way they passed on Crusoe's flare-gas Bitcoin pitch in ~2018. Instead, Prescience, Inc. is selling into "Series D companies, FAANG, MBB consulting, Goldman/JPM, Frontier Labs" -- the most consensus buyer logo set in YC Summer 2026 -- against Sana Benefits ($111M raised), Sidecar Health ($1.2B valuation), and Thatch ($40M Series B led by Index April 2025). The "$600 PMPM vs. $1,213 market" pitch and the "AlphaGo-like" framing read as the polished memo-speak my Pass Example warns against, not as the laughed-at thesis the magically-weird filter rewards.

Flock Safety (~2018) is the single anchor on physical-world verifiability that matters here, because I sourced Flock from a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café -- the camera was an object I could photograph in the wild within forty-eight hours of meeting the founder. Prescience, Inc.'s "$25.7K cost avoided per predicted intervention" and "$875K runway returned in Series B case study" are testimonial fragments with no named counterparty, and the customer logos cited as "Series D company, FAANG, MBB" are anonymized in a way Flock's first deployments never were. I do not need a press release. I need a thing in the world.

My 36-month forecast: Prescience, Inc. does not become the operating plan it pitches today; it pivots within eighteen months to an AI care-navigation and TPA-tooling layer sold into existing carriers or ICHRA platforms (a Nayya-adjacent posture), lands somewhere between $1-3M ARR off two to four mid-market employer pilots, and raises a bridge rather than a priced Series A by mid-2028. The mechanism is straightforward -- operating a plan against Sidecar's $328M and Thatch's Index-backed distribution requires actuarial data the Jains have not yet accumulated, and the polymath strength of the team naturally drifts toward the software layer where their research compounds.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the DeepMind exception -- Demis Hassabis arrived with elite PhD credentials that should have failed my lived-experience screen, but the polymath cross-domain compounding (chess prodigy, neuroscientist, game designer) overrode the bias, and Rishab Jain's Top Young Scientist + ICOR + Regeneron stack stapled to Aditya's HMS M.D. + Broad AI is structurally that same polymath pairing in a regulated frontier. Where the analogy breaks is that Hassabis was building toward AGI in a category with no incumbent moat; the Jains are selling a health plan against a $1.2B unicorn whose moat is carrier relationships and claims volume, neither of which polymath compounding generates in twenty-four months. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named licensed carrier partner disclosed publicly plus three to five named employer customers with employee counts in the next nine months -- without that, the regulated-swing dimension I scored well is theoretical, and Prescience, Inc. stays a pass from my own checkbook.
