# Hub.xyz -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

The first thing I notice is that a co-founder was appointed CEO of an entirely different company — a Vietnamese proptech firm — in October 2025, months before the YC batch. That's not ambiguous. When I evaluate "gene pool" quality, I'm looking at whether the founding team is all-in on a binary outcome. A co-founder running a separate company signals the opposite. The dossier notes Léonie Nguyen isn't even listed on the YC page anymore. This isn't a red flag — it's a flashing alarm. A company becomes the people it hires, and this company is losing people before it's even started.

Now let me apply my actual filter. If Hub.xyz fully succeeds — the distributed bandwidth network scales, the API works perfectly, enterprises adopt it — what happens to the world? AI labs get cheaper training data. That's it. Nobody's life fundamentally changes. The direct beneficiaries are AI companies optimizing their data acquisition budgets. This is a supply chain improvement, not a consequence explosion. Compare this to what I look for: Commonwealth Fusion succeeding means limitless clean energy. Impossible Foods succeeding means feeding billions without animal agriculture. Hub.xyz succeeding means Anthropic or Google saves some percentage on their data pipeline costs. The consequence magnitude here is a rounding error on a rounding error. Even if you chain the argument — "cheaper data → better AI → better everything" — that logic applies to every AI infrastructure company on earth. It's not a thesis, it's a tautology.

The technical approach concerns me more than the market does. The dossier states explicitly that "the DePIN bandwidth-sharing model is replicable," and names Grass at roughly a billion-dollar valuation and Nodepay with 1.5 million active users running nearly identical architectures. Six hundred DePIN projects existed by late 2024. Hub.xyz is not riding a technology discontinuity — it's one of hundreds of projects using the same playbook: idle bandwidth, token incentives, Ethereum smart contracts. Where is the 10x? Where is the step-function change? A "dual-layer P2P mesh plus smart contracts" architecture is commodity DePIN infrastructure. I look for companies where a technical insight creates an unfair advantage. Here, the "advantage" is network scale — 500,000 nodes — but that number was built on token-incentive campaigns and airdrop quizzes, not organic economic demand. I watched this movie during the cleantech era: artificial incentives (government subsidies then, token airdrops now) create metrics that look like traction but collapse when the incentive changes. The $HUB token TGE, the IQ Points system, the SwissBorg partnership providing "1M+ KYC-verified community members" — this is financial engineering dressed as a network effect.

The strongest bull case would be this: centralized web scraping is becoming legally untenable (the lawsuits against AI companies in 2024-2025 are real), and decentralized, user-consented data collection is the structural replacement. If Hub.xyz becomes the enterprise standard for legally-sourced training data — the Scale AI of the post-scraping world — that's a real business in a $16 billion market by 2033. The "why now" is genuine. But even this bull case produces a moderately successful enterprise SaaS company, not a world-changing outcome. And it requires beating Grass, which has ten times the funding and comparable network scale. I'm not going to bet that the company with $1.7 million raised and a departing co-founder wins a commodity infrastructure race against a billion-dollar competitor in a replicable category. That's not the kind of improbable I invest in — that's just improbable.

The founders have solid educational credentials — École Polytechnique, HEC Paris, Berkeley — but I see zero evidence of the rapid learning and first-principles thinking I prioritize over pedigree. No public GitHub repos. No technical depth visible in the team composition. The one job posting is for a "Founding Full Stack AI Engineer," which tells me the core AI/ML capability isn't in-house yet. The founding team is business-heavy in a space where the differentiation must be technical. And I can find no evidence of iteration, pivoting, or adaptation — no signal that these founders have changed their thinking since inception. The pitch appears to be the same DePIN-for-AI-data narrative that a dozen other projects are running.

I either lose my money or make fifty times my money. Hub.xyz is designed to produce neither outcome — it sits in the uncomfortable middle where moderate success looks like a 2-3x return in a commoditized market, and failure looks like getting outscaled by better-funded DePIN competitors. That's exactly the return profile I avoid. The world doesn't need me to fund another bandwidth-sharing network. Some bets are too important not to make; this is not one of them.
