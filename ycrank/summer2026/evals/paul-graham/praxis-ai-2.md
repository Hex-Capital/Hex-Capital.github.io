# Praxis AI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Praxis AI is a schlep-heavy business-development trio without a hacker at the core -- the highest and lowest dimensions on my ladder for this company. Rohan Seelamsetty (debater), Dev Karpe (Wharton Huntsman, ex-IB), and Tommy Li (ex-Morgan Stanley PE, ex-a16z) claim they have already embedded inside publicly listed conglomerates and reached 60,000 workers across 5 continents to capture egocentric video for humanoid model developers. That's exactly the kind of unpleasant, cross-jurisdictional, contract-heavy work Scale AI cannot pivot into without cannibalizing its gig pipeline. It is also exactly the kind of work three Penn finance graduates might be temperamentally suited to hustle through and temperamentally unsuited to build the tooling for.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the load-bearing analogy on the axis where Praxis AI scores highest. Brian Armstrong's edge was not that bitcoin was clever; it was that nobody else in his batch wanted to spend two years talking to state money-transmitter regulators. Praxis AI's claimed schlep is structurally similar -- negotiating data-rights carve-outs inside Fortune-scale conglomerates so 60k workers can legally record proprietary industrial environments and Praxis can resell that footage to Physical Intelligence and Figure. Where Praxis AI diverges from **Coinbase** (2012): Armstrong shipped an Android wallet prototype to overturn his written rejection; the Praxis AI dossier names 60k workers and 150+ environment types but names zero conglomerate partners, discloses zero signed contracts, and praxisrobotics.io shows only the tagline "Great machines will be forged by human intuition." The schlep is claimed. I can't yet see it shipped.

The MBA-team pass pattern from my "Founder Mode" (September 2024) essay is what I'm scanning for on the hacker-genealogy axis, and Praxis AI partially trips it. Tommy Li has real IEEE publications from NASA JPL and PerceptIn Robotics -- that's a genuine hacker credential and I weight it. But Seelamsetty's public trail is competitive debate and an Oxford Apollo fellowship on existential risk; Karpe's is Wharton Huntsman plus a Fortune 500 CEO's office; no founder has a GitHub the researcher found. Two of three founders read as the exact biz-dev-first profile the "Stripe (2010) hacker-first pattern" inverts, and the data-capture-and-pipeline product Praxis AI is selling is a systems-engineering problem where I'd want the CTO writing the ingestion code, not a JPL alum imported to lend the team its only shipped-artifact credential.

Tommy Li's a16z industrial-tech VC seat is what pins the organic-idea dimension for Praxis AI, and the pinning is weak. **Dropbox** (2007) worked because Drew Houston kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT -- the founder was the customer. Li watched physical-AI companies from the buy-side of a VC term sheet; that's proximity to the pain, not the pain itself. None of the Praxis AI founders trained a humanoid model, none ran out of Ego4D-style capture data at 3 a.m., none built a scraper for industrial footage because they personally needed it. This is market-spotting from an a16z desk, which is a real category of insight but is not the organic-idea filter from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012).

The mechanism by which the animal-resourcefulness score compounds or fails over 18 months for Praxis AI is straightforward: either the "60k workers across 5 continents" number converts into a named counterparty and per-hour data revenue by month 12, or it dissolves into a slide asset. Micro1 hit $100M ARR in eight months by shipping Ray-Ban kits to gig workers -- a concrete artifact I can point at. Praxis AI's equivalent artifact today is a tagline website. The absence of a named conglomerate partner in the dossier is not stage-appropriate silence; a pre-seed embed inside a publicly listed conglomerate would name that conglomerate if the embed were real and unblocked, because that name is the entire fundraise. I discount the 60k number accordingly.

For the co-founder configuration to flip a tier for Praxis AI, one of the three Penn graduates would need to be documented as having worked with another for 2+ years pre-startup in a way that produced a shipped artifact together -- **Reddit** (2005)'s Huffman/Ohanian dorm-roommate pattern, or **Twitch** (2007)'s Kan/Shear reunion after **Kiko** (2005). Praxis AI's dossier gives me three Penn affiliations with no described joint history. Trio, not solo -- that's the floor. But it's a shared-campus trio, not a shared-battle trio, and the YC interview usually surfaces which one it is; the dossier doesn't tell me.

36-month forecast for Praxis AI: acquired by Scale AI or Micro1 for under $50M as a rolodex-and-contracts acqui-hire, or pivoted into a single-conglomerate annotation-services relationship at under $5M ARR with the humanoid buy-side rerouted back to Scale AI's gig pipeline. The mechanism is that the schlep is real but the moat requires signed multi-year exclusivity Praxis AI has not demonstrated it can extract, and the well-capitalized incumbents ($29B, $2.5B, $110M raised respectively) can outbid on any conglomerate partnership Praxis AI surfaces the moment revenue proves the channel.

The bull case that stings: the embed-in-conglomerate model is precisely what Scale AI structurally cannot copy -- their gig-recruit pipeline is a sunk asset, and pivoting to conglomerate-workforce data would cannibalize it exactly as I argued in "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012). Li's a16z-industrials rolodex plus Karpe's Fortune 500 CEO-office access is a genuinely non-obvious GTM combination for opening those doors. If Praxis AI locks two exclusive multi-year conglomerate contracts before Micro1 wakes up, this becomes the data-supply layer for humanoid training and I'll have missed a defining company. What wins the argument against the bull case: Praxis AI volunteered the 60k number without volunteering a single conglomerate name, and my "black swan farming" thesis rewards founders who ship stunts, not founders who ship metrics. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named Fortune 500 conglomerate partner disclosed with a signed multi-year data-supply agreement, or first $1M of data-sale revenue booked with Physical Intelligence, Figure, or Generalist AI.
