# HyperProbe -- Elad Gil Evaluation

HyperProbe is a clean-single-miracle company trapped in a commoditized-primitive workflow -- Shailendra Singh has correctly identified that coding agents (Cursor, Claude Code, Codex) now write faster than humans can debug, but the "live probe from the IDE" primitive is already owned by Lightrun's $70M Series B (April 2025) and Dynatrace's 2023 Rookout acquisition. The catalyst is real; the index position is not.

**Catalyst dimension.** **Decagon** (2023) is the template I keep coming back to for HyperProbe: post-LLM customer support was a genuine 24-month re-opening, and Decagon planted a workflow layer in the exact spot every AI-authored ticket would eventually flow through. HyperProbe's catalyst -- coding agents shipping backend code faster than the humans reviewing it can grep logs -- is a real technological step-function, not a TAM slide. The dossier's own framing on hyperprobe.co (2026) names Cursor/Claude Code/Codex as the shift, and I buy that framing. Where HyperProbe diverges from the Decagon rung is that Decagon's workflow layer had no serious pre-existing incumbent inside the LLM window; HyperProbe's does -- Lightrun's April 2025 $70M raise was explicitly for "AI-SRE dynamic instrumentation," which is the same primitive named differently. Catalyst present, catalyst-window-already-crowded.

**Index position.** The **Lyft** Series C pass is the load-bearing precedent here, and I want to state the failure mechanism precisely: when a primitive is already staked by a well-funded #1 with network effects (or in this case, enterprise integrations and distribution), being #2 with a better UX doesn't invert share -- it just gets bought or bled. HyperProbe is not in Lyft's exact market shape, but the pattern rhymes: Lightrun ($110M total, Accel/Insight-backed) and Dynatrace-owned Rookout together own the enterprise probe-injection wedge, Sentry Seer and Datadog Bits AI SRE cover triage. HyperProbe's differentiation -- "agent-driven probe insertion from the IDE" -- is a UX skin over a primitive Lightrun can ship in a quarter. When I run the *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) test, live debugging is closer to "winners declared" than "wide open."

**Revenue velocity.** In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose. HyperProbe's dossier gives me two customer testimonials -- Aishwarya Maurya at CheQ Digital, Bhagwan Bansal at Housing.com -- no MRR, no pricing page, and a "Hiring: False" flag on the YC directory that at pre-seed is not damning but is also not the shape of a company compounding into a hire-immediately problem. Over the next 18 months this dimension either compounds hard (Cursor/Claude Code integrations produce a viral install pattern that shows up as usage-curve screenshots on X) or it doesn't move, in which case Lightrun's enterprise motion eats the mid-market before HyperProbe reaches a defensible ARR number. The dossier gives me nothing to distinguish "just working" from "false signal."

**Founder shipping rhythm.** Shailendra Singh actually clears my standard bar here, which is worth naming precisely: he built HyperTest from 2015 to 2025, raised $2.13M (Better Capital-led seed Dec 2022), and closed real B2B logos -- Urbancompany, Nykaa, Groww per Tracxn -- which is a shipping artifact, not a pedigree line. Compare to the **Perplexity** (2022) cold-inbound pattern: Aravind was showing up a week later with the thing we discussed, built. HyperProbe's evidence isn't week-over-week iteration in front of me, but it is a founder who has independently shipped a developer-tools product to paying customers and pivoted the entity into an adjacent runtime problem -- that's a different signal than an ex-FAANG resume with no artifacts. This is my highest-conviction dimension on HyperProbe.

**Single-miracle test.** HyperProbe's miracle is isolated: assume Cursor/Claude Code/Codex keep shipping backend code (already true), assume LLMs can consume runtime variable snapshots and produce fixes (Sentry Seer and Bits AI SRE already do this on pre-captured telemetry), and the remaining work is engineering (sub-1% CPU non-blocking probes across Node/TS/Java/Scala/Kotlin) plus GTM. That's one miracle, not three. What kills the score here is that the engineering miracle Rookout spent seven years and $28M to reach was acquired for $33.9M in July 2023 -- so even a clean single-miracle outcome may cap the exit at Rookout comps, not a compounding index.

**Forecast.** My 36-month base case for HyperProbe is a $25M-$60M acqui-hire to Datadog, GitHub, or Sentry -- Rookout's $33.9M July 2023 exit to Dynatrace is the template, and HyperProbe's agent-IDE wedge is arguably a cleaner strategic fit for GitHub-Copilot or Cursor than for a pure observability vendor. The mechanism producing this: Lightrun's $70M runway prices HyperProbe out of the enterprise motion; the agent-native UX becomes the acquisition thesis for a coding-agent parent that wants to close the write-to-debug loop natively.

**Strongest objection.** The bull case that stings: coding agents are the fastest-adopted developer primitive in a decade, and if Cursor/Claude Code become the default authoring surface, then a probe workflow *native to the agent* (not to the SRE console, which is Lightrun's shape) is not a UX veneer -- it is a different distribution channel entirely, and Shailendra has ten years of developer-tools sales muscle to compound it. If HyperProbe ships a first-party Cursor extension that captures runtime state directly into the agent's context window, Lightrun's enterprise motion is irrelevant because the buyer is now the individual developer, not the SRE org. The dossier evidence I use to dismantle this: no such integration is named, no MoM growth is disclosed, the go-to-market is founder-led Calendly demos, and Lightrun's Accel/Insight backing means they can ship the same integration next quarter. **Flip condition:** if HyperProbe posts a Cursor/Claude Code-native integration with >20% MoM active-team growth and one named F500 logo in the next 90 days, I re-underwrite at seed.
