# Kinect -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Kinect's core components — LLM-powered chat, behavioral targeting, dynamic page rendering — are each individually commoditized. The dossier states this explicitly, and I agree. When I look at Kinect, I see the exact archetype I pass on most frequently: a team calling an API, wrapping it in a clean interface, and pointing it at an obvious market. Rep AI has $8.2M+ and 500 merchants. Alhena AI has $4.9M and enterprise logos like Tatcha. Tolstoy has 2,000+ brand partners. OpenAI and Perplexity both launched AI shopping assistants in late 2025. Kinect is entering a room that's already crowded, without a weapon nobody else can build.

Kratik Agrawal led Conversational Intelligence at Reevo, an AI-native GTM platform that raised $80M. Varun Kandula co-led the same function and previously sourced $200K+ in contracted ARR consulting with Sephora and Ralph Lauren through NextGen Consulting. This is relevant experience. But relevant is not the same as non-replicable. Specific knowledge is what you'd still be doing at 3 AM even if nobody was paying you — it's the thing that looks like play to you and work to everyone else. Kinect's founders identified "personalized storefronts" as an opportunity through their professional roles, not through years of obsessive engagement with why e-commerce pages fail shoppers. If Kratik and Varun left tomorrow, a competent replacement team could rebuild Kinect's product in months using the same LLM APIs and Shopify integrations. That's the test, and Kinect doesn't pass it.

Kinect's leverage architecture concerns me more than the competition does. The website describes "white-glove service" — dedicated onboarding, direct team access, quarterly performance reviews, continuous weekly improvements. With a team of two, that's not a scaling strategy; that's a consulting engagement. Every hour Kratik or Varun spends in a quarterly review is an hour not shipping product. The moment Kinect needs to grow revenue, they need to grow headcount, and that's labor leverage — the worst form. Compare this to Notion at the same stage: a tiny team building a product that millions could adopt through word-of-mouth without ever talking to a human. Kinect's current model requires proportional human involvement per customer, which structurally caps the output-per-person ratio that I look for in every investment.

The bull case deserves a fair hearing. Kinect combines conversational AI with adaptive page rendering — competitors like Rep AI and Alhena do one or the other, not both. The Shopify platform creates channel conflict that may prevent the incumbent from building this natively, the way Google's ad model prevented them from building Perplexity. The shared Reevo working relationship between both founders is genuine — these aren't strangers from a co-founder matching event. And the data flywheel is real in theory: aggregated shopper questions and objection patterns across merchants could compound into a defensible intelligence layer that improves recommendations over time. If Kinect can sign 50 merchants and demonstrate that cross-merchant learning produces measurably better conversion lift than a competitor training on a single store's data, that would be specific knowledge encoded in data rather than in founders — a legitimate moat. But none of that exists today. The dashboard on Kinect's website shows "0%" for conversion lift and AOV increase alongside what appear to be illustrative demo figures. Potential flywheels are not flywheels.

Kinect's access democratization angle is real but shallow. Mid-market Shopify merchants getting AI personalization previously available only to Amazon-scale retailers is genuine access expansion. But it's incremental — these merchants already have chatbots, already have A/B testing tools, already have personalization plugins. Kinect makes these capabilities better, not newly possible. Compare this to AngelList, which gave startup founders access to angel investors they literally could not reach before, or Polymarket, which gave retail participants access to information markets that didn't exist. Kinect is a better tool, not a new door.

I pass on Kinect. The decisive dimensions are Specific Knowledge Productization and Permissionless Leverage Architecture. The founders have professional experience building conversational AI at Reevo, but that experience is transferable — it's a resume line, not a decade of obsessive domain immersion that would take competitors years to replicate. The leverage architecture is compromised by the white-glove model and dependency on third-party LLM APIs — Kinect rents its core intelligence from foundation model providers and delivers it through human-intensive service. Innovation built on rented leverage in a converging market is consumer surplus, not company value. The one condition that flips me: demonstrate a cross-merchant data flywheel producing conversion lift that exceeds any single-merchant optimization tool by a statistically significant margin, with 20+ paying merchants — that would transform Kinect from an AI wrapper into a proprietary intelligence platform.
