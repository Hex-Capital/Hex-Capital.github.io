# Lamina Labs -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Lamina Labs is a two-person MIT technical team — Kartikesh Mishra (CSAIL, drones-following-language-via-RL) and Sudip Rokaya — pointing real ML chops at the most consensus pre-seed sleeve of Spring 2026: "AI for EdTech video." The signature here is highest on founder substrate, lowest on category sleeve, and that pairing tells me exactly what I'm looking at — a credible engineering duo who picked the room everyone else is already shouting in, two months after Golpo AI closed $4.1M from Emergence, YC, and Afore for the same buyer.

Solana at $0.05/coin is what category-sleeve conviction looks like when I'm right — a sleeve everyone else had quit, priced like nobody cared, where the shared mechanism is "I get to be the only bid on an asset the market has temporarily declared uninvestable." Lamina Labs diverges from that mechanism on every axis: the EdTech-AI-video sleeve is not abandoned, it is actively being capitalized by Emergence and Afore through Golpo, and "visual infrastructure for EdTech AI applications" is the literal tagline I would expect a 2026 YC batch median deck to ship with. "AI is just more internet" (Lonsdale #113) applies cleanly here — Lamina Labs is not Solana, it is the inverse trade: paying consensus prices for the commodity-exposure half of a thesis I explicitly re-weight away from.

The pass example I keep in my head — the canonical "AI agent for vertical, $30M post, four tier-1s in" — fails because the financing structure is a generic SaaS cap table that needs five more rounds against a funded incumbent, and that is the LOW-rung pattern Lamina Labs replicates almost exactly. Free trial at app.laminalabs.ai/simi, no pricing, no revenue disclosed, two engineers splitting "visual infrastructure" platform ambition against a live vertical app (Simi), and a direct competitor with 4x the war chest already shipping Golpo 2.0 into education and training buyers. There is no Teamshares-style employee-equity wrapper here, no Mogilko-style 30-year earnings instrument, no holdco — this is a SAFE into a sub-scale SaaS sprint against a funded opponent, which is the structure I most reliably lose money on.

Mishra's CSAIL pedigree under Daniela Rus and Rokaya leaving MIT mid-degree is the one dimension where Lamina Labs actually loads — these are not sanitized consultants, the GitHub history is real (3DAttnVAE-GAN, 14 stars, mk314k), and "Leaving MIT to build the next generation" is at least a missionary noise. But conviction compounds over 18 months only if the founder has a non-obvious belief about why everyone else's approach to EdTech video is wrong, and the dossier surfaces zero public writing from either founder on pedagogy, animation primitives, or why deterministic rendering beats diffusion for explanation — the wedge is "8-11 seconds vs 15 minutes," which is a latency claim, not a thesis. Mishra and Rokaya have the temperament substrate; they have not yet shown the contrarian artifact.

Counterfactually on founder-as-brand: for Lamina Labs to clear a tier on this axis, one of these two would need to be running an "explain-it-with-math" YouTube channel with 50K subs, or Mishra would need a Substack on generative pedagogy with measurable readership before the round. What the dossier actually shows is GitHub at 22 followers, Twitter counts not even retrievable, one third-party testimonial from Aaron Siim, and zero Product Hunt presence (the existing /lamina entry is a 2018 collision). This is the modal pre-seed distribution profile and the John Fish / Bookshelved override does not trigger — there is no owned audience on this cap table to price.

The single-anchor parallel for round dynamics is Sublime Security, where I bought a security category before the next consolidation wave because the consensus security investors were still parked in late-stage SOC SaaS. Lamina Labs is the geometric opposite: Golpo's October 2025 round with Emergence and Afore is itself the consolidation wave starting, and Lamina Labs is entering after the signal, not before it. There is no price discount in the dossier — silence on round terms in a 2026 YC AI-vertical deck is, in my experience, almost always consensus pricing dressed as ambient.

My 36-month forecast on Lamina Labs: acqui-hired by Golpo, an LMS incumbent (Coursera/Chegg/Pearson tier), or a horizontal video tool (Synthesia/Pictory) at a sub-$15M outcome, with ARR ceilinged between $400K and $1.2M before the next bridge fails — the mechanism is that Golpo's $4.1M and existing enterprise channel through Emergence converts education buyers 12-18 months before Lamina Labs can fund a comparable GTM, and "deterministic rendering in 8 seconds" is not a moat that survives a funded competitor matching latency. Mishra's CSAIL background is exactly the profile a Golpo or Synthesia acqui-hire prices.

The strongest case against me: the MIT CSAIL substrate is real, deterministic generative rendering at frame-accurate precision is a genuinely harder technical problem than diffusion-video shops are solving, and the dossier's stated pivot to "visual infrastructure" — an API layer for other EdTech AI apps — is the kind of platform-bet that, if it lands, would echo the Ro override where regulatory or technical complexity itself becomes the moat that justifies capital intensity. The bear dismantle is that Ro had 50-state prescription rails as an irreducible schlep no software competitor could shortcut; Lamina Labs has "every frame is mathematically precise," which is a marketing sentence, not a licensing barrier, and the two-person team is currently shipping a vertical app (Simi) not an API with named integration partners. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed paid integration with a named LMS or course platform — Coursera, Khan Academy, Pearson, Chegg — at $50K+ ACV, OR a non-standard financing instrument (revenue share against course-creator GMV, royalty on rendered minutes) replacing the SAFE before the next round. Absent either, Lamina Labs is the trade I have publicly committed to not making.
