## The Idea

- **Problem:** 95% of businesses abandon their AI pilots, wasting "hundreds of billions in AI spend every year" because the bottleneck is shared understanding of customer, product, and codebase rather than models or prompts (YC Launch page, 2026).
- **Approach:** A humans-in-the-loop AI builder that operates inside the client's environment (Slack, Gmail, Chrome, Miro, Figma, GitHub, Vercel, Supabase, CLI) to capture business goals and stakeholder context, then ship a working AI product the same afternoon (deepinteractions.ai, 2026).
- **Differentiation:** Vs. Lovable / Bolt / v0 / Replit which generate code from a single prompt, Deep Interactions positions as "collaborative" — keeping multiple humans (PMs, designers, engineers, stakeholders) and agents in sync rather than a solo developer chat (deepinteractions.ai; Lovable comparison, 2026).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: No public pricing; site routes users to a Calendly call with the founder and a Typeform waitlist, suggesting hand-sold pilot/contract pricing typical of design-partner stage (deepinteractions.ai, 2026).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment; comparable AI app-builder Lovable reached $400M ARR by Feb 2026 (TechCrunch, Mar 2026) as a proxy for category demand.
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Direct founder-led sales to enterprise AI-pilot owners, evidenced by founder's personal Calendly link and waitlist form on the homepage (deepinteractions.ai, 2026).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** No defensibility signals found in public sources beyond claimed integration breadth across 9+ team tools (deepinteractions.ai, 2026).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: A proprietary "context layer" of customer/product/codebase data accumulated per client could create switching costs, but the YC launch language describes this as the product thesis, not a deployed asset (YC Launch page, 2026).
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: No structural barrier identified vs. Lovable, Bolt, Replit, v0 — all well-funded incumbents that could add multi-stakeholder collaboration features; Replit Agent 4 already supports parallel agents on a single project (Replit blog, Mar 2026).
- **Commoditization risk:** Lovable ($400M ARR, 146 employees, TechCrunch Mar 2026), Replit ($9B valuation, Series D, Mar 2026), and StackBlitz/Bolt ($135M raised, Sacra 2026) could each extend into the team/stakeholder workflow.

## Key Risks

- **Category commoditization by funded incumbents:** Lovable added $100M revenue in a single month with 146 staff (TechCrunch, Mar 2026) and Replit raised $400M at $9B (Mar 2026); both can add multi-stakeholder workflows before Deep Interactions establishes share. No public mitigation found.
- **Distribution-mechanism risk:** Sales funnel relies on a founder Calendly link and Typeform waitlist (deepinteractions.ai, 2026), which is unproven for reaching enterprise AI-pilot buyers at the scale required to compete with marketplace-distributed rivals (Bolt × Microsoft Marketplace, May 2026).
- **Traction concentration:** Only 12 named businesses live (YC Launch page, 2026); loss of a small number of design partners would materially compress usage metrics.
- **Unverified moat thesis:** The "context layer" is described as the differentiator (YC Launch page, 2026) but no proprietary data asset, IP filing, or benchmark is published; technical feasibility of synchronizing humans + agents + code at production quality remains unproven publicly.
- **Founder bandwidth and role definition:** Public materials list only Sruthi Viswanathan as "Founder" with three additional team members in undefined founding roles (YC page; deepinteractions.ai, 2026), creating ambiguity on technical leadership for an engineering-heavy product.
