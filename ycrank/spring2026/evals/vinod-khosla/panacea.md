# Panacea -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Panacea's signature in my framework is a competent founder pair — Haris Javed-Akhtar MD and Yaman Ziadeh (ex-Bloomberg, ex-Anterior) — applying AI to compress the FDA submission workflow at firms like MDI Consultants and EAS, with one named principal consultant (Harun Sinha) carrying the "hundreds of filings" claim on withpanacea.com. The high-relative dimension is founder learning rate via Yaman's Anterior pedigree; the low is technical and capital risk — Panacea is a services-plus-software business with no science miracle, no fleet, no plant, no foundation model of its own. That pairing tells me what Panacea is: an optimization play on an existing $2.11B regulatory affairs outsourcing growth pool (Future Market Insights), not a civilizational redirection. This is the exact pattern I described in my Pass Example — domain-expert founders, 20-30% workflow efficiency, indistinguishable from a feature any IQVIA product team ships in twelve months.

On consequence magnitude — the dimension I read first — the high rung is **OpenAI** (2019), where the success-state was a labor-cost displacement variable I later sized at $15T (Fortune 2026-03-06); the low rung is the GPT-sales-call-summarizer I pass on in 30 seconds. **DoorDash** (2013) sits in the middle as a consumer-commerce reshape. Panacea sits between the low rung and DoorDash, closer to the low rung: the dossier's success-state is "fixed-fee milestone pricing replaces hourly billing at MDI Consultants," with a customer testimonial of "12x faster" inside a single consultant's workflow on withpanacea.com. No incumbent regime collapses if Panacea wins. The FDA still gates submissions, biotechs still pay for filings, the 117.27-day average elapsed time (FY2025 FDA stats) is not a variable Panacea can move because the bottleneck is on the reviewer side, not the filer side. That is the structural reason this scores low — Panacea is selling speed into a queue it does not control.

Technical and capital risk is where Panacea sits flat on the floor. The high rung is **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) — $115M Series A I led when fusion physics was investor-toxic; the mid rung is **QuantumScape** (early, IPO 2020) with solid-state electrochemistry risk; the low rung is **Slash** (2024), a software-only fintech I funded only as a Known Exception via the Kanye pivot. Panacea sits below Slash. The dossier discloses no proprietary corpus, no fine-tuned model, no IP filings, no hardware. **Weave Bio**'s $20M Series A in October 2025, led by USVP with Innovation Endeavors, proves consensus VCs already underwrite this thesis — which is the inverse of what I look for. If USVP funds the AI-only version, I am over-resourced on Panacea and structurally redundant.

On founder defiance of expert consensus, the high rung is **Physical Intelligence** (2024) — Sergey Levine and Chelsea Finn publicly redefining what robotic foundation models can do; the mid rung is **Glean** (Arvind Jain, ex-Google search, a Known Exception I funded on consequence override); the low rung is the eight-years-at-Salesforce profile from my Pass Example. Panacea sits between the low and mid rungs. Haris's MD is domain-credentialing, not domain-defying — no public thesis, no controversial position in his field per the dossier. Yaman's Anterior tenure is real AI-for-healthcare experience but it is the consensus career arc, not the instigator pattern from my TED Talk on the 12 climate instigators. Learning rate is the one dimension where Panacea earns slightly more than the default — Yaman's Bloomberg→Anterior→Panacea progression shows movement toward harder problems, though it falls short of the **Slash** Kanye-pivot rung that is my real bar.

Required-capital profile sits at the low rung. The high rung is **Commonwealth Fusion** ($115M Series A) and **Genesis AI** ($105M co-led seed in July 2025); the mid rung is **Cognition** ($175M Series B); the low rung is commodity SaaS reaching $1M ARR on $5-10M. Panacea is the low rung — two founders, no upfront fees, milestone billing, San Francisco, free initial consultation booked through cal.com/haris-panacea. There is nothing in the roadmap that needs a Khosla check. Any seed fund can write this round.

My 36-month forecast: Panacea is either acqui-hired by Weave Bio or IQVIA for $15-40M, or sits as a profitable $3-8M ARR boutique that never raises a Series B — the modal outcome for AI-services hybrids when the AI-only competitor has $20M of USVP money and the consultant-only incumbents have 48 years of relationships (MDI since 1978). The mechanism is that Panacea's fixed-fee differentiation either gets copied by a consultancy that can afford the margin compression, or gets out-engineered by Weave Bio moving down-market into Panacea's biotech ICP.

The strongest bull case against me: FDA regulatory affairs is a microcosm of my own $15T labor displacement thesis (Fortune 2026-03-06), and Yaman's Anterior pedigree maps to the YC-pedigreed AI-healthcare pattern that produced my **Slash** Known Exception. If AI genuinely eliminates the regulatory writer role — which held 27.4% of the service segment in 2025 — Panacea is the wedge into a labor category I publicly want to collapse. That sting is real. But it does not survive the Weave Bio test: when USVP and Innovation Endeavors already fund the labor-displacement thesis at $20M, I am not the consequence-first first-money VC writing the check no one else will write — I am the seventh investor in a category. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Panacea signs an exclusive multi-year regulatory-partner agreement with a top-15 pharma or builds a proprietary submission corpus from 500+ filings that no software-only competitor can match — moat evidence the current dossier explicitly lacks.
