# Coasty -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Coasty is the 2026 YC archetype my Pass Example was literally written about -- a two-person San Francisco team shipping a computer-use agent against Anthropic's Claude Computer Use and OpenAI's Operator, claiming an 82% vs 72% OSWorld delta as the wedge. The dimensional signature: Coasty's highest mark is founder conviction (Kovuru's dev.to "AI agent wrecked my prod DB" origin is a genuine artifact, and Jannu's Microsoft/Purdue ML pedigree is real), and its lowest is category sleeve -- this is foundation-model-adjacent infrastructure in the most consensus seed bucket of the batch. That pairing tells me Coasty is a competent ML team running directly at a category I have publicly de-rated, which is a worse setup than an incompetent team in a sleeve I want.

The high rung on category for me is **Solana** at $0.05 -- pre-consensus, on-chain economic activity, a sleeve every generalist had quit. The mechanism that made Solana load-bearing was that the underlying asset existed outside the venture momentum game; the market would eventually have to translate it back into legible terms, and I got paid for the translation. Coasty inverts every variable: the category is the most-funded sleeve of 2026, the asset is a benchmark percentage on a public 369-task harness, and the translation back to a Series A buyer is "we're 10 points ahead of Anthropic until Anthropic ships their next model." When Coasty's own blog hedges its own number with the "82% Real, 73% Exploited" framing, that's not a moat, that's a leaderboard position with a half-life measured in model releases. I bought Solana because consensus had left; I'd be buying Coasty because consensus has arrived.

On capital structure, the low-rung failure mode is the generic SaaS cap table that needs five more rounds to reach default-alive -- and Coasty's pricing page ($19 / $50 / $99 tiers) against competitors who already raised $67.5M (Browserbase), $22M Series A (Kernel), and $17M (Browser Use) is exactly that pattern. Coasty will need to outraise Browserbase to stay in the conversation, and the financing instrument on offer is the most boring possible SAFE -- there is no revenue share, no royalty, no holdco, nothing in this dossier that would let me structure a non-consensus instrument the way I did with Mogilko's 5%-of-30-years deal. The capital structure here is venture-shaped only in the sense that it requires venture capital to keep up; that's not the same as being a venture moment.

Round pricing is where mechanism analysis matters most: pre-seed YC SAFE caps for an AI-agent team with an OSWorld #1 claim in S26 will get bid by tier-1 funds chasing the "computer use" label, and the price will not reflect that Coasty's benchmark lead is a depreciating asset on the same release cadence as Claude and GPT. Over 18 months, the benchmark delta compresses, the differentiation collapses to "sandboxed VM + reliability narrative," and the round Coasty raises today will look mispriced upward once the next Anthropic Computer Use release lands. I'm price-sensitive precisely because this is the dossier the rest of the market will overpay for.

On founder conviction, the counterfactual is clean: for Coasty's score to flip a tier here, I'd need Prateek Jannu or Nitish Kovuru to have published opinionated long-form writing about *why* RPA's UiPath/Blue Prism/Automation Anywhere model is structurally broken in a way frontier labs won't capture -- not just a dev.to post about a deleted database. The Kovuru incident is a good origin anecdote and the sandboxed-VM thesis follows from it, but origin stories aren't the same as missionary temperament across a non-linear arc, and nothing in the dossier shows either founder has been on this problem for 3+ years before the round.

Founder-as-brand at Coasty -- comparing on a single axis to John Fish / Bookshelved, where the cap table was literally underwriting an existing audience -- is essentially absent: @prateekjannu, @llmhubdev, a dev.to handle, an unretrievable GitHub star count, and a Product Hunt launch on 19 Feb 2026. That isn't owned distribution, that's the standard developer-marketing footprint every YC AI team ships with, and the Slow Creator Fund thesis doesn't load here because there is no creator on this cap table.

My 36-month forecast for Coasty: acqui-hired by Anthropic, OpenAI, or a second-tier RPA incumbent (UiPath, Automation Anywhere) for $15-40M in equity-and-retention, with Jannu and Kovuru absorbed into a computer-use product team -- the outcome consistent with high-conviction founders in a sleeve where the platform owners eat the wrapper. The mechanism is straightforward: Coasty's benchmark lead erodes inside 12 months, the $19-$99 self-serve revenue can't fund the enterprise SOC-2 motion that the Fortune 500 hesitance in the Humans-in-the-Loop interview demands, and the cleanest exit for two strong ML engineers is into the lab whose API they were arbitraging.

The strongest bull case against my pass is that enterprise trust for desktop-touching agents is a genuine schlep moat -- the same regulatory-complexity argument that made me override capital efficiency for **Ro**'s 50-state prescription rails. If Coasty's sandboxed-VM architecture plus a bespoke compliance posture compounds into the default trusted-middleware layer between frontier models and Fortune 500 internal systems, then 82% on OSWorld was never the point and I missed the actual asset. I don't buy it: the Ro exception required a regulatory regime no software could shortcut, and Coasty's "trust" story is a security-and-deployment narrative that Anthropic and Microsoft can replicate with a single enterprise SKU. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named Fortune 500 paid contract -- not an interview quote about hesitance, an actual logo with ARR -- inside the next 9 months, at which point Coasty stops being an OSWorld wrapper and starts being an enterprise-trust company I'd reprice.
