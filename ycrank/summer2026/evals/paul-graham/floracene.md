# Floracene -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Floracene is a schlep-zone company with no visible schlep artifact yet -- Asaph Kupferman and Jonah picked a problem (independent ASCs overpaying 20-30% on orthopedic and spine implants because surgeons default to the brand they trained on) that sits squarely in the kind of unpleasant, relationship-encrusted work I wrote about in "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012), but the Floracene dossier surfaces zero pre-funding stunts, zero named ASC pilots, and zero shipped demo. That pairing -- high schlep, low animal-track -- is the whole read.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the ladder I keep reaching for on schlep. Brian Armstrong walked into a bitcoin compliance grind that every other applicant in the batch was routing around; the ugliness of the work became the moat. Floracene's ugliness is different in shape but similar in character: device reps have surgeon relationships that existing incumbents can't touch without cannibalizing themselves, and the ~$22M ARR HST Pathways cannot ship a clinical-equivalency layer without breaking the GPO admin-fee flywheel that Innovatix and Premier depend on. That is a real structural schlep, not a claimed one. Where Floracene diverges from the Armstrong version is that Armstrong shipped an Android wallet prototype to overturn his rejection; Floracene's dossier names no equivalent artifact -- no SKU-mapping tool posted publicly, no pilot ASC named, no surgeon quoted. The schlep is spotted. It has not yet been ground on.

The MBA-team AI-wrapper pass I've written about elsewhere failed the animal test because the founders led with a deck and a $50B TAM slide instead of a shipped thing. Floracene doesn't fully sit in that failure mode -- Jonah's prior fax-automation startup for fertility clinics is exactly the kind of small, ugly, unglamorous shipping move I look for, the sort of thing an animal builds because nobody asked them to. But at the Floracene level the dossier is silent: no revenue, no LOI, no product-hunt post, no press, a name ("Floracene") that returns near-zero web results outside YC surfaces. In "Relentlessly Resourceful" (March 2009) I said animals leave tracks. Floracene, as of this dossier, leaves almost none.

On organic-idea fit, Floracene's founders are adjacent-to-pain rather than in-pain. Asaph Kupferman spent time on the Palantir hospitals team and was an early PM at Garner Health, a physician-cost-transparency company; Jonah was founding engineer at Metriport (YC S22). Neither is an ASC physician-owner watching a surgeon reach for a $6K implant when a $2K clinically-equivalent one sits on the shelf. Drew Houston forgot his own USB stick; the Collisons had been hacking commerce since **Auctomatic** (2007). Floracene's founders read contracts and claims data for four years, which is a strong second-order proxy but not the first-order "I hit this in my own workflow" tell that made me write the check on **Dropbox** (2007).

On the hacker-genealogy axis, Floracene compounds only if Jonah's shipping matters more than Asaph's consulting-adjacent trajectory (ZS ? Medtronic ? Palantir ? Garner). Jonah founding-engineered Metriport and previously founded a fax-automation startup -- the fax-automation detail is the single most PG-flavored line in the whole dossier -- but neither founder has a public GitHub, Asaph shows up on Kaggle rather than in open source, and Jonah's last name isn't even on the YC page, which means there's no verifiable hacker trail I can pull on. Over 18 months this dimension compounds through what they ship publicly; right now there is nothing to compound.

The counterfactual for cofounder configuration: Floracene would move from a mid-rung placement to a top-rung placement if Asaph and Jonah had built something together during their Palantir hospitals overlap rather than only having overlapped. They met on the same team roughly two years pre-founding, which clears the "met at a hackathon last month" red flag I laid out in "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010), but there is no described joint artifact from that Palantir period. Two-year working overlap without a shipped joint thing is a MID rung, not a HIGH one.

My 36-month Floracene forecast: Floracene lands 4-10 independent ASC customers and sits between $400K and $1.5M ARR, at which point HST Pathways -- freshly capitalized by Novo Holdings in June 2026 and being sold toward a ~$1B valuation -- ships a clinical-equivalency module inside its existing contract-and-case-data footprint, and Floracene either gets acquired by HST or a GPO for a low-eight-figure number, or pivots into selling equivalency mapping as data to the GPOs themselves. The mechanism producing this: the schlep is real, but the schlep is defensively valuable to the incumbent that already sits on the ASC's contract data, and Floracene has no shipped-artifact moat to buy time.

The strongest opposing case is that I've made this exact mistake before -- I mentally rejected **Airbnb** (2009) as "not a business" until the cereal boxes flipped me, and the cereal boxes were not in the deck; they were on the table. Floracene may have Armstrong-grade artifacts that simply aren't in a public dossier because the founders have been heads-down building. Jonah founding-engineering Metriport plus a fax-automation-for-fertility-clinics prior startup is a genuine hacker-plus-schlep signal, and the Palantir hospitals + Garner + Metriport claims-and-contracts stack is the kind of boring domain depth nobody else in an S26 batch has. Fair. What dismantles the bull for me is that the Airbnb cereal boxes existed as revenue when I met them -- $40 x 1,000+ boxes -- and Floracene's dossier shows no equivalent revenue, no named ASC pilot, and no shipped equivalency-mapping demo I can look at. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: three signed ASC pilots (named counterparties) with paid usage by demo day, or a public SKU-equivalency tool that a surgeon can actually query. Until then, Floracene is a plausible schlep bet without an animal track, and that is where I lose money.
