# PLAN0 AI -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

PLAN0 AI is a leverage-architecture story missing its specific-knowledge core: Arash Barati's CV reads as a real estate PE economist at Drake and a Director at the Ibanez family office's Explora Ventures — buyer-side fluency for the developer/investor ICP, but not the construction-vision-model expertise that the product itself is. PLAN0 AI markets "industry standard cost estimates in 30 minutes" trained on "tens of thousands of examples," yet the human stack capable of building that engine — CTO Shervin Barati — has no verifiable public footprint. The signature here is permissionless leverage with a hollow center.

The leverage ladder runs from Twitter at the top (Ev Williams' Blogger knowledge productized into zero-marginal-cost media for hundreds of millions), through Notion in the middle (pure SaaS scaling to millions on a small team), down to the AI-wrapper MBAs from my pass example renting OpenAI's leverage. PLAN0 AI sits between Notion and the wrapper — its CV models and "quant-finance inspired deep neural networks" are real software leverage, not a thin GPT-4 call, but the forecasting layer is a contested commodity: Togal at $299/seat, Kreo at $35/month, Beam at 2-3 day turnaround, and Provision already $7M-funded on the identical "AI co-pilots for pre-construction estimates" wedge. PLAN0 AI has code leverage; it does not have *unique* code leverage.

The access-democratization ladder runs from AngelList and CoinList at the top — products that opened structurally gatekept capital flows to populations of millions — through Notion in the middle (tools for builders, modest democratization), down to the incremental-tool-in-served-market floor. PLAN0 AI sits near the bottom rung. Construction estimating is already served by Togal, Beam, Kreo, ALICE, Ediphi, and Provision; PLAN0 AI compresses a 2-week task into 30 minutes for the same buyers — developers, GCs, investors — who already pay estimators today. There is no new population getting access to something previously locked away. Speed is consumer surplus; it is not democratization in the sense that produced AngelList.

Specific knowledge sits between the AI-wrapper floor and the Notion mid-rung: Arash Barati's Drake Real Estate Partners statistical-modeling background gives him real specific knowledge about *how investors underwrite* construction costs, which is why the "Bloomberg of construction" framing is coherent — but the company's core engine is computer vision over architectural plans, and there is no evidence that either Barati possesses non-replicable expertise in plan-parsing CV. Compare to Aravind Srinivas at Perplexity, whose Google Brain and OpenAI years *were* the search engine. Principal authenticity sits closer to the AI-wrapper floor than to Sahil Lavingia at Gumroad: a finance professional pivoting into construction software during a YC batch is not a founder who couldn't stop building this — it reads as opportunity identification, not obsession. Absence of a verifiable Shervin Barati public profile is the diagnostic detail; principals leave traces.

Team-to-value leverage is the cleanest dimension: 5 people, YC-lean, AI-first by description — Midjourney's 163-person / $500M revenue ratio is the high rung, Notion the mid, headcount-heavy services the floor. PLAN0 AI lands between Notion and Midjourney by team size alone, though without revenue evidence the placement is structural rather than demonstrated.

The 36-month forecast: PLAN0 AI gets acquired by Procore, Autodesk, or CoStar for $25-60M as a feature acqui-hire, or stalls under $2M ARR as Provision's $7M war chest and Togal's existing distribution capture the preconstruction-AI wedge. The mechanism is the missing leverage hierarchy — code leverage without unique specific knowledge in a category where the data owners (Autodesk holds plan files, CoStar holds cost data, Procore holds project data) can substitute by shipping the feature themselves, exactly the dynamic that protects incumbents whenever the founder's edge is buyer-fluency rather than productized expertise.

The bull case to steel-man: Arash Barati's Explora Ventures and Drake roles place him inside the developer/PE buyer's office in a way that Provision's and Togal's founders likely are not, and "Bloomberg of construction" is a genuine compounding-data thesis if PLAN0 AI's localized cost database becomes the reference feed cited in underwriting models. That is the Wish-exception structure — leverage substituting for specific knowledge — and Wish collapsed 95% from its IPO peak, which is precisely why I treat that override as a documented mistake rather than a precedent. PLAN0 AI lacks Wish's marketplace network effects to compensate. The condition that flips my verdict: a signed contract with a top-25 GC or a top-10 real estate developer disclosed alongside verifiable construction-ML credentials for Shervin Barati. Without one of those, this is consumer surplus, not a moat.
