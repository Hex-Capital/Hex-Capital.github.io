# Taiga -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Taiga is what happens when two Penn undergrads with physician parents look at a $63B RCM market in 2026 and conclude the unlock is GPT-flavored claim scrubbing — a perfectly reasonable inference that ~$400M of competing capital (Candid Health $99.5M, Adonis $95M, Akasa $205M) already drew before the Spring 2026 batch opened. The dimensional signature on Taiga is highest on capital efficiency (percentage-of-collections services revenue actually meters itself) and lowest on founder-as-brand (Nanda Guntupalli and Adam Wax have no Twitter, no GitHub, no public writing on coding accuracy or payer adversarial behavior). That high/low pairing tells me Taiga is a capable-services-business pretending to be a venture-scale company in a category where the venture-scale slots are filled.

The canonical 2026 YC pitch I described in my pass example — "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], four tier-1s already in" — is the LOW rung for non-consensus pricing, and Taiga sits one notch above it only because medical billing is slightly less crowded than legal-AI or sales-AI. Solana at $0.05 is the HIGH rung because the entire crypto sleeve had been written off; Taiga is the inverse — Candid Health raised a GenAI-themed round in February 2025 explicitly to do "touchless claims," Adonis posted 4x revenue growth in 2025, and YC partner Nicolas Dessaigne is shepherding the batch directly into the same wave. There is no quiet category here, no fund that publicly quit AI-RCM, no price discount I can find. Taiga's 98% touchless self-reported number is not differentiation from this field; it is the table-stakes claim every entrant in the sleeve is making, which is exactly the consensus-heat signal I de-rate.

John Fish and Marina Mogilko are on the cap table because the founder *is* the asset — owned audience, zero CAC, decades of compounding earning power. Taiga's dossier is the photographic negative: no Twitter accounts located for either co-founder, no GitHub, no LinkedIn engagement signal retrievable, no newsletter, no Discord, no specialty-medical-society relationship. Adam Wax and Nanda Guntupalli grew up around their fathers' practices, which is real founder-market context but is not distribution — it does not put a single independent practice on the phone with them that wasn't already reachable by Adonis's outbound team. The Slow Creator Fund thesis does not rescue this and was never meant to.

On capital structure, percentage-of-collections billing is one of the few healthcare-ops business models that actually self-funds — collections come in, fee comes out, no Stripe checkout to engineer, no enterprise sales cycle to underwrite. Over 18 months that compounds if Taiga can hold ~5-8% net take rate against a book of 50-100 independent practices doing $1-3M each in collections; the burn curve looks more like a staffing agency with AI gross margins than a software cap table that needs five more rounds. The structural problem is not the unit economics — it is that Candid and Adonis will price-compete down-market the moment Taiga shows pull, and a venture round priced to a SaaS comparable will not survive that gravity.

The counterfactual on founder conviction: for Taiga to flip a tier on dimension 3, I would need to find a Substack from Adam Wax in 2023 arguing why independent practices specifically — not multi-site groups, not health systems — are the missed segment in AI-RCM, with a non-obvious mechanism (e.g., payer-mix asymmetry, credentialing schlep arbitrage, single-physician override liability). What is in the dossier instead is "friends since 3rd grade, parents are physicians, Penn." That is a real founder-market fit story and I do not want to flatten it — but it is conviction-by-biography, not the missionary public artifact I underwrite. The "what if they're right?" inversion needs a "they" with a public claim; I cannot run the test on silence.

Healthcare appears in my portfolio through Ro, PillPack, Truepill — but that sleeve is specifically prescription-rail / 50-state licensing complexity, where capital intensity *is* the moat (the Ro known exception). Taiga is RCM, which is a different animal: payer integration is annoying but not state-licensed, EHR partnerships are commercial deals not regulatory ones, and the moat decays as soon as a competitor wires up the same 50 payers. Single anchor — Candid Health already did this against a 200+ org customer base. Taiga is in the broader healthcare-ops zone, not the prescription-rail zone, and the sleeve credit is partial.

My 36-month forecast on Taiga: acqui-hire by Candid Health, Adonis, or Tebra in the $5-20M range as a customer-book and engineering team pickup, with Adam Wax and Nanda Guntupalli rolling into a director-level RCM-product seat — the modal outcome for a second-mover services-shaped company in a sleeve where three competitors raised nine figures before this round priced. The mechanism is simple: independent-practice TAM is real but slower to consolidate than multi-site, Taiga runs out of differentiated story by the Series A starting line, and the most rational buyer is the incumbent whose down-market gap Taiga just spent two years mapping.

The bull case that should sting: Candid Health and Adonis explicitly do not target single-doctor independent practices because the ACV is too small for their sales motion, which leaves Taiga an actual unattended segment — and the Ro known exception applies if you squint, because payer-by-payer behavioral fingerprinting *could* become a regulatory-adjacent moat the way state-by-state prescription licensing did for Ro. I take that seriously, and the dismantle is this: Ro had a literal legal barrier no software could route around; Taiga has a commercial integration list any competent ML team can replicate in eighteen months, which is why three competitors already have. The verdict flips if Taiga shows me a signed pilot with a specialty society — AAFP, APA, AOA — that gives them a category-exclusive distribution channel into independent practices at zero CAC. Absent that, this is a pass on price and sleeve, not on team.
