## The Idea

- **Problem:** R&D teams' knowledge is fragmented across specialist tools (Benchling, SOLIDWORKS, Weights & Biases, Isaac Sim, GitHub, SAP), plus decisions and conversations "in people's heads," leading to duplicated experiments and repeated mistakes (osseus.ai website; YC company page).
- **Approach:** A permissioned memory layer that ingests 30+ specialist tools and preserves "how work evolved: what was tried, what failed, why decisions were made, and who holds the relevant context," plus per-person AI agents accessible via desktop app, Slack, and WhatsApp (osseus.ai website).
- **Differentiation:** Named category peers Glean and Guru index current-state content across generalist SaaS (Slack, Google Workspace, M365) (Glean.com; getguru.com); Osseus differs by ingesting R&D-specific systems (Benchling, SOLIDWORKS, Isaac Sim, W&B) and by preserving experiment/decision history rather than only current-state retrieval (osseus.ai feature list).
- **Business Model:** No pricing published on osseus.ai; [Inferred]: per-seat enterprise SaaS given per-person "personal AI agent" architecture and permission-scoped retrieval (osseus.ai product page).
- **TAM/SAM:** Enterprise Laboratory Informatics market USD 6.6B in 2026, projected USD 19.8B by 2036 at 7% CAGR (openpr.com / market research release, 2026); broader Knowledge Management Software market USD 16.22B–26.4B in 2026 depending on source (Fortune Business Insights; Mordor Intelligence, 2026).
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Founder-led enterprise sales — the site's primary CTA is a "founders intro" Cal.com booking link (osseus.cal.com/founders/osseus-intro), and no self-serve signup or job postings for GTM roles were found (osseus.ai; YC jobs page).
