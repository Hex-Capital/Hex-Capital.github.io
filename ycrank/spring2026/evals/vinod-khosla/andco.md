# Andco -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Andco is a competent-serial-founder workflow automation play in a fragmented vertical -- Ryan Xue and Michael Lemm already ran the Layup (YC W23 → Whatfix) loop, and now they are using that earned execution to compress 8 hours of personal injury staff time per case. The dimensional signature is unambiguous: Andco's highest axis is founder learning rate (prior YC exit, second at-bat with the same co-founder), and its lowest axis is consequence magnitude (the success-state is "personal injury firms run leaner," not "tort system rebuilt"). That pairing -- high execution credibility, low civilizational stakes -- is the exact profile my framework was built to pass on, regardless of how clean the founder résumé reads.

On consequence magnitude, **OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung because the success-state collapses the cost curve of cognitive labor across $15T of GDP; Andco's success-state at maximum ambition is that 50,435 US personal injury firms (IBISWorld 2025) save 24 hours per case intake-to-demand. The shared mechanism would be "labor displacement," but the analogy fails on scale: Andco's TAM ceiling is the $61.7B US PI legal services revenue line, of which the addressable software-services slice is a small fraction, and no incumbent regime disappears if Andco wins -- Filevine still sells seats, insurance carriers still pay claims, plaintiffs still sue. The dossier itself reveals the ceiling: "3x attorney efficiency" is an optimization frame, not a regime-collapse frame, and that is the **exact** language pattern my Pass Example flagged on the "GPT-4 summarizes sales calls" pitch.

The LOW-rung failure mechanism on technical/capital risk is the sales-call-summarizer pass: standard software-only execution risk, any Tier-1 generalist can write the check, no science requiring my capital depth. Does that pattern appear in Andco's dossier? Yes -- the $12M oversubscribed pre-seed led by YC and A.Capital is consensus-VC money, not Khosla-shaped money. Andco's "moat" is multi-channel agent retrieval across fax/mail/voice plus Filevine sync; that is integration engineering, not a falsifiable scientific miracle. The closest thing to genuine technical risk is reliable voice-agent calls to insurance adjusters, but EvenUp's $385M raise and $2B valuation (Fortune, Oct 2025) already prove the foundation-model substrate is investable at consensus terms. When EvenUp can raise $385M on adjacent agentic legal work, Andco is not capital-hostile -- it is capital-crowded.

Founder-defies-expert-consensus -- mechanism analysis: this dimension compounds when a founder has publicly committed to a position their field rejects, because that conviction shapes hiring, roadmap, and the willingness to absorb 18 months of dismissal before validation. Andco's dossier shows the inverse compounding -- Michael Lemm is Harvard Law + Kirkland & Ellis, which is paradigmatic expert-continuity, and the GTM story ("200 boxes of donuts" via DoorDash Dasher cosplay) is charming hustle, not a contrarian technical or market thesis. Over 18 months that profile produces better sales execution, not a defended moat against EvenUp extending upstream.

Learning rate -- counterfactual: for Andco to flip from MID to HIGH on this axis, the dossier would need to describe a specific Layup-era pivot with stated reasoning -- a "we were wrong about X at Layup, here is what we learned and how Andco's scope reflects that update." What the dossier actually gives me is a clean exit to Whatfix and a re-up with the same co-founder, which is execution credibility, not the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C. The exit is real and I weight it -- two YC cycles with the same partner is non-trivial -- but it is not a documented thesis update, so Andco sits between MID and HIGH and closer to MID.

Required-capital profile -- single-anchor parallel to **Cognition** (2024, $175M Series B on AI software engineers): Cognition's roadmap requires sustained foundation-model post-training spend and an agent harness that justifies nine-figure checks because the engineering displacement scope is the entire developer labor market. Andco's roadmap is per-carrier and per-portal integration work plus CMS connectors, which scales with sales headcount and engineering hires, not training compute -- the $12M pre-seed is appropriate for the scope, which is precisely why my capital is over-resourced here. Joe Montana / Liquid 2, SV Angel, Immad Akhund, Kevin Hartz -- that is a beautiful angel list, and it is exactly the cap table that signals "this does not need Khosla."

36-month forecast: Andco reaches $8-20M ARR serving mid-market plaintiff PI firms and is acquired by Filevine, Litify, or EvenUp for $80-200M between 2027 and 2029 -- a clean Layup-shaped outcome at one order of magnitude larger, generating real returns for A.Capital and YC and a rounding error against the $3.5B Khosla Ventures vehicle (TechCrunch, 2025-02-24). The mechanism producing this: a competent team with prior YC exit, strong angel signal, defensible upstream-retrieval positioning vs. EvenUp's downstream demand-package focus, in a fragmented buyer market where 50,435 firms (IBISWorld 2025) ensure no winner-takes-all dynamic but also no civilizational outcome.

The strongest bull case is the **Slash** (2024) Known Exception: YC-pedigreed, documented founder learning rate, and David Lieb's public X endorsement plus Joe Montana's angel check could argue Andco deserves the same consequence-magnitude override I granted Slash for the $100M Series C. The bull case stings because the founder-quality signal is genuinely high and Lemm's Kirkland background materially de-risks the UPL/HIPAA regulatory surface that would scare generalist VCs. It loses on the falsifiable test: Slash's override was a *specific* publicly told pivot (the Kanye moment) demonstrating extreme thesis flexibility, while Andco's "donut box" hustle is GTM grit, not a thesis update -- and consumer business banking has a 100x larger consequence ceiling than US plaintiff PI workup. **One verifiable condition would flip my verdict**: if Andco demonstrates within 12 months that its multi-channel agent stack generalizes beyond PI to mass-tort intake, healthcare prior-auth, or insurance-claim adjudication at carrier scale -- i.e., the platform is actually a horizontal "agent over fax/mail/voice/legacy-portals" substrate masquerading as a PI tool -- then the consequence-magnitude score climbs into OpenAI-adjacent labor-displacement territory and I write a follow-on check at Series A.
