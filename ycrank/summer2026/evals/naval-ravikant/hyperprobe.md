# HyperProbe -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

HyperProbe is a competent founder-market-fit bet on a code-leverage-*adjacent* category that ships through capital-and-labor-leverage GTM -- Shailendra Singh spent a decade building HyperTest into a $2.13M-raised API-testing product with Nykaa and Groww as logos, and HyperProbe is his second bite at the same developer-tools sale. The signature is a founder who clearly earned the right to build this, pointed at a wedge whose distribution architecture is a Calendly link at calendly.com/shailendra-hyperprobe/30min. That is the whole tension.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung on leverage because media is permissionless -- anyone posts, the platform charges nothing, and the marginal cost of replication is zero, which is why the 400x arrived. HyperProbe's leverage architecture is not that. The dossier names no GitHub repo, no open-source runtime agent, no Cursor marketplace plugin, no free tier -- the only stated distribution vector is a founder-led demo booking on Calendly for backend engineering teams whose prod services must accept a runtime agent claiming <1% CPU overhead. That is enterprise-permissioned distribution wearing dev-tools clothing. Replit shipped Repl.it as free-tier code-in-browser for a decade before the $1.16B round; HyperProbe launched an 8-person team into a Series-B-funded competitor field with a sales motion, not an open-source community. The "agent-native probe insertion" primitive could have been permissionless -- it isn't.

Perplexity is the LOW rung Naval already forgave on marketplace mechanics because criterion #1, #3, and category-timing cleared emphatically. HyperProbe cannot make the same trade. HyperProbe is single-sided B2B observability SaaS: the Nth customer's runtime snapshots do not make the (N-1)th customer's debugging faster except through a private model-training flywheel the dossier only *infers*. No network. No compounding user-added value. And unlike Perplexity, HyperProbe does not clear criterion #1 at top quartile to buy the waiver -- the exception precedent doesn't extend here.

On specific knowledge: Shailendra Singh's HyperTest years -- founded 2015, $1.5M seed from Better Capital in December 2022, shipped to Urbancompany, Nykaa, Groww -- is the real signal in this dossier. Ten years of shipping a developer-testing product to paying B2B customers is the non-trainable knowledge criterion #3 demands, and it maps directly onto the runtime-instrumentation sale. He is not an ex-FAANG PM who spotted a TAM slide. HyperProbe's founder discovered debugging pain as an operator, not as an analyst. This is the load-bearing reason the score is not lower.

On market and pivot room, HyperProbe sits inside a $3.35B observability tool market with Datadog's own analyst materials sketching $58-62B by 2026 -- the market absorbs pivots. The problem is that HyperProbe already burned one pivot converting HyperTest's API-testing product into a runtime-probing product for YC S26, and the Urbancompany/Nykaa/Groww logos do not transfer. The next pivot inside the same seed runway would need to happen against Lightrun's $70M April 2025 Series B and Dynatrace-owned Rookout -- less room than the raw TAM suggests.

The founder-trio read is clean but not magnetic. Shailendra ran HyperTest for a decade without a public integrity flag, exited engineering and product leadership at OYO Rooms, and volunteers no honesty-signaling in the dossier -- which passes the "if someone talks about how honest they are, they're probably dishonest" filter by silence. The 10-year-partner question is where HyperProbe stalls: nothing in the dossier -- no essays, no organic X following, no open-source track -- creates the pull that made Stack Overflow's Atwood-Spolsky a decade-partner read on sight.

36-month forecast: HyperProbe reaches $1-3M ARR selling to mid-market backend engineering teams by mid-2028 and is acqui-hired by Datadog or Dynatrace for under $50M -- the Rookout comparable was $33.9M in July 2023, and HyperProbe enters an already-consolidated field with worse timing. Alternative branch: it stalls against Lightrun's war chest inside 18 months and the team returns to founder-mode.

The bull case that stings: coding-agent adoption is a genuine step-change, and if Cursor, Claude Code, or Codex embed HyperProbe as the default runtime-evidence primitive their agents call, HyperProbe's distribution flips from Calendly-permissioned to agent-permissionless overnight -- the leverage architecture inverts, and Shailendra's HyperTest execution muscle finishes the multi-runtime engineering (Node, TS, Java, Scala, Kotlin) faster than Lightrun ships an agent-IDE-native workflow. That is the Anchorage-shaped override where a category-timing call rescues a permissioned-architecture bet. The dossier does not support it. HyperProbe's stated GTM is demo-gated; no coding-agent partnership is named, no plugin ships in Cursor or Claude Code's extension surface, and Lightrun's $110M and Dynatrace's owned IP mean the 12-month wedge is not the 3-5 year runway that made **MetaStable** (2014) and **OpenSea** (2018) work. Verdict flips to Invest if HyperProbe ships an open-source runtime agent on GitHub with organic contributions and a Cursor/Claude Code marketplace plugin with visible install counts before end of 2026 -- until then, pass.
