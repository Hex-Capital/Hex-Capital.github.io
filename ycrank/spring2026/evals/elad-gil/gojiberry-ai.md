# Gojiberry AI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is a high-velocity SMB AI SDR at $2.5M ARR in 10 months and 35% MoM, fighting for share inside a category where Clay just closed $100M at a $3.1B valuation on ~$100M ARR -- that is the signature: real revenue velocity stapled to an anti-index distribution position. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant, Román Czerny, and Dylan Teixeira have shipped and exited before (CoCo.ai, Edusign), so the curve is plausibly the "just working" kind rather than the "false signal" kind (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). The question I'm running is whether $99/mo SMB outbound, sold against Clay and Apollo, is an infrastructure position or a share fight, and the dossier reads as the latter.

The closest template on revenue velocity is **Harvey** (2023) -- the "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" pattern where the curve does the persuading and you pay up because the curve is real. Gojiberry AI shows $0 → $2.5M ARR in 10 months, profitable, 2,000+ customers, 35% MoM (YC company description, 2026), which at pre-seed is closer to "just working" than to anything I'd call a false signal. Where Gojiberry AI diverges from the Harvey rung is logo composition: Harvey's curve was anchored by named AmLaw firms; Gojiberry AI's curve is 2,000+ SMB seats at $99/mo with no named enterprise logo in the dossier and a customer testimonial that is self-reported ("2–5x more positive replies"). That delta matters because SMB at $99 churns faster than enterprise at $50K ACV, and "profitable in 10 months" at SMB volume is also the shape an arbitrage-on-LinkedIn product takes right before the channel tightens.

The **Lyft** Series C pass mechanism was structural: in a winner-take-all category, #2 captures a fraction of #1's economics no matter how good execution is (20VC interview). The relevant question for Gojiberry AI isn't whether AI SDR is winner-take-all -- it's whether the *data-and-workflow* layer of outbound is, and Clay's $204M raised, $100M ARR, $3.1B valuation suggests it's already consolidating around them and Apollo. Gojiberry AI's stated moat -- a "cross-customer learning loop" on signals and messages (YC description, 2026) -- is exactly the moat Clay and Apollo have more of by orders of magnitude, sitting on first-party enrichment graphs Gojiberry AI does not have. This is not an index position on the AI-SDR category; this is a share fight against funded incumbents who have already shipped agentic outbound. Same Lyft logic: a worse version of the obvious bet unless there's a distribution wedge I'm not seeing in the dossier.

On the catalyst dimension, the "why now" the dossier offers is LLM cost/quality crossing the threshold for autonomous reply handling in 2024–2025 and the AI SDR market at $5.81B growing 31.9% CAGR (Research and Markets, 2026) -- a real catalyst, but the universal AI catalyst every cohort batch is now riding, not a specific re-opening peers misread. Compounded over 18 months, a generic catalyst means competitive entry is unbounded: every Spring 2026 YC batch has an AI SDR, every Series B incumbent has shipped one, and the catalyst itself does not create scarcity of supply. This is the inverse of the **Anduril** (2022) Ukraine catalyst, which was specific, recent, and ignored by peers. Gojiberry AI gets credit for being in a real market, not for spotting a re-opening.

Founder execution rhythm is where Gojiberry AI earns its strongest signal that isn't revenue: Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Román Czerny built and sold CoCo.ai (~€500K ARR), Dylan Teixeira built and sold Edusign (multi-million ARR), and the documented pivot from a failed prior product to $33K in 4 months to $2.5M ARR is the kind of iteration trace that the **Perplexity** (2022) pattern rewards -- shipping artifacts, not pedigree. The counterfactual that would flip this dimension to a top-rung placement is a named US enterprise logo signed in the last 90 days at $50K+ ACV; without that, the shipping rhythm is impressive at SMB scale but doesn't yet prove the team can move upmarket into the ACV ranges where the Clay/Apollo fight is actually won.

On the single-miracle test, Gojiberry AI is cleaner than most -- the miracle (LLMs write outbound that gets replies) has already happened, and the remaining work is GTM, integrations, and channel access. The wrinkle is that LinkedIn platform dependency is a quiet second miracle: the entire intent-signal scraping + outreach motion runs through LinkedIn (SyncGTM review, 2026), and LinkedIn has historically tightened third-party automation when revenue threatens. That's not a science-project second miracle, but it is an "incumbent platform tolerates you" miracle that I treat as real probability mass on the failure side.

My 36-month forecast for Gojiberry AI: plateau at $15–25M ARR in the SMB AI SDR tier, then get acquired by Clay, Apollo, or HubSpot for 3–5x revenue as Clay's agentic outbound product compresses the differentiation, with a 25% tail probability of a LinkedIn ToS enforcement event that cuts ARR in half before the acquisition prints. The mechanism: SMB velocity is real but ceiling-bound at this price point and channel, and the index leader (Clay) has both the data graph and the capital to absorb the wedge Gojiberry AI is currently exploiting.

The bull case that actually stings: $2.5M ARR in 10 months at 35% MoM while profitable is the "just working" signature, and I'm the one who wrote that distinction (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) -- if I pass on Gojiberry AI at pre-seed and they ride the curve to $20M ARR in 18 months with the founder team that has already exited twice, I will have written exactly the kind of "consensus-paying" deal I claim to love, except late. The dismantling evidence is the index test: Clay at $3.1B with $100M ARR is the structural endpoint Gojiberry AI's curve is pointing at, not away from, and "profitable SMB SaaS in a category with a funded $3B leader" is a Lyft Series C, not a Harvey 2023. The one condition that flips my verdict to invest: a named Fortune 1000 logo at $50K+ ACV signed in the next 6 months, proving Gojiberry AI moves upmarket out of the Clay collision zone. Until that prints, Gojiberry AI is a respect-the-founders pass.
