# RASPIRE -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

RASPIRE is a domain-credentialed enterprise security team — Kareem Selim and Hassan Mostafa, years inside MENA bank and fintech app-security work — wrapped around a commodity primitive (post-compilation mobile RASP) that Appdome already markets as "no-code" with $39.6M in the bank. The signature here is decent category-adjacency married to zero founder-distribution surface, which is the profile of a competent regional security vendor, not a venture moment.

**Sublime Security** is the ladder rung I keep coming back to on category: pre-consensus enterprise security where the incumbents were stuck in SOC-SaaS late-stage and the seed window was genuinely open. RASPIRE is in the same neighborhood — mobile runtime protection is unsexy to the AI-agent cohort and tier-1s are not knife-fighting for it — but the analogy breaks on the consolidation curve. Sublime entered before the wave; RASPIRE is entering a G2-cataloged category with 25+ named vendors, where Appdome ($39.6M raised) is already running the exact "no-code binary fusion" pitch and Zimperium ($60.1M raised) bundles zShield + zDefend into MAPS. That's not pre-consensus; that's late-cycle me-too with an AI veneer, and the dossier offers no detection-accuracy or telemetry-volume number that would let me underwrite the "AI-driven autonomous response" moat as more than positioning.

**John Fish / Bookshelved** is the failure mechanism for the founder-as-brand dimension — that check only worked because Fish brought a measurable owned audience onto the cap table before the round. RASPIRE inverts that pattern completely: no founder Twitter, no GitHub repos, no published writing on RASP failure modes, no Selim essay on why Appdome's SDK channel structurally can't ship zero-integration, nothing. LinkedIn at 500+ connections is the corporate-rolodex default, not distribution. The Black Hat MEA 2025 booth and the Sadq partnership are conference-and-channel sales, which is fine for a regional enterprise vendor but is exactly the cost structure the Slow Creator Fund was built to avoid funding.

On price (Dimension 1), the dossier is silent on round size, and silence on round dynamics is itself the signal — I default down. RASPIRE's pre-YC cap table — 500 Global, Sanabil 500 MENA Seed Accelerator, Plus VC — is a MENA-accelerator stack, not a tier-1 SV mark, which actually helps the non-consensus read slightly; this is not the oversubscribed "five tier-1s already in" pitch. But mobile security at the YC P26 post-money standard is not Solana at $0.05 either, and without a disclosed discount to the batch median I can't grade this above the lower-middle.

The capital structure question (Dimension 2) compounds badly over 18 months in this specific shape. RASPIRE protects apps with 20M+ end users — credible — but the financing instrument on offer is a standard B2B SaaS SAFE into a category where the next two tacks of the regatta require US enterprise logos, SOC2/FedRAMP-adjacent compliance build-out, and a salesforce hire to escape MENA concentration. That's a five-round path, not a default-alive-in-24-months path, and no Teamshares-style structural twist (revenue share against protected-MAU, royalty against fraud-prevented dollars) is on the table to make the cap-table arithmetic interesting.

Founder conviction (Dimension 3) — counterfactual: if Selim had even one public essay diagnosing why post-compilation binary instrumentation is structurally undefendable by Appdome's partner-channel business model, I'd flip this dimension a full tier and probably take the meeting. The dossier shows years of domain time at banks and fintechs and a CyberTalents speaker slot, which is real founder-market fit, but the "what if they're right?" inversion requires an opinionated non-obvious belief I can read before the pitch, and there's nothing to read.

**Forecast — 36 months**: RASPIRE reaches $3–5M ARR concentrated in Saudi and Egyptian banking customers off the Sadq channel, fails to clear $10M ARR in US logos, and gets acqui-hired by Appdome or Zimperium for under $50M — channel-conflict pricing, not strategic. The mechanism: commodity primitives plus regional concentration plus no founder-distribution flywheel collapses into a regional vendor outcome, which is exactly what the incumbents pay defensively to absorb.

The steel-man bear-flip: 20M protected end-users is real distribution that most YC P26 security seeds would kill for, and Appdome's 400+ defense library is a feature-bloat moat with channel-partner dependence that genuinely cannot ship a true zero-integration product without cannibalizing its services revenue — RASPIRE could be the only credible bid for that gap. Saudi Vision 2030 procurement is a real tailwind, not a slide. I take this seriously, and it's the closest the dossier comes to a Ro-style "regulatory complexity is the moat" override. But Ro had 50-state prescription rails as the structural barrier; RASPIRE has Mordor's $2.59B RASP TAM and a G2 category page listing 25 vendors doing variations of the same thing, which is a competitive market, not a regulated rail. **Flip condition**: a disclosed US enterprise logo at >$250K ACV not sourced through the MENA channel, plus a founder-published technical post articulating why Appdome's architecture structurally cannot match the integration story — either alone moves this to a meeting; neither is in the dossier today.
