## The Idea

- **Problem:** Physical AI / humanoid model developers cannot source enough real-world egocentric video, 3D scans, and multimodal capture from inside industrial and residential environments, and today buy demonstration data from vendors like Scale AI or Micro1 (YC company page; MIT Technology Review, Apr 2026).
- **Approach:** Praxis embeds inside publicly listed and unicorn-scale conglomerates and uses their existing 60k workers across 5 continents and 150+ environment types as the capture workforce, converting each host company into a data vendor (YC company page).
- **Differentiation:** Scale AI recruits its own data recorders and runs a San Francisco R&D lab (TSG Invest; Sacra); Micro1 ships Ray-Ban glasses to gig workers (Forbes, Dec 2025); Encord operates a data infrastructure/annotation platform (Encord press, Feb 2026) — [Inferred]: Praxis's embed-in-conglomerate model differs by locking in captive worker fleets and proprietary industrial environments rather than gig or lab capture.
- **Business Model:** No public pricing data found; [Inferred]: revenue-share or per-hour data-sale to frontier labs and humanoid companies, with the host conglomerate as the data-supply partner, consistent with the "turns every company into a data vendor" tagline (YC one-liner).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for embedded egocentric-capture services; adjacent signal: robotics companies spend >$100M/year each buying real-world data from vendors like Micro1 (Forbes, Dec 2025); robotics sector raised $55.8B in 2026 YTD (KraneShares, 2026 via search snippet).
- **GTM / Distribution:** YC company page states Praxis is already "embedded within publicly listed and unicorn-scale conglomerates" reaching 60k workers across 5 continents and 150+ environment types (YC page); [Inferred]: sells captured datasets to frontier labs and humanoid companies as the buy-side.

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Claimed access to 60k workers across 5 continents and 150+ environment types via conglomerate embeds (YC page) — a proprietary supply channel not accessible via public gig marketplaces.
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Exclusive multi-year conglomerate agreements plus accumulated multimodal dataset scale could produce a data-network effect; unproven because contract terms, exclusivity, and dataset volumes are not publicly disclosed.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbent data vendors (Scale AI, Micro1) built gig-worker capture pipelines and San Francisco R&D labs (TSG Invest; Forbes, Dec 2025) — pivoting to embed-inside-conglomerates would conflict with their existing gig-supply operations and enterprise-labeling sales channels.
- **Commoditization risk:** Any well-capitalized data vendor (Scale AI at $29B valuation with Meta 49% stake, Encord with $110M total raised, Micro1 at $2.5B offer valuation) could sign conglomerate partnerships (TSG Invest; Encord press, Feb 2026; Forbes, Dec 2025).

## Key Risks

- **Customer concentration on frontier labs / humanoid vendors:** Buy-side is a small set of well-funded labs (Physical Intelligence, Generalist AI, Figure, humanoid companies) already served by Scale AI (TSG Invest; Sacra Figure AI profile); loss of any single account could be material. No mitigation disclosed.
- **Competitive substitution by capitalized incumbents:** Scale AI ($29B), Encord ($110M raised), and Micro1 ($2.5B offer) can outbid on conglomerate partnerships or exclusivity (TSG Invest; Encord press, Feb 2026; Forbes, Dec 2025). No mitigation disclosed.
- **Data-rights and conglomerate contract risk:** Model depends on host conglomerates permitting worker-captured video/3D scans of proprietary facilities to be resold; [Inferred]: labor, privacy, and IP constraints could restrict scope or pricing. No contracts publicly disclosed.
- **Name collision with prior YC company:** A separate "Praxis AI" (YC F24) builds manufacturing reasoning agents (Y Combinator launch page, Nov 2024; YC Twitter post), creating brand/search ambiguity — YC URL uses the "-2" suffix to disambiguate.
- **Website substance gap:** Praxisrobotics.io currently exposes only a tagline with no product, pricing, or team page (WebFetch, Jul 2026), limiting inbound customer discovery.
