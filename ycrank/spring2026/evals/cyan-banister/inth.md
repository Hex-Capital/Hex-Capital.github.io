# Inth -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Christopher Burns spent seven years running Everfund, a UK donation platform that moved money across 33+ countries under GDPR before he authored c15t -- so when Inth describes "engineering teams must comply with GDPR/CCPA/ePrivacy/Quebec Law 25," Burns is describing his own last decade, not a memo he read. That is the signature of Inth: a clean-governance, domain-soaked founder shipping into a category where six funded incumbents -- OneTrust at $1.144B, Transcend at $90M, DataGrail at $84.2M, Ketch at $43M, Privado at $17.5M, Osano at $19.4M -- have already trained the buyer. The founder profile is real; the lane is consensus. That pairing is the structural inverse of the bets I usually make.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before he built Anduril, and that prior-product trajectory is what made the defense pivot legible to me in 2017 -- the founder was *of* the problem. Burns clears a softer version of the same test: Everfund ($2M raised, 7-person team, payments in 33+ countries) is a real GDPR-regulated operating history, and c15t is now embedded in production at Vercel, Expo, Zed, Infisical, and Unkey with 2.6M npm downloads and ~6.5K/day. Inth diverges from the Anduril rung because Everfund is wound-down nonprofit infrastructure rather than a category-defining prior-product win, and because consent SDKs are buildable -- the shadcn/c15t fork already exists on GitHub, which means Inth has lived the problem but does not yet own a prior outcome that forces a re-rating.

The "consensus YC AI-agent darling" I pass on every batch fails my magically-weird filter because six top funds are already bidding, the deck opens with TAM, and the founders discovered the problem in a memo -- the round is priced as if the outcome is certain. Inth has only half of that failure mode. Burns is not memo-trained; he ran the GDPR business. But the category is unambiguously consensus: Transcend closed a $40M Series B in May 2024 on the exact engineering-led-DSAR thesis Inth is moving toward, and Privado has been shipping "compliance in the codebase" since their 2022 Series A with Insight and Peak XV. Inth is not laughed at the way Crusoe burning gas flares for Bitcoin was laughed at in 2018. Privacy compliance is a category investors are already paying up for, and "OSS headless SDK" is a wedge, not a secret.

c15t at 2.6M cumulative npm downloads with 6.5K/day, embedded at Vercel/Expo/Zed/Infisical/Unkey, is exactly the bottom-up developer-channel pattern that compounds over 18 months if -- and only if -- the paid SKU exists when the OSS hits critical mass; Inth's pricing page exists at inth.com/pricing but the tiers did not surface on fetch, the Inth DSAR managed platform is "in development," and the Apache 2.0 license plus the existing shadcn/c15t fork means a Ketch or Transcend can embed the runtime before Inth ever charges for it. The mechanism that makes this kind of distribution compound is the moment when free OSS users hit a regulatory audit and have to buy the managed evidence layer -- Inth has not yet shipped that layer, and Privado has.

Inth's governance is structurally clean in a way Inth's category is not: Christopher Burns is the sole founder on the YC page, Will De Ath is Head of Growth (not a 50/50 co-CEO), and the tiebreaker question that killed the GameCrush thesis and bled HQ Trivia is moot here on Day 1. That is the inverse of HQ Trivia, where the Yusupov/Kroll partnership eventually feuded the company into the ground. The cost of that cleanliness is single-founder execution risk while Inth expands from a consent SDK into a repo-scanning evidence platform -- one technical neck, two product lines, no co-founder CTO disclosed.

c15t as an artifact passes a weaker version of the Flock Safety test: I cannot photograph it in a parking lot the way I read a Wi-Fi network name at the Four Seasons, but I can npm install c15t, watch it render a banner with 89ms of consent-runtime overhead, and verify the install is live on a Vercel-shipped page. That is a real tactile loop, narrower than Pokémon Go on a phone screen but materially more observable than a B2B API whose value lives only in founder testimony. The category Inth occupies -- regulated, GDPR/CCPA/Quebec Law 25/EU AI Act -- is the "unsexy regulated" lane I funded into Vigil Labs in August 2025, just without the capital-intensity premium that makes Crusoe or SpaceX uncopyable; privacy law compounds, but consent SDKs do not require a launch pad.

My 36-month forecast for Inth: $1-3M ARR on a c15t-enterprise SKU and DSAR managed platform, followed by acquisition by Transcend or DataGrail in the $30-80M range as a developer-channel tuck-in -- a competent outcome for Burns, a non-outcome for my fund math. The mechanism is straightforward: c15t becomes the default OSS consent layer, the Inth paid layer ships late, and Privado or Transcend buys the developer relationships before Inth's evidence graph accumulates enough customer-specific data lineage to be unbuildable. Inth is the kind of company that returns the seed and validates the founder, not the kind that anchors a fund.

The strongest case against my forecast is the Sentry/Vercel/PostHog precedent: c15t already lives inside the devtool kingmakers (Vercel, Expo, Zed, Infisical, Unkey), the same companies whose own adoption certified Sentry and PostHog into category defaults, and Burns is a credible OSS maintainer who has been on Heavybit's Scaling DevTools and Open Source Ready -- the canonical OSS-go-to-market podcasts. If c15t becomes the "Sentry of consent," Inth's repo-resident scanning layer accumulates a per-customer data-lineage graph competitors cannot reconstruct retroactively, and a $30-80M tuck-in undershoots the outcome by an order of magnitude. The reason I do not buy that bull case today is that Sentry and Vercel won categories where no $1B incumbent existed; Inth is shipping into OneTrust's $1.144B raised, $5.1B-valuation moat with an Apache 2.0 license that competitors can fork without paying, and the shadcn/c15t fork is the leading indicator that the wedge is replicable. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: Inth publishes a paid Inth DSAR SKU with at least one named enterprise design partner -- Vercel, Expo, or a Fortune 500 -- inside the next two quarters, converting the 2.6M npm downloads into a contracted revenue line before Transcend's $40M war chest closes the lane.
