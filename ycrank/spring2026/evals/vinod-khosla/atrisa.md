# Atrisa (formerly Refortifai) -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Atrisa is a mid-batch pivot from "Runtime security for AI agents" (Refortifai) to an analog-circuit-design agent, and that pairing — a documented pivot sitting on top of a consequence-mundane EDA productivity story — is the signature. The highest-dimension placement is the pivot itself; the lowest is consequence magnitude. The Atrisa success-state is "analog designers ship a chip faster inside Cadence and Synopsys" — and that is a productivity gain inside an existing $20.78B EDA regime, not a regime collapse. When I read that the YC partner is Nicolas Dessaigne, the team is three, and Khosla Ventures already backs **Astrus** ($13.3M raised, BetaKit) in the adjacent analog AI category, I already know what this evaluation is.

On consequence, **OpenAI** (2019) sits at the top of my ladder because the success-state was civilizational — AGI rewrites the $15 trillion labor base I keep citing in Fortune 2026-03-06. Atrisa shares the surface vocabulary — "agent," "internalized intuition" — but the load-bearing mechanism is absent. Atrisa's maximum-ambition state is "analog IC teams build slightly faster" inside a market MarketsandMarkets pegs at $20.78B growing 8.1%. The economic mechanism that breaks if Atrisa works is the seat-license revenue at Synopsys (~31%), Cadence (~30%), and Siemens (~13%) — and Cadence already absorbed that disruption by acquiring ChipStack in February 2026. Atrisa diverges from the OpenAI rung exactly where it matters: there is no civilizational variable being moved. This is a faster-horse pitch dressed as an agent pitch.

The low rung on technical and capital risk is the pass-example sales-call summarizer — a pure LLM wrapper with no science miracle. Atrisa's launch claim that it "internalized the intuition of an analog engineer" is interesting framing, but the field already has Astrus ($13.3M), Quilter ($10M Series A from Benchmark), Silimate, and the now-acquired ChipStack (~$7M) attempting variants of the same build. When four funded teams plus the three incumbents are all reaching the same agentic-EDA ridge inside eighteen months, that is the definition of a build any Tier-1 generalist can underwrite. I am over-resourced for Atrisa. The Lux Capital "looming labor crisis in chip design" framing tells me the market sees the labor displacement — that means consensus money will fund it, which is the precise inverse of the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) condition where I led $115M because nobody else would touch the physics.

Founder-defiance: Atman Kar is an RTL Digital Design Engineer at Texas Instruments out of BITS Pilani Goa — that is exactly the "career domain expert continuing the same playbook" profile I flagged in my TED Talk citing the Berkeley 28,000-prediction expert study. Compounding mechanism over 18 months: a domain-expert founder team in a category where Cadence is rolling out the ChipStack AI Super Agent press-released February 2026 has no asymmetric read on incumbent product velocity — they will be benchmarked against the EDA suite their customers already pay $20.78B for. There is no public position where any of the three Atrisa founders contradicted the analog-design field. No "instigator" stance from my TED Talk "12 Good Instigators" framing appears anywhere in the Atrisa dossier.

Counterfactual on founder learning rate: for Atrisa to clear the Slash (2024) bar that justified my $100M Series C, the Refortifai-to-Atrisa pivot would need a written thesis update — "we were wrong about runtime AI security because X, and analog EDA is the right problem because Y." What the dossier shows is a domain-jump from AI security to analog silicon inside a single YC batch with no thematic continuity. Slash's Kanye pivot stayed inside fintech; Atrisa's pivot crossed two unrelated technical stacks mid-cohort. That is thrashing-shaped, not learning-shaped, until proven otherwise. The score flips a tier only if Sayan Mitra publishes the reasoning chain that connects Refortifai's failure to Atrisa's thesis — that document does not exist in the dossier.

On required-capital, the single-anchor parallel is **OpenAI** (2019) where the $50M first check was justified because the roadmap required foundation-model compute that no syndicate would assemble. Atrisa is the opposite shape: a 3-person team with 0 open job postings, software economics, sold per-seat into existing semiconductor design teams. The Atrisa capital ask will be a normal pre-seed YC round, and the path to $1M ARR is the standard EDA enterprise-sales motion. I am the wrong investor at the wrong stage for this profile — any generalist seed fund can write this check, which by my own TechCrunch Disrupt 2013 framing means most of the cap table will add negative value anyway.

Thirty-six month forecast: Atrisa is acqui-hired by Cadence or Synopsys for sub-$40M in talent-and-IP terms by mid-2028, or it shuts down — the ChipStack pattern Cadence proved in February 2026 is the template, and Atrisa's competitor set (Astrus at $13.3M, Quilter at $10M Series A) will reach the acqui-hire window first with more leverage. The mechanism producing this is the one I noted in the dossier: incumbent acquisition compression caps the multiple before Atrisa can build a defensible data moat from customer circuit designs.

The strongest bull case is the **DoorDash** (2013) known-exception logic — consumer logistics looked unsexy, no science risk, generalist-fundable, and it returned a $72B IPO because the founder ambition reshaped a flow. One could argue analog design is similarly tribal and underserved, and that Atman Kar's TI silicon experience plus a documented Refortifai pivot is a learning-rate signal I should weight per my Entrepreneur magazine quote. The dossier evidence that wins against this: DoorDash had no Khosla-portfolio direct competitor at decision time, while Atrisa is launching into a category where I already back **Astrus**. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Atrisa publishes a falsifiable benchmark showing the analog-design agent outperforms Cadence's ChipStack AI Super Agent on a named SPICE-level mixed-signal task by a margin no LLM-only wrapper could replicate — that would convert the "wrapper" prior into a "stack-deep" prior and force me back to the table.
