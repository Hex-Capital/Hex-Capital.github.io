# Modern -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Modern is a capable enterprise-IT execution play that fails the entry-point question before I read the founder section: Seb Poole and Aleks Tomovski are building "the AI-native ServiceNow," and ServiceNow is the canonical capital-and-labor-leverage business -- enterprise sales motions, multi-quarter procurement, demo-gated websites, SOC 2 Type 2 / ISO 27001 / GDPR compliance as table stakes. Modern's getmodern.ai homepage is itself the tell: the only call to action is "book a demo." That is the architecture I have spent twenty years explicitly *not* funding, and the dossier does nothing to argue otherwise.

Stack Overflow (2008) is the ladder rung for permissionless leverage in a builder-tools business -- Atwood and Spolsky shipped a Q&A site that compounded through Google search and organic developer-pull, marginal cost of replication near zero, zero gatekeepers between the code and the audience. Modern's distribution looks like the inverse of that mechanism. The integrations list -- ServiceNow, Workday, SAP, Salesforce, Atlassian, Cisco Meraki -- is a list of *gatekeepers Modern must negotiate with*, not a list of channels that pull users in. [Inferred]: founder-led enterprise sales given the demo-only website (the dossier's own language). The Anchorage exception sometimes lets me waive criterion #1 when regulatory moat becomes structural leverage in a pre-consensus category -- but ITSM with $315M-Moveworks and $127M-Serval already inside the wedge is the opposite of pre-consensus. There is no exception available here.

Moveworks is the failure mechanism for criterion #2. Moveworks raised $315M, built a credible AI-overlay for IT ticketing, and exited to ServiceNow at $2.1B -- which sounds like a win until you notice that $2.1B is roughly 7x cumulative raised, and the structural reason is that AI ITSM is *single-sided SaaS*. Each Modern customer's IT team is economically independent of the next; the 8,601st ServiceNow customer does not make the prior 8,600 more valuable. Modern's traction quotes -- "62% reduction in ticket handling at a Global IT team," "74% faster response time at a Large retailer" -- describe per-customer productivity wins, not network compounding. This is the Perplexity shape without the Perplexity exception, because Perplexity cleared criterion #1 and #3 emphatically and Modern clears neither.

Specific-knowledge mechanism analysis on criterion #3: Aleks Tomovski's 15+ years on developer platforms -- core team on Wix Code, leading Canva's internal developer platform -- is the load-bearing credential in the dossier, and it does compound over 18 months if Modern reframes itself as an *internal developer platform for IT workflows* rather than a ServiceNow replacement. Seb Poole's specific knowledge is *enterprise sales to workplace-software buyers* (Frankie Health acquired by Unmind Feb 2023, prior customers Disney, Uber, MLB) -- this is real, but it is sales knowledge, not IT-operator knowledge. Neither founder lived the IT-ticketing problem as a user the way Atwood and Spolsky lived Stack Overflow's. The dossier gives me competent operators adjacent to the domain, not the founder-product-market-fit signature.

Counterfactual on criterion #4: the ITSM market at $11.91B growing to $36.78B by 2032 is a genuinely large surface, and Modern's positioning -- "OS for IT" spanning hardware, procurement, security, on/off-boarding -- has at least two adjacent pivot vectors (HRIS-adjacent workflows, security/compliance automation) before refinancing pressure hits. For criterion #4 to flip a tier higher, Modern would need to show that one of those pivots is *already in motion* -- a Workday or Okta-adjacent wedge that escapes the ServiceNow gravity well. The dossier does not show that. The pivot room exists; the pivot vector is not yet articulated.

Single-anchor parallel on criterion #5: Notion's Ivan Zhao is my reference for the 10-year-partner test passing on a quiet, builder-shaped founder. Seb Poole reads cleanly on the trio -- the Frankie-to-Unmind exit (Feb 2023) closed without litigation, no public self-promotion about ethics, repeat-founder pattern with prior Hong Kong e-commerce venture ($20M+ AUD raised, 2016). I would do monthly calls with Poole for a decade. Tomovski reads "stealth" on LinkedIn with no public surface -- which by my own rule ("if someone is going around talking about how honest they are, they're probably dishonest") is *fine*, silence is neutral. The trio passes. It just is not enough on its own.

Forecast: in 36 months Modern either reaches $3-8M ARR and gets tucked into Atlassian, Freshworks, or ServiceNow itself at $80-250M as a feature acquisition, or it dies in the wedge between Serval's $127M war chest and ServiceNow's native AI-agent roadmap. Modern is built for the acquisition outcome -- the integration list reads like a future acquirer's due-diligence checklist, and Poole's Frankie playbook ended at Unmind acquisition. Either path returns 1-3x on a Naval check, not the 100x-plus my portfolio math requires.

The bull case stings: Seb Poole's prior enterprise rolodex from Frankie (Disney, Uber, MLB, Lendlease) is exactly the asset that compresses enterprise sales cycles from 9 months to 9 weeks, and the "80%+ automation across IT team requests" claim at YC stage with two people is unusually strong -- if real, this is product-market fit at pre-seed. Moveworks at $2.1B is the *floor* of the outcome distribution, not the ceiling, for a company that out-executes it on agentic depth. A skeptic should grant that Naval-pass companies in enterprise SaaS have made Khosla and Elad Gil very rich. The bull case loses on the leverage-architecture filter that I do not waive at angel stage -- I would rather miss the Modern outcome than dilute the discipline that produced Twitter (2007), Stack Overflow (2008), and Replit (2023). The verdict flips if Modern signs two named Fortune 500 logos at $250K+ ACV within six months *and* one of those contracts originates through inbound rather than Poole's prior rolodex -- inbound at enterprise ACV would mean permissionless distribution is actually present and I am reading the architecture wrong.
