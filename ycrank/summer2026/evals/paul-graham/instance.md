# Instance -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Instance is a credential-strong, hacker-trail-empty pair of MIT friends who met in middle school and pivoted from text-to-SQL to "physics-aware quality layer for synthetic video" between January 2026 and Summer 2026 -- which makes Instance the inverse of the pattern I keep writing checks on. The longest dimension and the shortest dimension here are both unusually clean: Cai and Mao's middle-school-to-MIT cofounder relationship is in the **Reddit** (2005) Huffman/Ohanian dorm-roommate tier, but their pre-funding artifact trail is empty in a way that puts Instance below even the polished-MBA pass example from my own playbook. Strong friendship, no shipped thing. That is a specific kind of company.

The animal-test dimension is where Instance loses me most concretely. The **Airbnb** (2009) reference point isn't the idea -- it's the cereal boxes: Brian, Joe, and Nate sold 1,000+ Obama O's and Cap'n McCain's at $40 each because nobody would fund them, and that stunt was the entire investment thesis. I look for the equivalent moment in Instance's dossier and find a landing page at tryinstance.app that reads "working on world models," a pivot completed inside the last five months, and no disclosed customer, no signed LOI with any NVIDIA Cosmos or Genie team, no labeled-dataset size, no demo video on Hacker News. The dossier is silent on what Cai and Mao did before the YC check arrived, and the schlep-blindness lens reads silence as evidence against -- animals leave tracks, and the tracks here are MIT Lincoln Lab internship spotlights and a Council for Math Majors presidency, not a shipped artifact that physics-violation researchers are already using.

Hacker genealogy is the other floor. The **Reddit** (2005) bet rested partly on Aaron Swartz having co-authored the RSS spec before the company existed -- a public hacker fingerprint -- and **Stripe** (2010) rested on Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize at 16. Instance's dossier says "GitHub: No public repos found" for both founders, no Twitter for either, no Hacker News presence, no open-source artifact. Lucy Cai's 2D-to-3D point-cloud ML work at Lincoln Laboratory is real and adjacent, but Lincoln Lab is a classified-adjacent research shop -- the work doesn't ship publicly, which is the opposite of the hacker-tribal-marker pattern. Credential-only is the explicit low rung of this dimension, and Instance lands there.

The organic-idea test compounds the credential problem over 18 months. The strongest version of Instance would have Cai or Mao having personally built a world-model training pipeline at Lincoln Lab, gotten burned by physics-violation noise in their own synthetic data, and built a scanner because they were tired of eyeballing rigid-body collisions. The dossier supports none of that. What it shows is two founders whose January 2026 product was "AI data analyst for databases" -- text-to-SQL, a category with no relationship to synthetic video -- pivoting into a thesis that maps cleanly onto a market story (NVIDIA Cosmos launched January 2025, T2VPhysBench published May 2025). This is "spotted a market opportunity" with the founder biography sanded off, which is exactly the pattern "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) is written against.

The schlep dimension is the one place Instance partly earns its keep. Physics-violation detection across open-domain video is unsolved in the literature -- LikePhys (October 2025) explicitly frames it as open research -- and layering human judgment on top of that is the same kind of unglamorous, manually-intensive grind that made **Coinbase** (2012)'s compliance work a moat. If Cai and Mao actually run that schlep, the proprietary labeled dataset of physics violations could compound. The counterfactual that would flip this dimension up a tier is one signed contract with a named frontier lab -- NVIDIA, a Genie team, a Skydio -- where Instance is doing the human-review work the lab refuses to staff internally. The dossier shows zero such contracts.

My 36-month forecast for Instance: pivot to a third concept within 18 months or quiet acquihire into Encord or Surge AI for under $15M, because four open-source academic benchmarks (T2VPhysBench, WorldModelBench, VideoPhy-2, LikePhys) already cover the evaluation surface for free, Meta-owned Scale AI has the human-labeling workforce to productize a physics module in a quarter, and Cai and Mao have not yet shown the relentlessly-resourceful pattern that lets a two-person team outrun that compression. The signature -- middle-school cofounders with no hacker trail -- predicts a team that stays together through the pivot but doesn't ship the artifact that would make the pivot matter.

The bull case that should sting: this looks structurally like **Loopt** (2005), where I funded Sam Altman before mobile location was real infrastructure on the basis of personality alone, and synthetic-video data is genuinely a real wave -- NVIDIA Cosmos shipped January 2025, the buyer set is small but rich, and Lucy Cai's Lincoln Lab 2D-to-3D ML work is closer to world-model evaluation than I'm crediting. The pivot itself is evidence of resourcefulness: they killed text-to-SQL fast rather than dying inside it. And the middle-school-cofounder configuration is the highest-trust pattern in the entire YC dataset. The reason this doesn't move me is that the **Loopt** (2005) exception was driven by Altman's force-of-personality animal test result in the interview room, and the Instance dossier surfaces no equivalent -- no stunt, no shipped demo, no founder quote that reads like an animal. I would flip to invest if Instance posts a public physics-violation demo on Hacker News scanning a Cosmos-generated clip frame-by-frame, with a named lab quoted as a user, before YC Demo Day. Until then, pass.
