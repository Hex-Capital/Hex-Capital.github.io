# Stage -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Dean Stratakos built an in-house coding agent at Five Rings and then noticed the bottleneck had moved from generation to review -- that is the organic-idea signature from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012), and it is the highest dimension Stage scores on. Stage's lowest dimension is the opposite: a code-review SaaS for human reviewers is a pure-software wedge with no compliance grind, no last-mile ops, no offline schlep that **Coinbase** (2012) or **DoorDash** (2013) would recognize. So Stage is an organic-pain bet without a schlep moat, which is a recognizable shape -- it is closer to **Reddit** (2005) than to **Stripe** (2010), and it sits in a category where six better-funded competitors can ship the same UX.

The HIGH rung on organic pain is Drew Houston's USB stick at **Dropbox** (2007) -- he forgot the stick, wrote sync for himself, and the demo that put **Dropbox** (2007) on Hacker News was a tool he was already using. The shared mechanism with Stage is that Stratakos shipped an internal coding agent at a quant trading firm (dastratakos.github.io) and felt the review bottleneck personally before naming it. Where Stage diverges from the **Dropbox** (2007) rung: Houston's pre-funding artifact went viral on Hacker News in a way that flipped my conviction in one sitting; Stage's Show HN post (news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47796818, Oct 2025) exists but its points and comments were not retrievable, and 204 GitHub stars on `ReviewStage/stage-cli` is respectable but not the kind of organic uptake that makes me lean across the table. Stratakos felt the pain; he has not yet demonstrated that anyone else feels it the same way he does.

The LOW rung on schlep is the polished AI-wrapper pitched with a $50B TAM slide -- the company in my own Pass Example, which I rejected because there was no unpleasant grind anyone was unconsciously routing around. The failure mechanism on the LOW rung is that pure software in a hot category attracts smart capital fast, and the wedge gets compressed before the founders find a moat. Stage's dossier walks straight into this pattern: CodeRabbit raised a $60M Series B at $550M with $15M+ ARR growing 20%/month (TechCrunch, Sep 2025); Greptile took $25M from Benchmark (SiliconANGLE, Sep 2025); Cursor bought Graphite in December 2025 (TechCrunch). Stage's positioning -- "5x faster than GitHub" (YC page) -- is exactly the diff-comprehension UX that GitHub, Cursor, and CodeRabbit can ship as a feature. The schlep that would have made Stage uncatchable -- self-hosted enterprise security review, manual onboarding into regulated codebases, a compliance grind around AI-generated-code provenance -- is absent from the dossier.

On animal resourcefulness, Stage's pre-funding artifact is the 204-star `stage-cli` repo (GitHub, MIT, TypeScript 97%) and the Show HN launch. That is a shipped artifact, which clears the threshold that the MBA-with-a-deck team fails. But it compounds weakly over 18 months unless Stratakos and Charles Pan add something that looks like the cereal boxes at **Airbnb** (2009) -- a stunt or a revenue improvisation that demonstrates they will do whatever it takes when CodeRabbit's sales team shows up at their accounts. The dossier does not name a single paying customer, signed LOI, or unscalable manual operation Stage's founders are running personally. Animals leave tracks; I see one repo and a Show HN.

The hacker-genealogy counterfactual: if Stratakos's GitHub showed a second non-trivial open-source artifact unrelated to Stage -- a Lisp project, a compiler, a competitive programming history -- this dimension would flip from "above default" to "top quartile" and the total would cross into Invest territory. As the dossier reads, Stratakos has Stanford BS+MS CS (AI), the Five Rings in-house coding agent, Apple Siri/Citadel internships, and `stage-cli` at 204 stars. That is a credible hacker trail. Pan is less visible -- no GitHub found, no Twitter, LinkedIn not disambiguated -- which means half the founding team is illegible on the dimension I weight most after the **Viaweb** (1995) Lisp lens.

Co-founder configuration parallels **Stripe** (2010): two founders, deep prior shared context. Pan and Stratakos overlapped at Stanford and both worked at Five Rings Capital (gostanford.com; dastratakos.github.io). That is the 2+ year working-relationship pattern from "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010), and it is the cleanest dimension in Stage's dossier. No solo-founder red flag, no hackathon-pairing red flag.

My 36-month forecast for Stage: acqui-hired by Cursor, CodeRabbit, or GitHub for under $30M before reaching $3M ARR, or pivoted into the AI-auto-reviewer category Stage's Show HN explicitly positions against ("Putting humans back in control of code review," HN Oct 2025). The mechanism is the schlep dimension: a pure-software diff-organization UX cannot hold off three competitors with $60M, $25M, and Cursor's distribution unless Stage either reaches a compounding data moat from review-pattern feedback (no public usage data supports this yet) or relocates into a schlep zone the incumbents won't enter.

The bull case that stings: Houston's first **Dropbox** (2007) demo also looked like a feature Microsoft could ship, and I funded it anyway because the founder had lived the pain and shipped the artifact. Stratakos has lived the pain at Five Rings and shipped `stage-cli` at 204 stars; this is the same shape, and I have been wrong about feature-vs-company before -- **Dropbox** (2007) is the canonical example. The dossier evidence that wins against the bull case: Houston's HN demo produced organic viral uptake that I could see in real time in 2007; Stage's traction signals are 204 stars, an unretrievable Show HN, and a YC LinkedIn promo post -- no documented user love at **Dropbox** (2007)-grade intensity. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Stage shows ten named paying engineering teams using it daily by end of 2026, or Stratakos ships a second piece of evidence -- a public revenue stunt, a self-hosted enterprise deployment, a regulatory wedge -- that moves Stage's schlep dimension from 6 to 11. Until then, Neutral.
