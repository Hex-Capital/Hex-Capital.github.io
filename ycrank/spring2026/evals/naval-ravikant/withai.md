# WithAI -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

WithAI is a deep-specific-knowledge bet wrapped inside a capital-and-labor-leverage business architecture, and that pairing is the whole story: Ian McInnis ran an equity book at Bridgewater leading "how can we use LLMs to invest?" while his co-founder Ben Finch built multi-agent coordination at Sentient Labs, but the product they shipped is a "Multiplier" that deploys inside each client's own AWS/Azure/GCP and sells through founder-led cal.com bookings to fundamental equity hedge funds at "huge ACV." The founders are exactly the kind of people I'd want to spend ten years with on a code-leverage problem. They are building a labor-leverage business instead.

Stack Overflow is the HIGH rung on specific knowledge -- Atwood and Spolsky were practicing developers writing for developers, and the artifact they shipped was indistinguishable from the problem they personally had. WithAI has the analog cleanly: Ian was the buyer at Bridgewater, Ben was the agent-infrastructure builder, Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour put their names on the cap table as angels, and the first paying client Mercator Partners reportedly inverted its "80% gathering, 20% acting" ratio. Where WithAI diverges from the Stack Overflow analog is that Atwood's specific knowledge compounded inside a permissionless code artifact -- a public Q&A site that grew while he slept. Ian's specific knowledge at WithAI compounds inside a single-tenant deployment that requires a six-to-twelve-month hedge fund security review per customer. The knowledge is real. The substrate it sits on is wrong.

The LOW rung on leverage architecture is the pattern I flag explicitly -- enterprise sales motions with SDRs and white-glove enablement, where distribution requires permission from each gatekeeper buyer. WithAI's own marketing copy says "white-glove enablement from founders" and the contact mechanism is a calendar booking link. Sixty-plus data integrations -- Bloomberg, Refinitiv, FactSet, Tegus, AlphaSense, Snowflake, JP Morgan -- is impressive engineering and the structural opposite of code-as-permissionless-leverage; every one of those integrations is a partner-permission surface. WithAI's >$100K ARR across "multiple clients" three months in is healthy enterprise execution and the velocity tells me nothing about whether the underlying architecture can ever escape labor leverage. The Anchorage exception lets regulated permission become moat, but WithAI's permission gates are sell-side data vendors and hedge fund procurement -- not a Treasury OCC charter that locks competitors out.

WithAI's network dimension fails mechanically over 18 months: each hedge fund deployment learns one firm's ontology in one firm's cloud, so the Nth client cannot make the (N-1)th client's agents smarter without violating the data-isolation promise that justifies the architecture in the first place. The product is structurally single-sided at the technical layer, and the moat narrative -- "fund-specific workflow knowledge per client could compound" -- is per-client compounding, not cross-client compounding. That is SaaS retention, not Metcalfe.

WithAI's market-and-pivot-room placement flips a tier only if "fundamental equity hedge fund command center" expands into a horizontal buy-side workflow layer that also serves credit funds, multi-strats, family offices, and corporate development -- the Rogo trajectory ($75M Series C at $750M post in January 2026, expanding IB-to-hedge-funds via OpenAI). The dossier shows no evidence WithAI has architected for that breadth; the 60+ integrations are all equity-research-oriented. Niche-first with adjacent paths visible only on a whiteboard is the "first idea never works" failure mode I've watched eat single-vertical seeds.

WithAI's founder trio reads like Notion's Ivan Zhao on the integrity axis without the adolescent-onward obsession history: Princeton '23, perfect GPA, Magna Cum Laude ECE, a co-founded student tech investment firm, and Bridgewater co-CIO angels who put real reputation behind the check. Clean, sharp, no red flags, no self-advertised honesty, no unresolved CEO question. I would happily take a meeting with Ian in three years on his next thing. That is not the same as funding this thing.

Forecast at 36 months: WithAI reaches roughly $5-15M ARR across 10-25 hedge fund logos and gets acquired by Hebbia, Rogo, or a sell-side incumbent (FactSet, S&P, MSCI) at a strategic premium -- not dead, not a unicorn, a clean enterprise outcome that returns 3-8x to a brand-name seed lead and roughly zero to my angel-math thesis where downside is one X and upside needs to be 1,000x to clear the funnel.

The strongest bull case invokes the Perplexity exception: single-sided product, deep founder specific knowledge, pre-consensus category timing, override the network criterion. The dismantle is that the Perplexity exception explicitly requires three of five gates clear emphatically, and the load-bearing gate was permissionless distribution via API and consumer product -- Aravind shipped to anyone with a browser. WithAI clears specific knowledge emphatically and clears nothing else emphatically; the distribution architecture is the inverse of Perplexity's. The verdict flips to Invest only on one verifiable condition: WithAI open-sources a developer-facing component -- an agent framework, an evaluation harness for non-verifiable financial reasoning, an integration SDK -- that accumulates organic GitHub stars and outside-contributor PRs from quant developers within twelve months. Permissionless code emerging from the enterprise wedge would re-rate criterion #1 and change the math. Until that artifact ships publicly, WithAI is a great team building the wrong shape of business for my check.
