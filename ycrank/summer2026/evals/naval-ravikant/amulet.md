# Amulet -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Amulet is a B2B customer success agent built by two ex-Meta software engineers -- Nithik Bala from Core Ads Growth and Varun Puru from Facebook product -- selling into a $3.61B CSP market against Gainsight ($1.2B+ ARR), Vitally, ChurnZero, and Planhat. The signature is a company that sits highest on market size and cleanly-legible engineering pedigree, and lowest on the two dimensions I actually underwrite from -- permissionless leverage architecture and founder-domain specific knowledge. That is the exact shape of a competent capital/labor-leverage business, which is the shape I structurally do not fund at angel stage.

Gainsight is the load-bearing analogy for Amulet's leverage architecture -- $1.1B Vista acquisition in November 2020, distribution built through enterprise field sales, services revenue tied to human-in-the-loop CSM workflows. Amulet's dossier does not name a code-native or media-native distribution channel; the buyer set (VP CS, RevOps) is reached through outbound and channel motions that require permission from procurement gatekeepers. Stack Overflow (2008) distributed by seeding Google's index with developer-written answers -- Jeff Atwood shipped the code, Joel Spolsky shipped the essays, and the community compounded without an SDR touching the funnel. Amulet's diverging evidence is the competitor set itself: every named alternative (Gainsight, Vitally $30M Series B, ChurnZero, Planhat) is a paid-acquisition, enterprise-motion incumbent, and the "hiring: false" flag on the YC page tells me nothing about a permissionless pull mechanic replacing that motion.

The lowest rung on my specific-knowledge ladder is the "ex-FAANG PM identifies market opportunity" pattern -- pedigree substituting for domain immersion, a red flag I flagged explicitly. Amulet exhibits the engineering variant of that pattern almost cleanly: Nithik Bala at Meta Core Ads Growth and Web Infra, Varun Puru at Meta on Facebook product, both with internships at Rockset, PagerDuty, Amazon backend, J.P. Morgan cloud infra. The load-bearing question is whether either founder has lived the CSM workflow -- watched a book of 80 accounts, drafted the renewal outreach, absorbed the political taxonomy of a churn conversation. The dossier surfaces no such artifact, no prior CS operator role, no open-source contribution to a CS-adjacent stack, no writing about post-sales. This is the inverse of the Stack Overflow origin, where the founders were the buyers.

Amulet's network dimension is a mechanism question over 18 months: does the multi-tenant data across post-sales signals -- CRM plus support plus product usage plus comms -- compound into a proprietary churn/expansion training set that a single-tenant Gainsight deployment cannot match? Possibly, but the compounding requires customer count, and at team size 2 with no disclosed logos the flywheel has not started; meanwhile Staircase AI ($5M seed) was tucked into Gainsight in July 2024 before its own dataset compounded, which is the empirical base rate for agent-native CS entrants meeting incumbent data gravity.

Amulet's TAM sits at $3.61B in 2026 growing to $8.3B by 2030 at 23.2% CAGR -- a real market with adjacent pivot vectors into RevOps automation, support deflection, and account-based orchestration. To flip this dimension a tier, the dossier would need to name a second market Amulet could credibly move into using the same agent primitives without re-founding -- something like a documented pivot path from post-sales agents to full revenue-cycle agents. The dossier does not name that path, so Amulet clears the "not a niche" bar but does not clear the "platform-shaped" bar; the score sits mid-range, defensible but not thesis-activating.

Perplexity is my parallel on the founder-trio axis -- Aravind Srinivas passed the 10-year-partner test on strength of visible depth (Berkeley ML PhD, retrieval obsession, public writing) rather than on absence of red flags. Amulet's founders read clean on integrity but are essentially unresolved on depth of conviction for the post-sales domain; two Meta SWEs with no Twitter/X footprint, no GitHub, no public writing on customer success. I cannot fail the trio, but I cannot pass it emphatically either, and at pre-seed, silent-on-depth in a category filled with well-funded incumbents is not neutral -- it is a shrug where I need a lean-in.

Forecast: Amulet is acquired by Gainsight, Vitally, or Totango within 36 months for under $25M as a tuck-in agent-orchestration team, mirroring the Staircase AI pattern of July 2024 -- or the company pivots into an internal-agent-platform serving a broader RevOps buyer once the initial CS wedge fails to differentiate against Gainsight's Agentforce/Staircase-derived layer. The mechanism is straightforward: capital/labor leverage against a $1.2B ARR incumbent with cheaper distribution ends in acquihire, not in category creation, absent a specific-knowledge founder edge that Amulet's dossier does not surface.

The strongest case against my pass is the Perplexity exception -- a single-sided AI product where the leverage architecture (API-and-product permissionless distribution), specific knowledge (Srinivas retrieval PhD), and pre-consensus timing all cleared at top quartile, letting me waive the network criterion. The bull argument for Amulet asks me to run the same waiver: agent-native CS is pre-consensus in 2026, LLM tool-use reached production reliability in 2024-2025, and Gainsight's $1.2B ARR is architecturally trapped in human-CSM workflows an agent-first substitute can cannibalize. The argument stings, but the dismantling is clean: Perplexity cleared three of five gates emphatically -- Amulet clears one at best (market), sits at default on trio, and fails on both leverage architecture and specific knowledge, which are the two gates the Perplexity waiver required to be at top quartile. The flip condition I would honor: if Nithik Bala or Varun Puru shipped a documented six-months-of-organic-adoption artifact -- an open-source agent orchestration framework with real GitHub stars, or a public case study of a design partner running unedited draft outreach at scale -- I would re-underwrite. The dossier as written does not clear that bar.
