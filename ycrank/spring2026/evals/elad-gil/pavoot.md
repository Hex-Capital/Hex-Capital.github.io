# Pavoot -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Pavoot is a single-miracle workflow agent with no index position, dropped into an event-tech category that just consolidated under Cvent's December 2025 acquisition of Goldcast for ~$300M — which is to say, Pavoot is architecturally clean on the one dimension that matters least when incumbents have already named the wedge. Gohar Tamrazyan and Ana Yoon Faria de Lima met in a 3-day ETH AI Center course, pitched on day four, and are now selling AI-event-to-pipeline against the precise feature Cvent paid $300M to bolt onto its installed base. That pairing — low index, decent single-miracle hygiene — is the analytical signature here, and it produces a specific kind of company, not a categorically broken one.

When **Anduril** (2022) came across, Ukraine was the named 24-month catalyst that reopened defense — a specific event with a specific date. Pavoot's "why now" is [Inferred]: "LLMs + ASR crossed a threshold in 2024-2025," which is the same generic AI tailwind sitting under every YC Spring 2026 batch deck. The Cvent–Goldcast deal *is* a real recent catalyst, but it cuts the wrong way for Pavoot — it's an incumbent buying the wedge, not Pentagon procurement opening to a software-native entrant. Without a Ukraine-equivalent dislocation that specifically disadvantages Cvent and Bizzabo's $43.9M revenue distribution, Pavoot is selling into a category whose 2025 catalyst was *consolidation*, not re-opening.

Generic AI startups with no growth curve disclosed are the textbook failure mode — "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss #863). Pavoot's only public traction artifact is a homepage sample showing 47 attendees → 24 meetings → 11 qualified leads → $48K pipeline, with no named customer attached. At pre-seed I don't penalize the absence of ARR, but I do read the silence: no design partners named, no waitlist count, no Launch YC engagement metrics disclosed beyond "live ~21 days." Pavoot's Launch went up three weeks ago and the dossier surfaces zero customer logos — in an AI cycle where real traction is trivially easy to disclose, that absence is itself the signal.

Pavoot's founder profile is the part that almost saves the deal: Faria de Lima was an AI Engineer at BTG Pactual and Itaú Unibanco with 20+ Scientific Olympiad medals, Tamrazyan is a 6-time Swiss Chess Champion at ETH CS. That is *problem-solving* pedigree, not *shipping* pedigree — and the **Perplexity** (2022) template was specifically about Aravind showing up a week later with the thing we discussed built, which is an artifact-level signal, not a credential-level one. Pavoot's dossier names zero prior shipped products from either founder, no GitHub repos, and the founder-meeting story is a 3-day class project. Olympiad medals and chess titles index for IQ and grit; they do not predict the weekly iteration rhythm I funded Perplexity for.

**Stripe** (2010) was an index on e-commerce — every new merchant had to touch the rails. Pavoot's positioning against Cvent (~$4.6B 2023 take-private), Bizzabo ($226M raised, Gartner MQ Leader), and the freshly-Cvent'd Goldcast ($300M Dec 2025) is the opposite — Pavoot has to *win share against* the registration/streaming layer that every B2B event already runs on. That's the **Lyft** Series C problem in a smaller market: an agent layer is a feature Cvent bundles next quarter, not a workflow position the category routes through. The commoditization vector is explicit in the dossier — "core capabilities… rest on commodity foundation-model APIs."

Counterfactually, on single-miracle architecture Pavoot is clean: the only required miracle is "LLM + ASR is good enough to capture and personalize event conversations," which is already validated, leaving GTM and product execution as the remaining work — the same shape **Decagon** (2023) took into customer support. To flip this dimension a tier higher Pavoot would need to expose a proprietary cross-event attendee graph that compounds with usage, which the dossier explicitly notes is "unproven now given pre-launch stage." Single-miracle hygiene is the strongest thing about Pavoot and it's worth roughly 8/12, but single-miracle alone has never been my reason to write a check — it's the screen I run *before* the catalyst and velocity tests, both of which Pavoot fails.

In 36 months Pavoot is either acquihired into Cvent, HubSpot, or a Salesforce-adjacent RevOps suite for sub-$50M, or pivoted into a narrow vertical (post-event outbound for a specific trade-show category) at under $2M ARR — most likely a sub-$30M Cvent-style absorption within 24-30 months, on the same template Goldcast just executed but at a fraction of the price because Pavoot hasn't compounded the customer base Goldcast had. The mechanism producing this forecast is the index-position failure: when incumbents validate your wedge by acquisition before you have customers, you are selling a feature into a distribution war you cannot win.

The strongest bull case against that forecast is the **Decagon** (2023) parallel — Decagon was also a thin agent on commodity LLM in a category dominated by Zendesk and Intercom, and it's a portfolio anchor of mine in *AI Market Clarity*'s "wide open" column. The Pavoot bull says ETH-grade engineering on a focused agent problem with a working $48K-pipeline POC compounds if Faria de Lima and Tamrazyan land 5-10 design partners in six months. The dismantling fact: Decagon entered customer support *before* a $300M consolidation event named the wedge for incumbents, and Decagon's founders had named shipped products at prior companies — Pavoot has neither. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: three named Fortune 500 design partners disclosed within six months with month-over-month attendee-capture growth above 30%. Until then Pavoot is the kind of YC company that looks reasonable on a single-miracle screen and fails every other one I run.
