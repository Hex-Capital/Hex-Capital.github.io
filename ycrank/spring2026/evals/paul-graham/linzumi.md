# Linzumi -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Sean Grove worked on OpenAI's model spec and deliberative alignment, and Linzumi's pitch is literally "deliberative alignment, repurposed from aligning LLMs to aligning companies" — that is the tightest organic-idea fit I've read in this batch, and it is paired with the weakest schlep score, because Linzumi is a chat bot orchestrating other people's agents. That pairing is the signature: a hacker building exactly the artifact his own résumé predicts, in a category whose offline grind is zero and whose incumbents are at $492M ARR.

**Stripe** (2010) is the load-bearing analogy for organic-idea fit, because the Collisons had been hacking commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007) and arrived at payments having already lived inside the problem for years. Linzumi has the same shape on paper: Grove keynoted AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on "The New Code," published with the-decoder on intent-as-spec, and is now shipping a product whose core mechanism — compiling team decisions into a source-of-truth spec that generates and verifies agent work — is the work he was doing at OpenAI. Where Linzumi diverges from the Stripe rung is that the Collisons had a $5M Auctomatic outcome already proving they shipped commerce code that customers paid for; Grove's Linzumi is a waitlist with 79 LinkedIn followers and no disclosed users, so the organic-idea fit is real but the demonstration is still on the deck, not in the artifact.

The MBA-team-with-glossy-deck pass pattern fails because no founder has personally suffered the problem and no artifact exists pre-pitch; the failure mechanism is that the founders cannot tell a real schlep from a TAM slide. Linzumi only partially escapes that trap. Grove has the demo-shipped credential from **OneGraph** (YC W19, sold to Netlify 2021), so this is not the MBA pattern — but the work Linzumi proposes is pure software wrapping other agents in chat, with no offline ops, no compliance grind, no manual onboarding the founders have to personally do. Cognition already integrates Devin with Slack per its product docs; the chat surface itself is a feature an incumbent at $492M ARR can ship in a quarter. **Coinbase** (2012) had Brian Armstrong volunteering to eat the bitcoin regulatory grind. Linzumi has Grove volunteering to eat… a multi-player chat UI.

Linzumi's animal-resourcefulness evidence is the absence I notice most. The dossier names no pre-funding stunt, no cereal-box equivalent, no unscalable manual revenue, no Hacker News demo that flipped me the way Drew Houston's did at **Dropbox** (2007). Grove's prior **OneGraph**→Netlify exit is real residue and earns him credit above the default, but that animal moment was 2019-2021, not the Linzumi check. Eighteen months from now the founders either show me a stunt — three named Fortune 500 design partners running on Linzumi with reported agent-task accuracy numbers, or some piece of unscalable hand-curation Grove personally did — or this score does not move and the company doesn't either.

Hacker genealogy: Linzumi has one publicly identified founder, and he is the right kind of one. Grove's GitHub @sgrove (489 followers), the AI Engineer World's Fair keynote, the OpenAI model-spec work, the OneGraph YC W19 lineage — this is hacker-tribal-marker stack of the sort that predicted **Reddit** (2005)'s Aaron Swartz RSS lineage. The discount is that two of Linzumi's three founders are not publicly identifiable; one search snippet floated "Assil Halimi" and "Drew Walker" but neither could be confirmed. I am giving Grove credit for the lineage I can read and reserving judgment on the two I cannot.

Co-founder configuration would flip up a tier if the dossier named the other two and described any prior working relationship — a Netlify colleague, an OpenAI alignment teammate, a roommate. Three founders is the right count; **Airbnb** (2009) was three and **Twitch** (2007) was three-to-four. But Linzumi shows me one name, two ciphers, and zero history, so I apply the absent-evidence default and stay neutral. Garry Tan as Group Partner will have surfaced the chemistry in the interview; I have not been in that room.

My 36-month forecast for Linzumi: acquihired by Cognition or Anthropic for sub-$100M, or pivoted away from the chat surface into a standalone spec/alignment-tooling layer sold to teams already on Devin or Cursor. The mechanism is straightforward — Cognition at $492M ARR growing 50% MoM (TechCrunch, May 2026) ships a Slack-native multi-player surface as a Q3 feature, Linzumi's $100/mo flat pricing is undercut from above by Devin Teams at $80/mo per user with a real customer base, and Grove's deliberative-alignment-for-companies mechanism becomes the most valuable thing in the company — which is exactly what gets acquihired.

The bull case that stings: Grove is one of perhaps a dozen people on Earth who has actually built deliberative-alignment infrastructure at OpenAI, the spec-compilation mechanism is genuinely novel rather than a Slack-bot wrapper, and Cognition cannot ship it in a quarter because they do not have the researcher. If Linzumi's "decisions compile into a source-of-truth spec" works at even 70% extraction accuracy on real engineering chat, the spec itself becomes the switching cost — every team that uses Linzumi for six months has a proprietary operating manual no incumbent can clone, and the chat surface was just the data-collection vehicle. **Airbnb** (2009) is the Known Exception precedent: I mentally rejected the idea and the founders shipped revenue via a stunt that flipped me. The dismantling fact is that Linzumi has shipped no stunt and no users — waitlist, 79 LinkedIn followers, no Product Hunt launch. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: three named design-partner companies (engineering teams of 20+) running Linzumi in production with a public agent-task accuracy figure above 60%, before Cognition ships a Slack-native multi-player Devin surface. Until then, this is a Neutral with a high-organic-idea / low-schlep signature, which is exactly the profile that gets acquihired rather than compounded.
