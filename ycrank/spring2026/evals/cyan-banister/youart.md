# YouArt -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

YouArt is the Pass Example almost word-for-word: two ex-TikTok ads scalers, Edward Ma and Harry Zheng, building a "Patreon for AI originals" bundle in the most consensus-attractive market of 2026 — generative video — where Passes already closed a $40M Series A and Patreon owns the fan-funding incumbency. The dimensional signature is brutal and specific: the highest-scoring axis here is Cascading Impact (mid, on par with Depop democratizing creator tooling) and the lowest is Contrarian Conviction (consensus AI in a consensus market). That pairing tells me what YouArt is — a competently-built creator-monetization bundle assembled by people who recognized a market gap, not people whose lives drove them into it.

On Founder's Biographical Forge, my high rung is Substrate, where I wrote about James Proud being "forged in fire and came out unbroken"; my mid rung is Crusoe, infrastructure people who happened to start with Bitcoin; my low rung is the Pass Example founder who told me "I was at Google and saw the opportunity." YouArt sits a hair above that low rung. Edward Ma's "founding ML engineer on TikTok and DoorDash ads, $0→$100M+" and Harry Zheng's "grew TikTok Shop Ads 0 to $100M" are real growth credentials, but they read as career optimization, not crucible. Where is the personal stake in AI-original storytelling? The dossier surfaces zero — no homeless-to-creator arc, no failed-artist-turned-engineer story, no bootstrap pivot through adversity. When I ask "tell me your story, not the business," the answer here is the resume. That is the data.

On Contrarian Conviction in Hard Spaces — my lowest-placed dimension for YouArt — my high rung is Contraline, where male birth control was so stigmatized that most VCs visibly squirmed; my mid rung is Superplastic, culturally weird without being regulated; my low rung is the Pass Example, "consensus AI in a consensus market." YouArt slots squarely on that low rung. Generative AI video sits inside three different market reports cited in the dossier — $946.4M, $0.47B, $847M projections, all 2026 — every one of which signals VC eagerness, not aversion. The Product Hunt launch on Nov 6, 2025 alongside Nano Banana, Seedream, Sora 2, Veo 3.1 places YouArt directly in the most-funded subcategory of the most-funded category. No room shifts uncomfortably when YouArt pitches. Nobody passed 300 times before me.

On Invisible System Made Visible, Affirm is my high rung (credit card opacity), Serve Robotics is mid (delivery access expansion through autonomy), and the Pass Example sits at the floor. YouArt is barely off the floor. Patreon's take rate is already transparent; Passes' is too. There is no medallion cartel, no hidden tax, no information asymmetry being attacked. The "creator gets funded by early fans" framing on the YC page describes a workflow, not a structural revelation. On Infrastructure-Grade Defensibility, Flock Safety's compounding camera network is the high rung, RTFKT (a Known Exception) is mid where cultural inflection substituted for moat, and HQ Trivia / IRL anchor the bottom. YouArt sits near the bottom — the dossier itself states the bundle is "replicable by a small team in 3–6 months," and the model-vendor dependency on Sora 2 / Veo 3.1 / Nano Banana means OpenAI, Google, or ByteDance can reprice the unit economics in a quarter. On Cascading Impact, my one source of upside: Uber high, Depop mid, enterprise SaaS low. YouArt sits at Depop's rung — letting non-technical creators orchestrate multi-model video pipelines does democratize a previously gatekept skill. That is real, and it is the only place I find myself nodding.

My 36-month forecast: YouArt gets acqui-hired by Passes, Fourthwall, or Whop for under $30M — or quietly shuts down as OpenAI's Sora app and Google's Veo surface ship native creator monetization and collapse the bundle thesis. The mechanism is the dimensional signature itself: a consensus market plus a replicable bundle plus model-vendor dependency plus founders without biographical inevitability is the exact recipe my Pass Example was built to describe.

Now the bull case, because I owe YouArt the steel-man. Harry Zheng didn't just work at TikTok — he grew TikTok Shop Ads from zero to $100M, which means he understands creator-side liquidity and ads-recommender flywheels at a depth most pre-seed founders never touch. Edward Ma's 1,500+ AI citations plus prior Speaking AI work means the technical orchestration layer is in genuinely capable hands. RTFKT is the Known Exception that haunts this analysis: cultural inflection at a narrow timing window can substitute for infrastructure moat, and AI-native originals could be that window. But RTFKT had a clearly identifiable acquirer landscape (Nike, fashion houses) and a specific cultural artifact (digital sneakers tied to physical brand identity); YouArt has neither — it has a workflow studio with a monetization layer bolted on, and the dossier itself flags positioning instability between the Product Hunt "creative agent" and the YC-page "Patreon for AI originals." That's not a narrow timing window I can name; that's a startup still deciding what it is. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a named flagship creator on YouArt pulling six-figure annualized fan funding for AI originals within twelve months, demonstrating that the patron side has cold-started. Absent that, this isn't a Long Journey bet.
