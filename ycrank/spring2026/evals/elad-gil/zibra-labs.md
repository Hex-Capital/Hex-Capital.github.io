# Zibra Labs -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Zibra Labs is a deep-infrastructure-pedigree pre-seed attacking a named-account market with no disclosed revenue -- Ibrahim Rabbani was tech-lead on Ray (~12M weekly downloads, PyTorch Foundation) and Zac Policzer was a LinkedIn Senior Staff engineer on Venice/Liquid/Espresso, but the dossier shows zero customer logos, no LOIs, and no pricing page on zibralabs.ai as of May 2026. That high/low pairing -- elite shipping history, silent revenue curve -- is the signature.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the rung I reach for on founder shipping: Aravind would show up a week later with the thing we'd discussed built. Zibra Labs has the analog *at prior employers* -- Rabbani led Ray Core, which xAI, Cursor, Bridgewater, P72, Man Group, and Two Sigma actually run on, and Policzer shipped LinkedIn's Venice derived-data platform documented on the LinkedIn Engineering blog. The divergence from Perplexity is that the Zibra Labs dossier surfaces no artifact *shipped at Zibra itself* -- no GitHub commit cadence on a Zibra repo, no demo, no design partner sandbox, just the cal.com "intro-to-zibra-labs" scheduler. Shipping Ray at Anyscale is a different muscle than shipping a managed multi-cloud orchestrator from a two-person company, and the dossier doesn't yet show me the second muscle firing.

**Harvey** (2023) is what revenue velocity looks like when it's "just working" -- $3B ? $5B ? $8B in months (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). The failure mechanism at the low rung is the AI company with no growth curve disclosed, which I treat as evidence rather than absence because real growth is easy to disclose. Zibra Labs's dossier says "No public data found" on revenue, no Product Hunt listing, no press, no Discord, and Hiring flag False -- this is a stage-appropriate silence but it is not a positive signal, and the buyer set (tier-1 quant funds) is precisely the buyer set that closes contracts privately, so I can't even comfort myself with "we'll see it leak." Until Zibra Labs names a counterparty, I'm assigning the absent-evidence default.

On market re-opening, the Zibra Labs catalyst claim is that AI-generated alpha candidates shifted quant research "from a handful of signals per quarter to hundreds per day" inside a 12-24 month window -- that's the right *shape* of catalyst, mechanistically identical to what made the Stripe-after-PayPal and Anduril-after-Ukraine entries work. The compounding question over 18 months: does the compute-bottleneck framing hold once foundation-model-grade signal generation becomes table stakes, or does it collapse back into "every quant fund already has a grid"? The dossier marks the catalyst [Inferred] rather than citing a Bridgewater CTO quote or an AQR earnings transcript, which is the difference between a catalyst I can underwrite and one I'm taking on faith.

On index-like distribution, the counterfactual for a tier-flip is straightforward: Zibra Labs would have to demonstrate it sits in the workflow path of *every* quant entrant, the way Stripe sits in the workflow path of every e-commerce entrant. The dossier instead describes a buyer universe of roughly thirty institutional quant funds, the largest of whom (Two Sigma's Cook cluster, Bridgewater's proprietary grid) are cited by Ray itself as in-house operators -- meaning the named accounts have already solved this problem internally. For Zibra Labs to become an index rather than a vendor, the mid-tier funds (the $500M-$5B AUM long tail) would need to standardize on it; nothing in the dossier suggests that wedge has been pulled yet.

On single-miracle architecture, **Color Genomics** (2015) is the single-anchor parallel: workflow infrastructure on top of a commodity substrate, where the bet is execution rather than chemistry. Zibra Labs is structurally that pattern -- Ray plus spot orchestration plus a backtesting-specific scheduler, not new distributed-systems physics. The Zibra Labs miracle compresses to one engineering claim (sub-50ms dispatch across 6.4M parallel tasks with checkpoint-restart robust enough for spot interruption) plus one GTM claim (selling compute to firms who build their own). The engineering miracle I'll grant Rabbani and Policzer on pedigree; the GTM miracle is independent and unaddressed.

My 36-month forecast: Zibra Labs lands one or two design-partner contracts with mid-tier quant funds, fails to displace the in-house grids at Two Sigma / Bridgewater / P72, and pivots toward general AI training orchestration by mid-2028 -- at which point Anyscale ($281M raised, $1B valuation per Intel Capital 2021) acqui-hires Rabbani back into the mothership for $40-80M. The mechanism is the customer-concentration math: a ~30-account TAM where the top decile already builds proprietary infrastructure leaves Zibra Labs selling to a long tail too small to compound into a venture outcome, which forces the pivot, which lands them in a horizontal market where their substrate's commercial owner outguns them.

The strongest bull case dismantles my Anyscale-overlap framing: Rabbani didn't leave Anyscale randomly -- he left because he saw, from inside Ray Core, which workload type generates the highest compute spend per customer, and he picked the one vertical where the buyer is rich, latency-sensitive, and structurally underserved by horizontal platforms. That's the Color Genomics workflow-on-commodity pattern with a richer buyer. The bear response that wins: even granting the workload-selection thesis, the named buyers (Bridgewater, Two Sigma, P72) appear in the dossier as *Ray users, not Zibra customers* -- so the founder's existing network has already had the conversation and not converted. The condition that flips my verdict: two tier-1 quant fund contracts at $1M+ ACV disclosed within 12 months of YC Demo Day, or one named design partner from the Ray customer roster signing a multi-year deal. Absent that, this is a credible-team pre-seed in a structurally small market, and at consensus price it doesn't pencil.
