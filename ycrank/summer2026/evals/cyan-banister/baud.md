# Baud -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Baud is a credibly-built frontier-silicon swing landing in the most over-bid AI hardware vintage of my career -- the right shape of company in exactly the wrong moment to find it before consensus. Sarang Zambare shipped Peloton Guide's ML to 100,000+ devices and Eric Taylor has 10+ years of ASIC architecture across NVIDIA, NXP, Arteris, and Enfabrica with three-plus tape-outs; that founder pair is *of* the silicon, not visiting it. But the category around them is Etched at a $5B valuation, MatX at $600M raised, Taalas at $219M, and $8.3B chasing AI chips in a single year. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing -- and the game everyone *thinks* they're playing here is the one I'd usually walk past.

SpaceX in 2008 is the load-bearing analogue for Baud on the frontier-hardware axis -- a multiplier-free ASIC at the same process node, claiming 100x efficiency against NVIDIA, is exactly the kind of capital-intensive bet most of my LP peers would call uninvestable on aesthetic grounds. The mechanism that made SpaceX work was that no one else was even *trying* to build the thing; Elon had the runway alone. Where Baud diverges is the funding gravity around it: Cerebras pulled $1B in 2026 alone, MatX closed $500M from ex-Google TPU operators, and Etched is fielding inbound at five-billion-dollar marks. Baud has the silicon DNA -- Taylor's three tape-outs are not a rsum line, that's a person who has actually carried a chip across the finish at NVIDIA and Enfabrica -- but SpaceX-shape companies don't get to be the seventh entrant in a category. They get to be the only one.

Crusoe ~2018 is the failure pattern I run when I look at Baud through the magically-weird lens, because Crusoe worked precisely *because* credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds. Baud's dossier shows the inverse: AI chip startups raised $8.3B in 2026, Diana Hu is the YC partner (top-of-funnel, not contrarian), and the only thing aesthetically transgressive about Baud is the math trick. The category itself is consensus. "Find them before it becomes consensus" -- this is the consensus. I cannot find a single named investor that has publicly passed on Baud, which is normally a red flag dressed as a blank space; here it just means the round will price like every other 2026 chip round, which is to say, not cheap.

Founder-market fit at Baud compounds in a specific way over the next 18 months: Zambare's compiler chops mean torch.distributed, FSDP2, and tensor parallelism integration is not vapor -- it's the half of the problem MatX has people for and Etched explicitly skipped (transformer-only inference) and Taalas refuses to solve (model-hardwired silicon). Taylor's two patents plus Zambare's four patents are real, and the founding-ASIC-architect RTL/FPGA role open on YC jobs tells me Baud knows what gap it has. The compounding works if the 50M-parameter FPGA demo at 1000+ tokens/sec scales losslessly; it doesn't if a Hopper-successor closes the gap on power-per-token before Baud tapes out. That is a real race, not a moat.

For Baud's single-CEO axis to flip a tier downward, the dossier would need to show Zambare and Taylor as co-CEOs or as titled "co-founders" without delineation -- the HQ Trivia 2017 / GameCrush ~2011 setup that I will not underwrite. It does not. Zambare is "Founder & CEO," Taylor is "Founder & Chief Hardware Architect," and the labor split between compiler-side and silicon-side is the cleanest tiebreaker structure I've seen in this batch. That alone doesn't earn a check, but it removes the governance landmine that killed two of my own portfolio lessons.

Niantic ~2015 is the single-anchor parallel for Baud's physical-world verifiability problem -- I bought Niantic because I was playing Ingress in 2012 and could feel the pull in my hands. With Baud, the artifact in 2026 is an FPGA emulating a 50M-parameter model and a Tally form for a beta waitlist. I cannot install Baud. I cannot photograph the chip. A normal human cannot put their hand on Baud's product within 48 hours, and ASIC startups by their nature won't deliver that tactility until first silicon, which on this team's timeline is 24+ months out. The artifact will come; it isn't here.

My 36-month forecast for Baud: tape-out happens but the Series A is a knife fight against MatX's and Etched's runway, and Baud lands as a $150M-$400M acqui-hire by a hyperscaler hungry for the Zambare+Taylor compiler-and-silicon pair -- most likely Meta's MTIA team or AWS Trainium, not an independent unicorn outcome. The mechanism is straightforward: Baud has the rarest founder pairing in the category but the smallest war chest, and frontier silicon does not reward seventh-place balance-sheets when six well-funded peers are racing the same NVIDIA roadmap.

The bull case that stings: DeepMind ~2013 is my Known Exception for pedigree-as-thesis, and the override was polymath cross-domain compounding -- Hassabis bridging neuroscience and game design. Zambare's IIT-Bombay-plus-Berkeley physics into deep-learning compilers into shipped consumer ML hardware *is* a polymath stack, and Taylor's NVIDIA-to-Enfabrica trajectory is the silicon polymath complement. If the multiplier-elimination math is real and the PyTorch compiler genuinely lights up FSDP2, Baud is the only entrant in the category that solves *both* the math and the developer surface -- Etched skipped the compiler, Taalas hardwired the model, MatX is closer but hasn't shown public PyTorch parity. That's a defensible bull case. What dismantles it for me today is the dossier's silence on a lead investor, on named design-partner labs, and on any third-party benchmark of the FPGA claim -- a 100x efficiency number with no independent confirmation in a category where everyone is claiming 10-100x is the kind of assertion that gets priced like a story until silicon proves it. The verdict flips to Invest if Baud surfaces a named frontier-lab design partner (Anthropic, xAI, Mistral) with paid compute commitments before Series A close. Until then, I'm out -- with a bubbe's "one foot in love" for these two operators specifically.
