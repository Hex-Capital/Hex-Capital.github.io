# Maquoketa Research -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Maquoketa Research is an Anduril-shaped silhouette on the hardware axis -- vertically integrated, RF/mixed-signal, airframes in-house -- arriving in 2026 when Anduril is already $30.5B and Shield AI is $12.7B, which means Maquoketa Research is doing the right *kind* of company at exactly the moment my "find them before it becomes consensus" filter inverts hardest. The dimensional signature is Capital-Intensive Hardware at the top, Magically Weird at the bottom, and that pairing is its own diagnosis: the category is no longer transgressive, it is the YC drone-stack arms race.

Anduril (2017) is the load-bearing rung for the hardware sleeve, and the mechanism was Palmer Luckey having already shipped Oculus from a garage to a Facebook acquisition *before* he walked into defense -- the founder had built a comparable physical product to scale in an adjacent domain, and that prior trajectory was what made me sign the tattoo, not the defense thesis itself. Maquoketa Research has Alejandro Hernandez out of the Berkeley Wireless Research Center on 140 GHz MIMO phased arrays, which is genuinely rare RF IP and the closest thing in the dossier to a prior-product trajectory -- but academic mmWave papers are not Oculus, and Evan Yeager coming from SpiderRock derivatives-trading software is the wrong half of an "ex-FAANG PM discovering the problem in a memo" pattern that I structurally avoid. The Maquoketa Research origin is technical adjacency, not autobiographical defense; nobody in the four-founder team appears to have served, watched a Switchblade fail in-theater, or grown up *of* the operator community the way Chase Lochmiller was *of* the oil patch.

The HQ Trivia (2017) failure mechanism was unresolved co-founder authority detonating after product-market fit -- Yusupov and Kroll could not name a tiebreaker when the bet got hard. Maquoketa Research lists four co-founders -- Yeager (CEO), Hernandez (CTO), Williams (Chief ME), Muchow (COO) -- which triggers my "multiple founders, always a recipe for disaster" reflex on count alone, but the role differentiation is unusually clean for a pre-seed: the titles are functionally non-overlapping rather than three people sharing a co-CEO smell. The dossier gives me no equity split and no operating agreement, so I am defaulting near midpoint with a small uptick for the clarity of the CEO designation, not the absence of risk. Four founders in a hardware company that will need to make procurement-cycle judgment calls under DoD pressure is the exact terrain where the HQ Trivia pattern shows up at Series A, not Day 1.

Mechanism analysis on Magically Weird: the loitering munition market is forecast at $13.26B by 2030, the Pentagon is buying ~30,000 OWA drones at ~$5,000/unit under Drone Dominance, and Defense One has already named AeroVironment, Performance Drone Works, and Swarm Aero as Replicator selectees -- Maquoketa Research is conspicuously not on that list. Over 18 months, the non-consensus discount compounds *backwards* here: every additional YC batch will produce more OWA startups, every additional Anduril contract will widen the incumbent's procurement moat, and the $5,000/unit ceiling will compress margins for whoever is not the default supplier. This is the inverse of the Crusoe (~2018) setup, where credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds and the founder still had the social license to walk the well pads -- here Maquoketa Research is wearing the same defense-hardware costume as ten better-funded peers and arriving without the operator-community origin that would make the costume real.

Capital-Intensive Frontier Hardware is where Maquoketa Research scores highest, and the parallel is SpaceX (2008) on the axis alone: vertically integrated US manufacturing including in-house RF, mixed-signal, power electronics, airframes, ground control software is the unsexy-and-hard category I will always show up for, and Hernandez's phased-array depth is a real technical artifact, not a slide. The category passes my filter; Maquoketa Research as the specific bet inside it does not, because the SpaceX analogy only loads when the founder is Musk-shaped on origin, and the Maquoketa Research dossier gives me Berkeley + derivatives-software, not lived defense.

Counterfactual on Physical-World Verifiability: to flip Maquoketa Research from a 5 to a 9 on the touch-and-observe axis, the dossier would need a public flight-test video, a named DoD test-range deployment, or a Pentagon SBIR Phase II announcement I could verify on my own. None of that exists -- the "quadruple mission success rates" claim references Ukraine data, not a Maquoketa Research engagement, and the YC page lists Elk Grove Village, IL while search snippets describe a San Francisco footprint, which means I cannot even confirm where the manufacturing line is. The Flock Safety (~2018) Wi-Fi-name origin is unavailable to me here on principle: this is a product I will never encounter at a Four Seasons.

Forecast at 36 months: Maquoketa Research either becomes a sub-$50M talent-and-RF-IP acqui-hire into AeroVironment or Anduril -- Hernandez's phased-array work is the asset, not the OWA airframe -- or it dies after failing to win a Replicator follow-on while $5,000/unit incumbents underprice it. The mechanism is consensus-category compression: in a 30,000-unit DoD procurement targeting $5K/unit, the second-best vertically-integrated startup loses to the better-capitalized vertically-integrated incumbent, and the IP that survives is the RF stack.

The strongest case against my forecast is the Anduril (2017) counter-precedent itself: in 2017 every credible VC I knew had moral or capital-intensity objections to defense, I wrote the tattoo check anyway, and the consensus penalty I am applying to Maquoketa Research now is the exact heuristic I overrode then. If Hernandez's 140 GHz MIMO phased-array work is genuinely a generational seeker advantage -- the dossier flags it as Berkeley Wireless Research Center caliber -- and DoD multi-sources OWA the way it multi-sources every critical platform, Maquoketa Research becomes a default second supplier and a $500M outcome before Series B. What dismantles the bull case for me is the founder-origin gap: Palmer Luckey had Oculus, Chase Lochmiller had the oil patch, John Hanke had Keyhole, and Maquoketa Research has four technically competent operators with no autobiographical contact with the defense problem -- which is exactly the "replaceable by a polymath generalist in a quarter" failure mode I now watch for. The single verifiable condition that flips this to an Invest: a named DoD program office signing Maquoketa Research to an SBIR Phase II or a Replicator follow-on selection before demo day, at which point actual procurement traction overrides the consensus-category penalty and I would re-engage on the round.
