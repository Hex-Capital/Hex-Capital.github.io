# Indexable -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Andrew Gazelka has shipped real artifacts -- Hyperion (1k stars), nushell contributions, "#1 r/rust for 24 hours" -- which makes Indexable a shipper-founder pre-revenue infra bet in a category where E2B already serves "roughly half the Fortune 500" (VentureBeat, Jul 2025) and Modal just closed $355M Series C at a $4.65B mark (Tech Startups, May 2026). That pairing -- demonstrated execution rhythm at the top of my ladder, zero revenue curve at the bottom -- is the analytical signature here. Indexable is the kind of company where I want to like the founder more than the market structure lets me.

Perplexity (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on the dimension I score Indexable highest: a founder who ships between conversations. Aravind Srinivas would "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and Gazelka's GitHub footprint -- Hyperion Minecraft engine at 1k stars, contributor to nushell at 39.6k stars, ex-xAI Rust/systems profile, prior BountyBot cofounder (acquired, per search snippet) -- is the closest shipping-history profile in this batch to what I look for at cold-inbound stage. Where Indexable diverges from the Perplexity template is that Aravind was attacking a category (consumer answer engines) where no funded incumbent had yet declared, while Gazelka is shipping a fork primitive into a sub-market where Modal, E2B, and Daytona already have customer logos and capital.

Revenue velocity is where Indexable's dossier reads worst against my framework, and the rung that defines the floor is the generic "AI company with no growth curve disclosed" pattern I've been calling out since Tim Ferriss #863 -- "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." Indexable shows 11 stars on `indexable-inc/index`, 11 on `docs`, no pricing page, no customer count, no MRR, no logos. That isn't a pre-seed default I can wave through, because the comparison set inside agent-sandbox infra -- E2B powering Perplexity/Groq/Manus/Hugging Face per VentureBeat -- has visible enterprise adoption. The Harvey (2023) test is "$3B→$5B→$8B in months"; the inverse, an infra company with no disclosed pilot, in a category whose buyers have already chosen, is the silence that reads as a signal.

The catalyst here is real but not Indexable's to claim. Coding-agent runtimes -- Claude Code, Codex, Devin -- hitting production in 2024-2025 is a genuine 24-month window, the same window E2B priced into its July 2025 Series A and Daytona pivoted into in early 2025 (Northflank, Apr 2026). Over 18 months, the mechanism by which this dimension compounds against Indexable is straightforward: each quarter Modal ($466M total) and E2B add primitives, the marginal value of Indexable's millisecond-fork advantage compresses unless Gazelka has signed reference customers locking in the fork API surface. The catalyst opened a window; the window is being closed by competitors with two-orders-of-magnitude more capital.

For Indexable's single-miracle architecture to flip from a 8/12 to a top-quartile placement, one condition would have to be true: a named agent-runtime customer (Anthropic, Cognition, an OpenAI Codex team) running production load on the KVM fork primitive with a published latency benchmark. The dossier isolates the miracle cleanly -- forking a running VM including process memory and DB state in milliseconds -- and Gazelka's xAI/Rust/Hyperion background is the systems profile to execute it. This is genuinely one miracle, not three. But "isolated miracle" without a counterparty validating the latency claim is an engineering hypothesis, not a Color Genomics-style workflow-on-top-of-hard-thing bet where the workflow is already in customer hands.

Color Genomics is the single anchor on index position. I co-founded Color in 2015 specifically because workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing harvests a category tailwind without taking chemistry risk -- and Indexable's pitch is structurally identical, workflow infrastructure on top of commodity virtualization harvesting the agent-runtime tailwind. The dossier evidence that pins Indexable below Color on this axis is the competitor stack: Color owned the workflow layer because no funded incumbent was running it; Indexable is trying to take a workflow position E2B already holds with Fortune 500 distribution and $32M of capital behind it. Same shape, occupied chair.

My 36-month forecast: Indexable gets acqui-hired by Modal, E2B, or a hyperscaler (Cloudflare/Vercel) for $20-80M between mid-2027 and late-2028, the fork primitive shipped as a feature inside an existing sandbox SKU, Gazelka takes a principal-engineer or founding-team role on the acquirer's agent-infra org. The mechanism is the one I named above -- Modal's $355M war chest funds the in-memory fork primitive, E2B's distribution makes procurement a one-decision purchase, and Indexable's technical edge becomes a hire-the-team outcome rather than an independent compounding business.

The strongest objection is that Gazelka's fork primitive is qualitatively different, not incrementally better -- if agent runtimes need to spawn thousands of parallel exploration branches per task with full memory/DB state forked, that workload doesn't run on Firecracker microVMs without months of hypervisor rework, and Modal's GPU-attached sandbox architecture isn't trivially refactorable either. Under that mechanism Indexable becomes the substrate every agent runtime needs and the "index on agent workloads" thesis kicks in -- structurally the Stripe (2010) shape. I don't buy it as the modal outcome because the dossier shows zero customer references, zero benchmarks, zero adoption signal at YC Demo Day proximity, and in the AI cycle real growth discloses itself -- per Tim Ferriss #863, silence on the curve is the curve. The verdict flips to Invest the moment Indexable publishes a named-customer benchmark (Cognition, Anthropic, a Cursor-tier agent platform) showing the fork latency at production load, because that is the one falsifiable artifact that converts the engineering claim into the velocity signal.
