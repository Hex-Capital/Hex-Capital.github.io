# Andco -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Andco is a competent repeat-founder team — Ryan Xue and Michael Lemm, fresh off the Layup→Whatfix exit announced November 2025 — pointing AI agents at personal injury paralegal workflows in a vertical where EvenUp already raised $385M at a $2B valuation. That single pairing tells me what kind of company Andco is: a high-quality operator team running into a market that is already being capitalized at thirty to fifty times their available oxygen, attacking a problem whose solved state is "PI firms hire fewer paralegals." This is the AI-copilot-for-a-vertical pattern I describe in my pass example almost word for word, dressed in agentic clothing.

On Consequence Magnitude — the only dimension I actually start with — Andco sits between the floor and the middle of my ladder. The HIGH rung is OpenAI: civilization-rewriting consequences that justified an apology letter to my LPs. The MID rung is DoorDash: not a technology breakthrough, but food access for hundreds of millions, which is why Keith led that seed despite the missing technical wedge. The LOW rung is the "AI for accountants, AI for recruiters" YC archetype I pass on weekly. Andco is one rung above that floor — plaintiff PI is a real $61B category per Fortune's October 2025 EvenUp coverage, and faster case workup arguably accelerates settlement for injured plaintiffs — but if Andco fully succeeds, the visible change in the world is that Filevine-using PI firms run leaner and process more cases per partner. Nobody's life is fundamentally different. That is incremental on a civilizational scale, even if it is meaningful on a P&L scale.

Non-Linear Technical Approach is where Andco is weakest, and this is the dimension that most often kills a deal for me. HIGH rung: Rocket Lab's Rutherford engine — electric turbopumps, 3D-printed combustion chambers, a genuine physics-and-manufacturing wedge that made small-sat launch radically cheaper. MID rung: Impossible Foods synthesizing heme — well-understood biochemistry pushed to industrial scale. LOW rung: my own pass example, the wrapper-not-wedge AI copilot. Andco sits at the LOW rung. The dossier explicitly says "no public IP, patents, regulatory licensing, or proprietary data assets disclosed." The differentiation versus EvenUp, Supio, and Eve is positioning — "multi-channel agentic execution across fax/voice/SMS" — which is a product roadmap, not a technical secret. EvenUp at $385M raised and Supio at $91M can build voice agents over carrier phone trees in a quarter. This is technology risk I am not being paid to take, because there is no proprietary technology to retire.

Founder as Learning Machine is Andco's strongest dimension and the one that prevents an outright dismissal. HIGH rung is the 19-year-old MIT dropout building Mach Industries against Lockheed — irreverent, outsider, learning velocity off the charts. MID rung is Tony Xu at DoorDash, immigrant outsider with the operator's grind. LOW rung is the scripted-resume founder reciting a playbook. Xue and Lemm sit between MID and HIGH on credentials but conspicuously below MID on the axis I actually care about: they are domain experts now, not naive outsiders. Lemm is a Harvard Law JD and ex-Kirkland & Ellis attorney — that is exactly the "domain expertise over learning rate" profile I deprioritize. The Layup→Whatfix exit is real evidence of execution, but a fast acqui-hire is not the same signal as a founder who pivoted from first principles in a technical domain. Repeat-YC competence: yes. First-principles audacity: not visible in the dossier.

Improbable Ambition Level: Andco scores near the floor. HIGH rung Hermeus is building Mach 5 passenger aircraft; MID rung Impossible Foods rewired animal protein; LOW rung is "a YC company solving a known problem with a well-understood approach." Andco is the LOW rung verbatim — pre-litigation case workup is a known workflow, the approach is the same agentic LLM stack every funded competitor is shipping, and the dossier's own framing ("hundreds of cases and thousands of requests each month") is execution metrics, not a stated ambition that provokes incredulity. Path to Radical Affordability is similarly thin: Curai aims at near-zero-cost primary care, Guardant collapsed the cost of biopsy, and Andco's value capture is a per-firm SaaS that lets law firms keep more of the contingency fee. Plaintiffs do not pay less; firms pay fewer paralegals. That is redistribution of existing spend inside the legal services value chain, which my framework explicitly flags as low-consequence by construction.

Forecast: in 36 months Andco is either acqui-hired by EvenUp, Supio, or Filevine itself for $40–150M, or it has reached $4–12M ARR and is a thinning point feature inside a larger PI platform's bundle. The mechanism is straightforward — when three competitors with a combined $523M of capital are growing ARR 2x to 5x annually in the same buyer base, a pre-seed entrant with founder-market fit but no technical moat compresses into either an exit or a feature. That outcome is consistent with the high/low signature: strong operators, weak wedge, in a market already past its window for a new platform winner.

The strongest opposing case invokes my DoorDash exception: execution-heavy, technically unremarkable, but a high-velocity team in a real category can compound into a planetary outcome. Lemm's Harvard Law plus Kirkland & Ellis pedigree is genuine founder-market fit for selling into PI partners; Xue and Lemm shipped Layup to a Whatfix outcome in under three years; the YC page reports the product is already live with "many high volume firms." That is the bull. It stings because pre-seed I usually need exactly this profile to get interested. What dismantles it is that DoorDash's consequence magnitude was food access for hundreds of millions of consumers — Andco's consequence ceiling is paralegal headcount inside ~50,000 U.S. PI firms, which is not a planetary number on any axis. The condition that would flip my verdict: Andco demonstrating, within six months, that its multi-channel voice/fax agent achieves a measurably higher first-call-resolution rate against named insurance carriers than EvenUp or Supio can replicate — i.e., proof of an actual technical moat in carrier-side automation rather than a positioning narrative. Without that, this is a Pass on the Khosla framework, and I would tell Xue directly: build the thing that replaces the PI firm, not the thing that makes it 3x more efficient.
