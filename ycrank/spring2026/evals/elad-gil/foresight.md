# Foresight -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Foresight is a real-catalyst, no-curve company -- the LLM behavioral simulation step-function that funded **Aaru** at $1B in Dec 2025 and **Simile** at $100M in Feb 2026 is the same window Foresight stepped through, but the dossier names exactly one customer (Caudalie) and zero revenue numbers, which in this cycle is the thesis-killing absence. The signature pairing here is *catalyst present, curve missing*, and in an AI cohort where "silence on growth is itself a negative signal" that pairing usually means a vertical app that arrived at the party after the bouncers (Simile, Aaru) and the longtime regulars (Quantilope) had already taken the booths.

**Anduril** (2022) is my highest-rung catalyst comparison and it's instructive precisely because Foresight fails the comparison in the same place every vertical-AI deal fails it. Anduril rode a *Foresight-specific* catalyst -- Ukraine revealing Pentagon software inadequacy in a 24-month window where Trae Stephens and Palmer Luckey had differentially earned the right to exploit it. Foresight's catalyst -- "LLM behavioral simulation quality crossed enterprise-usable threshold in 2024-2025" -- is real, but it is a *category-wide* catalyst that opened the door for Aaru's $50M+ Series A and Simile's $100M Series A on essentially the same week Foresight was launching at YC. Eytan Rozenblum and Antoine Bertrand are not differentially positioned for the window; they are walking through a door already propped open by checks 100x their size. That's the gap from Anduril to Foresight on this axis.

**Lyft Series C** is my low-rung reference for what happens when you back the #2-or-#3 against named incumbents in a market with already-declared leaders, and the Foresight dossier triggers that same pattern with unusual literalness. Quantilope has $40M raised and *300+ enterprise CPG clients including Nestle, Kraft Heinz, and Estee Lauder* -- which is to say Quantilope has already sold to the named buyers Foresight needs, and is layering AI onto incumbent panel relationships (YouGov acquired Yabble, Toluna launched HarmonAIze per the Ditto 2026 map). Above Foresight sit Simile ($100M, Index Ventures, Fei-Fei Li, Karpathy) and Aaru ($50M+, Redpoint, Accenture Song partnership). Foresight is sandwiched -- horizontal giants above, vertical incumbent below -- and the dossier offers no distribution wedge that explains why a Caudalie-style buyer doesn't just buy Quantilope's synthetic add-on next quarter.

On revenue velocity, the mechanism analysis is unforgiving over an 18-month horizon: Foresight's only public traction is "Caudalie 15 tests/week at 25x lower cost per insight" -- a unit-economics line, not a curve, and the dossier confirms no pricing page exists on foresight.tt as of May 2026. In the AI cohort, real growth is trivially disclosable -- **Harvey** (2023) tracked $3B → $5B → $8B in months and every angel knew it in real time. Foresight's silence on MRR, logo count, MoM, anything compounding, is the **Harvey**-inverse signal. Over 18 months either Foresight breaks out a second and third named CPG logo with disclosed test volumes, or the synthetic-prediction validity question (price elasticity, conjoint accuracy vs. real outcomes) becomes the gating procurement objection Quantilope's sales team weaponizes in every bake-off.

On founder shipping rhythm, the counterfactual is what would have to be true to flip Foresight a tier: Eytan Rozenblum did a prior YC in 2023 building PrompTessor / Reverse Prompt and pivoted Fluentive AI into Foresight with Antoine Bertrand -- that is shipping-and-iterating evidence, not a credentials-only profile, and it keeps Foresight above the **Lyft**-style promoter floor. To get to the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind tier where I write a check on rhythm alone, the dossier would need a public artifact cadence -- a Hugging Face accuracy benchmark against real CPG panel data, a published methodology paper, a second iteration of the Caudalie case with conjoint specifics -- not just a LinkedIn title change from Fluentive to Foresight. The 2023 pivot is honest evidence of resilience; it is not yet evidence of the between-meetings shipping cadence that triggered my Perplexity inbound.

On index position, the single-anchor parallel is **Color Genomics** (2015), which I co-founded explicitly as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing rather than a chemistry bet -- and that is the architecture Foresight gestures at but does not occupy. Foresight wants to be the workflow layer on top of commodity LLM cognition, but the workflow layer in consumer insights is *already owned* by Quantilope's 300+ CPG seats, and the "commodity LLM" layer underneath is being verticalized by Aaru's Accenture Song partnership. Color worked because the sequencing chemistry was genuinely commodity and the insurance-billing workflow was genuinely unowned; Foresight's analogue fails on the second clause. On the single-miracle test, Foresight is borderline -- the miracle is "LLM synthetic consumers predict real conjoint and price-elasticity outcomes with enterprise-defensible accuracy," and that miracle is unresolved across the entire category per the dossier's own validity flag, so I'd call it 1.5 miracles in practice.

My 36-month forecast: Foresight gets tucked into Quantilope or a hybrid-panel incumbent (YouGov, Toluna) by Q2 2029 at sub-$30M as a synthetic-test feature acquisition, or it narrows hard to beauty-vertical-only behind the Caudalie wedge and plateaus at $1-3M ARR. The mechanism is straightforward: Simile and Aaru capture the enterprise-horizontal logos with their $100M+ war chests, Quantilope retains the CPG buyer relationships and bolts on synthetic, and Foresight's vertical-packaged-test positioning becomes a feature inside someone else's roadmap rather than a standalone P&L.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the Color-Genomics-shaped one: vertical focus on CPG with *packaged* test types (copy, conjoint, price elasticity, brand lift) is the workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-hard-thing pattern, Eytan's 2023 pivot is shipping rhythm disguised as a setback, the Caudalie 25x-cost-reduction line is "just working" not "false signal" in my TechCrunch framing, and horizontal Simile/Aaru leave the CPG-vertical wedge structurally open. It stings -- this is exactly the template that worked for **Abridge** (2023) and **Decagon** (2023) in their respective verticals. What dismantles it is the Quantilope fact: my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy asks whether the sub-market is "still wide open" or has declared incumbents, and CPG consumer insights has Quantilope sitting on the buyer relationships Foresight needs, layering AI from a position of switching-cost strength. Abridge entered a vertical where the incumbent (Nuance) was hobbled by Microsoft integration; Foresight is entering one where the incumbent is actively shipping the same feature. The flip condition is specific: a second and third named non-beauty CPG logo (Nestle, Unilever, P&G tier) disclosed in the next 90 days with disclosed test volumes, plus a published accuracy benchmark against real-panel outcomes on price elasticity. Absent that, this is a Pass with a Neutral score reflecting the real catalyst and the real founder iteration history.
