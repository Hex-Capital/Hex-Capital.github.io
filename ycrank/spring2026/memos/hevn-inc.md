## The Idea

- **Problem:** Non-US global internet companies run fragmented finance stacks (Mercury, Wise, Deel, Stripe) because Brex/Ramp/Mercury require a US EIN and exclude non-US entities (Slash blog, 2026; HEVN YC page).
- **Approach:** Virtual USD account on stablecoin rails (USD/USDC treasury) plus API/CLI-driven payouts in local currencies via local bank partners across 82+ countries on SEPA, SWIFT, ACH, PIX, UAEFTS (gethevn.com).
- **Differentiation:** Mercury/Brex/Ramp restrict to US-EIN companies (Slash blog, 2026); Wise lacks treasury+API operational primitives; HEVN bundles USD account, FX, contractor/vendor/payroll payouts in one API surface "built for humans and AI agents" (HEVN YC page).
- **Business Model:** No pricing page disclosed (gethevn.com fetch, May 2026); product advertises "<0.5% FX fees" on USD card (gethevn.com). [Inferred]: Revenue from FX spread, card interchange, and treasury yield on USD/USDC float, typical of stablecoin neobanks (BVNK blog, 2025).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment; adjacent stablecoin-neobank category is described as "core financial infrastructure" in 2025 (BVNK Blog, Dec 2025).
- **GTM / Distribution:** Waitlist for EU/US/Swiss/UAE bank accounts announced via Peter Volnov (Traders Union, 2026); [Inferred]: founder audience leverage given @p_volnov 463.2K X followers (xo2.com).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** No defensibility signals found in public sources beyond pre-existing local-bank partnerships implied across 82+ countries (gethevn.com).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Switching costs from API-embedded payouts + accumulated KYC/compliance approvals across multiple jurisdictions (EU, US, Swiss, UAE per Traders Union, 2026); unproven because product is in waitlist phase.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: US chartered neobanks (Mercury, Brex) face entity-eligibility constraints serving non-US companies and depend on US bank partners (Slash blog, 2026), creating a structural opening for a stablecoin-rails alternative that does not require US EIN.
- **Commoditization risk:** Stablecoin-neobank infra is increasingly available via BVNK (powers Deel, Worldpay, Flywire — BVNK blog, 2025) and Fasset ($51M raise — CoinDesk, May 2026), lowering the build cost for new entrants.

## Key Risks

- **Regulatory risk on stablecoin banking:** Holding treasury in USDC and offering "USD accounts" to non-US entities crosses banking/MTL/securities regimes that vary by EU/US/Swiss/UAE (Traders Union, 2026 — same jurisdictions HEVN targets); product depends on local bank partners in 82+ countries (gethevn.com) whose stablecoin posture is jurisdiction-specific.
- **Commoditization by infra providers:** BVNK already powers Deel, Worldpay, Flywire (BVNK blog, 2025) and Fasset raised $51M for stablecoin-neobank in emerging markets (CoinDesk, May 2026); the stablecoin-rails layer HEVN advertises is increasingly available as B2B infra.
- **Pivot execution risk:** Prior product (HOT Labs / HOT Wallet, crypto consumer) was distinct from current fintech B2B account; founders "stepped back from HOT Labs to rebuild fiat banking" (search snippet on Peter Volnov) — distribution and GTM playbooks do not transfer cleanly.
- **Incumbent acceleration:** Capital One closed acquisition of Brex for $5.15B in April 2026 (search snippet via TechFundingNews); Mercury reached $650M ARR and $5.2B valuation (CNBC, May 2026) — capital-rich incumbents may extend coverage to non-US entities.
- **Disclosure/registration risk:** Company lists San Francisco operations (YC page) but corporate address is Dover, DE (gethevn.com); founders' prior entities span UAE/BVI/HK (HEVN YC page) — cross-border structuring complexity for a regulated fintech.
