# Asendia AI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Asendia AI is a real-revenue wedge player ($500K+ within months, per YC page) trapped in the worst index position in the AI-recruiting stack: Mercor at $10B post-money (CNBC, Oct 2025) already defines the category, and Rihab Lajmi's staffing-agency niche is precisely the channel where the ATS incumbents Asendia AI integrates against -- Bullhorn, JobDiva, Lever -- have the most native distribution. The signature is revenue-velocity-without-index-protection: a competent team executing a wedge in a market whose workflow path belongs to somebody else.

Harvey (2023) is the template I want to test Asendia AI against on revenue velocity. Harvey went "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) because the curve was monotonic and the buyers were named AmLaw 100 logos -- that's "just working," not pilot tourism. Asendia AI shows the front-half of that pattern: $500K+ revenue at pre-seed is genuinely above the bar for YC P26, and SGF Global across 35+ countries plus FSTAFF "doubled hires per recruiter" (asendia.ai, May 2026) are concrete deployments rather than design-partner letters. Where Asendia AI diverges from the Harvey rung is that the YC page gives me a cumulative stock figure, not a MoM compounding curve, and "few months" is the only velocity disclosure I have -- in the AI cycle, silence on MoM is itself a tell because real growth is easy to disclose. I credit the revenue but I cannot credit the slope.

Lyft Series C is the failure mechanism I keep coming back to on index position. I passed on Lyft because being #2 in a network-effect market means you capture a fraction of #1's economics regardless of execution quality (20VC interview), and the same pattern shows up in Asendia AI's competitive frame: Mercor at $10B with $450M+ raised has the capital to compress feature-parity timelines, Paradox.ai owns retail/hospitality conversational hiring, and Moonhub's "Stella" sits on top of tech hiring. Rihab Lajmi's wedge -- staffing agencies with 50+ ATS integrations -- is defensible against Mercor specifically because Mercor is a marketplace not a SaaS, but the same ATS dependency is the channel-conflict trap: JobDiva and Bullhorn sell the seat licenses Asendia AI's "recruiter cloning" pitch implicitly cannibalizes, and they can build or partner-around the agentic layer at their leisure.

Market catalyst is where Asendia AI's pitch is thinnest. The dossier offers "LLM voice-agent quality crossed a viable threshold in 2024-2025" as the [Inferred] why-now, which is the generic AI-cycle catalyst, not a staffing-specific re-opening -- compare Anduril (2022) where Ukraine specifically broke Pentagon software procurement in a 24-month window. US staffing grew 1-2.1% in 2025-2026 (Staffing Industry Analysts) which is the definition of a steady-state market, and "great teams die in steady-state markets" is the rule I built around Stripe (2010) and Coinbase (2013). Over 18 months this compounds against Asendia AI: as voice-AI commodifies, every ATS incumbent ships a "recruiter agent" feature, and the catalyst that opened Asendia AI's window also opens it for Bullhorn's product team.

Decagon (2023) is the single-anchor parallel on the miracle architecture. Decagon assumed one validated capability -- LLM accuracy at customer-support workflows -- and the rest was GTM and integration plumbing; that's the clean single-miracle shape I funded. Asendia AI fits this shape: voice/SMS/WhatsApp screening agents on top of off-the-shelf models, ATS integrations as deterministic engineering work, SOC 2 / HIPAA / GDPR / ISO 27001 as compliance execution (asendia.ai, May 2026). No new physics, no regulatory unlock required, no novel chemistry -- the dossier isolates the miracle properly, which is why I rate this dimension higher than the headline competitive picture would suggest.

Founder execution rhythm is the dimension where I'd want a counterfactual: for Asendia AI to flip from Neutral to Invest, Rihab Lajmi or Badis Zormati would need named prior shipped products with user-feedback loops, not the cloud-engineer-at-Microsoft-and-Google credential stack the dossier supplies (OK Reporter; affluense.ai). The Perplexity (2022) origin pattern was Aravind showing up "a week later with a thing we discussed built" -- I have no equivalent artifact for Asendia AI. What partially substitutes is that Lajmi and Zormati shipped Asendia AI itself to $500K+ revenue with 50+ ATS integrations in roughly a year, which is real execution evidence; it's just bottom-up evidence I have to infer from the company's existence, not founder-history evidence I can underwrite from prior products.

My 36-month forecast for Asendia AI: reaches $8-15M ARR by selling agentic screening into mid-market staffing agencies, then gets acquired by Bullhorn or a JobDiva-adjacent ATS for $60-120M as the incumbents bolt on agentic capabilities; survives as a product line rather than emerging as the category-defining workflow infrastructure. The mechanism is the channel-conflict trap I named -- Asendia AI's distribution depends on the ATS layer whose seat-license economics it threatens, which caps the independent-company outcome while making it an attractive tuck-in.

The strongest bull case against my forecast: Decagon (2023) looked like exactly this profile in 2023 -- workflow infrastructure on top of off-the-shelf LLMs in a category (customer support) where giants like Zendesk and Salesforce had native distribution and channel-conflict incentives, and Decagon compounded anyway because the agentic-completeness gap took the incumbents two years to close. If Asendia AI's ATS integrations become the de-facto agentic data layer for staffing in the same way -- and if "$500K+ in months" is actually 30%+ MoM rather than a flattering cumulative -- then the Bullhorn-acquisition forecast under-clubs the outcome. What dismantles this for me today is that AI Market Clarity (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) did not list recruiting as a wide-open sub-market the way it listed support and accounting, and Mercor's $10B already-funded position is a much harder ceiling than Decagon faced from Zendesk. The verifiable condition that would flip me to Invest: Asendia AI disclosing MoM revenue growth above 25% sustained over six months with three named enterprise (not agency) logos in the F500 -- at that point the index position concern resolves into a Harvey-style "consensus pays" entry and I'd write a check.
