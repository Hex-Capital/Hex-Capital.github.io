# Coasty -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Coasty is a permissionless-leverage shell wrapped around a single-sided AI SaaS — the OSS repo `coasty-ai/open-computer-use`, the dev.to authorship by Nitish Kovuru, the $19/$50/$99 self-serve tiers, and the Product Hunt 19 Feb 2026 launch all read as code-native distribution, but the product itself is one user calling one `/v1/predict` endpoint with no Nth-user-makes-N-minus-1-more-valuable mechanic anywhere in the dossier. That high/low pairing — strong distribution architecture, absent network compounding — is Coasty's analytical signature, and it's the same shape as **Perplexity** (2022), which I funded only because the specific-knowledge gate cleared at the Aravind-Srinivas-Berkeley-ML-PhD ceiling and the timing was pre-consensus AI. Coasty clears neither at that ceiling.

On leverage architecture, **Stack Overflow** (2008) is the HIGH-rung analog — Atwood and Spolsky distributed through developer writing and organic search before a single paid hire — and Coasty mirrors that shape directionally with the open-source `coasty-ai/open-computer-use` repo, Kovuru's dev.to posts, and the cal.com/coasty/15min booking funnel. The divergence is that Stack Overflow's distribution was the *product itself* (every Q&A page was both content and acquisition), while Coasty's open-source repo and PLG tiers are channels feeding a closed `/v1/predict` API; the leverage is permissionless but it doesn't compound the way developer-Q&A pages did. Coasty's GitHub star count returned 404 in research and the Product Hunt rank/upvote count was not retrieved, so the distribution is structurally right but the velocity is unverified — which is exactly the gap that keeps this dimension out of top-quartile territory.

The lowest-dimension ladder is network value, where the canonical failure mechanism is single-sided SaaS economics: each Coasty customer running screen-grounded agents in their own sandboxed VM generates trajectory data the founders claim could compound, but the dossier explicitly flags this as "[Inferred]" with "customer/usage volume undisclosed." This is the exact shape I waived for **Perplexity** (2022) under the Known Exception — but Perplexity cleared three gates emphatically (permissionless API distribution, Aravind's retrieval PhD, pre-consensus AI timing in early 2022), and Coasty's timing is post-Anthropic-Computer-Use-API (Oct 2024) and post-OpenAI-Operator (Jan 2025), which means the category narrative has already arrived. No exception clears.

Coasty's specific-knowledge dimension I'll evaluate by mechanism over an 18-month window: Prateek Jannu's ex-Microsoft ML Researcher background and the auto-grad engine on 200K+ parameters give him the floor to *implement* vision-grounded agents, but the moat compounds only if his trajectory data from 100+ step multimodal sessions becomes proprietary training fuel before Anthropic ships a Claude release that closes the 82% vs. 72% OSWorld delta. That is a race between Jannu's data accumulation and the frontier-lab quarterly release cadence — and frontier labs ship faster than a 2-person team accumulates labeled trajectory data. The mechanism degrades, it doesn't compound.

On Coasty's market and pivot room, the counterfactual is what would flip this dimension a tier. If the dossier had named even one Fortune 500 deployment matching the "Enterprises hesitant to deploy AI agents" framing from Jannu's Humans in the Loop interview, the desktop-RPA wedge would become defensible against Anthropic and OpenAI on trust grounds rather than benchmark grounds — and pivot room into vertical RPA (legal, healthcare, finance) opens cleanly. Without that named logo, Coasty is competing on a public benchmark against four well-funded adjacents — Browserbase ($67.5M), Kernel ($22M Series A), Browser Use (~$17M), Anchor Browser ($6M) — at pre-seed scale. The pivot room is structurally present, the runway to use it is not.

Coasty's founder trio compares cleanly to **Notion** (2013) on this axis — Ivan Zhao had no decade-long track record either, and the integrity reads were quiet rather than legible. Kovuru's origin story (AI agent deleted his prod database, motivating sandboxed VMs) is a personally-discovered-problem signal in my framework, not a competitor-response signal — that's the Red Flag I check for and Coasty passes it. Jannu and Kovuru both ship in public (LinkedIn, dev.to, GitHub org, Product Hunt), neither volunteers honesty unprompted, no contested prior exits surface. The 10-year-partner test reads clean but unproven — which is the Notion shape, not the Stack Overflow shape.

The 36-month forecast: Coasty is acquihired by UiPath, Anthropic, or OpenAI for $15-40M between 2027 and 2029 as the OSWorld benchmark delta closes on a Claude or GPT release, OR persists as a sub-$3M ARR vertical RPA niche serving 50-200 mid-market customers without breaking out. The mechanism producing this is the dimensional signature itself — permissionless distribution gets Coasty to product-market traction, but the absence of network compounding means each customer is economically independent and the moat decays linearly with frontier-model improvements rather than compounding with usage.

The strongest bull case against this forecast: Jannu's sandboxed-VM thesis is a real enterprise-trust wedge that Anthropic and OpenAI structurally cannot replicate without rebuilding their entire deployment model, and the trajectory data from 100+ step multimodal sessions becomes a proprietary RLHF corpus that the frontier labs don't have because they don't operate the sandboxed VMs. If Coasty signs even three Fortune 500 pilots in the next 12 months, the dimensional signature flips — network value emerges through the data flywheel, specific knowledge becomes top-quartile through proprietary trajectory data, and the leverage architecture compounds through customer-grounded fine-tuning. The dossier evidence that dismantles this case is the explicit "no enterprise logos, LOIs, or SOC 2 evidence found" — at Summer 2026 with this team size and disclosure thinness, the enterprise-trust wedge is a hypothesis the founders have not yet executed against. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict to Invest: a named Fortune 500 deployment or $250K+ ARR signed enterprise contract disclosed publicly before Coasty's seed extension.
