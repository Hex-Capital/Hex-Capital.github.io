# Surtr Defense Systems -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Anshul Ahluwalia and Cameron Fiore both shipped autonomy code inside Shield AI and Anduril before starting Surtr Defense Systems, which is the highest signal in the whole dossier and the reason I read it twice. The lowest signal is that Surtr Defense Systems is a software fusion layer I cannot install, photograph, or hand to a friend at a Four Seasons cafe -- ParallaxOS lives behind a website that returned 403 at fetch time, and no third-party benchmark exists. That pairing -- founders who lived the autonomy problem inside the two best programs in the country, selling an OS I cannot touch into a category every defense fund is now chasing -- is what Surtr Defense Systems actually is.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril in 2017 is the load-bearing analogy for Surtr Defense Systems on founder origin: not because Luckey shipped Oculus and then defense, but because the autonomy engineers who built Lattice's guts are now the talent pool that founds every credible C-UAS company in 2026, and Cameron Fiore was one of them on aircraft autonomy. Anshul Ahluwalia did planning and controls for Group 3 and Group 5 aircraft at Shield AI, which is the exact decision layer ParallaxOS now claims to replicate across third-party sensors. Where Surtr Defense Systems diverges from the Luckey rung is the absence of a prior shipped product owned by the founders themselves -- they were inside the systems, not the people whose names were on them -- so the lived-experience score is real but one notch below the Anduril template.

HQ Trivia in 2017 is the failure mode I always reach for on co-founder governance: Yusupov and Kroll without a tiebreaker, and the product imploded under the unresolved authority before the metrics did. Surtr Defense Systems names Ahluwalia as CEO and Fiore as CTO with clean role delineation on LinkedIn, and the two-person team has the Shield AI shared-foxhole history that usually predicts founder durability -- so the HQ pattern does not appear in the Surtr Defense Systems dossier in any obvious form. What is missing is a documented vesting and exit-conflict protocol, which at pre-seed I do not punish but I do note: silence here reads as "they have not had the conversation yet."

The "magically weird" filter is where Surtr Defense Systems struggles, and the mechanism is straightforward: in 2017 Anduril was the contrarian defense bet because credible VCs had moral objections, but in Spring 2026 with $29B in publicly announced C-UAS contracts in Q1 alone and Anduril's Oct 2025 Army Fire Control Solution win, open-architecture C-UAS C2 is the consensus DoD modernization slide. Surtr Defense Systems is not finding founders before consensus -- consensus is funding founders to compete with the company that already won the program of record. Eighteen months from now this thesis compounds badly: every YC batch will have a defense-autonomy software play, and the price of entry inverts.

Counterfactual on capital-intensity for Surtr Defense Systems: the score flips a tier if ParallaxOS ships with even one proprietary sensor reference design or a domain-specific fusion model trained on classified multi-INT data the founders accessed at Shield AI -- something tangible the primes cannot rebuild in a quarter. As written, Surtr Defense Systems is a software OS sitting on top of other people's radars, EO/IR, and RF detectors, which puts it in the lane of "polymath generalist could rebuild it in a quarter" rather than the Crusoe-class capital-intensive moat that compounds with time. The defense category is regulated, which earns partial credit -- ITAR, clearances, FOCI -- but no public evidence of cleared personnel exists in the dossier.

Flock Safety is the single-anchor parallel for physical verifiability, and it is the comparison Surtr Defense Systems loses cleanly. I sourced Flock from a Wi-Fi network name at a hotel; the cameras were on poles I could photograph; a normal human could observe the artifact in their own life within 48 hours. Surtr Defense Systems is a C2 layer behind ITAR and a 403 website, which is not a flaw -- it is the nature of the category -- but it removes my entire sourcing method from the deal. I am writing this check on someone else's testimony about a screen, which is the opposite of how I have ever made money.

Thirty-six-month forecast for Surtr Defense Systems: acqui-hired by Anduril, RTX, or Lockheed for $40-100M as a talent-plus-IP deal once channel conflict with hardware partners stalls the open-OS thesis, with a non-trivial chance of a pivot to becoming one specific prime's preferred fusion layer. The mechanism is the channel-conflict risk the dossier itself names: radar and RF vendors have no reason to integrate with a software layer that disintermediates them from primes, and primes have every reason to acquire the autonomy talent before it scales independently. Ahluwalia and Fiore are too good at the technical work to fail outright, and too early to displace Lattice.

The strongest objection to my forecast is that Surtr Defense Systems is the Anduril 2017 pattern run forward by exactly the right people -- ex-Anduril and ex-Shield AI engineers attacking the closed-stack incumbent with DoD's own open-architecture mandate as cover -- and that the bull case is a 10x outcome inside a $20.31B by 2030 market where the procurement window MarketsandMarkets describes is genuinely open. That case stings because the founder substrate is real and the policy tailwind is real. It loses on two facts: Surtr Defense Systems is selling into a category where the consensus has already arrived, and the open-OS thesis depends on hardware partner adoption that no signed integration LOI in the dossier supports. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest is a single named radar or RF OEM publicly committing to ParallaxOS as their default C2 layer, or a Five Eyes MoD pilot contract -- either one and I am calling Ahluwalia the same week.
