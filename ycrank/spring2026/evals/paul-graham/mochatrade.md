# Mochatrade -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Mochatrade is a compliance-arbitrage trio wrapped around a 20x leverage derivative — high on schlep, low on hacker genealogy, and the CEO Utkarsh Sinha already shipped one consumer-crypto artifact (Martian Wallet, 2M installs, $2B volume, exit) before walking into the Mochatrade YC batch. That single prior outcome is what keeps me reading. Three IIT friends of ten years, an INR/UPI rail into TSLA/NVDA perps, and a stated use-of-funds line item that reads "build compliant pathways." That is not the AI-wrapper deck I usually pass on.

The HIGH rung on schlep for me is **Coinbase** (2012) — Brian Armstrong wanted the regulatory grind everyone else in the batch was unconsciously routing around, and the grind itself became the moat. The shared mechanism is the same in Mochatrade: the FEMA/LRS perimeter in India, SEBI scrutiny of offshore derivatives, oracle/liquidation infrastructure for US equities that trade only 6.5 hours a day while the perp book runs 24/7 — these are exactly the unpleasant pieces a software-only YC applicant in the Spring 2026 batch is praying someone else solves first. Where Mochatrade diverges from the Coinbase analogue is the direction of the compliance vector. Armstrong was grinding *toward* a license a US regulator would eventually grant; Mochatrade is grinding toward a "non-US-person" carveout that depends on staying outside whichever regulator notices first. That is still schlep — it just may not compound into legitimacy the way **Coinbase** (2012) did.

No GitHub repos found for Utkarsh Sinha, Chetan Manda, or Parth Maheshwari. That is the low rung. The pattern that scares me here is the AI-wrapper pass example — a credentialed team with no hacker tribal markers, where the credentials (IIT, Columbia, McKinsey, Adobe, Navi, Sprinklr) do the work the GitHub trail would normally do. Hackers leave GitHub trails. Patrick Collison won the Young Scientist for Lisp before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz co-wrote RSS before **Reddit** (2005). Mochatrade's CTO Parth Maheshwari was on Photoshop Web at Adobe and built financial infrastructure at Navi — that is shipping real systems, not toy work, but it is shipping inside corporate org charts, which is exactly the signal my unstated pattern under-weights. The McKinsey line on Sinha's bio is the tell I have to actively talk myself past.

Animal resourcefulness pre-funding compounds differently when the founder has already done the unscalable lap once. Sinha building Martian Wallet to 2M installs and $2B in transaction volume is not a Mochatrade-specific cereal-box stunt, but over an 18-month horizon it is more predictive than a stunt would be — somebody who has already moved $2B in self-custodial crypto knows what onboarding 100,000 Indian retail traders onto a UPI-funded perp venue actually costs in support tickets, fraud, and oracle disputes. The dossier names no Mochatrade-specific hustle, which is the gap, but the prior $2B is a load-bearing receipt.

Organic-pain would flip a tier if the dossier contained one sentence — "I tried to buy NVDA from Bangalore and got rejected by my broker under LRS." It does not contain that sentence. The founders are Indian, the customer is the Indian demat holder (~210M of them per Business Standard, end-2025), and the inference that Sinha or Maheshwari personally wanted leveraged US equity exposure is plausible but unstated. Drew Houston told everyone about the USB stick. Mochatrade tells me about a "$100B+ market opportunity," which is the wrong register. The counterfactual is small but real: a single founder testimony about their own blocked trade would move this from 11/25 to 18/25 and the verdict with it.

Compare the cofounder configuration to **Airbnb** (2009) on one axis only: trio, long shared history, complementary coverage. Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk had RISD plus a technical hire; Mochatrade has CEO/CPO/CTO across IIT-KGP and IIT-G with ten years of friendship. The chemistry signal is as strong as anything I get from a dossier without an interview.

My 36-month forecast for Mochatrade: $8-20M run-rate from Indian and Southeast Asian retail perp volume by mid-2028, followed by an SEBI or RBI inquiry that forces re-domiciling the user-facing entity to UAE or Singapore, ending in an acquisition by a crypto-native perp venue (Bybit, OKX, or Hyperliquid's parent) in the $80-250M range as an equity-underlying module — not the Stripe-scale outcome the schlep framing tempts me to underwrite, because the compliance vector points away from legitimacy rather than toward it.

The strongest bull case against my middling read is that Mochatrade is **Coinbase** (2012) in 2010 clothing: Sinha is Armstrong with a prior exit instead of a rejected application, the schlep is named explicitly as a use of funds, the trio is **Airbnb** (2009)-shaped, and my Known Exception for **Stripe** (2010) — re-funding repeat YC founders who had already shipped commercially — would arguably apply to Sinha's Martian Wallet track record. The case stings because every individual element checks. What dismantles it is the product itself: 20x leverage on single-name volatile equities sold to emerging-market retail is not a schlep that compounds into a regulator's trust the way bitcoin custody did for Armstrong; it is a schlep that compounds into a regulator's enforcement file. The verifiable condition that flips me to Invest is a single signed memorandum with a GIFT City IFSCA-licensed entity or a public Mochatrade-authored technical writeup of their oracle and liquidation engine — either one closes the hacker-genealogy gap or converts the regulatory grind into the **Coinbase** (2012)-shaped moat. Without one of those, Mochatrade is Neutral.
