# Regbase -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Regbase is the cleanest single-miracle structure I've seen this batch paired with the loudest silence on revenue -- Natalie Aresta-Katz lived the manual ESG regulatory workflow at Paul, Weiss across "dozens of countries and all U.S. states," and Ethan Shea built agentic infrastructure at Vercel, which means the technical and customer-empathy halves are real. But the dossier discloses zero named logos, zero ARR, zero MoM, in a Spring 2026 cycle where Legora just printed a $5.6B mark (TechCrunch, Apr 2026) and any AI workflow company with actual velocity discloses it. That high/low pairing -- structurally sound miracle, commercially mute -- is what Regbase is.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on catalyst, because the move that made me lead that round was identifying a 24-month re-opening (Ukraine ? Pentagon software inadequacy) inside a market peers had written off. Regbase tries the same template: multilingual LLM extraction crossing a usability threshold in 2024-2025 supposedly re-opens regulatory tracking. The mechanism is real -- ingesting non-English draft bills at paralegal cost was not possible in 2022 -- but Regbase's catalyst is *generic to every AI workflow company shipping right now*, where Anduril's was specific to one geopolitical event and one customer (DoD). FiscalNote at $84.8M ARR shrinking 22% YoY (10-Q, Sept 2025) confirms the incumbent layer is decaying, which is half a catalyst, but the dossier names no regulatory-side event -- no EU directive, no SEC climate rule, no ESG enforcement wave -- that opened a buyer-side window in the last 24 months. Catalyst is present but unsharp.

The Lyft Series C pass mechanism was simple: I refused to back the #2 in a market where the curve hadn't compounded for the #2. Regbase isn't Lyft -- it's not network-effects -- but the underlying failure mode rhymes: "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss #863). Regbase's traction section gives me a START pod feature, a Hiretop profile, and a customer claim of "Fortune 500 companies, global consultancies, and leading law firms" with no logos. That is *exactly* the "false signal" framing -- enterprise-pilot tourism phrased as customer base. Regbase at three employees in Spring 2026 should be able to disclose one named Fortune 500 by initials if the claim were load-bearing; the absence is the data.

Founder shipping rhythm in Regbase is split asymmetrically and that asymmetry matters more than the average. Shea at Vercel led security observability and contributed to agentic infrastructure -- that's concrete shipping at scale, mechanism-compatible with the Perplexity/Srinivas pattern of building between meetings. Aresta-Katz is the customer, not the builder -- NYU undergrad, Columbia MBA, Paul, Weiss ESG research -- and the dossier names no independent shipped artifact under her hand. Over 18 months this compounds two ways: if Shea is the velocity engine and Aresta-Katz is the design-partner conduit through Paul, Weiss alumni, that's a viable Brex-shape execution machine; if Aresta-Katz is the CEO-by-domain but bottlenecks product calls because she's translating customer pain rather than building, Shea leaves within 24 months. Watch the GitHub.

On index position, the counterfactual that would flip Regbase a full tier is signed coverage agreements with two of the Big Four consultancies plus a Fortune 100 legal team within 12 months -- because that would mean Regbase is becoming the multilingual sub-national layer that *Harvey and Legora integrate against* rather than compete with. As written, Regbase is positioned to fight Harvey, Legora, FiscalNote, Corlytics, Bloomberg Government, and Quorum simultaneously on overlapping surface area, which is the opposite of an index position. The dossier's own competitive frame ("Legora, Harvey, ChatGPT, and local counsel") names horizontal players who can plausibly close the multilingual gap with capital Regbase doesn't have -- Legora at $5.6B in April 2026 funds a multilingual sprint trivially.

Single-miracle is where Regbase actually earns its score. Compare Decagon (2023): one miracle -- LLM accuracy at customer-support task -- and the rest is GTM and product. Regbase's miracle is multilingual LLM extraction with citation-grounded low-hallucination output on regulatory text, which is one miracle and one that the foundation-model layer has substantially de-risked. No new physics, no new regulatory approval, no consumer-behavior shift required. This is precisely the "workflow infrastructure on top of a hard thing" Unstated Pattern -- the hard thing being multilingual statutory ingestion -- and Regbase sits architecturally where I want it.

Forecast, 36 months: Regbase is acquired by Thomson Reuters or Wolters Kluwer for $30-80M as a multilingual coverage tuck-in, or it stalls below $2M ARR and the team dissolves into Vercel/Harvey by mid-2028. The mechanism is the single-miracle/no-velocity pairing: the architecture is sound enough to be valuable to a consolidator, but without a disclosed velocity curve at the catalyst window's peak, Legora and Harvey close the multilingual gap with their own capital before Regbase compounds independent distribution. Independent $50M+ ARR is the low-probability tail.

The strongest bull case on Regbase is that Aresta-Katz's Paul, Weiss origin is a Color Genomics-shape founder-market fit -- she built the manual workflow she's now automating, which is the highest form of founder-problem ownership, and Color (2015) is exactly the precedent where I overrode a stated avoidance because the company was workflow-on-top-of-hard-thing with operator-grade domain depth. The bear answer is that Color worked because I *co-founded and operated it*, not because I passively backed it -- Regbase has no operator-investor equivalent, no disclosed revenue, and the incumbent FiscalNote's 22% YoY ARR decline suggests the buyer-side budget is shrinking, not re-opening. Verdict flips to invest if Regbase discloses two named Fortune 500 customers and $500K+ ARR within six months -- that's the velocity signature that would mean the single miracle plus founder-market fit is converting into "just working" rather than false signal.
