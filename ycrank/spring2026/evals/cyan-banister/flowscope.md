# flowscope -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Samuel Mirpuri (Harvard MBA, MIT, Imperial, ex-QuantumBlack/McKinsey) and Javier Leguina (UCL MSc ML, Imperial Aero, founding engineer at Model ML) are pitching "AI-native consulting to map and automate business processes" out of a 2-person San Francisco team — and flowscope is, almost line for line, the pitch I described in my pass example. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, but flowscope is playing exactly the game it appears to be playing: pedigreed operators rebuilding the consulting product they sat inside, in the most consensus category of Spring 2026.

Anduril is the high rung on lived-experience: Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from a garage, then walked into defense already covered in solder burns from the problem. flowscope's claim on this dimension is Mirpuri's QuantumBlack tenure — and that does buy real domain proximity to the CFO/COO/PE-operating-partner buyer named on the YC page. But Luckey's relationship to the problem was autobiographical; Mirpuri's is analytical. He didn't grow up watching back-office finance teams suffer — he was the consultant selling them decks. That's a critical inversion. The founder of flowscope who would pass my test is the controller at a mid-market industrial who *was* the one drowning in reconciliation work, not the McKinsey alum who watched her drown from a slide. I place flowscope between the Pass Example and the DeepMind exception — pedigreed, problem-adjacent, but not problem-resident.

The HQ Trivia failure mechanism — Yusupov and Kroll with no named tiebreaker — is what I scan for on governance, and flowscope's dossier is silent. Two co-founders, no CEO designation, no public data on co-founder history. The natural read is Mirpuri-as-business / Leguina-as-engineering, which is the cleanest split a YC pair can have, but "natural split" is exactly what HQ Trivia looked like in 2017. Absent evidence here, I default down and note that the GameCrush lesson is a near-term blow-up risk for flowscope the moment the first big enterprise contract requires someone to be wrong.

Crusoe was "magically weird" because three credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds. flowscope occupies the inverse position on the consensus axis — Adept raised $415M, 11x.ai raised $76M with a16z leading the Series B, Cresta is at $52M ARR and a $1.6B valuation, and David Lieb's batch is going to have a dozen agent-automation pitches by demo day. The mechanism that compounds against flowscope over 18 months is that the well-capitalized horizontal platforms are going to productize the discovery layer flowscope sells as a 2-week shadow engagement, and the Big-4 GenAI delivery practices — QuantumBlack itself, by the way — will match the "ship code in days" cycle time using armies flowscope cannot field. The round is not underpriced; the category is fully priced.

For flowscope to score a tier higher on capital-intensive/unsexy, the dossier would need a regulatory wedge — SOX automation with auditor sign-off, healthcare revenue-cycle work under HIPAA, defense FedRAMP-bound process automation — anything that turns the moat from "execution speed" into "the only people allowed in the room." flowscope's stated wedge is finance, admin, ops, generically; the only counterfactual that flips this is a signed LOI with a regulated buyer where compliance is the moat. None exists in the dossier.

Flock Safety is my single-anchor parallel on physical-world verifiability — I read the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons because the product was a *camera* I could later photograph on a telephone pole. flowscope is the categorical opposite: a B2B agent that touches internal enterprise systems no normal human can install, observe, or hand to a friend. The website (flowscope.com) shows no demo a non-customer can run, no GitHub artifact beyond Leguina's 8 personal repos with no notable stars, no Product Hunt launch. The product exists only inside customer engagements that have not yet been disclosed.

My 36-month forecast: flowscope acqui-hires into a Big-4 or MBB GenAI delivery practice — QuantumBlack is the natural acquirer given Mirpuri's network — at a sub-$20M outcome by Q2 2028, or dissolves quietly after the seed without a Series A. The mechanism is that an AI-consulting boutique with a 2-person team in a category being attacked by Adept, 11x, Cresta, UiPath, *and* the very consultancies Mirpuri came from has no margin structure that survives 24 months of price compression on agent tooling.

The strongest bear I have to beat is the DeepMind exception — pedigree was overridable when Hassabis was a chess prodigy, neuroscientist, and game designer compounding across domains into a problem only that combination could see. Could Leguina-plus-Mirpuri be that polymath pair? Leguina has aeronautical engineering, ML research, and founding-engineer scars from Model ML (YC W24); Mirpuri has satellite engineering, Singapore Armed Forces command, and McKinsey AI delivery. On paper, it's not nothing. But Hassabis was pointed at AGI — a problem whose surface area required cross-domain compounding to even articulate. flowscope is pointed at finance/admin/ops process automation — a problem whose surface area is well-mapped by every RPA vendor since 2015. The polymath override doesn't load when the target is conventional, no matter how unconventional the founders' resumes are. I would flip this verdict on one verifiable condition: a signed engagement with a named regulated counterparty (a bank, a hospital system, a defense prime) where flowscope's automation is in production touching audited workflows by Q4 2026.
