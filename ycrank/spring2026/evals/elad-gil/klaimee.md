# Klaimee -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Klaimee is the rare pre-seed where the catalyst paragraph writes itself and the revenue paragraph is a blank page — Ines Boutemadja has a named, dated, regulator-stamped market re-opening (Chubb, Travelers, and Berkshire winning state approval to *exclude* AI damages, per PYMNTS 2026; California AB 316; EU AI Act enforcement August 2026) sitting on top of zero disclosed ARR, zero named customers, and an undisclosed capacity provider. That high-catalyst / no-velocity pairing is the analytical signature: Klaimee is a regulatory-arbitrage MGA play whose founder knows the carrier-paper game cold, in a window that just cracked open, with no curve yet to confirm it's "just working" rather than a false signal.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for Klaimee's catalyst dimension, and it's worth being precise about why. The Anduril check wasn't "defense is big" — it was "Ukraine revealed Pentagon software inadequacy inside a 24-month window," a specific re-opening peers were misreading as a steady-state graveyard. Klaimee has the structural twin: incumbents didn't fail to notice AI agent liability, they actively *retreated* — >80% of carrier exclusion requests approved per PYMNTS — and that retreat plus AB 316 plus EU AI Act August 2026 is exactly the named, recent, regulator-driven re-opening template I underwrite. Where Klaimee diverges from the Anduril rung is the founder leverage on the catalyst: Trae Stephens had built Palantir's forward-deployed muscle into the exact procurement surface defense was re-opening; Ines scaled SafetyWing's Nomad product $5M→$60M and structured a Tokio Marine MGA into its own Puerto Rico carrier — strong, but that's *travel insurance distribution*, not AI-deployer enterprise procurement, and the catalyst window will be won on the latter.

**Harvey** (2023) is the template I keep against any AI-adjacent company on revenue velocity, and Klaimee fails the template hard. The Harvey mechanism was "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" because real enterprise buying behavior was disclosable and disclosed; the failure mode of the LOW rung — the AI pitch with no growth curve — is "something is fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss #863). Klaimee's dossier shows no pricing on klaimee.ai, no Product Hunt, no press, no logos, 0 open jobs, and not a single named pilot — and unlike Harvey 2023 this is *insurance*, where competitors Armilla and Testudo have already disclosed Swiss Re/Chaucer and Lloyd's/Apollo/Atrium/QBE as paper, so silence here isn't pre-seed humility, it's a procurement-credibility hole. Pre-seed calibration earns Klaimee the absent-evidence default and not much more.

Ines's founder-market fit on the operating dimension is genuinely the best signal in the Klaimee dossier — taking a SafetyWing line from $5M to $60M, structuring a Tokio Marine MGA, and grinding out a Puerto Rico carrier license is the exact regulatory shipping rhythm Klaimee needs over 18 months, and it's the kind of thing my *High Growth Handbook* lens registers as "this person has seen the post-PMF mechanics." The compounding question is whether that rhythm transfers from a B2C travel-insurance MGA to an enterprise B2B AI-deployer MGA where the buyer is a CISO or General Counsel, not a digital nomad — different sales motion, different paper requirements, different reinsurance treaties. Julien Catonnet as CTO with Booking Shake product engineering and consulting is the weaker half; the dossier names no shipped AI artifact under his hand, which is exactly the gap where Perplexity-style between-meetings shipping would have moved Klaimee a tier.

The index test is where Klaimee gets uncomfortable. **Stripe** (2010) became an index on e-commerce because every merchant on the rising tide *had* to touch the rails — Klaimee's certification badge is only an index on AI-agent deployment if "Klaimee Certified" becomes the procurement-default reference the way SOC 2 did for security, and the dossier shows seven named competitors already underwriting AI risk (Armilla, Testudo, Vouch, Munich Re aiSure, Embroker, Corgi, Mayflower Specialty) with limits from $2M to $50M. That's not a category with an empty workflow slot; that's a category where the standard is up for grabs and Klaimee is entrant number eight. The counterfactual that flips Klaimee a tier here is naming a reinsurance partner of Swiss Re / Munich Re caliber inside 12 months and getting one Fortune 500 AI-deployer to make "Klaimee Certified" a procurement requirement — without that, this is share-fighting, not index-riding.

The single-miracle test is where I get most skeptical of Klaimee in private. Klaimee needs three independent things to land: (1) a capacity provider willing to write paper on AI-agent loss with no historical loss data — undisclosed today, unlike Armilla's Swiss Re or Testudo's Lloyd's syndicates; (2) the 8-dimension risk evaluation (scope, exfiltration, drift, etc.) actually predicting loss frequency, which has no third-party benchmark in the dossier; (3) AB 316 and the EU AI Act enforcing on schedule. Each of these is plausible; compounding three independent plausibles is how my pass list gets populated. The Color Genomics override doesn't rescue Klaimee here — Color isolated to workflow on commodity sequencing; Klaimee is workflow *plus* novel underwriting *plus* regulatory timing.

My 36-month forecast on Klaimee: acquired by a reinsurer or specialty carrier (Munich Re, Swiss Re, or a Lloyd's syndicate) for $40–90M as a certification + MGA bolt-on, with ARR in the $2–6M range at exit — Ines's carrier-license muscle is the asset, not the badge. The mechanism is straightforward: capacity providers want a defensible underwriting methodology and a founder who can navigate a state DOI, and Klaimee's founder profile is more valuable inside a reinsurer's balance sheet than it is competing for Lloyd's paper against Testudo. Failure mode is the same shape — sub-scale at <$2M ARR, talent-acquired by Armilla or a US specialty carrier for under $20M.

The bull objection that stings: Klaimee's catalyst is more legally enforceable than the Anduril Ukraine catalyst was in 2022 — AB 316 and EU AI Act are *statutes with dates*, not geopolitical inferences, and when carriers exclude a category by regulation, the MGA that builds the first defensible methodology can capture a disproportionate share of the runoff demand, the way cyber insurance MGAs did 2015–2019 before incumbents re-entered. Ines's Puerto Rico carrier license alone is a years-long moat that Armilla and Testudo would need to replicate to compete on US-domiciled paper. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull case is the silence on capacity — Armilla disclosed Swiss Re and Chaucer; Testudo disclosed Apollo, Atrium, QBE; Klaimee disclosed nothing, and in this market capacity *is* the product. The single verifiable condition that flips me to invest: Klaimee announces a named reinsurance or carrier partner of Swiss Re / Munich Re / Lloyd's-syndicate quality, with disclosed limits at $10M+ per insured, within 9 months of YC Demo Day. Until then, this is a pass — interesting, well-founded, wrong stage for me.
