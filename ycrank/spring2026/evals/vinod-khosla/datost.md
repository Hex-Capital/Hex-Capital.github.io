# Datost -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Datost is the Pass Example with a benchmark chart taped to it -- a Slack-front-end on Claude with a 75.2% BIRD-Interact score, pitched by a Virginia Tech 4.0 GPA engineer named Maceo Cardinale Kwik who saw analyst scarcity at Traba and concluded the answer was an @mention. The dimensional signature is unambiguous: Datost's least-weak axis is consequence-adjacency to my $15T labor-displacement thesis (analyst labor is labor), and its weakest axis is founder defiance of expert consensus. That pairing -- labor-thesis-adjacent + conformist-credential founder + commodity-SaaS capital -- is precisely the shape I described in the Pass Example I keep on file. The benchmark gap is interesting. Interesting is not a check.

**Consequence Magnitude.** Hold Datost next to **OpenAI** (2019) on the consequence ladder and the comparison is not flattering -- OpenAI's success-state collapsed the unit economics of cognitive labor across every desk job; Datost's success-state is that PMs at mid-market SaaS companies stop pinging the data team in Slack. Both touch my "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" frame (Fortune 2026-03-06), but consequence is not a binary -- it is the magnitude of the regime that disappears. Datost's dossier names no incumbent regime collapsing; it names 18,402 weekly queries and a 42-second time to first answer, which are metrics about a feature, not a civilization. The closest thing to a consequence claim is that "analyst capacity is scarce" -- true, and exactly the framing that lets Hex, Snowflake Cortex, and Databricks Genie ship the same workflow as a checkbox. Datost is consequence-adjacent, not consequence-magnitude.

**Technical and Capital Risk.** The failure mode I watched at the Pass Example -- "we use GPT-4 to summarize sales calls, integrate with HubSpot" -- is the failure mode of any thesis where the only described miracle is prompt engineering plus a semantic layer over customer schemas. Datost's dossier states the differentiator explicitly: a "semantic business-definitions layer" attached to a sandbox that explores schema and writes SQL. That is not a physics miracle, a biology miracle, or a fleet miracle -- it is software architecture any of Tracxn's 47 active competitors can replicate, including TextQL with $21.1M already raised (Series A April 2026) and Hex at $36/editor/month. The required-capital story for Datost is $5-10M to $1M ARR. That is exactly the profile where any Tier-1 generalist can write the check, which means I am over-resourced and my edge is wasted. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) needed $115M to find out if the science worked; Datost needs $5M to find out if Snowflake ships the feature first.

**Founder Defies Expert Consensus.** Maceo Cardinale Kwik's dossier reads as Virginia Tech CS, 4.0 GPA, Summa Cum Laude, full-stack engineer at a Series B staffing marketplace, top 0.1% on a trading-challenge leaderboard, founder of a college non-profit. That is a credential stack, not a defied-expert stack. Compare to Sergey Levine at **Physical Intelligence** (2024), who had publicly contradicted the dominant robotics-learning orthodoxy for a decade before I wrote a check; Maceo's public footprint -- 9 GitHub followers, top owned repo 1 star -- contains no published thesis, no controversial position in the analytics field, no instigator pattern from my TED Talk framing. Co-founder Jason Hy Wang has effectively no public technical footprint at all. The McKinsey 1986 forecast of <1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 (actual: 109M) is my standing reminder that experts extrapolate the past -- Datost's founders have not yet earned the right to be the people contradicting the experts because they have not yet said anything the experts would disagree with.

**Learning Rate.** Counterfactual: what would have to be true for Datost to clear the **Slash** (2024) bar that justified my $100M Series C into a YC-backed fintech? Slash had a publicly told pivot story -- the Kanye West moment -- that proved the founders had updated a thesis mid-flight under pressure. Datost's dossier contains exactly zero pivots, zero public thesis updates, zero "we were wrong about X" moments, because the company is months old and Maceo went from identifying the problem at Traba straight to building the answer. That is not a knock; it is an absence. Until Datost has a documented thesis update -- killed a vertical, abandoned a customer segment, rewrote the moat story -- I cannot underwrite learning rate, and I default to the middle of the range per my own framework.

**Capital Profile.** Single-anchor parallel: **Genesis AI** (2025), where I co-led a $105M seed because the company needed fleet-scale capital to train robot foundation models from scratch -- that check could only be written by a firm willing to deploy nine figures at seed. Datost's roadmap, as disclosed, is a Slack app with a semantic layer and a sandbox SQL runner. The honest capital number to get this to $1M ARR is well under $10M, possibly under $5M given a YC pre-seed and founder-led outbound through datost.cal.com. I do not have an edge at that check size; any Tier-1 generalist or YC continuity fund does the same deal on the same terms. The dossier confirms what the architecture implies -- no plant, no fleet, no model training run, no drug pipeline.

**36-Month Forecast.** Datost is acquired by Hex, Snowflake, or a mid-market BI incumbent for <$40M as an acqui-hire of the Maceo/Jason team within 24-36 months, or stalls at <$2M ARR while TextQL's $21.1M war chest and Snowflake Cortex's native Slack integration compress the category from both ends. The mechanism is the one I called out years ago at TechCrunch Disrupt: 47 competitors on Tracxn means the differentiator has to be data, distribution, or science -- Datost's is benchmark accuracy, which decays the moment Anthropic ships a better base model or Hex ships a Slack connector.

**Strongest Objection.** The bull case is **Glean** -- my own Known Exception. Glean violated my "Founder Defies Expert Consensus" rule because Arvind Jain was ex-Google search, a pure domain-expert profile, and I wrote the check anyway because the consequence magnitude on Fortune 500 information asymmetry justified overriding the founder prior. Could Datost be the same exception? The semantic-layer-per-customer story does have compounding properties: ingest a company's vocabulary, schema mappings, and prior queries, and switching cost rises. The 75.2% vs. 33% benchmark gap is not nothing. But Glean's Arvind Jain built Google's search infrastructure; Datost's Maceo built full-stack features at a staffing marketplace. Glean attacked the question "where is the answer inside my Fortune 500?" -- a question with named labor-cost figures across knowledge work; Datost attacks "how many active users did we have last week?" -- a question every BI vendor since Looker has competed to own. The override only fires when consequence magnitude is overwhelming, and Datost's success-state does not clear that bar. The condition that flips my verdict: one signed enterprise contract at >$250K ARR from a named Fortune 500 within 12 months, with documented retention past pilot and a second logo at the same price point. That would prove the semantic layer is a moat, not a benchmark.
