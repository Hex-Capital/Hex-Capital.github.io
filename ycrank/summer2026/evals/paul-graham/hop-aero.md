# Hop Aero -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Hop Aero is a hardware-credentialed defense-space team running hard at a real schlep -- ITAR, FAA Part 450, hypersonic thermal protection, austere-site precision landing -- on an idea they read in an Air Force PowerPoint rather than one they bled through personally. That pairing is the whole story. Matija Milenovic shipped a propulsion payload on SpaceX Transporter-3 via porkchop and led a 20-person supersonic UAV team at Destinus; Jacob Balaj integrated Falcon 9/Heavy boosters at SpaceX. These are builders. But the problem Hop Aero is solving -- resupplying Marines on Indo-Pacific island chains -- is not a problem either founder lived inside. That gap is what I keep circling back to.

On schlep, the ladder I run in my head goes **Coinbase** (2012) at the top -- Armstrong's appetite for the compliance grind became the moat itself -- with **DoorDash** (2013) in the middle running last-mile operations no software-only YC applicant in its batch would touch, and the polished AI-wrapper from my pass example at the bottom, pure software with no offline grind. Hop Aero lands closer to the **Coinbase** (2012) rung than the DoorDash one. The shared mechanism is regulatory unpleasantness as a barrier other smart founders unconsciously route around: FAA Part 450 launch licensing, ITAR export controls, USTRANSCOM CRADAs, SBIR phase ladders. Where Hop Aero diverges from **Coinbase** (2012) is that Armstrong personally wanted to use bitcoin -- the schlep was downstream of his own conviction. Hop Aero's $1.25M USAF SBIR is genuine schlep evidence, but it is evidence of a contract pursuit, not evidence the founders would still be doing this if the Pentagon weren't writing checks. Still, on this axis alone, Hop Aero is real.

On organic-idea fit, the failure mode I'm watching for is the **Airbnb**-inverse -- founders who spotted a market rather than founders who hit a wall. The pattern shows up in Hop Aero's dossier with uncomfortable clarity: the problem statement is a quote from the DoD Rocket Cargo Vanguard / P2PD program, not a quote from a frustrated logistics officer Milenovic or Balaj used to be. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick. The Collisons hacked commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007). Neither Hop Aero founder has ever waited for a C-17 to arrive on Tinian. They have credible founder-market fit as suppliers to the program, not as customers of the pain. This is the bottom rung's failure mechanism creeping into the dossier -- the AI-wrapper team that read a TAM slide and built toward it -- dressed up in much better hardware credentials.

On animal resourcefulness, the eighteen-month compounding question is whether Hop Aero ships a sub-scale flight artifact before the next raise or whether it ships another renderer. Right now hop-aero.com renders only a logo placeholder, which is the opposite of what Drew Houston did before **Dropbox** (2007) when he put the demo video on Hacker News and watched the conviction arrive in his inbox. Milenovic's porkchop payload on Transporter-3 is the strongest counter-evidence in the dossier -- that is a shipped artifact, not a credential -- but it belongs to a prior company. For Hop Aero specifically, the dossier names no stunt, no unscalable manual operation, no improvised revenue. $3M pre-seed and a $1.25M SBIR is institutional money walking in the front door, not cereal boxes.

The counterfactual on hacker genealogy is what would have to be true for Hop Aero to read like **Stripe** (2010) instead of like a defense-prime feeder. Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize wasn't load-bearing because Lisp matters to payments; it was load-bearing because it proved he built things alone, for himself, before anyone paid him to. Milenovic founding porkchop as a KTH spinout and getting it to orbit is the closest analog Hop Aero has -- it's a real hardware-hacker tell. Balaj's I&T work across SpaceX, Virgin Orbit, Masten, and SpinLaunch is the integrator's resume, not the hacker's. No GitHub trail, no open-source contributions, no Hacker News presence surfaces for either founder. Hardware hackers leave different tracks than software hackers, and I weight Milenovic's porkchop launch heavily here, but the dossier is conspicuously thin on the kind of pre-company building that flips my gut.

Compared to **Stripe** (2010) on cofounder configuration, Hop Aero is two founders with no disclosed prior working relationship -- not the brothers, not the **Reddit** (2005) dorm roommates, not the **Twitch** (2007) Kiko-survivors who'd already failed together. The dossier says nothing about how Milenovic and Balaj met, which is the in-person interview signal I usually defer judgment on. Six employees in LA, one open mech-eng req at $90-150K. Reads professional, not chemical.

My 36-month forecast on Hop Aero: no full hypersonic point-to-point demo flown, Series A in the $15-30M range on additional AFWERX / STRATFI milestones, squeezed between SpaceX's $102M Starship P2PD prime contract above and Inversion's $54M reentry vehicle below, and the most likely outcome is a pivot into hypersonic testbed services or sub-scale tactical resupply demos -- Varda-adjacent rather than Starship-adjacent. The mechanism producing this is the dimensional signature: real schlep, weak organic-idea fit, $3M of capital fighting incumbents who have raised between $54M and $990M. Schlep zones reward founders who would be doing the schlep anyway. The Pentagon's procurement cycle will outlast Hop Aero's runway-conviction match.

The bull case that stings is the **Coinbase** (2012) precedent. Armstrong was also not a former bank regulator; he was a Goldman engineer who wanted bitcoin to work and was willing to eat the compliance grind. By the cleanest read, Milenovic founding an electric-propulsion company that put a payload on Transporter-3 is exactly the prior-shipped-hardware tell I should reward, and the rocket-cargo segment is precisely the kind of "frighteningly ambitious" idea I wrote about in March 2012. Maybe Hop Aero is an Anduril-shaped bet that YC under-prices because the partners are still software-pattern-matching. The dossier evidence that wins against this: Anduril founders had used the defense procurement system from inside (Palmer Luckey's prior Oculus exit funded his patience), and Coinbase was a software-margin business once the licensing cleared. Hop Aero is reusable-thermal-protection hard tech competing against SpaceX on SpaceX's home ground. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: Hop Aero flies a sub-scale point-to-point hop with recovered hardware within 18 months on the current $3M plus SBIR -- a Houston-style demo, not a renderer. Until then, neutral.
