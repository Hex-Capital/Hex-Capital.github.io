# transload -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Julius Scheel, Nils Börner, and Jago Wahl-Schwentker have all shipped real artifacts before — Börner has an IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging paper, Wahl-Schwentker was first engineer at Veli and doubled ARR on a Bluetooth localization product, Scheel scaled avi medical to seven-figure ARR — and that is the most thesis-aligned signal in the transload dossier. The lowest signal is that transload is selling into a steady-state LTL/3PL market that I cannot map to any specific 24-month catalyst, and the website is rendering "0% BOOTING" as of May 2026. That high/low pairing tells me what transload is: a competent technical team aimed at a niche where the index position is already occupied by Loadsmart/NavTrac, not a market re-opening worth racing into.

Anduril (2022) is the HIGH rung on my catalyst ladder — Ukraine revealed Pentagon software inadequacy inside a 24-month window, and the catalyst itself was nameable, datable, and structurally specific. The mechanism that makes the Anduril analogy load-bearing for transload would be a named recent shift in freight measurement — a NTEP rule change permitting camera-only legal-for-trade dimensioning, an LTL carrier mandate, a tariff-driven trailer-utilization crisis. The transload dossier offers none of these; it offers an [Inferred] note that "monocular/multi-view 3D reconstruction and edge GPU inference have made dimensional measurement from standard CCTV feeds tractable," which is a capability story, not a market-opening story. Inferred catalysts are the kind of TAM math I explicitly down-weight in *First Round Review* — "market first and the strength of the team second," and transload has not named the market shift.

The Lyft Series C pass is the failure mechanism I keep returning to on transload's distribution dimension: a credible #2 in a market where #1 already has the distribution flywheel captures a fraction of #1's economics, no matter how clean the product is. Loadsmart acquired NavTrac in September 2023 explicitly to put computer vision on yard operations, and Loadsmart distributes Opendock to ~110,000 carriers and ~3,000 warehouses (FreightWaves, Sep 2023). That is the index position transload would need to occupy, and transload's dossier names no distribution wedge — no carrier LOI, no 3PL pilot, no integration partner — that would let it sit underneath Loadsmart's existing reach rather than fight it from outside.

Revenue velocity, mechanism analysis: the entire transload traction claim is a customer testimonial of "~$50k per site per month in rebillings and improved trailer utilization" with no named counterparty, no signed contract, no MRR figure. In an 18-month compounding window the Harvey (2023) signature is $3B → $5B → $8B in months because real value capture pulls revenue forward; transload's website renders a placeholder animation and the YC dossier lists "no users/revenue/press located." If transload were "just working" in the Decagon (2023) sense, the dossier would say so — silence on growth in an AI/CV company is itself the signal I called out on Tim Ferriss Show #863.

Single-miracle test on transload, counterfactual mode: for the score to flip a tier, transload would need to be a one-miracle company where the only hard thing is CV accuracy on commodity CCTV feeds. The dossier instead implies at least two independent miracles: (1) measurement accuracy from non-purpose-built cameras under variable lighting and occlusion, where vMeasure and Vimaan use drive-through rigs precisely because the fixed-geometry problem is easier, and (2) NTEP / legal-for-trade certification path for a camera-only system that is "not publicly addressed by transload." Compounding two independent miracles is exactly the math NFX *Unusual Signs* warned about, and transload's dossier does not isolate either one.

Founder shipping, single-anchor parallel: Perplexity (2022) is my template, and the Perplexity test isn't pedigree — Aravind would show up a week later with the thing we discussed built. Börner's IEEE T-MI co-authorship and Wahl-Schwentker's shipped sensor-data ML at Veli are real artifacts that put transload above the median pre-seed founder dossier, but the live website rendering "0% BOOTING" while the team is in YC Spring 2026 cuts against the between-meetings shipping rhythm. Scheel's McKinsey Fellow + avi medical scaling profile is the operationally-disciplined-CEO archetype from *High Growth Handbook* (2018), which I respect — but that profile alone has never been my trigger to write a check.

My 36-month forecast on transload: by mid-2029 transload reaches $1–3M ARR across 5–15 mid-market 3PL sites, then is acqui-hired into Loadsmart, vMeasure, or FreightSnap to bolt CCTV-mode onto an existing hardware SKU, with the founders rolling into a CV team at the acquirer. The mechanism producing this outcome is the high/low signature: a technically credible team good enough to build something that works at small scale, in a market with no catalyst pulling demand forward, against an incumbent (Loadsmart/NavTrac) that already owns the distribution path I would need transload to ride.

The strongest bull case against my pass is that CCTV-only dimensioning is exactly the workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-hard-thing pattern that made Color Genomics (2015) work — you don't bet on the sequencing chemistry, you bet on the workflow layer; transload doesn't bet on building dimensioner hardware, it bets on the software layer that turns every existing camera into a measurement device, and the "$50k per site per month" rebilling claim, if it holds, is the kind of ROI that closes 3PL deals fast. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull case is the absence of a single named carrier or 3PL using transload today combined with Loadsmart's September 2023 NavTrac acquisition — the incumbent has both the CV stack and the 110,000-carrier distribution, and transload has neither a wedge into that distribution nor a disclosed NTEP path. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed multi-site deployment with a top-25 LTL carrier disclosed within the next 12 months, at which point transload moves from a 43 to a re-evaluate.
