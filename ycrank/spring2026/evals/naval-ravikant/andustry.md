# Andustry -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Andustry is a two-sided industrial-procurement broker whose dimensional signature in my portfolio is a real network shape strangled by the most permissioned distribution architecture a YC company can pick -- Het Dave and Johann Stürken are running transatlantic phone lines (+1 415 and +49 172, per andustry.com) into SME manufacturers to source $1.2M CNC orders, which is the literal opposite of the code-or-media-native leverage that underwrote Twitter, Stack Overflow, and Replit. The marketplace pulse is there. The leverage architecture is wrong.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung I keep returning to on leverage because anyone could post, the platform charged nothing, and the marginal cost of replication was zero -- the architecture and the founder identity collapsed into a single permissionless surface. Andustry inverts that mechanic at every joint: the dossier describes AI agents "discovering suppliers" but the unit of work is a hand-qualified supplier network, a commission-only take rate on $1.2M+ industrial transactions, and dual-continent phone support staffed by a two-person team. That is labor leverage with an LLM wrapper, and "labor and capital require permission" (nav.al/product-media) is the exact wall Andustry is standing against -- every new supplier onboarded costs Het Dave and Johann Stürken human cycles, every customs/freight/QC handoff is permissioned by a counterparty, and the GTM the dossier infers is "direct outbound to SME manufacturers." The deck I would project onto this from the Red Flags list -- "hiring SDRs before the product has unprompted organic adoption" -- is structurally what Andustry has to become to grow.

Pinterest is the low rung I will not invoke here because Andustry does not even reach the criterion-1 bar that Pinterest cleared; the lower-rung pattern that actually maps is the "ex-FAANG PM / ex-McKinsey who identified a market opportunity in $X TAM" archetype I flagged as the absence-of-specific-knowledge tell. Johann Stürken is, per the dossier, ex-McKinsey with a family trading-goods business -- and the family-business half is the part that earns him real founder-market fit, the McKinsey half is the part that makes me read the rest of the deck more carefully. Het Dave's AppliedAI/FINN work on AI-agent evaluation is genuinely useful adjacent knowledge but it is not the multi-year domain immersion that Atwood and Spolsky brought to Stack Overflow or that Ivan Zhao brought to Notion. The Nikolas Keller name surfacing in one search snippet against a two-founder YC page is a small, unresolved data discrepancy that I would force into a 30-minute call before sending anything; "picking a co-founder is your most important decision" (Venture Hacks) means an ambiguous third name is a question I want closed.

On marketplace compounding (criterion 2), the 18-month mechanism cuts against Andustry rather than for it: in a brokered model the supplier does not see other suppliers, the buyer does not see other buyers, and Andustry sits as the intermediary that absorbs every additional connection rather than letting the (N-1) users become more valuable to the Nth. Xometry's $687M FY2025 revenue (Xometry investor release, Feb 2026) proves the category supports a public-scale outcome, but Xometry got there by owning the quoting engine and the production capacity layer -- Fictiv was acquired by MISUMI for $350M (The Robot Report, April 2025) for the same reason, the managed services around the network. Andustry's "AI-native broker" framing leaves the compounding asset thin: a quote-outcome dataset that, by the dossier's own admission, is unproven.

On counterfactual for criterion 4 (big market, pivot room): industrial procurement is structurally large -- the Xometry revenue line establishes that -- but for Andustry to clear the "room to maneuver" bar I want at least two adjacent pivot vectors the dossier could name, and what it actually names is a single workflow (multi-supplier quote normalization in 24-48h) inside a single buyer segment (SME manufacturers sourcing machines/parts). The score flips a tier if Andustry can articulate a pivot path into supplier-side software (an SRM the qualified suppliers actually adopt) or into financing the procurement transactions themselves -- neither is in the dossier, both are adjacent.

The single-anchor parallel for the founder trio (criterion 5) is Stack Overflow's Jeff Atwood, who was writing Coding Horror for years before Stack Overflow shipped -- the integrity and energy read through years of public artifact. Het Dave and Johann Stürken have no GitHub repos, no X presence, no public writing surface in the dossier. That is not a negative signal at pre-seed, but it is the absence of the unstated pattern I keep funding: media leverage on the founder identity itself. The Buffett trio reads clean by default; the 10-year-partner test is genuinely indeterminate from the available surface area.

The 36-month forecast: Andustry reaches $1-4M in commission revenue at sub-$50M GMV, fails to break Xometry's or MISUMI/Fictiv's grip on the AI-quoting workflow, and either gets acquihired into a European industrial distributor (Klöckner, Würth, or MISUMI itself) for $15-40M or pivots into a software-only SRM sold to existing brokers. The mechanism is leverage architecture: a 2-person team running phone-mediated supplier verification against incumbents who own production capacity and have already deployed AI in their quoting flows cannot out-scale them, and "transparent pricing with commission-based model only" is exactly the margin structure the buyers' own price transparency will compress.

The strongest bear case against my pass is the Anchorage exception -- regulated crypto custody violated criterion 1 on permissionless distribution and I wrote anyway, because pre-consensus category conviction since the 2014 MetaStable co-founding flipped permissionless leverage into permission-as-moat. The bull would argue Andustry's hand-qualified European/Chinese/Indian supplier network is the same kind of relationship-as-moat in industrial procurement that Anchorage built in institutional crypto. The dossier evidence that dismantles it: I have zero pre-existing position in industrial procurement, no MetaStable-equivalent 5-year accumulation in this category, and the Anchorage override required *me serving as advisor*, not merely writing a check. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is unprompted organic supplier-side adoption -- if 30 days from now Andustry shows 200+ suppliers self-onboarding without Het Dave and Johann Stürken on a phone call, the leverage architecture has quietly inverted and I rerun the math.
