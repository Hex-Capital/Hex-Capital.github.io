# Lucent -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

The pivot tells the story before I even get to the product. Lucent raised $1.3 million in 36 hours for "behavioural datasets for browser agents" — a thesis about training data infrastructure for the next generation of AI agents. Then somewhere between October 2025 and the W26 batch, the company became "AI that automatically watches your session replays to detect bugs." These are fundamentally different ambitions. The first had at least the outline of a category-creation play — building the data layer that browser agents would need to function. The second is a feature that sits on top of FullStory and PostHog. I don't inherently mind pivots — rapid iteration can be a signal of learning rate. But when the pivot moves *down* the ambition ladder, from infrastructure that enables a new technology paradigm to a workflow improvement for existing tools, that concerns me. The founder moved from a harder, more consequential problem to an easier, less consequential one.

Let me state the consequence-of-success question plainly: if Lucent fully succeeds — captures the entire AI session analysis market — what changes? Software teams find bugs somewhat faster. The session replay market is $267 million to $502 million today, projected to maybe $1.7 billion by 2035 at the generous end. Lucent would capture a fraction of that as a complementary layer, not the full stack. This is not a trillion-dollar system being disrupted. No industry becomes structurally obsolete. Nobody's life changes in a material way. The consequence of Lucent's success is a moderately useful developer tool in a sub-billion-dollar niche. I've spent four decades looking for bets where success reshapes entire industries — Juniper replacing Cisco's routing architecture, Impossible Foods replacing animal agriculture, OpenAI redefining what machines can do. A better way to watch session replays doesn't belong in the same conversation.

The technology risk here cuts the wrong direction. The dossier itself states the core concept — "applying LLMs or vision models to session recordings — is technically reproducible" and "any session replay vendor could add AI analysis as a feature." Sentry has $100 million in revenue and a $3 billion valuation. FullStory has $93 million in revenue. PostHog has $182 million raised and an open-source community that could build this organically. Lucent's entire value proposition is one product sprint away from being a checkbox feature for incumbents who already own the customer relationship, the data pipeline, and the session recordings. When I backed Juniper against Cisco, the TCP/IP routing architecture was a fundamentally different approach that Cisco's legacy systems couldn't replicate without rebuilding from scratch. Lucent has no equivalent structural advantage — it's applying the same broadly available AI capabilities that its competitors can deploy with greater resources and existing distribution.

The strongest bull case I can construct: Alisa Wu is 22 years old, already has a prior exit, was founding engineer at a company Canva acquired, raised her pre-seed in 36 hours, and got into YC. That's a velocity signal I respect — it reminds me of the kind of raw execution speed that matters more than credentials. If she's this fast at 22, her learning rate may be exceptional, and she could evolve Lucent into something far more ambitious than its current form. The multimodal AI timing is genuinely real — automated visual analysis of session recordings wasn't technically feasible two years ago, and that "why now" has substance. If Lucent used the session replay wedge to build toward a much broader automated QA or AI-driven software reliability platform that replaced manual testing entirely, the consequence calculus changes. But that's a hypothetical future product, not what's on the table today. And the "ChatGPT as CTO" characterization, while probably a throwaway line in press, raises a legitimate question about whether the team has the ML depth to build proprietary detection models that outpace what incumbents can ship with their own AI teams.

I'd tell Wu directly: you're clearly talented and fast, and you're wasting that on a problem that's too small. The session replay incumbents will absorb this feature the way Salesforce absorbs every useful CRM plugin. Go back to the browser agent data infrastructure idea, or find something else where success means the world works differently — not where success means engineering teams save a few hours per sprint reviewing recordings.
