# Maquoketa Research -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Maquoketa Research is a defense-hardware company with the atoms-heavy posture I look for and none of the monopoly trajectory that makes the posture pay. Evan Yeager and Alejandro Hernandez are building vertically integrated one-way attack drones at a target price the Pentagon has already published — roughly $5,000 a unit across 30,000 units — which means Maquoketa Research is entering the category at the exact moment the procurement architecture commoditizes it. That high/low pairing — atoms-heavy execution against a structurally commoditizing customer — is the signature.

Anduril (2017) is the right rung to test Maquoketa Research against on the contrarian-secret axis, and the comparison is unkind. Luckey's secret named Lockheed and Raytheon and the cost-plus contracting structure that prevented them from shipping software-defined defense at startup velocity — a blind spot the market had refused to price for fifteen years. Maquoketa Research names its incumbents cleanly — AeroVironment Switchblade for operator-in-the-loop guidance, Anduril Lattice for software-only autonomy without purpose-built OWA hardware — and that specificity is genuine signal. The secret, however, has already been exfiltrated. Ukraine ran the demonstration, *Defense One* (Jan 2026) wrote the procurement memo, and the Pentagon is now buying 30,000 units. By the time the secret is in a DefenseScoop headline about the March 2026 swarm crucible, it is consensus. Maquoketa Research is articulating a thesis the market actively *agrees* with — the inverse of the Zero to One question.

The pass example I keep returning to on monopoly trajectory is the consensus AI-for-X pitch where the moat collapses to "we have a better model." Maquoketa Research's moat collapses to "we have a better $5,000 drone," and the Pentagon's published unit price is the failure mechanism — sole-source pricing power does not survive a procurement designed around interchangeable attritable units across multiple Replicator selectees. AeroVironment, Performance Drone Works, and Swarm Aero are already on the list; Maquoketa Research is not. Anduril at $30.5B and Shield AI at $12.7B (TechCrunch, Mar 2026) hold the integration and capital depth that compounds at this stage, and Maquoketa Research's mission-data flywheel is a hypothesis without a single operational deployment to feed it. This is duopoly-or-worse equilibrium without the Lyft override of category-defining network effects.

The definite-plan axis is where Maquoketa Research does better than its peers in the batch — eighteen months of compounding here looks real. Hernandez's Berkeley Wireless Research Center work on 140 GHz MIMO phased arrays is precisely the seeker-and-datalink technical core, and the stated commitment to in-house RF/mixed-signal/power electronics plus airframes plus computer vision is a definite multi-year architectural bet, not iteration-mode optionality. Whether the compounding produces monopoly depends on whether vertical integration outruns the $5,000 price ceiling — which I doubt — but the plan itself is named.

Counterfactual on founder lineage: for Maquoketa Research to flip a tier here, one of the four founders would need to have shipped a hardware product to scale at a prior company — the Luckey-from-Oculus override that made Anduril (2017) the exception. Hernandez's mmWave research is genuine technical signal, the kind of credentialed-outsider pedigree I weight, but it is a research credential, not a shipped-to-scale operator credential. Yeager's path from UC Berkeley EECS through SpiderRock derivatives software to CEO does not contain the Levchin-equivalent re-up I score on. Muchow's "confusing-to-recruiters" framing on the YC page is the kind of biographical anomaly I notice — but anomaly without a verifiable shipping history is just noise.

Single-anchor parallel on atoms-heavy and regulated: SpaceX (2008) is the load-bearing precedent because the moat was specifically the capex-plus-regulation stack, not the software. Maquoketa Research has the ITAR posture, the DoD vendor qualification pathway, the US-only vertically integrated manufacturing claim, and a CTO whose phased-array work is genuinely export-controlled territory. The Elk Grove Village / San Francisco location ambiguity is sloppy, and I flag it, but the structural shape of the business is correctly atoms-heavy and correctly regulated. This is the dimension where Maquoketa Research earns its placement.

Thirty-six month forecast: Maquoketa Research secures a sub-$15M Replicator-adjacent contract in the second or third selection wave, fails to break into the top-tier OWA supplier shortlist as Anduril and AeroVironment absorb the bulk of Drone Dominance procurement, and is acqui-hired by AeroVironment or Kratos for the Hernandez RF stack at a price that is meaningful for the founders and immaterial for a power-law portfolio. The mechanism producing this is the $5,000 unit price ceiling colliding with vertical-integration capex — Maquoketa Research cannot out-scale Anduril and cannot out-margin a software autonomy layer on a commoditized airframe.

The bull case that should sting: Hernandez's 140 GHz MIMO phased-array work is exactly the kind of credentialed-outsider technical pedigree I have written checks on, and the prime contractors structurally cannot recruit that profile out of Berkeley Wireless Research Center because their cost-plus comp structure does not compete with equity. If autonomous OWA at attritable price points becomes the defining defense category of the late 2020s — and the Pentagon's 30,000-unit Drone Dominance buy says it might — then Maquoketa Research is the second Anduril, and the pedigree gap is the Asana exception in reverse: structural moat overriding missing operator lineage. The case stings because Luckey at Anduril (2017) was also writing into a category the consensus dismissed, and I almost passed. The case loses because Anduril's secret was that defense-as-software was uninvested; Maquoketa Research's secret is already in the procurement budget. Consensus has arrived before the company has. The flip condition I will watch: Maquoketa Research named in a Replicator or Drone Dominance selection wave with a public PO above $50M in the next twelve months — that would force me to re-underwrite the monopoly trajectory from scratch.
