# Korso -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Korso is the exact shape my Pass Example was written to describe — "Atlas" AI agents that triage RFQs and chase POs across SAP, NetSuite, and Plex — and the dimensional signature is a relatively-tallest score on consequence (manufacturing labor is real) paired with a floor-scraping score on technical/capital risk (software wrapper on commodity LLM tool-use). That pairing is the diagnostic. Korso is a workflow-optimization SaaS dressed in the language of an "intelligence layer," and the homepage dashboard advertising "$2.4M pipeline, 47 active quotes, 89% close rate" against the fictional "Northbridge Metals" and "Blue Harbor Components" tells me Korso has not yet earned the right to a customer logo, let alone a thesis-bending claim.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence — the success-state was a civilization that runs on machine cognition, which is why I wrote $50M into a no-revenue cap-table without a moat. Korso's success-state, taken at maximum ambition from korsoai.com, is "manufacturers respond to RFQs faster and lose fewer POs across seven ERPs." That is not the **Impossible Foods** (2011) pattern of collapsing animal protein production; it is the Salesforce-feature pattern. The dossier gestures at the $15T labor frame I anchored in Fortune (2026-03-06), and yes, manufacturing back-office headcount is part of that number — but Korso does not displace the factory, the supplier, or the ERP itself. It accelerates the email layer above them. The consequence-at-success diverges from OpenAI not by degree but by category: Korso captures workflow margin where OpenAI redraws the cognitive substrate.

The Pass Example I keep on the shelf — "GPT-4 to summarize sales calls and integrate with HubSpot" — fails on technical/capital risk because the science is commodity and any Tier-1 generalist can fund it. Korso's stack is the same shape: LLM tool-use, ERP connectors (Gmail, WhatsApp, WeChat, Slack, Teams on one side; SAP/NetSuite/Dynamics/Sage/SYSPRO/Plex/Acumatica on the other), verification layers, dry-run validation, audit trails. None of those are miracles; they are engineering. Lumari — same YC umbrella, $500K raised, ex-Google/Tesla/Amazon/Stripe engineers — and Turian's $3.78M Berlin raise prove the consensus VCs are already comfortable funding this exact thesis. When consensus is comfortable, my edge is gone. Commonwealth Fusion's $115M Series A repelled consensus because the physics could fail; Korso's tech can be cloned by a competent four-engineer team in a quarter.

The dossier names Martin Pan as a prior GM automations engineer and Daichi Hiraoka and Pan as Penn Electric Racing alumni from UPenn — credentialed, but inside the lines. Compounding mechanism over 18 months: Pan's GM exposure is real founder-market fit for the shop-floor pain, but founder-market fit is necessary, not differentiating. The TED Talk warning applies — the UC Berkeley study found 28,000 expert predictions only slightly more accurate than chance, and Korso's founders are presenting as competent domain practitioners, not as instigators who have publicly contradicted manufacturing-software orthodoxy. There is no defied-expert moment in the dossier; there is no public thesis Korso is willing to be wrong about. That silence is what compounds — without a controversial public position, Korso cannot recruit the asymmetric talent or attention that turns a six-person seed company into a category-defining one by month 18.

Counterfactual on learning rate: the **Slash** (2024) $100M Series C cleared my consequence-magnitude prior because the Kanye-pivot story documented a founder learning rate strong enough to override category. For Korso's score on this dimension to flip a tier, the dossier would need a documented pivot — a prior startup Hiraoka or Pan ran into a wall and reshaped, a public "we were wrong about full ERP coverage, we are going vertical on metals" moment, or a thesis update visible in writing. The dossier contains none of this; it contains a YC P26 listing, a Menlo Times "Launches of the Week #99" mention, and an OK Reporter profile. Applying my absent-evidence default of 7/15 would be generous given that the company is six months old and has had time to publish nothing.

**Genesis AI** (2025) is the single-anchor parallel on required-capital fit, and it cuts against Korso. I co-led $105M into Genesis because robot foundation models need that depth — training compute, simulation infrastructure, hardware partnerships — and no $5M check gets a team to the first commercial milestone. Korso's roadmap, as stated on korsoai.com, requires ERP integration engineering and enterprise sales motion, both of which are bounded burn. Pre-seed YC funding plus a $2-3M seed gets Korso to a credible pilot; another $10-15M Series A gets it to $5M ARR if the GTM works. I am over-resourced for that ramp. Khosla Ventures' $3.5B February 2025 fund is not built to write the check size that matches Korso's capital profile, and the deals where I have stretched downward — DoorDash (2013), Slash (2024) — had override signals on consequence or learning rate that Korso lacks.

Forecast, 36 months: Korso is either acqui-hired by an ERP integrator or mid-market procurement suite (Acumatica, Coupa, or a Plex/Rockwell channel partner) for $15-40M, or it loses the wedge to Lumari and dies at sub-$1M ARR by mid-2028 — with a 15% tail outcome of a vertical pivot into a single segment (specialty metals job-shops) at $2-4M ARR sustained on YC-network customers. The mechanism is competitive compression: Lumari is the same YC thesis with the same agent architecture, Turian has eighteen months and €3.5M of European mid-market traction ahead, and AWS Bedrock AgentCore is already shipping procurement-agent primitives that ERP incumbents will white-label before Korso can entrench switching costs.

The strongest bull case is that manufacturing back-office is genuinely under-automated, mid-market manufacturers genuinely cannot get SAP consultants to care, and the ERP-fragmentation Korso targets (seven systems including SYSPRO and Acumatica that incumbents ignore) is a real wedge no big vendor will serve because each ERP's channel economics resist a cross-platform agent layer. Pan's GM background is genuine founder-market fit. The bull case stings because it maps to the **DoorDash** (2013) exception — capital-light, consensus-fundable, but ambitious enough to win a category. The dossier evidence that wins against the bull case is the fictional dashboard companies, the YC-batchmate Lumari with identical positioning, and the absence of any single named customer or LOI; DoorDash had a working three-restaurant Palo Alto pilot at equivalent stage. Verifiable flip condition: if Korso shows three named mid-market manufacturers ($50M+ revenue) running production RFQ workflows on Atlas with measurable cycle-time deltas within 9 months, I revisit at the seed extension. Until then, this is the Pass Example with a manufacturing skin.
