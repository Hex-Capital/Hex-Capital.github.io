# Dayjob -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Dayjob is the cleanest possible illustration of what my framework refuses to fund: George Postlethwaite and Fred Fooks have genuine specific knowledge from three years inside waste-operator depots building Gaea ERP, and they are deploying that knowledge through founder-led direct sales into UK skip-hire fleets at $496K ARR -- specific-knowledge high, leverage-architecture low. That pairing is a competent vertical-SaaS company, not an asymmetric angel bet, and the entry-point filter resolves before the rest of the dossier finishes loading.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung on permissionless leverage -- media, zero marginal cost of replication, anyone posts without gatekeeper permission. Dayjob diverges from that mechanism at every load-bearing point. The Dayjob distribution motion is depot-by-depot integration into AMCS and Routeware back-ends with founder-led sales into named accounts like Allstone and Coastal Recycling -- this is the labor-and-capital leverage taxonomy from nav.al/product-media that I explicitly contrast against. The dossier does not name a self-serve sign-up, an open-source repo, an organic developer pull, or X-native virality; it names ERP integration depth as the moat. ERP integration depth IS gatekeeper permission, by definition. $496K ARR across roughly three customers in eighteen months is a respectable founder-led sales ramp and a structurally capped one -- each next logo requires a new depot conversation, not a new line of code.

The Pass on Pinterest taught me that personal-taste bias is the wrong tiebreaker when the leverage architecture and asymmetric math both pass; Dayjob is the inverse failure mode -- a vertically-narrow single-sided B2B subscription where each customer is economically independent of the next and the Nth waste fleet does not make the (N-1) prior fleets more valuable. The dossier hand-waves at an inferred "proprietary scheduling decision dataset" compounding across UK/US fleets, but at five customers that is hypothesis, not network. Dayjob lacks the Uber-shape, the Stack Overflow-shape, the AngelList-shape -- there is no two-sided density that turns supply into demand-side gravity.

George Postlethwaite spent five years at Deliveroo launching UK grocery, Fred Fooks ran ESG and Supply Chain Analytics at Deloitte, and the pair did depot ride-alongs with planners during the Gaea ERP build since 2022 -- this is the specific-knowledge filter passing at top quartile by my own anchor language. Eighteen months from now this dimension will keep compounding regardless of the leverage failure: the second-order effect of having lived inside the customer's workflow is that Dayjob will ship features faster than AMCS's AI routing module and faster than Routeware post-RUBICON, because product velocity in vertical B2B is bottlenecked on workflow knowledge, not engineering. That is the strongest line in the dossier, and it is the line that makes Dayjob a real company. It does not make Dayjob my company.

Counterfactual on market: the score on pivot-room flips a tier only if Dayjob's "AI Agents for Industrial Logistics" positioning becomes evidenced rather than aspirational -- a named ports customer, a named warehousing fleet, a named intermodal operator outside waste. The dossier today gives me waste-and-skip-hire across every cited logo and every cited percentage (8% efficiency, 11% revenue, 50% time-window hits, £100K per customer). That is one vertical with one workflow shape. The "first idea never works" axiom from Venture Hacks needs room to maneuver, and waste-trucks-in-the-UK is not where I see two adjacent pivot vectors -- I see one vertical with a US expansion attached, which is geography not pivot-room.

Postlethwaite and Fooks met at Oxford thirteen years ago, lived in five houses together, were ushers at each other's weddings, and co-founded Gaea before Dayjob -- this is the cleanest 10-year-partner signal in the dossier. Compare directly to the Stack Overflow Atwood-Spolsky template: long shared history, no integrity flags, no self-promotion-of-honesty red flag, no contested prior exit. The Gaea-to-Dayjob pivot in January 2025 is a working-the-problem signal, not a churn signal. If I were sizing on the founder trio alone, this is a check. I am not sizing on the founder trio alone -- the trio gates the check, the architecture writes it.

Thirty-six month forecast: Dayjob reaches $4-12M ARR concentrated in UK and early US waste fleets, gets acquired by AMCS or Routeware for a strategic $40-90M, or stays a regional waste-tech specialist below acquisition threshold. The mechanism is the high-low signature -- specific-knowledge high means product velocity beats incumbents at the workflow layer, leverage-architecture low means revenue scales linearly with sales headcount until an incumbent buys the team and the customer book to neutralize the agentic-overlay threat to their license revenue. One-X downside, maybe 20-40x upside on a strategic acquisition. That is a fine Khosla bet. It is not a Naval bet, because the upside geometry does not reach the 1,000-10,000x band that justifies the wide-funnel portfolio system.

The strongest bull case is Perplexity: I waived the network criterion when criterion #1 (permissionless distribution via API/product), criterion #3 (deep specific knowledge), and category-timing all passed at top quartile -- three of five gates clear emphatically and the second criterion becomes negotiable. Could Dayjob qualify under that same exception? Aravind Srinivas had Berkeley ML PhD-level specific knowledge AND Perplexity distributed through a permissionless web product and API that anyone could hit without depot integration. Dayjob has the specific knowledge but fails the distribution leg that made the Perplexity exception structurally legible -- depot ERP integration is not the same shape as a free web product. The Perplexity exception does not extend to Dayjob; only one of the three gates clears. Verifiable flip condition: if Dayjob ships a self-serve product that a US waste operator can sign up for and integrate within 24 hours without a sales call, and that motion drives the next $1M of ARR, I reopen the file -- because that would be the leverage architecture changing shape, not just the revenue number growing.
