# Markov -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Markov is the picks-and-shovels arms dealer to the computer-use AI race -- a two-person team led by Dev Mandal (IIT Madras aerospace '27, ex-Sarvam) and Harish Ashok (17, ex-Zenith Robotics) selling annotated task data and RL environments into Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, and Meta. The dimensional signature here is high on founder-outsiderness relative to a sector dominated by Surge AI ($1.2B 2024 revenue) and Scale (Meta 49% at $30B), and conspicuously low on the technical-and-capital-risk axis that has historically gated my checks. That pairing -- young unencumbered outsiders attacking a commodity-labor-arbitrage market against $25B-funded incumbents -- is not the Markov profile I write into.

**Impossible Foods** (2011) is the HIGH rung on consequence magnitude: when the success-state is named, the success-state was the disappearance of animal protein production. Markov's success-state is "frontier labs train better computer-use agents using our task data," which loads consequence onto Anthropic and OpenAI, not onto Markov. The Fortune 2025-07-01 thesis -- 80% of jobs displaced by 2030, $15T of GDP relocated -- is the right consequence frame, but Markov captures a sliver of the labeling spend, not the labor-displacement surplus. The dossier's own framing names Anthropic's >$1B RL-environment budget as the TAM Markov chases; that is a vendor SAM, not a civilizational consequence. The "wrapper on someone else's foundation model with no proprietary stack" red flag fires here in inverted form -- Markov is the wrapper on annotator labor with no proprietary stack of its own.

The Pass Example in my own framework -- the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer -- failed the technical-risk dimension because there was no miracle, only an integration. Markov is structurally closer to that pattern than to the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) profile that justified my $115M Series A: the @DevvMandal claim of "Scale AI for robotics, with a human fleet in India that can capture thousands of hours of data per day" is operational scaling of BPO, not catalyst chemistry. No physics, no biology, no hardware miracle is named in the Markov dossier. Capital intensity is real but commodity -- annotator workforce, India operations, sales motion -- which means any Tier-1 generalist or a $25B-funded Surge can fund it faster than I can.

Dev Mandal at IIT Madras '27 and Harish Ashok at 17 are technically outsiders to the data-labeling industry, but defying expert consensus requires a public position, not merely a missing resume. Neither founder has publicly contradicted a documented orthodoxy in RL data, computer-use evaluation, or annotator workflows. The TED Talk skepticism I apply to expert credentials cuts in Markov's favor versus a Surge incumbent profile, but absent a stated "instigator" thesis, Markov's founders score at the absent-evidence default. Youth is not, by itself, the **Physical Intelligence** (2024) Levine/Finn pattern -- that pattern required a publicly documented research thesis the field discounted.

Counterfactual on learning rate: for Markov to clear my MID rung here, the dossier would need to name a specific pivot with stated reasoning -- Markov pivoted from general data labeling to computer-use specifically because X, or Dev Mandal sold Sarvam-adjacent IP and chose RL environments because Y. What I have instead is Harish Ashok founding Boldmoon Cloud at 13 and Zenith at 17, and Dev Mandal moving from Sarvam to DevRev to Markov -- that is serial company-starting, not a thesis update. The **Slash** (2024) exception required a documented Kanye-pivot story that demonstrated learning rate strong enough to override consequence-magnitude weakness; Markov has the consequence-magnitude weakness without the offsetting pivot evidence.

Markov's capital profile compounds against the Khosla check size. A pre-seed YC company at ~$500K with an annotator-arbitrage business model needs $5-10M to a meaningful contract pipeline, not $50M to a science milestone. The **Genesis AI** (2025) $105M seed I co-led was capital-deep because the foundation-model training required it; Markov's "human fleet in India" requires operations capital, which is the kind of deployment any consensus VC funds. I am over-resourced for Markov, and that mismatch by itself is a structural pass signal.

36-month forecast for Markov: acqui-hired by Surge AI or Mercor for under $50M in 2027-2028, or pivoted into a narrow vertical RL environment (browser-use for a specific SaaS category) at under $5M ARR. The mechanism is buyer concentration on a handful of frontier labs whose procurement preference will route to the vendor with the deepest existing relationship and the largest annotator fleet -- which is Surge or Scale, not a two-person YC S26 team without a named lab customer.

The strongest objection is the **Slash** (2024) precedent: I wrote a $100M Series C into a YC-backed company in a commodity-feeling market because the founder pivot story was strong enough to override the consequence-magnitude prior. Could Markov's young outsider founders compound annotator-fleet advantages in India into a cost structure Surge cannot match, the way DoorDash compounded consumer-logistics density that the consensus dismissed? The dismantling: the Slash exception required documented learning-rate evidence the Markov dossier does not contain, and the DoorDash analogy fails because consumer logistics had end-user network effects that data-labeling RFPs structurally lack -- labs do not lock into a vendor the way consumers lock into a marketplace. The verdict flips if Markov publishes a signed contract with a named frontier lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or Meta) for >$5M in the next 12 months -- that single fact would prove the founder-led sales motion is breaking through the incumbent moat, and I would re-underwrite.
