# ProjectX -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

ProjectX is a half-articulated secret wrapped in a pure-software wrapper aimed at a category three hyperscalers already occupy ? a 23-year-old design-school CEO from Lovely Professional University proposing to rebuild the OS abstraction layer with $704K of total funding and a 14-follower Twitter account. The dimensional signature is a faint flicker of contrarian framing ("one cursor, one keyboard" as the named constraint of legacy OSes) sitting on top of the weakest possible atoms-heavy and regulated-moat posture. That pairing tells you what ProjectX is: a thesis-shaped pitch that wants to be Stripe-for-agents but has the structural exposure of a Neverinstall clone.

The Stripe (2010) analogue is what the ProjectX deck is implicitly reaching for ? Patrick Collison named the developer-experience failure mode of legacy payment processors and built a primitive the incumbent could not ship because PayPal's architecture was consumer-checkout, not developer-API. The shared mechanism would be: name the architectural reason the incumbent cannot follow. ProjectX has the *form* of this argument ? "conventional OSes constrain users to one cursor, one keyboard, one hardware, one app in focus" is a real observation about a real constraint, and "when half the users of a computer are not people" is a genuine Thielian secret-shaped sentence. But Rounak Adhikary's dossier does not name *why* AWS WorkSpaces or Azure Virtual Desktop structurally cannot ship per-app GPU isolation ? and the dossier itself concedes "no structural barrier preventing this is documented." Stripe's secret was load-bearing because PayPal's risk model could not be retrofitted; ProjectX's secret is a UX observation a hyperscaler product manager could ship in a quarter.

Pure-software SaaS in a commoditizing space with no regulated customer and no hardware capex moat ? this is the canonical low-rung failure mode the Founders Fund manifesto (2011) was written against, and ProjectX sits squarely in it. The GPU compute under ProjectX is rented from someone else's atoms; the "cold start in seconds" claim is a software-orchestration feat, not a capex barrier. DigitalOcean paid $111M cash for Paperspace in July 2023, which tells you exactly how this category clears ? a sub-nine-figure tuck-in to a hyperscaler-adjacent infra player, not a fund-returning monopoly. There are no Lockheed/Raytheon-class incumbents structurally precluded from competing; there are AWS, Azure, GCP, Kasm, Neverinstall, Shadow.tech, and Frame, all of whom can re-architect billing inside a roadmap cycle.

On monopoly trajectory, the mechanism by which ProjectX would compound over 18 months is the standard infrastructure-density loop ? if per-app GPU orchestration produces unit economics 2-3x better than whole-desktop streaming, the savings funnel into a self-serve wedge that the hyperscalers ignore until it's large enough to acquire. The dossier provides zero evidence this loop has started: no published benchmarks, no density numbers, no pricing page (the website returned a loading screen on WebFetch), no named lighthouse customer. Without a single COGS data point or signed enterprise LOI, the moat hypothesis on ProjectX terminates at "we have better session orchestration," which is exactly the "we have a better model" failure pattern from my standing pass example.

For ProjectX to flip from Pass to Neutral on the definite-plan axis, the founders would need to publish a multi-year technical commitment they refuse to pivot from ? say, a public roadmap stating "by Q3 2027 we will support 10,000 concurrent per-app GPU sessions at sub-2-second cold start at $0.X/hr, and we will not pivot to whole-desktop streaming even if revenue is faster there." The dossier shows the opposite shape: a co-founder transition between the 2022 incorporation (Adhikary, Banerjee, Das) and the YC P26 team (Adhikary, Karmakar, Majumder), which is the structural signature of an iteration-mode company that has already revised its founding thesis once. Definite-optimism founders do not swap two of three co-founders pre-seed.

The single-anchor parallel on founder lineage is Affirm (2014) ? Max Levchin was a PayPal Mafia re-up because he had shipped a payments company to a $1.5B exit and was bringing operator-grade conviction to a regulated-customer reload. Rounak Adhikary's analogue is Woldix, a tech-consulting venture he started at 19 that "funded ProjectX's initial stages," and Bishal Karmakar's signal is "Stanford ASES, Draper U, IIT Bombay affiliations" plus a stated focus on "systems, streaming, cloud infra." This is credentialed-outsider energy ? the Thiel Fellowship would not be embarrassed by these profiles ? but it is not shipped-to-scale operator pedigree, and none of the three has a GitHub presence I can verify the systems claims against. The WTC Innovation Award at IIT Bombay Eureka! is a student-competition signal, not a Levchin signal.

My 36-month forecast on ProjectX: dead or acqui-hired below $25M to a GPU-cloud or agent-infrastructure player (E2B, Browserbase, Modal, or a DigitalOcean-class tuck-in), with a 20% probability of pivoting from "OS for humans + agents" to a narrower "agent sandbox / computer-use environment" API sold to AI labs ? which is the only adjacent market where the per-app isolation primitive has a non-commoditized buyer. The mechanism producing this forecast is the dimensional signature itself: a software-only company with a half-named secret and no regulatory or capex moat does not compound into a Palantir or a SpaceX; it compounds into a feature inside a larger infrastructure platform.

The bear case I have to steel-man against is that the agent-sandbox interpretation is exactly the Thielian secret hiding inside ProjectX ? that "when half the users of a computer are not people" is, read Straussian-style, a bet that Anthropic, OpenAI, and the next wave of computer-use agents will need exactly this primitive, and that ProjectX is positioned to be the picks-and-shovels layer with two-year head start technical depth. If that read is correct, ProjectX is closer to my Asana (2010) override ? pure SaaS, consensus territory, but a real wave behind it ? and a $20-50M acquisition by Anthropic or a frontier lab is the central case, not the tail. The reason this objection does not hold: Asana's override was Moskovitz shipping Facebook to scale, and ProjectX has no such pedigree backstop, plus Modal and E2B and Browserbase are already inside this exact thesis with stronger teams and live customer logos. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed contract with a named AI lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, or equivalent) using ProjectX as agent sandbox infrastructure at >$500K ARR within 12 months. Until then, this is the indefinite-optimism Spring 2026 YC archetype dressed in better-than-average secret-language. Pass.
