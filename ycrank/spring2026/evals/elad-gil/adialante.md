# Adialante -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Adialante is a workflow pitch wrapped around a science project, and the highest-vs-lowest signature makes that diagnosis cleanly: the dossier names a real "why now" (Hyperfine's 64mT FDA clearance plus updated AUA/EAU pre-biopsy MRI guidance) which puts the market-reopening dimension on the upper half of my ladder, while the *single-miracle* test fails outright because Adialante simultaneously needs novel FREE gradientless physics to deliver diagnostic-grade prostate imaging, FDA clearance, CMS/commercial reimbursement coding for a non-hospital setting, and mobile-trailer unit economics to pencil. That high/low pairing is exactly the inverse of how I architected **Color Genomics** (2015) -- workflow *above* commodity sequencing that already worked -- and it's why I keep landing on pass.

**Anduril** (2022) is the ladder rung Adialante is implicitly reaching for on catalyst: a market peers had written off, a 24-month policy/technology re-opening, a software-on-hard-thing position. The mechanism that made Anduril work was that Trae Stephens and Palmer Luckey did *not* need new physics -- Ukraine plus DoD software-modernization budgets opened the window, and the hardware referenced existing autonomy stacks. Adialante's analog is weaker on both halves: the catalyst is real but soft -- Hyperfine 64mT FDA clearance and AUA/EAU guidance updates are tagged `[Inferred]` in the dossier rather than cited as a clean step-function -- and unlike Anduril, the hardware is *not* a referenced existing stack but a proprietary "frequency-modulated Rabi-encoded echo" architecture whose first physical system was only scheduled operational *by demo day*. Anduril rode a catalyst with shipped hardware; Adialante is asking me to underwrite the catalyst and the hardware simultaneously.

Pre-transformer ML companies -- the entire 2018-2021 cohort I deliberately skipped -- failed the single-miracle test because each required model capability *and* product *and* distribution *and* a category catalyst that hadn't arrived yet. Adialante's dossier shows the same multi-miracle stack: FREE physics must deliver clinical non-inferiority versus 3T high-field for prostate detection (unproven outside lab); FDA 510(k) or de novo clearance must be obtained (not referenced in any public source); CMS/CPT reimbursement must work for a non-hospital mobile setting (no strategy disclosed); and trailer-level unit economics with drivers, technicians, and maintenance must clear per-scan price. Compounding even generous independent probabilities on those four collapses the expected value -- this is the math that kept me out of fusion and novel battery chem, and Efra?n Torres's PhD pedigree from UMN's CMRR does not change the multiplication.

Revenue velocity, mechanism analysis: Adialante's pipeline -- $12.75M in LOIs, $30k in deposits from one top-10 urology clinic, 6 signed clinics -- compounds to nothing meaningful over 18 months without FDA clearance, because LOIs from urology clinics convert to revenue only after diagnostic clearance lets clinics bill payers. The dossier states the first scanner was only operational by demo day, so even the best 18-month path is FDA submission, clinical validation studies, and one or two paid pilots -- a curve that flattens, not compounds. **Harvey** (2023) at $3B?$5B?$8B in months is the velocity shape I pay for; Adialante's $30k of real money is a *false signal* by the TechCrunch (Nov 2025) framing -- enterprise-pilot tourism, not actual buying behavior.

Index position, counterfactual: for Adialante to become an "index on prostate cancer screening" rather than a clinic-by-clinic service business, the per-scan trailer would need to be the default referral path that every urologist plugs into -- meaning Adialante would need to control the scan-data corpus, the AI interpretation layer, and the workflow handoff back to biopsy. The dossier hints at this ([Inferred] proprietary scan-data corpus compounding AI quality) but the actual position today is a mobile service competing with Promaxo's in-office capital sale and incumbents' hospital relationships. To flip this dimension up a tier, Adialante would need to sign an exclusive scanning agreement with a national urology group (e.g., US Urology Partners or Solaris Health) inside 12 months -- not 6 individual clinics. Until then, Adialante must win share against Promaxo, not ride a category.

Founder execution, single-anchor parallel: **Perplexity** (2022) is my template for "founder ships between meetings" -- Aravind would build the thing a week after we discussed it. Efra?n Torres and Parker Jenkins are PhD-rhythm builders, not Aravind-rhythm builders -- 2 issued U.S. patents and an NSF SBIR Phase II ($1.18M) are real artifacts, but the cycle time is academic-quarterly, not weekly. The dossier confirms this: the first physical scanner was assembled *just in time for demo day* after years of UMN-CMRR research. That is shipping, but it is not the iterative-between-meetings shipping rhythm that gets me to write a check at pre-seed in a regulated category.

Forecast, 36 months: Adialante is most likely alive but underfunded, sub-$2M cumulative scan revenue, FDA 510(k) or de novo submission pending or in review, no strategic acquirer because Siemens/GE/Philips will not buy a per-scan model that cannibalizes their installed-base service contracts and Hyperfine is publicly listed and focused on brain. The mechanism: regulated diagnostic imaging has a clearance-gated revenue curve, and Adialante's $2.93M total raised plus SBIR runway gets them to clearance submission, not to scaled deployment, leaving a hard bridge round in 2027-2028 at flat-to-down terms.

The bull case worth steel-manning is **Color Genomics** (2015) as a known exception -- I co-founded a healthcare hardware-adjacent business with regulatory exposure, and Adialante looks superficially similar: workflow infrastructure on top of an imaging modality, per-scan economics, AI compounding on scan data. The disanalogy is what kills it: Color's architecture put workflow *above commodity sequencing that already worked at clinical quality*, isolating the miracle to data interpretation and consumer GTM. Adialante's architecture puts workflow *above its own unproven FREE physics* -- the hardware miracle is not isolated, it is the dependency. Same with the Anduril exception: Anduril didn't require new physics; Adialante does. The condition that would flip my verdict is specific and verifiable: FDA 510(k) or de novo clearance for prostate MRI with published diagnostic non-inferiority data versus 3T high-field within 18 months. Until then, Adialante is the kind of capitalization-plus-science-plus-market triple-miracle bet my Tim Ferriss Show #863 framing exists to keep me out of.
