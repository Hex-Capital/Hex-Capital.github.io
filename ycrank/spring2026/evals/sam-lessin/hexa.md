# Hexa -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Hexa is the canonical 2026 YC AI-agent-for-a-vertical pitch with the founder slot left blank — Ishaan Makkar's LinkedIn literally says "Stealth," there is no Twitter, no GitHub, no essay, no prior manufacturing employment in the public record, and the company sells "purpose-built AI agents" across SAP, NetSuite, Dynamics, Epicor, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, and Pipedrive with a three-person team. The dimensional signature is brutal and consistent: Hexa is lowest on founder-brand/community (no public surface at all) and least-bad on round-pricing only because I haven't seen a deck yet — but the category and shape make the price prior consensus by default. This is exactly the pitch from my Pass Example, swapped for "manufacturers and distributors."

On Round Is Mis-Priced — my high rung is Solana at $0.05 when crypto funds had quit the category, my mid rung is Sublime Security entering enterprise security before the next consolidation wave, and my low rung is the canonical "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $30M post, four tier-1s already in" pitch I wrote my own Pass Example about. Hexa sits one notch above that low rung purely because the dossier shows no named tier-1s and no disclosed round terms — but "B2B AI agents, S26 YC, David Lieb as group partner, eight ERP integrations advertised" is precisely the deck shape that prices to batch median or above. Silence on round dynamics at this category-fit is not neutral; it's near-certain consensus pricing, and my whole alpha is refusing to pay it.

On Founder-as-Brand — my high rung is John Fish/Bookshelved where the YouTuber's owned distribution literally was the asset, my mid rung is Marina Mogilko where 30-year earnings power justified a non-equity instrument, and my low rung is any founder with no public artifact whatsoever. Hexa is at the floor: Ishaan Makkar's LinkedIn headline is "Stealth," no Twitter account exists, no GitHub repos, no Product Hunt launch, no Discord, no newsletter, no public writing on manufacturing digitization beyond a single unverified search snippet, and the company name collides with eFounders/Hexa.com's $4.5B portfolio so even the SEO is taken. There is no creator-fund overlay, no community pre-existing the company by two years, and no reason to think the cap table includes any distribution asset I could underwrite separately from the software.

On Capital Structure — Teamshares is my high rung for venture-shaped-but-illegible (employee-ownership rollup nobody else underwrites), Allbirds is the DTC mid rung that took standard equity but on a sleeve Sand Hill dismissed, and the low rung is the generic SaaS cap table that needs five rounds to default-alive. Hexa is the low rung — eight-ERP-integration breadth on three engineers is engineering-intensive burn, write-back-to-ERP for invoicing and collections demands enterprise-grade reliability investment, and there is no non-standard instrument on offer. On Founder Conviction, against my own Drop.io arc (high) and Ben Silbermann (mid), Hexa fails the "Writing is thinking" filter completely — one quote about "54% of manufacturers on spreadsheets" via a search snippet is not a non-obvious belief, it's a stat anyone can pull. On Category Sleeve, against Solana (high) and Ro (mid, the healthcare-DTC exception), Hexa lands in the exact sleeve I de-rate explicitly — "AI is just more internet" (Lonsdale #113) — competing with Distro, Turian, Go Autonomous, Arphie, Altura (€8M), and AutoRFP.ai while SAP Joule and Dynamics Copilot ship inside the very ERPs Hexa integrates with.

Forecast over 36 months: Hexa stalls under $1M ARR by mid-2028, fails to differentiate against Distro on distributor order entry or Turian on procurement depth, gets squeezed by SAP Joule and NetSuite's native agent layer on the integration side, and either quietly winds down or acqui-hires into an Epicor/NetSuite-adjacent ISV for sub-$15M with founders moving on. The mechanism is straightforward: an eight-ERP write-back surface area cannot be defended by three people against either single-stack vertical competitors or the ERP vendors themselves, and there is no distribution moat, founder-brand, or non-standard instrument to compensate.

Strongest bull case — and it has to sting to count — runs through my own Ro exception: regulatory/integration complexity can itself be the moat when only venture capital can clear the schleps, and ERP integration depth across SAP/Oracle/Dynamics is genuinely a multi-year licensing-and-certification grind that incumbents-from-above (foundation-model players) won't bother with and incumbents-from-the-side (Distro, Turian) haven't yet matched in breadth. If the unverified "FR8 with Mann Patira" snippet is real and Ishaan actually shipped logistics software at scale, founder-market fit could be the missing piece, and the "54% on spreadsheets" stat does describe a real mid-market gap that SAP Joule is too enterprise-heavy to serve. I'd flip if Hexa shows me a signed multi-six-figure ACV with a named mid-market manufacturer running Epicor or Dynamics where Hexa replaced a manual quoting team — a single legible logo with write-back live in production would tell me the integration moat is real and the regatta has a first tack worth underwriting. Absent that, this is the Pass Example in a Carhartt jacket.
