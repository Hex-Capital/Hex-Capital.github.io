# Adialante -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Adialante is the **Glean** shape transposed into medical hardware: paradigmatic in-discipline experts — Efraín Torres and Parker Jenkins, both PhD-track out of UMN's Center for Magnetic Resonance Research — riding a genuine physics rewrite (the FREE gradientless architecture, 2 issued U.S. patents, $1.18M NSF SBIR Phase II) into a workflow most consensus VCs will not touch because it requires owning trailers and an FDA pathway. The highest dimension is technical and capital risk; the lowest is founder-defies-expert-consensus. That pairing tells me what Adialante is before I write another sentence — a science-deep, conformist-founder bet whose upside lives or dies on whether the physics actually clears 510(k) at diagnostic quality.

**Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) is the right HIGH-rung comparison for the technical-risk dimension because the mechanism is identical: rewrite a piece of physics that the incumbent industry treats as a settled capital-equipment problem, then deploy at a scale generalist VCs read as uninvestable. Where Adialante diverges from the Commonwealth analogue is the falsifiability count. Commonwealth had one big miracle (HTS-magnet confinement) with a clear physics readout; Adialante stacks three — FREE physics yielding prostate-grade image quality without gradients, FDA 510(k) or de novo on a novel architecture, and mobile-trailer unit economics at a per-scan price that beats a $1.88B U.S. prostate-imaging segment dominated by Siemens/GE/Philips capital sales. Each is independently falsifiable, which is what I want, but the first system was only scheduled operational by demo day per the YC launch post — so the catalyst-yield-equivalent miracle is still pre-readout, exactly the **KiOR** (~2008) position at decision time.

The LOW rung on founder-defies-expert-consensus is the "20 years at Salesforce, now applying AI to sales calls" profile I pass on in 30 seconds — the failure mechanism being that the founder extrapolates the past of their own discipline. Adialante does not present that pattern, but it presents the adjacent one: Torres and Jenkins are paradigmatic UMN MRI-physics insiders, the institution that pioneered fMRI, with two patents tied to their own thesis work. That is the **Glean** override case — Arvind Jain was ex-Google search, a pure domain expert, and the consequence magnitude carried the deal. Adialante's consequence magnitude does not carry as far as Glean's Fortune-500 information asymmetry, so the conformist-founder discount bites harder here than it did on Jain.

Consequence magnitude — Adialante's mechanism is access redistribution, not category collapse. Over 1M men/year in the U.S. need pre-biopsy MRI and the dossier states "virtually no urology clinics that treat them can offer one" — that is a named incumbent regime (the hospital-MRI capital-equipment bottleneck) and a quantifiable labor/access displacement, which is the **Impossible Foods** (2011) move applied to imaging rather than protein. But the success-state at maximum ambition is "MRI in every urology clinic via trailer," not "MRI as we know it disappears." That compounds nicely over 18 months as the clinic count grows from 6 to maybe 60, but it does not move a civilizational variable the way the $50M **OpenAI** (2019) check did.

Founder learning rate — counterfactual: what would have to be true to push Adialante from a 7 into the **Slash** (2024) tier on this axis? A documented pre-Adialante pivot — Torres or Jenkins killing a prior FREE-architecture variant and stating publicly why, or a thesis change from low-field portable (the Hyperfine lane) into mobile per-scan after seeing Hyperfine's ~$13.6M FY2024 revenue ceiling. The dossier shows neither. What it shows is a linear path from CMRR research to two patents to NSF SBIR Phase II to YC — competent but unswerving. No evidence on this dimension; I apply the default and do not penalize harder because the company is two years old.

Required-capital profile — single-anchor parallel to **Physical Intelligence** (2024). Both are stack-deep physical-AI plays where the roadmap (trailer fleet + FDA + per-scan AI compounding on a proprietary data corpus) plausibly absorbs $50M+ to a first commercial milestone and $200M+ to scale, against $2.93M raised to date per PitchBook. That is the right shape for a Khosla check; it is not over-resourced like a $5M-to-$1M-ARR SaaS deal. The deployment story — own the hardware, charge per scan, compound AI on scan data — is exactly the capital-deep configuration the Tier-1 generalists decline on capex anxiety.

My 36-month forecast: Adialante files a 510(k) on the FREE architecture for pre-biopsy prostate MRI within 24 months, operates 3–8 trailers against a converted slice of the $12.75M LOI pipeline reaching roughly $3–7M run-rate revenue, and raises a $30–50M Series A led by a hardware-friendly fund (Lux, 8VC, or a strategic from Siemens/GE pre-empting the channel threat) — conditional on the first trailer producing prostate images that radiologists score as diagnostic-equivalent to 3T high-field by month 12. If that readout fails, Adialante pivots to lower-acuity screening adjacencies (musculoskeletal, breast follow-up) and burns through 2027 raising a bridge.

The strongest objection to my Neutral-leaning lean comes from my own **DoorDash** (2013) and **Slash** (2024) exceptions: I have a documented willingness to override consequence-magnitude shortfalls when the operational mechanism is genuinely novel and the pipeline conversion is real, and $12.75M in LOIs plus $30k in actual deposits from a top-10 urology clinic is more pre-product commercial signal than most YC S26 hardware companies will show. The bear that wins anyway is the unstated pattern I keep hidden from myself — I prefer founders who have already had one exit or one publicly known intellectual reputation before raising from me (the Levine pattern at Physical Intelligence, the academic-spinout shape at Commonwealth Fusion), and Torres and Jenkins, while credentialed, are first-time founders without that prior intellectual scaffolding. The verifiable condition that flips me to Invest: a 510(k)-grade prostate image from the first operational trailer published or shown to me within 6 months of demo day, at which point the founder-conformity discount stops mattering and the capital-deep deployment story takes over.
