# Verdant -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Verdant is a two-person Georgetown-undergrad GovTech SaaS pitch entering the permit-review category exactly 30 days after GovWell printed a $25M Insight Partners Series A for the same staff workflow — which is the worst possible time to be the second AI-native permit reviewer at YC, and the dossier's defining feature is the absence of any structural reason Aidan Ng and Jason Yi win this race rather than get steamrolled by it. The signature here is high-ish on round-price ambiguity (GovTech isn't the hottest category on the YC S26 sheet) and floor-scraping on community/founder-brand. That pairing tells me Verdant is a generic vertical-AI SaaS dressed in municipal clothing, not a creator-shaped or holdco-shaped asset I can structure around.

On price discipline, the rung above Verdant is **Sublime Security** — the enterprise security seed I took before the next consolidation wave, where the alpha was buying into a category two years before consensus security capital arrived. The shared mechanism is "buy the GovTech / SecOps category before the late-stage money tunes in." Verdant fails that mechanism by exactly the May 2026 calendar: GovWell raising $25M from Insight Partners for AI AutoCheck targeting the same staff-review workflow means consensus capital has already arrived in this category, and Verdant is showing up to the regatta after the starting gun. PermitFlow's $91M across Seed/A/B at a ~$500M valuation in the adjacent applicant-side lane confirms this is a fully-priced sleeve, not a pre-consensus one. Verdant being a YC seed in GovTech rather than in AI-agents-for-legal is a marginal price improvement over the canonical "$30M post on $25K MRR" YC pass — but it isn't a Solana-at-$0.05 entry, it's a late entry into a category Insight already underwrote.

The lowest rung Verdant sits closest to on founder-as-brand is my canonical pass: a YC team with zero public writing, zero owned distribution, zero community footprint, depending entirely on outbound sales — the failure mechanism is that you become a budget line in a municipal RFP next to Accela, Tyler, OpenGov, SmartGov, Cloudpermit, and PSDcitywide (per the City of Astoria audit), and you lose on procurement infrastructure rather than product. Verdant's dossier is precisely this pattern: Aidan Ng has no Twitter, no GitHub, a LinkedIn that reads "Student at Georgetown University," and his only documented platform is a Georgetown Global Lab page about immigration policy interests. Jason Yi's profile is literally unverifiable — the linkedin.com/in/jason-yi1/ headline isn't even retrievable. There is no newsletter on permitting code, no Substack on Accela's failure modes, no Discord of frustrated municipal planners. The only path to a customer on verdantapp.com is a Calendly link. That's the John-Fish / Bookshelved comparison in perfect inverse: Fish brought a literary YouTube audience to the cap table; Verdant brings two unindexed LinkedIns.

On conviction visible pre-pitch — the "what if they're right?" inversion — I look for the non-obvious belief about permit review that Aidan Ng won't be talked out of, and the dossier surfaces nothing. Verdant's "backgrounds in public service" line on verdantapp.com is unverified in any search result; neither founder has documented planning-department, city-staff, or permit-review operating time. Compounded over 18 months this matters because the missionary-temperament test exists precisely to predict which founder grinds through the 18-month municipal sales cycle without quitting — Verdant has the Georgetown-undergrad profile that historically pivots to consulting or law school when the third RFP slips by two quarters.

Capital-structure-wise, Verdant would have to be true that municipal permitting is a default-alive-in-24-months business for the score to flip a tier — and the dossier evidence pins it the opposite way. Accela's average permitting contract is ~$382K (Civic IQ Blog, April 2026), municipal RFPs run 5+ vendors deep, and GovWell needed $34.5M cumulative to reach 130 municipalities across 34 states. Verdant at two people with no disclosed customers cannot regatta-tack to a Series A on the GovWell pace without raising the same $34.5M, which means this is a five-round SaaS cap table dressed as a seed — exactly the structure I de-rate. The **Ro** override doesn't apply: Ro's regulatory schleps were 50-state prescription rails that genuinely needed VC dollars to clear, whereas Verdant's regulatory complexity is "read a zoning code" which is the product, not a moat.

Sleeve-fit-wise, Verdant is not crypto, not DTC consumer, not Wizard-of-Oz services pivoting to software, not a creator instrument, not employee-equity rollup. **Solana** at $0.05 was the canonical sleeve bet — real on-chain economic activity at a price where the category had been left for dead. Verdant is vertical AI SaaS sold to government — "AI is just more internet" applied to a procurement-bound buyer with 12-month sales cycles. The only sleeve-adjacent argument is that GovTech is "boring enough" to be Slow-shaped, but the GovWell Series A is the proof that this stopped being non-consensus a quarter ago.

My 36-month forecast: Verdant is acquihired by GovWell, Accela, or Tyler for under $5M by Q3 2029, or pivots to applicant-side permit prep (PermitFlow's lane) and lands under $500K ARR before running out of pre-seed runway — the mechanism is that GovWell's 130-municipality head start compounds via reference selling in a procurement category that rewards incumbency, and a two-person undergrad team cannot out-execute Insight Partners' check on a 12-month cycle.

The strongest bull case: pandemic-era Accela/Tyler contracts ARE churning at renewal (YC company description, corroborated by Civic IQ Blog), LLM document parsing is genuinely production-viable for the first time in 2026, and the market is large enough ($1.85B NA SAM) that GovWell and Verdant can both win — Aidan Ng's Georgetown government affiliation could plausibly open 3-5 municipal pilots through the policy-network channel, and a focused two-person team often out-ships a $25M-funded org in year one. That case stings because GovWell raising $25M in May 2026 is also evidence that municipal LLM permit review actually works as a business — meaning Verdant is in a category that's real, just late. It dismantles on the procurement-cycle math: GovWell's 130 municipalities are reference customers that will close GovWell's next 130 before Verdant closes its first ten, and the dossier shows zero offsetting asymmetry — no founder distribution, no proprietary data, no non-standard financing instrument, no operator-friend channel into a city CIO. Verdict flips to Invest if Verdant signs three named municipalities at ≥$50K ACV within 12 months of YC demo day — that's the falsifiable condition. Until then, I'm out before the meeting starts.
