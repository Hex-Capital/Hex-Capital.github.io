# WithAI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

WithAI is a competent founder-market-fit pre-seed selling fund-specific workflow software to a buyer segment that is *shrinking* into the very platforms that the YC blurb names as the competitive set -- Ian McInnis from Bridgewater plus Ben Finch from Sentient Labs is a credible team, but the dimensional signature is *founder credibility relatively present, index position structurally absent*, and that pairing is what determines the bet. WithAI's >$100K ARR three months in is real, but the question is whether WithAI is riding a compounding category or selling bespoke installations into a flat one.

WithAI's strongest dimension is founder fit, and the natural ladder rung is **Decagon** (2023) -- workflow infrastructure built by operators who lived inside the buyer's pain. The shared mechanism: a buyer-side operator (Ian, having held an equity book at Bridgewater and led the "how do we use LLMs to invest" mandate) plus an agent-infrastructure builder (Ben, prompt security and multi-agent coordination at Sentient Labs, co-author on OML/IACR 2024/1573) covers both ends of the workflow surface. Where WithAI diverges from the **Decagon** rung: Decagon shipped into customer support, a category measured in tens of thousands of seats per logo on a workflow with verifiable outputs; WithAI ships into fundamental equity hedge funds where the founders themselves concede on the YC page that outputs are "non-verifiable" -- that admission is the kind of self-aware honesty I respect, but it also tells me the miracle isn't the same shape.

WithAI's weakest dimension is index position, and the LOW rung is **Lyft** Series C -- a company that had to *win share against* a network-effect incumbent rather than ride a compounding category. The Lyft failure mechanism wasn't quality; it was that the market structure made #2 economics permanently sub-scale. Trace it into WithAI: the buyer segment for "fundamental equity hedge fund" software is not expanding -- AUM is consolidating into Citadel, Millennium, and Point72 multi-strat platforms (the exact "huge platforms' in-house AI projects" called out in the YC description), and the independent fundamental shops WithAI sells into are the segment *losing* share. WithAI is selling into the shrinking half of the barbell. Hebbia at $130M Series B / $700M post and Rogo at $750M post-money (Sequoia-led Series C, Jan 2026) are not WithAI's main problem -- the main problem is that WithAI's TAM is the wrong end of an asset-management consolidation curve.

WithAI's market catalyst on dimension 1 -- mechanism analysis: the "why now" the dossier offers is long-context multi-document reasoning crossing a usability threshold in 2024-2025, which is true but generic across every knowledge-worker vertical and therefore not a *WithAI-specific* catalyst. Compounded over 18 months, a generic-capability catalyst attracts every competent team into the same category -- which is exactly what Hebbia's $130M raise and Rogo's $750M post-money already prove happened. WithAI does not get a 24-month uncontested window; WithAI enters a category that is already three rounds deep at the growth stage.

WithAI's revenue velocity on dimension 2 -- counterfactual: $100K ARR with multiple clients at three months post-launch is meaningful for hedge fund enterprise sales given the 6-12 month procurement cycles those buyers normally run, but for the score to flip a tier I would need WithAI to disclose month-over-month growth >20% with a named second and third logo beyond Mercator Partners, or a signed pilot with a recognized multi-billion AUM shop in Greg Jensen / Karen Karniol-Tambour's network. The dossier discloses neither. "Multiple clients" without count is the language teams use when the count is two or three; if it were six or eight, WithAI would say so.

WithAI's single-miracle test on dimension 5 -- single-anchor parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015): Color worked because the architecture isolated the miracle to workflow-and-data on top of *already-commoditized* sequencing chemistry. WithAI's architecture asks the agent to produce useful work on non-verifiable investment research, which the founders themselves flag on the YC page as the unresolved technical claim. That is one miracle, not three, so this dimension doesn't disqualify WithAI -- but it is a *harder* single miracle than Decagon's "LLM accuracy on support tickets" because there is no ground-truth evaluator for "good fundamental research," which means WithAI cannot run the standard eval-driven product loop that drove Harvey's curve.

WithAI 36-month forecast: WithAI reaches $3-6M ARR with 12-20 named hedge fund logos, gets acquired by Hebbia or AlphaSense for sub-$120M as a vertical-extension tuck-in, or pivots to become a Bridgewater-network specialist consultancy monetizing the Jensen/Karniol-Tambour relationship rather than a horizontal product. The mechanism producing this: WithAI's client-cloud single-tenant deployment is a real architectural advantage *per logo* but an anti-pattern at category scale, so it caps reachable ARR before the category consolidates around a multi-tenant winner -- which makes WithAI valuable as an acquihire rather than as an independent compounding business.

The strongest opposing case: hedge funds' paranoia about sending proprietary research and positions to multi-tenant SaaS is a genuine structural moat that Hebbia and Rogo's unit economics literally cannot match -- and Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour as angels is the most warm-intro-dense distribution wedge anyone could hand a finance-software seed. Bull case says WithAI becomes the "Palantir for hedge funds" -- bespoke, on-prem-feeling, high-ACV, sticky after integration. The dismantle: Palantir worked because government buyers couldn't and wouldn't switch and the category (defense + intel) was structurally *growing*; fundamental equity hedge funds are structurally *shrinking* as a share of AUM into multi-strats whose in-house AI teams are explicitly the competitive set WithAI's own YC page names. Sticky software in a shrinking buyer pool is a $20-80M ARR business, not a $1B outcome. **Condition that flips my verdict**: WithAI lands a signed contract with a named multi-strat platform (Citadel, Millennium, Point72, or Balyasny) within 12 months -- that would convert WithAI from "selling into the shrinking half" to "embedded in the growing half" and would make me reconsider the index-position score from 4 to 11.
