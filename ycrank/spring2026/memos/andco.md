## The Idea

- **Problem:** Personal injury pre-litigation workup (retrieving police/accident reports, medical and billing records, insurance coverage and claims correspondence) consumes 8+ hours of legal staff time per case across fax, mail, phone, email, and web channels (useandco.com, May 2026; YC page, May 2026).
- **Approach:** AI agents operate end-to-end across fax, email, mail, web, SMS, and voice to retrieve documents, manage insurance claims, identify coverage, and coordinate with medical providers, syncing files into the firm's case management system (e.g., Filevine) (useandco.com, May 2026).
- **Differentiation:** [Inferred]: EvenUp and Supio focus on demand-package generation and medical chronologies from documents already in hand, whereas Andco's stated scope is upstream retrieval/communication across non-digital channels (fax, mail, voice) before workup (useandco.com vs. supio.com vs. evenuplaw.com, May 2026).
- **Business Model:** No public pricing data found; site CTA is "Book a Demo" (useandco.com, May 2026). [Inferred]: Per-case or per-seat SaaS sold direct to PI firms, given demo-gated GTM.
- **TAM/SAM:** US Personal Injury Lawyers & Attorneys industry = $61.7B revenue in 2025, 2.5% CAGR 2020–2025, 50,435 firms, no firm above 5% share (IBISWorld, 2025).
- **GTM / Distribution:** Direct sales sourced via in-person prospecting — founders described going to law firms posing as DoorDash Dashers with donut boxes, reaching first customers after "200 boxes of donuts" (No Cap Blog, 2026; YC LinkedIn post, 2026).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Multi-channel agent infrastructure spans fax, mail, web, SMS, voice and CMS sync with Filevine (useandco.com, May 2026); no public data on proprietary data assets or patents found.
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Workflow lock-in via deep CMS integrations and trained agents per insurance carrier/provider portal could create switching costs; unproven now given March 2026 launch (YC page, May 2026).
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: PI law is highly fragmented (50,435 firms, no >5% share per IBISWorld 2025), and legacy practice-management vendors (Filevine, Litify, MyCase) monetize software seats rather than per-case agent labor, so an "agent-as-staff" pricing model is unit-economic-incompatible with their seat-based P&Ls.
- **Commoditization risk:** Direct competitors EvenUp ($385M raised, $2B valuation, Fortune, Oct 2025) and Supio (agentic AI platform for PI, supio.com, 2026) could extend upstream into retrieval; foundation models lower the barrier to building agent wrappers.

## Key Risks

- **Direct competition from well-capitalized incumbents:** EvenUp ($385M raised, $2B valuation, 2,000+ firms, 10,000 cases/week) and Eve ($103M, $1B+ valuation) target the same PI firm buyer (Fortune, Oct 2025); mitigation: Andco's stated upstream multi-channel retrieval scope differs from EvenUp/Supio's demand-package focus (useandco.com, May 2026).
- **Technical feasibility on legacy channels:** Reliable AI agents over fax, postal mail, and voice phone calls to insurance carriers and medical providers are unproven at scale in public benchmarks; no third-party accuracy data found.
- **Regulatory/UPL exposure:** AI-driven communication with insurance adjusters and medical providers on behalf of law firms may implicate state unauthorized-practice-of-law and HIPAA rules; no public data found on Andco's compliance posture.
- **Concentration on a single vertical and case type:** Product is scoped to plaintiff PI pre-litigation workup (useandco.com, May 2026); demand shocks in PI case volume (tort reform, insurance settlement patterns) would directly hit revenue.
- **Channel/CMS dependency:** Distribution and product depend on integrations like Filevine (useandco.com, May 2026); incumbent CMSs shipping native agentic workup features would substitute directly.
