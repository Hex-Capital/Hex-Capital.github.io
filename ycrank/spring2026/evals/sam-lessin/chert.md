# Chert -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Chert is an AI SDR that does outbound over iMessage, and that one-sentence description is the entire investment debate. The AI-SDR category is the most consensus-saturated corner of the YC P26 batch -- 11x.ai raised $74M from Benchmark and a16z, hit ~$10M ARR, then reportedly bled 70-80% of customers within months per TechCrunch. Artisan, AiSDR, Regie -- a parade of email/LinkedIn wrappers. Chert's wedge is the channel swap to iMessage, where Sendblue (also YC) and Project Blue already sell the underlying primitive. So the question isn't whether Chert is competent. It's whether the iMessage channel is a real contrarian mispricing or just a sticker on the same melting ice cube.

The bull case for Chert is genuinely worth steelmanning. iMessage outbound makes sophisticated investors squirm -- there is no official Apple business API per Sendblue's own copy, the operational stack requires Mac relay infrastructure, and the legal posture is TCPA-adjacent. That's the kind of category discomfort that produced Eaze. Most top-tier VCs won't touch it because it looks like spam-on-Apple, which means if iMessage outbound becomes a normalized B2B channel, Chert's positioning is mispriced. Their site claims 10x response rates and "127 leads sourced" in a sample week, and the testimonial logos -- Whop, Ramp, Databricks-affiliated -- aren't nothing for an undergrad team. That's the Eaze-shaped story: gray-area channel, real demand, incumbents structurally blocked.

I'm not buying it. Eaze had physical-world delivery operations and state-licensed dispensary relationships -- a regulatory moat that compounded. Chert has a Mac relay and a phone-number list. The iMessage primitive itself already exists at Sendblue and Project Blue, which means the moment Artisan or 11x decides iMessage is interesting, they plug in the API and Chert's channel differentiation evaporates in a sprint. This is the AI kill test in pure form: if the wedge is "AI SDR but iMessage," any incumbent AI SDR can become "AI SDR but iMessage" in eight weeks. The structural durability isn't there. Chert is a feature on Sendblue's API, not a company.

The Apple platform risk on Chert is the kind of thing I've watched kill scooter companies and crypto exchanges -- your unit economics depend on a regulator (here, Apple) deciding not to enforce. Apple has every commercial incentive to clamp down on commercial outbound iMessage the moment it becomes a real volume channel, because it degrades the trust property that makes iMessage valuable in the first place. Chert's pitch literally calls iMessage "a trusted, personal channel" -- that trust evaporates the moment B2B SDR campaigns become normalized. So the more successful Chert gets, the more it triggers the platform response that kills it. That's not a bug, that's the structure.

Ian Fong is an NYU undergrad with eight hackathon wins heading into a JPMC internship; Gary Gao is a Penn/Wharton sophomore with circuits-lab research credentials. These are smart, accomplished kids. But the sane-person/insane-idea calibration I look for requires founder observation across cycles -- I backed Raj on Solana because I'd watched him through three companies, not because he had a clean LinkedIn. With Chert there's no shared history between Fong and Gao that I can verify, no prior B2B sales-tooling experience, and one founder is heading to a bank internship. That's a focus signal, not a missionary signal. The idea also isn't insane in the productive sense -- it's a channel arbitrage on a saturated category, not a primitive bet. A reasonable person doing a reasonable thing in a hot category is the most boring quadrant of my matrix.

Chert fails the primitive test outright. Venmo created peer-to-peer payments as a verb. Patreon created direct creator monetization as a behavior. Chert is "11x.ai but iMessage" -- the underlying behavior (AI-driven cold outbound) already exists, and the channel underneath it is provided by someone else's API. No new market gets created here. After Chert succeeds, no one says "Chert me" -- they say "I got cold-pitched on iMessage now too, great." On capital discipline I have no real evidence either direction; managed-service AI-SDR companies historically need to scale headcount with revenue, which is the opposite of the lean wedge I want, but Chert is too early to score this hard.

The bear case wins on Chert. Pass. The single condition that would flip me: evidence that Chert has built proprietary lead-sourcing infrastructure on verified mobile numbers that materially outperforms the standard ZoomInfo/Apollo data layer -- not the channel, the data underneath. If the moat is "we know which phone numbers actually belong to which buyer at which company with 95%+ accuracy," that's a defensible asset that survives Apple shutting off iMessage tomorrow because it ports to SMS, WhatsApp, RCS, anything. Without that, Chert is a thin layer on someone else's API in a category where the leader is already churning out 70% of its book. The two dimensions that lock my verdict: Structural Durability (the channel is a Sendblue API call away from any competitor, and Apple holds the kill switch) and Primitive Creation (this is channel arbitrage on a saturated AI-SDR category, not a new behavior).
