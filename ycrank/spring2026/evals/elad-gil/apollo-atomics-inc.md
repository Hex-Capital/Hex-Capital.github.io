# Apollo Atomics, Inc. -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Apollo Atomics is the canonical shape of a deal I pass on: a real catalyst (hyperscaler power demand) wrapped around a multi-miracle science bet, with Assil Halimi's MIT PhD doing all the founder-market-fit work while the dossier quietly admits the 40x compact steam generator's two-phase flow performance is still in experimental testing under the April 2026 MIT NSE collaboration. The high-low signature here -- legitimate market re-opening paired with the most acute single-miracle violation in the cohort -- tells me exactly what Apollo Atomics is: the kind of company I've publicly said I "largely avoided" on the Tim Ferriss Show #863 because "there's capitalization issues and science risk and market risk."

The Anduril (2022) Series D is the load-bearing comparison on the catalyst dimension because the mechanism is structurally identical -- a category peers had written off (defense pre-Ukraine; civilian nuclear pre-AI-power-demand) reopened on a specific 24-month window, and Apollo Atomics is genuinely riding the Aalo $100M-data-center-pairing, hyperscaler-PPA wave that re-rated the entire SMR category in 2025. Where Apollo Atomics diverges from Anduril is the entry posture: Anduril came across at Series D with contracts already compounding and hardware referencing existing autonomy stacks, while Apollo Atomics is pre-seed with a 2028 pilot, a 2028 NRC construction permit target, and a steam generator whose geometry has no operational precedent -- the catalyst is real, but Apollo Atomics is paying for it with novel physics rather than novel distribution.

The single-miracle failure mode I flagged in my Lyft Series C pass was structural -- a #2 in a winner-take-all market with no inversion path -- and the analogous failure mode for Apollo Atomics is worse: it is not a single-miracle bet at all. Apollo Atomics requires the 40x compact steam generator to work at PWR conditions, an NRC construction permit on a novel design to land by 2028, a Series A/B that closes the capital gap against Aalo's $127M+ and X-energy's $700M+, fuel qualification, and a hyperscaler PPA before any of the above is proven -- five independent miracles compounding to a probability my framework forces me to walk away from. This is the exact pattern *Unusual Signs of Billion-Dollar Companies* (NFX) warns against, and Apollo Atomics fits it more cleanly than any deal I've seen in this batch.

On revenue velocity, Apollo Atomics offers a target cost of "3¢/kWh" published on the company website and zero -- literally zero -- units of demonstrated demand, pilot revenue, or signed offtake. The Harvey (2023) test of "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" is unfair to apply to a reactor company, but my own framework is unforgiving here: a 2028 pilot means the first revenue-velocity data point is 24+ months past the close of any check I'd write today, and the mechanism that compounds in software ("just working") does not exist in licensing-gated hardware where the curve is procurement timelines, not adoption. Eighteen months from now, Apollo Atomics will either have a permit filing accepted or it will not -- there is no MoM growth curve to inspect.

What would have to be true for index position to flip a tier? Apollo Atomics would need to become the reactor SKU that every new data-center buildout integrates against, the way Stripe (2010) became the rails every e-commerce entrant touched. The dossier gives the opposite picture: NuScale holds the only NRC-certified SMR design, Aalo is targeting July 2026 cold criticality at INL, X-energy has the DOE demonstration and Amazon's $500M+, and Apollo Atomics is one of at least five funded compact-reactor developers behind on licensing. To become an index Apollo Atomics has to *win* against named, better-capitalized competitors -- which is the inverse of index logic.

Halimi's MIT PhD (2021-2025) on compact iPWR steam generators under Prof. Shirvan is genuine founder-market fit -- this is the rare dossier where the technical co-founder's prior research output literally *is* the company's product, and Drew Walker's prior Blue Innovations Group (electric boats) plus White House operational background is a credible non-technical complement. Compared to Aravind Srinivas at Perplexity (2022) "showing up a week later with a thing we discussed built," Apollo Atomics cannot ship iteratively -- reactors don't have a weekly build cadence, and the MIT collaboration announced April 16, 2026 is the closest analogue to a shipped artifact. That's not a disqualifier on its own; it's a structural limit on how much founder-execution signal can ever exist in this category pre-permit.

My 36-month forecast for Apollo Atomics: still pre-revenue in mid-2029, NRC construction permit application filed but not granted, Series A of $20-50M closed at the cost of significant dilution against Aalo and X-energy's capital lead, no commercial PPA signed, and a non-trivial probability of being acqui-hired by Westinghouse, GE-Hitachi, or a better-capitalized SMR developer for the steam-generator IP and the MIT research pipeline at a modest valuation. The mechanism is straightforward: capital intensity compounds against Apollo Atomics every quarter the licensing clock runs, while better-funded competitors hit demonstration milestones (Aalo's July 2026 cold criticality, NuScale's existing certification) that make Apollo Atomics' fundraising narrative progressively harder.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the Anduril (2022) Known Exception template: Halimi has shipped his MIT PhD which *is* the steam generator design, the MIT NSE collaboration is the forward-deployed academic muscle equivalent of Palantir's, and hyperscaler power demand is the Ukraine-equivalent catalyst -- if Apollo Atomics lands a hyperscaler conditional PPA before the Series A, the deal looks like Anduril, not Color. The dossier dismantles this: Anduril at Series D had contracts compounding visibly and hardware that referenced existing autonomy stacks, while Apollo Atomics' compact steam generator is the novel physics bet itself -- the dossier explicitly states "two-phase flow and heat-transfer performance at PWR conditions are not yet demonstrated at full scale." Apollo Atomics is the seed-stage science project version of the Anduril pattern, not the growth-stage software-on-hardware version that triggered my check in 2022. **One verifiable condition would flip the verdict**: a named hyperscaler signing a conditional PPA tied to Apollo Atomics' A-10 pilot before the Series A closes -- that converts this from a multi-miracle science bet into a Mistral-style hyperscaler-coupled outcome I treat as a feature, not a bug.
