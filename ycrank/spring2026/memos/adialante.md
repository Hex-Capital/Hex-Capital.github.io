## The Idea

- **Problem:** Over 1M men/year in the U.S. require an MRI before a prostate biopsy, but virtually no urology clinics that treat them can offer one because conventional MRI systems cost millions, take months to install, and need specialist operators (YC launch post, 2026).
- **Approach:** Adialante operates mobile imaging trailers built around a proprietary "frequency-modulated Rabi-encoded echo" (FREE) gradientless MRI architecture redesigned with new physics, algorithms, and hardware to be compact, silent, and operable without dedicated MRI infrastructure (YC launch post, 2026; Fogarty Innovation, 2024).
- **Differentiation:** Named alternatives include Promaxo (in-office single-sided prostate MRI sold as a capital purchase), Hyperfine Swoop (low-field portable brain MRI, not whole-body/prostate), and Prenuvo (consumer full-body screening at fixed-site clinics) — Adialante differs by (a) bringing the scanner to the clinic in a trailer, (b) charging per scan rather than selling hardware, and (c) targeting urology-clinic workflow for prostate screening (YC launch post, 2026; promaxo.com; hyperfine.io; prenuvo.com).
- **Business Model:** Flat per-scan fee paid by the clinic with no equipment purchase or buildout, with Adialante owning and operating the hardware and continuously improving AI software after each scan (YC launch post, 2026; adialante.com).
- **TAM/SAM:** U.S. prostate cancer imaging segment of $1.88B in 2025 within a $4.37B global imaging segment and a $9.72B global prostate cancer diagnostics market growing at ~9.64% CAGR (Precedence Research / Global Growth Insights, 2025).
- **GTM / Distribution:** Direct sign-ups with urology clinics via an "early access" program; 6 clinics signed and $12.75M in letters of intent with $30k in deposits from a top-10 urology clinic disclosed at YC launch (YC launch post, 2026; adialante.com).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** 2 issued U.S. patents covering the FREE/gradientless MRI approach, plus an $1.18M NSF SBIR Phase II grant validating the technology (adialante.com; NSF I-Corps Hub, 2025).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: A proprietary scan-data corpus from per-scan operations could compound an AI-quality advantage as more clinics onboard, but unproven now since the first system was only scheduled to be operational by demo day (YC launch post, 2026).
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbents (Siemens, GE, Philips) sell multi-million-dollar capital equipment and bundled service contracts; pivoting to a per-scan trailer model would cannibalize their installed-base service revenue and conflict with their hospital sales channel.
- **Commoditization risk:** [Inferred]: Replicating the FREE physics requires PhD-level MRI expertise (the founding team came out of UMN's Center for Magnetic Resonance Research), and patents plus FDA pathway costs raise the barrier, but well-funded peers like Hyperfine ($90M Series D, Radiology Business 2024) or Promaxo ($99.1M raised, Tracxn 2026) could pursue adjacent mobile/per-scan models.

## Key Risks

- **FDA regulatory pathway:** A novel gradientless MRI architecture for diagnostic prostate imaging will require FDA 510(k) or de novo clearance, and no clearance is referenced in public sources (adialante.com; YC launch post, 2026); mitigation: NSF SBIR Phase II funding ($1.18M) is supporting technical validation (NSF I-Corps Hub, 2025).
- **Technical feasibility at clinical performance:** The first physical system was only scheduled to be operational by demo day, so diagnostic-grade image quality vs. high-field MRI for prostate cancer detection remains unproven outside lab settings (YC launch post, 2026).
- **LOI-to-revenue conversion:** $12.75M of disclosed pipeline is in letters of intent, which are non-binding; only $30k in actual deposits has been collected (YC launch post, 2026).
- **Reimbursement risk:** [Inferred]: Per-scan economics depend on CMS/commercial payer reimbursement codes for pre-biopsy prostate MRI; if Adialante operates under non-hospital settings, existing CPT/HCPCS rates may not apply cleanly. No public reimbursement strategy disclosed.
- **Mobile-trailer scaling/unit economics:** Owning and operating physical trailers across geographies creates capex per unit and operational complexity (drivers, technicians, maintenance) that pure-software businesses do not face; no public unit-economics data disclosed (YC launch post, 2026).
