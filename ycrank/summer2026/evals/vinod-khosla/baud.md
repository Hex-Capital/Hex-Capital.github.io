# Baud -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Baud is a credentialed-insider bet attacking NVIDIA at the arithmetic layer — strongest on the capital/technical risk axis that consensus VCs flinch from, weakest on the founder-defies-expert-consensus axis I cited in my TED Talk. Sarang Zambare's path runs IIT Bombay → UC Berkeley → Caper (Instacart) → Peloton Guide (100K+ devices), and Eric Taylor's runs NVIDIA → NXP → Arteris → Enfabrica with three tape-outs. That is the **Glean** profile of ex-incumbent credentials, not the Pat Brown profile of public field-defiance. The thing being defied is the math, not the founders' resumes.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence: a $50M first check because the success-state was the cost of cognition collapsing toward zero. Baud claims an arithmetic representation that "losslessly eliminates multiplications" and demonstrates 50M parameters at 1000+ tokens/sec on FPGA. If even a fraction of the "100x more efficient at the same process node and memory bandwidth" claim survives tape-out at frontier scale, the named regime that collapses is NVIDIA's CUDA-plus-GPU pricing power on training — the single most expensive line item in every frontier-model P&L. That is not "30% better." Where Baud diverges from the **OpenAI** analogy is the falsifiability of the miracle: OpenAI's bet was on a scaling curve that was already empirically observable in 2019; Baud's "lossless multiplication elimination" is a mathematical claim that has historically degraded at precision boundaries (log-number systems, posits, Mythic's analog flameouts). The consequence is OpenAI-sized; the secret is fogged the way I tolerate when consequence is large enough.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) is the LOW rung of capital-adequacy failure: a science bet where the capital required to clear the next miracle was systematically underestimated against incumbent peers. Etched has raised ~$1B at a $5B valuation for transformer-inference-only; MatX has raised $600M+; Taalas $219M; Cerebras pulled in $1B in 2026 alone. Baud at YC pre-seed is two engineers and an unfilled "Founding ASIC Architect / RTL & FPGA Lead" role staring across a $200M-to-$1B funding-gap chasm to a competitive tape-out. The Range Fuels failure mechanism — running out of capital between miracles — is exactly the pattern Baud must avoid, and the dossier shows no signaled lead investor, no announced Series A bridge, and no government-loan analog to the $75M Mississippi instrument that bought Range Fuels its final two years.

On Technical/Capital Risk that repels consensus VCs (the mechanism analysis): the multiplication-elimination thesis compounds over 18 months in one of two directions. If the FPGA demo extrapolates and a 1B-parameter run holds the efficiency curve, Baud becomes the only credible non-hyperscaler training alternative and the Series A prices itself; if precision collapses past 100M parameters or compiler coverage stalls below 70% of common PyTorch ops, the company is functionally an acqui-hire by month 24. There is no soft middle. CUDA ecosystem lock-in — what killed Graphcore, what is squeezing Cerebras into HPC niches — is the asymptote Baud's PyTorch-plus-FSDP2 compiler claim is meant to dissolve, and it is unbenchmarked.

On Founder-Defies-Expert (counterfactual): the score flips a tier if Baud surfaces a public artifact — a Zambare or Taylor paper, a preprint, a conference talk — where one of them stated publicly that multiplications were eliminable before founding the company. The McKinsey 1986 cellphone forecast (<1M by 2000, actual 109M) is my reminder that experts extrapolate; what I look for is the founder who publicly broke from their field before the company existed. Baud's dossier has 4+2 patents and a publication but no public contrarian artifact, so the dimension sits below default.

On Learning Rate (single-anchor parallel): **Slash** (2024) earned my $100M Series C precisely because the Kanye-pivot story was a documented thesis update. Baud has no analog. Zambare's path from Caper to Peloton to Baud is linear ML-leadership progression; Taylor's is linear ASIC architecture. Neither has a public "we were wrong about X" moment. This is a soft negative I score below the default because the absence is visible rather than ambiguous.

On Required-Capital fit, Baud is the cleanest match in the dossier. The peer set raising $200M-$1B establishes that I am exactly the kind of investor whose check size — concentrated, capital-deep, science-tolerant — is what the roadmap requires; this is the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) shape, not the **DoorDash** (2013) consumer-logistics exception.

Forecast, 36 months: Baud raises a $40-80M Series A from a strategic (AMD or a sovereign fund), tapes out a first-generation chip that demonstrates 8-15x efficiency rather than the claimed 100x, fails to clear CUDA's compiler-and-kernel ecosystem moat for external customers, and is acquired by AMD or Broadcom for $300-600M as a team-and-IP tuck-in by month 30. The mechanism: arithmetic-miracle bets historically degrade at the precision boundary, and the compiler surface area required to win paying frontier customers is a multi-year build the founding three cannot ship before capital runs out.

The bull case that stings: Etched validated a $5B valuation on a transformer-only inference chip with comparable team scale at comparable stage, and Baud targets the larger training prize with a more general PyTorch story. If I am wrong about the precision-degradation prior — if "lossless" actually means lossless rather than "lossless within a narrow numerical regime" — then Baud is the OpenAI-tier consequence bet of the 2026 batch and I miss it for credential-pattern-matching reasons that contradict my own TED Talk. The dossier evidence that wins the bear case anyway: no third-party benchmark, no named pilot customer, no announced lead investor at a stage where Etched had already published architecture details. The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict to a check: a public benchmark from a named third party (a hyperscaler research team or a top-3 AI lab) reproducing >20x efficiency on a >1B-parameter training run. Until then this is a watch-list Invest at the low end of the tier, not a wire-the-money decision.
