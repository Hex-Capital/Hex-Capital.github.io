# Incandor -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Incandor is a Stanford-CS enterprise security API selling behavioral biometrics into banks against BioCatch ($1.3B Permira sale, 60+ patents) and Feedzai ($2B October 2025), and the analytical signature is the worst possible pairing for how I underwrite: the highest dimension is "Round/category" only because the dossier is silent enough that I have to fall back to a default, and the lowest is "Founder-as-brand," which is a hard zero — Matthew Yekell and Luc Rosenzweig have no Twitter, no public writing, no community, and a single GitHub repo between them. That pairing describes a competent B2B SaaS team running straight into a category with $586M of incumbent war chests, and there is nothing in the regatta-tack picture that tells me where my edge comes from.

**Sublime Security** is the right HIGH-rung comparison on the round-pricing axis, because that was an enterprise security seed I bought into before the consolidation wave priced it in — the mechanism was getting underneath the "SOC SaaS" consensus while incumbents were still busy defending detection-rule moats. Incandor superficially shares the shape (enterprise security, API product, technical founders), but the dossier diverges from Sublime where it matters most: behavioral biometrics in 2026 is not pre-consensus, it is post-consolidation — BioCatch already exited to Permira at $1.3B in May 2024 and Mastercard already owns NuData. Incandor is not entering before the wave; it is entering after the wave already broke and the buyers already wrote checks to the winners. The dossier tells me nothing about the round size or price, and silence on round dynamics is itself the signal I punish — David Lieb as YC partner plus the "Security, AI" double-tag is the recipe for a $25-35M post-money SAFE I cannot make money at.

**Zero public writing, zero owned distribution, zero pre-existing community** — that is the failure pattern, and Incandor exhibits it cleanly. The LOW rung on the founder-as-brand axis is the canonical sanitized YC team where the only public artifact is a launch video on the YC page, and Incandor literally fits that template: Matthew Yekell's LinkedIn follower count is not even retrievable from search; Luc Rosenzweig has 307 connections and one GitHub repo named after his last name. Neither founder has published a single opinionated take on why label-free behavioral biometrics beats supervised models. This is the exact opposite of why I cut the John Fish / Bookshelved check in January 2024 — there is no pre-existing audience the venture instrument is wrapping around, so the cap table is just a cap table, and the company has to buy every single bank logo through cold outbound against incumbents whose sales teams have been inside JPM for a decade.

**Capital structure compounds against Incandor over the next 18 months**, not for it. The mechanism: behavioral biometrics enterprise sales into regulated banking buyers means SOC 2, SR 11-7 model risk review, biometric-data DPA negotiation under GDPR Art. 9 and BIPA — Incandor has disclosed none of these, and each one is a six-month tack that burns cash without revenue. With BioCatch's $239M raised and Feedzai's $347M sitting opposite, Incandor needs five-plus financing rounds to reach competitive sales coverage, which is exactly the "5+ rounds to default-alive" red flag from my own framework. The instrument on offer is a standard SAFE for a standard SaaS API, and I cannot structure my way around that — there is no royalty, no employee-equity, no holdco angle here.

**Asymmetric conviction would have to look like Matthew Yekell publishing a 5,000-word essay on why supervised fraud labels are structurally broken**, with a benchmark dataset and a public response from one of the BioCatch research leads — that is the counterfactual that flips Incandor from default-6/20 to something I would actually pay attention to. Today the dossier offers the >99% account-takeover accuracy claim with no published benchmark, no third-party validation, no customer case study. The Cboe and Vista Equity Partners background on Yekell tells me he understands the regulated-financial buyer; it does not tell me he has been obsessed with this problem for three years. Without the public artifact of conviction, the prior is that Incandor is responding to a market opportunity (GenAI bot traffic, mule fraud) rather than pursuing a personal mission, and that prior is what the dossier confirms.

**The category sleeve is the one place Incandor gets partial credit** — the parallel here is Sublime Security, where I was willing to underwrite a security seed because security is a sleeve I have conviction on even when it is software-shaped. But the "Security, AI" double-tag on the YC page is doing work against Incandor: AI is the sleeve I am explicitly re-weighting away from ("AI is just more internet," Lonsdale podcast #113), and behavioral biometrics is closer to the supervised-ML-as-product category than to the crypto / DTC / creator sleeves where my edge lives. Mordor's $3.45B 2026 TAM at 26.95% CAGR is real, but the TAM is already being captured by the incumbents the dossier names.

**My 36-month forecast for Incandor**: lands 2-4 mid-market fintech logos at $150-300K ACV reaching $750K-$2M ARR, raises a bridge or small Series A at $40-60M post in 2027, then either gets acqui-hired by Feedzai or Sift for under $40M in 2028 or stalls as NuData/Mastercard bundle a comparable unsupervised approach into existing card-rails contracts. The mechanism is straightforward: Stanford-CS-plus-NVIDIA-systems-chops gets Incandor a technically credible product, but no owned distribution against incumbent sales armies means the revenue curve plateaus before it justifies a real Series A, and the natural exit is a tuck-in to a behavioral-biometrics platform that already cleared bank procurement.

**The strongest bull case against my pass**: Luc Rosenzweig's NVIDIA-Apple-Epic Games systems background is genuinely the right pedigree for low-level mouse / keystroke / touch telemetry capture, the "no labels required, works from session one" claim is technically the right answer to GenAI-era fraud where historical labels are stale within months, and the **Ro** Known Exception applies — regulatory-heavy buyers (banking compliance is the analog to 50-state prescription rails) can constitute a genuine venture moment where capital intensity is the moat, not the failure mode. The bull case stings because if Incandor lands one tier-1 bank lighthouse in the next 12 months, the dossier flips fast. I dismantle it with the price-discipline filter: even granting the bull mechanism, a YC Spring 2026 Security-AI seed will price at consensus, and consensus pricing on a $586M-incumbent category is where I structurally cannot generate alpha — the Ro exception held because Slow entered when the category was illegible, and Incandor's category is not illegible, it is post-Permira. **The verifiable condition that flips my verdict**: Incandor closes a named top-10 US bank or top-5 neobank as a paying customer at >$400K ACV within 12 months, disclosed publicly, before the priced Series A — then the regatta tacks are underwritable and I will revisit at the A.
