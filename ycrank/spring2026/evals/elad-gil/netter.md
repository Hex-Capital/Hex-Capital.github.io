# Netter -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Netter is the inverse of the **Harvey** (2023) signature: a real catalyst sitting underneath the product (LLM natural-language interfaces crossing the usability threshold in 2024–25), zero revenue curve disclosed, and a logo wall (Carrefour FR, Tesco UK, Auchan ES) that the dossier itself flags as unverified. Samuel Ladroue and Amin Hamzaoui are pitching "Data Science Autopilot for the real economy" with 120+ connectors and an ontology layer — the architecture is reasonable, the dimensional signature is not. This is an AI company without a disclosed growth curve, which per Tim Ferriss Show #863 (April 2026) means "something's fundamentally broken" or, more charitably, hasn't been built yet.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing comparison on market re-opening — not because Netter resembles defense, but because Anduril's catalyst was *named and dated* (Pentagon software inadequacy revealed by Ukraine in a 24-month window), and Netter's catalyst is only inferable. The dossier explicitly tags "Why now" as "[Inferred]: LLM-driven natural-language interfaces crossed a usability threshold in 2024–2025… not stated by company." That gap matters: Anduril's founders walked in articulating the re-opening; Netter's homepage pitches "build with your data, not your engineers" against Palantir Foundry and Databricks without naming what changed in the last 24 months that makes mid-market ontology newly buildable. The catalyst exists — chat-to-pipeline genuinely became feasible post-GPT-4 — but Netter has not made it the spine of the pitch, which tells me Ladroue is selling tooling rather than skating to where the puck moved.

The **Lyft** Series C failure mode was different from Netter's, but the underlying mechanism — entering a category where the dossier shows no path to share inversion against named incumbents — rhymes. Netter's competitor list reads like a wall: Snowflake, Databricks, Microsoft Fabric, DataWalk, Alteryx, Palantir Foundry, all of whom are shipping LLM-driven "chat with your data" features per the IBTimes 2026 ranking. Netter's revenue velocity disclosure is the tell: no pricing page on netter.ai, no MoM growth, no contract values for Carrefour/Tesco/Auchan, no ARR figure, and a "$12M Seed" StartupHub.ai datum that contradicts the standard YC Spring 2026 $500K deal and is uncorroborated by Crunchbase or TechCrunch. **Harvey** (2023) went $3B→$5B→$8B in months *with the numbers in the open*; Netter has logos in the open and numbers nowhere, which in the AI cycle is itself the negative signal.

On founder shipping rhythm, the counterfactual is sharp: what would Netter need to show to flip Ladroue and Hamzaoui from a 6/20 to a 14/20? A GitHub history with a shipped open-source connector library, a prior side project with users, or even a Twitter account demonstrating the **Perplexity** (2022) pattern — Aravind pinging me on LinkedIn with a built thing a week later. The dossier shows none of this: "No public account found" on Twitter for both founders, "No public repos found" on GitHub for both, and Hamzaoui is listed on LinkedIn as a *student* at Mines Paris and ENS Ulm. Mines Paris quantitative training is a credential, not a shipping artifact, and at pre-seed shipping is the only legible signal.

The index-position mechanism is where Netter could compound or fail over 18 months. The bullish reading: per-customer ontologies accumulate, connector library deepens past 120, and Netter becomes the operational-data workflow layer mid-market operators integrate against — the way **Stripe** (2010) became the layer e-commerce entrants didn't think about. The bearish reading, which I weight higher: Snowflake Cortex, Databricks Mosaic, and Microsoft Fabric Copilot are all shipping the "chat with your data" primitive directly into the warehouses where the data already lives, and Netter's wedge — mid-market accounts too small for Palantir's FDE motion — gets absorbed before ontology switching costs materialize. Netter's index position is structurally weaker than Decagon's in customer support because Decagon sits in a workflow seam the warehouses don't occupy; Netter sits *on top of* the warehouses, which is the worst place to be when the warehouses get smarter.

**Color Genomics** (co-founded 2015) is the single-anchor parallel on single-miracle architecture, and Netter fails the comparison in a specific way. Color was workflow-on-top-of-commodity-sequencing — one miracle (cheap sequencing) was already validated, and Color's job was GTM and product execution on top. Netter needs three things to be simultaneously true: LLM-generated pipelines reliable enough for enterprise SaaS/ERP/IoT (unproven, no benchmarks cited), 120+ connector depth that outpaces hyperscaler-native equivalents, and ontology accumulation creating switching costs faster than Fabric/Cortex can ship comparable features. That's a multi-miracle dependency chain dressed as workflow infrastructure, which is exactly the architecture Color was deliberately built *not* to be.

Forecast, 36 months: Netter either does a sub-$50M connector-library acqui-hire to Astera, Fabric, or a mid-market ERP rollup, or pivots into a European retail-ops vertical wedge given the Carrefour/Tesco/Auchan footprint Ladroue already has — the math says one of those two, not a standalone breakout. The mechanism producing this: the catalyst is real but Netter hasn't claimed it, the revenue curve is absent in the cycle that punishes absence, and the index position sits underneath warehouse vendors who are shipping the same primitive natively.

The strongest bull case — and it stings — is that Carrefour FR, Tesco UK, and Auchan ES are *actually* paying production deployments, in which case Netter has done what neither Snowflake nor Palantir Foundry can do at mid-market and the $12M "Seed" figure on StartupHub.ai reflects a real lead I haven't traced. European retail operations data is fragmented enough (legacy ERPs, IoT shelf sensors, supplier EDI) that a connector-plus-ontology layer could be genuinely sticky there, and Ladroue/Hamzaoui's Mines Paris network plausibly explains the logo pattern. The dossier dismantles this for me: the StartupHub.ai $12M is "not corroborated by Crunchbase, TechCrunch, or a company announcement," the logos have "no press release, case study URL, or contract value publicly cited," and the dossier is explicit that "logo-on-site does not confirm paid production deployments." One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a named Carrefour or Tesco contract with disclosed ARR above $500K, which would convert Netter from an unverified-logo-wall company into the European mid-market wedge play that justifies the index argument. Until then, this is the **Lyft** Series C pattern in data infrastructure — a credible team in a category where the dossier shows no path to share inversion.
