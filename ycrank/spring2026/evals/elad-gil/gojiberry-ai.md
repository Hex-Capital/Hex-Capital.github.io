# Gojiberry AI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Pierre-Eliott Lallemant's Gojiberry AI is a real-velocity company sitting in a category where Clay just printed a $3.1B valuation on $100M ARR -- which makes Gojiberry AI's analytical signature the inverse of the **Harvey** (2023) template I keep citing: the curve is "just working" but the index position is *against* declared leaders, not alongside them. $112K MRR with 44% MoM growth in nine months is not a false signal; it's the velocity I pay up for. The problem is that I'm being asked to pay up for Gojiberry AI in the outbound-sales lane where the catalyst (LLM-quality personalization) has already been priced into Clay, Apollo, Amplemarket, Regie.ai, and Lemlist simultaneously.

**Harvey** (2023) is the right HIGH-rung comparison for Gojiberry AI on revenue velocity because the mechanism is identical: an LLM step-function unlocked a workflow that prior NLP couldn't touch, and dollars compounded faster than the consensus could re-price. Gojiberry AI's $0 to $1.4M ARR in nine months, with a third Product Hunt launch on Mar 2, 2026 and 1,000+ paying customers, is the kind of curve that earned **Harvey** the "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" arc (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). Where Gojiberry AI diverges from **Harvey** is the cohort question hiding under the headline -- the dossier flags a pivot from $33K revenue / 100 customers to the current "GTM brain" positioning (Superframeworks; wearefounders.uk, 2026), and the homepage cites "500+ B2B teams" while YC cites "1,000+ paying customers." That gap matters because pivot-era $99/mo Pro-plan adoption can compound fast on novelty and decay equally fast on retention, and nine months is not long enough to see the churn cohort settle. Velocity is real; durability is the open question.

**Lyft** Series C is the low-rung memory I keep against Gojiberry AI's index position -- not because rideshare and AI-SDR are alike, but because both are markets where being #2 against a capitalized #1 with structural distribution advantage compresses your share-of-economics to a fraction. Clay sits at $186M+ raised and $3.1B post; Apollo at $251M raised and 210M+ contacts with intent overlays already shipped. Gojiberry AI's pitch -- "intent-first, not data-first" -- is exactly the kind of feature framing that Clay can ship as a SKU inside its existing data-orchestration platform without channel conflict, and Apollo can bundle into its enterprise tier. Gojiberry AI doesn't sit in the workflow path the way **Stripe** (2010) sat across every commerce transaction; Gojiberry AI sits *beside* Clay and Apollo asking small sales teams to switch. That's a share-fight, not an index position, and my *AI Market Clarity* piece (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) is exactly the lens here -- AI-SDR is one of the categories edging from "open" toward "winners forming."

On the catalyst question, the dossier names LLM-quality crossover plus commoditized LinkedIn-signal access as the 24-month window -- credible, but generic. Compounding over the next 18 months, this catalyst doesn't asymmetrically benefit Gojiberry AI versus Amplemarket ($14.9M ARR) or Regie.ai ($30M Series B at 300% YoY); every entrant got the same Gemini-3-class capability at the same time. That's a rising-tide catalyst, not a re-opening that favors a specific team. Gojiberry AI gets partial credit for naming it, but the catalyst doesn't gate competitors out the way Ukraine gated procurement reform into **Anduril** (2022).

On founder shipping rhythm, Dylan Teixeira built Edusign from I'ÉSEG student years into 600,000+ users, multi-million ARR, and a 2025 exit; Lallemant and Czerny ran CoCo AI to ~€500K ARR on Shopify/WhatsApp and exited in 2025. Three repeat founders with three exits in the same year is closer to **Brex** (2017) operational-CEO discipline than to **Perplexity** (2022) cold-inbound shipping artistry -- nobody on Gojiberry AI is pinging me on LinkedIn with a thing built between meetings (as far as the dossier shows), but they have the legible shipped artifacts that the Hustle Commons summary of my framework requires. Counterfactual that would flip this to top-quartile: a public artifact from Teixeira or Lallemant showing a sub-week iteration loop on Gojiberry AI's optimization engine, ideally with a tweet-deck of self-deployed model swaps. Without that, this stays solidly mid-rung -- competent shippers, not Aravind-tempo shippers.

Single-miracle architecture is Gojiberry AI's cleanest dimension: one validated capability (LLMs personalizing outbound at intent-signal-conditioned moments), remaining work is GTM and product execution. No regulatory miracle, no chemistry, no new consumer behavior. This is the same architectural cleanliness that made **Decagon** (2023) and **Abridge** (2023) checks I wrote -- workflow infrastructure on top of one validated model capability. Gojiberry AI gets near full marks here; the architecture isn't the problem.

My 36-month forecast: Gojiberry AI reaches $6-12M ARR by mid-2028, growth decays to <8% MoM by month 18 as Clay ships native sequencing and Apollo bundles intent-first messaging into its enterprise SKU, and Gojiberry AI is acqui-hired by a tier-two sales platform -- Salesloft, Outreach, or Lemlist -- for $40-100M. The producing mechanism is the dimensional signature: revenue velocity inside a category where the index position belongs to the incumbents who already raised the war chest to defend it. Velocity without index position is the **Lyft** Series C shape, not the **Stripe** (2010) shape.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that AI-SDR is exactly the kind of "wide open" sub-market my own *AI Market Clarity* piece (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) flags as not yet winner-declared -- Clay is data-orchestration, Apollo is database, neither has shipped a credible end-to-end AI-SDR product, and Gojiberry AI's 44% MoM is the **Harvey**-template "just working" signal arriving exactly when the category needs a workflow consolidator. The bear-killer would be Gojiberry AI compounding through one full retention cohort at >85% net dollar retention while sustaining >25% MoM at >$500K MRR -- that's the Decagon-shape proof. The dossier doesn't give me that yet; it gives me nine months, a recent pivot, conflicting customer counts, and LinkedIn-platform dependency that has historically been existential for Dux-Soup and Phantombuster. One verifiable condition flips my verdict to invest: published cohort retention >85% across the six months post-pivot, plus a named enterprise logo signed at >$2K/month ACV. Until then, this is a respectable pass.
