# Klarify -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Klarify is a high-leverage, low-specific-knowledge company: four people serving thousands of therapists with permissionless code leverage, but neither Moody Abdul nor Alexander Bergholm has spent a day inside the clinical workflow they're automating. The "5,200 therapists" headline next to a "no public HIPAA/PHIPA/SOC 2 disclosures" line tells you exactly what kind of pre-seed this is — a B2B distribution play wearing a healthcare-AI costume. That pairing has a name in my taxonomy: it's the AI-wrapper archetype with better-than-average distribution.

Specific knowledge is where Klarify is weakest, and it is the dimension I weight most. The high rung here is Perplexity — Aravind Srinivas's years at Google Brain and OpenAI WERE the company; the low rung is the MBA-AI-wrapper archetype I pass on every YC cycle, where the founders identified the opportunity through market research rather than obsession. Klarify sits much closer to the low rung: Abdul's background is LinkedIn enterprise AE and Accenture consulting, Bergholm's is Workday ML/DevOps, and the dossier explicitly notes "neither founder has documented prior clinical/mental-health domain experience." The named clinician testimonials (Pusch, Copeland) and a "Future of Therapy" podcast are consultation, not productized expertise. If Abdul disappeared tomorrow, another competent B2B SaaS operator could ship Klara — that's the test, and Klarify fails it.

Team-to-value leverage is where Klarify is strongest. The high rung is Midjourney at ~$500M revenue with a small team and Cursor under 50 people at $500M ARR; the low rung is any company that needs 50+ hires to reach $10M ARR. Klarify with four people generating 309,121 notes and reaching some bounded subset of 5,200 therapists sits closer to the high rung than the mid — this is exactly the shrinking-company pattern I've been writing about since 2012. The catch: Midjourney's leverage came from proprietary model work; Klarify's leverage comes from OpenAI's API and a reasonably designed workflow on top.

Permissionless leverage architecture: the high rung is Twitter (pure code, hundreds of millions of users at near-zero marginal cost), the low rung is Poshmark (marketplace ops headcount). Klarify is real SaaS code leverage — per-therapist pricing of $16 to $71/month, no field operations — but it is rented leverage on foundation models, which is the same structural risk that killed my AI-wrapper pass example. When GPT-class scribing becomes a feature of every EHR, the "Klara" agent is consumer surplus flowing to OpenAI and to therapists, not to Klarify.

Principal authenticity sits between Sahil Lavingia continuing to build Gumroad solo (high) and the founder who picked the idea because it was fundable (low). Abdul ran CircleBack to $50K ARR and built a 27,000-therapist newsletter before scaling Klarify — that's principal-level distribution work, not pitch-deck theater. But the pattern is "serial founder finds a vertical to apply AI to," not "founder cannot stop solving the problem they live inside." Mid-low.

Access democratization is the weakest of my five but worth checking against AngelList (high — democratized angel investing) and Wish (low — no access expansion). Klarify gives solo and small-group therapists administrative leverage previously available only to larger practices with billing staff — a real but narrow democratization. Mid-low rung; nothing structural.

My 36-month forecast for Klarify: acquired by an incumbent — SimplePractice, Jane, or TherapyNotes — for $25-70M between months 18 and 30, after Upheal's $10M Series A war chest forces a feature race Klarify can't outspend and after one of the legacy EHRs ships a competent AI module that collapses the standalone-scribe category. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: lean team plus rented leverage plus no clinical specific knowledge equals a distribution asset (the 27K newsletter) that's more valuable to an acquirer than to Klarify standalone.

The bull case stings: Klarify's "Future of Therapy" newsletter at 27,000+ therapists is the kind of owned distribution that turned Notion's template-sharing community into a viral compounding engine, and the workflow-breadth strategy (notes plus claims plus supervision plus marketing pages) raises switching costs in a way pure scribes like Mentalyc cannot. If the 5,200-therapist figure is real paying users and the discrepancy with the 2,154 counter resolves in their favor, Klarify is at meaningful early revenue with a four-person team — exactly the shrinking-company profile I systematically back. What dismantles this case: Upheal already raised $14.35M positioning identically as "AI-native EHR for therapists," and the dossier shows no founder-specific knowledge that would let Klarify out-build them on clinical depth or out-integrate them on 837P/EDI claims plumbing. The condition that flips my verdict: Klarify discloses $2M+ ARR with documented HIPAA/PHIPA compliance and signs a clinician co-founder or Chief Clinical Officer with 10+ years in private practice. Without that, this is the AI-wrapper pass with a better newsletter.
