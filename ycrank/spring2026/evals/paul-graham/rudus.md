# Rudus -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Rudus is two ex-big-tech engineers ? Sahil Goel from Amazon and NASA JPL, Rishi Pankhaniya as Airbnb's first new-grad engineer in two years ? who picked concrete-subcontractor takeoffs from the outside. The signature is a schlep-y vertical chosen without a schlep-y origin story. The market repels normal founders, which I like. But neither of them has ever priced a slab pour, and the dossier states plainly that there is "no public evidence of prior concrete-estimating, AEC, or construction-tech experience." That mismatch ? right kind of market, wrong kind of discovery ? is what I have to evaluate.

On organic problem discovery, my low rung is the Pass Example I keep returning to: two ex-consultants who "identified a massive market opportunity" in B2B workflow automation. My high rung is Dropbox ? Drew Houston kept forgetting his USB drive, and the company fell out of that frustration. My mid rung is Stripe, where the Collisons hadn't been burned personally by Visa but had been close enough to payments through their own building to feel the friction. Rudus sits between the Pass Example and the Stripe rung, much closer to the Pass Example. Rishi was at Airbnb and Apple. Sahil was at Northrop Grumman. Neither of them woke up frustrated that they couldn't measure a footing on a plan set; they noticed concrete subcontractors as a vertical. The dossier doesn't even contain a sentence about how they found this. When the origin story is absent from the YC page of two technically strong founders, that absence is itself the data.

On schlep blindness ? the dimension Rudus is highest on ? my high rung is Stripe wading into PCI compliance and bank integrations, my mid rung is ClassDojo selling into school district procurement, my low rung is the consensus B2B SaaS that "sounds great to everyone." Rudus sits between Stripe and ClassDojo, but the dossier pulls it toward ClassDojo and not past it: Togal.AI has already raised $17.2M, STACK is doing $31.8M of revenue, and there is a recognizable list of AI-takeoff entrants (Beam, Handoff, Articulate, Boon). The schlep is real ? counting columns on a structural drawing for a concrete sub in Bakersfield is the kind of work no Stanford CS grad fantasizes about. But the consensus that this is a fundable AI category has already formed, which means Rudus is not in the "seems like a bad idea" quadrant of the Thiel diagram. They're in the "everyone agrees AI takeoff is a thing, fight over the wedge" quadrant.

On founder resourcefulness, my high rung is the Airbnb cereal-box story, my mid rung is Patrick Collison at Auctomatic shipping a real product as a teenager, my low rung is the polished consultants of my Pass Example. Rudus has no story in the dossier on either side ? no scrappy anecdote, no $40 cereal moment, no weekend-prototype tale. Rishi's Calendly link is the entire public sales motion. I default toward the middle but a notch below, because resourceful founders generate notable anecdotes and these two have not. On founder learning rate ? high rung Sam Altman at 19 at Loopt, mid rung the AppJet team building EtherPad during YC, low rung founders flat for a year ? Rudus is invisible. No iteration history, no weekly-growth signal, no pivot story. I have to default. On earnest builder identity ? high rung the Watsi founders, mid rung Amjad at Replit, low rung valuation-chasers ? Rudus shows neither obsession nor mercenary-ness. The website's "70% time reduction, 3-5? bids, 50% revenue per estimator" copy is the kind of marketing language I distrust when there are no named customers behind it.

My 36-month forecast for Rudus: they reach $400K-$1.2M ARR with a small base of concrete subcontractors won through Rishi's outbound, then either Togal.AI adds concrete-specific element detection and erases the wedge, or HCSS acquires Rudus for low single-digit millions as a feature for HeavyBid. The mechanism is straightforward: when two strong engineers without domain pedigree pick a vertical that already has a $17.2M-funded generalist, the generalist's distribution beats the vertical's schema before the vertical's schema becomes a moat.

The strongest case against me is ClassDojo. ClassDojo violated my "hackers building for hackers" pattern and won an unsexy bureaucratic market through pure execution, and I invested. Rudus could be the same shape ? two formidable engineers grinding HCSS HeavyBid integration depth that Togal.AI's generalist roadmap won't prioritize, until concrete subs won't bid without them. Rishi being Airbnb's first new-grad engineer in two years is not nothing; that is a real filter. But ClassDojo's founders were embedded in the education problem in a way that the dossier gives me zero evidence Sahil and Rishi are embedded in concrete. The Pass Example is the closer analogy than the ClassDojo Known Exception. I would flip to invest if I saw one named concrete subcontractor ? a real GC with a real bid calendar ? telling me on a call that Rudus saved them a bid they would have lost. That single artifact would tell me they're doing things that don't scale and that the discovery, however external it began, has become real.
