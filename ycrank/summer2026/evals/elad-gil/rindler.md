# Rindler -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Rindler is a catalyst-timed pre-seed with no curve — Michael Serrano and Arthur De Los Santos are wedging into the MCP standardization window (Anthropic/OpenAI/Google/Microsoft 2024–25) with zero disclosed revenue, no named customers, and three funded incumbents already sitting on the same distribution surface. That pairing — real market re-opening, silent velocity — is the analytical signature that decides this evaluation. Everything else is downstream.

The **Anduril** (2022) comparison is the load-bearing one for market catalyst, and it's the reason Rindler doesn't get a reflexive pass. Anduril worked because Ukraine opened a 24-month window in a market peers had written off; the equivalent window for Rindler is MCP going from an Anthropic proposal to a cross-hyperscaler standard between late 2024 and 2026, plus YC S26 explicitly running a "Software for Agents" thesis (Forbes, Feb 2026). That is a specific, dateable re-opening — not a TAM story. Where Rindler diverges from the Anduril template is founder-market fit against the catalyst: Trae Stephens and Palmer Luckey had shipped Palantir and Oculus before the window opened, so distribution execution was pre-underwritten. Rindler brings two MIT CSAIL grads from the class of 2025 to a wedge (auth, 2FA, bot-detection, per-site schema maintenance) where **Browserbase** (1,000+ customers, 20K developers per Built In SF, Jun 2025) and **Composio** ($1M+ ARR, PRNewswire Jul 2025) already have paying developer distribution. The catalyst is real; the "uniquely positioned for the catalyst's window" half is not proven.

The lowest rung on my revenue-velocity ladder is the "AI company with no curve disclosed" pattern I called out on Tim Ferriss #863 — "if you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." Rindler's dossier is silent: no pricing page, no logos, no waitlist, no Product Hunt, no press. In the AI cycle, silence is a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose — **Harvey** (2023) went "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" because the numbers wanted to be public. Rindler could plausibly be pre-launch and clean-slate, but the middleware-for-agents category has enough oxygen (Browserbase's $40M Series B at $300M in Jun 2025) that a working product would already have design-partner logos. I'm applying the default here, not punishing pre-seed norms.

Founder-ships-between-meetings is the dimension where I want to give Rindler benefit of the doubt and can't. Mechanism-wise, my **Perplexity** (2022) filter compounds over 18 months: a founder who ships a real artifact every week between conversations converts each investor meeting into a demo, which converts every design partner into a repeat user, which converts into the growth curve I want to see by Series A. Rindler's dossier gives me MIT CSAIL credentials, a Telexistence robotics internship for De Los Santos, and Serrano's stated interest in "infrastructure of the future web" — but no GitHub repos, no prior shipped products, no side projects named. That is pedigree, not shipping rhythm. It doesn't disqualify — Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn cold — but the dossier gives me no evidence to override the default.

Index position is a counterfactual question: what would have to be true for Rindler to become the workflow path every agent touches when hitting an API-less site? It would require Rindler's per-site schema library to become the de facto registry that Browserbase's Stagehand (MIT-licensed, on GitHub) and Composio's 1,000+ toolkits either integrate against or fail to reach. That's plausible in exactly one world — where Rindler ships coverage fast enough to make the maintenance treadmill (which YC's own description promises: "robust maintenance when sites change") an operating moat rather than a two-person tax. Without a coverage number or an integration count in the dossier, Rindler is currently the #3 or #4 entrant in a category where **Browserbase** ($66M+ raised) and **Composio** ($79.6M raised) already sit closer to the index position. The score can flip a tier if Rindler discloses >500 maintained sites with paying agent traffic.

Single miracle is the dimension where Rindler is cleanest — comparable to **Decagon** (2023) on this axis alone. Decagon assumed "LLMs are good enough to handle enterprise support tickets" and the rest was GTM. Rindler assumes "LLM-driven browser actions plus OAuth 2.0 PKCE session handling are good enough to make an arbitrary site behave like an API" and the rest is GTM plus per-site maintenance. That's one validated capability, not a chain. The residual work is execution, not physics. Rindler gets credit here.

My 36-month forecast: Rindler either signs an acqui-hire with **Browserbase** or **Composio** at sub-$50M for the schema library and the two engineers, or it's dead — I do not see the independent path to $10M ARR against those two incumbents' distribution unless the growth curve appears in the next 90 days. That prediction is the direct output of the signature — catalyst real, velocity absent, incumbents funded — and if Rindler is instead at $2M ARR in eighteen months with named enterprise logos, my velocity dimension was wrong, not my catalyst read.

The bull case that stings: MCP standardization is exactly the "market peers misread" pattern I ran on **Stripe** (2010) and **Coinbase** (2013), and every incumbent in Rindler's competitor set is optimized for a *different* wedge — Browserbase sells browser infrastructure and a scripting SDK, Composio wraps API-having services, Anon handles auth-only. Rindler's "MCP-native middleware for sites *without* APIs" is arguably the one unclaimed workflow slot in the taxonomy, and two MIT CSAIL grads with no prior baggage is exactly the Perplexity founder-inbound profile I've paid up for before. That case sits inside my **Decagon** template on paper. The dossier evidence that wins for me anyway: three funded competitors can extend into this surface faster than Rindler can extend into theirs, and my *AI Market Clarity* discipline says winner-declared categories don't reward the #3 entrant even when the wedge language sounds contrarian. Verifiable condition to flip me to invest: Rindler discloses >$50K MRR from at least two named enterprise design partners within 90 days, and Serrano or De Los Santos publishes shipping cadence I can inspect. Absent that, this is a Neutral I'd re-underwrite at seed, not one I'd angel today.
