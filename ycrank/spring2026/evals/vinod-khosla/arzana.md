# Arzana -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Arzana is what my framework calls an "optimization play" wearing industrial clothing -- William Alexander and Marshall Kools have built an LLM overlay that reads RFQs and POs into Epicor and NetSuite, and the success-state at maximum ambition is that back-office clerks at Zeiss and Milltown Paper file 70% fewer keystrokes. That is the signature: a mild outsider-founder profile (NYT, Feb 8, 2026 -- "Dreamers Don't Fit the Stereotype") paired with the lowest possible capital-risk profile in my taxonomy. The pairing tells me exactly what Arzana is -- a competent YC software company in a crowded P26 cohort (Korso, Smartbase, Tiny), not a company whose absence from the world would be civilizationally costly.

**Impossible Foods** (2011) is the HIGH rung on consequence magnitude because the success-state collapsed animal protein production -- a named incumbent regime, a $15T-adjacent variable, a civilizational redirection. Arzana's success-state, by the dossier's own framing, is "10x faster quote/order processing" and "<4 months to go-live" across ERPs that already exist. The shared mechanism would be labor displacement under my Fortune 2026-03-06 "$15 trillion of GDP is labor" thesis -- and Arzana technically rides it -- but the slice is so thin (back-office RFQ clerks at mid-market manufacturers) that no incumbent regime collapses. SAP and NetSuite do not disappear; they absorb the workflow per their 2026 embedded-AI roadmap (gurussolutions.com). Arzana is closer to the GPT-sales-call wrapper I pass on in 30 seconds than to anything in my portfolio.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) is the LOW rung for the opposite reason from what Arzana shows -- Range Fuels failed because the capital-adequacy bar was set against three required miracles and the burn ran out before the plant integrated. Does that pattern appear in Arzana? The opposite problem appears. Arzana requires zero scientific miracles -- LLM parsing of heterogeneous PDFs in 2026 is a Tier-1 generalist underwrite. The "99.9% accuracy" claim on arzana.com is unverified, but even if it falls to 95%, the failure mode is customer churn, not catalyst yield collapse. A roadmap that gets to $1M ARR on $5-10M of seed capital is not a deployment scale that no consensus VC would touch -- it is the deployment scale every Sand Hill SaaS investor is built to write. I am over-resourced for Arzana, which means my edge does not apply.

Marshall Kools is a Stanford MS Engineering+Policy graduate and D1 wrestler; William Alexander grew up around an Okoboji, Iowa manufacturing sourcing firm. Per NYT (Feb 2026), neither founder had a prior tech background -- a soft positive against my TED Talk skepticism of "20 years at incumbent, applying same playbook." Arzana is not the **Physical Intelligence** (2024) research-PI shape, and it is not the **Slash** (2024) documented-pivot shape either, but the founder profile here is at least adjacent to the outsider pattern I value. The mechanism over 18 months: Alexander's Iowa-sourcing childhood gives Arzana a real founder-market fit edge in the SMB-manufacturer GTM motion, but founder-market fit on a software overlay compounds linearly into ARR, not exponentially into category collapse. This dimension does not lift Arzana out of the optimization-play bucket.

What would have to be true for Arzana's learning-rate score to flip a tier? Alexander or Kools would need a documented prior pivot -- a public thesis update, an earlier company sold, or a stated "we were wrong about X" moment of the kind that justified my $100M Series C into **Slash** (2024). The dossier shows neither founder had prior companies, and Arzana was co-founded in 2025 with no internal pivot disclosed. I am applying my mid-default of 7/15 here -- not punitive, because short company history is not founder rigidity, but Arzana gets no upside on this axis either.

Compare Arzana's capital profile to **Cognition** (2024), where my Series B participation came in at a $175M round because AI software engineering required foundation-model training depth that consensus VCs were not built to underwrite. Arzana's deployment plan -- founders paying themselves $10K/year (NYT, Feb 2026), trade-show GTM at MD&M, ERP integration shipped as overlay -- prices out at standard YC seed-to-Series-A economics. That is not a knock on Arzana; it is a knock on the fit with my check. Any Tier-1 generalist writes this round and adds the same value I would, which by my TechCrunch Disrupt 2013 framing means I add negative value relative to a specialist SaaS lead.

In 36 months, Arzana reaches $3-8M ARR with the Zeiss, Tecton, Koike logo wall converting into mid-six-figure ACVs, and is acquired by Epicor, Infor, or NetSuite -- or absorbed via roll-up with Korso or Smartbase as the YC P26 cohort consolidates. The mechanism: Arzana's overlay workflow gets either commoditized by SAP S/4HANA and NetSuite embedded AI (gurussolutions.com 2026) or eaten by a manufacturing-ERP incumbent that needs the front-office AI bolt-on faster than they can build it. Arzana is not dead in the **KiOR** sense; it is the modest-success outcome that my framework explicitly does not optimize for.

The bull case stings here -- a NYT feature at pre-seed plus a Zeiss logo on the wall is genuinely unusual signal density, and Tom Blomfield as YC partner is the same partner who shepherded several recent breakouts. The strongest version: Arzana rides the same "$15 trillion of labor" wave I cited in Fortune 2026-03-06, manufacturing back-office is a real labor displacement frontier, and the Iowa-sourcing founder-market fit is the kind of edge that lets Arzana out-execute Korso and Smartbase. Does the **DoorDash** (2013) exception apply? No -- DoorDash reshaped consumer-commerce flow nationally; Arzana digitizes RFQ entry at mid-market manufacturers. Does the **Slash** (2024) exception apply? No -- that override required a documented Kanye-pivot demonstrating founder learning rate, and Arzana has executed one linear plan since 2025. Does the **Glean** exception apply? No -- Glean's override required Fortune-500 information-asymmetry consequence at capital scale, and Arzana's overlay does not match that magnitude. The condition that would flip my verdict: Arzana publishes verified $10M+ ARR with named Fortune-100 enterprise contracts within 12 months AND articulates a defensible data moat from accumulated parts-matching corpora that incumbent ERPs structurally cannot replicate. Absent that, this is the pass I make in 30 seconds.
