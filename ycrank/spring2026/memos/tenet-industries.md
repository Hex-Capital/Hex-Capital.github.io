## The Idea

- **Problem:** Western/European militaries and Ukraine-aligned buyers face high per-unit costs for strike drones built from US components, with a comparable drone costing roughly 10,000 SEK (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026).
- **Approach:** Tenet redesigns drones from scratch using design-for-manufacturing, reducing part count from ~30 to 14 and using plastic casting, injection molding, and milling rather than 3D printing for production (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026).
- **Differentiation:** vs. Anduril/Shield AI (higher-cost autonomous systems) and Helsing HX-2 (European strike drone) — Tenet targets ~3,000 SEK per drone, positioning as "the most affordable option in Europe" per CEO Hugo Frisk (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: Unit sales of drones to government/defense customers; no public pricing page found beyond the ~3,000 SEK/unit reference (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** Direct discussions with Swedish defense customers and Ukrainian actors for contracts (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Proprietary HW/SW platform optimized for automated manufacturing across FPV, fixed-wing, shahed-type, and missile categories (YC company page).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Manufacturing cost curve advantage from part-count reduction (30→14) could compound if production volume scales; unproven because no production volume or unit-shipment data is public (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026).
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: European sovereign-supply preference and ITAR/export-restriction friction on US-component drones may create a structural carve-out for European-domiciled producers (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026, notes "domestic European drone" framing).
- **Commoditization risk:** Multiple well-funded competitors (Helsing $695M round, Anduril, Shield AI $3.6B raised) are pursuing mass-producible attritable drones (Fortune, Dec 2025; TechCrunch via search snippet).

- **Competitors:**
  - Helsing (€830M+ raised including $695M June 2024 round led by Prima Materia, revenue unknown): European peer producing HX-2 strike drone at larger scale and broader product surface (Fortune via search snippet).
  - Anduril (>$6B raised, revenue ~$1B reported, Contrary Research): US incumbent with autonomous strike drones at higher price points (Contrary Research via search snippet).
  - Shield AI ($3.6B raised, $12.7B valuation Mar 2026, revenue unknown): VTOL strike drones (V-BAT, X-BAT) — higher-cost, higher-capability platforms (Fortune, Dec 2025).

  - AeroVironment (publicly traded, NASDAQ: AVAV): Switchblade loitering munition is the closest US analog to a kamikaze quadcopter (search snippet).
- **Why now:** Ukraine war demonstrated FPV/kamikaze drone consumption rates that outstrip Western production, and European governments have shifted procurement toward sovereign attritable munitions (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026, frames cost gap vs. US-component drones as the wedge).

## Key Risks

- **Technical feasibility at price target:** Achieving a fielded military-grade kamikaze quadcopter at ~3,000 SEK per unit with 14 parts is unproven at production scale; no shipped-unit volume or third-party performance test data is public (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026).
- **Well-capitalized incumbents in same wedge:** Helsing ($695M round) and Anduril are already pursuing low-cost European/attritable strike drones, with multi-year head starts on defense procurement relationships (Fortune via search snippet; Contrary Research).
- **Procurement/regulatory access:** Sales depend on Swedish, EU, and Ukrainian export licensing and defense procurement cycles; no signed contracts or LOIs are disclosed, only "discussions" (Nordic Defence Sector, Apr 2026).
- **Geographic split:** Operations span Stockholm and San Francisco for a 4-5 person team, which may strain coordination; no mitigation disclosed (tenetindustries.com; YC company page).
- **Dual-use export risk:** Strike drones face ITAR-like Swedish/EU dual-use export controls (ISP licensing) that can delay or block sales to non-EU buyers including Ukraine; no mitigation disclosed in public sources.
