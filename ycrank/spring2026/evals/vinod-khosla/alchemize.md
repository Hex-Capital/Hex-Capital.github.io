# Alchemize -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Alchemize is a domain-naive software wrapper on commodity LLMs aimed at a regulated $5.48B US customs brokerage market growing at 2.88% CAGR (Mordor Intelligence, 2026) -- the analytical signature is a company whose highest-scoring dimension is mere consequence-adjacency to $3T in US import flows, and whose lowest is the absence of any technical or capital risk that would repel a consensus VC. Samuel Fu's LinkedIn headline "Building AI-enabled customs brokerage" with Robert Hou as co-founder is the entire founder thesis I have to work with, and that signature is the **DoorDash** (2013) shape without **DoorDash**'s consumer-flow consequence -- it is the inverse of the bet I want to write.

Consequence Magnitude is Alchemize's highest-relative dimension and it still does not clear my bar. The HIGH rung here is **OpenAI** (2019), where I wrote $50M into a no-revenue, unconventional-structure company because the success-state collapsed cognitive labor as a category -- the literal "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" frame from Fortune (2026-03-06). The MID rung is **DoorDash** (2013), capital-light consumer logistics whose success-state at least redrew the commerce-fulfillment surface and returned a $72B IPO. The LOW rung is the GPT-4-wraps-Salesforce pass I described in my own pass example -- 20-30% efficiency in one workflow. Alchemize sits between LOW and MID: customs touches $3T in US imports annually (techbuzz.ai, 2025), but the success-state of Alchemize at maximum ambition is "human entry preparers replaced by software at the same incumbent brokers' price point" -- margin shift, not regime collapse. No incumbent disappears; Expeditors and C.H. Robinson keep filing entries, just with cheaper labor inside.

Technical or Capital Risk is Alchemize's lowest dimension and the most disqualifying. The HIGH rung is **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where my $115M Series A lead required tokamak physics miracles consensus VCs would not underwrite. The MID rung is **Cognition** (2024), where the AI-engineer thesis at least required proprietary agent architecture above the foundation-model layer. The LOW rung is, again, the SaaS-on-LLM pass case -- standard burn-to-ARR with software-only execution risk fundable by any Tier-1 generalist. Alchemize sits at the LOW rung. Flexport's October 2025 AI Customs Suite already ships an AI auditor at a claimed 0.2% error rate (flexport.com, 2025) and KlearNow.AI is sitting on $69M (Crunchbase, 2026) -- the AI capability layer for HTS classification and entry-summary auto-population is not a Khosla edge; it is a feature shipping into an existing distribution moat I do not own.

Founder Defiance of Expert Consensus is weak -- not because Fu and Hou are domain experts continuing a playbook (the **Glean** exception pattern of Arvind Jain ex-Google search), but because the dossier surfaces no public stake in any controversial position about customs, trade, or compliance. The **Impossible Foods** (2011) HIGH rung had Pat Brown publicly contradicting food-industry orthodoxy on protein structure; the LOW rung is the eight-years-at-Salesforce pattern from my pass example. Alchemize sits closer to LOW: Samuel Fu's Columbia education plus an unconfirmed Netflix-mutuals signal and Robert Hou's unretrievable LinkedIn give me neither expert continuity nor expert defiance -- a third, worse category of "no documented stake at all."

Founder Learning Rate has no evidence -- no prior pivot, no prior exit, no thesis update. The HIGH rung **Slash** (2024) earned my $100M Series C precisely because of the publicly-told Kanye West pivot story; Alchemize gives me nothing analogous, so I apply the absent-evidence default of 6-7/15. Required-Capital Profile is the cleanest miss: a software-light brokerage at YC pre-seed with no plant, no fleet, no foundation-model training run -- the opposite of the **Genesis AI** (2025) $105M co-led seed where capital depth was the deployment thesis. I am over-resourced for Alchemize at any check size that justifies a Khosla seat.

My 36-month forecast on Alchemize: by mid-2029, Alchemize either reaches $2-4M ARR as a niche SMB-importer broker and gets acqui-hired by KlearNow.AI or a Livingston-tier legacy broker for under $40M, or it quietly winds down after Flexport's AI Customs Suite saturates the mid-market importer base that was Alchemize's only realistic wedge. The mechanism producing this is the combination of weak consequence magnitude (no incumbent regime collapses) and zero technical moat (Flexport's 0.2% AI-auditor error rate is the ceiling Alchemize would have to beat with two founders and pre-seed capital).

The strongest opposing case is the **Slash** (2024) exception applied to Alchemize: YC-pedigreed founders in a regulated workflow where the customs broker license itself (sub-15% 2025 exam pass rate per Mordor Intelligence 2026) is a real gating function, and where Power-of-Attorney filings create transactional lock-in that compounds. The bull mechanism is that Alchemize wins the licensed-broker scarcity arbitrage, locks importers into PoA relationships before Flexport's enterprise sales motion reaches them, and rides a global customs market multiple times the US $5.48B figure into a $1-3B outcome. The dossier evidence that defeats this case: Slash's exception clause was a *documented* founder pivot demonstrating learning rate strong enough to override weak consequence magnitude; Alchemize provides neither pivot evidence nor a Glean-scale consequence to invoke either Known Exception. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Alchemize signs a Fortune-500 importer to a $5M+ ARR multi-year PoA commitment within 18 months AND adds a co-founder or first hire with prior customs-broker leadership at scale -- absent both, this is the GPT-4-summarizes-sales-calls pass with a regulatory wrapper.
