# PLAN0 AI -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Arash Barati's $7B of CRE PE, lending, and development experience is the load-bearing fact in this dossier, and it makes PLAN0 AI a domain-insider-led enterprise SaaS attacking real underwriting pain through a "Book a Demo" funnel — the highest dimension here is specific knowledge, the lowest is permissionless leverage, and that pairing is the inverse of the shape I write checks on. PLAN0 AI is a competently-built B2B tool for developers, lenders, and GCs; it is not a code-or-media-leverage business compounding without gatekeepers.

The leverage check fails first. **Twitter** (2007) was the canonical permissionless-media bet — anyone could post, marginal cost of replication was zero, and the platform charged nothing — and PLAN0 AI runs the opposite architecture. The plan0.ai site has no pricing, no self-serve sign-up, no open API, no free tier, no community — only a "Book a Demo" CTA gated by founder-network enterprise sales into a CRE/lending buyer set Arash Barati already knows from his $1.5B PE / $4.5B lending / $1.2B development past. That is labor-and-capital leverage requiring permission from procurement officers at developers, GCs, and lenders, exactly the thing nav.al/product-media was written to flag. The 2-week-to-30-minute speedup is real and useful, but the distribution that captures it is permissioned at every node.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the rung I want to test PLAN0 AI against on the network dimension, because Perplexity violated the marketplace-compounding criterion and I funded it anyway — the override was extreme specific knowledge plus permissionless API distribution plus pre-consensus timing, three gates clearing emphatically. PLAN0 AI has the specific knowledge leg but not the other two. The "$20B of projects already running through the platform" line gestures at a data network effect — more projects, richer localized cost benchmarks — but the dossier is silent on whether Togal, Kreo, or STACK could simply license RSMeans or Gordian cost feeds to neutralize the moat. A data flywheel inside a single-sided B2B SaaS is not the same architecture as user-to-user compounding, and PLAN0 AI's $20B claim is not yet a moat — it is a marketing assertion with no named partnership counterparties.

Specific knowledge is where PLAN0 AI scores genuinely well, and it is the only reason this evaluation is not a Strong Pass. Arash Barati lived the pain — Columbia MSRED, adjunct teaching, $7B of underwriting and development cycles — which is the **Stack Overflow** (2008) Atwood-and-Spolsky pattern of practitioners building for practitioners, even if the practitioners here are CRE underwriters rather than developers. Shervin Barati shipping AirPods hearing-health features at Apple is a credible CTO credential; Dimitris Pagonakis ex-Citadel and MIT clears the energy-and-intelligence legs of the Buffett trio cleanly. PLAN0 AI is not "ex-FAANG PM identified market opportunity in $X TAM" — the founders did not respond to a competitor's success, they responded to a problem Arash Barati personally underwrote against for fourteen years.

The market-pivot dimension is where PLAN0 AI's score would flip a tier if one fact changed: if the dossier showed PLAN0 AI's cost-database layer being consumed by architects via a self-serve API or by lenders as a stand-alone risk-analytics product, the pivot vectors would expand from "construction estimation" into "CRE underwriting infrastructure" — a platform-shaped market that absorbs the "first idea never works" axiom. As written, the dossier names commercial RE, lender stress-testing, and developer pro-forma as adjacent vectors, all inside one vertical. That is real pivot room but bounded, and Togal.AI's $17.2M war chest at 98% floor-plan accuracy is a credible blocker on the contractor side of any pivot.

The founder trio reads clean — no contested exits, no integrity self-promotion, no 50/50 split with unresolved authority (Arash is named CEO). The brother + best-friend co-founder structure I'd normally interrogate, because the **Epinions** (1999-2005) partner litigation calibrated me to founder-relationship fragility, but two brothers plus a long-tenure friend is closer to the **Twitter** Williams-Dorsey shipping pattern than to the smart-crook anti-pattern. I would be willing to spend ten years on a phone call with Arash Barati; I am simply not convinced the business he is building compounds the way the businesses I fund compound.

My 36-month forecast on PLAN0 AI: $4-12M ARR sold into 40-150 mid-market developers, lenders, and GCs, followed by acquisition by Procore, CoStar, or a strategic incumbent at $80-250M — a 3-5x outcome on seed dollars and a structural loss against the 1000x upside math my portfolio requires. The mechanism is the inverse of asymmetric payoff: permissioned distribution caps the upside geometry even when the founder-market fit is real, because the buyer's procurement cycle is the rate-limiter, not the product's marginal cost of replication.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Notion** (2013) parallel — Ivan Zhao spent years rebuilding personal productivity tools before the seed, just as Arash Barati spent fourteen years underwriting CRE before PLAN0 AI, and Notion looked B2B-ish at the seed before the bottoms-up creator wedge appeared. The bull says PLAN0 AI could ship a free architect-facing takeoff tool that pulls plans organically into the cost-database, converting capital-leverage GTM into code-leverage distribution. The dossier evidence that wins against this: plan0.ai (2026) has no free tier, no API, no developer surface, no public-facing artifact — only the demo gate. Until PLAN0 AI ships a self-serve free product that drives unsupervised adoption by architects or independent GCs and shows organic week-over-week growth on a public signup metric, the leverage architecture stays permissioned and I stay out. That is the single verifiable condition that would flip my verdict.
