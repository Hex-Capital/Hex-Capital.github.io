# Maquoketa Research -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Maquoketa Research is the inverse of the pattern I keep writing checks against: a real schlep with no animal. Alejandro Hernandez did mmWave phased-array work at the Berkeley Wireless Research Center, which is genuine RF lineage, and the category -- vertically integrated one-way attack drones for DoD procurement -- is exactly the kind of unpleasant, ITAR-saturated, hardware-heavy grind other YC applicants in Spring 2026 are routing around. But nowhere in the Maquoketa Research dossier does anyone hand me a cereal box. No demo video on Hacker News, no flight-test footage, no signed LOI, no revenue, no Replicator selection -- just a "company claim" that autonomous OWA "can quadruple mission success rates" extrapolated from Ukraine. That asymmetry -- high schlep, low animal -- is the whole evaluation.

The schlep ladder is where Maquoketa Research is strongest. **Coinbase** (2012) at the top: Brian Armstrong leaned into bitcoin compliance grind that no other YC applicant in his batch wanted to touch, and the regulatory overhead later became the moat. Maquoketa Research is in the same neighborhood -- ITAR, DoD vendor qualification, in-house RF and mixed-signal and power electronics, US-only manufacturing, plus the Replicator/Drone Dominance bureaucracy at $5,000/unit. That is not Lattice-style software autonomy; that is a 5-person team in Elk Grove Village trying to build the seeker, the airframe, the radio, and the ground software simultaneously. Where Maquoketa Research diverges from the Coinbase rung is that Armstrong personally hand-built an Android wallet to rescue a written rejection -- that was the animal moment that turned the schlep into conviction. The Maquoketa Research dossier names the schlep but does not name the equivalent stunt.

The "Idea Is Organic to Founders' Own Pain" axis is where Maquoketa Research collapses, and the failure mechanism is the one I described in the Pass Example: founders who spotted a market opportunity rather than founders who lived the problem. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT; that is why **Dropbox** (2007) exists. Evan Yeager was a derivatives-trading software engineer at SpiderRock in Chicago before Maquoketa Research -- nothing in that biography suggests he was lying awake at night thinking about Switchblade operator-in-the-loop latency. Hernandez's Berkeley mmWave research is adjacent to the technology but is not the same as having flown a drone in combat or having been the Marine who needed one. Aidan Williams led mechanical engineering teams on high-voltage power -- again adjacent, not lived. David Muchow's bio the YC page itself calls "confusing-to-recruiters," which is not the reassurance Maquoketa Research seems to think it is. The pattern is engineers picking a hot market in Spring 2026, not engineers building the tool they wished existed when they were the user.

On Animal Resourcefulness, the compounding question over 18 months is whether the Maquoketa Research team converts capital into demonstrable flight tests faster than Anduril ($2.5B raised at $30.5B) and Shield AI ($12.7B valuation) convert their existing contract relationships into the same $5,000/unit attritable airframe class. Without a pre-funding artifact in the dossier -- no GitHub trail for any of the four founders, no Twitter/X presence, no published demo -- I have no leading indicator that Maquoketa Research is the kind of team that ships before it pitches. Absence of tracks is itself the signal; animals leave tracks.

On Hacker Genealogy, the single-anchor parallel is **Stripe** (2010): Patrick Collison won the Young Scientist prize for Lisp work in 2005, a public technical artifact predating the company. Hernandez's BWRC publications on 140 GHz MIMO phased arrays are the closest Maquoketa Research analog, and they are real -- RF for drone seekers and datalinks is exactly the right technical depth. But Yeager's GitHub is empty in the dossier, Williams's LinkedIn was not retrieved, and Muchow's background is undocumented. One researcher with domain depth out of four founders is not the Collison-brothers density.

On co-founder configuration, the counterfactual for tier-flip is straightforward: if Maquoketa Research's dossier had named a multi-year prior working relationship between Yeager and Hernandez -- a shared lab at Berkeley, a prior startup, a co-authored paper -- the score moves up. What the dossier gives me instead is "both connected to UC Berkeley EECS" and "no public record of prior shared employer." Four founders with thin documented chemistry is closer to the Red Flag I listed (three-plus founders with no described prior working relationship) than to the Airbnb trio.

My 36-month forecast for Maquoketa Research: sub-$40M acquihire by AeroVironment or a Tier-1 prime contractor (L3Harris, Northrop), or dead after failing to win a Replicator slot in the 2026-2027 selection cycle while burning seed capital against Anduril and Shield AI's existing program-of-record relationships. The mechanism is the high-schlep / low-animal pairing: the schlep is real enough that Maquoketa Research will accumulate ITAR compliance and vertical integration as scar tissue, which makes them acquihire-attractive to a prime that needs the RF talent, but the absence of a pre-funding artifact and the absence of an organic founder-pain narrative is what separates Anduril-pattern (Palmer Luckey + Oculus exit + personal political appetite) from Kiko-pattern.

The strongest bull case for Maquoketa Research is the Stripe-via-Auctomatic exception: that Hernandez's BWRC mmWave depth is the same kind of unfakeable technical lineage that made me bend the YC-process rule for the Collisons, and that the Pentagon Drone Dominance window -- 30,000 OWA drones, $5K/unit, March 2026 swarm crucible -- is the rare moment when a hardware-first team can leapfrog software incumbents. That case stings because RF-for-seekers is genuinely hard-to-replicate and the timing is unusually clean. But Patrick Collison had shipped Auctomatic to a $5M outcome at age 19 before I bent the rule -- a prior closed loop of artifact-to-revenue. Maquoketa Research has shipped no artifact in the dossier, has no named DoD contract, and is not on the publicly reported Replicator selectee list alongside Performance Drone Works and Swarm Aero. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named DoD contract or Replicator program selection within 12 months, OR a public autonomous terminal-guidance flight-test video that crosses Hacker News the way Houston's did. Until one of those exists, Maquoketa Research reads as engineers running at a real schlep without the founder-signal that schlep-running requires. Pass.
