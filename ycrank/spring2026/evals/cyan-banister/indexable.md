# Indexable -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Andrew Gazelka is a credible Rust systems engineer who shipped Hyperion (1k GitHub stars) and did time at xAI on high-performance systems, and Indexable is what happens when that profile points at the most consensus infrastructure category in YC Spring 2026. The pairing is unmistakable: a HIGH-ish read on founder-substrate fit colliding with a near-bottom read on "magically weird." Indexable is a competent systems builder swinging at a lane that already contains Modal at $466M raised, E2B at $32M, and Daytona at $24M ? six well-funded incumbents and the YC darling-of-the-batch energy converging on the same primitive. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game everyone else is playing right here is "who wins sandbox-for-agents." That is not my game.

Anduril is the HIGH rung on founder-lived-experience because Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he ever touched defense ? a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain by a specific prior year. Indexable's Gazelka has the *substrate* of that pattern: Rust at xAI, a 1k-star Minecraft game engine, "#1 r/rust for 24 hours" per his GitHub bio. That is real adjacent-domain shipping. Where Indexable diverges from the Luckey template is the autobiographical contact with the *problem*. Luckey wanted VR because he was the kid soldering headsets in a trailer; Gazelka is building sandbox infra because coding agents need sandboxes ? a market observation, not a lived ache. The dossier gives me no moment where Gazelka watched his own agent corrupt its own state and decided rollback-of-running-memory was the thing he had to build. Without that origin, the founder reads as "the right systems engineer for the job" rather than "the only person who could have started this company."

The HQ Trivia failure mechanism is unresolved co-founder authority producing a feud that hollowed out an explosively viral product ? Yusupov and Kroll could not name a tiebreaker, and the company died on that fault line. Indexable's dossier lists a 3-person team where only Andrew Gazelka is publicly named, with two co-founders unverified on YC, the indexable-inc GitHub org, or the website. That is not the HQ Trivia failure ? it is the silence that *precedes* it. A single named founder reads as "Gazelka is the CEO by default" but I have no operating agreement, no equity split, no documented tiebreaker, and no demonstration that the conversation has even happened. The absence of co-founder names from any public surface, including ix.dev itself, is the conversation I would want to have on the term sheet before I sign.

On "magically weird," Indexable is in the YC batch's most-talked-about infrastructure category alongside Modal's $355M Series C in May 2026 led by General Catalyst, E2B's $21M Series A led by Insight, and Daytona's $24M February 2026 raise. The mechanism by which this dimension fails over 18 months is precisely visible in the dossier: Modal can add a fork primitive with a fraction of its $466M total raised, E2B already serves "roughly half of the Fortune 500" per VentureBeat, and CodeSandbox SDK (now inside Together AI) ships snapshot hibernation today. The technical claim ? millisecond fork of running memory + process + DB state ? is reproducible by any team with virtualization expertise. "Magically weird" requires the category to be ignored or laughed at; Indexable's category is the opposite, where the laughter is the *queue* of funded competitors.

On capital-intensity, the counterfactual flip is this: if Indexable were building bare-metal data-center hardware to host these sandboxes, or going after a regulated workload (HIPAA-bound agent execution, classified-network agent runtime), the dimension would jump a tier. As written, Indexable is software-on-commodity-KVM ? technically deep, but the moat is implementation skill, not capital or regulatory compounding. The dossier gives me no FedRAMP path, no on-prem appliance, no hardware artifact. That is software-only by default, and my rubric down-weights software-only by default because the absence of frontier signal is itself signal.

Flock Safety is the single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability ? I sourced it from a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons caf? because the artifact was in my physical environment. Indexable is the antipode: a B2B sandbox SDK with no Product Hunt listing, no Chrome extension, no app store presence, 11 GitHub stars on the public `index` and `docs` repos. A normal human cannot install Indexable, photograph it, or hand it to a friend within 48 hours. The product's value is legible only through founder testimony and benchmark claims that the dossier itself notes are unverified. This is the dimension where Indexable looks most like the consensus-YC-AI-agent-darling I described in my Pass Example.

My 36-month forecast: Indexable acqui-hires into Modal, E2B, or a frontier-lab platform (xAI being the dark-horse acquirer given Gazelka's prior tenure) for a $15M-$40M talent-and-tech outcome ? Gazelka's hypervisor work survives as a feature inside someone else's stack, not as the default agent sandbox primitive. The mechanism producing this outcome is the dimensional signature itself: a strong systems founder in a consensus category cannot out-distribute Modal's $466M or out-enterprise E2B's Fortune 500 footprint, so the realistic exit is the technology getting absorbed rather than Indexable becoming the standard.

The strongest case against my pass is the DeepMind override: I bought Hassabis despite elite credentials because polymath cross-domain compounding produced an unreplicable founder. Could Gazelka be the systems-polymath equivalent ? Rust + game-engine + xAI + KVM hypervisor ? whose specific stack stitch *is* the moat, the way Hassabis's chess-neuroscience-game-design stitch was AlphaGo's moat? It is a real argument, and the BountyBot acquisition mentioned in the search snippet (unverified) would strengthen it if confirmed. What dismantles the steel-man is the *category*: DeepMind in 2013 had no peers; Indexable in 2026 has at least six funded peers in the dossier and a YC partner (Pete Koomen) who places it inside the consensus infra lane. The polymath override requires category emptiness, and Indexable's category is crowded. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed enterprise design partner (named Fortune 500 or named frontier lab) using Indexable's fork primitive in production on a workload Modal or E2B *cannot* serve today ? the moment that exists, the consensus-category objection collapses and I re-engage.
