# Poth Labs -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Matthew Wong is an ex-Palantir Forward-Deployed Engineer and Mojmir Horvath is a 19-year-old who won the Austrian Federal AI Competition, and what they built together at Poth Labs is an agentic platform that auto-generates hypotheses about why users churn — which is to say, Poth Labs is the cleanest-governance entry I will see this batch attacking the most consensus YC AI-agent lane in 2026, with no lived founder origin into the CX manager's pain and nothing I can touch in the wild. That high/low pairing — adult titles on top of pitch-deck thesis — is Poth Labs's signature, and it is the exact shape of the pass I described in my own profile.

Palmer Luckey shipped VR from his parents' garage before he ever pitched Anduril, and that is the load-bearing analogue for the founder-lived-experience axis: the company materialized out of the founder's prior product life, not out of a market memo. Poth Labs diverges from the Luckey rung in the specific way that matters most to me — Wong's Palantir FDE work was *deploying* analytical software into enterprises (matthewwong.dev), which is real craft, but it is not the same as having been the product manager at 2 a.m. trying to tag a thousand churn tickets into useless categories. Horvath at Tietoevry was a data scientist for less than a competition cycle. Neither founder of Poth Labs has the autobiographical "I felt this" moment that put Luckey in front of defense or Hanke in front of location data; the problem was discovered analytically, and at pre-seed that is the tell that overrides résumé.

The HQ Trivia failure mechanism was unresolved co-founder authority — Yusupov and Kroll could not name a tiebreaker, and the product imploded around the feud. Tracing that pattern through Poth Labs: titles are clean — Wong is CEO, Horvath is CTO, no shared title, no 50/50 language in the dossier — but the brutkasten.com (2026) detail that the two founders *met through YC Early Decision* for the S26 batch means there is no prior shared trench, no fight they have already had and survived together. That is not the HQ Trivia red zone, but it is also not the documented-operating-agreement top of the ladder; it is the silence I treat as "they haven't had the conversation yet" because they have not yet had the time.

The magically-weird dimension compounds against Poth Labs over the next eighteen months in a specific way: Dovetail ($69.5M raised), Sprig (~$152M raised), and Maze ($90.5M raised) are all shipping AI-native research features into the same buyer in 2026, and BuildBetter and Reveall are pre-empting the "agentic feedback" wrapper from the lower end. Poth Labs's "$10B feedback industry" framing and the "47-second root cause" marketing demo are exactly what eight other YC S26 decks will say verbatim. Find them before it becomes consensus — Poth Labs *is* the consensus.

For the capital-intensive axis, the counterfactual to flip Poth Labs a tier would have to be that the product ingests not just feedback but operational data (employee schedules, inventory, supplier deliveries — the dossier hints at this) and writes back into a regulated workflow where a wrong hypothesis costs the customer money, the way Vigil Labs sits inside financial software. That would make Poth Labs unsexy-regulated rather than B2B-SaaS-wrapper. As written, Poth Labs is pure software abstraction selling into product/CX teams via "Book a demo" — the lowest-defensibility category I fund.

Physical-world verifiability for Poth Labs compares directly to the Flock Safety Wi-Fi-name moment in inverse: I cannot install Poth Labs, I cannot photograph it on a street pole, I cannot hand it to a friend at dinner. The pothlabs.com site exposes only a demo-request CTA, no Product Hunt launch, no Chrome store, no app store presence. The 47-second demo is a marketing reel, not an artifact. There is nothing tactile for my method to latch onto, and my method is the entire game.

Thirty-six month forecast for Poth Labs: acqui-hired into Sprig or a mid-market CX platform at sub-$30M total consideration, or quietly wound down after a Series A that does not clear, with Wong returning to a Palantir-adjacent enterprise role and Horvath starting a second company. The mechanism is straightforward — three funded incumbents ship the agentic-feedback feature inside their existing seat-based contracts before Poth Labs can find a wedge, and a two-person team with no design-partner LOI named in the dossier cannot out-distribute Dovetail's installed base.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the DeepMind polymath exception: Horvath at 19 with multiple AI-competition wins, Kemper Scholar at Andover, BWKI Austria 2024 — that is a cross-domain compounding profile, and I did write the check into Hassabis on exactly that override. But Hassabis was chess prodigy *and* neuroscientist *and* game designer at AlphaGo — the disciplines were genuinely orthogonal. Horvath's wins are clustered within AI/ML competitions, which is depth, not polymath spread; the exception does not fire here. The verifiable condition that would flip Poth Labs to neutral is a named Fortune-500 CX team signing a paid pilot within the next two YC office-hour cycles — that would tell me Wong's Palantir FDE muscle is converting in a way the dossier cannot currently show. Absent that, Poth Labs is the pass example from my own profile, almost word for word.
