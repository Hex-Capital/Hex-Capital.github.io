# Control Seat -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Control Seat is a software-thin optimization play sitting on top of the SCADA stack, and the dimensional signature is almost embarrassingly clean: the highest-scoring axis is consequence magnitude only because industrial control touches real plants, and the lowest is technical risk -- two Dartmouth '26 grads claiming 10k+ tags/sec and <100ms latency is web engineering, not a physics miracle. **Inductive Automation** at ~$41M ARR bootstrapped, with 69% Fortune-100 penetration, already proved this market rewards modernization -- but it rewards it at bootstrapped scale, not at the civilizational-redirection scale that justifies a Khosla check.

**Impossible Foods** (2011) is the consequence-axis HIGH rung I always come back to: the success-state was "displaces animal protein production," a named incumbent regime collapsing into a $1.7T food system. Control Seat's success-state, by the dossier's own math, is capturing share of a $20.05B SCADA market by 2030 at 9.2% CAGR (MarketsandMarkets, 2026) -- a respectable number that nonetheless leaves AVEVA Wonderware, Siemens WinCC, and Rockwell FactoryTalk as competing systems rather than collapsed ones. Control Seat does not name an incumbent regime that disappears; it names a stack it integrates against with a "competitive price guarantee" to beat integrator quotes. That is the inverse of the consequence-first framing -- it is the optimization-play framing my Outlook Business essay was written to warn against.

**Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) is the lowest-dimension parallel for technical risk, and its failure mechanism in reverse tells you what Control Seat is missing: I led that $115M Series A because no Tier-1 generalist would underwrite tokamak physics, and the capital risk was the moat. Control Seat's roadmap requires zero named technical miracles -- drag-and-drop dashboards, plain-language queries against tag data, anomaly detection on time-series. Any Tier-1 generalist can fund this; any Series B SaaS shop can replicate the wrapper. The dossier lists Ubidots, VTScada, ICONICS GENESIS64, and Tatsoft already shipping web-native SCADA -- which means the technical risk that should repel consensus VCs is instead attracting them.

On founder defiance of expert consensus -- the counterfactual question is what would flip Control Seat into Physical Intelligence (2024) territory. The answer is nothing in the dossier: Jack Grodnick's Tesla/SpaceX/Amazon internship stack and Warren Shepard's SpaceX/Microsoft path are paradigmatic expert-credential continuity, exactly the "20 years at incumbent" pattern my TED Talk McKinsey-cellphone slide was built to reject, just compressed into 22-year-old form. Shepard's mechanistic-interpretability research interest is interesting but adjacent; neither founder has publicly contradicted any field. To flip a tier I'd need a documented stance against, say, the OPC-UA orthodoxy or an IEC 62443 critique -- the dossier has none.

On learning rate, the mechanism over 18 months matters more than the snapshot. Control Seat incorporated January 2026 and launched a YouTube demo by mid-2026 -- five months of linear execution with no documented pivot, no public thesis update, no "we were wrong about X" moment. That is not a black mark; it is an absence of signal because the company is too young to have generated one. The Slash (2024) Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C cannot be tested here yet, so Control Seat gets the absent-evidence default rather than a penalty -- but it also gets none of the upside the dimension is supposed to surface.

The required-capital profile, compared on a single axis to **OpenAI** (2019), tells you why I am over-resourced for this deal: $50M first check into a no-revenue civilizational bet versus a $500K YC standard deal into a 2-person SCADA modernizer with SaaS-plus-integrator economics. Control Seat's roadmap fits in a $5-10M seed-to-Series-A, the deal could close at any Tier-1 generalist tomorrow, and Khosla Ventures' $3.5B 2025 vehicle (TechCrunch 2025-02-24) has nowhere productive to deploy at this check size. The capital match is structurally wrong, not just stylistically wrong.

36-month forecast: Control Seat reaches $1-3M ARR as a boutique integrator-plus-platform serving 5-15 mid-market plants, then gets acqui-hired by Inductive Automation, Rockwell, or a private-equity SI roll-up for under $50M -- the modal outcome for a YC-pedigreed SCADA modernizer that lacks the bootstrapped patience of Ignition and the capital depth of an AVEVA-class incumbent. The mechanism is straightforward: Control Seat's "competitive price guarantee" compresses gross margin into integrator territory, the AI-on-tags feature gets cloned by Ignition within 24 months because the LLM layer is commodity, and the founders take a soft landing that monetizes their SpaceX-pedigreed engineering rather than their thesis.

The strongest bull case borrows the **Glean** Known Exception: Arvind Jain was ex-Google search, paradigmatic expert continuity, and I funded it anyway because the consequence on Fortune-500 information asymmetry was large enough to override the credential pattern. Could Control Seat be the industrial Glean -- expert-track founders, big bounded market, consequence in displacing decades of OT lock-in? The case stings until you check the founder scale: Jain shipped Google search at planet scale before Glean; Grodnick and Shepard are 22 with summer internships, not shipped systems. Glean also commanded capital scale that justified the consensus-credential override; Control Seat at $500K YC standard does not. The verdict flips only on one verifiable condition: signed multi-year LOIs from two or more Fortune-500 industrial operators within 12 months, naming Control Seat as the prime SCADA platform, not the integrator. Absent that, this is a pass on the consequence-first dimension and the dossier never reaches my second meeting.
