# Imperfect -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Matin Tamizi processed $500M+ in card volume at Balanced, spent nearly a decade at Stripe across six functions, navigated CNBV regulatory approval for Cuenca in Mexico, and exited that company with a16z and Stripe on the cap table. That's a genuinely impressive operator resume. And he's using it to build... an AI fitness coach. Look, I've said this a hundred times: a brilliant founder in a consensus-hot category is a bad bet at any quality level because the price already reflects the upside. Imperfect is a talented fintech infrastructure builder pivoting into the single most crowded AI application category outside of copilots. Five direct competitors already exist — TrainerRoad with 250M+ training activities and $15M in revenue, Athletica, HumanGO, Spoked, plus whatever Garmin and Apple ship natively next quarter. There is no mispricing here. Every demo day has three of these. The market has this category priced exactly right, which means the asymmetry I need to get excited doesn't exist.

Imperfect's own framing tells you everything: "Waze for training." When you describe your company using an analogy to an existing product, you're telling me you're not creating a new primitive — you're creating a better version of something that already exists. Venmo became a verb. Patreon created the behavior of paying creators directly before "creator economy" was a phrase anyone used. Imperfect is not creating a new behavior. Athletes already train with coaches and plans. Imperfect is making that process marginally more adaptive using LLMs. That's an incremental improvement, not category creation. Nobody is going to say "Imperfect me" the way they say "Venmo me." The primitive test fails cleanly.

Tamizi's garth library — 810 GitHub stars, a Garmin Connect Python client — shows genuine domain investment in wearable data integration. But here's the brutal part: Garmin already broke the auth flow that garth depended on, and Tamizi deprecated it in March 2026. Imperfect's entire product sits on top of third-party wearable APIs that the platform owners can restrict or replicate at will. This is the structural durability problem I keep hammering on CNBC: software companies that had moats are learning they aren't moats anymore. Metropolis works because you can't replicate parking garages with an API call. Imperfect has no physical infrastructure, no protocol-level lock-in, no network effects that survive platform competition. Apple has HealthKit and first-party sensor data. Garmin has the devices on people's wrists. Google has Health Connect. Every one of them can ship a native AI coaching feature with better data access than Imperfect will ever have. This is the AI kill test at its most obvious — Imperfect is Adobe for fitness, and Apple is Adobe.

The bull case an advocate would make: Tamizi is a second-time YC founder with real exits, the multi-sport data aggregation across fragmented wearable platforms creates switching costs that deepen over time, and the timing convergence of mainstream wearable adoption plus LLM capabilities creates a genuine "why now." TrainingPeaks' business model depends on human coaches paying for the platform, so an autonomous AI coach would cannibalize their revenue — structural conflict that creates a window. And the endurance training market is growing at 14.55% CAGR. That's a real case. But it collapses under one question: can TrainerRoad, with 250M+ activities of training data and $15M in revenue, ship multi-sport adaptive coaching faster than Imperfect can build a comparable dataset from zero? Can Garmin ship native AI coaching using their own sensor data without any API dependency? The answer to both is obviously yes. The "data aggregation moat" is an API integration layer, not a moat. I checked my Known Exceptions — Slack got a pass on consensus pricing because real-time workplace messaging was a genuine new primitive replacing email. Imperfect is not replacing anything; it's making existing coaching incrementally smarter. The exception doesn't apply.

Imperfect is offered as a free download with no pricing page, no visible conversion mechanism, no public user counts, and no app store ratings as of April 2026. The team is five people covering AI/ML, multi-sport exercise science, wearable integrations, and consumer app development simultaneously with a solo founder whose entire career has been in fintech infrastructure, not sports science or consumer fitness. The sanity-insanity ratio here is inverted from what I want: Tamizi is clearly a sane, accomplished operator — but the idea isn't insane, it's obvious. Every engineer with a Garmin watch has thought about building this. I get excited when a reasonable person pitches something that sounds fucking crazy. An AI fitness coach in 2026 sounds like exactly what every reasonable person would build. That's the problem.

One condition that would flip me to Invest: if Imperfect showed me that it's actually building a protocol layer for wearable health data — an open standard that other apps build on top of, not a consumer coaching app — and Tamizi's fintech infrastructure instincts were aimed at becoming the Stripe of health data rather than the TrainerRoad of AI coaching. That would be a primitive, that would have structural durability, and that would be mispriced because nobody's pricing a YC fitness app as infrastructure. But that's not what's in the dossier. What's in the dossier is an AI coaching wrapper in the most crowded application category in tech. Pass.
