# Wealor -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Wealor is the canonical 2026 Pass Example wearing a wealth-management hat: Karim Bouri and Pierre Depuydt are pitching an "AI-native operating system" into RIAs the same quarter Orion shipped Denali AI, Addepar shipped Addison, Altruist bought Thyme into Hazel at $60/seat, and Anthropic released RIA plug-ins directly into the broker-dealer channel. The signature here is a credible operator on Criterion 3 (Bouri actually sold financial products to wealth managers, which is the only thing in the dossier that isn't generic) running headfirst into the worst possible sleeve mismatch on Criterion 5 ? "AI is just more internet" as a vertical agent layer that four better-funded vendors and two incumbents already occupy. That pairing doesn't produce a defining company; it produces an acqui-hire.

Solana at $0.05/coin is the high rung on round mis-pricing because the category had been abandoned ? nobody was bidding, so the price was real. Wealor sits at the opposite pole of that ladder: the dossier names Jump at $105M raised, Zocks at $65M raised, Vise at $124.5M raised and a $1B+ post, plus Orion and Addepar shipping native AI in February and March 2026 ? this is a category where every tier-1 has already paid up at the Series B, which means the YC P26 seed gets priced off that comp, not off a clearing price. There is no disclosed Wealor round size in the dossier, and I am scoring the silence the way I always do ? when the round dynamics aren't legible, the prior is consensus pricing, and the alpha is gone before I read the deck.

The canonical SaaS cap table that needs five more rounds to reach default-alive is what kills Criterion 2, and Wealor walks straight into it: "forward deployed engineers for RIAs and family offices" is Palantir-cosplay services revenue dressed as software, the GTM is long-cycle direct sales into fiduciary-regulated buyers, and the competitive set has already raised $170M+ combined to outspend a pre-seed on every pilot. Teamshares works as a structure bet because the employee-ownership rollup is an instrument nobody else underwrites; Wealor's instrument is a standard SAFE into a category where Insight, Lightspeed, QED, and Ribbit are already the marginal dollar at the Series B. There is nothing structural in the dossier ? no revenue share against AUM, no royalty on advisor productivity, no holdco across RIA equity ? that would let me underwrite this as anything other than a cap table that gets crushed in the next tack.

Bouri's biography is the one place Wealor registers signal: ENSAE + HEC + Polytechnique + Berkeley, ML at CANAL+, and ? the load-bearing line ? previously "sold financial products to wealth managers," which is the actual buyer relationship most YC AI-for-vertical teams fake with three customer-discovery interviews. Over 18 months that compounds usefully if Bouri converts five to ten of his prior buyers into paying logos before Jump and Zocks chew through their Series B runway and notice the segment. But the dossier shows zero public writing, no GitHub, no Twitter, no opinionated take on why Orion's Denali architecturally fails RIAs ? without an artifact of asymmetric conviction, Wealor's founder evidence is "competent insider responding to opportunity," which is exactly the temperament I derate because it does not weather the non-linear tacks.

For Criterion 4, the counterfactual that flips Wealor a tier is concrete: if Bouri were running a wealth-management Substack with 8K RIA principals subscribed, or if Wealor had any owned channel into the 17-of-top-20 Barron's RIAs that Orion already serves, the founder-as-brand line would carry weight against the incumbent distribution. Nothing in the dossier supports that ? LinkedIn counts not retrievable, no Twitter for either founder, no podcast, no community ? so Wealor is a paid-sales-only motion into a buyer that takes 9-month sales cycles to close $50K ACV. That is the exact distribution profile the Slow Creator Fund was built to avoid funding.

On sleeve fit, Wealor is the single worst placement in the persona's revealed-preference map. Compare to Ro, which I underwrote despite Criterion 2 burn because 50-state prescription rails were a real "venture moment" only VC capital could clear; Wealor's regulatory surface (Investment Advisers Act fiduciary and recordkeeping) is real but not a moat ? Jump, Zocks, and Vise have all already cleared it. The category is "vertical AI agent SaaS" which the persona file flags by name as a Red Flag, and Anthropic's RIA plug-ins released in April 2026 do to Wealor what foundation-model commoditization always does: collapse the connective-tissue layer into a config file inside the buyer's existing stack.

In 36 months Wealor is acqui-hired by Envestnet, SS&C, or a mid-tier custodian for under $30M with ARR under $3M, or pivoted into a single-RIA workflow consulting shop dressed as software ? the mechanism is that Jump's $80M Series B and Zocks' $45M Series B buy them 36 months of GTM dominance into the same 1,400-firm Addepar base, Orion's Denali eats the mid-market natively, and Wealor never gets the seven tacks it needs to reach a Series A buyer's frame. The legibility translation here is the thing that kills it ? at the Series A I have to story-broker "AI-native OS for wealth managers" into a deck where Jump and Zocks are not the obvious comp, and I cannot.

The bull case worth taking seriously is that Bouri's prior buyer-side relationships convert into five to ten paying RIA logos in 12 months at $100-200K ACV, producing $1-2M ARR with genuine forward-deployed integration depth that no Jump note-taker can replicate. Two of my own portfolio anchors ? Pinterest and Robinhood ? are Known Exceptions where operator-channel sourcing or earliest-stage entry overrode Criterion 1, so I have to ask: does the operator channel save Wealor? It doesn't, because the Pinterest override required missionary temperament evidence I cannot find in Bouri's or Depuydt's public footprint, and the Robinhood override required earliest-stage pricing I am not being offered. The condition that flips my verdict is a signed contract with a named top-50 Barron's RIA at $200K+ ACV before Series A; absent that, Wealor is the pitch I described in my Pass Example, ported to wealth.
