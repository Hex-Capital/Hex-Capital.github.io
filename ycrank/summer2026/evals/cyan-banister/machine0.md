# machine0 -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Barnaby Malet is a solo PhD-in-Cloud-Computing founder selling persistent VMs into a 2026 sandbox category that already has Fly.io Sprites, E2B ($35M), Modal ($466M at $4.65B), Daytona ($24M Series A), Cloudflare, Vercel, AWS AgentCore, and Google Agent Sandbox all swinging at the same workload -- 19+ named entrants per AgentMarketCap. So machine0's analytical signature in my portfolio is a structurally unambiguous solo CEO with a credentialed cloud founder dropped into the most violently consensus infrastructure category of the S26 batch. The single-CEO virtue here is a tautology of headcount, not a hard-won governance decision, and the consensus-category vice is the loudest signal in the dossier.

Crusoe (~2018) is the high rung on "magically weird" because burning North Dakota flares for Bitcoin sounded like three uninvestable things stapled together, and credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds before I wrote the check -- that was the tell. machine0 is the inverse mechanism: Fly.io shipped Sprites in January 2026 with 1?2 second creation and 300ms checkpoint, Daytona pivoted to agent infrastructure with $24M behind it in February 2026, and the AgentMarketCap survey names persistent-agent VMs as a defined category. The thesis says "find them before it becomes consensus" (Yahoo Finance, March 2025). machine0 is *after* it becomes consensus. The round will be priced like the outcome is already certain, and the founder cannot articulate a misread because there is no misread -- the hyperscalers agree with him.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on -- two ex-FAANG PMs with a $200B TAM slide -- failed because the founder origin was analytical, not autobiographical. Does that pattern appear in machine0? Partially, and this is where I am gentler than my own pass example deserves: Barnaby Malet holds a PhD in Cloud Computing from Imperial College London and was CTPO of Upflow (YC W20, $22.9M raised, customers Lattice and Front), so he is not a memo-reader. He is, however, a B2B cash-collection operator pivoting into agent sandbox infrastructure, not Palmer Luckey shipping defense after Oculus or John Hanke shipping AR after Keyhole. The lived contact with *this* problem -- persistent VMs for autonomous Claude Code sessions -- is one PhD removed from autobiographical. That is a strong default, not a HandUp-grade override.

Single CEO authority on machine0 scores high by mechanism, not by virtue (counterfactual mode): a solo founder cannot violate the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll failure pattern because there is no second principal. The score would only flip downward if Malet hired a co-CEO during seed, and the dossier shows no such intent. Conversely, the score does not flip *upward* into "magic" territory because solo-by-default is structurally different from the Musk-at-SpaceX (2008) clarity I treat as a deliberate governance act.

Capital intensity at machine0 looks like frontier-hardware until you read the pricing page (mechanism analysis): $0.013/hr small instances to $3.714/hr 6xl with H100, H200, L40S, and MI300X options. machine0 is reselling GPU capacity over KVM/QEMU, which is a thin orchestration layer on top of someone else's capex -- the Anduril (2017) hardware moat compounds with manufacturing scale; machine0's "moat" compounds with whatever differential rate it can negotiate from upstream cloud providers, which Fly.io and Modal can negotiate harder. Eighteen months out, the unit economics will be set by the marginal GPU supplier, not by machine0.

Physical-world verifiability on machine0 lives in the CLI -- `machine0 new my-vm` -- which means I can install it tonight and watch a VM provision (single-anchor parallel to the Flock Safety ~2018 hotel Wi-Fi moment). But machine0 is not a Flock camera bolted to a streetlight that I can point at; it is a developer abstraction whose value is only visible if I am already running an 8-hour Claude Code session. The product is observable, but it does not produce the "I am gifted with the ability to make these connections" environmental signal -- nobody at a Four Seasons caf? notices a persistent VM the way they notice a license-plate camera.

machine0 in 36 months is most likely an acquihire by Cloudflare, Fly.io, or Vercel for $5?15M with Malet running an internal agent-infrastructure team, or quietly winding down after Fly.io Sprites and AWS AgentCore commoditize the persistent-VM primitive at hyperscaler pricing. The mechanism: solo capacity cannot match a $466M Modal sales motion into Fortune 100 buyers (E2B already claims 88% Fortune 100 signup), and the 99.99% four-region SLA across US/UK/EU/Asia is operationally implausible for one person, which means either downtime erodes the brand or capital comes in to hire ops -- and that capital round will be priced against Fly.io's 1?2 second Sprites benchmark.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Barnaby Malet is a 2x YC founder with a PhD in the exact domain, and Notion ran from a solo-ish team into category dominance against Confluence and dozens of doc tools because the founder's product taste compounded faster than competitors' headcount. If Malet ships a persistence primitive that Claude Code and Codex adopt as a default runtime, the AgentMarketCap-listed 19 competitors become irrelevant in the way Linear made the Jira-clone field irrelevant. The bear-side dismantle: Notion's Ivan Zhao spent years iterating on the product taste from his own daily use; the dossier shows no evidence Malet is dogfooding multi-hour Claude Code sessions himself, and the moat language in the dossier is bracketed "[Inferred]" four times. I flip to Invest if machine0 publishes a named Anthropic or OpenAI integration as the default runtime, or hits $50K MRR within six months on developer-led adoption alone -- absent that, machine0 is a competent solo founder building a credible product in the wrong year of the cycle.
