# Thirdbrain Labs -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Margaret Zhang traveled to Kyiv to meet with Ukraine Ministry of Defence operators for her previous startup, Coalition Systems. That's the kind of high-agency move that catches my attention — most founders won't fly into a war zone to close a deal. But then she started Thirdbrain Labs, and the relationship between these two ventures is opaque. Did Coalition Systems fail? Pivot? Is it still running in parallel? When a founder moves from defense AI coordination software to fine-tuning infrastructure without a public explanation, I need to understand whether this represents a learning arc or a pattern of starting things. The dossier can't tell me, and that ambiguity matters at pre-seed when the founder IS the company.

The consequence question kills most of my interest. "Unlocking the next one billion specialized models" — what happens if Thirdbrain Labs fully succeeds? Domain experts can customize AI models without ML engineers. That's useful. It's also what Scale AI ($29B valuation, ~$2B revenue), Together AI ($3.3B valuation, 450K+ users), Labelbox ($100M+ ARR), and Snorkel AI ($1.3B valuation) are all building toward with vastly more capital and distribution. AWS Bedrock and Google Vertex AI offer managed fine-tuning natively. When I backed Rocket Lab, small-sat launch was a new market with no incumbent offering dedicated rides — Peter Beck was creating access that didn't exist. Thirdbrain Labs is competing for share of an existing and rapidly crowding market. The consequence of their success is a moderately profitable infrastructure company. Nobody's life fundamentally changes. That's fine for most investors. It's not fine for me.

The "expert decision capture" framing is Thirdbrain Labs' claim to technical differentiation, but the dossier is honest about the gap: "The differentiation between Thirdbrain Labs' 'expert decision capture' and standard RLHF/DPO workflows is not publicly documented in enough detail to assess defensibility." That's a polite way of saying there's no visible 10x. When free tools like Hugging Face AutoTrain and Axolotl let teams with modest ML capability fine-tune models without paying anyone, Thirdbrain Labs needs a genuine technical discontinuity to justify its existence — not a better UX wrapper around techniques the open-source community ships quarterly. David Huang's UC Berkeley Regents' Scholarship and top-1% finish in Two Sigma's Halite challenge demonstrate ML competence, but competence in the existing paradigm is precisely what produces incremental improvements. I need evidence of a non-linear insight, and none is publicly visible.

The strongest bull case: if "expert decisions" represents a genuinely novel approach to preference learning — something closer to how humans actually transfer expertise, not just labeled preference pairs — then Thirdbrain Labs could own a workflow layer that becomes embedded in production systems across thousands of domain teams. The data flywheel from accumulated expert feedback could create switching costs that Scale AI's annotation-workforce model can't replicate. The $3.2B fine-tuning orchestration market growing to $24.8B by 2034 suggests expanding demand. And Zhang's Coalition Systems experience, whatever its outcome, means she's built AI products for demanding users in high-stakes environments. If the technical approach is as differentiated as the positioning implies, and if they can establish the expert-feedback data loop before incumbents copy it, there's a real business here. But "real business" and "consequence explosion" are different categories. Predibase — the closest structural comp in this space — was acquired by Rubrik for somewhere between $100M and $500M. That's the ceiling this category has demonstrated. I don't invest to make 5x. I invest to make 50x or lose everything.

Thirdbrain Labs hits my red flag for "targeting redistribution of existing spend rather than creation of new demand" and my red flag for "the improvement is incremental rather than discontinuous." The two-person team is entering a space where the smallest funded competitor (Predibase, ~$28M raised) was acquired before achieving escape velocity, and the largest (Scale AI) has the resources to copy any workflow innovation within months. No Known Exception applies — this isn't a DoorDash-scale consequence magnitude that overrides the technical novelty requirement, nor an OpenAI-scale civilizational bet that overrides everything else. The dimensions that locked my verdict: Consequence Magnitude at 12/30 because the world doesn't visibly change if post-training workflows improve, and Non-Linear Technical Approach at 8/25 because no proprietary technical architecture distinguishes Thirdbrain Labs from commoditized fine-tuning tooling. Pass.
