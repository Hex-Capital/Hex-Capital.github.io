## The Idea

- **Problem:** Air traffic control runs on ground-based legacy infrastructure that is the subject of an FAA modernization program estimated at $32B (Government Market News / Federal News Network, 2025) and the target of GAO reports flagging urgent need for system modernization (GAO-25-108162, 2025).
- **Approach:** Build "satellite-based ATC systems that connect every aircraft, drone, rocket, and ground vehicle to a global network," claiming ~50% cost reduction in ATC operations and more direct flight paths (generalaviation.com, May 2026).
- **Differentiation:** Aireon already operates the only space-based ADS-B surveillance constellation (Aireon press release, May 2026); incumbents Thales, Raytheon/Collins Aerospace, L3Harris, Indra, Leidos, Frequentis are the FAA Common Automation Platform shortlist (The Air Current, 2025) — [Inferred]: General Aviation differs by proposing a full networked replacement architecture (aircraft-as-IP-endpoint) rather than surveillance overlay or controller-workstation software.
- **Business Model:** No public data found on pricing page; [Inferred]: long-cycle government/ANSP contracting plus possible per-aircraft connectivity subscription, given the FAA/ANSP customer set referenced in adjacent market research (MarketsandMarkets, 2025).
- **TAM/SAM:** Air Traffic Management market $14.7B in 2025 growing 9.7% CAGR through 2035 (GMInsights, 2025); ATC equipment sub-segment $5.03B in 2026 (Market Reports World, 2026); FAA modernization program ~$32B total with $12.5B initial appropriation (Government Market News, 2025).
- **GTM / Distribution:** No public GTM data found; [Inferred]: federal procurement (FAA) and foreign ANSP sales — the only customers with authority to award ATC contracts, per Reason Foundation aviation policy analysis (2025).
