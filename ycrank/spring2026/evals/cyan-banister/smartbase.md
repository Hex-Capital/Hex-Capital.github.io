# Smartbase -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Smartbase is a clean-governance B2B compliance wedge into a domain the founders *visited* rather than came *from* -- Sam Goldman and Taira Fujioka slept on a factory floor to study metal-finishing PO entry, which is the right instinct but the wrong biography, and that gap between observation and origin is the analytical signature of this company. The CEO line is unambiguous, the ITAR/CMMC/DFARS enclave is the kind of unsexy regulatory wrapper I usually love, and yet the founder-origin slot reads more like the consensus-YC pass example in my own playbook than the Anduril template.

Anduril-Luckey (2017) is the high rung on lived founder origin -- Palmer shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he ever touched a defense contract, and the defense product was downstream of a decade of hardware obsession. Smartbase's founders did the next-best thing a non-native team can do: Sam Goldman and Taira Fujioka physically embedded on shop floors and slept at a factory per the YC page. I respect the move, but it's research immersion, not autobiography. Neither founder grew up in metal finishing, neither was a roughneck on a job shop, and the pivot from Anytrace (YC S25, AI support-engineer) to Smartbase (YC P26, metal-finishing PO entry) tells me they went looking for a wedge rather than being driven into one by lived contact. That is the difference between Luckey welding in the garage and a smart pair of operators identifying a $26B clerical-labor TAM in a memo.

The consensus-YC AI-agent darling I passed on -- ex-FAANG PMs, $200B TAM slide, six funds chasing -- is the low rung on "magically weird," and the failure mechanism there was that the category was converging while the deck claimed contrarian. Smartbase doesn't look like that company on the surface; metal-finishing PO entry is exactly the unsexy lane I prowl. But trace the pattern: Leverage AI has raised $14.9M, Turian $3.78M, Evolinq is shipping a "Digital Twin" PO agent, and Yooz and Netfira are already in the OCR seat -- the tryleverage.ai 2026 comparison piece is itself evidence the AI-PO category is converging into consensus right now. Smartbase is unsexy without being non-consensus. That's the wrong half of the magically-weird ledger.

The ITAR/CMMC/DFARS-compliant U.S.-hosted enclave is the dimension where Smartbase compounds best over 18 months. Defense-supply-chain metal finishing is a regulated niche where Leverage AI, Turian, and the OCR incumbents have not advertised compliance posture; if Sam Goldman wires Smartbase into the qualified-supplier list for even one prime, the switching cost ladders out across the nine named ERPs (Acumatica, Epicor, Infor, MS Dynamics 365, NetSuite, QuickBooks, SAP, SYSPRO, Sage X3). The mechanism is regulatory drag-coefficient -- defense subcontractors do not rip-and-replace a system that has already been cleared. That is the Vigil Labs-shaped corner of this thesis and it is real.

For Smartbase to flip from Neutral to Invest on founder fit, one of two things would have to be true: either Sam Goldman's undisclosed prior $2M-raised DevOps platform was itself a metal-finishing or manufacturing-ops product (the dossier does not name it, so I cannot verify), or one of the "first customers" on five-figure contracts is a named defense prime that signed because of a personal relationship the dossier omits. Absent either, the counterfactual fails and the founder-origin score stays where it sits. The Anytrace-to-Smartbase pivot makes me read the gap as real rather than as missing dossier data.

Sam Goldman as CEO and Taira Fujioka as CTO with the title delineation explicit on LinkedIn is the inverse of the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll failure -- there is no 50/50 ambiguity, no co-CEO cosplay, no unresolved tiebreaker question. I don't have the operating agreement and I don't have the vesting schedule, but the surface signal is clean, and on a two-person team where one shipped AWS SageMaker Inference at scale and the other ran a prior venture-backed company, the role split tracks the skill split. That is the kind of low-drama governance I no longer have to litigate at the term sheet.

Smartbase fails the Flock Safety test on physical verifiability -- I cannot install Smartbase at home, I cannot photograph a Smartbase artifact in the wild, I cannot ride a Smartbase from the airport. The product is a parser that ingests faxes and emits ERP rows at metal-finishing job shops in industrial parks I will never visit. Niantic I could play; Postmates delivered to my door; Smartbase lives entirely inside a customer's back office. That doesn't disqualify -- plenty of B2B infrastructure compounds without tactile observability -- but it removes a sourcing edge I rely on and forces me to evaluate on founder and category alone.

Thirty-six months out, Smartbase is most likely acquired by Epicor, Acumatica, or SAP for a $25-60M strategic price as a defense-compliance feature module, or stalls at $2-4M ARR fighting Leverage AI and Turian for the same metal-finishing logos. The mechanism producing that range is exactly the dimensional signature: a clean two-person team with a defensible regulatory wedge, but no autobiographical moat and a tactile product nobody outside the buyer's office can see, in a category where ERP incumbents earn services revenue from the manual onboarding Smartbase is automating -- which makes them natural acquirers rather than natural enemies.

The strongest bear-cracking counter is that defense-supply-chain compliance is precisely the "ugly, regulated, capital-intensive, or aesthetically transgressive" lane I publicly say I fund when no rival on the council list will, and that I almost passed on Crusoe (~2018) for similar "three uninvestable things stapled together" reasons. Maybe Smartbase's ITAR enclave plus a CTO who ran AWS SageMaker Inference at scale plus a CEO who already raised $2M is the under-appreciated trifecta and I am pattern-matching on the wrong axis. The dossier evidence that wins for me is the Anytrace pivot -- Crusoe-Lochmiller did not pivot into oil-and-gas, he was already adjacent; Sam and Taira pivoted into metal finishing from an AI-support-engineer product one batch earlier, which is the analytical opposite of the Crusoe origin. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named defense-prime LOI or a signed contract with a Tier-1 metal-finishing shop that traces to a personal relationship one of the founders held before YC P26. Show me that, and I re-underwrite.
