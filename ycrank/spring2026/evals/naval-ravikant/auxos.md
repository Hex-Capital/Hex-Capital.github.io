# Auxos -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Auxos is a high-trust three-person team -- Ashton Daniel, Kerry Lu, Jerry Wu, fenced together for fifteen years on Team USA -- building permission-gated enterprise B2B research software in a category where Aaru just raised at a $1B headline and Simile took $100M Series A five months before Auxos's YC launch. That high/low pairing is the whole story: the humans pass the ten-year-partner test cleanly, and the leverage architecture fails my opening question before the founder section finishes loading.

Twitter (2007) is the canonical rung on permissionless leverage -- anyone posts, the platform charges nothing, marginal cost of replication is zero, media-as-protocol. Auxos does not share that mechanism. The CEO's primary distribution surface is a Cal.com demo link (cal.com/ashton-daniel), the pricing page is absent, and the GTM is founder-led outbound into Fortune 500 research buyers -- the BCG rolodex applied to selling twins. That is labor and capital leverage requiring permission from procurement, not code or media leverage compounding without gatekeepers (nav.al/product-media). No open-source repo, no public technical writing, no organic developer pull, no X-native virality on @TheAshtonDaniel or @kerry_lu_ that I can measure. The Auxos launch tweet came from @ycombinator, not from a founder whose audience grew through domain writing -- the inverse of the Airchat (April 2024) template.

Perplexity (2022) is the LOW rung on networked compounding -- a single-sided foundation-model app where the Nth user does not make the (N-1)th user more valuable -- and I overrode that gap because the other three gates cleared emphatically. Auxos sits on the same rung structurally: each customer's twin audience is built from interviews with that customer's ICP, so the data does not compound across the buyer base. The 847 packaging tests and 1,486 pricing tiers on useauxos.com are explicitly illustrative copy, not cross-customer network artifacts. The Perplexity-style override would require all three of: top-quartile specific knowledge, permissionless distribution, and pre-consensus category-timing. Auxos has one of the three.

Kerry Lu built the ad simulation infrastructure modeling 40% of US e-commerce traffic at Amazon Ads, and Jerry Wu shipped recommendation infra credited with $100M/year incremental revenue at Instagram -- that is real non-trainable simulation-systems knowledge, the kind that does not propagate from a Stanford CS clone. It compounds on the technical side: over eighteen months, the CTO's behavioral-simulation depth either produces measurably better twin fidelity than Aaru's purely-synthetic populations or it does not, and there is no public benchmark either way as of May 2026. The CEO drags the average down -- "ran surveys, panels, and interviews" at BCG is the consultant-identified-opportunity origin pattern (Almanack, 2020), not lived founder-as-user discovery the way Atwood and Spolsky discovered Stack Overflow (2008) by writing code daily.

For Auxos's market dimension to flip a tier, the dossier would need an articulated adjacent-pivot path beyond customer research -- a credible thread into agentic-decision simulation, synthetic governance modeling, or autonomous-agent training data -- because the $56B ESOMAR research-sector TAM is platform-shaped only if the company refuses to be only-a-research-tool. The current Auxos positioning ("digital twins for customer research") is one vertical inside a big stated number, and the "first idea never works" axiom (Venture Hacks, 2007-10) means the second idea needs somewhere to land. No such adjacency is named in the dossier.

Notion (2013) is the single-anchor parallel on the founder-trio dimension -- not because Ivan Zhao's solo-obsession profile matches Auxos's three-co-founder structure, but because the ten-year-partner test passes the same way: through accumulated time rather than promotional language. Daniel, Lu, and Wu have known each other fifteen years through fencing for Team USA; nobody is volunteering statements about their own honesty, the equity structure has a named CEO, no contested prior exits exist on any of the three CVs. This is the cleanest dimension in the dossier and the one I would underwrite without further diligence.

Thirty-six month forecast: Auxos lands a handful of Fortune 500 logos through Daniel's BCG network, reaches $1-3M ARR by mid-2028, and gets squeezed between Aaru's $1B-headline distribution muscle and Simile's $100M war chest into an acqui-hire by Qualtrics, Dynata, or one of the better-capitalized synthetic-research entrants at $20-60M. The mechanism is direct: in a crowded post-consensus category, the architecture that wins is permissionless distribution at zero marginal cost, and Auxos's founder-led B2B motion is the inverse pattern.

The strongest bear-on-my-pass is Kerry Lu's Amazon simulation infrastructure -- 40% of US e-commerce traffic modeled is genuinely rare specific knowledge, the kind that justified my Perplexity override on Aravind Srinivas's retrieval depth. If Auxos's twin fidelity benchmarks against real respondents materially beats Aaru's purely-synthetic populations, the technical moat compounds and the architecture argument loses to the depth argument. The dismantling fact is that Perplexity also had permissionless API distribution and pre-consensus AI-cycle timing; Auxos has neither -- Aaru's December 2025 $1B headline raise IS the consensus marker, and the GTM IS founder-led sales. Two of three override conditions fail. The verdict flips if Auxos ships a public API or self-serve product with measurable unprompted researcher pull -- organic signups, GitHub stars on a twins SDK, X-native traction on @kerry_lu_ -- within twelve months. Until then, the math is one X downside, capped upside, wrong architecture.
