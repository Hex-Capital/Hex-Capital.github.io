# Oddpool -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Oddpool is a picks-and-shovels SaaS sitting downstream of someone else's primitive ? Kalshi and Polymarket built the new behavior, and Avi Arora and Ritesh Malpani are wrapping a Bloomberg-style dashboard around two public APIs. The pm.wiki listing literally calls Oddpool "the Bloomberg for prediction markets," which is the most honest self-description in the dossier and also the reason I can't get there. When the founder's own analogy names a $30B incumbent in a different category, you're not creating a primitive. You're an aggregator of someone else's revolution.

The category itself is where Oddpool comes closest to my framework. HIGH rung: Solana, where Ethereum-won consensus left SOL at four cents and the entire upside was unpriced. MID rung: Eaze, where cannabis discomfort genuinely scared off mainstream VCs and produced real mispricing even though the outcome was poor. LOW rung: AI copilot YC pitches, where ten VCs are bidding and the upside is in the price. Oddpool sits between MID and LOW. Prediction markets carry CFTC ambiguity and gambling adjacency that makes some LPs squirm ? that's real discomfort. But the data-broker layer is consensus-crowded: Dome (YC F25, ex-Alchemy founders), Prediction Hunt v2, FinFeedAPI from CoinAPI's team, and open-source pmxt are all already serving the exact same buyer. YC funding Dome a batch ago, then funding Oddpool this batch, is the definition of factory-farm consensus pattern-matching. The risk is not baked into the price; the opportunity is.

Primitive creation is where Oddpool most sharply fails my filter. HIGH rung: Venmo, which created the verb. MID rung: Patreon, which formalized creator monetization into a behavior. LOW rung: any "better CRM" pitch, where execution in an existing market caps the upside. Oddpool sits at the LOW rung, full stop. The dossier describes ingesting Kalshi and Polymarket feeds and exposing a unified API ? this is normalization, not primitive creation. Nobody will say "I Oddpooled it." The 700K contracts indexed and 750 event dashboards are inventory metrics, not behavioral metrics. The PredictionMarketBench paper is a credibility signal, not a new category. After Oddpool succeeds, it doesn't become a reference point ? it becomes a line item under "data vendors" on a quant fund's expense report.

The AI kill test is uncomfortable but not the worst of it ? the venue kill test is. Metropolis is my HIGH rung here: physical parking that no LLM can replicate. Patreon is the MID rung: creator-audience relationships that compound. The LOW rung is any AI wrapper Adobe could replicate in eighteen months. Oddpool sits between LOW and MID. The historical orderbook archive from March 2026 with 1-5 minute granularity does genuinely compound ? a later entrant cannot rebuild the past, which is a real moat. But Polymarket bought Dome in early 2026. They showed the playbook. Two of Oddpool's API counterparties have demonstrated that the venue-side preference is to absorb the aggregator rather than tolerate one. The "structural opening for an independent broker" the dossier infers is exactly the opening Polymarket closed by writing a check.

On sanity-insanity: Avi at Microsoft Prompt Shields handling 20B classifications/yr and Ritesh at Bloomberg with 100K+ TPS trading systems are competent, sane engineers ? closer to my Stewart Butterfield mid-rung than to a Raj high-rung, because there's no pre-existing relationship I've watched across three companies. And the idea is sane. Sane founders pitching a sane idea is exactly what doesn't excite me. Worse, both LinkedIn headlines still say "Co-founder @ Benchspan (YC P26)" ? Oddpool is a same-batch pivot from an AI prompt-injection detector, which means under six months at the current product. That's not patient missionary commitment; that's a batch-cycle audible. Capital discipline I have no evidence on either way, so default 6/10 ? a two-person team on $30-$100/mo SaaS pricing isn't blitzscaling, but isn't a Gusto-grade discipline signal either.

My 36-month forecast: Oddpool gets acquired by Kalshi or Polymarket for sub-$30M as an acqui-hire of the two engineers ? a near-exact replay of the Dome?Polymarket transaction in early 2026 ? or loses commercial API access and shuts down. Standalone ARR will not exceed $2M. The mechanism is straightforward: every venue Oddpool depends on has a structural incentive to either own the aggregator or starve it, and the precedent is twelve months old.

The bull case that stings: the historical archive compounds, Kalshi's 40% institutional flow keeps growing, and Oddpool becomes the only neutral cross-venue backtesting source of record ? quant funds pay enterprise S3-dump prices ($100K+/yr per seat) and Oddpool ends up as the Refinitiv of prediction markets at $20M ARR. This is real. It's the same reason Bloomberg won. But Bloomberg owned the terminal hardware and proprietary data feeds; Oddpool's inputs are public APIs that Polymarket has already shown they will revoke or absorb. The dossier explicitly notes "either venue could rate-limit, alter ToS, or revoke commercial redistribution rights. No mitigation disclosed." That sentence wins the argument. Flip condition: if Oddpool announces a long-term commercial data licensing agreement with Kalshi or Polymarket ? not API access, but a paid licensing contract that makes them a partner rather than a parasite ? I'd reopen the file.
