# Imperfect -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Matin Tamizi shipped Garth, an 813-star open-source Garmin Connect client, before he ever pitched Imperfect — that is the one detail in this dossier that activates my framework, and almost everything else in the file works against him. Imperfect is a competent solo operator with a tactile artifact attacking a category that Strava already bought its way into and Garmin already shipped natively. The signature pairing is a clean single-CEO structure at the top of my ladder, mated to a "magically weird" score that's not weird at all — Imperfect is the consensus YC lane I describe in my pass example, except the founder is more interesting than the pitch deck would suggest.

Anduril is my anchor for unambiguous CEO authority: Palmer Luckey walked in with one name on the door and the tiebreaker conversation already had. Imperfect mirrors that structurally — the YC page lists Matin Tamizi as the sole named founder against a five-person team, so the HQ Trivia failure mode of a 50/50 Yusupov-and-Kroll feud is mechanically impossible here. Where Imperfect diverges from the Anduril rung is that solo founders concentrate fundraising, product, and platform-risk negotiation in a single throat, and Matin's prior tour at Balanced ended in a wind-down-and-transition-to-Stripe in March 2015 — clean exit, not a scaled exit. The governance is clean; the bandwidth is the question.

The pass-example failure mode is "consensus YC AI darling with six tier-one funds chasing and a model-as-moat" — and Imperfect is the endurance-coaching version of that script. Strava acquired Runna in April 2025, Garmin shipped Active Intelligence inside Garmin Connect, TriDot has been compounding training data for twenty years, Humango already brands a multi-sport AI coach named Hugo. Imperfect is not laughed-at-by-credible-VCs the way Crusoe burning flare gas was; Imperfect is the obvious thing to build the quarter after Strava bought Runna. I look for two sophisticated investors who have visibly passed on aesthetic or capital-intensity grounds — Imperfect's problem is the opposite, that the category is too obvious, not too weird.

Founder-market fit on the lived-experience axis is where Imperfect earns its real points and also where I have to be honest about a gap. Matin authored Garth and wrote the DZone piece "Building a Modern Training Assistant With Claude and Garmin" before Imperfect was a company — that is the Palmer-Luckey-shipped-Oculus-first pattern in miniature, scaled to a wearable-data hacker rather than a defense-hardware founder. Over 18 months that obsession compounds into integration depth no PM-founder can match in a quarter. The discount: Matin lived the Garmin-API problem more verifiably than he lived the amateur-endurance-athlete-balancing-work-and-life problem the YC blurb describes, and his prior two CEO tours were both in Mexican fintech — Cuenca's $7.4M Stripe-led Series A is operator credibility, not athlete credibility.

Capital-intensity-wise, Imperfect would have to become a hardware company or a regulated medical product for the score to flip a tier — neither is in the dossier. The Mindbloom-and-Vigil-Labs lane I fund is "ugly, regulated, capital-intensive, or aesthetically transgressive," and Imperfect is a consumer subscription app inferred from category norms with no pricing disclosed. If the dossier instead said "Imperfect is pursuing FDA clearance as a Class II software-as-medical-device for cardiac-rehab patients," I'd be on a plane. It doesn't.

Niantic is my parallel on the touch-it-yourself axis: I played Ingress obsessively from 2012 before I ever sat outside John Hanke's door. Imperfect passes the artifact test in principle — a normal human with a Garmin watch can install it inside 48 hours, the beta cohort includes a professional ultramarathoner who placed second at a 50-mile Puerto Vallarta race, and Garth's 813 stars means the integration plumbing is real. But Imperfect lives on top of Garmin's auth flow, and Matin's own library is marked DEPRECATED on GitHub because Garmin changed that auth flow. The artifact is tactile and the substrate is rented.

My 36-month forecast for Imperfect: acqui-hired by Strava, Garmin, or Whoop at under $30M, or quietly wound down, with ARR capping in the $2–5M band before incumbent bundling collapses pricing. The mechanism is the same one that produced the Runna sale — incumbents with distribution and first-party data don't compete on AI coaching, they absorb it, and Matin's wind-down-into-Stripe precedent from Balanced shows he knows how to negotiate that exit cleanly.

The strongest case against me: Matin is a two-time YC-and-a16z-backed CEO who already navigated one platform-dependency landmine at Cuenca with Stripe, and the Garth library is a genuine cross-platform wedge — Strava's Runna is running-only, Garmin's Active Intelligence is Garmin-only, and Imperfect could become the cross-wearable coach that neither incumbent will build because it cannibalizes their first-party data. The bear-side dismantling: Strava bought Runna *because* it intends to go cross-platform, and the substrate Imperfect depends on already broke once — Garth is DEPRECATED in Matin's own README. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict to Invest: a signed integration with Whoop, Oura, or Apple Health on terms Imperfect controls, paired with founder language confirming a regulated-rehab or insurer-reimbursed pivot. Until then this is a clean operator in a lane I do not fund.
