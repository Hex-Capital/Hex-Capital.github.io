# Vestris -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Vestris is a workflow-infrastructure play — replace title searchers, generate Schedule A/B, balance settlement statements — sitting on top of a legitimately hard, regulated, county-fragmented substrate, and its analytical signature is the pairing of a plausible *single-miracle-ish* architecture with a **founder section that literally says "No public data found"** and a team-size field of zero. That pairing tells me what kind of company Vestris is on paper: the Decagon/Abridge shape in title-industry clothing, evaluated blind. In this cycle, blind is a pass by default.

**Anduril** (2022) is my highest-rung comparison for the market-re-opening dimension, and it's the analogy Vestris's dossier is implicitly reaching for — a legacy, paperwork-choked category (title production; defense procurement) cracked open by a specific recent catalyst (browser-agent reliability crossing a threshold in 2025; Ukraine revealing Pentagon software inadequacy). The mechanism is the same shape: software-on-top-of-a-hard-thing harvests a tailwind without carrying the science risk. Where Vestris diverges from the Anduril rung is the specificity of the catalyst. Anduril's catalyst was *category-specific* — DoD budget re-allocation, named Ukraine-driven procurement reform. Vestris's catalyst per the dossier is that Browser Use hit 50k GitHub stars — a *horizontal AI tooling* catalyst that any competitor including Qualia ($207M raised, ~$1B valuation) and Snapdocs ($265M raised, ~$1.5B valuation) can also consume. That's a catalyst for the *category of browser-agent startups*, not specifically for title. Places Vestris between my mid and low rungs on this axis.

**Revenue velocity is where Vestris lands on the floor.** The pass-example failure mode I keep flagging in AI deals is the Harvey-inverse: an AI company where the dossier is silent on the growth curve because there is no curve to disclose. Vestris's dossier says "No public data found" for revenue, 0 open jobs on the YC page, no Product Hunt, no press, no Twitter, no funding announcement, no customer logo. Two live jurisdictions — DC and Montgomery County, MD — is all the traction surface exposed. In the AI cycle "something's fundamentally broken" is my default read on that silence, and I apply it here to Vestris rather than talk myself out of it. If Vestris had a $2K MRR line item or one named title agency counterparty, I'd re-rate the entire deal; the absence isn't stage-appropriate humility, it's the tell.

Founder-ships-between-meetings compounds negatively for Vestris over the next 18 months in a way that's mechanical, not vibes-based: county-by-county browser-agent buildouts are exactly the kind of grinding, unglamorous, tacit-knowledge-heavy work where founder shipping rhythm is the *only* thing that produces a moat. Each new jurisdiction — the dossier says "coming soon" for everything past DC and Montgomery County — is a bespoke integration against a county records system. Whoever the Vestris founders are, they need to be shipping a new county integration roughly every two weeks. The dossier does not name them, does not link a LinkedIn, does not name a prior product they built. My **Perplexity** (2022) filter is "who does that?" — who ships the thing we discussed a week later. I cannot answer that question about Vestris because I cannot identify the founders from the YC page or vestris.ai. That's not stage-appropriate; that's a diligence wall.

Index-like distribution for Vestris flips a tier only if county coverage becomes the underlying protocol every closing has to route through — the counterfactual is that Vestris gets to fifty jurisdictions before Qualia bolts on its own browser agent. **Stripe** (2010) became an index on e-commerce because Stripe *was* the API, and every new merchant category had to touch it. Vestris in DC + Montgomery County is not yet on that path; SoftPro alone has 14,000+ customer sites and Snapdocs "powers 1 in 4 U.S. mortgage transactions." For Vestris to become an index position rather than a share-fight, coverage has to compound faster than incumbents can retrofit — no evidence in the dossier that this is happening.

**Single-miracle math for Vestris compounds unfavorably.** Compare to **Color Genomics** (2015), where I explicitly architected the company as workflow-on-commodity-sequencing so the miracle was one layer (data/workflow), not chemistry. Vestris needs (a) browser agents reliable enough to survive county-portal drift, (b) title agencies willing to cannibalize their own searcher labor line — which is billed revenue, not just cost, per the dossier's own risk section — and (c) an underwriter/E&O posture that survives a bad Schedule B. Three miracles, each independent, none disclosed as solved. That's the NFX *Unusual Signs* compounding-small-odds trap I try to avoid. This is why Vestris is not the Decagon/Abridge template even though it dresses like it.

My 36-month forecast on Vestris: dead or acqui-hired for sub-$20M by Qualia or SoftPro (Fidelity National Financial) for the county browser-agent stack, having reached fewer than eight live jurisdictions and under $500K ARR. The mechanism producing it is the customer-labor cannibalization + county-integration-drag pincer — Vestris's ICP is an agency whose P&L includes searcher fees, so the enterprise pilot rate converts poorly, while the horizontal browser-agent commoditization (Browser Use, Hyperbrowser, Asteroid) lets incumbents ship a "good enough" version before Vestris hits meaningful coverage.

The bull case against my forecast is the *AI Market Clarity* one: title production is a "still wide open" back-office AI sub-category with no declared winner, and the customer-cannibalization argument is the same objection people made about **Harvey** (2023) versus BigLaw associate hours — Harvey ran anyway because the buyer is the partner, not the associate. If the Vestris founders turn out to be ex-Qualia or ex-First American with the internal relationships to close two mid-market agencies in the next quarter, this is exactly the Decagon (2023) shape and I'd re-rate to Invest. What sinks the bull case for Vestris today is not the market — the market is fine — it's that I cannot name the founders, cannot cite a customer, and cannot verify a shipping cadence, which means I'm being asked to underwrite three independent miracles blind. Flip condition, precise: a named title-industry-veteran founder with a prior shipped product, plus one signed paying agency logo disclosed in the next 90 days. Absent that, Vestris is a pass at consensus pre-seed pricing.
