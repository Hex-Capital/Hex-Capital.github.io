# Incandor -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Incandor is the dimensional inverse of the deals I write checks on: a credentialed Stanford pair attacking a category where the winners are already declared, with the dossier itself noting BioCatch at $185M ARR (Q4 2025) and 90 new customers in 2025 including Wells Fargo. The highest signal in Incandor is a plausible *engineering* claim (label-free cold-start clustering); the lowest is any evidence the founders ship between conversations or that the market just re-opened. That pairing -- technical-thesis-on-top, founder-shipping-and-catalyst-on-bottom -- is the exact pattern *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) tags as "winners declared, don't enter at consensus prices."

On Market Re-Opening, the ladder runs Anduril 2022 (Ukraine + Pentagon software gap in a 24-month window) at the top, Decagon 2023 (post-LLM customer support workflow opening) in the middle, and Lyft Series C at the bottom (steady-state oligopoly, no catalyst). Incandor sits at the Lyft rung, not the Decagon rung: the dossier admits "None of these catalysts are attributed by Incandor directly" and the cited drivers (GenAI synthetic identity, APP fraud regulation) are inferred by the researcher, not claimed by Incandor. Behavioral biometrics has been a named category since BioCatch's founding -- the Mordor 26.95% CAGR is a steady-state compound, not a re-opening. This is the inverse of my Stripe (2010) "merchant-accounts-replaced-by-APIs" entry pattern; there is no recent step-function Incandor is skating toward.

On Founder Ships Iteratively, the ladder runs Aravind at Perplexity 2022 ("showed up a week later with the thing we discussed built") at the top, Brex 2017 (operationally-disciplined CEO archetype) in the middle, and generic FAANG-pedigree-no-artifacts at the bottom. Incandor sits closer to the bottom rung: Matthew Yekell's background is Vista Equity, Cboe, Stanford Management Company -- finance and capital allocation, not product shipping -- and Luc Rosenzweig's NVIDIA/Apple/Epic stints are systems engineering at scale with zero named shipped artifacts, zero public GitHub repos, and no public Twitter for Rosenzweig at all. "Has this founder actually shipped products before -- not just at a big company, but independently?" (Hustle Commons summary) is the disqualifier here. Yekell brings banking-buyer credibility, which is real, but credibility isn't shipping rhythm.

Revenue Velocity is where I most quickly fold. Harvey 2023's "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch Nov 2025) sets the high rung; Perplexity's visible early usage curve is the mid; the low is any AI-adjacent company silent on growth -- "something's fundamentally broken." Incandor's dossier states plainly "No public revenue, customer logos, design partners, press coverage, or user counts found" and 0 open jobs on the YC page. Pre-seed dossiers can be light, but a B2B fraud-API targeting banks with zero named design partners after a YC batch is itself the signal.

On Index-Like Distribution, Stripe 2010 ("index on e-commerce") and Coinbase 2013 ("basket of every cryptocurrency") anchor the top, Decagon's workflow-path-in-LLM-CS sits middle, and Lyft as #2 in winner-take-all anchors the bottom. Incandor lands near Lyft: BioCatch already sits in the bank fraud-ops workflow path with $185M ARR, NuData is bundled inside Mastercard's authentication, and BehavioSec is inside LexisNexis -- Incandor must *displace*, not ride. On Single-Miracle, Decagon (LLM-accuracy-given, execute GTM) sits high and fusion-style multi-breakthrough sits low; Incandor requires (a) unsupervised label-free clustering at production-grade accuracy, (b) bank fraud-ops teams adopting a programmable-query API instead of a score they've consumed for a decade, and (c) channel displacement of incumbents inside Permira and Mastercard. That's three independent miracles compounded, not one -- this is exactly the math I refuse to underwrite.

My 36-month forecast for Incandor: acquihired into a broader fraud platform -- Sardine, Alloy, or Unit21 -- for under $40M in 2027–2028, after failing to land a tier-1 bank logo against BioCatch's procurement lock and burning through a Series A bridge. The mechanism is the *AI Market Clarity* pattern: in "winners declared" sub-markets, well-credentialed seed entrants with technical differentiation but no distribution wedge end up as feature acquisitions for the platforms that already own the buyer relationship.

The strongest bull case against my forecast: Sardine raised $145M and grew ARR 130% YoY (BusinessWire Feb 2025) precisely by entering this same "declared-winner" category through the fintech/neobank flank where BioCatch's bank-procurement strength is irrelevant, and Incandor's no-label cold-start is exactly the wedge a new neobank or crypto exchange with zero fraud history would need. If Rosenzweig's systems background actually produces a working label-free clusterer, Incandor has a real Decagon-style workflow-infrastructure shot in the fintech-not-bank segment. The reason that case loses: Sardine arrived in 2020 with Crossriver/Coinbase founder networks and shipped a fraud product within months -- Yekell and Rosenzweig have no comparable shipping artifact or named design partner in a YC batch already underway, and the dossier's >99% accuracy claim has no benchmark, no dataset, no third-party validation. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a signed design partnership with a top-20 US neobank or top-5 crypto exchange announced within 60 days, with cold-start performance documented against a labeled holdout. Absent that, this is a pass -- a competent Stanford team in a category where I have already named the winners.
