# Andustry -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Andustry is a two-person AI-agent broker for industrial sourcing pitching itself as "Alibaba on AI" -- which means the single most informative thing about Andustry isn't the $400K-saved testimonial on a $1.2M CNC order, it's that Het Dave and Johann Stürken are running an asset-light commission-only marketplace with zero owned distribution into a category where Xometry just printed $687M of FY2025 revenue. The signature: potentially capital-efficient structure paired with a complete absence of founder-brand or community on the cap table. That pairing tells me Andustry's entire alpha rides on cold outbound execution against incumbents who are deploying the same LLM agent stack the founders are deploying.

The highest rung on my capital-structure ladder is **Teamshares** -- an unusual instrument (employee-equity rollup) wrapping a boring asset (small-business cash flows) into something only Slow could underwrite. Andustry's commission-only model (andustry.com, no rate disclosed) shares the asset-light, no-inventory shape that makes a two-person team plausibly default-alive on real take-rate: 1% commission on $100M of brokered GMV is $1M of revenue with two heads. Where Andustry diverges from Teamshares is that the instrument here is a vanilla SAFE on a vanilla marketplace -- nothing about the financing structure is non-standard, and the same product that creates supplier price transparency for SME buyers is the product that compresses Andustry's commission take-rate over time as buyers learn the real prices. Teamshares' structure *was* the moat; Andustry's structure is just a thin layer of margin on top of someone else's negotiation.

On founder-as-brand, the failure pattern I associate with this dimension is the canonical 2026 YC pass: founders with no Twitter, no GitHub, no public writing, no community footprint, depending entirely on paid acquisition or hired sales. Andustry's dossier reproduces that pattern almost exactly -- "No public account found" for both Het Dave and Johann Stürken on Twitter, no GitHub repos, LinkedIn follower counts not even retrievable. **John Fish / Bookshelved** got my check because the YouTuber distribution pre-existed the company by years; Andustry has no analog. The Stürken family trading business is the closest thing to owned distribution in the dossier, and it's a private B2B relationship graph, not a published audience -- useful for sourcing the first ten suppliers, not for compounding into a category-defining brand.

Pricing-and-consensus mechanism: Andustry is a Spring 2026 YC company describing itself as "AI agents discover suppliers... and return standardized comparable offers within 24-48 hours" -- which is the literal template from my Pass Example ("AI agent for [enterprise vertical]"). The dossier discloses no round size, no valuation, no participating investors beyond YC, and the absence is itself the signal: silence on round dynamics in a Tom Blomfield-partnered P26 batch defaults to consensus seed pricing on a consensus thesis, and 18 months of "AI agents for procurement" decks have made this the modal YC pitch, not a contrarian one. There is no tier-1 visible pass, no strategic ambiguity, no illegibility for me to story-broker later.

Founder-conviction counterfactual: for Andustry to flip a tier on this dimension, I would need to see Het Dave or Johann Stürken with 3+ years of public writing on industrial sourcing -- a Substack on supplier negotiation dynamics, a thread of customs/QC war stories from the Stürken family business, anything indicating they were running this manually as a Wizard-of-Oz operation before AI agents became feasible. What the dossier actually shows is ex-McKinsey plus CDTM plus AppliedAI -- the credential stack of mercenaries, not missionaries. The family trading-goods business is the one genuine domain anchor and it goes uncited in any opinionated public artifact.

Category-sleeve parallel: the closest portfolio analog for Andustry is not a sleeve I'm convicted on, it's **Ro** -- the Known Exception where capital intensity itself was the moat because regulatory licensing across 50 state prescription rails could only be cleared with venture dollars. Andustry has no equivalent moat; customs, freight, and QC across Europe/China/India/US are real schleps, but they're schleps Xometry has been clearing for a decade and that any LLM-agent team can rebuild. The Ro override doesn't apply because Andustry's "venture moment" claim rests on AI capability, not on a regulatory thicket only patient capital can navigate.

36-month forecast: Andustry reaches $2-6M of annual commission revenue on roughly $100-300M of brokered GMV through outbound to mid-market US/EU manufacturers, fails to raise a competitive Series A as Xometry and MISUMI/Fictiv layer agent workflows over their existing supplier networks, and either acqui-hires into a Geomiq-tier platform or quietly winds down by mid-2029 -- the mechanism being that an asset-light broker with no proprietary supply, no proprietary demand, and no proprietary data loses the third financing tack of the regatta to incumbents with all three.

The bull case that stings: Stürken's family trading-goods business is genuine lived-in domain access that no Xometry product manager has, Dave's AppliedAI work on agent evaluation at FINN is real agent infrastructure expertise, and the dossier's $400K-saved and $800K-saved testimonials on multi-hundred-thousand-dollar orders are not vapor -- they're real cash savings on real industrial transactions, which is the kind of unsexy execution-machine signal I claim to weight. If Andustry compounds the supplier-quote dataset into pricing intelligence that Xometry's custom-parts focus can't replicate (off-the-shelf machines and raw materials sit in a different SKU universe than CNC parts), this becomes a Sublime-Security-shaped pre-consolidation enterprise seed. What dismantles the bull case is the commission-only economics under buyer-driven price transparency plus the absence of any non-standard instrument, founder-brand, or pre-consensus pricing to give me edge on entry. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Andustry discloses a sub-$10M post on this round AND surfaces $500K+ of monthly commission revenue from named SME buyers within six months -- at that point the price-discipline filter and the default-alive filter both pass and I revisit.
