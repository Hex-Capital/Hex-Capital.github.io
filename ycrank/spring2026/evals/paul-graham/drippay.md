# Drip -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Drip's YC page sells "enterprise contract operations," usedrip.ai sells a Mac app called "dripos" that indexes your Gmail and iMessage, and drippay.dev now redirects to something called "Memra: customer memory for B2B founders." Three positionings, three buyers, one two-person team in the Spring 2026 batch. The dimensional signature of Drip is straightforward: the highest thing about it is that Mike Levin worked on metered billing at GitHub before starting a company whose YC pitch includes billing as a pillar, and the lowest thing about it is that the live product is a local Mac index over chat.db and Stripe — which is exactly the kind of pure-software thin layer that smart founders reach for when they are unconsciously routing *around* the schlep, not toward it.

Mike Levin's GitHub metered-billing background is the most loaded fact in the Drip dossier on organic-idea fit, and it is the rung I keep returning to. **Stripe** (2010) is the analogy that does the work here: the Collisons had been hacking commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007), and the API they shipped was the API they themselves had wanted. Drip's Levin shipping usage-tracking systems at GitHub and then pitching pricing/entitlements/billing unification is a real founder-problem map — not invented, not consultant-flavored. But the analogy stops where the product stops mirroring the biography: the live usedrip.ai surface is "customer history for your next move," a Mac app indexing iMessage and Calendar, which is not the billing problem Levin lived. The Collisons did not pivot from payments to a Mac sidebar. Drip's biography pins it above the default but well below the rung; Levin's pain is real but Drip is no longer solving it.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the low-rung company I read against Drip on schlep. Brian Armstrong won the batch on appetite for bitcoin compliance grind — work other applicants flinched from. The schlep in Drip's stated category is procurement workflow, legal review cycles, SOC2 in enterprise data rooms, six-to-nine-month CLM implementations against Conga and Ironclad. Drip's actual shipping artifact reads chat.db locally and calls Stripe's API. That is not a schlep; that is a weekend hackathon for any competent engineer, and the dossier itself notes "a small team can replicate a local Mac index over Gmail/Slack/Stripe APIs." Worse, the same dataset is addressable by Salesforce Revenue Cloud and by every other AI-CRM peer in the Spring 2026 batch. Drip is sitting on the easy side of its own pitch.

Drip's animal-resourcefulness signal compounds backwards over the next eighteen months on mechanism: the drippay.dev → Memra → dripos rebrand churn is either a founder running stunts to find the wedge (Airbnb-coded) or a founder iterating because nothing is sticking (Kiko-coded). The dossier gives no cereal-box equivalent — no named revenue stunt, no demo that hit Hacker News, no LOI with a procurement lead at a named enterprise — so I cannot read the pivots as animal moves. With no Product Hunt launch ranked, no press, no public account on X for either founder, the churn reads closer to "searching for a problem" than "manufacturing demand."

Drip's hacker-genealogy score would have to flip on one specific counterfactual: a GitHub repo from Levin or Lucas He with non-trivial stars, a competitive-programming finish, or an open-source artifact predating the company. The dossier names @MichaelLevin5908 with "no notable repo with star count found," and Lucas He is undisambiguable across LinkedIn and GitHub — the dossier cannot even confirm he exists as a public technical entity. **Reddit** (2005) had Aaron Swartz with the RSS spec; **Stripe** (2010) had Patrick Collison with the Young Scientist Lisp prize. Drip's dossier has UCLA CS class of 2026 and a missing CTO trail. Hackers leave tracks.

Drip's co-founder pair sits at neutral by parallel to no specific anchor — two founders, role coverage (founder + technical co-founder), and absolutely no public data on whether Levin and He roomed together, hacked together, or met three weeks before applying. The Huffman/Ohanian dorm-roommate pattern from **Reddit** (2005) is the configuration I want to see; the dossier neither confirms nor denies it, so the in-person interview would have to surface it.

My 36-month forecast for Drip: a third pivot lands them at sub-$500K ARR by Q2 2028, followed by either a quiet acquihire into a mid-market CRM (Attio, Clay, or a Salesforce ecosystem buyer) or a soft shutdown — driven by the same mechanism the dossier already exposes, which is that the team is iterating across buyer segments (enterprise procurement → Mac power-users → B2B founders) without yet hitting a problem either founder personally suffered in a way that produces conviction. Levin's GitHub billing pain is real but Drip is no longer building for it.

The strongest case against my forecast is the **Airbnb** (2009) precedent applied to the pivots: Chesky and Gebbia rebranded from AirBedAndBreakfast, ran cereal-box financing, looked dumb at every step, and the churn was the animal behavior. Maybe drippay → Memra → dripos is the same pattern compressed into six months — founders sprinting through ideas until one sticks. The Known Exception that could apply is **Loopt** (2005), where I funded Sam Altman on force-of-personality before mobile location worked. The bear case wins on one piece of evidence: Airbnb's pivots were accompanied by a stunt I would not have thought of, and Altman's animal-test result was something I felt in the room. Drip's dossier contains no analogous artifact — no $40 cereal box, no Hacker News demo. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a single named enterprise LOI or a public Hacker News thread where Levin's working product gets organic upvotes from buyers, not friends. Until then, Drip is pass-shaped.
