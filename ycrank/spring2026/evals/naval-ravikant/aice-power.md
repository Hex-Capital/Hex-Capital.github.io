# AICE Power -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

AICE Power is a two-person hardware company building swarm UUVs for defense procurement -- ITAR-gated, capital-leverage-shaped, sold into multi-year DIU/SBIR cycles -- whose highest-scoring dimension (founder intelligence: MIT AeroAstro + ENS Paris-Saclay) cannot rescue its lowest (leverage architecture, where the entire business runs on permission from the U.S. Navy and the French export-control regime). The dimensional signature is "smart aerospace generalists pivoted 120 degrees on May 16, 2026 into the most permissioned distribution channel on Earth" -- Greg Chomette and Rémi Bouteiller are sharp, but AICE Power is structurally the opposite of what my ledger funds.

The leverage check is where AICE Power ends before it begins. **Anchorage Digital** is the only permission-gated bet on my sheet, and it cleared the bar only because I had been compounding crypto-category conviction since co-founding **MetaStable** in 2014 -- the regulatory moat itself flipped into structural leverage *because I owned a multi-year pre-consensus thesis underneath it*. AICE Power has no equivalent underwriting in my book: I have not been quietly accumulating into subsea defense since 2014, I have no Spearhead founder pulling subsea deal flow, and the dossier itself names ITAR/export-control compliance across the France?U.S. manufacturing border as a *cost*, not a moat. Code and media are permissionless leverage; Kongsberg-class subsea hardware sold through Pentagon contracting officers is the canonical example I use of capital-and-labor leverage that requires permission. AICE Power diverges from the **Anchorage** override at exactly the place that mattered -- the pre-existing category conviction is absent.

The marketplace dimension is the cleanest LOW-rung pattern in the dossier. Single-sided vertical SaaS without network mechanics is what I default-score below midpoint because the Nth customer does not make the (N-1) prior customers more valuable -- and a submarine drone sold to the U.S. Navy compounds nothing for the Royal Australian Navy, which is precisely why **Anduril's** $1.7B Ghost Shark contract and its $99M DIU Dive-XL contract are stacked sequentially rather than networked together. AICE Power's swarm-coordination software is offered as a hypothetical "future moat" with no shipped artifact; the actual revenue object is a hull, a sensor stack, and a procurement relationship. The Nth Navy buyer does not increase the value of the previous Navy buyer. Asymmetric upside math at angel stage requires the 10,000x geometry that **Uber's** ~4,000x exit made operationally real, and AICE Power does not have the user-value-compounding architecture that produces it.

The specific-knowledge filter is where AICE Power's resume cuts against the pivot. Chomette's MIT AeroAstro thesis with Raul Radovitzky on deep learning for jet-engine signals is real specific knowledge -- in *aerospace*. Bouteiller's founding-engineer work at Amira Health on the Terra hot-flash wearable is real specific knowledge -- in *consumer biosensors*. The dossier candidly notes neither founder's public record shows prior naval, subsea, or DoD-procurement experience. Eighteen months of compounding in subsea defense will not close the gap to **Anduril**, where Palmer Luckey hired Navy program veterans before raising the $4B round at $60B in March 2026. The "120? pivot on May 16, 2026" surfaced in pivotarchive.com is exactly the *responding-to-a-competitor* pattern my Red Flag list names -- the **Notion** / **Stack Overflow** / **Replit** origins are founders who discovered a problem as users; AICE Power discovered submarine drones as a market.

Pivot-room in a defense vertical is a counterfactual exercise. For AICE Power's TAM score to flip a tier, the $5.80B?$13.40B 2024?2034 military UUV market would need to function like consumer media did for **Twitter's** Odeo pivot -- adjacent categories absorbing two or three founder rebuilds inside a single seed runway. It does not. Each pivot vector inside the UUV market (mine countermeasures ? cable protection ? ASW patrol) requires a fresh ITAR classification, a fresh program-of-record relationship, and a fresh multi-year procurement cycle that the asdnews.com sourcing on the DIU?CAMP 2024?2026 timeline already telegraphs. AICE Power has pivot-room only in the sense that **Twitter** had pivot-room *if* each pivot took two years and required a security clearance.

The Buffett trio reads clean on AICE Power. Chomette's NASA asteroid-risk work and Radovitzky thesis signal intelligence and energy; Bouteiller shipped a six-figure-ARR sensor business before the pivot; no integrity contestation surfaces in the dossier, and neither founder is volunteering their honesty unprompted, which is the inverse Red Flag I actually watch for. This is not the **Clearview** exception -- there is no extreme specific-knowledge override here to flex the integrity bar around. The trio reads as the kind of European technical co-founders I would happily talk to for a decade about something else.

My 36-month forecast on AICE Power: lands one DIU/SBIR/Phase II contract in the $1M?$5M range, never crosses $10M ARR, and either gets acqui-hired into Saronic or HavocAI for the engineering team or runs out of runway before the second contract cycle closes. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- smart non-domain founders in a permission-gated single-sided market against **Anduril** ($1B 2024 revenue, $4B fresh capital) and **Saronic** ($1.75B Series D at $9.25B) cannot generate the asymmetric upside math that justifies an angel check at my filter.

The strongest bull case on AICE Power is the **Anchorage** precedent flipped forward: Replicator-era Pentagon demand for distributed subsea autonomy is a category-timing call, and ITAR is the moat, not the tax -- two French aerospace technicals with no incumbent baggage could be exactly the asymmetric profile DIU wants to fund around. That case stings until I notice the missing piece -- **Anchorage** worked because I had eleven years of crypto conviction underneath the regulated-custody bet, and I have no equivalent pre-consensus defense thesis in my ledger; the leverage-architecture exception does not transfer to a category I have not been dollar-cost-averaging into. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a named DoD program-of-record contract above $10M with AICE Power as prime (not subcontractor) inside the next 12 months, paired with a hire of a former Navy UUV program officer onto the founding team. Absent both, AICE Power is a pass at angel check size -- the downside is one X, but the upside math is capped by the procurement ceiling, and capped upside math is the filter I actually fund against.
