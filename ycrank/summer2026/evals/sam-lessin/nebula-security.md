# Nebula Security -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Nebula Security is a two-person offensive-research shop with real CVE credits ? CVE-2026-6307 Chrome V8 sandbox escape, CVE-2026-5865 V8 RCE, a $10,500 Linux kernel bounty on CVE-2026-23274 ? that just wandered into the single most consensus-priced YC S26 category on the board, where XBOW is already sitting at >$1B post a $120M Series C plus $35M extension (Bloomberg / GeekWire, Mar?May 2026). The dimensional signature that matters for Nebula Security: highest on technical/founder credibility (the writeups are real), lowest on round dynamics (this is "AI-native security" pitched to a market that just funded the $237M-raised incumbent). That pairing ? credentialed operators pitching into a resolved consensus curve ? is exactly the profile where my alpha goes to zero.

**Drop.io** is my honest HIGH rung for founder conviction: I spent years bootstrapping a file-sharing primitive against free incumbents because I had a specific non-obvious belief about ephemeral sharing, and the missionary posture was legible in public artifacts long before the seed. Nebula Security's Eten Zou has the raw substance ? the V8 sandbox escape writeup on nebusec.ai/research is not a marketing artifact, it is a real piece of vulnerability research ? but the dossier flags that the "first nginx RCE" credit in the YC blurb maps to CVE-2026-42945 credited to "depthfirst," not to Nebula, and Zou has no retrievable Twitter, no LinkedIn, no GitHub, no conference-talk trail. In offensive security, that absence is loud: the researchers I want to back publish under their own name because the reputation IS the product. Nebula Security has the CVE, but not the operator-brand around it, which puts Zou below Drop.io on the missionary-legibility axis and materially below where a Sublime Security founder sat when I underwrote that check.

The failure mechanism of the canonical 2026 YC pitch ? "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, $30M post, four tier-1s already in" ? is that the round price already encodes everything the founder can plausibly execute against, so my SAFE cap generates no alpha even if the team ships. Nebula Security is not quite that pitch: it's YC standard $500K in USDC (cryptobriefing.com, Jun 2026), not a $30M post, so the entry price itself is fine. What is not fine is the *category* price ? XBOW at >$1B, Semgrep at ~$5B secondary (Clay, 2026), Snyk at $8.5B ? which means Nebula Security's Series A buyer is being asked to underwrite category exposure that is already fully priced two tiers up. That is the "AI is just more internet" trap operationalized: the seed is cheap, but the exit curve is expensive to reach because the consolidation has already happened. Nebula Security fails the value-seed price test not at entry but at the *next* tack of the regatta.

On capital structure over the next 18 months, Nebula Security's services-plus-SaaS shape ? the "Audited by Nebula Security" mark alongside VEGA scanning ? mechanically compounds in an interesting direction and a scary one simultaneously. The interesting direction: a boutique audit practice on top of an AI scanner is closer to Trail of Bits economics than to a pure SaaS burn curve, which means Nebula Security could reach $3?5M ARR on the two founders plus two engineers without a Series A, i.e. default-alive without another tack. The scary direction: XBOW's $237M and Semgrep's $196M mean the SaaS side of Nebula Security is competing against war chests that will discount VEGA-equivalent scanning toward zero, forcing Nebula Security into the audit-services corner where the multiple compresses to 3?5x revenue and the venture math dies. The financing instrument here is generic YC-standard SAFE, not a structure that captures the services-cashflow reality of the business ? that mismatch is the actual capital-structure problem.

Counterfactually, for Nebula Security's founder-brand dimension to flip from LOW to MID, Eten Zou or the un-named second co-founder would need one of: a verifiable Pwn2Own placement, a Black Hat or DEF CON talk under their real name, a HackerOne top-100 profile, or a personal research blog with pre-Nebula publishing history. None of those appear in the dossier ? the X account @nebusecurity joined February 2026, the LinkedIn page has 87 followers and is actually name-colliding with a stealth LA company founded 2022 at nebulasecurity.io, and no founder-level personal accounts surfaced. Any one of those four artifacts would materially change how the "Audited by Nebula Security" mark compounds as a distribution primitive, because in security the audit brand IS the researcher's name. Absent that, Nebula Security is scored on the LinkedIn 87 followers and the X-account-joined-Feb-2026, which is exactly where the founder-as-brand default lives.

**Sublime Security** is the single-anchor parallel for category sleeve, and it flatters Nebula Security less than it looks. I entered Sublime Security when enterprise email security was mid-consolidation and the category was still legible to Series A buyers without an AI-native tag. Nebula Security enters "AI-native code security" *after* XBOW's Bloomberg-covered unicorn round, which is the difference between buying the sleeve before it's named and buying it after it's already been re-rated. Category-sleeve fit exists ? enterprise security is on my map ? but the timing inversion means Nebula Security is buying exposure to a resolved curve, not a pre-consensus one, which is the whole thing I try not to do.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Nebula Security gets acqui-hired by Snyk, Semgrep, or a mid-tier IR firm for $15?40M on the strength of the CVE research team ? Zou and the second co-founder end up as the offensive-research nucleus inside a bigger platform, VEGA gets folded into an existing SAST product line, and the "Audited by Nebula Security" mark quietly disappears. The mechanism producing that outcome: two-person team scope risk (dossier flags this explicitly against XBOW's Copilot-alumni engineering bench) plus a resolved category price that closes the independent-Series-A window, which routes Nebula Security's real value ? the CVE-discovery pipeline ? into the strategic-acquirer market rather than the venture-exit market.

The bear-to-my-forecast case: Nebula Security is actually Trail of Bits or NCC Group at seed ? a research-brand-led boutique that reaches $20?30M in audit revenue at 70% margin without ever needing a Series A, in which case my services-as-fatal frame is exactly the "lifestyle business" dismissal I've spent a decade telling other funds to stop making, and the Squarespace / Muck Rack / Zapier line applies. This stings because it's structurally the shape I claim to buy. The dossier evidence that wins for the pass: Trail of Bits and NCC compounded a *named* researcher brand over a decade of public talks and open-source contributions; Nebula Security's public artifacts are three CVE writeups on a domain registered in the last year, an X account four months old, and zero founder-name distribution. The boutique-audit thesis requires the *person* on the cap table, and I cannot find the person. Flip condition: if I can verify Zou or the second co-founder personally holds three or more pre-Nebula public CVE credits under a stable researcher handle, and the S26 round admits a structural side-letter treating the audit revenue as a distinct cashflow ? call it a services-carveout with founder-friendly economics ? I'll re-enter the conversation. Absent that, pass.
