# Savant -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Savant is the analytical mirror of every "Glean-but-for-AI-agents" pitch I've passed on in the last six months -- a clean single-miracle architecture (LLM-grounded context retrieval, one capability assumed, the rest GTM) paired with the lowest possible revenue-velocity signal (no logos, no pricing page, no Product Hunt, no LinkedIn follower count even retrievable, per dossier). Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu have built what looks structurally like a **Decagon** (2023) at the architecture layer, except Decagon entered a sub-category I had publicly named as "still wide open" in *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) and Savant is entering one where Glean ($300M ARR, May 2026 TechCrunch) and Dust (3,000+ orgs, 300K agents, theaiinsider.tech) are already the named consensus winners.

On market re-opening, the obvious analogue is **Decagon** (2023) -- post-LLM customer support was a genuine 24-month catalyst I funded into, and Savant is invoking the structurally identical claim: enterprise agent deployment in 2025-26 created demand for grounded operational context. The mechanism is real -- Dust's 300K deployed agents (theaiinsider.tech, May 2026) and Glean's ARR tripling from $100M to $300M in 15 months (TechCrunch, May 2026) are exactly the curve I'd want to see for a "now" thesis. But Savant arrives at the catalyst's window with two well-capitalized incumbents already harvesting it on the same integration surface Savant lists (Slack, Salesforce, Google Workspace, Office 365, per heysavant.com) -- the catalyst is real, the window-position is third or fourth.

The **Lyft** Series C pattern -- #2 in a category where #1 has already compounded distribution -- is the failure mechanism Savant's index dimension keeps brushing against. Lyft wasn't a bad company; it was structurally locked out of the economics that mattered because Uber had distribution lock-in on the same supply pool. Glean at $300M ARR and Dust at 3,000+ organizations are indexing the same Salesforce-Slack-Workspace surface Savant's homepage advertises, with Microsoft 365 Copilot, Atlassian Rovo, and Notion Custom Agents bundling the layer for free into the suites Savant would otherwise integrate against (Coworker AI 2026). Savant's stated differentiation -- capturing "undocumented procedures and judgment calls" (heysavant.com) -- is the wedge that *might* invert the pattern, but the dossier offers zero benchmark, case study, or customer testimonial validating that the mechanism even works.

On revenue velocity -- mechanism analysis over 18 months -- Savant's zero-disclosure posture compounds badly. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and the asymmetry is that real growth is trivial to disclose. Pre-seed silence is stage-default; pre-seed silence in a category where Glean publicly markets a 3x ARR jump and Dust publicly markets agent counts is competitive silence. Eighteen months from now, Savant either has a named Fortune 500 procedure-capture deployment (in which case the curve will be loud) or it has converged on a horizontal Glean-clone where the only differentiator is price, and the M365 Copilot bundle has eaten the floor.

On founder shipping -- counterfactual -- the dossier on Anthony Kim (Yale EECS) and Kevin Yu (Cornell CS) is pure pedigree: no GitHub repos surfaced, no Twitter, no prior shipped products named, no co-founder shared-history disclosed. For this score to flip a tier, the dossier would need either (a) a named prior product one of them built and iterated on with user feedback -- the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind-pings-LinkedIn pattern -- or (b) a working prototype with logged usage from an AI-native enterprise that I could verify. Neither exists in what's been gathered. The absent-evidence default holds, and in pre-seed AI, "shipped between meetings" is the only legible founder signal that beats incumbent capital.

On single-miracle architecture -- single-anchor parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015) -- Savant is doing the thing I structurally prefer: workflow infrastructure on top of a hard layer (the LLM) rather than betting on the hard layer itself. The miracle Savant assumes is roughly "an LLM-grounded retrieval system can capture and surface undocumented procedural knowledge from heterogeneous SaaS sources." That's one miracle, not three. This is genuinely the strongest dimension in the dossier and the reason Savant doesn't drop into Strong Pass territory -- the architecture is clean, the science risk is near-zero, the remaining work is GTM and product execution against named, well-funded incumbents.

Thirty-six months out, Savant is an acqui-hire to Notion, Atlassian, or Box in the sub-$50M range -- the procedure-capture IP gets folded into a suite that already owns the distribution, Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu join the acquirer's AI team, and the standalone brand disappears because "Savant" never decoupled from Glean and Dust in the buyer's mental model. The mechanism is straightforward: clean architecture without disclosed traction in a category with declared consensus leaders gets absorbed by the platforms whose bundling risk Savant cannot price around.

The strongest bear-of-the-bear argument is that "memory layer" is genuinely a different category from "search layer," and Glean's $300M ARR is on document retrieval, not procedural-judgment capture -- the way **Decagon** (2023) carved a real wedge inside what looked like crowded customer-support territory. If Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu have actually built the mechanism that extracts unwritten procedures from Salesforce-Slack-Zendesk traffic, that's a Decagon-shaped opening inside a Glean-shaped market, and the consensus-winner framing in *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) was about KM-as-search, not KM-as-memory. The dossier dismantles this for me: heysavant.com offers the claim with zero benchmark, zero customer testimonial, and zero technical artifact validating the capture mechanism -- and at Savant's stage, the mechanism is exactly what should be shippable and demoable, not promised. Flip condition: a named, verifiable enterprise deployment within six months where Savant's procedure-capture is producing measurably better agent outcomes than Glean's grounding -- in which case I'd reopen this immediately, because the Decagon parallel would then load.
