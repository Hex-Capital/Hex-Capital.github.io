# Andco -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Ryan Xue and Michael Lemm previously shipped Layup (YC W23) through to a Whatfix exit, and that is the load-bearing fact about Andco: this is a repeat-YC founder team with a Harvard-Law/Kirkland & Ellis COO running a hustle-mode GTM ("200 boxes of donuts" into law firm lobbies, No Cap Blog 2026) into a real but multi-front agentic-retrieval problem. The signature of Andco is execution-rhythm high, single-miracle architecture low — a team I would trust to ship, attacking a surface area (fax + postal mail + voice calls + insurance portals + Filevine sync) that requires several things to work simultaneously rather than one.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the founder-shipping template I return to — Aravind Srinivas would "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and the reason I cold-funded it was that shipping rhythm was the only legible pre-revenue signal. Andco's Xue/Lemm pairing has the analogous artifact set: a prior YC company taken to acquisition, Xue's pre-Layup operating reps at Origin (100K+ users across 40 countries), and a GTM motion (DoorDash-cosplay donut delivery to PI firm lobbies, No Cap Blog 2026) that is the offline equivalent of shipping between meetings. Where Andco diverges from the Perplexity rung is that the shipped artifact at Andco is a *services-shaped* agent layer rather than a consumer surface I can poke at — "hundreds of cases and thousands of requests monthly" (YC LinkedIn, 2026) is shipping evidence, but it's mediated, not direct. Closer to Perplexity than to a pedigree-only seed, but not Perplexity.

**Color Genomics** (2015) is my reminder of why the single-miracle test matters: I architected Color as workflow-on-top-of-commodity-sequencing precisely because betting on chemistry *and* clinical adoption *and* payer reimbursement compounds three small probabilities into a near-zero. Andco's dossier asks me to underwrite at least three independent miracles: (a) AI agents that reliably operate fax, postal mail, and voice calls to thousands of insurance-carrier and medical-provider intake desks where no public benchmark exists for accuracy; (b) state-by-state UPL and HIPAA posture on AI-driven communication with adjusters and providers, on which the dossier finds "no public data"; and (c) firm-side adoption against EvenUp's $385M war chest and 2,000-firm footprint. That is not the **Decagon** (2023) profile, where the miracle was "LLM at customer support task X" and the rest was GTM. Andco is closer to a multi-front engineering bet wearing an agent-wrapper jacket.

**Market re-opening** for Andco — mechanism analysis. The catalyst the dossier names is real: multi-modal voice and document models crossed the reliability threshold for fax/mail/voice retrieval in 2024–2025, which is the same step-function that drove EvenUp's 5x ARR growth and 90%-of-new-sales-from-AI metric (Lightspeed blog, 2025; Fortune Oct 2025). Over the next 18 months that catalyst compounds in EvenUp's and Eve Legal's favor more than in Andco's, because retrieval is a feature they will absorb into their existing $2B-valuation and $1B+-valuation distribution, whereas Andco must convert the catalyst into a standalone wedge before the incumbents collapse the upstream layer. A real window, but not a window Andco uniquely owns.

**Index-like distribution** for Andco — counterfactual. PI law is fragmented (50,435 firms, no firm above 5% share, IBISWorld 2025) and every PI workup must touch fax, mail, voice, and Filevine — that is structurally an index-like position on PI case volume, the **Stripe**-on-e-commerce shape in miniature. For the score to flip a tier, Andco would need to be the *default* retrieval layer that EvenUp itself integrates against rather than competes with — i.e., an API EvenUp's demand-package agents call into. The dossier shows Filevine sync but no carrier-side or competitor-side integration revenue, so Andco today is fighting for share inside the workup, not sitting in the rails underneath it.

**Revenue velocity** for Andco — single-anchor parallel to **Harvey** (2023), where I sized up specifically because the curve was "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" and the difference between "false signal" and "just working" was legible in dollars (TechCrunch Nov 2025). Andco's dossier gives me a $12M oversubscribed pre-seed and "hundreds of cases, thousands of requests monthly" across "many high volume firms" (YC LinkedIn 2026) but zero revenue disclosure, zero MoM growth number, zero named PI-firm logo. In the AI cycle, when real growth is easy to disclose, the absence of a number where Harvey would have shown one is itself the signal — not disqualifying at pre-seed but not the Harvey curve either.

My 36-month forecast: Andco gets acquired by EvenUp or Filevine for $80–200M as a retrieval-layer bolt-on by mid-2028, or pivots from "end-to-end workup" to a narrower records-retrieval API in the $3–8M ARR range, with the acquisition the higher-probability branch given the Layup → Whatfix precedent on this exact founding team. The mechanism is straightforward: Andco's upstream retrieval scope is a feature inside EvenUp's roadmap, EvenUp's $385M of capital makes "buy versus build" a quarterly decision, and the Xue/Lemm pair has already demonstrated comfort with an under-$500M exit path.

The strongest bull case against that forecast is that the 8 hours per case live in retrieval, not in demand-package generation — EvenUp and Supio are downstream of where the actual labor cost sits, and Andco owns the upstream choke point that every PI workup must pass through regardless of which demand-package vendor wins. If true, Andco becomes the **Stripe**-shape index layer on PI case volume and the EvenUp valuation gets recut. What dismantles that case is the dossier's silence on proprietary data, named carrier integrations, or any switching cost beyond "Filevine sync" that a $385M-funded incumbent couldn't replicate in two quarters. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Andco discloses $1M+ ARR with >20% MoM growth and at least one named integration partnership (Filevine, Litify, or a top-10 PI firm logo) by Q4 2026 — at that point the catalyst-plus-velocity math starts to pencil and I'd write a follow-on check at the next round.
