# Conifer -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Conifer is a downloadable Rust desktop app with 303 stars on `ConiferKit/sage` and hand-written Metal/CUDA kernels I could install tonight — and a founder, Michael Jeffords, whose LinkedIn, GitHub, and Twitter I cannot verify as his. That pairing is the whole story: a tactile artifact built by a ghost. I know what the product feels like before I know who made it, which inverts my usual read.

The tactile side is the part that keeps Conifer alive on my desk. **Niantic** (~2015) got a check because I had been playing Ingress since 2012 and could feel the pull in my thumb before anyone showed me a metric — the product was the diligence. Conifer's `sage` client at v1.2.14 with a fused-quantized-matmul binary I can actually run against my own hardware is closer to that mechanism than to a pitch deck; the "~80% AI bill reduction" claim on conifer.build is falsifiable in an afternoon. Where Conifer diverges from the Niantic rung is that the artifact is a developer CLI, not a consumer object I can hand to a friend at dinner — 57 org followers and a shell repo with the interesting binaries closed means the "normal human touches it" test only half-passes.

Michael Jeffords is where Conifer breaks against my hardest filter. The **consensus YC AI-agent darling** I pass on has ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, and a market-analytical origin — replaceable operators pitching a memo. Conifer's failure mode is not that Jeffords is credentialed and hollow; it is that he is *invisible*. No Twitter, no confirmed GitHub, no bio explaining why this particular human wrote fused RMSNorm kernels in Rust. The Rust + Metal/CUDA work implies a real systems person is somewhere on the team of 3, but the two unnamed teammates and the unretrievable Jeffords profile leave me unable to run the Palmer Luckey / **Anduril** (2017) test: did he live near this problem before it was a company? The dossier gives me nothing to answer with, and the persona-file default for silence is silence-as-signal.

OpenRouter closing $113M from Google and NVIDIA in May 2026 is exactly the "sophisticated investors have visibly passed on local-first" signal I usually love — cloud gateways cannot cannibalize their per-token economics by pushing customers on-device, so Conifer is playing a game the incumbents structurally cannot enter. That is a rebuttal to the market, not a hard no. Over 18 months the mechanism compounds if Conifer's "live intelligence index" becomes a proprietary prompt-to-cheapest-model dataset the way **Crusoe**'s (~2018) well-pad relationships compounded into Stargate positioning; it collapses if Ollama (52M monthly downloads Q1 2026, 174K stars) simply adds a routing bolt-on and eats the paid layer. The weirdness is real but thinner than Crusoe's.

For CEO clarity, Conifer would need to flip a tier by naming the other two of the three-person team and showing one of them is not a 50/50 co-CEO — the **HQ Trivia** (2017) Yusupov/Kroll pattern I cited as a Known Exception was tolerable only because the product was so viral I could feel it. Conifer's `sage` at 303 stars is not HQ-viral, so the override does not activate; the single-founder listing on the YC page is a mild positive but the two anonymous seats are a governance question mark I would ask about in the first meeting rather than a green light.

Compare Conifer's capital-intensity profile to **Anduril** (2017) on one axis and it does not clear the bar: hand-written GPU kernels are a real technical moat, but this is still software running on someone else's silicon, not a frontier-hardware or regulated-domain swing. The "too hard or too unsexy" filter that produced **Vigil Labs** (August 2025) and **Mindbloom** wants a domain where the moat compounds with time and regulators; Conifer's kernels can be re-implemented by a well-funded OpenRouter engineering team in a quarter, which is exactly the polymath-substitution risk I flagged in the pass example.

Thirty-six month forecast: Conifer gets acqui-hired by OpenRouter, Portkey, or a hyperscaler for the kernel talent at sub-$30M, or wanders to a $2-4M ARR local-inference niche and stalls — not dead, not a defining company. The mechanism is the pairing: a real tactile artifact keeps the lights on long enough for a strategic to notice the kernel work, but the missing founder-origin story means Conifer never earns the persistent conviction round that would fund a decade of compounding.

The strongest case against my pass is that Jeffords is a Palmer-Luckey-in-the-garage archetype who simply has not been surfaced publicly yet — hand-written Metal/CUDA fused attention kernels are not something an ex-FAANG PM ships, and the **DeepMind** (~2013) Known Exception says polymath cross-domain depth can override the anti-pedigree rule and the absence-of-lived-experience rule simultaneously. If a 30-minute conversation with Jeffords revealed he had shipped a comparable inference runtime at Apple's ML team or Anthropic's kernels group, I would flip. That is the verifiable condition: name a prior artifact of comparable technical density that Jeffords owned. Absent that, Conifer is a beautiful `sage` binary attached to a founder my method cannot see, and my method is watching the human first.
