# Zibra Labs -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Zibra Labs is a credible infrastructure-layer play undermined by thin pricing power and a mid-tier market-timing thesis -- two ex-Anyscale Ray Core engineers building a multi-cloud spot scheduler for a workload (quant backtesting) where the buyer already has internal infra teams and where the hyperscalers publish reference architectures for the same job. The dimensional signature here is "real infra credentials, weak structural window, commoditizable margins" -- which is precisely the profile that produces a $50-150M trade sale rather than a generational outcome.

Nonobvious Market Timing is where Zibra Labs most needs to convince me, and it's the dimension where the dossier is thinnest. The HIGH rung is Coinbase -- I wrote that check when crypto was dismissed and the structural shift in digital asset infrastructure was invisible to consensus. The LOW rung is the consumer social apps I pass on every batch, where iOS and the cloud have been stable for a decade and "our take on UX" is not a structural catalyst. Zibra Labs sits closer to the LOW rung than the MID-rung Harvey: the company's framing is that AI-generated alpha has shifted research output "from a handful of signals per quarter to hundreds per day," which is real but is not a stuck-market-cracking-open shift -- every quant CTO already knows their compute bill is climbing, and AWS published a Coiled-on-Batch backtesting reference architecture that consensus has very much noticed. Check the timestamp: the AI-generated-strategy thesis is a 2024 talking point, not a 2026 nonobvious window.

Infrastructure-Layer Positioning is where Zibra Labs is strongest, and where my framework wants to lean in. The HIGH rung is Stripe -- the payments API that an entire generation of internet businesses built on, compounding switching costs over time. The LOW rung is Wish, my own cautionary tale: consumer marketplace, no API layer, no infrastructure protection when growth slowed. Zibra Labs sits between the MID rung (Gusto-style boring infrastructure) and Stripe, but closer to MID: 50,000-node clusters and 6.4M-task scheduling are genuinely platform-shaped, and the founders' Ray Core pedigree is the right pedigree, but Zibra Labs sits ON TOP of AWS/GCP rather than replacing them, and Anyscale -- where both founders trained -- can credibly enter this niche. The infra layer is real, but it's a managed-service layer, not a category-owning API.

Single-Miracle Viability is mixed for Zibra Labs. Harvey is my HIGH rung -- one exogenous miracle (foundation model capability) and the rest was execution. Anduril is the MID rung Known Exception where I overrode my own framework because the market-timing signal was generational. Zibra Labs sits below Harvey: the technical miracle is mostly done (Ray works, spot scheduling at scale is hard but tractable for these founders), but a second miracle is required -- selling into hedge funds and prop shops without a quant-native founder, against firms whose reflex is to build internally. The dossier is explicit: "no public quant-domain experience surfaced for either founder." Two miracles compounding, not one.

Operator-Scaler Founder Profile is decent but not differentiating. The HIGH rung is Parker Conrad rebuilding Rippling after Zenefits -- someone who has personally navigated 10 to 1,000+. Zibra Labs sits at MID: Ibrahim Rabbani built Ray Core at Anyscale and Zac Policzer was tech lead on Venice at LinkedIn -- both are senior IC/lead profiles at scaled infra orgs, which is the right pedigree to build the product but not yet evidence of running a commercial org. Margin Architecture is where Zibra Labs is weakest against my Abridge HIGH rung (per-encounter outcome pricing that scales with value); Zibra Labs has no public pricing page and the [Inferred] model is consumption markup over spot, which structurally caps gross margin and exposes the company to the same compression that caught Wish at the LOW rung -- spot arbitrage is not pricing power, it's a temporary spread.

Forecast, 36 months: Zibra Labs reaches $3-8M ARR with 5-12 named quant-fund customers, and is acquired by Anyscale, Databricks, or a hyperscaler in the $60-180M range -- a good outcome for the founders, a 2-3x for pre-seed, not a fund-returner. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: real infrastructure credentials get them the first handful of design partners through the Bridgewater/Two Sigma Ray relationships, but commoditizable margins and a non-generational market window cap the trajectory before escape velocity.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the Gusto/Checkr unstated pattern -- "boring but critical" infrastructure where prestige-seeking founders don't compete and the absence of competition IS the moat. Quant infra is genuinely unglamorous, the founders are exceptional on the dimension that matters (distributed-systems execution), and a top-10 quant fund's compute budget is 9-figure annually -- if Zibra Labs lands two of those, the math works without needing a generational market shift. My counter: Gusto and Checkr sold into SMBs and mid-market with no in-house alternative; quant funds have in-house platform teams and a cultural bias to build, and the warm intros referenced in the dossier (Bridgewater, Two Sigma) belong to Anyscale's relationship, not Zibra Labs's. The condition that flips me to invest: a signed contract with a top-10 quant fund at >$1M ACV within 12 months, or evidence that a tier-one prop shop has retired internal backtest infra in favor of Zibra Labs. Absent that, I pass and watch.
