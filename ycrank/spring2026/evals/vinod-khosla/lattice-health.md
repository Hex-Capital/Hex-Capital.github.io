# Lattice Health -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Lattice Health is a thin orchestration middleware sitting on top of other people's FDA-cleared models -- Christine Park's pitch picks up consequence by *adjacency* to the radiology-AI labor displacement story (the same frame that justified my $187M Series C into radiology reporting in 2025), but the company itself is being asked to ship zero scientific miracles. That high-on-adjacent-consequence, low-on-own-technical-risk pairing is what Lattice Health actually is: a workflow layer dressed in the vocabulary of a deep stack.

On consequence, the high rung in my book is **OpenAI** (2019) -- a success-state where the cognitive labor market is redrawn -- and the load-bearing analogy to Lattice Health is that radiology *is* one of the cleanest labor-displacement targets in the $15T frame I laid out in Fortune (2026-03-06). Lattice Health diverges from the OpenAI shape in a specific way the dossier makes plain: Lattice Health does not *make* the radiology AI, it *routes* it between Aidoc, Gleamer, and Rad AI. When the consequence is "the radiologist's read disappears," the economic value accrues to whoever owns the model, not whoever owns the DICOM router. Lattice Health captures the integration tax, not the labor displacement -- which is the wrong side of my Fortune (2025-07-01) "80% of jobs by 2030" trade.

The low rung on technical risk is the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer I pass on in 30 seconds -- the failure mechanism is that no consensus VC declines on capital risk, so I am over-resourced for the deal and the moat thesis is whatever the founder learns in year two. That exact pattern appears in Lattice Health: the dossier names PACS/EHR/HL7/DICOM integration as the future moat, which is plumbing every health-IT vendor since 2005 has built. No named physics, no biology, no >$50M-to-first-commercial-milestone capital wall. Microsoft/Nuance Precision Imaging Network and Bayer/Blackford -- both named in the dossier as already operating AI orchestration marketplaces inside radiology workflows -- can replicate Lattice Health's surface area with a quarter of engineering time. This is the failure mode of **Range Fuels** inverted: there, capital was misjudged on the *high* side; here, capital is misjudged on the low side, which means I have no edge.

Christine Park's founder profile -- Duke MD/MEng-AI 2023, UW neurosurgery resident PGY-3, Stanford CS PhD candidate, arXiv 2511.18208 on multimodal brain MRI -- is the paradigmatic credentialed-domain-expert pattern my TED Talk warned about, the same shape as **Glean**'s Arvind Jain (ex-Google search). For Glean I made the exception because the consequence magnitude on Fortune-500 information asymmetry and the capital scale were both there; for Lattice Health, neither override is present. The Berkeley 28,000-prediction study I cite is the relevant prior: stacked credentials predict the existing field's extrapolation, not the contrarian bet that breaks it. Park has not publicly defied any orthodoxy in radiology AI -- she has trained inside it. Mechanism over 18 months: a credentialed insider building a *neutral* layer in a market where the incumbents are her future bosses tends to get acquihired, not to redraw the category.

On learning rate, the counterfactual is what would have to be true for Lattice Health to flip a tier: the dossier would need a documented pivot -- Park having previously built a single-vendor imaging AI, hit channel conflict, and *consciously* repositioned to orchestration. That story is the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C. Lattice Health's dossier has no such artifact; Park's research arc is linear from Duke to UW to Stanford, all inside one thesis. I am applying the absent-evidence default of 7/15 and not penalizing further because a solo Spring 2026 batch founder has had no time to pivot -- but I am also not rewarding what isn't there.

Lattice Health's capital profile parallels **Cognition** in surface (enterprise infrastructure SaaS) but inverts it in substance: Cognition's $175M April 2024 round funded model training and an agent loop that consumed compute at foundation-model scale, whereas Lattice Health's roadmap as the dossier describes it -- hospital integrations, monitoring dashboards, third-party model orchestration -- is a $3-8M-to-pilot business that any Tier-1 generalist seed fund will write. I am over-resourced. The check-size mismatch is itself a signal: when my $50M into OpenAI (2019) or $115M into Commonwealth Fusion (2019) would be absurd here, the deal is wrong-shaped for the Khosla Ventures balance sheet.

My 36-month forecast on Lattice Health: acquired by Bayer/Blackford or Microsoft/Nuance for under $50M in an acquihire-shaped exit, or quietly wound down after failing to raise a Series A against the named incumbent orchestration platforms. The producing mechanism is the one I described above -- credentialed solo founder building horizontal middleware in a market whose incumbents already own the workflow surface and whose AI-vendor counterparties have channel-conflict incentives to refuse neutrality. Park's clinical bandwidth split across UW residency and Stanford PhD compounds the timeline against her.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the **Glean** exception, sharpened: Park's neurosurgery-resident credibility is genuinely rare in radiology-AI sales rooms, hospital CIOs trust clinicians over engineers, and the proliferation of >700 FDA-cleared radiology AI devices (MarketsandMarkets 2025) is exactly the kind of fragmentation-creates-platform moment that produced Glean inside the enterprise search mess. The bear-side dismantling: Glean had Arvind Jain's distribution network, a co-founder team, and a horizontal play across every Fortune 500 function, not one vertical pinned against Microsoft/Nuance already-embedded in PowerScribe. Lattice Health is a solo founder, no LOIs disclosed, no website live, and faces a named incumbent (Nuance Precision Imaging Network) sitting inside the reporting workflow she would have to displace. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a signed multi-year contract with a top-20 US health system or a named radiology group of >50 radiologists, disclosed before Demo Day -- that single artifact would prove Park can sell into the buying surface my framework says she cannot reach, and I would take a second meeting.
