# Superlog -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Superlog is a credentialed-consultant-and-ex-Datadog-engineer pair shipping their third product in 18 months into a consensus AI-SRE category where Resolve AI just raised $125M at a $1B valuation from Lightspeed (Feb 2026) — high on category timing awareness, lowest on the biographical forge that I open every meeting with. That's not a forge. That's a founder hunting for the market that finally sticks.

Default 5 leans down here.

My 36-month forecast: Superlog either executes a fourth pivot inside 24 months or wind-down/acqui-hires for sub-$10M into a mid-tier observability vendor (Better Stack, Honeycomb, or similar) by 2029, as Datadog Bits and Resolve AI consume the autonomous-fix-PR category from above and below. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: a consensus market with a $125M-funded OTel-creator competitor, attacked by founders whose pattern shows market-hopping rather than mission-locking, with explicit anti-lock-in positioning that makes the customer base churn-friendly by design.

The strongest case against me: Shishaev was a real Datadog engineer, the category timing is genuinely hot, David Lieb is a strong YC partner, and the OpenTelemetry skills library could plausibly become a developer-adoption wedge if the npx-install path catches viral fire — RTFKT is my Known Exception that proves consumer-cultural timing can substitute for moat. But RTFKT had Jared Leto and Justin Timberlake co-investing into a genre-defying creative weirdness; Superlog has a 1-star GitHub repo competing against the team that *invented* OpenTelemetry sitting on $125M. The Known Exception doesn't apply because the timing window here isn't narrow — it's already crowded. What would flip my verdict: a published auto-PR merge-acceptance rate above 70% across ten or more named enterprise customers within twelve months, which would be structural evidence that Superlog's wedge works where incumbents' don't. Until then, I pass — warmly, but I pass.
