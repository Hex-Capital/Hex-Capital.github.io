# Torus -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Marcus Lima runs at the right problem with the wrong biography. Torus is chasing cross-document consistency checks in EPC substations and pipelines — a genuine schlep zone with SOC 2 Type 1 & 2 in progress, on-prem "sovereign" deployment for Fortune 500 industrial customers, and safety-critical error surfaces where a missed 450 vs 425 psig discrepancy can blow up a plant. That is the strongest thing about Torus. The weakest thing about Torus is that the CEO's pre-startup path runs through QVARTZ/Bain consulting and an Oxford MSc, and the CTO's public identity is ambiguous enough that the dossier itself flags "Rahul Thayil ? Orion" as unverified. Right schlep, wrong hackers.

Coinbase (2012) is the load-bearing analogy for Torus's highest dimension: Brian Armstrong ran toward bitcoin compliance grind that every other applicant in his batch was routing around, and the compliance moat compounded for a decade. Torus is going after EPC document reconciliation with on-prem deployment and Fortune 500 procurement reviews — the kind of work AI-native founders in the Summer 2026 batch are almost uniformly avoiding in favor of prosumer wrappers. Where Torus diverges from the Coinbase rung is that Armstrong personally shipped the Android wallet after being rejected, forcing me to re-evaluate; Torus's schlep is claimed on the YC page ("working with multiple Fortune 500 companies") but not yet accompanied by a personal-artifact stunt from Lima or Thayil. It's the right schlep, held by the wrong hands until proven otherwise.

The pass example I keep in my head — two MBAs, glossy deck, no shipped artifact — fails on hacker genealogy first, and Torus's hacker genealogy is where I get most nervous. Lima's biography reads QVARTZ ? Bain ? Oxford MSc ? Heimdal CEO. That is the consulting-to-founder pipeline, not the Lisp-to-founder pipeline that produced Patrick Collison at Stripe or Aaron Swartz at Reddit. Neither Torus founder has a public GitHub. No open-source EPC tooling, no Hacker News handle, no competitive-programming trace. Thayil's identity is confused enough in public sources that the dossier itself notes "identity match to Torus co-founder is unverified." That is the anti-pattern from my "Founder Mode" essay showing up two dimensions deep: consulting-manager thinking wrapped around an AI wedge.

Lima's prior YC S21 raise for Heimdal — $9.7M at a $63M valuation with Sam Altman and Marc Benioff on the cap table — is the strongest signal Torus offers on resourcefulness, and it's a real one. Alumni vouching is a near-deterministic pattern for me (Justin Kan going from Kiko to Twitch is the archetype), and Lima has already proven he can grind through industrial-enterprise fundraising once. But Kan-Shear returned to YC after a *failure* they metabolized; the dossier doesn't tell me what happened to Heimdal, and repeat founding without a clear success or acknowledged failure metabolization is a weaker signal than either. If Heimdal quietly declined, this is Lima's second at-bat with a fresh AI wrapper on a new industrial vertical — which reads closer to the "spotted an opportunity" mode than the "I had this exact problem" mode.

Dropbox's Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT; that is the reference class for organic ideas. Torus's founder-pain fit is adjacent-domain, not personal-workflow. Lima ran Heimdal in direct-air-capture concrete, which touches industrial construction — but there's no dossier evidence that Lima or Thayil personally sat in an EPC firm reconciling a P&ID against a datasheet at 2am and thought "someone should make this." The pilot ask on the website — "send us 5-6 documents from your current projects" — reads like a founder who learned the problem exists from customer conversations, not one who lived it. That's the difference between building for yourself and building for a market you researched. Over 18 months, this compounds: founders who lived the problem know which of the "50+ categories of consistency checks" actually matter to a lead process engineer, and which are ceremony.

The cofounder configuration on Torus counterfactually flips if Thayil's identity resolves into a real technical footprint — a Techstars '23 alum from Orion who also happens to hack in EPC domains would move Torus from "consultant plus mystery" to "operator plus technical partner." As the dossier stands, two founders with no described prior working relationship and a team of five is default territory: the YC in-person interview will surface chemistry, but I'm reading paper.

My 36-month forecast for Torus: 3-8 Fortune 500 pilots convert into paid deployments, ARR lands in the $2-5M range, and Torus gets acquired by AVEVA (Schneider Electric) or Bentley for $30-80M as an AI-agent bolt-on to their existing EDMS suite — the Auctomatic outcome, not the Stripe outcome. The mechanism producing this is the signature pairing: the schlep is real enough to build a business, the hacker genealogy is thin enough to cap the ceiling, and the incumbents Torus is displacing are also its natural acquirers once the AI-EDMS category clarifies. That's a fine outcome for Lima. It is not the outcome I'm underwriting when I write a personal angel check.

The strongest bull case against my forecast: Lima already convinced Altman and Benioff to write into Heimdal at $63M, which means he clears the industrial-enterprise fundraising bar most YC founders can't. "Working with multiple Fortune 500 companies" this early in a Summer 2026 batch is genuinely rare — most YC AI companies at this stage have zero enterprise motion. If Torus's pilot-to-paid conversion runs even 30%, this looks like Rippling circa 2017: boring B2B that becomes unignorable because the founder can grind through procurement cycles longer than anyone else. The bear rebuttal that wins: the dossier tells me Heimdal raised $9.7M but doesn't tell me it's alive, Thayil isn't verified as a hacker, and the SOC 2 status ("in progress") plus the pilot ask ("send us 5-6 documents") means every one of those Fortune 500 relationships is currently unpaid. Consulting-founder plus mystery-CTO plus unpaid pilots is exactly the profile my "Founder Mode" essay warned against. Flip condition: Thayil's GitHub or open-source EPC-adjacent shipping history surfaces, OR a named Fortune 500 EPC firm signs a paid contract before Demo Day. Either would move Torus from Neutral to Invest.
