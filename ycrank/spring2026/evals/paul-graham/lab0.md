# Lab0 -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Lab0 is what you get when founders find a real schlep but arrive at it through credentials instead of code. The highest dimension here is the schlep itself -- enterprise rollouts where, as Onkar Borade saw at Oracle, "the implementation team was bigger than the dev team" -- and the lowest is hacker genealogy: three IIT founders with zero public GitHub repos between them. That pairing tells me what Lab0 is before I read another line. It is a consulting-adjacent automation bet pitched by people who watched the schlep from the inside but haven't shown me the artifact.

Lab0's schlep dimension reads adjacent to **Coinbase** (2012). Armstrong wanted the bitcoin compliance grind because nobody else did, and the unpleasantness became the moat. Lab0 is gesturing at the same shape -- ERP configuration, discovery calls, custom integrations, the dirty work Accenture bills $64B a year to perform manually. That is a Stripe-class schlep on paper. Where Lab0 diverges from the Coinbase pattern is that Armstrong was personally going to sit in a compliance chair and grind; the Lab0 deck describes an agent doing it. The schlep isn't the moat if the founders are trying to automate themselves out of touching it. **DoorDash** (2013) earned its score on this axis because Tony Xu personally delivered burritos. I don't yet know what Borade, Lakshya Gupta, and Sujay Srivastava are doing with their own hands inside an Adobe rollout.

The hacker-genealogy dimension is where Lab0 hits my Pass Example almost directly. My pass note describes "a polished AI-wrapper company pitched by a two-MBA team... no shipped artifact." Lab0 is not MBAs -- they are IIT CS -- but the dossier line "GitHub: No public repos found" repeats three times under three different founders. Patrick Collison had an Irish Young Scientist Lisp prize on the table before **Stripe** (2010). Aaron Swartz had co-authored RSS before **Reddit** (2005). Lab0 has Lakshya Gupta's reported scaling of an Emergent Labs agent platform "from 0 to $100M ARR with 5M+ users," which would matter enormously if I could see the code. I can't. Hackers leave trails. The absence of a trail across all three Lab0 founders is itself the signal.

Animal resourcefulness for Lab0 is mixed, and I want to be honest about which direction. The Adobe pilot landing before a Spring 2026 batch demo day, with a three-person team, is not nothing -- enterprises don't sign up for agentic rollout software with a three-person no-name vendor unless someone in that Lab0 conversation was unusually persuasive. That's a small Armstrong-prototype-rescue moment. But there is no cereal-box story here, no stunt, no unscalable hack. Counterfactual: if the dossier had named two more enterprise logos beyond Adobe and a paid contract figure, Lab0 jumps a tier on this axis. The Adobe-plus-"a couple of SIs" framing is the kind of language founders use when only one of those is real.

Lab0's organic-idea fit is the dimension I keep oscillating on. Borade lived the Oracle implementation-headcount imbalance. Srivastava lived BFSI custom-rollout hell. That is genuine founder-problem fit -- not "we spotted a market," which would be the disqualifying language from my Pass Example. The mechanism over 18 months: founder pain that came from observing a workplace, rather than personally suffering as a consumer, tends to produce products that solve the wrong layer of the problem. Drew Houston lost his USB stick himself; the Collisons hated commerce APIs as users. Lab0's founders watched other people drown in rollouts. That is closer to consulting empathy than to **Dropbox** (2007)'s personal-itch origin, and it usually produces a feature, not a company.

The co-founder configuration is a quiet positive that I won't overweight. Three founders, two from IIT Bombay, one from IIT Madras -- this is the **Twitch** (2007) trio shape on paper, but the dossier explicitly notes "no public statement on the specific shared lab/cohort." That is the configuration question the in-person interview resolves, not the dossier.

My 36-month forecast for Lab0: acquihire into Accenture or Deloitte at $20-50M, or a pivot to selling implementation tooling to system integrators at $2-5M ARR, with the OpenAI Deployment Company having absorbed the agentic-FDE narrative in the enterprise mind before Lab0 reaches escape velocity. The mechanism: the OpenAI Deployment Company bought Tomoro's ~150 FDEs to do exactly this work first-party, and Accenture's revenue model makes Lab0's automated-delivery story a channel conflict, not a partnership. A schlep without hacker artifacts becomes an acquihire because the buyer is paying for the customer relationship, not the code.

The bull case that stings: Lakshya Gupta's reported 0-to-$100M ARR scaling at Emergent Labs is, if real, a Brian-Armstrong-grade animal artifact -- enterprise agent platforms don't scale to $100M ARR without the engineer who built them being an animal of a programmer. The absence of public GitHub may just reflect closed-source enterprise work, not absence of chops. **Airbnb** (2009) is my Known Exception precedent here: I rejected the idea in my head and the founders' off-deck stunt overturned the rejection. If Gupta walked into the interview and demonstrated the Emergent Labs build live, Lab0 jumps two tiers. What dismantles this in the dossier as written: the claim is sourced to "WebSearch snippet, 2026," not a verifiable artifact, and Emergent Labs hitting $100M ARR is itself an extraordinary claim I'd want to independently confirm. Verifiable flip condition: if Gupta's Emergent Labs scaling claim verifies and Lab0 names a second enterprise logo beyond Adobe within six months, I write the check.
