# Framewise Health -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Framewise Health is a plausible workflow-infrastructure idea sitting on top of three unvalidated capabilities at once, pitched into a steady-state $14.55B->$25.01B patient engagement market that no recent catalyst has actually re-opened -- the highest dimension is a half-credible index position via FHIR plumbing into Epic, Oracle/Cerner, athenahealth, ModMed, eClinicalWorks and NextGen; the lowest is revenue velocity, where Tane Kim and David Cui's dossier is silent on customers, LOIs, pilots, and dollars. That pairing -- a workflow-layer thesis with zero buying behavior in an AI cycle where buying behavior is the *only* legible signal -- is the signature.

The **Anduril** (2022) catalyst was specific: Ukraine, in a named 24-month window, exposed Pentagon software inadequacy and rewrote procurement assumptions. Framewise Health's dossier offers a market growing 11.4% CAGR (MarketsandMarkets, 2025) and an *inferred* claim that "text-to-video generative models crossed a quality threshold in 2024-2025... no analyst statement found tying this specifically to patient education." That is TAM math with a hypothesized catalyst, which is the inverse of the **Stripe** (2010) / **Coinbase** (2013) / **Anduril** (2022) entry template -- I started by asking what catalyzed patient discharge education in the last 24 months and the Framewise Health dossier cannot name one. Multilingual hospital discharge has been broken since the 1990s; nothing in the dossier explains why Q2 2026 is the moment, except that LLMs are cheaper, which is a margin-compression story, not a market re-opening.

The "AI company with no growth curve disclosed" red flag is what I flagged in Tim Ferriss Show #863 (April 2026) as "something's fundamentally broken," and the Framewise Health dossier is exactly that profile: $500K standard YC investment, a Founderland article, a launch tweet from @tanekimm and @sourdoggy8, X account created January 2026, and the explicit line "No revenue, customer count, paying-customer disclosure... no mitigating disclosed letters of intent." Six FHIR integrations live but zero hospital logos is the inversion of the **Harvey** (2023) signature -- Harvey at this stage already had named firms paying, and Framewise Health has integration plumbing without buyers. Pre-seed allowance applies, but I bought **Decagon** (2023) and **Abridge** (2023) at comparable stages with at least one anchor design partner; Framewise Health discloses none.

Tane Kim observed "500+ patient interactions across a dozen-plus institutions" per the YC page -- observation, not shipping. (Mechanism analysis.) The **Perplexity** (2022) filter is whether the founder has independently shipped artifacts and continues to ship between meetings; Framewise Health's CEO is a Brown BS/MD dropout with clinical exposure but no named prior product, and David Cui's resume is "computer vision research at Brown" plus an engineering internship at Freya (YC S25) with no public GitHub. Over 18 months this matters because the company will need to ship clinician-review tooling, FDA-aware content guardrails, EHR pilot adapters, and a multilingual video pipeline simultaneously -- the iterative-shipping muscle is the rate limit. The dossier shows clinical exposure (a real asset) without the matching execution rhythm signal, which is closer to a competent first-time-founder profile than a Perplexity-pattern founder.

Counterfactually, Framewise Health's distribution position would flip a tier if Epic, Oracle/Cerner, athenahealth, ModMed, eClinicalWorks and NextGen integrations translated into being the *default* discharge-video layer every EHR's hospital customers route through -- the **Stripe** (2010) "index on e-commerce" mechanic. They don't, yet, because Commure acquired Memora Health on 2024-12-20 (Tracxn) and now owns the EHR-adjacent patient-communication relationship at hospitals that already pay Commure for the rest of the stack. Framewise Health would have to either disintermediate Commure-Memora at hospitals already on Commure, or sell to the long tail of specialty groups and pharma where Commure has weaker reach. The FHIR integration list is real but it is *table stakes* for this category, not the moat -- which is why I score this dimension as the relative high without scoring it high in absolute terms.

The single-miracle test compounds against Framewise Health the way **Color Genomics** (2015) compounded *for* it. (Single-anchor parallel.) Color worked because I personally architected it as workflow/data on top of commodity sequencing -- the chemistry was someone else's miracle. Framewise Health stacks at least three independent miracles: clinical-grade generative video accurate enough at hospital discharge volume in 75+ languages, an FDA/SaMD regulatory posture for AI-generated medical instruction that doesn't bottleneck on clinician review, and an EHR-integrated hospital-procurement motion against Commure-Memora. The dossier's only mitigation is "Reviewed by clinicians" in the workflow, which solves the liability question by reintroducing the cost problem -- if every video is clinician-reviewed, the unit economics collapse toward Memora's per-message model and the auto-generated video isn't the wedge anymore.

My 36-month forecast for Framewise Health: acqui-hired by Commure or a mid-tier EHR (athenahealth/ModMed most likely) for under $40M, or pivoted into a narrower specialty-group multilingual discharge SaaS at under $2M ARR by mid-2029, with no Series A from a tier-1 fund. The mechanism is straightforward -- the regulatory + clinical-review overhead pulls Framewise Health toward content-services economics rather than software margins, Commure-Memora bundles "good enough" AI video into existing hospital contracts within 18 months, and Tane Kim and David Cui's clinical depth becomes more valuable as employees inside a Commure than as founders fighting for net-new hospital procurement cycles.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the **Color Genomics** (2015) Known Exception: Framewise Health is workflow-infrastructure on top of a hard-tech layer (generative video) rather than betting on the layer itself, Tane Kim has the same clinical-context-from-inside-the-system profile that worked for our Color thesis, and patient engagement is explicitly in the "still wide open" bucket per *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) -- no winner declared, unlike legal AI where Harvey closed the window. That case stings. It dismantles on one fact: the Color exception required me to *operate* the company and architect the miracle isolation myself; Framewise Health is a passive angel check into a two-person team with no shipping history facing Commure-Memora distribution incumbency, where the "single miracle" framing collapses on inspection into clinical accuracy + regulatory posture + EHR procurement run in parallel. I would flip to Invest on one verifiable condition: three named US health systems (not specialty groups, not pharma) signed as paying customers with disclosed contract values within 90 days of YC Demo Day. Absent that, Framewise Health is the AI-company-without-a-growth-curve that Tim Ferriss Show #863 named as the disqualifier.
