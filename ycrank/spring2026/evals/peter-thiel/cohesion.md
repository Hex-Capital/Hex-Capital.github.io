# Cohesion -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Cohesion is a domain-fit buy-side team — Devon Krapcho from Long Path Partners, Matt Munns from T. Rowe Price — executing into the most consensus-shaped corner of the 2026 YC batch, "agentic AI for finance," against AlphaSense at $500M ARR, Hebbia at $700M valuation, Rogo at $300M+ raised, and Brightwave at $21M raised. The dimensional signature is founder lineage propping up a company whose atoms-heavy moat is zero and whose contrarian secret reduces to a content-source feature: "we monitor podcasts and X/Twitter, they monitor licensed documents." That is not a secret. That is a roadmap item AlphaSense ships in a quarter.

Stripe in 2010 named a specific architectural failure mode in PayPal: the incumbent's API was institutionally unable to treat developers as the buying center, because PayPal's distribution depended on consumer-side identity, not seven-line integration. Cohesion's dossier names no equivalent failure mode in AlphaSense or Hebbia. The pitch is that fundamental analysts "spend hours per week" and "miss signals" — a workflow complaint, not an institutional blind spot. The closest thing to a secret is the claim that podcasts and X/Twitter are non-traditional sources incumbents ignore, but AlphaSense's own press release cites 33% QoQ growth in GenAI query usage and Rogo just closed $160M in April 2026 — these are firms that will ingest whatever data source the market validates, on a six-month lag at worst. Cohesion has named a feature gap, not a structural one, and the difference between Stripe in 2010 and Cohesion in 2026 is that Patrick Collison could explain why Braintree could not become Stripe; Krapcho cannot explain why AlphaSense cannot become Cohesion.

The pass archetype from my own pass file is "AI-powered platform for [vertical X]" with no named incumbent architectural blind spot and a moat hypothesis terminating at "we have a better model." Cohesion fits this archetype with near-perfect resolution: B2B SaaS, fintech tag, three-person team, ten unnamed hedge-fund customers, no public ARR, no public retention, no public signal-quality backtest, and a competitive landscape where four better-capitalized firms are already converging on the same workflow. The dossier flags X/Twitter API terms-of-service risk as a key risk — meaning Cohesion's stated differentiation is also its primary single-point-of-failure dependency. That is the inverse of a moat: it is a leased differentiator on which Elon Musk holds the lease.

Krapcho's five years at Long Path Partners and Munns' prior work building AI for investors at T. Rowe Price are real domain signal — this is not a Stanford CS pair guessing at hedge-fund workflow. Cohesion's founder-market fit is in the 70th percentile of YC P26 fintech. But my lineage criterion is not "domain fit"; it is "shipped to scale." Levchin shipped PayPal. Moskovitz shipped Facebook. D'Angelo carried Quora past the workflow-moat objection because he had been Facebook CTO. Cohesion has an analyst, an AWS engineer, and an internal-tools AI builder. The Quora exception does not extend here, because the override on Quora was a CTO-of-Facebook signal, not a senior-IC-at-T.-Rowe-Price signal — these are two tiers apart in operator pedigree.

The monopoly trajectory compounds backward over eighteen months: Cohesion's cited ten hedge funds and $10Bn+ combined AUM is a respectable pre-seed reference base, but the hedge-fund buyer is exactly the buyer most likely to run Cohesion in parallel with AlphaSense, evaluate signal lift on a quarterly basis, and consolidate onto whichever vendor reaches feature parity with the lowest seat price. Switching costs in this category are measured in weeks, not years; there is no integration depth analogous to Palantir's Gotham deployments, no regulatory certification depth analogous to Anduril's DoD program-of-record path. The pricing-power story terminates at "per-seat SaaS in a four-way price war by 2027."

A definite-optimism plan would name a two-to-five-year technical commitment Cohesion will not pivot from — proprietary podcast-transcript rights, an exclusive earnings-call audio partnership, a named regulatory wedge on alternative-data compliance. The dossier surfaces none of these. What would have to be true for the Definite Plan score to flip a tier is a signed multi-year exclusive on a non-replicable data source, or a named architectural commitment — for instance, a buy-side compliance layer that becomes the SOC2-plus-MNPI moat hedge funds cannot adopt from AlphaSense. Absent that, Cohesion is in iteration mode against four better-funded iterators.

My 36-month forecast: Cohesion reaches $1-3M ARR by mid-2027, fails to differentiate against Rogo's and AlphaSense's podcast-and-social monitoring features shipped on a four-quarter lag, and is acquired by AlphaSense or Hebbia as a feature-and-team tuck-in for $15-40M, or shuts down. The mechanism is the one the dossier itself names: third-party data dependency plus a feature-shaped differentiation plus four well-capitalized incumbents converging on the same workflow. This is not a power-law shape. This is a consolidation-target shape.

The strongest bull case is the Quora exception: D'Angelo's Facebook CTO pedigree carried a company past missing-contrarian-secret and missing-definite-plan because knowledge-graph network effects compounded at scale. The Cohesion analogue would be: fund-specific signal tuning becomes a per-customer compounding asset, ten funds becomes a hundred, and the proprietary index of which signals matter to which fund types becomes the moat. The dossier evidence that wins against this is the operator-pedigree gap — Munns at T. Rowe Price is not D'Angelo at Facebook, and signal-tuning across hedge funds does not have the cross-user network effect a knowledge graph has, because hedge-fund alpha is by definition zero-sum and non-shareable. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Cohesion announcing a named exclusive data partnership (e.g., a major podcast network's transcript rights, or a buy-side compliance certification) plus $5M+ ARR within twelve months at sub-5% logo churn. Absent both, this is a pass.
