# Touchmark -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Touchmark is the cleanest example I've seen this batch of a competent ex-fintech-PM-plus-ex-quant-intern pair force-fitting a consensus AI infrastructure shape into a YC seed cap table, with founder backgrounds (Ilia Bolgov's 6 years 9 months Product at Revolut; Roman Yanushevskyi's two Citadel Securities QR internships) doing all the lifting and literally zero owned distribution, public writing, or community footprint underneath it. The signature pairing -- real operator pedigree on top, zero conviction artifacts and zero brand surface below -- tells me Touchmark is the founders responding to opportunity, not pursuing a mission, and pricing themselves into the same SAFE cap as fifty other S26 AI infra teams.

The pass example I wrote for this exact dossier shape was "AI agent for enterprise vertical, three months out of YC, four tier-1 VCs in, $30M post." Touchmark sits one notch above that only because Stripe just *bought Metronome for $1B in December 2025* -- which is not a bull signal for Touchmark, it's the consensus-resolution event that tells me the AI billing category just got absorbed by the most well-capitalized incumbent in payments, and a TypeScript SDK that wraps an eval layer is precisely the kind of feature Stripe Billing will ship as a checkbox, not a company. Compare to Sublime Security where the entry happened *before* the security consolidation wave was visible; Touchmark is entering after the wave has already crested and the acquirer has already been named. That's not non-consensus pricing -- that's buying the post-print.

Teamshares is the canonical venture-shaped-but-non-standard structure I anchor on for capital structure: an employee-ownership rollup of small businesses that no other fund could underwrite because the instrument itself was illegible. Touchmark is the inverse -- a totally legible SaaS cap table aimed at a billing-infrastructure war against Stripe-owned Metronome ($128M raised), Orb (~$44M, Vercel/Replit/Pinecone/Perplexity as logos), Lago ($34M, AGPLv3 open source), and Solvimon ($9.6M Northzone seed). To compete on the infrastructure layer against that field, Touchmark will need five-plus financing tacks before the Series A starting line, and its own product copy says cross-customer price adjustments "average to zero" so the top-line take-rate is structurally capped. A billing platform that designs itself to be revenue-neutral has chosen to be a feature, not a company.

On conviction (mechanism analysis over 18 months): Yanushevskyi's Citadel QR background and Bolgov's Revolut usage-billing tenure are legitimate founder-market-fit ingredients on paper, and I want to give that weight. But conviction compounds through public artifacts -- essays, opinionated threads, a GitHub repo with three years of half-finished eval experiments -- and Touchmark's dossier returns "No public account found" on Twitter for both founders, "No public repos found" on GitHub for both, and no writing of any kind on why LLM-as-judge evals are stable enough to bill against. Eighteen months from now I will either find that one of them has been thinking publicly about output-quality evaluation for years and the dossier missed it, or I will find that they're going to discover their own eval reliability problem in customer disputes and have no missionary anchor to pull through the pivot. The prior is the second.

John Fish's Bookshelved is the founder-as-brand check I wrote in January 2024 because the YouTuber's distribution *was* the moat -- the company couldn't have been built without the channel. Counterfactual for Touchmark: what would have to be true for me to score this dimension a tier higher? Bolgov would need a Substack on usage-based pricing economics with 5K+ subscribers, or Yanushevskyi would need a public eval-framework repo with stars in the four digits, or one of them would need to be a known voice in the Orb/Metronome developer community already. None of those exist in the dossier. Touchmark has no distribution edge of any kind and will compete for AI platform integrations with sales motion against Stripe's installed base.

Single-anchor parallel on category sleeve: the sleeve I'm convicted on adjacent to this would be pre-consensus crypto -- Aleo, Klaytn, Lightning Labs -- where the *protocol* is doing economically novel work the consensus has dismissed. Touchmark is AI billing infrastructure, which sits directly inside the "AI is just more internet" sleeve I explicitly down-weight from the Lonsdale podcast frame. It is not foundation-model fine-tuning, so it dodges the worst version of that filter, but billing-on-top-of-LLM-evals is still a SaaS layer wrapped around a commoditizing primitive (LLM-as-judge is available in every major model API), and the entry price is whatever the S26 batch median demands. No category sleeve credit.

My 36-month forecast: Touchmark is acquihired by Orb or by Stripe Billing for sub-$30M by mid-2029, with the eval-scoring layer absorbed as a feature flag inside the acquirer's existing usage-billing product, OR Touchmark pivots away from billing into pure AI evals/observability (Braintrust-Langsmith adjacent) within 18 months when the first three pilot customers refuse to let their invoices fluctuate post-hoc. The mechanism is the same in both branches: Touchmark's "revenue-neutral" pricing copy is an admission that AI platforms won't accept downside on their own billing, which strands Touchmark with no take-rate leverage and no path to default-alive without becoming a different product.

The bull case that stings: Bessemer's 2026 pricing playbook explicitly validates outcome-based AI billing (the Leena AI example), per-seat SaaS fell from 21% to 15% in twelve months, Solvimon raised $9.6M from Northzone on a hybrid usage-plus-outcome thesis, and Yanushevskyi's Citadel quant pedigree means Touchmark could ship eval rigor that Orb and Lago genuinely cannot match -- making Touchmark the eval-quality wedge into an acquirer who otherwise meters dumb tokens. That's a real argument. It loses to the dossier on one specific fact: Stripe acquired Metronome for $1B in December 2025, which means the deepest-pocketed payments incumbent in the world now owns the channel into every AI vendor's billing path, and Touchmark's eval layer is a six-month feature roadmap for Stripe Billing rather than a defensible standalone. The condition that flips my verdict: Touchmark publishes a named multi-year contract with a tier-1 AI platform (Vercel, Replit, Anthropic, Perplexity) where the customer accepts >20% per-call price variance and the contract value clears $1M ARR. Until then this is the consensus AI SAFE I cannot make money on.
