# Standout -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Alexis Aftalion took Zealy from zero to $3M ARR and 1.5M MAU in twelve months, and Witold de La Chapelle previously built Zarobky into a 100K-MAU Polish jobs product before shipping infra at Dropbox, Samsara, and Chime -- so Standout is, on the founder axis, two repeat marketplace builders attacking a marketplace. The signature, though, is a high-founder-fit team parachuting into the single most consensus lane of YC Spring 2026 -- "agentic hiring marketplace" sitting downstream of Mercor at a $10B valuation and Paraform's $40M Series B closed in March 2026. That pairing -- real founder leverage, zero category leverage -- is the analytical fingerprint, and it tells me what Standout is before any of the traction numbers do.

On founder lived experience, my high rung is Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017), who shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before pivoting into defense -- the prior-shipped-artifact pattern. Standout is not there, but it's not the Pass-Example "ex-FAANG PM who discovered the problem in a memo" either. Aftalion's Zealy was a community-token marketplace that crossed real liquidity, and de La Chapelle's Zarobky was *literally* a jobs marketplace -- the dossier specifics that pin Standout above the consensus-YC-darling floor. What separates Standout from the Luckey rung is autobiography: I cannot find the moment either founder *felt* hiring spam in their own skin. They have built adjacent things; they have not lived this thing. That's the gap.

Crusoe Energy in 2018 is my magically-weird high rung because two or three credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds before I wrote the check -- that was the tell. Standout inverts the tell. Mercor has raised $492M and pays out $1.5M a day to contractors; Paraform just closed a Series B with Palantir, Rippling, and Cognition as customers; both publicly claim the agentic-hiring-marketplace position. Standout is not laughed at, not unsexy, not too-hard -- it is the *prototype* of the consensus YC AI lane my Pass Example describes almost verbatim. The line in the persona file is "find them before it becomes consensus," and the dossier shows Standout entering two to three years and $500M of capital after consensus already formed.

On governance: one named CEO (Aftalion), CTO clearly delineated (de La Chapelle), and a first hire (Côme) who is non-engineering -- which is the kind of unusual founder-led call I respect because it implies Aftalion knows the bottleneck is distribution, not code. The HQ Trivia (2017) failure traced to Yusupov/Kroll having no tiebreaker; Standout does not have that shape on paper. Eighteen months in, the test will be whether de La Chapelle accepts Aftalion's call on the humans-in-the-loop question -- because that is where Paraform has explicitly bet the other way, and a CTO-CEO split on technical philosophy in a fully agentic system is exactly the kind of unresolved authority that becomes existential. Today, the structure passes.

Counterfactual on the frontier-hardware criterion: what would have to be true for Standout to score above default here? It would have to operate inside a regulated or capital-intensive recruiting vertical -- defense clearances, healthcare credentialing, immigration-sponsored roles, union shops -- where the moat compounds with regulatory familiarity. Standout's stated ICP is "founding engineers, early product, GTM leaders" for early-stage companies, which is software-marketplace-on-software-buyers, the most commoditized cut of the category. The Vigil Labs (August 2025) and Mindbloom shapes -- regulated, unsexy, transgressive -- do not appear anywhere in the Standout dossier. Score does not flip.

On physical-world verifiability, Standout sits one rung below Postmates (2011), where the delivery person physically arrived at my door and the bet became obvious. I can sign up as talent on Standout, stay anonymous, and wait for an agent to surface an intro -- there is a tactile experience, but it's *waiting for a notification*, not riding an Uber or playing Ingress. The 100 intros in month one across 10,000 talent and 60 companies is a 1% intro-rate that I cannot translate into a felt product moment -- the kind of artifact I observe in the wild, not a dashboard claim. Standout is observable, barely; it is not Niantic-observable.

Forecast: 36 months out, Standout has either been acqui-hired by Paraform or a vertical ATS for $5-20M, or pivoted into a niche vertical recruiting tool somewhere in the $1-3M ARR range with the WhatsApp-v0-style consumer audience monetized via career services -- not the category winner against Mercor and Paraform, but not zero, because Aftalion has shipped real revenue before. The mechanism is straightforward: Standout entered the category late, on the riskiest technical wedge (no humans in the loop), against incumbents with 100x the capital, and the founder strength is sufficient to land a soft exit but not to win the structural fight.

The bull case that stings: Aftalion did $0 to $3M ARR in twelve months at Zealy and that velocity is not common, de La Chapelle has literally built a jobs marketplace before, and the persona file's own HandUp (~2014) exception says I will write checks where the founder mirrors my own trajectory regardless of commercial ceiling -- and Aftalion's outsider European trajectory through ESSEC and Alven is not the Stanford-Goldman pedigree I systematically avoid. Fair. But the override conditions for HandUp were personal mission alignment with homelessness, and Standout's mission is "fix hiring spam" -- not the lived-biography override. The DeepMind exception was polymath cross-domain compounding; Standout is one-domain repetition, not polymath. The condition that flips my verdict: if Aftalion can show me, in person, that the agent has placed a founding engineer into one of my own Long Journey portfolio companies inside the next 90 days -- a Postmates-style tactile moment where the artifact moves through my own physical environment -- I revisit. Until then, the consensus-category deselection holds.
