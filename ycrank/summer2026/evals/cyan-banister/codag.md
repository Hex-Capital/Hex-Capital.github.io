# Codag -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Michael Zhou is a solo founder who pivoted Codag five times, bootstrapped until his bank account hit zero, did door-to-door sales, and turned down seven-figure new-grad offers to keep going -- that biography registers with me before any slide about Drain3 or token windows. But Codag's dimensional signature is "clean operator on a commoditized substrate": its highest axis is the accidental clarity of having one CEO because there is only one human, and its lowest is that this is software-only dev-tools wrapping an open-source algorithm in a category that Datadog will quietly absorb. That pairing tells me what kind of bet Codag is -- a grinder building a feature, not a founder *of* a place.

Anduril-Luckey is the high rung on lived experience: Palmer shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he built drones, and the founder biography materially preceded the company. Codag's Michael Zhou has the adjacent tenure -- Shopify and Okta both operate high-volume infrastructure logs, and a production engineer at either has felt the on-call moment where a 26.6M-token CloudWatch dump is unreadable. That is real exposure, not memo exposure, and it is the only thing keeping Codag out of my pass-example bucket. Where Codag diverges from Luckey is that Zhou did not ship a log-compression product to scale before YC -- the 346-star `michaelzixizhou/codag` predecessor was a VSCode workflow visualizer, an entirely different artifact, which means the current product is iteration number six, not a continuation of a long-held obsession.

The pass-example I wrote -- two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, pure software abstraction, consensus AI-agent round -- is exactly the failure mode on capital-intensity and unsexiness. Codag is not as bad as that template (Zhou is not a four-logo team page, and the pricing tiers of $19 Hobby and $199 Team are honest bottoms-up rather than a $20M oversubscribed seed), but the underlying shape rhymes: Rust pipeline, MCP plug-ins for Claude Code and Codex, free 50 MB tier, no hardware to touch, no regulator to befriend, no roughneck to talk to. Codag sits in the lane that produced commoditized dev tools, not the lane that produced Crusoe burning gas flares in North Dakota. The moat compounds in domains other investors refuse to enter, and Codag has chosen a domain ten other investors will happily enter tomorrow.

Magically-weird gets tested by asking whether sophisticated capital has aesthetically passed and whether the founder can articulate the misread. Codag's "agents will be the primary consumer of logs within two years" thesis is interesting but it is also the thesis of every observability incumbent's next roadmap deck -- ClickHouse already published "improve logs compression with log clustering," and Compresr is a YC W26 batch-mate on the same compression vector. Over eighteen months this dimension fails by the most predictable mechanism: Datadog adds an agent-formatted JSON output to its existing log clustering and Codag's $199 Team tier loses its reason to exist. The compounding goes the wrong direction because the algorithm (Drain3) and the implementation (`codag-megalith/codag-drain`, 42 stars, MIT-shaped repo) are both public.

Single CEO authority would have to be the override here, the HQ-Trivia-inverse where the lack of a Yusupov-Kroll feud earns Codag a structural pass. Zhou is one person with zero open YC roles and unambiguous tiebreaker authority, which is the cleanest possible answer to the question that killed GameCrush. Were Codag a two-founder 50/50 split between Zhou and a co-equal infra engineer, this score would drop six points and the verdict would harden into a pass; the solo profile is what holds Codag in neutral territory. The cost is that the same dimension flags execution concentration -- algorithm, GTM, support, and fundraising all sit on one calendar.

Flock Safety is the single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability: I read the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café before I knew the company. Codag's tactile artifact is the homepage demo collapsing 1.2M log lines (108 MB) into 3,317 tokens -- an ~8,021× compression number a developer can actually run via the CLI. That is more touchable than a B2B API; it is less touchable than a license-plate camera on a real street. The demo is verifiable in the developer sense, not in the normal-human sense, which is why Codag clears the floor of my pass-example but does not reach the rung where I source deals from my own physical environment.

My 36-month forecast: Codag either gets acqui-hired by Datadog, Grafana, or an MCP-tooling consolidator for under $25M with Zhou taking an engineering role, or the open-source `codag-drain` repo plateaus under 500 stars while Compresr or a Datadog-bundled equivalent absorbs the agent-JSON output category, and Codag winds down or pivots into agent-observability proper. Annual revenue stays under $2M either way. The mechanism is straightforward: when the algorithm is public and the implementation is public, the buyer pays for the brand and the installed base, and Codag has neither.

The strongest case against my forecast is the DeepMind exception applied sideways -- if AI agents do become the primary consumer of logs by 2028 and MCP becomes the dominant interface for that consumption, Codag's early integrations with Claude Code and Codex could compound into a default-tool position the way a polymath compounds across disciplines, and Zhou's pivoted-five-times persistence could be the outsider-trajectory signal I claim to weight. I would flip to Invest if Codag ships a proprietary feedback loop -- customer log streams tuning template grouping in a way Drain3 cannot replicate -- and posts two named-logo paid customers above the Team tier within nine months. Until then, the Drain3 substrate is the tell, not the founder's grit. Pass with affection.
