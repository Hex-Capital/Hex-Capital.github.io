# Revnu -- Paul Graham Evaluation

George Jefferson bootstrapped artomate.app to 1K MRR within 24 hours and ran resold.app to 1,000+ paying users with over €10M transacted -- all from a university bedroom. Art Freebrey, 21, won the Mistral worldwide hackathon and specializes in reverse engineering antibots. Between them, Revnu's founders have shipped at least five revenue-generating products before taking a dollar of outside capital. That track record is rare at any stage. I've backed teenagers before -- the Bindwell founders were 18 and 19 when I invested -- because when very young people demonstrate that intensity of output, you're seeing slope, not position. George and Art clearly have the animal quality. They don't talk about building; they build, ship, and charge money. But Bindwell was attacking pesticide chemistry -- a domain so unsexy it repelled every other AI founder. Revnu is attacking AI marketing, which is the exact opposite problem: it attracts everyone.

Revnu's competitive landscape tells the story. Jasper raised $131M and has $88M in revenue. Gumloop just closed a $50M Series B in March 2026. Albert.ai raised $70.4M before being acquired. Fiber AI is pursuing adjacent territory on $500K. When I look at a market where this much capital is already deployed, I don't see opportunity -- I see the returns being competed away. Nobody hears "autonomous AI agents that handle your marketing" and thinks "that will never work." Everyone thinks it's a good idea. That's the tell. The best startup ideas initially seem bad to smart people. Revnu's idea seems good to everyone, which means the pricing on this bet has already been set by the crowd.

Revnu's origin story -- "AI made building easy but growth remained manual" -- is sourced from the founders' own experience growing bootstrapped products without marketing teams (revnu.app/about). That's better than a McKinsey deck. George and Art genuinely lived this frustration across artomate, resold, and vinta. But the insight is one that every technical founder shares, which means it lacks the specificity that produces defensible products. Drew Houston didn't build "file management" -- he built the exact tool he needed when he forgot his USB drive on the Chinatown bus. Revnu built "everything a technical founder needs for growth," which is the kind of scope that emerges from surveying a problem category rather than from a moment of acute personal pain. The stated differentiation -- a cross-channel intelligence layer where data from SEO informs ad decisions -- is architecturally interesting but unvalidated: no public revenue, no user count, no growth metrics exist for Revnu itself. The $15M in transactions cited on the about page is from prior ventures, not from Revnu.

The bull case for Revnu is real and I want to state it honestly. These founders have a better shipping track record than 90% of YC applicants. George's progression from AI Etsy posters to Vinted sniping bots to accounting software to a growth platform shows increasing ambition and sophistication across four products. Art's agent and reverse-engineering background is precisely the technical skill set needed for autonomous marketing orchestration. The GitHub PR integration -- connect your repo, review one AI-generated PR, merge to activate -- is a genuinely developer-native distribution mechanism that none of the well-funded competitors (Jasper, Albert, Gumloop) have attempted. If Revnu can make growth feel like a code review rather than a marketing dashboard, they might carve out the specific niche of developer-founders who refuse to use traditional marketing tools. That's a wedge strategy, and wedge strategies can work even in crowded markets. The Bindwell parallel on founder age and energy is valid. But the parallel breaks precisely where it matters most: Bindwell's market repelled competitors, while Revnu's market is a magnet for them.

Revnu's founders demonstrate high learning velocity -- five shipped products and hackathon wins across xAI, Cursor, Vercel, and Mistral platforms suggest they absorb new technical paradigms fast. What I cannot see is whether that velocity is being applied to Revnu specifically. No iteration stories, no pivot narratives, no weekly growth metrics, no evidence the product has evolved from its initial form. At pre-seed this absence isn't damning, but it means I'm scoring learning rate on prior-venture evidence alone, which caps my confidence. On earnestness, George and Art are plainly builders who ship because they can't stop -- George's portfolio page (ginger.sh) reads like someone who treats product creation as a compulsion, not a career strategy. But the choice of Revnu as a company feels more calculated than obsessive. Their prior products were specific, scrappy hacks: a Vinted sniping bot, Etsy AI automation, accounting for resellers. Revnu is a platform play designed to be a startup. I don't see evidence they'd be building this particular product in a garage with no funding and no YC acceptance letter.

The condition that would flip me to Invest: show me five paying Revnu customers who switched from manually running their own marketing and now refuse to go back -- the "crappy version one" test. If a small number of technical founders are using Revnu obsessively in its current form, the consensus-market concern becomes less relevant because the specific product-market fit within developer-founders may be defensible even as Jasper and Gumloop own the broader space. Right now, I like George and Art more than I like Revnu, and I've learned from the Reddit experience that sometimes the right move is to back the founders and let them find the right idea. But with Reddit, I gave Alexis and Steve their idea over dinner. Revnu's founders may need a similar redirect -- away from the crowded AI marketing platform and toward something uglier, more specific, and harder to replicate. The decisive dimensions are the consensus nature of the idea (scoring low on repulsion) and the generic quality of the problem discovery, which together prevent two exceptional young hackers from reaching the score their raw talent would otherwise warrant.
