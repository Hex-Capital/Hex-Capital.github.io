# AgentPhone -- Paul Graham Evaluation

AgentPhone is two brothers — Manav and Meet Modi — building telephony plumbing for agents, and the signature is unusual: the cofounder configuration is as good as it gets (brothers, like the Collisons), but the organic-pain story is the weakest part of the dossier. That pairing — Collison-shaped team, non-Collison-shaped origin — is what I have to evaluate. The Spring 2026 YC Launch page already lists Google's Agent Development Kit, Replit, LangChain, and Alchemy as users of AgentPhone, which is the kind of pre-launch logo set you do not get by sending cold emails.

On animal resourcefulness, the canonical rung is Brian Armstrong's hand-built Android wallet that rescued his rejected **Coinbase** (2012) application — a specific stunt I can name. AgentPhone has no cereal-box equivalent in the dossier, no anecdote of Meet driving down to a carrier office or Manav writing a 10DLC registration script over a weekend. What AgentPhone does have is a customer roster — Google ADK, Replit, LangChain, Alchemy — that founders without animal energy do not assemble before a YC Launch page goes live. The Modi brothers are not at the Armstrong rung, but they are above the demo-on-Hacker-News floor; they shipped a product that vendors who could have built it themselves chose to integrate.

The lowest rung on organic-idea fit is the AI-wrapper team that did "competitive analysis" instead of suffering the problem — the failure mechanism is that they flinch the first time the product hits an unglamorous edge case because they were never in pain to begin with. AgentPhone's dossier does not contain a "Meet was building an agent and realized it had no phone number" moment; the founders' bios describe Meet shipping AI-agent infrastructure on WhatsApp at Meta for 280M businesses and Manav scaling the Vogue app from 100K to 1M+ users. That is adjacency, not pain. Meet's WhatsApp work is the closest thing — he watched agents need to reach humans through a messaging substrate — but the dossier never quotes him saying he wanted this for himself. I dock for that.

On schlep blindness, AgentPhone is genuinely in the zone — A2P 10DLC registration, STIR/SHAKEN attestation, carrier anti-spam policy for autonomous outbound — and the compounding mechanism over 18 months is the **Coinbase** (2012) one: every carrier rejection and every fraud-rule negotiation becomes a moat that a Twilio SKU launched in a hurry cannot replicate, because the rules for "AI agents calling humans" do not exist yet and someone has to be in the room when they get written. The dossier names the risk explicitly and the team has not publicly described mitigation, which is honest but unfinished.

Hacker genealogy: Meet's All India Rank #1 in Computer Science is exactly the competitive-programming marker I disproportionately fund — it is the same signal class as Patrick Collison's 2005 Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010). Manav shipped the Vogue revamp that earned a Webby. Neither has a public GitHub trail in the dossier, which is the one missing piece; hackers leave tracks and I would feel better seeing a repo. The score would flip a tier upward if Meet had an open-source telephony or agent-runtime project with stars predating AgentPhone — he probably does and the researcher missed it, but I score what is in front of me.

The Collison brothers at **Stripe** (2010) are the single anchor for AgentPhone's cofounder configuration: two technical brothers, one with elite contest credentials, the other with shipping-at-scale experience, attacking developer infrastructure. The brother dynamic is rare and load-bearing — Patrick and John could disagree without the company breaking, and the Modis presumably can too. This is the highest-confidence dimension in the dossier.

Forecast, 36 months: AgentPhone is acquired by Twilio for $40-120M as their agent-identity SKU, or reaches $3-8M ARR as the MCP-native telephony layer for the agent-runtime crowd (Claude Code, Cursor, ADK). It is not dead and it is not the next **Stripe** (2010) — the mechanism is that brothers with strong execution and pre-launch enterprise logos in a hot category get bought before they get big, especially when Twilio reported voice-AI revenue +60% YoY in Q4 FY25 and has obvious motive to own the agent number SKU rather than lose it.

The bear case that stings: Bland AI has raised $65M, Vapi sits at a $130M valuation with millions in revenue, Retell claims $50M ARR — AgentPhone is entering a category where three competitors have a 12-24 month capital and distribution lead, and the "unified webhook" differentiation is a weekend of Twilio engineering work away from being replicated. My answer is that none of those three are positioned as the agent's *phone identity* — they orchestrate outbound campaigns — and the MCP-server distribution into Claude Code and Cursor is a channel the funded incumbents do not yet occupy. The condition that would flip my verdict to pass: if within 6 months Twilio ships an "Agent Number" SKU with a unified voice+SMS webhook before AgentPhone publishes a pricing page, the category compresses and the brothers become an acquihire rather than a company. I write the small check.
