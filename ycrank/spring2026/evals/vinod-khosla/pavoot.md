# Pavoot -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Pavoot is the Pass Example from my own framework written almost verbatim -- an AI agent that "turns events into pipeline" pitched by two ETH Computer Science graduates whose dossier explicitly concedes the core capabilities rest on "commodity foundation-model APIs." Pavoot's highest-scoring dimension is, perversely, founder non-conformity (Gohar Tamrazyan and Ana Yoon Faria de Lima are outsiders to events and B2B marketing entirely), and Pavoot's lowest is required-capital fit -- which together describe a small, capital-light SaaS bet by smart strangers to the buyer, not a science miracle that demands a $50M solo check. That pairing is not a Khosla shape.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the high rung on consequence magnitude because the success-state -- machine intelligence at human or super-human capability -- redrew the labor market I now describe as "$15 trillion of GDP" (Fortune 2026-03-06); the check was justified before the moat was visible because the world-state at maximum success was civilizationally distinct. Pavoot's success-state, in contrast, is that B2B marketers convert ~11 qualified leads instead of ~5 per 47-attendee event and book $48K of pipeline instead of $20K. Cvent still exists. Bizzabo still exists ($43.9M revenue 2024). HubSpot still exists. No incumbent regime collapses; no labor category disappears. Pavoot's homepage metric -- 24 meetings from 47 attendees -- is a productivity delta, not a consequence variable, and that is the exact inversion of my Outlook Business directive that you build the consequential company first.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) failed because the capital plan understated the burn required for the science to actually work, and I still defend that loss because the science was real and the consequence was redrawing liquid fuels. Pavoot's failure mode is the opposite: there is no science to underfund. The dossier names ASR transcription, LLM follow-up generation, and lookalike attendee sourcing -- three capabilities that any well-resourced incumbent or AI SDR vendor (11x, Regie, Clay) can re-implement in a quarter. Cvent acquired Goldcast for ~$300M in December 2025 and is already bundling AI-driven post-event activation into a suite with installed CRM integrations. Pavoot is shipping a feature into a market where the distribution-owning incumbent has already bought the comparable wedge. That is not capital risk repelling consensus VCs; it is commodity risk that consensus VCs will correctly avoid for a different reason.

Pavoot's founders defy expert consensus only in the trivial sense that they are not experts -- Gohar Tamrazyan is a 6-time Swiss Chess Champion with ETH CS, Ana Yoon Faria de Lima is a 20+ Olympiad medalist with ex-AI engineering at BTG Pactual and Itaú Unibanco. Neither has documented B2B marketing, RevOps, or events-industry operating experience. My TED Talk skepticism cuts both ways: I do not want a 20-year Salesforce veteran extrapolating the past, but I also do not get the **Physical Intelligence** (2024) Levine/Finn shape here -- there is no public intellectual reputation, no contradicted field, no "too important not to do" framing. Outsider status without a defied orthodoxy is just unfamiliarity with the buyer.

Learning rate at Pavoot is unscored rather than negative -- Tamrazyan and Faria de Lima met in an ETH AI Center course where they had three days to develop an idea before pitching on day four, which is too short a history to contain a pivot. The **Slash** (2024) Known Exception does not transfer: Slash earned the $100M Series C because the founders had publicly told a specific pivot story (Kanye West) demonstrating documented thesis update under pressure. Pavoot has no such story yet; the dossier shows linear execution from ETH course to YC P26 with one homepage case study and no disclosed design partners.

Required capital at Pavoot is the cleanest negative signal in the dossier. The roadmap is demo-gated SaaS at cal.com/team/pavoot/30min -- per-seat or per-event pricing, no foundation model to train, no fleet to deploy, no plant to build. Pavoot will reach $1M ARR on $3-5M if it reaches it at all. I am structurally over-resourced for that check; this is a Tier-1 generalist or pre-seed specialist deal, not a Khosla concentration bet of the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) $115M shape or the **Genesis AI** (2025) $105M co-led seed shape.

My 36-month forecast for Pavoot: dead, pivoted into an AI SDR niche, or absorbed by an event/CRM incumbent (Cvent, HubSpot, or a Bizzabo successor) for under $30M as an acqui-hire, with sub-$2M ARR at the time of outcome. The mechanism is straightforward: Cvent's December 2025 Goldcast acquisition signals incumbents will buy AI-event capability rather than let an agent layer disintermediate them, and Pavoot's commodity-API stack offers no defense against an incumbent feature release or a better-funded AI SDR vendor doing the same workflow.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash** (2024) precedent -- YC-pedigreed B2B with technical founders, where I overrode consequence-magnitude skepticism because the founder learning rate was documented and the YC channel proved out. One could argue Pavoot fits the same exception: YC P26, technical ETH founders, an incumbent (Cvent) that has just demonstrated willingness to pay ~$300M for the adjacent wedge, meaning a $20-50M acqui-hire is genuinely plausible. The bull case stings because that outcome is real -- but it is not a Khosla outcome. A 3-5x return on a small first check from a YC pre-seed is a great angel result and a rounding error in a $3.5B fund (TechCrunch 2025-02-24); my framework's job is not to harvest those, it is to write the **OpenAI** (2019) check. The verdict flips only if Pavoot publishes evidence of a proprietary cross-event attendee graph or conversation dataset that compounds across customers in a way the dossier currently does not show -- a moat that is data-structural, not API-replicable. Until then, Pavoot is the textbook pass.
