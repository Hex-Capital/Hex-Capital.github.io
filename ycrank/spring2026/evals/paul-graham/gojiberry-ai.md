# Gojiberry AI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is a commercial-animal team running in a software-only category that hackers wouldn't pick — Pierre-Eliott Lallemant, Rom?n Czerny, and Dylan Teixeira got from $0 to $2.5M ARR in 10 months on a $500K raise and stayed profitable, which is the kind of capital-efficiency number that makes me read the team twice. The trouble is that what I find when I read them twice is a YouTube-channel-plus-affiliate-marketer-plus-business-school-CTO trio attacking AI SDRs against Clay's $100M ARR and Apollo's $150M ARR — a non-schlep crowded by better-funded incumbents. So Gojiberry AI scores high where I weight hardest (resourcefulness) and low where I'd normally want a tiebreaker (hacker DNA and schlep posture).

Airbnb sold $40 cereal boxes to fund itself when no investor would; that is the high rung of the animal ladder, and the mechanism is the same one I'm watching at Gojiberry AI — founders generating revenue through unscalable hustle before the institutional money shows up. Pierre-Eliott and Rom?n already bootstrapped CoCo AI to ?500K ARR and sold it in 2025; Dylan independently built and sold Edusign in the same year; then the three of them pivoted Gojiberry AI from a 2,000-person waitlist with zero conversions into $2.5M ARR in 10 months and stayed profitable on $500K raised. That is three prior exits and a recovered pivot — not cereal boxes, but the same metabolic rate. Where Gojiberry AI diverges from the Airbnb rung is that the resourcefulness here reads as professional repeat-operator hustle rather than first-time founders who would rather sell cereal than die — a distinction I'll come back to.

The MBA-AI-wrapper team from my Pass Example fails the schlep test because their work is pure software with no offline grind, no compliance, no manual integration anyone would flinch from. Gojiberry AI sits closer to that rung than I'd like. The product scrapes 30+ public LinkedIn and web signals, drafts emails, books meetings — every byte of it is software a competitor can copy, and G2 reviewers already note Gojiberry AI "depends heavily on social and public signals" and degrades when buyers aren't LinkedIn-active. Coinbase's compliance grind became a moat because nobody else wanted to do it; Gojiberry AI is in a category where 11x.ai, Clay, Apollo, Lemlist, Outreach, Salesloft and Factors.ai all want to do exactly this. There is no schlep here. There is execution risk dressed as a moat.

Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and built Dropbox for himself — that's the organic-idea pattern, and Gojiberry AI has a respectable version of it. Pierre-Eliott and Rom?n explicitly cite outbound as the "most painful part" of operating CoCo AI, and the wearefounders.uk case study traces the Gojiberry AI pivot directly to that pain. Eighteen months out, this dimension compounds well: founders who lived the problem ship the next twenty product decisions correctly without focus groups, which is part of why Gojiberry AI got from a failed v1 with zero conversions to 2,000 paying customers so quickly. The organic-idea engine is real here.

Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist prize for Lisp work before Stripe; that's what hacker genealogy looks like on the page, and it is conspicuously missing from Gojiberry AI. Dylan Teixeira is the CTO and his listed education is I?SEG School of Management; no GitHub account was found for him, or for Pierre-Eliott, or for Rom?n. Pierre-Eliott's pre-founder background is eBay reselling and a YouTube channel; Rom?n's is affiliate marketing. These are growth-and-arbitrage operators, not hackers. For the score on this dimension to flip a tier I would need to see a shipped open-source artifact from Dylan, or a non-trivial technical demo of Gojiberry AI's signal-routing infrastructure that would be hard for Clay's engineering team to replicate in a quarter — neither exists in the dossier.

Reddit's Huffman and Ohanian were dorm roommates; Stripe was two brothers; the long-relationship pattern is what I trust. Gojiberry AI is a 3-founder team where Pierre-Eliott and Rom?n have the CoCo AI co-founding history and Dylan joined from his own Edusign exit. That's a 2-plus-1 structure rather than a unified trio, but the two operators with prior shared P&L plus a CTO who has independently sold a SaaS is a credible configuration — not Airbnb's tightness, but well clear of the solo or hackathon-paired red flag.

My 36-month forecast for Gojiberry AI: acquired by Apollo, HubSpot, or Salesloft for $150-350M at $15-30M ARR sometime in 2027-2028, as the agent layer consolidates into the data-and-distribution incumbents. The mechanism is straightforward — Gojiberry AI's signal-aggregation product runs on public LinkedIn data that Clay and Apollo can replicate, the SMB-only G2 cohort caps the durable contract size, and these founders have already executed the sub-$50M-ARR sell-to-a-US-fund move twice. CoCo AI and Edusign are the priors. The exit muscle is trained.

The strongest case against the acquihire-coded forecast is the bull mechanism I keep almost talking myself into: $2.5M ARR in 10 months profitable on $500K raised is the cheapest growth I've seen in the batch, and I wrote in 2024 that "there is surprisingly little connection between how much a startup spends and how fast it grows." If Gojiberry AI's 35% MoM compounds even six more months, the team is at $15M+ ARR before the Series A conversation, which changes the exit options entirely. The bear answer that wins is my own Red Flag list — "founders volunteer an acquihire-friendly framing, echoing the Kiko (2005) eBay-sale tell of insufficient ambition" — and the dossier shows two prior sub-?500K-ARR sales already executed by this exact team. That is not Kan-and-Shear-returning-from-Kiko energy; that is repeat-small-exit energy, which is a different animal. One verifiable condition would flip me to invest: Gojiberry AI lands a named enterprise logo (>1,000 employees) on a six-figure ACV within the next two quarters, breaking the SMB ceiling that the G2 cohort currently defines. Without that, I pass — politely, with respect for the metrics, and with a note to re-check in six months.
