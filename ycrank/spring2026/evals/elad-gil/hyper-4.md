# Hyper -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Hyper is a two-person team trying to sit as an ambient memory layer between Notion, Cursor, Claude Code, email, and LinkedIn DMs while Glean prints $200M ARR up from $100M nine months earlier (Fortune, Dec 2025) -- the high-low signature here is a real-but-consensus market timing thesis sitting on top of an integration-layer that does not pass the infrastructure test, and that pairing is what dictates the rest of the analysis. Shalin Shah and Kanyes Thaker, both Berkeley '21 with perception/autonomy backgrounds at Matic Robots, are technically credible for the sensor-fusion analogy of this product, but Hyper as positioned is a feature in a category where Glean's $610M war chest and Notion AI's connector roadmap define the ceiling.

On Nonobvious Market Timing, my high rung is Perplexity -- I wrote that seed check before ChatGPT launched because the AI capability curve hadn't crossed into consensus, and the low rung is the consumer social YC pile where no exogenous catalyst exists. Hyper sits below the Harvey mid-rung because the catalyst Hyper points to -- AI tool fragmentation across Cursor, Claude Code, ChatGPT, Notion -- is not nonobvious; it is exactly what every enterprise AI investor has been pricing into Glean's $7.2B valuation since June 2025. The Dimension Market Research figure of 46.7% CAGR for AI-driven KM is a tell that the market is *known*, not nonobvious. Hyper has a real market shift behind it, but the window has been read by consensus, which is the exact opposite of the Coinbase-2012 or Perplexity-2022 setup I look for.

On Infrastructure-Layer Positioning -- and this is where Hyper places lowest against its anchor -- Stripe is the high rung because every internet business built on the payments API, and Wish/ContextLogic is my low rung because no other business depends on a consumer marketplace. Hyper's YC description claims "infused into all your existing AI tools on every chat turn," which sounds like infrastructure but is actually the opposite: Hyper is a *consumer* of other platforms' integration surfaces, not a platform other businesses build on. There is no API-first architecture cited, no developer ecosystem, no signal that another business would write code against Hyper. That places Hyper closer to the Wish rung than the Gusto mid-rung -- Gusto at least owns the system of record for payroll. Hyper, as described, owns no system of record; it is a derivative layer dependent on integrations that platform owners (Notion, OpenAI, Anthropic) can sever or replicate.

Single-Miracle Viability is where my Anduril override has to be tested and rejected for Hyper. Anduril's high-rung override happened because the structural shift in defense procurement was generational; Harvey is the clean one-miracle case where foundation model capability did the work exogenously. Hyper needs at least three miracles compounding: native per-tool memory features (Cursor rules, ChatGPT memory, Notion AI) must stay bad enough to leave a cross-tool gap; enterprise security review must clear a product that passively ingests LinkedIn DMs and email; and Hyper must out-execute Glean and Dust ($21.5M raised, $7.3M ARR) on the same wedge with two people. That's not the Harvey profile -- that's the multi-miracle compounding-odds profile I explicitly filter against, without the generational structural override Anduril had.

On Operator-Scaler Founder Profile, Parker Conrad rebuilding Rippling after Zenefits is my high rung; first-time founders with no scaling experience are the low rung. Shah and Thaker are technically strong from Matic Robots, but neither has documented scaling experience, no prior exit, and the dossier flags a possible pivot from a March 2026 iOS voice product to a May 2026 cross-tool company-brain -- which is either commendable speed or scope thrash, and at two people without a designated CEO in the public record it raises my "every company needs a final decision-maker" concern. On Margin Architecture, no pricing page exists at heyhyper.ai and the likely path is per-seat SaaS, which sits in the Gusto mid-rung at best and far from the Abridge per-encounter high rung that maps to my "seats to cognition" thesis.

My 36-month forecast: Hyper either pivots into a narrower AI-native developer wedge or gets acqui-hired by Notion, Cursor, or an enterprise search incumbent for under $50M before clearing $5M ARR, because the integration-layer position cannot survive native memory shipping inside the underlying tools. The mechanism is direct: when Notion AI's external connectors and Cursor/Claude Code's per-project memory each get to "good enough," Hyper's cross-tool delta compresses faster than its enterprise security posture can mature to land Glean-class buyers.

The bull case that stings: Shah and Thaker's Matic perception backgrounds are not generic AI-wrapper resumes -- multi-source sensor fusion is *exactly* the technical primitive for cross-tool context fusion, and that's a nonobvious founder-market fit most investors will miss because they read "robotics" and don't see the analogy. Glean's nine-month ARR doubling proves enterprise budgets exist; if Hyper finds a wedge in AI-native Series A/B startups too small for Glean and too sprawled for Notion, the pricing power could compound. I dismantle this with the Wish lesson: technical credibility without infrastructure positioning and without margin architecture produces a growth story, not a defensible business -- and Hyper's product description gives me neither. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Hyper landing five paid pilots with named Series B+ AI-native companies showing 60%+ weekly active retention at day 90, with evidence of value-based pricing (per-decision, per-context-injection) rather than per-seat. Until then I pass.
