# Soria -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Adam Ron spent his career as a VP of Equity Research at Bank of America covering healthcare facilities and managed care -- which is exactly the buyer/user persona Soria is selling to -- and that single fact is the only thing about Soria that interests me. Strip Ron out and you have a Spring 2026 YC company whose one-line pitch is "AI Bloomberg Terminal built for Healthcare" -- a "the [Hot Sector] for [Vertical]" formulation I explicitly flag as a red flag. The signature here is a domain-credible operator wrapping the most consensus-shaped vertical-AI-SaaS asset in the 2026 batch, and the gap between those two things is the whole evaluation.

Solana at $0.05/coin is what a real top-rung non-consensus entry looks like on Dimension 1 -- a category nobody else would touch, priced at numbers that only made sense if you'd already metabolized that "when people 'agree' something is a good idea, it probably isn't." Soria sits nowhere near that rung. The dossier names Hebbia ($700M valuation, a16z-backed, $13M ARR), Daloopa ($13M July 2025 strategic round), Rogo, AlphaSense, and BloombergGPT all converging on AI-over-finance-data -- this is precisely the consensus heatmap, not a category my peers have quit. The dossier discloses no round size and no lead, which under my default-when-silent rule earns the bottom of the band; Soria's "trusted by Bulge Bracket Banks" self-description and YC partner Harj Taggar's involvement tell me the price will land at 2026 AI-SaaS-YC median, which is a price I cannot generate alpha from.

A purely paid-acquisition enterprise SaaS with no founder media footprint is the failure mode on Dimension 4 -- and Soria's dossier walks straight into it. Neither Adam Ron nor Cameron Spiller has a public Twitter, neither has GitHub, neither publishes. The "distribution" is Adam Ron's BofA equity-research rolodex, which is a sales channel, not a community on the cap table. The $60M Slow Creator Fund exists precisely because I think owned audiences compound into a moat no SaaS company can buy -- Soria has the opposite shape, and nothing in the 125-data-source pipeline or the DBOS case study changes that.

Cameron Spiller's CTO background -- B.S. CompE Virginia Tech, prior engineering at Pinwheel, Findigs, Trigo Data -- is the mechanism question on Dimension 2: how does Soria's capital structure compound over the next 18 months? The 125+ source pipeline is genuine schema work, but per-seat enterprise SaaS to bulge brackets is a standard cap table that will need three to five more rounds before default-alive, and Soria has disclosed zero ARR, zero named logos, zero seat counts. The financing instrument is generic, the buyer cycle is 9-12 months at bulge brackets, and there is no unusual structure (revenue share, holdco, data-licensing royalty) that would let me underwrite Soria in a shape Hebbia's term sheet cannot match.

The counterfactual on Dimension 3 -- Founder Conviction -- is what would have to flip for Adam Ron to move from "credible domain operator" to "missionary I'll back at consensus price anyway." It would take public writing -- a Substack on why sell-side healthcare research is broken, a five-year working dataset he maintained nights-and-weekends at BofA before quitting, an opinionated thread on why Bloomberg's healthcare module fails. Soria's dossier offers none of that: no Twitter, no GitHub, no essays, just a LinkedIn that reads "Co-founder of Soria (YC P26) | Former BofA." The FINRA Series 7 and the BofA VP title give Ron a real prior at the problem, which is why I'm not at the floor of the band -- but absent any artifact of pre-funding obsession, this reads as "smart analyst saw an opportunity," not "missionary who'd build this whether or not VCs funded it."

Ro is the single-anchor parallel on Dimension 5. Ro was a healthcare-DTC company I backed despite the capital intensity because the 50-state prescription-rail regulatory schleps constituted a genuine venture moment -- only VC dollars could clear the licensing work, and that was the moat. Soria is healthcare-data-for-finance, not healthcare-DTC -- the schlep here is licensing 125 vendor datasets and normalizing them, which is real engineering but not a regulatory moat that compounds against Hebbia bundling a healthcare data pack next quarter. "AI is just more internet" from the Lonsdale podcast applies cleanly: Soria is more internet for healthcare analysts, and that's a real product, but it's not the sleeve I'm convicted on.

My 36-month forecast on Soria: $2M-$5M ARR across four to eight healthcare research desks, then an acqui-hire into Hebbia, AlphaSense, or Daloopa at a 1-3x revenue multiple once one of those horizontal players ships a healthcare data pack and Soria's vertical wedge collapses. The mechanism is straightforward -- Adam Ron's BofA network gets Soria the first handful of bulge-bracket seats, the 125-source pipeline holds for 12-18 months, and then a $120M-raised competitor with a generalist sales motion bundles equivalent data and Soria's per-seat pricing has nowhere to go.

The bull case on Soria that should sting: Daloopa just took $13M in July 2025 to build vertical AI finance data infrastructure -- the market is paying for this exact shape, and Adam Ron's founder-market-fit (former VP Equity Research, healthcare facilities and managed care, FINRA Series 7) is sharper than Daloopa's founders' on the healthcare vertical specifically. Horizontal incumbents have historically been bad at vertical depth -- Bloomberg has had 40 years to build a real healthcare module and hasn't -- and Ro is the Known Exception precedent for backing a healthcare-adjacent company whose capital intensity is the moat. The case is real, and the dismantle is this: Ro's moat was 50-state prescription licensing, a genuine multi-year regulatory schlep; Soria's "moat" is 125 data-source integrations, which is six engineers and twelve months for a well-capitalized competitor. The flip condition is concrete: if Soria comes in at <$8M post-money with no tier-1 lead AND Adam Ron can show me a signed multi-year contract with a named bulge-bracket bank at $250K+ ACV, I revisit. Until then, this is the YC AI-vertical-SaaS pattern at the YC AI-vertical-SaaS price, and I pass.
