# HeyClicky -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

HeyClicky is the rare profile where the product is more touchable than the category is defensible: Farza Majeed has built a macOS overlay you can download and feel in five minutes, but he has built it directly into the lane Cluely ($20.3M raised, a16z) and Granola ($1.5B valuation, March 2026) and OpenAI's "Work with Apps" are already crowding. The signature is high physical verifiability plus a single unambiguous solo CEO, sitting on top of a commodity software stack in the most consensus AI category of the batch.

HeyClicky's strongest rung is Physical-World Verifiability, and on this ladder I anchor at Niantic/Ingress (~2015) -- the product I obsessively played myself before writing the check -- on the high end, HQ Trivia (2017) on the mid rung where tactile virality once made me override governance, and the Pass Example dev-tools API on the low end. HeyClicky sits between Niantic and HQ Trivia: 5.8k GitHub stars on farzaa/clicky, a 4.9/23 Product Hunt rating, and ~3M views on the launch demo say a normal human can install Clicky, point at their cursor, and feel the buddy-character pull within an hour. That is closer to the Ingress test than to abstract B2B vapor.

HeyClicky's weakest rung is Capital-Intensive / Unsexy-Regulated Swing, and on that ladder SpaceX (2008) is the high anchor, Flock Safety (~2018) is the mid rung where hardware met consumer aesthetics, and the Pass Example -- the consensus YC AI-agent darling I will not write into -- is the low rung. HeyClicky sits at the Pass Example end. The README documents a stack of Claude SSE + AssemblyAI + ElevenLabs that Farza himself open-sourced in April 2026; Windows clones (emreyilmaz46/clicky_windows, jasonkneen/openclicky) already exist. There is no defense, energy, fertility, regulated finance, or aesthetically transgressive lane here -- this is a menu-bar app whose moat compounds at exactly the rate the underlying models commoditize, which is fast.

Founder Surfaced From Lived Experience sits closer to the Anduril/Luckey high rung than to the Pass Example "ex-FAANG PM discovered the problem in a memo" low rung -- DeepMind/Hassabis is my mid-rung polymath exception. HeyClicky's pin is Farza's buildspace run (2019-2024, $10M Series A from a16z, shut down August 2024); he has spent five years inside the exact education-and-builders audience he is now selling Clicky to as "the future of education and the future of AI interfaces." This is autobiographical, not analytical, and it counts even though buildspace did not exit cleanly.

Magically Weird, Non-Consensus is HeyClicky's second-weakest rung, and here Crusoe (~2018) is the high anchor where credible VCs passed on aesthetic grounds, Niantic (~2015) is the mid rung, and the consensus YC AI-agent Pass Example is the low rung. HeyClicky lives at the low rung. Cluely raised $20.3M from a16z in June 2025, Granola hit $1.5B in March 2026, and the dossier itself lists OpenAI Mac and Anthropic computer use as direct overlaps. A 3M-view launch demo is the opposite of "find them before it becomes consensus" -- it is consensus already.

Single Unambiguous CEO is HeyClicky's quiet strength. SpaceX/Musk is the high rung, Anduril/Luckey the mid, and HQ Trivia's Yusupov/Kroll feud the low. HeyClicky is a solo founder, which sits high on this ladder by definition -- no 50/50 tiebreaker problem, no top-grading risk from a prior board. The cost is the inverse: solo execution capacity at zero-headcount on a consumer infra product is a single-point-of-failure, and buildspace shutting down August 2024 is the prior data point I cannot un-see.

My 36-month forecast for HeyClicky: Farza pivots within 12-18 months from the generic Cluely-adjacent overlay positioning toward an education-vertical product mining the buildspace alumni network, lands somewhere between $1M and $4M ARR on consumer subscriptions, and never crosses the $10M ARR threshold that would justify a venture-scale round; alternative outcome is acqui-hire by a tier-2 consumer AI player or quiet shutdown as OpenAI's "Work with Apps" absorbs the category. The mechanism is that the lived-experience biography is in education, not in screen-aware OS overlays -- when the category commoditizes, Farza will instinctively retreat to the lane he actually came from.

The strongest objection is the HQ Trivia exception: tactile, addictive consumer pull (~3M views, 5.8k stars, 89.3K @FarzaTV followers) is exactly the kind of "I can feel the viral pull before any metric exists" override I have written before. The objection stings because if Clicky becomes the character-led AI buddy the way HQ Trivia became the trivia app -- a tactile artifact with brand gravity -- then category consensus and commodity stack stop mattering. I dismantle it on this: the HQ Trivia override was specifically against unresolved co-founder governance, a domain where tactile pull genuinely substituted for the missing heuristic. HeyClicky's deficits are different in kind -- commodity stack the founder himself open-sourced, plus OpenAI and Anthropic shipping native equivalents on Mac. The override does not transfer cleanly across dimensions. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: HeyClicky posts $500K+ ARR concentrated in a paid education tier within six months, proving Farza's buildspace audience converts to paid Clicky users for tutoring -- not just downloads the free OSS binary. Until then, this is a rebuttal, not a hard no.
