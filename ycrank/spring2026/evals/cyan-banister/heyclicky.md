# HeyClicky -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Farza Majeed is a sole founder shipping a downloadable Mac app with 5.8K-6.1K GitHub stars in roughly four weeks and 108.6K followers on @FarzaTV behind him, which means HeyClicky scores cleanest exactly where most pre-seed pitches break -- governance and tactility -- and scores weakest exactly where my checks tend to live: capital intensity and weirdness. That is the signature. HeyClicky is a clean-CEO, installable-today consumer AI wrapper sitting one lane over from Cluely's $20.3M Series A and Granola's $1.5B valuation, which is the lane I structurally avoid.

Flock Safety is the right HIGH rung for HeyClicky on physical-world verifiability, because the mechanism that earned Flock the check -- a product I could observe in the wild before I read a deck -- is the same mechanism Farza is leaning on with HeyClicky: a free Mac download, a YouTube demo at youtu.be/ER8TCqFjaYI, and an MIT-licensed Swift repo any of us can fork tonight. Where HeyClicky diverges from Flock is that the artifact lives entirely inside the laptop and competes with Apple Intelligence at the OS layer; Flock's cameras compounded because Apple was never going to install a license-plate reader on a pole. HeyClicky's installability is real and earns the score, but the artifact is the kind I can touch and the kind Apple can replace in one keynote.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on -- two ex-FAANG PMs, $20M oversubscribed seed, six funds chasing -- is the failure pattern HeyClicky is closest to on the magically-weird axis, and I have to test whether HeyClicky actually shares it. Cluely raised $15M from a16z in June 2025 for screen-aware meeting overlays; Granola raised $125M at $1.5B in March 2026 for AI notetaking; Apple Intelligence sits underneath both of them. HeyClicky's "voice + screen-aware Mac agent" sentence could be airdropped into any of those decks. The mitigant is that Farza open-sourced the codebase and explicitly invited forks ("Go forth into the wild..." -- @FarzaTV 2041691382008705321), which is not a fundraising posture, but it is also not non-consensus -- it is consensus delivered with a different aesthetic. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, but I cannot find a hidden game underneath HeyClicky yet.

Farza Majeed running buildspace -- $10M raised at $100M from a16z and YC, OpenAI sponsorships, an education community he built and then voluntarily wound down in August 2024 because he could not "craft an updated direction that feels worth pursuing" -- is the founder-from-lived-experience evidence for HeyClicky, and it cuts both ways. The lived domain is real: HeyClicky's README frames it as an AI teacher next to the cursor, which is buildspace compressed into a Swift binary. That is not a memo-discovered problem. But over 18 months this dimension compounds only if the founder stays in the chair through the boring middle, and the buildspace closing letter is the documented inverse of that compounding -- a founder who has already shown he will voluntarily exit a well-capitalized vehicle when the direction stops feeling alive. HandUp is the Known Exception I reach for here -- mission-aligned founder, modest commercial ceiling, I wrote the check anyway -- but HandUp's exception was *my* biography mirrored back. HeyClicky's mission is education-adjacent, not homelessness-adjacent, so the exception does not transfer.

Sole founder, no co-founder, no 50/50 split, no unresolved tiebreaker -- HeyClicky is the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll feud I have cited for years. Farza is the named CEO and the only equity holder of consequence. This is the dimension where HeyClicky simply wins, and I am not going to score it down for any reason the dossier gives me.

Counterfactual on the capital-intensity axis: what would have to be true for HeyClicky to flip from a 5/15 to a 10/15? HeyClicky would need to be building something Apple structurally cannot ship -- a regulated workflow, a hardware peripheral, an unsexy enterprise integration into a domain Apple does not enter. Instead HeyClicky lists Notion, Gmail, Calendar, DaVinci Resolve, Figma, and After Effects integrations -- exactly the consumer-productivity surface Apple Intelligence is walking toward. The dimension does not flip on any plausible 2026 roadmap.

My 36-month forecast for HeyClicky: Farza either voluntarily shuts it down the way he shut down buildspace in August 2024, or HeyClicky gets acqui-hired for $15M-$40M by a mid-tier AI consumer company (Perplexity, Replit, or a Cluely/Granola roll-up) on the strength of Farza's 108.6K-follower distribution rather than the product's defensibility. Sub-$10M ARR, no IPO path, no venture-scale outcome from this seat. The mechanism is the signature itself: clean governance plus tactile artifact plus consensus category plus a founder with a documented pattern of voluntary exit equals a talent-and-distribution acquisition, not a Crusoe.

The strongest case against my forecast is the buildspace-as-feature read: Farza did not fail at buildspace, he refused to operate without conviction at $10M raised, and that refusal is exactly the founder authenticity I claim to underwrite. Add the 5.8K-6.1K GitHub stars in four weeks, the 108.6K X following, and the second-time-YC signal, and a bull would argue HeyClicky is the rare consumer AI bet where the founder *is* the moat -- the polymath educator-builder thesis I used to override pedigree concerns at DeepMind. The dossier evidence that wins for me is the category structure, not the founder: Cluely at $20.3M and Granola at $1.5B are not signals that the category is wide open, they are signals that the round HeyClicky will raise next is going to be priced as if the outcome is certain, which is the opposite of "find them before it becomes consensus." HeyClicky flips to an invest if Farza explicitly narrows to a regulated or unsexy vertical -- legal, accounting, clinical documentation -- where Apple Intelligence cannot follow and where the AI teacher framing has a domain moat. Until that narrowing is on the website, I am one foot in love and zero feet in the round.
