# Andustry -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Andustry is a two-person commission broker putting GPT-4-class agents on top of an existing RFQ workflow, and the most revealing line in the dossier is the "30% average price reduction" claim — because the product's success is the product's own margin compression. Het Dave and Johann Stürken are pitching a software wrapper on a workflow Xometry already prosecutes at $687M FY2025 revenue, and the success-state of Andustry leaves the global industrial-sourcing map looking exactly like it does today, only faster. That is the entry-point question I always run first, and Andustry fails it before I look at anything else.

**Impossible Foods** (2011) is the HIGH rung on consequence — backing plant protein at a moment when "alt-meat" was a fringe category, because the success-state displaced animal protein production as an industrial system. Andustry's success-state, by contrast, is "SME manufacturers get quotes in 48 hours instead of weeks," with one cited customer saving $400K on a $1.2M CNC order. That is a procurement-cycle compression, not a civilizational variable; no incumbent regime collapses, no $15T labor pool gets displaced in the Fortune 2026-03-06 sense, and the dossier does not even attempt a TAM figure. Andustry sits between my Pass-example sales-call summarizer and **DoorDash** (2013) — closer to the summarizer, because DoorDash at minimum reshaped consumer-commerce distribution, and Andustry reshapes nothing structural.

**KiOR** (~2008) is the LOW rung on technical/capital risk in the *inverse* sense I care about — KiOR repelled consensus VCs precisely because the catalyst-yield, feedstock, and refinery-integration miracles were each falsifiable physics bets, and that is why I led it through ~$160M of losses. Andustry's failure mode is the mirror image: zero scientific miracles, zero capital intensity, an asset-light broker stack any team with LangChain and a Stripe account can replicate in a quarter. The dossier itself concedes "the broker UX itself is replicable by any team with LLM agent infrastructure." Xometry and MISUMI/Fictiv ($350M acquisition, April 2025) have the supplier graphs *and* the capital to wrap AI quoting around them — Andustry's moat-claim is "hand-qualified" suppliers, which is the oldest non-moat in distribution.

On founder-defies-consensus, the mechanism that would compound here is a founder publicly contradicting their field — and Andustry has the opposite signal. Stürken comes from McKinsey and a family trading-goods business; Dave comes from CDTM/TUM and AppliedAI. That is the exact "career domain expert continuing the same playbook with AI" profile my TED Talk warned against, where UC Berkeley's 28,000-prediction study showed experts were "only slightly more accurate than chance." Over 18 months, Andustry's founder profile compounds into a respectable European industrial-broker boutique — not a category-collapsing instigator.

The counterfactual on founder learning rate: for Andustry to flip from mid-low to **Slash**-tier (the $100M Series C I led on the back of a documented Kanye-pivot), the dossier would need a named prior pivot from either Dave or Stürken — a shut-down company, a reversed thesis, a public "we were wrong about X" moment. There is none in the dossier; this is linear-plan execution by two first-time founders. I apply the absent-evidence default rather than penalize harder, because pre-seed founders often have not yet earned the chance to pivot publicly.

On capital fit, the single-anchor parallel is **Genesis AI** (2025), where I co-led $105M because the foundation-model-for-robots roadmap *required* that capital to hit first commercial milestone. Andustry's commission-only, two-person, asset-light architecture needs $1-3M to test the agent loop and prove unit economics — I am structurally over-resourced for this deal, and Andustry would be funded better by a focused seed shop than by Khosla Ventures. The dossier's transatlantic US-415/DE-172 footprint on a two-person team actually argues for *less* capital, not more, until the throughput question is resolved.

Andustry's analytical signature: a competent European industrial broker wrapped in LLM agents, with the lowest dimension being technical/capital risk that repels consensus VCs (it repels nobody) and the highest being a procurement-compression consequence that still does not clear my consequence bar. 36-month forecast: Andustry reaches $1-4M in annualized commission revenue, gets squeezed as Xometry and MISUMI/Fictiv ship competing AI-quoting flows into off-the-shelf machines, and either gets acqui-hired for <$30M by a European industrial distributor leveraging Stürken's family-trading network, or pivots to a vertical metals-trading focus (the 2,000 tonnes/month smelter signal in the dossier is the most defensible single thread they have).

The strongest bull case against my pass: Stürken's family trading-goods business is an unfair distribution wedge into European SME manufacturers that Xometry's US-centric sales motion cannot replicate, and the proprietary supplier-quote dataset — price/lead-time/quality outcomes per SKU per region — compounds into a pricing-intelligence moat the dossier explicitly hypothesizes. The **Slash** exception precedent matters here: I have overridden consequence-magnitude weakness before on YC pedigree plus founder-execution signal. But Slash had a documented pivot and a wedge into US consumer business banking with network-effect economics; Andustry has neither a documented pivot nor network effects — supplier matching is many-to-many transactional, not network-compounding. The one verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed multi-year sourcing agreement with a named Fortune 500 industrial buyer at $10M+ annual commission run-rate within 12 months, demonstrating that Andustry's agent loop produces a buyer lock-in Xometry cannot match. Absent that, I pass — and any Tier-1 generalist seed fund should take this deal instead of me.
