# Cohesion -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Cohesion is a founder-product-market-fit company wearing the wrong distribution chassis: Devon Krapcho spent 5+ years as a buy-side software analyst at Long Path Partners and Matt Munns built AI for investors at T. Rowe Price, which is the specific-knowledge gate clearing emphatically -- but the business that knowledge is being poured into is enterprise SaaS sold seat-by-seat into hedge funds, the textbook capital-and-labor leverage shape I score down before the founder section loads. The high/low pairing here is sharp. Cohesion's analytical signature is *deep-domain founders building permissioned, single-sided B2B software in a category where every adjacent player is better-capitalized*.

The leverage check runs first, and Cohesion fails it the way I expect enterprise fintech to fail it. The HIGH rung on this dimension is **Twitter** (2007), where media-as-protocol meant the marginal cost of distribution was zero and anyone in the world could compound the platform without permission. Cohesion's primary distribution motion is direct sales into long/short funds via Krapcho's hedge-fund-analyst network -- labor leverage, the kind that "requires permission" (nav.al/product-media). There is no GitHub for any of the three founders, no public X presence, no open-source artifact, no creator-distribution surface. The cited 10+ funds at $10Bn+ combined AUM were not won by code that replicated itself; they were won the way every analyst-targeting tool gets won, with meetings. That is the **Stack Overflow** pattern run backwards.

The lowest-rung company on the network-compounding dimension is the kind of single-sided enterprise SaaS where each customer is economically independent of the next -- and worse, in Cohesion's case, *actively wants* to be independent of the next. Hedge funds running fundamental long/short books treat alpha signals as zero-sum; the Nth Cohesion customer makes the (N-1) prior customers *less* valuable if the signal surfaces from the same podcast and tweet stream. The Perplexity exception (criterion #2 waived because criterion #1 and #3 cleared at top quartile) does not rescue Cohesion here, because criterion #1 fails too -- so the three-of-five-gates-clear math that justified the Perplexity check does not assemble.

Founder specific knowledge is where Cohesion earns its real points and where I have to be honest with myself. Krapcho covered software for 5+ years at Long Path Partners; Munns built AI-for-investors *inside* T. Rowe Price; these are not ex-McKinsey-PM origin stories, these are lived-problem-ownership origin stories of the kind I funded in Atwood and Spolsky at **Stack Overflow** (2008). The dossier shows none of the Red Flag tells -- no pedigree-as-thesis four-logos slide, no "responding to a competitor's success" framing, no premature integrity self-advertisement. If Cohesion were distributing through an open-source agent framework or a free creator-facing tier, this founder profile would put it at 75+. It is not.

Market pivot-room compounds against Cohesion over the next 18 months in a specific way. The TAM here is fundamental equity analysts at hedge funds -- a vertical that *feels* big because the AUM numbers are large, but the seat count is small and the buyer is exactly the buyer AlphaSense ($500M ARR, $4B valuation) and Hebbia ($700M valuation, $161M raised) and Rogo ($300M+ raised, $160M Series D April 2026) are already calling on. Pivot vectors exist -- sell-side research, corporate strategy, private credit -- but each requires re-doing the GTM from zero against a different incumbent. The "first idea never works" axiom says Cohesion will need 2-3 pivots inside the seed runway; the dossier shows one narrow lane and four well-funded incumbents in it.

The 10-year-partner test on the founder trio reads as neutral-positive but invisible. Counterfactual: for this dimension to flip from a default-7 into a top-quartile read, I would need to see Krapcho or Munns writing publicly about the buy-side workflow with the depth Spolsky brought to Joel-on-Software, or a clean prior shipped artifact under their names. None of the three founders has a discoverable Twitter, GitHub, or long-form writing footprint. That is not disqualifying -- silence is not negative -- but it is the absence of the organic media-leverage signal that makes me size up when the architecture passes.

Cohesion in 36 months: $4-9M ARR across 40-70 hedge fund accounts, then an acqui-hire or tuck-in by AlphaSense or Hebbia in the $30-80M range as the incumbents extend their agent surface into podcast/X monitoring and the standalone wedge collapses. The mechanism is the Rogo/AlphaSense/Hebbia capital overhang -- when four better-funded players are building agentic finance products and one of them (Rogo) just raised $160M in April 2026, the narrow-feature startup gets bought for the engineering team and the customer logos before it reaches escape velocity. This forecast is consistent with the signature: deep founder-market fit produces real revenue and a clean exit; absent leverage architecture caps the upside geometry below the 10,000x bound my math requires at angel size.

The strongest bull case against my pass leans on the Perplexity exception: Aravind Srinivas's Berkeley retrieval PhD plus pre-consensus AI timing waived the network-compounding criterion, and one could argue Krapcho's Long Path Partners experience plus Munns's T. Rowe AI work is the same trade -- specific knowledge so rare that asymmetric-payoff math tolerates the structural gaps. The objection stings because Munns building AI-for-investors *inside* a $1.5T asset manager is the kind of credential most YC fintech founders cannot fake. It does not win. Perplexity's permissionless distribution -- a free web app any user in the world could reach -- carried the leverage-architecture load while criterion #2 was waived. Cohesion has no such surface; its distribution is hedge-fund procurement, the most permissioned channel in software. Two gates fail (criterion #1 and #2), not one, and the exception precedent requires the opposite. The condition that flips me to invest: Cohesion ships a free, public, code-or-media-native product surface -- an open-source agent for tracking earnings transcripts, a public newsletter built off their indexing pipeline -- that distributes without hedge-fund procurement and acquires organic users at zero marginal cost. Until then, this is a competent company built on labor leverage in a category I do not fund.
