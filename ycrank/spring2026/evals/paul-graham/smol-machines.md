# smol machines -- Paul Graham Evaluation

BinBin He worked on Firecracker at AWS — the microVM engine behind Lambda and Fargate — and then left to build smolvm, a portable VM tool that boots in under 200ms and runs on macOS, which Firecracker cannot do. That pedigree is the most striking thing about smol machines. It's the kind of founder-market fit where someone didn't read about the problem in a blog post; they helped build the prior generation of the technology and saw exactly where it fell short. Drew Houston forgot his USB drive and built Dropbox. BinBin He shipped serverless compute infrastructure at Amazon and built what was missing for developers on their own machines. When someone has spent years inside a system's limitations, their understanding of what needs to exist next has a texture that market research can never replicate. smol machines scores high on organic problem discovery for exactly this reason — the origin is lived experience at the frontier, not a TAM slide.

Fu Qiao is listed as smol machines' co-founder on the YC page, but I can find almost nothing about them — no GitHub repos, no retrievable LinkedIn details, no documented background. The YC page itself lists team size as 1. I've said for twenty years that starting a startup is too hard for one person, and I mean it. Building a cross-platform VMM that targets four architectures (macOS Apple Silicon, macOS Intel, Linux x86_64, Linux aarch64) with a team that is functionally one person is an extraordinarily demanding engineering surface area. BinBin He has 476 commits and 2,500 GitHub stars on smolvm, which shows real output. But smol machines needs someone to brainstorm with, someone to challenge bad decisions, someone to handle the commercial side while the systems engineer is debugging virtio balloon memory allocation. The invisible co-founder is not a neutral signal — it's a gap I'd want to understand before writing a check.

smol machines sits squarely in the "hackers building for hackers" pattern that defines most of my best investments — Stripe, Replit, Retool. And there's genuine schlep here. Cross-platform hypervisor engineering, dealing with Apple's Hypervisor.framework and Linux KVM, building a custom kernel via libkrunfw — this is the kind of unglamorous complexity that makes other developers say "ugh, that sounds terrible." That disgust reaction is a competitive moat. Docker raised $393M and built its entire business model around shared-kernel containers; adding full hypervisor isolation would undermine the container abstraction Docker's ecosystem depends on. Firecracker is structurally Linux-only. These are real reasons incumbents won't replicate smol machines' approach quickly. The parallel to Stripe is instructive — the Collison brothers tackled PCI compliance and bank integrations because the schlep repelled every other developer who could see the opportunity. But Stripe had two brilliant co-founders working in lockstep, and they owned their payments infrastructure end-to-end. smol machines depends on libkrun, an open-source library maintained by Red Hat's containers team. That upstream dependency means BinBin He doesn't control his foundational virtualization layer. If Red Hat changes direction, smol machines faces significant re-engineering.

The idea itself — shipping an entire VM with your software the way Electron ships a browser — is clever but not truly contrarian. It's a "better mousetrap" positioned between Docker and QEMU, not something that makes knowledgeable people say "that will never work." E2B raised $21M for cloud-hosted sandboxes using Firecracker. Fly.io raised $115M building Firecracker-based machines. The broader thesis that developers need lightweight isolated environments is already validated, which means the return premium from being right when everyone else is wrong has been competed away. smol machines' specific angle — local, portable, macOS-native — is a narrower claim, but it reads more as a product differentiation than a worldview that initially repels. Nobody hears "faster, more isolated VMs for developers" and recoils. They nod. That's not where I find the best returns.

BinBin He's sustained open-source output — ERA with 303 GitHub stars, envmap with 125, plus smolvm itself — and his FIRST Robotics mentoring suggest someone who builds because they can't help themselves, not because they're optimizing for a TechCrunch headline. smol machines feels like a project born from genuine technical obsession, not from a brainstorming session about market opportunities. That earns real points on earnest builder identity. But I don't have the resourcefulness stories I need. Where are the creative workarounds? What happened when things broke? The Airbnb founders sold cereal boxes when they ran out of money — that story told me everything about what they'd do when the next impossible obstacle appeared. From smol machines I see competent engineering execution, but I don't see the "animal" moments. 2,500 GitHub stars and a Show HN post are standard developer-tool launch mechanics, not evidence of relentless resourcefulness under constraint. BinBin He may have those stories; they're just not in the record.

The strongest bull case for smol machines is this: AI coding agents — Claude Code, Codex, Cursor — increasingly need sandboxed execution environments to run untrusted generated code, and smol machines is building exactly the right primitive at exactly the right moment. A local, sub-200ms-boot VM with network-off-by-default is precisely what an AI agent orchestrator wants. If smol machines becomes the standard sandbox for AI-generated code execution, the growth could be superlinear — each new AI tool that adopts smolvm brings its entire user base. Combined with BinBin He's genuine Firecracker expertise and the schlep blindness moat, an advocate would argue this is Stripe-level founder-market fit in a nascent category. I take this seriously. But it doesn't overcome the structural concerns: a functionally solo founder on a four-platform engineering surface, upstream dependency on technology he doesn't control, no monetization plan, and an Apache-2.0 license that means anyone can fork the project if it gains traction. The condition that would flip me to Invest is simple — show me a real co-founder with complementary skills and a committed working relationship, and show me that AI agent platforms are actually adopting smolvm (not just discussing it in GitHub issues).

smol machines lands in Neutral territory because the organic problem discovery is genuinely strong — BinBin He lived inside Firecracker at AWS and built what was missing — but the functionally-solo-founder structure directly contradicts one of my firmest heuristics. Those two dimensions pull in opposite directions, and the absence of resourcefulness stories and monetization signal leave nothing to break the tie upward.
