# Salesgraph -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Salesgraph is a founder-credible team — Ricardo Nunez and Ruhan Ponnada, both first-ten at Mintlify where Ricardo built the internal business-case generator that became the prototype — occupying the worst available position on the sales stack: an orchestration layer sitting between Gong's $300M+ ARR call-data ownership below and the freshly-merged Clari+Salesloft ~$450M ARR revenue-orchestration platform above. That high-on-shipping, low-on-distribution-position pairing is Salesgraph's signature in my portfolio, and it tells you what kind of company this is before you read another paragraph: the right founders, the wrong rung.

The Founder Ships dimension is where Salesgraph earns its real points, and the load-bearing analogy is **Perplexity** (2022) — Aravind Srinivas pinging cold and showing up a week later with the thing built. Salesgraph diverges from the **Perplexity** rung in a specific way that matters: Ricardo's shipped artifact isn't a public consumer surface compounding on its own (Perplexity had query growth I could watch), it's an *internal* Mintlify business-case generator described in the YC blurb but with no public usage to inspect. The GitHub on @ricardonunez-io has no repo above 100 stars, and Ruhan's GitHub doesn't surface at all. So Salesgraph sits between **Perplexity** and a generic "ex-startup engineer" pre-seed pair — closer to **Perplexity** because the precursor product *is* the company, not adjacent — but the shipping rhythm is asserted in a YC paragraph rather than legible in artifacts I can click on.

The Lyft Series C pass is the failure mechanism that matters for Salesgraph's lowest dimension. **Lyft** failed my index test because the workflow path of rideshare belonged to **Uber**, and #2 in a network-effect category captures a fraction of #1's economics. Salesgraph runs the same pattern on a different surface: the orchestration layer over Gong, Salesforce, Outreach, Salesloft is a workflow path that the data-owning incumbents are *already* walking — Gong shipped Gong Engage, Clari+Salesloft closed in December 2025 specifically to own revenue orchestration end-to-end, and Salesforce Agentforce is repricing API access (CIO Dive 2025). Salesgraph doesn't sit in the workflow path the way **Stripe** sits in payments rails; it sits in a corridor the incumbents have publicly announced they're filling. That's the **Lyft** problem, just with Slack messages instead of rides.

Market Re-Opening — mechanism analysis. The dossier names LLM cost/latency reduction across 2024-2025 plus broad Gong/Salesforce/HubSpot API availability as the "why now." That's a real catalyst, but it's the *generic* AI catalyst — every Spring 2026 YC company in B2B SaaS is pulling on the same lever. Sales enablement was *not* a dead market re-opening (it was a $6.13B 2025 category growing at 17.20% CAGR per Fortune Business Insights — steady-state expansion, not a **Stripe**-after-PayPal or **Anduril**-after-Ukraine step-function). Over the next 18 months this catalyst compounds *for the incumbents harder than for Salesgraph*, because Gong and Clari can deploy the same orchestration on top of data they already own.

Revenue Velocity — counterfactual. The dossier discloses zero revenue, zero customer logos, zero pilot counts, no Product Hunt, no press as of May 2026. For the score to flip a tier, Salesgraph would need to disclose three named enterprise design partners with retained weekly usage within 90 days — the **Harvey** (2023) pattern was "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" off real ARR, and the disclosure was unmissable. Salesgraph's silence in May 2026 with a live site and 12+ integrations is conspicuous; "something's fundamentally broken" is the prior I apply (Tim Ferriss Show #863) until proven otherwise. Pre-seed default applies, but the absent-evidence default is already a negative signal in this cycle.

Single-miracle — parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015). Color worked because I structured the miracle as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing, isolating the bet from chemistry risk. Salesgraph isolates its miracle similarly — LLM orchestration over existing APIs is the one thing that has to work, and it largely does. The problem isn't that Salesgraph has *multiple* miracles; it's that the single miracle is *commodity* — every funded competitor named in the dossier (Momentum.io, Day.ai, Gong Engage, Clari+Salesloft's merged stack) is reaching for the same one capability over the same APIs, which collapses the miracle to a GTM-and-design contest. One miracle, low ceiling.

36-month forecast: Salesgraph is acquired by Gong, Outreach, or HubSpot for $25-70M as a feature tuck-in / acqui-hire by end of 2028, OR pivots to a vertical-specific context layer (likely dev-tools sales, where the Mintlify network is real). The mechanism is the **Lyft** mechanism — the workflow path belongs to the data owners, and Clari+Salesloft's December 2025 merger is the explicit signal that the incumbents intend to occupy the orchestration corridor. Two competent ex-Mintlify engineers with a Slack-native overlay don't beat a $450M ARR merged entity to that whitespace; they get bought by one of the platforms whose APIs they integrate against.

Strongest opposing case: Salesgraph is the Mintlify-alumni replay of the **Brex** (2017) pattern — operators who *lived inside* a YC enterprise sales motion, building the predecessor product against real Mintlify pipeline, now spinning it out as the Slack-native context layer before Gong's roadmap ships. Zapier worked as orchestration despite incumbents existing, because horizontal connective tissue is genuinely a different product than vertical depth. The bull mechanism is: integration breadth (12+ tools) plus Slack-as-surface plus founder-network distribution into YC and Mintlify-adjacent buyers compounds into a real wedge before Clari+Salesloft can ship a Slack-native equivalent. That case stings because the Mintlify-precursor detail is genuinely uncommon at pre-seed. It loses on one fact: Gong *owns the call data* that powers the most valuable Salesgraph outputs (pre-call briefs, post-call follow-ups), and the **Lyft** test asks whether you must win share *against* the data owner — Salesgraph must. The verdict flips if Salesgraph discloses three named enterprise design partners with retained weekly usage and a signed multi-seat ARR contract by August 2026; absent that, this is a pass at consensus seed prices and a tuck-in at exit.
