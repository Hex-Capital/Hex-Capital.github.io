# Litmus -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Litmus is a governance-clean, consensus-lane YC recruiting SaaS: Shaivi Rau is explicitly titled CEO with Elena Zhao as Co-Founder, which is the cleanest tiebreaker signal in the dossier, but the underlying bet is a repo-context LLM wrapper selling into ATS integrations with Ashby, Greenhouse, and Lever at $60K ARR — a category so nakedly on-trend it will price like every other 2026 AI-hiring pitch. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Litmus seems to be playing is the exact game I'm being asked to underwrite.

Palmer Luckey shipped VR from his parents' garage before Anduril in 2017; the founder had *touched* the domain before the company existed. Litmus's origin is Rau watching her Series A-founder brother struggle to hire (Columbia Spectator, May 2026) — that is second-hand lived experience, sibling-adjacent, not autobiographical. Zhao's Meta stint and incoming Two Sigma internship give her insider view of being *hired*, not of running the loop. Two Columbia '26 seniors have observed engineering hiring pain; they have not sat in the hiring manager's chair for a quarter and felt the panic of a bad Q4 headcount plan. That is the gap between Luckey and Litmus, and the dossier does not close it.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on opened with a TAM slide and a four-logo team page and was chased by six top funds — Litmus is a softer version of that same failure mode. The category is 2026's most obvious wedge: HackerRank at 553 G2 reviews, CodeSignal at 1,435, Codility at 904, plus Karat and CodeSubmit — a saturated lane where the pitch "but ours grades AI-prompt behavior" reads as a feature every incumbent will ship in two quarters. The Litmus dossier lists no VC passes, no aesthetic objection, no "this is uninvestable" tell. When Crusoe pitched flared-gas Bitcoin in 2018, credible investors had already balked — that silence-of-the-skeptics on Litmus tells me the round is not underpriced, it is priced *for* consensus.

Rau titled CEO, Zhao titled Co-Founder — no shared-CEO, no 50/50 title ambiguity, no HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll setup where two people wear the same crown until it cracks. This is the one dimension where Litmus compounds cleanly over 18 months: a single named tiebreaker at pre-seed usually holds through Series A cap-table dilution, and the governance blow-up risk I usually price into two-founder Columbia pairs is genuinely lower here. It is not enough to write a check on, but it is the load-bearing wall of the deal.

For the capital-intensity axis, the counterfactual is stark: Litmus would need to actually own the AI-prompt-to-code behavioral dataset as a proprietary corpus with a published grading-accuracy benchmark to escape the LLM-wrapper category — and the dossier itself flags this as "unproven" with 3,000 assessments and no accuracy artifact. Anduril earned its 15/15 by being physically impossible to reproduce in a quarter; Litmus, per the dossier's own risk section, is "buildable by any team with LLM access." That is the opposite of frontier.

The Niantic Ingress test — can a normal human touch this in the wild within 48 hours — Litmus half-passes: a candidate at a Litmus customer takes the assessment in their own IDE, which is tactile, but the B2B gating means I cannot install it, photograph it, or feel it the way I felt the HQ Trivia pull. It is closer to a developer-tools abstraction than to a scooter I can ride. The Ashby/Greenhouse/Lever integrations are real distribution surface, but distribution is not verifiability.

Thirty-six month forecast for Litmus: reaches $400K–$1.5M ARR against Ashby's own native assessment feature and CodeSignal's inevitable repo-context release, then either flatlines and quietly winds down or gets acqui-hired by Ashby or Greenhouse for the team and the integration work — no venture-scale outcome, and no Stargate moment hiding underneath.

The strongest case against my pass runs through HandUp (~2014): I wrote that check because the founder's biography mirrored my own homelessness origin, and modest commercial ceiling did not deter me. A bull could argue Rau's brother-watching-hiring-fail is the same class of autobiographical anchor, and that dismissing it is inconsistent. It stings, but it does not hold — HandUp's Rose Broome had lived the specific condition; Rau has watched a family member live a business-operator frustration. Those are different orders of contact. My flip condition is verifiable: if Rau or Zhao spent 18+ months actually running an engineering hiring loop at a scaled company before Litmus, or if Litmus publishes a grading-accuracy benchmark that beats a naive Claude-4.6 judge by a real margin on a held-out dataset, I re-open the file. Absent that, this is a rebuttal, not a hard no — but at pre-seed pricing in a consensus lane, a rebuttal is a pass.
