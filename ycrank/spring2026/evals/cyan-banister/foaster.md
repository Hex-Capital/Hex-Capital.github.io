# Foaster -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Foaster is the rare profile where the governance is the cleanest dimension on the page and the thesis is the weakest -- Raphaël Dabadie as named CEO with Alexandre Combes as CTO, two co-founders who co-built LLM benchmarks together, no equity drama telegraphed -- attached to "AI-native consulting" in the Spring 2026 YC batch, which is the most-pitched category in the room. That pairing is the diagnosis: a well-governed entrant into the single most consensus services-AI lane of the cycle. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Foaster is playing is the one everyone is playing.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is my high rung on founder-from-lived-experience: he had Oculus shipped to scale before he ever touched a defense contract, so the biography preceded the company. Foaster's founders sit two rungs down from that. Combes built Répondia handling 60,000+ customer calls for French restaurant groups -- that is a real production AI-agent artifact, and it is the strongest single sentence in the Foaster dossier -- but customer-support voice agents for restaurants are not the same lived domain as enterprise org-transformation consulting. Dabadie's biography is "spent the prior year helping enterprises think through AI deployment," which is exactly the analytical, advisory entry point the pattern punishes. Neither founder ran the back-office their product claims to map. The 400,000-person tennis community at 11 and joining Nadal's team at 18 tells me Dabadie can build distribution -- it does not tell me he lived inside a 5,000-person org watching a McKinsey deck land on the wrong desk.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling from my pass example is my low rung on magically-weird, and Foaster sits next to it rather than next to Crusoe. The Foaster dossier itself names Sierra ($4.5B), Decagon ($100M+ raise), Distyl ($32M Series A from Khosla), Cresta -- the "AI agents for enterprise" lane is crowded with funded incumbents and Foaster's differentiator is "we point the agents at employees instead of customers." That is a wrapper around positioning, not aesthetic transgression. Crusoe (~2018) had two or three credible VCs passing on aesthetic grounds; nothing in the Foaster dossier shows a sophisticated investor passing for any reason at all, which means the round is not yet priced through the rejection-as-signal filter I rely on. There is no roughneck here, no flared gas, no "burning Bitcoin in North Dakota" sentence that makes me lean forward.

Governance compounds well over 18 months when there is one CEO and one CTO with delineated remits: Dabadie carries the CEO title, Combes carries the technical build, they co-built two LLM benchmarks that reached 12M+ views together, and that is a falsifiable collaboration history -- not vibes. The Paris/SF geographic split is the asterisk; the CTO is in Paris while YC is pushing the US enterprise motion, and "split team plus French enterprise roots" is the kind of structural friction that turns into an unresolved tiebreak when the GTM market choice comes due. I weight this above the default because the CEO is unambiguously named and the prior collaboration is documented; I do not weight it to the top of the range because the geography has not been stress-tested yet.

For Foaster to clear my capital-intensity bar, the dossier would have to show one of: a proprietary workflow dataset already accumulating from named clients, a regulated-data certification (SOC 2, HIPAA-adjacent HR data handling), or a hardware/integration moat that BCG X cannot replicate by next quarter. None of those are present -- "No public data found" under Moat Signals, no SOC 2 disclosed on foaster.ai despite the product running AI agents through "every employee" on workflows and "informal habits," and the business model is "3 to 5x less expensive than traditional consulting" which is a pricing wedge, not a moat. To flip this tier I would need a named enterprise LOI with operational-data exclusivity baked into the contract. That counterfactual is concrete; the dossier does not satisfy it.

Uber (2010) -- I bet because I rode it before I read the pitch. Foaster fails that same tactile test the way the developer-tools low rung does: the product is gated behind a contact form, the only artifact I can touch is a Twitch video of Raphaël talking about the product, and the AI interview agent only runs once a paid enterprise engagement begins. I cannot install Foaster, I cannot photograph Foaster in a friend's office, I cannot scan a Wi-Fi name and find Foaster the way I found Flock Safety at a Four Seasons café (~2018). For a B2B services-with-agents company that gap is structural, not stage-driven -- a pre-seed Flock had hardware on a pole; a pre-seed Foaster has a sales call.

My 36-month forecast for Foaster: acquired by a tier-2 consultancy (Slalom, West Monroe, or a Big-Four AI services arm) for low-eight-figures by 2029 after BCG X and McKinsey QuantumBlack compress services margins -- or pivoted away from cross-functional org-mapping into a single vertical AI-agent product riding off Combes's Répondia muscle. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: clean governance survives the storm, weak capital-intensity and weak weirdness mean the storm comes from incumbents with C-suite Rolodexes BCG already monetizes at $3.6B of 2025 AI revenue.

The strongest bull case against me is the DeepMind exception. Hassabis was pedigreed and analytical and I wrote the check anyway because polymath cross-domain compounding overrode the outsider rule. Dabadie ran a 400K-person community at 11, became a Nadal agent at 18, built 12M-view LLM benchmarks on social intelligence and political behavior -- that is polymath surface area, and Combes shipped a production voice-agent system in a regulated-adjacent vertical. Steel-manned: two polymaths with distribution and shipping muscle in an expanding $11B-to-$91B market is exactly the cross-domain stack the Generalist piece pointed at. The dismantle: Hassabis's polymath stack compounded inside the *product* -- chess + neuroscience + game design all loaded into AGI research. Dabadie's polymath stack compounds inside *go-to-market* -- community, athlete representation, viral benchmarks -- which is a distribution skill grafted onto a consensus services lane, not a domain compounding into a moat. The verifiable flip condition: a signed enterprise LOI with a named Fortune 500 counterparty before October 2026 demonstrating that the interview-agent is producing operational artifacts incumbents cannot, plus a credible investor publicly passing on aesthetic grounds. Absent that, I pass.
