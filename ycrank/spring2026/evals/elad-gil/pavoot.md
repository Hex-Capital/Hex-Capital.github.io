# Pavoot -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Pavoot is a technical-founder AI agent sitting on top of someone else's infrastructure stack in a market where the structural catalyst is generic, not category-specific — Gohar Tamrazyan and Ana Yoon Faria de Lima are ETH CS talents (6-time Swiss Chess Champion, 20+ Olympiad medals) building a CRM-adjacent application against Vendelux ($16.4M raised, $2.1M 2024 revenue) and Grip ($14.5M raised), and that diagnosis — strong on relative single-miracle simplicity, weak on infrastructure-layer positioning — is what fixes Pavoot's place in my portfolio map.

On infrastructure-layer positioning, my high rung is Stripe — the payments API an entire generation of internet businesses got built on, with switching costs that compounded for fifteen years. My low rung is Wish, the consumer marketplace I backed that had no API, no B2B layer, and no structural protection when growth slowed. Pavoot sits much closer to Wish than to Stripe: by the dossier's own description it syncs to Salesforce/HubSpot, calls OpenAI/Anthropic APIs, and ships an agent workflow on top of CRM systems it does not own. That is the textbook "application on a contested infrastructure layer" red flag from my framework. Salesforce Einstein and HubSpot Breeze can ship overlapping native features inside the system of record where the buyer already lives, and Pavoot has no proprietary attendee graph, no developer ecosystem, no API surface that other businesses build on. The single live reference logo — START Global / START Summit via Leo Braun — is a design-partner relationship, not an infrastructure foothold.

On nonobvious market timing, my high rung is Perplexity — I wrote that seed check before ChatGPT launched because the AI capability curve was crossing a specific threshold that unlocked a market stuck for a decade. My low rung is the consumer-social-app pass: a stable platform layer with no exogenous shift, just a UX bet. Pavoot sits closer to the consumer-social rung than to Perplexity. The "why now" the dossier offers — LLM agents can now do multi-step workflows — is generic and applies equally to fifty other YC P26 companies; it is not a category-specific catalyst that cracks open event marketing. Vendelux's $2.1M 2024 revenue (up from $889K in 2023) tells me the market was already moving without a regulatory or capability step-function. Pavoot's pre/during/post-event scope expansion mid-batch from a media-distribution hub (the START Summit photo-tagging deployment) to a pipeline agent reads as positioning, not timing.

On single-miracle viability — and this is where Pavoot scores best, relatively — my high rung is Harvey, where the technology miracle was happening exogenously on the foundation-model curve and legal firms were already proven buyers. My low rung is the multi-miracle consumer social pass: distribution + monetization + retention all simultaneously. Pavoot needs roughly two: an enterprise GTM miracle (selling six-figure ACVs to CMOs without a buyer-side founder) and a substitution-survival miracle against Salesforce/HubSpot native features. The agent technology itself is the cheap part, which is precisely why a moat is hard.

On operator-scaler founder profile, my mid rung is Henrique Dubugras at Brex (operator from fourteen) and my high rung is Parker Conrad rebuilding after Zenefits — founders who lived inside the buyer's workflow before founding. Pavoot's founders are deeply technical (ETH CS, ETH AI Center course origin, 2023 ETH VIS Teaching Award for Gohar) but neither has run enterprise marketing or RevOps, and the dossier shows no GTM hire and no advisor coverage of that gap. On margin architecture, my high rung is Abridge's per-encounter pricing — selling units of cognition, not seats. Pavoot has no public pricing page and a demo-led CTA at cal.com/team/pavoot; the most likely default is event- or seat-based SaaS, which is fine but unproven and not differentiating.

My 36-month forecast: Pavoot pivots to a vertical event-organizer tool (the START Summit media-hub use case where it actually had product-market traction) or gets acqui-hired into Cvent, Bizzabo, or HubSpot at under $2M ARR. The mechanism is straightforward — without infrastructure positioning or a category-specific structural shift, two technical founders will discover that selling AI event agents to enterprise CMOs against Vendelux's $14M Series A war chest requires a GTM miracle they are not staffed to perform, and the path of least resistance is the workflow they already shipped live at START.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Anduril is my own override precedent — I joined the board of a company that needed three simultaneous miracles because the market structure signal was overwhelming. The bull would argue that AI agents replacing human SDR/marketing-ops headcount is a generational shift on the scale of defense modernization, and Pavoot's full-lifecycle scope (vs. Vendelux's pre-event-only positioning) is the kind of "few small product tweaks that dramatically differentiate" I wrote about in the First Round Review essay. That case stings because the AI-agent-for-GTM category genuinely could produce a winner. It loses on the dossier evidence: Anduril had bipartisan procurement reform as the exogenous catalyst — Pavoot has no equivalent category-specific shift, just the generic LLM curve that every YC P26 company is riding. One verifiable condition flips me: three named Fortune 500 marketing logos signed at $50K+ ACVs within six months with documented retention, which would prove the GTM miracle is happening and the buyer-side founder gap is being closed by product pull rather than founder-led selling.
