# Oddpool -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Oddpool is a competent micro-Bloomberg for prediction markets with a genuine compounding-data moat sitting inside a venue category whose civilizational consequence I cannot articulate. The signature: the highest dimension here -- captured tick-history that a later entrant cannot backfill -- sits inside the lowest dimension, a success-state where nothing in the world looks different after Avi Arora and Ritesh Malpani win. That pairing produces a recognizable shape in my portfolio: the **Slash** (2024) profile without the Kanye pivot, or the GPT-wrapper sales-call summarizer with a slightly better moat. Oddpool is real; Oddpool is small.

The captured-history dataset is the one place Oddpool earns a second look on the technical-risk ladder. **QuantumScape** (early, IPO 2020) is the wrong comparison and I am not going to reach for it; the honest single-anchor parallel is **Glean**, where Arvind Jain's ex-Google search pedigree justified a domain-expert profile because the dataset (Fortune-500 internal information graphs) compounded into a structural asymmetry consensus VCs could not replicate. Oddpool's 700,000+ indexed markets with orderbooks back to March 2026 at 1-5 minute granularity is the same shape of asset -- recoverable history is a one-way ratchet, and FinFeedAPI's REST-snapshot architecture cannot retroactively manufacture the orderbook depth Oddpool already has on disk. Where Oddpool diverges from the Glean analogy is scale: Glean indexes Fortune-500 knowledge graphs; Oddpool indexes Kalshi and Polymarket. The moat physics rhyme; the consequence physics do not.

The GPT-wrapper sales-call summarizer is the failure mode I want to test Oddpool against on consequence magnitude, because the failure mechanism is identical -- success-state is a 20-30% workflow improvement inside one persona (quant fund data engineer instead of SDR) with no incumbent regime collapsing. Oddpool's own pricing page tells the story against itself: Free / $30 Pro / $100 Premium / Enterprise custom is not a tier sheet for a company displacing $15 trillion of GDP labor. The dossier finds no public TAM and reaches for "Opinion reached ~31% of global prediction market volume at $8B+ monthly" as the adjacent reference, which is the wrong reference -- $8B monthly notional on a prediction venue does not translate into a data-layer TAM that justifies my check. No economic mechanism breaks if Oddpool wins. Bloomberg's mechanism does not break. CME's mechanism does not break. A new asset class gets a data vendor.

Avi Arora at Microsoft training prompt-injection models for 20B requests/year and Ritesh Malpani at Bloomberg making buy-side trading systems 40% faster is the exact "8 years at Salesforce" pattern my TED Talk warned against -- 18 months of compounding here looks like incrementally better orderbook coverage and incrementally more venues, not a founder thesis-update that would force me to rewrite my prior. The mechanism that would let founder-defiance score higher does not exist in this dossier: neither founder has publicly contradicted their field, neither has the "too important not to do" framing from Outlook Business, neither has the instigator posture. The Microsoft and Bloomberg lines are credentials, not defiance. McKinsey's 1986 forecast of fewer than 1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 is the cautionary tale about credentialed experts extrapolating the past; Oddpool's founders are not the inverse pattern, they are the pattern.

Ritesh Malpani's LinkedIn listing him as co-founder of Benchspan (YC P26) at the same time he is co-founder of Oddpool is the counterfactual for the learning-rate dimension -- and it is the wrong kind of update. A learning-rate signal looks like the **Slash** Kanye pivot, where the founder publicly told the story of why the original thesis was wrong and what replaced it. Oddpool's signal looks like split attention on a two-person team. The thing that would flip this dimension a tier is one of two founders telling me, in writing, why the venue-neutral aggregation thesis is wrong in some specific way that they have already corrected for -- a pivot story, not a second startup.

Required-capital fit is where Oddpool quietly disqualifies itself from my check book regardless of the rest of the analysis. The $50M first check into **OpenAI** (2019), the $115M Series A lead in **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), the $105M co-led seed for **Genesis AI** (2025) are not stylistic preferences -- they are the deployment shape my fund is built for. Oddpool at "several thousand dollars in MRR" with a $30/$100 SaaS ladder is a $1-3M seed round at most, and any Tier-1 generalist or a Bloomberg-spinout angel syndicate is over-resourced for it. I am the wrong investor; Oddpool is not the wrong company for someone.

The 36-month forecast follows directly from the signature: Oddpool is acquired by Kalshi, Polymarket, or a CME-tier data arm for $15-40M inside 18 months, mirroring the Dome → Polymarket Feb 19 2026 deal that Gabriel Jarrosson estimated at $10-20M after ~4-5 months post-Demo Day. The acquirer-substitution risk is not theoretical -- it is the most likely positive outcome, not the downside. The dataset compounds fast enough to make Oddpool tuck-in attractive; the venue count stays small enough that no neutral aggregator achieves escape velocity before an exchange decides to own the data layer rather than share it.

The strongest opposing case is that if CFTC-licensed venues actually proliferate the way the founders claim, prediction markets become a real asset class and the Bloomberg-of-prediction-markets is a 9-figure outcome -- and Oddpool already has the captured history that makes them the structural winner of that bull case. I am unconvinced because the very Dome exit at ~$10-20M after months, not years, is the dossier's own evidence that the exit shape here is feature-acquisition, not category-definition; Polymarket buying Dome to kill neutrality is precisely what tells me the upside ceiling is set by the exchanges, not by the data vendors. The condition that would flip my verdict is one named institutional contract -- Citadel, Jane Street, Jump, Two Sigma -- on the launch page within 12 months at six-figure ACV, because that would prove Oddpool is selling a Bloomberg terminal and not a prosumer arbitrage feed. Without that, Oddpool passes my desk in 30 seconds for the same reasons the GPT-wrapper does.
