# Vestris -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Vestris is a thesis-shaped hole where the founder should be standing. The dossier hands me a legitimately unsexy, regulated, county-portal-scraping title-production product — the exact lane that produced my Vigil Labs (August 2025) check — and then tells me Team Size = 0 and no founder names appear on the YC page, on vestris.ai, or in any search result as of July 2026. That is the highest-and-lowest pairing in one company: Vestris scores well where my thesis actively reaches (regulated back-office nobody wants to talk about at dinner), and scores nearly nothing where my thesis begins (who is this person, and why *them*).

Anduril (2017) is the load-bearing analogue on the unsexy-regulated axis: Palmer Luckey walked into a category — defense procurement — that other VCs refused on aesthetic and capital-intensity grounds, and the moat compounded because the regulatory surface was hostile to tourists. Vestris sits in a structurally similar lane. Title commitments, Schedule A/B, RESPA-compliant settlement statements, E&O liability against a $4.5B Q1 2026 ALTA premium base — that is the kind of "too hard or too unsexy" perimeter where I usually find alpha, and it is exactly the sales conflict Qualia's $207M-raised workflow platform cannot resolve without cannibalizing its own customers' billable-searcher hours. Where Vestris diverges from the Anduril rung is that Palmer had already shipped Oculus at scale; Vestris has shipped two counties — DC and Montgomery County, MD — with no disclosed underwriter partnership and no E&O coverage stated anywhere on vestris.ai.

The HQ Trivia (2017) failure mode was Yusupov-and-Kroll co-founder ambiguity — no named tiebreaker, and the product's tactile virality bought time until the governance blew up. Vestris cannot even reach that pattern because there is nothing to evaluate: the YC page renders no team section, the homepage lists no team, the only contact is a shared `founders@vestris.ai` inbox, and zero LinkedIn footprints are attributable. This is worse than the HQ Trivia risk, because with Yusupov I could at least *watch him at the dinner table*. With Vestris I cannot even locate the table. The DeepMind (~2013) exception — polymath pedigree overriding lived-experience — would require me to see the pedigree; I see neither pedigree nor outsider trajectory, only opacity, which I have to read as pitch-deck training rather than a missed detail.

Vestris passes the "magically weird" filter on mechanism: title searchers are a manual labor line inside an $18B-premium industry, and the browser-agent-into-county-portal thesis is the sort of thing sophisticated software VCs pattern-match to boring RPA and skip. Over 18 months, that mechanism compounds two ways — parsed-contract corpus and county-specific playbooks — but only if Vestris outruns the commoditization risk from Browser Use (50k GitHub stars), Hyperbrowser, and Asteroid, which hand any competitor the same primitives. The compounding curve is real; the head start is not obviously wide.

For the "single unambiguous CEO" dimension, the counterfactual to flip a tier is trivial: name the CEO, publish equity split, disclose one title-industry operator on the cap table. That is one page of website copy. Vestris has chosen not to publish it as of July 2026, and I treat deliberate founder opacity in a regulated category as a governance tell, not as YC modesty.

Physical-world verifiability tracks a single-anchor parallel to Postmates (2011): I bet Postmates because the delivery happened in front of me on the sidewalk. Vestris has an app at app.vestris.ai, but I am not a title agency in DC or Montgomery County, MD, so I cannot ride, scan, or install this product in my own life within 48 hours. It is B2B abstraction whose value can only be evaluated via founder testimony — and there is no founder to testify.

My 36-month forecast is that Vestris either gets absorbed as a bolt-on into SoftPro (already owned by Fidelity National Financial) or Qualia for under $25M, or dies below $500K ARR after failing to cross five counties — the county-by-county integration cost is the mechanism, and the customer-labor-cannibalization sales conflict is the accelerant. Both outcomes are consistent with the signature: strong lane, absent operator.

The strongest bull case leans on the DeepMind exception — that a pedigreed team I cannot currently see is quietly building the title-industry equivalent of a polymath compounding machine, and my founder-opacity red flag is just YC stealth theater that will resolve at Demo Day. It stings because I have been wrong on stealth before. But DeepMind resolved into Hassabis-the-chess-prodigy inside a quarter; Vestris has been silent through an entire batch cycle in a domain where underwriter relationships and RESPA-licensed operators are the *actual* moat, and those relationships cannot be built anonymously. One verifiable condition flips me to Invest: a named CEO with prior title-agency or title-underwriter operating experience, plus one signed underwriter partnership. Absent that, Vestris is the right lane, wrong ticket.
