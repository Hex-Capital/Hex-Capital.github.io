# Speko -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Speko is "OpenRouter for voice AI" pitched by Beknazar Abdikamalov, a solo founder whose highest dimension in my framework is accidental — he ticks the "single unambiguous CEO" box only because there is nobody else named — and whose lowest dimension is the one that matters most for a 2026 infrastructure bet: Speko is the picture of consensus in a lane where Inworld Router has already raised $117M at a $500M valuation and OpenRouter has already extended into TTS/STT. That pairing describes a technically credible commodity gateway walking into a knife fight it did not price into the pitch.

Crusoe (~2018) is the HIGH rung on "magically weird" because burning North Dakota gas flares to mine Bitcoin sounded like three uninvestable things stapled together, and the shared mechanism I'd need for Speko is a category so aesthetically off-putting that Inworld and OpenRouter would not touch it. Speko is the exact opposite. The dossier tells me Inworld Router is doing "$0-markup" across 220+ LLMs with voice pipeline, OpenRouter has already added TTS/STT endpoints, Vapi just closed $72M and won Amazon Ring over 40 rivals, and Bland is at $100M+ Series C with a vertically integrated stack. When five well-capitalized players are already inside the ring, "OpenRouter for voice AI" is not magically weird — it is the consensus pattern-match the YC S26 batch will produce twenty of. I am supposed to find them before it becomes consensus. Speko arrived after.

The Reference Voice pass example is the LOW rung on "founder surfaced from lived experience" — two ex-FAANG PMs who discovered the problem in a memo — and Speko's Beknazar is a more sympathetic version of the same failure mode. He is ex-Amazon SWE with an MS from UNIST Korea, and to his credit he was CTO at Hupo, which pulled $4M from DST Global, Meta, Goodwater, and Collaborative Fund. That is a real ship. But Hupo was leadership coaching in Singapore, not voice infrastructure — Speko is Beknazar reading the 2026 voice-AI market and deciding to route it, not Beknazar carrying a scar from having built voice pipelines himself. There is no autobiographical moment where he FELT the STT/LLM/TTS integration pain. The Anduril-Luckey shape is a garage full of shipped Oculus before the defense pivot; Speko is a talented engineer coming in laterally to a category on the fourth YC attempt (Luma).

Solo-founder status flips one way for me and one way for the market. My persona reads "multiple founders always a recipe for disaster," and Speko listing Beknazar as the only named founder means there is no HQ Trivia Yusupov-vs-Kroll tiebreaker problem waiting to detonate. Over eighteen months, however, the mechanism compounds against Speko: a four-person team with three unnamed operators means Beknazar is simultaneously the sales voice against Inworld's enterprise motion, the engineering leader against a benchmarking dataset that has to compound faster than OpenRouter's usage, and the compliance owner shepherding SOC 2 and HIPAA from "in progress" to signed. One person cannot outrun a $117M gateway on all three axes at once, and the dossier surfaces no co-founder, no CRO, no notable advisor.

Speko as a "frontier-hardware or unsexy-regulated swing" would need something SOC 2 and HIPAA "in progress" cannot deliver: for this score to flip a tier, Speko would have to name one signed healthcare or financial-services LOI where regulatory posture is the wedge that Vapi and Bland cannot serve. The counterfactual is not implausible — voice AI in regulated verticals is exactly the "too hard or too unsexy" lane that produced my Vigil Labs (August 2025) check. But the dossier gives me neither the LOI, the regulated design partner, nor the vertical thesis; it gives me a horizontal developer-tools API. Absent that anchor, Speko is a software gateway competing on latency and price against players with an order of magnitude more capital.

Niantic is the single-anchor parallel on physical-world verifiability, and it exposes what Speko is not. I sourced Niantic because I had personally played Ingress since 2012 — the artifact was in my hand before the pitch was on my desk. Speko ships a TypeScript SDK (@spekoai/sdk), a Python SDK (spekoai), an MCP server with OAuth, and a LiveKit adapter. A normal human cannot install, ride, scan, or photograph any of that in 48 hours; the 0.76ms voice-native retrieval across 100K documents and the ~340ms median turn latency are marketing-site numbers with no third-party benchmark. I evaluate by observation, and there is nothing here I can observe outside the founder's own claims.

My 36-month forecast for Speko: acquihire by Inworld or OpenRouter under $25M, or quiet wind-down with the SDK still on npm — driven by Inworld's $0-markup pricing collapsing gateway margins before Speko's benchmarking dataset compounds enough to matter, and by Vapi/Bland absorbing the mid-market that would have been Speko's developer wedge.

The strongest opposing case is that voice routing is a genuinely different problem than text routing — per-language STT accuracy varies wildly, TTS voice quality is subjective, latency budgets are sub-second — and Beknazar has already shown one time (Hupo, $4M from DST Global and Meta) that he can raise real institutional money and ship. If voice is enough of its own beast, OpenRouter's text-first DNA becomes a liability rather than a moat, and Speko's continuous cross-provider benchmarking dataset becomes a compounding asset the incumbents cannot easily replicate. That case stings because DST Global does not back nobodies. But it does not survive the dossier: Inworld Router already has a voice pipeline, not text-only, and Inworld raised $117M specifically to occupy this lane. The verifiable condition that flips me to Invest is Speko naming a paying enterprise design partner in healthcare or financial services within six months where SOC 2 / HIPAA is the reason Vapi and Bland lost the deal — that would tell me Beknazar is playing an unsexy-regulated game and not the game he seems to be playing.
