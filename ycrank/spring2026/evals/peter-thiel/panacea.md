# Panacea -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Panacea is a regulated-adjacent services arbitrage with no multi-year technical commitment — Haris Javed-Akhtar (MD) and Yaman Ziadeh (ex-Bloomberg, ex-Anterior) are wrapping LLM document drafting around ex-FDA consultants and selling fixed-fee milestone pricing against MDI Consultants' hourly model. The dimensional signature here is that Panacea scores highest on regulated-sector adjacency and lowest on definite plan — which tells me this is a consultancy with AI leverage, not a zero-to-one technology bet. That is a respectable small business. It is not the shape of a power-law check.

On the contrarian secret, the HIGH rung is **Anduril** (2017), where the named failure mode was that Lockheed and Raytheon cost-plus structures structurally cannot ship software-defined defense; the LOW rung is the "AI for X" pass archetype with no named incumbent and no named blind spot. Panacea sits closer to the LOW rung but not on it — Haris and Yaman do name the failure mode (MDI, EAS, EMMA, The FDA Group, IQVIA bill hourly and cannot convert to milestone pricing without cannibalizing utilization), which is a real innovator's-dilemma read on partner-led consultancies. But this is an operational secret about billing model, not an architectural secret about what the incumbent cannot build. Stripe's secret was that payments needed to be developer-first; Panacea's secret is that regulatory consulting should be priced like SaaS. One reshapes a category. The other reshapes an invoice.

On atoms-heavy or regulated-sector moat — Panacea's highest dimension — the HIGH rung is **Palantir** (2003), where government-as-customer created 15+ years of pricing power Silicon Valley refused to touch; the LOW rung is **Asana** (2010)'s pure-SaaS workflow with no regulatory anchor. Panacea sits in the lower-middle: the FDA pathway expertise (IND, NDA, BLA, 510(k), De Novo, PMA) is a credential moat, and the ex-FDA reviewer hiring pool is slow and relationship-driven per withpanacea.com. But Panacea is regulated-*adjacent* — it sells to companies navigating FDA; it is not itself a regulated customer relationship. Harun Sinha is the only named principal consultant. If Sinha walks, the moat walks with him.

On monopoly trajectory, the ladder runs from **Palantir**'s government-intelligence monopoly down through **Stripe**'s developer-default network effect to **Quora**'s consumer Q&A facing Stack Overflow with no defensible moat. Panacea sits near the **Quora** rung — Weave Bio raised $20M Series A from USVP and Innovation Endeavors in October 2025 with naming-VC syndicate, and the durable scarce input is ex-FDA personnel, not the AI software layer that any foundation-model improvement compresses. Pricing power for services firms is capped by labor cost. Ten years of pricing power is a company; one year is a feature. Panacea has a feature.

On definite plan — Panacea's lowest dimension — the HIGH rung is **SpaceX** (2008) with its 15-year reusability roadmap; the LOW rung is the YC-batch indefinite-optimism archetype that "iterates to product-market fit." Panacea's dossier lists a services menu across ten pathways and a cal.com booking link. There is no multi-year named milestone, no architectural commitment Haris and Yaman will refuse to pivot from. This is iteration-mode, not definite-optimism. On founder lineage, the ladder runs from **Affirm** (2014, Levchin) at the top through **Asana** (2010, Moskovitz from Facebook) in the middle down to the current-batch first-time-founder pass archetype. Yaman's AI Platform Engineer role at Anterior — itself a YC-backed AI healthcare automation company — is the strongest signal in the dossier and pins Panacea just above the LOW rung. Haris's MD is a credential, not a shipped-to-scale operator track record.

In 36 months, Panacea is a $3-8M ARR boutique regulatory services firm with 8-15 ex-FDA staff, acquired by IQVIA, ICON, or a mid-tier CRO for a strategic multiple, or alternatively absorbed by Weave Bio in a talent-and-customer-list tuck-in if Weave's enterprise platform wins the pharma top of market. The mechanism is straightforward: services-plus-AI-assist firms compound linearly with headcount, not exponentially with network density, so Panacea's terminal value is set by the labor pool it can recruit, which is the same constraint that kept MDI Consultants a private partnership for forty-eight years.

The strongest bull case against this forecast: the hourly-billing incumbent failure mode is real, milestone pricing is genuinely difficult for a partner-led consultancy to adopt without revenue-model collapse, and if Panacea's submission corpora compound into a proprietary fine-tuned model with measurable approval-rate uplift over consultant-only firms, the services business converts to a data moat. **Asana** (2010) is the Known Exception — pure SaaS without atoms-heavy structural moat, overridden by Moskovitz's Facebook lineage. The override here would require Yaman to be a comparable shipped-to-scale signal, and ex-Anterior AI Platform Engineer is not Facebook-CTO. The bull case fails because Panacea has no founder lineage strong enough to override the missing definite plan, and no data moat disclosed in the dossier strong enough to convert services into software economics. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Panacea ships a measurable approval-rate or median-elapsed-time delta versus the MDI baseline across 25+ submissions, published with regulatory pathway breakdown. Until then, this is a competent boutique, not a fund-returner. Pass.
