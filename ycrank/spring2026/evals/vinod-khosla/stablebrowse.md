# StableBrowse -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

StableBrowse is a pick-and-shovel infrastructure play sitting beneath the labor-automation thesis I care about -- the dossier names insurance quote binding, healthcare prior auth, and mortgage processing as target workflows -- but the company itself runs zero science risk and competes directly with Browserbase's $67.5M war chest. That is the signature: a credible consequence-adjacency built on a commoditizing substrate. The Jay Mehta "8+ years SWE in distributed systems" profile is exactly the founder shape my TED Talk warned against -- experts extrapolating the present forward, not instigators redrawing what is possible.

On consequence magnitude, the load-bearing analogy is **OpenAI** (2019), where the success-state was a civilizational rewrite of cognitive labor and the $50M check followed from that alone. StableBrowse gestures at the same labor frame -- prior authorization and mortgage processing are real chunks of the "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" displacement I described in Fortune (2026-03-06) -- but StableBrowse is the rails, not the agent. If StableBrowse wins at maximum ambition, the world has a more reliable Chromium-with-memory; the regime that collapses is brittle DOM scraping, not the insurance carrier or the EHR vendor. That is closer to the **DoorDash** (2013) consequence shape than the OpenAI shape, and even DoorDash had a 7,000-city marketplace network effect StableBrowse has not demonstrated.

On technical and capital risk, the failure pattern I keep in mind is the **GPT-4-sales-call-summarizer** archetype from my standard pass: software-only execution, no physics miracle, no plant to build, no fund-clock-defying timeline. That pattern is visibly present in StableBrowse -- a semantic graph layer above headless Chromium is an engineering project, not a science project. The dossier itself names Browserbase ($67.5M raised, ~$3M revenue), Kernel ($22M Series A from Accel), TinyFish ($47M Series A from ICONIQ), and open-source Browser Use as parallel attempts on overlapping capability. None of those checks were written because the underlying problem repelled consensus capital -- they were written because consensus capital wants in. That is the inverse of why I led **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019).

On founder defiance of expert consensus, the mechanism that should compound over 18 months is a founder who has publicly committed to a position their field rejects -- the Sergey Levine pattern at **Physical Intelligence** (2024). StableBrowse's Jay Mehta resume site reads "scalable, high-performance distributed systems," and Deepit Shah's prior is Amazon plus a UIUC RBF numerical-methods paper. Competent, but the dossier surfaces no contrarian thesis, no "the industry is wrong about X" public artifact, no instigator stance. Founder identity ambiguity -- multiple unrelated "Jay Mehta" and "Sarthak Awasthi" profiles -- means even the conformist signal is hard to verify. Absent evidence, I apply my 8/20 default and do not credit StableBrowse for a defiance posture it has not earned.

On founder learning rate, the counterfactual: for StableBrowse to flip a tier on this dimension, the dossier would need a documented pivot in the **Slash** (2024) shape -- a publicly told "we were building X, the Kanye moment told us to build Y" story that justified my $100M Series C. StableBrowse's founders are described as starting the company "after frustrations themselves while building browser agents," which is origin narrative, not pivot evidence. No prior exit, no public thesis update, no abandoned-then-resumed direction. I score the default and move on -- the company is too young to penalize aggressively, but there is nothing to reward.

On capital profile, the single-anchor parallel is **Cognition** (April 2024, $175M round) -- a software company where the roadmap genuinely consumed concentrated capital because frontier model training and agent reliability work cost real money. StableBrowse is a four-person YC Spring 2026 team running on roughly $500K of pre-seed and selling API access to a Chromium-with-memory layer. Browserbase reached $3M revenue on $67.5M total raised; that is the capital shape of this category, and it is a shape any Tier-1 generalist funds. I am over-resourced for the deal -- which is exactly when I pass.

My 36-month forecast for StableBrowse: acquired by Browserbase or a Notable Capital portfolio adjacent for $30-80M as a semantic-graph feature bolt-on, with under $2M ARR at signing. The mechanism is straightforward -- the persistent knowledge graph is a product layer Browserbase can replicate in two quarters, the regulated-vertical wedge stalls on the SOC 2 certifications still "in progress," and the four-founder team becomes more valuable as a team purchase than as a category challenger. That outcome is consistent with the consequence-pick-and-shovel-but-commodity-substrate signature; if I were predicting a $5B outcome, the dossier would have to show a science miracle or a labor-cost displacement metric it does not show.

The bull case that should sting: StableBrowse could be the **Glean** exception. Glean was Arvind Jain, ex-Google search -- a paradigmatic domain-expert profile that I overrode because capital scale and Fortune-500 information-asymmetry consequence justified the consensus credential. StableBrowse could argue the same -- regulated-vertical labor displacement at scale is the consequence, distributed-systems expertise is the credential, and the persistent knowledge graph compounds into a real cross-customer moat. The dismantle: Glean's Arvind Jain shipped Google search at planetary scale and had distribution credibility no competitor could replicate; StableBrowse's Jay Mehta has eight years SWE with founder-identity ambiguity in the dossier, and the "persistent site memory" moat is asserted without a single named customer, signed LOI, or revenue figure to anchor the network-effect claim. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed contract with a named top-10 U.S. health insurer or top-5 mortgage originator within twelve months, with disclosed seven-figure ACV. Until then StableBrowse is a 32/100 pass, and I do not write the check.
