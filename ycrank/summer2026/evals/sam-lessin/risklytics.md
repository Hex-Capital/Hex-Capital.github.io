# Risklytics -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Risklytics is what happens when two Harvard CS founders take a real regulatory tailwind -- California DOI release 052-2025 opening forward-looking wildfire models to rate filings in May 2025 -- and wrap it in the exact YC-AI-vertical shape my Pass Example was written to reject. The signature is a modest sleeve-adjacency to my prescription-rail health bets on the high end and a total absence of founder-owned distribution on the low end, which tells me Risklytics is a B2B data vendor pitching a Series A story it cannot yet fund, into a category where ZestyAI has already raised $76.97M and CAPE Analytics is now inside Moody's post-January-2025.

**Ro** is the load-bearing analogy on the one dimension where Risklytics scores best -- category sleeve. Ro violated my capital-efficiency rule because 50-state prescription licensing was a genuine "venture moment" that only VC capital could clear, and Risklytics is fishing in the same water: CA DOI approval of Verisk, KCC, and Moody's RMS as the only three filed-rate-eligible wildfire models is exactly the kind of regulatory schlep that could make capital intensity the moat. Where Risklytics diverges from the Ro rung is ownership of the rail -- Ro built the prescription infrastructure itself, while Risklytics is a data vendor sitting *outside* the three-model approved list, which means the CA DOI gate is not their moat, it is the wall between them and the only revenue line (filed rate models) that would justify the venture shape.

The low rung is my canonical Pass Example -- the 2026 YC AI vertical pitch with no public writing, no distribution, no non-obvious belief -- and the failure mechanism there is that the founder is responding to opportunity rather than pursuing a mission the market has written off. Samuel Gold left Harvard CS & Economics after one year with a Harvard Undergraduate Capital Partners line on the resume; Alexander Risio is an AB/SM in "systems, intelligence, biology"; neither has a Twitter account, neither has a public GitHub with retrievable stars, and neither has surfaced any prior insurance, actuarial, or wildfire-science work. That is the same pattern I flagged in the Pass Example almost verbatim -- the pitch is legible as an ML API because both founders can build one, not because either has a missionary claim about why 131,929 CAL FIRE inspections and a retrofit-recommendation output crack the underwriting problem in a way ZestyAI's 200B+ datapoint Z-FIRE has not.

Round dynamics compound the wrong direction over the next 18 months. Risklytics has no disclosed round terms, no lead investor beyond YC S26 group partner Ankit Gupta, and a category (climate + insurance + AI) that reads as a top-quartile 2026 batch consensus theme -- the exact profile where my absent-evidence default punishes silence because price-discipline is the entire alpha. Even if Risklytics closes at a batch-median $15M post, the ZestyAI comp already sitting at $76.97M raised with 40% CA homeowners carrier coverage means every follow-on tack in the regatta gets priced against an incumbent Risklytics has to out-execute on distribution it doesn't have.

For Risklytics's capital-structure score to flip up a tier, one specific thing would have to be true: the founders would need to have chosen a non-SaaS instrument -- a per-policy-bound royalty with a named carrier, a co-development structure with a state FAIR Plan carve-out, an equity-plus-retrofit-outcome share with a mitigation-services partner. None of that is in the dossier. What is in the dossier is a gated pricing page and an API-key application form, which is a generic SaaS cap table dressed with a climate label, and I have watched this exact shape need five rounds to reach default-alive too many times to underwrite it at consensus seed pricing.

Founder-as-brand is the axis where Risklytics compares directly to **John Fish / Bookshelved** and loses on every input the Slow Creator Fund thesis was built to identify -- no newsletter, no podcast, no owned community, no public essay explaining why the incumbents' modeling approach misses the retrofit-path-to-insurability insight. The founder-brand axis is where step-function value gets created when present, and Risklytics's LinkedIn snippets and a personal site with "resume and contact links only" are the exact negative signal my absent-evidence default was calibrated against.

My 36-month forecast: Risklytics is either acqui-hired by ZestyAI, Verisk, or Guidewire for under $20M in a talent-and-data deal, or pivoted into a multi-peril national scoring API selling to sub-scale E&S carriers at under $1M ARR -- the CAL-FIRE-only, California-only training corpus does not survive contact with a second peril without a fresh proprietary dataset the founders have shown no path to acquiring.

The strongest bull case cuts against me on the Ro exception: California's coverage gap is $800B-$1.3T, the FAIR Plan has ballooned from 126K to 450K+ policies with $650B exposure by June 2025, and a two-founder team with 13,685,446 homes already scored and 91% discrimination on the January 2025 LA fires validation looks structurally identical to a PillPack-shaped early bet on a regulator-mediated rail. The dismantle is distribution asymmetry -- PillPack sold direct to consumers while Risklytics has to sell to underwriters who are already paying ZestyAI and Moody's-owned CAPE, and the dossier discloses zero carrier LOIs, zero pilot names, zero board or advisor with a carrier relationship. The one condition that would flip my verdict: a signed pilot or bind-rate-improvement letter from a named California admitted carrier -- Mercury, CSAA, Farmers, anyone -- because that would convert the Ro-adjacency from a category story into a real venture moment underwritable at today's price.
