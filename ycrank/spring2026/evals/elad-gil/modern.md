# Modern -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Modern is a clean single-miracle architecture — Seb Poole and Aleks Tomovski bet that LLM agents are now reliable enough to resolve IT tickets end-to-end — pointed at a wedge where ServiceNow already paid $2.1B for Moveworks, Serval already raised $127M to a $1B mark from Redpoint/Sequoia, and Atomicwork already closed $40.3M with Khosla. That high/low pairing — tightest miracle isolation, worst index position — is Modern's analytical signature: the architecture is sound, the seat at the table is the problem.

Decagon (2023) is the load-bearing comp for Modern on the single-miracle axis — one validated capability (LLM accuracy at a structured enterprise workflow task) and the rest is GTM and integration work. Modern fits that template cleanly: the dossier doesn't ask me to believe in new model architectures, new compliance regimes, or new buyer behavior — it asks me to believe that Slack/Teams/Email/Voice agents with audit logs and policy checks can close IT tickets, which by 2026 is just working in adjacent categories. SOC 2 Type 2, ISO 27001, and the integration list (ServiceNow, Workday, Microsoft Graph, Salesforce, Atlassian) suggest Poole and Tomovski isolated the miracle correctly and are doing the GTM-and-plumbing work that follows. Where Modern diverges from Decagon: Decagon entered a sub-category my *AI Market Clarity* piece (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) flagged as "still wide open." IT service management in 2026 is not that.

Lyft Series C is the failure pattern that should worry anyone underwriting Modern at consensus seed prices — #2 (or #4) in a market with one obvious winner captures a fraction of the leader's economics, and the dossier didn't show me a credible share-inversion path (20VC). Trace that pattern onto Modern: ServiceNow holds ~40% ITSM share with 8,600 customers, *just acquired the AI-overlay leader for $2.1B*, and is shipping native agent features per The Register (Apr 2026); Serval at a $1B valuation is the consensus venture pick for the AI-native replacement; Atomicwork has Khosla and Blume. Modern at two people and a YC-standard check enters this ring as the fourth or fifth named entrant. The Moveworks exit didn't extend the runway for new entrants — it shortened it, because the obvious acquirer just bought.

Modern's revenue-velocity story is the part I cannot underwrite: the homepage cites "62% reduction in ticket handling" at an unnamed "Global IT team," "10 hours saved per new hire" at an unnamed "Series C healthtech," "80% faster approval" at an unnamed "Global enterprise" — quantified outcomes attributed to anonymous logos, no MRR disclosed, no pricing on the site, no named enterprise contract. That is exactly the "false signal vs. just working" line I drew in TechCrunch (Nov 2025). Harvey (2023) at $3B → $5B → $8B in months named the firms. Modern names percentages. In a hot AI category at Spring 2026, the silence on growth *is* the signal — and the Tim Ferriss Show #863 (April 2026) version of me would say something is fundamentally broken or the founders are being too cute.

On the catalyst dimension, Modern is leaning on a real but consensus re-opening — LLM agent reliability crossing the autonomy threshold in 2024–25, ratified by ServiceNow's $2.1B Moveworks acquisition — and the problem with consensus catalysts at the 24-month mark is that the window dynamics invert: in 2026 ITSM, capital follows the apparent winner (Serval) rather than the late entrant. Compounded over 18 months, the same catalyst that opened the wedge produces winner-concentration in it; Modern needs a distribution wedge invisible in the dossier to escape that compression. None is named.

Founder execution gets a partial pass from me on Poole — Frankie Health to Unmind (Feb 2023) with Disney, Uber, Lendlease, MLB as named customers is real enterprise-sales shipping history, and Tomovski's Wix Code and Canva internal developer platform work is genuinely relevant platform engineering. What I can't see in Modern's dossier is the Perplexity (2022) pattern — Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built. There are no GitHub repos, no public artifacts, no demo videos, no public Twitter from either founder. For a 2-person Spring 2026 YC company, that's not stage-default invisibility — it's a missing shipping-rhythm signal in the one period when shipping-rhythm signal is cheapest to produce.

My 36-month forecast: Modern is acquired by Atlassian, Freshworks, or a mid-tier ITSM platform for $40M–$150M in 2028–2029 as a small AI-feature tuck-in — ARR somewhere between $1M and $5M at exit — or it pivots into a vertical wedge (healthcare IT, given the "Series C healthtech" testimonial and Poole's Frankie background) and survives as a $10M–$30M ARR vertical SaaS that never breaks out. ServiceNow already bought Moveworks; the obvious acquirer is gone, which is why the exit ladder runs through #2 platforms, not #1.

The bull case that should sting: Poole has shipped and sold to enterprise (Frankie → Unmind, $100M+ raised across that cohort, Disney/Uber/MLB logos), Tomovski has shipped developer platforms inside two large product companies (Wix, Canva), and the Moveworks exit is *positive* signal for the category because it proves real dollars get paid for AI-on-ITSM — not negative signal. The single-miracle architecture is the cleanest of any company I'm seeing this batch in the IT category, and Decagon (2023) is in my portfolio precisely because that architecture works. Anduril (2022) is my Known Exception for entering a contested-incumbent market — defense had Lockheed; ITSM has ServiceNow; the parallel holds. The dossier evidence that wins the argument against the bull: Anduril's catalyst was Ukraine, a 24-month re-opening *outside* the incumbent's response window; Modern's catalyst is *already inside* ServiceNow's response window — they already responded by paying $2.1B for the leader. The condition that flips my verdict: Modern discloses $100K+ MRR with two named enterprise logos signed in the last 90 days, or a Series A markup from a Sequoia/Benchmark/Founders-Fund-tier fund inside 6 months. Absent either, this is the Harvey-clone shape at the wrong end of *AI Market Clarity* — pass.
