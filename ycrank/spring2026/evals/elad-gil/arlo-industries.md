# Arlo Industries -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Arlo Industries is a *catalyst-strong, curve-silent* pre-seed -- Deo Arlo is pitching the cleanest C-UAS market re-opening I've seen this batch (Shahed-136 proliferation, $1.8B in U.S. federal counter-drone funding per Airsight, 25.1% CAGR through 2030 per MarketsandMarkets Aug 2025) while the dossier discloses zero revenue, zero signed LOIs, and a team of three with two members publicly anonymous. That pairing is the entire investment question on Arlo Industries: same catalyst geometry as **Anduril** (2022), but at the seed stage I explicitly said I would not have written for Anduril.

On catalyst, **Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy -- Ukraine exposed a Pentagon-procurement window in the 24-month frame, and Arlo Industries is naming the *sharper* version of that same window: not "defense software broadly," but specifically counter-drone sensing against Shahed-class munitions and FPV swarms, the exact threat class that Iron Dome and legacy single-node radars were not designed against. Deo Arlo lived through Shahed engagements near his Israeli apartment per the YC bio -- that's the catalyst arriving as founder-market fit, not as TAM PowerPoint. Where Arlo Industries diverges from the Anduril template is the *stage at which I'm being asked to underwrite the catalyst*: my Anduril override explicitly reads "I wrote a growth-stage check, not a science-stage seed," and Arlo Industries is asking me to do exactly the latter on a passive-coherent-location mesh with no published field benchmarks. The catalyst is real; the entry point is wrong.

The "no public data found" line on revenue is what worries me on Arlo Industries -- this is the dimension that distinguishes **Harvey** (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" from the dozens of legal-AI clones I passed on. Arlo Industries discloses "field demos to Ukrainian militaries on the frontlines, as well as to operators across Europe and the USA," which in my taxonomy is the canonical *false signal*: frontline demos are enterprise-pilot tourism unless they convert into Ukrainian MOD or European MOD procurement dollars within 6-9 months, and the dossier names zero contract counterparties. In AI cycles I've said silence on growth is itself a negative signal; in defense the same rule holds with the caveat that contracts are lumpy -- but you still expect *one named pilot dollar figure* by Spring 2026 YC demo day if the demos were converting.

Mechanism on the founder-shipping dimension: Deo Arlo has a Technion robotics patent on an end-effector and was CTO/co-founder of Makrverse, which is a real shipped-artifact signal -- not pedigree-only. That's mid-rung, not Aravind-Srinivas-pings-LinkedIn-with-a-built-thing rung. What compounds or fails over 18 months here is whether the two unnamed teammates ("PhD in drones and autonomy and military experts") turn out to be the technical center of gravity Deo Arlo cannot be on his own; passive coherent-location at military SWaP/latency in cluttered EW is not a robotics-patent problem, it's a signal-processing-and-RF problem, and Arlo Industries hasn't shown me that hire publicly.

Counterfactual on index position: for Arlo Industries to flip from "must beat named incumbents on features" into "workflow path every entrant must touch," the mesh would need to become the *interoperability standard* that Anduril Sentry/Pulsar and Echodyne AESA radars integrate against, rather than compete with -- that's a protocol play, not a product play. Nothing in the dossier suggests Arlo Industries is pursuing that posture. Today Arlo Industries is selling sensing-only into a market where Anduril ships sensing + effectors integrated, Echodyne just landed a $490M USAF program (The Defense Post, Apr 2026), and Dedrone has $130M in the bank (Tracxn). Sensing-only as a wedge is reasonable; sensing-only as the *index position* requires a distribution thesis Arlo Industries has not articulated.

Single-anchor parallel on the miracle test: **Color Genomics** (2015) was the workflow-on-commodity-sequencing structure precisely because betting on the chemistry layer was a multi-miracle architecture I refused to underwrite. Arlo Industries requires (a) passive coherent-location performance at military range and EW resilience that academia has established but no one has fielded at SWaP, (b) defense procurement break-in against twelve named incumbents and ITAR posture not yet disclosed, and (c) mesh-density data flywheel that only compounds after large-scale field deployment Arlo Industries hasn't yet won. That's three independent miracles, compounding small odds -- the exact structure I avoided in fusion and novel-battery-chem, dressed in defense clothing.

My 36-month forecast on Arlo Industries: alive but sub-scale, $3-12M in Ukrainian and European MOD contract revenue, acquired by Anduril or BlueHalo as the passive-sensing layer of an integrated C-UAS stack at a $80-200M outcome -- not an independent index company on counter-drone. The mechanism is straightforward: the catalyst pulls one or two real frontline contracts in, the integrated primes notice that passive mesh complements their active-radar gap, and Arlo Industries' best exit is to *become* the sensing module rather than the sensing platform.

The bull case that stings: Anduril at YC-equivalent stage looked like exactly this -- defense graveyard consensus, a founder whose prior shipping (Oculus, Palantir) was not directly C-UAS, and a thesis that "software-on-defense distribution" was the single miracle. My own Known Exception explicitly covers this archetype. The dossier evidence that wins for me is the comparative founder shipping baseline: Palmer Luckey had shipped Oculus through acquisition before Anduril, Trae Stephens had built Palantir's forward-deployed muscle at scale -- Deo Arlo has a Technion patent and a Makrverse CTO line, which is a real signal but not the same magnitude, and the two unnamed co-founders mean I cannot yet evaluate the technical center of gravity. The condition that flips Arlo Industries to invest: one named procurement contract above $2M from Ukrainian MOD, a NATO member MOD, or a U.S. service branch within six months, *plus* public disclosure of the PhD co-founder's prior shipped autonomy work. Both, not either.
