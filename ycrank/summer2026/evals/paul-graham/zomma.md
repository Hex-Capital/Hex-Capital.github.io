# Zomma -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Zomma is a real schlep -- custodian-portal reconciliation, KYC refresh, SEC-ready audit trails -- attacked by a trio whose GitHub footprint is empty across all three names. That pairing is the signature: the problem is one I'd normally lean toward on "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) grounds, but the founders arrive without the hacker tracks that made me trust the Collisons at **Stripe** (2010) to actually build through it. Varun Arumugam, Cashel Fitzgerald, and Jihyun Kim have finance-industry résumés stapled to a computer-use-agent product, and I'm reading the résumés hoping to find the animal underneath.

On schlep, the ladder tops out at **Coinbase** (2012), where Armstrong's willingness to eat the bitcoin-compliance grind was the moat. Zomma's territory rhymes: hedge funds, asset managers, SEC audit trails, custodian portals "that will never expose APIs." That is the shape of a moat other applicants will unconsciously route around. But the divergence from **Coinbase** (2012) is that Armstrong personally embodied the schlep -- he wanted the regulatory pain. Zomma's dossier tells me the *problem* is schleppy; it does not tell me the founders are hungry for the specific pain of onboarding a fund administrator's vendor-risk review. The "Agent Trust Framework" is marketing copy without a SOC 2 to back it, and SOC 2 is the schlep. So Zomma sits above **Stripe** (2010)'s payments-plumbing rung but below **Coinbase** (2012)'s regulatory-appetite rung until someone shows me the compliance stunt.

On hacker genealogy, the failure mechanism I remember most cleanly is my own pass example -- the polished AI-wrapper team with a glossy deck and no shipped artifact, no GitHub trail, no hack history. That pattern loads: Zomma's dossier reports "No public repos found" for Varun, "No public repos found" for Cashel, and Jihyun Kim's profile could not even be disambiguated. Cashel's Cornell CS MS and ML Engineer role at Modern Intelligence is the strongest technical signal, but "ML Engineer at defense-AI shop" is a credential, not a Lisp prize. The **Reddit** (2005) bet had Aaron Swartz co-authoring RSS before the check; the **Stripe** (2010) bet had Patrick winning Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp work at seventeen. Zomma has neither analogue, and vision-based LLM agents are precisely the domain where I want to see someone who has been hacking on this since Anthropic shipped Computer Use in late 2024.

Varun Arumugam is a prior YC founder with a stated exit and $10-12M in sales -- the closest thing in the dossier to an animal signal. This is the **Twitch** (2007) re-fund pattern in miniature: I readmitted Kan and Shear after **Kiko** (2005) failed because coming back is itself the trait. Except Kan came back with Justin.tv livestreaming his own life 24/7 as the pre-funding artifact; Zomma's Varun comes back with a "/call" demo CTA and no linkable prototype video. The prior exit earns him the benefit of the doubt that gets Zomma above the pass floor, but it does not clear the artifact bar Houston cleared with the **Dropbox** (2007) Hacker News video.

For Zomma's idea-organic-to-founder-pain dimension to flip a tier, Varun would need to have personally reconciled JPMorgan back-office data against custodian portals as a line analyst, and Cashel would need to have shipped internal automation for Voyant's financial-planning reconciliation workflows before Zomma existed. The dossier gestures at ex-JPMorgan and Voyant SWE credentials but does not put either founder inside the specific screen-scraping pain the product addresses. Compare **Dropbox** (2007), where Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and the origin story was the founder's own Monday morning. Zomma's origin story reads more like "we noticed hedge funds have this problem," which is closer to competitive analysis than to the USB-stick pattern.

Zomma's cofounder configuration -- three founders, Varun and Cashel sharing UT Austin -- is the trio shape **Airbnb** (2009) and **Twitch** (2007) both had, and I'll take it. But the dossier reports no data on how long the three worked together, and Jihyun Kim's profile is not even disambiguated in public sources. This is a **Reddit** (2005)-Huffman-and-Ohanian-were-dorm-roommates gap: the surface shape is right, but the load-bearing history is missing.

My 36-month forecast: Zomma reaches roughly $500K-$2M ARR selling to two or three hedge funds, then either gets acquihired into SS&C, iCapital, or Juniper Square in the $15-40M range as those fund administrators shore up their own agent offerings, or gets squeezed by Anthropic's May 2026 finance-agent templates and pivots to a narrow KYC-refresh wedge. The mechanism is Anthropic simultaneously being Zomma's named model dependency and its most credible replacement -- shipping ten pre-built KYC and month-end-close agents while running a $1.5B JV with Blackstone and Goldman Sachs. When your supplier is your competitor and has ten times your distribution into your buyer, the ceiling is acquihire, not "Stripe is the next Google."

The strongest bull case I can make against my own forecast: Varun already did the YC-exit tour at $10-12M, and prior founder exits compound the way **Kiko** (2005) compounded into **Twitch** (2007). The animal test lives in the résumé line, not in the deck. If Zomma has a demo that actually completes an end-to-end reconciliation on a live Bloomberg AIM or SS&C Advent portal without the "stops for human approval" caveat that caps ROI, the schlep-blindness dividend is real and the Anthropic-template threat is neutralized because templates do not integrate with custodian portals -- Zomma does. The dossier evidence that wins for me anyway: every action "stops for human approval before anything submits," which is a labor-augmentation product priced against an automation-ROI expectation, and Zomma's dossier discloses zero customers, zero revenue, zero SOC 2. The condition that flips my verdict is a signed contract with a named hedge fund of $10B+ AUM at $200K+ ACV, disclosed with the SOC 2 date. Absent that, Zomma is a competent Neutral bet whose ceiling I can already see.
