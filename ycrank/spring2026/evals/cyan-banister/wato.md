# Wato -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Rahul Rejeev and Arihan Varanasi are two Stanford CS founders building agent memory in a category where Mem0 just closed $24M, Letta closed $10M at a $70M post, and Zep came through YC W24 -- which is to say Wato is the pass example I wrote down out loud, walking into the room wearing a name tag. The dimensional signature here is brutal: the lowest rung is founder origin -- the dossier gives me Mercor ML and Stanford undergrad classes of 2024-2028, not a person who *lived* the problem of agents losing memory inside an institution -- and the highest rung is, charitably, the default on co-founder governance because the dossier simply doesn't tell me who the CEO is. A company whose best dimension is silence on tiebreaker authority is not a company I'm wiring my own money into.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before Anduril, and that prior-product trajectory is what made the 2017 check on defense hardware tractable for me when peers had moral objections. Wato's founders are credentialed -- Stanford CS, Mercor ML, PlayAI -- but credentials are the opposite of what loads the Luckey analogy. The load-bearing question is whether Rahul or Arihan ran an "institution" choking on agent context loss and got tired enough of it to quit and build the fix. The dossier reads "BS Computer Science, Stanford (2024-2028)" for Arihan, which means the co-founder is mid-undergrad; that is not disqualifying on its own -- Luckey was nineteen -- but Luckey had *shipped a headset to scale*. I see no analogous prior artifact in Wato's dossier. This is the "discovered the problem in a memo" pattern, dressed in better technical clothing.

Crusoe burning North Dakota flares for Bitcoin in 2018 was magically weird because three credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds and the founder had social license to walk on the well pads. Wato is the mirror image: agent memory is now the most-funded picks-and-shovels category of the 2025-2026 cycle, with Mem0 at 41K+ GitHub stars and 14M downloads, Letta at $70M post, LangChain shipping LangMem natively, and a vendor landscape blog already comparing four named incumbents in April 2026. The round won't be underpriced because the category isn't ignored -- it's the consensus infrastructure bet. Wato's positioning ("team memory + governed tools + living artifacts") is a differentiation paragraph, not a moat, and "find them before it becomes consensus" inverts here: the consensus is already priced.

Single unambiguous CEO -- the dossier names two co-founders and zero CEOs; over 18 months that compounds badly when a Stanford-undergrad co-founder still in school (2024-2028) and a Mercor ML engineer disagree on whether Wato is a Slack-native consumer-of-Slack product or a horizontal governance layer for enterprise agent fleets, and there is no documented tiebreaker. The HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll feud started exactly here. I'm applying the silence-equals-near-term-blow-up-risk default and sitting below midpoint.

Capital-intensity counterfactual -- for Wato's score on the frontier/unsexy axis to flip a tier, the product would need to cross into regulated audit territory that single-API memory SDKs structurally cannot enter: SOC 2 isolation, on-prem deployment into a defense or healthcare institution where Mem0's cloud-first posture is disqualifying, an actual "governance" surface that compounds the way Vigil Labs compounds in regulated finance. The dossier hints at "scoped permissions" and "approved tools" but gives me no signed enterprise LOI, no regulated-vertical anchor, no on-prem SKU. Until one of those shows up, Wato is software-on-software and the moat is a wireframe.

Niantic was a single-anchor parallel I can use on physical verifiability: I played Ingress obsessively from 2012 onward and sourced the check from my own thumbs on glass. Wato's product is "shared memory and living artifacts for AI in teams" in private beta with no public pricing, no waitlist size, no Product Hunt launch (the one Product Hunt hit is an unrelated game), and a Slack-resident Cloud Agents announcement from May 17, 2026 that I cannot install in my own life within 48 hours and feel pull from. The tactile artifact is absent. This is the B2B-abstraction failure mode the Niantic parallel was built to catch.

Three-year forecast: Wato is either acqui-hired by LangChain, Mem0, or a Slack-adjacent platform for sub-$30M in 2027-2028 -- talent acquisition with the product wound down -- or it shuts down quietly as the memory layer consolidates into one or two winners with 100x its GitHub mindshare. The mechanism producing this is the dimensional signature: no lived founder origin to anchor a stubborn pivot, no weird-category lane to retreat into, no tactile artifact to drive bottoms-up love, and a competitive set with $34M+ of dry powder and existing distribution.

The strongest bull case is the DeepMind exception -- Hassabis was pedigreed but polymath, and cross-domain compounding can override the bias against credentialed founders. Maybe Arihan Varanasi's Mercor + PlayAI + Stanford ML stack plus YC Group Partner Nicolas Dessaigne (Algolia co-founder, who knows how developer infrastructure compounds) yields a polymath who out-builds Mem0's roadmap. The problem with the bull case is that DeepMind's polymath signature was *visible at investment* -- chess prodigy, neuroscientist, game designer, three domains already compounded. Wato's dossier gives me one domain compounding (ML engineering) and a 2024-2028 undergrad timeline that argues against the multi-domain depth the exception requires. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed enterprise LOI with a named regulated institution -- defense contractor, top-20 bank, hospital system -- where Wato's governance surface is doing work Mem0 and Letta architecturally cannot. Absent that, I pass, and I'd rather write the same check into the next Vigil Labs.
