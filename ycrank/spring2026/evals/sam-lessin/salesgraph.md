# Salesgraph -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Gong has raised $584M and runs $300M+ in ARR. Clari absorbed Salesloft in December 2025 with $520M in combined funding. People.ai pulled in $198M. And now Salesgraph — two people, no revenue, no public traction — walks in and says "we'll aggregate data from all of these players' APIs and summarize it with an LLM." This is the exact pitch I've been warning people about for two years. Every time you get pitched "AI for sales workflows," you should be asking: who already has the data, the distribution, and the customer relationship? The answer here is Gong. Clari-Salesloft. Salesforce itself. Salesgraph is building in the most consensus-hot, most crowded, most incumbent-friendly category in B2B SaaS right now. There is no mispricing — the entire market is already priced for AI-eats-sales. The risk isn't baked into the price; the hype is.

Salesgraph's dossier states its core capabilities are "data aggregation from APIs, LLM-based summarization, and document generation" using "generally available technologies." I don't need to run the AI kill test very hard here — Salesgraph fails it on the dossier's own terms. Gong already has the call transcripts. Salesforce already has the CRM data. When Gong's product team reads about Salesgraph generating pre-call briefs from call recordings and CRM fields, they don't think "oh shit" — they think "fuck yeah, we should ship that by Q3." This is the Adobe problem I keep talking about. Salesgraph is a feature for an incumbent, not a company. The two-person team is maintaining integrations across ten-plus third-party APIs — Salesforce, HubSpot, Gong, Clari, ZoomInfo, Clearbit, 6sense, Salesloft, Outreach — and any one of those providers can rate-limit them, deprecate an endpoint, or just build the same feature natively. That's not a moat. That's a dependency chain.

Ricardo Nunez and Ruhan Ponnada are clearly competent builders — both among Mintlify's first ten employees, Ruhan with a CS degree from Georgia Tech and PM experience at Microsoft Mesh, Ricardo with solid full-stack engineering chops (28 public GitHub repos, React Server Components work). They built the business case generator prototype at Mintlify that reportedly helped close enterprise deals at higher ACV. That's genuine founder-market fit. But the sanity-insanity ratio is inverted here. These are sane people pitching a sane idea. "Use AI to prepare sales reps for calls" is the most obvious application of LLMs that any sales leader in America has already thought of. When I backed Raj on Solana at four cents, the idea sounded insane — a new Layer 1 when Ethereum had "won." When I invested $1.7M in Marina Mogilko for 5% of her earnings for thirty years, people thought I'd lost it. Salesgraph doesn't make me think "that's fucking crazy" — it makes me think "yeah, obviously, and so does everyone else."

The bull case deserves real engagement. Salesgraph's cross-tool aggregation angle is structurally interesting: Gong won't deeply integrate Clari data because they're competitors, and Clari-Salesloft won't surface Gong transcripts natively. There's a genuine integration gap between these siloed platforms, and Salesgraph sits in that gap. The Mintlify prototype is real validation — not a slide deck hypothesis but a tool that worked in actual enterprise sales cycles. And the timing is defensible: LLMs only recently became capable enough to synthesize unstructured call transcripts, Slack threads, and news articles into coherent business cases. Five years ago this was impossible; now it's table stakes. But "table stakes" is exactly the problem. The enabling technology is generally available. Any well-resourced competitor — or a new YC company next batch — can replicate Salesgraph's technical approach because there's no proprietary data, no protocol-level lock-in, no physical-world asset, and no network effect. The condition that would flip me: if Salesgraph showed me $50K+ MRR with negative churn and enterprise customers who tried Gong's native briefing features and chose Salesgraph anyway, I'd reconsider. That would be evidence of a workflow moat I can't see from the dossier. But right now, I see nothing that Salesgraph's own integration partners can't replicate.

Salesgraph creates no new primitive. Pre-call preparation is an existing behavior being automated, not a new category being born. Nobody will describe a future company as "the Salesgraph of X" because Salesgraph doesn't introduce a behavior that didn't exist before. Compare this to Venmo, which created peer-to-peer mobile payments as a verb — people started "Venmo-ing" each other. Or Patreon, which created direct creator monetization before "creator economy" was a phrase. Salesgraph is "better sales prep," which is execution competition in an existing category against players with hundreds of millions in funding and tens of thousands of existing customers. The capital discipline picture is fine — two founders, no hiring, presumably tight burn — but capital discipline on a melting ice cube doesn't change the temperature. The decisive dimensions here are consensus mispricing (this is the most consensus category in B2B) and structural durability (pure software wrapper on commodity LLM capabilities). Both point the same direction: pass.
