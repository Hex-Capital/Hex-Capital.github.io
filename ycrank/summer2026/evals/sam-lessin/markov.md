# Markov -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Markov is a precocious-builder team running directly at the single most consensus YC infrastructure category of 2026 -- frontier-lab data and RL environments for computer-use agents -- which means the founder shape (Harish Ashok shipping Boldmoon Cloud at 13, Zenith voice-agent workflows at 17, Dev Mandal at Sarvam AI and IIT Madras aerospace '27) is the only dimension where Markov registers as anything other than a price-taker into a category I have spent two years saying out loud is "just more internet." That high/low pairing -- visible builder temperament on top of the consensus-priced infra sleeve I re-weight away from -- is Markov's analytical signature, and it is exactly the pairing the canonical Pass Example was built to refuse.

The canonical pass: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $30M post-money, four tier-1 VCs already in." Markov is the supply-side variant of that same trade -- not the agent, but the data and RL environments for the agent -- and the failure mechanism is identical: Surge AI's $1.2B 2024 revenue, Mercor's $10B Felicis-led Series C in October 2025, Scale AI's $30B Meta stake in June 2025, plus Applied Compute at $100M post-money and Micro1 at $500M are all named in Markov's own dossier as the exact buyer-set competitors. When the dossier cannot describe the deal without naming five capitalized incumbents selling to the same five labs, the round is consensus before I have seen the price, and Markov gives me no offsetting price-discount signal -- no public valuation, no "tier-1 visibly passed," nothing that indicates Markov is the bid no one else will make. That is the precise mode I reject regardless of velocity.

Drop.io in 2007 was the bootstrapped file-sharing primitive built against free incumbents that nobody on Sand Hill wanted to underwrite -- the HIGH rung on founder conviction is the missionary willing to grind on an unsexy substrate for years before anyone gives them money. Markov's Harish Ashok genuinely loads onto that rung in pattern terms: a 13-year-old running a web-hosting business for two years, a 17-year-old shipping voice-based hardware-engineering assistants, paired with a co-founder out of Sarvam AI -- this is not consulting-deck language, this is a person who has been compiling things since middle school. Where Markov diverges from the Drop.io shape is the absence of any public writing or opinionated artifact on *why* computer-use data is the right wedge specifically; the dossier surfaces a single @DevvMandal X line ("Scale AI for robotics, with a human fleet in India") and no long-form thesis, no essay, no public fight with the incumbents' approach -- so the missionary temperament is plausible but not yet *visible* in the form I underwrite from.

Markov's "human fleet in India that can capture thousands of hours of data per day" is a labor-arbitrage operational claim, and the mechanism question is whether that arbitrage compounds over 18 months or gets crushed. Compounding case: a trained annotator/operator workforce becomes proprietary if Markov locks recurring RL training cycles with one or two labs and the labs integrate Markov-shaped data into their eval pipelines, which is exactly how Surge's relationships with OpenAI and Anthropic ossified into $1.2B of revenue. Crushing case: Surge and Mercor already operate global annotator workforces, Scale AI has the deepest one on the planet, and "fleet in India" is a unit-cost arbitrage that any incumbent can replicate inside a quarter by adding an office -- the moat is the lab relationship, not the labor. At Markov's stage, with no disclosed pilot or LOI from Anthropic / OpenAI / Google / Meta, the compounding case is a hypothesis and the crushing case is the base rate.

Markov scores at the floor on owned distribution -- @markov__ai has 1,122 followers, the only inbound channel surfaced in the dossier is a cal.com/dev-markov/30min booking link, and the relevant buyer-set is five frontier labs reachable by phone -- and the counterfactual for this dimension to flip a tier is sharp and disciplined: Dev Mandal would need to have published the actual contrarian thesis on why incumbents' RL data is mis-shaped for computer-use agents, the kind of post that gets quoted by an Anthropic researcher and pulls a pilot inbound. That artifact does not exist in the dossier, which for an enterprise B2B selling to five buyers is survivable but not Slow-Creator-Fund relevant -- this is not a founder-as-brand asset, it is a sales-led deal, and the score reflects category not shortcoming.

Markov sits in the AI-infrastructure-at-seed sleeve, and the single-anchor parallel is Aleo -- the crypto-protocol seed I wrote because the on-chain economic activity was real and the category was pre-consensus. Markov inverts every variable: the activity (Anthropic discussing >$1B in RL environment spend) is real but the category is post-consensus, with eight named competitors in the dossier and Bloomberg pieces on Surge's $25B round. "AI is fundamentally not disruptive, it's just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024) is the exact re-weighting that pushes Markov out of the sleeve regardless of the team -- if I want exposure to RL environments at this point in the cycle, I should be a Surge LP, not a Markov seed buyer.

The 36-month forecast: Markov either gets acqui-hired into Surge, Mercor, or Scale at a modest premium to seed sometime in 2027-2028 once one of those incumbents needs a computer-use vertical team, or it pivots away from frontier-lab data sales into a robotics-adjacent or enterprise-RPA dataset business after losing the buyer-concentration fight -- either path produces a fine outcome for Harish Ashok and Dev Mandal, neither produces a venture-scale return at the implied YC S26 entry price. The mechanism is buyer-concentration physics: five frontier labs cannot sustain ten capitalized vendors, and Markov has the youngest team and the smallest balance sheet in the dossier's comp set.

The bull case stings and deserves to be named: Markov's India-fleet angle is genuine ops leverage in a market where Scale AI's American workforce costs and contractor disputes are real headwinds, Anthropic explicitly redirected spend away from Scale after the Meta deal, and a Sublime Security counter-precedent exists where I bought into a hot enterprise-infra category because the team was right -- so the "consensus category" deselection is not absolute when the operator profile is unusual, which Harish-at-17 plus Dev-out-of-Sarvam arguably is. What dismantles that case is the absence of any non-consensus belief about *why* incumbents' approach to computer-use data is wrong: Markov's positioning in the dossier is "narrower wedge on the same buyer set," not "Surge's data shape will fail because X" -- Sublime had a contrarian claim about email security architecture, Markov has a labor-cost claim that any incumbent can copy. The verifiable condition that flips this verdict: a disclosed paid pilot or signed data-supply agreement with Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, or Meta named on the record at the next financing event -- without that, Markov is a competent team selling commodity exposure to my most-deselected sleeve, and I pass.
