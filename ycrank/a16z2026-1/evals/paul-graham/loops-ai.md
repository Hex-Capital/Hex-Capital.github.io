# Loops AI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

AI for e-commerce is one of those ideas where everyone immediately nods. That's the problem. When OpenAI has launched native agentic checkout in ChatGPT and Google has built agentic commerce into Search and Gemini, when Gartner is forecasting 75% of shopping interactions will involve AI by 2026, when every second pitch at demo days involves putting a chatbot on a Shopify store -- you are not in the sweet spot of "seems like a bad idea but is a good idea." You are in the dead center of consensus. Loops AI is building semantic search, virtual try-on, and AI sales agents for e-commerce brands, and I cannot find a single person who would react to that description with "that will never work." The space has Gorgias at $80M+ ARR with $114M raised, Rep AI at $3.5M ARR, Tidio with $27M raised, and Phia with a $185M valuation. The competitive intensity here is the market's way of telling you the idea sounds good to everybody, which in my framework means the return premium has already been competed away.

The founding team is four people: Ari Nazir (CEO) from finance and crypto fund management, İlker Zorluoğlu (President) from marketing and the GoArt Metaverse, Yusuf Bahadır (CTO) from Istanbul Technical University, and Hakan Baş from Peak Games. Baş's $1.8B exit to Zynga is a genuinely impressive credential, and in isolation it triggers my "follow the founder" pattern -- the same instinct that led me from Patrick Collison's first company Auctomatic to Stripe. But Baş isn't the CEO here, his role is listed only as "Co-Founder" with no operational title, and his domain expertise is mobile gaming, not commerce infrastructure. More concerning: I look for teams where at least one founder can build the product themselves, and Loops AI's CTO has no public GitHub presence. None of the four co-founders show visible evidence of being deep technical builders. Ari's background is HSBC, the White House NEC, and running a tokenized crypto fund. İlker's is communications consulting and a metaverse startup. This reads like a team assembled to pursue an opportunity, not a team that grew out of obsessive problem-solving.

The origin story reinforces that impression. Ari and İlker met in Bali and "co-founded Loop Smart AI after a shared frustration with e-commerce customer support." Being frustrated with customer support while living in Bali is not what I mean when I say "live in the future, then build what's missing." Drew Houston forgot his USB drive repeatedly and built Dropbox. The Octopart founders were physics students who couldn't find electronic parts. Those founders were embedded in the problem so deeply it was part of their daily work. A generic frustration with customer support in Bali -- a frustration shared by literally every online shopper -- doesn't signal the kind of lived-in domain understanding that produces a product with genuine insight about what the market actually wants versus what it says it wants. Nobody on this team appears to have run an e-commerce store, managed a product catalog, or handled customer support tickets at scale before deciding to solve those problems with AI.

The strongest bull case for Loops AI is the reported $500K ARR on only $400K in pre-seed funding, with 2x growth in the last 45 days and zero churn across 50+ brands including P&G Fairy, Radisson, and Mövenpick. If those numbers are real, the capital efficiency is genuinely impressive -- approaching $1 of ARR per $1 raised, with no ad spend, is the kind of scrappy execution I respect. The Turkish and MENA market entry could function as a schlep that US-focused competitors overlook, giving Loops AI time to build product depth before expanding. Hakan Baş's network and exit history could unlock enterprise relationships that a first-time team couldn't access. For this to work, Loops AI would need the self-reported traction to be accurate, the geographic wedge to hold while they build defensible product differentiation, and the platform risk from OpenAI and Google to somehow not crush the independent AI commerce agent layer. That's a lot of things that need to go right simultaneously.

But I keep returning to the structural issue. The crypto fund background, the metaverse stint, the Bali origin story -- these trace a pattern of founders who follow emerging technology waves rather than founders who are obsessively building for a problem they can't stop thinking about. Serial wave-riding is the opposite of earnestness. And in a space where Gorgias has twelve thousand merchants and OpenAI is offering checkout natively inside ChatGPT, the window for an independent AI commerce agent to establish defensible market position is closing, not opening. The claimed $500K ARR is the single strongest signal in this dossier, but it's entirely self-reported, and "zero churn" across 50+ enterprise clients is the kind of claim that makes me more skeptical, not less -- at this stage, some churn is expected and healthy.

Loops AI fails on the two dimensions I weight most heavily: organic problem discovery (Bali frustration, no e-commerce operational background, generic pain point) and idea contrarianism (the most consensus space in current AI). Hakan Baş's $1.8B exit prevents this from being a strong pass -- that caliber of founder chooses their next company for a reason -- but his unclear role and the team's collective profile don't overcome the fundamental mismatch with how I evaluate pre-seed bets.
