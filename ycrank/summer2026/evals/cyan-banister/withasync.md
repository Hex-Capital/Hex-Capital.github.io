# Async -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Ben Smith and Connor Park are two University of Michigan classmates ? one dropout, one CS grad ? shipping "AI Agents for Service Businesses" into law, healthcare, and real estate, and the analytical signature of Async is a team with clean CEO/CTO delineation (Ben Smith, CEO; Connor Park, CTO per LinkedIn) pointed at a category where they have no autobiographical foothold. That pairing ? governance neat, biography absent ? is the profile of a competent build-shop chasing a consensus wave, not a founder-of-the-place bet. I am not playing the game I seem to be playing when I evaluate Async; I am not underwriting the Word add-in against Spellbook, I am asking whether Ben Smith or Connor Park has any social license to walk onto a law firm's back office the way Chase Lochmiller walked onto a well pad, and the dossier says no.

Palmer Luckey shipped VR from his parents' garage before Anduril (2017) ? the defense pivot compounded on a hardware biography that predated the company by years. Async's founder story runs the other direction: Ben Smith sold shoes and trading cards, dropped out of Michigan at 20 to build commercial software, and worked at Merit; Connor Park's GitHub (@connorpark24, 18 followers, 72 total stars) shows a top pinned repo that is his personal website. Neither has a documented tour through a law firm, a hospital, or a brokerage. The tell is inside the dossier itself: Async pivoted from nonprofit fundraising agents, to Skope outcome-based billing (Launch HN, Aug 2025), to Skope AI Agents for Law Firms, to Async AI Agents for Service Businesses ? four positions in twelve months. That is not a founder who *lived* the law-firm problem; that is a founder who found law-firm workflows by iteration. Luckey didn't pivot into defense from fundraising software.

Crusoe (~2018) is the low rung's inverse ? Chase Lochmiller was writing checks in a category two or three credible VCs had already walked away from on aesthetic grounds, and the aesthetic revulsion was the underpricing signal. Async is the opposite trade. Harvey just closed $200M at $11B post-money co-led by GIC and Sequoia (TAMradar, Mar 2026); Legora closed $550M Series D at $5.55B (Mar 2026); Spellbook took $50M from Khosla in Oct 2025 for a Word add-in that already does what Async's Word add-in does (GC.ai, 2026). Async is entering the most crowded, most credentialed AI vertical in venture right now, and doing it via YC S25 with a "Book a demo" cal.com link. This is not magically weird ? this is the consensus itself, and my thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus (Yahoo Finance, March 2025). The round will price like every other legal-AI seed in the batch.

On governance, Async is one of the cleaner pre-seed setups I've seen this cohort ? Ben Smith is titled CEO and Connor Park is CTO on LinkedIn, so the tiebreaker question I ask post-HQ Trivia and post-GameCrush is at least answered on paper. Over 18 months, this compounds well if the two of them stay disciplined; the failure mode I watch for is the same one that killed Rus Yusupov and Colin Kroll after HQ Trivia's tactile virality faded ? unresolved authority under duress. Async has not been under duress yet, but four pivots inside a YC batch is itself a stress test they seem to have survived without visible schism, which I take as a small positive signal on Ben Smith's authority.

Counterfactual on the frontier-hardware / unsexy-regulated dimension: for Async to jump a tier here, healthcare would need to be more than a bullet on withasync.com ? I'd want to see a HIPAA BAA signed, a named provider system running the agent, and evidence that Async is willingly taking on regulated scope beyond its current SOC 2 Type I posture. As written, Async lists three verticals but ships a Word add-in and Outlook integration, which is the software-only wrapper my Pass Example targets. Legal is regulated but it is the *consensus* regulated AI vertical ? Mindbloom's ketamine or Vigil Labs' financial software would be the shape I want. Async does not yet have that shape.

Physical-world verifiability is where Async gets a small anchor to Postmates (2011) ? I could ride the Postmates courier and feel the product; I could, in theory, install Async's Word add-in and watch it draft. Clifton Law PLLC (Adriena Clifton) is a named customer on withasync.com, which is a real tactile thread ? one solo attorney using the product in the wild. But Postmates delivered to my apartment; Async delivers into an SMB law firm's SharePoint that I do not have credentials to. The artifact exists, it just isn't ambient. I score this in the middle of the range, not the top.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Async does not reach $3M ARR under its current positioning; the most likely outcome is one more pivot ? probably narrowing from three verticals to a single wedge inside legal ops ? followed by an acqui-hire into a mid-tier legal-AI or vertical-SaaS player (Ironclad, Clio, or a Spellbook-tier acquirer) at sub-$40M, with Ben Smith and Connor Park landing as senior engineers or a small product pod. The mechanism is Harvey and Legora eating the top of the market with cheaper capital, Spellbook eating the Word-add-in interface with two years of head start, and Async's services-shaped "audit ? scope ? deploy ? maintain" motion never crossing into the SaaS gross-margin profile that would attract growth capital.

The strongest case against this pass is my own biography: Ben Smith dropped out of Michigan at 20, worked retail, and taught himself commercial software ? that is the outsider-trajectory pattern I wrote HandUp (~2014) for, and I have written checks on biographical resonance alone. But dropping out to build software at 20 is the modal YC founder story, not the HandUp override; HandUp's founder mirrored my homelessness, not my dropout. The other override to check is HQ Trivia ? explosive tactile product-led pull that overrides governance concerns ? and Async's traction is 55 Hacker News points for a *prior* product (Skope, Aug 2025) plus one named customer. That is not HQ Trivia. Nothing in Async's dossier meets the exception bar. The condition that flips my pass to an invest: Async lands three signed, named law-firm customers (not solo shops like Clifton Law PLLC) within six months, publishes a specific "why the founder ended up here" origin story that ties Ben Smith or Connor Park to a law firm they lived inside, and the round is priced as a services-shaped seed the consensus funds are avoiding on gross-margin grounds. Until then, Async is a competent execution team pointed at the wrong lane for my check.
