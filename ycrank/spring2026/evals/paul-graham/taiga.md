# Taiga -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Nanda Guntupalli and Adam Wax have been friends since third grade and are now Penn CS undergrads building the medical billing tool their physician fathers needed -- which makes Taiga's co-founder chemistry the strongest dimension in the dossier and its hacker genealogy the weakest, since neither founder has a public GitHub, an open-source artifact, or anything resembling Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010). That pairing -- overdetermined team bond plus unproven technical lineage, attacking parental pain -- is what Taiga actually is.

**Airbnb** (2009) sits at the top of my co-founder ladder because Chesky, Gebbia, and Blecharczyk had roomed together in San Francisco before the cereal boxes and could not be pried apart by failure. Taiga's pair is on a different mechanism but the same axis: a 3rd-grade-to-Penn friendship is longer than most YC marriages, longer than the Collison brothers' working relationship at **Auctomatic** (2007), and the kind of bond that survives the first time a payer rejects 4,000 claims in a week. Where Taiga diverges from the **Airbnb** rung is that Nanda and Adam are Class of 2028 -- meaning sophomores -- and the dossier surfaces no analog to the Obama O's / Cap'n McCain's stunt that flipped my skepticism in the interview; the relationship is durable, but durability is not yet evidence of the animal moment.

**Reddit** (2005) sits at the top of my hacker-genealogy ladder because Aaron Swartz co-authored the RSS specification before he ever wrote a Reddit line; the technical lineage was visible without anyone having to ask. Taiga's dossier has the inverse shape -- "No public GitHub account found" for both Nanda Guntupalli and Adam Wax, no competitive-programming wins, no Hacker News presence, just the LinkedIn line "Penn CS." Penn CS is a credential, not a hack history, and credential-only is exactly what I trained myself to discount after **Viaweb** (1995). The 10 EHR integrations and 50+ payer connections on usetaiga.com are real artifacts and they push the score up, but they are the company's artifacts, not the founders' pre-Taiga technical fingerprints, and that distinction is the whole point of this dimension.

Taiga's schlep score compounds the right way over 18 months because medical billing is genuinely the kind of work other YC Spring 2026 applicants are unconsciously routing around -- 50+ payer integrations, denial-appeal workflows, fraud exposure on miscoded claims under federal payer rules, the per-EHR integration grind across DrChrono, athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, NextGen, Tebra, Elation, Greenway, Practice Fusion, Office Ally, and Amazing Charts. This is **Coinbase** (2012)-shaped unpleasantness, and the fact that Candid Health ($99.5M) and Adonis ($95M+) both raised heavily on the same schlep tells me the unpleasantness is real, not that the space is closed -- incumbents who priced in labor margins cannot cannibalize their own billable hours. Taiga is in the schlep, and Taiga did not flinch when the schlep arrived; that earns the dimension most of its range.

For Taiga's organic-idea score to flip up a tier, one fact would have to be different: Nanda Guntupalli or Adam Wax would have to have personally worked the billing desk at their fathers' practices -- not just grown up watching it. The dossier says "grew up at his father's medical practice" and "parent is a physician with own practice," which is biographical proximity to the pain, not Drew Houston forgetting his USB stick at MIT for **Dropbox** (2007). It is closer to the Collisons-at-Stripe pattern than to nothing, because the founders saw the denials and the AR aging in their own kitchens, but the gap between "my father lost revenue to this" and "I lost revenue to this" is the gap between an 18/25 and a 22/25. The named customer Cheskis Family Therapy reads as a friend-of-family logo, which is consistent with the parental-pain origin and consistent with the missing personal-use moment.

On animal resourcefulness, Taiga's closest single-anchor parallel is Brian Armstrong's hand-built Android Bitcoin wallet that overturned the **Coinbase** (2012) rejection -- because in both cases the founder's answer to "what did you do before the institutional check" is "I shipped the thing." Taiga has 10 named EHR integrations and 50+ payer connections live on usetaiga.com built by two undergrads, and that is not nothing; shipping production integrations to athenahealth and eClinicalWorks as a sophomore is closer to the wallet-prototype moment than to the MBA-deck pattern I pass on. What Taiga's dossier is missing is the stunt -- no cereal boxes, no door-to-door photography, no revenue improvisation named -- and the 98% touchless claim rate is company-reported against an undisclosed denominator. Animals leave specific tracks, and the dossier shows the product but does not show the moment.

My 36-month forecast: Taiga reaches $1-3M ARR on percentage-of-collections from independent practices by mid-2028, gets squeezed as Candid Health pushes downmarket from its 200+ multi-site customer base, and is acquihired by Tebra or athenahealth in the $20-50M range -- which is the Kiko-shaped outcome, not the Twitch-shaped one. The mechanism is that the team's high co-founder bond and real schlep exposure get them to a wedge but the missing hacker lineage and missing pre-funding stunt mean they will not out-build Adonis (4x revenue growth in 2025) on raw velocity when the wedge needs to widen.

The strongest case against that forecast is that "incumbent will crush the kids" is exactly the bear case I rejected on **Coinbase** (2012) when Armstrong was supposedly outgunned by Coinsetter and TradeHill -- the schlep itself was the moat and the regulators kept the well-funded competitors slower than the founder who actually liked the grind. If Nanda Guntupalli and Adam Wax are animals on the compliance dimension the way Armstrong was, the $99.5M Candid Health raised is a liability, not an advantage, because Candid Health targets multi-site groups and cannot cost-effectively serve a 1-doctor practice. The dossier evidence that wins for me anyway is the absence of any named pre-funding stunt and the absence of a public hacker footprint for either founder -- Armstrong had a working wallet I could hold; Taiga has a working website I cannot independently verify the 98% on. The single verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: 20+ named independent-practice customers paying percentage-of-collections within six months, or one of the founders surfacing a non-Taiga shipped artifact (a clinic-side tool, an open-source coder, anything) that proves the technical lineage the LinkedIn lines do not.
