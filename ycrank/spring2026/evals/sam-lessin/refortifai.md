# Refortifai -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Microsoft shipped Refortifai's product for free on April 2, 2026. That's not a metaphor. The Agent Governance Toolkit — MIT license, five programming languages, sub-0.1ms latency, covers all ten OWASP agentic AI risks, plugs into LangChain, CrewAI, and Google ADK — is literally the open-source version of "intercept tool calls, enforce governance policies, block attacks in real time." Every time I get pitched a startup and the first thing I think is "this is perfect for Microsoft," I know the answer. Refortifai is building a security feature that the largest platform company on earth decided was valuable enough to ship and commoditized enough to give away. That's not a competitive landscape. That's a eulogy.

Noma Security has raised $132M and reported 1,300% ARR growth. Capsule Security raised $7M in the same month Refortifai entered YC. PromptArmor came through YC W24 with Accel and Lightspeed behind it. AI agent security is the definition of a consensus-hot category in Spring 2026 — every sophisticated investor in the ecosystem is already bidding on this space. When I backed Solana at four cents, every smart person told me I was wrong. With Refortifai, every smart person would tell me I'm right, and that's exactly the problem. The risk is not in the price. The upside is already priced in by a dozen competing checks, and the downside — open-source substitution, incumbent replication — is being actively ignored. Refortifai has zero mispricing advantage; the market loves this category, which means the expected returns are compressed before you even wire the money.

Refortifai's YC page says "runtime security for AI agents" while refortif.ai describes "anti-cheat for AI models" with weight obfuscation and secured runtimes. These aren't two features of one product — they're two different companies targeting two different buyers with two different threat models. The weight-obfuscation angle is actually more interesting to me because it's less consensus: model IP protection through obfuscation that maintains full inference speed is a genuinely harder technical problem than policy enforcement on tool calls. If Refortifai were purely a model-weight protection company, I'd give it a longer look on the primitive test. But the pivot toward agent runtime security — the YC framing — moves them squarely into a feature category that Microsoft, CrowdStrike, and Palo Alto can all absorb. Neither product creates a new behavior or financial instrument. "Intercept tool calls, apply policy, allow/deny" is not a primitive. It's plumbing that every security incumbent will build or acquire. Patreon created the behavior of paying creators directly. Venmo created the verb. Refortifai creates a dashboard.

Atman Kar's RTL design background at Texas Instruments and Tenstorrent is legitimate low-level systems expertise — building automotive RADAR hardware accelerators is real engineering, and his GitHub shows someone who writes operating systems and CPU emulators for fun. That's the kind of technical depth I respect. But Atman's skills map to the weight-obfuscation product, not the agent security product Refortifai is now pitching. Sayan Mitra's public background is essentially invisible — I can't find a confirmed LinkedIn, and a possible Zscaler connection is unverified. Rithik Jain pivoted from economics to CS at Wisconsin, which shows intellectual curiosity but not security domain depth. The co-founders are described as "friends," not as people who've been through professional fire together. I back founders I've watched across multiple cycles — Raj through three companies before Solana, Andrew Kortina from college through Venmo. Refortifai gives me no founder signal strong enough to override the structural problems, and the idea itself isn't "insane" — it's the safest possible pitch in the safest possible category. Sane people with sane ideas is the lowest-energy configuration.

The bull case, if I'm being honest with myself, goes like this: agent autonomy is genuinely new attack surface, the market is still early enough that the $574.9M SAM is going to 50x, and Refortifai's weight-obfuscation IP could become a defensible wedge that incumbents can't easily replicate because it requires Atman's hardware-level understanding of how inference actually works at the silicon layer. Maybe the YC framing is just go-to-market packaging and the real moat is in the obfuscation math. I've checked my Known Exceptions — Slack overrode consensus pricing because it created a genuine new behavioral primitive. Refortifai doesn't create a new behavior. Clubhouse overrode structural durability because of a new social primitive emerging in real-time. Refortifai isn't birthing new social behavior. No exception applies. One condition that would flip me: if Refortifai showed me proprietary weight-obfuscation benchmarks demonstrating performance parity with unprotected models on frontier architectures, with a patent filing, and abandoned the agent-security framing entirely — then we'd be talking about a company with real IP in a category most VCs haven't thought about yet. That would create mispricing.

Refortifai's total picture is a consensus-priced AI security wrapper in a crowded category where Microsoft has already open-sourced the core capability, built by founders without confirmed security domain expertise, with no visible traction and a confused product direction. The two dimensions that kill this deal are Consensus Mispricing — there is none, every VC in the market is excited about AI security — and Structural Durability, where Microsoft's free open-source toolkit is the AI kill test playing out in real-time, not in theory.
