# Chert -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Chert is the canonical shape of the deal I describe in my Pass Example: two technical founders -- Gary Gao (Penn CS/Wharton) and Ian Fong (NYU Math/Econ, 8x hackathon winner, ex-incoming JPMorgan) -- shipping an API wrapper on a protocol Apple controls, positioning as "Twilio for iMessage" against Sendblue, LoopMessage, Blooio, and Linq in a CPaaS sub-category Mordor sizes at $21.27B. The dimensional signature is consequence-floor paired with founder-iteration evidence -- a competent CPaaS micro-bet, not a civilizational redirection. My **Pass Example** exists in the persona file specifically so I do not waste a second meeting on this shape.

On consequence -- my entry point -- the HIGH rung is **Impossible Foods** (2011), where the success-state collapsed animal protein production as a category. Chert's maximum-ambition success-state is that blue-bubble outbound replaces a slice of SMS/email outbound for sales teams already using HubSpot, Close, and GoHighLevel -- the integrations listed on trychert.com. That is an "optimization play" in the exact sense my Outlook Business essay warns against: even if Chert wins its category outright against Sendblue's 5-year head start, no incumbent regime disappears, no $15T labor line moves, and Apple still owns the rails. McKinsey forecast under 1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 and missed by 108M because the success-state was civilizational; Chert's success-state, by contrast, is a 65% response rate inside one outbound channel.

On technical and capital risk, the LOW rung's failure mechanism is the one I named at TechCrunch Disrupt 2013: "any Tier-1 generalist can fund this" -- pure software execution risk with no physics, no biology, no fleet, no plant. That pattern is in Chert's dossier in full: the $500K YC standard check is the entire disclosed capital, there is no proprietary stack beneath the iMessage relay, and Blooio is already undercutting the category at $39/mo flat-rate. Apple is not a science miracle Chert is solving -- it is a counterparty risk Chert is absorbing. The same anti-spam line-banning regime that gates Sendblue gates Chert; "comprehensive line health checks" is a product feature, not a moat.

Founder-expert-defiance is the dimension where Chert is closest to my TED Talk warning that "experts extrapolate the past" -- except Chert's founders are not even experts yet defying anything, they are credentialed generalists (Penn CS/Wharton, NYU Math/Econ) building inside a category Sendblue defined. There is no public thesis from Gao or Fong contradicting their field, no "too important not to do" framing in the Launch HN post, no instigator posture. The dossier silence here is the signal -- not the absence of evidence, the evidence of absence.

Counterfactual on learning rate: for Chert to clear the **Slash** (2024) bar that justified a $100M Series C exception, I would need a documented pivot with stated reasoning -- the publicly told Kanye moment Slash's founder put on record. What Chert offers instead is "the last six months building projects on iMessage for leasing companies, DTC startups, and home service agencies" before launch. That is iteration inside one thesis, not a pivot off one. Real but mid -- the kind of evidence that moves Chert off the floor on this axis but cannot carry the other four.

Single-anchor parallel on capital fit: my $115M Series A lead in **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) worked because the roadmap required capital depth no consensus VC would write -- the check size was the moat. Chert's roadmap requires the opposite: $500K YC dollars to a 2-person team selling API calls. I am structurally over-resourced; the firm that should fund Chert's next round is a CPaaS-focused seed shop, not Khosla Ventures running a $3.5B 2025 vehicle.

My 36-month forecast: Chert plateaus under $3M ARR by mid-2029 and is acqui-hired by Sendblue, HubSpot, or a CRM consolidator for under $15M, or Apple formalizes a business-iMessage program and the entire category -- Chert, Sendblue, LoopMessage, Blooio, Linq -- compresses inside 18 months. The mechanism is the consequence-floor: small success-states attract small exits, and platform-dependent businesses do not get to choose when the platform owner shows up.

The strongest bull case is the **Slash** (2024) override -- YC-pedigreed founders, conversational-AI wave, David Lieb as YC partner, a Whop logo on the site, and 179 Product Hunt upvotes inside a category I have demonstrably funded around (Cognition, Glean) when stack depth is real. The bull argues Chert rides iMessage agentic outbound the way Slash rode consumer banking. The dossier breaks that analogy: Slash got the exception on a documented pivot at Series C scale, not a pre-seed founder with no public thesis update; Glean got the exception on enterprise information asymmetry consequence, not a sales-outreach channel feature. What would flip my verdict: Gary Gao or Ian Fong publishing a falsifiable thesis on why iMessage agentic outbound restructures a named labor market with cost figures attached -- the $15T frame, not the 65% response rate. Until that, I pass in the 30 seconds my Pass Example budgets for this shape.
