# ProjectX -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

ProjectX is a per-app GPU virtualization stack wearing an agent-OS costume, and the costume is the entire investment thesis -- Rounak Adhikary's company describes itself as "the first OS where humans and agents work with no limits," but the Tracxn competitor set is Canonical, OpenStack, and Neverinstall, and the dossier's own TAM anchor is the $10.5B Gartner DaaS number. That gap between the rhetoric and the comparables is the whole evaluation. The signature here is highest on systems-engineering risk, lowest on consequence magnitude, which describes a competent VDI reframing, not a civilizational variable.

On consequence -- the only place I start -- the **OpenAI** (2019) check worked because the success-state at maximum ambition was cognition itself getting recapitalized. ProjectX's maximum-ambition success-state is "AWS WorkSpaces, but each app gets its own VM," which collapses no incumbent regime and moves no $15T variable. The dossier's own framing -- "one cursor, one keyboard, one hardware, one app in focus" as the problem -- is a UX critique, not a labor-displacement thesis. When McKinsey predicted under 1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 and the real number was 109M, the miss was a category that did not yet exist. ProjectX is not naming a category that does not yet exist; it is naming a session-architecture choice inside a $9.82B category Research Nester already measures at 19.4% CAGR. That is the **Range Fuels** mistake inverted -- not "capital plan understates the science," but "consequence plan understates the world."

The technical-risk ladder is where ProjectX gets the most credit, and it still does not load the way **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) loaded when I led the $115M Series A into a magnet-physics bet no consensus VC would touch. Per-app GPU isolation with seconds cold-start is real systems work -- Bishal Karmakar's stated focus on "systems, streaming, cloud infra" is the right founder for it -- but it is virtualization engineering, not a physics miracle, and the failure mode is unit economics, not thermodynamics. The dossier flags this exactly: "each app in its own independent computer -- own GPU" implies a per-session GPU cost the company has published no density numbers against. DigitalOcean already paid $111M cash for Paperspace in July 2023; the hyperscalers will not need to invent new science to copy this, they will need to ship a billing SKU.

On founder defiance of expert consensus -- the dimension that flagged **Physical Intelligence** (2024) when Levine and Finn publicly bet against teleoperation orthodoxy -- ProjectX is silent. Adhikary's public footprint is a 2024 World Trade Center Innovation Award at IIT Bombay Eureka! and the Tiger Launch student competition at Princeton; Karmakar has Stanford ASES and Draper U affiliations. None of the three has staked a public, contrarian, falsifiable position in operating-system design or agent infrastructure. The Berkeley 28,000-prediction study tells me to discount expert validation; it tells me nothing favorable about founders with no public contrarian thesis at all. If you cannot articulate what conventional wisdom you are betting against, you are extrapolating the past.

The learning-rate evidence is the one place ProjectX has a genuine, if ambiguous, signal -- the 2022 incorporation listed Adhikary with Ronit Banerjee and Suvrakamal Das, and the YC P26 cap table lists Adhikary with Karmakar and Majumder. Co-founder turnover between incorporation and YC is either a pivot story or an instability story, and the dossier has no public explanation either way. If I imagine the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot version of this -- a documented thesis change with stated reasoning -- this would be a 12. With no narrative attached, it is a 7, the absent-evidence default with a modest lift for Adhikary having self-funded the company through Woldix consulting revenue. That is genuine entrepreneurial iteration, but it is small-business iteration, not science-thesis iteration.

Capital profile: $704K raised across two rounds since December 2023 tells me ProjectX is not asking for a Khosla-scale check and the roadmap as written would not absorb one. The GPU economics could eventually require real depth if the per-app architecture scales, but at pre-seed the company would be over-capitalized by a $5M check, let alone a $50M one. This is the **DoorDash** (2013) capital shape without the **DoorDash** consequence shape, which is the wrong half of that exception to inherit.

My 36-month forecast: ProjectX is acqui-hired by a DaaS or GPU-cloud incumbent following the Paperspace-to-DigitalOcean pattern at sub-$30M, or pivots into an agent-sandbox API competing with E2B and Modal at sub-$2M ARR. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- a real systems team building real infrastructure inside a category whose consequence ceiling caps the outcome. Hyperscalers and the GPU-cloud roll-ups buy this kind of team; they do not let it compound into a category-definer because there is no category to define.

The bull case that stings is the agent-sandbox repositioning. Anthropic's computer use, OpenAI's Operator, and the broader 2025-2026 computer-use-agent wave create genuine demand for isolated full-OS environments at per-agent granularity, and ProjectX's per-app VM architecture is closer to that shape than Kasm or Neverinstall. If Adhikary and Karmakar reposition from "OS for humans and agents" to "sandbox infrastructure for autonomous agents," the TAM rerates against agent volume, not desktop seats -- and the **Cognition** (2024) thesis says agent volume is where the $15T moves. The bear-case rebuttal is in the dossier: ProjectX has 14 Twitter followers since October 2022, no Product Hunt launch, no Chrome extension, and no public GitHub footprint across any of the three founders. E2B and Modal won that developer narrative with code in public; you do not sell agent infrastructure to AI engineers from a stealth posture with zero open-source surface. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: ProjectX ships a public agent-sandbox SDK with documented adoption by a named frontier-lab agent product within twelve months. Absent that, the acqui-hire outcome is the modal one and I pass.
