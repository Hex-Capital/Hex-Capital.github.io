# Perceptron ML -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Perceptron ML is the dimensional signature I see most often in S26 dossiers and almost never write checks against: two technically-credible brothers (Michael ex-NVIDIA AI Research; Peyton ex-PMARC/NASA-supported hardware) building a vertical workflow seam-filler in legal AI, a category my *AI Market Clarity* piece (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) explicitly lists in the "winners declared" column with Harvey on top. The highest dimension here is real founder shipping evidence; the lowest is index position. That pairing has a name in my portfolio history -- it's the Harvey-clone-at-consensus-price profile I told myself I wouldn't pay for.

On founder shipping, the load-bearing comp is **Perplexity** (2022) -- Aravind would ping me on LinkedIn and show up a week later with the thing built (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). Perceptron ML has the artifact half of that pattern: Michael Marcotte shipped production AI agents at NVIDIA for hardware debugging and formal verification (YC page), and Peyton Marcotte actually built astronaut exercise equipment that NASA, Brown, and the 1517 Fund put real money behind. That's not pedigree-only promotion -- both Marcottes have shipped artifacts independent of a big-co badge. Where Perceptron ML diverges from the Perplexity rung is the *rhythm* evidence: no public GitHub for either founder, no Twitter, no Product Hunt launch, no shipped demo I can poke at because perceptronml.com returned HTTP 403 and gates everything behind a demo form. I believe they *can* ship; I have no evidence they ship between conversations.

The Lyft Series C pass (20VC interview) is the failure mechanism I keep coming back to on index position -- #2 in a winner-take-all-or-oligopoly market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and no amount of execution closes that gap. Perceptron ML's dossier lights that pattern up. Harvey ran $3B?$5B?$8B in months (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) and is the named "current leader" in legal AI per my own essay. EvenUp sits at $385M raised and $2B post (lawnext.com, Oct 2025). Eve is at $164M and a $1B mark (prnewswire.com, Sept 2025). Darrow ($60M) owns the signal-detection half of Perceptron ML's loop; FirmPilot ($27.25M, prnewswire.com Feb 2026) owns the campaign half. Perceptron ML's pitch is that the *seam* between detect and act is the defensible position -- but that's a feature gap a $385M EvenUp can close in a quarter, not an index position on a compounding category.

Market re-opening: the 19%?79% law firm AI adoption jump from 2023 to 2024 (MyCase) is a real catalyst, but it's the catalyst that *Harvey already harvested*. The compounding mechanism over 18 months works against Perceptron ML, not for it -- every month of that 79% adoption curve further entrenches the incumbent that the buyer's general counsel already heard of. This isn't defense-post-Ukraine where the catalyst opened a window peers were ignoring; it's legal-AI-post-Harvey where the window opened, a leader was anointed, and Perceptron ML is showing up to a room that already has a vendor on the whiteboard.

Revenue velocity: for the score to flip a tier on this dimension, Perceptron ML would need to disclose month-over-month revenue compounding north of 20% with named plaintiff-firm logos in the last 90 days -- the Harvey "just working" signature (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). The dossier instead shows "No public data found," waitlist gating, and HTTP 403 on the marketing site. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is trivially easy to disclose -- and Perceptron ML is two months out of YC S26 demo day with a 478-LinkedIn-follower footprint and zero customer count. That's the silence pattern.

Single-miracle: comparing to **Decagon** (2023), the miracle there was one -- LLMs handle Tier-1 customer support accurately -- and the rest was GTM and integration execution into a wide-open category. Perceptron ML's loop requires at minimum three independent miracles: reliable cross-domain signal ingestion (filings, news, listings), automated outbound that converts through a human-approval bottleneck the company itself flags ("under human approval," YC description), and TCPA/state-bar-compliant solicitation across 50 jurisdictions. The 4-vertical sprawl across law, construction, investors, and trading desks with a 2-person team compounds the miracle count further -- not a single-miracle architecture.

36-month forecast: Perceptron ML gets acqui-hired by FirmPilot or Darrow for sub-$30M as a feature bolt-on, or pivots out of legal entirely into construction RFP bidding where the incumbent density is lower. The mechanism is the index-position failure -- a seam-filler in a winner-declared category with no distribution wedge gets absorbed by whichever well-capitalized neighbor wants the feature first, and the Marcottes' NVIDIA-grade engineering becomes the acquisition rationale.

The strongest bull case stings: plaintiff/contingency economics reward *speed* of client acquisition more than almost any market in professional services, FirmPilot is a content-marketing engine and Darrow is an enterprise insights product, and neither has structural incentive to descend into the messy outbound-campaign-automation seam Perceptron ML occupies -- it's beneath FirmPilot's price point and outside Darrow's enterprise sales motion. Michael Marcotte's production-agent background is a real wedge in a category where most legal-AI founders are lawyers who learned LangChain. If Perceptron ML lands two Morgan & Morgan-tier contingency firms in the next 90 days, the contingency-fee economics could produce a $5M ARR run-rate visible by Demo Day 2027. The dossier evidence that wins anyway: AI Market Clarity already named the seat, and my Lyft pass was right on framework even though it was wrong on outcome -- I will not pay consensus seed pricing for the #3 entrant in a category whose #1 is doing $50M+ ARR. **Verifiable flip condition**: three signed paid contracts with AmLaw-200 or top-20 contingency firms disclosed within 90 days, with month-over-month outbound-driven matter-acquisition data attached. Absent that, Perceptron ML is a pass.
