# Tenet Industries -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Hugo Frisk and his two KTH co-founders are running a capital-intensive, dual-use-export, ITAR-friction-arbitrage play -- exactly the lane that produced **Anduril** (2017) and **Crusoe** (~2018) -- but Tenet Industries arrives in a wedge where **Helsing** already closed $695M and **Anduril** is shipping. Tenet Industries is thesis-correct on the unsexy axis I fund hardest and thesis-late on the "find them before it becomes consensus" axis I fund hardest. That pairing is the entire evaluation: the right kind of company, possibly the wrong moment.

**Anduril** (2017) is the load-bearing analogy on the capital-intensity ladder, and Tenet Industries belongs in that conversation -- strike drones, automated manufacturing, sovereign-European procurement carve-out, dual-use ISP licensing exposure. The mechanism that worked for Palmer Luckey is that defense hardware compounds because the regulatory moat thickens the longer you operate inside it; Tenet Industries' part-count reduction from 30 to 14 using injection molding and plastic casting is a real manufacturing posture, not a deck claim, and Emil Falk's prior work at Falks Metall AB is the kind of metals-and-fab lineage that lets a 21-year-old actually walk a shop floor. Where Tenet Industries diverges from the Luckey template: Palmer shipped Oculus to a $2B exit before pivoting to defense. Hugo Frisk's LinkedIn says "Mass production." That is a posture, not a prior product.

**HQ Trivia** (2017) is the failure mechanism I watch for on co-founder governance: Yusupov and Kroll without a tiebreaker authority structure, and the company implodes when the partnership cracks. Tenet Industries names Hugo Frisk as CEO, which clears the bar **HQ Trivia** failed, but the dossier shows three KTH/SSE student co-founders with no documented operating agreement, no founder vesting, no exit-conflict protocol -- the same silence I read in **GameCrush** (~2011) before the activists stripped product DNA. Three twenty-something students who met in school have not yet had the conversation about what happens when one of them wants to take a $30M Helsing acquihire and the others want to keep going. That conversation always happens; the question is whether it happens before or after the Series A.

The Crusoe trifecta -- "Biz, Tiz, Rizz" -- runs differently on Tenet Industries because Emil Falk's Falks Metall AB and WindowGlass Recycling Sweden AB lineage is the closest dossier evidence of a founder being *of* the place rather than *visiting* it; metals fabrication is family-business adjacent in Sweden in a way Stanford CS is not in Palo Alto. But Chase Lochmiller at Crusoe (~2018) had spent years in quant finance adjacent to oil-and-gas operators before he wrote the Bitcoin-mining-on-flares thesis. Tenet Industries' founders are students who started ~5 months before the April 2026 article -- that is the analytical-founder window, not the lived-the-problem window, and the absence of a military, Saab, or Ukrainian-defense biography in the founding team is the gap that separates Tenet Industries from the **Anduril**-Luckey origin pattern.

The "magically weird" filter is where Tenet Industries scores lowest, and the counterfactual is sharp: if **Helsing** had not raised $695M in June 2024, if **Shield AI** were not at $12.7B in March 2026, if **Mach Industries** did not already have a Pentagon development contract, Tenet Industries at ~3,000 SEK per drone would be the magically weird European attritable-drone bet I write. The consensus has converged on this wedge in the eighteen months before Tenet Industries' YC batch, and being the cheapest entrant in a category three multi-billion-dollar incumbents already occupy is not the **Crusoe** profile -- it is the inverse. Tenet Industries is what the wedge looks like *after* it became consensus.

Physical verifiability is the dimension where I would normally walk into a Stockholm hangar and hold the Midsummer drone the way I rode an **Uber** (2010) before writing the check. Tenet Industries has two named products on the website and a 14-part bill-of-materials story, which is more tactile than the **consensus YC AI darling** I pass on -- but a strike drone is not an artifact I can install on my phone, photograph at a Four Seasons caf?, or hand to a friend the way **Flock Safety** (~2018) cameras eventually ended up on suburban poles. Export controls mean the only people touching this product are Swedish FMV procurement officers and Ukrainian operators, neither of whom is in my sourcing perimeter.

My 36-month forecast: Tenet Industries either gets acqui-hired into **Helsing** or a Swedish prime like Saab for under $50M by mid-2029, or runs out of runway after **Helsing**'s HX-2 and **Anduril**'s European push close out the Swedish and Ukrainian procurement slots Tenet Industries is currently "in discussions" about. The mechanism is procurement-relationship lead time, not technical capability -- defense buyers do not switch from a $695M-funded incumbent to three KTH students on cost-per-unit alone when attritable munitions also require sustainment, software updates, and supply-chain auditing.

The bull case that stings: Ukraine's FPV consumption rate already exceeds Western production, attritable economics genuinely favor the cheapest fielded drone, and Emil Falk's metals-fab lineage is exactly the outsider-trajectory founder profile -- non-Stanford, non-McKinsey, family-manufacturing background -- that **Hustle Fund** profiled me as preferring. A KTH student team that can hit 3,000 SEK with injection molding while **Anduril** prices at premium tier is the **HandUp** (~2014) override pattern: a founder biography I should weight over commercial-defensibility math. The dossier evidence that wins for me anyway is the $125K-for-7% stage-one YC structure with no signed LOI from FMV, no Ukrainian MOD contract, and no disclosed production volume -- the three pieces of physical-world verification that would have flipped this to an invest. **One verifiable condition flips my verdict:** a signed contract of $3M+ with FMV, the Ukrainian MOD, or a named NATO buyer disclosed before Tenet Industries' next round.
