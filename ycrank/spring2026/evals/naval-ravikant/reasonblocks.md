# ReasonBlocks -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

ReasonBlocks is a leverage-dense, knowledge-thin bet — Sajeev Magesh and Rohan Vij have built an AI-native runtime that two engineers ship into Series B customers, but neither founder arrives with the years-deep specific knowledge of production agent infrastructure that would make ReasonBlocks's core insight non-replicable. The 42% accuracy gain on SWE-bench Pro is real executed work; the question is whether it's the kind of work LangChain or Braintrust can replicate in a quarter once they decide to.

ReasonBlocks's strongest dimension is permissionless leverage. The high rung here is Twitter — Evan Williams' platform scaled to hundreds of millions on pure code-and-media leverage with no marginal cost per user. The low rung is Wish, which had marketplace leverage but no specific knowledge underneath and collapsed 95% from IPO. ReasonBlocks sits closer to Twitter than to Wish on architecture alone: a runtime that "plugs into your existing agent stack in minutes" is pure software leverage, AI-native, with the per-customer "private reasoning library that grows with every run" creating the data-flywheel structure I look for. It's not Twitter-level — ReasonBlocks rents foundation-model compute and lives downstream of LLM providers — but the leverage shape is correct.

ReasonBlocks's weakest dimension is specific knowledge productization, and this is where the bet wobbles. The high rung is Perplexity — Aravind Srinivas spent years at Google Brain and OpenAI building the exact systems that make AI search work, and the company IS that knowledge productized. The low rung is the AI-wrapper archetype I pass on every batch: two operators who identified an opportunity rather than founders who couldn't stop building. ReasonBlocks sits closer to the wrapper end. Magesh's *Scientific Reports* paper on ML-and-poverty and Vij's molecular-dynamics work at UC Davis are credible applied-AI signals, but neither founder has years inside production agent runtimes. If Magesh and Vij disappeared tomorrow, the SWE-bench Pro results would survive in the codebase, but the company would become a generic infra play in a sector with five funded incumbents.

ReasonBlocks's principal authenticity sits between the Notion founders (mid rung — principals who rebuilt from scratch after the first version failed) and the AI-wrapper MBAs (low rung — chose the idea because it was fundable). The 11-years-since-2nd-grade co-founder relationship is the strongest signal in the dossier — it predates any employer or university and answers the principal-agent problem between co-founders that I flag as a red flag when teams meet at startup weekends. But there's no evidence Magesh or Vij were running production agents and got burned by budget cap-hits. They didn't live inside the problem; they identified it.

ReasonBlocks's team-to-value leverage sits between Midjourney (high rung — 163 people, $500M revenue) and Notion (mid rung — small team for a long time). Two engineers reportedly "live with top Series B companies" is the exact density signal I want, and it's why I came back to this dossier twice. The shrinking-company thesis is satisfied. But customer-facing runtime infrastructure with "Hiring: False" is also where the leverage breaks down operationally — you cannot run reliability for AI-native Series B companies with two people indefinitely. On access democratization, ReasonBlocks sits closer to the low rung than to AngelList or CoinList. ReasonBlocks doesn't remove a gatekeeper between a previously-excluded population and a restricted resource; it makes existing builders' agents cheaper. That's consumer surplus for engineers, not democratization.

Forecast: in 36 months ReasonBlocks is acquired by Braintrust, LangChain, or ClickHouse (which already owns Langfuse) for $20-50M as a feature acquisition, or it's dead. The mechanism is the leverage-knowledge mismatch — incumbents own the trace data that feeds any reasoning library, and they ship runtime intervention as a SKU on top of distribution Magesh and Vij cannot match in two years with two engineers.

The bull case stings: ReasonBlocks's per-customer reasoning library compounds with every run, and a 90% repeat-usage claim — if true — means the data flywheel is already turning. The Notion outcome started this way; small team, no founder pedigree advantage, but the product compounded faster than incumbents could react. The dismantling fact is that LangSmith, Braintrust, and Langfuse already sit in the trace path. They have the inputs to build a reasoning library natively; ReasonBlocks must out-compound trusted incumbents with their own customers. Without specific knowledge that would take competitors years to acquire, ReasonBlocks is racing on execution speed — labor leverage dressed as code leverage. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a second benchmark beyond SWE-bench Pro showing runtime gains generalize to non-coding agents (sales, research, support), plus a publicly named Series B customer attesting to the 90% repeat usage. Until then, neutral.
