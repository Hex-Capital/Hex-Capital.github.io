# Hop Aero -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Hop Aero is a domain-deep founder team fighting on somebody else's water -- Matija Milenovic's porkchop heritage and Jacob Balaj's Falcon 9/Heavy integration scars are real, but the regatta they've entered is the one where SpaceX already has the $102M AFRL P2PD prime and Stoke has $990M in the bank. The signature is high founder-fit, low sleeve-fit. That's an analytical profile I respect on the resume and don't know how to underwrite from the cap table.

Milenovic at Hop Aero rhymes with my own arc at Drop.io in 2007 on exactly one axis: domain operator who has shipped the primitive before -- porkchop's payload flew on SpaceX Transporter-3, and Balaj integrated and tested Falcon 9 and Heavy boosters at the actual prime. That is the high rung on Criterion 3 and Hop Aero genuinely sits close to it on resume depth. Where Hop Aero diverges from the Drop.io analogy is the missionary-temperament artifact set: no public writing by either founder on why current airlift fails in the Indo-Pacific island chain problem, no Twitter footprint, no opinionated essay on why sub-orbital sub-Starship is the right form factor. Writing is thinking, and I can't find the thinking. The dossier gives me operator credentials without the contrarian belief I'd normally pre-read before the meeting.

The canonical "we'll be the OpenAI of X" pitch is my floor on Criterion 5 because it pays consensus pricing for commodity exposure in a sleeve where well-funded incumbents will outbid me on the next tack. Hop Aero is not that pitch -- but the failure mechanism still rhymes. Defense hardware in 2026 is what AI infra was in 2024: a sleeve where the consensus narrative ("rocket cargo is a venture moment, DoD is rerating, American Dynamism is hot") prices every Series A above what my entry math survives, and where SpaceX Starship, Inversion's $54M Series A, Stoke's $990M war chest, and Varda's $60M STRATFI are the well-funded incumbents who absorb the program-of-record dollars. Slow's portfolio has crypto sleeves, DTC sleeves, prescription-rail health sleeves, creator sleeves -- no defense-hardware sleeve I can point to. Hop Aero asks me to write the first defense check on a thesis I haven't built conviction on, against competitors with 18x to 330x the capital.

On Criterion 1, the mechanism over 18 months is what kills me on Hop Aero: $3M pre-seed buys roughly 12-18 months of six-person hardware burn in Los Angeles, after which Hop Aero will need a $15-25M Series A to fund a first sub-scale flight test, and that round will be priced into the current defense-tech rerate -- meaning Lunar Ventures, Outsized, Silicon Roundabout and Masia got in at a price that compounds against me on the next tack, not for me. The round itself isn't hot today, but the next one will be, and the price you pay at seed is set by the price you'll see at A.

On Criterion 2, the counterfactual is sharp and specific: for Hop Aero's capital structure to flip from drainage to venture-shaped, I would need a per-launch royalty instrument against future USTRANSCOM/AFRL P2PD task orders -- non-dilutive, milestone-gated, structured against the actual asset (delivered ton-miles) rather than equity in a holdco that will be diluted across five rounds. Ro is my override precedent because regulatory complexity in 50-state prescription rails was itself the moat; Hop Aero has the same shape (ITAR, FAA Part 450, range agreements) but no disclosed structural answer to why the $3M SAFE doesn't get crushed by Stoke's $990M when Stoke pivots one R&D budget line into sub-orbital cargo.

On Criterion 4, the single-anchor parallel is Sublime Security: enterprise security to a non-consumer buyer, where I never needed the founder to be a brand because the customer is procurement, not community. Hop Aero sells to DoD program offices. The absence of founder Twitter, LinkedIn followers, or GitHub repos is structurally fine for the GTM -- this is one dimension where the absent-evidence default isn't really a knock against the company, just against my sleeve.

My 36-month forecast on Hop Aero: by Q3 2029, Hop Aero is either acqui-hired by Anduril or Stoke Space for $30-80M as a propulsion-plus-launch-integration team tuck-in, OR has narrowed to a sub-orbital component supplier (reusable upper-stage avionics, precision-landing GNC) for a larger prime under a $5-15M ARR services line, with no fielded full-stack cargo vehicle yet flown. The mechanism is the signature: founders this strong don't fail by quitting, they fail by getting absorbed when the capital gap to the next demonstration becomes uncloseable at a venture-rational price.

The bull case that stings: this is Ro. Regulatory complexity (ITAR, FAA Part 450, DoD security accreditation) is the moat, capital intensity is the feature not the bug, USTRANSCOM CRADAs and the FY25 P2PD line are a genuine venture moment, and Milenovic-plus-Balaj is the rare propulsion-plus-integration pairing that defense primes cannot internally clone. American Dynamism funds will follow at A and underwrite the capital intensity Slow can't. Where the bull case loses to the dossier: Ro had owned consumer distribution and a margin-rich prescription pull-through; Hop Aero has $1.25M of SBIR concentration risk, a competitor set with 18-330x more capital, and a Slow portfolio that doesn't have the defense-LP base to keep writing into a multi-round hardware story. One flip condition I would actually pay attention to: a disclosed prime task order from USTRANSCOM or AFRL above $10M with milestone-gated non-dilutive draws, or a per-launch royalty instrument that converts Hop Aero's cap-table exposure into a structured asset I can price directly -- then we talk.
