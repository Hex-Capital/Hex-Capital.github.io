# Foresight -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Foresight is a pivoted-hacker bet on a crowded AI-wrapper category, and the analytical signature is the gap between Eytan Rozenblum's documented re-up after a bad 2023 YC cycle (his highest axis) and the complete absence of schlep in what Foresight actually does (its lowest). Synthetic consumer panels are pure software run by two people in New York against Simile's $100M Series A and Aaru's $1B headline -- there is no compliance grind, no offline ops, no manual integration that the dossier names. That pairing -- a founder who came back, building something with no moat -- is the shape of a company that scores on grit and loses on category physics.

Brian Armstrong at **Coinbase** (2012) is the HIGH rung on animal resourcefulness for me, because after I rejected him on paper he hand-built an Android Bitcoin wallet and came back -- that is the post-rejection artifact that flipped the check. Foresight's analogue is thinner but real: Eytan Rozenblum did one YC cycle in 2023 with PrompTessor / Reverse Prompt, called it "likely the worst year of my life," barely raised, pivoted through Fluentive AI, and came back to YC P26 with Antoine Bertrand and a paying customer in Caudalie running 15 tests per week. That is a Kan/Shear pattern -- prior YC failure as compounding hustle rather than disqualifying tell. But where Armstrong's wallet was a new artifact against my explicit "no," Eytan's return is a pivot in search of a market, and Foresight's dossier names exactly one customer to anchor the comeback story.

The pass example I keep returning to is the MBA AI-wrapper team with a glossy deck and a $50B TAM slide, and the failure mechanism there is that the work itself contains no schlep -- nothing prevents the next two people with API access from building the same thing. Foresight sits uncomfortably close to that mechanism: synthetic consumer simulation is exactly the work Simile raised $100M for in February 2026 and Aaru raised ~$50M for in December 2025, and the foresight.tt feature list (copy, conjoint, price elasticity, brand lift) is the same packaged-test surface Evidenza and Synthetic Users already ship. There is no regulatory exposure, no physical-world ops, no compliance grind in the Foresight dossier -- the vertical-CPG focus is a positioning choice, not a schlep. Quantilope's 300+ enterprise CPG relationships including Nestle and Estée Lauder are the switching-cost wall, and Foresight has named one customer against it.

On hacker genealogy, the compounding question over 18 months is whether Eytan's Hugging Face presence at @EytanRzb and the PrompTessor codebase ripen into a technical taste advantage that shows up in synthetic-panel calibration accuracy, or whether they remain the resume of a prompt-engineering tool that didn't work. Antoine Bertrand's profile is publicly empty -- no GitHub, no shipped artifact named in the dossier -- which means Foresight's technical lineage is effectively one-and-a-half hackers, not two, and the calibration moat I'd want to see (predicted-vs-real outcome loops) requires more engineering depth than a pair this thin tends to compound.

On organic founder-pain, the counterfactual that would flip Foresight a tier is straightforward: if either founder had spent two years inside a Unilever or L'Oréal insights team being personally tortured by 8-week Quantilope panel cycles, the dossier would read like Drew Houston and his USB stick. It does not. Eytan's prior product PrompTessor was an LLM-tooling play; Antoine's listed history is Fluentive AI, the immediate predecessor entity. Foresight reads as a team that pivoted toward a market with venture momentum (Simile, Aaru) rather than a team that lived inside CPG insights and built the tool they wished existed -- which is the inverse of the test from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012).

The single-anchor parallel on cofounder configuration is the Collison brothers at **Stripe** (2010): two technical founders with years of prior building together, complementary, durable. Foresight has two cofounders who worked together at Fluentive AI before pivoting -- a real prior-relationship signal, not a hackathon pairing -- and that is the cleanest positive in the dossier. It is also worth exactly ten points maximum, and cofounder chemistry has never rescued a category problem.

My 36-month forecast for Foresight: acquihired or wound down by mid-2029 at sub-$15M, most likely absorbed by Quantilope or YouGov as a synthetic-panel feature team, with ARR never crossing $2M. The mechanism is straightforward and matches the signature: a pure-software AI wrapper in a vertical where two competitors raised >$150M in the four months before Foresight launched gets compressed on price and outspent on enterprise sales before its calibration loop produces a defensible accuracy delta, and the hybrid incumbent (Quantilope, with 300+ CPG logos) buys the team rather than fights them.

The strongest bull case is the Kiko → Twitch precedent: Eytan Rozenblum is exactly the re-fund profile I built a thesis around, Caudalie's 15-tests-per-week claim is real per-customer pull, and the synthetic-research category went from speculative to $1B-headline-valuation in eighteen months -- meaning the tailwind that lifted Aaru can lift Foresight if the CPG vertical fragments away from horizontal players. That case stings because the Known Exception is real. What dismantles it is the comparison to Twitch: Justin Kan came back with Justin.tv, a consumer product millions of people used; Eytan came back with another B2B tool in a category three better-funded competitors already occupy. The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict: if Foresight names five Quantilope-tier CPG customers (Nestle / Estée Lauder / Kraft Heinz scale) under paid contracts within twelve months of YC launch, the calibration-data moat thesis becomes real and I write the check.
