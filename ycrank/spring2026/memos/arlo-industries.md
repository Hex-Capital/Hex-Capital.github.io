## The Idea

**Problem:** Legacy ground-based radar systems are built to defend single, centralised assets and are challenged by stealthy drones and drone swarms (company description, YC page). The company's stated framing references the Iron Dome and Shahed drone threats observed by the founder in Israel (company description, YC page).

**Approach:** A decentralised, passive aerial sensing mesh of networked sensors that triangulate aerial targets without actively emitting (company description, YC page). Per the company description: "the cost of adding sensors grows linearly, but the tracking accuracy increases exponentially" (YC page).

**Differentiation:** Distinguishes itself from active radar incumbents on (a) passivity (no emissions to geolocate) and (b) mesh scaling economics versus single-site radar (company description, YC page). Anduril's Lattice is also a "mesh networking platform that fuses data and inputs from sensors and effectors" (Tectonic Defense, 2026 via search snippet); Fortem DroneHunter and Elbit C-UAS are active-radar/effector platforms (Inside Unmanned Systems, 2026 via search snippet).

**Business Model:** No pricing page or public pricing tier was found on arlo1.com (WebFetch returned no detail). [Inferred]: Defense procurement via direct sales contracts to militaries / MoDs, given founder's stated demos to Ukrainian military and operators in Europe/USA (company description).

**TAM/SAM:** Anti-drone market $2.39B (2025) → $2.96B (2026) at 23.6% CAGR (Research and Markets, 2026 via search snippet). Alternate estimate $3.88B (2026) → $16.45B (2034) (Fortune Business Insights via search snippet). Counter-drone CAGR 27.9% (market.us via search snippet). No public SAM figure isolated for passive-radar mesh sub-segment.

**GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Direct sales to allied defense ministries and frontline operators, starting with Ukraine and select European/US customers based on the founder's stated frontline demos (company description, YC page). No public contract awards found.

## Key Risks

**Solo named founder for a hardware/defense build:** YC lists team size 2 but only Deo Arlo is publicly named (YC page). Hardware-software defense systems typically require deep engineering bench; the absence of a publicly visible CTO/co-founder is a hiring and execution risk.

**Incumbent platform substitution (Anduril Lattice):** Anduril already markets a sensor-fusion mesh and just won a 10-year, $20B ceiling Army C-UAS framework (DroneXL, March 2026 via search snippet). Customers may default to Lattice for any mesh-style integration; Arlo's wedge must be a sensing modality Anduril does not already cover.

**Technical feasibility of passive mesh at required accuracy:** Company asserts tracking accuracy "increases exponentially" with linearly added sensors (YC page); no published benchmarks, third-party test data, or peer-reviewed evidence of detection/track performance against stealthy drones or missiles were found.

**ITAR / export-control and sales-cycle friction:** [Inferred]: Selling to Ukrainian, European, and U.S. operators from a U.S.-incorporated entity implies ITAR/EAR compliance overhead and multi-year procurement cycles, which can stretch runway. No public licenses or contracts disclosed.

**Web and PR footprint near-zero at time of research:** arlo1.com returned no extractable content via WebFetch; no funding announcement, press article, or analyst coverage was found, limiting independent verification of all founder-stated claims (Israeli defense tenure, Ukrainian frontline demos).
