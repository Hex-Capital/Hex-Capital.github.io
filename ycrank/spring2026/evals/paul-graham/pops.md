# Pops -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Alon Zuman has shipped Reeroll, Babble, Voxel, Pragma, ByteDate, Mosaic, and a volunteer-management app that 500+ people used to support Holocaust survivors -- that catalog is the most interesting fact in the Pops dossier, and it's also why I keep circling back to the same problem: he is shipping all of it alone, into a category (AI-generated games) where Rosebud has $9.2M and Jabali has $5M and Playabl and CodeWisp are running the same race from the YC bench. Pops is a resourceful builder dropped into a consensus market. That pairing is the analytical signature, and it's what the rest of this evaluation is really about.

The founder-resourcefulness ladder is where Pops scores best, so let me put it on the rungs honestly. The HIGH rung is Airbnb -- Brian and Joe selling $40 cereal boxes to keep the lights on, the canonical example of the animal quality. The MID rung is Replit -- Amjad shipping a usable in-browser IDE before anyone thought it mattered. The LOW rung is the McKinsey-consultant pass example -- the people who confuse a 50-customer interview deck for evidence of grit. Zuman sits between MID and HIGH: the eight or nine prototypes on alonzuman.com aren't side-project theater, they're the same person figuring out consumer AI media over and over and shipping each iteration to the App Store or the open web. Pops at v1.0.144 with an update "1 day ago" three months after launch is consistent with that pattern. He has the running back quality on the evidence available -- but he has it for one person.

The idea-that-repels ladder is where Pops collapses, and this is where I'd argue with myself hardest. Stripe is the HIGH rung -- PCI compliance and bank integrations were a schlep so painful that no one at the Collisons' tier wanted to touch them, which is exactly why the Collisons' willingness mattered. ClassDojo is the MID rung -- K-12 sales cycles repelled VCs but the schlep was institutional, not technical. The LOW rung is the consultant brainstorming session from my pass example -- ideas every audience nods at. Pops sits one half-rung above that LOW. AI-game-creation as a category does not repel anyone in 2026; the dossier names four funded competitors plus Spawn.co, and a TechCrunch trend piece could substitute the Pops description for any of them. The mobile-feed framing is real differentiation but it is not a schlep -- it is a positioning choice that Roblox, TikTok, or Snap can copy in a quarter.

Organic problem discovery (Dropbox HIGH rung -- Drew Houston's USB drive; Reddit MID rung -- the idea I gave them at dinner; consultants LOW rung) is where Pops gives me almost nothing to work with. The dossier contains no founder origin story, no specific frustration, no "I kept trying to make a game with friends and X kept failing." Zuman's pattern of consumer-AI-media prototypes suggests genuine personal interest, which is closer to MID than LOW, but I'm filling that in -- absence of a discovery narrative is mildly negative because resourceful founders generate notable anecdotes and tell them. Founder learning rate (Altman at Loopt HIGH; Patrick at Auctomatic→Stripe MID; flat-metrics LOW) places Pops between MID and HIGH on shipping velocity -- 1.0.144 in three months is a real iteration cadence -- but with only 8 ratings on the App Store there is no user-feedback loop visible to learn FROM, just commits visible to learn IN. Earnest builder identity (Watsi HIGH; Replit MID; raise-bragging LOW) actually scores well: the Holocaust-survivor volunteer app and the App Store commitment to "everything free, no premium tiers" are not the moves of a status-driven founder. Pops sits closer to MID-toward-HIGH on earnestness, which is the one place the dossier outperforms my prior.

Three years out, Pops is most likely shut down or acqui-hired by Roblox or Snap for under $20M, with Zuman moving on to his next solo prototype. The mechanism is straightforward: a solo founder cannot out-execute Rosebud and Jabali combined on creator tooling, cannot out-distribute Roblox on UGC, and cannot out-monetize anyone while committed to "no premium tiers" -- the three pressures converge before he can hire a team capable of relieving any one of them.

The bull case stings, so I'll say it carefully. Zuman is precisely the founder profile that produces a viral consumer breakout when one is going to happen -- prolific, mobile-native, working at Playground, fluent in the gen-AI-plus-media stack that has already produced one-shot playable games. The auto-clip-to-TikTok loop is the correct 2026 mechanic, and consumer-social winners in mobile gaming have been power-law outcomes (Wordle, Subway Surfers) where being early and viral mattered more than being well-capitalized. If Pops hits 5-7% weekly on remix-driven retention, the funded competitors look slow because they're building creator IDEs while Pops is building a feed. The Watsi exception even applies in spirit -- earnestness can override sector concerns. What dismantles the bull case for me is the solo-founder clause from "The 18 Mistakes" combined with the unstated pattern: Pops is not hackers-building-for-hackers, it is one hacker building for Gen-Z, and the consumer-social cases I've backed (ClassDojo, Reddit) all had two co-founders who could trade the wheel during the inevitable trough. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Zuman finds a technical co-founder before the next funded competitor ships an iOS app, and Pops shows weekly active retention above 30% on a cohort of 10K+ users. Until both, this is a pass.
