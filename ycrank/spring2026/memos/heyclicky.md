## The Idea

- **Problem:** Mac users currently switch between separate AI chat apps (ChatGPT, Claude desktop) and their working context, with no single agent that can both see the active screen and execute background tasks across apps like Notion, Gmail, Calendar, DaVinci Resolve, Figma, and After Effects (heyclicky.com).
- **Approach:** A native macOS app (Swift) sits next to the cursor, captures screen context, accepts voice commands, and on the "HeyClicky Agent" trigger spawns a background agent to build, research, or operate across connected tools (heyclicky.com; YC page).
- **Differentiation:** Cluely is positioned around meetings/interview "cheat" overlays (TechCrunch, Jun 2025); Granola is a meeting notetaker (TechCrunch, May 2025); Clicky is positioned as a general voice + screen-aware agent runner for any Mac workflow with native Mac app spawning (YC page; heyclicky.com).
- **Business Model:** No pricing disclosed on website; Mac download is free with Windows version on Tally waitlist (heyclicky.com). [Inferred]: Likely freemium with paid agent/usage tier given consumer positioning and per-call LLM costs.
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Founder's personal audience (Farza Majeed, 108.6K followers @FarzaTV per twitterscore.io) plus open-source distribution via GitHub repo farzaa/clicky (GitHub).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Open-source repo farzaa/clicky has ~5.8K–6.1K stars across pages (GitHub, May 2026); founder's distribution channel (108.6K X followers, twitterscore.io) supplies launch reach.
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Cross-app integrations (Notion, Gmail, Calendar, Linear per founder description) plus per-user screen/voice history could create switching cost; unproven now because the product is roughly four weeks old (YC page).
- **Market structure:** No structural barrier identified at this stage; [Inferred]: Apple itself could integrate screen-aware agents into the OS (Apple Intelligence direction) creating platform-level substitution risk.
- **Commoditization risk:** The core repo is MIT-licensed and explicitly invited to be forked into companies by the founder ("Go forth into the wild and build the future of education and the future of AI interfaces" — @FarzaTV, X post 2041691382008705321); any team can clone the codebase.

## Key Risks

- **Open-source commoditization:** Founder open-sourced the full Swift app under MIT and publicly encouraged forks (@FarzaTV status 2041691382008705321), so a competing team can ship a near-identical product without R&D cost.
- **Platform substitution:** Apple Intelligence already ships system-level screen and app context on macOS; if Apple extends it to third-party app actions, the cursor-side agent layer becomes redundant ([Inferred] — no announcement cited).
- **Founder follow-through:** Buildspace shut down in August 2024 with the founder citing inability "to craft an updated direction that feels worth pursuing" despite $10M raised at $100M valuation (@FarzaTV status 1827030900456169849; therunway.ventures), creating a documented pattern of voluntary closure that investors will diligence.
- **Crowded incumbent-adjacent market:** Cluely raised $20.3M (TechCrunch Jun 2025) and Granola reached $1.5B valuation on $192M raised (Bloomberg Mar 2026) while pursuing overlapping screen-aware/voice AI use cases, raising the cost of paid acquisition and talent.
- **Name collision:** "Clicky" is also a long-standing web-analytics product at @clicky on X (x.com/clicky), which may dilute search and social discovery for the company.
