## The Idea

- **Problem:** Custom residential design today requires architects and weeks/months of iteration; founder's prior company Atmos found this was "an extremely operational business, kind of like a glamorized architecture firm" (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025).
- **Approach:** Drafted built a specialized multimodal generative model trained on real permitted house plans that lets users define footprints, room programs and square footage constraints to generate floor plans plus 3D exterior visuals in seconds (drafted.ai; company description).
- **Differentiation:** Versus Maket.ai (also AI floor plans) and Snaptrude (browser BIM tool), Drafted runs inference at "two-tenths of a penny per floor plan, compared to 13 cents for general-purpose AI" using its own trained model (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025).
- **Business Model:** Drafted's site advertises "Free AI House Plans with PDF & CAD Downloads" (drafted.ai, May 2026); earlier TechCrunch reporting cited "$1,000 and $2,000" per complete plan (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025), suggesting a freemium + paid export model.
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** Company states "120k users... through socials and word-of-mouth" generating 325k+ floor plans in the past month (YC company description, 2026); [Inferred]: consumer self-serve viral / social-led acquisition rather than enterprise sales.

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Proprietary model trained on real permitted house plans yields ~65x lower per-generation inference cost vs. general-purpose AI (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025); 85,000+ AI-created house plans now hosted on site (drafted.ai search snippet, 2026).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Cumulative dataset of user-generated + permitted plans plus CAD export integrations into downstream construction workflows could compound; unproven because product is <12 months old.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbent CAD vendors (Autodesk) face channel conflict selling a $1–2k consumer plan that bypasses their architect-licensed seat model, but no structural regulatory barrier identified.
- **Commoditization risk:** Maket.ai (Montreal, seed-stage) ships a directly comparable AI floor-plan generator (maket.ai; Crunchbase) and large model labs could fine-tune general models on plan corpora.

## Key Risks

- **Repeat-founder business-model risk:** Atmos shut down in part because the operational/services layer "never quite replaced the humans" and customers couldn't close after rates rose (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025); Drafted's pivot to pure-software AI generation is unproven for monetization at scale, with website currently offering free PDF/CAD downloads (drafted.ai).
- **Technical feasibility at CAD-grade fidelity:** Plans must export as "structured CAD files... ready to be sent to a structural engineer, with walls, windows, and doors grouped and sized to common standards" (drafted.ai search snippet); meeting permitting standards from generative output is technically unproven at scale and prior Atmos experience suggests human review was previously required.
- **Macro/rates exposure on end demand:** Atmos failed when "the Federal Reserve started jacking up interest rates, and suddenly clients who'd spent months designing their dream homes couldn't afford them anymore" (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025); Drafted's consumer demand is exposed to the same mortgage-rate dynamic.
- **Funding figure discrepancy:** Public TechCrunch reporting cites $1.65M at $35M post (Dec 23 2025), while YC company description cites $17.5M cumulative (2026); the larger figure has not been independently verified in press at time of research.
- **Direct competitor with similar product:** Maket.ai (maket.ai; Crunchbase) ships a comparable AI residential floor-plan generator and has been in market since 2019; differentiation rests on Drafted's proprietary trained model and cost-per-generation advantage (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025).
