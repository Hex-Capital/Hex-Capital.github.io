# Osmaura -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Osmaura is two recent MIT CS grads — Kali Abeje and Jity Woldemichael — selling a partner-facing BD opportunity surfacing layer into AmLaw firms, with zero disclosed revenue and a $23M Series B competitor (Nexl) that closed in October 2025 explicitly to fund "AI growth-platform development and acquisitions." That single pairing — founder shipping pedigree on the high end, no revenue curve on the low end, in a category that just got marked up by Tidemark for the incumbent — is Osmaura's analytical signature: a competent technical team launching into a sub-market where consensus capital has already chosen another horse. This is the Harvey-clone shape from my *AI Market Clarity* piece (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025), pointed at law firm BD instead of legal research.

Anduril (2022) is the template I run against any market-re-opening claim — Ukraine was a specific, datable, 24-month catalyst that broke open defense procurement, and Trae Stephens was uniquely positioned to harvest it. Osmaura's catalyst, per the dossier's own [Inferred] note, is "LLM-driven extraction over unstructured web, news, and CRM text" becoming cheap enough to link signals to partner practice areas. That's real — but it's the *generic* LLM catalyst, the one underwriting every YC S26 deck, not a specific re-opening of law-firm BD. Law firm partnership BD has been steady-state mediocre forever; Introhive ($99M raised over 8 rounds, latest June 2021) and Nexl (150+ law-firm customers) are already in-market with relationship intelligence. Osmaura is not entering a market peers misread as dead. They are entering a market peers correctly read as crowded and slow, with an LLM wrapper on top.

Harvey (2023) is the revenue-velocity portfolio anchor — "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) is what *just working* looks like, and the absence of that curve is the disqualifier I quote on Tim Ferriss Show #863. On Osmaura the dossier says "No public data found" against Revenue Signal, and the only traction artifact is "100+ partner customer-discovery interviews." Interviews are not revenue. Interviews are not even pilots. In an AI company at pre-seed in mid-2026, silence on growth is the loudest signal in the deck — if Osmaura had two AmLaw pilots signed in the last 90 days, founders@osmaura.com would have leaked them to YC's page. They didn't, because there aren't any yet.

Woldemichael's Meta infra work — reducing network patching from 6 hours to 2 minutes — plus Jane Street fellowship, DRW SWE internship, AppDev@MIT, and a GitHub at @Jity01 with 42 public repos, paired with Abeje's NLP research at MIT GOV/LAB on argument extraction, is a *real* technical bench for a 2-person pre-seed. Aravind at Perplexity (2022) shipped between every meeting we had; the Osmaura founders have shipped *inside structured employers*, which is a weaker signal than independent shipping artifacts but stronger than pure pedigree. The compounding mechanism over the next 18 months: if they actually ship product iterations weekly against the 100+ partners they've talked to, this score moves up; if they spend the next 18 months in customer-discovery-as-procrastination, it collapses. The dossier doesn't tell me which one, and there is no domain operator (legal BD, AmLaw partner relationships) on the founding team to compensate.

Stripe (2010) is an index on e-commerce because every merchant that comes online must touch payments rails. For Osmaura to be index-shaped, every AmLaw partner originating new matters would have to route through their surfacing layer. The dossier itself names the structural problem: "Product requires connecting to internal firm CRM such as InterAction or Nexl; vendors of those systems can restrict APIs or bundle competing features." That isn't index position — that's an app sitting downstream of two named incumbents who can squeeze the integration whenever they want, and one of whom (Nexl) just raised $23M to build the exact same feature surface. Osmaura must *win share against* the workflow owner, not ride the category. This is the inverse of the position I bought in Stripe.

The single-miracle test: would Osmaura ship if I granted only one capability? The dossier requires me to grant (1) signal-extraction accuracy good enough that partners trust the routing, AND (2) partner-level enterprise sales motion into AmLaw firms with no domain operator on the team, AND (3) durable CRM integration access against vendors with incentive to close it. That's at least two independent miracles, possibly three. Color Genomics (2015) worked because we isolated the miracle to *workflow* and bought commodity sequencing underneath. Osmaura has not isolated the miracle — they need accuracy *and* a sales motion they have not demonstrated *and* integration permission from competitors.

My 36-month forecast on Osmaura: acqui-hired by Nexl or Litera for under $15M, or quietly wound down, after plateauing below $500K ARR with one or two design-partner AmLaw firms that never expanded past the pilot. The mechanism is Nexl shipping the same opportunity-surfacing module on their $23M war chest into their 150+ existing customers before Osmaura can build distribution that doesn't depend on Nexl's API.

The bull case that should sting: the *partner-facing wedge* is genuinely different from CRM-replacement, and law firm partners hate Introhive's BD-team-centric posture. If Osmaura ships a tool partners actually open daily — not a dashboard for the BD team — they could leak into AmLaw via partner enthusiasm rather than procurement, the same bottoms-up motion that beat incumbents in horizontal SaaS. Abeje's MIT GOV/LAB argument-extraction research is genuinely closer to the legal-text problem than a generic NLP team. Counter: bottoms-up into AmLaw is a fantasy I've watched die a dozen times — partners don't install software, their assistants don't install software, and even Harvey had to go top-down through firm IT. Without a named partner-level design customer in the dossier — not "100+ interviews," an actual signed firm — this is Lyft-shaped: a worse-positioned #3 in a market where the #1 just got a growth round. **The verdict flips to Invest if Osmaura discloses, within 90 days, a signed paid pilot at an AmLaw 100 firm with month-over-month usage growth from a partner (not a BD team) seat.** Absent that, this is a pass on the template I've run since the Lyft Series C.
