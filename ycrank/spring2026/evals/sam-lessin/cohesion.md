# Cohesion -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

AlphaSense has $500M+ ARR, just acquired Tegus for $930M, and is out raising additional hundreds of millions according to Bloomberg as of March 2026. When AlphaSense's product team sees Cohesion's demo — AI agents autonomously monitoring earnings and X/Twitter posts for hedge fund analysts — they don't think "oh shit, we're disrupted." They think "fuck yeah, let's ship that as a feature by Q3." Cohesion is operating in the exact kill zone I talk about constantly: a software-only application layer built on commodity LLM APIs, targeting a workflow that the dominant incumbent with massive distribution can replicate by adding an agent mode to their existing platform. The dossier even notes that an open-source repo on GitHub (virattt/ai-hedge-fund) already demonstrates that basic investment research agents can be assembled from commodity APIs. When your core technology can be replicated by a weekend project on GitHub, you are not building a company — you are building a feature.

YC listed "AI-Native Hedge Funds" as an explicit Request for Startups category for Spring 2026. Cohesion shares the batch with Standard Signal, another AI hedge fund play. Trata came through YC W25 doing adjacent work. Hebbia raised $130M at a $700M valuation in July 2024. Over 35% of new hedge fund launches in 2025 branded themselves as AI-driven or AI-enhanced. This is the definition of consensus pricing — every sophisticated investor in the market is looking at AI-for-finance and bidding up the category. There is zero mispricing here. The upside is already baked into the price ten times over. When I backed Solana at four cents, every smart crypto person had written off non-Ethereum L1s as dead. Cohesion is the opposite dynamic: every smart fintech person thinks AI research tools for hedge funds is a great idea. That's exactly when I don't want to be buying.

Devon Krapcho spent 5+ years as a fundamental equity analyst at Long Path Partners covering software companies. Matt Munns built AI for investors at T. Rowe Price, a $1.5T+ AUM asset manager. Matthew McBrien built and maintained AWS Lambda, one of the largest compute platforms on earth. Let me be honest — this is a legitimately credentialed team with real domain expertise. Krapcho IS the target user. The bull case writes itself: a former analyst who lived the pain, paired with an ML engineer who built AI at a top-10 asset manager, already live with 10+ funds managing $10B+ combined AUM at pre-seed stage. That traction is real. If I were evaluating Cohesion purely on founder-market fit, I'd give them high marks. But my framework isn't about quality — it's about pricing. These are sane people with a sane idea, and sane-sane is boring. Nothing about "AI agents that monitor earnings transcripts for hedge fund analysts" makes anyone say "that's fucking crazy." It makes people say "obviously, someone should build that," which is exactly the problem — everyone already is.

Cohesion's "agentic teammate" framing — continuous autonomous monitoring versus AlphaSense's query-initiated search — is a real UX distinction. I'll grant that the shift from pull to push intelligence matters to busy analysts. But a UX innovation on top of commodity infrastructure is not a primitive. Venmo created peer-to-peer mobile payments as a behavior — "Venmo me" became a verb. Cohesion is not creating a new behavior; Cohesion is delivering an existing behavior (financial research monitoring) through a better interface (agents instead of search). Nobody is going to say "Cohesion me" as shorthand for a new category of human activity. The closest Known Exception would be Slack, where I paid consensus prices because workplace messaging was a genuine new primitive replacing email. But Cohesion isn't replacing anything fundamental — analysts will still use Bloomberg terminals, still read 10-Ks, still make investment decisions the same way. Cohesion just surfaces signals faster. That's incremental, not primitive.

Cohesion's structural durability is where the thesis collapses entirely. No physical-world operations. No protocol-level infrastructure. No network effects identified in the dossier. Data source access depends on X/Twitter API pricing (which has changed multiple times since 2023) and podcast feed availability — third-party dependencies Cohesion cannot control. The dossier explicitly flags that "the core technology (LLM agents monitoring public data sources) has low technical barriers." Meanwhile, AlphaSense is a $4B company actively expanding through acquisitions. When I seeded Metropolis, the thesis was that AI-powered parking required physical infrastructure that no software company could replicate — that moat is now worth ~$5B. Cohesion has no equivalent structural defense. Every earnings season, Cohesion's differentiation narrows as AlphaSense, Bloomberg, and a dozen well-funded startups ship agent features of their own.

The one verifiable condition that would flip me: if Cohesion demonstrated that its agent architecture generated proprietary, compounding data — not just monitoring public sources, but creating a feedback loop where analyst interactions with Cohesion's insights produce a dataset no competitor can replicate, and that dataset demonstrably improved signal quality over time in measurable alpha generation. Show me fund-level attribution data proving Cohesion's signals generated returns that AlphaSense's platform cannot match, with retention metrics proving analysts won't churn once exposed to it. That would be evidence of a real moat forming. But at pre-seed with "limited early trial access during earnings season" and no revenue data, that evidence doesn't exist. Cohesion has competent founders building a competent product in a category where competence isn't enough — the price already reflects the opportunity, the incumbents are coming, and there's no structural defense against commoditization. Pass.
