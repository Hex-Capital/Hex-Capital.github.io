On consequence, my ladder runs from **OpenAI** (2019) at the top -- civilizational AGI redirection -- through **DoorDash** (2013) in the middle as a real-economy reshape that is large but not civilizational, down to the GPT-4-sales-call-summarizer pass I described earlier as a 20-30% workflow nibble. It does not reach **OpenAI** or **Impossible Foods** (2011) altitude because Arlo Industries is a sensing layer in a kill chain Anduril already owns end-to-end -- the consequence is real, but bounded to the sensor BoM, not the full air-defense stack.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus -- the lowest rung -- my ladder runs from Sergey Levine and Chelsea Finn at **Physical Intelligence** (2024), publicly contrarian on robot foundation models, through Arvind Jain at **Glean** (a Known Exception where domain-expert credentials were overridden by capital scale), down to my pass-pattern of "I spent eight years at Salesforce, now I'm building Salesforce+AI." On technical/capital risk that repels consensus VCs, the ladder runs **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) at $115M Series A for tokamak physics, through **Physical Intelligence** (2024) for robot foundation models, down to standard SaaS. There is no fusion-class physics miracle here; the unverified claim that "tracking accuracy increases exponentially with linearly added sensors" is an engineering claim, not a thermodynamics one.

On founder learning rate, my ladder runs **Slash** (2024) at the top with the documented Kanye pivot that justified my $100M Series C, through founders who shipped and iterated, down to single-linear-plan founders. Deo Arlo moved from Technion to Makrverse (ex-CTO/co-founder) to Arlo Industries -- there is career motion but no documented thesis update or "we were wrong about X" moment surfaced in the dossier, so I apply my absent-evidence default. On required-capital match, Arlo Industries needs real hardware and ITAR/EAR money but at a YC pre-seed scale that does not map to my $50M **OpenAI** check or $115M **Commonwealth Fusion** lead -- this is a deal where I am over-resourced and the natural lead is a defense-specialist fund.

My 36-month forecast: Arlo Industries gets acquired by Anduril or a tier-2 defense prime for $30-80M as a passive-sensing tuck-in into Lattice, OR stalls at 1-2 paid pilots ($1-3M ARR) with Ukrainian and select European MoD customers without breaking into a US DoD program of record. The mechanism producing this outcome is the same one I watched compress every sensor-only defense startup of the last decade: Anduril's $20B Army C-UAS framework is a buyer-access moat, not a technology moat, and a passive-mesh sensor that plugs into Lattice is more valuable as an Anduril SKU than as an independent prime.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that drone warfare is the defining military shift of this decade and passive sensing is genuinely physics-differentiated from active radar -- no emissions means no geolocation means survival on a contested battlefield, which is exactly why Ukraine is the largest live air-defense testbed since Vietnam and why Arlo Industries's frontline demos there matter more than a US procurement timeline. The bear case wins on the dossier evidence: Anduril Lattice is already a "mesh networking platform that fuses data and inputs from sensors and effectors" per Tectonic Defense 2026, Arlo Industries is a 2-person team with only Deo Arlo publicly named, and the "exponential accuracy from linear sensors" claim has zero published benchmarks. The single condition that flips me to invest: a signed $5M+ program-of-record contract with a named NATO MoD or US DoD unit within 18 months, OR a third-party benchmark showing the Arlo Industries mesh outperforms centralized active radar against stealth drones at battalion scale.
