# Cerenovus -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Cerenovus is a three-Harvard-sophomore B2B knowledge-graph for executives whose distribution architecture, on the dossier as written, is founder-led enterprise sales in San Francisco in-person -- the structural inverse of the filter I run first. The signature is a decent-sized market (enterprise AI knowledge, where Glean just crossed $300M ARR in May 2026) wrapped around the leverage architecture I most reliably avoid: capital-and-labor leverage that requires permission from a procurement department. Jonathan Waldorf, Lucas Baur, and Oliver Moreland are intelligent kids -- QuEra Computing, Yao lab, Harvard Financial Analysts Club -- but the dimensional pairing here is "big market, wrong leverage," and that pairing has a name in my ledger: it's the shape of companies I pass on.

Twitter (2007) is the rung I anchor leverage-architecture against -- media, zero marginal cost, anyone can post, the platform charges nothing, the founder ships a side product off a failing podcast company and it compounds without anyone's permission. Cerenovus advertises the opposite: two open "Founding Engineer" roles requiring "SF in-person," pilot customers reached by founder hand-sell, no GitHub repos surfaced, no Twitter/X account for any of the three founders, no Product Hunt launch, no organic developer or creator pull. The product ingests Slack, email, PDFs, spreadsheets -- which means distribution requires the executive buyer granting access to all of those systems, which is the definition of permissioned. If Cerenovus open-sourced the markdown-knowledge-graph layer and devs started running it against their own corpora, this paragraph would read differently. The dossier shows none of that.

The Pinterest failure mechanism was personal-taste pattern-bias overriding framework -- I passed on a company that passed my criteria because "I don't get it." Cerenovus is the opposite failure pattern, the one I'm more comfortable acting on: a company that I do get, that does not pass criterion #1. The founder-trio at Cerenovus discovered the "executives rely on consultants" problem as undergraduates, not as executives -- there is no lived specific-knowledge gate the way Atwood and Spolsky lived Stack Overflow (2008) or Ivan Zhao lived Notion (2013). Waldorf's LinkedIn reads "Harvard Dropout | Cerenovus," Baur is a Harvard '28 finance student, Moreland's individual background is conflated with the others in search. This is the "memo founder" pattern -- responding to a category Glean made legible rather than to a problem owned for years.

Compounding mechanics on dimension two -- marketplace/network value -- fail by construction over an 18-month horizon at Cerenovus. Each customer's markdown knowledge graph is private corporate IP; the Nth Cerenovus customer does not make the (N-1) prior customers' graphs more valuable, because cross-tenant inference is exactly what enterprise procurement forbids. Without that compounding, Cerenovus is a single-sided SaaS subscription where the upside math collapses from 10,000x to maybe 30x, and the asymmetric-payoff geometry that justifies my wide funnel evaporates. Perplexity got a known exception from me on this dimension because criterion #1 and criterion #3 both cleared at top quartile; Cerenovus clears neither.

Counterfactual on dimension four -- the market itself is the kindest thing in the dossier. Glean at $300M ARR is the proof Cerenovus operates in a market large enough to absorb two founder pivots inside seed runway. For this dimension to flip from neutral to top-quartile, Cerenovus would have to articulate the adjacent pivot vector explicitly -- agentic workflow execution, vertical ops-graphs for specific industries, a developer-tool framing where the knowledge graph is a SDK rather than a SaaS. The dossier names none. The TAM is real; the pivot-room is theoretical.

Single-anchor parallel for the Buffett trio: Notion's Ivan Zhao at the 2013 seed was a person you wanted ten years of monthly contact with, because the depth of taste and problem-ownership was load-bearing. The Cerenovus founder package -- Waldorf at QuEra, Baur in the finance club, Moreland's background fundamentally unverifiable from public sources -- has nothing visibly disqualifying (no contested exits, no integrity flag, no self-promotional honesty signaling) and nothing visibly compelling for a ten-year partnership against the executive-operations problem they've chosen. Default of 7/14 is correct: silence is neutral, not negative.

Forecast, 36 months: Cerenovus reaches sub-$2M ARR with 8-15 pilot conversions, fails to raise a competitive Series A against Glean's distribution gravity and Microsoft Copilot's M365 bundle, and acquihires into a larger enterprise-search incumbent -- Glean, Guru, or a Salesforce property via Slack AI -- for the engineering team rather than the book. The mechanism is straightforward: a capital-leverage business in a category with a $300M-ARR incumbent ($765M raised) cannot out-distribute the incumbent without either a leverage-architecture inversion (open-source pull) or a specific-knowledge moat (founders who lived the executive-ops problem). Cerenovus dossier shows neither.

The strongest bull case against me: Tyler Bosmeny is the YC partner, and Bosmeny built Clever -- B2B enterprise sales done right by a young technical founder against entrenched incumbents. Glean itself was a YC company; the category proves enterprise AI knowledge can compound from cold-start to $300M ARR in under five years. Three Harvard quantum-and-finance kids may be exactly the IQ-and-energy package that out-executes Glean's now-bureaucratic motion. The bear answer: Glean had Arvind Jain, who shipped Google's enterprise search at scale before founding -- that's specific knowledge in the criterion #3 sense, and it is precisely what Cerenovus founders do not have. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a public GitHub repo for the markdown-knowledge-graph layer with organic external contributors landing PRs within six months -- evidence that Cerenovus has flipped from permissioned capital leverage to permissionless code leverage. Until then, I pass.
