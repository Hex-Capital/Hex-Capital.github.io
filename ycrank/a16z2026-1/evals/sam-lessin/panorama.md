# Panorama -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Every single VC at every single demo day right now is salivating over "AI workflow automation." Panorama is building in the exact center of the hottest consensus category in enterprise software. Zapier just got marked at $5B. n8n raised $254M at $2.5B. ServiceNow paid $2.85B for Moveworks. The market has priced in the upside of this entire category already — and then some. When I look at Panorama, my first question isn't "is this good?" It's "where's the mispricing?" And the answer is: there isn't one. If anything, the mispricing runs the wrong direction — the market is overvaluing AI application-layer startups relative to the incumbents who will actually capture the value. That's the opposite of what I'm looking for.

Here's the thing that should terrify anyone writing a check into Panorama: Microsoft Copilot Studio has native access to Teams, Outlook, and Office data. Google AgentSpace has native access to Workspace. Slack is owned by Salesforce. These platforms don't need to integrate via API to observe workplace patterns — they ARE the workplace. Panorama's entire product thesis is "we analyze your Slack messages and Notion docs to surface automations." You know who else can analyze your Slack messages? Slack. Panorama needs 1-2 weeks of observation through third-party APIs to generate recommendations. Microsoft can observe your patterns from day one because they own the data layer. This is the most textbook "perfect for Adobe" scenario I've seen in months. The dossier itself acknowledges it: "Large incumbents have access to the same workplace data through their own platforms." When the company's own research section identifies the kill shot, pay attention.

Jingwei Hao and Jaclyn Lunger are genuinely impressive individuals — I want to be clear about that. Jingwei went from senior engineer to staff to tech lead manager at Lyft, ran key infrastructure at Cash App, shipped real systems at Google and Twitter. Jaclyn has an MIT PhD in ML, built production ML systems, and was the first employee at Lila Sciences before it hit $1.3B. These are serious operators. But the sanity-insanity ratio is inverted. These are sane people pitching a sane idea. "Use AI to discover workplace automations" is what every product manager at Microsoft, Google, and Salesforce is already building internally. Nobody hears this pitch and thinks "that's fucking crazy" — they think "that's obviously correct, which is why Satya Nadella is already doing it." I also can't find any evidence of a deep pre-existing relationship between the cofounders — Jingwei's background is mobile infrastructure at fintech companies, Jaclyn's is materials science ML at MIT. That absence doesn't kill a deal, but it doesn't help either.

The primitive test is where Panorama falls flattest. A "TikTok-like recommendation engine for workflows" is a feature analogy, not a new behavior. Zapier already has 7,000+ integrations and $310M in revenue — the category of workflow automation is two decades old. Panorama's observational approach is a UX improvement on Zapier's user-configured model, not a new primitive. Nobody is going to say "Panorama me" the way people say "Venmo me." Compare this to Solana, which was a fundamentally different architectural bet on how blockchains compute — a new consensus mechanism at the protocol layer. Panorama is an application sitting on top of other people's platforms, dependent on their APIs, with no protocol-level anything. I invest in protocols, not apps built on someone else's rails.

The bull case requires believing three things simultaneously: that Microsoft, Google, and Salesforce all fail to add observational workflow discovery to their native platforms despite having superior data access; that Panorama's recommendation quality creates enough switching cost to retain enterprise customers; and that the cold-start data dependency (1-2 weeks minimum) becomes a moat rather than a conversion killer. The SOC2 Type I certification and CASA Tier 3 at pre-seed stage shows enterprise seriousness. Jaclyn's track record at Lila Sciences proves she can build ML systems that work in production. If Panorama's recommendation engine is 10x better than anything an incumbent ships — and if enterprise procurement teams are willing to grant broad Slack and Notion access to a four-person startup — there's a path. But that path requires the incumbents to be incompetent at exactly the thing they're most incentivized to build, and I don't bet on incumbent incompetence when the feature is sustaining rather than disruptive.

Panorama scores a clear pass driven by two dimensions. Consensus Mispricing is near the floor — this is the most consensus-exciting category in enterprise software, priced at a premium, with every major platform company building the same capability natively. Structural Durability fails the AI kill test about as cleanly as anything I've evaluated: pure SaaS, no physical moat, no protocol lock-in, dependent on API access to platforms owned by direct competitors. Strong founders, wrong bet for my money.
