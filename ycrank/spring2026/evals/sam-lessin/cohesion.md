# Cohesion -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Cohesion is the canonical 2026 YC pitch I wrote my Pass Example against, with one wrinkle: Devon Krapcho actually spent 5+ years as a buy-side software analyst at Long Path Partners, and Matt Munns built AI for investors at T. Rowe Price. The signature here is real founder-market fit stapled to the most consensus-priced category in the batch — an "agentic teammate" for hedge-fund analysts, fighting AlphaSense ($500M ARR), Hebbia ($700M val), and Rogo ($300M+ raised, $160M Series D in April 2026) for the same workflow. The conviction dimension is the only thing keeping this dossier out of the lowest tier; everything else is the deck I've stopped taking meetings on.

On round dynamics, the relevant HIGH rung is **Solana** at $0.05/coin in 2018 — a category every generalist fund had quit, where the price discount was the entire alpha. Cohesion sits at the opposite end of that ladder. Agentic AI for finance verticals is the most-bid sleeve in Spring 2026: Rogo just printed a Series D, AlphaSense's GenAI queries are growing 33% QoQ, Hebbia raised at a $700M markup on $13M ARR. Cohesion's dossier discloses no round size, no investors, no price — and in 2026, silence in an "agentic AI for finance" YC pitch is not bullish ambiguity, it's the SAFE cap I cannot make money at. The Pass Example was written for this exact dossier shape: "AI agent for enterprise vertical, three months out of YC, tier-1s already in." Nothing in Cohesion's page disconfirms it.

Capital structure compounds against Cohesion over the next 18 months in a specific way. The product is per-seat enterprise SaaS into hedge funds — a category where AlphaSense employs a dedicated content-licensing team and Rogo has $300M+ to outspend any seed-stage competitor on SOC2, model evals, and enterprise sales motion. Cohesion's 10+ funds at $10Bn+ combined AUM is real for a Spring 2026 company, but the cap table it implies needs five more financing tacks to reach default-alive against incumbents already at $500M ARR. There is no version of this where the financing instrument gets interesting — no revenue share, no holdco, no employee-equity wrinkle. It's a standard SaaS round in a category where the consensus has already priced the winner.

The LOW rung on founder-as-brand is the "lifestyle vlogger without a business system" the Slow Creator Fund explicitly avoids — failure mechanism: no owned distribution, every customer dollar costs CAC. That pattern's inverse appears in Cohesion's dossier in a different shape. Krapcho, McBrien, and Munns have no Twitter accounts I can find, no GitHub repos, no public writing on the category, no podcast, no Substack. Distribution is entirely Krapcho's analyst Rolodex from Long Path, which is real but cap-able — there are maybe 200 funds in his warm graph. That's enough for a $5M ARR business and not enough for the regatta to clear into a Series A buyer's frame against Rogo's 250+ institutional logo wall.

On the conviction dimension, what would have to be true for this to flip a tier is straightforward: Krapcho would need to be publishing opinionated long-form writing on why generalist platforms like AlphaSense structurally cannot serve fundamental equity workflows — the buy-side analyst's version of a non-obvious belief. The dossier shows 5+ years of relevant pattern data inside Long Path and zero artifacts of him processing that data publicly. The "writing is thinking" filter fails not because the thinking isn't there but because none of it is legible to a downstream Series A underwriter — and legibility translation is the job I'm being asked to do here.

Counterfactual on category sleeve: the only Sam Lessin portfolio company this resembles on the axis is **Sublime Security**, which I bought into the enterprise-security sleeve before the consolidation wave. The parallel breaks because security tooling in 2023 had identifiable consolidation candidates and depressed seed pricing; agentic AI for finance in 2026 has neither. AlphaSense at $500M ARR and Rogo at $160M Series D are the consolidation. Cohesion is shipping into an already-consolidated buyer that the dossier names as direct competitors, not adjacents.

36-month forecast: Cohesion reaches $2–5M ARR by mid-2029 on the back of Krapcho's Long Path network, then sells to AlphaSense or Rogo for a sub-$50M acqui-hire as the "podcast and X monitoring" feature gets built natively by the incumbent with $500M ARR and a content-licensing team. Not zero, not breakout — the regatta tacks resolve into a feature acquisition because the cap table can't fund a fight against Rogo's war chest, and the founders are credible enough engineers and analysts that AWS-trained McBrien plus Munns's T. Rowe Price pedigree make for a clean talent buy.

The strongest bull case is that Krapcho actually knows — from five years of doing the manual job — which non-traditional signals matter and which are noise, and that 10+ funds at $10Bn+ AUM is genuine Wizard-of-Oz validation in a market AlphaSense's licensed-content model structurally can't serve because podcasts and X/Twitter are not licensable corpora. That stings, because the Wizard-of-Oz pattern is exactly my **Fin** template and the founder-market fit is real. It loses to one fact: AlphaSense added podcast transcript indexing in 2024 and is growing GenAI usage 33% QoQ; the non-licensable-corpus moat does not exist for the buyer that matters. The single condition that flips my verdict is Cohesion disclosing a sub-$10M post-money round with at least one tier-1 visibly passing, or signed multi-year contracts at $200K+ ACV with named funds — either reframes the asset from "feature in a Rogo roadmap" to "underwriteable independent business."
