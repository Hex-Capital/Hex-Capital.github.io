# Modern -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Modern is a cleanly-governed two-person team led by Seb Poole walking straight into the most consensus YC wedge of Spring 2026 -- "the AI-native ServiceNow" -- with $315M-acquired Moveworks, $127M Serval, and $40.3M Atomicwork already sitting on the lawn. The signature is unmistakable: high marks on founder-CEO clarity and operating maturity, lowest marks on the "magically weird" filter and the tactile artifact test. That pairing tells me exactly what Modern is. It is the well-built, well-governed entrant into a category Sequoia, Redpoint, and Khosla have already priced -- which is the opposite of the lane Long Journey lives in.

Anduril-Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he ever touched a Lattice slide; that is what "founder surfaced from lived experience" means on the high rung. Modern's Seb Poole co-founded Frankie Health, sold it to Unmind in February 2023, and ran a Hong Kong e-commerce venture before that -- a real repeat-founder résumé in enterprise SaaS, but Frankie sold *workplace mental health* to Disney, Uber, and MLB, not IT service desks to IT admins. Aleks Tomovski built developer platforms at Wix Code and Canva, which is adjacent to internal tooling but is not the same as having sat under a flickering fluorescent light closing offboarding tickets at 11pm. Modern's founders discovered ITSM the way the pass-example founders discovered AI agents -- through market analysis, not through having *been* the person whose Slack-DM-to-IT goes unanswered for three days. Repeat founders, yes. Lived problem ownership, no.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I pass on by slide three is the LOW rung here, and Modern reads close enough to that template that the comparison stings. The wedge has at minimum $482M of disclosed capital aimed at it -- Moveworks $315M (now ServiceNow's), Serval $127M at a $1B valuation backed by Sequoia and Redpoint, Atomicwork $40.3M backed by Khosla and Blume -- plus ServiceNow and Salesforce shipping native AI agents into 8,600 existing ITSM seats. The Long Journey thesis is "find them before it becomes consensus." Modern is *after* it has become consensus, and the contrarians who would have made this magically weird -- the founder who ran helpdesk at a regulated hospital, the immigrant who automated her own visa paperwork -- are not the founders who showed up. No sophisticated investor has visibly passed on aesthetic grounds, because there is nothing aesthetically transgressive to pass on.

Single CEO clarity is the one place Modern earns its keep. Seb Poole is named CEO; Tomovski carries "Stealth" and "Co-Founder" without the title. This is the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll co-equal arrangement that I watched implode -- 18 months from here, when Modern hits the inevitable "do we go upmarket to ServiceNow's customers or downmarket to mid-market Slack-first IT teams" fork, there is a tiebreaker. Mechanically, this dimension compounds: clean Day 1 governance lets a small team move faster than Serval can re-org, and Poole's prior exit means he has felt the inside of an acquisition process. Modern earns most of the points available here, even if I am not buying the rest of the package.

The frontier-hardware / unsexy-regulated dimension would have to flip on a counterfactual that the dossier does not support. If Modern were the *secure AI-for-IT-in-regulated-healthcare* play -- HIPAA-bound, on-prem-capable, the thing the VA or Kaiser deploys behind their firewall -- the SOC 2 Type 2 and ISO 27001 would compound into a regulatory moat the way Vigil Labs (August 2025) does in financial software. Modern's site lists those certifications and integrations with Workday and BambooHR, but the wedge is described as horizontal enterprise IT, not vertical-regulated IT. Compliance hygiene is not the same as compliance moat. SaaS wrappers in horizontal categories do not pay the rent I need in this seat.

Flock Safety I sourced from a Four Seasons Wi-Fi name because the product was *in the room*; Modern is a demo-gated Slack/Teams/WhatsApp bot whose customer outcomes -- 62% ticket-handling reduction at a "Global IT team," 74% faster response at a "Large retailer" -- arrive without a single named logo. I cannot install Modern, photograph Modern, or hand Modern to a friend in 48 hours. The five percentage points on the homepage are not falsifiable. The HQ Trivia exception -- where extreme product-led tactile virality once let me override a co-founder governance worry -- does not apply in reverse: clean governance cannot override the absence of tactile pull.

The 36-month forecast: Modern reaches roughly $1-3M ARR with a handful of mid-market logos, then gets acqui-hired by Atlassian, Freshworks, or BMC for a sub-$50M outcome before Series B, while ServiceNow's native AI agents and Serval's $127M war chest swallow the enterprise half of the wedge. The mechanism is straightforward: Poole sells well enough to land pilots, the product works well enough to keep them, but the unit economics never escape a market where the incumbent owns 40% of seats and the well-funded startup owns the venture narrative.

The strongest bull case against this forecast is Affirm-Levchin -- a repeat founder with executional muscle entering a category dominated by incumbents and still building something defensible because the founder's craft outruns the cap table around him. Poole sold Frankie. He can sell. Tomovski shipped developer platforms used by millions of Wix and Canva users. Maybe the wedge is bigger than I think and Modern is the one that lives. The dossier evidence that wins for me is the absence of a single named customer logo paired with the $482M of disclosed competitor capital -- Affirm-Levchin had Levchin, but he also had a wedge (BNPL at point-of-sale) that the incumbents structurally could not enter. Modern's incumbents are *already in the wedge*. The verifiable condition that would flip me: Modern publishes two named Fortune 500 logos with audited automation percentages within six months, and a credible tier-1 visibly passes on the next round for aesthetic reasons rather than chasing it. Until then, this is a pass.
