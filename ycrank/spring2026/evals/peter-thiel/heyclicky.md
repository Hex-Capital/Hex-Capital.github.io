# HeyClicky -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

HeyClicky is the consumer-software archetype my Pass Example was written against: Farza Majeed has the personal-brand signal of a known builder (89.3K X followers, $10M a16z round at buildspace) wrapped around a screenshot-plus-LLM-plus-TTS pipeline whose architecture is published in his own GitHub README. The dimensional signature is operator-narrative-high, structural-moat-zero. That is the pairing of a content-distribution play, not a company with a secret.

HeyClicky's contrarian-secret dimension is where I anchor first, and it is conspicuously empty. The HIGH rung is **Stripe** (2010) -- Patrick Collison naming the developer-experience failure mode of legacy payment processors when PayPal had "won" the category. The MID rung is **Asana** (2010), where the secret was thin but Dustin Moskovitz's Facebook lineage carried the override. The LOW rung is the canonical Pass Example -- "AI-powered platform for [vertical X]" with no named incumbent and no architectural blind spot. HeyClicky sits a half-rung above LOW only because the consumer-character framing is *slightly* differentiated from Cluely's interview overlay and Granola's notetaker; but the dossier does not name a specific reason OpenAI's "Work with Apps" or Anthropic's "computer use" structurally cannot ship a voice-first overlay character. Without that named failure mode, the secret terminates at branding, and branding is mimetic energy, not contrarian thesis.

HeyClicky's monopoly-trajectory dimension is the lowest of all five and the most decisive. The HIGH rung is **Palantir** (2003) -- integration depth into intelligence agencies producing 15+ years of pricing power on a customer base Silicon Valley refused to touch. The MID rung is **Lyft** (2010), where duopoly was the override because mobility's network effects stabilized two winners. The LOW rung is undifferentiated SaaS with no moat hypothesis. HeyClicky is *below* the LOW rung because the founder himself open-sourced the architecture in April 2026 and clone repos (emreyilmaz46/clicky_windows, jasonkneen/openclicky) already exist; the moat hypothesis terminates at "Farza's personal brand," which a competitor reproduces in nine months of YouTube uploads. No pricing page, free product, API-stack COGS sensitivity at Claude-plus-AssemblyAI-plus-ElevenLabs scale. This is commodity pricing at maturity by construction.

Definite-plan dimension: HIGH is **SpaceX** (2008) with the 15-year reusability roadmap, MID is **Palantir** (2003) with the government-customer plan, LOW is "iterate to product-market fit." HeyClicky sits at LOW -- the dossier describes a viral launch demo (~2.9M views), a free open-source release, and a Windows waitlist, but no multi-year named technical milestone the founder commits not to pivot from. Buildspace itself iterated through multiple identities before winding down in August 2024; the revealed pattern is iteration mode, not definite optimism.

Founder-lineage dimension is HeyClicky's least-weak axis and worth honest weighting. HIGH rung is **Affirm** (2014) -- Levchin as PayPal Mafia re-up shipped to IPO. MID rung is **Asana** (2010) -- Moskovitz with Facebook scale under his belt. LOW rung is first-time founders with no shipped product. Farza Majeed sits between MID and LOW: a second-time YC founder who raised $10M from Andreessen Horowitz at a $100M valuation, but whose buildspace shut down in August 2024. Shipped-to-audience, not shipped-to-scale-outcome. That is operator-narrative, not the Moskovitz-grade lineage that overrode Asana's structural gaps. Atoms-heavy dimension is straightforward -- HIGH is **SpaceX** (2008) reusable launch capex, MID is **Anduril** (2017) defense integration, LOW is pure-software consumer SaaS. HeyClicky is at the floor: a Mac menu-bar app calling three third-party APIs.

HeyClicky in 36 months: dead or sub-$2M ARR consumer-education subscription, displaced by OpenAI's "Work with Apps" and Anthropic's native "computer use" shipping the same overlay surface inside the chat clients users already have open. The mechanism is the same one that ate every ChatGPT-wrapper consumer app of 2023-2024: when the model provider ships the feature OS-native, the differentiated wrapper collapses to its brand layer, and a brand layer at 89.3K followers does not survive Apple/OpenAI/Anthropic distribution at hundreds of millions. The buildspace wind-down is the base rate.

The strongest objection routes through the **Asana** (2010) exception. Moskovitz lacked structural moat and lacked a contrarian secret; the override was Facebook-scale operator pedigree, and one could argue Farza's buildspace-plus-89.3K-following is the same pattern -- distribution-as-moat for a consumer category. The objection stings until I check the actual mechanism: Moskovitz had shipped Facebook to a billion users and a public exit before Asana; Farza shipped buildspace to a $100M paper valuation and then a quiet shutdown. The lineage override requires shipped-to-scale-outcome, not shipped-then-wound-down. Asana's override also rested on workflow being a *retention* category with switching costs once installed; HeyClicky has stateless sessions and zero switching cost by the founder's own architectural choice. The exception does not transfer. The single verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: HeyClicky signs a paid education-vertical contract (a named school district, a named creative-tools company embedding Clicky as the in-app tutor with multi-year integration commitment) producing structural switching cost that the OS-native chat windows cannot replicate -- in which case the Palantir-shaped customer-integration thesis becomes available. Absent that, pass.
