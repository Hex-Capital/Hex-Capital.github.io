# Superfruits Inc. -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Superfruits Inc. (operating as Gojiberry AI) is a clean-governance, repeat-operator team executing competently in the single most consensus category of the Spring 2026 YC batch -- AI GTM agents for B2B sales -- and the signature of "well-run inside a crowded room" is exactly the shape I learned to step around. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant, Rom?n Czerny, and Dylan Teixeira have three prior SaaS exits between them (CoCo AI sold 2025, Edusign sold 2025), $2.5M ARR in ten months, and a Product Hunt #1 launch. None of that gets me past the fact that Clay just closed $100M at a $3.1B valuation doing a meaner version of the same buyer-intent workflow.

Crusoe ~2018 is the high rung on "magically weird": flared gas, North Dakota, Bitcoin, three uninvestable categories stapled together until they weren't. Superfruits Inc. is the inverse of that geometry. Clay ($210M raised), 11x ($76M), Artisan ($35M+), and Regie.ai ($65.6M) are already in the room, and the YC Spring 2026 batch will ship multiple variations on "AI SDR with intent signals" before demo day. Gojiberry AI is not ignored or laughed at -- it ranked #1 on Product Hunt the day it launched. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus, and Superfruits Inc. is the consensus. That isn't a rebuttal; it's a hard no on this axis.

Anduril 2017 sits at the top of the lived-experience ladder because Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he ever touched defense -- the biography preceded the company. Superfruits Inc. has a softer, secondhand version of the same pattern: Lallemant and Czerny shipped and sold CoCo AI (~$500K ARR, WhatsApp marketing for Shopify) and Teixeira ran Edusign to multi-million ARR before selling in 2025. They have lived *SaaS exits*, not lived *outbound sales*. The Gojiberry pivot from services-to-product is documented at superframeworks.com -- it is the move of operators who know how to find revenue, not of an SDR who got tired of stitching tools together at 11pm. Closer to the consensus YC pass example than to Luckey.

The HQ Trivia 2017 Yusupov/Kroll feud is what calibrates how I read founder authority, and Superfruits Inc. is the structural inverse of that failure. Lallemant is the named CEO, Czerny is CMO, Teixeira is CTO -- three roles, three lanes, and the two business co-founders already survived one venture together (CoCo AI, 2024-2025) before starting Gojiberry AI. That kind of pre-tested partnership is rarer than people credit and is the one place Superfruits Inc. genuinely earns marks. It is also the dimension that matters least when the surrounding category is this contested.

Mechanism on physical verifiability: a normal human can sign up for the $99/month Pro tier at app.gojiberry.ai today and watch LinkedIn messages go out under their own sender accounts. That is observable, which beats the pure-API pass example, but it is not tactile in the Flock Safety sense -- I cannot point at a camera on a pole, I cannot ride the scooter, I cannot watch Pok?mon Go pull strangers into a park. Eighteen months from now the artifact compounds only if the cross-customer signal flywheel produces meaningfully better reply rates than Clay's, and Superfruits Inc. has offered no third-party validation of that, only a self-reported "2-5x more positive replies." Observable, not load-bearing.

Counterfactual on the capital-intensive/unsexy axis: for Superfruits Inc. to flip a tier here, the moat would need to live somewhere LinkedIn's ToS enforcement and Clay's data marketplace cannot reach -- a proprietary intent signal Gojiberry AI owns rather than aggregates, regulated-industry verticalization, or an actual hardware artifact in the GTM stack. None of those exist in the dossier. The $99/month price point implies SMB/prosumer, which is the same segment that produced 11x AI's documented churn coverage in TechCrunch 2025. Pure software, contested category, externally-sourced signals -- this is the lane I exit, not the one I enter.

Single-anchor parallel on weirdness: Mindbloom does ketamine therapy in a category most investors will not say out loud at dinner, and the moat compounds in the regulatory friction. Superfruits Inc. operates inside LinkedIn's automation envelope -- the same envelope that already restricted Artisan AI's Ava in 2025 per completeaitraining.com -- which means the regulatory friction here cuts *against* Gojiberry AI rather than for it. The platform that hosts the mechanism is also the platform most motivated to break it.

My 36-month forecast: Superfruits Inc. gets acqui-hired into Clay, Apollo, HubSpot, or Outreach in the $30-80M range within 24-30 months, consistent with the Lallemant-Czerny pattern of selling CoCo AI in 2025 and Teixeira selling Edusign in 2025 -- this is a team that knows how to find a buyer. Alternative path: a LinkedIn ToS enforcement event collapses growth, Gojiberry AI plateaus near $4-6M ARR, and the team starts a fourth company. Either outcome is fine for the founders; neither is the outcome that earns its slot in a Long Journey portfolio.

The strongest bull case is that $2.5M ARR in 10 months, profitable, with 35% MoM growth is genuinely exceptional pre-seed traction, and that the trio's three-exit track record is the "operators who know how to ship and sell" pattern -- the version of HQ Trivia 2017 where the tactile virality overrides the category objection. The bull case stings because the numbers are real. It loses to the dossier on one fact: HQ Trivia's override was a product so addictive I could feel the viral pull in my own hand before any metric existed, and Superfruits Inc.'s product is competent outbound automation against four heavily-capitalized incumbents -- $99/month subscribers churning out of an AI SDR tool is not the same artifact as a country pausing at 9pm for trivia. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict: an independent third-party benchmark (not founder-reported) showing Gojiberry AI's reply rates exceeding Clay's by 2x on matched cohorts, sustained for two quarters. Absent that, this is a pass with one foot in love.
