# Stage -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Stage is a clean single-miracle UX bet sitting in a category where the index position has already been claimed -- CodeRabbit at $550M post and $15M+ ARR (TechCrunch, Sep 2025) and Cursor's December 2025 Graphite acquisition (TechCrunch) declared the lanes before Charles Pan and Dean Stratakos shipped their Show HN in October 2025 (HN id 47796818). The two-person team has the cleanest part of my framework and the worst part of it stacked on the same deal, which is exactly the shape that usually produces an acqui-hire rather than a defining company.

Anduril (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for Stage on the catalyst dimension -- not because Stage is defense, but because both ran the "market peers misread as steady-state" play with a specific 24-month window. The catalyst is real: Cursor, Claude Code, and Devin moved the bottleneck from code generation to code review in 2024-2025, and CodeRabbit's reported ~20% MoM growth (TechCrunch, Sep 2025) is the same kind of curve confirmation Ukraine produced for defense software. Stage diverges from the Anduril rung because the catalyst is now *consensus* -- Benchmark led Greptile's $25M Series A in September 2025 (SiliconANGLE) and Cursor paid for Graphite in December 2025 (TechCrunch), so Stage is entering the window 18 months *after* the smart money priced it, not before. That's not the Anduril template; that's showing up to a re-opened market with the rooms already filled.

Lyft Series C is the failure mechanism I keep in mind for Stage on index position -- I passed on Lyft because #2 in a winner-take-all category captures a fraction of #1's economics (20VC), and the pattern repeats whenever a company has to fight for share against named, better-funded incumbents in a declared category. Stage's dossier puts it in exactly that posture: GitHub native PR review is the incumbent Stage explicitly benchmarks against ("5x faster than GitHub"), CodeRabbit raised $96M+, Greptile drew Benchmark, and Cursor now owns Graphite. My *AI Market Clarity* piece (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) had legal AI's winners declared as Harvey and CaseText -- code review is the equivalent declaration, and Stage's wedge ("humans back in control of code review," Show HN, Oct 2025) is a *feature debate*, not a distribution position every entrant in a compounding category must touch. Stripe is an index on e-commerce; Stage is a UX preference inside a category where the index seats are taken.

On founder ships iteratively, Stratakos is the strongest signal in the dossier -- a Stanford MS CS who led the firm-wide AI initiative at Five Rings Capital and built an in-house coding agent (dastratakos.github.io), then shipped `ReviewStage/stage-cli` to 204 stars on GitHub. The mechanism that compounds over 18 months is this: a founder who has already built the *internal* version of the tool he's now selling has the muscle to ship between investor meetings, and Stratakos's prior Apple Siri personalization and Citadel TCP recovery work (dastratakos.github.io) is the shipping-history pattern, not the credential pattern. Pan's early-engineer stint at Yuzu Health (YC W22) adds startup-shipping reps. This is the part of Stage I cannot dismiss -- it is genuinely a Perplexity-shaped founder signal, not a McKinsey-shaped one.

Revenue velocity is the counterfactual dimension for Stage -- what would have to be true to flip this from pre-seed-default to Harvey-shaped? The dossier discloses a 14-day free trial and a "LAUNCH" promo for 25% off three months (YC page), gated pricing, and no MRR number. Harvey (2023) at this stage was already in pilots with named AmLaw firms; CodeRabbit at the same age was disclosing ARR. Silence on growth in the AI cycle is itself negative signal (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) because real growth is easy to disclose. To flip Stage one tier, the founders would need to disclose $50K+ MRR or three named enterprise design-partner logos within 90 days -- absent that, the 204 GitHub stars and Show HN post are the Perplexity-cold-inbound *shape* without the curve to confirm it.

Stage's single-miracle architecture is what saves it from a Pass and pins it at Neutral -- the miracle is "AI can semantically segment a diff into ordered chapters reviewers prefer over raw diffs," which is one validated capability (post-2023 LLM code understanding) plus GTM execution. Color Genomics (2015) is the parallel: workflow infrastructure on top of a hard layer (sequencing then, LLM code comprehension now), not a bet on the hard layer itself. Stage is not asking me to underwrite a new model, new chemistry, or new regulatory pathway. That's a clean miracle count, which is rare enough at YC that I weight it.

My 36-month forecast for Stage: acqui-hired by Cursor, GitHub, or CodeRabbit at $20-60M, with the modal outcome being a Cursor tuck-in absorbing Stratakos's coding-agent muscle within 18-24 months and the chapter-segmentation UX shipped as a Cursor PR-review feature. The mechanism is straightforward -- CodeRabbit and Cursor have the engineering capability and channel to ship overlapping diff-organization UX without channel conflict, and the human-reviewer-first wedge will either be absorbed as a toggle or commoditized. ARR at exit: sub-$2M. Not a zero, not a defining company.

The bull case I have to take seriously is that Stratakos has genuine founder-market fit and the AI-auto-reviewer narrative (CodeRabbit, Greptile, cubic) is itself the contrarian-wrong call -- that regulated industries, senior engineers, and post-mortem-burned eng leaders will pay specifically for *human-in-the-loop* diff comprehension as agent-generated code volume explodes, and Stage's positioning is the durable wedge while the auto-reviewers race to a commoditized bottom. If that's right, the human-reviewer-first category is wide-open per my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy, and a Stratakos-led team that already shipped a 204-star CLI is exactly the founder-market fit profile I funded Decagon (2023) and Abridge (2023) on. The dossier evidence that wins the argument against me: no disclosed revenue, no named enterprise design partner, no signed LOI -- in the AI cycle that's the diagnostic. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Stage discloses $75K+ MRR or three named enterprise customers (Fortune 500 eng org or comparable scale) within the next 90 days. Absent that signal, Stage is the right kind of founder in the wrong position on the index map.
