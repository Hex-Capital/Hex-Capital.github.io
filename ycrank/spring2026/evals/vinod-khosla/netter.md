# Netter -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Netter is what my Pass Example was written to describe -- not literally a sales-call summarizer, but structurally the same animal: a chat interface over 120+ connectors that promises to compress a mid-market retailer's analytics work from four hours to thirty minutes. The dimensional signature is unambiguous. Netter's highest-scoring dimension is founder learning rate, and that is only because Samuel Ladroue and Amin Hamzaoui have too short a public record to penalize. Its lowest is consequence magnitude, which is my entry point, and on that axis Netter never gets off the runway. A company whose success-state is "Carrefour FR recovers ~10% of dormant receivables faster" does not redirect a civilizational variable. That is the whole evaluation in one sentence; the rest is showing my work.

**OpenAI** (2019) anchors the high rung on consequence: the success-state was the displacement of cognitive labor across the $15T U.S. labor base, which is why I wrote a $50M check into a no-revenue, unconventional-structure entity. The shared mechanism a candidate would need is a named incumbent regime that disappears if Netter wins. Netter's dossier names Palantir Foundry, Snowflake, Databricks -- but as competitors Netter must coexist with, not categories Netter collapses. "Build with your data, not your engineers" is a productivity claim against the internal-data-engineering line item, not against an industry. Compare that to my Fortune (2026-03-06) framing: $15T of labor going away. Netter at maximum ambition removes some BI analyst seats from a few hundred mid-market operators. That is real revenue and zero consequence.

The Pass Example pattern -- "20-30% efficiency gain inside one workflow at one persona, indistinguishable from a feature any Salesforce or HubSpot product team ships in twelve months" -- is the failure mechanism on technical-capital risk, and Netter walks straight into it. Snowflake's Cortex, Databricks' Genie, and Microsoft Fabric are each shipping LLM-driven "chat with your data" surfaces; the IBTimes 2026 ranking already lists them as Palantir alternatives. Netter's 120+ connectors and ontology layer are real engineering, but neither is a miracle. There is no physics, no biology, no fleet, no plant. The dossier's own commoditization risk section concedes the point: "every major warehouse vendor" is adding this. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) required tokamak magnet physics no consensus VC would underwrite; Netter requires Postgres connectors and a function-calling loop.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus, mechanism analysis over 18 months is the relevant lens. Ladroue (Mines Paris Math/CS) and Hamzaoui (Mines Paris + ENS Ulm) are credentialed-elite-school students with no public thesis, no GitHub footprint surfaced, no Twitter presence, and no documented contrarian position in data tooling. That profile compounds badly in this specific market: when Snowflake and Databricks ship the agent layer in 2026-2027, the founder needs a publicly defended weird-shaped bet ("everyone is wrong about ontologies because X") to recruit a team that will fight the incumbent compression. Netter's dossier contains none of that. My TED Talk citation -- UC Berkeley's 28,000-prediction study, experts "only slightly more accurate than chance" -- cuts both ways: the absence of expert continuity is not a positive signal either when the founders are pre-career.

Counterfactual on founder learning rate: what would have to be true to move Netter from the absent-evidence default of 7/15 to the **Slash** (2024) tier that justified my $100M Series C? Slash had a documented public pivot -- the Kanye West moment -- that was the override signal letting me ignore the consequence-magnitude gap on consumer business banking. Netter would need a comparable artifact: a public "we built X first, killed it, here is what we learned, now we are building Netter" from Ladroue. The Spring 2026 YC profile shows no such story. Without it, Netter has the Slash gap on consequence with none of the Slash override.

Required-capital profile: single-anchor parallel to **Cognition** (2024), where the $175M Series B made sense because the foundation-model training and agent-evaluation infrastructure is genuinely capital-hungry. Netter's roadmap is connectors and an LLM-orchestrated workflow runtime. The StartupHub.ai "$12M raised, Seed" figure is uncorroborated and inconsistent with YC's standard Spring 2026 deal, but even taking it at face value, Netter is a $5-15M-to-PMF business. I am over-resourced. Any Tier-1 generalist funds this; my edge does not apply.

My 36-month forecast: Netter ends 2029 at $3-8M ARR in European mid-market retail, or absorbed sub-$50M by Snowflake, Databricks, or a Carrefour-adjacent systems integrator. The mechanism producing that range is the geographic-market mismatch in the dossier -- SF HQ with Carrefour FR, Tesco UK, Auchan ES as the only named deployments -- combined with Snowflake/Databricks shipping the agent layer that eats the wedge. The Mines Paris/ENS Ulm network in European retail is real distribution, which is why I do not predict outright zero; the lack of a US enterprise motion is why I do not predict $30M+.

The strongest bear-killer is the **Slash** (2024) exception: YC pedigree plus a real product in production at three named EU retailers might be the override pattern, with the bet being that mid-market ontology+workflow is the **Glean** (founder ex-Google search) shape -- a domain-expert play I have funded despite my own rhetoric. The dossier facts that win against this read: Glean's override was capital scale and Fortune-500 information asymmetry, both of which Netter lacks at mid-market retail receivables-recovery scale; Slash's override was a documented founder pivot, which Ladroue does not have. The Carrefour/Tesco/Auchan logos are unverified -- no press release, no case study URL, no contract value -- and logo-on-site is not paid production. The one verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed multi-year contract with disclosed seven-figure ACV at a named US Fortune-500 operator where Netter is displacing an internal data-engineering headcount of disclosed size. That artifact would re-open the consequence-magnitude scoring against the $15T labor frame. Until then, Netter is the deal I pass on in thirty seconds so I can spend the afternoon on the next **Physical Intelligence** (2024).
