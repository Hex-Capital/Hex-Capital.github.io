# Andustry -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Andustry is a plausible-catalyst / absent-curve company -- the LLM-agent thesis underneath industrial sourcing is the most defensible thing in the dossier, and the strongest dollar figure on the page ($400K saved on a $1.2M CNC order, andustry.com May 2026) is a customer testimonial, not a revenue line. That pairing -- a real "why now" wrapped around testimonial-grade traction -- is the analytical signature for Andustry, and it's exactly the shape of company that punishes you in the AI cycle for paying consensus prices on narrative.

On the catalyst dimension, **Decagon** (2023) is the template I keep next to Andustry: post-LLM workflow infrastructure in a category where agent reliability crossed a threshold in 2024-2025, layered on top of a sourcing flow buyers already hate. Andustry's "Alibaba on AI" framing in the Y Combinator LinkedIn post (Spring 2026) plus the inferred US/EU non-China diversification tailwind is a credible re-opening -- multi-turn supplier negotiation and quote normalization genuinely weren't feasible pre-GPT-4-class models. But unlike **Decagon**'s customer-support surface, Andustry's catalyst is *inferred* in the dossier rather than named by the founders, and the "Why now" line is bracketed [Inferred]. That's the difference between a market re-opening I can underwrite and one I'm constructing for the founders.

**Lyft** Series C is the analogue I reach for on index position, because the failure mode was structural rather than executional -- a #2 seat in a market where #1 captured the share-inversion economics. Andustry sits in the broker layer of a category where Xometry ($687M FY2025 revenue, Xometry investor release Feb 2026) and MISUMI/Fictiv ($350M acquisition, The Robot Report Apr 2025) already operate AI-driven quoting flows with deeper supplier networks and managed-QC stacks. The broker UX itself is replicable by any team with LLM agent infrastructure -- Andustry's dossier names no proprietary supplier-onboarding mechanism, no exclusive carrier-of-record status, no regulated quote standard they sit inside. That's not an index on industrial procurement; that's a fight against an incumbent with $687M of revenue and capital to extend.

Revenue velocity is where Andustry hits the "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth, something's fundamentally broken" line directly (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). The 18-month mechanism: commission-only pricing (andustry.com) on a product whose entire value proposition is *creating buyer-side price transparency* -- that's a take-rate curve that compresses as the product succeeds, not compounds. Three testimonials with savings dollar figures and a 30% average price reduction claim is not the **Harvey** (2023) $3B→$5B→$8B-in-months pattern; it's the false-signal shape I called out in TechCrunch (Nov 2025). Andustry's dossier discloses zero MRR, zero MoM growth, zero named repeat-buyer logos.

Counterfactually on founder ship-rhythm: for Andustry's 8/20 here to flip into the 13-15 band, I'd need to see Het Dave's AppliedAI/FINN work surface as named shipped artifacts -- specifically an agent-evaluation framework or negotiation prototype with public usage -- and I'd need Johann Stürken's family trading-goods business to translate into a concrete supplier-network seed (named smelters, named SME buyers from that book). Andustry's dossier gives CDTM/TUM/RWTH Aachen/McKinsey credentials and zero GitHub repos for either founder. That's the *promoter-not-shipper* archetype I disqualify on, not the **Perplexity** (2022) cold-inbound founder who shows up with a built thing.

On single-miracle architecture, Andustry's miracle stack mirrors **Color Genomics** (2015) in ambition but not in isolation: workflow on top of a hard layer is the right pattern, but Andustry requires (1) agent reliability on multi-turn industrial negotiation across language/customs/freight, (2) supplier-network density that beats Alibaba's keyword surface, (3) buyer trust on first-time $1M+ transactions with no escrow or carrier-of-record disclosed in the dossier, and (4) margin durability against Xometry's AI-quoting extension. That's a compounding-small-odds problem, not a single-miracle bet (NFX, *Unusual Signs of Billion-Dollar Companies*).

36-month forecast: Andustry quietly winds down or gets acqui-hired by a mid-tier procurement SaaS or a freight-forwarder for under $20M, while Xometry and Alibaba ship agent layers that absorb the broker UX Andustry productized. ARR plateaus under $2M; no Series A from a top-decile fund. The mechanism is the take-rate compression I called out -- their own product creates the transparency that erodes their commission.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Stürken's family trading-goods business is exactly the kind of operator-network access that doesn't publicize, and it's the pattern I quietly use to gate "market first" deals through founder-graph access. If Andustry's testimonials -- $400K, $800K, 2,000 tonnes/month -- are repeat buyers from that family network rather than one-shot trades, then Andustry has a distribution wedge that doesn't show up in the dossier and that incumbents structurally can't replicate. The bear-case dismantle: the dossier doesn't disclose repeat purchase, the customer-discrepancy footnote ("Nikolas Keller" surfaced in search but not on the YC page) suggests the founder graph isn't yet legible, and the commission-only model with no rate card is the opposite of the operational discipline (**Brex** 2017, **Rippling** ~2019) I underwrite on. One verifiable condition flips this: $500K MRR with >20% MoM growth across at least 10 named SME logos within 9 months -- if Andustry shows me that curve, the catalyst + founder-graph thesis re-opens and I'll write the check at the markup.
