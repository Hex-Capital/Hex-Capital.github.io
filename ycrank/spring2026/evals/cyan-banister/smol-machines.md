# smol machines -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

BinBin He is a solo aerospace engineer who built a sub-200ms microVM runtime alone, which means smol machines maxes out my governance dimension and collapses on the one I care about most -- the magically-weird non-consensus filter, where E2B just took $21M from Insight and Daytona took $24M from FirstMark in February 2026 to chase the exact same AI-agent sandbox wedge. The signature is "single-operator systems craft inside a category the herd is already pricing." That's a profile I respect on the bench and almost never write a check into at pre-seed.

SpaceX in 2008 is my HIGH rung on unambiguous CEO authority -- one person owns the rocket, owns the spec, owns the firing call -- and smol machines clears that bar by structural default, because BinBin He is the only name on the YC page and the only committer on smol-machines/smolvm. The mechanism that made SpaceX legible to me was that Elon could not be out-voted on a 50/50 deadlock because there was no 50/50 deadlock to construct; smol machines inherits that same property cheaply. Where it diverges is the loneliness tax: SpaceX shipped because Musk recruited a deep bench within months, and the dossier shows smol machines is one human pushing v0.8.0 on May 26 2026 against Docker, Firecracker, Cloud Hypervisor, E2B, and Daytona simultaneously -- the governance dimension scores well precisely because there is nobody else, which is the same fact that worries me on execution.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I described in my pass example failed my filter because six tier-1 VCs were chasing it and the moat was the model itself; smol machines fails the same filter from the OSS direction, where 499 HN points and 3.4k GitHub stars are the dev-tool equivalent of "oversubscribed seed." The failure mechanism on the LOW rung is that when the category is already legible enough for Insight Partners to write a $21M Series A check into E2B and FirstMark to write $24M into Daytona, you are no longer "finding them before it becomes consensus" -- you are arriving on the same train. smol machines's positioning materials at particula.tech literally frame the product against E2B and Daytona, which means the wedge is defined by competitors who have 40x the capital. I would have wanted to see this product in April 2024, not April 2026.

Founder-lived-experience compounds or evaporates over 18 months on a single mechanism: does the founder's biography keep generating product decisions a competitor cannot copy because they did not live the same life? BinBin He's resume runs through aerospace trajectory tooling at Illinois Tech, Node.js concurrency speedups for NASA satellites, and serverless compute -- that's polymath-adjacent systems work, and the DeepMind exception tells me polymath compounding can override pedigree concerns. But the dossier never names the moment BinBin He *felt* the deployment-isolation pain; the LinkedIn says "Stealth Startup" and the smolmachines.com copy reads as a technical thesis, not an autobiography. Eighteen months from now the format wars will be won by whoever talked to a thousand agent-platform engineers and absorbed their friction; smol machines has one set of ears for that.

The counterfactual on the capital-intensive / unsexy-regulated dimension: smol machines would flip a tier if BinBin He were shipping a hardware appliance, a regulated-industry compliance wrapper, or a kernel-level primitive that required signing keys from Apple and Microsoft. Instead smol machines is Apache-2.0 licensed software running on top of libkrun, Hypervisor.framework, and KVM -- three open-source primitives any competent systems team can wrap, which is exactly the commoditization risk the dossier names. The frontier-hardware sleeve in my portfolio -- SpaceX 2008, Anduril 2017, Crusoe ~2018 -- earned its weight because the moat compounded with regulatory exposure, capex, or supplier relationships. smol machines has none of those; it has a clever file format.

On physical-world verifiability, the closest single-anchor parallel in my book is not Flock Safety -- which a normal human can photograph in a parking lot -- but the Niantic / Ingress moment, where I could actually feel the product pull me through a city block. smol machines is installable by a developer in about a minute and you can watch a microVM boot in 200ms, which is tactile in the dev-tools sense and a real signal compared to the pure-abstraction pass example. But it is not consumer-tactile; I cannot hand it to my bubbe. The 3.4k stars say developers can touch it, the 499 HN points say developers liked touching it, and that is the ceiling on the dimension.

My 36-month forecast: smol machines gets acqui-hired by E2B, Daytona, or HashiCorp for $5-15M between months 18 and 30, BinBin He becomes a principal engineer on the acquirer's microVM team, and the .smolmachine format does not become a standard. The mechanism is straightforward -- Apache-2.0 OSS with no pricing page, a solo operator competing with two $20M+ funded teams in the same wedge, and a hot acquirer market for systems talent in AI infrastructure means the equilibrium is talent absorption, not category victory.

The strongest case against my forecast leans on the DeepMind exception and the HQ Trivia exception together: BinBin He is a polymath compounding across aerospace, concurrency, and hypervisors, and the 499 HN points are tactile developer virality strong enough to override governance and category-consensus concerns -- exactly the override that pulled me into HQ Trivia in 2017. The bear-case-against-the-bull-case is that HQ Trivia's tactile pull was a normal-human pull (my mother played it), while smol machines's pull is bounded to a developer audience already being shoveled $45M of capital across two competitors; polymath compounding is real here, but the polymath has not yet articulated a contrarian origin story that explains *why him* on this specific problem. I would flip the verdict if BinBin He published, in his own voice, a specific lived moment of deployment-isolation pain that produced the .smolmachine format -- the Anduril-Luckey "I shipped Oculus from a garage" equivalent -- or if a named AI-agent platform (LangChain, CrewAI, OpenAI's agent runtime) publicly standardized on the .smolmachine artifact within the next two quarters.
