# HeyClicky -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

HeyClicky is a learning-rate story attached to an optimization play -- Farza Majeed pivoted out of buildspace's August 2024 wind-down into a Mac menu-bar overlay that pipes screenshots and transcripts through Claude, AssemblyAI, and ElevenLabs. The dimensional signature is unmistakable: his pivot is the strongest thing in the dossier and the consequence-of-success is the weakest. That pairing tells me exactly what HeyClicky is -- a founder I would take a meeting with on a different idea, attached to a product whose maximum-ambition world-state is a 30% better Figma onboarding experience.

On Technical/Capital Risk -- the lowest rung -- HeyClicky sits at the floor next to my Pass Example, the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer. The HIGH rung is **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where I led $115M Series A into magnet science that consensus VCs refused on capital risk. The MID rung is **Cognition** (2024), where the $175M round funded a deep-stack autonomous engineer, not an API call. HeyClicky's own README documents the architecture -- screenshot + transcript -> LLM -> TTS -- and the Windows clone repos (emreyilmaz46/clicky_windows, jasonkneen/openclicky) already exist. There is no miracle to fund. Any Tier-1 generalist can write the check; that is precisely why I should not.

On Founder Learning Rate -- the highest rung -- HeyClicky sits between the MID rung (**Physical Intelligence** 2024, Levine and Finn rotating from research into product) and the HIGH rung (**Slash** 2024, where the Kanye-pivot story justified my $100M Series C). Farza raised $10M Series A from a16z at $100M post for buildspace in November 2022 and watched it shut down in August 2024 -- that is a documented, public, costly thesis update, the kind of artifact I told Entrepreneur I rank "above everything else." HeyClicky is closer to Slash than to Physical Intelligence on this axis, and that is the only axis where it earns its way into the conversation at all.

On Consequence Magnitude, HeyClicky sits between my Pass Example and **DoorDash** (2013), much closer to the Pass Example. The HIGH rung is **OpenAI** (2019), where the success-state was civilizational and I wrote $50M against no revenue. The success-state for HeyClicky at maximum ambition is "users get faster at DaVinci Resolve and Figma." No incumbent regime collapses. The $15 trillion labor displacement frame I gave Fortune (2026-03-06) does not engage at the in-app-tutor layer; OpenAI's "Work with Apps" and Anthropic's "computer use" already ship the screen-aware substrate natively per the dottie.ai 2026 roundup. HeyClicky captures a workflow; it does not break an economic mechanism.

On Founder Defies Expert Consensus, HeyClicky sits at the LOW rung -- the "20 years at Salesforce" pattern from my TED Talk -- not because Farza is a career expert, but because his thesis ("the future of education and the future of AI interfaces," per his April 2026 X post) is continuity from buildspace, not defiance. The MID rung here is **Glean**'s Arvind Jain, a Known Exception where I waived the defiance test because capital scale and Fortune-500 information-asymmetry consequence carried the deal. HeyClicky has neither override available. On Capital Profile, HeyClicky sits well below the **OpenAI** $50M MID rung and the **Commonwealth Fusion** $115M HIGH rung -- a free, open-source consumer Mac app with no pricing page is exactly the deal where I am over-resourced and my check size distorts the cap table.

Thirty-six month forecast: HeyClicky ships a paid tier, fails to clear $3M ARR against OpenAI's native Mac agent, and either gets acqui-hired into an education platform sub-$30M or quietly winds down while Farza pivots into his third company -- the buildspace pattern repeating because the consequence-magnitude problem was never solved at the idea stage. The mechanism is straightforward: distribution from 89.3K X followers and 5.8k GitHub stars buys top-of-funnel, but the API-wrapper COGS stack of Claude + AssemblyAI + ElevenLabs against an OS-native free substitute crushes the unit economics before brand can compound.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash** (2024) precedent -- a Known Exception where I overrode the consequence-magnitude prior because the founder's documented pivot demonstrated learning rate strong enough to carry a YC-pedigreed consumer bet. Farza fits the shape: prior raise, public wind-down, second YC company, real distribution. But Slash had a paying customer base and a Series C ARR ramp when I wrote the check; HeyClicky has zero revenue, zero pricing, and a forkable codebase the founder himself open-sourced in April 2026. The Slash override required learning-rate plus a concrete monetization mechanism; HeyClicky offers learning-rate alone. The flip condition is specific: if HeyClicky reaches $5M ARR on a paid education-vertical SKU with >50% twelve-month retention inside eighteen months, the dossier rewrites itself and I take a follow-on look at Farza's next round. Until then, this is the McKinsey-cellphone forecast inverted -- a product the experts will love because it extrapolates the present, not because it breaks anything.
