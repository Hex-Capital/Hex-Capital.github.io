# Tenet Industries -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Tenet Industries is a schlep-rich, pain-poor company: the highest rung Tenet Industries earns is on the schlep dimension — Swedish ISP dual-use export controls, injection-mold tooling, defense procurement cycles — and the lowest is on organic founder pain, since Hugo Frisk, Fabian Andersson, and Emil Falk are KTH/SSE students who read the Ukraine war from a Stockholm classroom rather than founders who personally needed a 3,000 SEK kamikaze quadcopter. That pairing — high schlep, low founder-problem fit — is the entire analytical signature of Tenet Industries in my notebook, and it's the pairing that historically separates a Coinbase from a clever defense-tech pitch.

On the schlep ladder, **Coinbase** (2012) is the HIGH rung — Brian Armstrong walked toward bitcoin compliance precisely because it was the part of the work nobody else wanted, and that grind became the moat. Tenet Industries is genuinely close to that rung: Swedish ISP licensing, EU dual-use rules, Ukrainian end-user certification, injection-mold capex, part-count reduction from 30 to 14 using plastic casting and milling rather than 3D printing — these are not the schleps an AI-wrapper applicant in Spring 2026 is choosing. Where Tenet Industries diverges from the Coinbase rung is that Armstrong had personally tried to move bitcoin and hated the experience; the Tenet Industries dossier shows no founder who personally needed to procure a drone, only a market they correctly identified as expensive at ~10,000 SEK per US-component unit. The schlep is real. The schlep was not chosen because it was personal.

The MBA-deck pass case is my reference for the organic-pain failure mode — a team that read a market rather than lived a problem. Tenet Industries does not look like that team — Emil Falk's prior roles at Falks Metall AB and WindowGlass Recycling Sweden AB give him genuine metals/manufacturing exposure, and Frisk's LinkedIn self-description "Mass production" is at least honest about where his head is. But Frisk is still a student, Andersson is still a student, and the founder narrative reads as "Ukraine consumption rates outstrip Western production, here is our wedge" — that is competitive analysis, not Drew Houston losing a USB stick. The closest thing in the Tenet Industries dossier to a personal-pain origin is Falk's metals background, which is upstream of the *solution* (DFM, injection molding), not upstream of the *problem* (needing a cheap drone in a contested airspace).

**Airbnb** (2009)'s cereal boxes are my one-anchor parallel on animal resourcefulness — revenue conjured from a stunt nobody else would think of, before the institutional check. Tenet Industries has shipped two named products, Midsummer and TenetTX, in roughly five months from incorporation, and is reportedly already in discussions with Swedish defense buyers and Ukrainian actors — that is not nothing for a 4-5 person team across Stockholm and San Francisco. But "discussions" is the word the dossier uses, not "signed," and there is no Tenet Industries equivalent of the $40 cereal box — no unscalable revenue stunt, no rejected-then-rescued application, no Hacker News demo that flipped me. The animal tracks are faint.

Mechanism analysis on hacker genealogy: over the next 18 months, defense-drone competition compounds on the side that can iterate firmware, autonomy stacks, and electronic-warfare resilience faster than the cost curve compresses — and Tenet Industries enters that race with zero public GitHub footprint across Frisk, Andersson, or Falk, and no shipped open-source artifact or hacker-community presence. Patrick Collison had the Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz had the RSS spec before **Reddit** (2005). The Tenet Industries dossier offers "Mass production" as a LinkedIn headline. KTH is a real engineering school, and the DFM thesis is technically credible, but mass-producing 3,000 SEK airframes does not by itself produce the kind of software moat that survives a Helsing whose HX-2 has a multi-year autonomy head start.

Counterfactual on co-founder configuration: for the Tenet Industries trio to flip from neutral to a HIGH-rung **Airbnb** (2009)-style team on this axis, I would need evidence the three roomed or built together for two-plus years before Tenet — a robotics club at KTH, a prior project, anything. The dossier says they are all KTH/SSE students, which is suggestive but not load-bearing; three founders who met in the same lecture hall last semester are not the same as Chesky/Gebbia from RISD plus Blecharczyk. Tenet Industries probably clears the bar — three founders, complementary, geographically split but not solo — without distinguishing itself on it.

My 36-month forecast on Tenet Industries: a Swedish defense framework contract or Ukrainian aid-procurement order lands them under €5M ARR by mid-2028 and Helsing's HX-2 production ramp plus its ~€830M war chest forces Tenet Industries into either a niche sub-segment (FPV-only, Nordic-only) or a tuck-in acquisition by a larger European prime — Saab or Helsing itself — at a modest multiple. The mechanism is the signature: high schlep without organic pain produces companies that execute the unglamorous work competently but get outflanked by a competitor whose founders are *obsessed* rather than *correctly analytical*, because in defense the procurement cycle is long enough that obsession outlasts thesis.

The strongest case against me: **Airbnb** (2009) is a Known Exception precisely because I rejected the idea and the founders overrode my judgment with a stunt, and one could argue Tenet Industries' DFM number — 30 parts to 14, 10,000 SEK to 3,000 SEK — is the stunt-equivalent that should override my "they're students, not soldiers" skepticism the way the cereal boxes overrode my couch-surfing skepticism. The bull mechanism is that schlep is the moat, Helsing is building higher-end systems, Anduril is American with ITAR friction in European procurement, and a sovereign-European 3,000 SEK attritable drone with real metals-manufacturing DNA from Falks Metall is genuinely contrarian. What dismantles it for me is that the Airbnb override required a *demonstrated* stunt I could touch — 1,000 cereal boxes sold at $40 — and Tenet Industries' 3,000 SEK number is a *target*, not a shipped-unit price with a Swedish defense purchase order behind it. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed contract or firm LOI with a named counterparty — Swedish FMV, the Ukrainian Ministry of Defense, or a prime like Saab — at the 3,000 SEK unit price for a meaningful volume before the Spring 2026 batch demos. Until then, Tenet Industries is a Neutral.
