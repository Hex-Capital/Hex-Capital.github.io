# LATO -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

LATO is an optimization play sold to the people who write my checks, which is the most diagnostic sentence I can write about it. Tymek Staniszewski spent six years at Verdane's ~$11B AUM and is now building a tool to make commercial diligence cheaper for funds like Verdane — the success-state of LATO is that VC and PE associates stop paying Bain $500k for customer-interview studies and pay LATO $50k instead. That is a real business. It is not a civilizational variable. The signature here is a relatively-highest score on Consequence only because LATO brushes against AlphaSense's $600M ARR adjacent category, paired with a near-floor on Technical Risk and Capital Match — the exact profile my Pass Example was written about.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence because the success-state was "intelligence itself becomes a utility" — I wrote $50M into a no-revenue, unconventional-structure entity because that world-state was civilizationally distinct from the one we lived in. LATO's success-state, even at maximum ambition, is "diligence cycles compress from six weeks to six days and Bain loses a $500k-$1M line item per engagement" (TechCrunch DiligenceSquared, Mar 2026). That is the **DoorDash** (2013) shape at best — workflow compression in a real but bounded category — and DoorDash had a $72B IPO in part because consumer commerce is structurally larger than VC research budgets. LATO never collapses an incumbent regime; it routes around AlphaSense+Tegus for the subset of diligence that requires *new* interviews rather than archived ones. My Fortune 2026-03-06 frame of "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" does not load here — the labor being displaced is expert-network call-takers and McKinsey associates, which is a rounding error on that figure.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) failed because the capital plan understated the burn required for the science to work, and the science was the moat. LATO has the inverse problem: no science, no required burn. The "market simulation that predicts participant behavior" claim on latolabs.io is the one place the dossier waves at a technical miracle, but it is research-flavored without being falsifiable — there is no published accuracy metric, no benchmark, no named physics. Bridgetown Research ($19M Series A from Accel and Lightspeed, Feb 2026) is executing the same AI-voice-interview thesis with roughly 38x LATO's YC default capital, and the gap is not technical, it is GTM tempo. When the differentiator is "we built the agent first," consensus VCs do fund it — Accel already did. That is the tell that LATO is not my edge.

On Founder Defies Expert Consensus, the mechanism that compounds over 18 months is whether the founder is willing to publicly contradict the field that trained them. Tymek's six years at Verdane is buyer-side exposure, which is useful for sales cycles — but the dossier shows no public position where Tymek has argued *against* the orthodoxy of his prior employer. The brother-co-founded-ElevenLabs note is a network artifact, not a defiance signal. Tien Chu's eight years of ML/SWE with @chutien at 8 repos and no notable stars is competent engineering continuity, not the Sergey Levine / Chelsea Finn profile from **Physical Intelligence** (2024) where the founders had publicly committed to controversial bets in their field before raising. My TED Talk citation of the UC Berkeley study — 28,000 expert predictions "only slightly more accurate than chance" — cuts both ways here: I do not weight the Verdane pedigree as a positive, and there is no defiance evidence to override it.

Counterfactually on Learning Rate, the score flips a tier only if the dossier surfaces a documented pivot — the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C into a YC-backed fintech. LATO's dossier shows a linear plan from inception: voice interviews, market simulation, sold to VC funds, four named pilot customers (Blume Equity, FoodLabs, Montis VC, Concept Ventures). No "we were wrong about X" moment, no prior company sold, no public thesis update from either founder. At pre-seed I do not penalize aggressively for this — short company history is a fair excuse — but I also cannot reward what is not there.

Compared on Capital Match to **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where my $115M Series A lead was the deployment scale no consensus VC would touch, LATO is at YC default $500K with commodity SaaS economics and a path to $1M ARR on under $5M. I am over-resourced for this deal by roughly two orders of magnitude. The capital-intensity signature that justifies a Khosla check — fleet, plant, foundation model training, drug pipeline — is absent. Any Tier-1 generalist funds this; the fact that Accel and Lightspeed already funded Bridgetown is the market clearing this exact thesis.

My 36-month forecast: LATO reaches $1-3M ARR on the VC/PE buyer base, gets squeezed between Bridgetown Research's better-capitalized GTM and an AlphaSense-shipped AI-voice-interview module on the $600M ARR distribution rail, and is acquired by AlphaSense or Bridgetown for under $40M by mid-2029, or quietly shut down. The mechanism is straightforward — when the moat is "we built the agent first" and a direct competitor has 38x your capital plus tier-1 boards, the outcome compresses to acquihire.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash** (2024) exception: YC-pedigreed, founder embedded in the buyer category, capital-efficient, and the Tymek-brother-ElevenLabs node could surface unusual distribution. Slash overrode my consequence-magnitude prior because of a documented pivot demonstrating learning rate, plus YC pedigree. LATO has the YC pedigree but not the pivot — the dossier is silent on any thesis update, and the "Built in Europe 100 selected founders" feature is a marketing artifact, not a pivot. The bull case stings only if LATO ships a market-simulation layer with a falsifiable accuracy claim that Bridgetown cannot replicate within two quarters. That is the verifiable flip condition: a published, third-party-validated prediction-accuracy benchmark on a real market outcome, plus one PE LOI at $500K+ ACV. Absent both, this is the GPT-4-sales-call-summarizer of investor tooling, and I pass in roughly the 30 seconds my Pass Example specifies.
