# ReasonBlocks -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

ReasonBlocks is what happens when you take two genuinely impressive childhood friends — Sajeev Magesh and Rohan Vij, building together since second grade per the dossier — and point them at the most consensus-saturated category in venture right now. The signature is a real founder profile aimed at a target the AI kill test eats alive: middleware that sits in the agent call path next to LangSmith, Braintrust, Langfuse, Helicone, and Latitude. Sane founders, sane idea, hot category. That's not the asymmetry I'm looking for.

The AI kill test ladder is where ReasonBlocks lives or dies, and ReasonBlocks dies on it. High rung: Metropolis, where the parking infrastructure is physical and AI amplifies but cannot replicate the asset — the seed is now ~$5B because software couldn't eat it. Low rung: every AI copilot wrapper in the YC batch where Adobe ships parity in eighteen months. ReasonBlocks sits at the low rung, full stop. The dossier itself flags it: "Two-engineer teams at AI-observability incumbents or model labs can plausibly build runtime-intervention layers." Langfuse just got bought by Clickhouse in January 2026. LangSmith already owns the trace data that would feed any "private reasoning library." Braintrust runs the eval surface. ReasonBlocks's runtime intervention is a feature on someone else's roadmap, and that someone else has the customer relationship and the trace pipe already plumbed in.

Sanity-Insanity is where ReasonBlocks scores its best — and it still isn't enough. High rung: Raj Dittmar at Solana, an engineer I'd watched through three companies who calmly explained why Ethereum's win was overstated. Low rung: synthetic co-founders matched at a networking event. ReasonBlocks sits well above the low rung — Sajeev with Stanford CS, USAMO, a *Scientific Reports* paper, Rohan with CMU AI and ENGIE distributed-energy work, eleven years of shared history since second grade. That's not synthetic. That's a real bond, and the academic credentialing is legit. But "sane person, insane idea" requires the *idea* to be insane. ReasonBlocks's idea is the most sensible idea any pair of CS kids could pick in 2026: "infra for AI agents." The sanity is there. The insanity is missing.

Consensus Mispricing — Solana at four cents is the high rung; the YC AI-copilot wrapper batch is the low rung; ReasonBlocks sits much closer to the wrappers. Five named venture-backed competitors in the dossier, every observability seed funded, this is the hottest seed category in software. There is no asymmetry left in the price. Primitive Creation — Venmo as the verb is high, Slack as the workplace-messaging primitive is mid, "better CRM" is low. ReasonBlocks's "private reasoning library that grows with every run" has primitive-flavor in the way a feature has flavor; it's a cache plus a learning loop, not a new behavior. Nobody will say "ReasonBlocks me." Capital Discipline — Gusto high, Allbirds factory-farm DTC low. ReasonBlocks is two engineers, "Hiring: False" in the YC data — that's actually disciplined, and the only dimension where ReasonBlocks doesn't actively contradict my framework. Default-plus.

Thirty-six months out: ReasonBlocks gets acqui-hired by Braintrust, LangChain, or a model lab for $15-40M after one of them ships runtime-intervention as a feature SKU and the SWE-bench Pro numbers stop being a differentiator. The mechanism is straightforward — incumbents own the trace data that ReasonBlocks needs to compound its reasoning library, and the founders are good enough hires that the acquisition math works long before the company math does. The 90% repeat usage at "top Series B companies" is a search-snippet claim with no named customer, so I'm not pricing it in.

The bull case stings, and I want to name it honestly. It looks like Clubhouse — a moment where a genuine new behavior is being born and Lessin's durability rule should be overridden. The argument: production multi-step agents are a real new operational surface, the per-customer reasoning library is a compounding data asset that read-only observability tools structurally cannot build, and the SWE-bench Pro 42% accuracy lift is executed work that competitors will need quarters to match. If ReasonBlocks compounds customer-specific reasoning faster than LangSmith can bolt on parity, the data asset becomes the moat. But Clubhouse was a *consumer social behavior* — synchronous audio at scale during a pandemic. ReasonBlocks is *coding-agent middleware* benchmarked on a single workload, SWE-bench Pro, with no public evidence the gains transport to sales, support, or research agents. That's not a primitive being born. That's a feature being optimized. The verdict flips if ReasonBlocks publishes verifiable named-customer ARR above $1M with cross-vertical adoption (not just coding agents) in the next two quarters — that would mean the reasoning library is compounding faster than the incumbents can ship parity. Until then: pass.
