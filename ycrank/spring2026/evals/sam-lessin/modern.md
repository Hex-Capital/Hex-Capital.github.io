# Modern -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Modern is a competent repeat-founder pitch — Seb Poole sold Frankie Health to Unmind in February 2023 — running directly into the one category I have publicly redlined: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical]" at YC seed pricing. The dimensional signature is exactly that pairing: the founder line is the strongest thing in the dossier, and the sleeve is the thing I won't write into at consensus terms. That is not a coincidence — it is the modal 2026 YC archetype, where the operator credentials are real but the round is the Pass Example I wrote down out loud before I ever saw Modern.

Solana at $0.05 in 2018 is what the non-consensus rung actually looks like — everyone had quit the category, the price reflected that, and the entry absorbed the next decade of markup. Modern is the opposite picture on the same axis: ServiceNow paid $2.1B for Moveworks, Serval is already at a $1B mark on $127M raised, Atomicwork has $40.3M from Khosla and Blume, and Aaron Epstein is partnering a 2-person team into that wall. The shared mechanism with Solana is "where is the crowd standing" — and on Modern the crowd is standing on top of the wedge with twenty times Modern's capital and named Sequoia / Redpoint logos doing the bidding. There is no price discount in the dossier to offset that; this is a consensus round in a post-consensus category, and the regatta tack count to even reach the Series A starting line against Serval's balance sheet is the tell.

The Pass Example I wrote — "$25K MRR, three months out of YC, $30M post-money, four tier-1 VCs already in" — fails because the SAFE cap cannot generate alpha against the factory-model competitor set. Modern's cap structure looks identical in mechanism even though the dossier doesn't disclose a post: a 2-person SF team with YC-standard funding will need five-plus rounds to clear the enterprise sales cycle Poole himself ran at Frankie, where the named customers (Disney, Uber, MLB) took quarters not weeks to close. The Teamshares-style structural alpha is absent — Modern is not a rollup, not an employee-equity vehicle, not a royalty on Poole's distribution; it is a generic per-seat enterprise SaaS pretending the $11.91B ITSM TAM line item makes the cap table venture-shaped when in fact the cap table needs to survive ServiceNow shipping native agents on the same quarterly cadence.

Mechanism analysis on Founder Conviction: Poole's Frankie-to-Unmind arc is the load-bearing asset in Modern's dossier — repeat founder, enterprise logos, prior exit — and that does pull the score off the default-6. What it does not do is compound over the next 18 months in this category, because there is no public writing from Poole on ITSM, no opinionated thread on why Moveworks failed inside ServiceNow, no Wizard-of-Oz evidence that Modern ran tickets manually for six months before automating. Aleks Tomovski's Wix Code and Canva developer-platform background is the kind of platform-engineer depth I want — but "Stealth" on his LinkedIn and zero GitHub means I have no pre-pitch artifacts to underwrite missionary temperament on, only resume.

Counterfactual on Founder-as-Brand: for Modern to flip from the bottom of this dimension into the John Fish / Bookshelved zone would require Poole or Tomovski to own a measurable IT-leader audience — a CIO newsletter, an r/sysadmin presence, a podcast — pre-existing the round by two years. Neither has a Twitter account I can find, no GitHub footprint, no published essays. The dossier explicitly notes "No public data found" on follower counts, Product Hunt, press. The Slow Creator Fund framing does not rescue this — Modern is not a creator dressed as a startup, it is the inverse, a B2B sales motion with zero owned distribution attempting to out-execute a $127M-funded competitor on outbound. Modern will pay for every customer in cash, and the cash is not there.

Single-anchor parallel on Category Sleeve: the analog in my own portfolio is the Joe Lonsdale podcast line — "AI is just more internet" — operationalized as "the seed price of an AI Series B is not a venture moment." Modern is the cleanest possible instance: agentic LLM workflows on top of ServiceNow, sold per-seat, indistinguishable in category from Serval and Atomicwork and Aisera and Leena and Rezolve and Kore. Sublime Security is the counter-example inside my own list — that one I bought because the security wedge was pre-consolidation; Modern is post-consolidation by the day ServiceNow closed Moveworks. The sleeve is not just neutral here, it is actively the one I have the loudest public position against.

Modern in 36 months: acqui-hired into Freshworks, Atlassian JSM, or a mid-market ITSM consolidator for sub-$75M, with Poole landing a VP role and Tomovski moving to a platform team — most likely path; secondary path is a pivot into vertical IT for healthcare given Poole's Frankie network, sub-$3M ARR, raising a hard down-round Series A by late 2027. The mechanism producing that forecast is the exact pairing in the signature: Poole's enterprise-sales credibility is precisely what makes Modern acquirable rather than dead, while the sleeve density is what makes it unable to compound to a standalone outcome against Serval's war chest.

The strongest objection is that Poole is a known executor and Modern's 62%-ticket-reduction / 80%-automation customer numbers are the kind of early signal that, in 2019, would have read as Hinge-Health-shaped — a repeat founder grinding enterprise logos until the category bends. Ro is the Known Exception that maps closest: high-burn, contested category, won by capital intensity becoming the moat. Ro does not apply to Modern — Ro had 50-state prescription rails as the actual schlep; Modern's "moat today" line is SOC 2, ISO 27001, and a generic integrations list that every competitor in the dossier ships. There is no regulatory clearance, no licensing schlep, no illegible asset that justifies the capital-intensity override. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Modern raises this round below $8M post with no tier-1 co-investor, AND Poole publishes a public teardown of why Moveworks-inside-ServiceNow is structurally underperforming, AND I can verify 6+ months of paid manual ticket-resolution delivery before the agent layer. None of those three are in the dossier. Pass.
