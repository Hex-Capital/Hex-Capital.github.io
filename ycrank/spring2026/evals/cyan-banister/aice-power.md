# AICE Power -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

AICE Power is two French aerospace-and-embedded engineers — Grégoire Chomette (MIT AeroAstro, NASA asteroid risk) and Rémi Bouteiller (ENS Paris-Saclay embedded systems, Amira Health wearable for hot flashes) — who executed a 120° pivot from building-energy sensors to submarine defense drones on May 16, 2026, six weeks before YC Spring 2026. The dimensional signature here is brutal in its own way: AICE Power sits perfectly inside my capital-intensive frontier-hardware lane and almost entirely outside my founder-surfaced-from-lived-experience lane. It is a category bet without a founder bet, which is the inverse of the trade I actually make.

Anduril-Luckey 2017 is the load-bearing analogy on capital-intensity, and AICE Power's posture passes that test cleanly — subsea hardware with ITAR exposure, France-to-U.S. manufacturing route per aicepower.com, naval procurement cycles, the whole texture of a domain other investors call "too hard or too unsexy." The mechanism that made Anduril work was that Luckey had already shipped Oculus to scale from a garage; the hardware-shipping muscle was real before the defense pivot. AICE Power diverges from the HIGH rung precisely there: the prior product was an energy-sensor company that reached "six figures in ARR" per Bouteiller's portfolio site and then was abandoned. The category fit is Anduril; the shipping trajectory is not.

HQ Trivia 2017's failure mechanism on CEO authority was that Yusupov and Kroll were peer co-founders with no tiebreaker, and the feud eventually ate the company. That pattern is not what I see at AICE Power — the YC page and LinkedIn both name Grégoire Chomette as CEO with Bouteiller as Engineering co-founder, which is the clean asymmetric structure I want at signing. With a 2-person team and explicit role differentiation, AICE Power passes the screen that GameCrush ~2011 failed; whatever blows this company up, it will not be unresolved CEO authority.

Founder-lived-experience is where AICE Power's mechanism compounds against it over 18 months. Defense procurement is a cleared-relationships game — DIU, SBIR, Navy program offices — and the social license that Chase Lochmiller had to walk on a North Dakota well pad has no analog in Chomette's NASA-asteroid-risk biography or Bouteiller's hot-flash wearable. With Anduril at a $60B valuation closing $4B in March 2026 and Saronic at $9.25B closing $1.75B the same month, the cleared-relationship gap widens with every quarter AICE Power spends learning what a Navy program manager actually buys. The lived-experience deficit does not stay constant; it accrues interest.

Counterfactually on magically-weird: for AICE Power's score to flip a tier on this dimension, two named tier-1 funds would have to have visibly passed on aesthetic or capital-intensity grounds and Chomette would have to articulate the misread. The dossier shows the opposite — submarine defense drones in 2026 is consensus weird, not actual weird. With 41.24% UUV market share forecast for naval/coastal defense per Globe Newswire May 2026 and Anduril, Saronic, Saildrone, HavocAI, Kongsberg, Saab, Teledyne, L3Harris all named in the same space, AICE Power is not the magically-weird trade. It is the obvious trade dressed in YC swarm-AI tags.

Flock Safety ~2018 is the single anchor on physical-world verifiability — I read the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café and could later see cameras on telephone poles in real neighborhoods. AICE Power offers no parallel artifact: aicepower.com lists only "100m–600m depth markers" with no prototype, no demo, no hull photograph, no pool test, no recorded swarm coordination. I cannot touch, photograph, or hand this to a friend; I have to take the founder's word that subsea hardware is being built six weeks after a 120° pivot.

The 36-month forecast for AICE Power: pivots a second time — most likely toward commercial subsea pipeline inspection or offshore-wind cable monitoring where Bouteiller's actual embedded-sensor stack from the prior product partly compounds — raises a small bridge, lands between $0 and $500K ARR, and either gets acqui-hired by Anduril or Saronic under $15M or dies. The mechanism producing this is the lived-experience gap collected over 36 months of DoD procurement cycles burning cash a 2-person YC-funded team cannot outlast against a $60B incumbent that just closed $4B.

The strongest bull case stings: Palmer Luckey did not have naval experience either when Anduril started — he had VR — and the Anduril template is precisely "polymath hardware shipper pivots into defense." Chomette's MIT AeroAstro work on deep learning for jet-engine signals is real autonomy research, and Replicator-era contracting genuinely rewards small fast hardware teams. The dismantling: Luckey shipped Oculus to scale before pivoting; the AICE Power founders pivoted *because* the prior product had stalled at six figures ARR, which is the inverse trajectory. Pivots from strength compound; pivots from stagnation usually compound twice. AICE Power is the latter. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a working autonomous swarm of three or more subsea drones operating below 100m within 12 months AND a named DIU, SBIR, or allied-navy counterparty on a signed contract — at that point the lived experience is being built in real time and I will revisit.
