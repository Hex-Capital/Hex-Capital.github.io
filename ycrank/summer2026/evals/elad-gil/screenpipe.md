# screenpipe -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Louis Beaumont's prior Mediar — SAP data-entry automation via screen input — is the exact rehearsal for screenpipe's screen-capture-to-Pipes thesis, and that founder-market rehearsal is the strongest dimension in the dossier. The weakest is revenue velocity: 250k users and 19k+ GitHub stars are engagement snapshots, not the $3B→$5B→$8B curve I paid up for at Harvey. Screenpipe reads to me as a shipping-fluent solo founder in a genuinely catalyzed local-first-capture window whose paid revenue curve has not yet declared whether this is a Rewind-shaped consumer-memory story or a real enterprise privacy wedge — and until it declares, I can't write the Perplexity-shape check the dossier keeps gesturing at.

Aravind at Perplexity (2022) is the HIGH rung on founder-ships-between-meetings — he'd cold-ping me on LinkedIn and show up a week later with the thing built. Screenpipe's Louis Beaumont sits close to that rung but not on it: the Mediar → screenpipe arc, 19k+ stars on a Rust codebase, 48+ shipped integrations (Slack, Gmail, Linear, Zoom, Salesforce, HubSpot), and a proprietary on-device PII-scrubbing model are the exact artifacts I want to see from a pre-seed founder. Where screenpipe diverges from the Perplexity rung is that Aravind was the sole final decision-maker inside a co-founded structure that eventually consolidated; screenpipe is *literally* solo per the YC page with a team of six, which is the shipping-throughput ceiling I flag in *High Growth Handbook* — one founder cannot cover macOS + Windows + Linux + 48 integrations + an on-device model + enterprise sales indefinitely.

An AI company with no MoM growth curve disclosed is "something's fundamentally broken" per Ferriss #863, and that's the LOW rung's failure mechanism — the exact reason I passed on a dozen 2024 AI startups whose only traction slide was logo salad. Screenpipe's dossier tests uncomfortably against that pattern: the customer logos (Microsoft, Google, NVIDIA, Intel, Shopify, Atlassian, Datadog, Adobe, Salesforce, Thomson Reuters, MIT, Stanford, Harvard) are almost certainly individual seats on a free tier, not procurement-signed enterprise contracts, and the dossier explicitly notes "no publicly disclosed signed enterprise contracts" on the $150/seat tier. 250k users at $0 is not the Harvey signal; it's Rewind's pre-acquisition shape, and Rewind ended at $8.7M ARR before Meta absorbed it. Until screenpipe puts a paid-seat MoM number on the page, the velocity dimension prices as Rewind-adjacent, not Harvey-adjacent.

*Mechanism analysis on the catalyst dimension.* Rewind's desktop app disabled Dec 19, 2025 after the Meta acquisition, plus Microsoft Recall's repeated security-researcher disclosures one year post-launch, plus Granola's $1.5B Mar 2026 raise pushing into enterprise — that's a specific 12-month re-opening for local-first, source-available, cross-OS always-on capture, and screenpipe is positioned squarely in the gap. Over 18 months this catalyst compounds if screenpipe converts the Rewind-orphan cohort into paid seats before Recall ships a Mac/Linux port or Meta re-releases Rewind under new branding; it decays if Microsoft closes the OS gap or if the enterprise privacy narrative gets absorbed by Granola's expansion path. This is a real catalyst — closer to Anduril-post-Ukraine than to Lyft-C — but the window is narrower than defense modernization was in 2022.

*Counterfactual on the index-position dimension.* Screenpipe today is a productivity app that must beat Recall on OS coverage and beat Granola on scope; that is a share-fight, not a Stripe-shaped index. For screenpipe to sit in the actual workflow path of a compounding category, the Pipes layer would have to become the default memory/action substrate that every third-party desktop agent (Anthropic's computer-use, OpenAI's operator agents, Adept-successors) reads from and writes to — a *personal-agent MCP*, effectively. If that were the framing in the dossier, with even one signed agent-vendor integration, screenpipe's index score flips from mid-single-digits to double-digits and I'm running the Decagon comparison. It isn't in the dossier, so I score what's on the page.

*Single-anchor parallel on the miracle dimension.* Color Genomics (2015) worked because we isolated the miracle to workflow-on-commodity-sequencing and refused to make chemistry bets; screenpipe's dossier requires me to underwrite trusted always-on capture + a working on-device PII model + Pipes-that-ROI + platform survival against Microsoft's $0-marginal-cost Recall — that's not one miracle, and the Copilot+ PC substitution risk is exactly the "capitalization + platform + market" triple that keeps me out of most science projects. Screenpipe is not a science project, but the miracle architecture is closer to Anduril's software-on-defense-distribution bet than to Decagon's single-capability bet, and I only wrote Anduril at growth stage after contracts were compounding visibly.

*Forecast.* In 36 months, screenpipe is either acqui-hired by Notion, Atlassian, or an observability/MDM vendor (Datadog is the sharpest fit given the enterprise-privacy overlap) in the $60–180M range, or it settles as an independent mid-market privacy-first Recall alternative at $4–9M ARR, gated below $25M ARR by Recall bundling and by a Meta-relaunched Rewind consumer product. The mechanism producing that ceiling is the exact high/low signature: a shipping-fluent solo founder can execute the wedge, but a paid-seat curve that hasn't yet declared cannot outrun a free OS-bundled substitute over three years.

*Strongest objection.* The bull case is that screenpipe is the Perplexity-2022 template hiding in plain sight — cold-inbound founder, prior shipping artifact (Mediar), a category re-opened by a specific 12-month catalyst (Rewind-disabled + Recall-insecure), and 250k users is a real usage signal peers are misreading the way they misread Stripe's early developer count. If Louis is *actually* shipping Pipes weekly and the agent-memory framing is the true category, screenpipe becomes the index on personal-agent workflow the same way Stripe became the index on e-commerce. That case stings — I've made this exact misread before. What dismantles it here is that Stripe in 2010 didn't have Microsoft shipping a competitor at $0 marginal cost on every new PC, and Perplexity in 2022 wasn't a solo founder against Meta's acquired incumbent; screenpipe faces both simultaneously with no disclosed paid-seat MoM. The verifiable condition that flips me to invest: screenpipe discloses >20% MoM paid-seat growth *or* one signed Fortune 500 Enterprise contract at the $150/seat tier within 90 days — either would convert the usage snapshot into "just working," and I'd write the check at consensus.
