# Touchmark -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Touchmark is a TypeScript SDK that scores LLM outputs and adjusts the bill -- two founders, Ilia Bolgov out of six-plus years of Product at Revolut and Roman Yanushevskyi out of two Citadel Securities quant internships, dropped into the most-contested billing-infra lane of 2026, where Stripe just paid $1B for Metronome in December 2025 and Orb is already metering for Vercel, Replit, Pinecone, and Perplexity. The signature is a company with a genuinely adjacent-credentialed pair pointed at the exact opposite of "too hard or too unsexy" -- a pure-software billing wrapper in a category where five funded incumbents and an AGPLv3 open-source competitor (Lago) are already standing. The founder math is the strongest thing in the dossier and the category math is the weakest, and that pairing is, in my book, the structure of a pass.

Palmer Luckey at **Anduril** in 2017 is the high rung on lived-experience -- he had already shipped Oculus from his parents' garage and *then* walked into defense with an artifact under his arm. Bolgov is not that, but he is not the consensus YC darling either; running Product for ~6y9m at Revolut means he has actually watched a usage-priced financial product get billed, disputed, and refunded at scale, which is the closest analogue to Touchmark's "auto-refund when output falls below baseline" mechanism that the dossier offers. Yanushevskyi's Citadel QR internships and his Lovable AI Engineer stint give the eval-stack side some real fingertips. Where Touchmark diverges from the Luckey template is that neither founder built a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain before -- Bolgov was a PM inside someone else's billing rails, not the operator who shipped them, and that is the difference between *of* the place and *visiting* it.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling is the low rung on "magically weird," and Touchmark has the inverted problem of that pass example: not too many VCs chasing it, but too many funded competitors already sitting in the chair. Metronome at $128M raised and now inside Stripe, Orb at ~$44M with Mayfield's Series B, Lago at $34M from FirstMark and open-source AGPLv3, Solvimon at $9.6M from Northzone -- the round Touchmark is trying to raise into is the round where four prior incumbents have already drawn the map. "Magically weird" requires that two or three credible investors have publicly passed for aesthetic reasons; here the smart money has *funded* the adjacent thesis four times over. Output-quality scoring on top of metering is a feature, not a category, and Stripe Billing can ship it on a Tuesday.

On single-CEO authority -- the **HQ Trivia** 2017 lesson is that Yusupov and Kroll never resolved the tiebreaker and the feud helped kill an app I genuinely loved playing. Touchmark's dossier shows two "Co-Founder @ Touchmark (YC S26)" titles on LinkedIn, no CEO designation, no shared employer or university across Revolut/Imperial and Citadel/Lovable/NUS in any search result, and "no public data on co-founder history." That is the exact shape of the conversation the founders have not yet had, and the absence of a documented operating agreement at pre-seed is when the conversation is cheapest to have. I am not going to predict feud, but I am going to apply the default and note that the structural pattern is there.

On capital-intensity and unsexy-regulated swing -- this is where Touchmark sits furthest from where my checks land. **SpaceX** in 2008 was rockets, **Anduril** in 2017 was drones, **Crusoe** circa 2018 was burning flared gas on well pads, **Vigil Labs** in August 2025 was regulated finance. Touchmark is a TypeScript SDK in B2B infrastructure with no hardware, no regulatory perimeter, no domain that compounds defensibility with time. The moat described in the dossier -- a cross-customer eval dataset -- is exactly the kind of moat that does *not* compound when LLM-as-judge frameworks are open-source and any of the four incumbents can wrap one in a quarter. If Touchmark were billing dialysis clinics or fertility platforms by outcome, this score would be different.

Physical-world verifiability -- **Flock Safety** sourced from a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons, **Niantic** sourced from my own Ingress play since 2012, **Postmates** because the delivery happened in front of me. The Touchmark counterfactual is that for this dimension to flip a tier I would need a tactile artifact -- a public dashboard, a live eval scoreboard, a customer who would let me watch a refund hit a real invoice in real time. What the dossier actually has is a touchmark.ai landing page that returned minimal content on WebFetch, no public pricing page, no Product Hunt launch, no named customer logo. The SDK is precisely the developer-tools abstraction my pass example flags: I cannot install it, photograph it, or hand it to a friend at a dinner table.

My 36-month read on Touchmark is acqui-hire into Orb or Stripe (via Metronome) for under $20M in talent value, or quiet wind-down -- the eval-pricing layer becomes a checkbox feature in Stripe Billing by mid-2027 and Touchmark's two-person team gets absorbed onto whichever incumbent moves first, with Yanushevskyi most likely landing on a Stripe ML team. The mechanism is straightforward: when the category's distribution sits inside an incumbent who just paid $1B for the adjacent primitive, the greenfield path Touchmark needs is closed before they ship, and "average to zero" revenue-neutrality removes the upside lever that would have made AI platforms switch.

The strongest bull case is Bessemer's 2026 AI pricing playbook -- per-seat SaaS dropped from 21% to 15% in twelve months, Leena AI is the cited proof point for outcome-based pricing working, and 2026 is the first renewal cycle where buyers force ROI tie-back. If outcome billing genuinely becomes the new primitive rather than a wrapper, Touchmark's eval-scoring engine could be the metering layer that incumbents have to license rather than rebuild, and Bolgov's Revolut billing chops plus Yanushevskyi's Citadel eval rigor are the right pair to ship that engine. The bear answer that wins for me is the dossier's own quote -- per-customer adjustments "average to zero, your top-line revenue is preserved" -- which means Touchmark has explicitly removed the pricing-upside lever a Stripe-distributed AI vendor would integrate for, leaving only dispute-reduction as the value prop, which Orb can wrap in a quarter. The verifiable condition that flips the verdict: two named AI-platform customers (Vercel, Replit, Anthropic, OpenAI tier) shipping Touchmark in production with public dollar figures, and Bolgov holding a named CEO title with a documented founder agreement. Until then, this is a pass that doesn't sting -- it just isn't my lane.
