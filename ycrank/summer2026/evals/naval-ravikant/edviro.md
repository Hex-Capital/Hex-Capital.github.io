# Edviro -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Hursh Shah and Tanuj Siripurapu are 18 and 19, one has a NeurIPS 2024 paper, the other shipped a $20K agency in high school, and they have decided to sell SaaS into K-12 school district procurement. That sentence is the signature. Edviro's highest-trait dimension is the founder trio -- these are clearly smart, energetic, integrity-neutral kids -- and Edviro's lowest is the leverage architecture itself. That pairing names exactly what kind of company Edviro is: builder-type founders pointing their builder energy at the most permissioned distribution channel in American B2B. The product is code; the go-to-market is a phone call to a superintendent.

Twitter (2007) is the leverage rung I measure every check against -- anyone can post, the platform charges nothing, marginal cost of replication is zero, and the distribution compounds without anyone's consent. Edviro is the structural inverse. The dossier names "1 district partner, 7 school sites, 16 meters" and frames GTM as "starting with founders' hometown high schools" expanding via "district-level reference accounts." That is labor leverage with a procurement gate on top of it. School districts don't viral-adopt; they RFP. The HVAC software market itself is $737.7M today -- a number that tells you the entire category has been chewed on for a decade by permissioned-distribution incumbents and stayed under a billion dollars. Edviro might be a real business inside that envelope. It is not the leverage architecture I write checks on, and Notion at the mid rung had self-serve sign-ups from solo users on day one; Edviro has procurement.

The single-vertical-no-pivot-room failure mode is what killed every dental-SaaS-for-orthodontists pitch I ever passed on -- the founder cannot maneuver because the market doesn't absorb a second swing. Edviro's dossier shows that pattern in formation: "1 district partner, 7 school sites" is the entire traction story, the competitor set is GridPoint and 75F and Atrius (all hardware-coupled BMS plays), and the GTM is described entirely in K-12-district language. Where does Edviro pivot if school superintendents don't return calls fast enough? Commercial real estate is a different sales motion. Industrial is a different sales motion. The "energy world model" claim could in theory port to data centers or municipalities, but the dossier does not articulate that pivot vector, and the first idea never works. The pivot room exists on paper inside a $26.3B facility management TAM and evaporates the moment you remember the founders just turned 19.

Mechanism on network effects: Edviro is single-sided SaaS where the 17th meter at the 8th school does not make the 16th meter at the 7th school more valuable. The "energy world model trained on multi-site BMS telemetry" is the only mechanism in the dossier that could compound across the install base, and it compounds as a *model quality* improvement, not as a user-value network. That's a data flywheel, which is real, but it doesn't deliver the asymmetric-user-value math that put Uber on the ladder. Eighteen months in, Edviro either has 30 districts and a slightly better anomaly detector, or 5 districts and the same one.

Counterfactual on specific knowledge: for Edviro's score here to flip a tier, I'd need to see Hursh or Tanuj having shipped something in energy systems before -- an open-source BACnet/Modbus library with stars, a published benchmark on HVAC anomaly detection, a prior internship at a utility, anything. What the dossier actually gives me is NeurIPS 2024 (domain unspecified), Skyglass (smart glasses), MediHacks (healthcare hackathons), and RTX (defense). That's a builder profile, not an energy-domain profile. They discovered the problem by walking past their hometown high schools -- observer-discovery, not practitioner-discovery. Atwood and Spolsky were developers writing Stack Overflow for developers. Edviro is two CS kids writing software for facilities managers they have never been.

Single-anchor parallel on the founder trio: Ivan Zhao at Notion was a quiet builder with adolescent-onward obsession over personal productivity tools -- the integrity and energy read through the work, not the resume. Edviro's Hursh and Tanuj read closer to Ivan than to a ex-McKinsey memo-founder; the NeurIPS paper and the $20K agency are shipped artifacts, not pedigree theater. I'd happily spend ten years with kids like this. They will build something interesting. The question is whether *this* is the thing.

Thirty-six month forecast: Edviro pivots within 18 months away from K-12 district sales toward either a horizontal energy-anomaly API for BMS integrators or a generic agentic-ops platform that drops the energy framing entirely, lands somewhere between $150K and $600K ARR, and gets acqui-hired by an incumbent in the GridPoint/75F/Atrius cluster or quietly winds down. The mechanism producing that forecast is the signature: builder founders pointed at permissioned distribution rediscover, around month 12, that their actual leverage is the model and the code, not the district relationships, and they pivot toward the channel that matches their talent.

The bull case Anchorage-Digital-style override would say: regulated/permissioned distribution can flip from "permissionless" to "permission-as-moat" when the category timing is right, and AI-for-physical-infrastructure in 2026 has the same pre-narrative shape that crypto had in 2014 when I co-founded MetaStable. Hursh's NeurIPS paper is the specific-knowledge wedge; school districts are the wedge customer; the world-model becomes the moat. That argument stings because it's structurally the Perplexity exception -- top-quartile specific knowledge plus pre-consensus category timing can waive the network-effect gate. It loses to one piece of dossier evidence: Perplexity's Aravind Srinivas had a Berkeley ML PhD *in retrieval*, the exact subfield of his product. Edviro's NeurIPS work has no disclosed connection to energy systems or world models for physical infrastructure -- it's generic ML credentialing, not domain-specific knowledge. The flip condition: if Edviro ships an open-source energy world model or anomaly-detection library that gets organic adoption from 5+ BMS vendors or independent facilities engineers without paid acquisition, the leverage architecture has converted from labor to code and I reconsider. Until then, pass.
