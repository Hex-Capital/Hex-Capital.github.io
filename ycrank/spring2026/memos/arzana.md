## The Idea

- **Problem:** Manufacturing back-office staff process RFQs, POs, and customer updates manually across email/PDF/ERP, generating repetitive, error-prone admin work (arzana.com).
- **Approach:** Custom AI agents read RFQs and POs from email/PDF, match parts, validate pricing, and write into ERP/CRM systems (Epicor, JobBoss, NetSuite, SAP, Salesforce, HubSpot) (arzana.com).
- **Differentiation:** vs. Korso (RFQ/quote follow-up agents) and Smartbase (order-entry conversion), Arzana bundles quoting + order entry + customer updates + an "Arzana ERP" option with pre-installed agents (arzana.com); vs. NetSuite/SAP S/4HANA embedded AI, Arzana ships as an overlay on incumbent ERPs (arzana.com).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: Enterprise SaaS/annual contracts; no pricing published on arzana.com, but customer logos include Fortune 500 names (Zeiss) and the site cites "<4 months to go-live" implying implementation-led deals (arzana.com).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** Trade-show presence (MD&M) plus direct outreach by the CEO (LinkedIn post on MD&M trade shows, 2026) and ERP-ecosystem integrations listed on the site (arzana.com).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Named customer logos including Zeiss, Tecton, Koike, Tier1, Milltown Paper, Iowa Mold, Kuebler, Givi, plus integrations with 10+ ERPs (arzana.com).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Proprietary training data from historical job costs and parts-matching could create accuracy lock-in; unproven because pricing/accuracy at scale is not independently verified beyond the company's "99.9% accuracy" claim (arzana.com).
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbent ERPs (SAP, NetSuite, Epicor) face channel conflict adding agentic front-office automation that cannibalizes their professional-services revenue, leaving room for an overlay vendor (arzana.com integration list).
- **Commoditization risk:** Multiple YC P26 peers (Korso, Smartbase, Tiny) target adjacent RFQ/PO/ERP-automation workflows for manufacturers, indicating low barrier to entering the category (YC P26 batch listing).

## Key Risks

- **Direct competitive density in YC P26:** Korso, Smartbase, and Tiny target overlapping manufacturer back-office workflows in the same batch (YC P26 listings), creating fundraising and customer-acquisition overlap with no public differentiation in technical approach.
- **Incumbent ERP encroachment:** NetSuite, SAP S/4HANA, and QAD Adaptive are shipping embedded AI/agentic features in 2026 (gurussolutions.com; top10erp.org, 2026), which could absorb the order-entry/quoting use cases Arzana automates as an overlay.
- **Technical feasibility at claimed accuracy:** "99.9% accuracy" on RFQ/PO parsing across heterogeneous PDFs and emails is unverified by third parties (arzana.com); failure modes in production could trigger trust loss given that quoting errors flow directly to revenue.
- **Sales-cycle / implementation drag:** "<4 months to go-live" with ERP integrations across 10+ systems (arzana.com) implies professional-services-heavy deployment that can compress per-rep capacity and slow ARR growth.
- **Founder domain depth in software:** NYT reports neither founder had a tech background prior to Arzana (NYT, Feb 2026), creating execution risk on building a multi-ERP integration platform with claimed enterprise-grade accuracy.
