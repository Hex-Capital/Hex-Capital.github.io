# Soria -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Adam Ron was a Bank of America VP in equity research covering healthcare facilities and managed care, and Soria is the Excel sector model he maintained at that desk -- which means the highest thing about Soria is that the idea is organic, and the lowest thing about Soria is that nobody on the founding team leaves a hacker trail you can grep for. That pairing -- domain insider with the right scar tissue, engineering side with no visible Lisp prize or RSS spec or HN demo -- is a specific kind of company, and I have funded the shape before, but never at the top of the distribution.

Soria's organic-idea dimension is the one I keep returning to, because Drew Houston's lost USB stick at MIT is the reference case and Adam Ron's situation rhymes with it more than most pitches I read. Houston built **Dropbox** (2007) because he forgot the thing he needed; Adam Ron at BofA was the analyst manually pulling CMS data and state agency files into Excel, which is exactly the workflow Soria automates. The dossier says Soria aggregates 125+ data sources into a sector model, and that is not a TAM slide -- that is a former sell-side healthcare analyst building the tool he wished existed while he was the one maintaining it. Where Soria diverges from **Dropbox** (2007) is that Houston shipped the demo video to Hacker News before any check; Soria's organic-pain claim rests on Adam Ron's resume rather than on a pre-funding artifact Ron shipped while still at BofA on nights and weekends.

The hacker-genealogy dimension is where Soria thins out, and the failure mode I worry about is the one from my Pass example -- finance-pedigreed founder, engineer hired alongside, no shipped open source visible anywhere. Cameron Spiller is a Virginia Tech computer engineer who shipped at Pinwheel, Findigs, Trigo Data, which is a real engineering track record, but the dossier explicitly notes "No public repos found" for Spiller and the same for Ron. Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz co-authored RSS before **Reddit** (2005); Soria's CTO has a clean resume and no tribal marker, which is not disqualifying but is conspicuous in a company pitched as an AI Bloomberg.

Soria's schlep score works on mechanism rather than on a ladder. Aggregating 125+ heterogeneous public and private healthcare sources -- state agencies, nonprofits, vendors with different rate limits and licensing terms -- compounds the way **Coinbase** (2012)'s compliance grind compounded: every additional source Soria normalizes is a source a horizontal incumbent like Hebbia or Daloopa has to replicate before they can credibly extend into healthcare. Eighteen months from now the question is whether Soria has 250 sources and a sticky Excel add-in workflow at named desks, or whether Hebbia at $13M ARR and a $700M valuation simply bundles a healthcare data pack and Soria's wedge dissolves. The schlep is real; whether Soria is willing to keep grinding it for five years is unknown from the dossier.

Soria's animal-resourcefulness score would flip a tier if the dossier named a single stunt -- Adam Ron cold-calling fifty BofA analyst friends for paid pilots before the YC check, or Spiller shipping the Excel add-in as a standalone product to Hacker News six months before incorporation. None of that appears. What appears is a Fintech Sandbox membership, a DBOS.dev case study, and a self-reported "trusted by Bulge Bracket Banks" claim with no named logos. Brian Armstrong rebuilt his **Coinbase** (2012) application by hand-coding an Android wallet after I rejected him; Soria's dossier has no equivalent post-rejection moment, no cereal-box, no demo that flipped a skeptic. The artifact exists -- Excel add-in, MCP server, 125+ sources live -- but the animal anecdote does not.

The cofounder dimension is a single-anchor comparison to **Airbnb** (2009), where Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk had years of RISD and roommate history before the company. Soria lists team size 3 but the dossier cannot name the third founder, and there is no documented prior relationship between Rutgers/BofA Adam Ron and Virginia Tech/Pinwheel Cameron Spiller. The configuration is structurally fine -- not solo, not four strangers -- but the chemistry that made **Airbnb** (2009) survive 2008 is unproven in the dossier and would only surface in the YC interview itself.

My 36-month forecast for Soria: $4-8M ARR selling to roughly twenty healthcare desks at banks and mid-tier hedge funds, acquired by FactSet or S&P Capital IQ in the $40-90M range, or quietly absorbed by Hebbia in a talent-and-data deal. That forecast follows directly from the signature -- organic-idea founder with no hacker mythology builds a competent vertical wedge that an incumbent rationally buys rather than rebuilds, because Soria is exactly the kind of company that gets to a real number and a real ceiling at the same time.

The strongest objection is **Airbnb** (2009) itself as a Known Exception: I rejected that idea on first read and the founders overrode my skepticism with a cereal-box stunt, and somebody could argue Adam Ron's BofA-VP-quits-to-build-the-tool-he-needed is a quieter version of the same animal signal -- that I am pattern-matching on the absence of a HN demo when the demo is the 125+ pipeline itself. That objection stings because Soria's product does exist and Adam Ron did leave a VP seat to build it. The dossier evidence that wins is the silence on pre-funding hustle: Soria's traction page makes an unverified bulge-bracket claim with zero named logos, and an animal would have named one logo. The verdict flips if Soria walks into the YC interview and names three paying banks with seat counts and an MRR figure -- at that point the **Airbnb** (2009) exception applies and I write the check.
