# Coasty -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Nitish Kovuru built Coasty because an AI agent deleted his production database on his laptop -- that one line in the dev.to post is the most important sentence in the entire dossier, and it is the reason Coasty registers at all. The signature of Coasty in my portfolio is high on organic-idea origin, low on cofounder-genealogy disclosure: a company where the *why we built this* is grade-A Drew Houston, but the *who we are together* is a blank page. That asymmetry is rarer than it looks. Most pre-seed AI-agent dossiers I read in 2026 are inverted -- two impressive resumes, zero personal scar tissue. Coasty has the scar.

Drew Houston of **Dropbox** (2007) kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT, went home one weekend, and built the thing he wished existed. Kovuru watched an autonomous agent wipe his prod DB and built a sandboxed VM with self-correction so it would not happen to the next person. Coasty's "vision-based, sandbox-per-session, self-corrects mid-task" architecture is not a market-opportunity readout -- it is a direct artifact of one founder's bad afternoon. That is "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) hitting the mark: something the founders themselves want, that they themselves can build, that few others realize is worth doing the *right* way. Where Coasty diverges from Houston is that Houston's pain was personal-productivity-grade, infinitely repeatable; Kovuru's pain is operator-grade, and I want to see Coasty re-tell that story with a paying enterprise customer who had the same incident -- the dossier does not yet have that named customer, only the founder's own incident.

Coasty's two-founder pair -- Prateek Jannu and Nitish Kovuru -- has no described prior working relationship in the dossier, and this is the dimension where my pen hovers. The **Reddit** (2005) Huffman/Ohanian dorm-roommate pattern, the **Stripe** (2010) Collison-brothers pattern, the **Airbnb** (2009) Chesky/Gebbia RISD pattern -- in each case I knew before the interview that the team had survived something together. Coasty's dossier names Jannu's Microsoft/Purdue/TruGen ML lineage and Kovuru's dev.to and sandboxed-VM thesis, but the connective tissue between them is absent. That failure mechanism is what killed YC bets where two strangers paired up at a hackathon weeks before applying. I do not see evidence Coasty fits that anti-pattern, but I cannot rule it out from public data, and at $19/mo Starter tiers, the team will eat each other before they eat UiPath if the bond isn't there.

Coasty's animal resourcefulness reading -- mechanism over 18 months -- compounds positively in one specific way and decays in another. Shipping an open-source repo at `coasty-ai/open-computer-use`, a Product Hunt launch on 19 Feb 2026, a Substack interview, a public `/v1/predict` API, and a self-reported 82% OSWorld score versus Anthropic's 72% is the artifact-first behavior I screen for; that is Coasty doing the **Dropbox** (2007) demo-video move at twice the surface area. What does not compound is that all of these are *visibility* moves, not Brian Armstrong's **Coinbase** (2012) post-rejection wallet-build, where the resourcefulness was specifically *adversarial* -- against a no. Coasty has shipped, but has not yet been told no by anyone the dossier records, so the test is incomplete.

Coasty's schlep-zone score flips a tier only if one thing becomes true: a named Fortune 500 deploys Coasty inside their internal systems and Coasty's founders personally do the SOC 2 / VPC / compliance grind to make it land. The Humans in the Loop interview cites Fortune 500 hesitance toward agents touching legacy desktop tools -- that is the **Coinbase** (2012) compliance-grind opportunity sitting on the table. Coasty is currently positioned with a $99/mo self-serve Unlimited tier and a cal.com/coasty/15min booking link, which is PLG-coded, not schlep-coded. UiPath and Blue Prism did not get built by people selling $99 subscriptions; they got built by people who flew to Hartford and sat in insurance back-offices for six months. Coasty's *problem* sits in the schlep zone; Coasty's *behavior* does not yet.

Coasty's hacker-genealogy reading parallels **Reddit** (2005) only weakly. Aaron Swartz co-authored RSS before Reddit; Jannu has a Microsoft ML research stint and a 200K-parameter auto-grad engine, which is real but not Swartz-tier public technical identity, and the dossier finds no confirmed GitHub handle for either founder despite the `coasty-ai` org. Kovuru writes on dev.to. That is a 2026-flavored hacker footprint, not a 2005-flavored one, and I have learned over twenty years to weight the latter more, though I acknowledge the medium has shifted. The 82% OSWorld number is real technical work; I take it at face value while noting Coasty's own blog post flagging "82% Real, 73% Exploited" framing, which is either admirable epistemic honesty or a tell.

My 36-month forecast for Coasty: the horizontal computer-use position collapses under Anthropic's next Computer Use API release closing the 82%-to-72% gap, and Coasty pivots into a vertical RPA wedge -- most likely finance back-office or insurance claims -- landing at $1.5M to $4M ARR with a single anchor enterprise customer, then gets acquired by UiPath or Automation Anywhere for under $40M in 2028-2029. The mechanism is straight commoditization: Coasty's stated moat is a public benchmark, and public benchmarks are the easiest thing in software for a frontier lab to target on its next training run. The organic-pain signature predicts Coasty survives by *narrowing*, not by riding the horizontal.

The bull case against my forecast is that Coasty's 82% OSWorld is not the moat -- the *trajectory data* from 100+ step multimodal sessions is, and at scale Coasty owns a proprietary corpus that Anthropic does not. **Airbnb** (2009) is the precedent for me overriding idea-level skepticism: I thought strangers-in-homes was a non-business and the cereal boxes flipped me. Could Coasty's 82% be the cereal-box-equivalent stunt that signals operator quality I should trust on faith? The dismantling: cereal boxes were *revenue from non-investors*, an unscalable improvisation against a closed market. Coasty's 82% is a self-reported number on a public benchmark Anthropic itself contributes to -- it is clever, not adversarial. One condition flips me to Strong Invest: Coasty publishes a named Fortune 500 design partner with a signed contract for desktop-agent deployment inside an internal legacy system within the next six months. Until then, Coasty is a check I write small, not big.
