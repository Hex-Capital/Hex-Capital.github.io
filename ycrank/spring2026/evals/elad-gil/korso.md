# Korso -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Korso is an architecturally ambitious cross-ERP agent layer with no curve underneath it -- the dossier hands me a homepage dashboard that cites "Northbridge Metals" and "Blue Harbor Components" as exemplar customers, and both are fictional. That single fact is the analytical signature for Korso: structural ambition (seven ERPs spanned -- SAP, NetSuite, Dynamics 365, Sage, SYSPRO, Plex, Acumatica) paired with a revenue-velocity slot that is not just empty but actively papered over with mockups. In my taxonomy, this is the inverse of the **Harvey** (2023) profile -- Harvey was $3B → $5B → $8B in months and I could see the curve; Korso is showing me what it wishes the curve looked like.

**Stripe** (2010) is the load-bearing analogy on Korso's strongest dimension -- index position on a compounding category. Stripe didn't have to beat PayPal at any specific merchant; it sat in the workflow path that every e-commerce entrant eventually touched, and the underlying category (online checkout) compounded for fifteen years. Korso's seven-ERP integration sheet is gesturing at the same structural play -- be the agent layer every mid-market manufacturer's SAP/NetSuite/Plex install touches, regardless of which ERP they chose. The mechanism is real. Where Korso diverges from the Stripe analogy is that Stripe in 2010 was the only credible developer-channel play; Korso in 2026 shares this exact position claim with Lumari (YC, same vintage, ex-Google/Tesla/Amazon/Stripe engineers), Turian ($3.78M raised, Berlin, founded 2022), Smartbase, and qomplement. Four-way index-position contention at pre-seed is not an index position; it's a race.

The **Lyft** Series C failure mechanism on revenue velocity was that the dossier could not show me a credible path to share inversion, only narrative -- I passed and it was the right call even though Lyft survived. Korso's dossier is structurally worse: no revenue disclosed, no named pilot, no paying logo, and the only "traction" artifact -- the homepage dashboard with "$2.4M pipeline, 47 active quotes, 89% close rate" -- references invented companies. In an AI cycle where my stated heuristic is *if you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken* (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), a fictional dashboard is worse than silence. Silence is ambiguous; mockup-as-screenshot is a tell that Korso is showing what they wished was true rather than what shipped.

On the catalyst dimension, Korso's "why now" reduces to LLM tool-use and MCP maturity in 2024-2025 -- the same catalyst every agent startup in this batch will cite. That is not a manufacturing-specific re-opening; it's the wave that lifted Lumari and Turian and Smartbase simultaneously into the same RFQ-and-PO-expediting wedge. The compounding question over the next 18 months: does a manufacturing-specific catalyst arrive (re-shoring tariff regime, named ERP incumbent failure, a specific procurement-compliance shift) that lets Korso pick off the field, or does the generic LLM-agent catalyst keep funding all four entrants equally until one of them ships revenue? Without a Korso-specific catalyst named in the dossier, the default outcome is the four-way race compresses everyone's margins before any of them index.

The founder-ships dimension would flip a tier if Daichi Hiraoka, Alex Liu, or Martin Pan had a single named independent shipped artifact -- a GitHub project, a side tool with users, a public Twitter showing iteration. The dossier surfaces Penn Electric Racing (Hiraoka and Pan) and Pan's automations-engineering role at GM, which is real founder-market fit for manufacturing-floor systems but is shipping-inside-an-institution, not the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind-pings-LinkedIn-and-shows-up-a-week-later-with-the-thing-built signature. Counterfactual: if the dossier had named one independent product Pan built and iterated on with users at GM or after, this dimension moves from a 7-8 to a 14-15.

On single-miracle architecture, **Decagon** (2023) needed one validated capability -- LLM accuracy at CS triage -- and the rest was GTM. Korso requires at minimum two independent miracles compounded: (a) agent reliability on multi-week checkpointed workflows acting on production ERPs, a class of problem the dossier itself notes is *unproven publicly at scale by any vendor*, and (b) enterprise sales penetration into mid-market manufacturers running SAP/Oracle/NetSuite with a three-engineer team and no disclosed enterprise-sales hire. The dossier's own risk section names AWS Bedrock AgentCore procurement agents and native ERP-vendor agentic features as encroachment vectors -- so the miracle list arguably extends to (c) outrunning SAP/Oracle/Microsoft's own roadmap. That's the compounding-small-odds geometry I avoid.

My 36-month forecast on Korso: either a single-ERP vertical pivot (most likely SAP-manufacturing or NetSuite-manufacturing) at sub-$1M ARR, or an acqui-hire by an ERP systems integrator or consolidator (Rootstock, Plex-tier) at sub-$30M -- with dead-or-absorbed by mid-2029 as the modal outcome. The mechanism producing this: four-way pre-seed contention on the same RFQ/PO wedge, no Korso-specific catalyst, no disclosed velocity, and ERP incumbents shipping native agentic layers that compress the cross-ERP value proposition before Korso accumulates the switching-cost data flywheel its future-moat claim depends on.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-forecast is actually a bull case for Korso, and it stings: Lumari is YC-backed with ex-Google/Tesla/Amazon/Stripe engineers in the same batch window, and the fact that YC underwrote *two* manufacturing-agent companies in P26 is itself a category-conviction signal from a partner pool that sees the deal flow. Martin Pan's GM automations background and the Penn Electric Racing collaboration history between Pan and Hiraoka are non-trivial founder-market fit -- formula-team shipping discipline is real. The "long-running workflows that span weeks with checkpointing that never loses progress" wedge, if Korso actually built it, is a technically defensible differentiator against Lumari and Turian. But this case dismantles on my own AI Market Clarity framework: when four named entrants share a wedge inside 24 months and none has disclosed revenue or named logos, the consensus seed bet on the fourth-named entrant does not pencil, and Korso's fictional-customer dashboard is the worst possible epistemic posture for that bet. One verifiable condition flips this verdict: Korso discloses three or more named mid-market manufacturer logos at ≥$25K ACV inside six months. Until then, pass.
