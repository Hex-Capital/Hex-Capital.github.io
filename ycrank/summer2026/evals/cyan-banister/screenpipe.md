# screenpipe -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Louis Beaumont ran Mediar in 2024 automating SAP data entry via screen input, and screenpipe is the same thesis widened to 48 apps — that's the signature I actually care about, a solo founder who already spent a startup inside this exact problem, dropped into a consensus AI-screen-recorder category where Microsoft Recall ships at zero marginal cost on every Copilot+ PC. The strongest dimension is the unambiguous CEO — Louis is the only founder on the YC page, no 50/50, no Yusupov/Kroll tiebreaker fight waiting in Q3. The weakest is the frontier-hardware or aesthetically transgressive swing — screenpipe is a B2B productivity SaaS with source-available Rust, and that is not **Crusoe** burning gas flares in North Dakota, no matter how nice the codebase is.

**Anduril**'s Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before pivoting to defense, and that prior-product trajectory is what made me tattoo the logo. Louis Beaumont's Mediar analogue is real but narrower — it was a Techstars '22 / OrangeDAO F1 automated-data-entry play against SAP, not a shipped-at-scale prior exit. What loads the analogy is the same lived-through-the-problem pattern: you don't decide to build a local-first screen agent because you read the Rewind acquisition news, you build it because you already spent 2024 gluing screen capture into legacy-software workflows and watched the same repetitive motions bleed out of a hundred admin roles. Screenpipe diverges from the Anduril rung on scale and stakes — Mediar was a small Founders Inc portfolio bet, not Oculus — but the biographical vector points the right way, and 479 GitHub followers plus a repo with 19k stars means Louis has been *of* this problem publicly since well before YC S26.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on had six top-tier funds circling a $200B TAM slide and a four-logo team page, and its category — enterprise AI SaaS — is the same lane screenpipe now enters. The failure mechanism there wasn't the founders' intelligence; it was the model being the moat rather than the founder. Does that pattern appear in screenpipe? Partially. The category "AI screen recorder" is where Granola just raised $125M at a $1.5B valuation, where Meta bought Rewind in December 2025, where Microsoft Recall ships for free on Copilot+ hardware. That is a crowded, well-funded consensus lane and the round will price like it. The moderating fact is that Louis is threading a local-first, cross-OS, source-available needle none of those three incumbents will match without an architectural rewrite — but "magically weird" is not the word I'd use for AI screen memory in 2026. It's "well-positioned inside a consensus wave," which historically is not where I write my best checks.

Solo-founder execution risk over 18 months compounds nonlinearly on a product spanning macOS + Windows + Linux + 48 integrations + a proprietary PII-scrubbing model. Screenpipe's team of six behind one founder is thin for that surface area, and the mechanism that kills companies here isn't founder quality — it's that Louis becomes the bottleneck on every hiring decision, integration bug triage, and enterprise SSO/SAML review while Microsoft ships Recall v3 and Granola's $192M war chest funds a screen-capture SKU. The compounding failure mode is not dramatic; it's slow, quarter-over-quarter drift while incumbents pattern-match the roadmap.

Counterfactual on the "unsexy-regulated" dimension: screenpipe would flip from a 5/15 to a 10/15 if the dossier showed one signed Fortune 500 SSO/SAML enterprise contract at the $150/seat tier with a named legal-cleared always-on-capture clause — because always-on screen and audio capture in a Salesforce or Thomson Reuters compliance environment is genuinely a regulated-frontier problem, and the customer logos on the homepage today are user-installs, not procurement wins. The dossier explicitly says "no publicly disclosed signed enterprise contracts." Without that, the moat lives on GitHub stars and 250k self-reported users, which is a top-of-funnel number, not a defensibility number.

Single-anchor parallel on physical-world verifiability: **Niantic** I could source because I played Ingress obsessively starting in 2012 and felt the pull before any metric. Screenpipe I can `git clone` tonight, run the Rust agent on my own laptop, watch it index my Slack and Gmail, and hand it to a friend by Wednesday. That is real tactile verifiability — the 19k GitHub stars are not vanity, they're evidence that thousands of engineers already did exactly that. It doesn't reach Flock-camera-on-a-pole tangibility, but for a software artifact it's near the ceiling of what this dimension can register.

Screenpipe in 36 months: acqui-hire by Microsoft, Anthropic, or a post-Meta Rewind competitor in the $50-150M range, with Louis Beaumont absorbed as a founding engineer on an on-device agent team, before crossing $10M ARR standalone. The mechanism is the signature — a strong solo founder with real domain rehearsal, but inside a consensus category where the three best-capitalized incumbents (Recall free, Granola $1.5B, Rewind-Meta) will make standalone Series B economics brutal, and where the differentiated asset (local-first Rust capture + PII scrubbing) is exactly the kind of talent-and-tech bundle that a large platform buys rather than builds.

The steel-man bear-against-invest case: this is the polished AI SaaS wrapper pattern with better marketing. Fortune 500 logos on a homepage are user-installs, not contracts. The recent license change from open-source to source-available already forked the community — atzamis, EvolvingSoftware, AllyourBaseBelongToUs are live forks on GitHub — meaning the 19k-star moat is legally reversible in a Chrome-tab. Louis's prior Mediar had a co-founder, Matt Diakonov, who is conspicuously absent from screenpipe's team; I want to know why, because unresolved co-founder history is a **GameCrush** tell. The category is where Granola just closed $125M at $1.5B — that is textbook consensus, and the S26 batch price will reflect it. That case stings because it is materially correct on three of four points. What wins on the margin is the founder-domain fit and the fact that Louis was already building screen-to-automation before the Rewind-Meta window opened, not because of it. The condition that flips me from Neutral to Invest: one signed enterprise contract at $150/seat with a named Fortune 500 counterparty and a documented answer for the Diakonov exit — those two facts move screenpipe from a "smart solo founder in a crowded lane" to a "solo founder with procurement traction in a regulated always-on-capture lane," which is a different bet entirely.
