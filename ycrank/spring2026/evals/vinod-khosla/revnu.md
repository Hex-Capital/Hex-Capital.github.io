# Revnu -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Revnu is the canonical software wrapper my Pass Example was written to describe -- George Jefferson and Art Freebrey at 21, building autonomous agents for SEO, Meta/LinkedIn/Reddit/TikTok ads, and cold outbound on the standard YC $500K SAFE. The dossier's own framing -- "distribution is now the competitive advantage since AI has democratized product development" (revnu.com) -- inverts my entry point. That sentence describes capturing an economic mechanism, not breaking one. When I map the world-state at maximum Revnu success, what I see is startups shipping cold-outbound sequences 3x faster than they did with 11x or Artisan. That is not a civilizational variable.

**OpenAI (2019)** is the HIGH rung on consequence magnitude -- I wrote the $50M check because the success-state was a redrawn map of cognitive labor, the same $15T-of-U.S.-GDP frame I gave Fortune in March 2026. Revnu's success-state, by the dossier's own description, is that founders stop doing growth themselves -- replacing one SDR or one growth marketer per customer. That is a labor-displacement story at the headcount scale of tens of thousands of growth hires in U.S. startups, not the tens of millions in the radiology, trucking, or knowledge-worker layers I underwrote with Physical Intelligence (2024) and Waabi (Jan 2026). Revnu does not name an incumbent regime that collapses; it names Jasper, Copy.ai, 11x, and Artisan as the regime it joins.

**KiOR (~2008)** sits at the bottom of my Capital-Risk ladder for the right reason -- the failure mode I publicly defended in the 2014 "Smack Down" letter was that the catalyst, feedstock, and refinery miracles were each independently falsifiable and each required real capital to test. Revnu has zero miracles of that shape. The "single intelligence layer" claim (revnu.com) where every channel makes the others smarter is an orchestration assertion on top of commodity LLM APIs and platform ad SDKs. A Tier-1 generalist can write this $500K check on a Tuesday. The KiOR failure mode does not even apply to Revnu, because Revnu has no science to fail at -- which sounds like a feature until you remember it is precisely why I am over-resourced for the deal.

On founder defiance, the mechanism analysis matters more than a ladder. Defiance compounds when a founder's contrarian position forces them to build something the market structurally cannot reproduce -- the Sergey Levine pattern at Physical Intelligence (2024). George Jefferson and Art Freebrey dropping out of UK universities the same day to ship multi-agent growth is a biographical anomaly, not a thesis defiance. Their stated thesis -- distribution beats product in the AI era -- is the modal 2026 VC tweet. Eighteen months from now the defiance discount compounds against Revnu because every YC S26 batchmate with the same thesis (and the same Tom Blomfield partner office hours) ships a competing orchestrator.

Counterfactual on learning rate: Revnu's score flips a tier upward if the dossier showed a documented pivot inside Revnu itself -- the Slash (2024) Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C. What it shows instead is two founders who bootstrapped three software businesses each to £100K during university. That is iteration evidence, real shipping muscle, and a non-zero learning signal -- but it is prior-product iteration, not a thesis update on Revnu. Without a "we were wrong about X" moment on the current company, learning rate stays mid-band.

Single-anchor parallel on required-capital: compare Revnu to **Genesis AI (2025)** where I co-led $105M seed because the roadmap genuinely required foundation-model training compute the founders could spend in twelve months. Revnu's roadmap, as disclosed, requires LLM API spend, ad-platform API integrations, and CRM connectors. There is no $50M deployment thesis here; there is a $2M-to-$5M-ARR thesis that any seed fund can chase. I am the wrong investor on capital fit, not because Revnu is small but because nothing in the dossier converts incremental capital into incremental moat.

Thirty-six month forecast: Revnu plateaus below $3M ARR by mid-2028 and gets acqui-hired into Copy.ai or a similar AI-GTM platform for sub-$20M, with George and Art landing as senior product leads on a multi-agent orchestration team. The mechanism is the one I called out under Risk in the dossier -- 11x at $76M raised and Artisan at $36.5M raised are an order of magnitude better capitalized, LinkedIn rate-limited Ava-driven activity in Q1 2026 (the-ai-corner.com) which means any cold-outbound agent inherits the same platform throttle, and the disputed 11x ARR episode (claimed $14M, ~$3M actual per TechCrunch/CheckThat.ai) has primed buyers to distrust autonomous-GTM efficacy claims exactly when Revnu needs to make them.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash (2024)** exception -- YC-backed, young founders, consumer-adjacent software, consequence magnitude that did not pass my consequence prior, and I still wrote the $100M Series C because the documented Kanye-pivot showed learning rate strong enough to override the consequence gap. A bull would argue Revnu is structurally Slash: YC pedigree, UK outsider founders, bootstrap track record, an underserved buyer (technical founders who hate growth). The argument stings for thirty seconds and then breaks on the specific Slash override condition -- Slash had a documented public pivot story, a verifiable learning-rate moment that I cited explicitly. Revnu's dossier contains no equivalent thesis-update moment on the current company; the £100K bootstraps are prior-product. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: George or Art publishes a credible "we were building Revnu wrong, here is what we learned and what we changed" post within twelve months that names a specific abandoned channel or a specific buyer-segment pivot, AND Revnu posts >$1M ARR with a named Fortune-500 logo. Absent both, I am out before the demo.
