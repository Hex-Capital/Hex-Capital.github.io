# Humwork -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Humwork is what I'd call a well-bonded operator-pair shipping a marketplace wedge on borrowed platform rails -- Yash Goenka and Rohan Datta have been friends for 16 years out of UC Berkeley, which is the strongest signal in the whole dossier, and the weakest signal is that neither of them carries the Lisp-prize-or-RSS-spec hacker lineage I tend to over-index on. That pairing -- deep cofounder chemistry, thin hacker tribal markers -- tells me what kind of company Humwork is: a marketplace operator bet, not a technical-moat bet.

The cereal-box test at **Airbnb** (2009) is what I keep reaching for on the animal-resourcefulness axis, and Humwork doesn't get there, but it isn't the inverse either. Yash already shipped **Qualify.bot** (YC S25), Phonecall.bot, Instawrite, and somehow holds a graphene supercapacitor patent; Rohan ran an AI voice platform through >1M call minutes before this. They recruited 3,000+ verified experts and resolved 2,858 beta questions before the April 15 2026 YC launch, which is real pre-funding hustle on the supply side of a two-sided market -- the unsexy half. The divergence from the **Airbnb** (2009) rung is that the cereal boxes were a stunt nobody else would have run; Humwork's pre-launch grind is competent marketplace seeding, the kind a good operator does, not the kind that makes me lean forward in the interview. Yash being CEO of **Qualify.bot** (YC S25) *and* CEO of Humwork (YC S26) simultaneously, with no public clarification of Qualify.bot's status, is the exact founder-bandwidth tell that makes me discount the animal read by a notch.

A hacker-genealogy ladder bottoms out at the MBA-with-a-deck team I described in my pass example, and Humwork is clearly not that -- but the ladder *top* is Patrick Collison winning Ireland's Young Scientist (2005) for Lisp work before **Stripe** (2010), or Aaron Swartz co-authoring the RSS spec before **Reddit** (2005), and Humwork doesn't trade on that frequency. Yash's GitHub @yashgoenka shows 25 public repos with no top-repo star count surfaced; Rohan has no public GitHub at all. Berkeley Data Science and a Berkeley MS in Civil Engineering / Energy / Climate are competent technical credentials, but I've watched the hacker-tribal-marker pattern compound across the portfolio and Humwork's founders read as builders-by-employment, not hackers-by-disposition. This is the dimension that most distinguishes Humwork from the upper-quartile YC pattern.

On the organic-idea axis, the mechanism that matters is whether the pain compounds in the founder's own workflow over 18 months of building. Yash shipping **Qualify.bot** AI phone agents and Rohan running an AI voice platform through >1M minutes means both founders were almost certainly hitting the Claude Code / Cursor / Lovable failure modes -- the "loops on the same bug, hallucinates a legal nuance" pattern described in Humwork's own launch post -- as customers of those tools while building their own agentic systems. The dossier doesn't quote them saying so explicitly, which I'd want, but the founder-problem distance is short. Eighteen months from now, if Humwork's expert routing data starts encoding *which* agent failure modes recur and *which* experts resolve them fastest, the founders' own agent-building history will be the reason they noticed that data was worth collecting.

The counterfactual on schlep: for Humwork to clear the **Coinbase** (2012) compliance-grind bar on the schlep axis, the work would need to be unpleasant enough that **HumanLayer** ($500K seed, $660K revenue per Getlatka) and the **Skills.sh** / **Agensi** crowd are unconsciously routing around it. Recruiting 3,000+ verified experts, sustaining <2-minute first-reply SLAs across legal/marketing/strategy domains, and absorbing liability when an expert's wrong answer flows into an automated agent workflow -- that *is* a schlep, and it's a schlep the pure-software skill-marketplace competitors are flinching from. The score would flip a tier upward if the dossier named a specific regulatory or insurance grind Humwork had already swallowed (E&O coverage, jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction expert vetting); as written, the schlep is real but generic.

**Reddit** (2005) is my single-anchor parallel on the cofounder axis: Steve Huffman and Alexis Ohanian as dorm roommates at UVA, a relationship that predated the company by years and survived through the Conde Nast sale and Aaron Swartz's later departure. Yash and Rohan as 16-year friends out of UC Berkeley is the same kind of pre-startup bond. The Humwork interview question I'd actually ask is whether they've ever shipped anything together before this -- the dossier shows parallel founder histories (Qualify.bot, MicroGrid Labs voice work) but doesn't confirm a prior joint build. The 16-year friendship is load-bearing on its own; a prior joint shipped artifact would push this dimension to the top rung.

My 36-month forecast on Humwork: by mid-2029 the company either lands in the $3-8M ARR band as the default expert-routing layer inside one vertical (software engineering, where the dossier already cites 130% net retention) or gets acquihired by Cursor, Anthropic, or a coding-agent incumbent for $20-60M after one of them ships a first-party "ask a human" provider and compresses Humwork's wedge. The mechanism producing the forecast is the MCP platform-dependency risk crossed with the marketplace cold-start arithmetic -- 2,858 beta resolutions against 3,000+ experts is less than one resolution per expert, which means the supply side is currently subsidized goodwill that will not survive flat demand growth.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the one I have to take seriously: Yash is a repeat YC founder running a second company in consecutive batches, both Qualify.bot S25 and Humwork S26, which is the kind of operator velocity that **Kiko** (2005) → **Twitch** (2007) taught me to re-fund aggressively, and the MCP-substrate timing bet is exactly the "live in the future" pattern from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012). What dismantles the bull case for me is the inverse of the **Kiko** → **Twitch** pattern: Justin Kan sold Kiko on eBay and then went all-in on the next thing, while Yash is publicly listed as CEO of *both* companies simultaneously with no clarification of Qualify.bot's status -- that's not the re-fund-the-failed-founder signal, that's the founder-bandwidth red flag from "Founder Mode" (September 2024). The verdict flips to Invest if Yash publicly confirms Qualify.bot is wound down or fully handed off and Humwork posts >$50K MRR on agent-paid (not human-paid) credits by Q4 2026.
