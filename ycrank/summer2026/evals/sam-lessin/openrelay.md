# OpenRelay -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

OpenRelay is a domain-credentialed pair of infrastructure engineers -- Prashant Patel (founding member of Amazon Bedrock) and Jaden Wang (Lead Engineer at TensorDock post-Voltage Park) -- building the most consensus-priced sleeve of the 2026 YC batch: GPU-inference aggregation. The signature is a high founder-technical-fit floor sitting under a rock-bottom category-sleeve and founder-brand ceiling. That inversion tells you exactly what kind of deal this is: two operators who know the plumbing better than anyone, running straight at a category where four venture-funded incumbents are already price-competing at nine-figure ARR, with no non-obvious belief, no owned distribution, and no financing structure that makes OpenRelay anything other than the fifth check into a table stakes fight.

The **canonical YC AI-agent pitch from my Pass Example** is the load-bearing analogy for OpenRelay on Criterion 1 -- same mechanism, same anti-signal: a YC seed inside the category where "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale #113, 2024) and where the round is priced against a market that's already resolved. OpenRelay's dossier makes the parallel worse, not better -- RunPod just took $100M from Summit Partners at a $1B valuation in June 2026 at ~$240M ARR, Together AI is at ~$1B ARR, Fireworks ~$800M, Baseten ~$600M, and Parasail raised $32M in April 2026. The regatta ahead of OpenRelay is not "how do we tack from seed to Series A" -- it's "how do we tack past four fully-funded aggregators who own supply relationships we don't." When five well-capitalized players have already sprinted the first three tacks, buying into tack one at YC-standard SAFE pricing is exactly the price-insensitive momentum bet I built the value-seed frame to avoid.

**No founder Twitter, no GitHub, no LinkedIn confirmed, no blog** -- OpenRelay fails the "Writing is thinking. If you can't write, you can't think" filter (Antoine Buteau, ~2023) on the most literal level available. The low-rung failure mechanism here is the founder who has built no artifact of conviction before the round, so I cannot test whether Patel or Wang holds any non-obvious belief about why the four incumbents will lose. The dossier gives me domain resume -- AWS Bedrock, TensorDock, Voltage Park -- and nothing about what they think is wrong with RunPod's owned-fleet model or Parasail's tokenmaxxing model. The absence is doubly punishing because their prior employer, Voltage Park, now owns TensorDock, meaning the OpenRelay founding thesis has to include a story about why the marketplace model they just left is worth rebuilding independently, and that story exists nowhere I can find. A missionary founder would have published it before the batch.

Mechanism analysis on Criterion 2 -- capital structure. OpenRelay's own pricing page ("up to 90% cheaper than AWS, Azure, and GCP," $0.29/hr RTX 4090, $2.30/hr H100) is the tell: the take rate on hourly GPU rental at those prices, minus what OpenRelay must pay providers, minus routing/failover infrastructure, does not compound to default-alive without recruiting a supply base OpenRelay has not disclosed a single node of. Over 18 months, the "Dedicated provider program" either produces enough marginal-cost supply to hold the 90%-discount claim (in which case OpenRelay needs marketplace-liquidity capital that only a $30M+ Series A can fund) or it doesn't (in which case OpenRelay is reselling RunPod-equivalent capacity at RunPod-equivalent margins). Either branch of the tree is a five-round SaaS/marketplace cap table -- exactly the "founder-drainage" shape the regatta model was built to identify and refuse.

Counterfactual on Criterion 4 -- for OpenRelay to flip a tier on founder-as-brand, Patel would need to be publishing a Bedrock-insider newsletter on inference infrastructure with a five-figure developer following, or Wang would need to have run the Voltage Park engineering blog into a category-authority channel. Neither exists. What would have to be true is a pre-existing developer audience OpenRelay could route through the OpenAI-compatible endpoint at zero CAC -- the same mechanism the Slow Creator Fund underwrites and the mechanism John Fish/Bookshelved (January 2024) had in place before I wrote the check. OpenRelay has a $5-deposit-yields-$10-credit signup incentive, which is paid acquisition dressed as a promotion, and is the exact opposite signal.

Single-anchor parallel on Criterion 5 -- category sleeve. **Sublime Security** is the recent enterprise-security seed I entered because most security investors had rotated into late-stage SOC SaaS and the category was pre-consolidation. OpenRelay is the inverse configuration: the category is mid-consolidation with four visible ARR leaders, not pre-consensus; the buyers (AI application teams) are already habituated to Together/Fireworks token pricing; and the "crypto is fundamentally disruptive, AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale #113, 2024) re-weight tells me exactly where OpenRelay's sleeve sits -- the wrong side of the sleeve barbell I've publicly staked out.

Thirty-six month forecast for OpenRelay: acqui-hired by Voltage Park or RunPod for $8M-$25M in talent-plus-tech terms, with Patel and Wang absorbed into an inference-routing team at the acquirer -- a structurally consistent outcome given the highest dimension (real infra pedigree at hyperscaler and marketplace levels) and the lowest (no distribution, no non-consensus belief, no room to run against four funded incumbents). The mechanism is straightforward: OpenRelay's engineering is real enough to be worth buying, but the standalone marketplace cannot out-liquidity RunPod's owned fleet or Parasail's token-per-second pricing, so the founders monetize the resume rather than the equity.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that OpenRelay is genuinely structurally different from RunPod -- RunPod owns capex-heavy fleets, OpenRelay aggregates zero-marginal-cost third-party supply, and the aggregator model is precisely the capital-efficient shape my Slow letter praises. If Patel's Bedrock experience produces routing/failover software that no incumbent can match, and Wang's TensorDock scars mean he knows exactly why prior marketplaces died, OpenRelay could be Cloudflare-for-inference. The dossier evidence that wins against this case is Vast.ai and TensorDock itself -- both ran the aggregator playbook OpenRelay is running, and TensorDock ended up sold to Voltage Park in March 2025 rather than reaching escape velocity, which is the same acquirer I'm forecasting for OpenRelay. My verdict flips if OpenRelay publishes a signed enterprise LOI from a named AI application company (Anthropic-tier, Cursor-tier, Perplexity-tier) with committed monthly GPU-hour minimums before the Series A -- a durable demand-side anchor is the one thing that would prove the aggregator model has a wedge that Vast and TensorDock never found.
