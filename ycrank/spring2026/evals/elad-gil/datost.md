# Datost -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Datost is a clean single-miracle company sitting in the wrong distribution position -- text-to-SQL is the one validated capability, the semantic layer over business definitions is real engineering, and the 75.2% vs. 33% gap on BIRD-Interact is a legible artifact -- but Maceo Cardinale Kwik and Jason Hy Wang have built a Slack skin in a category where Hex, Snowflake Cortex, Databricks Genie, and a $21.1M Series-A TextQL are already living in the same workflow path. The architecture is disciplined; the position is exposed.

**Harvey** (2023) is the load-bearing analogy on revenue velocity because Harvey crossed my "just working" line on dollars, not benchmarks -- "$3B ? $5B ? $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) was buying behavior compounding visibly, and that's the only signal I trust in this AI cycle. Datost shows me 18,402 queries in a measured week and a 42-second time-to-first-answer, neither of which is a revenue number, neither of which is a named enterprise logo, and neither of which discloses MoM growth. The dossier carries no pricing page and routes prospects to datost.cal.com for demos -- in the AI vintage where "if you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), silence on dollars is itself the answer. Datost is not "false signal" because there's no curve to falsify; it's pre-signal.

Lyft Series C is the pattern I keep returning to on Datost's distribution position -- I passed on Lyft because being #2 in a winner-take-all market just means a worse version of the obvious bet (20VC interview), and Datost's published moat is benchmark accuracy on BIRD-Interact, which is the most fungible asset in the AI stack. Tracxn lists 47 active competitors against Datost; the named five include Hex at $36/editor/month with a notebook workspace, Alkemi which is *also* an "AI data teammate in Slack," and Snowflake/Databricks who own the warehouse Datost has to query. The "@mention in Slack" surface is not a moat -- it's a Slack-app submission. Datost's distribution dependency on a single chat platform that ships its own AI features is the exact "must win share against the category leaders" shape I flagged in *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025).

On Datost's catalyst, the dossier gives me a real one but underweights it -- frontier LLMs crossed usable text-to-SQL accuracy in the last 12-18 months, and that's a step-function, not a steady-state. The problem is mechanism: this catalyst opens the door for *every* entrant equally, including Snowflake Cortex and Databricks Genie who already sit in the data plane. A market re-opening that benefits incumbents more than entrants is half a catalyst. Compare to **Anduril** (2022), where the post-Ukraine catalyst specifically rewarded a software-native entrant against Lockheed-style integrators -- Datost has the opposite topology, where the catalyst rewards whoever already owns the warehouse.

Counterfactual on revenue velocity for Datost: to flip from Pass-tier into Invest-tier on this dimension alone, the dossier would need a named enterprise logo signed in the last 90 days plus a disclosed ARR number compounding >20% MoM -- the **Harvey** (2023) shape. Maceo's launch tweet from @ycombinator and his LinkedIn launch post are distribution-of-existence, not distribution-of-revenue. Eighteen thousand queries in a week could be one design partner running batch jobs; it could be a hundred Slack workspaces in free pilot. Without the denominator I can't underwrite the curve.

On founder execution rhythm, Maceo Cardinale Kwik has the credentialed shape -- VT CS Summa Cum Laude 4.0, ex-Traba full-stack engineer, founder-market fit from watching analyst scarcity block decisions inside Traba -- but the shipped-artifact trail is thin: GitHub @maceoCK shows 9 followers, 47 repos, top owned project at 1 star, and the OpenDSA contribution is to someone else's repo. Jason Hy Wang has no public technical footprint -- no GitHub, no retrievable LinkedIn detail. This is **not** the Aravind Srinivas / Perplexity (2022) "ships a thing between meetings" pattern; this is pedigree-plus-problem-exposure, which is the **Brex** (2017) operator archetype on paper but without the prior-scale shipping evidence Brex's founders carried in from Pagar.me. Datost's founders may yet ship like Aravind -- the dossier just doesn't show it yet.

36-month forecast: Datost gets acqui-hired into Hex, Snowflake, or Databricks for sub-$30M by mid-2029, or it shuts down without crossing $5M ARR independently -- the mechanism is that the @mention-in-Slack surface plus a semantic-layer benchmark gap is not durable against a warehouse-vendor's native build, and TextQL's $21.1M war chest funds the GTM motion Datost's two-person team cannot match before incumbents close the accuracy gap. The signature is single-miracle-meets-commodity-distribution, and that signature historically resolves into a talent-and-tech acquisition by the platform that owns the data plane.

The strongest case against me here: Datost's per-customer semantic graph could become a real switching cost the way **Decagon** (2023) built workflow stickiness in customer support -- ingesting internal vocabulary, schema mappings, prior queries -- and "AI in Analytics" is exactly the kind of "wide open" sub-market I named in *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) where I expect a workflow-infrastructure winner to emerge. Maceo's Traba scar tissue is a legitimate founder-market signal; the BIRD-Interact gap is not nothing if it holds in production. The bear-killer would be a named Fortune-500 logo running Datost in production with disclosed seat expansion -- that's the **Harvey** (2023) "just working" shape. Without it, the dossier reads as "benchmark plus Slack app plus YC stamp," and I've watched too many of those get out-distributed by the warehouse. **One verifiable condition flips my verdict**: a publicly-named enterprise customer (>1,000 employees) running Datost in production with disclosed seat count or ARR by Datost's first anniversary post-batch.
