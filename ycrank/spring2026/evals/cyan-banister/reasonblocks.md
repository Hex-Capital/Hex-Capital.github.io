# ReasonBlocks -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

ReasonBlocks is two credentialed best friends building consensus AI infrastructure ? Sajeev Magesh (Stanford CS, USAMO qualifier) and Rohan Vij (CMU AI), building together since 2nd grade ? in the most-funded category of 2026, with no opaque system being made visible and a moat that depends on out-running the exact incumbents who already own the trace pipe. The childhood-friendship co-founder dynamic is the single most interesting fact in the ReasonBlocks dossier and the only thing that keeps this from being indistinguishable from twenty other YC P26 agent-infra companies.

On biographical forge, my high rung is Substrate's James Proud ? a founder I literally tattooed onto my spine because he was forged in fire and came out unbroken ? and my low rung is the pass-example founder who tells me "I was at Google and saw the opportunity." ReasonBlocks sits closer to the low rung than the middle. Sajeev's *Scientific Reports* paper on ML applied to poverty and air pollution is real intellectual work, and the 11-year friendship from 2nd grade is uncommon enough that I take it seriously ? it preempts the HQ Trivia red flag in a way a six-month YC partnership never can. But when I ask "tell me their story, not the business," the answer is two smart kids from Amador Valley who optimized hard and ended up at Stanford and CMU. Nothing in the ReasonBlocks dossier tells me Sajeev or Rohan were personally broken by an agent burning through a token budget at 3am. The problem found a market; it did not find these founders.

On contrarian conviction, my high rung is Anduril ? defense AI when Silicon Valley wouldn't touch it ? and my low rung is, again, my pass example: consensus AI in a consensus market. ReasonBlocks sits at the low rung with almost no daylight. The competitive set named in the ReasonBlocks dossier ? LangSmith (Sequoia Series A), Braintrust, Langfuse (acquired by Clickhouse in January 2026), Helicone, Latitude ? is the most over-funded developer-tools category I can name. The thesis "agents waste tokens, we sit in the call path and fix it" is articulable at any dinner party in SOMA without anyone shifting in their chair. There is no 300-investor-pass story here. There is no stigma, no regulation, no political toxicity. This is exactly the room where I am usually the only one leaning forward, and ReasonBlocks gives me nothing to lean toward.

On invisible system made visible, my high rung is Affirm attacking credit card opacity and Ownwell attacking the property tax appeal racket; my low rung is HQ Trivia, where there was no system at all, just attention. ReasonBlocks tries to make token waste visible ? a 52% reduction on SWE-bench Pro is real engineering ? but the "system" being exposed is an inference bill paid by a Series B engineering team, not an extractive structure exploiting people who don't understand it. The customer is the AI-native company with budget; the value is operational efficiency. That is a fine business. It is not the Uber-taxi-medallion pattern that makes me write checks.

On defensibility, my high rung is Flock Safety's camera network and Carta's switching costs; my low rung is IRL, where the moat was fabricated metrics. ReasonBlocks pitches a "private reasoning library that grows with every run" ? in principle this is the right shape, per-customer data compounding into switching costs, and it is the only structural argument in the ReasonBlocks dossier that I respect. But the dossier also tells me the truth: LangSmith and Braintrust already own the trace data that would feed any reasoning library, and a runtime-intervention SKU is a quarter of work for them with channel distribution ReasonBlocks does not have. Two engineers, "Hiring: False," serving Series B customers with mid-call-path infrastructure ? the SRE math alone tells me this team is one outage away from a churn event. On cascading real-world impact, ReasonBlocks doesn't even pretend ? it makes inference cheaper for engineering teams that already have venture funding, which is the opposite of the Uber-Wonderschool-Postmates pattern that gets me to score above the default.

My 36-month forecast for ReasonBlocks: acquihired by an observability incumbent (Braintrust or LangChain most likely) for $20-50M within 24 months, with the "private reasoning library" rolled in as a runtime SKU and Sajeev and Rohan staying on as senior engineers. The mechanism producing this is the dimensional signature: smart-credentialed-team + zero-contrarian-signal + incumbent-feature-substitution-risk = a competent build that gets absorbed before it can compound, exactly because the founders are too credentialed and too coachable to die in the desert the way Crusoe nearly did in 2020.

The strongest case against my pass: SWE-bench Pro gains of 42% accuracy and 52% token reduction are not nothing, the unverified "90% repeat usage at top Series B companies" snippet ? if true ? is the early shape of Together AI's growth curve, and the 11-year co-founder bond does genuinely neutralize my single most important governance red flag from HQ Trivia. RTFKT is my standing precedent that cultural prescience at a narrow timing window can substitute for invisible-system disruption, and one could argue agent runtime infrastructure is at exactly that narrow window right now. I take the bull case seriously enough to name the flip condition: if Sajeev can put a named frontier-lab or Cursor-tier customer on the record describing ReasonBlocks as load-bearing infrastructure, AND can articulate an opaque structure being made transparent that goes deeper than "tokens cost too much," I would re-engage. Until then, this is consensus AI in a consensus market with credentialed operators, and that is not a Long Journey bet.
