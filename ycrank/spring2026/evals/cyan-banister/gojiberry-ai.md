# Gojiberry AI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is the rarest profile I see in this cohort and the one I trust least: founders who actually lived the problem -- Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Rom?n Czerny bootstrapped CoCo AI to ~?500K ARR doing the exact cold outbound they're now automating -- dropped into the single most consensus category of Spring 2026, AI SDR agents, where 11x.ai already collapsed from $14M claimed ARR to ~$3M actual with 70-80% churn (gtmlens.com Q2 2026). High lived experience, low magical weirdness. That pairing tells me Gojiberry AI is a competent operator's company, not a defining one.

Anduril-Luckey (2017) is the high rung for lived experience -- Palmer shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever pitched defense -- and Gojiberry AI sits surprisingly close to that rung on this single axis. Pierre-Eliott and Rom?n co-founded and sold CoCo AI together, a WhatsApp marketing SaaS for e-commerce that they grew through cold outbound to ~$50K MRR before selling to a US fund in 2025; Dylan Teixeira independently scaled and sold Edusign the same year. The "finding quality leads" pain that Pierre-Eliott names as the founding insight (wearefounders.uk, 2026) is autobiographical, not analytical -- it is the part of CoCo AI that broke them, and they are now selling the fix. Where Gojiberry AI diverges from the Anduril rung is consequence magnitude: Luckey was rebuilding defense hardware because he had touched the moral edges of VR; Pierre-Eliott is rebuilding the outbound stack because he had touched the cost of an SDR. Same epistemic shape, much smaller ceiling.

The pass example I wrote into my own framework -- "the consensus YC AI-agent darling, two ex-FAANG PMs, deck opens with TAM" -- is exactly the low rung that Gojiberry AI maps onto for magical weirdness. Gojiberry AI is not laughed at; it is the category six top funds are racing into. The moat the YC page describes -- "every campaign makes Gojiberry smarter" on public LinkedIn signals -- is the same moat Clay claimed at $3.1B (TechCrunch, June 2025), the same moat Apollo claims on a 250M-contact DB at $150M ARR (Sacra, 2025), and the same moat 11x.ai's "Alice" claimed before the churn surfaced. G2 reviewers already note the product "depends heavily on social and public signals," meaning the moat is rentable from LinkedIn, not owned. I am never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Gojiberry AI is playing is the one everyone is playing.

Single-CEO clarity compounds well here over 18 months. Pierre-Eliott is the named CEO, Rom?n CMO, Dylan CTO -- titles are delineated and, critically, two of the three already survived a full cycle together: founding, scaling, and selling CoCo AI. That is real conflict-resolution telemetry, the structural opposite of the Yusupov/Kroll partnership I watched implode at HQ Trivia (2017). The risk vector here is not the founder pair I have data on; it is Dylan as the outside partner inheriting a relationship he did not build. I would want to know which of the three controls the tiebreaker vote when the inevitable enterprise-versus-SMB roadmap fight arrives -- the dossier does not say.

The capital-intensity score would flip a full tier only if Gojiberry AI were doing something the dossier never claims: owning a proprietary first-party data layer (a Flock-Safety-style sensor network, a SpaceX-style hardware stack, a Vigil-Labs-style regulated wedge). Gojiberry AI is a $99/month SaaS on top of scraped public signals. The category has no permitting, no sensors, no regulators, no physical artifact. This is the lane I structurally underweight; nothing in the Gojiberry AI dossier pulls it out of that lane.

Flock Safety (~2018) is the single anchor for physical verifiability -- I read the founder's Wi-Fi name at a Four Seasons caf? before any pitch -- and Gojiberry AI passes a much weaker version of the same test. A normal SMB operator can sign up at $99/month, point it at a LinkedIn-active ICP, and feel the reply lift or the lack of it inside a week. The Product Hunt launch on March 2, 2026 with ~40% positive sentiment (syncgtm.com), the G2 5.0 across 11 sub-50-employee reviews, the 2,000 paying customers at $2.5M ARR -- these are tactile, observable artifacts I can verify without the founders in the room. Not Pok?mon-Go-in-the-park observable, but observable.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Gojiberry AI lands between $12-25M ARR and is acqui-hired by Apollo, HubSpot, or Salesloft for $80-180M, or it follows the 11x.ai trajectory into 60%+ SMB churn once the LinkedIn signal arbitrage normalizes. The mechanism is the high/low signature: founders this competent at bootstrapped B2B SaaS reliably reach modest scale, and categories this consensus reliably commoditize before they fund-return. The asymmetric upside that justifies a Long Journey check -- the Crusoe-to-Stargate path -- requires either a category nobody else can enter or a founder doing something magically weird. Gojiberry AI has neither.

The bull case stings: Postmates (2011) and Uber (2010) looked like consensus consumer apps when I wrote those checks, and what I was actually buying was distribution infrastructure in disguise. Maybe Gojiberry AI is the outbound-rails layer that becomes default for the SMB segment Clay and Apollo cannot economically serve at their cost structures, and Pierre-Eliott's bootstrapped instinct -- profitable at $2.5M ARR, 35% MoM -- is exactly the operator DNA that wins SMB durability where 11x.ai's enterprise-priced AI SDR could not. I take the analogy seriously and still pass, because Uber's defensibility was a two-sided physical network and Postmates' was a courier graph; Gojiberry AI's "defensibility" is a learning loop on scraped LinkedIn signals any competitor with Clay-scale capital can rebuild in two quarters. The verdict flips if Gojiberry AI lands a proprietary first-party signal partnership -- a CRM data exclusive, a payments-rail integration, a private intent network -- that cannot be scraped. Until then, this is a great little business and not my check.
