# Soria -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Soria is a former Bank of America healthcare equity-research VP building a better Excel model for other Bank of America healthcare equity-research VPs, and that sentence is the entire investment thesis collapsed into one line. Adam Ron's LinkedIn -- "Former BofA, VP Equity Research, healthcare facilities and managed care" -- is the cleanest example of domain-expert-continuity I have read in this batch, and the product (125+ data sources aggregated into a "sector model") is the cleanest example of an optimization play. The dimensional signature here is highest on founder-market fit and lowest on consequence magnitude. That pairing names the company precisely: a useful tool built by exactly the right person for a workflow that does not, if Soria wins, change a civilizational variable.

On consequence, my anchor is **OpenAI** (2019) -- I wrote the $50M first VC check because the success-state was the redrawing of cognitive labor itself, the $15T frame I have repeated since. Soria's maximum-success state is "healthcare equity analysts at bulge-bracket banks and the largest hedge funds maintain models faster and with fresher CMS/state-agency data." That is a productivity gain inside an existing $24K/seat Bloomberg buyer category, not the disappearance of an incumbent regime. The dossier does not even attempt a TAM and instead anchors to Bloomberg's 3,430 customer companies -- which tells me the ceiling Soria is reaching for is a vertical slice of an already-served seat, not a new economic mechanism. I do not see the **Impossible Foods** (2011) pattern of "animal protein production goes away." I see "healthcare analysts spend less time in Excel."

The KiOR (~2008) failure mechanism was capital absorbed against falsifiable scientific miracles -- catalyst yield, feedstock economics, refinery integration -- where the consequence was redrawing the global liquid-fuels market. Soria contains zero falsifiable miracles. DBOS-based pipelines, an Excel add-in, an MCP server, LLM-assisted extraction of state-agency files -- none of this is a miracle, none of it would repel a consensus VC, and any Tier-1 generalist can underwrite this exact thesis at a $10-15M post. The Daloopa $13M July 2025 round and Hebbia's $13M ARR / $700M valuation tell me capital is already pricing the adjacent layer. Soria's wedge -- vertical depth on healthcare data -- is a product decision, not a technical risk only Khosla Ventures will hold.

Founder-defies-expert-consensus is where Soria scores lowest in absolute terms and where my TED Talk applies directly: experts extrapolate the past, and Adam Ron is bringing the BofA managed-care coverage playbook into software form. There is no contradicted-his-own-field moment, no public thesis that drew ridicule from sell-side colleagues, no instigator posture. Cameron Spiller's resume (Pinwheel, Findigs, Trigo Data, Virginia Tech 2015) is solid data-platform engineering and tells me the system will get built; it does not tell me anyone defied anything. The third founder is not even identified in the dossier, which is itself a small signal about external legibility.

Learning rate is the dimension I score most generously because the dossier is silent rather than negative -- Soria is too young for a documented pivot, and I do not punish absence on a four-month-old company. But there is no **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot story, no prior exit, no thesis update. Counterfactual to flip this tier: if Adam Ron had sold a prior healthcare-data company and explicitly said "I built the wrong shape last time, the right shape is vertical-first sector models," I would re-rate Soria's learning rate dimension upward by a full tier. The dossier does not give me that.

Required capital profile is where Soria most clearly is not my deal. A 3-person team in New York building enterprise SaaS for buy-side and sell-side desks reaches $1M ARR on $3-5M of seed capital and gets to $10M ARR on another $15M Series A. I am structurally over-resourced for this round -- the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) $115M Series A pattern is the inverse profile. Soria is a deal Bessemer, Insight, or a YC-aligned seed fund underwrites correctly. It is not a deal that requires my balance sheet or my tolerance for a 90% failure prior.

My 36-month forecast: Soria is at $4-9M ARR with 15-40 enterprise seats across 3-6 named healthcare desks, and is acquired by S&P Global, FactSet, or Daloopa for $40-90M -- a fine outcome for the founders, a non-event for a Khosla fund. The mechanism is straightforward: vertical depth wins a beachhead, horizontal incumbents (Hebbia, AlphaSense, Bloomberg's BloombergGPT layer) bundle "good enough" healthcare data into existing seats, and Soria's most rational exit is to the data-infrastructure layer that needs healthcare verticalization without building it.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Glean** exception -- Arvind Jain was ex-Google search, a paradigmatic domain expert, and I overrode my Founder-Defies-Consensus prior because the consequence magnitude on Fortune-500 information asymmetry was real. The bull on Soria runs the same play: Ron is the right insider, vertical-first compounds into a multi-sector Bloomberg killer, and the $24K/seat incumbent economics are large enough that a 5% capture is a billion-dollar outcome. The bear that wins is that Glean's exception was earned by horizontal consequence across every Fortune 500 knowledge worker, while Soria's dossier explicitly caps the product at healthcare and offers no disclosed ARR, no named bulge-bracket logo, and no path-to-second-sector commitment. The flip condition is concrete: three named enterprise contracts at $500K+ ACV with disclosed customer names plus a shipped second sector model within 18 months. Absent that, this is a pass at pre-seed and a likely pass at Series A.
