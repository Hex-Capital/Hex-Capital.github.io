# Walter -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Walter sits in a real schlep zone with founders who show no hacker trail to mine it -- that is the signature. Nikolas Keller is a CDTM dropout claiming "multiple 9-figure customers in packaging and defense" while his CTO Lukas Postulka has no public profile this research could confirm, and that combination -- genuinely unpleasant enterprise work paired with founders who left no GitHub footprint -- is the entire diagnosis I need to write a check or not.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the right HIGH-rung comparison on the schlep axis, and Walter loads the analogy honestly: SAP / Oracle NetSuite / Microsoft Dynamics 365 write-back, SOC 2 and ISO 27001 "in progress," 270+ languages for packaging-and-defense back offices nobody at a Spring 2026 batch dinner wants to talk about. Armstrong won his check on appetite for the bitcoin-compliance grind that other applicants flinched from; Walter is running at an analogous grind in industrial document workflows where Rossum's $100M Series A and Hyperscience's $300M+ have already proven the work is unpleasant enough to fund. Where Walter diverges from the Coinbase rung is that Armstrong personally built the Android wallet that rescued his application -- Keller's analogous artifact, per the dossier, is a CDTM "life-paths" tool and an earlier customer-support-automation product that was pivoted into manufacturing, which is a thinner pre-funding artifact than the schlep deserves.

The Pass Example haunts the hacker-genealogy axis -- the two-MBA AI-wrapper team with the polished deck and no shipped artifact -- because the failure mechanism there is "no GitHub trail, no Hacker News presence, no demo on the table." Walter is not that team, but Walter is not the inverse either: the dossier records no public repos for Keller, no public repos for Postulka, no confirmed identity for Postulka at all beyond a RocketReach hit that this research explicitly declined to verify. Patrick Collison had Ireland's Young Scientist Lisp prize on file before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz co-authored RSS before **Reddit** (2005); the dossier on Walter offers a culinary CV at Zeitlang and Le 1051 for Keller, which is charming but not a hacker tribal marker. Hackers leave tracks, and Walter's tracks point at restaurants and CDTM coursework, not at Lisp or compilers or a weekend project that hit HN.

On organic-idea fit, the mechanism that should be compounding over the next 18 months -- founder using own product daily, founder dreaming about edge cases in the shower -- is missing from Walter because Keller has never worked a manufacturing back office. The dossier flags the pivot: first customers hired Walter for "customer support and software development" automation, current positioning is manufacturing. That is the inverse of Drew Houston forgetting his USB stick; that is "spotted market opportunity," which is exactly the language from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) I score against. Walter's organic-idea score does not compound -- it decays each month Keller does not become an SAP shop-floor user himself.

On animal resourcefulness, the counterfactual is sharp: if Walter's "multiple 9-figure customers" in packaging and defense were named and the contracts were $200K-$500K each, this score flips a tier and I revisit the whole verdict. A college dropout from Munich landing Lockheed-adjacent defense logos before a Spring 2026 YC check is genuinely Armstrong-shaped behavior; an unnamed claim in a YC description is not, because every Spring 2026 company says some version of this. Walter's resourcefulness placement is pinned by the verifiability gap, not the ambition.

The Collison brothers at **Stripe** (2010) are the single-anchor parallel for co-founder configuration, and Walter does not get to sit near them: Patrick and John roomed together for two decades and had already shipped **Auctomatic** (2007) together. The Walter dossier reports "no public data on co-founder history" between Keller and Postulka -- no shared employer, no shared university confirmed, no shipped prior project together. Two founders is the right number; unknown chemistry is the wrong evidence base, and the YC interview is where Harj Taggar will have already resolved this in a way I cannot from a dossier.

My 36-month forecast for Walter: stuck between $2M and $6M ARR with 6-12 industrial logos, acquired by Rossum or a mid-market ERP vendor for under $50M, or pivoted into a defense-procurement-only niche where the 9-figure-customer claim was always concentrated. The mechanism producing that forecast is the signature -- Walter has a real schlep but a non-hacker team to defend it, which is the profile of a competent vertical integrator that gets rolled up rather than the profile of a "next Google" outcome. Rossum already raised $100M to do the same write-back at horizontal scope; Workist already positions on manufacturing in Germany; the acquirer is already on the cap-table of competitors.

The bull case that stings: Brian Armstrong was a non-obvious hacker too -- a Goldman engineer with a hand-built wallet, not a Lisp prodigy -- and the **Coinbase** (2012) outcome came from his refusal to leave the schlep, not from his GitHub. If Keller's unverified 9-figure customers are real, the dropout-plus-enterprise-traction pattern is Armstrong-shaped and the hacker-genealogy gap stops mattering, because schlep moats outlast hacker moats in regulated verticals. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull is the pivot from customer-support automation to manufacturing: Armstrong did not pivot from a different vertical into bitcoin -- he started there because he wanted to. Walter started somewhere else and arrived at manufacturing because the contracts were available. That is the wrong direction on the organic-idea axis, and the schlep moat does not compensate for it inside my framework. The condition that flips my verdict: Keller publishes the names of two 9-figure customers, posts the contract sizes, and Postulka surfaces a shipped technical artifact -- any one of those is necessary, all three would make me wire the money.
