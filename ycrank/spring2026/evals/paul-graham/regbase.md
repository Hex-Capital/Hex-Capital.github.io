# Regbase -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Regbase is the lawyer-who-lived-the-pain archetype with the pre-funding-stunt slot empty. Natalie Aresta-Katz spent her Paul, Weiss years personally tracking laws across dozens of countries and every U.S. state for the firm's ESG practice — that is exactly the organic-idea signature from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012). What is missing from the Regbase dossier is the cereal-box moment, the Armstrong-wallet moment, the thing the founders did before the YC check arrived that proves they will outwork the thing.

Drew Houston shipped his **Dropbox** (2007) demo to Hacker News because he kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT — the product was a literal translation of his own friction, and the demo existed before the seed. Regbase has the friction half of that pattern in a way most YC applicants fake: Aresta-Katz was the human doing the multilingual regulatory crawl manually at a top-tier law firm, and her co-founder Isiah Godoy was the paralegal next to her doing the same grind. Where Regbase diverges from the **Dropbox** (2007) rung is the artifact — the dossier names no shipped prototype, no recall benchmark against Harvey or Legora, no public source-list, no demo video. Aresta-Katz has the founder-problem fit; she has not yet shown the founder-artifact fit, and at Spring 2026 pre-seed I would expect at least one to exist.

The MBA-team pass — the polished AI-wrapper with the $50B TAM slide and the "we'll hire engineers" plan — fails because the founders had no skin in the problem and no pre-funding hustle. Regbase escapes the first half of that failure mode cleanly (Aresta-Katz lived inside the workflow at Paul, Weiss; Godoy was a paralegal there) but the second half hangs over the Regbase dossier uncomfortably. NYU B.S., Columbia MBA, Paul, Weiss ESG research — that is the credentialed bio order, not the animal bio order. There is no stunt revenue, no unscalable manual onboarding anecdote, no Aresta-Katz-stayed-up-three-nights-to-crawl-the-Bundestag story. Absence of artifact in a Spring 2026 application is the loudest signal in the Regbase folder.

Multilingual, sub-national, draft-bill regulatory ingestion is a genuine schlep — non-English statutory text from prefecture-level Japanese sites or Brazilian state assemblies is exactly the kind of work FiscalNote's $84.8M ARR pipeline was not architected for and which Harvey and Legora consistently route around. Over 18 months this dimension compounds well if Regbase actually does the unglamorous coverage work jurisdiction by jurisdiction, because each added source is a citation incumbents cannot match without re-plumbing their ingestion stack. It compounds poorly if Regbase leans on a generic agent calling a generic LLM over generic web search — then Legora's $5.6B war chest, announced TechCrunch April 2026, eats the wedge in one product release. The schlep is real in Regbase; whether the founders are doing it the slow correct way is unverifiable from the dossier.

Ethan Shea was Staff Engineer at Vercel doing security observability and agentic infrastructure — that is a credible technical bio, not a hacker-tribe bio. Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp work before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz co-wrote RSS before **Reddit** (2005); these were hacker-genealogy markers visible from outside the company. For Regbase's hacker-genealogy score to flip a tier, Shea would need a public GitHub with non-trivial agentic-extraction work, an OSS contribution to a crawler or LLM-eval framework, or a Hacker News presence predating Regbase — the dossier finds none of these, only the @ethshea handle with no count. The Vercel-staff-engineer credential is a real positive against the MBA-team failure mode; it is not the Collison-prize positive that pulls a check across the line on technical taste alone.

The Regbase trio configuration is technically correct — three founders beats solo, per "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) — and Aresta-Katz plus Godoy have the Paul, Weiss shared-trench history that **Reddit** (2005)'s Huffman and Ohanian had at the UVA dorm. The crack in Regbase's team configuration is that the CTO Shea has no documented prior relationship with either of the two ex-Paul-Weiss founders; he is the technical hire wearing a co-founder title, which is a different organism than the **Airbnb** (2009) trio who had lived together for years before Obama O's. This is not disqualifying. It is the kind of thing the in-person YC interview surfaces in twenty minutes.

My 36-month forecast on Regbase: $1.5M–$4M ARR by mid-2029, distributed through the Paul, Weiss network and adjacent AmLaw 50 firms, then acquired by Thomson Reuters or LexisNexis for a low-nine-figure number once Legora and Harvey ship their multilingual sub-national modules and the coverage moat starts compressing. This forecast sits exactly on the high-organic-pain / low-animal-resourcefulness signature: founders who deeply understand the problem but did not show the pre-funding stunt almost always build a real, mid-sized, acquirable business — they do not build the **Stripe** (2010) outcome, because the **Stripe** (2010) outcome requires both.

The bull case on Regbase is that I am applying the artifact filter to a domain where the artifact looks like a database, not a demo video — and the database is being built right now in YC, invisibly. Aresta-Katz is the **Coinbase** (2012) Armstrong of regulatory compliance grind, Shea is the Vercel-grade agentic engineer who can actually build the crawler, and the schlep of non-English sub-national ingestion is exactly the "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) pattern Alexandr Wang named as **Scale AI**'s origin. That case stings because it is structurally identical to the move I made on **Coinbase** (2012). What dismantles it for me on Regbase specifically is the bio order — Aresta-Katz's narrative leads with Columbia MBA and Paul, Weiss, not with a shipped artifact, and Armstrong led with the wallet. I would flip to Invest if Regbase publishes within six months a recall benchmark showing >40% catch-rate on non-English sub-national developments that Harvey and Legora miss, with a named non-Paul-Weiss enterprise customer attached. Until then, Neutral with a downward lean.
