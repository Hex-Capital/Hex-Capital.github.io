# Hub.xyz -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

500K contributors. 10M+ replies. 321K Twitter followers. These are big numbers for a pre-seed company, and they're also exactly the numbers you get when you dangle unredeemed crypto tokens in front of people on the internet. Hub.xyz has built what looks like a data collection network but functions as an airdrop farming operation — IQ Points convertible to future $HUB tokens, ambassador programs, Bitget listings, SwissBorg backing. The question I always ask is "what if they're right?" and here I have to be specific: right about *what*? That airdrop farmers will produce training data good enough for OpenAI and Google to buy? That's the bet, and I don't think the market is wrong to be skeptical. The AI training data market is one of the hottest categories in venture right now — Scale AI at a $14.3B implied valuation through the Meta deal, Surge AI doing $1.4B in bootstrapped revenue, Toloka with $72M and Bezos backing, Labelbox at $189M raised. Hub.xyz is tagged "Artificial Intelligence, Data Labeling, Big Data, AI" on YC. This is consensus territory. There's no mispricing here — every smart AI investor is looking at data infrastructure. The crypto wrapper doesn't make it contrarian; it makes it confusing.

Hub.xyz's pitch is that crypto-incentivized contributors collecting multimodal data across 150+ countries creates something Scale AI can't replicate. Let me steelman this hard because the timing angle deserves it: Meta's acquisition of 49% of Scale AI in June 2025 genuinely fucked the market. OpenAI and Google cut ties with Scale. That fragmentation is real, and it creates a window for alternative providers. If Hub.xyz had named enterprise customers — even one signed LOI with a frontier lab — I'd take the "data network as infrastructure primitive" thesis seriously. This could be the Eaze play: a category where discomfort (crypto-native vendor selling to enterprise AI) creates genuine mispricing while the underlying demand is undeniable. But Eaze at least had paying customers buying cannabis through the app. Hub.xyz has zero disclosed revenue, zero named enterprise buyers, and a contributor base whose primary motivation appears to be accumulating tokens for a future TGE. The condition that would flip me: show me a signed contract with a top-20 AI lab purchasing Hub.xyz data at meaningful ACV. That single fact would restructure everything.

Armin Kiani and Tim Sprecher connected through Q42 venture fund — Armin as an LP, Tim as an advisor. Neither has a prior exit. Neither has documented deep AI/ML research credentials. Tim started his first company at 18 and worked in growth ops at RevX. Armin is a CS graduate who's been an LP at small funds. The co-founder relationship through a VC fund is one of my explicit red flags — I've seen the best returns come from founders with years of shared history, like Kortina and Magdon-Ismail building Venmo after knowing each other since college. Hub.xyz doesn't give me the "sane person, insane idea" signal. The idea isn't insane in the way that makes me excited — it's not "we're going to make people invest in other people's future earnings" (that's insane) or "we're going to build a new Layer 1 blockchain when everyone says Ethereum won" (that's insane). Hub.xyz is "we're going to use crypto tokens to incentivize a distributed data collection network" — which is an idea that a dozen DePIN projects have already tried. It's complicated, not crazy. There's a difference.

Hub.xyz fails the AI kill test in a way that matters structurally. Toloka already runs 200K+ crowdsourced annotators. Scale AI already has global annotator operations. Adding token incentives to an existing annotator workforce is a product feature, not a new company. I invest in protocols, not applications — Solana was a new consensus mechanism, Lightning Labs was Bitcoin L2 infrastructure, Aleo was a privacy protocol. Hub.xyz calls itself an "API for real-world training data" but it's a marketplace sitting on top of Ethereum smart contracts. The Ethereum layer is someone else's protocol. The data collection is an application. Hub.xyz has no physical-world moat (compare to Metropolis running actual parking infrastructure), no protocol-level network effects (compare to Solana's validator economics), and no creator-audience relationship that AI can't replicate. It's software that wraps other people's infrastructure, which is exactly the kind of thing I've been saying publicly gets commoditized. The $1.7M in total funding against competitors with $189M to $14.3B in backing means Hub.xyz would need to be 100x more capital-efficient to compete, and the 10-person team with one active job posting at $70K-$100K suggests modest burn — which I respect — but the token model undermines that discipline. When your business is designed around a future Token Generation Event, you're not building to survive on revenue. You're building to raise through token sales. That's a fundraising operation, not a business.

The L?onie Nguyen situation captures something about Hub.xyz that bothers me more broadly: she's listed as co-founder on Tracxn but angel backer on SwissBorg's page, and she's simultaneously CEO of Meey Group in Vietnam. Armin Kiani appears under multiple names. The website returned a 403 error during research. These aren't dealbreakers individually, but they compound into a picture of a company where the presentation layer doesn't match the reality layer — and when you're asking enterprise AI companies to trust your data provenance and compliance frameworks, that gap matters. Hub.xyz claims GDPR and CCPA compliance with "full provenance and audit trails," but the core product involves anonymous crypto-incentivized contributors running a "Dataminer app" that shares bandwidth. The tension between "enterprise-grade data compliance" and "anonymous airdrop farmers running bandwidth-sharing software" is not something a GDPR checkbox resolves.

Pass. Hub.xyz sits in a consensus-hot category (AI training data) with no meaningful mispricing — the upside is already in the price across competitors with hundreds of millions in funding. It creates no new primitive; crypto-incentivized data collection is a distribution mechanism for an existing market, not a new behavioral category. The founders lack the deep domain credentials or shared history that would overcome these structural issues. The single condition that flips me: a signed, named enterprise contract with a frontier AI lab proving that token-incentivized contributors produce data worth paying for. Without that, Hub.xyz is a crypto distribution engine looking for an enterprise business model, and I don't see why Scale AI, Toloka, or Surge AI can't add token incentives faster than Hub.xyz can add enterprise credibility.
