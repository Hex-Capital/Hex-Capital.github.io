# Superset -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Three ex-CTOs walk into a YC batch and build the most consensus product in the most consensus category of 2026 -- that is Superset in one line. The high rung on my ladder is Founder Surfaced From Lived Experience: Kiet Ho was CTO at Onlook (W25), Satya Patel CTO at Untether Labs (W23), Avi Peltz CTO at Adam (W25), and they are building the orchestration shell for the exact agent CLIs they used as operators. The low rung is Magically Weird: Conductor has already raised $24.5M and ships the same git-worktree primitive, with Crystal, Emdash, cmux, Vibe Kanban, Agentastic, FleetCode, and Sculptor crowding the same surface. Superset is a competent operator-built tool in the loudest room of the batch, and I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing -- the game here is "be the last wrapper standing," which is a melee, not a moat.

Anduril sits at the top of my lived-experience ladder because Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus to scale before he ever touched a defense contract -- the prior-product trajectory was the qualification. Superset's three co-founders share a real version of that pattern in miniature: each one was the CTO of a developer-facing AI company that depended on Claude Code, Codex, and Copilot, so they have authentically felt the parallel-agent management pain that Superset addresses, and Vlad Arbatov at Loyal and Leo at Outlit calling it "default tools" reads as believable operator-to-operator distribution. Where Superset diverges from the Luckey rung is autobiography depth -- being a former CTO at Onlook is a credential earned in 2025, not a decade-long scar like Luckey's hardware-from-the-garage origin, and the dossier shows no specific physical moment any of the three felt the problem before they were employed near it. This is founder-market fit in the conventional sense, not the autobiographical sense I underwrite.

Conductor at $24.5M raised plus an eight-tool open-source pile-up is precisely the failure mechanism I am scanning for on the Magically Weird axis -- the pattern where no one passed for aesthetic reasons because everyone is already in. Superset's response is breadth across more agent CLIs and a local-first posture versus Conductor's macOS/Claude Code narrowness, which is a real product distinction but not a contrarian one; it is the second-mover argument that every well-built wrapper makes. The Launch HN drew a clean 512-upvote Product Hunt #1, Vercel wrote a blog post, and YC partner Nicolas Dessaigne signed off -- those are exactly the consensus signals that invert my thesis. Find them before it becomes consensus; Superset is already consensus.

Three ex-CTOs and zero named CEO in the dossier is the *exact* GameCrush-and-HQ-Trivia setup I have been burned by before -- when none of the founders has organizational tiebreaker authority by background, the unresolved governance question doesn't get easier as the company grows, it gets harder. Superset's team page lists all three symmetrically and the YC page does not designate a CEO, which I read as "they have not had the conversation yet," not as "they are coordinated and the dossier omitted it." The mechanism here compounds over 18 months: three CTOs argue about architecture, none has the operating-authority muscle that one of them needed to build at the prior YC company, and the first hard pivot -- which is coming, because Cursor will move down-stack or Anthropic will ship first-party orchestration -- becomes a three-way negotiation instead of a CEO call.

Superset is pure software wrapper over third-party CLIs under Elastic License 2.0, and on the Capital-Intensive/Unsexy axis it is the inverse of every check I have written that I am proud of. SpaceX in 2008 was rockets; Anduril in 2017 was defense; Crusoe was flared gas in North Dakota. The counterfactual that would flip Superset's score on this axis would be if Superset were building a hardware substrate -- a local accelerator, an on-prem agent runtime, a regulated-industry compliance layer for code agents in healthcare or finance -- because that is the lane where my edge compounds and the polymath generalist cannot rebuild it in a quarter. As shipped, a polymath could rebuild Superset's wrapper in a quarter; the dossier even names eight people who already did.

Postmates is my single-anchor parallel for Physical Verifiability: I bet on it because the delivery happened in front of me, and I can install Superset from `github.com/superset-sh/superset` tonight and watch a Claude Code worktree spin up. 11.4k stars and 952 forks is a real, observable, hand-it-to-a-friend artifact, and that is the one dimension where Superset earns close to the upper end of its ladder rung. The product exists, it is downloadable, engineers at OpenAI and Vercel apparently use it -- but a developer tool's tactility is not the same tactility as Pokémon Go on a street corner, and the wild I observe it in is GitHub trending, which is also where the eight competitors live.

Forecast at 36 months: Superset reaches $3-8M ARR through Pro and Team seats, gets acquired by Vercel (whose engineering blog already featured them) or GitHub for $40-150M, or merges into Conductor; it does not return a Long Journey fund. The mechanism is straightforward -- the founder layer is real enough to ship and sell, but the category lacks the contrarian-origin economics that produce a Crusoe-shaped outcome.

The bull case stings on one front: DeepMind is the Known Exception where I overrode my anti-pedigree default because polymath cross-domain compounding (chess prodigy, neuroscientist, game designer) beat the lived-experience filter -- and three ex-CTOs of three different YC W23/W25 companies is a small-scale polymath stack, where each founder brings a distinct prior agent-tooling reference architecture (Onlook visual, Untether infra, Adam CAD). Cursor proved the category can hit $2B ARR in 18 months from a wrapper that nobody thought was defensible either, so dismissing dev-tools wrappers as commodity has been wrong before. What dismantles the bull case is the dossier's own competitor list -- DeepMind had no eight-tool peer set in 2013, and Cursor had no $24.5M-funded Conductor sitting on the same git-worktree primitive. Superset is the third or fourth mover in a category where the first mover is already funded and the incumbent (Anysphere) is at $50B-talks scale. **The condition that flips my verdict: Superset signs two named Fortune 500 enterprise contracts at $100K+ ACV within nine months, demonstrating the wrapper has converted into a real distribution channel before Conductor or Cursor closes the gap.**
