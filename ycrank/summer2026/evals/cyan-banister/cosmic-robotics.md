# Cosmic Robotics -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

James Emerick is a four-generation construction kid who went to Built Robotics before he started Cosmic Robotics, which is exactly the biography I hunt for and almost never see in a solar-adjacent deck. What Cosmic Robotics is, in my portfolio grammar, is a bloodline-authentic frontier-hardware founder attacking a category that has already stopped being weird — Terabase Energy raised $130M and Terafab V2 is in the field, which means the "nobody wants to touch this" premium I paid for at Crusoe in 2018 is not on offer here.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril in 2017 is the load-bearing rung for Cosmic Robotics on the founder-lived-experience axis: a hardware kid who shipped a comparable-scale product to a prior domain (Oculus for Luckey; the Built Robotics autonomous-earthmover fleet for Emerick) and then walked into a physical-world category most of the Sand Hill circuit refused to underwrite. Emerick's four-generation construction family is not a resume line — it is the same "of the place, not visiting it" quality that made me trust Chase Lochmiller on a well pad. Where Cosmic Robotics diverges from the Anduril rung is authorship density: Luckey personally invented the hardware category he was selling, whereas Emerick was an early employee at Built Robotics rather than the founder, so the "shipped a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain BEFORE" test is passed by adjacency rather than authorship. That is still top-quartile founder-lived-experience for a pre-seed dossier, and Lewis Jones layering Relativity Space + SpaceX + NASA JPL underneath as CTO is the aerospace-grade reliability spine that makes the lunar mandate more than a slide.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on failed on a specific mechanism — six top funds bidding, priced as if the outcome were already certain, moat sitting in a model rather than in a founder. Trace that mechanism through Cosmic Robotics: the round is a $4M pre-seed led by Giant Ventures with HCVC and MaC, which is not a hot-round pattern, but AES Maximo has already put 100 MW in the ground, Sarcos is partnering with Blattner, and Terabase has ~30x Cosmic Robotics's capital. Cosmic Robotics is not consensus-priced, but the *category* is now consensus, and that is the version of the failure mode I have to sit with. The saving grace is that traditional EPCs have channel-conflict against building this in-house — SunRobi becoming Cosmic Robotics's first certified operator is the shape of a real distribution wedge — but "magically weird" this is not.

Emerick is the CEO and Jones is the CTO with no dossier signal of 50/50 title ambiguity, which is the clean structural inverse of the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll pattern I keep having to remind myself about — I will take the absence of governance drama at face value for a two-person team where the operating spine (construction robotics) and the technical spine (aerospace hardware) don't overlap enough to fight over.

The capital-intensive-frontier-hardware axis is where Cosmic Robotics compounds over 18 months in a way a software wrapper cannot: every panel install at the 30–40 second rate feeds a terrain-and-edge-case dataset that a Terabase field-factory approach structurally cannot collect, because Terabase's tent-based prefab line never leaves a controlled environment. The DOE American-Made Solar Prize plus the JLL Foundation grant is the same non-dilutive validation trellis that pattern-matches to how the frontier-hardware sleeve of my book actually gets built. The NASA lunar contract not appearing in CLPS/LSII/SBIR public listings is a real hair I want the founder in a room to answer for, not a deal-killer.

Physical-world verifiability sits where Flock Safety sat for me in ~2018: a tactile artifact I cannot casually install in my kitchen but can observe in the wild if I drive to it. Cosmic Robotics has put its Cosmic-1A on "some of the largest solar farms in the US" for "tens of thousands of panel installs" — that is not an abstraction, that is a machine I can watch place a panel every 30 to 40 seconds. It is a notch below the Postmates-in-front-of-me / Uber-I-rode-it standard because a normal human cannot summon it in 48 hours, but it is a full tier above any B2B SaaS dossier I read this batch.

My 36-month forecast: Cosmic Robotics raises a $25–45M Series A led by a climate or deep-tech fund inside 18 months, lands 3–5 certified operators beyond SunRobi, reaches $5–15M ARR through the robotics-as-a-service licensing model, and gets acquired by a larger integrator — Terabase, an EPC roll-up, or an AES-scale strategic — rather than surviving as the independent OEM that defines the category. The mechanism producing that ceiling is exactly the highest/lowest signature: Emerick's founder-of-the-place quality is real enough to generate a Series A but the category is too consensus and too well-capitalized at the top of the stack for Cosmic Robotics to run all the way through as the standalone winner without a capital surge that this round does not portend.

The bull case that stings is this: Anduril in 2017 also faced a well-capitalized adjacent incumbency (Lockheed, Raytheon, Northrop) with 30x-plus the capital, and I wrote the check anyway because Palmer was of the place and the incumbents had a channel-conflict blind spot. Cosmic Robotics has the same shape — Terabase's $130M is a factory-line bet, not a mobile-robot bet, and traditional EPCs cannot build this in-house without cannibalizing their own labor billings. If that reads is correct, my $5–15M ARR forecast is a lazy anchor and the correct forecast is a category-defining OEM. The reason I do not fully cross to Strong Invest today is that Emerick was an early employee at Built Robotics, not its founder, which puts him one step off the Luckey authorship pattern that made the Anduril check unmissable. Verdict flips to Strong Invest on one verifiable condition: an independently confirmable NASA or DoD lunar-construction contract award with a named program number, or a signed master services agreement with a top-5 US utility-scale solar EPC beyond SunRobi. Until then I am writing the check but not sizing it like Anduril.
