# qomplement -- Elad Gil Evaluation

qomplement is a cleanly-architected single-miracle bet sitting in the worst possible structural position for a single-miracle bet: a vertical document-ops overlay in a category where Pivot just stacked $70M Series B (SiliconANGLE, May 2026) to consolidate horizontally. The high/low signature here is *clean miracle, weak index* -- Kerim Taray and Andres Garza have isolated the technical risk to LLM document extraction accuracy on grocery and trucking POs, which is the right architecture, but qomplement does not sit in the workflow path of a compounding category the way **Stripe** (2010) sat in payments. It sits in a knife fight.

**Decagon** (2023) is the load-bearing analogy on the single-miracle axis -- one validated capability (LLM accuracy at customer-support workflows) plus GTM execution, no chemistry risk, no regulatory miracle, no new consumer behavior. qomplement runs the same template: assume LLMs can extract POs and freight invoices reliably (a 2024-2025 step-function), and the remaining work is enterprise integration and sales. Where qomplement diverges from Decagon is the catalyst clarity -- Decagon launched directly into a post-ChatGPT customer-support reset where every CX leader was being told to deploy agents this quarter; qomplement's catalyst is inferred from the dossier, not named, and the 10,000+ docs/day across 20+ companies claim is the kind of usage statistic that's easy to disclose but hard to verify without logos, which in this AI cycle is exactly the *false signal vs. just working* distinction I worry about (TechCrunch, Nov 2025).

**Lyft** Series C is the cautionary analog on the index dimension -- I passed because rideshare was winner-take-all and #2 captures a fraction of #1's economics. qomplement isn't network-effect winner-take-all, but it has a structurally similar problem: Pivot processes ~€2.5B in invoices annually with $70M of capital, ORO Labs is positioning as procurement orchestration's "Category King," and SAP, Oracle, and NetSuite are all publishing agentic roadmaps that could ship native overlays inside the systems qomplement integrates with (SAP "Supply Chain Trends for 2026"). qomplement's vertical wedge (grocery + trucking) is a real wedge, but a wedge is not an index -- this is a share fight against a horizontal entrant with 14x the capital, not a *basket of every cryptocurrency* position.

On the catalyst dimension, the mechanism question is whether LLM document-extraction accuracy crossed an enterprise-usable threshold in a specific 24-month window the way Pentagon software inadequacy crossed one for **Anduril** (2022). The dossier hedges this as [Inferred], not sourced -- there's no named regulatory shift, no Walmart memo, no grocery-chain procurement mandate in the last 24 months that opens this market specifically. The Pivot Series B confirms category formation, which is consensus-pays evidence in the *Harvey* template, but qomplement is the venture-stage entrant chasing Pivot's wake, not the catalyst-rider sitting in front of it.

On revenue velocity, the counterfactual is sharp: if qomplement disclosed $200K MRR growing 25% MoM with two named Fortune-500 grocery logos, this score flips a full tier and the analysis becomes whether to pay up the way I paid up on **Harvey** (2023). qomplement's dossier instead gives me 10,000+ docs/day and 20+ companies with no revenue, no MoM curve, and no named logos -- in the AI cycle, silence on the curve is itself the signal, because real growth is the easiest thing to disclose.

On founder shipping rhythm, qomplement is the closest thing to a positive surprise. Kerim Taray and Andres Garza built RPA implementations for Mexican enterprises before qomplement, ran a university AI research lab (15,000 minutes of wildlife computer vision), and shipped a *prior* product on Product Hunt in May 2025 ("AI agent that controls your personal computer") that they then pivoted out of -- which compares directly to Aravind at **Perplexity** (2022) shipping between meetings. The pivot itself is informative, not damning: it shows they iterated off a generic agent thesis into vertical document-ops within twelve months, which is the right learning loop. The gap versus the **Perplexity** template is that Aravind's prior shipping was at scale and legible; the Taray/Garza prior CTO role is at "a high-growth startup" unnamed in the dossier.

My 36-month forecast: qomplement gets acqui-hired by SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, or Pivot for $30-90M between 2027 and 2029, having reached $4-10M ARR concentrated in LATAM retail/trucking accounts before US horizontal entrants close the integration gap. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- a clean single-miracle execution team in a non-index position gets bought by the platform that needs the integrations, not built into a standalone outcome, because they don't sit in a workflow path that compounds independently.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that vertical depth in grocery and trucking is exactly the *workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-hard-thing* pattern I run -- **Color Genomics** (2015) was workflow on commodity sequencing, **Anduril** (2022) was software on defense hardware, and qomplement is agents on legacy ERP. Pivot's $70M validates that the category will be capitalized, and qomplement's vertical focus could be the *Brex*-style operational wedge that Pivot's horizontal motion can't replicate. The rebuttal: **Color Genomics** and **Anduril** had founders with prior shipping at scale that I knew personally, and qomplement's dossier names no institutional investors beyond AWS-as-backer, no logos, no revenue curve. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a named grocery or trucking customer at $1M+ ACV disclosed in the next 90 days with month-over-month expansion -- absent that, this is a pass in the Pivot-shadow.
