# Silmaril -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Silmaril is a clean **single-miracle** build (a ModernBERT-variant classifier hitting P90 20ms and 95.60% across 131 attack techniques) sitting in a category where the **distribution position has already been claimed and bundled**: Lakera went to Check Point in 2025, Protect AI Guardian went to Palo Alto Networks in 2025, and the snippet even suggests Promptfoo went to OpenAI. That high/low pairing tells me what Silmaril is — two competent ex-Amazon security builders with a real technical artifact entering a market the incumbents already exited *into* hyperscaler channels. That is the **Lyft Series C** shape dressed up as a YC pre-seed: a credible #2-or-#3 in a category the consensus has already routed around them.

**Anduril** (2022) is the HIGH rung on catalyst — Ukraine in a 24-month window, a market peers had written off, software-on-defense as the displacement path. Silmaril does have a catalyst: agentic frameworks (LangGraph, Claude Code) shipping to production in 2025-26 and the Lakera/Protect AI acquisitions creating a theoretical displacement opening. But where Anduril's catalyst opened a *dormant* market, Silmaril's catalyst opens a *crowded* one — HiddenLayer at $56M raised, Promptfoo at $23.6M, plus the now-bundled Check Point and PANW offerings, plus "over a dozen guardrail providers" per the TrueFoundry review, plus OSS (LLM Guard, NVIDIA NeMo Guardrails). The Silmaril catalyst is real but derivative; this is closer to *Decagon* mid-rung than Anduril high-rung, and it doesn't get the Anduril "everyone misread it as dead" multiplier.

**Lyft** Series C is the LOW rung on index position — #2 in a winner-take-all-or-oligopoly market, distribution structurally inferior, and I passed (and it was a regret, but the framework was right). The Silmaril dossier exhibits that exact failure mechanism in a different guise: prompt-injection defense is not winner-take-all by network effect, but it has the worse property of **bundling-into-platform**, where Check Point and Palo Alto can ship "good-enough" guardrails inside the existing enterprise security SKU customers already buy. The TrueFoundry market review explicitly flags "category absorption risk" — whether LLM-security remains standalone or gets folded into AppSec/observability. Silmaril's "5 lines of code into LangGraph" SDK posture is the *opposite* of an index position: it's a thin developer-tool wedge in a category where the buyer is a CISO who wants one platform invoice. This is the **Lyft** shape, not the **Stripe** shape.

Revenue velocity — apply the absent-evidence default and then some. The dossier reports **$28M cumulative damages prevented** and two case studies at $68M and $20M, but damages-prevented is not revenue; it is the marketing artifact a security startup ships when it cannot yet ship MRR. The customer logos — Microsoft, OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Perplexity, Dropbox — are explicitly described as "targets of whitehat findings rather than paying customers." That's a tell. In the **Harvey** (2023) template, growth was easy to disclose because it was real ($3B→$5B→$8B in months, TechCrunch Nov 2025); silence on ARR in an AI security pitch in 2026 is itself signal that the curve isn't there yet. If Silmaril had even $200K ARR with two named Fortune 500 design partners, that would be in the dossier.

Founder shipping rhythm is where Silmaril gets interesting and almost saves the deal. **Brex** is the closest single-anchor parallel — operationally disciplined builders, ex-payments depth, attacking from a precise founder-market-fit angle. Aum Upadhyay built the AWS security/privacy framework cited at $1.8B in damages prevented; Eduardo Velasco ran low-latency ML at Amazon cited at $400M revenue; and the pair "hacked OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Microsoft 15 times in two weeks" as whitehats per the arion-research LinkedIn post. That last fact is the closest thing in this dossier to the **Perplexity** (2022) "ships between meetings" pattern — they didn't write a pitch deck, they popped four hyperscaler LLM products and used the findings as the cold open. That's a real shipping artifact, not pedigree-only. Founder-market fit on this axis is strong; the problem is the market they shipped into.

Single-miracle architecture is Silmaril's cleanest dimension and closer to the **Decagon** HIGH rung than to the multi-miracle science-project failure mode. The miracle is: "a fine-tuned ModernBERT classifier can hit production-grade latency and accuracy on prompt-injection detection, and a retraining loop can keep up with novel attacks within an hour." That's *one* capability. There's no chemistry risk, no regulatory miracle required, no consumer-behavior change. Silmaril doesn't need new physics; they need GTM and a benchmark someone other than silmaril.dev publishes. That's why this isn't a Strong Pass — the architecture is right; the market position is wrong.

**Forecast (36 months):** Silmaril gets acqui-hired into a security platform — most likely Cloudflare, Snyk, Datadog, or a second-wave PANW/Check Point bolt-on — at $40M–$150M on $1M–$4M ARR, before reaching independent escape velocity. The mechanism: the bundling pressure that absorbed Lakera and Protect AI in 2025 is not a one-time event; it's the steady state of this category, and Silmaril ships a feature, not a platform.

The strongest bull objection: Silmaril is the **Decagon** of the AI-security workflow layer — Aum and Eduardo have founder-market fit that maps better than anyone else's in the YC P26 cohort to this exact problem, the Lakera/Protect AI exits created a *real* enterprise-buyer vacuum for a credible independent (because CISOs hate single-vendor lock-in), and the self-retraining loop could become a genuine data-network-effect moat as attack traces accumulate. That sting is real and I take it seriously — *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) does list security as one of the open sub-markets, not winner-declared. What dismantles it is the dossier itself: the logos are not customers, the revenue is undisclosed, the benchmarks are self-published, and the SDK-into-LangGraph distribution is the thin-wedge anti-pattern, not the workflow-path-everyone-must-touch position. **The verdict flips to Invest if Silmaril discloses $500K+ ARR with two named Fortune 500 paying customers and a third-party benchmark confirming the 95.60% / P90 20ms claims within the next two YC batches.** Until then, pass — clean miracle, wrong index.
