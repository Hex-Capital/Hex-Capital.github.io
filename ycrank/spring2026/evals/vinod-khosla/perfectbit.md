# PerfectBit -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

PerfectBit is what I call a buyer-side picks-and-shovels play — two senior Meta Superintelligence Labs operators (Peter Vajda, ex-Director of Media Generation; Seiji Yamamoto, ex-Core Llama Senior Staff Research Scientist) selling verifier-grounded training data into the 5–10 frontier labs they used to work alongside. The signature is sharp: PerfectBit's highest dimension is consequence-adjacency (it touches the AGI supply chain) and its lowest is founder-defiance-of-expert-consensus (it is the purest possible expression of Meta-pedigreed domain-expert continuity). That pairing tells me what kind of company this is — a high-quality acquihire candidate, not a civilization-redirecting bet.

On consequence, the high rung in my book is **OpenAI** (2019), where the success-state was AGI itself; PerfectBit sits well below that because it is upstream of AGI, not AGI. The shared mechanism worth taking seriously is that verifier-grounded data — formal proofs, simulators, executable tests — is precisely the substrate that 2025 RLVR research (arXiv 2506.15522) identified as the next unlock for verifiable-reward post-training. If PerfectBit nails this for code, math, and physics, frontier labs train better Llamas, Claudes, and GPTs. But PerfectBit's success-state is "Scale AI loses 20% of its $2B ARR to a better methodology" — not "the global liquid-fuels market gets redrawn" the way **KiOR** (~2008) was meant to. The dossier names no incumbent regime that *disappears* when PerfectBit wins; Scale, Surge, and Mercor get displaced from a slice of a $10B annual pool. That is a real business. It is not the $15T labor displacement frame I cited in Fortune (2026-03-06).

**Glean** is the cleanest parallel for PerfectBit's founder profile, and it is the parallel that hurts. Arvind Jain was ex-Google search — paradigmatic domain-expert continuity — and I funded him anyway because the consequence on Fortune-500 information asymmetry was large enough to override the credential-conformity prior. PerfectBit's Vajda and Yamamoto are the same shape: 11 years and 9 years inside Meta's frontier model program, now selling to the buyer they just left. The failure mechanism the LOW rung warns about — founders who "extrapolate the past" in the McKinsey-1986-cellphone sense from my TED Talk — shows up here as the absence of any documented contrarian position. Neither founder has publicly contradicted their field. Neither has the "too important not to do" framing from my Outlook Business essay. The Glean override required consequence magnitude PerfectBit does not have.

The technical-risk dimension compounds the wrong way for PerfectBit over 18 months. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) consumed $115M at Series A because tokamak physics required capital depth no consensus VC would touch; PerfectBit, by contrast, is a two-person team integrating Z3, Isabelle, and physics simulators — work that arXiv 2505.15960 (FOVER, May 2025) already demonstrated is reproducible by academic groups. The compounding problem: as more verifier papers publish in 2026–2027, the in-house build cost for OpenAI or Anthropic to replicate PerfectBit's pipeline drops faster than PerfectBit can accumulate proprietary verified domains. This is the inverse of the **QuantumScape** dynamic, where a decade of solid-state battery IP became un-replicable. PerfectBit's moat is racing against arXiv.

Counterfactually, PerfectBit's learning-rate score flips a tier only if the dossier surfaced a documented pivot — the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C into a YC fintech with otherwise modest consequence. No such pivot exists in PerfectBit's record. Both founders executed long linear careers inside Meta; the company itself is too young for a thesis update. I apply my absent-evidence default rather than penalize, but I cannot reward what isn't there.

On capital fit, PerfectBit is the single-anchor inverse of **OpenAI** (2019). The $50M first VC check I wrote into OpenAI was justified because the roadmap required GPU spend at a scale no consensus VC would underwrite. PerfectBit is two engineers with a software pipeline selling B2B contracts; the company can reach first revenue on a YC check plus a seed round. I am over-resourced for this deal, which means any Tier-1 generalist — or any frontier lab's corp-dev arm — can fund or acquire it. That is the structural tell: when I am over-resourced, the deal usually belongs to someone else.

My 36-month forecast: PerfectBit gets acquihired by Anthropic, OpenAI, or Meta itself for $80–250M before reaching $20M ARR, with the verifier-grounded pipeline absorbed into the buyer's internal post-training data stack. The mechanism is straightforward — customer pool of 5–10 buyers, founders with deep buyer-side relationships from Meta Superintelligence Labs, published methods (FOVER, RLVR) lowering the build-vs-buy threshold, and Meta's $14.3B Scale investment proving labs now prefer to *own* their data pipelines rather than rent them.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that PerfectBit becomes the Mercor of verifier-grounded data — Mercor went from seed to $10B valuation and ~$450–500M ARR in roughly two years (TechCrunch, Oct 2025) by riding the same "labs need better data" wave, and PerfectBit's Core Llama pedigree is arguably stronger founder-market fit than Mercor's expert-sourcing model. Steel-manned, this is a real possibility: if RLVR becomes the dominant post-training paradigm in 2026–2027, the company that owns the verifier library compounds fast. I dismantle it on two facts in the dossier: Mercor sells human experts (a labor arbitrage no lab can fully internalize), whereas PerfectBit sells *software* verifiers that a lab can rebuild from arXiv in a quarter; and the dossier shows zero signed contracts, zero named lab customers, zero pilot logos. The flip condition is concrete: a multi-million-dollar signed contract with a named frontier lab (OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google DeepMind) by YC Demo Day Spring 2026 — that single fact would force me to re-underwrite the moat thesis and reconsider.
