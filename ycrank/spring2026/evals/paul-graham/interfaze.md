# Interfaze -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Yoeven Khemlani already built one AI API company called JigsawStack with $1.5M pre-seed from Antler, and now he and Harsha Khurdula are building a second one called Interfaze that sits in roughly the same conceptual neighborhood -- task-specific developer AI. That is the central fact about Interfaze: the idea is unusually organic to the founders, but the work itself is pure software in a knife-fight market against Reducto, LlamaParse, Unstructured, Mistral OCR, and the frontier labs themselves. The signature is high organic-fit, low schlep -- a hacker building the thing he wishes existed, but in a category where ten other smart teams are building the same thing.

The **Dropbox** (2007) test for organic ideas is whether the founder bumped into the pain in their own prior workflow before anyone asked them to solve it. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT; the Collisons had been hacking commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007) before they shipped **Stripe** (2010). Yoeven Khemlani fits this pattern more cleanly than most pre-seed founders I see -- he spent eight years building edge-ML and OCR/scraping pipelines, then founded JigsawStack as a unified AI API, and Interfaze is the architectural refinement of that same thesis after he watched real developers hit hallucinated keys and broken JSON in production. The divergence from Dropbox is that Houston's pain was a consumer pain that millions shared mutely, whereas Interfaze's pain is a developer pain that Reducto and LlamaParse and Unstructured have already named, measured, and built against. Organic, yes -- but the organic well is crowded.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the reference case for schlep blindness as moat: Brian Armstrong's appetite for bitcoin compliance grind built a wall around the company that pure software competitors could never climb. The failure mode of low-schlep companies is that the work is replicable by any competent team with GPUs and Hugging Face access, so the first competitor with better distribution wins. Interfaze's dossier names no grind -- no regulatory exposure, no manual sales motion, no physical-world ops, no unpleasant onboarding ritual the founders must personally suffer through. Training specialist DNN/CNN models and exposing an OpenAI-compatible Chat Completions endpoint is real engineering, but it is the kind of engineering a Mistral team or a Reducto team can match in a sprint. The IEEE CAI 2026 paper at arxiv.org/abs/2602.04101 is a moat-shaped artifact but not a moat -- papers get read and reimplemented.

On animal resourcefulness, Yoeven's prior travel/property startup that hit US$1M ARR in year one and US$3M in year two before acquisition is a real pre-Interfaze data point -- he has actually shipped revenue at scale before, which most pre-seed founders cannot say. The dossier contains no Interfaze-specific stunt -- no cereal-box equivalent, no demo video that lit up Hacker News, no unscalable manual operation. What it has instead is a co-hosted SF Developer Meetup on May 28, 2026 and a self-serve API. That is competent founder behavior, not animal behavior.

If frontier OCR commoditizes another tier over the next 18 months -- Gemini 2.0 Flash already scored ~80% on Reducto's RD-FormsBench in March 2025 -- the specialist-routing thesis behind Interfaze gets squeezed from above by OpenAI's pricing and from the side by open-source assemblies of Marker, Docling, and olmOCR. The compounding question is whether Interfaze's production traffic generates proprietary routing data fast enough to widen the accuracy gap before the frontier closes it. Nothing in the dossier suggests Interfaze has the developer-volume tailwind that would make that flywheel spin.

For Interfaze's hacker genealogy to flip from MID-tier to HIGH-tier, Harsha's GitHub at @Khurdhula-Harshavardhan would need to show a starred open-source repo with real adoption, or Yoeven would need a Hacker News presence that predates JigsawStack. What the dossier actually shows is an IEEE CAI 2026 paper, a Google Scholar profile, and eight years of ML work -- legitimate technical credentials that read like a strong applied-ML team rather than the Lisp-prize, RSS-spec hacker lineage that flipped **Stripe** (2010) and **Reddit** (2005) for me. Real, but credentialed-real rather than hacker-cultural-real.

The Collison brothers built **Stripe** (2010) together because they had already built **Auctomatic** (2007) together -- the relationship was the asset, not the configuration. Yoeven and Harsha worked together at JigsawStack, Yoeven as CEO and Harsha as Founding Member, which is a real prior working relationship and not a hackathon pairing. The unresolved question the dossier flags is that Yoeven is still publicly identified as JigsawStack's founder on LinkedIn and the Ada Ventures post is still live -- that corporate boundary needs to be clean, and it is not obviously clean.

My 36-month forecast for Interfaze: reaches $1-3M ARR serving developer document-extraction workloads, then either gets acquired by a mid-tier dev-infra company in the Vercel / LlamaIndex tier for under $75M, or gets squeezed by frontier-model OCR commoditization into a slow decline. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- organic founder fit gets Interfaze to product-market traction faster than a generic AI-wrapper team would manage, but the absence of schlep means the ceiling is acquihire territory rather than category-defining. This is a Wufoo-shaped outcome, not a Stripe-shaped one, and I funded Wufoo too -- but at Wufoo prices, not at frontier-AI prices.

The strongest case against my forecast is that JigsawStack's existence proves Yoeven has already learned what does not work in the unified-AI-API space, and Interfaze is the v2 with sharper architectural conviction -- the specialist-routing thesis is exactly the kind of contrarian fact about a category that frontier labs have a structural disincentive to copy because it cannibalizes their token revenue. **Airbnb** (2009) is the precedent for "I rejected the idea silently and was wrong" -- maybe deterministic developer AI is the cereal-box-shaped insight I am undervaluing because the work looks like pure software from outside. The dossier evidence that wins for me is the JigsawStack overlap itself -- the prior company exists, the prior thesis is unfinished, and Yoeven is publicly attached to both at once. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Interfaze ships $50K+ MRR within six months from named developer customers Reducto and LlamaParse are losing, and Yoeven publicly closes or transfers JigsawStack. Until then, neutral.
