# Totalis -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Eric Liu and Pravesh Mansharamani are two Waterloo math classmates pitching a request-for-quote parlay layer on top of Kalshi and Polymarket, and the signature that registers for Totalis is regulatory-wedge-without-lived-founder: the highest-loading dimension on my scorecard is that Totalis sits in a regulated/aesthetically-transgressive lane (operating outside the US, settling in USDC on Solana, $500K disbursed entirely in stablecoins per Tekedia, Apr 2026), and the lowest is that nowhere in the Totalis dossier is there a single named CEO with tiebreaker authority. That pairing -- transgressive substrate, unresolved governance -- is the exact structural setup that produced the HQ Trivia (2017) implosion, except now in a crypto derivatives substrate where the failure modes compound faster.

SpaceX (2008) is my HIGH rung on the capital-intensive/unsexy-regulated axis because Elon was building a rocket nobody would underwrite, and the regulatory and physical moat compounded with every test fire. The shared mechanism with Totalis is using regulatory friction as a wedge -- Kalshi is constrained by CFTC DCM status from offering crypto-settled offshore products (si.com, 2026), and Totalis is exploiting that with a Solana-vault parlay book. But the divergence is severe: SpaceX's wedge was hardware that took a decade to replicate; Totalis's wedge is a software RFQ layer that any offshore venue -- Polymarket, Novig, OG.com -- can ship in a quarter, and Kalshi has already shipped native combos *inside* its own order book (si.com, 2026), eating the wedge from the regulated side. The "first-ever YC stablecoin check" framing in Crowdfund Insider and PANews is doing more aesthetic work than structural work for Totalis.

HQ Trivia's Yusupov/Kroll partnership collapsed because there was no tiebreaker when the disagreement came, and that is the precise pattern Totalis is replicating before it has even hit liquidity scale. The Totalis YC page and the founders' LinkedIns list both Liu and Mansharamani as co-founders with zero CEO designation -- Pravesh's headline reads "founder @ totalis (yc p26)," Eric's still reads "Notion." Two Waterloo math classmates with no public delineation of who calls the shot when a market-maker concentration crisis hits, or when Kalshi rate-limits the API, or when the SEC sends a letter about the offshore Solana vault. The HQ Trivia exception in my book required *explosive tactile product virality* to override this red flag; Totalis has no virality signal in the dossier -- no user counts, no Twitter follower counts, no Discord retrievable -- so the override doesn't trigger.

The Founder-Lived-Experience axis is where Totalis grades closest to my Pass example, and the mechanism is straightforward over 18 months: an RFQ derivatives layer requires the founders to have either traded multi-leg theses themselves or operated inside a market-maker desk, because the product DNA gets calibrated against pricing pain the founders personally felt. Eric Liu at Coinbase (data) and Faire (ML) is the closest thing in the Totalis dossier to relevant lived contact, but data-ML adjacency to crypto exchanges is not the same as having been a Polymarket whale running rollover trades manually, which is the autobiographical origin I'd want to see. Pravesh's "crypto/blockchain hackathon background" is biographically thinner still. The Totalis founders discovered this problem analytically -- Kalshi's combo gap, Polymarket's combo gap -- not by bleeding capital on a four-leg election parlay themselves.

To flip Totalis on the Magically-Weird axis would require two or three sophisticated crypto-native investors to have publicly passed on aesthetic grounds; instead Totalis has the inverse profile -- press in Crypto Briefing, KuCoin, Crowdfund Insider, Tekedia, PANews, WEEX, and RWA Times within a six-week window (Apr-May 2026). That is consensus-crypto-Twitter behavior, the opposite of Crusoe (~2018) where peers literally laughed at the gas-flare-Bitcoin thesis. The "first stablecoin check from YC" framing makes Totalis the most-talked-about plumbing story of the batch, which is itself a deselection signal under my "find them before it becomes consensus" rule (Yahoo Finance, March 2025).

Niantic (~2015) is my single-anchor parallel for Physical-World Verifiability because I sourced it from obsessively playing Ingress on my own phone for three years before writing the check. The Totalis parallel breaks at the verifiability step: Totalis is "currently available outside the US for regulatory reasons" per the YC page, which means I, in San Francisco, cannot actually go to totalis.trade, fund a Solana vault, and place a five-leg parlay across politics and weather to *feel* whether the RFQ pricing is tight or wide. The Totalis product is tactile in principle and unobservable in practice from where I sit -- which is structurally worse than a B2B API I could at least watch a friend deploy.

Totalis at 36 months: dead or pivoted to onchain prediction-market data routing for institutional crypto desks, sub-$2M ARR, never crossing $200M cumulative notional, because Kalshi's native combo product eats US demand and Polymarket either ships its own combo builder or rate-limits the integration. The mechanism is straightforward -- the Totalis wedge is software-thin, the founders cannot defend it with a regulatory or hardware moat, the upstream venues are also the substitutes, and the absence of a designated CEO means the inevitable pivot conversation happens without a tiebreaker.

The bull case that stings: prediction-market volume scaled ~5x from <$5B (Sep 2025) to ~$24B (Apr 2026) per TRM Labs, and Eric Liu's Coinbase data plus Faire ML stack is genuinely fitting for an RFQ pricing engine in a market that is structurally short multi-leg liquidity. Crusoe (~2018) looked like three uninvestable things stapled together too, and Totalis combining crypto + derivatives + offshore-regulated has the same aesthetic signature. The DeepMind exception suggests I sometimes let polymath quant-math founders override the lived-experience screen. The dismantle: Crusoe's Lochmiller was *of* the oilfields; the Totalis founders are not *of* the Polymarket trader cohort -- they are adjacent to it via Coinbase data, which is the analytical-discovery pattern, not the autobiographical one. The condition that flips my verdict: if within 12 months Totalis posts verifiable cumulative parlay notional above $250M with named market-maker counterparties and a designated CEO emerges in the docs, I'll re-underwrite.
