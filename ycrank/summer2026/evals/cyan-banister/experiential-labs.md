# Experiential Labs -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Silen Naihin shipped the AutoGPT agent benchmark to NeurIPS (arXiv:2311.10538) before he ever pitched Experiential Labs, and Kion Fallah ran world-model simulation at Waabi before any of this -- which means the founder layer of Experiential Labs is genuine. Then I read the YC tagline -- "world models for AI agents" -- and the whole thing inverts. Experiential Labs is a credentialed-builder team aimed at the single most consensus AI-infrastructure category of 2026. The dimensional signature is loud: highest on lived founder experience, lowest on magically weird. That pairing tells me what Experiential Labs is -- the right people in the most crowded room.

Palmer Luckey and Anduril is the ladder rung Naihin's biography wants to sit beside, because the mechanism is the same: ship the precursor artifact at scale in an adjacent domain first, then start the company. Naihin's AutoGPT contribution helped drive a 150K+ star repo and produced the "Testing Language Model Agents Safely in the Wild" paper -- he built the eval harness before he sold the eval harness. Fallah is the Waabi staff research scientist who actually knows what 99.7% reconstruction fidelity costs to chase outside of constrained driving scenes. Where Experiential Labs diverges from the Anduril rung is mission texture -- Luckey's defense thesis was a moral and aesthetic stance most VCs refused; Experiential Labs is solving a problem every AI engineer at every YC company already complains about. The lived-experience box is checked. The "no one else sees this" box is not.

The pass example I wrote about the consensus YC AI-agent darling -- two ex-FAANG PMs, six funds chasing, $200B TAM slide -- fails on aesthetic grounds because the round prices in certainty. Trace that pattern into Experiential Labs: World Labs at $1.23B raised, AMI Labs at $1.03B seed at a $3.5B valuation, Decart drawing Bezos and NVIDIA money, Fortune (May 2026) literally headlining "AI field's biggest names are betting billions on world models." Diana Hu's batch will have ten of these. Experiential Labs is not Crusoe burning flares in North Dakota; Experiential Labs is the third world-model deck at the YC demo day mixer. The category is the opposite of "find them before it becomes consensus" -- the consensus has already arrived and brought a billion dollars with it.

Single CEO authority -- mechanism analysis. Kion Fallah is named CEO; Silen Naihin's LinkedIn still reads "Co-founder & CEO @ Endflow | AutoGPT founding team | YC W24." Endflow was Naihin's W24 YC company, and a co-founder running another YC company's CEO seat while founding Experiential Labs is the kind of unresolved authority question that compounds badly over eighteen months. It is not the HQ Trivia Yusupov-Kroll feud yet, but it is the seed of the same disease -- two founders without a documented wind-down of the prior commitment is exactly the conversation my Tim Ferriss answer was warning about. The dossier explicitly flags "no public confirmation found" of Endflow winding down.

Capital intensity -- counterfactual. For Experiential Labs to flip from a 6/15 to a low-teens score on the unsexy/frontier axis, the company would need to commit to actually building physical-world simulation infrastructure -- the GPU-cluster, real-sensor, captured-environment kind that World Labs is doing -- rather than the trace-replay abstraction the YC page implies. The dossier reads software-only: "automatically generate/execute agent evals from telemetry traces." That is a Braintrust extension, not a Waabi extension. The moat-compounding domain is sitting one floor below where Experiential Labs has set up.

Physical-world verifiability -- single-anchor parallel to Niantic. With Niantic in 2012 I could install Ingress and feel the geographic pull on my own legs. With Experiential Labs I can clone world-model-harness (21 stars, last touched Jun 29, 2026) and openclaas (54 stars) on my own laptop, which is more tactile than most B2B AI infrastructure, but I cannot ride it, photograph it in the wild, or hand it to a non-engineer friend. The product is real enough to git-clone and abstract enough that its value lives entirely in founder testimony about what the simulator is doing under the hood.

Thirty-six month forecast: Experiential Labs gets acqui-hired into LangSmith, Braintrust, or one of the world-model heavyweights (World Labs or AMI Labs) for $30-80M, because the eval-from-telemetry wedge does not produce standalone paying-customer escape velocity against incumbents who already own the trace workflow and can ship a "simulation mode" feature within two quarters. Fallah lands as a research lead inside the acquirer; Naihin returns to Endflow or starts a third thing. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- strong founders in a consensus category get bought, not built, because the acquirers have the distribution and the founders have the talent the acquirers can't hire fast enough.

The strongest case against this forecast is the DeepMind exception. Hassabis arrived pedigreed and the polymath cross-domain compounding -- chess prodigy plus neuroscience plus game design -- carried him past my outsider-founder filter. Naihin's AutoGPT-to-Endflow-to-Experiential trajectory plus Fallah's Waabi-to-agents jump is genuine cross-domain compounding -- self-driving simulation translated into agent telemetry is a real intellectual arbitrage, not a memo. But the dismantle is the Endflow concurrency and the absence of any non-consensus angle. DeepMind in 2013 was not the third world-model company at the table; Experiential Labs in Summer 2026 is. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Naihin publicly winds down Endflow within ninety days AND Experiential Labs lands a named, paying enterprise customer at $100K+ ACV that is not also a YC company -- because that combination would prove single-CEO authority and would prove the wedge can survive contact with the buyers who can also write checks to LangSmith.

I'm a pass with affection. The founders are real. The room is too full.
