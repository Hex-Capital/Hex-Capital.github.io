# Drafted -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Nicholas Donahue is on his second pass at residential home design after Atmos shut down in March 2025, and Drafted is the Quora-shaped exception pattern without the Adam D'Angelo override -- the highest dimension is founder lineage (Patrick Collison and Ben Silbermann personally re-upped on a YC S20 alum who shipped $200M of homes), the lowest is monopoly trajectory (the site currently advertises *free* PDF and CAD downloads while Maket.ai has been generating AI floor plans since 2019). That pairing is the analytical signature: a credentialed operator pivoting from a services failure into a category where the moat hypothesis is weaker than the cap table. Drafted is the kind of deal that gets done because Patrick wrote a check, not because the secret survives the Zero to One question.

**Stripe** (2010) is the high rung on the contrarian secret ladder because Patrick Collison named the specific architectural failure of legacy payment processors -- developer experience as the unbuilt API surface -- and Drafted does not earn that rung. Drafted's nearest analog to a secret is the claim that custom-home design must be pure model inference rather than the glamorized architecture firm Atmos became, which is a *post-mortem* on Donahue's last company, not a hidden truth about the market. The TechCrunch line about "two-tenths of a penny per floor plan, compared to 13 cents for general-purpose AI" is real engineering, but a cost delta on inference is not a contrarian thesis -- it is a fine-tune away from being replicated by Maket.ai or by any open-weights team that scrapes the same permitted-plan corpus. The named incumbent failure mode that Drafted needs -- why Autodesk structurally cannot ship a consumer floor-plan generator -- is unstated in the dossier, and absent that, Drafted is closer to the Pass Example archetype than to Stripe.

The low rung on monopoly trajectory is the canonical "we have a better model" moat that any competitor replicates in nine months, and Drafted's evidence puts it squarely on that rung. Drafted shipped to 120k monthly users and 325k generated plans, then dropped its price from "$1,000 and $2,000" per complete plan (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025) to "Free AI House Plans with PDF & CAD Downloads" on drafted.ai by May 2026 -- that is the trajectory of a commoditizing market discovering it cannot charge, not a pricing-power hypothesis. Maket.ai has been in market since 2019 with the same product description, Snaptrude has raised $21.8M for the professional BIM cut, and there is no government customer, no certification depth, no integration moat into permitting authorities named anywhere in the dossier. Atoms here are floor plans rendered as bits; the moat is a dataset that anyone with $50M and a Zillow scraping budget can reproduce.

**Definite plan vs. indefinite iteration** -- the mechanism analysis matters here because Drafted's 18-month compounding story requires a roadmap commitment the dossier does not surface. Drafted grew from 6 employees in December 2025 to 9 by Spring 2026 and went from paid SKU to free downloads in the same window, which is iteration-mode behavior, not the SpaceX-style multi-year technical milestone Zero to One was written to reward. Donahue's prior Atmos arc -- raised $20M, designed $200M of homes, then closed when rates moved -- is itself revealed evidence that this founder ships responsively to market signals rather than committing to a non-pivotable architectural thesis. No public artifact names what Drafted will refuse to pivot from in 2028.

**Founder lineage** -- the counterfactual: what would have to be true for this score to flip from mid to top quartile? Donahue would need to be a Levchin or a Moskovitz, not a Forbes 30 Under 30 with a closed prior company. The dossier does name real signal: Patrick Collison wrote a personal check, Ben Silbermann wrote a personal check, Jack Altman and Alex Blania participated -- that investor stack is informationally heavier than the typical Spring 2026 pre-seed and is the single strongest fact in the dossier. Drafted's lineage is closer to Asana on the rung-to-rung comparison because Donahue did ship a venture-scale company before it died, and four Atmos employees followed him into Drafted, which is the proxy signal for "people who worked with him will work with him again."

**Atoms-heavy moat** -- the single-anchor parallel is **Asana** (2010), which Thiel's own exception list flagged as pure SaaS with no regulated-customer moat, where the override was Moskovitz's Facebook lineage. Drafted is pure software for consumer house plans with output that "must be sent to a structural engineer" (drafted.ai) -- meaning Drafted is explicitly *not* crossing the regulated boundary into permit-stamped output, which is the only place the atoms-heavy moat would have lived. There is no FAA-equivalent certification, no GovCloud equivalent, no Anduril-style integration depth with a regulator. The override Asana got was Facebook-CTO-level pedigree; Donahue's pedigree is real but thinner.

The 36-month forecast: Drafted reaches $3-8M ARR on a freemium-to-paid-export funnel, never escapes the Maket.ai shadow, and gets acqui-hired by Autodesk, Zillow, or a national homebuilder in the $40-90M range -- returning the $1.65M seed at a respectable multiple for Buckley but not a fund-returner for anyone holding power-law exposure. The mechanism producing this outcome is exactly the high/low pairing: the Collison-grade lineage gets Drafted to a real distribution moment, but the absent monopoly trajectory caps the terminal value at strategic-acquisition prices rather than category-defining ones.

The strongest bull case is the Quora precedent and the Facebook-at-sub-1M-users analogy: 120k MAU acquired through "socials and word-of-mouth" with zero paid acquisition in a category nobody has cracked at consumer scale is a real network signal, and a compounding corpus of user-generated plans plus permitted-plan training data could become the structural advantage no general model can replicate -- which is the **Facebook** (2004) shape of zero-to-one. Patrick Collison does not write personal checks indiscriminately. The bear case dismantles this: the Maket.ai-since-2019 fact means the dataset advantage is contested, the macro-rate exposure that killed Atmos is unchanged for Drafted's end customers, and the move to free pricing is revealed evidence that willingness-to-pay has not been found. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is Drafted demonstrating $5M+ ARR within 18 months at a defended per-plan price above $500 with permit-stamped CAD output -- a pricing-power signal, not a usage signal. Until then, Drafted is a pass.
