# Shepherd -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Three high school seniors -- Philip Meng (Stanford-bound CS+EE), Ishan Ramrakhiani (Canterbury '26), and Elijah Renner (Thetford '26) -- are building Shepherd as "company memory, one text away," and the dimensional signature is this: cleanest possible org chart (named CEO, CPO, CTO at incorporation) wrapped around the softest possible wedge (a pure-SaaS ingestion layer in a category where Mem0 already raised $24M, Letta closed at $70M post, and Interloom took $16.5M in the eight months before Shepherd's batch even started). That pairing -- titles solved, lane crowded, founders never having held a job inside the kind of company whose "memory" they propose to ingest -- is what I'm actually scoring.

Anduril and Palmer Luckey sit on the high rung of the lived-experience ladder because Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he ever touched a defense contract; the biography put him in front of the problem. Shepherd's founders shipped real artifacts -- Renner's Pathformer with Stanford CAIMI, Ramrakhiani's Auteur Adobe extension, Meng's Davidson Fellow work -- but none of those artifacts sit inside the enterprise-SaaS sprawl Shepherd promises to unify. The lived problem here is "company knowledge is fragmented," and Shepherd's three founders have, between them, zero company-employee months. They read about this problem in the same Diana Hu memo that produced Hyper. That is the analytical-not-autobiographical pattern I pass on.

Crusoe is the inverse of Shepherd on the magically-weird axis -- gas flares plus Bitcoin plus North Dakota was so aesthetically uninvestable that credible VCs passed on principle, which is exactly what made the round underpriced. Shepherd's dossier reads in the opposite direction: Mem0 ($24M Series A from YC, Peak XV, Basis Set, October 2025), Letta ($10M led by Felicis), Interloom ($16.5M, Fortune March 2026), Hyper inside the very same YC batch, plus Glean and Notion AI as incumbent substitution risk. This is the most-funded micro-category of the 2026 cycle, and Shepherd is the pre-revenue high-school entrant. "Find them before it becomes consensus" -- consensus already arrived, with term sheets.

Single CEO authority is where Shepherd scores highest, proportionally, and even that is a courtesy. Meng is titled CEO, Ramrakhiani CPO, Renner CTO -- structurally cleaner than the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll setup that haunts me. But three 17-and-18-year-olds who met on the pre-college AI-competition circuit have never had the disagreement that tests tiebreaker authority. The titles are a Form D, not a governance protocol. Eighteen months in, when Meng wants to stay in San Francisco and Renner wants to return to Dartmouth-adjacent research, no operating agreement in the dossier tells me who decides.

SpaceX in 2008 anchored my frontier-hardware tolerance, and Shepherd sits at the exact opposite pole: a pure-software ingestion-plus-retrieval stack with an SMS skin, no hardware, no regulatory moat, no rural-industrial social license. The defensibility section of the dossier reads "No defensibility signals found in public sources," and the future-moat note is an [Inferred] data-network-effects hypothesis that every competitor in the set could write verbatim. This is the lane I explicitly deprioritize because the moat would have to be the AI itself, and a polymath generalist could rebuild it in a quarter -- which, ironically, is the rebuttal Renner himself could write.

The SMS interface is the one place Shepherd flirts with tactile verifiability -- I could text it and feel the loop, the way I felt HQ Trivia's pull before any metric existed. But the dossier says the website returned 403/ECONNREFUSED at research time, no phone number is published, no design partner is named, and no Product Hunt or Launch HN exists. There is no artifact I can touch in the next 48 hours. The "one text away" tagline is a promise, not a product, and A2P 10DLC carrier policy means the SMS surface is not even fully under Shepherd's control.

My 36-month forecast: Shepherd does not reach $1M ARR, the founders return to Stanford and their respective freshman years by late 2027, and the shell is acqui-hired for under $8M by Mem0, Glean, or a Notion-tier incumbent who wants the three names on a press release more than the codebase. The mechanism is consensus-category compression -- when Mem0, Letta, Interloom, and Hyper are all selling adjacent memory layers to the same buyer, the smallest pre-seed entrant with no enterprise-sales DNA gets squeezed out before Series A pricing recovers.

The strongest case against my pass is the DeepMind exception. Hassabis, Suleyman, and Legg had elite credentials I would normally distrust, but polymath cross-domain compounding overrode the rule -- chess prodigy plus neuroscientist plus game designer was a profile no one else could assemble. Renner doing transformer-based pathology at Stanford CAIMI, Ramrakhiani shipping a drug-interaction transformer and an Adobe extension, Meng a Davidson Fellow in CS+EE -- that is three polymaths converging young, and the bull case is that I am pattern-matching on age and missing a Hassabis-shaped trio. The reason it does not sting enough: Hassabis's polymath compounding pointed at a non-consensus problem (general intelligence in 2010), where Shepherd's points at the single most-funded sub-category of the 2026 batch. The exception requires polymath AND magically-weird; Shepherd has the first half and the inverse of the second. The condition that would flip me: a named Fortune 500 design partner signing an LOI in the next two quarters, or a public benchmark where Shepherd materially beats Zep's 63.8% on LongMemEval. Absent either, I pass and stay in touch with Renner for whatever he builds in clinical AI after this.
