# machine0 -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Barnaby Malet is a solo founder shipping a four-region 99.99% SLA persistent-VM fleet from a CLI, and that one sentence contains both what makes machine0 interesting and what makes me uneasy. The schlep is real -- KVM/QEMU, GPU supply across US/UK/EU/Asia, suspend-resume snapshotting, per-minute billing down to $0.013/hr -- and the founder has the credentials to run it (MEng AI + PhD Cloud Computing from Imperial, ex-Apple, prior YC W20 with Upflow's $22.9M raised). But every time I read the dossier I land on the same word: alone. Team size 1, no co-founder pipeline named, against Fly.io Sprites, Modal at $4.65B, E2B with Fortune 100 logos.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the right HIGH-rung comparison on the schlep axis, and it's where machine0 partially earns its keep. Armstrong won me over because he was running toward bitcoin compliance -- the unpleasant, regulatory, operationally grinding work other YC applicants would not touch. machine0 is the infrastructure analog: persistent VMs with static IPs, GPU procurement, four-region uptime, on-call rotations. That is a schlep zone -- the AgentMarketCap survey lists 19+ entrants but most are ephemeral sandboxes precisely because persistence is operationally harder. The divergence from the Coinbase pattern is that Armstrong was schlepping toward a problem hyperscalers refused to touch; Malet is schlepping toward a problem Fly.io shipped a competitor for (Sprites, January 2026) and AWS AgentCore and Google Agent Sandbox have already bundled into platforms developers pay for. Same posture, worse defensible perimeter.

The two-MBA AI-wrapper team I described in my own pass example fails because they had no shipped artifact and no founder had personally suffered the problem. machine0 inverts the first half cleanly -- the CLI is live, pricing is live, the Show HN went up -- but the dossier is silent on whether Malet himself ran 8-hour Claude Code sessions and hit the ephemeral-sandbox wall. The Upflow product was B2B cash collection; the pain that produced machine0 is inferred, not narrated. This is the gap between Drew Houston's USB stick at **Dropbox** (2007) (a sentence anyone can repeat) and a founder whose origin story has to be reconstructed by his investors. Organic-idea fit is plausible but not declared, and I have learned to weight what founders say about their own pain over what their CV suggests they might have felt.

Animal resourcefulness compounds or fails over the next 18 months on one question: does Malet, alone, ship faster than Fly.io's Sprites team iterates? The dossier shows real output -- four regions, NixOS + Ubuntu, H100/H200/L40S/MI300X GPU SKUs, a remote MCP server, the full price ladder from small to 6xl. That is a lot of surface area for one person. Solo founders who ship this much in pre-seed either become the **Stripe** (2010) animal class or they hit the wall when on-call collides with feature velocity. I don't see the cereal-box moment in machine0's dossier -- no post-rejection stunt, no unscalable revenue improvisation, no Show HN comment count I can verify -- so I'm pricing the artifact itself as the animal evidence and discounting because the artifact is the only evidence.

For the cofounder dimension to flip a tier, machine0 would need a named co-founder by demo day with complementary ops/SRE coverage -- the kind of person who runs the 99.99% pager while Malet writes the CLI. "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) is explicit that solo is the configuration most correlated with failure in the YC dataset, and the rationalization "Malet did Upflow with co-founders so he knows how" cuts the other way: he knows how and chose solo anyway. That's a signal, not a neutral data point. Default penalty stands.

On hacker genealogy, the single-anchor parallel is **Reddit** (2005)'s Aaron Swartz -- a technical founder with real lineage but where the dossier shows the lineage rather than declaring it. Malet has @barnaby on GitHub, an Imperial PhD in cloud computing, and prior shipping at Apple-scale, but the dossier returns "stars not retrievable" and names no open-source artifact, no Lisp/Haskell/Rust footprint, no conference talks. Credential-strong, hacker-tribal-weak in the public record. I lean down from the MID rung, not up.

machine0 in 36 months: acquired by Fly.io, Vercel, or Cloudflare in the $5-25M range for the brand and codebase, or quietly dead, with a 30% tail probability of a thin profitable lifestyle business at $1-3M ARR serving agent-builder hobbyists Sprites doesn't bother with. The mechanism is the signature: a solo founder doing high-quality schlep work in a category where three hyperscalers and four funded startups are running the same play with 10-100x the headcount. Schlep without scale is a feature shop, not a company.

The bull case that should sting: Malet is a 2x YC founder, the only one in this category with a PhD in cloud computing, and Upflow's $15M Series A proves he can recruit and operate. Persistent VM state -- long-lived filesystems, static IPs, snapshots -- creates real switching costs once an agent is deployed; the first mover with a clean CLI may compound before Sprites' developer mindshare catches up. **Airbnb** (2009) is the Known Exception that applies: an idea I'd mentally rejected, a configuration I'd normally pass on, overridden by founder evidence. The dismantling: Airbnb had three founders who'd sold 1,000 cereal boxes at $40 each. machine0 has one founder and an SLA claim I don't believe a solo can hold across four regions. The verdict flips if Malet names a technical co-founder with SRE credentials before demo day and shows me one named customer running 24/7 agent workloads -- absent that, the asymmetry against Sprites is the story.
