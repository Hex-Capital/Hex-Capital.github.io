# Datost -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Maceo Cardinale Kwik watched analyst scarcity bottleneck decisions at Traba and built Datost to unblock himself, which is the right origin shape — but Datost lives in the cleanest, most-software, least-schlep corner of enterprise AI, and that pairing tells me what Datost is: a competent organic-pain company in a zone where 47 funded competitors and Snowflake Cortex are already running the same play. The 75.2% BIRD-Interact number against Claude Opus 4.6's 33% is the most interesting fact in the dossier, but a benchmark is a paper artifact, not a cereal box.

**Dropbox** (2007) is the high rung on organic founder-pain — Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and built the thing he needed. Datost's claim to that ladder is Maceo at Traba seeing "lots of data but not enough analysts," and that is genuine adjacency, but it is not Houston-grade because Maceo was the engineer next to the bottleneck, not the business operator trying to get a Monday answer out of Tuesday's data. The 18,402 queries answered in a measured week tells me someone is using Datost, which is more than most pre-seed YC companies can show, and that nudges Datost up the ladder past the "market opportunity" pretenders in my Pass Example. But Datost is not Houston — Datost is "I saw my coworkers suffer," which is real, not transformative.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the low-rung lesson here, but inverted: Brian Armstrong's edge was that bitcoin compliance was an unpleasant grind no other YC applicant in his batch wanted to touch, and that schlep became the moat. Datost's product is a Slack bot that writes SQL against a sandbox — there is no regulatory exposure, no offline operation, no manual onboarding the founders have to personally suffer. The dossier names Hex, Snowflake Cortex, Databricks Genie, and Slack-native AI as substitution risks; that list exists precisely because Datost is operating in the most pleasant, most-replicable category in enterprise data. Datost has not named a schlep, which usually means Datost has not hit one yet, which usually means Datost will flinch when it arrives.

On animal resourcefulness, the test compounds over 18 months by whether the founders pulled off something unscalable before the institutional check — Airbnb's cereal boxes, Armstrong's Android wallet to overturn his Coinbase rejection. Datost has shipped a working benchmark and a Slack app, which is non-trivial, but the dossier contains zero pre-funding stunt: no improvised revenue, no rejection-recovery, no manual onboarding of a marquee customer. Maceo's 4.0 from Virginia Tech and Top 0.1% IMC Prosperity finish are credentials of a smart, conscientious person; they are not animal tracks. I default to 12/35 when the dossier is silent on what founders did before the check, and the benchmark work pulls Datost only modestly above that.

The hacker-genealogy score would flip a tier if Jason Hy Wang turned out to have a real GitHub footprint or open-source presence — but the dossier says no public repos, no Twitter, LinkedIn returns 999 status. Maceo's GitHub shows 9 followers and a top-owned repo with 1 star, plus contributions to OpenDSA. Patrick Collison had won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp work before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz had co-authored the RSS spec before **Reddit** (2005). Datost's hacker-genealogy evidence is "CS summa cum laude," which is the credential version, not the tribal version. Hackers leave trails. Datost's trail is thin.

On co-founder configuration, the single parallel is **Twitch** (2007)'s Kan/Shear — a pair with deep prior working history that survived Kiko. Datost is a two-person team with no documented shared employer, university, or prior project; Jason is essentially absent from the public record beyond a LinkedIn role title. I stay near the default 5/10 because pre-seed YC interviews usually surface chemistry, but the dossier gives me nothing to lean on.

My 36-month forecast for Datost: tuck-in acquisition by Snowflake, Databricks, or a BI incumbent at $15-50M in 2027-2028, after the benchmark-to-production gap narrows and Hex or Slack-native AI ships an equivalent @mention surface. The mechanism is straightforward — Datost's only published differentiator is benchmark accuracy attributed to a semantic layer, and a semantic layer is exactly what Snowflake Cortex and Databricks Genie are racing to own; Datost is on the wrong side of a vendor consolidation, not the right side of a schlep.

The strongest bull case is that the per-customer semantic graph becomes a real switching cost in the **Coinbase** (2012) pattern — Armstrong's compliance grind looked like commodity overhead until it became the moat that kept Square and PayPal out. If Datost's semantic-layer ingestion of each customer's CRM, docs, codebase, and internal vocabulary is genuinely a 6-12 month grind per logo, then 47 competitors collapses to maybe 3 winners and Datost could be one. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull case is that Datost itself does not frame the semantic-layer ingestion as a schlep — there is no mention of unpleasant per-customer onboarding, no manual fieldwork narrative, only a benchmark number. Armstrong talked about compliance constantly; Datost talks about accuracy. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a Series A announcement within 12 months with named Fortune-500 customer references showing 90-day-plus retention and expansion seats — that would tell me the semantic layer is actually a schlep-moat and I read Datost wrong.
