# Blueprints -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Blueprints is a generic-AI-agent-shell wearing a crypto-adjacent costume, run by two ex-interns whose CTO's personal site reads "building for the web since 2023" -- and that signature, high-ish category sleeve paired with rock-bottom founder-as-brand, is exactly the kind of YC S26 pitch the pass example was written about. Ryan Morrissey and Bence Redmond left the University of Limerick ISE programme to build an "agentic harness for financial markets" priced at $20/mo, competing against Polystrat (Olas, $13.8M financing + $4M seed) and Simmer, whose Finance Magnates write-up uses the same "agent harness" framing word-for-word. The asset on offer is a SaaS skin on Polymarket's own open-source agents repo, and I cannot make this round work at any plausible YC S26 entry price.

Take the Solana entry at $0.05/coin against Blueprints on the round dimension: Solana cleared the value-seed test because the consensus crowd had walked off the field post-ICO crash, leaving the price genuinely orphaned for a protocol with real on-chain settlement. Blueprints inverts that mechanism -- Polymarket monthly volume crossed $10B in March 2026, combined Polymarket+Kalshi hit $24B by April, Pew Research ran a piece, TRM Labs ran a piece, and every funded competitor named in the Finance Magnates "bot playground" sweep is racing in. This is the moment the category is being aggressively bid, not abandoned, and the YC S26 batch will spit out a dozen variations on "AI agent for prediction markets" -- Blueprints is the consensus take, not the orphan.

John Fish at Bookshelved is the relevant ladder rung for founder-as-brand precisely because the check only made sense once a creator-built literary hub had owned distribution from day zero -- that's the failure mechanism to test Blueprints against. The Blueprints dossier returns "Twitter not found" for Morrissey, "Twitter not found" for Redmond, no GitHub handle for either, no Discord, no Product Hunt launch, no newsletter on prediction-market microstructure -- the entire founder-distribution column is hollow. Irish trade press in Silicon Republic and ThinkBusiness is downstream YC-coverage signal, not owned distribution. There is nothing on this cap table that would survive Polymarket launching a first-party hosted no-code builder next quarter.

Capital structure here compounds badly across 18 months. Blueprints is selling $20/mo SaaS with a 2,000-credit free tier into a customer base whose retention requires beating latency-advantaged arbitrage bots that already extracted $40M from Polymarket in twelve months on execution speed, not strategy quality. Every Blueprints customer who underperforms a Polystrat self-custody agent churns, every churned cohort forces more paid acquisition, and the "co-located execution infra" claim is a feature an Olas-funded competitor with $13.8M can replicate inside a quarter. This is the SaaS cap table that needs five rounds to reach default-alive, not a venture moment.

Counterfactually on founder conviction, the Blueprints score flips a tier only if Morrissey turns out to have a multi-year prediction-market trading footprint -- a Polymarket wallet history, a public thread on why the bot-versus-bot equilibrium breaks for retail tooling, a single piece of long-form writing on market microstructure. The dossier explicitly says "no documented prior trading, hedge-fund, or market-making credential" for either founder. Stripe-intern plus AWS-intern plus University of Limerick is a competent technical pairing, but it's exactly the sanitized opportunity-response profile, not the missionary-temperament profile that Drop.io demanded of me when I was bootstrapping into a consumer file-sharing primitive against free incumbents.

Solana is the single-anchor parallel for the category sleeve, and the comparison is unflattering. Solana was the protocol layer where the on-chain economic activity actually settles; Blueprints is the AI tooling layer wrapping Polymarket, which is itself the protocol layer. The crypto sleeve in the Slow portfolio -- Algorand, Aleo, Lightning Labs, Fold -- bought protocols, not the AI wrappers on top of them. Blueprints inherits the Polymarket platform-dependency risk without owning any of the on-chain economic activity, which is the exact inversion of why I was willing to write into Aleo at seed.

My 36-month forecast on Blueprints: sub-$500K ARR by mid-2029, retention crushed by Polystrat-class self-custody competitors and by Polymarket's own builders.polymarket.com program shipping first-party no-code tooling, and an acqui-hire outcome into Polymarket or Kalshi rather than an independent Series A -- the founders are good engineers and someone will want them, but Blueprints the company does not survive the regatta. This forecast is consistent with the signature: a crypto-sleeve-adjacent surface masking a generic AI-agent-shell underneath, with no founder distribution to compound against funded competition.

The strongest bear-case I can mount against my own pass is that this looks superficially like Sublime Security -- buy the niche enterprise category before the consolidation wave -- and the prediction-market automation layer might consolidate the way email security did, and the Slow crypto sleeve (Algorand, Aleo, Lightning) was bought precisely when every generalist VC wrote crypto off as un-investable in 2018. The honest counter is that Sublime had founders with deep Proofpoint domain pedigree and Blueprints has two ISE-programme dropouts, that Algorand had Silvio Micali and Aleo had a zero-knowledge cryptography moat, and that Blueprints has Polymarket's own GitHub repo competing against it for free. The single verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Morrissey publishes a long-form public artifact -- not a YC launch tweet -- demonstrating a non-obvious belief about why retail bot tooling beats self-custody agents over a 36-month arc, and Blueprints either prices the round below the S26 batch median or restructures the instrument as a revenue share against trading volume rather than a standard SAFE. Absent both, I'm out before the meeting starts.
