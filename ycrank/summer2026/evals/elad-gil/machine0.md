# machine0 -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Barnaby Malet's machine0 is a clean single-miracle infrastructure bet — KVM/QEMU plus a CLI, no new physics — that arrives in a category where Fly.io shipped Sprites in January 2026, Daytona pivoted in early 2025, and AWS AgentCore plus Cloudflare Sandbox SDK already occupy the workflow path. The signature pairing here is *technical cleanliness without revenue legibility*: machine0's strongest axis is that the dossier requires only one thing to be true (persistent agent sessions become the default), and its weakest is that the dossier discloses zero revenue, zero named customers, and a Show HN post (Hacker News item 48543245) whose engagement isn't even retrievable. That's a diagnostic profile, not an indictment — but it's the profile of a company I write a small check on, not lead.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for machine0 on the catalyst axis, and it's worth being precise about why. Anduril rode Ukraine revealing Pentagon software inadequacy inside a 24-month window — a specific, dated, externally-legible re-opening. machine0 rides a real catalyst too: Claude Code and Codex running 4–8 hour sessions, Hermes/OpenClaw running 24/7, all crossing the practical threshold of ephemeral sandboxes in late 2025–2026 (machine0.io; Blaxel blog, 2026). Where machine0 diverges from the Anduril template is that the catalyst is *legible to everyone* — Fly.io shipped Sprites in Jan 2026 specifically for this workload, Daytona repositioned to sub-90ms agent sandboxes, and the AgentMarketCap (Apr 2026) census lists 19+ entrants. A real catalyst that the hyperscalers also saw is not the same trade as defense post-Ukraine in 2022, when peers were still calling it a graveyard. machine0 is on the right curve; it's just not skating to a puck no one else is chasing.

**Harvey** (2023) is the velocity rung — $3B → $5B → $8B in months, "just working" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). The failure mode at the low rung of this ladder is the AI company that quietly omits its growth curve, which is exactly what the machine0 dossier does: "No public revenue, paying customer count, Product Hunt launch, or press coverage found." Per-minute pricing from $0.013/hr to $3.714/hr is disclosed; what those rates *produce* is not. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose, and machine0 launching via Show HN with no retrievable engagement data while E2B claims 88% of Fortune 100 signups (E2B blog, Jul 2025) tells me machine0 is pre-curve, not on-curve.

Barnaby Malet's shipping history is the most interesting line in the dossier, and it compounds over 18 months in a specific way: Upflow (YC W20) raised $22.9M total with a $15M Series A in Jun 2021 and landed Lattice, Front, and Adikteev as customers (Upflow blog; Crunchbase), so machine0 isn't a first-rodeo founder hoping to ship — it's a second-time YC founder who already took a B2B SaaS through Series A. Add a PhD in Cloud Computing from Imperial College London and machine0 has founder-domain alignment that most pre-seed cloud infrastructure plays don't. What's missing is the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind Srinivas pattern — the "show up a week later with the thing we discussed built" rhythm — because Malet is solo and the dossier shows a single launch artifact, not a string of weekly shipped iterations. Founder quality is real here; the in-meeting shipping cadence is not yet demonstrable.

For machine0 to clear the index-position bar, the counterfactual that would flip this dimension a tier is a distribution wedge — an Anthropic or OpenAI partnership where Claude Code provisions machine0 VMs by default, or a NixOS-specific developer community where machine0 is the canonical persistent target. Neither shows up in the dossier. Instead, machine0 sits in a workflow path that Fly.io Sprites (1–2s creation, 300ms checkpoint, 100GB NVMe), Vercel Sandbox, Cloudflare Sandbox SDK, AWS AgentCore, and Google Agent Sandbox all also occupy. **Stripe** (2010) was an index on e-commerce because it sat in the only path; machine0 sits in one of nineteen paths. That's a share fight, not an index position.

The single-miracle test is where machine0 looks cleanest, and the parallel is **Decagon** (2023): one validated capability — LLMs accurate enough for customer support — and everything else is GTM and product execution. machine0 needs one thing to be true: persistent agent workloads become a standing line item in developer infrastructure budgets. The underlying stack is KVM/QEMU plus snapshots plus a CLI — no new chemistry, no regulatory unlock, no consumer behavior shift required. The 99.99% four-region SLA is an operational claim, not a science claim, and operational claims can be repaired with hires.

My 36-month forecast on machine0: acqui-hired by Fly.io, Vercel, or Cloudflare for $15–40M before reaching $2M ARR, or quietly wound down, because a solo founder defending a CLI-over-KVM thesis against Sprites (Jan 2026), AWS AgentCore, and a $466M Modal Labs cannot win the share fight that the absence of an index position forces. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: clean single miracle plus no revenue legibility in a category with declared incumbents resolves to a small outcome, not a zero and not a unicorn.

The bull case on machine0 stings, and I want to name it cleanly: Malet is a 2x YC founder with a PhD in Cloud Computing and an MEng in AI from Imperial, he took Upflow from zero to a $15M Series A with named enterprise logos (Lattice, Front), and persistent agent infrastructure is a *real* catalyst that Fly.io's January 2026 Sprites launch only validates. If Anthropic or OpenAI signs machine0 as the Claude Code managed-VM backend, this is a $100–300M outcome inside 24 months — the **Mistral** (2023) hyperscaler-coupling-as-feature pattern. The bear case wins on dossier evidence: machine0 is one founder, the 99.99% SLA across US/UK/EU/Asia is operationally implausible solo (no on-call coverage), and unit pricing of $0.013/hr at the low end is a race-to-commodity against providers with KVM fleets already amortized. The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict: machine0 discloses a named enterprise contract with Anthropic, OpenAI, or a top-three agent framework, or crosses $500K ARR with >20% MoM growth inside six months. Until then, machine0 is a Neutral — I'd take a small allocation if offered into the round at YC standard terms, but I'm not leading and I'm not following on at the next mark without the revenue curve.
