# Madrone -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Madrone is a frontier-hardware swing in industrial cooling whose physical proof-points don't yet exist outside the founders' claims -- the right unsexy lane, with Erik Meike's compressor-less A/C patent-pending sitting in the dossier as the lived-experience anchor, and zero pilot deployment sitting next to it as the absence. Two-person team out of Mountain View, NVIDIA Inception sticker, no installed unit anywhere you or I can walk up to. That pairing -- frontier category, pre-artifact -- is the analytical signature.

Anduril-Luckey (2017) is the load-bearing analogy for Madrone on the founder-lived-experience axis: Palmer shipped Oculus to scale in adjacent VR hardware before pivoting to defense, and that prior-product trajectory was the entire reason I wrote the check before consensus formed. Madrone's Erik Meike isn't Luckey, but he is the closest thing a pre-seed cooling company can put on the page -- he designed a compressor-eliminating residential A/C system with patent pending out of Harvey Mudd, then went to Apple Hardware, then was Founding Engineer at Watney Robotics doing autonomous datacenter infrastructure. That is a founder whose hands have been *inside* the thermodynamic problem twice before, not someone who read a Dell'Oro report and incorporated. Where Madrone diverges from the Anduril rung is that the prior-product trajectory is patent-pending residential A/C, not a shipped consumer hardware franchise -- it is the right pattern at one-tenth the proof.

The HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll collapse is the failure mechanism I watch for on physical-world verifiability -- the inverse here, because HQ Trivia I could literally play on my phone and feel the viral pull before any metric existed, which is why I overrode the co-founder governance heuristic. Madrone has none of that. The dossier describes "modular dew-point cooling units that sit outside the data center" delivering "20?C supply water at 45?C+ ambient" -- a claim, not an artifact. There is no pilot customer named, no Texas hyperscaler signed, no field-validated thermal curve I can photograph at a Four Seasons or anywhere else. The Flock Safety Wi-Fi-scan move is structurally impossible for Madrone in 2026; the product exists in a CAD file and on a marketing site, and the physical thing a normal human can touch is zero.

The magically-weird filter is where Madrone gets quietly punished, and it hurts. Crusoe (~2018) was three uninvestable things stapled together -- crypto, energy infrastructure, rural roughnecks -- and credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds, which was the tell. Madrone in Spring 2026 is the opposite weather pattern: "data center cooling" is now a consensus AI-infrastructure category, Vertiv is a $9B-revenue public stock the market is bidding up, Corintis just closed a $24M Series A on microfluidic chip cooling in September 2025, and Dew Point Systems out of the University of Hull is running the same thermodynamic principle in the UK. Madrone is non-consensus on the *technical approach* (chiller-less, dew-point) inside a consensus *category* (AI cooling), which is the most dangerous quadrant -- priced like the category, judged like the approach.

Capital-intensive frontier-hardware is where Madrone scores cleanly. SpaceX (2008) was the template -- the first angel check I ever wrote, into a category most peers refused on capital-intensity grounds. Madrone is industrial cooling hardware sold into hyperscale procurement, which means multi-year qualification cycles, capex-heavy field trials, and channel conflict against Munters/Vertiv/Nortek whose largest SKUs would be cannibalized by a chiller-less product. That is exactly the lane that produced Crusoe and Anduril for me, and it is the lane Long Journey is structurally built to underwrite. The 2-person team and absent funding round disclosed in the dossier is the stage-appropriate version of the SpaceX-era objection, not a disqualifier.

Single-CEO authority on Madrone reads adequate, not exceptional -- Akshay Trikha is named CEO/Co-founder, Erik Meike is Co-founder without the title, and there are two of them, which is the cleanest possible configuration short of a solo founder. Counterfactual: if the dossier showed a documented founder-vesting protocol and explicit operating authority, I would move this to high-rung; absent that, I am scoring above the absent-evidence default but well below the Anduril ceiling because two co-founders without a public tiebreaker clause is the GameCrush (~2011) risk surface I have been burned by.

My 36-month forecast on Madrone: either one to two Texas hyperscale pilots translating to $2-5M of pilot revenue with a Munters or Vertiv acquisition conversation by 2029, or the company is dead because dew-point output is bounded by ambient wet-bulb temperature and Texas summer humidity collapses the 20?C-at-45?C claim to something the hyperscaler thermal team rejects. The signature -- frontier-category, pre-artifact -- forces a binary: either Madrone gets the artifact into a real well-instrumented pilot fast and the physics holds, or it doesn't and the incumbents absorb the team.

The bear case that stings: Madrone is the consensus YC pattern I described in my own pass example, wearing hardware clothing. Two technically-credentialed founders, hot AI-infrastructure category, NVIDIA Inception sticker that functions exactly like a four-logo team page, and a TAM slide that says $4.07B growing to $27.65B at 31.5% CAGR -- this is the deck I pass on at slide 3. Meike's compressor-less A/C and Watney Robotics history is the one piece of dossier evidence that wins against that read; he has actually been inside the adjacent thermal problem with his hands, which is not something the ex-FAANG-PM consensus pitch ever has. The verifiable condition that flips me to invest: a named Texas hyperscale or colocation operator (CoreWeave, Lambda, Crusoe itself, or a tier-1 like Meta/Microsoft) signs a paid pilot with field-instrumented thermal performance data within twelve months. Until then Madrone is a rebuttal, not a hard no, and I am watching from one rung over.
