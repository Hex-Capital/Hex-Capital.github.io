# Glen -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Nikos Dritsakos is running a platform-scope product alone, and that pairing -- highest on schlep, lowest on cofounder configuration -- is the entire diagnosis of Glen. The dossier says team size 1, and it says Glen reads from "code and PRs, issues, docs, and meetings" with per-observation access control and OAuth 2.1 against MCP clients Claude, Cursor, and Codex. That is a connector-by-connector enterprise integration grind being carried by one person, and the question I keep coming back to is not whether Glen's idea is right but whether Nikos can be three people for the eighteen months it will take to find out.

The schlep dimension is where Glen scores best, and the reference point is **Coinbase** (2012). Brian Armstrong's moat turned out to be the regulatory work other YC applicants were unconsciously routing around -- the compliance grind became the asset. Glen has the same structural shape on the technical side: per-source enterprise authorization across code/PR/issue/doc/meeting systems, plus SOC 2 review, plus access-control-per-observation that the dossier flags as architecturally distinct from Mem0's vector-first SDK and Letta's OS-paging runtime. Where Glen diverges from the **Coinbase** (2012) analogy is that Armstrong was personally hungry for the compliance work in a way the application revealed; the Glen dossier shows the schlep but does not show Nikos eating it. "In production today with waitlist wave onboarding" is a phrase that could mean ten deeply-onboarded design partners or two. I cannot tell from the artifact which.

The cofounder dimension is where Glen is at the bottom rung, and the failure mechanism I worry about is the one I described in "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) when I wrote that "empirically it seems to be hard to start a startup with just one founder" -- not because solo founders are dumber but because the morale collapse during the trough has no second person to absorb it. Glen's dossier lists no cofounder pipeline, no advisor names beyond Jared Friedman as YC partner, no technical hire. Mem0 has $24M and 48,000 GitHub stars and AWS Agent SDK distribution; Letta has $10M at a ~$70M post. Nikos versus that field, alone, with connector breadth as the product surface -- this is the exact configuration where the trough kills companies before the schlep moat compounds.

On animal resourcefulness, the eighteen-month mechanism cuts against Glen. The dossier names a prior acquisition -- SalesBop sold to SellWell and rebranded as FliteHouse, where Nikos was VP Tech & Product -- which is real evidence of shipping. But the **Kiko** (2005) → **Twitch** (2007) pattern I use to re-fund failed YC alumni is specifically about *failed* YC founders coming back hungrier; Nikos exited a non-YC company into an operating role, which is closer to acquihire-coded biography than to animal-coded biography. The dossier shows zero pre-funding stunt, no Hacker News demo moment, no Glen-equivalent of the cereal boxes. Absence here is not neutral -- animals leave tracks.

Counterfactually, on organic founder-pain, what would have to be true for Glen to clear this dimension? The dossier would need to say Nikos hit fragmented agent context personally inside FliteHouse's AI sales product, watched his Claude and Cursor agents misread "deliberate choices as defects," and built Glen because he could not stop hitting the wall. The dossier says none of that. It says he co-founded an AI sales coaching company that got acquired -- adjacent, but the [Inferred] prefix on founder-market fit is doing too much work. Drew Houston's USB stick at **Dropbox** (2007) is in the founding mythology because Houston put it there. Glen's mythology is silent on the equivalent moment.

The hacker-genealogy dimension is where my unstated pattern fights me hardest on Glen. I disproportionately fund the Lisp-literate and the competitive-programming founders -- Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist before **Stripe** (2010), Aaron Swartz co-authored RSS before **Reddit** (2005). The Glen dossier says "GitHub: personal handle -- No public repos found" and "Twitter/X: personal handle -- No public account found." Brock University CS with a growth-agency background and a sales-coaching exit is a perfectly respectable operator profile, but it is not the hacker-trail profile that flips my conviction the way the Collisons' Auctomatic code did. The MCP standardization tailwind is real -- and the why-now is plausible -- but standardization wedges reward the team that ships the cleanest primitives, and I cannot judge primitives from a waitlist page.

Thirty-six month forecast: Glen gets acquihired by Mem0 or by a larger MCP-platform vendor (Glean, possibly Anthropic itself) for under $15M, or it pivots to a narrower single-source context product like "shared memory for Cursor" before Series A. The mechanism is the signature from above: solo-founder bandwidth divided by platform-scope surface area equals burnout before moat. Not dead -- Nikos has shipped and exited before, so the floor is acquihire, not zero. But the **Stripe** (2010) ceiling requires the trio that Glen does not have.

The bull case that stings: this is the **Airbnb** (2009) known exception in reverse. I rejected Airbnb's idea silently and got rescued by the cereal boxes; here the idea is clearly good -- agent context fragmentation is real, MCP is the right wedge, "anything learned by one agent is learned by all" is the right one-line product description -- and I am rejecting the founder configuration. If Nikos is in fact an animal and the dossier just under-reports it (waitlist gating could mean genuine demand throttling, the prior FliteHouse exit shows he finishes things, and Jared Friedman picked him into S26 from a competitive pool), then I am making the exact "clever idea, soft founders" inversion error I claim to avoid -- except inverted again, into "good idea, founder I cannot read." Known Exceptions: Loopt was not solo (Altman/Sivo); the Collisons had each other; Airbnb was a trio. I cannot find a top-revealed-outcome solo bet in my own portfolio to anchor on. The flip condition is simple and verifiable: if Nikos signs a technical cofounder with shipped open-source credentials before Demo Day, or if Glen surfaces two named enterprise design partners (not waitlist count, named logos) with weekly active usage from multiple agents per org, I reconsider. Until then, pass.
