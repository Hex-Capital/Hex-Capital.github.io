# Stage -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Stage is what happens when two ex-Five Rings engineers with real lived exposure to AI-generated-code review pain build a single-sided SaaS wedge into a category where CodeRabbit already raised $60M at $550M post and Cursor just absorbed Graphite -- the specific-knowledge gate clears cleanly on Dean Stratakos's in-house coding agent at Five Rings, but the network-compounding axis is empty, and that pairing tells me exactly what kind of bet Stage is.

The leverage check is the first thing I run, and Stage sits between Notion and the cleaner code-native templates. The 204-star `ReviewStage/stage-cli` on GitHub is real permissionless distribution -- a CLI shipped MIT-licensed is the **Stack Overflow** (2008) pattern of code-as-its-own-marketing -- and the Show HN post at item 47796818 with the framing "Putting humans back in control of code review" is media leverage on the founder's own writing. Where Stage diverges from the Stack Overflow rung is that the actual product behind the CLI is a sign-in-gated SaaS with a 14-day trial and a "LAUNCH" 25%-off code, which is capital-permissioned distribution wearing a code-leverage costume. The CLI seeds, the SaaS converts, and the SaaS half is the half I underwrite -- it's PLG-shaped, not Twitter-shaped, and that gap is the difference between a 28 and what I'm actually scoring.

On network compounding, Stage looks like **Perplexity** (~2022) structurally -- single-sided, the Nth reviewer does not make the (N-1) reviewer's experience meaningfully better -- and Perplexity is on my Known Exceptions list precisely because that axis failed and I funded anyway. The Perplexity override required three other gates to clear at top quartile: extreme founder specific knowledge (Aravind's Berkeley retrieval PhD), pre-consensus category timing (pre-ChatGPT scale), and permissionless API/product distribution. Stage's Stratakos has lived AI-coding-agent experience but it isn't a research-depth credential of that magnitude; the category is post-consensus with CodeRabbit at $15M+ ARR growing 20%/month and Greptile sitting on a Benchmark-led $25M Series A; and the distribution is open-source CLI plus YC LinkedIn promo, not API-as-product. The exception precedent does not transfer.

Charles Pan was an early engineer at Yuzu Health (YC W22) and Dean Stratakos led the firm-wide AI initiative at Five Rings and built an in-house coding agent -- that is founder-product-market fit by the definition I posted in May 2018. Stratakos used coding agents at production scale inside a quant trading firm and watched human reviewers drown in agent-generated diffs; that is not "ex-FAANG PM identified TAM opportunity." It is lived problem ownership of the exact kind that put **Stack Overflow** (2008) and **Replit** (April 2023) in my ledger. Stage does not clear at the Amjad-Masad-since-2011 rung because the obsession is months old rather than a decade, but Stratakos's `@dastratakos` GitHub presence and the stage-cli shipped artifact mean specific knowledge is the dimension carrying this dossier.

Pivot-room on Stage is the dimension I'd want to flip but can't honestly. Developer tooling is platform-shaped in the abstract, but the specific wedge -- AI-organized diff "chapters" for human reviewers -- is feature-shaped, and the dossier names GitHub, Cursor, CodeRabbit, Greptile, and cubic as parties with both the capability and the distribution to ship it natively. For this dimension to flip from a 7 to a 12, Stage would have to articulate a credible expansion vector into agent observability, repo-level review-pattern memory, or a builder-side market that compounds Stratakos's coding-agent expertise into something that isn't a PR view -- and the Show HN framing locks them harder into the human-reviewer UX than the pivot math wants.

The founder trio reads clean and silent. Stanford CS for both, D1 athletes (Stratakos tennis, Pan fencing), a shared Five Rings tour, no contested exits, no self-promotion about ethics that would trip my "if someone says they're honest" filter from Knowledge Project #18. Nothing to underwrite the 10-year-partner bar emphatically, nothing to flag against it -- the dossier earns the absent-evidence default and no more, because integrity shows through prior behavior at scale and the prior behavior here is short.

My 36-month forecast: Stage is acqui-hired by GitHub, Cursor, or CodeRabbit for under $30M, or sunset, because the diff-restructuring UX is a feature that the $96M-funded incumbents can ship in a quarter -- the Stratakos-and-Pan duo lands at one of those acquirers as a small senior team and the equity story closes there. The mechanism is straightforward: a single-sided B2B SaaS in a category where three competitors have 10-100x the capital and existing GitHub-app distribution does not get to compound into platform shape before the incumbents copy the wedge.

The strongest bear case against my pass is actually a bull case I have to dismantle: the "human-reviewer-back-in-control" framing could be contrarian-correct exactly because the AI-auto-reviewer cohort is racing toward comment-spam noise, and Stratakos's Five Rings agent experience is rarer signal than the dossier prices in. If buyer sentiment flips against AI-auto-review and toward AI-organized-human-review, Stage's wedge widens rather than narrows. What dismantles it: the dossier shows zero signed LOIs, zero revenue disclosure, and a "5x faster than GitHub" claim with no usage data, while CodeRabbit's $15M+ ARR is documented in TechCrunch -- the buyers have already voted, and they voted for auto-review. The verifiable condition that flips me to invest: Stage ships a repository-level review-memory layer (not a diff UX) that compounds with Stratakos's coding-agent specific knowledge into a builder-tool the incumbents cannot trivially clone, and the open-source CLI crosses 2,000 stars with organic enterprise adoption before the next round.
