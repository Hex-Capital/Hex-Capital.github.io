# Framewise Health -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Framewise Health is the right founder walking into the wrong room. Tane Kim dropped out of Brown's BS/MD program after observing 500+ patient interactions across a dozen-plus institutions -- that is a real clinical hand on the discharge-instructions problem, not a memo -- but the room he walked into already has Memora Health (acquired by Commure December 2024), Klara, Hyro, CipherHealth, and PatientPoint sitting in it, and the EHR vendors themselves (Epic, Oracle/Cerner) sitting on top. The dimensional signature is high founder-touch into a low-weirdness, post-consolidation category, and that is almost always a feature acquisition, not a company.

Palmer Luckey at **Anduril** (2017) is the high rung on founder-lived-experience -- a kid who shipped Oculus before pivoting to defense because he had already done the hard thing once. Framewise Health's Tane Kim does not have that prior-product trajectory, but he does have the autobiographical contact the consensus YC AI-agent darling lacks: the BS/MD program he abandoned, the 500+ rooms he stood in, the clinician-reviewed workflow that exists because he watched discharges fall apart in English-only paper. He is not Luckey, but he is also not the Stanford-CS-ex-FAANG-PM who read about hospitals in a McKinsey deck. Framewise Health diverges from the Anduril rung because David Cui's computer-vision research and Freya-S25 internship is engineering depth, not adjacent-domain shipping; the founder reached the problem honestly, but neither founder has shipped a comparable product at scale before.

**Crusoe** (~2018) is the inverse of what Framewise Health is doing. Crusoe was three uninvestable things stapled together -- crypto, oil, North Dakota -- and the credible-VC passes were the tell. Framewise Health is patient engagement SaaS in 2026, a category MarketsandMarkets pegs at $14.55B growing to $25.01B by 2030 at 11.4% CAGR, with at least five named competitors and a recent Commure?Memora roll-up consolidating the buyer side. Nobody is laughing at Framewise Health, and nobody passed on aesthetic grounds; the dossier shows a Founderland writeup and a YC launch tweet, which is the opposite of "find them before it becomes consensus." When the round looks rational to everyone, I am usually playing the wrong game.

Counterfactual for the CEO-clarity dimension: Tane Kim is named CEO on LinkedIn and David Cui is named CTO; they met at Brown freshman orientation, which is a long relationship with calibrated roles, not a 50/50 vanity split. For this score to flip down a tier I would need to see equal-equity language, dual-CEO titling, or any whiff of unresolved authority -- none of that appears in the dossier. The **HQ Trivia** (2017) Yusupov/Kroll fracture is not the analog here; this is closer to a quiet, settled founder pair, and I score it accordingly without celebrating it.

Eighteen-month mechanism on the regulated/capital-intensive axis: Framewise Health's FHIR integrations with six EHRs plus HIPAA and SOC 2 are real plumbing, and AI-generated medical discharge instructions carry genuine FDA software-as-medical-device exposure -- this is not a SaaS wrapper, this is regulated healthcare with malpractice surface area. That compounds in Framewise Health's favor only if the clinician-review workflow holds up under one bad-comprehension incident in one of the 75+ languages; if it doesn't, the same regulatory wall that should be the moat becomes the legal liability that ends the company. **Mindbloom** sits on a similar regulated edge for ketamine and survives because the founder authentically lives in that domain; Framewise Health has the regulatory exposure but does not yet have a public posture (FDA classification, clinical-validation studies) showing it can carry the weight.

Single-anchor parallel on physical-world verifiability: **Flock Safety** (~2018) I found by reading the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons because the product was a camera I could literally see on a pole. Framewise Health is a B2B SaaS sold through hospital procurement; the only tactile artifact is an SMS video that I would have to be a non-English-speaking discharged patient to receive in the wild. A nurse manager at a regional hospital could install it, but I cannot ride it, scan it, or run into it. That is the dev-tools-abstraction problem dressed in scrubs, and it is why I rarely write checks into hospital-IT SaaS regardless of how good the founder is.

**Forecast, 36 months out:** Framewise Health is acquired by Commure (the most likely buyer, given the December 2024 Memora roll-up) or athenahealth for a $15-40M aqui-product price as an AI-video bolt-on to an existing patient-communication stack, or it stalls below $1M ARR after 24 months of pilot purgatory inside three regional hospital systems and quietly shuts. The mechanism producing this is the dimensional signature: a clinically-honest founder walking into a category whose buyers already have an incumbent vendor relationship and whose incumbents can ship "AI personalized video" as a roadmap feature in two quarters.

The strongest bear-side objection to my own forecast -- which is itself a non-investment -- is the **HandUp** (~2014) exception: I have written checks where the founder biography mirrors my own outsider trajectory and the commercial ceiling looks modest. Tane Kim dropping out of Brown's BS/MD program is exactly the kind of credentialed-exit-from-credentialism that should activate that override. The dossier breaks the analogy in one specific way: HandUp's dental-financing thesis mirrored *my* Tucson homelessness; Framewise Health's discharge-instruction thesis mirrors *Tane Kim's* clinical observation, which is his lived experience, not mine, and I do not get to use the HandUp override on someone else's biography. The verdict flips to Invest if Framewise Health produces, in the next six months, two named hospital-system contracts (not pilots, contracts) at any ARR figure, because the consolidation argument inverts the moment a non-Commure buyer chooses Framewise Health over the incumbent. Until then, a respectful neutral pass.
