# KelAI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

KelAI is a credentialed-domain-expert-without-a-curve — Jeremie Cohen's 8 years as a WorldQuant PM plus the Millennium ML team lead role is the strongest signal in the dossier, and "signals running in production since October 2025" with zero disclosed revenue, customers, or logos is the weakest. That pairing is exactly the AI-cycle profile I avoid: pedigree carrying the pitch where the curve should be. KelAI maps to the "founder is a *promoter* who hasn't shipped" disqualifier I run on every deal — except here it's softened by genuine buy-side domain, which is why I don't reject KelAI outright in the first paragraph.

**Harvey** (2023) is my anchor on the revenue-velocity axis — "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) was the entire reason I paid up at consensus. The mechanism that made Harvey load-bearing was simple: enterprise legal buyers were writing real POs, not running tourism pilots, and the curve was disclosed in numbers anyone could verify. KelAI diverges from Harvey on exactly the dimension that matters: the dossier discloses "signals running in production since October 2025" and *nothing else* — no ARR, no customer count, no named fund, no MoM. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose, and KelAI's silence after seven months of "production" is what I'd call false-signal-shaped, not just-working-shaped.

The lowest-rung failure mode on the founder-ships axis is the *promoter* pattern — title, credential, deck, no shipped artifact you can click. KelAI's dossier shows the canonical version: no GitHub, no Twitter/X, no named side project, no shipped product from Cohen's WorldQuant or Millennium years that I can inspect, and a solo-founder structure where he's "Hiring senior ML engineers and quant engineers" via kelaitech.com/careers rather than co-founding with them. **Perplexity** (2022) came in because Aravind pinged me and "would show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" — that's the only shipping signal I trust at pre-seed. KelAI doesn't fail this test catastrophically, because being a quant PM at WorldQuant for 8 years is a form of shipping (signals into a book), but it's *internal* shipping, not the public-artifact rhythm that predicts founder execution at venture scale. Mid-low.

On the market-catalyst dimension, KelAI cites "LLM agentic workflows have crossed a usable threshold in 2024–2025" — but that's the generic AI catalyst that *every* YC Spring 2026 fintech is invoking, and YC itself put "AI-Native Hedge Funds" on the Spring 2026 RFS list. Compounded over 18 months, a generic catalyst attracts a crowded field rather than opening a window — the **Anduril** (2022) version of this was Pentagon-specific, post-Ukraine, with a 24-month visibility horizon no one else was pricing in. KelAI doesn't have the hedge-fund-specific catalyst equivalent. There's no Reg-NMS shift, no Bloomberg outage, no incumbent failure mode named. The "why now" reads like the why-now for fifty other agentic-AI pitches.

Counterfactual on the index-position dimension: for KelAI to score like **Stripe** (2010) — "an index on e-commerce" — KelAI would have to become the workflow layer every systematic fund touches, the way payments rails are the layer every e-commerce entrant touches. That would require KelAI to handle the universal substrate of quant research (signal storage, backtest infrastructure, attribution) in a way buy-side funds adopt rather than build. The dossier instead describes "autonomous AI agents" running the end-to-end loop — that's a *replacement* play against in-house quant teams at AlphaSense ($1.4B raised, $4B valuation), Hebbia ($159M raised), and Rogo (~$350M), not an index position underneath them. Replacement plays in finance lose to incumbents with relationships unless the replacement is 10x better on a measurable axis, and KelAI hasn't disclosed that axis.

KelAI as a parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015) on the single-miracle axis: Color worked because we isolated the miracle to *workflow* and bought the chemistry from commodity sequencers. KelAI claims a similar architecture — workflow on top of LLMs — but the miracle stack is heavier than it looks. KelAI needs (a) agentic LLMs producing institutional-grade backtests, (b) hedge-fund procurement trusting an external autonomous system with alpha generation, and (c) signal quality competitive with in-house WorldQuant/Millennium teams. That's three independent miracles compounding, and procurement-trust at hedge funds is the one no AI startup has cracked at scale — meetingnotes.com flags SOC2, model validation, and data lineage as table-stakes, and KelAI's dossier shows no compliance posture.

My 36-month forecast on KelAI: KelAI raises a YC-extension seed at $15–25M post by Q4 2026, fails to land a disclosed marquee hedge-fund logo (Citadel, Millennium, Point72, Two Sigma) within 12 months because procurement at those funds requires audited model validation Cohen's three-person team can't ship while also building product, and either pivots to research-copilot tooling for long-only asset managers by mid-2027 or winds down by 2028. KelAI is not an AlphaSense acquisition target because there's no customer book to acquire — the mechanism producing this forecast is the high/low pairing itself: pedigree opens conversations, but the missing curve means none of those conversations convert before runway compresses.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Cohen's 8 years at WorldQuant gives KelAI procurement access at the top 5 systematic funds that no AlphaSense rep can replicate, and if KelAI lands one $2M ACV pilot at a Millennium pod, the per-customer compounding-intelligence loop becomes a real moat — that's the **Brex** (2017) "operationally-disciplined-CEO selling into a network they know" pattern. It stings because it's real: hedge-fund procurement is relationship-gated, and Cohen has the relationships. What dismantles it is the dossier's silence on those relationships — no named LOI, no advisor from a tier-1 fund, no design partner, no Cohen-as-shipper artifact, and a solo-founder structure where his time is split between selling, building, and recruiting the senior ML engineers he's still hiring. The "AI company with no growth curve disclosed" rule applies: if Cohen had a Millennium pilot, KelAI would have disclosed it. **Verifiable flip condition:** KelAI publicly names a top-15 systematic fund as a paying customer (not pilot) by Q1 2027 — at that point I'd revisit at the next round.
