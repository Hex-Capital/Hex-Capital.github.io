# WithAI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

WithAI is the highest-founder-fit version of the exact YC archetype I de-rate hardest in 2026: Ian McInnis ran the "how will AI affect investing" workstream inside Bridgewater under Greg Jensen, and now sells an AI command center back to the buy side — which means the signature here is real founder-market depth (Dimension 3) wrapped around the most consensus-shaped asset class on my sleeve list (Dimension 5). The dimensional pairing tells me what WithAI actually is: a competent enterprise AI SaaS being underwritten by Bridgewater's reputational shadow at a price that's already moved because Rogo just printed a $75M Series C at 27x ARR growth in January 2026.

Category sleeve is where WithAI loses me hardest, and the ladder is brutal. The HIGH rung is Solana at $0.05 in 2018-2019 — pre-consensus crypto with real on-chain economics when most generalist funds had quit the category; the MID rung is the DTC consumer sleeve where Allbirds and Hipcamp were lonely-bid by Slow for years; the LOW rung is the canonical 2026 YC pitch — "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, $30M post, four tier-1s in." WithAI sits one inch above that LOW rung. Multiplier is literally an "AI agent for hedge funds" with 60+ integrations into Bloomberg/FactSet/Snowflake/Enfusion, going head-to-head with Rogo ($154M raised), AlphaSense (Generative Search launched Jan 2026), and Hebbia. "AI is just more internet" was written about exactly this dossier.

Founder conviction is where WithAI earns its real points, and the ladder shows why. The HIGH rung is the Drop.io / Silbermann missionary type — three-plus years of unpaid obsession, public artifacts of belief; the MID rung is a Sublime Security operator with domain credibility but no published thesis; the LOW rung is the strategic-consulting pitch with no public writing. WithAI sits between MID and HIGH but closer to MID: Ian McInnis personally led the AI-in-investing R&D at Bridgewater — the exact in-house program he's now selling against — and Ben Finch was a founding researcher at Sentient Labs and co-authored the OML paper (IACR ePrint 2024/1573). That's real depth on both axes. The gap is the missing artifact — no public essays, no opinionated thread on why managing agents is insufficient, just the marketing-page version of the thesis. Writing is thinking, and I can't find the writing.

Round dynamics on WithAI fail the value-seed test even without a price disclosed. The HIGH rung is Solana at $0.05; the MID rung is Sublime Security entering security before the consolidation wave; the LOW rung is the four-tier-1-VCs-bidding YC darling. Bridgewater's co-CIOs Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour writing angel checks into WithAI is exactly the consensus heat-signature I deselect on — when the apex buyer in your category is on your cap table at seed, the round is not mis-priced, it's the most legibly hot deal in the batch. WithAI sits adjacent to that LOW rung.

Capital structure on WithAI is a generic enterprise SaaS cap table where the HIGH rung (Teamshares employee-equity rollup) and MID rung (Sublime Security with defensible ACVs) both offer something WithAI does not: a structurally unusual instrument or a self-evidently capital-efficient unit economic. WithAI has white-glove implementation, no published pricing, one named customer (Mercator Partners), and a "book a consultation" CTA — that's the pre-revenue services-heavy SaaS profile that needs five tacks before default-alive, with no royalty or holdco shape available. Founder-as-brand is the lowest rung outright — the John Fish / Bookshelved HIGH rung had measurable owned distribution; WithAI's Twitter handles @imjmcinnis and @btsfinch don't even surface retrievable follower counts, and the distribution moat is borrowed from Bridgewater's network, not owned by the founders.

My 36-month forecast on WithAI: acquired by Rogo or AlphaSense for $80-180M after reaching $4-12M ARR across 6-15 mid-market asset managers, or alternatively absorbed into an OpenAI/Anthropic native vertical agent offering — either way the entry price at this seed gets washed out by the Series A markup that the Bridgewater-CIO cap table will absolutely produce, and I make no money. The mechanism is straightforward regatta math: WithAI needs four to five financing tacks to reach a defensible standalone outcome in a category where Rogo is already four tacks ahead with 27x growth, and the M&A clearing price for vertical AI agents in 2027-2028 will be set by the loser, not the winner.

The strongest bull case on WithAI is the Pinterest-override: Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour aren't normal angel signals — they are the two most credible buyers in fundamental investing, and that operator-friend channel is the exact mechanism I admit overrode price discipline on Ben Silbermann in 2010. Mercator Partners going from "existentially threatened" to "tremendously accelerated" is a real customer-love artifact, not a fake pilot. Client-cloud deployment with firm-specific ontology across 60+ integrations is a genuine switching cost once embedded. But Pinterest worked because the deal arrived through personal-network sourcing at a non-competitive price; WithAI is arriving through YC demo day with Bridgewater's co-CIOs visibly on the cap table — that is the opposite of non-competitive sourcing, that's the deal heat itself. I'd flip to invest if I learned WithAI is raising on a sub-$12M post SAFE with the Bridgewater angels having structured the round as a closed friends-and-network deal at a discount to YC consensus pricing — meaning the operator channel actually delivered the price advantage rather than amplifying the deal heat. Absent that, I pass.
