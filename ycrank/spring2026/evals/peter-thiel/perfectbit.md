# PerfectBit -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Peter Vajda ran Media Generation at Meta Superintelligence Lab and Seiji Yamamoto was Senior Staff on Core Llama pre/post-training — PerfectBit is, structurally, two frontier-lab insiders selling back to the ~6 buyers whose data-procurement pain they personally signed off on. That is the analytical signature: PerfectBit scores highest on founder lineage and lowest on atoms-heavy moat, which makes it an **Affirm**-shaped company — operator re-up into a category the operator already knows from the buyer side — wearing the costume of a Scale AI replacement. The interesting question is not whether the founders can build it. It is whether a 5-to-10-customer TAM can support monopoly pricing when every one of those customers has the in-house talent to reproduce **FOVER** (arXiv 2505.15960, May 2025) over a quarter.

**Stripe** (2010) is the load-bearing analogy on the contrarian-secret axis: Patrick Collison named the developer-experience failure mode of legacy processors at a moment when consensus said PayPal had "won" payments. PerfectBit names a comparable incumbent failure mode — Scale AI, Surge, and Mercor have unit economics built on contractor networks paying "$1.5 million per day" (TechCrunch, Oct 2025), so the human-labeling marketplaces structurally cannot cannibalize that contractor revenue to pivot into verifier-grounded generation. That is a real named-incumbent blind spot, not "AI for X" consensus restated. Where PerfectBit diverges from the **Stripe** rung is that the secret is leaking in public — **FOVER** (Z3/Isabelle, May 2025) and **RLVR** (arXiv 2506.15522, Jun 2025) are publishing the technique into the academic commons, which means the contrarian half-life is measured in quarters, not the half-decade Collison enjoyed before Adyen and Braintree understood what had happened.

**Quora** (2010) is the cautionary rung on monopoly trajectory: Adam D'Angelo's Facebook-CTO pedigree had to override a structurally weak monopoly story (consumer Q&A facing Stack Overflow and Yahoo Answers), and the override only barely held. PerfectBit's monopoly trajectory is worse on the dimension that matters — "frontier AI labs" is a market of 5-to-10 named buyers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta, xAI), and customer concentration on a buyer pool that small is anti-monopoly by construction, not pro-monopoly. Every one of those buyers also employs people who could rebuild the verifier pipeline in-house; that is the **Quora**-style pricing-power problem, except the substitutes here are not Yahoo Answers, they are the customers themselves. Pricing power five years out depends on a proprietary library of verified domains that the dossier does not yet evidence.

The definite-plan axis is where the dossier goes silent. PerfectBit's product page describes "formal proof systems, simulators, executable tests, and oracle databases" (perfectbit.ai, 2026), which is a technical posture, not a multi-year roadmap. There is no named milestone, no architectural commitment Vajda and Yamamoto have publicly refused to pivot from, no equivalent of **SpaceX**'s 15-year reusability arc. Over the next 18 months that absence compounds the wrong way: a verifier-grounded data company without a domain-sequencing plan (math first, then code, then physics, then biology, with each verifier creating switching costs in the next) will look indistinguishable from Snorkel AI's $100M Series D pitch (BusinessWire, May 2025), which is exactly the indefinite-optimism mode Zero to One was written against.

Counterfactual on founder lineage: for PerfectBit to score a tier higher here, the dossier would need either a third co-founder from the PayPal Mafia proper or a signed anchor contract from one of the founders' former Meta-adjacent buyers — neither is present, but the existing pedigree is strong on its own terms. Vajda's 11-year Meta arc ending as Director of Media Generation at the Superintelligence Lab and Yamamoto's nine years on Core Llama are senior-IC-at-a-known-frontier-lab signals; this is the closest 2026 equivalent to the PayPal Mafia I have seen in a YC batch. The Meta non-compete and IP overhang are real — Meta put $14.3B into Scale at a $29B mark in June 2025 (Sacra, 2025) — but I treat that as a friction tax, not a disqualifier.

PerfectBit on the atoms axis parallels **Asana** (2010) — pure software in a category my framework would otherwise de-weight, where the override has to come from operator pedigree alone. Moskovitz had shipped **Facebook** (2004) to a billion users; Vajda and Yamamoto have shipped Llama and Meta's generative-media stack. The parallel holds on pedigree-as-override, but **Asana** had a workflow-network compounding story PerfectBit lacks: a verifier library is closer to a proprietary corpus than a network, and corpora depreciate as the frontier moves.

Thirty-six month forecast: PerfectBit reaches $15-40M ARR on two or three frontier-lab contracts (most likely Anthropic and xAI, given Scale AI's post-Meta customer churn), and is acqui-hired by one of those labs in the $250-600M range before it can compound into independent monopoly territory. The mechanism producing this is the buyer-pool size itself: when your TAM is six customers and three of them have the talent to build your product in-house, the rational exit for both sides is absorption, not a public company. That is the **Affirm**-pattern operator re-up that does not reach **Affirm**-scale outcomes because the buyer-side concentration caps the topology.

The bull case that stings: if reinforcement-learning-from-verifiable-rewards becomes the dominant post-training paradigm — and the **RLVR** literature (arXiv 2505.04842, 2025) suggests it is heading there — then verifier-grounded data is not a feature, it is the substrate of frontier training itself, and PerfectBit's proprietary verifier library compounds into a Stripe-shaped developer-substrate moat with switching costs measured in fine-tuned model generations. In that world the **Quora**-style buyer-concentration concern inverts: six customers each paying $50-200M annually is a $1B+ ARR business with pricing power deeper than Scale's contractor-margin model could ever sustain. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull case is the academic leak: **FOVER** and **RLVR** are publishing the core technique into reproducibility within a year of PerfectBit's founding, and well-resourced labs do not pay rent on capabilities their research teams can rebuild in a quarter. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: a signed multi-year anchor contract from one named frontier lab (Anthropic, xAI, or DeepMind) at $10M+ TCV, which would convert the buyer-concentration risk into a buyer-concentration moat. Absent that, I pass at neutral.
