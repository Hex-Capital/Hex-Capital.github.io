# Zomma -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Zomma is a five-person YC S26 team pointing vision-based LLM agents at hedge-fund custodian portals, and the pairing that defines it in my framework is a credible repeat founder (Varun Arumugam, ex-JPMorgan, prior YC exit doing $10-12M in sales) attached to a consequence-of-success that is roughly "hedge-fund middle-office headcount shrinks 20-30% at Arrowpoint-shaped firms." One dimension leans in, four lean out. That is the signature of a competent B2B automation company, not a company I write a personal check to.

Zomma's consequence ladder runs from **OpenAI** (2019) at the top -- civilizational redirection, $50M first VC check -- through **DoorDash** (2013) in the middle, which reshaped a consumer-commerce flow without any science miracle, down to the "GPT-4 summarizes sales calls into HubSpot" pass I make in thirty seconds. Zomma sits between **DoorDash** and that pass, and closer to the pass: the dossier's success-state is that ops teams at asset managers stop logging into custodian portals manually, which is real dollars but names no incumbent regime that disappears. Compare the load-bearing mechanism: **DoorDash** could plausibly restructure how restaurants reach customers; Zomma's ceiling is that SS&C and Juniper Square keep their fund-administrator revenue while Zomma sells software into the same buyers. When my Fortune 2026-03-06 frame says "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor," Zomma is claiming a rounding error inside one regulated vertical -- and the dossier does not even attempt a labor-cost figure.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) failed for a specific reason worth naming here: the capital plan understated the burn required for the underlying science to work, and I flagged this pattern explicitly as a red flag. Zomma inverts that pattern in a way that hurts it for me -- the capital plan does not understate the burn because there is no science. Zomma is a vision-LLM wrapper on Claude and (per the dossier) Hermes, running on top of foundation-model capability released by Anthropic in late 2024, and Anthropic itself shipped 10 finance agent templates including KYC and month-end close in May 2026 alongside a $1.5B JV with Goldman, Blackstone, and H&F. The Zomma dossier lists Claude as a supported agent on its own site -- the vendor Zomma depends on is now the vendor Zomma competes with. That is a SaaS economics profile any Tier-1 generalist can fund, which by my own scoring means I am over-resourced for this deal.

Zomma's founder profile is a mechanism-analysis problem, not a ladder-ranking problem. Cashel Fitzgerald (UT Austin BS, Cornell CS MS, ML engineer at Modern Intelligence, prior SWE at Voyant financial planning software) and Varun Arumugam (ex-JPMorgan) are exactly the domain-expert continuity my TED Talk warned against -- 1986 McKinsey predicted <1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 (actual: 109M) because experts extrapolate the past, and finance-ops veterans building finance-ops automation is the same extrapolation. Over 18 months this compounds badly: the founders will out-execute non-finance competitors on SEC audit-trail specifics, but they will not out-imagine the world-state in which fund administration itself gets restructured. No public thesis-defiance, no controversial position taken in their field, no "too important not to do" framing anywhere in the dossier.

Zomma's learning-rate signal is a counterfactual: if Varun Arumugam's prior YC-backed exit at $10-12M in sales had been described as a public pivot -- a Kanye-moment on the **Slash** (2024) pattern that justified my $100M Series C into a YC-backed fintech -- this dimension would flip a tier. It is not. A prior YC exit at $10-12M ARR is a competent linear execution signal, not a documented thesis update. I score this above the absent-evidence default because a founder who has closed a company once carries real learning; I do not score it at the **Slash** level because there is no stated "we were wrong about X" moment in the record.

Zomma's capital profile compares directly to my $105M co-led seed for **Genesis AI** (July 2025) on the wrong side of the axis. Genesis AI required that scale because robot foundation models need training compute, fleet, and data collection that consensus VCs decline on capital risk. Zomma is a five-person team selling per-seat enterprise software with a "/call" demo funnel, no pricing page, and no disclosed customer -- which is exactly the profile where $500K from a YC allocation carries the company to Series A and my check adds nothing the round would not otherwise fill.

My 36-month forecast on Zomma: acqui-hire by a fund administrator (SS&C, Juniper Square, or iCapital) in the $20-60M range, or absorption into Anthropic's finance-agent template library as it expands from the May 2026 launch -- Zomma reaches $2-5M ARR by late 2027, stalls when Anthropic ships the equivalent KYC-refresh and reconciliation agent, and settles for a talent-and-tech acquisition where the founders join the acquirer's ops-automation team. The mechanism is direct: the model vendor is competing in the workflow layer, the fund administrators own the buyer relationship, and Zomma's "Agent Trust Framework" is marketing copy with no SOC 2 or ISO certification disclosed to defend against either side.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash** (2024) exception -- YC-backed, prior founder pivot, capital-light consumer/B2B fintech that I underwrote at $100M Series C despite the consequence-magnitude gap. Bulls argue Zomma is the same shape: YC S26, Vercel AI Accelerator, prior YC exit, ex-JPMorgan founder-market fit, and a regulated enterprise buyer that Anthropic's horizontal templates will never satisfy on custody-specific portal workflows. That case stings because **Slash** genuinely violated my consequence-magnitude prior and I still wrote the check. What dismantles it: the **Slash** override was a *documented* Kanye-pivot demonstrating learning rate strong enough to overpower the prior, and Zomma's dossier contains no equivalent public thesis update from Varun Arumugam -- a prior exit at $10-12M is competence, not pivot evidence. Verifiable flip condition: if Zomma signs a top-5 fund administrator (SS&C, Northern Trust, State Street) or a $50B+ AUM asset manager on a $1M+ ACV contract before Q3 2027, I revisit at Series A -- absent that, the Anthropic-plus-SS&C pincer closes the window.
