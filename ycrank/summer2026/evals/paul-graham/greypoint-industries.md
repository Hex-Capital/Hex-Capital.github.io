# Greypoint Industries -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Greypoint Industries is a schlep-positioned defense bet with no findable hacker trail and a CEO whose background does not appear to exist on the public internet. The strongest fact in the dossier -- Tyler Bosmeny's S26 RFS explicitly calling for "counter-swarm stack: high-capacity interceptors" -- is also the most worrying fact, because Greypoint Industries reads as an RFS-response rather than an organic itch, and six other YC batchmates (Tenet, 9 Mothers, Surtr, Arlo, Perseus Defense, Seeing Systems) appear to have read the same memo. That is the dimensional signature: a category where the schlep is real but the founder evidence is thin, and where the thinness is concentrated in exactly the place I cannot bluff my way past.

The schlep dimension is where Greypoint Industries earns its keep. **Coinbase** (2012) is the analogy that loads here -- Brian Armstrong walked toward bitcoin compliance because everyone else flinched, and the regulatory grind became the moat. Greypoint Industries is staring at ITAR, Canadian DND procurement cycles, US DoD program-of-record timelines, and the unit-economics problem of building a guided autonomous projectile cheaper than the $400 quadcopter it shoots down. Defense primes like Raytheon cannot produce sub-target-cost expendables at infantry scale; Anduril's Roadrunner and ACS's Bullfrog are both vehicle/turret-mounted, leaving the "man-deployable" rung genuinely open per the UBC Venture Founder listing. Where Greypoint Industries diverges from the Coinbase analogy is that Armstrong had shipped an Android wallet to overturn his rejection; Greypoint Industries has shipped a hackathon slide deck about Arctic surveillance, which is not the same artifact.

The hacker-genealogy dimension is where I lose the thread. The Pass-Example pattern I keep flagging is "CS degree but no shipped open source, no demo, no hack history" -- and Greypoint Industries presents exactly this profile in inverted form: four UBC Faculty of Applied Science affiliates with zero public GitHub repos across the founding team, no open-source contributions, no competitive programming wins, no Hacker News presence. Nic Chu and Jordan Khoo are credentialed engineers but credentialed-only on the public record. The CEO, Darcy Zhang, has "no public background found" -- no LinkedIn surfaced in search, no Twitter, no GitHub, and he is absent from the Sep 2025 hackathon team and the Oct 2025 UBC cohort, meaning he was parachuted in somewhere between October 2025 and YC admission. Patrick Collison won the 41st Young Scientist for Lisp work before **Stripe** (2010); I cannot tell you what Darcy Zhang did last year.

Animal resourcefulness pre-funding: the $5,000 Defence Tech Hackathon win at TMU DMZ in Sep 2025 and "Best Presenter" at the 2026 Red Team Hackathon are hustle, but they are credentialed-pathway hustle -- the same circuit any motivated engineering undergrad in Vancouver runs. Compounded over 18 months, the Greypoint Industries trajectory needs to convert hackathon wins into a deployed prototype with a hit-rate number and a unit-cost number, and the dossier shows no such number; greypointindustries.ca returned 403 on fetch, which is the artifact equivalent of an empty room. The cereal-box pattern at **Airbnb** (2009) is what an animal moment looks like under capital constraint; a UBC cohort slot is what a credentialed pathway looks like.

Organic founder pain: the counterfactual that would flip Greypoint Industries a tier here is one of the four founders having been in uniform -- Canadian Armed Forces, a reservist, anyone who has personally watched a Shahed-136 saturate an air-defense screen. Greypoint Industries has none of that on the public record. The pitch reads as "engineering students saw the news from Ukraine and the Bosmeny RFS and built a deck," which inverts the Dropbox-USB-stick filter from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012). The default penalty applies and it bites.

Co-founder configuration: parallel to **Twitch** (2007)'s Justin Kan / Emmett Shear / Kyle Vogt trio, where the prior **Kiko** (2005) co-shipping was the load-bearing fact. Greypoint Industries has three UBC Applied Science co-founders (Chu, Khoo, Xu) who genuinely did build together at the Sep 2025 hackathon -- that piece is real. The Darcy Zhang parachute breaks the pattern. Three-plus founders with one bolted on after formation is exactly the configuration that surfaces badly in the YC interview, and I cannot tell from the dossier whether Greypoint Industries solved this or papered over it.

My 36-month forecast: Greypoint Industries does not ship a deployed interceptor at the promised sub-target unit cost, fails to win a US DoD or Canadian DND program-of-record, raises a modest seed extension on the Bosmeny-RFS tailwind, and by mid-2029 is either acquihired by Anduril or ACS for under $20M or absorbed into a Canadian prime as a sensor/software layer -- the kinetic-effector ambition having collided with the physics of cheap guidance. The mechanism is the gap between the schlep being real (compliance, manufacturing, procurement) and the founder evidence being thin (no shipped artifact, no organic pain, no hacker trail), with **Coinbase** (2012)'s Armstrong-shaped overrider absent from the cap table.

The strongest objection is the **Loopt** (2005) precedent. I funded Sam Altman before mobile location was usable infrastructure, on force-of-personality alone, overriding my preference for ideas riding clear tailwinds. Could Darcy Zhang be Greypoint Industries's Altman? The dossier cannot tell me, because the dossier cannot find Darcy Zhang at all. That is the bear-case-becomes-bull case I have to entertain: the YC interview surfaced something the public record does not show. But the Loopt override required me to have met Altman; the dossier has no compensating signal -- no demo video, no working prototype unit cost, no signed LOI with a named ministry of defense. One verifiable condition would flip me: a Canadian DND or US DoD demonstration contract -- even a sub-$500K SBIR-equivalent -- with a delivered interceptor showing a hit on a target drone at any unit cost. Until then, Greypoint Industries is a clever-idea-soft-founders shape, and that is the reverse pattern where I lose money. Pass.
