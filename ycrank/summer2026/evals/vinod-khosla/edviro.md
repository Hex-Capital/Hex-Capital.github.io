# Edviro -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Edviro is what happens when two teenagers with real learning rate point an agentic-AI stack at a problem whose maximum-success state is "school districts pay 10% less on their utility bill" -- and the gap between those two facts is the entire evaluation. Hursh Shah pivoted out of Skyglass (smart glasses) into building energy software, which is a non-trivial cross-domain update at age 18; meanwhile the dossier's TAM ceiling is the global HVAC software market at $1.5B by 2035 per Future Market Insights. Strong founder signature, mundane consequence variable. That asymmetry is Edviro's signature in my portfolio.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the ladder rung I use to test consequence magnitude, because the mechanism that justified the $50M first VC check was that the success-state -- general-purpose intelligence -- was civilizationally distinct from the pre-2019 world. Edviro's success-state is that facilities managers at 7 school sites (and eventually thousands) get their boiler anomalies caught before the utility bill prints, with $400K of cumulative customer savings claimed on edviroenergy.com. No incumbent regime collapses. Diesel does not get displaced, the grid does not restructure, the gas utility does not lose its meter. Edviro is on the efficiency side of the energy ledger, not the displacement side, which is exactly the line **Impossible Foods** (2011) crossed on protein and **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) crossed on baseload. Edviro stays on the wrong side of it.

The pass-example pattern -- the GPT-4-summarizes-sales-calls deal I clear in thirty seconds -- is technically a software-only B2B SaaS with standard burn-to-ARR economics and no science miracle. Edviro is in that family. The "energy world model" phrase appears once on edviroenergy.com with no published benchmarks, no NeurIPS paper on the model itself (Hursh's NeurIPS 2024 work is unrelated per the dossier), and the protocol layer is BACnet/Modbus -- commodity. Any YC S27 batch can reproduce the agentic-anomaly-plus-M&V loop on the same open BMS protocols. GridPoint sold for $584M cumulative raise into Twenty First Century Utilities in October 2025 because their hardware was the moat; Edviro explicitly does not have that. Software-only on commodity protocols is the inverse of the **QuantumScape** (IPO 2020) pattern I underwrite.

Hursh Shah at 18 and Tanuj Siripurapu at 19 are not career domain experts -- which is good, since my TED Talk cited the UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study showing experts beat chance only slightly. But "young" is not the same as "publicly defied their field." There is no published Edviro position contradicting building-science orthodoxy, no controversial thesis on why utility-tied M&V is broken, no instigator stance. **Physical Intelligence** (2024) earned the consensus-defying score because Sergey Levine and Chelsea Finn had public records arguing for end-to-end learned robotic policies when the field was modular. Edviro's founders have firsthand exposure to "hometown high schools" wasting taxpayer money -- a motivation story, not a defied-expert story.

Hursh's Skyglass-to-Edviro pivot is the single most thesis-positive fact in the dossier, and it is why I am not at the **DoorDash** (2013) override threshold but I am paying attention. The mechanism that made me write the **Slash** (2024) $100M Series C check was a documented cross-domain thesis update -- the Kanye pivot story -- which signaled learning rate strong enough to override a consequence-magnitude shortfall. Edviro shows the same shape in miniature: consumer AR hardware to enterprise energy software is not a small jump. Tanuj scaling a digital agency to $20K in high school plus an RTX stint adds execution diversity. Eighteen months from now, this pair will know things about agentic deployment that incumbents never learn -- which is a multiplier on whatever vertical they end up in, not necessarily energy.

Required-capital profile is where Edviro disqualifies itself from my check-size pattern. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) absorbed a $115M Series A because the tokamak required it; **Genesis AI** (2025) took a $105M co-led seed because robot foundation models require it; the **OpenAI** (2019) $50M was deployed against compute reality. Edviro's roadmap -- BYO-meter SaaS sold per-district to K-12 -- is the kind of capital profile where I am over-resourced and any Tier-1 generalist clears the round. A counterfactual: if Edviro were proposing to build the world's first multi-modal foundation model for building physics, trained on petabytes of acquired BMS data, with a $40M Series A roadmap to first commercial benchmark, the dimension would flip a tier. The dossier proposes none of that.

My 36-month forecast for Edviro: $400K-$2M ARR across roughly 20-60 K-12 districts, then either acqui-hired below $25M by an incumbent BEMS player (Atrius/Acuity is the named candidate from the dossier) or pivoted by Hursh and Tanuj into a different agentic vertical when they learn that district procurement cycles destroy the unit economics of per-meter SaaS. The mechanism producing this forecast is the same one that made **Range Fuels** (~2007) instructive in the opposite direction: when consequence-magnitude is small, the deal must clear on capital-efficient execution -- and selling agentic software into school-district CFOs is precisely where capital-efficient execution goes to die.

The strongest objection to this forecast is the **Slash** (2024) override pattern: I have publicly admitted that documented learning rate plus YC pedigree can override consequence-magnitude shortfall. Hursh's Skyglass-to-Edviro pivot is real, the YC S26 stamp is real, and the "energy world model" framing could -- if it compounds -- become a proprietary foundation model trained on years of BMS telemetry that eventually displaces incumbent controls software at scale across commercial real estate, not just K-12. That bull case stings because it is structurally identical to how I justified Slash. It loses on one fact: Slash had a consumer-banking total addressable user base in the tens of millions, while Edviro's stated ceiling is the global HVAC software category at $1.5B by 2035 (Future Market Insights) -- smaller than the 2024 OpenAI SPV I wrote alone. The civilizational variable is missing and learning rate cannot manufacture one. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Edviro signs three multi-state district contracts inside twelve months AND publishes a "world model" benchmark demonstrating >40% improvement on HVAC anomaly precision against an Atrius/GridPoint baseline. Absent both, Edviro is the wrong shape for my check.
