# Gravy -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

*Evaluating Gravy, Spring 2026.* Two founders, Ali Tabba and Max Church, shipping a Mac desktop app that runs personal finance agents against Plaid using the user's own OpenAI, Anthropic, or Ollama keys. I run the leverage check first, and Gravy fails it on the side I do not normally see fail -- not because the architecture is wrong, but because the architecture is inert. Local-first with bring-your-own-key is not permissionless distribution; it is just a download. No GitHub, no organic Twitter following on either founder, no Product Hunt launch, no open-source MCP server seeding a developer community around the wedge. Gravy is a single-sided consumer subscription wrapper in a $207B TAM where the highest dimension is "the market is big enough to pivot inside" and the lowest is "the product gets better as more users join" -- and that pairing describes a clone-able PFM app, not a Naval bet.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung on the leverage ladder I want to walk Gravy up: code-or-media-native distribution where the marginal cost of replication is zero and the channel is unowned. Gravy's BYO-LLM-key story sounds like permissionless code leverage at first read -- the user runs Ollama locally, the inference cost lives on-device, Gravy doesn't pay OpenAI per-token. That is capital efficiency, which I like, but it is not distribution leverage. Twitter compounded because every tweet was a billboard for Twitter; Gravy's local-first architecture is the opposite -- by design, nothing about a user's Gravy session leaves the device, which means nothing about Gravy propagates either. The dossier names a Mac download and a Windows waitlist as the entire GTM. That is a download page, not a leverage engine.

Perplexity (2022) is the LOW rung I am forced to invoke on the network dimension, and the comparison is unkind to Gravy. I waived criterion #2 on Perplexity because Aravind Srinivas's Berkeley retrieval PhD and the pre-consensus AI-cycle timing reset the math -- three of five gates cleared at top quartile, so single-sidedness got a pass. Gravy's dossier asks me to make the same waiver with none of the same compensating evidence: no public technical artifact from Tabba or Church, no pre-consensus category (AI-PFM already has Monarch at $95.5M raised, Cleo at Series C, Copilot at Series A), and an explicitly local-first architecture that *prevents* any cross-user value compounding by design. Each Gravy user is an island. That is the pattern Perplexity got forgiven for; Gravy is not Perplexity.

Founder specific knowledge is where the dossier silence does real damage to Gravy. Ali Tabba's public footprint is "Founder (YC P26)" on LinkedIn and a University of Warwick degree; Max Church has no surfaced LinkedIn, no GitHub, no Twitter. The Stack Overflow (2008) template -- developers writing for developers, founder-as-user with lived specific knowledge -- is the standard I apply to builder-tools, and Gravy's category is adjacent enough that I apply it here. The dossier surfaces no prior fintech role, no Plaid-integration background, no banking domain immersion, no shipped artifact in personal finance. Applying my absent-evidence default at 7/20 is generous; the explicit competitive set -- Monarch's couples-and-goals product, Cleo's Gen-Z chat, Copilot's iOS budgeting -- is being attacked by founders whose public record does not yet show they have lived the problem.

The market dimension is where Gravy scores its highest, and I want to be honest about why. Personal finance apps at $207B in 2026 with a 25.2% CAGR is platform-shaped TAM; consumer finance, prosumer CFO tooling, small-business agentic accounting, and creator-economy money management are all real pivot vectors from where Gravy stands. Over an 18-month horizon, the market gives Gravy room for two product rewrites without refinancing -- the Twitter-from-Odeo, Uber-from-black-car pattern is structurally available. What it cannot do is manufacture the leverage architecture or the network compounding that the first three dimensions require. Big market is necessary, not sufficient, and on Gravy it is the only dimension doing real work.

Forecast at 36 months: Gravy is either dead with under $500K ARR, or has pivoted into a prosumer/small-business CFO-agent adjacency where BYO-LLM-key friction is a feature rather than a bug -- because Monarch ($95.5M raised) and Copilot will ship local-first and BYO-key consumer modes within twelve months once the category narrative arrives, and Gravy's consumer wedge collapses the moment the incumbents copy the architecture without restructuring their revenue. The mechanism is the lowest-rung dimension: no network means no defensibility against feature-copy, and a two-person team without documented domain specific knowledge does not out-execute three funded incumbents on the same Plaid pipes.

The bull case stings, and I want to name it plainly: Gravy is Notion (2013) in disguise -- a single-sided productivity tool that I funded against criterion #2 because Ivan Zhao's specific knowledge and code-leverage distribution carried it; privacy-conscious power users who adopted Obsidian, Linear, and Raycast form a cult following around Gravy's local-first wedge; Plaid-plus-MCP becomes the consumer-AI default interface; the Mac-only beachhead becomes the prosumer CFO platform. The case sting is real. The case fails on the specific evidence: Ivan Zhao had a documented adolescent-onward obsession with personal productivity tooling before Notion; Gravy's dossier surfaces zero comparable public artifact from either founder, no GitHub repos, no domain writing, no prior shipped product in fintech. The leverage architecture I am being sold as code-leverage is actually onboarding friction -- asking mainstream PFM users to provision API keys is the documented anti-pattern, not the wedge. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Tabba or Church ships a public open-source Plaid-MCP server or a written technical artifact about agentic finance that earns organic developer pull -- that would re-rate criterion #1 and criterion #3 simultaneously, and I would re-read the dossier. Until then, Gravy is a pass.
