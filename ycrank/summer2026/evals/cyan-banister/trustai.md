# TrustAI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Hannah Chung and Medha Venkatapathy walk into the room with MIT stamps, a Washington DC summer internship (2025), a Physics Olympiad medal (2021), and a Lincoln Lab line item -- and TrustAI is "suggested cross-tool automations that run in one-click in background." That sentence is the Pass Example I already wrote, almost verbatim. TrustAI's highest-scoring dimension is that Hannah is explicitly the named CEO, so governance is clean; its lowest is that TrustAI is pure software abstraction in the most consensus-crowded lane of Summer 2026 YC. Governance-clean plus thesis-invisible is not a check I write.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before Anduril (2017), and John Hanke had a decade of Keyhole and Google Earth behind him before Niantic (~2015) -- that is the HIGH rung on founder-surfaced-from-lived-experience, and it is load-bearing because both founders were *of* the problem, not visiting it. TrustAI's founders are, per the dossier, an MIT undergrad who interned in DC last summer and an MIT CS+Physics student out of Lincoln Lab -- no HubSpot admin hours, no Salesforce ops seat, no CS/RevOps night shift. The problem TrustAI is selling ("knowledge workers repeat cross-tool busywork") was discovered in a memo, not at a desk. Hannah's LinkedIn says "Co-founder, CEO @ TrustAI (YC S26)" and that is the whole surface area on domain contact. This is analytical origin, not autobiographical.

HQ Trivia (2017) is my anti-portfolio anchor on co-founder governance, and the failure mode was Yusupov and Kroll never resolving who broke ties. TrustAI does not have that pattern -- Hannah is explicitly listed as CEO, Medha as CTO, roles cleanly split between product/GTM and technical. There is no dossier evidence of a 50/50 tiebreaker vacuum, no "we're figuring out titles" hedge, no third co-founder muddying the vote. This is the one dimension where TrustAI actively passes the screen rather than defaulting through it, and it earns real points -- but a clean cap table on two-person MIT founding teams is table stakes at YC S26, not a differentiator.

Bardeen raised $25.3M with Insight Partners leading and Dropbox and HubSpot Ventures strategically in (TechCrunch, August 2024); Zapier is doing roughly $300M ARR; Magical raised $60M+; Lindy and n8n are both funded. That is the consensus wall TrustAI is walking into with a Calendly demo funnel and a "no installation required" claim the dossier flags as technically undocumented. Over 18 months, this compounds badly: the moat TrustAI needs -- a behavioral dataset of "how top performers work" -- requires customer volume TrustAI does not have, while Bardeen and Salesforce Flow can bolt suggestion generation onto existing seats. My "find them before it becomes consensus" filter (Yahoo Finance, March 2025) inverts here; ten funded competitors before Series A is the deselection signal.

SpaceX (2008) was my first angel check because rockets were uninvestable, and the moat compounded in a domain other capital would not enter. TrustAI is the counterfactual: to flip capital-intensive/unsexy-regulated up a tier, TrustAI would need to be selling into a category the incumbents actively refuse -- defense workflow, regulated pharma ops, oilfield back-office -- not HubSpot and Gmail. The integration logo lineup on auto.trytrust.ai (HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Sheets, ChatGPT, Gmail, Slack, Google Docs, Google Drive) is exactly the surface every AI-agent YC company in this batch is targeting. Zero frontier-hardware signal, zero regulatory moat, zero "aesthetically transgressive" edge.

The Niantic (~2015) parallel is the cleanest way to expose TrustAI on physical-world verifiability: I got into Niantic because I had been playing Ingress obsessively since 2012 and could *feel* the pull of the product in my own hands. TrustAI has no Chrome Web Store listing, no Product Hunt launch, no waitlist, no public demo -- only a Calendly booking. The dossier is explicit: I cannot install TrustAI, I cannot photograph it running, I cannot hand it to a friend at dinner. The "no installation required" claim is the one detail that could make TrustAI tactile, and the dossier notes the mechanism is undocumented and technically implausible without OAuth per app.

My 36-month forecast on TrustAI: quiet acqui-hire by Bardeen or HubSpot for under $10M in stock, or a shutdown, most likely by mid-2028. The mechanism is straightforward -- TrustAI enters a category with ten funded competitors, no lived-experience moat on the founders, no tactile artifact to generate bottom-up pull, and a demo-only funnel against Bardeen's Dropbox/HubSpot distribution. The behavioral dataset moat requires customer density TrustAI will not achieve before Zapier or Salesforce Flow ships a suggestion layer to their existing seats.

The bull case against me stings, and it is the DeepMind exception: Hassabis, Suleyman, and Legg were pedigreed PhDs I bet on because polymath cross-domain compounding overrode the outsider-founder screen. Medha with USA Physics Team (2021) plus MIT CS is arguably a polymath signal -- the age-of-the-polymath thesis I articulated to The Generalist (2024-25) says AI reweights *who* the next founder is. But the DeepMind override required the founders to be doing something no one else was even attempting; TrustAI is doing what Bardeen, Lindy, Magical, and n8n are all doing right now with more funding and more distribution. Polymath signal without a contrarian domain does not override consensus-crowded market. The one verifiable condition that would flip TrustAI to a check: a signed enterprise LOI with a named Fortune 500 RevOps or CS org that Bardeen and Zapier had already courted and lost -- proof that Hannah's team is winning knife-fights against funded incumbents, not decorating the same funnel.
