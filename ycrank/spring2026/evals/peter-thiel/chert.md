# Chert -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Chert is a "Twilio for iMessage" pitch from Gary Gao and Ian Fong, and the formulation alone tells me what kind of company this is: the literal "Uber-for-X" / "Twilio-for-Y" mimetic construction Zero to One was written against, sitting on top of a protocol Apple does not license and can revoke at will. The dimensional signature here is a founder pair with credible quantitative training (Penn CS/Wharton, NYU Courant, eight hackathons) but zero contrarian secret and zero structural moat -- which is precisely the indefinite-optimism profile that the 2024-26 YC batches are saturated with. Chert is the median pre-seed of its cohort, distinguishable from Sendblue, LoopMessage, Blooio and Linq only by recency.

Stripe in 2010 is the load-bearing comparison on the contrarian-secret axis because Patrick Collison named PayPal's failure mode -- developer experience -- and bet that the "won" category had a hidden seam. Chert names no such seam. The Launch HN tagline is "Twilio for iMessage," which is the analog-of-an-analog -- mimetic by construction, not contrarian. The closest thing to an incumbent failure mode in the Chert dossier is "comprehensive line health checks" against deliverability bans, which is a feature, not a thesis. The actual incumbent Chert must displace is Sendblue, a fellow YC company with five years of head start and broader feature surface; framing your YC predecessor as the thing to beat is the competition-as-validation pattern I treat as a failure mode, not a signal.

The lowest-rung pattern on monopoly trajectory is the "AI for X with a better model" pass -- moat that any competitor reproduces in nine months. Chert exhibits the same shape with the protocol substituted for the model: every iMessage-API vendor is reverse-engineering the same Apple-controlled rails, so the ceiling on differentiation is whatever Apple tolerates this quarter. The Chert dossier lists Blooio at $39/month flat-rate unlimited already undercutting on price, Sendblue holding feature breadth, and Linq positioned on AI agents. Four funded competitors in a category whose underlying protocol could be deprecated by a single Apple developer-relations memo is not a monopoly trajectory -- it is a commoditizing race held inside someone else's walled garden. There is no pricing-power story at five years because there is no protection at five years.

On definite plan, the mechanism that compounds over eighteen months is whether the founder has named a 2-5 year technical milestone they will not pivot from. Chert's public artifacts -- trychert.com, the Product Hunt page, the Launch HN -- describe CRM integrations (HubSpot, Close, GoHighLevel, Vapi, Attio) and "scalable pricing structure," which is the language of iteration, not definite optimism. The roadmap collapses to "more integrations, more lines, more messages." Gary Gao and Ian Fong started in June 2025 and pivoted through "leasing companies, DTC startups, and home service agencies" before landing here; that is the lean-startup customer-discovery mode operationalized, not the SpaceX-style multi-year commitment.

On founder lineage, the counterfactual is what would have to be true for Chert to clear the bar: one of Gary Gao or Ian Fong would need to have shipped a product to scale at a prior company, or hold a Patrick-Collison-style proxy signal like a verified Olympiad result or a meaningful acquired side project. The dossier gives me Penn CS/Wharton, NYU Math/Econ, Courant fast-algorithm research, eight hackathon wins, and an incoming-but-untaken JPMorgan offer. Hackathons are college achievement, not operator pedigree -- the Affirm/Levchin standard is shipped-to-scale, not won-a-weekend. No PayPal Mafia adjacency, no Thiel-portfolio prior role, no acquired prior company. This is a competent first-time technical pair, which scores at the absent-evidence default and not above it.

Anduril is the single-anchor parallel for atoms-heavy / regulated moat, and Chert is its inverse on every axis. Palmer Luckey owned hardware capex and a regulated customer base where Lockheed and Raytheon structurally could not compete; Chert owns no hardware, no regulated customer, and operates as a tolerated guest on Apple's consumer messaging protocol. The "regulatory" dimension here cuts backward -- Apple is the regulator, and every iMessage-API vendor is one terms-of-service update from extinction. That is anti-moat. SMS/RCS fallback is mentioned in the Chert dossier as mitigation, but if the fallback is the business, then Chert is just a thin CPaaS reseller competing against Twilio proper.

My 36-month forecast for Chert: acqui-hired by Sendblue, HubSpot, or Close for under $15M, or dead after an Apple anti-spam enforcement wave wipes out outbound-iMessage line economics across the category. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- median-quality technical founders with no contrarian secret in a commoditizing category sitting on a platform they don't control produce small acqui-hires or quiet shutdowns, not power-law outcomes. The 65% response and 90% action rates Chert cites are unverified and, even if real, are exactly the metrics Apple uses to identify the outbound senders it will throttle next.

The strongest bull case is that Sendblue itself went through YC, that CPaaS is a real $21B market growing at 14% CAGR, that Gary Gao and Ian Fong have spent six months building for actual customers across leasing, DTC, and home services, and that Chert has ten logos including Whop on the site within weeks of launch. The closest Known Exception is Asana -- pure SaaS with operator-pedigree override -- but Gary Gao and Ian Fong are not Dustin Moskovitz; there is no Facebook-scale shipped product behind them, so the exception does not transfer. Citing TAM growth as the bull case is precisely the indefinite-optimism failure mode the 2011 Founders Fund manifesto attacked. The one verifiable condition that would flip my verdict on Chert: Apple publicly grants Chert (and not its competitors) a sanctioned business-iMessage API partnership -- a regulatory monopoly conversion that the dossier gives no evidence is even being pursued. Pass.
