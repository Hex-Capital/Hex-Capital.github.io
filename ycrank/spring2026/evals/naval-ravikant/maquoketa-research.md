# Maquoketa Research -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Maquoketa Research is a Berkeley-RF specific-knowledge team selling DoD-permissioned hardware -- Alejandro Hernandez's mmWave phased-array research at Berkeley Wireless Research Center is exactly the non-trainable depth I underwrite, and the $5,000-per-unit Pentagon Drone Dominance procurement is exactly the gatekeeper-channel I avoid. The signature is real engineering knowledge wrapped inside the wrong leverage shape. The whole question collapses to whether the Anchorage Digital exception applies, and I do not think it does.

**Permissionless Leverage (my lowest rung for Maquoketa Research).** The Stack Overflow (2008) failure mechanism in reverse is what I am looking at here: Atwood and Spolsky shipped a free public Q&A site on the open web with zero gatekeeper between the founder's code and the developer reading it, and the company compounded because every answer was indexed by Google forever. Maquoketa Research's distribution is the structural opposite -- the customer is the DoD, the channel is Replicator/Drone Dominance program selection, and the dossier explicitly notes Maquoketa Research is "not named in publicly reported Replicator selectees" alongside AeroVironment, Swarm Aero, and Performance Drone Works. ITAR is not Anchorage Digital's OCC charter; the Anchorage exception required pre-consensus category timing plus permission-as-moat, and defense autonomy at Anduril's $30.5B and Shield AI's $12.7B March 2026 valuation is the loudest consensus on the board, not the quiet 2014 MetaStable corner. I do not get to invoke the override.

**Specific Knowledge (my highest rung for Maquoketa Research).** Aravind Srinivas at Perplexity (2022) brought a Berkeley retrieval-ML PhD into a problem the rest of the market was still narrating; Alejandro Hernandez at Maquoketa Research brings Berkeley Wireless Research Center work on 140 GHz MIMO phased arrays into the exact RF/seeker stack a loitering munition needs. That is load-bearing -- you cannot train a McKinsey alum into a phased-array designer in twelve months. Where Maquoketa Research diverges from the Srinivas analogue is that the CTO's specific knowledge is bolted onto a CEO whose prior life was derivatives trading software at SpiderRock and a fourth co-founder David Muchow whose resume the YC bio itself describes as "confusing-to-recruiters." The depth is concentrated in one chair, not distributed across the cap table the way Stack Overflow's was.

**Marketplace / Compounding User Value.** Mechanism analysis over 18 months: Maquoketa Research sells attritable hardware to a single buyer category, and the Nth drone delivered to the DoD does not make the (N-1)th drone more valuable to any other user -- the value compounds only inside a closed mission-data flywheel that the dossier itself flags as "no operational deployments are public yet." There is no Uber-style two-sided supply-demand reinforcement and no Twitter-style protocol effect; one customer, one direction, hardware revenue per unit. The dimension is structurally absent, not merely under-evidenced.

**Big Market with Pivot Room.** Counterfactual: the $13.26B-by-2030 loitering munition market (MarketsandMarkets, 2026) clears the TAM bar on paper, and the score would flip a tier if Maquoketa Research could credibly pivot into adjacent categories -- ISR, swarm coordination, civilian inspection drones -- without re-qualifying through ITAR, DoD vendor certification, and a different sales motion. It cannot. The "first idea never works" axiom assumes pivot-room measured in months of founder iteration; Maquoketa Research's pivot-room is measured in years of compliance buildout. Vertical depth without lateral mobility.

**Founder Trio.** Single-anchor parallel: Notion (2013) at the seed had Ivan Zhao with adolescent-onward product obsession and clean integrity reads -- no red flags, but also no public writing or shipped artifacts the way Atwood and Spolsky brought. Maquoketa Research is similar in shape: Evan Yeager and Alejandro Hernandez present no integrity contradictions, no contested prior exits, no self-promotion about ethics that would trip the "if someone says they're honest" filter. But there is also no GitHub, no public writing, no Twitter footprint -- nothing that lets me run the 10-year-partner test from any signal other than the YC bio. Default applies cleanly.

**36-month forecast.** Maquoketa Research is acquired by AeroVironment or Anduril for under $75M inside 30 months as an RF/seeker-stack tuck-in around Hernandez's phased-array IP, or absent Replicator selection runs out of runway below $3M revenue -- the specific-knowledge sleeve gets a home, the company entity does not become a standalone outcome, because permission-gated hardware does not produce the 10,000x geometry that justifies the angel slot.

**Strongest objection.** The bull case is the Clearview exception (criterion #5 flexed because specific knowledge was extreme and downside was capped at one X) reapplied here: Hernandez's mmWave depth is genuinely rare, the $5,000-per-unit Pentagon procurement window is a generational defense procurement reset, and a small attritable-drone team could ride the Replicator wave to a $500M acquisition the way Anduril rode the early defense-tech opening. It stings because the precedent exists in my own ledger. What dismantles it is the Anchorage half of the framework: Clearview's exception was at angel-check size with one-X downside and asymmetric upside in a non-consensus category; Maquoketa Research is competing in the loudest consensus category in venture against $2.5B-raised Anduril and Shield AI's $12.7B March 2026 valuation, and the asymmetric math collapses because the upside is bounded by procurement-cycle reality, not protocol-effect physics. The condition that flips my verdict is a named DoD contract -- Replicator selection or a direct OTA award above $5M to Maquoketa Research specifically -- inside the next nine months. Until then this is the wrong leverage shape for my book.
