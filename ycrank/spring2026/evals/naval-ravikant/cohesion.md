# Cohesion -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Devon Krapcho spent five years as a fundamental equity analyst at Long Path Partners covering software companies. That's real. He knows the pain of manually scanning earnings transcripts, X posts, and podcasts for signals across a coverage universe. But specific knowledge of a problem is not the same as specific knowledge that becomes the product's engine. When I backed Perplexity, Aravind Srinivas didn't just understand that search was broken — his years at Google Brain and OpenAI meant he had built the exact systems required to fix it. Cohesion's core product — LLM agents monitoring public data sources — does not require Krapcho's five years of analyst workflow knowledge to build or improve. Matt Munns built AI at T. Rowe Price, McBrien scaled AWS Lambda. Relevant resumes, yes. But the dossier itself admits the quiet part: "Open-source projects like the ai-hedge-fund repo on GitHub demonstrate that basic investment research agents can be assembled from commodity LLM APIs." Krapcho's specific knowledge pointed the compass. It does not power the engine.

Cohesion's leverage architecture looks clean on the surface — three people, AI agents, zero marginal cost per additional monitored company. But run the audit one layer deeper and the leverage is rented. The foundation model providers supply the intelligence. X's API supplies the social data, and X has repriced that API multiple times since 2023. Podcast feeds supply the audio. Cohesion is assembling leverage from components it does not control and cannot lock down. Contrast this with Notion, where the product IS the leverage — every template, every database, every workspace integration compounds into switching costs that belong to Notion. Cohesion's compound interest test is weak: there is no obvious mechanism by which the product gets better with more users in a way that a competitor with the same LLM APIs cannot replicate. The data advantage the dossier hypothesizes — "proprietary mappings of which non-traditional sources yield actionable signals" — is speculative and would take years to become meaningful.

AlphaSense at $500M+ ARR and a $4B valuation is the elephant. The bull case for Cohesion says AlphaSense is search-and-retrieval, architecturally unable to go agent-first without cannibalizing its query-driven UX. I've seen this argument before — it's the principal-agent case I made for Perplexity against Google. But the structural dynamics are different. Google's ad revenue model creates existential conflict with providing direct answers. AlphaSense has no such conflict. Their revenue comes from giving analysts better intelligence tools. Adding autonomous agent features is an extension of their existing value proposition, not a cannibalization of it. AlphaSense is actively acquiring capabilities — Tegus for $930M, Sentieo, Carousel — and seeking hundreds of millions in additional funding per Bloomberg (March 2026). Cohesion is a feature that a well-capitalized incumbent can absorb.

The principal authenticity question: Krapcho left a stable analyst career to build Cohesion, which signals genuine conviction. That counts. But the co-founder relationships concern me — Krapcho (Notre Dame, finance), McBrien (Georgia Tech, AWS), and Munns (T. Rowe Price, ML) share no overlapping employers or universities, and the dossier finds no public data on their co-founder history. I don't automatically disqualify teams without shared history, but the principal-agent problem between co-founders is real. The strongest authenticity signal would be evidence that these three were already collaborating on this problem before forming a company. The dossier provides none.

Ten-plus fundamental equity funds managing combined $10Bn+ AUM are live on Cohesion — at pre-seed, that is meaningful. Here is the strongest bull case: Krapcho's Long Path Partners network gives Cohesion direct access to the exact buyer persona. Hedge funds pay premium prices for information edge — $20K-$50K per seat is normal in this market. If Cohesion converts these trials to paid contracts at enterprise fintech pricing, the revenue per customer supports a real business even with a narrow initial wedge. The timing is defensible: YC listed "AI-Native Hedge Funds" as an explicit RFS category, over 35% of new hedge fund launches in 2025 branded as AI-driven per Hedgeweek, and LLM capabilities crossed the quality threshold for parsing nuanced financial commentary. A determined advocate would argue Cohesion is the picks-and-shovels play in a gold rush. I take this seriously. But "limited early trial access during earnings season" is not the same as paying customers, and trials at hedge funds are notoriously cheap to extend and expensive to convert. The condition that would flip me: evidence that two or more of those 10+ funds have signed paid contracts at $30K+ per seat annually, demonstrating willingness to pay at enterprise rates and validating that the agent workflow creates value analysts can't replicate with AlphaSense plus a weekend of prompt engineering.

Cohesion does not expand access to a new population. It automates an existing workflow for hedge fund analysts who already have Bloomberg terminals, AlphaSense subscriptions, and research teams. Compare to AngelList, which gave thousands of angel investors access to deal flow previously restricted to a few hundred connected insiders. Cohesion is an efficiency tool for an already well-served, affluent customer base. Efficiency tools can build good businesses. They rarely produce the asymmetric returns I look for at the angel stage.

The decisive dimensions: Specific Knowledge Productization scores below the midpoint because Cohesion's founding team has domain-relevant experience but not the non-replicable expertise that IS the company's core advantage — the solution (LLM agents on public data) is commoditized, as the dossier's own commoditization risk section confirms. Permissionless Leverage Architecture scores neutral because the leverage exists structurally (code, AI, small team) but is rented rather than owned, with no compounding data flywheel. Cohesion is a competent team solving a real problem in a real market, but competition through authenticity requires something the market cannot replicate. I don't see what that is here. Pass.
