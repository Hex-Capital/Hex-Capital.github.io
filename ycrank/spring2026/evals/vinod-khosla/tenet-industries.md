# Tenet Industries -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Tenet Industries is a consequence-rich wedge attacked with an execution playbook, not a miracle — Hugo Frisk's LinkedIn headline literally reads "Mass production," and the company's pitch reduces to dropping a kamikaze quadcopter from ~10,000 SEK to ~3,000 SEK by collapsing part count from 30 to 14 with injection molding instead of 3D printing. That high/low signature — defense attritable consequence on top, science-miracle absence underneath — is the entire analytical fingerprint. Tenet Industries sits in the same wedge Helsing's $695M round and Anduril's $6B-plus war chest are already paving, which means the consequence is real but the moat is execution-mode, not physics-mode.

On consequence magnitude, the right ladder rung is **DoorDash** (2013), not **OpenAI** (2019). DoorDash earned the consensus-bet exception in my portfolio because consumer-commerce flow was a category-redrawing variable even without a science miracle; Tenet Industries' attritable-munitions thesis is similar — Ukraine's FPV consumption rates have already revealed that the diesel-era incumbent procurement regime (AeroVironment Switchblade at five-figures-per-unit, US-component supply chains, ITAR friction) is structurally mispriced for a peer war, and a 3,000-SEK kamikaze drone is the kind of unit-economics rewrite that collapses an incumbent regime rather than nibbling at it. Where Tenet Industries diverges from the DoorDash analog is scale of named consequence: the dossier never quantifies the displacement — no $15T-labor-style anchor, no European munitions procurement TAM, no Ukraine consumption-rate figure tied to Tenet Industries' production roadmap. Consequence is present; consequence-at-scale is asserted by inference.

On founder learning rate the cautionary rung is the **Glean** override I had to talk myself into — Arvind Jain was pure ex-Google domain-expert continuity, and I funded it anyway because capital scale and consequence magnitude carried the consensus-credential profile. The failure mechanism that worried me at Glean — a founder applying the same playbook they already knew — is inverted at Tenet Industries: Frisk, Andersson, and Falk are KTH/Stockholm School of Economics students with no playbook to repeat, no prior exit, no public thesis pivot, and a five-month operating history. That is not domain-expert continuity; it is domain-naivety. Emil Falk's prior stint at Falks Metall AB is the only metallurgical-manufacturing breadcrumb in the entire dossier. Apply the absent-evidence default and do not pretend the silence is a signal.

The technical-capital-risk dimension compounds the wrong way over 18 months. The mechanism: Helsing is already producing HX-2 at scale with $695M of dry powder; Anduril is shipping at higher price points with >$1B revenue; Mach Industries has a Pentagon development contract. Tenet Industries' edge — DFM, injection molding, part-count collapse — is a manufacturing-engineering edge, not a physics edge, which means it is a learning-curve race against incumbents with 100x the capital. By month 18, either Tenet Industries has a signed FMV or Ukrainian procurement contract that locks in the cost curve, or Helsing's next iteration eats the 3,000-SEK price point and the wedge closes. This is not the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) shape, where capital depth itself repelled consensus VCs; this is the shape consensus VCs gladly fund — and have funded — at 100x Tenet Industries' check size.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus, the counterfactual is clean: for Tenet Industries to flip a tier on this axis, one of three founders would need a publicly documented contrarian position — a published manifesto on European sovereign munitions, a defection from a Swedish defense prime like Saab, or a stated heresy on attritable-drone doctrine that the Helsing and Anduril founders explicitly reject. None of that exists in the dossier. Frisk's "Mass production" LinkedIn line is a posture, not a heresy. The pattern my TED Talk warned against — experts extrapolating the past — does not apply here because Tenet Industries' founders are not yet experts; but neither have they earned the right by defying any.

On required-capital profile, compare directly to **Genesis AI** (2025), the $105M co-led seed for robot foundation models. Genesis required that capital for a stack-deep training run; Tenet Industries' $500K YC ticket plus modest injection-molding tooling is the inverse — capital-light hardware where any Tier-1 generalist is over-resourced for the deal, and the next round will be a $5-15M seed extension that twenty European defense-tech funds will compete for. The Khosla concentration-bet pattern that defined the $50M OpenAI check and the $115M Commonwealth Fusion lead does not load here.

My 36-month forecast for Tenet Industries: acquihired into a European defense prime — Saab, Helsing, or a Swedish FMV-favored consolidator — at sub-$60M, with one to three small Swedish or Ukrainian pilot contracts totaling under $8M of cumulative revenue and no breakout production volume. The mechanism is straightforward: a cost-curve race in a capital-intensive procurement market is won by whoever already has the procurement relationships and the dry powder, and Helsing has both. Tenet Industries gets bought for the DFM IP and the team.

The strongest case against my pass leans on the **DoorDash** exception. Bull mechanism: Ukraine's FPV consumption is so structurally underserved that even a third-place European attritable producer captures real volume; Anduril is ITAR-encumbered for non-US buyers; Helsing's HX-2 is priced above Tenet Industries' target; and the EU sovereign-supply preference plus Swedish ISP licensing creates a regulatory carve-out where a domestic European producer at 3,000 SEK is structurally preferred. That story stings because it is the **DoorDash** (2013) shape — capital-light, consensus-funded, but consequence-rich enough that the override fires. What dismantles it is the Unstated Pattern I quietly enforce: I favor founders with one prior exit or one publicly known intellectual reputation before I write — Bob Mumgaard at Commonwealth, Levine and Finn at Physical Intelligence, even Tony Xu had operated DoorDash through a recognizable pivot before my check landed. Frisk, Andersson, and Falk are five months in with no shipped units, no signed LOI, only "discussions." The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed FMV or Ukrainian Ministry of Defense purchase order for ≥10,000 units at the 3,000-SEK economics, dated before YC Demo Day. Absent that, this is a pass.
