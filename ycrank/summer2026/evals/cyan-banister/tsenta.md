# Tsenta -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Tsenta is what happens when an authentic founder-pain story walks into a category my thesis tells me to refuse. Agnay Srivastava and Pulkit Gupta applied to 1,500 jobs each as international students at Rose-Hulman -- that is the kind of autobiographical bruise I look for. But Tsenta sells auto-apply Chrome extensions in a lane where Jobright already claims 520K users on $7.7M and Simplify took $4.35M from Craft Ventures. The signature is a high lived-experience score paired with a near-floor magically-weird score, and that pairing names the company: a sympathy-buy I am explicitly trained not to make.

Anduril-Luckey (2017) is my high rung on lived-experience -- he shipped Oculus from a garage before he shipped Lattice, and the biography preceded the pitch by years. Tsenta's founders pass the autobiographical-contact test in a thinner way: 1,500 applications per person as international students on OPT pressure is real lived pain, not a McKinsey-deck pain. That places Tsenta above the consensus-YC-AI-agent rung I described in my pass example, where ex-FAANG PMs discovered the problem in a memo. Where Tsenta diverges from the Anduril rung is depth of prior shipping -- Agnay's prior artifact is a cafeteria app with 100+ weekly actives, which is a dorm-room artifact, not a decade in Keyhole/Google Earth like Hanke before Niantic. The lived pain is genuine; the prior trajectory that would let me underwrite the next decade is not.

HQ Trivia is the failure mode I keep replaying on the weird-and-non-consensus axis -- Yusupov and Kroll had a tactile viral product but the round was already obvious and the co-founder governance was unresolved. Tsenta's category is the inverse of "magically weird": Jobright at 520K users, LazyApply selling lifetime fees, JobCopilot free forever, Sonara, Simplify, Rezi, FastApply. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus, and this consensus already named itself. No credible investor has passed on Tsenta on aesthetic or moral grounds -- they have passed because Jared Friedman's partners at YC have funded this exact wedge before and the auto-apply lane is a feature, not a company. Nothing in the Tsenta dossier articulates a misread of the category the way Chase Lochmiller could articulate why oil-and-gas operators would let him on the well pad.

On single-CEO authority, the Tsenta dossier is silent -- no named CEO, no equity split, no tiebreaker protocol between Agnay Srivastava and Pulkit Gupta. (Mechanism analysis.) Silence on governance is the GameCrush trigger; it reads not as "they're equal partners" but as "they have not had the conversation yet," and the conversation that gets postponed at Rose-Hulman gets postponed at Series A, by which point the cap table makes it surgical. Eighteen months in, the failure mode is not dramatic feud -- it's that one founder ends up doing 80% of the ATS-integration grind while the other does growth, and the equity split that nobody negotiated becomes the wedge. The absence of a named CEO is itself the signal.

Tsenta is pure software wrapping LLM form-understanding -- no hardware, no regulatory moat, no capital intensity. (Counterfactual on the unsexy-regulated dimension.) For this score to flip a tier, Tsenta would have to be operating in a domain the ATS vendors actively cannot enter -- e.g., union-hall placement, licensed-trade credential verification, or visa-constrained applicant workflows where the founders' international-student status is itself the moat. The OPT/H-1B angle is the only thread in the dossier that could have produced that moat, and Tsenta has chosen instead to compete on $19-$99/mo SaaS pricing against a freemium incumbent claiming 520K users. The compounding asset is "ATS coverage breadth," which Jobright's $7.7M check can replicate.

Flock Safety is my single anchor on physical verifiability -- I read the Wi-Fi name at the Four Seasons before I read the deck. Tsenta passes a thin version of that test: I can install the Chrome extension today, watch it autofill a Workday form, and feel whether the artifact works. That is more than a developer-tools abstraction can offer. But 353 installs and 2 ratings on the Chrome Web Store as of June 2026 means I would be one of the first humans observing it in the wild, and the testimonial-hires at Goldman/JPMorgan/Netflix on tsenta.com are unverifiable founder testimony, not artifacts I can photograph. Tactile, but at a volume where my hand is the test population.

My 36-month forecast on Tsenta: dead or acqui-hired by Jobright or Simplify for under $10M by mid-2029, with Agnay and Pulkit landing FAANG seats on resolved visas. The mechanism is straightforward -- the auto-apply category compresses into one or two winners with distribution moats (LinkedIn-integrated or aggregator-pipeline), the $99/mo Power tier gets undercut by JobCopilot's free tier, and ATS vendors begin rate-limiting submissions in response to the application-spam backlash CBS News already documented in 2025. The Tsenta wedge -- 19 ATS platforms, iMessage, MCP -- is feature surface area that Jobright's $7.7M war chest replicates in a quarter.

The strongest case against me is HandUp -- I wrote that check because the founder's homelessness origin mirrored my own, knowing the commercial ceiling was modest. The bull on Tsenta says: international students applying to 1,500 jobs each is a lived-hardship origin in the same shape, and you should override the consensus-category screen the way you overrode the capital-defensibility screen for HandUp. The reason it doesn't carry: HandUp's override was mission alignment in a category nobody else would fund, not founder-market fit on a SKU where Jobright has 520K users and Craft Ventures already wrote the check. Tsenta's autobiographical pain is real but the moat is identical to five funded competitors, and personal-mission override does not extend to commodity wrappers. The condition that flips Tsenta to an invest: Jobright shuts down or pivots away, OR Agnay/Pulkit produce a public artifact showing they shipped a comparable agent platform to scale at Rose-Hulman before YC. Pass.
