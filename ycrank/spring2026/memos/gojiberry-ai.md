## The Idea

- **Problem:** B2B teams cobble together SDR hires, lead lists, enrichment tools and sequencers to chase cold prospects, with low reply rates as the status quo (YC company description, 2026).
- **Approach:** Single AI agent scans LinkedIn for intent signals (job changes, competitor interactions, group joins, funding announcements), enriches via 15+ data providers, then runs personalized LinkedIn/email outreach end-to-end (SyncGTM review, 2026; Prospeo pricing, 2026).
- **Differentiation:** Vs. Apollo.io (database-led prospecting) and Clay (workflow builder requiring an outreach tool like Smartlead/Instantly), Gojiberry bundles intent detection + outreach in one platform; vs. Artisan (deep-research personalization at low volume), Gojiberry emphasizes real-time LinkedIn buying signals (Gojiberry blog comparisons, 2026; Salesforge review, 2026).
- **Business Model:** Pro plan $99/month (2 LinkedIn senders, unlimited campaigns, CRM/API integrations) plus Custom enterprise tier with pricing on request (Prospeo pricing review, 2026; app.gojiberry.ai/pricing).
- **TAM/SAM:** AI SDR market valued at $5.81B in 2026, projected $17.58B by 2030 at 31.9% CAGR (Research and Markets / Business Research Company, 2026); no public SAM data found for the LinkedIn-intent sub-segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** Founder-led growth via LinkedIn posts ("18 demos in 5 days" post, etc.) plus Product Hunt launch and content/SEO blog comparing to Clay, Apollo, Artisan, Instantly (Romàn Czerny LinkedIn, 2025; blog.gojiberry.ai, 2026).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Cross-customer learning loop — system reportedly learns "which buyers convert, which signals matter, which messages get replies" across similar companies (YC company description, 2026); no third-party validation found.
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Proprietary intent-signal dataset across LinkedIn activity could compound if customer base scales; unproven because incumbents (Clay, Apollo) sit on far larger first-party data.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: No structural barrier identified — Apollo, Clay and others have data and channel advantages and have shipped agentic outbound features (Clay $100M ARR / $3.1B valuation Series C, TechCrunch Aug 2025).
- **Commoditization risk:** LinkedIn-signal scraping + LLM-written outreach is replicable by Clay templates, Apollo workflows, Artisan, Outreach, and dozens of "AI SDR" entrants (Salesforge review, 2026; getfuzzy.ai alternatives, 2026).

## Key Risks

- **Commoditization by funded incumbents:** Clay closed $100M at $3.1B valuation with $100M ARR in Aug 2025 (TechCrunch, 2025) and competes in the same enrichment + outbound workflow space; Gojiberry's $99/mo SMB pricing tier (Prospeo, 2026) leaves limited room to outspend on data partnerships.
- **LinkedIn platform dependency:** Core intent signals and outreach run through LinkedIn (SyncGTM review, 2026); LinkedIn has historically restricted third-party automation, creating ToS/API enforcement risk with no mitigation found.
- **Product-pivot recency:** Company pivoted to current intent-based LinkedIn outreach product after a failed prior AI product, hitting $33K revenue in 4 months post-pivot (Superframeworks, 2025); $2.5M ARR in 10 months (YC description, 2026) is recent and not independently audited.
- **Self-reported metrics:** Headline ARR, customer count and "2–5x reply" claims come from the company's YC blurb and founder LinkedIn posts; no third-party audit, independent press coverage in tier-1 outlets, or named enterprise logos found.
- **Geographic / team distribution:** CMO based in Lisbon, founders are French nationals, while the YC listing is San Francisco (LinkedIn profiles, 2026); [Inferred]: split US sales presence vs. EU team may slow US enterprise motion.
