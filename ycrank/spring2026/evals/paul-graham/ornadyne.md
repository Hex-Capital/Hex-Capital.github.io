# Ornadyne -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Geourg Kivijian wrote his Cal Poly MS thesis on a "novel bio-mimetic drive mechanism that is cable-driven and can produce variable amplitude and frequency flapping stroke" -- and then started Ornadyne to commercialize that exact mechanism. That single fact tells me what kind of company Ornadyne is: a hard-tech bet where the moat is graduate-level technical immersion, paired with the weakest dimension in my framework -- there isn't a single Airbnb-cereal-box story in the dossier, and Ornadyne is two people trying to build a hardware product against state-backed Chinese flapping-wing programs already demonstrating 4+ hour endurance.

The dimension where Ornadyne ranks highest is **Idea That Repels Before It Attracts**, and the relevant ladder runs from Stripe at the top -- payments compliance was a schlep that repelled every developer until the Collisons walked into it -- through Replit in the middle, where browser-based IDEs drew real "that won't work" reactions in 2017, down to the consensus B2B workflow startups my pass example mocks. Ornadyne sits between Stripe and Replit. Flapping-wing flight has a decades-long graveyard; the company's own description concedes that ornithopters "have lacked the performance, endurance, and control required for real-world deployment," and the 2025 Sage Journals review surfaces open challenges in endurance, payload, and control. That is the right kind of repulsion -- the technical kind, where serious people roll their eyes. The reason Ornadyne doesn't reach the Stripe rung is that "robot birds for surveillance" sounds *cool* to most listeners even if it sounds *impossible* to aerodynamicists. The schlep is real but the aesthetic is seductive, and seductive ideas attract the wrong kind of competition.

The dimension where Ornadyne ranks lowest is **Founder Resourcefulness Under Constraint**, where the ladder goes from Airbnb -- the cereal boxes proved they could sell anything -- through Stripe, where the Collisons ground through bank integrations one phone call at a time, down to the McKinsey-deck founders from my pass example who "identified an opportunity" and waited for term sheets. Ornadyne sits between Stripe and the McKinsey rung, but closer to the middle than I'd like. Geourg Kivijian's multi-year thesis grind on variable-amplitude flapping plus an AIAA Region VI 1st-place award is *technical* resourcefulness -- not nothing -- and Armen Arakelyan's role on the USC Rocket Propulsion Lab team that set student/amateur solid-rocket-motor world records is the closest thing to an "animal quality" signal in the dossier. But there is no story of a working prototype on a customer's airfield, no pilot LOI, no SBIR award, no creative-workaround narrative. Hardware companies need to ship metal, and the YC profile lists hiring as False on a two-person team trying to displace Teledyne FLIR Black Hornet from the U.S. Army Soldier-Borne Sensor program.

Drew Houston kept forgetting his USB drive -- that's the **Organic Problem Discovery** rung I anchor against, with Stripe in the middle (the Collisons saw payment friction adjacently, not as victims of it) and the consultant-brainstorm pass example at the bottom. Ornadyne's CEO didn't personally need to surveil anything; he built a cool flapping mechanism in graduate school and is now applying it to ISR. That is technical "live in the future" but not user-pain "live in the future." Kivijian sits between Stripe and the consultant rung -- he discovered the mechanism organically; the customer he discovered through inference. The reconnaissance/ISR positioning reads as a chosen application of a pre-existing artifact, not a frustration that pulled the artifact into existence.

Bindwell anchors the **Founder Learning Rate** ladder for me -- two teenagers who pivoted from selling AI tools to owning the molecules on my own advice, in months. Sam Altman at Loopt sits there too; Auctomatic in the middle. Ornadyne provides no weekly metrics, no iteration cadence, no pivot evidence -- the dossier is a static technical résumé. Default territory, slightly below the midpoint because hardware iteration is structurally slower and Kivijian has been on this mechanism since graduate school, which can be read as either deep mastery or as the obstinacy I warned about in "The Right Kind of Stubborn." For **Earnest Builder Identity**, Watsi sits at the top, generic technical YC founders in the middle, status-chasers at the bottom; Ornadyne sits in the upper-middle. Kivijian devoted a graduate degree to this drive mechanism before there was a company, and Arakelyan came from Starship and a record-setting student rocket lab -- these are people who build for the love of the artifact. No buzzwords in the company description. That registers.

Thirty-six months out: Ornadyne raises a Series A on a working prototype plus a DoD SBIR Phase II award, generates $0-3M in pilot revenue with one of the SRR Tranche 2 primes (Skydio or Red Cat) as a sub or with a Special Operations end-user, hits the same endurance ceiling that has bounded flapping-wing aircraft for two decades, and gets acquired by AeroVironment or Teledyne FLIR for talent and IP in the $30-80M range. The mechanism producing this outcome is the signature itself: technical-immersion-high, scrappiness-low. Companies with that pairing make beautiful prototypes that the right strategic acquirer values, and rarely become standalone billion-dollar businesses.

The strongest case against my pass leans on Boom Supersonic -- the precedent where I overrode capital efficiency because the founder articulated frightening ambition with missionary conviction. Ornadyne could be Boom: hard-tech, pre-revenue, two technical founders, a domain incumbents have abandoned. The bull case says I'm penalizing exactly the kind of "important for America" hardware bet I made my largest personal check on. But Boom required Blake Scholl articulating civilizational stakes; Ornadyne's positioning is utilitarian "stealth surveillance" -- a defense product in a crowded LA defense-tech cluster, not a transcendent ambition. Closer to ClassDojo's schlep-blindness exception except the schlep here (defense procurement, ITAR, flapping-wing aerodynamics) is one every LA hard-tech founder is currently trying to walk into. The verdict flips if Ornadyne ships a flight demonstration exceeding 4 hours endurance with a sub-50dB acoustic signature at 50 feet, on video, before Demo Day -- that one artifact would prove the mechanism works at deployable performance and would push this from Neutral into Invest.
