# OpenRelay -- Elad Gil Evaluation

OpenRelay is a credentialed infrastructure team -- Prashant Patel from Amazon Bedrock, Jaden Wang from TensorDock/Voltage Park -- entering a mid-cycle inference-cloud market at position N+5 with no revenue disclosed and a "90% cheaper than AWS/Azure/GCP" claim as the wedge. The dimensional signature: real shippers, wrong entry point. When founders this real show up in a category where **RunPod** already has $240M ARR at a $1B valuation (TechCrunch, Jan 2026), **Together** is at ~$1B ARR, and **Fireworks** at ~$800M, the question stops being "can they build" and becomes "did they arrive on time." OpenRelay did not.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for market re-opening, and it's exactly the analogy that fails OpenRelay. Anduril worked because Ukraine created a specific 24-month window where Pentagon software inadequacy was newly legible -- a dead market re-opened by a named catalyst. OpenRelay's dossier gestures at the TensorDock ? Voltage Park acquisition (BusinessWire, Mar 2025) and incumbent ARR crossing $500M-$1B as "why now" signal, but that isn't a re-opening -- that's a mid-cycle crowding event. When *inference* is the market and the top five players already collectively clear $3B+ ARR, OpenRelay is skating to where the puck was in 2023, not where it's going. This is the inverse of the **Stripe** (2010) / **Coinbase** (2013) / Anduril (2022) template -- the market is neither dead nor recently re-opened, it's mature and crowded.

The **Lyft** Series C pass mechanism was: #2 in a winner-take-all market captures a fraction of #1's economics regardless of execution quality. OpenRelay's index-position problem rhymes but is worse -- they're not even #2. **RunPod** owns/leases fleet, **Together** and **Fireworks** own the token-inference layer, **Baseten** owns managed serving, **Parasail** raised $32M in April 2026 for the same distributed-supercloud thesis. OpenRelay pitches "The CDN of inference" (openrelay.inc/contact), which is the language of an index position, but a CDN moat comes from PoP density and enterprise contracts, and OpenRelay has neither disclosed provider count nor a single named customer. This is a share-fight framed as an index bet, and I've seen that movie -- it's the Harvey-clone pattern from *AI Market Clarity* applied to inference infrastructure.

Revenue velocity is the dimension I use to distinguish "just working" from "false signal" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025), and OpenRelay's dossier is silent -- no revenue, no user counts, no Product Hunt, no Discord size, no press. In AI infrastructure in 2026, silence is signal. Pre-seed defaults would give partial credit for absent data, but OpenRelay's competitors publish ARR curves because the curves are the moat -- RunPod's $122M raise at $1B was priced off the $240M ARR trajectory. OpenRelay ships a $5-deposit-for-$10-credit signup incentive (openrelay.inc), which is the marketing motion of a company optimizing for signups, not enterprise inference contracts.

Founder-shipping is where OpenRelay actually earns points, and it's the one dimension I won't discount. Prashant Patel was a founding member of Amazon Bedrock "managing inference infrastructure for models up to trillions of parameters" (YC page); Jaden Wang was Lead Engineer at TensorDock post-acquisition and previously founded Heaviside Compute. This is not a promoter pairing -- both have shipped the exact primitives OpenRelay needs. Counterfactually, for OpenRelay to flip a tier on this dimension, I'd need to see the **Perplexity** (2022) signature -- artifacts built between conversations, not just credentials. The dossier gives me credentials, which is a MID-rung pattern (**Brex**-style operational discipline) rather than a Perplexity-style shipping cadence.

The single-miracle test is where OpenRelay compounds unfavorably. Miracle one: recruit sustained supply-side liquidity from independent GPU operators through the "Dedicated provider program" -- unproven at team size 2. Miracle two: build routing and failover software delivering "millisecond automatic failover" at aggregator quality. Miracle three: sustain a "90% cheaper" price gap that hyperscalers and RunPod's owned fleet can compress. Miracle four: earn enterprise-grade SLA trust despite the dossier's own admission that non-dedicated capacity ships without one. That's a two-sided marketplace layered on a routing plane layered on a price-compression bet -- exactly the multi-miracle compounding the NFX framework says to pass on.

**Forecast (36 months):** OpenRelay is acqui-hired by RunPod, CoreWeave, or their former employer Voltage Park for under $30M in an aqui-hire structured around Patel's Bedrock experience, or pivots to a specialized inference-router SDK for a single vertical (voice, video-gen, or agents) after failing to build two-sided marketplace liquidity against RunPod's owned fleet economics. The mechanism: incumbents at $240M-$1B ARR amortize capex faster than an aggregator can recruit marginal-cost supply, and OpenRelay's pricing claim collapses inside 18 months.

The strongest bull case: Patel's Amazon Bedrock DNA is exactly the routing-layer expertise that could route around incumbents, and Wang's TensorDock lineage means they've already run the marketplace playbook once -- if OpenRelay hits 20%+ MoM on GPU-hours-billed inside YC Summer 2026, this becomes the RunPod arc one cycle later, and RunPod took years plus $122M to reach $240M ARR. That case stings because it correctly identifies that founder-market fit here is real. It loses to the dossier: RunPod entered when the field was empty; OpenRelay enters against RunPod, Together, Fireworks, Baseten, and a freshly-funded Parasail with no capital disclosed, no fleet, and no named enterprise logo. **Verdict flips** on a single verifiable condition: OpenRelay discloses a signed inference contract with a Fortune 500 or a top-10 AI-native (Anthropic, OpenAI-tier customer, Cursor, Perplexity, Harvey) before Demo Day, or shows three consecutive months of 20%+ MoM GPU-hours-billed compounding. Absent that, this is the Lyft-Series-C pattern applied to inference -- a worse-positioned version of the obvious bet.
