# RentAHuman -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Alexander Liteplo took a trip to Japan, saw a human-rental storefront, came home, and built a marketplace where Claude can hire a meatworker for $50-$175/hr with stablecoin escrow ? and RentAHuman is the rare dossier where the product is overwhelmingly more touchable than the founder origin is lived. That inversion is the signature. The Google Play app is live, the MCP server plugs straight into the agent runtime I actually use, and a half-million humans signed up in two weeks; the founder, meanwhile, surfaced the idea as a tourist, not as someone who has ever stood on the supply side of a gig economy. RentAHuman is HQ Trivia's profile rotated ninety degrees ? virality I can feel, origin I cannot.

Flock Safety is the rung RentAHuman keeps reaching for on physical verifiability ? Garrett Langley's company was real to me the moment I read the Wi-Fi network name at the Four Seasons because the artifact existed in the room. RentAHuman clears the same bar a different way: I can install the app from play.google.com today, list myself as a meatworker, or point Claude at rentahuman.ai/mcp and watch it dispatch a human to sign a document. The 700k+ signups and ~4M site visits in February 2026 are not abstract API traffic; they are humans who picked up a phone. Where RentAHuman diverges from Flock is that Flock's cameras kept producing evidence after install ? RentAHuman's $20k MRR against 500k signups is roughly four cents of revenue per signup, which tells me people are *watching* the artifact more than *using* it, and tactile-curiosity is not the same as tactile-engagement.

The pass-example FAANG-PM-discovers-the-problem-in-a-memo is the rung I most fear with RentAHuman, and Liteplo's "Japan trip observing human-rental services" is exactly that pattern in a passport stamp. His real autobiography is LayerZero Labs and UMA Protocol ? which is genuine lived expertise in *stablecoin escrow rails*, not in gig labor, not in the meatworker side of the marketplace, not in dispatch operations, not in the regulatory thicket a human-task platform inherits. Patricia Tani's background at Lemon AI (sunsetted) and Emily Carr art school does not close the gap on the supply side either. Neither founder has ever been a TaskRabbit. The Anduril-Luckey test ? did you ship a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain before this company ? returns null for the marketplace itself; it returns *yes* for the payment layer, which is a tell about where this company actually lives.

WIRED, Forbes, Business Insider, Futurism, Gizmodo, Inc., The Information, Built In, TechRadar, spiked, 36Kr ? 30+ publications in February 2026, plus a Product Hunt feature, plus a YC partner attached, plus a named direct competitor in MeatLayer pitching identical positioning. In YC-batch terms RentAHuman is the consensus darling of Spring 2026, and the thesis is to find them *before* it becomes consensus. The aesthetic transgression rescues some of this ? spiked literally framed RentAHuman as descending into AI dystopia, which is the kind of moral-aesthetic objection that protected my Mindbloom and Vigil Labs entries ? but the dystopia framing is in opinion columns, not in a sophisticated investor's quiet pass. The press wave inverts the signal I want.

Liteplo holds the "Founder" title while Tani holds "Co-Founder," and the third team member is unidentified in any public coverage, which is the thinnest possible governance disclosure ? better than 50/50 but worse than a documented operating agreement. The HQ Trivia / Yusupov-Kroll lesson is what I am pattern-matching against: two UBC classmates with no public tiebreaker, a viral product, and a press cycle moving faster than the cap table. The mechanism that broke HQ Trivia by 2020 was that the co-founder feud arrived right when the growth curve flattened ? RentAHuman's growth curve will flatten the moment MeatLayer ships, and that is exactly when governance ambiguity becomes existential.

RentAHuman is a software marketplace, not the SpaceX/Crusoe/Anduril frontier-hardware sleeve, and the regulatory exposure that *could* qualify it ? AI dispatching humans into physical environments with stablecoin payouts ? is conspicuously unengaged by the team: no public T&Cs, no insurance scheme, no published safety protocol. The 36Kr article asking whether RentAHuman is a crypto scam because of the unrelated "RENT" token, with no clarifying response from the company, tells me Liteplo has not yet started playing defense on the regulated-perimeter problems his own product creates. The unsexy-regulated lane only counts when the founder is *running toward* the regulation; RentAHuman is running away from it.

Thirty-six months out my forecast on RentAHuman is that the marketplace either dies after a publicized worker-safety or fraud incident plus MeatLayer commoditization on the buy side, or it pivots ? led by Liteplo's actual lived expertise ? into being an agent-to-human stablecoin payment rail acquired for $20-$80M by Coinbase, Stripe, or a wallet provider; I am assigning very low probability to RentAHuman crossing $10M ARR as a standalone marketplace by May 2029. The mechanism is that supply liquidity built on curiosity signups decays without repeat-hire density, and the team's autobiographical center of gravity is the payment layer they will retreat to.

The strongest objection is the HQ Trivia Known Exception itself: explosive product-led traction in a tactile consumer artifact can temporarily override the co-founder governance heuristic, and 500k signups in two weeks is precisely that profile. If RentAHuman's reported numbers reflect real human meatworkers ready to take real AI-dispatched gigs, the network-effect compound on the supply side could outrun MeatLayer regardless of founder origin. What dismantles this case is the four-cents-per-signup MRR ? HQ Trivia was viral because I could open it at 9pm and *feel* the pull of a million simultaneous players; RentAHuman's signup-to-dollar ratio says the artifact is being *previewed*, not *transacted*. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is RentAHuman reporting $500k+ MRR with documented repeat-hire rates above 30% by Q4 2026, sourced from AI-agent buyers rather than human curiosity ? at that point the HQ Trivia exception applies and I would chase the round.
