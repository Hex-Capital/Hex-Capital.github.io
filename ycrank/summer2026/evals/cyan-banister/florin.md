# Florin -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Florin is a regulated-category swing without the founder biography to break through the gravity well -- three engineers from Google's TPU team, AWS/Twitch, and Orchard robotics, building a "we own the ledger" neobank in the exact month Mercury closed $200M at $5.2B explicitly to chase a bank charter (CNBC, May 2026). The capital-intensity sleeve activates -- this is the lane that produced Vigil Labs and that I've defended elsewhere -- but Florin reads to me like Shaurya Aggarwal and Aydin Sorensen discovered fragmented startup banking the way the consensus YC AI-agent founders discover workflow pain: by noticing it, not by living inside a treasurer's seat. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Florin seems to be playing is "Mercury but better infrastructure," which is precisely the game.

Vigil Labs (August 2025) is the cleanest portfolio analogue on the dimension where Florin scores highest -- regulated finance, unsexy to most of the cohort, where the moat compounds in the months it takes anyone else to assemble the licensing posture. The shared mechanism is that money-transmitter scaffolding and sponsor-bank exclusivity, once won, become a structural toll on every dollar moved. Where Florin diverges from Vigil is the absence of any disclosure that the toll booth is being built: the dossier surfaces no charter status, no named sponsor bank, no money-transmitter license filings, and a team size of three at the very front of a multi-year regulated build. Mercury just paid $200M to escape its own BaaS dependency (CNBC, May 2026); Florin claims to have started there, but the dossier shows the claim, not the receipts.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on in my Pass Example failed because the round was priced as if the outcome were certain and the founders had discovered the problem in a memo -- and Florin's lowest-dimension trace runs the same wiring. Florin is entering a category where Mercury is $650M ARR (Fortune, November 2025), Brex just sold to Capital One for $5.15B (April 2026), Rho has raised $235M, and Meow already owns the T-bills niche. Aggarwal and Sorensen are infrastructure-builders, not infrastructure-livers; neither ran a startup's treasury, neither was a CFO who watched wires fail at 5pm Friday, neither shipped fintech to scale before. The "magically weird" filter doesn't fire -- this is the most-consensus B2B fintech wedge in the Summer 2026 batch.

Mechanism analysis on the CEO governance dimension: Florin lists Aggarwal as "Founder & CEO," Pant as "Co-Founder & CTO," and Sorensen as just "Founder" with no title. Over 18 months in a regulated build, an untitled third founder doing customer-facing work becomes either the de facto president or the equity dispute -- the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll fault line opens along exactly this kind of ambiguity, and the GameCrush (~2011) lesson is that activist investors exploit unresolved titles when the regulated runway forces a down round. The CEO is named, which is more than a 50/50 split, but a third unlabeled founder in a charter-dependent business is a small unresolved seam in the keel.

Counterfactual on physical-world verifiability: Florin is invite-only membership for venture-backed companies (florin.inc), which means I cannot install it, photograph it, or hand it to a founder friend in the next 48 hours. For this score to flip up a tier, Florin would need to be observable in the wild the way Flock Safety's Wi-Fi name was -- a portfolio CEO I trust would have to say "we moved $4M to Florin last quarter and the wire cleared in eleven minutes." The dossier surfaces no such artifact, no Product Hunt presence, no press, no follower counts, no signed-LOI counterparty. Invite-only is a posture; physical verifiability is a check that the posture clears.

Single-anchor parallel on founder-market fit: Sorensen scaling TerraFirma revenue from $500K to $30M in construction robotics is the most interesting single fact in the Florin dossier -- it's the kind of resume line that pattern-matches to Chase Lochmiller at Crusoe (~2018), an outsider operator who could walk onto a well pad. The disanalogy is that Lochmiller was *of* North Dakota oil-and-gas through quant-energy adjacency before Crusoe; Sorensen is *of* construction robotics and Amazon, not of the back-office of a venture-backed startup's finance stack. The skill is transferable; the social license inside the problem domain is not, and Florin's wedge is sold on living inside that domain.

My 36-month forecast: by June 2029 Florin is either quietly acquihired by Rho or a regional sponsor bank for under $15M, or dead, with ARR never crossing $2M -- the mechanism is that Mercury's $200M war chest and Capital One's Brex distribution close the window on a three-engineer team's owned-ledger claim before Florin secures the charter that would justify the architectural bet. The forecast follows directly from the signature: a regulated-category swing without lived founder authority gets out-spent by the incumbent that just bought itself the same architecture.

The bull case that stings: Aggarwal worked on Google's TPU team and shipped at-rest/in-transit encryption to 100,000+ hosts; Sorensen ran tech leadership at AWS and Twitch and then scaled a hardware-software company 60x. That is not a "Stanford CS + McKinsey" deck -- that is two operators who have actually built planet-scale infrastructure, and the Stripe-Collison precedent says engineering-first fintech founders without prior banking experience can absolutely win. Affirm's Max Levchin is the portfolio rhyme. What dismantles the bull case is that Mercury's $5.2B round (CNBC, May 2026) was explicitly raised to *acquire* a bank charter and rebuild the rails Florin claims as its differentiator -- so the contrarian secret is no longer secret, and a three-person team without disclosed regulatory progress is racing a $650M ARR incumbent that started running the same race two months before Florin announced. The condition that flips my verdict: Florin publicly secures either a national bank charter, a named exclusive sponsor-bank partnership, or a signed treasury LOI from a recognizable Long Journey or Founders Fund portfolio company within 12 months. Absent that, I pass and keep watching Aggarwal -- because the founder I want to fund next from this team is the one who lives through this loss and comes back having actually run a CFO seat.
