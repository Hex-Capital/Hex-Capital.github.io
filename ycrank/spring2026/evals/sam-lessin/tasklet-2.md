# Tasklet -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Andrew Lee killed his own email client to build Tasklet, and that is the most interesting fact in this dossier -- a missionary technical founder running the canonical 2026 horizontal-AI-agent consensus play at $175M post on a $20M Series A led by USV and Lightspeed with Jeff Dean and both Collisons in the syndicate. The signature of Tasklet in my book is "highest founder conviction I've seen this batch, sitting inside the lowest-conviction category sleeve I underwrite, at a price that is almost a parody of my pass example." Everything downstream of that pairing falls into place.

Drop.io is the conviction rung I actually trust on this axis -- I bootstrapped a file-sharing primitive against free incumbents because I refused to be talked out of the thesis, and the only reason it sold to Facebook in 2010 was the missionary temperament across the non-linear arc. Andrew Lee at Tasklet has the rare version of that profile: he publicly killed the Shortwave email client product on the Cognitive Revolution podcast under the thesis "always bet on the models," carved the agent runtime out, and bet 12 years of accumulated Firebase-and-Shortwave operating capital on a horizontal agent stack. That is not a sanitized YC deck -- that is a founder who will not be talked out of his non-obvious belief, and the $5M ARR with >1,200% growth since January is the early evidence the market is paying him to be right. Where Tasklet diverges from Drop.io is that the conviction is pointed at an asset class I structurally don't believe is venture-shaped at seed prices, not at a misunderstood primitive nobody else wanted.

The pass example I wrote -- "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $30M post, four tier-1 VCs already in" -- is the failure mechanism, and Tasklet is that pass example with two extra zeroes and a better founder. The mechanism is simple: when USV, Lightspeed, Jeff Dean, Patrick Collison, and John Collison are already on the cap table at $175M, the next buyer of this paper is paying for terminal value in a category where Lindy ($53.6M raised), Gumloop ($20M raised), Relay.app ($8.2M), Zapier Agents, n8n, Make, plus native OpenAI and Anthropic agent runtimes are all building the same product. Andrew Lee himself frames Tasklet as a race to be "a horizontal platform" -- that is a verbatim consensus framing, and "AI is just more internet" applies the moment the category sleeve becomes commodity LLM-orchestration. There is no asset-class mispricing to exploit at Tasklet's round; the round IS the mispricing, in the wrong direction.

The capital structure dimension compounds badly over the next 18 months on the mechanism: Tasklet has 9 open roles ranging up to $410K plus 1.2% equity, enterprise readiness ("logging, security controls, cost management") is on the roadmap not GA, and the implied path from $5M ARR to a Series-B-justifying $30M+ ARR will burn against Lindy and Gumloop's parallel hiring sprints. That is a standard SaaS cap-table treadmill -- five more rounds to default-alive, every one priced off the next ARR print, every print contested by a better-funded peer. Tasklet's $175M post is the entry point to that treadmill, not an exit from it.

The counterfactual on category sleeve is the cleanest tier-flip test I can write. For Tasklet to move out of my "AI is just more internet" bucket and into the crypto-style "fundamentally disruptive" sleeve, the underlying primitive Tasklet ships -- triggered cloud agents with computer-use against real browsers -- would have to be something OpenAI and Anthropic cannot natively replicate in their consumer chat surfaces within 18 months. Andrew Lee's own argument on the podcast -- that frontier model UIs will generate themselves on the fly -- is the exact argument against Tasklet's defensibility. The dossier offers no escape hatch here; the founder has publicly stated the thesis that commoditizes his own platform.

Marina Mogilko is the single-anchor parallel on founder-as-brand, and Tasklet fails the comparison usefully. Mogilko brought millions of language-learning subscribers and twenty years of compounding earning power -- the asset was the person, and the instrument followed. Andrew Lee brings 7,376 X followers at @startupandrew, a respected developer brand from Firebase, and the inherited Shortwave user base that "asked for the agent without the email client." That is a credible operator footprint, not a creator distribution moat -- it accelerates GTM marginally but does not let Tasklet bypass the paid-acquisition treadmill that Lindy and Gumloop will fight on. The Slow Creator Fund thesis does not load on Tasklet; this is a B2B SaaS with a technical-founder twitter presence, which is the modal YC profile, not a step-function.

Tasklet's 36-month forecast: acquired by Salesforce, Microsoft, or Google for $300M-$600M after stalling somewhere between $25M and $45M ARR as Zapier Agents, native OpenAI agent products, and one of Lindy/Gumloop consolidate the horizontal mid-market -- a fine outcome for Andrew Lee and a poor outcome for anyone buying Tasklet paper at the $175M post. The mechanism is the one Andrew Lee himself articulated: models eat the orchestration layer, the horizontal-platform race collapses to two or three survivors, and Tasklet's engineering lead from the Shortwave-era runtime gives it credible acquihire optionality but not a fund-returner trajectory from this entry price.

The Robinhood exception is the strongest bull case against my pass, and I want to take it seriously. The argument runs: Andrew Lee is exactly the technical-founder profile Slow has ridden through consensus markups before, $5M ARR with 1,200% growth in five months is genuine product-market fit not vapor, and "factory model" winners absorb subsequent consensus rounds when the earliest-stage entry was right. The dismantle is that the Robinhood exception explicitly governs follow-on pricing on a position Slow already owned -- Tasklet at $175M post is initial entry for me, not follow-on, and price discipline applies on entry without exception. The verifiable flip condition: if Tasklet prints $50M ARR by Q4 2026 with net-dollar-retention above 110% and the next round prices flat or down at the same $175M post, I write the follow-on -- the operator has earned the consensus premium retroactively, and the asset-class mispricing has resolved in the direction of "actually venture-shaped." Until that print lands, I'm out before the meeting starts.
