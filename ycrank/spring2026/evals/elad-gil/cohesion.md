# Cohesion -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Cohesion is a clean single-miracle architecture pointed at a category where consensus already declared winners -- and that pairing, not the customer logo count, is the analytical signature. Cohesion's miracle is one I'd happily underwrite (LLM agents extracting signal from earnings + podcasts + X for buy-side analysts), but AlphaSense at $500M ARR (Oct 2025) and Rogo's $160M Series D (April 2026) tell me the catalyst window for "AI on finance research" opened in 2023 and has already attracted the capital that closes it. The dossier names 10+ funds at $10Bn+ AUM as customers -- real, but exactly the kind of pilot-tourism signal I've learned to distinguish from "just working" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025).

**Anduril** (2022) is the high rung on catalyst because Ukraine was a specific, datable 24-month re-opening that peers misread. Cohesion's implied catalyst is "LLM agent reliability crossed a usable threshold in 2024-2025" -- which is the *same* catalyst Harvey, Decagon, Abridge, Rogo, and AlphaSense's GenAI pivot all cited. That's not a re-opening Cohesion uniquely owns; it's the ambient backdrop. The dossier itself flags that AlphaSense GenAI query usage grew 33% QoQ -- meaning the incumbent already metabolized the catalyst. When the catalyst has already been priced into a $4B-valued incumbent doing $500M ARR, the window is not opening for Cohesion; it's the window AlphaSense is now defending.

The pattern I associate with **Lyft** Series C -- backing a #2 in a structure where the #1 captures most of the economics -- is the failure mechanism worth tracing here. Cohesion is not literally #2 because the buy-side equity-research workflow may be oligopolistic rather than winner-take-all, but the dossier reads like a fourth or fifth entrant: AlphaSense ($1B+ raised), Hebbia ($161M raised), Rogo ($300M+ raised), Brightwave ($21M raised), all building agentic finance products with capital and distribution Cohesion lacks. My *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy puts legal AI in the "winners declared" bucket because Harvey and CaseText took the oxygen; buy-side equity research with AlphaSense at $500M ARR looks structurally identical. The "podcasts and X/Twitter" wedge is real but it's a feature AlphaSense can ship in two quarters, not a company.

Cohesion's revenue-velocity placement compounds the wrong way over 18 months. The dossier discloses customer count (10+ funds, $10Bn+ AUM combined) but not a single revenue figure, no MoM growth rate, no named anchor logo. In the AI cycle, silence on the curve is itself the signal -- if Cohesion were running the Harvey arc, Krapcho would be saying so. Eighteen months from now, either Cohesion has disclosed a curve that lets a Series A fund underwrite it, or the existing 10+ funds churn as AlphaSense/Rogo ship the podcast-monitoring feature and the procurement department consolidates the seat back to the incumbent.

If Cohesion scored a tier higher on founder shipping rhythm, I'd reconsider -- but the counterfactual doesn't hold. Krapcho is a 5-year Long Path Partners analyst (customer-side domain, not builder), McBrien is an Amazon/AWS engineer with no named shipped product, and Munns is described only as having "built AI for investors at T. Rowe Price" with no artifact named. No GitHub repos found for any of the three. This is the **Perplexity** (2022) anti-pattern: Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing we'd discussed built. Cohesion's dossier names titles, not shipped artifacts. For the score to flip to "Invest," I'd need to see Krapcho ship two product iterations between investor conversations or a named tool Munns built at T. Rowe Price that's in use.

The single-miracle test is where Cohesion actually earns marks -- the **Decagon** (2023) parallel holds cleanly. Decagon's miracle was LLM accuracy on customer-support tickets; everything else was GTM. Cohesion's miracle is LLM agent accuracy on financial-signal extraction from semi-structured sources; same architectural shape, no new physics, no regulatory dependency, no capital-intensive infrastructure. This is why the score isn't lower -- Cohesion is not a science project, and Munns' T. Rowe Price background suggests the team understands what "investing signal" actually means to a portfolio manager versus a generic summarizer.

My 36-month forecast for Cohesion: acquired by AlphaSense or Hebbia for $30-80M as a feature-and-team tuck-in, or pivoted to a narrower buy-side workflow (compliance monitoring, IR-team-side competitive intel) where the incumbent footprint is lighter. ARR caps in the $3-10M range before the AlphaSense/Rogo feature roadmap closes the podcast-and-X differentiation. This is consistent with the signature: clean single-miracle execution + closed catalyst window = good engineering team that becomes someone's product line.

The bull case that stings: Munns and Krapcho have the founder-graph access pattern I privately reward -- Krapcho is *from* the customer (5 years at Long Path Partners, the exact buyer persona), and Munns shipped AI inside T. Rowe Price, meaning Cohesion has the buy-side trust-and-compliance posture AlphaSense built over a decade. The 10+ funds at $10Bn+ AUM in pre-seed is not nothing if those are anchor design partners rather than free pilots. If AlphaSense's licensed-content model is legally constrained from indexing unlicensed podcasts and X content the way Cohesion can, the wedge is more durable than I'm crediting -- and AlphaSense's $4B valuation makes it a slow mover, not a fast one. But the dossier doesn't give me revenue, doesn't name a top-10 fund, and gives me three founders with no shipped artifacts. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Cohesion discloses a signed contract with a top-10 buy-side fund (Citadel, Point72, Millennium, Bridgewater, Two Sigma) at $250K+ ACV, plus three months of >30% MoM revenue compounding. Until then, this is a pass on catalyst-window-already-closed grounds, not on team or architecture.
