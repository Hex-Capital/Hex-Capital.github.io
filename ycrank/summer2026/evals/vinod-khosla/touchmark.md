# Touchmark -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Touchmark is a billing-plumbing wrapper on commodity LLM-as-judge evaluations, and its analytical signature is the inverse of every check I have written that mattered: its weakest axis is Required-Capital Profile (a TypeScript SDK that any Tier-1 generalist can fund for $3M) and its least-weak axis is a default-applied founder learning rate I cannot even verify. When the highest-scoring dimension is "absence of evidence, applying the default," the deal is already over.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the load-bearing comparison for Consequence Magnitude, because the question I asked myself before writing that $50M check was not "what fraction of NLP spend does this capture" but "what happens to human cognition if this works." Touchmark's success-state, by contrast, is that AI vendors invoice by output quality instead of tokens -- a re-pricing of an existing line item. The Touchmark dossier itself states cross-customer adjustments "average to zero, your top-line revenue is preserved" (search snippet, 2026); the company has literally written that the consequence of its success is revenue-neutral. That is not a civilizational variable -- it is an accounting re-bucketing. **OpenAI** at no-revenue had a success-state that collapsed search, knowledge work, and tutoring; Touchmark at maximum ambition leaves the AI vendor ecosystem exactly where it was, with cleaner invoices.

The pass example in my own framework -- "we use GPT-4 to summarize sales calls and integrate with HubSpot" -- is the failure mode for Technical/Capital Risk, and Touchmark sits on that rung with almost no daylight. The failure mechanism there is the same one here: LLM-as-judge evals are commodity (open-source frameworks, judge APIs from every major model provider, as the dossier itself notes); Stripe (which paid $1B for **Metronome** in December 2025 per Sacra) and Orb (Vercel, Replit, Pinecone, Perplexity per AIBilling.dev) already sit in the AI billing path and can ship an eval-scoring add-on as a feature release. Touchmark's "moat" in the dossier is explicitly "No public data found." This is the **Range Fuels** inverse -- there, the capital intensity was so large that consensus VCs refused to underwrite the science; here, the capital intensity is so small that consensus VCs are precisely the right buyers and I am the wrong one.

Mechanism analysis on Founder Defies Expert Consensus: Ilia Bolgov spent ~6 years 9 months in Product at Revolut, a usage-priced fintech, and Roman Yanushevskyi did two Citadel Securities QR internships plus an AI Engineer stint at Lovable and ICPC 2025. Over 18 months, the Touchmark founder profile compounds into "credible billing-infra operators executing the consensus 2026 thesis that AI pricing is shifting from seats to outcomes" -- which is the Bessemer AI pricing playbook thesis verbatim. That is expert continuity, not expert defiance. My TED Talk citation of the UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study is precisely about this profile: Touchmark's founders are extrapolating the pricing-shift consensus, not contradicting it.

Counterfactual on Founder Learning Rate: for Touchmark to flip from default 7/15 into the **Slash** (2024) range that justified my $100M Series C, the dossier would need a documented prior pivot with stated reasoning -- a pre-Touchmark product that died, a public thesis update from Bolgov on why per-seat billing breaks, a Yanushevskyi blog post arguing against the Bessemer outcome-pricing consensus. None of that exists in public sources. The Touchmark dossier shows two linear-execution founders on their first company, which is the pre-seed stage default and I do not penalize aggressively -- but I also cannot reward a learning rate I cannot see.

Single-anchor parallel on Required-Capital Profile: **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) needed $115M just for the Series A because the SPARC tokamak required real plant and real magnets; Touchmark's roadmap, per the dossier, is a TypeScript SDK (@touchmark/sdk) and an eval library. Touchmark will reach $1M ARR on $3-5M, which means I am roughly fifteen-to-thirty-x over-resourced for the deal. This is the dimension where the math is cleanest: Khosla Ventures is the wrong cap-table participant for Touchmark even if I liked the thesis.

Forecast: in 36 months Touchmark is either acquired by Orb or by Stripe-via-Metronome for sub-$25M as a feature tuck-in into an existing AI billing product line, or it has pivoted out of billing entirely into an AI eval tooling product competing with Braintrust and LangSmith. The mechanism is direct -- the "average to zero" revenue-neutrality copy means Touchmark cannot capture a take-rate large enough to justify standalone scale, and the incumbents already in the billing path will either build the eval layer or buy the smallest credible team building it. McKinsey predicted under 1 million U.S. cellphones by 2000; actual was 109 million. Touchmark is not that asymmetry -- it is the opposite shape, a feature priced like a company.

The strongest opposing case is that Bolgov's ~6.75 years at Revolut is real usage-billing-adjacent domain depth, that Solvimon raised $9.6M from Northzone on a hybrid usage+outcome thesis (so the category is being underwritten by serious capital), and that the 2026 AI renewal cycle is the first real window where enterprise buyers demand outcome-tied ROI per the Bessemer playbook -- meaning Touchmark could become the standard quality-scoring layer that Stripe and Orb eventually have to buy at a premium. This stings because it is the **Glean** known-exception shape: a domain-expert profile attacking a real consensus-pricing-shift window. The dossier evidence that wins for me is the explicit company copy that adjustments "average to zero" -- Touchmark has self-described its consequence as revenue-neutral, which is the Outlook Business "money takes care of itself" framing inverted; the company is telling buyers there is no consequence. The single condition that flips my verdict: a signed deployment with a named top-ten AI vendor (Anthropic, OpenAI, Vercel, Replit, Cursor, or similar) where Touchmark is metering production traffic and the vendor has publicly committed to quality-adjusted billing as a primary pricing axis. Without that, Touchmark is a feature, and I do not write checks into features.
