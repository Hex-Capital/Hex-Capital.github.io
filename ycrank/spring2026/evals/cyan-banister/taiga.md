# Taiga -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Nanda Guntupalli and Adam Wax are Penn undergrads — Class of 2028, friends since 3rd grade — whose parents both run independent medical practices, and they're selling AI-native billing back to that exact customer. That pairing makes Taiga the rare regulated-category swing with a credible autobiographical hook, but the product is an invisible workflow living behind an EHR integration — I can't ride it, scan it, or photograph it in the wild the way I could a Flock camera or a Postmates courier. Taiga's signature in my book is "Vigil-Labs-class regulatory lane, Flock-Safety-class verifiability vacuum," and that pairing is what I have to price.

Anduril sits at the top of my founder-lived-experience ladder because Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus to scale before he ever touched a defense contract — the biography put him in the room before the pitch deck did. Taiga's founders pass the *adjacency* test: they grew up watching billing eat their parents' practices, which is exactly the dinner-table origin I weight when the dossier names a specific physical moment of contact with the problem. Where Taiga diverges from the Anduril rung is operator scar tissue — Nanda and Adam watched the problem, they didn't run the back-office themselves through a denial-appeals cycle. That's autobiographical proximity, not autobiographical operation, and at age 19-20 with no prior shipped product, it lands closer to the DeepMind-Hassabis polymath-with-pedigree rung than to the Luckey-from-the-garage rung. Real, but not load-bearing on its own.

The pass-example AI-agent darling failed my verifiability test because a normal human couldn't install it, photograph it, or hand it to a friend — and Taiga has the same problem in a different costume. Taiga is a billing service routed through DrChrono, athenahealth, Tebra, and seven other EHRs; the artifact is a clean claim that I, as Cyan, will never see, touch, or feel the viral pull of. There's no Ingress to play, no scooter to ride, no Wi-Fi name to read at a Four Seasons café. The single named customer, Cheskis Family Therapy, is a reference, not a sensory experience I can have on a Tuesday afternoon. The verifiability pattern that produced **HQ Trivia** as a productive failure — tactile virality overriding governance risk — is structurally unavailable to Taiga.

On *magically weird*, Taiga is the wrong kind of unsexy. Medical billing is unglamorous, yes, but Candid Health has raised $99.5M with 200+ org customers and ~250% YoY growth in 2024, Adonis raised a $40M Series C in March 2026 with 4x revenue growth, and Akasa has pulled $205M for the hospital tier — over eighteen months the AI-RCM category compounds *toward* incumbent scale, not away from it, and Taiga's stated wedge (independents, two-week onboarding) is the slice every one of those competitors will move down-market into the moment the multi-site TAM saturates. This isn't Crusoe-burning-gas-flares weird; this is "third entrant in a hot category" weird, which is consensus dressed in scrubs.

On *single unambiguous CEO*, I want to know what would have to be true to flip Taiga from the absent-evidence default toward the Anduril structural-clarity end. The dossier shows Adam Wax's LinkedIn reads "Founder @ Taiga" while Nanda Guntupalli's reads "Co-Founder @ Taiga" — a small asymmetry that could mean Adam is the operating CEO, or could mean the conversation hasn't happened yet. Friends-since-3rd-grade equity pairs are exactly the configuration that produced the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll collapse I cite as a permanent warning. To flip this dimension up a tier, I'd need a documented operating agreement naming a CEO with majority authority and a vesting/exit-conflict protocol on Day 1. Nothing in the dossier gets us there.

On *capital-intensive or unsexy-regulated*, Taiga maps cleanly onto the Vigil Labs / Mindbloom lane — federal payer fraud rules, HIPAA, and AI-generated coding liability are the kind of regulatory surface I happily fund because the moat compounds in domains other investors refuse to enter. The 98% touchless claim rate is self-reported and the company itself notes "physician oversight," which is the contradiction that has to be resolved before any acquirer will diligence the claim, but the *category* is mine. This is the dimension where Taiga earns real weight.

Thirty-six month forecast: Taiga gets acquired by a mid-tier EHR or practice-management incumbent — Tebra, Greenway, or athenahealth's corporate-dev arm — for $25-60M as an AI-bolt-on, or it stalls under $3M ARR while Adonis moves down-market into the independent-practice slice with a $40M war chest. The lived-experience-without-operator-scar plus invisible-artifact plus crowded-incumbent-category combination doesn't produce a breakout; it produces a credible aqui-hire.

The bull case stings: Taiga is HandUp-shaped — the Known Exception where I wrote a check because the founders' biographies mirrored the pain. Nanda and Adam watched billing eat their parents' practices the way I watched homelessness eat my adolescence, and independent practices genuinely are underserved by Candid (multi-site) and Akasa (hospitals). But HandUp earned the exception because the founder mirrored *my own* outsider trajectory; Taiga founders are Penn undergrads whose parents are physicians, which is privilege-adjacent-to-pain, not outsider-lived-pain, and that distinction matters. The flip condition is concrete: if Taiga signs 25+ independent practices in the next six months with audit-defensible coding and a documented 20%+ collections lift per practice, I take a second meeting and reconsider the verifiability discount.
