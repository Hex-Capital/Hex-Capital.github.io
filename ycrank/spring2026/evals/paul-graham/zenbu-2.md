# Zenbu -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Rob Pruzan shipped **React Scan** to 800k weekly installs before he ever filed a YC application -- that single dossier fact is what Zenbu lives or dies on, and it pulls the whole evaluation in two directions at once. Zenbu is a hacker with a distribution artifact who refused to find a cofounder. That is the signature: highest on the hacker-genealogy and animal-resourcefulness dimensions, lowest on the cofounder configuration, with everything else following from that pairing.

**Dropbox** (2007) is the right anchor for Zenbu's strongest dimension -- Drew Houston shipped a working demo to Hacker News before the seed check, and the artifact itself was the conviction-trigger. Zenbu's analog is sharper than most: Rob's React Scan repo (22k stars, 800k weekly installs per the YC page, 15.8k on the underlying aidenybai/react-scan) is not a demo video, it is a deployed tool that React developers already depend on weekly. Where Zenbu diverges from the Dropbox rung is that the artifact isn't the Zenbu IDE itself -- zenbu.js has 222 stars, which is a normal pre-launch number, not a Houston-style flip. The animal moment happened in Rob's prior life, not on this company. That still counts -- the hand that built React Scan is the hand building Zenbu -- but it is one step removed from the cereal-box pattern where the resourcefulness was the company.

The **MBA-pair pass example** I wrote about elsewhere fails on cofounder configuration for the inverse reason Zenbu does: that team had two people who met for the deck, Zenbu has one person and no deck problem. "Empirically it seems to be hard to start a startup with just one founder" -- I wrote that in October 2010 and have not had reason to retract it. Zenbu lists team size of 1, and the dossier line "Hiring: False" is the part that worries me more than the solo bit itself, because it suggests Rob is not actively closing the cofounder gap while Cline raises $32M and Anysphere closes another round at $50B pre. Solo against funded competition is the Loopt situation without an Altman force-of-personality override visible in the dossier.

**Organic idea fit** -- mechanism analysis. Zenbu is being built by someone whose day-job artifact (React Scan) is itself a developer-pain tool, so the founder-problem distance is short. But the dossier never says Rob personally ran four Claude Code agents in parallel and got frustrated reviewing diffs -- it quotes the YC page boilerplate about "reviewing changes nobody wrote by hand." Over 18 months this dimension compounds well only if Rob is the heaviest user of Zenbu, the way Houston was the heaviest user of Dropbox. The Vercel/Next.js core team background makes that plausible without proving it.

**Schlep zone** -- counterfactual. For Zenbu to move from the bottom of this dimension to the middle, the company would have to be doing something other than pure TypeScript IDE work: a regulatory grind, a manual onboarding loop, an offline operation. None of that is in the dossier. The "hackable plugins" thesis is a product choice, not a schlep, and Cursor's $2B ARR exists precisely because the IDE-for-AI category is now the opposite of a schlep -- it is the most crowded room in developer tools. **Coinbase** (2012) had compliance nobody wanted; Zenbu has a category every well-funded company is sprinting into.

**Hacker genealogy** -- single-anchor parallel to **Stripe** (2010). Patrick Collison won the Young Scientist Lisp prize before Stripe; Rob co-authored React Scan and was on the Next.js core team at Vercel before Zenbu. Both are the kind of pre-company technical lineage I over-index on, and the 800k weekly installs number is the closest thing in this batch to a Collison-grade prior artifact. The gap from Stripe is that the Collisons were two and had already shipped Auctomatic for ~$5M; Rob is one and React Scan is open source, not an exit.

My 36-month forecast for Zenbu: acquihired by Vercel or rolled into Cursor's ecosystem for under $30M, with the zenbu.js framework continuing as Rob's open-source line item, because a solo founder cannot out-ship Anysphere's headcount on a pure-software IDE category where the incumbent already has $2B ARR. The mechanism is not founder quality -- Rob will out-build most engineers in the batch -- it is that the schlep-zone moat is absent, so the contest collapses to feature velocity, and feature velocity is where solo loses to 200-engineer Cursor.

The strongest objection: **Stripe** (2010) is my own Known Exception for breaking process around a hacker-founder with a prior distribution artifact, and Rob's React Scan numbers are quantitatively in that zip code. If I forced the Stripe lens onto Zenbu, the call is invest, find Rob a brother, and let the artifact compound. What dismantles the bull case is that the Collisons were two and had a $5M cash exit behind them before Stripe -- the exception was about magnitude of prior signal across a pair, not a single open-source repo solo. The condition that would flip my verdict: Rob names a technical cofounder from his Vercel or React Scan circle within 90 days, and I would write the check that week.
