# Minicor -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Minicor is a pivot-validated founder team loaded into a consequence-bounded deal -- Faizaan Chishtie and Saheed Akinbile have the learning-rate signature I pay for, but they have pointed it at "expose desktop workflows as API endpoints via MCP," which is plumbing for someone else's labor displacement, not the labor displacement itself. The 25,000 patients/day production number is real enterprise traction for a pre-seed, and the rebrand from Laminar Run to Minicor is exactly the kind of documented thesis update I reward in the **Slash** (2024) framework. But when I run the **OpenAI** (2019) test on Minicor -- map the world at maximum success -- I get "AI vendors selling into Epic and Cerner have a 93-96% reliable click layer." That is a feature inside other people's revolutions.

On consequence magnitude, the high rung is **OpenAI** (2019), where the $50M check sat against a success-state of redrawing the cost curve of cognition itself. The shared mechanism I am hunting for is incumbent-regime collapse. Minicor's dossier names the incumbent regime as UiPath and Automation Anywhere, and the collapse mechanism as "self-healing recovery when UIs change rather than script failure" -- that is a 20-30% reliability delta inside RPA, not the disappearance of RPA. The Gartner $3.6B 2024 number is the entire global RPA market; even monopolizing it puts Minicor an order of magnitude below the $15T labor-displacement frame I have been writing publicly since Fortune 2025-07-01. The success-state of Minicor is "AI vendors close more enterprise deals faster," not "human work in healthcare admin disappears."

The low rung on technical and capital risk is the YC GPT-wrapper that summarizes sales calls -- the deal I pass on in 30 seconds because any Tier-1 generalist can fund it and software-only execution risk is not my edge. The failure mechanism on that rung is that the underlying model improves and the wrapper's edge evaporates. Trace that pattern in Minicor's dossier: Anthropic computer-use, OpenAI Operator, and YC-backed Skyvern at $2.7M raised are all converging on the same self-healing UI primitive. Minicor's claimed 93-96% click accuracy versus 80-85% baseline is exactly the kind of unbenchmarked delta that disappears when claude-computer-use ships its next checkpoint. Minicor is not **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) where I led the $115M Series A because the physics required miracles no consensus VC would underwrite -- Minicor is software a Salesforce product team could rebuild in twelve months once Anthropic crosses 95%.

Founder-defies-expert: the dossier shows Chishtie and Akinbile met in a 2019 UX class at University of Ottawa, built Laminar (low-code integration), and have now rebranded into adjacent RPA-with-AI. That is domain-continuity, not field defiance. Over 18 months this dimension compounds against Minicor specifically because the incumbents -- UiPath's ~$1.5B ARR FY24 sales motion and Microsoft Power Automate's M365 bundle -- are the experts whose orthodoxy a founder would need to publicly contradict, and there is no public position from either founder challenging that orthodoxy. The TED Talk McKinsey lesson applies: experts extrapolate the past, and Minicor's founders are extrapolating their Laminar past.

For founder learning rate to flip from a 10 to a 13, the dossier would need Chishtie or Akinbile to articulate the specific Laminar thesis they killed and why -- not just that the URL redirects. The "25,000 patients/day" number is currently ambiguous between Laminar carry-over and Minicor net-new, and that ambiguity is the counterfactual lever. If Minicor disclosed that the healthcare deployment was originated post-rebrand against a thesis Laminar explicitly abandoned, the learning-rate score moves into **Slash** (2024) territory. As written, the pivot is real but reads more like product evolution than a Kanye-grade thesis rupture.

Required-capital profile is where Minicor most cleanly excludes itself from my check-writing pattern. Compare to **Genesis AI** (2025), where I co-led a $105M seed because robot foundation models required capital depth no commodity SaaS economics would support. Minicor's roadmap -- Windows VM hosting, on-prem/Citrix deployment, SOC 2 maintenance, Forward Deployed Engineers at $125K-$200K -- is a standard $8-15M seed-to-Series-A burn that any Sequoia or Benchmark partner can write without thinking about my fund. I am over-resourced for this deal, which is itself a signal: when the capital profile is commodity, the consequence profile usually is too.

My 36-month forecast: Minicor reaches $4-9M ARR concentrated in three to five healthcare and financial-services accounts, then gets acquired by UiPath or Automation Anywhere for $40-90M as the incumbents buy the agentic-RPA narrative they failed to build organically -- a respectable founder outcome and a non-event for a fund of my size. The mechanism is straightforward: the 93-96% accuracy edge erodes as Anthropic computer-use ships through 2027, the SOC 2 + HIPAA moat becomes table stakes once Power Automate ships the equivalent inside M365, and Chishtie and Akinbile -- who have already shown they will pivot -- take the strategic exit rather than raise a hard Series B into a commoditized category.

The bull case that should sting: Minicor is the **Slash** (2024) exception in disguise -- YC-pedigreed founders with a documented prior-company pivot, and "25,000 patients/day in production" is real enterprise distribution most pre-seeds never touch. The argument is that desktop-AI integration becomes the Plaid layer for every healthcare AI vendor, and Minicor's accumulated UI-change telemetry across customer VMs becomes a genuine data moat the foundation models cannot replicate from training data alone. I take it seriously and reject it: Slash's override was a publicly-told, idiosyncratic founder pivot story plus a consumer category where distribution compounded -- Minicor's pivot is normal product evolution and the category is enterprise infrastructure where the customer is itself an AI startup whose own runway and model-vendor lock-in dictate Minicor's ceiling. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Minicor publishes a third-party benchmark showing its accuracy edge holds *after* claude-computer-use's next major release, and names two Fortune-500 healthcare systems (not AI-vendor middlemen) as direct contracted customers. Absent that, this is a pass.
