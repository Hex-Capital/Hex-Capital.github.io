That trajectory is the indefinite-optimism mode ? velocity through fast iteration on what is working this quarter ? which is admirable execution and a Red Flag against my framework simultaneously.

Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Dylan Teixeira are the strongest signal in the dossier, and the Asana (2010) parallel is where Gojiberry AI's lineage case lives or dies. Dustin Moskovitz overrode my missing-structural-moat objection because he had shipped Facebook to scale ? a company that generationally redefined the social graph. Gojiberry AI gets credit for operator pedigree; it does not get a Known Exception override.

That is a respectable outcome for the founders. It is not a check from my capital.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that Lallemant and Teixeira have now shipped three companies to profitable revenue between them, and in a commoditizing category the winner is whoever has the most efficient GTM engine ? exactly the muscle this team has demonstrated twice. 35% MoM growth while profitable at $2.5M ARR is execution most YC teams cannot match. The dismantling evidence: profitability at $2.5M ARR signals capital efficiency, not category dominance, and the Founders Fund manifesto attacked TAM-as-moat reasoning precisely because fast execution in a crowded category produces the consolidation outcome above, not the monopoly outcome.
