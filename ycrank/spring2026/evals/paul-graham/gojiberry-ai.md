# Gojiberry AI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is what happens when three repeat SMB-SaaS operators -- Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Rom?n Czerny out of CoCo.ai (~€500K ARR exit), Dylan Teixeira out of Edusign (multi-million ARR exit) -- point their second-time-founder muscle at the most crowded AI category of 2026. $2.5M ARR in 10 months, profitable, 35% MoM. The animal test is satisfied; the hacker test is not. That asymmetry, more than any TAM number, is the whole evaluation.

Airbnb's cereal boxes are the high rung of the resourcefulness ladder, and Gojiberry AI is not there, but it is closer to Brian Armstrong's hand-built Coinbase wallet than to the MBA-deck pass example. The shared mechanism is the same one I keep paying for: shipped revenue before the institutional check. Gojiberry AI shipped $33K in four months post-pivot, then $1M ARR at month nine, then $2.5M at month ten, profitable, with 2,000+ paying customers (YC blurb, 2026). Where Gojiberry AI diverges from the cereal-box anchor is in the *texture* of the hustle -- there is no unscalable stunt in the dossier, no Obama O's. It is just clean SaaS execution by people who have done it before. That is animal of a different species: the second-time operator who knows how to compound, not the first-time founder who improvises survival.

The pass example I wrote about -- polished AI wrapper, two-MBA team, $50B TAM slide, "hire engineers post-funding" -- is uncomfortably close to the lowest rung on Gojiberry AI's hacker-genealogy ladder. The failure mechanism there is technical-by-credential-only: nobody on the team has left the GitHub trail that Patrick Collison left before Stripe (2010) or that Aaron Swartz left before Reddit (2005). Does the pattern appear in Gojiberry AI's dossier? Dylan Teixeira is CTO but his lineage runs through ISEG School of Management, not a CS program, and the dossier explicitly notes "No public account found" for GitHub across all three founders. The product itself -- LinkedIn scraping plus LLM-written outreach -- is replicable by any competent engineer with an Apify subscription. This is the dimension where Gojiberry AI most resembles the company I wrote my pass example about, and the one where my unstated pattern about Lisp-literate founders most loudly votes no.

On the organic-idea test, Gojiberry AI's founders have *been* B2B SaaS sellers selling CoCo.ai and Edusign into the same SMB ICP they now sell Gojiberry AI to, which means they have personally felt the cold-outreach pain. But the dossier never says "we built this because our own outbound was broken" -- the narrative reads as a market pivot off a failed prior AI product (Superframeworks, 2025), not as Drew Houston forgetting his USB stick. Organic-adjacent, not organic.

On schlep, Gojiberry AI fails the test in a particular way: AI SDR at $5.81B and 31.9% CAGR is the *opposite* of schlep blindness. Every YC batch since W23 has had four of these. Clay just raised $100M at $3.1B doing a more general version. The only real schlep here is LinkedIn's ToS enforcement risk on automated scraping and sending -- which Gojiberry AI has not addressed in the dossier and which over 18 months either becomes a moat (if they survive a platform crackdown that kills weaker competitors) or becomes the obituary. Mechanism-wise, this compounds the wrong way: as Gojiberry AI scales LinkedIn senders past 2,000 customers, the probability of platform action rises faster than the probability of regulatory clarity. Coinbase's compliance grind became a moat because compliance has a clearing price; LinkedIn ToS does not.

The co-founder configuration is the cleanest signal in the dossier. Pierre-Eliott and Rom?n built and sold CoCo.ai together; this is the multi-year working relationship I look for in the Huffman-Ohanian Reddit dorm pattern. The score here would flip a tier higher if Dylan had been the CoCo.ai technical co-founder rather than a third operator joining from Edusign -- but a trio of three exited founders with two of them already battle-tested as a pair is well above the median Spring 2026 batch.

My 36-month forecast for Gojiberry AI: profitable plateau in the $10-25M ARR range, then acqui-merger by Apollo, Outreach, or HubSpot in the $50-150M range as Clay's bundled agentic outbound features compress the standalone AI-SDR category. The mechanism producing this: Gojiberry AI's founders are good enough operators to ride the AI-SDR wave to real revenue, but the absence of a hacker-genealogy moat and the absence of true schlep exposure means the category will be commoditized faster than Gojiberry AI can entrench. This is a Wufoo-shaped outcome -- competent SaaS, good return for a $20K check, not a black swan.

The strongest objection cuts at exactly the spot I am weakest: Airbnb (2009) is my known exception precisely because I had mentally rejected the idea and the founders overrode me via the cereal-box stunt. Gojiberry AI's bull case is that $2.5M ARR profitably in 10 months *is* the cereal-box -- it is the unscalable revenue stunt, just denominated in MRR instead of breakfast cereal, and I am pattern-matching on absence-of-Lisp when I should be pattern-matching on presence-of-revenue. The reason this does not flip me is that Airbnb's cereal stunt happened *before* any investor money and was orthogonal to the core product thesis (it proved founder character, not market demand); Gojiberry AI's revenue happened *inside* the consensus AI-SDR wave that is lifting every boat in 2026, which means the revenue measures market timing more than founder exceptionality. The condition that flips my verdict to Invest: a documented platform-level event -- LinkedIn ToS crackdown, or an Apollo/Clay agentic launch -- where Gojiberry AI's revenue keeps growing through the headwind. That would prove the moat is the founders, not the wave.
