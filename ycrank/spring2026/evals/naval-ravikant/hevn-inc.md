# HEVN, inc -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Peter Volnov ran HOT Labs to 15M users and $100M TVL before pivoting into HEVN, inc, and that fact is the entire reason this dossier registers as more than another YC neobank — HEVN, inc is a deep-specific-knowledge team building a single-sided B2B account product whose distribution is permission-gated by 82 bank partners. The signature is high on founder domain, low on network compounding. Everything in the evaluation flows from that pairing.

Stack Overflow (2008) is the load-bearing analogy on specific knowledge: Atwood and Spolsky were practicing developers writing for developers, and the company became inevitable because the founders *were* the user. HEVN, inc's claim is structurally similar — the team operated across SF, UAE, BVI, and HK and "felt the pain of global payments automation" firsthand while running HOT Labs through a stablecoin-adjacent consumer wallet that hit 15M MAU. Andrei Zhevlakov's first SWE role at 15, his ITMO CS background, and the $250K Stellar Foundation grant for chain abstraction (Chainwire, May 2025) tell me the CTO is a stablecoin-rails native, not a fintech PM who read about USDC. Where HEVN, inc diverges from the Stack Overflow rung is that the consumer-crypto-to-B2B-fintech jump is a category change — the specific knowledge transfers on rails and treasury, not on selling USD accounts to non-US internet companies. That is real but partial founder-product-market fit.

Anchorage Digital is the relevant mirror on the lowest dimension — single-sided infrastructure where each customer is economically independent of the previous one, no Nth-user-makes-(N-1)-better compounding. I waived criterion #2 for Anchorage because pre-consensus crypto-category conviction and regulatory permission-as-moat flipped the leverage math; HEVN, inc does not get that same waiver, because the dossier describes a payments account, not a custody charter, and Mercury already sits at $650M ARR and $5.2B post (CNBC, May 2026) running the same playbook with a US-EIN restriction. HEVN, inc is single-sided B2B SaaS with FX spread, card interchange, and float yield — there is no mechanism by which adding the 1,000th non-US internet company makes the 999th one's account more valuable. This is the Perplexity problem without Perplexity's pre-consensus AI-cycle timing.

The leverage check is where HEVN, inc gets most uncomfortable for me. The product surface is "API/CLI-driven payouts… built for humans and AI agents" (HEVN YC page), which sounds like code leverage, and Peter Volnov's 463.2K X following is genuine media leverage of the kind that drove **Airchat** (April 2024) co-founding logic. But underneath the API sits a network of local bank partners across 82 countries on SEPA/SWIFT/ACH/PIX/UAEFTS, and each one of those is a permissioned relationship with KYC, MTL, and jurisdiction-specific stablecoin posture. Eighteen months out, this compounds the wrong direction — every new corridor is a partner negotiation, not a git push. That is labor-and-capital leverage wearing a code-leverage costume, and "labor and capital… require permission" (nav.al/product-media) is the line that scores this down.

On market size, the counterfactual is what would flip this a tier. HEVN, inc targets non-US global internet companies excluded by Mercury/Brex/Ramp's US-EIN gate (Slash blog, 2026) — that is a real structural opening, but the TAM line in the dossier reads "No public data found." For HEVN, inc to clear top quartile on criterion #4, the pivot vectors would need to be legible: stablecoin payroll (BVNK already powers Deel), treasury-as-a-service for crypto-native companies, agent-driven AP automation. Those adjacencies exist; the dossier does not name them, which means I am crediting room-to-maneuver from category shape rather than from articulated founder intent.

The Buffett trio reads clean on HEVN, inc — Peter Volnov, Nik Briuzgin, Andrei Zhevlakov, and Pavel Mitsevich worked together at HOT Labs for four years (HackerNoon), no partner litigation, no self-promotional integrity signaling, and the prior product shipped at 15M-user scale with measurable outcomes (#1 DappRadar for 8 months). The Replit (April 2023) parallel holds on energy and ship-rate — Amjad Masad had been building Repl.it since 2011 before the AI cycle arrived, and Volnov has been shipping wallets and payments rails since HERE Wallet. I would take the 10-year call on this team.

Thirty-six-month forecast: HEVN, inc reaches $4–15M in revenue from FX spread and float on USDC treasury, gets acquired by Mercury or Stripe in the $120–350M range as the non-US-EIN gateway acquisition, or pivots into stablecoin-payroll infrastructure and sells the rails wholesale to Deel-shaped buyers. The mechanism producing this is the dimensional signature: founder depth carries the product to a real revenue base, but single-sided economics and bank-partner-permission distribution cap the upside geometry below the 1,000x line I need to write the angel check.

The bull case that stings: HOT Labs founders are exactly the *builder-for-builders* pattern I quietly over-index on — they built a 15M-user stablecoin product, they live the cross-border pain, YC began funding Spring 2026 in USDC (TechCrunch, Feb 2026) signaling stablecoin operationalization at the startup-finance layer, and Volnov's 463.2K X following is the **Airchat** (April 2024) media-leverage template applied to fintech distribution. If agent-driven finance ops is the next category I should have been dollar-cost-averaging into since **MetaStable** (2014), HEVN, inc is the pre-consensus check. The dossier evidence that wins the argument against this read is Mercury at $650M ARR (CNBC, May 2026) plus Capital One's $5.15B Brex acquisition (April 2026) plus BVNK already powering Deel — the incumbents have the capital to extend non-US coverage and the infra layer is commoditizing underneath, which means HEVN, inc gets squeezed between a Mercury feature release and a BVNK API. The condition that flips my verdict to Invest: HEVN, inc ships an open API with developer-pull adoption (measurable on a public GitHub or status page) from 100+ non-US internet companies inside 9 months, *without* paid acquisition — that would prove the leverage architecture is code-and-media, not labor-and-capital.
