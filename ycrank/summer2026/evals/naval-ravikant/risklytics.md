# Risklytics -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Risklytics is two Harvard CS kids selling a permissioned API into a regulator-gated California underwriting workflow, and the leverage-architecture read is the whole evaluation. Samuel Gold and Alexander Risio have built Ember — a 0–100 wildfire score trained on 131,929 CAL FIRE inspections that hit 91% discrimination on the January 2025 LA fires. The dimensional signature is a founder-integrity read that is quietly clean paired with a specific-knowledge read that is conspicuously absent, wrapped around a distribution motion that structurally requires permission from the CA DOI, ZestyAI-installed carriers, and the incumbent Verisk/KCC/Moody's model queue. This is the anti-Twitter, run through my exact filter in reverse.

Anchorage Digital is the honest analogy on leverage architecture, and Risklytics does not clear that bar. Anchorage sold permission-as-moat — an OCC charter that made regulation itself the compounding structure, and I still had to override criterion #1 to write it because I'd been dollar-cost-averaging into crypto infrastructure since MetaStable in 2014. Risklytics wants the same override without the category-timing conviction: the CA DOI release 052-2025 approved only Verisk, KCC, and Moody's for forward-looking wildfire models in filed rates, which means Ember cannot enter the rate-filing workflow that is the actual money in this category. Risklytics is left with underwriting-eligibility and non-filed decisions, sold one carrier at a time through an API-key application form. That is capital-and-labor leverage requiring permission from three different gatekeepers, and I have no MetaStable-equivalent thesis under it.

Clearview AI is the low-rung analogy on specific knowledge — I overrode the integrity leg there because Hoan Ton-That's facial-recognition depth was genuinely rare. The Risklytics dossier inverts that trade: no integrity red flag, but no rare specific knowledge to override anything with. Samuel Gold left Harvard after one year, ran Harvard Undergraduate Capital Partners, and has zero public insurance/actuarial/wildfire/CAT-modeling artifacts. Alexander Risio holds the AB/SM in CS with a systems-and-biology focus. Neither founder has been inside a carrier, a re-insurer, or CAL FIRE. The 131,929 CAL FIRE inspections are a *public* corpus — the dossier flags this explicitly — and ZestyAI's Z-FIRE already trains on 200B+ datapoints across 1,500+ wildfires with 40% of the CA homeowners market installed. Ivan Zhao rebuilt Notion three times because he'd been obsessed with productivity tools since adolescence; there is no adolescent-onward wildfire obsession here.

Marketplace mechanics compound over 18 months when the Nth user makes user N-1 more valuable; Risklytics's API does the opposite. Each carrier customer is economically independent — Farmers scoring an address does not increase State Farm's score quality. The retrofit-outcome feedback loop the dossier hopes for requires carriers to (a) act on Ember recommendations, (b) rescore post-mitigation, and (c) share loss outcomes back to a two-person pre-seed vendor. That is three simultaneous behavior changes across an industry that took a decade to accept forward-looking cat models at all. Perplexity got my exception because criterion #1, criterion #3, and category-timing all cleared top-quartile; Risklytics clears none of the three.

Counterfactual on market pivot-room: for Risklytics to move from a 7/16 to a 12/16 on criterion #4, the founders would need a credible adjacency into flood, hurricane, or earthquake — but each requires a new proprietary corpus (NFIP claims, NOAA HURDAT, USGS ShakeMaps), a new regulator (FEMA, state DOIs), and a new carrier persona. The Twitter-from-Odeo pivot worked because media substrate was unified; wildfire-to-flood is three substrates. California-only wildfire is a niche vertical with no documented adjacent path that fits inside a seed runway, and the "first idea never works" axiom is the exact place Risklytics will refinance-or-die.

Single-anchor parallel on the founder trio: I sat with the Notion 2013 seed because Ivan Zhao read as a ten-year human before the product read as a company. Reading Samuel Gold and Alexander Risio through that lens, I see no integrity flags — no contested exits, no ethics-signaling, no partner litigation — but also no depth artifact I can spend ten years next to. Both LinkedIn snippets, no X presence, no GitHub of note, no public writing. The default 7/14 is exactly right: neutral silence, not negative signal. The problem is that silence on the trio does not rescue a dossier that is loud in the wrong places on criteria #1 and #3.

Forecast, 36 months: Risklytics is acqui-hired by ZestyAI or a Guidewire-adjacent player for under $10M in stock, or it is dead, with the modal outcome being quiet wind-down after failing to close a paid enterprise contract with an admitted CA carrier inside 18 months. The mechanism is the distribution asymmetry — ZestyAI's Z-FIRE installed base at 40% of the CA homeowners market, CAPE Analytics now inside Moody's post-January 2025 acquisition, and HazardHub bundled inside Guidewire since 2021 — a two-founder API without an insurance operator cannot navigate carrier procurement cycles that run 12-18 months per logo.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that Risklytics reframes the category — "retrofit path to insurability" is not what ZestyAI, CAPE, or HazardHub sell. Carriers are drowning in the 2.8M non-renewed policies and the 450K-policy FAIR Plan at $650B exposure; a per-address ranked retrofit list that converts a decline into a bind is a genuinely different product motion, and 13,685,446 California homes already scored plus 91% discrimination on the LA fires is not a nothing artifact for a pre-seed. YC + Ankit Gupta could open two carrier doors that flip the distribution math. That case would sting except for one dossier fact: the retrofit-to-bind claim requires carrier underwriting rules to accept the recommendation, and *no public carrier partnership has been announced*. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a named admitted CA carrier — Mercury, CSAA, Farmers, or the FAIR Plan itself — signing a paid pilot with retrofit-conditional binding inside the next twelve months. Absent that signature, this is a pass, and Pinterest-style aesthetic regret is not the risk here — the leverage architecture actively fails the filter.
