# Klarify -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Moody Abdul is a competently-governed repeat YC founder running Klarify directly into the most crowded vertical of the Spring 2026 batch -- AI scribes for therapists, where Upheal has already raised $14.35M and Blueprint sits on a $9M Series A with 1M+ sessions analyzed. That high-governance / low-weirdness pairing is the analytical signature: Klarify is a pitch-deck-perfect company in a pitch-deck-perfect category, and I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing.

Crusoe in ~2018 is the HIGH rung on the "magically weird" axis because three credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds when I wrote the check -- burning gas flares for Bitcoin in North Dakota was uninvestable in polite company, and that was the tell. Klarify is the structural inverse. The Klarify dossier names Upheal ($14.35M total, 70,000+ providers), Blueprint ($9M Series A, 4,500 clinicians across 34 states), Mentalyc (30,000+ therapists, ~$2.9M revenue per Latka), Heidi, and Freed -- five funded entrants with multi-year head starts, and Klarify is launching with a freemium SaaS at $16/$32/$71 tiers. No one will laugh at this pitch at a Sand Hill dinner. Everyone will nod. The thesis is to find them *before* it becomes consensus, and Klarify launched into a fully-consensus lane.

The Pass Example I keep on file -- the two ex-FAANG PMs with Stanford CS and a $200B TAM slide -- failed my founder-origin test because they discovered the problem in a memo, not by running the back-office their product claimed to automate. Klarify's founder dossier rhymes with that pattern more than I want it to. Moody Abdul co-founded Circleback.ai (YC W24, $2.5M seed, AI meeting notes) twelve to eighteen months before Klarify, and Alexander Bergholm built LLM infrastructure at Workday with a UBC computer vision patent and Finnish Defence Forces deep-learning research. Both are credible builders. Neither has a documented day in a clinical setting -- no LMFT in the family that I can see, no prior intake-coordinator job, no autobiographical contact with therapist burnout. The lane was selected the way Circleback's lane was selected: "AI agent for X profession that takes a lot of notes." That is a market-analysis founder origin, not an Anduril-Luckey or Niantic-Hanke origin.

Moody as named CEO and Bergie as CTO is the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll collapse I cite as the governance cautionary tale -- two-person team, clear delineation, no 50/50 ambiguity in the dossier. The compounding worry over 18 months is not the tiebreaker; it's the founder concentration footnote. The Klarify dossier flags that Moody's current status at Circleback is undocumented, which means I'd be writing a check unsure whether I'm getting 100% of a CEO or 60% of one whose attention is split across two YC companies twelve months apart. That is a diligence call I can resolve in one conversation, but the absence of clarity in the dossier itself is the kind of thing that becomes a board-meeting conversation in year two.

Klarify scoring a 7 on the unsexy-regulated swing would require the CPT coding and insurance-claim filing to function as the real moat -- HIPAA + PIPEDA + PHIPA tri-jurisdiction compliance is genuinely the most regulated thing in the dossier, and incorrect CPT extraction is the kind of liability exposure that scares off generalist scribes like Heidi and Freed. To flip this dimension a tier, Klarify would need a clearinghouse partnership announced (Change Healthcare, Availity, Office Ally) plus a published accuracy benchmark on coding -- neither exists in the dossier. Without those, "we file claims" is a feature claim that competitors can copy in a quarter once the regulatory liability shape becomes clear.

Compare Klarify's tactility to Niantic in ~2015, which I sourced by playing Ingress obsessively for three years before John Hanke would take my call. Klarify offers a free tier (10 sessions/month) that a real therapist could install in 48 hours, which is non-trivial -- but I am not a therapist, and the value of a SOAP note's accuracy is not legible to me the way the pull of an HQ Trivia round was, or the way watching a Flock camera read my license plate was. The dossier reports 309,121 notes generated against a 2,154-therapist live counter, which conflicts with the YC page's "nearly 6,000 therapists" figure -- definition of "user" is undisclosed, which is exactly the diligence gap that separates a real tactile artifact from a vanity counter.

Thirty-six month forecast: Klarify lands somewhere between $3M and $8M ARR by mid-2029 and gets absorbed as a strategic tuck-in by SimplePractice or TherapyNotes for $25-60M, because the no-EHR-integration positioning that Klarify markets today becomes the ceiling on group-practice expansion the moment Upheal and Blueprint deepen their write-back integrations. The mechanism is straightforward: in a feature-parity AI scribe race, distribution wins, and the practice-management incumbents own the distribution. Moody's LinkedIn enterprise-AE background ($20M in contracts) is exactly the skill set that closes the acquihire conversation cleanly.

The strongest bull case for Klarify cites Mentalyc -- $200K convertible note, ~$2.9M revenue, 30,000 therapists -- as proof that even a sub-scale entrant in this category can build a real business, and argues that Moody's Circleback experience (shipped to $50K ARR in 4 months) plus Bergie's production LLM infra means Klarify outexecutes the existing field on speed. The HQ Trivia exception in my own framework says explosive product-led traction in a tactile consumer artifact can override governance worries, and Klarify's 309K notes generated at YC launch is the closest analogue here. The bear case wins because Klarify is B2B, not consumer -- there is no viral pull I can feel in my own hand, only a counter on a webpage -- and because the category does not need another funded entrant; it needs an EHR-native distribution channel that Klarify explicitly disclaims. I would flip to invest the day Klarify announces a clearinghouse partnership *and* a paying-customer count (not free-tier signups) above 1,500 -- both verifiable, neither in the dossier today.
