# Imperfect -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Matin Tamizi built the Garth Garmin Connect Python library (813 stars on GitHub) before he built Imperfect, and that single fact is the entire shape of this evaluation: Imperfect is a hacker scratching his own itch alone, inside a category that **Strava** just bought **Runna** to own (April 2025, Front Office Sports). The strongest dimension here is organic idea-fit. The weakest is co-founder configuration. That pairing — a self-using hacker building solo while the incumbents acquire their way into his roadmap — is what Imperfect actually is.

On organic founder-pain, the **Dropbox** (2007) analogue lands cleanly. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT, then wrote the demo. Tamizi published an open-source Garmin Connect client and a DZone piece titled "Building a Modern Training Assistant With Claude and Garmin" before Imperfect existed as a YC company. That is not a market-opportunity pitch; that is a programmer who trains, hit the wearable-data wall personally, wrote the library, then wrote the product on top of the library. Where Imperfect diverges from the Houston pattern is that the artifact is now marked **DEPRECATED** in the Garth readme because Garmin changed auth flows — a real-world reminder that Tamizi's organic pain is also Garmin's platform leverage.

The **Kiko** (2005) pattern is the one I keep tripping over when I read Tamizi's bio. Justin Kan and Emmett Shear sold Kiko on eBay; I re-funded them for **Twitch** (2007) because the failure read as timing, not as soft founders. Tamizi's prior tour at **Balanced** (YC, a16z, $500M+ card volume in 2014) ended with a "transition deal to Stripe" in March 2015 (TechCrunch). That is structurally a Kiko outcome — wind-down with a graceful off-ramp to the bigger competitor. **Cuenca** raised $7.4M Series A led by Stripe (Contxto) but has not produced a public exit. So Imperfect's founder has already done the Kiko move once at Balanced and is now solo against Strava+Runna and Garmin Active Intelligence (Triathlete, 2026) — the same shape, with the same likely buyer in the room before the company has shipped pricing.

On schlep blindness, the Garmin/Strava/Apple Health integration grind is real schlep work — auth flows break, APIs deprecate, data is messy — and Tamizi has 813 stars of evidence he will personally do it. The mechanism that makes this *not* a **Coinbase** (2012)-grade moat over 18 months: Garmin's Active Intelligence and Strava's bundled Runna AI plans mean the platforms doing the schlep-absorbing are the same platforms Imperfect depends on for data. Schlep that the incumbent is *also* doing inside their walled garden is not a moat; it is a feature backlog the incumbent will close.

On hacker genealogy, the counterfactual is clean: strip the 813-star Garth library and the DZone Claude+Garmin article from Tamizi's bio, and Imperfect drops two tiers. With them in, Tamizi is unambiguously of the **Reddit** (2005) Swartz-RSS-spec lineage — open-source artifact predating the company, recognizable in hacker-tribal terms. The DEPRECATED tag on Garth is the only smudge on an otherwise clean hacker trail.

On co-founder configuration, the comparison is to **Stripe** (2010): Patrick and John Collison built together, brothers, complementary, two years of Auctomatic before Stripe. Imperfect's YC page lists Tamizi as the sole named founder against a 5-person team. "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) was explicit that most big successes have two or three; I have written this enough times that I should mean it. A 5-person team with one named founder usually signals employees, not co-founders. Garry Tan's interview will surface whether there's a real number two; the dossier does not show one.

My 36-month forecast: Imperfect is acquired by Strava or Garmin for under $50M as a feature absorption, or winds down with users migrated to Strava+Runna — echoing Tamizi's own Balanced→Stripe transition pattern from March 2015. The mechanism is straightforward: the platforms owning the data are shipping the same product natively, and a solo founder cannot out-distribute Strava's installed base.

The strongest case against that forecast is the **Stripe** (2010) Known Exception — I funded the Collisons partly because Auctomatic at ages 19/17 was a $5M exit that outweighed YC-process concerns. Tamizi has two prior CEO tours and an a16z relationship; one could argue Balanced+Cuenca is that override. The case stings because Tamizi clearly ships. It loses because Balanced ended in a transition deal to the dominant competitor — the exact Kiko-shaped tell I treat as "data about timing failure, not a positive metric" — and Imperfect is now structurally identical: solo founder, dominant-competitor-as-likely-acquirer, deprecated dependency. The verdict flips if Imperfect names a technical co-founder of comparable hacker pedigree and ships paid revenue from athletes who switched off Strava+Runna within 90 days of launch. Until then, Neutral.
