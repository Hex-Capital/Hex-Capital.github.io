# Result -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Result is a creator-with-distribution wrapped around the wrong asset: Savio Martin's 50.5K X followers and Product Hunt Maker of the Year 2021 are a genuine owned channel into exactly the maker/founder ICP Result targets, but the surface he's pointing that distribution at is a seven-headed bundle (incorporation + banking + payments + hosting + marketing + support + content) competing with Mercury at $700M raised and $5.2B post and Doola at $12M raised. The high dimension is the founder-as-channel; the low dimension is the category. That pairing is Result's analytical signature, and it points away from venture and toward something else entirely.

John Fish / Bookshelved is the right HIGH-rung comparison for Result on owned distribution -- a named creator with a measurable audience pointed at a specific product, where the cap-table thesis was always "we are buying the channel, the product is the expression." Savio Martin's 50.5K X handle @saviomartin7, his 2,000 GitHub followers, his 961-star Codehouse repo, and his 588-star Slickr repo are the real artifact in the Result dossier -- a developer-tools audience that overlaps the ICP of a founder-tools product almost perfectly. Where Result diverges from the Fish analogy is that Bookshelved's product *was* the audience's interest; Result is asking Savio's maker audience to route their incorporation, banking, and payments through a 2-person team, which is a much heavier ask of a follow than "read this newsletter" or "buy this book club." The distribution is real; the conversion from follow to fiduciary trust is the unmodeled gap.

The Pass Example I wrote -- "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], four tier-1 VCs already in" -- is the LOW-rung mechanism on category, and Result reproduces the failure mode in consumer clothing. Result's YC description ("AI agents performing the operational work alongside the founder") is the Spring 2026 YC default template applied to the founder-tools vertical, which is precisely the substitution I treat as consensus exposure dressed as a vertical wedge. "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024) means the agent layer Result is selling is something Mercury, Stripe Atlas, and Doola can ship as a feature next quarter -- and Mercury, sitting on 300K+ startup customers and $650M ARR, has the distribution to ship it into existing trust, not into a waitlist landing page.

Capital structure is where Result fails by mechanism, not by absence: bundling seven operational surfaces on a 2-person team compounds badly over 18 months. Each surface (banking, payments, incorporation) requires a regulated partner Result has not disclosed on result.dev as of May 2026, which means either undisclosed BaaS dependency at thin margins or a build-out that turns Result into a five-round capital sink to chase Mercury's $700M war chest. Result's cap table will look like the generic SaaS template I deselect -- not Teamshares' employee-equity rollup, not the Mogilko 5%-of-30-years instrument, just a SAFE that needs the next SAFE.

For Result's price-discipline score to flip a tier, the dossier would need either a named tier-1 pass at a sub-batch-median valuation, or a non-standard instrument disclosed on Savio's distribution (a revenue share on his audience, a holdco wrapper around multiple products he'll ship). Neither is present -- result.dev is a waitlist, no round terms are disclosed, and the YC Spring 2026 default is the prior I apply when silence is the only evidence. Silence on round dynamics in a YC AI-agent pitch is itself a signal: the founders have no reason to disclose a discount because there isn't one, and the round will clear at the batch median because Savio's following will get it oversubscribed regardless.

Sublime Security is the single-anchor parallel for the founder-conviction axis, and Result fails the comparison cleanly. Sublime's founder had years of opinionated public writing on detection engineering before the round; Savio Martin has shipped Codehouse, Slickr, Moodly, and one App Store top-5 product -- a beautiful builder resume but zero public writing on why incorporation-plus-banking-plus-agents is the non-obvious bet versus the obvious one. Aaryan Kushwah's profile compounds the concern: a 17-year-old CEO listed as a Summer 2026 Coinbase Crypternship participant overlapping the YC batch period is the unresolved-bandwidth setup I screen against. Builders shipping into opportunity is not the same as missionaries shipping into a thesis.

My 36-month forecast for Result: Savio's audience carries the waitlist to a launch, the regulated surfaces (banking, payments, incorporation) get quietly de-scoped because no BaaS partner closes for a 2-person team without revenue, Result narrows to a content-and-marketing AI-agent product for solo founders distributed through Savio's channel, clears sub-$1M ARR, and either gets acquihired by Doola or Firstbase for the audience or winds down with Savio launching the next consumer product by 2029. The mechanism is the high/low pairing -- the distribution is durable, the category is commodity, so the durable asset (Savio) outlives the commodity wrapper (Result).

The strongest case against the pass is that I am pattern-matching Result to the YC-AI-agent template when the actual asset is Savio Martin's audience -- and the right move is the Mogilko move: ignore the company entirely, structure a 5%-of-Savio's-next-decade instrument, let the YC SAFE be somebody else's problem. That objection stings because it is consistent with my own Unstated Patterns -- I have written exactly this check before. The dossier evidence that wins is co-founder structure: this is not a Savio solo-creator deal, it is a 2-person YC C-corp with a 17-year-old CEO whose attention is already split across 90nine.in, beVisioneers, and the Coinbase Crypternship, which means the creator-instrument is unavailable to me at this entity. The verifiable condition that would flip the verdict: Savio spinning out a personal holdco or signing a revenue-share instrument on his distribution outside Result's cap table, at sub-$10M effective valuation -- then I am the only bid at a price that works.
