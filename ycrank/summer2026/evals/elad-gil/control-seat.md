# Control Seat -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Control Seat is two Dartmouth '26 grads -- Jack Grodnick and Warren Shepard -- attacking SCADA with no revenue disclosed, no named pilot, and an integrator price-guarantee instead of a velocity curve. The signature is founder-fit-meets-no-velocity: SpaceX manufacturing exposure on one axis, total silence on growth on the other, in a category where Inductive Automation already ground its way to ~$41M ARR bootstrapped. That pairing tells me what kind of company Control Seat is in my book -- a credible-looking integrator wrapping LLM tag-queries around AVEVA/Siemens/Rockwell territory, not an index position on a catalyzed re-opening.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing ladder rung for Market Re-Opening, and the analogy is exactly why Control Seat doesn't clear the bar. Anduril had a named 24-month catalyst -- Ukraine -- that rewrote Pentagon software inadequacy in public, in dollars, in months. Control Seat's "why now" is inferred: LLMs crossed a plain-language-query threshold in 2024-25. That's a capability drift, not a re-opening. SCADA was $12.89B in 2025 growing 9.2% CAGR -- a steady-state market, the kind *First Round Review* warns me great teams die in. There is no Ukraine here. There is no Stripe-replacing-merchant-accounts moment. Control Seat's dossier names AVEVA Wonderware, Siemens WinCC, and Rockwell FactoryTalk as "decades-old desktop-rooted stacks" but decades-old is not a catalyst; it's the steady state itself.

**Lyft** Series C is the rung I want to revisit on Index Position, because the failure mechanism was structural intolerance for #2 in a market with an entrenched #1 -- and Control Seat's dossier hands me four named #1s. Inductive Automation's Ignition runs at 69% Fortune-100 penetration with unlimited-tag licensing from ~$15K/server; AVEVA owns "the largest installed global SCADA base"; Siemens dominates process industries; Rockwell ships FactoryTalk View bundled with PLCs. Control Seat is not the workflow path every entrant must touch the way **Stripe** (2010) was for e-commerce -- it has to *displace* one of four incumbents per plant, with rip-and-replace cycles that "span years" per the dossier's own risk section. The Ignition wedge against Wonderware took two decades of licensing innovation; Control Seat doesn't have a licensing innovation, it has a price guarantee against integrator quotes, which is margin compression dressed up as differentiation.

Revenue Velocity compounds against Control Seat over the next 18 months in a specific way: SCADA sales cycles are 6-18 months, IEC 62443 procurement reviews extend that, and the dossier discloses zero MRR, zero named LOIs, zero customer logos, only a YouTube launch video titled "Real-time internal dashboards." In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself the signal -- "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). Control Seat may not yet *be* an AI company in deployment, but it's pitching as one, and the absence of a single named plant runs against the **Harvey** (2023) pattern where the curve was the pitch.

Founder Ships gets Control Seat its strongest read, but only on a counterfactual: for this dimension to flip a tier, Grodnick and Shepard would need a shipped industrial-tag artifact from SpaceX or Tesla I can verify -- a telemetry tool, a manufacturing dashboard, an internal PLC integration. The dossier gives me titles (Tesla, SpaceX, Amazon for Grodnick; SpaceX, Microsoft for Shepard) and a salutatorian GPA, which is pedigree, not the **Perplexity** (2022) "showed up a week later with the thing built" pattern. Grodnick's GitHub has no notable star counts; Shepard's GitHub has no public repos. SpaceX manufacturing exposure is real founder-market fit and I credit it -- but my disqualifier per Hustle Commons is whether the founder has *independently shipped*, and the dossier doesn't name what they shipped.

Control Seat fails the Single Miracle test the way **Color Genomics** (2015) almost did before I architected it as workflow rather than chemistry. The miracle chain Control Seat needs: (1) LLM accuracy on industrial tag time-series, (2) displacement of an entrenched OT incumbent per plant, (3) integrator-services delivery that scales without compressing margin to zero, (4) IEC 62443 compliance with a 2-person team. Each is independent. Compound them and the odds collapse -- this is the NFX *Unusual Signs* math, and it's the exact reason Color was structured as workflow on commodity sequencing, not a sequencing bet itself. Control Seat doesn't have the "infrastructure on top of hard thing" architecture that gave Color and **Anduril** (2022) their Known-Exception overrides; it has "AI on top of legacy stack the incumbent already owns."

My 36-month forecast on Control Seat: alive but small, $500K-$2M ARR from 2-5 custom integrator deployments at industrial plants, having quietly repositioned from "AI-native SCADA platform" to "AI analytics layer on top of existing Ignition/AVEVA installs" -- not acquired, not dead, not breakout. The mechanism is the price-guarantee positioning compressing them into services revenue while Ignition continues compounding its bootstrapped ~$41M ARR base, leaving Control Seat as a sub-scale specialty integrator. The dimensional signature -- credible founder fit, zero velocity, no catalyst, no index -- is exactly the profile that lives at that ARR band.

The bull case that should sting: SpaceX runs the highest-volume custom industrial telemetry and control systems on Earth, both Grodnick and Shepard worked inside that, and **Anduril** (2022) is the precedent where ex-Palantir/ex-Oculus founders turned operational exposure to a hard-tech category into the software layer the incumbents had to integrate against. If Control Seat has internalized SpaceX's tag-schema and PLC pain points the way Trae Stephens internalized Palantir's forward-deployed muscle, the integrator framing could be a Trojan horse for a SCADA-replacement platform that compounds once two or three brand-name plants standardize on it. The dismantling: Anduril shipped with a defense-procurement catalyst named in public dollars and a $4.6B post-money signal from later-stage capital; Control Seat has neither, and a $500K YC standard check is not a markup from a credible later-stage fund. I'd flip my verdict on one verifiable condition: a signed, named contract with a Tier-1 industrial operator (a Tesla Gigafactory line, a SpaceX Starbase cell, a named Fortune-500 manufacturer) disclosed within 6 months -- that turns the founder-fit thesis into a velocity thesis and re-runs the math. Until then, pass.
