# PerfectBit, Inc. -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Péter Vajda spent eleven years as Meta's Director of Media Generation shipping Emu and Movie Gen, and Seiji Yamamoto spent nine on Core Llama pre- and post-training -- which means PerfectBit, Inc. is the rare pre-seed where the founders personally watched the noisy-web-data hallucination problem ruin training runs they owned. That is the highest dimension on PerfectBit, Inc.'s ladder. The lowest is animal resourcefulness pre-funding: the dossier names a "pilot program" on perfectbit.ai and zero else -- no demo video, no stunt revenue, no cereal-box equivalent. The signature is domain-insider-with-no-shipped-stunt, and that pairing is the whole evaluation.

**Dropbox** (2007) is the load-bearing analogy for PerfectBit, Inc. on organic founder-pain. Drew Houston kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT and built the thing for himself; Yamamoto led Llama pre-training and watched ~10% annotation error rates ship from human-vendor pipelines (AOL / TechBuzz 2025) into models he owned. The mechanism is the same -- you build the tool because the existing tool failed *you*, not because a deck told you the TAM was $3.87B. Where PerfectBit, Inc. diverges from the **Dropbox** (2007) rung is that Houston published the demo on Hacker News and the artifact did the convincing; PerfectBit, Inc.'s site offers a pilot-program contact form and "selective engagements" framing, which is the language of enterprise sales, not the language of someone who shipped a thing and let the thing speak. The pain is real. The artifact-out-the-door evidence is not.

The MBA AI-wrapper team from my own Pass Example is the failure mechanism on the low rung: no shipped artifact, "we'll hire engineers post-funding," glossy deck. PerfectBit, Inc. does not look like that team -- Vajda and Yamamoto can obviously hire themselves -- but the *tell* of the pattern is the same absence: the dossier surfaces zero pre-funding stunts, zero side-channel revenue, zero unscalable hand-grinding. Vajda's LinkedIn says "Director." Yamamoto's says "Senior Staff Research Scientist." Neither has a public GitHub, neither has a Twitter the dossier could locate, and the most resourceful pre-funding act recorded is launching a website with a contact form. The "Relentlessly Resourceful" (March 2009) filter wants tracks. PerfectBit, Inc. has not left tracks I can see, and animals leave tracks.

Schlep blindness, evaluated by mechanism: PerfectBit, Inc.'s pitch is integrating formal proof systems, physics simulators, executable tests, and scientific databases into a single data-generation pipeline with SHA-256 batch integrity -- this *is* schlep work, the kind Surge AI's >$1B revenue marketplace would have to tear up its contractor unit economics to replicate. Over 18 months the verifier-integration library compounds: every prover, every simulator, every scientific DB hooked up is one more thing a Mercor-style human-expert marketplace cannot bolt on cleanly. The risk that breaks the compounding is domain reach -- formal verifiers cover math, code, and physics cleanly and basically nothing else cleanly, and the dossier names no path past that ceiling.

Hacker genealogy, by counterfactual: Yamamoto co-authored PNAS and Physical Review Letters papers with a Fields Medalist, and Vajda's EPFL CS PhD (2007-2011) plus Emu/Movie Gen tenure are real artifacts at planetary scale. What would flip this dimension from mid to high is one Yamamoto GitHub repo with a verifier prototype, or a Vajda Hacker News comment thread from 2019 about training-data quality. Neither exists in the retrieved dossier. The hacker tribal markers I over-index on -- the Patrick Collison Young Scientist Lisp prize (2005), the Aaron Swartz RSS spec before **Reddit** (2005) -- are absent. What's present is corporate-shipped artifacts, which is a different signal and a weaker one for my filter.

**Stripe** (2010) is the single anchor on co-founder configuration. The Collisons were brothers who had hacked commerce together at **Auctomatic** (2007) before they shipped Stripe; PerfectBit, Inc.'s two founders co-existed in Meta's GenAI / Superintelligence Labs and the dossier surfaces "ScienceSwarm.ai" appearing in both histories, suggesting a shared prior project. Two founders, prior working relationship, complementary technical coverage (media generation + LLM pre-training + physics). This is the right shape. It is not the **Airbnb** (2009) trio shape, but it is not solo, and the configuration alone would not stop me.

PerfectBit, Inc. in 36 months: two or three paid pilots with frontier labs that are not Meta (the $14B Scale entanglement closes that door), $3-8M ARR concentrated in math, code, and physics verifier datasets, and acquisition by Anthropic or Google DeepMind for $40-80M as a specialized data team rather than independent scale-up. The mechanism is the signature: deep founder-problem fit gets PerfectBit, Inc. into rooms at the labs, and the verifier-integration schlep produces real datasets the labs cannot quickly build themselves; but the absence of an animal-resourcefulness stunt means PerfectBit, Inc. never breaks into the consumer-of-AI distribution that produces a **Stripe** (2010)-shaped outcome, and the buyer set is the same five labs that have in-house data orgs and acquihire reflexes.

The bull case I have to steel-man: Yamamoto led Llama pre- and post-training for nine years, and that specific knowledge -- which evals break on which data slices, which verifier signals correlate with which post-training gains -- is the kind of moat Naval would call "specific knowledge" and I would normally hire-around. Here you cannot hire around it; the **Stripe** (2010) Known Exception applies when credentialed founders had already shipped a $5M outcome (Auctomatic) at 19/17, and PerfectBit, Inc.'s founders shipped Emu, Movie Gen, and Core Llama at planetary scale, which is an order of magnitude larger. The bear answer: shipping inside Meta is shipping with Meta's distribution, Meta's compute, Meta's reviewers, Meta's data org -- it is not evidence of animal-mode operation under resource starvation, which is the test that flipped **Coinbase** (2012) and **Airbnb** (2009). One verifiable condition would flip me to invest: a public artifact -- a verifier-grounded eval dataset, a GitHub repo, a Hacker News post with a working demo -- that PerfectBit, Inc. shipped before this dossier was assembled. The pilot program does not count. Until then, I want the meeting, but I do not want to write the check from my own money yet.
