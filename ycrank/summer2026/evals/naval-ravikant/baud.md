# Baud -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Baud is a deep-knowledge team building on the wrong leverage axis for my checkbook -- Eric Taylor's three-plus tape-outs at NVIDIA and Sarang Zambare's ML lead role shipping Peloton Guide to 100,000+ devices clear the specific-knowledge gate emphatically, but the business they have chosen requires TSMC's permission, an AI lab's purchase order, and a $200M-$1B Series A to reach a competitive tape-out. That is capital and labor leverage by structure, not code or media. The asymmetry I write checks against -- one X down, ten thousand X up -- does not live inside an ASIC roadmap.

Twitter (2007) is my canonical permissionless-leverage HIGH rung: media, zero marginal cost of replication, no gatekeeper between Dorsey's twttr and the next user. Baud lives at the opposite pole. Baud's distribution requires beta-form intake on baudlabs.ai, an enterprise sale into a buyer set of roughly five AI labs, and a fab slot at a competitive process node before a single chip ships -- this is the textbook capital-and-labor architecture I described in nav.al/product-media as the *permissioned* leverage you take only when the founder *is* the leverage. Etched at ~$1B raised and MatX at $600M+ tell you the going rate for a credible swing at NVIDIA. Baud at YC pre-seed against that capital wall is not the leverage shape I underwrite.

Pinterest is my standing reminder of where this dimension fails me -- I passed on permissionless media because "I don't get it." The inverse pattern is harder to misread: Baud does *not* have the permissionless media-or-code architecture Pinterest had, and that absence is not a taste call, it is structural. Baud's compiler -- torch.distributed, FSDP2, tensor parallelism -- is code, but it is code *bound to silicon Baud must tape out*, not code that replicates on a laptop. The PyTorch surface is a wrapper around a permissioned object. Stack Overflow (2008) shipped on a server and compounded through Google. Baud cannot ship until Baud taxes out.

Taylor's NVIDIA-Freescale-Arteris-Enfabrica path with two patents and Zambare's four-patent deep-learning record is genuinely top-quartile specific knowledge -- the kind that fails the "ex-FAANG PM identified market opportunity" anti-pattern decisively. Aravind Srinivas at Perplexity had this and I waived criterion #2 for him. The question is whether Baud's specific-knowledge magnitude justifies waiving criterion #1, and the honest answer is no -- Perplexity's API distribution was still permissionless code; Baud's ASIC is not. Specific knowledge without permissionless leverage is the Clearview exception's shape, and that one I tolerated only because the downside truly was capped at one X. Here the downside is one X plus the opportunity cost of a team this good aimed at the wrong axis.

The market dimension cuts against Baud through pivot-room, not size. $8.3B of 2026 AI-chip funding makes the category large, but ASIC economics make Baud non-pivotable in the way "first idea never works" demands -- once Baud commits to a multiplier-free arithmetic representation at a specific node, the optionality to become anything else inside the seed window is gone. Compare to Uber (2010), which pivoted from black-car-only to UberX inside the seed -- a software platform absorbs that pivot. An FPGA-emulated 50M-parameter demo at 1000+ tokens/sec is impressive evidence for the *thesis*, but it is the opposite of pivot-room: it locks Baud's identity to the arithmetic-representation claim.

The founder trio reads clean -- no contested exits between Taylor and Zambare, no self-promotional ethics signaling, Zambare shipped Peloton Guide cleanly into Instacart's acquisition of Caper. I would happily spend ten years with this pair. That is not the binding constraint. The binding constraint is that this pair has aimed an exceptional skill stack at a market where the winning move is a $500M Series B from Bloomberg's front page, not a Spearhead-shaped check from me.

My 36-month forecast: Baud raises a difficult $40-120M Series A on a successful first tape-out and a single named frontier-lab pilot, then is acqui-hired by NVIDIA, AMD, or a hyperscaler (Meta MTIA or Amazon Trainium team most likely) at a $100-300M outcome for the IP and the two founders. No 1,000x media-leverage geometry exists in this shape. The forecast follows directly from the high/low signature: top-quartile specific knowledge in a capital-permissioned architecture produces teams that get bought for their knowledge, not platforms that compound 10,000x on their own distribution.

The strongest case against my pass is Anchorage Digital -- I broke criterion #1 there because the OCC charter became permission-as-moat in regulated crypto custody, and Baud's patents-plus-compiler stack could plausibly become a similar non-permission moat in frontier training silicon. The case stings because Taylor and Zambare's combined six patents plus a working FPGA demo plus genuine PyTorch compatibility is the closest thing to "permission-as-moat" you can build in this category. It dies on category-timing: Anchorage was 2018 pre-consensus in institutional crypto, when MetaStable (2014) had been compounding my conviction for four years. Baud arrives in 2026 *after* Etched at $5B, *after* MatX at $500M Series B, *after* Taalas at $219M -- consensus has crystallized and Baud is the late entrant to a thesis I should have funded in 2023 if I were going to fund it at all. The verdict flips if Baud produces a third-party benchmark confirming the 100x claim against an NVIDIA H200 baseline *and* publishes a signed pilot with a named frontier lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, xAI) before the Series A -- absent both, this is a pass with respect.
