# Probe -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Probe is a category-right, leverage-wrong bet: a 3-person YC S26 team building agent-native incident management into a $14.34B IT/DevOps market that's real, but distributing through the same App Store of engineering buyers that already cut checks to **incident.io** ($96M+ raised, $9M ARR, $400M valuation -- TechCrunch April 2025). The market passes my pivot-room axiom. The leverage architecture does not. Mahit Namburu shipping LoRA inference at Databricks is a non-trivial specific-knowledge signal, but he's pointing it at a category where the buyer is the VP of Engineering, the integration surface is PagerDuty/GitHub/Slack, and the distribution motion is "founder-led direct sales" [Inferred] -- which is labor leverage requiring permission, not code or media leverage that compounds without it.

On the leverage dimension, the HIGH rung is **Twitter** (2007) -- media protocol, zero marginal cost, anyone posts, the platform charges nothing -- and Probe is nowhere near that mechanism. The dossier describes integrations into incumbent on-call stacks and a "proprietary knowledge base accumulating per-customer incident history," which is per-tenant data accretion, not permissionless distribution. The architecture Probe ships is the inverse of what I wrote checks for at **Stack Overflow** (2008) and **Replit** (April 2023): there is no public GitHub presence for Mahit, no open-source artifact pre-dating the round, no organic developer pull. Zero job postings and no Product Hunt launch tell me the team is heads-down -- fine -- but they also tell me there is no media leverage running on the founder identity itself, the way **Airchat** (April 2024) ran on Brian Norgard and me. Probe will need to buy distribution from the same channels incident.io already owns.

The LOW rung on the network dimension is the single-sided SaaS subscription where each customer is economically independent of the next -- the structural pattern absent from my **Uber** (2010) and **Stack Overflow** (2008) wins. Probe's knowledge base is exactly that pattern: incident corpus accrues per team, switching cost lives inside the customer, but the Nth customer does not make the (N-1)th more valuable. There is no cross-customer graph the way Stack Overflow's answer corpus compounded across every developer who searched. **Perplexity** (2022) was my Known Exception on this axis, and it cleared the override because criteria #1 and #3 both passed at top quartile -- Probe clears neither.

Counterfactual on founder-product-market fit (criterion #3): for Probe's score to jump a tier, Mahit Namburu would need either documented prior on-call/SRE operating experience or a shipped open-source artifact in the incident-triage retrieval space that pre-dates the YC batch. The dossier gives me Databricks LoRA fine-tuning and Apple machine translation -- real ML chops -- but the dossier also flags "no documented prior SRE/incident-response operating experience." That is an ML founder solving an SRE problem he has not lived. Aravind at **Perplexity** (2022) had Berkeley retrieval-PhD specificity aimed at the exact substrate he was building on. Mahit declining a Together AI MLE offer is energy and conviction; it is not specific knowledge of the domain he chose.

Mechanism analysis on market and pivot room: 15.74% CAGR through 2035 is genuine permission to maneuver, and the adjacencies -- AIOps, observability, autonomous code-repair, dev-agent infrastructure -- are real pivot vectors. Probe could credibly rotate into being a retrieval-grounded engineering-agent runtime if incident-management proves crowded. That is the "first idea never works" insurance the Venture Hacks essay (2007-10) was built around, and it is the one criterion where Probe genuinely scores above midpoint.

Single-anchor parallel on the integrity/10-year trio: my **Epinions** (1999-2005) partner litigation taught me to read the team composition before the pitch, and the Probe dossier discloses only Mahit Namburu by name for a 3-person company. Two unnamed teammates, no public equity split, no named CEO is the unresolved-authority pattern Venture Hacks flagged as the most-important-decision-still-pending. I do not read it as a red flag -- pre-seed teams often under-disclose -- but I cannot underwrite a 10-year partnership with two people whose names the dossier does not surface.

Forecast: in 36 months Probe is either acqui-hired by incident.io, Rootly, or PagerDuty for under $30M, or quietly dead, because the agent-native wedge gets compressed between incident.io's $62M AI-SRE warchest (April 2025) and the cannibalization-tolerant incumbents who can absorb per-seat ARR loss faster than a 3-person team can build distribution. The signature -- big market, weak leverage -- is the exact profile of a category where the funded incumbent wins by default unless the challenger ships a permissionless distribution mechanic the dossier does not describe.

The bear case I should steel-man against my own pass is this: incident.io and Rootly are workflow platforms re-skinning AI features onto human-led incident response, and incumbents structurally cannot rebuild around an autonomous agent without cannibalizing the seats that pay their salaries. Mahit's LoRA-inference specific knowledge is *exactly* the rare lever for shipping cheap, fast, fine-tuned triage agents that beat generic GPT-4 wrappers on cost and latency -- the innovator's-dilemma play. If Probe ships a measurable precision/recall lead on autonomous remediation within 12 months, the agent-native bet flips from "wrapper" to "category re-architect." It stings because it is the **Replit** (April 2023) shape of bet on a smaller stage. But the dossier shows zero shipped benchmarks, zero named customers, zero open-source artifact, and the **Sentrial** (YC W26) entry tells me YC itself is dollar-cost-averaging across multiple agent-failure bets in the same batch. The flip condition is concrete: a paying F500 engineering org logo plus a public retrieval/triage benchmark within 12 months, or an open-source agent framework with material GitHub traction. Until then, Probe is a category bet without a leverage architecture, and I do not write the check.
