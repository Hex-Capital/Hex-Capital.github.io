# Thomas -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Thomas is a media-leverage persona stapled to a code-leverage agent, run by a founder who automated his own $40k/month freelance income before deciding the next thing to automate was the founder seat itself -- the highest dimension here is permissionless distribution architecture, the lowest is network compounding, and that pairing is the entire investability question. The two-person "team" where one of the two is the AI persona is not a gimmick to me; it's a declaration that the founder understands the product IS the distribution surface, which is the **Airchat** (April 2024) move applied to operations instead of voice-social.

**Airchat** (April 2024) is the load-bearing analogy on the leverage axis: Brian Norgard and I built a media-native product where the founder identity was itself the channel, marginal cost of replication zero, no gatekeeper between post and audience. Thomas runs the same architecture one layer up -- the @madebythomasai handle and the LinkedIn /in/madebythomasai are not marketing surfaces for a separate product, they ARE the product's customer-facing instance, and the underlying agent is code-leverage compounding underneath the media-leverage layer. Where Thomas diverges from **Airchat** is that I cannot retrieve a follower count (WebFetch returned 402 on X, 999 on LinkedIn per the dossier), so the leverage architecture is unambiguously right but the leverage *fill rate* is unmeasured -- I am pricing the structure, not the traction.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the failure-mode precedent on the network axis, and it's also the override: a single-sided AI product where the Nth user does not make the (N-1) prior users meaningfully more valuable. Thomas reproduces that pattern -- no two-sided marketplace, no developer ecosystem, no creator graph in the dossier; the "companies he runs" do not network-compound with each other in any cited way. This is the same dimensional miss that almost cost me the **Perplexity** check, and the answer there was the three-of-five override: when permissionless distribution, specific knowledge, and category-timing all clear at top quartile, criterion #2 gets waived. Thomas qualifies for that same waiver, which is why the missing network mechanic does not kill the bet.

Founder bio on Thomas reads as lived specific knowledge, not pedigree-as-thesis: game bots from age 13, NeurIPS at 18, freelance scaled to $40k/month and then automated -- that last step is the **Notion** (2013) signature of Ivan Zhao having rebuilt personal productivity tools since adolescence before the company existed. The product premise (an AI that does the founder's work) is the same problem the founder already solved for himself once at smaller scale, which is the discovered-as-user origin pattern that **Stack Overflow** (2008) and **Replit** (April 2023) both ran. I do not need the YC page to give me a legal surname; I need it to give me a shipped artifact, and the $40k/month self-automation IS the shipped artifact.

Market sizing: 18 months out, the dimension that matters is whether "AI runs companies" is platform-shaped enough to absorb the inevitable pivots, and the answer is yes -- the adjacent vectors include agent-operator tooling for solo founders, persona-economy infrastructure, autonomous-ops middleware for agencies, and AI-customer-success per the same computer-use substrate. This is the inverse of the dental-SaaS niche red flag; the market is large enough that even three pivots stay inside the original runway. The Synthesia ($333M raised, ~$100M ARR per dossier), Adept ($415M), and Manus ($75M) comparables establish that capital wants this category, which I read as pivot-room insurance more than as competitive threat.

Counterfactual on the founder trio: for this dimension to flip from 9 to 13, I'd need to see Human Thomas under his legal name, with a public X history showing organic domain writing that predates the YC batch -- the **Replit** (April 2023) Amjad Masad pattern of years of public artifact. The anonymized "Human Thomas" framing is not a Clearview-style integrity red flag, but it is opacity I would normally do a coffee to resolve, and at pre-seed I am pricing for a 10-year relationship I cannot fully run the Buffett trio on yet. Energy and intelligence are loud in the bio; integrity is silent, and silence is neutral, not negative, per my default.

Forecast, 36 months: Thomas does not become "the first AI founder running a portfolio of companies" -- that framing is the persona, not the durable product. Thomas pivots into AI-agent-operations tooling sold to human solopreneurs and small agencies, lands in the $5-20M ARR band, and is either acquired by an agent-platform incumbent (Anthropic, OpenAI, or a Manus-class buyer) or raises a Series A from a fund that wants the persona-distributed GTM channel. The mechanism producing this outcome is exactly the dimensional signature: high media-leverage distribution carries the company through the first pivot, and the absence of network-compounding forces the pivot toward a tooling product where each customer is independently monetizable.

Bear case worth steelmanning: Thomas is a gimmick wrapped in a YC slot, the autonomous-operation tech does not exist at production reliability as of May 2026 (the dossier is explicit -- no cited demo, no MRR, no operated-company list on madebythomas.ai), and the "first AI founder" line is a pitch-deck flourish that will not survive contact with a real customer who needs a refund processed. The Red Flag closest to triggering is the brand collision -- madebythomas.com, madebythomas.dk, and "Thomas" as a search term is contested ground, which means the media-leverage thesis is operating with structural friction the dossier does not propose to solve. The bear case stings because the entire investability rests on a persona-distribution channel whose current reach I literally cannot measure. The dossier evidence that wins is that the founder already once executed the underlying playbook -- automated his own labor with code -- which is exactly the specific-knowledge gate **Notion** (2013) and **Replit** (April 2023) cleared before traction existed. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: if by month 12 the @madebythomasai account has not crossed roughly 50k engaged followers and the persona is not generating inbound from solopreneurs, the media-leverage thesis is broken and this becomes a generic agent product I would not have written at seed.
