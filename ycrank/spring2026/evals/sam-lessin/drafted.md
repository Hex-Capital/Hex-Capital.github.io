# Drafted -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Nick Donahue is a missionary-temperament founder on his second turn at the same residential-design problem, and Drafted is being priced like a hot YC consensus deal — $1.65M at $35M post with Patrick Collison, Ben Silbermann, Samsung, Jack Altman, Josh Buckley, Convective, Alex Blania, Kevin Mahaffey, Ryan Tedder and Moses Moody all stacked onto the SAFE. That high/low pairing — a founder I'd want to be in business with at a price I can't make money from — is Drafted's analytical signature, and it's the exact configuration where the discipline has to override the affection.

The reference rung on the price ladder is the canonical 2026 YC AI pitch from my Pass Example: $30M post, four tier-1 VCs already in, no room left for a seed investor to generate alpha from the SAFE cap. Drafted is *hotter* than that — thirteen named backers including two of the most reputation-bearing names in the Valley (Collison, Silbermann) and a strategic (Samsung), at a *higher* post on a *free* product. When the round itself is the marketing asset, the entry price has already absorbed the next two tacks of the regatta, and the Solana lens — buy at $0.05 when everyone has quit — inverts to "buy at $35M when nobody has quit." Drafted fails this filter on the dossier's most explicit datapoint, and the absence of any "tier-1 publicly passed" signal pins it to the bottom of the ladder, not the middle.

Atmos is the lowest rung on the capital-structure ladder I keep walking back to: $20M raised from Khosla and Sam Altman, 40 employees, $7M revenue, designed $200M of homes, shut down March 2025 because the operational layer never replaced the humans and rates broke the customer. Drafted's dossier reads as a deliberate inversion — pure software, 9 employees, 0.2¢ per generation versus 13¢ — but the monetization layer is *currently free PDFs and CAD downloads* with no revenue cited and the earlier $1,000-$2,000 per-plan reference now absent from drafted.ai. That is not yet a venture-shaped business; it is the Atmos pivot story still mid-tack, financed at a price that demands the tack has already completed. There is no non-standard instrument here, no royalty, no holdco — generic equity at a generic SAFE.

Donahue grew up in a homebuilding family, ran Atmos for ~5 years, watched the Fed kill his customers and chose to come back to the *same problem* with a different technical wedge — that is the "so many turns of the crank" temperament I screen for, and it's the strongest dimension Drafted has. The 18-month compounding question is whether the cumulative dataset of 325k floor plans per month produces a permitting-grade fidelity moat that Maket.ai's seven years in market never built; if Donahue ships a CAD export that a structural engineer signs without redlining, the conviction compounds into a real asset. The dossier is silent on his public writing, which costs him the top of the ladder, but the repeat-founder-on-same-problem signal is unambiguous and rare in this batch.

For Drafted to flip from a 6/15 to a 12/15 on the founder-as-brand axis, the 120k users-via-"socials and word-of-mouth" line would need to resolve to a specific owned channel — Donahue's @nickldawg account, a homebuilder Discord, a permitted-plan subreddit — with measurable engagement before the round. None of that is in the dossier; the @DraftedAI account at 9,399 followers since November 2025 is a company handle riding TechCrunch and Retrofit Home Magazine coverage, not founder-built distribution. Counterfactually: if Donahue published one opinionated essay on why every architect's billable hour is mispriced, Drafted moves a full tier on this dimension.

Allbirds is the single-anchor parallel on the category-sleeve dimension — a consumer product the SaaS crowd dismissed where I was the only fund in the category for years. Drafted is *not* that; Drafted is an AI-tooling layer on consumer real estate, which lands in the awkward seam between "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast, 2024) and the DTC sleeve, with none of the margin profile that made Allbirds underwriteable. The Maket.ai comparison in the dossier is the tell — a 2019-vintage Montreal competitor still in market at $2.47M raised tells me the category itself doesn't naturally venture-scale, regardless of Drafted's 65x inference-cost advantage.

My 36-month forecast: Drafted clears $4-9M ARR on a paid-CAD-export tier, raises a Series A at $80-120M anchored by Buckley or Collison's follow-on, and at a $35M entry the SAFE returns 2-3x to existing seed holders — a perfectly respectable outcome that does not pay for the misses in a Slow-shaped portfolio. The mechanism is Donahue's repeat-founder execution capacity meeting a real but rate-sensitive consumer demand curve; the ceiling is capped by Autodesk's eventual channel response and by the same mortgage-rate dynamic that killed Atmos.

The strongest objection is Pinterest: a Silicon Valley darling well before my check, where the operator-network sourcing and Silbermann's missionary temperament *overrode* my price discipline and produced a fund-defining outcome. Donahue has the Pinterest-style founder profile — family-trade obsession, second-time founder, weathered a public shutdown without going manic — and Silbermann is *literally on this cap table*, which is the closest thing to an operator-friend signal the dossier offers. The dismantle: the Pinterest override worked because I came in *through* the operator channel at a non-competitive price; Drafted's cap table is already saturated with the names that would normally source me into the deal, which means the channel has *closed* rather than opened, and the $35M post reflects exactly that closure. The verdict flips if Drafted reopens a structured secondary or a non-equity instrument — a royalty on per-plan revenue, a holdco wrapping Donahue's next two companies — at a price reflecting the actual asset rather than the consensus narrative. Until then, Drafted is a pass I'd happily revisit at the next tack.
