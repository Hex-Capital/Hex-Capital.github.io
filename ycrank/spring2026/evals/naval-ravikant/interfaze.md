# Interfaze -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Yoeven Khemlani is a second-time AI-API founder shipping a deterministic-output layer at $1.50 per million input tokens through an OpenAI-compatible endpoint, and Harsha Khurdula brings a Purdue computer-vision M.Sc. with a peer-reviewed paper on the architecture -- that is Interfaze's analytical signature: top-quartile specific knowledge welded to a single-sided API tool with no compounding-user-value mechanism. Builder-tool by builders, which is exactly the sleeve I quietly accumulate into. But Interfaze enters a category where Reducto, LlamaParse, Unstructured, and Mistral OCR are already funded and benchmarking publicly, which means the timing is post-consensus on the *category* even if the deterministic-output thesis is pre-consensus on the *architecture*.

Replit (April 2023) is the load-bearing analogy on leverage architecture -- Amjad Masad shipped a code-environment that distributed through permissionless developer pull, no SDR motion, no App Store gate, every dev with an API key was a self-serve user. Interfaze runs the same distribution shape: OpenAI Chat Completions compatibility means "any AI SDK that supports OpenAI works out of the box" per interfaze.ai, and Yoeven is co-hosting the Agents & APIs SF Developer Meetup on May 28, 2026, which is the founder-media-leverage move I recognize from Airchat. Where Interfaze diverges from the Replit rung is that Replit owned the runtime; Interfaze sits as a routing layer above frontier models whose token economics it must under-price *and* out-accurate simultaneously, and the $1.50/$3.50 per-million pricing is not meaningfully cheaper than the frontier it routes around.

The low rung is the single-sided AI app that lacks compounding user-added value -- the same axis where Perplexity (2022) only cleared the gate because three of five criteria passed at top quartile and category-timing was pre-consensus. Interfaze is structurally Perplexity-shaped on this axis: each Interfaze customer's API call does not make the next customer's call more valuable, there is no two-sided dynamic, no developer-to-developer compounding the way Stack Overflow's answers compounded across askers. The Perplexity exception held because Aravind Srinivas's Berkeley retrieval PhD plus pre-ChatGPT-scale timing reset the math; Interfaze has Harsha's vision-research depth but lands into a 2026 specialist-OCR market with five named funded competitors in the dossier, so the timing leg of the Perplexity override does not extend cleanly here.

Specific knowledge is where Interfaze scores hardest -- Yoeven's 8 years of edge-ML, his JigsawStack pre-seed at $1.5M from Antler and Ada Ventures, and a prior travel/property startup he took from $1M to $3M ARR before acquisition, plus Harsha's IEEE CAI 2026 paper acceptance. This is not an "ex-FAANG PM identified market opportunity" profile, which is the founder pattern I systematically discount. The mechanism over 18 months: deterministic-output evaluation is itself a specific-knowledge bet, because every percentage point of accuracy on dense tables compounds into enterprise procurement decisions, and the founders have shipped through this loop before at JigsawStack.

Counterfactually on market and pivot-room: Interfaze's TAM-shaped placement would flip a tier upward if the dossier named a concrete adjacent pivot vector -- agentic browser automation, structured-output for voice agents, vertical document AI for regulated industries -- that the team has already prototyped. Instead the dossier gives me five competitors (Reducto, LlamaParse, Mistral OCR, Unstructured, JigsawStack itself) all attacking the same OCR-and-structured-extraction wedge. The market is platform-shaped enough that Interfaze has room to pivot once, but the "first idea never works" axiom from Venture Hacks expects two-to-three pivots inside seed runway, and the crowded competitor map narrows the corridor.

Founder-concentration risk is the single dossier line that sits adjacent to a red flag without crossing it -- Yoeven is still publicly identified as JigsawStack's CEO, and JigsawStack's positioning ("unified AI API suite") overlaps Interfaze's positioning ("deterministic developer-task API"). I don't read this as integrity-leg failure -- there is no contested-exit pattern, no Clearview-style controversy, no self-promotion-about-ethics signal -- but the 10-year-partner test wants a single load-bearing identity, and Interfaze v. JigsawStack is two. Single dossier sentence would clear this: "JigsawStack wound down" or "Interfaze acquired JigsawStack's IP."

Forecast (36 months): Interfaze reaches $2-6M ARR as a specialist deterministic-output API serving mid-market document workflows, then either acquihires into Reducto or Unstructured for a 2-4x outcome, or Yoeven re-merges it with JigsawStack to consolidate the AI-API surface area. Not a power-law outcome in my portfolio's geometry, because the single-sided architecture caps the upside math at roughly 50-100x at the absolute high end, not the 1,000-10,000x that underwrites my asymmetric-payoff thesis.

The bull objection is real and stings: Reducto's own March 2025 benchmarks documented Gemini 2.0 Flash at ~80% and Mistral OCR at ~45% on RD-FormsBench, which means the deterministic-output gap is empirically measurable and a genuinely better specialist could ride enterprise document budgets into Unstructured-scale ($65M Series B) ARR territory. If Interfaze's self-reported 98-99% structured accuracy holds against an independent third-party benchmark, the math changes -- the Perplexity exception precedent applies and three gates clear. The dossier breaks the objection: the cited arxiv ID 2602.04101 is future-dated and the benchmark numbers are self-reported, with no Reducto-equivalent external validation cited. Verifiable flip condition: an independent benchmark (RD-FormsBench, OmniDocBench, or equivalent) published by a non-Interfaze party showing Interfaze beating Gemini 2.0 Flash by 10+ accuracy points on dense-table extraction. With that, I write the check and size up. Without it, Interfaze is a competent builder-tool in a crowded specialist market, and I pass on the architecture math, not the founders.
