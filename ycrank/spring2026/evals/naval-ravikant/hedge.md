# Hedge -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Hedge is a domain-credentialed services business dressed in the language of AI ? Luke Button has the highest specific-knowledge density I've seen in commercial insurance pre-seed (he ran Fernstone, YC F25, an AI insurance brokerage, before this), and yet Hedge has the weakest permissionless leverage architecture I've evaluated this batch. That pairing ? real founder-market fit fused to a structurally labor-leveraged wholesale brokerage operating as Taven Insurance Services LLC under CA license #6018729 ? is the entire story. Specific knowledge without leverage is consulting with better margins.

On specific knowledge, the ladder runs from Aravind Srinivas at Perplexity (years at Google Brain and OpenAI productized directly into AI search) at the top, through Notion's team in the middle (a real productivity insight but not unreplicable expertise), down to the AI-wrapper MBAs I always pass on (anyone can call an API). Hedge sits closer to Notion than to Perplexity. Button's Fernstone tenure and Luke Rosa's "$20MM+ in revenue managed by AI agents" line at Traba are real domain credentials ? but Fernstone launched in YC's F25 batch, which is months of operating experience, not Srinivas's years of obsessive engagement. Button knows what an ACORD form is and what a stamping-office state is, and that already puts Hedge above the AI-wrapper floor; it does not put it near a portfolio anchor.

On permissionless leverage, the ladder runs from Twitter (pure code, hundreds of millions of users on minimal marginal cost) at the top, through Uber in the middle (network leverage with real operations drag), down to Poshmark and Wish (marketplace shells that needed armies and collapsed against the leverage criterion). Hedge sits below Uber and roughly alongside Poshmark. The dossier tells me exactly what Hedge is: a licensed surplus-lines broker with 30+ direct carrier relationships, "named brokers per account," and a $125K?$200K Founding Underwriter open requisition. That is labor leverage with a regulatory permission slip wrapped around it ? the worst form Charlie would recognize, gated by capital (carrier appointments) and labor (underwriters) at every step. The "AI native" tagline lives at the intake layer; the P&L lives in commissions paid per bound policy by a human broker. Software was eaten by AI ? but commission-based wholesale brokerage was not.

On principal authenticity, my ladder runs from Sahil Lavingia (kept coding Gumroad alone after Series A failure) at the top, through the Notion team's rebuild in the middle, down to founders who pick the idea because it's fundable. Hedge concerns me here. Button is publicly listed as CEO of Fernstone (F25) AND founder of Hedge (S26); Fernstone's site is still live; the relationship is undisclosed. Two AI-insurance-brokerage YC entries within twelve months is either obsession or agent-pattern ? someone optimizing for what gets funded. Signals are what people do, despite what they say. The dossier cannot tell me which one this is, so I anchor below the default.

Team-to-value leverage and access democratization compound the leverage problem. Against Midjourney's ~163 employees doing $500M and Cursor's <50 doing $500M ARR at the top of the ladder, and Poshmark's headcount-coupled marketplace at the bottom, Hedge is structurally Poshmark-shaped: every new state requires licensing, every new carrier requires an appointment, and every new book requires an underwriter ? that's why the open role exists. On democratization, AngelList and CoinList stripped out gatekeepers (connected angels, securities lawyers); Hedge inserts itself as a faster intermediary between retail agents and carriers. 140+ retail agencies in 36 states is real distribution, but it's distribution to other intermediaries, not access expansion to a previously gatekept population.

My 36-month forecast: Hedge reaches $8?20M in annual commission revenue, employs 30?60 people across underwriting and licensing, and is acquired by Amwins, CRC, or Ryan Specialty as a tech-forward bolt-on for under $100M ? never reaching venture-scale outcomes because the leverage architecture is services, not software. The mechanism is exactly the one the dossier already describes: a funded incumbent bolts AI ingestion onto its existing book and Hedge's workflow speed advantage compresses to the ten percent that survives, while AM Best's stable-to-softening 2026 E&S outlook squeezes commissions on the way through.

The bull case is Vouch: a vertical-insurance founder built a venture-scale outcome by accumulating proprietary loss data and underwriting relationships into a structural moat, and Naval's Known Exception for Wish proves the leverage criterion can sometimes carry a category. But Wish's exception was retrospectively a mistake ? marketplace leverage without specific knowledge collapsed 95% from IPO. Hedge has the inverse profile (specific knowledge without leverage), and that's the profile I most consistently pass on, because compound interest can't run when each new dollar of revenue requires a new dollar of headcount. The condition that flips my verdict: Hedge demonstrates that its AI intake layer has produced a delegated underwriting authority (MGA) with proprietary loss-ratio outperformance against carrier book ? a data flywheel I can point to ? not just faster broker turnaround. That moves Hedge from intermediary speedup to principal underwriter with a code-leveraged moat. The dossier shows no such thing today. I pass.
