# Chert -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Chert is a shipping-credible founder pair stranded on a hostage protocol: Ian Fong's 8x-hackathon-winner profile and Gary Gao's neuromorphic-chip research year are the only above-baseline signals in a dossier that otherwise describes a thin wrapper on Apple's iMessage in a category Sendblue has been working for five-plus years without breaking out. The high/low pairing matters more than the aggregate -- real builders, structurally non-defensible position -- and that pairing is what determines whether Chert is a 36-month acqui-hire or a pivot.

Anduril (2022) is the comparison I keep wanting to make on Chert's market-catalyst dimension, and it's exactly the comparison that fails. Anduril sat in a 24-month re-opening with a named catalyst (Ukraine + Pentagon software inadequacy), software-on-hardware single-miracle architecture, and founders who'd already shipped Palantir's forward-deployed motion. Chert's nearest claim to a catalyst is "LLM conversational quality crossed a threshold," which is real but is the same catalyst available to Sendblue, LoopMessage, Blooio, and Linq -- the dossier explicitly notes Sendblue has been in iMessage-API for 5+ years and holds the feature-breadth lead. Chert is not entering a re-opened market; Chert is entering an already-contested wrapper market where the dominant player has not broken out, which is the *opposite* signal from Anduril's defense-post-Ukraine setup. There is no named technological step-function or regulatory shift in the Chert dossier that opened a window in the last 24 months -- there is only LLMs-make-outbound-better, which everyone has.

Lyft Series C is the failure mechanism I want to flag on Chert's single-miracle architecture: structural dependency on a counterparty I don't control whose interests are not aligned with mine. Lyft was #2 in a WTA network market; Chert is one of five wrappers on Apple's iMessage, a protocol Apple can deprecate, formalize, or directly serve at any moment. The dossier names this as "Apple platform dependency" and notes "no public mitigation found beyond SMS/RCS fallback." That's not a single miracle; that's at least three independent ones -- (1) Apple doesn't shut the unofficial APIs, (2) outbound at scale doesn't get line-banned despite Chert's own claim of "comprehensive line health checks" being externally unverified, (3) Chert beats Sendblue on a feature surface Sendblue has been compounding for half a decade. Each miracle's probability is fine in isolation; the product is what fails me.

On revenue velocity -- mechanism analysis. Chert discloses zero revenue numbers, no MRR, no pricing page, sales gated to a cal.com booking, and the only quantitative claims are "up to 65% response rates and 90% action rates" with no third-party validation and no named case study. Ten logos on the site (Whop is the only one I recognize as a real distribution surface) and 179 Product Hunt upvotes is launch theater, not revenue compounding. In an AI cycle where "if you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss #863, April 2026), the silence on growth at the moment of YC Launch HN is itself the signal -- real growth is easy to disclose. Eighteen months from now this either becomes the Harvey curve made visible or it doesn't, and the absence at launch makes the latter the base rate.

On founder shipping -- single-anchor parallel to Perplexity. Aravind Srinivas pinged me cold on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with a built thing; that is the pattern I underwrite. Chert's Ian Fong has 8 hackathon wins across UPenn/MIT/Harvard/Princeton and built fast-algorithm research at Courant, and Gary Gao spent the prior year on ML architectures before the pair "spent the last six months building projects on iMessage for leasing companies, DTC startups, and home service agencies." That is genuine iterative shipping with feedback loops in the target use case -- not Aravind-level pattern but materially above the median pre-seed YC founder who lists "ex-FAANG" and nothing built. This is the one dimension where Chert is meaningfully above default.

On index position -- counterfactual. For Chert to score in the upper tier here, the company would have to sit in the workflow path that every entrant in a *compounding* category must touch, the way Stripe is an index on e-commerce. The compounding category here would have to be "conversational agents on consumer messaging," and the index position would have to be CRM-write-back-plus-deliverability-reputation data accumulated across all customers. The dossier's named CRM integrations (HubSpot, Close, GoHighLevel, Attio, Vapi) gesture at this, but Chert's "index" is on a single Apple-controlled channel, not on the underlying category -- which makes it a thin position, not a Stripe-shaped one. The score would flip a tier only if Chert demonstrated multi-channel agent infrastructure where iMessage is one of several rails, but the dossier says the opposite: iMessage is the product.

My 36-month forecast on Chert: acqui-hired by a CPaaS or CRM player (Twilio, HubSpot, or Sendblue itself) for sub-$25M, or pivoted away from iMessage-primary into multi-channel agent infrastructure -- standalone scaling past $5M ARR is unlikely because Apple platform risk plus Sendblue's five-year feature lead caps the upside, and Fong and Gao are talented enough to be valuable hires before the cap binds. This forecast is consistent with the signature: real shippers + hostage-protocol position = the talent gets harvested, the surface area gets absorbed.

The strongest bull case against this: my own *AI Market Clarity* essay (Nov 2025) lists categories that are "still wide open" precisely because no one has broken out, and iMessage-API has had five years and four-plus competitors with no breakout -- so by that logic Chert plus LLM agents could be the entrant that finally compounds, with the CRM-write-back wedge (HubSpot/Close/GoHighLevel) as the real product, not the iMessage layer. Fong is a serial builder; Gao did neuromorphic chip work; the dossier shows six months of paid-pilot iteration with leasing/DTC/home-services customers; YC + Z Fellows + Betafund is a clean cap table. The dismantling: "wide open" in my taxonomy means a *new* market without a clear winner; iMessage-API is an *old* market without a clear winner, which is the failure pattern, not the opportunity pattern -- Sendblue's failure to break out after five years is evidence the ceiling is structural (Apple), not competitive. **Flip condition**: Chert discloses $50K+ MRR with >30% month-over-month growth across three of the listed logos within six months, AND ships a multi-channel agent layer that materially reduces iMessage dependency. Until then, this is a pass, and I'd revisit at Series A if the curve appears.
