# Praxis AI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Praxis AI is a rev-share brokerage wearing an AI-data-startup costume — the "turns every company into a data vendor" tagline is genuinely the most interesting sentence in the dossier, and Rohan Seelamsetty, Dev Karpe, and Tommy Li are three Penn-flavored operators without a single piece of public writing between them on why humanoid data supply belongs inside conglomerates rather than on Micro1's Ray-Ban gig fleet. The structural insight and the founder legibility are pointing in opposite directions, which is what determines whether I can price this at all.

Start with the structure, because that's where Praxis AI is most interesting. **Teamshares** is my anchor for the "financing instrument no other fund can underwrite" trade — an employee-ownership rollup that reads like a services business to a SaaS-trained VC and reads like a captive equity engine to me. Praxis AI's "60k workers across 5 continents and 150+ environment types" embedded inside publicly listed conglomerates is structurally the same move: the host companies are the supply-side asset, and the right instrument is probably a revenue-share on captured hours plus a warrant on the conglomerate embed, not a standard SAFE. The divergence from Teamshares is that Praxis AI has disclosed zero terms — no partner names, no contract length, no exclusivity clause, no take-rate — so I can't tell whether the founders see the structure they're sitting on or whether they're pitching it as vanilla equity into a hot data-labeling comp set. Without those disclosures, the Teamshares analogy is aspirational rather than underwritable.

The canonical 2026 YC pitch I pass on — "$25K MRR AI agent, $30M post, four tier-1s in" — fails Criterion 4 by having no public writing and no owned community, and that failure mechanism appears in Praxis AI in almost identical form. Rohan Seelamsetty has an X handle @ro_seelamsetty that isn't even retrievable for follower count, Dev Karpe has no discoverable public account, and Tommy Li — the strongest technical resume, ex-a16z industrial-tech and a NASA JPL/PerceptIn IEEE researcher — has published exactly nothing publicly about physical-AI data supply. The Praxis AI dossier confirms three finance/consulting-adjacent operators (Huntsman, Morgan Stanley PE, F500 CEO office) who look nothing like John Fish's Bookshelved distribution engine and nothing like the "Writing is thinking" filter I apply before every meeting.

On Criterion 1, the counterfactual for a tier flip is precise: I'd need one named tier-1 fund to have publicly passed on Praxis AI's category, plus a sub-$10M post disclosed, for this to clear the value-seed price test. The dossier is silent on both, and the adjacent category is the exact opposite of quiet — Scale AI at $29B with Meta's $14.3B check, Encord raising $60M Series C in February 2026, Micro1 fielding $2.5B offers on $100M ARR. Praxis AI is being underwritten into a data-supply wave the consensus crowd has explicitly re-entered, which is the mirror image of my Solana entry at $0.05.

Criterion 3 compounds the wrong direction over 18 months. The mechanism a conglomerate-embed business needs is a missionary founder who will spend three years walking F500 procurement floors, negotiating data-rights clauses through legal, and eating labor/privacy pushback from works councils in five countries — the "so many turns of the crank" Venmo temperament. Praxis AI's founding trio has real firepower on the buy-side (Tommy Li's a16z industrial-tech seat is genuinely useful) and real firepower on the ops-side (Dev Karpe's F500 CEO-office supply-chain role maps directly to the embed GTM), but I see no evidence any of them has been obsessed with humanoid data capture for three-plus years — Rohan Seelamsetty's Apollo Fellowship was on AI existential risk, which is a category away, and the team's median resume is investment banking and PE. That's a mercenary temperament for a missionary contract cycle.

Criterion 5 is the one where I have to be honest that Praxis AI is closer to my Pass Example than to any portfolio anchor. **Ro** is my override for "capital-intensive category as venture moment" — regulatory schleps across 50-state prescription rails were themselves the moat. Praxis AI's schlep is contract-locking conglomerates for worker-captured video, which is a real moat if it happens, but the buy-side is Physical Intelligence, Generalist AI, and Figure — a small set of frontier labs Scale AI already sells to, and "AI is just more internet" reweights me away from paying consensus prices for exposure to that buyer set no matter how creative the supply channel is.

Thirty-six months from now Praxis AI is either an acqui-hire into Scale AI or Micro1 at sub-$50M for the conglomerate relationships, or a single-partner captive data subsidiary earning $5–15M in per-hour licensing from one F500 host — it does not reach $20M ARR as an independent multi-conglomerate broker, because the founders' finance-flavored resumes and zero public conviction footprint won't out-execute Alexandr Wang or Micro1's gig-ops team on the multi-year contract arc that the embed model actually requires.

The strongest bull case against that forecast is the Teamshares override in disguise: if Praxis AI has already signed even one exclusive multi-year data-supply agreement with a named publicly listed conglomerate — the dossier's phrase "embedded within publicly listed and unicorn-scale conglomerates" is doing enormous unverified work — then the switching cost for that conglomerate to migrate to Scale AI's gig-recruit model is prohibitive, and Praxis AI becomes the only channel to a 60k-worker industrial capture surface Scale can't replicate without dismantling its San Francisco R&D lab. That would be a genuine venture moment on the Ro precedent. The dossier doesn't clear that bar — no partner name, no contract term, no exclusivity clause, no take-rate — and the website praxisrobotics.io displays only a tagline. Flip condition is verifiable: a disclosed multi-year exclusive supply contract with a named F500 or unicorn conglomerate at stated unit economics. Until then Praxis AI is a structurally interesting brokerage priced into a consensus wave, and I pass.
