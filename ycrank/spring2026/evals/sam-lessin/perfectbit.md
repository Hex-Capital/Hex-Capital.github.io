# PerfectBit -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

PerfectBit is two ex-Meta Superintelligence Labs directors — Peter Vajda and Seiji Yamamoto — selling "verifier-grounded" training data into a buyer pool of five to ten frontier labs they personally know, which gives PerfectBit the highest founder-domain-conviction signal in this dossier and the lowest category-sleeve fit, because the sleeve in question is the exact one I publicly de-rate: "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024). That high/low pairing is the entire analytical signature here — credentialed insiders attacking the most picked-over slice of the 2026 YC batch.

Solana at $0.05/coin is the right ladder reference for the category sleeve, and PerfectBit is the inverse. The mechanism that made Solana load-bearing was buying a category after the consensus crowd had quit — most generalist funds had walked away from crypto post-ICO crash, so the round was non-consensus on entry and the price reflected it. PerfectBit sits on the opposite end of that mechanism: "training data for frontier AI labs" is the most fashionable infra sleeve a 2026 YC seed can occupy, Scale just took $14.3B from Meta at a $29B mark, Mercor quintupled to $10B in October 2025, and Snorkel closed a $100M Series D — there is no category abandonment to exploit. PerfectBit is selling picks-and-shovels into the gold rush every Sand Hill seed fund is already underwriting at the same multiples.

The canonical YC AI-agent pitch from my own pass example — "$25K MRR, three months out of YC, $30M post-money, four tier-1 VCs already in" — is the failure mode for the founder-as-brand dimension, and PerfectBit walks straight into it. Vajda's Twitter identity is unconfirmed, Yamamoto has no public account found, neither has retrievable GitHub repos, there is no Product Hunt launch, no press coverage, and the only owned channel is a LinkedIn page with a count "not retrievable." This isn't a creator-shaped asset I can structure around — there is no John Fish/Bookshelved analogue here, no newsletter, no opinionated category essay from either Vajda or Yamamoto that I can read before the meeting. The "Writing is thinking" filter (Antoine Buteau, ~2023) returns zero artifacts.

Round-pricing mechanism over 18 months: PerfectBit is Garry Tan's batch, the tagline "Training data for frontier AI labs" is engineered to land in three tier-1 inboxes by demo day, and ex-Meta Superintelligence directors selling into Meta's own competitive set will trigger a SAFE bidding war that compresses entry into precisely the consensus pricing my "VERY price sensitive" filter rejects. The dossier is silent on round size — that silence is itself the signal under my absent-evidence default, because dossiers with discount-priced rounds usually say so. The compounding failure mode is that each subsequent tack in the regatta gets marked up by the AI-infra momentum trade, not by a real Series A buyer underwriting the underlying revenue.

Capital structure counterfactual: for PerfectBit to flip into the venture-shaped tier, it would need either an unusual instrument — a per-verified-domain royalty against a named lab's eval-set, a Teamshares-style holdco rolling up multiple verifier-domain shops, something — or a credible default-alive path. Neither is present. A two-person team selling bespoke datasets into five-to-ten frontier labs with "$1.5 million per day" contractor unit economics on the incumbent side (TechCrunch, Oct 2025) means PerfectBit will need to staff a domain-engineering org, a research org, and a sales org against Scale, Surge, Mercor, Snorkel, and Micro1 simultaneously. That is a five-round burn profile, not a regatta with underwritable tacks.

Founder conviction parallel: Vajda led Media Generation at Meta Superintelligence and Yamamoto worked Core Llama pre-training, post-training, and inference — this is the same shape as **Sublime Security** in my early-check list, where ex-defenders attacked the category they had just lived inside. The conviction is real but it lives entirely on the CV, not in public artifacts. Sublime had operators-from-inside plus an enterprise security sleeve that the late-stage SOC-SaaS crowd was actively ignoring; PerfectBit has operators-from-inside plus a sleeve that every fund is racing into. The asymmetry that made Sublime work is missing.

PerfectBit in 36 months: acqui-hired into Anthropic, OpenAI, or back to Meta for $30–80M, with the verifier-grounded pipeline folded into the acquirer's internal RL-from-verifiable-rewards team; standalone ARR does not cross $50M because FOVER (arXiv 2505.15960, May 2025) and RLVR (arXiv 2505.04842) have already lowered the build-vs-buy line below the threshold where labs outsource this. The mechanism is structural — labs that pulled work from Scale (TechCrunch, Oct 2025) pulled it to bring training-data control in-house, not to route it through a different vendor, and PerfectBit is selling exactly the workflow labs have the strongest reason to own.

The bull case that stings: this is the **Sublime Security** analogue where two senior operators from inside the buyer build the infrastructure their former employer's org dysfunction can't ship, and "lost lab customers from Scale post-Meta" is the exact dislocation that creates a venture moment for a credible alternative — Vajda and Yamamoto are arguably the most-credentialed founders any verifier-data startup will field this cycle. The bear evidence that wins: labs aren't switching vendors, they're internalizing the function, and the published academic methods mean any well-resourced lab can reproduce PerfectBit's pipeline with a quarter of engineering work. PerfectBit flips to invest if Vajda and Yamamoto announce a signed >$5M ACV contract with a named frontier lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, xAI, Google DeepMind) before the Series A, on a non-standard instrument — a per-domain royalty or eval-set revenue share — that I can underwrite as a structure no other fund will touch. Absent that, PerfectBit is a consensus-priced bet in a consensus sleeve, and my alpha doesn't live here.
