# Ara -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Adi Singh and Sven Myhre are pitching me a Mac-native AI cursor that lives in the notch — I can download Ara, watch the cursor move across my own screen, hand it to a friend in 48 hours. That tactile observability is real, and it's the one rung of my ladder where Ara climbs high. The trouble is the category sitting underneath the notch: Anthropic Claude Computer Use, OpenAI Codex Background Computer Use (launched April 16, 2026), and Google Gemini Computer Use from Project Mariner are all shipping the exact primitive Ara wraps. So the dimensional signature is *tactile artifact wrapping a consensus primitive* — the inverse of the Crusoe shape I look for, where the artifact was strange and the category was empty.

Flock Safety is my anchor for physical-world verifiability — I read Garrett's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café and the product existed in the wild before he ever pitched me. Ara loads the same way on this one axis: a Mac app I can install, a cursor I can watch click through Gmail, a notch UI I can photograph. Where Ara diverges from Flock is what surrounds the artifact — Flock cameras went on streetlamps in suburbs where no VC was looking, while Ara's cursor moves across the same desktop that Anthropic and OpenAI are already automating from inside the model layer. The artifact passes my hand-test; the *category around the artifact* does not.

The Pass Example I wrote about the consensus YC AI-agent darling — two ex-FAANG PMs, oversubscribed seed, $200B TAM slide, problem discovered in a memo — is the failure mechanism I have to test Ara against directly. Tracing the pattern through Ara's dossier: Adi Singh is a Berkeley Math/CS undergrad who built Dereference, a Rust IDE; Sven Myhre is a Berkeley/NTNU EECS student who won the Norwegian AI Championship 2025. Neither one has *been* the knowledge worker drowning in cross-app SaaS toil that Ara claims to liberate. They discovered this problem the way a polymath generalist discovers any problem in 2026 — by noticing that computer-use models crossed a usability threshold and wrapping a UX around them. That is analytical sourcing, not autobiographical sourcing, and Palmer Luckey shipping Oculus from his parents' garage before Anduril is not the comparison Ara earns.

Ara on the magically-weird axis compounds the wrong direction over 18 months. Crusoe in 2018 was three uninvestable things stapled together and the laughter was the moat — by the time consensus arrived, Chase Lochmiller had already walked the well pads. Ara is the opposite trajectory: the dossier flags Ara is on its *fourth* Product Hunt launch under shifting taglines ("Make your Mac an AI supercomputer" on ara.so, "Voice-first computer use for Mac" on YC, "Open source computer use to replace Openclaw" on PH), which is the cadence of a team relaunching into a category that keeps getting more crowded, not less. Granola just raised $125M Series C at $1.5B in March 2026 doing the *adjacent* Mac-resident motion. The round Ara is in will not be underpriced.

Counterfactual on capital-intensity: for Ara to flip a tier on my unsexy-regulated dimension, Ara would have to be doing computer-use against a domain where the regulator, not the foundation model, is the gate — clinical EHR automation, broker-dealer back-office, defense IL5 environments. Ara today is a horizontal Mac wrapper with 1,000+ generic integrations (Gmail, Slack, Notion, GitHub), which is the most commoditizable possible surface; Mindbloom's ketamine moat or Vigil Labs's regulated-finance moat have no analogue in the Ara dossier.

HQ Trivia is the single anchor for governance: Yusupov and Kroll were a 50/50 partnership that detonated when no one could call the tiebreaker, and I cited that failure in my own framework. Ara's dossier titles Adi Singh as "Founder" (singular) and Sven Myhre as "Co-founder, CTO," which is the cleaner structural read than HQ Trivia had on day one — Adi reads as CEO by title even though no operating agreement is documented. That's enough to keep Ara off the HQ Trivia rung but not enough to score it high; the documented vesting and conflict protocol I want to see does not exist in the dossier.

My 36-month forecast on Ara: acqui-hired by Anthropic or OpenAI for under $20M for the notch-UX team and the Mac accessibility plumbing, before reaching $2M ARR, with the trigger being Apple shipping native "Actions" in Apple Intelligence or OpenAI making Codex Background Computer Use free in the ChatGPT Mac app. The mechanism is straightforward — Ara's value lives entirely in the layer between the LLM and the OS, and both the LLM lab above and the OS vendor below are economically motivated to collapse that layer themselves.

The strongest bull case against my pass routes through the HQ Trivia exception: tactile, addictive product-led traction in a consumer artifact can override every other heuristic on my sheet, and Ara is precisely the kind of thing I could *feel* go viral if it actually did. Sven winning Norway's national AI championship plus Adi's claim that Dereference hit 2k users and 300k visits in 24 hours could be read as a DeepMind-shaped polymath bet. I don't buy it — the HQ Trivia override required virality I could measure in my own hand within a week (I was playing it on the subway), and Ara's traction section reads "no user/revenue/follower counts public" with a fourth Product Hunt relaunch as the headline signal, which is the cadence of a team searching for traction rather than riding it. The DeepMind exception required Hassabis-grade cross-domain compounding (chess prodigy + neuroscientist + game designer), not a student AI championship. The single verifiable condition that would flip my verdict on Ara: 100K weekly active Mac users with >40% week-4 retention by month six, sourced organically without a paid push — show me Ara going viral the way HQ went viral and I will write the check on the spot.
