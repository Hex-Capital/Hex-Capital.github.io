# flowscope -- Paul Graham Evaluation

flowscope is a company that has correctly identified a schlep but has not yet shown me it will do the schlep. Two founders — Javier Leguina out of Model ML (YC W24) and Encord, Samuel Mirpuri out of McKinsey QuantumBlack and HBS — pitching "AI agents shadow employees for two weeks" into mid-market CFO/COO accounts. The dossier names no shipped artifact, no pilot customer, no pre-YC stunt revenue. That asymmetry — high on schlep recognition, low on animal evidence — is flowscope's signature, and it is the exact pattern where I most often misread a company in either direction.

On the animal-resourcefulness ladder, **Airbnb** (2009) sits at the top with cereal boxes funding the company at $40 a box; **Dropbox** (2007) sits in the middle with Drew Houston's demo video on Hacker News before the seed check; the polished MBA AI-wrapper team from my pass example sits at the bottom with a deck and no shipped artifact. flowscope lives between the middle and bottom rung: Mirpuri's HBS/QuantumBlack credentials and Leguina's prior-startup employment are not the same thing as a stunt or a shipped demo, and the dossier explicitly logs "no revenue, customers, press" and `Hiring: False` as of May 2026. Animals leave tracks; the absence of tracks is itself the read. The Model ML lineage is real but it is a *prior employer* signal, not a flowscope-specific resourcefulness signal.

On the organic-idea ladder, **Dropbox** (2007) sits at the top because Houston actually forgot his USB stick at MIT; **Stripe** (2010) sits at the top with the Collisons hacking commerce since **Auctomatic** (2007); the MBA AI-wrapper pass sits at the bottom doing "competitive analysis." flowscope lands in the middle: Mirpuri lived inside QuantumBlack and presumably watched McKinsey deliver slide decks where shipped automation would have been more useful, but the dossier never says he was personally enraged by a specific engagement, only that the offering "maps directly" to the pairing. That is founder-market fit by resume, not founder-market fit by scar tissue. The two are not the same, and the difference shows up later when the schlep gets unpleasant.

On the schlep ladder, **Coinbase** (2012)'s bitcoin compliance grind sits at the top; **DoorDash** (2013)'s last-mile operations sits in the middle; the polished AI-wrapper pass sits at the bottom as pure software. flowscope is closer to the top than I initially expected — shadowing employee workflows requires email/ERP/ticketing access, GDPR review, works-council clearance, and enterprise procurement cycles that the dossier calls out explicitly. trust.flowscope.com exists. This is the kind of work other Spring 2026 batch teams unconsciously route around because it looks like "services" rather than "software," and that flinching is exactly what made Alexandr Wang's reading of my January 2012 essay seminal for Scale AI.

On the hacker-genealogy ladder, **Stripe** (2010)'s Patrick Collison winning Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp sits at the top; **Reddit** (2005)'s Swartz/Huffman/Ohanian dorm hackers sit at the top alongside; the credential-only CS-degree founder sits at the bottom. flowscope's Leguina has 8 GitHub repos, MSc Machine Learning from UCL, and shipped ML work at Encord and Model ML — that is a real hacker pin. Mirpuri has no GitHub, no shipped open source, and the bio reads HBS/MIT/Imperial plus Singapore Armed Forces platoon commander. The pair is half-hacker, half-consultant, which is coherent for the offering but is not the Collison-brothers-both-hack profile. On the co-founder configuration ladder, **Airbnb** (2009)'s three-person Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk trio sits at the top; founders-who-met-in-college sit in the middle; hackathon pairs sit at the bottom. flowscope's two founders share Imperial College London as the only confirmed acquaintance vector — the dossier says "likely prior acquaintance," which is inference, not the dorm-roommate or sibling-relationship anchor I prefer.

Thirty-six months from now I expect flowscope is at $2M–$5M in services revenue, still 4–8 people, and gets acquihired by either a big-four AI practice (Deloitte AI, QuantumBlack itself) or by a platform vendor like Celonis or UiPath buying agent-services capability to bolt onto AgentC. That outcome is consistent with the signature: the schlep is real enough to generate revenue, but the absence of an animal moment makes the run-it-to-public-company outcome less likely than the run-it-to-Mirpuri's-old-employer outcome. This is the **Kiko** (2005) eBay-sale shape, not the **Twitch** (2007) shape.

The strongest case against my pass is this: Mirpuri's QuantumBlack rolodex into Fortune 500 CFOs is not a normal pre-seed asset, and **Stripe** (2010) was a Known Exception where I bent the YC-process rule because the Collisons had already proven they could ship and sell. One could argue Leguina-from-Model-ML plus Mirpuri-from-McKinsey is the equivalent founder-quality override, and that the $5M Auctomatic precedent licenses me to weight prior credibility heavily. The objection stings because services businesses with warm-intro distribution into mid-market CFOs *do* get to $5M ARR quickly and that *is* what looks like animal behavior in retrospect. The dossier wins the argument anyway: Leguina was a *founding engineer at* Model ML, not a founder of it, and Mirpuri's animal moment is asserted by inference, not by a named cereal-box equivalent. One verifiable signal would flip this: a named mid-market customer logo with disclosed ACV >$100K or a shipped automation artifact I can inspect. Until then, pass.
