# Amulet -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Amulet is two ex-Meta ads engineers, Nithik Bala and Varun Puru, pointing agents at Gainsight's category — and the thing that jumps off the page is that the founders themselves have never been the customer. Amulet's highest dimension is cofounder configuration (a clean pair with overlapping Meta tenure), and its lowest is organic founder-problem fit (neither has ever staffed an account or written a save-motion). That pairing describes a specific archetype: two competent hackers who picked a market off a Medium review, not two people scratching their own itch. The dossier reinforces this — amulet.so returns HTTP 403, no GitHub trails, no demo video, no Product Hunt hit, no revenue disclosed.

On animal resourcefulness, the reference rung is **Airbnb** (2009) and the cereal boxes — Chesky and Gebbia sold Obama O's at $40 a box to fund the company when no investor would. That is what pre-funding hustle looks like when it's real. Amulet's dossier contains no analog: no Hacker News demo like Drew Houston's for **Dropbox** (2007), no rejected-then-resubmitted prototype like Brian Armstrong's Android wallet for **Coinbase** (2012), no scrappy revenue stunt, no unscalable manual pilot with a named logo. What Amulet shows me instead is a landing page that 403s to research bots and two LinkedIn snippets. Animals leave tracks; I don't see any tracks around Amulet yet, and absence in this dimension I treat as evidence against, not neutral.

**Dropbox** (2007) is the rung for organic-idea fit — Houston kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT and built the tool he wanted for himself. The failure mechanism at the low rung is the MBA-AI-wrapper pattern from my pass example: founders who selected a market rather than lived inside one. Amulet exhibits that pattern almost cleanly. Nithik Bala's documented experience is Core Ads Growth and Web Infra at Meta; Varun Puru's is Facebook product engineering plus Amazon backend and J.P. Morgan cloud internships. Neither has been a CSM, a RevOps lead, or a post-sales operator. The Amulet product — draft customer-specific outreach that a human CSM sends unedited — requires exactly the customer-facing judgment neither founder has demonstrated pulling ads dashboards at Meta.

Schlep-wise, Amulet is doing the opposite of what **Coinbase** (2012) did. Armstrong ran toward BitLicense grind that scared every other applicant in his batch; the compliance schlep became the moat. Amulet's work — wire up CRM, support, and product-usage APIs, run an LLM over the joined table, draft messages — is the schlep-free path. Staircase AI already built this, sold to Gainsight in July 2024 for a seed-stage exit, and I read that as the market telling me the technical barrier is a few months of engineering, not a moat. Over 18 months this dimension compounds against Amulet: as Vitally's AI Copilot and Gainsight's Agentforce-plus-Staircase layer ship, the agent-native pitch stops being a wedge and starts being a checkbox.

On hacker genealogy, the counterfactual is what would flip Amulet from a 5 to a 10 on this axis: a public GitHub with a non-trivial repo, a competitive-programming placement, an open-source contribution to LangChain or an agent framework, a Lisp or Rust side project that predates the company. None of that surfaces in the dossier for either Nithik Bala or Varun Puru — both read as technical-by-credential (Georgia Tech, Michigan, Meta SWE) rather than hackers with a public trail. The **Reddit** (2005) parallel would be Aaron Swartz co-authoring RSS before the company existed; there is no equivalent artifact for Amulet.

Cofounder configuration is where Amulet scores its best. It's a two-person team with documented Meta overlap, which is closer to the **Stripe** (2010) two-hacker mold than to the solo founder I explicitly avoid per "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010). It's not the Collison brothers — no prior startup together, no shared multi-year building history disclosed — but it's a real pair, not a hackathon marriage, and I'll credit it accordingly.

My 36-month forecast for Amulet: acqui-hired into Gainsight or Vitally for $3-10M in engineering value by mid-2029, mirroring the Staircase AI ($5M seed → Gainsight, July 2024) tuck-in pattern the incumbents have already run once. The mechanism is the signature: two strong engineers with no organic wedge cannot out-distribute a $1.2B ARR incumbent that already bought this exact playbook, so the exit becomes the team, not the company.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Loopt** (2005) exception — I funded Sam Altman before mobile location was usable infrastructure, on force of personality alone, weak PMF and all. Amulet's bull case would run: CS is a data-plumbing problem, ex-Meta ads engineers are literally trained on messy multi-source pipelines, and the incumbents are architecturally slow. Fair. But the Loopt override was Altman's animal-test result in the room, and the Amulet dossier gives me no equivalent — no shipped stunt, no rejected-then-rebuilt artifact, no LOI with a named logo, no revenue improvisation. What would flip my verdict: Amulet posting a public demo agent that runs against a real CRM with a named design partner (a real logo, not "no public data found"), or evidence that Nithik Bala and Varun Puru personally ran the CSM motion at Meta before quitting. Until then, this is clever-idea-soft-founders — the pattern that has lost me money.
