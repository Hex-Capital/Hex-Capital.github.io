# Expanse -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Expanse is the rare YC pitch where the unfashionability is the asset and the moat is the liability ? Ismaeel Bashir's master's thesis on multimodal HPC resource prediction lands them in a corner of infrastructure (Slurm, national supercomputing centers, quant fund batch jobs) that hyperscaler-aligned tooling has structurally ignored, but the product itself is a prediction layer that NVIDIA, SchedMD, or any motivated incumbent could ship in eighteen months by reading arXiv 2604.02158.

On consensus mispricing, my high rung is Solana at four cents ? everyone said Ethereum won, the price reflected that, and the asymmetry was extreme. The low rung is the half of every YC batch pitching me an AI copilot wrapper. Expanse sits closer to mid than high: HPC/Slurm optimization is genuinely uncomfortable for Sand Hill ? nobody's writing $5M seeds for "we make Edinburgh's supercomputer 30% more efficient" ? and the NVIDIA Run:ai acquisition closing December 2024 vacuumed all the orchestration mindshare into Kubernetes, leaving the Slurm world genuinely underserved. But "GPU" is a 2026 hot word, so the discount isn't OnlyFans-deep. The dossier specific that pins Expanse here is the customer profile: national HPC centers and quant funds running batch schedulers, exactly the buyers hyperscalers don't optimize for because they'd rather you use their cloud.

On structural durability, the AI kill test, my high rung is Metropolis ? you cannot software-eat physical parking infrastructure, and the moat compounds. Mid is Solana, where protocol-level network effects create real lock-in. The low rung is Clubhouse: pure software, no physical asset, incumbents replicated the feature inside a quarter. Expanse sits at the low rung, hard. Bashir's predictor "beat every published baseline" per the YC description, but it's a single-author MSc dissertation with no peer review, the underlying academic literature is publicly available (PMC8974354, arXiv 2604.02158), Slurm is open-source, and NVIDIA is actively open-sourcing Run:ai. The dossier specific that pins Expanse is "passive telemetry collection, one-click deployment" ? that's a feature description, not a moat description. SchedMD ships this as a release note.

On primitive creation, my high rung is Venmo ? a verb, a behavior, a category that didn't exist. Mid is Slack, a real new primitive that I paid consensus prices for. Low is "better CRM." Expanse is closer to low: GPU resource prediction is not a new behavior, it's a better implementation of an existing scheduler function. No one will say "Expanse me this cluster." On sanity-insanity calibration, my high rung is Raj at Solana ? quiet engineer, insane bet ? and Expanse's Bashir reads as the sane half (Edinburgh first-class, EPCC thesis, engineer-grounded), but the idea is the most sensible idea on the planet: "researchers over-provision 2-3x, we predict better." Sane person, sane idea ? that's a consultancy, not a venture. Mid rung at best.

On capital discipline, four founders, no job postings, no factory-farm signaling. The high rung is Gusto's disciplined deployment, the low rung is Allbirds burning rounds on retail expansion. Expanse defaults to mid; nothing in the dossier suggests blitzscale ambitions, and the founder-led `book.getexpanse.io` outbound motion implies tight burn. The brand fragmentation across expanse.sh / expanse.org.uk / getexpanse.io is amateur but not capital-indiscipline; it's just messy.

My 36-month forecast: Expanse gets acqui-hired by NVIDIA, SchedMD, or a Tier-1 quant fund (G-Research itself, given Melake and Mendi's history) for $15-30M, or quietly winds down after a Series A that doesn't materialize once Run:ai's open-source release extends to batch schedulers. The mechanism is the signature: high mispricing, low durability ? you can find an underserved corner, but if the moat is academic IP that's already in arXiv, the corner gets eaten by the first incumbent who notices.

The strongest objection is that I'm under-weighting founder-market fit. Bashir wrote the predictor, Melake and Mendi sat inside G-Research watching quants over-provision, and HPC procurement is genuinely sticky ? once you're in Oak Ridge or a quant batch pipeline, you're a multi-year contract. Slack was hot consensus and I bought it because the primitive was real; maybe Expanse is Slack-shaped where HPC-batch-optimization-as-a-service is the new primitive I'm dismissing. The dismantle: Slack created a new behavior (real-time team channels replacing email). Expanse does not create a new behavior ? it does a known thing better. The Known Exception doesn't apply because there is no primitive here, just productization. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed multi-year contract above $500K ARR with a named national HPC center (Oak Ridge, ANL, NERSC) or a Tier-1 quant fund displacing in-house tooling. Show me that LOI and I write the check tomorrow ? until then, this is a feature looking for an acquirer.
