# Lattice Health -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Lattice Health is a missionary clinician-engineer wrapped around a force-fit enterprise SaaS cap table -- Christine Park has the most credentialed founder-conviction profile I'll see in this batch (Duke MD/MEng-AI, UW neurosurgery PGY-3, Stanford CS PhD, peer-reviewed brain-imaging papers including arXiv 2511.18208 in 2025) running a horizontal "operating system for clinical AI in imaging" pitch that already has Microsoft/Nuance and Bayer/Blackford sitting in the workflow. The signature is Park's conviction (high) paired against a venture-shape (low) that her actual advantage cannot pull through hospital procurement. Read the structure first, then the founder -- and the structure here is wrong for the founder.

On founder-conviction, the load-bearing analogy is **Ben Silbermann at Pinterest** -- not because Lattice Health looks like Pinterest, but because Silbermann was the canonical case where I overrode price discipline for missionary temperament in a problem someone had been living inside for years. Park clears that bar more cleanly than most pre-seeds will: she has published deep-learning imaging research (medRxiv 2022 "Point-of-Care MRI with AI to Measure Midline Shift," Google Scholar profile, the 2025 arXiv on multimodal AI for brain MRI), she has clinical scar tissue from neurosurgery residency, and she has been working on this category in some form since the Duke MEng. Where Lattice Health diverges from the Silbermann template is the publishing surface that should already exist for a founder with this conviction -- I want to read her opinionated essay on why >700 FDA-cleared imaging models orphans the orchestration layer, and @sparkcpark with no retrievable follower count and no public long-form is conspicuous absence, not just missing data. Writing is thinking; if she has the take, I should be able to find it.

**Teamshares** is the canonical capital-structure win in my book because the employee-equity rollup is an instrument other funds literally cannot underwrite -- the structure itself is the alpha. Lattice Health is the inverse: a 6-18 month enterprise hospital sales cycle, no signed LOIs disclosed, no revenue, solo founder, sold to health systems whose procurement processes assume a vendor that exists full-time. That is a standard SaaS cap table that will need five rounds to reach default-alive, and the founder is also a PGY-3 neurosurgery resident plus a Stanford CS PhD candidate -- bandwidth is not a venture-shaped input here, it is the single binding constraint. The Ro exception does not bail this out: Ro's capital intensity was the 50-state prescription-rail schlep, a regulatory wedge only VC could clear; Lattice Health is glue code on top of other people's FDA clearances, no regulatory moat that justifies the burn.

Round dynamics -- nothing in the Lattice Health dossier says "oversubscribed," but nothing says "non-consensus" either; no tier-1 has publicly passed, no co-investor is named, and the YC tagline "operating system for clinical AI in imaging" tracks the 2026 batch consensus theme of vertical-AI infrastructure shells. Eighteen months of compounding here looks like: the round prices at the YC batch median, Aidoc and Rad AI extend their own platforms to host third-party models (channel-conflict argument cuts both ways once they need the ARR), and Microsoft Nuance Precision Imaging Network -- which already lives inside PowerScribe radiology reporting -- ships a competitive marketplace at zero CAC. Lattice Health does not survive that compounding without a structural discount on entry that the dossier gives me no reason to expect.

Founder-as-brand fails cleanly against the **John Fish / Bookshelved** template -- Fish brought a measurable owned-distribution channel to a literary product on day one, and the check priced his audience, not his LLC. Lattice Health has @sparkcpark with no retrievable follower count, no LinkedIn confirmation at the Lattice Health headline, no GitHub, no newsletter, no Discord, and worse, a name collision with latticehealth.ai (biotech AI agents) and a Crunchbase "Lattice Health Systems" entity polluting the search surface. Hospital CIOs Google before they take a meeting; the SEO surface is already lost.

Counterfactual on category sleeve: this would flip a tier if Lattice Health were positioned as a clinical-AI evaluation/monitoring layer for the FDA-cleared device universe -- a regulatory-compliance shape closer to the prescription-rail logic that bailed out the Ro exception. As written, "operating system for clinical AI in imaging" is the AI-on-top-of-AI shell I dismiss as "just more internet" (Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024), and Blackford Analysis selling to Bayer in 2023 is the comp that already cleared this category at the price point a strategic pays, which is not a venture moment.

Thirty-six month forecast: Lattice Health does not cross $1M ARR, Park finishes residency and either folds the company into a clinical-AI role at Rad AI or Aidoc, or accepts an acquihire under $15M from Microsoft/Nuance or Bayer/Blackford -- driven by the binding constraint that a solo founder splitting time across neurosurgery residency and a Stanford PhD cannot run multi-year hospital enterprise sales while two incumbents already own the radiology workflow.

The bull case that stings: Park's profile is genuinely rare, the >700-FDA-cleared-device fragmentation is a real integration tax, and the right read is that I'm pattern-matching her to "solo healthcare founder" when the right precedent is the Ro override -- regulatory complexity around clinical AI monitoring (FDA post-market surveillance, hospital governance) is an emerging schlep that only VC capital can underwrite, and Park's clinical credentials are the unfair advantage to clear it. The dossier dismantles this: Lattice Health is not described as a post-market-surveillance / regulatory-monitoring layer, it is described as deployment and orchestration -- the workflow seat Microsoft already owns through Nuance. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed pilot LOI with a named academic medical center where Lattice Health is specifically the FDA post-market monitoring / model-drift compliance layer, not a marketplace shell. Until then, I pass and watch for Park's next company at the end of residency.
