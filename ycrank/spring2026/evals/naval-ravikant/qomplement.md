# qomplement -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

*qomplement is the inverse of my book.* Kerim Taray books 20-minute demos through qomplement.cal.com/kerim/20min, and that single URL tells me the entire leverage architecture before I read the founder section. The high dimension is specific knowledge — Kerim and Andres Garza ran RPA consulting for Mexican enterprises out of Tec de Monterrey, which is exactly the lived-problem ownership I underwrite at criterion #3. The low dimension is permissionless distribution — every customer is acquired by the CEO personally walking a grocery-chain procurement team through a PO-extraction demo. Specific knowledge plus founder-led enterprise sales is a profile I respect operationally and decline structurally; that pairing is the whole evaluation.

**Stack Overflow** (2008) is the HIGH rung on permissionless leverage — Atwood and Spolsky shipped code, Google indexed it, developers pulled it, and the cost of the (N+1)th user was zero. qomplement does not share that mechanism. The dossier names "10,000+ documents per day across 20+ companies" but the acquisition motion behind those 20 companies is Cal.com bookings, Mexican-language press in Expansión and Milenio, and bespoke integrations into SAP/Oracle/NetSuite — labor and capital leverage that "require permission" (nav.al/product-media), permission from the buyer's procurement committee and permission from the incumbent ERP's API surface. There is no GitHub repo, no PLG funnel, no developer pull, no media-native distribution that compounds while Kerim sleeps. When the dossier's distribution channel is a personal Cal.com link, criterion #1 has already scored itself.

The LOW rung on network effects is the single-vertical SaaS sleeve absent from my 200+ ledger because each customer is economically independent of the next. qomplement sits one step above that floor: the document corpus from grocery and trucking *could* compound into a vertical extraction moat, the "Inferred" line in the dossier flagging exactly this. But a data flywheel inside one vendor's training set is not the asymmetric-user-value compounding that drove **Uber** (2010) to 4,000x — the 21st qomplement customer does not make the prior 20 measurably better off. Pivot's €2.5B annual invoice volume (SiliconANGLE, May 2026) means the larger horizontal corpus is already being accumulated by a $70M-funded competitor, so even the inferred flywheel is being out-compounded before qomplement names a logo.

Specific knowledge is where qomplement actually earns its score. Kerim and Andres spent pre-qomplement years building RPA implementations for Mexican enterprises — the exact "costly and slow manual processes" the YC page now names as the wedge. Compare to **Notion** (2013): Ivan Zhao had been rebuilding personal productivity tools since adolescence, and that lived ownership survived three product rebuilds. The qomplement founders carry an analogous, narrower version of the same gate — they discovered carrier-invoice extraction as practitioners, not as ex-McKinsey PMs reading a Gartner report. This is the one dimension where qomplement clears the bar I care about emphatically, and it is the dimension that, in isolation, the **Clearview AI** exception teaches me can flex the rest of the framework. The rest of the framework does not flex here, but the knowledge is real.

Market and pivot-room compound badly over 18 months for qomplement. SAP's "Supply Chain Trends for 2026" roadmap, Pivot's $40M Series B in May 2026, and ORO Labs' "Category King" positioning are three vectors converging on the same overlay-on-legacy-ERP wedge. The "trillions of dollars in goods" framing is real but it is gated — every dollar of that TAM passes through a procurement committee that will ask whether SAP's native agent is shipping next quarter. The pivot vectors I can name from the dossier (grocery → trucking → freight invoicing) are all inside the same buyer persona, which means "the first idea never works" axiom (Venture Hacks, 2007-10) collides with a 12-month enterprise sales cycle before qomplement has time to reach the second idea.

The Buffett trio reads clean on qomplement and that is the kindest thing the dimension allows. Kerim and Andres met at Tec de Monterrey, collaborated through a university AI lab, and co-founded together — no partner litigation, no self-promotion about ethics, no contested exit. The one thing that itches is the May 3, 2025 Product Hunt page describing qomplement as "an AI agent that controls your personal computer," which is a different product than the current Agentic ERP. Pivots are fine — **Twitter** (2007) pivoted from Odeo — but a 12-month pivot from consumer-desktop-agent to enterprise-supply-chain-agent is not the kind of pivot-within-thesis my framework rewards; it is a thesis change. I do not score this as integrity-negative. I score it as a reason the 10-year-partner question still has open variables.

*Forecast, 36 months.* qomplement either gets acqui-hired by an incumbent — SAP, Oracle, Workday, or one of the YC-darling procurement platforms — for $30–80M as a team-and-tech deal that buys 20 logos and a working extraction stack, or it stalls at $3–8M ARR squeezed between Pivot's European expansion into North American retail and the native AI-agent SKUs that SAP's 2026 roadmap is already telegraphing. The mechanism is direct: enterprise sales motions without permissionless distribution compound linearly while incumbent platforms with channel reach compound exponentially, so the window in which qomplement's RPA-domain-knowledge edge is decisive is narrower than the window in which capital-leverage competitors close the technical gap.

*The strongest bull case.* qomplement is the **Anchorage Digital** exception — a permission-required architecture where the permission itself becomes the moat, integration depth into SAP/Oracle/NetSuite plus a proprietary grocery-and-trucking document corpus producing a position no horizontal entrant can cheaply replicate. That case stings because Anchorage is the one bet I made knowingly outside criterion #1, and it worked. It fails here for two reasons: I had been compounding into crypto since co-founding **MetaStable** in 2014 before the Anchorage check, and I have no such accumulation in agentic-ERP; and Anchorage's permission moat was a Treasury OCC charter — qomplement's permission moat is a 20-customer integration map a $70M-funded Pivot can replicate inside 18 months. The verdict flips to Invest if qomplement publishes a signed multi-year contract with a named top-10 global grocer or top-5 North American trucking carrier — a logo specific enough that Pivot cannot simply outspend the GTM. Until that logo lands, qomplement is a competent team in the wrong leverage architecture for my book.
