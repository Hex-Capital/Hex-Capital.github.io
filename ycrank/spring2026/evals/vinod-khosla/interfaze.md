# Interfaze -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Interfaze is a competent second-attempt at the same thesis Yoeven Khemlani already ran at JigsawStack -- "unified AI API for developers" -- now repackaged as "deterministic AI" with 98-99% structured-output claims and a $1.50/$3.50 per-million-token price card. That diagnostic pairing -- a founder with real learning-rate evidence (prior $3M ARR exit, prior $1.5M pre-seed from Antler/Ada) building toward a success-state where no incumbent regime collapses -- is Interfaze's signature. It is the inverse of the profile I write checks on. My **OpenAI** (2019) $50M was a civilizational bet with a foggy moat; Interfaze is a clear-moat bet with a mundane civilizational footprint, and that ordering is the one my MIT Rose Lecture (2019) said never to invert.

**OpenAI** (2019) sits at the top of my consequence ladder because the success-state -- machine cognition at human-economic scale -- maps directly to the "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" frame I gave Fortune (2026-03-06). Interfaze's success-state, by the dossier's own framing, is "OpenAI-compatible Chat Completions interface" routing OCR, scraping, and STT to small specialist models so developers get cleaner JSON. That is a developer-ergonomics improvement, not a category collapse. No incumbent regime named for displacement in Interfaze's positioning -- Reducto, LlamaParse, Unstructured, Mistral OCR, Docling, Marker, Nanonets all already serve this workflow per reducto.ai/document-parser-comparison (2025), and Gemini 2.0 Flash already scores ~80% on RD-FormsBench. Interfaze wins by being 15-20 points more accurate inside an existing developer call. That is exactly the "30% better" pattern my Red Flags section names.

The low rung on founder-defies-expert-consensus is the "eight years at Salesforce, now with AI" pass example -- domain-expert continuity executing the same playbook. Does that pattern appear in Interfaze? Yoeven Khemlani has eight years in edge-ML and OCR pipelines and his immediately prior company JigsawStack was a "unified AI API suite" -- the same architectural thesis Interfaze now sells with a sharper name. Harsha Vardhan Khurdula brings Purdue M.Sc. computer-vision credentials and an IEEE CAI 2026 paper accepted, which is paradigmatic expert credentialing, not the defiance my TED Talk demanded when I cited Berkeley's 28,000-prediction study where experts beat chance "only slightly." Nowhere in the dossier does either founder publicly contradict their field; the IEEE acceptance is the opposite signal.

Mechanism analysis on technical/capital risk: the 5-person team, OpenAI-compatible surface, and self-hosted "hybrid DNN/CNN + LLM" router compounds over 18 months into a thinner moat, not a deeper one. Every quarter that Gemini, Claude, and GPT close the structured-output gap, Interfaze's routing-layer advantage decays -- Reducto's own March 2025 benchmark already shows Gemini 2.0 Flash at ~80% on dense tables. Interfaze's claimed 98-99% accuracy is self-reported and the arxiv ID 2602.04101 is future-dated and unverifiable in the dossier. This is software-only execution risk that any Tier-1 generalist can fund; it does not require the capital depth that made **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) uninvestable for consensus VCs.

Counterfactual on founder learning rate: for Interfaze to clear a 12/15 here rather than the 9 I am giving it, Yoeven would need to articulate -- in the dossier -- why JigsawStack's "unified AI API" framing was wrong and what specifically changed in his thesis between that company and Interfaze. The dossier shows a trajectory (Singapore travel startup -> acquisition -> JigsawStack -> Interfaze) but no documented "we were wrong about X" moment of the kind that justified my $100M Series C into **Slash** (2024) after its Kanye-pivot story. Without that explicit thesis update, Interfaze reads as iteration, not pivot.

Single-anchor parallel on capital fit: my $50M into **OpenAI** (2019) was deployable because the roadmap required compute at a scale no consensus VC would underwrite. Interfaze's roadmap -- inference routing, an OpenAI-compatible endpoint, 50 req/sec capacity -- runs comfortably on a YC pre-seed check and a Series A in the $5-10M range. I am structurally over-resourced for Interfaze; the deal does not need Khosla Ventures, and that absence of capital-gravity is itself a signal that Interfaze is not in my lane.

My 36-month forecast on Interfaze: acqui-hired by a developer-tools or model-infrastructure buyer (Vercel, LangChain, Cloudflare, or a frontier lab like Anthropic looking for OCR/STT pipeline talent) at sub-$50M, more likely sub-$30M, as Gemini/Claude/GPT close the deterministic-output gap and Reducto/LlamaParse compress price-per-token from below. Yoeven lands softly because he is a proven operator; the company does not become a defining one. This is the "JigsawStack v2" outcome -- the same trajectory his prior $1.5M pre-seed produced, scaled up by the YC P26 stamp.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Reducto's own March 2025 benchmarks proved frontier OCR hallucinates on dense tables and handwriting, which is a real failure-mode that creates real demand for a deterministic-output layer -- and the **Slash** (2024) Known Exception shows I will override consequence-magnitude when founder learning-rate is strong enough and the YC pedigree is intact. Yoeven cleared $3M ARR on his first company and raised $1.5M from Antler/Ada on his second; that is a serious operator. The dismantling: **Slash**'s override required a *specific, publicly-told* pivot story (the Kanye moment) that demonstrated thesis revision under pressure. Interfaze's dossier shows continuity from JigsawStack, not revision. And **Slash** was a $100M Series C with revenue traction; Interfaze is pre-seed with self-reported benchmarks and zero disclosed usage volume. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: Interfaze publishes independently-validated benchmarks (Artificial Analysis, Reducto's RD-FormsBench, or a peer-reviewed third party) showing the 98-99% structured-output claim holds against Gemini 3 / Claude 4.x on Reducto's adversarial test set, AND demonstrates $1M+ run-rate from named enterprise customers, not just SF meetup co-hosting at luma.com/agents-ai-api-may. Until then, Interfaze scores like the sales-call-summarizer in my Pass Example, with one extra point for founder pedigree.
