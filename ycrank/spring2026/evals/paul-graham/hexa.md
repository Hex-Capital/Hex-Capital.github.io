# Hexa -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Hexa is a real-schlep company hiding a hollow founder file: eight named ERP integrations (SAP, Oracle NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics, Epicor, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, Pipedrive) advertised by a three-person team where only Ishaan Makkar is publicly named and his LinkedIn reads "Stealth." That pairing — high schlep surface, near-zero founder evidence — is the whole signature. The work is the kind of unpleasant integration grind I usually like. The people doing it are, on the page, ghosts.

On the schlep ladder, the HIGH rung is **Coinbase** (2012), where Brian Armstrong's appetite for bitcoin compliance was the moat; the MID rung is **DoorDash** (2013), last-mile delivery operations no software-only YC applicant in its batch wanted to run; the LOW rung is the polished AI-wrapper team from my standing pass example, pure software with no offline grind. Hexa sits between MID and HIGH. Eight production write-back ERP integrations across SAP/Oracle/Dynamics/Epicor on three people is genuinely awful work, and the dossier names Distro, Turian, Go Autonomous, Altura, and Arphie as competitors who have each picked a single slice (order entry, RFQ, RFP) precisely because the full surface is too painful. That is schlep blindness in the competitor set, and it is the one place Hexa earns real points.

On the organic-pain ladder, the HIGH rung is **Dropbox** (2007) — Drew Houston's USB stick at MIT, a problem he hit personally before he built for it — and **Stripe** (2010), the Collisons hacking commerce since **Auctomatic** (2007); the MID rung is **Reddit** (2005), founders building the link-aggregator they wanted to read; the LOW rung is again the MBA AI-wrapper team that did competitive analysis instead of suffering. Hexa sits at the LOW rung. The dossier explicitly says no prior manufacturing, distribution, ERP, or supply-chain employment is documented for Makkar. The "54% of manufacturing companies still rely on spreadsheets" line in his quoted material is a McKinsey-flavored market observation, not the voice of someone who lost a Tuesday afternoon copy-pasting a PDF quote into NetSuite. Manufacturers and distributors run on spreadsheets the way insurance brokers run on faxes — both are real, neither is an organic idea unless you've been the one cursing at the spreadsheet.

On the animal-resourcefulness ladder, the HIGH rung is **Airbnb** (2009)'s cereal boxes and Armstrong's hand-built Android wallet that rescued his rejected **Coinbase** (2012) application; MID is the **Dropbox** (2007) Hacker News demo video; LOW is, again, the deck-first AI-wrapper team. Hexa sits at LOW. There is no shipped artifact in the dossier — no demo video, no public design partner, no waitlist count, no $X of revenue, no named pilot manufacturer, just a Calendly link to "ishaan-hexaagents/30min." Animals leave tracks, and I cannot find any. On hacker genealogy, the HIGH rung is Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize and Aaron Swartz's RSS spec; LOW is the credential-only CS-degree founder. Hexa sits near LOW: no GitHub, no open-source footprint, no Twitter, no shipped technical artifact attached to Makkar's name. On the co-founder ladder, HIGH is the **Airbnb** (2009) trio and the Collison brothers, LOW is the solo founder. Hexa is technically three people but the YC page lists Makkar as sole founder and an unverified snippet mentions a "Mann Patira" — closer to LOW than MID.

In 36 months Hexa is either dead or pivoted to a single-ERP deep-stack agent (most likely NetSuite, given the mid-market positioning) with sub-$500K ARR, squeezed from above by SAP Joule and Microsoft Dynamics Copilot shipping native agents inside the suites Hexa is overlay-ing, and squeezed from below by Distro and Turian who picked one workflow and built it deeper. The mechanism is the signature itself: real schlep without real founder-pain rarely finishes the schlep, because eight integration backlogs on three people without the felt urgency of someone who lived the problem becomes a roadmap of half-finished connectors.

The strongest bull case is that I am applying the **Coinbase** (2012) Known Exception backwards — Armstrong's appetite for compliance grind was itself the moat, and an AI-agent team willing to maintain eight ERP write-backs in 2026 is the same shape of unpleasant work other YC batch-mates are unconsciously routing around. **Scale AI**'s Alexandr Wang publicly named "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) as his trigger, and Hexa's competitor matrix is a textbook schlep-blindness map. The reason that case does not win: Coinbase had Armstrong's prototype and Wang had data-labeling operations he had run by hand. Hexa's dossier has no prototype evidence and no founder who has personally suffered the workflow. Schlep without an animal in front of it is just future technical debt. I would flip this verdict if Makkar shows me a demo video processing a real RFQ end-to-end into a named mid-market manufacturer's NetSuite, with that customer named — that single artifact would move Hexa two ladders at once. Pass for now.
