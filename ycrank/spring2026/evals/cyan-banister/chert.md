# Chert -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Chert is a clean-governance software wrapper sitting on top of someone else's platform -- one named CEO in Gary Gao, two technical co-founders who started in June 2025, and a product that lives entirely at the mercy of Apple's iMessage terms of service. That high/low pairing -- tidy operating structure, zero frontier-hardware or regulatory-moat exposure -- is the analytical signature, and it tells me what Chert actually is: a pre-seed Twilio-for-X play in the most consensus YC category of Spring 2026, pitched into a competitive set (Sendblue, LoopMessage, Blooio, Linq) that already has a five-year incumbent. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing -- and the game Chert wants me to play is "CPaaS TAM is $21B growing to $41B," which is exactly the analytical framing I distrust.

Anduril's Palmer Luckey is the high rung on lived founder origin -- he had already shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he ever opened a defense-hardware deck, and the biography did the underwriting. Chert's Gary Gao is Penn CS and Wharton with the prior year spent on "ML architectures and neuromorphic chip research"; Ian Fong is NYU Math/Econ, eight-time hackathon winner, "incoming at JP Morgan" before pivoting in June 2025. The dossier says they built iMessage projects "for leasing companies, DTC startups, and home service agencies" over the last six months -- which is six months of market discovery, not a lived autobiographical contact with the problem. That is precisely the pattern in my Pass Example: credentialed polymath generalists who discovered the problem in a memo, not in their own life. Neither Gary Gao nor Ian Fong is a roughneck who walked iMessage well pads for a decade; they are talented kids who chose this market last summer.

The Consensus YC AI-Agent Darling -- the archetype I pass on before slide 3 -- is the low rung on magical weirdness, and Chert's dossier triggers that pattern in three independent places. Chert's own Launch HN headline reads "Twilio for iMessage (YC P26)," which is the most legible YC-pitch frame possible; Sendblue, LoopMessage, Blooio, and Linq already occupy the same lane with Blooio undercutting at $39/month flat; and the Product Hunt #7 day-rank with 179 upvotes is a consensus-aesthetic artifact, not a "magically weird" tell. I want to invest before something becomes consensus -- Chert arrived already consensus. Nobody credible has passed on aesthetic or moral grounds here because there is nothing aesthetically transgressive to recoil from.

Gary Gao is the named CEO on LinkedIn, Ian Fong holds the un-titled Co-Founder slot, and the two have a documented "still hit random hackathons together for fun" friendship dating to June 2025 -- this is the cleanest dimension in the dossier for Chert. The mechanism that compounds over eighteen months is whether the title hierarchy survives the first hard product disagreement; with no shared prior employer (Gary at Penn, Ian at NYU) and an unstated equity split, the HQ Trivia / GameCrush failure mode is dormant but not impossible. I score this favorably because the absence of red flags is itself a signal at pre-seed, but it is the dimension on which Chert most resembles my portfolio, and it is doing a disproportionate share of the work in this evaluation.

For the score on Capital-Intensive, Frontier-Hardware or Unsexy-Regulated Swing to flip a full tier, Chert would need to be building an unofficial protocol layer with FCC- or Apple-adjacent regulatory exposure that compounds defensibility over time -- the Sendblue/Linq compare page makes clear that all five competitors rely on the same unofficial techniques against an Apple-controlled protocol, which is platform-dependence risk, not regulatory moat. The "comprehensive line health checks" claim on Product Hunt is the closest gesture to a defensible artifact, but line-health-as-a-feature is not Anduril's Lattice, it is a deliverability heuristic any competent CPaaS engineer will replicate in a quarter. Chert is the structural inverse of the SpaceX 2008 check.

Postmates is the single-anchor parallel on Physical-World Verifiability -- I bet on Postmates in 2011 because the delivery happened in front of me on a sidewalk, a tactile artifact I could photograph. Chert's blue bubble is technically tactile -- a friend could receive a Chert-powered iMessage and you would see Apple's blue UI render it -- but the sales motion is gated to "cal.com/team/chert" booking, the customer logos (Tour, Symbal, Whop, Rubbrband, Autosana) are mid-market B2B that I cannot organically encounter, and the "65% response rate, 90% action rate" claim is unverified company-stated. Chert sits closer to the developer-tools-API low rung than to a Postmates-class tactile artifact, but the blue bubble keeps it from being pure abstraction.

Forecast for Chert at 36 months: Apple either tightens iMessage anti-spam line-banning in 2027 and the category dies in unison, or the more likely outcome is an acquihire into a CRM platform -- HubSpot, GoHighLevel, or Attio, all already integrated -- in the $8-25M range, with Gary Gao and Ian Fong landing as product managers on a messaging team. The mechanism is straightforward: thin API wrappers on platforms they don't own commoditize into integration features of the systems they integrate with, and Chert's GTM (CRM write-back to HubSpot, Close, GoHighLevel) is already telegraphing the acquirer.

The strongest bull case is the Twilio precedent itself: Jeff Lawson's "carrier API" sounded equally unsexy in 2008, and 179 Product Hunt upvotes with named customers like Whop pre-launch is genuinely faster than Twilio's pre-seed signal. The Known Exception that could apply is HQ Trivia -- I overrode the governance heuristic when tactile virality dominated -- and Chert has clean governance but lacks the tactile pull, so the exception does not invert. The dismantling fact is that Jeff Lawson had shipped StubHub and Amazon's communications stack before Twilio; he was *of* the carrier world. Gary Gao did neuromorphic chip research six months before Chert. Wrong founder for this market at this moment. Verifiable flip condition: if Apple announces a formal iMessage Business API in the next twelve months and names Chert as a launch partner, I reopen the file immediately -- absent that, Chert is a pass.
