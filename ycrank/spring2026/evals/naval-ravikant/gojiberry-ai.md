# Gojiberry AI -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Román Czerny sold CoCo AI; Dylan Teixeira sold Edusign at 600,000 users — three repeat founders who ship, stapled to an architecture I structurally avoid. Gojiberry AI is a labor-and-capital-leverage business in AI-SDR clothing: $99/mo self-serve SaaS, an SDR hire posted on the YC jobs page, and a product whose primary distribution channel is LinkedIn — a platform that has historically banned exactly this category of automation. The dimensional signature on Gojiberry AI is high-on-founder-execution, low-on-leverage-architecture, which is the canonical *competent-team-wrong-vehicle* profile that drops to the lower half of my funnel before I open the founder section.

Twitter (2007) is the canonical permissionless-media-leverage check — anyone posts, the platform charges nothing, marginal cost of replication is zero, no gatekeeper can revoke the channel. Gojiberry AI inverts every clause of that sentence. The 30+ intent signals are scraped from LinkedIn engagement; the outreach is sent through "2 LinkedIn senders" per the Pro plan; the channel itself is owned by Microsoft and has historically restricted Dux-Soup and Phantombuster under the same usage pattern. Code-leverage means the marginal cost of replication is zero AND nobody can switch you off. Gojiberry AI fails the second clause — LinkedIn's TOS is the kill switch, and the dossier names no countermeasure. This is capital and labor leverage routed through someone else's platform, which is the exact configuration I sat through during the Pinterest pattern-bias era and *still* would not write today.

Vertical SaaS where each customer is economically independent of the next is the low-rung failure mode for compounding-user-value — and Gojiberry AI's "performance compounds over time" claim is a per-customer learning loop, not a cross-customer network. Customer 1,000 does not make customer 1 more valuable; there is no two-sided marketplace, no developer ecosystem, no creator graph. The 1,000+ paying customers figure (which conflicts with the homepage's "500+ B2B teams" — already a yellow flag on metric discipline) is a stack of independent subscriptions. The asymmetric math I run — downside one X, upside 10,000 X — needs an architecture where the Nth user multiplies the prior N−1. Gojiberry AI's architecture multiplies the founders' churn surface, not user value.

Lallemant and Czerny shipped CoCo AI to ~€500K ARR on WhatsApp-for-Shopify and sold it in 2025; Teixeira built Edusign to multi-million ARR and 600K users over six years before selling in 2025 — three documented exits inside twelve months. This is the dimension where Gojiberry AI scores cleanly. The specific knowledge is *operating SaaS* and *shipping*, not *outbound sales infrastructure* — CoCo was WhatsApp marketing, Edusign was attendance — so the founder-product-market fit is adjacent rather than lived. Compare to Aravind Srinivas at Perplexity with a Berkeley retrieval PhD attacking retrieval, or Amjad Masad obsessing over web-based REPLs since 2011: Gojiberry AI's founders are excellent operators who *picked* the AI-SDR category, not developers who *lived* the cold-outbound pain for a decade.

Outbound sales tooling is genuinely big — Apollo at $150M ARR and Clay at $100M ARR demonstrate the market absorbs multi-hundred-million revenue lines — so TAM is not the bottleneck. The bottleneck is *pivot room inside the current architecture*. Gojiberry AI already pivoted once from the $33K-revenue iteration to the GTM-brain positioning; the next pivot would have to leave LinkedIn-signal scraping behind, which means leaving the product behind. The market is wide, but the runway inside this specific build is narrow, which is the inverse of the Uber-pivot pattern where black-car → UberX preserved the architecture.

The Buffett trio reads clean on Gojiberry AI — three documented exits, no partner litigation, no integrity-volunteering, French repeat founders running a transparent self-serve SaaS playbook with Product Hunt launches and founder podcasts. This is the dimension where I have no concern. The problem is not the humans; it is that I would happily spend ten years with these three building something else.

My 36-month forecast on Gojiberry AI: acqui-hired by Apollo, Clay, or Salesloft in the $25-60M range, OR flat-lining at $5-15M ARR after a LinkedIn TOS enforcement event compresses the funnel — the 44% MoM growth does not survive contact with Clay shipping intent-signal modules onto an existing 100M-ARR distribution surface, and the founders' demonstrated pattern is *clean sale at moderate scale* (CoCo €500K, Edusign multi-million), not *category-defining outcome*. The architecture caps the upside at one of those two paths.

The strongest bull case on Gojiberry AI is the AI-cycle-timing override that worked for Perplexity: a single-sided product can clear my bar when the founder's specific knowledge and the category-timing both peak simultaneously, and Lallemant's "RIP traditional outbound. Gemini 3" post signals he's reading the model-quality step-change correctly. Add $112K MRR in 9 months with 44% MoM — that is genuine velocity, not pitch deck math, and three founders who have each banked exits inside twelve months is rare execution density. The bear-case dismantling: Perplexity's override hinged on Srinivas's *non-replicable* domain depth and *permissionless API-and-product distribution* that no incumbent could revoke. Gojiberry AI clears neither — the founders' depth is adjacent, and the distribution sits inside Microsoft's LinkedIn TOS. The verdict flips if Gojiberry AI demonstrates a non-LinkedIn distribution surface with comparable signal quality (own-scraped public web, API-native enterprise data) inside the next 9 months, OR if the founder team publishes open-source instrumentation that turns the customer-side learning loop into a builder community. Until then, this is a pass on architecture, not on people.
