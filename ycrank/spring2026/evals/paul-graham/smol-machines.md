# smol machines -- Paul Graham Evaluation

BinBin He shipped a working cross-platform microVM runtime, got 3.4k GitHub stars and 499 Hacker News points on the Show HN, then walked into YC -- and he did all of it alone. That pairing is the analytical signature of smol machines: a solo hacker working in a genuine systems-software schlep zone, with the artifact already in the world before the check. The high dimension is the schlep itself; the low one is the single chair at the table. Every line of what follows is downstream of that pairing.

Brian Armstrong's hand-built Android Bitcoin wallet is the HIGH rung I keep returning to on the resourcefulness ladder -- a founder shipping the unreasonable artifact before institutional money arrived. BinBin He's smolvm v0.8.0, released May 26 2026, and the 499-point Show HN thread at news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47808268 are the same kind of evidence: an animal left tracks. Where smol machines diverges from the Armstrong rung is the absence of a rejection-and-comeback arc -- BinBin's resourcefulness is shipped code and public validation, not a stunt that overturned a "no." That puts smol machines on the upper half of the ladder, but not at the top. 3.4k stars and a sub-200ms cold start are the artifact; what's missing is the cereal-box moment.

The MBA-team pass case is the LOW rung on organic-idea fit -- founders who "spotted a market opportunity" rather than scratched their own itch. Does that pattern show up in smol machines? Partly. BinBin's resume reads "serverless compute" and "NASA satellite trajectory tooling at Illinois Institute of Technology," and the dossier never lands the Drew-Houston-USB-stick moment -- the specific instance where BinBin himself got burned by "works on my machine" or by Docker's isolation gap. The smol machines narrative is positioned around AI-agent sandboxing (particula.tech, Apr 2026), which is a real market wave but is read FROM the market, not FROM the founder's own workflow. That is the soft spot.

On schlep -- libkrun, Hypervisor.framework, KVM, a cross-platform microVM runtime with single-file packaging -- this is the dimension where smol machines compounds. Over 18 months, the schlep gets worse, not better: Windows support is absent because BinBin "hasn't used Windows in a decade" (HN, Apr 2026); Docker-in-VM was "actively worked on" at launch; libkrun's security boundaries got pushed on in the HN thread. Each of those is a moat-builder if smol machines grinds through them, exactly the way Coinbase's compliance grind became its asset. Schlep work compounds because the next entrant has to redo it.

Hacker genealogy at smol machines is the counterfactual case. The dossier lists no prior open-source presence for BinBin He pre-smolvm, no competitive programming, no Lisp prize -- nothing that would have made me lean in the way Patrick Collison's Young Scientist win did for Stripe. What WOULD flip this dimension a tier: a second non-trivial shipped artifact in BinBin's GitHub history, or a recognized contribution to libkrun or Firecracker upstream. Absent that, the 3.4k stars on smolvm are doing all the genealogy work by themselves -- and they're doing more than I'd usually grant, because the artifact is a hypervisor-layer runtime, not a Next.js wrapper.

Single-anchor parallel for the cofounder dimension: Sam Altman at Loopt is my Known Exception for solo-ish founders, and Altman cleared the bar by being a force-of-personality animal. The smol machines dossier gives me the artifact but not the personality evidence -- no public talks, no recruiting pipeline disclosed, no second hire announced, LinkedIn still reads "Stealth Startup." YC team size of 1 against E2B's $21M Series A team and Daytona's $24M Series A team (PRNewswire, Feb 2026) is the structural problem: smol machines is trying to set a packaging standard against two well-capitalized hosted competitors with engineering rosters BinBin cannot match alone. The Altman exception requires the animal evidence; smol machines has the artifact evidence instead, and those are not interchangeable.

My 36-month forecast for smol machines: acquired by Docker, Cloudflare, or HashiCorp for $25-80M as a team-and-tech tuck-in, OR pivots to a hosted AI-agent sandbox and gets out-raised by E2B and Daytona within 18 months. The mechanism: Apache-2.0 licensing on the core runtime plus solo-founder execution capacity means smol machines cannot defend a standard-setting position against $45M of competing capital, but the smolvm artifact is good enough that an incumbent buys the codebase and the founder. This is the Kiko-eBay-sale shape, not the Twitch shape.

The bull case that stings: 3.4k stars in roughly a month is real developer love, and if `.smolmachine` becomes the portable artifact format the way `Dockerfile` did, smol machines owns the agent-runtime layer that E2B and Daytona are both renting from upstream primitives. "Black Swan Farming" tells me to invert -- the best ideas look initially like bad ideas, and "VM packaging format from a solo founder" is exactly the kind of thing I'd dismiss in 2010 and regret in 2017. What dismantles the bull case is the comparison the dossier hands me: Docker is a daemon, Firecracker is a VMM, Cloud Hypervisor is a VMM, and all three are Apache-or-Linux-Foundation licensed -- the standard-setting game is open, but smol machines is the lone solo entrant against incumbents and two funded hosted plays. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a technical cofounder with hypervisor or systems-security pedigree joins smol machines within six months, AND the .smolmachine format gets adopted by one named agent framework (LangChain, CrewAI, or an Anthropic/OpenAI agent SDK) as a default. Either alone is insufficient; both together would put smol machines back on the Stripe-shaped path.
