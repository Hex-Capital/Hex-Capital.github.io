# Superfruits Inc. -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Superfruits Inc. is the 2026 YC archetype I wrote my Pass Example about, only this time the founders actually shipped — Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Román Czerny dragged Gojiberry from a $33K services experiment to $2.5M ARR in ten months, profitable, 35% MoM, and the highest dimension here is the CMO's owned distribution (@romanbuildsaas threads doing 2M views, Product Hunt #1) while the lowest is that the category is "AI GTM agents for B2B teams," which is the exact sentence I quoted when I said "AI is just more internet." That pairing — a real distribution-led operator running on the most price-poisoned sleeve in the batch — is the whole problem with Superfruits Inc. as a check from me.

My Pass Example was literally written for Superfruits Inc.: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, three months out of YC, $30M post-money seed, four tier-1 VCs already in." Superfruits Inc. is the upgraded version of that pitch — $2.5M ARR instead of $25K MRR, which makes the round price worse, not better, because every momentum buyer in the Valley reads "$2.5M ARR profitable AI SDR with 35% MoM out of YC Spring 2026" and bids. I don't have the round size in the dossier, but I don't need it; Clay just printed at $3.1B on $100M ARR and 11x raised $76M on contested numbers, so the comp set for Superfruits Inc. has set a price floor I literally cannot underwrite. The HIGH rung on this dimension is Solana at $0.05 when every generalist had quit crypto — Superfruits Inc. is the antipode of that, a company being priced *because* the consensus loves the sentence on the YC page.

The LOW rung on category fit is the canonical "We're the OpenAI of X" pitch, which fails because it pattern-matches to a consensus leader rather than naming a non-obvious belief — and Superfruits Inc. literally positions itself against Clay, 11x, Artisan, and Regie.ai inside a single paragraph of the dossier. That isn't a contrarian secret; that's a competitive grid. The LinkedIn-ToS dependency makes it worse: Artisan's Ava already got hit with platform restrictions in 2025, and Superfruits Inc. meters its Pro plan in "LinkedIn senders" at $99/month, which means the company's unit economics live or die on a third party that has already shown its teeth to competitors. This is the sleeve I publicly de-rate, with a regulatory-substitute risk on top.

Capital structure on Superfruits Inc. is the dimension where I have to actually argue with myself — they're profitable at $2.5M ARR, which by the strict reading of my "default-alive in 24 months" filter means they pass the Slow letter's capital-efficiency bar today. The three founders have three prior exits between them (CoCo AI sold 2025, Edusign sold 2025, CoCo AI again), so this team knows how to keep a SaaS company alive on its own cash. But the cap table is generic, the instrument on offer is a standard YC SAFE, and the roadmap in the dossier (inbound, CRM updates, account expansion) is a five-round trajectory chasing Clay's positioning, not a "regatta" of underwritable tacks. Profitable doesn't fix consensus-priced; it just means I'm paying a premium for a company that doesn't need my money.

Founder conviction on Superfruits Inc. is where the dossier looks better than I expected and still doesn't get there — three exits is signal, not noise, and Lallemant and Czerny choosing to do this *together again* after CoCo AI is the kind of operator-pairing I take seriously. The counterfactual for flipping this to a top-quartile score is straightforward: I'd need Lallemant or Czerny to have published opinionated long-form writing on *why outbound is structurally broken in a way Clay misses* — a non-obvious belief I could test. What I get instead is "How to rank #1 on Product Hunt" and build-in-public threads about ARR milestones. That's mercenary execution content, not missionary category writing. Serial exiters with a playbook is a different temperament than founders who can't be talked out of a heretical view, and Sublime Security's bet on pre-consensus security category structure is the kind of conviction artifact I look for that Superfruits Inc. simply doesn't produce.

Compare Superfruits Inc. on founder-as-brand to Marina Mogilko: Mogilko had millions of language-learning subscribers and twenty years of compounding earning power, and the right instrument was 5% of 30-year earnings, not equity in an LLC. Czerny's @romanbuildsaas reach is real — 2M-view threads on X, Product Hunt #1, founder-led GTM that the dossier credibly ties to traction — but it's distribution *for this specific SaaS product*, not a person-shaped asset I could write a creator instrument against. The community didn't pre-exist the company by two years; it was built *as* the company. That puts Superfruits Inc. in the middle of this ladder rather than near the top: real distribution, wrong structural shape for the kind of bet I make on people.

My 36-month forecast on Superfruits Inc.: Gojiberry hits $8M–$15M ARR by mid-2028, gets squeezed between Clay moving down-market and 11x/Artisan consolidating, and exits via acquihire to HubSpot or Clay itself in the $30M–$80M range — a fine outcome for the founders and a flat-to-1x outcome for anyone who entered at the YC seed price. The mechanism is consensus-pricing compression: in a category with $200M+ raised by direct competitors and LinkedIn ToS overhang, the ceiling on Superfruits Inc. as an independent company is set by whichever incumbent decides to bundle intent signals into their existing distribution, and the floor is set by a platform policy change none of these companies control.

The bull case that should sting: Superfruits Inc. *is* a Wizard-of-Oz story — they ran the manual services version first ($33K in 4 months before the pivot, per superframeworks.com), then productized, which is the exact pattern I lived at Fin and the one I claim to look for in every deal. Three founders, three prior exits, profitable in ten months, real distribution, and a CMO who can sell — by my own stated criteria this is a stronger team than most of my early checks at entry. The dismantling: Fin had the Wizard-of-Oz pattern AND I founded it AND it still had to pivot to workplace analytics by 2019; the pattern is necessary, not sufficient, and it doesn't override consensus pricing on a category I've publicly written off. The condition that flips my verdict: if the round is structured non-standard — a revenue-share against the profitable cash flow, or a holdco wrapper across Lallemant and Czerny's next two companies given they're now 2-for-2 — at a discount to the implied SaaS comp, I'm interested. At a market-clearing SAFE on a 2026 AI SDR comp, I'm out before the meeting.
