# ProjectX -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Here is the problem with calling yourself a "cloud OS": it invites the question of what changes if you win. If Infinity succeeds completely -- if every student in India and every enterprise engineer runs GPU applications through browser tabs instead of local workstations -- the world gets a more convenient way to access software that already exists. AWS still runs the compute underneath. NVIDIA still makes the GPUs. Blender and Unreal Engine are still the same applications. You have built a better pipe, not a new destination. The consequence of success is a well-run infrastructure business in a market with 95 tracked competitors and $20.8 billion already deployed against you. That is not a consequence that justifies my attention.

I respect the underlying problem. Over 75% of Indian students lack access to quality computing hardware -- that is real, that is painful, and it limits human potential in ways that matter. But solving compute access through a streaming layer is the definition of an incremental improvement to existing infrastructure. Shadow PC does it for $38-55 a month. Paperspace was acquired for $111 million doing GPU VMs. Google bought Cameyo specifically for app virtualization. The approach is validated, which means it is also commoditized. When established players and hyperscalers are converging on your exact space, you are not disrupting an incumbent system -- you are entering one. Compare this to Juniper, where Cisco's CTO told me they would never build a TCP/IP router. Nobody is telling ProjectX that browser-based GPU streaming is impossible. They are telling ProjectX they are already doing it.

The founding team restructuring demands scrutiny. Between July 2024 and the YC application, three of four original co-founders departed and were replaced. At pre-seed, the team is the entire asset. Losing three co-founders is not a personnel adjustment -- it is a near-complete reconstitution of the company. Rounak Adhikary is 23, founded a tech consulting firm at 19, and has accumulated an impressive string of accelerator and award credentials. That shows hustle and ambition. But hustle in a consensus market is not the same as contrarian courage. I look for founders who are attempting something the establishment actively dismisses -- Peter Beck building rockets from New Zealand, Pat Brown trying to make meat from plants. Streaming GPU apps in browsers is something Microsoft, Google, and Amazon are all working on. The courage required here is operational, not conceptual.

The strongest bull case goes like this: sub-3-second cold starts for per-app GPU compute in browser tabs, if truly achieved, represents a genuine systems engineering breakthrough. The per-app model versus full desktop VMs is an architectural bet that, if GPU costs decline on the exponential curves the market forecasts suggest, could deliver 10x cheaper access than Shadow or traditional VDI. The $2.4 million in signed LOIs and an IIT Bombay pilot are real institutional demand signals -- not vaporware. India is a massive underserved market, and being the first to nail this user experience for Indian education could build a distribution moat before hyperscalers localize. If Rounak is a genuine fast learner -- and building a functioning GPU streaming beta by age 22 suggests something -- the rate-of-change story could look very different after another year of iteration. The timing on WebGPU maturation and GPU cost curves is genuinely favorable.

But even that bull case describes a good SaaS company, not a category-defining one. The money either expires or you build a very successful company -- there is no middle ground in how I invest. And when I look at ProjectX, I see a middle ground outcome as the ceiling. A streaming convenience layer atop commodity GPU infrastructure, in a market where the biggest players on earth are spending billions, does not produce the asymmetric return profile I need. The problem is important but the solution does not make the incumbent approach obsolete. It makes it slightly more accessible. That is not the same thing.
