# Andustry -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Andustry is a credentialed-outsider founder pair pointed at a category that already has two capitalized public-or-acquired incumbents — Het Dave from CDTM/TUM and Johann Stürken from RWTH Aachen plus a Mittelstand trading family, pitching "Alibaba on AI" against Xometry's $687M FY2025 revenue base. The dimensional signature is founder-market fit attached to a monopoly geometry that does not exist. That is the entire problem with Andustry in one sentence, and the rest of the evaluation is severity.

The Stripe (2010) analogy is the one Andustry implicitly invokes — developer-first payments against an incumbent (PayPal) that had "won" the category. The load-bearing mechanism in Stripe was that the Collison brothers named the *specific* architectural failure mode: legacy processors could not ship a seven-line integration because their internal organization was sales-led, not API-led, and could not refactor without breaking enterprise revenue. Andustry's dossier does not name an equivalent failure mode in Xometry or Fictiv. The framing is "AI-native broker" versus "AI-driven quoting" — a velocity claim, not a structural one. Xometry already operates AI instant-quote across CNC, sheet metal, and 3D print; Fictiv was acquired by MISUMI for $350M in April 2025 precisely because the broker layer is now table-stakes. Andustry's contrarian secret reduces to "we will do agent negotiation across off-the-shelf machines and intermediates instead of custom parts" — a category-adjacency claim, not a Zero to One secret. This is the pass-example archetype the 2024-26 YC batches over-produce: AI for procurement, no named incumbent blind spot, no Straussian read.

Xometry at $687M FY2025 revenue is the monopoly-trajectory problem. Cleantech 1.0 failed because commodity economics swallowed the differentiated capex; Andustry's commission-only model with no published rate card faces the same structural fate from a different angle. The product Andustry builds — price-transparent multi-supplier quote comparison — is the product that compresses brokerage margin, and Andustry is the broker. There is no switching-cost hypothesis in the dossier, no integration depth a buyer cannot replace next quarter, no regulated certification moat. The proprietary supplier-quote dataset is the most plausible future moat, but Xometry and MISUMI/Fictiv compound that same dataset from larger transaction bases starting today. Andustry is structurally a fast-follower in a category where the slow-follower already cleared $600M.

Counterfactual on the definite-plan dimension: for Andustry to clear the SpaceX-style multi-year-commitment bar, Het Dave and Johann Stürken would have to name a five-year technical roadmap — say, a closed-loop quality-feedback layer on top of supplier outcomes, or a customs-and-freight orchestration stack that takes two years to build and cannot be bought. The dossier names neither. What it names is 30% average price reduction, 24-hour first response, and 2× faster sourcing — operational KPIs, not architectural commitments. This is iteration-mode framing, the indefinite optimism Zero to One was written against, and at pre-seed it is the modal failure pattern.

Mechanism on founder lineage over 18 months: Johann Stürken's family trading-goods business is the strongest signal in the dossier — it is the channel-access analogue that gave Affirm's Levchin re-up its loading. But family-trading-business is not shipped-to-scale operator pedigree in the Moskovitz-from-Facebook sense; it is founder-market fit, which is a different and weaker signal. Het Dave's AppliedAI/FINN work on AI agent evaluation is the relevant technical pedigree, but FINN is a car-subscription company, not a procurement primitive. The 18-month compounding looks like German Mittelstand pilot conversions through family networks, which is real but bounded — it does not scale to the Xometry buyer base without a US enterprise sales motion that two founders split across a +1 415 and +49 172 phone line cannot run.

Single-anchor parallel on the atoms-heavy axis: Andustry is the inverse of Anduril (2017). Anduril took the atoms-heavy side of defense-tech because Lockheed and Raytheon could not iterate software fast enough on hardware they manufactured. Andustry is asset-light brokerage *over* atoms it does not own — the manufacturers own the capex, the suppliers own the inventory, Andustry owns the agent workflow. That places Andustry on the wrong side of the Founders Fund 2011 manifesto. The atoms in the transaction do not become a moat for Andustry; they become a moat for whoever owns the supplier relationship, which today is Alibaba, Xometry, and MISUMI.

Andustry in 36 months reaches $1-4M in commission revenue on GMV concentrated in German Mittelstand and US SME manufacturers, fails to raise a competitive Series A because Xometry and MISUMI extend AI-broker functionality natively, and either acquihires into Xometry or MISUMI for under $25M or pivots into a vertical-specific procurement layer (likely CNC or industrial castings, given the $1.2M and $800K testimonial concentration). The mechanism is incumbent-extension speed beating fast-follower differentiation in a category where the dataset compounds with transaction volume and the incumbents already have the volume.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass: Stürken's family trading business could be the Quora-style override — Adam D'Angelo's Facebook CTO pedigree carried Quora past a missing definite-plan and a contested monopoly trajectory because the founder lineage was load-bearing enough alone. If Stürken's family relationships convert into exclusive multi-year framework agreements with named German industrials — Bosch tier-two suppliers, Siemens procurement, ZF Friedrichshafen — that becomes a switching-cost moat invisible to Xometry's American sales motion. The dismantling: Quora's override was a single founder with a public, verified senior IC role at a generation-defining company; Stürken's family business is a private trading firm with no disclosed scale or named counterparties in the dossier. The verifiable condition that flips Andustry to invest is a signed, named, multi-year framework agreement with one of the top-twenty European industrials. Without that, this is consensus-shaped AI-for-procurement, and the rival fund herding into it can write the check.
