# Madrone -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Madrone is a real-atoms company built around a known-physics secret — Akshay Trikha and Erik Meike are shipping modular dew-point cooling units into Texas hyperscale buildouts, which is hardware capex with a clear thermodynamic mechanism, but the "different thermodynamic process" on madrone.cool is evaporative cooling that any HVAC PhD has known about for fifty years. The dimensional signature is atoms-heavy-high, contrarian-secret-low: a company that makes a physical thing in a sector starved for physical things, but whose thesis terminates at a 30% efficiency claim rather than at a structural reason Johnson Controls cannot ship the same unit next quarter.

On the contrarian-secret ladder, the HIGH rung is **Anduril** (2017) — Luckey naming Lockheed's cost-plus structure as the specific reason primes cannot ship software-defined defense — and the LOW rung is the "AI-powered platform for vertical X" pass archetype that saturates the 2024-26 YC batches. Madrone sits one notch above LOW, not at MID. The dossier names an incumbent class (Johnson Controls, Carrier, Trane) and even a cannibalization mechanism (installed-base service revenue), but Madrone's founders do not name the structural reason JCI cannot build a chiller-less unit themselves. JCI's May 2026 acquisition of Alloy Enterprises is the dossier's own evidence that the incumbent failure mode is "buy, not can't" — which is the opposite of the Anduril secret. Madrone has a product hypothesis, not a Zero to One secret.

On the atoms-heavy ladder, the HIGH rung is **SpaceX** (2008) — reusable launch as multi-year capex moat — and the LOW rung is the pure-software YC SaaS default. Madrone sits between MID (**Modern Meadow**) and HIGH: modular cooling units sold capex-style to hyperscalers in arid Texas climates is genuinely atoms-heavy and the kind of bits-not-atoms inversion the Founders Fund manifesto called for. This is the dimension where Madrone earns real credit. The 40% data-center cooling power figure on madrone.cool points at a category where physical units, not code, are the bottleneck — and where capex barriers do compound if Madrone gets even one hyperscaler reference deployment. The atoms are real; the question is whether the secret behind them is.

On the monopoly ladder, **Palantir** (2003) is HIGH — government-as-customer with 15-year integration moat — and the LOW rung is the consensus AI SaaS pitch where the moat hypothesis terminates at "we have a better model." Madrone sits closer to LOW: the dossier itself flags commoditization risk, names dew-point cooling as a known thermodynamic principle, and notes that **Corintis** ($58M raised, ~8-digit revenue, Microsoft partner per Sacra) and **Accelsius** ($50M, Equinix relationship) are already operating in the adjacent chip-cooling category with real revenue. There is no pricing-power hypothesis 5 years out — only a 30% efficiency wedge that an incumbent can match by acquiring Alloy or Madrone itself. On the definite-plan ladder, **SpaceX**'s 15-year reusability roadmap is HIGH, "iterate to PMF" is LOW, and Madrone sits at MID-LOW: the greenfield-plus-retrofit product split is a configuration choice, not a multi-year named milestone the founders have committed not to pivot from.

On the founder-lineage ladder, **Affirm** (2014, Levchin) is HIGH — PayPal Mafia operator who shipped to scale — **Asana** (2010, Moskovitz from Facebook) is MID and the relevant Known Exception, and the first-time-Stanford-CS pass archetype is LOW. Madrone sits between LOW and MID. Meike's Apple iPhone wireless power role is real hardware experience, and Trikha's QuantumScape ML and Berkeley interatomic-potentials work is technically substantive — but neither shipped a company, and the Asana override required Moskovitz to have built **Facebook** (2004), not contributed a subsystem to someone else's product. Neither founder has disclosed HVAC, refrigeration cycle, or data center facility experience, which is the domain credibility hyperscaler procurement teams actually price.

Madrone gets acquired by Johnson Controls, Carrier, or Trane for $30-80M within 36 months if it lands a hyperscaler pilot, or winds down in pilot purgatory if it does not — the JCI-Alloy and Flex-JetCool precedents in the dossier are not bull signals, they are the modal outcome compressing Madrone's upside ceiling below fund-returner shape. The mechanism is straightforward: the incumbent cannibalization risk the dossier flags is also the incumbent acquisition appetite, and a two-person Harvey Mudd team selling capex equipment into multi-year hyperscale procurement cycles is exactly the profile that gets rolled up before it gets to monopoly pricing.

The strongest bull case is the **Asana** exception: operator pedigree overriding missing structural moat, plus the Texas data center buildout being a real demand wave where even the second-best dew-point unit could ride a 31.5% CAGR liquid-cooling market to a meaningful business. Meike at Apple iPhone wireless power is not nothing — that is shipping atoms inside a billion-unit product. NVIDIA Inception could become a real GPU-customer introduction channel. The dismantling: the Asana override required Moskovitz to have shipped Facebook end-to-end, not a subsystem; Madrone's secret terminates at a thermodynamic process the incumbents can replicate or acquire, which is the opposite of the integration-depth moat that made Palantir uncopyable; and a 31.5% CAGR market with active incumbent M&A is a category where capital herds, not where contrarian secrets are mispriced. The verifiable condition that flips the verdict: a signed pilot with a named Tier-1 hyperscaler (Microsoft, Meta, AWS, Google) at production water volumes within 12 months — that would convert the founder-domain-gap concern into a validated outsider-credentialed thesis, and would force a re-read of the secret.
