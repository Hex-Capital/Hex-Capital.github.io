# Intelligence Factory -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Intelligence Factory is the robot-foundation-model equivalent of a Harvey-clone arriving after the winners have been declared -- the catalyst (VLA generalization step-function in 2024-2026) is real and named, but Yash Sinha and Jalaj Shukla are entering a category where Skild AI is already at $14B post-money on $30M of 2025 revenue and Physical Intelligence is at $5.6B on a $470M war chest. That high-catalyst / low-index-position pairing is the analytical signature, and it's the same signature that made me pass on most "OpenAI for X" pitches once *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) listed the winners.

**Anduril** (2022) is the right HIGH rung for catalyst quality -- Ukraine re-opened defense procurement in a 24-month window, and the dossier on Intelligence Factory does name an analogous step-function: ?-zero and Skild Brain crossed a generalization threshold and $1.4B + $600M + $52M (Skild, Physical Intelligence, RoboForce) flowed in within twelve months. That is a credible re-opening, not a steady-state TAM-math pitch. Where Intelligence Factory diverges from the Anduril template is the *timing inside* the window: Anduril was a $4.6B post-money lead that I wrote into a market with no consensus winner; Intelligence Factory is a pre-seed entrant into a market where Figure is already at $39B and the foundation-model layer has three funded incumbents shipping commercial deployments. The catalyst is real -- I score it accordingly -- but a real catalyst with the winners already capitalized is the **Lyft** Series C setup, not the **Anduril** setup.

The **Lyft** Series C pass mechanism was structural: #2 in a winner-take-all-or-oligopoly market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and no dossier evidence showed a path to share inversion. That same pattern is present in Intelligence Factory's distribution position -- the dossier names Skild AI ($14B, omni-bodied VLA, $30M revenue), Physical Intelligence ($5.6B, ?-zero licensing), Figure ($39B vertically integrated), 1X (~$10B), and Tesla Optimus as the field Sinha and Shukla must beat for the same OEM-licensing dollar. Intelligence Factory's *only* claimed wedge is tactile/force data from a custom human-demonstration device, which is a modality argument, not a distribution argument; Skild and Physical Intelligence can hire equivalent UPenn GRASP graduates and stand up a tactile pipeline if the modality proves to matter. This is the dimension that breaks the deal for me.

Revenue velocity for Intelligence Factory is the **Harvey** (2023) inverse -- Harvey gave me $3B → $5B → $8B in months and the curve told me to pay up; Intelligence Factory gives me "No public data found" on revenue, a near-blank landing page at intelligence-factory.com labeled "Generalized Robotics Manipulation," and a single Founding Engineer job posting at $80K-$200K + 0.10-5.00% equity. Pre-seed companies don't owe me revenue, but "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) -- and the absence of any benchmark, model release, or video demo from Intelligence Factory in a category where Skild publishes commercial deployments is not stage-appropriate silence, it's category-laggard silence.

The single-miracle test is where Intelligence Factory compounds against itself over 18 months. A clean single-miracle architecture would isolate tactile-policy accuracy as the validated capability and treat throughput, GTM, and OEM integration as execution. Intelligence Factory instead requires *three* independent miracles: tactile/force scaling laws must work (unproven publicly), the custom data-collection hardware must produce demonstrations at throughput and cost that beat video-pretrained baselines, and Sinha and Shukla must outmaneuver $2B+ of incumbent capital on talent and compute. Compound three independent low-probability events and you get the math from the NFX *Unusual Signs* essay -- this is the science-project capitalization risk I named explicitly on Tim Ferriss #863.

Founder execution rhythm for Intelligence Factory is a counterfactual exercise: what would have to be true to flip this dimension a tier? I'd need Sinha or Shukla to have a public GitHub of contact-rich manipulation policies, a named prior shipped product from Kaio Labs or the UPenn GRASP Lab that an industrial customer used in production, or the **Perplexity** (2022) pattern -- a working tactile-demo artifact pinged to me between meetings. The dossier gives me ETH Zurich RSL, UPenn GRASP, DRDO, Sauber F1, and a co-founder/CTO title at Kaio Labs with no shipped artifact named. That is research pedigree, which is the *promoter-versus-shipper* distinction from the Hustle Commons summary of my framework -- credentials without artifacts is the disqualifier.

My 36-month forecast for Intelligence Factory: acqui-hired by Skild AI or Physical Intelligence for sub-$50M for the tactile-data pipeline and the Sinha/Shukla team, or pivoted to a vertical pick-and-place niche (warehouse manipulation or contract assembly) with sub-$5M ARR and a flat Series A. The mechanism is the high-catalyst/low-index-position signature -- the category grows fast enough that the incumbents need bolt-on tactile capability, but Intelligence Factory does not sit in the workflow path that compounding adoption flows through.

The strongest objection is the **Color Genomics** (2015) Known Exception: tactile/force data is a genuinely scarce modality that internet-video pretraining cannot replicate, and if Sinha and Shukla compound a proprietary dataset on top of a commodity hardware substrate, that *is* the "workflow infrastructure on top of a hard thing" pattern I keep funding. The bear case answer is that Color worked because I operated it and architected the workflow layer myself, and because Othman Laraki had shipped at Twitter -- Intelligence Factory's dossier shows zero shipped artifacts from either founder, a single-page placeholder website, and three concurrent miracles rather than one. The verifiable condition that flips me to Invest: a publicly released tactile-manipulation benchmark from Intelligence Factory beating ?-zero on a contact-rich task, *plus* one named industrial OEM pilot, inside six months. Absent that, this is a Harvey-clone in robot brains at the wrong end of the catalyst window.
