# Foaster -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Let me be direct: "AI-native consulting firm" is four words that describe a thousand YC applications this batch. When I see a company whose stated ambition is to make McKinsey 3-5x cheaper, I ask the same question I always ask — if this fully works, does the world look different? And the answer here is no. Cheaper consulting for enterprises is a cost optimization in a B2B services market. It doesn't collapse the price of a basic human need. It doesn't create new demand that didn't exist before. Nobody's life fundamentally changes because their company's process mapping engagement cost $150K instead of $500K. The consequence of full success is a large, profitable professional services company. That's fine for most investors. It's not fine for me.

The competitive landscape tells the story. Distyl AI has $202 million. Aily Labs has $101 million. PromptQL has $136 million. Skan.ai has $57 million and $22 million in revenue. Dialogue AI is already conducting AI interviews at scale, just pointed externally instead of internally. Foaster is a two-person pre-seed team entering a market where well-capitalized competitors are converging from every adjacent angle. And the core technology — LLM-based interviews and synthesis — runs on generally available foundation models. There's no proprietary science here. No step-function technical breakthrough. When I backed Rocket Lab, Peter Beck had built fundamentally new rocket engine architecture — electric turbopumps, 3D-printed combustion chambers. That's a non-linear approach. Using GPT-class models to conduct employee interviews and generate recommendations is the definition of a wrapper, not a wedge. The 3-5x cost improvement confirms it — that's incremental, not discontinuous.

The strongest signal in this dossier is the founders themselves. Two 23-year-old French data science students from ENSAE Paris attacking McKinsey's business model is exactly the outsider irreverence I celebrate. Combes built Repondia, where AI agents handled 60,000+ real customer calls — that's direct operational experience with the core mechanism. Their Werewolf Benchmark on LLM social intelligence got 300,000+ views, was shared by OpenAI's president, and drew contact from xAI's co-founder. That's not nothing. These are young people who have clearly demonstrated high learning rates across very different domains — Dabadie went from building a 400K-user tennis community at age 11 to LLM behavioral research to enterprise consulting. The intellectual trajectory is real. If I were evaluating these founders on a different company — one where the consequence of success was civilizational — I'd be more interested.

Here's the bull case I genuinely considered: if Foaster builds a persistent operational map of every client organization — a living digital twin of how companies actually work — that becomes an intelligence asset with compounding value. Each engagement generates proprietary data that no competitor can replicate without re-interviewing the entire organization. Over time, this could evolve from engagement-based consulting into a continuous organizational intelligence platform. The innovator's dilemma is real — McKinsey, BCG, and Bain are structurally incapable of offering a 3-5x cheaper alternative because it would cannibalize their leverage model. And if the AI interview quality crosses a threshold where it genuinely captures organizational dynamics that human consultants miss through their sampling approach, you could see consulting outcomes that are not just cheaper but actually better. The "100% of teams included" claim is a meaningful differentiation from the traditional approach of interviewing 15 people and extrapolating. That's a scenario where I'd look more carefully.

But even that bull case produces a consequence bounded by the consulting market. Consulting is not healthcare, energy, food, education, or legal services. It's not a basic human need being held hostage by legacy cost structures. It's a service purchased by companies that already have money. Making it cheaper doesn't democratize anything that matters to the median human being. I don't invest to generate 3-5x returns on enterprise SaaS economics. I want to either lose my money or make 50 times my money. This company is designed to generate the returns profile I explicitly avoid — competent execution in a competitive market producing moderate outcomes. The founders deserve a better swing than this.
