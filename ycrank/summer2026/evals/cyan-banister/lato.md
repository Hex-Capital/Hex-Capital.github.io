# LATO -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

LATO is what happens when a buyer-side authentic founder walks into the most crowded software lane of 2026 -- Tymek Staniszewski spent six years at Verdane ($11B AUM) actually paying for the $300k commercial studies he is now trying to replace, and that biography is real, but he is replacing them in the exact same week Bridgetown Research closes a $19M Series A from Accel and Lightspeed for the identical thesis. The dimensional signature on LATO is a high lived-experience score paired with a near-zero "magically weird" score, and that pairing has a name in my portfolio: it is the inverse of Crusoe. Crusoe was a founder *of* a strange place doing a thing nobody else would touch. LATO is a founder *of* a place doing a thing five funded competitors are touching this quarter.

Anduril is my high rung on founder-lived-experience -- Palmer Luckey shipping VR from a garage before he ever pitched defense -- and the load-bearing analogy to LATO is that Tymek's six years at Verdane gave him the social license to walk into Blume Equity, FoodLabs, Montis VC, and Concept Ventures and not flinch, the way Luckey could walk into Anduril's first DoD meetings. Where LATO diverges from the Anduril rung is that Tymek was a *consumer* of the diligence artifact, not a *builder* of it -- he commissioned the studies; he did not staff the McKinsey desk that produced them. Tien Chu's eight years in ML and a CS-Math-Philosophy degree from Warsaw is real polymath texture and the brother-ran-ElevenLabs proximity is the kind of family voice-AI lineage that I would normally lean into, but the lived-problem ownership on the LATO dossier sits one rung below the Anduril template -- closer to a senior PE associate who watched the line item than to the founder who *was* the line item.

Bridgetown Research closing $19M from Accel and Lightspeed in February 2026, DiligenceSquared closing $5M from Relentless in March 2026, Listen Labs at $100M total raised in January 2026, AlphaSense at $600M ARR with the Tegus archive already inside every fund LATO would sell to -- that is not a competitive set, that is a deselection signal. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus, and LATO is arriving after consensus has been priced, capitalized, and distributed across three rounds. The Crusoe move was that two or three credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds before I wrote the check; the LATO move is that ten credible VCs have already *written* the check, just to someone else. The pattern in the LATO dossier is not a rebuttal I can argue with -- it is the YC AI-agent darling pattern with a slightly more authentic founder bolted onto the front.

On governance, LATO lists two co-founders with no designated CEO in the dossier, no public history of prior collaboration, no documented operating agreement -- this is the silence case I default at 6/16, because the HQ Trivia post-mortem taught me that two unranked founders is a structural fault line waiting for the first hard quarter. LATO has not had the tiebreaker conversation in any way the public record can confirm, and a B2B sales war against a Series-A-funded direct competitor is exactly the kind of crucible where that conversation gets forced at the worst possible moment.

LATO is software end-to-end -- Gmail, Slack, Excel integrations, AI voice agents, a "Company Brain" knowledge graph, a market-simulation layer of unproven accuracy -- which puts the capital-intensity score in the bottom rung where the SaaS wrapper lives, not the Anduril or SpaceX or Crusoe lane that defines my hardware sleeve. The eighteen-month compounding question is whether the proprietary corpus of investor-conducted interviews becomes a switching-cost moat or stays a feature; with Bridgetown's $19M war chest and AlphaSense's distribution, LATO does not have the runway to win the corpus race on the merits, and the moat I would normally pay for here is structurally available to the incumbent first.

Physical verifiability on LATO is the dimension that would flip if I could install it, ride it, or watch a normal human use it -- I cannot. The product is a B2B abstraction sold to fund GPs; I do not get the Niantic-Ingress moment of playing it on a subway platform, and I do not get the Flock Safety Wi-Fi-name-at-the-Four-Seasons moment of stumbling into it in my physical environment. The closest thing in the dossier is the "100 selected founders" feature Tymek built in Europe, which is a participant-side artifact but not the tactile object I rely on to verify a thesis without founder testimony.

LATO at 36 months: most likely a sub-$5M ARR acqui-hire into AlphaSense or a tooling layer absorbed by Bridgetown after Accel doubles down -- not dead, but not a fund-returner, because the lane is being consolidated by capital LATO does not have and an incumbent corpus LATO cannot rebuild. The mechanism is the AlphaSense-plus-Tegus distribution loop closing around the diligence workflow before LATO can convert its four named VC logos into a defensible PE book.

The strongest bull case is that Tymek's Verdane network is the actual product -- six years of European growth-PE relationships plus the brother-ElevenLabs voice-AI lineage means LATO sources warm GP introductions Bridgetown cannot, and the four named clients (Blume Equity, FoodLabs, Montis VC, Concept Ventures) are leading indicators of a relationship moat that compounds quietly while better-funded competitors burn cash on outbound. That case stings because relationship-sourced GTM is exactly the unstated pattern I respect. It loses on the math: Bridgetown raised 38x LATO's $500K YC check the same quarter LATO entered the batch, AlphaSense already sits inside every fund Tymek would call, and a relationship moat does not survive a competitor offering the same product for free as a loss leader. The condition that would flip my verdict: LATO showing $1M+ ARR sourced from *PE* buyers, not VC pilots, within twelve months -- because PE budgets do not contract on the LP cycle and a PE book would prove the founder-network thesis at a scale Bridgetown cannot easily replicate.
