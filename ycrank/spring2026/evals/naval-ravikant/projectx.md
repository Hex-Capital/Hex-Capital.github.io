# ProjectX -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

ProjectX is a single-sided GPU-virtualization play in a large DaaS market -- highest on pivot-room inside a $10.5B Gartner-sized category, lowest on network-effect compounding because each "infinity" session is economically independent of the next, and that pairing tells me what ProjectX actually is: a capital-leverage infra company wearing browser-delivered clothing. Rounak Adhikary's pitch -- "each app in its own independent computer with its own GPU" -- is a sentence about GPU rental cost structure, not about media or code leverage.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung on leverage architecture: media-as-protocol, marginal cost of replication is zero, anyone posts without permission. ProjectX's architecture inverts that mechanism -- per-app dedicated GPUs (YC page) means the marginal cost of every new "infinity" session is a metered GPU-hour from a hyperscaler or a colo, and the distribution surface is browser-delivered but the economics are capital-permissioned all the way down. ProjectX diverges from the Twitter rung not on the front-end (browser delivery is fine) but on what compounds underneath -- compute that costs real money per session does not compound the founder's specific knowledge the way a tweet or a Stack Overflow answer does. This is the same category where I scored Anchorage Digital down on criterion #1 before overriding for category-timing; ProjectX has no equivalent override available because GPU-DaaS is not a pre-consensus category in 2026 -- DigitalOcean already paid $111M cash for Paperspace in July 2023.

Single-sided SaaS subscription products where each customer is economically independent of the next -- that is the LOW-rung failure mechanism on network value, and ProjectX's per-app VM session model sits squarely inside it. The 1,000th ProjectX user does not make the 999 prior users' sessions cheaper, faster, or more valuable; there is no creator side seeding a consumer side, no developer community whose answers compound, no marketplace flywheel where supply attracts demand. Compare to Uber (2010), where every additional driver lifted ETA-density for every rider in a coupled loop -- ProjectX has no such coupling documented. The dossier names "Marketplace" as a YC tag but supplies zero mechanics to justify it; this is single-sided infrastructure billed by consumption.

Stack Overflow's Atwood and Spolsky shipped Coding Horror and Joel-on-Software for years before the company existed -- that is the specific-knowledge bar. Bishal Karmakar's LinkedIn lists "systems, streaming, cloud infra" focus with Stanford ASES and Draper U affiliations, and Rounak Adhikary self-funded ProjectX through his prior Woldix consulting venture -- credible but not load-bearing. No GitHub repos are found for any of the three co-founders; no public technical writing on GPU virtualization, no open-source hypervisor contributions, no shipped artifact in the per-app-VM space predating ProjectX. Over 18 months this dimension compounds only if Karmakar ships something publicly that the cloud-infra X/HN community circulates organically -- the dossier as it stands shows zero such artifact, and absent that, ProjectX's technical credibility is asserted not demonstrated.

DaaS at $10.5B in 2026 (Gartner) growing 19.4% CAGR (Research Nester) is genuinely platform-shaped -- adjacent pivot vectors include agent-sandbox-as-a-service for computer-use models, GPU dev environments for ML practitioners, and cloud gaming. ProjectX's "when half the users of a computer are not people" framing (Inc42) is the agent-sandbox pivot already pre-articulated; that is real optionality and the strongest line in the dossier. For this dimension to flip into top-quartile, ProjectX would need to show one signed enterprise design-partner for the agent-sandbox use case -- not a generic LOI but a named lab or coding-agent company committing to ProjectX as runtime. The dossier names no such counterparty.

ProjectX's original 2022 founding triad was Adhikary, Ronit Banerjee, and Suvrakamal Das per Tracxn; the YC P26 triad is Adhikary, Karmakar, and Majumder -- two of three original co-founders are gone with no public explanation. "Picking a co-founder is your most important decision. It's more important than your product, market, and investors" (Venture Hacks). I am not assuming bad behavior -- co-founder churn at age-22-CEO startups is statistically ordinary -- but at angel-check size I want to spend ten years with this human, and the dossier shows me a CEO who has already replaced two-thirds of his original partnership inside four years. Integrity reads clean, energy reads high (WTC award, Princeton Tiger Launch, IIM Bangalore Launchpad), but the partnership track record is the cold spot.

36-month forecast: ProjectX is acquired by a tier-2 cloud or GPU-cloud incumbent (CoreWeave, Lambda, DigitalOcean, or a regional hyperscaler) in the $20-60M range as an acqui-hire for the per-app session orchestration stack, OR pivots explicitly to agent-sandbox-runtime and reaches $1-3M ARR selling to AI-agent startups -- the DigitalOcean-Paperspace template ($111M cash, July 2023) is the precedent and ProjectX is structurally smaller and earlier on that same curve.

The bull case that stings: ProjectX is Anchorage Digital's structural analogue -- capital-permissioned leverage that becomes its own moat when the category timing arrives, and "agent-native OS" in 2026 is the pre-consensus window the way regulated crypto custody was in 2018. If Karmakar's per-app-GPU orchestration genuinely cold-starts in seconds at a density hyperscalers cannot match, ProjectX becomes the default sandbox runtime for the coming agent economy and the leverage rule flips from "permissionless" to "permission-as-moat." Dismantling this: Anchorage had a Treasury OCC charter -- a hard regulatory wall that took years and capital to clear. ProjectX has no analogous moat clause in the dossier, no patents named, no exclusive GPU supply, and the named competitors (Neverinstall, Kasm, Frame, AWS WorkSpaces, plus DigitalOcean-via-Paperspace) are all already in market. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: ProjectX publishes a per-session GPU cost curve and density benchmark beating AWS WorkSpaces GPU SKUs by 3x or better, AND ships an open-source orchestration component that earns organic GitHub traction in the agent-runtime community within six months. Until then, this is capital leverage in a category with active incumbent M&A -- I pass.
