# Hexa -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Hexa is, on my framework, the textbook signature of a low-consequence vertical SaaS wrapper with the founder profile silent on the one axis I weight as a multiplier -- automating quoting, RFQs, invoice matching, and customer service inside SAP, NetSuite, Dynamics, and Epicor for manufacturers. The dimensional signature is low Consequence Magnitude paired with absent Founder-Defies-Expert-Consensus evidence; that pairing is the Pass Example I described from memory, not a borderline case.

Consequence Magnitude is where Hexa lands the worst, and the ladder makes the placement unambiguous. The HIGH rung is **OpenAI** (2019), where my $50M check bought exposure to a civilizationally distinct success-state -- 80% of economically valuable work, $15T of GDP shifting, per Fortune 2026-03-06. The MID rung is **DoorDash** (2013), capital-light but consequence-real because it reshaped how consumer commerce flows. The LOW rung is the GPT-4-summarizes-sales-calls pitch from my Pass Example -- a 20-30% efficiency gain inside one workflow. Hexa sits one step above that LOW rung: the success-state described on hexaagents.com is "manufacturers and distributors stop copy-pasting from email into ERP" -- which is real labor but does not collapse SAP, does not displace a category, and does not move a named civilizational variable. Ishaan Makkar's own framing -- "54% of manufacturing companies still rely on spreadsheets" -- is the spreadsheet-to-software substitution that consensus VCs fund every quarter.

Founder Defies Expert Consensus is where Hexa is lowest relative to my anchors. The HIGH rung is **Physical Intelligence** (2024), where Sergey Levine and Chelsea Finn carried documented contrarian research positions in robotics. The MID rung is **Glean** -- Arvind Jain is ex-Google search, a paradigmatic domain-expert profile, which I admitted was a known exception powered by consequence scale. The LOW rung is the "20 years at Salesforce, now applying same playbook with AI" archetype from my TED Talk warning. Ishaan Makkar's LinkedIn headline is "Stealth"; the dossier returns no education, no prior employment, no published thinking, no contrarian position, and no manufacturing or ERP operator history. No evidence -- applying absent-evidence default of 8/20, then pulling it down because the dossier silence is total, not partial. The Glean override does not rescue Hexa here because the Glean override required consequence-magnitude top-quartile, and Hexa has consequence-magnitude bottom-quartile.

Technical or Capital Risk that repels consensus VCs is also low for Hexa. The HIGH rung is **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) at $115M Series A on a physics miracle that locked out generalist funds; the MID rung is **Cognition** (2024) where stack-deep AI engineering required real foundation-model investment; the LOW rung is any LangChain-orchestration wrapper. Hexa is closer to LangChain than to Cognition: the dossier names n8n/LangChain-class orchestration toolchains as direct entrants, lists eight ERP integrations a three-person team is allegedly maintaining, and the named competitor set -- Distro, Turian, Go Autonomous, Arphie, Altura, Tessera -- is the exact "consensus-fundable" crowd I dodge. There is no science risk here that scares Sequoia off.

Founder Learning Rate and Required-Capital Profile both fall to defaults. My HIGH rung on learning rate is **Slash** (2024) with the documented Kanye-pivot story; the LOW rung is a founder who has executed one linear plan. The dossier produces no pivot, no prior exit, no public thesis update for Ishaan Makkar -- absent-evidence default of 7/15, no upward adjustment justified. On capital, the HIGH rung is **Commonwealth Fusion**'s $115M Series A; Hexa's roadmap is SaaS-commodity, demo-led, $5-10M to $1M ARR territory -- I am over-resourced for this deal, and the $3.5B fund I raised in February 2025 has no productive home in a Hexa-shaped check.

My 36-month forecast for Hexa: by May 2029 the company is either dead, acqui-hired by an RPA or mid-market ERP integrator for under $30M, or has narrowed to a single-ERP wedge (most likely NetSuite quoting) at sub-$3M ARR -- the mechanism is incumbent substitution by SAP Joule and Dynamics Copilot eating the integration surface from above while Distro and Turian eat the vertical specialization from the side, with a 3-person team unable to defend eight ERP integrations on either flank.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the Glean override pattern: AI-for-back-office-of-an-underdigitized-vertical can be a Fortune-500-scale outcome, and "54% of manufacturers on spreadsheets" is a labor-cost displacement story that maps cleanly to my own $15T GDP frame from Fortune 2026-03-06. If Hexa is the Glean of manufacturing operations, I should be writing the check. But the Glean override required Arvind Jain's documented PI-level technical reputation plus a Fortune-500 information-asymmetry consequence -- Hexa has neither. Ishaan Makkar is publicly "Stealth," and the consequence ceiling is mid-market manufacturer back-office labor, not the $15T frame at full extension. McKinsey predicted under 1M U.S. cellphones by 2000; the actual was 109M -- but McKinsey's failure was on direction, not on whether RPA-for-manufacturing-quoting could become civilizational, which it cannot. One condition flips my verdict: Ishaan Makkar turns out to have shipped production ERP integrations at a Tier-1 manufacturer for five-plus years AND Hexa lands three signed LOIs with named mid-market manufacturers before Demo Day. Absent both, this is the 30-second pass I described.
