# Alchemize -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Alchemize is what happens when "AI is just more internet" gets refracted through the 2026 YC batch and pointed at a regulated logistics workflow -- Samuel Fu and Robert Hou are running at a $5.17B US customs brokerage TAM with two engineers and no disclosed CBP license, while Amari AI already closed $4.5M from First Round and Pear in February 2026 and Caspian closed $5.4M from Primary in July 2025 with the broker license already in hand. The dimensional signature here is bleak in a specific way: the highest-scoring axis is "category has a regulatory wrap" -- which is a generous read -- and the lowest is founder conviction, because nothing in the dossier shows Samuel Fu or Robert Hou has ever filed a Type 11 entry, written publicly about HTS classification, or even maintained a Twitter account. That pairing is the analytical signature: Alchemize is a vertical AI wrapper applied to a real regulated category by founders whose pre-pitch artifacts are indistinguishable from any other Spring 2026 P26 cohort team.

Take the canonical Pass Example I keep returning to -- the 2026 YC seed pitch that's "AI agent for [vertical], four tier-1s already in." Alchemize is structurally the same trade, just with the consensus heat shifted one tick earlier: Amari AI emerged from stealth three months before Alchemize's batch with First Round and Pear AND 30 customers AND $15B of goods already cleared, so the round Alchemize is raising isn't "pre-consensus" -- it's "post-consensus arriving late." The HIGH rung on this dimension is Solana at $0.05/coin where I bought because every crypto fund had quit; the divergence is that the customs-AI category has *already attracted* $4.5M and $5.4M priced seeds in the last 10 months, plus Flexport's incumbent AI auditor reporting 0.2% error rate. Alchemize's round is sitting at standard YC SAFE pricing into a category two better-capitalized peers already validated. There is no price discount to absorb the disadvantage.

The LOW rung on founder conviction is the sanitized strategic-consulting pitch with zero public writing on the category -- the dossier shows Alchemize matches that pattern almost exactly: no Twitter for either founder, no GitHub repos, no essay on why HTS classification automation is non-obvious, no LinkedIn snippet revealing customs operating background for Samuel Fu beyond "Columbia graduate," and a Robert Hou profile so ambiguous the research couldn't even disambiguate him from the Confido S21 founder. Compare the Amari team in the same dossier: a former senior LinkedIn engineer plus an operator co-founder, named publicly. The "what if they're right?" inversion requires a claim to invert; Alchemize hasn't published one. This is the absent-evidence default territory and the absence is not coy -- it's actual.

Capital structure on Alchemize compounds the wrong way over 18 months. Per-entry brokerage fees are decent unit economics for a licensed broker at scale, but the *path* to scale is multi-round: get the CBP license (or partner with a licensed broker and pay the rake), hire compliance staff per state, build enough entry volume to amortize the AI tooling, then raise a Series A against KlearNow's 2,000-customer head start. That's a four-tack regatta minimum, and Alchemize hasn't shown me tack one. The Slow letter line about "capital efficiency as the guiding light" inverts cleanly here: this is the opposite of default-alive in 24 months on a 2-person team competing against $66M of KlearNow capital.

Counterfactual on founder-as-brand: for this dimension to flip a tier on Alchemize, Samuel Fu would need to be the licensed customs broker himself with a public newsletter read by trade-compliance officers at mid-market importers -- a John Fish / Bookshelved profile inside customs Twitter. He's not. Customs is a domain where named operator trust *would* be the moat, which makes the absence of any founder media footprint more damning, not less, even though B2B logistics doesn't technically require it. The Slow Creator Fund framing doesn't rescue Alchemize because there's no creator here to fund.

Single-anchor parallel on category: Alchemize wants me to read it like Ro -- regulated rails, licensing schleps, "the licensing IS the moat." Ro is the Known Exception that lets me underwrite capital intensity when regulation is the moat. But Ro had Zachariah Reitano building DTC distribution against a fragmented prescriber market with no incumbent platform; Alchemize is building against Flexport ($2B+ raised, AI auditor already shipping) and KlearNow (2,000 customers) and two peer seed companies that started earlier. The Ro override requires the regulatory complexity to *exclude* competitors. CBP licensing has not excluded Caspian, Amari, KlearNow, or Flexport. The exception doesn't apply.

Forecast: by May 2029 Alchemize is either acqui-hired by Flexport or KlearNow for sub-$15M as a team-and-tech tuck-in, or it's dead and the founders are at their next YC company. The mechanism is straightforward -- Amari and Caspian convert their capital advantage into the licensed-broker partnerships and enterprise importer logos first, Flexport's auditor eats the mid-market wedge from the incumbent side, and Alchemize runs out of differentiation before its Series A.

The strongest objection: the Trump tariff regime in 2025 genuinely did 10x broker workloads (NCBFAA, June 2025; Washington Post, November 2025), the market is fragmented with 65.46% held by 3PL-integrated operators, and a sharp 2-person team with Andrew Miklas as YC partner could ride that wave into a real business -- regulated rollups historically reward execution machines over visionaries, which is the "rizz and tizz" operator profile I'm supposed to like. The bear-case dismantling: that wave is being ridden by Amari ($15B cleared already) and Caspian (licensed already), and Alchemize hasn't shown me a single named customer, a single LOI, or a single founder signal that they'll out-execute peers with 10x the capital and a 6-month head start. The condition that flips my verdict: Alchemize discloses CBP broker licensure plus a signed enterprise contract with a named Fortune 1000 importer at >$500K ACV before September 2026. Without that, I'm out before the meeting.
