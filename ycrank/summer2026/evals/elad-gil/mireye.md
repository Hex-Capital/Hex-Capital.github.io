# Mireye -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Mireye is a shipping-founder team standing in a workflow lane that Google Maps, Mapbox, and CARTO already paved with their own MCP servers in 2025-2026 — Ansh Chokshi's reported 0→$250K ARR run at Poker Pit reads like real execution rhythm, but the index position is the one that decides this, and the index position is taken. That's the signature: a credible shipper attacking a category whose distribution path is already inside the incumbent stack.

Stripe (2010) is the canonical index-position template — Stripe didn't have to beat Braintree feature-for-feature, it sat in the developer workflow path every e-commerce entrant had to touch. Mireye wants the same posture for physical-world AI agents, and the wedge is real in theory: federal-grade geospatial as a citation-bearing MCP endpoint. The dossier breaks the analogy in one line — "Google Maps Platform, Mapbox, and CARTO have already shipped MCP servers/grounding for AI agents in 2025-2026." Stripe in 2010 was *building* the rail because no incumbent had one; Mireye in 2026 is the fourth MCP into a category where the developer who already pays Google Maps just toggles a flag. That is the opposite of an index position — it is share-fighting against named workflow incumbents, which is the **Lyft** Series C pass pattern dressed in MCP clothing.

Lyft Series C failed my test because #2 in a winner-take-all distribution market captures a fraction of #1's economics — the underlying mechanism was that the channel (driver/rider liquidity) belonged to Uber, and Lyft had to subsidize against a structural disadvantage. The same mechanism shows up in Mireye's dossier: the channel for "geospatial answers in agent workflows" is the MCP listing inside Claude/Cursor and the default tool registries, and Google Maps Platform ships into that channel as the assumed default. Mireye's dossier names US-only coverage versus xMap's 200+ country footprint and offers no developer-signup or integration counts to suggest the channel is being won. Without that, the geospatial-MCP market looks oligopolistic-toward-incumbents, not re-opening — closer to my Lyft pass than to my Decagon yes.

The catalyst question is where Mireye gets its best honest hearing. MCP shipped from Anthropic in late 2024 and adopted broadly through 2025-2026 — that *is* a specific 24-month catalyst, the same shape as the post-ChatGPT step-function that made me lean into Mistral (2023) and Harvey (2023). Mireye is correctly skating to where the puck is. The compounding problem over 18 months is that the catalyst opens the door for *everyone* simultaneously, including the players who already own the geospatial data licenses and the developer relationships — so the catalyst's value to Mireye decays as Google/Mapbox/CARTO's MCP endpoints mature, rather than compounding the way Stripe's API-first wedge compounded against merchant-account incumbents who literally could not ship modern developer surfaces.

On revenue velocity, the dossier is silent — no MRR, no signed logos, no integration counts, no developer signups. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and silence is a negative signal in this cycle because real geospatial-MCP usage is trivial to disclose. The counterfactual that flips this tier: Mireye discloses, say, 40+ enterprise pilots with named real-estate, insurance, or logistics counterparties and >20% MoM call-volume growth — at which point this becomes a Decagon-shaped workflow-infra bet inside a still-open sub-market. Absent that disclosure I apply the AI-pre-seed default and assume the growth would be on the page if it existed.

Ansh Chokshi reportedly took Poker Pit from 0 to $250K ARR in four months before acquisition, and Shashwat Kapoor spent six years on data-infrastructure at Tools for Humanity and Lyra Health — that is the **Perplexity** (2022) shape on founder execution, not the credential-only profile I screen out. Chokshi has a named shipped artifact with a revenue arc, which is the *exact* thing I look for when separating shippers from promoters. The dossier just doesn't pair it with the Aravind-style "showed up a week later with the thing we discussed built" evidence, because there's no investor-meeting feedback loop documented yet. The shipping signal is the strongest thing in Mireye's dossier and the reason this is a Pass with a real watch-list, not a Strong Pass.

Single-miracle architecture is fine — Mireye doesn't need new physics, it needs one validated distribution wedge over incumbent MCP servers, with federal-dataset ETL and citation quality as the asserted moat. That's a clean one-miracle bet, structurally closer to **Color Genomics**' workflow-on-commodity-data architecture than to a science project, and I credit it accordingly. The miracle is just the hard one in this dossier: winning developer mindshare against Google's distribution.

Forecast, 36 months: Mireye either pivots from horizontal geospatial-MCP into a vertical agent product (real estate due-diligence, insurance underwriting, or wildfire/flood risk) where federal-dataset depth actually compounds against a domain-specific buyer, or it gets acqui-hired by CARTO or Mapbox under $25M to plug into their existing MCP surfaces — staying horizontal lands below $2M ARR. The mechanism is the index-position deficit: without owning the workflow path, Chokshi's shipping rhythm can only buy the team an exit, not a category.

The bull case against that forecast is real and stings. Incumbent MCP servers from Google/Mapbox/CARTO are often checkbox launches by platform teams that don't ship — Mireye, run by a founder who took a side project to $250K ARR in four months, can out-execute a Google product manager on developer experience, citation quality, and federal-dataset breadth. Chokshi plus Kapoor's six years of data-infra is genuinely the shape of a team that could build a defensible ETL/index layer that incumbents won't bother to match, and the *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) does treat physical-world agent infrastructure as still-open. The bear-case dismantle is simple: the channel is the MCP registry inside Claude/Cursor and the developer's default tool list, and that channel rewards the brand a developer already pays — Google Maps Platform's billing relationship with every geospatial-touching team in the US is the moat Mireye doesn't have. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: Mireye discloses 20+ named enterprise integrations with >20% MoM API-call growth and a non-Google distribution partner (Anthropic, Cursor, or a named vertical SaaS) wiring it as a default tool — at which point this becomes a Decagon-shape and I write the check.
