# Justinian -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Parth Badhwar has a real White House line on his LinkedIn and 38 followers on X, and Justinian is trying to sell "elite lobbyists with AI agents" to early-stage companies -- which means the highest thing Justinian has going is a mildly illegible services-plus-software structure that the SaaS-trained crowd can't cleanly price, and the lowest thing is that the CEO of a government-affairs firm has effectively no owned distribution and a name-collision with a competing YC S26 agent literally called "Justinian" at vulcan.ai. That pairing tells you exactly what Justinian is: a credentialed political operator's services shop wearing an AI wrapper, being force-fit into a venture cap table it probably shouldn't sit on.

On the round-pricing axis, the load-bearing analogy is **Sublime Security** -- a category tier-1s hadn't yet crowded, priced accordingly, with a real product wedge. Justinian shares the surface trait that "AI-native government affairs firm" is not the median 2026 YC deck, and FiscalNote, Quorum, POLITICO Pro, and Parsagon are all software-only, which does leave a hybrid seat open. Where Justinian diverges from Sublime is that the illegibility here is bundled human lobbyists on billable time -- the dossier's explicit "services-margin trap" flag -- not a technical wedge, and Vulcan Technologies (YC 2026) is already publicly shipping an agent named "Justinian" at vulcan.ai/agents/justinian, which means the category is more crowded inside this batch than the framing suggests. No round size disclosed, and silence on price is not a friend of mine.

The lowest ladder is founder-as-brand, and the failure mechanism is the one the Slow Creator Fund's screening explicitly avoids: no owned distribution, no compounding audience, no organic top of funnel -- a founder trying to sell to startups without any surface area to startups. Justinian's founder has 38 X followers, joined Feb 2025, no GitHub, no essays, no podcast, no policy Substack, and the demo flow runs through a personal Cal.com link. In a business where the entire thesis is "startups can't afford Brownstein Hyatt, so buy from us instead," Justinian has zero pre-existing surface with the buyer persona and will be paying for every first meeting the hard way.

Capital structure -- mechanism analysis over 18 months. Justinian is hiring a Policy Staff role at $72K-$200K plus 0.10%-2.00% equity, which tells you the plan is to buy a lobbyist bench on cash, not on brand-name partner economics; each named account will require human retainer coverage that scales roughly linearly with revenue, and the AI layer compresses the drafting hours per account but not the relationship hours. That's a 40-55% gross-margin services business dressed as a 75% SaaS story, and the "hybrid unit economics" risk the dossier itself surfaces is the correct one -- 18 months in, Justinian is either a $2M ARR boutique lobbying shop that shouldn't have taken venture, or it's on round two funding lobbyist headcount which no fund on Sand Hill wants to underwrite.

Founder conviction -- counterfactual. For Justinian to move a tier on this axis, Parth Badhwar would need to be publishing opinionated writing on why traditional GA firms fail startups, what the LDA registration schleps actually cost, or why AI drafting changes the lobbyist-hour equation -- the "Writing is thinking" filter, which he currently fails cleanly. His resume is legitimate (Deloitte, Biden-Harris White House, Harris for President, Clipbook GTM) but that's the mercenary CV of a competent operator responding to opportunity, not a missionary who has been obsessing about startup access to government for 3+ years. A single well-argued essay on the Justinian thesis would flip this dimension; its absence is the tell.

Category sleeve -- single-anchor parallel. Against my own **Ro** anchor, which is the closest thing in the portfolio to a regulated-services rollup, Justinian fails the specific test that made Ro underwritable: Ro's 50-state prescription rails were clearable only by VC-scale licensing capital, so capital intensity was the moat. Justinian's lobbying registration under the LDA is a per-lobbyist filing any registered practitioner clears in a week; the compliance overhead is real but it is not a moat, and GovTech-AI is not a Slow sleeve the way pre-consensus crypto or DTC consumer are. This is closer to the pass-example archetype -- "AI agent for [enterprise vertical]" -- than to Ro.

Forecast: 36 months out, Justinian is either wound down or quietly running as a sub-$2M ARR boutique government-affairs shop with two or three lobbyists on staff, having either failed to raise a Series A on services margins or pivoted to a software-only policy intel product that gets acqui-hired into FiscalNote or Quorum by 2029. The mechanism producing that is the services-margin trap compounding against a founder with no owned distribution: every dollar of ARR requires a human lobbyist behind it, and every meeting requires paid outbound because Parth has no audience.

The strongest bull case: Parth's White House and Harris-campaign network is a real Rolodex, AI labs spent ~$6M on federal lobbying in 2025, and a founder-led services firm serving 20 Anthropic-shaped clients at $250K retainers is a $5M revenue business inside 24 months -- and someone will invoke the Ro exception, arguing regulatory complexity is the moat. That case stings because Rolodexes do close first contracts. It loses because the Ro override requires that only venture capital can clear the regulatory schlep, and lobbying registration demonstrably can't -- Brownstein, Akin Gump, and every K Street shop cleared it decades ago with partner draws, not Series B dollars. The Ro exception doesn't apply; this is a services firm that should be an LLC drawing on Parth's network, not a VC-shaped equity story. **Verifiable flip condition:** three named AI/tech clients (Anthropic-tier, $250K+ retainers) signed inside 12 months with the AI drafting layer demonstrably compressing lobbyist hours per account by 50%+ -- that would prove the hybrid margin thesis and I'd reopen.
