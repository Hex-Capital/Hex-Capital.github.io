# Prototyping.io -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Prototyping.io is the affordability-curious, technically-undifferentiated middle of my ladder — Revanth Bodepudi and Prerit Oberai are pitching "autonomous manufacturing for mechanical parts" into a market where Xometry already books $686.6M of revenue doing approximately the thing they describe. The high dimension here is the *potential* to make CNC prototyping near-free; the low dimension is that the technical wedge is a feature Xometry and MakerVerse are already shipping. That pairing diagnoses Prototyping.io as a margin-compression bet, not a category-creation bet.

On non-linear technology — my lowest-rung dimension here — the high rung is Commonwealth Fusion Systems with HTS magnets that compress fusion plasma in a way nobody else could; the low rung is the YC AI-copilot for a vertical that wraps GPT around an existing workflow. Prototyping.io sits closer to the low rung than I would like. The dossier is explicit: MakerVerse already "combines AI price calculation with manual engineering review," Xometry runs instant quoting, and Prototyping.io's claimed surface — DFM + machine programming + QC at ±0.0001" across 3/4/5-axis milling, turning, and EDM — is asserted on a waitlist homepage with no demos, no part galleries, no proprietary dataset disclosed. A two-person team can ship a CAD-upload-plus-LLM-DFM frontend in a quarter. That is a wrapper, not a wedge, until proven otherwise.

On consequence magnitude — my highest-weighted dimension always — the high rung is OpenAI, where the consequence of success was civilizational and I wrote an apology letter to my LPs. The mid rung is Rocket Lab, where dedicated small-sat launch unlocked thousands of new customers who could not afford a Falcon 9 ride-share. The low rung is the YC vertical AI copilot generating a 3-5x outcome. Prototyping.io sits below Rocket Lab and well above the wrapper floor: if every hardware startup gets parts in 24 hours instead of two weeks, you do unlock more hardware founders, which is genuinely interesting given the YC RFS framing from Nicolas Dessaigne. But the addressable consequence — Online CNC at $1.77B in 2026 growing to $3.56B by 2035 — is a measurable market, not a civilizational one. Faster prototyping is not free healthcare.

Path to radical affordability is where Prototyping.io's pitch lights up against my unstated pattern. Impossible Foods is the high rung — protein eventually cheaper than the cow. Curai is the mid rung — primary-care doctor visit at marginal cost. The low rung is premium enterprise SaaS pricing on top of a service that still costs the same to deliver. Prototyping.io's "no minimum order quantity" language and the automated-CAM premise gesture toward collapsing the per-part cost of small-batch CNC, which is a real affordability story for hardware founders. But the dossier admits the business model is undisclosed and the most likely path is "per-part transactional pricing consistent with peers Xometry/Fictiv" — which is margin capture between quote and shop floor, not a step-change in unit economics. That places Prototyping.io between the low and mid rungs.

Bodepudi (UT Austin MS Aerospace, Schlumberger, IIT Bhubaneswar) and Oberai (UIUC, Microsoft AI systems) are competent — the high rung on rate-of-learning is the 19-year-old MIT dropout running Mach Industries against Lockheed; the low rung is the scripted-resume founder repeating their pitch from six months ago. Prototyping.io's founders sit in the middle: credentialed, not outsiders, and the dossier offers no evidence of a pivot, a surprise, or a learning velocity I can measure. They check the immigrant-founder unstated pattern (Bodepudi from India), which I notice. They do not check the naive-outsider pattern. On improbable ambition, Hermeus aims at Mach 5; Rocket Lab built a real engine; Prototyping.io's stated ambition is CNC parts in days. That is competent, not improbable — the language "autonomous manufacturing" outruns the dossier evidence, and Hadrian has already raised $730M attacking the autonomous-factory ambition with vertical integration. Prototyping.io is the asset-light shadow of that ambition.

My 36-month forecast: Prototyping.io is acquired by Xometry, Fictiv, or Protolabs for under $75M as a feature-and-team tuck-in to accelerate AI quoting, or it shuts down and the founders join Hadrian. The mechanism is the dimensional signature itself — when your highest dimension is affordability potential and your lowest is technical novelty, the incumbents who own the shop network capture your margin before you accumulate proprietary data, and the only exit is selling the AI layer you built to the marketplace that already has the demand. This forecast is consistent with the signature: no wedge, real-but-bounded affordability story, $1.77B addressable.

The strongest case against my forecast is the DoorDash override. DoorDash violated my non-linear technology rule entirely — there was no proprietary tech, just execution against a marketplace problem — and it produced a $72B IPO because Tony Xu was a high-velocity learning machine attacking a planetary consumer market. A bull would say Prototyping.io is the DoorDash of CNC: ride YC's hardware-renaissance tailwind, out-execute Xometry's legacy shop network with AI-native quoting, and become the default for the next 10,000 hardware founders. The reason this case does not win is twofold. First, DoorDash's consequence magnitude was hundreds of millions of consumers eating differently; Prototyping.io's ceiling is the population of mechanical engineers buying CNC parts — a $3.56B 2035 market is not a planetary outcome. Second, Tony Xu showed his learning rate in his first six months at YC; the Prototyping.io dossier offers no analogous trajectory evidence on Bodepudi or Oberai, only resumes. The single condition that would flip my verdict: Prototyping.io publishes a working end-to-end demo where a CAD upload produces a multi-axis CAM program in minutes with measured tolerance ≤±0.001" on a real part, and ships its first 100 paying customers at gross margin above any incumbent — that would be evidence of the technical wedge the current dossier lacks.
