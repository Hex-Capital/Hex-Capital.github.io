# Touchmark -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Touchmark is a real-catalyst, multi-miracle company -- Ilia Bolgov and Roman Yanushevskyi have correctly read that per-seat SaaS pricing fell from 21% to 15% of SaaS in 12 months (Bessemer's 2026 AI pricing playbook) and that the 2026 renewal cycle is forcing outcome pricing, but the *wedge* they've chosen -- LLM-as-judge scoring that auto-refunds and "averages to zero" across customers -- piles three independent miracles on top of a billing primitive that Stripe just paid $1B to own. The signature here is *right window, wrong architecture*, and that's not a profile I underwrite at consensus pre-seed prices.

**Anduril** (2022) is the HIGH-rung comparison on market re-opening because Ukraine catalyzed defense procurement in a clean 24-month window, and Touchmark is genuinely working a real catalyst of the same shape -- the per-seat-to-outcome shift documented in Bessemer's 2026 playbook and the Stripe-Metronome $1B deal in December 2025 are both inside the 24-month gate I require. Where Touchmark diverges from the Anduril template is that Anduril's catalyst opened a *distribution* path (Pentagon software contracts), while Touchmark's catalyst opens a *pricing-model* path that any of the incumbents already sitting in the billing flow -- Stripe Billing, Orb's Vercel/Replit/Pinecone/Perplexity footprint, Lago's AGPLv3 self-host base -- can address by bolting on the same eval layer Touchmark is shipping. The catalyst is real; the question is whether Touchmark is the vehicle for it, and that's where the comparison stops loading.

Pre-2022 ML startups are the LOW-rung pattern on revenue velocity -- "AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) -- and the Touchmark dossier offers no revenue, no customer logos, no waitlist count, no Product Hunt launch, and no press. I weight this proportionally for pre-seed S26 stage and don't penalize the absence of an ARR number, but the failure mode I'm watching for is *no design-partner name at all*, and Touchmark's dossier has zero named AI-vendor counterparties despite a TypeScript SDK being the entire product surface. That's the silence that converts stage-default into signal.

On founder shipping rhythm, the **Perplexity** (2022) anchor is what I'm holding Touchmark against -- Aravind would "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built," and the filter is shipped artifacts, not pedigree. Bolgov's 6y9m at Revolut Product and Yanushevskyi's 2x Citadel QR intern + Lovable AI Engineer intern + ICPC 2025 are *credential descriptions*, not shipping descriptions; the Touchmark dossier names zero GitHub repos for either co-founder, zero prior independent products, zero side projects, no Twitter presence to track build cadence. Quant-intern-plus-fintech-PM is exactly the archetype that scores LLM outputs cleanly in a notebook but has never shipped a billing integration that survives a customer dispute -- and disputes are the entire mechanism Touchmark's product creates.

Touchmark's index position would have to flip a tier for me to underwrite it, and the counterfactual is specific: if the dossier showed Touchmark as the eval-scoring layer that *Orb and Metronome themselves* integrate against -- the way Plaid sits beneath fintech billing rather than competing with it -- this becomes a Decagon-shaped workflow infrastructure bet. As written, Touchmark is a greenfield billing platform that has to displace Metronome (now Stripe-owned), Orb ($44M raised, Vercel/Replit/Pinecone/Perplexity logos), Lago ($34M, open-source), and Solvimon ($9.6M, already pitching hybrid usage+outcome billing) in a category where "switching costs for billing primitives are documented to be high" per the dossier itself. That's #2-in-winner-take-all-adjacent dynamics -- the **Lyft** Series C pass pattern, where I won't pretend a worse version of the obvious bet is a contrarian one.

Single-miracle is where Touchmark fails my hardest test. The Touchmark architecture requires (1) LLM-as-judge evals being accurate and stable enough across customers to bill on -- inter-rater agreement is a known-hard problem and the dossier surfaces no benchmark; (2) AI vendors willingly integrating a system whose own copy says price adjustments "average to zero, your top-line revenue is preserved" -- meaning Touchmark is asking high-margin AI platforms to adopt a pricing layer that explicitly removes their pricing upside; (3) winning channel against Stripe Billing, which now owns Metronome. Each miracle is independent, and I compound them the way I compounded the fusion and novel-battery passes. Compare to **Harvey** (2023), which only had to assume "LLM accuracy at legal task X" and execute GTM -- one miracle, one curve, $3B?$5B?$8B in months.

My 36-month forecast on Touchmark: by mid-2029 the company has either pivoted to a pure AI-evals/observability product competing with Braintrust and HumanLoop and gotten acqui-hired by Orb or Stripe for under $30M, or it's dead. The mechanism is the revenue-neutrality clause -- "average to zero" structurally caps both Touchmark's take-rate and AI-vendor willingness to integrate, so the eval engine becomes the only salvageable asset, and the eval engine without a billing wrapper is a feature, not a company.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that Bolgov's Revolut tenure and Yanushevskyi's Citadel quant background are exactly the rare combination required to make probabilistic billing actually work in production, and Stripe's $1B Metronome acquisition in December 2025 telegraphs that quality-adjusted pricing is the next layer Stripe will buy rather than build -- which would make Touchmark a 24-month acqui-hire at a real multiple rather than a sub-$30M tuck-in. I take the steel-man seriously: probabilistic pricing infrastructure is genuinely hard and quant-trained founders are non-obviously well-suited. The dossier evidence that wins is the revenue-neutrality copy itself -- a billing layer that explicitly does not grow vendor top-line will not be adopted at the rate needed to generate the dataset that would attract Stripe, and without that dataset the acqui-hire price compresses to talent-only. I'd flip my verdict if Touchmark discloses, within 6 months, signed pilots with 2+ named AI-native vendors (Cursor, Lovable, Decagon-tier) where vendor net revenue *increases* post-integration via reduced customer churn -- that single data point would invalidate the revenue-neutrality objection and convert this from pass to lean-in.
