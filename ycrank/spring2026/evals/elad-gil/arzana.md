# Arzana -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Arzana is a clean single-miracle architecture sitting on top of a category with no disclosed revenue curve -- William Alexander and Marshall Kools have isolated the LLM-on-RFQ thesis correctly, but the dossier shows founders paying themselves ~$10K/year and zero published ARR figures, which is exactly the silhouette of a vertical-AI startup where the *thesis* is right and the *just-working* test is unanswered. That high/low pairing is the whole company. Arzana looks like Decagon's structural cousin -- workflow overlay on a recently catalyzed category -- without Decagon's revenue receipts.

On the catalyst dimension, **Anduril** (2022) is the high rung -- a specific 24-month Ukraine-induced Pentagon software re-opening that you could name in one sentence. Arzana's catalyst is the LLM-accuracy step-function on unstructured PDFs and emails, which is real but the dossier flags it [Inferred] and offers no regulatory shift or named incumbent failure to anchor the window; the closest analogue here is the post-Whisper opening that produced **Abridge** (2023), but Arzana never names its equivalent of the Whisper moment. Arzana is riding a *general* AI tailwind into a vertical, not a *specific* manufacturing re-opening -- which is the difference between scoring as **Anduril** and scoring as a generic AI-for-X play.

The revenue-velocity dimension is where Arzana fails the **Harvey** (2023) test outright -- Harvey's "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" curve was the entire reason I paid up (TechCrunch, Nov 2025), and the failure mechanism for AI companies without that curve is the one I named on Tim Ferriss Show #863: "if you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." Arzana's dossier names Zeiss, Tecton, Koike, Kuebler, Givi as logos but discloses no MRR, no ACVs, no MoM growth, no contract-to-pilot ratio, and the founders are on $10K/year salaries per the NYT piece (Feb 8, 2026). That is not categorically broken at pre-seed, but it is conspicuous silence in an AI cycle where real curves are easy to disclose, and "Fortune 500 logo wall" without revenue is the canonical false-signal pattern -- pilot tourism, not buying behavior.

Founder execution rhythm compounds or fails over 18 months through a single mechanism: does the team ship product faster than the competitive set converges? Arzana's dossier names *zero* prior shipped artifacts for either Alexander or Kools, no GitHub footprint, no Twitter, and the NYT explicitly notes neither founder had a tech background prior to Arzana. Against Korso, Smartbase, and Tiny in the same P26 batch chasing overlapping manufacturer back-office workflows, the founder who ships fastest wins the category; Arzana enters that race with Alexander's Iowa manufacturing-sourcing exposure as the only legible shipping signal, and that's domain context, not shipping rhythm. This is the **Perplexity** (2022) anti-pattern -- Aravind showed up a week later with the thing built; Arzana's dossier shows up with a logo wall.

Index position is the counterfactual exercise: for Arzana to score in the **Stripe**-on-e-commerce tier, the overlay on Epicor/JobBoss/NetSuite/SAP/Salesforce/HubSpot would need to be the layer *every* manufacturer touches as ERP-AI adoption compounds, regardless of which ERP wins. The 10+ integrations listed on arzana.com gesture at that position, and the channel-conflict thesis -- that NetSuite and SAP can't ship aggressive front-office automation without cannibalizing services revenue -- is the correct read. To flip Arzana from neutral to invest on this axis, the dossier would need either (a) one ERP integration generating exclusive data flywheel effects or (b) a signed reseller relationship with Epicor or JobBoss that makes Arzana the default agentic layer. Neither is present; the overlay is currently a feature list, not a distribution position.

Single-miracle architecture is where Arzana most closely parallels **Color Genomics** (2015) -- workflow infrastructure on top of a commodity primitive (LLM accuracy on PDFs, the way Color sat on commodity sequencing). Arzana doesn't need new model architectures; it needs current models to hit production-grade accuracy on heterogeneous RFQs and POs, and the remaining work is GTM and ERP integration plumbing. The "99.9% accuracy" claim is unverified and the 10+ ERP integration surface introduces execution complexity, but this is not a multi-miracle dependency chain -- it's one capability bet plus enterprise execution. That's the cleanest dimension in Arzana's dossier and the reason this doesn't score as a Strong Pass.

Forecast: in 36 months Arzana is acquired by an ERP mid-tier -- Epicor, QAD, or Infor -- in the $40-120M range, after reaching roughly $2-6M ARR but losing the standalone-category race to whichever of Korso, Smartbase, or NetSuite's embedded Champion-AI-equivalent gets to $20M ARR first. The mechanism is direct: Arzana's overlay thesis is correct, but in a YC P26 batch with three direct competitors and incumbent ERPs shipping embedded agents in 2026, the company that doesn't have a disclosed velocity curve at pre-seed will not be the one that consolidates the category -- it will be the tuck-in that an incumbent buys to skip building.

The bull case stings: Zeiss is a real Fortune 500 anchor logo, the NYT feature on Feb 8, 2026 is distribution most pre-seeds never get, Alexander's childhood inside an Iowa manufacturing sourcing firm is genuine founder-market fit of the kind I overweight, and the overlay-on-incumbent-ERPs strategy is structurally correct -- exactly the **Decagon** (2023) template applied to a different vertical. If Arzana is at $1M ARR growing 30% MoM right now and simply didn't disclose it, this is an invest. The dismantling: my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy explicitly flags that "Fortune 500 logos without disclosed ACVs" is the canonical false-signal in the 2025-2026 vertical AI cycle, and the NYT framing of two non-technical Stanford grads paying themselves $10K is a *story* signal, not a *just-working* signal. The condition that flips my verdict: Arzana discloses ≥$50K MRR with ≥20% MoM growth across at least three of the named logos (Zeiss, Tecton, Koike) within 90 days -- absent that, this is a pass and I wait for the markup that confirms the curve.
