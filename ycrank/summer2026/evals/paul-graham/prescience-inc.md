# Prescience, Inc. -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Prescience, Inc. is a Stripe-shaped team attacking a problem they have not personally suffered: two Jain brothers, one a Harvard ML/neuro hacker and one a fresh Harvard MD out of the Broad Institute, building a full-stack employer health plan for 100-250 employee companies neither of them has ever run. The signature pairing — top mark on co-founder configuration, bottom mark on organic-to-own-pain — is what I keep circling. The Diamond Plan headline ("$0 employee premium, $0 deductible, $600 PMPM vs. $1,213 market") is the claim of people who studied the buyer, not the claim of someone who got the invoice.

The Collison brothers at **Stripe** (2010) are the right rung for the Prescience, Inc. co-founder configuration: siblings, complementary, working together long before the institutional check, technical depth on one side and operational range on the other. Rishab Jain and Aditya Jain, M.D. fit that shape almost exactly — ML papers and a GitHub at @RJain12 on one side, a Broad Institute AI researcher with an HMS clinical credential on the other, raised together. Where Prescience, Inc. diverges from the **Stripe** (2010) rung is in the second half of the Collison story: by the time I wrote the **Stripe** (2010) check, the Collisons had already shipped **Auctomatic** (2007) through YC and sold it for $5M — they were funded on revealed commercial taste, not pedigree. The Jains' acquired companies (Qurios, ICOR Therapeutics) are real but read as science-fair-era academic spinouts; the Prescience, Inc. dossier does not put a comparable commercial scar on the table.

The MBA-pair AI-wrapper team I keep using as my pass example is the LOW rung on "organic to founder's own pain" — they spotted a market, ran a TAM slide, and built nothing they themselves wanted. Prescience, Inc. is not that team; the Jains have genuine domain output, ICOR's BMC Bioinformatics codon paper and Aditya's Broad Institute AI work. But the failure mechanism on this rung is subtler than non-technicality — it is *distance from the buyer's invoice*. The Prescience, Inc. buyer is a 150-person company HR lead writing a $16,800-per-employee PEO check; neither Jain has been that person, and the dossier offers no anecdote where Rishab Jain or Aditya Jain, M.D. forgot a USB stick the way Drew Houston did at **Dropbox** (2007). The "AlphaGo-like Time Machine" framing is the tell — it is the language of someone modeling the problem, not living inside it.

Animal resourcefulness, /35: the mechanism that compounds over the next 18 months is whether the Prescience, Inc. founders convert credentialed pedigree into a Prescience-specific stunt. Rishab Jain's America's Top Young Scientist win at 13 and the Regeneron $50K are real animal tracks, but they are pre-Prescience tracks. The dossier shows a cal.com/prescience/30min booking link and a Rippling partnership reference — that is sales hygiene, not a cereal-box equivalent. The case studies cited on getprescience.com ("$25.7K cost avoided per predicted intervention," "$875K runway returned in Series B case study") are unnamed; no logo, no carrier partner disclosed, no licensure status. Without a named first employer client at $X PMPM under a specific state regulator by month 12, the animal score does not move up from credentialed-hustle into Armstrong-rebuilt-the-rejected-app territory.

Schlep zone, /15: counterfactually, this score flips a tier the moment Prescience, Inc. discloses a specific named licensed carrier partner and a state-by-state licensure plan. Operating an employer health plan is exactly the **Coinbase** (2012) shape of schlep — the regulatory grind is the moat — and routing it through ICHRA the way Thatch does ($84.5M raised, Index-led) is the obvious flinch. The Prescience, Inc. dossier explicitly chooses the harder path ("Prescience operates the plan itself rather than enabling employee-chosen plans"), which earns the schlep credit, but credits without evidence of engagement are forecasts. No carrier name. No state. Right now Prescience, Inc. is claiming the Coinbase posture without showing the Coinbase compliance hire.

Hacker genealogy, /15, against a single anchor: Aaron Swartz at **Reddit** (2005) co-authored the RSS specification before any company existed — that is the high water mark for pre-startup technical artifact. Rishab Jain's 10+ ML/health papers and @RJain12 GitHub plus Aditya Jain, M.D.'s Broad Institute AI work clear the technical-credential bar, but the dossier does not name a recognizable open-source artifact or HN presence the way Swartz's RSS work or Houston's MIT demo video did at **Dropbox** (2007). Strong scientific lineage; weak hacker-tribal markers.

My 36-month forecast for Prescience, Inc.: by mid-2029 the company has pivoted off operating-the-plan into an AI care-navigation and predictive-routing layer sold into existing carriers, PEOs, or Rippling's benefits surface, sits at under $2M ARR, and either raises a soft Series A at a flat mark or is acquihired by Thatch or Rippling for sub-$30M. The mechanism is the signature: a strong technical pair attacking a buyer pain they did not personally live tends to discover the schlep — state insurance licensure, carrier underwriting, claims operations — is not the part they actually want to do, and retreats up the stack to the predictive layer where their **Broad Institute** hacker genealogy is load-bearing.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the **Coinbase** (2012) precedent — Brian Armstrong was not a payments-industry veteran either, and the regulatory schlep itself became the moat for a team most other YC applicants in the batch were routing around. If the Jain brothers genuinely run at state-by-state licensure and emerge with a transparent-pricing carrier relationship in 18 months, the Prescience, Inc. dossier converts from "claim" to "schlep moat" and the comparison becomes Armstrong, not the MBA AI-wrapper team. What dismantles this bull case in the present dossier is the absence — no carrier name, no licensure jurisdiction, no claims-volume disclosure, no founder anecdote that puts Aditya Jain, M.D. or Rishab Jain personally inside the schlep the way Armstrong's hand-built Android wallet put him inside Coinbase's. One verifiable condition would flip me to invest: Prescience, Inc. names a specific licensed-carrier partner and a first paying 100-250 employee customer by their YC Demo Day, with lives covered disclosed. Until then, neutral.
