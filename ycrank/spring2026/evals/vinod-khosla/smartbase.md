# Smartbase -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Smartbase is a learning-rate-rich, capital-light pivot wearing a labor-displacement costume -- Sam Goldman and Taira Fujioka moved from Anytrace (YC S25, AI support engineer) into Smartbase (YC P26, PO entry for metal finishing shops) inside a single batch cycle, which tells me the founders update. It also tells me they updated *into* a crowded document-AI category where Leverage AI ($14.9M raised) and Turian ($3.78M, Berlin) are already running the same agentic-PO play. The dimensional signature here -- highest on pivot evidence, lowest on required-capital profile -- is the exact shape of a deal where I am over-resourced and any Tier-1 generalist writes the check.

On consequence magnitude, the load-bearing analogy is my pass example, not **OpenAI** (2019). Smartbase claims a "$26 billion clerical labor TAM" (YC page, company-asserted, no third-party source) and its ROI calculator promises "$81K savings / 1.4 headcount per shop" -- those are the exact units my Outlook Business framing rejects. The success-state of Smartbase at maximum ambition is: metal-finishing shops re-key fewer POs and Epicor onboarding services lose a sliver of revenue. No incumbent regime collapses. Contrast **Impossible Foods** (2011), where the success-state was animal-protein production itself disappearing as a category. Smartbase's "$15T labor" gesture is rhetorical drafting on my Fortune 2026-03-06 framing; the company actually attacks 1.4 FTEs per metal-finishing shop, which is a feature Acumatica ships in eighteen months.

The **Range Fuels** (~2007) failure mode was capital adequacy misjudged against the science -- they ran out of runway before the plant integration miracle resolved. Smartbase has the inverse failure mode and it is more banal: there is no miracle to underfund. The dossier states plainly that "LLM-based PO parsing is replicable by any team with API access" (tryleverage.ai, 2026). Taira Fujioka's AWS SageMaker Inference background is real engineering credibility, but it maps to model-serving, not to a defended technical secret. The ITAR/CMMC/DFARS-compliant U.S. enclave (smartbase.so) is a deployment artifact, not a science miracle -- AWS GovCloud commoditizes that posture for any competitor willing to pay the compliance tax. This is the SaaS execution-risk profile I told TechCrunch (2013-09-11) the consensus VCs are perfectly equipped to underwrite.

Founder-defiance-of-experts compounds in one direction over 18 months: either Sam Goldman publicly contradicts an orthodoxy in ERP integration or manufacturing operations, or he becomes a more polished version of the "8 years at incumbent" pattern from my TED Talk. The dossier supplies neither -- no public thesis, no controversial position, no instigator behavior. "Slept at a factory" (YC page) is founder-market diligence, not the kind of public-contradiction signal I look for. Compare the Levine/Finn pattern at **Physical Intelligence** (2024) where the founders had already published positions that the field debated. Smartbase founders have a LinkedIn, an Anytrace pivot, and a website -- no public field-level argument is visible, which under my UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction skepticism reads as conformity by default.

For founder learning rate to flip a tier on Smartbase, the Anytrace-to-Smartbase pivot would need to read like **Slash** (2024) -- a documented public thesis update with a sharp narrative that justifies overriding consequence-magnitude priors. What we have instead is a quiet within-YC pivot from one B2B AI agent to another B2B AI agent. That is learning, but it is intra-genre learning, not the Kanye-pivot magnitude that earned Slash my $100M Series C. I credit Sam Goldman and Taira Fujioka for moving fast off a non-working idea; I do not credit the pivot as evidence they will keep updating into a civilizationally large success-state.

**Genesis AI** (2025) absorbed $105M co-led because the roadmap -- robot foundation models, simulation infrastructure, training compute -- could not be executed on $5M. Smartbase's roadmap is nine ERP connectors (already shipped), a document-extraction pipeline (already shipped), and direct sales into metal-finishing shops on "five-figure annual contracts." This roadmap reaches $1-2M ARR on a standard YC seed plus an A. I would be deploying $30-50M into a company whose next eighteen months of value creation require $3M. That is the textbook over-resourced mismatch.

My 36-month forecast for Smartbase: $2-5M ARR on the metal-finishing wedge, then an acquisition by an ERP incumbent (Acumatica or Epicor) or a bolt-on into Leverage AI in the low-nine-figures or less -- a perfectly respectable YC outcome and an outcome that does not move any portfolio of mine. The mechanism is straightforward: the founders are competent and the wedge is real, but the category compresses as Leverage AI ($14.9M) and Turian ($3.78M) and incumbent ERPs converge on the same agentic-PO feature set, leaving Smartbase's defensibility resting on ITAR-compliant deployment and shop-specific labeled data -- both consolidatable into a larger platform.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash** (2024) Known Exception: YC-pedigreed pivot company with a documented learning-rate moment, where I overrode my consequence-magnitude prior and wrote the $100M Series C. Smartbase has the YC pedigree (P26, Harj Taggar) and a real pivot (Anytrace S25 → Smartbase P26). Why is this not Slash? Because Slash's pivot story was public, narrative, and into a consumer-banking surface with viral mechanics; Smartbase's pivot is private, intra-B2B-AI-agent, and into a wedge that the dossier itself flags as crowded by five named competitors. The Slash override required learning rate *plus* an outcome distribution with viral upside -- Smartbase has the first input and not the second. **I would flip to invest if Smartbase signed two named defense-prime metal-finishing supply-chain customers (a Lockheed or RTX tier-three shop) under ITAR enclave in the next nine months at six-figure ACVs -- that would convert the regulatory-deployment posture from artifact to moat and would name an incumbent regime worth collapsing.**

Pass. Make a great company and the money takes care of itself; this is not yet a great company by my framework, it is a competent one.
