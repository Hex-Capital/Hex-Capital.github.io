# Clawvisor -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Eric Levine sold Berbix to Socure for $70M in June 2023, and now he's back solo at YC Spring 2026 building an "authorization gateway" in Go for AI agents -- the signature here is high founder learning rate paired with the lowest possible capital profile, which is the exact analytical shape of a deal I am structurally over-resourced for. Clawvisor wants to be Okta for agents. That is not nothing -- but it is a tollbooth on the AI labor revolution, not the revolution itself. My $50M into **OpenAI** (2019) was a bet that cognition itself gets redirected; Clawvisor is a bet that one of the seventeen middleware layers built on top of that redirection accrues rent. Different deal, different fund.

On Required-Capital Profile, Clawvisor sits at the floor of the ladder, and this is the dimension that disqualifies it from my checkbook before any other. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) needed $115M in a Series A because the physics required it and consensus VCs would not touch the capital-risk profile -- that is the deployment shape I exist to underwrite. Clawvisor is a solo founder, an OSS repo with 202 GitHub stars, 14 adapters in Go, and a free-trial-no-credit-card landing page. The roadmap to $1M ARR here is $3-5M of seed capital that any Tier-1 generalist -- Sequoia, a16z infra, Index -- funds in their sleep. When my $115M check would crowd out three downstream rounds in a company that does not need it, I am the wrong investor by construction, not by judgment.

The **Cognition** (2024) $175M Series B was a stack-deep AI bet: proprietary model post-training, agent loops, eval infrastructure -- a software company that nonetheless required real technical surface area. Clawvisor on Technical Risk is the inverse: a gateway that vaults OAuth tokens, scores anomalous parameters with rules, and routes approvals to Telegram. There is no named technical miracle in the Clawvisor dossier -- no novel ML for purpose-verification, no formal-methods policy engine, no proprietary anomaly model with published benchmarks. Arcade.dev raised $12M seed in March 2025 and Composio raised $29M with $1M+ ARR and 200+ paying customers by July 2025; the category is being executed by larger teams on the same primitives. Clawvisor's "purpose-based verification" is advertised on the website with zero third-party audits or deployment data. This is software-only execution risk in a contested category -- the exact pattern that fails my "repels consensus VCs" test.

Consequence Magnitude compounds backwards over 18 months for Clawvisor specifically because the success-state collapses no incumbent regime. If Clawvisor wins maximally, it becomes a line item in an enterprise AI security budget -- not the disappearance of human radiologists I underwrote at $187M Series C in 2025, not the displacement of animal protein production I funded at **Impossible Foods** (2011), not the rewriting of cognitive labor I priced into **OpenAI** (2019). The dossier's own framing -- "authorization layer for AI agents" -- is infrastructure-tax language, not category-collapse language. McKinsey told AT&T in 1986 there would be fewer than 1 million U.S. cellphones by 2000; the actual number was 109 million. The interesting bet in that timeline was not "build the billing system for cellular." The interesting bet was the cellular network. Clawvisor is the billing system.

On Founder Defies Documented Expert Consensus, Clawvisor would have to flip to a higher tier for me to override the consequence-magnitude prior, and the dossier facts do not support that flip. Levine's resume is paradigmatic domain-expert continuity: BS CS from UCSD, Google 2010-2012, Airbnb Trust & Safety Engineering Manager 2012-2017, Berbix identity verification 2018-2023, YC Visiting Group Partner, now agent authorization. This is the **Glean** (Arvind Jain ex-Google search) profile -- and Glean is in my portfolio as a known exception that required capital scale and Fortune-500 consequence to override the consensus-credential anti-pattern. Clawvisor is the Glean shape without the Glean scale. The TED Talk warning applies: the founder is extrapolating his own past expertise into the adjacent agent category, which is competent, but it is not the instigator pattern.

Founder Learning Rate is where Clawvisor scores highest, and **Slash** (2024) is the single anchor: a YC-pedigreed founder who had publicly demonstrated a thesis update earned my $100M Series C despite the consequence-magnitude prior failing. Levine has a real prior exit -- Berbix to Socure for $70M in 2023 -- which is harder evidence than most pre-seed founders bring. But Slash's override was a documented pivot inside the company, not a sequential second startup; Levine is doing the linear next-thing in identity, not a publicly contradicted thesis update. The learning-rate score is genuinely positive, just not override-tier positive against the consequence-magnitude floor.

My 36-month forecast: Clawvisor is acquired by Okta, Auth0, or Cloudflare for $30-80M as an "agent identity" feature tuck-in by Q2 2029, or it settles at $1-3M ARR while Composio (already $1M+ ARR, 200+ paying customers, 100K developers in July 2025) and Arcade.dev extend purpose-based enforcement into the same surface. The mechanism is straightforward: adapter-library moats commoditize when the underlying SaaS providers (Google, Slack, Atlassian) ship native agent-permission APIs, which is a 2027-2028 event on current public roadmaps. The exit math works for Levine personally and returns 3-5x to seed investors; it does not return a Khosla Ventures fund.

The bull case stings if you take Slash seriously: Levine has the precise Trust & Safety scar tissue from Airbnb plus a $70M identity exit, the agent-auth category is genuinely real, and being early with 202 stars and 14 adapters could compound into the default enforcement layer the way Stripe compounded into payments-default. If agents do 80% of economically valuable work by 2030 -- the Fortune 2025-07-01 frame I publicly committed to -- then the rent on the authorization layer is a $10B revenue line, and Clawvisor at $500K seed valuation is the asymmetric trade. The dismantle: Slash earned its override on a documented pivot that demonstrated learning-rate above consequence-magnitude; Clawvisor's dossier shows linear domain continuity, no pivot, and a competitor (Composio) already past $1M ARR with 25+ engineers. The condition that would flip my verdict: a second founder joining from a frontier lab's agent-safety team -- Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind -- shipping a proprietary ML-based purpose-verification model with a published benchmark advantage over rule-based scoring. Absent that, Clawvisor is a competent founder building a fundable company that is not my company.
