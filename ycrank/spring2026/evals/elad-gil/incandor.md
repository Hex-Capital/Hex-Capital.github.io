# Incandor -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Incandor is a clean single-miracle architecture wrapped around zero revenue velocity in a category where Mastercard already owns one of the rails — Matthew Yekell and Luc Rosenzweig are pitching label-free behavioral biometrics as an API into a fraud-tooling market whose incumbents (BioCatch's $1.3B Permira exit, Feedzai's $2B October 2025 round, NuData inside Mastercard) have $239M-$347M war chests and 60+ patents. That's the signature: technically tidy, commercially unproven, attacking a slot the consolidators already occupy.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on the catalyst dimension because the thesis there was a *specific* 24-month window — Ukraine had named, dated, congressionally-documented Pentagon software inadequacy. Incandor's "why now" gestures at GenAI bot traffic, deepfake social engineering, and APP/mule fraud, which is directionally real, but the dossier marks it `[Inferred]` and cites a single Biometric Update piece from August 2025 rather than a regulatory clause or named bank failure. Anduril had the FY23 NDAA; Incandor has a market-research CAGR. The catalyst is plausibly there — bank fraud teams *are* burned by unsupervised attack surfaces post-ChatGPT — but the dossier doesn't pin a step-function, and "market first" means I need the step-function named, not inferred from secondary trade press.

**Lyft Series C** is the failure pattern I track on index position, and Incandor sits closer to it than I'd like. The Lyft mistake-shape was paying for a #2 in a market with declared leaders; Incandor's competitor table reads identically — BioCatch (acquired), Feedzai ($2B), Sift ($1B, 700+ brands), NuData (Mastercard subsidiary, *distributed via card rails*), BehavioSec (LexisNexis-owned). Behavioral biometrics is not a wide-open *AI Market Clarity* category — it's a consolidated one where the index position has already been claimed by the company that ships inside Mastercard's authorization flow. Incandor's API-first, label-free wedge is real architecturally but it isn't a distribution wedge, and fraud is sold to ~300 buyers globally who all already have an incumbent contract.

On revenue velocity — mechanism analysis. Incandor's site shows "Request early access" with no pricing, no customer logo, no published case study, and the YC page lists "Not hiring" with zero team-size signal. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative because *real* growth is easy to disclose — that's the Tim Ferriss Show #863 (April 2026) frame. Over the next 18 months, a label-free fraud API either compounds via published bank benchmarks (the only credible enterprise procurement currency under SR 11-7 model risk management) or it stalls in pilot tourism. The dossier shows neither pilots nor benchmarks, and bank procurement cycles mean the 12-month forward curve is already mostly written.

On founder shipping rhythm — counterfactual. For Incandor's score to flip a tier here, I'd need Rosenzweig's GitHub to show a published behavioral-telemetry SDK or Yekell to have shipped a fraud-adjacent product at Cboe with named throughput. Instead the dossier surfaces one Rosenzweig repo, no Twitter, no GitHub for Yekell, and credentials reading "Stanford CS + Vista Equity + Cboe / Stanford CS + NVIDIA + Apple + Epic Games." That's the pedigree-without-artifacts profile my framework explicitly disqualifies — the inverse of Aravind Srinivas pinging me on LinkedIn with a built thing a week later. Vista + Cboe is regulated-buyer GTM credibility, which I weight, but it isn't a shipping signal.

On single-miracle architecture — single-anchor parallel to **Decagon** (2023). Decagon's miracle was that post-Whisper / post-GPT-4 LLM accuracy was high enough to handle Tier-1 CS tickets — one validated capability, rest is GTM. Incandor's miracle is structurally similar in *shape*: assume unsupervised behavioral fingerprinting hits production-grade accuracy from session one, rest is API integration and bank sales. The architectural cleanliness is what saves the score here — Incandor isn't asking me to underwrite three independent breakthroughs. The difference from Decagon is that LLM accuracy was already empirically validated outside the company when I wrote that check; Incandor's >99% ATO claim is self-reported on incandor.com with no benchmark, no third-party validation, and no dataset disclosure. One miracle, unverified.

My 36-month forecast: Incandor gets acqui-hired by Feedzai, Sift, or LexisNexis (BehavioSec's owner) in the $8-25M range as a label-free research feature inside a supervised stack, or runs out of runway pre-Series A. ARR stays under $2M. The mechanism is mundane — consolidated category, no distribution wedge, regulated-buyer cycles longer than YC seed runways, an incumbent set whose research teams can replicate unsupervised-baseline approaches inside two quarters once they see Incandor's first published benchmark.

The strongest bull case maps Incandor to the **Decagon** template: a real catalyst (post-GenAI fraud floods banks can't label fast enough), a single-miracle architecture (label-free unsupervised modeling), workflow-infrastructure positioning (API into every fintech), and a founder mix (Cboe + NVIDIA/Apple) that's actually well-suited to regulated-buyer GTM and low-level input telemetry. If banks are genuinely past supervised-label fraud models in a 12-month window, Incandor is sitting in the right corridor. The bear case wins because Decagon had visible enterprise pull from month one and Incandor's dossier shows a "Request early access" form, zero LOIs, and a competitor with Mastercard's distribution already pre-installed at the point of card authorization. **One verifiable condition would flip my verdict**: three named bank or top-20 fintech pilots disclosed within six months with a publishable accuracy benchmark against BioCatch on a shared dataset. Absent that, this is a Harvey-clone-shaped pass — except the category leader here isn't even Harvey, it's a Mastercard subsidiary.
