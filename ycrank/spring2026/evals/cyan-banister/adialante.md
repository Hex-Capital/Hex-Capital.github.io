# Adialante -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Adialante is a frontier-hardware bet wearing healthcare clothes -- highest on the capital-intensive-unsexy-regulated axis, lowest on physical-world verifiability because the first FREE gradientless MRI system was only scheduled operational *by demo day*, which means I am being asked to write a check on a trailer that has not yet rolled into a urology parking lot. Efra?n Torres and Parker Jenkins came out of UMN's Center for Magnetic Resonance Research with two issued U.S. patents and a $1.18M NSF SBIR Phase II grant, and that is the kind of dossier where the moat compounds in a domain other investors refuse to enter -- exactly the lane that produced the SpaceX check in 2008.

SpaceX in 2008 is the load-bearing rung on capital-intensity, and the mechanism is identical: a PhD-grade team rebuilding a category that everyone else considers uninvestable because the hardware costs are six- to seven-figures-per-unit and the regulatory clock runs in years, not quarters. Adialante is redesigning the magnet itself -- "frequency-modulated Rabi-encoded echo," gradientless, silent, no shielded room -- which is the *physics* of MRI, not a software wrapper on a Siemens scanner. Where Adialante diverges from the SpaceX rung is the business architecture: per-scan trailer-as-a-service rather than capital sale, which means the unit economics of owning, parking, driving, and servicing physical trailers across geographies become the company even if the magnet works. That is a different shape of capital intensity than rockets -- distributed instead of concentrated -- and it is where Crusoe's roughneck-logistics analog matters more than the rocket one.

The lowest rung on physical-world verifiability is the dev-tools abstraction I cannot touch, and the failure mechanism is that I am forced to take founder testimony as the only signal. Adialante is not there -- a mobile MRI trailer is the most tactile artifact in this YC batch, you can in principle walk up to it -- but the dossier confesses the first system is *scheduled* to be operational by demo day, $30k in deposits against $12.75M of non-binding LOIs, and zero scanned patients in the public record. Flock Safety I could see on a Wi-Fi scan at the Four Seasons because it was already deployed; Adialante I am being asked to imagine deployed. The pattern is not the dev-tools failure pattern, it is the pre-shipment hardware pattern, and it gets a middling score not a punitive one.

Founder Surfaced From Lived Experience -- counterfactual mode: for Adialante to flip a tier on this axis, Torres would need a biographical contact with prostate cancer screening itself, not just with the MRI physics. The dossier gives me the *physics* version of lived experience (UMN CMRR, two patents, co-inventor relationship between Torres and Jenkins) but not the *patient* version (no family-member story, no rural-clinic shadowing detail, no urologist-in-the-family note). That is the DeepMind/Hassabis pattern -- polymath domain expertise overriding the lack of autobiographical problem-contact -- and the polymath exception is the one I have invoked before.

Magically Weird -- mechanism analysis: an MRI trailer that pulls into a urology clinic and charges per scan is genuinely transgressive aesthetically because Siemens, GE, and Philips have spent forty years training the medical-imaging market that MRI lives in a shielded room behind a glass wall, and Adialante is saying it lives in a parking lot. The 18-month compounding question is whether Adialante's per-scan data corpus from those trailers becomes a proprietary AI quality flywheel that Hyperfine's brain-focused 64mT Swoop cannot replicate, or whether Promaxo's $99.1M war chest funds a mobile variant fast enough to commoditize the format. The round is not consensus -- I see Brown Venture Group, Fogarty Innovation, Walleye Tank, NSF, and YC on the cap table, which is decidedly not the six-tier-1-funds-chasing pattern that inverts my interest.

Single Unambiguous CEO -- single-anchor parallel to Anduril: Palmer Luckey was the named CEO with clear tiebreaker authority on Day 1, and the structural inverse was HQ Trivia's Yusupov/Kroll collapse. Adialante has Torres as CEO, Jenkins as COO, with a documented graduate-school working relationship at UMN before the 2022/2023 founding. The titles are delineated, the technical and operational roles are separated, and Kyle King sits as CFO -- the governance dossier reads "they have had the conversation."

The 36-month forecast: Adialante secures FDA 510(k) clearance for the FREE gradientless prostate MRI system within 24 months, converts roughly 30-40% of the $12.75M LOI pipeline into binding per-scan contracts, operates 8-15 trailers across the Sun Belt urology markets, and raises a Series A from a healthcare-deep-tech fund at $80-150M post -- with an outside acquisition shot from Hyperfine or a Siemens/GE imaging division by month 36 for $150-300M. The mechanism is that the trailer-and-per-scan format is genuinely "magically weird" to incumbents but obvious to urology clinic operators once the first deployment ships, and the FDA pathway risk is the gate, not the ceiling.

The bear case stings. Adialante has $30,000 in actual deposits against $12.75M of non-binding LOIs, which is the LOI-to-revenue conversion risk in its purest form -- the kind of pipeline that evaporates when CMS reimbursement codes for non-hospital pre-biopsy prostate MRI turn out to need bespoke negotiation. No FDA clearance is referenced in the public sources, mobile-trailer unit economics across drivers/technicians/maintenance are pure capex pain, and Hyperfine -- already publicly listed on NASDAQ:HYPR with $90M Series D dry powder -- could decide tomorrow that prostate MRI is the obvious extension of its 64mT portable platform and arrive with FDA precedent already in hand. The dossier evidence that wins is the patent-protected physics plus the NSF SBIR Phase II validation: Adialante's gradientless architecture is not a software repackaging, it is a different magnet, and PhD-level MRI physics talent does not commoditize in a quarter the way an AI agent wrapper does. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict to a pass is simple: if Adialante's first trailer is *not* operational with at least one diagnostic-grade prostate scan completed by the end of YC Demo Day as the dossier promises, the founder credibility on shipping collapses and I do not write the check.
