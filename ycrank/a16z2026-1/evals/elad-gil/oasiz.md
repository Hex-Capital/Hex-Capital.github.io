# Oasiz -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Oasiz is building in one of the most crowded corridors of the current AI wave. "AI-generated games" isn't a nonobvious market -- it's practically a consensus category. Rosebud AI raised $15M from a16z and Khosla, CodeWisp and Playabl.ai are both YC-backed, and Roblox with its 380M MAU and $4.8B in projected 2025 revenue is already layering AI-assisted creation into its existing engine. When I invested in Coinbase in 2012, crypto infrastructure was dismissed by nearly every mainstream investor. When I backed Perplexity in 2022, AI-powered search was considered a failed concept. The structural shift behind Oasiz -- LLMs capable of generating functional game code from prompts -- is real, but it's the opposite of overlooked. Every major gaming investor and multiple well-funded startups have already identified this exact window. I can't find the nonobvious angle. The dossier cites 78% of game developers ranking audience reach as their top priority and projects UGC gaming at $24.8B by 2032, but these are widely circulated market reports, not hidden signals. The timestamp check here says: you're arriving at the party alongside everyone else.

The infrastructure-layer test is where Oasiz falls hardest for my framework. Oasiz is a consumer mobile application -- a TikTok-style vertical feed for AI-generated mini-games. There's no API, no developer platform, no B2B revenue model. The AI game generation layer relies on generally available LLMs and generative models that Oasiz doesn't control. Compare this to Stripe, which became the payments API that thousands of companies built their businesses on, creating compounding switching costs with every integration. Oasiz's "platform" depends entirely on consumer network effects -- content library and player base -- which are real but fragile at 230K plays and 21 App Store ratings. The dossier itself flags this: "The defensible bundle is creation + social distribution + community in a single mobile app, not any single component." That's the application-layer bet on a stable platform that I routinely pass on. Oasiz is building on top of someone else's infrastructure rather than becoming the infrastructure.

The single-miracle test fails explicitly here. Oasiz needs at least three independent things to go right simultaneously: the AI generation layer must produce games compelling enough to retain users against polished AAA mobile titles (a technology quality miracle that depends on third-party model improvements Oasiz doesn't control); the TikTok-driven distribution must convert into a self-sustaining social network with retention mechanics strong enough to compete with Roblox's entrenched community (a distribution miracle); and the newly introduced Jemz virtual currency must convert free users into paying ones at rates that sustain the business (a monetization miracle). The 6.5M organic TikTok views are a real signal, but TikTok views are not the same as retained users -- Wish grew explosively on social media distribution too, and that's exactly the comparison that concerns me. Consumer gaming monetization is notoriously difficult, and 7,000 user hours across 230,000 plays implies roughly 1.8 minutes per session -- casual enough that monetization conversion will be an uphill battle.

The strongest bull case for Oasiz centers on Abel Dagne. A founder who previously built a social app to #1 on the App Store with 500K+ downloads has demonstrated something rare: the ability to crack consumer mobile distribution. That's not a credential I dismiss. Jonathan Dinh's Berkeley AI research background and top-0.01% mobile gaming ranking add relevant technical and domain depth. The team draws from Google, Tesla, Nintendo, Amazon, and Superhuman -- impressive breadth for a four-person pre-seed company. If Oasiz becomes the default social layer for AI-generated interactive content the way TikTok became the default for short video, the network effects could compound quickly and the TAM is genuinely large. The Atari IP interest, even unconfirmed, suggests brand-level recognition of the category potential. For this to work, Oasiz needs AI-generated game quality to improve faster than incumbents can adapt, and the social feed mechanic needs to create a retention loop strong enough that users come back without TikTok pushing them there. Both are possible. Neither is probable without some structural advantage Oasiz doesn't currently demonstrate ownership of.

My pass comes down to two dimensions. Nonobvious Market Timing scores below my default because I have positive evidence of market obviousness -- three funded competitors in the exact same category, a public company (Roblox) actively moving toward AI-assisted creation, and mainstream analyst coverage of the AI gaming opportunity. Infrastructure-Layer Positioning scores near the bottom of the range because Oasiz is a pure consumer application building on generally available AI models, with no pathway to becoming infrastructure that other businesses depend on. Dagne's consumer distribution track record is the one signal that pulls meaningfully above baseline, but a strong founder in an obvious, crowded market building at the application layer is exactly the pattern where I've seen great operators get crushed by market structure. I'd rather invest in whatever boring, unglamorous infrastructure layer every AI game platform -- including Oasiz -- needs to function.
