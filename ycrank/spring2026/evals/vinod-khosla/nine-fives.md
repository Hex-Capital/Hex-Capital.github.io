# Nine Fives -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Nine Fives is a competent hardware engineering team building a 30-percent-better RF test instrument -- the dimensional signature is real founder substance on the Falcon 9 Radar Altimeter and Starship S-Band radios paired with a success-state in which no incumbent regime collapses, and that pairing tells me exactly what this is: an optimization play dressed in SpaceX credentials. Noah Levy spent 2016-2023 designing flight-qualified radios; the consequence of Nine Fives winning is that hardware engineers stop fighting the January 1997 USB-HID driver stack. That is a workflow improvement, not a civilizational variable.

The highest rung on my consequence ladder is **OpenAI** (2019) -- $50M into a no-revenue company because the success-state redrew what intelligence costs. The shared mechanism I look for is a named incumbent regime that disappears when the company wins. Nine Fives names Keysight, Rohde & Schwarz, and NI/Emerson as targets, but the dossier's own framing is "drop-in replacement positioning" with compatibility to NI TestStand and Keysight Connection Expert -- the opposite of collapse. The TAM is $3.99B growing to $8.48B by 2034 at 8.74% CAGR (Precedence Research); the **Impossible Foods** (2011) comparison would be "displaces animal protein production at $1.5T scale," and Nine Fives's analogue would have to be "the entire SCPI-driver-based test paradigm ends." The dossier instead describes a faster way to plug in an attenuator. That is the sales-call-summarizer pattern from my Pass Example wearing rack-mountable clothes.

The low rung on my technical-risk ladder is the GPT-wrapper sales tool I pass on in 30 seconds because any Tier-1 generalist can fund it -- the failure mechanism is that the moat lives entirely in UX polish on a commodity substrate. Does that pattern appear in Nine Fives? DS Instruments already ships a 6 GHz 90 dB programmable USB/Ethernet attenuator at $649-$749. Mini-Circuits ships >400 attenuator SKUs to 67 GHz. The Nine Fives hardware substrate is, by the dossier's own competitor table, replicable. The differentiation rests on NineVue and a "skill.md" LLM layer for converting rack diagrams into test code -- a software UX wrapper on commodity RF hardware. The **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) $115M Series A required catalyst-yield, plasma-confinement, and HTS-magnet miracles each of which was falsifiable; Nine Fives requires zero physics miracles. No consensus VC declines this deal on technical risk.

Noah Levy and Andrew Kurtz are the exact founder shape my TED Talk flagged -- "spent eight years at Salesforce, now building Salesforce tools," translated to SpaceX RF. The **Physical Intelligence** (2024) pattern I back is a research-PI defying their field's orthodoxy on what is achievable; Levy and Kurtz are extending their incumbent expertise into adjacent tooling, which is domain-expert continuity. They have not publicly contradicted RF test orthodoxy -- they have built a nicer version of it. **Glean** is the Known Exception where I tolerated ex-Google domain-expert continuity, but Glean's override was consequence magnitude on Fortune-500 information asymmetry plus capital scale; Nine Fives has neither.

On learning rate: over 18 months, what compounds for a founder is the documented pivot -- the **Slash** (2024) Kanye moment that justified my $100M Series C. The Nine Fives dossier shows one linear plan since 2025 incorporation, no public thesis update, no prior exit, no "we were wrong about X" moment. Founded 2025, YC P26, 4 SKUs listed, ~7-day-old YC Launch at research date. That is not rigidity evidence -- the company is too young -- but it is also not Slash-style override evidence. The dimension defaults.

On capital fit, what would have to be true to flip this to a Khosla-shaped check is a roadmap requiring >$50M to first commercial milestone -- foundation-model training, a fab, a fleet, a plant. Nine Fives ships sub-$1,500 ASP rack instruments with a 2-person team via Shopify-style e-commerce; the implied burn-to-cashflow is single-digit millions. I am structurally over-resourced for Nine Fives the way I would be for the sales-call summarizer. **Genesis AI** at $105M co-led seed needed that scale to train robot foundation models; Nine Fives does not.

My 36-month forecast: Nine Fives reaches $3-8M ARR selling driverless RF instruments into a few hundred hardware shops, then gets acquired by Keysight, Tektronix, or NI/Emerson for $40-100M as a software-UX talent tuck-in -- or it stays a profitable, capital-efficient niche hardware business that never crosses $20M ARR and never becomes a venture-scale outcome. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: real engineering substance plus a workflow-improvement success-state produces real products and real customers but bounded consequence, which produces an industry acqui-hire, not an IPO.

The strongest bull case is the **DoorDash** (2013) Known Exception -- capital-light, consensus-fundable, no science miracle, returned $72B IPO. Maybe Nine Fives is DoorDash for hardware test workflows: founder ambition plus a "$15T labor displacement" thread if AI-generated test code via skill.md actually rewrites how electrical engineers spend their day. The argument stings because hardware engineer time is exactly the labor-cost displacement frame from my Fortune 2025-07-01 thesis. The dossier evidence that wins for me: DoorDash's consequence was reshaping the consumer-commerce flow at population scale; Nine Fives's consequence is reshaping a rack-configuration workflow at hardware-engineer scale -- a population of tens of thousands, not hundreds of millions. The McKinsey 1986 cellphone forecast missed because the success-state was a phone in every pocket; the Nine Fives success-state is a REST endpoint in every test rack. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Nine Fives signs design wins worth >$5M in committed POs from two of {Apple, SpaceX, Anduril, Waymo} within 12 months, demonstrating that the skill.md/LLM layer is actually displacing test-engineer headcount rather than improving instrument UX. Absent that, I pass.
