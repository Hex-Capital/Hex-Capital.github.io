# Prototyping.io -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Prototyping.io is a clean schlep-zone problem walked into by two competent engineers who have not yet shown me they are animals. Revanth Bodepudi did aerospace materials research at UT Austin and turned wrenches on oilfield hardware at Schlumberger; Prerit Oberai built embedded systems and NASA satellite mission-ops tooling. That is a credible pair for "AI ingests CAD and spits out machine programs across CNC, sheet metal, injection molding, extrusion, and die casting." But the dossier on Prototyping.io shows me a gated waitlist, no demo, no first paying engineer, no cereal-box equivalent. The high/low pairing -- strong schlep, missing animal -- is the entire diagnosis.

On the schlep axis the load-bearing analogy is **Coinbase** (2012): Brian Armstrong won me over because he was sprinting toward bitcoin compliance work that every other YC applicant in his batch was unconsciously routing around -- "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) in its purest form. Prototyping.io is sitting on the same kind of unloved terrain. Closing the loop from CAD to runnable G-code across six manufacturing processes with ±0.0002" tolerances is the opposite of a weekend AI wrapper; it is years of unpleasant edge-case work on fixturing, tool wear, sheet-metal bend allowances, and gate placement in injection molds. Where Prototyping.io diverges from the Coinbase rung is the artifact: Armstrong rescued his rejected application by hand-building an Android wallet; Prototyping.io shows me a waitlist page and a tolerance claim with no third-party validation. The schlep is real. The schlep-being-walked-into is unproven.

The pass-example pattern that haunts Prototyping.io is the two-MBA AI-wrapper team with a glossy deck and no shipped artifact -- the failure mechanism there is that the dossier reads as "we have analyzed the market" instead of "here is the thing we built last weekend." Prototyping.io is not that team -- Bodepudi and Oberai have real engineering hands -- but the artifact column is conspicuously empty in the same way. No GitHub stars retrievable for @PreritO, no demo video, no "we machined our first parts for Engineer X at Company Y last week," no unscalable stunt where the two of them personally CAM-programmed parts overnight for a hardware founder on Hacker News. Drew Houston's pre-seed **Dropbox** (2007) HN demo flipped me on a crowded category; Prototyping.io's equivalent move would be a public ingest-this-STEP-file-and-watch-the-toolpath-appear demo, and it doesn't exist yet.

Idea organicity for Prototyping.io is a mechanism question: does the auto-DFM feedback loop compound if Bodepudi specifically felt the pain at Schlumberger waiting on machined oilfield parts? Plausibly yes -- ex-Schlumberger Houston engineers spend real life-hours arguing with machinists about tolerances on downhole tooling -- and that is the **Dropbox** (2007) USB-stick pattern in latent form. But the dossier never says Bodepudi built Prototyping.io because he was personally tired of waiting two weeks for a CNC quote on a fixture. Without that sentence, I default down. Eighteen months from now this either compounds into "the founders kept dogfooding on their own mechanical projects and the DFM engine got eerily good" or it stays a generic AI-quoting layer indistinguishable from Factorem's "AI-native full-stack" marketing.

Hacker genealogy on Prototyping.io is a counterfactual: what would have to be true for this to clear the bar Patrick Collison cleared before **Stripe** (2010) with his Young Scientist Lisp prize? Oberai's CU Boulder Presidents Leadership Class, embedded work at POSSIBLE Mobile, and NASA satellite tooling get him to "real engineer." A public @PreritO repo with non-trivial stars, or a Bodepudi side project that auto-generated toolpaths from STEP files before Prototyping.io existed, would push this up a tier. Neither is in the dossier. The dimension reads as competent-by-credential rather than hacker-by-trail.

Co-founder configuration on Prototyping.io rhymes with the weaker end of YC pairs rather than the **Stripe** (2010) Collison-brothers anchor: IIT Bhubaneswar → UT Austin → Schlumberger and CU Boulder → POSSIBLE Mobile → NASA tooling are non-overlapping tracks, and no public source establishes that Bodepudi and Oberai built anything together before Prototyping.io. Two is the right number. The chemistry is the question the dossier cannot answer, which is exactly the case where the YC interview matters more than the application -- and I am writing from the application.

My 36-month forecast: Prototyping.io is dead or has pivoted to a software-only DFM/auto-CAM tool licensed to existing shops (or quietly absorbed by Xometry or Fictiv as a small acqui-hire), with under $2M ARR. The producing mechanism is the signature itself -- real schlep zone, no demonstrated animal -- because automating end-to-end production across CNC, sheet metal, injection molding, extrusion, and die casting with two founders, no announced capacity, and no shipped artifact runs straight into Xometry's $687M FY2025 marketplace and Protolabs' in-house factory fleet before the auto-CAM engine has a chance to compound.

The strongest bear-of-my-bear case is **Airbnb** (2009): I rejected that idea silently in my own head, and the founders converted me anyway. Maybe Prototyping.io's Bodepudi-Schlumberger-machining lineage plus Oberai's embedded-systems hands is the rare pairing that actually closes the CAD-to-G-code loop, and the schlep is the moat the way Coinbase's compliance work became one. The dossier evidence that wins against that case is the absence of the Armstrong wallet equivalent -- every Known Exception I have made (Airbnb, Loopt, Stripe-via-Auctomatic) had a pre-funding artifact or stunt that overrode the skepticism, and Prototyping.io's dossier has a waitlist. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a public demo where Prototyping.io ingests an unseen customer STEP file and ships a real, in-tolerance machined part within a week, posted with the customer named. Until then, pass.
