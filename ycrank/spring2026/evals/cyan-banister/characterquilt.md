# CharacterQuilt -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

CharacterQuilt is the cleanly-governed, ICP-fluent team building exactly the company I wrote my pass template against -- Bhairav Mehta as CEO and Clint Burgess as CMO with no co-founder ambiguity, attacking AI-for-marketers in a category where Jasper alone is doing $88M ARR. The signature here is "high on Biz, near-zero on weird," and that pairing in my book is a polite no.

On founder-from-lived-experience, my HIGH rung is Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) -- a kid who shipped Oculus from his garage before defense hardware was investable; my MID rung is Hassabis at DeepMind (~2013) -- pedigree overridden by genuine cross-domain polymath compounding; my LOW rung is the consensus YC AI-agent pass -- two ex-FAANG PMs who found the problem in a memo. CharacterQuilt's Clint Burgess spent 10 years at Bloomreach moving SDR to Sales to Product Marketing, which is *literally the ICP* they're now selling to, and he personally interviewed 51 CMOs/VPs in three months -- that pins him meaningfully above the LOW rung. But Bhairav Mehta is MIT EECS on leave with NVIDIA Seattle Robotics and NASA-JPL stints -- the pedigree stack I deselect by default -- and his prior YC company Buzzle (S21) was sales-conversation analytics, adjacent but not the marketer's chair. CharacterQuilt sits below the MID rung: one genuine ICP-native founder, one credentialed researcher who studied the problem rather than ran the Marketo instance.

On magically weird, my HIGH rung is Crusoe (~2018) burning North Dakota flares for Bitcoin -- three uninvestable things stapled together; my MID rung is Niantic (~2015) -- AR walking simulators before they were obvious; my LOW rung is the consensus YC AI darling I pass on reflexively. CharacterQuilt is a deployment layer over HubSpot, Marketo, LinkedIn, and Salesforce Marketing Cloud in a category where Jasper has raised $131M, Copy.ai exists, Typeface exists, Writesonic exists, Anyword exists -- the dossier's own competitive landscape table reads like the pass example I wrote. The Grand View Research $82.23B-by-2030 TAM slide and the demo-only CTA with no pricing page tell me this round will be priced as if the outcome is decided. CharacterQuilt sits squarely on the LOW rung. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game here is "ride the AI marketing CAGR," which is exactly the game.

On single CEO authority, my HIGH rung is SpaceX (2008) with Musk's uncontested tiebreaker; my MID rung is Niantic (~2015) under Hanke; my LOW rung is HQ Trivia (2017) and the Yusupov-Kroll feud that imploded the company. CharacterQuilt has Mehta titled CEO and Burgess titled CMO on the YC page -- a clean functional delineation between technical and commercial, which is the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia post-mortem. No dossier evidence of equity split or tiebreaker protocol, so I'm not at the HIGH rung, but I'm closer to it than the middle. This is the dimension where CharacterQuilt actually scores well.

On capital-intensive or unsexy-regulated swing, my HIGH rung is SpaceX (2008) -- my first-ever angel check into hardware everyone called uninvestable; my MID rung is Flock Safety (~2018) -- regulated cameras with municipal sales cycles; my LOW rung is the AI-app SaaS wrapper from the pass example. CharacterQuilt is API orchestration over public martech endpoints with no patents, no proprietary models, no regulatory moat -- the dossier itself flags "agent-based orchestration over public APIs is buildable by funded peers." This sits on the LOW rung. On physical-world verifiability, my HIGH rung is Niantic where I played Ingress obsessively from 2012; my MID rung is Postmates because the delivery happened in front of me; my LOW rung is the developer-tools abstraction I cannot install or photograph. CharacterQuilt has a demo-only CTA, 27 Product Hunt followers as of February 2025, and customer logos with zero case-study metrics -- I cannot touch this product in the wild. LOW rung.

CharacterQuilt in 36 months ends one of two ways: acqui-hired by HubSpot, Salesforce, or Adobe for a low-nine-figure team-and-tech outcome by mid-2028, or grinding at $3-6M ARR while Jasper's $88M ARR base and Copy.ai's funded GTM platform absorb the deployment-orchestration wedge. Mehta is a 2x YC founder who will land somewhere good; the company itself does not become the defining campaign-AI platform because the category's defining platform is already capitalized at $1.5B and consolidating.

The strongest case against my pass is the HandUp exception -- I wrote that check because the founder's biography mirrored the problem, and Clint Burgess is exactly that: ten years inside the Bloomreach marketing org building the tool he wished existed, plus 51 CMO interviews of pre-product diligence. Bhairav Mehta's Buzzle was a B2B-revenue-workflow company, so this is his second swing at the same operator. That is a real founder-market-fit story and I take it seriously. What dismantles it: the category is the consensus YC AI darling I have a literal pass template for, and unlike HandUp's modest-ceiling biographical override, CharacterQuilt has a 9-figure-plus ceiling premise that requires beating Jasper -- biographical alignment doesn't override a funded incumbent who is shipping "100+ specialized AI agents" into the same accounts. **One condition flips me**: if Mehta and Burgess can show me a working installed deployment inside one named enterprise customer -- 1upHealth or Emplifi -- where the marketing ops team has self-served three or more campaigns end-to-end without CS hand-holding, that's the tactile artifact I need and I reconsider at the next round.
