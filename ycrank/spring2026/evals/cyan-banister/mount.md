# Mount -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Mount is the right *category* attacked by the wrong-shaped team at the wrong-shaped moment -- a regulated, capital-intensive insurance carrier swing (the dimension where Mount scores highest) sitting inside a consensus YC P26 sub-batch where Klaimee is the literal cohort-mate and AIUC closed a $15M Nat Friedman-led seed in July 2025 (the dimension where Mount scores lowest). The dossier shows Fabian Amherd and John Bachmann, two Swiss founders with real scrappy trajectories -- a web-dev shop at 16, a $400K-revenue magazine business at 18 -- standing in front of a Lloyd's-shaped problem they have never personally underwritten, claimed, or regulated. Mount is what happens when the unsexy-regulated lane I love gets discovered by every YC team at once.

Anduril (2017) is the HIGH rung on lived experience -- Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before he ever talked to a DoD contracting officer, so when he pitched defense hardware he was already *of* that physics. Mount's founders are admirable outsider-trajectory operators -- Amherd at ETH on CNNs at MESH, Bachmann running Horizonn's five-magazine ad book at 18 -- and that is the "Stanford/Goldman" pedigree-pitch I would not pass on for credentials alone. But neither one has stood inside an insurance carrier's claims department, read a coverholder binder, or felt the moment a silent cyber policy ate someone alive at 2am, and Mount's product *is* a binder. The biography that put them in front of AI-agent liability is analytical, not autobiographical -- closer to my pass example than to Luckey.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I pass on is the LOW rung for "magically weird," and Mount's dossier reproduces that pattern with unusual fidelity. AIUC at $15M from Nat Friedman, Armilla on Chaucer paper at $25M limits, Testudo at $9.25M via Atrium/QBE, Munich Re's aiSure since 2018, and Klaimee inside Mount's own P26 batch with an ex-SafetyWing GM who took an insurance product from $5M to $60M ARR -- that is not "find them before it becomes consensus" (Yahoo Finance, March 2025), that is arriving fifth to a five-horse race. The category is correct; the round is structurally over-shopped.

On capital-intensive/unsexy-regulated, Mount's strongest axis, the mechanism that should compound is proprietary loss data from red-teaming AI agents -- 18 months of policies-in-force would give Mount actuarial signal that AIUC and Armilla also covet, but Munich Re has eight years of it and still caps at $15M (Risk & Insurance, 2026). Mount markets itself as a "carrier" without naming a single capacity provider, reinsurance partner, or admitted/surplus-lines license, while every named competitor sits on Lloyd's syndicate paper. The right answer here is not a software moat; it is a balance sheet, and Mount's dossier shows no balance sheet.

On single-CEO authority, the counterfactual that would flip my score is straightforward: name a CEO. Mount lists Amherd as "Product & Strategy" and Bachmann as "Operations & Growth," with fabian@mount.insure as the YC contact -- the only public tell that Amherd is probably the tiebreaker. The HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll feud and the GameCrush (~2011) activist collapse both happened because nobody answered this question on Day 1, and Mount has not answered it on the YC page in Spring 2026.

Flock Safety (~2018) -- the single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability -- I sourced from a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café before a deck ever crossed my desk; the camera was a *thing* I could photograph. Mount's product is a risk-scoring dashboard, an eight-category liability policy, and a calendar-booking quote flow at mount.insure -- nothing a normal human installs, scans, or photographs in 48 hours. The artifact is a PDF binder, and binders do not teach me anything by being in the wild.

Mount in 36 months: most likely dead or acqui-hired into Munich Re, Chubb, or Armilla as an underwriting-data input for under $10M, with a small chance of a pivot to a red-teaming/risk-scoring SaaS layer sold *into* AIUC and the Lloyd's coverholders once Mount cannot raise reinsurance capacity. The mechanism is straightforward -- without disclosed capacity, Mount cannot bind real limits, and without real limits the enterprise buyer chooses Armilla's $25M Chaucer-backed paper or Munich Re's eight-year book over a two-founder Swiss pre-seed every single time.

The bull case worth steel-manning: incumbents are actively retreating -- 80%+ of carrier requests to exclude AI from general liability have been state-approved (PYMNTS, 2026) -- and Mount has Pete Koomen as YC group partner, two genuinely scrappy teen-founder operators, and the unsexy-regulated lane that produced Vigil Labs (August 2025) and Mindbloom. HandUp (~2014) is the Known Exception I would invoke if Amherd or Bachmann had been personally bankrupted by an AI-agent failure, the way HandUp's founder mirrored my own homelessness. They were not. The verifiable condition that would flip me to Invest: Mount discloses a named Lloyd's syndicate or admitted-carrier partner *and* names a single CEO with majority operating control before Demo Day -- without both, the structural deficit against AIUC and Klaimee compounds every month, and I would rather write this check into a Vigil Labs-class regulated swing where the founder authentically lives in the domain.
