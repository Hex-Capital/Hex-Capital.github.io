# ZeroDrift -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

ZeroDrift is the archetype of the company I pass on — competent, well-positioned, and designed to generate exactly the kind of return that wastes my time. Kumesh Aroomoogan has 12 years building AI for financial services at Accern, raised $40M there, got the Forbes 30 Under 30 stamp, and is now building his second company in the same vertical for the same buyers. Every mainstream VC will love this. I don't invest in pattern-matched founder-market fit narratives. I invest in consequences. If ZeroDrift executes flawlessly and captures the entire pre-send compliance communication market in financial services, what changes? Compliance officers at Citi and Vanguard review fewer flagged messages. Financial advisors send emails 30% faster. The world looks identical. Nobody's life is materially different. That's a $1-3B outcome at ceiling, and a 3-5x return — the exact return profile I avoid because it doesn't justify the opportunity cost of my attention.

The technical approach confirms the pattern. ZeroDrift encodes SEC and FINRA rules into "rulepacks" and validates messages before send using LLM-based content checking. The dossier itself concedes the core risk: "LLM-based content checking is technically reproducible. Any company with access to foundation models could build a basic compliance checker." The differentiation is domain encoding — knowing which FINRA rules apply to which message types — not a novel architecture or proprietary model. Pre-send versus post-send is a product design choice, not a technology discontinuity. Smarsh, with 6,500+ customers and relationships at 18 of the 20 largest financial institutions, could ship a pre-send module in a quarter if the market demanded it. Global Relay, doing $189M in revenue, has the same option. ZeroDrift's moat is integration work and regulatory knowledge, not physics or computation. That's an execution advantage, not a technology wedge.

Aroomoogan is the opposite of the founder profile I gravitate toward. He's a deep domain expert building a second company in the same industry for the same customer base. His Accern background — NLP for financial services, working with banks on regulatory filings — is precisely the 10-year resume I discount relative to first-principles reasoning and learning velocity. When I backed Peter Beck at Rocket Lab, a self-taught engineer from New Zealand with no aerospace degree building rocket engines in his garage, I was betting on the learning rate of someone who didn't know what couldn't be done. Aroomoogan knows exactly what can be done because he's done adjacent work for a decade. That produces reliable execution, not breakthrough ambition. The claimed "$1B valuation" at Wand AI that "could not be independently verified" against Crunchbase's $33M raised number is a credibility flag I'd probe hard in a meeting.

The bull case deserves attention. $150K ARR with 50%+ month-over-month growth roughly two months after launch is real signal — that's not a waitlist, that's paying customers. The $2M qualified pipeline naming Franklin Templeton, EQT Partners, Nasdaq, Citi, Two Sigma, and Vanguard suggests enterprise pull, not founder push. And the timing argument has substance: Global Relay reports ChatGPT capture surging ~3,000% year-over-year in regulated workflows, creating a genuinely new compliance surface that post-send surveillance tools weren't built to address. If ZeroDrift becomes the default pre-send enforcement layer across financial services as AI-generated content proliferates, the market could be larger than today's compliance-as-a-service TAM suggests. For this to matter to me, though, ZeroDrift would need to be the beginning of making compliance expertise itself free — replacing the $200K/year compliance officer, not giving her a better tool. Nothing in the product or positioning suggests that ambition. The three modules — Compose, Guard, Command — are designed to augment existing compliance teams, not eliminate them.

I check my known exceptions. DoorDash violated my technical approach requirement, but the consequence magnitude was planetary — transforming food access for hundreds of millions. ZeroDrift's consequence magnitude is microscopic by comparison: faster regulatory email review for financial professionals. Oscar Health accepted regulatory risk because it had a path to radically affordable healthcare. ZeroDrift makes compliance cheaper for firms that already budget for it — no consumer affordability angle exists. None of my exception triggers activate. ZeroDrift is a well-built compliance SaaS company that will likely succeed on its own terms. Those terms — a profitable enterprise software business serving a regulatory niche — are not my terms. I either want to lose my money or make 50 times my money. ZeroDrift is designed to return 5x. Pass.
