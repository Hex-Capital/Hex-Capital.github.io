# Care GP -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Care GP is what happens when a capital-efficient business with real numbers -- 150+ GP clinics, zero churn, profitable per Globe and Mail -- shows up wearing the exact costume my Pass Example was written to reject: "AI agents for [vertical]" in a hot 2026 YC batch. Melvin Chen has built a default-alive business; the question I keep landing on is whether the round being offered prices that asset, or prices the AI-agent narrative wrapped around it. Those are not the same trade, and Care GP keeps quietly insisting they are.

**Solana at $0.05** is the high rung on price discipline because most generalist funds had quit the category and the entry was illegible to the consensus crowd. Care GP is the inverted picture on that axis -- it sits one rung above my canonical pass-example deck only because the company is genuinely profitable and Australian rather than Bay-Area consensus. But the dossier flags Heidi Health at ~$138M raised and ~$700M valuation, Lyrebird at ~$12M, and Best Practice Software shipping its own native scribe and admin agent. Care GP enters a YC S26 batch where "AI agent for primary care admin" is exactly the deck I told myself I would not read. The press wave in October 2025 -- MobiHealthNews, Globe and Mail, Yahoo Finance, Cantech Letter, National Law Review, all the same month -- is the tell that this round will be marketed as a hot deck, not a value-seed entry, and the dossier is silent on round size or pricing, which under my default is the worst version of silence.

The low rung on founder-as-brand is the canonical YC enterprise founder with no public writing, no community footprint, no published opinion on the category by name -- the inverse of John Fish or Marina Mogilko. Care GP's Melvin Chen has no Twitter/X account discoverable, no GitHub, an unretrievable LinkedIn follower count, and one Scale My Clinic podcast appearance. The Forbes 30 Under 30 in Finance & VC is a pedigree marker, not a distribution channel; Oxford-Valedictorian-into-Terra-Firma-and-Goldman is a CV that closes B2B sales meetings but does not own a single GP audience that Care GP didn't have to buy or partner its way into. The dossier names Best Practice Software as a listed-partner distribution path -- which is also the exact entity launching the competing native admin agent. Care GP's distribution sits inside a counterparty that is becoming its competitor; the founder-channel score has to absorb that there is no founder-owned audience to fall back on when the partner page goes cold.

On capital structure, Care GP is the strongest dimension and the only one that genuinely activates my "Our Letter" frame: profitable, zero churn, 10-person team, 140% average MoM usage growth. That is closer to the Squarespace/Muck Rack/Zapier shape I keep namechecking than to the burn-and-Series-C archetype I reject. The mechanism that compounds here over 18 months is real: a profitable SaaS at this stage either accelerates without dilution or absorbs venture capital as fuel for a credible expansion, not as life support. The thing Care GP does not give me is a non-standard instrument -- no revenue-share, no employee-equity rollup, no holdco shape -- just a standard SAFE on a standard B2B SaaS cap table. Care GP gets credit for the underlying business shape; it does not get credit for asking me to underwrite an asset other funds can't.

The counterfactual on founder-conviction: for Care GP to flip a tier on Criterion 3, Melvin Chen would have had to spend two years writing publicly about why Australian GP admin is a category nobody at Y Combinator understood -- the way I want a founder to have been wrong-out-loud before the round. Instead the Startmate writeup frames the arc as "from sabbatical to super agents," which is the opposite signature: opportunity-response rather than mission. The PE-to-AI-agent path is legible, well-credentialed, and exactly what I de-rate. Care GP is being run by a competent operator, not a missionary; the dossier does not contain the public-writing artifact that would change that read.

Category sleeve: my unstated pattern is healthcare-DTC and prescription-rail -- Ro, PillPack, Truepill, Hinge Health, Livongo -- not B2B clinic admin SaaS. Care GP is healthcare-adjacent in a way that does not load onto the sleeve I'm actually convicted on; it loads instead onto the "AI agents for enterprise vertical" sleeve I have explicitly written into the Pass Example. The Joe Lonsdale-podcast line -- "AI is just more internet" -- is doing real work here: Care GP is OCR-plus-routing of clinical documents made viable by GPT-4-class models in 2024, which is exactly the "more internet" framing, not a fundamentally disruptive primitive.

Forecast, 36 months: Care GP reaches AUD $8-20M ARR concentrated in Australia, gets squeezed by Best Practice Software's native admin agent and Lyrebird's bundled expansion, and is acquired by either Best Practice or Telstra Health for AUD $40-100M -- a fine outcome for Melvin Chen and a non-event for a seed fund that paid YC-S26-AI-agent pricing. The mechanism is straightforward: the channel partner becomes the channel competitor, the unit economics that look great at 150 clinics compress as Heidi and Lyrebird use ~$150M of combined capital to undercut admin-agent pricing as an attach to scribing, and Care GP's profitability becomes the exit-readiness signal rather than the platform-scale signal.

The bull case worth steel-manning is the Ro exception: high-burn capital-efficiency-violating health businesses can become venture moments when regulatory schleps are the moat, and Care GP's ISO27001 onshore-data certification plus Best Practice PMS integration could be that schlep at Australian-clinic scale. Heidi at ~$700M valuation on scribing is the proof-of-concept that the local market funds these outcomes. The dossier evidence that wins for me: Ro's exception was 50-state prescription rails -- jurisdictional complexity Care GP does not carry, because Care GP is one country, no Rx licensing, and the "moat" is integration into a counterparty (Best Practice) that is actively building the substitute. ISO27001 is table stakes, not a Ro-shaped regulatory moat. The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Care GP raises this round at a pre-money under USD $10M with a tier-1 fund visibly passing on the AI-agent framing, and Melvin Chen publishes something opinionated about why scribe-companies-extending-into-admin will fail in Australian primary care -- give me the price discipline plus the conviction artifact and I'll re-read the deck.
