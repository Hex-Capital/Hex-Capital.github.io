## The Idea

- **Problem:** Organizations want to deploy AI agents but lack a systematic way to identify which repetitive, high-cost desktop tasks are worth automating, currently relying on ad-hoc surveys or consulting engagements (YC Launch page; autostep.ai).
- **Approach:** A desktop app installed across an organization that observes employees' work, surfaces repetitive tasks with dollar-value rankings and automation feasibility scores, then auto-generates agents, prompts, templates, or recommends existing tools (autostep.ai feature list).
- **Differentiation:** Versus enterprise task-mining incumbents (UiPath Task Mining, Skan, Mimica, Celonis), Autostep frames itself as the "operational intelligence layer" that also builds/recommends the remediation (agent or workflow), not just the discovery map (YC Launch page; autostep.ai).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: Likely per-seat SaaS for the desktop agent given the "installed across your organization" model and demo-only sales motion (Calendly demo booking, no pricing page on autostep.ai); no published pricing.
- **TAM/SAM:** Process mining software market $3.66B in 2025, projected $5.45B in 2026 growing to $58.18B by 2034 at 34.4% CAGR (Fortune Business Insights, 2025); no public TAM specific to task-mining-plus-agent-building.
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Founder-led outbound + YC/Neo network referrals to ops leaders, supported by Discord community and demo bookings via Calendly (autostep.ai; YC page lists 0 open jobs).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Marketing copy claims "compounding context" — a queryable record of company operations that improves weekly — though no published data, customer counts, or retention numbers substantiate the moat (autostep.ai).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Proprietary dataset of task-to-automation mappings across customers could yield a benchmark/recommendation engine; unproven now because of solo-founder stage and no disclosed customer base.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: No structural barrier identified — incumbents (UiPath, Celonis, Skan, Mimica) already operate task-mining products and could extend into agent-generation without channel conflict.
- **Commoditization risk:** Established task-mining vendors with $40M+ funding (Skan $54M total; Mimica $32.2M total) and platform incumbents (UiPath FY2025 revenue >$1.5B) could replicate the "observe + generate agent" loop (Crunchbase; Tracxn; UiPath IR).
