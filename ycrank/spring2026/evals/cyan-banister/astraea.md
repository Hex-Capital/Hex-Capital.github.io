# Astraea -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Joshua Wang and Sanmay Sarada are two Stanford CS graduates pitching multi-agent biometrics automation against Saama's already-shipping agentic SDTM Navigator -- which means Astraea sits in the lane I fund (regulated, unsexy pharma data plumbing) but arrives via the founder profile I most often pass on (analytical, not autobiographical). The signature is a regulated workflow I cannot touch built by founders whose contact with the SDTM/ADaM grind is, on the public record, professional adjacency rather than lived obsession. That pairing is the whole evaluation.

Vigil Labs (August 2025) is my anchor on the unsexy-regulated axis -- financial software where the compliance perimeter is itself the moat -- and Astraea's CDISC/21 CFR Part 11/HIPAA/GDPR surface area is genuinely the right shape of category for my lane. Where Astraea diverges from the Vigil rung is that the regulated wrapper here sits atop a pure software pipeline that Saama, Medidata, and Veeva already monetize from inside their EDC seat base; the Saama site already markets an "Agentic AI framework" SDTM Navigator with 50+ pharma customers and 1,500+ studies behind it. Astraea's regulated moat is real but it is not compounding faster than the incumbents'; it is compounding inside the same buyer with the same standards.

The pass example in my own playbook is the consensus YC AI-agent darling -- two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, who discovered the problem in a memo -- and Astraea's founder dossier rhymes with it more than I want it to. Joshua Wang's bio reads "Stanford Math/CS; previously built multi-agent AI systems and clinical data automation workflows," which is the analytical posture, not the autobiographical one. There is no Palmer-Luckey-shipping-Oculus-first moment, no Hanke-spent-a-decade-on-Keyhole moment, no founder who ran a biostatistics team at a CRO and felt the nine-month SAS-programming purgatory in their own hands. Sanmay Sarada's BCM Data Science Research intern line is the only thread of lived contact in the dossier, and an internship is not a biography.

The "magically weird" filter inverts on Astraea: YC P26 is shipping multi-agent vertical SaaS into every regulated category at once, and Saama's contemporaneous agentic SDTM launch is the tell that the capability frontier moved into CDISC mapping inside the last twelve months, which means Astraea is racing the consensus rather than arriving before it. The "trusted across 10+ global pharma and biotech sponsors" claim has no named logo attached on tryastraea.com -- in eighteen months either that converts into a Top-3 pharma case study and the wedge holds, or it stays anonymous and Saama/Veeva price the feature to zero inside the EDC bundle.

Astraea would need a counterfactual on founder origin for the score to flip a tier: one of Wang or Sarada having shipped a SDTM/ADaM pipeline at a named CRO or pharma sponsor before this company, the way I want to see the Anduril-Luckey shipping-VR-first pattern. The dossier does not have it. The CTO's "clinical and hospital data infrastructure for diagnostic software systems" is the closest thread and it is too vague to load-bear -- I cannot tell if Sanmay Sarada owned a regulated submission pipeline or interned next to one.

Niantic was the artifact I played in 2012 before I funded it in 2015 -- I could feel the pull of Ingress in my own hands. Astraea has a cal.com demo-request CTA and no installable surface; the product is by definition invisible to anyone outside a pharma sponsor's biometrics team, which means my entire physical-environment sourcing method (the Four Seasons Wi-Fi-name move, the dinner-table observation move) is structurally inapplicable. That is not Astraea's fault -- enterprise pharma SaaS is never going to be a tactile artifact -- but it does mean I have no independent read on whether the 99.8% validation claim or the 30-50% biometrics-cycle claim is real.

My 36-month forecast on Astraea: acquired by Saama, Veeva, or Medidata at a $50-150M outcome that returns roughly 2-4x on a preseed entry, or absorbed silently as Saama's agentic SDTM Navigator subsumes the wedge before Astraea reaches Series B. The mechanism is straightforward -- regulated pharma SaaS that does not bring proprietary data or a sponsor-network effect to the table gets bought, not scaled, because the incumbent's EDC seat base is the distribution moat that Astraea cannot replicate from a team of two with no disclosed enterprise sales hires.

The bull case stings: if "10+ global pharma and biotech sponsors" is real and includes even one Top-3 pharma running an active Phase III submission through Astraea, the switching cost on validated audit-traced artifacts mid-NDA is enormous, and Joshua Wang's claim of "prior multi-agent AI + clinical data automation" could be the rare instance where the analytical-sounding bio is hiding genuine domain depth. I have funded DeepMind through the pedigree-as-thesis red flag before. What dismantles the bull case is the absence of a single named pharma logo on tryastraea.com paired with Saama already shipping the same agentic SDTM wedge from a position of 1,500-study incumbency -- the analogous DeepMind override required Hassabis's chess-prodigy-neuroscientist-game-designer polymath stack, and the Astraea founder dossier surfaces no such cross-domain compounding. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named Top-3 pharma case study on the public site within twelve months, ideally one where Astraea displaced a Saama or Medidata SDTM pipeline rather than greenfielding a new sponsor.
