# Oasiz -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Let me start with the question I always start with: if Oasiz fully succeeds, does the world visibly change? The answer is that a new entertainment platform emerges — interactive content becomes as shareable as a TikTok video, casual games become a social communication primitive. That is a large company outcome. Roblox built a $4.8B revenue business with 380M monthly active users on the premise that user-generated games are a category. But making casual games easier to create and share is fundamentally a redistribution of entertainment attention, not the creation of something the world desperately needs. Nobody's life is materially different because they can generate a multiplayer game from a text prompt. Compare that to Commonwealth Fusion Systems, where success means limitless clean energy, or Impossible Foods, where success means protein without animal agriculture. Oasiz's consequence ceiling is "a very successful consumer entertainment company." I don't lose sleep over entertainment.

The technical architecture concerns me more than the market. The dossier is explicit: Oasiz's AI game generation layer "relies on generally available LLMs and generative models." Rosebud AI has $15M and is building 3D game creation from natural language. CodeWisp and Playabl.ai — both YC companies — are building text-to-game tools. The dossier itself concludes that the defensible unit is "creation + social distribution + community in a single mobile app, not any single component." When the defensibility argument is "the bundle," not the technology, that tells me there is no proprietary technical wedge. This is a distribution and network effects bet dressed in AI clothing. I prefer technology risk to market risk, and Oasiz has inverted that preference — the technology is commodity, the risk is entirely market execution.

Abel Dagne's track record is the most compelling signal in this dossier. A Stanford student who built apps with 500K+ downloads that reached #1 on App Store social charts — that is not resume padding, that is demonstrated consumer distribution instinct. The iteration velocity is visible: v1.0 launched February 13, 2026, and by April 6 they had shipped v1.4 with a virtual currency system (Jemz) introduced in v1.3. Four releases in under two months from a four-person team signals urgency. Jonathan Dinh brings Berkeley AI research credentials through the ROAR lab and a top-0.01% mobile gaming ranking that gives genuine domain empathy. The 6.5M organic TikTok views and 230K platform plays are real traction for a pre-seed company. But I have no evidence of the first-principles reasoning I prize — no signal of what surprised them, what they pivoted on, what they learned that contradicted their assumptions during the batch. Execution velocity is necessary but not sufficient for the kind of outsized outcome I invest for.

The strongest bull case: social media primitives have evolved from text to photos to video, and interactive content is the next logical step. If Oasiz captures the "interactive" slot in that sequence, the outcome is TikTok-scale, not Roblox-scale. The Atari IP offer — unconfirmed but reported — suggests brand partners see this platform as a distribution channel, which would accelerate the flywheel. And the timing is genuinely novel: AI-generated functional games from text prompts was not feasible 24 months ago, so there is a real "why now." But I have seen this exact pattern — "we are TikTok for X" — hundreds of times. The pattern produces 3-5x returns when it works, not 50x. The 230K plays and 21 App Store ratings do not yet evidence the kind of organic retention that would signal a new social primitive taking hold. For this to be my kind of bet, I would need to see Oasiz creating entirely new behavior patterns — people communicating through interactive experiences in ways they never did before — not just a more convenient way to play casual games.

The DoorDash comparison is instructive. I backed DoorDash despite it being an execution bet without a technology discontinuity because the consequence magnitude was planetary — transforming food access for hundreds of millions of people is a basic human need. That exception required both enormous consequence AND a founder (Tony Xu) demonstrating extraordinary learning velocity. Oasiz has the execution-heavy profile without the consequence magnitude that triggers the exception. Entertainment is not food. A more convenient way to create and share games does not solve a problem "too important not to work on." The company is well-executed for what it is, but what it is — a consumer entertainment platform competing for attention against Roblox, TikTok, and three funded AI game-creation competitors — does not generate the asymmetric return profile I require. I either want to lose my money or make 50 times my money. Oasiz is designed to produce a moderate outcome in a contested market. Pass.
