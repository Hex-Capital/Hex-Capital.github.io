# Callab AI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Retell AI hit $40M ARR. PolyAI raised $200M at a $750M valuation. Vapi scaled to millions in revenue within six months of launch. The AI voice agent category is one of the hottest consensus plays in the entire YC ecosystem right now, and Callab AI is swimming in that exact pool. My first question is always "what if they're right and the market is wrong?" But here the market isn't wrong -- the market is *very* enthusiastically right about AI voice agents. Every sophisticated investor I know has this category circled. Callab AI's twist is legacy PBX integration, and sure, most VCs haven't focused on on-prem telephony stacks specifically. But "underserved deployment niche within the hottest AI category" is not the kind of structural mispricing I get excited about. It's a targeting choice, not a contrarian bet. When I seeded Solana at four cents, the entire smart money establishment had written off alternative L1s. Nobody has written off AI voice agents -- they're fighting to get in.

Callab AI's core pitch -- plugging AI voice agents into CUCM and SIP trunks without rip-and-replace -- is practical, sensible, and incremental. That's exactly the problem. This is not a new primitive. Callab AI didn't invent AI voice agents; they built an integration adapter for legacy infrastructure. Nobody will say "Callab me" the way they say "Venmo me." When I backed Venmo, peer-to-peer mobile payments was a genuinely new behavior that created a category from nothing. Callab AI is competing in an existing, well-defined market (contact center AI) with a deployment-method differentiator. "We work with your old phone system" is a feature, not a category. The primitive test fails cleanly here -- and the closest competitor, Trillet AI, already claims nearly identical legacy PBX integration with Avaya, CUCM, Mitel, and Asterisk, which tells me the integration itself is not that exotic.

The bull case an advocate would make: 35% of Fortune 500 contact centers still route legacy trunks. That's real installed base, real enterprise budget, real pain. Cloud migration for large enterprises takes years, sometimes a decade. Callab AI could own the bridge -- the way these enterprises adopt AI voice without a $50M infrastructure overhaul. The founders co-authored the 101 Billion Arabic Words Dataset (arXiv 2405.01590), which is a legitimate research contribution and gives Callab AI a genuine edge in Arabic dialect support that PolyAI and Retell haven't prioritized. Karim Beguir -- co-founder of InstaDeep, acquired by BioNTech for ~$680M -- wrote the $600K pre-seed check, which is a meaningful endorsement from someone who built and exited a serious AI company. Kchaou and Dhaouadi have six years of co-founder history dating back to ClusterLab in 2020, and Dhaouadi built platforms serving 200,000+ users for Tunisian government entities. These are capable, grounded operators with real networks across MENA. I take all that seriously. But capable founders building a sensible product in a hot category is not what gets me to write a check. Sane people pitching sane ideas is just... Tuesday. I need the idea to be insane. "AI voice agents that work with your old PBX" does not make me think "what you're doing is fucking crazy."

Callab AI fails my AI kill test on two vectors. First, the product itself is an AI application layer -- voice agents built on LLMs. Every time I get pitched "AI for contact centers," I think about what happens when NICE (which just acquired Cognigy for $955M), Genesys, or Five9 ships the same capability natively. They have the distribution, the existing enterprise contracts, and the incentive. Callab AI's response would be "but we integrate with legacy PBX," which brings me to the second vector: the legacy PBX market is shrinking by definition. Building your moat on infrastructure that enterprises are actively decommissioning is building on a melting ice cube. The Metropolis parallel would apply if Callab AI owned the telephony infrastructure itself -- parking garages don't get cloud-migrated -- but Callab AI integrates *with* someone else's legacy infrastructure, which is a fundamentally weaker position. When the infrastructure migrates, Callab AI's differentiation evaporates.

Callab AI's capital posture is reasonable -- $600K pre-seed, team of seven, operating from UAE/Tunisia where burn is lower -- and the software directory listings (Capterra, GetApp) suggest organic GTM rather than growth-at-all-costs. Callab AI has clients across three continents and names DunkinDonut as a customer. That's real for pre-seed. But capital discipline is a hygiene factor, not a thesis driver, and the "clients across three continents" language without revenue disclosure tells me the numbers aren't yet compelling enough to headline. The condition that would flip me to Invest: if Callab AI showed me $500K+ ARR specifically from Fortune 500 legacy telephony accounts with multi-year contracts and 150%+ net revenue retention -- proof that the legacy PBX niche is not a bridge but a wedge into enterprise telephony writ large, and that Callab AI is becoming embedded infrastructure rather than a point solution. Short of that, this is a sensible AI application-layer company in a consensus-hot category with a deployment differentiator that erodes over time. Pass.
