# Minicor -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Faizaan Chishtie and Saheed Akinbile have spent six years inside legacy-system integration -- Laminar before Minicor, Morgan Stanley Java pipelines saving $30M/year in fees, QuickBooks and Sage and Citrix scar tissue -- and they are now running that specific knowledge through an enterprise sales motion with a Forward Deployed Engineer role at $125K-$200K. That is the analytical signature of Minicor: top-quartile founder-market fit bolted to a bottom-quartile leverage architecture. The founders are real. The distribution is permissioned.

Twitter (2007) is the leverage-architecture rung that matters here, and Minicor sits nowhere near it. The shared question is whether the marginal cost of one more customer is near-zero through code and media, or whether it requires a human in a suit and a Citrix deployment ticket. Twitter charged nothing, anyone could post, replication was free; Minicor sells SOC 2 Type II and HIPAA-attested Windows VM hosting into healthcare, automotive, logistics, financial services through what the dossier explicitly describes as "Direct enterprise sales to AI vendors needing legacy integrations," with a Forward Deployed Engineer being hired to land each account. Labor and capital require permission; Minicor's GTM is labor leverage by construction. The "25,000 patients/day in production" line is impressive operationally and dead silent on permissionless distribution -- I cannot point to a single mechanism in the Minicor dossier where the Nth customer lowers acquisition cost for the (N+1)th.

Perplexity is the only single-sided app I funded, and the failure mechanism on the network dimension is that each user's value does not compound the previous user's value -- a structural ceiling on the asymmetric-payoff math. Does that pattern appear in Minicor? Yes, more starkly than in Perplexity. Each Minicor deployment -- the EHR integration at one health system, the dealer-management automation at one auto group -- is economically independent of the next. The dossier's gestured future moat of "accumulated UI-change recovery telemetry" is the kind of inferred network-effect-by-data story I have heard a hundred times at seed; it is unproven, undisclosed, and structurally weaker than Perplexity's distribution-by-API. Minicor lacks even the API-distribution upside that let me waive criterion #2 for Aravind Srinivas.

Specific knowledge is where Minicor earns its keep. Saheed Akinbile's RPA domain immersion across QuickBooks, Sage, and Citrix is not a McKinsey deck's worth of TAM analysis -- it is the kind of lived-problem ownership the Stack Overflow (2008) founders had on the other side of the developer fence. Faizaan Chishtie ran Laminar before Minicor on the same legacy-integration problem, and the rebrand path from laminar.run to minicor.com is the dossier's own evidence that this is a multi-year obsession, not a memo-founder pivot off a competitor's traction. That said, Minicor's specific knowledge is the kind that compounds inside services delivery, not the kind that compounds inside a code-leverage moat -- it tells me the founders will execute, not that the architecture will compound.

The market math on Minicor over the next 18 months is structurally squeezed from two sides: $3.6B RPA today growing to $35.27B by 2026 sounds like pivot-room until you remember UiPath has $1.5B ARR and Automation Anywhere has agentic features shipping in 2025, while Anthropic's computer-use API and Skyvern's open-source repo (YC, $2.7M raised) are collapsing the build cost for the underlying primitive. Minicor's 93-96% click accuracy vs. 80-85% baseline is a 12-point edge that foundation models will close on their own timeline. Pivot vectors exist -- browser agents, vertical SaaS in healthcare RCM, an SDK -- but each pivot consumes Minicor's seed runway against incumbents with billions in revenue and category-defining brand.

Counterfactual on the founder trio: for Minicor's score to flip a tier here, I would need a prior clean exit by Chishtie or Akinbile -- the dossier shows neither, just Homeboard at mid-five-figure ARR and Laminar rebranded into Minicor. That is not a red flag; it is the absence of a strong positive signal. The 10-year-partner test on Minicor reads clean, quiet, executional -- which is exactly the profile I default to neutral on at 7/14.

Minicor in 36 months is most likely a $4-10M ARR forward-deployed automation business inside healthcare RCM and Citrix-bound financial services workflows, acquired by UiPath or Automation Anywhere for $60-150M as the incumbents buy agentic recovery rather than build it -- a respectable outcome that returns 1-3x on my angel check and zero of my portfolio's 10,000x upside math. The mechanism producing this forecast is the same labor-leverage architecture that prevents Minicor from compounding without gatekeeper permission: every revenue dollar requires a Forward Deployed Engineer touch, every Citrix deployment is a procurement cycle, and every accuracy point gets closed by Anthropic's next model.

The strongest bull case on Minicor is the Anchorage Digital precedent: a regulated, permissioned business where the permission itself becomes the moat. HIPAA, SOC 2 Type II, on-prem and Citrix deployment, and 25,000 patients/day of PHI handling are not weaknesses -- they are exactly the kind of compliance moat that foundation-model vendors cannot casually replicate, the same way OCC-chartered crypto custody became Anchorage's structural leverage. UiPath built $1.5B ARR in this exact gravity well; Minicor could be the agentic-RPA primitive UiPath should have built. But the Anchorage exception required a category I had been DCA-ing into pre-consensus since MetaStable (2014); RPA is a twenty-year-old category I have never funded, and Faizaan Chishtie's compliance posture does not substitute for the category-timing call. The verifiable condition that would flip Minicor's verdict is an open-source Minicor SDK on GitHub with organic developer-pull adoption -- the Stack Overflow (2008) and Replit (2023) pattern of code shipping before equity story. Until that exists, Minicor is a competent enterprise services business pretending to be permissionless leverage, and I pass.
