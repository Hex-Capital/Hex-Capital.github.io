## The Idea

**Problem:** AI tools have reduced game creation time from weeks to seconds, but distribution infrastructure has not kept pace (a16z Speedrun portal). Existing UGC gaming platforms like Roblox require significant development skill and time via Lua scripting. Incumbent social platforms (TikTok, Instagram) distribute video and images but not interactive software. [Inferred]: Creators who can now generate games from text prompts lack a native distribution channel optimized for viral sharing of interactive content.

**Approach:** Oasiz provides AI-powered tools enabling users to generate multiplayer games in seconds, then distributes them via a TikTok-style vertical feed (GamesPress, Oct 2025 via search snippet). The app features instant multiplayer, live leaderboards, customizable avatars, a personalized discovery feed, and weekly new game releases (App Store listing, Apr 2026). Users can play, remix, and create games with friends (App Store listing).

**Differentiation:**
- vs. **Roblox:** Oasiz targets casual "playable content" creation in seconds via AI prompts rather than Roblox's developer-oriented scripting environment; distribution is social-feed-native rather than search-based (GamesPress via search snippet).
- vs. **Rosebud AI:** Rosebud focuses on 3D game creation tools for developers; Oasiz bundles creation with social distribution in a consumer mobile app (Rosebud.ai via search snippet).
- vs. **CodeWisp:** CodeWisp generates web games from text prompts but publishes to the open web rather than an integrated social feed with community features (YC listing via search snippet).

**Business Model:** Free app with no visible pricing tiers (App Store, Apr 2026). v1.3 introduced "Jemz," a shared in-app currency system (App Store version history, Mar 31, 2026). [Inferred]: Most likely monetization path is virtual currency for in-app purchases, potentially supplemented by brand/IP licensing partnerships.

**TAM/SAM:**
- UGC Gaming market: $12.4B in 2025, projected $24.8B by 2032 at 10.3% CAGR (6W Research via search snippet).
- UGC Platform market: $9.85B in 2025, projected $43.92B by 2031 at 28.32% CAGR (Mordor Intelligence via search snippet).
- [Inferred]: SAM is the mobile-first, casual AI-generated game distribution segment within UGC gaming — a fraction of the $12.4B total.

**GTM / Distribution:** The platform demonstrated organic virality on TikTok with 6.5M+ views (a16z Speedrun portal). [Inferred]: Primary distribution is social media virality (TikTok) driving App Store downloads, with in-app social mechanics (friend tagging, leaderboards, remixing) supporting retention and organic growth loops.

## Defensibility

- **Network effects (nascent):** Each game created adds to the content library; each player adds social/competitive value. 5,000+ playable experiences built in closed beta (GamesPress, Oct 2025 via search snippet). [Inferred]: Network effects strengthen as the content catalog and player base grow but remain nascent at current scale.
- **Brand partnerships:** Atari reportedly offered to bring their IP catalog to the platform (a16z Speedrun portal; no independent confirmation found).

**Market structure:** Roblox's business model depends on developers investing significant time building complex experiences that monetize through in-experience purchases; pivoting to AI-generated casual content could cannibalize developer engagement and the economics of their $1B+ annual developer payout structure (Business of Apps, 2025 via search snippet). [Inferred]: TikTok could add playable mini-games but faces the complexity of building a game engine and the distraction from core video content.

**Commoditization risk:** The AI game generation layer relies on generally available LLMs and generative models. Rosebud AI ($15M Series A; Leads on Trees via search snippet), CodeWisp (YC), and Playabl.ai (YC) are building similar creation tools. [Inferred]: The defensible bundle is creation + social distribution + community in a single mobile app, not any single component.

## Key Risks

**TikTok distribution dependency:** 6.5M organic TikTok views are a primary growth signal (a16z Speedrun portal). TikTok algorithm changes, regulatory action (potential US ban/forced sale), or content policy shifts could disrupt this channel. Mitigation: in-app social features (friend tagging, leaderboards) reduce external platform dependency.

**Well-funded direct competitors:** Rosebud AI ($15M, backed by a16z and Khosla Ventures; Leads on Trees via search snippet), CodeWisp (YC), and Playabl.ai (YC) are all building AI game creation tools. Oasiz's differentiation depends on the social distribution layer reaching critical mass before competitors add similar features.

**Roblox expansion into AI-assisted creation:** Roblox has 3.5M active creators and $1B+ in annual developer payouts (Business of Apps, 2025 via search snippet). If Roblox adds AI-assisted casual game creation, it could absorb Oasiz's value proposition into its 380M-MAU ecosystem.

**Unproven monetization:** Jemz currency introduced in v1.3 (App Store, Mar 31, 2026) but no public revenue data. Conversion from free engagement to paying users is unvalidated.

**IP partnership execution risk:** The Atari IP offer is self-reported (a16z Speedrun portal) with no independent confirmation. Executing IP partnerships requires legal and content moderation infrastructure that may strain a 4-person team.
