# Prototyping.io -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Prototyping.io is a domain-credentialed two-person team — Revanth Bodepudi (UT Austin MS Aerospace, ex-Schlumberger) and Prerit Oberai (ex-Microsoft AI engineer) — attempting an asset-light AI-native CNC quoting layer in a market where Xometry has already shipped $686.6M of FY2025 revenue against the same workflow. The dimensional signature is a relatively credible founder pairing sitting at the wrong layer of a market whose "AI quoting" moment was captured two years ago by incumbents. That's the pattern that drives my pass.

On Infrastructure-Layer Positioning, my ladder runs Stripe (the payments API everyone built on) at the top, Gusto (workflow-embedded payroll, sticky but not a developer platform) in the middle, and Wish at the bottom — the consumer marketplace with no API layer that collapsed 99% post-IPO because there was nothing structural underneath the growth. Prototyping.io sits between Wish and Gusto, closer to Wish: the homepage is a CAD-upload-and-quote waitlist with no published API, no developer surface, no disclosed shop network it owns, and an [Inferred] hybrid model coordinating partner CNC shops. That's a transactional brokerage with a CAM frontend, not infrastructure that other businesses embed and can't rip out. Xometry already occupies that brokerage layer at scale.

On Nonobvious Market Timing — my entry point — the ladder is Perplexity at the top (I wrote the seed before ChatGPT, reading the capability curve), Harvey in the middle (GPT-4 unlocking a market that had been a graveyard for a decade), and the consumer social pass at the bottom (no exogenous shift making it possible NOW). Prototyping.io sits between Harvey and the consumer-social floor, but closer to the floor. The Spring 2026 RFS framing from Nicolas Dessaigne is real, and LLM-driven DFM is genuinely better than 2022, but MakerVerse already ships "AI price calculation with manual engineering review" and Xometry has been building instant-quote automation since 2022. The window cracked open already; Prototyping.io is showing up after consensus, not before it. Check the timestamp.

On Single-Miracle Viability, the ladder runs Harvey at the top (one exogenous miracle, foundation-model capability), Stripe in the middle (developer adoption plus payments compliance, two manageable miracles), and Anduril at the bottom of my heuristic — multi-miracle, which I overrode only because the bipartisan defense-procurement shift was generational. Prototyping.io needs at least three: end-to-end multi-axis CAM automation at the claimed ±0.0001" tolerance (unproven publicly, no demos, no part galleries), a shop network deep enough to compete with Xometry's decade of integration or the capital to vertically integrate like Hadrian's $730M, and customer acquisition against incumbents with existing logos. That's Anduril-shaped risk without Anduril's structural tailwind to override the framework.

On Operator-Scaler Founder Profile, the ladder is Parker Conrad rebuilding Rippling after Zenefits at the top, Henrique Dubugras at Brex in the middle (operator-from-14, no prior scaling exit), and first-time founders with zero scaling exposure at the bottom. Bodepudi and Oberai sit between the floor and Dubugras: real domain credentials (aerospace research, Microsoft AI systems), no prior 10-to-1,000 scaling experience, no named advisors or angels disclosed, and a two-person team covering CAD ingestion, CAM/DFM ML, shop-floor integration, sales, and ops. Domain-credible, scaling-unproven. On Margin Architecture, my ladder runs Abridge per-encounter pricing at the top, Gusto in the middle, and Wish at the bottom for low-margin commoditized marketplace economics. Prototyping.io with [Inferred] per-part transactional pricing against Xometry's mature shop network sits closer to Wish than Gusto — that's the margin profile of a brokerage taking spread, not a software business with pricing power.

My 36-month forecast: Prototyping.io is acquihired by Xometry or Fictiv at sub-$30M, or dead, with under $2M ARR. The mechanism is straightforward — asset-light CNC quoting plus AI DFM is a feature inside Xometry's roadmap, not a company. The two founders are exactly the kind of mechanical+AI pairing Xometry would want to absorb after the waitlist fails to convert into the contract-manufacturing customer base that already lives inside the incumbent's quoting funnel.

The strongest bull case: Hadrian's $1.6B valuation and $260M July 2025 round prove the structural shift in domestic hardware manufacturing is real, the online CNC market is growing 7.4% CAGR to $3.56B by 2035, and Dessaigne explicitly named Prototyping.io in the YC RFS — that's a market-structure signal worth more than any traction snapshot at pre-seed. Anduril is my own Known Exception precedent for overriding multi-miracle risk when the structural shift is generational. The reason that override doesn't apply here: Hadrian's valuation came from owning the factory, not from automating quoting on top of partner shops, and the AI-quoting layer Prototyping.io is building has already been claimed by an incumbent doing $686M in revenue. The flip condition is concrete: a signed enterprise pilot with a named hardware OEM at >$500K ACV that demonstrates the ±0.0001" multi-axis automation actually works at production volume, OR public benchmark results showing their CAM stack outperforms Mastercam/Fusion on multi-axis programming time. Without one of those, this is a feature company in a category where the platform has already been built.
