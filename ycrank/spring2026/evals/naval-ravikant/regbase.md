# Regbase -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Natalie Aresta-Katz ran regulatory research for the ESG practice at Paul, Weiss across dozens of countries and all U.S. states — that is the dossier line that pins Regbase's signature. The highest dimension is specific knowledge: she IS the user, and the manual workflow she is automating is the one she personally executed at a top-five law firm. The lowest dimension is leverage architecture: Regbase sells enterprise subscriptions to Fortune 500 in-house legal and compliance teams through a Paul, Weiss alumni network, which is exactly the labor-and-capital leverage that requires permission. That high/low pairing is the whole story — Regbase is a domain-expert founder building a labor-replacement tool inside a permissioned distribution channel, which is the founder I am tempted by wrapped around the business architecture I avoid.

Run the leverage check first, the way Twitter (2007) ran it. Twitter was media-as-protocol — anyone could post, marginal cost of replication was zero, no gatekeeper between the founder's product and the next user. Regbase is the inverse on the dimension that matters most to me: the regbase.com customer description names "Fortune 500 companies, global consultancies, and leading law firms," which is enterprise sales motion, not permissionless distribution. There is no pricing page on regbase.com as of May 2026, no free tier, no organic developer pull, no published API, no open-source surface, no founder X following — Ethan Shea has @ethshea with unretrievable counts and Aresta-Katz has no public personal account. The product compounds in a database, not in the public commons, and the distribution depends on warm introductions from two ex-Paul, Weiss founders. Capital and labor leverage require permission. Regbase requires permission from general counsels.

Clearview AI is the Known Exception I check against the founder-trio dimension when integrity reads quiet — there I let extreme specific knowledge in facial-recognition training override the partner-risk concern at one-X downside. The opposite pattern shows up cleanly on Regbase: nothing in the dossier suggests integrity contestation. Aresta-Katz (NYU B.S., Columbia MBA), Shea (Microsoft, Citadel, Staff Engineer at Vercel on agentic infra), and Isiah Godoy (Dickinson, ex-Paul, Weiss paralegal, ex-FrameworkESG) read as clean professionals with no contested exits and no chest-beating about ethics. The dimension is neutral, not negative — quiet founders without public writing or organic following are not penalized, but they also do not signal the 400x media-leverage trajectory Dorsey and Williams were already on at twttr.

Specific knowledge is where Regbase actually earns its check size, and the mechanism is the Notion (2013) parallel — not the product analogy, the founder-as-original-user pattern. Ivan Zhao had been rebuilding productivity tools since adolescence; Aresta-Katz built the multi-country, multi-jurisdiction regulatory-tracking process at Paul, Weiss before the company existed. The Fondo START pod (2026) cites the manual baseline she ran. Pair that with Shea's Vercel security-observability and agentic-infra background, and the founder-product-market fit is genuinely intact on the engineering side as well. This is not the ex-FAANG-PM-identified-TAM red flag pattern — this is lived problem ownership, which is the gate that has to clear before any other consideration matters.

The market dimension compounds against Regbase over the next 18 months rather than for it. Legora hit a $5.6B valuation in April 2026 per TechCrunch, FiscalNote runs at $84.8M ARR per the SEC 10-Q (down 22% YoY, which is worse for Regbase, not better — it means the incumbent has activist pressure to ship multilingual sub-national coverage as a growth narrative), and Harvey is funded to extend wherever regulatory text lives. The market is real but bounded — regulatory intelligence is one vertical with adjacent pivots into public affairs, policy alerts, and ESG attestation, but no path I see to the platform-shaped TAM that absorbs two or three founder pivots inside a $500K YC runway. No public TAM data appears in the dossier, which I default to seven out of sixteen, and the competitive density pulls it no higher.

The 36-month forecast: Regbase reaches $3M–$8M ARR through the Paul, Weiss-adjacent enterprise network and gets acquired by Thomson Reuters or FiscalNote at a strategic-premium price that returns 3x–8x on the seed but is not a venture outcome at my asymmetric-payoff math. The mechanism producing that forecast is the same one producing the score: deep founder specific knowledge generates real enterprise traction inside a permissioned distribution channel, which caps the geometry at low double-digit multiples rather than the 400x and 4,000x outcomes that Twitter and Uber paid for the whole portfolio system.

The strongest counter is Perplexity (2022), the Known Exception where I waived the marketplace requirement because specific knowledge plus permissionless API distribution plus pre-consensus AI timing collectively reset the math. Could Regbase be Perplexity-shaped? Aravind Srinivas had the Berkeley retrieval PhD; Aresta-Katz has the lived workflow — close enough on criterion three. The timing — agentic multilingual ingestion crossed a usability threshold in 2024–2025 — is real. The pattern breaks at distribution: Perplexity sells through a free web product that compounds organically through search-replacement behavior, while Regbase sells through general counsel approvals at Fortune 500s. The Perplexity override required permissionless distribution to substitute for the missing network — Regbase has neither. The verdict flips to invest if Regbase publishes an open multilingual regulatory corpus or a free citation-lookup tool that gets organic adoption from solo practitioners and compliance bloggers within twelve months — that would re-architect the leverage from permissioned to permissionless and reset the math.
