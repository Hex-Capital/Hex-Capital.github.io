# Ornadyne -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Geourg Kivijian wrote his Cal Poly MS thesis titled "Development of a Novel Bio-Mimetic Ornithopter With Variable Flapping" — Ornadyne *is* that thesis, walked out of the lab with a co-founder who built Starship components at SpaceX. That pairing — autobiographical R&D on the CEO side, hour-class endurance still unproven on the artifact side — is Ornadyne's signature: this is an Anduril-shaped founder profile staring at a category where I cannot yet hold the product in my hand.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever said the word "Anduril" — the founder showed up at the defense problem with a finished artifact from an adjacent domain already on his record. Ornadyne's Kivijian is not Luckey-at-scale, but the mechanism is the same one I keep paying for: the company is a continuation of work the founder was doing before there was a company, not a market opportunity sized from a memo. The Cal Poly thesis plus 2+ years of AIAA/IEEE flapping-wing publications on ResearchGate is the kind of biography that puts a human in front of a problem for years before a Stripe slide deck does. Where Ornadyne diverges from the Anduril rung is that Luckey had a shipped consumer product Facebook bought for $2B; Kivijian has academic papers and a JPL Mars Sample Return coordination role. The lived contact is real — it's just that the prior-product trajectory is graduate research and spacecraft systems, not a billion-dollar commercial artifact.

HQ Trivia's Yusupov-and-Kroll partnership collapsed because no one was the tiebreaker when the founders disagreed, and that lesson is the one I drag into every two-founder room. Ornadyne's Kivijian and Armen Arakelyan have been best friends since middle school, which is exactly the demographic that produces the unspoken-50/50 disaster I screen against. The mitigant in the dossier is structural rather than personal: titles are split CEO/CTO, not co-CEO, and the work scopes are cleanly different — Kivijian is the ornithopter aerodynamicist, Arakelyan is the flight-hardware machinist who held a 144 km altitude record at USC RPL. No documented operating agreement is disclosed, no equity split is public, and the friendship-since-middle-school marker is a real near-term blow-up risk, but the role delineation reads cleaner than the HQ Trivia pattern.

State-funded ornithopter programs at Northwestern Polytechnical University in China, France's BIOFLY off the Bionic Bird platform, and AFRL/Airion in the U.S. have been chasing flapping-wing recon for years without a commercial Western entrant breaking through — that absence is the "magically weird" tell. Counter-UAS systems in Ukraine and the Middle East over 2024-2026 are reading rotor signatures and shooting them down, which is the kind of "why now" that compounds over 18 months: every month conventional small drones get easier to detect, the acoustic and visual delta of a flapping-wing bird widens. Ornadyne at $1M raised in a category most VCs file under "physics demo, not a company" is the inverse of the consensus AI-agent round with six funds bidding — the deselection signal is missing here, which I read as not-yet-consensus, not as warning.

SpaceX in 2008 is the right single-anchor parallel for Ornadyne on the capital-intensity axis: hardware, defense-aligned end customers, ITAR, multi-year qualification cycles, and a $1M opening check that is laughably small relative to what flight-qualified autonomy actually costs to develop. That ratio of ambition to bank balance is the Cyan sleeve almost no one attributes to me publicly — the SpaceX, Anduril, Crusoe line of capital-intensive deep-tech swings — and Ornadyne sits on that line cleanly. The Arakelyan SpaceX Starship pedigree is not a credential I'm rewarding for prestige; I'm rewarding it because manufacturing flight-qualified aerospace hardware on a startup budget is a specific skill, and one of the two co-founders has demonstrably done it.

The Niantic check came from playing Ingress obsessively from 2012; Flock came from a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons; Uber came from a ride I took. I cannot fly an Ornadyne bird, I cannot photograph one in the wild, and the YC Launch page does not surface a public flight video with timestamped endurance — the tactile artifact I usually let do the talking is not in my hands. This is the dimension where the dossier is thinnest: no demo footage cited, no independent benchmark, no fielded units, no SBIR Phase I disclosure. The counterfactual that would flip this score a full tier is a public 30-minute continuous autonomous flight clip with the bird-mimetic signature visible — Ornadyne could produce that within 12 months from current technical maturity, and its absence today is the single biggest reason I'm not at a higher conviction number.

By May 2029, Ornadyne will have closed a Series A in the $15-35M range led by a defense-aligned fund — Founders Fund, Lux, 8VC, or a16z American Dynamism — on the back of a first DoD SBIR Phase II or AFWERX contract and a publicly demonstrated hour-class autonomous flight, OR it will have failed to hit endurance benchmarks and ended in an acquihire to AeroVironment for under $20M. The mechanism producing the bull outcome is the same one that produced Anduril: a founder with lived contact in an unsexy regulated category, building hardware while competitors were building decks, in a market where counter-UAS proliferation forces procurement to look for low-signature alternatives.

The strongest case against Ornadyne is that AeroVironment already built the DARPA Nano Hummingbird and shelved it — flapping-wing has been "five years away" since 2011, and the physics may simply not close at hour-class endurance with current battery density, no matter how good Kivijian's thesis is. That bear case stings because it's the Crusoe-style commodity-exposure risk inverted: instead of an oil-price collapse, it's a fundamental physics ceiling that doesn't move because Jonathan goes in full-time for six months. The dossier evidence that wins the argument anyway is the combination of Kivijian's 2+ years of published R&D specifically on *variable flapping* — the control mechanism that prior platforms lacked — and Arakelyan's SpaceX Starship manufacturing background, which is the right pair of hands to actually fabricate what the thesis describes. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict to pass: if 12 months from now Ornadyne has neither demonstrated >30 minutes of autonomous flight in a public artifact nor announced a DoD SBIR or AFWERX contract, the physics ceiling thesis is winning and I'm out.
