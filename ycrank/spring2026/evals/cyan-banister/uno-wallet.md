# Uno Wallet -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Uno Wallet is a tactile consumer artifact dropped into a category so crowded it stopped being weird three rounds of funding ago — Mordi Shadpour's tap-to-pay double-click is the kind of thing I could photograph in my hand, except Kudos already raised $17.2M and MaxRewards already has 900K members doing essentially the same job. The signature here is high-touch product, low-weirdness market. That is the inverse of every check I've written that actually returned.

Anduril is the high rung on founder origin: Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus before he ever pitched defense, which is what made the second act underwriteable. Uno Wallet's origin story, by contrast, is two Harvard students bonding over "pointsmaxxing credit cards" at a Harvard entrepreneurship event in 2023. Mordi Shadpour finished his HBS MBA in 2025; Saket Joshi did the Harvard MS Data Science route after IIT Tirupati and xAI. I am not saying credentials disqualify anyone — DeepMind cleared the bar as a polymath exception — but neither of these founders lived the problem before the problem became their pitch. Pointsmaxxing is a hobby, not the homelessness-to-HandUp autobiographical hook. The Uno Wallet origin is exactly the "ex-FAANG/IIT, MBA, identified market opportunity" Pass Example template I run away from.

HQ Trivia is the low rung on category weirdness — not because trivia was a bad bet, but because what made HQ a check was that I could *feel* the viral pull in my hand before any metric existed; the weirdness was the live-cash-prize variety hour, not the genre. Uno Wallet has no equivalent. The category is "AI picks the best card," and the dossier lists Kudos, MaxRewards, CardPointers, Uthrive, and Pogo all doing some flavor of it; QED led Kudos's Series A in May 2024. "Find them before it becomes consensus" — consensus arrived here in 2021. There is no aesthetic, moral, or capital-intensity reason credible VCs would have to flinch at Uno Wallet. That absence of flinch is itself the deselection signal.

Mordi Shadpour is labeled "CEO, Co-Founder" in the dossier, which clears the bar on tiebreaker authority better than the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll structure. The mechanism that compounds over 18 months when you have a clean CEO designation is that pivots happen fast and one voice owns the customer — Uno Wallet will need that, because the moment Apple changes an NFC policy or Chase tightens affiliate terms, somebody on a 2-person team has to ship the new product in a weekend. No equity-split or vesting disclosure in the dossier, so I am taking the title at face value and not more.

What would have to be true to flip Uno Wallet's frontier/regulated score from a 3 to a 9? The product would need to credibly become *payments rails* rather than a recommender on top of someone else's rails — a stored-value account, a CFPB-supervised credit product, an issuer relationship Uno itself underwrites. Nothing in the dossier hints at that. Tap-to-pay on iOS routing to existing cards via NFC is software garnish on Apple Wallet and the card networks; Saket Joshi's xAI background is ML-on-categorization, not core-banking. Affirm is my single anchor here, and Affirm cleared this dimension because Levchin built the underwriting model and bore the credit risk. Uno Wallet bears affiliate risk and category-detection risk, which is a different game.

On physical-world verifiability, Niantic is the parallel — I bought Niantic because I was already playing Ingress in 2012, walking around looking at portals. Uno Wallet *would* pass that test if I could install it. The 20,456-person waitlist and Q2 2026 launch reference tell me it is gated. I cannot tap it at a deli counter today, cannot feel whether the double-click-to-pay UX actually beats just opening Apple Wallet by muscle memory. Tactility you cannot touch is a promise, not an artifact, and promises get scored below the midpoint.

My 36-month forecast on Uno Wallet: dead or acqui-hired into Kudos or a card issuer's product team for under $15M, with the more likely outcome being a quiet wind-down once the affiliate economics meet the CAC of competing against MaxRewards' 900K-member head start. The mechanism is straightforward — in a category where five funded competitors execute the same recommendation logic and the moat is "personal spending data accumulated over time," whoever has users today compounds; Uno Wallet has a waitlist.

The strongest case against my pass is the HQ Trivia exception: tactile consumer virality can override category-crowding and pedigree concerns if the product itself produces an undeniable felt experience. The bull says Apple's NFC opening plus an on-device LLM merchant categorizer is a genuinely new substrate, and the double-click-to-pay gesture could create the same in-hand "oh" moment that made HQ Trivia worth the governance risk. That stings because I have written the override before. It does not win here, though, because the HQ override required me to play the product and feel the pull — Uno Wallet is private beta and I cannot. Kudos at $17.2M and 200K members is not a sleeping incumbent; it is a moving target with QED's distribution muscle behind it. One verifiable condition flips this: if Mordi Shadpour ships to the App Store, I install Uno Wallet, tap it at a Blue Bottle, and the card-pick saves me $3 I would have left on the table — that is the Ingress test passing, and I will reconsider. Until then, it's a rebuttal, not a yes.
