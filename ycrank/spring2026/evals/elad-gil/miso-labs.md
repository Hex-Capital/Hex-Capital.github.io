# Miso Labs -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Miso Labs is a two-person Stanford team shipping an 8B open-weight TTS foundation model into a voice category where Cartesia's Sonic Turbo already clocks sub-40ms TTFA against Miso One's headline 110ms -- the analytical signature is *capable technical shipper, anti-index distribution position*, which is the pairing that historically rewards an acqui-hire, not an angel check. Aoden Teo has clearly built a real artifact (RVQ 32×2048, MLX community port on Hugging Face, MarkTechPost coverage Jun 4 2026), but Miso Labs sits in the exact spot my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) flags as "winners declared" -- ElevenLabs at $11B post-Feb-2026 and Cartesia at ~$100M Oct 2025 are not pre-PMF incumbents you flank, they are the price of admission Miso Labs has not yet matched.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the load-bearing high rung on the *founder-ships* dimension, and Aoden Teo's launch arc -- 8B parameters trained, weights pushed to Hugging Face, mlx-community/MisoTTS-8bit already forked, launch tweet at 312K views on Jun 3 2026 -- is the genuine shipping rhythm I look for. The mechanism that made Perplexity work for me was Aravind showing up a week later with the thing we'd discussed *built*; Teo has demonstrably built. Where Miso Labs diverges from the Perplexity template is co-founder composition: Cassidy Dalva's profile (Stanford Econ, Stanford Daily News Managing Editor, King Center R-based health research) is journalism-and-policy, not voice ML, and the dossier surfaces no shipping artifact from her side -- so the shipping rhythm is a single-founder phenomenon at Miso Labs, which is a thinner version of the pattern than Perplexity's.

**Lyft** Series C is the low rung on the *index-position* dimension and the relevant failure mechanism is structural: when you're #2 in a market with a named #1 whose unit economics dominate, you capture a fraction of the winner's value regardless of how well you execute. That pattern absolutely appears in Miso Labs's dossier -- ElevenLabs ships v3 with 70+ languages and >51% enterprise revenue mix, Cartesia's Sonic-3 hits ~90ms and Sonic Turbo sub-40ms TTFA, Fish Audio ranks #1 on TTS-Arena blind tests, and Hume AI owns the "emotive" positioning that Miso Labs claims as its differentiator. Miso Labs has to *beat* these on features (the dossier's own latency comparison chart concedes 110ms while Cartesia already ships faster), which is the inverse of the Stripe-index template where you sit in the workflow path that all entrants touch. Open-weighting an 8B model into this fight collapses the only moat -- mlx-community has already redistributed the weights.

On *market re-opening*: the voice category did get a specific 2025-26 catalyst -- sub-200ms latency thresholds crossed, ElevenLabs Series D Feb 2026 at $11B, Cartesia round Oct 2025 -- so I credit Miso Labs partially here. But the mechanism that makes a catalyst *load-bearing* for me (Stripe 2010, Anduril 2022) is that the window is open *for the first entrant with the right wedge*. By the time Miso Labs ships in Jun 2026, the window has produced four well-funded incumbents and a Stanford spinout (Cartesia) that already won the latency race on architecture (SSMs). This is a catalyst Miso Labs is *late to*, not one Miso Labs is *first through*.

On *revenue velocity*: counterfactual -- what would have to be true for Miso Labs's score here to flip a tier? The dossier would need to show hosted-API revenue compounding MoM, or a named enterprise logo, or any usage data on the open-weight tier beyond 66 GitHub stars and disabled Hugging Face download tracking. None of that exists. API access is "coming soon" per the YC Launch page Jun 3 2026, which in the voice category in mid-2026 is the answer that makes the Tim Ferriss Show #863 phrase apply directly: "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." 312K tweet views is reach, not revenue.

On *single-miracle architecture*: **Color Genomics** (2015) is the single-anchor parallel -- I co-founded it precisely because the miracle was isolated to workflow on top of commodity sequencing, not new chemistry. Miso Labs *almost* fits that template -- the miracle is "emotive expressiveness at low latency," workflow on top of TTS rather than a new vocoder physics bet. But the dossier reveals the miracle is already contested (Cartesia's SSM architecture beats Miso Labs's RVQ on the headline metric), and a second miracle has been smuggled in: "convert open-weight downloads into hosted-API revenue against incumbents with 100x the capital." That's not Color's clean single-miracle structure -- that's two miracles in a trench coat.

Forecast, 36 months: Miso Labs is acqui-hired by ElevenLabs, Cartesia, or a hyperscaler at sub-$75M for Aoden Teo and the RVQ research, OR pivots from "foundation model for voice" into a specific voice-agent vertical (customer support voice, dubbing) before reaching $5M ARR. The mechanism producing this is the *anti-index* placement: when the category leader beats you on the differentiating metric and open-weights are reproducible, the team's value compounds and the company's value doesn't.

The strongest bull case is the **Mistral** (2023) parallel -- open-weight voice could ride developer adoption the way Llama-shipped Mistral did, and my Known Exception for Mistral was that hyperscaler-coupled outcomes are features-not-bugs. The case stings because Aoden Teo *is* technically shipping at a rate that resembles the early Mistral cadence. The dossier evidence that wins against this is asymmetric: Mistral's founding team had shipped Llama at Meta with a €105M seed; Miso Labs is two people, one non-ML, no disclosed advisors, no follow-on capital signaled, and the open-weight wedge is undermined by the open-weight competitor (Fish Audio) already ranking #1 on TTS-Arena. The verifiable condition that would flip me to Invest is a named enterprise voice-agent platform (Decagon, Sierra, Vapi) committing to Miso One as default TTS in the next 90 days at disclosed volume -- absent that, this is a strong technical demo that does not yet meet the "consensus pays when the curve is real" bar from Tim Ferriss Show #863.
