# StableBrowse -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

StableBrowse is an API layer for AI agents browsing the web -- the leverage architecture reads code-native on paper, but the dimensional signature is a large-market infrastructure bet shipped by competent generalist engineers into a category where Browserbase has already raised $67.5M and crossed $3M revenue. Four co-founders -- Sarthak Awasthi, Jay Mehta, Deepit Shah, Somansh Shah -- with no named CEO, building a "semantic execution graph" above headless browsers, against three priced incumbents and an open-source frame called Browser Use. That is the shape: real category, real product idea, generic founders relative to the depth the category will demand.

The leverage check runs first. **Stack Overflow** (2008) is the HIGH rung -- developers pulled the product through organic traffic, and code distributed code. StableBrowse is structurally in the same family: an API consumed by developers building agents, no SDR motion implied, no App Store gatekeeper. That is what I want to see. Where StableBrowse diverges from Stack Overflow is the target wedge -- the dossier names "insurance quote binding, healthcare prior authorization, mortgage processing" and claims "HIPAA compliant" with "SOC 2 Type I and Type II certifications in progress." The moment StableBrowse leans on regulated verticals it inherits permissioned distribution -- procurement, BAAs, security reviews -- which is structurally the **Anchorage Digital** override pattern, except StableBrowse has neither the institutional moat nor a co-founder role to underwrite the flip from permissionless to permission-as-moat. The leverage architecture is real on the developer side and partly self-undermined by the GTM framing.

**Perplexity** (~2022) is the lowest-rung warning on dimension two. Perplexity is single-sided -- each user's queries do not compound the prior user's experience -- and I made an exception there because Aravind's specific knowledge and the AI-cycle timing both cleared at top quartile. Does StableBrowse trigger that exception? The pitch is that the per-site knowledge graphs become a cross-customer asset: every agent that figures out how Geico's quote flow works updates the graph for the next agent. If that is real, it is closer to **OpenSea** (2018) data-aggregation dynamics than to Perplexity's single-sided shape. The dossier flags "no usage or customer data published to validate network effects" -- it is the right architecture asserted without the evidence that it is actually compounding. I credit StableBrowse for naming a network mechanic at all; most browser-infra peers do not.

Specific knowledge is where StableBrowse softens. Jay Mehta has "8+ years" in distributed systems and Deepit Shah brings UIUC CS plus Amazon plus a co-authored numerical-methods paper -- competent, not non-trainable. The Stack Overflow founders had *built developer tools for developers* before; Amjad Masad had been hacking on Repl.it since 2011. The dossier says StableBrowse was started after the founders' "frustrations themselves while building browser agents" -- one sentence, no shipped artifacts named, no open-source repo, no GitHub presence found for any of the four. Eighteen months from now this dimension either compounds because the team ships hard and the graph turns real, or it does not, and the founders get out-iterated by Browserbase's existing 20K-developer pull. The absence of prior browser-automation specific knowledge is the load-bearing risk.

The market is large enough to absorb pivots -- Browserbase at $300M post and TinyFish at a $47M Series A are evidence the category is being underwritten at scale, and the adjacent pivot vectors are obvious (pure infra, vertical agents, RPA replacement, scraping-as-a-service). What would have to be true for StableBrowse to flip from neutral to invest on dimension four is a counterfactual: that the regulated-vertical wedge (HIPAA healthcare, mortgage) closes one paying enterprise logo with disclosed contract value before the SOC 2 Type II finishes. Until then, StableBrowse is fishing in the same pond as four better-capitalized boats.

The trio reads quiet. No public X presence for any of the four StableBrowse founders, no GitHub, follower counts "not retrievable." That is silence, not negative signal -- the default holds. What does register as a soft flag is the four-co-founder structure with no named CEO surfaced in the dossier; "picking a co-founder is your most important decision" (Venture Hacks), and four-way equity without explicit authority is the decision still pending. StableBrowse does not surface any integrity contestation, so the floor stays at default; the absence of a 10-year-partner read pulls toward the lower end of the band.

Thirty-six month forecast: StableBrowse is acquihired by Browserbase or Kernel for sub-$30M, or runs out of runway and shutters quietly, with ARR never crossing $2M. The mechanism is straightforward -- the semantic-graph layer is a *feature* in a category where the *distribution* lives with whoever holds the developer relationship, and Browserbase already holds 20K developers. The Naval framework that drove **Replit** (2023) at $1.16B post says: when the leverage architecture is right *and* the founder is the leverage-user, the check writes itself. StableBrowse passes the architecture half and not the founder half.

The strongest opposing case is the cross-customer execution graph. If StableBrowse actually accumulates verified workflows for the 200 highest-value enterprise sites -- carrier portals, EHRs, lender systems -- the graph becomes the **Notion** (2013) compounding pattern in a different vocabulary, and the regulated verticals that look like a leverage-drag in my framing become the moat that keeps Browserbase out because Browserbase will not pursue HIPAA. That is the bull case worth respecting. What dismantles it from the dossier is that the "98% success rate" and "70-80% fewer tokens" are unaudited self-reports with no third-party benchmark and no named customer, and SOC 2 Type II is "in progress" -- meaning the regulated wedge that justifies the graph thesis is not actually open yet. The verifiable flip condition: one named healthcare or insurance enterprise logo on a paid contract with disclosed value above $250K ARR before SOC 2 Type II completes, plus evidence that a graph trained on customer A measurably accelerated customer B's onboarding. Until that lands, StableBrowse is a neutral pre-seed bet in a category I would rather access through the developer-channel leader.
