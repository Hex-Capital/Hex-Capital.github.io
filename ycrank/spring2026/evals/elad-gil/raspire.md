# RASPIRE -- Elad Gil Evaluation

RASPIRE is a single-miracle execution play -- clean, no science risk, the primitives are commodity -- dropped into a category where Appdome ($39.6M raised) is already shouting "no-code mobile RASP" from the same positioning slide that the RASPIRE YC page uses. That pairing is the analytical signature: high marks for *what it isn't* (it isn't a fusion startup, isn't a battery chemistry bet, isn't waiting on a regulatory unlock), and low marks for *where it has to win* (against a better-capitalized incumbent in a G2 category with 25+ vendors). Kareem Selim and Hassan Mostafa have built real production deployments -- the 20M+ end-user figure isn't fake -- but RASPIRE is being asked to be a #2 in an occupied niche, and that's the Lyft-Series-C pattern I keep on a sticky note above my desk.

The **Anduril** (2022) template is what RASPIRE wants to be on the market-catalyst axis: a category peers had written off, with a specific 24-month catalyst (Ukraine, in Anduril's case) that re-opened the procurement window. The mechanism that made Anduril load-bearing was that the catalyst was *named, recent, and forced incumbent failure into the open*. RASPIRE's dossier gestures at two catalysts -- Saudi Vision 2030 digital-transformation procurement and the "vibe-coding" surge of AI-generated mobile binaries reaching production without hardening -- but the first is a regional spend cycle that benefits every MENA security vendor, not a category re-opening, and the second is inferred by the founders rather than evidenced by a named customer saying "we bought RASPIRE because our Cursor-generated banking app shipped without obfuscation." RASP as a category has been growing at ~28% CAGR since 2019; that's a steady-state market with a tailwind, which is exactly the configuration *First Round Review* warned about when I said great teams die in markets like this.

Lyft Series C is the relevant low-rung memory for RASPIRE's index-position problem: I passed because rideshare's winner-take-all structure meant #2 captured a fraction of #1's economics, and the dossier showed no path to share inversion. The failure mechanism there was *fighting for share against a named incumbent on the same axis the incumbent already owned*. Trace that into RASPIRE's dossier: Appdome's marketing page explicitly leads with "no-code mobile RASP and app shielding" with a 400+ defense library, which is the precise wedge RASPIRE is claiming. Zimperium ($60.1M raised) sells the same buyer (regulated banks) with a broader MAPS suite. Guardsquare's only weakness -- build-system integration -- is real, but it's a wedge against Guardsquare, not against Appdome. RASPIRE isn't sitting in the workflow path of mobile security the way **Stripe** (2010) sat in the workflow path of internet payments; RASPIRE is showing up at a knife fight that Appdome already won the no-code framing in.

On revenue velocity, the mechanism that matters compounds over 18 months and RASPIRE's dossier doesn't show it starting. "Apps protecting 20M+ end users" is a deployment-count metric, not a buying-behavior metric -- it tells me a handful of MENA banks shipped RASPIRE into production, which is real but not the **Harvey** (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" curve I'm trained to recognize as "just working." No ARR disclosed, no MoM growth, no named US logo, hiring flag set to False on the YC profile. Silence on growth in the AI cycle is itself a signal because real growth is trivially easy to disclose -- and RASPIRE positions itself as AI-driven.

Counterfactually on founder shipping rhythm, RASPIRE's score flips a tier only if Selim or Mostafa surfaces a public artifact cadence -- a GitHub with active commits, a demo URL that changes between investor meetings, a CyberTalents talk with a shipped-tool walkthrough. The dossier names "years building and securing apps for banks and fintech companies" and a Cyshield tenure for Mostafa, which is legitimate domain time, but neither founder has a discoverable GitHub and neither has a Twitter tied to RASPIRE. The **Perplexity** (2022) signal I cared about was Aravind pinging me on LinkedIn and showing up a week later with the thing built -- that's an artifact-per-conversation rhythm. RASPIRE's founders have a *career* of shipping; what I don't have is evidence of *cadence*, and at pre-seed cadence is the only legible signal.

The one place RASPIRE scores well is single-miracle architecture, and the parallel is **Color Genomics** (2015): workflow infrastructure on top of a hard-tech layer that someone else solved. RASPIRE doesn't need new cryptography, doesn't need a new compiler, doesn't need regulatory approval -- the binary instrumentation primitives are known, and the "miracle" is GTM execution plus AI-driven autonomous response on accumulated telemetry. That's a clean single-miracle structure and it's why RASPIRE isn't a Strong Pass. But Color was *operated* by me as an infrastructure layer in a category with no Appdome equivalent; RASPIRE has to win share, and a single-miracle architecture without a market re-opening just means the company will execute cleanly into a fair fight.

My 36-month forecast: RASPIRE reaches $2-5M ARR concentrated in MENA banking/fintech via the Sadq channel and Sanabil's regional network, fails to land a top-20 US bank or top-5 US fintech, and exits via acqui-hire into a security platform (Palo Alto, Cloudflare, or Appdome itself rolling up the no-code competitor) at a price that returns 1-3x to pre-YC backers and underwater for any seed buyer paying YC consensus markups. The mechanism is straightforward: commoditized primitives + better-capitalized incumbent on the same positioning + regional revenue concentration + no disclosed velocity = a competent regional security vendor, not an index on mobile security.

The strongest bear-case-for-my-pass-being-wrong runs as follows: Appdome's $39.6M-raised business model depends on professional-services revenue and channel partners, which creates genuine channel conflict with a true zero-integration product, and the "vibe-coding" surge -- if AI-generated mobile apps actually flood production unhardened over the next 18 months -- creates a mid-market RASP segment Appdome's enterprise sales motion can't reach efficiently. RASPIRE's 20M+ end-user footprint at pre-seed is unusual; most YC P26 security companies are still pitching slides. Selim being in SF Bay Area and Mostafa anchoring the Cairo engineering muscle is the cost-structure that lets RASPIRE undercut Appdome on price and out-ship on cadence. That's a real bull case and it stings -- but the dossier doesn't show RASPIRE *winning* that fight; it shows RASPIRE *entering* it. I'd flip to Invest on one verifiable condition: a named top-20 US bank or top-5 US fintech logo signed within 6 months of YC Demo Day, OR disclosed ARR above $3M growing >20% MoM. Until then, RASPIRE is the kind of clean-execution play I respect and pass on -- a worse version of the Appdome bet, not a contrarian one.
