# RealPact -- Elad Gil Evaluation

RealPact is a shipping-rhythm undergrad team -- Ranvir Deshmukh and Erik Peterson, who put CourseMe in front of 90% of Dartmouth students before selling it -- pointed at a vertical workflow category where Dotloop already owns roughly half the US transaction-management market. That is RealPact's signature: top-quartile founder execution evidence colliding with a bottom-quartile distribution position. It is the inverse of the **Decagon** (2023) setup I funded -- there the market was wide-open and the founders had to prove they could ship; here RealPact has proved it can ship, but the market is occupied.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the ladder rung I keep returning to on founder execution, because Aravind would ship a new artifact between every conversation -- that is the only legible signal at pre-seed and it is the one that made me write the check despite a category (search) that was supposedly closed. RealPact's CourseMe history loads the analogy honestly: not a credential, an artifact -- 4,000+ MAU, acquired, plus the Magnuson $20K and four brokerage logos including Four Seasons Sotheby's signed before YC demo day. Where RealPact diverges from the Perplexity rung is that Deshmukh and Peterson shipped CourseMe inside a captive Dartmouth user base where distribution was free; the New Hampshire brokerage beachhead currently looks like the same trick (Dartmouth/Hanover geography, per *The Dartmouth*, May 2026), which means I haven't yet seen RealPact ship into a market where it had to *win* distribution rather than inherit it. That is the gap between a 14/20 and an 18/20 on this axis.

**Lyft** Series C is my reference failure on distribution position, and the mechanism was that #2 in a network-effect/incumbent-dominated workflow market captures a fraction of #1's economics regardless of product quality (20VC interview). RealPact's dossier reproduces the pattern with uncomfortable fidelity: Dotloop is a Zillow Group subsidiary controlling ~50% of US transaction management with MLS-level distribution, SkySlope serves 400,000+ professionals on the compliance side, and RealPact's differentiation -- upstream public-records auto-fill plus a voice interface -- is, per the dossier itself, "buildable by Dotloop, SkySlope, Brokermint, DocuSign... given general LLM availability." RealPact is not in the workflow path that every brokerage entrant *must* touch the way **Stripe** (2010) is on e-commerce; RealPact is fighting Dotloop for the same seat. That's a feature-velocity race against an incumbent with distribution -- the exact race I tell founders not to enter.

On market re-opening, the dossier's why-now is the [Inferred] claim that voice LLMs and CV-OCR crossed a reliability threshold in 2024-2025. That is true and it is also true of every Summer 2026 YC AI company, which means it is not a RealPact-specific catalyst the way Ukraine was an **Anduril** (2022) catalyst or the Stripe-API substrate was a **Stripe** (2010) catalyst. There is no regulatory shift in real estate brokerage workflow named in the dossier, no NAR settlement consequence cited, no MLS-data-access change -- the catalyst is generic model capability, which compounds *for the incumbent's roadmap as much as RealPact's*. Over 18 months this dimension fails by Dotloop shipping the same agent stack on top of its existing 50% distribution.

On revenue velocity, the counterfactual is what would have to be true to flip RealPact from Pass to Invest: I would need to see >20% MoM ARR growth against the four named brokerages already converting to paid seat expansion, plus two of the four expanding to multi-office deployments inside 90 days. The dossier discloses no pricing page, no MRR, no contract value -- four logos including Sotheby's is meaningful as buying behavior signal (better than pilot tourism) but in the AI cycle, silence on the curve is itself a signal because real growth is easy to disclose. RealPact is at the floor of this dimension's defensible range, not the ceiling.

On single-miracle architecture, RealPact resembles **Color Genomics** (2015) structurally -- workflow on top of a hard substrate (public records / state-specific contract law instead of sequencing chemistry) rather than betting on the substrate itself. The miracle is "LLMs accurately parse non-standardized county deed/tax records and auto-fill legally-binding contracts." That's *one* technical miracle, but RealPact then carries a non-trivial legal-liability tail (parsing errors create brokerage exposure, per the dossier's risk section) and a per-state re-platform cost (each new jurisdiction requires re-mapping county registries and state contract forms). Not multi-miracle, but not as clean as **Decagon's** (2023) "LLM accuracy at task X and the rest is GTM."

My 36-month forecast: RealPact reaches $2-5M ARR across 5-15 brokerages concentrated in New England, then gets acquired by Dotloop, SkySlope, or a title/escrow incumbent for $20-50M as a feature-and-team tuck-in -- the Zillow-bought-Dotloop pattern (Aug 2015) replayed one cycle down. The mechanism is that RealPact's shipping rhythm produces a credible AI-native product faster than incumbents, but incumbents close the gap with distribution before RealPact closes the gap with distribution, and the cleanest exit becomes selling the team into the distribution they couldn't build.

The bull case that would sting: Zillow-owned Dotloop has been stagnant since 2015 acquisition, brokerages openly hate it, and an AI-native rebuild with voice plus auto-fill from public records is not a "feature" -- it is a category re-architecture that incumbents structurally cannot ship without cannibalizing per-seat ARPU. Deshmukh and Peterson already converted Sotheby's at pre-seed without YC distribution, which is real buying behavior, and CourseMe-at-90%-of-Dartmouth says they execute saturation playbooks. That case is real, but the dossier dismantles it: SignPact-rename ambiguity, no disclosed CEO authority structure between three founders (Veer B Patel's role unconfirmed), zero revenue visibility, NH-only footprint, and the explicit acknowledgment that the tech is buildable by Dotloop. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: RealPact crosses three states and 50+ paying brokerages with >$3M ARR and >20% MoM growth inside 12 months -- at that point the Dotloop-incumbent thesis breaks and I lean in at the next round.
