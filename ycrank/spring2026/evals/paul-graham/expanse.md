# Expanse -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Ismaeel Bashir built the multimodal HPC resource predictor as research at EPCC before Expanse existed as a company, which means Expanse is a research-spinout where the organic-idea axis is genuinely strong but the team was assembled around an artifact rather than around years of shared rooms. The dimensional signature -- highest on founder-problem fit, lowest on cofounder configuration -- tells me what Expanse is: not a Stripe-shaped hacker pair who grew up hacking commerce together, but a postdoc-shaped company where one person had the wedge and three more were recruited to ship it. That is a real kind of company. It is just not the kind that historically returns the fund.

On the organic-pain axis, Expanse sits between Dropbox and Reddit on my ladder, closer to Dropbox. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and then built **Dropbox** (2007); the load-bearing mechanism is that the founder was the first user of his own product before any market existed for it. Bashir was inside EPCC watching researchers fail to predict job resource needs, and Yafet Melake at G-Research was running the same workloads at "the largest quant funds" the YC blurb names. Where Expanse diverges from the Houston pattern is that the lived pain belongs to two of four founders rather than the whole team, and there is no equivalent of the Hacker News demo video -- the artifact exists but it lived inside an academic paper, not on the open internet where strangers stumbled into it. Still, this is the strongest dimension in the dossier and the one that would get Expanse into the interview room.

The MBA AI-wrapper team I'd pass on -- glossy deck, $50B TAM, hire-engineers-post-funding -- fails because no founder has personally suffered the problem and there is no GitHub trail. The failure mechanism is that absence of public hacker tracks correlates with absence of private hacker depth. Expanse only partially escapes this. Bashir's EPCC predictor "beat every published baseline" per the YC blurb, which is a real hacker signal -- but the dossier reports no GitHub account confirmed for Bashir, no Twitter, no Hacker News footprint, and the same blank for Bieniek and Melake. Hackers leave tracks. The EPCC research is the track Expanse has; that is one track, attributable to one founder, and it is the dimension I weight down hardest.

On animal resourcefulness, the mechanism that matters over the next eighteen months is whether Expanse keeps generating pre-funding-style stunts after the YC check arrives -- the free OS Wastage Scanner at wastage.expanse.sh is a small one, lead-gen disguised as a gift, which is exactly the right instinct. It is not a cereal box. It is also not nothing. The 32-GPU example at 37% utilization on the landing page reads like a real customer rather than a hypothetical, but the dossier does not name the counterparty, which is the difference between a stunt I can verify and a screenshot.

On schlep, the counterfactual that would push Expanse from "real schlep" into "Coinbase-grade schlep" is the addition of on-prem deployment into classified or export-controlled HPC environments -- DOE labs, MoD, the parts of national supercomputing that no SaaS founder will touch. The dossier names "national supercomputing centres" generically; if one of those is Oak Ridge or Lawrence Livermore under a real contract, this dimension flips a tier. As written, Expanse has the right shape of schlep (slow procurement, on-prem, Slurm integration) without the regulatory exposure that made **Coinbase** (2012) uncopyable.

On cofounder configuration, the single-anchor parallel is the **Airbnb** (2009) trio, where Chesky, Gebbia, and Blecharczyk had lived and worked together for years before the cereal boxes. Expanse has four founders and an "Edinburgh nexus" that the dossier itself flags as "[Inferred]" and "not confirmed for all four." Four founders with an inferred prior relationship is the configuration that breaks most often -- not because four is too many, but because the relationships were forged around the cap table rather than before it.

Expanse over 36 months: acquired by NVIDIA as a Run:ai feature-bolt-on in the $40-120M range, or alternatively reaches roughly $2-5M ARR with five to ten national-lab and quant-fund logos and plateaus there on procurement velocity. The mechanism is that Bashir's predictor is real and valuable, the buyer set is narrow and slow, and NVIDIA already owns the channel via Run:ai -- which makes Expanse a feature inside someone else's platform rather than a platform itself. That is consistent with the signature: organic founder-pain produces a real artifact; weak cofounder genealogy and the absence of a hacker-community presence produces a team that ships the artifact but does not compound it into a category.

The strongest case against this forecast is Alexandr Wang and **Scale AI** -- another research-adjacent founder who attacked an unpleasant infrastructure problem that the rest of the batch was unconsciously routing around, and who cited "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) as the trigger. If Bashir reads as an animal in the room -- if the EPCC paper turns out to be the first of many shipped artifacts rather than the last -- then Expanse is in that lineage and I am underpricing it. The dossier evidence that wins against the bull case is the name collision: "Expanse" is already SDSC's supercomputer at UC San Diego, the exact buyer Expanse is selling to. Founders who pass the animal test do not pick a brand that collides with their primary customer's flagship system; that is a tell about how much friction they noticed before shipping. The condition that would flip my verdict is a named lighthouse customer -- Oak Ridge, Lawrence Livermore, Citadel, Jane Street -- with a signed contract value disclosed by the Demo Day update. Until then, Expanse is a company I want to interview, not one I write the check on from the dossier.
