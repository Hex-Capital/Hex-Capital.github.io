# Deep Interactions -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Sruthi Viswanathan is an Oxford DPhil candidate in Human-AI Interaction productizing her own discipline, and Deep Interactions is what that produces: a company strongest on idea-organicity and weakest on hacker genealogy. The dossier tells me she has spent years in UX research at Google, Naver Labs, and Limbic AI watching non-engineers fail to extract product context from stakeholders -- that is the literal subject of her PhD -- and the company is the artifact of that obsession. But there is no GitHub profile for either Sruthi or Beni Ngwamah, and "humans-in-the-loop" is doing a lot of the lifting that, in a **Stripe** (2010)-shaped company, would be done by code. Deep Interactions reads like a researcher's company, not a hacker's company.

On animal resourcefulness, the HIGH rung is **Airbnb** (2009)'s cereal boxes funding the company at $40 a pop; the MID rung is **Dropbox** (2007)'s Hacker News demo flipping me before the check; the LOW rung is the polished-MBA AI-wrapper deck I described in my pass example. Deep Interactions sits between MID and LOW: Sruthi has 10 named design partners (Jack Fertility, Canopie, FitMatrix), and the YC line "ship a working AI product in the same afternoon" implies real personal hustle, but there is no stunt -- no Obama O's, no rejected-then-rescued prototype, no public demo that got passed around. Animals leave tracks; I see footprints, not a kill.

On hacker genealogy, the HIGH rung is Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010); the MID rung is Aaron Swartz co-authoring RSS before **Reddit** (2005); the LOW rung is the technical-by-credential-only team with a CS degree and no shipped artifact. Deep Interactions sits closer to LOW than MID: Sruthi's Google Scholar profile and OpenReview pubs are real, but they are HCI research papers, not shipped open source -- "No public GitHub found" appears for both founders, and Beni Ngwamah's prior was three years at Barclays Investment Bank plus a fintech called WEKA. This is the inverse of the Lisp-literate, competitive-programming pattern that quietly drives my best outcomes.

On organic-idea fit, the HIGH rung is Drew Houston's USB stick birthing **Dropbox** (2007); the MID rung is the Collisons hacking commerce flows at **Auctomatic** (2007) before **Stripe** (2010); the LOW rung is "spotted a market opportunity" pitches. Deep Interactions sits between MID and HIGH because Sruthi's Limbic AI mental-health background maps cleanly onto the disclosed Canopie/mental-health customer wedge -- she is selling into the exact world she came from -- but the founder-pain story is academic rather than visceral. On schlep, **Coinbase** (2012)'s compliance grind is the HIGH rung and a pure AI-wrapper is the LOW rung; Deep Interactions sits mid-ladder because "we ship a working AI product in the same afternoon" is a genuine services schlep that **Lovable** ($400M ARR by Feb 2026) will not personally perform, but it is not a regulatory or physical-world grind that compounds into a moat. On co-founder configuration, the Collison brothers anchor HIGH and a solo-founder pitches LOW; Sruthi plus Beni meeting through Oxford is a credible pair, but the dossier lists team size 4 with two unnamed members, which is the kind of ambiguity my essay flagged.

Thirty-six-month forecast for Deep Interactions: services-heavy revenue grinds to $1-3M ARR, the team-collaboration wedge gets absorbed by **Lovable** or Replit as a feature within twelve months of their next product cycle, and Deep Interactions ends as an acqui-hire by a workflow vendor (Notion, Asana, or a mid-tier dev-tools acquirer) or pivots into enterprise AI consulting for healthtech buyers Sruthi already knows. The mechanism is the one I keep watching: a researcher-led company with no hacker artifact and no founder stunt cannot outrun a vibe-coding incumbent that raised $330M at a $6.6B valuation in December 2025 and will copy the team-context layer the moment it matters.

The strongest objection to my pass is that Sruthi's Human-AI Interaction DPhil is a "specific knowledge" advantage Naval would back and that I sometimes underweight -- **Lovable** has scale but no published research lineage on how humans actually hand context to AI builders, and the **Airbnb** (2009) Known Exception reminds me that I once mentally rejected a team whose idea I dismissed and was wrong because the founders were animals I had not yet recognized. The dossier evidence that wins the argument against the bull case is the absence of a shipped pre-funding artifact and the absence of a Brian Armstrong-style stunt: Sruthi is impressive and Deep Interactions is plausible, but plausible-with-impressive-founders is the consensus pre-seed AI company in this batch, not the dumb-looking-with-animal-founders pattern that returns the fund. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict: if Sruthi ships a public, viral artifact in the next ninety days -- a working Deep Interactions demo on Hacker News that the audience plays with and praises unprompted, the way the **Dropbox** (2007) video did -- I write the check.
