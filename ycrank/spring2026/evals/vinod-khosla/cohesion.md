# Cohesion -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Cohesion is a domain-expert-continuity SaaS productivity tool aimed at buy-side analysts, and Devon Krapcho's 5+ years as a software analyst at Long Path Partners plus Matt Munns building AI-for-investors at T. Rowe Price tell me everything about what kind of company Cohesion is before I read another line. The dimensional signature is unmistakable: Cohesion's highest placement is on founder learning rate, and only because the dossier is silent so I apply the default -- its lowest is on founder-defies-expert-consensus, where Cohesion's profile is the textbook pattern my TED Talk warned against (the UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study, "experts beat chance only slightly"). When the floor of the company is set by a default rather than by evidence, you have already learned what the deal is.

**OpenAI** (2019) is my consequence-magnitude HIGH rung -- a no-revenue company I led at $50M because the success-state was civilizational, not because the math worked. The shared mechanism with Cohesion is that both promise to displace human cognitive labor; the divergence is in the size of the labor pool. OpenAI's success-state moves a $15 trillion variable (Fortune 2026-03-06); Cohesion's success-state, even at full ambition, makes equity analysts at "10+ funds with $10Bn+ combined AUM" faster at reading earnings transcripts and X/Twitter. No incumbent regime collapses. AlphaSense at $500M ARR (Oct 2025) and Hebbia's Matrix do not disappear -- Cohesion just monitors podcasts and tweets they do not. That is the **pass example** from my own framework: a 20-30% efficiency gain inside one persona's workflow, dressed in agent language.

Cohesion's founder pattern is the failure mode my Outlook Business essay names directly: career domain continuity dressed as innovation. Krapcho is a buy-side analyst building software for buy-side analysts; Munns built AI for investors at T. Rowe Price and is now building AI for investors at a startup. This is not the Pat Brown leaving Stanford biochemistry to attack animal agriculture at **Impossible Foods** (2011) shape. It is not the Sergey Levine / Chelsea Finn academic-PI shape at **Physical Intelligence** (2024). Nowhere in the Cohesion dossier does a founder publicly contradict their own field, and the absence is itself the signal -- McKinsey's 1986 forecast of fewer than 1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 (actual: 109M) was wrong precisely because the people inside the industry extrapolated their own playbook. Cohesion is being built by the playbook.

The technical-and-capital-risk dimension compounds against Cohesion over the next 18 months in a specific way: there is no underlying scientific miracle to buy time. Cohesion is an LLM orchestration layer over podcasts and X/Twitter, where X's API pricing has already proven to be a unilateral switch the platform can flip. **Rogo** at $300M+ raised and 250+ finance institutions, **Hebbia** at $161M raised, **AlphaSense** at $500M ARR -- any one of them extends into podcast-and-X monitoring as a feature in a single quarter and Cohesion's wedge narrows to nothing. Consensus VCs will fund Cohesion exactly because the risk profile is conventional SaaS; that is the inverse of why I funded **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) at $115M.

For Cohesion's score on founder learning rate to flip a tier, the dossier would need to surface a specific prior pivot from Krapcho or Munns -- a "we were wrong about X, here is what we learned" moment akin to the publicly told Kanye pivot at **Slash** (2024) that justified my $100M Series C. The dossier names none. No pre-seed pivot, no public thesis update, no prior company sold. I do not aggressively penalize this at pre-seed -- the absent-evidence default holds -- but I also do not get to invoke my **Slash** exception, which only triggered because the pivot was documented and load-bearing on the thesis.

Cohesion's capital profile mirrors my **pass example** more than any portfolio anchor: per-seat enterprise SaaS sold to hedge funds reaches $1M ARR on $5-10M of capital, which means I am structurally over-resourced for this deal. Khosla Ventures wrote $50M into OpenAI (2019) and co-led the $105M **Genesis AI** (2025) seed because the roadmap demanded capital depth. Cohesion's roadmap demands a sales motion, not a capital miracle.

My 36-month forecast on Cohesion: acqui-hired by AlphaSense or Rogo for $15-60M between 2027 and 2029, with ARR settling in the $2-8M range before the acquisition closes, the founders absorbed as a buy-side product team rather than a continuing brand. The mechanism is the one Cohesion's own dossier names: well-funded incumbents with distribution and content licenses extend into agentic monitoring as a feature, and Cohesion's 10+ fund customer base becomes the rationale for an acquihire rather than a Series B.

The bull case against this forecast -- and it has to sting -- is the **DoorDash** (2013) override: capital-light consumer/enterprise plays that I did fund despite contradicting my science-risk rhetoric, returning $72B at IPO. Could Cohesion be the consensus-bet exception? The dossier specifics say no. DoorDash had a consequence-of-success that reshaped consumer-commerce flow at a national scale; Cohesion's success-state is bounded by the global population of fundamental equity analysts at long/short funds -- a customer base measured in thousands of seats, not tens of millions. **Slash** (2024) is the closer adjacent exception (YC-pedigreed, consumer-banking), but Slash's override required a documented founder pivot, which Cohesion lacks. The single condition that flips my verdict: Cohesion ships proprietary indexing of a non-API'd data source -- something genuinely defensible like first-party transcripts of 10,000+ private investor podcasts under exclusive contract, or signed multi-year LOIs from 3+ funds north of $50Bn AUM each at six-figure ACVs. Absent that, Cohesion is the pass I described in 30 seconds in my framework's pass example, with hedge funds substituted for sales teams.
