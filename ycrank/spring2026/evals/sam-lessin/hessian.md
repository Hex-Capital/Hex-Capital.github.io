# Hessian -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Hessian is the canonical 2026 YC pass example wearing a slightly more interesting financing coat -- "we forward deploy with AI agents to automate business operations" is the literal template I described in my own pass note, but the services-revenue model gives it one degree of structural separation from the pure SaaS cap table I reflexively de-rate. The dimensional signature here is a company that scores lowest where my conviction is loudest (AI-agent sleeve, the category I explicitly call "just more internet") and highest where I actually look for difference (a services-shaped revenue line that isn't a seat-expansion SaaS deck), and that pairing tells me exactly what Hessian is: a Distyl mini-me running the right structural pattern in the wrong category at what is almost certainly the wrong price.

On round mis-pricing, the HIGH rung is Solana at $0.05 -- a category every generalist had quit, where being the only bid was the entire alpha. Hessian shares zero mechanism with that placement. The dossier names Distyl AI at $1.8B post with $207M raised executing the identical "forward-deployed engineers plus proprietary agent platform" thesis, and Sierra at $10B, Decagon at $1.5B, Cresta at $1.6B all sitting in adjacent enterprise-agent boxes -- this is the most consensus-priced sub-sleeve of the most consensus-priced sleeve in the 2026 batch. Hessian's dossier reveals no round size, no price, no passing tier-1, no signal of category abandonment. Silence on round dynamics in this exact sector is not neutral; it's confirmation that Hessian is being shopped into the standard AI-agent SAFE.

The LOW rung on founder-as-brand is the dossier with zero public writing, zero community footprint, paid-acquisition-only -- the filter the Slow Creator Fund was built to flip. Hessian fits that LOW rung cleanly. Bao Nguyen has no Twitter the dossier could find, no GitHub repos surfaced, no public essays on agents or FDE economics. James Smith: same. Tom Watson has a @watsontom100 handle with no retrievable follower count, which is the dossier-tells-you-everything signal. "Writing is thinking. If you can't write, you can't think" -- and I can't find the thinking. The one named customer is Fazeshift, a fellow YC company, which is operator-network sourcing without the founder-brand compounding that would justify it.

Capital structure is the dimension where I want to give Hessian credit and where the dossier almost lets me. Forward-deployed services revenue from day one is structurally closer to Teamshares-shaped earnings than to a "we'll be default-alive after Series C" SaaS plan -- there is real cash for real work, which is the Slow letter's whole point. The mechanism that breaks the analogy: FDE services on a 3-person team is headcount-bound, and Hessian explicitly bets that "coding agents let 3 engineers scale this." That bet is unproven externally, and Distyl needed $207M to scale the same pattern. Eighteen months out, either Hessian's gross margin per engineer compounds as the agents do more of the work and this is a real venture moment, or it stays a boutique consulting shop dressed in YC clothing. The dossier gives me no margin data to adjudicate, so I default toward the pessimistic reading.

Counterfactual for founder conviction: Bao co-founded Zettafleet (decentralized LLM training, out of stealth February 23, 2026 per Sifted) before Hessian -- so he's a serial founder with ML systems publications including DeepMind work, and James and Tom both shipped real production infrastructure at Bloomberg, DRW, and GoCardless. That's a real builder team. What would have to be true to flip this to mid-tier conviction? One of them publishing a non-obvious essay on why generic AI-agent SaaS will lose to operate-the-agents services, or three years of unpaid obsession with the back-office automation problem specifically. Neither artifact exists in the dossier. Two Durham CS grads who met at Bloomberg starting an AI agent company in 2026 is a competence signal, not a missionary signal.

The category sleeve parallel is straightforward: this is the inverse of Aleo. Aleo was crypto infrastructure at seed pricing when every generalist had quit; Hessian is AI-agent infrastructure at seed pricing when every generalist is bidding. "AI is kind of like mobile. It's not disruptive. Crypto is fundamentally disruptive" (Lonsdale podcast). Hessian's own dossier tags itself Developer Tools, Machine Learning, Workflow Automation, AI -- four words, all four in the consensus sleeve I explicitly re-weight away from. There is no override mechanism available.

My 36-month forecast: Hessian raises a bridge or a small priced seed, lands 2-4 more YC-network customers, fails to break out of the Distyl shadow at the enterprise tier, and either gets acqui-hired into a larger FDE-shop (Distyl or a Cresta-adjacent buyer) for $5-25M in late 2027 or 2028, or pivots into a vertical-specific ops agent (likely fintech ops given Bloomberg/DRW/GoCardless backgrounds) below $3M ARR. The mechanism producing this: well-capitalized FDE incumbents win the enterprise logos Hessian needs, and a 3-engineer team cannot out-execute $207M of Distyl payroll on the same playbook.

The strongest case against my forecast is the Ro override. Ro violated my capital-efficiency rule because regulatory complexity made capital intensity itself the moat -- and a bull on Hessian would argue that bespoke enterprise integrations across Slack, Postgres, AWS, GitHub for each customer create exactly the same "only VC capital clears the schleps" dynamic. Plausible, and it stings because the founder team genuinely has the chops to do the schleps. It loses to one dossier fact: Ro was building prescription rails across 50 state regulators, an asset no other fund could replicate; Hessian is building Postgres-and-Slack integrations that Distyl already replicated for Fortune 500s with two orders of magnitude more capital. The schleps are real but they aren't proprietary. Verifiable flip condition: Hessian discloses 3+ non-YC-network enterprise customers at >$500K ACV with multi-year contracts within 12 months, OR restructures the financing as a services-holdco / earn-out vehicle I could underwrite directly rather than a standard YC SAFE. Absent either, I pass.
