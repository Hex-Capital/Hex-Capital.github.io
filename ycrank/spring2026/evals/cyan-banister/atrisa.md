# Atrisa (formerly Refortifai) -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Atman Kar's RTL Digital Design Engineer badge from Texas Instruments is the one thing in the Atrisa dossier that makes me lean in, and the rest of the file pulls me right back out. Atrisa is an analog-circuit-agent company whose CTO has actually sat on the silicon side of the table while its CEO Sayan Mitra has no analog footprint I can verify — and the category around Atrisa is being swept up in real time by Cadence's February 2026 ChipStack acquisition, Quilter's $10M Benchmark Series A, and Astrus's $13.3M Khosla round. The dimensional signature is a company with a credible technical hand on the inside but no tactile artifact for me to feel, dropped into the most consensus AI-EDA moment in a decade. That is the wrong shape of bet for the way I write checks.

Anduril's Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever pitched defense, and that is the HIGH rung against which I'm measuring Atrisa on lived founder experience. The mechanism that made Anduril work was that the *CEO* was the one with the prior artifact in hand — the social license sat with the person holding the tiebreaker vote. Atrisa inverts this: the lived hand is Atman Kar at TI doing RTL, but Kar is CTO, and CEO Sayan Mitra's analog credentials are absent enough from the public record that the dossier flags a name-collision risk with the UIUC ECE professor of the same name. Atrisa has one analog founder, not an analog-founder-CEO, and the difference is the difference between Luckey-Anduril and any number of competent-CTO-quarterback-CEO pairs I've passed on. The pivot from runtime AI security (Refortifai) into analog EDA mid-batch sharpens that worry — the team's prior product wasn't in silicon at all, which suggests Kar's domain pulled the company into his lane rather than the company having been Kar's idea from day one.

The pass example I keep on file is the consensus YC AI-agent darling — six tier-1 funds chasing, $200B TAM slide, model-as-moat — and Atrisa sits uncomfortably close to that LOW rung on the magically-weird dimension. Cadence acquiring ChipStack within three months of its launch and immediately shipping the ChipStack AI Super Agent is not a category-opening signal for Atrisa; it is the incumbent telling every other agent startup that the ceiling here is an acqui-hire. Quilter has Benchmark, Astrus has Khosla, Silimate is the first EDA company YC backed, and now Atrisa is the *second* — that is not the "two credible VCs passed on aesthetic grounds" tell I was looking at in 2018 Crusoe. The Atrisa round will be priced as if the agentic-EDA outcome is already a known quantity, which is the opposite of finding founders before they become consensus.

Sayan Mitra carries the CEO title cleanly, with Atman Kar as CTO and Rithik Jain as COO, and the titles read like the founders did have the conversation — that is meaningfully better than the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll co-CEO ambiguity I cite as a cautionary structure. The 18-month mechanism here is that a clean title hierarchy lets Atrisa avoid the slow-bleed governance failure mode but does nothing to resolve the deeper question of whether Mitra, lacking the public analog credentials Kar carries from TI, will hold authority when Kar's domain expertise becomes the binding constraint on every product decision. Titles are a Day-1 artifact; tiebreaker authority compounds or erodes from Month 4 onward, and the dossier gives me no documented operating agreement to anchor on.

EDA is not pure-software SaaS wrapper territory — Atrisa is selling into a regulated, capital-intensive customer base where Synopsys, Cadence, and Siemens hold ~70% combined share and where the buyer is a chip-design team whose simulation cycle is the regulatory artifact. To flip Atrisa's capital-intensity score up a tier I would need to see Atrisa shipping into a customer's actual tapeout flow, not just an agent that wraps frontier LLMs over circuit notation; the dossier shows zero open job postings and no disclosed customer engagement, so the unsexy-regulated lane is theoretical rather than embodied. This is the lane that produced Crusoe and Vigil Labs for me, but those companies were in the ground in their domain. Atrisa is, today, adjacent to the domain through Kar's resume.

Niantic let me play Ingress in 2012 and feel the pull on my own thumbs three years before I wrote the check — that is the single-anchor parallel I run every B2B developer-tools pitch against, and Atrisa fails it cleanly. I cannot install an analog-circuit agent in my own life, I cannot photograph one at the Four Seasons, and I cannot read its Wi-Fi network name across the room at The Lobby conference. Atrisa is a tool whose value can only be evaluated through founder testimony and an analog engineer's nod, which means the persistence-after-cold-outreach sourcing method that found Flock Safety has no surface to grip onto here.

My 36-month forecast for Atrisa: acqui-hired by Cadence, Synopsys, or Siemens for $15-50M in talent-and-tech terms, mirroring the ChipStack-into-Cadence playbook from Feb 2026, with Atman Kar absorbed as a staff engineer and the agent folded into an incumbent suite — or, in the downside, shut down inside 24 months as Quilter and Astrus's funding advantages compound into customer logos Atrisa cannot catch. The mechanism is that the incumbents have demonstrated they will pay acqui-hire prices to neutralize the category before any single agent startup reaches escape velocity, and Atrisa starts the race with the smallest war chest among the named competitors.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass — actually the bull case for Atrisa — is the HandUp known exception: I have written checks into modest-ceiling companies because the founder biography mirrored my own, and if Sayan Mitra is in fact the UIUC ECE professor who spent a career on formal verification of cyber-physical systems, that is a polymath-cross-domain compounder in the DeepMind exception lane and Atrisa is a different evaluation. The dossier is explicit, though, that public sources do not confirm the identity, the LinkedIn handles differ, and the dossier writer flagged investor verification as required. I will not score against an unverified upside, and the verifiable condition that flips my verdict is concrete: Sayan Mitra publicly confirming a decade of analog-or-formal-verification academic work and a documented agreement that Atman Kar's TI domain expertise carries operational veto on technical scope. Until then, Atrisa is a Pass at the careful end of Pass — close enough to Neutral that I would take the meeting, far enough from Invest that I would not lead.
