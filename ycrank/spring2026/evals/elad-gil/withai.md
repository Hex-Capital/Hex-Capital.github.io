# WithAI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

WithAI is a catalyst-real, growth-silent company -- a workflow layer for fundamental hedge funds at a moment when frontier-model reasoning genuinely crossed the finance-synthesis threshold, but the dossier discloses exactly one named logo (Mercator Partners) and zero MoM numbers, which is the specific shape my Tim Ferriss #863 framework treats as "something fundamentally broken." Ian McInnis (Bridgewater AI workstream lead) and Ben Finch (Sentient Labs founding researcher) have a real wedge into a real catalyst; what they don't have on this page is the curve.

**Anduril** (2022) is my high rung on the catalyst dimension -- a 24-month Ukraine-driven re-opening that re-priced defense from graveyard to growth. **Lyft** Series C is my low rung -- a steady-state market with no specific recent catalyst, where TAM math was the whole pitch. WithAI sits closer to Anduril than to Lyft but materially below: the dossier names a credible catalyst (Bridgewater's AIA Labs program plus AlphaSense Generative Search Jan 2026 plus Rogo's 27x ARR growth on o1-class reasoning), which is a real 24-month step-function in finance-grade synthesis. But this is a *consensus* re-opening that already has well-capitalized leaders ($154M into Rogo, $75M Series C this January) -- closer to legal-AI-post-Harvey than to defense-pre-Anduril, which is why I score the dimension in the middle of the range rather than the top.

**Harvey** (2023) is my high rung on revenue velocity -- "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025), the canonical "just working" signature. The pre-revenue science project with no curve is my low rung. WithAI sits much closer to the low rung: one named client, a testimonial ("existentially threatened" → "tremendously accelerated"), no ARR, no pricing, no logo count, no MoM growth disclosed. In a category where Rogo is shouting 27x ARR growth and AlphaSense is launching products quarterly, WithAI's silence on its own curve is the negative signal, not the absence of a pitch deck. Pre-seed allowance doesn't get you out of this -- Decagon and Harvey at pre-seed had real usage stories from the first call.

**Perplexity** (2022) is my high rung on founder shipping -- Aravind would "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss #863). The ex-McKinsey/ex-FAANG promoter with no artifacts is the low rung. WithAI sits in the middle: Ben Finch was a founding researcher at Sentient Labs ($85M-funded) and co-authored the OML cryptographic paper (IACR ePrint 2024/1573), which is a shipped technical artifact but academic, not iterative-product. Ian McInnis led "how can we use LLMs to invest" inside Bridgewater, which is real domain immersion but invisible from the outside -- no public repos, no consumer artifacts. Princeton class of 2023 means three years post-grad; the founder execution rhythm is plausible but unproven against the Aravind template.

**Stripe** (2010) is my high rung on index position -- the workflow path every e-commerce entrant has to touch. **Lyft** is my low rung -- #2 in a winner-take-all market that compounds toward #1. WithAI sits between but lower than I'd like: client-cloud deployment plus 60+ integrations (Bloomberg, FactSet, Snowflake, Enfusion) creates per-customer switching cost, but that's bespoke embedding, not index position. WithAI does not become more valuable as fundamental investing grows -- it has to win deal-by-deal against Rogo, AlphaSense, Hebbia, and in-house AIA-Labs-style builds at every large fund. This is workflow infrastructure, but the wrong shape of workflow infrastructure: per-firm ontologies don't compound across the category the way Stripe's API surface compounded across merchants.

**Color Genomics** (2015) is my high rung on single-miracle architecture -- workflow on commodity sequencing, no chemistry bet. WithAI's miracle is frontier-LLM reasoning being accurate enough for finance synthesis, which is largely validated. But the founders themselves flag a second miracle in the dossier: "do you check whether a stock is good by shorting it?" -- non-verifiable outputs require an eval/trust infrastructure that doesn't exist yet, which is why this scores middle-of-range rather than top. Honest framing of the problem, but it's still a second miracle.

WithAI in 36 months reaches $3-8M ARR with 8-15 mid-size asset manager logos, then gets acquired by FactSet, S&P Global, or AlphaSense for $40-120M as the hedge-fund workflow layer their seat-based business cannot natively build -- a respectable founder outcome that does not return a concentrated angel portfolio. The mechanism: the Bridgewater-angel distribution wedge converts efficiently in year one, the consultative-implementation motion caps growth at enterprise sales cadence, Rogo's $154M war chest funds a hedge-fund product line that compresses WithAI's TAM, and the non-verifiability problem keeps deployment timelines long enough that revenue velocity never matches the Harvey signature.

The strongest case against my pass: Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour are not vanity angels -- they are co-CIOs of Bridgewater, and their cap-table presence is a warm-intro pipeline into every fundamental fund that wants to sound like Bridgewater, which is a real distribution moat that Rogo (IB-focused) and AlphaSense (search-led) structurally cannot replicate. Ian McInnis saw what AIA Labs built from the inside and knows exactly which gaps mid-size funds need filled -- that's the *Color Genomics* "operator who architected the workflow layer" pattern, which is one of my Known Exceptions. The bear answer: distribution-from-angels gets you 5-20 logos at enterprise pace; it does not get you to the explosive MoM curve that defines AI breakouts, and Rogo at 27x ARR growth is what the category leader looks like on this exact axis -- WithAI's dossier has zero comparable disclosure. I flip to invest if WithAI discloses 5+ named hedge-fund logos at $200K+ ACV and >20% MoM revenue growth within 12 months; until then, "market first, team second" tells me the team is plausible but the curve is missing and the index position isn't there.
