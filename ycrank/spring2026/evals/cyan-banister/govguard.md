# GovGuard -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

GovGuard is an unsexy-regulated wedge undermined by founders who discovered the FOIA problem rather than lived it -- Aditya Sabharwal came from Centene and BoardWatch, Gleb Hulting from BoardWatch and EGO Logistics, and the dossier surfaces zero hours spent inside a municipal clerk's office before this YC batch. That high/low pairing is the whole signature: the category is *exactly* the kind of ugly, statute-bound corner I usually overweight, but the people walking into it look like generalists who picked it off a YC problem-list, not insiders who have ever held a Sharpie over a redaction.

Anduril-Luckey is the high rung on lived experience -- Palmer had shipped Oculus to scale in an adjacent hardware domain before he ever touched a defense contract, and that biography is what gave him social license to walk onto a base. GovGuard's BoardWatch lineage is the mechanism inverse: a prior consumer-search side project shared between Aditya and Gleb that has nothing structurally in common with state public-records law, exemption codes, or the procurement reality of selling to a Tucson city clerk. I keep looking in the dossier for the moment one of these founders sat in a FOIA queue, watched a parent get denied a record, or worked the desk at a county office, and what I find instead is "20x faster FOIA response" marketing copy and a Contact Sales button. The Anduril analogy doesn't load here because the autobiographical contact point is missing.

The low rung on physical verifiability is the developer-tools API I cannot install, photograph, or hand to a friend -- the same failure mode that made me pass on the consensus AI-agent darling. GovGuard's product surface is gated behind support@govguard.com and a "Contact Sales" wall, with no public demo, no clerk-facing screenshot I can poke at, no Product Hunt drop, no Chrome extension, no observable artifact in the wild. I cannot file a FOIA in Oakland tomorrow and watch GovGuard route it. The pattern from the developer-tools pass appears intact in this dossier: the value of the product is fully mediated by founder testimony, which means I have to trust the deck rather than my own hands.

On *magically weird* -- the category is not laughed at, which is actually the problem. GovWell just closed a $25M Series A from Insight Partners positioning as the "AI operating system for modern government," NextRequest sits inside CivicPlus, GovQA inside Granicus via Frontier Growth's rollup. The mechanism that made Crusoe work -- credible VCs passing on aesthetic grounds, leaving the round underpriced -- is the inverse of what's happening above GovGuard in this stack. By month 18, agentic FOIA capability is a feature GovWell ships on top of an already-funded GovOS, not a wedge GovGuard owns. Weirdness has been priced out of this lane by an Insight check.

The counterfactual on capital-intensive/unsexy/regulated: GovGuard would flip from a 9 to a 13 here if Aditya could point me to one signed pilot with a named county where the redaction engine has been legally cleared under that state's specific exemption code, because then the regulatory surface stops being abstract and becomes a per-jurisdiction asset that compounds. The dossier instead gives me an unsourced "pre-built state exemption code library" claim with no jurisdiction count. The category is genuinely in my lane -- public records law is exactly the kind of statute-thick corner Vigil Labs and Mindbloom live in -- but the moat is asserted, not demonstrated.

Mindbloom is the single-anchor parallel on CEO clarity: a regulated category where one named CEO held tiebreaker authority through the ketamine compliance gauntlet. GovGuard's titles -- Aditya as CEO, Gleb as President/CTO -- are delineated on paper, which clears the HQ Trivia / GameCrush bar that has burned me before. I don't see a documented operating agreement or vesting cliff, but the BoardWatch co-founding history at least means these two have shipped through a disagreement before. This is the dimension where GovGuard scores cleanest, which is also faint praise -- "your governance won't kill you" is table stakes, not a thesis.

36-month forecast: GovGuard gets acqui-hired by Granicus or CivicPlus for sub-$15M as a bolt-on FOIA-agent module, or dies quietly after burning 24 months on municipal procurement cycles without breaking past three pilot counties -- because the GovWell Series A is going to consolidate the agentic-GovOS layer one floor above GovGuard's wedge while NextRequest's existing CivicPlus distribution closes the door from below. The lived-experience gap means Aditya doesn't have the relationships to shortcut an RFP, and the physical-verifiability gap means no clerk in Phoenix is going to tweet about the product and pull a procurement officer into the funnel organically.

The strongest objection is the BoardWatch-repeat-founder + Tom-Blomfield-as-YC-partner + "Jira vs. work-completer" framing -- a steel-man bull case argues this is exactly the agentic-wedge play that eats ticketing incumbents the way Rippling ate Zenefits, and the 2x-YoY FOIA volume tailwind from AI-generated requests is a real why-now that the GovWell horizontal can't service as precisely. The HandUp exception would apply if Aditya or Gleb had a personal mirror to the FOIA-requester experience -- a parent denied records, a journalist sibling, a homelessness-adjacent origin like my own -- but the dossier surfaces none of that, so the override doesn't trigger. **Flip condition: a signed paid pilot with a named city of 100k+ population and a clerk-facing testimonial I can verify by phone, within the next 90 days, moves GovGuard from Pass to Neutral and earns a second meeting.**
