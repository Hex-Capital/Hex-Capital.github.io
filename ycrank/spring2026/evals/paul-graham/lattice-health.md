# Lattice Health -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Christine Park is a PGY-3 neurosurgery resident at UW concurrently pursuing a Stanford CS PhD pitching an "operating system for clinical AI in imaging" -- which is to say Lattice Health is a thesis-perfect schlep walking through the door inside a solo-founder body with no shipped product surface and no website field on the YC page. That pairing -- schlep zone in the bullseye, animal-resourcefulness evidence missing -- is the entire analytical signature of Lattice Health, and everything below follows from it.

The **Coinbase** (2012) analogy is the one that loads here for Lattice Health, because Brian Armstrong's whole edge was running toward bitcoin compliance grind while every other YC applicant in his batch routed around it. PACS/EHR/HL7/DICOM integration across >700 FDA-cleared imaging devices is the radiology-AI version of that grind, and Lattice Health is explicitly positioning as the horizontal layer above Aidoc, Gleamer, and Rad AI -- which is to say the unpleasant integration work those single-application vendors will not do because it commoditizes their own models. Where Lattice Health diverges from Armstrong is that Armstrong shipped a working Android wallet to overturn his rejection; Lattice Health's dossier shows no product surface, no demo, no pilot logo, just a YC page with the tagline. The schlep is the right schlep. The artifact proving Park will personally do the schlep is not yet in the dossier.

A polished two-MBA AI-wrapper team with a glossy deck and a "we'll hire engineers post-funding" plan is the canonical shape of the founder I pass on, because no founder has personally suffered the problem and no artifact predates the check. Lattice Health does not look like that team -- Park published "Point-of-Care MRI with AI to Measure Midline Shift" in 2022 and the arXiv multimodal-brain-MRI paper (2511.18208, 2025), which is research a neurosurgery resident did because she was the user of imaging AI inside her own call shifts. The MBA-wrapper pattern is absent. What is also absent is the pre-funding stunt -- no cereal-box equivalent, no Hacker News demo, no scrappy revenue improvisation -- so Lattice Health sidesteps the red flag without yet reaching the **Dropbox** (2007) bar.

Park's idea-origin story is the strongest single column in Lattice Health's profile, and the mechanism compounds for an obvious reason: a neurosurgeon who has personally read brain MRIs at 3am has lived inside the workflow that the operating system is supposed to fix, in the same way Drew Houston had personally forgotten his USB stick. Over 18 months that organic-pain origin will keep Lattice Health from building a product that PMs imagine radiologists want -- Park already knows what they want because she is one tier adjacent to being one. The risk is that her pain is neurosurgery imaging specifically, not the cross-modality enterprise-PACS pain hospital CIOs buy against, and Lattice Health could end up building a tool for Park's call shift instead of for a health system's procurement office.

For Lattice Health to flip a tier on hacker genealogy, the dossier would need a GitHub footprint or an open-source artifact ahead of the company -- right now we have Duke MEng in AI (2023), Stanford CS PhD in progress, and peer-reviewed deep-learning papers, which is academic-hacker rather than the Patrick Collison Young Scientist Lisp prize or Aaron Swartz RSS-spec lineage I usually look for. The counterfactual is narrow: a single repo with non-trivial DICOM tooling or a published model card would move Lattice Health from the "credentialed researcher" rung to the "shipping hacker" rung, and the absence of a found GitHub for @sparkcpark is the specific gap.

Lattice Health is solo, and on this axis the only honest comparison is **Twitch** (2007), which had three-to-four founders who had already roomed together through the **Kiko** (2005) failure. Park has neither a co-founder nor a named pipeline for one, and she is simultaneously a UW neurosurgery resident and a Stanford CS PhD candidate -- which means the bandwidth coverage problem and the cofounder-chemistry problem arrive on the same day. "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) was explicit: I have not seen the top-tier outcomes come from solo bets. Lattice Health is the configuration I am structurally most cautious about, against a backdrop where the rest of the company looks thesis-shaped.

My 36-month forecast for Lattice Health: Park does not reach $5M ARR, the company narrows to a neurosurgery-imaging vertical tied to her clinical network at UW and Stanford, and Lattice Health is acquired under $20M by Bayer/Blackford or Microsoft/Nuance Precision Imaging Network -- both of which already operate the orchestration marketplace Lattice Health is trying to build -- or alternatively gets absorbed into a Stanford-Duke clinical-AI research spinout. The mechanism is straightforward: a solo founder splitting time across residency and a PhD cannot personally run 6-18 month hospital sales cycles against Microsoft-owned Nuance, and the schlep wins only when the founder can pay it full-time.

The bull case that stings is the **Coinbase** (2012) precedent: Armstrong was effectively the operator at the start, and Park's clinical-plus-engineering credential stack is deeper than his -- her arXiv 2511.18208 paper functions as a kind of demo video for what she can build, and her UW residency is a built-in design partner. If you squint, Lattice Health passes the schlep test more cleanly than half the companies in the batch. What dismantles the case is that Armstrong shipped a customer-facing Android wallet, not a research paper -- the artifact was a product, not a publication -- and Park's bandwidth is split across two full-time commitments that Armstrong did not have. The single condition that would flip my verdict to Invest before Demo Day: a named technical cofounder onboard and one signed pilot with a real health system (UW Medicine being the obvious one given Park's residency). Without both, Lattice Health is the right schlep in the wrong configuration.
