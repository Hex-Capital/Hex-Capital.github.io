# 83 Sciences -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

83 Sciences is a domain-expert services play dressed as a materials-AI moat -- highest on capital-risk-that-repels-consensus-VCs only because materials science *sounds* hard, lowest on founder-defies-expert-consensus because Naccarella (Stanford ChemE, HBS, ex-Sila, ex-BCG), Riesel (MIT chem PhD), and Yang (BCG Principal) are three of the most orthodox résumés Y Combinator will fund this batch. The dossier names a $251.17M materials informatics TAM (Precedence Research, 2026) growing 20.22% CAGR -- that is not a number I underline. That is a number I cross out.

On consequence magnitude, my anchor is **Impossible Foods** (2011), where the success-state was the collapse of animal-protein production as an industrial regime -- a named incumbent system disappearing. 83 Sciences frames its success-state as "R&D teams can learn from failed experiments," which is a 20-30% efficiency lift across chemicals, batteries, semis, and pharma R&D departments. That is the exact "optimization play" language I told Outlook Business I do not fund. Nothing in the 83 Sciences dossier names an incumbent regime that ceases to exist if the Lab Brain works -- pharma R&D still runs, semiconductor fabs still run, Sila still runs. The consequence variable is "corporate R&D productivity," and the closest labor-displacement figure I can reach for is not in the dossier at all. When I asked at MIT in 2019 to "focus on the consequences of success," 83 Sciences would have failed the question at the door.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) failed on the low rung of technical-risk-that-repels-consensus-VCs in a specific way -- the science required three coincident miracles and the capital plan understated the burn to prove any of them, so the plant closed in 2011. 83 Sciences has the inverted problem: the *approach* is capital-light -- three people, in-person data capture at labs, models on top -- and Riesel's Crystalyze work in JACS 2024 hit 67% accuracy on real RRUFF experimental patterns, which tells me the model layer is neither impossible nor magical. There is no fusion tokamak here, no catalyst-yield miracle, no $75M state loan required to reach the first data point. Any Tier-1 generalist can fund 83 Sciences. That is the definition of "not my edge."

Founder-defies-expert-consensus is where 83 Sciences bleeds hardest. My TED Talk cited McKinsey's 1986 forecast of <1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 (actual: 109M) because experts extrapolate the past -- and 83 Sciences's founding team is three experts extrapolating the past. Yang scaled BCG's internal AI tool to 30,000 users; Naccarella did strategy at Sila; Riesel is a published MIT chemistry PhD whose paper the dossier features as a credential. There is no "I was wrong about X" moment, no publicly contradicted field position, no instigator posture. This is the exact founder pattern my Outlook Business essay warned about -- the pitch will always look reasonable to reasonable people, which is why reasonable outcomes are all it will produce.

On learning rate, 83 Sciences shows no documented pivot -- the mechanism that would compound over the next 18 months is the founders' willingness to abandon the Lab Brain framing when the first three enterprise pilots stall in procurement, and nothing in the dossier tells me they will. Absent evidence, my default is 7/15 and I hold it. Compare to **Slash** (2024): the Kanye West pivot story is why I wrote the $100M Series C into what was otherwise a consumer-banking business I would not touch. 83 Sciences has no equivalent story, and pre-seed teams that execute one linear plan against enterprise R&D buyers typically discover, at month 14, that the plan was the problem.

Required-capital profile is where 83 Sciences most nakedly fails to match my check size. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) required a $115M Series A because the roadmap demanded a tokamak -- deployment for the capital was legible. 83 Sciences's roadmap requires a laptop, three people flying to labs, and API credits. My $50M **OpenAI** (2019) check was not because I like big numbers -- it was because the compute roadmap swallowed the money. CuspAI is reportedly raising $400M at $2.6B (SiliconANGLE, Jun 2026) and Citrine has already absorbed $81.3M (Tracxn 2025); if capital were the moat in materials AI, those companies would already be winning, and the dossier tells me they are not yet. 83 Sciences is over-served by any $2M pre-seed round -- which means I am over-resourced for the deal.

My 36-month forecast: 83 Sciences reaches $1-3M ARR through 2-4 pilot contracts with mid-tier chemicals or battery R&D groups, then gets absorbed by Uncountable or Citrine for under $50M in an acqui-hire structured around the negative-results dataset and Riesel's ML team -- or, more likely, it winds down quietly in 2028 after failing to convert a semiconductor or pharma pilot into an ARR contract before the pre-seed runway ends. The mechanism is enterprise procurement math: multi-quarter cycles into IP-sensitive R&D orgs, no signed LOI counterparties disclosed, and CuspAI's compute budget arriving at every RFP.

The bull case that should sting: 83 Sciences's on-site data-capture approach genuinely does something Citrine and Uncountable structurally cannot -- customer-upload platforms will never see the negative results, and if Riesel's Freedman-Group network converts even three national-lab partnerships into exclusive negative-results corpora, 83 Sciences becomes the *data layer* that CuspAI eventually has to buy at nine figures. The **Glean** exception applies here in spirit: a domain-expert founder (Arvind Jain) who I nonetheless backed because consequence magnitude and capital scale bailed out the orthodox résumé. The reason Glean's exception does not extend to 83 Sciences is that Glean was rewiring Fortune-500 information asymmetry -- a $15T labor frame -- while 83 Sciences is rewiring R&D lab notebooks. Verifiable flip condition: two signed data-sharing contracts with named Fortune 500 chemicals or battery incumbents within 12 months, each including exclusivity on negative-results data -- if that appears, the moat thesis has legs and I reconsider. Absent that, 83 Sciences is a pass, and I write the check nowhere near it.
