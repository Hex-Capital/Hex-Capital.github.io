# 6thSense -- Paul Graham Evaluation

6thSense is the rare dossier where the work itself looks like a real schlep -- eight-modality synchronized tactile/egocentric capture, per-channel calibration, drift documentation -- and yet the four founders behind it (James Baek, Ronak Agarwal, Alex Hyungwoo Noh, Matt Wulff) are invisible. That pairing is the analytical signature: a Coinbase-shaped problem attached to a team I cannot see. The schlep is the moat in theory, and in practice I have no animal to point at.

The schlep test is where 6thSense scores best, and it scores best because the problem genuinely rhymes with Coinbase (2012). Brian Armstrong's compliance grind was a moat precisely because every other YC applicant in his batch was routing around it; tactile sensor calibration across eight aligned modalities -- pressure, RGB-D, hand pose, IMU, secondary cameras, labeled commentary -- is the kind of work software-only founders unconsciously avoid. The 6thSense site explicitly frames the offering as taking on "multi-month setup and QC work" so customers don't have to. That is schlep blindness, named correctly. Where 6thSense diverges from Coinbase is that Armstrong shipped a working Android wallet prototype to overturn his rejection -- a concrete artifact -- whereas the 6thSense dossier contains no demoable capture run, no published calibration benchmark, no customer reference. The schlep is identified; the willingness to grind it out has not yet been proven by an artifact.

The pass example in my own file is the polished MBA team with no shipped artifact, and the mechanism that kills them is invisibility-of-the-founder. That mechanism appears in 6thSense's dossier with uncomfortable directness: searches for James Baek return no confirmable LinkedIn, no GitHub, no Twitter; Ronak Agarwal maps to a possible @ronak-agarwal GitHub handle but identity is unconfirmed; Alex Hyungwoo Noh's LinkedIn is "identity match not confirmed"; Matt Wulff possibly worked at Tesla/Skygauge but "none confirmed as the 6thSense founder." Animals leave tracks. Drew Houston's Dropbox (2007) demo hit Hacker News before the check. The 6thSense founders, on the eve of a Summer 2026 batch, have left no public technical artifact I can verify -- no repos, no papers, no prior hardware project, no tactile-sensing publication. I am not penalizing them for being early; I am noting that four founders with zero recoverable footprint is itself a data point.

Counterfactual on organic-idea fit: for 6thSense to clear the Dropbox (2007) USB-stick test, the dossier would need to show that one of these four founders personally tried to collect a tactile dataset in a prior robotics lab and got burned by sync drift -- a specific founder-prior-pain story. It doesn't. The site language reads as market-observation ("robot learning teams pursuing dexterous manipulation face datasets that miss touch timing"), not as autobiography. That is the framing of someone who spotted an opportunity, not someone who hit the rake.

Mechanism analysis on the four-founder configuration: a four-person team with no described prior working relationship is not a Stripe-brothers compounding bet; it's coverage-by-headcount, and over 18 months coverage-by-headcount fragments under equity disputes and unclear decision rights. Airbnb (2009)'s trio had RISD-roommate chemistry; 6thSense's quartet has no described chemistry of any kind. If the four of them roomed together, hacked together, lost a hackathon together -- the dossier would say so.

Single-anchor parallel on hacker genealogy: Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp work at sixteen, years before Stripe (2010), and that lineage was load-bearing in why I leaned in. For 6thSense, the GitHub trail is a single unconfirmed @ronak-agarwal handle. I am not asking for a Young Scientist prize. I am asking for one repo, one paper, one hardware build log. The absence is itself the signal.

My 36-month forecast for 6thSense: outflanked by Human Archive's $8.2M lead and 1,000-headset deployment, 6thSense ends up sub-$2M ARR and either acqui-hired into Physical Intelligence or a similar vertically-integrated lab, or pivoted from selling datasets to selling calibration tooling. The mechanism is the buyer-concentration trap -- Physical Intelligence at ~$11B and Genesis AI with its proprietary glove will build, not buy, and the remaining buyers are exactly the tier Human Archive is already serving with capital 6thSense doesn't have.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass invokes the Coinbase (2012) exception: Armstrong looked unpromising on paper -- rejected the first time -- and the schlep itself eventually became the moat. If these four 6thSense founders are heads-down hardware animals whose lack of public footprint reflects shop-time rather than absence, my pass is a Brian-Armstrong-shaped mistake. The dossier evidence that wins against that read: Armstrong rescued his rejection with a working artifact I could hold; 6thSense has no equivalent shipped object in the dossier. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is one named frontier lab -- Physical Intelligence, Genesis AI, a Figure-tier customer -- publicly running on a 6thSense capture stack with a demo I can watch. Until then this is a real schlep with ghost founders, and the reverse-pattern -- soft founders, plausible idea -- is exactly where I have historically lost money.
