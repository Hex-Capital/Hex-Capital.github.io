## The Idea

- **Problem:** Software companies spend hours producing launch videos, social graphics, and ads — or pay freelancers — to market product updates (YC company description); customer segment is software companies shipping product changes against tight launch cadences (tday.com homepage).
- **Approach:** Reads a connected GitHub repo or website to capture product changes, auto-records end-to-end product walkthroughs with voiceover and captions, generates matched graphics, then publishes across Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, X, Facebook, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Threads, Bluesky, and Google Business (tday.com homepage, WebFetch).
- **Differentiation:** Vs. Arcade (manual screen-recording-based launch videos), tday triggers off git/PRs and auto-regenerates when UI changes (tday.com homepage, WebFetch); vs. AdCreative.ai / Omneky / Pencil (image/video ad generators driven by product feeds and briefs for performance ads on Meta/TikTok), tday is tied to the product codebase rather than ad-feeds (Arcade blog, AdCreative.ai, Omneky sites via search snippet).
- **Business Model:** No pricing disclosed on homepage (WebFetch tday.com); [Inferred]: SaaS subscription tied to publishing volume / connected channels, with a "Book a call" CTA on /book indicating sales-assisted onboarding (tday.com homepage, WebFetch).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Direct-to-software-company sales via founder outbound and YC network, supported by /book sales-call CTA and featured customer showcases (Relay, Drift, Slate, Forge, Inkwell) on the homepage (tday.com homepage, WebFetch).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Co-founder Shrestha states the team has "built the architecture for a fundamentally different kind of creative pipeline" producing "designs that are both AI-driven and fully deterministic" — i.e., structured, editable, brand-coherent outputs vs. raster image generators (Adelaide University news, 2026).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Performance data closing the loop from shipped product to ad/social outcomes could become a proprietary feedback dataset for creative ranking, unproven now because no customer count or performance metrics are public.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: No clear structural barrier preventing well-funded incumbents (Canva, Adobe, Figma, Arcade) from building git-triggered product-to-creative pipelines.
- **Commoditization risk:** Multiple funded competitors (Arcade, AdCreative.ai, Omneky, Pencil, Lapis) already generate launch/ad creative from product inputs; deterministic, code-based design output is the principal technical claim differentiating tday (Adelaide University news, 2026; Arcade blog; AdCreative.ai site).

- **Why now:** [Inferred]: Deterministic, brand-coherent design output from LLM/image models has only recently become feasible per founder statement that the team built "a fundamentally different kind of creative pipeline" (Adelaide University news, 2026); social-platform fragmentation (10+ channels supported on homepage) raises the manual cost of per-channel reformatting (tday.com homepage, WebFetch).
