# TrustAI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

TrustAI is the platonic form of the pitch I sketched in my Pass Example -- MIT technical founders, AI agent for cross-tool workflow automation, B2B SaaS shape, S26 YC batch, demo-Calendly funnel -- and the dimensional signature says everything: TrustAI's least-bad axis is founder credentialing (Medha Venkatapathy MIT CS+Physics, USA Physics Team '21, Lincoln Lab), and its worst axis is category-sleeve fit, because "suggested cross-tool automations" is exactly the AI-agent shell I said was "just more internet" on the Lonsdale pod. That pairing -- competent MIT builders pointed straight at the 2026 consensus category -- is not a venture moment. It's a factory-model input.

On category sleeve, the anchor is Solana at $0.05 -- pre-consensus crypto that no generalist would touch because the ICO crash had cleared the room, which is the exact opposite condition TrustAI is being born into. TrustAI is landing in the single most crowded seed sleeve of 2026: Bardeen has already raised $25.3M with Dropbox and HubSpot Ventures on the cap table, Zapier is bootstrapped at ~$300M ARR shipping 8,000+ integrations, Magical has $60M+, Lindy and n8n are both funded, and every one of them is racing to add exactly the "suggestion layer" TrustAI describes. The dossier's own "Why Now" paragraph is [Inferred] from a listicle titled "20 Best AI Agent Workflow Tools 2026" -- when the "why now" is a listicle, the sleeve is over.

Founder-as-brand fails through the John Fish / Bookshelved mechanism inverted: Fish had a YouTube audience compounding for years before Slow wrote the check, so the distribution predated the company by half a decade. TrustAI has Hannah Chung at 715 X followers, Medha at an unretrievable count, no GitHub repos found for either, no Product Hunt launch, no Chrome Web Store listing, no press, no newsletter, no category writing. There is nothing on the cap table that isn't a standard SAFE for equity in a SaaS shell -- and in a market where Bardeen already owns the search term, TrustAI's demo-Calendly funnel is paid CAC dressed up as PLG.

Round-and-price for TrustAI is scored on absence: the dossier reveals nothing about post-money, no tier-1 passes, no unusual instrument, no discount. Over 18 months this dimension compounds the wrong way -- an S26 AI-agent seed with MIT founders will get bid up by momentum funds regardless of underlying economics, which pushes the entry price further from where TrustAI's actual regatta tacks (get to $1M ARR against Bardeen-and-Zapier land-grab) can clear. I default the score down because silence on structure in an AI-agent seed almost always means consensus SAFE at a consensus cap, and TrustAI gives me zero evidence to override that prior.

Capital structure would flip a tier only if TrustAI came in as, say, a services-first "we manually run the automations for HubSpot ops teams for $8K/month before we ship the model" Wizard-of-Oz shape -- the Fin playbook, the thing I actually lived. Instead the site claims "no installation required" cross-tool context capture, which is a technical feasibility claim that in practice requires an extension, an OAuth-per-app graph, or a browser agent, none of which the dossier documents. That's a founder-drainage cap table: TrustAI will need to raise a Series A to prove the "no install" claim, then a B to survive the Bardeen counter-punch, then a C to reach default-alive. Five tacks minimum, all at consensus prices.

Founder conviction, single-anchor: compare Hannah Chung's public footprint to Ben Silbermann's before Pinterest -- Silbermann was a missionary weirdo pattern-matched through Kortina-style personal-network sourcing, and even that check violated my price-discipline criterion (it's a Known Exception for a reason). TrustAI's Chung has a 2025 MIT Washington DC summer internship on her CV and a LinkedIn that says "Co-founder, CEO @ TrustAI (YC S26)" -- there is no published writing on workflow automation, no three-year unpaid obsession with the category, no operator-friend sourcing path back to my graph. The founder is responding to the S26 opportunity space; she is not on a decade-long mission to fix HubSpot busywork.

**36-month forecast:** TrustAI raises a $3-4M seed at ~$20M post from consensus AI-momentum funds, reaches $200K-$600K ARR against Bardeen's expanding suggestion feature, fails to close a Series A in 2028, and is acqui-hired by HubSpot or Salesforce for talent at <$15M -- an outcome that returns nothing meaningful to any seed investor and confirms the sleeve was uninvestable at S26 prices.

**Steel-man against the pass:** The bull case is that Bardeen's $25.3M from Dropbox and HubSpot Ventures is itself proof that strategic acquirers value this exact shape, which means TrustAI's downside is bounded by acqui-hire liquidity, and MIT-caliber founders in a strategic-buyer-hungry category is a decent risk-adjusted bet even at consensus prices. Ro is the Known Exception that could apply -- capital-intensive category where only VC money clears the schleps. But Ro's schlep was 50-state prescription rails; TrustAI's schlep is "beat Bardeen to better UX," which is not a moat, it's a race. The verdict flips only if TrustAI shows me either (a) a signed enterprise pilot with a named F500 buyer at >$50K ACV before the seed closes, or (b) a founding-team member with 3+ years inside HubSpot/Salesforce revenue operations -- neither exists in the dossier, and until one does, this is the Pass Example with the serial numbers filed off.
