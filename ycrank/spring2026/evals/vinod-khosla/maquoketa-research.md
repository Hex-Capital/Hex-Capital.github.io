# Maquoketa Research -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Maquoketa Research is a hardware-deep team — Alejandro Hernandez out of Berkeley Wireless Research Center's mmWave phased-array work, paired with a vertically integrated RF/airframe/CV stack — entering a category where Anduril already sits at a $30.5B valuation and Shield AI at $12.7B (March 2026). The signature is loud: high on technical depth, low on contrarian thesis. Maquoketa Research is building the kind of object I want to see built, in the kind of crowded procurement lane I usually avoid, with no documented expert-defiance moment from the founding team. That pairing — competent stack-builder, no contrarian spine — is the diagnosis.

On capital-and-technical risk that repels consensus VCs, the load-bearing comparison is **Genesis AI** (2025), where Eclipse and I co-led a $105M seed precisely because foundation-model robotics required hardware + data + training capital at a scale generalists would not underwrite. Maquoketa Research shares the *posture* — in-house RF, in-house mixed-signal, in-house power, in-house airframes — and Hernandez's 140 GHz MIMO phased-array research is the kind of credential a SaaS-only fund cannot price. Where Maquoketa Research diverges from the Genesis AI rung is the $5,000/unit Pentagon price point cited in the Defense One January 2026 piece: attritable economics force the company toward volume manufacturing rather than fundamental-physics-miracle territory. That collapses the consensus-repellent property. Anduril and Shield AI are already in this lane with billions; the capital risk is competitive, not scientific.

**Glean** is the cleanest cautionary parallel on founder-defies-expert-consensus — Arvind Jain ex-Google search, paradigmatic domain-expert profile, which I accepted only because the consequence magnitude on Fortune-500 information asymmetry was overwhelming. Maquoketa Research's failure mode is that the *same* override does not apply. Evan Yeager is ex-SpiderRock derivatives trading, Hernandez is a Berkeley RF researcher, Aidan Williams led mechanical engineering teams, David Muchow's resume is described in the YC bio as "confusing to recruiters" — nowhere in this profile does any founder publicly contradict drone-warfare orthodoxy or stake out a contrarian position on autonomy that the AeroVironment/Anduril/Shield AI consensus rejects. They are credible builders entering the consensus lane, not instigators redirecting it. My TED Talk's UC Berkeley study (28,000 expert predictions, only slightly better than chance) was the framework for *avoiding* this profile unless the consequence-magnitude override kicks in — and here it does not at Glean's scale.

On consequence magnitude, the mechanism analysis matters more than the ladder. Maquoketa Research's success state is "US troops field autonomous one-way attack drones that quadruple mission success rates" — that is a lethality-regime shift inside one procurement channel, not a $15T labor-displacement variable. The TAM citation of $24.13B loitering munitions by 2034 (Fortune Business Insights) is real but bounded; compounded over 18 months, the consequence flattens because the Pentagon's Drone Dominance ceiling of ~30,000 units at $5,000 caps the near-term economic gravity at ~$150M of procurement that will be split across AeroVironment, Performance Drone Works, Swarm Aero, and the named incumbents. The McKinsey-1986-cellphone failure mode does not apply in reverse here — Maquoketa Research is not under-forecasting a category, it is entering a forecast that the Pentagon has already written.

The counterfactual on founder learning rate: for Maquoketa Research to clear the **Slash** (2024) exception threshold I'd need a documented pivot — a public "we were building X, Ukraine taught us Y, now we ship Z" inflection from Yeager or Hernandez. The dossier reports none. Yeager moved from SpiderRock derivatives software into defense hardware, which is a career change, not a thesis update under fire. The default of 7/15 holds with a downward tilt because the company is too young to have generated the pivot evidence, but I don't get to imagine it.

The single-anchor parallel on required-capital fit is **DoorDash** (2013): capital-light by my standards, fundable by any Tier-1 generalist, and the only reason I wrote that check was founder ambition plus consumer-logistics consequence. Maquoketa Research at pre-seed needs $5–15M to first DoD prototype contract, plausibly $30–50M to qualify under ITAR and ship volume — meaningful, but inside Andreessen, Founders Fund, Lux, and 8VC's standard defense-tech range. I am over-resourced for the round and under-differentiated as the lead.

Forecast, 36 months: Maquoketa Research either gets tuck-in acquired by Anduril or AeroVironment for $40–90M as an RF/CV talent-and-IP deal, or fails to win a Replicator/Drone Dominance slot — they are conspicuously absent from the Defense One January 2026 selectee list naming Performance Drone Works and Swarm Aero — and winds down with the Hernandez phased-array IP licensed out. The mechanism is straightforward: in a procurement category where the buyer has already chosen its primes, a pre-seed entrant without a named contract becomes acquisition feedstock, not a category-collapser.

The strongest bull case stings: Ukraine's 2022–2026 drone war proved that small, fast, vertically integrated teams shipping attritable autonomous munitions repeatedly out-iterated the AeroVironment Switchblade incumbent that I would have called consensus-locked in 2021. If Maquoketa Research's CV stack genuinely delivers the quadrupled mission-success claim, and if the Pentagon's March 2026 "drone swarm crucible" exercise (DefenseScoop) functions as a real meritocratic bake-off rather than an incumbent-confirmation ritual, then a stack-deep $5K/unit entrant beats the $30B valuation incumbents on unit economics — the inverse of the **KiOR** capital-trap. What dismantles this case is that the dossier provides zero flight-test data, zero signed LOIs, zero named DoD program office relationship, and Maquoketa Research is not among the publicly named Replicator selectees. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named DoD program-of-record contract or a Replicator/Drone Dominance selection announcement within 12 months. Without it, this is the wrong tier of my portfolio.
