# Foaster -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Foaster is the rare profile where the founders look like animals and the idea looks like a deck — Alexandre Combes already shipped Répondia with 60K+ AI customer calls in production for French restaurant groups, and Raphaël Dabadie co-built LLM benchmarks that hit 12M+ views, but the artifact they're selling is "AI-native consulting" priced at "3 to 5x less expensive than traditional consulting." That mismatch — shipped builders working on a consulting-shaped problem they advised into rather than lived inside — is Foaster's signature, and it pulls in opposite directions on the two dimensions I weight most.

Combes shipping Répondia is the load-bearing fact. The closest portfolio analogy on the animal-resourcefulness axis is **Dropbox** (2007), where Drew Houston's MIT-hacker demo video on Hacker News flipped the seed conversation before the deck mattered — the artifact pre-existed the pitch. Combes' 60,000 production AI calls handled for restaurant groups is a shipped-before-pitched signal of the same family, and Dabadie's 400K-person tennis community built at age 11 plus the 12M-view LLM benchmarks are exactly the kind of unscalable stunts I look for. Where Foaster diverges from the **Dropbox** (2007) rung is that the shipped artifact (Répondia, the benchmarks) is *not the company* — it's adjacent evidence of capability, not the wedge product Foaster is now selling. That's why I can't park Foaster at the top of this ladder: the animal tracks are real but they're in the next field over.

The pass case I keep re-using is the polished two-MBA AI-wrapper team with a $50B TAM slide and "we'll hire engineers post-funding" — the failure mechanism is that the founders haven't personally suffered the problem, so they're optimizing for a market frame instead of a workflow they hate. Foaster doesn't fail this test the same way, but it bends toward it: Dabadie "spent the prior year helping enterprises think through AI deployment," which is the advisor-into-founder path, not the **Stripe** (2010) path where the Collisons had been hacking commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007). Foaster's idea-origin is "we noticed McKinsey leaves and execution dies," not "we couldn't ship our own AI rollout so we built this for ourselves." The dossier doesn't name a single moment where Dabadie or Combes was the customer of Foaster, and that absence — given how loudly organic ideas usually announce themselves — is the silence I trust.

On the schlep dimension, the mechanism worth tracking over 18 months is whether running AI agents to interview every employee about workflows, bottlenecks, and "informal habits" compounds into a trust moat or stalls at the procurement gate. **Coinbase** (2012) turned bitcoin compliance grind into a moat because Armstrong personally wanted to do the grind; Foaster is wading into employee-level HR-sensitive data with no SOC 2, ISO, or data-handling certification disclosed on foaster.ai. The schlep is genuinely present — mid-market 50+ employee orgs do not casually let agents interview every person — but Foaster has not yet shown it will run *toward* the compliance grind the way Armstrong did. If they post a SOC 2 Type II in twelve months, this score moves up a tier.

The hacker-genealogy question flips on a counterfactual: if Combes had a public GitHub showing the Répondia agent architecture, or Dabadie had open-sourced the 12M-view LLM benchmark harness, Foaster moves from the middle of this ladder up toward the **Reddit** (2005) Swartz/RSS-spec rung. As the dossier stands, "No public GitHub account found" appears for both founders, and ENSAE Paris is a math/stats grande école, not a hacker lineage I read instinctively the way I read MIT or the Collisons' Lisp prize. The 12M-view benchmarks and the production Répondia shipment are real technical artifacts; they keep Foaster from the MBA-team floor, but the missing GitHub trail prevents the top-rung read.

Combes and Dabadie are a pair, not a solo, and they co-built the LLM benchmarks and co-advised enterprises for roughly a year before Foaster — the closest single-anchor parallel is **Reddit** (2005), where Huffman and Ohanian had dorm-roommate years rather than project-collaborator months. Foaster's pair has shorter prior shared history than the **Reddit** (2005) pattern, and the Paris-CTO / San Francisco-CEO geographic split adds a real execution tax: French enterprise gravity (Répondia's restaurant footprint) versus YC's US-market push. Two founders, real collaboration, real geographic friction — neutral on this axis.

My 36-month forecast for Foaster: by mid-2029, the consulting framing is gone and Foaster has pivoted into a productized employee-workflow-mapping SaaS sold to AI-transformation buyers at mid-market accounts, landing in the $3-8M ARR range — alive, capable, not category-defining, not acquired by BCG or McKinsey. The mechanism is the signature: animal founders working on a non-organic problem almost always re-find their organic problem inside the data they collect, and the workflow maps Foaster generates from employee interviews are a more defensible artifact than the consulting wrapper around them.

The bear case that stings: this is closer to the two-MBA pass pattern than I'm admitting. Foaster's pitch leads with "3 to 5x less expensive than traditional consulting" — that's TAM-and-pricing framing, not "make something people want." The whole product description reads like a roadmap deliverable, the kind of language **Founder Mode** (September 2024) flagged as manager-mode thinking. Combes' Répondia is real, but it's an AI-agents-for-restaurants product that didn't become a venture outcome — it's a credential, not a compounding business, and credentials are exactly what I told myself to discount versus shipped wedges. What wins against the bear case is the 12M-view benchmark distribution stunt plus the 60K-call production deployment — those are two independent animal signals on different axes (distribution and shipping), and that's more than the pass-example MBA team ever had. The condition that flips my verdict to invest: a paying mid-market customer named publicly within six months who renewed past the assessment SKU into the Continuous AI Transformation tier. Until then, Foaster is the Neutral-lean-pass shape — capable founders, wrong-shaped wedge, watch for the pivot.
