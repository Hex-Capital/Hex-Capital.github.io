# Drafted -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Drafted is a second-time-founder shipping bet on a thin consumer category -- Nicholas Donahue's prior swing at residential design, Atmos (YC S20), died in March 2025 when rates broke the customer, and Drafted is him running back at the same problem with a 65x cheaper inference stack (0.2¢/plan vs. 13¢ per TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025). The signature here is high founder-shipping-rhythm paired with low index-distribution position -- which is the Paul Graham profile, not mine. I back market re-openings with index positions; Drafted is a founder-quality bet in a category that has no clear infrastructure path and where the underlying demand is rate-coupled to a Fed I cannot model.

Donahue's shipping ladder lands him between **Perplexity** (2022) and **Brex** (2017) on the dimension I weight most for founders. Aravind at Perplexity is the high rung because he showed up a week later with the thing built (Tim Ferriss Show #863) -- that's the rhythm. Drafted's evidence is real: 120k monthly users and 325k+ floor plans generated in a month (YC company description, 2026), team scaled from 6 in December to 9 by Spring 2026 batch, four early Drafted employees followed Donahue over from Atmos (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025), which is its own signal -- people who watched a founder fail will only re-enlist if the shipping is real. Where Drafted diverges from the Perplexity rung is consequence: Aravind's prior artifacts were research code; Donahue's prior artifact, Atmos, raised $20M from Khosla and Sam Altman, scaled to 40 employees and $7M revenue, designed $200M of homes, and shut down. That's not a disqualifier in my book -- it's domain pain -- but it puts Drafted closer to the Brex operational-rhythm rung than the Perplexity cold-inbound-magic rung.

**Lyft** Series C is the low rung on index-distribution and the failure mechanism is the one I have to test Drafted against: a thin consumer layer fighting for share in a market that does not automatically compound your position as the category grows. Drafted's distribution surface is "120k users... through socials and word-of-mouth" (YC description) and a 9,399-follower X account opened November 2025 -- consumer self-serve. Drafted does not sit in the workflow path that every entrant in a growing category must touch the way **Stripe** (2010) sits in payments rails. Maket.ai has been shipping a directly comparable AI floor-plan generator since 2019 (Crunchbase), Snaptrude raised $14M from Foundamental and Accel for the professional BIM wedge (TechCrunch, Nov 2023), and Autodesk owns the architect-licensed seat. Drafted's "85,000+ AI-created house plans on platform" (drafted.ai snippet) is a dataset, not a distribution moat -- the *index* question is whether US residential construction is a compounding category at all, and at current rates the honest answer is no.

The catalyst question -- my standard entry point -- splits cleanly for Drafted. The *tool-side* catalyst is real: multimodal models crossed CAD-grade structural fidelity in 2024-2025, which is exactly why Donahue says Atmos couldn't replace the humans before but Drafted can now (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025). That's a legitimate 24-month step-function. But the *market-side* catalyst is missing. Drafted's end demand is custom residential homebuilding, and the dossier-cited reason Atmos died is the Fed -- "clients who'd spent months designing their dream homes couldn't afford them anymore" (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025). Drafted has a re-opened toolchain inside a market that did not re-open. That's the inverse of the Anduril (2022) setup, where the *market* re-opened post-Ukraine and the toolchain was already there.

Revenue velocity at Drafted is where the dossier actively concerns me, and the 18-month compounding logic goes the wrong way. Drafted's earlier monetization was $1,000-$2,000 per complete plan (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025); the current Drafted.ai homepage advertises "Free AI House Plans with PDF & CAD Downloads" (May 2026). That is a monetization *regression*, not the Harvey (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" curve I size up against. Usage is compounding -- ~1,000 DAU in December to 120k MAU by Spring -- but in my framework that's "false signal vs. just working" territory until I see a paid conversion curve. When an AI consumer product moves from $1-2k ASP to free in six months, the company is buying a usage chart with margin, and I've watched that movie. The Tim Ferriss #863 line applies: if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive *revenue* growth, something's fundamentally broken.

Drafted fails my single-miracle test cleanly. Counterfactual: what would have to be true to flip this dimension a tier? The miracle stack as written requires (a) generative output meets permit-grade structural standards without human review, (b) consumers convert from free PDF/CAD downloads to a paid tier at unit economics that work, and (c) the underlying residential construction market re-opens via a rates cycle Drafted cannot influence. Drafted only flips this tier if Donahue isolates the bet to a single miracle -- e.g., abandon consumer and become the floor-plan generation API that Zillow, Lennar, and Toll Brothers integrate against. Color Genomics (2015) is my reminder here: I architected it as workflow on top of commodity sequencing, not a chemistry bet, precisely to compress the miracle count. Drafted as currently scoped is the chemistry-bet shape.

My 36-month forecast: Drafted reaches $3-8M ARR on a converted freemium model, gets acquired by Autodesk or a national homebuilder (Lennar/D.R. Horton tier) for $40-150M as a workflow tuck-in, or shuts down on the same rate-cycle mechanism that killed Atmos. The mechanism producing this is the high/low pairing in Drafted's signature: Donahue's shipping rhythm and the four-Atmos-alumni team will compound usage and product quality fast, but the absence of an index-distribution position means upside compresses to a strategic exit rather than a venture-scale outcome, and the rate-sensitivity in the underlying market caps the floor.

The bull case that stings: Donahue is the textbook founder-market-fit profile -- grew up in a homebuilding family, ran Atmos for five years, designed $200M of custom homes, is on his second swing with the exact pain knowledge, and is shipping at a rhythm that pulled four prior employees back. The 65x inference cost advantage (0.2¢ vs. 13¢) is real infrastructure, not a marketing line. The investor list -- Patrick Collison, Ben Silbermann, Josh Buckley, Bill Clerico, Jack Altman, Samsung, Alex Blania, Kevin Mahaffey -- is the kind of consensus markup I'd normally lean into per "when consensus pays." This could be Color Genomics (2015): workflow infrastructure on top of a hard-tech layer (generative models + permitting) that compounds as the underlying gets cheaper. What dismantles it: Color was an operating bet I co-founded and architected to isolate the miracle; Drafted is a passive consumer angel check where the founder is regressing pricing toward zero in a rate-coupled end market. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a paid-tier conversion curve -- if Drafted shows 20%+ MoM paid revenue growth with a named B2B integration (Lennar, Toll Brothers, Zillow) signed by Q4 2026, I'd write the next round at consensus.
