# OpenProse -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

OpenProse is a fast-iterating developer-tools play where Dan Barrett has shipped 14 releases to v0.14.0 by May 19 2026 — admirable cadence — but the success-state at maximum ambition is "LangChain, but in Markdown," and that is not a world I am trying to bring into existence. When I read a YC dossier my first question is what economic mechanism breaks if this works, and the honest answer for OpenProse is: agent builders write `### Requires`/`### Ensures` blocks instead of Python. No incumbent regime collapses. No $15T labor variable moves. 7,000 installs of a Markdown parser is not a civilizational signal.

**Cognition** (Series B April 2024, $175M) is the right HIGH-rung anchor on consequence magnitude because Cognition and OpenProse both sit in the agentic-software stack, but Cognition's success-state is "the labor market for software engineers re-prices," and that loads against my Fortune 2026-03-06 framing of $15 trillion of U.S. GDP-as-labor. OpenProse's success-state, by contrast, is "agent-framework authors port their prompt chains from LangChain Python SDK to `*.prose.md` files." That is a refactor of how prompts are written, not a redrawing of what gets done by whom. The dossier's own positioning — "framework-agnostic and portable across Prose Complete harnesses" — concedes the ceiling: OpenProse is plumbing under someone else's labor-displacement engine, not the engine.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) is the LOW-rung lesson on capital risk that repels consensus VCs — the failure mechanism there was real physics that no Tier-1 generalist would underwrite. The inverse pattern is what I am looking at with OpenProse: MIT-licensed Markdown contracts plus a memoizing Reactor runtime is software any Tier-1 generalist can fund on a $500K SAFE, and LangChain ($260M raised, $1.25B Oct 2025) or Anthropic's own skills primitive can replicate the surface in a sprint. Range Fuels' science was hard and wrong; OpenProse's "science" is parsing and dependency injection. There is no capital moat because there is no capital requirement, and that is precisely the symptom my rubric scores low.

On founder defiance of expert consensus, OpenProse's dossier names Dan Barrett as "12 years software engineering, 5 years agent development, two-time founder" with prior companies undisclosed and disambiguation across LinkedIn profiles unreliable. That is the exact "career domain expert applying the same playbook with AI" profile my TED Talk warned against — the UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study where experts performed only slightly better than chance. There is no public contrarian thesis from @irl_danB in the dossier, no instigator moment, no documented pivot from an adjacent field. Compare to Sergey Levine entering **Physical Intelligence** (2024) with a decade of contrarian published positions on imitation learning — OpenProse's founder has tweets, not theses.

Mechanism analysis on learning rate: 14 releases since launch and dogfooding via 13 internal "responsibilities" running OpenProse's own GTM is real iteration velocity, and over 18 months that compounds into either (a) a tight Markdown-contract spec that earns harness-vendor blessing, or (b) a feature surface that LangChain copies in a single release and zero customers care to migrate from. The dossier provides no documented thesis pivot — only forward shipping — which is iteration, not learning. My Slash (2024) check rewarded a Kanye-moment pivot story; OpenProse has cadence without a documented "we were wrong about X" inflection.

Counterfactual on required-capital profile: for OpenProse to clear the 7/10 threshold here, the roadmap would need to require >$50M to first commercial milestone — a hosted Reactor runtime processing billions of agent calls, or proprietary models trained to consume `*.prose.md` natively. Neither is in the dossier. The actual capital story is OSS distribution via `npx skills add openprose/prose` plus a "Request Access" CTA, which is a $2M seed business, not a Khosla check. I am over-resourced for OpenProse by an order of magnitude — the same reason I do not lead pre-seed SaaS rounds.

**Forecast:** Within 36 months OpenProse is either absorbed as an acquihire under $20M by Anthropic, LangChain, or Vercel — most likely Anthropic, given the `npx skills add openprose/prose` install path explicitly piggybacks on Claude Code's skills primitive — or it persists as a 5–10K-star OSS project with under $2M ARR on a thin hosted tier, with Barrett moving to his stated "third" company. The mechanism is structural: MIT license plus a category leader at $1.25B valuation plus a foundation-model vendor shipping native skills primitives is the same three-front commoditization squeeze that killed every framework-layer startup of the 2015–2018 web era.

The strongest bear-flip — meaning the case OpenProse becomes a real outcome — runs through the **Glean** Known Exception: Arvind Jain was a paradigmatic domain-expert profile, and I funded him anyway because the consequence-at-scale on Fortune-500 information asymmetry was large enough to override my expert-defiance prior. The OpenProse analog would be: `*.prose.md` becomes the canonical agent-contract format the way `package.json` became canonical for Node, switching costs compound across a registry of reusable responsibilities, and Barrett sits on the de facto standard. That argument stings because Markdown-as-contract is genuinely more legible than Python SDK calls, and developer-tools standards do sometimes emerge from one-person OSS projects. The dossier evidence that wins against it: LangChain at $260M raised and Anthropic's already-shipped skills mechanism are both structurally positioned to absorb a Markdown-contract spec under their own brand before OpenProse hits 50K installs, and Glean had capital scale and Fortune-500 enterprise distribution that OpenProse does not. The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Anthropic or OpenAI publicly adopts `*.prose.md` as a referenced canonical format in their official SDK documentation, naming OpenProse as the reference implementation — at that point the standard-setter premium rerates the company and I want to be in the next round.
