# Prism -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Prism is, at its core, a services-margin recruiting agency wearing an AI-native skin -- and the dimensional signature is a competent-shipping founder pair (Theo Kitsberg, Thiel Fellow with a recruiting business he started at 15; Axel La Pira, ex-Alan founder associate on internal recruiting infra) parked in the structurally weakest position my framework recognizes: a 15% + $500 contingency agency competing for share against Mercor ($10B post Oct 2025), Paraform (First Round + Khosla Series A), and incumbent contingency desks. The founders are the asset; the business shape is the problem.

**Founder shipping rhythm -- the highest rung.** When Aravind pinged me about Perplexity in 2022, the thing that moved me was the cadence -- a week later there'd be a working artifact tied to whatever we'd discussed. Prism's team clears the *artifact* test in a literal sense: they shipped HireGlide in F25, pivoted to Prism in P26, published a benchmark paper (PeopleSearchBench, 89.6 self-reported), and ran a Product Hunt launch to #3 of day with 224 upvotes. That is real shipping rhythm. Where Prism diverges from the Perplexity rung is the *direction of iteration* -- Aravind iterated toward a clearer wedge as usage compounded; Theo and Axel iterated *off* HireGlide entirely within six months because the prior wedge didn't take, and the Prism Product Hunt launch is the only public proof point of the second attempt. Shipping is necessary; shipping toward a curve is what closed Perplexity.

**Index position -- the lowest rung, and the one that decides this.** The Lyft Series C pass framework was clean: in a market with strong network economics, the #2 doesn't take a discounted version of the leader's economics, it takes a fraction. Prism is in a worse structural position than Lyft was -- recruiting agencies don't share network effects, but they also don't compound into infrastructure. Prism *is* the agency, sourcing on Slack, billing 15% of first-year salary. Stripe sat under every e-commerce dollar; Coinbase sat under every crypto purchase; Prism sits next to Mercor's $492M-raised marketplace and a long tail of contingency desks, and has to win each customer one Slack channel at a time. The benchmark paper is a sales asset, not a distribution position -- and the dossier names zero customer logos and zero placement counts.

**Market catalyst -- mechanism analysis.** The implicit "why now" in Prism's pitch is that LLM sourcing crossed a usability threshold in 2024-25, which is the same generic catalyst every YC AI-services company is leaning on this batch. Compounded over 18 months, this catalyst doesn't open a *closed* market the way Ukraine opened defense for Anduril or post-PayPal API ergonomics opened payments for Stripe -- it intensifies an *already-open* market where Mercor raised $350M at $10B (TechCrunch, Oct 2025) and Paraform is on a Series A. A catalyst that lifts all boats doesn't reward the boat that arrived second.

**Revenue velocity -- counterfactual.** To flip Prism from Pass to Invest on this axis alone, the dossier would need named tech-company logos signed in the last 90 days plus month-over-month placement count compounding above 20%. What's there instead: a retainer waived during launch week, a Product Hunt rank, and zero disclosed revenue. The HireGlide-to-Prism pivot within six months is itself a velocity signal pointed the wrong way -- if the F25 wedge had been "just working," the P26 rebrand wouldn't exist. Silence on growth, in an AI services play, is the signal.

**Single-miracle architecture -- single-anchor parallel.** Color Genomics was structured as workflow on top of commodity sequencing -- isolate the miracle to "can we operationalize this," not "can we invent the chemistry." Prism's miracle is similarly isolated: "AI sourcing is meaningfully better than a human sourcer at the same dollar cost." That's one miracle, and the 89.6 PeopleSearchBench number gestures at it. But Color's workflow layer rode commodity-cost decline on sequencing; Prism's services-margin economics don't ride any cost curve unless AI sourcing *fully* displaces recruiter labor, and even then the savings flow to clients at 15% vs the incumbent 20-30% rather than to gross margin.

**Forecast.** Over 36 months Prism most likely lands as a $2-5M revenue UK/EU boutique tech-recruiting agency, or pivots again -- probably toward selling its sourcing model as software to in-house recruiters (the hireEZ shape) within 18 months -- with no strategic acquirer at a meaningful multiple. The mechanism: services-margin businesses with no index position don't escape velocity unless they verticalize into a category-defining brand, and Prism is launching second on its own team's clock against better-funded competitors.

**Strongest objection.** The bull case borrows the Color Genomics template -- Theo has operator experience in recruiting from age fifteen, the 15% vs 30% pricing is a real wedge, every filled role generates labeled fit data that compounds the sourcing model, and the team has demonstrated they will ship and re-ship until something works. Maybe Prism is the workflow-infra-on-top-of-hard-thing play in talent, with the "hard thing" being LLM-based candidate ranking, and the Slack-briefed agency motion is just the wedge before the data moat appears. The objection stings because the founder profile genuinely fits the Brex/Decagon archetype I fund. It loses on one fact: Color was operated, not invested, and the data moat there rode a commodity-cost curve underneath; Prism's data moat is hypothetical until placement volume is disclosed, and the six-month pivot tells me the team's read of their own wedge has already changed once. **Flip condition:** if Prism discloses $1M+ ARR within nine months at >70% gross margin and names three tech-company logos hiring through them, I revisit -- that would convert this from a services bet to "just working" in the Harvey sense.
