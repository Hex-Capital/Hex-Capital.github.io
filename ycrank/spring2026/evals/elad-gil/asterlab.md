# Aster -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Emmett Bicker is shipping like Aravind Srinivas in 2022 — arXiv 2602.07040 in February, a 95.2s NanoGPT Speedrun world record, a 1.114ms TriMul kernel, Erdős Minimum Overlap SOTA, and PR #217 into KellerJordan/modded-nanogpt — but he is shipping into a category where Sakana AI at $2.65B post-money and Autoscience with a fresh $14M from General Catalyst already occupy the index path. That is Aster's signature: highest on founder-execution-rhythm, lowest on index-distribution-position, which makes Aster the kind of company that gets read on arXiv before it gets bought on a PO.

Aster's shipping cadence is where the Perplexity (2022) template loads. Aravind's pattern was "ping on LinkedIn, show up a week later with the thing built" — the dimension that registered as signal for me beyond any pedigree assessment. Bicker's analog is denser than most pre-seeds I see: five distinct SOTA claims across math, kernels, biology, neuroscience, and ML training in a single arXiv drop, plus a merged-class PR into a public benchmark repo that ML Twitter actually watches, plus a METR citation on 2026-04-21. Where Aster diverges from the Perplexity rung is that the GitHub account @EmmettBicker was created 2026-05-07 with no public repos shown and the LinkedIn announcement drew 72 reactions — Aravind by contrast had a years-deep public scholarly footprint and a multi-person co-founder team. Aster is a real shipper, but a solo one whose public-artifact history begins three months ago.

Sakana AI is the failure mechanism on the index dimension — not because Sakana is failing, but because Sakana has *already taken the position Aster needs*. ~$30M ARR per getlatka, a Nature paper for AI Scientist v2, $479M raised, plus Autoscience explicitly marketing "managed-service automated AI research lab for Fortune 500 ML training" — that is the workflow path. My Lyft Series C pass logic applies here without much modification: when the dossier shows named, better-capitalized incumbents who already occupy the layer every entrant must touch, the new entrant is fighting for share rather than riding the wave. Aster's dossier names no distribution wedge against Sakana — no exclusive frontier-lab partnership, no proprietary trajectory data moat (the dossier explicitly marks that one as "unproven now"), no enterprise design partner. This is the inverse of Stripe's 2010 position on e-commerce.

The market-catalyst dimension reads cleaner. Aster's why-now — frontier coding LLMs crossing the threshold where multi-step agentic program search produces compileable, performance-improving code — is the same Decagon (2023) catalyst applied to ML research instead of customer support. The catalyst is real and the window is 18-24 months old; I would not pass on Aster the way I pass on steady-state TAM-math pitches. But the dimension compounds against Aster over 18 months because the same catalyst that opened the window for Bicker opened it for Sakana, Autoscience, FutureHouse, and the frontier labs themselves — the catalyst is non-exclusive and the well-capitalized are already in the room.

Revenue velocity is the conspicuous silence. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" — Tim Ferriss Show #863. Aster's dossier reports "Revenue, paying-customer count, and user count: No public data found" and every cited traction signal is a benchmark or a citation, not a buying behavior. The counterfactual to flip this tier: a signed six-figure agent-run contract with a named frontier lab or pharma research org disclosed in the next 90 days would move Aster from research-credibility curiosity to the Harvey (2023) "just working" curve. Until then, the gap between arXiv reactions (72) and ARR (unknown, likely zero) is exactly the false-signal-vs-just-working distinction I apply.

Aster's single-miracle architecture is closer to clean than not. Bicker is not betting on new physics or new chemistry; he is betting that frontier LLMs plus evolutionary program search can be wrapped into a research-agent product. That is one miracle — *generalization from curated benchmarks (NanoGPT, TriMul, ZAPBench) to open-ended customer ML problems*. It is a real miracle and the dossier flags it as "undemonstrated in public sources," but it is one miracle, not three. This is the dimension on which Aster most resembles Color Genomics (2015) structurally — workflow infrastructure layered on a hard capability rather than a bet on the hard capability itself.

My 36-month forecast on Aster: acqui-hired by a frontier lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, or Bicker's prior employer Magic) at sub-$50M for the talent and the agent trajectory data, with the Aster technique absorbed into the acquirer's internal research-automation stack rather than commercialized as standalone SaaS — the mechanism is that solo-founder shipping velocity is exactly what frontier labs buy, but standalone distribution against Sakana and Autoscience is the wall Aster will not climb in this funding climate.

The bull case that stings: Aster's benchmark wins ARE the distribution. Frontier-lab research directors read arXiv and watch the NanoGPT leaderboard — that is exactly the developer-marketing channel that worked for Stripe's API-first wedge in 2010, when the customer (developers) and the marketing surface (technical credibility) were the same population. If Bicker converts two or three frontier-lab research teams into six-figure agent-run contracts on the strength of PR #217 and the METR citation, that is Harvey-shaped velocity emerging directly from research credibility rather than enterprise sales motion. What dismantles it is the dossier itself: Sakana already has ~$30M ARR with broader scope and a Nature paper, Autoscience is selling the managed-service version to Fortune 500 with a $14M war chest, and Aster has one engineer. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named, paid frontier-lab or top-20-pharma customer disclosed within 90 days at >$250K ACV, with a second logo within 180 days. Absent that, Aster is a brilliant individual contributor wearing a startup costume in a market where the index position is taken.
