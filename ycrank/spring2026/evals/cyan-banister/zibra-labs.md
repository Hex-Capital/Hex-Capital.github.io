# Zibra Labs -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Zibra Labs is a substrate-builder selling back into the substrate's biggest users — Ibrahim Rabbani led Ray Core at Anyscale (~12M weekly downloads, PyTorch Foundation), and Zibra Labs now wants to sell managed Ray-shaped HPC into the same Bridgewater, Two Sigma, P72, and Man Group quant funds that the dossier already cites as Ray users (zibralabs.ai, 2026). High founder-infra fit, near-zero tactile artifact. That pairing is the entire shape of Zibra Labs, and I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, so I do not score it as "quant trading bet" — I score it as "can a substrate maintainer extract margin from the substrate's whales before the substrate parent eats him."

Palmer Luckey shipped VR from his parents' garage before he pivoted to defense — that is the lived-product-before-the-company template anchoring my Anduril (2017) check, and Rabbani sits on that template on the infrastructure axis: he didn't read a McKinsey memo about distributed compute for finance, he built the open-source standard the quants already depend on. Where Zibra Labs diverges from Luckey is the problem-domain axis. Luckey was a defense *customer* in his bones before he was a defense vendor; Rabbani lived the substrate, not the alpha. The dossier puts Rabbani adjacent to Bridgewater and Two Sigma through their Ray dependency, not embedded inside any research team, and that one-degree-of-separation gap is the difference between Zibra Labs scoring near my Anduril rung versus mid-ladder.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on failed my tactile-artifact test before slide 3 — I could not install it, photograph it, or hand it to a friend, and the Flock Safety (~2018) Wi-Fi-name method had no surface to bite into. Zibra Labs sits in that same room. There is a cal.com "intro-to-zibra-labs" scheduler, no pricing page on zibralabs.ai as of May 2026, no Product Hunt listing, no app I can open, and the 6.4M-parallel-task / sub-50ms-dispatch claim only resolves into something I can observe through founder testimony. Zibra Labs is real engineering — Rabbani's Ray Core lineage and Policzer's Venice/Liquid/Espresso work at LinkedIn are not vaporware — but my heuristic does not grade engineering, it grades whether a normal human can touch the artifact in 48 hours, and Zibra Labs cannot pass that test by category.

Ibrahim Rabbani is named Founder & CEO and Zac Policzer is named Founder & CTO on the YC company page — clean title delineation, no co-CEO ambiguity, no 50/50 tiebreaker silence of the kind that detonated HQ Trivia (2017) in the Yusupov/Kroll feud. Over 18 months that compounds favorably for Zibra Labs because the actual work — hardening spot orchestration, signing the first named quant fund, surviving an interruption-driven backtest restart — is a CTO problem with a CEO setting customer cadence, and Zibra Labs has the titles right where my heuristic wants them. No equity split or operating agreement is disclosed; at pre-seed that silence reads as "they haven't had the conversation yet," not as a fatal flag.

YC Spring 2026, Tom Blomfield as Group Partner advisor, two ex-Anyscale and ex-LinkedIn infra leads pitching quant HPC — that is the texture of a competent, well-supported infrastructure bet, not the magically-weird Crusoe (~2018) shape where credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds. For Zibra Labs's D2 score to flip a tier, the dossier would have to show two or more sophisticated investors visibly refusing the round because quant outsourcing is "uninvestable" the way rockets were in 2008 or gas-flare Bitcoin was in 2018. Instead Zibra Labs has Anyscale ($281M raised, $1B valuation per Intel Capital, December 2021) sitting next to it as the substrate parent that everyone in the room already knows can either build or buy this wedge, and YC pedigree partner-attachment is consensus signal, not contrarian-pricing inversion.

Crusoe (~2018) was capital-intensive in a way Zibra Labs structurally is not — Chase Lochmiller bolted steel containers onto well pads in North Dakota; Zibra Labs schedules spot instances across AWS, Azure, and GCP that someone else owns. The "unsexy regulated" lane only catches Zibra Labs one degree removed: quant funds are regulated, Zibra Labs sells infrastructure to them. The moat that compounded with time at Crusoe was the founder's physical relationship to roughnecks on the well pad; the moat Zibra Labs claims is scheduler telemetry across 6.4M parallel tasks, which the dossier itself flags as buildable by any team combining Ray, Dask, and Kubernetes (zibralabs.ai, 2026). That is real engineering work, but the moat does not compound the way Crusoe's did.

Anyscale ($281M raised, $1B Series C, December 2021 — the commercial Ray parent Rabbani previously led) is the most probable acquirer of Zibra Labs inside 36 months at a $40-90M acqui-hire-plus, after Zibra Labs lands two to four mid-tier quant fund contracts but fails to penetrate the Two Sigma / Bridgewater tier whose in-house grids refuse to outsource core compute. The mechanism is substrate gravity: Anyscale either ships a quant-vertical SKU and crushes Zibra Labs's positioning, or it absorbs Zibra Labs to inherit the quant wedge before some other Ray-adjacent startup gets there first. The high-infra-fit / low-tactile-artifact signature predicts exactly this — a clean technical team building a clean product the substrate parent will eventually want.

DeepMind (~2013) is the steel-man for Zibra Labs — pedigreed founders compounding across domains overrode my outsider-trajectory bias because Hassabis's chess + neuroscience + games stack was genuinely irreplaceable. Rabbani's Ray Core leadership stacked on Policzer's planet-scale database work could plausibly form a quant-HPC layer no other team rebuilds in a quarter, and the YC Spring 2026 Blomfield attachment could open one tier-1 door I am underweighting. What sinks that bull case for Zibra Labs is the Anyscale overhang — Rabbani's prior employer holds $281M, the substrate, the brand, and direct access to every Ray customer Zibra Labs would want, and Hassabis's polymathy crossed *unrelated* domains while Rabbani and Policzer compound *within* distributed systems, which is depth, not polymathy. My verdict on Zibra Labs flips if Zibra Labs publishes a signed contract with a top-five quant fund — Citadel, Two Sigma, Bridgewater, P72, or Man Group — inside nine months; that single named-counterparty LOI is the one signal that overrides both the substrate-incumbency risk and the absent tactile artifact at once. Until then this is a clean pass with one foot in love for the engineering.
