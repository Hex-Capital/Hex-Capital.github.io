# YouArt -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

YouArt is a Hollywood-adjacent creator-network founder (Keith Zhang's SOULSCAPE, partnered with Matrix VFX lead John Gaeta) wrapped in a three-cofounder ex-TikTok ad-ops cap table, pitching a "video agent + Patreon rail" YC seed in the most consensus-priced AI sleeve of Spring 2026 -- and that mismatch between where the actual asset lives (Keith's creator supply pipeline) and the shape of the round being offered (standard SAFE on a generative-AI tool wrapper) is the entire story.

Let me start with the round, because that's where I always start. The Solana entry at $0.05/coin worked because the category was abandoned and the price reflected abandonment; YouArt sits at the literal opposite pole -- "Generative AI, Video, Creator Economy" is the three-tag combo I'd guess if you asked me to write the median Spring 2026 YC pitch deck blindfolded. The dossier discloses no round size, no price, no lead, no passes -- and silence on round dynamics in a category this hot operationalizes to "consensus until proven otherwise." YouArt's nearest substitute, Fable/Showrunner, already pulled Amazon Alexa Fund money in July 2025 (Variety), which means the late-stage validators have already declared the category live; the seed pricing downstream of that doesn't reset, it inflates. The diverging fact from the Solana analogy isn't the founder quality -- it's that Anatoly was selling a $0.05 coin nobody wanted, and YouArt is selling a SAFE in a sector every YC partner can pattern-match in eight seconds.

Capital structure is where YouArt fails me hardest, and the failure mechanism is exactly the one that killed a hundred 2022 creator-tool seeds: a generic SaaS-shaped SAFE on a business whose actual underlying asset is a person's network. The LOW rung pattern -- generic cap table that needs five rounds to reach default-alive while burning into third-party model APIs -- shows up directly in the YouArt dossier as "video agent orchestrating third-party models" (Product Hunt, Nov 2025), meaning every margin point flows through OpenAI/Google/Kling/Runway pricing decisions YouArt does not control. The Marina Mogilko deal worked because I refused the equity shape entirely and bought 5% of 30 years of her earnings; the YouArt-shaped instrument I'd actually want is a revenue-share on SOULSCAPE-network originals routed through Keith, not a standard preferred on a three-cofounder C-corp where the monetization rail competes with Patreon's $412M of accumulated lifetime capital.

On founder conviction, the mechanism that matters at 18 months is whether one of these three founders is *missionary* about AI cinema specifically versus *opportunistic* about an AI category that is presently funding-rich. Keith Zhang is the only one of the three who passes that test on dossier evidence -- SOULSCAPE summit, John Gaeta and Bruce Hunt partnerships, an actual film-festival posture (Hollywood Reporter, 2025). Edward Ma's headline credential is "TikTok Shop Ads 0?$100M" and Harry Zheng's is "ex-Google, ex-TikTok, ex-Alibaba" -- those are monetization-operator resumes, not film-people resumes, and the dossier flags that the same Speaking-AI / TikTok-Shop credential is *attributed to both* in public snippets, which is the kind of attribution slop that surfaces when the founders haven't done enough public writing to disambiguate themselves. No essays, no manifestos, no public take on why AI cinema fails the way YouArt thinks it fails -- the Antoine Buteau "writing is thinking" filter scores this team on Keith alone.

Counterfactual for founder-as-brand: for YouArt to flip from a 5/15 to a 10/15 here, Keith's SOULSCAPE community would have to be on the *cap table* of YouArt -- not partnered with it, not loosely networked -- such that creators routing originals through YouArt did so because they were already in Keith's orbit. 4,490 X followers on @YouArtStudio (joined Oct 2025) and a 4.7/35 Product Hunt rating is not owned distribution; it's launch-week noise. John Fish came to me with a YouTube subscriber base that *was* the company. The "JUNKY CREAM Original powered by YouArt" showcase is a single demo, not a pipeline, and the dossier's prior-employer logo wall (Google, ByteDance, OpenSea, BlueFocus, Tencent, Alibaba) is explicitly flagged as founder-employer brands, not customers -- which is the exact tell of a deck reaching for legibility it hasn't earned.

Category sleeve is the one place YouArt gets a partial pass parallel: the Slow Creator Fund (February 2025, $60M, Megan Lightcap) was built precisely to underwrite creator-economy infrastructure that doesn't fit traditional SaaS math. YouArt is half in that sleeve -- the fan-funding rail side -- and half in the sleeve I actively de-rate, which is "AI tool wrapping foundation models." Joe Lonsdale podcast Ep 113: "AI is just more internet, crypto is fundamentally disruptive." YouArt's video-agent layer is just more internet; the SOULSCAPE-anchored creator marketplace is the part that maps to a thesis I actually fund, and the company has welded the two together into a single SAFE I'd have to buy whole.

Thirty-six month forecast: YouArt does not survive in its current shape -- by mid-2029 it is either acqui-hired into Patreon or Runway for the team (most likely Patreon, because the monetization rail is the part that has commodity value), or it pivots hard into a SOULSCAPE-branded creator-marketplace under Keith with the video-agent stack deprecated, reaching $500K-$2M ARR as a niche AI-filmmaker community-and-funding layer. The mechanism is the squeeze: Fable/Showrunner consumes the production-quality narrative from above, Patreon adds a generation surface from the side, and YouArt's foundation-model dependency means it cannot win on either margin or capability. This forecast follows directly from the high/low signature -- Keith's network is the only asset that survives, and the SAFE cap table is the wrapper that kills the deal economics in the meantime.

The strongest bull case against me is the John Fish / Bookshelved precedent: the Slow Creator Fund exists *because* I publicly committed to the thesis that entrepreneurial creators with niche infrastructure businesses are venture-shaped, and Keith's SOULSCAPE -- Hollywood VFX partnerships, an annual summit, a cinema lab posture -- is a more defensible creator-supply asset than most of the named Creator Fund checks. If I let category dismissal ("AI video tool") override the underlying asset ("Keith's filmmaker network monetized through fan subscriptions"), I'm making the exact mistake I accused other VCs of making with Mogilko. The objection stings because it's directionally correct -- but the dossier dismantles it on instrument shape: this isn't a Keith-anchored creator deal, it's a three-cofounder C-corp SAFE where Keith is one of three equal principals and the headline product is a video agent, not a SOULSCAPE membership. I'd flip to Invest if Keith spun YouArt out as a creator-network holdco with a revenue-share instrument and Edward/Harry's monetization work re-shaped as a separate operating arm -- otherwise the round is consensus-priced exposure to a category I score down by default, and I pass.
