# Intelligence Factory -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Intelligence Factory is a credentialed-research robotics shop entering the single hottest YC category of 2026, and Yash Sinha and Jalaj Shukla's ETH-RSL-meets-UPenn-GRASP biographies are exactly the kind of pedigree-as-thesis profile that pattern-matches to replaceable operators in a market where Skild AI just raised $1.4B at $14B and Figure prints at $39B. The signature is *competent researchers, peak consensus category, nothing tactile to touch* -- and the dossier's single-page placeholder website labeled "Generalized Robotics Manipulation" tells me Intelligence Factory has not yet shipped an artifact I can scan, ride, or play.

Anduril's Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he pivoted to defense -- that is the shape of founder-surfaced-from-lived-experience I'm hunting for, and Intelligence Factory does not quite have it. Yash Sinha has real adjacency -- ETH Robotic Systems Lab, Sauber F1 systems work, prior CTO at Kaio Labs -- and Jalaj Shukla's UPenn GRASP work on multimodal manipulation policies is domain-deep. But neither founder has the autobiographical "I felt this problem on a factory floor / on a well pad / in my own home" moment. They surfaced the problem from a research lab, which is closer to the DeepMind exception than to the Luckey template -- and the DeepMind exception only fired for me because Hassabis was a chess-prodigy polymath compounding across neuroscience and game design. Intelligence Factory's founders are excellent within robotics; I don't see the cross-domain compounding that overrides the bias against pedigree.

Crusoe Energy is the canonical "magically weird" check -- burning North Dakota gas flares to mine Bitcoin sounded like three uninvestable things stapled together, and two or three credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds before I came in. Intelligence Factory is the *inverse* of that pattern. Robotic foundation models is the most-bid category in venture in 2026: Skild AI at $14B with $30M revenue, Physical Intelligence at $5.6B, Figure at $39B, 1X reportedly at $10B, RoboForce just took $52M in March. Intelligence Factory is entering this lane at pre-seed with Comma Capital and Allen Gannett -- a respectable but unremarkable cap table for the category. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus, and Intelligence Factory is buying into the consensus at the moment of peak narrative.

Mechanism: tactile and force-feedback data is a real wedge against vision-language pretraining, and if Intelligence Factory's custom data-collection device produces a dataset class incumbents lack, that compounds. But compounding over 18 months requires throughput -- human-demonstration imitation learning is historically bottlenecked by operator scaling, and Intelligence Factory has 5 people and one open Founding Engineer: Manipulation role at $80K-$200K plus 0.10%-5.00% equity. Skild can hire 50 of those tomorrow and reproduce the rig. The tactile-data moat is real for a quarter and porous by Series A.

Counterfactual on Single CEO authority: Intelligence Factory has clean title separation -- Sinha as CEO, Shukla as CTO, no co-CEO language -- which is the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll feud I am always replaying. To flip this dimension into the top tier, I would need a documented founder-vesting and tiebreaker protocol, and the dossier does not surface one. Two-founder, role-delineated, no documented operating agreement is the median pre-seed posture; I score it at the default-plus-clarity, not the ceiling.

Single-anchor parallel on capital intensity: Anduril is the right comparison for "frontier hardware with a real moat" -- Palmer Luckey was building physical drones with regulatory and capital walls compounding over years. Intelligence Factory has hardware-flavored work (the custom data-collection device) but the end product is a software policy licensed to OEMs, which is closer to the Physical Intelligence model. That is frontier-adjacent, not frontier-anchored. The capital intensity is real enough to keep tourists out and shallow enough that the $14B incumbent can replicate it.

Physical-World Verifiability is where Intelligence Factory fails my hand-test outright. Niantic I sourced because I had been playing Ingress obsessively since 2012; Flock Safety I sourced from the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons. Intelligence Factory's public surface is a single-page website and a YC company page -- no demo video, no benchmark, no model release, no deployment. I cannot install it, photograph it, or hand it to a friend. The custom data-collection device exists in a search snippet describing Jalaj Shukla and Julien Stocker's work; it does not exist anywhere I can observe. Until there is a tactile demonstration I can watch, Intelligence Factory is a research thesis I am asked to believe on credentials.

36-month forecast: Intelligence Factory gets acqui-hired by Skild AI or Physical Intelligence at a flat-to-modest markup for the tactile-data pipeline and the two founders, $0-$2M ARR, no standalone outcome -- the mechanism is that the consensus incumbents have the capital and the compute to absorb the tactile wedge before Intelligence Factory can build distribution, and Sinha and Shukla are exactly the engineering hires those incumbents would write a check to land.

The strongest opposing case is the DeepMind exception: tactile is genuinely the missing modality in VLA models, and Shukla's GRASP Lab work on multimodal manipulation policies could be the technical secret that the vision-language incumbents structurally cannot replicate because their data pipelines are video-pretrained. If Intelligence Factory cracks scaling laws for contact-rich manipulation before Skild pivots, the asymmetry inverts. The reason this case doesn't win the check today: the DeepMind exception required Hassabis's polymath cross-domain trajectory, and Intelligence Factory's founders are deep-within-robotics rather than compounding across disciplines -- which means a polymath generalist at Skild or Physical Intelligence could plausibly rebuild the tactile pipeline in a quarter. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a public demonstration of a contact-rich manipulation task (deformable-object handling, precision insertion, bimanual assembly) generalizing zero-shot to a held-out object class, with a video I can watch and a benchmark I can read. Until then, pass with warmth -- I would rather wait for the tactile-data thesis to either prove itself in the wild or get bought, and write the check into a category where the consensus has not already priced the outcome.
