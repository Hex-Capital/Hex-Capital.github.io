# PerfectBit, Inc. -- Elad Gil Evaluation

**PerfectBit, Inc.** is a credentialed Meta-research spin-out where the highest signal is *founders who actually shipped frontier models* (Vajda on Emu and Movie Gen, Yamamoto on Core Llama) and the lowest signal is *zero disclosed revenue velocity and zero customer logos* against a buyer set whose largest member just spent $14B locking in the incumbent vendor. That high/low pairing is the entire story: PerfectBit, Inc. is two of the most domain-loaded founders I'll see this batch, selling into the exact six rooms they used to sit inside, with no evidence yet that those rooms want to buy rather than rebuild.

**Péter Vajda** ran Director of Media Generation at Meta for eleven years and **Seiji Yamamoto** was Senior Staff on Core Llama for nine — that is a stronger shipped-artifact list than most of what crosses my desk, and it puts PerfectBit, Inc. above the "ex-FAANG with no named products" floor. But the **Perplexity** (2022) template I keep returning to wasn't pedigree — it was Aravind pinging me on LinkedIn and showing up a week later with the thing we'd discussed *built*, independently, outside the org that paid him. The PerfectBit, Inc. dossier shows zero GitHub, zero Twitter, zero public artifacts from either founder outside Meta's walls, and 0 job postings — Emu and Movie Gen are real shipped products but they were shipped *with* Meta's distribution, GPUs, and data pipeline. I don't yet know what Vajda and Yamamoto look like when the Meta scaffolding is gone, and that's the gap between a strong score here and a Perplexity-grade one.

The pilot-program framing on perfectbit.ai with no customer logos, no dataset volume, no dollar figures, and 0 job postings is the exact "AI company with no growth curve" pattern I called out on Tim Ferriss #863 — *something's fundamentally broken or it just hasn't started yet, and at pre-seed I can't tell which, but the dossier silence costs them either way.* Over an 18-month window the failure mode compounds mechanically: foundation labs run 60-90 day pilots, verifier-data quality is hard to A/B against human-labeled baselines, and if PerfectBit, Inc. doesn't convert a pilot to a six-figure contract by month nine the labs default back to Scale and Surge because the procurement relationship is already there. Real revenue velocity is easy to disclose; the absence is itself the signal.

The 24-month "catalyst" PerfectBit, Inc. would point to is Meta's $14B Scale AI investment and the reported TBD Labs complaints about Scale data quality — and I want to be careful here, because that's an incumbent-disruption event, not a category re-opening on the **Anduril** (2022) / **Stripe** (2010) template. Defense post-Ukraine opened a window where new buyers (DoD software procurement) suddenly *needed* a new layer. The Meta-Scale entanglement is the opposite: it concentrates the largest training-data buyer *into* the incumbent vendor and gives OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google reason to diversify *but also* reason to build internally rather than trust a two-person YC team with their pretraining mix. The catalyst is real but it cuts both ways, and that's not what a clean re-opening looks like.

PerfectBit, Inc. fails the index test the way **Lyft** failed it for me in Series C — not on network effects, but on buyer geometry. **Stripe** (2010) is an index on e-commerce because every new merchant needs payments rails; PerfectBit, Inc. is selling into a market of roughly six foundation labs, every one of which has an in-house data team and three of which (Meta, Google, Anthropic) have publicly disclosed verifier-grounded data research programs. The "basket of every cryptocurrency" architecture that made **Coinbase** (2013) work doesn't exist here — PerfectBit, Inc. has to *beat* Scale ($870M revenue 2024), Surge (>$1B revenue 2024), Mercor (~300K experts), and Micro1 ($500M valuation) on a procurement axis where those incumbents already have the labs relationships and can fund their own verifier-pipeline R&D the moment PerfectBit, Inc. proves the wedge.

The single-miracle math is where PerfectBit, Inc. concerns me most, and it's the dimension that maps directly onto **Color Genomics** (2015) — the lesson there was *workflow infrastructure on top of a hard layer*, not a bet on the hard layer itself. PerfectBit, Inc. needs three independent things to land: (a) formal-proof / simulator verifiers have to generalize beyond math, code, and physics into the breadth of pretraining tasks, which no public research has demonstrated; (b) labs have to prefer machine-verified data over human-labeled data at unit prices the labs find compelling; (c) PerfectBit, Inc. has to out-iterate Surge and Scale's verifier teams, which are now funded at >$1B revenue scale. Compound those probabilities and you don't get a single-miracle deal — you get the kind of multi-miracle chain I pass on by reflex.

My 36-month forecast: PerfectBit, Inc. gets acqui-hired by Anthropic, Google DeepMind, or Meta itself for $40-120M as a verifier-data tuck-in by end of 2028, with Vajda and Yamamoto returning to a labs research role rather than scaling an independent revenue line. The mechanism is exactly the founder/buyer geometry — the same labs that are PerfectBit, Inc.'s only viable customers are also the natural acquirers, and once one of them runs a serious pilot the cheaper path is to absorb the team and the verifier library rather than write recurring eight-figure contracts to a two-person company. That's the dominant outcome for "ex-Meta-research founders sell verifier infra to ex-colleagues" deals; it is not catastrophic, but it is not the kind of compounding outcome that earns a pre-seed angel check from me.

The strongest bull case against my pass is that Vajda and Yamamoto know the *exact* data deficiencies that hallucinate Llama and Emu, that verifier-grounded data for reasoning is the single largest unsolved bottleneck in 2026 frontier training, and that if PerfectBit, Inc. ships the verifier library Anthropic uses as default for math/code/RL post-training they become structural Scale-replacement infrastructure on a **Stripe**-grade index position. That bull case stings, because the founder-market fit on PerfectBit, Inc. is genuinely top decile for this batch. What kills it is the same fact that powers it: Meta just paid $14B to own that infrastructure, which is the loudest possible signal that the labs intend to *internalize* this layer rather than rent it from a YC company. I'll flip my verdict if PerfectBit, Inc. discloses a signed paid pilot with OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google DeepMind at >$500K contract value within the next nine months — that single fact would change the buyer-geometry math and I'd write the check at the next round.
