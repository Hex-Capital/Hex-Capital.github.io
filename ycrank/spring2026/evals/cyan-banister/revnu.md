# Revnu -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

George Jefferson bootstrapped three software businesses to €100K+ during university — artomate.app hit 1K MRR within 24 hours, resold.app served 1,000+ paying users and transacted over €10M on Vinted, and he generated over €100,000 selling AI posters on Etsy. Art Freebrey, 21 years old, won the Mistral worldwide hackathon and specializes in reverse engineering antibots. These two shipped products from a university bedroom and earned their way to San Francisco. I respect the hustle — I built my first real company from nothing too, and I know what it looks like when someone figures out how to make money before they have permission. But when I ask my first question — tell me your story, not the business — what I hear is "we're prolific builders who noticed that growth was the hard part." That's a market observation from smart young people, not a biographical crucible. The artomate and resold.app products are arbitrage tools that exploit marketplace mechanics for sellers. Revnu grew out of friction they experienced, not a system that broke them. I need to see the founder who would be working on this problem even if no one funded them, and the dossier gives me builders who are very good at finding the next product to ship.

Revnu enters the AI marketing automation space at a moment when Jasper has raised $131M and serves 100K customers, Gumloop just closed a $50M Series B in March 2026, Albert.ai was acquired after raising $70.4M, and every enterprise marketing platform from HubSpot to Braze is embedding autonomous AI agent capabilities into existing products. This is the opposite of a space that makes sophisticated investors shift in their chairs. Nobody at a dinner party would flinch if you said "I'm building AI agents for marketing." When I wrote my check into SpaceX, rockets were blowing up on launch pads. When I backed Anduril, defense tech was politically toxic in my world. When I championed Contraline, male birth control made VCs physically uncomfortable. Revnu requires no courage to articulate. The competitive landscape confirms it: this is consensus territory, heavily funded, with well-capitalized incumbents adding identical capabilities to platforms that already have distribution. The dossier itself notes that Braze acquired OfferFit specifically for agentic marketing decisioning.

Revnu's value proposition — autonomous AI agents executing across SEO, paid ads, cold email, A/B testing, and competitor research — solves a real problem of time and coordination for technical founders. But marketing is already one of the most measured, transparent industries on earth. Founders know what an ad costs, what a conversion rate means, what SEO does. There is no taxi medallion cartel here, no credit card opacity that Affirm exposed, no property tax system that Ownwell made visible. When I invested in Carta, it attacked the genuine mystery of cap table management — founders literally didn't know what their equity was worth, and the system was designed to keep it that way. Revnu automates known activities across known channels. The problem is effort, not opacity. I lived inside systems that exploited information asymmetry when I was fifteen and had no power — I see those hidden structures everywhere, and I don't see one beneath Revnu's surface.

The bull case is real, and I want to give it full weight: George and Art have $15M in transactions processed across prior ventures, genuine paying customers, hackathon wins (2026 Grokathon xAI winner, 2025 London Cursor Hackathon winner), and a code-native distribution mechanism — connect your GitHub repo, review one PR, merge — that is genuinely clever for developer-native adoption. Tom Blomfield took them into YC, which carries signal. The cross-channel intelligence layer, where data from SEO informs ad spend which informs outreach targeting, could theoretically create switching costs as optimization history accumulates. If Revnu aggregated performance data across hundreds of founder accounts, the data flywheel might become infrastructure. I checked my known exceptions: RTFKT overrode weak defensibility because of cultural prescience at a narrow timing window, and Superplastic overrode the contrarian criterion because it was genuinely genre-defying weird. Neither applies — Revnu's timing window is wide (every AI company is doing this simultaneously), and the product is straightforward, not magically weird.

Revnu's defensibility at this stage is the orchestration layer across channels, but the dossier states clearly that "the underlying capabilities (LLM-generated content, programmatic ad management, email automation) are individually available via APIs and existing tools" and that "multiple startups (NoimosAI, Fiber AI) are pursuing adjacent approaches." The brand name collision — with Revnu Consultancy in the UK, revnu.io in India, revnu.ai as a Google Ads tool — is particularly ironic for a company whose product includes SEO services. On cascading impact, Revnu serves technical founders at pre-seed and seed stage, which is a relatively privileged population. Helping small software companies grow faster has some second-order value, but it's not structurally expanding access to something previously gatekept from underserved communities the way Wonderschool expanded childcare access or Thumbtack connected people to affordable services.

One verifiable condition would flip me to Invest: if George or Art showed me that their prior products generated proprietary optimization data — from the 25,000 monthly Etsy posts through artomate, from the €10M transacted through resold.app — that gave Revnu a structural insight into growth mechanics that no competitor has, and that this data was already powering Revnu's cross-channel decisions in a way that compounds with each new customer. That would change the defensibility score and give me the infrastructure-beneath-the-surface story I need. Without it, Revnu is talented builders in a consensus market without a hidden system to disrupt, and that's not a Long Journey bet.
