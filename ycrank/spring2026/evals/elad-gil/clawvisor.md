# Clawvisor -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Eric Levine's resume is the most interesting thing in the Clawvisor dossier, and the timing of the market is the least interesting -- that pairing is the signature. A founder who already shipped Trust & Safety at Airbnb (2012-2017), built Berbix, and exited to Socure for ~$70M in June 2023 is exactly the operator profile I trust to navigate identity infrastructure. The problem is that Clawvisor is showing up to a market that Lightspeed, Laude, USV, and Auth0 already see clearly -- Composio raised $25M Series A in July 2025, Arcade raised $12M seed in March 2025, Anon raised $6.5M in April 2024, Auth0 shipped Token Vault for AI Agents. The window I look for -- structural shift the consensus has missed -- is closed. Clawvisor is a clean execution bet on a category where the timing alpha has already been priced in.

Nonobvious Market Timing is where Clawvisor sits lowest, and the ladder makes the gap obvious. Perplexity is the high rung -- I wrote the seed before ChatGPT launched because I was reading the GPT capability curve myself, not the cap tables of other agentic-search startups. Consumer social apps are the low rung -- no exogenous shift, just an application-layer bet on a stable platform. Clawvisor sits between Harvey (mid -- a real GPT-4 threshold unlocking a stuck market) and the consumer-social floor, closer to Harvey but on the wrong side of the timestamp. The structural shift Clawvisor names -- MCP standardization, agents calling Gmail/Slack/Stripe APIs at scale -- is real, but it is consensus. Composio at $29M total and Nango at 700+ APIs are the timestamp evidence. When the dossier itself has to cite Auth0, WorkOS FGA, and HashiCorp Vault all shipping agent-auth modules in the same paragraph, you're not early -- you're racing.

Infrastructure-Layer Positioning is where Clawvisor sits highest, and this is the one dimension that keeps me from passing immediately. Stripe is the high rung -- the payments API that an entire generation of internet businesses compiled into their stack. Wish is the low rung -- a consumer marketplace with no API layer, no B2B revenue, and the 99% post-IPO collapse to prove it. Gusto is the mid rung -- boring critical infrastructure, sticky workflow embedding, but more application than platform. Clawvisor sits between Stripe and Gusto on architectural intent: a Go gateway that vaults credentials, enforces purpose-based per-call authorization, and writes the audit trail. That is infrastructure positioning, not application positioning. The pin holding Clawvisor below Stripe is adapter breadth -- 14+ shipped integrations against Composio's 500+ and Nango's 700+. The gateway architecture is right; the catalog isn't there yet, and catalog is what creates the compounding switch cost in this layer.

Single-Miracle Viability is the dimension Clawvisor passes most cleanly. Harvey is the high rung -- the technology miracle was happening exogenously on the foundation-model curve, and law firms were already proven buyers. The drug-development comparison is the low rung -- tech AND FDA AND reimbursement compounding to near-zero odds. Clawvisor needs exactly one thing: enterprises to adopt a dedicated agent-auth gateway rather than absorbing the function into Auth0 or WorkOS. That is a distribution miracle, not a tech miracle. The product works -- the public Go repo at 86 stars demonstrates the gateway exists. The miracle is winning the buyer.

Operator-Scaler Founder Profile is where Levine carries Clawvisor. Parker Conrad rebuilding Rippling after Zenefits is the high rung -- operator with explicit scaling scars. First-time founders with no scaling experience are the low rung. Levine sits closer to the Conrad end: Airbnb Trust & Safety during hypergrowth (2012-2017), CEO of Berbix through a $70M exit to Socure, post-acquisition SVP running DocV at Socure, YC Visiting Group Partner with Garry Tan as the listed Group Partner on the Clawvisor YC page. The pin keeping Clawvisor below the high rung is solo-founder bandwidth at team size 1 with hiring=false, against Composio and Arcade fielding multi-engineer teams. Margin Architecture has no evidence -- no pricing page, free trial, open-core repo -- so I apply the default. The gateway-and-audit-trail framing suggests usage-based pricing that would scale with agent call volume, which is the right "selling units of cognition" architecture, but until there's a pricing surface there's no signal.

My 36-month forecast: Clawvisor lands at sub-$5M ARR and gets acqui-hired by Auth0/Okta or WorkOS for $30-80M, with Levine running an "AI Agent Identity" product line at the acquirer. The mechanism is the signature -- single-miracle viability is favorable, the founder is a known quantity to identity acquirers (Berbix-to-Socure is the template), but the category timing belongs to companies with bigger war chests and bigger adapter catalogs. Levine's prior exit pattern is the tell -- this is a founder who builds clean identity infrastructure and sells it to a larger identity platform, which is exactly the playbook the Berbix/Socure outcome scripts.

The strongest case against my forecast is the Anduril override: when a structural shift is generational, multi-miracle risk gets absorbed by the market opening, and a competent founder with the right architectural intent can win even from behind. The bull would say Clawvisor's purpose-based per-call enforcement is architecturally distinct from Composio's integration-first framing or Auth0's identity-first framing -- that the gateway-with-audit-trail layer becomes the compliance system of record for agent actions the way Vault became the secrets system of record. Levine's Trust & Safety and identity-verification background makes him uniquely positioned to build that audit corpus into a defensible policy/risk-scoring moat over time. That sting matters. What dismantles it is the consensus problem: Anduril worked because defense modernization was a generational structural shift bipartisan investors had ignored, not a category where Lightspeed, USV, and Okta were already deployed. The agent-auth market does not have an Anduril-shaped opening. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is Clawvisor landing two named Fortune 500 enterprise design partners on a paid contract within 12 months -- that would prove the buyer prefers a dedicated gateway over the incumbent identity-platform module, which is the only outcome that makes the structural-timing critique wrong.
