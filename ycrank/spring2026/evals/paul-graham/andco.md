# Andco -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Andco is the profile I see most often after a recent acquisition: Ryan Xue and Michael Lemm sold Layup (YC W23) to Whatfix in November 2025, and three months later Andco launched into personal injury legal AI — a category where EvenUp is already at a $2B valuation. That tempo tells me the founders' learning rate is real but their problem discovery is suspicious. The dimensional signature here is execution-rich, discovery-poor: a repeat YC team that has clearly shipped, attacking a consensus market they did not live inside.

The dimension where Andco places highest is Founder Resourcefulness, and even there the placement is honest rather than enthusiastic. The high rung on this ladder is Airbnb — Brian and Joe selling $40 cereal boxes when nobody would fund them; the low rung is the McKinsey-consultant duo who "identified an opportunity" in B2B workflow automation. Andco sits closer to the middle, anchored by the Layup→Whatfix exit. That outcome is not nothing — Xue and Lemm shipped a YC company through to acquisition, which puts them above founders who have only ever built decks. But the dossier offers no cereal-box story: no anecdote of them personally calling Geico claims lines until they understood the phone tree, no weekend prototype that converted a skeptical PI partner. Repeat-founder credentialing is a different muscle than relentless resourcefulness, and Andco shows me the first without the second.

The dimension where Andco places lowest is Idea That Repels Before It Attracts, and this is where my Black Swan instincts start firing in the wrong direction. The high rung on this ladder is Stripe — payments was a regulatory schlep that repelled every developer who looked at it; the low rung is anything that "could appear unchanged in a TechCrunch trend piece." AI for personal injury workups, in May 2026, is the trend piece. EvenUp ($385M raised, doubling ARR), Supio ($91M raised, 4x ARR growth), Eve ($47M Series A, 5x revenue) — the consensus has already arrived, the capital has already been deployed, the returns have already been competed for. Andco's one redemption on this axis is genuinely schleppy: fax retries, insurer phone trees, UM/UIM coverage discovery across Geico and Progressive — that is the kind of work EvenUp's demand-letter focus actively avoids. So Andco sits above the McKinsey rung but well below Stripe: there is real schlep here, but it is schlep inside a consensus category, which is the worst combination because incumbents with $385M can hire engineers to do unpleasant things.

On Organic Problem Discovery, the high rung is Dropbox — Drew Houston kept forgetting his USB drive — and the low rung is again the consultant pair brainstorming workflow automation. Michael Lemm is a Harvard Law JD with Kirkland & Ellis on his resume, and that is the only thread pulling Andco up the ladder; but Kirkland is corporate-defense and M&A litigation, not plaintiff personal injury work. The dossier contains no founder narrative explaining why PI specifically, no specific moment where one of them watched a paralegal lose a day to fax retries. Andco places below the middle — the Layup→PI pivot in three months reads as market-spotted, not pain-discovered. On Founder Learning Rate, the comparable rungs are Patrick Collison going Auctomatic→Stripe (mid) and Sam Altman compounding from Loopt (high); Andco's W23→exit→new launch tempo puts them above flat founders but the cycle is too fresh to verify slope, so they place mid. On Earnest Builder Identity, against Watsi at the top and the buzzword-heavy resume-stackers at the bottom, Andco's Cornell/Harvard/McKinsey/Google-fellow stack and the co-founders' continuing advisory roles at Whatfix tilt toward the middle — competent and credentialed, but not visibly obsessed with the problem itself.

My 36-month forecast: Andco gets acquired between $25M and $80M by Filevine, Clio, or one of the well-funded incumbents (most likely Supio or EvenUp bolting on multi-channel intake), reaching perhaps $3-8M ARR before the deal. The mechanism is straightforward — the same Layup→Whatfix pattern that produced these founders' last outcome will produce this one, because consensus categories with three unicorn-track incumbents do not leave room for a fourth, but they do leave room for a clean tuck-in by a buyer who needs the pre-lit workflow piece. That is a fine outcome. It is not the outcome I write a check from my own money for.

The bull case worth steel-manning: Lemm is an actual lawyer, not a McKinsey tourist — Harvard Law and a Kirkland practice means Andco has a founder who can sit across from a managing partner at a PI firm and not be condescended to, and the multi-channel agent work (fax, voice, insurer phone trees) is genuinely the kind of schlep EvenUp's document-centric product avoids. ClassDojo is the Known Exception that nags me here: I broke my "hackers building for hackers" pattern for ClassDojo because the schlep blindness in K-12 procurement was real and the founders' execution was exceptional. Could Andco be the legal-tech version? It could. But ClassDojo's founders had spent years inside classrooms; Lemm spent his years inside corporate defense, not plaintiff PI intake. The Layup→Andco pivot was three months, which is pattern-matching to opportunity, not living in the future and building what was missing. The dossier evidence that wins the argument is the absence — no customer names, no revenue figures, no founder origin story tying them to a paralegal's bad day. The single condition that would flip my verdict: a credible story from Lemm explaining which specific PI partner or paralegal he watched lose a day to fax retries, paired with two named firms paying real money. Without that, Andco is a competent acqui-hire candidate, not an angel bet.
