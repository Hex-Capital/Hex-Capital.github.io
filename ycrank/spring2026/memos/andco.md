## The Idea

**Problem:** Plaintiff personal injury (PI) firms perform pre-litigation "case workups" — pulling police/accident reports, opening claims with insurance carriers, identifying UM/UIM coverage, requesting medical records and bills, and discovering treating providers (useandco.com). These tasks are coordinated across fax, mail, web portals, email, SMS and voice channels (YC company page). Today firms staff paralegals/case managers to manually drive these workflows; PI firms compete on case volume per headcount.

**Approach:** AI agents that run pre-litigation workups across all firm channels in parallel — filing/retrieving accident reports, opening claims with carriers like Geico and Progressive, identifying UM/UIM coverage, requesting billing and medical records, and discovering providers (Fundreef Substack via search snippet; useandco.com). Includes a client self-serve portal for document uploads and integrates with case management systems (Filevine listed as compatible) (useandco.com).

**Differentiation:** Positions across the full pre-litigation workflow rather than a single artifact. Direct competitors specialize: EvenUp on demand letters/case valuation, Supio on medical-record analysis, Eve on case lifecycle drafting (proplaintiff.ai; supio.com/supio-vs-eve; supio.com/supio-vs-evenup). [Inferred]: Andco's positioning emphasizes multi-channel agentic execution (fax, voice, SMS, mail, web) versus document-centric AI, based on its YC description and homepage feature list.

**Business Model:** No pricing disclosed on website (useandco.com). Homepage frames value as "no limits on cases handled without additional headcount" and quantifies "24+ hours saved per case" and "3x faster attorney efficiency" (useandco.com). [Inferred]: Most likely monetization is per-firm/per-seat or per-case SaaS based on legal-tech category norms (EvenUp, Supio, Eve all subscription-based per LawNext/TechCrunch coverage).

**TAM/SAM:** EvenUp positions the U.S. personal injury space at "$61 billion" (Fortune, Oct 2025). No public SAM estimate found for AI-driven PI case workup tooling specifically.

**GTM / Distribution:** YC blurb says product is "already live with many high volume firms across the country, handling hundreds of cases and thousands of requests each month" (Fundreef Substack via search snippet). Website CTA is "Book a Demo" (useandco.com). [Inferred]: Direct sales to mid/high-volume PI firms with Filevine integration as channel hook, based on the integration mention and demo-led funnel.

- **Workflow data + integrations:** [Inferred]: Repeated agent runs across carriers (Geico, Progressive) and providers create proprietary playbooks (carrier-specific claim filing patterns, provider intake quirks) — unproven moat at this stage.
- **Switching costs:** [Inferred]: Filevine CMS integration and self-serve client portal increase embed depth once a firm routes intake through Andco; not yet validated by retention disclosures.
- **No public IP, patents, regulatory licensing, or proprietary data assets disclosed in any source surveyed.**

**Market structure:** No structural barrier identified at this stage. Competitors EvenUp ($385M raised, $2B valuation per Fortune Oct 2025), Supio ($91M raised per TechCrunch Apr 2025), and Eve ($47M Series A per a16z Jan 2025) all have substantial capital to extend into pre-litigation workup adjacent to their current focus.

**Commoditization risk:** [Inferred]: Multi-channel agent orchestration over fax/voice/email is technically buildable by funded incumbents (EvenUp, Supio, Eve) and by horizontal AI agent platforms; defensibility hinges on speed-to-market and customer lock-in via case management embedding.

**Well-funded, faster-scaling incumbents in same niche:** EvenUp ($385M raised, $2B valuation, 2,000+ firms — Fortune Oct 2025), Supio ($91M raised, 4x ARR growth — TechCrunch Apr 2025), and Eve ($47M Series A — Fortune Jan 2025) are all doubling/quadrupling annually and have product/sales footprint to bundle pre-lit workup. Mitigation: founders' Layup→Whatfix exit demonstrates execution and exit relationships (whatfix.com newsroom).

**Voice/fax agent reliability for legal-grade workflows:** Multi-channel agents must reliably handle insurer phone trees, fax retries, and medical-record portal idiosyncrasies; failure modes carry legal/malpractice consequences. No public benchmarks or accuracy metrics disclosed (useandco.com). [Inferred]: This is a technical feasibility risk distinct from market risk.
