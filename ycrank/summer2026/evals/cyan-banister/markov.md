# Markov -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Markov is two India-origin operators -- Harish Ashok and Dev Mandal -- selling computer-use RL data into the exact buyer set already being courted by Surge AI ($1.2B 2024 revenue), Mercor ($10B valuation), Scale AI ($30B with Meta as 49% holder), Applied Compute, Micro1, Fleet, Habitat, and Matrices. That is eight named, capitalized competitors in a single dossier paragraph. The signature reads: clean enough governance, conventional enough pedigree, slotting into the most-crowded RL-environments race in YC S26. This is precisely the silhouette of my Pass Example -- the consensus AI darling -- only earlier and thinner.

Crusoe (~2018) is the HIGH rung on the "magically weird" axis I keep coming back to: burn flared gas in North Dakota to mine Bitcoin, a sentence three credible VCs passed on for aesthetic reasons before I wrote the check. Markov's pitch is the inverse: "Scale AI for robotics, with a human fleet in India that can capture thousands of hours of data per day" (X/@DevvMandal). That is not weird -- that is Scale AI's 2016 origin story re-narrated in 2026, with the same offshore-labor arbitrage and the same buyer list (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Meta). Crusoe worked because the category was empty and the founder was *of* the place. Markov is entering a category where the dossier itself names eight funded entrants, including one already at $1.2B in revenue selling the adjacent SKU. There is no rebuttal here, only a hard no on the non-consensus filter.

HQ Trivia (2017) sits at the LOW rung of my governance ladder because the Yusupov-Kroll partnership feud literally hollowed out the company by 2020. The failure mechanism was unresolved tiebreaker authority dressed up as creative partnership. Does Markov carry that pattern? The dossier labels Harish Ashok "Founder" and Dev Mandal "Co-Founder" -- a quiet hierarchy that reads cleaner than 50/50, and unlike HQ neither founder has a public-facing personality conflict with the other. The 17-year-old Founder title is unusual but it is not ambiguous. This is the one dimension where Markov gets to a respectable middle of the range; the HQ failure mode does not appear to be loaded into Markov's cap table at the seed.

Founder-market fit for Markov compounds badly over 18 months, not well. Dev Mandal at Sarvam AI touched data pipelines at an Indian foundation-model lab, and Harish Ashok built a voice agent for hardware workflows at Zenith -- both adjacent, neither autobiographical to the problem of *sourcing computer-use tasks for American frontier labs procuring through enterprise contracts.* The Palmer Luckey / Anduril (2017) test is whether the founder shipped a comparable artifact to scale in an adjacent domain before the current company; Markov's two founders shipped student-stage projects in a different geography and a different buyer-context. Eighteen months in, the absence of prior lab-procurement relationships will show up as elongated sales cycles against Surge's existing Anthropic and OpenAI account managers.

For the capital-intensity dimension to flip Markov a tier, the "human fleet in India that can capture thousands of hours of data per day" would need to be a verifiable, on-the-ground operation -- a Bangalore or Chennai facility with named operator headcount, recurring weekly throughput, and an exclusive vendor contract with at least one frontier lab. Markov's dossier supplies one X post and zero photographs, headcounts, or facility addresses. The Crusoe parallel only loads if I can walk onto the well pad; here the well pad is alleged via a tweet. Without that physical substrate, Markov is software-services pretending to operational depth, which is the lane Mercor already won at $10B.

Flock Safety (~2018) is my single anchor on physical-world verifiability -- I sourced it from reading the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons cafe, then watched the cameras appear on actual telephone poles in actual neighborhoods. Markov offers markovstudios.com (landing copy plus a cal.com/dev-markov/30min booking link) and an X account with 1,122 followers. There is no dataset I can download, no environment I can step into, no operator I can shadow. The product exists as a B2B promise to four buyers I will never personally observe. This is the developer-tools-abstraction floor of the rubric.

My 36-month forecast for Markov: acqui-hired by Surge AI or Mercor for under $30M in talent-value paper by mid-2028, with Harish Ashok and Dev Mandal absorbed as engineering hires into the acquirer's RL-environments team. The mechanism: Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, and Meta will consolidate RL-data spend among two or three incumbent vendors who already hold security reviews and master service agreements, and the long tail of YC S26 entrants in this category -- Markov among them -- will be rolled up rather than displaced through frontal competition. The "human fleet in India" claim is the asset being acquired, not the company.

The strongest bull case against my forecast leans on the TechCrunch (Sep 2025) datapoint that Anthropic alone discussed $1B+ in RL-environment spend over the next year, and the Meta-Scale deal explicitly caused Google and OpenAI to redirect labeling spend away from concentrated incumbents -- a deliberate multi-sourcing wave that would reward a new India-anchored vendor with cost advantages. Even 0.5% capture of Anthropic's stated budget is $5M ARR within 18 months, well into Series A territory. The Known Exception I am checking is DeepMind (~2013), where Hassabis's polymath compounding overrode my bias against credentialed founders -- could Dev Mandal's Sarvam-plus-IIT trajectory be a polymath-in-progress signal? It cannot, because Hassabis had already won the Mind Sports Olympiad five times by his twenties; Mandal is a 2027 graduating undergrad with one prior internship. The bull case dies on the buyer-relationship gap: frontier-lab procurement is gated by named human relationships that Surge and Mercor own and Markov does not. **The verdict flips if Markov announces a named >$1M ACV contract with Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or Meta within 12 months of YC Demo Day** -- absent that, I am writing the check into Vigil Labs (August 2025) or the next Crusoe instead.
