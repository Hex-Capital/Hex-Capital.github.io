# Zomma -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Zomma is a company with a real 24-month catalyst underneath it and no revenue curve on top of it -- that pairing is the whole story. Anthropic shipped Computer Use in late 2024 and OpenAI shipped Operator in early 2025, which is a specific technological step-function that made vision-based GUI navigation of custodian portals feasible for the first time. That is the exact shape of catalyst I look for. But Zomma's dossier discloses zero customers, zero revenue, zero waitlist, and a "/call" demo CTA -- and in the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a signal because real growth is trivial to disclose.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on the catalyst dimension: Ukraine opened a defense software window in a 24-month period that peers misread as a graveyard, and the miracle was *software distribution* on top of existing autonomy stacks, not new physics. Zomma's Computer Use step-function is directionally the same kind of re-opening -- legacy custodian portals will never ship APIs, and vision-agent reliability just crossed the threshold that makes non-API automation tractable. Where Zomma diverges from the Anduril rung is that Anduril's catalyst was accompanied by a distribution position (forward-deployed software into DoD workflows Palantir had already carved) whereas Zomma's catalyst is being ridden simultaneously by Anthropic itself, which shipped 10 pre-built finance agent templates including KYC and month-end close in May 2026 and stood up a $1.5B JV with Goldman, Blackstone, and H&F -- the model vendor Zomma's site names as a supported agent is now the competing GTM.

Zomma has no revenue curve at all -- this is the low rung, and it's where "something's fundamentally broken" gets applied to AI companies that can't produce a number. **Harvey** (2023) is the counter-template: $3B to $5B to $8B in months, disclosed publicly because real velocity is easy to disclose. The Harvey failure mode I watch for at Zomma's stage is *founder narrative without curve* -- pitching regulated-buyer trust framework language ("SEC-ready audit trails," "scoped identity") in place of signed enterprise logos. Zomma's dossier does exactly this: the Agent Trust Framework is marketing copy without SOC 2, ISO, or a single named hedge fund. Regulated-buyer sales cycles are the standard excuse for why the curve isn't visible at month six, and Zomma's cycle profile makes that excuse structurally available -- which is precisely why I discount it.

Varun Arumugam is a prior YC founder with a stated exit and $10-12M in sales, and Cashel Fitzgerald was an ML engineer at Modern Intelligence with prior finance-software SWE work at Voyant. Zomma's shipping-rhythm signal is therefore *asymmetric* -- Arumugam has demonstrably shipped a company to revenue before, which is a real artifact and puts Zomma above the pedigree-only floor. What's missing is the **Perplexity** (2022) pattern -- Aravind pinging me on LinkedIn and showing up a week later with the thing we discussed built. Zomma's dossier lists no GitHub for either founder, no public Twitter, no shipped side-projects, no Product Hunt launch, no iterative artifacts I can point at between the demo and the raise. Prior exit is a lagging signal; between-meeting shipping is the leading one, and Zomma shows me only the lagging one.

On index position, Zomma is trying to sit in the workflow path of every hedge fund's custodian-portal operation -- the *shape* of an index bet on back-office automation. But the compounding category has two adjacent aggregators already collecting the rents: SS&C and iCapital-class fund administrators monetize managed services and have every incentive to ship self-serve agents that cannibalize their own outsourced-ops line before Zomma reaches them, and Anthropic's Blackstone/Goldman JV is now the default vendor conversation for exactly this buyer. Zomma is not the layer everyone must touch; Zomma is a wrapper competing with the model vendor's own wrappers and the fund administrator's own roadmap -- closer to Lyft's #2 dynamic than to Stripe's e-commerce index.

The single-miracle test at Zomma is cleaner than average -- it requires that computer-use agents operate legacy custodian portals reliably under human-in-the-loop approval, which is one validated capability and everything else is GTM. That would score well except the miracle is *contested by the miracle's own supplier*. Zomma's site names Claude as a supported agent while Anthropic ships the finance templates directly; if the miracle compresses into the model vendor's SKU, Zomma's wrapper needs a distribution wedge that isn't in the dossier.

My 36-month forecast on Zomma: acquihired by SS&C, iCapital, or Juniper Square for under $50M, or dead. The mechanism is direct -- Anthropic's finance agents and the Blackstone JV eat the horizontal KYC/close/reconciliation workflows, Zomma either pivots into a compliance-audit-packaging wedge or gets absorbed by a fund administrator that needs the SEC-audit-trail wrapper to defend its own ops-services revenue against agent commoditization. That is the "workflow layer squeezed between the model vendor and the incumbent aggregator" outcome my *AI Market Clarity* piece flags for exactly this configuration.

The bull case that stings: Arumugam's ex-JPMorgan network plus a prior YC exit is exactly the founder-graph access that turns "the regulated buyer won't sign a two-year-old startup" into three hedge-fund logos at $500K ACV in twelve months, at which point Zomma has the Harvey-shaped curve I said was missing. The Agent Trust Framework -- vendor risk review packaging, scoped identity, approval chains -- is precisely the wrapper Anthropic's raw templates cannot ship, because Anthropic sells cognition and Zomma sells the compliance envelope around it. The **Color Genomics** (2015) Known Exception rhymes: workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-a-hard-tech-layer, not a bet on the hard-tech layer itself. What dismantles this for me right now is that the dossier gives me a JV between the model vendor and Zomma's target buyers announced two months before Zomma listed on YC's directory -- Blackstone and Goldman aren't going to run vendor risk review on Zomma when Anthropic is already inside their walls. The verifiable condition that flips me to Invest: three named hedge fund or asset manager logos with disclosed ACVs above $250K, signed after May 2026, with month-over-month usage growth on those accounts. Absent that, Zomma is a competent team on the right catalyst in the wrong seat.
