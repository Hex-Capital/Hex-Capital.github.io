# Stage -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Stage is the archetype I described in my pass example almost line-for-line -- the "5x faster than GitHub" pitch on the YC page is a workflow-efficiency wedge, and the dimensional signature is unambiguous: Stage's highest-rung dimension is a thin founder-market-fit signal from Dean Stratakos having built an internal coding agent at Five Rings, while its lowest is consequence magnitude, where Charles Pan and Stratakos are explicitly anchoring success to a multiple-of-GitHub review-speed metric rather than collapsing an incumbent regime. That high-low pairing makes Stage a competent developer-tools optimization play, not a Khosla bet.

**OpenAI** (2019) sits at the top of my consequence ladder because the success-state was a civilizational rewrite of cognitive labor -- the $15T-of-GDP-is-labor frame I gave Fortune (2026-03-06) is downstream of that single check. Stage's success-state, by contrast, is reviewers stepping through "ordered chapters" of AI-generated diffs at 5x the speed of GitHub PR UX. No incumbent regime collapses if Stage wins; GitHub PR review gets a better skin, CodeRabbit's $15M+ ARR auto-comment loop gets a competing UX, and developers save some hours per week. That is the **DoorDash** (2013) shape minus the consumer-flow reshaping, and well short of the **Impossible Foods** (2011) "animal-protein production goes away" bar. The dossier never names a labor-cost figure being displaced, never names a system that disappears -- it names a UX metric being multiplied. That is the pass-example pattern from my framework, not the **OpenAI** (2019) pattern.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) is on my low rung for the inverse reason most people think -- not because it failed, but because the science risk was the moat, and when consensus VCs would not touch the capital-and-physics combination, the deal was mine to lead. Stage inverts that pattern: a 2-person team, a 204-star MIT-licensed TypeScript CLI on GitHub, a Spring 2026 YC pre-seed -- there is no physics here, no plant to build, no catalyst to invent. Any Tier-1 generalist, any solo angel, any YC alum can fund this. The repels-consensus filter is the single sharpest tool I have for separating my edge from the median check, and Stage fails it cleanly. CodeRabbit raising $60M at a $550M valuation (TechCrunch Sep 2025) and Cursor acquiring Graphite (TechCrunch Dec 2025) tells me the capability surface is already commodity-funded at materially larger scale; Stage has no science risk shielding it from that overhang.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus -- mechanism analysis over 18 months -- Stratakos's Stanford BS+MS CS, Apple Siri internship, Citadel TCP-recovery internship, and Five Rings AI-initiative lead is a paradigmatic domain-continuity resume; Pan's Stanford CS 2022 plus Yuzu Health plus Five Rings is the same shape. Neither has publicly contradicted their own field, neither has the "instigator" posture from my TED Talk on climate, and the Show HN framing "Putting humans back in control of code review" is a category-defensive crouch, not an instigation. Over 18 months this compounds badly: as CodeRabbit and Greptile ($25M Series A, Benchmark, SiliconANGLE Sep 2025) keep training auto-reviewer agents, Stage's "humans in the loop" framing becomes the conservative reading of the future of code review, not the contrarian one.

On learning rate -- counterfactual -- the dossier shows zero documented pivot for Stage, no public thesis update, no "we were wrong about X" moment from Pan or Stratakos. To flip this from default 7/15 into Slash-territory I would need a dossier line like "originally built an auto-reviewer agent, killed it after watching CodeRabbit's growth curve, pivoted to human-reviewer UX" -- that would be the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot shape that justified my $100M Series C. None of that is here. The company is weeks old at the Show HN launch (HN id 47796818, Oct 2025), which is forgivable; the absence of any prior-life pivot evidence from either founder is not.

On capital profile -- single-anchor parallel -- compare Stage to **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where I led the $115M Series A because the SPARC tokamak roadmap required deployment scale no generalist would underwrite. Stage's roadmap is a SaaS product with sign-in-gated tier pricing and a "LAUNCH" 25%-off promo, executable by a 2-person team on standard pre-seed economics. I am structurally over-resourced for this deal; any check I write here is a YC-batch favor, not a Khosla Ventures thesis bet. That matters because the $3.5B fund I raised in Feb 2025 (TechCrunch 2025-02-24) is sized for **Genesis AI** (2025) co-leads and **Waabi** (2026) follow-ons, not for $500K notes into capital-light developer tools.

My 36-month forecast for Stage: acquired in an acqui-hire range of $8-40M by GitHub, CodeRabbit, or Cursor -- most likely Cursor given the Graphite precedent (TechCrunch Dec 2025) -- or quietly dead, having failed to outpace CodeRabbit's ~20%/month ARR growth and Cursor's post-Graphite roadmap. The mechanism is straightforward: Stage's wedge is a UX layer over a diff, the underlying chapter-segmentation model trains on data any of the three incumbents has 100x more of, and the founders' YC P26 distribution is structurally smaller than CodeRabbit's $60M war chest. The signature -- founder-market-fit high, consequence low -- forecasts a respectable engineering exit, not a fund-returner, which is precisely the outcome shape I designed my framework to filter out.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Cursor paid real money for Graphite in Dec 2025, signaling that AI-native review primitives have strategic-acquirer pull at outsized multiples, and Stage's 204-star CLI plus YC P26 batch placement could compound into a $50-150M outcome on a $500K check -- a 100-300x angel return that would look great on any cap table. That sting is real. It loses to the dossier on two counts: first, I do not optimize my portfolio for $50-150M outcomes -- the $50M I put into **OpenAI** (2019) and the $115M Series A I led into **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) reveal a check-size discipline that makes the math of a Stage acqui-hire irrelevant to a $3.5B fund; second, the bull case requires Stage to out-execute CodeRabbit and Greptile on a feature surface they can ship in a quarter, and the dossier surfaces no proprietary stack, no data moat, no defied-expert moment that would shift that race. The single verifiable condition that would flip me: Stage demonstrating, within 12 months, that its chapter-segmentation models outperform CodeRabbit and Greptile on a public reviewer-comprehension benchmark by a margin that named Fortune-500 engineering orgs sign LOIs against -- absent that, this is a pass, and I move on.
