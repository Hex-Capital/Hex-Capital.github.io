# Hub.xyz -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Here's what jumps out immediately: Hub.xyz is the third or fourth entrant into a DePIN bandwidth-sharing-for-AI-data category that already has a clear frontrunner in Grass, sitting at roughly a billion-dollar valuation with $17.5M raised. Nodepay has 1.5M active users and $7M. There are over 600 DePIN projects in the wild. When I apply my most basic filter -- is this a sane person with an insane idea? -- the answer comes back inverted. The idea isn't insane at all. It's validated, funded, and crowded. And the founders, while educated (HEC Paris, Berkeley, École Polytechnique), don't bring the kind of deep infrastructure-building credibility that would make me believe they're the team to win a commodity race against better-capitalized competitors doing the same thing. This is not Brian Armstrong at Coinbase in 2012, where a credible engineer was pursuing something the entire market thought was delusional. This is a well-credentialed team chasing a category that every crypto fund already has a position in.

The structural moat question is where this really falls apart for me. My whole framework right now centers on one question: does this business get stronger or weaker as AI commoditizes software? Hub.xyz has a bizarre relationship with that question because the business doesn't just use AI -- it exists entirely because of AI demand. The "cherry on top" test is straightforward: would Hub.xyz be a good business if AI didn't exist? No. It wouldn't be a business at all. That's not disqualifying on its own -- I've backed protocol-layer crypto that depended on crypto adoption -- but the data they're collecting is from public internet sources, assembled through other people's bandwidth, which means the moat isn't proprietary data, it isn't physical infrastructure, it isn't a new cryptographic primitive. The dossier itself flags that "the underlying data is not proprietary." So what exactly is defensible here? API usability and data structuring quality? Those are features, not moats, and they're exactly the kind of software differentiation that dissolves fastest.

I care deeply about whether a company is creating a new primitive or just building an application on someone else's stack. My biggest crypto returns -- Solana, Aleo, Helium -- came from protocol-layer bets that established new foundational infrastructure. Hub.xyz runs smart contracts on Ethereum. It is, structurally, a DApp with a token attached. The "API for real-world training data" positioning sounds like infrastructure, but it's a service layer, not a protocol. Nobody is building their products on top of Hub.xyz as a standard. Compare this to what Anatoly Yakovenko was doing with Solana -- inventing a novel consensus mechanism that created an entirely new computational substrate. Hub.xyz is aggregating bandwidth through existing pipes and selling the output. That's an application, and applications on other people's protocols face a permanent ceiling on their compounding dynamics.

Let me steelman the bull case, because the market tailwind is real. AI training costs are escalating, centralized scraping is getting sued into oblivion, and distributed, user-consented data collection addresses a genuine regulatory gap. If Hub.xyz could establish itself as the enterprise API standard for ethically-sourced training data -- if the SwissBorg partnership's 1M+ KYC-verified community translates into a geographically diverse, legally clean data network -- there's a version of this that looks like picks-and-shovels infrastructure for the AI gold rush. The 500K user base, even if token-incentivized, represents real supply-side scale. And the "why now" around scraping litigation is not hand-waving -- it's a structural shift that favors this model. For this to be a great investment, you'd need to believe that (a) data structuring quality creates durable differentiation against Grass and Nodepay, (b) the team can execute the crypto-to-enterprise go-to-market transition that very few DePIN projects have pulled off, and (c) they can do all of this with $1.7M against competitors with ten times the capital. I don't see evidence for any of those three beliefs.

The co-founder situation adds another layer of concern. Léonie Nguyen, listed as co-founder and CBO, was appointed CEO of Meey Group -- a Vietnamese proptech company -- in October 2025. She's not on the YC page. At a ten-person company entering YC, a co-founder departure or divided attention is not a detail. It's a foundational question about team integrity. The remaining founders' backgrounds -- industrial engineering, business school, financial data at Tiffany -- are respectable but don't scream "these are the people who will build a distributed data infrastructure protocol that outcompetes billion-dollar competitors." The airdrop campaign, IQ Points, Discord quizzes -- this is crypto mercenary user acquisition, not organic product-market fit. Those 500K users are chasing tokens, and token-incentivized growth without demonstrated enterprise revenue is narrative without cushion.
