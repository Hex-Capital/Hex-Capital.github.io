# Greypoint Industries -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Greypoint Industries is a thesis-correct catalyst read with a kinetic-SKU body — the highest dimension (the counter-swarm market re-opening Tyler Bosmeny explicitly RFS'd for, against the Ukraine/Middle East backdrop and DoD's >$70B counter-drone spend) is doing all the work, and the lowest (an interceptor projectile that lives or dies on per-unit cost against Allen Control Systems' $2.2B-post turret program, not a workflow-layer index position) tells you what this actually is. Greypoint is the Anduril *catalyst* with none of the Anduril *founder shipping history* and none of the Stripe *index geometry* — that is the signature.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on catalyst, and it's a real one: Greypoint Industries is reading the same market re-opening I read when I led the Series D, just earlier and from the Canadian DND side. Ukraine showed Pentagon software inadequacy; the same conflict plus Houthi/Hezbollah swarms showed kinetic-interceptor *unit economics* inadequacy — a $2M Patriot against a $400 Shahed is the math everyone now agrees is broken, and Bosmeny made it a literal YC RFS line item ("counter-swarm stack: high-capacity interceptors"). Where Greypoint Industries diverges from the Anduril rung is the *founder readiness for the catalyst*: Stephens had shipped Palantir's forward-deployed muscle and Luckey had shipped Oculus when I wrote the $4.6B check, whereas Darcy Zhang appears on the Greypoint YC page as CEO with no Globe and Mail, no UBC cohort, no LinkedIn, no GitHub — a CEO who post-dates the founding team is not a fact I can ignore at pre-seed.

**The Lyft Series C pass** is the failure mechanism to study on the index dimension — I passed because #2 in a winner-take-all/oligopoly market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and the same mechanism appears in Greypoint Industries's dossier in a different shape. Greypoint Industries is not a workflow layer that every counter-UAS entrant must touch; it is a *projectile SKU* competing against Allen Control Systems' Bullfrog turret ($242M+ raised, $200M Series B at $2.2B post in May 2026), Anduril's Anvil/Roadrunner ($2.5B+ raised, ~$1B run-rate), and Epirus's Leonidas HPM ($550M+ raised) in the *same kinetic-effector* category Bosmeny just printed an RFS for. "Man-deployable" is a real differentiator on the PEO Soldier procurement line, but a different SKU in the same category is not an index — it's a fight for share, and ACS already has the cap-table to outspend Greypoint's S26 standard YC deal by three orders of magnitude.

Revenue velocity, by mechanism: in the AI cycle I treat dossier silence on growth as a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose — in defense hardware pre-seed, the equivalent positive signal is a signed LOI with a named prime or a DoD SBIR Phase II, and Greypoint Industries has neither. What Greypoint Industries has is a $5,000 hackathon prize at TMU's DMZ (Sep 2025) and "Best Presenter" at the 2026 Red Team Hackathon — that compounds to nothing over 18 months because hackathon prizes are not enterprise pilot tourism *or* "just working"; they're a third category, "demo-day judging," which is upstream of both. Peer 9 Mothers in YC Sp26 is already shipping EDDA to USG and primes per Founderland; Greypoint Industries does not have that sentence in the dossier and the gap will widen with each procurement quarter.

Counterfactual on founder shipping rhythm: for Greypoint Industries to clear into "Invest" on this dimension, the dossier would need a named artifact one of Nic Chu, Jordan Khoo, or Steven Xu has shipped *independently* — an interceptor prototype with a published hit-rate, a manufacturing-cell video, a GitHub flight-controller repo with commits. The Sep 2025 Arctic-surveillance integration of drone + satellite data was a real shipped artifact in 48 hours and I credit it; what I don't have is the **Perplexity** (2022) pattern of "ping me on LinkedIn, week later show up with the thing built" iteratively across three meetings. Three UBC Applied Science co-founders winning a Canadian hackathon together is *team formation* signal, not *between-meeting shipping* signal — those are different functions and the dossier currently shows the first.

Single-miracle, parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015): I co-founded Color as workflow on top of commodity sequencing precisely so the bet wasn't sequencing chemistry — one miracle (clinical-grade workflow), commodity layer underneath. Greypoint Industries inverts that structure. The Greypoint stack requires (1) a guided autonomous interceptor at sub-target unit cost — an open hardware problem with no public prototype data, the site returned 403 on fetch — (2) Canadian DND plus US allied procurement penetration from a 4-person team against ACS's production scale-up, and (3) manufacturing learning-curve compression to hit the cost target before ACS or Anduril ships a man-deployable SKU of their own. That's three independent miracles I'd compound, and the product is the kind of number NFX's *Unusual Signs* essay tells me to pass on.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Greypoint Industries is acqui-hired by Anduril or Allen Control Systems for under $40M by mid-2028, with the Canadian DND relationship and any qualified-supplier paperwork being the assets that priced the deal, not the interceptor IP. The mechanism is the signature from above — the catalyst is real and well-read, which means the *category* will produce a winner; the founder/index/miracle profile says Greypoint Industries is not the company that *becomes* that winner, but is exactly the company a $2.2B-post ACS or a $2.5B-raised Anduril sweeps up for the talent plus the allied-procurement beachhead a year into the production race.

The bull case that stings: Bosmeny's RFS is YC partner-level conviction that the man-deployable infantry interceptor is a *distinct procurement program* from vehicle-mounted turrets, which means ACS's $2.2B post-money is not the comparable — Greypoint Industries is fighting for PEO Soldier dollars where ACS doesn't compete, and the *exact* Anduril Known Exception structure (workflow on existing hard-tech, single miracle isolated to software-on-distribution) plausibly applies if the autonomy stack rides commodity drone components. That objection is real and it would move me if Darcy Zhang had a shipping artifact attached to his name, or if there were a US DoD SBIR Phase I or a named prime LOI in the dossier — neither exists. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Greypoint Industries discloses a signed DoD or Canadian DND contract (any size, named program) or a published prototype intercept video with a unit-cost figure under $2,000, within 90 days of YC Demo Day. Absent that, Greypoint Industries is the consensus-paying-for-consensus pattern in a category where the well-funded winner is already declared — and "when consensus pays" only applies when the revenue curve confirms, which here it does not yet.
