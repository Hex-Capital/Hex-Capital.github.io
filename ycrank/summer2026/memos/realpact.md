## The Idea

- **Problem:** Real estate brokerage staff manually retrieve deed, tax, permit, parcel, and MLS records and re-key them into contracts, disclosures, and deadline trackers per transaction (realpact.ai company description; YC company page).
- **Approach:** Voice-powered AI agents (Deed Agent, Tax & Vision Agent, Property Agent, Transaction Agent) automatically pull county registry records, parse ownership/encumbrances, use computer vision on tax assessments, auto-fill contracts, attach disclosures, route for signature, and track deadlines (realpact.ai, Jun 2026).
- **Differentiation:** [Inferred]: Versus Dotloop/SkySlope/Brokermint (document management + e-signature + compliance workflows), RealPact's claimed difference is automated upstream data retrieval from public registries plus voice interface, rather than templates that humans fill (realpact.ai product description).
- **Business Model:** No pricing page disclosed on realpact.ai (Jun 2026); [Inferred]: per-brokerage or per-seat SaaS sold to brokerages, consistent with the "OS for brokerages" framing on YC page.
- **TAM/SAM:** Real Estate Transaction Management Software market estimates range from ~$2.1B to $11.03B in 2025 with CAGR 5–12% depending on source (Report Prime, Market Research Future, Marketintelo, 2025–2026); no SAM data found for AI-agent transaction automation specifically.
- **GTM / Distribution:** Direct sales to brokerages, currently New Hampshire, with named customers Four Seasons Sotheby's International Realty and Black House Real Estate plus two additional brokerages (realpact.ai, Jun 2026); founders' Dartmouth/NH location supports this beachhead ([Inferred]).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Logged paying brokerage customers (4 named/total per realpact.ai) plus integrations into county registry data extraction pipelines; no patents or proprietary datasets disclosed publicly (realpact.ai, Jun 2026).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Switching costs from contracts/compliance audit trails residing in RealPact and accumulated brokerage-specific templates; unproven at 4-customer scale.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbents Dotloop (Zillow Group subsidiary) and SkySlope monetize document/compliance workflows and may avoid full deal-paperwork automation if it cannibalizes per-seat ARPU; not a regulatory or technical barrier outsiders cannot cross.
- **Commoditization risk:** Public-records scraping plus LLM contract autofill is buildable by Dotloop, SkySlope, Brokermint, DocuSign, or new entrants given general LLM availability ([Inferred] from competitor feature stacks per Paperless Pipeline comparison, 2026).

## Key Risks

- **Domain experience gap:** Founders are first-time real estate operators with consumer-software (CourseMe) backgrounds (Dartmouth Admissions blog; The Dartmouth, May 2026). Mitigation: 4 brokerage design partners signed early (realpact.ai).
- **Public-records data reliability:** County deed/tax/permit records are non-standardized across jurisdictions; auto-filled contracts based on parsing errors create legal exposure for brokerages ([Inferred] from feature description on realpact.ai). No public mitigation disclosed.
- **Incumbent substitution:** Dotloop (Zillow Group) controls ~50% of US transaction management (Paperless Pipeline, 2026) and could ship AI auto-fill on top of existing distribution; SkySlope serves 400,000+ professionals (skyslope.com).
- **Single-state concentration:** All disclosed customers are in New Hampshire (realpact.ai, YC page); each new state requires re-mapping county-registry data sources and state-specific contract forms ([Inferred]).
- **Entity/branding clarity:** realpact.ai footer reads "SignPact, Inc." while product is branded "RealPact" (realpact.ai, Jun 2026) — possible recent rename; no public statement found.
