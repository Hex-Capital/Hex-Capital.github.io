# Kuli -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

"Claude Cowork for social media and creator marketers" — Kuli's own tagline is a red flag I can score in three seconds flat. Every time I see a startup positioning itself as "AI agent for [existing enterprise workflow]," I hear the same pitch that half the YC batch is making, and I ask the same question: who else can build this? CreatorIQ has $80.8M raised and $70.7M in annual revenue across 300 enterprise customers. GRIN raised $145M. OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic all ship multimodal video understanding APIs that any competent engineering team can integrate. Kuli's dossier literally acknowledges that "large AI model providers offer multimodal video understanding APIs that could lower the barrier for new entrants or enable incumbents to add video analysis features." When the company's own research brief concedes the commoditization vector, I don't need to look much further. Kuli is building a feature for CreatorIQ, not a company.

Michael Hodara's background is solid — EPFL MSc in Robotics, AI/data lead at Aircall managing $20M+ in marketing spend. He lived the pain point. Jonathan Hassan brings six years of KPMG M&A advisory and a St. Gallen education. These are competent, credentialed people building exactly the thing you'd expect competent, credentialed people to build. And that's the problem with Kuli through my lens: the sanity-insanity ratio is all wrong. Sane founders, sane idea. Nothing about "AI analyzes influencer videos instead of humans reviewing them manually" makes anyone say "that's fucking crazy." It makes people say "oh yeah, obviously." The idea is so logical, so inevitable, that it virtually guarantees multiple teams are building it simultaneously — which is exactly why the pricing is wrong for me as an investor. The upside is consensus.

Kuli's named users — Supercell, Disney, Havas — are genuinely impressive for a 3-person pre-seed team, and the bull case writes itself: Hodara experienced the $50K/month agency pain firsthand, the product already processes video content frame-by-frame in a way incumbents' metadata-filter architectures can't easily replicate, and the innovator's dilemma protects Kuli while CreatorIQ and GRIN resist rearchitecting their core product. The "world's largest analysed social media content database without storing any video" could compound into a real data asset. I've seen this movie before with Slack — a company in a consensus-hot category that won despite every VC wanting in because it was creating a genuinely new behavioral primitive. But Kuli doesn't pass that test. Real-time workplace messaging replacing email was a new verb, a new behavior. Kuli replaces manual spreadsheet review with automated AI review — that's workflow acceleration, not category creation. Nobody is going to "Kuli me" the way people "Venmo me." The Slack exception requires primitive creation, and Kuli doesn't have it.

Kuli's structural durability is where the thesis collapses hardest. The company's tags — "Artificial Intelligence, SaaS, B2B, Social Media" — read like a checklist of categories I avoid. Pure software, API-dependent, no physical moats, no protocol-level lock-in. The "proprietary content index" depends on continued API access to TikTok (which faces US regulatory uncertainty), Instagram, and YouTube. One platform policy change and Kuli's core asset degrades. Compare this to Metropolis, which I seeded — AI-powered parking management sitting on top of physical parking infrastructure that no LLM can replicate. Kuli has no equivalent. Its entire moat is "we prompted the multimodal model better for influencer marketing," and that moat has an 18-month half-life at best.

Kuli's capital discipline is the one dimension where the company shows well — a 3-person team with Disney-level enterprise clients and custom pricing suggests founder-led sales efficiency and tight resource management. Seven dollars of capital is doing real work here. But capital discipline alone doesn't overcome the fundamental problem: Kuli is an AI application-layer startup in a market where the consensus is correct, the incumbents are well-funded, and the underlying technology is commoditizing in real time. The condition that would flip me to Invest: if Kuli demonstrated that its video content index was generating proprietary behavioral data that no incumbent could replicate — something closer to a protocol layer for creator intelligence rather than an analytics dashboard — and if the founding team had been through multiple ventures together demonstrating judgment under adversity. Neither condition is met. The decisive dimensions are Consensus Mispricing (the market is correctly excited about AI marketing tools, leaving no asymmetry) and Structural Durability (CreatorIQ adds multimodal video analysis within 18 months and Kuli's differentiation evaporates).
