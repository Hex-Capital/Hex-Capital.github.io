# Torus -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Torus is an architecturally clean single-miracle bet on cross-document LLM accuracy that is running its Fortune 500 EPC sales motion faster than it is disclosing traction — Marcus Lima's outfit sits in exactly the workflow slot my *AI Market Clarity* piece would call "wide open" (industrial workflow AI, post-large-context-window), but the dossier's headline traction claim is "working with multiple Fortune 500 companies" with zero named logos, zero ARR, and SOC 2 Type 2 still "in progress." The high dimension (single-miracle isolation) and the low dimension (absent revenue curve) are Torus's signature: the shape of the deal is right, the *evidence* the shape is compounding is not yet on the page.

**Decagon** (2023) is the load-bearing analogy on the miracle-isolation axis — one validated LLM capability (accuracy on a bounded workflow domain), the rest is GTM and product execution. Torus fits the template cleanly: the miracle is LLM accuracy running 50+ consistency-check categories across P&IDs, datasheets, and specs — pressure ratings, tag numbers, materials — and the remaining work is enterprise deployment plus the on-prem "sovereign" tier that customers like the Fortune 500 EPC accounts referenced on the YC page will demand. Torus is not asking me to believe in new physics, new chemistry, or new regulation; the incumbent stack (AVEVA, Bentley, Hexagon) already trains customers to expect document-workflow tooling, and the miracle Torus needs is *cross-document* accuracy on a corpus where the ground truth is legible. That's Decagon-shaped, not Anduril-shaped, and the persona file rewards that.

Torus's revenue-velocity disclosure is where the deal thins. In the AI cycle, "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" — that's the Tim Ferriss #863 line, and the reason **Harvey** (2023) got sized up at $3B→$5B→$8B was that the curve did the talking. Torus's dossier does the opposite: no MRR, no named EPC logo (not Bechtel, not Fluor, not Kiewit), no pilot conversion numbers, no month-over-month anything. "Working with multiple Fortune 500 companies" is the exact phrasing that in my experience distinguishes pilot tourism from paid deployments — real Fortune 500 revenue gets named because founders know it prices the round. The pilot ask on the website — "5-6 documents from current projects" — is a discovery motion, not a paid one. This is the strongest single reason the score doesn't clear the Invest tier.

**Marcus Lima**'s Heimdal (YC S21) raised $9.7M at $63M post from Sam Altman and Marc Benioff on direct-air-capture materials for construction — a founder who has already sold into industrial construction buyers and has an Oxford MSc in Energy Systems Engineering. That's a real shipped artifact and real founder-market fit for the data-center and energy-installation buyers Torus is targeting. Over 18 months, that background compounds specifically on the *procurement pathway* — Lima will know how EPC procurement organizations actually buy software, which is a channel most generic-LLM-for-industry founders learn on the customer's dime. The drag on this dimension is Rahul Thayil: the dossier's own LinkedIn resolution surfaces "Orion" (Techstars '23, $3.5M seed led by Dynamo, November 2025), and if that identity match is right, he's actively CTO'ing another funded startup — a diligence problem that gets resolved on a call, but the dossier doesn't resolve it.

The catalyst dimension is a counterfactual test. Torus would flip a tier here if the dossier named the catalyst crisply: "$X in US data-center capex announced in the last 18 months requires Y net-new substations, and every substation ships a document package Torus operates on." That specific chain would map the deal onto the Anduril-post-Ukraine template — a defensible 24-month window with a named driver. What the dossier gives me instead is "[Inferred]: U.S. reindustrialization capex surge" with no independent analyst source. The catalyst is *real* — hyperscaler data-center buildout and grid-scale energy capex are genuinely re-opening industrial engineering as a software market — but Torus has not made me do the compounding math from a named number, and I don't reward inferred catalysts the way I reward cited ones.

Distribution position on Torus reads as a smaller-vertical **Stripe** parallel: not an index on all of e-commerce, but a workflow layer every capital project's document set must touch as it moves from FEED through detailed engineering to procurement. The valuable structural feature is that Torus does not have to *beat* AVEVA, Bentley, or Hexagon on their single-document turf — those tools remain the system of record; Torus sits *above* them as the cross-document reasoning layer. That is the same architectural posture that let Stripe (2010) sit above merchant-account infrastructure instead of replacing it. The commoditization risk RTInsights flags — incumbents adding AI to existing EDMS suites — is real but slow; Schneider-owned AVEVA does not ship cross-document agents this year.

Forecast, 36 months: Torus is either at $8–15M ARR with 2–3 named Fortune 500 EPC logos and a Series A led by an infrastructure-software fund at $80–150M post, or acquired by AVEVA/Schneider or Bentley for $30–60M as an AI-tuck-in — and the acquisition path is the more likely single outcome given incumbent bundling pressure and Torus's disclosed sales-cycle drag against a runway that pre-seed math doesn't stretch to closing enterprise deals in 18 months without a bridge. The signature — clean miracle, unproven curve — produces this bimodal distribution, not a middle outcome.

Steel-man against the pass-leaning read: Lima has *already done this once*. Heimdal sold direct-air-capture materials into the same industrial-construction procurement organizations Torus now targets — the muscle for enterprise EPC sales is not being learned from scratch, and the Altman/Benioff cap table on Heimdal means Lima can raise a bridge if pilots convert slowly. Torus's on-prem sovereign deployment plus in-progress SOC 2 Type 2 is not a hedge, it's the procurement checklist Lima learned from Heimdal, which means the enterprise-cycle-vs-runway risk I'm pricing may already be discounted. That objection stings — Lima is exactly the "shipped a comparable product to scale at a prior company" founder the persona rewards. It doesn't win, though, because the dossier still refuses to name a single Fortune 500 counterparty, and in the AI cycle silence on logos is itself the signal. The verdict flips to Invest if a Torus follow-up confirms one named Fortune 500 EPC contract with disclosed dollar value inside 90 days — that single fact would move Torus from Neutral to a check I write.
