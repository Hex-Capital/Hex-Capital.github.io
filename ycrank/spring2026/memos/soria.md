## The Idea

- **Problem:** Healthcare equity analysts at banks and hedge funds individually maintain sector models in Excel by manually aggregating dozens of long-tail public/private data sets (federal/state agencies, nonprofits, vendors), creating duplicated work and stale data (Soria YC page; soriaanalytics.com via search snippet).
- **Approach:** Soria aggregates 125+ data sources continuously into a single "sector model" delivered as Excel-like auditable dashboards, layered with search, AI chat, alerts, an Excel add-in, and an MCP server (Soria YC page; search snippet).
- **Differentiation:** Bloomberg Terminal — generalist, not sector-specific (TrustRadius, 2026); Hebbia/Rogo — general AI research over user-uploaded docs, not pre-aggregated sector data (Hebbia.com competitor pages, 2026); Daloopa — extracts filings into structured data but does not assemble live sector models (Daloopa product pages); [Inferred]: Soria's wedge is the vertical, pre-built healthcare sector model rather than a horizontal research tool.
- **Business Model:** No public pricing page found; [Inferred]: per-seat enterprise SaaS sold to banks/hedge funds, consistent with the "trusted by Bulge Bracket Banks, Boutique Research Firms and the Largest Hedge Funds" positioning on the site (search snippet of soriaanalytics.com).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment; reference point: Bloomberg Terminal has 3,430+ customer companies at ~$24,000/yr/seat with Asset Management (383) and Investment Banking (250) as top sectors (6sense, 2026; Koyfin blog, 2026).
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Direct enterprise sales to buy-side and sell-side healthcare desks, given founder Adam Ron's BofA equity-research network in healthcare/managed care (LinkedIn).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Data-aggregation pipeline pulling 125+ healthcare-specific public and private sources, normalized into a live sector model (soriaanalytics.com via search snippet; DBOS.dev case study).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Switching costs as analysts embed Soria dashboards into recurring workflows (Excel add-in, MCP) and as additional vertical sector models compound; unproven because customer count and seat-level retention are not public.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Bloomberg/FactSet/Capital IQ are horizontal generalists whose unit economics depend on broad cross-sector seats, making deep vertical builds (healthcare-only with state/agency long-tail data) economically unattractive to replicate sector-by-sector.
- **Commoditization risk:** Horizontal AI research platforms (Hebbia, Rogo, AlphaSense, Daloopa) could add healthcare-specific data packs; Daloopa already raised $13M in July 2025 to expand AI finance data infrastructure (PRNewswire, Jul 2025).
