# ProjectX -- Paul Graham Evaluation

ProjectX is a systems-ambitious project led by consulting-background founders running at a real infrastructure schlep -- per-app isolated GPU VMs cold-starting in one browser session -- without the hacker trail that usually wins those fights, and with a co-founding team that turned over between the 2022 incorporation (Adhikary + Banerjee + Das) and the YC P26 lineup (Adhikary + Karmakar + Majumder). The dimension where ProjectX sits highest is the schlep zone; the dimension where it sits lowest is hacker genealogy. That pairing is the whole evaluation.

The schlep claim is the most interesting thing in the ProjectX dossier and the reason I read it twice. **Coinbase** (2012) is the load-bearing analogy here: Armstrong won by running toward the regulatory grind nobody else in the batch wanted, and the grind itself became the moat. ProjectX is doing the GPU-virtualization equivalent -- "each application in its own cloud computer with dedicated GPU" with "cold start in seconds" is exactly the kind of unglamorous systems work that software-only YC applicants route around. Where ProjectX diverges from the Coinbase pattern is that Armstrong shipped a hand-built Android wallet to overturn his rejection -- a working artifact under his own hands -- and the ProjectX dossier reports the website itself "returned a loading screen on WebFetch" with no benchmarks, no density numbers, no public per-session cost data on a stack whose entire pitch is per-app-GPU economics. A schlep without a shipped demo is just a hard problem someone has described.

The hacker-genealogy hole is where my AI-wrapper-with-two-MBAs pass example starts whispering. That pass failed because there was no GitHub trail, no shipped artifact, no Lisp-prize equivalent -- and the dossier on ProjectX says, for every one of the three named co-founders, "GitHub: No public repos found." Rounak Adhikary's degree is interaction design; Sourya Majumder's prior is scaling a consulting team to ~120K MRR; only Bishal Karmakar's LinkedIn even names systems and cloud infra as a focus. **Reddit** (2005) had Aaron Swartz, who co-authored the RSS spec before the company existed; **Stripe** (2010) had Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize from age 16. ProjectX is proposing to build a multi-VM browser-delivered OS competing with Amazon WorkSpaces and the post-DigitalOcean Paperspace stack, and the public hacker trail across the three founders is empty.

On animal resourcefulness, Rounak Adhikary did one thing I respect: he ran a tech-consulting business called Woldix at 19 and used it to self-fund ProjectX through 2023-2024, and the company reached YC P26 on only $704K total raised across two rounds since Dec 2023. That is not the cereal-box stunt, but it is a real bootstrapping signal -- the founder built a revenue engine outside the cap table to keep the actual idea alive. The WTC Innovation Award at IIT Bombay Eureka! is a credential, not a stunt; the IIM Bangalore Launchpad affiliation is a credential, not a stunt. The mechanism that compounds over the next 18 months is whether Adhikary can keep manufacturing runway from non-dilutive sources while the per-GPU unit economics get worked out -- a habit Woldix proves he has, but only at consulting scale.

On organic founder-problem fit, the counterfactual is the cleanest way to test ProjectX: for the score on this dimension to flip a tier, one of the three founders would need to have personally been a heavy AI-agent user hitting the "one cursor, one keyboard, one app in focus" constraint in their own daily work, and the dossier would name a tool they built for themselves first. Instead the framing in the YC page is third-person and market-shaped -- "when half the users of a computer are not people" reads as a thesis about the world, not a USB stick that Drew Houston kept leaving at MIT. **Dropbox** (2007) is the contrast that matters; ProjectX is the inverse posture.

The co-founder configuration on ProjectX is where I get most uncomfortable in a way the rubric understates. **Airbnb** (2009) was Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk, who had lived together; **Twitch** (2007) was Kan/Shear coming back after **Kiko** (2005). ProjectX is three co-founders with no documented shared university, prior employer, or working history, after a turnover from the 2022 founding trio. That's not the long-relationship pattern; that's a team that assembled around the idea.

My 36-month forecast on ProjectX: the per-app GPU unit economics fail to close against incumbent VDI cost curves, the company pivots to an agent-sandbox API targeting the AI coding-agent batch-mates rather than a full consumer OS, and lands somewhere under $500K ARR before a sub-$15M acquihire by a tier-two cloud in the Paperspace-to-DigitalOcean shape. The mechanism is the one the dossier already names: "each app in its own independent computer ? own GPU" implies a COGS curve that hyperscalers can underprice the moment they care, and AWS WorkSpaces plus a re-architected Azure Virtual Desktop will care the moment the agent-sandbox use case shows revenue.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Coinbase** (2012) rhyme: Armstrong was an Airbnb engineer, not a Lisp-prize hacker, and the schlep itself -- bitcoin compliance -- became the asset precisely because the founder had unfashionable appetite for it. ProjectX's CTO Bishal Karmakar genuinely names systems and streaming as his domain, the agent-OS thesis is timely in a way the dossier captures with "half the users of a computer are not people," and the $704K total raised is the kind of cheap-and-default-alive number I praised in 2024. That stings. The reason it does not flip me is that Coinbase Armstrong had a shipped prototype to overturn the rejection, and ProjectX as it stands has a website that didn't load for the researcher, zero GitHub repos across three named founders, and a co-founder set assembled after the original 2022 team scattered. The single verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a public demo of multiple Windows and Linux VMs cold-starting in seconds inside one browser session, with two or three named YC P26 agent-company batch-mates using it as their sandbox in production. Ship that and I will reread.
