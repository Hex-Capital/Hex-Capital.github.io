# Hessian -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Hessian is what I'd call a schlep-positive, pain-negative company: Bao Nguyen, James Smith, and Tom Watson are running toward the unglamorous forward-deployed-engineer model that Distyl raised $207M to scale, but nothing in the Hessian dossier says any of them personally suffered the month-end-close or invoice-reconciliation grind they're now selling to fix. That asymmetry -- real schlep, borrowed pain -- is the analytical signature, and it's the pattern I've seen produce competent $3-8M ARR services companies more often than it produces a Stripe.

The HIGH rung on the schlep axis is **Coinbase** (2012) -- Brian Armstrong volunteering for the bitcoin compliance grind nobody else in his batch wanted to touch, and that grind compounded into the moat. Hessian's forward-deployed model is genuinely in the same family: three engineers flying on-site to map a customer's invoice flow is the kind of work the Decagons and Sierras of the world deliberately route around because it breaks their seat-expansion math. The dossier names the channel conflict explicitly -- pure-SaaS incumbents at $1.5B-$10B valuations cannot easily copy the "we operate it" model without cannibalizing their own GTM. Where Hessian diverges from the Coinbase rung is that the schlep is already crowded: Distyl at $1.8B is two years and ~$200M ahead on the same FDE-plus-platform thesis, and Palantir wrote the original playbook two decades ago. Schlep blindness only protects you when the other smart founders are still blind.

The LOW rung on the organic-idea axis is my own pass example -- the two-MBA team pitching an AI wrapper they never personally needed. Hessian isn't that team; Bao published ML systems research with DeepMind and James spent five years at Bloomberg building entity-disambiguation pipelines, so they can build the thing. But read the dossier for the founder-pain origin story and there isn't one. There's no Bao-forgot-his-USB-stick moment, no Collison-brothers-hacking-commerce-since-Auctomatic moment. They are not the customer. They are consultants who picked a category -- "mid-market back-office knowledge work" -- because coding agents matured in 2024-2025 and made the FDE model cheaper to staff. That's a market observation, not a Drew Houston observation, and "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) tells me to discount it.

On animal resourcefulness, the Hessian dossier has exactly one pre-funding artifact worth a real check mark: Bao co-founded Zettafleet, a decentralized LLM training company that came out of stealth on Feb 23 2026 per Sifted. That's a second-time-founder signal and it isn't nothing. But Zettafleet emerging from stealth three months before the YC P26 batch is closer to "running two startups concurrently" than to Armstrong's hand-built Android wallet that flipped a rejection -- there's no stunt revenue, no unscalable hack, no Hessian-specific moment of resourcefulness. The 18-month compounding question is whether the forward-deployed model lets three engineers serve five customers or fifty; the dossier shows one named customer, Fazeshift, and that customer is a fellow YC company, which is the friendliest possible logo.

The counterfactual on hacker genealogy is the cleanest tell. Three Durham CS graduates, Cambridge scholarships, GoCardless zero-downtime payments DB migration, Bloomberg ML pipelines -- on paper this is hacker-credentialed. But the dossier line that actually stops me is: "GitHub: No public repos found in search results" for Bao, "No public repos found" for James, "No public repos found" for Tom. My unstated pattern is that hackers leave tracks -- Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize, Aaron Swartz's RSS spec -- and the Hessian founders' tracks are all inside proprietary employer codebases. To flip this dimension up a tier I'd need one shipped open-source agent runtime under Tom's GitHub or one Hacker News post from Bao with a Show HN of the Hessian observability layer. Neither exists in the dossier.

On cofounder configuration, the parallel is **Twitch** (2007) -- Kan, Shear, and Vogt had years of shared context from Kiko before they ran at Justin.tv. Hessian's three founders share a Durham CS undergraduate cohort, two of them stacked Cambridge scholarships, and two overlapped at Bloomberg for years. That's a real long-relationship trio, complementary across CEO/COO/CTO, and it's the dimension where Hessian most cleanly clears my bar without qualification.

My 36-month forecast: Hessian reaches $2-5M of mostly-services revenue serving 8-15 mid-market customers, the three founders hit the headcount wall the FDE model always hits, and Hessian gets acquired by Distyl, a Big-4 consulting AI practice, or a larger agent-platform company at a low-nine-figure outcome -- a respectable result that is not the No. 1 portfolio company beating the next 199 combined. The mechanism is the high/low pairing: real schlep generates real revenue, borrowed pain caps the ceiling because the founders don't have the customer-instinct edge to compound past services into a product company.

The strongest bear case against my own neutrality runs through the **Stripe** (2010) known exception -- I funded the Collisons after Auctomatic even though they skipped parts of the YC process, because the prior-founder signal was real. Bao with Zettafleet is arguably the same shape: a second-time technical founder with ML-infrastructure scars. If Zettafleet turns into a real outcome and Bao is operating Hessian with that credibility, the ceiling moves. The reason this doesn't flip me: Auctomatic had a $5M exit at ages 19 and 17 before Stripe; Zettafleet emerged from stealth three months before the batch with no disclosed outcome, and Hessian's idea is downstream of a market observation rather than a pain Bao lived inside. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is five disclosed paying enterprise customers outside the YC network within twelve months, with the three founders still being the only engineers -- that would prove the "coding agents let 3 people scale FDE" claim is actually true rather than a pitch line, and at that point I'd write the check on the Coinbase-schlep-as-moat logic alone.
