# Minicor -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Faizaan Chishtie and Saheed Akinbile have been building together since a 2019 UX class at Ottawa, and the cleanest thing about Minicor is the partnership itself -- one CEO, one CTO, three ventures deep (Software For Love, Laminar Run, now Minicor). The least clean thing is that I cannot install Minicor on my laptop, ride in it, scan a license plate with it, or play it on the subway -- it is a Windows-VM agent layer selling into hospital back offices. That pairing -- governance-clean and tactile-invisible -- is the signature. Minicor is a disciplined two-person enterprise infrastructure shop pitching the most consensus YC-P26 category I can think of, and that is a different animal than the magically-weird checks I write.

Anduril/Luckey is the high rung on lived founder origin: VR from the parents' garage in 2014, then defense in 2017, the biography preceding the company. Minicor's claim on that rung is the Laminar Run lineage -- the same two founders ran a low-code legacy-integration platform that has now redirected to Minicor's domain, and Saheed brings real RPA scars from QuickBooks/Sage/Citrix work plus a Morgan Stanley stint where his Java apps reportedly saved $30M/year in fees. That is a prior-product trajectory, which is more than the consensus YC AI-darling I would pass on, but it is not Palmer Luckey's autobiographical contact with the problem -- nobody in Minicor's bio grew up inside a hospital's medical-records desk watching nurses click through a legacy EHR. The founders built the previous SaaS for legacy integration; they did not live the legacy integration. That puts Minicor a rung below Anduril and a rung above the credentialed-PM pass.

Crusoe was magically weird because three credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds before I wrote the check -- burning flared gas for Bitcoin in North Dakota sounded uninvestable, which is exactly why the round was underpriced. Minicor is the opposite shape on that axis. UiPath is a public company at ~$1.5B ARR, Automation Anywhere has raised over $1B, Skyvern (YC, $2.7M) and Browserbase ($68M) and TinyFish ($47M, ICONIQ Series A) are all attacking the adjacent agentic-automation surface, and Gartner is forecasting the RPA market to hit $35.27B in 2026. Tom Blomfield is the YC partner. This is not a category two sophisticated investors have just passed on for moral or capital-intensity reasons -- this is the lane every multi-stage AI fund has a bet in. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Minicor seems to be playing is "the most fundable wrapper of P26."

Single CEO is the dimension where Minicor most clearly clears the bar. The HQ Trivia post-mortem and the GameCrush activist-investor collapse both traced to unresolved authority, and that pattern shows up early -- in title ambiguity, in equal-equity language, in a founding team that met three months ago. Minicor's founders met in 2019, shipped a non-profit through the pandemic, then a low-code startup, then this pivot/rebrand. Faizaan is CEO, Saheed is CTO, the delineation is documented across LinkedIn and the YC page, and seven years of operating together is the closest pre-seed proxy I get for "they have already had the hard fight." This is one of the few times I am scoring a co-founder pair near the top of this rung.

The capital-intensity dimension compounds over 18 months only if a software company crosses into either hardware, energy, defense, or a regulatory moat that gets deeper with each customer. Minicor is pure software -- Python automation plus reflection agents -- but the HIPAA exposure from "25,000 patients/day in production" plus SOC 2 Type II is the only thing pulling Minicor out of undifferentiated AI-SaaS territory. PHI handling is genuinely unsexy and genuinely regulated; it is also genuinely a place Anthropic's computer-use API will not casually wander into. That is a real but partial pull toward the lane that produced Mindbloom and Vigil Labs -- enough to clear the absent-evidence default, not enough to match a Flock Safety camera on a pole.

What would have to be true for Minicor to flip from a 4 to a 9 on physical-world verifiability? I would need to be able to watch a hospital admissions clerk's screen and see Minicor click through the EHR in front of me -- a demo video on the website, a public reference customer named, a recorded session of the self-healing recovery firing when Epic ships a UI patch. None of that is in the dossier. The 93-96% click-accuracy figure is self-reported with no third-party benchmark, the customer logos on the homepage are unextracted, and there is no Product Hunt artifact. Minicor is asking me to take its tactile claims on founder testimony, which is the exact criterion I default below midpoint on.

My 36-month forecast: Minicor is acquired by UiPath, Automation Anywhere, or a healthcare-IT roll-up for $40M-$120M as a self-healing-recovery feature bolted onto an existing RPA suite, with the founders earning out for two years. The mechanism is the signature -- governance-clean teams in consensus categories with regulated-vertical traction get bought for the SOC 2/HIPAA-certified pipeline and the agent-recovery telemetry, not built into standalone billion-dollar platforms, because Anthropic's computer-use API and incumbent agentic-RPA features will compress the wrapper's accuracy delta toward zero before Minicor reaches Series B scale.

The bull case that should sting: Crusoe pivoted from Bitcoin mining to AI infrastructure and that pivot was the company -- the prior product was the apprenticeship. Minicor *is* the Laminar pivot, the founders have been compounding in legacy-integration since 2019, and "25,000 patients/day in production" is not nothing at pre-seed -- it is the kind of HIPAA-regulated production traction I praised Vigil Labs and Mindbloom for. Maybe Minicor is the unsexy healthcare-desktop RPA shop that quietly becomes the integration layer for every AI vendor selling into Epic and Cerner shops, and I am pattern-matching on the consensus category and missing the regulated-vertical compounding underneath. The dossier evidence that wins for me: the prior product was not Laminar succeeding and the founders graduating to a harder problem -- it is Laminar redirecting its domain to Minicor's, which reads as a rebrand under pressure rather than compounded conviction, and the founder biographies do not put either of them inside a hospital, an EHR vendor, or an RPA enterprise buyer before this venture. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: a named healthcare-system reference customer (not "patients/day") plus a third-party benchmark on the 93-96% accuracy claim against Anthropic computer-use on the same EHR workflow. Until then, this is a rebuttal, not a check.
