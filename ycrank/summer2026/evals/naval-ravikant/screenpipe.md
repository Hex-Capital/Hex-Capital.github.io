# screenpipe -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

screenpipe is a builder-tool wearing enterprise-productivity clothing: Louis Beaumont's Rust agent has 19k+ GitHub stars and a source-available core, which is where my check gets written, and it is a single-sided memory product where the Nth user does not compound the (N-1)th user's value, which is where the payoff math strains. The signature is Stack Overflow's distribution architecture bolted onto Perplexity's single-sided consumption pattern — that pairing, not the total, is what I am underwriting.

Stack Overflow (2008) is the load-bearing analogy on leverage architecture: a developer product distributed by developer-pull through public code, zero marginal cost of replication, no App Store gate, no SDR pipeline. screenpipe rhymes — 19k+ stars on screenpipe/screenpipe, a Rust binary users install locally, and organic reach into engineers at Microsoft, NVIDIA, Datadog, Adobe from the homepage logo wall. Where screenpipe diverges from the Stack Overflow rung is the license-update blog: Louis Beaumont walked the repo from open-source to source-available, and forks (atzamis, EvolvingSoftware, AllyourBaseBelongToUs) already exist. That is permissionless leverage with a footnote — the founder is still standing on code-and-media distribution, but he is trying to close the door behind him, which is legible and defensible but not the pure Stack Overflow template.

Pinterest is the ladder rung I keep returning to on the network dimension — a product I passed on because "I don't get it, it's images." screenpipe has the inverse problem: I *do* get it, and the mechanism is single-sided. Your captured screen memory does not make my captured screen memory more valuable. There is no supply side pulling a demand side. The 250k users cited from Louis Beaumont's LinkedIn are 250k independent accounts, not a compounding graph. This is the Perplexity shape (criterion #2 waived because #1 and #3 clear at top quartile), and I have to consciously invoke that exception rather than pretend the network math is there. The "Pipes" library is the one place a community-added-value mechanic could emerge — shared automations others reuse — but the dossier gives me no evidence yet that Pipes are being published cross-org.

On specific knowledge, Louis Beaumont's Mediar (2024, SAP screen-input data entry) is the rehearsal that matters — screenpipe is the same mechanism at a larger surface area, and the video-game-bots-at-14 detail is the biography of a builder who has been staring at screens as automatable objects for two decades. This is not an ex-FAANG PM who read a market map. That is a real specific-knowledge signal even without the PhD credential Aravind Srinivas brought to Perplexity.

On pivot room, screenpipe's mechanism compounds well over 18 months: a local-first screen-and-audio memory layer has natural adjacencies into RPA, personal agent runtime, compliance recording, and sales-call intelligence — Granola at $1.5B validates the audio slice alone. The Rewind desktop-app shutdown on Dec 19, 2025 after the Meta acquisition is the exact category discontinuity that widens the pivot corridor for screenpipe rather than narrowing it. The market absorbs at least two founder pivots before refinancing.

On the founder trio, the counterfactual is what would flip this tier: a named co-founder with a distribution or enterprise-sales complement, or a public partner-litigation-free exit from Mediar, would move screenpipe from mid to high. What I have is a solo founder on YC S26, team of six, Techstars '22, OrangeDAO F1, and no volunteered ethics-signaling — quiet builder profile, integrity reads clean because it is not asserted. Solo-founder execution risk across macOS/Windows/Linux plus 48+ integrations is real, and the Venture Hacks rule ("picking a co-founder is the most important decision") means the most-important decision is still pending on the screenpipe cap table.

36-month forecast: screenpipe either gets bought by Atlassian, Notion, or Salesforce in the $150-400M range as the local-first memory layer beneath their agent products, or Louis Beaumont pivots the core out from consumer productivity into enterprise agentic-RPA and lands at $4-10M ARR on the $150/seat Enterprise tier. Death case is real but not the base case — the mechanism producing this range is that Rewind sold to Meta and Granola is at $1.5B, which sets a clear acquirer floor and comparables ceiling around screenpipe's exact category.

The bear case that stings: Microsoft ships Recall free on every Copilot+ PC and Meta re-releases Rewind as a Facebook-tier consumer feature, and screenpipe's $50/seat Pro tier gets squeezed out of consumer while enterprise procurement stalls on always-on capture legal review — Recall's repeated post-launch security disclosures (GeekWire, 2026) are the reason every CISO says no. Solo-founder throughput cannot outrun two platform incumbents shipping bundled substitutes at $0. The rebuttal is architectural: Recall is Windows-only with no cross-app automation and Meta-Rewind is now a consumer product, and screenpipe's cross-OS + Pipes automation layer is the actual product wedge, not the recording itself. The verifiable flip condition: if 12 months from now screenpipe cannot show a signed Enterprise contract with a named F500 counterparty at the $150/seat tier, the bear thesis is correct and I mark the position down.
