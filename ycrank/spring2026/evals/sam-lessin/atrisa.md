# Atrisa (formerly Refortifai) -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Atrisa is the textbook 2026 YC vertical-AI-agent pitch wearing a slightly more obscure jersey -- "AI agents for analog circuit design" -- assembled by a team that was selling "runtime security for AI agents" under the name Refortifai inside the same batch before the pivot. The dimensional signature here is ugly in a specific way: the only thing Atrisa has going for it on my ladder is that "analog EDA" is a niche enough label that it's not the literal Pass-Example sentence, and the thing it lacks most is any sleeve fit, any founder distribution, and any structural reason for me to be the bid. That's a company shaped exactly like the one I described in my own Pass Example, just with the vertical noun swapped.

On round pricing, the HIGH rung is Solana at $0.05/coin in 2018 when every generalist had quit crypto -- the mechanism is *the consensus has visibly walked away from the category, so the entry price reflects abandonment, not enthusiasm*. Atrisa is the opposite shape: Astrus took $11M CAD from Khosla in 2025, Quilter took a $10M Benchmark Series A in February 2026, Cadence acquired ChipStack and shipped the AI Super Agent in three months, and YC just stamped Silimate as their first EDA bet. The consensus didn't walk away from agentic chip-design AI -- it *arrived* in early 2026, which is precisely when Atrisa is raising. There is no Solana-style abandonment to price into Atrisa's seed; this is a category where Benchmark is already setting the Series A comp, which means the YC post-money is going to print at a price where my fund math doesn't work even on a clean acqui-hire outcome.

The LOW rung on conviction is my own Pass Example -- "we're building an AI agent for [enterprise vertical], no public writing, no obsession with the category, just a competent response to where the puck is going." The failure mechanism is that the founder cannot tell you a non-obvious belief they will not be talked out of, because the belief is "agents are hot, this vertical is open." Atrisa fits the pattern almost too neatly: Sayan Mitra, Atman Kar, and Rithik Jain were running Refortifai on runtime AI-agent security and pivoted into analog EDA mid-batch -- that's not a missionary tacking through the regatta, that's a team optimizing for whatever vertical clears the YC demo-day bar. No Twitter presence for any of the three founders, no long-form writing on why frontier LLMs specifically choke on analog tradeoffs, and a name-disambiguation problem on the CEO that the dossier itself flags. Atman Kar's RTL Digital Design Engineer stint at Texas Instruments is the one real domain artifact in the whole file, and RTL is digital, not analog -- it's adjacent, not load-bearing.

Capital-structure-wise, Atrisa will need five rounds to reach default-alive selling enterprise EDA seats to fabless semiconductor design teams against Synopsys, Cadence, and Siemens at ~70% combined share. The mechanism that compounds against me over 18 months is straightforward: every quarter Atrisa doesn't close a paid customer, Astrus's $13.3M and Quilter's Benchmark cap table buy more engineering headcount and more design-partner LOIs, and the price for Atrisa's next tack goes up while the differentiation gap widens. There is no revenue share, no royalty on customer-fine-tuned models, no employee-equity holdco wrapper -- just a SAFE into a three-person team that's going to need a Series A from a tier-one before the incumbents finish their build-vs-buy decision tree. That is the factory model I've spent two years writing against.

Counterfactually on the founder-brand dimension, the score flips a tier only if one of Sayan Mitra, Atman Kar, or Rithik Jain turns out to run a 10K-subscriber Substack on analog design intuition that the dossier missed, or if Kar is publicly known inside the TI mixed-signal community in a way that converts to design-partner intros without a sales motion. The dossier explicitly says no Twitter, no GitHub stars retrievable, no community footprint. There is no Marina Mogilko here -- there isn't even a John Fish. There's a three-person team with LinkedIn pages and a YC company page, which is the founder-distribution profile I assume is zero by default.

On category sleeve, compare Atrisa to Sublime Security, which is the closest thing in my recent enterprise-software book. Sublime worked for me because email security was a category late-stage SOC SaaS investors had stopped underwriting at seed and the wedge was a non-obvious technical posture. Atrisa's category is the inverse: agentic EDA is the *most* underwritten seed category in chip design right now -- Quilter, Astrus, Silimate, ChipStack, and the Cadence Agentic AI Solutions page are all 2025-2026 entrants. This is "AI is just more internet" wearing a semiconductor lab coat, and the Joe Lonsdale podcast position re-weights me explicitly away from it.

My 36-month forecast: Atrisa is acqui-hired by Cadence, Synopsys, or Siemens for a sub-$30M total consideration on a sub-$2M ARR base, following the exact ChipStack template Cadence ran in February 2026 -- or Atrisa dies quietly as Astrus and Quilter's funding leads compound into the design-partner relationships that determine which two startups the incumbents end up bidding on. The mechanism is the one Zach's blog laid out: incumbents prefer rapid acqui-hire over native builds, the window is short, capital-leader startups win the bake-off, and the YC entry price for the also-rans doesn't return a seed fund on a $25M outcome.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the Ro Known Exception: Ro burned capital and needed many rounds, but the regulatory complexity across 50-state prescription rails was itself the moat, so I overrode my capital-efficiency criterion. Someone could argue Atrisa is shaped similarly -- analog design is *tacit*, simulation-driven, and frontier LLMs documentably fail at it, so the data moat from real customer circuits could be the technical equivalent of Ro's licensing schleps. That stings for about a paragraph, then it stops stinging: Ro was the only fund underwriting prescription pharma rollup at that price, whereas Atrisa is the *fifth* fund being asked to underwrite agentic EDA at a 2026 hot-batch price, and the moat-via-customer-data argument requires Atrisa to win those customers *before* Quilter's Benchmark cap table buys the same logos. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed paid pilot with a named fabless semiconductor company (not a design-partnership MOU) AND a round priced under $10M post-money with terms that aren't a vanilla SAFE. Until then, I'm out.
