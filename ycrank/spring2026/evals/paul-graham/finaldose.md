# FinalDose -- Paul Graham Evaluation

FinalDose is what happens when three Oxford DPhils try to build Intellia from scratch on YC pre-seed money: the schlep dimension is sky-high (a novel regulatory class for a programmable DNA kill switch, no FDA precedent), and the hacker-genealogy dimension is the lowest of any company I've seen this week — Jeff Liu, Li-Yao Huang, and Steven Lin have three doctorates and zero verifiable GitHub repos between them. That pairing — maximal schlep, minimal hacker tribal markers — tells me FinalDose is a credentialed academic spinout, not a YC-native hacker company, and I have to evaluate it as such.

On schlep, the high rung is **Coinbase** (2012), where Armstrong's appetite for bitcoin compliance grind became the moat itself; the mid rung is **DoorDash** (2013), with its last-mile delivery ops; the low rung is the polished AI-wrapper pitched by two MBAs in my Pass Example. FinalDose sits above DoorDash and below Coinbase — the dossier names IND-enabling tox, GMP manufacturing, and an ad-hoc CMC pathway because "a programmable small-molecule-style kill switch triggered by DNA recognition has no clear FDA/MHRA precedent class." That is a real schlep. The problem is that Coinbase's schlep could be conquered by Armstrong personally staying up all night reading FinCEN guidance; FinalDose's schlep gets conquered by writing $300M of checks the way Caribou did at IPO, and pre-seed YC capital is the wrong currency for it.

On hacker genealogy, the high rung is **Stripe** (2010), where Patrick Collison's 2005 Young Scientist Lisp prize predated the company, and **Reddit** (2005), where Aaron Swartz co-authored the RSS spec; the mid rung is a credentialed CS-degree founder who at least shipped one demo on Hacker News; the low rung is the AI-wrapper team with no shipped code. FinalDose lives below the mid rung — Steven Lin did ML on coevolution at Gutsee Biotech, Li-Yao Huang ran CRISPR screening pipelines at MRC Weatherall, and Jeff Liu has an oncology DPhil, but the dossier explicitly returns "No public repos found" or "No public data found" for every founder's GitHub. They're wet-lab and comp-bio technical, not the Lisp-and-RSS lineage I over-index on. This is the dimension where my unstated pattern works against FinalDose hardest.

On animal resourcefulness, the high rung is **Airbnb** (2009)'s cereal boxes at $40 each; the mid rung is Drew Houston's Hacker News demo for **Dropbox** (2007); the low rung is a deck with no shipped artifact. FinalDose sits at the mid rung purely on Jeff Liu's Vivid Dx history — $5M seed plus $10M Pre-Series A, Oxford Science Enterprises backing, diagnostic time compressed from 5 days to 30 minutes, John Sperzel hired as CEO in July 2025. That's a real prior win, and it's the single most load-bearing fact in the dossier. But there is no FinalDose-era stunt — no equivalent to the cereal boxes, no preclinical efficacy posted publicly, no pre-funding revenue improvisation. The animal moment lives in the previous company.

On organic-idea fit, the high rung is **Dropbox** (2007) and Houston's forgotten USB stick; the mid rung is **Stripe** (2010) and the Collisons' commerce hacking from **Auctomatic** (2007); the low rung is the AI-wrapper team that "spotted a market opportunity." FinalDose sits at the mid rung — Huang spent five years on CRISPR-BRCA1 screening at Weatherall, Liu did oncology at Oxford, they live inside the undruggable-targets problem professionally. But this is research-domain familiarity, not personal pain the way an MIT student losing USB sticks is personal pain. On cofounder configuration, **Airbnb** (2009)'s Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk trio is the high rung, **Twitch** (2007)'s Kan/Shear pair from **Kiko** (2005) is the mid rung, and a hackathon-paired duo is the low rung. FinalDose has three founders, all Oxford DPhils, but the dossier says "Likely overlap at Oxford" — likely, not confirmed. Without a shared lab or prior shipped artifact together, this is closer to mid than high.

My 36-month forecast for FinalDose: alive but pre-IND, having raised $20-40M of follow-on after the YC check, with no FDA filing yet and one announced research collaboration with a pharma like AstraZeneca or Sanofi serving as validation theater. Not dead, not a Caribou-tier IPO, not acquired — stuck in the capital-intensity gap between YC seed and Phase 1, the exact gap that forced Caribou to cut 32% of its workforce in 2025 and Intellia to cut 27%. The mechanism producing this forecast is the high-schlep/low-hacker-genealogy signature: the schlep guarantees the work is real, but the hacker-genealogy gap means the founders won't compress the capital curve the way Armstrong did at **Coinbase** (2012).

The strongest bear case against my Neutral verdict is that I'm pattern-matching FinalDose to my software-portfolio frame when therapeutics rewards a different filter — and Jeff Liu's $15M-raised Vivid Dx is genuinely the kind of prior-exit-adjacent track record that, in any biotech investor's frame, would justify a check. The counter is that none of my Known Exceptions are therapeutics — **Stripe** (2010), **Loopt** (2005), and **Airbnb** (2009) all bent rules I had about process, market timing, and idea quality, but none bent the rule that I write checks where my edge is reading hackers, not reading IND pathways. FinalDose belongs to a specialist, not to me. The verifiable condition that would flip this: a named oncology pharma — Roche, AstraZeneca, Merck — signs an option or LOI on the FinalDose platform within 12 months, externally validating the DNA-sensor mechanism before the capital-intensity gap kills the company. Until then, polite pass; I'd happily route it to someone who actually invests in therapeutics.
