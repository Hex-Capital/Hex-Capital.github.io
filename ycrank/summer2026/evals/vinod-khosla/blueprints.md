# Blueprints -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Blueprints is a $20/month SaaS wrapper on Polymarket built by two University of Limerick dropouts -- the dimensional signature is a capital-light consumer-fintech tool with no scientific miracle, exactly the profile my framework treats as a default pass. Ryan Morrissey and Bence Redmond are competent young engineers shipping a "Prince" LLM-to-strategy translator with 250 beta users and $500K processed volume, but the success-state at maximum ambition is "retail traders extract slightly more PnL from prediction markets than they do today." No incumbent regime collapses. No civilizational variable moves. That is the entry-point answer, and in my framework the entry point is dispositive.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence -- I wrote the $50M first VC check because the success-state was a redrawing of cognitive labor itself, the same frame I now extend publicly to the $15T U.S. labor displacement thesis (Fortune 2026-03-06). Blueprints' success-state, by contrast, is that retail Polymarket users capture a larger share of the $40M/year that arbitrage bots currently extract (Yahoo Finance, 2026) -- a redistribution inside a niche derivative market, not a regime change. Even granting the bullish framing that combined Polymarket+Kalshi hit ~$24B monthly volume by April 2026 (Pew, May 2026), the consequence at full ambition is "Robinhood for prediction-market bots," which is a feature, not a civilization. Blueprints is closer to the GPT-4-wraps-sales-calls pattern from my Pass Example than to anything in the OpenAI register.

**DoorDash** (2013) is the LOW rung on the technical-and-capital-risk dimension within my own portfolio -- capital-light consumer logistics that any Tier-1 generalist could have funded, and which I openly flag as a Known Exception to my "science risk" rhetoric. Blueprints sits below even DoorDash on this axis: the moat claims in the dossier are "co-located execution infrastructure" and "Polymarket backtest dataset since 2022," both of which are reproducible in a quarter by any of the four named competitors -- Polystrat (Valory's $13.8M + $4M seed), TurbineFi, Rainmaker, Simmer -- and by Polymarket's own open-source `Polymarket/agents` SDK. No physics miracle. No biology miracle. No capital wall that repels consensus VCs. The pattern that justified DoorDash -- a founder-ambition override on consequence -- is not visible in Blueprints' dossier.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus, the mechanism that compounds over 18 months is whether the founders have a contrarian thesis the market is structurally wrong about. Morrissey's prior is a Stripe internship and an Induct/1517 Medici grant; Redmond's prior is an AWS SDE internship. Neither has publicly contradicted a field, neither has the Sergey Levine pattern from **Physical Intelligence** (2024), and the "Prince" LLM-to-strategy product is not a contrarian technical bet -- it is the consensus 2026 LLM-tooling pattern applied to a niche exchange. In 18 months this dimension does not compound for Blueprints because there is no original thesis to be vindicated against expert consensus; the product either gets distribution or it does not.

Counterfactually, on founder learning rate, Blueprints would need a documented prior pivot -- a **Slash** (2024)-style "we were wrong about X, here is what we shipped instead" moment -- to flip from the absent-evidence default into the high range. The dossier shows Morrissey dropping out of the University of Limerick ISE programme to pursue Blueprints full-time, which is commitment, not a pivot; it shows no public thesis update, no prior company sold, no "Kanye moment." If the YC S26 Demo Day in September 2026 reveals that Blueprints already pivoted off a v1 product inside the batch with a stated reason, the score moves. Today the dossier is silent and I apply the 7/15 default.

On capital profile, Blueprints is the inverse of **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where I led a $115M Series A specifically because the science required capital scale no generalist would underwrite. Blueprints' published Pro tier is $20/month against a free tier with 2,000 credits; the roadmap implies single-digit-million seed economics to reach low-seven-figure ARR. I am structurally over-resourced for this deal, which means any decent generalist fintech fund -- not Khosla Ventures -- is the right capital partner. The capital-intensity profile actively repels the kind of conviction check I write.

Blueprints' 36-month forecast: dead or acqui-hired sub-$10M by Polymarket, Kalshi, or one of the funded competitors (Polystrat/Olas most likely), with peak ARR under $500K before Polymarket's first-party "Build on Polymarket" program ships managed no-code tooling and compresses the retail edge -- the mechanism is platform-dependency collapse plus a crowded automation layer where Polystrat already claims to outperform 37% of human Polymarket participants (Whalesbook, 2026) on $13.8M of capital Blueprints does not have.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the **DoorDash** (2013) Known Exception: a YC-pedigreed, capital-light consumer product where founder ambition and consumer-flow reshaping override the absent science-risk -- prediction markets could plausibly become the next consumer-finance category, $24B/month is not a toy number, and Blueprints' "Prince" natural-language interface could be the consumer wedge that bots-by-API will never have. The case stings because YC-pedigreed capital-light consumer fintech is exactly where I have made my consensus exceptions -- **Slash** (2024), **Affirm** (2013), **DoorDash** (2013). The case fails on distribution: DoorDash had a defensible ops moat and a two-sided network; Blueprints has neither, competes with the exchange itself shipping `Polymarket/agents` open-source, and has no documented founder pivot to clear the Slash-style learning-rate bar. The single condition that flips my verdict: a signed exclusive integration with Kalshi or Polymarket as the platform's preferred no-code automation surface, paired with $1M+ ARR by month 12 post-YC. Absent that, Blueprints is the 30-second GPT-wrapper pass from my own framework.
