# WithAI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

WithAI is the 2026 YC archetype with slightly above-median founder pedigree: Ian McInnis ran an equity book at Bridgewater leading AI-for-investing initiatives, Ben Finch built multi-agent coordination at Sentient Labs, and the deck is "AI command center for hedge funds" -- vertical AI SaaS dressed as a venture moment. The dimensional signature for WithAI is highest on founder domain depth and lowest on owned distribution / category sleeve fit, which means WithAI is a competently-staffed entry into the most consensus YC sleeve of the batch with no asset-class mispricing for me to harvest. The Greg Jensen / Karen Karniol-Tambour angel checks are the single most interesting fact on the page, and they're a pricing problem, not a pricing gift.

Ian McInnis's Bridgewater seat is the rung that matters here -- compare to **Sublime Security**, where the founders had lived inside the email-security stack long enough to have a non-obvious belief about why incumbents couldn't ship the product they wanted. WithAI's analogue is real: Ian was explicitly tasked at Bridgewater with "how can we use LLMs to invest?" and held the equity book at the same time, which is the rare combination of buyer-side and builder-side context that the typical YC AI-agent founder lacks. Where WithAI diverges from the Sublime rung is that I cannot find a published artifact of Ian's non-obvious belief -- no essay on why hedge fund agent workflows fail, no public writing on the verifier problem he himself flags on the YC page, just a LinkedIn tagline. Domain depth without writing is the most reliable signal that the founder is responding to a market opportunity rather than executing a mission, and WithAI gives me credentials without conviction artifacts.

The **Pass Example** failure mode -- "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, three months out of YC, $30M post" -- is the LOW rung WithAI maps onto for category sleeve. WithAI is literally that template with the vertical filled in: AI command center for fundamental equity hedge funds, >$100K ARR three months in, Hebbia at $700M and Rogo at $750M sitting on $273M of combined dry powder above them. "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024) is the operating frame -- WithAI isn't a fundamentally disruptive category, it's a workflow wrapper on top of frontier models in a vertical where Hebbia already claims ~30% asset-manager penetration. The Bridgewater angels and Princeton degrees re-rate the founders, not the sleeve.

On round mispricing the mechanism that kills WithAI over the next 18 months is obvious: Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour are the most legible enterprise-AI angels a hedge fund vertical startup could land, and that legibility is exactly what makes this round consensus-priced rather than mispriced. Their signal compounds into a competitive seed process at YC Demo Day, not into a discount for me. WithAI's dossier doesn't name a single tier-1 fund that passed -- silence on round dynamics, and I punish silence here because price discipline is the entire alpha.

On capital structure the counterfactual is what would have to be true for WithAI to flip into venture-shaped territory: the financing instrument would need to be something other than a standard SAFE on a standard Delaware C-corp -- a revenue-share against the Bridgewater-angel-driven enterprise pipeline, a holdco wrapping Ian's expert-network earnings, anything structurally illegible to other funds. The dossier shows none of that. WithAI is taking a vanilla cap table into a category where Rogo just raised $75M Series C at $750M post in January 2026; WithAI will need five-plus rounds at escalating prices to clear the same regatta tacks Rogo has already cleared, and each tack will be priced against Rogo's velocity, not WithAI's.

WithAI's founder-as-brand axis is where the dossier is loudest in its silence. Ben Finch has 303 Twitter followers. Ian's LinkedIn says "AI for fundamental public equity investors." Neither founder has a newsletter, a podcast, a Substack, a Discord, or any organic surface that compounds outside of warm Bridgewater intros. Compare to **John Fish / Bookshelved** in January 2024, where the entire underwrite was "this person already owns the distribution channel and is now choosing to point it at a business." WithAI has zero of that, which is fine for an enterprise sales motion but removes the only mechanism that lets me underwrite around a consensus-priced round.

My 36-month forecast for WithAI: acqui-hired by Hebbia or Rogo for $15-40M in 2027-2028 as the funded incumbents consolidate the hedge-fund workflow layer, OR stuck at $3-8M ARR with a small named-fund customer base and a flat Series A from a finance-vertical specialist like FinTech Collective. Either outcome returns the founders modestly and returns the seed-priced angel approximately zero net of dilution -- this is the canonical factory-farmed-unicorn trajectory in a sleeve where the factory is already running someone else's product.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass is the **Pinterest** override: operator-friend channel sourcing through Bridgewater's co-CIOs overrode price discipline for Ben Silbermann, and Greg Jensen / Karen Karniol-Tambour are structurally that kind of channel for WithAI -- they will route every fundamental equity hedge fund seeking an AI workflow product directly to Ian's calendar. That's a real distribution moat hiding inside an enterprise sales motion. But the Pinterest override required a category I had conviction in (consumer social, pre-consensus) plus missionary temperament, and WithAI fails the category test directly -- "AI is just more internet" means the angel-channel advantage is being deployed inside the wrong sleeve, where Hebbia's $130M and Rogo's $143M of capital flatten any distribution edge within 24 months. The one verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: WithAI signs three named multi-billion-dollar funds at >$500K ACV apiece within six months, demonstrating that the client-cloud single-tenant deployment is a structural moat Hebbia cannot copy with its multi-tenant architecture -- absent that proof, I'm out before the meeting starts.
