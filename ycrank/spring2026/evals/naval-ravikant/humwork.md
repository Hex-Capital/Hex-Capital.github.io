# Humwork -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Humwork is a real two-sided marketplace whose distribution lives on rented land. Yash Goenka and Rohan Datta are routing AI agents to 3,000+ verified experts through an MCP server embedded inside Claude Code, Cursor, Lovable, Replit, Cline, and OpenClaw -- which means Humwork's network mechanics are top-quartile in my framework while its leverage architecture is structurally permissioned by whoever controls those agent surfaces. That high-network / low-leverage pairing is the signature: a Stack Overflow-shaped supply graph riding on an App Store-shaped distribution graph.

Uber (2010) is the load-bearing analogy for Humwork on the network dimension -- two-sided liquidity where adding one verified supply node increases the value of every demand-side query, and where match latency itself becomes the moat once it falls below a threshold no incumbent can match. Humwork's 2,858 beta resolutions across 3,000+ experts at an 87% resolution rate and <2-minute first reply (theagenttimes.com, April 15 2026) is the early shape of that compounding -- the 130% net retention in the Software Engineering domain is the first real signal that one resolved agent-failure pulls the next one. Where Humwork diverges from the Uber template is that Uber owned the consumer endpoint; Humwork's "consumer" is an agent inside someone else's IDE, which means the network grows but the relationship with the buyer never lands on Humwork's surface.

Anchorage Digital is the only entry in my ledger that flunks the permissionless-leverage gate the way Humwork does, and Anchorage cleared the bar only because an OCC Treasury charter became its non-permissioned moat. Humwork has nothing equivalent -- MCP is a protocol Anthropic shipped and Anthropic can extend, and the moment Claude Code ships a first-party "ask a human" provider the way Cursor shipped first-party background agents, Humwork's wedge is a feature flag inside someone else's product. The dossier itself names this in the risk section: distribution depends entirely on third-party MCP surfaces. This is capital-and-labor leverage dressed in code-leverage clothing -- the code is real, the permissionless-ness is borrowed.

Specific knowledge compounds well here over an 18-month horizon. Yash Goenka shipped Qualify.bot (YC S25) doing AI phone agents for commercial lenders and previously built Phonecall.bot/Orange AI; Rohan Datta operated an AI voice platform that pushed >1M call minutes. Real-time low-latency agent-to-human routing over MCP is the exact technical surface both founders have shipped before -- this isn't an ex-McKinsey identifying TAM, it is two operators rebuilding a sub-30-second routing system in a new substrate. The specific-knowledge gate clears at top of MID rung, comfortably above the absent-evidence default.

Pivot room for Humwork is real but narrower than the dossier's $674B gig-economy framing suggests -- the only credible adjacencies are agent-side RLHF/training-data labeling (the Scale AI vector), HumanLayer-style approval infrastructure, or vertical expert networks for specific failure modes like legal review of LLM-generated contracts. That is enough to absorb one pivot inside seed runway but not the two-to-three Twitter (2007) had when it walked away from Odeo. The market shape is platform-adjacent rather than platform-shaped.

The founder trio is where I have to flag what my framework treats as quiet but serious: Yash Goenka is publicly listed as Co-founder & CEO of both Qualify.bot (YC S25, launched 2025) and Humwork (YC P26, launched April 2026) with no public clarification of the relationship between them. The Buffett test is energy as well as intelligence and integrity. A founder running two YC companies eight months apart is either winding the first one down silently -- in which case say so -- or splitting attention across two CEO seats, which is the inverse of the 10-year-partner posture I underwrite. Nothing about Yash's behavior reads dishonest; the integrity leg is fine. The energy leg is the unresolved one.

Forecast at 36 months: Humwork is acqui-hired by Anthropic, Cursor, or Replit for $30-80M as a native "ask a human" provider inside the agent surface, OR it pivots into RLHF/expert-labeling for AI labs once a first-party MCP human-handoff provider compresses the original wedge. The mechanism producing this is the dimensional signature -- a strong network growing on a distribution surface Humwork does not own, which is exactly the pattern that resolves into a strategic exit rather than a standalone outcome.

The strongest bear case against my own neutrality is the Anchorage precedent: I waived permissionless-leverage there because a regulatory charter became permission-as-moat, and a first-mover MCP integration with 3,000+ verified experts and trust-and-safety infrastructure across legal/financial domains could theoretically become a similar non-permission-based moat -- the expert pool and the liability layer compound even if Anthropic ships a competing provider. The bull says Humwork becomes the Stripe of agent-to-human, where the integration was open but the network effect was won early. What kills that bull case in the Humwork dossier specifically is that Stripe owned the API relationship with the buyer; Humwork's buyer is an agent invoked by a user whose loyalty is to Claude Code or Cursor, not to Humwork. The condition that would flip my verdict from neutral to invest is a signed multi-year partnership making Humwork the default human-fallback inside Anthropic, Cursor, or Replit -- absent that, the leverage architecture stays borrowed and the asymmetric upside compresses into a strategic-acquisition geometry rather than a 1,000x-plus geometry. At neutral, I pass at angel size and watch the next two quarters for whether a first-party provider ships against them.
