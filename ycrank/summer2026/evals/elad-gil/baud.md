# Baud -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Baud is two credible silicon engineers — Sarang Zambare (Peloton Guide to 100K+ devices) and Eric Taylor (3+ tape-outs at NVIDIA and Enfabrica) — taking a textbook multi-miracle bet on a proprietary "arithmetic representation that losslessly eliminates multiplications," and that high-on-shipping, low-on-single-miracle pairing is the exact "capitalization issues and science risk and market risk" stack I told Tim Ferriss in April 2026 I have largely avoided. The 50M-parameter FPGA demo at 1000+ tokens/sec is a real artifact, not vaporware, but it's the opening move of a $200M-to-$1B capital game Baud has not yet entered.

On founder shipping rhythm — Baud's highest-scoring dimension — the **Perplexity** (2022) template is Aravind pinging me on LinkedIn and showing up a week later with the thing built. Baud's analog is partial: Zambare actually shipped ML for Peloton Guide "from concept to 100,000+ devices shipped" on Qualcomm silicon, and Taylor has "three-plus tape-outs, two major IP releases" across NVIDIA, NXP, Arteris, and Enfabrica. That is genuine prior-shipping muscle on both halves of the silicon-plus-compiler problem, which is rarer than the dossier makes it sound. Where Baud diverges from the Perplexity rung is the between-meetings cadence — the dossier shows one FPGA demo and a Tally beta form, not the weekly artifact compounding that turned Aravind's pings into a conviction check. Prior shipping is necessary but not sufficient; the Perplexity pattern is the second derivative.

The **Lyft** Series C pass is the load-bearing analogy for Baud's lowest dimension, the index-distribution-position axis. I passed Lyft on winner-take-all-but-not-by-you math — #2 in an oligopoly captures a fraction of #1's economics — and the same structural problem appears in Baud's competitive set: Etched (~$1B raised, $5B valuation), MatX ($600M+, ex-Google TPU team), Taalas ($219M), Cerebras ($1B in 2026), Tenstorrent. Baud must *beat* these named incumbents on either training-versus-inference scope, programmability, or efficiency to win share — there is no workflow path every AI-lab procurement must touch the way Stripe sits in every checkout. **Color Genomics** (2015) is the inverse template I built myself: workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing, not betting on the sequencer. Baud is betting on the sequencer.

Market catalyst — mechanism analysis: the AI-compute window is real ($8.3B of 2026 chip-startup funding per Dealroom, Etched's $5B valuation round) but Baud doesn't sit in a *re-opening*; it sits in a froth. A re-opening is **Anduril** (2022) in post-Ukraine defense, where peers had misread the market as dead. Baud's market is the opposite — peers have correctly read it as the hottest silicon category in a decade, which is why MatX raised $500M in February and Taalas raised $169M the same month. Over 18 months that froth compounds against Baud, not for it: every dollar that goes to a better-capitalized competitor narrows Baud's path to a credible tape-out before the next AI-compute funding cycle resets.

Revenue velocity — counterfactual: for Baud to flip from a 6/25 to a 15/25 on this axis in the next 12 months, the dossier would need a named tape-out-contingent purchase commitment from an OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, or hyperscaler with dollar figures attached. That is the **Harvey** (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" pattern translated to silicon. The current state — Tally beta form, no pricing page, no disclosed waitlist count — is the absence of a curve, and in the AI cycle silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose.

Single-miracle — the killer dimension and Baud's lowest. **Decagon** (2023) requires one miracle: LLMs work at customer support. Baud requires at least five independent miracles: (1) the multiplier-elimination math is genuinely lossless at frontier-model scale, not just at 50M params, (2) the ASIC tape-out lands at a competitive process node, (3) the PyTorch compiler (torch.distributed, FSDP2, tensor parallelism) reaches CUDA-ecosystem parity, (4) AI labs procure non-NVIDIA training silicon at scale despite Google TPU, Meta MTIA, and Amazon Trainium absorbing internal demand, and (5) Baud raises into the $200M–$1B band its competitors already occupy. Compound those probabilities and the math doesn't pencil — this is exactly the NFX *Unusual Signs* test I almost never frame publicly but which gates every science-adjacent pass I write.

Forecast, 36 months: Baud reaches first silicon tape-out only if it raises a $40M-plus Series A on the Zambare/Taylor pedigree alone within 12 months; absent that lead, Baud runs out of runway short of tape-out and acqui-hires to NVIDIA, AMD, or one of the better-capitalized peers — most likely MatX or Etched — for under $50M, with the arithmetic-representation IP and Taylor's RTL muscle as the assets in motion. This is consistent with the high-shipping / low-single-miracle signature: credible engineers exit credibly when the multi-miracle math defeats the capital plan.

The strongest bull case is that this is a hidden **Color Genomics** (2015) pattern — workflow infrastructure (the PyTorch-compatible compiler) on top of a hard-tech layer (the ASIC) — and Taylor's three-plus tape-outs at NVIDIA and Enfabrica de-risk the silicon-execution miracle the way Trae Stephens's Palantir muscle de-risked Anduril's distribution miracle. If the FPGA-emulated 1000 tokens/sec generalizes losslessly, the math-architecture step-function is exactly the kind of contrarian secret peers will misprice. That bull case stings because Taylor's tape-out history is genuinely scarce. It loses to the dossier on capital: Baud at YC pre-seed is staring at competitors who have *already* raised $219M to $1B, and the "PyTorch-compatible compiler" claim is asserted on baudlabs.ai with zero third-party benchmark — Color worked because sequencing chemistry was commodity and the workflow layer was the bet; for Baud, the silicon *is* the bet, and the compiler is downstream of a tape-out that hasn't happened. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a named AI lab (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI) signs a tape-out-contingent purchase commitment with disclosed dollar value in the next 12 months. Until then, this is a pass on the same logic I applied to fusion and novel battery chem — market first, but the single-miracle test before everything.
