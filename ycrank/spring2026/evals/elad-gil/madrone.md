# Madrone -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Madrone is a two-person hardware cooling startup pitching dew-point chillers into Texas hyperscale -- which is to say, Madrone is the exact shape of company my "I've largely avoided science projects" line (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) was built to filter out, with Erik Meike's compressor-less A/C patent-pending as the one shipped artifact saving it from a same-day pass. The high/low signature is unmistakable: Meike's prior shipping is a real founder-execution signal, but Madrone requires three independent miracles -- dew-point thermodynamics holding at 45?C+ Texas wet-bulb, hyperscale procurement qualification against Vertiv's NVIDIA GB200 reference-design slot, and hardware manufacturing scale-up off a YC standard deal -- and that's the architecture I compound probabilities on and decline.

**Anduril** (2022) is the ladder rung this market-catalyst story wants to climb -- defense post-Ukraine was a specific 24-month re-opening, and Madrone's pitch ("Texas hyperscale + 45?C ambient + AI-induced grid pressure," per Data Center Dynamics 2025) is genuinely a real catalyst in the same template. The divergence is that Anduril's catalyst was procurement-side -- the Pentagon *itself* changed its buying behavior -- while Madrone's catalyst is a derivative thermal exposure created by someone *else's* buildout, which means Vertiv ($9B+ 2025 revenue, NVIDIA GB200 reference partner per 24/7 Wall St Jan 2026) gets to harvest the catalyst inside their existing channel before a 2-person Mountain View team gets a pilot signed. The catalyst is real; Madrone's positioning to capture it is not Anduril-grade.

**Harvey** (2023) is the revenue-velocity rung -- "$3B ? $5B ? $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) is what "just working" looks like, and on this axis Madrone is at the opposite end of the ladder with zero disclosed revenue, zero LOIs, zero pilots, and a YC LinkedIn launch post (Jan 2026) as the headline distribution event. Hardware cooling at hyperscale doesn't compound month-over-month at angel stage the way Harvey did, which is fine for hardware -- but it also means I lose the single signal I rely on most in the AI cycle to distinguish "false signal" from "stuff that is just working," and NVIDIA Inception membership is a marketing affiliation, not a customer.

Erik Meike's compressor-less residential A/C system with patent pending (Harvey Mudd magazine) plus Founding Engineer at Watney Robotics on datacenter infrastructure is the strongest single dossier fact in Madrone's favor -- it's a real shipped artifact in an adjacent thermal-hardware problem, and that's what I'm scoring on, not the Apple Hardware Development line. The mechanism over 18 months: hardware-shipping rhythm doesn't compound between investor meetings the way Aravind's **Perplexity** (2022) builds did -- a thermal prototype iterates in quarters, not weeks -- so Meike's shipping signal won't *grow* during diligence the way software founders' signals do. Akshay Trikha's UC Berkeley materials-science + QuantumScape LLM-tooling background is adjacent but not load-bearing on the dew-point thermodynamics question, which is where the science risk actually lives.

Madrone's distribution position is structurally non-index: to win, Madrone has to *beat* Vertiv, Munters, and Nortek (Facebook's StatePoint partner, per Facebook Engineering 2018) on a chilled-water output spec that Munters explicitly doesn't deliver and Vertiv delivers via chillers -- this is a feature-fight inside an incumbent-qualified procurement channel, not the **Stripe** (2010) pattern where every new e-commerce entrant touched the rails by default. The counterfactual that would flip Madrone a tier: a signed pilot with a named hyperscaler (Meta, Microsoft, or an Equinix-class colo) inside Q3 2026, which would convert the NVIDIA Inception affiliation from marketing-program to procurement-grade and put Madrone in the workflow path the way Anduril sits in defense modernization's workflow path. Absent that, Madrone is fighting for share against $9B+ incumbents with $500K.

The single-miracle test is where Madrone fails most cleanly -- compare to **Decagon** (2023), where the miracle was "LLM accuracy at customer support" and the remaining work was GTM and product execution on a validated capability. Madrone needs (1) dew-point cycle holding 20?C supply water at Texas wet-bulb conditions that the thermodynamic literature says will eat the performance margin in humid summers, (2) hyperscale procurement qualification against Vertiv's NVIDIA reference-design lock, and (3) industrial hardware manufacturing scale-up off pre-seed capitalization -- and these miracles are independent, so I compound the probabilities and the math doesn't pencil.

Forecast at 36 months: Madrone is either dead or acqui-hired by Munters, Nortek, or a thermal-systems OEM for sub-$50M with Meike absorbed as a thermal-engineering hire, never crossing $5M ARR -- the wet-bulb ceiling shows up in the first Texas summer pilot data and procurement cycles outrun the runway.

The strongest case against the pass runs through **Color Genomics** (2015), my own exception: workflow infrastructure on top of a commodity hard-tech layer beats betting on the hard-tech layer itself, and a bull could argue Madrone is exactly that -- a thermal-infrastructure layer that every Texas AI data center integrates against as the buildout compounds, with NVIDIA Inception as the analog to Color's lab-channel anchor. The dismantling: Color worked because I *operated* it and architected the workflow around commodity sequencing where the chemistry was already solved -- Madrone's dew-point cycle is *not* solved at Texas wet-bulb, the science risk lives inside the box, and NVIDIA Inception is a brand program, not Genentech-grade lab access. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a named-hyperscaler pilot with measured 30% energy-and-water reduction reported in field conditions above 35?C wet-bulb by end of 2026. Until then, Madrone is the multi-miracle hardware bet my framework was built to decline.
