# General Instinct -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

General Instinct is a credentialed-outsider technical pair selling a software-only compression runtime into atoms-heavy customers without owning any atoms themselves -- Guanming Wang from DeepMind and Bill Jiao from Siemens' first multimodal foundation model is a strong pedigree signal sitting on top of a structurally weak moat surface. The signature, in one phrase: high lineage, low atoms. Wang has the Cambridge MPhil and the Google DeepMind AI Masters Scholarship; the runtime ships into Jetson, Snapdragon, ARM, and Apple Neural Engine -- silicon General Instinct does not control and whose vendors monetize their own first-party compilers.

Stripe (2010) is the load-bearing analogy for the contrarian-secret axis: Patrick Collison named a specific incumbent failure mode -- legacy processors structurally could not ship developer-first payments because their business model rewarded enterprise integration revenue, not seven-line APIs. General Instinct gestures at a parallel structural truth -- NVIDIA TensorRT, Qualcomm AI Engine, and Apple Core ML cannot ship a cross-silicon runtime because they monetize silicon, so the only entity that can serve a robotics OEM with a multi-chip BOM is a third party. That is a real secret. General Instinct does not name it. The YC page reads "any frontier model, any edge device, within any latency budget" -- a tooling claim, not a thesis. Where Collison opened with "Stripe is for developers and everyone else has the wrong incentives," General Instinct opens with a feature list. The secret is latent in the dossier and absent from the founders' articulation, and the Zero to One filter penalizes the gap between the two.

Cleantech 1.0 is the relevant failure mechanism for the monopoly-trajectory axis -- a wave of capital that mistook a real technological frontier for a defensible business and got commoditized by Chinese manufacturing and incumbent absorption. The pattern in General Instinct is the same shape on a faster clock: Qualcomm acquired Edge Impulse on March 10, 2025, llama.cpp and ONNX Runtime and TensorRT-LLM ship the same primitives open-source, and Modular has already raised $380M at $1.6B post to be the cross-hardware runtime layer for the datacenter-plus-edge category. General Instinct's pricing power story terminates at "we compress better than the open-source default into a latency budget" -- a feature, not a moat. The dossier's single production proof (111ms cold start, 100% inside 150ms on Jetson Orin NX) is a benchmark, not a monopoly trajectory.

Mechanism analysis on the definite-plan axis: the named device list -- Jetson, mobile NPUs, ARM CPUs, Apple Neural Engine, Snapdragons -- is concrete, and the latency-budget formulation ("model + device + budget ? runtime") commits General Instinct to a definite engineering surface rather than the iterate-to-PMF mode that Zero to One was written against. Over 18 months this compounds in one direction: each new device profile compresses faster to add than the last, and the accumulated profiles become switching cost for any customer with a multi-chip product line. General Instinct gets credit here. What it does not get credit for is a multi-year roadmap of the SpaceX shape -- there is no "by 2029 we run a 70B-parameter VLM on a $40 NPU at 60Hz" commitment in the dossier, only the current capability statement.

Counterfactual on founder lineage: for General Instinct's score to flip a tier upward, either Wang or Jiao would need to have shipped a frontier-class model to production at DeepMind or Siemens with attribution -- not "worked on Siemens' first multimodal foundation model" but "shipped the inference stack that runs in N Siemens factories." The Cambridge MPhil, the DeepMind Scholarship, and the Agno hackathon win ($5,000) are credentialed-outsider technical signal of the Collison-at-Stripe genus, but the dossier shows research pedigree, not shipped-to-scale operator status. Asana (2010) cleared this bar because Moskovitz had already shipped Facebook to a billion users; Quora (2010) cleared it because D'Angelo had been Facebook CTO. General Instinct is one shipped product short of that override.

Single-anchor parallel on the atoms axis: Anduril (2017) is the reference, and General Instinct is its photographic negative. Luckey owned the hardware, sold to a regulated customer with multi-year switching costs, and built capex barriers no pure-software competitor could match. General Instinct ships software into robotics, drones, and manufacturing customers -- the atoms are in the customer's BOM, not General Instinct's balance sheet. No defense contract, no government customer, no regulated-certification depth named. The verticals are atoms-heavy; General Instinct's position in them is atoms-light. The Founders Fund manifesto channels capital toward people who own the atoms, not the people who help others' atoms run inference faster.

The 36-month forecast: General Instinct is acquired by NVIDIA, Qualcomm, or a robotics OEM (Skydio, Figure, Physical Intelligence) for $30-80M as a talent-plus-IP tuck-in within 30 months, with the compression profile library folded into the acquirer's first-party SDK and the founders absorbed as edge-inference leads. The mechanism is the dimensional signature itself: high lineage plus low atoms is the exact profile chipmakers and robotics primes buy rather than compete with, because the founders are valuable and the moat is not. Sub-$5M ARR at acquisition. Not a fund-returner; not even close to the power-law math that Founders Fund concentrates against.

The strongest bull case against that forecast is the chipmaker-channel-conflict secret. NVIDIA cannot ship a Snapdragon-friendly compiler, Apple cannot ship a Jetson runtime, and Qualcomm's Edge Impulse acquisition locks Edge Impulse to Snapdragon -- which means a genuinely cross-silicon compression runtime is exactly what a multi-chip robotics OEM needs and exactly what no incumbent can sell. If General Instinct becomes the default cross-chip layer for physical AI, this is Modular's shape at an earlier cap table, and Wang's DeepMind pedigree is the right pedigree to ship it. The dismantle is the dossier's own commoditization paragraph: llama.cpp, ONNX Runtime, and TensorRT-LLM already ship the primitives open-source, the per-deployment licensing model implied has no pricing-power anchor against free alternatives, and General Instinct names zero defense, government, or regulated customer -- the only sector where Thiel writes cross-chip tooling checks because it is the only sector where switching costs compound. The verifiable flip condition: a signed $1M+ ARR contract with a US defense robotics prime (Anduril, Saronic, Shield AI) within 12 months. That would convert General Instinct from a Modular-shape bet to a Palantir-shape bet, and the check follows.
