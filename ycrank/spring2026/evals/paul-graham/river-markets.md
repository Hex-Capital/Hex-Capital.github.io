# River Markets -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Antonin Parrot was a quant researcher at Valkyrie Trading and presented at the Kalshi research conference before starting River Markets, which is the part of the dossier I cannot stop re-reading; the part I cannot read at all is Oscar Levy, who has no LinkedIn, no GitHub, no Twitter, no biography surfaced anywhere. River Markets is the rare profile where the organic-idea dimension is genuinely high -- a prediction-market quant building prime brokerage for prediction-market quants -- and the hacker-genealogy dimension is a ghost. That pairing makes River Markets a domain-insider company with half its founding team invisible to diligence, and it is the entire story of how I score this.

Drew Houston shipped a demo video on Hacker News before the **Dropbox** (2007) seed because he had been losing his own USB stick at MIT -- the canonical organic idea, founder-as-customer, built for himself first. River Markets is the closest analog I have seen in a while on that specific axis: Parrot was literally a junior quantitative researcher at a Chicago prop shop trading these venues, then presented research at Kalshi's own conference, then built the tool that desk would have wanted. Where River Markets diverges from the **Dropbox** (2007) rung is that Parrot is not a hacker on the public record -- no GitHub, no shipped open-source artifact, no Hacker News footprint -- so the organic pain is real but the "they themselves can build it" half of the November 2012 essay is asserted by resume, not by demo. The four named institutional clients on the homepage (Chimera Capital Management, Game Point Capital, Cleat Street, Skywalk) substitute for the demo video as the artifact: those desks signed up to something that works.

The **Reddit** (2005) founders had Aaron Swartz, who co-authored the RSS specification before the seed check; that is what hacker genealogy looks like at the high rung. River Markets has the inverse: Oscar Levy is a confirmed email address. No public profile, no repos, no presence. The failure mechanism at the low rung is that I cannot tell whether River Markets's Ed25519-signed API, server-side execution algos, and Python SDK were built by a real systems hacker or papered over by a contractor, and at pre-seed that is exactly the question the dossier should answer for me and does not. Parrot's Valkyrie pedigree is quant-research, not infrastructure-shipping, and the silent second founder is supposed to be the technical complement. The dossier does not confirm he is.

River Markets sits in a real schlep zone, and this is the part of the analysis where my framework starts pulling me back toward a check. Dual CFTC-regulated and crypto-native routing in one OMS, broker-dealer-adjacent compliance, venue API key management, integrating five separate event-contract venues one at a time -- this is the kind of unglamorous infrastructure work that **Coinbase** (2012) turned into a moat. The compounding question over 18 months is whether River Markets's integrations and OMS state become switching costs faster than Polymarket and Kalshi build native cross-venue execution themselves. The Polymarket acquisition of Dome in February 2026 cuts both ways: it removed the leading neutral aggregator, opening the lane River Markets is now running in, and it announced that venues will buy this layer rather than tolerate it.

Resourcefulness pre-funding is what makes me lean forward on River Markets despite the Levy void. Four named institutional clients live -- Chimera, Game Point, Cleat Street, Skywalk -- on a 2-person pre-seed launch site is not nothing; those are real prop shops, not logos in a Figma mockup. That is the **Airbnb** (2009) cereal-box pattern in a different medium: the founders went and got revenue-shaped commitments before the institutional check arrived. The counterfactual that would flip River Markets's score a full tier is a single screenshot of monthly routed volume in the millions; without it, four logos read as serious but unverified hustle rather than the unambiguous animal test.

The co-founder configuration is a two-person team where I can verify one person and not the other -- which against my "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) frame leaves me at neutral rather than negative, because the YC interview will have surfaced what the dossier cannot, and Harj Taggar funded them.

My 36-month forecast on River Markets: acquired by Clear Street or by Kalshi itself for $15-50M as an institutional-execution bolt-on, with Valence having taken the larger share of the independent broker lane on the back of its already-reported 1B+ contracts and $200M+ monthly volume. The mechanism is straightforward -- venues will not tolerate a third-party prime broker capturing institutional flow indefinitely, and incumbent prime brokers (Clear Street already partnered with Kalshi per Finance Magnates 2026) have the balance sheet, credit lines, and institutional sales motion that River Markets's two-person team cannot match in time.

The strongest case against my forecast is that prediction-market volume went from under $5B/month to ~$24B/month in seven months, ~40% of Kalshi is institutional, and the leading neutral aggregator just got removed from the board -- River Markets is the only independent prime broker positioned to absorb that flow, with Parrot's Valkyrie/Kalshi credibility opening doors Valence cannot reach because Valence is pitched as a "trading superapp" for the retail-adjacent buyer. That case stings. It dismantles on two facts: Clear Street is already inside Kalshi via partnership, and the dossier reports zero on Levy's technical contribution -- prime brokerage is an infrastructure business, and I do not know who is building the infrastructure. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict to Invest: Levy turns out to be a real systems hacker with a shipped artifact (HFT infra, exchange connector, a serious open-source repo), OR River Markets discloses routed monthly volume in eight figures within six months. Either one converts neutral to a check. Until then, River Markets is the right kind of schlep with the wrong kind of dossier gap.
