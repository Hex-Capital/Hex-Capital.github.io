# Nine Fives -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Nine Fives is a company where the founders lived inside the exact problem they're solving for seven years before they pitched it, but where the hacker tribe markers I instinctively scan for -- the GitHub trail, the Hacker News handle, the Lisp competition prize -- are conspicuously absent. Noah Levy spent 2016-2023 as an RF engineer at SpaceX building the Falcon 9 Radar Altimeter, the Dragon 2 S-Band Transceiver, and Starship GPS/S-Band radios, which means he was the customer of the "January 1997 revision of the USB HID specification" gear he's now replacing. That pairing -- organic founder-pain at the ceiling, but hardware-hacker rather than software-hacker lineage -- is Nine Fives's signature.

Drew Houston forgot his USB stick on a bus and built **Dropbox** (2007) for himself before anyone asked. Noah Levy debugged production RF test racks for seven years at SpaceX and got six "kickass awards" doing it, then built Nine Fives because he had personally suffered the schlep of legacy SCPI driver stacks on flight-qualified hardware. The dossier evidence that pins Nine Fives near the **Dropbox** rung but not on it: Houston's pain was personal-consumer (lost files) and instantly relatable; Noah's pain is industrial-niche (driver-bound attenuators at 6 GHz), so the organic-pain signal is unusually deep but unusually narrow. The Falcon 9 Radar Altimeter line in the LinkedIn bio is the equivalent of Houston's HN demo video -- the artifact predates the pitch.

The pass example I keep in my head -- MBA team, glossy deck, no GitHub -- fails because no founder has ever opened the IDE. Nine Fives does not exhibit that failure mechanism; Noah shipped four physical SKUs (PD8X, POE-ATTEN-6G, POE-SWITCH-6G, POE-PMETER-6G) before YC Launch, with a 2-year warranty and a 30-day money-back guarantee, which is more concrete artifact than 80% of pre-seed teams I see. But the *flavor* of hacker tribe I over-index on -- Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010), Aaron Swartz co-authoring RSS before **Reddit** (2005) -- shows up as "no public Twitter, no GitHub, no HN handle found" for both founders. They are hackers; they are not *my* hackers. I have to discount my instinct here because Noah's six SpaceX kickass awards designing flight-hardware radios is a stronger hacker signal than most GitHub stars, just denominated in a currency I'm slower to read.

Animal resourcefulness compounds over 18 months in a specific way for Nine Fives: hardware companies either ship the next three SKUs on the roadmap or they don't, and the gap between "four SKUs live with money-back guarantee" and "twelve SKUs with named SpaceX/Anduril/Skydio design wins" is the visible animal test. The Brian Armstrong wallet-prototype pattern that rescued **Coinbase** (2012) is what I'm looking for as the next data point: did Noah personally drive his POE-ATTEN-6G into a former SpaceX colleague's test rack the week after YC Launch? The dossier doesn't say, but the SpaceX alumni network is the unscalable channel here, and it's there for the taking.

Schlep blindness is where Nine Fives shines without needing to argue. RF test equipment for cellphones, drones, and satellites is the kind of work no software-only YC batchmate is going to touch -- inventory, RMA, FCC-adjacent qualification cycles, multi-quarter enterprise design-in. **Coinbase** (2012) won its batch because Armstrong wanted the bitcoin compliance grind; Nine Fives wins this axis because the Keysight/R&S/NI incumbents collectively hold ~55% of a $3.99B market behind a moat of bundled driver suites, and dislodging that requires shipping rack-mountable hardware with PoE backplanes, which is exactly the kind of physical-world grind that scares off the AI-wrapper applicants in the same Spring 2026 batch.

The co-founder configuration would flip from a 7 to a 9 if the dossier named a specific pre-SpaceX collaboration -- a college project, a shared internship, a hackathon -- because right now Nine Fives reads as "two ex-SpaceX engineers who overlapped at the same employer," which is closer to the **Reddit** (2005) dorm-roommate pattern than to it. Noah and Andrew both passed through SpaceX (Noah RF 2016-2023, Andrew on Falcon 9 first stage and Fairing 2.0), which is a strong institutional bond, but the "decade at SpaceX" framing on the about-us page is jointly-counted, not joint-built. The pair is real; the chemistry I'd verify in person.

Forecast: in 36 months Nine Fives is at $4-10M ARR selling into the SpaceX/Anduril/Skydio/Astranis alumni-adjacent hardware tier, profitable on hardware unit economics, and either independent or acquired by Keysight for $80-150M as a software-defined-rack acquihire. The mechanism is that Noah's SpaceX network converts faster than Keysight's incumbent driver stack can be rewritten, but the ceiling is bounded because RF test instruments are not a winner-take-all category -- this is a Wufoo-shaped outcome, not a **Stripe** (2010)-shaped one, and "Black Swan Farming" (September 2012) tells me to write the check anyway if the founders pass, because category ceiling is not how I pick.

The bear case stings: DS Instruments already ships a 6 GHz 90 dB programmable USB/Ethernet attenuator for $649, which is the same form factor as POE-ATTEN-6G, and Mini-Circuits has over 400 SKUs up to 67 GHz. If the moat is the NineVue rack-config web app and the LLM skill.md layer, then Nine Fives is a software company wearing hardware clothes, and the software is unproven with zero customer adoption shown. Keysight owns Connection Expert and could ship driverless REST in one product cycle, cannibalizing their own TestStand revenue only when forced -- but they will be forced. What dismantles the bear case is the schlep nobody wants to do: flight-qualified test racks at SpaceX-grade are not where DS Instruments lives, and Keysight will not voluntarily REST-ify their highest-margin software. The verifiable flip condition is twelve months out: if no named hardware OEM -- ideally a former SpaceX or Anduril colleague of Noah's -- has put a Nine Fives PD8X into a production test rack with a written reference, the organic-pain thesis was narrower than the market needed and I take the loss.
