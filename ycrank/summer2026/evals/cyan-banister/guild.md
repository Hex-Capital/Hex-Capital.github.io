# GUILD -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Kaya Celebi and Erim Gurlemis are two Columbia MSc / Duke BSc computer scientists who shipped ML at Hartree Partners and Morgan Stanley and now want to be the "AI-Native Neoprime" reshaping a $300B defense supply chain -- and the dimensional signature of GUILD is a high-rung regulated-category wrapper around a low-rung biographical origin. The hottest dimension on my rubric (capital-intensive, unsexy-regulated swing) is doing the heavy lifting; the load-bearing dimension (founder surfaced from lived experience) is missing. That pairing is exactly the inversion of my pass example, dressed in defense camo.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is my high rung on lived experience: he shipped Oculus from his parents' garage at 19, sold it to Facebook, and then walked into the Pentagon already carrying a product trajectory the buyers could touch. GUILD's founders shipped commodities ML at Hartree and a Series-66 registration at Morgan Stanley -- there is no CAGE code in their past, no DIU contract, no roughneck-equivalent they can call on a Tuesday inside a NATO ministry. The dossier is explicit: "founders' prior employers (Hartree, Morgan Stanley) are not defense entities." Luckey *was* of the place; Kaya and Erim are visiting it with a Neoprime deck. The DeepMind-Hassabis exception sometimes overrides this for polymath cross-domain compounding, but Hassabis was a chess prodigy and neuroscientist before AGI -- Celebi is an ML engineer who moved from energy trading to defense procurement, which is one domain hop, not three, and it is a hop *into* the consensus category of the year.

Crusoe (~2018) is the low-rung failure mechanism I watch for on "magically weird" -- except the mechanism there was that nobody else would touch it. GUILD walks into the inverse: techbuzz.ai puts $38B of defense-tech VC into 2025, Anduril is at $30.5B, Hadrian raised $260M from Founders Fund + Lux in July 2025, Shield AI is at $5B, Mach at $185M, and Cipher Brief has already coined "Neoprime" as a category. When the category has a name and a tier-1 league table before GUILD has a CAGE code, the round is the opposite of underpriced -- I am supposed to find them before it becomes consensus, and GUILD is showing up to the consensus party with a single-page website.

Single CEO authority (mechanism analysis over 18 months): GUILD lists Kaya Celebi as Co-Founder & CEO and Erim Gurlemis as Co-Founder without title -- which is the right structural answer on Day 1, the inverse of the Yusupov/Kroll HQ Trivia trap. But two Columbia classmates who shared a Morgan Stanley desk and a Hartree desk and now share a defense pre-seed have not yet been forced to disagree about anything load-bearing. The first real disagreement inside GUILD will be whether to chase an SBIR Phase I that pays the rent or a Tier-1 Neoprime award that takes 18 months and a cleared facility neither of them has -- and the dossier is silent on equity split, vesting, and tiebreaker protocol. Title clarity buys partial credit; the conversation has not happened.

Capital-intensive frontier swing (counterfactual): GUILD says it will "source, fund, produce, and deliver" defense contracts end-to-end with a team of two. To flip this dimension into the Anduril / SpaceX upper tier I would need to see one of three things in the next disclosure -- a signed teaming agreement with a named tier-2 supplier, a CMMC Level 2 certification timeline, or an SBIR/STRATFI award. None are in the dossier. What is present is the regulated-category aesthetic without the regulated-category artifact, which is why this dimension lands in the middle rather than the top.

Physical verifiability (single-anchor parallel to Flock Safety): Flock I sourced from reading a Wi-Fi network name at the Four Seasons because the product was a *thing* a founder was deploying into the world that I could see before he knew I was looking. GUILD's product, per guildai.co in June 2026, is a one-page tagline that reads "the AI-Native Neoprime." There is nothing to install, nothing to scan, nothing to photograph, and the brand is name-disambiguated against Guild Education, Yield Guild Games, and the "guildai" ML tooling project. I cannot ride GUILD the way I rode Uber in 2010.

Thirty-six months out, GUILD does not deliver an end-to-end prime contract against the Hadrian / Anduril / Shield AI gravity well; it pivots to AI-orchestration SaaS sold *to* primes -- a Procurement Sciences clone -- reaches sub-$500K ARR by mid-2029, and either gets aqui-hired by a tier-2 integrator or quietly winds down when the Series A round it needs to actually carry working capital does not materialize. The mechanism is the inversion of Crusoe: Crusoe's founders were of the oilfield and the category was uninvestable, so the round was underpriced and the pivot was supported; GUILD's founders are of the bank and the category is the most-funded defense thesis of 2025, so the round will be priced for execution the team is not staffed to deliver.

The bull case stings here and I want to honor it: the Neoprime tailwind is real, Diana Hu is a serious YC partner, and my own DeepMind exception (~2013) admits that pedigreed polymaths can override the lived-experience filter when cross-domain compounding is genuine -- Kaya and Erim are quants with an AI thesis entering a regulated category, and the orchestration layer above Anduril's hardware and below the DoD program office is a real white space that Anduril itself is too vertically integrated to occupy. I dismantle it on one fact: Hassabis built games and won at chess and did neuroscience before DeepMind, three domains compounding; Celebi and Gurlemis have one domain (ML on financial data) being pointed at a second (defense procurement), and the moat in defense is not the model, it is the cleared facility and the past-performance history they do not have. The condition that flips my verdict: a named prime contract award or a documented teaming agreement with a tier-1 supplier within nine months. Until then, GUILD is a Pass -- a Stanford-Goldman shape in Neoprime clothing.
