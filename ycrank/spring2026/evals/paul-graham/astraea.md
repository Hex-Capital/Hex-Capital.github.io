# Astraea -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Astraea is a right-problem, unverified-animals company: the SDTM/ADaM/TFL grind Joshua Wang and Sanmay Sarada have chosen to attack is exactly the regulatory schlep my essays were written to surface, but the dossier gives me no cereal-box, no Hacker News demo, no pre-funding stunt that tells me these two specific humans are the ones who'll outlast Saama's $430M war chest. The signature here is a HIGH schlep score paired with a LOW animal-resourcefulness score, and in my experience that pairing produces companies that get acquihired by the incumbent whose business they were trying to disrupt.

On schlep, the HIGH rung is **Coinbase** (2012) -- Armstrong's appetite for the bitcoin compliance grind that every other YC applicant in his batch was unconsciously routing around. The LOW rung is the AI-wrapper team from my Pass Example, where no offline grind, no regulatory exposure, nothing unpleasant exists for the founders to suffer through. Astraea sits closer to the Coinbase rung than to anything else in this dossier: 21 CFR Part 11 alignment, Pinnacle 21 validation, FDA submission defensibility, and the manual QC handoffs across "fragmented legacy systems" they describe are textbook schlep blindness for software-only founders. CDISC mapping is the kind of work smart people flinch from in exactly the way I described in January 2012. This is the strongest single signal Astraea has, and I am not going to undercount it.

On animal resourcefulness, the HIGH rung is **Airbnb** (2009) -- $40 cereal boxes generating real revenue from a stunt I wouldn't have invented. The LOW rung is, again, the MBA-deck team with no shipped artifact. Astraea sits well below the midpoint. The dossier lists "Top-3 Pharma" and "Top-10 Pharma" as customer categories without a single named logo, no contract value, no case study, no signed LOI with a named sponsor, and "hiring = false." Product claims of "30-50% faster biometrics cycles" without a named customer is exactly the kind of slide-deck language that looks like traction and isn't. An animal founder targeting Pfizer would tell me they got Pfizer to look at a CSV last Thursday. I don't see that here.

On organic founder-pain, the HIGH rung is **Dropbox** (2007) -- Houston's USB stick at MIT, the problem he himself had been suffering. The LOW rung is the MBA team running competitive analysis. Astraea sits in the bottom half: Sanmay Sarada's reported Baylor College of Medicine data-science internship is an adjacent biomedical-data anchor, but nothing in the dossier says either founder personally wrote a SAS program at 2am for a Phase III submission and decided to rebuild the toolchain. Joshua Wang's biometrics background is "not publicly verifiable," which is the dossier's polite way of saying nobody can find evidence he's lived inside this problem.

On hacker genealogy, the HIGH rung is **Stripe** (2010) -- Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize at 16, the kind of pre-startup technical artifact that compounds into "Stripe is the next Google." The LOW rung is non-technical founders with credentials but no shipped code. Astraea sits closer to the low rung than I'd like: no GitHub for Wang, no GitHub for Sarada, no Twitter presence, no open-source contributions, no Hacker News footprint surfaced in research. Sarada at Stanford Symbolic Systems is a positive prior, but it isn't an RSS spec contribution. Hackers leave trails, and the trail here is thin.

On co-founder configuration, the HIGH rung is **Airbnb** (2009)'s trio with two years of shared living before the company. The LOW rung is a solo founder. Astraea is a two-person team, which is the right shape, but the dossier explicitly notes "no public data on co-founder history visible" -- I take the absent-evidence default and stay neutral.

My 36-month forecast for Astraea: acquihired by Saama, Veeva, or a top-five CRO for under $30M, or dead. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- the schlep is real and the FDA-defensible mapping work is genuinely valuable, which is precisely why Saama's BRAIN/SDTM Navigator with a Pfizer deployment and a $430M 2021 financing will either out-execute Astraea on validated outputs or absorb the team. Sales cycles to Top-10 pharma run twelve-plus months; two founders with no disclosed BD pipeline don't cover that surface area before runway forces a decision.

The bull case stings: my Airbnb override is the precedent that should worry me here. I rejected Airbnb in my head before the cereal boxes flipped me, and I could be rejecting Astraea on the same axis -- unverified founders attacking a problem I find plausible. CDISC mapping is the rare schlep where LLMs in 2025-2026 actually cross the structured-transformation reliability threshold the dossier names, and if Wang and Sarada genuinely have a real Top-3 Pharma engagement behind those unnamed logos, the trajectory inverts completely. What would flip me: one named pharma sponsor with a signed paid pilot for a live Phase II/III dataset, on the record, with a contract value disclosed. Until then, the schlep is real but I haven't seen the animals.
