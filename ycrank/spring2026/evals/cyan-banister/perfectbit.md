# PerfectBit -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Peter Vajda ran Media Generation at Meta Superintelligence Lab and Seiji Yamamoto was Senior Staff on Core Llama pre- and post-training -- which means PerfectBit's two founders spent the last several years sitting on the *buyer* side of the exact problem they are now selling into, and that single fact is the only reason I am still reading the dossier instead of closing the tab. Everything else about PerfectBit -- "training data for frontier AI labs," Garry Tan as YC partner, Spring 2026 batch -- reads like the most consensus pitch in the most consensus category of the most consensus YC class of the decade. The signature is a credentialed-insider founder pair with genuine problem ownership wrapped around a category where the price discovery has already happened on Sand Hill.

Anduril is the high rung for founder-lived-experience: Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from a garage before pivoting to defense, and the lived contact with the problem space predated the company. PerfectBit's Vajda and Yamamoto pass a softer version of that test -- they were *inside* the training-data bottleneck at Meta, not reading about it in a memo -- but they were *of* the lab buyer, not *of* the verifier-substrate. Compare to Crusoe's Chase Lochmiller, who grew up adjacent to the oil-and-gas operators whose well pads he had to walk on. PerfectBit's founders did not grow up adjacent to formal proof systems and Z3 solvers; they grew up adjacent to the labs that will write the checks. That's a real form of lived experience -- closer to the DeepMind-Hassabis Known Exception of credentialed polymaths -- but it's the *customer*-side biography, not the *substrate*-side biography, and that asymmetry matters when the substrate is doing the work.

The pass example in my framework is the consensus YC AI-agent darling -- two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, $20M oversubscribed seed, $200B TAM slide -- and PerfectBit's competitive frame trips that wire in a way I cannot un-see. Scale AI at $14.3B from Meta, Surge AI raising at $15B, Mercor at $10B Series C, Snorkel at $1.3B Series D, Micro1 at $100M ARR -- five named incumbents with $5B-plus war chests are already inside the room PerfectBit is trying to enter, and the academic substrate (FOVER, RLVR per arXiv 2505.15960 and 2506.15522) is published and reproducible by the very labs PerfectBit wants as customers. That is the opposite of magically weird. That is six funds bidding before Demo Day on a thesis that *Anthropic could build in a quarter* with the founders they already employ.

PerfectBit's defensibility paragraph contains the phrase "Inferred from product positioning" three times and "No public data found" five times across moats, distribution, and revenue -- which on its own is fine for pre-seed and not something I penalize, but the *kind* of defensibility being inferred is precisely the AI-model-not-founder moat that my framework treats as structurally replaceable. If the moat compounds in a proprietary library of verified domains, the question for 18 months from now is whether OpenAI's or Anthropic's internal team -- the team Yamamoto just left -- builds the same library in-house once they read the FOVER paper. The mechanism that ought to compound a moat (closed customer feedback, embedded engineers in lab fine-tuning loops) cannot get going with a customer pool of five to ten frontier labs, where any one churn is a 10-20% revenue event.

The dossier names two co-founders, no CEO, no tiebreaker, no equity split -- both senior Meta directors, both alpha-coded by tenure, both with PhDs from EPFL and Rice respectively. For PerfectBit's score on unambiguous-CEO authority to flip from default to high, I would need to see one of them named as CEO with documented operating authority, the way Palmer Luckey was at Anduril Day 1 -- not the HQ Trivia structure where Yusupov and Kroll were peers and the partnership collapsed in feud. Two senior researchers from the same Meta org, both accustomed to leading their own programs, is exactly the configuration that becomes a governance question in year two.

Flock Safety I sourced by reading the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons -- a tactile artifact I could observe in my own physical environment before any metric existed. PerfectBit's product is a B2B data pipeline shipped to five-to-ten frontier-lab buyers under what is almost certainly NDA; no normal human can install it, ride it, scan it, or photograph it. The dossier confirms no Product Hunt launch, no press coverage, no retrievable Twitter or LinkedIn follower counts, no public customer logos. This is the most physically invisible product I could be asked to evaluate, and that invisibility is structural to the business, not an artifact of pre-seed stage.

PerfectBit in 36 months is acqui-hired back into Meta, OpenAI, or Anthropic at a $40-120M range, the founders return to running data programs inside a frontier lab where the verifier-grounded technique gets absorbed into the incumbent's internal pipeline, and the independent company does not survive as a standalone -- the mechanism being that a five-to-ten-customer market with $14B-funded incumbents and a published academic substrate cannot sustain a venture-scale third-party data vendor when the founders' own most natural buyer is also their most natural acquirer.

The bull case stings: Vajda and Yamamoto are the DeepMind Known Exception in miniature -- credentialed insiders whose polymath compounding (physics PhD plus Llama pre-training plus media generation plus EPFL CS) could produce a verifier-substrate library that one frontier lab signs a $50M+ annual contract for, at which point PerfectBit is profitable on a two-person team and the customer-concentration risk inverts into customer-intimacy moat. The way I dismantle that case is the consensus signal: Scale lost lab customers post-Meta-investment per TechCrunch October 2025, and the labs that defected are talking to *every* alternative vendor including Surge, Mercor, Snorkel, Micro1, and any sufficiently credentialed ex-lab two-person team -- PerfectBit is one of N, not the one. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a signed contract with a named frontier lab (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, or Google DeepMind) at $5M+ ARR disclosed before YC Demo Day; absent that, PerfectBit is a Pass-leaning Neutral that I would happily watch Garry Tan write the check on without me.
