# Async -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Async is a three-vertical custom-agent services shop pitching law, healthcare, and real estate off a two-person team, where the single dimension that reads cleanly is the *miracle count* (one -- foundation-model tool use) and the dimension that reads worst is *revenue velocity* -- the dossier for Async discloses zero ARR, zero paid logo count, and one named testimonial (Adriena Clifton, Clifton Law PLLC), which in the current AI cycle is itself the tell. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and Async is an AI company one year into YC S25 with no numbers to show.

**Anduril** (2022) is the market-catalyst template I keep in my head: a specific 24-month re-opening (post-Ukraine Pentagon software inadequacy) that reset the math on a category peers misread as dead. Async's dossier reaches for a "why now" and lands on "[Inferred]: Frontier model tool-use and long-context capabilities crossed a threshold in 2024?2025" -- inferred, not company-stated, and the same catalyst every YC S25 AI-agent company is claiming. Async doesn't name a regulatory shift, an incumbent failure mode, or a distribution rupture in law/healthcare/real-estate SMB. Legal AI's re-opening was **Harvey** (2023) and CaseText -- which is why *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) puts legal in the "winners declared" column. Async is skating for a puck that already went past two years ago.

**Lyft** Series C is the ladder rung I pattern-match to when a company enters a market whose leaders are already indexing the category, and the mechanism is share inversion at scale -- which the challenger almost never gets. Async's Word add-in interface is the same surface Spellbook ($63M raised, Oct 2025 Series B led by Khosla) and Harvey ($1.22B raised, $11B post-money) already occupy, and Legora sits at $5.55B on $700M+ raised. That is not a "wide open" sub-market from *AI Market Clarity* -- that is the closed one. Async's dossier tries to escape by spanning three verticals with two people, which reads as a positioning hedge, not an index position. Adding HIPAA scope on top of a SOC 2 Type I posture to reach healthcare, while Harvey's growth round compounds, is fighting the wrong incumbent from the wrong altitude.

Async's revenue-velocity dimension compounds badly over 18 months on a specific mechanism: services-model engagements ("partner & audit ? scope & deploy ? prove & expand ? maintain & compound," withasync.com) at team size 2 gate throughput at founder hours, so even if Ben Smith and Connor Park close ten firms, the shape is a consulting P&L, not the Harvey curve. There is no disclosed ARR, no MoM number, no logo count beyond Clifton Law PLLC -- and the predecessor product Skope's headline metric is 55 Hacker News points from August 2025, which is Launch HN tourism, not "just working" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025).

For founder-ships-between-meetings, the counterfactual that would flip Async a tier: Connor Park's GitHub (@connorpark24, 18 followers, 11 repos, 72 total stars) would need to show a shipped independent product with real users, and Ben Smith's Merit tenure would need a named product artifact he owned. Neither is in the dossier. What the dossier *does* show is three pivots inside 12 months -- nonprofit fundraising agents ? Skope outcome-based billing ? Skope AI Agents for Law Firms ? Async AI Agents for Service Businesses -- which reads to some investors as shipping rhythm and reads to me as positioning thrash. Aravind at **Perplexity** (2022) shipped the *same product* better every week; Async shipped a different company every quarter.

Single-miracle-wise, Async does earn its cleanest score against **Decagon** (2023) as an anchor -- one validated capability (LLM tool use on document workflows) plus GTM/product execution, no chemistry risk, no regulatory approval risk in the base case. The wobble is that spanning law, healthcare, and real estate turns one execution problem into three GTM motions, and healthcare specifically drags HIPAA into a company still at SOC 2 Type I. Decagon picked one wedge (post-LLM customer support) and rode it; Async picked three and hasn't demonstrated it can staff any of them.

My 36-month forecast for Async: dead or acqui-hired for under $25M by a mid-tier legal-AI player (Spellbook-adjacent, not Harvey) as an engineering team, having pivoted at least once more toward a single vertical -- most likely narrowing to solo/boutique law firms like Clifton Law and abandoning the healthcare and real-estate legs. The mechanism is straightforward: no disclosed velocity plus three-vertical spread plus a Word-add-in interface that Spellbook already owns equals margin compression before Async reaches $2M ARR, which in this funding market is not enough to raise a real Series A.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Async's "audit ? scope ? deploy ? maintain" engagement model is exactly how forward-deployed services businesses (Palantir, and to a lesser extent early Applied Intuition) built proprietary workflow data that later productized into moats -- and SMB service firms are genuinely underserved by Harvey and Legora, who both chase AmLaw 100. That case stings because it is my own *"infrastructure on top of hard thing"* pattern applied to services on top of foundation models. It fails because Palantir had Alex Karp's operator discipline and a decade of government-contract lock-in; Async's dossier shows neither operator archetype nor structural lock-in, and the SMB-legal wedge is where Spellbook already lives. Flip condition on Async: ten paid firms with disclosed >20% MoM revenue growth in a single vertical within nine months, and I re-underwrite -- until then, pass.
