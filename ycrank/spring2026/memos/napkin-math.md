## The Idea

- **Problem:** Calorie counting in incumbent food trackers is tedious for "real people" who want to understand eating patterns without numeric targets, with users currently relying on MyFitnessPal-style manual logging (YC company page).
- **Approach:** AI-powered food journal with gamified, social meal logging — users log meals visually and receive personalized insights and "realistic goals" instead of calorie/macro targets (napkinmath.club via search snippet; YC company page).
- **Differentiation:** Positioned as "Strava for food" (search snippet) — vs. MyFitnessPal (manual calorie/macro entry), vs. Cal AI (photo-to-calorie tracker), vs. MacroFactor (adaptive macro coaching); Napkin Math removes calorie targets and emphasizes social sharing ("share your table with friends," YC description).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: Consumer freemium/subscription model typical of iOS health apps; App Store listing exists ("Napkin Math: Gamify Nutrition," App Store ID 6758642182) but pricing not disclosed publicly.
- **TAM/SAM:** MyFitnessPal alone generated $310M revenue in 2025 with 220M registered users and 30M MAUs (Business of Apps, 2025/2026); no public TAM/SAM data found for the specific "anti-calorie-counting AI food journal" segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** iOS App Store + TestFlight beta with invite-based onboarding (search snippet from App Store/TestFlight listing); [Inferred]: social/viral acquisition through the "share your table with friends" feature, consistent with the "Strava for food" framing.

## Key Risks

- **Incumbent absorption of differentiator:** MyFitnessPal acquired Cal AI in March 2026 (Athletech News) and is integrating AI photo logging into a 220M-user base (Business of Apps); a "no-calorie-counting" mode could be added as a setting rather than requiring a new app, compressing the differentiation window.
- **Crowded AI food-tracker category:** Cal AI scaled to $34M-$50M ARR bootstrapped (Latka; Yadegari statements), MacroFactor to ~$500K MRR (Prospeo), and roundups list Vora, Cronometer, FatSecret, Fitia, Welling.ai, SnapCalorie, Lose It as active alternatives (Hoot Fitness, Nutrola, 2026) — Napkin Math must win on positioning rather than feature parity.
- **Naming and discoverability collision:** "Napkin Math" is a widely used phrase and overlaps with a popular podcast on Spotify/Apple Podcasts and a separate Every newsletter column (search results) — risk of brand confusion and SEO dilution; legal entity is "Paper Star, Corp." (App Store listing).
- **Traction not yet visible:** App Store has insufficient ratings to display (App Store, 2026), no public revenue, user counts, press, or social follower data, and no funding announcement located — execution-stage risk on demonstrating consumer pull-through.
- **AI food-recognition accuracy:** MyFitnessPal's database (14M+ items) is positioned as a moat that "most independent apps do not have" (Vora Blog, 2026); Napkin Math's reliance on AI inference rather than verified databases could produce errors that erode trust if its insights layer is built on inaccurate logs.
