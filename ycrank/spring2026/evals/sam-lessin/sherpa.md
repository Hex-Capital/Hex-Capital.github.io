# Sherpa -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Optimizely does $400M in ARR. VWO just got acquired for $200M. Webflow bought Intellimize in April 2024 specifically to add ML-driven optimization to its platform. And now Sherpa walks in and says "we use LLMs to generate A/B test variants and deploy winners automatically." My immediate question isn't "what if they're right?" -- it's "what if Optimizely's product team ships this as a feature by Q3?" Because that's the actual competitive dynamic here. Sherpa is building exactly the kind of capability that every incumbent in the CRO space is already roadmapping. The dossier itself says it: "Optimizely and VWO have AI roadmaps and could add generative variant capabilities as features." When I see that sentence in a company's own competitive analysis, I know the founders see the risk. The question is whether they have an answer, and I don't see one.

Sherpa's category -- AI-powered marketing automation -- is the single most consensus-hot space in venture right now. Every tag on Sherpa's YC page screams it: "Artificial Intelligence, SaaS, B2B, Marketing, AI." That's not a list of tags, that's a list of reasons every other investor at demo day is bidding up the price. When I seeded Solana at four cents, the entire market had written off alternative Layer 1s. The mispricing was enormous because the consensus was wrong. With Sherpa, the consensus is that AI agents automating workflows is a massive opportunity -- and the consensus is probably right about the category! The problem is that the upside is already in the price, and the value will accrue to the platforms with distribution, not the wrapper. This is the mobile playbook repeating itself: great technology, not disruptive. Every CRO incumbent is cloud-native and subscription-based. Sherpa is building their next feature.

Ethan Kinnan and Norbu Sonam both worked at Lindy, and Sherpa's flagship case study is... Lindy. Forty experiments in 30 days with +30% conversion lift -- at their former employer. Everett Butler from Lindy gave them a testimonial. I don't doubt the result is real, but I need to see Sherpa replicate that with a company where the founders don't have the CEO's phone number from their old job. Gauge and Yeti Co are mentioned on the website without detailed case studies, which tells me the Lindy result is doing all the heavy lifting. The co-founder relationship -- meeting at a prior employer -- is fine but not the deep multi-venture history I look for. When I backed Raj on Solana, I'd watched him through three companies. Ethan has a BS in Finance from University of Washington and was Head of Partnerships at Lindy. Norbu did internships at Microsoft on Copilot image generation and IBM. These are competent early-career operators, but the idea they're pitching -- "AI automates A/B testing" -- is the most sensible, obvious application of LLMs to marketing I can think of. That's the wrong ratio. I want a sane person pitching an insane idea. Sherpa is a sane team pitching a sane idea in a hot market. Nothing about this makes me think "that's fucking crazy."

The bull case, honestly constructed: CRO incumbents are distracted by consolidation -- VWO and AB Tasty merging under Everstone, Intellimize absorbed into Webflow -- and Sherpa can sprint while they integrate. The 4.3M visits of learning data could compound into a real data flywheel. The "single line of code" integration creates switching costs once embedded. And the CRO market is growing from $1.2B to $2.8B by 2032. A focused AI-native entrant that owns the full loop -- analysis, variant generation, testing, deployment -- could capture meaningful share before incumbents ship equivalent automation. Sherpa could be the company that makes A/B testing as effortless as installing a pixel, unlocking experimentation for the long tail of mid-market companies who never had the resources to run proper CRO programs. That's a real argument. But here's why it falls apart: the "data flywheel" claim is identical to what every AI startup says, and 4.3M visits is a rounding error compared to what Optimizely sees in a day. The full-loop automation advantage is temporal, not structural. And the core capability -- LLM-generated page variants -- relies on foundation models available to everyone. Sherpa's differentiation is execution speed in a window that's closing. I checked my Known Exceptions: Slack got a pass on consensus because it created a genuinely new behavioral primitive. Sherpa creates no new behavior -- A/B testing is forty years old. No exception applies.

Sherpa fails the AI kill test more cleanly than almost any company I've seen this batch. Pure software, no physical moats, no protocol-level infrastructure, no network effects beyond a modest data accumulation claim. The dossier's own risk section states: "Barriers to replication are low at the technology layer." When I backed Metropolis at seed, AI-powered parking had a physical moat -- you need cameras, leases, real-world infrastructure that no LLM can replicate from a laptop. Sherpa has none of that. It's a software layer on top of generally available models, competing against incumbents who already own the customer relationship and the experimentation infrastructure. Webflow acquiring Intellimize for exactly this capability pattern tells me the endgame: Sherpa either gets acqui-hired for a modest sum or gets outrun by a platform that adds variant generation as a feature checkbox. The one condition that would flip me: if Sherpa showed me $500K+ ARR from ten or more customers with zero founder-network overlap, proving the product sells to cold prospects on its own merits, and if the conversion data flywheel demonstrably produced variant quality that measurably exceeded what a competitor could achieve with the same foundation models. That's a high bar, but that's what it would take to overcome the structural vulnerability.

Sherpa's total score is driven by two dimensions where the evidence is unambiguous. Consensus Mispricing scores near the floor because AI-powered B2B SaaS is the most consensus-hot category in venture capital in 2026 -- there is no asymmetric pricing opportunity here. Structural Durability scores near the floor because the AI kill test is lethal: Optimizely, VWO, and Webflow can and will add LLM-powered variant generation as features within their existing platforms, and Sherpa has no physical, protocol-level, or network-effect moat to prevent it. This is a competent team building a real product in a real market, and it's not my bet.
