# Trellis -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Trellis's analytical signature is a domain-deep operator-founder pairing — Lodovico Benvenuti shipped AI agents at HostAI/Conduit (YC W24) *and* ran a 150-listing, ~$2M-revenue PM company himself — sitting on top of an invisible revenue curve in a category where Guesty ($409M raised, $900M valuation) and Hostaway ($543M raised, $925M valuation) already own the customer relationship. That's the entire deal in one sentence: maximum founder-market fit, zero disclosed velocity, capitalized incumbents in the same workflow path. It's the inverse profile of the deals I write checks on.

Market re-opening is where Trellis lives between **Decagon** (2023) and the Lyft Series C pass. The Decagon analogy is the load-bearing one — same template: LLM agent reliability crosses a threshold in 2024-2025, and a workflow that used to be rule-based scripts (customer support tickets there; guest comms + turnover dispatch here) suddenly becomes an autonomous loop. Trellis's "feature parity in two weeks" line from Benvenuti is exactly the inflection signal I want to hear. Where Trellis diverges from Decagon is the *market state at the moment of inflection*: customer support in early 2023 had no AI-native incumbent with $409M in the bank, whereas STR ops in 2026 has Guesty and Hostaway — both raised at $900M+ valuations inside the last 24 months specifically to defend this surface. The catalyst is real; the window is half-closed before Trellis stepped through it.

Revenue velocity is the dimension where the Lyft Series C failure mechanism shows up most uncomfortably for Trellis. Lyft taught me that in markets with a clear capitalized #1, the absence of share-inversion evidence is itself the signal — narrative cannot substitute for a curve. Trellis's dossier discloses exactly two numbers: "84.5% automation rate" and one "100+ units" testimonial saving "20 hours a week." No customer count. No ARR. No MoM. "Get started for free" on the landing page. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal — Harvey didn't have to disclose $3B → $5B → $8B; the curve leaked because real curves leak. Trellis is six people out of Spring 2026 batch, so I weight this proportionally, but the burden of "just working vs. false signal" sits unmet.

On founder shipping rhythm, the single-anchor parallel is **Perplexity** (2022) — Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built. Benvenuti's analog is weaker but real: he didn't just *think* about STR AI agents, he built them at HostAI/Conduit (YC W24) and then went and operated a 150-listing portfolio to $2M revenue. That is two shipped artifacts in the exact problem space, which is rarer than it sounds — most AI-for-X founders have shipped neither the AI nor the X. Sahagun running STR Tech Report and attending 20+ industry events gives Trellis a distribution wedge that pure-eng teams lack. This dimension is where Trellis earns its strongest pin.

Index position is the counterfactual question for Trellis: what would have to be true for this to flip from "fights Guesty for share" to "rides STR digitization as it grows"? It would require Guesty and Hostaway to decline to ship competing AI agent layers, *and* the 200+ integrations to compound into a switching-cost moat before incumbents close API access. Neither is the base case — Benvenuti's own "feature parity in two weeks" admission is a confession that integrations and agent loops are not the moat he thinks they are. Trellis becomes valuable as STR grows only if it is in the workflow path *every* operator must touch; right now it is one of four overlapping paths, and the other three are funded at 100x its level.

Single-miracle architecture is the one dimension where Trellis cleanly compounds over 18 months. The miracle is "LLM agents reliable enough to autonomously close guest comms + turnover dispatch loops" — that's it. There's no new chemistry, no regulatory unlock, no consumer behavior change required. Integration maintenance and incumbent API politics are execution risks, not miracles. This is the **Color Genomics** (2015) architecture — workflow on top of a hard layer someone else has already validated — and it's the reason the score on this dimension is the second-highest, not the lowest.

My 36-month forecast: Trellis reaches $3-8M ARR by mid-2028 serving the 10-50 unit mid-market that Guesty under-serves, and gets acquired by Guesty or Hostaway in the $40-120M range as the cheapest way for either incumbent to plug their AI agent gap. Not a zero, not a fund-returner — the small-acquisition outcome that the dimensional signature (high founder-market fit, low revenue velocity, capitalized #1 and #2) almost always produces. It's the **Lyft** pattern rerun at small scale, with a kinder ending because Benvenuti has a real domain pedigree and a buyer audience.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the *AI Market Clarity* counter: STR operations is exactly the kind of "wide open" workflow category I named in the November 2025 piece — accounting, compliance, financial tooling, sales agents, security — and Benvenuti is unusually qualified, having both operated *and* built AI in this surface. If Guesty and Hostaway are structurally allergic to cannibalizing per-seat SaaS with autonomous agents (which incumbents always are), Trellis could be the post-LLM PMS that compounds while incumbents protect ARPU. That case stings because it's how Decagon won. What dismantles it for Trellis specifically is the capital gap — Guesty's $409M and Hostaway's $543M against a 6-person YC pre-seed is not symmetric, and the integration dependence on those same incumbents means Trellis's distribution can be throttled the moment it becomes a threat. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Trellis discloses or leaks a customer count above 200 paying property managers with >20% MoM growth before YC Demo Day, *or* signs a channel deal with one of Airbnb/VRBO directly that bypasses the Guesty/Hostaway middle layer. Absent either, I pass and watch.
