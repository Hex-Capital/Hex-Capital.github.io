# Trellis -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Trellis claims "software is being commoditized" and says it reached feature parity with competitors in two weeks. That sentence is doing two jobs at once — it's the pitch and the obituary. If Trellis can replicate Guesty's feature set ($409M raised) in fourteen days, then Guesty can replicate Trellis's AI agents in some comparable window. Boom ($12.7M raised) is already positioned as AI-native PMS with its own agentic system "BAM." Conduit, a YC W24 company where co-founder Lodovico Benvenuti previously built AI agents, is operating across 77,000+ properties. The commoditization thesis is honest, but honesty about your lack of moat doesn't create one. When I backed Perplexity, the moat wasn't that AI search was hard to build — it was that Aravind's specific knowledge from Google Brain and OpenAI was years ahead of any competitor's. Trellis needs an equivalent density of non-replicable knowledge, and the dossier doesn't fully deliver it.

Lodovico Benvenuti has a genuine specific knowledge intersection — he scaled a property management company to ~$2M revenue and 150+ listings, then built AI customer service agents at Conduit. That's operator knowledge layered on AI engineering in the exact vertical. Jan Sahagun adds commercial depth from sales roles at Besty AI, Guestway, and Boom, plus the STR Tech Report media platform that functions as an owned distribution channel into the target buyer persona. The combination covers build, sell, and operate within STR. But here's the test I always apply: if these founders disappeared tomorrow, would the core insight survive? Trellis's insight — that STR operators are drowning in 5-10 disconnected tools and need unified AI orchestration — is observable by anyone in the industry. Conduit's team knows it. Boom's team knows it. Hostaway ($543M raised, first STR PMS unicorn) knows it. Lodovico's operator-plus-AI-builder intersection is above average but not singular enough to constitute the kind of specific knowledge that IS the company. Compare this to Ev Williams at Twitter — nobody else had spent years at Blogger accumulating that exact understanding of public communication at scale.

Trellis's leverage architecture is genuinely interesting at the unit level. The claim that one person is operating an entire PM company's operations, backed by an 84.5% AI automation rate, is the kind of team-to-value leverage signal I look for. If real, Trellis is demonstrating the shrinking company thesis — Midjourney doing $500M revenue with ~163 employees, Cursor hitting $500M ARR with fewer than 50 people. A PM company run by one human plus AI agents is structurally beautiful. But Trellis's leverage is at the customer level, not at the company level relative to its competition. The 200+ tool integrations create switching costs, but integrations are effort, not knowledge. The "cross-company knowledge network" where resolution patterns and SOPs compound is the right aspiration — it's the data flywheel that could create durable advantage. But it's aspirational with one design partner. Network effects that don't yet exist aren't network effects.

The principal-agent diagnostic cuts in a direction that concerns me. Trellis's founders both left direct competitors — Lodovico from Conduit, Jan from Boom — to build in the same market. This could signal principal-level conviction: they saw from the inside that existing approaches were wrong and couldn't stop themselves from building the right version. Or it could signal something more agent-like: they learned the playbook and are executing a variation. I can't distinguish between these from a dossier. What I notice is that the principal-agent dynamic between Trellis and the STR incumbents doesn't favor Trellis the way it favored Perplexity against Google. Google's ad revenue model makes its search team structurally unable to cannibalize itself. Guesty and Hostaway face no such constraint — adding AI features enhances their per-listing pricing power rather than destroying it. Trellis is a principal fighting agents who are incentive-aligned to do exactly what Trellis is doing.

The bull case deserves honest construction. Jan Sahagun's STR Tech Report is a media distribution asset directly into the buyer persona — owned media is permissionless leverage, and it's rare for a pre-seed company to have a content channel this targeted. Lodovico's dual background as operator and AI builder at a YC company in the same space is a real intersection that narrows the competitive field of founders. The "AI that builds your software" framing — dynamically generating workflows and interfaces per customer rather than shipping static SaaS — could be genuinely different from what incumbents or Boom are building. And the fact that $950M+ in combined incumbent funding hasn't solved the problem Trellis identifies (operators still drowning in fragmented tools) suggests the incumbents' SaaS architecture may be structurally unable to solve it. If Trellis's agent layer truly orchestrates across operational boundaries in a way monolithic PMS platforms cannot, the Wish parallel is wrong and the AngelList parallel is closer — a new architecture that makes the old one obsolete. The condition that would flip me: if Trellis showed me three independent operators running 100+ listings each through the platform with measurable RevPAR improvement and a sub-20-person team, I'd see compound evidence of specific knowledge productized into working leverage. That's verifiable, and it would change my score meaningfully.

Trellis lands in neutral territory because the specific knowledge is real but replicable, and the leverage architecture is promising but undefended. The 84.5% automation claim and one-person-operating-a-PM-company signal are exactly what I want to see from an AI-native approach — but against Guesty's $409M, Hostaway's $543M, Boom's AI-native positioning, and Conduit's 77,000+ properties with the same YC pedigree, Trellis needs a moat that goes beyond "we built it faster." The founders' STR industry depth earns them above-default marks on specific knowledge and principal commitment. The commoditization dynamic they openly acknowledge keeps the leverage score constrained. Access democratization is mild — Trellis gives mid-market operators better tools, but doesn't structurally remove a gatekeeper the way AngelList removed the gatekeepers from startup investing. I pass, not because the founders are wrong about the problem, but because I don't see the non-replicable advantage that lets them win it against this much well-funded competition pursuing the same thesis.
