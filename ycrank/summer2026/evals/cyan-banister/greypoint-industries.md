# Greypoint Industries -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Greypoint Industries is a thesis-correct hardware bet that arrived precisely on consensus -- frontier defense in the lane I love, sourced from the YC Summer 2026 RFS Tyler Bosmeny published asking for "counter-swarm stack: high-capacity interceptors," with at least five YC batch peers (Tenet, 9 Mothers, Surtr, Arlo, Perseus Defense, Seeing Systems) racing the same prompt. The category is right. Greypoint Industries' position inside the category is the problem.

Anduril in 2017 is the HIGH rung on founder-lived-experience, and the analogy is load-bearing because Palmer Luckey had already shipped Oculus to scale before pivoting to defense -- he had a prior physical artifact and a documented contact with the hardware-shipping discipline. Greypoint Industries diverges from that template in the part of the dossier that matters most: Darcy Zhang is named CEO on the YC page but is absent from the September 2025 Globe and Mail hackathon story and the October 2025 UBC Venture Founder cohort listing, which means he parachuted onto a founding team of UBC Applied Science undergrads (Chu, Khoo, Xu) after they had already self-organized as Team Greypoint. None of the four have military service, prior defense-prime experience, or a shipped hardware artifact in the public record. The founder origin here is "we won a defense hackathon, then YC put out an RFS for our exact category" -- which is analytical pattern-matching dressed as conviction, not the Luckey-of-Oculus or Hanke-of-Keyhole biography I write checks against.

The YC AI-agent darling I passed on -- two ex-FAANG PMs, six funds chasing, TAM slide first -- failed because consensus collapsed the entry price and because the moat was the model, not the founder. Greypoint Industries triggers the same red flag in a different costume: Bosmeny's RFS literally summoned this company into existence, and five Greypoint Industries peers in the same batch are pursuing overlapping mass-produced expendable-interceptor theses. "Find them before it becomes consensus" is the Long Journey thesis, and I'd be writing into the consensus itself. The Allen Control Systems $200M Series B at $2.2B post in May 2026 is the rising-tide argument the bulls will make, but ACS is the lesson cutting the other way: the well-funded incumbent is already 14 months ahead with a turret-mounted Bullfrog shipping into US procurement, and Greypoint Industries has zero contracts, zero LOIs, and a 403 on its own website.

On governance -- the HQ Trivia 2017 lesson I cite constantly -- Greypoint Industries fails the structural test in a way the dossier states plainly. Four co-founders, a CEO who joined after the original three UBC classmates formed the team, and no documented operating agreement or vesting schedule. The GameCrush failure mode and the Yusupov/Kroll feud both started exactly here: ambiguous CEO authority introduced after the founding social contract was already set. I have no evidence Darcy Zhang has the operating chops to be the tiebreaker, and I have no evidence the other three have agreed he is.

On capital-intensive frontier-hardware (Dimension 4) -- this is where Greypoint Industries earns its real points and the only place I light up. Defense kinetic interceptors with ITAR exposure, soldier-deployable form factor, sub-target unit-cost design challenge, and a category structure where defense primes (Raytheon, Lockheed) genuinely cannot produce expendables at infantry scale: this is the lane that produced SpaceX in 2008 and Anduril in 2017. If the founder profile matched the category profile, I'd be writing the check before slide three. Over 18 months this dimension compounds if Greypoint Industries lands one Canadian DND test contract, because qualified-supplier status with an allied military is non-fungible learning-curve capital -- the moat the bulls correctly identify.

On physical-world verifiability, the counterfactual is what pins the score. For Greypoint Industries to flip from a mid-low rung to a high-rung tactile artifact I'd need a published prototype intercept video, a unit-cost figure under $500, or a photographable interceptor in a Canadian Forces test range. None of those exist. The greypointindustries.ca site returned 403 on fetch, no Twitter/X account, no Product Hunt, no demo. A man-deployable autonomous interceptor is in principle the most touchable artifact a defense company could ship -- a thing a soldier holds in their hands -- and Greypoint Industries has shipped nothing observable to date.

My 36-month forecast: Greypoint Industries does not reach a US DoD or Canadian DND production contract; the company either gets acqui-hired for $10-30M by Allen Control Systems or a Tier-2 defense prime absorbing the engineering team, or pivots off man-deployable interceptors into a software/sensing niche after the first prototype fails to clear sub-target unit cost. Sub-$1M revenue at month 36, raising a bridge, not a Series A. The mechanism is straightforward: consensus YC defense batch + crowded subcategory + no founder defense-shipping pedigree + four-cofounder governance + parachuted CEO. None of these are individually fatal. Together they are the Pass profile.

The bull case stings and I want to name it honestly: the DoD's $70B+ counter-drone spending plan combined with the ACS $2.2B comp means even mediocre execution in this category can attract SBIR Phase II dollars and a Series A markup, and "man-deployable" is a genuine white space between Anduril's vehicle-launched Roadrunner and ACS's turret Bullfrog -- there is a real product wedge here. The 9 Mothers precedent (already shipping to USG and primes from YC Sp26) shows allied-defense YC companies can move fast. My dismantling: 9 Mothers had product shipping before YC; Greypoint Industries has a hackathon trophy and a 403. The HandUp exception doesn't apply because no Greypoint Industries founder biography mirrors the problem space the way Rose Broome's homelessness work mirrored mine. The DeepMind polymath exception doesn't apply because none of the four founders are documented polymaths -- they are early-career engineers. The flip condition I'd actually act on: a signed Canadian DND SBIR or US DoD contract with a named program office, OR public evidence that Darcy Zhang has prior defense-hardware shipping experience the dossier missed. Until one of those two facts exists, this is a pass.
