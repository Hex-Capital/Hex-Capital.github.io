# Tenet Industries -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Tenet Industries is a heads-down manufacturing-arbitrage play whose highest dimension is Emil Falk's family metals pedigree at Falks Metall AB and whose lowest is the total absence of any owned distribution or founder-brand surface area -- a profile that reads as "three KTH students who actually know how to mill plastic" parachuted into the most consensus-hot defense category of the Spring 2026 batch, targeting 3,000 SEK per drone against Helsing's HX-2 with $695M of dry powder behind it.

On round pricing, Solana at $0.05/coin in 2018 is the HIGH rung because every generalist had quit crypto post-ICO crash and the entry price was a category-wide mispricing, not a company-specific one. Tenet Industries does not load that analogy -- the dossier shows YC standard $125K for 7% on stage one, which is the same SAFE template stamped onto roughly 200 other Spring 2026 companies, and the category itself (low-cost attritable strike drones) is where Helsing just took $695M, Anduril sits on $6B+, and Shield AI cleared a $12.7B mark in March 2026. Defense drones in 2026 is the new "AI agent for enterprise vertical" of my pass example -- the round is not mispriced, the category is the price ceiling, and there is no visible tier-1 pass to give me an asymmetric entry.

Teamshares is the canonical capital-structure win because the employee-ownership rollup of small businesses is an instrument no other VC will underwrite, which is exactly why the entry was clean. Tenet Industries inverts that pattern: hardware defense at scale is one of the most capital-intensive paths in venture, and the dossier itself flags that Helsing has already raised €830M+ to build the same wedge. The mechanism that kills Tenet Industries is not the founders -- it is that going from a 14-part injection-molded prototype to a fielded military-grade kamikaze quadcopter at 3,000 SEK requires tooling capex, ISP export licensing in Sweden, multi-year procurement cycles, and probably four more rounds of dilution before a Swedish MoD contract clears. This is the opposite of a Slow "venture moment" -- this is force-fitting a capital-heavy industrial bet into a SAFE cap table that will be underwater by the B.

On founder conviction, the asset I can actually price is Emil Falk's prior time at Falks Metall AB plus Hugo Frisk's literal "Mass production" LinkedIn headline -- two specific data points that suggest the DFM thesis (part-count 30→14, injection molding over 3D printing) is a real operator belief rather than a deck slide. Over 18 months, this compounds only if Tenet Industries ships a few hundred units to a named buyer and the manufacturing curve bends visibly; it fails if the next twelve months are spent on simulation videos and government-relations theater while Helsing's HX-2 line gets the production contracts. Five months in with founders who are still students at KTH/SSE, the conviction is real but unproven, and there are no essays, no Twitter, no public artifacts of category obsession -- the missionary-temperament test from the Antoine Buteau quotes lands somewhere between absent and TBD.

Founder-as-brand: zero. No Twitter accounts found across three co-founders, no public writing, no Discord, no newsletter. For a B2G defense play this is partially excusable -- John Fish / Bookshelved logic does not apply when the customer is the Swedish MoD -- but it means the cap-table-as-person thesis that the $60M Slow Creator Fund formalizes is structurally inapplicable to Tenet Industries, and I have to underwrite on pure execution without the distribution downside cushion I prefer.

Category sleeve flip-test: for Tenet Industries to move from "defense hardware" (not a Slow sleeve) into "venture moment" (Slow Our Letter), the category would need to look more like Ro's 50-state prescription rails than like Anduril's procurement-relationship moat -- i.e., regulatory complexity that only venture capital can clear, where the capital intensity itself becomes the moat. The dossier hints at this with the dual-use ISP export-control language and the European sovereign-supply carve-out from ITAR friction, but it does not yet show Tenet Industries owning a regulatory wedge that Helsing cannot replicate with a fraction of its war chest. Without that, the sleeve fit is wrong -- defense in 2026 is on my "AI is just more internet" list, not my Aleo/Lightning Labs list.

My 36-month forecast: Tenet Industries does not become the European Anduril; it either pivots to selling subsystems/DFM-optimized components to a Swedish or German prime (Saab, Rheinmetall) and gets acqui-hired sub-$50M, or it raises a hot follow-on at a 3-5x markup on defense-tech FOMO in 2026-2027 and then runs into the procurement-cycle wall by month 24 with <$2M in revenue and a cap table no Series B will clear. The mechanism is the gap between three students with a manufacturing thesis and the relationship infrastructure required to sell attritable munitions to NATO governments -- that gap closes with money and time, both of which Helsing already has more of.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Ukraine's drone consumption rate is in the millions of units per year and Helsing is structurally too premium to chase the attritable bottom of the market -- meaning a 3,000 SEK quadcopter from a European-domiciled producer with Emil Falk's actual manufacturing chops could land a 10,000+ unit Ukrainian contract within 18 months and pull a Ro-style "regulatory complexity is the moat" exception, where Swedish ISP licensing and EU sovereign-supply preference create a carve-out that no US-component drone can enter. That stings because the unit-economics gap (3,000 vs 10,000 SEK) is real and the European-carve-out logic is exactly the kind of illegible-assets pattern I look for. What dismantles it: Tenet Industries shows zero signed LOIs in the dossier -- only "discussions" -- and Helsing absolutely will build a stripped-down HX line the moment the attritable RFP appears, with relationships Tenet does not have. The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named, signed production contract with the Swedish FMV or a Ukrainian state buyer for ≥1,000 units at the stated price point before the next round closes -- short of that, Tenet Industries is the wrong shape of capital at the wrong moment in a category I do not price.
