# Deep Interactions -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Deep Interactions is a cleanly-governed academic-researcher founder shipping a services-flavored vibe-coding wrapper into the most contested AI category of the cycle -- Sruthi Viswanathan, Oxford DPhil in Human-AI Interaction, building a "humans-in-the-loop AI builder" while Lovable sits at $400M ARR and a $6.6B Series B valuation right next door. The signature here is high governance, low weirdness. One founder, four people, ten SMB design partners, zero structural reason this isn't a feature inside Lovable, v0.app, or Replit Agent by Christmas. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, but I also have to ask whether anyone is playing a different game on this one.

On founder-lived-experience, the HIGH rung is Palmer Luckey at **Anduril** (2017) -- shipped Oculus from his parents' garage *before* turning to defense, biography stitched to the artifact. The MID rung is Demis Hassabis at **DeepMind** (~2013) -- pedigreed credentials redeemed by polymath cross-domain compounding. The LOW rung is the pass example: ex-FAANG PMs who "discovered the problem in a memo." Deep Interactions sits between MID and the lower half of MID: Sruthi Viswanathan has spent years inside Limbic AI (mental health) and Naver Labs Europe researching human-AI interaction, and her customer concentration (Canopie, Jack Fertility, mental-health adjacencies) traces directly to that prior life. That is real. But she is a UX *researcher*, not a builder who shipped a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain. The Hassabis polymath override doesn't quite fire either -- HCI research is the same domain as the product, not a cross-domain compounding.

On magically-weird and non-consensus, the HIGH rung is **Crusoe** (~2018) burning gas flares for Bitcoin -- a thing two or three credible VCs had passed on for aesthetic reasons before I wrote the check. The MID rung is **Niantic** (~2015), niche-but-interesting AR. The LOW rung is again my pass example: the consensus YC AI-agent darling with six top funds bidding. Deep Interactions sits one inch above the LOW rung. The category is "AI app builder for teams" inside a YC Spring 2026 batch, in a market where Lovable raised $330M Series B in December 2025, v0 rebranded to v0.app in January 2026, and Gartner is projecting 60% of code AI-generated by year-end. There is no aesthetic moat, no regulatory friction anyone is avoiding, no founder articulation of why incumbents misread the wedge. This is the opposite of "find them before it becomes consensus." The thesis here *is* the consensus.

On single-CEO authority, the HIGH rung is **SpaceX** / Musk, the MID rung is Luckey at **Anduril**, the LOW rung is the **HQ Trivia** (2017) Yusupov/Kroll feud. Deep Interactions sits between MID and HIGH -- the YC page lists only Sruthi Viswanathan as founder, Beni Ngwamah reads as team, and the four-person org has unambiguous tiebreaker authority. This is the cleanest dimension on the page. On capital-intensity and unsexy-regulated -- HIGH being **SpaceX** (2008) and **Anduril** (2017), MID being **Mindbloom** ketamine, LOW being the pure-software AI SaaS wrapper -- Deep Interactions is firmly on the LOW rung. Slack/Gmail/Chrome/Miro/Figma/GitHub/Vercel/Supabase connectors are the moat claim, and any two engineers can wrap an LLM with those in a quarter. On physical-world verifiability -- HIGH being **Flock Safety** (~2018) cameras I can photograph or **Niantic** (~2015) Ingress I played for years, MID being **Postmates** (2011) delivery to my door, LOW being the B2B API I can only evaluate via founder testimony -- Deep Interactions sits at the LOW rung. The only public funnel is a Calendly link and a Typeform waitlist; the "10,000 real-world usage instances" metric is undefined; there is no install, no scan, no ride.

My 36-month forecast for Deep Interactions: pivots out of the generic "AI app builder for teams" positioning into a vertical wedge in mental-health or fertility AI tooling (leveraging Sruthi Viswanathan's Limbic AI background and the Canopie/Jack Fertility customer thread), settles at $1-3M ARR by 2029, and is most likely acqui-hired by Replit, Vercel, or a mid-tier vertical SaaS player for a sub-$30M outcome. The mechanism producing this is the asymmetry between Lovable's $400M ARR self-serve flywheel and Deep Interactions' founder-led-services delivery model -- by the time Deep Interactions can productize the "context layer," Lovable will have shipped team-collaboration, and the only survivable position will be the verticalized one Sruthi is best-positioned to own.

The strongest bull case is Sruthi-as-Hassabis: an Oxford researcher whose actual published work on human-AI interaction gives her a product-design edge that Lovable's growth-engineering team simply cannot replicate, and the "humans-in-the-loop" framing is genuinely orthogonal to one-shot prompt-to-app generation -- it could compound into the Notion of internal AI builders, the way **DeepMind** (~2013) overrode my pedigree skepticism through polymath cross-domain stacking. That stings. My answer: Hassabis was a chess prodigy and a neuroscientist and a game designer compounding *across* unrelated domains; Sruthi is a HCI researcher building an HCI product, which is depth within one domain, not polymath compounding -- the **DeepMind** exception does not fire. And the **HandUp** (~2014) mission-alignment override doesn't fire either, because nothing in the Deep Interactions story mirrors my own biography the way HandUp's dental-financing-for-the-homeless thesis did. The verifiable condition that would flip me: a signed enterprise contract (not SMB design partner) with a named Fortune-1000 buyer, plus Sruthi articulating in one sentence why Lovable structurally cannot serve that buyer, within 12 months. Until then, this is a pass with warmth -- one foot in love for the researcher, both eyes open on the category.
