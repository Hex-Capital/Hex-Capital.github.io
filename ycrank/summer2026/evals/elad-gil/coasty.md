# Coasty -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Coasty is a shipping-fluent two-person team selling a benchmark number into a category whose catalyst is owned by their substitutes -- Prateek Jannu and Nitish Kovuru have a working `/v1/predict` API and an 82% OSWorld score, but the dossier discloses zero revenue, zero named enterprise logos, and the catalyst that opened computer-use (Anthropic's Computer Use API Oct 2024, OpenAI Operator Jan 2025) is shipped by the two companies most likely to compress Coasty's lead. That's the signature: high on builder execution, structurally low on the dimension that matters most in the 2026 AI cycle.

On revenue velocity, Coasty looks nothing like **Harvey** (2023), which I sized up as it ran "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" -- that's what "just working" looks like in dollars. Harvey's dossier at the same stage would have named enterprise law firm logos and pilot conversions; Coasty's pricing page lists $19/$50/$99 self-serve tiers with no MRR, no logo, no LOI counterparty named. The Humans in the Loop interview talks about Fortune 500 hesitancy in the abstract, not a single signed customer. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" -- the absence of a curve in Coasty's dossier isn't a pre-seed disclosure norm, it's the negative signal itself, because the Product Hunt launch in Feb 2026 gave them four months to surface buying behavior and they surfaced a benchmark instead.

The pattern that kills companies on the Index dimension is **Lyft** Series C -- structural #2 in a market whose dynamics route economics to #1. Coasty's analog is sharper than rideshare's because the #1 isn't a peer startup, it's Anthropic and OpenAI shipping Computer Use and Operator natively against the same OSWorld benchmark Coasty is leading 82% to 72% to 38%. Coasty does not sit in a Stripe-like workflow path that every entrant must touch; Coasty sits on top of frontier models that ship competing surfaces on each release, and the trajectory-data flywheel the dossier inferentially gestures at requires customer volume that is not in evidence. This is the inverse of an index position -- it is a benchmark lead held against the people who set the benchmark's ceiling.

On the catalyst dimension, the *mechanism* is real but mis-routed: vision-language models crossed a screen-grounded action threshold in late 2024, which is a credible 24-month re-opening, and it explains why YC funded Coasty rather than another browser-agent clone. But the same catalyst funded Browserbase ($67.5M total, $300M valuation June 2025), Kernel ($22M Series A led by Accel), Browser Use (~$17M), and Anchor ($6M) -- the catalyst opened a door for everyone, and Coasty entered at pre-seed against four players each capitalized one to two stages ahead. The catalyst exists; Coasty's positional advantage inside it does not.

The counterfactual that would lift Coasty's founder dimension into Aravind/**Perplexity** (2022) territory is shipping artifacts between investor conversations that name *paying customers*, not benchmark scores. Jannu built a 200K-parameter auto-grad engine at Purdue and led ML at TruGen; Kovuru wrote the origin story of an AI agent deleting a production database and shipped `coasty-ai/open-computer-use` plus dev.to posts plus a Substack interview -- they ship. But "ships between meetings" in my Perplexity template meant Aravind showed up the next week with the *thing we discussed* built into the product loop. Coasty's shipping rhythm points at OSWorld leaderboards and Product Hunt launches, which is promoter-shaped output, not customer-loop output. To flip the tier I would need to see two named enterprise pilots converted in the next 90 days.

Compare Coasty's single-miracle architecture to **Color Genomics** (2015), which I co-founded explicitly as a workflow layer on commodity sequencing rather than a chemistry bet. Coasty has the right architectural instinct -- sandboxed VM plus vision-grounded action, isolating the miracle to "VLM accuracy on screen-grounded tasks" with GTM and reliability as remaining work. That is a clean single-miracle frame and it is why Coasty isn't a Pass on architecture alone. The crack is the dossier's own "82% Real, 73% Exploited" framing on the OSWorld blog -- the miracle isn't fully landed in production, which is the gap between the Color template and Coasty's current state.

My 36-month forecast: Coasty is acquihired by UiPath, Automation Anywhere, or a frontier lab for the team and the sandboxing IP at a sub-$25M outcome, with no standalone ARR exceeding $2M before the talks. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- builders who ship into a category where the catalyst's owners ship faster will compound product quality without compounding distribution, and the Browserbase/Kernel/Browser Use capitalization gap closes the window on independent scale before the trajectory-data flywheel reaches the volume needed to compound.

The strongest opposing case borrows my own **Color Genomics** (2015) override: Coasty is workflow infrastructure on a hard-tech layer, the miracle is isolated to model capability that is *already* validated, and if Anthropic and OpenAI deprioritize first-party computer-use (the way they've deprioritized browser plugins) Coasty inherits a category. That argument stings because the architectural frame is right. It loses to the dossier on one fact: Operator shipped January 2025, six months later OpenAI is still investing in it, and the Coasty blog itself flags benchmark exploitation -- the frontier labs are not retreating from this surface, and Coasty has no enterprise revenue cushion to ride out the substitution risk. The condition that flips this from Pass to Invest: two named Fortune 500 logos in paid production by end of Q1 2027, with MoM revenue growth above 20%. Until then, market first and the strength of the team second -- and this market belongs to the catalyst's authors.
