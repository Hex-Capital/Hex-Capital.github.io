# Autostep -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Autostep is a discovery-layer SaaS sitting on top of someone else's automation wave -- highest dimension is its capable but domain-continuous solo founder Aidan Pratt, lowest is consequence magnitude, and that pairing tells me exactly what kind of company Autostep is: a competent meta-tool whose maximum success-state leaves the world structurally unchanged. The dossier line that pins it for me is the marketing claim "$100K+ in operational waste with 10 people" -- a sentence written from inside the optimization frame, not the displacement frame. My pass example in this persona was the "GPT-4 sales-call summarizer," and Autostep is its enterprise-ops cousin.

On consequence, the high rung is **OpenAI** (2019) -- I wrote the $50M first check because the success-state was a civilizational redirection of cognitive labor, not a productivity bump inside one workflow. Autostep's success-state, even at maximum ambition, is that ops leaders at Fortune 5000 companies get better visibility into which tasks to hand to agents that *other* companies build. That is a referral layer on top of the labor-displacement wave I described in Fortune (2026-03-06) as $15 trillion of U.S. GDP -- Autostep captures a vig on the migration, it does not cause the migration. Compare to **Impossible Foods** (2011), where the success-state was "animal protein production disappears"; Autostep's equivalent sentence is "BPM consultants get displaced by software," which is real but small. No incumbent regime collapses if Autostep wins.

The low-rung pattern on capital/technical risk is the SaaS sales tool from my pass example -- standard burn-to-ARR economics, software-only execution risk, fundable by any Tier-1 generalist. Does that pattern appear in Autostep's dossier? Directly. A desktop observer plus an LLM-driven recommender, sold per-seat through Calendly demos, with SOC 2 and HIPAA as the technical claims. There is no physics here, no foundation model being trained, no fleet, no plant. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) needed $115M Series A because the tokamak required capital depth no consensus VC would touch; Autostep needs roughly the YC + Neo check it already has. I am over-resourced for this deal, and so is every other Tier-1.

Counterfactual on founder-defies-experts: what would have to be true for Aidan Pratt to score above mid-tier on this axis? He would need to have publicly contradicted the task-mining orthodoxy -- e.g., argued that the entire UiPath/Celonis discovery paradigm is wrong, or that desktop telemetry is the wrong substrate. Instead the YC Launch page repeats the consensus thesis ("the bottleneck isn't tooling anymore, it's discovery"), which is the same line **Mimica** and **Skan** have been selling since 2021. Pratt's resume -- Georgia Tech ML/CS, Kleiner Perkins fellow, 8VC fellow, Neo fellow, "6 years at seed-Series A automation startups" -- is paradigmatic domain-expert continuity, the exact pattern my TED Talk warned against when I cited McKinsey's 1986 forecast of <1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 against the actual 109M.

Mechanism analysis on learning rate over 18 months: Autostep is Pratt's first company as CEO with no disclosed pivot, no public thesis update, solo team. The compounding question is whether the absence reflects rigidity or simply a short company history -- I default mid-low because I cannot punish a founder for lacking a pivot story at month six. The **Slash** (2024) exception that earned my $100M Series C required a documented Kanye-pivot moment as override; Autostep has no analogous evidence. If Pratt ships a public "we were wrong about horizontal task mining, going vertical into legal-ops" post in the next nine months, the learning-rate score moves; until then, the dossier is silent and I score the silence honestly.

Single-anchor parallel on capital fit: **Mimica** raised $6M Series A in November 2021 *led by Khosla Ventures* and another round from Paladin in 2025 (Tracxn). Mimica is the closest portfolio analog to Autostep on substance -- "automation twin" task observation, exactly the substrate Autostep describes. I already own this thesis at a more mature stage, with a more capitalized team, and Autostep would be a competitor to my own portfolio company. That is not a small frame; it is the deal-killer my Unstated Pattern about concentration would surface anyway. The check size Autostep needs (likely a $500K-$2M pre-seed slot) is structurally below my Khosla Ventures rhythm and would not even justify a partner meeting if Mimica did not exist.

My 36-month forecast: Autostep is either acqui-hired by **ServiceNow** (which already absorbed UltimateSuite in 2024 for the same capability) or by Mimica/Skan for sub-$15M and Pratt rolls into a PM role, OR Autostep pivots to a vertical-specific agent-discovery wedge (legal-ops or RevOps) and lands between $500K-$2M ARR before raising a seed extension. Dead is also live -- solo founder, four well-funded incumbents, no proprietary stack. The mechanism producing this distribution is that the discovery layer compresses into the agent-execution layer as foundation models get cheaper, and the pure-discovery vendors get bundled by whoever owns deployment.

The strongest bull case: Pratt's KP/8VC/Neo fellowship pedigree plus YC Spring 2026 plus Neo is the exact YC-pedigree-plus-network shape that drew me to **Slash** ($100M Series C), and a solo founder with six years of automation context who ships a working desktop product in a $58B-by-2034 process-mining market (Fortune Business Insights) can absolutely run to $5M ARR before the incumbents notice. The Known Exception precedent exists. What dismantles it: Slash had a documented pivot demonstrating learning rate as the override; Autostep has no pivot, no consequence-magnitude story, and a direct conflict with my own Mimica position. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a signed enterprise contract with a named Fortune-500 logo at >$200K ACV within twelve months -- that would prove distribution defeats incumbent substitution, and I would re-underwrite. Absent that, this is a 30-second pass.
