# Result -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Result is a lived-experience founder building the consensus pitch — Savio Martin, 19, Product Hunt Maker of the Year 2021, genuinely felt the pain of stitching Stripe Atlas to Mercury to a marketing stack, and is now building exactly the platform a 2026 YC batch would tell him to build. The dimensional signature is a founder whose biography earns him the right to attack this problem, mounted on a product thesis that already has Doola at $12M raised and Mercury at $700M raised, $5.2B valuation, sitting on the same lawn. I'm interested in Savio. I'm not interested in this version of the company.

Palmer Luckey is the high rung on the founder-lived-experience ladder — he had shipped Oculus before pointing the same hands at defense, and the prior artifact was the underwriting. Result's Savio Martin has the right shape of that signal at consumer-prosumer scale: Codehouse at 961 GitHub stars, Slickr at 588, an App Store top-five in under a month, 50.5K followers on @saviomartin7 — he is, literally, a new internet entrepreneur who has run into the wall the product names. Where Result diverges from the Luckey rung is the adjacency gap: Savio shipped consumer micro-tools, not a regulated banking-and-incorporation substrate. He has lived the pain of fragmented tooling; he has not lived the BaaS partnership, the compliance posture, or the multi-jurisdiction incorporation surface that Doola has been grinding on for four rounds. Aaryan Kushwah at 17 is a high-school journalism founder and TreeHacks winner, not a co-conspirator who has touched the same wall — the lived experience is one-and-a-half-founders deep, not two.

The pass example in my own playbook is the consensus YC AI-agent darling with the four-logo team page and the six funds circling — Result sits closer to that low rung than I want to admit. The mechanism that kills those companies is not founder quality; it is that the moat lives in the model, not the human, and an incumbent with distribution and a balance sheet adds the agent layer as a feature. Result's dossier names that exact failure mode out loud: Mercury already has the 300,000-customer founder ICP, Doola already has the formation flow, and the AI-operational-agent layer is — per the dossier — "buildable by any of them." Pre-launch waitlist, no signed partner BaaS, no disclosed design partner, two-person team trying to span incorporation + banking + payments + hosting + marketing + support + content. That is not "magically weird." That is a YC application essay.

Counterfactual on governance: the dossier names Aaryan as CEO and Savio as CTO, which clears the bare HQ Trivia trap of a co-CEO 50/50 with no tiebreaker, but two teenage co-founders with no documented operating history is the kind of governance posture that I have watched go sideways under the first real disagreement — the score flips a tier only if there is a documented founder-vesting and decision-rights protocol, and the dossier shows none. Mechanism analysis on the capital-intensity axis: Result is consumer-software-with-regulated-edges, which compounds badly over 18 months — every banking-and-incorporation partner integration adds compliance drag without adding moat, because the partner owns the regulated primitive. This is the inverse of the Crusoe pattern, where the physical-world friction *was* the moat. Single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability: Flock Safety I could read off a Wi-Fi name at a Four Seasons; Result I can read off a waitlist page on result.dev, which is the absence of the signal, not the presence of it.

My 36-month forecast on Result is acquihire to Mercury, Doola, or Brex in the $5M–$20M range, with Savio landing as a product lead on a "founder agents" surface inside the acquirer — not dead, because his distribution buys runway and optionality, but not an independent $10M ARR business either. The mechanism is exactly the dimensional signature: a real founder on a consensus product gets bought for the founder, not the company.

The strongest case against my pass is the HQ Trivia exception — tactile virality from a creator with real audience can override the consensus-market objection, and Savio's 50.5K X followers plus a 961-star repo plus a Product Hunt Golden Kitty is closer to a creator-led distribution machine than to a Stanford CS deck. If Result launches and Savio's audience drives 20,000 incorporations in the first quarter at a price point Mercury cannot match without cannibalizing its banking margin, the bull case is real. The dossier evidence that wins against it: HQ Trivia was a tactile consumer artifact you could literally play and feel pull on your own phone — Result is a B2B operations layer with a waitlist, where audience converts to signups but signups do not convert to the kind of viral coefficient that survives a Mercury feature-add. I flip to invest if Result ships a public product Savio's audience can install in 48 hours and it shows a retention curve that out-paces Doola's bookkeeping attach rate within two quarters.
