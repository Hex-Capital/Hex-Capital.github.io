# Drip -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Drip is the inverse of the company I want -- a clean single-miracle architecture sitting on top of a steady-state market with no disclosed velocity curve and a positioning that has changed three times in public artifacts. Mike Levin's GitHub metered-billing background is genuinely the right shape for the billing pillar, but a 2-person team whose YC page says "enterprise contract operations," whose usedrip.ai markets "dripos: customer history" via a Mac app indexing Gmail/Slack/iMessage, and whose drippay.dev now redirects to "Memra: customer memory for B2B founders" is not a company that has found its catalyst -- it is a company auditioning for one.

**Anduril** (2022) is the ladder rung I reach for on market re-opening: Ukraine compressed a specific Pentagon-software window into 24 months and the dossier *named* the catalyst on page one. Drip's dossier names no such thing. The closest the file comes is the [Inferred] line that "LLM-based context retrieval over Gmail/Slack/Stripe became commodity-cheap in 2025-26" -- and the giveaway is the inferred tag itself. CPQ/CLM is not a re-opening market; it is a *consolidating* market where Ironclad just crossed $200M+ ARR (PRNewswire 2026), Conga sits at $198.2M ARR (Latka 2025), and DealHub just took a $100M Riverwood round in January 2026. Drip is not Anduril skating into a window peers misread as dead -- Drip is walking into a room where DealHub raised $100M five months ago. "Market first and the strength of the team second" (*First Round Review*) tells me to move on within a paragraph.

The Lyft Series C pass is the failure pattern on revenue velocity -- not the absence of a curve but the absence of any credible *mechanism* by which the dossier's silence resolves into screaming growth. Drip's dossier shows "No public data found" on revenue, paying customers, or user count, and the Product Hunt team profile @getdrip exists with no ranked launch. For a pre-seed I do not penalize the absence of an MRR figure; what I penalize is the combined silence with the positioning pivot, because the pivot tells me Drip has not yet found the buyer who pulls the product out of the team's hands. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863) -- the corollary is that you should be able to *see* the growth signal in a YC dossier, and Drip's is empty.

Mike Levin's GitHub metered-billing line is the strongest individual signal in the Drip file -- and the mechanism by which it compounds or fails over 18 months is what I'd test next. GitHub metered billing is a real shipped product at scale, the exact infrastructure Drip's "pricing/entitlements/billing" pillar requires, and that founder-market fit is the kind of thing that produced **Brex** (2017) when Henrique and Pedro mapped Pagar.me payments experience onto corporate cards. Where Drip diverges: Lucas He is listed as Co-Founder & CTO with "no disambiguated profile found" across LinkedIn and GitHub. A pre-seed with one verifiable infrastructure builder and one unverified technical co-founder is not Brex's two-shipper structure -- it's a single legible engineer plus a black box, which means execution risk concentrates on Levin alone.

On index position, the counterfactual is sharp: for Drip to hit the **Stripe** (2010) template of "index on e-commerce," Drip would need to be in the workflow path that *every* enterprise deal flowing through Salesforce, Conga, and Ironclad eventually touches. The dossier's own framing -- "a horizontal context layer cutting across CPQ + CLM + billing would require [incumbents] to cannibalize separate product P&Ls" (G2/Gartner, 2026) -- is the giveaway that Drip is not in the path, Drip is *between* the paths, hoping incumbents won't unify. That is not an index position; it is a wedge bet that requires Salesforce Revenue Cloud and Conga's $198.2M ARR business to keep their P&Ls separate for the next five years. I have seen that bet lose more often than win.

**Color Genomics** (2015) is the single-anchor parallel I use on the single-miracle test, and this is where Drip actually scores best. Color was workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing -- I deliberately isolated the miracle to software, not chemistry. Drip's architecture is the same shape: the underlying capability (LLM context retrieval over Gmail/Slack/Stripe APIs) is commodity, and the remaining work is GTM and product execution, not new science. No new model has to train, no regulatory clearance is required, no consumer behavior must shift. That is a clean single-miracle structure, and it is the reason Drip clears my baseline filter even though the rest of the dossier is thin.

Drip's 36-month forecast: acquihire by a Series A or B sales-tech company (DealHub-adjacent, or a YC-batch peer that found product-market fit faster) at sub-$3M ARR, or quiet shutdown with Levin recycled into a stronger team -- driven by the positioning pivot resolving in favor of whichever buyer pulls hardest first, which in a crowded CPQ/CLM/customer-memory field means no buyer pulls hard enough. The mechanism: scope ambiguity in pre-seed is not a "we'll figure it out" signal, it is a "the market has not pulled us into a corner yet" signal, and in a market with four named $100M+ ARR incumbents already pulling enterprise buyers, the corner does not appear.

The bull case worth steel-manning: Mike Levin built metered billing at GitHub, which is the most credentialed billing-infrastructure resume a 2-person YC team can have, and the **Decagon** (2023) template says workflow infrastructure in an open-LLM-enabled market can compound from zero to category-defining inside 24 months. If Drip commits to one positioning -- say, the customer-history Mac app for B2B founders pulling Gmail/Slack/Stripe -- and Levin's shipping rhythm matches Aravind Srinivas's at **Perplexity** (2022), the architecture and the founder-market fit are both there. The dismantle: Perplexity had one product on day one, and Drip has three (usedrip.ai vs YC pitch vs drippay.dev/Memra) on month four. That is not a founder shipping between meetings; that is a founder repositioning between meetings. The flip condition is specific and verifiable: if Drip lands on a single public positioning and discloses one named enterprise logo within 90 days, I'd take a second meeting -- until then, this is a pass.
