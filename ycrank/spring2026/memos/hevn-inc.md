## The Idea

**Problem:** International (non-US) companies spending in USD-denominated services (e.g., Google/Meta ads) incur FX conversion fees and cannot easily open USD accounts without a US EIN (gethevn.com; YC page).

**Approach:** B2B platform offering: (1) USD virtual cards with 0.5% cashback and no FX fees, (2) named local accounts in EU (Lithuania), US, and UAE for receiving/sending in local currency, (3) ACH/SEPA/wire/stablecoin payment rails, (4) "soon" FDIC-insured USD accounts in a US bank without requiring an EIN, (5) invoice creation, payment collection, bill automation, passkeys/2FA/RBAC (gethevn.com).

**Differentiation:** Positions explicitly as "Brex for international companies" (YC page). Versus Brex/Mercury, which historically require US incorporation/EIN, HEVN markets the ability for non-US-incorporated firms to hold USD; versus Wise/Airwallex (multi-currency wallets), HEVN's pitch centers on a US-bank FDIC-insured account without EIN plus a corporate card with cashback (gethevn.com).

**Business Model:** No public pricing page; the website discloses 0.5% card cashback and account opening "in 1–2 days" (gethevn.com). [Inferred]: Likely interchange-driven revenue (card cashback funded via interchange share) plus FX spread on local-currency rails, with potential SaaS tier later — same playbook used by Brex/Mercury/Airwallex.

**TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for the "USD banking for non-US companies" segment. Adjacent reference point: Airwallex disclosed $1.2B ARR (March 2026) growing ~85% YoY in cross-border SMB banking (Airwallex blog "Celebrating $1 Billion in ARR"; CNBC, Aug 2024).

**GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Most likely distribution path is direct/self-serve onboarding to non-US founders running USD ad spend (Google/Meta) — the homepage hero copy targets that buyer explicitly. Founders' prior crypto-wallet audience (HERE Wallet 600K+ users; HOT Wallet 30M+ users per Chainwire/Grit Daily, Nov 2024) is a plausible adjacent pool for cross-marketing.

## Defensibility

No defensibility signals found in public sources beyond standard regulated-fintech moats. [Inferred]: Potential moat could develop via (a) compliance/licensing footprint across EU/US/UAE banking partners — same regulatory drag that protects Airwallex/Mercury, (b) vertical integration of FDIC-insured no-EIN US accounts if delivered as advertised, and (c) data on cross-border SMB cash flows. None proven at this stage.

**Market structure:** [Inferred]: A structural barrier exists in that US neobanks (Brex, Mercury) underwrite primarily US-incorporated entities and are tied to US bank partners with KYC frameworks built around EIN; serving non-US incorporated entities with USD requires a different sponsor-bank stack and KYB pipeline, creating a meaningful (but not insurmountable) integration cost for incumbents.

**Commoditization risk:** Airwallex, Wise Business, and Payoneer already serve cross-border SMBs with multi-currency wallets (Airwallex blog, 2026; PhotonPay competitor list, 2026). Airwallex specifically markets local accounts in 20+ currencies (Airwallex Brex-alternatives blog, 2026). The "no-EIN FDIC USD account" remains the most novel hook and is the most replicable feature pending bank-partner contracts.

## Key Risks

**Regulatory/banking-partner dependency:** Product depends on "regulated partner institutions" for FDIC eligibility and named accounts in EU/UAE/US (gethevn.com). The "FDIC-insured USD account without EIN" is marketed as "soon" — not live. Sponsor-bank attrition or KYC tightening on non-US KYB would materially impair core differentiation.

**Direct competition with $1B+ ARR incumbent:** Airwallex ($1.2B ARR March 2026, Sacra) already serves cross-border SMBs with local-currency accounts globally and is pre-IPO with $12B valuation (Dec 2025). HEVN's wedge narrows to no-EIN USD accounts and ad-spend cashback.

**Non-US KYB and sanctions exposure:** Targeting non-US-incorporated entities banking in USD raises elevated AML/sanctions screening burden, especially given founders' prior product (HOT Wallet) operated in TON/crypto ecosystems with global retail users (Chainwire, 2024). No public evidence of compliance staffing.

**Founder pivot from crypto to regulated fintech:** Three of four founders are concurrently or recently associated with HOT Labs (HackerNoon 2024; LinkedIn). Bandwidth split and lack of prior US-bank-regulated operating experience are open questions; HOT Labs is reported as still operating as of 2024–2025 press.

**Brand confusion/name disambiguation:** "HEVN" and the website domain "hevn.finance" overlap with the crypto-adjacent prior identity; the company also operates as gethevn.com. Search results conflate Volnov's HOT Labs/HERE Wallet identity with HEVN, which may complicate enterprise sales positioning.
