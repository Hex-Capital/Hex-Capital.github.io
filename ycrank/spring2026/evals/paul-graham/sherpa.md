# Sherpa -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Sherpa is the rare case where the CTO has literally done the job the product replaces — Norbu Sonam ran 50+ growth experiments at Lindy and "tripled paywall conversion" before he and Ethan Kinnan left to build a tool that does exactly that — but the schlep he's automating sits inside the most AI-saturated software category of 2026, where VWO, Optimizely, Kameleoon, and the new Everstone-merged $100M+ platform are all racing to ship the same LLM-generated variants. Strong organic-idea fit, weak schlep moat. That pairing tells you what Sherpa is.

Drew Houston at **Dropbox** (2007) kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT and built the thing for himself before anyone asked; the Collisons at **Stripe** (2010) had been hacking commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007). Sherpa's organic-idea claim is closer to Houston than I expected when I opened the dossier — Norbu didn't just notice the CRO market, he was the human bottleneck inside Lindy's growth funnel running A/B tests by hand, and Ethan was the forward-deployed engineer teaching 10,000+ people to wire Lindy agents into their workflows. Where Sherpa diverges from the Dropbox rung is that Houston had no incumbent shipping a competing USB-replacement; Norbu is shipping the same "AI auto-variant" feature that Kameleoon markets on its homepage and that VWO already wired into its GPT-3.5 copy generation in 2025. Organic, yes. Uncontested, no.

The MBA AI-wrapper team from my Pass Example fails because they did no schlep — pure software, no offline grind, no regulatory exposure, no contrarian recruiting overhead — and the category collapses into feature parity within a year. Sherpa's CRO snippet is structurally the same kind of work: a JS tag, an LLM call, an A/B harness. There is no compliance grind here like **Coinbase** (2012), no last-mile ops like **DoorDash** (2013), no developer-channel hand-building like **Stripe** (2010). The dossier names five direct competitors with similar AI features and a Jan 2026 consolidation (Wingify + AB Tasty under Everstone) that gives the largest incumbent a $100M+ war chest. Sherpa is running into the schlep zone everyone else is already standing in. That's not schlep blindness from competitors — it's the opposite, schlep crowding.

Animal resourcefulness pre-funding: I judge this by what got shipped before the check, and Sherpa's number is 7,338,896 sessions analyzed with named paying-or-using logos (Lindy, Insforge, Gauge) and a +30% Lindy lift cited on the launch page. That is real artifact, not deck. It's not the **Airbnb** (2009) cereal-box stunt — there's no improvised side-channel revenue, no story about how Sherpa survived a quarter no investor would fund them — but it clears the demo-before-deck filter that flipped Dropbox. The 18-month compounding question is whether Norbu and Ethan keep shipping at Lindy-speed once the customer base extends beyond the founder network; the three named logos are all YC-adjacent, which is where the resourcefulness signal weakens.

On hacker genealogy, Sherpa is a split team: Norbu Sonam has a GitHub (@norbusonam), shipped on Microsoft Copilot and Microsoft Designer out of an RPI CS degree, which is a real hacker trail; Ethan Kinnan came through University of Washington Foster — a business school — with no GitHub found in the dossier. One Patrick Collison, one non-Patrick. The score would flip a tier upward if the dossier surfaced an open-source project of Norbu's with material adoption, or a Hacker News demo from before the YC application, the way Houston's pre-seed Dropbox video did. Without that, this is competent-hacker-plus-competent-operator, which is the **Reddit** (2005) configuration but without Aaron Swartz's RSS-spec footprint.

Sherpa's co-founder configuration is a clean pair with a prior working relationship at Lindy — Norbu in growth engineering, Ethan in forward-deployed engineering and partnerships — which is the **Stripe** (2010) shape rather than the solo-founder anti-pattern from "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010). Not roommates-since-college like Huffman and Ohanian at Reddit, but they were in the same growth war room shipping experiments together at a YC-alum company, which is enough to clear the bar. This dimension neither saves nor sinks Sherpa.

My 36-month forecast: Sherpa reaches $2-5M ARR on SMB and YC-network CRO seats, then gets acquired by Statsig, Optimizely, or the Everstone/VWO/AB Tasty combine for $40-100M as an AI-feature accelerator — a respectable angel outcome and exactly the **Kiko** (2005)-to-eBay shape rather than the Twitch one. The mechanism is that the organic founder-pain fit and the Lindy-network distribution carry Sherpa to product-market fit fast, but the schlep crowding means incumbents close the feature gap before Sherpa builds a category-defining moat, and the buyers absorb the team to plug their AI roadmap.

Steel-manning the bull case: the +30% lift at Lindy and the 2.5x Gauge demo-booking number are not LLM vaporware — those are real revenue events on real funnels, and the 7.3M-session dataset compounds into a cross-customer prior that no incumbent can replicate until they retrofit. If Norbu and Ethan ride the Lindy-alumni Trojan horse into the entire AI-native SMB cohort the way **Stripe** (2010) rode developer Twitter into payments default, the seat-based incumbents have a structural channel conflict they cannot resolve, and Sherpa becomes the CRO substrate for every Series-A AI company in the next batch. That sting is real, and the **Airbnb** (2009) Known Exception is the precedent — I'd be funding a team whose idea I'd discount on category, on the founder evidence alone. What dismantles it is the dossier specific that VWO already shipped GPT-3.5 copy generation in 2025 and Kameleoon already markets "AI for A/B Testing" — the schlep is not unowned, it's contested, and contested-schlep companies in saturated categories rarely become Black Swans regardless of founder quality. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named non-Lindy-network enterprise customer at >$50K ACV with documented lift inside six months, the way Armstrong's wallet prototype overturned the **Coinbase** (2012) rejection. Until then, Sherpa is a strong neutral.
