# Netter -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Netter is a mid-market data infrastructure bet whose highest dimension is the ontology-layer positioning Samuel Ladroue and Amin Hamzaoui are pointing at, and whose lowest dimension is the founder profile underneath it -- a 4-person Paris team with a CTO whose LinkedIn headline still reads "Student at Mines Paris and ENS Ulm." That pairing tells me what kind of company Netter is: a technically credible infrastructure thesis attached to operators who have not yet been tested at the 1-to-100 transition, attacking a category where Fivetran has $200M+ ARR and Airbyte advertises 550+ connectors against Netter's 120+.

On infrastructure-layer positioning -- Netter's strongest axis -- the ladder runs Stripe at the top (the payments API an entire generation built on), Harvey in the middle (platform-shaped but application-flavored, embedded in legal workflows), and Wish at the bottom (pure consumer surface, no API, no leverage). Netter sits between Harvey and Stripe, closer to Harvey, because the "living ontology" wired into a customer's TMS, ERP, loyalty, and accounting systems is genuinely the kind of layer other workflows compound on -- if Carrefour standardizes nursing-home-style AR recovery on Netter's ontology, ripping it out across 120+ connectors gets expensive. The reason Netter doesn't reach Stripe's rung is that the ontology is bundled with chat-driven app-building aimed at non-technical operators, which is an application surface, not an API other businesses program against, and the dossier shows zero evidence Netter has accumulated reusable vertical schemas yet.

On operator-scaler founder profile -- Netter's weakest axis -- the ladder runs Parker Conrad rebuilding Rippling after Zenefits at the top, a strong technical first-time founder in the middle, and the equal-co-founders-with-no-CEO pattern at the bottom. Netter sits between the middle rung and the bottom: Hamzaoui's Mines Paris + ENS Ulm pedigree gives me technical conviction, but the CTO is still a student and Ladroue's pre-Netter operating history could not be verified -- the dossier flags "Almanax" as unconfirmed and LinkedIn was blocked. I have no evidence Ladroue has scaled a sales org, hired through hypergrowth, or made the painful decisions the High Growth Handbook is about. Selling horizontal data infrastructure to retail, healthcare, manufacturing, logistics, and real estate simultaneously with a 4-person team and zero open jobs is a focus problem that an experienced operator-CEO would have already solved.

On nonobvious market timing, the ladder is Perplexity (AI capability curve crossing a threshold before consensus) at the top, Gusto (boring-but-critical, no specific timing catalyst) in the middle, and the consumer social apps I pass on at the bottom. Netter sits closer to Gusto than to Perplexity. The NL-to-SQL/Python reliability threshold is real, but the dossier itself names DataSci.Pro from YC X25 chasing the same wedge, and incumbents from Fivetran ($565M+ raised) to Palantir Foundry ($2.8B 2023 revenue) to Workato ($100M ARR) are all moving on chat-driven mid-market data. This isn't crypto in 2012 or AI search in 2022 -- this is consensus, and the timestamp shows it.

On single-miracle viability, the ladder runs Harvey at the top (one exogenous miracle: foundation model capability), Gusto in the middle (regulatory execution miracle), and Anduril at the bottom (three simultaneous miracles, overridden only by a generational market signal). Netter sits between middle and bottom: the company needs NL-to-pipeline reliability at production quality, plus GTM execution across five verticals, plus defensibility against Fivetran/Airbyte/Palantir, plus a connector arms race against an open-source competitor with 4.5x more connectors. That's not one miracle; that's compounding small odds, and unlike Anduril, the market structure signal isn't generational enough to override.

On margin architecture, the ladder is Abridge (per-encounter, value-scaled) at the top, standard per-seat SaaS in the middle, Wish (commoditized low margin) at the bottom. Netter has no public pricing page, so I apply the absent-evidence default -- but I'd note Netter is exactly the kind of "selling units of cognition" opportunity my 2025 essay called out, and an outcome-based price (per workflow recovered, per dormant receivable collected -- the marketed "10% recovered within weeks" is the natural unit) would push this toward the Abridge rung. Whether they'll price that way is unknown.

My 36-month forecast on Netter: lands at $1-3M ARR in EU mid-market off the Carrefour/Tesco/Auchan beachhead, gets compressed between Palantir Foundry pushing down-market and Workato/Celonis pushing up, and either gets acqui-hired into an iPaaS or vertical SaaS player for sub-$75M or pivots from horizontal to a single vertical (most likely logistics route+load optimization, since that's the case with the cleanest measurable ROI in the dossier). Mechanism: connector commoditization plus five-vertical sprawl plus an unverified commercial CEO produces a respectable EU mid-market business that doesn't reach venture-scale escape velocity.

The strongest objection to my pass: Netter could be the "boring but critical" Gusto pattern in EU mid-market, where prestige-seeking US investors won't compete, where Palantir is too expensive, where Fivetran sells data movement without the workflow layer the dossier's nursing-home-AR and empty-trucks examples actually require, and where founder-led direct sales in Paris produces sticky deals that compound into a category-defining EU data autopilot. That case stings because the unstated pattern about "boring infrastructure with no prestige-seeking competition" genuinely applies. What dismantles it is the dossier evidence that this category is already crowded -- DataSci.Pro is named in the dossier, Palantir is moving down-market, the connector layer is being commoditized by open-source -- and the Gusto comparison breaks because Joshua Reeves had scaling instincts and a sharp focus on one category. Netter has neither yet. Flip condition: if within 12 months Netter shows two paid six-figure contracts (not pilots) on Carrefour or Tesco with disclosed ACVs, and narrows to two verticals with named GTM hires, I write a check on the next round.
