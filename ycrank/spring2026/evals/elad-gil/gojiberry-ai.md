# Gojiberry AI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

$1.4M ARR in nine months, bootstrapped, 1,000+ paying customers, 44% month-over-month growth -- these are real numbers, and at pre-seed they demand attention. If I only looked at Gojiberry AI's growth curve, I'd be reaching for my checkbook. But I've seen this movie before. Growth velocity in a commoditized market without structural protection is exactly the pattern that burned me with Wish -- fast adoption, no durable advantage, and eventually the market grinds you down. The core question with Gojiberry AI isn't whether they can acquire customers at $99/month. They've clearly proven they can. The question is whether anything structural prevents the next 50 AI outbound tools from doing the same thing.

AI-powered sales outreach is one of the most obvious markets in SaaS right now. Artisan has raised $46.1M building an AI SDR. Amplemarket raised $12M for a multi-channel sales copilot. Persana, Valley, Salesforge -- the list goes on. Every seed investor I know has seen 20 pitches in this category in the last year. When I look for nonobvious market timing -- the structural shift that consensus hasn't recognized -- I find the opposite with Gojiberry AI. The enabling technology shift (LLMs capable of personalized message generation) crossed the capability threshold two years ago, and every founder building in outbound sales knows it. The LinkedIn-intent-signal angle is a feature differentiation, not a structural catalyst. Compare this to when I invested in Coinbase: crypto infrastructure was structurally shifting but the market was dismissed as a toy. With Gojiberry AI, the market is recognized and crowded. There's no timestamp mismatch to exploit.

Gojiberry AI's stack position concerns me more than the competition. The entire product is LinkedIn-native -- intent signals drawn from LinkedIn activity, outreach executed via LinkedIn DMs, the Pro tier capped at 2 LinkedIn senders. This is an application built entirely on someone else's infrastructure layer. LinkedIn controls the API, the data access, the acceptable use policies. The dossier notes Reddit complaints about spam behavior associated with Gojiberry AI's platform. One policy change from LinkedIn and the core product breaks. I explicitly avoid companies building applications on contested infrastructure layers -- I'd rather invest in the infrastructure layer itself. When I backed Stripe, I was backing the payments API that thousands of companies would depend on. Gojiberry AI occupies the opposite position: it depends on LinkedIn's API, and LinkedIn has every incentive to build native intent-based features or restrict third-party automation.

Pierre-Eliott Lallemant, Román Czerny, and Dylan Teixeira bring legitimate repeat-founder credentials -- CoCo AI sold in 2025, Edusign scaled to 600K+ users and also exited. The pivot from a failed AI sales assistant (2,000-person waitlist, zero conversions) to intent-based lead generation shows decisiveness, which I value. But two flags pull the founder score down. First, Pierre-Eliott is co-founder of both Gojiberry AI and Standout, both in YC Spring 2026 -- splitting CEO attention across two startups during the same batch is the opposite of the speed advantage I believe is a startup's only edge against incumbents. Second, none of these founders has operated inside a company during hypergrowth scaling (10 to 1,000+ employees). CoCo AI and Edusign were small exits. Building a $99/month SMB business to $1.4M ARR is excellent execution; scaling it to $50M ARR against Artisan's $46.1M war chest requires organizational capabilities I can't verify from the dossier.

The margin architecture is where Gojiberry AI's bull case breaks down for me. At $99/month -- roughly $1,200 ACV -- this is an SMB volume play. SMB SaaS churn rates typically run 3-5% monthly. The Pro tier's 2-sender cap limits natural expansion within accounts. I see no evidence of pricing power, negative net revenue churn, or outcome-based pricing that scales with value delivered. My framework says the best signal of product-market fit is the ability to raise prices repeatedly without losing customers. Gojiberry AI's price point and feature structure suggest a race to the bottom against competitors like Salesforge (unlimited senders, multi-channel) rather than a race to capture more value per customer. The "Custom plan" exists but with no pricing data, I can't assess whether there's a credible path to higher ACV.

The strongest bull case: these founders have proven they can build and sell SaaS products, they pivoted with conviction, and $1.4M ARR bootstrapped is top-decile execution for any YC batch. The market is large ($16B global sales automation in 2025). Perhaps Gojiberry AI's LinkedIn-native focus creates a wedge into SMB teams that larger multi-channel competitors ignore, and the compounding optimization data per customer creates real switching costs over time. I've checked my Known Exceptions -- none applies here. There's no overwhelming structural shift that would override the crowded-market concern (unlike Anduril's defense modernization wave), and no deep personal domain expertise creating conviction that pure market analysis can't replicate (unlike BioAge). The condition that would flip me to Invest: evidence that Gojiberry AI's per-customer optimization flywheel produces measurably superior conversion rates over time -- say, 2x reply rates versus competitors at month 6 -- combined with net revenue retention above 120%, demonstrating that the data compounding effect is real and creates genuine lock-in. Without that, this is a growth story in an obvious market, and I've seen how those end.
