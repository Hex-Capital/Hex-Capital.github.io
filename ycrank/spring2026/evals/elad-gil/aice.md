# AICE -- Elad Gil Evaluation

AICE is the inverse of the **Anduril** (2022) template I keep getting asked to apply to every defense pre-seed that walks through YC: a real market re-opening (subsea sabotage as grey-zone conflict, France's May 2024 underwater-acoustics AI program per *Defense News*) attacked by a 2-person team with no naval-systems shipping history, two weeks after pivoting off an energy-sensor product. The catalyst is the strongest dimension here; the index position against **Anduril**'s Dive-LD and **Helsing**'s SG-1 Fathom is the weakest. That pairing — right window, wrong wedge — is AICE's signature in my book.

**Anduril** (2022) was my canonical defense market-re-opening: Ukraine in a 24-month window, Pentagon software inadequacy made legible, software-on-defense as the wedge. AICE has a structurally similar catalyst — Nord Stream, Baltic cable cuts, the France DGA underwater-acoustics AI program (*Defense News*, May 2024) — and the *MarketsandMarkets* AUV report explicitly frames subsea infrastructure as "prime targets in grey-zone conflict." That is a named catalyst inside the 24-month window I look for, not steady-state TAM math. Where AICE diverges from the **Anduril** rung is that **Anduril**'s catalyst arrived attached to Trae Stephens (Palantir forward-deployed muscle) and Palmer Luckey (Oculus shipped at scale); AICE's catalyst arrives attached to an MIT AeroAstro thesis on asteroid risk and a 282-star ESP32 airplay repo. The window is real. The team holding the door open is not the team I would have picked to walk through it.

**Lyft** Series C is my standing reminder that the *position in the market structure* dominates the *quality of the market*: rideshare was a great market; #2 in a winner-take-all was a worse version of the obvious bet (20VC). AICE's distribution position is a version of that pattern in subsea — **Anduril** Dive-LD is shipping with 6,000m depth and 10-day endurance, **Helsing** SG-1 Fathom is a 60kg glider doing 90-day surveillance and Helsing already acquired Australia's Blue Ocean (Oct 2025), **Saronic** just closed $1.75B at $9.25B post (PRNewswire, Mar 2026). AICE's claimed wedge is "modular swarm at the small form factor," which is a feature differentiation pitched into a procurement cycle that rewards incumbents with prior program-of-record relationships. This is the **Lyft** geometry: AICE doesn't sit *in the workflow path* the way **Stripe** (2010) sat across e-commerce; AICE must *beat* named, better-capitalized incumbents on features inside the same RFPs.

Revenue velocity — the **Harvey** (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" test — is where I evaluate AICE on a mechanism-over-18-months basis. Naval procurement compounds the opposite direction from SaaS: ITAR qualification, security clearance gating, multi-year program-of-record cycles. Even if AICE wins a French DGA pilot in 2026, the curve I would see is a single six- or seven-figure contract followed by a two-year integration tail, not the MoM compounding I look for. AICE pivoted ~2 weeks before the dossier was assembled (Pivot Archive, May 2026) — there is no curve to "just work" yet, and the procurement physics of the chosen market mean there won't be one inside my angel-stage horizon. Silence on growth in an AI/autonomy company is itself a signal, and here it's compounded by the fact that the market structure won't produce the signal even if AICE executes.

Founder shipping is best diagnosed counterfactually: what would have to be true for me to put AICE in the **Perplexity** (2022) bucket, where Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built? I would need to see Chomette or Bouteiller having shipped a prior autonomous hardware artifact — even a hobby AUV with a swarm-coordination demo on YouTube, even an open-source ROS stack with real commits. Bouteiller's airplay-esp32 (282 stars, github.com/rbouteiller) is evidence of an embedded engineer who finishes things, which is non-zero. Chomette's NASA Asteroid Risk Assessment Engineer 2019-2021 role is research, not shipping. The dossier shows no naval, marine, or defense-systems artifact from either founder. To flip this dimension from "Pass" tier to "Invest" tier I'd need a tank-test video and a named ex-DCNS/Naval Group advisor on the cap table. Neither is in the dossier.

Single-miracle — compare AICE on this axis to **Color Genomics** (2015), the cleanest workflow-on-top-of-hard-thing in my portfolio. **Color** isolated the miracle to data/workflow and bought the chemistry layer commoditized. AICE asks for three independent miracles to land simultaneously: (1) modular swarm coordination software that works in subsea acoustic environments, (2) submersible hardware rated to 600m manufactured in France at competitive cost against Riptide and **Anduril**'s acquired Dive Technologies stack, (3) ITAR/export-control navigation plus French and allied-navy procurement wins as a 2-person team. That is the multi-miracle compounding I pass on. The **Anduril** Known Exception doesn't apply here — I overrode my "avoid science projects" rule for **Anduril** *because* Stephens and Luckey had shipped at scale and the round was a growth-stage check, not because defense itself is exempt.

Forecast: in 36 months AICE is either acqui-hired by a European defense prime — **Thales**, Naval Group, or **Helsing** in a tuck-in similar to Helsing-Blue Ocean (Oct 2025) — for under $30M, or it is dead, having failed to land a first French DGA contract before **Anduril** Dive-LD or a Helsing-Fathom subsea variant wins the NATO subsea ISR program-of-record this catalyst is actually producing. The mechanism is the **Lyft** geometry compounding against the **Anduril** team-gap: real catalyst, wrong wedge, undermatched against incumbents who are already inside the procurement cycle.

The strongest objection to a Pass is the **Anduril** (2022) precedent itself: defense looked like a graveyard in 2022, peers had "arrows in the back" (*First Round Review*), and I overrode my own science-project rule. Why not run that override again for AICE? Because the override required three pieces — shipping-at-scale founders, single-miracle isolation, and a growth-stage check where the velocity was already visible — and AICE has none of the three at pre-seed. The **Color Genomics** (2015) exception also doesn't apply, because I *operated* Color and architected it as workflow-on-commodity-sequencing; AICE is the opposite, betting on the hard-tech layer (subsea hardware) directly against incumbents who already own that layer. One verifiable condition would flip my verdict: a signed French DGA or allied-navy contract (not LOI, signed) within the next 6 months, which would prove the procurement cycle is compressing for AICE specifically — at that point I'd reconsider on the **Anduril** template rather than the **Lyft** one.
