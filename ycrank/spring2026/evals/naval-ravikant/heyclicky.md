# HeyClicky -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

HeyClicky is a pure media-leverage bet wrapped around Farza Majeed's 89.3K-follower FarzaTV channel, attached to a single-sided consumer tool with no network mechanic -- a 2.9M-view launch demo distributing a tutor overlay where the Nth user does nothing for the (N-1)th. That high/low pairing -- permissionless founder-media distribution stapled to a Perplexity-shaped single-player app -- is exactly the geometry of the Known Exception I had to talk myself into in 2022, except HeyClicky enters the AI cycle three years later, post-consensus, into a category where OpenAI's "Work with Apps" and Anthropic's "computer use" already ship the substrate.

Permissionless leverage is where HeyClicky places highest. The HIGH rung is Twitter -- media as protocol, anyone posts, zero marginal cost, the architecture that paid 400x. The LOW rung is Anchorage Digital, which I funded only because I had been compounding into the crypto category since co-founding MetaStable in 2014, and which required a Treasury OCC charter to distribute at all. HeyClicky sits much closer to Twitter than to Anchorage: the farzaa/clicky repo crossed ~5.8k GitHub stars, the launch post drew ~15,000 likes and ~2.9M views off FarzaTV alone, XDA Developers wrote it up unprompted, and the founder open-sourced the whole stack in April 2026. That is the exact code-plus-media compounding loop nav.al/product-media describes -- the gatekeeper is not Apple, not the App Store, not a sales team; it is a menu-bar binary and a tweet. The only thing dragging HeyClicky off the Twitter rung is that the surface is a Mac app, not a URL, which caps the velocity of replication.

Network compounding is where HeyClicky places lowest, and it is the dimension that historically gates my upside math. The HIGH rung is Uber -- adding a driver mechanically improves the rider experience, which is what makes the 4,000x outcome geometrically possible. The MID rung is Stack Overflow, where each answered question compounds the corpus for every subsequent developer. The LOW rung is Perplexity, a single-sided foundation-model app where each user's session is economically independent of the next -- the very reason I had to invoke an exception. HeyClicky pins below Perplexity on this axis: Clicky is a screenshot-plus-transcript pipe into Claude with ElevenLabs TTS on the way back, no shared corpus, no user-generated artifact, no two-sided lift. The Joel Fickson testimonial about "YC companies building on top of this" hints at a developer ecosystem, but the dossier shows zero evidence that one user's Clicky session makes any other user's session better.

Specific knowledge places HeyClicky in the upper-middle band. The HIGH rung is Replit's Amjad Masad, who had been obsessing over web-based code environments since 2011 before I came in alongside a16z Growth at the $1.16B post in April 2023. The LOW rung is the "ex-McKinsey identified opportunity" pattern Almanack warns about. Farza Majeed sits between Notion's Ivan Zhao and Replit -- he ran buildspace from 2019 to 2024, took a $10M a16z Series A at $100M post in November 2022, and built a learning community for builders before that company wound down in August 2024. He is shipping a tutor product for learners, which is exactly the audience he spent five years talking to, and HeyClicky is his second YC swing. The buildspace shutdown ticks integrity ambiguity, not specific-knowledge weakness; the domain fluency is real and load-bearing.

Big market with pivot room places HeyClicky between Uber and a vertical-SaaS LOW rung like "CRM for HVAC." Consumer Mac AI assistants is platform-shaped -- pivot vectors run into education, creative-tool walkthroughs, enterprise onboarding, voice-first interfaces -- but the pivot space is compressed by OpenAI and Anthropic shipping native screen awareness, which is the inverse of Twitter's wide-open Odeo-podcasting-to-microblogging maneuver in 2007. The Buffett trio on founder reads clean enough -- Farza has been public on X since July 2011, ships obsessively, no litigation, no integrity-advertising tells of the kind I learned to flag after Epinions -- but he is solo, and Venture Hacks ~2007-10 is explicit that picking a co-founder is the most important decision. HeyClicky has effectively deferred that decision, which sits between the Notion-Ivan-Zhao MID rung and the Stack-Overflow-Atwood-Spolsky HIGH rung, closer to Notion.

HeyClicky in 36 months: either Farza pivots HeyClicky into an education-creator platform riding the FarzaTV audience to $2-5M ARR by 2029, or Anthropic/OpenAI absorbs the team as a consumer-onboarding acqui-hire under $30M, with a ~30% probability the company winds down like buildspace did in August 2024 because the Claude+AssemblyAI+ElevenLabs COGS stack never finds a monetization tier that survives the free open-source build the founder himself shipped. The mechanism is the high/low pairing -- the FarzaTV media leverage produces top-of-funnel velocity, the absent network mechanic prevents that velocity from compounding into structural lock-in, and the gap gets resolved either by audience-monetization (education pivot) or by an incumbent acquisition.

The strongest bull case I owe a real hearing: the Perplexity exception is on the table. Criterion #1 leverage passes at top quartile via FarzaTV and the open-source repo, criterion #3 specific knowledge passes through five years of buildspace domain immersion, and the unstated builder-tools-for-builders pattern -- the lens that ties AngelList 2010, Spearhead 2017, Airchat April 2024, and Quill Notes February 2026 -- explicitly tolerates single-sided products when the founder is the leverage user. That argument stings; it is the same shape I used on Perplexity. What dismantles it for HeyClicky is category timing: Perplexity entered in 2022 pre-ChatGPT-scale, and I had been dollar-cost-averaging into AI since before consensus. HeyClicky launches in Spring 2026 post-Cluely's $20.3M raise, post-Granola's $1.5B mark, post-OpenAI-Work-with-Apps -- the narrative has arrived, and the leverage architecture is now competing with two trillion-dollar distributions. The verdict flips to Invest if HeyClicky lands a paid tier above $1M ARR within twelve months on FarzaTV distribution alone, because that single number would prove the founder-as-media-leverage closes the network-mechanic gap on its own.
