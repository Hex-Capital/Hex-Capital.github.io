# Rise Reforming -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Rise Reforming is the inverse of the **Color Genomics** (2015) architecture I co-founded around: instead of workflow infrastructure sitting *on top* of a commoditized hard-tech layer, three UChicago undergrads are betting the company on being the hard-tech layer itself -- a containerized biogas reformer with 1,800 hours of syngas proof-of-concept and $0 of realized revenue against a competitor (Emvolon) that has raised $10.5M and is closing a $30M Series A. The highest-scoring dimension for Rise Reforming is a plausible IMO-2024 / RNG-incentive catalyst; the lowest is single-miracle architecture, and that pairing tells you exactly what kind of company this is -- a science-adjacent hard-tech seed pretending to be a workflow bet, in a category where a better-capitalized #2 has already declared.

On single-miracle architecture -- my lowest ladder placement for Rise Reforming -- the failure mechanism I flagged when I stayed out of most pre-transformer ML and virtually all of fusion, novel battery chem, and therapeutics is the same one Vinod tolerates and I don't: "capitalization issues and science risk and market risk" compounding (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). Rise Reforming's dossier lists at least four independent miracles required for the check to pencil -- catalyst durability against biogas-derived syngas coking (flagged as a known industry problem in the ScienceDirect citation), containerized-scale commercial DME/methanol yield beyond the 1,800-hour POC, converting a "conditional" $5M offtake and non-binding marine LOIs into contracted volumes, and hitting unit economics that undercut Topsoe's SynCOR at licensed scale. That is not the **Anduril** (2022) override, where the miracle was isolated to software-on-defense-distribution and the hardware referenced existing autonomy stacks; Rise Reforming is asking me to underwrite the chemistry, the reactor, the offtake, and the GTM simultaneously.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing rung for the market-catalyst dimension because Ukraine gave defense a specific 24-month re-opening peers were misreading. Rise Reforming's dossier names two candidate catalysts -- federal RNG incentives and IMO 2024 low-carbon marine fuel rules -- and both are real, but both are prefixed "[Inferred]:" in the dossier, and neither is a step-function of the Ukraine-to-Palantir-inadequacy magnitude. IMO 2024 is genuinely why marine methanol demand is compounding, which is why the two marine LOIs are the most interesting artifact in this dossier; Rise Reforming diverges from the Anduril template because the catalyst opens a window for *methanol supply* broadly, not for containerized biogas reforming specifically, so the same tailwind lifts Emvolon, Modular Plant Solutions, and Topsoe's SynCOR at the same time.

The revenue-velocity dimension is where Rise Reforming most directly violates the "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" test -- and while this isn't AI, the equivalent for hard-tech is contracted, not "MOU-ed," backlog. The mechanism that compounds over 18 months is that MOUs are enterprise-pilot tourism unless they convert, and Rise Reforming's dossier shows a 6.5x discrepancy between PitchBook's $14M ARR figure (1 binding + 6 MOUs) and the YC company page's $92M ARR-worth-of-chemicals figure (7 supply agreements) -- that gap is exactly the "false signal vs. just working" distinction from my TechCrunch piece (Nov 2025). By month 18, either the $5M DME offtake has become contracted volume with recognized revenue or it has quietly disappeared from the deck; the base rate on "conditional" hard-tech offtakes converting on schedule is not kind.

Founder-ships-iteratively for Rise Reforming would flip a tier only if George Rose, Lucas Zubillaga Maharg, and Jona van Oord had shipped a *prior* containerized reactor as UChicago undergrads and were now iterating on version two -- the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind pattern of showing up a week later with the thing we discussed built. The dossier gives us a DOE EnergyTech UP $50K prize, a 776 Climate Fellowship for Rose, 1,800 hours of syngas POC, and a Gagliardi Group / Patel Lab / Argonne lineage that is respectable Molecular Engineering training -- but the earlier UChicago and 776 coverage describes the product as *plastic waste to DME*, and the YC S26 pitch is *biogas* to DME/methanol. That is a feedstock pivot mid-pre-seed, and the counterfactual where I score this higher requires the dossier to name which artifacts survived the pivot; absent that reconciliation, this is undergrad domain-competent, not shipping-veteran.

Rise Reforming's index-position problem is the **Lyft** Series C problem in miniature. Emvolon has a Montauk Renewables JV, a Pioneer Energy partnership, ~$8.3M in DOE grants, and a multi-year lead as an MIT spin-off; Modular Plant Solutions is already selling MeOH-To-Go? and DME-To-Go? skids; Advanced Energy Materials LLC has USDA NIFA funding for the same modular-biogas-to-liquid-fuels wedge. Rise Reforming is not the workflow layer every biogas producer must touch -- it is one of at least four modular reactor vendors competing for the same wastewater and agricultural sites, and the moat cited ("patent-pending process" plus one exclusive supply agreement) is not the developer-channel compounding that made **Stripe** (2010) an index on e-commerce.

My 36-month forecast for Rise Reforming: raised a bridge round on the back of one commercial containerized unit deployed at the Chicagoland wastewater pilot, under $2M in recognized revenue against the $92M "ARR-worth-of-chemicals" claim, and either absorbed by a strategic in the RNG/waste-conversion consolidation wave -- Waste Management, Montauk, or an Emvolon roll-up -- at under $60M, or still fundraising against dilutive terms with the DME offtake having quietly gone unmentioned. The mechanism is the single-miracle-becomes-four-miracles compounding: at each stage the probability the next miracle lands is independent, and Emvolon burns capital faster on the same wedge.

The strongest opposing case for Rise Reforming is that IMO 2024 marine methanol is a genuine category re-opening, the two marine LOIs are the leading edge of a supply-constrained buy-side, and the "workflow infrastructure on top of hard thing" pattern I used to justify **Anduril** (2022) could plausibly apply here -- the reforming reactor is *software plus catalyst on top of* commodity biogas the same way Anduril is software on top of existing autonomy hardware. That case stings because the marine methanol demand curve is real and I've written about the accounting/compliance/energy-tooling adjacencies as "open markets" in *AI Market Clarity*. It fails on Rise Reforming's specifics: Emvolon is not Lockheed -- Emvolon is a well-funded direct competitor with the same technical thesis and a shipping JV, so this is not defense modernization where the incumbents are structurally incapable of the software layer; it is a modular-reactor knife fight where the leader has 20x the capital. The verifiable condition that flips my pass to an invest is a binding, non-conditional offtake north of $10M annualized executed inside the next six months with a named counterparty like Maersk or Enterprise Products -- until then, this is a science project with a marine-fuel tailwind, and I stay out.
