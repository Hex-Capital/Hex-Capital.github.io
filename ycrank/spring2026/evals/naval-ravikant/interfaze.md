# Interfaze -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Interfaze published its core architectural innovation — the hybrid DNN/CNN perception routing that IS the product — in a public arXiv paper (arXiv:2602.04101) accepted at IEEE CAI 2026. This is the most analytically revealing decision in the entire dossier. A company whose differentiation is an architectural insight just handed that insight to every well-funded competitor. Firecrawl has $16.2M. Google Document AI has infinite resources. The paper demonstrates technical competence, but competence you can teach is not specific knowledge — it's engineering skill. When I backed Perplexity, Aravind Srinivas had years of building search systems at Google Brain and OpenAI. That knowledge was fused into his nervous system. Yoeven Khemlani built a prop-tech startup (Stayr) until 2022, became a technical lead at Staytion Singapore, then founded JigsawStack in July 2024, then rebranded to Interfaze in late 2025. That's a founder tracking opportunity, not a founder who couldn't stop thinking about deterministic perception models. Harsha Vardhan Khurdula brings Purdue ML research in CV/NLP — legitimate credentials, but the kind of knowledge any strong graduate program produces. Interfaze's specific knowledge density is thin.

Interfaze's leverage architecture is more interesting than its founders' knowledge stack. Consumption-based pricing at $1.50/M input tokens, OpenAI-compatible API, specialized perception modules routing to LLMs for final reasoning — this is structurally a code leverage play with zero marginal cost per query. Five people shipping OCR, web scraping, speech-to-text, and document classification behind a unified API is genuine leverage. But Interfaze is partially renting someone else's leverage — the Groq partnership and LLM dependency for the reasoning layer means the foundation model providers capture the deepest value. When I evaluate leverage, I ask whether the company owns the leverage or borrows it. Notion built its own editor, its own data model, its own everything — the leverage was internal. Interfaze owns the perception routing layer but rents the reasoning layer. The custom web engine for bot-protected sites is proprietary and continuously evolving — that's real leverage. But the overall architecture is a hybrid of owned and borrowed.

Yoeven relocated from Singapore to San Francisco in 2025 to build Interfaze — that's a principal-level sacrifice. But the trajectory from prop-tech (Stayr, 2020-2022) to AI APIs (JigsawStack, mid-2024) to rebranded AI model company (Interfaze, late 2025) reads like a founder iterating toward product-market fit rather than a principal whose identity is fused with the problem. The email domain is still jigsawstack.com. The rebrand is incomplete. I weight revealed behavior over stated passion, and what I see is adaptability — which is a survival trait, not a conviction signal. Harsha's GitHub bio — "I have a dream, NLP for everyone!" — suggests genuine passion for NLP democratization, which maps to Interfaze's mission. But no evidence exists that either founder was solving the deterministic-perception problem for themselves before deciding to start a company around it.

The bull case an advocate would make: Interfaze is building the perception infrastructure layer for AI. The architectural insight — separate what LLMs are bad at (deterministic perception) from what they're good at (reasoning) — is genuinely sound. IEEE peer review validates it. The Vercel AI Accelerator and Groq partnership provide distribution leverage. Five people shipping a multi-capability platform with 98-99% structured output accuracy and sub-5-second response times is the kind of team-to-value ratio I look for — this is the shrinking company thesis in action. If Interfaze becomes the default perception API the way Stripe became the default payments API, the bundling advantage compounds as each new capability makes the unified API harder to leave. Against this: the 98-99% accuracy claim lacks independent verification. No public revenue exists despite the product being live since mid-2024. The JigsawStack GitHub repos top out at 173 stars on the deep-research repo — compare that to Firecrawl's open-source community. Interfaze is competing simultaneously against Google Document AI in OCR, Firecrawl ($16.2M) in scraping, Whisper in STT, and specialized classifiers — each domain has dedicated teams optimizing for that single task. Bundling only wins if the integration value exceeds the depth penalty, and no evidence in this dossier proves that threshold has been crossed.

Interfaze scores reasonably on access democratization — unifying fragmented AI capabilities behind a single OpenAI-compatible API removes a real integration tax for developers. But it democratizes convenience, not access to a previously restricted resource. OCR, STT, and scraping are already available from multiple providers. Compare this to AngelList, which gave founders access to angel investors who were previously invisible behind country club doors, or CoinList, which removed securities lawyers from token launches. Interfaze makes existing capabilities easier to consume together. That's useful. It's not structural access expansion.

One verifiable condition would flip me to Invest: evidence that Interfaze's unified perception layer generates compound returns — specifically, that multi-task usage data from OCR queries improves scraping accuracy, or that the routing layer learns cross-task optimizations that single-task competitors cannot replicate. If the architecture compounds, the published paper becomes a feature (academic credibility) rather than a bug (competitive exposure). Without that data flywheel evidence, Interfaze is a well-executed bundling play with replaceable specific knowledge and borrowed leverage — a bet on execution speed in a commoditizing space.
