# ValCtrl -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

ValCtrl is, at the highest dimension I can find for it, a documented pivoter — Sarthak Garg and Gaurav Paliwal moved from an Excel "Analyst Copilot" IDAE to "Kassandre Search" for prediction finance — and at the lowest, a software wrapper on a $24B/month venue layer whose consequence-at-maximum-success is "better routing to a Polymarket contract." That is the signature: a learning-rate signal sitting on top of a near-zero consequence variable. I have written one check against that pairing before — **Slash** (2024) — and I will explain below why ValCtrl is not it.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence magnitude — $50M into a no-revenue structure because the success-state was a civilizational redirection of cognitive labor, the $15T figure I keep returning to in Fortune 2026-03-06. The mechanism that made OpenAI load-bearing was that if it worked, an entire category of economic activity moved; the question was not "capture share of NLP," it was "what breaks." Run the same question on ValCtrl: if Kassandre Search wins maximally, forecasters and YC founders type a belief in English and get the right Polymarket or Kalshi contract back. No incumbent regime collapses. The $24B/month Pew figure is venue volume, not consequence — ValCtrl is a search bar on top of two exchanges that together already hold 97.5% share. That is the **DoorDash** (2013) consequence ceiling at best, without DoorDash's distribution moat, and it is far closer to the GPT-4-summarizes-sales-calls pass example I described than to any anchor in my portfolio.

The LOW rung failure mechanism I watch for on technical risk is the Pass Example: GPT-wrapper on commodity model, replicable by a solo developer in a weekend, no proprietary stack. ValCtrl trips this directly — Hacker News item 47088680 ("Attena") is a Show HN where one developer already shipped natural-language search across Kalshi and Polymarket using Claude, around February 2026, before ValCtrl's YC batch even started. The "backtrace and quality labels" data-normalization claim in the YC description is the entire moat surface area, and it is the same surface area Polymarket just internalized by acquiring Dome (The Block, 2026). Compare this to **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) where the $115M Series A bought ARC magnet physics no garage team could replicate; ValCtrl's stack is the inverse — reproducible at LLM-API cost, with the venue incumbents already demonstrating willingness to absorb the layer.

Sarthak Garg's profile — Columbia undergrad, John Jay Scholar, SocGen IB, Grata — is on the founder-defiance axis what I would call earnest expert-continuity, not contrarianism. The dossier surfaces zero public position where either Garg or Paliwal contradicted prediction-market orthodoxy, no "instigator" framing of the kind I described in my TED Talk on the 12 climate instigators. Over 18 months this dimension compounds the wrong way for ValCtrl: belief-search is a category where credibility accrues to people who publicly take and defend forecasts (the **Physical Intelligence** Sergey Levine analog would be a forecaster with a public track record), and neither founder has shipped that artifact. The dimension does not flip without one of them publishing a controversial calibration record or a public dispute with Polymarket pricing.

The pivot from Analyst Copilot to Kassandre Search is the one place ValCtrl earns real points — this is the **Slash** (2024) shape, and I paid $100M for the Slash shape, so I take it seriously. For the learning-rate score to flip ValCtrl a tier higher, the dossier would need the pivot reasoning: what data killed Analyst Copilot, what observation about Polymarket fragmentation pulled them in, and ideally a third update already visible. Instead the valctrl.com homepage as of May 2026 still markets Analyst Copilot while the YC page markets Kassandre — that is not a clean pivot, that is two products running concurrently at a 2-person company, which reads as scattered focus rather than disciplined thesis update.

On capital profile, ValCtrl is **Glean** inverted on every axis — Glean violated my founder-defiance criterion but the capital scale and Fortune-500 information-asymmetry consequence were large enough to override. ValCtrl violates *consequence* and the capital plan is a $500K YC check into a 2-person team that needs roughly $5-10M to reach whatever take-rate revenue "gated bet/trade placement" produces. I am over-resourced for this deal by an order of magnitude — the Khosla Ventures $3.5B February 2025 vehicle (TechCrunch 2025-02-24) is built to lead $50M-$115M rounds where consensus VCs decline on capital risk. Here every Tier-1 generalist and every prediction-market-native angel can fund this.

My 36-month forecast: ValCtrl is acquired by Polymarket or Kalshi for under $25M in an acquihire-shaped transaction by mid-2028, or it pivots a third time into prediction-market trading tooling and disappears. The mechanism is the Dome precedent (The Block, 2026) — Polymarket has now demonstrated, with one prior transaction, that the aggregation/search layer gets internalized rather than allowed to become an independent equity outcome, which caps ValCtrl at a Dome-shaped exit regardless of execution.

The bull case that should sting: ValCtrl is the Slash pattern in a market that just 5x'd in seven months — a documented pivoter with YC pedigree riding a $5B-to-$24B monthly-volume wave (Pew Research, May 2026), and Slash itself violated my consequence-magnitude prior precisely because the learning-rate signal was strong enough to override. The dismantling is that Slash's pivot was *one* clean public thesis change (the Kanye story) into a single focused consumer-banking product; ValCtrl is running two product pages on two unrelated verticals concurrently in May 2026, and that is not a learning-rate signal, that is unresolved focus. Further, Slash's exit path was a consumer-banking customer base that no venue could acquihire away — ValCtrl's exit path runs straight through Polymarket's M&A team, which just rehearsed the playbook on Dome. The verdict flips if Garg and Paliwal kill valctrl.com/Analyst Copilot publicly and ship a Kassandre product with a documented forecaster user cohort and revenue from "gated bet/trade placement" within nine months — without that, I pass.
