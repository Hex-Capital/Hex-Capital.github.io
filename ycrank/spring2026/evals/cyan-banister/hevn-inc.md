# HEVN, inc -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Peter Volnov shipped HOT Wallet to 30 million users before walking into YC with HEVN, inc — and that's the dimensional signature for me. HEVN, inc is a company with a real invisible-system thesis (non-US founders gatekept from USD banking by EIN requirements and FX spreads on Google/Meta ad spend) sitting inside a consensus-attractive fintech category where Airwallex is already pulling $1.2B ARR at a $12B valuation. High on opacity-disruption, low on contrarian space — that pairing tells me what kind of bet HEVN, inc actually is.

The strongest dimension for HEVN, inc is the invisible-system test, and here's the ladder. Affirm is the high rung — Max Levchin attacked credit card opacity by making lending terms transparent, exposing a hidden financial extraction most consumers never saw. Carta is the mid rung — cap table opacity made visible, a real but narrower asymmetry. The low rung is any standard SaaS productivity tool with no opaque structure to break. HEVN, inc sits between Carta and Affirm: the "you must have a US EIN to hold USD" rule is a genuine information-asymmetry tax on non-US founders, and the 0.5% cashback plus zero-FX-fee positioning on USD ad spend names the extractive system explicitly. The dossier pins it there with the gethevn.com hero copy targeting non-US founders running USD ads — that's a hidden tax made visible, not invented.

The weakest dimension is contrarian conviction in hard spaces, and this is where HEVN, inc bleeds. Anduril is the high rung — defense tech when Silicon Valley wouldn't touch it, real social cost to taking the meeting. RTFKT is the mid rung from my own Known Exceptions — culturally weird at a narrow timing window, but I count that as an override, not a default pattern. The low rung is my Pass example: the AI wrapper in a consensus market with no courage required at the dinner party. HEVN, inc lands closer to the low rung than I'd like — Capital One is paying $5.15B for Brex, Mercury raised at $3.5B in March 2025 and applied for an OCC charter in December 2025, and Airwallex is pre-IPO at $1.2B ARR. This is consensus fintech. No VC shifts uncomfortably when you say "Brex for international companies" — they nod and ask about CAC.

On founder forge, the high rung is Substrate's James Proud, who I described as "forged in fire and came out unbroken"; the mid rung is Crusoe's infrastructure operators who lived inside the flaring problem in North Dakota; the low rung is the Pass-example "I was at Google and saw the opportunity." HEVN, inc places mid — Volnov, Briuzgin, and Zhevlakov co-built HERE Wallet (600K users) and HOT Wallet (30M users per Chainwire November 2024), which is real shipping muscle and likely firsthand experience of the "non-US founder can't open a USD account" pain. But this isn't the homeless-teen forge; it's a competent serial team executing on adjacent pain, and a fourth co-founder (Pavel Mitsevich, CMO) has zero retrievable public profile, which is its own quiet signal.

On infrastructure defensibility, the high rung is Flock Safety's compounding camera network; the mid rung is Postmates' partial route density; the low rung is HQ Trivia's pure-attention business with zero moat that I watched evaporate. HEVN, inc lands closer to HQ Trivia than Flock — the marquee "FDIC-insured USD account without EIN" is marked "soon" on gethevn.com, not live, which means the most novel hook depends on a sponsor-bank contract not yet signed. Airwallex already markets local accounts in 20+ currencies. On cascading impact, Uber expanded transportation beyond taxi-hailers and is the high rung; Depop democratizing resale is the mid rung; affluent-only enterprise SaaS is the low rung. HEVN, inc is mid — non-US SMBs gaining USD rails is real economic-access expansion, but the buyer is a founder running paid ads, not a vulnerable population.

My 36-month forecast for HEVN, inc: acqui-hire or feature acquisition by Airwallex, Payoneer, or Rho in the $30M–$100M range, contingent on shipping the no-EIN FDIC account live with a named US sponsor bank. Mechanism: the wedge is real and the team can ship product (30M HOT Wallet users proves that), but the regulated-banking moat belongs to whoever signs the sponsor-bank deal first, and Airwallex has the capital and compliance footprint to either out-bid or out-build HEVN, inc on the same primitive within 18 months.

The bull case stings, so let me steel-man it. Mercury's December 2025 OCC charter application and Capital One's Brex acquisition are both signals that incumbents are pulling further into US-regulated, US-centric charters — which structurally orphans non-US SMBs at exactly the moment USD-stablecoin rails (which HEVN, inc already advertises) make non-US USD movement cheap. That's the RTFKT-style narrow timing window: the acquirer landscape is identifiable (Airwallex, Payoneer, even Stripe), and the founders have shipped consumer-scale international payments before. But the bear case wins on dossier evidence: the differentiating feature is "soon," the team has zero US-bank-regulated operating history, three of four founders are concurrently associated with HOT Labs which Grit Daily reported still operating in 2024–2025, and AML/sanctions exposure for non-US KYB is exactly where this team's crypto-adjacent prior identity becomes a regulatory drag rather than an asset. The verdict flips to Invest if HEVN, inc ships the no-EIN FDIC USD account live with a named US sponsor bank and demonstrates $500K+ MRR within nine months — that proves the wedge is structural and the team can navigate US bank regulation. Until then, this is consensus fintech in a market where the $1B-ARR incumbent already builds what HEVN, inc promises.
