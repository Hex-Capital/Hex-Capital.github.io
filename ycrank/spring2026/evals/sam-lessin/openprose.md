# OpenProse -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Dan Barrett at OpenProse is building "an AI agent framework" with `*.prose.md` Markdown contracts — and that single phrase, framework + agent + Spring 2026 YC, is almost verbatim my Pass Example. The dimensional signature here is a modestly-above-default founder-conviction read (5 years agent dev, dogfooding 13 internal responsibilities, active @irl_danB build-in-public) paired with the worst-possible category-sleeve placement (foundation-model-adjacent AI infra, MIT, no monetization). That pairing — committed builder, terminally consensus surface — is exactly the profile where I cannot make money no matter how much I like the operator.

LangChain is the load-bearing analogy. OpenProse is pitching the same conceptual move LangChain made in 2022 — abstract the prompt-orchestration layer, win developer mindshare, monetize later — and LangChain executed it into a $1.25B valuation on $260M raised against roughly $16M ARR (TechCrunch, Oct 2025; Latka). The mechanism that worked for LangChain — be early enough to the abstraction that the category names itself after you — is exactly where OpenProse diverges: OpenProse is arriving in May 2026 with 1.3k GitHub stars into a category where LangChain has $260M of capital, CrewAI has $18M+ and ~$3.2M ARR, and Anthropic's Claude Code already exposes a native "skills" primitive that OpenProse literally installs through (`npx skills add openprose/prose`). The install command is the tell. OpenProse is not a runtime; it's a payload riding inside Anthropic's runtime, which means Anthropic ships the v1 of this idea natively and OpenProse becomes a tutorial.

My Pass Example fails on category-sleeve placement before anything else, and OpenProse fails on the same axis with even less ambiguity. "AI is just more internet… not disruptive. Crypto is fundamentally disruptive" (Lonsdale #113, 2024) is the sleeve allocation I actually run, and an MIT-licensed agent-orchestration framework at a Spring 2026 YC seed is the densest possible expression of the sleeve I down-weight. There is no Known Exception that rescues OpenProse here: Pinterest got the operator-channel override; Ro got the regulatory-moat override; Robinhood got the early-entry-price override. OpenProse has none of these — it has a generic OSS distribution motion in the most-funded category of the cycle.

The financing-structure read on OpenProse compounds badly over 18 months. MIT-licensed core + no pricing page + future-commercial-layer-TBD is the canonical OSS-to-SaaS template that requires four-to-five rounds of dilution before "default-alive" is even a question, and the regatta tacks in OpenProse's case all run through categories LangChain and CrewAI have already raised against. The Slow letter line — "capital efficiency and sound businesses on the road to profitability should be the guiding light" — inverts cleanly here: OpenProse is structurally a high-burn brand-and-mindshare race against incumbents with 20-to-100x more capital, and the financing instrument on offer is a standard SAFE that gives me no way to underwrite the brand asset directly the way I did with **Marina Mogilko**'s 30-year earnings instrument.

Counterfactual on the founder-conviction dimension: what would have to be true for OpenProse to clear the bar I cleared on **Sublime Security**? Dan Barrett would need a published opinionated essay corpus on why every other agent framework is structurally wrong — not product-update tweets at @irl_danB, but the kind of long-form writing where "writing is thinking" actually gets exercised. The dossier shows dogfooding (13 internal responsibilities) and shipping cadence (v0.14.0 by May 19 2026, 14 releases) — both real signals — but no public artifact of the contrarian belief that this Markdown-contract abstraction is right when LangGraph and CrewAI are wrong. Conviction-as-shipping is a weaker signal than conviction-as-published-argument, and at the price an AI-framework seed will clear in 2026, I need the stronger one.

The single-anchor parallel on founder-as-brand is **John Fish / Bookshelved** — the deal where I underwrote a creator's owned audience as the asset. OpenProse has 1.3k GitHub stars, 104 forks, and 7,000+ self-reported installs (openprose.ai, May 2026); that's a developer audience, which is the right shape of asset for this category, but it's roughly two orders of magnitude below where the **Bookshelved** thesis kicks in, and Dan Barrett's @irl_danB follower count is not even retrievable from public sources. Owned distribution is on the dossier; it just isn't loud enough yet to override the category-sleeve problem.

Forecast, 36 months: OpenProse is acqui-hired into LangChain, Anthropic, or a harness maintainer (OpenCode/Amp) for sub-$30M total consideration by May 2029, or quietly abandoned as the active repo, with the `*.prose.md` convention surviving as a community pattern absorbed into Claude Code's native skills format. The mechanism is straightforward: MIT licensing plus the `npx skills add` install path means Anthropic can adopt the contract surface without paying for it, and LangChain/CrewAI can ship a competing declarative surface inside their existing SDK at any time without cannibalizing a customer base OpenProse hasn't built yet.

Strongest bull case against that forecast: Markdown-as-contract becomes the de facto interop layer the same way Markdown itself did for documentation — OSS-led, harness-agnostic, and so cheap to adopt that even Anthropic ends up implementing the spec rather than inventing a parallel one. In that world OpenProse is the Hashicorp of agent contracts and 7K installs at month 0 is the foundation of a real standards-body play. The case stings because OSS-standard plays really do work when the abstraction is right. It loses to one dossier fact: OpenProse's own install path runs *through* Anthropic's skills primitive, which means Anthropic already owns the runtime surface and OpenProse owns a syntax convention on top of it — a syntax convention is not a venture-shaped asset, it's a pull request. Flip condition: if OpenProse closes a paid enterprise contract with a named F500 customer within 12 months, or if a non-Anthropic harness (Codex, Amp, Cline) adopts `*.prose.md` as a first-class native format without OpenProse runtime in the loop, the standards-body thesis becomes underwritable and I will revisit at the next tack.
