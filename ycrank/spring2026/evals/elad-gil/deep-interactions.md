# Deep Interactions -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Deep Interactions is a single-miracle company sitting in the wrong half of a market where the winner is already declared -- Sruthi Viswanathan's Oxford-HCI workflow layer is structurally clean (one validated capability, LLM app-gen, plus GTM execution), but it is shipping into a category where Lovable hit ~$400M ARR by Feb 2026 and raised $330M at $6.6B (TechCrunch, Dec 2025). That high/low pairing -- clean architecture, terrible distribution position -- is the analytical signature, and it is the same pairing I see on every "Harvey-clone" pitch I now reflexively pass on.

On index position -- my lowest-confidence dimension for Deep Interactions -- the ladder runs from **Stripe** (2010, an index on every e-commerce transaction by sitting in the workflow path) through **Decagon** (2023, sitting in the post-LLM customer support workflow path before a clear winner emerged) down to my **Lyft** Series C pass, where the company had to beat a named #1 in a winner-take-all market. Deep Interactions sits between Decagon and Lyft, much closer to Lyft: the dossier names Lovable, Bolt.new, v0 (rebranded v0.app Jan 2026), and Replit Agent as incumbents, and the "team collaboration" wedge is a product-extension surface any of them can build in a quarter. Ten SMB customers in fertility/mental-health/wellness (Jack Fertility, Canopie, FitMatrix) is not an index position -- it is a consultancy roster.

On single-miracle architecture -- my highest-confidence dimension for Deep Interactions -- the ladder runs from **Decagon** (2023, one miracle: LLMs accurate enough for tier-1 CS, rest is GTM) through **Mistral** (2023, foundation-model training capital-intensity but post-ChatGPT category catalyst) down to the fusion and novel-battery-chem pitches I categorically avoid for compounding miracle-stacking. Deep Interactions sits near Decagon: the team is not betting on a new model capability, they are wrapping existing frontier models with Slack/Gmail/GitHub/Vercel/Supabase connectors. The miracle has already happened. The problem is the miracle is also available to every competitor, so single-miracle cleanliness here doesn't translate into a moat -- it just means I can't kill the company on "science project" grounds the way I would with a chemistry bet.

Market re-opening catalyst is the dimension I always lead with, and Deep Interactions has the wrong shape of catalyst. **Anduril** (2022) had Ukraine; **Stripe** (2010) had developer APIs replacing merchant-account paperwork; the LLM-app-gen catalyst is real (Lovable $1M → $200M ARR in ~12 months per Sacra is the proof) but Deep Interactions is arriving 18 months *after* that catalyst has already been harvested by a $6.6B incumbent. This is the same temporal mistake I flag in *AI Market Clarity* -- entering a sub-category I already wrote up as having "current leaders." Closer to my Lyft-pass rung than to the Decagon rung.

Revenue velocity on Deep Interactions is the dimension where I get genuinely uncomfortable. **Harvey** (2023) gave me "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" -- that is what "just working" looks like in dollars. The dossier discloses zero revenue, no pricing page, a Calendly link and a Typeform waitlist as the entire funnel, and "10,000+ real-world usage instances" with no definition. Per my own Tim Ferriss #863 (April 2026) line -- if an AI company in 2026 isn't disclosing screaming growth, something is fundamentally broken or there is no growth. Sits near the bottom rung; the absent-evidence default applies and I am not generous with it in this cycle.

Founder shipping rhythm puts Deep Interactions in the middle band. **Perplexity** (2022) is the high rung -- Aravind built things between meetings off a cold LinkedIn ping. Sruthi has shipped (TheSpaceship.ai prior co-founder, Limbic AI research), her DPhil in Human-AI Interaction maps cleanly to "context capture from stakeholders," and Beni Ngwamah's WEKA founding is real. But the dossier names credentials (Oxford, Google UX, Naver Labs, Barclays IB) more than artifacts, and "Stealth Startup" on her LinkedIn headline at a company with 10 design partners reads as positioning theater rather than ship-velocity. Mid rung, not high.

My 36-month forecast: Deep Interactions plateaus as a 4-to-8-person AI-consultancy-with-platform in the wellness/femtech vertical, hits a services-revenue ceiling around $1-3M ARR, and is acqui-hired by Lovable or Replit by mid-2029 for talent at a sub-$30M outcome -- the mechanism being that the team-collaboration wedge gets shipped as a feature by a capitalized incumbent within 12 months, collapsing the differentiation while Deep Interactions's SMB consumer-health roster proves non-expandable to enterprise contract sizes.

The strongest case against this forecast is the **Decagon** (2023) precedent: I funded a workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-LLM bet in a category I had publicly flagged as "still wide open" in *AI Market Clarity*, and the bear argument at the time was identical -- "any LLM provider will ship this as a feature." The Deep Interactions bull case says Lovable is fundamentally single-user vibe-coding, the team-collaboration-with-stakeholder-context-capture layer is a genuinely different product, and Sruthi's HCI research depth (the Oxford DPhil isn't decorative -- it's literally the discipline that studies the captured-stakeholder-context problem) is real founder-market fit. That argument stings. What kills it is the dossier: a services-delivery motion ("you give us a problem and we ship a working AI product in the same afternoon"), zero disclosed revenue in a cycle where real growth is trivial to disclose, and customer concentration in three early-stage consumer-health startups likely sourced from the founder's Limbic AI network. The flip condition is specific: if Deep Interactions surfaces >$500K ARR growing >20% MoM with a named enterprise logo outside the femtech/mental-health cluster within 6 months, I revisit -- because at that point the "team collaboration" wedge has demonstrated buying behavior Lovable doesn't have. Until then, this is a pass.
