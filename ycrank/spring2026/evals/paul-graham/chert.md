# Chert -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Two undergraduates building B2B outbound on a channel Apple has no business API for — that's the sentence that should make me lean in. Chert is running iMessage agents into customers' phones, which means Mac relays, ToS ambiguity, and exactly the kind of unglamorous infrastructure that repels Salesforce and Outreach from ever shipping it natively. That part of Chert I like. The part I don't like is that Ian Fong and Gary Gao are sophomores who, as far as the dossier shows, have never been SDRs, never run a sales team, and never felt the specific pain their product addresses. They picked AI SDR — the most consensus category in B2B AI right now — from somewhere outside their own lived experience.

Ian Fong's "8x hackathon winner" line and Gary Gao's circuits-lab research at Penn tell me both Chert founders can ship. Hackathon kids are exactly the demographic I keep funding — Bindwell teenagers, Patrick at Auctomatic, the Replit team I found on Hacker News. So the building muscle is there. What concerns me is Fong's "Incoming @ JPMC" headline on LinkedIn. A founder who is also onboarding to JPMorgan is sending mixed signals about whether Chert is the company or the side bet. I want my founders to have already burned the boats. Gao seems all-in (gary@trychert.com, the personal site, the YC P26 byline); Fong's status flag tells me something I'd want to ask him about in a 10-minute interview, and the dossier can't answer it.

Chert's origin story is the most important thing missing from this dossier, and its absence is informative. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick. The Octopart founders couldn't find a capacitor. The Stripe brothers had wired payments themselves and hated the schlep. Chert's founders are undergrads selling to companies like Whop and Databricks-adjacent buyers — they are not the future user, they're building for a customer segment they don't belong to. The idea reads as if it came from observing that "11x raised $74M, email is dead, what other channel is there?" rather than from frustration. The way to get startup ideas is not to try to think of startup ideas, and AI SDR is the canonical example of a category people brainstormed their way into.

Chert's competitive landscape is the bear case in one paragraph. 11x.ai raised $74M from Benchmark and a16z, hit roughly $10M ARR by September 2024, and then reportedly lost 70-80% of customers within months. That is not a category churn problem you fix with a different channel — it's a problem where the AI doesn't actually book meetings real humans want to take. Sendblue (also YC) and Project Blue already sell the iMessage primitives, which means Chert's channel moat is one Artisan integration away from disappearing. The defensibility, as the memo correctly notes, has to come from lead-sourcing accuracy on verified phone numbers and deliverability — not from iMessage itself. Nothing in the dossier shows Chert has built that data moat yet.

The bull case I'd construct for Chert: the logos are unusually strong for a P26 pre-seed — Whop and Autosana are real YC companies, and testimonials reference leaders at Ramp and Databricks. Hackathon-winner-plus-research-kid is a profile I've funded before. iMessage genuinely is schlep-y in a way that creates a window. The "10x response rates" claim, even discounted heavily for it being self-reported, points at something real: people read iMessages. If Chert can convert that channel premium into retention where 11x couldn't, the upside is meaningful. And these founders are sophomores — the slope of their learning curve over the next two years matters more than where they are today.

Where I land: this is closer to the Reddit pattern than to Stripe — I'd be funding the founders, not the idea, and the idea would need to mutate. The difference is that with Alexis and Steve, the idea was disposable because they had the running-back quality and I could redirect them. With Chert, I don't have direct evidence of that quality; I have hackathon wins (which select for sprints, not pivots) and one founder who hasn't fully committed. I'd want a 10-minute working session with Fong specifically to test whether JPMC is a signal or a fallback.

The condition that flips Chert to Invest for me: Fong publicly declines the JPMC internship and Chert shows me one month of cohort retention on three of those named logos — Whop, Autosana, plus one more — at a price point that implies $20K+ MRR. That would tell me both founders are all-in and that the channel premium converts to durable revenue rather than the 11x decay curve. Until then, the AI SDR category consensus, the absence of an organic origin story, and the half-committed co-founder are too much to overcome on hackathon credentials alone.

The two dimensions doing the work here are Organic Problem Discovery — Chert's founders don't appear to have lived this problem — and Idea That Repels, where iMessage is genuinely schlep-y but AI SDR overall is the opposite of contrarian. Resourcefulness is propped up by 8x hackathon wins and the logo list, but those are general-purpose builder signals, not sales-domain ones.
