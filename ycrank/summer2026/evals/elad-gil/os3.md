# OS3 -- Elad Gil Evaluation

OS3 is a catalyst-adjacent hardware startup whose highest dimension is a real market re-opening in humanoid robotics and whose lowest is a completely silent revenue curve -- and that pairing, more than any single fact in the dossier, tells me what kind of company OS3 is: a science-project bet dressed as an infrastructure play, where Rishabh Chanana and Chris Hailey are trying to ride a Goldman-revised TAM curve ($38B by 2035) with a two-person team, an in-house custom semi-humanoid, and zero disclosed customers beyond "already deploying in hospitality."

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogue for OS3's strongest dimension. Ukraine was a specific 24-month catalyst that re-priced Pentagon software; for OS3, Goldman's 6x TAM revision citing video-based policy learning crossing a usability threshold, combined with Apptronik closing $520M at $5B post in Feb 2026, is a legitimate catalyst signal that the humanoid category re-opened. But OS3 diverges from the Anduril template on the mechanism that actually mattered: Anduril entered as *software on top of existing autonomy hardware pathways*, isolating the miracle to distribution. OS3 is designing custom "semi-humanoid" hardware "in our SF lab, not sourced" (os3robotics.com, May 2026) *and* training policies from egocentric human video *and* selling into a regulatorily-untested hospitality channel. The catalyst is real; OS3's position inside the catalyst window is not the Anduril-shaped one.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure mechanism I keep circling back to for OS3's index-position dimension: a #2 in a market with structural capital asymmetry captures a fraction of #1's economics, and no dossier evidence overrides that math. OS3 faces Figure at $39B valuation, Apptronik at $5B post, Physical Intelligence at $2B pre-product, Unitree G1 shipping at commodity prices, and the same Apptronik already publicly targeting hospitality as one of its verticals ([CNBC, Feb 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/11/apptronik-raises-520-million-at-5-billion-valuation-for-apollo-robot.html)). OS3 does not sit in the workflow path that every entrant *must* touch -- OS3 must *beat* named better-capitalized entrants on hardware, model, and hospitality GTM simultaneously. That is the exact anti-pattern from my *AI Market Clarity* piece: this isn't a "wide open" sub-market like accounting or sales agents, this is a category where the winners are already declared in dollars.

Revenue velocity for OS3 fails the "just working" test the way most pre-transformer ML startups did -- silence. YC page shows "already deploying in hospitality" with no venue names, no per-unit pricing, no MRR, no per-shift utilization figures, and zero open roles despite scope requiring mechanical, electrical, ML, and BD hires (YC page, 2026). Over 18 months, the mechanism compounds badly: hospitality operators are notoriously slow-paying, food-safety liability drags deployment cycles, and every month OS3 doesn't publish a named venue is a month Apptronik or a Unitree-based integrator eats the pilot slot. In the AI cycle, silence on growth *is* the signal.

Chanana and Hailey do not clear the founder-shipping bar in the way that would flip my score a tier. What would have to be true for OS3 to score in the top rung here: Chanana's UCSD background would need to include a named robotics artifact deployed at scale (a Waymo perception stack, a Skydio autonomy module) rather than a 2024 mobile-notifications ML patent; Hailey's @chaileyUSC GitHub would need to show robotics repos with real stargazer signal rather than 0 stars across 6 IoT coursework repos. Neither exists. The founders are technically credible engineers; they are not the Aravind-Srinivas-ships-between-meetings archetype and they are not the Trae-Stephens-forward-deployed archetype. This is the disqualifier: not pedigree, but the absence of prior independent shipping artifacts at the scope OS3 requires.

**Color Genomics** (2015) is the single-miracle parallel that makes OS3's architecture look worse than it first appears. I structured Color as workflow infrastructure on top of *commodity* sequencing -- one miracle, isolated to GTM. OS3 stacks four independent miracles: (1) the embodiment gap between internet human video and OS3's proprietary semi-humanoid morphology gets closed, (2) custom in-house hardware achieves reliability in a wet, chaotic hospitality environment, (3) health-code and liability regimes accept mobile manipulators in customer-facing venues, (4) two founders out-execute Figure, Apptronik, and Physical Intelligence on GTM. Compound those probabilities and the math doesn't pencil, which is exactly why I've largely avoided this kind of dossier.

My 36-month forecast for OS3: acqui-hire by a foundation-model-for-robots player (Physical Intelligence or Skild tier) at sub-$30M by mid-2029, with the "asset" being the egocentric hospitality-deployment video dataset rather than the hardware or the policies. The mechanism is that OS3's custom hardware burn will outpace hospitality contract revenue within 18 months, the two-person team can't hire fast enough on YC pre-seed dollars to close capital-asymmetry with Apptronik, and the most valuable thing OS3 will have created is task-labeled manipulation data that a $2B-post foundation-model lab will absorb cheaply.

The bull case that stings: hospitality labor shortages are structural, the "semi-humanoid" scope reduction genuinely dodges the bipedal-locomotion research bottleneck that consumes Figure and 1X's capital, and Chanana/Hailey collecting proprietary teleoperation data from live venues could compound into a task-specific policy dataset no full-humanoid lab bothers to build. This is the Anduril-exception shape: workflow infrastructure on top of a hard-tech layer, with the miracle isolated to data collection rather than chemistry. But OS3 fails the exception's prerequisite -- Trae Stephens had shipped Palantir forward-deployed at scale before I wrote the Series D check; OS3's founders have a mobile-notifications patent and a USC IoT GitHub. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: OS3 publishes three named hospitality venue deployments with per-unit utilization data and a signed enterprise MSA with a top-20 hotel chain within nine months. Absent that, this is the exact "capitalization + science + market risk" triple I told Tim Ferriss I stay out of.
