# RealPact -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Ranvir Deshmukh and Erik Peterson built CourseMe to 90% penetration of Dartmouth undergraduates and exited it, and now RealPact is the workflow-SaaS sequel pointed at New Hampshire brokerages — which makes this the Asana archetype without the Facebook-scale override. The highest-loading dimension is operator lineage at student scale; the lowest is the contrarian secret, because RealPact's own description — "AI-native OS for Real Estate Brokerages" — is consensus 2026 YC framing, the exact phrase pattern I was written against. The signature is a credentialed-but-not-yet-credentialed pair shipping into a category where Dotloop already owns roughly half of US transaction management. That is not a power-law shape.

Stripe (2010) is the HIGH rung on contrarian secret — Patrick Collison named that legacy processors could not ship a developer-first API because their organizations were optimized for merchant-acquirer sales, a specific architectural blind spot at a specific incumbent. RealPact does not name an analogous failure mode in Dotloop or SkySlope; the dossier shows commoditization risk explicitly — "Public-records scraping plus LLM contract autofill is buildable by Dotloop, SkySlope, Brokermint, DocuSign" — which is the inverse of a secret. The Stripe analogy fails not because RealPact lacks ambition but because Zillow Group, which owns Dotloop and ~50% of US transaction-mgmt distribution, has every incentive and capability to ship AI auto-fill on top of its installed base. A secret has to terminate at a reason the incumbent structurally cannot ship it. RealPact's dossier supplies no such reason.

The pass example I wrote — "AI-powered platform for [vertical X]" with no named architectural blind spot — is the LOW rung on monopoly trajectory, and RealPact sits adjacent to it. The mechanism that kills these companies is not execution; it is that the moat hypothesis terminates at "we have a better model," and within nine months the incumbent with distribution ships an adequate version. RealPact's named customers — Four Seasons Sotheby's, Black House Real Estate, two additional NH brokerages — are four design partners in one state, not a network whose density compounds against Dotloop's 50% share. Switching costs from "contracts/compliance audit trails residing in RealPact" are real but symmetric: the same lock-in works in Dotloop's favor for the agents already there. No pricing-power hypothesis at five-year horizon survives this.

On definite plan, the dossier surfaces no multi-year technical milestone Ranvir Deshmukh commits not to pivot from — only a product surface (Deed Agent, Tax & Vision Agent, Property Agent, Transaction Agent) and a New Hampshire beachhead. Over 18 months this compounds in the wrong direction: each new state requires re-mapping county-registry schemas and state-specific contract forms, which is schlep work that does not become a moat because Dotloop can hire the same schlep at 100x the headcount. Indefinite-optimism iteration in a category with an entrenched incumbent is the canonical 2011-manifesto failure mode.

Founder lineage is where RealPact earns its only real points. CourseMe being acquired with 90% campus penetration is a non-trivial shipped-to-scale signal for undergraduates — the closest portfolio parallel is Dustin Moskovitz at **Asana** (2010), where the operator-pedigree override carried a company through a missing structural moat. But Moskovitz had shipped Facebook to global scale before Asana; Deshmukh and Peterson shipped a course advisor at one Ivy. The Asana exception is a real precedent, not a fake one, but the magnitude of the prior outcome is two or three orders below what the exception historically required.

For atoms-heavy or regulated-sector moat, the counterfactual to flip a tier would require RealPact to be the system-of-record for state-level real estate compliance with regulatory certification depth — something like a multi-state e-recording integration that takes three years and a legal team to build. The dossier shows none of that: pure software, public-records scraping, no patents, no regulatory clause. The realpact.ai footer reading "SignPact, Inc." next to "RealPact" branding is also a minor signal that the entity itself is still finding its name. Atoms-light, software-only, indefinite — the YC batch default.

My 36-month forecast: RealPact reaches $1-3M ARR concentrated in New England regional brokerages and is acqui-hired by Dotloop (Zillow Group) or SkySlope in the $10-30M range as a feature tuck-in once those incumbents ship competing AI auto-fill. The mechanism is straightforward — Dotloop's ~50% distribution share converts any RealPact feature into a Dotloop roadmap item within a year, and the founders' Dartmouth credentials make them attractive talent the acquirer wants more than the standalone company.

The strongest objection is the Asana exception itself. Moskovitz had Facebook; Deshmukh and Peterson have CourseMe — but CourseMe was acquired, the campus penetration was 90%, and Magnuson committee plus LeapYear Fund chose them. The bull case is that the operator-pedigree override scales down proportionally: at pre-seed, "shipped a product to acquisition with 90% market penetration of your reachable market" is the under-23 analogue to Moskovitz, and the contrarian-credentialed-outsider pattern (Dartmouth dropouts shipping into real estate while their cohort goes to Anthropic) is exactly the signal the Thiel Fellowship was built to catch. I dismantle this with the dossier itself: Asana's missing-moat override required a Facebook-magnitude prior, not a campus-magnitude prior, and the named-incumbent gate — Dotloop owns half the market and can ship the same product — is the gate that even Moskovitz would have had to clear, and did, because workflow SaaS in 2010 had no entrenched incumbent. RealPact in 2026 does. The verifiable condition that flips the verdict: RealPact signs a top-3 national brokerage (Compass, Keller Williams, or RE/MAX) as an integrated platform customer within 12 months. That is a distribution event Dotloop cannot replicate by shipping a feature, and it would prove the secret I do not currently see. Until then, pass.
