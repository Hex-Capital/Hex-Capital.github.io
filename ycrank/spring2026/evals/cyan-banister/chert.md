# Chert -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Chert is selling AI agents that spam strangers' personal iMessage threads to book B2B sales meetings, and that opening sentence is the entire problem. iMessage is, as Chert's own site admits, "a trusted, personal channel" (trychert.com) ? and the founders' wedge is to convert that trust into outbound capacity for SDR teams. My whole framework is built on backing companies that make extractive, opaque systems transparent. Chert does the inverse: it takes a transparent, intimate channel and quietly turns it into a new extraction surface. That's not an "invisible system made visible" ? that's a fresh invisible tax being installed on the rest of us.

Ian Fong and Gary Gao have impressive student-mode credentials ? Fong is an 8x hackathon winner heading to a JPMC internship, Gao is a Penn/Wharton sophomore who did real research in Marc Miskin's circuits lab on oscillator-based learning networks (curf.upenn.edu). I respect both of those resumes. But when I run my opening question on Chert ? tell me your story, not the business ? I cannot find a biographical reason why these two specific people MUST solve B2B outbound saturation. Neither has carried a quota. Neither has run an SDR team. Neither has the IronPort-style scar tissue of having lived inside email deliverability hell. Chert reads like two very talented kids who scanned the YC consensus market for a clever channel angle and shipped fast. That's career optimization, not the forge.

The 11x.ai precedent in Chert's own dossier is the bear case I cannot wave away. 11x raised $74M from Benchmark and a16z, hit ~$10M ARR by September 2024, then reportedly lost 70?80% of customers within months (TechCrunch via search snippet). That is the AI-SDR cohort fragility pattern in neon lights. The buyers churn because the agents don't actually book qualified pipeline at the rate the demo promised. Chert's "10? response rates" and "127 leads sourced in a sample week" are company-stated numbers from their own marketing page; Gary's personal site claims "75% reply rates and 90% actions" (garyzgao.com). I have seen exactly this shape of metric ? unverified, founder-published, no third-party cohort data ? on companies in my own avoidance set. It rhymes with the IRL fabricated-metric failure mode, even when the founders are honest, because the underlying channel novelty inflates early numbers that don't survive contact with scaled, repeated outreach.

Chert's defensibility story is the part that genuinely worries me as an infrastructure investor. Sendblue (a YC company itself) and Project Blue already sell the iMessage messaging primitives as APIs. Apple has no official business iMessage API, which means every operator in this space is running Mac-relay infrastructure against ambiguous terms of service ? not a moat, a shared liability. Artisan, AiSDR, and 11x can plug Sendblue into their stacks in a quarter. The only durable Chert wedge would be a proprietary verified-phone-number lead database, and the dossier offers no evidence that Chert has built one beyond what campaigns naturally accrete. Pair that with Apple's ability to disable the channel with a single policy update and you have the opposite of Crusoe Energy ? Crusoe owned physical infrastructure on stranded gas fields nobody else would touch; Chert rents a channel Apple can revoke on a Tuesday.

The strongest bull case for Chert is the one I want to argue out loud and then dismantle. Channel arbitrage is real. Email and LinkedIn outbound are saturated; that's why the AI-SDR backlash is happening. iMessage genuinely converts higher in the narrow window before consumers learn to treat blue bubbles like spam. Two scrappy undergrads shipping a managed service to YC-batch peers ? Whop, Tour, Symbal ? is exactly the kind of distribution loop that builds a fast first $1?2M of ARR. There's even a Known Exception precedent: RTFKT was a consumer hype bet I made with no infrastructure moat because the timing window was narrow and the acquirer landscape was identifiable. Could Chert be acquired by Outreach or Salesloft for the iMessage capability before the channel saturates? Maybe. But RTFKT had genuine cultural weirdness and an artist-founder who lived inside digital fashion. Chert has channel novelty and two undergraduates ? the cultural prescience axis isn't there. The override doesn't apply.

Chert fails my contrarian test in a subtle way that matters. Defense AI made VCs squirm. Male birth control made VCs squirm. AI SDR with an iMessage twist makes VCs nod and ask about MRR ? it sits squarely in a consensus-attractive vertical (B2B sales tooling) with a slightly unusual channel choice. That's differentiation, not contrarianism. And the cascading real-world impact dimension lands where Postmates and Wonderschool didn't ? Chert's success expands SDR productivity for B2B sellers, with second-order effects that mostly fall on consumers receiving more unsolicited blue bubbles. There's no new economic participant being created here, no gatekept resource being opened up.

The condition that would flip me to Invest is concrete: show me one of these founders has the biographical scar to be in this space ? a prior company they ran, a quota they carried, a deliverability war they survived ? AND show me a proprietary lead-data asset that doesn't exist at Sendblue or 11x. Absent both, Chert is a clever wedge play in a category that has already publicly chewed up a $74M-funded leader. The dimensions that lock my verdict are Founder's Biographical Forge (no evidence either founder was forged for this problem ? both are bright generalists) and Invisible System Made Visible (Chert is on the wrong side of this test ? it's installing opacity into iMessage rather than removing it from somewhere else). I wish Ian and Gary well; they will build something interesting in their careers. This isn't the Long Journey bet.
