# flowscope -- Elad Gil Evaluation

flowscope is a two-person AI-native consulting firm out of YC Spring 2026 whose dimensional signature is the worst pairing in my taxonomy: a generic catalyst with no index position. Javier Leguina and Samuel Mirpuri are pitching what the dossier explicitly calls "AI-native consulting" -- agents shadow employees for ~2 weeks, then ship automations -- which is engagement-throughput economics dressed in agent vocabulary, fighting Adept ($415M raised), 11x ($76M), Cresta ($276M, $52M ARR), UiPath, *and* QuantumBlack on the same surface. That's not a re-opening trade; that's a services firm in a crowded category.

flowscope's highest-rung comparison on catalyst is **Decagon** (2023), and the load-bearing mechanism is identical: LLM agent reliability crossed a usable threshold in 2024-2025 (function calling, computer-use models), which the dossier names as the "why now." But Decagon picked one workflow surface (customer support) where the agent miracle isolates cleanly and the buyer is already shopping. flowscope's dossier names "finance, admin, and ops processes" as the surface -- which is to say, no surface. The catalyst is real but it has already been priced into every horizontal agent platform and every Big-4 GenAI practice; this is the generic "LLM got good" catalyst, not the specific Ukraine-revealed-Pentagon-software-inadequacy that made Anduril (2022) penciled at $4.6B post.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure mechanism that haunts flowscope on the index dimension: I passed on Lyft because being #2 in a network-effect market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and flowscope is structurally worse -- it's #15 in a market with no network effects at all. The dossier lists Adept, 11x.ai, Cresta, UiPath, Distyl AI as competitive overlap, and explicitly names McKinsey/QuantumBlack and Accenture as encroaching. flowscope does not sit in the workflow path the way Stripe sits in payments rails; flowscope sits *next to* the workflow, billing for the engagement that maps it. That's a services P&L. The dossier confirms "no defensibility signals found in public sources beyond the process-mapping data captured during 2-week shadowing engagements" -- which is the inverse of an index position.

On revenue velocity, flowscope's dossier shows the silence I treat as a negative signal in the AI cycle. No revenue, no customer logos, no LOIs, no Product Hunt launch, no press, no Discord, hiring=False. I'm not penalizing pre-seed stage -- I'm noting that *real* growth is easy to disclose, and the comparable wedge plays (Decagon, Abridge in 2023, both of which I funded) had named design partners by YC Demo Day. The mechanism risk over the next 18 months: services revenue compounds linearly with headcount, and a two-person team that takes two weeks per engagement has a throughput ceiling that doesn't bend until a product ships. By month 12, flowscope either has a repeatable template library or it has the gross margin of a boutique.

Founder shipping rhythm is where flowscope splits in half. Leguina was Founding Engineer at Model ML (YC W24) and ML Engineer at Encord (YC W21) -- that's real shipping pedigree inside two YC companies, and his GitHub @jleguina has 8 repos. Mirpuri is QuantumBlack/McKinsey + Harvard MBA + MIT + Imperial with no GitHub and no LinkedIn flowscope linkage retrievable. That's the *exact* archetype my framework flags -- "has this founder actually shipped products before, not just at a big company" -- and McKinsey consulting is the canonical "not really." The counterfactual for this dimension flipping a tier: if Mirpuri had been a forward-deployed engineer at Palantir rather than a strategy consultant at QuantumBlack, this becomes a Palantir-pattern services-to-platform bet. He wasn't.

The single-miracle test is the one place flowscope earns marks. Compare to **Color Genomics** (2015), which I co-founded as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing -- the miracle was assumed (sequencing works), the work was GTM. flowscope is structured the same way: the miracle is "agents reliably execute on existing enterprise systems," everything else is sales and delivery. That's one miracle, not three. The problem is that the same miracle is being bet by Adept's $415M, 11x's $76M, Cresta's $276M, and every Distyl-pattern boutique -- when the miracle is shared, the moat has to come from somewhere flowscope's dossier doesn't show.

My 36-month forecast: flowscope reaches $2-6M in services revenue, fails to productize the process-template library into a self-serve SKU, and is either acqui-hired into Accenture/Deloitte's GenAI delivery practice or pivots into a vertical CFO-ops SaaS wedge at sub-Series-A scale. The mechanism is straightforward: consulting revenue caps at human throughput, the named competitors have 10-100x capitalization to commoditize the underlying agent layer, and Mirpuri's McKinsey network buys *engagements*, not equity-bearing platform deals.

The strongest objection is Palantir-shaped: forward-deployed consulting *did* become a $50B+ company, and Distyl AI is running this exact playbook with a reportedly nine-figure valuation today. Mirpuri's QuantumBlack rolodex into CFOs and PE operating partners is a genuine distribution wedge that pure agent platforms like Adept don't have, and the dossier's "ship working software not decks" framing against McKinsey is a real cycle-time advantage. The dismantle: Palantir owned Foundry as the data layer underneath the forward-deployed work, and Distyl has a named OpenAI partnership plus federal-vertical specificity -- flowscope's dossier shows neither a productization roadmap nor a vertical wedge, just "finance, admin, and ops" as undifferentiated surface. The flip condition is verifiable: if flowscope ships a productized process-mapping SKU adopted by three or more customers without bespoke engagement inside 12 months, this becomes an index bet and I revisit. Until then, flowscope is a Lyft-shaped pass with consulting-firm economics, not an Anduril-shaped re-opening.
