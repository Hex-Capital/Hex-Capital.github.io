# Nebula Security -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Nebula Security is a boutique offensive-research shop dressed as a platform play — Eten Zou's team ships elite CVE output (Chrome V8 sandbox escape CVE-2026-6307, Linux kernel CVE-2026-23274 with a $10,500 bounty), but the success-state at maximum ambition is "better Snyk," not a civilizational variable moved. That high-on-craft, low-on-consequence pairing is Nebula Security's signature, and it is the pairing I have passed on for twenty years.

**OpenAI (2019)** is the HIGH rung on consequence magnitude because the success-state redrew what human cognitive labor is worth — my Fortune 2026-03-06 framing of "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" being on the table. Nebula Security's success-state, by contrast, is displacing Snyk ($8.5B valuation, $1.32B raised per Tracxn 2026) and Semgrep ($196M raised) inside a SAST category the dossier itself sizes with "no public TAM/SAM data found." The mechanism I care about — an incumbent regime that disappears — is absent; the mechanism Nebula Security offers is 20-30% deeper bug discovery than pattern-based scanners plus a premium "Audited by Nebula Security" trust mark. That is the exact optimization-play shape my Outlook Business essay warned against: nibbling at margin inside an existing workflow that Palo Alto or GitHub Advanced Security ships as a feature in twelve months. No civilizational variable moves whether Nebula Security wins or loses.

On my LOWEST-scoring dimension, Required-Capital Profile, the failure mechanism is the one I named passing on the "GPT-4 summarizes sales calls" YC pitch: any Tier-1 generalist funds this at $500K, which means I am structurally over-resourced. Nebula Security's YC S26 $500K in USDC is the entire disclosed capital plan; there is no fleet, no plant, no foundation-model training run, no drug pipeline that would require Khosla check depth. The dossier lists Team Size 2 against XBOW's Copilot-alumni engineering bench and $237M raised — meaning if capital ever becomes the bar, Nebula Security loses on the axis where I am supposed to have an edge. This is the DoorDash exception profile without the DoorDash consequence.

Technical risk is where Nebula Security scores best — and it still does not clear my bar. The Chrome V8 sandbox escape and the V8 RCE CVE-2026-5865 are real offensive-research artifacts, not marketing; over 18 months, a proprietary corpus of exploitation traces from VEGA scans could compound into a data moat, which is the only mechanism by which Nebula Security escapes the SAST commodity gravity well. But compounding requires scan volume and paying customers, and the dossier shows "$0K+ in bug bounties earned" as the site counter, 87 LinkedIn followers, and no pricing page. The technical miracle is falsifiable and partially demonstrated; the deployment miracle has not started.

On founder defiance of expert consensus, the counterfactual to flip Nebula Security a tier is narrow and specific: Eten Zou would need to be independently, publicly credited as sole discoverer of the V8 sandbox escape — the way Sergey Levine's name carries at **Physical Intelligence** (2024). Instead, the dossier flags that "first nginx RCE" appears credited to "depthfirst" per cybersecuritynews.com, not to Nebula Security's team. That is not a founder defying expert consensus; that is an unverified attribution in the YC blurb, which by my TED Talk skepticism of "experts extrapolating the past" I must discount, not credit.

Founder learning rate on Nebula Security is a data vacuum — no LinkedIn, no GitHub, no Twitter for Eten Zou, and the second co-founder is unnamed on the YC page. Compared to **Slash** (2024), where the documented Kanye West pivot justified my $100M Series C because the founder had publicly updated a thesis in real time, Nebula Security offers no pivot, no prior exit, no public thesis change I can weigh. I apply the absent-evidence default and move on — this is not a penalty, it is stage-appropriate silence.

My 36-month forecast: Nebula Security is acqui-hired by Snyk, Palo Alto Networks, or Semgrep in the $20-60M range as a talent-plus-brand pickup for the offensive-research team, with Eten Zou taking a Director of Offensive Research title, before crossing $3M ARR as a standalone. The mechanism is straightforward — the CVE output is real enough to command a talent premium, the consequence is small enough that no strategic acquirer needs to pay platform money, and XBOW's $237M war chest (Bloomberg March 2026; GeekWire May 2026) sets the ceiling on standalone AI-security valuations that Nebula Security's two-person team cannot reach.

The strongest opposing case is the **Glean** exception: Arvind Jain was a paradigmatic ex-Google domain expert, violating my "founder defies experts" criterion, and I still wrote the check because capital scale and consequence on Fortune-500 information asymmetry justified the credential-scale override. A bull could argue Nebula Security follows the same pattern — real offensive-security credentials, real CVE output, and AI-generated code volume creating a genuine "$15T labor" adjacency in security engineering. That case stings because the V8 discoveries are not cosmetic. It fails on two facts. Glean's consequence variable was Fortune-500 knowledge work; Nebula Security's is the AppSec buyer inside a category XBOW and Semgrep already own with 400x the capital. And Glean's founder credentials were independently verifiable at Google; Nebula Security's YC-credited nginx RCE traces to a different researcher named "depthfirst." I flip to Invest on one condition: a named Fortune-100 customer contract disclosed at six figures or an independently attributed Tier-1 zero-day credited by name to Eten Zou. Absent that, this is the pass I described in my Pass Example, dressed in better exploit craft.
