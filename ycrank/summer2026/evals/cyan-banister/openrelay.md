# OpenRelay -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Prashant Patel was a founding member of Amazon Bedrock managing inference for trillion-parameter models, and Jaden Wang was Lead Engineer at TensorDock before Voltage Park bought it in March 2025 — so OpenRelay is one of those rare pre-seed dossiers where the founders actually *lived* the exact seam they're now trying to arbitrage. That earns a real look. But the lane they've chosen — "CDN of inference" against RunPod at $240M ARR, Together at ~$1B, Fireworks at ~$800M, and Parasail's $32M Series A in April 2026 — is the loudest, most-VC-chased corner of the AI batch. OpenRelay is the shape of company where the biography passes my first test and the *category* fails my second.

Anduril-Luckey (2017) is the HIGH rung of my founder-origin ladder because Palmer had already shipped Oculus at scale from his parents' garage before he ever thought about defense — the founder was of the domain, not visiting it. OpenRelay's Patel-plus-Wang combination genuinely rhymes with that mechanism: one built the hyperscaler inference layer that the "90% cheaper than AWS, Azure, GCP" pitch is priced against, the other built the GPU marketplace that the aggregator model is copying. Where OpenRelay diverges from the Anduril rung is that Luckey's Oculus was a tactile artifact he could hand to a Marine, whereas Patel's Bedrock work is invisible plumbing, and the origin story here is *career-adjacent* rather than *autobiographical* — no scar, no place-of-origin, no roughneck-pad social license. That's why OpenRelay clears the founder bar in the mid-20s and not the high-20s.

The Pass Example I already wrote — "consensus YC AI-agent darling, two ex-FAANG PMs, $20M seed round oversubscribed" — is the LOW rung of my magically-weird ladder, and its failure mechanism is *category convergence*: when six tier-one funds are chasing the same shape, price destroys any founder-driven return. OpenRelay does not have six funds chasing (that we know of), but the dossier lists *five* named incumbents with a combined ARR north of $2.7B, plus Parasail, Prime Intellect, Vast.ai, and TensorDock all crowding the aggregator lane. This isn't magically weird — it's the most-forecasted YC 2026 category, the one Brad Flora is probably placing a dozen bets in. "Find them before it becomes consensus" (Yahoo Finance, March 2025) — OpenRelay arrived *after* consensus.

The CEO dimension is a compounding governance risk: OpenRelay is a two-person team out of the same prior employer, with no named CEO, no disclosed equity split, and no tiebreaker protocol in any public source. Over eighteen months, that ambiguity typically detonates precisely when the pivot conversation arrives — and in a contested aggregator lane where RunPod just raised $100M at a $1B valuation from Summit Partners in June 2026, the pivot conversation *will* arrive. I've watched the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll dynamic play out; the absent-evidence default is doing real work here, not a bookkeeping shrug.

For OpenRelay to be a capital-intensive frontier bet in my sense, the score would have to flip on the founders actually *owning* something — a fleet, a datacenter footprint, a signed multi-year power contract, a regulated license. Instead, OpenRelay is explicitly asset-light: aggregates third-party GPU capacity, rents by the hour, ships a Docker deploy under two minutes. The counterfactual that flips this tier is a dossier line that reads "OpenRelay has signed 40MW of stranded-power supply with three named North Dakota operators" — the Crusoe (~2018) shape. Absent that, OpenRelay is a routing layer, not a frontier-hardware swing, and the moat has to come from software the incumbents can copy.

Postmates (2011) is my single-anchor parallel for physical verifiability — I wrote the check because the delivery showed up at my door and I could feel the flywheel. OpenRelay is more tactile than a B2B API abstraction: a developer can drop $5, get $10 credit, deploy a Docker container, and hit an OpenAI-compatible endpoint in a lunch break. That's real. But I cannot photograph it in the wild the way I read the Wi-Fi name off a Four Seasons café for Flock Safety (~2018); the artifact only exists inside a developer's terminal. Partial pass.

My 36-month forecast for OpenRelay: tuck-in acquisition by RunPod, Baseten, or ironically Voltage Park itself for $20-60M in an acqui-hire that values the Bedrock+TensorDock resumes more than the routing layer, at roughly $4-9M ARR — the classic domain-native-operators-wrong-lane exit. The mechanism producing that outcome is the signature — genuine founder-market fit trapped inside a category where incumbents have already sunk $2B+ into owned fleets and can undercut a hardware-agnostic aggregator on any price OpenRelay quotes.

The strongest bull case against my forecast leans on my own DeepMind (~2013) exception: pedigreed founders in a crowded lane can still compound if the polymath cross-domain edge is real, and Patel-plus-Wang is a genuinely rare cross-domain pair — hyperscaler inference architect *plus* GPU marketplace operator — that neither RunPod nor Together has in one room. If the "Dedicated provider program" quietly onboards even 200 independent operators in the next six months, the two-sided marketplace liquidity flywheel starts turning and OpenRelay becomes uncatchable in cost. I dismantle it with the dossier's own silence: zero disclosed suppliers, zero customer logos, zero press, zero social presence, "no public data found" appearing seven times in the traction section. The verifiable condition that flips OpenRelay to Invest is a signed supply-side agreement — even one named counterparty contributing >5MW of aggregated GPU capacity, publicly disclosed — because that would show OpenRelay is playing supply-side liquidity rather than demand-side price competition. Until then, pass with warmth toward the founders.
