# Conifer -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Conifer is an AI-infrastructure open-source dev tool competing against OpenRouter's $113M Google/NVIDIA war chest, with a 303-star GitHub repo as its highest-conviction signal and a category — LLM routing — that sits inside the exact "AI is just more internet" sleeve I've publicly de-rated. Michael Jeffords is the only named founder on the YC page, and I cannot verify his LinkedIn, GitHub, or Twitter. That combination — modest dev-tool distribution attached to an unverifiable operator inside my most de-rated sleeve — is the diagnostic signature, not the aggregate score.

On the sleeve dimension, Conifer sits closest to the canonical pass I described in the persona brief: the 2026 YC seed pitching "AI agent for [vertical]" at $30M post with four tier-1s already in. Conifer isn't quite that — it's the systems-flavored variant, "least cost routing for token spend," which reads as a smarter deck. But the underlying category exposure is identical to what I said out loud on the Lonsdale podcast: AI infra at seed prices is commodity exposure dressed as venture. Ollama moved from ~100K to 52M monthly downloads and llama.cpp/MLX are open-sourcing kernel work every quarter — Conifer's hand-written Metal/CUDA fused matmul is real engineering, but it's engineering that the OSS base rate absorbs on an 18-month timeline. I don't get paid to buy commoditizing base layers at YC batch pricing.

Michael Jeffords is the low rung on the conviction dimension in a way that specifically triggers my Red Flag list: no confirmed public profile, no essays on inference economics, no thread on why local-first beats cloud gateways, no evidence of 3+ years working on this problem before Summer 2026. My "writing is thinking" filter isn't a nice-to-have — it's how I distinguish founders responding to a Ycombinator opportunity window from founders who've been in the category long enough to have a non-obvious belief. Conifer's dossier gives me exactly zero non-obvious beliefs from Jeffords himself. The sage repo README describes proprietary compiled binaries; that's a distribution choice, not conviction I can underwrite.

Round dynamics on Conifer are absent from the dossier, which is where my "silence means consensus" default kicks in — Gustaf Alstromer as YC group partner plus B2B/Security/AI tags is the Summer 2026 archetype tier-1 seed funds are pricing at $25-35M post before demo day even ends. Over 18 months the compounding question is whether Conifer's routing dataset — the prompt-to-cheapest-model map implied by the "live intelligence index" — becomes a data moat before OpenRouter, which has $113M and every incumbent's per-token telemetry, adds an on-device execution path. That's a race Conifer is on the wrong side of by two orders of magnitude of capital.

Capital structure on Conifer is a counterfactual exercise: what would flip it a tier? If Conifer were structured as a consumption-only royalty on cloud fallback savings — customer pays a % of the OpenAI/Anthropic bill it avoided — that's a venture-shaped instrument in a bootstrappable business, and I'd re-read the deck. What's actually inferred in the dossier is seat-or-consumption pricing with a "Coming soon" marketplace, which is the vanilla SaaS cap table that will need five more rounds to reach default-alive against a $113M-funded incumbent. No revenue, no signed LOIs, marketplace unshipped — the burn-to-milestone ratio is the classic founder-drainage setup.

Conifer's distribution — 303 stars on ConiferKit/sage, 57 org followers, @coniferbuild handle without retrievable counts — parallels the earliest phase of a bottom-up dev tool but at a scale that hasn't compounded yet; John Fish had a YouTube channel with orders of magnitude more owned audience before Bookshelved's check, and even LinguaTrip's Mogilko deal required millions of subscribers to justify the 30-year royalty structure. 303 stars in July 2026 for a Summer 2026 YC company is a first-week-of-launch metric, not a distribution moat, and the dossier gives me no engagement curve to underwrite compounding.

My 36-month forecast on Conifer: acquihired by a cloud gateway or security incumbent — most likely Portkey-under-Palo-Alto-Networks, OpenRouter, or an observability player — for under $20M, purchased primarily for the Metal/CUDA kernel talent rather than the routing business, with the marketplace shipped or unshipped mattering little to the outcome. The mechanism is Ollama's OSS layer commoditizing the local runtime while OpenRouter's incumbent capital swallows the gateway routing layer, leaving Conifer's middle position squeezed on both sides within 24 months of the seed.

The strongest bull case on Conifer is that enterprise data-sovereignty demand — banks, defense, healthcare that literally cannot send prompts to external APIs — is a structural buyer segment cloud gateways can never serve because doing so cannibalizes their per-token revenue, and Conifer's hand-written kernels give it a genuine performance edge over Ollama on Apple Silicon fleets. That case stings because the incumbent-conflict thesis is real; OpenRouter genuinely cannot ship on-device without breaking its own P&L. But the dossier gives me zero signed enterprise contracts, zero named F500 pilots, and an unverifiable founder — the compliance-buyer thesis requires enterprise sales credibility Conifer hasn't yet demonstrated, and the OSS substitutes (Ollama Enterprise, vLLM, MLX) are already competing for that same regulated-buyer pipeline. I flip to Invest if Conifer publishes a signed contract with a named F500 bank, hospital system, or defense integrator buying local-first for a compliance clause I can point at. Absent that, this is a pass.
