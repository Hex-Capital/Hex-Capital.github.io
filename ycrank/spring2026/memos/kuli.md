## The Idea

**Problem:** Influencer marketing teams at large consumer brands manually review creator content across TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube to assess brand fit, scout creators, and analyze campaign performance. The YC listing states this work has historically required "more tools, more tabs, more spreadsheets" and that brands pay agencies up to "$50K/month...for manual tasks" (YC company page). Existing platforms provide databases filtered by surface-level metrics (follower count, engagement rate, audience demographics) and CRM tools, but do not analyze the actual video content (Influencer Marketing Hub review, 2026).

**Approach:** Kuli's AI agent analyzes video content frame by frame using multimodal AI, indexing "visual elements, spoken language, brand mentions, and narrative patterns" into structured, queryable signals (Influencer Marketing Hub review, 2026). Marketers interact via natural language prompts rather than traditional filter-based search. The platform covers creator discovery, automated vetting with continuous monitoring, campaign execution, performance analysis, and brand safety surveillance (Influencer Marketing Hub review, 2026; Camille Jourdain blog, 2025).

**Differentiation:** Unlike CreatorIQ, GRIN, and Upfluence, which rely on metadata-level filtering (follower counts, hashtags, audience demographics), Kuli's core differentiator is content-level video analysis — it processes what creators actually say and show in their videos (Influencer Marketing Hub review, 2026). The company claims "validation happens during discovery rather than after," compressing campaign timelines (Influencer Marketing Hub review, 2026). Kuli positions as a single AI agent replacing multi-tool workflows, contrasting with incumbents that offer modular SaaS suites.

**Business Model:** Custom pricing with plans tailored to mid-market and enterprise brands; pricing available upon request (Influencer Marketing Hub review, 2026). Free trial available (Influencer Marketing Hub review, 2026). [Inferred]: Likely a SaaS subscription model given B2B enterprise positioning and custom pricing approach typical of mid-market/enterprise influencer platforms.

## Defensibility

- **Proprietary content index:** Kuli claims to be building "the world's largest analysed social media content database without storing any video" (EPFL Investor Day 2024 profile). The structured video analysis data, accumulated over time, could create a data advantage as the corpus of indexed content grows.
- **Switching costs:** [Inferred]: Enterprise clients who build workflows, creator shortlists, and campaign history within Kuli would face switching costs, though these are typical of any SaaS platform at this stage.
- **Technical complexity:** Frame-by-frame multimodal video analysis across multiple social platforms requires significant ML engineering. [Inferred]: This is replicable by well-funded teams but represents a non-trivial technical investment.

**Market structure:** Incumbent platforms (CreatorIQ, GRIN, Upfluence) are built around database-and-filter architectures with established integrations. [Inferred]: Rebuilding their core product around agentic video analysis would require rearchitecting their technology stack and potentially disrupting existing customer workflows — a classic innovator's dilemma where the new approach requires a fundamentally different product architecture.

**Commoditization risk:** Large AI model providers (OpenAI, Google, Anthropic) offer multimodal video understanding APIs that could lower the barrier for new entrants or enable incumbents to add video analysis features. [Inferred]: The defensibility lies more in the domain-specific application layer (influencer marketing workflows, brand context understanding) than in the raw video analysis capability.

## Key Risks

**Name collision and discoverability:** "Kuli" returns results for Kuli Kuli Foods (a moringa superfood company), Kuli Media, Kuli Digital, and other unrelated entities across web and social searches. This may impair organic discovery and SEO.

**Incumbent feature absorption:** CreatorIQ ($80.8M raised, $70.7M revenue per Latka) and GRIN ($145M raised) have the resources and existing enterprise relationships to add AI video analysis features to their platforms. CreatorIQ already uses AI for database curation (Influencer Marketing Hub review, 2026). The risk is that video analysis becomes a feature within existing platforms rather than a standalone product category.

**Platform dependency:** Kuli's core capability depends on access to video content from TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube. API access changes, rate limiting, or content access restrictions by these platforms could disrupt the product. TikTok faces ongoing regulatory uncertainty in the US market.

**Concentrated customer base:** Three named enterprise clients (Supercell, Disney, Havas) with no public data on contract size or pipeline breadth (YC company page). [Inferred]: At pre-seed stage with a 3-person team, revenue concentration risk is elevated if a small number of enterprise contracts constitute the majority of revenue.
