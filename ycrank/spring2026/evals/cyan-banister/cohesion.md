# Cohesion -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Devon Krapcho spent five-plus years as a buy-side software analyst at Long Path Partners, which means Cohesion clears my first and hardest screen -- the founder actually sat in the seat the product is built for -- and then immediately runs into my second and harder one, because "AI agent for hedge-fund equity analysts" is the most consensus lane in the entire Spring 2026 YC batch. That pairing -- high founder-market fit, near-zero "magically weird" -- is the analytical signature of Cohesion in my book: a competent operator-founder swimming in a current already churned by AlphaSense at $500M ARR, Hebbia at $161M raised, Rogo at $300M+ raised including a $160M Series D in April 2026, and Brightwave at $21M. The lane is the problem, not the founder.

Krapcho's biography is where Cohesion earns its real points, and the right comparison on this axis is Max Levchin at Affirm -- a domain-native founder whose lived contact with payments was the substrate, not a memo. Cohesion isn't quite there: Krapcho lived the analyst's pain at Long Path Partners and Matt Munns built AI for investors at T. Rowe Price, which is the right kind of biography, but neither has shipped a prior product to scale in an adjacent domain the way Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus before Anduril. Cohesion sits closer to Levchin than to the FAANG-PM-with-a-memo founder I pass on every batch, and the diverging detail is that there's no evidence Krapcho built or ran an internal tool at Long Path that pre-existed the YC application -- the lived pain is real, the prior artifact is not visible in the dossier.

The pass example in my playbook is the consensus YC AI-agent darling with six top funds chasing the round, and Cohesion lines up against that template almost cleanly -- AlphaSense, Hebbia, Rogo, and Brightwave are already running the same play with $1B+ in collective capital, and the YC framing of "agentic teammate monitoring podcasts and X/Twitter" reads as a feature on a Hebbia or Rogo roadmap rather than a category. The mechanism that kills companies in this lane isn't competition on quality, it's revenue concentration meeting incumbent bundling: Cohesion's cited 10+ funds at $10Bn+ combined AUM is a real signal, but AlphaSense's existing distribution into every one of those funds means the bundle pricing is the bear case. I'm meant to find them before it becomes consensus -- Cohesion is the consensus.

Three co-founders, one named CEO in Krapcho, no public disclosure of equity split or tiebreaker protocol. Over an 18-month horizon, the failure mode this compounds into is the HQ Trivia / GameCrush pattern only if the technical co-founder (McBrien, ex-Amazon/AWS) and the AI-for-investors co-founder (Munns, ex-T. Rowe Price) disagree on whether Cohesion is a research product or an inference-infra product -- a real fork in this category, because Rogo and Brightwave have already split that way. The dossier doesn't tell me whether that conversation has happened, so I default mid-range and note that "Co-Founder & CEO" appearing once in Krapcho's title is the only governance signal Cohesion has surfaced.

For the score on Cohesion to flip a tier on capital-intensity, the company would have to be building proprietary acoustic indexing of earnings calls and podcast audio at a scale that requires real GPU spend, or licensing primary alt-data the incumbents can't touch -- something with a compounding technical or regulatory moat. The dossier shows neither; it shows monitoring of third-party podcasts and X/Twitter, which is the exact TOS-dependency risk the dossier itself flags, and which makes Cohesion structurally a software wrapper rather than the Vigil Labs / Crusoe / Anduril class of bet I write large checks for.

On physical verifiability, the single-anchor comparison is Postmates in 2011 -- I bet because the delivery happened in front of me at the curb, an artifact a normal human could touch in 48 hours. Cohesion is the inverse: a B2B SaaS sold to hedge funds behind a sales cycle I cannot enter, with no public app, no observable artifact, no Product Hunt presence, no Discord, no Twitter follower count for any of the three founders. I cannot install Cohesion. I cannot watch a friend use Cohesion. The dossier itself reports "No public data found" across the entire founder-reach row, which is the absence-of-signal that the absent-evidence default was written for.

My 36-month forecast on Cohesion: acquired by AlphaSense or Hebbia for $30-80M as a talent-and-customer-book tuck-in by mid-2029, with the alternate scenario being a pivot into a narrower vertical like credit-fund alt-data where the incumbents are weaker. The mechanism is straightforward: AlphaSense already sells into the same 10+ funds, Rogo just raised $160M to extend agentic coverage past investment banking, and Cohesion's monitoring-of-podcasts-and-X angle is a feature rather than a wedge -- which is exactly how acqui-hires get priced.

The strongest case against my pass is that hedge-fund willingness-to-pay is genuinely extreme and 10+ funds at $10Bn+ AUM at YC stage is not a normal traction signal -- if Krapcho's analyst network compounds, Cohesion could hit $5M ARR inside 18 months and price a Series A above $50M, which is the Affirm-Levchin override of my "consensus lane" rule. I take the objection seriously and still pass, because the Affirm exception required Levchin to be a serial founder with a prior exit, and Krapcho is a first-time founder whose deepest moat -- his analyst rolodex -- is exactly what AlphaSense's enterprise sales team already owns. The condition that would flip my verdict is verifiable proof that Cohesion has signed a multi-year, six-figure-per-seat contract with a named fund that has already churned off AlphaSense or Hebbia for Cohesion specifically -- not "10+ funds" in aggregate, one named displacement. Until then, I'd rather write the check into the Vigil Labs class of regulated, unsexy lane where the founder authentically lives in the domain *and* the category isn't already consensus.
