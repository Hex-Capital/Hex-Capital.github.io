# Result -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Result is a teenage-shipper play wrapped around a seven-headed scope chart -- Savio Martin's 961-star Codehouse repo and Product Hunt Maker of the Year 2021 sit on one side of the ledger, and on the other sits a 2-person team promising to ship incorporation + banking + payments + product hosting + marketing + support + content + growth agents into a market already populated by Mercury ($650M ARR, $5.2B post, 300K+ customers per Crunchbase News May 2026), Stripe Atlas, Doola ($12M raised), Firstbase, and Clerky. The signature here is *real founder shipping rhythm aimed at a multi-miracle bundle in a non-re-opened market*. That is not the **Stripe** (2010) shape and it is not the **Perplexity** (2022) shape -- it is closer to a Product Hunt launch with a banking partner attached.

On founder shipping rhythm -- the dimension where Result places highest -- the reference rung is **Perplexity** (2022), where Aravind would "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863). Result's CTO Savio Martin is genuinely in that template at the artifact level: Codehouse at 961 GitHub stars, Slickr at 588, Gradientking at 347, a prior product into the App Store top five, 50.5K followers on @saviomartin7, all of it built independently before age 19. That is *shipped, distributed, measured* output, not pedigree, and it is the only part of the Result dossier that activates my normal entry filter. Where Result diverges from Perplexity is the second founder: Aaryan Kushwah at 17 brings a journalism agency (90nine.in), a TreeHacks 2026 win, and an in-progress Coinbase Crypternship for Summer 2026 -- which overlaps the YC batch and reads as a bandwidth conflict on a team that already has too many surfaces to cover.

Revenue velocity is where Result places lowest, and the failure mechanism I want to name comes from my own *AI Market Clarity* "false signal" framing (TechCrunch, Nov 2025): a pre-launch waitlist on result.dev with no public signup count, no revenue, no named design-partner logos, no Product Hunt launch yet for Result itself -- this is not "just working," and in the AI cycle silence on growth is itself the signal. Mercury is doing $650M ARR with 300K founders today; the comparison is not flattering to a waitlist. If Result were quietly compounding $50K MRR across even a hundred LLC formations, I'd expect it in the dossier; absence here is informative because real velocity is easy to disclose.

Market catalyst -- the dimension I weight heaviest -- is where Result fails my **Stripe** (2010) entry test. The dossier's "why now" is [Inferred]: "LLM-based agents crossed a usability threshold in 2024-2025." That is a *capability* statement, not a *market re-opening*. Stripe Atlas has existed since 2016 and Mercury since 2017; the founder-tooling stack was never a dead market with arrows in incumbents' backs the way defense was pre-**Anduril** (2022) or payments were pre-Stripe. The catalyst Result is implicitly claiming -- "agents can now do marketing/support/content" -- is the same catalyst every YC Spring 2026 batchmate is claiming, which means it is not a window peers are ignoring; it is the consensus AI thesis applied to a non-re-opened category. Over 18 months, this compounds badly: the catalyst becomes table stakes, the incumbents bolt on agent features, and Result is left competing on bundle UX against Mercury's distribution.

Index position fails by counterfactual: for Result to score a tier higher here, it would need to sit *under* Stripe Atlas and Mercury rather than *over* them -- i.e., be the rails every founder-platform routes against, the way **Stripe** (2010) became "an index on e-commerce." The dossier shows the opposite topology: Result is a presentation layer atop incorporation partners and BaaS providers it has not yet disclosed (result.dev, May 2026, no partner named). When founder-tooling grows, Mercury's deposits grow and Stripe's TPV grows; Result captures take-rate only on customers who choose its bundle over the specialists, which is a share fight, not an index.

Single-miracle architecture -- compared one-anchor to **Color Genomics** (2015), where I deliberately isolated the bet to workflow on top of commodity sequencing rather than a chemistry miracle -- Result inverts the discipline. The Result roadmap requires (1) agentic reliability across marketing/support/content simultaneously, (2) BaaS/incorporation partnerships at competitive economics, (3) bundle UX that beats specialist incumbents on each surface, and (4) consumer distribution to founders who currently default to Mercury or Stripe Atlas. Each is independent; the NFX *Unusual Signs* math compounds against you when miracles stack like that on a 2-person team.

My 36-month forecast: Result pivots within 12 months from the seven-headed bundle to a single founder-ops point product (most likely an "AI marketing/support agent for solo founders" that plugs into Mercury and Stripe Atlas rather than replacing them), reaches <$1M ARR by month 36, and is acqui-hired by Mercury, Doola, or a YC-network buyer for Savio Martin's distribution muscle and Codehouse-style consumer instincts. The mechanism is straightforward: scope sprawl forces a pick, and the only surface where Result's CTO has demonstrated real shipping leverage is the consumer-product/distribution layer, not the regulated banking/incorporation rails -- so the pivot lands where the founder-market fit actually is.

The strongest bull case against me is the **Notion** (2015) analogy: Notion was a "platform for everything teams do" run by young founders with a design-led bundle thesis, and I funded it. If Savio Martin is the next Ivan Zhao -- a young, taste-driven shipper who can turn a bundle into a religion through community distribution -- then Result's scope is a feature, not a bug, and the 50.5K X following is the pre-distribution seed. The case stings because Notion did violate my "single miracle" framing. The dossier evidence that wins: Notion shipped *one* deeply-loved document surface for years before bundling; Result is launching seven surfaces simultaneously on a 2-person team with a co-founder (Aaryan) carrying a parallel Coinbase Crypternship through the batch. Notion also was not picking a fight with a $5.2B incumbent (Mercury) on day one. The verifiable flip condition: if within 9 months Result ships one component -- pick any of incorporation, banking-routing, or a founder-ops agent -- to >20% MoM revenue growth with a named ICP, I revisit at the next round. Until then, pass; this is a "Harvey-clone at consensus prices" structural setup, just in consumer fintech instead of legal AI.
