# Andco -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Andco is a vertical workflow-SaaS bet with one genuine specific-knowledge gate -- Michael Lemm out of Kirkland & Ellis -- bolted to a distribution architecture that is the structural opposite of what I fund: AI agents sold demo-by-demo into 50,435 personal injury firms via, literally, "200 boxes of donuts" handed out by founders posing as DoorDash Dashers. The high-low signature is Lemm's legal-domain depth pulling one direction and the donut-box GTM pulling the other, and the donut box wins the argument for me. This is a respectable Filevine acquisition target, not a Hit Forge check.

Twitter (2007) is the leverage architecture I keep against my own ledger -- anyone can post, marginal cost of distribution is zero, the platform asks no permission from a gatekeeper. Andco lives at the opposite pole of that ladder. The shared mechanism a permissionless business would need is that PI firms discover and self-onboard the product through code or media without a human in the loop; the Andco dossier instead names "Book a Demo" as the site CTA, in-person prospecting with donut boxes as the founding-customer playbook, and Filevine CMS sync as the integration substrate. Every one of those is a permission asked of a gatekeeper -- the firm's managing partner, the demo calendar, the Filevine integrations team. The "many high volume firms... hundreds of cases and thousands of requests" is real revenue, but it is labor leverage compounding through founder hustle, not code leverage compounding through near-zero-marginal distribution. The Andco architecture would have had me sit out **Replit** (April 2023) if Replit had looked like this.

The Pinterest pass is my standing reminder that single-sided products can compound to multi-billion outcomes, but Pinterest had a media-network engine where the Nth pinner made the (N-1) prior pinners' boards more discoverable. Andco has none of that user-added-value mechanic in the dossier -- a PI firm using Andco to retrieve a police report does not make the next PI firm's retrieval better, faster, or cheaper, except through whatever private model improvements Andco harvests internally. The dossier itself concedes the moat is "multi-channel agent infrastructure" plus a Filevine integration, not network. Andco is the single-sided SaaS subscription where each customer is economically independent of the next, which is exactly the bottom-quartile anchor on this dimension.

Michael Lemm at Kirkland & Ellis is the one rung Andco genuinely climbs. Specific knowledge is "knowledge you cannot be trained for" (Almanack, 2020), and a Harvard Law graduate who actually practiced at K&E and then co-founded Layup (W23, exited to Whatfix) carries domain texture that an ex-FAANG PM with a TAM slide does not. Over 18 months this dimension compounds -- Lemm knows which insurance-carrier portals matter, which UPL clauses bite in which states, which Filevine fields the paralegals actually use -- and that is why Andco is "live with many high volume firms" five months after a March 2026 launch. Ryan Xue's resume reads closer to the McKinsey-Google-policy pattern I'm wary of, but the Layup W23 exit with Lemm gives the pair a clean prior-cycle artifact. The dimension is real; it just doesn't carry the other four.

PI pre-litigation workup is a $61.7B revenue category by IBISWorld 2025 numbers, which sounds like room, but it is one case type inside one practice area inside one country -- the inverse of the platform-shaped TAM that lets a founder pivot twice without refinancing. The counterfactual: Andco's market score flips a tier only if the founders can name two adjacent pivot vectors the agents extend into without a rebuild -- workers' comp intake, mass-tort medical chronology, employment-law retrieval -- and the dossier's "Concentration on a single vertical and case type" risk line concedes the opposite. Tort reform or a settlement-pattern shift hits Andco revenue directly. Uber (2010) survived because ride-sharing absorbed the black-car pivot; Andco's market needs to absorb a pivot from PI to something else, and the agent infrastructure built around Filevine, insurance carriers, and medical providers is not portable to, say, developer tools or consumer media.

Andco's founder trio reads clean against the integrity leg -- Layup exited to Whatfix without litigation noise, Lemm's K&E pedigree is real, the cap table has Joe Montana / Liquid 2, SV Angel, Immad Akhund, Kevin Hartz -- a roster that signals other angels ran their own 10-year-partner test and stayed. Compared to **Notion** (2013), where Ivan Zhao's adolescent-onward tool obsession was the trio's load-bearing leg, Andco's energy reads as repeat-YC-founder hustle (donut boxes, $12M oversubscribed inside one batch) rather than monomaniacal product obsession. I would take the meeting; the trio passes. It just isn't the dimension that decides this check.

My 36-month forecast: Andco reaches $5-15M ARR inside PI workflow, then gets acquired by Filevine or Litify at $50-200M to plug an agentic-retrieval gap before EvenUp ($385M raised, $2B valuation, ARR doubling) extends upstream from demand-package generation into the same retrieval surface Andco occupies. The mechanism is the donut-box GTM converting fine inside a fragmented vertical but capping out at the speed founders can sign firms one-by-one, while EvenUp and Eve Legal ($103M Series B at >$1B) compound their distribution into the same buyer pocket with ten times the sales firepower.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that PI law's 50,435-firm fragmentation with no firm above 5% share is exactly the consolidation-play structure where a founder-led, lawyer-built workflow product wins by going deep on a buyer no incumbent CMS can credibly serve at agent-as-staff pricing -- the dossier flags that Filevine and Litify "monetize software seats rather than per-case agent labor," meaning their P&L cannot copy Andco's pricing without cannibalizing their own. That sting is real. The dismantling fact is that Andco's distribution is itself seat-based founder hustle, not the permissionless leverage that would let it ride the fragmentation faster than EvenUp can buy growth with $385M. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Andco ships a self-serve onboarding flow where a PI firm signs up, connects Filevine, and processes its first ten cases without a human Andco salesperson in the loop, and that flow drives a majority of new firm activations inside 12 months. Until then, this is a competent workflow SaaS in a category I do not fund.
