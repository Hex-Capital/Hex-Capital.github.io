# Refortifai -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Refortifai is what the pass example in my deck looks like when it actually walks through the door: "AI Agents for Analog Circuit Design," YC Spring 2026, three founders, website returning 403, sitting in a category where Cognichip just raised a $60M Series A with Intel's Lip-Bu Tan on the board. The dimensional signature here is a moderately credible founder-market-fit profile (Atman Kar did real RTL work at Texas Instruments and Tenstorrent) attached to the most consensus-priced sleeve a 2026 YC batch can produce — AI agents for an enterprise vertical with four well-funded competitors and two incumbents already shipping the feature. That's the exact archetype I've spent two years saying I cannot generate alpha against.

Solana at $0.05/coin is what the HIGH rung of price-discipline looks like — buying a pre-consensus protocol when the generalist crowd had quit the category and the only people in the room were already-paid believers. Refortifai is the structural inverse: EE Times wrote "Next Gen AI EDA Startups Have Potential to Disrupt Design Automation" in April 2026, Khosla led Astrus's $8M USD seed in October 2025, Benchmark led Quilter's $10M Series A in February 2026, and Seligman put $60M into Cognichip the same month — every tier-1 VC who could be in this category already is. Refortifai isn't the lone bid on an illegible asset; it's the fourth or fifth bid on a category that EE Times itself just declared as "opening up to AI-driven assistance." That's the consensus heat I deselect on reflex, regardless of how good Atman Kar's RTL chops are.

The pass-example template — "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, four tier-1 VCs in, $30M post" — is the LOW rung on my category-sleeve ladder for a reason, and Refortifai's "AI Agents to build, debug and automate analog circuit design" sits cleanly inside that template with the verticalized noun swapped in. The failure mode I named in that example wasn't team quality; it was that no SAFE cap structure absorbs the markup you need when Cognichip is raising at 30x your price and Synopsys is bundling Cadence.AI-equivalent agentic flows into the EDA seats those 9,000 analog designers already pay for. Refortifai's dossier shows zero customers, zero revenue, no patents, no published benchmarks — the moat has to materialize from RL environments that don't yet exist as artifacts, against incumbents who own the distribution and a Series-A-stage competitor who's signed 30 semiconductor companies. The category itself is the disqualifier; Atman Kar's TI badge is a feature on a structurally bad asset.

Capital structure for Refortifai compounds the wrong direction over 18 months: matching Cognichip's $93M total or Astrus's Khosla-backed runway requires Refortifai to raise a Series A inside a year against a stronger-funded incumbent set, and every tack in that regatta gets priced off a comp set where Refortifai is the laggard, not the leader. The mechanism doesn't reverse — analog EDA is not bootstrappable on subscription revenue from sceptical TI design managers while three competitors give product away for logo acquisition. Founder conviction on Refortifai cuts the same way: counterfactually, if Sayan Mitra had a five-year public writing record on analog automation, or Atman Kar had an OSS project with thousands of stars in EDA tooling rather than 15 stars on Trotbot, I might rerate this on the "missionary temperament for non-linear time" axis — but the dossier surfaces no public artifacts of category-specific obsession from any of the three, only credentials. Founder-as-brand for Refortifai is the cleanest single-anchor comparison to John Fish / Bookshelved: Fish had owned YouTube distribution and a literary niche pre-check, Refortifai has no Twitter accounts found across three founders, an unreachable website, and a LinkedIn company page with unretrievable follower count — there is no community on the cap table to underwrite.

My 36-month forecast for Refortifai: acqui-hired by Cadence, Synopsys, or a second-tier EDA player for $15-40M in equity-and-retention by Q2 2028, with the RL-environments codebase folded into an existing Virtuoso or Cadence.AI module — no venture-shaped Series B markup, no independent path to the $50M revenue line my "venture moments" framing requires. The mechanism is straightforward: Cognichip wins the foundation-model frame, Astrus owns the layout sub-niche with Khosla's follow-on capacity, Synopsys eats the standalone-tool surface area with bundled pricing, and Refortifai's CTO becomes more valuable inside Cadence's analog group than running a sub-scale AI-EDA startup against three better-capitalized peers.

The strongest bull case on Refortifai is the Ro override pattern from my Known Exceptions: regulated-or-technically-hard categories where only VC capital clears the schlep can convert capital intensity from failure mode into moat, and analog circuit design — which EE Times explicitly called "historically resistant to automation because of reliance on engineering intuition" — could be one of those categories where the technical difficulty itself is the barrier and whoever cracks RL-on-analog first compounds a data moat the incumbents can't replicate. Atman Kar's RTL background at TI and Tenstorrent is a real wedge, and a signed TI design-partnership through that alumni channel would constitute genuine non-consensus evidence. The bear-case dismantling: Ro had the FDA-licensing schlep as a 50-state regulatory moat that compounded over years; Refortifai has a technical-difficulty claim against four competitors and two incumbents all attacking the same difficulty simultaneously with more capital — that's not Ro, that's the commodity AI-infra red flag I named explicitly. **The condition that flips my verdict: a named, paying pilot with Texas Instruments or a comparable analog house disclosed before the next round, sourced through Atman Kar's TI network, at terms showing genuine ARR rather than logo-credit.** Absent that specific evidence, Refortifai is the canonical 2026 pass.
