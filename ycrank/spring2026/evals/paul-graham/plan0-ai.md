# PLAN0 AI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Arash Barati spent eight years in CRE private equity, three in lending, three in development -- $7B of projects underwritten -- and PLAN0 AI is what he built after sitting through the two-week takeoff wait one too many times. That is the load-bearing fact in this dossier. PLAN0 AI scores highest on idea-organic-to-founder-pain and lowest on pre-funding animal resourcefulness, and the gap between those two is the entire story: a domain-perfect CEO building exactly the tool he wished someone would make for him, but lacking the cereal-box moment that would flip my skepticism about an AI-takeoff category where six benchmarked competitors already exist.

**Stripe (2010)** is the right HIGH rung for the organic-idea dimension -- the Collisons spent years hacking commerce flows through Auctomatic before they built the payments API they personally wanted -- and PLAN0 AI's Arash sits closer to that rung than to the middle. Fourteen years of underwriting deals at three different firms is the construction-finance equivalent of the Collisons' commerce-hacking apprenticeship; the 2-week pre-construction takeoff is a pain Arash felt as the buyer, not as the vendor. Where PLAN0 AI diverges from Stripe is that Arash is the operator who suffered the problem and Shervin is the engineer who didn't -- the Collisons were both hackers and both customers. PLAN0 AI's organic-idea fit is real but asymmetric across the founding team, which is why this dimension lands strong but not maximal.

The MBA-deck pass example I keep in my head -- two consultants pitching an AI wrapper with no shipped artifact and a $50B TAM slide -- is what I scan for on the animal-resourcefulness dimension, and PLAN0 AI partially escapes that trap and partially doesn't. The escape: "$20B of projects already running through the platform" pre-YC, if true, is a serious pre-funding signal. The trap: that figure appears via a search snippet attributed to a launch announcement, with no named customer, no disclosed ARR, no pricing page, and demo-only CTA on plan0.ai. I have read the Brian Armstrong Coinbase rejection-rescue story enough times to know what an animal moment reads like, and "we have $20B on platform" without a named lender or developer counterparty is not it -- it is the shape of a stunt without the substance of one. The dossier itself flags a founding-year conflict (2024 on YC, 2021 on StartupHub) which makes me discount the unverified throughput claim further.

Construction-cost estimation for developer/lender underwriting is genuinely schlep-adjacent in a way pure AI wrappers are not -- PLAN0 AI has to assemble "localized historical and real-time cost data" via industry partnerships, which is the kind of grind Togal and Kreo have routed around by staying in contractor takeoff. Over 18 months this schlep compounds if Arash's $7B-network actually converts partnership LOIs into a data corpus competitors cannot replicate; it collapses if RSMeans or Gordian license an AI takeoff layer to Togal first. The dossier names no partnership counterparties, which is the same evidentiary gap as the $20B claim -- a real schlep with the receipts missing.

Shervin Barati ran AirPods hearing-health features at Apple and studied Robotics at Toronto; Dimitris Pagonakis is ex-Citadel MIT. For the score on hacker genealogy to flip from mid to high, I would need to see one of three things: a GitHub trail with non-trivial open-source contributions, a competitive-programming or Young-Scientist-style prize the way Patrick Collison had before Stripe, or a shipped technical artifact under either Barati or Pagonakis's name predating PLAN0 AI. None appears. Apple-manager-shipping-AirPods-features is a credential signal, not a hacker tribal marker, and PLAN0 AI's team reads as "strong engineers with good resumes" rather than as hackers in the Reddit/Swartz/Collison lineage I over-index on.

The single anchor here is **Stripe (2010)** again, but on team configuration rather than idea-origin: brothers plus a long-trusted third. PLAN0 AI has Arash and Shervin Barati as brothers and Dimitris Pagonakis described as Arash's best friend, which is the canonical pair-or-trio-with-prior-relationship structure from "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010). This is the cleanest dimension in the dossier and the one I will not penalize.

My 36-month forecast for PLAN0 AI: $4-12M ARR selling to mid-market developers and regional lenders via Arash's network, squeezed on one side by Togal.AI's better-funded contractor wedge ($17.2M raised vs. PLAN0 AI's $4M) and on the other by RSMeans or Gordian licensing AI-parsing into the cost-database layer they already own, ending in either a flat Series B or a $75-150M acquisition by Procore or Autodesk. The mechanism: founder-market-fit drives strong initial enterprise sales, but the unverified "industry partnerships" moat collapses when an incumbent with the cost data licenses commodity computer vision, and PLAN0 AI exits before the data network effect compounds.

The bull case that stings: Arash Barati's $7B of relationships across PE, lending, and development is exactly the relationship-density Togal does not have -- contractor-focused vendors cannot pivot to lender underwriting without channel conflict, and if the $20B-projects claim is even directionally accurate, PLAN0 AI is already inside a buyer segment Togal structurally cannot reach. **Airbnb (2009)** is the Known Exception precedent: I rejected the idea, the founders had a stunt I would not have credited from a deck, and I was wrong. The dismantling: Airbnb's cereal-box revenue was verified at the table -- I asked, they answered with specifics. PLAN0 AI's $20B figure has no named counterparty in any source, and a founding-year conflict (2024 vs. 2021) in the same dossier. The condition that would flip my verdict: PLAN0 AI publishes two named lender or developer customers with disclosed contract values, and Arash names three of the "industry partnerships" feeding the cost database. Until then, PLAN0 AI is a Neutral with strong founder-market-fit and an unverified moat -- I'd want to meet Arash in person before writing, not from the dossier alone.
