# Kuli -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Kuli is the Pass Example from my own playbook with the serial numbers barely filed off: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], named-logo references after a few months, three months out of YC." Michael Hodara ran AI-driven paid spend at Aircall against a $20M budget and that is genuinely the strongest founder-market-fit signal in the dossier — but Kuli's dimensional signature is competent operator-founders walking straight into the most consensus-coded sleeve of the entire Spring 2026 batch. The high dimension (Hodara's domain depth) and the low dimension (an "AI coworker" agent shell sitting on commoditizing video LLMs) don't rescue each other; they describe a company I literally wrote a pass example about before I read the dossier.

**Sublime Security** is the comparison Kuli wants to be — a pre-consensus enterprise wedge where the tier-1 funds were still parked in late-stage SOC SaaS when I wrote the check. The mechanism that made Sublime load-bearing was category mispricing: I was bidding into a sleeve other underwriters had categorically deprioritized. Kuli inverts that mechanism. The dossier names CreatorIQ at $35K–$200K/yr, Aspire at $2,499/mo, Modash at $299/mo — a fully-priced, fully-staffed incumbent shelf that every growth fund is already mapping for "AI-native disruptor" theses. No tier-1 has visibly passed; Spring 2026 YC AI-agent rounds are clearing at prices I cannot make money at, and silence on the round dynamics in the dossier is exactly the signal my Value Seed essay says to punish. Kuli is what Sublime would have looked like if I'd shown up two years late and paid the consensus mark.

The Pass Example failure mechanism — "standard SaaS cap table that will need five more rounds to reach default-alive" — runs cleanly through Kuli's structure. The product is seat-replacement software sold into mid-market and enterprise marketing teams on "custom monthly plans" per the InfluencerMarketingHub review; that is a land-and-expand SaaS template indistinguishable from fifty other 2026 YC seeds. There is no revenue-share against the campaign budgets Kuli orchestrates, no royalty on creator-deal flow Kuli sources, no holdco wrapping the Disney/Nivea/Havas relationships — none of the financing-instrument creativity that made Teamshares or the Mogilko deal venture-shaped without being a software cap table. Kuli is a generic SAFE on a generic enterprise-software trajectory, and the dossier gives me nothing to restructure.

Mechanism check on the "watches every video" moat over 18 months: Kuli's claim to analyze hook, pacing, audio, caption against the brand's context is a feature, not a system. Multimodal video understanding is rolling down the foundation-model cost curve every quarter — by mid-2027 a CreatorIQ product team with their ~20M-profile graph can bolt frame-level analysis onto existing seats and compress Kuli's wedge into a workflow-ownership fight Kuli has not yet won. The dossier discloses no proprietary dataset, no per-brand outcome flywheel beyond [Inferred], no benchmark. The moat compounds only if the campaign-outcome data loop locks in before incumbents ship the same primitive, and 5 months of operating history with one named anchor customer is not enough runway to bet that direction.

Counterfactual for founder conviction: Hodara's Aircall arc and the ten-year side-hustle partnership with Jonathan Hassan are real, but to flip this dimension a tier I'd need to find an opinionated essay or thread from Hodara on why every existing creator-marketing platform is structurally wrong — the "Writing is thinking" artifact I look for before the meeting. The dossier finds no Twitter for either founder, no GitHub, no public long-form. EPFL Robotics + KPMG M&A is a competent résumé but not a missionary one; nothing in the dossier tells me Hodara is the person who cannot be talked out of this take on creator marketing specifically. Without that artifact, the prior is opportunity-response, not personal mission.

Single anchor on founder-as-brand: **John Fish / Bookshelved** is the standard I underwrite to when distribution is the asset. Fish brought a YouTube audience that pre-existed the company by years and made his check a price on his own attention. Kuli has zero of that — both founders are LinkedIn-only with follower counts not even retrievable, the company Twitter @kuli_ai is unmeasured, there is no newsletter, no Discord, no operator audience on the buyer side. Kuli will pay for every customer it acquires, which is fine for an enterprise SaaS — it just means Founder-as-Brand contributes nothing to underwriting.

36-month forecast: Kuli reaches $3–8M ARR on the back of Hodara's enterprise execution, then plateaus as CreatorIQ ships native video analysis into its existing $35K–$200K seats and Aspire bundles a comparable agent at $2,499/mo, and Kuli gets acqui-hired by one of CreatorIQ, Aspire, or Sprout Social for under $40M before a clean Series B clears — a respectable founder outcome and a flat-to-negative outcome for the entry price the 2026 AI-agent market is asking me to pay.

The strongest objection: Hodara's Aircall background ran an AI system over $20M of paid spend, and after 5 months Kuli has Disney, Nivea, and Havas in its mouth — those are not pre-seed-typical logos, and the **Ro** Known Exception teaches that capital intensity into a regulated workflow can itself be the moat when only VC dollars clear the schleps. The bull case is that the schlep here is enterprise integration into 50+ brand-side martech stacks, and Kuli's operator-CEO is the one founder in this batch who can actually execute it. That case stings — until you notice Ro's override required a genuine regulatory clearance moat across 50-state prescription rails, and Kuli's "moat" is a software integration any incumbent with existing seats can replicate by Q4 2026. The Ro precedent does not extend to commodity martech wedges. Verdict flips to invest if Kuli restructures the round as a revenue share against the campaign budgets Kuli orchestrates — say 3% of routed creator-spend across 5 years instead of equity — because that would price the underlying asset (campaign flow) instead of the software cap table; absent that restructure, I'm out before the meeting starts.
