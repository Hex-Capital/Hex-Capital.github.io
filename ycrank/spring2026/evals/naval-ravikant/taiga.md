# Taiga -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Taiga sells "Full-stack Medical Billing Services" to independent doctor's offices, and the noun in that phrase is *services* -- which means before I read another line of the dossier, the leverage architecture has already answered the only question I open with. This is labor leverage dressed in AI clothing, sold via direct outbound into a regulated vertical, priced (inferred) on percentage of collections. Taiga's highest dimension is the $60.69B TAM that absorbs almost any RCM bet; Taiga's lowest dimension is the one I read first. That pairing -- huge addressable market married to permissioned distribution -- describes a respectable services business and a structurally wrong angel check for me.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung on leverage architecture because anyone could post, the platform charged nothing, marginal cost of replication was zero, and the media compounded the founder's distribution without asking a gatekeeper. Taiga's distribution surface is the opposite physics: ten named EHR integrations (DrChrono, athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, Tebra, NextGen, Elation, Greenway, Practice Fusion, Office Ally, Amazing Charts) where each platform can throttle Taiga's data access on quarterly notice, plus inferred direct outbound to independent practices one logo at a time, plus a percentage-of-collections fee structure that scales with labor displaced rather than with code shipped. The Anchorage exception doesn't rescue Taiga either -- Anchorage's regulatory moat (Treasury OCC charter, January 2021) became *permission-as-moat* in institutional crypto; Taiga has no comparable charter, only an integration dependency on platforms like athenahealth that already ship native billing modules. Nanda Guntupalli and Adam Wax are not building media or open code; they are building a service.

Uber (2010) is the LOW rung I'll invoke for the network dimension, but inverted: Uber's mechanism was the Nth driver making the (N-1)th rider's wait time shorter -- the marketplace got better with scale and that compounding is what underwrote the 4,000x. Taiga's customer geometry is single-sided in the strictest sense: Cheskis Family Therapy's claim submissions tell Taiga nothing useful about the next orthopedic practice's payer mix, and the inferred denial-feedback loop the dossier mentions compounds *inside Taiga's model weights*, not across customers in a way users would feel. Each independent practice Taiga lands is economically independent of the next, which is the literal definition of the bottom-quartile anchor in my framework. The Perplexity exception requires top-quartile specific knowledge plus pre-consensus category timing to override the network gap -- Taiga has neither.

Specific knowledge at Taiga is the dimension I want to be charitable on and cannot get there. Nanda Guntupalli is Penn Class of 2028 -- a sophomore -- and Adam Wax's profile reads the same; both founders' parents operate independent medical practices, which is *lived adjacent exposure* but not *lived domain ownership*. Stack Overflow (2008) was Atwood and Spolsky writing for the developer community they were already inside as practicing developers shipping code; Notion (2013) was Ivan Zhao rebuilding personal productivity tools since adolescence. Watching your dad run a billing fight with Aetna at the dinner table is a real source of motivation -- it is not the same as having coded against the X12 837 claim format for five years or having sat as a coder inside an RCM firm. The dossier names no shipped artifact, no GitHub, no prior medical-billing operator role for either founder.

Market size and pivot-room is a counterfactual exercise: for Taiga's market score to flip a tier upward, the dossier would need to name at least two adjacent expansion vectors that aren't downstream of the same payer-permission stack -- prior authorization, patient eligibility, clinical documentation, scheduling -- and credibly argue Taiga's data position lets it pivot there inside seed runway. The $60.69-63.32B 2025 RCM TAM expanding to $137.37B by 2033 is real, and Candid Health's ~250% YoY 2024 growth plus Adonis's 4x 2025 growth confirm the category is absorbing dollars. But every adjacent move from medical billing lands inside healthcare's permission stack, which means Taiga gets *size* without getting *pivot-room* -- the first idea has to work, or the second idea has to work inside the same regulatory perimeter. That is the inverse of the Uber black-car-to-UberX pivot space.

The founder trio reads clean -- no contested exit history, no self-promotion about ethics, friends since third grade at Penn -- and applying the absent-evidence default lands me near neutral. Compare to Stack Overflow on this single axis: Atwood and Spolsky brought a decade of public technical writing and shipped products before the seed; Guntupalli and Wax bring a friendship and a Penn affiliation. There's no red flag here, just the absence of the load-bearing signal a ten-year-partner test wants to see. I would write that check on intelligence and energy -- the integrity leg I can't yet evaluate because there's no surface area.

Taiga's 36-month forecast: acqui-hired into Candid Health or Adonis for the engineering team at a price between $15M and $40M, or alternatively plateaus as a regional independent-practice billing firm at $2-5M ARR with margins that look like a labor business because the underlying economics *are* a labor business with AI cost reduction. The mechanism producing that forecast is the leverage-architecture diagnosis: percentage-of-collections services scale linearly with sales effort, the EHR platforms above Taiga capture optionality on the data, and the $300M+ already deployed across Candid, Adonis, and Akasa means the consolidation buyer set is pre-formed and motivated.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass: Y Combinator has been minting AI-vertical-services companies that look like labor businesses on paper and behave like software businesses on the P&L because the AI cost curve is collapsing faster than the headcount denominator -- and the Y Combinator Spring 2026 batch is exactly where that arbitrage compounds. Nicolas Dessaigne as partner is a real edge. The bull says: Taiga's lived exposure through both founders' parents is a non-trivial wedge into a fragmented buyer set that Candid Health (multi-site SaaS) and Akasa (650+ hospitals) structurally cannot serve, and 1.3M independent U.S. physicians at a 5% take rate on collections is a $20B services pool that can support a $1B outcome without ever touching a marketplace dynamic. Where that argument fails on the dossier: the 98% touchless claim rate is self-reported against one named customer (Cheskis Family Therapy), the inferred percentage-of-collections model caps multiple at services-business levels, and the leverage architecture remains permissioned no matter how good the cost curve gets. The verdict flips if Taiga ships an open-source claim-scrubbing or X12 837 parsing library that becomes the default tool independent-practice billers reach for -- a code-leverage substrate underneath the service -- and the dossier shows no such artifact and no founder GitHub from which one would plausibly emerge.
