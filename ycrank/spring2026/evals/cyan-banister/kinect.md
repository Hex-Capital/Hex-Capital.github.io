# Kinect -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Kinect is the cleanest possible governance story (Kratik Agrawal named as CEO, Varun Kandula as co-founder, two people from the same UCLA CS class who already shipped together at Reevo) parked inside the single most consensus lane of the entire Spring 2026 YC batch -- "AI shopping agents," a category Modern Retail literally titled "the wars that will heat up in 2026." That pairing is the whole tell. When the team is tidy and the category is the loudest room at the party, I am usually leaving the party.

Crusoe is the HIGH rung on my "magically weird" ladder -- burning North Dakota gas flares to mine Bitcoin sounded like three uninvestable things stapled together in 2018, and credible VCs were passing on aesthetic grounds. Kinect's pitch is the structural inverse: Daydream raised $50M from Forerunner and Index, Alhena raised ~$13.9M, Rep AI is in market, Shopify shipped native Agentic Storefronts to all US merchants on March 24, 2026, OpenAI embedded checkout in ChatGPT in 2025, Amazon expanded Rufus auto-buy, and Modern Retail is writing trend pieces on the category. Kinect is not the kind of founder VCs are quietly passing on at a dinner; Kinect is the kind of company three more YC Spring '26 teams are also building under a different name. Find them before it becomes consensus -- this is already consensus.

HQ Trivia is the LOW rung on my "single CEO" ladder, and its failure mechanism was Yusupov/Kroll never resolving who held tiebreaker authority. Kinect does not trip that wire. Kratik Agrawal is explicitly listed as Co-Founder, CEO; Varun Kandula is Co-Founder without CEO title; they are a two-person team that already worked together leading Conversational Intelligence at Reevo, which is the closest thing pre-seed gets to a documented operating cadence. This is the one dimension where Kinect cleanly clears the bar I usually use to disqualify two-founder shops. It just is not enough on its own to overturn what is happening elsewhere on the page.

The DeepMind exception is what I have to check next -- polymath cross-domain compounding can override the bias against pedigreed founders. Kratik's resume reads Google Commerce, Anduril ML clustering, Verkada, Reevo. That is competent, even adjacent (Reevo's Conversational Intelligence is the same primitive Kinect now sells), but it is not Hassabis-grade compounding across chess, neuroscience, and game design. Kinect's founder-market fit is "we built the adjacent thing at our last job" -- a 13/32 placement on the lived-experience ladder, not a 25/32. Neither Kratik nor Varun describes a physical moment they *felt* the DTC merchant pain; the origin reads analytical, the kind I pattern-match to the pass example.

Shopify Agentic Storefronts is the eighteen-month compounding mechanism I cannot get around on the capital-intensity axis. Kinect is a SaaS layer on top of Shopify catalogs, chat plus adaptive PDP plus agent-readable feed. Shopify launched the native version of that feed for free to all US merchants two months before Kinect's YC launch tweet. The moat does not compound with time the way Crusoe's well-pad relationships or Anduril's hardware did; it gets squeezed with time. The "brand-owned data layer" is a phrase, not a moat -- if Shopify standardizes the schema, Kinect's per-merchant intent data is a Shopify report.

Counterfactual on physical verifiability: I can go install Kinect right now on Slumberkins or Hearthy Foods and chat with the assistant, which is more tactile than the pass-example B2B API and earns Kinect 7/13 rather than the 5/13 default. For this dimension to flip a tier, I would need to feel the *viral* pull HQ Trivia had -- the kind where I stop investing and start playing. A chat widget on a children's stuffed-animal site does not give me that. It works. I do not feel it pulling.

My 36-month forecast: Kinect gets acqui-hired by Shopify, Klaviyo, or a mid-market commerce platform for $15-40M between mid-2027 and 2029 as Shopify's native Agentic Storefronts absorbs the standalone-vendor wedge; Kratik and Varun land good PM/eng-lead roles, LPs get pennies on the dollar, the company does not die loudly. The mechanism is exactly the signature -- clean team, consensus category -- which is the profile that gets shopped rather than scaled.

The bull rebuttal that stings: Kratik and Varun already led Conversational Intelligence at Reevo together, which means they shipped this exact primitive before Kinect, and ten named DTC brands (Slumberkins, Sousa, Zupz, Ketro, ALC, Bloom, Hearthy Foods, Huppy, VapeTM, Loosey Goosey) are live at pre-seed with cited 20% conversion lift and 14% AOV bumps. That is a comparable-product-shipped-at-adjacent-scale signature, which is the **Anduril**-Luckey template I claim to weight heaviest. If those metrics are attributable per-brand under audit and the customer count doubles to 20+ by August 2026 with a paid plan published and one logo that is *not* a sub-$10M DTC shop, I want to see the round again -- that would prove Kinect is selling something Shopify's native feature does not replace, and the consensus-category objection gets weaker. Until then, the dossier's own risk section is doing my arguing for me: unattributed metrics, hyperscaler buyer-agent capture, well-funded incumbents, platform substitution by the host. Pass, with the door cracked.
