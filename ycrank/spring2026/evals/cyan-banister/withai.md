# WithAI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Ian McInnis ran an equity book at Bridgewater and was tasked with "how can we use LLMs to invest?" before WithAI existed — that is the part of this dossier that lights up my lived-experience filter. The rest of WithAI is a consensus AI-for-finance category sitting between a $700M Hebbia and a $750M post-money Rogo, which is the part that kills my "magically weird" score. The signature is "domain-native founder, but in the most-crowded YC-adjacent lane of 2026." That pairing tells me what WithAI actually is: a competent enterprise wedge that probably becomes someone's acquisition or someone's internal tool, not the next Anduril.

Palmer Luckey at **Anduril** (2017) is my high rung on lived-experience — he had shipped Oculus and lived the hardware before the defense pivot. Ian McInnis at WithAI is a softer version of that pattern: he actually held the equity book and led the "use LLMs to invest" workstream at Bridgewater, which means he was *of* the desk, not visiting it. Where WithAI diverges from the Anduril rung is the absence of a prior shipped-to-scale artifact — Luckey had Oculus revenue history; Ian has a Princeton '23 graduation date and roughly two-and-a-half years inside one shop. The lived contact is real but the trajectory is one rung short of the Palmer template, and the Princeton-math-perfect-GPA framing is the exact "Stanford or Harvard" credential surface I usually flinch at, redeemed only because the work he did inside Bridgewater was the actual product.

The pass-example YC AI-agent darling failed my magically-weird filter because six tier-1 funds were already chasing it; WithAI is one cohort downstream of that exact failure mode. Rogo just closed $75M at $750M post-money in January 2026 with Sequoia leading and an OpenAI collaboration. Hebbia is already inside roughly 30% of asset managers per their own claim. The Bridgewater co-CIOs Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour are on the WithAI cap table as angels, which is wonderful social proof and also exactly the signal that the round is *not* underpriced — when the two most prominent macro investors in the world are validating your seed, you are not finding them before it becomes consensus, you are the consensus. WithAI is the opposite of the **Crusoe** (~2018) flared-gas pitch where credible VCs passed on aesthetic grounds; nobody is passing on WithAI for aesthetic grounds, they are passing because Rogo got there first with ten times the capital.

Tiebreaker authority on the WithAI cap table reads cleaner than most two-handers I see — Ian is the named CEO at ian@withai.co with the Bridgewater origin story, Ben Finch is the CTO with the Sentient Labs and Princeton ECE Magna trajectory, and the domain split (buyer-side workflow vs. multi-agent infrastructure) is non-overlapping. Over 18 months this dimension compounds well or fails fast: if Ian keeps the customer-facing surface and Ben keeps the agent-coordination layer, the **HQ Trivia** (2017) Yusupov-Kroll co-CEO collision pattern does not appear here. The risk I cannot price from the dossier is that they met at Princeton co-founding a "technology investment firm" together, which means the relationship is collegial-equal, not boss-and-builder; the document does not name a tiebreaker.

If the WithAI moat depended on regulated single-tenant deployment in hedge fund VPCs the way I'd want a Vigil Labs (August 2025) type bet to depend on regulatory texture, the score on capital-intensive/unsexy-regulated would flip up a tier. It almost does — client-cloud deployment in AWS/Azure/GCP, 60+ data integrations including Bloomberg and Snowflake, hedge fund security review cycles running 6-12 months. But the actual artifact is a software platform, not a hardware or compliance moat that compounds outside the founders' selling motion. For the score to flip, WithAI would need either an exclusive integration relationship (Bloomberg API tier nobody else gets) or an SEC-adjacent licensing posture, and the dossier shows neither.

Compared to **Postmates** (2011), where I bet because I watched a deli sandwich arrive at my door — a tactile artifact I could observe in my own life — WithAI fails the Four-Seasons-Wi-Fi test by category. I cannot install Multiplier, I cannot ride it, I cannot photograph it. The only physical-world verifiability is the Mercator Partners testimonial about shifting from "80% gathering, 20% acting" to the reverse, which is founder-mediated testimony, not an artifact I can touch. The >$100K ARR with multiple clients three months post-launch is real and informative for a B2B pre-seed, but it is the wrong *kind* of evidence for the dimension I weight here — it tells me sales work, not that the product has the viral physical pull of an HQ Trivia or a scooter on a curb.

My 36-month forecast on WithAI: $3-8M ARR ceiling as an independent company, then an acquisition by Hebbia, AlphaSense, or a Bloomberg-class incumbent for $40-120M — or absorbed back into Bridgewater as an internal tool given Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour are already on the cap table. The mechanism is exactly the signature: a domain-native founder generates real early revenue in a category where two well-funded incumbents have already raised hundreds of millions, the founder-led consultative sale scales to a respectable enterprise book, and then the exit math caps because the moat is sales-trust-plus-customization rather than category-defining product.

The strongest case against my pass is that Ian McInnis with Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour on the cap table is *not* a normal consensus-YC pattern — it is a closed-loop trust network where Bridgewater alumni and principals route WithAI into every major fundamental equity shop through warm intros, and the client-cloud architecture is the precise wedge multi-tenant Hebbia cannot copy without re-architecting. **DeepMind** (~2013) is the known exception that haunts me here: I backed Hassabis despite the pedigree because the polymath compounding overrode my outsider-trajectory bias, and Ian-plus-Ben (Princeton math + Princeton ECE + Bridgewater investment seat + Sentient Labs agent research) is structurally that same polymath cross-domain stack. The dossier evidence that wins for me anyway is Rogo's $75M January 2026 round and the OpenAI collaboration — WithAI is racing a category leader with 10x capital, and the Bridgewater-angel network is a real edge in a footrace that has already started. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: a signed multi-year contract with a named multi-strategy fund above Mercator's tier (Citadel, Point72, Millennium, or Two Sigma) at six-figure-plus ACV before the next round, evidencing the trust network actually closing the biggest accounts.
