# Armature -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Armature is a lean three-person wedge with a clean leverage profile and almost no specific knowledge underneath it ? a 3-headcount team running self-serve SaaS at $49-$149/mo against an MCP-testing surface, founded by ex-Palantir and ex-Roland Berger operators whose Joko investor cap-table mirrors their last employer. That pairing ? high team-to-value leverage on top of near-zero specific knowledge ? is the analytical signature, and it is exactly the structure of the AI wrapper archetype I pass on at demo day.

Specific knowledge density is where I start, and Armature lands close to my pass example. Perplexity sits at the top of this ladder ? Aravind Srinivas spent years inside Google Brain and OpenAI building the actual systems that make AI search work, knowledge a competitor cannot hire its way into. Notion sits in the middle ? real product taste, but not founder-irreplaceable. The bottom is the two-MBA AI wrapper team I described in my pass example ? competent operators who chose a fundable problem. Th?odore Otzenberger spent five years at Palantir and then Joko; Louis Scremin moved through Roland Berger Paris/Singapore into Joko business ops. Joko is a French consumer cashback app. Nothing in the dossier shows either founder obsessively building agent test harnesses before Spring 2026 ? no GitHub history, no prior eval-tooling project, no public artifacts. Armature pins just above the AI wrapper rung and well below Notion. If the founder disappeared, would the company's core insight survive? On the dossier, yes ? which is the wrong answer.

Permissionless leverage is the strongest dimension, and even here Armature is mid-ladder. Twitter sits at the top ? Evan Williams' Blogger knowledge productized into a media platform with zero marginal cost per user. Notion sits in the middle ? code leverage, real, but team-bound. Wish sits at the bottom ? marketplace mechanics with no underlying creator utility, leverage that evaporated when speculation ended. Armature is SaaS with code leverage and three live pricing tiers, which puts it near Notion. The drag is the integration treadmill the dossier names explicitly ? Claude Code, Codex, OpenClaw, ChatGPT, Gemini, every frontier model. That is labor leverage wearing a code-leverage costume. Each new harness is human work, not zero marginal cost. Armature sits between Notion and Wish, closer to Notion only because the SaaS pipe itself is genuine.

Principal authenticity is where Armature looks most like an agent. Gumroad sits at the top ? Sahil Lavingia kept coding solo after the Series A failed because he could not stop. The bottom is again the AI wrapper team that picked a fundable problem. The Armature founders' investor list ? Palantir, Joko, Tsuga ? overlaps directly with the founders' prior employers. That is not non-consensus capital; that is the network giving its alums a graduation gift. There is no evidence either Otzenberger or Scremin was burning nights on agent regression testing before YC told them to. Pin closer to the wrapper rung than to Gumroad.

Team-to-value leverage is where Armature genuinely earns marks ? three people, self-serve pricing live, classic Midjourney-shaped denominator. Midjourney is the top rung at 163 people and ~$500M revenue. Notion is the middle rung. Varda is the known-exception bottom rung where capital-intensive physics overrode the team-density rule. Armature with three people shipping a B2B SaaS sits between Notion and Midjourney on team density alone. Access democratization is the weakest. AngelList sits at the top ? I know, I built it ? democratizing angel investing was a structural unlock. Notion in the middle democratized lightweight tooling for builders. Clearview AI is my known-exception bottom ? an anti-democratization product I held my nose on for raw leverage. Armature is a B2B test runner for product teams shipping MCPs. It expands no gatekept resource to a new population. It sits just above Clearview only because it isn't actively anti-democratizing.

My 36-month forecast: Armature is acquihired by Braintrust, Langfuse, or LangSmith for under $50M, or it pivots into a narrower MCP-specific test-generation tool inside one harness ecosystem. It does not clear $5M ARR independently. The mechanism is the one DeepEval already telegraphed ? eval-platform incumbents with $36M+ war chests added MCP metrics in 2025 (args correctness, task completion, tool correctness), and they will ship multi-harness execution before three founders without specific knowledge can build a defensible integration matrix. Innovation without a moat is consumer surplus, and Armature's "moat" is the speed at which it integrates other people's harnesses.

The strongest case against me is that MCP standardization is a genuine regime change, agent evaluation is architecturally distinct from LLM evaluation (Latitude's argument), and a lean three-person team with a Palantir/Joko enterprise pipeline could compound a regression-suite switching cost faster than incumbents notice. Bolt.new hit $4M ARR in month one; small teams can move very fast in this regime. The dismantle is that Armature is renting leverage from the harness ecosystem ? Claude Code, Codex, ChatGPT ? rather than owning any layer of it, and the founders have no visible specific knowledge to compound a non-obvious test-generation advantage. The condition that flips my pass: Armature publishes a third-party benchmark where its auto-discovered test plans materially beat DeepEval and Braintrust on real customer MCPs, and shows ten unaffiliated paying enterprises ? meaning outside the Palantir-Joko network ? within six months. Until then, I pass.
