# Smartbase -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Sam Goldman and Taira Fujioka have pivoted twice in roughly two years -- from Sphinx Labs (smart contract DevOps, $2M raised) to Anytrace (AI support engineering, YC S25) to Smartbase (manufacturing PO automation, YC Spring 2026). Three distinct problem domains across three ventures is the signal I want to unpack first, because it cuts both ways in my framework. On one hand, speed of iteration is the only advantage a startup has against an incumbent -- if it took as long to debate the pivot as to build and test the new product, just build it. On the other hand, I back founders who have personally lived through the scaling challenges of the category they're attacking, and neither Goldman nor Fujioka has manufacturing experience. The dossier notes they "worked on factory floors" and "slept at a factory" to do primary research, which shows hustle, but manufacturing domain expertise typically accrues over years, not weeks. Fujioka's AWS SageMaker Inference background -- youngest SDE 2, building inference infrastructure for JP Morgan and Salesforce -- is directly relevant to the AI extraction core of Smartbase, and that's a real technical credential. But operational scaling in manufacturing requires understanding procurement workflows, ERP politics, and factory floor change management that neither founder's background demonstrates.

Smartbase's market structure timing is where I spend the most time, and the honest assessment is: the structural shift is real but not nonobvious. LLM-based document understanding crossing the accuracy threshold for heterogeneous PO formats (handwritten, faxed, emailed) at costs viable for SMBs -- that's a genuine capability jump. Two years ago, this required expensive per-customer OCR template configuration; now GPT-4-class models handle it out of the box. ERP vendors expanding REST APIs (Acumatica, NetSuite SuiteTalk) simultaneously reduces integration costs. Both shifts are real. But both are also visible to every AI automation startup, every ERP vendor, and critically, to Conexiom, which launched an "AI-Powered Ideal Order Platform" in January 2025 (PR Newswire). When I backed Coinbase in 2012, crypto infrastructure was dismissed by consensus. When I wrote the Perplexity seed check in 2022, AI-powered search was considered a failed category. Smartbase is entering a space where the AI document extraction use case is among the most obvious applications of current LLMs, and where a $170M-funded incumbent with ~$75M in annual revenue and 600+ clients is already investing in the same capability upgrade.

The strongest bull case for Smartbase maps to my Gusto thesis: boring operational infrastructure in an unglamorous category where prestige-seeking founders don't compete, targeting an SMB segment structurally underserved by an enterprise incumbent. Conexiom targets the top 20 industrial distributors with enterprise sales and pricing; Smartbase targets small-to-mid-sized manufacturers with five-figure annual contracts. That's the same structural gap ADP left for Gusto in payroll. Smartbase's nine named ERP integrations (Acumatica, Epicor, Infor, Dynamics 365, NetSuite, QuickBooks, SAP, SYSPRO, Sage X3) create real switching costs once configured with customer-specific business rules -- this is workflow dependency, not just software subscription. The $3.6B purchase order software market growing to $8.2B by 2033 at 9.8% CAGR (Verified Market Reports) provides adequate market size, and the payer is clear: manufacturers with identifiable headcount budgets replacing manual data entry clerks at ~$33,750/year per facility according to Smartbase's own ROI calculator. Every element of the Gusto parallel is present except the critical one: Gusto's timing was nonobvious because SMB cloud payroll was genuinely underestimated. Smartbase's timing is consensus -- AI document automation is one of the highest-frequency Y Combinator categories in 2025-2026.

Smartbase scores best on my single-miracle test. The technology miracle -- LLM document extraction reaching production accuracy -- is happening exogenously on the foundation model capability curve. Smartbase doesn't need to invent the AI; it needs to reliably engineer the extraction-to-ERP pipeline with sufficient accuracy to replace manual clerks. Manufacturers are proven software buyers with clear budget authority. Distribution to SMB manufacturers requires sales effort but not a distribution miracle. That's one engineering miracle, and it's a well-defined one. Compare this to the companies I pass on that need simultaneous breakthroughs in technology, distribution, and regulation -- Smartbase needs the product to work reliably, and the rest follows from standard execution.

On infrastructure-layer positioning, Smartbase occupies an ambiguous middle ground. It's not a platform that other businesses build on -- it's a vertical workflow automation tool that sits between PO receipt and ERP order creation. The nine ERP integrations create meaningful switching costs, but each integration is a separate maintenance surface. With a two-person team, maintaining parity across nine ERP APIs as vendors update schemas and deprecate endpoints is a genuine engineering bandwidth risk that Conexiom, with ~$75M in revenue, solves with dedicated integration teams. Smartbase embeds in a critical workflow, which is infrastructure-adjacent, but it doesn't compound through network effects or developer ecosystem adoption the way Stripe compounds through its payments API. The margin architecture is plausible -- SaaS with annual contracts, clear ROI story, and AI processing costs declining over time -- but at pre-seed with first customers being onboarded onto five-figure contracts, there's no evidence yet of retention dynamics, expansion revenue, or pricing power.

The condition that would flip me to Invest: evidence that Smartbase's AI extraction accuracy on heterogeneous manufacturing POs materially exceeds what Conexiom's new AI platform delivers, demonstrated through a measurable reduction in error rates or review time that creates a genuine product gap -- not just a price gap -- against the incumbent. If Smartbase can show 95%+ first-pass accuracy on handwritten and faxed POs where Conexiom's system still requires manual correction, that's a defensible technology wedge in a boring-but-critical category with real switching costs. Without that evidence, Smartbase is a competent pre-seed company in a real market with a real structural tailwind, but the market timing isn't nonobvious enough and the competitive positioning isn't differentiated enough to clear my bar. The two most decisive dimensions are Nonobvious Market Timing -- where the AI document extraction shift is genuine but consensus-visible, with Conexiom already responding -- and Operator-Scaler Founder Profile, where three ventures across three unrelated domains in two years and zero manufacturing background weigh against the strong SageMaker technical credential.
