# Gojiberry AI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is a profitable, capital-efficient B2B SaaS that does not actually need venture capital, getting force-fit into the most consensus YC sleeve of 2026 -- "AI GTM agents for B2B teams" -- which is the exact pitch I used as my standing Pass Example. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Dylan Teixeira have done the harder thing twice (CoCo AI to ~€500K ARR sold, Edusign multi-million ARR sold in 2025), and Gojiberry's $2.5M ARR in 10 months at 35% MoM on $500K raised is genuinely rare execution. The problem is not the operators. The problem is that the asset they have built is a bootstrappable SMB sequencer that Apollo ($1.6B valuation, $150M ARR) and Clay ($3.1B Series C, CapitalG-led) are going to either acquire or commoditize, and I cannot generate alpha from a YC SAFE on top of that.

Solana at $0.05 is the high rung on my price-discipline ladder because the category had been left for dead and the round had no consensus bid. Gojiberry AI is the structural inverse: AI SDR agents in 2026 are not a category anyone has quit -- they are the category every YC partner is funding, every Series B AI infra fund is double-clicking, and where 11x.ai already raised $74M before its $14M-claimed-vs-$3M-actual ARR blew up. Gojiberry AI is not pre-consensus; it is downstream of consensus, fishing in the wake of an 11x.ai stumble that every other YC AI GTM startup in S26 is also fishing in. The dossier names no tier-1 pass, no pricing discount, no asset-class ambiguity I could story-broker later -- it reads like the canonical $20M-30M post YC AI deal where the round is the product.

"AI is just more internet" is the load-bearing line on my category ladder, and Gojiberry AI sits inside the exact sleeve that line was written to deselect -- LLM-wrapped SDR automation built on scraped public LinkedIn signals, which the G2 reviewers themselves flag as the ceiling ("if your target audience is not active on LinkedIn... you may get fewer intent signals"). The low-rung failure mechanism here is not that AI agents do not work; it is that the moat reduces to "we aggregate the same 30+ public signals as everyone else, but our prompt is better right now," and Clay with 50+ data sources and Apollo with 250M contacts will close that delta with a feature release, not a five-year R&D arc. Gojiberry AI's own "cross-customer learning loop" claim is a 2,000-SMB cohort -- a real number, but not a data moat against incumbents who have an order of magnitude more signal under management.

Capital structure is where Gojiberry AI actually scores. $2.5M ARR, profitable, 35% MoM, $500K raised -- this is the Squarespace / Zapier / Muck Rack shape I keep pointing at when I say capital-efficient consumer-of-its-own-product execution can build incredible billion-dollar companies without the factory. Eighteen-month mechanism: if Gojiberry AI keeps the burn discipline and the 35% MoM holds even partially, they print $10-15M ARR on the existing balance sheet without another round, which is the only world where the YC SAFE entry price matters less than the founder execution. The risk that compounds against them is the SMB-only G2 cohort: every reviewer is under 50 employees, churn is the unspoken metric, and a v1 with a 2,000-waitlist and zero conversions sits two product cycles behind us.

Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Dylan Teixeira have each sold a B2B SaaS, which is the "rizz and tizz" execution-operator profile I score highly -- but the conviction dimension is about asymmetric belief, not asymmetric resume, and Gojiberry AI's public artifacts read as growth-playbook LinkedIn content, not a non-obvious thesis on why every other AI SDR is going to fail. The counterfactual to flip this dimension into Invest range: if Pierre-Eliott had published a 3,000-word piece arguing that intent-signal aggregation is structurally wrong and that the next outbound primitive is, say, agent-mediated reverse-RFP, I would re-read the deal. He has not. The pitch is "2-5x reply rates via 30+ signals," which is a benchmark claim, not a missionary one.

Marina Mogilko on the founder-as-brand axis had millions of YouTube subscribers and twenty years of compounding earning power; Gojiberry AI's distribution surface is Pierre-Eliott's 4,179 X followers and a Product Hunt launch with mixed sentiment (~30% negative per syncgtm.com). That is not owned distribution at a level that justifies pricing the founder as the asset rather than the company -- and Gojiberry AI is not pretending otherwise, which is honest but does not earn the Slow Creator Fund's structural attention either. This dimension is small for a reason; Gojiberry AI is neither a creator deal nor a community deal.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Gojiberry AI reaches $15-25M ARR on minimal additional capital, then gets acquired by Apollo or HubSpot for $100-180M as the agentic-outbound layer consolidates into the incumbent data platforms -- a fine outcome for the founders given $500K raised, a mediocre outcome for a YC SAFE holder pricing entry at the 2026 AI consensus, and a non-event for a Slow check.

The bull case that stings: Sublime Security looked like another enterprise security seed in a "solved" category and turned out to be an early entry before the consolidation wave, and Gojiberry AI's profitability plus repeat-founder execution is closer to Sublime's profile than to a generic AI agent shell -- if 11x.ai's churn truly opens a multi-billion-dollar AI SDR vacuum, the team that prints $2.5M ARR in 10 months on $500K may be exactly who fills it. The reason it does not win the deal: Sublime entered a category where two tier-1 funds had visibly passed and where the financing instrument I would have written was non-obvious; Gojiberry AI entered the most-funded category of the batch at standard SAFE terms with named, well-capitalized incumbents (Clay $100M Series C, Apollo $251M raised) building the same agent layer downhill from larger data assets. The one verifiable condition that would flip me: Gojiberry AI ships an enterprise (1,000+ employee) logo cohort with net retention >120% by month 18, demonstrating the SMB ceiling is not the ceiling. Until then, pass.
