# Sazabi -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Datadog has $2.7B in ARR, 47,000+ customers, and is already shipping AI features into its observability platform. Dash0 raised $110M at a $1B valuation in March 2026 with 600+ customers and its own AI agent for observability. Every major incumbent in this space is bolting AI onto their existing product right now. And Sazabi walks into this and says "we use LLMs to derive metrics and traces from logs." I look at that and my reflex is: this is perfect for Datadog. This is the canonical version of the thing I keep warning people about — "every time you get pitched Adobe for AI, you're like, this is perfect for Adobe." Sazabi is building an AI feature for an incumbent's roadmap and calling it a company. The observability category is one of the hottest consensus categories in enterprise infrastructure right now. Angels from Anthropic, Vercel, Replit, LangChain — that's not contrarian deal flow, that's a who's-who of developer tools consensus. The risk is not in the price. The enthusiasm is in the price.

Sherwood Callaway's background is legitimately strong — started Brex's Infrastructure and Observability teams at employee #70, two YC batches, Opkit acquired by 11x in September 2024, a16z scout. The co-founder dynamic with Justin Ko is exactly the kind of relationship I value: roommates who worked together at Crunchbase, Brex, co-founded Opkit, and went through the 11x acquisition together. That's multi-cycle observation of a partner, not a networking-event matchup. Callaway clearly knows observability from the inside. But "sane person, insane idea" requires BOTH parts. Sazabi's logs-only thesis — "fundamentally, logs are just events, metrics are aggregated events and traces are basically correlated events" — is mildly contrarian relative to the OpenTelemetry standard, but "AI-native observability" as a category is what every second YC company is pitching. A competent founder doing something the market is already excited about is just... a competent founder in a crowded field. The sanity is there; the insanity isn't.

The bull case someone would make to me: Sazabi's logs-only approach isn't just "AI applied to observability" — it's a structural rejection of the three-pillars paradigm that Datadog's entire revenue model depends on. Datadog bills separately for logs, metrics, and traces. Collapsing those into a single log stream would cannibalize their existing revenue, creating a classic innovator's dilemma. Callaway built Brex's observability stack and knows where the bodies are buried. And the agent-aware observability angle — monitoring AI-generated code from Claude Code, Cursor, Codex — is genuinely new territory that existing tools weren't designed for. That's the case. And honestly, there's a version of the Slack Known Exception here — where a company in a consensus-hot category gets backed because the underlying primitive is real. But Sazabi isn't creating a new behavioral primitive the way Slack did. Slack replaced email as a coordination medium and became a verb. Sazabi is simplifying an existing workflow. Nobody's going to "Sazabi" something. It's a better mousetrap, not a new category of trap.

The structural durability question is where Sazabi collapses for me. The dossier says it plainly: "The logs-only-plus-AI approach is architecturally reproducible. Any team with LLM infrastructure expertise could attempt a similar product." Dash0 already has AI agents doing observability. Sazabi has no physical-world moat, no protocol-level network effects, no regulatory license, no creator-audience lock-in. SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certifications are table stakes — every serious observability vendor has them. The "Perfect Memory" feature that accumulates customer-specific log history creates some switching costs, but Datadog has the same dynamic at 100x the scale. When I think about Metropolis — AI-powered parking seeded early, now ~$5B — the moat is the physical parking infrastructure that no LLM can replicate. Sazabi has no equivalent. It's pure software wrapping an API capability that improves every quarter for everyone simultaneously.

Sazabi's ten-person team pre-launch with a full platform scheduled for end of 2026 also raises questions about burn rate relative to learning. No revenue, no disclosed customer count, a pilot waitlist — that's standard pre-seed, and I don't penalize for it. But the competitive reality is that Sazabi needs to achieve product-market fit while Dash0 (600+ customers, $1B valuation) and Datadog (47K+ customers) iterate on the same AI-powered observability thesis with orders of magnitude more resources. The capital discipline question is hard to assess without numbers, but targeting early-stage tech companies with smaller budgets and higher churn as your initial segment means you're fighting for the least valuable customers in the market while burning runway against billion-dollar competitors.

One verifiable condition would flip me: show me that Sazabi's logs-only architecture makes observability 10x cheaper per dollar of infrastructure monitored — cheap enough to create a market among the thousands of startups that currently use no observability at all because Datadog costs too much. That's a new market, not a feature war. That would be genuine primitive creation — "observability for everyone" the way Venmo was "payments for everyone." But nothing in the dossier suggests this pricing thesis exists. Without it, Sazabi is a well-built AI application in a consensus category with no structural moat — exactly the bet I've been telling people not to make since 2023. Pass.
