## The Idea

- **Problem:** Real estate developers, general contractors, and investors currently spend roughly two weeks producing pre-construction cost estimates and takeoffs from architectural drawings (plan0.ai, 2026), which delays underwriting, pro-forma validation, and bid preparation.
- **Approach:** Computer vision models parse architectural plans to auto-generate quantity takeoffs, cost estimates, 3D models, and scenario simulations (e.g., concrete vs. steel swap), with a stated turnaround of ~30 minutes vs. ~2 weeks (plan0.ai, 2026).
- **Differentiation:** Vs. Togal.AI (takeoff automation focus at 98% floor-plan accuracy, $299/mo per user — Togal blog, 2026) PLAN0 layers scenario simulation and a localized historical/real-time cost database; vs. Kreo ($35/mo Caddie AI — Togal blog, 2026) PLAN0 targets developer/lender underwriting workflows rather than contractor BIM takeoffs (YC page).
- **Business Model:** No public pricing page; site offers only "Book a Demo" (plan0.ai, 2026). [Inferred]: Per-seat or per-project SaaS to developers/GCs/lenders given enterprise demo-led GTM and B2B classification (StartupHub.ai, May 2026).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Direct enterprise sales via founder networks — Arash Barati's prior $7B CRE PE/lending/development relationships (search snippet) plus YC Spring 2026 partner channel (YC page); demo-gated funnel on plan0.ai (2026).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Proprietary localized cost database built via "industry partnerships" combining historical and real-time pricing data (YC description, 2026); company states "$20B of projects already running through the platform" as a data-accumulation signal (search snippet referencing launch announcement).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Data network effect — more projects processed → richer localized cost benchmarks → better forecasts → more customers; unproven because partnership count, coverage, and refresh cadence are not disclosed publicly.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbent takeoff vendors (Togal, Kreo, STACK) optimized for contractor workflows would face channel conflict pivoting to lender/developer underwriting, which requires capital-markets data layers rather than quantity precision (Togal blog, 2026; YC description).
- **Commoditization risk:** Computer-vision plan parsing is a documented capability across at least 6 AI estimating platforms benchmarked in Feb 2026 (Robotics & Automation News, Feb 2026); the differentiated layer is the cost database, which is replicable by any well-capitalized competitor with partnership reach.
