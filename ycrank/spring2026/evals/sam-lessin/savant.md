# Savant -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu's Savant is the canonical 2026 YC AI seed pitch I described in my own pass example almost verbatim: enterprise AI knowledge layer, two CS-degreed founders with zero prior operating history in enterprise software, dropped into a category where Glean already prints $300M ARR at a $7.2B valuation and Dust just took $40M from Sequoia. The dimensional signature on Savant is flat-low across every axis I underwrite, but the sharpest read comes from pairing the absent founder footprint (no Twitter, no GitHub, no LinkedIn signal surfaceable) with the most consensus-saturated sleeve in the batch -- this is commodity AI exposure dressed as a memory layer, and "AI is just more internet" was exactly the line I used to stop writing these checks.

Round dynamics on Savant are the absent-evidence default, and I treat silence here the way I treat silence everywhere: as a tell. The HIGH rung I price against is Solana at $0.05 -- a seed I could underwrite because the consensus crowd had quit the room and I was the only bid that mattered. Savant gives me the opposite room: Glean's ARR tripled from $100M to $300M in fifteen months, Dust is at 3,000 organizations and 300K agents deployed, and every tier-1 in 2026 has an enterprise-AI thesis with a Savant-shaped slot in it. There is no disclosed price in the dossier, no named tier-1 pass, no strategic ambiguity -- just a category so hot that the round, whatever it's marked at, is almost certainly consensus-priced for an "AI agent for enterprise context" wrapper. I cannot generate alpha from this SAFE.

Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu's pre-pitch footprint is where the LOW rung does real damage. The failure mode I look for is the sanitized-pitch-with-no-public-artifacts founder -- someone responding to opportunity rather than pursuing a personal mission -- and Savant matches that template precisely. Two CS degrees, Yale and Cornell, no prior companies, no domain employers in knowledge management or enterprise search, no Twitter accounts findable, no GitHub repos surfaceable, no opinionated essays on why Glean's index-everything approach is wrong, no public take on what "undocumented procedures and judgment calls" even mean in production. Writing is thinking; if Kim and Yu have a non-obvious belief about why memory layers beat search layers for AI-native enterprises, the dossier surfaces zero evidence they've ever written it down.

Capital structure on Savant fails the mechanism test over an 18-month window. The pitch is a per-seat enterprise SaaS integrated into Google Workspace, Office 365, Salesforce, Slack, Zendesk, and Intercom -- the identical surface Glean, Dust, Guru, Microsoft 365 Copilot, Atlassian Rovo, and Notion AI all sit on. Savant needs to fund integration parity, enterprise sales motion, SOC 2, and customer-specific knowledge capture against incumbents who've raised aggregate ~$700M+; default-alive in 24 months is not a credible path, which means five more rounds of dilution against a SAFE cap I'd be writing into today. That is founder-drainage, not venture-shaping.

Founder-as-brand on Savant is the counterfactual case. For Savant to clear the 5/15 default and move into the range where John Fish / Bookshelved sits, I'd need to find Kim or Yu running a popular AI-ops Substack, a Discord of 1K+ enterprise-AI practitioners, or a year of public writing on tribal-knowledge capture that pre-dated the company. The dossier searches all of these and surfaces nothing -- not even a personal site. Owned distribution is a step-function input to early enterprise pilots in a crowded category, and Savant enters the regatta with none of it.

Category sleeve is where I'll do the single-anchor parallel: Sublime Security versus Savant. Both are enterprise plays at YC seed; Sublime cleared my filter because email security was a sleeve tier-1s were under-allocated to relative to SOC tooling, and the founder had operating context. Savant is the inverse -- AI knowledge management is the sleeve every generalist fund is overweight on in 2026, market.us pegs the AI-KM category at >$5B in 2026 with double-digit CAGR, and the founders carry no operating history to justify a non-consensus take. This is consensus exposure to a consensus sleeve; I get this beta from a Glean follow-on, not a Savant seed.

My 36-month forecast on Savant: dead or acqui-hired into Glean, Notion, or Atlassian for sub-$15M, with a non-trivial probability of a mid-cycle pivot to a vertical knowledge agent (legal ops, customer-support runbooks) running at sub-$2M ARR with a single named design partner. The mechanism is straightforward -- platform vendors bundle the memory layer into the same SaaS suites Savant integrates against, Glean's $600M war chest funds the feature-match in two quarters, and Kim and Yu run out of differentiation before they run out of cash.

The bull case worth steel-manning is Dust. Dust's $40M Series B and 300K agents deployed prove a memory/agent layer can scale outside the platform vendors, and Savant's positioning on "undocumented procedures, exceptions, judgment calls" is genuinely distinct from indexing-existing-docs if -- and only if -- the capture mechanism actually works in production. If Kim and Yu have built a knowledge-capture loop that turns Slack threads and ticket resolutions into structured procedural memory, that is the kind of illegible asset I theoretically over-pay for. But the dossier offers zero evidence the mechanism exists beyond the marketing copy on heysavant.com -- no benchmark, no case study, no design partner named, no demo described. Known Exceptions check: Robinhood, Pinterest, and Ro each cleared a different filter (early-entry markup, operator-friend sourcing, regulatory moat); Savant clears none. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed multi-year contract with a named AI-native enterprise (Anthropic, Cursor, Harvey, Glean's own customer base) where the procurement reason in writing is "undocumented procedure capture," priced below $10M post -- absent that, I'm out before the meeting.
