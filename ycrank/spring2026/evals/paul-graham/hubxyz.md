# Hub.xyz -- Paul Graham Evaluation

The first thing I notice is that Hub.xyz is hiring a "Founding Full Stack AI Engineer." That tells me everything I need to know about the team's technical composition. The three co-founders have backgrounds in industrial engineering, business school, venture fund LP work, luxury retail, and supply chain logistics. None of them are programmers. None have public GitHub repos. The core product -- an API that structures real-time data streams from a distributed network into AI training datasets -- is deeply technical work. And the person who will build it hasn't been hired yet. This is a PowerPoint, not a startup. I told Drew Houston he needed a co-founder because one person wasn't enough. Here we have three co-founders and still nobody who can build the product.

The origin of this idea sets off every alarm I have. How did these founders find this problem? There's no story of an ML engineer struggling with stale training data, no researcher who hit the wall of data scarcity in their own work. What I see is two hot categories -- AI and crypto -- combined in a brainstorming session. "AI needs data, crypto has token incentives, let's build a DePIN network for AI training data." That's the kind of idea smart people generate when they sit down and try to think of startup ideas, which is exactly how you're not supposed to do it. The founders' backgrounds give no indication they lived inside the AI training data problem. Armin was an LP at venture funds. Tim worked on financial data at Tiffany & Co. in Japan. Léonie ran supply chains for fashion brands. These are capable people, but they didn't stumble into this problem through personal experience -- they found it through market analysis.

The 500,000 "users" number deserves scrutiny. These aren't users in any sense I care about. They're people running a bandwidth-sharing node to earn IQ Points redeemable for $HUB tokens. The pull isn't toward the product; it's toward the token. This is a well-understood pattern in crypto -- you can bootstrap impressive-looking numbers by promising future financial rewards, but the moment the token economics shift, the network evaporates. Compare this to what genuine user pull looks like: Airbnb hosts who needed the rental income to pay rent, or Wufoo users who genuinely wanted better forms. When I ask "would these 500,000 people be disappointed if the product disappeared tomorrow," the honest answer is they'd just switch to Grass or Nodepay -- the competitors running nearly identical DePIN networks with better funding. That's not demand for Hub.xyz. That's demand for token rewards.

The strongest bull case is this: AI training data is genuinely becoming scarce. Models are exhausting existing internet data. Regulatory friction around centralized scraping is increasing -- lawsuits against AI companies for training data usage multiplied in 2024-2025. A distributed, user-consented collection model could become the compliant alternative. If Hub.xyz could establish itself as the structured-data API layer on top of a distributed network, it would be a picks-and-shovels play for AI training infrastructure, which is exactly the kind of thing I normally like. The SwissBorg partnership with 1M+ KYC-verified users is a real distribution channel, not vaporware. And the AI training dataset market growing at 22% CAGR means the tide lifts boats. But this bull case has a fatal problem: it requires Hub.xyz to win against Grass, which has a $1B valuation and $17.5M in funding doing the same thing. Being in a hot, crowded category with better-funded competitors running identical architectures is one of my clearest red flags. The differentiation Hub claims -- "structured, multimodal output rather than raw bandwidth" -- is an aspiration stated on a website, not a technical moat built by hackers.

There's also a team stability issue I can't ignore. Léonie Nguyen, listed as co-founder and CBO, was appointed CEO of Meey Group, a Vietnamese proptech company, in October 2025. She's not listed on the YC page. When a co-founder takes the CEO role at a different company five months before your YC batch, that's not a minor detail. I've seen what happens when co-founder commitment fractures early -- it poisons everything. And the remaining team has no visible technical founder, which means the company's most critical capability gap has widened, not narrowed.

The schlep in AI training data is real, but Hub.xyz is avoiding it rather than embracing it. The actual schlep is what Scale AI does: the tedious, unglamorous work of quality control, annotation consistency, edge case handling, and building trust with enterprise customers who need guarantees about data provenance. Hub.xyz's DePIN model attempts to route around that schlep by distributing the collection and using token incentives to manage the workforce. But the hard part was never collection -- it was curation. Bolting a crypto token layer onto a bandwidth-sharing network doesn't solve the quality problem that enterprise AI teams actually care about. It solves the supply-side acquisition problem, which is the easy part.
