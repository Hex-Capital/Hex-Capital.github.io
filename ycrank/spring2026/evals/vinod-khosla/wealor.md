# Wealor -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Wealor is, on my framework, a credentialed-consultant SaaS layer pitched at the seam between Orion's Denali AI (Feb 2026) and Addepar's Addison (Mar 2026) — its highest dimension is a default I assign in the absence of pivot evidence, and its lowest is the dimension I weight as my actual edge: technical or capital risk that repels consensus VCs. That pairing is the analytical signature. Karim Bouri and Pierre Depuydt are pitching a horizontal "AI-native operating system" with "forward deployed engineers for RIAs and family offices" into a wealth management platform TAM Fortune Business Insights pegs at $4.23B in 2026 growing to $9.99B by 2034. That is not a civilizational variable; it is a slice of an existing market, and the dossier says so in its own numbers.

OpenAI (2019) is the high rung on consequence — $50M into a no-revenue, unconventional-structure company because the success-state was a civilizational redirect. The shared mechanism a Wealor bull would invoke is "AI rewrites a labor-heavy white-collar function" — the same frame I used in Fortune (2025-07-01) when I said AI will do 80% of all economically valuable work by 2030. But Wealor does not displace the advisor; it makes the advisor's CRM-to-portfolio handoff smoother. The dossier names the success-state explicitly: "fix workflow bottlenecks between their tools." That is the McKinsey 1986 cellphone forecast in reverse — extrapolating a present workflow forward instead of asking what economic mechanism breaks if Wealor wins. Nothing breaks. RIAs keep RIA-ing, 5-10% more efficiently. That is the YC pre-seed pitch from my Pass Example with "wealth manager" search-replaced for "sales rep."

The low-rung pattern on technical risk is the GPT-4-sales-call-summarizer I pass on in 30 seconds — software-only execution risk, any Tier-1 generalist can fund it, no scientific miracle, no capital depth required, no thermodynamic or biological constraint to negotiate. Wealor's dossier reproduces that pattern almost line-for-line: no proprietary stack disclosed, the explicit risk factor labeled "foundation-model commoditization" via Anthropic's RIA plug-ins, and a moat described in the dossier's own words as "no defensibility signals found." This is the exact "wrapper on someone else's foundation model with no proprietary stack" red flag from my framework — the inverse of why I wrote Cognition (April 2024, $175M round) where the stack-depth on agentic engineering was the underwriting case.

On founder consensus-defiance — mechanism analysis over 18 months — the ENSAE Paris + HEC + École Polytechnique + UC Berkeley + BCG + Tiffany & Co. + French military profile of Bouri and Depuydt compounds in exactly the wrong direction. Credentialed founders selling to credentialed buyers (RIAs) in a regulated category default to enterprise-sales hygiene, not contrarian positioning. The dossier surfaces zero public thesis, zero controversial industry post, zero "instigator" pattern from my TED Talk. Eighteen months out, that profile produces a polished deck and a 14-month enterprise sales cycle into a $5T-AUM custodian that ends in a pilot, not a category collapse. This is the UC Berkeley 28,000-expert-predictions study cited in my TED Talk applied to founders: credential density is "only slightly more accurate than chance" on consequence-scale outcomes.

Founder learning rate — counterfactual: for Wealor to flip a tier here, the dossier would need a documented prior pivot from Bouri or Depuydt — Wally with Parpex and Zappelli either failed publicly and Karim wrote about why, or Pierre's BCG-to-Tiffany-to-military arc produced a public thesis update on financial workflows. Neither exists in the dossier. I apply the 7/15 default not because the founders are rigid but because the dossier is silent, and silence is not the Slash (2024) Kanye-pivot signal that justified my $100M Series C into a YC-backed fintech against my own consequence-magnitude prior.

Required-capital — single-anchor parallel: Wealor is the structural opposite of Commonwealth Fusion (2019), where I led a $115M Series A because the capital intensity itself was the moat that kept consensus VCs out. Wealor's roadmap, as a per-seat SaaS plus forward-deployed-engineering business into RIAs and family offices, reaches first revenue on $2-4M of YC + seed capital. I am over-resourced for this deal. That is not a virtue in my framework; it is a signal that any Tier-1 generalist can fund it, which means I have no edge and Khosla Ventures has no reason to be on the cap table.

Thirty-six month forecast: Wealor either dies quietly below $1.5M ARR by mid-2028 as Jump ($105M raised), Zocks ($65M raised), and Altruist's Hazel ($60/seat/month, built on the acquired YC-backed Thyme) compress the per-seat price, or it gets acqui-hired by Orion or Addepar for under $30M as a forward-deployed-engineering team to staff their own AI rollout. The mechanism is direct: horizontal OS layers across multi-vendor stacks lose to native vendor AI when the vendors themselves ship native AI, and the dossier already names both Orion's Denali (Feb 2026) and Addepar's Addison (Mar 2026) shipping inside the platforms the same RIAs already pay for.

The strongest bull case worth steelmanning is Glean as Known Exception: Arvind Jain is paradigmatic ex-Google domain-expert continuity, and I funded him anyway because consequence magnitude on Fortune-500 information asymmetry plus capital scale overrode the consensus-credential profile. A Wealor bull would argue Bouri's direct buyer-side exposure ("sold financial products to wealth managers") plus the forward-deployed-engineering motion is a Palantir-shaped wedge into an industry whose information asymmetry is real. The case stings because the FDE motion does occasionally produce category-defining outcomes. But Glean's consequence variable is enterprise knowledge across the Fortune 500; Wealor's is a $4-10B platform-software TAM where two incumbents at $5.8T and $9T of AUM control the desktop. The Glean override required civilizational consequence to dominate; Wealor cannot clear that bar with the numbers in its own dossier. Verifiable flip condition: a signed multi-year contract with a top-10-by-AUM RIA at >$500K ACV inside 12 months — published, named counterparty. Absent that, the consensus-credential profile plus the horizontal-OS-against-native-vendor-AI structural problem produce a 27/100 in my framework and never reach a second meeting.
