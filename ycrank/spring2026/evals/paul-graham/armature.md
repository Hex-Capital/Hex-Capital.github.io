# Armature -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Armature is a credentialed-insider bet whose organic-pain signal is real and whose hacker-tribal signal is missing -- Louis Scremin led "AI Agents & MCP servers" at Joko before Armature, which is exactly the prior-workflow origin pattern I look for, but neither he nor Theodore Otzenberger surfaces a GitHub account in the dossier, and that is the kind of absence I notice. If I had to name the shape: Armature looks like a Stripe-adjacent organic-idea profile sitting on top of a Palantir/Polytechnique credential stack rather than a Lisp-prize hacker stack, and that mismatch is the whole evaluation.

The dimension where Armature places highest is organic founder-pain, and the right analog is the Collisons at **Stripe** (2010) -- they had been hacking commerce APIs at **Auctomatic** (2007) before they built the thing they wished existed. Scremin sat inside Joko shipping MCP servers for a real product before Armature existed, which means the Armature pitch -- "you can't see what the agent thought and missed" -- is almost certainly something he hit in his own console at Joko first. Where Armature diverges from the Stripe rung is that the Collisons had already sold Auctomatic for $5M and were writing payments code as teenagers; Armature's organic story is one founder's job, not a decade of artifact-shipping, and the dossier gives me no anecdote of Scremin building an internal Armature-shaped tool at Joko before incorporating. That gap is why I land Armature on the upper-mid of this ladder rather than on the Stripe rung itself.

The pattern that worries me on hacker genealogy is the **Pass Example** AI-wrapper MBA team -- credential-rich, artifact-poor, no shipped code visible to a stranger -- and I have to ask whether Armature shows that pattern. The dossier says Otzenberger studied at École Polytechnique and spent ~5 years at Palantir; Scremin joined Joko as a "Business Operations Analyst" before moving into AI work. Neither founder has a public GitHub. Neither has a Twitter following that the researcher could retrieve. Neither has an open-source contribution named. This is not the Pass Example failure mode -- both founders did ship MCP infrastructure inside their employers -- but the public hacker-tribal trail that makes me lean in on **Dropbox** (2007) or **Reddit** (2005) is conspicuously absent for Armature, and I weight that absence the way "Hackers and Painters" (May 2003) tells me to.

Animal resourcefulness pre-funding -- mechanism analysis. The way this dimension compounds over 18 months is that a founder who pulled an Obama-O's-style stunt before the check tends to pull a comparable stunt again the next time runway tightens; the absence of any such anecdote in Armature's dossier means I have no behavioral evidence to extrapolate from. The site says "We've deployed MCPs used by millions of customers," which is prior-employer scope, not pre-funding Armature hustle. Palantir and Joko appearing as investors is interesting -- ex-employer money is a vouch -- but a vouch is not a stunt. Armature gets the default-leaning-down score here because animals leave tracks and the dossier has none.

Schlep zone -- counterfactual. What would have to be true for Armature to vault from the lower-mid of this ladder to the **Coinbase** (2012) rung? The dossier would need to name a concrete piece of unpleasant offline or regulatory work the Armature founders are personally doing -- SOC2 with audit-log access into customer agent runs, manual harness reverse-engineering for closed systems like ChatGPT apps, or recruiting design partners through cold outbound that no software-only YC team would tolerate. Instead Armature lists Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, ChatGPT, and Gemini CLI as targets and exposes an MCP endpoint back -- this is hard software, but it is software. The non-deterministic-test-signal problem flagged in the risks section is a genuine schlep no incumbent has solved, and that is the one place Armature could plausibly out-grind Braintrust ($80M Series B at $800M, Feb 2026), but the dossier does not claim Armature has.

Co-founder configuration -- single-anchor parallel to **Twitch** (2007). Kan and Shear came back to YC because they had worked together long enough at **Kiko** (2005) that the chemistry was already priced in. Armature has three people on the team, two named, both with Joko on their LinkedIn -- Scremin running MCP work, Otzenberger surfacing the same employer in search snippets. That is real prior-relationship signal, not a hackathon pairing, and Joko itself wrote a check, which is the strongest possible reference. I cannot tell from the dossier how long the two overlapped at Joko or whether the third founder shares that history, so Armature sits above the solo-founder failure mode but below the Chesky-Gebbia-Blecharczyk multi-year configuration of **Airbnb** (2009).

My 36-month forecast: Armature reaches $1-3M ARR on the Pro and Enterprise tiers, signs ~30 design-partner logos through the Palantir/Joko network, and is acquired by Braintrust or Datadog for between $40M and $90M before raising a Series A -- a respectable Auctomatic-shaped exit, not a Stripe-shaped one. The mechanism is the signature: an organic-pain founder without a hacker-tribal trail tends to build a real product that a better-distributed incumbent absorbs, because the product judgment is correct but the surrounding pull -- HN demos, open-source community, Lisp-prize tribal markers -- never materializes to compound into category ownership.

The bull case worth taking seriously: Scremin spent two years shipping MCP servers at Joko, which is more time inside the actual Model Context Protocol than almost any other Spring 2026 applicant has logged, and the non-deterministic-agent-testing problem is exactly the kind of unpleasant work Braintrust ($80M Series B, Feb 2026) and LangSmith ($125M at $1.25B, Oct 2025) keep routing around because passive trace logging is easier to ship than driving live agents through a customer's MCP. If MCP wins the protocol war and Armature accumulates a multi-harness behavior corpus across hundreds of customers, the data moat I have penalized as "[Inferred]" becomes the actual asset and the **Coinbase** (2012) pattern applies -- the regulatory-style grind nobody else wanted becomes the company. What dismantles this for me is that Braintrust already ships an MCP server integration per the dossier, and the founders have not produced a single public artifact -- no HN post, no open-source MCP tool, no demo video -- that would make me believe they will out-ship a venture-backed competitor with 100x the capital. The condition that flips my verdict: Scremin or Otzenberger publishes an open-source MCP testing harness on GitHub that hits the Hacker News front page before the Spring 2026 Demo Day, the way Houston's video flipped **Dropbox** (2007). Until then this is a Neutral, not an Invest.
