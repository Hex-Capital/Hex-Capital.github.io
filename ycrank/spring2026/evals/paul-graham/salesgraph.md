# Salesgraph -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Ricardo Nunez and Ruhan Ponnada were among the first ten employees at Mintlify and built an internal business-case generator there that became the conceptual seed for Salesgraph -- that single sentence in the dossier is the strongest thing in the file, and it tells me what kind of company Salesgraph is: engineers who built the tool they wanted at their last job, now sitting in the easy middle of a market where Gong owns the call layer and the new Clari+Salesloft ($450M combined ARR, Dec 2025) owns everything else. The high signal is organic founder-pain origin. The low signal is that Salesgraph has parked itself in the least schleppy zone imaginable -- LLM orchestration over twelve APIs the incumbents control.

Drew Houston's lost USB stick is the cleanest precedent for what Salesgraph claims to be. Houston built **Dropbox** (2007) because he personally hated forgetting his thumb drive at MIT; the Collisons at **Stripe** (2010) built payments because they had been hacking commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007). The mechanism is the same -- a tool the founders wanted to use before they wanted to sell it. Salesgraph plausibly fits this pattern: Ricardo as full-stack engineer and Ruhan as founding solutions engineer at Mintlify both lived inside the enterprise dev-tools sales motion, and built the predecessor against their own pipeline. Where Salesgraph diverges from the Houston precedent is that Houston shipped his demo to Hacker News before the seed; Salesgraph's predecessor lived inside Mintlify and there is no public artifact -- no demo video, no Show HN, no open-source release -- that did the pre-funding talking for them. The origin story is real; the artifact-on-the-wire is missing.

The polished AI-wrapper company I described in my pass example -- two-MBA team, $50B TAM slide, no shipped artifact, "we'll hire engineers post-funding" -- failed because nothing about its surface required animal behavior, since every layer of the stack was someone else's API. Salesgraph is not that team; Ricardo and Ruhan are engineers who shipped at Mintlify. But the structural problem from the pass example does recur in the Salesgraph dossier: the product is an orchestration layer over Gong, Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, Salesloft, Attio, Grain, Granola, Circleback, Artisan, and Parallel.ai. That is not a schlep. **Coinbase** (2012) was a schlep because Brian Armstrong wanted to do the compliance work no other YC applicant in his batch would touch; **DoorDash** (2013) was a schlep because Tony Xu and his cofounders were going to personally run deliveries. Salesgraph has named no compliance grind, no manual sales motion the founders will personally run, no enterprise-data-cleaning unpleasantness. Twelve API integrations is convenience, not difficulty -- and convenience compresses fast when the API owners ship competing agents, which Gong Engage and Clari's post-merger roadmap have already announced.

Ricardo's GitHub -- @ricardonunez-io with queue-benchmarks comparing Bun vs Go, plus a servercomponents repo, and the self-description "Typescript fella" tied to the Million JS community -- is the kind of hacker tribal marker I notice. Not a Patrick Collison Young Scientist prize, but a real technical-taste signal. Ruhan's hacker genealogy is thinner: Georgia Tech CS 2023, no confirmed public GitHub. The pair is asymmetric on the dimension that has always biased me -- when Patrick paired with John at **Stripe** (2010), both had shipped code; here one founder has a Bun-versus-Go benchmark in public and the other has a LinkedIn that reads like Solutions Engineering, which is closer to applied-CS than to the hacker-painter lineage in "Hackers and Painters" (May 2003). It is not a disqualifier. It is a half-credit.

On co-founder configuration, Salesgraph is a 2-person pair who worked together as early-employees at Mintlify on the exact predecessor product, which is precisely the long-prior-relationship pattern I described in "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010). This is what Huffman and Ohanian at **Reddit** (2005) looked like -- dorm roommates who had built things together before the YC check. The Salesgraph configuration counterfactual is narrow: a solo Ricardo would have dropped this score by half; a freshly-paired hackathon team would have triggered my explicit Red Flag. Neither is the case.

On founder animal resourcefulness, the dossier names no Salesgraph stunt -- no cereal-box equivalent, no Hacker News demo that flipped a rejection, no signed-LOI counterparty extracted from a cold-email sequence, no waitlist counter. The internal Mintlify tool is the closest artifact, but it was built on company time for a company purpose. My default when the dossier is silent on pre-funding hustle is 12/35, because animals leave tracks and I see only the faintest set here. Salesgraph gets a small lift above that default because Ricardo's open-source posture and the documented Mintlify-internal precedent at least prove the founders ship.

My 36-month forecast for Salesgraph: $1-3M ARR by Q2 2029 followed by an acquihire into Gong or the merged Clari+Salesloft entity in the $15-40M range, because the orchestration-layer thesis gets eaten from above when the API owners ship competing agents, and Ricardo and Ruhan are exactly the kind of competent engineers an incumbent buys to staff their own agent team. The mechanism is the one I flagged in "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) -- the absence of a moat-by-unpleasantness means the moat has to come from somewhere else, and over a 36-month window with Gong Engage and the Clari+Salesloft roadmap converging, somewhere else is unlikely to arrive.

The strongest objection to that forecast is that **Stripe** (2010) was also originally a "wrapper" -- the Collisons did not own the underlying payment rails any more than Salesgraph owns Gong's recordings -- and Stripe still became the next Google. The bull case for Salesgraph runs through the same door: Ricardo and Ruhan have organic founder-problem fit, a working predecessor, and a cofounder pair with two years of shared shipping at Mintlify. That sting is real. What dismantles it: Stripe's wrapper was around an interface (card networks) that legally required schleps -- KYC, banking partnerships, regulatory licensing -- which is why the **Stripe** (2010) moat compounded and a Salesgraph-style API-orchestration moat will not. The schlep is the asset, and Salesgraph has not chosen one. The condition that would flip my verdict: Salesgraph ships a proprietary deal-graph dataset with named enterprise design partners within 12 months, or Ricardo publishes a hacker-canonical artifact -- not a benchmark, a real piece of infrastructure -- that does the pre-funding talking they have not done yet. Until then, pass.
