# Lamina Labs -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Lamina Labs is a single-sided AI generation tool with code-native distribution and a generic-ML founder team -- highest on leverage architecture (Simi ships as a free-trial web app at app.laminalabs.ai/simi), lowest on network compounding (the Nth Simi user makes the (N-1)th user no more valuable). That pairing is the shape of Perplexity without the Perplexity-grade specific knowledge or the pre-consensus timing -- and Lamina Labs lands in my pass pile because the override that saved the Perplexity check does not load here.

**Notion** (2013) is the right HIGH rung for leverage architecture: Ivan Zhao distributed a builder-tool through a free web app, no App Store, no SDR motion, and the product itself was the channel. Lamina Labs distributes the same way at the top of the funnel -- laminalabs.ai serves a free trial of Simi, no pricing gate, no enterprise procurement on slide one. Where Lamina Labs diverges from the Notion template is the founder-led sales seam: the homepage routes to lamina-labs.cal.com/lamina for founder calls, and the YC positioning is "visual infrastructure for EdTech AI applications" -- infrastructure framing usually means an API motion that turns into B2B sales the moment the first three logos show interest. Code leverage is present in Lamina Labs, but it is not the *only* leverage, and that compromises the upside math at angel stage.

The LOW rung on network compounding is the kind of single-sided SaaS product I have spent twenty years declining: each Lamina Labs customer renders their own whiteboard explainer and walks away, and the next customer renders theirs independently. Simi is a generation tool, not a network -- the dossier names no shared template library, no creator marketplace, no embed graph, no second side. The Perplexity exception is the only path I have to fund a single-sided AI app, and Perplexity cleared because Aravind Srinivas's Berkeley retrieval PhD was extreme specific knowledge against a pre-consensus category. Lamina Labs has neither: Golpo AI's $4.1M seed in October 2025 from BNVT, Emergence, YC, and Afore is the visible mark that the consensus has already arrived in AI explainer video, and Kartikesh Mishra's CSAIL work was on training drones to follow natural-language commands under Daniela Rus -- credible ML, not deterministic generative video for education.

Specific knowledge in Lamina Labs is the dimension where I want to talk myself into a higher score and cannot. Counterfactual: for Lamina Labs to clear the criterion #3 bar that Stack Overflow's Atwood-and-Spolsky cleared, I would need to see Mishra or Rokaya shipping an open-source animation engine, a Manim contribution graph, or a multi-year personal obsession with deterministic educational video predating the YC application. Instead the dossier shows Mishra's top GitHub repo at 14 stars and 22 followers (github.com/mk314k), Rokaya with no public repos, and the public artifact is one LinkedIn post titled "Leaving MIT to build the next generation." MIT EECS plus CSAIL is intelligence and energy; it is not the specific knowledge that makes the leverage compound.

The market dimension for Lamina Labs sits inside the "first idea never works" axiom and the question is whether EdTech-explainer-video gives Mishra and Rokaya pivot-room. Eighteen-month mechanism: if Simi's wedge is the cited 8-11 second render time and Pictor follows as a second SKU, the natural pivots are corporate training video, marketing explainers, and a developer-facing API for "visual infrastructure" -- three adjacent vectors inside a horizontal AI video market that Synthesia has shown is real at >$150M raised. The dossier gives no TAM, and the YC tagline "visual infrastructure for EdTech AI applications" telegraphs vertical-first positioning, which is the inverse of the Twitter (2007) / Uber (2010) pivot-room template. Default applies, with slight upside for the adjacency map.

Mishra and Rokaya read clean on the Buffett trio -- intelligence (MIT EECS, CSAIL), energy (Rokaya on leave, Mishra shipping at MEng), no integrity red flag in the dossier, no contested prior exit, no self-promotion about ethics. Parallel: this is the founder profile Stack Overflow had at seed minus the prior-shipped-artifact ledger Joel Spolsky brought from Fog Creek. I apply the silence-is-neutral default; the trio passes the check-writing gate but does not push the score above midpoint without a 10-year-relationship signal the dossier does not contain.

**36-month forecast for Lamina Labs**: acqui-hired into Coursera, Khan Academy, or a corporate-training incumbent at sub-$30M total consideration by mid-2029, or wound down -- driven by Golpo AI's $4.1M October 2025 lead converting into category-default before Lamina Labs reaches Series A, with Mishra and Rokaya's MIT EECS pedigree clearing the talent bar for an aqui-hire even if Simi never reaches $2M ARR. The signature pushes here: single-sided generation tool plus generic specific knowledge in a now-consensus category does not produce the 10,000x geometry I need; it produces a credible team that gets a soft landing.

**The strongest bull case** is the Perplexity exception itself: Lamina Labs is a single-sided AI app distributed by code, founded by MIT ML researchers, in a category where the buyer (educators, course creators) is structurally a builder-segment, which is the unstated pattern I have over-indexed on for fifteen years. If that pattern fires, Lamina Labs is in the Notion / Replit / Quill Notes sleeve and the network-compounding miss is forgiven. It stings because builder-tools-for-builders is the exact lens no other angel on this list uses, and the dossier hands me a thread to pull. The thread breaks on two facts: Aravind Srinivas brought a retrieval PhD when Perplexity was pre-consensus, and Lamina Labs brings drone-RL research into a category where Golpo AI just priced the consensus round. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is Lamina Labs shipping the "visual infrastructure" API with three named third-party EdTech applications integrating it inside twelve months -- if Simi becomes the engine other builders ship on, the network compounding I marked absent retroactively appears, and I write the next check.
