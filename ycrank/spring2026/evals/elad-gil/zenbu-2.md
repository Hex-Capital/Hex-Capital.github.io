# Zenbu -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Rob Pruzan shipped React Scan to 800k weekly installs (YC page, 2026) and is now building Zenbu into a category where Cursor already prints $2B ARR (TechCrunch, Feb 2026) and OpenAI owns Windsurf via a $3B acquisition — Zenbu is the rare profile of a top-decile devtools shipper landing in a market my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy would put squarely in the "winners declared" bucket. That asymmetry — founder execution at the **Perplexity** (2022) tier, distribution position at the **Lyft** Series C tier — is Zenbu's signature, and it determines the entire evaluation.

Aravind Srinivas is the rung I measure Zenbu's founder against — the **Perplexity** (2022) pattern was a founder who "showed up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and Rob Pruzan's actual artifacts make the analogy load-bearing rather than rhetorical: ex-Vercel Next.js core team, co-author of React Scan at 22k GitHub stars and 800k weekly installs, plus zenbu.js as a precursor framework already in public. The divergence from the **Perplexity** rung is not founder rhythm — it's that Aravind shipped into a search market where Google's grip was a *liability* and the LLM catalyst was unambiguous; Pruzan is shipping into an IDE market where the catalyst already minted Cursor at $2B ARR before Zenbu wrote its first line. Same execution archetype, wrong window phase.

**Lyft** Series C is the rung where I lost money by being right, and the failure mechanism was simple: in a winner-take-all-or-oligopoly market, the #2 captures a fraction of #1's economics regardless of product quality (20VC interview). I trace that exact pattern through Zenbu's dossier. Cursor at $2B ARR and ~40M users is the Uber; Windsurf inside OpenAI is the inevitable Microsoft-coupled #2; Superset (YC P26) is shipping "IDE to orchestrate 100+ coding agents" in Zenbu's own batch (Hacker News Launch HN, May 2026); and the underlying Pi agent isn't even Zenbu's — it's github.com/badlogic/pi-mono, an external open-source dependency. That is not an index position on AI coding; that is a thin plugin-shell betting that "hackability" inverts share against a $50B-pre incumbent (TechCrunch, April 2026). My *AI Market Clarity* piece (Nov 2025) named this category structure explicitly — AI IDEs have current leaders.

Mechanism analysis on the market re-opening: multi-agent coding workflows did become viable in 2025–26 as Cursor compounded from $1B to $2B ARR in roughly a quarter, and "reviewing changes nobody wrote by hand" is a real new burden — that's a genuine catalyst, not a fabricated one. But catalysts compound for the *first* infrastructure layer to occupy the workflow path; by the 18-month mark of a catalyst this loud, the equilibrium is set by who has enterprise contracts and 40M-user distribution, not by who has the most editable TypeScript plugin surface. Zenbu is arriving at month ~18 of a window that opened in late 2024.

Counterfactual on revenue velocity: to flip this dimension from absent-evidence default into **Harvey**-template territory ($3B→$5B→$8B in months, TechCrunch Nov 2025), Zenbu would need to disclose a curve — $50K MRR in March compounding to $200K in May, named enterprise pilots, anything. The dossier discloses none: no pricing page, no revenue signal, no Product Hunt launch, 222 stars on zenbu.js, and Rob Pruzan's own Twitter at 4,431 followers. In an AI category where "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863), silence is itself the signal — Cursor and Cline both disclosed their curves at this stage.

Single-miracle parallel: **Color Genomics** (2015) worked because we isolated the miracle to a workflow layer on top of commodity sequencing, not the chemistry itself. Zenbu's structure is analogous in form — a hackable IDE shell on top of the Pi agent, isolating the miracle to "developers will adopt an editable IDE wrapper." That is genuinely one miracle, not three. The complication the **Color** parallel doesn't have: Zenbu's "commodity layer" (Pi) is maintained by a single external author, and "developers adopt editable IDE wrapper" is a miracle Cursor has already disproven by winning with the *opposite* product philosophy.

**36-month forecast:** Zenbu does not reach $10M independent ARR; Rob Pruzan's React Scan distribution muscle gets the company acquihired by Vercel, Replit, or a mid-tier AI tooling buyer for sub-$50M in 2027–2028, or zenbu.js pivots into a standalone open-source framework while the IDE product is wound down. The mechanism is the **Lyft** mechanism: a credentialed shipper in a winners-declared market harvests an exit on the founder's prior distribution, not the product's category position.

The bull case stings and I want to name it honestly: Cursor itself was a VS Code fork competing against GitHub Copilot when Copilot was the Microsoft-backed incumbent, and Cursor won on a "faster, more hackable" wedge — exactly Zenbu's pitch. Rob Pruzan has shipped a devtool to 800k weekly installs, which is closer to the Cursor-team profile than 99% of YC batchmates. If the "plugin-editable IDE" wedge compounds the way Cursor's "predictive autocomplete" wedge did, this is a 100x angel check. What dismantles the bull case is timing arithmetic: Cursor entered when the incumbent was Copilot at low-hundreds-of-millions ARR and shipping slowly; Zenbu enters with Cursor at $2B ARR shipping weekly, OpenAI's capital behind Windsurf, Cline at $32M funded (GlobeNewswire, July 2025), and Superset inside Zenbu's own YC batch. That is the *post-consolidation* phase of an AI category, not the *re-opening* phase, and my entire framework is built around not confusing the two. **Flip condition:** Zenbu discloses $1M+ ARR within 12 months OR Vercel/Replit announces a distribution partnership — either would prove the Pruzan-distribution wedge is real and I'd take the meeting at a markup.
