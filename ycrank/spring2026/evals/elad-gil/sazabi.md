# Sazabi -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Sherwood Callaway has the shipping rhythm I look for and almost none of the revenue curve I require -- Sazabi is the analytical inverse of **Harvey** (2023), where I paid up because the dollars confirmed the founder story; here the founder story is real but the dossier itself admits "platform delivery scheduled by end of 2026" against a direct competitor, Dash0, that just printed $110M at a $1B post in March 2026. That pairing -- credentialed shipper, no curve -- is exactly the "false signal vs. just working" boundary I drew in the TechCrunch piece (Nov 2025), and Sazabi sits on the wrong side of it today.

Callaway is the strongest dimension for Sazabi, and the analogy that loads is **Perplexity** (2022): Aravind pinged me cold and showed up a week later with the thing built, which is the only legible signal at pre-seed. Callaway's GitHub footprint -- semantic-cache-chatbot, gmail-mcp-server, dspy-gepa-example -- plus selling Opkit (YC W23) to 11x and then running AI infra there for "Alice," described in the Zypsy memo as "one of the largest agentic systems in production," is genuine shipping history, not pedigree theater. Where Sazabi diverges from the **Perplexity** rung is that Aravind shipped a *consumer-visible artifact* I could use the day we talked; Sazabi's site shows a waitlist and a chat demo with Ed Carrel and Henry Ventura, not a binary I can point at the next production incident, and that gap is what separates a 16/20 from a 19/20 on this axis.

The pattern that kills companies on dimension two is **Lyft** Series C: a real market, a real team, but a #1 already at scale with the same product-market positioning -- I passed on winner-take-all dynamics and it was the correct framework even though Lyft survived. Sazabi triggers the same pattern in a sharper form. Dash0 raised $155M cumulative at a $1B valuation in March 2026 with the *literally identical* "AI-native observability" positioning, and Sazabi exited stealth in April 2026 with no revenue, no pilot conversion data, and platform GA deferred to end of 2026. Per my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025), this is a category where the well-funded entrant is already ahead on every legible axis except "we focus on early-stage startups," which is the same segment defense Lyft tried and which collapses the moment Dash0 ships a startup tier.

Coding agents -- Claude Code, Cursor, Codex -- generating production code that humans didn't write is a real catalyst, and Sazabi's "AI behavior as a new class of telemetry" framing is on the right side of it; if that compounds over the next 18 months the way LLM agent output compounded since GPT-4, the debugging-surface-area argument becomes load-bearing rather than rhetorical. But this catalyst is softer than the post-Ukraine defense window that opened **Anduril** (2022) or the post-November-2022 ChatGPT moment that re-priced **Mistral** -- it's a tailwind for *all* observability vendors, including Datadog (47,431+ customers, per OpenObserve 2026) and Dash0, not a specific re-opening that uniquely advantages Sazabi.

For Sazabi to flip from a 7/15 to a 12/15 on index position, one thing would need to be true: the "logs are just events, metrics are aggregated events, traces are correlated events" thesis would need a published third-party benchmark showing log-only fidelity at p99 latency for a real workload. Right now Sazabi has SOC 2, SOC 1, ISO 27017, ISO 27001, and GDPR -- which is table stakes, not a position -- and a "Perfect Memory" feature with zero customer-tenure data. Without the benchmark, Sazabi has to win share *against* Datadog and Dash0 rather than sit in a path they all integrate against, which is the **Stripe**-on-e-commerce template I actually score highly on.

Compare Sazabi's miracle architecture to **Color Genomics** (2015), which I co-founded specifically to be workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing rather than a chemistry bet. Sazabi is attempting the same maneuver -- workflow AI on top of commodity log ingestion -- but it carries 1.5 miracles, not one: the validated LLM-triage capability is miracle one, and "logs alone are sufficient for production observability" is miracle 0.5 because it's a contested technical claim that the dossier itself flags as "unproven for full-fidelity observability." That's not fusion-level multi-miracle, but it isn't the clean single-miracle isolation that made the **Color** structure defensible either.

My 36-month forecast for Sazabi: acquired by Datadog or New Relic for $40-120M as an acqui-hire-plus-tech tuck-in by mid-2028, with Callaway running an "AI observability" pod inside the acquirer; the alternative is a pivot to specifically debugging coding-agent output (the actual differentiated wedge buried in the dossier) at sub-$5M ARR. The mechanism is straightforward -- Dash0 wins the AI-native general-purpose category because it shipped first and raised the war chest, Datadog wins the enterprise default, and Sazabi's "startup-focused" defense collapses into the same #2-in-a-consolidating-market pattern that defined my **Lyft** pass.

The strongest bull case against this forecast is the backer graph: individuals from Vercel, Graphite, Daytona, Browserbase, LangChain, Mastra, and Replit are listed as backers on sazabi.com, plus Village Global and Agent Fund. That's the exact distribution graph for selling AI-native observability to the AI-native startups generating the coding-agent output Sazabi instruments -- if those eight companies become design partners with logo-able usage in the next two quarters, Sazabi has the **Decagon** (2023) shape, not the **Lyft** shape, because the distribution is pre-wired into the catalyst. I dismantle it on one fact: the dossier shows zero named pilot conversions and a waitlist funnel as of April 2026, and "backer endorsements" without paying-customer logos is the exact false signal I called out for AI in TechCrunch (Nov 2025). The verifiable condition that flips my verdict from Neutral to Invest is concrete: signed paying pilots with three or more of the named backer companies (Vercel, Cursor-tier, Replit-tier) disclosed before YE 2026, with month-over-month log-volume growth that Callaway will put in writing.
