# WithAI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

WithAI is the rare credentialed-insider bet where the organic-idea axis is the strongest signal and the animal-resourcefulness axis is the weakest — Ian McInnis literally ran "how can we use LLMs to invest?" inside Bridgewater while holding an equity book, then walked out to build the tool he had been asked to imagine. That pairing — high organic fit, soft pre-funding stunt — is what WithAI is, and the rest of the evaluation flows from it.

The **Dropbox** (2007) analogy carries the organic-idea paragraph: Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and built the thing he personally wanted, and Patrick Collison had been hacking commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007) before **Stripe** (2010). WithAI sits one rung below Dropbox on this axis because Ian McInnis didn't merely encounter the problem — he was paid to think about it inside the world's largest hedge fund, which is closer to the Collisons' situation than to Houston's. The dossier specific that pins WithAI here is that Bridgewater's two co-CIOs, Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour, are angels in the company — buyers of the product effectively wrote the seed check. Where WithAI diverges downward from Dropbox is the absence of a "I built it for myself first, then noticed others wanted it" artifact — there's no equivalent of the Hacker News demo video, just a polished website and a calendar booking link at cal.com/imj-mcinnis.

The low-rung paragraph belongs to my polished-MBA-AI-wrapper pass case, where the failure mechanism is that founders skipped the pre-funding hustle and went straight to enterprise sales motion. WithAI partially echoes this — the path from Princeton '23 to Bridgewater to YC Spring 2026 with co-CIO angels is the most greased pre-seed path I've seen this batch, and the dossier contains no equivalent of Brian Armstrong's hand-built Android wallet that rescued **Coinbase** (2012) after rejection, no cereal-box stunt, no demo that flipped a skeptic. The mitigating fact is that WithAI did ship — >$100K ARR with multiple clients three months post-launch, and Mercator Partners by name reporting a workflow flip from "80% gathering, 20% acting" to the reverse. That is revenue, and revenue is a real animal signal. But it was won with warm Bridgewater intros, not stunts. WithAI passes the animal test on outcome, not on the path that earned it.

On the schlep axis, the mechanism that compounds over 18 months is unglamorous and on WithAI's side. Deploying single-tenant inside a hedge fund's AWS/Azure/GCP, surviving security review, and wiring up 60+ integrations including Bloomberg, Refinitiv, FactSet, Tegus, AlphaSense, Snowflake and Databricks is exactly the work Hebbia (multi-tenant SaaS, $130M Series B, $700M valuation) is structurally unwilling to do. This is the **Coinbase** (2012) shape — the unpleasantness is the moat. WithAI's competitive set wants horizontal SaaS unit economics; WithAI is deliberately taking the consultative, white-glove path the YC page describes. Eighteen months in, that posture either compounds into per-fund ontology no competitor can replicate, or it caps WithAI at a services-revenue ceiling. The dossier doesn't yet distinguish which.

For hacker genealogy, the counterfactual test is sharp: if Ben Finch weren't co-founder, WithAI would lose this dimension entirely. He is Princeton ECE Magna Cum Laude with an MS, founding researcher and chief of staff at Sentient Labs, co-author on "OML: Open, Monetizable, and Loyal AI" at IACR 2024/1573, and has a GitHub presence at @BinFench. That is real hacker lineage on the technical side. Ian McInnis has no public GitHub — he is the buyer-side founder, not the builder-side one. For WithAI's score to flip a tier on this dimension I'd need to see a shipped technical artifact from Ben — an OSS framework, a HN-front-page demo of the agent orchestration — and the dossier doesn't surface one beyond the academic paper.

**Stripe** (2010) is the single anchor on co-founder configuration: the Collison brothers had years of working-together history through **Auctomatic** (2007) before the payments check. WithAI's Ian and Ben met at Princeton and co-founded a technology investment firm together before WithAI, which is real prior collaboration but shorter and lower-stakes than the Collison pattern. They are a three-person team and the dossier names only two; the third founder is invisible in the research, which is the kind of ambiguity that the YC interview resolves and I cannot.

My 36-month forecast: WithAI lands at $4-10M ARR by mid-2029 and either gets acquired by S&P Global, FactSet, or MSCI in the $150-400M range, or survives as a profitable niche tool serving 30-80 fundamental equity funds — but it does not become a fund-returner, because Rogo's $75M Series C at $750M post-money in Jan 2026 with Sequoia plus an OpenAI collaboration is already moving into the hedge fund workflow space WithAI claims, and the buyer-side angels who got WithAI in the door are the same angels who will introduce Rogo six months later. The mechanism producing this ceiling is exactly the signature: organic idea + soft hustle = real company, not Black Swan.

The strongest bear case against my own merely-neutral lean is that I'm under-weighting the Bridgewater co-CIO signal. Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour writing angel checks into a tool for their own asset class is closer to the **Loopt** (2005) Known Exception than I admitted — Sam Altman got funded on force-of-personality with weak product-market-fit timing, and WithAI has the inverse: weaker hustle signal but excellent insider distribution. The bull would say: this is the only YC company in Spring 2026 whose angels can call any fundamental hedge fund CIO in the country. The dossier evidence that wins the dismantling, though, is that the warm-intro distribution model is exactly what loses against Rogo's $143M+ of cumulative capital — insider intros don't compound the way **Stripe** (2010)'s developer channel did, because each hedge fund sale is bespoke. The condition that flips my verdict from Neutral to Invest is straightforward: WithAI passes 10 named hedge fund logos and $2M+ ARR by EOY 2026, demonstrating that the consultative motion scales beyond the Bridgewater Rolodex.
