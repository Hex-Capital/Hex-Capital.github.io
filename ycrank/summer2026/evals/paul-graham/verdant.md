# Verdant -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Verdant's signature is a real municipal schlep being attacked by a Georgetown Government undergraduate two years after **GovWell** ($34.5M raised, 130+ municipal customers across 34 states by May 2026) showed up with the same product wedge -- the work is genuinely unpleasant in the **Coinbase** (2012) sense, but Verdant has not produced the pre-funding artifact or biographical fit that would convince me Aidan Ng and Jason Yi are the team to outrun an Insight Partners-backed incumbent in a procurement cycle measured in years.

On the schlep dimension Verdant is the most interesting thing in the dossier. Selling AI plan-review software to 19,000 American municipalities is exactly the work other YC applicants route around -- the City of Astoria audit cited in the Civic IQ blog listed five vendors per evaluation, the average Accela contract is ~$382K, and nobody in the S26 batch dreaming up another Slack-bot wants to spend two years learning the difference between a CUP and a variance. **Coinbase** (2012) is the right rhyme: Brian Armstrong's appetite for compliance grind was the asset, not the bitcoin thesis. Where Verdant diverges from **Coinbase** is that Armstrong had hand-built an Android wallet before he walked in, and the Verdant dossier shows me a Calendly link and a marketing page -- the schlep is real, but I have no evidence the founders have personally bled in it yet.

Stripe Collison-brothers pattern is the one I run on hacker genealogy, and Verdant fails it cleanly. Patrick won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp work before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz had the RSS spec before **Reddit** (2005); Houston had the **Dropbox** (2007) demo on Hacker News before the seed. Verdant gives me a Government major at Georgetown affiliated with a policy lab, a co-founder whose LinkedIn headline is not even retrievable, and zero GitHub footprint for either. Hackers leave trails and I'm not finding any. This isn't the **Loopt** (2005) exception where Altman's animal personality overrode the technology timing -- there's no force-of-personality signal in the dossier either.

Animal resourcefulness pre-funding is where this gets thin. Eighteen months from now the founders who win in govtech will be the ones who personally drove to a county planning office in Modesto, sat next to the reviewer for a week, and shipped a one-jurisdiction prototype that the staff actually used on Monday morning -- the **Airbnb** (2009) door-to-door photography pattern from "Do Things that Don't Scale" (July 2013). Verdant's dossier contains no such stunt: no named pilot municipality, no signed LOI, no unscalable manual review the founders ran themselves on a real applicant packet. The absence compounds because GovWell will use its $25M Series A to buy the procurement officers Verdant hasn't yet met.

Organic founder-pain: the counterfactual that flips Verdant a tier here is straightforward -- if Aidan Ng had spent two years as a planner in a municipal permitting office and built this because the Accela queue was destroying his weekends, I'd score this like Houston's USB stick. The dossier instead gives me "backgrounds in public service" (unverified) and a Georgetown undergraduate transcript. That's the AI-wrapper-MBA-team distance from the product, not the **Dropbox** (2007) distance.

Co-founder configuration is the one place Verdant escapes a penalty by being unremarkable: two founders, which is the right number, but the dossier surfaces no prior working relationship -- no shared employer, no shared school, no shared open-source repo. Compare to **Reddit** (2005), where Huffman and Ohanian were dorm roommates. I'm holding at the default; the YC interview would tell me what the dossier can't.

My 36-month forecast: Verdant is acquihired by GovWell or an Accela/Tyler incumbent for sub-$5M, or dead, having failed to cross $300K ARR because municipal procurement cycles outlast pre-seed runway and the competitor with 130 customers and $25M from Insight has already eaten the early-adopter segment. The mechanism is simple -- in enterprise govtech the second-mover with the same wedge and a tenth the capital does not catch up; this isn't a consumer market where distribution can be re-won with a viral loop.

The steel-man cuts the other way: 19,000 municipalities means GovWell's 130 customers is 0.7% penetration, the market is structurally fragmented, and incumbent Accela contracts churning at renewal is real oxygen. **Airbnb** (2009) is the Known Exception that haunts this -- I rejected the idea in my head and the founders overrode me with cereal boxes. The condition that would flip my verdict: Verdant shows me three signed pilot municipalities with named counterparties in the next six months and one of the founders has personally sat in a planning department doing manual review for a month. Until then this is the clever-idea-soft-founders inverse of the pattern that makes me money. Pass.
