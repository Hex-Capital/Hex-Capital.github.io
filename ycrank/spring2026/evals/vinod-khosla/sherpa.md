# Sherpa -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Sherpa is what happens when two competent ex-Lindy growth engineers automate the workflow they used to run by hand -- Norbu Sonam literally "tripled paywall conversion" running 50+ growth experiments at Lindy, and now ships a JS snippet that does the same for SMBs. The dimensional signature is unambiguous: Sherpa scores its highest on founder learning rate (real, embodied, dossier-evidenced) and its lowest on consequence magnitude (a $1.7B CRO market growing to $5B by 2035 is the definition of an optimization play, not a civilizational variable). That pairing tells me exactly what this is: a well-built efficiency tool sold into a category that already exists, by people who know the workflow cold. That is a fundable company. It is not my company.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence, and the analogy is load-bearing only insofar as both Sherpa and OpenAI ship software -- and that is where the analogy ends. The success-state of OpenAI was the redefinition of what a machine can do; the success-state of Sherpa at maximum ambition is that VWO, Optimizely, and the Everstone-combined Wingify/AB Tasty $100M+ platform lose share to an autonomous-variant agent that captures a chunk of an 11.6% CAGR market. No incumbent regime collapses. No labor market gets displaced at the $15T scale I keep citing from Fortune (2026-03-06) -- in fact the explicit Sherpa pitch is to replace the "designer, developer, and analyst" loop *inside one workflow at one buyer*, which is the exact shape of the GPT-sales-call-summarizer pass I described in my own framework. Sherpa lands between the LOW rung pass example and a MID rung, closer to LOW.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) failed because the underlying science miracle did not converge -- catalyst, feedstock, refinery integration each had to work, and the capital-intensity was the moat. That is the LOW-rung failure mechanism for technical risk done right: real miracles, real capital, real consequence if any single one hits. Sherpa's "miracle" is generating on-brand HTML/CSS variants with an LLM, a capability VWO already ships via GPT-3.5 (VWO blog, 2025) and Kameleoon markets explicitly as "AI for A/B Testing." There is no physics, no biology, no fleet, no plant. The Sherpa dossier names exactly zero independent technical breakthroughs that a Tier-1 generalist would decline to underwrite -- this is software a YC SAFE funds to MVP, and the absence of repelling capital risk is itself the signal.

Counterfactual on founder-defies-expert: for Sherpa to flip from a low score to a mid score on this dimension, Ethan Kinnan or Norbu Sonam would need to have publicly contradicted the prevailing CRO orthodoxy -- a position like "A/B testing as currently practiced is statistically fraudulent at SMB sample sizes" or "the entire experimentation industry is measuring noise." Instead the Sherpa dossier reads as pure expert continuity: Norbu ran growth experiments at Lindy, and now sells growth-experiment automation. Ethan was Lindy's first forward-deployed engineer and Head of Partnerships, and Sherpa's GTM is forward-deployed JS snippet plus partnership distribution into the Lindy-adjacent YC network (Lindy, Insforge, Gauge as the three named logos). This is the McKinsey-1986-cellphone-forecast pattern in reverse -- the founders are extrapolating their last job, not betraying it.

Mechanism analysis on learning rate over 18 months: the Sherpa dataset claim of 7,338,896+ sessions is the only compounding asset in the dossier, and the question is whether cross-customer experiment outcomes train a model that predicts winners pre-deployment faster than VWO and the Everstone-combined Wingify/AB Tasty ($100M+ Jan 2026) can layer the same loop onto their existing customer bases. Norbu's documented "50+ growth experiments" at Lindy is real learning-rate evidence -- it is the rare pre-seed dossier where the CTO has personally executed the exact workload the product automates -- and that is why Sherpa scores meaningfully above the absent-evidence default here. But there is no documented pivot, no public thesis update, no "we were wrong about X" moment of the kind that justified the $100M Series C into **Slash** (2024). Sherpa earns a mid score, not a high one.

Single-anchor parallel on capital profile: **Genesis AI** (2025) took a $105M co-led seed because robot foundation models require fleets, simulation infrastructure, and multi-year training runs -- the capital itself was the moat. Sherpa's economics are the inverse. A two-person team in San Francisco with a JS snippet, zero job postings on the YC page, and named SMB customers reaches $1M ARR on $3-5M of YC-and-seed capital. I am structurally over-resourced for Sherpa, and every Tier-1 generalist plus every applied-AI seed fund can write this check. That is not a Sherpa flaw; it is a Sherpa-Khosla mismatch.

My 36-month forecast: Sherpa reaches $2-6M ARR by mid-2029, gets pulled into an acqui-hire or strategic acquisition by Statsig, the Everstone-combined Wingify/AB Tasty platform, or Optimizely at a $25-80M outcome, as incumbent AI-variant features close the differentiation gap and the dataset moat fails to compound past the founder-network customer concentration (Lindy, Insforge, Gauge). The mechanism is the dimensional signature: high founder learning rate gets Sherpa to credible ARR; low consequence magnitude caps the terminal value at "useful feature, not company."

The strongest bull case I can construct from the dossier is the **DoorDash** (2013) exception -- a capital-light consumer/SMB business with no science miracle that I funded anyway because the consequence-of-success reshaped a flow at scale, and which returned a $72B IPO in 2020. Could Sherpa be that exception? The Lindy customer testimonial of +30% conversion across 40 experiments in 30 days is the bull-case anchor, and if that lift generalizes outside the founder-network the SaaS attach rate across SMB websites is genuinely large. The bear answer is that DoorDash's success-state restructured how restaurant inventory reaches consumers -- a flow with network effects between two-sided participants. Sherpa's success-state is "more conversions on landing pages," a flow with no network effect between customers and a category in which Kameleoon already books $22.1M revenue (GetLatka, 2024) and the combined Everstone platform crossed $100M (TechCrunch, Jan 2026) without anyone calling it civilizational. I pass. The verifiable condition that flips this: Sherpa demonstrates a non-network customer cohort (say, 25+ logos outside the Lindy/YC orbit) producing a median +20% conversion lift at 90-day follow-up, AND publishes a cross-customer model that beats single-customer A/B baselines -- at that point the dataset moat is real and the consequence frame can expand. Until then, this is the pass example in my own framework, written almost word-for-word.
