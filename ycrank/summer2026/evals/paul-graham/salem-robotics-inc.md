# Salem Robotics Inc -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Salem Robotics Inc is the rare pre-seed where the founder-problem fit is almost embarrassingly tight -- Caleb Horan spent five years at Los Alamos and Janak Panthi six years deploying quadrupeds in nuclear facilities before they incorporated -- and yet the dossier contains not one instance of the unscalable stunt or pre-funding artifact that flips me from "interesting" to "check written." The signature is organic-idea-high, animal-resourcefulness-low: two PhD researchers automating the exact chore they personally did in radiation suits, with a sim demo and five "active relationships" but zero named revenue. That is a specific, diagnosable shape.

On the organic-idea ladder, Salem Robotics Inc sits closer to Drew Houston at **Dropbox** (2007) than to anyone in the middle of my portfolio. Houston kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT and built a tool for himself; Horan and Panthi walked contaminated rooms at LANL taking manual instrument readings and paper compliance notes, then co-authored "Radiation Surveys in Active Nuclear Facilities with Heterogeneous Collaborative Mobile Robots" (arXiv 2402.15008) before they had a company. The mechanism that makes this analogy load-bearing is the same in both cases -- the founder's daily annoyance became the product's spec sheet. Where Salem Robotics Inc diverges from Houston is that Houston shipped a viral demo video to Hacker News that people wanted to use the next day, whereas Salem Robotics Inc's artifact is a peer-reviewed paper and a simulation. Both are organic, but one has audience-shaped feedback loops and the other has journal-shaped ones.

The pass I keep imagining is the polished AI-wrapper MBA team with a deck and a TAM slide -- no shipped artifact, no personal pain, no revenue via stunt. Salem Robotics Inc does not resemble that team on organic-idea grounds, but on animal-resourcefulness the pattern uncomfortably rhymes: the dossier lists "active relationships with five commercial nuclear plants" without qualifying whether any is paid, "pilot scoping" without a signed LOI counterparty named, and an end-to-end demo "in high-fidelity simulation" rather than in a plant. Brian Armstrong hand-built an Android bitcoin wallet after **Coinbase** (2012) was rejected; the Salem Robotics Inc equivalent would be pushing a Spot through a decommissioning site on a weekend using borrowed credentials, and I do not see it. Absence is evidence here -- animals leave tracks, and the tracks in this dossier are academic tracks.

Salem Robotics Inc sits in a genuine schlep zone, and this is the dimension where I lean in hardest. Nuclear vendor qualification, NRC reporting, DOE site access, radiological safety clearances -- this is the **Coinbase** (2012) compliance grind wearing a different hazmat suit, and it is exactly the kind of work Gecko Robotics's $1.25B valuation (CNBC, June 2025) demonstrates the market will pay for once you survive it. Over 18 months, this compounds: every survey Salem Robotics Inc runs generates auditable records that themselves become the switching cost, because the next auditor wants continuity with the last one. Schlep blindness is doing its job here -- no software-first YC batchmate is going to touch nuclear.

The hacker-genealogy question is a counterfactual: what would have to be true for me to move Salem Robotics Inc a tier higher on this axis? A public GitHub with the mission-planning stack open-sourced, or Horan's VLM autonomy work released as a package with stars, or either founder maintaining a nontrivial open project pre-startup. The dossier explicitly returns "No public repos found" for both. Peer-reviewed publication is one form of hacker signal, but it is credential-shaped where I want artifact-shaped -- Patrick Collison won the Young Scientist for Lisp *and* shipped Auctomatic. Salem Robotics Inc has the first half.

Horan and Panthi are the strongest structural check-mark Salem Robotics Inc gets: both PhDs in the UT Austin Nuclear and Applied Robotics Group, both LANL alumni, co-published, 2+ years shared lab time. This is the **Reddit** (2005) dorm-roommate pattern with a hazard rating -- long prior working relationship, complementary coverage (CEO with radiation-inspection VLM research, CTO with quadruped product experience and U.S. Army background). No solo-founder red flag, no hackathon-pair red flag.

My 36-month forecast for Salem Robotics Inc: two to four paid pilots totaling $400K-$1.5M ARR with commercial nuclear operators, no in-facility autonomous production deployment at scale, and a Series A led by a strategic (Constellation-type utility or Gecko itself as an acqui-hire) rather than a pure-tech VC -- the organic-idea and schlep dimensions carry them into revenue, the missing animal moment caps the trajectory below breakout. This forecast follows directly from the signature: deep-domain founders with real problem fit but no evidence of the unscalable hustle that turns a good company into **Airbnb** (2009).

The strongest case against my forecast is the **Coinbase** (2012) parallel: Armstrong was not obviously an animal in his written application either -- he was a serious, credentialed Airbnb engineer running toward a regulatory schlep, and the wallet prototype only surfaced after rejection. If Horan or Panthi shows up in-batch with a Spot-and-radiation-payload demo inside a real DOE site rather than a simulation, Salem Robotics Inc is the same shape as Armstrong's rescue. The dossier evidence that keeps me from that bet today is the simulation-only status of the end-to-end system and the unqualified "active relationships" language -- Armstrong shipped a working artifact into a hostile environment, and Salem Robotics Inc has shipped a working artifact into Gazebo. Verifiable flip condition: one signed paid contract with a named commercial nuclear operator or DOE site, at any dollar amount, within the S26 batch window. That flips this from Neutral to Invest and I would double the check.
