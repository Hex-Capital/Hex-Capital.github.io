# Gojiberry AI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is a profitable SMB SaaS run by serial bootstrappers whose learning-rate signal is the only dimension that registers in my framework -- everything else is the pattern from my **Pass Example**, the "GPT-4 summarizes sales calls and integrates with HubSpot" pitch I pass on in thirty seconds. The signature pairing here is HIGH on documented founder pivot (the v1-with-2,000-waitlist-and-zero-conversions rebuild is real evidence) and LOW on consequence magnitude (an "AI GTM agent" at $99/month does not collapse any incumbent regime). When I run my Entry Point question -- what world-state exists if Gojiberry AI hits maximum ambition -- the answer is "Apollo.io has a sharper competitor and SDRs at sub-50-employee firms get cheaper." That is not a civilizational variable. That is a feature.

**Cognition** (2024) is the right HIGH rung to test Gojiberry AI against on consequence, because both companies sell themselves into the "AI replaces labor" narrative I anchor to in Fortune 2026-03-06 -- $15T of U.S. GDP is labor, and outbound SDR work is part of that labor. Cognition collapses the software-engineer cost curve at the level of an entire profession; the success-state names a category that disappears. Gojiberry AI's pitch is "2-5x higher reply rates" against 11x.ai's Alice and a $99/month Pro plan with 2 LinkedIn senders -- the dossier never claims that B2B outbound, as a category, ends. It claims B2B outbound gets cheaper for sub-50-employee firms running campaigns against LinkedIn-active buyers. The diff between Cognition's frame and Gojiberry AI's frame is the difference between "labor displaced" and "labor tooling refined," and that is the exact distinction my Outlook Business essay turns on.

The **Range Fuels** (~2007) failure mechanism was capital adequacy misjudged against the physics required to produce cellulosic ethanol at scale -- the science was hard, the capital was insufficient, and the plant closed in 2011. The reason I name that pattern here is to invert it: Gojiberry AI has no physics, no capital risk, no plant. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant, Romàn Czerny, and Dylan Teixeira have already hit $2.5M ARR with $500K raised and are profitable. That is the opposite failure shape from Range Fuels -- it is the shape of a business that any Tier-1 generalist can fund and that does not need me. When I look at the dossier and see "Apollo ($251M raised, $150M ARR), Clay ($100M Series C at $3.1B), 11x.ai ($74M raised)" -- those are the consensus VCs already funding the category at scale. I am over-resourced for Gojiberry AI by an order of magnitude. The Range Fuels pattern teaches me to write where capital depth is the moat; Gojiberry AI's roadmap reveals capital depth is irrelevant.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus, the mechanism that compounds over 18 months is whether Pierre-Eliott Lallemant publicly stakes a position that LinkedIn-active outbound is structurally wrong, or whether he refines the same playbook he ran at CoCo AI -- the prior WhatsApp marketing SaaS he sold for ~€500K ARR. The dossier reads as domain-expert continuity: a founder who built a B2B SaaS using cold outbound, then built a B2B SaaS that automates cold outbound. McKinsey predicted under 1M U.S. cellphones by 2000 because they extrapolated the past; Gojiberry AI's founder thesis extrapolates Pierre-Eliott's prior SaaS into the next SaaS. There is no instigator moment in the dossier.

On founder learning rate -- the one dimension where Gojiberry AI scores -- the counterfactual that would flip the tier is whether the v1-to-v2 pivot was a real thesis update or a marketing rebrand. The dossier (wearefounders.uk 2026) describes v1 as an "AI sales assistant" with a 2,000-person waitlist that converted zero, and v2 as a signal-first agent that hit $0→$1.4M ARR in 9 months. That is a documented pivot with a stated reason, the exact pattern that justified my $100M Series C into **Slash** (2024). Plus Dylan Teixeira independently founded and sold Edusign in 2025, and Pierre-Eliott and Romàn sold CoCo AI in 2025 -- three founders, two exits. The learning-rate signal is genuine. It is also the only signal.

On capital match, Gojiberry AI compares directly to **Slash** (2024) as the single anchor, because Slash is the YC-pedigreed exception I made on learning-rate override. The diff is scale: Slash justified $100M because consumer business banking has distribution-network compounding that warrants capital depth. Gojiberry AI has 2,000 SMB customers paying $99/month and is already profitable -- the company does not need a Khosla-shaped check, and a Khosla-shaped check does not buy the company any miracle it could not otherwise execute. The capital profile here is "$5-10M Series A from any Tier-1 generalist," which is the Low rung in my framework verbatim.

My 36-month forecast for Gojiberry AI: acquired by Apollo.io or HubSpot for $150-400M at $20-40M ARR, with a non-trivial tail probability (call it 30%, anchored to 11x.ai's 70-80% churn per gtmlens.com Q2 2026) of customer-retention collapse driving the company sideways at $5-10M ARR before any acquisition closes. The mechanism is straightforward: Gojiberry AI's "moat" is a cross-customer learning loop on LinkedIn-public signals, which G2 reviewers themselves flag as platform-dependent -- the moment LinkedIn tightens scraping or Apollo ships intent-signal targeting on top of its 250M-contact DB, the differentiation evaporates and Gojiberry AI's path to liquidity is M&A, not IPO.

The strongest objection is the **Slash** (2024) precedent: I overrode my consequence-magnitude prior on a YC-backed consumer fintech because the founder pivot was documented and the learning rate was visible, and Slash is now in my portfolio. Gojiberry AI has the same pivot evidence (v1-to-v2), the same YC pedigree (Spring 2026), and arguably better unit economics ($2.5M ARR profitable on $500K raised). Why not extend the Slash exception? Because Slash's consequence-state plausibly reshapes SMB banking distribution at scale, while Gojiberry AI's consequence-state caps at "Apollo with sharper targeting." And because the **Glean** exception I made on Arvind Jain required capital and consequence at Fortune-500 information-asymmetry scale -- Gojiberry AI's <50-employee G2 cohort is structurally the opposite signal. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Gojiberry AI signs a named enterprise (Fortune-500 logo) on a six-figure ACV within 12 months, breaking the SMB cap and proving the signal stack generalizes. Until then, this is the pass I take in thirty seconds.
