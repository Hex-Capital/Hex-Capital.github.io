# Hedge -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Hedge is a single-miracle GTM bet on AI-wholesale placement led by two operators with real shipping histories -- Luke Button (Fernstone YC F25, ex-Traba growth) and Luke Rosa (ex-Head of Scaled Systems Traba, "replaced humans with AI agents to manage $20MM+ in revenue") -- but it lands in a category where Novella just closed $21M Series A from Brewer Lane and Arch in May 2026 to execute the *same wedge*, and Hedge's dossier discloses zero revenue, zero named logos, and a self-reported 30-minute response time as the headline KPI. That high/low pairing -- credible shipping founders, no visible curve in a cycle where the curve is the whole point -- is the analytical signature, and it's the inverse of the Harvey (2023) template I underwrite to.

Hedge's strongest dimension on my ladder is single-miracle architecture, and the load-bearing analogy is **Color Genomics** (co-founded 2015) -- workflow infrastructure on top of a commodity capability, where the miracle was the workflow, not the chemistry. Hedge structurally assumes LLM reliability on ACORD forms / loss runs / broker emails (validated by the cluster of 2024-26 AI-broker rounds) and treats the remaining work as carrier appointments, state-by-state licensing, and underwriting judgment loops -- which is GTM, not new physics. Where Hedge diverges from Color: Color had an operator (me) deeply embedded who could resolve workflow-vs-science ambiguity at every fork; Hedge is two ex-growth operators against judgment-heavy E&S underwriting on non-standard data, which is exactly where the "one miracle" can quietly become two if loss-ratio performance turns out to require human-graded data they don't have.

Revenue velocity is where Hedge sits closest to the LOW rung, and the LOW rung's failure mechanism is the one I named on Tim Ferriss #863 (April 2026): an AI company without explosive growth visible early means "something's fundamentally broken." Hedge's dossier offers three unnamed testimonials, a self-reported median response time, and the phrase "collaborating with some of the fastest-growing brokerages" with no counts and no logos -- the "false signal vs. just working" distinction (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) collapses entirely toward false signal when the company is itself a licensed CA broker (#6018729) that could be quoting actual bound premium and isn't. Harvey (2023) was disclosing the curve at every valuation step; Hedge is disclosing testimonials. In an AI cycle, that silence isn't reticence, it's information.

Market re-opening via specific recent catalyst: the LLM-reliability step-function on unstructured insurance documents in 2024-25 is real, and the $105.31B U.S. E&S premium pool growing 7.8% YoY in 2025 (S&P Global) is a tailwind -- but mechanism analysis matters here. Over 18 months this catalyst compounds *against* Hedge as much as for it, because Novella ($21M with Arch as a strategic), Harper ($46.8M with Emergence), and Outmarket ($17M Series A) are all using the same catalyst with 20-40x the capital. The catalyst is real; the *re-opening* in my sense (Stripe 2010, Anduril 2022) requires peers to be ignoring it, and peers are not ignoring this one. That's consensus without the curve to justify paying consensus.

Index-like distribution: the counterfactual that would flip Hedge from a 7/15 to an 11/15 is exposing an API or data layer that the *other* wholesalers route through -- the Stripe-on-payments shape, where every entrant in the category has to touch you. The dossier instead describes a full-stack wholesale + underwriting firm across 45+ classes with 30+ carriers and Lloyd's, which is the same shape as Amwins and RT Specialty just AI-faster. That's a share-fight against entrenched wholesalers and a parallel-build against Novella -- not an index position on E&S growth.

Founder shipping rhythm: Luke Button's Fernstone (YC F25, 100+ business clients, launched Oct 2025) is the single anchor here, and it's a double-edged data point -- on one side, it proves Button ships YC-backed insurance products to live customers in months, which is the Perplexity (2022) pattern of demonstrated rhythm; on the other side, Fernstone launched eight months before Hedge and the dossier cannot clarify whether it's wound down, spun out, or running in parallel. The "founder ships between meetings" pattern requires focus to compound; an unresolved prior YC company is the structural opposite of focus, and it's the specific thing I'd ask Button to clarify in the first thirty seconds of a call.

My 36-month forecast: Hedge ends up either acqui-hired by a regional wholesaler or by Novella itself in the $15-40M range, or pivoted back into a retail-brokerage shape resembling Fernstone v2 -- low probability of independent breakout to $50M+ ARR given the $21M Novella overhang and the absence of a disclosed revenue curve at YC Demo Day. The mechanism is straightforward: in a capital-asymmetric race for carrier appointments and broker relationships in a regulated category, the side with $21M and an Arch strategic relationship out-hires and out-licenses the side with two people and pre-seed money, unless the two-person side has a curve that lets it raise at a markup inside 90 days, which the dossier gives me no evidence to expect.

The strongest bull case is that Button is exactly the cold-inbound, ships-between-meetings founder I underwrote in Perplexity (2022) -- he founded Fernstone, co-founded Contrast (acquired), ran growth at Traba closing JB Hunt, and is now attacking the same insurance category with a sharper wedge (E&S wholesale vs. retail SMB). Rosa shipped AI agents managing $20M+ at Traba, which is the rare pre-seed dossier where both founders have *quantified* AI deployment history. Add the *AI Market Clarity* framing -- insurance workflows are still in the "wide open" column, not a winners-declared category like legal AI -- and the bull says I should pay up before the curve appears, because the team is real. The bear that wins: paying up "when consensus pays" requires the curve, and Hedge has Harper at $46.8M and Novella at $21M both racing the same play with the same catalyst, which means the consensus price is being set by *their* curves, not Hedge's. I'd flip to invest the moment Hedge discloses named retail-agency counterparties and a month-over-month bind-premium figure -- one verifiable condition, easy to produce if the business is "just working."
