# Complir -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Complir is a regulatory-catalyst arbitrage play with a credentialed-but-not-shipping-visible team -- the GPSR clock that started ticking on 13 December 2024 is doing more of the work in this dossier than any founder artifact, and Gustav Bang, Marc Kaa Brejner, and Tine Køhnel are described in titles (Head of Denmark at Legora, DTU engineer, DTU designer) rather than in shipped products I can point to. The €1.7M pre-seed at €6.8M post is a Vendep-led Scandinavian round, not a hot-deal markup, which means Complir lives or dies on the strength of one dimension I score high and one I score low.

**Anduril (2022)** is the right HIGH rung for Complir's market re-opening dimension because the shared mechanism is identical: a regulatory or geopolitical event in a 24-month window reorders the buyer's compliance obligations and forces every incumbent into a forced-purchase posture. With Anduril it was Ukraine exposing Pentagon software inadequacy; with Complir it is GPSR 2023/988 expanding the "Responsible Person" regime to every non-food consumer product sold into 27 EU markets, enforceable 13 December 2024 (DLA Piper, Feb 2025). Where Complir diverges from the Anduril template is the *durability* of the catalyst -- Pentagon software modernization is a multi-decade rebuild, while GPSR compliance becomes a solved problem once retailers like Matas and COOP have audit-ready workflows in place, after which Complir is selling maintenance rather than riding a wave. The catalyst is real and specific and recent, which is why this dimension scores well, but it is a one-shot catalyst, not a compounding one.

The LOW rung on founder-shipping is the **"promoter who hasn't shipped"** archetype I've passed on repeatedly -- founders whose dossiers list ex-FAANG, ex-McKinsey, or ex-Head-of-Country titles but contain no named artifacts the founder personally built and iterated to users. Complir's dossier reads exactly this way: Bang is "10 years in startups" and "former Head of Denmark at Legora" (a sales role at a legal AI company, not a build role); Brejner is "engineer, DTU" and "prior consultant building large-scale system architecture for Danish authorities" (consulting, not product); Køhnel is "designer from DTU" with "10+ years building digital platforms" (which platforms?). No GitHub, no Twitter, no named side projects, no products from prior employers. This is the inverse of the Aravind Srinivas pattern that got me into Perplexity (2022) -- there is no evidence in this dossier that any of these three ship between meetings, and at pre-seed the shipping artifact is the only legible signal that isn't a vanity metric.

On revenue velocity, the **mechanism** running underneath Complir's reported 45% MoM (ArcticStartup, Dec 2025) is the question I care about, not the percentage itself -- 45% on €5K MRR is design-partner tourism, 45% on €50K MRR with Matas and COOP signing real procurement contracts is "just working" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). The dossier discloses 109,886 products monitored as a unit but no ARR, no ACV, no logo-by-logo revenue split, which is itself a tell because real growth is easy to disclose. Over 18 months this dimension either compounds because GPSR enforcement scares every European retailer above 100 SKUs into a forced procurement cycle, or it plateaus because the seven named customers turn out to be the addressable Scandinavian early-adopter set and the rest of Europe waits for Assent to ship an AI module.

The **counterfactual** on index-like distribution is what would have to be true for Complir to sit in the workflow path the way Stripe sits in payments. Today Complir is the output layer for GPSR documentation across 40+ markets (complir.io), which is workflow-adjacent but not workflow-default -- Assent at $350M raised and Source Intelligence at $17.5M sell into overlapping enterprise buyers with deeper supply-chain hooks, and Reglyr is explicitly positioned as a direct alternative (Reglyr blog, 2026). For Complir to flip from "vendor" to "index" the dossier would need to show that a critical mass of EU retailers route every new SKU through Complir before they can list it -- the 109,886 SKUs is suggestive but not yet structural, and the named customer list is Scandinavian-concentrated (Matas, Flying Tiger Copenhagen, COOP, Konges Sløjd) rather than pan-European.

On single-miracle architecture, the **Color Genomics parallel** is closer than it looks -- Color was workflow on commodity sequencing, Complir is workflow on commodity LLM document generation, and in both cases the miracle is GTM-into-a-regulated-buyer rather than a new technical capability. Complir doesn't need a new model, it needs to convince European retailers that an AI-generated declaration of conformity will hold up in front of a market surveillance authority. The wrinkle is liability: GPSR's Responsible Person regime makes the declaration legally binding (DLA Piper, Feb 2025), and the dossier has no public statement on how Complir allocates liability with its customers, which is the kind of unisolated dependency that turns a one-miracle pitch into a one-and-a-half-miracle pitch.

My 36-month forecast: Complir reaches €5-15M ARR serving EU mid-market retailers and gets absorbed by Assent or a PLM incumbent like Trace One at a €40-80M enterprise value as GPSR enforcement plateaus into steady-state compliance work and Assent ships a competing AI-output module against its $350M war chest. That outcome is consistent with the dimensional signature -- a real but non-compounding regulatory catalyst paired with a founder team that can execute a Scandinavian-then-French enterprise motion but doesn't have the shipping-rhythm signal that produces a category-defining outcome. Not a zero, but not the kind of return profile that justifies an angel check from me at consensus seed prices when I can be in Decagon or Abridge at the same stage.

The strongest bull case is the one I have to take seriously: 45% MoM growth with named anchors Flying Tiger and COOP, an angel cap table including ex-Coop CEO Kraen Oestergaard Nielsen and GS1 board member Per Thau, and a regulatory window that incumbents like Assent are structurally slow to attack because their per-supplier seat revenue cannibalizes if they pivot to retailer-side output generation. That's the Stripe-after-PayPal pattern -- the incumbent's revenue model prevents them from following. What dismantles it for me is the absence of any disclosed ARR figure in the press cycle (ArcticStartup, TechFundingNews, Vestbee, Øresund Startups all covered the round and none cite revenue), combined with the founder-shipping silence and the explicit competitive listing alongside Reglyr in the same retailer-AI-compliance frame. One verifiable condition would flip me: Complir publicly reporting €3M+ ARR with sustained >30% MoM growth by their Series A, with at least one signed top-10 European retailer outside Scandinavia. Until then this is a pass at consensus, a closer look at a discount.
