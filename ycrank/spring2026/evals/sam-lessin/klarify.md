# Klarify -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Klarify is the platonic Adobe-for-AI pitch dressed up as an EHR play: a sane four-person team building an AI scribe + workflow tool in a category where Upheal already raised a $10M Series A from Headline in September 2025 doing materially the same thing. The dimensional signature is a company that scores its highest on capital discipline (small team, tiered SaaS, free tier on the bottom rung) and its lowest on primitive creation -- which is exactly the wrong shape. Disciplined execution on a non-primitive in a consensus category is how you end up as a feature inside SimplePractice's next release notes.

Consensus Mispricing is where Klarify dies for me. My HIGH rung here is Solana at four cents -- a category every smart person had written off as a settled question. My LOW rung is the YC AI-copilot pitches I've been shrugging at since 2024 -- consensus-hot, premium-priced, ten VCs bidding. Klarify sits right next to that LOW rung: "AI scribe for therapists" is not a contrarian framing, it is the framing, and Upheal's $14.35M total raise, plus Mentalyc, plus Blueprint, plus Heidi, are all the proof I need that the upside is already priced in. Moody Abdul isn't getting rejected by mainstream VCs because the category is uncomfortable -- he's competing inside the most crowded behavioral-health AI subsegment in YC's pipeline. There's no "what if they're right?" question to ask here, because everyone already agrees.

Primitive Creation is the other floor. My HIGH rung is Venmo -- "Venmo me" became a verb because peer-to-peer mobile payments didn't exist before. My LOW rung is "better CRM, AI-powered." Klarify's own pitch is that legacy practice management software "records what therapists do" and Klara "actually does it" -- that's a faster horse, not a new animal. Therapists already write SOAP notes, file claims, and prep supervision; Klarify automates the existing verbs rather than minting a new one. Nothing in the dossier -- not the 309,121 notes generated, not the 20+ note formats, not the marketing profile pages -- describes a behavior that didn't exist on May 8, 2026. After Klarify "wins," nobody describes a new category as "the Klarify of X." That's the test, and it fails.

On Sanity-Insanity: my HIGH rung is Raj on Solana -- engineer who builds things that work, pitching a thing every smart person thought was dead. Klarify's Moody Abdul (ex-LinkedIn AE, ex-Accenture, took CircleBack to $50K ARR) and Alexander Bergholm (ex-Workday ML) are sane. Fully sane. But the idea is sane too -- a B2B SaaS subscription for a workflow most therapists already pay SimplePractice for. Sane founder, sane idea is not the trade. The 27K-therapist Future of Therapy podcast is the most interesting asset in the dossier and the one thing that flickers toward my unstated pattern of owned distribution -- but it's a moat for a feature, not for a primitive. Place Klarify between my MID rung (Slack, a reasonable founder with a borderline-reasonable idea redeemed by genuine primitive creation) and my LOW rung -- closer to LOW because there's no insanity to redeem.

Structural Durability is the AI kill test, and Klarify fails it cleanly. My HIGH rung is Metropolis -- physical parking infrastructure that an LLM cannot commoditize. My LOW rung is any pure-software workflow wrapper that Adobe could ship Tuesday. Klarify is closer to LOW: SimplePractice, Jane, and TherapyNotes own the EHR distribution and can bolt an AI scribe module onto their existing seats faster than Klarify can match their billing/clearinghouse depth. The 837P/EDI claims-filing feature with a four-person team is precisely the kind of "AI does insurance" pitch that breaks on first contact with a real payer. Capital Discipline is where I give Klarify its only defensible mark -- four people, tiered pricing from $0 to $71/month, no announced megaround, no blitzscale language; that's MID rung at worst, default 6/10. Not enough to save it.

Forecast: in 36 months Klarify is either acquired by SimplePractice, Jane, or Headspace Health for under $50M as an AI-feature tuck-in, or it's plateaued at $2-5M ARR while Upheal raises a Series B and consolidates the AI-native therapist EHR position. The mechanism is simple: AI scribe + workflow is a feature, the incumbent EHR vendors have the distribution, and Upheal already has the funding lead inside the AI-native frame. Klarify's 27K-therapist newsletter is a real asset and probably what makes the acquihire happen instead of the slow death.

Steel-manning the bull case: Klarify's owned audience -- 27,000+ therapists via the Future of Therapy podcast -- is a Patreon-style distribution moat that competitors can't easily replicate, and Moody Abdul's prior LinkedIn AE plus podcast operator combo is exactly the founder-channel fit that lets a content-led GTM compound into a category leader. If you squint, this looks like Patreon's creator-distribution thesis applied to therapists, and a 5,200-therapist user count (per the YC page) at pre-seed is real adoption. Here's what dismantles it: Patreon's audience moat compounded because the underlying product was a primitive, not a feature; Klarify's audience compounds onto a commoditizing AI scribe that Upheal's $10M war chest will outpace on feature surface within 12 months. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Klarify shows $3M+ ARR with documented HIPAA + PHIPA + SOC 2, AND a signed clearinghouse partnership for live US claims filing within 12 months. Absent that, this is a pass.
