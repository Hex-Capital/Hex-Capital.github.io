# Ontora -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Ontora is a services-flavored consulting-displacement bet wearing AI-agent clothing, and its analytical signature is a relatively-OK capital structure ($50K per engagement against $500K+ McKinsey work) paired with the worst possible category sleeve for me ? "AI agents that interview every employee" is the literal sentence in my Pass Example. Leon Iwanowitsch, Max Arnold, and David Korn have built something real enough to put Vertiv's Strategy Manager on the homepage, but the dossier specific that pins Ontora ? the YC tagline "AI agents that interview every employee to map how work gets done" ? is indistinguishable from forty other Spring 2026 P26 batchmates I will see this cycle.

Ontora's strongest dimension is capital structure, and the load-bearing analogy here is Teamshares, where the financing instrument itself ? employee-equity rollup of small businesses ? was the thesis, not the equity check. The shared mechanism with Ontora is engagement-based revenue at a price point that approximates services: $50K per project means twenty customers is a million dollars, and a three-person team in San Francisco can plausibly run default-alive on that math without five more rounds of dilution. Where Ontora diverges from the Teamshares rung is that nothing about the actual cap table is unusual ? this is a standard YC SAFE wrapped around what is functionally a productized-consulting business, and I am not being offered revenue-share, royalty, or holdco structure. The asset is venture-acceleratable but not venture-required, which is precisely the Slow Our Letter "venture moments" question Ontora's pitch never answers.

The pure-AI-agent-for-enterprise category is exactly the sleeve I have publicly written off, and the failure mechanism is the one I named on the Joe Lonsdale podcast: "AI is just more internet," meaning Ontora is not selling a fundamentally disruptive primitive, it is selling commodity LLM + voice agents wrapped around an enterprise interview script. That pattern ? a thin agent layer on a stack any competent shop can rebuild in a quarter ? is visible directly in Ontora's dossier: KYP.ai already markets "Agentic Process Intelligence," Celonis has shipped AI features on its PI Graph since 2023, and the technical claim of 100 parallel ~20-minute voice interviews is buildable on commodity TTS/STT today. Ontora has no on-chain economic activity, no DTC consumer brand, no creator distribution, and no Wizard-of-Oz manual-to-automated arc disclosed ? it is in the sleeve I am explicitly re-weighting away from.

On Round Pricing ? Mechanism analysis. Ontora's pricing was not disclosed in the dossier, which by my absent-evidence default means I assume consensus YC pricing because price-silence has correlated with price-consensus on every batch I've underwritten since 2022. The compounding mechanism over 18 months is brutal: if Ontora raised at a typical $15-25M post-money P26 cap on the strength of one Vertiv testimonial and a Tom Blomfield partner assignment, every subsequent tack in the regatta has to clear that mark, and the next round happens into a market where Celonis ($16B, ?1.3B ARR) or a McKinsey-internal product can blunt the wedge. There is no version of this pricing where I generate alpha from the SAFE cap.

On Founder Conviction ? Counterfactual. For Ontora to flip from a 6/20 to a 12+/20 on this dimension, I would need to find a Leon Iwanowitsch essay arguing why tacit-knowledge capture beats event-log process mining, or a Max Arnold long-form post from his Porsche years on why operational interviews fail at scale, or a David Korn GitHub history of process-mining tooling built before Ontora existed. The dossier gives me none of these ? "GitHub: No public account found" for all three, no public writing on the category, and a founding origin story ("Stockholm tour of fastest-scaling startups, all three quit their jobs") that reads as opportunity-seeking rather than missionary obsession. The "Writing is thinking" filter fails cleanly.

On Founder-as-Brand ? Single-anchor parallel against John Fish / Bookshelved. The Bookshelved check worked because the creator brought a YouTube audience that was the entire downside cushion ? distribution was on the cap table before the round closed. Ontora has three Twitter handles ? @LeonIwanowitsch, @maxonary, @dav1dk0rn ? with follower counts the dossier could not even retrieve, which is the data signature of accounts under a few thousand followers. There is no newsletter, no podcast, no community, no Discord, no pre-existing audience for process-mining content. Ontora is entirely dependent on outbound enterprise sales into Fortune 500 C-suites, which is the most expensive channel in B2B and the one where Celonis and McKinsey already own the relationship.

My 36-month forecast: Ontora is alive but illegible at $2-5M ARR by mid-2029, having pivoted into a specific vertical (most likely European industrial HR/works-council process audits, given the HSG + Porsche + CODE founder backgrounds), raises a flat or down extension rather than a tier-1-led Series A, and Celonis or UiPath ships an interview-agent feature on existing distribution by late 2028 that caps Ontora's enterprise wedge ? no acquisition, no shutdown, just stuck. The mechanism producing this forecast is the high/low signature directly: services-flavored revenue keeps Ontora default-alive (Dim 2 strength), but the consensus AI-agent category (Dim 5 weakness) prevents any legibility translation into a Series A buyer's frame.

The strongest case against my pass is the Ro override: Ro was high-burn DTC pharma I funded despite Criterion 2 because regulatory complexity across 50-state prescription rails was a real "venture moment" only VC capital could clear. The bull version of Ontora invokes the same logic ? European works-council compliance, GDPR, employee-consent frameworks, and Fortune 500 procurement schleps could constitute a regulatory moat around AI-conducted employee interviews that only venture capital can clear, and Max Arnold's Porsche tenure is real founder-market fit for exactly that buyer. That case stings. It loses because Ro's schlep was a licensed prescription rail with hard state-by-state legal gating that locked in the moat permanently once cleared ? Ontora's "schlep" is just enterprise sales plus a privacy policy, which is what every B2B SaaS company does, and the dossier explicitly notes "no privacy/consent framework documented on ontora.com." The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Ontora discloses three named Fortune 500 customers beyond Vertiv at $50K+ ARR each by Q4 2026 AND publishes a works-council-compliant interview protocol that becomes a referenceable standard ? then the schlep is real, the regatta tack is underwritable, and I would re-engage on price.
