# Kinect -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Kinect is a well-paired UCLA-CS duo — Kratik Agrawal and Varun Kandula, who co-led Conversational Intelligence at Reevo before this — running straight at a layer Shopify itself shipped natively on March 24, 2026. That collision between the strongest thing in the dossier (a real cofounder pair with shared prior shipping) and the weakest (the host platform is now the competitor) is the entire shape of Kinect as a bet.

Airbnb's cereal boxes are my reference case for animal resourcefulness, and Kinect does not clear that bar — but it does clear something. Kandula sourced "$200K+ contracted ARR across 10+ brands" through NextGen Consulting at UCLA, working with Sephora and Ralph Lauren as a college operator. That is the kind of pre-funding fact I notice. The mechanism that made Airbnb a cereal-box story was founders generating revenue via a stunt no investor would have imagined; Kinect's founders generated revenue via outbound sales to DTC brands as undergraduates, which is unglamorous but real. The ten named logos on trykinect.ai — Slumberkins, Sousa, Zupz, Ketro, ALC, Bloom, Hearthy Foods, Huppy, VapeTM, Loosey Goosey — read as founder-led outbound, not inbound demand. Kinect diverges from the Airbnb rung because there is no improvisational stunt; it converges with it because Kandula was already selling enterprise contracts before the YC check.

Polished AI-wrapper teams with $50B TAM slides are how I lose money, and the Kinect schlep profile rhymes with that failure mode more than I would like. The schlep test from January 2012 asks whether the work is unpleasant enough that other smart founders are unconsciously routing around it. Kinect's dossier names Daydream ($50M seed), Alhena (~$13.9M), Rep AI, Phia, OneOff, plus OpenAI ChatGPT checkout, Amazon Rufus, and — most damaging — Shopify Agentic Storefronts launched natively to all US merchants on March 24, 2026. That is not a schlep zone; that is a feature race against the platform owner. Coinbase's regulatory grind was a moat because Brian Armstrong wanted the work nobody else wanted. Kinect wants the work Shopify just decided to do for free.

Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT, and that single fact is what makes Dropbox an organic-idea story. Kinect's founders did not run a DTC store and suffer through static product pages — they worked at Reevo on conversational intelligence and then sold that capability to merchants. That is adjacent-pain rather than own-pain, and over 18 months the difference shows up as a product-roadmap problem: when the catalog-feed standard shifts (it will, because Shopify and OpenAI both have a vote), founders solving their own problem iterate from instinct, while founders solving someone else's iterate from customer calls. Kinect will be iterating from customer calls.

The hacker-genealogy axis is where I want to see what Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize gave Stripe, and Kinect's dossier gives me CS degrees and employer brands instead — Google Commerce, Anduril, Verkada, MongoDB, Capital One. Counterfactual: if either Agrawal or Kandula had a starred open-source repo around catalog-feed normalization, or a shipped artifact predating Reevo, the score flips a tier because the "brand-owned data layer" claim acquires technical-taste backing. The dossier explicitly notes "no notable repo star counts retrieved" for both GitHubs. Credentialed engineers without hacker tracks are the modal YC application, not the Stripe pattern.

Kinect's cofounder configuration is the strongest single dimension and the cleanest parallel to Stripe — two technically-trained classmates who already shipped together at Reevo before founding. That is the long-relationship pattern from "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010), not the hackathon-pair anti-pattern. Two founders, shared prior collaboration, complementary coverage (Agrawal commerce-platform tenure at Google, Kandula enterprise sales chops). On this axis alone Kinect would get a check.

My 36-month forecast for Kinect: pivot-acquihire by Shopify or a commerce-infrastructure player in the $15–40M range, with Kinect's brand-owned data layer folded into Agentic Storefronts as a third-party integration SKU; ARR plateaus under $5M because the Mar 24, 2026 Shopify launch caps merchant willingness to pay for what their platform offers natively. The mechanism is straightforward — when the platform ships the substitute in the same quarter you launch, the schlep moat never forms, and without the moat the outcome shape is talent-and-code absorption, not a Twitch.

The strongest case against my pass is that Daydream raised $50M and Alhena $13.9M in this exact category, which means the market believes merchant-side agentic commerce is venture-fundable even with Shopify in the picture, and Kinect has stronger founder-market fit than either — Kandula has already closed Sephora and Ralph Lauren contracts as an undergraduate, which is the kind of pre-funding fact the Airbnb known exception was built on. The bear-case dismantling: Airbnb's cereal-box stunt overrode my idea skepticism because the idea was contrarian and the founders were animals; Kinect's idea is consensus (six named competitors in the dossier, plus three hyperscalers) and the founder hustle is conventional B2B SaaS outbound. The Airbnb exception does not apply when the idea is crowded — it applies when the idea looks dumb and the founders are animals. Kinect's idea looks smart and the founders look competent. That is the reverse pattern. **The condition that flips my verdict: Kinect reaches $1M ARR with at least three of its current ten logos expanded to multi-product contracts before Shopify Agentic Storefronts reaches feature parity with Kinect's adaptive PDPs — that would prove the schlep is real and I missed it.**
