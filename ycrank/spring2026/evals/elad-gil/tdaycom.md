# tday.com -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Cyrus Kelly's tday.com is a crowded-category AI creative tool whose strongest signal is that the team has shipped *something* before (markup.one, communities.one) and whose weakest signal is that the dossier names five funded competitors -- Arcade, AdCreative.ai, Omneky, Pencil, Lapis -- before naming a single revenue datapoint. That high/low pairing is the signature: tday.com is a *shipping-without-a-curve clone* in a sub-market I have not flagged as re-opening, which is the exact profile my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy says to avoid at consensus seed prices.

On catalyst, my reference rung is **Anduril** (2022), which I led at $4.6B post because Ukraine had carved a named 24-month re-opening into a market peers were calling a graveyard. tday.com has no such catalyst in the dossier -- the "why now" reduces to a founder claim that deterministic brand-coherent design "has only recently become feasible" (Adelaide University, 2026) plus "social-platform fragmentation," which has been true since approximately 2018. AI-marketing-content is a 2022-vintage category, not a 2026 re-opening; Omneky and Pencil were already shipping into this exact buyer before ChatGPT launched. tday.com is entering after the window, not skating to where the puck is going -- the inverse of the **Stripe** (2010) / **Coinbase** (2013) / **Anduril** (2022) entry template.

The **Lyft** Series C pass is my reference for the lowest dimension: I passed because the dossier showed a #2 entering a market with declared leaders and no share-inversion mechanism. tday.com's revenue-velocity section is structurally similar -- no MRR, no MoM, no named ACV, no Product Hunt rank, no follower counts, just $500K of YC capital and five logos (Relay, Drift, Slate, Forge, Inkwell) whose "usage depth, paying status, and whether names refer to the well-known companies... is not disclosed." In the AI cycle, silence on growth is the signal, per Tim Ferriss Show #863 -- "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." Arcade is the **Harvey** of this niche -- $11.5M Series A from Greylock, same product-launch-video use case -- and tday.com has not shown me the $3B→$5B→$8B kind of curve that would justify entering as the #6 funded entrant.

On founder shipping, tday.com clears the *minimum* bar that disqualifies most pre-seed pitches: Kelly and Shrestha co-shipped communities.one, then markup.one, now tday.com -- that is three iterations of shipped artifacts, not a deck-only pitch. But the dossier surfaces no GitHub handles, no verified LinkedIn, and no public output samples beyond marketing-site demos. That compounds badly over 18 months: founders who don't publish artifacts to public channels tend not to build the cold-inbound flywheel that drove **Perplexity** (2022) into my portfolio -- Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and shipped a thing between meetings, and there is no analogue in tday.com's dossier because none of the three founders surface a verifiable public footprint.

On index position, tday.com would have to flip from "marketing tool for software launches" to "the creative layer every shipped product update flows through" for the score to move a tier. The git-repo-triggered regeneration is genuinely the right wedge for that -- it is the workflow path -- but the dossier explicitly names Canva, Adobe, Figma, and Arcade as parties with "no clear structural barrier" against building the same trigger. Counterfactual: if tday.com had a signed integration with GitHub or Linear as the default release-note-to-social pipeline, that flips to index-territory and I revisit; absent that, tday.com is competing on features against entrenched creative tools, which is the **Lyft** structural problem.

On single-miracle architecture, tday.com is structurally closer to **Decagon** (2023) than to a science project -- the bet is one validated capability (deterministic, code-based brand-coherent design output) plus product and GTM execution, not three independent breakthroughs. That is the architecturally correct shape. The problem is not miracle *count*, it is miracle *validation*: Shrestha's claim that the team built "a fundamentally different kind of creative pipeline" producing "fully deterministic" outputs is unverified by third parties, and the dossier flags exactly this as a risk.

My 36-month forecast: tday.com is dead or acqui-hired sub-$15M by Canva, Adobe, or Arcade by mid-2029, having plateaued under $1.5M ARR after burning the $500K YC check plus one bridge, with the markup.one → tday.com rebrand repeating once more into a developer-tools-only vertical pivot before the wind-down. The mechanism: no catalyst + no disclosed velocity + five funded competitors with distribution wedges (Arcade's screen-recording installed base, AdCreative.ai's >$10M ARR claimed footprint) means tday.com fights for share in a sub-market my *AI Market Clarity* piece would classify closer to "winners declared" than "wide open."

The steel-man against this pass: Kelly graduated Adelaide at 18 and has mentored 300+ founders as Chief Student Entrepreneur, Shrestha is a ThincLab/ThincSeed resident, and the team has now reshipped the same thesis twice (communities.one → markup.one → tday.com), which is the operationally-disciplined-CEO archetype I funded in **Brex** (2017) -- a team that keeps execution-iterating on a thesis until the market shape clicks. The bear case wins on the dossier, though: that pattern requires the third iteration to show *velocity*, and tday.com's dossier discloses zero revenue, zero MoM, and zero named paying ACV against five funded competitors who do have revenue. **Color Genomics** (2015) is my Known Exception for "workflow on top of a hard thing," but I *operated* Color -- I am not operating tday.com, and the deterministic-design pipeline is not yet validated the way commodity sequencing was. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a named software-company contract at >$50K ACV with disclosed >20% MoM growth across two consecutive months, ideally with the GitHub integration as the wedge. Absent that, tday.com is a pass.
