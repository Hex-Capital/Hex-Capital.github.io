# Andco -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Andco is a strong-team second-act execution play in a personal injury legal-tech market that consensus has already priced in -- Ryan Xue and Michael Lemm are exactly the operator profile I want, but EvenUp's $2B valuation, Supio's 4x ARR growth, and Eve's $47M Series A tell me the structural window Andco is walking through has already been kicked open by three well-funded incumbents with bundling capacity. The signature here is high founder quality paired with a market that is no longer nonobvious, and that pairing historically produces capable acqui-hire outcomes rather than category-defining companies.

Nonobvious Market Timing is where Andco sits lowest on my ladder. My HIGH rung is Harvey in early 2022 -- the GPT-4 capability threshold cracked open a market that had been a graveyard for a decade, and consensus had not yet rerated it. My MID rung is Gusto -- boring, no exogenous catalyst, but a real category with a clear payer. My LOW rung is the consumer social YC apps I pass on every batch because no platform shift makes them feasible NOW versus two years ago. Andco sits between Gusto and Harvey but closer to Gusto: yes, the multi-modal LLM and voice-agent cost curve does make multi-channel fax/SMS/insurer-phone-tree automation feasible for the first time, but EvenUp's $385M raised, Supio's $91M, and Eve's $47M from a16z mean Andco is entering AFTER consensus has formed, not before it. Check the timestamp -- the structural window opened in 2024, and the capital is already on the field.

Operator-Scaler Founder Profile is where Andco sits highest. My HIGH rung is Parker Conrad rebuilding after Zenefits with full scar-tissue context. My MID rung is the Harvey founders -- first-timers with one strong domain anchor. My LOW rung is the equal-cofounder team with no designated CEO that I see deadlock during scaling. Andco's Xue/Lemm pairing sits between Parker and Harvey, closer to the upper half: they already ran Layup through YC W23 to a Whatfix acquisition announced November 25, 2025, and Lemm specifically brings a Harvard Law JD and time as a Kirkland & Ellis attorney -- meaning the COO has actually billed hours adjacent to the buyer he is now selling to. David Lee adds two prior CTO/founding-engineer M&A outcomes (Seek AI to IBM, DataFox to Oracle). The Whatfix advisory commitments concern me modestly but not decisively.

Infrastructure-Layer Positioning puts Andco between my Wish LOW rung and my Harvey MID rung. Stripe is my anchor on this dimension because Stripe didn't just process payments -- it became the API thousands of businesses built on. Andco's Filevine integration plus a self-serve client portal creates workflow embedding, but Andco is a vertical app for PI firms, not a layer other businesses build on top of. Single-Miracle Viability puts Andco in similar territory: my HIGH rung is Harvey (one exogenous tech miracle, proven buyer), my LOW rung is Anduril (three miracles, only justified because the structural market signal was overwhelming). Andco needs both the voice/fax agent reliability miracle for legal-grade workflows AND a GTM miracle against EvenUp's 2,000+ firm footprint -- two miracles, compounding odds, and no Anduril-grade structural override. Margin Architecture is the dimension where the dossier gives me almost nothing: no pricing disclosed, no retention data, only product-effect claims of "24+ hours saved per case." My Abridge HIGH rung prices per encounter, capturing units of cognition; Andco could plausibly price per case in that same shape, but I am applying the absent-evidence default.

In 36 months I expect Andco to be acquired by EvenUp, Filevine, or Whatfix for $30-80M at $4-10M ARR, having shipped impressive product but unable to outraise a Series B against incumbents that extended into pre-litigation workup as a feature. The mechanism is simple: when the founder profile is strong but the market timing is consensus, you get a credible product that becomes acquisition fodder for the capitalized leader. Xue and Lemm have already demonstrated they can engineer this exact outcome -- Layup to Whatfix is the template, and the Whatfix advisory relationship is arguably the soft landing already in place.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that PI is fragmented enough -- "$61 billion" per the Fortune citation -- to support multiple vertical winners specializing on different artifacts (EvenUp on demand letters, Supio on medical records, Andco on multi-channel execution), and that Xue/Lemm's repeat-founder execution speed could compound a real wedge before incumbents wake up. The Anduril Known Exception is instructive here: I overrode the multi-miracle rule when the structural signal was overwhelming. But Andco's structural signal isn't overwhelming -- it's the same signal EvenUp's investors saw in 2023, just two years later. The bull case loses to the dossier fact that EvenUp is "doubling ARR year-over-year" with 200,000+ cases already resolved through 2,000+ firms; the surface area Andco needs to cover to differentiate is exactly where the incumbent's capital is being deployed. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: Andco signing two top-50 plaintiff PI firms to six-figure annual contracts with disclosed 12-month retention before Demo Day, demonstrating that multi-channel agentic execution is a genuinely separable buying decision from EvenUp's demand-package wedge rather than a feature destined for bundling.
