# Foaster -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Foaster is what the ladder calls a *shipping team without an index position* -- Raphaël Dabadie and Alexandre Combes have a genuine artifact history (Combes's Répondia handled 60K+ AI customer calls in French restaurants; the pair's LLM benchmarks cleared 12M views) but the business they've wrapped around it is an AI-native services firm competing on price against McKinsey, BCG X, Accenture, and Distyl AI rather than sitting in a workflow rail every entrant must touch. That high-shipping / low-index pairing is Foaster's signature, and it's the exact opposite signature of **Decagon** (2023) or **Abridge** (2023), both of which I funded because the workflow position was load-bearing and the founder rhythm was a bonus.

On founder execution rhythm, the rung I anchor to is **Perplexity** (2022) -- Aravind would ship the thing we'd discussed a week later, which is the only legible pre-revenue signal I trust. Foaster's founders have the right artifact profile for this rung: Combes shipped a production AI-voice-agent product (Répondia, 60K+ calls handled for restaurant groups) that is mechanistically *the same primitive* as Foaster's employee-interview agent, and Dabadie compounded a 400K tennis community at age 11 into an enterprise-AI advisory practice and 12M-view LLM benchmarks. Where Foaster diverges from the Perplexity rung is that the dossier shows no between-meeting shipping cadence with prospective customers -- there's a Twitch interview and a YC launch page, not a "we built the thing you asked about last week" loop with named enterprise pilots. The artifacts are real; the rhythm with buyers isn't legible yet.

On index-like distribution -- the dimension where Foaster sits lowest -- the failure mechanism I keep returning to is the **Lyft** Series C pass: a perfectly competent #2 in a market with structural ceilings on the runner-up's economics. Foaster isn't network-effect #2, but it has the services-firm version of the same problem -- the dossier names McKinsey QuantumBlack, BCG X ($3.6B of $14.4B 2025 revenue already AI-related), Accenture ($3B GenAI bookings FY24), and Distyl AI ($32M Series A, Khosla-led, 18 months ahead on the FDE thesis), and Foaster's claimed differentiation is "3-5x less expensive." Price is the worst possible wedge against incumbents who own the C-suite relationship and are themselves rotating into AI delivery. Services-firm-on-top-of-LLMs is not "an index on a compounding category" -- it's a margin-compression bet dressed as infrastructure.

On the catalyst dimension, the mechanism that compounds over 18 months is real: BCG projecting ~40% of 2026 revenue as AI-related and McKinsey logging ~500 AI engagements in the past year is the willingness-to-pay step-function I look for, and it's the kind of "consensus pays" setup the Tim Ferriss Show #863 (April 2026) framing endorses. But the catalyst opens the window for *everyone* -- Foaster doesn't have a structural reason the BCG-AI-revenue curve bends toward Foaster instead of toward BCG X itself or Distyl. The catalyst is necessary; it isn't sufficient.

On revenue velocity, the counterfactual that would flip Foaster's tier is narrow and specific: the **Harvey** (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" template requires shipped enterprise logos and disclosed ARR -- not pricing language. Foaster's dossier discloses zero revenue, zero named customers, and a "pricing adapted to company size" SKU page. At pre-seed I don't penalize the absence of metrics, but I do penalize the absence of *signed counterparties* when the founders have a year of enterprise advisory under their belt -- that prior year should have produced at least one LOI worth naming. It didn't.

On single-miracle architecture, the parallel is **Color Genomics** (2015), which I architected explicitly as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing so the bet wasn't a chemistry bet. Foaster's miracle structure is the inverse: it needs LLM interview accuracy *and* enterprise HR-data trust posture (no SOC 2 disclosed) *and* the ability to displace incumbent advisor relationships *and* services-margin scaling on a 2-person team with a Paris/SF split. That's three miracles minimum, and the third -- displacing C-suite advisory incumbents on price -- is the one I've never seen a pre-seed services firm win.

My 36-month forecast on Foaster: lands at $3-8M ARR as a boutique AI-transformation shop serving 15-40 mid-market clients, then either gets acqui-hired by Distyl AI or a Tier-2 consultancy (Slalom, West Monroe, or a BCG X tuck-in) for talent and methodology rather than multiple, or pivots into a productized vertical workflow tool (most likely employee-research / org-diagnostics SaaS) by month 24. The mechanism producing that range: shipping founders + real catalyst + zero index position = a real business, capped by the services-firm gravity well.

The strongest objection cuts hard: Distyl AI raised $32M from Khosla on essentially the FDE-services thesis Foaster is running, which means a top-tier fund already validated that AI-native services can absorb growth capital, and Combes's Répondia track record is exactly the "founder shipped a comparable product to scale" criterion from my Invest Signals. If Foaster compounds the BCG/McKinsey catalyst with YC distribution into a few Fortune 500 logos quickly, the $3-8M ARR forecast looks pessimistic. The dossier evidence that wins against this case is the absence of a single named enterprise counterparty after a year of advisory work plus the price-wedge positioning -- "3-5x cheaper than McKinsey" is a sales script, not an index position. **One verifiable condition flips my verdict**: a signed $1M+ ACV contract with a named Fortune 1000 customer disclosed within 6 months, OR a pivot announcement repositioning Foaster as a productized workflow layer rather than a services firm. Until then, neutral -- a competent team in a real market without the distribution shape my framework requires.
