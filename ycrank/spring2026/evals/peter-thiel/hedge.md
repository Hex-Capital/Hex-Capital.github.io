# Hedge -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Hedge is a competently-distributed me-too in a category where Federato, Sixfold, and Cytora are already pursuing the same "AI ingests ACORD forms" thesis — its highest dimension is real agent pull (140+ retail agencies across 36 states with a 30-minute median first response), and its lowest is the monopoly secret, because Luke Button's pitch is precisely the consensus that every insurtech VC is currently nodding at. The dimensional signature here is a faster wholesale broker, not a new financial layer.

Palantir sits at the top of my Monopoly Secret ladder — a contrarian thesis that intelligence-grade data integration could be productized when every defense incumbent insisted it could not — and Wish sits at the bottom: no secret, just discount commodity routing. Hedge sits one rung above Wish. The dossier names Federato, Sixfold, and Cytora as funded competitors building the same AI-underwriting workflow, and the "AI native specialty insurance" tagline appears in roughly a dozen pitches per YC batch. Hedge's actual claim — 30+ carrier appointments and a CA surplus-lines license #6018729 — is regulatory throughput, not a secret. Every wholesale broker in America has those. CRC and Amwins have thousands. There is no important truth here that few people agree with.

PayPal sits at the top of my Distribution ladder — eBay sellers embedding the logo unprompted — and the bottom is a generic AI SaaS that buys Google ads against rising CAC. Hedge sits closer to PayPal than to that floor, which is the most surprising thing in the dossier: 140+ retail agencies in 36 states at pre-seed, a customer testimonial about Thursday-4pm-to-Friday-morning quote turnaround, and a 3x claimed hit ratio on hard risks. Retail agents are pulling Hedge into their submission flow because the wholesale incumbents respond in days, not minutes. Luke Button has clearly solved the sales problem with the same rigor as the product — which Zero to One demands and most founders neglect. This is the only dimension where Hedge earns conviction.

SpaceX defines my Definite Conviction ladder — a specific reusable-rocket plan with irreversible commitments — and the floor is the lean iterator who pivots toward product-market fit. Hedge sits in the lower-middle: Luke Button founded Fernstone (YC F25) as an "AI-powered insurance brokerage" and is now founder of Hedge (YC S26) as an "AI-native specialty insurance" wholesaler, with Fernstone's site still live and the relationship undisclosed. That is the lean playbook in two batches. The vertical is the same; the wedge moved from direct-to-SMB to wholesale-to-agents because the first wedge did not work as planned. On Power Law magnitude, Lyft is my mid-rung — a #2 in a category large enough to matter — and Wish is the floor. Hedge sits below Lyft: even a dominant US wholesale broker is a Newfront or RT Specialty, a multi-billion private outcome, not a fund-returner. AM Best revising the E&S outlook from positive to stable in November 2025 with rate softening into Q1-Q2 2026 compresses commission economics on the way in. On the atoms dimension, General Matter and Anduril sit at the top and Hedge sits near the floor: a CA surplus-lines license is a regulatory wrapper around what is fundamentally a software-and-relationship business. Sixfold could rebuild the ingestion layer in months; CRC could buy the license overnight.

My 36-month forecast for Hedge is acquisition by Amwins, Newfront, or a CRC-funded skunkworks at $50M-$150M on $5M-$15M in commission revenue, with Luke Button taking a head-of-AI-underwriting role inside the acquirer. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: real distribution traction creates a desirable acquihire target for incumbents who already own the carrier appointments and the data, while the absence of any monopoly secret prevents independent escape velocity. This is the inverse of the Palantir trajectory — Hedge embeds in agent workflows but the agents themselves are not the monopoly point; the carriers are, and Hedge does not own them.

The strongest case against my pass is the network-and-repeat-founder pattern that has produced my own highest-conviction checks. Luke Button is attempting essentially the same vertical twice in two YC batches, which is a definite-plan signature dressed as a pivot — Max Levchin built Affirm after PayPal in the same financial-rails domain. The 140+ agencies in 36 states pre-seed is genuine organic pull, not a vanity metric. Surplus lines crossed $90.3B in 2025, the AI-exclusion wave is forcing new categories of risk into E&S, and a wholesale broker that captures the AI-liability vertical the way early cyber underwriters captured cyber could become the category-defining placement shop. The Lyft exception applies when category magnitude overrides monopoly structure. But Lyft was a duopoly in a hundred-billion-dollar consumer category; Hedge is a 100th-funded entrant in a fragmented commission pool against incumbents with carrier relationships measured in decades. The flip condition is specific: if Hedge converts from pure brokerage to MGA with delegated binding authority across five-plus carriers in a defined vertical such as AI liability — building a proprietary loss-data pool that incumbents cannot replicate — I would re-underwrite. Until then, this is competition, and competition is for losers.
