# InsForge -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

InsForge is the consensus AI-infra trade dressed in open-source clothes -- a competent Apache-2.0 BaaS where the highest-scoring axis is mundane (Hang Huang is clearly the CEO, Tony Chang clearly the CTO) and the lowest-scoring axis is the one I actually care about most at pre-seed: nothing about "MCP server for Claude Code" registers as magically weird in Spring 2026, it registers as the deck every other YC P26 team is pitching. The 10.7k GitHub stars and 1.6x self-reported speedup over Supabase are real artifacts, but InsForge sits inside a category that Supabase ($544M raised, $5B valuation as of October 2025) and Convex are already shipping into.

Anduril is my HIGH rung for founder lived experience because Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever wrote a defense pitch -- the biography put him in front of the problem first. InsForge's Tony Chang at Databricks on Networking Connectivity and Monitoring is the closest the dossier comes to that template, and it is real infra credibility I respect; you don't fake your way onto Databricks's networking team. But the problem InsForge is solving -- "agents can't provision backends through human dashboards" -- was discovered by watching Claude Code and Cursor in 2025, not by living inside a backend-ops job that hurt. Hang Huang's Yale MBA plus ex-Amazon PM plus pro League of Legends is colorful and I'll grant a sliver of polymath credit, but the LoL years don't put him in front of the agent-provisioning problem; they make him interesting at a dinner table, which is a different test than the one this dimension is asking.

The pass example I keep in my head is the two ex-FAANG PMs with $20M oversubscribed and a $200B TAM slide -- the failure mechanism there is that the founder discovered the problem in a memo and the moat is the model, not the human. InsForge does not quite present that way; Tony's hands are dirty in infra and the team genuinely shipped 4,147 commits and v2.1.9. But the deeper failure pattern -- "find them before it becomes consensus" -- is screaming through this dossier. Four Show HN posts, 198 Product Hunt upvotes, a Discord at 1,036, and a self-published benchmark page (insforge.dev/alternatives/insforge-vs-supabase) claiming 47.6% vs 28.6% accuracy -- this is a company optimizing for the exact distribution surface every other agent-infra YC team is optimizing for. When everyone in the batch is fishing the same pond with the same lure, that is itself a deselection signal.

On CEO authority InsForge passes cleanly -- Hang Huang is named CEO, Tony Chang is named CTO, the LinkedIn titles match, no dossier hint of 50/50 equity ambiguity or unresolved tiebreaker -- the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia 2017 problem I keep relearning. I'll give InsForge full credit above my silence-default here because the dossier explicitly differentiates roles rather than leaving it ambient.

On capital intensity and unsexy-regulated swing, InsForge is squarely on the wrong side of the line for my thesis -- this is open-source developer tools, an Apache-2.0 GitHub repo and a managed-hosting tier on a pricing page. There is no hardware to walk a well pad with, no defense procurement cycle, no ketamine-clinic regulatory moat. The counterfactual that would flip this dimension is InsForge moving downstack into something physical -- on-prem appliance for regulated industries, FedRAMP for agent-operated backends -- and nothing in the current dossier suggests that turn.

On physical verifiability InsForge does better than the pure-API pass example because I can actually install it, point Claude Code at it, and watch a backend provision itself -- the same way I rode Uber in 2010 to feel the product. That is a tactile loop, not a founder-testimony loop. The 2,000+ databases hosted and the self-host on Hugging Face mean the artifact exists in the wild. It's not Flock Safety's camera bolted to a pole, but it's not a sales-deck abstraction either.

My 36-month forecast for InsForge: acqui-hired by Supabase, Vercel, or Cloudflare in the $25M-$80M range, or quietly faded as Supabase's native MCP server (already shipping per the Sealos comparison) compresses the interface differentiation -- I do not see InsForge reaching $20M ARR as an independent company, because the open-source flywheel that produced 10.7k stars cannot outrun $544M of incumbent capital with the same protocol surface.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the DeepMind exception -- pedigreed founders can win when they're polymath compounders, and the Hang/Tony pair (LoL pro + Yale MBA + Amazon PM paired with Databricks networking-infra + Meta Ads ML internships) is genuinely multi-domain. The bull would say: open source plus a 1.7x accuracy lead plus 919 forks compounds into the default agent-backend the same way Supabase compounded into the default Postgres BaaS, and Supabase took years to add MCP because of UX inertia. That stings because the benchmark lead, if reproduced by a third party, is the kind of asymmetry that catches incumbents flat-footed. What dismantles it is the absence of any third-party reproduction of the 1.6x/30%/1.7x numbers and the Product Hunt review flagging that "maturity comes with time" -- self-published benchmarks against a $5B incumbent that already ships your headline feature is a thin moat. The condition that flips me to invest: InsForge crosses $1M ARR with three named enterprise logos (not "5 launch partners") within 12 months AND an independent benchmark by a Claude Code or Cursor team confirms the accuracy delta. Absent those, I pass and watch.
