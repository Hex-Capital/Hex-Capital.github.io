# Async -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Async is a pivot-machine in search of a consequence. The dossier's own timeline — nonprofit fundraising agents → Skope outcome-based billing (Launch HN, Aug 2025) → Skope AI Agents for Law Firms → Async AI Agents for Service Businesses — is the single most informative fact in the file, and it tells me exactly what Ben Smith and Connor Park are: fast iterators chasing SMB service-firm workflow automation with a Word add-in against Harvey, Spellbook, and Legora. That is Async's analytical signature — highest on founder learning rate, lowest on consequence magnitude — and that pairing predetermines the shape of the bet.

Consequence-first, which is where I always start, puts Async next to my Pass Example, not next to **OpenAI** (2019). The **OpenAI** check was written because the success-state redraws what "the $15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" (Fortune 2026-03-06) even means. Async's stated success-state is that a solo firm like Clifton Law PLLC replaces manual document analysis and email drafting with custom agents wired into Word, Outlook, SharePoint, and a CRM. No incumbent regime collapses. No civilizational variable moves. Harvey at $11B post-money (TAMradar, Mar 2026) and Legora at $5.55B are already displacing exactly the labor Async names — the "law, healthcare, real estate" framing on withasync.com describes a slice of the workflow-automation market, not the collapse of billable-hours pricing or the disappearance of paralegal labor as a category. This is the "20-30% efficiency gain inside one workflow at one persona" profile I pass on in 30 seconds.

**Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) is the mechanism I compare Async's technical-risk profile against, and Async is not remotely on that curve. I led the $115M Series A into fusion because the science required three named miracles no consensus VC would touch — HTS magnets, plasma confinement, tritium fuel cycle — each falsifiable, each priced into a >$50M-to-first-milestone capital plan. Async's stack is a services engagement ("partner & audit → scope & deploy → prove & expand → maintain & compound") built on foundation-model tool-use, integrations to Microsoft Office, SOC 2 Type I certification, and Zero Data Retention. That is not a technical miracle — that is a systems-integration business. Spellbook already ships a Word add-in for contracts (GC.ai, 2026); Eve ships plaintiff lifecycle; Harvey ships everything. Any Tier-1 generalist can underwrite Async on execution risk alone, which is precisely why I would not.

Founder-defiance is where I look for the "earn the right" (TechCrunch 2013-02-26) signal, and Async's dossier is silent in a specific way. Ben Smith dropped out of Michigan at 20 to build commercial software and came from Merit and retail; Connor Park is a Michigan CS grad with a personal site and eleven public repos. Neither has documented law, healthcare, or real-estate operator experience, and neither has publicly contradicted an orthodoxy in any of those verticals — that is not the **Impossible Foods** (2011) profile of Pat Brown defying food-industry orthodoxy on animal protein, nor the Chelsea Finn / Sergey Levine **Physical Intelligence** (2024) profile of research PIs shipping against academic consensus. Async's founders are neither instigators nor domain experts — they are outsiders with an integration playbook, which is a neutral signal, not a positive one.

Learning rate is the one dimension where Async lights up, and it maps directly to my **Slash** (2024) Known Exception. Slash pivoted publicly through a Kanye West moment and I wrote a $100M Series C because the pivot demonstrated a founder learning function stronger than the consequence-magnitude prior. Async has executed three pivots inside twelve months, all documented, all shipped — that is quantifiable learning rate. What it is not, however, is thesis update. Slash pivoted within consumer/business banking; Async pivoted from nonprofit fundraising to SaaS billing to legal AI to three-vertical service businesses. That is not "we were wrong about X and here is Y" — that is thrash across incompatible customer bases, and the current withasync.com positioning across law, healthcare, and real estate with a two-person team suggests the pivots have not converged.

Required-capital is where Async fails my check-size test cleanly. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) at $115M Series A, **OpenAI** (2019) at $50M first check, **Genesis AI** (2025) at $105M co-led seed — these are deals where the roadmap required capital depth no one else would deploy. Async is a two-person YC S25 pre-seed at roughly $500K, running a services-plus-retainer model on commodity foundation models. I am over-resourced for this deal by two orders of magnitude, and there is no capital-intensity story in the dossier that would grow into a Khosla-sized follow-on.

My 36-month forecast: Async pivots a fourth time to a single productized vertical (most likely contract review for SMB law firms), reaches $1–3M ARR against Spellbook and Harvey encroachment, and is acquihired by a mid-tier legal-tech player at a modest multiple — or shuts down when the services model fails to compound into SaaS margins. The mechanism is straightforward: the learning-rate signal is real but the consequence ceiling is capped by the incumbents already owning the Word add-in surface, and services engagements at two founders across three regulated verticals do not scale to the labor-displacement magnitude I underwrite for.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash** (2024) exception itself — a YC-backed serial pivoter where I overrode the consequence-magnitude prior because the learning-rate evidence was overwhelming, and Async is arguably the same profile at earlier stage. It stings because the pattern-match is real: YC S25 batch, documented pivots, technical founders shipping fast, live customer in Clifton Law PLLC, SOC 2 Type I already earned. But **Slash** pivoted *within* consumer/business banking with a coherent through-line; Async's pivots crossed nonprofit fundraising, billing infrastructure, single-vertical legal, and now three-vertical services — that is not learning rate applied to a thesis, that is a thesis being searched for. And Harvey ($1.22B raised) plus Legora ($700M+ raised) are not the incumbent posture Slash faced in neobanking; they are already inside the exact Word add-in Async leads with. Verifiable flip condition: if Async lands three signed multi-firm contracts at $100K+ ACV in a single named vertical within six months, and publicly commits to killing the other two verticals, I re-underwrite as a **Slash**-shaped exception. Absent that, I pass.
