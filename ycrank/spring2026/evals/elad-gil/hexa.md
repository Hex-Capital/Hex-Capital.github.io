# Hexa -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Hexa is a vertical-AI-agent-for-manufacturers wrapper whose strongest dimension is that it doesn't require new physics and whose weakest is that the dossier contains zero revenue evidence in a 2026 AI cycle where silence on growth is itself a signal. Ishaan Makkar's LinkedIn reads "Stealth," the YC page lists no co-founders by name, the website routes to a Calendly link for "ishaan-hexaagents/30min," and the Key Facts table reads "No public data found" against Traction, Revenue Signal, Moat Signals, and Distribution Signals. That pairing -- a clean miracle architecture sitting on top of nothing observable -- is the dossier-silent AI seed pattern I keep passing on this cycle.

My entry-point question is what specifically catalyzed the manufacturer/distributor agent market in the last 24 months, and Hexa's dossier cannot name one. The HIGH rung on this ladder is **Anduril** (2022), where Ukraine produced a named, dateable Pentagon-software-inadequacy re-opening; the LOW rung is my **Lyft** Series C pass, where rideshare was a steady-state market dressed up as growth. Hexa sits closer to Lyft than to Anduril: the dossier offers an "[Inferred]" LLM-accuracy-crossed-a-threshold line and a 54%-still-on-spreadsheets stat from Makkar himself, but no specific recent regulatory shift, no named incumbent failure mode, no procurement-cycle change in the last 24 months. Manufacturing ERP has been "least digitized" for a decade -- that's a steady-state complaint, not a catalyst. "Market first and the strength of the team second" is precisely why I score this low.

On revenue velocity, the HIGH rung is **Harvey** (2023) at $3B->$5B->$8B in months -- the canonical "just working" curve from the TechCrunch Nov 2025 piece -- and the LOW rung is any AI seed where the dossier is silent on growth ("something's fundamentally broken," Tim Ferriss #863). Hexa is at the LOW rung outright: no customer count, no ARR, no pilot disclosures, no Product Hunt launch, no press, no public social accounts, no active hiring. A YC Spring 2026 company that has chosen to disclose nothing in May 2026 either has nothing to disclose or has a "false signal vs. just working" problem. Either way, my prior is that real growth is easy to disclose and Hexa hasn't.

The founder-shipping ladder runs from **Perplexity** (Aravind pinging me on LinkedIn and showing up a week later with the thing we discussed built) at the top, through the **Brex** operationally-disciplined-CEO archetype at the middle, down to promoter-without-shipping at the bottom. Hexa's CEO Ishaan Makkar has a "Stealth" LinkedIn, no documented prior exits, no GitHub repos surfaced, no Twitter, no education record retrieved, and an unverified search-snippet reference to a "Mann Patira" co-founder that the dossier explicitly flags as unconfirmed. This is closer to the LOW rung than to Brex -- and certainly not Perplexity -- because the dossier provides no shipped artifacts to point at. The persona-default of 7/20 is the right anchor and I'm going slightly below it because the YC page also lists no co-founders by name, which in 2026 reads as either pre-launch tidiness or a team-formation question.

On index position, the HIGH rung is **Stripe** as an index on e-commerce and **Decagon** as the workflow path every post-LLM CS team touches; the LOW rung is **Lyft** fighting for share in a winner-take-all market. Hexa's pitched position -- the agent layer across SAP, Oracle NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics, Epicor, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, Pipedrive -- *sounds* index-like, but the dossier names SAP Joule and Dynamics Copilot as native agent layers already shipping, plus Distro, Turian, Go Autonomous, Arphie, and Altura (eight million euros raised) as vertical competitors. Hexa has to win share against *both* the ERP incumbent agents *and* the specialized vertical agents. That's a Lyft-shaped fight, not a Stripe-shaped one. On single-miracle architecture -- HIGH rung **Color Genomics** as workflow on commodity sequencing, LOW rung stacked-miracle science projects -- Hexa is the cleanest: LLM document parsing is validated capability, and the rest is GTM. But integration depth across 8 ERPs with a 3-person team is a second execution miracle hiding inside the GTM line, which is why I'm at the persona default rather than above it.

My 36-month forecast: Hexa is acqui-hired for sub-$30M by an ERP integrator or absorbed into one of the better-specialized RFQ competitors (Distro is the most likely buyer given its distributor-order-entry depth), or it pivots to a single workflow -- most likely quoting-only for industrial distributors -- and never crosses $3M ARR. The mechanism is the high/low signature: a clean miracle architecture cannot rescue a company that has neither a catalyst-driven market window nor a disclosed revenue curve, while SAP Joule and Dynamics Copilot eat the mid-market from above and Distro/Turian out-specialize Hexa from the side.

The strongest case against my pass is that manufacturer/distributor workflow *is* a category re-opening I should have named -- 80% of CPOs naming AI a top 2026 priority (tryleverage.ai), agentic frameworks crossing the write-back-to-ERP threshold in the last 12-24 months, and **Decagon** (2023) and **Abridge** (2023) both proving vertical agents in legacy-workflow categories can compound. The known-exception bear-check: Decagon and Abridge both landed in *AI Market Clarity* "wide-open" sub-markets and both showed early enterprise pilots within a quarter of launch. Hexa shows none of that, and my own *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy doesn't name manufacturer/distributor agents as wide-open -- it names accounting, compliance, financial tooling, sales agents, security. Flip condition: a signed pilot with a named mid-market manufacturer at $50K+ ACV disclosed within 60 days, with month-over-month expansion. Until then, this is a Pass -- not a regret-Pass, an obvious-Pass.
