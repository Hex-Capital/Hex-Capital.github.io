# Lattice Health -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Christine Park reads MRIs at 2am as a PGY-3 neurosurgery resident at UW and then writes deep-learning code on those same scans for her Stanford CS PhD — Lattice Health is one of the rare imaging-AI dossiers I open where the founder is *of* the imaging suite, not *visiting* it, and yet the company itself is a solo-founder horizontal-platform pitch with no website, no logos, and no tactile artifact I can touch. That high-low pairing — lived clinical domain on top, zero physical surface underneath — is Lattice Health's analytical signature, and it puts the company between a Hanke-shaped founder and a developer-tools abstraction I cannot ride.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is the rung Lattice Health is reaching for on founder-lived-experience: somebody who shipped a complicated thing in an adjacent technical domain before pointing the rifle at the bigger problem, with the social license to walk into the customer's environment without flinching. Park has the published deep-learning imaging research on arXiv (2511.18208, 2025) and the medRxiv point-of-care MRI midline-shift paper from 2022, which is the rare biography where the dataset, the diagnosis, and the model architecture have all passed through the same set of hands — the way Luckey's hands had passed through Oculus optics before Anduril. Where Lattice Health diverges from the Luckey rung is severity of commitment: Luckey left VR; Park is concurrently a UW neurosurgery resident *and* a Stanford CS PhD candidate per the UW Neurosurgery bio, which is two more-than-full-time commitments stacked on top of a YC company. Lived experience is necessary; it does not substitute for being in the building.

Two ex-FAANG PMs with a $200B TAM slide is the failure mode I keep on file, and the mechanism is that the product is a pure abstraction nobody can install, photograph, or hand to a friend over the weekend. Lattice Health does not fail this test for the same *reason* — Park is not a memo-founder — but Lattice Health fails it on the *artifact*: the YC page shows no website field at time of fetch, there is a name-collision with latticehealth.ai serving a completely different biotech-automation customer, and the entire pitch is an "operating system" layer sitting between PACS, EHR, and third-party FDA-cleared models. I cannot scan, ride, deploy, or photograph Lattice Health from my couch. Niantic I could play. HQ Trivia I could play. Postmates delivered to my door. Lattice Health is the kind of infrastructure where the customer is a hospital CIO and the artifact is a Helm chart.

Magically weird — Lattice Health is not. The YC tagline "operating system for clinical AI in imaging" lands inside the most-consensus structural pitch of the 2025-26 batches: a horizontal orchestration layer over a fragmented vendor stack, competing directly with Microsoft/Nuance Precision Imaging Network and Bayer-owned Blackford Analysis, both of which already operate the marketplace embedded inside the radiology reporting workflow. Crusoe (~2018) burning North Dakota gas flares for Bitcoin had three credible VCs passing on aesthetic grounds before I wrote the check — that is the texture I am looking for, and Lattice Health's category has Aidoc at $250M+ raised and Rad AI at $140M+ raised crowding it. The category is not laughed at; it is bid up.

Solo founder is a clean read on tiebreaker authority — Lattice Health has team size 1 per the YC page, which is the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll feud I have spent years citing. Eighteen months from now, if Park brings on a technical co-founder before Series A, the question becomes whether the founder agreement establishes Park as the unambiguous CEO with operating tiebreaker, because the moment a hospital enterprise deal slips and the board wants someone "more operational," the GameCrush (~2011) lesson reactivates. For now, Lattice Health scores cleanly on this axis simply because there is one named human and her name is Christine Park.

Capital-intensity and regulated-unsexiness is where Lattice Health partly earns its keep — to flip this dimension from a mid-tier read into a Vigil Labs-class swing, Park would have to demonstrate that Lattice Health is selling into the parts of the hospital that nobody else will touch (FDA-cleared model monitoring, post-market surveillance, HIPAA-bounded model-drift telemetry on PHI-laden imaging) rather than the marketplace layer that Bayer already paid to own when they acquired Blackford in 2023. The dossier reads "B2B Infrastructure" without specifying which side of that line; the >700 FDA-cleared imaging devices the MarketsandMarkets 2025-2030 report references are the regulated surface I want Lattice Health to live on, not the orchestration surface where Microsoft already lives.

Forecast — 36 months out, Lattice Health is acqui-hired by Microsoft/Nuance or Bayer/Blackford for $10-30M as a talent-and-IP tuck-in for Park's clinical-domain credibility, or Park returns to full-time neurosurgery practice and Lattice Health is wound down; the mechanism is that the lived-experience signal is real but a 0.2-FTE solo founder competing with three nine-figure-funded incumbents inside 18-month hospital sales cycles does not get to Series A on the strength of one neurosurgeon's nights and weekends.

The strongest bear-flip against my own pass is the DeepMind (~2013) exception — Hassabis was a pedigreed polymath whose cross-domain compounding (chess prodigy, neuroscientist, game designer) overrode my bias against credentialed founders, and Park's Duke MD/MEng-AI plus Stanford CS PhD plus practicing neurosurgery is structurally the same kind of polymath stack. The reason it does not save Lattice Health here is that Hassabis arrived full-time with Suleyman and Legg as co-founders — three brains, all in. Park is solo, and the residency plus PhD math means Lattice Health gets a fraction of one polymath. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: Park drops the Stanford PhD or pauses the residency *and* a named health system (UCSF, Duke Health, UW Medicine) signs an LOI or pilot — at that point Lattice Health stops being a side project with an impressive CV and starts being a Hanke-of-imaging swing I would size into.
