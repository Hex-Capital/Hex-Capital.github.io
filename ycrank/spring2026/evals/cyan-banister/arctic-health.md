# Arctic Health -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Arctic Health is a regulated-B2B back-office swing where the unsexy lane is real but I cannot touch the product and the founders never lived inside the workflow they are automating -- Sam Slezek covered insurance equities at Morgan Stanley and built product at Scale AI, which is adjacent reading, not credentialing a multi-state clinic at 2am because a payer rejected a CAQH packet. The signature here is "regulated swing without lived ownership," and that pairing is precisely what the YC description gives away when it concedes the system is "trained by experienced industry experts who handle every edge case" -- meaning the domain knowledge sits in contractors, not founders.

The lived-experience ladder runs from Palmer Luckey at **Anduril** (2017), who had shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he ever pitched defense, down through **DeepMind** (~2013), where Hassabis was pedigreed but the polymath compounding overrode my anti-credential bias, and lands at the consensus YC AI darling I passed on -- ex-FAANG PMs who discovered the problem in a memo. Arctic Health sits between the Pass darling and DeepMind, closer to the Pass darling: Slezek's Morgan Stanley insurance-equity coverage and Mehrish's Zenefits engineering are *near* the payer-data domain but no one on the founding team ran credentialing operations, which is why "experienced industry experts" are quietly doing the actual work behind the agents. Arctic Health does not have a Palmer-in-the-garage moment, and absent that, the AI agent is downstream of contractors who could equally well power Verifiable's CredAgent.

**Crusoe** (~2018) is the high rung on magically-weird because two or three credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds before I wrote the check; **Niantic** (~2015) is the middle rung where the weirdness was real but Ingress had a quiet cult; the consensus YC AI darling is the low rung. Arctic Health sits at the low rung, not the middle: Medallion has raised $130M and shipped an "AI Credentialing Hub," **Verifiable** raised $47.13M and shipped CredAgent in February 2026 -- three months before Arctic Health's batch -- and **Assured** already pulled $6M from First Round with the NCQA cert and the same 2-day credentialing claim. Five funded companies converging on identical AI-agent credentialing is the definition of consensus, and Menlo Ventures arriving early means the round is priced as a known category, not "find them before it becomes consensus."

Arctic Health does pass the single-CEO ladder cleanly -- Slezek as CEO, Clark as COO, Mehrish as CTO is the structural inverse of the **HQ Trivia** (2017) Yusupov/Kroll co-CEO collapse, and closer to the **SpaceX** (2008) clean-line rung than the **GameCrush** (~2011) governance failure. That earns Arctic Health more than the absent-evidence default, even though I have no operating agreement to read.

The unsexy-regulated ladder is where Arctic Health scores best: **SpaceX** (2008) and **Anduril** (2017) are the high rung, **Vigil Labs** (August 2025) is the middle rung as regulated-finance compounding, and the AI-app SaaS wrapper is the low rung. Arctic Health sits between Vigil Labs and the SaaS floor -- NCQA gating, 300+ payer portals, and 50-state licensure are genuinely regulated terrain where coverage compounds, but Assured is already NCQA-certified and Arctic Health is not, so the regulatory moat is currently *against* them. On physical verifiability, **Flock Safety** (~2018) is the high rung where I read the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café and **Uber** (2010) is the rideable mid-rung; Arctic Health is at the developer-tools floor -- I cannot install it, photograph it, or hand it to a friend without being a clinic admin, which is exactly the abstraction problem that loses me at default minus a point.

My 36-month forecast: Arctic Health is acquired by Medallion, Symplr, or a PE-backed CVO roll-up at a $20-60M outcome by mid-2029, or it grinds at $1-3M ARR after Verifiable's CredAgent locks in the SMB segment with NCQA-cert distribution. The mechanism producing this is the lived-experience gap meeting the consensus-category gap -- when the moat is contractor knowledge and five funded competitors are racing the same browser-automation library, the acquirer's logo on the cap table is worth more than the founder's biography, and that is the wrong end of my barbell.

The bull case stings, so I will name it: Slezek's Scale AI product instincts plus Clark's Instabase inside-sales DNA plus Mehrish's Airbnb-scale engineering is a genuinely competent SMB-stack, the YC wedge of "small and mid-sized organizations priced out by Medallion" is a real underserved cut, and Menlo Ventures does not write a check for nothing. If Arctic Health gets NCQA-certified before Verifiable's CredAgent productizes down-market, and the inside-sales motion delivers 100 small-clinic logos in 18 months, this is a $200M+ outcome and I will have missed it on lived-experience purism. What dismantles the bull case is the YC page's own concession that "experienced industry experts handle every edge case" -- that is the structural inverse of Palmer Luckey shipping the prior product before founding the company, and it means the moat lives in the contractors, not the cap table. The flip condition is concrete: if it turns out Slezek spent two-plus years actually operating credentialing inside a multi-state provider organization rather than covering insurance equities from a Morgan Stanley desk, I reopen the file. Until then, this is the consensus-AI-agent pass, dressed in a regulated-category cardigan.
