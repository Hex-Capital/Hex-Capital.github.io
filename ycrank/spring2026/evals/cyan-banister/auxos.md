# Auxos -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Auxos's signature is a clear-CEO consumer-pedigree team selling into the most consensus AI category of the cycle -- Ashton Daniel runs the ship, Kerry Lu has real simulation chops from Amazon Ads, and the three of them met fencing for Team USA at Columbia/Duke/Penn before BCG and Meta. Strongest on governance, weakest on weirdness. That pairing -- clean leadership inside a crowded room -- describes a company that will execute fine and still get outraised into irrelevance. Aaru raised $50M+ at a $1B headline in December 2025 and Simile pulled $100M Series A; Auxos is the YC pre-seed walking into that room.

The high rung on my Founder-Lived ladder is Palmer Luckey in 2017, who shipped Oculus from a garage before pivoting to defense -- the problem owned him before he named it. Auxos's CEO Ashton Daniel "ran surveys, panels, and interviews" at BCG on Fortune 500 due diligence, which is the buyer's seat, not the problem's seat -- he watched panel research be slow and expensive on someone else's clock. That is not Luckey-in-the-garage; it is a consultant noticing a category. Kerry Lu is the one piece that resists the pattern: building "the infra for ad simulations modeling 40% of US e-commerce traffic" at Amazon Ads is genuine simulation-adjacent lived experience, and ex-Vorticity (YC S19) seismic-wave simulation is the kind of polymath cross-domain compounding the DeepMind exception was built for. But the CEO is Ashton, not Kerry, and Ashton's origin is a BCG slide deck.

The low rung on my Magically-Weird ladder is the consensus YC AI-agent darling I described in my pass example -- six top funds chasing, deck opens with TAM. Auxos is the same shape, one rung removed: the category is already trading at $1B headlines (Aaru, TechCrunch Dec 2025) and nine-figure Series As (Simile, ai2.work 2026) before Auxos has a pricing page. Five named direct competitors in the dossier -- Aaru, Evidenza, Artificial Societies, Synthetic Users, Simile -- is not "find them before it becomes consensus"; it is arrive after consensus has been priced. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, but here the game is plainly the one being played by everyone, and Auxos is not the funded version of it.

Single CEO authority -- mechanism analysis. Ashton Daniel holds the CEO title, Kerry Lu is CTO, Jerry Wu is CPO, and the three "have known each other for 15 years" through fencing. Over an 18-month compounding window, fencing-team cohesion is actually a positive: shared adolescence is a stronger glue than co-founders-met-at-Stanford-CS-class, and the title delineation is unambiguous in the dossier. Where this dimension fails is silence on operating-agreement tiebreakers or vesting -- Auxos has the title but not the documented protocol. Mid-high on this axis, not top.

Capital-intensive frontier swing -- counterfactual. For Auxos to flip from a 3/15 to a 10/15 on this dimension, the dossier would have to show first-party panel recruitment infrastructure that competitors can't replicate -- field operations, regulated-population access (medical, defense, financial), or a hardware capture loop. What Auxos actually has is "AI" tagged on the YC page and a Cal.com demo link to the CEO. The "one real person behind every digital twin" line is the closest gesture toward an unsexy operational moat, but the recruitment unit economics that would make that capital-intensive are unverified. This is software, in the saturated software lane.

Physical verifiability -- single-anchor parallel to Flock Safety. Flock's Garrett Langley moat was that I could read his Wi-Fi name at a Four Seasons and then watch a camera in a neighborhood. Auxos has no app I can install, no artifact I can photograph, no self-serve checkout, no Product Hunt presence, and a demo-booking link as the entry point. The "847 packaging tests, 2,104 ad creative A/B tests" figures on useauxos.com are explicitly illustrative, not disclosed usage. I cannot hand Auxos to a friend; I cannot ride it.

The 36-month forecast on Auxos: acqui-hired by Qualtrics, Dynata, or a marketing-cloud incumbent for under $30M in cash-and-stock, or alive at $1-3M ARR after pivoting into a vertical (pharma respondent simulation, political polling) where regulated-population recruitment becomes the actual moat. The mechanism is that Aaru and Simile will absorb the enterprise-logo air with their balance sheets, and Auxos's only structural advantage -- Kerry's simulation infra -- is not a wedge a customer buys; it is a craft a customer assumes.

The strongest objection to passing on Auxos is the DeepMind exception: Hassabis, Suleyman, and Legg arrived with elite pedigree, not outsider trajectories, and the polymath cross-domain compounding overrode my bias. Kerry Lu compounding seismic-wave simulation (Vorticity) and ad-traffic simulation (Amazon, 40% of US e-commerce) into human-behavior simulation is the polymath signature, and Ashton's NCAA fencing championship plus BCG plus Columbia CS is exactly the rare cross-domain stack I have funded before. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull case is the *category timing*: DeepMind in ~2013 was inventing a field; Auxos in 2026 is the fifth named entrant in a field where the leader just printed a $1B headline. Polymath-pedigree overrides consensus-category penalty only when the category itself is non-consensus. One verifiable condition flips this: a signed enterprise pilot with a named regulated-population customer (FDA-track pharma, defense contractor, central bank polling) at six-figure ACV in the next two quarters -- because that would mean Auxos found the unsexy lane the funded peers can't enter, and I would rebut my own pass.
