# WithAI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Ian McInnis ran the "how can we use LLMs to invest" workstream inside Bridgewater before starting WithAI, which is the highest mark on my founder-lived-experience axis I have seen in this batch -- and yet WithAI is a B2B software wrapper that I cannot install, ride, or photograph, and that competes directly with Rogo's $75M Series C from January 2026. That high/low pairing tells me exactly what WithAI is: a Bridgewater-insider distribution play dressed as an enterprise AI product, where the founder is unusually *of* the customer but the artifact is the most consensus possible shape for 2026.

On founder origin, the HIGH rung is Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) -- shipped the prior product, lived the domain. The LOW rung is the consensus YC AI-agent darling whose founders "discovered the problem in a memo." WithAI sits closer to the high rung than I expected: Ian McInnis was an Investment Associate at Bridgewater leading the exact AI-in-investing workstreams the company now productizes, and Ben Finch was a founding researcher at Sentient Labs co-authoring the OML paper (IACR 2024/1573). The pin is Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour as angels -- the co-CIOs of Bridgewater wrote checks into their own former associate. That is autobiographical, not analytical. The drag is Princeton class of 2023, three years out, no prior shipped product at scale -- so this is mid-to-high on the ladder, not Luckey-tier where someone already built and sold an Oculus.

On physical-world verifiability, the HIGH rung is Niantic where I played Ingress for three years before John Hanke pitched me, and the LOW rung is the developer-tools B2B API I cannot evaluate without founder testimony. WithAI is the LOW rung in pure form. The product, "Multiplier," deploys inside the hedge fund's own cloud so neither WithAI nor Anthropic can see it, which is structurally the opposite of "a normal human can install, ride, scan, or photograph it within 48 hours." There is one named client, Mercator Partners, and a testimonial quote -- "tremendously accelerated" from "existentially threatened" -- that I cannot touch, observe in the wild, or verify by playing with the thing the way I played Ingress. WithAI fails the Flock Safety Wi-Fi-name test by category, not by accident.

On magically weird, Crusoe burning North Dakota gas flares is the HIGH rung and the consensus YC AI darling is the LOW. WithAI sits between LOW and MID. The lane -- AI for fundamental hedge funds -- is exactly where Rogo ($154M raised, 27x ARR growth disclosed), AlphaSense (Generative Search launched January 2026), and Hebbia are visibly fighting; that is not a "two credible VCs passed on aesthetic grounds" setup, it is a "ten funds are circling" setup. Bridgewater co-CIO angels are a warm-pedigree signal, the inverse of the magically-weird filter. WithAI is not yet consensus the way Rogo is, but the round is priced into an obvious lane.

On single CEO authority, the HIGH rung is one named founder with majority authority; the LOW rung is the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll co-CEO collapse I keep on my desk as a calibration. WithAI sits MID-to-HIGH: Ian is CEO, Ben is CTO, roles delineated -- two Princeton classmates who already co-founded a "technology investment firm" together, which is friction-tested. The dossier names no equity split or tiebreaker protocol, so I apply my default with an upward nudge for the clear title delineation. On capital-intensity and unsexy-regulated, the HIGH rung is SpaceX/Anduril/Vigil Labs; the LOW rung is undifferentiated AI SaaS. WithAI is closer to LOW -- hedge-fund tooling is regulated at the customer, not at the moat. The "60+ integrations" with Bloomberg, FactSet, Snowflake, and Enfusion are not a compounding regulatory moat; they are sales-engineering work that Rogo or a well-funded competitor can replicate.

Forecast, 36 months: WithAI rides Jensen/Karniol-Tambour intros to 6-12 fundamental hedge fund logos and lands at $4-10M ARR, services-heavy, then gets squeezed by Rogo expanding its hedge-fund SKU and either tucks into Rogo/AlphaSense for a modest acqui-hire or grinds as a boutique implementation shop with single-digit-million revenue. The mechanism is straightforward: Ian's Bridgewater social capital opens warm doors that horizontal competitors cannot cold-call into, but the artifact has no commodity-AI escape velocity, and the moat is per-customer workflow setup rather than anything that compounds across the network.

The bull case that stings is HandUp -- I wrote the check there because the founder mirrored my own trajectory, overriding the commercial-defensibility filter, so why not write the same exception for Ian when his Bridgewater origin mirrors the buyer in a way no other AI-for-finance founder can replicate? Because HandUp was a personal-mission override on a non-venture-scale category I was willing to take a loss on; WithAI is asking me to fund competition against a $154M-raised Rogo on enterprise-sales economics, which is a different ask entirely. The override applies to mission, not to commercial moat. One verifiable condition flips me: if WithAI signs three or more tier-1 hedge funds outside the Bridgewater warm-intro network -- Citadel, Millennium, Point72-class -- in the next twelve months, then Ian's lived-experience distribution is more durable than I am scoring it and I would revisit at the bridge.
