# Gojiberry AI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is the cleanest case I have read this batch of a high-learning-rate team aimed at a low-consequence success-state -- $2.5M ARR in 10 months and a documented v1 wipeout meet "AI SDR market $5.81B in 2026" in a deck that never names an incumbent regime that disappears if Gojiberry AI wins. The Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Dylan Teixeira founder profile -- CoCo AI exit, Edusign exit, a pivot from a 2,000-person waitlist with zero paying customers into a profitable 35% MoM business -- is exactly the kind of founder texture I write checks behind. But my Entry Point is not founders. It is world-state at maximum ambition, and Gojiberry AI's maximum ambition is "B2B sales teams book more meetings on LinkedIn." That is the **pass example** from my own framework, not a portfolio anchor.

Start with the dimension where Gojiberry AI scores highest, which is founder learning rate. **Slash** (2024) is the load-bearing analogy: a YC-pedigreed team whose publicly told Kanye West pivot story demonstrated learning-rate strong enough to override my consequence-magnitude prior, which is why I wrote $100M into a Series C for consumer business banking I would otherwise have ignored. The mechanism is identical here -- Gojiberry AI v1 was an "AI sales assistant" that converted zero of a 2,000-person waitlist into paying customers (superframeworks.com), and the same two founders rebuilt into intent-led LinkedIn outreach and crossed $2.5M ARR profitable inside ten months. That is real learning, compounded across the CoCo AI ~$500K ARR exit and Dylan Teixeira's Edusign multi-million ARR exit. Where Gojiberry AI diverges from the Slash override is that Slash was a growth-stage check where the pivot was already priced into a $100M Series C; at a $500K YC pre-seed I am paying for a consequence I refuse, not for execution I already see.

Then look at the dimension where Gojiberry AI scores lowest, which is consequence magnitude. The failure mechanism in my **Pass Example** -- "we use GPT-4 to summarize sales calls and integrate with HubSpot" -- is that the success-state is a 20-30% workflow gain at one persona inside one motion, indistinguishable from a feature Apollo.io or HubSpot ships in twelve months. That mechanism is alive in Gojiberry AI's own positioning: Pro plan $99/month for two LinkedIn senders against Apollo.io database-sequencer competition (blog.gojiberry.ai/blog/best-alternatives-to-apollo-io), Pierre-Eliott explicitly framing the wedge as "Clay requires a GTM engineer, Gojiberry AI runs end-to-end." No incumbent regime collapses if Gojiberry AI wins -- SDR agencies were already dying, and the labor displaced is a sliver of the "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" frame I gave Fortune (2026-03-06), not the radiology-read or long-haul-diesel kind of collapse.

On technical and capital risk -- the dimension that decides whether consensus VCs are repelled -- the mechanism analysis is unforgiving over 18 months. Gojiberry AI's stack is LinkedIn intent scraping plus 15+ enrichment providers plus LLM message generation; every component is commodity, and the moat the dossier asserts ("every campaign makes Gojiberry smarter") is the same flywheel claim Artisan ($46.1M raised, $5M ARR), 11x.ai ($76M raised, $10.5M ARR), and Clay ($204M raised, $100M ARR at $3.1B per TechCrunch August 2025) all make. By month 18 either the cross-customer data network at 2,000+ customers compounds into measurably higher reply rates than 11x.ai's Alice, or it is a marketing line. There is no science risk for me to underwrite -- this would have been funded by any Tier-1 generalist seed, which is precisely where I am over-resourced and structurally wrong as the buyer.

On founder defies expert consensus -- the counterfactual question is what would have to be true for Gojiberry AI to clear this bar. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant, Román Czerny, and Dylan Teixeira are repeat B2B SaaS operators productizing the exact cold-outbound motion they ran at CoCo AI to ~$500K ARR. That is domain-expert continuity, not the TED Talk McKinsey-1986-cellphone pattern I look for. To flip a tier I would need Gojiberry AI to have publicly contradicted SDR-industry orthodoxy -- a stated thesis that human SDRs are obsolete inside 24 months, a refusal to integrate with HubSpot/Pipedrive on principle, a Sergey Levine-style public research position. Instead the dossier shows founders integrating into existing CRMs at $99/month. That is conformity priced cheap.

On capital profile -- the single-anchor parallel is **OpenAI** (2019), where my $50M first check matched a roadmap that required foundation-model training depth no commodity SaaS economics could service. Gojiberry AI is the inverse: $500K YC standard deal, already profitable at $2.5M ARR, 35% MoM. The roadmap does not require $50M to a first commercial milestone -- it requires more LinkedIn senders and a CSM hire. I am structurally over-resourced for Gojiberry AI by an order of magnitude, and a $250K angel position from me adds no signal Andrew Miklas's YC partnership does not already provide.

Forecast at 36 months: Gojiberry AI either reaches $20-40M ARR and is acquired by HubSpot, Apollo.io, or Salesforce for $200-400M sometime in 2028-2029, OR LinkedIn enforces against sender-account automation and Gojiberry AI shrinks below $5M ARR and pivots into a Clay-style enrichment infra play. The mechanism producing that bimodal outcome is the LinkedIn platform-dependency in the dossier -- core product runs on LinkedIn sender accounts (prospeo.io) with no disclosed mitigation -- combined with the founder pattern of selling early, which is what Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Dylan Teixeira did at CoCo AI 2025 and Edusign 2025. Strong execution + commodity stack + sellable founders = mid-nine-figure acquisition, not a $72B DoorDash IPO.

The strongest objection runs through the Slash exception directly: I overrode consequence-magnitude on a YC-backed fintech because the founder pivot was documented and the learning rate was undeniable, and Gojiberry AI presents exactly that profile -- v1 zero-paying waitlist, pivot to $2.5M ARR profitable in 10 months, two prior exits between the founders, YC pedigree under Andrew Miklas. Why is Gojiberry AI not the Slash trade? Because Slash's $100M Series C priced a pivot already validated by two years of revenue retention in a market with banking-grade lock-in, while Gojiberry AI's pivot is 10 months old, the moat claim is unaudited ("self-reported ARR ... 11x.ai precedent shows reported AI-SDR ARR can mask retention issues" -- Sifted 2025), and the platform Gojiberry AI rides can revoke access in one ToS update. The condition that flips my verdict is specific: an 18-month NRR figure above 110% and disclosed evidence that Gojiberry AI's cross-customer model outperforms Clay's enrichment graph on a falsifiable reply-rate benchmark. Until then I pass -- not because Gojiberry AI is a bad company, but because backing it would mean optimizing for probability over consequence, which is the framework inversion my **KiOR** (~2008) loss was meant to make impossible.
