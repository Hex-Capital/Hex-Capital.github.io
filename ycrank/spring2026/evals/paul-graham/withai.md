# WithAI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

WithAI is the rare credentialed-insider play where the highest dimension -- Ian McInnis leading the "how can we use LLMs to invest" workstream inside Bridgewater itself -- runs directly into the lowest dimension, which is the complete absence of an animal pre-funding stunt. The dossier gives me one named client (Mercator Partners) and two Bridgewater co-CIO angels (Greg Jensen, Karen Karniol-Tambour), which is channel-via-rolodex, not cereal-via-grind. WithAI is what happens when smart insiders build for the world they just left -- and that is a different species of company than the one I keep finding in my top decile.

On animal resourcefulness pre-funding, the high rung is **Airbnb** (2009) selling 1,000+ cereal boxes at $40 each because nobody would write the check, and the low rung is the polished two-MBA AI-wrapper team from my Pass Example with a deck and no artifact. WithAI sits between **Dropbox** (2007)'s Hacker News demo (mid rung) and that MBA pass, closer to the MBA end -- the Mercator testimonial moving a client from "existentially threatened" to "tremendously accelerated" is real, but it arrived after the Bridgewater angels were already on the cap table, not before. Ian and Benjamin Finch have not, per the dossier, shown me the equivalent of Brian Armstrong's Android wallet built to overturn his **Coinbase** (2012) rejection. The tracks an animal leaves are missing.

On the schlep dimension WithAI is its strongest. The high rung is **Stripe** (2010)'s payments-compliance grind, **Coinbase** (2012) is the regulatory bitcoin schlep, and the low rung is any horizontal chatbot whose work ends when the API call returns. WithAI sits between **DoorDash** (2013) (mid) and **Stripe** (high): client-cloud deployment so neither WithAI nor Anthropic touch the data, 60+ named integrations (Bloomberg, FactSet, Snowflake, Enfusion), and a white-glove implementation model are exactly the kind of work that scares the AI-wrapper crowd. **Rogo** raised $154M and routes around this by shipping IB deliverables; **AlphaSense** routes around it by indexing its own content library. WithAI runs toward the part nobody wants. This is real.

Organic founder-pain places WithAI between **Coinbase** (2012) (mid -- Armstrong wanted bitcoin and built the compliance layer) and **Dropbox** (2007) (high -- Houston's lost USB stick). Ian McInnis was an Investment Associate at Bridgewater leading the AI-in-investing workstream, which is as literal as founder-problem fit gets in this category, and Benjamin Finch's Sentient Labs and OML paper (IACR ePrint 2024/1573) make him the credible second half. The gap from **Dropbox** is that the dossier doesn't show me Ian using Multiplier himself before he left, only that he saw the AIA Labs threat from inside.

Hacker genealogy puts WithAI between **Reddit** (2005) (Aaron Swartz, RSS spec, high) and **Dropbox** (2007) (Houston shipped at MIT, mid). Finch as a founding researcher at Sentient Labs and co-author on OML is genuine Lisp-lineage-adjacent hacker output -- but there is no GitHub footprint listed for either founder, which is the trail my Unstated Pattern says hackers leave. Cofounder configuration -- two Princeton '23 classmates who "became fast friends" co-founding a prior investment firm together, plus an unnamed third -- sits between the **Airbnb** trio (high) and pairs-who-met-at-a-hackathon (low), closer to the high rung, though the unnamed third is a small unknown.

My 36-month forecast: WithAI reaches $4-9M ARR with 10-18 named hedge-fund clients, fails to escape the Bridgewater-network gravity well, and gets acquired by **FactSet** or **AlphaSense** in 2028-2029 for $80-180M. The mechanism is exactly the signature: organic insider knowledge produces a real product that real customers want, but the absence of animal mode caps the trajectory at strategic acquihire rather than a "next Google" outcome the way I once said about **Stripe** (2010).

The strongest case against my lean leans on my **Stripe** Known Exception. I funded the Collisons despite Patrick declining the full YC bootcamp because the **Auctomatic** (2007) outcome at ages 19/17 overrode my process preference -- specific founder facts beat the rule. Could Ian McInnis leading the LLM-in-investing workstream inside Bridgewater itself, with the two Bridgewater co-CIOs writing personal checks, be the equivalent override? The bull says yes: this is specific knowledge no outsider can replicate, and the angels are the most credible distribution signal in fundamental investing. The counter is that my Unique Lens hunts a contrarian fact about the founders, not specific knowledge -- specific knowledge can be hired, animals cannot, and the dossier shows credentials plus warm intros plus one client, not the cereal-box moment. The verifiable condition that would flip me: three named hedge-fund clients outside the Bridgewater network within six months, with Ian and Ben personally running each implementation door-to-door the way **Airbnb** (2009) photographed New York apartments themselves. Until that artifact arrives, I pass at the upper edge of neutral.
