# qomplement -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Kerim Taray and Andres Garza did RPA consulting for Mexican enterprises before qomplement, which is the one thing in this dossier I cannot dismiss -- they actually touched the manual PO/invoice misery they are now selling agents against. But qomplement is also "Agentic ERP for supply chain operations," which in Spring 2026 is the single most-crowded sentence a YC founder can put on a slide, and Pivot just pulled $40M in a Paris Series B with €2.5B of invoices already running through it. That high-low pairing -- real founder-market touch in a category the entire venture industrial complex is converging on -- tells me what kind of company qomplement is. It is the competent founder team in the consensus lane, and the consensus lane is exactly where my thesis says to look away.

Anduril is the high rung on founder-lived-experience because Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus to a billion-dollar exit before he ever drew a Lattice diagram -- the biography put him in front of the problem, not the memo. Kerim and Andres are not at that rung. They are at the mid rung: two Tec de Monterrey CS grads who built RPA for Mexican enterprises and watched grocery and trucking back-offices drown in paper, which is closer to "lived adjacent to the problem as the implementer" than "of the place." That is genuine -- the Expansión and Milenio coverage corroborates it -- and it puts qomplement materially above the absent-evidence default. It does not put them where Chase Lochmiller was when he walked onto a North Dakota well pad. The RPA consulting is a credible origin, just not an autobiographical one in the sense that triggers a check on its own.

The consensus-YC-AI-agent-darling pass example exists in my framework precisely for cases like qomplement. Six top funds chasing, a $200B TAM slide, two ex-FAANG PMs -- I described the failure mechanism and walked. qomplement is not a perfect overlay onto that pattern, because Kerim and Andres are LATAM RPA operators, not Stanford-FAANG PMs, and that is a real differentiator from the script. But the structural pattern is intact: Pivot at $70M total raised, ORO Labs positioning as "Category King," SAP and Oracle publishing native agentic roadmaps inside the very ERPs qomplement integrates against. qomplement is being built in the wedge between three well-capitalized horizontal players and the incumbents whose channel conflict is the only thing keeping the wedge open. That wedge closes when Pivot decides to vertical-specialize, and Pivot has $40M of fresh capital to do exactly that.

Counterfactual on the CEO-clarity axis: qomplement has Kerim Taray titled CEO and Andres Garza titled CTO, with university-era collaboration dating back to a Tec de Monterrey AI research lab and a shared RPA consulting practice. The titles delineate. What I cannot see in the dossier is the tiebreaker -- who breaks ties on product scope when grocery wants one thing and trucking wants another, and whether there is a documented operating agreement. For the score to flip a tier here, I would need to see Kerim hold a majority operating share or a public signal of unambiguous final authority; absent that, the title delineation gets credit but not full credit, and the GameCrush ghost stays in the room.

Mechanism analysis on the unsexy-regulated-capital-intensive axis: qomplement is software-only riding on enterprise SaaS contracts, with no hardware, no regulatory wedge, and no energy or fertility-tier moral defensibility. The category is genuinely unsexy -- carrier invoices and item-master updates are not Twitter-glamorous -- but unsexy alone is not what funded Crusoe or Anduril; the moat there compounded against time because hardware and defense procurement get harder, not easier, for a follower. qomplement's claimed moat is "customer-specific document templates and vendor mappings accumulate per account," which is the same moat every horizontal extraction player claims, and over 18 months that moat erodes the moment an SAP-native agent ships inside the customer's existing license envelope. Unsexy without compounding physics is not the trade.

Single-anchor parallel on physical-world verifiability: HQ Trivia was my override case because I could literally open the app at 9 PM Eastern and feel the viral pull in my own thumb before any metric existed. qomplement is the inverse artifact -- a B2B agent processing 10,000 documents/day across 20+ unnamed grocery and trucking customers, with a Cal.com link to Kerim's calendar as the closest thing to a touchable surface. I cannot install qomplement, I cannot photograph it in the wild, I cannot hand it to a friend at dinner the way I handed Postmates a delivery order in 2011. The Product Hunt page from May 2025 advertises a different product entirely ("an AI agent that controls your personal computer"), which suggests qomplement pivoted in the last twelve months and the prior-product traction does not transfer. There is no Ingress-like artifact for me to play my way into conviction on.

Forecast: 36 months out, qomplement reaches $3-7M ARR concentrated in LATAM mid-market grocery and trucking, then gets absorbed by Pivot or by NetSuite/Oracle as a vertical-extension tuck-in at low-nine-figures, with Kerim and Andres rolling into the acquirer's procurement-AI division. The mechanism is direct: the founder-market touch is real enough to get them to product-market fit in their home geography, but the category is consensus-converging and the well-funded horizontal player will either lap them or buy them before they compound their document corpus into a defensible vertical model.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the Niantic-Hanke override: John Hanke had spent a decade on Keyhole and Google Earth before Niantic, and the prior-product trajectory in an adjacent domain was the thing that overrode all other concerns. Kerim and Andres ran RPA consulting in Mexican enterprises for years, which is the closest thing to "prior product to scale in an adjacent domain" this dossier shows, and the LATAM-press density (Expansión, Milenio, Startups Latam) is exactly the sourcing-from-your-own-environment signal I would normally credit -- if I were sitting in Monterrey rather than San Francisco. The bear case wins anyway because RPA-implementation is not "shipped a comparable product to scale" in the Luckey/Hanke sense -- it is consulting revenue, not product trajectory -- and the Pivot $40M Series B is the consensus-converging tell that this category is being priced as if the outcome is already certain. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named Fortune-500 grocery or trucking customer disclosed publicly with a multi-year contract and ACV in the high-six-figures, demonstrating the vertical lock-in is real rather than self-reported. Until then, I'm out, with one foot still in love with the founders.
