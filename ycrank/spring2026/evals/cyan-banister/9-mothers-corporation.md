# 9 Mothers -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

9 Mothers is an Anduril-shaped product strapped to a Rainforest QA-shaped founder bench, and that mismatch is the whole story. Russell Smith, Roman Khomenko, and Bogdan Pyzh spent eight years together shipping a SaaS QA platform, and now they are building EDDA -- a 23 kg autonomous kinetic turret with 1.5 arcminute tracking accuracy meant to put rounds into Group 1 FPV drones. The frontier-hardware swing scores; the biography behind the trigger does not.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril is the load-bearing analogue for 9 Mothers on lived founder experience, and it is exactly the comparison that hurts. Luckey shipped Oculus to scale out of his parents' garage before the defense pivot -- the founder was *of* the hardware before he was of the contract. 9 Mothers' dossier gives me Smith as ex-CTO of Rainforest QA (YC S12, $41.1M raised, still independent), Khomenko as Lead AI Data Scientist on computer vision pipelines, and Pyzh as a data scientist out of Baylor. The closest thread to weapons or war is Khomenko's "service in Police of Ukraine" line in his profile -- one bullet point in a getprog.ai scrape, not a biography that put a man on a well pad with the operators. The computer-vision DNA from Rainforest QA legitimately maps to EDDA's passive optical targeting; the kinetics, the ITAR program execution, the DoD procurement choreography -- none of that is in the founders' documented past, and the dossier lists no defense advisors to compensate.

Counter-UAS is *not* magically weird in May 2026 -- that is the second wound. Crusoe in ~2018 was magically weird because credible VCs were passing on aesthetic grounds, and the room was empty. 9 Mothers' room is loud: Y Combinator's Summer 2026 RFS explicitly calls out counter-swarm defense, Q1 2026 global C-UAS contract announcements crossed $29B, Anduril just won the Army's counter-drone fire-control selection (Breaking Defense, Oct 2025), Epirus is at a $1.35B valuation, and -- most pointedly -- Allen Control Systems closed a $30M Series A led by Craft Ventures in March 2025 building the Bullfrog autonomous turret for the exact same Group 1 threat envelope, out of the exact same city of Austin, with a December 2025 US Army combat-vehicle integration already announced. 9 Mothers is competing in the consensus YC weird-bucket. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus; this is after.

Governance is where 9 Mothers earns points outright. Russell Smith is the named CEO, Khomenko is CTO, Pyzh is COO, and the three of them survived eight years of co-working at Rainforest QA without the partnership exploding -- which is the cheapest, most reliable tiebreaker test I have. This is the inverse of the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll failure that I cite when I warn about unresolved co-founder authority. The dossier doesn't quote an operating agreement, but a triad with delineated titles and a decade of shared scar tissue is a structurally healthier governance signal than I usually get at pre-seed.

EDDA itself is the kind of capital-intensive, ITAR-registered, regulated, aesthetically transgressive object that other investors on this council will not touch, and that is the slot in my book that produced SpaceX in 2008 and Anduril in 2017. 9 Mothers has CAGE code 11NF2D, DUNS 14-483-9928, ITAR registration confirmed, and a 23 kg turret claimed to be shipping to "US Government and prime contractors." That regulatory plumbing is real and expensive to forge. The moat compounds in domains other investors refuse to enter -- this is that domain. The reason this dimension doesn't fully save the file is that Allen Control Systems already proved the category is enterable with $30M and a clear hardware lineage 9 Mothers doesn't have.

Physical verifiability is the slot where 9 Mothers fails the Flock test in a particular way. Flock Safety I sourced off a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons; Niantic I sourced off playing Ingress for three years; Uber I sourced off riding it. EDDA, by design, is a thing I will never touch -- it lives behind ITAR walls on a well pad I will never stand on, and the dossier offers no third-party live-fire video, no public test range footage, no Army Applications Lab announcement of the kind Allen Control Systems already has. The product exists, and the shipping claim is plausible given the regulatory paperwork. But I cannot observe it in the wild and the dossier does not describe anyone outside the company who has.

My 36-month forecast for 9 Mothers: by mid-2029 the company either stalls under $10M in disclosed DoD obligations and gets passed in the Group 1 kinetic point-defense niche by Allen Control Systems -- whose Army combat-vehicle integration is a year of programmatic momentum 9 Mothers cannot easily catch -- and ends up as a tuck-in vision-AI acquisition for a defense prime in the $40-120M range, or pivots to selling the EDDA targeting stack as a software subsystem to integrators rather than a complete kinetic platform. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: when the hardware swing is real but the founder biography is not *of* the domain, the company gets out-executed by founders who are, and the salvageable asset is the computer-vision IP the team genuinely owns from their Rainforest QA decade.

The strongest case against that forecast is that Roman Khomenko trained as a computer vision researcher in Kharkiv, the city currently being hammered by FPV drones daily, and that "service in Police of Ukraine" line is not a footnote -- it is a man building a counter-FPV weapon because he watched his country get hit by them. If that biography is what the dossier is understating, then 9 Mothers is exactly the autobiographical founder origin I write checks for, and the Allen Control Systems comparison falls because Allen is solving a market and Khomenko is solving a memory. What dismantles the bull case is that the dossier gives me no quote from Khomenko placing him in that fight, no advisors from Ukrainian armed forces, no public statement of mission anchoring -- the inference is doing all the work, and I do not write checks on my own inferences when Allen Control Systems is sitting at $30M Series A with an Army selection already on the board. The condition that flips my verdict: Khomenko or Smith publicly disclose a named DoD program contract above $10M, OR 9 Mothers adds a defense-hardware co-founder or operator with prior shipped weapon-system experience -- either fills the founder-origin gap that currently keeps 9 Mothers at neutral.
