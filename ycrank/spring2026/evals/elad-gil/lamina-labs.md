# Lamina Labs -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Lamina Labs is a thin vertical wrapper riding a real catalyst into a market where a better-funded direct competitor already raised $4.1M from Emergence and Afore eight months before this YC batch -- the high/low signature here is "credible category re-opening, zero disclosed revenue curve," which is exactly the profile I learned to walk away from after the **Perplexity** (2022) cold-inbound taught me what real shipping rhythm looks like. Kartikesh Mishra and Sudip Rokaya have an MIT CSAIL pedigree and a live Simi product that claims 8-11 second generation, but the dossier offers one third-party X testimonial (aaronsiim) and zero MRR, zero user counts, zero waitlist size. That is not a Harvey curve. That is a demo.

The catalyst question is where Lamina Labs gets closest to a template I recognize. **Decagon** (2023) was the workflow-infrastructure-in-an-open-market pattern after the post-ChatGPT November 2022 re-opening of customer support -- a specific transformer step-function plus a clear buyer who had been waiting for it. Lamina Labs is trying to ride the analogous re-opening in generative video, and the Golpo AI $4.1M seed in October 2025 from BNVT/Emergence/Afore is *exactly* the kind of "capital concentrating on a recently-catalyzed category" signal I respect. But where Decagon walked into an open AI sub-market with no clear leader, Lamina Labs is walking into a sub-market where Golpo 2.0 has already been positioned for "education, training, and technical content" by a Series-A-quality syndicate -- the catalyst is real, but Lamina Labs is not the index entrant, it's the second mover with no disclosed wedge beyond a latency claim.

**Lyft** Series C failed my test because the dossier couldn't show a credible path to share inversion against a better-capitalized #1 in a market where the structure rewards concentration -- I called it on "winner-take-all versus oligopoly" (20VC) and ate the regret. The Lamina Labs revenue-velocity dimension is the same failure mode in miniature: Golpo has $4.1M, named investors, and a 2.0 product targeting the same buyer set, while Lamina Labs publishes no pricing on app.laminalabs.ai/simi, books founder calls via lamina-labs.cal.com, and the only quantitative growth fact in the entire dossier is "11 seconds" of render time. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) -- and Lamina Labs is silent on the only metric that would save it.

Founder shipping rhythm at Lamina Labs is the dimension where I'd extend the most charity and still come up short. Mishra's GitHub @mk314k has 22 followers and a 14-star 3DAttnVAE-GAN repo from CSAIL research with Daniela Rus; Rokaya has no public GitHub. They built Simi -- that's a shipped artifact, which is more than half the YC Spring 2026 batch can say -- but the **Perplexity** (2022) test is not "did you ship once," it's "do you ship a new thing between every conversation." Aravind Srinivas pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with what we'd discussed *built*. The Lamina Labs dossier has founder LinkedIn activity posts and one "Leaving MIT" announcement (activity 7438096805084495872); it does not have a cadence of weekly product drops, and that absence in a two-person team executing both an "infrastructure" tagline and a vertical "Simi" application is exactly the scope-split warning I'd want resolved before any check.

Index position is the dimension where the YC tagline lies. Lamina Labs is described as "visual infrastructure for EdTech AI applications" -- that's the **Stripe** (2010) language, the "index on a compounding category" language. If Lamina Labs were the API layer that every Khanmigo-style EdTech AI app called for visual generation, that would be the workflow path I write checks for. But the live product is Simi, a self-serve vertical app for course creators, with no disclosed API customers, no integrations, no third-party developers building on top. Eighteen months from now this compounds against Lamina Labs: every additional month spent as an end-user app rather than infrastructure is a month Golpo or Samvity captures the educator buyer, and the "infrastructure" repositioning becomes a pivot story rather than a thesis. Compare to **Color Genomics** (2015), which I architected explicitly as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing from day one -- Lamina Labs has the tagline without the architecture.

Single-miracle architecture is the one place Lamina Labs scores cleanly. The miracle isolates to "deterministic generative animation at sub-15-second latency from a text prompt" -- that's one capability bet, and if it holds, everything else is GTM and product execution. This is the dimension that prevents Lamina Labs from sliding into Strong Pass territory; it's not a science project, it's not a multi-miracle dependency chain, it's a single technical claim plus distribution work. The problem is the single miracle as stated -- speed -- is the *thinnest* possible miracle to anchor a moat on, because rivals reaching comparable latency is a quarter of engineering work, not a year of physics.

My 36-month forecast for Lamina Labs: acqui-hired sub-$10M by either Golpo AI or a mid-tier LMS (Coursera, Quizlet, Chegg) on the way to a written-down outcome, with Mishra absorbed for the CSAIL/ML talent and Simi sunset within 12 months of acquisition. The mechanism is straightforward -- Golpo's $4.1M Emergence-led seed gives it 18-24 months of runway to compound the same buyer relationships Lamina Labs is trying to win self-serve, and the speed claim does not survive the first competitor reaching 12-second generation. ARR at acquisition: under $500K.

The bull case worth steel-manning: Mishra's CSAIL deterministic-rendering background may actually be load-bearing IP -- Manim-style mathematical precision rendered in 11 seconds is genuinely hard, and if the "visual infrastructure" tagline becomes literal (an API consumed by Khanmigo, Magic School, and the next ten EdTech AI apps), Lamina Labs becomes the **Decagon**-shaped workflow layer for educational visual generation rather than a Synthesia-clone. Pete Koomen as YC partner -- a product-rigor signal -- supports that read. The dismantle: the dossier shows zero API customers, zero integration partnerships, and a free-trial self-serve funnel pointed at individual creators, which is the precise opposite of infrastructure GTM. Golpo's Emergence syndicate will out-execute the educator self-serve race on capital alone. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed API integration with a top-10 EdTech AI application (Magic School, Khanmigo, Quizlet AI) plus $50K MRR within six months -- absent that, Lamina Labs is a #2 in a category where the #1 already has the capital and the consensus thesis is already paying *Golpo*, not Lamina Labs.
