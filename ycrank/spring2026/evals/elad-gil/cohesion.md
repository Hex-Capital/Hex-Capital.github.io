# Cohesion -- Elad Gil Evaluation

AlphaSense crossed $500M ARR in 2024, acquired Tegus for $930M, and is reportedly seeking additional hundreds of millions in fresh capital. Hebbia raised $130M at a $700M valuation for financial document analysis. YC published an explicit Request for Startups category for "AI-Native Hedge Funds" in Spring 2026. Over 35% of new hedge fund launches in 2025 branded themselves as AI-driven or AI-enhanced. When I backed Coinbase in 2012, crypto infrastructure was dismissed by the mainstream -- that's a nonobvious window. When I wrote Perplexity's seed check before ChatGPT launched, AI search was considered a dead concept. Cohesion is entering a market where the structural shift -- LLMs enabling reliable financial data extraction -- is fully consensus. Every institutional investor, every incumbent platform, and YC itself has identified this exact opportunity. That's a crowded market with obvious timing, and it's the hardest starting position for my framework.

Devon Krapcho spent 5+ years as a fundamental equity analyst at Long Path Partners covering software companies. He is literally the target user persona, and that matters -- Cohesion is live with 10+ long/short and long-only funds managing a combined $10B+ AUM, which is meaningfully strong early traction for a pre-seed company. The bull case writes itself: Krapcho knows which signals matter because he spent half a decade sifting through earnings transcripts, podcasts, and X posts manually. Matt Munns built AI for investors at T. Rowe Price, a $1.5T+ AUM asset manager. Matthew McBrien worked on Lambda at AWS. The functional coverage across domain expertise, ML engineering, and infrastructure is well-matched to the problem. If I were evaluating founder-market fit in isolation, Cohesion would score well. But I've seen great founders repeatedly get crushed by terrible market structure, and market structure here is the binding constraint.

Cohesion's core architecture is an application built on commodity LLM APIs, monitoring publicly available data sources. The dossier notes open-source projects like virattt/ai-hedge-fund on GitHub that demonstrate basic investment research agents assembled from commodity LLM APIs. Cohesion is not building infrastructure that other businesses depend on -- no API, no platform, no layer that compounds switching costs. Compare this to how Stripe became the payments API that thousands of companies integrated into their core systems, or how Harvey is embedding into legal workflows as a platform. Cohesion is a workflow tool for a narrow set of fundamental equity analysts. If AlphaSense adds autonomous agent capabilities -- and at $500M+ ARR with aggressive M&A appetite, they will -- Cohesion's standalone monitoring tool competes against an incumbent with deep enterprise relationships, comprehensive data licensing, and existing seat-level distribution across every major fund. My red flag on "building an application on a contested infrastructure layer" applies directly: the underlying LLM layer hasn't stabilized, and Cohesion has no proprietary position in the stack.

Cohesion's single-miracle test lands in moderate territory. The technology miracle -- LLMs parsing unstructured financial data reliably -- is happening exogenously on foundation model providers' improvement curves. Hedge funds are proven buyers of research tools with clear budget authority, so the "no clear payer" concern I apply to categories like EdTech does not apply here. Cohesion needs primarily a product-market fit miracle: can they build agents that surface genuinely differentiated insights versus what an analyst achieves with AlphaSense, Bloomberg, and their own manual monitoring? That's one clear miracle. But there's a secondary distribution challenge -- hedge funds are notoriously difficult to sell into, with long procurement cycles, security requirements, and a preference for established vendors. And there's a lurking defensibility miracle: staying ahead of incumbents when the underlying technology is commodity. I count roughly 1.5 miracles, which is acceptable but not the clean single-miracle profile I look for in my strongest bets.

Cohesion's margin architecture is opaque at this stage -- no pricing page, no revenue data, no retention metrics. The "limited early trial access during earnings season" framing suggests Cohesion has not yet converted trial users to paying customers, or at least has not disclosed doing so. The narrow initial SAM -- fundamental equity funds willing to pay for autonomous monitoring agents -- is a subset of a subset. The dossier's TAM figures ($2.9B AI in investment management growing to $7.4B by 2030) include categories far broader than Cohesion's initial wedge. I'd want to see evidence that Cohesion's pricing captures value delivered (per-insight or per-alpha-signal) rather than per-seat, which would compress as AI amplifies per-analyst output. Without that evidence, I can't assess whether the margin architecture supports the kind of expansion revenue and negative churn that differentiates a great SaaS business from a commodity tool.

The verifiable condition that would flip me to Invest: if Cohesion can demonstrate a genuine data flywheel -- where their agent architecture produces measurably better signal-to-noise ratios for funds that have used the platform for 6+ months versus new users or versus comparable tools -- that would indicate a compounding advantage that moves Cohesion from pure application toward proto-infrastructure. That's the difference between a monitoring tool that any competent ML team can replicate and a system with embedded intelligence that deepens with usage. Absent that evidence, Cohesion is a competent team with strong domain fit building in a consensus market without structural defensibility. The two dimensions that lock this verdict are Nonobvious Market Timing -- where the market is explicitly consensus, not nonobvious -- and Infrastructure-Layer Positioning, where Cohesion is an application on commodity infrastructure, not a platform that compounds switching costs.
