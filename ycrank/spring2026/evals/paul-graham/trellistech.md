# Trellis -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Lodovico Benvenuti scaled a property-management company to ~$2M revenue across 150+ listings before Trellis existed, which makes Trellis the rare case where the highest-scoring dimension is organic founder-pain and the lowest is hacker genealogy — an operator-built vertical tool, not a hacker-built one. That pairing tells me what kind of company Trellis is before I read another line: a founder who ran the schlep at small scale is now selling the schlep-reducer to people who run it at the same scale, which is a recognizable shape but not the shape that produced the **Stripe** (2010) outcome. The two-week feature-parity quote is the tell.

**Dropbox** (2007) is the HIGH rung on organic founder-pain — Drew Houston kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT, and the product was a tool he wanted before it was a company. Trellis loads the same mechanism: Benvenuti ran 150+ listings across "5 to 10 disconnected tools" and Jan Sahagun built STR Tech Report after consulting across the sector and attending 20+ industry events. Where Trellis diverges from the **Dropbox** (2007) profile is that Houston's pain was universal-feeling — every grad student lost a USB stick — whereas Trellis's pain is felt by a population of small-to-mid STR operators who already pay Guesty ($409M raised, $900M valuation) for an adjacent answer. The pain is real, the founder lived it, but the competitive set already addresses it imperfectly rather than not at all. That's the Houston-pattern attenuated by the **DoorDash** (2013) reality that incumbents exist.

The MBA AI-wrapper team from my pass example is the LOW rung on hacker genealogy — no shipped artifact, no GitHub, no demo, just a deck — and the failure mechanism is that the company has no technical lineage to outlast the next AI wrapper. Trellis is not that team, but the dossier reports "No public repos found" for both Benvenuti and Sahagun, "No Twitter/X account found" for either, and the only technical credential is that Benvenuti "built AI customer service agents" at HostAI/Conduit, the W24 YC company that is now Trellis's most direct AI-native competitor. The CEO's own line — "feature parity with competitors in two weeks" — is exactly what a non-hacker founder says with pride and what a hacker founder would never volunteer, because a hacker knows the line will be quoted back at them when the next two-week clone arrives. The **Coinbase** (2012) compliance grind worked as a moat because the schlep took years; "two weeks" is not a moat duration.

Animal resourcefulness pre-funding compounds differently for operator-founders than hacker-founders, and over 18 months Trellis's $2M-revenue prior business and Sahagun's STR Tech Report distribution will compound into pipeline before they compound into product — Trellis will close customers faster than it deepens defensibility. That is the inverse of the **Stripe** (2010) compounding shape, where the product deepened faster than the sales motion, and it predicts a revenue ramp that looks healthy to a Series A board and brittle to a strategic acquirer's diligence team.

On schlep, Trellis claims 200+ live integrations against Airbnb, VRBO, Guesty, and Hostaway, which is genuine schlep work, and the integration-maintenance burden against competing PMSes is the kind of grind I respect — but it would have to be true that Guesty and Hostaway refuse to ship comparable AI agents over the next 18 months for that schlep to flip a tier into real **Coinbase** (2012)-shaped moat territory. Both raised over $400M each in 2024. They will ship.

The co-founder configuration parallel I keep coming back to is **Airbnb** (2009)'s Chesky/Gebbia operator-designer pairing — Benvenuti is the operator, Sahagun is the distribution voice — except the dossier reports no prior shared employer or university between them, and the **Airbnb** (2009) trio had RISD and years of shared apartments behind them. Trellis's pairing is complementary on paper and unverified on history.

My 36-month forecast on Trellis: $3-8M ARR, acquired by Guesty or Hostaway as an AI-agent tuck-in at a $30-80M outcome — not dead, not a category-definer, a competent operator exit that returns a fund's check but does not return a fund. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: organic pain plus weak hacker genealogy plus incumbent capital asymmetry yields a feature-shaped company at a category-priced ceiling, and Trellis's own CEO already told me the feature ships in two weeks.

The strongest case against that forecast is the Known Exception from **Airbnb** (2009): I rejected the idea silently and the founders overrode my rejection through a stunt I had not imagined. Could Benvenuti's $2M-revenue 150-listing operator past be the cereal-box equivalent for Trellis — pre-funding revenue from an unscalable grind that proves the animal test? It is real revenue and it is real grind. But cereal boxes were a stunt no one else would have done; running a normal property-management company to $2M is a thing thousands of operators do, and the **Airbnb** (2009) override required an idea so strange the stunt was the only way to fund it. Trellis's idea is not strange. It is a better Guesty. The condition that would flip my verdict: if Trellis discloses paying customer count above 200 within twelve months of YC Demo Day, or signs a named enterprise STR operator (Vacasa, Evolve, AvantStay) as a flagship before the Series A, I would re-rate this toward Invest. Until then, Neutral.
