# Clara -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

George Favvas ran Circle Medical for ten years to $100M ARR before starting Clara, which makes Clara a company whose highest dimension is founder-of-the-place lived experience and whose lowest is "magically weird" non-consensus — a competent re-run by operators who own the exact muscle memory, dropped into the most-pitched lane of the Spring 2026 YC batch. That pairing tells me what kind of bet Clara is before I open the cap table: a respectable single by people who have actually shipped multi-state telehealth primary care, not the kind of laughed-at deal that anchors a fund.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus before pivoting to Anduril in 2017, and the load-bearing mechanism of that bet was a founder who had taken a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain *before* the current company — not someone who discovered the problem in a memo. Clara's Favvas plus Caitlin Swift (ex-VP Clinical Ops, FNP, scaled Circle Medical to ~1M telehealth appointments/year with 425 providers) plus Zeeshan Ahmed is the closest analog to that template I have seen in this batch. Where Clara diverges from the Anduril rung is the domain itself — Luckey was in defense where every other VC's moral and capital-intensity objections kept the round non-consensus, whereas Favvas is in AI primary care where Hippocratic AI sits at a $3.5B valuation, K Health has raised $439M, and Hims just launched Labs AI in 2026. The founder bar is genuinely Anduril-class; the lane is not.

Crusoe in ~2018 was "burning gas flares to mine Bitcoin in North Dakota," and the reason the magically-weird filter triggered was that two or three credible funds had already passed on aesthetic grounds before I wrote the check. Clara's round inverts that pattern — $12M pre-seed led by Y Combinator and A.Capital with Liquid 2 (Joe Montana, Matthew Mulvey), SV Angel, Immad Akhund, Vinny Lingham all stacked in. That is not a deal sophisticated investors are visibly passing on; that is a deal sophisticated investors are visibly stacking into, with Garry Tan as YC partner on a Launch post timed to the consensus. "Find them before it becomes consensus" — Clara is already consensus inside its batch.

Favvas is named CEO and the two other co-founders sit in clinical and design lanes, which means Clara's tiebreaker authority is structurally cleaner than HQ Trivia in 2017 was (where Yusupov and Kroll's unresolved partnership eventually fed the 2020-21 implosion); the trio has also already worked together for years at Circle Medical, so the governance has been pressure-tested in the lower-stakes prior company. I would still ask Favvas the specific question I ask every co-founded company before signing — name the tiebreaker in writing — but nothing in the dossier signals 50/50 ambiguity.

LegitScript certification, primary-care licensure in all 50 states, and 150,000+ EHR/lab/pharmacy integrations is exactly the kind of unsexy regulated moat I fund — sitting alongside Mindbloom in ketamine therapy and Vigil Labs (August 2025) in regulated finance. Clara is not Anduril-class capital-intensive frontier hardware; it is the Mindbloom/Superpower (April 2024) middle rung where regulatory and integration work compounds with time but the moat is operational rather than physical. Over 18 months, the question is whether Clara's clinician-sign-off architecture compounds into a data and trust advantage faster than Hims and Hippocratic AI can copy the regulatory footprint with their dramatically larger capital bases.

Compared to Postmates in 2011, where I made the bet because the delivery literally happened in front of me at a deli counter, Clara passes the tactile test cleanly — askclara.com is a consumer surface a normal human can sign up to and feel within 48 hours, not a B2B API abstraction whose value lives only in founder testimony. That is unambiguous, but the absence of a public price point on askclara.com (the site routes to a contact/pricing page) tells me Clara has not yet decided what it is going to charge, which makes the artifact observable but the unit economics not yet observable.

Forecast at 36 months: Clara reaches $40-90M ARR as a credible Circle-Medical-with-AI, gets acquired by Hims & Hers or a payer (CVS Health / UnitedHealth Optum) in the $400-800M range, or raises a Series B at a $400M-$600M valuation from a tier-1 fund — a respectable Founders-Fund-era single given the Favvas operator pedigree, not the magically-weird multi-billion outcome the Crusoe/Anduril sleeve has trained me to fund for. The mechanism is straightforward: operator advantage closes the first $20M of ARR fast, and then Hippocratic AI's $404M war chest and Hims's $2.7-$2.9B revenue base catch up on the product surface, capping the asymmetric upside.

The strongest case against my own lean: Favvas is the rarest profile in this batch — a founder who has already run the exact category to $100M ARR, which is the literal definition of my deepest unstated pattern (founder shipped a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain *before* the current company). DeepMind in ~2013 was a Known Exception precisely because Hassabis's polymath cross-domain compounding overrode my outsider-trajectory bias, and Favvas's Circle-Medical-to-Clara compounding deserves the same exception treatment. The dismantle: the DeepMind exception was about *cross-domain* polymath compounding into a genuinely uninvested frontier (AGI in 2013), whereas Favvas is doing *same-domain* extension into the most-bid lane of Spring 2026 — that is operator excellence, not exception-class. I would flip to Invest if two tier-1 funds visibly pass on Clara's seed extension within six months and Favvas can articulate a wedge that Hims and Hippocratic AI structurally cannot copy without cannibalizing their existing P&L.
