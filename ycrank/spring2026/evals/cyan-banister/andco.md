# Andco -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Andco is the rare profile where the governance reads clean and the team executes, but the underlying bet sits right in the middle of the loudest YC consensus lane of Spring 2026 -- AI agents for plaintiff-side legal work, a category where EvenUp is already at a $2B valuation with 2,000+ firms and 10,000 cases a week. The signature here is well-formed-but-mid-table on weirdness: Andco is a competent, repeat-founder team running a recognizable agentic-SaaS playbook into a vertical the smart money has already priced. That is the opposite of what I am usually looking for.

Anduril-Luckey is my reference rung for the founder-lived-experience axis: Palmer shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he pointed the same instincts at defense, and the biography did the underwriting. Andco's analog would be a plaintiff PI paralegal who spent five years chasing fax-back acknowledgments from Geico adjusters and finally built the tool. Instead, Andco gives me Michael Lemm out of Kirkland & Ellis -- a fine legal credential, but K&E is corporate litigation, not plaintiff PI; the lived contact with hospital records departments and insurance carrier mailrooms is a proxy, not a primary source. Ryan Xue's Layup (YC W23) exit to Whatfix is real founder reps, but Layup was developer-tooling, not legal -- so the Andco team's relationship to the PI workup problem is "we picked a hot vertical and learned it," not "we lived it." That is the analytical pattern I am structurally biased against.

The consensus-YC-AI-agent darling I walk past in my Pass Example is the cleanest mirror for Andco on the weirdness axis. That pass turns on three signals -- ex-FAANG/credential team, software abstraction I cannot touch, and a round that is already crowded -- and Andco trips two of them cleanly. Andco closed a $12M oversubscribed pre-seed led by YC and A.Capital with Joe Montana / Liquid 2, SV Angel, Immad Akhund, Kevin Hartz, and Jude Gomila all stacked on the cap table; that is the exact "ten funds nodding" social proof that Long Journey treats as a deselection signal. The donut-box GTM -- founders showing up to PI firms with DoorDash bags after "200 boxes of donuts" -- is genuinely charming and the kind of physical-world sourcing I respect, but charming sales theater is not weirdness in the thesis sense. Andco is in the most crowded YC vertical of the batch.

On governance, the Andco cap table and YC page name Ryan Xue as CEO with David Lee as CTO and Michael Lemm as COO -- a three-founder structure but with one designated tiebreaker on paper, and a pre-existing Xue/Lemm partnership from Layup that has already survived one full company cycle. That is the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll problem: when co-founders have already executed an exit together, the unresolved-authority blow-up risk compounds *down* over the next eighteen months, not up. I will give Andco real credit here even though the three-cook count makes me twitch.

Counterfactual on capital-intensity: for Andco to flip a tier on the unsexy-regulated axis, the UPL and HIPAA exposure flagged in the dossier would have to be the *product*, not the risk -- a compliance-native architecture that makes Andco the only agent a state bar will bless, the way Vigil Labs (August 2025) makes regulated finance the moat rather than the obstacle. Right now Andco is software-on-top-of-fax with a Filevine sync; the regulatory texture is friction the team has to manage, not a moat that compounds. If the next twelve months produce a state-bar opinion letter or a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement framework that Andco owns and competitors can't replicate, I revisit. Absent that, Andco is a SaaS wrapper in a vertical where seat-priced incumbents will fight the agent-as-staff pricing on their own P&L grounds.

Single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability: Postmates (2011) earned its check because the delivery happened in front of me -- I could literally watch a stranger hand a bag of food to a stranger and reason about the unit economics in my body. Andco's product runs over fax, mail, and voice to insurance carriers; I cannot install it, ride in it, or scan it at a hotel café. What I *can* observe is the donut-box ritual at PI law firm front desks, and the YC LinkedIn claim of "hundreds of cases and thousands of requests monthly" across high-volume firms -- which is meaningful pre-seed traction but still trusted-by-testimony, not tactile in my hands. That is a developer-tools abstraction profile, modestly rescued by the sales motion being legible in physical space.

My 36-month forecast on Andco: somewhere between $4M and $12M ARR, then absorbed by EvenUp, Eve, or Filevine for $60M-$180M as the "upstream retrieval module" the incumbent never wanted to build in-house -- a fine outcome for the Layup-repeat founders and Joe Montana's check, a meaningfully sub-scale outcome for a Long Journey fund-returner thesis. The mechanism is straightforward: EvenUp grew 5x ARR to $2B with 90% of new sales coming from AI products, which means the buyer has both the budget and the strategic rationale to acquire upstream coverage rather than rebuild it, and Andco's multi-channel agent infrastructure is exactly the bolt-on shape that gets bought, not the platform shape that buys.

The bull case on Andco that genuinely stings: Xue and Lemm have already shipped one YC company to exit together, the $12M round was led by a partner (David Lieb) who is publicly endorsing on X, the early customer base is real with thousands of requests a month two months post-launch, and the differentiation the dossier names -- upstream retrieval across fax/mail/voice versus EvenUp and Supio's downstream demand-package focus -- is a defensible product wedge that the incumbents have not chosen to build because their seat-based pricing P&Ls can't absorb per-case agent labor economics. That is a real argument, and in the DeepMind known-exception spirit, repeat-founder competence can occasionally override the lived-experience bias. What dismantles it for me is the consensus density: Andco is operating in the most fundraised legal-AI sub-vertical of 2025-2026 with three competitors above $1B valuation, the founders' PI domain ownership is credential-by-proximity rather than autobiographical, and the round itself reads as the kind of warm YC-darling stack that telegraphs "priced as if the outcome is already certain." The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named plaintiff PI managing partner (not a YC partner, not an angel) lead-investing the next round with a check that signals the buyer-side has chosen Andco as the category winner. Until then, I pass with warmth.
