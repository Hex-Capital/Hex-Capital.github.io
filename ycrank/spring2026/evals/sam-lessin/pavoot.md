# Pavoot -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Let me think about Pavoot the way I think about every YC P26 AI-agent-for-vertical pitch that lands in my inbox: financing structure first, founder second, market third. Pavoot is the Pass Example with the serial numbers filed off — "AI that turns events into pipeline," two ETH-trained technical co-founders (Gohar Tamrazyan and Ana Yoon Faria de Lima), no disclosed price, no disclosed customers, one homepage anecdote of $48K of "pipeline" that is not attributed to a named buyer. The signature here is a relatively-okay capital-structure profile (the burn is presumably small, two people, demo-gated) paired with a category placement that I've publicly said is anti-thesis — and that pairing tells you what kind of company Pavoot is: a competent technical duo running into the most-consensus 2026 YC sleeve with the least-defensible wedge inside it.

On round dynamics, the comparison that matters is **Solana** at $0.05/coin in 2018-2019 — pre-consensus crypto after most generalist funds had quit the category, where the round was cheap because nobody else was bidding. The shared mechanism is supposed to be: an entry price the consensus crowd has already abandoned. Pavoot does the opposite. The dossier doesn't disclose the post-money, but Pavoot is sitting inside the YC P26 batch, with David Lieb as partner, in the category every other VC at every other AI demo day is bidding on right now — "AI agent for B2B marketing/sales." There is no evidence any tier-1 visibly passed on Pavoot, no evidence of a discount, no evidence of structural ambiguity. Where Solana had a category-wide bid-ask collapse, Pavoot is in the part of the auction where the bid stack is deepest and my edge is zero.

The lowest-rung failure mode on category sleeve is the one I literally wrote down in my Pass Example: "We're building an AI agent for [enterprise vertical]." That's the failure mechanism — commodity LLM + ASR primitives wrapped around a vertical wedge that the incumbent stack will absorb the moment it matters. Pavoot's dossier contains the exact pattern: the "differentiation" is foundation-model-API-stack functionality (attendee sourcing, transcription, follow-up generation), and Cvent acquired Goldcast for ~$300M in December 2025 specifically to bundle "AI-powered post-event activation" inside an installed CRM-integrated suite. Pavoot's wedge is the wedge Cvent just paid $300M to own. "AI is just more internet, it's not disruptive" (Joe Lonsdale Ep 113, 2024) is the through-line, and Pavoot is the operationalized counterexample to my own portfolio sleeve allocation.

Capital structure for Pavoot is the one place the score isn't catastrophic, by mechanism: two ETH grads, no disclosed revenue, no disclosed burn, a demo-gated funnel. Over 18 months the question is whether Pavoot needs five SaaS-style rounds to reach default-alive (Slow letter language) or can land 10-20 mid-market RevOps logos at $30-60K ACV and become bootstrappable. With foundation-model COGS eating gross margin and an enterprise sales motion that Pavoot has not staffed (no GTM hire visible, no design partner named), the capital tack-count looks closer to five than two — venture-shaped only in the unfortunate sense that it requires venture to survive, not in the sense that venture accelerates a real underlying compounding asset.

On asymmetric conviction, the counterfactual that would flip the score is straightforward: if Gohar Tamrazyan had been publishing opinionated long-form writing on B2B event ROI, or had spent three years inside a RevOps team watching marketing-attributed pipeline rot, Pavoot would score completely differently. What Pavoot actually has is LinkedIn posts about "from chess champion to co-founding a startup" — biographical, not categorical. "Writing is thinking. If you can't write, you can't think" (Antoine Buteau, ~2023) — there is no public Pavoot artifact arguing a non-obvious belief about why incumbent event platforms have failed at follow-up, and no evidence either founder lived inside the problem before the 3-day ETH AI Center course where they met. This is opportunity-response, not mission.

The single-anchor parallel on founder-as-brand is **John Fish** / Bookshelved (January 2024): a creator with a measurable owned audience, where the cap-table asset was literally the distribution. Pavoot has the inverse — no Twitter, no GitHub, LinkedIn follower counts not even retrievable in the dossier, hello@pavoot.com as the only public surface. The Slow Creator Fund thesis ($60M, Megan Lightcap, Axios February 12 2025) does not apply because there is no community pre-existing the company; Pavoot will buy every customer through paid outbound, which compounds the capital-structure problem above.

The 36-month forecast: Pavoot gets acqui-hired by Cvent, Bizzabo, or a horizontal AI-SDR vendor (Clay, 11x, Regie) for under $20M as a feature-team acquisition, or quietly winds down after a Series A reset fails to clear. The mechanism is Cvent's December 2025 Goldcast deal — the incumbent has now publicly priced "AI event-to-pipeline" as a $300M feature, which both validates the wedge and forecloses Pavoot's independent path, because Cvent's installed Salesforce footprint and Goldcast's existing $10M+ ARR book is the distribution moat Pavoot's two-person ETH team cannot replicate before commodity LLM gross margins compress the unit economics.

The strongest case against my pass is the Cvent-Goldcast comp itself: if incumbents are paying $300M for this wedge, and Pavoot ships a meaningfully sharper agent than Goldcast's roadmap (plausible — Goldcast is video-first, Pavoot is conversation-capture-first), then a $50-100M acqui-hire to Cvent or Bizzabo in 24 months is a 5-10x outcome on a pre-seed entry and you don't need to win the market, you just need to be the next bolt-on. That bull case stings — it's the **Ro** override pattern, where regulatory or distribution complexity makes the wedge buyable rather than buildable. But it dies on the Pavoot dossier: there's no regulated rail like Ro's 50-state prescription licensing creating an actual moat, the founders have zero buyer empathy from the demand side (no RevOps experience), and the homepage's unattributed $48K pipeline number is not the kind of evidence I'd underwrite an acqui-hire path on. **The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: three named Fortune 500 paying logos with disclosed ACV inside 12 months of the YC P26 demo day** — short of that, Pavoot is the Pass Example I already wrote down.
