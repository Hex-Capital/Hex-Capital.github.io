# Auxos -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Auxos is three Team USA fencers selling simulated-audience research to Fortune 500 marketing teams via the BCG network Ashton Daniel built before YC -- which means the high rung on my ladder is the Buffett-energy trio (elite-athlete discipline reads as the rarest thing in the dossier) and the low rung is permissionless leverage (the GTM is consultant-relationship outbound into enterprise marketing, the structural opposite of code-or-media compounding). That high/low pairing tells me what Auxos actually is: a disciplined services-adjacent enterprise SaaS, dressed in AI-research clothing, in a category where Aaru already absorbed the $1B Series A oxygen in December 2025.

The leverage architecture is where Auxos breaks against my thesis hardest. The HIGH rung here is **Twitter** (2007), where media-as-protocol meant any user could post and the marginal cost of replication was zero; the MID rung is **Notion** (2013), where the product was code-leverage but the early motion still required some hand-holding; the LOW rung is **Anchorage Digital**, where I waived the rule only because the OCC charter itself became a non-permission-based moat in regulated crypto. Auxos sits below the Anchorage exception. The dossier names zero open-source surface, zero Product Hunt launch, zero developer community, no viral self-serve sign-up, and Daniel's distribution edge is explicitly his BCG Fortune 500 rolodex -- that is labor leverage requiring permission from procurement gatekeepers, not code leverage. Anchorage at least had a structural regulatory moat; Auxos has a consultant's contact list, which any of the six named competitors (Evidenza, Aaru, Synthetic Users, Viewpoints.ai, Brox.ai, Synthelos) can also reach.

Founder trio is the only dimension where Auxos genuinely lifts. The HIGH rung is **Stack Overflow** (2008) -- Atwood and Spolsky as developers writing for developers with clean prior exits; MID is the broad band of credible, clean-integrity founders I have funded across 273 checks; LOW is **Clearview AI**, the Hoan Ton-That exception where I tolerated reputational contestation because the specific knowledge was extreme. Auxos sits between the high and mid rungs. NCAA fencing champion, world-#1 ranking, three D1 athletes across Columbia/Duke/UPenn -- the energy leg of Buffett's trio reads near-maximum, and nothing in the dossier triggers the "smart crook" filter or the self-promoted-honesty red flag (Knowledge Project #18). I would happily spend ten years with these three. The problem is that the trio test gates *whether* I invest, not *why* -- and the why has to come from the other four dimensions.

Specific knowledge splits the team. The HIGH rung is **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind Srinivas at Berkeley retrieval ML; MID is **Replit's** Amjad Masad shipping Repl.it since 2011; LOW is the "ex-McKinsey/ex-FAANG PM identified $X TAM" anti-pattern flagged in my Red Flags. Daniel reads as the LOW rung almost verbatim -- "BCG consultant noticed Fortune 500 clients spending a fortune on panels." Kerry Lu redeems the team: Amazon Ads simulation infrastructure modeling 40% of US e-commerce traffic plus Vorticity (YC S19) seismic-wave simulation is genuinely non-trainable simulation-engineering depth. Wu's Meta ranking infra is competent but generic. Net: one founder with extreme specific knowledge, two without, pinning Auxos between mid and low.

Market-with-pivot-room and network compounding are both middling. On pivot-room, the HIGH rung is **Twitter** pivoting from Odeo podcasting and **Uber** from black-car to UberX; LOW is vertical SaaS like "CRM for HVAC technicians." Auxos has the $24B-to-$215B AI-marketing TAM the dossier cites and adjacent pivot paths into ad-creative testing (Viewpoints.ai's lane) or UX validation (Brox.ai's lane) -- pivot-room exists, but the NN/g "hypothesis generation only" validity ceiling caps every adjacent market the same way. On network value, the HIGH rung is **Uber's** two-sided marketplace; MID is **Perplexity**, where I waived the criterion because three other gates cleared at top quartile; LOW is single-sided SaaS where each customer is economically independent. Auxos sits at LOW -- each customer's simulated audience is calibrated on their own first-party data, which is the opposite of compounding across users. The Perplexity exception does not apply because Auxos clears neither the permissionless-distribution gate nor the extreme-specific-knowledge gate emphatically.

My 36-month forecast: Auxos lands at $500K-$2M ARR via founder-led enterprise outbound, stuck under the NN/g validity ceiling that caps synthetic research at hypothesis-generation contracts, and gets acqui-hired by Qualtrics, Momentive, or a martech roll-up for the engineering team -- with Lu being the asset that actually drives the price. The mechanism is straightforward: the leverage architecture is labor-bound, the category already has a $1B-valuation incumbent and a profitable bootstrapped competitor with 100+ enterprise logos including Salesforce and GE, and three fencers cannot out-execute Aaru and Evidenza on enterprise distribution from a standing pre-seed start.

The bull case worth steel-manning: Lu's Amazon Ads simulation pedigree is the kind of specific knowledge I funded **Perplexity** on, the fencing-trio energy signal is a leading indicator I have historically under-weighted, and HBR validated the category in November 2025 -- meaning Auxos could be the developer-grade simulation infrastructure layer that Aaru (election polling) and Evidenza (C-suite personas) leave open. That case stings because Lu's background is genuinely rare. But it does not survive the leverage test: even if Auxos builds the best simulation engine in the category, distribution to enterprise marketing buyers is consultant-relationship-bound, which is exactly the labor/capital architecture I avoid since the May 2018 framing. The condition that would flip my verdict: Auxos open-sources the persona-calibration framework and shows organic GitHub stars or unprompted developer adoption within six months, converting the engineering depth into permissionless code leverage. Until then, pass -- this is the consultant-identified-market-opportunity pattern with great athletes attached.
