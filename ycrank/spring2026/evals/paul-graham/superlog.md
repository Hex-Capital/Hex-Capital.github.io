# Superlog -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Superlog is the rare dossier where the CTO's biography is the entire investment thesis and the CEO's biography is the entire objection: Arseniy Shishaev built data pipelines at Datadog before quitting to build the thing that eats Datadog, while Nicolò Magnante came up through Bain, BCG Milan, and HEC Paris. That pairing -- organic founder-pain at full strength on one side, consulting-coded credentials on the other -- is Superlog's signature, and it is the pairing I have to price.

Dropbox in 2007 worked because Drew Houston kept forgetting his USB stick at MIT and built the tool for himself before he built it for a market; Superlog has the same structural mechanism, but routed through Arseniy's ex-Datadog telemetry-pipeline work rather than a personal artifact. The CTO of Superlog has stood inside the incumbent's data plane and watched what alerts look like when they land on an engineer at 2am, and the product -- a wizard that installs OpenTelemetry, groups errors into incidents, and posts a mergeable PR in Slack -- is a direct rebuttal to what he saw there. Where Superlog diverges from the Dropbox rung is that the pain is professional rather than personal, and the CEO's HEC/BCG/Bain résumé is not the biography of someone who got woken up by a PagerDuty alert. The organic-idea filter mostly fires on Arseniy alone.

Coinbase in 2012 sits at the top of my schlep ladder because Brian Armstrong ran toward the bitcoin compliance grind that every other applicant in the batch was unconsciously routing around -- the unpleasant work *was* the moat. Superlog's schlep claim is thinner: framework-specific instrumentation skills for NextJS, FastAPI, and React Native, plus the operational drudgery of keeping an auto-instrumentation wizard from over-logging a customer into bill-shock. That is real grind, but it is software grind, not regulatory grind, and the dossier itself notes Datadog and Dynatrace already ship native OTel auto-instrumentation. When the schlep is "we will do the OTel integration work the incumbents already do, but better," you do not get a Coinbase-style moat; you get a head start measured in quarters.

Animal resourcefulness at Superlog reads as competent rather than feral. Show HN at 73 points is a respectable launch, the superloglabs CLI and skills repos are public, and a customer quote of "80-90% PR accept rate" is sitting on Hacker News -- but compounded over 18 months, none of these are the cereal-box stunt or the post-rejection Android wallet. The Bluco prior round (STATION F top 40, named logos like McDonald's and Volkswagen) shows Superlog's founders can sell into enterprises and ship a second startup together, which is the most underrated signal in the dossier, but Bluco did not become a breakout and the resourcefulness here looks more like seasoned operators than animals. I would meet them to find out which it actually is.

Hacker genealogy at Superlog would flip from a mid score to a high score if Arseniy's GitHub (@dd-ashishaev) and personal site arseniy.wtf showed a substantial pre-Datadog open-source footprint, or if the superloglabs CLI repo were at 600 stars instead of 6. As written, École 42 Paris '22 plus three years inside Datadog's data systems gives me a real hacker on the technical side -- this is the **Reddit** (2005) Aaron Swartz shape, scaled down -- but the CEO is on the other side of the "Hackers and Painters" line. A pair where one founder is BCG-coded is not disqualifying, but it does mean Superlog's hacker DNA lives entirely in one person, and that concentrates execution risk in a way the Collison brothers at **Stripe** (2010) never had.

Stripe is the right single-anchor parallel for Superlog's cofounder configuration: two founders who had already shipped something together before the YC check arrived, with the technical/commercial split running cleanly down the middle. Superlog's Bluco history -- co-founded in 2024, STATION F top 40 in 2025, enterprise customers named -- is the analog of the Collisons' Auctomatic. It is not as strong an anchor (Auctomatic was a $5M outcome at 17 and 19), but the structural fact is the same: these two have already discovered whether they can stand each other under stress, and the answer was yes long enough to start a second company together. This is the dimension where Superlog most clearly clears my bar.

My 36-month forecast for Superlog is acquisition by Datadog or Sentry at $30-80M after reaching roughly $1-3M ARR via bottoms-up developer adoption, rather than an independent path to a Sentry-scale outcome. The mechanism is the one the dossier already admits: Datadog and Dynatrace ship native OTel auto-instrumentation, the AI-fix-PR layer is replicable by any vendor with a Claude API key, and the rational move for both Superlog and the incumbent is a tuck-in once Superlog has proven the workflow but before the incumbent has rebuilt it. This is consistent with the signature -- strong organic founder-pain produces a real product, but a software-only schlep zone produces no defensible moat against the company Arseniy used to work at.

The bull case that should sting: Sentry in 2012 also looked like a thin wrapper on top of existing logging infrastructure, and I would have been wrong to dismiss it for the same reason I am about to dismiss Superlog. If Arseniy's Datadog-insider knowledge translates into a 10x better auto-instrumentation experience, and if the fix-PR accept rate genuinely holds at 80-90% as the customer base expands beyond friendly design partners, Superlog becomes a category-defining wedge before Datadog's product org can ship a credible response, and the bottoms-up OSS distribution compounds into Sentry-scale ARR. The mechanism that wins for me anyway: the CEO's BCG/Bain/HEC résumé is exactly the "Founder Mode" anti-pattern I flagged in September 2024, and the dossier offers no founder artifact -- no Magnante GitHub, no shipped technical demo from the CEO -- to override it. One verifiable condition would flip the verdict: if Superlog publishes a third-party benchmark on fix-PR accept rates across 20+ unaffiliated customers within six months and the number holds above 70%, the moat thesis is real and I write the check.
