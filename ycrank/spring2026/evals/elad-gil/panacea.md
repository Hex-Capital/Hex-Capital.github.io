# Panacea -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Panacea is a services-firm-with-AI-bolted-on riding a genuinely re-opening FDA regulatory market with no index position and no revenue curve disclosed -- the signature is "real catalyst, wrong structure to harvest it." Haris Javed-Akhtar (MD) and Yaman Ziadeh (ex-Bloomberg, ex-Anterior) have paired clinical authority with an AI-platform engineer, but Panacea's go-to-market is fixed-fee milestone consulting against MDI Consultants and EAS Consulting -- a hand-to-hand services fight, not an infrastructure position.

On market re-opening, my ladder runs **Anduril** (Ukraine collapsing Pentagon software inertia inside a 24-month window) at the top, **Decagon** in the middle (post-LLM customer-support workflows that *I funded*), and **Lyft** Series C at the bottom (a steady-state market with no recent catalyst). Panacea sits between Decagon and Anduril, closer to Decagon: the catalyst is real -- LLM drafting crossed the regulatory-narrative threshold in 2024-2025, Weave Bio's $20M Series A in October 2025 validates third-party belief, FDA 510(k) volume grew 12.8% YoY to 4,049 submissions in FY2025, and FDA's own AI/ML submission guidance is evolving. That's not nothing. It's the strongest dimension Panacea has, and it's the reason I am writing more than two paragraphs.

On index-like distribution position, my ladder is **Stripe** (an index on e-commerce, every transaction touches the rail) at the top, **Abridge** in the middle (the scribe layer every clinician-vendor must eventually integrate against, which is why I funded it), and **Lyft** at the bottom (forced to win share *against* Uber rather than ride a wave). Panacea sits at the Lyft end. Fixed-fee, milestone-priced, project-bounded engagements with biotech clients are not an infrastructure position -- they're a consulting P&L. The dossier explicitly notes switching costs accrue only if Panacea wins lifecycle work; the proprietary submission corpus that *could* compound into something Stripe-like is not disclosed to exist. Panacea doesn't sit in the workflow path of every FDA submission -- it competes for each one against EAS's 200+ contractor advisors and MDI's 2,000+ filings of history.

Revenue velocity is the second-worst rung. My ladder is **Harvey** ($3B → $5B → $8B in months, the canonical "just working" curve) at the top, **Perplexity** in the middle (early DAU compounding I could see in the cold-inbound pings), and the generic AI pre-seed with no growth disclosure at the bottom -- "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." Panacea sits at the bottom rung. No revenue, no logo count, no MoM curve -- the "12x faster" line is a consultant testimonial, not Panacea's P&L. Pre-seed stage earns some grace, but the silence is conspicuous when Weave Bio is already publicly capitalized.

Founder-ships-between-meetings: my ladder is **Aravind at Perplexity** (built the thing we discussed a week later) at top, **Brex's operationally-disciplined CEO** archetype in the middle, and pedigree-only founders at the bottom. Panacea sits between middle and bottom. Yaman's prior role as AI Platform Engineer at Anterior (a YC AI-healthcare-automation company) is the most relevant biographical fact in the dossier; Haris's MD recruits ex-FDA talent. But no shipped artifacts are named, no GitHub, no Twitter, no product-iteration cadence. On single-miracle architecture, **Decagon** (one miracle: LLM accuracy at support) is HIGH, **Color Genomics** (workflow on commodity sequencing) is MID, multi-miracle deep-science bets are LOW. Panacea needs at least two miracles to compound: (a) LLM drafting reliably crosses FDA reviewer acceptance, and (b) FDA does *not* impose human-author attestation that nullifies the "12x faster" claim per the Innolitics 2025 guidance trajectory. Closer to Color than Decagon, but not cleanly.

My 36-month forecast: Panacea reaches $4-12M ARR as a boutique regulatory consultancy serving sub-Series-B biotechs, then gets acquired by IQVIA, Veeva, or Weave Bio for a low-to-mid eight-figure outcome -- a fine business, not a fund-returner, because services economics cap multiples and Weave Bio's capitalized enterprise wedge prevents Panacea from indexing the category.

The bull case worth steel-manning is the **Color Genomics** (2015) precedent -- workflow infrastructure on top of a hard regulated thing, exactly Panacea's architecture, and that exception explicitly violates my "stay out of bio" prior. If Haris recruits five more Harun Sinhas under exclusive fixed-fee contracts, the talent-side scarcity becomes the moat Weave Bio cannot replicate, and Panacea becomes the *only* shop that bundles ex-FDA labor with AI under a pricing model the hourly incumbents (MDI, EAS, EMMA, The FDA Group) cannot convert to without cannibalizing partner draws. That argument stings. But Color I *operated* -- I didn't passively angel it -- and Panacea today has one named principal consultant, no disclosed exclusivity, two founders with no shared employer or shipping history, and a competitor with $20M in fresh capital. The single condition that flips me to Invest: Panacea publicly discloses 10+ exclusive ex-FDA consultants under contract AND month-over-month revenue compounding above 20% with three named biotech logos. Until then, this is a services business riding a wave I believe in, sold to me by a team I cannot yet verify ships.
