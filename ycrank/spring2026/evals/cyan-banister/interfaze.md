# Interfaze -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Interfaze is what happens when a competent second-time developer-API founder lines up his strongest dimension — Yoeven Khemlani's eight years of edge-ML and a $1M-ARR exit — against his weakest, which is that "AI model built for deterministic developer tasks" sits dead-center in the most consensus YC lane of Spring 2026. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game here is an OpenAI-compatible Chat Completions endpoint at $1.50/$3.50 per million tokens, competing with Reducto, LlamaParse, Mistral OCR, Unstructured, Docling, Marker, and Nanonets. The signature is "skilled operator, crowded lane, software-only swing" — and that signature does not produce the outcomes I underwrite for.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is the founder-lived-experience HIGH rung because he had already shipped Oculus to scale from his parents' garage before the defense pitch — the biography preceded the company. Yoeven gets partial credit on the same mechanism: he co-founded JigsawStack, raised $1.5M pre-seed from Antler and Ada Ventures, and his earlier Singapore travel startup hit $3M ARR before acquisition. He has touched the OCR/scraping pain that Interfaze's blog describes. But here Interfaze diverges from Luckey: JigsawStack is publicly described as "a unified AI API suite" — Yoeven's prior company is the SAME thesis as Interfaze, still alive, and he is still publicly identified as its founder. That isn't a Luckey-shipping-VR-then-pivoting-to-defense arc; that is a second swing at the same pitch with a YC stamp on it, and the dossier even flags "risk of split focus or unclear corporate boundary."

The pass example in my playbook — two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, oversubscribed seed, $200B TAM slide, four-logo team page — fails because the founder origin is analytical not lived, the product is pure software abstraction nobody can touch, and the round is consensus. Interfaze inherits two of those three failure mechanisms cleanly. Harsha Khurdula's M.Sc. from Purdue and IEEE CAI 2026 paper acceptance is the credentialed-researcher pattern, not the outsider-trajectory pattern that put me into Thumbtack or HandUp. The category is exactly where six top funds are chasing — every YC batch since 2023 has shipped a "deterministic LLM" or "structured-output" API, and Reducto, LlamaParse, Mistral, and Unstructured are already there with capital. The Pass-example mechanism is present in Interfaze.

Crusoe (~2018) was magically weird because burning gas flares in North Dakota was three uninvestable things stapled together; the round was underpriced because credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds. Interfaze's competitive-context paragraph names six funded incumbents in the same lane and a frontier model — Gemini 2.0 Flash at ~80% on RD-FormsBench — already closing the gap from above. Over 18 months this dimension compounds against Interfaze: every Anthropic or OpenAI release that tightens structured-output reliability eats a percentage point of the wedge the IEEE CAI 2026 paper is built on. Nobody has passed on Interfaze for aesthetic reasons; they're passing because the lane is full. That is not Crusoe weird; that is a developer-tools API priced into the consensus.

Counterfactual on capital-intensity: Interfaze would have to be wrapping a physical artifact — a vision system on a camera, an edge-inference chip, a regulated-health pipeline with HIPAA-bound document flows — to flip from a 4-tier score to a 9-tier score on the Anduril/SpaceX axis. The dossier instead describes "any AI SDK that supports OpenAI works out of the box" and "up to 50 requests per second." That is the software-abstraction signature of my pass example, not the rocket-or-drone signature of my Anduril tattoo. No flip.

HQ Trivia (2017) is my standing reminder that unresolved co-founder authority is what blows up companies even when the product is viral — Yusupov and Kroll feuded into the 2020-21 implosion. Interfaze's structure is a single anchor parallel to JigsawStack itself: Yoeven was CEO there, Harsha was a Founding Member, so the implicit hierarchy carries over. The dossier does not name a CEO at Interfaze, which is the soft version of the red flag — not 50/50 anonymity, but not the documented tiebreaker authority I want either. I'd score this above the HQ Trivia floor but below the Anduril clarity rung.

My 36-month forecast: Interfaze lands at $1-3M ARR by mid-2029 as a niche OCR-and-extraction API serving Series A/B startups, then gets acqui-hired by Unstructured, Reducto, or rolled back into JigsawStack at a sub-$30M outcome, with frontier model commoditization eating the structured-output moat from above. The mechanism is exactly the lowest-dimension signature: software-only API in a crowded lane where the moat is benchmarks the frontier labs will match within two release cycles.

The bull case that stings: Yoeven hit $1M ARR in year one at his Singapore travel startup and built a funded AI API company; "deterministic AI" is a real pain Reducto's own benchmarks documented, so calling this consensus may be confusing crowded-with-investors and crowded-with-revenue. He may have spent JigsawStack figuring out exactly which sub-task — OCR for dense tables, or audio transcription, or web scraping — is the wedge, and Interfaze could be the focused second swing rather than a rebrand. The dismantle: the dossier explicitly notes Yoeven is still publicly identified as JigsawStack's founder and the two companies' positioning overlap; second-time founders who haven't wound down the first company are not running the focused second swing, they are hedging. Verdict flips if Yoeven publicly winds down or sells JigsawStack and Interfaze posts an independently-audited 99% structured-output benchmark from a third party like Reducto's RD-FormsBench within 12 months. Until then, pass — with one foot in love, because Yoeven is the kind of operator I'd back on a different swing.
