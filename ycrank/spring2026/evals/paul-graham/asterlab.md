# Aster -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Emmett Bicker shipped a NanoGPT Speedrun world record at 95.2 seconds and a TriMul kernel at 1.114 ms before he had a funded company, and that single fact is Aster's analytical signature: an animal hacker who passed the pre-funding artifact test as cleanly as anyone I've seen in this batch, working solo on a problem that sits in the most overcrowded zone in AI -- the opposite of a schlep. High on animal, low on schlep blindness. That is the whole evaluation in one sentence; the rest of this is showing my work.

Brian Armstrong's hand-built Android wallet rescued his rejected **Coinbase** (2012) application because the artifact was load-bearing -- it wasn't a demo, it was proof of work against a real-world benchmark nobody else had cleared. Aster's NanoGPT PR #217 to KellerJordan/modded-nanogpt is structurally the same move: Bicker submitted a kernel that beat the public leaderboard, not a deck claiming he could. The arXiv 2602.07040 preprint claims SOTA across five distinct domains -- NanoGPT, TriMul, ZAPBench (190x compute reduction), Erdős Minimum Overlap, scRNA-seq denoising -- and the leaderboard PR makes at least one of those independently verifiable. Where Aster diverges from the Armstrong rung is that the artifact was produced by Bicker's *system*, not by Bicker's hands directly; the animal here is partly the agent. I lean toward counting it anyway, because building the animal counts as being one.

The pure-software AI-wrapper team I described in my pass example failed the schlep test because nothing about their work was unpleasant -- no compliance grind, no offline operations, no recruiting overhead. Aster is closer to that failure pattern than any thesis-fit signal in the dossier suggests. Sakana AI sits at $2.65B post-money with $479M raised and "AI Scientist v2" published in Nature. Autoscience just took $14M from General Catalyst in March 2026 to build "the world's first automated AI research lab" -- the exact phrase Aster could use. Lila Sciences has $550M for autonomous wet-labs. FutureHouse/Edison spun out at a $250M valuation. This is the most crowded glamour category in AI right now, which means the marginal "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) signal is inverted: every smart founder is running *toward* this, not around it. Aster does not name a single unpleasant grind in the dossier -- no manual customer onboarding, no regulatory exposure, no offline ops.

Bicker's organic-pain fit is real and I want to mark it specifically: he was a post-training researcher at Magic working on long-context coding LLMs before starting Aster, which means he lived inside the slowness of iterative ML experiments before automating them. That is the **Dropbox** (2007) USB-stick pattern in a different domain -- the tool is the one he wished existed at his last job. The "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) filter passes cleanly here; I do not need to infer it.

The hacker-genealogy signal compounds favorably over 18 months if the arXiv work draws contributor energy the way **Reddit** (2005) drew Swartz, but the dossier flags one anomaly that nags at me: the @EmmettBicker GitHub account was created 2026-05-07 with no public repos beyond the modded-nanogpt PR. A real Lisp-lineage hacker leaves a longer trail than that. The Magic post-training role and the arXiv first authorship carry the weight, but the absent GitHub history is the kind of detail my unstated-pattern filter notices.

Solo founder. Team size 1 on the YC page. I have written explicitly that "empirically it seems to be hard to start a startup with just one founder" and none of my top-tier outcomes is solo. The known exception is **Loopt** (2005) -- I funded Sam Altman solo because the animal test came back so loud that the structural rule bent. Bicker's NanoGPT record and five-domain arXiv paper are loud in the same register. I am applying the Loopt exception here, but I am applying it nervously, because Loopt did not become a top-decile outcome -- the override worked as a YC bet and not as an angel return.

My 36-month forecast: Aster gets acquired by Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google DeepMind for $80-200M as a research-team purchase within 24 months, before reaching $5M ARR. The mechanism is the high/low signature -- Bicker's solo animal output is exactly the profile a frontier lab will write a $100M check to absorb, and the crowded competitive field (Sakana, Autoscience, Edison) plus the solo capacity constraint against a five-domain product surface makes the standalone path narrower than the acquisition path. This is structurally a **Kiko** (2005) eBay-sale outcome, not a **Twitch** (2007) outcome.

The bull case stings: Alexandr Wang built **Scale AI** as a solo-founder animal who shipped before he pitched, and my own "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) essay was the trigger he cited. If Bicker is Wang-tier and the agentic-research-lab category is the one that produces the decade's compounding data moat -- proprietary trajectories from customer runs feeding the next agent generation -- then the acquihire forecast underprices him by an order of magnitude and I miss a **Stripe** (2010)-class outcome by passing on the override. The dossier evidence that wins against this case is the Autoscience $14M seed in March 2026 from General Catalyst: well-capitalized direct competition with identical positioning means Aster does not have the contrarian-fact-about-founders that **Coinbase** (2012) had when Armstrong was alone in wanting the compliance grind. The verifiable condition that would flip me to a clear Invest: Bicker recruits a technical cofounder with shipped open-source history before the YC interview, or Aster announces a paying frontier-lab customer (Anthropic, DeepMind, a national lab) running production research on the platform by month 12. Either fact lands this at 78+ and I write the check.
