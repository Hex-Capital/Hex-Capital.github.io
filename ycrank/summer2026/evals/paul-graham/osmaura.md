# Osmaura -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Osmaura is a competent technical pair walking toward a real schlep without yet showing the animal behavior that schlep will demand -- Kali Abeje and Jity Woldemichael have done 100+ partner interviews and produced zero shipped artifacts that the dossier surfaces, which is the inverse of the pattern I keep funding. Their highest dimension is the unpleasantness of their problem; their lowest is what they personally did before applying to YC. That pairing has a name in my portfolio and it usually doesn't end well.

The **Airbnb** (2009) cereal-box story is the reference case I reach for on animal resourcefulness, and Osmaura is not in that picture. Chesky and Gebbia sold a thousand $40 cereal boxes at $40,000 in revenue before any institutional check, which is the kind of stunt that flipped my idea-level skepticism. Osmaura's equivalent line in the dossier is "founders spoke with 100+ partners pre-launch." That is customer discovery, which any McKinsey associate can do, not the unscalable improvisation Brian Armstrong used to rescue his rejected **Coinbase** (2012) application with a hand-built Android wallet. Osmaura's founders are 22-year-old MIT CS graduates and I'd happily extend rope for a stunt I haven't seen yet -- but the dossier surfaces no demo, no paid pilot, no signed LOI with a named AmLaw firm, no revenue, no prototype usage data. Animals leave tracks, and Osmaura's tracks so far are conference-room tracks.

The AI-wrapper MBA-team pass-example fails because no founder has personally suffered the problem -- and Osmaura inherits the same defect in milder form. Neither Abeje nor Woldemichael has been a law firm partner missing origination targets. Abeje's biography names "mock trial" and a public policy minor, which is the thinnest possible founder-problem link I have ever scored above zero on. The product was found through 100+ interviews, not built for the founders themselves the way Drew Houston's missing USB stick produced **Dropbox** (2007). When the idea is downstream of market research rather than personal pain, founders typically flinch when the product needs to be rebuilt three times to fit the actual workflow -- and law firm partners will demand exactly that.

Osmaura's schlep posture is the one place I lean forward. Selling partner-facing software into AmLaw partnerships is genuinely unpleasant work: partnership-governed procurement, slow cycles, hostile incumbents like Introhive ($99M raised) and Nexl ($23M Series B October 2025) sitting on the InterAction integration surface Osmaura needs. This is the **Coinbase** (2012)-shaped grind other YC S26 applicants are routing around to build pure-software demos. Over 18 months the schlep compounds if Osmaura does the unglamorous work -- onsite at firms, hand-writing the first signal-to-matter mappings per partner practice area -- and collapses if they wait for the product to "scale" before doing it. The dossier does not yet tell me which way they go.

Hacker genealogy is mid -- Woldemichael's Jane Street fellowship, DRW internship, and Meta infra work compressing patching from 6 hours to 2 minutes is a real shipping story, and GitHub @Jity01 has 42 public repos. Abeje's NLP research at MIT GOV/LAB on argument extraction is on-thesis for what Osmaura claims to build. They are not the Collison brothers at **Stripe** (2010) with a Young Scientist Lisp prize, but they are nowhere near credentials-only. If Osmaura had shipped a public demo on Hacker News this week, this dimension would jump a tier. It hasn't.

Counterfactual on cofounder configuration: for the 10-point bucket to clear into "strong pair" territory, the dossier would need to evidence a multi-year working relationship -- a shared hackathon shipping history at AppDev@MIT, a co-authored research paper, anything beyond "both studied CS at MIT." It doesn't. Two MIT-CS technical founders with no prior co-shipped artifact is acceptable, not exciting, and the absence of any domain operator on a team selling into law firm partnerships is the gap that worries me more than the team size.

My 36-month forecast: Osmaura either acquihires into Nexl or Litera at sub-$15M before reaching $1M ARR, or dies quietly when the law firm sales cycle outlasts their pre-seed runway -- because the founders have shown me no pre-funding stunt and the incumbents are already $122M ahead on the same surface area. The mechanism is straightforward: partner-level enterprise sales require either a founder who has been a partner or a founder who behaves like an animal in lieu of being one, and Osmaura's dossier evidences neither.

The strongest case against me is **Scale AI**: Alexandr Wang was also a young technical MIT-adjacent founder who started from my "Schlep Blindness" essay (January 2012), and I'd be embarrassed to pass on the next one. The bear-killer in Osmaura's dossier is that Wang shipped product and revenue inside the schlep, not interviews about it -- and the Collisons at **Stripe** (2010) had already exited **Auctomatic** (2007) before I funded them, which is the prior-artifact evidence Osmaura does not have. One verifiable thing would flip me: a signed paid pilot with a named AmLaw 100 firm inside the next six months. Until then, Osmaura is the clever-idea-soft-founders pattern from my Reference Voice, and that is where I lose money. Pass.
