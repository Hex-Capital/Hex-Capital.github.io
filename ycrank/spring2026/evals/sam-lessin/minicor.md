# Minicor -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Minicor's signature is a domain-deep enterprise B2B team -- Faizaan Chishtie and Saheed Akinbile, who spent years building Laminar Run against the same legacy-integration problem -- selling into the single category I've publicly de-rated the hardest, "AI agents for [vertical]," with a standard YC SAFE wrapper and zero owned distribution. The high rung on Minicor is founder-market continuity; the low rung is founder-as-brand. That pairing tells you what this is: a competent execution-machine team running at a consensus AI-RPA wave, not a mispriced asset I can underwrite at an angle nobody else sees.

Faizaan and Saheed -- on the conviction axis, the load-bearing analogy is **Sublime Security**, where I wrote in because the operators had been inside the email-security problem long enough to have a non-obvious belief the consolidating SOC SaaS crowd didn't share. Minicor has the persistence half: Laminar Run → Minicor is the same two-person team pushing on legacy-system integration for 3+ years, and Saheed's Morgan Stanley Java work plus QuickBooks/Sage/Citrix RPA reps is the kind of grimy domain rep most YC pairs don't have. Where Minicor diverges from Sublime is the missionary artifact -- no public writing on why click-accuracy at 93-96% versus 80-85% is the right wedge, no opinionated essay on why computer-use models will fail in healthcare desktop apps specifically, no GitHub repo I can read as a thesis. The Laminar-to-Minicor pivot itself is the only available evidence of conviction, and pivot-as-conviction is a much weaker signal than 3 years of public obsession with the problem.

The pattern Minicor runs straight into is the canonical YC AI-agent pass: zero community footprint, enterprise B2B sales motion, founders with no public following, and a category that depends entirely on outbound and Forward Deployed Engineers ($125K-$200K postings on the YC page) to grow. That's the antithesis of the **John Fish / Bookshelved** check or the Slow Creator Fund mandate -- and it's not a flaw in Minicor's plan, it's just that Minicor lives in the half of the portfolio where founder-as-brand is structurally impossible. CTO Saheed has 43 GitHub repos but no retrievable star counts, neither founder has a confirmed Twitter, and the GTM motion will be 100% paid enterprise sales. This dimension is just absent -- not failed, absent -- and at Minicor's stage that means the criterion contributes nothing to the upside case.

On round pricing -- mechanism analysis over 18 months: Minicor is positioned in a category where **Skyvern** ($2.7M seed, OSS, web-only), **Browserbase** ($68M raised), **TinyFish** ($47M Series A led by ICONIQ), Anthropic computer-use, and OpenAI Operator are all compounding into the same primitive. That's the definition of a hot YC deck -- "RPA platform for deploying AI into legacy desktop systems" could have been written by any of fifteen P26 teams. Tom Blomfield as partner plus the "AI + legacy industries" framing virtually guarantees Minicor is priced at or above batch median. There is no dossier evidence of a tier-1 visibly passing, no sub-median price disclosed, and the regatta tack count to default-alive runs through Series A and B on Windows-VM infra costs. The mechanism that punishes this over 18 months is straightforward: each Anthropic/OpenAI model release compresses the 93-96% vs 80-85% accuracy claim, which is Minicor's entire stated moat.

On capital structure -- counterfactual: for Minicor to flip a tier on this axis, the financing would need to look non-standard. A royalty against the 25,000-patients/day customer's per-claim throughput. A revenue-share with the Forward Deployed Engineer cost amortized against named-account contracts. An employee-ownership wrapper across the legacy-system operators Minicor automates. None of that is in the dossier; what's there is a 7-person team in San Francisco hiring two enterprise roles, which prices like every other YC P26 B2B infra seed and will need the standard 5-tack regatta to reach default-alive. **Teamshares** got an oversized check from me because the cap structure couldn't be replicated by a generic SaaS underwriter; Minicor's cap structure can be replicated by every other Slow competitor in the room.

On category sleeve -- single-anchor parallel: the only relevant Known Exception is **Ro**, where I overrode capital-efficiency discipline because 50-state prescription rails were a regulatory moat only venture capital could clear. Minicor processing 25,000 patients/day under HIPAA with SOC 2 Type II is the dossier evidence that gestures at a Ro-shaped argument -- compliance schleps as a wedge. But Ro's moat was the licensing graph itself; Minicor's "moat" is a 13-point click-accuracy delta against base models that are improving on a quarterly cadence, plus a SOC 2 cert that every enterprise infra YC seed eventually clears. "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024) applies here cleanly -- this is AI-flavored RPA, not a disruptive sleeve.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Minicor is acquired by UiPath or Automation Anywhere as an agentic-RPA tuck-in for $30-80M sometime in 2027-2028 -- the healthcare desktop traction and HIPAA cert make it a clean bolt-on for a Magic Quadrant incumbent extending into self-healing agents -- OR Minicor stalls at $2-5M ARR competing against Skyvern OSS and Anthropic computer-use, with no path to $50M+ revenue without three more rounds. Either outcome is a non-event for the Slow regatta math at YC P26 entry prices; neither is a 2000x like Solana, and the modal exit doesn't return a $165M Fund VI line item.

The bull case stings on Saheed specifically -- a Morgan Stanley engineer who allegedly built Java apps saving $30M/year in fees, paired with deep QuickBooks/Sage/Citrix reps, is a more credible RPA operator than 90% of the YC AI-agent cohort, and 25,000 patients/day in production is not nothing if it's current-product rather than Laminar carry-over. The Ro analogy could load -- HIPAA + Citrix + on-prem deployment is a regulatory-and-deployment graph that Skyvern's open-source web-only repo literally cannot touch, and that asymmetry compounds with every named healthcare logo. What kills that case is the pivot-continuity ambiguity (laminar.run redirects to Minicor, traction attribution unclear), the unbenchmarked self-reported accuracy delta, and the structural fact that UiPath/Automation Anywhere/Microsoft Power Automate all announced agentic features in 2025 with existing enterprise contracts already foreclosing the GTM. The verifiable condition that flips Minicor for me is a non-standard financing instrument -- a per-transaction royalty against the healthcare desktop throughput, or 3+ named Fortune 500 multi-year contracts disclosed at a sub-$15M post -- and absent that, this is a pass on price discipline, not on team.
