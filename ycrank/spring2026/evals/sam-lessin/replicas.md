# Replicas -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Replicas is the platonic instance of my Pass Example given a name and a logo: two Waterloo undergrads wrapping Claude Code and Codex in sandbox VMs, three months out of YC, competing into a category where Cognition just printed $1B at $26B post-money and Anthropic itself shipped Claude Code Remote Tasks on March 20, 2026. The dimensional signature is brutal -- Replicas's *highest* dimension is "founder-market plumbing fit" (Loi shipped Helicone's AI Gateway, Arora was Applied AI at Ramp), and its *lowest* is round non-consensus, because this is exactly the YC P26 archetype every tier-1 is bidding on. A high plumbing-fit and a low price-discipline score is the signature of a competent wrapper getting force-fit into a valuation curve I cannot make money at.

On price discipline, the HIGH rung is **Solana** -- $0.05/coin in 2018-2019 when the consensus crowd had quit crypto, which is what 2000x exposure actually looks like on entry. The shared mechanism is *category-abandonment pricing*: I get paid for underwriting an asset other funds have written off. Replicas inverts the mechanism on every axis. The dossier shows Cognition at $26B post-money, Cursor pursuing a $50B pre, Factory at $1.5B post-money in April 2026 -- this is the most-bid category in the YC P26 batch, not the least. There is no scenario where Replicas's seed clears at a price that compensates for the regatta of follow-on rounds it will need to outlast Anthropic's native Remote Tasks feature. If the round is priced to the heat of the category, my edge is exactly zero.

The LOW rung on capital structure is my Pass Example itself -- the AI agent for [enterprise vertical] on a standard SAFE that needs five more rounds to reach default-alive. The failure mechanism is *cap-table dilution into commodity exposure*: each tack of the regatta requires raising into incumbents whose ARR doubles every two quarters, so the founders own a sliver of a derivative of someone else's model by Series B. Replicas tracks the pattern precisely. The product wraps Claude Code and Codex (tryreplicas.com), the billing is per-active-compute-time with no disclosed dollar tiers, and the open Founding Engineer req at $150K-$250K plus 2-4% equity implies a burn profile that needs follow-on within 12-18 months. There is no version of "default-alive in 24 months" here -- Replicas will be in market raising against Cognition's $492M ARR every time it tacks. That is founder-drainage, not a venture moment.

Founder conviction compounds or fails over 18 months as the category narrows -- and Replicas's dossier reads as opportunity-response, not mission. Saai Arora's LinkedIn headline is literally "Building something new and Incoming @ ramp," which tells me Replicas is a YC bet with a back-pocket return ticket, not a missionary commitment that will survive Anthropic eating the wrapper layer. No public writing from either founder on why their take on background agents is non-obvious; no thread, no essay, no opinionated artifact. Loi's @connortbot account exists but the dossier could not retrieve follower count, and his GitHub sits at 30 followers. By month 18, when Cognition has $1B ARR and Claude Code Remote Tasks is the OS default, missionary temperament is what gets you through the pivot to vertical-specific agents -- and I cannot find evidence of it in Replicas's public record.

On founder-as-brand, the counterfactual to flip a tier is concrete: Replicas would need Loi or Arora to have a 10K+ engaged developer audience with an opinionated POV on coding-agent failure modes -- the **John Fish** profile applied to dev tools. The dossier shows the opposite: 30 GitHub followers on @connortbot, no surfaced Twitter for Arora, a single V1 launch tweet with no retrievable engagement, and a customer roster (Mintlify, Composio, Helicone, Dart, Knowunity, Trellis, Chronicle Labs) that is entirely YC-network sourced. Replicas has Harj Taggar's rolodex, not owned distribution -- and that channel reprices to zero the moment the next YC batch ships its own background-agent darling.

Sublime Security is the single-anchor parallel for category sleeve, and the comparison is unflattering. I bought Sublime when most security investors were piled into late-stage SOC SaaS -- the sleeve was pre-consensus. Replicas sits in the inverse position: foundation-model AI infrastructure at seed pricing of an AI Series B, which is the exact sleeve my Joe Lonsdale podcast line ("AI is just more internet... it's not disruptive, crypto is fundamentally disruptive") was designed to deselect. Replicas is BYO-credential for Claude Code and Codex -- which is to say, Replicas is a thin orchestration layer above two vendors whose own native features (Anthropic's Remote Tasks, March 20, 2026) directly substitute for the wrapper. That is commoditization risk wearing a seed-round costume.

My 36-month forecast: Replicas either acquihires into a mid-tier dev-tools buyer (ClickUp/Codegen template, sub-$25M) by Q2 2028 or winds down quietly, with ARR capped under $3M as Anthropic's native Remote Tasks plus Cursor Background Agents collapse the BYO-orchestration wedge. The mechanism is clean: the wrapper layer between a model vendor and a developer IDE has negative durability when the model vendor ships the same feature natively and the IDE vendor ships it inside the editor where developers already live.

The strongest case against my pass is that Loi shipped Helicone's AI Gateway -- meaning he knows LLM-infra plumbing at a level most YC teams don't, and "30% of pull requests shipped" through Replicas at 20+ YC customers is a real engagement number for a 2-month-old product. The bull frame says BYO-credential is a structural wedge because cost-conscious engineering teams will not pay Cursor's token markup or Cognition's seat price when they can route their own Anthropic spend. It stings -- it's the same argument that made Helicone a real business. But the dossier kills it: every named Replicas customer is a YC company, which means the wedge is *Harj's rolodex*, not BYO economics, and Anthropic's Remote Tasks removes the orchestration reason-to-exist before the non-YC enterprise validation ever lands. The single verifiable condition that flips me: Replicas posts a non-YC enterprise logo (Fortune 1000 or comparable) with >$100K ACV by Q4 2026, proving the wedge is product, not network. Absent that, this is a pass before the meeting starts.
