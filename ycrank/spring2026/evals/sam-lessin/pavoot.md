# Pavoot -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Pavoot is two technically excellent ETH Zürich researchers — Gohar Tamrazyan, a 6-time Swiss Chess Champion, and Ana Yoon Faria de Lima with 20+ Scientific Olympiad medals — building a consensus-hot AI workflow agent in a category Vendelux ($16.4M raised, $2.1M 2024 revenue) and Grip ($14.5M raised) already occupy, and that Salesforce Einstein and HubSpot Breeze will replicate natively within eighteen months. Sane founders, sane idea, crowded category. The signature is brains-without-asymmetry, and it's the asymmetry I pay for.

On the **AI kill test**, my lowest-conviction dimension here, the high rung is Metropolis — physical parking infrastructure across thousands of garages that an LLM cannot conjure into existence — now ~$5B from seed. The low rung is the AI-copilot pitch I pass on every demo day: "we wrapped GPT-4 for industry X." Pavoot sits on the bottom rung. Read the dossier: "LLM-based personalization, CRM sync, photo recognition... widely available via OpenAI/Anthropic APIs and existing CRM SDKs." Pavoot is *exactly* the company where, when Marc Benioff sees the demo, he says "fuck yeah, ship that as an Einstein feature in Q3." There is no physical-world operation, no protocol, no proprietary attendee graph spanning recurring conferences, no creator-audience lock-in. Pipeline-from-events is a feature inside Salesforce, not a company.

On **Consensus Mispricing**, my highest-weighted dimension and Pavoot's most damning ladder, the high rung is Solana at four cents when every smart crypto person told me Ethereum had won — risk fully baked into a price of essentially zero. The low rung is the AI-wrapper YC batch I described in my pass example. Pavoot sits one notch above the bottom: AI + B2B GTM + agents is the single most consensus-bid category in venture in 2026. Vendelux raised at FirstMark in late 2023 and Grip at Kennet — the smart money has already paid up for "events → pipeline" once. Where is the contrarian frame? "What if they're right?" — but nobody's said no to event-tech AI. They're saying yes loudly. The risk isn't priced in; the upside is.

On **Primitive Creation**, the high rung is Venmo — peer-to-peer mobile payments became a verb, a behavior that didn't exist before. The low rung is "better CRM, faster analytics." Pavoot sits near the bottom: "AI agent that operates across the event lifecycle: pre-event… during the event… post-event" is a stitched-together workflow, not a new behavior. Marketers were already sourcing attendees, capturing conversations, and syncing to Salesforce — Pavoot automates the existing motion. Nobody will say "let me Pavoot this conference" the way they say "Venmo me." The Pavoot pivot from the START Summit media-hub product into pipeline-agent inside the YC batch is itself a tell — they don't yet know what behavior they're creating.

On **Sanity-Insanity**, my highest-placed dimension for Pavoot and still not enough, the high rung is Raj at Solana — watched through three companies, calm engineer pitching a different L1 architecture against the consensus. The mid rung is Butterfield at Slack — proven founder, but the idea was a primitive, which is what got me there. The low rung is incubator-matched co-founders with synthetic commitment. Pavoot's founders are clearly sane and brilliant — ETH CS, teaching awards, chess championship, Olympiad medals — but they met at an ETH AI Center course in 2025 and the idea isn't insane. It's reasonable. A reasonable person with a reasonable idea is not the trade. The ratio is wrong.

On **Capital Discipline**, the high rung is Gusto's disciplined deployment; the low rung is Allbirds' factory-farm DTC scaling. Pavoot is pre-seed with no data, so I default — but the demo-only CTA on pavoot.com, the single reference customer (START Global), no visible GTM hire, and a buyer persona (CMO/Demand Gen, six-figure ACVs) that requires founder-led enterprise selling tells me Pavoot will need an A round to hire sales the founders can't run themselves. That's the factory-farm path I think is dead.

**My 36-month forecast:** Pavoot raises a small seed extension on YC momentum, fails to land a second logo at HubSpot Breeze pricing, and either acqui-hires into Cvent or Bizzabo for sub-$30M, or narrows back to the START Summit media-distribution wedge and tops out at $1–3M ARR before native CRM features eat the lifecycle pitch. The mechanism is simple: when the buyer is a Salesforce shop and the moat is "we prompt better," the incumbent ships the feature before you ship the second renewal.

The strongest case against me is **Slack**. I paid consensus prices because Butterfield was creating a real new primitive — synchronous workplace messaging replacing email. If Pavoot is creating a genuinely new event-attribution behavior the way Slack created real-time work chat, I'm wrong to pass. But the Slack override required *primitive creation*, and Pavoot's product description is automating existing pre/during/post-event motions, not birthing a new verb. Vendelux already does pre-event. Grip already does in-event matchmaking. Salesforce already does CRM sync. Pavoot is the connective tissue, and connective tissue gets absorbed by whichever side has distribution. The ETH AI Center origin and chess-champion-plus-Olympiad-medalist pedigree is real, but I've seen this movie — brilliant technical founders with no enterprise GTM background selling to CMOs is how you burn three years finding out your buyer wanted the feature inside their existing tool. **One condition flips me:** Pavoot publishes a proprietary cross-event attendee graph spanning 25+ recurring B2B conferences with measurable identity-resolution lift over CRM-native tools — that would be a data moat that compounds, and I'd run the meeting.

Pass.
