# Sirius Technology -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

The first thing that strikes me about Sirius Technology is the gap between the numbers and the narrative. Three million in ARR, 200% net revenue retention, 75% month-over-month growth — if these are real, this is a rocket ship. But every one of those figures appears exclusively on the a16z Speedrun listing, self-reported, with zero third-party verification, no named customers, no press, and 137 LinkedIn followers. I've been investing long enough to know that extraordinary claims without independent evidence are just claims. The founders' LinkedIn profiles weren't even accessible at the time of research. When I invested in SpaceX, the rockets were blowing up, but I could see the rockets. When I backed Uber, a driver handed me his card — the product was in the world, being used by a real person who was moved enough to proselytize for it. Sirius Technology asks me to trust metrics printed on a listing page, and that's a different kind of ask entirely.

Now, tell me the founders' stories. Azamat Khamidov previously built PointAI, an AI e-commerce chatbot that he claims scaled to 5M MAU in four months and became "the first ever acquisition in Telegram" — again, unverifiable. Benazir Toleubekova's bio is genuinely arresting: full scholarship to Beijing University of Technology at fifteen, joined Taobao at seventeen, supposedly architected Alibaba's core recommendation engine serving 500 million daily users. If that Alibaba claim holds up, Benazir has touched personalization infrastructure at a scale that almost nobody on Earth has. That's a real biographical forge — not for this specific problem, but for building large-scale prediction systems. What I don't hear in either founder's story is why subscription churn keeps them awake at night. They pivoted from PointAI, an e-commerce support tool, to Sirius, a retention platform, in 2025. That reads as smart operators iterating toward a bigger market, not as people whose life experience made this problem impossible to ignore. When I ask "what happened to you that made this idea find you?" — the answer appears to be "we saw the market opportunity after building a related product." That's competent. It's not forged.

Subscription churn is the most measured metric in the subscription economy. Every company with a Stripe integration already has a churn dashboard. Gainsight, ChurnZero, LiveX AI, Subsets, Churned.io, QuadSci — the dossier lists five funded startups and two established platforms competing in overlapping retention use cases. This is not a space that makes anyone shift uncomfortably in their chairs. Nobody at a dinner party would flinch if you said "I'm building AI to reduce subscription churn." Compare that to when I told people I was backing Anduril — defense AI in a Silicon Valley that considered the Pentagon morally toxic — or Contraline, which was male birth control, a category so stigmatized most VCs couldn't even say it out loud. Sirius Technology operates in a consensus-attractive, well-funded space where the idea provokes nods, not discomfort. And critically, there's no invisible system being exposed here. Churn isn't a hidden tax. It's not an opaque structure extracting value from people who don't understand it. Subscription companies know exactly what their churn rate is — they just want a tool to fight it. That's optimization software, not structural revelation. My portfolio pattern is Affirm attacking credit card opacity, Ownwell attacking property tax darkness, Carta pulling cap table mystery into the light. Sirius Technology is building a better mousetrap for a problem everyone already sees clearly.

The defensibility question worries me in a specific way that connects to my hardest-learned lessons. Sirius Technology sits on top of Stripe, Chargebee, Amplitude, and Mixpanel via API integrations. That's the value chain. If Stripe decides to build a native cancellation-interception feature — and Stripe has every incentive and every capability to do so — Sirius's entire product becomes a feature inside someone else's platform. This is the pattern Lee and I talk about when we say "not some B.S. SaaS company or AI wrapper." The claimed data flywheel from "millions of interactions daily" could theoretically compound into a moat, but that data lives on top of billing platforms that own the customer relationship. I think about my IronPort experience — we built email security infrastructure that was genuinely hard to replicate because the data and the system were deeply intertwined. Sirius Technology's retention models, however sophisticated, are processing data that flows through platforms controlled by others.

The strongest bull case: Benazir's recommendation engine experience at Alibaba scale, if verified, is a genuinely rare technical credential. A team that built PointAI to 5M MAU and then pivoted toward a stronger business model shows adaptive intelligence. If the $3M ARR is real, Sirius Technology has already outgrown most pre-seed companies and might be building a data advantage that becomes self-reinforcing before Stripe or LiveX AI catches up. Consumer AI subscription churn is accelerating — RevenueCat data shows AI-powered apps churn 30% faster than traditional apps — so the timing could be a narrow window. What would have to be true: the traction would need to be independently verifiable, the data flywheel would need to already be producing measurably better retention outcomes than competitors like LiveX AI's claimed 35% retention improvement, and the founders would need to demonstrate that their prior experience gave them a proprietary insight into cancellation-moment psychology that no one else possesses. I'd want to hear Benazir describe exactly what she learned building Alibaba's recommendation engine that applies here — that would be the biographical thread I'm looking for.

Sirius Technology scores lowest on the two dimensions that drive my highest-conviction bets. Contrarian conviction: this is a crowded, consensus-positive category with six-plus competitors and no social, regulatory, or institutional discomfort attached to the thesis. Invisible system: subscription churn is visible, measured, and well-understood — there's no hidden structure to expose. The founders show technical competence and prior startup experience, which lifts them above a bare default, but the absence of a biographical connection to subscription retention — and the inability to verify the extraordinary claims in the dossier — keeps the forge score moderate. This is a capable team building optimization software in a transparent market. I wish them well, but this isn't a Long Journey bet.
