# Gojiberry AI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Pierre-Eliott Lallemant just sold CoCo AI in 2025, Dylan Teixeira just sold Edusign in 2025, and within months the three of them are in YC Spring 2026 selling an AI-SDR product against Clay ($3.1B), Apollo ($1.6B), Amplemarket, Regie.ai, Smartlead, Outreach, Salesloft, and Lemlist. Gojiberry AI is the dimensional signature I see most often in 2026 YC batches and write checks into least often: high execution velocity ($1.4M ARR in 9 months, 44% MoM) from repeat-exited operators, paired with maximally consensus category placement and zero category-specific conviction artifact. Gojiberry AI is a mercenary execution machine pointed at the hottest YC sleeve of the year -- which is exactly the profile my Pass Example was written about.

The Pass Example I wrote almost verbatim describes Gojiberry AI: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], three months out of YC, four tier-1 VCs already in." Round price isn't disclosed but the absent-evidence prior on a $1.4M-ARR 44%-MoM AI-SDR coming out of S26 is a $25-40M post-money with a competitive process -- "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale Ep 113, 2024) and the 2026 batch is wall-to-wall AI-SDR. Gojiberry AI's product page tags -- "30+ intent signals," "LinkedIn engagement on competitors," "auto-generates personalized outreach" -- is the same screen I've now read in Clay's own product copy, Amplemarket's blog, and DigitalApplied's AI-SDR roundup; there is no category mispricing here to underwrite, just access to a high-velocity SAFE that doesn't pay me for the regatta tacks it still has to run against incumbents who already shipped this.

Take the Pass-Example failure mechanism on the brand/community axis: pure paid-and-cold-acquisition distribution with no founder-published category conviction. Gojiberry AI's distribution stack is "founder-led inbound on LinkedIn plus Product Hunt launches" and an SDR-hire job post -- Lallemant has a Twitter account whose follower count is "not retrievable," Czerny has no public Twitter, Teixeira has no public Twitter, and none of them ship public writing on outbound that I can find beyond a "RIP traditional outbound. Gemini 3" LinkedIn post. John Fish brought a YouTube literary audience to Bookshelved; Marina Mogilko brought a multi-million-subscriber language channel; Gojiberry AI brings three LinkedIn profiles at "500+ connections" -- which is the LinkedIn default badge, not a distribution moat.

Founder conviction over 18 months is where I run a mechanism check, not a ladder. The CoCo AI exit at ~€500K ARR and the Edusign exit at multi-million ARR / 600K+ users are real operating credentials, and Teixeira's six-year arc at Edusign passes my "3+ years on the problem" test for *that* problem -- but Gojiberry AI is not that problem. The mechanism that compounds in my portfolio is missionary obsession with a specific category through a non-linear arc -- "**Venmo** took so many turns of the crank." The mechanism I see in Gojiberry AI is serial-exit founders pattern-matching to the hottest YC category 90 days after their last close, which compounds the opposite way: at the first hard tack, the cheapest action is to sell into a Clay or Apollo roll-up, not to keep cranking for seven more years.

The counterfactual for capital structure: what would have to be true for Gojiberry AI to score venture-shaped rather than venture-drainage? At $1.4M ARR on YC seed dollars with 44% MoM, Gojiberry AI is genuinely closer to default-alive than the median S26 deck -- the unit economics imply they could likely stop raising. But the cap table on offer is the vanilla SaaS SAFE that requires five more rounds to outrun Clay's $186M war chest, not the Teamshares-style holdco or the Mogilko-style royalty I would write into. The structure flips venture-shaped only if Gojiberry AI raises a single small round, prices it below batch median, and commits to a regatta tack toward profitability rather than the Series A pageant -- nothing in the dossier suggests that's the plan.

Single-anchor parallel on the category sleeve: this is the opposite of the Sublime Security trade. Sublime was a pre-consensus enterprise security entry before the consolidation wave; Gojiberry AI is a mid-consensus AI-SDR entry *during* the consolidation wave, with Regie.ai already at $30M Series B and 300% YoY (Scale Venture Partners, Feb 2025) and Clay at $100M ARR with capital to ship intent-native modules. The category sleeve I'm convicted on -- pre-consensus crypto, DTC consumer with margin, creator infrastructure -- is the inverse of generic AI-on-LinkedIn-scraping, which the dossier itself flags as platform-dependent on the same LinkedIn that throttled Dux-Soup and Phantombuster.

Forecast, 36 months: Gojiberry AI reaches $4-8M ARR by mid-2027, hits a growth wall as Clay or Apollo ships native intent-sequencing, and exits as a tuck-in acquisition to one of Clay / Apollo / Salesloft / Outreach at a 3-5x ARR multiple sometime in 2028 -- a fine outcome for the three founders, a non-event for a Slow check at a S26 consensus seed price.

The strongest bear-case-on-my-pass: I'm watching three repeat-exited founders execute 44% MoM on real revenue with capital efficiency that satisfies my own "default-alive" filter, and the Robinhood Known Exception says my earliest-stage entry price absorbs subsequent consensus rounds. The Elad Gil counter would be: pay up for the velocity, the operators have proven they ship and sell. I dismantle this on two facts: Robinhood worked because Slow entered *before* consensus, and Gojiberry AI is already inside the consensus wave -- four named AI-SDR competitors with $50M+ raised and two incumbents with $1.6B+ valuations are shipping the same product description today. And the CoCo AI / Edusign exits are evidence of *exit temperament*, not missionary temperament -- I want founders who refuse to sell at the first acqui-hire offer, not operators with two priors of doing exactly that. Flip condition: if Gojiberry AI's seed comes in at sub-$10M post-money with a tier-1 visibly passing, AND Lallemant publishes a non-obvious essay on why intent-first outbound structurally beats Clay-style orchestration in the small-team segment -- I'd re-open at Neutral.
