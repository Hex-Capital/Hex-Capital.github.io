# Miso Labs -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Cassidy Dalva and Aoden Teo are two Stanford alums shipping an 8B open-weights TTS model into the same hallway where ElevenLabs just closed $500M at $11B and Cartesia is already clocking sub-40ms TTFA, and the signature that jumps out of the Miso Labs dossier is a tactile artifact you can pull down from Hugging Face today wrapped around a founder pair whose biographies do not touch voice. The highest rung is the thing I can hear; the lowest rung is the *why* of who is making me hear it. That asymmetry -- verifiable artifact, unverifiable origin -- is the entire Miso Labs read.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before he ever pitched Anduril, and that prior-product trajectory is the rung I measure every founder pair against on lived experience. Miso Labs does not clear that bar and does not even clear the polymath-Hassabis exception: Cassidy Dalva's resume is Stanford Economics, the Stanford Daily, a King Center research fellowship in Bangladesh health outcomes, and journalism in the Washington Post -- a credential stack with no acoustic, ML, or voice-product origin moment. Aoden Teo's combinatorics work touches the math under residual vector quantization in the abstract, but combinatorics publications are not the same thing as having lived inside the problem of voice agents reacting in 160ms. There is no autobiographical moment in the Miso Labs dossier where either founder felt the 700ms ElevenLabs lag as a personal injury -- they discovered it the way the Pass Example founders discover problems, through a market read.

HQ Trivia (2017) is the failure rung I keep returning to on governance: Yusupov and Kroll were a 50/50 partnership with no tiebreaker, and the feud that followed is half of why that company died on a hot product. The Miso Labs dossier names two co-founders, lists no CEO, names no equity split, and gives me no operating-agreement signal -- Cassidy Dalva's email is the only one published, which is a thin tell and not a structural one. I am not accusing Miso Labs of the HQ Trivia pathology; I am noting that the dossier is *silent* on the exact question I learned to ask after HQ Trivia imploded, and silence at pre-seed is the moment to ask, not the moment to assume resolved.

On magically weird, the eighteen-month compounding math runs against Miso Labs. ElevenLabs closed $500M Series D at $11B in February 2026 and Cartesia raised ~$100M in October 2025 -- the capital is already inside the room, the press is already covering the room, and Miso Labs's 312K-view launch tweet is itself a tell that this category is the opposite of ignored. The open-weights wedge is the one non-consensus seam, but mlx-community shipped an MisoTTS-8bit quantization within days of launch, which means the wedge commoditizes Miso Labs *into* the ecosystem rather than away from it. I am supposed to find them before it becomes consensus; voice AI in Spring 2026 *is* the consensus.

The frontier-hardware-or-unsexy criterion flips only if Miso Labs were doing voice for something other investors refused to touch -- defense voice agents, regulated clinical telehealth voice, prison-call voice, religious confessional voice. The Miso Labs dossier shows none of that: an 8B TTS foundation model with an API "coming soon" is software-shaped, B2B-developer-shaped, and aesthetically the most fundable category of 2026. Counterfactual: if the Miso Labs dossier said "we ship into Medicare-compliant tele-mental-health voice agents with HIPAA-traceable emotive logs," I would re-score this dimension at the top of its range and probably re-open the founder dimension on mission grounds. It does not.

Where Miso Labs actually earns its score is the Flock Safety axis: I can install MisoTTS from Hugging Face, run the 8-bit MLX port on a laptop, and hear whether the emotive control claim is real in an afternoon. That is closer to the Niantic-Ingress test than to the Pass Example developer-API abstraction. The verifiability is real -- and yet it is *because* the weights are open that the moat is fragile, which is the strange double-edge of Miso Labs's strongest dimension feeding directly into its weakest competitive position.

My 36-month forecast for Miso Labs: acqui-hired by ElevenLabs, Cartesia, or a hyperscaler for under $40M as a talent-and-weights tuck-in within 24 months, with the hosted-API plan never crossing $2M ARR, because Cartesia's Sonic Turbo sub-40ms TTFA already invalidates the 110ms headline before Miso Labs's monetization page even ships. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: a verifiable artifact (high) without a lived-experience founder (low) gets out-iterated by the better-capitalized incumbent who can hire the talent that built the artifact rather than buy the company around it.

The strongest bull case against my forecast: Aoden Teo's combinatorics depth on the 32-codebook 2048-way RVQ architecture is genuinely novel work, the 312K-view launch tweet plus MarkTechPost plus the mlx-community port shows the developer-distribution flywheel turning on day one, and Miso Labs could become the Hugging Face / MosaicML of expressive voice -- open-weights gravity converting to a $100M+ acqui-hire by a hyperscaler that wants the team and the model card. The DeepMind exception lives here: pedigreed founders compounding across math and ML can override the lived-experience screen. I am dismantling the bull case on one fact: DeepMind's Hassabis had a *decade* of chess-prodigy-neuroscientist-game-designer compounding visible in public artifacts before the check; Miso Labs's Teo has combinatorics papers and a World Cube Association profile, which is real but not the same magnitude of cross-domain stack. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a named enterprise design partner in a regulated voice domain (clinical, defense, or financial-services compliance recording) signed in the next 90 days, with Aoden Teo demonstrably on-site rather than remote -- the Crusoe walk-on-the-well-pad test, applied to voice.
