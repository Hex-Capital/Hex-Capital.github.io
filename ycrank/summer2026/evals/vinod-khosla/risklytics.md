# Risklytics -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Risklytics is what my Pass Example essay was written about, dressed in a wildfire jacket. Two Harvard CS founders -- Samuel Gold (left after one year) and Alexander Risio -- run gradient-boosted trees on 131,929 publicly available CAL FIRE damage inspections and sell a 0-100 score to California insurers. The dossier's high-water mark is a 91% discrimination rate on the January 2025 LA fires across 30,469 homes. The low-water mark is that ZestyAI's Z-FIRE is already deployed with carriers writing 40% of the CA homeowners market on 200B+ datapoints. Highest-relative dimension is Consequence -- the California FAIR Plan ballooned from 126K policies to 450K+ with $650B exposure by June 2025, so the problem is civilizationally-scaled even if Risklytics's slice is not. Lowest is Technical/Capital Risk -- Risklytics is a software API that any Tier-1 generalist could fund with $3M.

**Impossible Foods** (2011) is the anchor at the top of my Consequence ladder because the success-state was "animal protein production disappears," not "alt-meat gets 30% better." Risklytics's success-state is that California underwriters price wildfire risk with a 91%-discrimination model instead of an 85%-discrimination model, and homeowners retrofit vents to stay insurable. That is a repricing inside an existing regulated regime, not the collapse of one. The $800B-$1.3T coverage gap (Stanford Woods / taxpayer.net, 2025) is the market Risklytics *lives in*, not the market Risklytics *ends*. The gap closes when reinsurance capital returns and state politics on Prop 103 give -- neither of which a per-address API from Risklytics causes. This is the **DoorDash** (2013) exception's mirror image without DoorDash's category-remaking distribution: consumer-facing scale changed commerce flows; Risklytics changes an underwriting spreadsheet. Consequence lands mid-low on the ladder -- closer to the sales-call summarizer than to Impossible Foods.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) is my lowest rung for Technical/Capital Risk because its failure mode -- capital plan understating the burn required for the science to work -- was at least *attached to real science*. Risklytics has the inverse pathology: no science at all. GBTs on CAL FIRE inspections is the ZestyAI Z-FIRE recipe, and Z-FIRE has 20+ years of claims data across 1,500+ wildfires against Risklytics's 435. The CAL FIRE corpus is *public*. The 91% discrimination is impressive as a class-project number and unimpressive as a moat number, because incumbents can replicate it with more data. No falsifiable miracles are being attempted here -- no new sensing modality, no physical model of ember transport, no closed-loop retrofit-efficacy dataset yet (the dossier explicitly flags this as "unproven now because no customer deployments are publicly disclosed"). Risklytics sits below the Range Fuels rung: no science risk at all is worse for my framework than science risk that failed publicly.

Founder Defiance -- mechanism analysis. Samuel Gold's Harvard Undergraduate Capital Partners tag and Alexander Risio's "ambitious software" phrasing are the exact expert-continuity pattern my TED Talk warns about, minus even the domain expertise. Neither founder has an insurance, actuarial, wildfire-science, or catastrophe-modeling role in the dossier. Over 18 months this compounds against Risklytics because CA DOI's May 2025 approval of Verisk / KCC / Moody's RMS forward-looking models means the room that matters is filled with people who have filed rate models before -- and the founders have not. There is no "instigator" position taken publicly against ZestyAI's approach, only a packaging claim about retrofit paths. Absent-evidence default applies, adjusted down for the visible domain gap.

Founder Learning Rate -- counterfactual. For Risklytics to score into my **Slash** (2024) exception zone, the dossier would need a documented Risklytics pivot -- e.g., "started as a consumer wildfire-risk app, hit a wall on homeowner willingness-to-pay, repositioned to carrier API after conversations with three underwriters." Nothing of the kind is in the dossier. Zero jobs open, zero public pivots, no thesis-update artifact from Gold or Risio. This is a linear-plan pre-seed. Absent-evidence default holds; I do not aggressively penalize because the company is young, but I do not credit either.

Required-Capital -- single-anchor parallel. Compare Risklytics to **Genesis AI** (2025), where I co-led a $105M seed because robot foundation models eat capital in a way that pre-commits the deployment path -- GPU clusters, teleoperation fleets, sim infrastructure. Risklytics's roadmap needs an ML engineer, an AWS bill, and a sales rep to walk into Mercury Insurance. My check size is the wrong instrument for this deal. Any $3M pre-seed generalist -- and any of the 30 seed funds that already backed ZestyAI's cap table -- is the right instrument. When I am over-resourced for a deal, that is itself a signal that the deal is inside the consensus, not outside it.

Forecast, 36 months: Risklytics is acquired by Guidewire, Duck Creek, or Majesco for under $25M as an underwriting-workbench feature, OR it is dead after ZestyAI ships a retrofit-recommendation module in 2027 and CA DOI declines to review a two-person startup's cat model. The mechanism is distribution asymmetry -- Z-FIRE's 40% carrier install base compounds every quarter Risklytics is not in a carrier, and CAPE Analytics-through-Moody's (Jan 2025) closes the enterprise-procurement door from the other side.

The bull case that stings: California just cracked open forward-looking cat models in May 2025 for the first time in US history, the FAIR Plan is a political emergency, and Risklytics's retrofit-to-insurability wedge is a genuinely different API output than Z-FIRE's pure risk score -- because it makes the *homeowner actionable*, not just the underwriter. If a California admitted carrier signs Risklytics to power a "path to renewal" program for non-renewed policyholders, that is a labor-cost displacement of the entire manual re-underwriting workflow and a **Glean** (2024) exception -- domain-expert-adjacent founders riding a regulatory window. The dossier evidence that wins against this: zero disclosed carrier LOIs, zero press, zero pricing transparency, and a name-collision with a German factoring-risk company that already owns risklytics.de. The single condition that flips my verdict: a signed pilot with a top-10 CA admitted carrier (Mercury, CSAA, Auto Club, State Farm CA) *plus* a CA DOI pre-filing meeting on the record. Absent both, Risklytics is a Pass -- a competent optimization play in a market where the incumbents already have the data and the distribution.
