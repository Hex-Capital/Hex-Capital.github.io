# Foaster -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Foaster is two technically interesting founders -- Raphaël Dabadie, who built a 400K-person tennis community at age 11 and co-authored LLM benchmarks with 12M views, and Alexandre Combes, who ran 60K+ AI calls through Répondia for French restaurant groups -- pointed at the single 2026 category I have publicly declared off-limits: "AI agents for [enterprise vertical]" sold as consulting transformation. The dimensional signature is asymmetric founder artifacts strapped to an anti-sleeve category, and that's a pass for me almost regardless of price, because the price isn't even the part I can fix.

Foaster's Pass Example in my own deck reads: "We're building an AI agent for [enterprise vertical], three months out of YC, $30M post-money seed, four tier-1 VCs already in." Foaster is a 2026 YC company pitching "AI agents that drive your AI transformation" against McKinsey/BCG/Bain at "3-5x less expensive." Round terms aren't disclosed in the dossier, but the silence is the tell -- when a YC Spring 2026 AI-services company doesn't lead with non-consensus price language, I assume Foaster is being priced into the same SAFE band as the rest of the batch. Distyl AI already raised ~$32M Series A from Khosla on essentially the same AI-native-services thesis; that's the consensus heat sitting on top of Foaster's category, not a tailwind. I don't get paid to be the seventh fund into a thesis Khosla already validated at the A.

Solana at $0.05/coin was the sleeve test: a category Sand Hill had publicly quit, where the disagreement itself was the alpha. Foaster sits at the exact opposite pole. BCG explicitly guides ~40% of 2026 revenue to be AI-related and McKinsey reports ~500 AI client engagements in the past 12 months -- the incumbents are not asleep, they are the bid, and Foaster's "3-5x cheaper" framing is a price-undercut pitch into a market where the buyers' existing vendors are already shipping the product. "AI is just more internet" (Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024) was specifically built to handle this Foaster shape: a generalist AI-services wrapper with no proprietary data network, no on-chain economic primitive, no DTC margin, no employee-equity structure, no creator on the cap table. There is no sleeve I'm convicted on that Foaster lives inside.

Raphaël Dabadie's biography is genuinely interesting on the missionary-temperament axis -- a kid who built a 400K community, talked his way onto Rafael Nadal's team at 18, and co-shipped LLM benchmarks that hit 12M views is not running the sanitized McKinsey-alum playbook I de-rate. The "what if they're right?" inversion partially loads here: Dabadie clearly does things other people wouldn't try. But conviction over 18 months compounds when it's pointed at a thing the founder cannot be talked out of and the market is wrong about; here it's pointed at "consulting is broken and AI agents will replace it," which is a thesis every other YC AI-services founder is also shouting from the same stage. The temperament is asymmetric; the bet the temperament is being spent on is not.

Capital structure is the dimension where Foaster could have surprised me and didn't. A services business with forward-deployed engineers, monthly retainer SKUs, and "3-5x cheaper" pricing is structurally a consulting firm -- the kind of thing I'd respect if it were running on services revenue from month two and treating venture as optional acceleration, the Squarespace/Muck Rack/Zapier shape I publicly defend. Instead Foaster is a 2-person team in the YC SAFE funnel with an FDE-heavy delivery model that does not scale without aggressive hiring; the dossier discloses no revenue, no LOIs, no named customers, and no non-standard financing instrument. That's a generic SaaS cap table on a business whose unit economics are actually services-shaped -- the worst of both, where the founder takes dilution to fund headcount that should have been funded by client invoices.

Combes' Répondia background -- 60K+ AI calls handled for French restaurant groups -- is the closest thing to a real artifact in the dossier, and Dabadie spent the prior year advising enterprises on AI deployment before founding Foaster. That's two founders with relevant reps, not LARPing. CEO X account at 6,315 followers plus 12M-view benchmark distribution is non-trivial for a pre-seed, though it's tech-Twitter reach, not John Fish's owned-audience-as-asset. If Foaster had shown up six months from now having quietly run the "AI interview every employee" service manually for 20 mid-market companies and decided to productize the highest-leverage piece -- the Wizard-of-Oz template I lived at Fin -- I'd take a second meeting. The dossier shows no such operating period.

36-month forecast: Foaster does not become an AI-native McKinsey. Most likely outcome is that Foaster reaches $1-3M services revenue in 18 months, hits the FDE-scaling wall, pivots from "AI consulting firm" to a vertical AI-agent product (probably the employee-interview agent itself, sold as a standalone HR/ops-diagnostics tool), and either raises a hard seed extension at flat or gets acqui-hired into Accenture/BCG X / Deloitte AI practice in the $10-30M range for the two-founder team. The mechanism is straightforward: the services revenue funds the runway long enough for the founders to discover that the productizable IP is the interview agent, not the roadmap deliverable, and the incumbents will pay talent-acquisition prices because they need AI-native operators inside their partner-leverage model anyway.

The strongest bull case is Distyl AI -- Khosla led a ~$32M Series A on essentially the same AI-native-services premise, which means a major fund underwrote this exact shape and Foaster could be the seed-stage version of that trade. The Ro known exception also looms: I made an explicit override for a capital-intensive services-shaped company when the "venture moment" was real, and one could argue AI transformation services in 2026 is genuinely a multi-year window where only VC capital clears the GTM schleps. I don't buy it. Ro's override hinged on 50-state prescription-rail regulatory complexity that physically required venture money; Foaster's "schlep" is enterprise sales, which is a hiring problem, not a regulatory moat. And Distyl's A is exactly the consensus marker that tells me the seed window for this shape is already priced -- the alpha left at Foaster's seed price is the same alpha I'd get buying any other Spring 2026 AI-services SAFE, which is to say none. Flip condition: if Foaster shows up with a signed multi-year revenue-share deal with a named mid-market buyer (not a logo wall, an actual contract structure that converts roadmap-execution outcomes into Foaster equity in client savings), I'd take the meeting -- because then the financing structure has stopped being a SAFE and started being the thing I underwrite.
