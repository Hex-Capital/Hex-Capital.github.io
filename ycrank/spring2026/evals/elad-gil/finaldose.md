# FinalDose -- Elad Gil Evaluation

FinalDose is, with apologies to the three Oxford DPhils, the platonic example of the deal I tell people I don't do: a programmable DNA-sensing kill-switch molecule from Jeff Liu, Li-Yao Huang and Steven Lin that requires the science to work, the delivery to work, the regulator to invent a precedent class, and the capital stack to survive a CRISPR-peer winter where Intellia just cut 27% and Caribou cut 32%. The dossier itself flags "capital intensity vs. pre-seed funding" against $300M+ comparable raises -- this is the "capitalization issues and science risk and market risk" stack from Tim Ferriss Show #863 (April 2026), all three present, none isolated.

On my single-miracle ladder, the HIGH rung is **Decagon** (2023) -- one assumed capability (LLM accuracy at support-task) and the rest is GTM. The MID rung is **Anduril** (2022), where the miracle was software-on-defense distribution on top of hardware stacks that already existed. The LOW rung is the fusion and novel-battery-chem deals I've passed on for a decade. FinalDose sits at the LOW rung: the dossier requires (1) single-base-pair specificity in heterogeneous human tissue from a hijacked DNA-sensing protein with "no public preclinical efficacy or off-target data identified," (2) an unaddressed in vivo delivery problem that the Intellia/Caribou/Mammoth peer set has been bleeding on for a decade, (3) a regulatory pathway that "has no clear FDA/MHRA precedent class," and (4) a capital ramp the dossier itself benchmarks against Mammoth's $375M and Caribou's $300M IPO. That is four independent miracles, and you compound the probabilities the way NFX described in *Unusual Signs of Billion-Dollar Companies*.

On founder shipping -- FinalDose's strongest dimension -- the HIGH rung is **Perplexity** (2022), where Aravind shipped a thing we discussed a week later. The MID rung is **Brex** (2017), operationally disciplined but not weekly-iteration-fast. The LOW rung is the credentialed-promoter who lists ex-FAANG and ships nothing. FinalDose lands meaningfully above the LOW rung: Jeff Liu actually did the Vivid Dx loop ($5M seed + $10M Pre-Series A, John Sperzel installed as CEO July 2025 per GlobeNewswire), Li-Yao Huang compressed CRISPR hit-validation from months to 7 days at MRC Weatherall, Steven Lin built ML coevolution pipelines at Gutsee. That is real shipping evidence -- but biotech ship cycles are six-month wet-lab loops, not the between-meetings cadence that defined the **Perplexity** entry. So FinalDose sits between MID and HIGH on this axis, and it is the only axis where it does.

On market re-opening, the HIGH rung is **Anduril** (defense post-Ukraine, named 24-month window), MID is **Harvey** (legal AI post-ChatGPT), LOW is the **Lyft** Series C era of mature steady-state markets. FinalDose's dossier is explicit: "No explicit catalyst cited by company," with the [Inferred] catalyst being generic maturation of structure prediction and CRISPR-derived programmable proteins. Cancer therapeutics at $232.2B in 2026 is not a re-opening; it is the textbook steady-state TAM-math pitch I screen out in the first paragraph. Sits at LOW. On revenue velocity, the HIGH rung is **Harvey** at $3B?$5B?$8B in months (TechCrunch, Nov 2025); LOW is the AI company with no growth disclosed -- "something's fundamentally broken." FinalDose has no revenue and won't for years; that's stage-appropriate for biotech but the dimension itself does not reward stage forgiveness when the platform is supposed to "collapse R&D cycles to near 0."

On index position, the HIGH rung is **Stripe** (2010) as an index on e-commerce; MID is **Decagon** as workflow on a compounding category; LOW is **Lyft** fighting for share in a winner-take-all market. FinalDose claims index-like economics -- "same molecule, new guide, new drug" -- but a single therapeutic asset is not infrastructure in the workflow path. Pharma has acquired platform companies before and the dossier flags this directly: large pharma is organized around per-indication asset P&Ls, which means FinalDose either becomes a single-asset bet (not an index) or gets absorbed before it compounds. Between LOW and MID.

My 36-month forecast on FinalDose: dead or pivoted to a DNA-sensor diagnostic adjacency leveraging the Vivid Dx playbook Jeff Liu already ran, having burned $3-8M without IND-enabling tox data and without a named delivery partner. Mechanism: the four-miracle compound probability collides with a CRISPR-therapeutics funding environment where the public comps just executed 27-32% RIFs. Pivot to diagnostics is the rational move because Liu has already shipped one diagnostics company with Oxford Science Enterprises support, and DNA-sensing ? in vitro detection is a one-miracle reframe of the same molecule.

The strongest objection is **Color Genomics** (2015) -- I co-founded a bio company by isolating workflow on top of commodity hard-tech, so why not extend the exception to FinalDose? Because the exception's load-bearing word was "commodity": Color rode commodity sequencing chemistry and built data/workflow on top. FinalDose *is* the chemistry bet -- the engineered DNA-sensor protein is the novel hard-tech, not a layer on top of one. The **Mistral** (2023) exception also fails: that override required a post-ChatGPT category re-opening in a 24-month window, and the FinalDose dossier explicitly states no catalyst. The **Anduril** override fails because Trae Stephens and Palmer Luckey had each shipped at hundred-million-user scale; the FinalDose founders have shipped a $15M-raised diagnostics seed, which is real but is not the Palantir/Oculus tier the override required. **Verifiable flip condition**: peer-reviewed in vivo specificity data at single-base-pair resolution in a mammalian model, paired with a named delivery-partner term sheet -- if both surface in the next 12 months, I revisit. Until then, pass.
