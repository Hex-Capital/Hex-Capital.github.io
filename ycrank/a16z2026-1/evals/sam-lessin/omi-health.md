# Omi Health -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

"Function Health for Pets." That's the tagline. And right there, you've told me everything I need to know about the pricing problem. When your one-liner is literally "[existing company] for [adjacent market]," you are by definition not creating a new primitive — you're executing a known playbook in a new vertical. Omi Health is a DTC wellness subscription transplanted from humans to dogs. Every consumer-focused seed fund in America has "pet humanization" on a slide somewhere. Function Health raised $53M in 2024. The thesis that millennial and Gen Z pet parents will pay subscription prices for preventative diagnostics is consensus among investors who can spell "TAM." There is no mispricing here. The opportunity is real, but it's priced in.

Sindu Chaparala is clearly competent — HBS MBA, decade in human healthcare across CVS, Aetna, and MIT Longevity Lab, then Chief of Staff at Ritual where she helped scale to $250M+ ARR. That Ritual experience is precisely the credential you'd want for building a DTC health subscription brand. She's a sane person. But the idea isn't insane — it's the opposite. A pet health subscription with biomarker testing and an AI chat? Every HBS startup bootcamp has generated this concept, and in fact that's literally where Omi Health originated. My framework requires a specific ratio: sane founder, insane idea. Omi Health inverts it — sane founder, extremely sane idea. That's a bet on execution in a known category, which is fine, but it's not my kind of bet. I need to feel like most smart people would say "that's fucking crazy" and the founder can calmly explain why it's inevitable. Nobody thinks pet wellness subscriptions are crazy. They think it's obvious.

The AI kill test is where Omi Health falls apart for me. The 48+ biomarker blood panel? That's a vet lab partnership — any company can source that. The at-home saliva swab? Embark and Basepaws already do genetic testing at home. The 24/7 AI vet chat? That's an LLM with a veterinary prompt and a disclaimer that says "does not provide veterinary medical advice." Zoetis is a $90 billion company that already acquired Basepaws in 2022. Mars Petcare owns Banfield and VCA with thousands of vet clinics. Embark has raised $91M and has an established canine diagnostics brand. Any of these incumbents could launch a competing subscription with existing distribution in 12 months. The dossier's own defensibility section is honest — the data moat "does not exist today at 200+ users," the switching costs are "modest at early stage," and the AI model advantage is "speculative at current scale." Seven App Store ratings. Seven. The projected "$1M estimated ARR" from 2,000 waitlist signups is a fantasy until those signups convert to paid memberships, and there's zero evidence they have.

The bull case I'd need to believe: Sindu's Ritual playbook transfers perfectly to pet health, the vet industry's structural incentive to keep pets sick (Mars/Banfield makes money from treatment, not prevention) creates a real business model conflict that slows incumbent response, and the longitudinal data from thousands of annual pet health tests becomes a proprietary dataset no one else can replicate. If Omi Health can get to 50,000 dogs with multi-year biomarker histories, that dataset genuinely becomes valuable for breed-specific health prediction. And 2,000 waitlist signups in three weeks from a March 18 launch does show consumer pull. But compare this to Metropolis — my seed bet in AI-powered parking that's now worth ~$5B. Metropolis has physical parking infrastructure that no LLM can replicate. Omi Health has lab partnerships, an app, and a subscription billing system. The moat differential is structural, not just a matter of execution speed.

Jakob Spiess brings a second HBS MBA and a prior startup (SeaCycle, chitosan from seafood waste), plus military operational experience — solid COO profile. But the co-founders met at business school. My best returns come from founders I've watched across multiple ventures, people with years of shared history and proven trust under pressure. A business school co-founding relationship doesn't pattern-match to the deep relational signal I look for — it's closer to the "matched by an incubator" red flag than the Kortina/Magdon-Ismail dynamic at Venmo. The team is capable, the domain fit is real, but there's nothing here that makes me think the risk-reward is asymmetric in my favor.

Consensus Mispricing and Structural Durability are the two dimensions that lock this as a Pass. Pet wellness is a consensus-warm category where the upside is already in the price, and named incumbents with 100x Omi Health's resources (Zoetis at $90B, Embark at $91M raised, Mars Petcare) can replicate every component of the product. Sindu Chaparala is the right founder to build a DTC pet health brand — but "right founder for a consensus idea with no structural moat" is a bet on execution at a fair price, not a mispriced contrarian opportunity. Pass.
