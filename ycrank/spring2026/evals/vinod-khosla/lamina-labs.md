# Lamina Labs -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Lamina Labs is two MIT kids -- Kartikesh Mishra and Sudip Rokaya -- pointing a deterministic animation engine at whiteboard explainer videos for course creators, and the signature that matters is the inversion of my framework: highest dimension is founder pedigree (CSAIL under Daniela Rus, MIT EECS BS'24/MEng'25), lowest is consequence magnitude (an 8-to-11-second whiteboard video versus Manim). That inversion is the exact silhouette of the pass example in my own playbook -- credentialed founders, a feature-shaped success-state, no civilizational variable moving. Lamina Labs does not name an incumbent regime that collapses if Simi wins; it names After Effects and Manim, which is a 30% productivity argument, not a $15T labor-displacement argument.

On consequence magnitude, the HIGH rung is **OpenAI** (2019), where the success-state was civilizational redirection of cognition itself and I wrote $50M against no revenue because the world-state at maximum ambition was unrecognizable. Lamina Labs's maximum-ambition world-state is "course creators ship explainer videos faster," which sits next to my standing pass example of GPT-wrapped sales-call summarization -- the success-state is a workflow upgrade inside one persona, not the disappearance of a category. The dossier reaches for "visual infrastructure for EdTech AI applications" in the YC tagline, but there are zero API customers, zero integrations disclosed, and Simi is shipping as a vertical app at app.laminalabs.ai/simi. When I asked the consequence question -- what economic mechanism breaks if Lamina Labs wins -- the answer is "Adobe After Effects loses a long-tail seat segment," and that is not a mechanism I underwrite.

On technical/capital risk that repels consensus VCs, the LOW rung's failure mechanism is the GPT-wrapper pattern: software-only execution risk, $5-10M to $1M ARR, fundable by any Tier-1 generalist, no miracle required. Lamina Labs's stated moat is "deterministic" frame-accurate animation -- a software claim, not a physics claim, and Golpo AI just raised a $4.1M seed in October 2025 from BNVT, Emergence, YC, and Afore targeting the same education-and-training buyer with more capital. There is no catalyst yield to prove, no fusion magnet to build, no foundation model to train. Lamina Labs is not capital-hostile science; it is capital-trivial software where the next round of latency improvements from a competitor erases the wedge. This is precisely the deal where I am over-resourced and add no edge.

Founder-defies-expert-consensus is where the mechanism analysis matters: over 18 months, the path that compounds is a founder publicly contradicting an entrenched field -- Levine on robot foundation models, the Commonwealth team on tokamak timelines. Lamina Labs's public posture is Rokaya's LinkedIn post "Leaving MIT to build the next generation?" and "You don't need a $2M pre-seed to start building," which is enthusiasm, not heterodoxy. Mishra's CSAIL work under Daniela Rus on language-conditioned drone control is credentialed continuity, not field defiance. Nothing in the Lamina Labs dossier shows either founder taking a controversial public position the EdTech video field rejects -- the absence is the signal.

Counterfactually on learning rate: to flip Lamina Labs a tier on this dimension, the dossier would need a documented prior pivot of the **Slash** (2024) shape -- a public thesis change with stated reasoning, the kind of Kanye-moment that justified my $100M Series C into a YC fintech that otherwise violates my consequence-magnitude prior. Lamina Labs has Mishra's Google PM Hackathon finalist note and Rokaya's MIT leave, but no pivot, no "we were wrong about X," no prior company sold. The Spring 2026 batch is too early to penalize aggressively, so I apply the default rather than the floor -- but I do not get to apply the Slash exception either, because the Slash exception requires the pivot evidence to exist, not to be plausible.

Lamina Labs against **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) on capital profile is the cleanest single-anchor parallel: I led $115M into Commonwealth because the roadmap to first plasma required capital depth no generalist would touch, and the deployment was legible -- magnets, vacuum vessel, plant. Lamina Labs's capital story is a free trial with no published pricing and a Cal.com link to book the founders. The Khosla check sizes that defined the last decade -- $50M into OpenAI, $115M into Commonwealth, $105M co-led into Genesis AI -- are inert here; a $500K pre-seed is the entire deployment surface, and the SaaS economics mean my dollars displace no risk no one else will absorb.

The 36-month forecast: Lamina Labs is either a sub-$30M talent-acquihire into a Canva, Coursera, or Khan Academy by Q2 2029, or it pivots into a courseware-API serving one larger EdTech platform with ARR plateauing under $2M as Golpo AI's better-funded $4.1M seed and Synthesia's $150M-plus war chest compress pricing on the explainer-video category. The mechanism producing this is straightforward: the deterministic-rendering speed wedge is a six-month moat in a category where Golpo 2.0 already shipped, and two MIT undergrads cannot simultaneously hold an infrastructure roadmap and a vertical-app roadmap against a competitor with 8x the capital.

The strongest opposing case is the **Slash** (2024) exception: YC pedigree, Pete Koomen as YC partner, an MIT-credentialed team, and the possibility that what looks like a thin wedge today becomes the EdTech-video infrastructure layer the way Slash became a real consumer-business bank after its pivot. That case stings because I have funded YC companies that violated my consequence-magnitude prior before. It loses on the dossier: Slash earned the override through a documented pivot demonstrating learning rate; Lamina Labs has no pivot, no thesis update, no founder-defiance moment, and the consequence-state is "faster Manim" rather than "displaces a category." One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a signed LOI from a named Fortune-500 corporate-training or K-12 district counterparty at >$500K ACV explicitly displacing internal video production budget -- a labor-cost displacement anchor, not a tool-substitution anchor. Absent that, Lamina Labs scores where my framework says it scores, and I pass in 30 seconds.
