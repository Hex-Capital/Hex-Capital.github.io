# Inth -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Inth's signature is shipped-OSS-distribution paired with silent-revenue -- Christopher Burns has c15t installed at Vercel, Expo, Zed, Infisical, and Unkey with 2.6M npm downloads and 6.5K/day, but the dossier carries no ARR, no paying-customer count, and an "Inth DSAR" platform still flagged as "in development." That pairing is the analytical fact. This is a developer-tools founder with a real installed base trying to wedge into a privacy-ops category sold to legal buyers, not the screaming-growth AI-revenue profile I'm normally underwriting in 2026.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on founder execution. Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and would show up a week later with the thing we'd discussed, built -- that's the shipping-rhythm signal I underwrite, not pedigree. Burns has the shipped-artifact half of that pattern in legible form: c15t, 1,691 GitHub stars, 141 forks, named adopters in the devtools elite, plus a prior at Everfund running multi-tenant GDPR-regulated donation infrastructure across 33+ countries. Where Inth diverges from the Perplexity rung is cadence-under-observation -- I have evidence Burns *has* shipped, not evidence he ships between *my* meetings, and the rebrand from Consent.io to Inth in October 2025 plus a DSAR product still in alpha says the second act is mid-execution rather than already-compounding. That puts Inth credibly above Brex-tier operational-shipping on this axis but short of the Perplexity rung.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure pattern on revenue velocity I want to test against -- I passed because the dossier showed a real product without a credible curve to share-inversion, and the framework was correct even though the outcome stung. The Lyft pattern in Inth's dossier is the silence: a pricing page at inth.com/pricing whose tiers I cannot retrieve, zero disclosed ARR, zero paying logos named, and the managed DSAR platform "in development." Tim Ferriss Show #863 (April 2026): "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." Inth is privacy infra, not pure AI, so I'll soften the rule -- but 2.6M cumulative npm downloads is a *usage* curve on a free Apache-2.0 SDK, not a *revenue* curve, and the absence of a single named paying customer in a YC dossier that otherwise enumerates Vercel and Expo as adopters is itself information. That's the "false signal vs. just working" distinction from my TechCrunch piece (Nov 2025), and Inth currently sits on the false-signal side until a paid SKU lands.

The market catalyst Inth cites -- "AI agents writing code that moves user data" plus the EU AI Act of May 2024 -- is the right shape of argument but appears as `[Inferred]:` in the dossier, not as a named regulator action or a named incumbent failure in the last 24 months. **Anduril** (2022) had Ukraine; **Coinbase** (2013) had post-skepticism institutional curiosity. Inth has GDPR (2018) and an agent-code-gen narrative that is real but not yet load-bearing in privacy-buyer budgets. Over 18 months the catalyst either compounds -- DPAs start citing AI-generated code lineage in enforcement actions, which would re-open the category the way Ukraine re-opened defense -- or it stays a deck slide, and Inth is selling into a steady-state CMP/DSAR market against OneTrust ($1.144B raised), Transcend ($90M raised, $40M Series B May 2024 from StepStone), DataGrail ($84.2M), and Privado ($17.5M with the closest direct overlap on repo-scanning). "Market first and the strength of the team second" (*First Round Review*) -- the market here is partially catalyzed, not fully re-opened.

For the index-position dimension, the counterfactual is the cleanest way to think about Inth. To flip from MID to HIGH here Inth would need to be the consent/lineage runtime that every AI-coding-agent integration eventually loads -- the way Stripe became the rail every e-commerce app touched. The plausible mechanism exists: c15t is headless, tree-shakable, 89ms overhead, and already installed in production at Vercel and Expo. The blocker is Apache 2.0 plus the existing shadcn/c15t fork, which means any of Ketch, Transcend, or Privado can embed the SDK without paying and ship a competing managed layer above it. Inth has *distribution* in the workflow path; it does not yet have *capture* of the workflow path. That's a MID-rung position, not a Stripe one.

Single-miracle test, applied directly: Inth needs (1) OSS-installed developers to convert into paying legal/privacy buyers despite the buyer-user gap that has killed multiple OSS-to-enterprise plays, (2) the repo-scanning evidence-generation product to ship and out-execute Privado's existing dynamic data maps, and (3) the AI-agent-code-gen catalyst to translate into enforcement budget within the funding runway. That's the compounding-small-odds geometry I flagged in NFX *Unusual Signs* and used to stay out of multi-breakthrough deals. Compare to **Color Genomics** (2015), where I isolated the miracle to workflow on commodity sequencing -- one validated layer, GTM execution as the rest. Inth has not yet isolated its miracle to one layer.

My 36-month forecast: Inth is acquired by Transcend, DataGrail, or Vercel for $25M-$80M as a consent-SDK + lineage-scanning component layer, with c15t continuing as OSS post-acquisition and the standalone Inth managed platform never reaching $5M ARR independently. The mechanism is the buyer-user gap -- developers adopt c15t, but DSAR/lineage budgets sit with legal/privacy ops who already have OneTrust/Transcend seats, and Privado's $17.5M head start on dynamic data maps makes the repo-scanning layer a feature acquisition rather than a category win.

The strongest bull case against that forecast is the **Decagon** (2023) parallel -- workflow-infrastructure-in-a-newly-open-category, bottom-up developer install becoming the wedge into a legacy-CMP-displacement cycle as AI agents force auditable lineage from a nice-to-have into a board-level requirement. Burns has the shipping artifact (c15t at Vercel/Expo/Zed) that Decagon-stage founders need, and the rebrand to Inth plus the rebuild around repo-resident scanning is exactly the *AI Market Clarity* "compliance" sub-bucket I named as still open (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025). If c15t's 2.6M downloads convert at even 0.5% to $20K ACV the company clears $25M ARR fast. What dismantles the bull case is the Apache 2.0 + shadcn/c15t fork pairing -- the OSS layer is not the moat, it's the wedge, and a single-founder team with a Head of Growth (Will De Ath) but no second technical cofounder is the wrong shape to out-build Privado on lineage *and* out-sell Transcend on DSAR simultaneously. Flip condition that would move me to Invest: Inth publishes ?10 named enterprise paying logos at ?$50K ACV with month-over-month logo growth disclosed, within 12 months of YC Demo Day. Absent that, this is Neutral -- a real founder in a partially-catalyzed market without the revenue curve that would make me pay up.
