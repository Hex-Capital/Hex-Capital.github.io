# PerfectBit -- Paul Graham Evaluation

PerfectBit is the rare pre-seed where the founders sat on the buyer's side of the exact problem they're now selling into -- Peter Vajda ran Media Generation at Meta Superintelligence Lab and Seiji Yamamoto worked Core Llama pre- and post-training -- but the dossier contains zero pre-funding artifact, zero stunt, zero evidence either of them shipped a thing into the world before the YC check landed. Strong organic-pain signal paired with a missing animal signal is the analytical signature here, and it is the exact signature that produces good-not-great outcomes in my book.

Drew Houston shipped a demo video to Hacker News before the **Dropbox** (2007) seed check; Brian Armstrong rebuilt his rejected **Coinbase** (2012) application as a working Android wallet. That is what the animal-resourcefulness rung looks like at the top. PerfectBit's dossier names a Twitter handle @PerfectbitAI and a LinkedIn page and tells me nothing about what Vajda or Yamamoto built between leaving Meta and arriving in Garry Tan's batch -- no GitHub repo, no demo dataset published, no "we generated 10K verifier-grounded examples last weekend and here's the eval lift" anecdote. Two senior Meta directors walking into YC with the credential and the network is not the same thing as two animals walking in with cereal boxes. Absence of tracks gets the absent-evidence default, lightly nudged because the credentials are real.

The **Dropbox** (2007) USB-stick pattern -- founder hits problem in own workflow, builds tool for self -- is what I'm testing PerfectBit's organic-pain claim against, and it actually loads. Yamamoto was running post-training on Llama; Vajda was generating training media at scale. They were the customer for Scale AI and Surge AI inside Meta. They felt the data quality ceiling that the perfectbit.ai copy describes as "progress in model performance is gated on high-quality data." This is not a market-opportunity pitch; this is two people who suffered the schlep on the buyer side and left to sell a different shape of it. The dossier doesn't show me they built a prototype for themselves at Meta, which is the rung above, but founder-problem distance is short. This is the dimension PerfectBit is strongest on.

**Coinbase** (2012) made the regulatory grind the moat -- nobody else in Armstrong's batch wanted to file money-transmitter paperwork in fifty states. PerfectBit's claimed schlep is gluing "formal proof systems, simulators, executable tests, and oracle databases" into one data pipeline, which is real engineering schlep and not the kind a one-weekend-hackathon team will replicate. But the dossier flags FOVER (arXiv 2505.15960) and RLVR (arXiv 2506.15522) as 2025 academic methods already published in the open -- meaning the labs PerfectBit is selling to can build it in-house with a quarter of headcount. Schlep that incumbents are publicly solving is not Coinbase-grade schlep. It is medium schlep with a closing window.

Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp work before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz co-authored RSS before **Reddit** (2005). That is hacker genealogy. PerfectBit gives me PhD Physics from Rice with PNAS and Physical Review Letters publications, and PhD CS from EPFL with a Stanford visiting professorship -- credentialed-technical, indisputably. But the dossier explicitly notes "No public repos found" for both Vajda and Yamamoto. Academic researchers and Meta directors are not the same population as the HN-poster, open-source-shipping hackers I over-index on. Counterfactual: one named open-source project from either founder -- a published verifier-grounded eval harness on GitHub with 200 stars -- would move this dimension two tiers.

The two-cofounder pair worked together at Meta Superintelligence Labs / AGI Foundations on the same frontier-model program, which is the long-relationship pattern that **Reddit** (2005)'s dorm-roommate setup gave me. PerfectBit clears the cofounder bar cleanly. Not the **Airbnb** (2009) trio, but a pair with shared trench time, complementary coverage (Vajda on generative media, Yamamoto on Llama pre/post training), and an obvious split of who-builds-what.

My 36-month forecast on PerfectBit: lands one or two frontier-lab contracts on warm Meta network access in the next twelve months, hits $5-15M ARR by month 24, and gets acquired by a frontier lab -- most likely Anthropic, OpenAI, or Meta itself -- in the $200-500M range by month 36 as labs internalize verifier-based generation. Not a zero, not a Stripe. The mechanism producing this is the customer-concentration risk the dossier already names: 5-10 buyers means the most likely exit is one of those buyers swallowing PerfectBit rather than PerfectBit growing into an independent data layer. This is the **Kiko** (2005)-shaped outcome dressed in better credentials.

The strongest bull case against my forecast: frontier-lab buyers respond to credentials, and Vajda walking into OpenAI with one warm intro from his Meta network closes a $20M annual contract that no two-MBA team could close in a year of cold outbound. If PerfectBit signs Anthropic on a multi-year exclusive for physics-verified data and the verifier library compounds into a domain-specific moat the labs cannot rebuild fast enough, this is **Stripe** (2010)-shaped, not **Kiko** (2005)-shaped. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull case is the published FOVER and RLVR methods -- labs with $14.3B Meta checks (Scale AI, June 2025) absolutely will rebuild in-house when the recipe is in arXiv. A signed, named contract with OpenAI or Anthropic disclosed in the next six months would flip me to Invest at this round; until then, PerfectBit is a credentialed pass-adjacent neutral.
