# Cova -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Cova is a Michigan-licensed home care agency claiming "the entire operation runs on AI agents," and that single sentence in the YC page tells me Cova is a labor-leverage business wearing code-leverage costume. The signature is a top-quartile market ($61.48B US home healthcare per Towards Healthcare 2026) wrapped around a bottom-quartile distribution architecture (state agency license, Medicaid Home Help reimbursement at $12.25/hour, phone intake at 734-707-7330). That pairing is not what I fund. **Stack Overflow** (2008) and **Notion** (2013) both started in big markets too, but the leverage was code from line one; Cova's leverage is a state license and W-2 caregivers, with AI bolted onto the back office.

The leverage check is where this ends for me. **Twitter** (2007) sits at the top rung of permissionless media -- anyone posts, zero marginal cost, no gatekeeper -- and that architecture is what underwrote the 400x. Cova's distribution requires three permissions before a single caregiver is dispatched: a Michigan state agency license, a Medicaid Home Help authorization, and a payer claim approval per episode of care. That is the *exact inversion* of "code and media are permissionless leverage" (Naval on X, May 2018). The AI agents handling intake and billing don't change the architecture; they're labor substitution inside a permissioned channel, which is capital leverage in disguise. The dossier names no GitHub repo, no developer pull, no open-source flywheel -- Tandon's personal site lists no public repos. When Cova wants to enter Ohio, it doesn't push to npm; it files paperwork with another state's licensing board.

The "ex-FAANG PM identified market opportunity" red flag is the failure mode I want to test against, and Cova's founder profile maps onto it more cleanly than I'd like. Ansh Tandon is Purdue CS, ex-Apple AI, ex-Tesla vehicle software -- credible engineering pedigree, zero documented healthcare operations or Medicaid billing experience. Jordan Ibe's identity isn't even confirmed in the dossier (Cornell CS+Stats is a *maybe* match). Compare to **Stack Overflow** (2008), where Atwood and Spolsky had been writing for working developers for years before shipping; the specific knowledge predated the company. Cova's founders discovered home care as a *market*, not as users -- there is no lived experience of being a family caregiver in Michigan, no prior shipped artifact in Medicaid claims automation, no public writing on long-term care. That's the silhouette I've seen pass and miss many times, and the misses dominate.

The network dimension fails on a mechanism, not a count. A two-sided marketplace like **Uber** (2010) compounds because the Nth driver in San Francisco makes the (N-1) riders more valuable -- supply density lowers wait times globally inside the geography. Cova has caregivers on one side and clients on the other, but the unit of compounding is a *match*, not a network -- the 50th caregiver in Detroit does not make the 49th client's care better, because care is delivered one-to-one inside a home. Over 18 months this dimension does not compound; it scales linearly with hires and licenses. That is staffing-agency economics, and staffing agencies do not deliver 10,000x.

On pivot room, the counterfactual is the cleanest test: what would have to be true for Cova to have **Twitter**-style maneuvering space? Cova would need to be able to redirect the same team, the same code, and the same customer base into an adjacent product without re-licensing. It cannot. The Michigan home care agency license is the asset, and that asset is non-portable -- Medicaid Home Help in Michigan does not become Medicaid Waiver in Ohio without re-credentialing. The pivot path the dossier hints at -- becoming a software vendor to other agencies, i.e., becoming **Sage Care** or **MochaCare** (YC W26) -- is real, but that pivot abandons the moat and lands Cova in a knife-fight inside the YC batch itself.

The Buffett trio is mostly silence here, which is neither bullish nor bearish -- Tandon's Apple and Tesla tenures are clean engineering roles with no contested-exit signal, and Ibe is too unconfirmed to score either direction. The single anchor is **Notion** (2013): Ivan Zhao was someone I wanted monthly contact with for a decade because the depth of his thinking on tools-for-thought was visible in conversation one. Nothing in the Cova dossier gives me that read on Tandon or Ibe -- not yet, and the absence of any public writing, podcast appearance, or domain essay from either founder leaves the 10-year-partner test in the default-neutral zone.

Cova at 36 months: still Michigan-only, somewhere between $1M and $4M in reimbursement revenue, having either failed to obtain a second-state license at economic scale or pivoted into selling its agent stack to other agencies and competing directly with Sage Care and MochaCare (YC W26) -- with a small-multiple acqui-hire by Honor or a regional incumbent as the most likely terminal outcome. The mechanism is that the AI-cost-advantage claim ("highest base rate in the state") meets Medicaid rate compression and audit overhead, the unit economics tighten rather than loosen with scale, and the state-by-state expansion playbook proves too capital-intensive for a pre-seed team of two to outrun software competitors who never needed a license.

The strongest objection is the **Anchorage Digital** exception -- I waived the permissionless-leverage criterion when regulatory friction itself became the moat in institutional crypto custody. Could Cova be Anchorage for home care, where the Michigan license plus AI cost structure is permission-*as*-moat? It is not, and I want to be precise about why. Anchorage worked because I had been dollar-cost-averaging into pre-consensus crypto since **MetaStable** (2014) and the category was years before its narrative; home care is the structural opposite -- a mature, mass-market services category where incumbents like Honor have already raised ~$625M and reached $1.25B (TechCrunch Oct 2021) and the AI-for-agencies wedge has at least six YC entrants (Sage Care, MochaCare, Clara, Oma Care, Kaigo Health, Helpcare AI). The exception requires category timing I do not have. **What would flip me**: Tandon or Ibe publicly shipping an open-source Medicaid-claims agent framework with documented organic adoption by a dozen non-Cova agencies within twelve months -- that would convert the business from permissioned services into permissionless code leverage with a services beachhead, which is the **Anchorage** silhouette redrawn for this category. Until then, this is a pass.
