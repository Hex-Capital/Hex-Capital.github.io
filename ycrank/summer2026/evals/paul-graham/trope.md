# Trope -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Trope is a company where the schlep is unusually real and the founders' relationship to that schlep is unusually thin — two UBC undergrads, Matthew Chow and Victor Vannara, walking into SAP ABAP fit-gap workshops and NetSuite cutover plans they have never personally suffered. That pairing — high schlep, low organic-idea fit — is the whole diagnostic. It is not the profile of a company where I lose money on soft founders; it is the profile of a company where competent hackers picked an unpleasant market from the outside because it looked underserved on a slide.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the ladder rung Trope is reaching for. Armstrong's moat became the compliance grind nobody in his batch wanted to touch — the schlep itself was the asset. Trope is claiming an analogous grind: agentic edits across SAP configurations, NetSuite SuiteScript, D365 AL, plus the SOW/cutover/training-guide paperwork that makes ERP consulting the most-hated corner of enterprise software. The 70% ERP-implementation failure rate on the trope.ai homepage is the kind of statistic that scares off software-only YC applicants, which is exactly why I like the zone. Where Trope diverges from the Coinbase rung is that Armstrong lived in bitcoin before he built a wallet; Chow shipped Cybertruck firmware and Zipline autonomy code, and neither co-founder has one documented hour inside a Deloitte ERP engagement. The schlep is real. The founders are visitors.

**Dropbox** (2007) is the anti-rung on organic-idea fit — Houston forgot his USB stick on a bus, went home, and started building the thing he wanted for himself. When I look at Trope for that same tell, it is not there. No line in the dossier says either founder cleaned up a botched SAP go-live, no story about a fit-gap document they hand-wrote at 2 a.m., no mention of billing partners who bled margin on a stalled implementation. The pattern I watch for — founder biography producing product — is instead: Tesla Cybertruck software, Zipline autonomous delivery, Cansbridge Fellowship, then a pivot into ERP because 70% of them fail. That is the "spotted a market opportunity" opening from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012), not the "few others realize it is worth doing" ending. Trope is attacking a schlep they read about.

Chow's Tesla Cybertruck and Zipline shipping is the one dossier fact that keeps the hacker-genealogy score above my absent-evidence default. Production autonomy code is a real animal-of-a-programmer signal; you cannot ship that without staying up nights. But there is no GitHub link for either founder in the dossier, and Vannara's public footprint is a Devpost portfolio — hackathon-shaped, not open-source-shaped. Hackers leave GitHub trails the way animals leave tracks in snow, and Trope's trail is faint. Over 18 months this compounds one of two ways: either Chow's Tesla-grade shipping cadence produces an agentic prototype that reliably rewrites ABAP without breaking prod (in which case the moat starts forming), or the team discovers that "agents that take action across documents, configurations, and code" is a materially different problem than autonomous vehicle software and the demos never cross the reliability threshold that enterprise buyers require.

Trope's animal-resourcefulness placement would flip a tier if the dossier surfaced one specific pre-funding artifact: a signed pilot with a named mid-market SI, a shipped Chrome extension that ingests SAP transport requests, revenue from a single implementation partner paying $2K/month before YC's check cleared. None of that is here. The homepage CTA is "Get a demo," there are zero open jobs, no Product Hunt launch, no press, no revenue disclosure — none of which I penalize as pre-seed defaults, but none of which I get to credit either. Compared to Armstrong hand-building an Android wallet to overturn his Coinbase rejection, Trope's dossier is quiet. Not damning. Quiet.

The **Stripe** (2010) parallel on co-founder configuration is the honest read: two people from the same school (UBC in Trope's case, the Collisons as brothers) with a real prior relationship, not a hackathon-weekend pairing. Trope clears my solo-founder red flag cleanly. It does not clear the higher bar the Collisons set — commerce hacking through Auctomatic before Stripe — because Chow and Vannara's shared history is undergraduate rather than shared-shipping. A pair is enough to pass; it is not enough to lean in.

My 36-month forecast on Trope: sub-$2M ARR, a pivot from "full lifecycle" implementation partner tooling to one specific vertical inside one ERP (most likely NetSuite migrations for mid-market SIs, because that is where LLM-editable artifacts are cleanest), and either a Tier-2 systems-integrator acquihire in the $10–30M range or quiet fade. Not zero. Not Airbnb. The mechanism is the signature: real schlep zone attacked by founders who have not lived the schlep, so the product gets built to a spec inferred from Mordor Intelligence reports rather than from the specific pain of watching a $14.45B ERP integration services market bill by the hour.

The strongest case against my forecast is the Airbnb-shaped exception: I mentally rejected Airbnb because the idea looked wrong, and the founders overrode my skepticism with cereal boxes. Trope's bull case says Chow's Cybertruck-and-Zipline shipping cadence *is* the animal signal, the ERP-vendor Copilot rollouts (Microsoft Finance Copilot GA October 2025) actually validate the "why now," and the partner-incentive misalignment I flagged as a risk resolves itself because SIs facing Copilot commoditization will license anything that lets them hold billings. The bear response wins on one dossier fact: not one line establishes that either founder has felt the pain of an ERP implementation from the inside, and every top outcome I have funded — Houston's USB stick, the Collisons' commerce hacking, Armstrong's compliance appetite — had that inside-pain signal before the check. Verifiable flip condition: a signed pilot with a named mid-market implementation partner (Protiviti, RSM, BDO Digital, or a specific NetSuite Alliance Partner) at contracted ARR before Demo Day would move Trope from Neutral into Invest. Absent that, pass.
