# PerfectBit -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

PerfectBit is a credentialed ex-Meta picks-and-shovels AI infrastructure play -- Peter Vajda (11 years at Meta running Emu/Movie Gen) and Seiji Yamamoto (9 years on Core Llama post-training, PhD physics with PNAS pubs) are exactly the founder pair you'd pre-write the deck for, attacking the exact sleeve I keep re-weighting away from. "Training data for frontier AI labs" with Garry Tan as group partner, two Meta research directors, and ten-to-twenty frontier-lab buyers as the entire TAM -- this is the canonical 2026 YC AI seed I described in my own pass example, just dressed up in a verifier-stack thesis instead of an agent shell.

Round dynamics are where PerfectBit dies for me. On the price-discipline ladder, the HIGH rung is **Solana** at $0.05/coin when the consensus crowd had quit crypto entirely; the MID rung is **Sublime Security**, a category two tier-1s had visibly passed on before I bought in; the LOW rung is the canonical "$30M post AI agent seed with four tier-1s already circling." PerfectBit sits between MID and LOW, closer to LOW -- the dossier doesn't disclose terms, but the silhouette (Garry Tan partner, two ex-Meta directors, AI infra into frontier labs in May 2026) is exactly the deck the entire batch is bidding up. There is no evidence of any tier-1 having passed, no evidence of strategic ambiguity, no evidence the round is illegible. Silence here is not neutral; silence here means consensus.

Category fit is the other floor. On the sleeve ladder, the HIGH rung is **Aleo** -- pre-consensus crypto with real on-chain economic activity at seed; the MID rung is **Allbirds** -- DTC consumer that Sand Hill dismissed as lifestyle but I underwrote anyway; the LOW rung is the generic foundation-model AI infra shell I described as "just more internet" on the Lonsdale podcast. PerfectBit sits below MID, basically on the LOW rung -- "verifier-grounded training data for LLMs, robotics, and AI for Science" is the literal definition of AI picks-and-shovels for foundation-model trainers. Open arXiv literature (EigenData, Jan 2026), an open GitHub list of RLVR resources, and the fact that Meta itself -- Vajda and Yamamoto's former employer and a candidate buyer -- already runs these pipelines internally tell me the technical scarcity isn't there. This is the anti-sleeve.

Founder conviction is the highest dimension and it's still mid-pack. The HIGH rung is **Pinterest**'s Silbermann, missionary temperament visible years before the deck; the MID rung is **Sublime Security**'s founders, operator-network-sourced with deep domain depth; the LOW rung is the sanitized strategic-consulting pitch with no public writing. PerfectBit's Vajda and Yamamoto are credentialed -- Emu/Movie Gen, Core Llama post-training, PhDs, Stanford ties -- but the founder-reach line in the dossier is brutal: no Twitter found, no public GitHub, LinkedIn follower counts not retrievable. Zero pre-funding artifacts of opinionated conviction on RLVR, verifier stacks, or why frontier labs should buy from outsiders rather than insource. Pedigree is not the same thing as missionary -- they pass the credentials filter and fail the "writing is thinking" filter.

Capital structure: ladder runs **Teamshares** (employee-equity rollup nobody else could underwrite) at HIGH, **Ro** (high-burn but regulatory-moat-justified) at MID, generic SaaS cap table at LOW. PerfectBit is between MID and LOW -- the underlying business is arguably bootstrappable (bespoke dataset-delivery to ten labs, high-margin, "selective about engagements"), but nothing in the dossier indicates the founders are pursuing anything other than a standard YC SAFE into a venture cap table they'll need to refill three more times. Founder-as-brand: ladder runs **Marina Mogilko** (5% of 30-year earnings, owned distribution) at HIGH, **John Fish / Bookshelved** (named creator with engaged community) at MID, paid-acquisition cold-outbound at LOW. PerfectBit is at LOW -- the GTM is Meta-alumni relationships into ten labs, which is network, not community, and there is no creator surface to underwrite.

Forecast, 36 months: PerfectBit lands two to four frontier-lab dataset contracts, prints $5-15M of one-time-ish revenue, and gets acqui-hired by Meta, Anthropic, or OpenAI for the verifier-engineering team at a $30-80M talent-shaped exit -- or insourced-out as the buyers fold RLVR pipelines into their own post-training stacks, at which point PerfectBit pivots toward AI-for-Science verifier tooling with a much smaller ceiling. The mechanism is straightforward: when your TAM is ten to twenty buyers who themselves can hire your team, the dominant termination case is being bought by the buyer rather than scaling against them.

The strongest bull case is the Sublime Security analog: Vajda's and Yamamoto's procurement relationships at Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind are a real wedge that Scale ($14B val) and Surge ($15-25B 2025 discussions) can't replicate at speed because their entire operational moat is human-contractor recruiting, not verifier engineering. If RLVR genuinely displaces RLHF over the next 24 months -- and the llm-stats.com 2026 snippet says it's already happening inside frontier labs -- then PerfectBit could be the named outsourced vendor for a regime shift that orphans Scale's $1.6B raised. The bear-case dismantle is that the regime shift is the exact reason buyers will insource rather than outsource, because verifier stacks are software that scales without contractor overhead, and the labs already have the talent (Vajda and Yamamoto walked out of one). The flip condition: if PerfectBit comes in below $15M post-money with no tier-1 lead and a named LOI from OpenAI, Anthropic, or xAI in the room, I'd take a second meeting. At consensus YC AI infra pricing, with two ex-Meta directors and Garry Tan as partner, this round is unbuyable for me on price discipline alone.
