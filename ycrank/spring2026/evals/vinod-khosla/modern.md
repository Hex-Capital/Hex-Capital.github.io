# Modern -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Modern is a competent repeat-founder team — Seb Poole, ex-Frankie Health, sold to Unmind February 2023 — running a contested AI-overlay play against an $11.91B ITSM market where ServiceNow already paid $2.1B for Moveworks. The signature is high founder learning rate stapled to near-zero technical-capital risk; in my framework that is the exact profile any Tier-1 generalist funds, and I am over-resourced for the deal.

**Cognition** (2024) is where I parked the AI-software-engineer thesis because the success-state collapses a named labor category — "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor," per Fortune 2026-03-06 — and the stack-depth was non-trivial. Modern wants to ride the same labor-displacement frame ("80%+ automation across IT team requests," YC page 2026), but Modern's success-state is "ServiceNow gets disrupted in ITSM" — a $36.78B-by-2032 software category, not a civilizational variable. Modern collapses a software vendor, not a labor market; the 62% ticket-handling reduction at an unnamed "Global IT team" is real productivity but it does not move a Fortune-2025-07-01-scale number. The consequence ceiling here is "$2-5B acquisition," not "redraw global IT labor."

**KiOR** (~2008) is the inverse of Modern on capital and science risk: catalyst yield, feedstock economics, refinery integration — three falsifiable miracles, $160M of deployment, a regime no consensus VC would touch. That failure mode does not appear in Modern's dossier; the opposite does. Modern is two people in San Francisco wrapping LLM agents around ServiceNow, Zendesk, Workday, Microsoft Graph integrations with SOC 2 Type 2 and ISO 27001 as the only listed moat. There is no physics, no biology, no plant, no fleet, no foundation-model training run. Sequoia and Redpoint are already inside Serval at $127M and $1B valuation; Khosla Ventures itself is already on the cap table of Atomicwork ($40.3M, Palo Alto). Modern is the third or fourth funded version of the same wedge — the dimension that would force a $50M concentrated check is conspicuously absent.

Seb Poole's pivot from workplace mental health (Frankie Health, Disney/Uber/MLB customers) to enterprise IT agents is real cross-domain motion and earns Modern most of its learning-rate score — over 18 months, that pivot compounds if Poole's enterprise-sales relationships convert into named-logo case studies the homepage currently lacks (testimonials read "Global IT team," "Series C healthtech," "Large retailer" — no logos). It compounds against Modern if Poole defaults to the Frankie playbook of mid-market design-partner sales while Serval and ServiceNow eat the Fortune 500.

For founder-defiance, Modern would need to score a tier higher only if Poole or Tomovski had publicly contradicted ITSM orthodoxy — a thesis post arguing the ticket itself is the wrong primitive, a 10-year horizon claim about IT-as-API. The dossier has none of this. Aleks Tomovski's Wix Code and Canva internal-developer-platform credentials are paradigmatic domain-expert continuity — the McKinsey-1986-cellphone-forecast profile my TED Talk warned against. "Stealth" on LinkedIn is not a defied-expert moment; it is a launch tactic.

**Cognition's** Series B was $175M because training and inference for autonomous coding agents is genuinely capital-deep. Modern's roadmap, by contrast, is YC-standard ~$500K to a $5-15M Series A — the math works on commodity SaaS economics. I do not need to be in this round; the dimension that pulls me into **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) at $115M lead — capital scale that repels consensus — runs in reverse here.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Modern reaches $3-12M ARR on mid-market design partners, gets acqui-hired by Atlassian, Freshworks, or a private-equity ITSM roll-up at sub-$250M — well below the Moveworks/ServiceNow $2.1B comp because Serval has already taken the venture-scale slot and ServiceNow's native AI agents commoditize the overlay before Modern reaches enterprise-logo escape velocity.

The strongest case against this pass is the **Slash** (2024) exception: YC-backed, repeat founder, documented pivot, consequence magnitude that did not clear my civilizational bar — and I still wrote a $100M Series C. Modern fits three of those four — YC Spring 2026, Poole's Frankie-to-Modern pivot, consequence-magnitude shortfall. The fourth is where Modern fails the analogy: Slash had a publicly told, narratively vivid pivot (the Kanye West moment) that demonstrated a learning event, not a job change, and consumer business banking had network effects Modern's enterprise IT motion does not. Modern's pivot is sectoral, not epistemic — Poole moved categories, he did not publicly update a thesis. I would flip to a yes if within nine months Modern publishes two named Fortune-500 logos with externally verifiable ARR figures and Poole posts a public thesis explaining why the ITSM ticket is the wrong primitive — absent both, Modern is a competent acqui-hire candidate, not a Khosla check.
