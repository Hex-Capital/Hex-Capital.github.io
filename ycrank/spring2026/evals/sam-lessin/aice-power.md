# AICE Power -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

AICE Power is a 120-degree pivot from energy sensors to submarine defense drones executed on May 16, 2026 by two French engineers — Grégoire Chomette (MIT AeroAstro, ex-NASA asteroid risk) and Rémi Bouteiller (ENS Paris-Saclay embedded systems, ex-Amira Health wearables) — and the signature here is a high-on-capital-intensive-defense-category paired with low-on-founder-conviction. AICE Power had six-figure ARR in the prior product and walked away from it to chase Anduril's wake, which tells me almost everything I need to know about which dimension dominates: this is opportunity-chasing dressed in the hot YC SP26 vertical, not missionary work.

The Ro analogy is the only Known Exception that loads here, and it's worth working through carefully because it's the bull case for AICE Power. Ro violated my capital-efficiency criterion entirely — heavy burn, multi-round dependency, regulatory schleps across 50 state pharma boards — and I overrode the rule because the regulatory schlep itself was the moat. Defense procurement to the U.S. Navy is structurally similar: ITAR, security clearances, multi-year DIU/SBIR cycles, cleared-personnel relationships. If AICE Power can clear those, the capital intensity becomes the structural barrier rather than the disqualifier. But Ro had Zachariah Reitano operating inside family-pharma rails for years before the company existed; AICE Power's dossier shows zero naval, subsea, or DoD-procurement experience between Chomette and Bouteiller, and the company manufactures in France while headquartered in San Francisco — an ITAR exposure surface they will discover the hard way. The Ro override doesn't transfer without domain operators on the cap table.

The "AI agent for enterprise vertical" pass example is the rung AICE Power most resembles on founder conviction, with the failure mechanism running like this: the founder responds to where capital is flowing rather than to a non-obvious belief, and the pitch could have been written by half the SP26 batch with "Anduril for swarms" swapped in. Defense drones in spring 2026 — after Anduril's $4B round at $60B in March and Saronic's $1.75B Series D at $9.25B in the same month — is exactly the consensus heat I deselect on reflex. AICE Power pivoted INTO this market two months after the consensus rounds closed. Chomette has no public writing on subsea autonomy, no Twitter account I can find, no GitHub presence on the category; Bouteiller's GitHub @rbouteiller has no notable repo on marine robotics. The 120-degree pivot off paying customers is the tell — missionary founders do not abandon six-figure ARR to chase a category that just had two unicorns minted.

On round pricing and consensus heat — mechanism analysis over 18 months — AICE Power sits inside a category where the procurement budget is being structurally captured by Anduril's $99M DIU Dive-XL contract and the $1.7B Royal Australian Navy Ghost Shark XL-AUV award, with Saronic eating the surface layer. The compounding works against AICE Power: every quarter that Anduril ships, the cleared-vendor list narrows, and a two-person pre-seed team with no naval pedigree is not on it. YC SP26 defense companies are pricing to the consensus heat, not the access reality. If AICE Power is raising standard YC terms in a category where the LPs of the funds upstream have already decided who the winners are, the entry price is mathematically wrong before I read the deck.

Counterfactual on capital structure: AICE Power would flip a tier if the financing were shaped to acknowledge the multi-year procurement cycle — a Teamshares-style holdco of defense-niche IP, or revenue-share against allied-navy contracts in France via the Direction Générale de l'Armement, or a manufacturing-partner JV with a French prime that absorbs the ITAR cost. AICE Power's dossier shows none of that. It's a standard SAFE on a category that needs $50M+ of patient capital before the first contract clears, which is precisely the founder-drainage failure mode the Slow letter inverts.

Single-anchor parallel on category sleeve: Sublime Security is my recent enterprise-security entry and the closest portfolio analog to AICE Power's defense framing, but the mechanism is opposite. Sublime was pre-consensus when most security investors were stuck in late-stage SOC SaaS; AICE Power is post-consensus in a vertical where the consensus checks were written in March 2026 — sixty days before YC even announced AICE Power's pivot. Defense hardware is also not in my revealed-preference sleeve: my crypto sleeve (Solana, Aleo, Klaytn, Lightning Labs) and DTC sleeve (Allbirds, Hipcamp, Everlane) are categories I underwrote when nobody else would. Subsea drones in mid-2026 is the inverse — everyone is underwriting it, and AICE Power has the worst seat at the table.

Forecast for AICE Power over 36 months: dead or pivoted a second time by mid-2029, having raised one bridge round on top of YC standard, with at most a sub-$1M SBIR Phase I award and no Navy program of record, while Anduril and Saronic absorb the distributed-maritime procurement budget. The mechanism is straightforward — defense procurement rewards cleared incumbents and operator-network sourcing, and AICE Power has neither; the six-figure ARR they abandoned was the only legibility translation I could story-broker to a Series A buyer, and they walked away from it.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass — meaning the bull case for AICE Power — is the Replicator-program tailwind: Pentagon procurement explicitly wants distributed, attritable, modular subsea autonomy rather than the Dive-XL monolith, and two MIT/ENS engineers shipping a swarm prototype fast is exactly what DIU rhetoric claims to want. The 41.24% naval/coastal share of the UUV market in 2026 (Globe Newswire May 2026) is real, and the subsea-cable sabotage incidents of 2023-2025 are real demand drivers. If AICE Power signs a $1M+ DIU contract within 12 months and brings on a cleared CEO with prior Navy program-office experience, the Ro exception activates and I revisit. Absent that single verifiable condition — DIU contract above $1M plus a domain operator added to the cap table by Spring 2027 — AICE Power is the SP26 defense-vertical analog of the canonical AI-agent pass, and I am out before the meeting.
