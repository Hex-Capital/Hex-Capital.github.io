# Gojiberry AI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is the unusual case of three repeat-exited founders -- Lallemant and Czerny out of CoCo AI (~$500K ARR, sold 2025), Teixeira out of Edusign (600K+ users, sold 2025) -- running hard at the most crowded AI-wrapper category in the 2026 batch. The dimensional signature is clear: high on animal resourcefulness, low on schlep. $112K MRR in nine months with 44% MoM growth is not a deck claim; that is the artifact. But the artifact lives in the AI-SDR aisle between Clay ($3.1B valuation, $100M ARR) and Apollo ($1.6B valuation, $150M ARR), where every wall is already painted.

**Airbnb** (2009) is the load-bearing analogy on resourcefulness, and the mechanism is the same one I keep re-extracting: founders who already converted hustle into revenue before the institutional check. Chesky's team shipped 1,000 cereal boxes at $40 each because nobody would fund them; the Gojiberry AI team shipped two prior companies to exit before Spring 2026 and then bootstrapped to $1.4M ARR annualized in nine months. Where Gojiberry AI diverges from the Airbnb rung is the absence of a single legible stunt -- no cereal box, no door-to-door photography of users, just compounding SaaS revenue and a third Product Hunt launch on Mar 2, 2026. That is animal behavior expressed as a P&L rather than as a story, which is rarer than it sounds but reads less viscerally.

The pass example I keep returning to -- the MBA team with the TAM slide and no shipped artifact -- failed because the work was pure software with no offline grind, no regulatory exposure, no unpleasant integration. Gojiberry AI does not fail that test the same way (the founders shipped), but the *category* fails it. Scraping 30+ LinkedIn signals and auto-generating outreach is exactly the work every other AI-SDR startup in the Salesforge/SyncGTM/DigitalApplied comparison grids is also doing -- Amplemarket, 11x, Regie.ai, Lemlist, Smartlead, plus the incumbents above them. The closest thing to a schlep in the Gojiberry AI dossier is LinkedIn platform-policy risk, the same exposure that ate Dux-Soup and Phantombuster, and that is a schlep being *taken on* unconsciously, not the **Coinbase** (2012) compliance grind being chosen as a moat.

On organic-idea fit: the Lallemant/Czerny pain at CoCo AI was WhatsApp marketing for e-commerce, and Teixeira's pain at Edusign was paperless attendance for schools -- neither founder lived inside the B2B SDR cold-outbound problem before building Gojiberry AI. The 18-month compounding question is whether the "performance compounds over time" data-loop claim becomes real cross-customer learning or stays marketing copy. If Gojiberry AI's outreach engine reaches enough volume that reply-rate optimization across 1,000+ customers produces a measurably better hit rate than Clay+Smartlead duct-taped together, the organic gap stops mattering; if not, this is a tools company built on a market read rather than founder pain, and Apollo's data advantage wins by 2027.

For Gojiberry AI to flip a tier on hacker genealogy, one specific thing would have to be true: at least one of the three founders would need a visible technical artifact -- an open-source repo, a Lisp-era hack, a competitive programming track, a Hacker News demo that predates the company. The dossier explicitly notes "No public repos found" for all three. Teixeira scaled Edusign to 600K users so the CTO is clearly not non-technical, but the hacker-tribal-marker signal that I over-index on -- the **Stripe** (2010) Collison Young Scientist win, the **Reddit** (2005) Swartz RSS spec -- is conspicuously absent. This stays mid-low until one of them ships something I can read.

Stripe is the single anchor for cofounder configuration: two brothers who had hacked commerce together at Auctomatic before Stripe. Gojiberry AI has two-of-three with prior shared shipping (Lallemant/Czerny at CoCo AI) and a third (Teixeira) joining from an unrelated exit. That is a stronger pairing than 80% of pre-seed teams I see, but it is not the Collison-brothers density and it is not the Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk roommate density. Trio of repeat founders, partial shared history, complementary CEO/CMO/CTO coverage -- this is a good team configuration that does not by itself trigger a check.

My 36-month forecast for Gojiberry AI: acquihire-coded exit to Apollo, Outreach, or Salesloft in the $25-70M range, with ARR plateauing somewhere between $4M and $12M as Clay and Apollo bolt intent-signal modules onto their existing distribution. The mechanism producing this forecast is the dimensional signature -- animals in a commodity category. Animals do not die at this scale; they sell. The **Kiko** (2005) tell -- the eBay sale -- is exactly what worries me, because Lallemant and Czerny just did the small-exit thing twelve months ago at CoCo AI, and the muscle memory for "sell at $X and move on" is fresh.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is that AI-SDR is genuinely consolidating and Gojiberry AI's 44% MoM growth implies it is one of the two or three consolidators, not one of the twenty that get rolled up -- and that the "performance compounds" loop, if real, is the kind of data moat that looks invisible at $1.4M ARR and dominant at $50M ARR. That case stings because Regie.ai just raised a $30M Series B at 300% YoY growth, validating the category is real. The dossier evidence that wins for me is the founder-pain gap plus the LinkedIn dependency: Gojiberry AI is selling a tool the founders did not personally wish someone would build for them, and the substrate (LinkedIn scraping) is owned by a platform that has historically banned its abusers. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: independent benchmark data, not founder-cited, showing Gojiberry AI's reply-rate optimization beats a Clay+Smartlead baseline by 2x or more across a cohort of 100+ accounts -- that would convert the "compounding performance" claim from marketing into moat. Until then, Gojiberry AI is a competent pass.
