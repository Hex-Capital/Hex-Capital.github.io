# Praxis AI -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Praxis AI is a labor-and-capital-leverage business dressed as an AI data company, embedded inside 60k workers across 150+ industrial environments belonging to publicly listed conglomerates that Rohan Seelamsetty's team does not own. The dimensional signature is stark: the big-market rung is real (robotics vendors spending >$100M/year each per Forbes Dec 2025, $55.8B robotics sector 2026 YTD), but the leverage architecture is the structural opposite of every check I've written that mattered. Twitter (2007) needed nobody's permission to post; Praxis AI needs permission from a conglomerate, from that conglomerate's workers, and from a frontier-lab buyer, in that order, before a single dollar moves.

Twitter is the HIGH rung on permissionless leverage -- media as protocol, zero marginal cost, no gatekeeper -- and the mechanism that made Twitter work was that adoption compounded without asking anyone for approval. Praxis AI diverges from that mechanism on every axis the dossier discloses: the "embedded within publicly listed and unicorn-scale conglomerates" line on the YC page is the tell, because the entire supply engine is gated by contracts with host companies whose names Praxis AI has not disclosed. This is labor leverage (60k workers) stacked on top of capital leverage (conglomerate balance sheets and facilities), which is precisely the two-leg leverage stack I wrote nav.al/product-media to argue against funding. Even Anchorage Digital, which I let through as a known exception, had category-timing conviction from MetaStable (2014) underwriting the permission-as-moat flip; Praxis AI has no comparable pre-consensus category call I've been dollar-cost-averaging into for a decade.

Perplexity is the LOW rung failure mechanism on the marketplace-network dimension -- single-sided, each user does not compound the previous user -- and I only waived that gate because criterion #1 (permissionless API distribution) and criterion #3 (Aravind Srinivas's Berkeley retrieval PhD) both cleared at top quartile. Praxis AI shows the same lack of compounding: adding the 61,000th warehouse worker to the capture fleet does not make the 60,000th worker's data more valuable to Physical Intelligence or Generalist AI, it just adds another hour of egocentric footage to a linearly-priced inventory. Data-supply pipes are not networks. The Perplexity waiver does not apply here because criterion #1 is not passing at top quartile -- it is failing at bottom quartile.

Tommy Li's dossier line -- ex-Morgan Stanley PE, ex-a16z industrial tech, IEEE-published at NASA JPL and PerceptIn Robotics -- is the one piece of specific knowledge in the Praxis AI founder set that survives my Almanack filter, and it's genuinely non-trivial: PerceptIn is a real robotics shop and JPL publication is not fungible with a McKinsey memo. But Rohan Seelamsetty's Oxford Apollo Fellowship and #1-on-India-debate-team reads as intelligence-plus-energy without a shipped artifact in physical AI, and Dev Karpe's Wharton Huntsman + F500 CEO's-office supply-chain + investment banking is exactly the pedigree stack I flag as absence-of-specific-knowledge in different vocabulary. Two-of-three founders passing the pedigree filter and one passing the specific-knowledge filter is not the Stack Overflow (2008) profile where Atwood and Spolsky were the users of their own product.

Praxis AI's market has genuine pivot-room over the next 18 months: from egocentric video capture to 3D scan services, to synthetic data generation off captured priors, to robot deployment services inside the same conglomerate footprints, to labeling. That is real optionality -- more than a single-vertical SaaS play would have. But the compounding mechanism on this axis fails because every pivot still routes through the same permissioned conglomerate contracts; Praxis AI cannot pivot away from asking a Fortune-500 chairman for access without abandoning the entire supply channel it pitches as its moat.

The founder-trio integrity read on Praxis AI is clean -- no contested exits, no self-promotion about honesty, no partner-litigation smell that would trip the Epinions (1999-2005) scar tissue. Counterfactual for a tier flip on this dimension: Rohan Seelamsetty would need three years of public writing on physical AI, robotics data economics, or humanoid training, evidencing that he discovered the problem as a user rather than as a market analyst. The dossier reports no GitHub, no technical writing, no shipped artifact -- which at pre-seed is stage-normal but leaves the specific-knowledge and 10-year-partner questions genuinely open.

36-month forecast: Praxis AI gets acquihired by Scale AI or Micro1 for its conglomerate relationship map in the $30-120M range, or dies quietly when Scale AI's Meta-backed $29B balance sheet signs direct data-supply deals with the same conglomerates Praxis AI is currently embedded in. The mechanism producing this is the "labor and capital require permission" rule -- when the moat is contract access to someone else's workforce, the party with the biggest checkbook wins the recontracting cycle, and Meta's $14.3B stake in Scale AI is a bigger checkbook than a YC S26 pre-seed will ever assemble.

The bull case that stings: Tommy Li's a16z industrial-tech Rolodex plus Rohan Seelamsetty's debate-champion closing ability could lock exclusive multi-year data-supply contracts with conglomerates that Scale AI's San Francisco lab culture cannot penetrate, and if the exclusivity clauses are real, a data-network effect compounds inside a walled garden that even $29B cannot outbid. The dismantling: exclusive multi-year contracts with 60k-worker conglomerates are exactly the kind of terms that Scale AI's enterprise sales motion is built to renegotiate, and the dossier discloses no named conglomerate partner, no contract length, no exclusivity clause -- meaning the moat is currently a claim, not a document. Verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed, disclosed, multi-year exclusive with a named Fortune-500 host that Scale AI can prove it tried and failed to close. Absent that document, Praxis AI is a permissioned-leverage business, and I do not fund permissioned leverage at angel stage.
