# Napkin Math -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Napkin Math is what happens when three competent MIT friends — Manuel Castro out of Sonos and Verkada, Claire Nord out of Apple and Samsara, Jynnie Tang out of Retool — pick a consumer category and try to iterate their way into specific knowledge they don't yet have. The signature is a clean five on the 10-year-partner test paired with a soft three on the specific-knowledge gate: a *trio-first, domain-second* company. That ordering is the inverse of how **Stack Overflow** (2008) or **Notion** (2013) reached me, and the inversion is the whole story of why my pen does not move.

Run the leverage check first, the way I always do. Napkin Math ships through the iOS App Store under Paper Star, Corp. with App Store ID 6758642182 — that is the **Anchorage Digital** end of the ladder, gatekeeper-permissioned distribution where Apple decides if you exist. The redeeming structural piece is "share your table with friends," which is genuine media leverage at zero marginal cost if the sharing artifact travels off-platform. **Twitter** (2007) is the HIGH rung here because the unit of content was the protocol; Napkin Math diverges because the unit of sharing is a meal photo wrapped in App Store chrome, which travels to friends already on iOS — a closed graph, not a permissionless one. The "Strava for food" framing names the ambition, but Strava itself only became media-leverage once the GPX file and the segment leaderboard left the app; Napkin Math has not yet shown me the equivalent shareable atom, and the App Store listing carries "not enough ratings to display."

The lowest rung on specific knowledge is the *ex-FAANG-PM-identified-an-opportunity* pattern that I flag explicitly in my red-flag list — pedigree-as-thesis without a shipped artifact in the domain. Napkin Math does not fully sit there because the trio has *shipped* — Tang took first place at the 2018 MIT Web Programming Competition, Nord has the 6-3 and an MEng — but the shipping was in consumer hardware tracking, dev tooling, and gaming UX, *not in food, nutrition, or behavioral health*. Aravind Srinivas brought a Berkeley retrieval PhD to **Perplexity** (2022); Ivan Zhao had been rebuilding personal productivity since adolescence before **Notion** (2013); Amjad Masad had been hacking on Repl.it since 2011 before the **Replit** (April 2023) round. Napkin Math's founders met at MIT a decade ago and chose food journaling as the wedge — the problem was selected, not lived. That is the "memo founder" silhouette, softened by genuine technical competence but not erased by it.

On marketplace mechanics, the compounding question is whether the Nth Napkin Math user makes user N-1 more valuable, and the answer over 18 months depends entirely on whether the social graph activates inside the meal-log loop. **Uber** (2010) had the driver-rider compounding wired in at the unit level; Napkin Math has it bolted on as a sharing feature inside a fundamentally single-sided personal-tracking product, which is the **Perplexity** shape without the API leverage. The path to network compounding exists — group challenges, friend feeds, shared "tables" as durable objects — but the dossier shows TestFlight invite-gated beta and zero disclosed cohort retention. The mechanic is hypothesized, not observed.

For pivot-room, the counterfactual that would flip my score is straightforward: if Napkin Math reframed from "anti-calorie food journal" to "consumer health social graph" with food as the wedge, the market opens from a single contested vertical into the **Postmates** (2011)-shaped consumer-behavior category where the first idea is allowed to die. The dossier already names the adjacencies — fitness, digital health, gamification tags on the YC page — and consumer health is platform-shaped, not vertical-shaped. What pins Napkin Math below the high anchor is that MyFitnessPal generated $310M in 2025 across 220M registered users and *just acquired Cal AI in March 2026*, which means the incumbent is now compressing the wedge from above with a 30M-MAU distribution funnel. There is pivot-room in the market; there is not pivot-room in the *current positioning*.

The trio test is where Napkin Math scores best by my framework. Castro, Nord, and Tang met at MIT roughly a decade ago and chose to start a company together after a decade of separate operator roles at credible shops — Apple, Sonos, Verkada, Retool, Samsara. That is the **Stack Overflow** (2008) Atwood-Spolsky pattern of *people who already know they can spend years in a room together*, which is the load-bearing piece of the 10-year-partner question. No litigation history surfaces, no self-promotion-about-ethics surfaces, no contested prior exits surface. The integrity leg of the Buffett trio reads clean — and that is exactly why this is hard. I have passed on clean-trio companies before; **Pinterest** is the canonical reminder that clean-trio plus media-leverage plus huge market is the *invest* configuration, and the lesson from that miss is that aesthetic discomfort is not a tiebreaker. Napkin Math is not Pinterest, because the specific-knowledge gate is meaningfully weaker and the distribution is App-Store-bound rather than web-native.

My 36-month forecast: Napkin Math gets acqui-hired into MyFitnessPal, Whoop, or Strava for $8-25M between mid-2027 and early-2029, with the social-sharing IP and the three engineers being the asset and the consumer brand being shut down. The mechanism is the same one Cal AI just lived through at $34-50M ARR: a differentiated AI food app in a category where MyFitnessPal owns the 14M-item verified database and the 220M-user funnel does not get to compound to escape velocity — it gets bought before the category settles, on terms favorable to the buyer and acceptable to YC.

The bull case that stings is the **Replit** (April 2023) analogy: Amjad Masad shipped a code environment into a category that already had Cloud9, CodeSandbox, and JSFiddle, and won by being *founder-product-market-fit* aligned with the AI cycle's arrival. If the Napkin Math trio's gaming/social UX background from Tang's One More Multiverse and the tracking-systems depth from Nord's Samsara compound into a genuinely novel social-food product surface, the "Strava for food" line is not a tagline — it is the architecture, and Strava is an $8B outcome. The reason this case does not move my pen is that **Replit** had Masad's specific knowledge dating to 2011 and a developer audience that adopts product-first; Napkin Math has consumer users acquired through an invite-gated iOS beta with zero disclosed retention, against an incumbent who *just bought the AI photo-logging differentiator three months ago*. The condition that would flip my verdict is a published 90-day cohort retention curve above 35% with K-factor above 1.0 on the "share your table" feature — that single piece of evidence would force me back to the dossier and re-run the leverage check from scratch.
