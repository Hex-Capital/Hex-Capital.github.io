## The Idea

- **Problem:** Australian GP clinic admin teams spend hours on manual document allocation, phone bookings, and billing, with customer Sonya (Yarrawonga Medical Clinic) citing 6 hours of reception work needed daily (caregp.com.au testimonials).
- **Approach:** Specialised AI agents named per workflow — Samantha reads/extracts/files clinical documents into practice management software; Veronica handles 24/7 phone and bookings; Bill handles billing (caregp.com.au; Startmate writeup).
- **Differentiation:** Heidi Health and Lyrebird Health are AI medical scribes for clinical consultations, whereas Care GP targets back-office admin (documents, phone, billing) rather than transcription (Medical Republic, 2026; aivy.com.au).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: SaaS subscription per clinic given B2B clinic deployment and "profitability" claim, but no pricing page is publicly disclosed (caregp.com.au).
- **TAM/SAM:** Australia has 7,800+ general practices and 39,449 GPs as of 2023 (AIHW/HWD; HotDoc, 2024); no public AUD TAM figure found for clinic admin automation specifically.
- **GTM / Distribution:** Listed partner of Best Practice Software practice management system (bestpracticesoftware.com partner page); also backed by Startmate accelerator (startmate.com).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** ISO27001 certification with onshore data encryption and integration into Australian practice management systems (caregp.com.au; bestpracticesoftware.com).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Deep integration with Best Practice and other PMS workflows plus accumulated clinic-specific document classification data could create switching costs once a clinic has Samantha mapped to its filing taxonomy.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Best Practice Software already ships its own AI admin agent and integrates Lyrebird natively, creating a channel-conflict risk rather than a structural moat for third parties (Medical Republic, 2026).
- **Commoditization risk:** Heidi Health (383 employees, ~$138M raised, ~$700M valuation) and Lyrebird Health are extending from scribing into admin/agents, and Best Practice has launched a native scribe/admin agent (Medical Republic, 2026).

## Key Risks

- **Channel conflict with Best Practice Software:** Care GP is a listed partner of Best Practice (bestpracticesoftware.com), but Best Practice has launched its own scribe and AI admin agent and integrates Lyrebird natively (Medical Republic, 2026), creating direct substitution risk from the platform Care GP relies on for distribution.
- **Adjacent competitor expansion:** Heidi Health (~$138M raised, ~$700M valuation, claims 50% GP share) and Lyrebird are extending from scribing into admin agents (Medical Republic, 2026), with capital and distribution advantages over Care GP.
- **Geographic concentration:** All disclosed customers and 7,000-clinic expansion plan are Australia-only (Globe and Mail, Oct 2025); regulatory and PMS integration work would need to be repeated per country.
- **Clinical document accuracy / safety:** Samantha routes clinical documents with "human oversight for exceptions" (Globe and Mail, Oct 2025); misallocation of pathology or referral documents in primary care has patient-safety implications — no public third-party audit of accuracy found.
- **Solo-founder execution risk:** Single named founder operating a 10-person team scaling toward 7,000-clinic national footprint (YC page; press release Oct 2025).
