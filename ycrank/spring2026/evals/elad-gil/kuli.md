# Kuli -- Elad Gil Evaluation

CreatorIQ generated $70.7M in revenue with $80.8M raised, serving 300 enterprise customers through a metadata-driven CRM architecture. GRIN sits at $145M in funding. Kuli's bet is that multimodal AI -- GPT-4V, Gemini, the whole stack that became commercially viable in 2023-2024 -- cracks open video-level content analysis that these incumbents can't deliver from their existing architectures. That's a real technology threshold crossing, and I've written checks on exactly this kind of structural enablement before. But I have to be honest about whether this timing is *nonobvious* or whether it's the most obvious application of multimodal AI that any product team at CreatorIQ is already building. When I backed Perplexity before ChatGPT launched, I was reading a capability curve that consensus hadn't internalized -- AI search was considered a dead category. Kuli's market structure is different: influencer marketing is growing at 37% CAGR (MarketsandMarkets), every brand team knows content analysis would be better than follower-count filtering, and the only barrier was the technology. Now that barrier is gone for *everyone*, including the $80.8M-funded incumbent with 300 enterprise accounts. The structural shift is real but consensus, and consensus timing dramatically reduces the alpha.

Kuli's infrastructure-layer positioning concerns me more than the timing. The company describes itself as "a single AI agent replacing multi-tool workflows" -- that's an application, not a platform. The "world's largest analysed social media content database without storing any video" (EPFL Investor Day 2024 profile) is a data asset, but it's not an API that other businesses build on, not a developer tool, not a compliance layer. Kuli sits squarely in the application tier, built on top of multimodal AI APIs from OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic. That's one of my explicit red flags: building an application on a contested infrastructure layer where the platform providers or well-resourced incumbents can replicate your capabilities. The dossier's own defensibility section acknowledges this -- "the defensibility lies more in the domain-specific application layer than in the raw video analysis capability." That's an honest assessment, but domain-specific application layers without switching-cost compounding or network effects don't generate the kind of returns I target. Stripe didn't just process payments -- it became the payments API. Kuli processes influencer content, and that processing layer is commoditizing in real time.

Disney, Supercell, and Havas as named users at pre-seed with a 3-person team -- that's genuinely remarkable traction, and I don't want to underweight it. The bull case writes itself: Michael Hodara managed $20M+ in annual marketing spend at Aircall and experienced this pain as a buyer, the classic innovator's dilemma applies because incumbents would need to rearchitect their metadata-driven stacks, and Kuli compresses campaign timelines by 4x (per their claim). If I'm wrong about the commoditization risk and Kuli's content index compounds into a proprietary advantage that incumbents can't replicate by bolting on API calls, this could be a strong outcome. The Microsoft GenAI Studio selection and VivaTech 2025 showcase on Microsoft's stand add credibility. I've seen cases where speed advantage in a structural shift creates enough of a data moat to hold off incumbents -- but those cases typically involve infrastructure-layer companies where the switching costs compound, not application-layer tools where the switching cost is "learn a new UI." One verifiable condition that would change my mind: if Kuli showed me that their content index creates measurably better creator matching over time (network-effect-like improvement) that can't be replicated by running the same multimodal APIs over the same public video content, that would shift the infrastructure test.

Kuli's single-miracle profile is its strongest dimension. The technology miracle -- multimodal AI capable of video analysis at enterprise-grade accuracy -- is happening exogenously and is already past the critical threshold. Kuli doesn't need to invent new AI capabilities; it needs to apply existing ones faster than incumbents can rearchitect. That's one miracle: execution speed. Hodara's EPFL robotics MSc combined with his hands-on experience managing influencer spend at Aircall gives Kuli technical credibility and domain specificity in a single founder. Hassan's 6 years of M&A advisory at KPMG provides enterprise sales DNA. The clear CEO/CRO split avoids my red flag about equal co-founder structures with no decision-maker. Neither founder has scaled a company through the 10-to-1,000 transition, but at pre-seed that's not a disqualifying gap -- I've seen great markets compensate for founder scaling inexperience.

Kuli's margin architecture is unproven -- no public revenue figures, custom enterprise pricing with no tiers disclosed. Enterprise SaaS positioning suggests reasonable gross margins, but the underlying cost structure depends on multimodal AI API calls that are currently expensive and pricing trajectories that Kuli doesn't control. The $14.2B TAM growing to $50.3B by 2028 is large enough for venture returns, and the clear payer (brand marketing teams with budget authority, not the EdTech problem of misaligned incentives) passes my basic market structure test. But Kuli is competing for wallet share against established platforms with deep integrations, not entering an underserved "boring infrastructure" category where the absence of prestige-seeking competition IS the advantage. This is a crowded, well-funded market where differentiation through AI features is the most predictable competitive vector of 2025-2026.

The decisive dimensions for Kuli are Nonobvious Market Timing and Infrastructure-Layer Positioning -- both score below my default thresholds. The technology enablement is real but consensus, and the product is an application layer on commoditizing AI infrastructure, not a platform that other businesses build on. Kuli's enterprise traction with Disney, Supercell, and Havas is genuinely strong pre-seed signal, and the single-miracle profile is favorable. But in my framework, strong execution in a consensus market on an application layer produces moderate outcomes, not the 10x-to-1000x returns that justify the risk. I'm passing, but I'd revisit if Kuli demonstrates that its content index creates compounding, non-replicable advantages -- evidence that it's becoming infrastructure rather than remaining an application.
