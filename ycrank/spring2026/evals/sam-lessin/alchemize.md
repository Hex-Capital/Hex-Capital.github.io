# Alchemize -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Alchemize is a regulated-transactional asset wearing a 2026 YC AI-vertical-SaaS costume -- Samuel Fu and Robert Hou are pitching "AI-native customs brokerage" into a category where Flexport shipped a 0.2% error-rate AI Customs Suite in October 2025 and KlearNow.AI is sitting on $69M of dry powder. The dimensional signature reads: the most interesting thing about Alchemize is the licensed-brokerage structural shape (customs broker license, Power-of-Attorney lock-in, sub-15% exam pass rate as a regulatory gate), and the least interesting thing is the sleeve itself, which is exactly the "AI is just more internet" zone I de-rate at seed pricing.

Round-pricing ladder: **Solana** at $0.05/coin in 2018-2019 is the HIGH rung, where I bought a category that two-tier-1 funds had publicly quit; **Sublime Security** is the MID rung, where I entered enterprise security before the next consolidation wave but at a defensible-not-cheap entry; the canonical 2026 YC pitch ("AI agent for [vertical], $30M post, four tier-1s in") is the LOW rung. Alchemize sits between MID and LOW, closer to LOW, because "AI-Native Customs Brokerage" reads as the literal template of the Spring 2026 YC batch -- Flexport already moved, Amari AI raised $4.5M from First Round and Pear in 2025, and the dossier surfaces no signal that any tier-1 visibly passed or that Alchemize is priced below the P26 batch median. Silence on round dynamics is itself the tell; I punish silence here because price discipline is the entire alpha and the category is competing for the same SAFE allocations every AI-vertical-SaaS deal in this batch is competing for.

Sleeve ladder (my lowest-placed dimension on Alchemize): **Lightning Labs / Aleo / Klaytn / Fold** are the HIGH rung -- pre-consensus crypto with real on-chain economic activity, the sleeve I keep buying without ever headlining; **Ro** is the MID rung where prescription-rail regulatory complexity earned a "venture moment" exception; the LOW rung is the generic AI-agent-shell on top of an LLM that I will not underwrite at seed pricing. Alchemize sits on the LOW rung, full stop -- the US customs brokerage market is a 2.88% CAGR base market (Mordor Intelligence, 2026), the digital-first sub-segment growing at 10.45% CAGR is exactly where Flexport's $2.4B+ historical raise and KlearNow's $69M are pointed, and "the intelligence layer for supply chain teams" reads as commodity LLM-extraction-and-classification wrapped in a brokerage license. Crypto is fundamentally disruptive; this is more internet, applied to HTS codes.

Capital structure ladder: **Teamshares** sits at HIGH (employee-ownership rollup, structure no other VC could underwrite); **Allbirds** at MID (DTC margin discipline inside a venture cap table); the standard SaaS land-and-expand needing five rounds to reach default-alive is LOW. Alchemize sits between MID and LOW, with one redeeming structural feature -- the customs broker license itself is a real regulatory gate (sub-15% 2025 exam pass rate per Mordor Intelligence) and brokerage P&L is per-entry transactional rather than seat-based SaaS, which gives the cap table a different downside shape than the typical AI agent pitch. But the dossier shows no disclosure of which founder holds the license, no revenue, no pricing page on tryalchemize.com, and a competitor set whose deepest pocket has 480x Alchemize's likely seed cash -- the regatta tack count to clear Series A against Flexport is long.

Conviction ladder: **Drop.io** in 2007, bootstrapping a file-sharing primitive against free incumbents, is HIGH; **Sublime Security** founders with public domain writing is MID; LOW is the founder with zero pre-funding artifacts on the category. Alchemize -- Samuel Fu's LinkedIn headline is "Building AI-enabled customs brokerage" (LinkedIn, May 2026), no public writing on customs, no prior customs/freight/trade-compliance role surfaced for either Fu or Robert Hou. Founder-as-brand ladder: **John Fish / Bookshelved** is HIGH with a YouTube subscriber base on the cap table; **Marina Mogilko / LinguaTrip** is HIGH in the other direction with a millions-strong language-learning audience; LOW is the founder with no community. Alchemize is LOW -- Fu's ~4K LinkedIn followers, no Twitter, no GitHub, no Discord, no HN Launch is the absent-evidence default and importers do not buy customs brokerage from a personal brand anyway.

36-month forecast: Alchemize is either acqui-hired into Flexport or KlearNow.AI for sub-$30M by 2029, or pivots away from the importer-direct brokerage model to Amari AI's shape -- selling AI compliance tooling TO existing licensed brokers -- because the regatta tack against a $2.4B-funded incumbent that already shipped the AI auditor is too many turns of the crank for a $500K pre-seed without disclosed customs domain expertise on the founding team. The mechanism producing this is structural: Alchemize's only durable wedge is the broker license plus customer relationships, and incumbents already have both.

The bull case worth steel-manning is the **Ro** Known Exception: regulatory complexity across a fragmented compliance surface (50-state prescription rails for Ro, HTS-classification-plus-CBP-ACE-plus-tariff-volatility for Alchemize) can itself be the venture moment, and the sub-15% broker exam pass rate plus aging workforce is a labor-supply moat that LLMs genuinely arbitrage. If Fu or Hou turns out to hold a customs broker license (the dossier doesn't say) and the tariff regime stays as chaotic as Amari AI's 2025 launch framing implied, Alchemize could be a $50M revenue business in five years without needing to beat Flexport -- just by taking share from the legacy 1990s-software mid-market. That stings because Ro is exactly the precedent. It loses on the dossier evidence: Ro's Zachariah Reitano was a missionary with deep healthcare conviction visible pre-pitch, while neither Fu nor Hou has any surfaced customs domain history or public writing on the category, and Flexport already moved on the AI layer before Alchemize raised. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a disclosed founding-team customs broker license plus a signed POA from a named mid-market importer above $50M in annual entries, before Series A. Until then, Pass.
