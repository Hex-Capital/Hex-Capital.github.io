# Lattice Health -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Lattice Health is, in Thielian terms, a credentialed-outsider founder draped over an indefinite-optimism product — Christine Park's MD/MEng-AI/Stanford-CS-PhD/neurosurgery-resident stack is exactly the unusual intellectual identity the median Sand Hill GP would discount, but the thing she is building, an "operating system for clinical AI in imaging," is the horizontal-orchestration shape that competitors replicate within a release cycle. The signature is high pedigree, low definite plan. That pairing is the failure mode of credentialed founders who have not yet chosen what to be monopolistic about.

Stripe (2010) is the load-bearing comparison on the contrarian-secret axis, because Patrick Collison named a specific incumbent failure mode — PayPal's developer experience — and refused the consensus that payments were "won." Lattice Health does not name a comparable secret. The dossier surfaces real incumbents — Microsoft/Nuance Precision Imaging Network embedded in PowerScribe, Bayer/Blackford acquired in 2023, AWS HealthLake Imaging — and then describes Lattice Health as a "horizontal platform rather than a single diagnostic model," which is a category description, not a secret. The Collison move would have been: "Nuance cannot ship a neutral orchestration layer because PowerScribe's reporting workflow is the lock-in they will not commoditize, and Blackford-inside-Bayer will be optimized for Bayer's contrast portfolio within 24 months." That sentence is not in the Lattice Health dossier, and its absence is itself the signal. Park may hold the secret; she has not yet been forced to articulate it.

The Pass Example pattern — "AI-powered platform for [vertical X]" with a $50B TAM slide and no named architectural blind spot — is the failure mode I want to test Lattice Health against on monopoly trajectory. The dossier confesses the commoditization risk explicitly: "PACS incumbents (GE Healthcare, Philips, Sectra) and AI marketplaces (Blackford, Nuance Precision Imaging Network, Sirona/Nvidia MONAI) could replicate orchestration features." That is the founder-dossier equivalent of competitors-as-validation. Lattice Health's prospective moat is described as "integration depth (PACS/EHR/HL7/DICOM connections) and accumulated model-performance/monitoring data" — every Healthcare IT company since 2010 has written that sentence. Pricing power five years out, against Microsoft and Bayer as the alternative buyers, is not a hypothesis Lattice Health has yet earned the right to make.

Mechanism on the definite-plan axis: solo founder, concurrent PGY-3 neurosurgery residency at UW, concurrent Stanford CS PhD, no website at time of YC fetch, no public product surface. Over 18 months, the compounding goes the wrong way — residency hours are fixed, PhD milestones are fixed, and the enterprise health-system sales cycle the dossier itself flags at 6–18 months requires the founder's full calendar to compress. Lattice Health's roadmap is not indefinite because Park is iteration-mode by temperament; it is indefinite because the bandwidth to be definite about anything does not exist yet. This is the structural version of the "we'll iterate to PMF" red flag.

Counterfactual on founder lineage: for Lattice Health to clear the Anduril-override bar I applied to Palmer Luckey, Park would need a prior shipped-to-scale artifact — not the arXiv 2511.18208 multimodal brain-MRI paper, which is real signal but academic, and not the medRxiv point-of-care MRI work from 2022. Luckey had Oculus-to-Facebook-billion-dollar-exit before Anduril; Moskovitz had Facebook-to-scale before Asana. Park has peer-reviewed deep-learning imaging research and a Duke MEng in AI, which is the credentialed-outsider half of the Thiel pattern without the shipped-operator half. If Lattice Health surfaced a deployed inference pipeline running in a real radiology department with measured throughput, the score on this axis flips a tier. The dossier does not show it.

Palantir (2003) is the single anchor on the regulated-sector axis. Palantir's moat was not that healthcare or government were "regulated"; it was that the integration depth and security-clearance trust took a decade to compound and Silicon Valley refused to do the schlep. Lattice Health sits inside HIPAA, FDA-cleared device adjacency, and PACS/EHR plumbing — that is the right structural neighborhood. But Lattice Health is software orchestration over other people's FDA clearances, not a regulated artifact itself. The regulatory moat accrues to the model vendors (Aidoc, Gleamer, Rad AI) and to the EHR incumbents whose security reviews Lattice Health must pass. The category is regulated; Lattice Health's position inside it is not yet load-bearing on regulation.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Lattice Health does not become the orchestration OS for clinical AI imaging. The more probable terminal state is that Christine Park narrows to a vertical neuro-imaging inference product anchored in her actual research — radiation-necrosis-versus-progression on brain MRI is the specific arXiv work — and either raises a seed against that pivoted thesis or winds down as residency and PhD obligations reassert. Acquisition above $50M by Bayer/Blackford or Microsoft/Nuance is the upside tail and I price it under 10%. The mechanism is bandwidth, not talent.

The strongest objection is the Anduril/Asana override pattern: credentialed outsiders with unusual intellectual identity attacking categories that pure-software pattern-matchers misprice. Park's neurosurgeon-plus-Stanford-CS-PhD biography is exactly the kind of profile a rival fund will herd into precisely because it scans as Thiel-shaped. The override worked for Luckey because Oculus-to-billion-dollar-exit was the operator pedigree that made the credentialed-outsider half decisive; it worked for Moskovitz because Facebook-to-scale was. Lattice Health does not yet have its Oculus. The bull case requires Park to drop residency or the PhD and concentrate; absent that, the credentialed-outsider signal is a Thiel Fellowship aesthetic without the shipping. The condition that flips my verdict: a signed enterprise pilot with a top-20 US health system for the multi-vendor orchestration product — not a single-model neuro-imaging deal — within twelve months, with Park having visibly compressed her clinical or doctoral calendar. Until then, pass.
