# Deep Interactions -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Deep Interactions is a Lovable-shaped wrapper with a UX-research founder -- Sruthi Viswanathan, Oxford DPhil in Human-AI Interaction, ex-Limbic AI, leading a 4-person team -- where the highest signal is founder-thesis fit on "humans-in-the-loop" methodology and the lowest signal is technical/capital risk, because Deep Interactions ships Slack/Gmail/Figma/GitHub/Vercel connectors on commodity LLMs against an incumbent (Lovable, ~$400M ARR Feb 2026 per Sacra, $6.6B Series B Dec 2025 per TechCrunch) that has 1000x the capital and the natural product-extension surface. That high/low pairing -- credible founder-market fit on a category whose technical moat is observable to any competitor in a weekend -- is the entire story of Deep Interactions.

On Consequence Magnitude, my ladder runs from **OpenAI** (2019), where the $50M first VC check funded a civilizational AGI redirection, to **DoorDash** (2013), where consumer-commerce flow was reshaped without civilizational stakes, down to the pass-example YC pre-seed that "uses GPT-4 to summarize sales calls and integrate with HubSpot" -- a 20-30% efficiency gain inside one workflow. Deep Interactions sits one half-rung above the pass example and well below DoorDash: the success-state described on the company website is "teams build internal AI apps without a spaghetti mess of code" with named customers Jack Fertility, Canopie, and FitMatrix shipping faster. No incumbent regime collapses if Deep Interactions wins -- Salesforce does not disappear, Workday does not disappear, the $15T labor market I framed in Fortune 2026-03-06 is not displaced, only modestly accelerated. The success-state is "more SMBs ship internal tools," which is the exact 20-30% optimization play my Outlook Business framing warns against.

On Technical and Capital Risk That Repels Consensus VCs -- the lowest-rung dimension for Deep Interactions -- the ladder runs from **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where I led a $115M Series A on real plasma physics no Tier-1 generalist would touch, through **Cognition** (April 2024, $175M) where the stack-depth on AI software engineers justified the check, down to the pass-example commodity-wrapper. Deep Interactions sits at the bottom rung and arguably below it: connectors across "Slack, Gmail, Chrome, Miro, Figma, GitHub, Vercel, Supabase, CLI" (company website, 2026) is exactly the surface that a Lovable PM can ship in a quarter, and the dossier explicitly flags "commoditization risk: high" with "a vendor team of 1-2 engineers can wrap an LLM with Slack/GitHub/Vercel connectors." Lovable's $330M Series B at $6.6B is not a fundraising data point -- it is the structural fact that Deep Interactions has no capital-hostile science requiring a Khosla check.

On Founder Defies Expert Consensus, my ladder runs from **Impossible Foods** (2011), where Pat Brown contradicted food-science orthodoxy on plant protein at scale, through **Glean** (Known Exception, where Arvind Jain's ex-Google-search credential was paradigmatic expert continuity but capital scale overrode), down to the pass-example "eight years at Salesforce" domain-continuity profile. Sruthi Viswanathan's career -- Google UX, Naver Labs Europe, Limbic AI mental health research, Oxford DPhil in Human-AI Interaction -- is direct expert continuity into a "humans-in-the-loop AI builder" product. Deep Interactions is applying Sruthi's field, not defying it. That maps cleanly to the Glean-MID rung, but Deep Interactions lacks Glean's capital-scale override.

On Founder Learning Rate, the ladder from **Slash** (2024, $100M Series C with the documented Kanye-pivot story) through **Physical Intelligence** (Chelsea Finn and Sergey Levine's academic-to-startup pivot in 2024) down to a linear-plan founder gives Deep Interactions a mid-low placement: Sruthi previously co-founded TheSpaceship.ai (Castbox/RocketReach), which establishes second-time-founder status, but no public pivot narrative or thesis-update is documented in the dossier. The Slash Known Exception requires a *documented* pivot strong enough to override low consequence magnitude -- absent here. On Required-Capital Profile, the ladder from **OpenAI** ($50M first check) through **Slash** ($100M Series C) to a pre-seed SaaS deal I am over-resourced for places Deep Interactions firmly at the bottom: a 4-person team with no plant, no fleet, no foundation model to train, no drug pipeline, and a Calendly link as the sole inbound funnel.

My 36-month forecast for Deep Interactions: acquired as an acqui-hire by Lovable, Replit, or Vercel for $15-40M, or quietly dead after running through YC capital plus a small bridge -- the mechanism is straightforward: Lovable adds a team-collaboration layer in 2026-2027, that layer absorbs the "context across Slack/Figma/GitHub" wedge, and Deep Interactions's 10 design partners migrate to whichever vibe-coding incumbent ships it cheaper. This forecast is consistent with the signature: a UX-research founder with real founder-market fit cannot out-execute a $6.6B-valued competitor on a software-only product surface.

The strongest case against my pass is the **DoorDash** (2013) Known Exception -- capital-light consumer logistics that returned a $72B IPO in 2020 despite zero scientific miracle. Bull case: Deep Interactions's "humans-in-the-loop" methodology is its analogous unsexy wedge; Sruthi's Oxford HAI research could be the proprietary process IP that Lovable's self-serve cannot replicate; the no-code AI platform market grows from $8.6B to $75.14B by 2034 (Grand View Research) and a category that big can support multiple winners. That argument stings on the consequence-magnitude dimension. It loses on the override mechanism: DoorDash had a founder ambition signal -- Tony Xu publicly framing on-demand commerce as a multi-decade reshaping -- that Deep Interactions's dossier lacks entirely, replaced by Typeform waitlists and a "Stealth Startup" LinkedIn headline. **One verifiable condition flips my verdict:** Deep Interactions ships a proprietary context-capture model or human-AI interaction protocol that Lovable cannot reproduce by adding connectors, with at least one Fortune-500 paid contract signed in the next 12 months -- absent that, the pass holds and consequence-first discipline costs me nothing.
