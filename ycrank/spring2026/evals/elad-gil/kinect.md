# Kinect -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Kinect is the cleanest example I've seen this batch of a company with a **real catalyst and a captured-rent position** -- Shopify shipped Agentic Storefronts natively to all US merchants on Mar 24, 2026, which is the same event Kratik Agrawal and Varun Kandula are pitching as their tailwind. When the platform ships the wedge for free in the same quarter you launch, you are not riding the catalyst; the catalyst is riding over you. That's the signature: highest dimension is the *existence* of the agentic-commerce step-function, lowest dimension is Kinect's *index position* on it.

**Anduril** (2022) is the high rung on catalyst -- Ukraine cracked open Pentagon software procurement in a 24-month window, and Trae Stephens was uniquely positioned because no defense-prime had Palantir's forward-deployed muscle. Kinect has a comparably real catalyst on paper -- OpenAI ChatGPT checkout in 2025 plus Shopify Agentic Storefronts on Mar 24, 2026 (TechCrunch) -- and the dossier names it specifically rather than waving at TAM. Where Kinect diverges from the Anduril template is that the catalyst itself *creates* the substitute: Shopify's native rollout to all US merchants is the merchant-side feature Kinect sells. Anduril's catalyst made the *prime contractors* slower; Kinect's catalyst made the *platform* faster. That's not a re-opened market for a middleware plugin -- that's a market re-opened *for the platform*.

**Lyft** Series C is the low rung on index position -- a #2 in a winner-take-all network market where I couldn't see the path to share inversion (20VC). Kinect's version of that failure mechanism is structurally worse because Kinect is squeezed from both sides: Shopify Agentic Storefronts on the merchant side and OpenAI/Amazon Rufus on the buyer-agent side (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) mean the "brand-owned data layer" Kinect pitches sits between two parties who would prefer to talk directly. Stripe became an index on e-commerce because every merchant had to touch payments and no platform was credibly going to build them. Kinect's catalog-and-policy layer is exactly the thing Shopify *already builds*. That's the Lyft pattern with the network effects reversed against Kinect, not for it.

Revenue velocity on Kinect is the *Harvey* counterfactual -- Harvey gave me $3B→$5B→$8B in months (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) and that's what "just working" looks like in dollars. Kinect gives me 10 named logos (Slumberkins, Sousa, Hearthy Foods, et al.) and three customer-cited percentages (20% conversion, 14% AOV, 24% time-on-page) with no per-brand attribution, no sample size, no methodology, no ARR, no MoM. For the score to flip a tier on this dimension I'd need a single screenshot of net-revenue retention or paid-logo count compounding >20% MoM over the last 90 days -- the dossier silence on this is itself the signal, because in this cycle real growth is trivial to disclose.

Kratik Agrawal's resume -- Google Commerce, Anduril, Verkada, Reevo (led Conversational Intelligence) -- and Varun Kandula's NextGen Consulting record ($200K+ contracted ARR across 10+ brands including Sephora and Ralph Lauren) is the closest thing in the dossier to founder-market fit, and it is real fit, not pedigree theater. The two of them co-led the same Conversational Intelligence function at Reevo before founding Kinect, which is the rarer signal. The mode I want to apply here is mechanism over 18 months: does this founder-pair compound *shipping artifacts* the way Aravind Srinivas did into the Perplexity origin (Tim Ferriss #863), or do they compound *consulting-style deployments* the way the NextGen background suggests? The dossier shows no GitHub stars, no prior shipped consumer artifact, no between-meeting iteration anecdote -- which doesn't disqualify, but it doesn't earn the Perplexity comparison either.

Single-miracle test on Kinect: the company requires (1) merchants to install a middleware layer instead of using Shopify's native Agentic Storefronts, (2) external agents at OpenAI/Perplexity/Amazon to *prefer* Kinect's brand-owned feed over scraping or platform-direct APIs, and (3) agentic-commerce protocols to standardize on something other than the hyperscaler-and-Shopify-defined defaults. That's three independent miracles compounding, which is the NFX failure pattern I almost never override. **Color Genomics** (2015) worked as a workflow layer on commodity sequencing because the chemistry was already shipping and the workflow was the only miracle; Kinect is workflow on top of a platform that ships the *same workflow*, which inverts the Color template.

Forecast for Kinect over 36 months: acqui-hired by Shopify, Klaviyo, or Gorgias for sub-$30M with ARR plateauing under $3M, or quietly dead -- the captured-rent signature does not produce $1B outcomes because the rents accrue upstream. The bull case worth steel-manning is Alhena's ~$13.9M raise and Daydream's $50M Forerunner+Index round, which prove capital is flowing into agentic-commerce middleware regardless of platform overlap; if Kinect's "brand-owned data layer" became the schema.org of agentic commerce, the index position flips entirely. The dossier evidence that wins against the bull case is the Mar 24, 2026 Shopify launch date -- the platform shipped the substitute *before* Kinect could lock in the schema, which is the inverse of the Stripe-versus-merchant-accounts timing in 2010. The condition that would flip my verdict: Kinect signs three or more non-Shopify enterprise merchants (BigCommerce, Salesforce Commerce Cloud, or equivalent) at $100K+ ACV in the next nine months, demonstrating that the merchant-side wedge survives outside the platform that just commoditized it.
