# Prized -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Hudson Griffith and Marinos Eliades are two engineers building a natural-language Retool alternative in a 2026 YC batch where the incumbent ships AppGen and Agents at $120M ARR and Superblocks just took $23M explicitly to own the "vibe-coding governance" lane. Prized's analytical signature is a clean-CEO two-engineer team aimed straight into the most consensus dev-tools category I've seen this cycle -- governance-clean on paper, magically-weird-empty in the wild. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Prized seems to be playing is the one six other YC decks are also playing.

Hudson Griffith is listed as Co-founder & CEO and Marinos Eliades as Co-founder -- that is the structural inverse of the Yusupov/Kroll HQ Trivia post-mortem I keep on the wall, and it is the single dimension where Prized lands closest to my SpaceX rung. The Gander YC F24 cohort already exited to Archer with these same two humans on the founding team, which means the 50/50 tiebreaker question has been *lived through* a full company cycle, not just answered on a term sheet. That is real. The dossier does not surface a vesting or operating agreement, and I would ask for it, but a named CEO who has already shipped one venture-backed company alongside the same co-founder is exactly the configuration GameCrush did not have. Prized passes the governance filter the way few pre-seed dossiers do, and it is the only dimension where I lean in.

The pass example I keep returning to -- two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, $20M oversubscribed seed, a $200B TAM slide -- begins with founders who discovered the problem in a memo rather than at a desk where ops was waiting six weeks for an engineer to write a Salesforce query. That failure mechanism is *analytical origin*, and I look for its trace in Prized. Hudson built consumer AI at Suno, Marinos was a Stanford CS198 section leader; both shipped Gander, which the dossier doesn't describe in a way that puts either of them *inside* the ops/sales/CS persona Prized claims to serve. Neither founder has a "I sat next to the RevOps lead at 2am and watched them paste customer data into Claude Code" moment in this dossier -- and that paste-into-Claude-Code moment is the actual wedge Prized is selling. The pattern that killed the pass-example deck is faintly present here: engineers building for non-engineers, with the lived contact one persona away.

Crusoe sounded like three uninvestable things stapled together in 2018, and that was the *point* -- credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds, which made the round underpriced. Prized's competitive frame is the opposite shape: Retool at $120M ARR shipping AppGen in April 2025 and Agents in May 2025, Superblocks at $60M total raised with a May 2025 governance-positioned extension, plus Refine, Vybe, and Softr all running the same "describe-it-in-English" wedge in the Vybe 2026 listing. Magic weirdness compounds when no one else wants the deal; here, five funded competitors already want exactly this deal. The round is not underpriced because the category is not unloved -- it is the most-loved B2B AI category of the moment.

Capital intensity and unsexy-regulated swing -- the SpaceX/Anduril/Vigil Labs lane -- is where I write checks no one else on this council list will. Prized is pure SaaS over connectors to Salesforce, Snowflake, and Postgres, with a governance veneer that is real but not a regulatory moat. Over 18 months the governance angle either compounds into SOC 2 + HIPAA + named Fortune 500 logos that create switching costs on a per-tool library (the prized.dev "library of governed tools" pitch), or Retool's enterprise team ships the same audit log in a quarter and the wedge closes. The dossier shows no FedRAMP path, no regulated-industry design partner, no hardware. This is the undifferentiated AI-app SaaS wrapper anchor, almost verbatim.

Physical verifiability is where my hand reaches for the product and finds nothing to touch. Niantic I could play; Postmates delivered to my door; Flock I read off the Wi-Fi at a Four Seasons. Prized's only public CTAs on prized.dev are "Schedule a call" and "/sign-in," with the on-site case study being a fictional "Acme" with $1.2M ARR across 142 accounts -- a demo scenario, not a customer. To evaluate Prized I would need a Salesforce/Snowflake stack and a sales call. That is the B2B-API abstraction my pass-example anchor flagged: I cannot install it, photograph it, or hand it to a friend in 48 hours.

My 36-month forecast on Prized: acquihired by Retool, Superblocks, or a Salesforce-adjacent buyer for under $25M with the two founders rolling into a product role, or shuttered, with sub-$3M ARR before any priced Series A. The mechanism is incumbent encroachment compounding faster than a two-person sales-led GTM can convert -- Retool already shipped the AI generation feature, Superblocks already raised on the governance line, and Prized has no disclosed paying logo to anchor against either.

The bull case on Prized stings, and I want to be honest about it. Hudson and Marinos already shipped one YC company together to an Archer acquisition in 2024, which is *exactly* the "founder shipped a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain BEFORE the current company" invest signal I look for -- the Palmer Luckey / Anduril structural template. Superblocks' May 2025 $23M raise validates that governance for AI-generated apps is a real wedge, not a feature. Two founders who already exited together is the inverse of the HQ Trivia feud risk. Where that case breaks for me is that the Gander exit price is undisclosed and reads more like an engineer aqui-hire than a market-proven product trajectory, and shipping a consumer AI tool at Suno does not put Hudson at the RevOps desk where Prized claims its wedge lives. The Anduril analogy needs Luckey to have *been* a defense-adjacent person before the company, and Prized's founders are domain-adjacent to *AI infrastructure*, not to internal-tools buyers. One verifiable condition flips me: a named Fortune 500 ops or sales team paying Prized $50K+ ARR with an executive sponsor inside the buyer in the next six months -- a real Acme, not the demo one -- and I will reread the dossier with one foot in love instead of one eyebrow up.
