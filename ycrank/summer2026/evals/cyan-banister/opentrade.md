# OpenTrade -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

OpenTrade is a tactile-verifiable consumer artifact sitting in the most consensus lane of Summer 2026 -- Ojas Kandhare's swipe-a-thesis card sits at opentrade.live where anyone can touch it, but it lives in a lane where Robinhood shipped MCP-connected agent trading on May 27, 2026 and Public.com already ships prompt-to-basket "generated assets." The signature I'm reading: highest on physical verifiability, lowest on magically-weird non-consensus. That is a fatal pairing for me, because I fund the swipe-native artifact only when the founder is *of* the domain and the market is laughing at the category -- OpenTrade is neither.

Anduril/Palmer Luckey is the load-bearing rung on the founder-lived-experience axis for me -- Luckey shipped Oculus out of a garage before he ever touched defense, so when he pitched drones I was investing in a person who had already compounded through one adjacent hardware trajectory. OpenTrade's Ojas Kandhare is a University of Washington undergraduate whose prior artifact is Qualty, an AI travel companion, and who holds a FIDE chess rating (ID 30993725) -- interesting texture, but none of it puts him in front of the retail-investor problem before he pitched it. The dossier surfaces no securities-industry background, no clearing-firm apprenticeship, no personal moment where OpenTrade's Kandhare felt the "opaque charts and tips" problem in his own account. This is a consumer-AI builder discovering an investing thesis via market analysis, and I read the absence of an autobiographical origin the same way I read a Stanford CS deck opening with TAM -- the founder is structurally replaceable by any other polymath undergrad who can wrap an LLM in a swipe UI.

The Pass Example failure mechanism is the "consensus YC AI-agent darling" -- six top funds chasing, priced as if the outcome is already certain, moat that lives in the model rather than the founder. OpenTrade sits inside that exact mechanism. Public.com raised $421M and reports ~$174.4M revenue while shipping generated assets; Robinhood carries 13.0M MAU as of Q4 2025 and launched MCP AI-agent trading in May 2026; Composer/SoFi already ships rules-based backtestable strategies. OpenTrade's differentiator per the dossier is the swipe-native card format -- a UX veneer, not a category the market has refused to enter. I am not seeing sophisticated investors passing on aesthetic or regulatory grounds; I am seeing the opposite -- LinkedIn is posting a "$500k" comped growth-marketer role for OpenTrade before the product has disclosed a single user metric, which is a fundraising cadence, not a build cadence.

Mechanism analysis on OpenTrade's regulatory posture: consumer trade execution compounds only if OpenTrade owns the broker-dealer registration or locks in a clearing partner early, because the swipe-to-position UX implies actual order flow. The dossier is explicit -- "no public data on OpenTrade's regulatory posture or clearing relationship" -- and eighteen months of TikTok CAC without that stack means OpenTrade will either be a lead-gen funnel handing users to Public.com's rails or a compliance letter away from a shutdown. Vigil Labs earned my regulated-swing points by *living* inside the compliance moat; OpenTrade earns partial credit for being in fintech and loses most of it because the moat is undisclosed.

Counterfactual on OpenTrade's single-CEO tiebreaker: the dossier lists team size of 3 but names only Kandhare, which means the score flips a tier only if OpenTrade discloses the two remaining founders, their equity split, and an operating agreement naming Kandhare's tiebreaker authority. Without that, I am applying the near-absent-evidence default and treating unresolved governance as a near-term blow-up risk -- the HQ Trivia lesson I paid tuition to learn. The chess rating suggests Kandhare has the temperament to operate under pressure, but temperament is not a cap table.

Single-anchor parallel to Niantic on the physical-verifiability axis: I sourced Niantic by playing Ingress obsessively since 2012, which means my check followed my own thumb. OpenTrade's opentrade.live is live and the tagline "Swipe positions, not charts" is exactly the kind of tactile pull I look for -- but the dossier surfaces no App Store presence, no Product Hunt rank, no Discord community, no download count, and no morning-thesis samples I could screenshot. A marketing site is not a Poke Stop. If OpenTrade shipped an installable app I could swipe on the train and feel the pull the way HQ Trivia pulled at 9 p.m. Eastern, the score on this axis moves; today it does not.

My 36-month forecast on OpenTrade: acqui-hire by Public.com or a mid-tier neobroker for $5-15M as a UX/design team, or a quiet shutdown after the seed burns on paid social without securing a clearing partner. The mechanism is straightforward -- the swipe-card format is defensible for one product cycle before Robinhood clones it into their MCP agent flow, and OpenTrade has neither a founder who can talk to a FINRA examiner without flinching nor a distribution wedge that compounds faster than incumbent CAC.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the HQ Trivia + DeepMind exception stack -- Kandhare could be a polymath (chess + consumer AI + undergrad hustle) whose swipe UX goes explosively viral on TikTok the way HQ Trivia pulled me in before the metrics existed, and virality could carry OpenTrade past the co-founder governance gap the same way it carried Yusupov and Kroll. That case stings because I have written the exception check before. But the DeepMind exception requires polymath compounding into a *hard technical moat* -- Hassabis's neuroscience-into-AGI stack -- and Kandhare's prior artifact is Qualty, another consumer-AI wrapper, not a cross-domain compounding trajectory. The HQ Trivia exception requires a tactile pull I can feel in my own thumb, and OpenTrade has not yet shipped the installable artifact that would let me test it. I would flip to Invest on one verifiable condition: OpenTrade ships an installable iOS app before end of Q1 2027 that I can swipe on my own phone AND names a clearing/broker-dealer partner in the same announcement. Absent that, this is a pass -- and I would rather write the check into a Vigil Labs-class founder who is *of* the regulated domain rather than visiting it.
