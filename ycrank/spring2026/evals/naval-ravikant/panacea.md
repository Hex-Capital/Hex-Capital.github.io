# Panacea -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Panacea is a fixed-fee FDA consulting firm with an LLM productivity layer dressed as a startup, and the signature is the gap between Yaman Ziadeh's Anterior-era AI engineering credibility and the fact that Panacea's distribution is a cal.com link on withpanacea.com pointed at Haris Javed-Akhtar's calendar. That pairing — real founder-market fit on the AI engineering side, zero permissionless leverage on the distribution side — tells me what Panacea actually is: a boutique services arbitrage against hourly incumbents like MDI Consultants and EAS Consulting, not a code-leverage business that compounds without gatekeepers. Services arbitrage can be a fine business; it is not the asymmetric-payoff math that underwrites my check.

Leverage architecture is the entry point and Panacea fails it concretely. The HIGH rung on this ladder is **Twitter (2007)** — media-as-protocol, zero marginal cost of replication, anyone posts without permission. The MID rung is **Replit (April 2023)** — code leverage with a platform layer that still distributes through developer pull. The LOW rung is the red-flag pattern I named explicitly: B2B founder outbound with no organic product pull, where labor is the product and AI is internal tooling. Panacea sits on the LOW rung — "consultations booked via cal.com/haris-panacea/call" is founder-calendar distribution, "ex-FDA consultants with 50+ years combined experience" is labor as the deliverable, and the "12x faster" testimonial is internal consultant productivity, not customer-facing code leverage. The AI here makes Harun Sinha cheaper to staff. It does not distribute Panacea.

Network/compounding is the second axis and Panacea has none. HIGH rung is **Uber (2010)** where each driver makes each rider's wait shorter; MID is **Notion (2013)** where templates created a mild network on a single-sided product; LOW is the **Perplexity (~2022)** profile I explicitly waived via Known Exception because three other gates cleared at top quartile. Panacea is single-sided B2B services — biotech client N gets no benefit from biotech client N-1 submitting an IND last quarter, beyond Panacea's internal corpus. The dossier names no shared workflow, no marketplace, no two-sided dynamic. Panacea sits below even Perplexity on this axis, and the Perplexity exception does not transfer because criterion #1 and #3 do not clear at top quartile here.

Specific knowledge is where Panacea earns its best score and it is still mid-range. HIGH rung is **Stack Overflow (2008)** — Atwood and Spolsky were practicing developers writing for developers. MID rung is **Notion (2013)** — Ivan Zhao's adolescent productivity-tool obsession. LOW is the "ex-McKinsey identified $X TAM" pattern. Panacea sits between MID and LOW: Yaman Ziadeh's prior role at Anterior (YC-backed AI healthcare automation) is genuinely on-thesis specific knowledge for the AI side, and Haris's MD credential supplies clinical authority. But the load-bearing FDA submission knowledge sits with **Harun Sinha, Principal Consultant** — not a co-founder. The specific knowledge is rented, and the dossier flags "talent concentration around single named consultant" as the top risk. That is structurally fragile.

Big market with pivot room: HIGH rung is **Twitter (2007)** (consumer media), MID rung is **Notion (2013)** (productivity platform with template/database/wiki pivot vectors), LOW rung is "CRM for HVAC technicians." Panacea is healthcare regulatory affairs outsourcing — 6.7% CAGR with $2.11B absolute growth over a decade. That is a small, slow, regulated vertical with bounded pivots into post-market surveillance, EMA filings, or clinical trial regulatory work. The dossier provides no adjacent expansion vector beyond more FDA pathways. Pivot room exists, but inside a regulated vertical where every adjacency requires new credentialed staff. Founder trio reads clean — no integrity flags, no contested exits, but also no shared history between Haris and Yaman per Phase 3 findings, which puts the most-important-decision (Venture Hacks ~2007-10) on freshly-formed footing. Default-ish on both ends.

My 36-month forecast: Panacea reaches $3–8M ARR as a profitable boutique FDA regulatory services firm, gets acquired by IQVIA or ICON for $20–60M, or stays private as a lifestyle services business — does not return the 1,000x that angel asymmetric math requires. The mechanism is straightforward: fixed-fee milestone pricing against hourly incumbents is real margin arbitrage in a fragmented market, but the scarce input is ex-FDA staff willing to work under fixed-fee comp, which scales linearly with hiring, not exponentially with code. The 117.27-day average FDA elapsed time means each engagement consumes consultant calendar weeks. Services revenue ramps; it does not compound.

The bull steel-man stings: Weave Bio just raised $20M Series A in October 2025 on pure software, and the dossier flags FDA's evolving stance on AI-drafted submissions (Innolitics 2025 Year in Review). If the FDA tightens human-author attestation rules on submission narratives, Panacea's consultant-plus-AI hybrid is the structurally correct architecture and Weave Bio's pure-software bet breaks. Three years of submission corpora could fine-tune into a defensible model. Aaron Epstein as YC partner is non-trivial signal. I take the bear side anyway because even in the bull case Panacea grows into a category-leading services firm — capped at services multiples — and the **Anchorage Digital** exception does not apply here because Panacea is not the regulatory-moat-as-leverage play (that would require Panacea owning a piece of FDA-facing infrastructure, which the dossier does not describe). The flip condition is verifiable: if Panacea ships a self-serve AI submission product that biotechs use without a Panacea consultant in the loop and that product crosses 100 organic signups in 6 months, the leverage architecture has converted from labor to code and I revisit. The cal.com link suggests we are nowhere near that.
