# PerfectBit -- Elad Gil Evaluation

PerfectBit is a real-catalyst, no-velocity company -- Peter Vajda and Seiji Yamamoto have correctly identified RLVR (Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards) as a frontier-lab post-training step-function and are selling verifier-grounded data into the ~10-20 labs that matter, but the dossier discloses zero revenue, zero named lab logos, and zero shipping cadence since YC Spring 2026. That high-catalyst/low-velocity pairing is the analytical signature, and it's the exact pairing that historically traps thesis-correct teams in oligopsony purgatory rather than producing breakouts.

On *catalyst*, the high rung is **Anduril** (2022) where Ukraine opened defense procurement in a specific 24-month window; the low rung is my **Lyft** Series C pass where the market was steady-state rideshare with no re-opening. PerfectBit sits closer to Anduril than to Decagon (2023, post-LLM customer support): the dossier names a real technical step-function -- RLVR / GRPO / DAPO becoming a core frontier-lab technique per llm-stats.com 2026 -- compounded with the Epoch-style projection that high-quality human text exhausts between 2026-2032 (invisibletech.ai, 2026). That is a specific, named catalyst, not TAM math. PerfectBit is in the right doorway. Anduril had Trae Stephens and a procurement re-opening; PerfectBit has Yamamoto from Core Llama and a data-exhaustion re-opening -- structurally the same template, one tier weaker because the catalyst is shared with every well-funded incumbent (Scale, Surge, Mercor) who can also read the same llm-stats.com piece.

On *revenue velocity*, the high rung is **Harvey** (2023) ripping $3B → $5B → $8B in months, the low rung is the generic AI startup with no disclosed curve where "something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863). PerfectBit sits near the floor. The dossier states "No revenue, customer logos, or paying-customer counts disclosed" and the website is at v0.4. The NDA-cover argument -- that frontier-lab contracts are confidential -- is plausible but unverifiable, and my standard is that real revenue at this stage leaks through hiring posture, fundraise markups, or a single named design partner. None of those are present. The pre-seed calibration says don't penalize missing metrics as defaults, but PerfectBit is selling into a buyer set of ten companies; a single Anthropic or DeepMind LOI would be the entire signal, and we don't have one.

*Founder execution*: the high rung is Aravind at **Perplexity** (2022) shipping between meetings, the low rung is the ex-FAANG founder with credentials but no named artifact. PerfectBit's founders sit closer to my **Brex** (2017) operationally-disciplined-leader archetype than to Aravind -- Vajda led Emu/Movie Gen, Yamamoto led Core Llama post-training, both real shipped artifacts at Meta over 9-11 year tenures -- but my own dictum is "not just at a big company, but independently," and there is no independent shipping history named here, no GitHub, no public side projects, no demo cadence in the dossier. *Index position*: the high rung is **Stripe** (2010), an index on e-commerce; PerfectBit sits below Decagon (2023) because the dossier itself flags buyer concentration in ~10-20 labs plus active insourcing -- "Frontier labs themselves are running similar pipelines internally" per llm-stats.com. That is an oligopsony, not an index. *Single miracle*: PerfectBit isolates one bet -- frontier labs prefer to buy verifier-grounded data rather than build it -- which is a GTM/trust miracle, not a science miracle. That's closer to **Decagon** (2023) than to a fusion bet, and it's the dimension where PerfectBit cleanest passes my filter.

36-month forecast: PerfectBit gets acquired by Scale AI or Surge AI for $80-180M as a verifier-product team-tuck within 24-30 months, sub-$15M ARR at exit, having signed two or three frontier-lab paid pilots but failing to land an exclusive that justifies standalone scale. Mechanism: the catalyst is real, the buyer set is ten companies, and at least one of Scale/Surge/Mercor decides verifier-grounded data is a feature they need on their existing relationship with OpenAI/Anthropic. PerfectBit's founder network gets them in the room; the oligopsony economics decide the exit.

The bull case stings. **Mercor** went from zero to $450M+ ARR selling into this exact frontier-lab buyer set in roughly the same window -- proof that a small team with the right relationships can compound fast when lab capex is climbing (Foundation Capital, 2026). If Vajda and Yamamoto's Meta-era access translates to two named contracts with OpenAI and Anthropic at $5-20M ACV each, PerfectBit looks like Harvey-shaped revenue velocity and my "when consensus pays" framing kicks in. The counter is that Mercor's moat was contractor sourcing operations -- a wet-and-messy human-marketplace asset -- whereas PerfectBit's verifier stack is software described in arXiv 2601.22607 (Jan 2026) and the opendilab/awesome-RLVR repo. Software a frontier lab can read on GitHub and insource is not Mercor's moat. The verdict flips if PerfectBit names a $5M+ signed contract with OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google DeepMind within 12 months -- that single data point would convert this from a thesis-correct pre-seed into a Harvey-template velocity bet I'd write into.
