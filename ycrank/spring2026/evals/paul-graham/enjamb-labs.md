# Enjamb Labs -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Enjamb Labs is a schlep-zone company without the hacker lineage to survive the schlep -- Rayan Mubarak picked FDA-regulated drafting (INDs, NDAs, SAPs, SDTM/ADaM) as the target, which is exactly the kind of work other smart founders unconsciously route around, but the dossier returns "GitHub: No public repos found" for both Enjamb Labs co-founders, and that absence is what I keep staring at.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the rung I want Enjamb Labs to sit on. Armstrong picked the bitcoin compliance grind because nobody else wanted it, and that grind became the moat -- the exact pattern from "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012). Enjamb Labs has chosen a structurally similar zone: 21 CFR Part 11, GxP validation, auditable regulatory artifacts, the SDTM/ADaM programming that CROs currently bill for and therefore have a channel-conflict reason to avoid automating. That is a real opening. But Armstrong shipped a hand-built Android wallet to rescue his rejected YC application -- the artifact preceded the schlep claim. Enjamb Labs's dossier offers only "trusted by leading institutions" with no named sponsor, integration counts (136+ databases, 250M+ papers) that read like an academic literature tool rather than a regulatory drafting tool, and "Free to use. Forever" on enjamb.ai. Picking the schlep is not the same as having begun it.

The pass example in my own essays -- the polished AI-wrapper team with no GitHub footprint -- is the rung Enjamb Labs is closer to on hacker genealogy, and this is where I get nervous. Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist prize for Lisp work before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz had co-authored RSS before **Reddit** (2005). Hackers leave tracks. Rayan Mubarak has biomedical ML publications from University of Florida College of Medicine on cancer immunotherapy biomarkers, which is a research credential, not a hacker credential. Maadhav Deekshitha's RocketReach snippet lists Broadcom, Dell, FIRST -- enterprise engineering rsums, no shipped open source, no demo on Hacker News, no Twitter follower signal retrievable. Building a "single auditable workspace" that pharma compliance officers will trust against FDA audit is a software engineering rigor problem before it is a domain problem, and the dossier gives me no evidence Enjamb Labs has the lineage that ships that kind of artifact.

Mechanism on organic-idea fit: Rayan Mubarak's published work on ML and genomic biomarkers is adjacent to the regulatory drafting and SDTM/ADaM programming Enjamb Labs targets, but adjacency compounds the wrong way over 18 months -- a researcher who has read papers about cancer biomarkers has not personally drafted an IND at 2 a.m. the night before an FDA submission deadline. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT; Enjamb Labs's founders have not, as far as the dossier shows, sat in a biostatistician's chair and hated their tooling.

Counterfactual on animal resourcefulness: the $650K from YC and Founders, Inc. is stage-default, not signal. For Enjamb Labs to flip from the 12/35 default into the upper half of this dimension I would need to see one specific pre-funding artifact -- a named pharma sponsor who let them draft a real protocol section, a public benchmark on SDTM compliance, a stunt where they hand-converted a CRO's clinical dataset for free to win the relationship. None of those appear. The dossier instead offers integration counts and "Free to use. Forever," which is the opposite of the cereal-box pattern.

Single-anchor parallel on co-founder configuration: **Reddit** (2005) was Huffman and Ohanian as dorm roommates with years of prior trust before they shipped the link aggregator. Enjamb Labs is a two-person team, which is the right shape, but "No public data on co-founder relationship" means I am taking the configuration on faith. Pair is correct; pair without history is the configuration the YC interview usually has to surface, and I cannot surface it from this dossier.

My 36-month forecast on Enjamb Labs: quiet wind-down or sub-$15M acquihire into a Saama- or Medidata-tier eClinical incumbent, with no disclosed ARR, "Free to use. Forever" never having converted to enterprise seats, the "Agentic Workspace for Research" positioning on enjamb.ai having drifted further from the YC-page biopharma scope rather than converging on it. The mechanism is that the schlep Enjamb Labs has named -- 21 CFR Part 11-grade regulatory drafting -- requires both compliance grind and hacker rigor, and the dossier shows the founders have neither yet shipped.

The strongest case against my pass is the schlep-blindness case itself: Alexandr Wang named that essay as **Scale AI**'s seminal trigger, and Enjamb Labs is doing the same kind of zone-selection -- picking unpleasant regulated work that CROs have channel-conflict reasons to ignore. If Rayan Mubarak's biomedical research background lets him grind FDA edge cases the way Armstrong ground bitcoin compliance, the missing GitHub does not matter because the moat is the regulatory work, not the code. The reason this case does not move me: Armstrong shipped the wallet before he ground the compliance, and the wallet is what told me he would grind anything. Enjamb Labs's dossier shows zone selection without the precedent artifact. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named biopharma sponsor running an actual IND section or SDTM dataset through Enjamb Labs within 12 months, disclosed publicly. Until then, pass.
