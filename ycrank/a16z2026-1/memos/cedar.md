## The Idea

**Problem:** The best candidates are found through employee referrals, not job boards, but referral programs break down as companies scale (a16z Speedrun listing). Companies like Stripe reportedly invested 100 people and $10M/year to maintain referral-based hiring at scale (a16z Speedrun listing). Current referral tools (ERIN, Boon, Teamable) digitize the workflow but do not systematically map or expand employees' professional networks.

**Approach:** Cedar conducts 15-minute AI-agent-driven calls with employees to map their trusted professional relationships, surface high-signal candidates, and build a proprietary relationship graph (a16z Speedrun listing). Each customer interaction expands the graph, creating a compounding data asset. The website describes the model as "introductions only" — curated, invitation-based matchmaking between candidates and hiring teams (getcedar.ai).

**Differentiation:** Existing referral platforms (ERIN, Boon, Teamable) automate referral submission workflows but depend on employees to self-identify candidates. Cedar's agent-driven approach proactively extracts relationship data from employees, building a graph "no one else is building" (a16z Speedrun listing). Unlike AI sourcing tools (Perfect, hireEZ) that search public profiles, Cedar's data comes from private, trust-based relationships.

**Business Model:** No pricing page is publicly visible (getcedar.ai, accessed April 2026). The one-liner states "$2.5M contracted rev" while the company description states "Closed $3M in revenue while in stealth" (a16z Speedrun listing). [Inferred]: Likely an enterprise SaaS or per-seat model given the B2B, relationship-mapping nature of the product.

## Defensibility

- **Data network effect:** Every 15-minute employee call adds private relationship data to a proprietary graph; each new customer makes the graph more valuable for all customers (a16z Speedrun listing). This is a compounding data asset that competitors without the same access cannot replicate.
- **Switching costs:** [Inferred]: Once a company's employee network is mapped into Cedar's graph, migrating that data to a competitor would require re-conducting all relationship-mapping conversations.
- **No defensibility signals exist today at scale** — the graph's value depends on achieving sufficient customer density.

**Market structure:** LinkedIn owns the dominant professional graph but monetizes it via job postings, recruiter seats, and advertising (LinkedIn Talent Solutions). Building an agent-driven referral extraction layer would cannibalize LinkedIn Recruiter's per-seat revenue model and potentially antagonize enterprise customers by surfacing their employees' private networks. [Inferred]: This business-model conflict creates a structural barrier for LinkedIn specifically.

**Commoditization risk:** AI sourcing tools (Perfect, hireEZ, Gem) could add referral-mapping features, but they currently operate on public data. Any competitor could replicate the 15-minute call format, but the accumulated proprietary graph data creates a time-based advantage that grows with each customer. [Inferred]: The core technology (LLM-driven conversational extraction) is not proprietary, but the dataset is.

No public information on Cedar's data governance or compliance certifications was found.
