# Auxos -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Auxos is a three-fencer execution team — Ashton Daniel ex-BCG, Kerry Lu ex-Amazon Ads simulation infrastructure, Jerry Wu ex-Meta Reels — selling LLM-driven synthetic audiences into the most consensus-priced corner of the 2026 YC batch, six weeks after Aaru cleared a $1B Series A headline (TechCrunch, Dec 2025) and three weeks after HBR ran "The AI Tools That Are Transforming Market Research" (hbr.org, Nov 2025). The signature here is brutal: the dimension where Auxos scores highest is founder execution credibility, and the dimension where it scores lowest is category sleeve fit — which is exactly the profile of a company I cannot make money on, because the price the market is willing to underwrite is set by the consensus wave Aaru just minted, not by the actual asset on offer.

The price ladder for Auxos runs from **Solana** at $0.05/coin (pre-consensus crypto when the generalist funds had quit the category) at the top, through **Sublime Security** (enterprise security entered before the consolidation wave) in the middle, down to the canonical YC AI-agent pitch from my Pass Example at the bottom. Auxos sits one rung above that floor and not a ticker higher: Aaru's December 2025 markup is the textbook "the deck screams hot deal" — Evidenza is already at 100+ enterprise clients including Salesforce and GE bootstrapped, Viewpoints.ai has Pear VC and Sarah Smith Fund, Synthetic Users, Brox.ai, Synthelos all crowd the floor. When HBR validates the category and a $1B Series A prints in the same quarter as Auxos's P26 batch, the YC seed price is being set by the markup, not by the underwriting. "When people 'agree' something is a good idea, it probably isn't" — and right now everyone agrees synthetic audiences are a good idea.

The capital structure ladder for Auxos runs from **Teamshares** (employee-ownership rollup, an instrument no traditional fund could underwrite) at the top, through **Allbirds** (capital-efficient DTC the SaaS crowd dismissed) in the middle, down to the five-rounds-to-default-alive SaaS cap table at the bottom. Auxos pins to the bottom rung: no pricing on the site, the $29/$49/$79 tiers are demo content, no disclosed revenue, no LOIs, no creative instrument on offer — just a workspace-or-seat SaaS template indistinguishable from fifty other YC seed pitches selling LLM-wrapped enterprise tooling. There is nothing here a Slow DAO sleeve, a revenue-share structure, or a holdco frame would unlock; it's a plain SAFE into a plain SaaS in a plain category, and that's the criterion failing loudly.

On founder conviction the ladder runs **Drop.io** (the missionary arc I lived myself) at the top, **Pinterest** / Silbermann at mid, sanitized strategic-consulting-language at the floor; Auxos lands closer to the floor because the CEO is *literally* a BCG consultant who ran customer-research panels for Fortune 500 clients — there is no public writing from Ashton Daniel on why synthetic audiences fail in this vertical, no Twitter, no GitHub, no opinionated take you'd find before the meeting. Lu's seismic-wave-simulator work at Vorticity and the 40%-of-US-e-commerce simulation at Amazon Ads is the strongest single signal in the entire dossier, but execution chops are an input, not a contrarian belief. On founder-as-brand, the ladder runs **Marina Mogilko / LinguaTrip** (millions of YouTube subscribers, an asset I structured 5% of 30-year earnings against) at the top, **John Fish / Bookshelved** in the middle, no community at the bottom — Auxos sits at the floor with no Twitter accounts, no GitHub presence, no Product Hunt, no press, no Discord. On category sleeve, against **Solana** at the top and **Sublime Security** at mid, Auxos is the **canonical YC AI pitch** at the floor: this is AI-in-marketing, which is exactly what "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024) was written to deselect.

Forecast, 36 months: Auxos plateaus under $1M ARR, gets out-distributed by Evidenza's bootstrapped enterprise motion and out-funded by Aaru's $1B-valuation balance sheet, and exits as a sub-$30M acqui-hire into a martech research-ops player (Qualtrics, UserTesting, or a Sprig-adjacent buyer) — Lu and Wu get pulled into the acquirer's ML org, Daniel returns to a consulting-adjacent role. The mechanism is straightforward: the NN/g validity ceiling ("hypothesis generation and early-stage testing, not final design decisions") caps contract size precisely where Evidenza and Aaru already own the budget, and three pre-seed fencers without owned distribution don't out-enterprise-sell a profitable bootstrapped competitor sitting on the Salesforce logo.

The strongest opposing case is Lu. The bull frame: Amazon Ads simulation infrastructure modeling 40% of US e-commerce traffic plus Vorticity's seismic simulator is a genuinely rare technical depth at pre-seed, and if Auxos calibrates each customer's audience on their first-party CRM data the per-customer tuning becomes a switching-cost moat that the LLM-wrapper competitors can't replicate — this would track the **Sublime Security** known-exception pattern of buying technical depth in a crowded category before consolidation. Where this fails: my Sublime check was non-consensus *on entry* before the category had a $1B markup; the Auxos round is being priced *after* Aaru's December 2025 print, which means I'm paying consensus for execution depth rather than buying execution depth at a non-consensus price. The thing that flips this for me is one verifiable condition: signed paid pilots with three Fortune 500 marketing teams via Daniel's BCG network within six months, at a round size below the P26 batch median — give me proof the operator-friend channel converts and the price-discipline filter clears, and I'll re-read.
