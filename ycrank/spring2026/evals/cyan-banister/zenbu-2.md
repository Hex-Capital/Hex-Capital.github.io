# Zenbu -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Zenbu is Rob Pruzan, solo, building "the extensible IDE for coding agents" in the same YC P26 batch where Superset launched an "IDE to orchestrate 100+ AI coding agents" on Hacker News in May 2026 -- the dimensional signature is a clean-governance solo bet from a real distribution-bearing builder, dropped into the single most consensus category of this batch. A 222-star framework wrapper around `pi-mono` (a third-party open-source agent maintained by an external author) sitting downstream of Cursor at $2B ARR (TechCrunch, Feb 2026) and OpenAI's $3B Windsurf swallow (late 2025). The cleanest founder profile, in the loudest room.

The Niantic rung is John Hanke spending a decade on Keyhole and Google Earth before location-AR became a category -- prior-product trajectory at scale in the *exact* adjacent domain. Rob Pruzan co-authored React Scan -- 22k GitHub stars, 800k weekly installs per the YC page -- and sat on the Next.js core team at Vercel. That is a real developer-tooling shipment at scale, in the adjacent domain to coding-agent IDEs, and it's why Zenbu does not pattern-match to the ex-FAANG-PM AI-darling I pass on by slide three. Where Zenbu diverges from the Hanke rung is the autobiographical *moment* -- Hanke had Keyhole's satellite-imagery decade as his first contact with location data; Pruzan's first contact with coding-agent orchestration in the dossier is "I run multiple agents and verifying their work is hard," which is the same sentence every Zenbu competitor is saying. The distribution is real. The lived-problem origin is dev-tools-broadly, not coding-agent-specifically.

The consensus YC AI-darling I passed on -- six VCs chasing, $200B TAM slide, no contrarian aesthetic -- failed me on *Magically Weird* because the round priced certainty into a category everyone agreed on. Zenbu sits inside that exact failure mechanism: AI Code Tools Market $9.46B in 2026 projected to $22.2B by 2030 (Research and Markets), Cursor in talks at $50B pre (TechCrunch, Apr 2026), GitHub Copilot shipping agent mode in March 2026, and Superset pitching the *identical* sentence in the same batch. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing -- and the game everyone is visibly playing right now is "hackable IDE for coding agents." When the same dossier names five direct competitors and a same-batch YC twin, the round is not going to be underpriced; it is going to be priced as if Zenbu has already won, or already lost, and either way I do not get the asymmetry I came here for.

Rob Pruzan is solo founder, no co-founder ambiguity, no tiebreaker question. The HQ Trivia / GameCrush failure mode I screen for -- the 50/50 partnership with no designated CEO -- is structurally absent from Zenbu. This is the cleanest dimension in the dossier, and it is the dimension my framework asks the *least* of when other signals are weak. A solo CEO is necessary, not sufficient.

Mechanism analysis on capital-intensity: Zenbu is pure TypeScript IDE shell sitting on top of a third-party open-source agent (`github.com/badlogic/pi-mono`). Over 18 months, the compounding question is whether a plugin ecosystem (ZenbuJS at 222 stars today) accumulates switching costs faster than Cursor at $2B ARR or OpenAI/Windsurf can ship "hackable" as a feature. The Crusoe / SpaceX / Anduril sleeve of my portfolio compounds because regulatory or physical-world capital becomes a moat that time alone deepens. Zenbu has neither. The moat is a product choice -- "we are the hackable one" -- which any well-resourced incumbent can match in a sprint. This is the lane the polymath-rebuilds-it-in-a-quarter warning targets directly.

Counterfactual on physical-world verifiability: Zenbu *is* installable -- I could download it tonight, the way I once played Ingress before Niantic. For the score to flip a tier here, I would need to see the tactile-virality signal HQ Trivia gave me -- the moment I felt the pull before any metric existed. The dossier shows zenbu.js at 222 stars, Discord with no member count, no Product Hunt launch. React Scan at 800k weekly installs is Rob's reputation, not Zenbu's pull. Tactile but not yet viral.

Forecast, 36 months: Zenbu is acqui-hired by Vercel, Replit, or a second-tier AI-tooling vendor for under $75M, with Rob Pruzan landing as a principal engineer on a coding-agent surface. The mechanism is the high/low pairing -- clean solo CEO with real distribution gets absorbed cleanly, but the absence of frontier-weirdness means no independent compounding path against Cursor and OpenAI-Windsurf consolidating the category. The React Scan brand survives; Zenbu becomes a line on a résumé.

The bull case that stings: Rob Pruzan has 800k weekly installs already in his hands, which is the Postmates-style "consumer wrapper that's actually distribution infrastructure" pattern. If hackability resonates with the long-tail of devs who experience Cursor as closed -- the Neovim-for-agents thesis -- the plugin ecosystem could compound faster than incumbents can copy, because Cursor's revenue depends on *not* being hackable. And Pete Koomen as YC group partner is a serious dev-tools signal. The dismantle: the same-batch Superset launch (Hacker News, May 2026) tells me the contrarian wedge is already consensus inside YC P26, which inverts my "find them before consensus" filter. The defensibility comes from the framework, not from a lived founder origin in coding-agent orchestration specifically, which means a polymath rebuilds it in a quarter. One verifiable flip condition: if Zenbu.js crosses 5,000 GitHub stars with twenty external plugin authors shipping production plugins by mid-2027 -- the Neovim signature -- I re-engage. Otherwise the consensus category and the third-party Pi dependency win.
