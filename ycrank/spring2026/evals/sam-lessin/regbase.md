# Regbase -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Regbase is a Wizard-of-Oz domain-owner pitch trapped inside a consensus AI-legal-vertical wrapper -- Natalie Aresta-Katz spent years inside Paul, Weiss manually tracking ESG laws across dozens of countries and all U.S. states, which is exactly the operator-baseline I look for, but the rest of the dossier reads like the canonical 2026 YC pass example. No funding announcement beyond YC's standard ~$500K, no pricing innovation, no owned distribution, three people selling a SaaS subscription to Fortune 500 legal departments against FiscalNote ($84.8M ARR) and a $5.6B Legora. The asset is real; the wrapper is wrong.

Aresta-Katz's Paul, Weiss tenure is the closest Regbase comes to the **Fin** founding template -- two operators who ran a fully manual process before automating it -- and that earns Regbase real credit on founder-market fit because she was literally the regulatory-research labor inside an AmLaw 100 ESG practice tracking the multilingual sub-national bills the product now scrapes. Where Regbase diverges from that rung is the absence of any public artifact of conviction: no Twitter account found for Aresta-Katz, no essays, no opinionated thread on why FiscalNote's pipeline can't ingest non-English draft bills, no GitHub from CTO Ethan Shea despite a decade at Microsoft, Citadel, and Vercel. The Wizard-of-Oz credential is present in the biography; the missionary-temperament artifact that would let me underwrite a long non-linear arc is not on the public record.

The Pass Example I wrote about -- "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], post-YC, four tier-1s already in" -- is exactly the silhouette Regbase casts on the round-pricing dimension, with one mitigating note: there is no evidence of four tier-1s circling, no oversubscription language, no markup signal, which means Regbase is more likely commodity-priced at the YC standard than competitively bid up. That doesn't make it non-consensus -- it makes it generically-consensus, which is the exact zone where I cannot generate alpha from the SAFE cap because the asymmetry just isn't there. Regbase is not Sublime Security buying into a category before consolidation; it is Regbase landing in the legal-AI category exactly as Harvey and Legora reach unicorn escape velocity and start scope-creeping downward into regtech.

Mechanism on the capital-structure dimension: Regbase is an enterprise-sales SaaS where each Fortune 500 procurement cycle takes 6-12 months, the product hinges on building proprietary multilingual ingestion pipelines that demand engineering payroll well ahead of revenue, and the dossier shows no revenue, no logos, and no pricing page as of May 2026. That compounds over 18 months into the textbook five-rounds-to-default-alive cap table I de-rate -- not because the business can't work, but because the financing instrument on offer is a generic SAFE into a burn-then-raise enterprise SaaS, with no structural creativity (no consortium-licensing deal with a Big 4, no Paul, Weiss anchor-customer warrant, no royalty on documents priced per jurisdiction-month). Regbase needed a non-standard structure given the natural anchor-tenant dynamic and didn't build one.

Counterfactual on founder-as-brand: for Regbase to flip from the absent-evidence floor into a real score on this axis, Aresta-Katz would need to be publishing a "ESG Regulatory Weekly" newsletter with 5K+ in-house counsel subscribers, or Shea would need to be the public face of an open-source multilingual statute parser pulling stars on GitHub. Neither exists in the dossier -- @theregbase has no retrievable follower count, founder X accounts are unfindable or anonymous, and the GTM is referral-flow from a single law firm's alumni network. That is the opposite of what John Fish / Bookshelved bought me in January 2024, where the YouTube channel pre-existed the company by years and was the underlying asset.

Single-anchor on category sleeve: compare Regbase to **Ro**, the one Known Exception I grant for capital-intensive regulated-rail businesses. Ro earned the override because 50-state prescription licensing was a literal moat only VC dollars could clear -- the regulatory schleps were the asset. Regbase's "regulatory" exposure is the opposite direction: it sells software *about* regulation, not infrastructure *through* regulation, which puts it squarely in the vertical-AI sleeve I dismiss as "just more internet," not in the legitimate compliance-rail sleeve where Ro lives. Wrong kind of regulatory company for the exception to apply.

Forecast: in 36 months Regbase is either acquired by FiscalNote or Thomson Reuters for $25-50M as a multilingual sub-national tuck-in once Legora's downward scope-creep has cratered standalone pricing power, or it has pivoted into a narrower "ESG-bills-for-asset-managers" niche after the horizontal vision failed to differentiate against Harvey. ARR landing in the $1.5M-$4M zone, never reaching the Series-B legibility threshold a Slow check would need.

The bull case that stings: Aresta-Katz is the rare founder who genuinely owned the manual workflow -- not "former lawyer building legal AI" but the specific human who *was* the regulatory-research function at an AmLaw firm tracking ESG across jurisdictions Paul, Weiss bills out at $1,500/hour, and Shea built agentic infrastructure at Vercel which is the only relevant production credential for multi-agent crawl pipelines in 2026. That combination might be the rare vertical-AI pitch where the founder pair *can* outbuild Harvey on a narrow axis because Harvey isn't going to hire a Paul, Weiss ESG paralegal-turned-CEO and a Vercel staff engineer to chase the sub-national multilingual edge case. What dismantles it: the dossier shows zero third-party validation of the coverage claim, no benchmark, no named customer, and the GTM concentration on a single firm's referral network is the same pattern that killed every "ex-BigLaw builds legal AI" seed in the 2023-2024 cohort. **Flip condition:** if Regbase publishes signed contracts with two non-Paul-Weiss AmLaw 50 firms plus one Fortune 100 compliance team at $100K+ ACV within six months -- proving the wedge travels beyond the founder's alumni graph -- I'd reopen at the next tack.
