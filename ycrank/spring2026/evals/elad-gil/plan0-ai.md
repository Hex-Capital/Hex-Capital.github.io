# PLAN0 AI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

PLAN0 AI is what I'd call a quant-trained vertical SaaS in a market where the structural window cracked open eighteen months ago and the consensus has already poured in -- Arash Barati's Drake Real Estate Partners and Explora Ventures pedigree gives him unusually clean founder-market fit for a "Bloomberg of construction" pitch, but the product requires two independent technical miracles (sub-hour CV takeoff *and* 24-month trade-level cost forecasting "with high accuracy") in a category where Provision already raised $7M for the same wedge and Togal, Beam, and Kreo are in head-to-head benchmarks. That high CEO/low miracle-count pairing is the analytical signature here.

On nonobvious market timing -- where Harvey sits at the top of my ladder because the GPT-4 step function cracked legal AI after a decade of dead startups, and where the consumer social companies I pass on every batch sit at the bottom because no exogenous shift makes them feasible NOW -- PLAN0 AI lands closer to my Gusto rung than my Harvey rung. There IS a real vision-language threshold that made architectural plan parsing viable around 2024-2025, and Beck Technology's commentary on cost volatility through 2026 gives the forecasting layer a tailwind. But six AI estimating products were already benchmarked head-to-head in February 2026 (Robotics & Automation News) -- that is the definition of consensus catching up. PLAN0 AI is entering the open window late, not reading it early. Check the timestamp: this is not 2022 Harvey, this is 2024 Jasper.

On operator-scaler founder profile -- where Parker Conrad rebuilding Rippling after Zenefits anchors the high rung and equal co-founder structures with no decision-maker anchor the low rung -- PLAN0 AI sits in the low-middle. Arash Barati's REPE-quant background is the most interesting fact in the dossier and directly maps to the "quant finance inspired deep neural networks" positioning, which is rare authenticity. But Shervin Barati, the CTO, has no verifiable LinkedIn, GitHub, or domain footprint that I can pull from outside diligence (WebSearch May 2026), and the shared surname suggests a family co-founder pair with no stated CEO-decision-maker hierarchy. I'd want a 30-minute call with Shervin before I'd write a check; the dossier doesn't let me underwrite the technical leadership PLAN0 AI needs to deliver the forecast-accuracy claim.

On infrastructure-layer positioning, PLAN0 AI lands between my Stripe rung (the payments API everyone built on) and my Wish rung (a pure consumer marketplace with no platform). The "CoStar or Bloomberg of construction" framing is the right ambition -- a localized cost database compounding with every estimate is the only durable moat in this category -- but today PLAN0 AI is a vertical SaaS app delivering 30-minute estimates, not a data feed other businesses are integrating. The infrastructure thesis is aspirational, not architectural, and CoStar, Autodesk, and Trimble already own the underlying data layers PLAN0 AI would need to displace.

On single-miracle viability, PLAN0 AI fails the test the way most AI-applications-on-AI-applications fail it: the CV takeoff miracle is largely exogenous (Togal claims 98% floor-plan accuracy, so the technology is there), but the 24-month trade-level cost forecast in a non-stationary labor and commodity market is a separate, much harder quant miracle that the dossier asserts without third-party validation. That's two miracles -- closer to my Anduril rung than my Harvey rung -- and unlike Anduril, PLAN0 AI has no generational structural shift large enough to override the multi-miracle discount. On margin architecture, sitting between my Abridge rung (per-encounter outcome pricing) and my Wish rung (commodity margins), the absence of any pricing page on plan0.ai (WebFetch May 2026) means I'm guessing -- the CoStar/Bloomberg framing implies high-ACV data subscriptions, which would be excellent, but per-seat estimator pricing would put PLAN0 AI directly in the "selling seats while AI eliminates seats" trap I called out in my 2025 essay.

My 36-month forecast: PLAN0 AI raises a modest Series A led by a proptech or real-estate-focused fund on the strength of Arash Barati's network, reaches $1-3M ARR concentrated in developer and family-office customers who pay for the cost-intelligence layer, loses the general contractor and bid-side market to Provision and Togal, and ends up acqui-hired by CoStar, Procore, or Autodesk for the localized cost dataset rather than reaching venture-scale outcome. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: Arash's REPE network gets the company to revenue with the buyer profile he knows, but two miracles plus a contested market plus an unverifiable CTO ceiling out the scale path.

The strongest objection: Arash Barati's combination of quant-trained real-estate-PE pedigree and family-office distribution access is exactly the operator-founder profile I usually pay a premium for, and construction cost data is genuinely intractable enough that a winner could become a $5-10B data company on the CoStar comparable. The bull case is that PLAN0 AI's quant-finance framing is not marketing -- it's a real methodological edge no other entrant in the Robotics & Automation News benchmark has, and a Bloomberg-of-construction thesis with a quant at the helm is exactly the boring-but-critical infrastructure I look for. The dossier evidence that wins for me anyway: Provision already has $7M for the same wedge, the 24-month forecast accuracy claim has no third-party benchmark, and the CTO is unverifiable -- three independent reasons the bull mechanism doesn't fire. I'd flip my verdict on a single fact: a signed contract or LOI with a top-25 ENR general contractor or a paid pilot with a CoStar/Procore-tier data partner, demonstrating that PLAN0 AI has cleared the enterprise distribution bar that the better-funded peer hasn't.
