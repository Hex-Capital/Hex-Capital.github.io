# Kuli -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Kuli is a clean single-miracle workflow agent (multimodal video-LLMs are the miracle; the rest is GTM) bolted to a category where the revenue curve is conspicuously undisclosed -- Michael Hodara cites a Fortune 100 entertainment customer "shipping 3x more campaigns" but no ARR, no MoM, no logo count. That high/low pairing -- clean architecture, silent curve -- is the analytical signature, and in the 2026 AI cycle silence on the curve is the loudest data point in the dossier.

On single-miracle architecture, **Decagon** (2023) is the load-bearing analog: one assumption (LLMs are accurate enough for tier-1 customer support), everything downstream is product and enterprise GTM. Kuli sits in the same shape -- assume video-LLMs can reason about hook/pacing/audio/caption, and the remaining work is workflow ownership inside consumer-brand marketing orgs. Where Kuli diverges from Decagon is the *durability* of the miracle: Decagon's accuracy edge has been a multi-quarter moat because tuning + tool-use + escalation logic is hard to copy, while Kuli's "watches every video" capability rides directly on foundation-model APIs that CreatorIQ ($35K-$200K/yr per Syncly) can bolt on next quarter. The architecture is single-miracle; the miracle just isn't very proprietary.

The **Lyft** Series C pass mechanism was simple: in a market with a clear category leader and unfavorable share dynamics, the #2 captures a fraction of #1's economics regardless of execution quality. Run that pattern against Kuli's revenue-velocity story and it's structurally worse -- Kuli is competing against three priced incumbents (CreatorIQ, Aspire at $2,499/mo, Modash at $299/mo) for marketing-org budget and the dossier discloses zero MoM growth, zero ARR, zero pipeline numbers after five months of operation. The Fortune 100 anchor with Disney/Nivea/Havas name-drops is exactly the "false signal vs. just working" failure mode I flagged in TechCrunch (Nov 2025): enterprise pilot tourism reads identically to real buying behavior in a launch post. If Kuli were on a Harvey-curve, Hodara would have disclosed it.

Market re-opening for Kuli compounds or fails on a single mechanism over the next 18 months: do brand marketing teams treat "watches every video" as the wedge that displaces seat-based creator-database licensing, or do CreatorIQ and Aspire ship video-understanding inside their existing contracts before Kuli's data flywheel matures? The dossier's stated catalyst -- multimodal video models reaching production cost in 2024-2025 plus "brand-creator partnerships doubling in 2026" -- is real but symmetric: the same catalyst that enabled Kuli enables every incumbent's AI roadmap. This is not a defense-post-Ukraine asymmetric re-opening; it's a feature-availability shift that helps everyone in the category at once.

Counterfactual on Kuli's founder-shipping dimension: Hodara would need to be the **Perplexity** template -- something built between the YC partner meeting and the next one, with the product surface area expanding in publicly-traceable increments. What the dossier actually documents is operationally credible but pedigreed: EPFL Robotics MSc, AI-driven $20M ad-spend allocation at Aircall, $25M marketing budget context at Logitech, and Jonathan Hassan's 6 years of KPMG M&A advisory. That profile maps to the **Brex** (2017) operational-CEO archetype more than the Aravind-ships-between-meetings archetype, and the dossier names zero shipped artifacts -- no prior product, no side project, no public repo. To flip this dimension into Invest range, Kuli would need a named prior shipped product or visible weekly product cadence; "10 years of side hustles together" without naming one is the absence of evidence I default against.

Index-position parallel for Kuli is **Decagon** (2023), not Stripe. Decagon owns the workflow path every customer-support team in a post-LLM world has to integrate against; Kuli is attempting the same wedge for creator marketing. The problem is Decagon entered a wide-open sub-market (per my own *AI Market Clarity*, Nov 2025), while creator marketing has three incumbents with priced distribution and named consumer-brand contracts already in place. Kuli isn't on my open-market list -- accounting, compliance, sales agents, security -- and "AI coworker for marketing teams" is precisely the kind of horizontal framing that has produced ten me-too pitches per YC batch.

Thirty-six month forecast: Kuli reaches $3-8M ARR as a mid-market consumer-brand tool, then gets acquired by CreatorIQ or Aspire for $30-80M as a video-understanding feature buy once those incumbents finish their own multimodal builds. The mechanism producing that outcome is the high/low signature itself -- the architecture is clean enough to ship a real product and land Fortune 100 logos (hence not zero), but the miracle commoditizes faster than Kuli's per-brand data flywheel can compound (hence the ceiling).

The strongest bull case against that forecast is that Kuli is shaped exactly like **Decagon** (2023) at the same stage -- a workflow agent in a recently-catalyzed AI category with a named anchor customer producing a concrete outcome metric ("3x campaigns") and operationally-credible founders. Decagon was also pre-revenue-disclosure when I wrote the check, and I didn't penalize it. The dismantle is that Decagon's category was on my open-market list and Kuli's isn't, Decagon's miracle was *task accuracy* (genuinely hard to replicate) and Kuli's is *API access to video models* (genuinely not), and Decagon's founder shipped products at scale at Niantic before Decagon while Hodara's prior work is internal AI systems inside a marketing org. **Verifiable flip condition**: if Hodara discloses $1M+ ARR from 2+ named Fortune 500 brands within 6 months *or* a credible Series A markup from a named growth fund, I'll write the check at consensus -- the "when consensus pays" pattern is exactly when I lean in. Until then this is a Neutral, and Neutral at angel stage is a pass.
