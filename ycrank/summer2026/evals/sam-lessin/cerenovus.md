# Cerenovus -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Cerenovus is the 2026 YC AI-agent-for-enterprise dossier I described in my own Pass Example, with the cosmetic twist that the three founders are Harvard sophomores instead of ex-FAANG PMs — three undergrads (Jonathan Waldorf, Lucas Baur, Oliver Moreland) pitching a markdown knowledge graph that "infers inefficiencies" against an incumbent (Glean) already at $300M ARR. The signature is brutal: the dimension I weight hardest (price/non-consensus) is where Cerenovus is most exposed, and the dimension I rely on as a tiebreaker for AI plays (founder-as-brand) is where the dossier is emptiest. That pairing is not a "promising rough edge" — it is the modal SAFE I get pitched fifty times a batch.

Solana at $0.05/coin in 2018 is the high rung on the price-discipline ladder because the entire category had been left for dead by generalist VC — I was bidding into an empty room. Cerenovus is the opposite room: Glean has raised $765M, Notion AI ships at $10/user/month, Guru just launched MCP in March 2026, Microsoft Copilot is being force-distributed through M365, and Slack AI is bundled. The dossier doesn't disclose round size, and that silence is itself the tell — when a Harvard-dropout founding team launches an "AI knowledge graph for executives" out of S26, the prior on a competitive, consensus-priced SAFE at YC-batch-median terms is overwhelming. There's no evidence any tier-1 visibly passed; there's no evidence the round is structured below the AI-agent comp line. Cerenovus diverges from Solana in the only way that matters at entry: I am not the only bid, and the category is not unloved.

The lowest rung on the founder-as-brand ladder is my own Pass Example — the founder with zero pre-funding public writing and no community footprint, where the only acquisition channel left is paid sales. That failure mechanism is the Cerenovus dossier verbatim: Waldorf's LinkedIn says "Harvard Dropout | Cerenovus," Baur's says "Harvard '28 | Finance, Economics," Moreland has no verified LinkedIn at all, none of the three has a Twitter/X account surfaced in search, none has a GitHub repo surfaced in search, and there is no Product Hunt launch, no press, no newsletter, no podcast, no Discord. Cerenovus is selling to executives — the buyer most resistant to cold outbound — with a founder set that has zero owned distribution and zero published thinking on the operations-inference problem. The Slow Creator Fund exists precisely because I believe distribution-on-the-cap-table is a step-function; Cerenovus has none of it.

On capital structure, the eighteen-month mechanism is unforgiving: Cerenovus needs to ingest documents/Slack/email/meetings, build per-customer knowledge graphs, and run inference workloads on top — that is a GPU-heavy, forward-deployed-engineer-heavy cost structure, which is why two of the three current job posts are in-person SF Founding Engineer roles with "paid room and board." That burn profile against pilots-not-revenue means Cerenovus is structurally a multi-round company in a category where Glean has already raised six rounds totaling $765M to defend the same workflow. There is no employee-equity twist, no royalty instrument, no holdco — just a SAFE on the path to a SaaS Series A I have no edge in.

The counterfactual on founder conviction: for Cerenovus's 7-ish score to flip into Invest range, I'd need to find that Waldorf had been publicly writing about enterprise-ops inference for two years before dropping out, or that one of the three had run an actual operations role inside a 500-person company and was building from scar tissue. Neither exists. What does exist is a QuEra quantum-computing internship and a Harvard Financial Analysts Club analyst line — credentials that are impressive in absolute terms but orthogonal to the C-suite operations-inference buyer. The Harvard-dropout signal is real and I give it some credit; it is not the same as the missionary-temperament-for-non-linear-time signal that took Venmo through "so many turns of the crank."

On category sleeve, the single-anchor parallel is the Pass Example itself rather than any portfolio anchor — because Cerenovus does not sit in any sleeve I'm convicted on. It is not crypto with on-chain economic activity. It is not DTC consumer with margin. It is not employee-equity rollup. It is not creator-economy infrastructure. It is enterprise AI agent SaaS, the exact archetype where I've said publicly that "AI is just more internet" and "AI is kind of like mobile. It's not disruptive." Cerenovus's J&J-MedTech name collision on top of this is a minor procurement headache, but the real problem is that the underlying asset Cerenovus is building has no sleeve fit and no mispricing edge.

My 36-month forecast for Cerenovus: pivot into a narrow vertical (most likely legal-ops or PE-portfolio-ops knowledge graphs where Glean's horizontal indexing is weakest) and limp to roughly $300K-$1.5M ARR before either shutting down or getting acqui-hired by a mid-tier knowledge-management vendor — not Glean, not Notion, because the J&J "Cerenovus" trademark collision makes the brand a liability in any strategic acquirer's portfolio review. The mechanism producing this is incumbency: Glean compounds distribution faster than Cerenovus can compound graph quality, and the Harvard founder team has neither the operator-friend channel to skip the line nor the owned-community distribution to bypass paid sales.

The strongest bull case against my Pass: Cerenovus's markdown-graph + agent-native architecture is a genuine technical bet that Glean's retrieval-first architecture cannot retrofit — if MCP-style agent orchestration becomes the dominant enterprise pattern in 2027, a graph-first inference layer is a structurally better substrate than Glean's search-first one, and three technically strong Harvard physics/EE founders are exactly the team that could ship it. I steel-man that and then return to my Unstated Patterns: pure-software AI plays without operator-friend sourcing, without a non-consensus price, and without founder distribution have a brutal failure rate in my own portfolio when I've taken them, and none of my Known Exceptions apply — Cerenovus is not a Robinhood-style early entry that pre-dated consensus, not a Pinterest-style operator-friend deal, and not a Ro-style regulatory moat where capital intensity is the moat. The one verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Cerenovus publicly discloses three named Fortune 500 paid pilots converted to ARR above $500K within twelve months, at a SAFE cap below $15M post — that combination would prove both that the technical bet is differentiating and that I'm getting a non-consensus entry price. Absent that, Cerenovus is a pass.
