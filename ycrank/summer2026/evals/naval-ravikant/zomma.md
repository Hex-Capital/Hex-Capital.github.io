# Zomma -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Zomma sells Computer Use Agents to hedge funds via a "/call" demo CTA on zommalabs.com — a domain-native founder team (Varun Arumugam ex-JPMorgan, Cashel Fitzgerald ex-Voyant) building a permissioned enterprise product. That pairing is Zomma's whole signature: the specific-knowledge leg reads real, and the leverage architecture reads wrong. The business is labor-and-capital leverage sold through SDR-shaped motions to SEC-audited buyers, dressed in an "Agent Trust Framework" that is marketing copy until a SOC 2 report exists. This is the shape of company I have been passing on since **Venture Hacks** (2007-10), not the shape I have been funding.

Twitter (2007) is the leverage-architecture rung I measure against — media that anyone posts, zero marginal cost, no gatekeeper between the founder and the next million users. Zomma inverts every axis of that mechanism: distribution is a booked demo with a hedge fund's vendor-risk committee, replication requires custodian-portal integration and audit trails, and the growth motion is founder-led enterprise sales into 30+-headcount ops teams (AutomationEdge, 2026). Zomma's own site names Claude as a supported agent while Anthropic simultaneously ships 10 finance-agent templates and a $1.5B JV with Blackstone/Goldman/H&F (Fortune, May 2026) — meaning the "code" underneath Zomma is rented from a vendor that is now a direct competitor at the workflow layer. Permissionless code compounds without a counterparty; Zomma requires two — the buyer and the model provider — and both can revoke.

Perplexity (2022) is the single-anchor parallel on specific knowledge — Aravind Srinivas brought a Berkeley retrieval PhD to a category before ChatGPT scale. Zomma's founders bring a lighter but real version of the same mechanic: Arumugam ran a prior YC-backed exit reportedly at $10-12M in sales and worked JPMorgan; Fitzgerald built at Voyant (financial planning software) and Modern Intelligence on ML. This is not "ex-FAANG PM identified market opportunity" — Zomma's team has touched the reconciliation and KYC workflows they now automate, and the prior-founder scar tissue matters. It does not reach the Srinivas rung because nothing in the dossier names a shipped artifact in computer-use-agent research; the edge is operator empathy, not non-replicable model IP.

Single-sided SaaS to hedge funds — that is the failure mechanism on the network dimension, the one I keep off the ledger because the Nth customer does not make the (N-1)th more valuable. Zomma's product is a per-fund agent operating one fund's portals under one fund's approval chain; the audit trail for Citadel does not train the agent for Millennium in any way the dossier names. There is a thin argument that workflow-specific training data from custodian portals could compound, but Zomma discloses no customers, no retention, and no shared learning layer — and hedge funds are the last buyers on earth who will consent to cross-tenant data pooling. The dimension is structurally absent, not merely unproven.

Zomma's market has pivot-room if you squint — GUI agents for regulated back offices could jump from hedge funds to insurance ops to broker-dealers — but every pivot lands in another SOC-2-gated, sales-cycle-heavy vertical, which is not the maneuvering room **Venture Hacks** (2007-10) had in mind. Eighteen months of compounding here looks like: Zomma signs 3-6 named funds, Anthropic ships template #11 that eats the KYC workflow, and Zomma's differentiation collapses to "the Trust Framework" — a wrapper thesis. The counterfactual that would flip criterion 1 to mid-tier is a shipped open-source computer-use framework with organic GitHub contribution — the **Stack Overflow** (2008) or **Replit** (April 2023) pattern of code shipping before equity story. The dossier names no GitHub for either founder.

Founder trio reads clean — prior YC exit for Arumugam, no partner litigation, no self-promotion about honesty, integrity leg is quiet in the way I prefer. I would take a monthly call with these founders for ten years without flinching. The problem is that the 10-year-partner test is necessary and not sufficient; **Pinterest** (~2010) had it and I still passed for the wrong reason, and Zomma does not have the compensating leverage-architecture pass that would make me override my own framework the way Pinterest should have.

Forecast, 36 months: Zomma reaches $2-5M ARR across a handful of mid-sized asset managers, then gets absorbed at $30-80M by a fund administrator like **SS&C** or **iCapital** (cited FundCount, 2026) that needs the workflow data plus the team, before Anthropic's finance-agents JV eats the standalone case. The mechanism is straightforward: services-vs-software cannibalization plus foundation-model vendor encroachment closes the window before Zomma reaches escape velocity, and the acquirer buys the ops-team-empathy that the model vendor cannot replicate cheaply.

The strongest bull case is the **Perplexity** (2022) exception applied to Zomma — deep specific knowledge plus AI-cycle timing waives the single-sided-network gate, and legacy custodian portals genuinely will never expose APIs, which is a structural moat Anthropic templates cannot cross without buyer-side integration work. That case stings because it is half-right: the API-refusal moat is real, and Arumugam's prior-exit-plus-JPMorgan combination is a stronger founder-market-fit signal than the dossier's flat presentation suggests. It fails because Perplexity had permissionless product distribution via API and app, and Aravind's Berkeley retrieval PhD was category-defining specific knowledge — Zomma has neither. The verifiable condition that flips me to Invest: Zomma ships an open-source computer-use agent framework that accumulates 1,000+ GitHub stars organically within six months, converting the business from labor-leverage-sold-by-demo to code-leverage-distributed-by-developers. Absent that, this is a competent enterprise SaaS bet for a different council member's ledger.
