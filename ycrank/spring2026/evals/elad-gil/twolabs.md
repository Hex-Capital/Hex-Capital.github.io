# Twolabs -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Twolabs is a 2-person Cornell+Georgia Tech team building "Tobi," a semi-humanoid for nursing-home ADL assistance, with zero disclosed pilots and a recent pivot off an ag-robotics dataset thesis. The signature is brutal: a real catalyst (humanoid component-cost collapse, 1X opening a 100K-unit/yr California factory, Fourier's GR-3 at CES 2026) wrapped around a multi-miracle architecture that violates the exact pattern I learned in 2015 with **Color Genomics**. Twolabs is not workflow infrastructure on top of a hard thing; Twolabs *is* the hard thing.

**Anduril** (2022) is the right comparable on catalyst, and it's exactly why Twolabs doesn't clear the bar. Anduril's catalyst was specific (Ukraine in a 24-month window) and the founders sat in a workflow position adjacent to existing hardware pathways. Twolabs cites a real macro catalyst -- VLM/world-model maturity plus actuator-cost declines -- and the dossier's RBC-cited "$9T humanoid opportunity" is not phantom. But the catalyst that matters for Twolabs is *eldercare-specific*, and the dossier names none: no CMS reimbursement code change, no named nursing-home operator RFP, no post-COVID staffing-mandate shift cited. Diligent has worked the hospital-logistics adjacency since 2017 at ~$68M raised and has not converted that into resident ADL deployments; that's the tell that the eldercare humanoid window is not yet open the way defense was post-Ukraine.

**Harvey** (2023) is what real revenue velocity looks like at the angel stage -- "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) -- and the Twolabs dossier is the photo-negative. The line in my Tim Ferriss Show #863 framework was unambiguous: "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." Twolabs has no revenue signal, no pilot site, no LOI, no waitlist number, no Product Hunt rank. The pre-seed calibration says don't penalize absence of metrics at this stage, and I won't penalize as if this were a Series A -- but hardware-robotics for regulated care environments is precisely where I want a named operator (Brookdale, Atria, Sunrise) on a paid pilot before I underwrite. The absence is structural, not stage-defaulted.

Sardor Rahmatulloev's shipping history is the most interesting line in the Twolabs dossier and the one that most resembles the **Perplexity** (2022) cold-inbound pattern that earned my check -- Cornell Combat Robotics is a real artifact, not a credential. But the mechanism that made Aravind a buy was *recurring* shipping between conversations on the *current* product; Twolabs shows a thesis swap from egocentric-video datasets for ag-robotics (per startupbase.uz and the shakhzodismoilov Substack) to an eldercare semi-humanoid named Tobi, with no public artifacts of Tobi in the wild. Compounded over 18 months, the question is whether Sardor and Danyal Ahmad iterate Tobi weekly with facility feedback loops, or whether the pivot signals thesis-shopping. Meta-departure-after-three-months is either decisiveness or restlessness; the dossier can't tell me which.

Twolabs's distribution position would need to flip an entire tier to reach the **Stripe** (2010) index-like archetype, and it can't from here. To flip, Twolabs would need to be the *software/data layer* every humanoid maker integrates against for eldercare deployment -- think the "scribe layer for facilities" play -- rather than the vendor selling a single semi-humanoid SKU against 1X NEO, Fourier GR-3, and Labrador's retriever. The dossier positions Twolabs as a vertical hardware product competing for facility procurement budgets against players with $50M-$1B in capital. That's a #2-in-winner-take-all setup adjacent to the **Lyft** Series C pass logic, not a basket-on-the-category position.

The **Color Genomics** (2015) Known Exception is the one I checked hardest before writing this, because it superficially rhymes: a workflow company on top of a hard-tech substrate, vertical into a regulated buyer. It doesn't apply to Twolabs. Color worked because the chemistry layer (commodity sequencing) was *already solved* and the miracle was workflow/data on top. Twolabs requires (1) safe physical manipulation of frail elderly bodies -- unsolved at any humanoid company, (2) hardware unit economics from a 2-person team versus 1X's 100K-unit factory, (3) facility-pilot procurement and liability sign-off in a regulated environment, and (4) a behavior/interaction data moat that doesn't yet exist. That's four independent miracles compounding, which is the exact NFX *Unusual Signs* anti-pattern.

My 36-month forecast on Twolabs: dead or pivoted by Q2 2029 to a humanoid behavior/data layer selling into 1X/Fourier/Figure as the customers, with ARR under $500K and no Series A from a tier-1 robotics fund. The mechanism is straightforward -- the capital gap to ship safe ADL hardware against $50M-$1B-funded peers closes faster than Twolabs can raise on a 2-person team with no pilot data, and the natural escape hatch reverts to where Sardor started: the dataset/perception infrastructure thesis from the prior ag-robotics product.

The strongest bull case against my pass leans on the **Anduril** Known Exception -- a market peers misread plus founders who can ship hardware-adjacent software fast enough to outrun better-capitalized incumbents. The sting is that Sardor's Meta Superintelligence Labs background plus Danyal's Salesforce video-model fine-tuning are exactly the VLM-on-hardware skill stack that would let a small team eat the perception layer while bigger players burn cash on actuators. I don't buy it. Anduril's founders had shipped Palantir's forward-deployed muscle and Oculus to consumer scale before the Series D check; Sardor left Meta after three months and Danyal's prior role was an IC video-model job -- neither has shipped a product to scale independently, which is the disqualifier from my Hustle Commons framework. The verifiable condition that flips Twolabs from pass to a second meeting: three named, paid nursing-home pilots (Brookdale-class operator or equivalent) inside 12 months, OR an explicit repositioning of Twolabs as the perception/behavior SDK that 1X and Fourier license. Without one of those, this is a science project wearing an AI-cycle jacket, and I'll skate for the puck elsewhere.
