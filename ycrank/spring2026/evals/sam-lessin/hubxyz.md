# Hub.xyz -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Here's what jumps out immediately: one of the co-founders is now CEO of a completely different company. Léonie Nguyen, listed as co-founder and CBO, took the CEO job at Meey Group — a Vietnamese proptech company — in October 2025. Five months before the YC batch. She's not even listed on the YC page anymore. I've said it a hundred times: I watch people across multiple ventures to understand who they really are. What I learn when a co-founder takes a CEO role at an unrelated company during your YC batch is that the team is dissolving. Synthetic co-founder relationships are one of the strongest negative signals I track. Armin and Tim may be plenty capable, but this isn't a founding team that's been forged through shared suffering over multiple cycles. It's a team that's already losing people before the product has enterprise revenue.

Now, the mispricing question — and there isn't one. DePIN for AI training data is one of the most crowded intersections in the crypto-AI Venn diagram right now. Six hundred plus DePIN projects. Grass is already at roughly a billion dollar valuation doing the exact same thing — distributed bandwidth sharing converted into AI training data — with $17.5M in the bank. Nodepay has 1.5M active users and $7M raised. Meanwhile, on the demand side, Scale AI is printing $870M in annual revenue at a $29B valuation and every Fortune 500 company's AI team already knows their phone number. Where exactly is the asymmetric bet here? When I seeded Solana, every smart person in crypto had written off non-Ethereum L1s. The risk was in the price — four cents per SOL. Hub.xyz is operating at the precise intersection of two categories that every VC in San Francisco is salivating over. There is no mispricing. The consensus already loves AI data AND DePIN. If anything, this space is overpriced.

The primitive test is where it really falls apart for me. Hub.xyz calls itself "the API for real-world training data." That's a service description, not a primitive. Venmo created peer-to-peer mobile payments as a behavior that didn't exist. Solana was a fundamentally new consensus mechanism — a different architectural bet on how blockchains work. Hub.xyz is a data collection pipeline that uses someone else's protocol (Ethereum smart contracts) to coordinate incentives for bandwidth sharing. The DePIN concept itself is arguably a primitive, but Hub.xyz didn't create it — they're building an application on top of it. And their competitors got there first with more capital. I invest in protocols, not applications running on protocols. This is an application.

Let me steelman this for a second, because I want to be honest about what would make this a great bet. If the regulatory crackdown on web scraping accelerates — and there's real momentum there with all the lawsuits against AI companies in 2024 and 2025 — then consented, distributed data collection becomes structurally necessary rather than optional. In that world, whoever owns the largest network of opted-in contributors with the best data structuring wins. Hub.xyz's 500K nodes plus SwissBorg's 1M+ KYC-verified community would be a genuine supply-side moat. And the "API for real-world training data" positioning, if executed well, becomes the clean alternative that enterprise compliance teams demand. That's the bull case. But notice what it requires: a specific regulatory outcome going a specific direction. I've been explicit about this — if your business model needs regulators to cooperate for you to win, I don't like the bet. "I don't think the cities are going to let the scooter companies be profitable" — same pattern, different category. And even if the regulatory thesis plays out perfectly, Grass is better positioned to capture it. Being the third mover in a consensus category with a regulatory dependency isn't contrarian investing. It's hope.

The AI kill test lands somewhere in the middle but skews negative. The 500K node network is a real physical asset — you can't replicate that with software alone, and that's the kind of thing I usually like (think Metropolis with parking infrastructure). But the underlying data is from public internet sources. It's not proprietary. Scale AI could build an incentivized node network tomorrow if they wanted to — they have the enterprise relationships, the data quality infrastructure, and $15.9B in cumulative funding. The token layer creates some network stickiness, sure, but it also creates regulatory fragility. The $HUB token, the TGE, the airdrop campaigns — these are SEC-shaped liabilities, not moats. And the 500K "users" aren't really users in any meaningful product sense — they're speculators earning IQ Points redeemable for tokens. Remove the token incentive and ask how many of those 500K contributors remain. That's your real user base.

Pass. This is a consensus play in a consensus category with dissolving co-founder dynamics, well-funded competitors doing the identical thing, and no primitive creation. The market is not wrong about AI training data or DePIN — it's extremely right, and the prices reflect it. There's no asymmetry left to capture.
