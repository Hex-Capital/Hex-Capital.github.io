# Revnu -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Revnu is a two-UK-kid bet where the cofounder bond carries almost the entire signature and the category is the weakest part of the page: George Jefferson and Art Freebrey met at 14, co-bootstrapped three software businesses to £100K+ each during university, and dropped out the same day to join YC Spring 2026 — that biographical fact registers harder for me than the "autonomous growth agents" framing on revnu.com.

The Reddit dorm-roommate pattern with Steve Huffman and Alexis Ohanian is what I keep reaching for when I read the Revnu cofounder section. The mechanism that mattered at **Reddit** (2005) was not the link-aggregator idea — that idea looked dumb — it was that two people who had already chosen each other long before the startup existed could fight through years of being wrong about what to build. George and Art met at 14, shipped three revenue-generating products together before applying, and resigned from university on the same day; the only thing missing from a top-rung placement is that the Huffman/Ohanian pair came in with Aaron Swartz's RSS-spec hacker pedigree attached, and Revnu's dossier shows no GitHub for either founder. That absence keeps Revnu just below the HIGH rung but well above the MID — and on this dimension, it is the closest thing in the dossier to a pre-funding artifact.

The MBA-team AI-wrapper I described in my Pass Example fails on schlep because the work is pure software with no offline grind, no compliance, no manual integration the founders must personally do. Revnu's surface looks adjacent to that failure pattern — orchestrating Meta, LinkedIn, Reddit, TikTok ad accounts is software-on-software. But the dossier's own risk section is the tell: LinkedIn began rate-limiting Artisan's Ava-driven activity in Q1 2026, which means the actual day-to-day of running Revnu is going to be a platform-policy whack-a-mole that softer founders will flinch from. It is not **Coinbase** (2012)'s regulatory schlep — there is no FinCEN equivalent here — but it is a real grind that the well-funded incumbent (11x at $76M raised, Artisan at $36.5M) cannot brute-force past with capital. I score it middle of the range because it is schlep-adjacent, not schlep-core.

On animal resourcefulness pre-funding, Revnu's signal compounds in a specific way over 18 months. Three bootstrapped businesses each crossing £100K+ in revenue while George and Art were still university students is not a demo video on Hacker News — it is shipped revenue, three times over, before the YC check. The $500K standard SAFE matters less for Revnu than for the median S26 company because the duo has already demonstrated the ability to convert code into cash without institutional money. Drew Houston shipped a demo at **Dropbox** (2007); George and Art shipped P&Ls. Where Revnu sits below the Houston rung is that the £100K businesses are not Revnu — they are prior products, so the resourcefulness signal applies to the founders' general operating system, not to this specific product.

The organic-idea test would flip from a mid score to a top score for Revnu if the dossier explicitly stated that George and Art built the first version of Revnu to run growth on their own three prior £100K bootstrapped businesses. The dossier comes close — "[Inferred]: Both founders cite that across the prior bootstrapped businesses, 'growth was the bottleneck'" — but the inferred tag is doing real work, and I read [Inferred] as weaker than a direct founder quote. If George tells me in person that they wrote Revnu v0 to handle SEO for their previous startup and it 3x'd traffic, that is **Dropbox** (2007)'s USB-stick moment and the score goes up a tier. Until then it is plausible but not pinned.

Patrick Collison won Ireland's 41st Young Scientist for Lisp work in 2005 before **Stripe** (2010); Art Freebrey won the xAI London Hackathon before Revnu. The parallel is not perfect — a single hackathon win is thinner than a national prize — but the shape is the same: a young technical founder who showed up in a competitive arena before the company existed and beat the field. The dossier's "No public repos found" for both founders is the part that keeps Revnu off the top rung; hackers leave GitHub trails, and I notice when they don't. Three shipped revenue-generating products partly compensates because shipping is the harder test than starring on GitHub.

My 36-month forecast on Revnu: the duo abandons the omni-channel "single intelligence layer" framing within 12 months because no two-person team can out-execute 11x's $76M on outbound and Jasper's $125M on content simultaneously, narrows to one wedge — most likely autonomous ad-ops for early-stage SaaS, since that is the workflow they personally ran on their three £100K businesses — reaches $1-3M ARR by month 30, and either gets acquired by HubSpot/Attentive for talent + product around month 36 or raises a Series A from a YC-adjacent fund on the strength of the cofounder pairing rather than the category. The mechanism producing this is the high/low signature: an exceptionally bonded two-person team in a category where the right move is wedge-and-survive, not platform-and-conquer.

The strongest bear case against Revnu is my own Founder Mode (September 2024) red flag: the website pitch — "autonomous agents take ownership of multiple growth channels" — reads exactly like the manager-mode framing I explicitly rejected, where the founder's job is to hand off and let the agents (the "good people") do their jobs. The disputed 11x ARR episode (claimed $14M, actual ~$3M per TechCrunch) and the G2 collapse in the AI-SDR category prove buyers in 2026 are already burned. If Revnu is selling the same dream with a thinner team and 1/150th the capital, the bear case wins. What dismantles it is that George and Art are not pitching a sales team replacement to enterprises — they are pitching technical founders like themselves, who they were six months ago, and the organic-idea filter from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) still loads. The single verifiable condition that flips me to pass: if in the YC interview George cannot name a specific dollar figure of paid ad spend they personally managed for their prior three businesses, the organic claim collapses and Revnu becomes the AI-wrapper I pass on.
