## The Idea

**Problem:** Retail insurance agents struggle to place "hard-to-place" commercial risks — accounts that standard admitted carriers decline (e.g., cat-exposed property, distressed cyber, contractor liability). Today they route these to wholesale brokers/MGAs, where typical turnaround is multi-day. Customer testimonial on company site: "I sent them a contractor's property submission at 4pm on a Thursday. Had a quote back with subjectivities the next morning. That used to take a week" (hedgespecialty.com).

**Approach:** Hedge operates as a specialty placement shop / wholesale broker for E&S risks. Operating entity is "Hedge Specialty dba Taven Insurance Services LLC," CA license #6018729, NPN 22154671 (hedgespecialty.com). Lines covered: Commercial Property, General & Excess Liability, Inland Marine, Commercial Auto, Product Liability, Cyber & Tech E&O, Miscellaneous E&O, Medical Malpractice, Management Liability, Employment Practices, Environmental & Pollution (hedgespecialty.com). Workflow: flexible intake (email/ACORD/screenshots), placement curation, real-time file visibility, bind delivery (hedgespecialty.com). YC tagline labels the company "AI native" (YC company page); the website does not detail specific AI features publicly.

**Differentiation:** Site claims 30-minute median first response, 30+ direct carrier relationships, named brokers per account, 3× hit ratio on hard risks vs. industry baseline (hedgespecialty.com). Differentiates from existing wholesalers (CRC, Amwins, RT Specialty) on speed and from AI-underwriting SaaS (Federato, Cytora, Sixfold) by being a licensed placement shop rather than a tool.

**Business Model:** [Inferred]: Commission-based revenue as a licensed surplus-lines broker/MGA — standard E&S commissions are typically 10–15% of premium. No public pricing page; commissions are paid by carriers, not insureds. Job posting: Founding Underwriter $125K–$200K + 0.25%–1.50% equity (YC company page).

**TAM/SAM:** US surplus lines premium $90.3B through year-end 2025 across 15 stamping-office states, +7.8% YoY (Insurance Journal, Jan 2026). Global E&S market $90.73B in 2024, projected $93.37B in 2025 (Global Growth Insights, 2024 via search snippet). E&S premium growth +9.7% through Q3 2025 vs +13.5% prior-year (Insurance Journal, Feb 2026). [Inferred]: SAM is the wholesale-brokered slice of US E&S — likely tens of billions in commissionable premium given typical wholesale commission structures.

**GTM / Distribution:** Site states 140+ retail agencies in 36 states as of 2026 (hedgespecialty.com). [Inferred]: Direct outbound to retail agents and submission-driven inbound via word-of-mouth in agent networks; standard wholesale-broker GTM.

## Defensibility

- **Regulatory barrier:** Holds CA surplus-lines license #6018729, NPN 22154671 (hedgespecialty.com). Surplus-lines licensure required state-by-state; non-trivial onboarding cost.
- **Carrier appointments:** Claims 30+ direct carrier markets (hedgespecialty.com). Carrier appointments are relationship-driven and slow to acquire.
- **Data:** [Inferred]: Submission flow could become a proprietary dataset of declined/hard-to-place risks usable to train pricing/triage models, but this is unproven at this stage.

**Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbent wholesale brokers (CRC, Amwins, RT Specialty) operate on legacy email/ACORD workflows; rebuilding ingestion/triage on AI risks cannibalizing existing FTE-heavy underwriting teams. AI-underwriting SaaS vendors (Federato/Cytora/Sixfold) sell into carriers and lack the broker license to disintermediate the placement.

**Commoditization risk:** [Inferred]: A funded wholesale broker (e.g., Newfront, or a CRC-funded skunkworks) could replicate the AI-intake layer. The licensure + carrier appointments slow but do not block this; the structural moat is modest at pre-seed.

- **Incumbent wholesalers** (CRC, Amwins, RT Specialty): Multi-billion-dollar specialty wholesalers; Hedge competes against them on speed and AI-driven workflow. Funding/ARR: large private/public players; figures not retrieved.

**Why now:** [Inferred]: (1) US surplus-lines premium has crossed $90B and posted 7+ years of double-digit growth before moderating in 2025 (Carrier Management, Sept 2025; Insurance Journal, Feb 2026), expanding the addressable wholesale-brokerage commission pool. (2) The "AI exclusion wave" is forcing AI-liability and emerging risks into the E&S market, mirroring early-cyber insurance dynamics (Insurance Intel Substack via search snippet). (3) LLM-based document/ACORD ingestion has matured to where unstructured submissions (email, screenshots) can be triaged programmatically — Sixfold (founded 2023) and Cytora's commercial offerings are evidence the threshold has been crossed.

## Key Risks

**Prior-venture overlap with Fernstone:** Luke Button is publicly listed as co-founder/CEO of Fernstone (YC F25) and as founder of Hedge (YC S26). Fernstone's website remains active per search snippets. Public sources do not disclose whether Fernstone has been wound down, pivoted into Hedge, or operates in parallel. Investor diligence should clarify the relationship and any cap-table / IP / time-allocation implications.

**Carrier-relationship dependency:** Site claims 30+ direct carrier markets (hedgespecialty.com); a wholesale brokerage's economics collapse if carriers withdraw appointments after losses or if they cap binding authority. No public data on whether Hedge has delegated underwriting authority (MGA) or is purely brokering — these are very different risk profiles.

**Regulatory / compliance burden:** Operating as Taven Insurance Services LLC under CA surplus-lines license #6018729 and NPN 22154671 (hedgespecialty.com) requires state-by-state licensing as the company scales beyond 36 states. AI-driven recommendations in regulated insurance placement face NAIC model-bulletin scrutiny on bias/explainability.

**Soft-market headwind:** AM Best revised the US E&S outlook from positive to stable in November 2025; rate reductions expected through Q1–Q2 2026 (search snippet, The Insurer / Insurance Journal, Feb 2026). Commission-based revenue compresses with premium softening.

**Commoditization by wholesale incumbents:** [Inferred]: CRC, Amwins, and RT Specialty have the carrier relationships, data, and capital to bolt AI ingestion onto existing books; the AI-native moat is workflow speed, not structural.
