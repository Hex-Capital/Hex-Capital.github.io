# Sherpa -- Elad Gil Evaluation

The first thing I check with any AI-powered startup in 2026 is whether the structural window they're exploiting is nonobvious or whether it's the first thing every PM in San Francisco thought of on the day ChatGPT launched. "Use LLMs to generate marketing copy variants and A/B test them automatically" is firmly in the latter category. This is not a market that was stuck and is now cracking open due to a technology threshold nobody else recognizes. It's a market where every incumbent is already shipping the same capability. Optimizely has $400M in ARR and a full platform team. VWO has 6,000+ customers and has been adding AI features. Mutiny raised $72.6M specifically to do AI-powered website optimization for B2B marketers and already has $20.7M in revenue. When I invested in Perplexity in 2022, AI-powered search was considered a failed concept by most investors -- the structural shift was real but consensus hadn't caught up. Here, consensus is fully caught up and well-capitalized. That's the opposite of the bet I want to make.

The infrastructure-layer test makes this harder to justify. Sherpa is an application that sits on top of well-understood building blocks: foundation model APIs for copy generation, a JavaScript injection layer for deploying variants, and standard statistical testing for declaring winners. None of these components are proprietary, and the assembly doesn't create a platform that other businesses build on. Compare this to Stripe, which became the payments API that an entire generation of internet businesses integrated into their core transaction flow and couldn't rip out. Sherpa's JS snippet deploys to a customer's website but doesn't embed in their core workflow the way payroll software (Gusto) or developer tools (Retool) do. If the AI-generated variants stop performing, the customer reverts to their original page and evaluates three other tools with essentially identical capabilities. The claimed data flywheel from 1M+ visits sounds meaningful until you realize VWO has been accumulating conversion data across 6,000+ customers for over a decade. The data advantage actually accrues to the incumbents here.

To be fair, the bull case exists and I want to articulate it clearly. The incumbents' business models depend heavily on enterprise contracts and professional services ecosystems -- Optimizely in particular has consultants and agencies building on their manual experimentation workflows. Fully autonomous CRO could be a genuine business model conflict for them, the way Kodak couldn't cannibalize film revenue with digital cameras. If Sherpa's autonomous loop genuinely delivers 15-20% conversion lift in 30 days with zero manual setup, that's a radically simpler value proposition than anything Optimizely or VWO offers, and it could capture the massive long tail of companies that know they should be doing CRO but can't afford the tooling and headcount. The Lindy testimonial citing "+20% lift" and "dozens of tests a week" suggests the product works at least for one customer. If Sherpa could become the default autonomous CRO layer for every Shopify store, every SaaS landing page, every growth-stage startup -- that's a big outcome. But "if" is doing a lot of work in that sentence, and every incumbent is racing to ship the same automation.

On the founding team: both Ethan Kinnan and Norbu Sonam come from Lindy, which gives them direct exposure to AI agent workflows and product-led growth motions. Sonam's Microsoft experience shipping Copilot image generation to GA is genuine engineering credibility. But neither founder has scaled a company, and Kinnan graduated in 2024. The only named customer is their former employer. I've seen early-career founders build significant companies -- I don't weight founder biography as heavily as market structure -- but when the market is this competitive, you need either a structural positioning advantage or exceptional execution speed, and I don't have evidence of either here. The name collision problem ("Sherpa" is used by at least three other tech companies) is a surprisingly practical obstacle to SEO and brand-building in a category where discoverability matters.

The single-miracle test reveals the core problem: the technology miracle (LLMs generating quality marketing copy) is real but happening for everyone simultaneously. What Sherpa actually needs is a distribution miracle -- getting marketing teams to adopt an unknown pre-seed tool over Mutiny, Optimizely, or VWO -- combined with a data quality miracle -- proving the autonomous loop outperforms human CRO experts at scale. That's two miracles, and neither is exogenous. This is a well-understood market with well-funded competition and no structural barrier to entry. I'm passing.
