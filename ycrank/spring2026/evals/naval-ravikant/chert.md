# Chert -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Chert's entire business runs on a channel that Apple has explicitly chosen not to support — there is no official business iMessage API, and operators reach scale through Mac-relay infrastructure that lives in Apple's terms-of-service gray zone (sendblue.com 2026 via search snippet). That is not a moat. That is borrowed time on someone else's platform. My framework starts with leverage, and "leverage that the platform owner can revoke on a Tuesday" is the structural opposite of permissionless. Chert is selling the inverse of code leverage — they have rented a fragile, permissioned channel and dressed it in an AI wrapper.

Ian Fong is an NYU math/econ undergrad with eight hackathon wins and an incoming JPMorgan Chase internship; Gary Gao is a Penn CS/Wharton sophomore whose research is in oscillator-based physical learning networks at the Penn Circuits Lab (LinkedIn; curf.upenn.edu). I look for specific knowledge productized — the founder's non-trainable expertise IS the company. Nothing in either Chert founder's background touches B2B sales tooling, deliverability, ICP modeling, or enterprise GTM. Gao's specific knowledge is in analog AI hardware; Fong's is in competition programming. Neither stack is what Chert sells. If you replaced these two with any other pair of sharp YC undergrads, Chert's core insight ("use iMessage instead of email") survives untouched. That is the failure condition for my first criterion.

Fong's incoming JPMC internship (LinkedIn snippet) is the principal-agent tell I cannot ignore. Principals don't have an investment-banking optionality fallback; principals have burned the boats. I am not saying he won't take Chert seriously — I am saying the dossier shows a founder whose identity is not yet fused with this company. Compare to Sahil Lavingia continuing to build Gumroad solo through years of failed Series A attempts. That is the principal signal I underwrite. Chert's principal signal is, charitably, undetermined; uncharitably, contradicted.

The bull case for Chert deserves a fair hearing. AI-SDR incumbent 11x.ai raised $74M from Benchmark and a16z, hit ~$10M ARR, then reportedly lost 70-80% of customers within months (TechCrunch via search snippet, 2024). That churn is real category data, and it implies the email/LinkedIn channel is genuinely exhausted. iMessage has 10x the open rates of email because incumbents like Salesforce and Outreach structurally cannot enter without putting Apple Business Chat partnerships at risk — a real principal-agent moat for Salesforce that Chert can exploit. Customer logos including Whop and testimonials from leaders at Databricks, Ramp, and Corgi (trychert.com) are non-trivial for a Spring 2026 batch company. An advocate would frame this as Polymarket-style: operate in the gray zone the regulated incumbents won't touch, harvest the asymmetric returns until the rules clarify.

That parallel breaks where it matters. Polymarket's "gray zone" is jurisdictional regulatory arbitrage — the underlying technology (on-chain settlement) is permissionless and cannot be revoked by any single counterparty. Chert's "gray zone" is Apple's tolerance, and Apple is a single counterparty with a centralized kill switch. Worse, Sendblue (also YC) and Project Blue already sell iMessage primitives as APIs (sendblue.com; tryprojectblue.com). The durable layer in this stack is the API provider, not the managed-service wrapper on top. Artisan, AiSDR, and 11x can plug Sendblue's API into their existing pipelines in a quarter and erase Chert's channel-only differentiation. This is the AI-wrapper anti-pattern I flagged in my Pass example: rented model leverage, rented channel leverage, no specific-knowledge floor underneath.

Permissionless leverage architecture scores below midpoint: AI agents are real leverage (12-13/25 baseline), but the channel dependency on Apple ToS plus the API-provider commoditization layer pulls it down. Team-to-value is the one place Chert scores cleanly — two builders, no enterprise sales hires, lean by default (this is YC pre-seed table stakes, not a distinguishing signal). Access democratization is negative: Chert is not opening a gatekept resource to a new population — it is enabling B2B sales teams to penetrate a personal communication channel users specifically chose because it was not commercialized. AngelList democratized angel investing for retail; Chert commercializes iMessage for sales teams. That is the inverse pattern.

The single condition that would flip me on Chert: evidence that one of the founders has spent obsessive years inside the deliverability and verified-phone-number sourcing problem — the actual durable layer in this stack — and that Chert is building proprietary lead-data infrastructure that compounds. The dossier shows neither. "127 leads sourced in a sample week" (trychert.com, company-stated) is output, not infrastructure. Until I see specific knowledge in the lead-sourcing engine rather than in the channel choice, Chert is a channel-arbitrage trade, not an investment.

Two dimensions lock the verdict at Pass: Specific Knowledge Productization (Fong and Gao's expertise stacks have nothing to do with what Chert sells) and Permissionless Leverage Architecture (the channel is revocable by Apple and the differentiator is commoditizable by Sendblue's customers). Innovation without a moat flows to consumer surplus and then to the platform owner. Pass.
