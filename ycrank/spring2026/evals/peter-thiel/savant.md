# Savant -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Savant arrives as an attempted-secret pure-software memory layer in the year Glean crossed $300M ARR, and the analytical signature is exactly that asymmetry: the dimension where Savant scores best — a thin contrarian framing about "undocumented procedures versus indexed documents" — is being asked to carry weight on top of the dimension where it scores worst, an atoms-light SaaS sitting on the same Slack/Salesforce/Workspace integration surface as four better-capitalized incumbents. Anthony Kim from Yale EECS and Kevin Yu from Cornell CS have built what the Founders Fund manifesto (2011) called the modal mistake of the era: bits layered on bits, in a wave where every other founder is shipping the same layer.

Stripe (2010) is the HIGH rung on contrarian secret — Patrick Collison named a specific incumbent (PayPal) and a specific architectural blind spot (the developer-experience failure mode of a checkout-first product trying to retrofit APIs), and the secret was load-bearing for fifteen years of compounding. Savant gestures at a related move: Glean indexes the written corpus, Savant claims to capture the unwritten — "procedures, exceptions, judgment calls" per heysavant.com. That is a distinction, not a secret. The named incumbent failure mode is missing: the dossier does not explain why Glean ($300M ARR, $7.2B valuation, TechCrunch May 2026) structurally cannot ship procedure-capture, or why Microsoft 365 Copilot Agents and Atlassian Rovo — already inside the customer's tenant — would lose to a two-person seed company on this axis. Stripe's secret terminated in PayPal's architecture; Savant's terminates in a marketing adjective.

The 2024-26 "AI for X" pass example is the LOW rung on monopoly trajectory, and its failure mechanism is precise: the moat hypothesis collapses to "we have a better model or a better wrapper," which any of five competitors replicates within nine months. Savant's competitive map reads as the canonical instance of that mechanism — Glean, Dust ($60M+ raised, 3,000+ organizations, 300K+ agents deployed per theaiinsider.tech May 2026), Guru ($63M revenue per GetLatka), Credal, Notion Custom Agents, Microsoft Copilot, Atlassian Rovo — all operating the same integration surface heysavant.com lists. No regulated customer, no certification depth, no capex barrier, no disclosed network density. Pricing-power story at year five: there isn't one in the dossier, and the default ("we assume commodity pricing at maturity") is the active assumption a Thiel check has to overcome.

Definite plan — mechanism analysis: the dimension compounds when a founder publicly commits to an architectural claim they will not pivot from, because the commitment itself is the recruiting and capital-allocation flywheel. Eighteen months of indefinite iteration in enterprise knowledge produces a feature, not a company, because the surface area of "consolidate written knowledge plus capture unwritten procedures plus serve AI agents" across Google Workspace, Office 365, Salesforce, Slack, Zendesk, Intercom, and custom databases (heysavant.com) is precisely the shape Microsoft and Notion bundle into the suite for free. Savant's dossier contains no 2-5 year named milestone — absent evidence, the default applies.

Founder lineage — counterfactual: for Savant's score on this axis to flip a tier, the dossier would need either a PayPal Mafia / Facebook / Stripe / Palantir senior-IC trace on Kim or Yu, or a prior shipped-to-scale product, or a Thiel Fellowship / Olympiad / acquired-side-project credentialed-outsider signal. The dossier gives Yale EECS and Cornell CS and nothing else — no GitHub, no prior employer, no Twitter, no public artifact. This is precisely the profile the Asana (2010) and Quora (2010) overrides cannot be invoked for, because both overrides ran on Moskovitz's Facebook ship-credit and D'Angelo's Facebook CTO pedigree respectively. Savant has no such override available.

Atoms-heavy / regulated — single-anchor parallel to Palantir (2003): Palantir's monopoly came from the customer, not the code — fifteen years of intelligence-community integration depth Silicon Valley refused to touch became the moat. Savant integrates into Slack and Zendesk, which is the opposite of integration depth — it is integration surface, the part of the stack platform vendors bundle and re-bundle every product cycle. Microsoft 365 Copilot Agents, Atlassian Rovo, Notion Custom Agents (Coworker AI 2026) are the bundling vector, and a two-person memory layer sitting above the same SaaS sources has no regulated-customer, certification-depth, or capex anchor against them.

Forecast, 36 months: Savant is either acqui-hired by Notion, Atlassian, or Glean for under $30M with revenue below $3M ARR, or dead — the bundling vector closes faster than a two-person team without a contrarian secret or operator pedigree can build switching-cost density, and the Spring 2026 batch will produce three indistinguishable variants of the same memory-layer pitch competing for the same design partners.

The strongest opposing case is Dust: $40M Series B from Sequoia in May 2026, 3,000+ organizations, 300K+ agents — the category is real, the wave is funded, and Savant could ride a sub-segment of it. The bear-side dismantle is the Known Exceptions test: Asana's override was Moskovitz's Facebook ship-credit, Quora's override was D'Angelo's Facebook CTO pedigree, Lyft's override was duopoly power-law structure in mobility. Kim (Yale EECS) and Yu (Cornell CS) carry none of these, and the memory-layer category has at least seven entrants rather than a two-winner equilibrium. The verdict flips only on one verifiable condition: a signed, named enterprise contract — Fortune 500 or AI-native unicorn, six-figure ACV, displacing Glean or Notion in a documented procurement — surfaced in the next two quarters. Absent that, this is the indefinite-optimism software bet Zero to One (2014) was written against. Pass.
