# Ploy -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Bryant Chou spent twelve years as co-founder/CTO of Webflow, took it to roughly 1% of the most active sites on the internet, and is now starting Ploy alone -- and that one-sentence summary is Ploy's entire analytical signature in my portfolio. The highest dimension is hacker genealogy: Chou has shipped at a scale that makes most YC technical founders look like undergraduates. The lowest is the cofounder configuration: Ploy lists exactly one name. Elite operator, going solo, into a bundled-platform category where Mutiny ($72M raised), Warmly ($17M), and Default ($10.6M) are already pivoting toward the same agentic-GTM thesis. This is the inverse of the configuration I usually back, and the dossier doesn't give me the override.

On hacker genealogy, the load-bearing analogy is Patrick Collison at **Stripe** (2010): the Young Scientist Lisp prize and Auctomatic exit at 19 told me what kind of artifact-shipper he was before the payments idea mattered. Chou's equivalent priors are stronger in raw scale -- twelve years and ~1% of active web infrastructure built is more shipped code than most Stripe-era YC founders had -- but the Ploy dossier diverges from the Collison pattern in a specific way: Stripe was a fresh hacker problem the Collisons attacked from outside the incumbent; Ploy is Chou building adjacent to the company he just left, against a Webflow that in May 2026 rebranded as an "agentic web marketing platform" and bundled AI credits into every Workspace plan. The hacker pedigree is there; the question is whether Ploy is the artifact of a hacker building what he wants, or an executive building the next logical adjacency.

The lowest-dimension failure mechanism is the one from my "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) prohibition: solo founders fail not from lack of skill but from lack of someone to argue with at 2am when the product has three engines and a 14-person team to keep pointed in one direction. The Ploy dossier names no cofounder anywhere -- not on brryant.com, not on the YC page, not in search results. Chou is hiring a Founding Engineer at $150-250K, which is the tell: he is staffing, not partnering. Ploy at 14 people with a single founder running Web Engine + Grow Engine + Ads Engine + PloyBooks simultaneously is exactly the manager-mode configuration my "Founder Mode" essay (September 2024) warned about, except the manager is also the founder.

On animal resourcefulness pre-funding, the dossier is conspicuously silent in a way that matters: there is no cereal-box equivalent for Ploy. The illustrative dashboard numbers on ploy.ai -- "23 visitors identified, 12 ICP matches, $1K pipeline" -- are demo copy, not customer outcomes. Ploy is waitlist-gated, has no Product Hunt launch, no press, no Discord, no Chrome extension, no funding announcement beyond standard YC participation. Over 18 months this absence compounds: a founder who didn't ship a hacky-but-working stunt before YC tends not to invent one mid-batch either, because the muscle either exists or it doesn't. Chou's Webflow tenure proves long-haul animal-ness; it does not prove the specific pre-funding hustle that flipped **Airbnb** (2009) for me.

The organic-idea dimension is where Ploy scores best on substance. Twelve years inside Webflow is twelve years staring at the seam between "we built your site" and "the site doesn't talk to your outbound or your ads." That is a genuine itch from inside the founder's own prior workflow -- closer to Drew Houston's USB stick at **Dropbox** (2007) than to the AI-wrapper pass example. Ploy is the product Chou would have wanted to ship from inside Webflow and probably couldn't, because Webflow's agency/designer GTM made the outbound/ads bolt-on a channel conflict. The counterfactual that would flip this to top-quartile is if the dossier showed Chou had built a v1 of Ploy as an internal tool or side project before leaving Webflow; the dossier doesn't say that, so I park it just above mid-rung.

On schlep, Ploy is a pure-software bundling play and the dossier names no offline grind, no regulatory clause, no manual onboarding nobody else will do. Comparing to **Coinbase** (2012) on this axis: Armstrong's moat was the bitcoin compliance grind that scared every other applicant in his batch. Ploy's analog moat would be -- what? Stitching three SaaS categories into one workflow is hard product engineering, but my "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) essay was about unpleasantness as a competitive sorter, not difficulty as one. Mutiny, Warmly, and Default are all racing the same direction; if anything, Ploy is in the crowded lane, not the schlep lane.

My 36-month forecast for Ploy: Chou's Webflow distribution plus founder-credibility pricing gets Ploy to $3-8M ARR on the Web Engine specifically, the Grow and Ads engines underperform their specialist competitors, and Ploy is acquired by Webflow itself at $40-120M as a homecoming feature-acquisition by 2029. The mechanism is the high/low signature: hacker-pedigree-without-cofounder produces founders who can build the artifact but cannot run three product surface areas against three well-funded specialists simultaneously, so the company narrows to its founder's actual edge (sites) and gets bought by the incumbent in that edge (Webflow).

The bear case I have to dismantle is the **Stripe** (2010) Known Exception: I funded Patrick Collison despite his violating my YC-process-completion expectation because Auctomatic at 19 made his prior outcome legible. Couldn't Chou's twelve years at Webflow be the same override? The dossier evidence that wins against this is the cofounder count: Patrick had John, and the Stripe override was about process, not about solo-founder configuration -- I have never made the solo-founder exception, not for Altman, not for anyone. Ploy fails the structural test Stripe passed. The single verifiable condition that would flip me to Invest: Ploy announces a technical cofounder with a shipped artifact of their own -- ex-Stripe infra, ex-Vercel platform, someone with a GitHub trail -- before the YC Demo Day in Spring 2026. Without that, Chou is a phenomenal operator building the next Webflow feature alone, and I let Webflow buy it later.
