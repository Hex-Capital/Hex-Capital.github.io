# Nebula Security -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Eten Zou walks in with a founder story I want to believe -- "first nginx RCE," "first to root Android 17 through a full browser-to-kernel exploit chain" -- and I cannot find his handle. No Twitter, no GitHub, no LinkedIn. Offensive-security researchers of the caliber Nebula Security is claiming leave a trail like a comet; the whole social contract of that world is public credit. When I search for the nginx RCE, the credit goes to someone named "depthfirst," not to Nebula Security or Zou (cybersecuritynews.com, CVE-2026-42945). That is the entry-point signal before I even open the deck: a founder pitching offensive-research pedigree while offering zero of the environmental artifacts that pedigree normally produces. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Nebula Security seems to be playing is "boutique research lab"; the game it might actually be playing is "AI code scanner in the same lane as $237M-funded XBOW, dressed up in CVE trophies."

**Anduril / Palmer Luckey (2017)** is the load-bearing rung for founder-lived-experience in security: Luckey shipped Oculus at scale before defense, and the biography was auditable down to the garage. Nebula Security's Eten Zou is asserting the same shape -- shipped serious exploits before building the scanner -- and the CVE-2026-6307 Chrome V8 sandbox escape writeup on nebusec.ai/research is a real artifact on NVD. But the divergence from the Anduril rung is exactly the part that makes me cautious about writing the check: Luckey's prior work was independently, publicly, unambiguously his. Nebula Security's marquee "first nginx RCE" claim in the YC description is contradicted by the public CVE credit going to a different researcher, and Zou has no discoverable offensive-security handle. That is not a missing-detail default; that is a positive counter-signal. I score this closer to the 11/32 absent-evidence floor than to the Anduril ceiling.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling in my pass example is the failure mechanism for dimension two: six top funds chasing, deck opens on a $200B TAM slide, and the moat is the model. Trace that pattern onto Nebula Security's dossier and it lights up. Snyk sits at an $8.5B valuation, Semgrep at ~$5B on secondary, and XBOW closed $120M Series C in March 2026 plus a $35M extension in May at >$1B (Bloomberg; GeekWire) -- all targeting the identical "AI-native security" buyer. Nebula Security is not the flare-gas-in-North-Dakota bet; it is the sixth entrant in the year's hottest security category, and the persona filter I use for Crusoe is precisely inverted here. Nothing in Nebula Security's positioning says "two credible VCs passed on aesthetic grounds"; everything says "priced on the XBOW comp."

Nebula Security lists Team Size 2 on the YC page but names only Eten Zou as CEO & Founder. Over 18 months, this dimension compounds badly when the second co-founder is a black box: audit-services businesses live or die on which human signs the report, and if the co-founder is the actual exploit hand while Zou is the front, the tiebreaker question is unanswered on Day 1. It is not yet the HQ Trivia (2017) failure mode -- there is at least a single CEO title -- but it is the shape that becomes one. I score at the default with a slight nudge for the title being unambiguous.

Counterfactual for dimension four: what would have to be true for Nebula Security to move from software-abstraction to my Mindbloom / Vigil Labs (August 2025) unsexy-regulated lane? The audit-brand pivot would have to become the primary business -- "Audited by Nebula Security" as a SOC-2-adjacent trust mark that regulated buyers pay a premium for, with the VEGA scanner as loss-leader. That is a real path, and it is the one that would earn a check. Today's dossier does not show it: no pricing page, no named enterprise design partner, the counter still reads "$0K+ in bug bounties earned." Nebula Security is a software company selling to software teams until proven otherwise.

Flock Safety (~2018) is the single-anchor parallel for physical-world verifiability -- a normal human can drive past a Flock camera. Nebula Security's tactile artifact is the CVE writeup on nebusec.ai/research/v8-cve-2026-6307-writeup/, and to Nebula Security's credit I can read the Longinus post and cross-check CVE-2026-6307 against NVD in an afternoon. That is more observable than a pure dev-tools API. What I cannot do is install VEGA, deploy it against my own repo, or hand it to a friend -- there is no public product surface. Nebula Security sits above the dev-tools floor because of the CVEs and below the Flock ceiling because the product itself is a founder-testimony artifact.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Nebula Security acquihired for $5-25M by a mid-tier AppSec vendor (Sonatype, Checkmarx, or Veracode class) or quietly shuttered, with XBOW and Semgrep having absorbed the AI-native code-security narrative and the boutique audit brand failing to compound past the founding team's bandwidth. The mechanism is the consensus-category squeeze from dimension two colliding with the founder-verification gap from dimension one: without independently corroborated researcher identity, Nebula Security cannot charge the premium that would make the audit brand a real business, and the scanner alone loses to XBOW's warchest.

The bull case that stings: the CVEs are real, the CVE-2026-6307 Chrome V8 sandbox escape is on NVD, and if VEGA is genuinely finding zero-days that Snyk and Semgrep miss, then Nebula Security is DeepMind-shaped for offensive security -- pedigreed polymaths whose credential wrapping I initially dismissed. The Known Exception applies: I overrode my bias against credentials for Hassabis because the compounding was real. What dismantles it is the specific dossier fact I keep returning to: the "first nginx RCE" attribution collapses under a thirty-second search (depthfirst, cybersecuritynews.com), and offensive researchers who inflate one credit inflate others. Hassabis's chess and neuroscience records were independently verifiable; Nebula Security's Zou has no handle. I flip to invest the day Eten Zou publishes a defcon or Pwn2Own credit under a name I can trace, or names a Fortune 500 CISO as an "Audited by Nebula Security" design partner. Until then, this is the pass example wearing a research lab's clothing.
