# FinalDose -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

FinalDose is a maximum-consequence civilizational bet wrapped in an academic-spinout package -- Jeff Liu, Li-Yao Huang, and Steven Lin claim a programmable DNA-sensing kill switch that addresses the ~80% of disease-relevant proteins currently called "undruggable," and that consequence magnitude is what activates my framework before I read a single line on traction. The dossier names the incumbent regime FinalDose would collapse: small-molecule-and-antibody oncology, a $232.2B market in 2026 that fails on undruggable targets by definition. That is the **Impossible Foods** (2011) pattern of naming what disappears if you win, not the **DoorDash** (2013) pattern of capturing a flow that already exists.

Consequence Magnitude is FinalDose's highest rung, and the ladder runs from **OpenAI** (2019) at the top -- civilizational AI displacement, the $15T labor frame I keep citing -- down through **DoorDash** (2013) in the middle, where the success-state reshaped consumer commerce but did not move a civilizational variable, to the GPT-4-sales-call-summarizer pitch I pass on in 30 seconds because the success-state is 20-30% workflow efficiency. FinalDose sits just below the **OpenAI** rung: the dossier's claim of "destroying all cancers" and unlocking 80% of targets, anchored to a $697.59B oncology market by 2034, names a specific incumbent system that disappears if it works -- exactly the inverted question I ask, "what economic mechanism breaks if this works," because per-indication pharma R&D economics break when the same molecule plus a new guide becomes a new drug. The "all cancers" framing slightly overreaches versus what Oxford DPhils can credibly promise pre-clinically, which is why FinalDose does not equal **OpenAI** at the top rung -- but it clears Impossible Foods on civilizational consequence.

FinalDose's lowest rung is Founder Learning Rate, and that ladder runs from **Slash** (2024) at the top -- the Kanye-pivot story I funded at $100M Series C precisely because the documented thesis update was the signal -- through **Physical Intelligence** (2024) at mid-rung where Levine and Finn made the academic-to-commercial jump but with no public pivot moment, down to the linear-plan founder profile I pass on. FinalDose's Jeff Liu raised $5M seed plus $10M Pre-Series A for **Vivid Dx** (Raman-spectroscopy sepsis diagnostics, John Sperzel installed as CEO July 2025), then crossed from diagnostics into oncology therapeutics -- which is an adjacent leap, not a documented thesis update with a public "we were wrong about X" moment. FinalDose pins between **Physical Intelligence** and the lower rung: there is a second-company-in-harder-problem shape that I reward, but no Slash-grade pivot narrative, and Li-Yao Huang and Steven Lin have no prior-company evidence at all.

Technical and Capital Risk on FinalDose runs against my **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) high rung -- $115M Series A when fusion timelines were investor-toxic -- and stays well above **DoorDash** at the bottom. FinalDose requires three named miracles each falsifiable: single-base-pair in vivo specificity for the DNA-sensing conformational switch, the unaddressed delivery problem that pushed Intellia (27% layoff 2025) and Caribou (32% layoff 2025, $300M+ raised) into refocusing, and CMC plus regulatory-class definition for a novel kill-switch modality with no FDA precedent. That is the **KiOR** (~2008) miracle-count shape, which is the point -- if the dossier described low-risk software, I would be over-resourced and any Tier-1 generalist could fund it.

FinalDose's Founder-Defies-Consensus dimension is the most ambiguous. Against the **Impossible Foods** (2011) high rung -- Pat Brown defying food-industry orthodoxy -- and the **Physical Intelligence** (2024) mid rung of credentialed academic spinouts who are not personally heretics, three Oxford DPhils read as paradigmatic expert continuity. Yet the FinalDose claim that "R&D cycle and cost collapses to near 0" is exactly the kind of heretical economic claim that big-pharma per-asset P&L structures cannot internalize -- the unstated platform-vs-asset conflict the dossier names. The McKinsey-1986-cellphone failure -- predicting <1M U.S. handsets by 2000 against an actual 109M -- is the analog: experts on undruggable targets have been extrapolating their pocket-binding past, and FinalDose's mechanism does not extrapolate from it. Required-Capital Profile, against the **Commonwealth Fusion** ($115M) high rung and **Genesis AI** ($105M co-led seed July 2025) mid rung, matches my check pattern almost perfectly given that Caribou and Mammoth peer-set capital is $300-375M raised -- FinalDose is not a $5-10M-to-$1M-ARR SaaS company I am over-resourced for.

My 36-month forecast: FinalDose raises a $50-100M Series A by Q2 2028 on preclinical specificity data showing target-sequence kill-switch activity in cell culture plus one murine xenograft model, with the delivery problem still unsolved and the lead indication narrowed from "all cancers" to one liquid-tumor or hereditary-driver indication where ex vivo delivery is tractable. The mechanism producing that outcome is the same one that produced the Intellia/Caribou refocus in 2025: the specificity miracle tracks, the delivery miracle wobbles, and capital scales only after the team picks one indication where delivery is solvable.

The strongest objection to my Invest lean comes from my own unstated patterns. I quietly favor founders who had one exit or one publicly known intellectual reputation before raising from me -- the Sergey Levine pattern at **Physical Intelligence** -- and FinalDose's Jeff Liu has $15M raised but no exit, while Huang and Lin surface no public profile depth at all (no LinkedIn confirmation for Huang, no GitHub for any, no verified Twitter). The bear case is sharp: this is **Range Fuels** (~2007) in molecular biology -- the "R&D collapses to near 0" claim mirrors the over-promised KiOR catalyst economics, delivery is the unaddressed elephant that broke $300M+ peers, and the dossier shows no patents, no advisors, no named investors. I take the bear case head-on: consequence magnitude per my MIT Rose Lecture (2019) directive forces me to focus on the success-state, and a programmable oncology kill switch is exactly the "too important not to do" Outlook Business framing, which justifies the 70-80% failure prior I publicly defended after KiOR. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict to pass: if FinalDose's first preclinical readout in the next 18 months shows the kill switch firing on off-target sequences at >1% frequency in mammalian cell culture, the specificity miracle is dead and the delivery problem becomes moot -- and I walk.
