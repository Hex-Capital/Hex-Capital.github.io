# Twolabs -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Twolabs is a hardware-ambitious swing without a biographical anchor — Sardor Rahmatulloev and Danyal Ahmad are building a semi-humanoid named Tobi for nursing homes after pivoting mid-batch from an egocentric-video dataset platform for *agricultural* robotics, and that pivot tells me everything about how this founder pair arrived at eldercare. The high-dimension signature (frontier hardware in a regulated, unsexy lane) is exactly the lane I fund — Anduril, Crusoe, Mindbloom all live there. The low-dimension signature (no lived contact with frail elderly, no caregiver in the family surfaced anywhere in the dossier) is exactly the lane I refuse to fund on its own. Twolabs is the cross-section of those two truths, and the cross-section scores Neutral.

Anduril is my ladder rung here, and it is load-bearing because Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage *before* he ever pitched defense — the biography put him in front of the problem first. Twolabs inverts that arrow. Sardor's Cornell Combat Robotics and ex-Meta Superintelligence Labs lineage gives him hardware-AI fluency, and Danyal's Salesforce video-model fine-tuning is real, but neither founder appears in the dossier with a grandparent in a nursing home, a stint as a CNA, a sibling with mobility needs, nothing. The prior product was robotizing *agriculture* per startupbase.uz; six months later it is robotizing *grandma*. That is not a Hanke-walking-into-Niantic-after-a-decade-on-Keyhole story — that is two technically excellent operators surveying robotics-adjacent verticals from outside and selecting eldercare because the TAM slide reads $9.85B by 2033. I have written that check before and I do not write it again.

The Pass-Example failure mechanism is "founders discovered the problem in a memo, not by running the back-office their product claims to automate," and Twolabs's website artifact reproduces this almost cleanly. There is no installable, observable, photographable Tobi in the wild — no pilot site at Brookdale, no demo at a Sunrise community, no video of a resident eating soup with Tobi's help. The Flock Safety move was reading the founder's Wi-Fi name at a Four Seasons; the Niantic move was playing Ingress for three years before writing the check. With Twolabs I have a render, an X handle @twolabsai, and an F4 Fund AgeTech directory listing. I cannot ride this, scan this, or hand it to my mother. That is the texture of dev-tools abstractions, not the texture of Tobi-in-the-hallway.

Twolabs's *category* is the one part of the dossier I love unreservedly. Humanoid hardware + regulated long-term-care procurement + liability-laden physical assistance with frail elderly is exactly the "too hard or too unsexy" filter — 1X is raising $1B at $10B valuation per Tech Startups for the consumer home, Diligent took ~$68M to put Moxi in hospital hallways doing logistics, not ADLs. Nobody has actually fielded a humanoid that feeds and stabilizes a 90-year-old, because the safety problem is genuinely unsolved. Eighteen months of compounding here looks like either a regulatory moat (CMS pilot, state-level eldercare waiver, FDA pre-sub) or nothing — there is no middle path. Twolabs has none of those certifications referenced on twolabs.ai today.

The counterfactual on co-founder governance: Twolabs has Sardor as Co-Founder & CEO and Danyal as Co-Founder & CTO with clear title delineation per LinkedIn, which clears the HQ Trivia / GameCrush bar of "name the tiebreaker." For this score to flip a tier upward I would need a documented operating agreement or visible majority-equity disclosure. For it to flip downward I would need to see equal billing on press releases or the kind of "we decide everything together" language Yusupov and Kroll used pre-implosion. The dossier shows neither, so Twolabs sits at the absent-evidence midpoint with a small upward lean from the title structure itself.

Crusoe is the single-anchor parallel for the "magically weird" axis, and Twolabs does not load onto it. Crusoe was three uninvestable things stapled together — crypto, energy, rural industrial — at a moment when zero VCs were in that intersection. Twolabs is one *very* hot thing (humanoid robotics, $9T per RBC, Mar 2026) pointed at one *moderately* unsexy vertical (eldercare facilities). The round is not yet consensus because the team is two people pre-seed, but the *category* is the most chased category in venture in 2026. That is not "find them before it becomes consensus" — that is "find them inside the consensus, at the corner everyone is ignoring." I will pay slightly for the corner, not for the category.

My 36-month forecast: Twolabs pivots away from frail-elderly ADL assistance to a lower-liability humanoid vertical — concierge in assisted-living lobbies, medication-reminder companion robots, or a hospitality/retail wedge — by month 24, raises a modest seed extension or party Series A in the $5–15M range, and ends 2029 alive but without paying nursing-home contracts, more likely acquired-for-team by a larger humanoid platform than independent. The mechanism: the ADL safety problem is unsolved by players with 50–200x the capital, and Sardor and Danyal will hit that wall, recognize it, and pivot — exactly as they pivoted from agriculture six months ago. They are good at pivoting, which is both a strength and the reason there is no biographical anchor.

The strongest bull case against me is the DeepMind exception — polymath cross-domain compounding overrode my bias against pedigreed founders in 2013, and Sardor's Cornell-robotics-plus-Meta-AI-Infra-plus-immigrant-outsider profile (first Uzbek YC founder, per UzDaily) has a polymath shape. The Crusoe precedent also stings: Chase Lochmiller pivoted from Bitcoin to Stargate AI infrastructure and I rode it, with Jonathan embedded for six months. Why not embed alongside Sardor through a pivot? Because Lochmiller was *of* the oilfield place — Sardor is not *of* the nursing home, and was not *of* the farm either. The pivot pattern in Twolabs is "smart engineers scanning verticals," not "operator following a problem they have lived through two contexts." One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a named, signed pilot with a top-50 senior-living operator (Brookdale, Sunrise, Atria, Holiday) with Tobi performing a documented ADL on video. Until that ships, Twolabs is a Neutral, and a respectful one.
