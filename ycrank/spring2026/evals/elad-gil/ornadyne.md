# Ornadyne -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Ornadyne is a real-catalyst, no-index, multi-miracle hard-tech bet -- the counter-UAS proliferation across Ukraine and the Middle East over 2024-2026 is a legitimate market re-opening, but Geourg Kivijian and Armen Arakelyan are building bird-shaped *hardware* that has to beat AeroVironment's Nano Hummingbird lineage, France's BIOFLY, and a state-funded Chinese ornithopter program on flight hours, not a software layer that sits in everyone's workflow. That high-catalyst / low-index pairing is the signature, and it's the pairing that made me lead **Anduril** (2022) Series D but skip the seed-stage hardware-first attempts that preceded it.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on market re-opening, and it's worth being specific about why Ornadyne *partially* loads onto it. The mechanism in Anduril was Ukraine-era Pentagon software inadequacy in a 24-month window, plus a procurement appetite that had visibly shifted -- a catalyst peers were still mispricing. Ornadyne points at the same shelf: the YC description and Atoms Not Bits coverage explicitly cite counter-UAS proliferation degrading rotor and fixed-wing platforms, which is a real, dateable shift in the operator's problem. Where Ornadyne diverges from Anduril is that the catalyst opens demand for *low-signature ISR*, not specifically flapping-wing -- tethered drones, loitering munitions with reduced acoustic signature, and bird-mimetic platforms are all candidate answers, and Ornadyne is one bet on one form factor rather than the obvious software layer the catalyst routes through. The catalyst is named and credible; Ornadyne is one of several geometric answers to it.

**Lyft** Series C is the mechanism I keep returning to on the index dimension, and the failure mode there was structural -- a #2 in a market where #1's economics compound faster than challenger share gains. Ornadyne's competitive set tells you the same story in a different language: AeroVironment is a public company at roughly $717M FY24 revenue with the DARPA Nano Hummingbird heritage and DoD program-office relationships, Airion is already inside the AFRL micro-UAV program since 2021, and Northwestern Polytechnical University and BIOFLY are state-funded competitors who don't need to be capital-efficient. Ornadyne does not sit in the workflow path the way **Stripe** (2010) sat across e-commerce or **Decagon** (2023) sat across post-LLM support -- Ornadyne is one hardware SKU that has to *beat* incumbents on endurance, signature, and control, and the dossier names no software-layer wedge (no autonomy stack, no fleet-management plane, no signature-database moat) that would convert hardware sales into an index position. This is the dimension that most kills the angle for me.

Revenue velocity: $1M raised per StartupHub.ai with zero disclosed revenue, zero named DoD contract, zero LOI, and zero pilot is the absent-evidence default in defense-hardware-at-pre-seed, and I weight it less harshly than I would for an AI workflow company because hardware doesn't ship in the same cadence as **Harvey** (2023) ran from $3B to $5B to $8B. The mechanism I want to see compound over 18 months is SBIR/AFWERX progression -- Phase I to Phase II to a program-of-record line item -- and Ornadyne hasn't shown the first Phase I yet in public sources. Without that, I'm modeling Ornadyne as pre-traction and scoring at the lower end of the default.

Founder shipping rhythm is the dimension where Ornadyne reads better than the headline suggests, and the counterfactual is illuminating: if Geourg Kivijian's Cal Poly MS thesis on "Development of a Novel Bio-Mimetic Ornithopter With Variable Flapping" and his 2+ years of AIAA/IEEE conference output were instead just "ex-NASA JPL" with no thesis artifact, this would be a pedigree-not-shipping profile and I'd default lower. The thesis plus the publication trail is a shipped artifact in academic form, and Armen Arakelyan's USC RPL Machining Team Lead role contributing to the 144km amateur altitude record is a real hardware-shipping resume, not a SpaceX-by-association credential. What Ornadyne does *not* show is the **Perplexity** (2022) pattern of independent product iteration between conversations -- there's no GitHub presence for either founder, no consumer prototype, no field-test cadence visible. The shipping evidence is academic and component-level; the question is whether it converts to product cadence under YC pressure.

The single-miracle test is where Ornadyne fails my standard screen the way most non-Anduril hard-tech does. Compare to **Color Genomics** (2015), where I architected around the chemistry by making it a workflow layer on commodity sequencing -- the miracle was distribution, not science. Ornadyne requires (1) hour-class flapping-wing endurance that no commercial platform has independently demonstrated, (2) closed-loop autonomy on a non-standard flight envelope, and (3) DoD program capture against incumbents with existing contract vehicles. Each is independent; you compound the probabilities and you get a number that doesn't justify a hardware-stage check at consumer-seed prices. Anduril is the **Known Exception** precedent -- but I led that round at $4.6B post with shipped-at-scale founders and a software-on-hardware abstraction; Ornadyne is the inverse profile, betting on the hardware miracle itself at pre-seed.

My 36-month forecast: Ornadyne lands one or two AFWERX/SBIR Phase II awards in the $1-3M range, raises a bridge or small Series A in the $5-12M band from defense-tech-specific funds (Founders Fund affiliates, Lux, 8VC), demonstrates a prototype with sub-hour endurance, and either gets acqui-hired by AeroVironment or Anduril for the IP and team or remains a capital-starved hardware program at under $2M revenue with no program-of-record. I do not see the $100M-ARR path inside 36 months given the procurement cadence and the sovereign-competition density.

The strongest bear-of-my-bear case is the **Anduril** override pattern: defense was also "a science project with arrows in the back" when I led Series D, and the bull says Ornadyne is the seed-stage version of that template -- a catalyst peers misread, founders with real domain artifacts (Kivijian's Cal Poly thesis is more specific founder-market fit than most defense-tech seeds have), and a sovereign-competition signal that *confirms* rather than refutes the market. The dismantle: Anduril's miracle was software distribution on top of hardware they could build straightforwardly, and I wrote a growth-stage check against compounding contracts -- Ornadyne's miracle is the flapping-wing physics itself plus DoD capture plus autonomy, three independent miracles, at $1M total raised. The verdict flips if Ornadyne announces a named DoD program-of-record contract (not an SBIR Phase I) above $5M within 12 months -- that single data point would convert the index argument from "must beat incumbents" to "is the workflow path," and I'd revisit at the next round.
