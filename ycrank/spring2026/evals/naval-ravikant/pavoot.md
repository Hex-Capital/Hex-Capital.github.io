# Pavoot -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Pavoot is what happens when two exceptional ETH technicians meet in a 3-day class at the ETH AI Center, ship the prototype on day four, and point it at a B2B sales motion that is the structural opposite of permissionless distribution -- a demo-gated cal.com booking funnel selling AI event follow-up to RevOps buyers. The dimensional signature on Pavoot is high founder horsepower (Gohar Tamrazyan as 6-time Swiss Chess Champion, Ana Yoon Faria de Lima with 20+ Olympiad medals and AI engineering at BTG Pactual and Itaú) paired with a leverage architecture that requires permission from every CRM admin, every event organizer, and eventually every Cvent procurement gate. That pairing is the bet I do not write.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung on leverage architecture because the marginal cost of posting was zero, the platform charged nothing, and the only gatekeeper was the user's own keyboard -- media as protocol. Pavoot does not share that mechanism. The Pavoot homepage routes intent to cal.com/team/pavoot/30min, the pricing is demo-gated, the GTM motion is direct outbound to B2B marketing/RevOps buyers per the dossier, and the product sits inside the customer's CRM and event-CMS stack. That is capital-and-labor leverage by construction -- exactly the "labor and capital require permission" inversion of the nav.al/product-media taxonomy. No GitHub repos exist for either Pavoot co-founder. No Twitter/X presence exists for either Pavoot co-founder. The two LinkedIn posts the dossier surfaces ("from chess champion to co-founding," "what Pavoot YC P26 was doing before YC") are personal-update posts, not domain media that compounds an audience the way Atwood's Coding Horror compounded into Stack Overflow.

Stack Overflow (2008) is the LOW-rung cautionary inverse on specific knowledge for Pavoot: Atwood and Spolsky shipped a developer product because they had been writing for developers for years, and the failure mode they avoided was the "ex-FAANG-PM identifies opportunity" memo-founder pattern. Pavoot's founder origin story -- met in a class, given 3 days to invent a startup, pitched on day 4 -- is the canonical memo-founder template wearing AI-engineer clothes. Tamrazyan and Faria de Lima have zero documented prior experience in events, RevOps, B2B marketing operations, or sales tooling per their LinkedIn profiles. The dossier flags this directly as a "domain expertise gap." The Pavoot founders did not discover this problem as users; they discovered it as a class assignment. That is the Red Flag pattern from my own framework: "founder is responding to a competitor's success rather than to a personally-discovered problem."

Mechanism analysis on the network dimension: Pavoot's pitch is per-event AI agent -- sourcing, capture, follow-up -- which is a single-sided workflow SaaS where customer N does not make customer N-1 more valuable. The dossier hints at a future "proprietary cross-event attendee graph" as a compounding asset, but that thesis only works if Pavoot becomes the dominant capture layer across thousands of events first, which is the chicken-and-egg every CRM vendor has hit. Over 18 months, this dimension does not compound; it commoditizes. The Pavoot $48K-pipeline sample on the homepage is one event, not a network.

Counterfactual on market and pivot room: B2B marketing/events is a real market, and Pavoot's wedge has at least two adjacent pivot vectors -- AI SDR (Clay, 11x, Regie territory) and conversation intelligence for field sales. For Pavoot to clear top-quartile on this axis I would need the dossier to name a platform-shaped TAM with creator-economy or developer-tooling characteristics; instead the dossier lists incumbents (Cvent, Bizzabo, Goldcast, Zuddl, Splash) that define the market as event-ops software. Cvent acquired Goldcast in December 2025 for ~$300M -- Pavoot is targeting a wedge an incumbent just bought to bundle.

Single-anchor parallel on the founder trio: Ivan Zhao at Notion (2013) had been rebuilding personal productivity tools since adolescence before I wrote the check -- multi-year specific-knowledge-as-energy. Pavoot's Tamrazyan and Faria de Lima clear the intelligence leg (ETH CS, Swiss chess title, IME-USP, ResearchGate publications) and clear the energy leg (Olympiad-medal density is a real signal) and clear the integrity leg cleanly -- no contested exits, no self-promotional ethics talk. But the 10-year-partner test on Pavoot is not about whether I would enjoy a decade with these humans; it is whether the *product they are building* is one they would still be building in a decade. Three days in a class is not the answer to that question.

Pavoot in 36 months: quiet acqui-hire by a mid-tier sales-tech or event-ops vendor in the $4-12M range with ARR under $800K, or a pivot into AI SDR adjacency where the founders' AI-engineering depth is more load-bearing than their (absent) events-domain knowledge. The mechanism producing that forecast is the leverage-architecture mismatch: Pavoot is selling AI capability built on commodity foundation-model APIs into a B2B segment that Cvent and HubSpot are bundling for free with installed CRM integrations, and the Pavoot founders have neither permissionless distribution to escape that compression nor specific-knowledge moat to survive it.

The bull case on Pavoot is that vertical-AI wedges into B2B workflows are exactly where ETH-caliber technical teams have been winning the 2025-2026 cycle, that Cvent's $300M Goldcast purchase price proves the wedge has a real buyer, and that Tamrazyan's chess-champion energy and Faria de Lima's bank-AI shipping discipline could carry Pavoot to $2-5M ARR and a strategic exit inside three years -- which at pre-seed entry is a fine cash-on-cash outcome. The bear case wins anyway: my Known Exceptions (Anchorage, Clearview, Perplexity) all required either pre-consensus category timing, extreme specific knowledge, or permissionless distribution at top quartile, and Pavoot clears none of the three. Pavoot is consensus AI applied through capital-leverage distribution by founders with no domain immersion. The verdict flips if Pavoot ships an open-source attendee-intelligence protocol with organic GitHub stars or if either founder builds a public RevOps media presence that compounds before the priced seed -- the Stack Overflow / Replit precondition. Until one of those exists, I pass.
