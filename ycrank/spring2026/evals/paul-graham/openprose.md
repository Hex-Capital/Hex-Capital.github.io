# OpenProse -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Dan Barrett shipped v0.14.0 of `openprose/prose` on May 19 2026 with 14 releases, 1.3k GitHub stars and a self-reported 7,000 installs before the YC check cleared — that is the most interesting fact in the OpenProse dossier, and it's also the only fact that matters. Strip everything else away and OpenProse is a hacker scratching his own itch in public, building the agent framework he wanted to use after five years inside Claude Code, OpenCode, and Codex. The signature is high on organic-idea-from-real-pain and low on team-transparency-and-schlep: a single named hacker doing the right kind of work in the wrong kind of zone.

Drew Houston's pre-seed **Dropbox** (2007) demo on Hacker News is the load-bearing analogy for OpenProse on the organic-idea axis. Houston shipped a thing he wanted because he kept forgetting his USB stick; Barrett shipped `*.prose.md` because he'd been writing brittle prompt chains across four different harnesses for five years. OpenProse even dogfoods to the point that "13 internal responsibilities" run its own marketing and GTM — that is the Wufoo pattern of using your own product as your operating system. Where OpenProse diverges from the **Dropbox** (2007) rung is that Houston's pain was felt by every laptop owner on earth, while Barrett's pain is felt by the few thousand humans currently writing agent harnesses. That ceiling is real, but "Black Swan Farming" (September 2012) reminds me that **Reddit** (2005) also looked like a tool for a few thousand link-sharers.

The pass example I keep returning to — the polished AI-wrapper deck with no shipped artifact — is what OpenProse is *not*, and that's the strongest thing I can say in its favor on the hacker-genealogy axis. MIT-licensed, 104 forks, `npx skills add openprose/prose`, a Reactor runtime with memoization claims — Barrett is leaving GitHub tracks the way an animal leaves them. What's missing is the **Stripe** (2010) Lisp-prize tell or the Aaron Swartz RSS-spec footprint. "Two-time founder, 12 years engineering" is the kind of bio that could mean Patrick Collison or could mean a competent staff engineer at a B-tier SaaS; the dossier doesn't disambiguate, and neither does LinkedIn search. I score the hacker-genealogy as present-but-unverified, which in my book means slightly above the absent-evidence default, not the **Stripe** (2010) rung.

OpenProse fails the schlep test the way every pure-framework company fails it. **Coinbase** (2012) had compliance; **DoorDash** (2013) had last-mile ops; OpenProse has Markdown and a dependency-injection container. The work is elegant — that's the problem. "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) was about the work other smart founders unconsciously route around, and there is no smart founder routing around `*.prose.md` parsing. The competitive set is LangChain at a $1.25B valuation and CrewAI at $18M+, both of whom can clone a Markdown-contract surface in a weekend under MIT precedent. OpenProse has no regulatory exposure, no physical-world operation, no unpleasant sales motion the founder must personally run. Frameworks die from commoditization, not from execution failure, and that is exactly the failure mode the schlep filter is designed to flag.

The animal-resourcefulness score for OpenProse compounds over the next 18 months only if Barrett keeps the release cadence — 14 releases since launch is animal-tier shipping, but the half-life of GitHub-star momentum in the agent-framework category is about six months before LangGraph or Anthropic's native skills primitive eats the surface area. Barrett has shipped the artifact pre-funding, which is what flipped me on **Dropbox** (2007); he has not shipped the cereal-box stunt, the post-rejection wallet prototype, or the unscalable manual conversion of a named enterprise. The 7,000 installs is real but undifferentiated — I can't tell from the dossier whether that's 7,000 curious `npx`'s or 7,000 weekly-active developers, and the gap between those two numbers is the entire investment thesis.

The co-founder configuration is where OpenProse trips my loudest "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) alarm. YC lists a 3-person team and the dossier names exactly one human: Dan Barrett. The other two are absent from the website, the GitHub org page, and the YC profile. This is the pattern where the in-person interview either resolves into "two engineers Barrett's known for years" — fine — or "two contractors hired last month" — not fine. The Huffman/Ohanian dorm-roommate pattern at **Reddit** (2005) and the Collison-brothers pattern at **Stripe** (2010) are what I'm looking for; the OpenProse dossier gives me a one-named-founder team and a Twitter account at @irl_danB. I'm applying the absent-evidence default and noting that this is the single dimension most likely to flip on a real conversation.

My 36-month forecast for OpenProse: subsumed by Anthropic's native Claude Code skills primitive or LangChain's declarative contract surface by Q2 2028, with the OSS repo remaining alive at ~3k stars but commercially inert, and Barrett either rolling into a frameworks role at Anthropic or pivoting OpenProse to a hosted enterprise Reactor tier that tops out under $2M ARR. The mechanism is straightforward — `npx skills add openprose/prose` is itself the install command that proves Anthropic owns the distribution layer underneath OpenProse, and platform-dependent tools get the platform's economics, not the platform's outcome.

The bull case that stings: LangChain itself looked exactly like this in early 2023 — a thin Python wrapper around prompt chains, MIT-licensed, easily cloned by OpenAI in a weekend, run by founders nobody had heard of — and it is now worth $1.25B per the October 2025 TechCrunch piece. If `*.prose.md` becomes the de facto contract format the way LangChain's `Chain` class did, the Forme registry compounds switching costs and OpenProse gets the category. The dossier evidence that wins against this case is the *position in the stack*: LangChain sat above the model API, where it captured value; OpenProse sits inside the harness, where the harness captures value. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a named enterprise design partner — a Stripe, a Shopify, a Ramp — running production agents on `*.prose.md` contracts within the next six months. Until that exists, OpenProse is a beautifully shipped hacker artifact in a zone where beautifully shipped hacker artifacts get cloned.
