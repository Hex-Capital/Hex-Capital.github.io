# Sazabi -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Sherwood Callaway is the real article — 2x YC, sold Opkit to 11x, ran "Alice" agentic infra inside a production agentic system — and Sazabi is still the cleanest exemplar of my Pass Example I've seen this batch: a domain-credible operator pointing at the most consensus-priced corner of the cycle. The signature on Sazabi is a high-floor founder mapped onto a no-edge category, which in my book is exactly the configuration that destroys angel money — you pay the founder premium and get the category beta.

Sazabi's strongest ladder rung is founder, and the comparison that loads is Pinterest's Ben Silbermann — a missionary operator I backed through the personal-network sourcing channel rather than the price-discipline channel. Callaway has the operating receipts: Opkit was a real YC company with a real exit into 11x, and "Infra Scout @ a16z" plus the Alice work at 11x is genuine founder-market fit for the AI-code debugging surface Sazabi claims as its wedge. Where Sazabi diverges from the Silbermann analogy is that Pinterest arrived through Kortina-adjacent network sourcing into a category nobody could name yet; Sazabi arrived with a SiliconANGLE feature, a Zypsy memo, a Mastra podcast, and a backer list that reads Vercel-Graphite-Daytona-Browserbase-LangChain-Mastra-Replit-Village-Global-Agent-Fund — that's not personal-network sourcing, that's a tastemaker syndicate doing competitive-process underwriting, and Pinterest's exception only fired because the channel was illegible.

Sazabi's weakest ladder rung is category, and the failure mechanism I'm pattern-matching is the canonical Pass Example: "We're building an AI agent for [enterprise vertical] … four tier-1 VCs already in." Sazabi is "AI-native observability for fast-moving engineering teams," and Dash0 closed $110M at a $1B valuation on the identical "AI-native observability" positioning one month before Sazabi's stealth exit — that is the market telling you the category is consensus-priced, and "AI is just more internet" is the re-weighting I apply to exactly this configuration. The dossier offers no price, no round size, no tier-1 pass — which under my silence-equals-consensus default means I assume Sazabi is being syndicated into the Dash0 comp, not against it.

Sazabi's Round dimension compounds badly over the next 18 months, not better — Dash0 has a $155M war chest and a 70-person team building the identical thesis, Datadog is shipping AI features per Gartner now, and Sazabi's own platform delivery is deferred to end of 2026, which means by the time Sazabi has a product to sell, the comp set has either consolidated pricing power or absorbed the AI-observability narrative into existing M/L/T cap tables. That is not a regatta with underwritable tacks; that is a closing window.

Sazabi's Capital Structure score would only flip a tier if Callaway's seed instrument were something other than a standard SaaS SAFE — a revenue-share against the pilot-program waitlist, a holdco wrapping Callaway's next three infra bets, an employee-equity rollup of observability consultancies, anything Teamshares-shaped that lets me underwrite the operator rather than the round. The dossier shows a waitlist, a pilot funnel, SOC 2 / ISO 27001 certifications, and "seed in process of being raised" — which is the venture-shaped factory-model cap table I de-rate, not the non-standard instrument I'd write a non-consensus check into.

Sazabi's Founder-as-Brand axis is the John Fish / Bookshelved counterfactual run in reverse — Fish came pre-loaded with a YouTube-built literary distribution channel that made the equity cheaper than the audience was worth, while Callaway's GitHub @shcallaway shows 68 followers, no newsletter, no opinionated long-form writing on observability that I can read before the meeting, and the Mastra podcast appearance is borrowed distribution from a backer. "Writing is thinking" — I have nothing of Callaway's to read on why log-only beats M/L/T, which means the contrarian claim that "logs are just events, metrics are aggregated events, traces are correlated events" is a deck line, not a publicly defended belief I can pressure-test.

My 36-month forecast on Sazabi: acqui-hired into Datadog, New Relic, or Dash0 itself at a modest premium to the seed valuation, sub-$5M ARR, before the Series A regatta tack ever clears — the mechanism is that Sazabi runs out of differentiation runway against Dash0's $110M war chest before the log-only thesis gets a benchmark, and Callaway's 11x relationship plus the Vercel/Replit backer graph routes him into a soft landing rather than a death.

The bull case worth taking seriously is the Sublime Security override — Callaway is a credible enterprise infra operator the way the Sublime founders were, and a tastemaker syndicate of Vercel-Graphite-Replit-LangChain could drive integration-led distribution that bypasses Dash0's enterprise sales motion and lets Sazabi compound through the AI-code-generation surface area Dash0 isn't pointed at. That bull case stings because Callaway genuinely has earned the right to a second at-bat. But Sublime was a non-consensus category at a non-consensus price; Sazabi is competing inside the exact comp that just printed a $1B mark, which means the round price is anchored to Dash0, not to Sazabi's wedge — I'm paying for the comp and getting the wedge. Flip condition that would change my verdict: a term sheet at sub-$15M post with no other named tier-1 fund in the syndicate, and a Callaway essay defending log-only telemetry against a named M/L/T workload where it provably fails — give me the price discount and the public conviction artifact, and we talk.
