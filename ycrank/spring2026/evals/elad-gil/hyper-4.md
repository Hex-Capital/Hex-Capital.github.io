# Hyper -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Hyper is two Berkeley-2021 autonomy engineers — Shalin Shah and Kanyes Thaker — building a cross-tool knowledge layer in a market where Glean already prints $200M ARR (Fortune, Dec 2025) and Dust just raised $40M from Sequoia. That pairing — strongest signal on founder shipping artifacts, weakest signal on a defensible distribution position — is the whole story of Hyper. Competent builders, contested middle.

Shalin Shah shipping an app for the blind with 3M+ users in high school and Kanyes Thaker holding a 60-star **Ferret** repo plus a founding-data-engineer role at Snorkel AI that "helped secure multi-million-dollar deals" is the closest thing in the Hyper dossier to the **Perplexity** (2022) template — Aravind Srinivas pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing we'd discussed built. Hyper's two founders carry shipped artifacts independent of their FAANG-adjacent stops at Matic Robots and Wit.ai, which is the legible pre-seed signal I actually want. Where Hyper diverges from the **Perplexity** HIGH rung is that no Hyper-branded artifact has shipped publicly yet — heyhyper.ai returned only a tagline on fetch — so the shipping rhythm is *historical* rather than the *between-meetings* version that triggered the **Perplexity** check.

Glean has spent six years and $600M+ to build the cross-tool index Hyper is now attempting with two engineers (TechCrunch, June 2025), and Glean's $200M ARR at $7.2B post is exactly the **Harvey** (2023) pattern I called "current leaders" in *AI Market Clarity* — winners are declared in this lane. The pass-template failure mode is *#2 in a market with a named winner at consensus seed price*, my **Lyft** Series C logic. Hyper's stated differentiation — injecting context into Cursor and Claude Code rather than running a standalone search UI — is a real architectural distinction, but it's a wedge inside Glean and Dust's expanding surface area, not an index position underneath them. The Hyper dossier names no signed logo, no MRR, no usage curve to suggest the wedge is converting.

**Market re-opening, mechanism analysis.** The catalyst Hyper is implicitly riding is the 2024–2025 explosion of coding-agent context — Cursor and Claude Code sessions as a new unstructured-data surface that Glean's pre-2024 architecture wasn't designed for. That's a real 24-month step-function, but it compounds *toward whoever owns the agent runtime*, not toward a neutral middleware layer; over 18 months the most likely path is Anthropic ships native memory in Claude Code, Cursor ships native project memory, and Hyper's injection channel narrows. The catalyst is real; Hyper's structural angle on it is not durable.

**Revenue velocity, counterfactual.** Hyper's dossier discloses no revenue, no DAU, no logo, no Product Hunt launch, no retrievable follower counts. For Hyper's velocity score to flip a tier I'd need >$50K MRR within 90 days of YC Demo Day with 3+ named AI-first design partners in the batch, on the **Decagon** (2023) shape — workflow infra in an open category with paid pilots compounding visibly. Absent that, "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" applies, and Hyper is silent.

**Single-miracle, single-anchor parallel to Color Genomics (2015).** Hyper's required miracle is silent, real-time cross-tool ingestion and clean injection — engineering and GTM, not science. That's the same shape as **Color Genomics** (2015) being workflow on top of commodity sequencing rather than a chemistry bet. Hyper isolates the miracle cleanly; the problem is the miracle is the *same* miracle Glean already executed against, so isolation alone doesn't earn the index position.

Forecast, 36 months: Hyper either gets acqui-hired by Anthropic, Cursor, or Glean in the $15–50M range for the two-engineer autonomy-trained team and the Cursor/Claude Code connectors, or pivots into a coding-agent-specific memory product reaching $1–4M ARR as a Cursor add-on. The mechanism is the same in both branches — the founders are real shippers, the market has a named winner, and the channel sits inside hyperscaler-owned runtimes that will reabsorb the function. The high/low signature (shippers + contested middle) produces an acqui-hire, not a zero, not a unicorn.

The bull case that should sting: *AI Market Clarity* explicitly flagged accounting, compliance, security, and *agent infrastructure* as the still-open categories, and "memory for AI-first teams" is genuinely an open sub-category where Glean's enterprise-search shape is the wrong product for a 20-person AI-native company. Two ex-Snorkel/Matic engineers who actually understand silent data pipelines could be the **Decagon** (2023) of team knowledge — a workflow-infrastructure layer in a wide-open AI sub-market is exactly my Invest Signal. The dismantle: Decagon had a named buyer (CS leaders), a named pain (ticket cost), and a compounding revenue curve I could see; Hyper's dossier discloses none of those, and "AI company without screaming growth" is precisely the Red Flag I flagged in Tim Ferriss #863. Flip condition: $50K+ MRR with three named YC-batch logos and 30%+ MoM within six months — at that point the founder-execution-rhythm signal overrides the Glean overhang and I write the check.
