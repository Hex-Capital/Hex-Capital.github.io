# YouArt -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

YouArt is a company whose most tangible artifact -- "JUNKY CREAM Original powered by YouArt," watchable on the site -- sits on top of its weakest moat layer, a video-agent orchestrating Sora, Veo, and Kling that any of those labs can collapse with a feature ship. That pairing -- observable product, zero capital-intensity floor -- tells me what YouArt is: a creator-economy wrapper trying to ride the AI-video curve, not a frontier-hardware swing and not a founder-from-the-soil bet. Harry Zheng is the named CEO of three co-founders, which is the one structural thing the dossier got right.

Anduril is my high rung on lived founder experience because Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he ever pitched defense, and the biography did the underwriting. YouArt has one founder who almost loads against that rung -- Keith Zhang, whose SOULSCAPE cinema lab pulled in John Gaeta from the Matrix and AD Bruce Hunt per the Hollywood Reporter, which is a real, lived, in-the-room relationship to AI-native filmmakers. But YouArt is not Keith's solo company. Edward Ma and Harry Zheng are ex-Google, ex-TikTok ad-tech operators whose headline credential -- "TikTok Shop Ads from 0 to $100M" -- is attributed to BOTH of them in public snippets, which is the exact "Stanford-Goldman" deck pattern I screen against, just translated into Bay Area AIGC dialect. The Hollywood guy is one of three. The other two are monetization-layer people who would have built this exact product no matter what category they were assigned, and that's the tell.

Showrunner is my low-rung mirror on the magically-weird dimension, but actually the consensus-YC-AI-darling from my own Pass Example is the closer match for YouArt. The pattern there is "AI category with multiple credible substitutes already shipping," and YouArt walks straight into it: Fable's Showrunner is doing AI episodic generation with Amazon Alexa Fund money as of July 2025 per Variety, Patreon already owns the monetization rail with ~$412M of lifetime capital, and Mighty Networks, Ko-fi, Curious Refuge each occupy adjacent floor space. YouArt's pitch is to bundle two consensus categories -- AI video generation and creator subscriptions -- in a season when both have a half-dozen funded competitors. That is the opposite of "find them before it becomes consensus." Crusoe was uninvestable on paper in 2018; YouArt is investable on every paper, which is itself the deselection signal.

Harry Zheng is named CEO with two co-founders, and that alone clears the HQ Trivia / GameCrush trap that ended badly for me twice -- so the governance dimension is genuinely fine on YouArt rather than just defaulted. What I cannot resolve from the dossier is whether the TikTok Shop Ads story belongs to Harry or Edward, because both founder bios claim it. That is not a fatal flaw; it is a diligence ask. I'd want twenty minutes with each of them separately and a question about who actually owned the P&L for the 0-to-$100M chapter, because over 18 months at YouArt the same ambiguity will compound into "who is the tiebreaker when Keith wants to chase a Hollywood deal and Harry wants to chase a B2B agent contract."

Pure orchestration over third-party foundation models is the SpaceX-inverse. SpaceX in 2008 was uninvestable because the capital intensity was so brutal that no one else would touch it; YouArt in 2026 is investable because there is no capital intensity at all, which means there is also no compounding moat from time spent in the trenches. If OpenAI ships a native crowdfunding primitive next to Sora, or Runway adds a Patreon tier, the YouArt "video agent" becomes a UX shim -- and the founders' margin is whatever OpenAI decides to leave on the table that quarter. This is exactly the "moat is the model, not the founders" failure mode I now apply to every AI-app pitch.

JUNKY CREAM is the most interesting fact in the dossier and the one that almost dragged me into a yes -- you can actually watch the original on the site, which is the closest YouArt comes to my Postmates-deli-counter test where the artifact shows up in front of me. The Product Hunt 4.7 across 35 reviews on November 6, 2025 is real, not a leading indicator yet -- 35 reviews is a launch-day rally, not a community. The 4,490 X followers since October 2025 is what an AIGC studio with John Gaeta-adjacent partners can buy with two viral clips, not a flywheel. The artifact is there to touch; the pull on me is not yet there.

Within 36 months I expect YouArt sub-$2M ARR with a pivot away from "Patreon for AI originals" toward a white-label AI production service sold into established Hollywood-adjacent shops that Keith Zhang's SOULSCAPE network can introduce -- or quiet shutdown as Showrunner's Amazon-funded distribution layer and Patreon's eventual AI-video tab eat the white space from both sides. The mechanism is straightforward: a software-only stack with no capital-intensity moat and three founders straddling two opposite GTMs (Hollywood creator network vs. TikTok-style ad monetization) will not survive the foundation-model providers shipping into the same surface.

The strongest case against me is that Keith Zhang's SOULSCAPE relationships with John Gaeta and Bruce Hunt are exactly the kind of unscalable, lived, in-the-room industry-network moat that I funded in Niantic -- John Hanke had a decade of location-data social capital before Pokemon Go, and Keith has a year of AI-cinema social capital that no Stanford CS PM can replicate by reading a memo. If YouArt is actually Keith's company with two strong commercial operators in support, this is a Niantic-shaped bet I am about to whiff on. What dismantles the bull case is the founder ordering on the YC page: Harry Zheng is the CEO, not Keith, and the email harry@youart.ai is the contact, not keith@. The Hollywood-network founder is not driving. One verifiable condition flips this: if Keith Zhang is the CEO of record and Harry/Edward are running monetization in support, I would take the meeting and pursue it like I sat outside the Niantic door.
