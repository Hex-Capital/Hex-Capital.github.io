# AgentPhone -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Manav Modi scaled the Vogue app from 100K to 1M users — that's a real slope, the kind of compounding evidence I notice. But AgentPhone is what happens when a competent mobile engineer reads the room: MCP standardized in late 2024, Vapi raised $20M, Retell hit $50M ARR, and someone decided phone numbers for AI agents was the wedge. The dossier never tells me Manav or Meet were building an agent of their own and hit a wall on Twilio's webhook model. That absence is the whole evaluation. AgentPhone has the shape of a startup found by scanning the market rather than living in the future.

Organic Problem Discovery is where AgentPhone sits lowest, and it's the dimension I weight the most after founders. The HIGH rung here is Dropbox — Drew Houston kept forgetting his USB drive, and the company was a one-line origin story that wrote itself. The MID rung is Stripe, where the Collison brothers had felt the friction of accepting payments online before they sat down to fix it. The LOW rung is the Pass Example I keep in my head — two ex-consultants who "identified an opportunity in B2B workflow automation." AgentPhone sits between MID and LOW, leaning LOW: the dossier shows Manav's prior work on Vogue and UIUC's Rokwire platform — consumer mobile and university services — with zero documented contact with telephony, carrier compliance, or agent-loop development before the company existed. The "why now" paragraph is even labeled `[Inferred]` because the company itself didn't supply one. When the founders can't tell me the moment they got frustrated, it's because there wasn't one.

Founder Resourcefulness is where AgentPhone is highest, but only because the HIGH rung — the Airbnb cereal-box story — is so far above where AgentPhone actually lands. The MID rung is Patrick Collison shipping Auctomatic before Stripe, demonstrating he could finish things. The LOW rung is, again, my Pass Example consultants whose only artifact is a deck. AgentPhone sits between MID and LOW: Manav has shipped real software (the Vogue 10x and the Rokwire repos in Dart and Go), and the team has published an MCP server, SDKs for CrewAI and OpenAI Agents, and integrations with Claude Code, Cursor, and Windsurf. That's builder behavior. But there is no resourcefulness *story* — no "we couldn't get a 10DLC registration so we drove to a carrier office," no "we had no money so we ran calls through our personal phones for the first ten customers." Meet Modi has no verifiable public footprint at all. With a 2-person team and zero cereal-box anecdotes, I default near 14/30 and adjust modestly down for the thin Meet Modi profile.

Idea That Repels Before It Attracts is where AgentPhone fails my Black Swan test. The HIGH rung is Coinbase launching into crypto when serious investors thought the category was a joke, or Stripe wading into PCI compliance. The LOW rung is any pitch that sits comfortably inside a TechCrunch trend piece. AgentPhone is closer to LOW: Vapi at ~$130M post-money, Retell at $50M ARR, Bland with a $40M Series B, and YC's own telli already in the adjacent tile. The category is consensus. Telecom regulation (10DLC, STIR/SHAKEN, A2P) has genuine schlep texture, but the dossier gives me no evidence AgentPhone is doing the schlep work — the GitHub org has 10 followers and the flagship MCP repo has 13 stars, which is a thin protocol layer Twilio could ship in a sprint.

Founder Learning Rate is the one dimension where Manav gives me real signal — the Vapi-style HIGH rung is Sam Altman compounding from Loopt at 19 to running OpenAI, and the MID rung is Patrick Collison going Auctomatic → Stripe. AgentPhone sits at MID-minus on Manav's evidence (a documented 10x scaling event) but I have no slope on the AgentPhone product itself, no weekly growth numbers, no iteration narrative. Earnest Builder Identity, my final lens, places AgentPhone above the LOW rung of pitch-deck founders and below the Replit MID rung — the team is shipping MCP integrations and framework SDKs rather than fundraising pageantry, but I see no evidence of the obsessive, "I would build this even if it weren't a company" quality that put me on Watsi's board.

The 36-month forecast: AgentPhone either gets acqui-hired by Twilio, Vonage, or Vapi for under $20M as agent-native packaging gets absorbed into incumbent SDKs, or pivots into a vertical voice-agent product (most likely outbound sales or appointment-setting) once the telephony-primitive layer commoditizes. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: a learning-capable team without organic problem discovery, in a consensus market, will execute well enough to be valuable but not differently enough to escape the platform layer above (Vapi/Retell) or the carrier layer below (Twilio).

The bull case that stings is Bindwell — two teenagers in an unsexy domain where I personally invested, which proves I'll back young founders without deep public footprints if the schlep is real. Telecom regulation *is* a schlep, MCP standardization *is* a genuine "why now," and Manav's Vogue scaling *is* better than most YC pre-seed credentials. If AgentPhone's pricing page actually reflects $50K+ ARR from agent-framework developers using their numbers obsessively, my "consensus market" objection collapses into "early share of a real wedge." The dossier evidence that wins for me: Bindwell's teens had a specific molecular insight that pivoted them from selling tools to owning IP — a contrarian secret. AgentPhone's positioning ("phone numbers for AI agents, with MCP") is not a secret; it's the most obvious thing two competent engineers would build after reading Anthropic's MCP announcement. The flip condition is concrete: if AgentPhone can show 5-7% weekly growth on paid usage from a small set of agent developers who would scream if it disappeared, plus a real origin story where Manav or Meet personally hit the Twilio-for-agents wall on a prior project, I reverse to Invest.
