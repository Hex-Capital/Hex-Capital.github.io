# Replicas -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Replicas is a YC-network wrapper on Claude Code and Codex whose dossier reads almost line-for-line like my Pass Example: the success-state at maximum ambition is that Saai Arora and Connor Loi help engineering teams ship "30% of pull requests" through a sandbox VM, and the lowest dimension on my ladder -- technical and capital risk that repels consensus VCs -- is conspicuously absent, because sandbox primitives are already available from E2B, Daytona, Modal, and Anthropic's own Claude Code Remote Tasks shipped 2026-03-20. That high/low pairing -- a labor-displacement consequence frame I am philosophically receptive to, mounted on a stack with zero science risk -- is what makes Replicas analytically legible: a thin coding-agent reseller in the most consensus AI category of the 2026 cycle.

On consequence magnitude, the HIGH rung is **OpenAI** (2019), where I wrote the $50M first VC check because the success-state collapsed the cognitive-labor regime, not because Sam had a pricing page. Replicas shares the surface mechanism -- automating engineering labor is a sliver of the $15T figure I quoted to Fortune 2026-03-06 -- but the dossier itself names where Replicas diverges from the OpenAI pattern: Cognition is at $492M ARR and $26B post-money (TechCrunch 2026-05-27), Anysphere/Cursor at $2B ARR chasing a $50B pre-money (Next Web), and Factory.ai closed a $150M Series C that I led at $1.5B post-money on 2026-04-16. The civilizational mechanism is being captured by the layer above and below Replicas. Arora and Loi are not collapsing the incumbent regime; they are routing tickets to someone else's agent.

The LOW rung on technical/capital risk is the GPT-4-sales-call-summarizer pitch I pass on in thirty seconds, and the failure mechanism there is identical: a thin orchestration layer over a vendor whose roadmap will eat the wrapper. Trace it through Replicas's dossier and the pattern lights up -- "bring-your-own-credentials for Claude Code/Codex" is the literal definition of API resale, and Anthropic shipped Claude Code Remote Tasks on 2026-03-20, sixty days before this dossier was assembled. The Founding Engineer post at "$150K-$250K + 2-4% equity" tells me Replicas's roadmap requires a software-team burn, not a fusion-magnet burn or a foundation-model burn. There is no miracle to underwrite. **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) required me to lead a $115M Series A because the SPARC tokamak needed REBCO magnet physics to work; Replicas requires a Docker image and a webhook.

Founder defiance of expert consensus is the dimension I treat as a multiplier, and the mechanism analysis here is unfavorable. Arora's resume reads Ramp-Databricks-Shopify-IBM intern; Loi's reads Helicone-Cohere-TD Bank. These are paradigmatic credential-continuity profiles inside the LLM infra clergy -- competent, but the inverse of the Chelsea Finn / Sergey Levine **Physical Intelligence** (2024) pattern where a research PI publicly contradicts their field. Compounded over 18 months, that founder profile does not produce contrarian roadmap choices; it produces feature parity with whatever Cursor shipped last quarter. My TED Talk cited the UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study because experts extrapolate -- and Replicas's dossier reads like an extrapolation of February 2026.

On founder learning rate, the counterfactual is clean: to flip this to a tier 1 Slash-pattern signal, the dossier would need to name a documented Replicas pivot, a public thesis update from Loi, or a prior shipped-and-sold company by either founder. None of those appear. Loi shipped Helicone's AI Gateway and Prompts -- a credit, not a pivot. Replicas V1 launched roughly two months before this dossier; there has not been calendar time for a pivot to exist. I apply the absent-evidence default and refuse to penalize aggressively, but I also refuse the Slash override that demands an explicit Kanye-grade story.

Required-capital profile is the cleanest single-anchor parallel: compare Replicas to **Genesis AI** (2025), where I co-led a $105M seed because the robot foundation model required that scale of compute and data-collection deployment. Replicas's roadmap is two Waterloo undergrads, one open Founding Engineer slot, and a sandbox-VM orchestrator. The dossier discloses no funding figure, which at YC pre-seed implies ~$500K. I am over-resourced for Replicas by two orders of magnitude. Any Tier-1 generalist or YC continuity check funds this. My edge does not price here.

Forecast, 36 months: Replicas reaches roughly $1-4M ARR on YC-network logos (Mintlify, Composio, Helicone, Knowunity), fails to break into non-YC enterprise where Factory.ai and Cognition own the procurement conversation, and is acqui-hired before May 2029 by Linear, Vercel, or ClickUp -- following the Codegen-into-ClickUp pattern from 2025-12-24 -- at sub-$50M. The mechanism is wrapper compression: as Claude Code Remote Tasks and Cursor Background Agents mature, the BYO-credential orchestration layer migrates into the model vendor and the IDE, leaving Replicas with integration breadth as its only remaining moat -- which the dossier itself flags as commoditizable.

The strongest bear-to-bull objection is the **DoorDash** (2013) exception: capital-light, consensus-category, no science risk, returned a $72B IPO. One could argue Replicas is the DoorDash of agentic engineering work -- founder ambition, network compounding inside YC, riding a "why now" wave where Cognition's 13x YoY revenue growth proves category velocity. The objection stings because the labor-displacement frame is genuinely my frame. It loses on one specific: DoorDash's success-state restructured how consumer commerce reached the home; Replicas's success-state is that engineering teams route Linear tickets to Anthropic's API through a different URL. The Slash exception does not apply -- no documented Loi pivot. The Glean exception does not apply -- Glean had Fortune-500 distribution and Arvind Jain's Google-search credential lever, neither present here. I pass. The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Replicas crosses $20M ARR by May 2027 with majority non-YC enterprise logos and a published proprietary eval where it beats Claude Code Remote Tasks on autonomous PR merge rate -- demonstrating the wrapper has become a stack.
