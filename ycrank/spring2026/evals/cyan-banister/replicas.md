# Replicas -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Connor Loi shipped Helicone's AI Gateway at YC W23 before starting Replicas, and Saai Arora was an Applied AI Engineer at Ramp -- which means Replicas has the one signal I actually weight at the pre-seed stage, dropped into the one category where the signal cannot save the bet. Cognition just printed $492M ARR (TechCrunch, May 27 2026), Cursor is at $2B ARR chasing a $50B valuation, Factory.ai closed a $150M Series C in April 2026, and Anthropic itself shipped Claude Code Remote Tasks on March 20 2026. Replicas is the textbook 2026 consensus YC AI-agent darling -- the exact silhouette of my Pass Example -- carried by two founders whose biographies are better than the category deserves.

Anduril-Luckey is the high rung on lived experience: Palmer shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever pitched defense, and the prior product was real. Replicas does not reach Luckey but it is not the Stanford-CS-discovered-it-in-a-memo failure either -- Loi spent eight months inside Helicone (YC W23) building the AI Gateway and Prompts product, which is the exact LLM-tooling substrate Replicas now wraps. That is adjacent-domain shipping, not market analysis. Where Replicas diverges from the Luckey trajectory is in the founder's *autobiographical* contact with the problem: Loi built observability *for* LLM apps, he did not feel the pain of a backed-up Linear queue at a 200-engineer org and then quit to fix it. The biography is competent and the problem-touching is real, but it is one layer removed from the lived-experience pattern that makes me write a check on the founder alone.

The Pass Example in my own persona file is almost verbatim Replicas's competitive picture -- "the consensus YC AI-agent darling" with multiple tier-1 VCs chasing. The failure mechanism is that the category has already priced certainty: Cognition at $26B post-money and Cursor at a $50B target are not "find them before it becomes consensus" -- they *are* the consensus, and Replicas is a thinner wrapper sitting downstream of both, plus downstream of Anthropic's own Claude Code Remote Tasks (March 20 2026). The pattern absolutely appears in Replicas's dossier: 20+ YC startups as customers, no non-YC enterprise validation, BYO-credential wrapper architecture, and a launch tweet from @connortbot rather than a category-defining product moment. Magically weird is the opposite of what Replicas is. The trade is not aesthetic and it is not transgressive -- it is the most-funded software category of the YC P26 batch.

Replicas's YC page lists Team Size = 1 while tryreplicas.com names two founders, and Arora's LinkedIn still reads "Building something new and Incoming @ ramp" -- which means the operating CEO question is publicly unresolved in May 2026. HQ Trivia (2017) is my own portfolio scar tissue on exactly this seam: Yusupov and Kroll were tactile-product geniuses whose unresolved leadership authority collapsed the company in 2020-21. Two Waterloo CS classmates, one titled "Founder/CTO" and the other titled "Founder," no public statement of tiebreaker authority, and an open Founding Engineer search at 2-4% equity -- the dossier reads like the conversation has not happened yet, and my default of 6/16 for governance silence is calibrated to exactly this absence.

On capital intensity, the eighteen-month compounding question is whether Replicas accumulates any moat that is not commoditized by the model vendor underneath it. Sandbox primitives are available from E2B, Daytona, Modal, and now from Anthropic directly; Replicas's stated differentiation is integration breadth across Slack/Linear/GitHub plus BYO-credential support for Claude Code and Codex. None of that is frontier hardware, none of it is regulated, none of it is aesthetically transgressive -- it is the cleanest possible software-only wrapper, sitting precisely in the lane where a polymath generalist could rebuild the core in a quarter once the model vendors expose richer APIs. The Crusoe analogue is absent: there is no well-pad, no roughneck-trust, no commodity arbitrage, no embedded social license. Replicas is *visiting* the AI-coding space, not *of* it.

For physical-world verifiability, Replicas would need to be something a normal human can install or photograph in their own life within 48 hours to flip a tier -- and the closest artifact is a GitHub PR opened by an agent in someone else's repo. The counterfactual that would move this score is a public sandbox where I could fire a task at a real codebase and watch a VM clone, run, and ship the PR in front of me, the way I rode Uber in 2010 and played Ingress in 2012. Replicas's product page describes the sandbox flow but the dossier surfaces no live demo and no Product Hunt listing -- this is developer-tools abstraction whose value can only be evaluated via founder testimony, the exact pattern my persona file scores below midpoint by default.

Thirty-six month forecast: Replicas is acqui-hired under $50M by Anysphere, Vercel, or Anthropic by late 2027, or it pivots back into LLM observability adjacent to Loi's Helicone DNA, or it is shut down -- the wrapper layer between Claude Code Remote Tasks and the Linear/Slack triggers gets compressed by the model vendor or by Cursor's editor-embedded background agents, and the only durable asset on the cap table is Loi himself. The mechanism is straightforward: when the underlying model vendor ships a competing native feature six weeks before your launch, "BYO-credential and trigger-agnostic" is not a moat, it is a feature waiting to be absorbed.

The strongest bull case is that I am pattern-matching too hard to the Pass Example and missing the Helicone-DNA Unstated Pattern: Loi is, in fact, an outsider-trajectory Waterloo CS founder who shipped real LLM infrastructure at YC W23, which is the same adjacent-domain template that worked for John Hanke (Keyhole into Niantic) and arguably for Max Levchin's relationships with later Affirm. If Cognition's $492M ARR at 13x YoY proves that the category is real and the incumbents are the wrong shape -- proprietary models locking out enterprise BYO buyers -- then Replicas's BYO-credential bet is the contrarian wedge and Loi is the unfair operator inside it. The bear answer is that the consensus signal in this dossier is not VC herding, it is *vendor* herding: Anthropic shipped the substitute on March 20 2026, which is structurally different from a crowded cap table and considerably harder to outrun. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a signed non-YC enterprise customer at >$100K ACV by Q1 2027, with public-statement evidence that they chose Replicas *over* Claude Code Remote Tasks -- not alongside it.
