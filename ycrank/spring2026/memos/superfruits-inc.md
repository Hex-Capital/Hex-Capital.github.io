## The Idea

- **Problem:** B2B sales teams running outbound currently stitch together "buying tools, hiring SDRs & agencies, testing lists, writing messages, and guessing what works" (YC company description, 2026).
- **Approach:** Gojiberry tracks 30+ buying-intent signals (competitor engagement, funding rounds, job changes, LinkedIn post interactions), enriches prospects, and runs personalized LinkedIn + email outreach with reply handling (ycombinator.com, salesforge.ai review, 2026).
- **Differentiation:** vs. Clay — Clay is a self-serve GTM data/enrichment workflow tool ($100M ARR, clay.com blog, 2025), while Gojiberry is an end-to-end agent that "books meetings" without user-built workflows (YC description, 2026); vs. 11x/Artisan AI SDRs — Gojiberry emphasizes intent-signal sourcing on LinkedIn rather than persona-based volume outbound (salesforge.ai review, 2026).
- **Business Model:** Pro tier $99/month (2 LinkedIn senders, unlimited campaigns, 30+ intent signals); Custom tier with negotiated pricing for more senders/CSM (app.gojiberry.ai/pricing via prospeo.io, 2026).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** Founder-led content on LinkedIn/X (Romàn Czerny @romanbuildsaas posting build-in-public threads with ~2M-view tweets, x.com, 2026) plus Product Hunt launch ranked #1 (linkedin.com Czerny post; producthunt.com/products/gojiberry, Nov 2025).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Self-reported compounding data flywheel — "every campaign makes Gojiberry smarter… learns which buyers convert, which signals matter" (YC description, 2026); no third-party validation of model improvement found.
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: A cross-customer signal/messaging dataset could yield better-than-baseline reply rates as customer count grows, but unproven because the company is <12 months old (per YC Spring 2026 batch) and competitors (Clay, 11x) operate similar feedback loops at larger scale.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: No structural barrier identified — LinkedIn outbound automation is a contested category with Clay, 11x, Artisan, Regie.ai already operating at higher funding levels (Crunchbase/TechCrunch, 2024–2026); LinkedIn ToS enforcement against automation (Artisan LinkedIn bans, completeaitraining.com, 2025) is an external risk that constrains all entrants equally.
- **Commoditization risk:** Multiple well-funded entrants offer overlapping intent-based outbound (Clay $210M raised, 11x $76M, Artisan $35M+, Regie.ai $65.6M — TechCrunch/Crunchbase, 2024–2026); the underlying signal sources (job changes, funding announcements, LinkedIn activity) are accessible via third-party data providers.

## Key Risks

- **LinkedIn platform dependency:** Core mechanism is automated LinkedIn outreach via sender accounts (pricing page lists "LinkedIn senders" as the metered unit — prospeo.io, 2026); LinkedIn has enforced bans on competing AI SDR tools, with Artisan AI's Ava reportedly hit with LinkedIn restrictions in 2025 (completeaitraining.com, 2025).
- **Self-reported metrics, limited third-party validation:** $2.5M ARR, 35% MoM, 2,000+ customers all sourced to YC description and founder posts (YC page; linkedin.com 2025–2026); no audited or third-party confirmation. Comparable AI SDR 11x AI had reported ARR figures publicly contested with documented churn (TechCrunch, 2025), establishing category-level scrutiny.
- **Heavily capitalized incumbents:** Clay ($210M raised, $100M ARR, $3.1B valuation — TechCrunch Aug 2025) and 11x ($76M raised — Crunchbase 2024) hold multi-year head starts on data partnerships and brand; Gojiberry's only disclosed funding is the YC standard deal (Crunchbase, 2026).
- **Customer-segment fragility:** $99/month Pro tier (prospeo.io, 2026) implies an SMB/prosumer base; AI SDR category has documented retention problems at this segment (11x churn coverage, TechCrunch 2025) which may also affect Gojiberry's reported 35% MoM growth durability.
- **Pivot recency and product scope creep:** Company pivoted from a services model within the last ~12 months ("$33K Revenue in 4 Months — The Pivot That Worked," superframeworks.com, 2025) and the YC description outlines an expanding roadmap (inbound, CRM updates, account expansion) that overlaps directly with Clay's positioning (clay.com Series C announcement, 2025).
