# Fuchsia -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Fuchsia is a clean single-miracle bet (LLMs parse thousands of pages of FCC/FDA/CE standards into structured requirements) sitting on top of a market with no specific 24-month re-opening catalyst and zero disclosed revenue velocity -- which makes Tony Gao's company architecturally honest but commercially invisible at exactly the moment three YC-funded entrants (Saphira S24, HardwareCompliance March 2026, Fuchsia Spring 2026) are racing the same workflow. That high-architecture / low-signal pairing is the signature, and it's the pairing I've historically passed on even when the underlying thesis is real.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for the catalyst dimension because Anduril had a *specific* 24-month re-opening -- Ukraine exposed Pentagon software inadequacy on a dated timeline that any peer could verify. Fuchsia's dossier offers no equivalent: the "why now" is inferred as "LLMs crossed a threshold for parsing standards," which is the same generic catalyst that justifies every AI workflow company in YC Spring 2026. There is no named FCC rule change, no FDA 510(k) modernization act, no CE Marking deadline that opened a window in 2024-2026. The Testing/Inspection/Certification market grows at 3.6% CAGR globally and 2.2% in the US (Grand View / MarketsandMarkets, 2025) -- those are steady-state numbers, the kind I've watched great teams die inside. Anduril had a dated geopolitical event; Fuchsia has a model-capability tailwind shared with every entrant.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure pattern I keep returning to on the revenue-velocity dimension -- not because Fuchsia is a network-effects bet, but because Lyft taught me that when the dossier is silent on share inversion math, the silence is the answer. Fuchsia's dossier discloses zero MRR, zero pricing page, zero customer count, zero waitlist, and zero open roles -- the founder's `cal.com/tony-gao-fuchsia` link is the entire GTM artifact. The "products certified for Amazon, Apple, and Walmart" line is a testimonial without counterparty, contract value, or date, which is the textbook "false signal" pattern I called out in TechCrunch (Nov 2025). In the AI cycle, silence on growth *is* the negative signal because real growth ships itself into a deck the morning after the demo day rehearsal.

The catalyst dimension compounds badly over 18 months when there isn't one: Saphira AI raised its $500K pre-seed in September 2024 and has had 18 months to lock in safety-critical-robot logos under ISO 26262/10218/IEC 61508, while HardwareCompliance.com launched March 2026 with the broader FCC/CE/FDA/UL stack Fuchsia also claims. Three undifferentiated entrants in an oligopoly market (UL Solutions at ~$2.9B FY2024 revenue, Intertek, SGS, NOA Labs with 10,000+ clients since 2012) is the configuration where the *category* compounds and the *startups* fight for the same 50 hardware-startup pilots.

On the founder dimension, Tony Gao reads closer to the **Perplexity** template than to the pedigree-trap antipattern -- ICRA 2026 co-author on "Subsecond 3D Mesh Generation for Robot Manipulation" at Yale's Apollo Lab is a shipped research artifact, not an MBA line item. What I don't see in Fuchsia's dossier is the Aravind cold-inbound rhythm: no GitHub repos, two unnamed co-founders, no prior independent product shipped to users. Robotics-perception research predicts compliance-workflow shipping cadence weakly at best; the founder-market-fit story relies on Tony Gao having *encountered* FCC/UL pain at Apollo Lab, which the dossier doesn't establish.

The counterfactual that would flip Fuchsia's index-position score: if Fuchsia owned the structured corpus of *post-test outcomes* (which standard sub-clause caused which lab failures across 500+ historical reports), it would become the data layer every incumbent lab eventually integrates against -- a **Stripe**-on-e-commerce position for hardware compliance. Today Fuchsia's dossier shows no such corpus, no lab-partnership count for the two-sided marketplace, and a workflow position *upstream* of the labs that hold the actual certification rails. Upstream of the rail is not the rail.

My 36-month forecast on Fuchsia: acqui-hire by Intertek or UL Solutions at sub-$30M, or quiet wind-down, with a tail probability of pivot to a single-vertical wedge (FCC-only for consumer IoT, where the standards corpus is narrowest) reaching $1-3M ARR but not breakout velocity. The mechanism is the configuration -- single-miracle architecture without a dated catalyst, three competitors inside an 18-month window, incumbents who earn margin on the exact consulting hours Fuchsia automates and can therefore layer AI documentation onto existing customer relationships before Fuchsia reaches escape velocity.

The bull case that stings: **Decagon** (2023) is exactly the workflow-infrastructure-on-an-open-market template I funded, and Fuchsia is structurally identical -- LLM parses unstructured corpus (CS tickets / FCC standards), drafts artifact (response / technical file), human reviews. If Tony Gao is shipping between investor meetings the way Aravind did, and if the Amazon/Apple/Walmart testimonial is actually a $200K+ ACV contract he hasn't disclosed, then Fuchsia is Decagon eighteen months earlier and I should pay up. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull case: Decagon had explosive enterprise revenue visible in the room within months -- "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863). Fuchsia has zero open roles, zero disclosed ARR, and a personal Calendly link as its sales motion. The flip condition is specific: a signed, dollar-disclosed enterprise contract with a named hardware OEM at $200K+ ACV within 90 days, or a published metric showing >20% MoM revenue growth -- absent that, Fuchsia is a Pass for me and I revisit at Series A only if the curve materializes.
