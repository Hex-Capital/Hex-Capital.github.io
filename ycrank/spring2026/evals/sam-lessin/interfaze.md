# Interfaze -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Yoeven Khemlani's second AI-API company inside 18 months, an OpenAI-compatible Chat Completions wrapper priced at $1.50/$3.50 per million tokens, lands as the textbook profile of a competent repeat builder shipping inside the exact sleeve I've publicly de-rated -- "AI is just more internet." Interfaze's analytical signature is a real founder (highest dim) caught in a structurally consensus category (lowest dim), and that pairing produces a deal I cannot make money in regardless of how sharp the team is. Interfaze is not a fraud and not a bad company; it is a wrong-shaped asset at a wrong-shaped round for my book.

Drop.io is my own missionary-temperament rung: a file-sharing primitive bootstrapped against free incumbents for three years before Facebook bought it in October 2010, where the founder kept tacking because nobody else would build it. The shared mechanism is the founder who will not be talked out of the problem -- and Yoeven partly clears it with 8 years of edge-ML pipelines plus the prior travel startup that hit US$3M ARR before acquisition, plus Harsha's IEEE CAI 2026 paper. Where Interfaze diverges from the Drop.io rung is the JigsawStack overhang: Yoeven is publicly still identified as JigsawStack's CEO on LinkedIn and the dossier explicitly flags "unclear corporate boundary" between JigsawStack's "unified AI API suite" and Interfaze's "OpenAI-compatible AI API." Missionary founders don't usually run two companies pointed at the same wedge -- that's opportunism on a hot category, not conviction about an illegible asset, and there is no public long-form writing from Yoeven on why his architecture is non-obvious that I can pre-read.

The canonical YC AI pass from my own pass example -- "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $30M post, four tier-1s in" -- fails because the category is consensus, the round is mis-priced for the regatta tacks ahead, and the financing structure is a SAFE cap I cannot generate alpha against. Trace that failure pattern through Interfaze: the dossier lists Reducto, LlamaParse, Unstructured.io ($65M Series B), Mistral OCR, Docling, Marker, Nanonets, and olmOCR all targeting the same deterministic-extraction wedge. Reducto's own March 2025 benchmark already shows Gemini 2.0 Flash at ~80% on RD-FormsBench -- the substitution curve from frontier models is already closing on Interfaze's claimed 98-99% structured-output gap. Interfaze is the AI-infrastructure sleeve I treat as commodity exposure, not the pre-consensus crypto sleeve where Aleo and Lightning Labs lived for two years before anyone else would underwrite them.

Price is the mechanism that compounds against Interfaze over the next 18 months even if the team executes. The dossier discloses no round size, no valuation, and no anchor investor passing -- silence on round dynamics is the default-9-of-30 I apply because P26 AI-infrastructure pricing in 2026 is the most consensus tape in venture. Interfaze's deliberate OpenAI-compatibility ("any AI SDK that supports OpenAI works out of the box") means switching cost between Interfaze and a frontier model is one base URL change; that is great for adoption and catastrophic for pricing power. Token-price compression from OpenAI and Anthropic, plus Reducto and Unstructured.io discounting to defend installed base, walks Interfaze's $3.50/M output economics down the curve before a Series A can be priced.

Counterfactual on capital structure: what would have to be true for Interfaze to clear my venture-shape filter? The instrument I'd want is something like the Mogilko deal pointed at Yoeven himself -- royalty or holdco exposure to whatever AI-API products he ships across the next decade, since JigsawStack-then-Interfaze suggests serial product-shipping is the actual asset. Instead Interfaze offers a standard usage-priced SaaS cap table on top of inference economics it does not own, with zero disclosed revenue and 50 req/sec stated capacity. To flip Interfaze from default-7-of-22 into the upper rung I'd need to see either a structured royalty on Yoeven's portfolio of AI-API ventures or a Teamshares-style rollup of the parsing/OCR specialist incumbents -- neither of which is on offer.

John Fish / Bookshelved is the founder-as-brand rung I underwrote in January 2024: a creator with measurable owned distribution before the round, where the cap table is buying the audience as much as the product. Yoeven co-hosting the "Agents & APIs SF Developer Meetup" on May 28, 2026 is a meetup, not owned distribution -- the dossier could not even retrieve his @yoeven follower count, Harsha has no public Twitter, and the GitHub @Khurdhula-Harshavardhan star count was not retrievable. Interfaze has zero pre-existing developer community on the cap table; this is paid-API self-serve adoption against named incumbents with bigger funnels, which is the inverse of the Bookshelved setup.

My 36-month forecast for Interfaze: acqui-hire into a parsing/document-platform incumbent (Reducto or Unstructured.io the likeliest buyers) at a $5-25M outcome, or a quiet shutdown after a pivot into a vertical-specific document workflow that fails to clear $1M ARR. The mechanism is the high/low signature: Yoeven and Harsha are good enough to land somewhere soft, and the category is consensus enough that frontier-model substitution and incumbent discounting compress the standalone outcome before Series A.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Reducto's own March 2025 numbers prove frontier OCR really does hallucinate on dense tables, which means the specialist-routing thesis has measured ground truth behind it, not vibes. Yoeven shipped to US$3M ARR once and got JigsawStack to $1.5M pre-seed from Antler and Ada -- this is a builder who finishes things, and Harsha's IEEE CAI 2026 acceptance is a real academic signal that the architecture is non-trivial. Known Exceptions check: does Interfaze look like Robinhood (early entry absorbs later consensus) or Ro (capital intensity is the moat)? Neither -- Interfaze has no early-entry price advantage disclosed and no regulatory schlep that only VC capital can clear; the OCR API stack is software-only and reproducible by any competent ML team, exactly what the dossier flags under commoditization risk. The bull case stings because Yoeven is real, but the dossier evidence that wins is the JigsawStack-Interfaze category overlap plus zero pricing-power moat. The single verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Yoeven publicly winds down or fully merges JigsawStack into Interfaze AND Interfaze closes a sub-$15M-post round with named enterprise LOIs (Fortune 500 counterparty signed) before the YC demo day price discovery. Until then, pass.
