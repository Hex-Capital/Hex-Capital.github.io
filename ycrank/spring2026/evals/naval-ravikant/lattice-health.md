# Lattice Health -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Christine Park is a practicing UW neurosurgery resident with a Duke MEng in AI and a Stanford CS PhD in progress who has shipped arXiv 2511.18208 on multimodal MRI differentiation -- and she is building enterprise infrastructure that lives or dies on hospital procurement cycles and PACS/EHR/HL7/DICOM gatekeepers. That pairing -- top-quartile specific knowledge channeled into a bottom-quartile leverage architecture -- is Lattice Health's signature: the right founder pointed at the wrong leverage primitive for the kind of check I write.

**Anchorage Digital** is the only precedent in my book where I funded a regulated, gated, permission-required infrastructure play, and the override was a four-year pre-consensus crypto thesis I had been compounding since co-founding **MetaStable** (2014) plus an advisor seat. Lattice Health asks me to make a similar exception on leverage architecture without any of the underlying setup -- I have no analogous multi-year pre-consensus position in clinical AI orchestration, no co-founder seat, and the gatekeepers in Lattice Health's path are not the Treasury OCC granting a single charter but a fragmented field of hospital CIOs, IRBs, FDA 510(k) reviewers, Epic, and the Nuance Precision Imaging Network already embedded in PowerScribe radiology workflow. "Permission-as-moat" only flips into a positive when the permission is rare and singular; Lattice Health's permissions are many, slow, and held by parties who do not benefit from a neutral orchestration layer existing.

**The Pinterest miss** is the failure mechanism I now watch for on the network dimension -- I passed because I did not see the compounding user-added value clearly enough, and the lesson was to score the network math, not the aesthetic. Lattice Health does not present the inverse problem; it presents the dimension genuinely missing. Each hospital deploying Lattice Health's orchestration layer does not make the next hospital's deployment more valuable in any direct mechanic -- there is no two-sided flywheel between model vendors (Aidoc, Gleamer, Rad AI) and health systems that an orchestration platform unlocks beyond what Bayer/Blackford already aggregates. The dossier names zero signed LOIs, zero hospital logos, zero model-vendor partnerships -- single-sided enterprise SaaS until proven otherwise, and the Nth customer is economically independent of the (N-1)th.

Christine Park's specific-knowledge profile is the closest thing in this cohort to the **Stack Overflow** (2008) pattern -- a practicing operator in the domain building tools for the domain. The arXiv 2511.18208 paper on radiation necrosis vs. brain metastasis differentiation and the 2022 medRxiv work on point-of-care MRI for midline shift are not market-opportunity-identification artifacts; they are lived specific knowledge that society cannot train into a Stanford CS clone in 18 months. This is genuinely top-quartile on criterion #3 and it is what almost pulls Lattice Health into the Perplexity-style override (Aravind Srinivas's retrieval PhD waiving the network criterion). The waiver does not transfer here because Perplexity passed criterion #1 -- product distribution via permissionless API -- and Lattice Health does not.

The market dimension would have to compound across 18 months in a specific shape for Lattice Health's score to flip -- the >700 FDA-cleared imaging AI devices cited from MarketsandMarkets 2025-2030 would need to fragment further while hospitals refuse to standardize on Microsoft/Nuance or Bayer/Blackford, leaving a true neutral-layer vacuum. That is the world Lattice Health is betting on. The counterfactual that pushes the market score from Pass to Invest is a signed enterprise pilot with a non-academic health system in the next nine months -- absent that, the pivot room Christine Park has is from radiology orchestration into a clinical-research / foundation-model-for-imaging product where her specific knowledge becomes the primary asset rather than the integration layer.

Christine Park's trio -- intelligence is extreme, integrity reads clean across UW Neurosurgery, Duke DIHI, and Stanford bios, and energy is the open question because she is concurrently a PGY-3 neurosurgery resident and a CS PhD student building solo. I would happily spend a decade with this human; the question is whether the next 24 months of her bandwidth can clear an enterprise-sales motion against incumbent platform sales teams at Microsoft and Bayer. Solo founder with two clock-eating institutional commitments is a known-execution-risk profile, not a Buffett-trio failure.

**36-month forecast:** Lattice Health gets acqui-hired by Bayer/Blackford or Microsoft/Nuance in the $15-40M range, or pivots within seed runway into a clinical-research diagnostic-model product (neuro-oncology MRI, point-of-care MRI for stroke) where Christine Park's published specific knowledge -- not orchestration-layer integration -- becomes the primary asset and the company raises a Series A on the pivoted thesis.

The bull case that stings is the **Anchorage Digital** template applied forward: regulated, gated, hard-to-replicate infrastructure can become permission-as-moat when the regulatory complexity itself is the durable barrier, and the >700 FDA-cleared device count is precisely the kind of regulatory complexity that rewards an orchestration layer over time -- Christine Park's clinical credentials are exactly the kind of asset that opens hospital procurement doors a non-clinician founder cannot. The dossier evidence that wins against this bull case is the named incumbent stack -- Nuance Precision Imaging Network already embedded in PowerScribe, Blackford already inside Bayer, GE/Philips/Sectra holding PACS -- meaning Lattice Health is entering a market where the orchestration shelf is occupied by parties with existing hospital contracts and existing radiologist workflow integration. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: two signed paid pilots with named non-academic health systems disclosed by month nine, or a Spearhead-style founder-angel reference from someone who has shipped enterprise clinical AI before.
