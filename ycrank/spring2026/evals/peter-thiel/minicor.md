# Minicor -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Minicor's analytical signature is a regulated-customer anchor (HIPAA, 25,000 patients/day in production) bolted onto a monopoly thesis that six named competitors and two foundation-model labs are converging on simultaneously — the strongest dimension is the healthcare lock-in, the weakest is the moat that lock-in is supposed to defend. Faizaan Chishtie and Saheed Akinbile have built something real; they have not built something whose pricing power survives Anthropic shipping a Windows-native computer-use endpoint in 2027.

The contrarian-secret test is where Minicor lives or dies, and it dies on the first read. **Stripe** in 2010 named a specific incumbent failure mode — PayPal's developer experience was architecturally incompatible with API-first commerce, and the seven Collison brothers' secret was that developers, not merchants, were the actual buyer. Minicor names UiPath's script-brittleness as the failure mode, which is correct as far as it goes, but the dossier itself lists Skyvern ($2.7M, YC, open source), Browserbase ($68M), TinyFish ($47M Series A led by ICONIQ), plus Anthropic computer-use and OpenAI Operator all attacking the same self-healing UI primitive. When the "secret" has six funded competitors and two frontier labs converging on it inside eighteen months, it is not a secret — it is the 2025-26 consensus thesis restated with a Windows skin. The Straussian read of Minicor's pitch is that Chishtie chose the one substrate (legacy Windows desktops, Citrix, EHRs) where the frontier labs will arrive last, which buys time but does not build a moat.

UiPath at ~$1.5B ARR is the relevant low-rung mechanism on monopoly trajectory: a category leader whose moat turned out to be enterprise procurement inertia rather than technical defensibility, which is why Automation Anywhere and Microsoft Power Automate compressed margins the moment the platform shifted. Minicor inherits the same structural problem one layer up — the 93-96% click accuracy claim versus an 80-85% baseline is self-reported, unbenchmarked, and exactly the kind of single-digit edge that disappears the quarter Claude 4.x ships native Windows automation. The dossier explicitly flags that UiPath and Automation Anywhere both announced agentic features in 2025, which means Minicor is racing incumbents downhill while frontier labs race them uphill. There is no monopoly trajectory here, only a window.

Definite-plan analysis: the Laminar Run → Minicor pivot is the diagnostic. Definite optimism compounds when founders commit to architectural decisions they refuse to reverse — **SpaceX** (2008) and reusability is the canonical case. Minicor's founders ran Laminar as a low-code integration platform for three years, did not reach escape velocity, and rebranded into desktop RPA when computer-use models crossed the accuracy threshold in late 2024. The charitable read is founder-market fit through accumulated domain pain; the uncharitable read is wave-riding, and the dossier does not contain the multi-year technical milestone language that would let me distinguish. No named 2027 or 2028 commitment, no architectural line in the sand.

Counterfactual on founder lineage: for Minicor's lineage score to flip a tier, Chishtie or Akinbile would need to be a Levchin-class PayPal-Mafia re-up or a Moskovitz-class shipped-to-scale operator, and they are neither. Chishtie's Phoenix (YC W21) engineering stint and Akinbile's Morgan Stanley Java work saving "$30M/year in fees" are competent IC signals, not founder-pedigree signals. Two University of Ottawa software engineers who built a non-profit together in 2019, then a low-code integration startup, then pivoted — this is earnest, second-time-founder energy, which Paul Graham would weight higher than I do. Determination without contrarianism is mimetic energy. I see no intellectual identity in either founder that the median YC P26 partner would discount and I would value.

Anduril (2017) is the single-anchor parallel on the regulated-sector dimension, and Minicor falls short of it on the axis that matters. Anduril's defense customer base created a moat because the buyer's procurement cycle, certification depth, and integration trust took years to replicate. Minicor's HIPAA certification plus 25,000 patients/day in production is genuine regulatory exposure, but healthcare RPA is not defense RPA — Epic and Cerner integrations are switching-cost moats Minicor would need to win at the EHR-vendor layer, not the hospital-IT layer, and the dossier shows no named EHR partnership. The regulated anchor is real and worth 5/10, but it does not become a Palantir-shaped pricing-power machine on this evidence.

Thirty-six month forecast: Minicor reaches $3-8M ARR in healthcare-vertical RPA and gets acquired by UiPath, Automation Anywhere, or a healthcare-IT consolidator for $40-120M as a self-healing module tuck-in, with the founders rolling into the acquirer for a 24-month earn-out. Not dead — the HIPAA traction is too real for that outcome. Not a fund-returner — the competitive convergence forecloses the pricing-power vacuum that would have made this a power-law bet. The mechanism is straightforward: foundation-model accuracy improves, the 93-96% edge compresses to parity, and Minicor's enterprise contracts become a distribution asset for whichever incumbent buys them.

The strongest bull case I can construct: the Laminar pivot is the feature, not the bug — Chishtie and Akinbile spent three years inside the legacy-integration problem and emerged with the specific insight that desktop automation in regulated verticals is a different business than browser automation, which is why Skyvern and Browserbase are not actually competitors. HIPAA + Citrix + on-prem deployment is a 24-month head start that Anthropic structurally cannot match because frontier labs cannot ship into PHI environments at startup velocity, and that head start compounds into Epic/Cerner integration depth that becomes the actual moat. This is the **Quora** (2010) shape — violate the monopoly purist criterion, override on operator domain depth plus a network/integration effect that compounds at scale. It stings because it is consistent with the dossier. What dismantles it is that Quora had D'Angelo's Facebook-CTO pedigree carrying the override, and Minicor has two competent Ottawa engineers carrying it — the override weight is not there. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a signed multi-year contract with Epic, Cerner, or a top-3 EHR vendor at $2M+ ACV before the end of 2026. Without that, Minicor is a competent acquisition, not a Founders Fund check.
