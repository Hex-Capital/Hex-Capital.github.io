# HEVN, inc -- Elad Gil Evaluation

HEVN, inc is the analytical signature of "scaled consumer crypto operators pivoting into the most consensus fintech category of 2026" — Peter Volnov and Andrei Zhevlakov shipped HOT Wallet to 30M users and HERE Wallet to 600K, which is real distribution muscle, but they're aiming it at a market where Airwallex just printed $1.2B ARR at 85% YoY growth. The founders score genuinely well; the market read scores backwards. That pairing is the whole evaluation.

Nonobvious Market Timing is where HEVN, inc fails my entry-point test. The HIGH rung is Coinbase in 2012 — I wrote a check when crypto infrastructure was dismissed and consensus had not yet formed. The LOW rung is the consumer social apps I pass on every YC batch — stable platform, no exogenous catalyst, founders selling "our specific take." HEVN, inc sits one notch above LOW: cross-border USD banking for non-US SMBs is not a stuck market cracking open, it is the most contested fintech category in 2026, with Airwallex at $1.2B ARR, Mercury at $650M ARR applying for an OCC charter in December 2025, and Capital One closing a $5.15B Brex acquisition. The dossier's "why now" — Brex consolidation creates a vacuum, stablecoin rails matured — is inferred by the analyst, not claimed by HEVN, inc. Check the timestamp: every cross-border SMB neobank already noticed this, which is why Airwallex has 20+ local-currency accounts shipped. The "no-EIN FDIC USD account" is the only genuine wedge and the dossier explicitly marks it "soon," not live.

Operator-Scaler Founder Profile is where HEVN, inc actually earns points. The HIGH rung is Parker Conrad rebuilding Rippling after Zenefits — repeat scaling operator who already navigated the 1-to-100. The LOW rung is a first-time team with no shipping history. HEVN, inc sits closer to HIGH than MID: Volnov, Briuzgin, and Zhevlakov are confirmed multi-year co-founders at HOT Labs who scaled HERE Wallet past 600K users and HOT Wallet past 30M, which is non-trivial international consumer onboarding — exactly the operational muscle HEVN, inc needs for non-US KYB at volume. The discount is real, though: shipping a TON-ecosystem wallet to 30M retail users is not the same as running an FDIC-sponsored US banking program. Pavel Mitsevich, the listed CMO, has zero public footprint, and HOT Labs is reportedly still operating, which raises the bandwidth question.

Infrastructure-Layer Positioning places HEVN, inc between Stripe (HIGH — payments API as default infra) and Wish (LOW — pure consumer marketplace). HEVN, inc is closer to the MID rung — Gusto-style workflow embedment via invoicing, AP, RBAC — but it is an application stitched on top of sponsor banks in EU, US, and UAE rather than an API layer other businesses build on. There is no developer story, no embedded-finance pitch, no platform play disclosed on gethevn.com. Single-Miracle Viability is worse: against Harvey's exogenous one-miracle setup, HEVN, inc is closer to my Anduril rung (multi-miracle) without the generational market timing that justified the Anduril override. HEVN, inc needs a sponsor bank to deliver FDIC USD without an EIN at GA, needs to peel non-US SMB ad-spenders away from Airwallex's existing footprint, and needs an AML/sanctions stack robust enough that bank partners don't pull the plug given the founders' TON/crypto adjacency. Three independent breakthroughs, compounding odds.

Margin Architecture sits between Stripe's interchange take-rate (MID) and Wish's commoditized margins (LOW). HEVN, inc advertises 0.5% card cashback and presumably earns interchange share plus FX spread — standard fintech economics, no evidence of pricing power, no outcome-based pricing, no expansion-revenue signal. At pre-seed I don't penalize hard for absent margin proof, but the contested-category context makes pricing pressure from Airwallex and Wise the base case rather than a tail risk.

My 36-month forecast for HEVN, inc: sub-$3M ARR with the company either acqui-hired into a sponsor-bank stack or pivoted back to a stablecoin-native cross-border rail where the HOT Labs operating experience is actually load-bearing. The mechanism is the dimensional signature — strong operator profile in a consensus market produces a competent product that cannot escape Airwallex's gravitational pull, and the founders' real edge (crypto-native distribution, stablecoin rails) gets repurposed once the neobank thesis hits Airwallex's wall.

The strongest bull case for HEVN, inc invokes my Anduril override: when the market structure shift is generational, accept multi-miracle risk. A bull would argue Capital One absorbing Brex genuinely de-prioritizes international SMBs, Mercury's OCC charter pulls it further into US-only KYB, and the founders' 30M-user crypto onboarding playbook plus stablecoin rails is the right stack to serve a non-US SMB ad-spender who Airwallex's enterprise-leaning machine doesn't touch. It stings because the distribution channel — ad-spend cashback to international founders — is exactly the niche where HOT Labs' growth instincts could compound. I dismantle it on the Anduril test itself: that override required a generational shift, and HEVN, inc has incremental shifts plus a $1.2B ARR incumbent already executing the cross-border SMB thesis. The verifiable condition that would flip me: HEVN, inc signs a named US bank-charter partner delivering FDIC-insured no-EIN USD accounts at general availability and reaches $1M MRR within 12 months on the non-US ad-spender wedge. Until then, this is the consensus market with a good operator team, and consensus markets crush good operator teams.
