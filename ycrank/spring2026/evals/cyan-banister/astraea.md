# Astraea -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Astraea is governance-clean software wearing the wrong jacket -- a two-person agentic-biometrics startup whose strongest dimension is the boring one (Joshua Wang as named CEO, Sanmay Sarada as named CTO, no co-CEO ambiguity) and whose weakest is the one that actually starts my checkbook (a Stanford undergrad with a Baylor data-science internship and a co-founder whose clinical-biometrics background is "not publicly verifiable" per the dossier). Astraea is the inverse of the bet I want to make: clean cap-table optics, hollow origin story.

On Founder Surfaced From Lived Experience -- my heaviest-weighted dimension -- the HIGH rung is Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017), shipping VR from his garage years before the defense pivot, and the LOW rung is the consensus YC AI-agent darling I described in my pass example, two ex-FAANG PMs who discovered the problem in a memo. Astraea sits one rung up from that floor: Sarada's Baylor College of Medicine data-science internship is a real biomedical-data touchpoint, and that pulls Astraea off the absolute bottom, but Joshua Wang as CEO has no verifiable clinical trial, biostatistics, CDISC, or sponsor-side biometrics history that the dossier could surface, and the buyers here are statisticians at Top-10 pharmas who decide on trust signals I don't see Astraea earning. Pinnacle 21 alignment doesn't substitute for a founder who has actually shepherded an SDTM package through an FDA inspection.

On Physical-World Verifiability -- my lowest score -- the HIGH rung is Niantic (~2015), where I had been playing Ingress since 2012 before John Hanke ever pitched me, and the LOW rung is "developer-tools abstraction or B2B API whose value can only be evaluated via founder testimony." Astraea is closer to the LOW rung than I'd like: a 21 CFR Part 11-aligned biometrics platform sold to pharma sponsors via cal.com demo booking is by definition something I cannot install, ride, scan, or photograph in the wild. Astraea's claimed "30-50% faster biometrics cycles" and "12-30 days off post-lock timelines" are exactly the kind of artifact that lives entirely inside a founder's slide -- I'd need a named sponsor to ride along on a study, and the dossier offers only categorical "Top-3 Pharma" logos with no contract values, no case studies, no reviewer-acceptance precedent.

On Magically Weird, the HIGH rung is Crusoe (~2018) burning North Dakota gas flares for Bitcoin and the LOW rung is again that consensus YC AI agent. Astraea is squarely on the LOW side -- "agents for clinical trials" is a 2026 consensus YC pattern, and the dossier names Saama's BRAIN/SDTM Navigator and Bioforum's JETConvert as direct overlap with the same agentic CDISC mapping pitch, with Saama already attached to Pfizer. This isn't a category two credible VCs have passed on for aesthetic reasons; this is the category every AI-vertical fund has a thesis slide on. On Single Unambiguous CEO -- where the LOW rung is the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll feud -- Astraea actually clears my floor: Wang is named CEO, Sarada is named CTO, two-person team, no 50/50 muddle. That is the one heuristic Astraea passes cleanly.

On Capital-Intensive, Frontier-Hardware or Unsexy-Regulated, the HIGH rung is SpaceX (2008) and the MID rung is Vigil Labs (August 2025) in regulated finance. Astraea is software-only but the regulated wrapper is real -- 21 CFR Part 11, HIPAA, GDPR, FDA-defensible outputs are not table stakes for a typical YC SaaS, and biometrics is genuinely unsexy in the way I respect. That pulls Astraea above the "undifferentiated AI app" floor toward the Vigil Labs zone, but without a founder who has lived inside an FDA submission, the regulatory moat compounds for the incumbent (Saama, with $75.8M plus a 2021 financing up to $430M and Pfizer in the door), not for Astraea.

Astraea in 36 months: dead or acqui-hired into a mid-tier CRO for under $20M, because Saama's BRAIN platform deepens its Pfizer-anchored pharma relationships while Astraea's two-person team -- hiring=false per the YC data -- cannot run multiple Top-10 pharma procurement cycles in parallel against an incumbent whose statisticians the buyers already trust. The mechanism is the same one that kills most agentic-vertical bets: the regulated buyer indexes on reviewer precedent, and Astraea has none disclosed.

The bull case to steel-man: this is my DeepMind exception in disguise -- Sarada is a Stanford Symbolic Systems student with BCM research, the polymath cross-domain compound that overrode my pedigree-skepticism for Hassabis (~2013). And Astraea's regulated/unsexy posture is exactly the lane that produced Mindbloom and Vigil Labs. I have to dismantle this honestly: DeepMind worked because Hassabis had already shipped novel game AI and neuroscience publications by the time I saw him -- a polymath with *output*, not a polymath with an internship. Sarada's footprint is an undergraduate research stint and an Instagram handle. The Mindbloom/Vigil parallel breaks because those founders were *of* their regulated category before they pitched it; Astraea's founders are *visiting* clinical trials. The condition that would flip me: Astraea producing one named Top-10 pharma sponsor with a signed Phase II/III biometrics contract and a single FDA-accepted submission package generated end-to-end by the platform. Until then, I pass -- this is the consensus AI-agent vertical bet I trained myself to walk past.
