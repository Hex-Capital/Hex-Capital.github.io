# Andco -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Andco is a software-only entrant with respectable distribution sense and no monopoly secret — exactly the high/low pairing I systematically pass on. Ryan Xue and Michael Lemm bring credible execution from Layup's W23-to-Whatfix exit announced November 25, 2025, and Lemm's Kirkland & Ellis pedigree gives Andco real plaintiff-firm vocabulary. But Andco is the fifth named entrant in a category where EvenUp has already raised $385M at a $2B valuation — meaning the consensus has not only formed, it has priced.

Monopoly Secret is where Andco fails my first test. The HIGH rung here is Palantir — the contrarian thesis that hostile government bureaucracies would integrate proprietary intelligence software so deeply that displacement became structurally impossible, an insight the entire enterprise-software establishment rejected in 2003. The MID rung is Facebook — real-identity networks in closed communities, a real secret but more legible. The LOW rung is Wish — no-moat discount e-commerce competing on price with Amazon. Andco sits between Wish and the empty space below Facebook: "AI agents do pre-litigation case workup across fax/voice/email" is not a secret when EvenUp, Supio (4x ARR growth), and Eve ($47M Series A from a16z) are publicly executing variants of it and the proplaintiff.ai blog is already cataloguing Andco-style tooling. The dossier itself names five competitors — that is not a competitive landscape slide, that is the absence of a secret rendered in bullet form.

Physical-World Moat is the dimension where Andco places lowest, and it matters more than the 10-point weighting suggests because it correlates with everything I have actually written checks for since 2015. The HIGH rung is General Matter — HALEU enrichment, $900M DOE contract, atoms a competitor cannot replicate by raising more capital. The MID rung is Palantir, the one software exception, and only because its government embedding makes displacement a physical-world migration problem. The LOW rung is the generic YC SaaS wrapper. Andco is the LOW rung — a multi-channel agent orchestrating fax retries and Geico claim portals, technically buildable by Eve's $47M war chest in a quarter. We wanted flying cars; Andco is a software layer that books medical-records faxes.

Definite Conviction places Andco between SpaceX (HIGH — reusable rockets on a published roadmap) and the indefinite lean iterator (LOW), closer to MID-rung Affirm but without the contrarian credit thesis. Xue and Lemm have made specific commitments — Filevine integration, multi-channel agent stack, plaintiff-PI vertical — but the homepage's "24+ hours saved per case" framing is operational ROI, not a 10-year vision. Power Law places Andco between Lyft (MID — the known exception, where category magnitude overrode monopoly absence in a duopoly) and Wish (LOW — capped commodity outcome). The $61B PI TAM cited from Fortune October 2025 is real, but Andco enters as #4 or #5 against EvenUp's 2,000+ firms already onboarded — the Lyft exception requires a category that produces a $20B #2, and AI legal workup will not, because B2B switching costs favor the embedded leader, not the runner-up. Distribution is Andco's strongest dimension and sits between PayPal's eBay embedding (HIGH) and an undifferentiated B2B funnel (LOW), closer to MID — "many high volume firms" plus Filevine integration is a real wedge, and Lemm's Kirkland network is the kind of relational moat my unstated patterns actually reward.

In 36 months Andco is acquired by EvenUp, Filevine, or Clio for $40-90M as a talent-and-workflow tuck-in — a Layup-to-Whatfix replay with the same founders, not an independent monopoly outcome — because the category leader at $2B will buy the multi-channel execution layer rather than build it, and Xue and Lemm's demonstrated willingness to sell to a consolidator makes them the obvious sellers.

The strongest case against my pass is the Lyft exception. A $61B PI category, an AI inflection demonstrably moving buyer behavior (Supio's 4x ARR, Eve's 5x in eight months), and a repeat founding team that already executed one acquisition could produce a fund-relevant #2 outcome — Founders Fund made the Lyft bet on exactly this logic. The bear answer wins on dossier evidence: Lyft was a two-player duopoly with consumer-side network effects compounding ride density; Andco enters a five-player B2B field where Filevine's case-management layer is the actual platform and EvenUp has already locked in the demand-letter standard. The condition that flips my verdict: Andco signs an exclusive multi-year integration with Filevine or a top-three plaintiff CMS that makes Andco the default workup engine for >30% of mid-market PI firms within twelve months. Absent that specific embedding, Andco is a competent acquihire candidate, not a fund-returner. Pass.
