# Edviro -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Edviro is two unusually strong teenage builders -- Hursh Shah (Skyglass, NeurIPS 2024) and Tanuj Siripurapu (UCSB CS, MediHacks, ex-RTX) -- pointed at K-12 facilities energy management, a market with no named catalyst in the last 24 months and $400K of *customer savings* standing in for what should be revenue. The dimensional signature is high founder-shipping rhythm paired with the lowest possible revenue-velocity reading; that pairing produces a Paul Graham bet, not an Elad Gil bet, and "market first, team second" (*First Round Review*) is the line I keep coming back to as I read this dossier.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for the catalyst dimension and Edviro fails the comparison cleanly. Anduril worked because Ukraine had visibly broken Pentagon software procurement inside a 24-month window -- a specific, datable re-opening peers were misreading as a graveyard. Edviro's dossier offers no equivalent: the "why now" line is literally bracketed "[Inferred]: LLM-driven agentic workflows crossed the threshold... (no company statement found)," which is the dossier author admitting the catalyst is reverse-engineered rather than named by the founders. K-12 facilities energy management has been a steady-state $26.3B facility-management software market growing 7.6% CAGR per Future Market Insights -- that's the *opposite* of a Stripe-2010 or Coinbase-2013 re-opening. Without the catalyst, the "great teams crushed by terrible markets" failure mode is the base rate.

**Harvey** (2023) is what revenue velocity looks like when something is "just working" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) -- $3B → $5B → $8B in months, with the curve doing the talking. Edviro's traction line reads "$400K+ cumulative customer energy savings" across "1 district partner, 7 school sites, 16 meters," and that's the entire disclosed numerator. Customer savings is not revenue; it is a value-prop slide. There is no MRR, no ARR, no signed-LOI count beyond the single district, and "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) -- in the 2026 AI cycle, silence on growth from a YC S26 company is itself a negative signal because real curves are easy to disclose.

On founder shipping, Edviro is the one place the dossier earns its keep. Hursh Shah at 18 has already shipped Skyglass and landed NeurIPS 2024 research; Tanuj Siripurapu has @TanujKS on GitHub, scaled a digital agency to $20K in high school, and co-founded MediHacks. That's the shipping-artifact pattern I funded **Perplexity** (2022) on -- Aravind pinging me on LinkedIn and showing up a week later with the thing built. The 18-month mechanism here is that these two will out-iterate any BMS incumbent's product team, but iteration speed compounds value only when the underlying market is opening; absent a catalyst, fast shipping into K-12 procurement just means faster discovery that district sales cycles are 9-18 months and reference accounts don't transfer cleanly across state procurement regimes.

**Decagon** (2023) is the single-anchor parallel on index position -- it sits in the workflow path every post-LLM customer-support deployment eventually has to touch, which is why I funded it. Edviro is trying to occupy the analogous workflow seat for building energy, but the dossier flags the structural problem: GridPoint ($584M raised, acquired Oct 2025), 75F, and Atrius/BuildingOS *already sit on the meter data*. Edviro's "software-only, BYO-meter" wedge is a feature on the entry slide and a substitution risk on the exit slide -- the moment any hardware-coupled incumbent ships an agentic anomaly module on data they already own, Edviro's insertion point closes. That's the inverse of Decagon, which sat on a category with no incumbent data-gravity.

Counterfactual on the single-miracle test: for Edviro to flip from a 6/12 to a 9/12 on this dimension, the dossier would need to isolate the "energy world model" as the one validated capability with everything else being GTM execution. Instead it stacks three independent miracles -- world-model accuracy across heterogeneous HVAC/boiler/heat-pump assets (no benchmarks published), reliable agentic dispatch into physical building systems (liability surface unaddressed), and K-12 district procurement at scale (founders are 18 and 19 with no prior energy-industry employment). Compounding three independent ~30% probabilities is the math that keeps me out of science-adjacent deals, and "energy world model" is closer to a research claim than a shipped capability today.

Forecast: 36 months out, Edviro is either acquired-for-talent by a BMS incumbent like the successor to GridPoint or by Atrius/Acuity for under $20M, or it has pivoted off K-12 into datacenter energy management where the AI-power-demand catalyst is actually live and the buyer is a hyperscaler rather than a school district business manager. The mechanism is straightforward -- iteration speed without catalyst plus single-district concentration plus hardware-incumbent data gravity equals a stall under $500K ARR before Series A.

The strongest bull case is the **Anduril** Known Exception: I overrode "avoid science projects" because Stephens and Luckey had shipped at scale and the catalyst was specific. The Edviro bull would argue Shah and Siripurapu are the K-12-energy equivalent -- shipping founders in a market peers ignore, with the agentic-workflow catalyst being the 2024-25 step-function. The case stings because I've been wrong on age before, and the workflow-on-top-of-hard-thing pattern (Anduril on defense hardware, Color on commodity sequencing) does map structurally to "software on existing meters." It dies on two facts the dossier puts on the page: Anduril's founders had decade-plus shipping track records inside the *exact* customer's procurement world; Shah and Siripurapu have zero disclosed energy-industry employment. And Anduril had a *named* 24-month catalyst (Ukraine); Edviro's catalyst is an inferred LLM-capability claim with "no company statement found." The verdict flips to Invest if Edviro discloses MoM revenue growth above 20% across three or more named district contracts within 6 months, or signs a hyperscaler datacenter pilot that re-anchors the company into the actually-catalyzed energy market.
