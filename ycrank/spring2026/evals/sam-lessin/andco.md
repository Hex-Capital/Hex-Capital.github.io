# Andco -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Andco is the cleanest expression of "Adobe-for-AI" I've seen this batch — sane, credentialed repeat founders (Ryan Xue and Michael Lemm just sold Layup to Whatfix in November 2025) attacking the single most consensus-priced legal-AI niche in venture, where EvenUp already sits at a $2B valuation with $385M raised. The signature is high founder quality, low everything-else: nothing about Andco itself is mispriced, new, or structurally durable. It is a feature that EvenUp, Supio, or Eve will absorb — and probably one of them eventually buys these guys.

The lowest rung for Andco is Consensus Mispricing, and the ladder is brutal here. HIGH rung: Solana at four cents when every smart crypto person told me Ethereum had won — that's what mispricing looks like, the risk fully baked in, the category radioactive. MID rung: Venmo, where P2P mobile money wasn't hot but wasn't hated either. LOW rung: any 2025 AI copilot at a premium, ten VCs bidding, upside already in the price. Andco sits *below* the LOW rung. EvenUp doubling ARR YoY, Supio up 4x since August 2024, Eve up 5x in eight months, $523M of disclosed competitor capital, "record high" PI legal-tech funding per Crunchbase October 2025 — Andco is the most consensus-pumped category I could pick out of the X26 batch with a dartboard. Where's the asymmetry? It isn't here.

Primitive Creation is the second-lowest. HIGH rung: Venmo, which became a verb and birthed a behavior. MID rung: Patreon, direct creator payment as a new primitive. LOW rung: "Better CRM," execution in an existing market. Andco is closer to LOW: the dossier literally describes the workflow ("pulling police/accident reports, opening claims with Geico and Progressive, requesting medical records") as the existing paralegal job — Andco automates it, it doesn't invent it. Nobody will say "I Andco'd the carrier" the way they say "Venmo me." The "24+ hours saved per case" framing on useandco.com is an efficiency claim, which is the tell — primitives don't measure themselves in hours saved, they measure themselves in behaviors that didn't exist.

The highest dimension for Andco — and it's not that high — is Sanity-Insanity. HIGH rung: Raj before Solana, three companies of watching him build, calm engineer pitching a new L1 that everyone said was dead on arrival. MID rung: Butterfield at Slack, proven founder, sensible idea. LOW rung: combative founder pitching the validated thing. Andco's team — Xue (ex-Layup CEO, Cornell, McKinsey, Origin), Lemm (Harvard Law JD, Kirkland & Ellis, ex-Layup COO, *actual lawyer*), Lee (CTO with Seek AI→IBM and DataFox→Oracle on his resume) — clears the sanity bar cleanly. The Layup→Whatfix exit November 25, 2025 is a real datapoint. But the ratio breaks on the other axis: nothing they're pitching is insane. "AI for plaintiff PI paralegals" is the most reasonable, most VC-approved idea in the room. Sane person + sane idea = a fine business, not my kind of bet.

Structural Durability fails the AI kill test on contact. HIGH rung: Metropolis parking, which I seeded and is now ~$5B because parking infrastructure cannot be commoditized by an LLM. LOW rung: AI wrappers Adobe will replicate in a quarter. Andco is at the LOW end — multi-channel agent orchestration over fax, voice, SMS, email is exactly the capability EvenUp's $385M war chest can build, and Supio's medical-records depth means they're already halfway there. The Filevine integration is a switching-cost claim, not a moat; Filevine itself can build this, or partner with the highest bidder. No IP, no regulatory license, no proprietary dataset disclosed. Capital Discipline gets the absent-evidence default — the founders just had an exit, so they're presumably not desperate, but there's no signal either way and the category dynamics will force them into the factory-farm raise cadence to keep up with EvenUp.

Thirty-six month forecast: Andco gets acquihired by EvenUp, Supio, or Filevine for $25–60M before reaching $10M ARR — the same outcome shape as Layup→Whatfix, which is the pattern these founders have now executed once. Mechanism: incumbents with $91M–$385M of capital extend laterally into pre-litigation workup faster than Andco can build defensible lock-in, and the cleanest exit becomes a tuck-in to a CMS or a category leader who needs the multi-channel agent stack. That's a fine outcome for the founders. It's not a fund-returner.

The bull case stings, so let me actually engage it. Lemm is a real Kirkland attorney, which is genuine founder-market fit on the *legal* side that EvenUp's team doesn't obviously have; Xue and Lemm have shipped a YC company to exit before, so this isn't first-rodeo execution risk; and "live with many high volume firms… hundreds of cases and thousands of requests each month" by March 2026 launch is a real traction signal at pre-seed. Slack is the Known Exception that haunts me here — I paid consensus prices for Butterfield because the primitive was real. Does Andco get that override? No. Slack created synchronous workplace messaging as a new behavior; Andco automates an existing paralegal job. The override requires a primitive, and there isn't one. **Verdict-flipping condition**: if Andco shows me a signed enterprise contract with a top-20 PI firm at $500K+ ACV with named retention and EvenUp/Supio explicitly evaluated and rejected — meaning the buyer believes the multi-channel execution layer is a *different product* from demand-letter generation — I'll reconsider. Until then, pass.
