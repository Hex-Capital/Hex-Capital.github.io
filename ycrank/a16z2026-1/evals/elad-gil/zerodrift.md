# ZeroDrift -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Global Relay's data shows ChatGPT capture in regulated financial communications surged roughly 3,000% year-over-year. That single statistic encodes the structural shift driving ZeroDrift's timing. When AI agents — not just humans — generate outbound communications at scale inside broker-dealers and asset managers, post-send surveillance architecturally breaks. Smarsh (6,500+ customers, Gartner MQ Leader) and Global Relay ($189M revenue, 20K+ customers) built their entire business models on archiving communications *after* delivery. Adding pre-send enforcement doesn't just require new technology for them — it cannibalizes their core product positioning and conflicts with the buyer relationships they've spent decades cultivating, where compliance teams purchase surveillance tools to monitor *what already happened*. This is a genuine innovator's dilemma dynamic, and it maps to a pattern I've seen work: Harvey entered legal technology when incumbents were built around legal research, not AI-assisted document production. ZeroDrift is entering communication compliance at a structurally analogous moment. The timing is real. But I'd grade the nonobviousness at moderate rather than high — Greenboard just raised $20.5M from General Catalyst and Base10 for a compliance operating system targeting the same buyer persona, and "AI compliance" as a category is entering investor consciousness. ZeroDrift isn't seeing something nobody else sees; it's seeing something specific — the pre-send enforcement layer — that most competitors haven't yet built.

The infrastructure-layer question is where ZeroDrift gets more interesting than a typical compliance application. The "communication firewall" architecture — sitting between an organization's internal tools and external recipients — is middleware, not a point solution. The rulepack library, encoding SEC, FINRA, and firm-specific policies into machine-readable rule sets, is the real asset. Every new client adds regulatory edge cases to ZeroDrift's encoding corpus; every integration across Outlook, LinkedIn, ChatGPT, and Copilot deepens the switching cost. Ripping out ZeroDrift means re-encoding your firm's entire compliance policy stack and re-integrating across every communication channel. That compounds. It's not Stripe-level infrastructure — ZeroDrift isn't an API that other companies build products on — but it's substantially more embedded than a typical SaaS application. The three-module design (Compose for rule definition, Guard for enforcement, Command for audit trails) suggests platform thinking, not feature thinking. SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certifications, already achieved at pre-seed, reinforce this: regulated buyers require these as table stakes, and achieving them early creates a real time-cost barrier for new entrants targeting the same accounts.

Kumesh Aroomoogan spent 12 years building AI/NLP products for financial services clients at Accern, working directly with the global banks and asset managers who are now ZeroDrift's target buyers. Accern raised approximately $40M across nine rounds, was acquired by Wand AI in 2025, and Aroomoogan carries Forbes 30 Under 30 recognition from 2018. This is exactly the operator-founder profile that gets my attention: someone who lived inside the scaling challenges of enterprise AI sales to financial institutions and is now attacking a specific pain point he watched compliance teams struggle with for a decade. His claim of leading fundraising to a "$1B valuation" at Wand AI couldn't be verified against Crunchbase data showing $33M raised — that discrepancy warrants diligence but doesn't invalidate the core founder-market fit. The single-founder structure with no publicly identified co-founder is a moderate concern at six FTEs; the High Growth Handbook is explicit that clear decision-making hierarchy matters, but so does having complementary leadership. At this stage, it's a watchpoint, not a dealbreaker.

ZeroDrift's single-miracle profile is clean. The technology miracle — LLMs capable of real-time, accurate compliance checking of outbound communications — is happening exogenously as foundation models improve on their own capability curve. SEC and FINRA frameworks already exist; no regulatory breakthrough is required. Financial institutions are proven, budget-authorized buyers of compliance technology — there's no "who pays?" problem here (unlike EdTech, where I've never invested). The one miracle ZeroDrift needs: can they shift compliance teams from a reactive, post-send surveillance model to a proactive, pre-send enforcement model? That's a behavioral adoption miracle, not a technology or regulatory one. The $150K ARR with 50%+ month-over-month growth — if accurate — suggests that adoption miracle may already be underway, roughly two months post-launch. The $2M qualified pipeline naming Franklin Templeton, Nasdaq, Citi, Two Sigma, and Vanguard is directionally encouraging, though "qualified pipeline" at enterprise financial institutions is a long way from closed ARR.

The thesis-specific risk I'd watch most closely: platform dependency. ZeroDrift's enforcement layer requires integration at the point of content creation across Microsoft Outlook, LinkedIn, and ChatGPT. If Microsoft restricts third-party intercept of outbound messages — which they've done before in other contexts — ZeroDrift's core enforcement mechanism breaks. This isn't speculative; platform API changes have killed companies in this position. The mitigation would be achieving enough enterprise penetration that Microsoft treats ZeroDrift as a partner rather than a parasite, which requires rapid scaling of the enterprise book of business. Evidence that would flip me from Invest to Pass: if the $150K ARR turns out to be heavily concentrated in one or two pilot accounts rather than distributed across multiple firms, or if Smarsh announces a pre-send enforcement module bundled into existing contracts. Evidence that would increase my conviction: independently verified ARR growth, a signed contract (not pipeline) with any of the named institutions, and Microsoft partnership or marketplace listing.

ZeroDrift fits my framework on the dimensions that matter most at pre-seed. The market structure timing is real — AI-generated content is creating a new compliance surface area that incumbents are architecturally mispositioned to address — even if the category is entering early consensus rather than being deeply nonobvious. The rulepack-based enforcement middleware creates genuine switching costs that compound with each client and integration. Aroomoogan's 12-year track record building and exiting an AI company in this exact vertical gives me confidence he can navigate enterprise sales cycles at Citi and Vanguard. The single-miracle profile — behavioral adoption, with technology and regulation already favorable — is the kind of bet I'm comfortable making with my own capital.
