# Intelligence Factory -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Intelligence Factory's signature in my book is a credentialed-roboticist contrarian thesis -- "robots fail not because they can't see but because they can't feel" -- paired with zero documented founder pivot evidence, which means Yash Sinha and Jalaj Shukla are betting the right inverted bet against vision-language-action orthodoxy while giving me no read on whether they can update when the tactile-data scaling law wobbles. The technical-risk dimension is where Intelligence Factory scores highest; the learning-rate dimension is where it scores lowest. That pairing tells me what kind of company this is: a thesis-correct pre-seed in a category whose incumbents already raised $2B+, where the open question is not "is the science interesting" but "will these two founders rewrite the plan when Skild buys a tactile rig in 2027."

**Genesis AI** (2025) is the load-bearing analogy for Intelligence Factory on technical and capital risk -- Eclipse and I co-led $105M of seed into a robot foundation model because the stack-deep play required custom data, custom model, and a deployment surface no consensus VC would underwrite at seed. Intelligence Factory is running the same shape one rung smaller: a custom hardware device that captures force and tactile feedback from human demonstrations, then a foundation policy trained on that proprietary modality. Where Intelligence Factory diverges from Genesis is throughput -- Genesis went full-stack with a working demo I could watch (TechCrunch 2026-05-06), while Intelligence Factory's intelligence-factory.com is a single-page placeholder and there is no released benchmark, no video, no policy weights. The miracle count is real (tactile data collection at scale, contact-rich policy generalization, sim-to-real on force feedback), each is falsifiable, and consensus VCs will decline -- but the absence of any shipped artifact at YC Spring 2026 stage drops Intelligence Factory below the Genesis rung rather than next to it.

The **GPT-4-sales-summarizer pre-seed** I pass on in 30 seconds fails on learning rate because the founder narrative is one linear plan with no pivot, no public thesis update, no "we were wrong about X" moment -- and that exact silence shows up in Intelligence Factory's dossier. Yash Sinha was Co-founder/CTO at Kaio Labs before Intelligence Factory, which is a prior company but the dossier records no exit, no documented pivot, no public reasoning about what he learned and discarded. Jalaj Shukla's path is GRASP Lab to DRDO to Blue Sky Robotics to Applied Materials to Ansys to Intelligence Factory -- linear robotics employment, not the Slash-Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C. I am not penalizing Intelligence Factory for being early; I am penalizing the absence of any "I changed my mind" signal that would tell me whether the tactile thesis survives contact with reality.

On consequence magnitude, Intelligence Factory loads onto the right civilizational variable -- general-purpose manipulation is a direct claim on the $15 trillion of U.S. GDP that is labor (Fortune 2026-03-06) -- but the dossier names no specific incumbent regime that collapses. "Industrial manipulation" is not "displaces diesel long-haul" or "removes the need for animal protein"; over 18 months the consequence frame either compounds (Intelligence Factory publishes a contact-rich benchmark that names which factory tasks die) or it dilutes into the same generic "robot brain" pitch Skild and Physical Intelligence already own. Intelligence Factory scores into the labor-displacement frame by association, not by articulation.

On founder defiance, Intelligence Factory would flip a tier upward if Yash Sinha or Jalaj Shukla had published the inverted thesis publicly -- a paper, an essay, a tweet arguing that vision-language pretraining is structurally insufficient for contact-rich manipulation and tactile-first imitation is the only path. The dossier carries the contrarian claim ("can't feel, not can't see") only as a search snippet describing them, not as a position they have staked publicly the way Pat Brown staked his Stanford reputation on plant protein at **Impossible Foods** (2011). The defiance is latent in the architecture choice, not committed in the public record -- which is exactly the "instigator" gap I flagged in my TED Talk framing.

On capital profile, Intelligence Factory parallels **Physical Intelligence** (2024) -- Levine and Finn from Berkeley and Stanford, capital-deep roadmap, license-to-OEM business model. Intelligence Factory's roadmap will need Khosla-scale capital ($50M+ to a credible policy, $200M+ to scale data collection against Skild's $1.4B war chest), but at YC Spring 2026 pre-seed with Comma Capital and Allen Gannett on the cap table, I am over-resourced for the round and the round is under-resourced for the science. That is a follow-on signal, not a today signal.

My 36-month forecast for Intelligence Factory: acqui-hired by Skild AI, Physical Intelligence, or Figure for $40M-$120M on the strength of the tactile-demonstration rig and the GRASP/ETH talent, with no independent foundation-model commercial release. The mechanism is capital asymmetry -- Skild's $1.4B raise at $14B valuation (TechCrunch 2026-01-14) means the incumbents will buy tactile capability faster than Intelligence Factory can compound it from a YC seed, and the proprietary hardware-plus-dataset is exactly the shape that gets bought rather than out-competed.

The strongest objection to my forecast is the bull case that vision-language-action models are hitting a contact-rich wall publicly -- pi-zero generalizes on grasping but stumbles on assembly, and Skild Brain's omni-bodied claim does not solve insertion tasks -- which means Intelligence Factory's tactile-first inversion is not a feature an incumbent buys, it is the only path to the next capability tier, and Skild would need to acquire Intelligence Factory at a premium that resembles **DeepMind-Google** rather than acqui-hire economics. That bull case stings because it matches my own Physical Intelligence thesis. What dismantles it is the dossier evidence: zero published benchmark, zero released model, zero customer LOI, and a five-person team against Skild's $30M revenue and Figure's $39B valuation -- meaning even if Intelligence Factory is thesis-correct, the incumbents will replicate the tactile stack faster than Intelligence Factory can defend it. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a published Intelligence Factory benchmark on a contact-rich manipulation suite (e.g., NIST assembly board, cable routing, deformable insertion) that beats pi-zero or Skild Brain by a margin Yash Sinha will commit to publicly -- that artifact, before Series A, makes Intelligence Factory the Physical Intelligence of tactile and I write the check.
