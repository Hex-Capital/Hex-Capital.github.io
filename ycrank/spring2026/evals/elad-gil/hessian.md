# Hessian -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Hessian is what happens when a high-execution founder team picks the analytical inverse of the position I want to own: Tom Watson migrated a >£1B/day GoCardless payments database with zero downtime and James Smith ran billions of Bloomberg observations through entity-disambiguation pipelines, but Hessian's chosen wrapper around that talent is a forward-deployed services motion competing against Distyl AI at $1.8B post and $207M raised (SiliconAngle, Sep 2025). The signature pairing here is *founder shipping rhythm at the top of the ladder, index-distribution at the bottom* -- which is the diagnostic profile of a consultancy that scales linearly in headcount, not the **Stripe** (2010) profile of infrastructure that compounds in the rails.

**Perplexity** (2022) is my reference for founder execution rhythm -- Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing we'd discussed built (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). Hessian's three founders score real on the *prior* shipping artifact: Watson's GoCardless DB migration is a named, externally verifiable production ship, Smith's 5-year Bloomberg data-platform tenure plus 1-year DRW commodities-desk build is direct fintech-ops domain time, and Bao Nguyen's Zettafleet came out of stealth Feb 23 2026 per Sifted, so this isn't paper credentials. Where Hessian diverges from the **Perplexity** rung is that the dossier shows *prior-employer* artifacts, not the between-meetings cadence -- the GitHub profiles aren't found, there's no public side-project trail, and the only live telemetry on hessian.sh (15 runs/24h, 7% failure rate, P95 14.2s) is consistent with one named customer (Fazeshift) rather than a shipping-machine rhythm.

**Lyft** Series C is where I learned that being #2 in a market with a clear leader is just a worse version of the obvious bet (20VC interview), and Hessian's distribution position is structurally the same failure mode one rung down: Distyl AI is already running forward-deployed engineers plus a proprietary agent platform at $1.8B and $207M raised, Sierra is at $104M ARR and $10B post, Decagon is at ~$35M ARR with a $1.5B mark (Sacra, Nov 2025), and Cresta did $52M ARR (getlatka). Hessian doesn't sit in the workflow path that every entrant in AI ops must touch -- Hessian sits *next to* four better-capitalized FDE shops fighting for the same Fortune-500 procurement slot. The services model is anti-index by construction: each customer is bespoke, each integration is one-time, and the "operating data compounds into templates" mechanism is unproven at a 3-person team with one disclosed logo.

The catalyst Hessian names -- coding agents matured enough that 3 engineers can run forward-deployed engagements -- is real and 24-month-recent, but it's not Hessian-specific. The same tool-use inflection that lets Hessian run lean is what funded Distyl's $207M round; the catalyst opens a window for the category, not a moat for the entrant. Over 18 months this dimension compounds against Hessian rather than for it, because every additional month of frontier-model capability narrows the FDE-headcount advantage Hessian's whole pitch depends on -- the leverage Hessian needs in order to undercut Distyl is the same leverage Distyl gets for free.

Revenue velocity is the dimension where the score would flip a tier on one verifiable disclosure: if Hessian published five named non-YC customers at $100K+ ACV signed in the last 90 days, this moves from "AI company with no growth curve disclosed" -- which I've publicly called "something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) -- into the **Harvey** (2023) "just working" zone. The dossier shows one customer, Fazeshift, who is themselves a YC company, plus a "saving customers hundreds of hours a month" claim on the YC page with no dollar figure and no pricing on hessian.sh. Counterfactually, if any of the four Distyl/Sierra/Decagon/Cresta logos churned to Hessian, that would be the share-inversion signal -- the dossier doesn't show it.

**Decagon** (2023) is the single-anchor parallel for single-miracle architecture: Decagon assumed one validated capability -- LLM accuracy on support transcripts -- and treated the rest as GTM execution, which is why I funded it. Hessian's single miracle is similar in shape ("frontier models + tool-use let small teams run bespoke ops automation"), but Hessian piles a second commercial miracle on top: the services-to-product transition that has killed every consultancy that tried to escape into recurring software margins. Two stacked miracles is where I usually pass; the only reason I'm not at floor here is that Bao Nguyen's Zettafleet work and Watson's GoCardless infra background suggest the team understands the platform layer, not just the engagement layer.

My 36-month forecast on Hessian: $3-12M services-heavy ARR by mid-2029, acquihired into Distyl, Palantir's commercial group, or a Big-4 AI practice (Accenture/Deloitte) at $40-150M, or pivoted into a vertical finance-ops product wedge (invoice reconciliation or month-end close specifically) to escape the Distyl shadow. That outcome is consistent with the signature -- elite execution rhythm without an index-distribution position is exactly the profile of a team that becomes a valuable absorption target rather than a durable standalone, the *consigliere* outcome rather than the empire-builder outcome.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Color Genomics** (2015) precedent in my own Known Exceptions: I architected Color as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing, and Hessian could be read as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity frontier models -- the same pattern that lets me harvest a hard-tech tailwind without taking the hard-tech risk. Watson and Smith's fintech-ops depth maps directly onto my *AI Market Clarity* "wide open" sub-categories (accounting, compliance, financial tooling), and three Durham CS graduates with one Cambridge MPhil and three Cambridge scholarships shipping at GoCardless and Bloomberg is exactly the operationally-disciplined archetype *High Growth Handbook* (2018) profiles. Where this case breaks: **Color** was a category I *operated* and where no $1.8B incumbent was running the same playbook -- Hessian's category has Distyl already executing the identical FDE-plus-platform thesis at 50x the capitalization, and at consensus YC pre-seed prices the math doesn't pencil unless Hessian shows a distribution wedge the dossier doesn't disclose. The single verifiable condition that flips this to invest: five named non-YC enterprise logos signed in the next 6 months at disclosed six-figure ACVs -- absent that, this is a pass I would write the same way I wrote **Lyft**.
