# PerfectBit -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

PerfectBit is a pedigree-stacked pure-software bet on a category whose technical core is already on arXiv ? two former Meta foundation-model leaders, Peter Vajda (11 years, Director of Media Generation, Emu/Movie Gen) and Seiji Yamamoto (9 years, Senior Staff in Core Llama / AGI Foundations), selling "verifier-grounded training data" into the ten-to-twenty frontier labs that ship the actual models. The signature of PerfectBit is high founder lineage and low structural moat: a credentialed-operator team in a category that, per llm-stats.com 2026 and arxiv.org 2601.22607, is being run in-house by their own former employer.

The contrarian-secret ladder runs from **Stripe** (2010) at the top ? Patrick Collison naming the developer-experience failure mode of legacy processors PayPal had supposedly "won" ? through **Affirm** (2014) in the middle, where Levchin named the underwriting blind spot of card issuers ? down to the generic "AI for X" pass at the floor. PerfectBit's secret is that frontier-lab training data should be "correct by construction" via formal proof systems and physics simulators rather than human contractor labeling ? this names Scale AI, Surge AI, and Mercor as the specific incumbent failure mode and points to a real architectural blind spot in their contractor-operations moat. The problem is that the secret is not actually hidden: the EigenData paper (arxiv.org 2601.22607, Jan 2026) and the opendilab/awesome-RLVR list publish the technique openly, and llm-stats.com 2026 confirms frontier labs are already running verifier pipelines internally. PerfectBit sits between the MID and LOW rungs ? a real failure-mode call against the human-labelers, but a thesis the frontier labs themselves already share, which by definition is not a Zero to One secret. Vajda and Yamamoto came from inside the building where this is being built; that is conviction, not concealment.

The atoms-heavy / regulated-sector ladder runs from **SpaceX** (2008) and **Anduril** (2017) at the top ? Lockheed/Raytheon cost-plus failure mode plus literal capex moat ? through **Palantir** (2003) in the middle, where the moat was government certification depth on pure-ish software ? down to commoditizing SaaS at the floor. PerfectBit is a two-person operation in San Francisco delivering software-generated datasets to commercial frontier labs; there is no regulator in the loop, no hardware capex, no security-clearance compounding. The dossier records "no defensibility signals" in public sources and the ten-to-twenty buyer set is the same one Scale AI ($14B), Surge AI ($15-25B 2025 talks), and Mercor ($450M+ ARR) are already inside. PerfectBit sits at the LOW rung, the canonical pattern Founders Fund's 2011 manifesto was written against ? bits, not atoms, in a category where the buyer can insource.

The monopoly trajectory for PerfectBit looks like **Stripe** at the high rung (integration moat compounding), **Palantir** at mid (government switching costs), and the AI-for-X pass at the bottom (commodity pricing at maturity). PerfectBit has no named pricing-power hypothesis in the dossier, faces direct expansion threats from Snorkel AI on the programmatic-labeling axis and AfterQuery on the expert-curation axis, and the buyer base is the most sophisticated insourcing population on earth ? Meta, where the founders came from, has Yamamoto's old team. PerfectBit sits near the LOW rung. On the definite-plan dimension ? **SpaceX**'s 15-year reusability roadmap at the top, **Palantir**'s government plan in the middle, lean-iteration at the bottom ? PerfectBit's site describes a workflow (verifier-stack selection, agent-verifier loops, trace delivery) but commits to no multi-year named milestone the founders will not pivot from across LLMs, robotics, and AI-for-Science; that breadth is itself an indefinite-optimism tell, three domains rather than one definite bet.

Founder lineage is where PerfectBit actually scores. The ladder runs from **Affirm** (Levchin, PayPal Mafia) at the top through **Asana** (Moskovitz, shipped Facebook to scale) in the middle to current-batch first-timers at the bottom. Vajda led Emu and Movie Gen at Director level; Yamamoto led architectural and post-training work inside Core Llama with PNAS and Physical Review Letters publications. This is the **Asana** rung ? not PayPal Mafia, but operators who shipped frontier-grade product to scale at a company whose generative-AI stack is one of three or four in the world that matter. PerfectBit sits between MID and HIGH on lineage, and this is the only dimension carrying the dossier.

PerfectBit in 36 months: acqui-hired by Meta, Anthropic, or Google DeepMind for $40-100M as a verifier-engineering team-buy, with no standalone category leadership and ARR under $15M at the point of sale ? the **Asana** override does not apply here because Moskovitz had Facebook-scale operator pedigree against a software-only consumer-workflow moat that compounded through network effects, whereas PerfectBit's buyers are the same six companies that can hire Vajda and Yamamoto directly. The mechanism is buyer-concentration plus open-research commoditization: when ten labs can each insource the thing, the equilibrium is acquisition of the team, not a power-law standalone outcome.

The strongest bull case for PerfectBit is the **Palantir** (2003) shape ? a category Silicon Valley initially misread, where the moat turned out to be integration depth and trust with a concentrated buyer base that VCs systematically mispriced. If verifier stacks across physics, theorem-proving, and scientific oracles accumulate into a library that takes three years and a PhD-heavy team to replicate, and if Vajda and Yamamoto's Meta relationships compound into exclusivity arrangements with two of the top five labs, PerfectBit becomes the Scale AI of the RLVR era at $200M+ ARR. The case stings because PNAS-and-Phys-Rev-Letters-grade founders building verifier stacks is exactly the credentialed-outsider technical-pedigree profile I overweight. It loses to the dossier because the buyers are not government ? they are the most technically capable insourcing population in the world, three of whom Yamamoto has worked alongside. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a disclosed multi-year contract with Anthropic, OpenAI, or xAI carrying volume commitments above $10M and a meaningful exclusivity clause on a specific verifier domain. Absent that, PerfectBit is a team-buy, not a fund-returner.
