# Zomma -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Zomma is a consensus computer-use-agent pitch dressed in a regulated-finance coat, and the coat is the only interesting garment on the rack. Three co-founders, no named CEO, ex-JPMorgan and ex-Modern Intelligence pedigrees, "/call" demo funnel, and Anthropic itself shipping ten pre-built KYC and month-end-close agent templates plus a $1.5B JV with Goldman, Blackstone, and H&F in May 2026 (Fortune, May 2026). The dimensional signature is a company that lives in a category I like -- unsexy, regulated, hedge-fund back office -- inside a lane that is now the most crowded fairway in the S26 batch. That pairing is the whole story.

Anduril in 2017 is the ladder rung Zomma is being asked to stand next to on lived-experience -- Palmer Luckey shipped VR from his parents' garage, then walked the defense problem because he had already lived the hardware-shipping half. The mechanism that made me write Anduril was that the founder brought a shipped artifact and a biography to the problem, not a market memo. Varun Arumugam is ex-JPMorgan, Cashel Fitzgerald did a tour at Voyant on financial-planning software, and Jihyun Kim's public profile Zomma's own researcher could not disambiguate. Ex-JPMorgan is exactly the "Goldman or McKinsey" surface I flag as pedigree-as-thesis; the Voyant SWE stint is the one honest finance-adjacent line in the whole team page, and it is not enough to say any of these three founders sat inside a hedge-fund ops chair at 11pm on the last day of the quarter reconciling a broken custodian portal by hand. That's the moment Zomma is selling, and no one on the Zomma team has publicly lived it.

HQ Trivia is the ladder rung I keep returning to on co-founder governance, and its failure mechanism was that Rus Yusupov and Colin Kroll never resolved who broke ties, so the product-led virality could not survive the first real disagreement. Zomma has three co-founders, the dossier names no CEO, and there is no equity split, no operating agreement, no tiebreaker language anywhere on zommalabs.com. HQ at least had a tactile viral product I could feel pull in my thumb before the governance blew up. Zomma has no equivalent override; the governance question is live, unresolved, and structurally identical to the pattern I have said out loud is "always a recipe for disaster."

Vercel AI Accelerator, YC Summer 2026, Anthropic Finance Agents in May 2026, Rogo at a $160M Series D, Hebbia inside "40%+ of largest asset managers by AUM" -- Zomma is standing in the middle of the most consensus intersection any pre-seed has picked this year. The mechanism that compounds against Zomma over the next 18 months is that the foundation-model vendor whose API Zomma's own site names as a supported agent is the one shipping ten pre-built templates for the exact workflows -- KYC, month-end close -- Zomma is selling into. When the model vendor becomes the workflow vendor, the thin orchestration layer above it gets squeezed from both directions, and Zomma has disclosed no proprietary training data, no customer logos, no SOC 2, and no switching cost that survives an Anthropic template refresh.

Vigil Labs (August 2025) is the closest thing in my sleeve to Zomma on the unsexy-regulated-finance axis, and the parallel is what earns Zomma most of its points. Hedge funds and asset managers are structurally regulated buyers, the "Agent Trust Framework" language on zommalabs.com is at least pointed at the correct SEC-audit-trail conversation, and RPA-in-banking is the exact "fast-ROI with 30+ ops/compliance staff" lane the incumbents like UiPath already prove pays. This is the one dimension where Zomma is not a follower -- the category itself is a place I fund and most rivals on this council will not.

Zomma has to be observable to a human in the wild for my sourcing method to work, and it isn't. Counterfactually, what would flip this dimension a tier: a public sandbox where I could point Zomma's agent at a fake Fidelity Prime Brokerage portal from a laptop in an airport lounge and watch it complete a KYC refresh with the human-approval gate firing on my phone. Absent that, Zomma is invisible behind a "/call" CTA, and my rule since Flock Safety in 2018 is that if I cannot photograph the artifact in someone else's environment, I am investing on founder testimony, and this founding team has not given me enough biography to do that.

Forecast at 36 months: Zomma is either acqui-hired by a fund administrator like SS&C or iCapital for $15-30M as a talent-and-tech tuck-in, or it is dead below $2M ARR after Anthropic's template library plus one incumbent RPA vendor with existing SOC 2 and a Goldman MSA absorb the workflow. The mechanism is straightforward: the "Agent Trust Framework" is marketing copy today, and the buyers Zomma has named -- hedge funds, asset managers -- do not buy from a five-person pre-seed team without a security certificate when the model vendor itself is selling a compliant version down the hall.

The bull case that would sting: someone has to win the regulated orchestration layer between Claude and the custodian portal, Anthropic templates are horizontal by design and always get out-executed vertically, and Varun's JPMorgan rolodex plus Cashel's finance-software SWE reps could be the thin edge that lands three anchor hedge-fund logos before Series A. Rogo's $160M Series D proves the finance-agentic category funds real winners. I hear it, and the dossier still refuses to answer the two questions that would move me: which co-founder is CEO, and which specific back-office ops seat did any of the three actually sit in. One verifiable condition flips my verdict -- a named CEO plus a signed pilot with a top-25-AUM asset manager disclosed publicly within six months. Absent both, I pass, and I write the same-size check into a Vigil Labs-shaped founder who has lived the regulated workflow instead of read about it.
