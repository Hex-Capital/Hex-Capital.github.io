# Gojiberry AI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

The first thing I noticed about Gojiberry AI isn't the traction — it's the TrustMRR listing. On March 9, 2026, one week after hitting #1 on Product Hunt and while actively participating in YC S26, the company was listed for sale at $11.15 million. I have a simple filter for this: founders who frame success as a profitable exit rather than a changed world are telling you something about the ceiling of their ambition. You don't list your company for sale during the most intensive accelerator batch in the world if you believe you're building something that matters. You do it if you've built a nice cash-flowing asset and you're shopping for the best outcome. That's fine for most investors. It's disqualifying for me.

Let me back up to where I always start: what happens to the world if Gojiberry AI fully succeeds? Small B2B sales teams book more meetings through LinkedIn. Reply rates improve from 3.4% to maybe 10-15%. A few thousand sales reps save a few hours a week on prospecting. I don't mean to diminish the founders' execution — getting to $112K MRR with 1,000+ paying customers on $380/month in marketing spend is genuinely impressive operating discipline. But the consequence of success here is a moderately profitable SaaS company in a crowded category. Nobody's life fundamentally changes. No expensive necessity becomes free. The world with a successful Gojiberry looks almost exactly like the world without one, except some sales reps are slightly more productive. That's the kind of outcome I don't get out of bed for.

The technical approach compounds this problem. Gojiberry monitors LinkedIn signals and uses LLMs to generate personalized messages. Every piece of this stack is available to anyone: LinkedIn's data, off-the-shelf LLM APIs, standard enrichment providers. The dossier itself acknowledges "LinkedIn signal detection and automated messaging are technically reproducible." Apollo just acquired Pocus to do the same thing with $251 million in funding behind it. Instantly, Lemlist, HeyReach — multiple bootstrapped competitors have already reached $10-40 million ARR in adjacent categories. There is no technology discontinuity here, no proprietary science, no step-function improvement. This is a well-packaged wrapper on existing capabilities. I prefer technology risk to market risk, and this company has all market risk and zero technology risk.

The strongest bull case: these founders are legitimate operators with real learning velocity. The pivot from a CRM auto-fill tool with zero paying customers to $112K+ MRR in roughly eight months is a credible demonstration of adaptability. All three have prior exits — CoCo AI and Edusign both sold in 2025. Roman's documented twelve-channel distribution strategy on $380/month is scrappy and creative. If I were evaluating these founders for a company attacking a consequential problem — making healthcare radically affordable, replacing an entire cost center, creating something that didn't exist before — I'd be genuinely interested. The DoorDash exception in my framework exists for exactly this profile: execution-heavy founders with immigrant energy and high learning rates. But the DoorDash exception required planetary-scale consequence magnitude — transforming food access for hundreds of millions of people. Making LinkedIn outreach 2-3x better for small sales teams doesn't clear that bar by orders of magnitude. Good founders in an inconsequential category is still a pass.

The affordability angle is the one dimension where Gojiberry has a real signal — bringing enterprise intent data from $25K-$150K/year down to $99/month is a genuine 250x cost reduction. But I care about affordability of basic human needs: healthcare, energy, food, education, legal access. Sales tooling for B2B teams is not a basic need being held hostage by legacy cost structures. It's a competitive market where prices already span a wide range. The founders have built a real business. They've demonstrated they can sell. But I either want to lose my money or make fifty times my money, and this company is designed to produce the kind of 3-5x return that is a waste of my time and attention. Build the thing that eliminates the need for outbound sales entirely — not the thing that makes it 40% more efficient.
