## The Idea

- **Problem:** Public-equity analysts at hedge funds spend hours per week manually tracking earnings, podcasts, and X/Twitter and still miss investing signals (YC company page).
- **Approach:** Autonomous AI agents continuously monitor earnings, news, podcasts, and X/Twitter to surface investing signals for fundamental equity analysts (YC company page).
- **Differentiation:** Versus AlphaSense (search-first market intelligence library) and Hebbia Matrix (user-uploaded document analysis), Cohesion is positioned as an agentic "teammate" tracking non-traditional sources like podcasts and X/Twitter rather than a search or document-extraction tool (YC company page; Hebbia, AlphaSense product pages).
- **Business Model:** No public pricing page found; [Inferred]: per-seat or per-fund enterprise SaaS sold to hedge funds, given the cited customer base of 10+ funds with $10Bn+ AUM (YC company page).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Direct sales into long/short and long-only fundamental equity funds, leveraging CEO Krapcho's 5+ year hedge-fund analyst network at Long Path Partners (LinkedIn; YC company page).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Cited deployment with 10+ equity funds managing $10Bn+ combined AUM provides early workflow embedment and reference accounts (YC company page).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Proprietary indexing of non-traditional sources (podcasts, X/Twitter) plus fund-specific signal tuning could create data and switching-cost moats; unproven because product is at YC Spring 2026 stage with no public retention data.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbents (AlphaSense, Hebbia) are optimized for search/document extraction over curated content libraries (AlphaSense product page; Hebbia product page) and may face revenue cannibalization if they pivot to autonomous-agent monitoring of non-licensed sources like podcasts/X.
- **Commoditization risk:** Rogo ($300M+ raised), Hebbia ($161M raised), AlphaSense ($500M ARR), and Brightwave ($21M raised) are all building agentic finance products and could extend into the same monitoring workflow (PRNewswire Apr 2026; Clay/Hebbia funding profile; AlphaSense press Oct 2025; BusinessWire Oct 2024).

## Key Risks

- **Well-funded incumbents and adjacents:** AlphaSense ($500M ARR, $4B val), Hebbia ($700M val), and Rogo ($300M+ raised) are scaling agentic finance products with capital and distribution Cohesion lacks; mitigation is Cohesion's narrower focus on podcasts/X/Twitter monitoring (AlphaSense press Oct 2025; TechCrunch Jul 2024; PRNewswire Apr 2026).
- **Data source dependency / TOS risk:** Tracking podcasts and X/Twitter relies on third-party platforms whose access terms (e.g., X API pricing/rate limits) can change unilaterally and constrict the product (YC company page describes these as core inputs).
- **Name disambiguation / brand risk:** "Cohesion" is also used by a smart-building company founded by Thru Shivakumar/Raj Gupta and an investment-banking firm "Cohesion IB," potentially diluting search discoverability (search results, cohesionib.com).
- **Trust/compliance bar for hedge-fund deployments:** Buy-side adoption requires SOC2-type controls and signal-quality guarantees; no public certification or backtest data found for Cohesion as of YC Spring 2026 listing (YC company page).
- **Concentration risk in early customer base:** Cited traction of 10+ funds with $10Bn+ combined AUM implies revenue concentration; loss of a small number of accounts could materially impact ARR (YC company page).
