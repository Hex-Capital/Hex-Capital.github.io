## The Idea

- **Problem:** Engineering firms executing capital projects (substations, pipelines, treatment plants, data centers) suffer costly rework when parameters diverge across P&IDs, datasheets, and specifications — e.g., "process datasheet says 450 psig and P&ID says 425" (usetorus.com).
- **Approach:** AI agents hold the full document set in context and run 50+ categories of cross-document consistency checks proactively, then auto-draft procurement approvals, transmittals, and RFI responses via email threads (usetorus.com).
- **Differentiation:** vs. AVEVA/Bentley/Hexagon (single-document, workflow-only tools per Owler/Sumble competitive landscape) Torus operates across the entire document set simultaneously (usetorus.com); vs. generic LLM chatbots, it surfaces discrepancies without being asked (usetorus.com).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: Enterprise SaaS with managed-cloud and on-prem ("sovereign") deployment tiers targeting Fortune 500 EPC/owner customers; no pricing published on the website (usetorus.com pricing not disclosed).
- **TAM/SAM:** Engineering Document Management Software market $3.95B in 2025 → $7B by 2035 at 5.9% CAGR (WiseGuy Reports, 2025); broader EPC market $864.59B in 2025 (Expert Market Research, 2025).
- **GTM / Distribution:** Direct enterprise sales via pilot programs requesting "5-6 documents from current projects" from prospect teams (usetorus.com); YC description states Torus is "working with multiple Fortune 500 companies" (YC page).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** SOC 2 Type 1 & 2 certification in progress plus on-prem sovereign deployment option addresses enterprise data-sensitivity switching barrier (usetorus.com).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Proprietary corpus of firm-specific procurement approvals, transmittal formats, and RFI patterns learned per customer becomes a switching cost as each firm's workflows are trained in; unproven because Torus is pre-launch with no disclosed retention data.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbents (AVEVA, Bentley, Hexagon) have perpetual-license and services-attached revenue models tied to single-application document workflows (Owler, Sumble), making cross-document agentic replacement a channel-conflict and cannibalization risk relative to their existing EDMS suites.
- **Commoditization risk:** [Inferred]: Foundation-model vendors and incumbent EDMS vendors could ship 50+ consistency-check categories once training data on P&IDs/datasheets is aggregated; RTInsights notes AI-powered classification and intelligent search are already the direction of the EDMS category (RTInsights, 2025).

## Key Risks

- **Enterprise sales cycle vs. runway:** Fortune 500 EPC/owner customers referenced on the YC page typically require SOC 2, procurement, and IT security reviews; Torus's own site lists SOC 2 Type 1 & 2 as "in progress" (usetorus.com), meaning paid deployments may lag pilots.
- **Incumbent bundling:** AVEVA (Schneider Electric-owned), Bentley, Hexagon, and AspenTech already sell into the same EPC and owner-operator accounts and are adding AI features to existing EDMS suites (Owler; RTInsights, 2025), creating a substitution threat if incumbents ship cross-document checks natively.
- **Model-accuracy liability:** Missed inconsistencies (e.g., pressure-rating mismatch) in safety-critical designs like substations and pipelines could produce physical-plant liability; no public data found on Torus's error rate, indemnification, or evaluation methodology.
- **Founder identity ambiguity:** Public LinkedIn results for "Rahul Thayil" primarily surface Orion (Techstars '23) rather than Torus (SiliconANGLE, Nov 2025); [Inferred]: this creates diligence risk until CTO background is confirmed directly.
- **Name collision:** At least three unrelated companies operate under "Torus" — Torus (energy storage, $200M raise Sept 2025 per LinkedIn/BusinessWire), Torus Capital VC (torus.vc), and Torus Robotics (Tracxn) — creating brand and SEO conflict.
