# Manicule -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

The first thing I see is the business model, and it stops me cold. Manicule is a services business. Five people serving five clients at $15K MRR. That's $3K per client per month — a documentation agency with AI tooling bolted on. "Fortunes require leverage. Business leverage comes from capital, people, and products with no marginal cost of replication." Documentation services have marginal cost that scales linearly with each new client engagement. The AI layer — testing code snippets, auditing broken links — makes the humans faster but doesn't remove them from the production function. This is labor leverage dressed in AI-native clothing. I've seen this pattern hundreds of times: a services company at YC Demo Day that hopes the "AI" modifier will convince investors to ignore the unit economics of people doing work for other people.

Now the specific knowledge question. Naman ran a technical writing studio for three years in the devtools space. Shreyans was a founding engineer at Supermemory. Together they noticed that good documentation drove enterprise deals at Supermemory — a legitimate discovery through lived experience. But I have to ask my standard question: if these two founders disappeared tomorrow, would the company's core insight survive? The answer is yes. "Developer docs matter and most startups neglect them" is not a proprietary insight. It's a widely known pain point that any competent technical writer has observed. Contrast this with Aravind at Perplexity, whose years inside Google Brain gave him knowledge about retrieval systems that would take a competitor years to accumulate. Naman's three years of tech writing experience is valuable but trainable — you could hire someone with equivalent knowledge within a few months of searching.

The leverage audit is where this falls apart most clearly. Mintlify has raised $21M and hit $10M ARR building the SaaS version of this market — a documentation platform with zero marginal cost per user. Mintlify is code leverage. Manicule is labor leverage competing in the same ecosystem. When I compare the two, Mintlify is Notion (small team, permissionless product leverage, compounds with every user) and Manicule is Poshmark (marketplace operations requiring proportional headcount). The dossier notes "no public indication of a planned SaaS product pivot." Without that pivot, the ceiling is a profitable lifestyle business, not a venture-scale outcome. And even if they pivot, Mintlify already acquired Trieve for RAG infrastructure and has a16z's capital behind them. The window for building the documentation platform is closing.

The strongest bull case: Manicule is using services as an intelligence-gathering wedge. Every client engagement teaches their AI agents what good developer documentation looks like. Eventually the human layer thin enough that the AI does 90% of the work, and a team of five serves five hundred clients instead of five. Services-to-SaaS transitions have worked — Palantir started as glorified consulting. And Manicule is already profitable at pre-seed, which signals capital discipline. The YC network gives them a concentrated, high-intent customer base. The Supermemory case study going viral on Twitter shows they can produce work that generates organic pull. If the founders have the vision to productize their internal tooling into a self-serve platform, the specific knowledge they're accumulating through client work could compound into something defensible. But that's a story about a future company, not the one in front of me. The company I'm evaluating today requires hiring more writers to serve more clients. That's the wrong side of leverage.

The principal authenticity signal is the one bright spot. These founders found the problem through genuine work — Shreyans building Supermemory, Naman writing its docs, both seeing the impact firsthand. They didn't identify "AI documentation" through market research or trend analysis. The prior relationship through Duelance adds trust density between the co-founders. And profitability at $15K MRR with a team of five means they're building something people pay for, not burning capital to manufacture growth metrics. I respect the authenticity. But a principal building a labor-leverage business is still building a labor-leverage business. Passion doesn't change physics.

I pass. The business structure is fundamentally misaligned with how I think about wealth creation. Code and AI are permissionless leverage — they work while you sleep. Human services stop when the humans stop. Manicule might build a great consulting practice. It won't build a company where five people generate a billion dollars in value.
