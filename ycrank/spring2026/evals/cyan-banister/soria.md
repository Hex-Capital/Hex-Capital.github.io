# Soria -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Adam Ron spent years as VP of Equity Research at Bank of America covering healthcare facilities and managed care, which means Soria is the rarest thing in a YC batch: a vertical AI tool being built by the literal buyer. That is the high rung. The low rung is that Soria is also pitching "AI Bloomberg Terminal for Healthcare" in a Spring 2026 batch where Hebbia is at $700M post a16z, Daloopa just took $13M strategic in July 2025, and Rogo and AlphaSense are already in every banker's inbox. Soria's signature in my book is a domain-native founder building the most consensus product of the year. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Soria is playing is the one everyone else is already playing.

Anduril is the high rung on lived experience -- Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before he ever drew a drone, so the defense pivot inherited a working builder, not a memo-reader. Soria has the autobiographical contact: Adam Ron actually maintained the Excel sector models Soria is now selling back to his former peers at BofA, JPM, and the hedge-fund desks he covered. Where Soria diverges from the Luckey rung is the verb -- Ron *used* the workflow, he didn't *ship a product* at scale before Soria. That's the difference between a founder who lived the problem and a founder who lived inside the problem with a soldering iron. Strong, real, but not tattoo-worthy.

The pass-example AI-agent darling -- two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, deck opens on TAM, six funds chasing -- is the low rung on "magically weird," and the failure mechanism is that the round is priced as if the outcome is certain before the work is done. That pattern shows up in Soria's dossier with discipline: the competitor set is Hebbia ($120M+ raised, $13M ARR), Daloopa (two rounds in July 2025), Rogo, AlphaSense, and Bloomberg's own BloombergGPT bundled into the Terminal. No sophisticated investor has visibly passed on Soria for aesthetic or regulatory reasons -- the category itself is the 2026 consensus YC trade. "Vertical AI for finance research" is not laughed at; it is line-itemed in every megafund's thesis memo. Magically weird this is not.

Soria's CEO/CTO line is clean -- Adam Ron as CEO, Cameron Spiller (Virginia Tech CompE, Pinwheel, Findigs) as CTO, with a third unnamed team member -- so the **HQ Trivia** governance ghost doesn't haunt it; this is a structured two-hander with role separation, which is what I want to see on Day 1. The compounding question over 18 months is what happens when the third founder gets named and how the equity is split, because the dossier is silent on operating agreement -- I'd want to see that before any check, but the structural posture is healthy, not the GameCrush/HQ pattern.

For Soria to flip into my capital-intensive-or-unsexy lane, the product would have to extend into regulatory plumbing the incumbents won't touch -- CMS claims-level data, state Medicaid filings, FDA adverse-event aggregation as primary infrastructure, not as one of 125 inputs. Right now Soria is an Excel-like dashboard with an MCP server and a Bloomberg analogy, which is the software-wrapper profile, not the **Crusoe** profile of being *of* a physical place. Counterfactual: if Soria had said "we are licensing exclusive feeds from three state health agencies that no horizontal competitor can replicate," the dimension would jump a tier. It does not say that.

The **Flock Safety** test is whether a normal human can scan, install, or photograph the artifact in 48 hours; the camera on the pole at the Four Seasons was the entire investment thesis. Soria has no consumer surface, no app store, no Chrome extension, no observable artifact -- the "trusted by Bulge Bracket Banks" line on soriaanalytics.com is self-reported copy with zero named logos. I cannot ride it the way I rode Uber in 2010 or play it the way I played Ingress in 2012. This is the dimension where Soria sits in the exact developer-tools-abstraction bucket I default below midpoint on, because if the dossier cannot describe a tactile artifact, I infer nothing tangible has shipped to a customer I can call.

Soria in 36 months: $2-6M ARR in healthcare sell-side seats, acquired by FactSet, S&P Global, or Daloopa for $40-90M, or quietly stalled because Hebbia shipped a healthcare data pack and Bloomberg's BloombergGPT closed the "good enough" gap for the buyer. The mechanism is that vertical depth in a category with three well-funded horizontal aggregators eventually loses on bundling, not on product quality -- the buyer doesn't add a fifth vendor when the fourth is integrated into the terminal already on their desk.

The strongest case against my pass is that Adam Ron has the rarest asset in B2B fintech: a warm Rolodex into the exact buyer at every bulge-bracket healthcare desk in New York, and Daloopa's July 2025 $13M strategic round proves strategics are actively writing checks into AI finance data infra. That is real, and it's the kind of distribution edge I have rewarded before -- the **Affirm** override where Levchin's network beat the analytical-origin penalty. But Levchin had shipped PayPal; Ron has shipped sell-side reports, which is a different verb. The flip condition is concrete: if Soria publishes a named bulge-bracket logo on a multi-seat contract before Demo Day, I re-underwrite, because that single fact would prove the Rolodex converts, and a converting Rolodex in a regulated vertical is a thing I have written checks for. Until then, Soria is a founder I'd watch from across the dinner table -- one foot in love, one foot waiting for the artifact.
