# Netter -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Netter is the cleanest version of the trap I described in my pass example: well-organized governance on top of a consensus AI category, where the highest-scoring dimension on my ladder (a clear CEO in Samuel Ladroue and a clear CTO in Amin Hamzaoui) is doing exactly nothing to offset the lowest, which is that "chat to build pipelines on top of 120+ connectors" is the most-pitched B2B AI thesis of YC's Spring 2026 batch. The dossier hands me a Mines Paris/ENS Ulm pair, three European retailer logos with no case study URLs, and a $12M "seed" figure that StartupHub.ai asserts and nobody corroborates. I am never playing the game I seem to be playing -- and the game Netter seems to be playing is the one every Snowflake and Databricks roadmap is already playing back.

Anduril is the HIGH rung on lived founder experience: Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever touched a defense contract, so the biography preceded the company. Netter's founders are mathematicians from Mines Paris and a student still at ENS Ulm, neither of whom has surfaced a single hour of operating time inside a nursing home, a Tesco UK back office, or a Carrefour FR receivables team -- the verticals their own homepage names. The problem here was discovered in the way I describe in my pass example: analytically, from a market map, not from having sat in front of a TMS at 11pm trying to reconcile an IoT feed. Amin Hamzaoui's LinkedIn headline literally reads "Student at Mines Paris and ENS Ulm." DeepMind is my known exception for pedigreed founders, but Demis Hassabis was a chess prodigy, neuroscientist, and game designer compounding across three disciplines by his thirties; two French grandes-écoles math degrees do not yet clear that bar for me.

Crusoe is the LOW-rung failure mechanism for "magically weird" inverted -- a company so weird that three credible VCs passed on aesthetic grounds, which is the tell I am hunting for. Netter inverts Crusoe perfectly. Netter's homepage tagline -- "build with your data, not your engineers" -- is the exact sentence Databricks, Snowflake, Microsoft Fabric, DataWalk, and Alteryx are all printing in 2026 marketing collateral per the IBTimes ranking. The round is not visibly underpriced; the StartupHub.ai $12M seed figure, if real, is the opposite of underpriced for two founders with no prior exit. There is no founder articulating why the smart money was wrong, because the smart money is not passing -- it is racing.

Samuel Ladroue is named CEO, Amin Hamzaoui is named CTO on Netter's YC profile, which is structurally the inverse of the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll problem I have publicly cited. This is the one dimension where Netter looks adult: two co-founders, clear title delineation, no third-cook governance fog. That said, the dossier surfaces no vesting protocol, no operating agreement detail, no documented tiebreaker -- the title is clean but the mechanism is not verified. Eighteen months from now, if the European GTM and the SF HQ split start pulling in opposite directions, the question is who breaks the tie, and the dossier cannot answer it.

The counterfactual on Netter's frontier-hardware/unsexy-regulated dimension: this score flips a tier only if Netter were attacking nursing homes, ambulance routing, or oilfield logistics as the *primary* product surface -- the way my Vigil Labs check sits inside regulated finance. Netter's homepage lists healthcare, logistics, retail, manufacturing, real estate as targets, which is everyone, which is no one. It is a horizontal SaaS abstraction priced like one, not a regulated swing where the moat compounds because regulators slow incumbents down. Crusoe earned its score by being *of* North Dakota; Netter is not yet of any of the five verticals it lists.

Uber is the single-anchor parallel for physical-world verifiability that Netter cannot reach. I bet on Uber in 2010 because I rode it; I bet on Postmates in 2011 because the delivery happened in front of me; I bet on Niantic because I had been playing Ingress since 2012. Netter is a chat interface bolted onto enterprise SaaS/ERP/IoT plumbing -- the only humans who can touch it are the data ops leads at Carrefour FR, and even they would need their CIO's signoff and a connector deployment. I cannot install Netter on my phone tonight. There is no Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café that introduces me to Samuel Ladroue's product in the wild.

Forecast over 36 months: Netter is acquired for talent and connector library by Snowflake, Databricks, or Microsoft Fabric in the $25M-$80M range, or has flatlined below $4M ARR as native "chat with your data" features from those same incumbents absorb the wedge before switching costs accrete on Netter's ontology. The mechanism is straightforward -- 120 native connectors is real engineering, but every warehouse vendor is shipping the same chat layer in 2026, and a 4-person team in SF selling to French hypermarkets does not out-distribute Microsoft's Fabric sales motion at the mid-market.

The strongest bull case I can build: Mines Paris/ENS Ulm is the French polymath stamp, and the DeepMind exception in my own framework says cross-domain compounding can override the bias against pedigreed founders; Carrefour FR, Tesco UK, and Auchan ES are three of the largest grocery retailers in Europe, and if even one of those logos is a paid production deployment, the ontology accumulates per-account switching cost the way Palantir Foundry's does, but at a fifth the price; the receivables-recovery customer quoting 10% recovery is the kind of physically verifiable outcome I weight heavily. Here is what dismantles it: the homepage logos are unaccompanied by a case study URL, contract value, or press release; the $12M funding figure is uncorroborated; and the founders have no prior data-platform operating history surfaced anywhere. The verifiable condition that would flip me from pass to invest: a signed, public, named-counterparty contract at one of those three retailers with disclosed ACV above $500K, OR Samuel Ladroue publicly demonstrating one full year of prior operating time inside a retail or logistics back office. Without that, Netter is the pass example I wrote about, in French.
