# Blueprints -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Blueprints is the inverse of the pattern I write checks on: a technically-credentialed pair building infrastructure for a problem they observed across the fence rather than one they lived inside. Ryan Morrissey did an internship at Stripe and Bence Redmond did one at AWS, and they shipped a working harness to 250 beta users processing $500K of Polymarket volume — that is competence. What is missing in the Blueprints dossier is the moment the founders themselves got beaten by a Polymarket bot and decided to fix it for themselves. That asymmetry — strong schlep posture, weak organic-pain origin — is what this evaluation turns on.

Coinbase (2012) is the right ladder rung for the Blueprints schlep test, because Brian Armstrong's appetite for compliance grind was the moat, not the wallet UI. Blueprints does have schlep surface: co-located execution infrastructure for low-latency Polymarket order placement, a proprietary backtest dataset since 2022, and inherited CFTC exposure on prediction markets where Blueprints publishes no compliance disclosure. That is real work other YC S26 applicants in the AI-wrapper bucket are routing around. The Blueprints divergence from the Coinbase rung is that the schlep here is mostly latency infrastructure rather than regulatory grind — co-location is a hard engineering task but it is not a multi-year licensing slog, and the Polymarket platform itself absorbs most of the regulatory blast radius, not Blueprints.

The MBA-deck pass example I keep on file fails because no founder ever personally suffered the problem — and the Blueprints dossier is conspicuously silent on whether Morrissey or Redmond ever lost money to a Polymarket arbitrage bot, ever placed a manual prediction-market trade, or ever ran a strategy they wanted automated. The product reads as "spotted a market opportunity" — 14 of the top 20 wallets are bots, $40M extracted by arbitrage between Apr 2024 and Apr 2025, therefore retail needs tooling. That is the inversion of Drew Houston forgetting his USB stick. Blueprints' founders pattern-matched on a market structure asymmetry visible from the outside; they did not build it because they themselves were the losing retail trader. This is where Blueprints loses the most points relative to its surface polish.

On animal resourcefulness, the 18-month compounding question is whether $500K in processed volume and 250 beta users came from Morrissey doing unscalable manual onboarding the way the Airbnb founders door-knocked photography in New York, or from a Telegram thread that grew passively. The dossier shows demo calls booked via cal.com/ryan-morr and Irish press in Silicon Republic and ThinkBusiness, which is light hustle; what the Blueprints dossier does not show is a cereal-box-equivalent stunt, a viral demo on Hacker News, or a hand-built integration the founders did at 3am. Leaving the University of Limerick ISE programme mid-stream to pursue Blueprints full-time is genuine commitment and it is the strongest animal signal in the file. It is not enough to mark Blueprints up to the Dropbox rung.

On hacker genealogy, what would have to be true for Blueprints to flip a tier is the existence of a GitHub trail I cannot currently find — a published open-source library, a Polymarket agents PR, a competitive-programming placement, anything Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize at 17 would be analogous to. The Blueprints dossier confirms a Stripe internship and an AWS internship and the Immersive Software Engineering programme at Limerick, which is credential-by-employer, not artifact-by-shipping. Hackers leave GitHub trails. Blueprints' founders may have private ones, but the public absence is itself the signal and it places Blueprints below the Dropbox MIT-hacker rung.

Stripe (2010) is the single-anchor parallel for the Blueprints cofounder configuration, because Patrick and John Collison were brothers who had built Auctomatic together before Stripe and the long prior working relationship was load-bearing. Morrissey and Redmond were both enrolled in the same University of Limerick ISE cohort, built the first version of Blueprints together, and Morrissey recruited Redmond on returning from Stripe. That is a real working relationship measured in years and a shipped artifact, not a hackathon pairing. Blueprints clears the cofounder bar cleanly even if it does not reach the brothers-since-childhood density of Stripe.

My 36-month forecast for Blueprints: alive but stuck at $200K–$500K ARR, having pivoted from retail Polymarket strategies toward B2B execution infrastructure for prop firms after retention collapsed on the consumer side, acqui-hired by Polymarket or a Kalshi-adjacent listing venue for under $15M. The mechanism is zero-sum: Blueprints' retail users are competing on latency against bots that already extracted $40M, and Polystrat (Valory, $13.8M financing) launched Feb 2026 with 4,200 trades in its first month — Blueprints' free-tier users will lose money, churn, and the Pro $20/mo conversion will not survive cohort PnL transparency.

The strongest objection to that forecast is the Stripe Known Exception. The bull case says Blueprints is structurally a Stripe analog at Stripe-1.0 timing: two technically-credentialed founders, a market growing from $10B to $24B in monthly volume in the span of a quarter, a developer-feeling product with co-located infrastructure, and a Stripe internship in the founder's actual biography. I funded the Collisons even when they declined to redo the YC bootcamp. The bear case I deploy against my own bull case: Stripe's customers won when they used Stripe — their conversion rates went up. Blueprints' customers are mostly going to lose money to faster bots, because the dossier's own framing concedes that "Blueprints customers competing against latency-advantaged bots may underperform, damaging retention." Stripe was positive-sum for its users; Blueprints sits on top of a zero-sum game where the bots already won. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Blueprints publishes 90-day cohort PnL showing the median Pro user is net positive after fees versus holding cash — if that exists, the entire bear case collapses and I write the check.
