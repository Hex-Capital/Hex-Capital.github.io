# Pavoot -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Pavoot is two ETH AI Center classmates -- Gohar Tamrazyan, six-time Swiss Chess Champion, and Ana Yoon Faria de Lima, twenty-plus Olympiad medals, ex-AI Engineer at BTG Pactual -- who met in a course where they had three days to invent a startup and pitched on day four, and the startup they're now pitching is an AI agent that follows up on B2B marketing events. That sentence is the whole evaluation. The founders are obviously bright; the problem was assigned to them by a syllabus.

Anduril-Luckey (2017) is the load-bearing analogue for the lived-experience axis because Palmer Luckey had already shipped Oculus to scale from a garage before he ever touched defense -- the biography pre-dated the company by years, and the company existed because the biography demanded it. Pavoot inverts that arrow: Tamrazyan and Faria de Lima are credentialed technical operators (ETH CS, IME-USP CS, AI engineering inside two Brazilian banks) whose public record shows zero prior contact with event marketing, RevOps, or the conference-floor reality of a B2B field marketer trying to justify a $200K booth spend. The Pavoot homepage's example -- 47 attendees, 24 meetings, 11 leads, $48K pipeline -- is unattributed, which is what an internal demo looks like, not what a lived problem looks like. I read the absence of a founder story about a missed lead at a specific Dreamforce booth and apply something close to my absent-evidence default.

Cvent acquired Goldcast in December 2025 for roughly $300M -- that is the LOW rung on the magically-weird ladder for Pavoot, and it is not a passing observation but the entire competitive geometry. The incumbents have publicly priced "AI turns events into pipeline" at nine figures, which means by the time Pavoot's YC P26 Launch post is twenty-one days old, every Bizzabo and Cvent account executive is already pitching the same wedge into the same buyer with a five-year integration head start. This is the pass-example pattern from my own file -- "consensus YC AI-agent darling, deck opens with a TAM slide" -- transposed into events. I am supposed to find these before they are consensus, and Pavoot arrives after the consensus has already minted a $300M outcome for the incumbent that bought the wedge.

On single-CEO authority, the mechanism that compounds over eighteen months is simple: a 50/50 co-founder pair without a documented tiebreaker spends the first product-market-fit pivot relitigating who decides, and the Pavoot dossier names neither a CEO nor an equity split, only "co-founder" twice. That is the GameCrush-shaped silence -- not yet broken, possibly fine, but I have watched it break enough times to score the default rather than the hope. On capital-intensity, the counterfactual is what would have to be true for Pavoot to clear my "too hard or too unsexy" bar: a proprietary on-site hardware capture device, an exclusive data deal with a top-five event organizer, or a regulated-industry vertical wedge (pharma compliance follow-up, defense-industry classified-event capture) -- none present; the Pavoot product as described rides commodity ASR and commodity LLM APIs. On physical verifiability, the single-anchor parallel is Flock Safety, which I sourced because I could read the founder's Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons -- a tactile artifact in my environment; Pavoot is demo-gated behind cal.com/team/pavoot/30min with no installable product, no public app, no observable artifact a normal human encounters in the wild within forty-eight hours.

Pavoot 36 months out: no acquirer of consequence, sub-$500K ARR if any, founders either pivot toward a narrower AI-SDR adjacency (Clay / 11x territory) by late 2027 or wind down before Series A, with the most probable outcome being a sub-$10M acquihire into Cvent, Bizzabo, or HubSpot for the two engineers, not the product. The mechanism is the Goldcast precedent eating the wedge before Pavoot can plant a flag and the founder pair lacking the events-domain rolodex to out-distribute incumbents on their own turf.

The strongest case against my pass is the DeepMind exception in my own file: Hassabis was a chess prodigy compounding across neuroscience and game design, and I overrode my anti-pedigree bias for him because the polymath cross-domain compounding was the moat -- Tamrazyan is a six-time Swiss Chess Champion, Faria de Lima holds twenty-plus Olympiad medals, and the pattern superficially rhymes. It stings until I press on it: Hassabis was compounding INTO the problem domain (games-to-AGI), whereas Pavoot's polymath signals point sideways -- chess and Olympiad math do not compound into knowing why a Gainsight CSM ignores the post-event email. The verifiable condition that would flip me is a named enterprise design partner -- a Snowflake, a Datadog, a Rippling -- publicly running three or more field events through Pavoot with disclosed attribution lift before the end of P26 demo day; absent that, the consensus-AI-agent gravity and the Cvent-Goldcast $300M overhang win, and I pass with warmth but without a check.
