# Alloovium -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Zander Schweitzer drove dump trucks at the Port of Brisbane before he was old enough to drink, then spent his early career as a Site Engineer at Brady Marine & Civil, and now Alloovium is building the document-intelligence layer for the exact paperwork he watched foremen lose their evenings to. That is the highest-dimension signal in the Alloovium dossier and it is the reason I did not close the tab at the words "construction AI." The lowest-dimension signal is that Trunk Tools already raised $70M doing a broader version of this pitch to US general contractors, and Doxel is at $56.5M -- Alloovium is a founder-of-the-place bet inside a category the market has already declared investable, which is a very different animal than Crusoe was in 2018.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before he built Anduril, and the whole reason I tattooed conviction onto that 2017 check was that the biography preceded the company by a decade. Alloovium's Zander Schweitzer is not Palmer Luckey, but the mechanism rhymes: a civil engineer who grew up on Port of Brisbane sites, quit a six-figure construction job (per Cielo Nicolosi's LinkedIn post), and is now automating the RFI-and-monthly-report grind he personally lived. Where Alloovium diverges from the Anduril rung is that Luckey brought a shipped consumer hardware franchise into a defense category no VC would touch on aesthetic grounds -- Alloovium's Schweitzer brings site-engineer credibility into a category three funds have already stamped as hot. The lived-experience signal is genuine, and it is the reason I would take the meeting; it is not, by itself, the reason I would write.

Consensus is the failure mechanism at the bottom of my Magically Weird ladder, and it is exactly the mechanism working against Alloovium. The pass example I keep in the drawer is the ex-FAANG-PM AI-agent darling: consensus category, well-funded competitors, TAM slide, everyone bidding. Alloovium is not that -- Schweitzer is not ex-FAANG and the founder-market fit is real -- but the round pricing signal I trust runs through the *category*, not the founder, and the category has Trunk Tools ($40M Series B from Insight in July 2025), Doxel ($56.5M from Insight and a16z), and Buildots ($45M Series D) all clearing throats in it. Alloovium's founder-stated target of $1M ARR and 75 partner companies by year-end 2026 is a Series A narrative sitting on a currently-undisclosed revenue base, in a category where a US-headquartered competitor is already six innings ahead of the Australia-to-SF relocation. That is the opposite of "find them before it becomes consensus."

Alloovium's compliance-by-default architecture -- human sign-off gated on every AI-drafted variation, RFI, and IMS check -- is the axis where this compounds interestingly over 18 months if the founder holds it. IMS/ITP terminology is Australian regulatory vernacular, AIA contract standards are American; the re-engineering cost of that switch is what I would be paying Schweitzer to solve, and if he does, the per-customer template library becomes a real switching-cost moat that Procore Copilot cannot casually clone. That is the Anduril-Luckey lane at 15% strength: unsexy, regulated, adjacent to physical infrastructure, moat-compounds-with-time. It does not qualify Alloovium as frontier hardware, but it is not the undifferentiated SaaS wrapper I punish by default.

CEO clarity in the Alloovium dossier reads clean on the surface: Schweitzer CEO, Nicolosi CTO, no shared title, no 50/50 equity language in public materials. The counterfactual for the tier flip is documented: a public founder-vesting protocol and a named tiebreaker on product-versus-compliance disputes would move Alloovium from the middle of my ladder toward the top -- because Nicolosi's self-reported first exit at age 15 and CS-dropout profile is exactly the kind of technical co-founder who eventually wants product authority, and I have watched that fight kill companies (GameCrush ~2011, HQ Trivia 2017) when the tiebreaker was never written down. Silence on governance is not a red flag yet; it is a conversation I would have before signing.

Alloovium fails the Flock Safety test that anchors my Physical-World Verifiability axis. I sourced Flock from a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café -- I could not source Alloovium from any environment a normal human occupies, because the entire product lives behind "Book a demo" with no pricing page and no observable artifact in the wild. The >$400M in active construction project deployments is real, but it is founder testimony to me, not something I can photograph on a job site the way I could ride an Uber in 2010 or play Ingress in 2012. That is the structural discount pre-seed enterprise SaaS pays against my method, and Alloovium wears it.

My 36-month forecast: Alloovium lands between $3M and $8M ARR concentrated in Australian and APAC head contractors, gets outrun by Trunk Tools in the US mid-market despite the SF relocation, and either sells to Procore or Aconex as a document-intelligence bolt-on for under $150M or grinds as a regional category leader. The mechanism is the Magically-Weird floor: Schweitzer's lived-experience edge is real enough to win a defensible geography, and not weird enough to catch the category winner from six innings back with a $500K YC check against Trunk Tools' $70M.

The steel-man against that forecast is the HandUp exception -- I broke my capital-defensibility rule for Rose Broome because her biography mirrored mine, and Schweitzer's civil-engineering-family Brisbane origin is the equivalent authentic ownership of a domain the credentialed founders will always mis-read. The bull case says Alloovium's clause-level IMS citation architecture is a genuine wedge no US competitor is bothering with because AIA contracts do not require it, and Trunk Tools is optimizing for a different regulatory grammar; Schweitzer's persistence quitting a six-figure job to co-found is exactly the pattern I lean into after cold outreach. What kills that bull case in the dossier: Alloovium's own founders publicly frame their year-end 2026 goal in ARR-and-partner-count language, which is fundraising-cadence-as-operating-cadence, the specific tell I flagged in Full Ratchet #180. The verifiable condition that would flip me to invest: Alloovium closes two paying US general contractors on AIA contracts before Demo Day at a disclosed ACV that implies $500K+ current ARR -- proving Schweitzer can translate his Brisbane site fluency across regulatory grammars faster than Trunk Tools can translate its capital into GTM. Absent that, I am one foot in love with the founder and both feet out of the round.
