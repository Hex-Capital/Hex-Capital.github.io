# Blueprints -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Blueprints is two University of Limerick undergrads, Ryan Morrissey and Bence Redmond, building a hosted no-code agent harness that routes orders into Polymarket -- which means the load-bearing question I run first is whether the leverage is permissionless, and the answer is that the entire product lives at the pleasure of a single venue whose first-party `github.com/Polymarket/agents` repo is already shipping the same primitive for free. The signature here is "bright young builders chasing a hot category without the specific knowledge to defend it" -- highest on market-size optionality ($24B combined Polymarket+Kalshi monthly volume by April 2026), lowest on the founder-specific-knowledge gate, with everything else dragged downward by the Polymarket dependency.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung on leverage architecture I keep returning to -- media as protocol, zero marginal cost, no gatekeeper between Jack Dorsey's product and the user. Blueprints diverges from that rung at exactly the structural point that matters: every Blueprints order is permissioned by Polymarket's API and competes with Polymarket's own open-source agent SDK plus the `builders.polymarket.com` program. This is not code-leverage in the nav.al/product-media sense; this is App Store leverage with one app store. Anchorage Digital is the only Known Exception I have for permission-as-moat, and that override required a Treasury OCC charter and a category-timing call from my **MetaStable** (2014) seat -- Blueprints has no regulatory moat, no charter, and is one Polymarket product-team sprint away from substitution.

The pattern I learned to flag from passing on the wrong things is the "memo founder" responding to a competitor's success rather than to a personally-discovered problem -- the inverse of how Jeff Atwood and Joel Spolsky arrived at **Stack Overflow** (2008) as practicing developers writing for developers, or how Aravind Srinivas arrived at **Perplexity** (2022) with a Berkeley retrieval PhD. Morrissey did a Stripe internship and Redmond did an AWS SDE internship; the dossier records "no documented prior trading, hedge-fund, or market-making credential" for either. The Blueprints origin story reads as smart engineers noticing that 14-of-20 top Polymarket wallets are automated and building a tool for the other side of that trade, not as traders who lived the disadvantage and shipped their own escape hatch. That is the specific-knowledge gate failing in its cleanest form.

On the network-compounding axis, Blueprints today is a single-sided SaaS at $20/month Pro -- each user's strategy sits in isolation, the Nth blueprint does not make the (N-1)th more valuable. The compounding mechanism would have to be a strategy marketplace or copy-trading layer, which the dossier flags as inferred and undocumented. Eighteen months of mechanism analysis: if Blueprints ships shareable blueprints with a public PnL leaderboard before Polystrat (Olas, $13.8M financing + $4M seed, already 4,200+ trades in its first month) ships the same, the **Uber** (2010)-style asymmetric-user-value math turns on; if not, Blueprints stays a productivity tool with linear retention economics and a churn problem against funded incumbents.

On market size, the counterfactual for a tier-flip is narrow: $24B monthly notional across Polymarket+Kalshi is genuinely platform-shaped, and adjacent pivot vectors exist into Hyperliquid, sports books, and on-chain perps. For Blueprints to score in the top quartile here I would need to see Morrissey articulate two concrete adjacent venues with shipped connectors -- not just Polymarket with Kalshi-someday. The Carbon Copy Markets Inc. legal wrapper is venue-neutral, which is the right signal; the product surface is venue-locked, which is the wrong one.

The founder-trio dimension reads clean but illegible -- a single-anchor parallel to **Notion** (2013) at the Ivan Zhao seed, where the dossier was thin and the integrity bar was satisfied by absence of red flags rather than presence of track record. Blueprints presents no contested exits, no self-promotional honesty signaling, no partner litigation. The 10-year-relationship test is unanswerable on the public record; the default neutral applies. The Patch / NDRC / YC S26 stack tells me they pass the bright-and-energetic filter, not the would-I-spend-a-decade-with-this-human filter.

Thirty-six month forecast: Blueprints either pivots away from Polymarket-as-sole-venue into a multi-venue agent router (the Kalshi+Hyperliquid+perps expansion) and survives at sub-$2M ARR as a niche tool, or it gets out-shipped by Polystrat / Simmer and Polymarket's own first-party agent tooling and quietly winds down or acqui-hires into Polymarket or a YC peer by mid-2028 -- the mechanism is platform-dependency plus crowded funded competition plus an unproven "Prince" LLM-to-strategy translation layer with no published benchmark. The asymmetric-payoff math at one X downside still works, but the upper-bound geometry caps at roughly 30-50x rather than the 1,000-10,000x range that **Twitter** (2007) and **Uber** (2010) cleared.

The bull case I have to steel-man is the **MetaStable** (2014) analogy: prediction markets crossed $10B Polymarket monthly volume in March 2026 and 840K unique wallets by February, two young hungry founders with YC distribution can compound faster than Olas's heavier on-chain agent stack, and "Prince" could become the natural-language strategy interface for retail across all on-chain markets -- exactly the pre-consensus category dollar-cost-average I take when the rest of the council is laughing. That case stings because category-timing is a real Naval pattern. What dismantles it is the **MetaStable** override required co-founding conviction sourced from years of crypto immersion, and Blueprints' founders have months of category exposure, not years -- the override is precedent for me, not for them. Flip condition: if Morrissey ships a public strategy marketplace with organic copy-trading across Polymarket+Kalshi+one perps venue within 6 months of Demo Day and the Prince benchmark posts above 60% live-PnL accuracy, I write the check; absent that, Blueprints is a pass.
