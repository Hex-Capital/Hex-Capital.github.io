# Soria -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Soria is a vertical-wedge SaaS company whose highest signal is a named-incumbent secret about Bloomberg's economics, and whose lowest signal is the complete absence of any atoms-heavy or regulated moat around Soria itself — a profile that produces real product-market fit and zero power-law shape. Adam Ron, ex-VP Equity Research at Bank of America covering healthcare facilities and managed care, has built the tool he wished he had on the sell side; the question is not whether Soria works, it is whether working is enough.

**Stripe (2010)** is the load-bearing analogy on Soria's strongest dimension — both companies name a specific incumbent failure mode rather than a generic complaint. Patrick Collison's secret was that PayPal's monetary infrastructure could not be re-architected developer-first because PayPal's revenue model depended on the opposite. Soria's analogous secret, implicit in the 125-source healthcare aggregation pipeline, is that Bloomberg Terminal at ~$24K/seat across 3,430+ customer companies cannot economically build sector-deep verticals because Bloomberg's unit economics require cross-sector seat amortization. That is a real Thiel-shaped observation — incumbent, mechanism, blind spot. But Soria diverges from the Stripe rung at the secret's *durability*: developer-first payments compounded into an architectural moat over a decade, whereas "we aggregated 125 healthcare data sources" is a moat measured in engineer-months, not in architectural inversion. Hebbia at $13M ARR and $700M valuation, a16z-backed, can fund a healthcare data pack in a quarter.

**Pure-software SaaS in a commoditizing AI-finance category** is exactly the failure mode the Founders Fund manifesto attacked in 2011 — bits, not atoms, in a sector where four well-funded horizontals (Hebbia $120M+ raised, Daloopa $13M strategic in July 2025, Rogo, AlphaSense) are already crowding the lane. Soria's only regulatory adjacency is that its *customers* are regulated banks and hedge funds; Soria itself ships a Python pipeline on DBOS with an Excel add-in. There is no FedRAMP-adjacent certification depth, no hardware capex, no integration moat the way Palantir built one inside classified networks. The Anduril override does not apply here. Soria is the canonical YC software-only wedge against which "we wanted flying cars" was written.

On **monopoly trajectory**, the mechanism that would have to compound over 18 months is sector-model network effect — each new healthcare data source making Soria more indispensable than the last. This breaks under inspection. The 125+ sources are largely public agency data (federal, state, CMS, nonprofit); they are not exclusive licenses, they are scraping and normalization labor. A competitor with Hebbia's capital can replicate the data layer; the moat then collapses to UX, which is not a Thiel moat. Pricing power five years out terminates at "we are slightly better at healthcare than the generalist AI research tools" — that is a feature, not a company.

On **definite plan**, the counterfactual: for Soria to flip a tier here, the dossier would need a multi-year named architectural commitment — for example, Adam Ron stating that Soria will spend three years building the regulated data clearinghouse that becomes the FactSet of healthcare-specific alternative data, with named milestones at year one, year three, year five. Instead the public framing is "AI Bloomberg Terminal built for Healthcare (and beyond)," and the parenthetical "and beyond" is the tell — it signals optionality across sectors rather than a definite commitment within one. That is iteration-mode optionality dressed as ambition.

On **founder lineage**, the single-anchor parallel is **Asana (2010)** — except inverted. Dustin Moskovitz overrode missing structural moat by virtue of having shipped Facebook to scale; Soria has the inverse profile, where Adam Ron has genuine healthcare-equity-research domain fit but no shipped-to-scale operator signal, and Cameron Spiller's engineering tenure at Pinwheel, Findigs, and Trigo Data is competent IC work without the prior-exit pedigree that makes the lineage criterion load-bearing. There is no PayPal Mafia connection, no Thiel-portfolio adjacency, no Olympiad or Fellowship signal, no third founder publicly identified despite the YC team-size-of-3. Founder-market fit is real; founder lineage in the Thiel sense is not.

**Forecast, 36 months:** Soria reaches $3–8M ARR with a concentrated book of healthcare-focused hedge funds and sell-side desks, then is acquired by AlphaSense, FactSet, or S&P Global for sub-$75M as the horizontal incumbents bundle equivalent vertical packs — a competent founder-market-fit outcome that returns the angel check at 2–4x and does nothing for power-law math. The mechanism producing this is the high/low signature: a real vertical-economics secret that wins early customers, but no atoms-heavy or regulatory anchor that prevents Hebbia or Bloomberg itself from compressing the wedge once it visibly works.

**The strongest bull case** is that Daloopa's $13M July 2025 round validates a category where structured financial data infrastructure becomes the *picks-and-shovels* layer beneath every AI finance tool, and Soria's vertical-first wedge becomes the wedge into a multi-sector data utility — each new sector compounding into a Bloomberg-replacement at one-tenth the price. Adam Ron's BofA network is genuine distribution; the Fintech Sandbox membership opens regulated counterparty doors; the "trusted by Bulge Bracket Banks" claim, if true at even two named logos, is real enterprise traction for a pre-seed. I dismantle this with the dossier's own gap: no named customer logo appears anywhere in the dossier, no ARR figure, no seat count, and the moat hypothesis still terminates at engineer-months of aggregation that a $700M-valuation Hebbia can match in a quarter. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a signed multi-year enterprise contract with a named top-five investment bank disclosed publicly within twelve months at six-figure-plus ACV — at that point Soria has crossed from vertical SaaS into integration-moat territory, and the secret becomes load-bearing rather than rhetorical. Until then: pass.
