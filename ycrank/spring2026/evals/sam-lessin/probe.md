# Probe -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Mahit Namburu's resume — UC Berkeley EECS, Databricks LoRA inference, Apple machine translation, turned down a Together AI MLE seat — is the most interesting thing in the Probe dossier, and it is nowhere near interesting enough to overcome the fact that Probe is "agent-native incident management" pitched into a category where incident.io just raised $62M at a $400M valuation in April 2025 to build the exact same product. The signature here is a credentialed ML-research founder running directly at my Pass Example: AI agent for an enterprise vertical, three months out of YC, going head-on against three funded incumbents who already ship the feature.

Mahit's "declined the Together AI offer" line is the only asymmetric-conviction artifact Probe gives me, and I want to credit it the way I credited my own Drop.io arc — bootstrapping a primitive against free incumbents because you cannot be talked out of it. The mechanism that made Drop.io load-bearing was years of obsession with a specific file-sharing primitive before anyone funded it; Probe's dossier shows zero public writing from Mahit on SRE, on incident response, on why agent-native beats co-pilot, on anything. No essays, no threads, no GitHub footprint surfaced in search. Turning down one job offer is a career decision, not a missionary thesis. Probe sits one rung above the canonical opportunity-responder profile because of the Databricks pedigree, not two.

The lowest rung on the category-sleeve ladder is my own Pass Example verbatim — "AI is just more internet," the consensus AI-agent shell where four tier-1 VCs are already bidding and the alpha is gone before the meeting starts. Probe is that pattern with the serial numbers barely filed off: incident.io ($96M raised, $9M ARR, AI SRE shipped), Rootly ($15.3M, "AI-native" positioning), FireHydrant ($32.5M, runbook automation), PagerDuty (public, 700+ integrations and AIOps). Probe is not pre-consensus crypto with on-chain economic activity, not a DTC brand with margin, not a creator with owned distribution — it is the densest part of the 2026 YC consensus curve, and the dossier even names Sentrial (W26) as adjacent.

Round dynamics: the dossier is silent on price, on lead, on whether any tier-1 visibly passed — and silence on round dynamics, in my book, defaults to consensus pricing because if Probe had been priced like a Sublime Security pre-consensus enterprise check, somebody would have said so. Apply absent-evidence default and lean to the lower half: incident.io's April 2025 $62M round set the comp ceiling that every Probe term sheet will be priced against, and YC SAFE caps in Spring 2026 on "agent-native [X]" deals are running well above what the regatta math supports.

Capital structure compounds the problem over the next 18 months: Probe needs to ship enterprise-credible autonomous remediation, displace incident.io and Rootly inside paying engineering orgs, and survive the per-seat ARR cannibalization argument all while burning against incumbents with $96M and $15M of dry powder respectively. That is not a Teamshares-style instrument I can underwrite as a non-standard structure; it is a standard SaaS cap table that needs five more tacks before it sees the Series A starting line, and every tack is priced against funded competitors shipping the same feature.

Founder-as-brand: @MahitNamburu exists, @ProbeYC exists, follower counts not retrievable, no newsletter, no community, no Discord, no pre-existing SRE audience — Probe has the John Fish / Bookshelved structural problem inverted, where the deal only works if the founder brings owned distribution, and there isn't any. Default 5 of 15, leaning lower because for B2B incident tooling the relevant distribution would be a name SREs recognize, and Mahit's prior surfaces are ML research, not on-call rotations.

Forecast, 36 months: Probe is either acquihired by PagerDuty or one of the funded incumbents for sub-$20M as a small talent-and-tech tuck-in, or it is dead, with ARR never crossing $1.5M before incident.io's AI SRE and Rootly's AI-native feature set reach functional parity inside accounts Probe is trying to win. The mechanism is not founder quality — Mahit will land somewhere good either way — it is that the category clock started in April 2025 with incident.io's $62M and Probe is showing up in Spring 2026 with three people and no traction.

The bull case that actually stings: incident.io raising $62M specifically for AI agents is itself the "venture moment" signal from the Slow letter, Mahit's LoRA-inference work at Databricks is genuinely the right technical substrate for retrieval-grounded production agents, and an agent-native architecture really could cannibalize per-seat ARR in a way incumbents structurally cannot follow without breaking their existing pricing — the same dynamic that let Slack eat HipChat. I take the point, and it does not save Probe, because incident.io shipped AI SRE before Probe incorporated and Rootly is already positioning as AI-native; the architecture argument requires that incumbents cannot pivot, and they already have. None of the Known Exceptions apply — Probe is not a Robinhood-style earliest-stage entry absorbing later consensus, it IS the later consensus; not a Pinterest operator-friend sourcing override (no Kortina-tier personal connection disclosed); not a Ro-style regulatory-moat capital intensity. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Probe books five named Series A or larger logos at $50K+ ACV within 18 months while incident.io's AI SRE renewal rate inside those same logos visibly cracks — then the cannibalization thesis is real and I will pay up at the A.
