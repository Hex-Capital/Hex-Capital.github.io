# Perceptron ML -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Perceptron ML is the YC pre-seed shape I pass on in under a minute: an LLM-orchestration loop pointed at plaintiff law-firm marketing, two brothers in San Francisco, and a success-state that reads "client outreach in hours instead of days." The dimensional signature is a moderately credentialed Stanford-NVIDIA-plus-Brown-undergrad founding pair (Michael and Peyton Marcotte) attempting an optimization play against $636M of adjacent incumbent capital -- FirmPilot, Darrow, EvenUp, Eve. The high-dimension reading on this dossier is founder learning rate, not consequence; the low-dimension reading is required-capital profile. That pairing tells me everything: this is a competent two-person team that may build a real product, in a category where the success-state is "law firms get marketing leads 30% faster," and where I am structurally over-resourced.

Consequence first, as always. **OpenAI** (2019) at the HIGH rung of my consequence ladder is the $50M check I wrote because the world-state at maximum ambition was civilizationally distinct from today -- AGI redirects every cognitive labor market and the $15T of U.S. GDP that is labor (Fortune 2026-03-06). Perceptron ML's maximum ambition, per the YC description, is helping plaintiff/contingency firms "outpace rivals in client outreach" on mass torts and class actions. The incumbent regime that collapses if Perceptron ML wins is... traditional legal marketing agencies, the same category Conroy Creative Counsel (2025) is already documenting being eaten by FirmPilot. That is not a civilizational variable. The dossier names four target verticals -- law, construction RFP, investors, trading desks -- but the YC description leads with legal and only legal has product evidence. No incumbent system disappears; a workflow gets 30% faster. This is the GPT-4-summarize-sales-calls pass example wearing a legal coat.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) is the failure mechanism on my technical-risk axis: capital adequacy misjudged on the way to plant closure, but the underlying premise required real catalyst-yield miracles that consensus VCs would not touch. The inverse pattern -- zero technical miracles required -- is what Perceptron ML's dossier shows. Michael Marcotte built "production AI agents for hardware debugging and formal verification" at NVIDIA, which is real engineering, but the Perceptron ML loop as described (ingest filings/news/listings, trigger response, launch campaign under human approval) is commodity agentic orchestration on top of someone else's foundation model. Two people, no disclosed proprietary signal sources, no hardware, no biology, no physics. Any Tier-1 generalist or any Sequoia seed partner could fund Perceptron ML; the science risk that historically repelled consensus and created my edge is absent.

Founder defiance, evaluated as mechanism analysis over 18 months: my TED Talk skepticism of expert continuity demands evidence that the founders publicly contradicted their field. Peyton Marcotte's PMARC -- astronaut exercise equipment with NASA, Brown, and 1517 Fund support -- is the closest thing to an "instigator" signal in the Perceptron ML dossier, but it's an undergraduate hardware project, not a publicly defended thesis against an industry orthodoxy. Michael Marcotte's Stanford-NVIDIA path is precisely the expert-continuity pattern. Neither brother has legal-domain experience and neither has staked a controversial public position on what plaintiff law firms get wrong. Over 18 months this dimension fails to compound because there is no contrarian wedge to defend when EvenUp ($385M, $2B valuation, lawnext.com Oct 2025) decides to add event-triggered outbound to its post-intake stack.

Founder learning rate, the counterfactual: for this dimension to flip a tier, the dossier would need to describe a documented Perceptron ML pivot with stated reasoning -- the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot pattern that justified my $100M Series C check. The Marcotte brothers founded Perceptron ML and are executing one linear plan; the only adjacent pivot evidence is Peyton's category jump from PMARC's spaceflight hardware to legal-marketing SaaS, which is a founder-level pivot rather than a company-level thesis update. That's worth something but it's thin -- I score this mid because the company history is too short to penalize aggressively, not because I see learning-rate signal.

Capital profile, the single-anchor parallel: **Genesis AI** (July 2025) absorbed a $105M co-led seed because the foundation-model-for-robots roadmap required exactly that depth before product-market fit. Perceptron ML is a 2-person team running an agentic SaaS that will hit $1M ARR on $3-5M if it works at all. The $3.5B vehicle (TechCrunch 2025-02-24) is built to deploy concentration into capital-hostile science, not to write $500K YC standard-deal checks into legal marketing automation. I am over-resourced for Perceptron ML by an order of magnitude.

My 36-month forecast on Perceptron ML: acqui-hired by FirmPilot, EvenUp, or Eve for sub-$30M between mid-2027 and 2029, OR pivoted out of legal into trading-desk signal-to-execution where the consequence is larger and the incumbent landscape less crowded. The mechanism producing this forecast is structural: a 2-person team building commodity agentic orchestration against $636M of adjacent capital, with no proprietary data, no disclosed customers, and no defensibility signal, gets bought for its NVIDIA-pedigreed engineering before it gets out-shipped. The MyCase data point -- law-firm AI adoption from 19% (2023) to 79% (2024) -- accelerates the timeline of incumbent extension, not Perceptron ML's escape velocity.

The strongest opposing case is that Perceptron ML's event-trigger-to-campaign loop is structurally orthogonal to FirmPilot's always-on content engine and Darrow's detection-only product, which means Perceptron ML could become the seam-stitching layer that EvenUp eventually pays $200M+ to acquire -- and Stanford-NVIDIA engineering pedigree against incumbents whose differentiation is fundraising scale rather than AI-agent depth could compound into proprietary signal-source coverage faster than the bear case allows. That bull case stings, but it dies on two dossier facts: Perceptron ML explicitly requires "human approval" before launching campaigns (YC description), which caps the speed advantage that is the entire value proposition, and the same dossier names four verticals -- law, construction, investors, trading -- which is the Range-Fuels-style focus dilution pattern signaling Perceptron ML does not know which incumbent regime it is collapsing. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict on Perceptron ML: a signed enterprise contract with a top-50 plaintiff firm at disclosed ARR above $500K within 12 months, demonstrating the human-approval loop runs at the promised "hours" SLA without throughput collapse. Absent that, this is a pass that no Known Exception rescues -- DoorDash had founder ambition framing the consequence at consumer-commerce scale, Slash had a documented pivot, Glean had capital scale -- Perceptron ML has none of the three.
