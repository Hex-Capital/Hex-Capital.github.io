# StableBrowse -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

StableBrowse is the canonical 2026 YC AI-agent infrastructure pitch attacking a sleeve where Browserbase already has $67.5M and ~$3M of revenue, Kernel has $22M from Accel, and TinyFish has $47M from ICONIQ — and StableBrowse is bringing four engineers, a semantic-graph wrapper, and zero public founder footprint to that fight. The dimensional signature is a company that's lowest on category-sleeve fit (this is exactly "AI is just more internet") and least-bad on round-pricing only because the insurance/healthcare/mortgage vertical wedge is a half-step off the pure-horizontal-browser-infra consensus. That's not a venture moment; that's a feature inside someone else's roadmap.

Solana at $0.05 is the high rung on round-pricing because the entire crypto category had been abandoned after the ICO crash — I was bidding against silence. StableBrowse is the inverse mechanism: I'd be bidding against Notable Capital, Accel, and ICONIQ, all of whom have already underwritten that the agent-browser layer is a category. The dossier's only price-discipline angle is that StableBrowse is wedging into HIPAA-regulated verticals (insurance quote binding, prior auth, mortgage), which is at least one degree off the horizontal commodity — but SOC 2 Type I and II are both still "in progress," so the regulated-vertical moat that would justify a non-consensus read is asserted, not built. The price StableBrowse will clear at in a hot S26 batch with that competitive set is the price I cannot make money at.

The canonical pass-example founder — sanitized pitch, no public writing, no GitHub, no opinionated thread on the category — is essentially the StableBrowse founder profile as the dossier renders it. Jay Mehta has eight years of distributed-systems SWE and a resume site; Deepit Shah has UIUC CS and an Amazon stint plus an RBF numerical-methods paper; Sarthak Awasthi and Somansh Shah are name-only entries the researcher could not even confirm against LinkedIn. Zero Twitter accounts across four cofounders is itself the signal — "writing is thinking, if you can't write you can't think" — and StableBrowse founders have published nothing about why browser-agent reliability fails in the specific shape their semantic graph fixes. The Wizard-of-Oz test (six months of manual workflow before automating) cannot be evaluated because StableBrowse provides no narrative of having run insurance prior-auths by hand for any carrier before pitching the graph.

The capital-structure question compounds badly over eighteen months: StableBrowse is a consumption-API SaaS cap table competing against an incumbent ($67.5M raised) that can simply ship a "persistent site memory" feature on top of its existing 20K developer signups and 50M monthly browser sessions. To stay in the game, StableBrowse must raise a $5M seed, then a $15M A against Browserbase's $40M Series B at $300M post, then a $40M B — three more tacks before the regatta starting line, each at a pricing the company cannot control because the comp set sets it. This is the inverse of Teamshares, where the financing instrument itself is the moat; StableBrowse has the most fungible cap-table shape in the YC batch.

What would flip the founder-as-brand dimension to a tier? StableBrowse would need at least one cofounder running a developer-focused YouTube or technical blog with 10K+ engaged followers writing publicly about why HTML-parsing agents fail on insurance carrier portals — the kind of organic-distribution wedge the Slow Creator Fund exists to underwrite when the founder IS the channel. Instead, the dossier records "no public account found" four times in a row, and the "Trusted by engineers at Meta, Google, Amazon, Citi, EY, Purdue, Stanford, UCSD" line on stablebrowse.com is the LinkedIn-affiliations-of-friends play, not owned distribution. The founder-channel cap-table contribution is zero.

StableBrowse on category sleeve is the single clearest call in this evaluation — pure agent infrastructure at seed pricing is the exact axis where "AI is kind of like mobile, it's not disruptive — crypto is fundamentally disruptive" re-weights my allocation away. Compared to Aleo, which I bought when the zk-proof category had two real economic actors and no consensus, StableBrowse is buying a fifth-place slot in a category where the consensus has already deployed nine figures and the open-source alternative (Browser Use) is shipping the same chain-of-thought primitive for free.

My 36-month forecast for StableBrowse: acqui-hired by Browserbase or Kernel for sub-$30M in talent-and-tech terms by mid-2028, or pivoted into a vertical RPA-as-a-service for insurance prior-auth running a manual ops team behind an API and quietly never raising the Series A. The mechanism is straightforward — the semantic-graph layer is a feature, not a company, and the only escape from being a feature is to become the Wizard-of-Oz ops shop for one regulated vertical, which is a fine business but not the round StableBrowse is currently raising.

The strongest bull case for StableBrowse leans on the Ro exception: regulated verticals (HIPAA healthcare, insurance carrier portals, mortgage lender systems) are full of integration schleps that no horizontal infrastructure provider will do, and the company that grinds through 200 carrier-specific knowledge graphs becomes structurally un-displaceable by Browserbase. That argument stings because it's the one shape under which I'd override price discipline. It loses on the dossier evidence: Ro had to clear actual 50-state prescription rails — a regulatory primitive — while StableBrowse's "HIPAA compliant" claim sits next to "SOC 2 Type I and II in progress," meaning the regulatory schleps are aspirational marketing, not the moat. The condition that would flip me to invest: two signed pilot contracts with named top-15 US health insurers or top-10 mortgage originators at >$100K ACV apiece, evidencing the vertical-wedge thesis is operating, not pitched.
