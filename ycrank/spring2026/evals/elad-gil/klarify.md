# Klarify -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Klarify is a competent repeat-founder team executing into a category where the winners have already been declared -- Moody Abdul shipped Circleback (YC W24, $2.5M seed, $50K ARR in four months) and Bergie shipped LLM infrastructure at Workday, but they are arriving at therapy-scribe roughly twenty-four months after Upheal closed its $3.25M seed and Blueprint closed a $9M Series A. The dimensional signature is high on founder shipping history, low on index-like distribution position, and that pairing tells me exactly what Klarify is: a Harvey-clone problem applied to vertical clinical scribing, where the entry price will be consensus but the exit will be share-fight, not category capture.

Klarify scores highest on founder execution rhythm, but the relevant rung is not **Perplexity** (2022). When Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn, the load-bearing signal was that he shipped a new artifact between every meeting -- not that he had a prior exit, but that the iteration cadence was visible in real time. Klarify's dossier gives me Moody's prior Circleback build (legitimately a shipped artifact, $50K ARR in four months is real) and Bergie's Workday LLM infrastructure plus a UBC computer-vision patent, which is genuine technical shipping history. What it does not give me is the Aravind pattern of cadence-between-conversations, and it gives me one outright concern: Moody co-founded Circleback approximately 12-18 months before Klarify's Spring 2026 batch and the dossier cannot resolve whether he is time-split across both. Two strong shipping résumés, no observed iteration tempo, one unresolved concentration question -- that lands above Brex-archetype execution discipline but below the Perplexity bar.

Klarify scores lowest on index-like distribution position, and the failure mechanism here is the **Lyft** Series C pattern I passed on -- not because Lyft was bad, but because #2 in a market with a declared leader captures a fraction of #1's economics regardless of how well it executes. Trace the pattern through Klarify's dossier: Upheal has 70,000 providers and $14.35M raised with full EHR + scheduling + billing, Blueprint has 4,500 clinicians across 34 states with 1M+ analyzed sessions and measurement-based-care lock-in, Mentalyc has 30,000 therapists with SimplePractice and TherapyNotes write-back integrations already shipped. Klarify is entering at position four or five with "works with all EHRs, no integration needed" -- which is not a wedge, that is a ceiling, because group practices specifically need write-back into SimplePractice. Klarify does not sit in the workflow path every entrant must touch; Klarify must beat named incumbents on features. That is the Lyft-shape, not the **Stripe** (2010) shape.

Klarify's market catalyst dimension is where the mechanism analysis matters: the LLM step-function for clinical documentation is real, but it is a catalyst that has been *compounding for competitors since 2023*, not opening for Klarify in 2026. Upheal's seed closed Feb 2024, Blueprint's Series A closed in 2023, Mentalyc raised in 2022. The 24-month catalyst window I used to enter **Anduril** (2022) on Ukraine was a window peers were ignoring; Klarify's window has been the most-pitched YC vertical-AI thesis for two batches running. Late catalyst entry without a distribution wedge is not the **Stripe** (2010) template, it is the inverse.

The counterfactual on revenue velocity: for Klarify's score to flip a tier on this dimension, the dossier would need MoM revenue growth disclosed in numbers, not the "~6,000 therapists" YC-page figure that the klarify.ca live counter contradicts at 2,154. That gap between 6,000 and 2,154 is exactly the "false signal versus just working" distinction I drew in the November 2025 TechCrunch piece -- free-tier signup tourism reads as 6,000, actual usage reads as 2,154, and paying base reads as neither because Klarify did not disclose it. Harvey at $3B→$5B→$8B disclosed the curve because the curve was real; AI companies with explosive growth disclose it. Klarify did not.

Single-miracle parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015): I architected Color as workflow on top of commodity sequencing precisely so we only owed one miracle -- the workflow layer, not the chemistry. Klarify's stack owes more than one. Miracle one is LLM accuracy on therapy-specific notes across 20+ formats including EMDR/IFS/EFT, which competitors have validated is achievable. Miracle two is CPT code extraction accurate enough that therapists trust Klarify to "complete insurance claims" without triggering payer audits -- that is a regulatory-liability surface, not a documentation surface. Miracle three is the "no EHR integration needed" claim holding up at group-practice scale where Mentalyc and Blueprint have write-back. Three independent miracles compound to a probability my framework rejects.

My 36-month forecast on Klarify: reaches $2-5M ARR on the back of Moody's LinkedIn enterprise-sales muscle, then exits to SimplePractice or TherapyNotes for under $50M as the EHR incumbents bolt on AI modules and close the no-integration ceiling -- or Upheal and Blueprint pull further ahead and Klarify winds down or pivots into therapist-supervision tooling specifically. The mechanism is straightforward: late catalyst entry plus #4 position plus multi-miracle architecture equals share-fight economics, and share-fight economics in clinical SaaS get acquired, not compounded.

The bull case on Klarify that stings: Moody sold $20M in enterprise contracts at LinkedIn and shipped Circleback to $50K ARR in four months, which means he has the rare combination of vertical-sales chops and product-shipping rhythm that most YC pre-seed founders lack -- and the therapy market is fragmented enough across 200,000+ US licensed therapists that even a #4 entrant can build a $50M ARR business if the claim-filing wedge is real, because no incumbent owns insurance-coding workflow today. Steel-manned, this is the **Decagon** (2023) template in vertical clinical: workflow infrastructure in a still-fragmenting category. The dismantle: Decagon entered customer support when the category had no declared winner; Klarify enters therapy scribing when Upheal has 70,000 providers and Blueprint has 1M+ sessions in the bank. That is the *AI Market Clarity* "winners declared" quadrant, not the "wide open" quadrant. The verifiable flip condition: Klarify discloses >20% MoM paid-tier revenue growth in the next 90 days AND signs a group practice of 50+ clinicians on annual contract -- either of those alone would not flip me, both together would force me to re-run the index test from scratch.
