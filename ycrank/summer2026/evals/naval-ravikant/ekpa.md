# Ekpa -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Ekpa is a portfolio-aggregation dashboard from two MIT undergrads — Yuga Patel and Anant Asthana — pitching LLM-generated share-count recommendations off read-only brokerage feeds, and the signature that jumps out is a moderately real retail-fintech market wrapped around a permission-gated distribution stack and pedigree-not-specific-knowledge founders. That pairing — an expandable market underwritten by everything I discount most — is the reason Ekpa doesn't clear my angel bar, not the pre-launch state.

Anchorage Digital is the load-bearing analogy on leverage architecture, because both Anchorage and Ekpa route through permissioned rails as a structural feature, not an accident: Anchorage traded that off for an OCC charter as moat, whereas Ekpa's read-only brokerage integration via the Plaid/SnapTrade layer is a commodity API that any team can wire up in a weekend. The Anchorage exception let me override the permissionless rule because pre-consensus crypto-category timing and advisor-level involvement collapsed the leverage question into a regulatory-moat question. Ekpa has no equivalent override — a consumer subscription funnel gated by "not a broker-dealer or investment adviser" disclaimer language on goekpa.com is the inverse of the Twitter (2007) template, where anyone could post at zero marginal cost. The "share count recommendations" phrasing is the tell; if the SEC forces even light-touch adviser registration, Ekpa's distribution architecture inverts from consumer-permissionless to compliance-permissioned overnight.

Founder-market fit is where Ekpa fails the hardest, and the failure mechanism is the one I flagged in the Pinterest post-mortem reversed: pedigree standing in for specific knowledge. Yuga Patel's LinkedIn headline reads "CS/AI + Econ @ MIT" and Anant Asthana is listed as an MIT student — no prior hedge-fund seat, no shipped finance product, no open-source repo found in searches, no GitHub artifacts named in the dossier. Contrast with Aravind Srinivas arriving at Perplexity with a Berkeley retrieval PhD, or Atwood and Spolsky *being* the developer audience they wrote Stack Overflow for. Anant's anant.us blog post on autonomous AI trading agents is the single specific-knowledge signal in the dossier, and one essay is not multi-year immersion. The "ex-FAANG PM identified $X TAM opportunity" red flag I keep watching for reads here as "two MIT undergrads identified retail-investor pain point" — same category error, different vocabulary.

Ekpa's network dimension compounds backwards over 18 months: each retail user's holdings are economically independent of every other user's, so the Nth signup adds nothing to the (N-1) prior users the way a Twitter follower graph or an OpenSea listing does. LinqAlpha's $22M Series A in July 2026 with 70+ institutional customers is the demonstration that even in this exact category, the winners so far are single-sided B2B — not the marketplace geometry that underwrites my 10,000x math (Knowledge Project #18).

For Big Market to flip up a tier, Ekpa would need to describe at least two adjacent pivot vectors inside retail fintech — say, from position-dashboard to creator-portfolio-publishing, or from research-agent to a builder-tool sold to independent RIAs. The dossier instead contains a scope contradiction: goekpa.com's product page frames a portfolio-aggregation dashboard while a search snippet from the same domain describes a "hold S&P 500 above long-term trend, cash below" trend-following product. Two products on one landing page at pre-seed is not pivot-room, it is unfixed thesis.

On the ten-year-partner check, Ekpa's founders read clean — no partner litigation, no self-promotional integrity signaling, no contested prior exits. That is the absent-evidence default holding, not a positive signal, and I do not punish quiet undergrads for it. Notion (2013) at Ivan Zhao's equivalent age would have looked similar on this axis; what Notion had that Ekpa does not is the adolescent-onward productivity-tool obsession that made criterion #3 clear at top quartile.

My 36-month forecast on Ekpa: shut down or acqui-hired into a brokerage-aggregation layer like SnapTrade for sub-$5M, with sub-$500K ARR at the point of exit — the signature (thin specific-knowledge base, no network compounding, permission-gated distribution) does not survive its first product-market-fit cliff, and incumbents Public.com and Robinhood ship the same LLM-per-holding research feature natively before Ekpa builds a defensible user base.

The strongest bull case leans on the Perplexity exception: single-sided AI product, MIT-caliber founders, pre-consensus category (agentic retail research), and permissionless-via-API distribution. Aravind cleared that bar because his specific knowledge in retrieval was extreme and pre-ChatGPT-scale category timing was mine to catch. Ekpa's Anant blog post and Yuga's coursework do not clear the specific-knowledge bar the Perplexity exception required — the override was three-of-five gates passing emphatically, and Ekpa passes at most one. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Yuga or Anant ship an open-source agent framework parsing 10-Ks that earns 1,000+ organic GitHub stars before the priced round closes — the Stack Overflow / Replit pattern where founder-code precedes founder-equity-story. Absent that, Ekpa is a pass.
