# Gojiberry AI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and his two co-founders are exactly the operator profile I respect -- three documented 2025 exits between them (CoCo AI ~$500K ARR, Edusign multi-million ARR with 600K+ users) -- attached to a success-state that, at maximum ambition, does not move a single civilizational variable. Gojiberry AI is a high-learning-rate repeat-founder team selling a $99/mo intent-first sequencer to small sales teams; the dimensional signature is loud -- founder quality high, consequence-of-success near the floor -- and that signature is the one I have passed on for forty years.

The pass example I keep in my head reads almost verbatim like Gojiberry AI's dossier: "we use GPT-4 to summarize sales calls and integrate with HubSpot." Swap "summarize calls" for "auto-generate LinkedIn outreach against 30+ intent signals" and you have the same shape. The success-state for Gojiberry AI is a 3-5x reply lift inside one workflow for one persona, not the collapse of an incumbent regime. Apollo at $150M ARR and Clay at $3.1B valuation already own the data layer; if Gojiberry AI hits maximum ambition, the world-state is that small B2B sales teams send better cold emails. Compare that to **Impossible Foods** (2011), where maximum ambition meant the global animal-protein supply chain shrinks -- a $1.5T regime collapse. Gojiberry AI is the inverse: the consequence is an efficiency gain inside a workflow that the McKinsey 1986 cellphone forecast wouldn't even notice ten years out.

**Cognition** (2024, $175M round) is the AI-stack bet I want to compare Gojiberry AI to on technical-risk, and the comparison is unkind. Cognition trained models, owned the agent loop, and absorbed compute risk no consensus VC would underwrite without my conviction frame. Gojiberry AI scrapes LinkedIn engagement, pipes 30+ public signals through someone else's foundation model, and writes outreach -- a wrapper on commodity AI, the exact pattern my framework rejects as "API-deep, not stack-deep." The founder's own LinkedIn post "RIP traditional outbound. Gemini 3" is the tell: the moat is downstream of a model Google trained. Any Tier-1 generalist funds this; I have no edge.

Lallemant, Czerny, and Teixeira do not defy any documented expert consensus -- they are repeat SaaS operators running the SaaS playbook with better LLMs, and that is the **Slash** exception structure I have to test against. Slash got my $100M Series C despite violating consequence-magnitude because the Kanye West pivot demonstrated learning rate strong enough to override the prior, plus YC pedigree. Gojiberry AI has YC pedigree and a documented pivot ($33K revenue / 100 customers under prior positioning to $1.4M ARR under GTM-brain framing), so the exception structure is technically present. The differential: Slash's success-state still moved a meaningful slice of SMB banking flow; Gojiberry AI's success-state moves a sales workflow inside the same SMB. The exception requires both signals; one is not enough.

The capital-fit dimension is where Gojiberry AI is most explicit about being the wrong shape for my check. Pro plan at $99/mo, 7-person team, $1.4M ARR in 9 months on what looks like seed-friendly burn -- this is the commodity SaaS economics line in my Required-Capital anchor, $5-10M to $1M ARR, already done. I wrote $50M into **OpenAI** (2019) and led $115M into **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) because the roadmaps required capital depth that consensus VCs declined. Gojiberry AI's roadmap requires the opposite -- distribution velocity, not capital depth. I am over-resourced for the deal; any seed fund on Sand Hill funds this without me.

The 44% MoM growth and 1,000+ paying customers in 9 months are real, and on the founder-learning-rate axis Gojiberry AI scores genuinely well: Teixeira scaled Edusign to 600K+ users before selling, Lallemant and Czerny built and sold CoCo AI together, and the dossier shows a willingness to pivot mid-stream from $33K revenue to a new GTM positioning. This is the dimension where Gojiberry AI sits above my **Physical Intelligence** (2024) academic-spinout founders on demonstrated commercial learning -- but learning rate is a multiplier on consequence, not a substitute for it, and the consequence is the floor.

My 36-month forecast for Gojiberry AI: $8-20M ARR, then acquired by Apollo, Clay, or Outreach for $80-200M in a consolidating AI-SDR field -- a clean operator win, not a fund-returner. The mechanism is straightforward: the team executes, Gojiberry AI compounds to the $10-20M ARR band where AI-SDR companies plateau against Apollo's data depth, and Clay's $3.1B valuation gives it acquisition currency to absorb threats. Regie.ai's $30M Series B at 300% YoY ARR growth (Feb 2025) is the comparable arc -- the consolidation absorbs the breakout.

The strongest case against my pass invokes the **DoorDash** (2013) and **Slash** (2024) precedent: repeat-founder execution at YC-pedigree velocity with a documented pivot has, twice, overridden my consequence-magnitude prior and produced fund-defining outcomes. Gojiberry AI has the operator profile, the pivot, the YC stamp, and 44% MoM growth -- if learning rate is a multiplier on consequence, three exits and a working pivot is a high multiplier. The dossier evidence that wins the argument is Clay at $3.1B valuation and Apollo at $150M ARR sitting on the same intent-signal substrate Gojiberry AI is scraping; DoorDash had no incumbent of that scale sitting on its data layer, and Slash exploited a regulatory wedge in SMB banking that Gojiberry AI has no analog to. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Gojiberry AI demonstrates a proprietary signal source that Clay and Apollo structurally cannot replicate -- not 30 public LinkedIn signals, but a closed-loop outcome dataset across 5,000+ customers showing reply-lift compounding the rest of the field cannot match. Until that data exists, this is the pass example, not the **Slash** exception.
