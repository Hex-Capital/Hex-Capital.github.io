# HEVN, inc -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

HEVN, inc is a company whose strongest dimension — handing non-US founders a USD account without requiring an EIN — collides with its weakest: a founding trio whose specific knowledge sits in NEAR-protocol crypto wallets, not US-regulated banking. Peter Volnov, Nikita Briuzgin, and Andrei Zhevlakov co-founded HOT Labs and HERE Wallet (30M+ HOT Wallet users per Chainwire, Nov 2024), then in Spring 2026 emerged with HEVN as "Brex for international companies." That sequence — crypto consumer wallet to regulated B2B neobank — tells me most of what I need to know about HEVN, inc before I read another line.

Specific knowledge first, as always. The high rung on this ladder is Perplexity's Aravind Srinivas — Google Brain and OpenAI tenure productized into AI search, knowledge a competitor needs years to accumulate. The low rung is the AI-wrapper archetype I pass on at every demo day — MBAs calling an API. HEVN, inc's founders sit closer to the low rung than the mid: their specific knowledge is consumer crypto-wallet UX and stablecoin rails on NEAR, not sponsor-bank KYB pipelines, FDIC trust agreements, or non-US AML frameworks. The dossier flags this directly — "They have not previously operated a US-regulated banking product, which is the core operational capability HEVN requires." Stablecoin payment rails are a real adjacent skill, which keeps HEVN, inc from sitting at the bottom rung, but the productized core of HEVN, inc — a no-EIN FDIC-insured USD account — is not what Volnov's stack is built from.

Permissionless leverage is the dimension where HEVN, inc most directly fails my framework. The high rung is Twitter — code and media, zero marginal cost, billions of users on a small team. The low rung is Poshmark — marketplace operations requiring proportional headcount. HEVN, inc is structurally permissioned: every USD account, every FDIC wrapper, every "named local account in EU/US/UAE" is gated by a sponsor bank that can be revoked by the next compliance review. The dossier is explicit — "regulated partner institutions" underpin the FDIC eligibility, and the FDIC-no-EIN account is marketed as "soon," not live. This is capital and regulatory leverage masquerading as code leverage, which sits below Uber's marketplace-network mid rung. Airwallex already runs this playbook at $1.2B ARR and a $12B valuation (Sacra, March 2026), and they got there by absorbing exactly the licensing drag HEVN, inc still has ahead of it.

Principal authenticity is where HEVN, inc most resembles my pass example. Gumroad's Sahil Lavingia is the high rung — kept building solo after the Series A failed, identity fused with the product. The low rung is the founder who picks the most fundable adjacent category. Volnov, Briuzgin, and Zhevlakov are concurrently associated with HOT Labs, which the dossier reports as still operating into 2024–2025. HEVN, inc reads less like an obsession the founders couldn't stop building and more like a category pivot from a crypto consumer product into a fundable Brex-shaped wrapper — the YC tagline "Brex for international companies" itself is positioning, not specific knowledge. Signals are what people do, despite what they say; the signal here is that HEVN, inc's pitch leads with a comparable, not with a problem the founders lived inside.

Team-to-value leverage favors HEVN, inc structurally — four founders shipping at pre-seed sits near my Notion mid rung — but regulated fintech is the domain where my Midjourney high rung breaks down. Compliance, KYB review, and sanctions screening on non-US entities require the proportional headcount my framework penalizes; HEVN, inc cannot be Cursor with five engineers. Access democratization is HEVN, inc's strongest axis: no-EIN USD accounts for non-US-incorporated companies is genuine structural gatekeeping removal, in the same family as CoinList stripping securities lawyers out of token launches. It does not reach AngelList's high rung, but it sits cleanly above the Wish low rung — Wish was undifferentiated discount commerce, and Wish, as a Known Exception, ultimately collapsed 95% from IPO precisely because Naval-leverage without specific knowledge proved insufficient. HEVN, inc echoes that same shape.

My 36-month forecast for HEVN, inc: acqui-hired by Airwallex, Mercury, or a payments incumbent for under $40M, with ARR below $5M, after the no-EIN FDIC USD account either ships and gets cloned by Airwallex within two quarters or fails to clear sponsor-bank diligence at all. The mechanism is the dimensional signature — a real democratization wedge built by founders whose specific knowledge sits in the wrong stack, attacking a category where the $1.2B-ARR incumbent has the regulatory drag already paid down.

The strongest case against my forecast leans on the Wish exception inverted: HEVN, inc's leverage angle — disintermediating EIN as the gatekeeper for USD banking — could be sufficient even without founder-specific knowledge, the same way Wish's marketplace leverage briefly rode without it. The HOT Wallet 30M-user execution proves the team can ship at consumer scale, and Capital One's pending $5.15B Brex acquisition genuinely does open a non-US gap that incumbents may de-prioritize. I find this less compelling because Wish itself is the cautionary tale, not the green light — Naval's leverage criterion alone, without specific knowledge, was insufficient there and is insufficient here, and Airwallex is not Capital One's acquired Brex; Airwallex is the awake, growing, $1.2B-ARR competitor whose Brex-alternatives blog is already indexed for the keywords HEVN, inc wants to own. I would flip my verdict on HEVN, inc on one verifiable condition: a named US sponsor bank delivering live FDIC-insured no-EIN USD accounts to non-US entities, paired with $1M+ ARR within 12 months. Until then, HEVN, inc is a pass.
