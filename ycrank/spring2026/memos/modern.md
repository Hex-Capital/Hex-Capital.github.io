## The Idea

**Problem:** IT, HR, and Finance teams in mid-market and enterprise organizations spend significant time on high-volume, repetitive service requests — password resets, access provisioning, expense approvals, onboarding/offboarding checklists (getmodern.ai). These requests typically flow through legacy ITSM platforms (ServiceNow, Zendesk) that manage tickets but do not resolve them autonomously. [Inferred]: The acute pain is that these teams scale headcount linearly with ticket volume, creating a cost and speed bottleneck, especially as organizations expand.

**Approach:** Modern deploys AI agents that take tickets from request to resolution across IT, HR, and Finance workflows (getmodern.ai). Teams define workflows in plain language, set approval rules and policies, and Modern handles execution — password resets, access provisioning, expense approvals, onboarding checklists — with full audit trails, role-based approvals, and enterprise-grade security (YC page). The platform integrates with existing ITSM systems (ServiceNow, Zendesk), identity providers (Microsoft Graph, Google Workspace), HRIS (Workday, BambooHR), ERP (SAP), CRM (Salesforce), collaboration tools (Slack, Atlassian), and monitoring tools (Datadog, Cisco Meraki) (getmodern.ai). The system also learns from manual execution to suggest new automations (getmodern.ai). Modern additionally offers "Modern Managed," a fully managed IT services option where the company operates the service desk on behalf of the customer (getmodern.ai).

**Differentiation:**
- vs. legacy ITSM (ServiceNow, Zendesk): Modern is AI-native rather than ticket-management-first; it aims to eliminate tickets rather than route them (getmodern.ai). Legacy tools require extensive configuration and professional services; Modern claims go-live in under a day (getmodern.ai).
- vs. Moveworks (acquired by ServiceNow for $2.85B; LinkedIn post via search snippet): Moveworks operated as an AI layer on top of existing ITSM; post-acquisition, its roadmap is now tied to ServiceNow's platform strategy (clearfeed.ai, 2026 via search snippet). [Inferred]: Modern's independence and overlay-or-standalone flexibility may appeal to non-ServiceNow shops.
- vs. Ravenna ($15M seed; Khosla Ventures, Madrona; April 2025; finsmes.com): Ravenna is Slack-native and focused on mid-market (Madrona blog, 2025). [Inferred]: Modern differentiates with broader channel support (any corporate chat), deeper workflow automation across HR/Finance (not just IT), and the managed-services offering.
- vs. Espressive ($53M total funding; acquired by Resolve Systems, September 2025; Crunchbase via search snippet): Espressive's BaristaGPT focused on IT help desk; post-acquisition direction unclear.

## Defensibility

- **Workflow data compounding:** As customers define workflows in plain language and Modern's system learns from manual execution to suggest automations (getmodern.ai), each deployment generates proprietary workflow patterns. [Inferred]: Over time, this creates a data advantage — a library of enterprise-tested workflow templates that new competitors would lack.
- **Switching costs:** Once Modern handles access provisioning, onboarding/offboarding, and approval chains with custom policies and audit trails (getmodern.ai), migration requires re-creating those workflows and re-validating compliance posture. [Inferred]: Switching costs increase with the number of integrated systems and defined policies.
- **Integration surface:** Connections to 12+ enterprise systems (getmodern.ai) create a multi-tool dependency that raises replacement friction.

No structural barrier identified at this stage beyond integration lock-in. [Inferred]: Modern's combination of overlay deployment + managed services is unusual but not structurally protected from incumbents.

**Market structure:** ServiceNow's acquisition of Moveworks for $2.85B (LinkedIn post via search snippet) signals that the largest ITSM incumbent chose to buy rather than build autonomous resolution capabilities. [Inferred]: For ServiceNow, cannibalizing its own ticket-volume-based pricing model by eliminating tickets creates a business model conflict that may slow full deployment of autonomous resolution. Other ITSM incumbents (Zendesk, BMC) face similar cannibalization risk if their pricing is tied to ticket or agent volume.

**Commoditization risk:** The core technology (LLM-powered ticket resolution with workflow orchestration) is buildable by well-funded competitors. Ravenna ($15M raised; Khosla, Madrona; finsmes.com, April 2025) and Workativ (workativ.com) offer similar AI-agent-for-ITSM products. [Inferred]: The barrier is execution speed, integration depth, and accumulated workflow data rather than proprietary technology.
