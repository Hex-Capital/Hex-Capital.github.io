# Standard Signal -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Michael Royzen has shipped — Phind (YC S22) hit 150M+ searches per the AWS case study, and that is a real artifact, not a credential — but Standard Signal is a solo founder selling LP units in a hedge fund vehicle at $100K minimums with zero disclosed AUM, zero realized returns, and no quant-finance résumé. The signature here is high on builder-rhythm, low on index position: this is a talented LLM operator pointing his hammer at a returns game where the scoreboard is Sharpe and the incumbents are Renaissance, Two Sigma, and a Numerai that just took $500M of JPMorgan capacity (ainvest.com, 2026). That pairing — shipper-founder, anti-index market — is not the Anduril (2022) template. It's closer to the Lyft Series C template I passed on.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the load-bearing comparison on founder execution rhythm, because Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and Royzen has a structurally similar shipping artifact in Phind — YC S22, 150M+ searches, Paul Graham-backed per Humanloop. Where Standard Signal diverges from the Perplexity rung is the *what was shipped*. Royzen has shipped LLM productization in developer search; he has not shipped a P&L, a risk book, a custodian relationship, or an SEC-registered vehicle. The dossier lists open roles for an ML Research Engineer and a Quantitative Researcher but no compliance, ops, or trading hire, and Royzen's LinkedIn shows zero portfolio-management history at Lyft, Cloudflare, or Microsoft. The shipping rhythm is real; the shipping *surface* is the wrong one for the bet being made.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure pattern I want to put on Standard Signal directly: a #2 position in a market where the #1's economics compound away from you, and I passed on the 20VC framework of winner-take-all-versus-oligopoly. Standard Signal sits in the same shape on index position. A hedge fund is not the workflow layer that every entrant in a category must touch — it *is* the entrant, and it must out-Sharpe Numerai, which already reported 25%+ net returns in 2024 and locked $500M from JPMorgan with seven years of crowdsourced ensemble data Royzen does not have. Stripe (2010) was an index on e-commerce because every merchant had to touch it; Standard Signal is the opposite — LPs choose between funds, and Standard Signal must *beat* the named ones with no track record. The dossier confirms it: market-neutral strategy, Sharpe 2.0+ target, $100K minimum, no audited performance disclosed (standardsignal.com).

The catalyst question gets a soft answer. YC issued an "AI-Native Hedge Funds" RFS for Spring 2026 (modelence.com), and reasoning-capable LLMs at sub-second latency are a real 24-month step-function — that's not nothing. But the *market re-opening* I underwrite is one where incumbents structurally cannot adopt the new capability, the way the Pentagon couldn't ship software pre-Anduril. Renaissance and Two Sigma have been running model stacks against multi-asset signals for two decades; they will adopt frontier LLMs internally with better data, better execution, and audited risk. The catalyst is real for *AI in trading*; the catalyst for *a new solo-founder fund* is much weaker, which is why Standard Signal lands closer to a Decagon-adjacent mid-rung than to an Anduril top-rung on this axis.

Revenue velocity compounds — or doesn't — on a brutal clock for fund vehicles: an AI company without explosive growth in 12 months is broken (Tim Ferriss Show #863), and a hedge fund without a 24-month live track record cannot raise institutional LP capital regardless of pitch quality. Standard Signal has neither. The dossier shows YC batch participation, two open roles, one launch tweet from @MichaelRoyzen, and the marketing claim of "87.3%-94.2% confidence levels" with no methodology — that is brochure data, not curve data. Eighteen months from now, either Standard Signal has audited monthly NAV and a Sharpe number an allocator can underwrite, or it is going to look like Quantopian in 2020.

Counterfactual on single-miracle architecture: for Standard Signal to land in the top quartile here, the dossier would need to isolate the miracle as "frontier LLM accurately reasons about asset X" and leave everything else as GTM-and-execution. Instead it stacks four independent miracles — LLM alpha generation actually works on multi-asset chains-of-thought (industry-unverified per the dossier's own note on FinGPT), a solo founder builds compliance and custodian infrastructure, LPs commit before track record, and autonomous execution doesn't blow up in an unhedged regime. That's the multi-miracle compounding I avoid in fusion and novel-chemistry bets, ported into finance. Color Genomics (2015) worked because I *operated* it and structured it as workflow infrastructure above commodity sequencing — Standard Signal is the inverse, betting on the hard layer itself.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Standard Signal does not reach a fund-scale outcome, and the most likely terminal state is a pivot to selling its LLM trading research stack as infrastructure to existing funds (QuantConnect-adjacent SaaS or a Bloomberg-add-in), under $5M ARR, or a quiet wind-down with Royzen returning to AI-search or starting an unrelated YC-graduate company. The mechanism is the index-position failure compounded by the regulatory clock — Standard Signal cannot raise meaningful LP capital without audited performance, cannot build audited performance without proprietary capital, and cannot raise proprietary capital from non-LP sources at a valuation that pencils versus selling the same tech as software.

The bull case stings, and I want to name it: Royzen is a YC-alum founder who already shipped one breakout LLM product, the AI-native hedge fund category has an explicit YC RFS and a Numerai-at-$500M-JPMorgan capacity validation, and a solo operator with $5-10M of friends-and-family proprietary capital could plausibly produce a clean 18-month track record that triggers a Series A from a fund-of-funds LP. The bear-killer in that case is that Numerai's $500M came after seven years of crowdsourced model data and a published methodology — Standard Signal has neither, has a solo headcount, and is fundraising LP units before audited NAV exists, which is the inverse of how this market clears. I would flip my verdict on one verifiable condition: Standard Signal publishes a third-party-audited monthly NAV series with Sharpe above 1.5 over twelve consecutive months on at least $5M of capital, at which point this becomes a revenue-velocity conversation rather than a category-fit conversation. Until then, pass — same logic as Lyft Series C, not the same logic as Anduril.
