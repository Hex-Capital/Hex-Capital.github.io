# Runtime -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Gus Trigos shipped four full-stack products inside Nuvocargo after they bought his last company (Mentum, YC S21), then watched his non-engineering colleagues struggle to do the same -- that is Runtime's origin, and it is the only sentence in the dossier that makes me lean in. Everything else about Runtime reads as the most-pitched lane in YC Spring 2026: a control plane for Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, Gemini CLI, Devin. So Runtime's signature in my portfolio is a lived-experience founder I'd like to back, dropped into a consensus category I'd normally walk past -- a Trigos-shaped check that the market has already priced.

**Anduril / Palmer Luckey is my high rung on founder origin** -- Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before he ever touched a defense contract, and the biography put him in front of the problem first. Runtime's analogy load-bearing because Trigos did the same shape of thing in miniature: a YC S21 founder whose AI-agent procurement startup got acquired, then who personally felt the "I shipped four products, my teammates can't reuse my setup" pain inside Nuvocargo. Where Runtime diverges from the Anduril rung is consequence -- Luckey's lived experience routed him to a $100B regulated category with a hardware moat; Trigos's lived experience routed him to a SaaS dashboard sitting on top of seven other vendors' CLIs. The founder origin is real. The territory the origin opened up is not Anduril-shaped.

**HQ Trivia is my low rung on the consensus-category dimension** -- a tactile product so addictive I overrode my own co-founder governance heuristic, then watched Yusupov/Kroll implode partly because the lane became saturated faster than the moat compounded. Does that failure mechanism appear in Runtime's dossier? Yes, but transposed: the saturation arrived *before* the check. E2B closed a $21M Series A led by Insight in July 2025; Daytona closed $24M from FirstMark in February 2026 with a stated $1M-to-$2M ARR ramp in six weeks; Runloop took $7M from The General Partnership in 2025. Anthropic and OpenAI both have enterprise SKUs. Runtime's "team control plane vs. agent-builder API" wedge is real positioning, but it's the kind of distinction that gets compressed when a $24M Series A competitor moves up-stack. I'm supposed to find them *before* it becomes consensus. This is consensus arriving on schedule.

Carlos Volante scaled Modern Treasury to $350B in annual payments volume at 99.995% automation -- that is the kind of regulated-infrastructure résumé that, in 18 months, either compounds into a real enterprise audit/governance moat (the SOC2, the encrypted secrets, the spend caps Runtime already markets) or it gets neutralized when Anthropic ships native team admin for Claude Code. The mechanism is: Volante's depth lets Runtime sell into security/compliance reviews other YC dev-tools companies fail. The mechanism's failure mode is: the buyer eventually asks Anthropic directly. Runtime needs to anchor a non-YC enterprise logo before that asymmetry resolves against it.

Single-CEO governance: Trigos is Co-Founder & CEO, Volante is Co-Founder & CTO, and a third unnamed team member is not a founder per the YC page. For the score on this dimension to flip down a tier, I'd need to see equity disclosed at 50/50 between Trigos and Volante with no tiebreaker protocol, or "top-grading" language from a prior board -- neither appears in the dossier. The Mentum-to-Nuvocargo acquisition also means Trigos has already gone through one full founder/board/exit cycle as the named decision-maker, which is the inverse of the GameCrush failure mode I cite. This is the cleanest dimension Runtime hits.

**Niantic is my single anchor on physical-world verifiability** -- I funded Hanke partly because I had been playing Ingress obsessively since 2012 and could feel the artifact in my hand. Runtime is observable: there's a YouTube launch video, a Product Hunt listing, an open-source repo at `runtm-ai/runtm` with 119 stars, a dashboard the website shows rendering "$2,184 total cost across 35 team members." A normal engineering manager could install it this afternoon. But the artifact is a governance dashboard, not Pokémon GO -- I can observe it, but I cannot feel the viral pull the way I felt HQ Trivia. Tactile, yes. Magnetic, no.

My 36-month forecast for Runtime: $2M-$5M ARR with YC-adjacent mid-market customers, then an acqui-hire by Anthropic, GitHub, or Cursor in the $30M-$80M range as the enterprise team-admin layer those vendors didn't want to build themselves. That outcome is consistent with the signature -- a real founder generates a real product that solves a real pain, but the consensus lane and the platform-dependency on third-party agent CLIs caps the upside before it compounds into a defining company. Trigos walks away whole. I do not 10x my fund on it.

The strongest bull case against my forecast goes like this: Trigos is exactly the outsider-trajectory founder the Hustle Fund profile (2024) said I structurally prefer -- Hult finance degree, self-taught C++ since high school, BlackRock quant, repeat YC founder with an exit, *not* Stanford/Goldman/McKinsey. Volante's Modern Treasury and Finix scars are the regulated-infrastructure DNA Runtime needs. They have 40+ countries with zero ad spend. This is the Affirm/Max Levchin shape: a serial founder I should override category bias for. That case stings. I dismantle it on one point -- Affirm's moat was a balance sheet and a regulated lending license, not a UX layer over other people's CLIs. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict to Invest: Runtime lands a named non-YC Fortune 1000 anchor at six-figure ACV in the next two quarters, sourced outside Trigos's existing network. Until then, this is a check I admire and don't write.
