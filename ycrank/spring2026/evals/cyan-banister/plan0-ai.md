# PLAN0 AI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Arash Barati ran $7B of commercial real estate across PE, lending, and development before he ever pitched PLAN0 AI, and that single biographical fact is the only thing in this dossier that activates my lens — the rest is a consensus AI-SaaS shape I have seen six times this batch. PLAN0 AI is a domain-expert founder strapped to a category my hand cannot touch: an enterprise demo-gated workflow tool with no installable artifact, no public pricing, and five named competitors already benchmarked in the same accuracy tournament. The signature is Anduril's Palmer Luckey arriving at Togal.AI's pre-existing wedding, which is a tense pairing and not a clean one.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus to scale before he ever touched a defense contract, which is why Anduril was a tattoo and not a gamble — the founder *was* the buyer profile. PLAN0 AI's Arash Barati passes the same test more literally than most founders I see: he didn't read about underwriting in a memo, he ran $4.5B of lending and $1.5B of CRE PE, which means the developers and lenders PLAN0 AI is selling to are people he sat across the table from for fourteen years. The divergence from the Luckey rung is that Palmer also built the artifact himself in a garage; PLAN0 AI's CV models are built by his brother Shervin, the ex-Apple AirPods engineering manager, which is competent but means the founder lived the *problem* without ever living the *solution*. That gap usually shows up later as a founder who can sell the first ten contracts and then needs a different operator for the eleventh.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I pass on without finishing the deck — two ex-FAANG PMs, $200B TAM slide, six funds chasing — is the rung PLAN0 AI sits one notch above on weirdness, and not a comfortable notch. Six AI estimating platforms were benchmarked side-by-side in February 2026 (Robotics & Automation News), Togal.AI is already at $17.2M raised with 98% floor-plan accuracy, Kreo is shipping at $35/month, and InEight is owned by Kiewit. PLAN0 AI is not the laughed-at idea; it is the *fifteenth* idea, with a slightly better cost-database story. I am supposed to find them before it becomes consensus, and construction-estimation AI became consensus eighteen months ago. The "$20B of projects already running through the platform" line is the kind of metric a launch announcement produces and a customer list does not — I would want named lenders, not aggregate notional.

Brothers as CEO/CTO with a best friend as third co-founder is not the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll fracture pattern on its face, because Arash is the named CEO and the dossier does not telegraph 50/50 equity. But brothers carry their own tiebreaker problem: the disagreement that ends a co-founder relationship in a non-family company gets papered over for years in a family one, and then detonates at Series B when a board seat finally forces the conversation. Dimitris Pagonakis from Citadel/MIT is unaccounted for in role — the dossier literally says "role not publicly specified" — which is exactly the governance silence I default-discount on. The Banister rule is to find the tiebreaker before the cap table is set, and PLAN0 AI's cap table is opaque to me.

Construction is unsexy in the right way — developers, lenders, GCs are not the rooms a Stanford CS grad volunteers into — but PLAN0 AI's *product* is a pure software abstraction sitting on top of computer vision, which is the lane any well-funded competitor can replicate by licensing RSMeans or Gordian cost feeds. The unsexiness here is in the buyer, not the moat. Crusoe's defensibility was that you had to physically own a generator on a well pad in North Dakota; PLAN0 AI's defensibility is "industry partnerships" that are not named in any source I can verify. If the partnerships are Arash's $7B Rolodex turned into data licenses, that is interesting and worth a call. If they are aspirational, this is a CV wrapper.

I cannot install PLAN0 AI, I cannot photograph it, I cannot hand it to a developer friend in 48 hours — the site offers "Book a Demo" and nothing else, no pricing page, no sandbox, no public accuracy benchmark against the six competitors who *have* published theirs. Flock Safety I sourced from a Wi-Fi name; Niantic I played for three years before I wrote the check; Uber I rode. PLAN0 AI I would have to call Arash and ask him to walk me through a screen share, which is exactly the founder-testimony evaluation mode I down-weight on principle. The absence of a tactile artifact at pre-seed is normal; the absence of any *demonstrable* output — a sample takeoff, a scenario simulation video, a public bench — is a choice.

Thirty-six month forecast: PLAN0 AI settles at $4-12M ARR serving a tight cluster of mid-market CRE developers Arash already knew, gets acqui-hired by Procore, CoStar, or a RSMeans/Gordian parent for $40-120M as a workflow feature, and never becomes the company that defines the category — because Togal.AI raises a $40M Series B in the next eighteen months, compresses pricing, and PLAN0 AI's cost-database moat turns out to be a licensing relationship that Togal can replicate in two quarters. The mechanism is the high/low pairing: a domain-expert founder produces real revenue inside his network, but a software-only product in a six-competitor field cannot escape its category without a tactile or regulatory wedge PLAN0 AI does not appear to have.

The bull case stings here, and I want to honor it: Arash Barati's $7B network is not a Rolodex, it is a distribution channel that Togal — a contractor-first tool — structurally cannot reach without burning its installed base. Lender-side underwriting workflows are a different buyer than GC-side takeoffs, and the channel conflict is real. If PLAN0 AI signs Blackstone Real Estate Debt, KKR, or a top-five CRE lender as a named design partner in the next twelve months, the founder-lived-experience score I gave is dramatically underpriced and this becomes the rare case where the Rolodex *is* the wedge — the Affirm/Levchin override, founder-as-distribution. The dossier evidence that holds me back today is that the named partnerships, the named customers, and the named lender LOIs are all absent from every source the researcher could find, and "$20B of projects" without a single counterparty name pattern-matches to launch-announcement framing, not to underwriting reality. Flip condition: a named top-ten CRE lender LOI surfaces within twelve months — then I am writing a check at the next round, not this one.
