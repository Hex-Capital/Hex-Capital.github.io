# Pavoot -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Pavoot is what you get when two ETH Zürich computer science students take an AI Center course, ship an agent across the event lifecycle, and call it a company — Gohar Tamrazyan is a six-time Swiss Chess Champion and Ana Yoon Faria de Lima collected twenty-plus Olympiad medals, and that pairing tells me exactly what this is: a credentialed-technical sprint into a consensus B2B AI category. The highest dimension here is biographical pedigree, and the lowest is contrarian conviction — that combination, in my experience, almost always produces a beautifully-built product in a space where the moat dissolves the moment Salesforce ships Einstein for events. Pavoot is competent. It is not magically weird.

Tell me their story, not the business — and the story I get is ETH AI Center, course-to-startup, with no biographical encounter with the event-marketing problem itself. Compare that to my Crusoe rung: those founders watched gas being flared in North Dakota fields, lived inside the wasted-energy problem, and built portable data centers because they couldn't unsee it. The mid rung is Substrate's James Proud — forged in fire, came out unbroken — semiconductor conviction earned through prior burn. The low rung is my Pass Example: "I was at Google and saw the opportunity." Pavoot sits one notch above that low rung — Gohar and Ana didn't optimize a career, they built a thing during a course and got a real customer at START Summit — but neither founder shows a personal scar from running an event-driven sales motion. The problem did not find them. They found a problem.

Anduril when defense tech was politically toxic in the Valley, Contraline when male contraception made VCs squeamish — those are my high rungs for contrarian conviction, and Pavoot is the inverse. The dossier hands me Vendelux at $16.4M raised and $2.1M of 2024 revenue, Grip at $14.5M raised, Brella, Lensmor, Cvent, Bizzabo, plus the entire YC P26 Demo Day fielding more AI agents than I can count. This is the consensus AI room. Nobody at a Sand Hill dinner shifts uncomfortably when you describe Pavoot — they nod and ask about ACV. The pivot mid-batch from media-hub-with-photo-tagging to pipeline-agent-with-CRM-sync is not contrarian repositioning, it is consensus chasing. Pavoot sits at the low rung with my AI-wrapper Pass Example, not near Anduril.

Pavoot fails the invisible-system test the way most B2B SaaS fails it. Uber attacked the taxi medallion cartel; Affirm attacked credit-card opacity that extracts from people who can't read the disclosures; Ownwell attacks property-tax appeal asymmetry. These are systems that exploit information asymmetry against ordinary people. Pavoot moves enterprise marketing dollars more efficiently into CRM — there is no opaque structure being made transparent for someone without power. The buyer is a Demand Gen leader who already has Cvent, HubSpot, and a six-figure event budget. Pavoot sits near my HQ Trivia / IRL low rung on this dimension; there is simply no hidden tax to surface.

On infrastructure defensibility at inflection, Flock Safety's camera network and Flexport's logistics graph are my high rungs — each new customer makes the system harder to displace. Pavoot's dossier explicitly notes the components are "widely available via OpenAI/Anthropic APIs" and that competitors could replicate within "6–12 months," with Salesforce Einstein and HubSpot Breeze sitting upstream of the entire CRM-sync claim. Pavoot is closer to HQ Trivia's no-moat profile than to Carta's switching costs. On cascading real-world impact, Uber and Wonderschool expanded access for people previously gatekept; Pavoot helps enterprises convert event attendees into pipeline. There is no new economic participant. It sits at the low rung.

My 36-month forecast: Pavoot either gets acquihired by Cvent, HubSpot, or Bizzabo for $5–15M as a talent-and-tech tuck-in by 2029, or it fizzles below $500K ARR after Vendelux ships an execution layer on top of its predictive intelligence. The mechanism is straightforward — Vendelux already covers 200K+ B2B events with paying customers; Pavoot's lifecycle-agent positioning is a feature inside Vendelux's roadmap, not a category Vendelux can't reach. Two ETH technical founders without enterprise GTM scar tissue do not out-sell a FirstMark-backed Series A team to CMO buyers.

The strongest case against me is the RTFKT exception — cultural inflection at a narrow timing window where Nike-style acquirers validate the category before the structural moat matures. The bull argument: agentic AI crossed a real threshold in 2025, Pavoot already has START Summit shipping live, and the ETH AI Center origin gives them genuine technical depth that the Vendelux team — pre-LLM-agent — cannot match without a rewrite. Maybe Pavoot is the agent-native event layer that gets bought by HubSpot for $80M before the market notices. The reason it doesn't sting enough: RTFKT had a culturally singular moment (digital fashion before NFTs collapsed) and a clear acquirer landscape. Pavoot has a crowded acquirer landscape that already builds these features in-house — Salesforce, HubSpot, Cvent, Bizzabo all ship "AI for events" today. The flip condition: if Pavoot lands two named Fortune 500 marketing orgs on multi-year contracts within six months, or pulls a third co-founder with a CMO/RevOps scar from a prior exit, I'd reopen the file. Until then, this isn't a Long Journey bet.
