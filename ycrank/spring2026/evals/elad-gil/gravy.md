# Gravy -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Gravy is a two-person consumer PFM app shipping a Mac binary into a category where Monarch raised a $75M round in May 2025 led by Forerunner/FPV/NewView — which tells you everything about the dimensional signature here. Gravy scores highest on founder shipping (a Mac app exists, Windows is in waitlist, per gravy.finance) and lowest on index-like distribution position (consumer PFM is the canonical winner-take-share market where Monarch, Copilot, Cleo, and Rocket Money already have wallet-share). That pairing — competent shipping into a category with no compounding distribution — is the profile of a polished indie product, not a defining company.

**The catalyst test fails the Anduril (2022) template.** Anduril worked because Ukraine produced a specific 24-month re-opening in a market peers had written off; the catalyst was exogenous and named. Gravy's claimed catalyst — MCP and Ollama crossing reliability thresholds in 2024-2025 — is a *capability* catalyst, not a *market* catalyst. The market Gravy is entering (consumer PFM, "$3.03B SAM" per Market Growth Reports) was not closed and then re-opened; it has been continuously contested since Mint sold to Intuit in 2009, and the most recent signal is Monarch consolidating share with a $95.5M cumulative raise (PitchBook). When I ran the Stripe (2010) entry, payments looked "done" *to peers* — but the API catalyst was specifically what made merchant-account incumbents obsolete. Gravy has no equivalent: Plaid already exists, OpenAI keys already exist, Ollama already exists, and Monarch can paste BYO-key support into a sprint.

**Lyft Series C is the analogous pass on the index dimension.** I passed on Lyft because being #2 in a winner-take-all market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and the dossier showed no credible path to share inversion. Gravy's positioning is structurally worse: it's not #2, it's #5 behind Monarch ($95.5M raised), Copilot Money (Series A), Cleo (Series C), and Rocket Money (owned by NYSE: RKT) — all of whom monetize the *exact* wallet Gravy targets, with cloud-trained data advantages the local-first architecture explicitly forecloses. The "local-first + BYO-LLM" wedge is a feature posture, not a distribution position. Stripe (2010) became an index on e-commerce because every entrant had to touch it; nobody has to touch Gravy to participate in consumer finance.

**Revenue velocity — the mechanism analysis.** In the Harvey (2023) template, real revenue velocity compounds because each enterprise pilot expands seats inside the same customer and references the next logo. Gravy's mechanism cannot compound that way: the dossier discloses zero revenue, no pricing page (per WebFetch on gravy.finance), and a demo showing "3 connected accounts and 3 automations" — which is the founder's own test data, not user behavior. Over 18 months, consumer PFM growth requires CAC-funded paid acquisition or a viral loop; Gravy has neither disclosed channel nor inherent virality (financial dashboards are private artifacts). My Tim Ferriss #863 line applies cleanly: "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken." Silence at pre-seed is the default, but the BYO-key onboarding friction means even latent growth would be visible as waitlist counts — and Gravy publishes none.

**Founder shipping — the counterfactual.** For Gravy's score on this axis to flip a tier, the dossier would need to name a prior shipped artifact for Ali Tabba beyond "Stealth Startup" on LinkedIn, or any public footprint at all for Max Church (the dossier explicitly notes "No public profile data surfaced"). The Perplexity (2022) entry required Aravind Srinivas demonstrably shipping new builds between conversations; Gravy has shipped *one* Mac app, which is necessary but not the Perplexity signature. A University of Warwick CEO and a CTO with no GitHub or LinkedIn surface is not a disqualifier at pre-seed, but it is also not what I write checks on.

**Single-miracle — the Color Genomics (2015) parallel.** When I co-founded Color, the architectural choice was to put workflow infrastructure on top of *commodity* sequencing — isolate the miracle to one place (the workflow layer) and let the underlying capability commoditize beneath you. Gravy attempts a similar move (workflow on top of commodity LLMs + Plaid) but the miracle stack is longer than it looks: (1) LLM accuracy reliable enough that users trust agentic actions on their bank accounts, (2) consumer willingness to provision OpenAI/Anthropic keys or install Ollama, (3) regulatory durability of storing Plaid OAuth tokens locally under GLBA without published SOC2, (4) Plaid maintaining consumer-tier API access on terms that work for a free/cheap consumer app. That's not one miracle gated by GTM execution; that's four independent miracles compounding small odds.

**My 36-month forecast: Gravy is sub-$500K ARR or dead by mid-2029, with the most likely live outcome being a quiet pivot to solopreneur bookkeeping or a small acqui-hire by a larger PFM (Monarch, Copilot) hunting for a local-first feature team.** The mechanism: consumer PFM CAC against Monarch's funded ad spend will exhaust a ~$500K pre-seed before Gravy compounds organic distribution, and the BYO-LLM-key onboarding friction makes mainstream conversion structurally harder than the incumbents Gravy is fighting.

**The strongest bull case against this forecast** is that a major PFM breach in 2026-2027 — Plaid token leak, Monarch data incident — creates a privacy-first refugee cohort, and Gravy's local-first architecture becomes the obvious safe harbor exactly when Tabba and Church have the only shipped product in that posture. This is the *AI Market Clarity* "hyperscaler-coupled-as-feature" inversion applied to data custody, and it would be a real catalyst. I don't buy it because privacy-driven consumer migrations historically route to brand-trusted incumbents (1Password, Apple), not unknown two-person teams; and because Monarch can publish a "local mode" announcement in 30 days. **The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Gravy crosses 10,000 paying subscribers within 6 months of launch, OR announces a named distribution partnership with Apple, 1Password, or a privacy-branded counterparty.** Absent either, this is a Lyft-style pass — a worse-positioned entrant in a market where the obvious bet is already taken.
