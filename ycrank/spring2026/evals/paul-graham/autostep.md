# Autostep -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Autostep is a solo-founder schlep play in incumbent territory, and that combination -- the highest-scoring axis being the unpleasantness of the work, the lowest being the absence of a second body to do it -- is the entire diagnosis. Aidan Pratt is one person in San Francisco trying to install a desktop-monitoring agent across enterprises while UiPath does $1.5B in FY2025 revenue selling roughly the same observation primitive bundled into a platform 10,000+ companies already buy. The work is real. The headcount to do it is one.

The schlep dimension is where Autostep registers strongest, and the comparison rung is **Coinbase** (2012) -- Armstrong's appetite for the bitcoin compliance grind was the asset, not the bitcoin. Autostep claims SOC 2 and HIPAA compliance on autostep.ai before any customer count is disclosed, which means Pratt has eaten paperwork before he has eaten growth -- a Coinbase-shaped instinct. Employee-monitoring software runs into works-council resistance, IT procurement cycles, and privacy review boards that pure-software YC applicants in this batch are unconsciously routing around, exactly the "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) pattern. Where Autostep diverges from the Coinbase rung is that Armstrong's schlep was load-bearing on a contrarian thesis (Bitcoin will matter); Autostep's schlep sits inside a consensus thesis (agents will matter) that UiPath, Celonis, Skan ($54M raised), and Mimica ($32.2M raised) are already funded to attack. Schlep without contrarianism is just hard work in a crowded room.

The solo-founder rung is where Autostep lands hardest, and the failure mechanism comes straight from "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010): solo founders lack the second brain that catches the mistake at 2am, and the YC outcomes I care about -- **Airbnb** (2009)'s trio, **Stripe** (2010)'s brothers, **Reddit** (2005)'s dorm roommates -- are all 2-3 person teams whose chemistry predated the company by years. Autostep lists Team Size: 1 on its YC page. The product requires org-wide desktop deployment, SOC 2 audit response, HIPAA controls, enterprise security review, and a founder-led sales motion through Calendly demo bookings. That is a four-person workload assigned to one person whose Twitter has 1,384 followers. Pratt has shipped GitHub side projects under @Adawg4 (openapi-autospec, Hitsnag, Pinpoint.city), so he is technical -- but technical does not subdivide into sales-engineer, security-lead, and CEO.

On founder resourcefulness, the mechanism that compounds over 18 months is whether the founder generates evidence other people will follow. **Dropbox** (2007)'s Houston shipped a demo video to Hacker News and the demand pulled the company forward; Autostep has shipped a YC Launch page, a Discord, and a marketing claim that "the smallest team we've worked with uncovered $100K+ in operational waste with 10 people" -- with no customer count attached to the claim. That is a sentence written by someone who has talked to customers but is not yet willing to name one. Pratt's biography is a credential ladder -- Kleiner Perkins, 8VC, Neo, Keel, Opto Investments -- which reads as the inverse of the cereal-box stunt. Credential-stackers can become animals, but the dossier shows no moment where Pratt did the thing nobody asked him to.

On organic founder pain, the counterfactual that would flip the tier is whether Pratt himself is the user. The "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) filter is: does the founder reach for this tool before anyone asks them to. Autostep is sold to ops leaders inside organizations large enough to have ad-hoc task-mining surveys -- a buyer Pratt has observed across "6 years working on automation-based products at seed-Series A startups" but has not been. He has seen the pain in others' workflows. Houston forgot his own USB stick. The distance between those two origins is the dimension's whole content.

On hacker genealogy, the single-anchor parallel is Aaron Swartz before **Reddit** (2005) -- the GitHub trail exists, the technical artifact exists, but the hacker-community recognition that turned Swartz into a deterministic signal is absent. Pratt's @Adawg4 repos (openapi-autospec, TweetWidget, Ramble) are real projects with unretrievable star counts; his Georgia Tech ML/CS degree is real. What is missing is the Young-Scientist-prize-shaped event -- the recognized hacker-tribal marker that **Stripe** (2010) carried in. Autostep clears the technical-by-credential-only bar; it does not clear the named-hacker-event bar.

My 36-month forecast on Autostep: acquihire by ServiceNow or UiPath at sub-$15M outcome, mirroring the UltimateSuite path into ServiceNow (2024), with the desktop-observation primitive absorbed into an incumbent platform and Pratt taking a director-level role. The mechanism is the signature itself -- the schlep is real enough to produce a product that someone wants to own, the solo configuration is thin enough that the company cannot independently reach the distribution incumbents already have. Schlep + solo = good acquihire, not a fund-returner.

The strongest bull case on Autostep invokes **Loopt** (2005) as the Known Exception: Sam Altman was effectively the force-of-personality bet before mobile location was real infrastructure, and I funded him because the animal test overrode the weak product-market timing. Could Pratt be a Loopt-shaped exception -- solo, credentialed, attacking a market that is about to break open as LLM agents cross the usability threshold he names in his Launch page thesis ("the bottleneck isn't tooling anymore, it's discovery")? The case stings because the "why now" is correct and the SOC 2/HIPAA work shows real grit. It loses on this: Altman's animal moment was visible inside YC's first batch interviews -- the dossier on Autostep produces no equivalent moment, only a credential ladder and a marketing claim with no named counterparty. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a single named enterprise customer with a disclosed dollar contract on the YC Launch page, or a co-founder addition with shipped open-source weight. Until then, Autostep is the clever-idea-soft-founder pattern from my reference voice -- and that is where I lose money. Pass.
