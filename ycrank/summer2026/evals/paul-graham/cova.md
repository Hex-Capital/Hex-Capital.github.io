# Cova -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Cova is a schlep without an animal — Ansh Tandon and Jordan Ibe picked Michigan Medicaid home-care billing, which is genuinely unpleasant work in the **Coinbase** (2012) sense, but the dossier shows no evidence either of them ever lived inside that pain or did anything stunt-like to fund their way in. Tandon's bio reads as Purdue CS, Apple AI, Tesla vehicle software — an engineer who scanned for a regulated market the way a consultant scans for a TAM slide, then registered a Michigan home care agency license. That inverted relationship — schlep chosen before pain experienced — is what makes Cova hard to place.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the HIGH rung on the schlep ladder I keep returning to, because Brian Armstrong's appetite for bitcoin compliance grind was the asset; the regulatory work became the moat once he stayed in it longer than smarter people would. Cova's chosen terrain — Medicaid Home Help reimbursement at $12.25/hour, Michigan-only licensing, billing across Medicaid/LTCI/private-pay — is genuinely the kind of work other SS26 founders are unconsciously routing around toward Sage Care and MochaCare's software-vendor wedge. Where Cova diverges from the **Coinbase** (2012) pattern is the origin: Armstrong wrote his own Android wallet because he cared about bitcoin; Tandon's anshtandon.com lists Purdue ML Club and BoilerBlockchain, not a family caregiver in Michigan or a Medicaid intake form he'd suffered through. The schlep is real. The pull toward it isn't visible.

The Pass Example I keep in my drawer is the polished AI-wrapper team with a glossy deck and "we'll hire engineers post-funding" — Cova doesn't read that way, but it fails the same root test from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012): nobody on this team can credibly say they built it for themselves first. Tandon's documented work is Apple AI infrastructure and Tesla vehicle software platforms. Ibe is not even confirmable in the dossier — a possible Cornell CS/Stats LinkedIn match, no Twitter, no GitHub. Neither founder has a Medicaid story, a caregiver-in-the-family story, an "I tried to enroll my grandmother and it took three weeks" story. The cova.care landing page positions toward "family caregivers in Michigan unaware they qualify" — that's a market observation, not a wound.

Cova's animal-resourcefulness signal is the dimension where absence hurts most — the dossier names no pre-funding stunt, no revenue, no caregiver count, no Product Hunt launch, no press, no Hacker News demo. They did obtain a Michigan home care agency license, which is real paperwork and non-trivial, and that's the only artifact in the file. Compounded over 18 months, the missing signal matters because the **Airbnb** (2009) lesson was that animals leave revenue tracks even when the idea looks dumb — Chesky cleared $40,000 selling cereal before I wrote the check. Cova has a phone line at (734) 707-7330 and an email. That's the entirety of the trace.

The counterfactual on organic-pain fit: what would have to be true for Cova to clear a tier? One of two founders would need a documented caregiver story — a parent in Michigan Home Help, a sibling burned out doing unpaid care, a stint working agency intake. None of that is in the dossier and absence at pre-seed is usually absence, not omission. The Tandon biography is unusually well-documented (personal site, Apple, Tesla, Medium handle) which makes the silence on healthcare motivation louder, not quieter.

On hacker genealogy, the **Stripe** (2010) anchor is unforgiving — Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp at sixteen before the Collisons hacked commerce. Tandon has Purdue CS with a math minor, Apple AI, Tesla, and BoilerBlockchain membership, but no GitHub repos surfaced and no shipped open-source artifact is named. That's credentialed-technical, not hacker-trail technical. It's the difference between "could ship" and "has shipped something strangers used."

Co-founder configuration is the dimension I leave at default. Cova has two founders, which clears the solo red flag from "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010), but the dossier has no public data on whether Tandon and Ibe roomed together, worked together, or met at the YC application weekend. The **Reddit** (2005) dorm-roommate pattern and the Collison-brothers pattern both require evidence I don't have.

My 36-month forecast for Cova: the Michigan-only unit economics on $12.25/hour Medicaid reimbursement do not support paying caregivers "the highest base rate in the state" at scale, so by mid-2029 Cova has either pivoted to selling its AI-agent stack to other licensed agencies — becoming Sage Care or MochaCare's direct competitor — or quietly wound down at sub-$500K of operating revenue with one Medicaid audit finding in the file. The mechanism is the high/low signature: a schlep correctly identified by founders who did not personally suffer it tends to get abandoned the first time the schlep bites, because there was never an internal reason to absorb the pain.

The strongest bull case against my forecast: this is **Coinbase** (2012) before anyone understood compliance was the moat — Tandon's Apple/Tesla AI background is genuinely unusual for a Michigan agency operator, the LLM reliability inflection of 2024-2025 is real, and a Michigan license is a regulatory asset competitors can't paper-trade around. Armstrong, after all, didn't have a personal "I was unbanked" story either; he had appetite for the grind. Maybe Tandon and Ibe have the same appetite and the dossier just doesn't capture it. The dossier evidence that wins against this objection: Armstrong shipped a working Android Bitcoin wallet to overturn his rejection — a pre-funding artifact aimed at the exact regulatory surface he wanted to attack. Cova has shipped a landing page and a phone number. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Cova reaches 50 active Michigan Home Help cases with documented Medicaid claim acceptance rate above 90% within twelve months, demonstrating the AI-agent billing actually clears the schlep rather than describing it. Until then, pass.
