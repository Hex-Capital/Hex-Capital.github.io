# Hexa -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Hexa is a clean-cap-table quote-automation SaaS with one named CEO and a problem the founder seems to have discovered in a LinkedIn essay rather than on a shop floor -- that high/low pairing is the whole signature. Ishaan Makkar quotes the "54% of manufacturers still on spreadsheets" stat the way someone who read it does, not the way someone who lived inside Epicor for five years does, and Hexa's $9K MRR across three customers in six weeks is real motion but it is motion inside the most-crowded YC category of Spring 2026.

The Anduril-Luckey rung is what founder-lived-experience looks like fully present -- Palmer had shipped Oculus to scale from his parents' garage before he ever drew a defense schematic, so the second product was a continuation of a biography, not a market scan. Hexa's founder dossier has the inverse shape: Ishaan Makkar's only public artifact is a thesis-shaped LinkedIn post, Auri Nayak is a hackathon line-item as "Aurideep Nayak, Hexa YC X26," and nowhere in the dossier does anyone surface a prior employer in manufacturing, distribution, an ERP implementation shop, or an oil-and-gas back office. The HandUp exception that lets me write checks into modest-ceiling companies requires the founder's biography to mirror the problem -- nothing in Hexa's dossier shows me Ishaan ever sat in a sales coordinator's chair at a $40M family-owned distributor printing PDFs off Outlook at 7am. The absence is the signal; I am not penalizing him for a thin LinkedIn, I am noting that the pitch reads memo-discovered.

Didero closed a $30M Series A on February 12, 2026, co-led by Chemistry and Headline with M12 -- "manufacturers and distributors who need to source raw materials," same ICP, 37x the capital -- and Traza pulled $2.1M for the same quoting-workflow wedge. Hexa is sitting in exactly the round shape I deselect on: agentic procurement is the consensus 2026 YC AI-agent category, and the moment six funds are circling a wedge before anyone has crossed $50K MRR, the price has already left the building. Crusoe in 2018 worked because three credible VCs had *passed* on aesthetic grounds before I leaned in; Hexa's dossier shows the opposite -- competitors are stacking capital, SupplyChainBrain is publishing "76% of supply chain professionals" optimism pieces, and the category has a TechCrunch narrative. That is not magically weird; that is consensus dressed in an unsexy vertical.

Hexa's one structural credit: Ishaan Makkar is labeled Founder/CEO and Auri Nayak is labeled Founder, which clears the 50/50 ambiguity that detonated HQ Trivia's Yusupov-Kroll partnership and the GameCrush activist-investor mess I still carry as a scar. I cannot find a documented operating agreement or vesting protocol in the dossier, so the default penalty doesn't fully lift, but the title delineation alone puts Hexa above the silent-governance default rather than below it. This is the dimension Hexa scores best on, and it is faint praise -- a non-blow-up on governance is table stakes, not a thesis.

Capital-intensity-as-moat compounds over 18 months only when the domain itself resists software-only entrants -- defense procurement, energy arbitrage, regulated health -- and Hexa's product is an AI overlay on SAP, NetSuite, Dynamics, Epicor, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, and Pipedrive. The customer industry is unsexy (industrial distributors, family-owned manufacturers), but Hexa itself is pure SaaS sitting on top of those ERPs, which means the moat has to come from workflow data and integration breadth, not from any physical or regulatory asset Hexa is uniquely positioned to hold. Eighteen months from now either Hexa has aggregated enough catalog and pricing graph data to make ripping it out painful, or NetSuite ships its own native agent and the integration list becomes a substitution map for whichever ERP the customer already pays.

For Dim 5, the counterfactual is simple: what would have to be true for me to score Hexa as physically verifiable? I would need to be able to forward an RFQ email to a Hexa address and watch a priced quote come back in my inbox within minutes -- a tactile artifact a normal human could trigger in 48 hours. The hexaagents.com site exposes a Calendly demo-booking flow and no self-serve trial; there is no Product Hunt listing, no Chrome extension, no public sandbox. Hexa exists at the founder-testimony layer of verifiability -- I have to take their word that the agent works, the way I do not have to take Flock's word that the camera at the Four Seasons existed.

My 36-month forecast for Hexa: acqui-hired by an ERP-adjacent buyer (most likely Epicor, Infor, or a procurement-suite incumbent like Coupa) for under $20M while ARR sits below $750K, or dead with founders moved to FAANG roles -- driven by Didero's $30M war chest outspending Hexa on the same ICP before Hexa's $9K MRR compounds into customer-concentration-resistant revenue. The mechanism is straightforward: when a category gets a Series A leader at 37x your capital before you have your tenth customer, the wedge closes around you, and "OS for manufacturers and distributors" is too broad a surface to hold against a focused procurement-only competitor with M12 behind it.

The strongest bull case against my pass is that $9K MRR in 6 weeks from manufacturers -- a buyer class famous for 9-month sales cycles -- is genuinely abnormal velocity, and the HQ Trivia exception says tactile product-led pull can override my other heuristics when the signal is strong enough. Three paying industrial customers in six weeks could be the early signature of a wedge that compounds faster than Didero's enterprise sales motion can deploy that $30M. The dossier evidence that wins the argument anyway: HQ Trivia's exception was triggered by a product I could *literally play and feel the viral pull of* before the metric existed -- Hexa offers me no such tactile artifact, only a Calendly link, and the MRR is too thin to substitute for the felt pull. The verifiable condition that would flip me: if Hexa reaches $75K MRR across 15+ named industrial distributors by Q4 2026 without a priced round above $15M post, and Ishaan publishes a teardown of a real customer's pre-Hexa workflow that reads like he sat in that chair, I revisit.
