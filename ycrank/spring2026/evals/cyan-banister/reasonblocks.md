# ReasonBlocks -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

ReasonBlocks is two young engineers with clean titles -- Sajeev Magesh as CEO, Rohan Vij as CTO, eleven years of friendship since 2nd grade -- building precisely the company the Spring 2026 YC consensus says to build: a runtime layer for AI agents, sitting in the same lane as Mem0's $24M Series A and LangChain's $125M Series B. The structural signature is high governance clarity, near-zero "magically weird." That pairing names what ReasonBlocks actually is in my book: a tidy, credentialed, thesis-orthogonal bet in the most-crowded sub-market of the most-consensus YC category, with no biographical wedge that would make me lean in.

The Anduril rung -- Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before pivoting into a defense category every VC had moral objections to -- is the lived-experience high water mark, and the mechanism is that the founder owned the problem before the company existed. ReasonBlocks diverges on exactly that mechanism: Magesh is a Stanford CS graduate with a Nature Sustainable Agriculture ML paper and a USAMO qualification, Vij is CMU Information Systems with molecular-dynamics research and an ENGIE AI stint, and neither has shipped a production agent-infrastructure system to scale. Their first contact with production-agent token waste is analytical, not autobiographical. I read the Magesh/Vij dossier and I see two strong students; I do not see anyone who has been paged at 3am because a LangChain run ate $40K of tokens in a runaway loop. That is the Pass Example pattern almost verbatim -- ex-credentialed engineers identifying a problem from the outside.

Mem0 is where ReasonBlocks's lowest dimension fails most loudly: persistent memory layer, $24M Series A in October 2025 led by Basis Set, 41K GitHub stars, 14M downloads, already claiming 72% token reduction via its memory SDK. The "magically weird" failure mechanism in the Pass Example was six top funds chasing a consensus AI-agent darling at a price that pre-bakes the outcome. ReasonBlocks shows that signature with the surrounding wallpaper: Mem0 capitalized, LangSmith capitalized, Langfuse open-source, AgentOps shipping, BentoLabs adjacent, and the YC pitch ("plugs into existing agent stacks in minutes") is the same pitch every other runtime middleware company runs. The agent-infrastructure layer attracted ~9% of capital across 18 deals 2022-2025 -- that is a saturated row, not a hidden one. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game I'd be playing here is "pick the runtime-correction winner from a six-way bake-off priced to perfection."

ReasonBlocks's strongest dimension is governance, and the eighteen-month mechanism is what compounds: an eleven-year friendship pre-dating any equity conversation usually buys cohesion through the first existential pivot, the kind of cohesion the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll partnership famously could not produce. Magesh holds the CEO title and Vij the CTO title cleanly per the YC page; there is no co-CEO ambiguity, no third co-founder dilution, and the 2nd-grade origin means the tiebreaker question has been answered informally for a decade. The gap is that no operating agreement, vesting protocol, or documented conflict-resolution mechanism is disclosed publicly, which is normal at pre-seed but means the governance score gets the upside of the friendship without the documented backstop.

The capital-intensity counterfactual: ReasonBlocks would move a tier on this dimension only if the "private reasoning library that grows with every run" turned out to be a data-accumulation moat that demanded real infrastructure spend -- GPU inventory, custom inference hardware, regulated-vertical certifications. What the dossier actually shows is a SaaS middleware that "plugs into existing agent stacks in minutes," self-reports a 42% lift on SWE-Bench Pro, and lists no hardware, no regulated vertical, no oilfield. The Vigil Labs and Superpower checks I wrote in 2024-2025 are the lane I fund; ReasonBlocks is structurally absent from it.

ReasonBlocks's physical-world dimension is the Pass Example test applied directly: reasonblocks.com returned only a page title to WebFetch on 2026-05-27, no public app, no GitHub repo for the product, no Product Hunt launch, no Chrome extension, and Vij's personal GitHub pins LoconomicFulcrum, Memes4Discord, and a remote_keylogger repo with 1 star. I cannot install the artifact, ride it, scan a parking lot for it, or photograph it. Mem0 at least has 14M downloads and 41K stars I can verify in the wild; ReasonBlocks is founder testimony plus an unreproduced benchmark. The Flock Safety / Niantic / HQ Trivia method does not have a surface to grip on here.

Forecast, 36 months: ReasonBlocks is acqui-hired by LangChain, Mem0, or a foundation-model provider for sub-$15M in talent value, or sunsets quietly after failing to clear $1M ARR as OpenAI and Anthropic ship native prompt caching and mid-run reasoning correction into their default API surface. The mechanism is the foundation-model substitution risk the dossier itself names: a runtime layer that intervenes mid-run is a feature, not a company, in a world where Anthropic and OpenAI already control the inference loop and Mem0 already claims 72% token reduction in the adjacent memory primitive.

The strongest bull case leans on the DeepMind exception -- Hassabis was pedigreed, not lived, and the polymath cross-domain compounding overrode my outsider-founder bias. Magesh has Nature-published ML research plus USAMO; Vij has molecular dynamics plus energy-sector AI plus IoT; the agentic AI market is genuinely going from $7.84B to $52.62B by 2030, and a 52% token reduction on SWE-Bench Pro is a real number if it holds. The bear answer is that the DeepMind exception required Hassabis to have actually shipped a commercial chess game, published peer-reviewed neuroscience, AND designed games at scale -- three compounding domains with shipped artifacts. ReasonBlocks has a Nature paper and an ENGIE internship, which is strong-student polymath, not Hassabis polymath. The verifiable flip condition: a signed enterprise contract with a named Fortune-500 customer paying for production runtime usage AND an independent third-party reproduction of the 52% token-reduction benchmark on a non-SWE-Bench workload. Absent both, I let this batch pass.
