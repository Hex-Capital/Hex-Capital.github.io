# KelAI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

KelAI is Jeremie Cohen's 8-year WorldQuant career continuing under a new logo, wrapped in agentic orchestration -- a domain-expert productivity layer for a workflow Cohen already personally ran, dressed in language about "compounding intelligence" but pointed at a world-state where hedge funds get faster, not at a world-state that does not currently exist. Pre-seed YC, three people, software, no proprietary stack disclosed, competing for the same buy-side procurement budget that **Rogo** (where I led the $18.5M Series A at ~$350M valuation, March 2025) already targets one workflow over. The dimensional signature is exactly the **sales-call summarizer pass example** from my own framework -- expert continuity, SaaS economics, incremental gain -- with the cosmetic difference that the seat is a quant PM seat instead of an AE seat.

KelAI's consequence-at-maximum-success is the load-bearing question and it does not survive contact with my **OpenAI** (2019) rung -- where the success-state was a civilizational redirection that justified writing $50M into a no-revenue entity. KelAI's success-state, fully realized, is that systematic equity desks at Millennium, Citadel, and a long tail of $1-5B funds run 5-10x more research cycles per analyst and capture incremental alpha faster than the competing desk down the street. That is real money for the LPs of those funds. It is not the $15T labor displacement frame I built the AI portfolio around (Fortune 2026-03-06). No incumbent regime collapses -- Bloomberg keeps its terminal, S&P keeps Kensho, the prime brokers keep clearing, and the hedge-fund industry as an institution is not redrawn; it just gets a more efficient research stack. The dossier names no labor-cost figure being displaced, no civilizational variable moving, no category disappearing.

The **sales-call summarizer** pass pattern fails because the founder profile is career-continuity rather than expert-defiance, and that exact failure mode is stamped on KelAI's dossier: Cohen's path reads École Polytechnique → SolveBio → Dassault → Millennium ML lead (2017-2018) → WorldQuant PM (2018-present) → KelAI. This is the linear domain-expert resume my TED Talk warned about -- the McKinsey-1986-cellphone-forecast pattern of experts extrapolating their past forward. KelAI's founding insight is not a contradiction of quant orthodoxy; it is the application of agentic LLMs to the exact workflow Cohen has been paid to execute for nearly a decade. There is no published controversial position, no public thesis Cohen has staked against his field, no "instigator" moment of the kind that drew me to the Levine/Finn pair at **Physical Intelligence** (2024).

On technical and capital risk -- the dimension where I tolerate 70-80% failure priors -- KelAI compounds in the wrong direction over 18 months. The roadmap requires zero physics miracles, zero biology miracles, zero hardware miracles; it requires agent orchestration, retrieval, and backtesting infrastructure that **AlphaSense** ($1.4B raised, ~$500M ARR), **Hebbia** ($159M raised), and **Rogo** can each ship as a feature inside their existing distribution to the same buyers. KelAI's "October 2025 signals running in production" claim is unverified by any third-party Sharpe number, drawdown profile, or named fund-counterparty in the dossier, which means by month 18 KelAI is either differentiated by a track record it has not yet shown, or it is a thinner version of what the well-capitalized incumbents bolt onto their existing seats.

KelAI's learning-rate score would only flip a tier if the dossier surfaced a documented pivot -- a prior company Cohen sold, a public thesis he reversed, a moment where he stopped doing quant and then came back to it from a contradicting angle. None of that exists in the public record: no prior exit, no GitHub footprint, no X account, no published essay reversing a position. The **Slash** (2024, $100M Series C) Known Exception held because the founder pivot was loud, documented, and Kanye-anchored; absent that signal here, KelAI does not earn the override that lets me ignore the consequence-magnitude prior.

Required-capital profile -- comparing KelAI to my **Genesis AI** (2025, $105M co-led seed) anchor -- is the cleanest tell that I am the wrong investor on this cap table. Genesis required $105M at seed because foundation-model training for robots is capital-deep and no Tier-1 generalist would write that check on technical risk alone. KelAI requires the standard YC pre-seed $500K, then a $3-8M seed extension, to reach pilot deployments at a handful of funds -- a check size where I am over-resourced and any fintech-vertical seed fund is the correct lead. The dossier offers no roadmap item that requires $50M+ to clear a science gate; the moat ambition is per-customer data lock-in, which is a software flywheel, not a capital flywheel.

My 36-month forecast: KelAI is acquired between 2027 and 2029 by AlphaSense, S&P Global (Kensho), or a tier-2 prime broker for a $25-75M tuck-in price, reaching $1-4M ARR with two to six named hedge-fund logos before the acquisition; the alternative path is quiet shutdown when the seed extension does not clear because **Rogo** or AlphaSense has shipped the systematic-research SKU first. This forecast follows directly from the signature -- domain-expert founder plus software-only stack plus incremental consequence equals a Rogo-shaped outcome where Rogo is the comparable that already exists at $350M and KelAI is the second mover into the same buyer.

The strongest bull case against my pass is that Cohen's WorldQuant-plus-Millennium pedigree is exactly the buy-side trust signal that **AlphaSense** and **Rogo** cannot synthesize -- hedge-fund procurement is relationship-gated, the YC RFS Spring 2026 explicitly named "AI-Native Hedge Funds" as a wanted shape, and the "compounding intelligence" loop could become per-customer data lock-in that compounds into a $100M+ ARR private business by 2030. That case stings because it mirrors how I justified backing **Glean** despite Arvind Jain's paradigmatic domain-expert profile. It loses on the consequence axis: even fully realized, KelAI is a Rogo-class outcome, and I funded Rogo a year earlier in the adjacent workflow, which means writing KelAI's pre-seed is portfolio overlap on a thesis I already expressed -- not the contrarian science bet my fund exists to make. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: KelAI publishes third-party-audited live-trading results showing agent-generated signals at Sharpe > 1.5 net on $100M+ of real capital across two or more tier-1 funds within 12 months -- a falsifiable miracle worthy of the framework, which the current dossier does not even claim.
