# Clawvisor -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Eric Levine sold Berbix to Socure for $70M in 2023 and ran Trust & Safety engineering at Airbnb for five years -- that founder profile is the strongest signal in the Clawvisor dossier, and the weakest is the conspicuous silence on any revenue or usage curve in an AI security category where Composio is already at "$1M+ ARR, 200+ paying customers" (SiliconANGLE, Jul 2025). Clawvisor is the analytical inverse of the **Harvey** (2023) template: a domain-credentialed shipper attacking a real catalyst-driven window with no disclosed velocity yet, while two well-funded adjacent players bundle his wedge into their roadmaps.

On founder execution rhythm, the **Perplexity** (2022) template is the load-bearing comparison -- Aravind Srinivas pinged me on LinkedIn and "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and what mattered was prior independent shipping, not pedigree. Clawvisor's Eric Levine clears that bar in a different shape: Berbix (YC S18) was an identity-verification product he shipped to a $70M Socure acquisition in 2023, and Trust & Safety at Airbnb 2012-2017 is exactly the operating muscle relevant to per-request authorization enforcement. Where Clawvisor diverges from the Perplexity rung is iteration *cadence visibility* -- the dossier shows 14+ adapters live and a GitHub repo that moved from 186 to 202 stars, but nothing about weekly artifact velocity, and 419 X followers on @ericlevine is not the public shipping signal Aravind broadcast.

The **Lyft** Series C pass anchors the bottom rung on revenue velocity, but the failure mechanism there was structural (#2 in a winner-take-all market) -- the more relevant failure mode for Clawvisor is the one I keep flagging in *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025): an AI-cycle company without a disclosed growth curve. Clawvisor's dossier lists "free trial, no credit card required" with no public pricing, no customer logos, no ARR figure, no pilot count -- in a category where Composio openly discloses 200+ paying customers and $1M+ ARR, Clawvisor's silence on the same metrics is itself the signal. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" applies, with the standard hedge that pre-seed YC companies legitimately have no curve yet -- but adjacent funded competitors *do*, which means the curve comparison is live, not hypothetical.

On market re-opening, the catalyst is real but unclaimed by Clawvisor itself. *Mechanism*: 2024-2026 agent deployment with write-access to Gmail/Slack/Drive created a per-request scoping problem that OAuth-era Okta/Auth0 don't solve, and Arcade.dev's $12M seed (Mar 2025) and Composio's $25M Series A (Jul 2025) are the market evidence that capital agrees. Over 18 months this dimension compounds *for the category* -- the question is whether Clawvisor's positioning as a separate "authorization gateway" rather than a bundled integration layer survives the next round of category consolidation. The catalyst is Anduril-shaped (specific 24-month window) but Clawvisor isn't sitting in it alone the way Anduril sat in defense-post-Ukraine.

On index-like distribution, the counterfactual matters. For Clawvisor to tier up here, the market would have to bifurcate -- agent *integration* (Composio, Arcade.dev) becomes one purchase, agent *authorization* (Clawvisor) becomes a separate compliance-driven purchase, the way Okta and Workday are separate buys even though they overlap on identity. The dossier evidence pointing toward that bifurcation is thin: 14+ adapters is below Composio's 500+, and the open-source repo at 202 stars is not yet a developer-channel index. The dossier evidence pointing *against* it is loud: Arcade.dev explicitly ships "built-in auth," meaning the bundling has already happened in the dominant competitor's positioning.

On single-miracle architecture, Clawvisor passes cleanly on the **Color Genomics** (2015) parallel -- workflow infrastructure on top of a hard layer (LLM agents) without taking science risk on the agents themselves. The miracle Clawvisor needs is GTM/market-structure: that enterprise security buyers purchase agent authorization separately from agent integration, rather than accepting whatever Composio/Arcade.dev bundle. That is one miracle, not three, which is why this dimension doesn't sink the deal -- but it is a *market-structure* miracle rather than a *technical* one, which historically has lower base rates than my Decagon/Abridge templates where the miracle was LLM accuracy at a workflow task.

Forecast: in 36 months Clawvisor is either acqui-hired by Composio or Arcade.dev for sub-$30M as a credentialing/policy feature absorption, or it stabilizes at $1-3M ARR as a niche gateway for security-conscious enterprises (financial services, healthcare) that refuse to put credentials into a general integration layer -- it does not become the index on agent authorization, because the index already has $1M+ ARR and is called Composio.

The bull case that stings: Levine is the rare solo founder who has already run an identity-vertical company to exit, and his Trust & Safety background means he understands the enterprise security buyer in a way that Arcade.dev's and Composio's developer-tools founders likely do not -- enterprise security procurement is *structurally* separate from developer tooling procurement (Okta vs. Auth0 vs. Vault), and that procurement separation is the wedge that lets Clawvisor be a real company even after Composio bundles "auth." The Decagon (2023) parallel is sharper than I want to admit: customer support also "had" incumbents (Zendesk, Intercom) before the LLM-era workflow layer carved out a separate buy. The dismantle: Decagon disclosed revenue velocity early and publicly, and Clawvisor's dossier discloses zero. The verifiable condition that flips me to Invest: Clawvisor publishes $250K+ ARR with three named enterprise logos (especially financial-services or healthcare buyers separating their authz purchase from their integration purchase) within 90 days -- at which point I am buying the Levine-shipping-into-a-real-catalyst story at consensus pricing without further deliberation.
