# Sherpa -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Sherpa is the kind of deal where two ex-Lindy operators -- Ethan Kinnan and Norbu Sonam -- have authentic founder-market fit for a problem (Norbu literally "tripled paywall conversion" running 50+ growth experiments at Lindy) but are pointing that fit at the most consensus AI-agent category of the Spring 2026 batch. The signature is high on founder-as-operator, low on magical weirdness and capital-intensity, which puts Sherpa squarely in the lane I've called "competent polymath-adjacent operators in a category a polymath generalist could rebuild in a quarter." I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Sherpa is playing is the one everyone else is also playing this batch.

Norbu Sonam's biography is the strongest thing in this dossier and worth sitting with. The Niantic-Hanke template is a founder who spent a decade in location data via Keyhole and Google Earth before Pokémon Go; Sherpa's analogue is a CTO who shipped Copilot image generation at Microsoft and then ran the exact experimentation loop at Lindy that Sherpa now automates. The mechanism is the same -- founder builds the product they wished existed at the prior job. Where Sherpa diverges from the Hanke rung is duration and weight: one Lindy stint and one Microsoft stint is real, but it isn't a ten-year arc of becoming *of* the place. Ethan's forward-deployed engineering role at Lindy reinforces it, but the lived-experience signal here is the upper end of "competent operator who automated their own prior job," not the lower end of "this human has been pointed at this problem their whole life."

The consensus-AI-agent darling I passed on -- two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, deck opens with TAM -- failed on the *Magically Weird* axis because six top funds were chasing a model-thin wrapper everyone could see. Sherpa is structurally adjacent to that failure mode. The dossier names VWO shipping GPT-based copy generation in 2025, Kameleoon marketing "AI for A/B Testing" in 2026, and Optimizely adding AI variant generation -- three distribution-advantaged incumbents already shipping the load-bearing capability, plus a $100M Everstone roll-up of Wingify+AB Tasty in Jan 2026. That is not "before it becomes consensus." That is during consensus, with the moat (LLM-generated on-brand variants) explicitly commoditizing in the dossier's own Risk section. No credible investor has visibly passed on aesthetic grounds because nothing here is aesthetically transgressive.

Sherpa's governance is the cleanest dimension in the dossier and the inverse of the HQ Trivia post-mortem. Ethan Kinnan is named CEO, Norbu Sonam is named CTO, the roles trace cleanly to their Lindy histories (partnerships/FDE vs. growth engineering), and there is no 50/50 split language anywhere. Equity split isn't disclosed, but the title delineation and the complementary skill stacks read as the conversation having happened. Not a worry.

The Capital-Intensive lane compounds the wrong direction for Sherpa over 18 months. A JS snippet doing CRO on SMB websites has neither hardware moat, regulatory moat, nor energy-arbitrage compounding -- the things that turned Crusoe from "burning flares to mine Bitcoin" into a Stargate anchor. Every additional month of LLM commoditization lowers the floor for a generative-variant wrapper, while the floor for a defense or energy or fertility play *rises* with regulatory accumulation. Sherpa's "7,338,896+ sessions" dataset is the only compounding asset claimed, and it isn't large enough relative to VWO or Optimizely's incumbent traffic to be a real wedge yet.

Sherpa's free 30-second website audit is genuinely installable and the named Lindy testimonial (+30% conversion, 40 experiments in 30 days) is a tactile artifact I could observe -- closer to the Niantic-Ingress pattern than to a pure dev-tools API. Counterfactual: if Sherpa shipped a public Chrome extension that showed me, on any site I visited, what variant it would generate and why, that artifact would move this dimension a full tier and might rescue the overall verdict. As shipped, the snippet is observable but not viral in the HQ-Trivia sense, so the Known Exception that overrode governance there does not extend here.

My 36-month forecast: Sherpa gets acqui-hired by HubSpot, Webflow, or Vercel in the $25-75M range as an AI-CRO feature tuck-in, or absorbed by the Everstone Wingify/AB Tasty platform once the incumbent shipping cycle catches up; not zero, not a fund-returner. The mechanism producing that range is the founder quality (Norbu's Microsoft/Lindy stack makes them an attractive acqui-hire) combined with the commoditization curve (the moat decays faster than ARR compounds), which is exactly what the high-founder/low-weirdness signature predicts.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass is the HandUp override: I have written checks into commercially modest deals because the founder mirrored my own trajectory, so why penalize Norbu and Ethan for picking a category whose ceiling is bounded? The dismantling is that HandUp's override was *personal mission alignment with homelessness*, not "ex-employee automating a prior job," and Sherpa's founders pattern-match to the latter, which is a YC archetype, not a Cyan archetype. The bull-case-on-execution is real -- ex-Lindy operators ship faster than VWO's product org -- but the dossier already names VWO shipping GPT copy gen in 2025, so the race is on and Sherpa is the underdog in distribution. One verifiable condition would flip me: a signed enterprise logo outside the Lindy/Insforge/Gauge YC-adjacent network -- a non-network mid-market customer at $50K+ ACV -- would prove the wedge generalizes and earn a second look.
