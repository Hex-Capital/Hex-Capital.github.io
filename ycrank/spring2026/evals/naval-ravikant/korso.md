# Korso -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Korso is three engineers selling AI agents into SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, Plex, and SYSPRO -- a leverage architecture that requires permission from the seven incumbent ERPs whose integration list is literally Korso's product surface. Martin Pan's prior tour as a General Motors automations engineer is the strongest single signal in the dossier, and the weakest thing about Korso is the channel that signal has to travel through. That pairing -- real-but-narrow specific knowledge routed into a permissioned enterprise-sales motion -- is the analytical shape of the company.

Twitter (2007) is the HIGH rung of leverage architecture because anyone could post, the platform charged nothing, and the marginal cost of replication was zero -- media as protocol, no gatekeeper between founder and user. Korso is the structural inverse: korsoai.com's value proposition is integration depth with SAP, NetSuite, Dynamics 365, Sage, SYSPRO, Plex, and Acumatica, which means Korso's distribution is gated by IT-security reviews, implementation partners, and the channel goodwill of seven incumbents that AWS Bedrock AgentCore and SAP's own native agents are already encroaching on (aws.amazon.com 2025-2026 per the dossier). This is capital-and-labor leverage requiring permission at every step, the exact structure nav.al/product-media was written to contrast against. The Anchorage exception does not rescue Korso -- Anchorage's permission-as-moat was a Treasury OCC charter, not an SAP partner-program slot.

The "ex-McKinsey / ex-FAANG PM" red flag pattern fails because pedigree-as-thesis substitutes credential for specific knowledge -- the founder discovered the problem in a deck, not as a user. Korso does not fully exhibit this failure mode: Martin Pan was an automations engineer at GM, which is the closest thing in the dossier to lived ownership of the RFQ/PO/supplier-coordination pain Korso targets, and Daichi Hiraoka plus Pan both built on Penn Electric Racing, which is real shipped hardware. But Korso is not Atwood-and-Spolsky-at-Stack-Overflow either -- there is no public GitHub from any of the three co-founders, no domain writing, no open-source artifact that predates the priced round. Korso sits one notch above the red flag and several notches below the specific-knowledge bar that justified the **Perplexity** (2022) override.

The network-effects mechanism over 18 months: Korso's value-add does not compound across customers the way **Uber** (2010) did. Customer N's RFQ workflows on NetSuite do not make customer N-1's SAP deployment more valuable; each enterprise install is an economically independent island with bespoke integration debt. Even if Korso accumulates exception-handling data, that data is per-tenant and contractually walled. The 89% close rate on korsoai.com's sample dashboard is decorative -- "Northbridge Metals" and "Blue Harbor Components" are fictional, and Korso has not disclosed a single real logo. Without cross-customer compounding, the asymmetric upside math collapses from 10,000x to roughly the multiple of whatever ARR ceiling enterprise mid-market ERP layers earn -- a $50-300M outcome at best, not the geometry I write angel checks against.

For the big-market criterion to flip a tier on Korso, the dossier would need to show at least two adjacent pivot vectors off the manufacturing-ERP wedge -- supplier-side marketplace, agent-orchestration developer platform sold horizontally, or a creator/operator tool that escapes the vertical. None are present. The Precedence Research $294B AI-agent TAM is the wrong TAM for Korso; the relevant TAM is mid-market manufacturers running one of seven ERPs and willing to install an agent layer on top, which is where Lumari (also YC, $500K raised) and Turian ($3.78M, Berlin) are already swinging. "Niche markets don't work because the first idea never works" -- Korso's first idea has no documented room to maneuver.

Founder-trio integrity reads clean on Korso -- no contested exits, no self-promotion about ethics, no 50/50 unresolved-CEO red flag visible in the LinkedIn profiles -- but the dossier offers no positive 10-year-partner signal either. No public writing, no X presence on any of Daichi Hiraoka, Alex Liu, or Martin Pan, no organic community around the work. Default scoring applies. Korso did not earn a deduction here; Korso did not earn a premium either.

Forecast, 36 months out: Korso is either acqui-hired by a NetSuite or SAP implementation partner (Accenture, Deloitte, or a tier-two systems integrator) for under $40M, or it is operating at sub-$1.5M ARR in 2029 while Lumari and Turian split the same enterprise pipeline, or it has pivoted into a single-ERP wedge (likely NetSuite-only, given mid-market gravity). The mechanism is straightforward: permissioned-channel businesses with a three-engineer team cannot outrun ERP-incumbent native agent rollouts and a same-batch YC competitor at the speed required to escape the gravity well of enterprise sales cycles.

The bull case that should sting: **Perplexity** (2022) is the precedent for waiving criterion #2 (network effects) when specific knowledge and category timing both clear at top quartile, and one could argue Martin Pan's GM operator background plus the 2024-2026 agent-tool-use maturity window is exactly that configuration. Manufacturing is genuinely huge, the pain is real, and the convergence of Lumari, Turian, Smartbase, and qomplement validates the wedge rather than crowding it out. The dismantling: Perplexity's override required permissionless API distribution (criterion #1 at top quartile), and Korso fails criterion #1 at the floor -- this is the leverage architecture the Almanack was written against, not a borderline case. Aravind Srinivas's Berkeley retrieval PhD was non-replicable specific knowledge; Martin Pan's GM tour is replicable by any of the next five YC batches' manufacturing-savvy founders. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Korso ships an open-source agent-orchestration framework with documented organic GitHub adoption (1,000+ stars unprompted) AND announces a named Fortune 500 manufacturer running a multi-week production workflow in audit-trail logs by Q4 2026. Until then, pass.
