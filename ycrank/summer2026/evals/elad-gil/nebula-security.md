# Nebula Security -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Eten Zou has shipped published CVE writeups (CVE-2026-6307 Chrome V8 sandbox escape, CVE-2026-5865 V8 RCE, CVE-2026-23274 Linux kernel at a $10,500 bounty), which is the *only* dimension where Nebula Security clears my bar — and Nebula Security's revenue disclosure is the string "$0K+ in bug bounties earned" on its own homepage, which is the dimension where Nebula Security fails hardest. The signature is a two-person offensive-research shop with real technical artifacts and zero commercial curve, entering a category where XBOW already raised $237M on the same "AI does the offensive work" thesis. That pairing is what Nebula Security is; the aggregate score just follows from it.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for Nebula Security on founder-shipping: Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), which is exactly the "artifact between meetings" signal I underwrite on. Nebula Security's CVE-2026-6307 Longinus writeup and the $10,500 Linux kernel bounty are the shipping-artifact equivalent for offensive research — that's not slideware. Where Nebula Security diverges from the Perplexity rung is attribution surface: Eten Zou has no retrievable Twitter, LinkedIn, or GitHub, the "first nginx RCE" claim in the YC description surfaces "depthfirst" as the actual credited researcher on cybersecuritynews.com, and the second co-founder is unnamed on the YC page. When I couldn't independently verify Aravind's prior shipping I could still watch him ship weekly; Nebula Security gives me research output I can't tie to a person, which is a fainter version of the same signal.

$0K+ in bug bounties earned. That's Nebula Security's own counter on nebusec.ai as of July 2026, alongside no pricing page, no customer logos, and 87 LinkedIn followers on a company page that also collides with a stealth LA entity from 2022. The failure mechanism at the low rung of this ladder is what I called "false signal" versus "just working" on TechCrunch, Nov 2025 — and Nebula Security isn't even at false signal, it's at *no signal disclosed*, in a batch (YC S26) where competent teams disclose ARR by demo day. Harvey ran $3B → $5B → $8B in months because the revenue was there to be shown; Nebula Security's silence on velocity, in a category as hot as AI code security, is the specific pattern I flagged as "something's fundamentally broken."

Market catalyst — mechanism analysis: the "AI-generated code raises vulnerability density" thesis (per nomadlab.cc and vibe-eval.com, 2026) is a real re-opening, but it's an 18-month-old thesis that funded Snyk-alternatives, XBOW at >$1B, and Semgrep's $100M Series D in Feb 2025 *before* Nebula Security entered YC S26. The catalyst compounds against Nebula Security, not for it: every month the "AI writes vulnerable code" narrative strengthens, XBOW's $237M war chest converts more of the buyer conversations, and the window Nebula Security would need to ride narrows. This is the inverse of the Anduril (2022) setup where I entered *because* Ukraine had opened a window peers were still ignoring.

Index position — counterfactual: for Nebula Security to move up a tier here, VEGA would need to sit in the workflow path every AI-code-gen tool must call — a CI hook the Cursor/Copilot/Windsurf tier integrates against, or a scanning API baked into the LLM-codegen loop itself. What the dossier actually shows is a scanner-plus-boutique-audit product competing head-on with Snyk ($1.32B raised, $8.5B), Semgrep ($196M raised), and XBOW ($237M raised) on features. That's the *Lyft-Series-C* structural position — #2-or-later in a category with declared leaders — and my *AI Market Clarity* piece (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) already listed AI code security as trending toward winners-declared.

Single miracle — anchor to **Color Genomics** (2015): Color worked because we structured it as workflow on top of commodity sequencing rather than betting on new chemistry, isolating the miracle to distribution and clinical workflow. Nebula Security is *almost* the same shape — the miracle is "an AI agent finds deeper bugs than pattern-based SAST," and the rest is GTM and audit-brand construction. I score this reasonably because a two-person team plus published V8 CVE artifacts is at least *evidence* the miracle has happened once; the discount is that XBOW already claims the same miracle at much larger scale, so "single miracle achieved" doesn't confer position when someone else achieved it eighteen months earlier with 100x the capital.

My 36-month forecast on Nebula Security: acqui-hired by an incumbent security platform (Snyk, Semgrep, or a hyperscaler security team) for the CVE-research talent at under $40M, or reduced to a boutique "Audited by Nebula Security" services shop under $3M ARR. The mechanism is the low-dimension of the signature — no revenue curve, no distribution wedge, entering after XBOW's $237M and Semgrep's Series D have already collected the enterprise-security buyer conversations Nebula Security would need. Real research talent gets bought; unclear category positions don't compound into standalone outcomes.

The bull case that would sting: Anduril (2022) was my own violation of "no science projects" precisely because Trae Stephens and Palmer Luckey had *shipped at scale* and the market re-opening was crisp — and one could argue Eten Zou's V8 sandbox escape (CVE-2026-6307) is the offensive-research equivalent of Palmer's Oculus artifact, with AI-generated code as the crisp catalyst. The rebuttal is that Anduril arrived with contracts already compounding at Series D and verifiable operator pedigree, while Nebula Security has $0K disclosed bounties on its own site, unverifiable attribution on the flagship nginx RCE claim (cybersecuritynews.com credits "depthfirst"), and an unnamed second co-founder on the YC page. The verifiable condition that flips me to invest: Eten Zou personally credited on a named V8 or kernel CVE bounty *and* Nebula Security disclosing >$500K ARR from three named enterprise buyers within 12 months. Until then, Nebula Security is a pass — a boutique research shop, not the workflow index on AI code security.
