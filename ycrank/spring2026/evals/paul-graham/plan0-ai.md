# PLAN0 AI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

PLAN0 AI is what happens when someone trained in quant finance applies frameworks to a domain rather than living inside it. Arash Barati was an economist at Drake Real Estate Partners doing statistical modeling for real estate PE, then a director at the Ibanez family office — and now PLAN0 AI is a "quant finance inspired" cost-forecasting product pitched as "the CoStar or Bloomberg of construction." The signature pairing here is a real schlep market (construction estimating) with a non-organic discovery story (an economist who watched developers underwrite, not a GC who did takeoffs at midnight). That's the Pass Example archetype in proptech clothing.

The dimension where PLAN0 AI sits highest is the one I care about least when it stands alone — *Idea That Repels Before It Attracts*. Stripe is the high rung on this ladder: payments was a regulatory and integration schlep no one with options wanted to touch, and the Collisons walked into it anyway. The low rung is the consultant pitch from my Pass Example — "B2B workflow automation" that everyone smiles at. PLAN0 AI sits between Stripe and the mid rung (ClassDojo's K-12 procurement schlep), closer to the middle: construction underwriting is a genuine schlep, but the *AI estimating* wedge has already attracted Togal at $299/seat, Kreo at $35/month, Beam doing 2-3 day human-reviewed estimates, and Provision with $7M in the bank for the same "AI co-pilot for pre-construction estimates" pitch. Six tools were already in head-to-head benchmarks in February 2026. The schlep is real; the consensus around the wedge is also real. That's not the Stripe end of the ladder.

PLAN0 AI's lowest dimension is *Organic Problem Discovery*, and this is where my framework lights up. Drew Houston built Dropbox because he kept forgetting his USB drive — that's the high rung, the purest case of "live in the future and build what's missing." The low rung is the McKinsey duo who "identified an opportunity" in B2B workflow through 50 customer interviews. PLAN0 AI sits closer to the low rung than the mid rung (Stripe-as-Collisons-frustrated-with-payments-during-Auctomatic): Arash sat *above* the construction buyer — a family-office director and a real estate PE economist — not inside the estimator's chair. The dossier gives me no scene of frustration, no moment where a GC's takeoff sheet went sideways at 2am. The "quant finance inspired deep neural networks" decomposing costs into "market observable factors" is the giveaway phrasing. That's a framework imported from Drake, not a problem discovered while building.

*Founder Resourcefulness Under Constraint* — Airbnb cereal boxes at the high rung, Stripe at mid, the Pass Example consultants at the low rung. PLAN0 AI sits closer to the low rung because the dossier offers no anecdote: no scrappy first customer, no manual estimate done by hand to bootstrap the dataset, no shipped prototype, no pricing page (just "Book a Demo"). The "tens of thousands of examples" training set claim is unverified, and Shervin Barati — the CTO — has no public footprint at all in WebSearch as of May 2026. I cannot tell you what he has shipped. That absence is itself information.

*Founder Learning Rate* — Sam Altman at 19 at Loopt is the high rung; Patrick Collison shipping Auctomatic before Stripe is mid; founders flat on a single idea for a year are low. PLAN0 AI is unscored territory: no metrics, no visible iteration, YC Spring 2026 batch, hiring set to false. I default modestly. *Earnest Builder Identity* — Watsi at the high rung (board seat for zero ROI), Bindwell teens at mid (obsessed with pesticide chemistry to the point of licensing molecules), Pass Example consultants at the low rung (status and TAM). PLAN0 AI tilts toward the low rung: the "Bloomberg of construction" framing is a category-aspiration statement, the kind I associate with founders optimizing for valuation comparables. Earnest builders describe the problem; status-builders describe the analog.

My 36-month forecast: PLAN0 AI either gets out-executed by Provision (already $7M ahead) and absorbed into Procore, Autodesk, or Trimble in a low-tens-of-millions acqui-hire by mid-2029, or it narrows toward a data-product wedge for the real estate investor segment Arash actually knows — ARR somewhere in the $1-3M band, alive but no longer the Bloomberg of anything. The mechanism is simple: a finance-trained outsider in a contested AI category gets compressed between better-funded peers (Provision) and incumbent data owners (CoStar, Autodesk) who already hold the underlying feeds.

The strongest case against me runs through ClassDojo, the Known Exception. ClassDojo violated my "hackers building for hackers" pattern and won because the schlep was extreme and the founders' execution was exceptional. PLAN0 AI's bull mechanism is similar: Arash's Drake and Explora rolodex puts him one phone call from exactly the developer, GC, and investor buyers who pay Bloomberg-tier prices, and construction estimating is a schlep that repels polished founders. Procurement access plus a real schlep can carry a company a long way. But ClassDojo's founders had spent years adjacent to the classroom; Arash sat *above* the construction buyer in a family office and ran statistics on real estate PE — the wrong side of the table. Procurement access is not the same as product taste, and CTO Shervin Barati has no verifiable shipping history I can find. I would flip this verdict on one signal: a signed multi-year contract with a named top-20 GC or institutional developer, or public evidence that Shervin shipped a non-trivial computer vision product before PLAN0 AI. Without one of those, this is a pass.
