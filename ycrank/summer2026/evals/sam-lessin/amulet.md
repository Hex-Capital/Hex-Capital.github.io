# Amulet -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Amulet is the pass example I wrote down a year ago showing up in the flesh — two ex-Meta engineers (Nithik Bala, Core Ads Growth; Varun Puru, Facebook product) building "AI agents for customer success" against Gainsight ($1.1B Vista acquisition, $1.2B+ ARR), Vitally ($30M Series B), ChurnZero, Planhat, and Staircase AI (already tucked into Gainsight in July 2024). The dimensional signature is bleak in a specific way: Amulet's highest dimension is founder conviction, and only because ex-Meta ads-infra engineers clear a competence bar, while its lowest is category-sleeve fit — this is the exact "AI is just more internet" archetype I explicitly deselect. A commodity agent shell in the most-consensus 2026 YC category, wearing a generic SaaS cap table, with zero owned distribution and no non-obvious belief visible on the record.

On category sleeve, the HIGH rung is Solana at $0.05/coin — pre-consensus in a category tier-1 funds had abandoned — and Amulet is not within shouting distance of that mechanism. The load-bearing analogy isn't Solana; it's the anti-analogy. Solana was pre-consensus with real on-chain economic activity nobody would underwrite; Amulet is post-consensus with a category already saturated by Gainsight/Vitally/ChurnZero/Planhat plus Staircase AI as a live proof that incumbents will buy this exact wedge at $5M seed valuations before it reaches distribution. When the Medium market review from April 2026 already lists AI copilots inside Vitally and Agentforce-derived agents inside Gainsight, Amulet isn't entering a sleeve — it's entering a pile.

Take the canonical Pass Example verbatim: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical]," standard SAFE, no owned distribution, no public writing, no non-obvious belief about why the incumbent playbook fails. Does that failure mechanism appear in the Amulet dossier? Founder-market fit is engineering not CS (search snippets confirm both Bala and Puru are Meta SWEs with no post-sales or GTM background); founder reach is Twitter-not-found for both, LinkedIn "Nithik B." with no thought-leadership footprint, GitHub not found, no essays on why Gainsight's architecture is wrong; and Amulet.so returned HTTP 403 with no pricing or customer logos indexed. That is the Pass Example. I am not going to pretend otherwise because two smart engineers left Meta to build it.

Round pricing compounds against Amulet over 18 months, not just at entry. YC S26 AI-agent SAFEs are clearing $15–25M post at the median, and Amulet has no signal — no revenue disclosed, no LOIs named, no pilot logos — that would justify a discount from the batch consensus. Eighteen months forward, if Amulet raises a seed extension at markup, it's because the round momentum carried it, not because the underlying unit economics unlocked; and I don't get paid for buying momentum at consensus prices. This is precisely the "linear momentum game played for access by salespeople, not underwriters" line from the Slow letter.

Counterfactual on capital structure: what would have to be true for Amulet to flip a tier on Criterion 2? The financing would need to be something other than a standard Delaware C-corp SAFE — a revenue-share on a specific incumbent's channel, a services-first Wizard-of-Oz book that converts to software, a holdco wrapping three adjacent CS-tooling assets. None of that is in the dossier. Amulet is the SaaS cap table that will need five more rounds to reach default-alive against Gainsight's $1.2B ARR distribution advantage, and the criterion penalizes exactly that shape.

Founder-as-brand is a single-anchor comparison to John Fish / Bookshelved, and the anchor makes Amulet's absence visible: Fish arrived with a YouTube audience Slow could underwrite as the actual asset, distribution pre-existing the company by years. Amulet arrives with no Twitter tied to the company, no substack, no OSS following, no CS-operator community, and a name ("Amulet") that returns unrelated hits on Product Hunt and social search. There is no cap-table asset here beyond two engineers' time, which means every dollar of ARR has to be bought through paid acquisition or hired AEs against Gainsight's incumbent sales motion — the worst possible starting position for capital efficiency.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Amulet is acqui-hired by Gainsight, Salesforce, or HubSpot for sub-$20M as a talent-and-agent-IP tuck-in following the Staircase AI template, or it winds down quietly after burning the seed on GTM into a market where the buyer already renewed their Gainsight contract. Sub-$5M ARR at the 36-month mark is the most likely operating outcome; the acqui-hire path is the mercy exit. The mechanism producing this is the specific pattern from the Staircase acquisition — Gainsight has demonstrated it will buy the agent-native wedge at pre-seed valuations before the startup reaches distribution, which caps Amulet's upside at exactly the price incumbents will pay to neutralize it.

Steel-man: the Staircase AI precedent is genuinely the bull case — $5M seed, acquired by Gainsight within a year, and if Amulet at a $20M post-money seed exits for $40–60M as a talent-and-IP acqui-hire, the check returns 2–3x within 24 months. Ex-Meta engineers make credible acqui-hire targets. But 2–3x on a pre-seed check is not the shape of return I am underwriting from my own book — my alpha comes from illegible-asset mispricing at entry, and Amulet is a fully-legible commodity priced at consensus, where the ceiling is set by the incumbent's willingness to pay. The verdict flips if Amulet publishes a signed pilot with a named Fortune-500 CS org at seat-based pricing before Demo Day, or if the round comes in below $10M post with a non-standard instrument (services-revenue kicker, employee-equity structure) — neither is in the dossier.
