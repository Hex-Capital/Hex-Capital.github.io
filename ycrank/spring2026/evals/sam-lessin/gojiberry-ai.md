# Gojiberry AI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is the cleanest example I've seen this batch of capital-efficient repeat operators running the single most consensus-priced category in 2026 ? "AI GTM agents for B2B teams," $2.5M ARR in 10 months and profitable, against a Clay that just closed $100M at a $3.1B valuation on ~$100M ARR. The signature here is a venture-shaped P&L wearing the wrong category jersey. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant, Rom?n Czerny, and Dylan Teixeira have shipped and sold real software before (CoCo.ai, Edusign), and the unit economics already work ? but Gojiberry AI is selling LLM-written LinkedIn outreach at $99/month into a market where my own podcast quote ? "AI is just more internet" ? is the entire pricing problem.

Take the canonical YC pass I described publicly: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], three months out of YC, four tier-1 VCs already in." Gojiberry AI is a more capital-efficient version of that exact pitch ? same category, same SAFE-shaped cap table, same playbook. Sublime Security sits on the HIGH rung of this dimension because security buyers were under-funded at the seed stage and the consolidation wave hadn't started; Gojiberry AI sits on the opposite end because the consolidation wave already happened upstream of them ? Clay raised $204M, Apollo crossed $100M ARR years ago, Artisan and Outreach already occupy the named-brand slots. PitchBook shows $500K raised, which is YC standard, and there is zero dossier evidence of a price discount, a passed tier-1, or any asset-class ambiguity I could exploit. This is consensus at consensus, and Gojiberry AI's growth rate doesn't fix the entry price.

Pure foundation-model AI infrastructure at seed pricing is the failure mode I name explicitly ? Gojiberry AI is one layer up the stack but in the same conceptual bucket: an LLM wrapper over LinkedIn scraping with a learning loop that Clay's first-party data graph dwarfs by definition. The pattern I avoid is "we'll be the [hot category] for [vertical]" without a non-obvious belief, and Gojiberry AI's blog literally consists of "alternatives to Clay / Apollo / Artisan / Instantly" SEO pages. There is no contrarian secret here about why the incumbents fail; there is a faster-moving team executing the consensus playbook well. That's a fine business and a bad fit for the sleeve.

Mechanism analysis on the capital-structure dimension: Gojiberry AI is genuinely capital efficient ? $2.5M ARR in 10 months, profitable per the YC blurb, $500K total raised ? which over 18 months compounds into one of two outcomes. Either 35% MoM cools to flat at ~$8?15M ARR as Clay and Apollo bundle the same intent signals into existing seats, in which case Gojiberry AI is a clean tuck-in acquisition; or LinkedIn's ToS enforcement clips the intent-scraping motion overnight and the cost structure stays default-alive while the team rebuilds on a new channel. Both endings are venture-shaped without being venture-required ? which is exactly the Squarespace / Muck Rack / Zapier framing I use on the SafeGraph podcast, except those companies never raised because they didn't need to. Gojiberry AI shouldn't be raising the round I'm being shown.

The dossier surfaces "18 demos in 5 days" LinkedIn posts and a stale 100K-subscriber YouTube channel from 2020 ? distribution exhaust, not category conviction. Repeat-founder pattern-matching is not the same as missionary temperament, and Gojiberry AI's public footprint reads as operator-mode, not founder-as-essayist mode.

Single-anchor parallel on the founder-as-brand dimension: Marina Mogilko brought millions of language-learning subscribers and twenty years of compounding earning power into a deal I structured as 5% of 30-year earnings, because the asset was the person. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant brought a 2020-era 100K YouTube channel that isn't category-aligned with B2B sales, and Rom?n Czerny brought LinkedIn growth posts that read as standard founder-led content rather than owned community ? There is no instrument I'd structure around the Gojiberry AI founders specifically; the distribution belongs to the company, not the people, and the company's distribution is SEO comparison pages against Clay. That's a marketing motion, not a cap-table asset.

My 36-month forecast on Gojiberry AI: acquired by Apollo, HubSpot, or Clay itself for $60?150M between 2027 and 2029 after reaching $8?15M ARR and stalling as the AI SDR category compresses into existing sales platforms, with founders taking meaningful secondary and never raising a priced Series A. That outcome is consistent with the signature ? venture-shaped operators in an un-investable sleeve produce tuck-in M&A, not category-defining outcomes, and the $99/month SMB price point caps enterprise expansion before the regatta even leaves the harbor.

The bull case that genuinely stings: Gojiberry AI is profitable at $2.5M ARR in 10 months with 35% MoM and a three-time exited founding team ? this is the exact Squarespace / Muck Rack / Zapier profile I publicly celebrate as "incredible billion dollar companies that raised nothing." If Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Dylan Teixeira execute a CoCo.ai-style outcome at 10x the scale, the LinguaTrip-style structural play would be to skip equity entirely and write a revenue-share check against the next $50M of bookings. Two facts dismantle it: first, the LinkedIn platform dependency is not a "tack" you can navigate, it's a single regulator with a kill switch (Salesforge, SyncGTM both flag this); second, Clay at $3.1B post with $100M ARR is not a competitor Gojiberry AI tacks past ? it's the acquirer or the executioner. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Gojiberry AI lands a named enterprise logo above $250K ACV without LinkedIn as the primary channel within 12 months ? proving the company is a sales-execution layer, not a LinkedIn-scraping wrapper. Absent that, this is a tidy business at a bad price in the wrong sleeve, and I'm out before the meeting starts.
