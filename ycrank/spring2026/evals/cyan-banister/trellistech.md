# Trellis -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Lodovico Benvenuti spent years running a 150-listing short-term rental portfolio to ~$2M in revenue and then built AI customer service agents at HostAI/Conduit (YC W24) before founding Trellis — that's the highest signal in this dossier, and it sits inside the lowest-signal market I've looked at this week. Trellis is the rare profile where the founder origin is fully authentic but the lane is consensus AI-agent-for-vertical-X, with his own prior employer already raising $3.1M to do something adjacent. The signature is "operator-founder in a saturated AI wrapper category" — and that pairing is what determines the check.

Benvenuti's biography is closer to the Anduril/Luckey template than almost any pre-seed founder I see — Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus before pivoting into defense; Benvenuti ran 150 listings and built AI agents at a YC STR company before launching Trellis. The mechanism that made Anduril load-bearing was that Luckey had already shipped a comparable hardware-software artifact to scale, so the second act was compounded knowledge rather than discovered curiosity. Trellis diverges from the Anduril rung on the magnitude of the prior artifact — $2M revenue and 150 listings is real operator credibility, but it is small-business scale, not Oculus scale, and the second co-founder Jan Sahagun's STR Tech Report is a media platform rather than a shipped product. The lived-experience signal is genuine; the prior-product altitude is one rung below the template.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I pass on every batch has the same shape Trellis has: a vertical pick (STR), a "we hit feature parity in two weeks" boast, and incumbents with hundreds of millions of dollars already in the room. Benvenuti's own admission that Trellis "reached feature parity with competitors in two weeks" is the exact tell — if his four engineers got to parity in two weeks, the polymath at HostAI/Conduit ($3.1M, YC W24) gets there in three, and KKR-backed Guesty ($409M raised, $900M valuation) gets there with a feature flag. The pattern from my Pass Example sits in the Trellis dossier almost verbatim, only swapping the four-logo Stanford-Goldman team page for an authentic operator backstory. The founder is better; the category dynamics are identical.

200+ live integrations is the kind of moat-claim that compounds backwards over 18 months once Airbnb, VRBO, Guesty, and Hostaway notice Trellis is routing their economic gravity into a thin orchestration layer — those are competitor-controlled APIs, not partner-controlled, and the historical pattern is that channel managers throttle access the moment the orchestration layer threatens the per-seat SaaS model. Trellis's integration breadth reads as a moat at month 6 and as a dependency at month 24.

Counterfactual on the single-CEO dimension: Trellis would clear the bar if the dossier named Benvenuti as CEO with majority operating control and disclosed the equity split with Sahagun — instead the dossier names them both "Co-founder" with no governance disclosed, which under my default reading is "they haven't had the conversation yet." Benvenuti's quotes carry the technical-CEO timbre and Sahagun's profile is GTM/community, so the de facto split probably works; the unresolved question is what happens when the product roadmap collides with the community-channel demands of Sahagun's STR Tech Report network. Two named co-founders without a documented tiebreaker is exactly the structural shape that took HQ Trivia (2017) down.

The Niantic comparison on physical-world verifiability is the one Trellis cannot earn — I played Ingress in 2012 for years before John Hanke ever pitched me, which means by the time he was raising I had already lived inside the product. Trellis is B2B SaaS for property managers; I can stay in a short-term rental and feel the underlying domain, but I cannot install Trellis, I cannot photograph it, and the "84.5% automation rate" on the landing page is a vendor-supplied number, not a tactile artifact. The closest I get is a single testimonial from a "100+ units" operator saving 20 hours a week, which is testimony, not observation.

36-month forecast: Trellis reaches $4-9M ARR by mid-2029 and is acquired by Guesty or Hostaway for $40-90M, or — the failure branch — is rolled up by a private-equity STR consolidator at acqui-hire economics under $20M. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: the authentic operator-founder gets Trellis to credible early traction inside a year because Benvenuti actually knows which 200 integrations matter, but the founder-admitted two-week feature-parity ceiling means whoever owns the property-manager relationship at scale wins the consolidation, and that is Guesty ($900M) or Hostaway ($925M), not Trellis.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass is actually a bull case for Benvenuti specifically — he is the closest thing in this dossier to the Anduril-Luckey operator-becomes-builder pattern, and HandUp (~2014) is my own precedent for backing a founder whose biography mirrors the problem regardless of category ceiling. The override would say: write the check because Benvenuti is *of* the STR industry the way Chase Lochmiller was *of* the oilfields, and trust the operator to find the pivot when consolidation arrives. What dismantles the override is the consensus-market filter — HandUp ran in a category nobody else would enter, Trellis runs in a category Guesty, Hostaway, Breezeway, and HostAI/Conduit are already running in with combined funding north of $990M. Lived experience is necessary but not sufficient when the category is already three deep with funded competitors. The verifiable condition that would flip me: Trellis discloses signed annual contracts at named multi-thousand-listing managers (Vacasa, AvantStay, or i Trip tier) at $150K+ ACV before demo day, which would prove the operator-channel is unlocking enterprise rather than long-tail SMB, and that flips Trellis from "consolidation target" to "consolidation threat."
