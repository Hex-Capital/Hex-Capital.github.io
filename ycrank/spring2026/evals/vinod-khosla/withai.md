# WithAI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

WithAI is the dimensional inverse of every check I want to write: its strongest axis is a competent technical co-founder pairing (Ian McInnis ex-Bridgewater, Ben Finch ex-Sentient Labs with an OML paper at IACR 2024/1573), and its weakest is the one that matters most to me — the success-state. The maximum-ambition world in which WithAI wins is a world where fundamental equity hedge funds research stocks 30% faster. That is not a civilizational variable. That is a productivity wedge inside an existing $15T capital-markets machine that already functions.

WithAI sits near the bottom of my consequence ladder. The HIGH rung is **OpenAI** (2019), where the success-state is the $15T labor-displacement frame I described to Fortune (2026-03-06) — an incumbent regime (human cognitive work) collapses. The MID rung is **DoorDash** (2013), a Known Exception where the consequence was reshaping consumer-commerce flow, not civilizational but at least category-redefining at a $72B IPO. The LOW rung is the Pass Example — the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer where success means a 20-30% efficiency gain inside one persona. WithAI's "custom command centers for hedge funds" sits one notch above that LOW rung: the named consequence is that Mercator Partners went from "existentially threatened" to "tremendously accelerated," which is a testimonial about a single mid-size asset manager's workflow, not a sentence about which incumbent system disappears. Bloomberg terminals do not die because WithAI ships; hedge funds do not become a labor category I would name in my $15T figure.

WithAI's technical/capital-risk profile sits at the LOW end of the ladder where I have no edge. The HIGH rung is **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) — $115M Series A I led when fusion was investor-toxic and three independent physics miracles were required. The MID rung is **Cognition** (2024), stack-deep AI engineering that still required a proprietary model investment. The LOW rung is the wrapper-on-someone-else's-foundation-model pattern I flagged as a Red Flag. WithAI explicitly states the product "integrates frontier agents" including Anthropic and has "no data advantage at the model layer" — that is the wrapper pattern verbatim. Sixty integrations and a client-cloud deployment are competent engineering, not the kind of capital-hostile science where consensus VCs decline and I get my pricing edge. Any Tier-1 generalist can fund this. I am over-resourced.

WithAI's founder-defies-consensus dimension sits between LOW and MID — Ian McInnis is a Princeton Math 2023 grad who went straight to Bridgewater as an Investment Associate and led their "how can we use LLMs to invest" workstream, which is the exact domain-expert-continuity profile my TED Talk warned against (the UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study, experts "only slightly more accurate than chance"). The MID rung here is **Glean**, a Known Exception where Arvind Jain's ex-Google search profile was acceptable because consequence-magnitude carried the deal; the HIGH rung is **Physical Intelligence** (2024) where Chelsea Finn and Sergey Levine had publicly defied robotics orthodoxy. Ben Finch's Sentient Labs/OML work is a real research credential and pulls WithAI off the LOW rung, but Ian's Bridgewater pedigree is the founder narrative I expect to hear in the pitch — and there is no Glean-scale consequence to override it.

WithAI's learning-rate dimension defaults to my mid-low prior. The HIGH rung is **Slash** (2024), where the publicly told Kanye-pivot was the override that justified my $100M Series C; the LOW rung is the linear-plan founder with no documented thesis update. The WithAI dossier reports two Princeton 2023 classmates who "became fast friends" and built one company together — no prior pivot, no public thesis change, no prior exit. That is not a founder-rigidity signal at 24 months out of school, but it is not the Slash override either. Required-capital sits below my check size — enterprise SaaS with consultative sales reaches $1M ARR on $3-5M. The HIGH rung **Commonwealth Fusion** $115M Series A and the $50M **OpenAI** (2019) first check are not the deployment profile WithAI's roadmap demands.

WithAI's 36-month forecast: $4-9M ARR across 20-40 mid-size hedge fund logos, squeezed between Rogo's $154M war chest expanding from IB/PE into hedge-fund workflows and in-house "AIA Labs"-style builds at the larger funds, ending in a sub-$150M acquisition by FactSet, S&P Global, or Bloomberg defending terminal seat counts. The mechanism is the consequence-magnitude ceiling: a workflow optimizer for a fixed-size customer base of fundamental hedge funds has a natural revenue ceiling that consolidates into the data-vendor stack rather than collapsing it.

The strongest bull case against my pass is the angel sheet. Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour are co-CIOs of Bridgewater on WithAI's cap table — that is not a normal pre-seed signal, and it implies a warm channel into every fundamental shop in New York and Greenwich that respects Bridgewater. The bull mechanism is that WithAI becomes the default "AIA Labs for funds that can't build their own" and rides a winner-take-most dynamic to $50M+ ARR and a $500M-1B strategic outcome. I take the objection seriously and still pass: the angel signal compresses distribution risk but does nothing to the consequence-magnitude ceiling, and my framework does not fund 10x outcomes in capital-markets workflow tooling when the same dollars can underwrite a Genesis AI co-lead. The verifiable condition that flips me: WithAI demonstrates within 12 months that Multiplier generates novel, falsifiable investment signals — not synthesis of existing signals — producing measurable alpha above the fund's prior baseline. Verifiable alpha, not "tremendously accelerated" testimonials, would put this into the labor-displacement frame I actually fund.
