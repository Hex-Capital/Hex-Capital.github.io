# Scope -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Scope is a timely-thesis bet on a multi-miracle category-creation problem executed by a solo first-time founder, Anand-Arnaud Pajaniradjane, working from a single-line YC blurb and a cal.com booking page. With Scope, the dossier evidence puts it closer to the median.

On Nonobvious Market Timing, my ladder runs from Perplexity at the top -- where I read the GPT capability curve and wrote the seed before ChatGPT shipped -- through Gusto in the middle (real digitization, no generational catalyst), down to consumer social YC apps where there is no exogenous shift at all. But the consumer half being already consensus means Scope is selling a derivative of a thesis that has a timestamp on it, not a Perplexity-grade nonobvious window. Check the timestamp: this is timing-positive, not timing-overwhelming.

On Single-Miracle Viability, the ladder runs Harvey at the top (one exogenous miracle: foundation-model capability rising on its own curve, with law firms already proven buyers), Gusto in the middle (execution-heavy regulated category, no miracle), and Anduril at the bottom -- three simultaneous miracles in autonomous tech, defense procurement reform, and hardware manufacturing, which I only accepted because the bipartisan modernization signal was generational. The strongest case against my pass invokes Anduril directly: when market timing is overwhelming, I have accepted multi-miracle risk before, and MCP standardization plus the agent-as-buyer shift could be the early innings of a vendor-side measurement layer that becomes the Profound of the agent era -- with Profound's own $1B mark proving the willingness-to-pay. Until then I'm a pass with watchlist interest -- the timestamp is right, the layer and the team are not yet.
