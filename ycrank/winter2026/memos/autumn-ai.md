## The Idea

**Problem:** B2B go-to-market teams manually piece together buying signals from scattered sources — LinkedIn posts, GitHub commits, blog announcements, incorporation filings, conference attendee lists — leading to slow, incomplete prospect research and rapid signal decay. Current workflows involve cycling through multiple tools and tabs, with sales reps spending 3–5 hours on prospect research that often surfaces stale information (Founderland article, 2026). Existing solutions like ZoomInfo and 6sense provide contact databases and intent data but do not offer a unified, real-time feed of public signals filtered by custom ICP criteria and intent definitions.

**Approach:** Autumn monitors public signals across LinkedIn, GitHub, X (Twitter), conference data, corporate filings, blogs, and announcements in real time. Users define their Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and the specific signals that matter (e.g., new hires, product launches, funding events), and Autumn delivers a condensed, filtered feed of buying signals as they appear. Alerts are delivered via email or Slack (Founderland article, 2026; YC page). Example use cases include fintechs prospecting newly incorporated tech companies, deep-tech companies preparing pre-conference outreach, and enterprise account monitoring of entire departments within target accounts (YC LinkedIn launch post, 2026).

**Differentiation:** Unlike contact database platforms (ZoomInfo, Apollo.io) that primarily sell enriched lead data, Autumn focuses on real-time behavioral signal aggregation and filtering. Unlike intent data platforms (6sense, Demandbase) that rely on proprietary web-visit and third-party intent data, Autumn ingests publicly observable signals (social posts, commits, filings) and lets users define custom signal definitions. Signum.AI is the closest comparable — also tracking changes across companies and individuals — but Signum.AI focuses on competitive intelligence and client insights rather than real-time buying signal feeds for outbound sales.

## Defensibility

No public patents, proprietary datasets, or documented network effects at this stage. The product aggregates publicly available signals, which does not constitute a proprietary data moat on its own. [Inferred]: Potential defensibility could develop via (1) accumulated signal-processing logic and filtering models tuned to specific verticals, (2) user-defined ICP and signal configurations creating switching costs as teams build workflows around the platform, and (3) a data flyback loop where user engagement data improves signal relevance over time. These are unproven at this stage.

**Market structure:** Large incumbents (ZoomInfo, 6sense, Demandbase) are primarily structured around proprietary contact databases and third-party intent data purchased from data brokers. Their business models depend on selling data access, which creates a structural conflict with building an open-signal aggregation product that monitors freely available public data. Rebuilding around public signal monitoring would cannibalize their core data-licensing revenue model. However, incumbents could add signal monitoring as a feature layer without disrupting their core product. No structural barrier identified at this stage that would prevent incumbents from replicating this approach as an add-on feature.

**Commoditization risk:** The core technology — monitoring public APIs and web sources, applying NLP/AI to extract intent signals — is technically reproducible. Multiple startups already operate in adjacent spaces (Signum.AI, Crayon, RivalHunt). LLM-based signal extraction is increasingly commoditized. Differentiation would need to come from signal coverage breadth, filtering accuracy, UX for sales workflows, and integrations.

## Key Risks

**Brand confusion with multiple "Autumn" companies:** At least three other companies use similar branding: Autumn (YC S25, billing infrastructure for AI startups), Autumn AI (Berlin, 2015, ML framework), and Autumn.ai (chatbot/compliance). This creates SEO competition, marketplace confusion, and potential trademark challenges. The company's domain (autumnai.com) partially mitigates this.

**Incumbent feature absorption:** ZoomInfo, 6sense, Apollo.io, and Demandbase all have large engineering teams and existing GTM customer bases. Real-time public signal monitoring could be added as a feature to any of these platforms without disrupting their core business. ZoomInfo has already launched AI-driven prospecting via ZoomInfo Copilot (Seeking Alpha, 2025 via search snippet), demonstrating willingness to expand into AI-powered signal features.

**Data source fragility:** The platform depends on continued public API access to LinkedIn, GitHub, X (Twitter), and other platforms. These platforms have progressively restricted API access and scraping — X significantly raised API pricing in 2023, and LinkedIn actively enforces anti-scraping measures. Any further restrictions could degrade Autumn's signal coverage or increase operating costs.

**Pre-revenue with no disclosed customers:** The product is in early access with no named customers, pricing, or revenue publicly disclosed (Founderland article, 2026). Product-market fit remains unvalidated in public evidence. The two-person team must simultaneously build the product and conduct founder-led sales.
