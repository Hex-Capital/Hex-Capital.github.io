# Arlo Industries -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Arlo Industries is a catalyst-rich, slot-poor defense sensing bet -- the market window (Ukraine, Shahed-class drones, the U.S. Army's $20B Anduril C-UAS framework announced March 2026) is exactly the re-opening I rotated into when I led **Anduril**'s Series D in 2022, but the structural position Deo Arlo is staking out -- passive sensor mesh -- sits directly in the path of Lattice rather than alongside it. The signature here is "right market, wrong slot," and the dossier reads like the inverse of the **Anduril** template: the catalyst is louder than the founder's shipping rhythm, and the index position is owned by the company I already funded.

On market re-opening, **Anduril** (2022) is my high rung -- defense was a graveyard until Ukraine exposed Pentagon software inadequacy inside a 24-month window, and that's a textbook catalyst. **Lyft** Series C is my low rung -- a steady-state market where #2 economics never invert. Arlo Industries pins very close to the Anduril rung: Deo Arlo's stated six years inside the Israeli defense ecosystem, Iron Dome and Shahed exposure, and YC's Summer 2026 RFS explicitly naming drone defense all converge on the same re-opening I priced into Anduril at $4.6B post. The DroneXL-reported $20B Army C-UAS ceiling is not a headwind here -- it's confirmation the buyer is awake. This dimension is genuinely strong.

On index position -- the dimension where Arlo Industries is weakest -- **Stripe** (2010) is my high rung as the index on e-commerce, **Anduril** sits mid as the software layer US defense modernization integrates against, and **Lyft** Series C is the low rung where the #2 slot in a winner-take-all network never compounds. Arlo Industries pins between Anduril and Lyft, closer to Lyft: per Tectonic Defense, Lattice is already "a mesh networking platform that fuses data and inputs from sensors and effectors," and Anduril just won the 10-year, $20B Army C-UAS framework. Arlo Industries is sensor-only/passive while the workflow-fusion-and-effector layer above it is already owned by the company peers will default to. Passive RF/acoustic sensing as a sub-component does not become "an index on counter-UAS" -- it becomes a BoM line item Lattice procures, especially as Hall Lidar's April 2026 acoustic-sensing announcement signals the modality is not Arlo's alone.

On revenue velocity, **Harvey** (2023) at $3B → $5B → $8B in months is the high rung and the **Decagon** workflow-velocity-in-an-open-market profile is the mid rung. Arlo Industries pins at the low rung: "demonstrated technology to Ukrainian militaries on the frontlines and to operators in Europe and the USA" is dossier language with no contract, no LOI, no named MoD counterparty, and zero open jobs. Defense pre-seed gets a discount on this dimension -- I'm not penalizing absence of MRR -- but the absence of any *named* procurement counterparty after stated frontline demos is the false-signal-versus-just-working line I drew explicitly in TechCrunch (Nov 2025).

On founder execution rhythm, **Perplexity** (2022) and Aravind Srinivas's "show up a week later with the thing built" is my high rung; **Brex** (2017) operational-CEO discipline is mid; the credentialed promoter who hasn't shipped is the low rung. Arlo Industries pins between mid and low: Deo Arlo has a Technion-patented robotic end-effector and a Makrverse CTO seat per deo.lol -- those are real artifacts -- but neither is in passive aerial sensing, and the second team member listed in YC's "team size 2" is unnamed in every public source. For a hardware-software defense build, a publicly invisible technical co-founder is the structural concern *High Growth Handbook* (2018) is implicitly a profile against. On the single-miracle test, **Color Genomics** (2015) -- my own Known Exception -- worked because the architecture isolated workflow software on top of commodity sequencing rather than betting on chemistry; Arlo Industries is structurally less clean: it requires (1) passive mesh accuracy that scales "exponentially" with linear sensor adds (a physics-and-algorithms claim with no published benchmarks), (2) defense procurement adoption, and (3) coexistence with Lattice rather than substitution by it. That's at least two miracles, possibly three.

My 36-month forecast: Arlo Industries is acquired by a tier-2 defense integrator or a Lattice-adjacent prime for sub-$50M in talent-plus-IP terms, or pivots to selling passive sensor pods as a component into Anduril Lattice's BoM rather than competing with it -- either way it does not become "an index on counter-UAS." The mechanism is simple: the catalyst is real and Deo Arlo's frontline access is real, but every counter-UAS dollar in the next 36 months flows through the framework Anduril just won, and a sensor-only startup either feeds that framework or starves outside it.

The strongest bear-flip case against my pass is that I literally violated my own "no science projects" rule for **Anduril** in 2022 because Trae Stephens and Palmer Luckey had shipped at scale and the catalyst was overwhelming -- and Arlo Industries has the same catalyst. The bull argues: one signed Ukrainian MoD contract converts the "frontline demos" line into Harvey-style velocity in a buyer environment where contracts compound, and passive sensing solves a geolocation problem active radar genuinely cannot. That case stings. But it falls apart against the Anduril analogue: Stephens had built Palantir's forward-deployed muscle and Luckey had shipped Oculus to acquisition -- Deo Arlo's prior shipping artifacts are unrelated to passive aerial sensing, the second founder is invisible, and Anduril at Series D had revenue compounding visibly while Arlo Industries at YC Spring 2026 has zero disclosed counterparties. Flip condition: a named, dollar-disclosed contract with a Ukrainian, European, or U.S. MoD within 12 months, OR a public Anduril/Lattice partnership announcement that converts the substitution risk into a distribution wedge -- either one and I take a meeting and likely a check.
