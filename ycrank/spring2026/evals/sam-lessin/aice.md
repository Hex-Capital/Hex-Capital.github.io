# AICE -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

AICE is a 2-person team that pivoted from "sensors to cut energy bill by 30%" to swarming submarine defense drones approximately two weeks before this dossier was assembled, which tells me everything about the signature: highest dimension is the category sleeve (defense-hardware has at least a "why now" tailwind in 2026), lowest is founder conviction (you cannot have asymmetric conviction about subsea warfare that you adopted fourteen days ago). That pairing — a recent opportunity-chase into a hot consensus sleeve crowded by Anduril's $60B and Helsing's $18B — is the entire AICE thesis in one sentence.

Take the pass example from my own playbook: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], four tier-1 VCs already in" — I'm out before the meeting. AICE rhymes with that pattern, just with "subsea drones for defense" swapped in for the agent. Defense-tech is the 2026 YC consensus sleeve the way AI agents were the 2024 one — Saronic just printed $1.75B at $9.25B in March, Anduril cleared $4B at $60B, Helsing is raising $1.2B at $18B as I read this. AICE is entering at the exact moment the category has resolved into mega-funded incumbents, which is the inverse of how I bought Solana at $0.05 when generalist funds had quit crypto. Where AICE diverges from the Solana mechanism is total: there is no abandoned category here, there are five well-capitalized competitors actively shipping product, and a 2-person seed entering this regatta is buying the most expensive tack on the course.

The Pass Example failure mode — "no public writing I can find on why their take is non-obvious" — is the lowest-rung pattern, and it appears in AICE's dossier verbatim. Chomette has no Twitter, no GitHub, an MIT AeroAstro thesis on asteroid risk, and two years in energy consulting; Bouteiller has 22 GitHub followers and an airplay-esp32 repo with 282 stars. Neither founder has published a single public artifact on subsea acoustics, mine countermeasures, ITAR, or naval procurement, because they weren't working on it a month ago. "Writing is thinking" is the filter, and AICE fails it not because the founders are unintelligent — the MIT pedigree is real — but because there is no missionary temperament for a problem you onboarded in May 2026. AICE founders are responding to opportunity (defense-tech is hot), which is precisely the deselection signal.

The capital-structure mechanism for AICE compounds badly over 18 months: subsea hardware at 600m depth, manufactured in France, with no announced LOIs or government partners, requires the founders to fund (a) prototype iteration, (b) ITAR or French equivalent certification, (c) naval qualification trials that take 24-36 months minimum, and (d) classified-contract clearance — that's four tacks before AICE sees its first dollar of revenue, each of which needs a financing round. Ro got the capital-intensity override because 50-state prescription rails were a genuine regulatory moat I could underwrite as a venture moment; AICE's defense procurement is also a moat, but it protects Anduril and Helsing from AICE, not AICE from anybody. This is the inverse of capital-efficiency.

For founder-as-brand, the counterfactual is what would have to be true to flip AICE one tier: Chomette would need an active newsletter or Substack on autonomous maritime systems with named naval reservist or DoD subscribers, or Bouteiller would need a subsea-robotics open-source project that French Navy engineers had starred. Neither exists. AICE's distribution into defense procurement is going to be cold inbound to ministries, which is the most expensive customer-acquisition channel in venture and the one the $60M Slow Creator Fund was explicitly designed to avoid funding.

Compare AICE's category placement to Sublime Security as a single anchor: Sublime was a pre-consensus enterprise security seed when most security investors were still chasing late-stage SOC SaaS — the category itself was unfashionable at entry. AICE is the opposite: subsea defense in 2026 is the category every generalist fund wants exposure to after Anduril's run, which means whatever price AICE is raising at is being set by competitive consensus, not by a Slow-shaped underwriter being the only bid. The "Crypto is fundamentally disruptive, AI is just more internet" frame from the Lonsdale podcast applies analogously here — defense-hardware is "just more aerospace," not a sleeve where I have an underwriter's edge over Lux or Founders Fund.

My 36-month forecast for AICE: dead or acqui-hired to a European defense prime (Naval Group, Thales, or Helsing itself) for sub-$15M total consideration, with the YC pre-seed taking a near-zero return — the mechanism is that AICE runs out of cash before completing its first naval qualification cycle and the founders' MIT/NASA credentials make them attractive talent for a strategic, not their swarm-AUV IP attractive as a product line. This is consistent with the signature: a 2-week-old pivot into a consensus sleeve with no founder conviction does not survive a 36-month procurement cycle against $60B-incumbent competition.

The strongest bull case for AICE I can construct: YC Group Partner Nicolas Dessaigne is French, AICE's hardware is in Paris, France launched an AI underwater-acoustics program in May 2024, and the modular-swarm-versus-monolithic-AUV positioning against Anduril's Dive-LD is a genuine architectural bet — small cheap swarms could beat large expensive AUVs the way cheap drones beat tanks in Ukraine. If French DGA hands AICE an early evaluation contract through the Dessaigne network, the company becomes a real European defense seed. This bull case stings because operator-friend deal sourcing through YC partner relationships is a real Slow pattern. But the dossier evidence that wins is the founder-domain gap: neither Chomette nor Bouteiller has a documented naval, undersea, or marine-hardening project anywhere in their public history, and you cannot Wizard-of-Oz a submarine. The verdict flips if AICE announces a named French DGA or Naval Group LOI within six months and one of the founders has a credible naval-systems advisor on the cap table — until that artifact exists, I'm out.
