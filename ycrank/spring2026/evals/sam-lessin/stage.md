# Stage -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Stage's signature is two competent Stanford/Five Rings engineers with real founder-market fit -- Dean Stratakos literally led the AI initiative and built the in-house coding agent at Five Rings -- entering an AI code-review category that priced consensus eighteen months ago when CodeRabbit raised $60M at $550M, Greptile took $25M from Benchmark, and Cursor swallowed Graphite. Founder conviction is the high pole; round non-consensus is the low pole. That pairing describes a company I respect operationally and cannot make money on as an angel.

Take my canonical Pass Example -- the 2026 YC AI agent pitch with four tier-1s already in. Stage isn't quite that deck, but it lives on the same block. CodeRabbit is doing $15M+ ARR growing 20%/month (TechCrunch, Sep 2025); Greptile pulled Benchmark; Cursor's Graphite acquisition (TechCrunch, Dec 2025) consolidates the workflow layer. When the category leader is already at $550M post and the closest YC peer (cubic, X25) cleared $500K on the same wedge a year before Stage launched, the round is structurally consensus-priced regardless of what number the SAFE prints. My alpha at seed comes from being one of the only bids -- Stage is being underwritten by the same pattern-match machine that funded the last six AI-dev-tool seeds, and there is no Solana-at-$0.05 here.

The lowest-ladder failure mode in capital structure is the generic SaaS cap table that needs five more rounds to reach default-alive -- the inverse of Teamshares' employee-ownership rollup, where the structure itself was the moat other funds couldn't underwrite. Stage's dossier reads exactly to that failure mode: 14-day free trial, "LAUNCH" promo code for 25% off three months, tier pricing gated behind sign-in. That's a B2B SaaS template indistinguishable from fifty other Spring 2026 batchmates. There's no revenue share, no royalty on the open-source CLI, no creator instrument, no holdco play -- nothing about Stage's financing shape that requires Slow to write the check rather than any momentum fund on Sand Hill.

Stratakos's dastratakos.github.io and the Apple/Siri + Citadel pedigree give Stage real founder-market fit -- this is closer to the Sublime Security operator-temperament profile than to the sanitized-deck archetype. The "what if they're right?" inversion on Stage's contrarian claim -- "Putting humans back in control of code review" while the rest of the category sprints toward auto-reviewers -- is actually the most interesting line in the dossier. But neither Pan nor Stratakos has published opinionated writing on the category by name; the Show HN title is the closest thing to a public position, and "Writing is thinking" means a personal site listing past jobs doesn't clear the bar. Conviction by resume, not by mission.

On founder-as-brand: 204 GitHub stars on ReviewStage/stage-cli is a polite developer-tool footprint; it is not the John Fish / Bookshelved owned-distribution profile that the Slow Creator Fund formalized into a $60M vehicle. No founder Twitter discoverable, LinkedIn not disambiguated for Pan, no newsletter, no podcast, no community pre-existing the company. The Slow Creator filter screens for entrepreneurial creators with distribution; Stage screens through that filter as "no." Mechanism over 18 months: in a category where CodeRabbit owns SEO and GitHub owns default placement, an absent founder-brand means Stage will pay for every user in cash or sales effort -- exactly the burn-treadmill that capital-efficient venture moments avoid.

Counterfactual on category sleeve: for Stage to flip a tier here, the company would need to be on the crypto sleeve, the DTC sleeve, the prescription-rail sleeve, or the creator sleeve. It is none of those -- it is AI-dev-infra-SaaS, which is the exact sleeve where "AI is just more internet" applies (Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024). Single-anchor parallel for round non-consensus: contrast Stage with Aleo at seed, where the crypto sleeve had been left for dead by every generalist fund and the entry price reflected that abandonment; Stage's category, by the dossier's own competitor map, is the opposite -- four named well-funded incumbents, a Microsoft-owned default, and a Cursor consolidation already underway.

Forecast, 36 months: Stage is acqui-hired by GitHub, Cursor, or CodeRabbit for sub-$20M in talent value, or quietly shut down -- I do not see the independent path to $5M ARR against a category leader compounding at 20%/month with eight-figure ARR and Microsoft distribution risk on the platform itself. The mechanism is straightforward: Stratakos is genuinely good, the "chapters" UX is a defensible feature inside someone else's product, and Microsoft/Cursor will pay for the team before they pay for the company.

The strongest bull case invokes the Robinhood exception: Slow entered Robinhood when it was still a YC bet and let subsequent consensus rounds mark up the position -- so why not Stage? Because Robinhood's entry was pre-category-consensus, and Stage's is post. CodeRabbit at $550M is not a future markup that vindicates a Slow entry today; it is the price ceiling already set by a competitor with 20%/month growth, and Stage enters underneath it without a structural reason the ceiling falls. The cubic precedent ($500K, X25) shows YC will fund this category at seed -- it does not show the seeds compound. ONE condition that flips my verdict: Stage signs a paid pilot with a named GitHub-Microsoft-tier enterprise customer (10K+ engineers) on the human-reviewer wedge, validating that the auto-reviewer category has an actual buyer-side backlash rather than a Show HN-side preference -- absent that, Stage is a competent team in a consensus-priced category and I am out before the meeting.
