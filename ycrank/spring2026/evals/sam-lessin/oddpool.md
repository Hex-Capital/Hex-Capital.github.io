# Oddpool -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Oddpool's analytical signature is a crypto-adjacent picks-and-shovels infrastructure play sitting inside a sleeve I'm naturally convicted on, executed by two engineers with zero owned distribution and one of them already running a second YC company. The high/low pairing here is category-sleeve fit against founder-as-brand absence, and that combination produces a very specific kind of outcome -- Oddpool is a Dome-clone candidate, not a Solana candidate, and the dossier knows it: Dome raised $5.2M, sold to Polymarket four-to-five months post-Demo Day for an estimated $10-20M, and Oddpool's entire wedge is "we remain venue-neutral now that Dome no longer is."

Solana at $0.05/coin is what the category-sleeve ladder looks like at the HIGH rung -- pre-consensus crypto with real on-chain economic activity, bought when every generalist fund had quit the category. Oddpool sits in the crypto-adjacent fintech-data slot: Polymarket is on-chain, Kalshi is CFTC-licensed, and the institutional data-layer pick is exactly the kind of "boring infrastructure under a sleeve that's becoming legible" bet I underwrite. But Oddpool diverges from Solana on the mechanism that actually mattered -- Solana was the protocol with the on-chain economic activity, not the Bloomberg terminal pointed at it. Oddpool's 700K indexed markets and tick-level history is a derivative claim on Polymarket and Kalshi continuing to exist as neutral counterparties, and Polymarket already showed in February 2026 that it would rather own the data layer than license it. The sleeve fit is real; the position inside the sleeve is fragile.

Founder-as-brand is where Oddpool's dossier collapses, and the failure mechanism rhymes with the canonical pass I run on YC pitches with no public writing footprint. @oddpool_alerts has ~233 followers, joined October 2025; Avi Arora's @c0delemons and Ritesh Malpani's @RiteshMalpani follower counts aren't even retrievable, which means they're small enough to not register. The Awesome-Prediction-Market-Tools GitHub repo is the only artifact of pre-funding obsession with the category, and a curated list is not the same as opinionated writing. Oddpool's GTM is inferred outbound to quant funds via a founders@oddpool.com inbox -- pure paid-and-pushed sales motion, no compounding distribution asset on the cap table. There is nothing in Oddpool's dossier that the Slow Creator Fund or the Bookshelved logic could touch.

The round-pricing dimension I'd evaluate by mechanism: prediction markets data is a 2026 consensus theme -- Polymarket spent acquisition dollars on Dome four months ago, Opinion hit $8B+ monthly volume, the CFTC pipeline is filling up -- which means Oddpool's seed will get bid by AI/fintech generalists who've read the same Block article I have. Over 18 months that compounds into a markup driven by Polymarket-acquisition narrative rather than fundamentals, which is exactly the "factory model" dynamic I've stopped paying for. No price is disclosed in the dossier, so I'm applying the silence-equals-consensus default and pricing the round at YC P26 median, which is too high for the exit ceiling Dome just established.

Capital structure is best evaluated as a counterfactual: for Oddpool to flip from a standard SaaS cap table into a venture-shaped structure I'd actually write into, the founders would need to either (a) run this as a holdco that owns the dataset and licenses to multiple acquirers, or (b) restructure as a data-royalty instrument paid out of Polymarket/Kalshi feed revenue. Neither is on offer. Oddpool's $30/Pro and $100/Premium tiering plus "several thousand dollars in MRR" reads as a clean prosumer-to-enterprise SaaS funnel, which is the cap-table shape that needs five tacks to reach default-alive and exits to a strategic at Series B, not the regatta I want to underwrite.

Founder conviction at Oddpool is anchored to one named dossier fact that decides this dimension: Ritesh Malpani's LinkedIn lists him as Co-founder of Benchspan, a separate active Spring 2026 YC company. Single-anchor parallel: Fin's first year worked because Andrew Kortina and I were both fully inside one Wizard-of-Oz problem -- no second company, no split attention, manual operations every night. Oddpool is a two-person team with one founder on two YC P26 cap tables simultaneously, which is the most reliable manic-bias setup I screen for, and it directly fails the "weather the downs, not get too manic at the ups" filter. Avi's Microsoft prompt-injection-model credential is real, Ritesh's Bloomberg buy-side latency work is real, but the missionary-for-non-linear-time evidence isn't here.

My 36-month forecast for Oddpool: acquired by Kalshi or a CoinAPI-tier data incumbent for $12-25M before Series A, or ARR plateaus under $750K and pivots to Polymarket-exclusive analytics inside 24 months -- the Dome trajectory replayed with one venue swap. The mechanism is straightforward: Oddpool's value to a strategic acquirer is the captured March-2026-onward orderbook history that cannot be backfilled, and the cheapest way for Kalshi to neutralize Polymarket's Dome acquisition is to buy the next neutral aggregator before it tilts. That outcome is a fine trade for the founders and a perfectly respectable Dome-clone result, but it's not a return shape my fund can use.

The strongest case against my pass is the Sublime Security analogy: a pre-consensus enterprise-infrastructure seed in a category about to consolidate, where the early entry price absorbed the subsequent markups -- if Oddpool is the venue-neutral data spine for institutional prediction-market trading the way Sublime is for email security, the Dome exit at $10-20M is the floor, not the ceiling, and a Bloomberg-Refinitiv-tier outcome is on the table once CFTC venues proliferate. That case stings because I do believe the institutional prediction-markets sleeve is real. It loses to the Ritesh-on-two-cap-tables fact and the absence of any pricing discount in the dossier -- Sublime cleared the price-sensitivity test on entry, and Oddpool's silence on round dynamics tells me it won't. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Ritesh exits Benchspan in writing and Oddpool's seed prices at or below $8M post with no tier-1 lead. Until then, I'm out.
