# WithAI -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

WithAI is a high-specific-knowledge / low-permissionless-leverage company -- Ian McInnis ran the "how can we use LLMs to invest" workstream inside Bridgewater before shipping Multiplier, but the primary CTA on withai.co is "book a consultation" and the product deploys into the client's own cloud with 60+ white-glove integrations. That pairing is the entire signature: the founder is exactly the right human, attacking exactly the wrong shape of business for an angel check from me.

Leverage architecture is where WithAI breaks against my ledger, and this is the dimension I read first. The HIGH rung is **Twitter** (2007) -- media as protocol, zero marginal cost, anyone can post without asking; the MID rung is **Replit** (April 2023), code-leverage that still required some enterprise motion as it scaled into the AI cycle; the LOW rung is the B2B enterprise-SaaS-with-SDRs pattern I sat through during the **Pinterest** misread era and learned to flag earlier. WithAI sits *below* the LOW rung's midpoint -- "white-glove implementation," "consultative sale," 60+ integrations into Bloomberg/FactSet/Snowflake/Enfusion, and a hedge-fund customer who needs a deployed VPC -- this is labor and capital leverage, the two leverage forms that explicitly *require permission* (nav.al/product-media). The Anchorage exception was earned by category-timing conviction I'd been compounding since **MetaStable** (2014); I have no equivalent pre-positioned thesis in hedge-fund middleware, so the exception does not transfer.

Specific knowledge is where WithAI compensates, and it compensates meaningfully. The HIGH rung is **Stack Overflow** (2008) -- Atwood and Spolsky writing for the developers they were; the MID rung is **Notion** (2013), Ivan Zhao's adolescent productivity-tool obsession; the LOW rung is the "ex-McKinsey identified a market opportunity" template I price-down on sight. WithAI sits between Stack Overflow and Notion: Ian McInnis was a Bridgewater Investment Associate leading the firm's internal AI-in-investing R&D, which is the closest thing to lived problem ownership a 2023 Princeton Math grad can have for this exact customer; Benjamin Finch was a founding researcher at Sentient Labs ($85M raised) and co-authored the OML paper (IACR ePrint 2024/1573). Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour writing angel checks is the legible market proof that the founder-market fit is not self-described.

Marketplace and network mechanics are absent, and this is structural, not pre-traction. The HIGH rung is **Uber** (2010) -- the Nth driver makes the (N-1) riders more valuable; the MID rung is **Notion** (2013), where multiplayer documents compound somewhat; the LOW rung is the **Perplexity** (2022) shape, single-sided, which I waived only because three other gates cleared at top quartile. WithAI sits at the LOW rung -- Mercator Partners using Multiplier creates exactly zero value for the next hedge fund customer, the data is siloed by design in the client's own cloud, and the moat narrative ("firm-specific ontology") *requires* the absence of cross-customer compounding. Perplexity-exception math does not rescue WithAI because permissionless distribution (the gate that carried Perplexity) is failed, not borderline.

Big market with pivot room ranks the lowest of my structural dimensions for WithAI. The HIGH rung is **Twitter** (2007), which survived the Odeo-to-twttr pivot because media was the substrate; the MID rung is **Postmates** (2011), which iterated delivery scope repeatedly inside a large consumer market; the LOW rung is the "CRM for HVAC technicians" single-vertical SaaS I keep absent from my 200+ ledger. WithAI sits closer to the LOW rung than the MID -- "custom command centers for hedge funds" is a single vertical, and the differentiation thesis (firm-specific workflow templates) gets *weaker* if WithAI pivots to private equity, family offices, or insurance. Rogo at $154M raised and a $75M Series C (January 2026) plus AlphaSense with Generative Search (January 2026) bracket the pivot vectors WithAI would need.

Founder trio reads clean. The HIGH rung is **Stack Overflow** (2008); MID is most of my ledger; LOW is **Clearview AI** (exception). WithAI's Princeton-2023 cohort, Bridgewater-network angels, and absence of any self-promotion-of-honesty signal -- no integrity flag fires. I'd happily spend ten years with Ian and Ben; the question is whether the business architecture earns ten years of compounding.

Forecast at 36 months: WithAI lands at $4-12M of mostly services-flavored ARR with 8-25 hedge fund customers and is acquired by AlphaSense, FactSet, or Bloomberg for $60-180M between Q4 2027 and Q2 2029 -- the consultative-implementation model produces a credible reference-able revenue stream but compresses outcome geometry into a strategic-acquihire band rather than a 1,000x outcome, which is exactly what the LOW-leverage / HIGH-specific-knowledge signature predicts mechanically.

The bull case that stings: Anchorage was my own override on criterion #1, and the steel-man here is that regulated finance -- like regulated crypto custody -- can flip "permissionless" into "permission-as-moat," with Bridgewater-co-CIO angel backing functioning as the institutional channel that brand-name funds cannot replicate. If Multiplier becomes the default agent layer that compresses Bloomberg terminal seat counts across the long tail of independent asset managers, the **Notion**-shaped multi-billion outcome is real. The reason I do not bite: Anchorage's permission-moat was sovereign-grade (OCC charter, January 2021) and my conviction had compounded for seven years pre-check; WithAI's permission-moat is a relationship graph that Rogo's $154M war chest can buy into within 18 months, and I have no pre-positioned thesis in this category to underwrite the override. **The condition that flips the verdict**: a second non-Bridgewater-network hedge fund of >$2B AUM signed as a paying customer within the next two quarters, AND a self-serve product surface (not "book a consultation") that any sub-$500M-AUM family office can deploy without WithAI's services team -- that pair would convert the leverage architecture from capital-permissioned to code-permissionless and re-open the file.
