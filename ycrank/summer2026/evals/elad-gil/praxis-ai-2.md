# Praxis AI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Praxis AI is a supply-side-position story with no revenue disclosure -- a claim on the pipe with no throughput visible. The dossier's single most load-bearing sentence is that Rohan Seelamsetty and team are "embedded within publicly listed and unicorn-scale conglomerates" reaching 60k workers across 5 continents and 150+ environment types (YC page), and the dossier's single most damning silence is that there is not one dollar of revenue, one named humanoid-lab customer, or one named conglomerate partner anywhere in it. That pairing -- index-like supply position at the top, revenue velocity at the floor -- is Praxis AI's signature in my portfolio.

**Stripe** (2010) is the HIGH rung for index-like distribution -- payments rails that every e-commerce entrant must touch, so Stripe compounded as the category compounded without having to *beat* anyone. Praxis AI is running a supply-side version of the same move: if humanoid and VLA labs -- Physical Intelligence, Generalist AI, Figure -- must license real-world egocentric capture at $100M/year per vendor (Forbes, Dec 2025), then a company embedded in 150+ environment types across 60k workers is potentially the workflow layer every buyer eventually integrates against. That is exactly the "an index on X" template. Where Praxis AI diverges from the Stripe analogue is exclusivity: Stripe's rail position was earned by developer-channel compounding; Praxis AI's rail position is asserted via conglomerate embeds that are not named, not dated, and not disclosed as exclusive, and Scale AI at $29B with Meta's 49% stake (TSG Invest) can outbid on any of those contracts the moment they become visible.

The Harvey-clone pass is my template for the revenue-velocity floor -- a company in a category with declared leaders, pitching at consensus prices, without the $3B ? $5B ? $8B curve (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) that separates "just working" from "false signal." Praxis AI presents the same shape from the other direction: "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss #863, April 2026), and the dossier's Revenue Signal row literally reads "No public data found." In the physical-AI data market Scale AI has already logged >100,000 humanoid training hours and Micro1 posted $100M ARR in eight months (Forbes, Dec 2025) -- so silence from Praxis AI on any comparable number is a signal, not a stage default. The 60k-workers figure is a *capacity* claim, not a buying-behavior claim, and I have learned not to confuse the two.

Praxis AI's market-re-opening dimension compounds well over 18 months if the "why now" holds: humanoid foundation-model training has moved from scraped web data to real-world egocentric capture as the binding constraint (MIT Technology Review, Apr 2026), and robotics raised $55.8B YTD 2026 (KraneShares) -- that is a named, recent, technological catalyst on the scale of defense post-Ukraine. The mechanism that either validates or kills Praxis AI over the next six quarters is whether the buy-side consolidates onto one or two data vendors before Praxis AI can convert its capacity into contracted supply -- Scale AI's Meta relationship is already that consolidation event beginning.

Praxis AI's founder-shipping dimension would flip a tier only if the dossier named a specific artifact any of the three founders had built and iterated on with users. Rohan Seelamsetty is described as "#1 on Indian national debate team, best speaker/winner of ICYD 2020 and 2021" (The Wire) -- that is promoter-vector credential, not shipping-vector credential. Dev Karpe is Wharton Huntsman with F500 supply-chain ops. Tommy Li is the most operationally interesting -- ex-a16z industrial-tech, ex-Morgan Stanley PE, published IEEE researcher at NASA JPL and PerceptIn Robotics -- but "published IEEE researcher" is not a shipped product with a user-feedback loop, and the praxisrobotics.io website exposes only the tagline "Great machines will be forged by human intuition" (WebFetch, Jul 2026). Perplexity's Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built; I need to see that rhythm on Praxis AI before the founder dimension scores in the top half.

The single-miracle test parallels **Color Genomics** (2015), which I architected as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing precisely so the miracle was distribution, not chemistry. Praxis AI's stated architecture is the same shape -- workflow infrastructure on top of commodity capture hardware and existing worker fleets. But when I list the independent miracles required, I count more than one: (1) conglomerates permit worker-captured video and 3D scans of proprietary facilities to be resold at scale, (2) labor/privacy/IP frameworks in 5 continents clear for that resale, and (3) the resulting dataset is differentiated enough that frontier labs pay for it rather than for Scale AI's already-100k-hour humanoid corpus. That is a three-miracle chain compounded against a $29B incumbent.

Forecast: within 36 months Praxis AI is either acquired by Scale AI or Micro1 in a low-nine-figure aqui-supply deal for whichever conglomerate contracts turned out to be real and durable, or it is dead when the 60k-worker capacity claim fails to convert into signed exclusive supply against Scale AI's Meta-backed cheque book. I do not see the independent nine-figure ARR outcome from here, because the buy-side is too concentrated and too well-served, and there is no disclosed revenue curve to update on.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Tommy Li's a16z + NASA JPL + PerceptIn background is a genuinely rare stack that maps to the exact buyer graph -- Physical Intelligence, Generalist AI, Cobot are all in the a16z industrial-tech orbit -- and Karpe's F500 CEO-office supply-chain role is the exact network to sign conglomerate exclusivity that Scale AI's SF-recruited gig fleet structurally cannot replicate (TSG Invest). If the "embedded in publicly listed and unicorn-scale conglomerates" claim resolves into even one real exclusive multi-year supply contract, this is a Scale AI acquisition at nine figures inside 24 months -- and my own **Anduril** (2022) exception is precedent for backing a workflow-infrastructure-on-hard-thing play at a premium when the catalyst window is open. The dismantle is that neither the conglomerate names nor the buyer contracts are disclosed, the founder set has no shipped artifacts to point at, and in this cycle real growth is trivially easy to disclose -- so the silence is the data. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a publicly announced multi-year data-supply agreement with a named Fortune 500 conglomerate OR a first paid contract with Physical Intelligence, Figure, or Generalist AI within six months.
