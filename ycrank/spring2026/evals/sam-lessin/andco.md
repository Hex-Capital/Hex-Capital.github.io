# Andco -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Andco is the company my Pass Example was warning me about, almost on the nose: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $12M oversubscribed pre-seed led by YC and A.Capital, Joe Montana / Liquid 2, SV Angel, Immad Akhund, Kevin Hartz, Jude Gomila on the cap table." The dimensional signature is a repeat-YC founder team with genuine domain depth — Michael Lemm came out of Kirkland & Ellis and Ryan Xue already exited Layup (W23) to Whatfix — pinned to the lowest possible category-sleeve fit in my framework, which is agent-shaped SaaS at a consensus YC seed price into a vertical EvenUp has already taken to $2B. The founders are good. The round is the trade I do not make.

EvenUp is the ladder rung that decides Andco for me on category. EvenUp raised $385M, hit $2B in October 2025, runs across 2,000+ PI firms and 10,000 cases a week, and Lightspeed publicly reports 90% of new sales coming from AI products — which means the consensus that "AI eats personal injury workup" was fully priced into the Series E twelve months before Andco's seed. Eve closed $103M at >$1B from a16z stacking the same thesis. Andco's stated differentiation — upstream multi-channel retrieval across fax, mail, voice — is a real wedge, but it sits one product cycle away from EvenUp shipping the same retrieval layer on top of its existing 2,000-firm distribution. Sublime Security worked because I bought enterprise email security before the consolidation wave; Andco is the inverse, where I am being asked to underwrite the workup layer after the wave has already named its winner.

Founder-as-brand is the dimension where Andco posts a near-zero. Lifestyle vloggers are the Slow Creator Fund anti-pattern — no public writing, no owned distribution, no community pre-existing the company by two years — and Andco's three founders collectively show zero Twitter accounts found, zero public GitHub, no newsletter, no podcast, no Discord, no Product Hunt footprint, no company social count retrievable. Distribution is in-person prospecting with donut boxes — which I respect as Wizard-of-Oz hustle, but it is paid-acquisition-by-foot, not owned. Nothing in the Andco dossier resembles the John Fish / Bookshelved pattern where the creator's audience was the asset that justified the check. The donut-box story is charming; it is not 10K engaged free-acquired users.

Round dynamics compound the wrong direction over 18 months for Andco. "Oversubscribed $12M pre-seed led by YC and A.Capital" with Montana, SV Angel, Akhund, Hartz, Gomila layered in is the exact heat signature my Value Seed Investing essay describes as deselection — when this many tier-one names converge at seed, the price has already absorbed the upside narrative, and the regatta tacks ahead (Series A into EvenUp's shadow, Series B against Eve's a16z relationship) get harder, not easier, because each subsequent round must be marked against incumbents who are two product generations further down their curves. Andco will need four to five tacks to reach default-alive against well-funded competitors, and each tack is priced relative to EvenUp's $2B comp, which means dilution compounds against the founders precisely when they should be regaining leverage.

Capital structure for Andco would flip a tier if the round were a per-case rake or revenue-share instrument instead of a standard SAFE — that is the counterfactual. PI firms charge contingency, and an "agent-as-staff" priced per resolved case would be a genuinely venture-shaped non-standard instrument that incumbent CMS vendors like Filevine and Litify literally cannot underwrite on their seat-based P&Ls (the Unstated Pattern I keep returning to). Nothing in the Andco dossier indicates the founders pursued that structure; the demo-gated "Book a Demo" GTM and standard equity round read as a generic SaaS cap table being force-fit onto a workflow that has natural rake-based unit economics. Teamshares got my check because Joe Silberzweig structured around the asset; Andco appears to have structured around the YC default.

Single-anchor parallel on conviction: Ryan Xue's Layup-to-Whatfix exit and Lemm's Kirkland tenure give Andco real founder-market fit — that is the Drop.io-arc signal that I weight, and it is the dimension where Andco scores meaningfully above the absent-evidence default. But conviction-as-temperament, in my framework, is visible in public artifacts before the round: opinionated writing on the category, multi-year obsession with the problem. The Andco dossier returns "no Twitter found" for all three founders, no GitHub, no essays on PI workflow. The Layup exit demonstrates execution; it does not demonstrate the missionary temperament for the seven-year non-linear arc against EvenUp's war chest. I take the founders seriously. I do not yet see the public artifact that says they will not be talked out of this when EvenUp ships the retrieval layer.

My 36-month forecast for Andco: acqui-hired or tuck-in acquired by Filevine, Litify, or EvenUp itself in the $25M–$60M range, with the upstream retrieval IP folded as a feature and the founders rolled for 18 months — Andco does not reach $20M ARR standalone because EvenUp's distribution into 2,000+ firms compresses the window before Andco can install switching costs. This is the natural endpoint of the signature: strong founders, wrong sleeve, consensus-priced round, no owned distribution to outrun the incumbent.

The strongest objection is that Andco is actually a Sublime Security analog — pre-consensus enterprise infrastructure inside a fragmented buyer base (50,435 PI firms, no firm above 5% share per IBISWorld 2025), already live across multiple high-volume firms doing "hundreds of cases and thousands of requests monthly," with David Lieb as a YC partner publicly endorsing and Lemm's Kirkland network providing inside-baseball distribution. Bull case says EvenUp built the demand-package layer and Andco is building the unsexy pipes underneath it, which is exactly the kind of category-bisection I miss when I default to the EvenUp comp. The dismantle: Sublime entered before the consolidation wave was named; Andco's $12M oversubscribed round with seven named angels and YC + A.Capital co-leading is the wave naming itself in real time, and the IBISWorld fragmentation cuts both ways because EvenUp has already proven 2,000-firm penetration is achievable from the demand-package wedge. Flip condition: if Andco re-prices the seed at $10M post-money with a per-case rake structured into the commercial terms and the founders publish a public thesis on why upstream retrieval is structurally separable from downstream workup, I write the check. Until one of those, Andco is the trade I sit out.
