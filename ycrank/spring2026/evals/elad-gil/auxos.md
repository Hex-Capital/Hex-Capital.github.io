# Auxos -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Auxos is a clean single-miracle architecture entering a category where someone else has already claimed the index spot -- Ashton Daniel, Kerry Lu, and Jerry Wu are betting LLM persona-simulation fidelity is the only miracle they need, but Aaru already closed a $1B Series A in December 2025 (TechCrunch) and Evidenza is sitting on 100+ enterprise customers including Salesforce and GE while bootstrapped. The architecture is right; the timing puts Auxos somewhere between third and seventh in a race that consensus has already priced.

On my highest-weighted dimension -- market re-opening via specific recent catalyst -- the ladder runs from **Anduril** (2022) at the top, where Ukraine cracked Pentagon software procurement in a clean 24-month window, down through **Decagon** (2023) in the middle, where post-LLM customer support was a real but more diffuse re-opening, to **Lyft** Series C at the bottom, where the catalyst was steady-state rideshare growth and the math just didn't pencil. Auxos sits between Decagon and Lyft: the LLM-fidelity threshold for aggregate-level persona simulation crossed in 2023-2024 (PyMC Labs cites 85-95% aggregate accuracy at ~10% of cost), HBR validated the category in November 2025, and Aaru's $1B mark is the institutional signal. That is a real catalyst -- but it fired 24+ months ago and the window's index position has been bought. When I ran the *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy in November 2025, synthetic research was already drifting from "wide open" toward "leaders declared." Auxos is entering after the consensus, without the revenue curve that lets you pay up anyway.

On my lowest-rated dimension -- revenue velocity -- the ladder is **Harvey** ($3B → $5B → $8B in months, TechCrunch Nov 2025) at the top, **Perplexity** (2022) in the middle where the usage curve was visible but not Harvey-shaped, and at the bottom every AI pre-seed where the dossier is silent. Auxos sits at the bottom rung: no customer logos, no revenue disclosed, no Product Hunt launch, no press, no signed LOIs, no community signals. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself information -- "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). Daniel has the BCG Fortune 500 rolodex to convert, but the dossier doesn't show a single named buyer, which is exactly what the *false signal vs. just working* test asks for.

On founder shipping -- where **Perplexity**'s Aravind Srinivas is the high rung (built a thing between every meeting), **Brex**'s Henrique Dubugras is the mid rung (operationally disciplined but more conventional), and the McKinsey-pedigree promoter is the low rung -- Auxos splits the team. Kerry Lu shipped the "fastest seismic wave simulator in the world" at Vorticity and Amazon Ads simulation infrastructure modeling 40% of US e-commerce traffic; Jerry Wu shipped Meta Reels/Feed ranking infra cited at $100M+ incremental revenue. Those are legitimate shipped artifacts. Daniel, the CEO, is a BCG "Incoming Associate" with NCAA fencing credentials and no named shipped artifact -- closer to the promoter rung. The fencing-team co-founder graph is a real bond but doesn't substitute for a CEO who has shipped a product to users.

On index position -- **Stripe** at the top as the index on e-commerce, **Decagon** in the middle as the workflow-infra-in-an-open-market template, and **Lyft** at the bottom as #2 in a winner-take-all -- Auxos lands near Lyft. Six named direct competitors (Aaru, Evidenza, Viewpoints.ai, Synthetic Users, Brox.ai, Synthelos), with the leader at $1B valuation and a bootstrapped profitable player at 100+ Fortune-500-grade logos. Auxos has to win share *against* those names, not ride past them. On single-miracle architecture -- **Color Genomics** at the top with workflow on commodity sequencing isolating the miracle cleanly, **Anduril** in the middle with software on existing autonomy stacks, fusion startups at the bottom -- Auxos actually scores well: the one miracle is LLM-driven persona accuracy at aggregate-level marketing decisions, and that miracle has already been validated by PyMC Labs and HBR. The flip side: a miracle that's commoditized doesn't moat the company, which loops back to why the index dimension is so weak.

My 36-month forecast: Auxos lands at under $2M ARR, fails to raise a venture-scale Series A, and exits via acqui-hire to a mid-tier marketing-research incumbent (Qualtrics, Forsta, or a better-funded synthetic peer like Aaru rolling up the long tail) below $40M. The mechanism is direct: the category catalyst already fired, the index position is taken by Aaru on funding and Evidenza on customers, the founder graph carries genuine simulation chops but a non-shipping CEO, and there is no disclosed revenue curve to override the structural read.

The strongest objection: Evidenza proves a bootstrapped enterprise-research wedge into Fortune 500 marketing budgets works, and Daniel's BCG Fortune 500 network is exactly the distribution channel Evidenza rode. Lu and Wu have simulation and ML-ranking infra chops that exceed anything I can verify on Evidenza's team. If Daniel converts even a thin slice of his BCG accounts, Auxos becomes a $10-20M ARR profitable business inside 36 months. The dossier evidence that wins against this case: Evidenza got there bootstrapped, meaning the venture math requires Auxos to *out-grow* Evidenza meaningfully -- and Auxos has zero disclosed logos versus Evidenza's named Salesforce and GE, while Aaru's $1B mark sets a valuation ceiling Auxos has to chase. A "great small business" outcome is not a venture outcome. One verifiable condition flips me: five named Fortune 500 paid contracts inside nine months -- the same "just working" signature I tracked on Decagon and Harvey. Absent that, Auxos is the #3+ entrant in a market where the index seat is filled.
