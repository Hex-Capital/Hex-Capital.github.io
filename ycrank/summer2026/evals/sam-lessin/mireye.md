# Mireye -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Mireye is the textbook 2026 YC AI-infra seed I trained myself to pass on before opening the dossier: "Infrastructure for Physical World AI Agents" is a category label I have publicly called "just more internet," and Mireye is two founders wrapping a Model Context Protocol server around public USGS and NOAA datasets in a market where CARTO, Mapbox, and Google Maps Platform have already shipped their own MCP endpoints in 2025-2026. The high/low signature on Mireye is competent-data-infra-operators (Kapoor's six years at Tools for Humanity and Lyra Health is real) building in the exact sleeve I've re-weighted away from at the worst possible price-discipline moment.

The closest portfolio analogy on **non-consensus pricing** is **Solana** at $0.05, where the category had been abandoned by every generalist post-ICO crash and there were no bidders to compete with -- that's where my edge lives. Mireye is the opposite shape: an S26 YC AI-agent-infrastructure company in a batch where roughly a third of the cohort is some flavor of "MCP server for X," which means Mireye is being priced into the exact consensus heat curve I de-rate. The dossier names CARTO ($92M raised, $28.9M ARR), Mapbox ($220M+ historical raise), xMap ($4M+ pre-seed with 200-country coverage), and Google Maps Platform all shipping the same MCP surface Mireye is wedging into -- this isn't a category two tier-1 funds quietly passed on, this is a category every incumbent is racing into in public. No round-size or price disclosed in the dossier, and I treat that silence as consensus by default.

**Capital structure** is where the Mireye pitch dies most quietly. The failure mode my Pass Example catalogues -- "standard SaaS cap table that will need five more rounds to reach default-alive" -- maps onto Mireye almost line for line: a usage-based API business competing on price and breadth against Google Maps Platform requires either continuous fundraising to subsidize developer acquisition or a strategic acquirer to absorb it before the burn curve crosses Google's free tier. There is no signal in the Mireye dossier of an unusual instrument (no revenue share, no holdco, no employee-equity wrapper), no signal of a bootstrappable revenue floor, and no signal that Kapoor and Chokshi have considered any financing tack other than the YC default SAFE. Teamshares this is not.

**Founder conviction** -- mechanism analysis over 18 months: Chokshi's Poker Pit arc (reportedly $250K ARR in four months pre-acquisition) and Seismic ML lead role plus Kapoor's data-infra tenure at Tools for Humanity gives Mireye real shipping credibility, but operator credibility compounds into a moat only when it's paired with an opinionated public stance on the category that competitors cannot copy. I searched the dossier for any writing, talks, or public artifact from Chokshi or Kapoor on geospatial agent grounding, citation auditability for AI, or why federal data beats global POI data, and the answer is nothing -- two Twitter handles (@ansh_chokski, @shshwt_) with follower counts not even retrievable. Eighteen months from now the version of Mireye that breaks out is the one where Chokshi has been the public voice on "why agents hallucinate about places" -- and there is zero leading indicator of that today.

**Founder-as-brand** -- counterfactual: for Mireye to flip a tier on this dimension, one of Chokshi or Kapoor would need to be running a substack or YouTube on geospatial-for-agents with a four-figure engaged subscriber base, or Mireye would need to demonstrate a Discord or developer community pre-existing the YC batch -- the John Fish / Bookshelved test or the Slow Creator Fund filter. The Mireye dossier has neither. This is a B2B infra company sold through MCP server listings, which is the same channel xMap and Mapbox already occupy, so there is no founder-distribution wedge to underwrite even if I wanted to make this a creator-shaped deal.

**Category sleeve** -- single-anchor parallel: contrast Mireye to **Aleo**, where I bought into pre-consensus crypto infrastructure at a moment most generalist funds had quit the category and the underlying primitive (zk privacy) had no incumbent racing to ship the same SDK. Mireye sits at the inverse coordinate -- foundation-model-adjacent agent infrastructure in 2026 is the most-crowded sleeve in venture, and the underlying data (USGS, NOAA, Census) is public domain that any team with ETL can replicate. "AI is just more internet" was a deliberate provocation but operationally it means I'm not the buyer for Mireye at any YC-batch-median price.

My 36-month forecast on Mireye: acqui-hire by Mapbox, CARTO, or a mid-tier geospatial player at sub-$15M, or quiet wind-down after failing to land paid integrations against Google Maps Platform's free MCP grounding; Mireye ARR stays under $500K through 2028. The mechanism is straightforward -- the underlying data is public, the channel (MCP listings) is shared with every incumbent, and the founders haven't built the conviction artifacts that would let Mireye out-narrate Google in a developer's tool-selection screen.

The strongest case against my pass on Mireye is that focused startups historically beat incumbents' checkbox MCP shipments -- Google Maps Platform announcing an MCP endpoint in a blog post is not the same as Google caring, and Kapoor's six years of normalization work at Tools for Humanity is exactly the unsexy ETL labor that produces a real schema moat if agents start demanding citation-grade geospatial answers for insurance, climate-risk, or wildfire underwriting. That bull case stings because it's the Wizard-of-Oz pattern I claim to look for. It loses to one dossier fact: Mireye is US-only while xMap already ships 200-country coverage at a $600K revenue run-rate, which means the "focused startup beats sleepy incumbent" story requires Mireye to first beat a more-focused competitor that is already further along. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Mireye lands three named enterprise integrations (insurance carrier, climate-risk underwriter, or federal contractor) at $50K+ ARR each within 12 months of YC Demo Day -- absent that, Mireye is a pass.
