# Maquoketa Research -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Maquoketa Research is what a hardware-defense team looks like when the technical depth is real but every other axis I underwrite on is absent -- Alejandro Hernandez doing 140 GHz MIMO phased-array work at the Berkeley Wireless Research Center is the highest-signal fact in the dossier, and "no public Twitter, no GitHub, no writing, no community, customer is DoD" is the lowest-signal stack. That pairing tells me exactly what Maquoketa Research is: a competent RF-engineering shop being force-fit into a consensus 2026 procurement window, sold to me through the most-legible buyer on earth (the Pentagon). That's the inverse of the asset class I buy.

Solana at $0.05 is the right ladder anchor for how I think about round pricing, because in 2018-2019 the consensus had quit the category and I could price the underlying without competing against momentum. Maquoketa Research sits at the opposite end of that arc -- Anduril at a $30.5B valuation (June 2025), Shield AI marked at $12.7B (March 2026, +140% in one print), the Pentagon publicly committing to ~30,000 OWA drones at ~$5,000 a unit, and a March 2026 "drone swarm crucible" exercise. Defense autonomy in spring 2026 is the loudest consensus trade in the YC batch behind general-purpose AI agents. The dossier doesn't disclose Maquoketa Research's round size or post-money, and silence on price in a screaming category is exactly the absent-evidence case I punish; I'd bet the deck reads "oversubscribed" by demo day.

The Pass-Example founder is the right comparison for distribution: "$25K MRR, three months out of YC, four tier-1 VCs already in, no public writing I can find" -- I'm out before the meeting. Maquoketa Research's founders trace a milder version of the same pattern on the brand/community axis. Evan Yeager has a LinkedIn and an ex-derivatives-trading resume from SpiderRock; Aidan Williams and David Muchow have no retrievable public footprint at all; nobody is writing about loitering-munition autonomy under their own name. The dossier even flags that "Maquoketa" collides with an Iowa town and a geological formation, meaning the founders haven't bothered to claim the name in search. For a DoD-procurement company that may be rational -- the customer is a contracting officer, not a Discord -- but it means zero of the $60M Slow Creator Fund machinery applies and the founder-as-brand cap-table feature is just structurally not on offer.

Ro is the single-anchor parallel for capital structure, and it's the closest Maquoketa Research gets to a Known Exception I'd ride. Ro violated my capital-efficiency rule because 50-state prescription rails were genuine regulatory schleps that only VC dollars could clear -- capital intensity *was* the moat. Maquoketa Research has a structurally similar pitch: ITAR controls, DoD vendor qualification, and Replicator/Drone Dominance program gating mean the procurement schlep is real. The problem is Maquoketa Research is *not* named in the publicly reported Replicator selectees -- AeroVironment, Swarm Aero, Performance Drone Works are. So the Ro override requires the regulatory schlep to be cleared by *this* company, not just to exist in the category, and the dossier shows the schlep being cleared by everyone else.

Hernandez's mmWave phased-array research is the mechanism analysis for founder conviction: over 18 months, RF/seeker IP either compounds into a defensible subsystem that primes want to buy (the realistic exit) or it gets out-spent by Anduril's Lattice integration of equivalent talent. The dossier gives me Hernandez's domain depth but nothing on Yeager beyond "led BD and computer vision R&D" and a derivatives-trading prior -- and Muchow's bio is literally described as "confusing-to-recruiters." Missionary-temperament conviction in my book shows up as essays, threads, or 3+ years pre-funding obsession with the category; Maquoketa Research shows me one researcher's publication record and three resumes.

Counterfactual for category sleeve: what would have to be true for defense to register as a Slow sleeve? The revealed portfolio is pre-consensus crypto (Lightning Labs, Aleo, Klaytn, Fold), DTC consumer (Allbirds, Hipcamp, Everlane), prescription-rail health (Ro, PillPack, Truepill), and named-creator (Bookshelved, Mogilko). Defense primes and defense autonomy are conspicuously absent. For Maquoketa Research to fit, I'd need to be telling myself loitering munitions are "illegible" the way Solana was at $0.05 -- but a $5.37B-growing-to-$24B market with Pentagon publishing unit economics in Defense One is the most-legible thing I've read this batch. Defense isn't my sleeve; it's the consensus 2026 venture trade with better PR.

My 36-month forecast for Maquoketa Research: alive but sub-scale, $2-5M in prototype/development contract revenue, no Replicator production award, and the most likely exit is an acqui-hire of Hernandez's RF/phased-array team by Anduril or AeroVironment at a price that returns nothing meaningful to a pre-seed cap table -- because the autonomy IP itself is duplicative with what Shield AI's $240M-Mar-2025 round and the $12.7B March 2026 mark have already funded internally. The mechanism is straightforward: at $5,000/unit Pentagon procurement, gross margin doesn't fund the next financing tack, and a hardware company that misses Replicator selection on round one has to wait 18-24 months for the next window while burning seed capital on flight tests.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass is the Ro override: capital intensity is the moat, ITAR/DoD qualification creates real switching costs, Hernandez's BWRC pedigree is genuinely scarce, and "Pentagon buying 30,000 units" is a $150M revenue opportunity that even fractional capture funds the next round. Fine -- but Ro had Zachariah Reitano running 50-state prescription rails as a personal mission with public writing on telehealth, and Maquoketa Research has neither the public conviction artifact nor the Replicator slot that's the equivalent regulatory tollbooth. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Maquoketa Research named in a Replicator Tranche 3 selection or equivalent DoD program-of-record award above $10M before March 2027. Until then, this is a hardware company priced into a consensus category with no Slow-shaped asymmetry I can underwrite.
