# Probe -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

When I read Probe, I see the exact silhouette of the pass I described in my own playbook -- a competent Berkeley EECS engineer with Databricks and Apple ML internships building "agent-native incident management" on top of a $14.34B IT/DevOps software category (Market Reports World 2026) where incident.io already raised $62M at a $400M valuation explicitly to ship the same AI SRE feature (TechCrunch April 2025). Probe's highest-scoring dimension is founder learning rate -- and only by default, because Mahit Namburu has no documented pivot, only a declined Together AI MLE offer -- and Probe's lowest-scoring dimension is consequence magnitude. That high/low pairing diagnoses Probe as a credentialed-engineer optimization play inside an incumbent-occupied workflow category. That is the Pass Example shape, not the OpenAI shape.

On consequence magnitude, the high rung is **Impossible Foods** (2011), where Pat Brown's success-state was animal-protein production disappearing -- a named incumbent regime collapsing, not improving. Probe's success-state, taken at maximum ambition from the YC page, is "the agent is the primary actor rather than a co-pilot inside a human workflow" -- which is not a category collapse but a re-skinning of incident.io's and Rootly's existing per-seat ARR pool. The mechanism Probe would have to break is on-call human labor, fine, but the dossier never names what disappears -- no diesel long-haul, no animal slaughter, no human radiology read. A $14.34B TAM at 15.74% CAGR is exactly the "slice of an existing $X billion market" framing I use as a low-rung anchor. Probe places below the **DoorDash** (2013) mid-rung because DoorDash at least reshaped consumer-commerce flow; Probe rearranges the seating chart on the bridge of incident.io.

On founder defiance of expert consensus, the low rung is the Pass Example founder who "spent eight years at Salesforce" and reapplies the playbook. Probe's pattern is structurally identical with a different employer label -- Mahit Namburu's documented arc is Berkeley EECS plus SpecDec research, Databricks LoRA inference, Apple machine translation, and a declined Together AI MLE offer (YC page). That is expert continuity inside the LLM stack, not defiance of it. The dossier surfaces no controversial public position, no "instigator" moment from my TED Talk framing, no thesis Mahit has publicly bet against the consensus of his field. A 2014 McKinsey-style failed forecast would land here: the experts will not be wrong about incident management because the founder is one of the experts.

On technical and capital risk that repels consensus VCs, the mechanism over 18 months runs the wrong direction for Probe. Retrieval-grounded LLM agents over logs, code, and runbooks is precisely the workload every Tier-1 generalist will fund this year, and arXiv 2508.14231 already documents agent unreliability in production triage -- meaning the risk is execution-quality, not a falsifiable scientific miracle whose resolution would create a moat. Probe has zero capital intensity in the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) sense; a $2-5M seed gets to a product, which is the opposite of the $115M Series A I led when no one else would touch fusion. There is no plant, no fleet, no foundation-model training run -- only API calls and a knowledge graph.

On required-capital match, the counterfactual is sharp: for Probe to flip a tier on this dimension, the roadmap would need to require >$50M to first commercial milestone, which would imply Probe is training a proprietary incident-response foundation model on a large labeled corpus of resolved incidents -- a credible thesis nobody in the dossier has articulated. As written, Probe is commodity SaaS economics where I am over-resourced and Sequoia, Greylock, or a16z is equally well-placed. That is the explicit low-rung anchor in my own framework.

On founder learning rate, the single-anchor parallel is **Slash** (2024) -- the $100M Series C I wrote because the founder's publicly told Kanye West pivot demonstrated a learning-rate signal strong enough to override the consequence-magnitude prior on consumer business banking. Probe has no equivalent. The dossier records exactly one career inflection -- declining a Together AI MLE offer -- which is a hiring decision, not a thesis update. With only Mahit named on a three-person team and the other two co-founders unidentified, the dossier cannot even tell me whose learning curve I am betting on. I default to mid because the company history is short, but the Slash override does not apply.

My 36-month forecast for Probe: acquihired by incident.io, Rootly, or PagerDuty for under $25M, or quietly shut down with ARR below $1M, because the agent-native positioning is a feature roadmap incident.io will ship inside its existing enterprise distribution before Probe builds one. The mechanism is straightforward -- incident.io's $9M ARR (GetLatka 2025) and existing customer base means the same AI SRE capability ships with zero new-logo acquisition cost, while Probe must displace those incumbents from a standing start with three people and no disclosed GTM.

The strongest objection is the **Slash** (2024) known exception: YC pedigree plus a documented founder learning-rate signal can override my consequence-magnitude prior, and Probe sits inside the YC Spring 2026 batch with David Lieb as partner and a technical founder credentialed at the two most relevant AI infrastructure shops. The bear case dismantles it on dossier specifics -- Slash's override hinged on a publicly told pivot story; Probe's dossier contains no pivot, no thesis update, no "we were wrong about X" moment, only linear credential accumulation. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Probe ships a public benchmark showing autonomous-remediation precision/recall on a real production incident corpus that meaningfully beats incident.io's AI SRE -- a falsifiable technical claim, exactly the kind of "earn the right" evidence my 2013 TechCrunch op-ed demanded. Until then Probe scores roughly where the Pass Example scored, and for the same reasons.
