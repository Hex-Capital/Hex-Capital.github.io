# transload -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Julius Scheel is a McKinsey Fellow who scaled avi medical to seven-figure ARR; Nils Börner co-authored an IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging paper; Jago Wahl-Schwentker was the first engineer at Veli doing Bluetooth localization for care homes — and not one of these three founders has ever stood on a loading dock at 4 a.m. watching a forklift driver argue with a shipper about a mis-declared pallet. transload's signature in my portfolio is *clean cofounder governance wrapped around a memo-discovered problem*, and the fact that transload.io renders only "0% BOOTING" the day I am asked to evaluate it tells me everything about which end of that signature is load-bearing.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is my HIGH rung on single-CEO clarity — one named face, tattooed conviction, no ambiguity about who breaks ties when defense procurement gets ugly. transload gets partial credit: Scheel is the unambiguous CEO, which keeps transload out of the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll collapse zone where I have publicly said multiple co-founders are "a recipe for disaster." But transload diverges from the Anduril pattern by splitting the technical authority across two CTO titles — "CTO AI" for Börner and "CTO Engineering" for Wahl-Schwentker — and that split is where governance debt accumulates the first time a model-accuracy decision conflicts with a shipping-deadline decision. Three TUM classmates with adjacent titles and no documented operating agreement is the GameCrush (~2011) failure mode in slow motion, not the Anduril one.

A B2B API whose value can only be evaluated via founder testimony is my LOW rung on physical-world verifiability — the Pass Example I gave for the consensus YC AI darling I would not write into. The failure mechanism is that I cannot install it, photograph it, hand it to a friend at dinner, or read its Wi-Fi name across a Four Seasons café the way I read Flock Safety's (~2018). Does that pattern appear in transload's dossier? Yes, in the most literal form I have ever seen: the public website is a placeholder animation as of May 2026, no Product Hunt page, no Twitter, no Discord, no public artifact a normal human can observe in the wild. I source from my physical environment; transload has built nothing in my physical environment to source from.

On *founder surfaced from lived experience*, the mechanism that worries me is how the deficit compounds across 18 months of LTL sales calls. Chase Lochmiller could walk a North Dakota well pad because he was *of* that world; transload's founders will walk a Cleveland 3PL dock as TUM-trained consultants asking the dock supervisor to explain what an NMFC class is. The customer-quoted "15% of shipments turn out to be bigger than what the sender claimed — worth ~$50k per site per month" is a McKinsey-shaped sentence, not a sentence a freight forwarder would say unprompted, and that is the tell.

On *magically weird*, the single anchor is Crusoe (~2018), and transload is its inverse. Crusoe was three uninvestable things stapled together — crypto, oil and gas, rural industrial ops — that nobody was building. transload is one obvious thing — CV on existing CCTV for freight dimensioning — that Loadsmart already bought via its NavTrac acquisition in September 2023, that vMeasure and Vimaan PalletSCAN 3D and VITRONIC and FreightSnap FS 5000 and Cubiscan all ship in adjacent form. The dossier names six competitors before transload has named one customer. That is not the "find them before it becomes consensus" lane; that is arriving second to a category already being consolidated.

On *capital-intensive, unsexy-regulated swing*, the counterfactual that flips this dimension a full tier is NTEP / legal-for-trade certification. The dossier flags it as a risk and notes transload has not publicly addressed it. If transload were pursuing that certification path — the way Cubiscan and FreightSnap have for their hardware — the regulatory moat would compound the way Vigil Labs (August 2025) compounds in regulated finance, and the unsexy-regulated lane would do its work. As written, transload is software-only riding on cameras it does not own, in a category where rebilling without legal-for-trade weights and measures certification is the thing that makes the $50k/site/month number collapse on contact with a shipper dispute.

36-month forecast: transload reaches $400K–$1.5M ARR with two or three mid-tier 3PLs, fails to clear NTEP certification for direct rebilling, gets acqui-hired by Loadsmart into the NavTrac stack for a low-eight-figure outcome by mid-2028 — Börner and Wahl-Schwentker stay, Scheel leaves within a year for the next thing. The mechanism is straightforward: the dual-CTO structure holds long enough to ship a working pilot, the lived-experience deficit caps enterprise penetration, and the obvious strategic acquirer is named in transload's own competitor list.

The bull case that should sting: Börner's IEEE T-MI publication is not a vanity credential — medical imaging is exactly where you learn to extract metric information from low-quality occluded video, and that *is* transferable to warehouse CCTV in a way most YC CV teams cannot match. DeepMind (~2013) is the Known Exception precedent — Hassabis was pedigreed, not lived-experience, and I wrote the check anyway because polymath cross-domain compounding was the override. Could Börner be that? No — one IEEE paper is a credential, not a polymath stack the way chess + neuroscience + game design was for Hassabis, and "AI is the age of the polymath" cuts the other way here: a polymath generalist with a depth camera and a GPU could rebuild transload in a quarter. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is one signed LTL carrier paying for rebilling under a documented NTEP-certified pipeline within 12 months — until then, transload is the Pass Example with a German accent.
