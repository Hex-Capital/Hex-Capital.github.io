# HEVN, inc -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

HEVN, inc is the kind of company my Pass Example was almost written about: competent immigrant outsider founders — Peter Volnov, Nikita Briuzgin, Andrei Zhevlakov out of HOT Labs / HERE Wallet — attacking a derivative wedge ("Brex for international companies," per the YC page) in a category where Airwallex already prints $1.2B ARR. The dimensional signature is clear: HEVN, inc's highest rung is founder pedigree (a team that shipped HOT Wallet to 30M+ users per Chainwire 2024), and its lowest rung is improbable ambition — the company's own one-liner is a copy of an existing US neobank.

Consequence magnitude is where I start, and HEVN, inc lands far below the rungs that make me write checks. The HIGH rung here is OpenAI — civilizational consequence, the bet I sent an apology letter to my LPs over. The MID rung is DoorDash — restructuring food access for hundreds of millions, where Tony Xu's execution overrode my technical-novelty preference. The LOW rung is the YC AI-copilot-for-accountants archetype I described in my Pass Example — useful, substitutable, no world-change. HEVN, inc sits between MID and LOW: if it fully works, a non-US founder running Google ads stops paying a 2-3% FX spread and gets 0.5% cashback (gethevn.com). That is real money for that founder. It is not a different world. Cross-border SMB banking is a redistribution of existing spend, not the creation of new demand — and that is one of my explicit Red Flags.

Non-linear technical approach is where HEVN, inc is weakest on substance. The HIGH rung is Rocket Lab — Peter Beck's Rutherford engine, electric turbopumps, 3D-printed combustion chambers, $28M in that became $1.7B at IPO. The MID rung is DoorDash, an operational play with no novel science that I funded anyway because the consequence justified it. The LOW rung is the LLM-wrapper SaaS I pass on weekly. HEVN, inc sits squarely between MID and LOW: the dossier offers stablecoin payment rails, sponsor-bank integrations across EU/US/UAE, and a "soon" FDIC-insured no-EIN USD account (gethevn.com). That last item is the only genuinely novel hook, and the dossier itself flags it as the most replicable feature pending bank-partner contracts. There is no Rutherford engine here. There is regulatory plumbing.

Founders are the one rung where HEVN, inc has a real argument. The HIGH rung on this dimension is the Mach Industries founder — 19-year-old MIT dropout building weapons against Lockheed. The MID rung is Tony Xu, Chinese immigrant with no logistics resume who out-learned the incumbents. The LOW rung is the rehearsed-pitch domain expert reciting a playbook. HEVN, inc sits between MID and HIGH: Volnov, Briuzgin, and Zhevlakov are immigrant outsiders (ITMO University, Dubai-based design lead) who shipped a consumer crypto wallet from zero to 30M+ users (Chainwire, Nov 2024). That is real shipping velocity. The discount: none of the four has operated a US-regulated banking product, three are concurrently associated with HOT Labs which the dossier reports still operating into 2024-2025, and the fourth co-founder Pavel Mitsevich has no public footprint at all — that bandwidth split, sitting next to Mercury's December 2025 OCC charter application, makes me uneasy.

Improbable ambition is where HEVN, inc most directly fails my filter. The HIGH rung is Hermeus (Mach 5 passenger flight) and Commonwealth Fusion Systems — companies whose stated goal makes experts laugh. The MID rung is Instacart — hard, not impossible. The LOW rung is the YC company that frames itself as "X for Y." HEVN, inc literally pitches itself as "Brex for international companies" on its own YC page. That is a derivative anchor, not a provocation. Most informed observers would say "yes, that can be built" — which in my framework is the wrong answer. Path to radical affordability is similarly thin: Impossible Foods (HIGH) collapses the cost of protein; Guardant Health (MID) replaces invasive biopsy with a blood draw; HEVN, inc (between LOW and MID) saves a non-US founder a few hundred basis points on FX. That is a discount, not a free service. My 2026 framing of "free healthcare, free education, free legal services" does not extend to "slightly cheaper USD wires."

Forecast, 36 months: HEVN, inc reaches $3-8M ARR on interchange and FX spread, fails to escape Airwallex's gravitational field, and is either acquired sub-$75M by a mid-tier cross-border player (Payoneer, Rho) or quietly pivots back toward stablecoin payment rails where Volnov's HOT Labs distribution is actually a wedge. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: capable founders without an improbable goal converge on capable execution in a crowded market, which produces capable mid-cap outcomes — exactly the 3-5x return profile I said in my Pass Example is a waste of attention.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass — i.e. the bull case for HEVN, inc — runs through DoorDash as Known Exception. The argument: Volnov is the same archetype as Tony Xu, an immigrant outsider with planetary-scale shipping pedigree, and Capital One's pending $5.15B Brex acquisition (Airwallex blog, 2026) plus Mercury's OCC charter application (Dec 2025) genuinely de-prioritize the non-US SMB; consequence magnitude rises if you believe USD-banking-for-the-rest-of-the-world is a billion-customer market underserved by every US incumbent. It stings, and DoorDash is the right precedent to reach for. It loses on the dossier evidence: DoorDash created new demand (food orders that didn't previously happen); HEVN, inc is redistributing FX spread that already flows through Wise, Airwallex, and Payoneer at $1.2B ARR scale. The condition that flips my verdict: HEVN, inc ships the FDIC-insured no-EIN USD account live with a named US sponsor bank in 6 months, and pulls $1M+ MRR from non-crypto SMBs paying for ad spend — proving the regulatory wedge is real and the distribution doesn't depend on Volnov's prior crypto audience.
