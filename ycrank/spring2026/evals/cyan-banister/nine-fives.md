# Nine Fives -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Noah Levy spent seven years inside the most demanding RF shop on the planet — the engineer-of-record on the Falcon 9 Radar Altimeter, the Dragon 2 S-Band Transceiver, and the Starship GPS/S-Band radios — and he came out the other side angry enough at his test bench to go build a different one. That is the entire Nine Fives thesis in one sentence, and it is exactly the founder-origin shape I look for. The highest-dimension axis on Nine Fives is founder-surfaced-from-lived-experience; the lowest is magically-weird-non-consensus, because RF test instruments are unsexy in the workmanlike way, not aesthetically transgressive in the Crusoe-burning-gas-flares way. That pairing tells me what kind of company Nine Fives is: a Palmer-Luckey-of-picks-and-shovels bet, not a Chase-Lochmiller-arbitrage bet.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus before he shipped Anduril, and the reason I tattooed conviction on that 2017 check was that he had already sweated the headset supply chain in a garage before defense became the thesis. Nine Fives' Noah Levy is the analogous shape on the RF axis — the "January 1997 revision of the USB Human Interface Device specification" line in the YC launch is not a joke he heard at a dinner, it is a wound he carried out of seven years of Starship integration. Where Nine Fives diverges from the Anduril rung is consequence magnitude: defense reorders nation-states, RF test instruments reorder a $3.99B market growing 8.74% to $8.48B by 2034 (Precedence Research, 2025). Smaller ceiling, same biographical authenticity, and the 6× SpaceX "kickass award" detail is the kind of internal-recognition signal a deck cannot manufacture.

The magically-weird ladder is where Nine Fives sits closest to the consensus-YC-AI-darling I would pass on without finishing the deck. RF test equipment is not laughed at the way Crusoe burning North Dakota flares was laughed at — it is simply boring, and boring is a weaker form of non-consensus than weird. The "magically weird" filter caught Crusoe because credible VCs had passed on aesthetic grounds; the Nine Fives dossier surfaces zero competitive round dynamics, no named pass list, no investor signal at all, so I am applying the absent-evidence default. The saving grace is that the category is unsexy enough — RF test, PoE backplanes, SCPI compatibility shims — that I doubt six top funds are circling, which inverts the social-proof red flag in Nine Fives' favor even though it does not generate the "magically weird" upside.

Andrew Kurtz is named COO on Nine Fives' about-us page and Noah Levy is named CEO — the titles are pre-resolved, not the 50/50 GameCrush-shaped landmine that has burned me before. Over 18 months the failure mode this dimension would surface is the unspoken tiebreaker: two ex-SpaceX engineers, both used to a Musk-as-arbiter culture, suddenly having to be each other's arbiter on a product roadmap. The mechanism that protects Nine Fives is functional specialization — Noah owns the RF/software stack, Andrew owns the mechanical and manufacturing integration from his Fairing 2.0 and Aurora Flight Sciences staff-ME background — which is the structural inverse of two co-CEOs fighting over the same surface.

Counterfactual on capital-intensity: for Nine Fives' frontier-hardware score to flip a tier upward, the NineVue rack-config app and the LLM-readable skill.md layer would need to graduate from positioning artifacts on ninefives.com to a software-lock-in surface customers version-control their test racks against. Today Nine Fives is hardware-with-API; that is real and unsexy enough — 4 SKUs (PD8X, POE-ATTEN-6G, POE-SWITCH-6G, POE-PMETER-6G), 30-day MBG, 2-year warranty, $1,500 max storefront price — but it is not SpaceX-2008 capital-intensity. The buyers, though, are cellphone, drone, and satellite manufacturers, which means Nine Fives sells the picks to my kind of frontier-hardware portfolio without taking the rocket risk themselves.

Flock Safety is the single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability — I sourced that check by reading a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café, because the product was a tactile artifact existing in my physical environment before any pitch reached me. Nine Fives passes this filter as hard as any hardware company can: there is a Shopify storefront, four SKUs publicly listed, a $1,500 ceiling, and a 30-day money-back guarantee. I can order a POE-ATTEN-6G, rack it, point a REST client at it, and feel within ten seconds of plugging it in whether Noah's "within 10s of plugging it in" claim is real. That is the test no developer-tools API dossier ever passes.

My 36-month forecast on Nine Fives: $4M–$12M ARR built on direct sales to drone and satellite RF labs introduced through the YC network, with Keysight or NI/Emerson opening acquisition conversations in the $60M–$180M range once Nine Fives crosses 50 named customer manufacturers — the mechanism being classic disruption-from-below where incumbents who hold ~55% market share (Precedence Research) cannot ship driverless REST without cannibalizing the TestStand and Connection Expert software-services revenue that funds their channel. The forecast is consistent with the signature: high lived-experience founder pedigree generates the design-win flywheel, low magical-weirdness caps the outcome at picks-and-shovels acquisition rather than category-defining IPO.

The bear case that stings is the commoditization argument: DS Instruments already ships a 6 GHz 90 dB Ethernet-programmable attenuator at $649–$749, Mini-Circuits has >400 attenuator SKUs to 67 GHz, and the moat Nine Fives is selling is a software UX layer — NineVue plus skill.md — that Keysight could ship as a firmware update inside two quarters if they cared. The Nine Fives software stack has zero disclosed customer adoption, no LOIs, no logos, no Product Hunt launch, no revenue figures, and the YC launch was seven days old at research time. That is the bear paragraph, and it lands. What dismantles it is the innovator's-dilemma physics: Keysight and R&S enterprise revenue depends on bundled driver suites and Connection Expert seat licenses, so shipping driverless REST cannibalizes their highest-margin software line — they will not move fast, the way Cisco did not move fast on IronPort's perimeter-mail thesis until it was easier to buy us in 2007 than build it. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: if Keysight or Rohde & Schwarz announces an open-API REST-controlled instrument line before EOY 2026, the Nine Fives moat compresses to founder pedigree alone and I would not re-up at Series A.
