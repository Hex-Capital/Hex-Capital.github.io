# Markov -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Markov is a real catalyst sitting on a thin index position -- two India-origin operators (Harish Ashok, 17; Dev Mandal, IIT-M '27 ex-Sarvam) pointing a "human fleet" at computer-use data sourcing for a buyer set of four labs already being served by Surge ($1.2B 2024 revenue), Mercor ($10B valuation), and Scale (Meta 49% at $30B). The market re-opening is legitimate -- Anthropic publicly discussing $1B+ on RL environments in the next year is exactly the kind of named 24-month catalyst I look for. The problem is everything downstream of that catalyst: Markov shows no revenue, no logos, no shipping rhythm in the frontier-lab data category, and no index position in a category where the entrants are all racing the same four customers.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on catalyst, and it's where Markov's strongest dimension lives. Anduril worked because Ukraine was a *specific* 24-month re-opening of a market peers had written off, and the catalyst was big enough that being late to the wedge didn't matter. Markov's catalyst is structurally similar in shape -- Meta's June 2025 Scale AI investment caused Google and OpenAI to redirect labeling spend (TechCrunch, Sep 21, 2025), and Anthropic's stated $1B+ RL-environment budget is a named pool of dollars that did not exist 18 months ago. Where Markov diverges from the Anduril template is the entry posture: Anduril entered with shipped hardware references and Palmer Luckey's prior distribution muscle into DoD; Markov enters with a cal.com/dev-markov/30min booking link and 1,122 Twitter followers against Surge's existing Anthropic/OpenAI/Google/Meta contracts. The catalyst is real. The wedge is not yet visible.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure pattern on index position, and the mechanism that killed that bet was structural: #2 in a winner-take-all-or-oligopoly market captures a fraction of #1's economics regardless of execution quality (20VC interview). Markov's dossier reads adjacent to that pattern -- Surge AI does full-stack labeling plus RL environments for the exact lab customers, Mercor just raised at $10B with Felicis, Scale has Meta's balance sheet, and Applied Compute closed $20M Benchmark-led pre-launch specifically for RL environments. Frontier-lab data procurement is not winner-take-all, but it is oligopoly-shaped, and "Scale AI for robotics, with a human fleet in India" (Dev Mandal, X) is a positioning claim, not a distribution wedge. If the relevant comparison is Mercor's India workforce already operating against the same buyers, Markov is selling a feature, not a moat.

Revenue velocity for Markov compounds or fails on a single question over 18 months: does one of Anthropic/OpenAI/Google sign a recurring data contract, or does Markov stay in pilot purgatory while Surge harvests the budget? The dossier has zero disclosed revenue and zero named customers, which at pre-seed is stage-default and not penalizable on its own. What is penalizable is the structural setup: frontier-lab data deals are decided by procurement teams who already have Surge and Mercor on paper, and the "false signal vs. just working" distinction (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) here is whether Markov gets a recurring SOW or a one-off eval contract. Without that signal in the dossier, I default low -- in this cycle, real lab revenue is easy to disclose if it exists.

The founder shipping question for Markov flips a tier only under one specific counterfactual: if Harish Ashok or Dev Mandal had previously sold a data contract into a frontier lab, or had shipped a computer-use dataset publicly that labs were already evaluating. Zenith (voice assistant for hardware engineering) and Boldmoon Cloud (web hosting, age 13) are real shipped artifacts -- this is not a McKinsey-deck founder, which matters -- and Dev Mandal's Sarvam AI tenure is the most relevant data-pipeline exposure in the dossier. The miss is the rhythm-versus-frontier-lab-buyer gap: shipping a voice assistant at 17 predicts execution velocity, not procurement velocity inside Anthropic. The **Perplexity** (2022) shipping signal was Aravind building the thing we discussed by the next meeting; the Markov equivalent would be a public dataset benchmark drop between investor calls, which the dossier does not show.

Single-miracle architecture is the dimension where Markov scores cleanest on a single-anchor parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015): both are workflow infrastructure on top of a hard-tech layer rather than bets on the hard-tech layer itself. Markov does not need new model architectures, new chip economics, or new regulatory frameworks -- the assumption is that computer-use models need task data, which Surge benchmarks (>40% workplace task failure, ~30% enterprise agentic task solve rate, TechCrunch Oct 2025) have already validated. Remaining work is GTM and operations: recruit and train operators in India, build task pipelines, sell into procurement. That's one miracle, not three, and it's the right structural choice for the category.

My 36-month forecast for Markov: acqui-hired by Surge, Mercor, or Scale for $20-60M in a talent-and-workforce deal, or operating as a niche computer-use specialist at $3-12M ARR if one of Anthropic or OpenAI anchors. Not a defining outcome. The mechanism producing this is the dimensional signature -- strong catalyst, no index position, no velocity yet -- which historically resolves into "the incumbent buys the team to consolidate the workforce" rather than "the entrant becomes the category." This is the **Lyft** shape (oligopoly market, late entrant, no structural wedge) re-skinned for AI data vendors, not the **Anduril** shape (catalyst + shipping pedigree + structural moat in distribution).

The strongest bull case against this forecast is the India-fleet cost structure: if Markov can capture computer-use trajectories at materially lower unit cost than Surge's US-led operation, and if Anthropic's $1B budget genuinely fragments across multiple specialist vendors rather than consolidating on one full-stack provider, Markov can ride the catalyst into a real business -- and Dev Mandal's Sarvam AI background is exactly the network that opens that door. That case stings because the catalyst dollars are real and the buyer set is *not* winner-take-all the way rideshare was. It loses to the dossier evidence on three counts: Mercor already runs the India operator playbook at $10B, Scale and Surge have years of trust capital with the same labs, and Markov's discovery surface is a 1,122-follower X account against a name that collides with MarkovML and Level. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a named Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or Meta SOW disclosed in the next 90 days -- at that point Markov is selling into the right pool of dollars at the right cycle moment, and the velocity dimension goes from absent to live.
