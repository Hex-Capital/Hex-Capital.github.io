# Omi Health -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Sindu Chaparala helped scale Ritual to $250M+ ARR as Chief of Staff to its CEO. That's a credential most pre-seed founders would trade a kidney for, and it means she knows the exact playbook Omi Health is running — DTC subscription, wellness branding, millennial/Gen Z targeting, social media distribution. When I look at Omi Health, I see someone who mastered one playbook and is applying it to an adjacent vertical. That's a strong foundation for a business. It's not what I invest in. Ritual wasn't built by someone who'd been failed by the supplement industry in a moment of desperation — it was built by someone who understood how to sell to a demographic. Omi Health has the same DNA: it originated during the HBS Startup Bootcamp, and Sindu named it after her mini Goldendoodle. That's affection, not a crucible. Jakob Spiess brings repeat-founder experience from SeaCycle and a military background, which signals operational discipline, but his connection to veterinary care is equally absent. When I ask "what happened to you that made this idea find YOU?" — I hear a business school project, not a biographical inevitability.

"Function Health for Pets" is a pitch that makes every VC in the room nod. That's precisely the problem. Loyal has raised $150M+ to develop longevity drugs for dogs. Embark has $91M+ and an established brand in canine diagnostics. Zoetis — a $90B+ market cap company — already acquired Basepaws and sits on distribution infrastructure Omi Health can't match for a decade. "Pet humanization" isn't a contrarian thesis; it's on the cover of every consumer trends deck in venture capital right now. Nobody shifts in their chair when you say you're building preventive health testing for dogs. The AI vet chat layer uses generally available LLM technology — the dossier says this plainly. Lee would call this what it is: an AI wrapper on top of commodity lab testing, bundled into a subscription. I've been burned by that pattern before, and I'm not the investor who needs to learn the lesson a fourth time.

There's something beneath the surface worth naming: veterinary care IS an opaque system. Pricing is invisible, bloodwork results are incomprehensible to most pet owners, and the Mars Inc. veterinary empire (Banfield, VCA) profits from episodic, reactive visits — not from keeping your dog healthy enough to avoid the visit. Omi Health grazes this structure. But grazing isn't disrupting. Omi isn't making vet pricing transparent or restructuring the information flow between veterinary professionals and pet parents. It's selling a $500 annual membership to affluent dog owners who already spend heavily on premium pet products. Compare this to Affirm, which embedded transparent lending terms at the point of financial decision — that was structural. Omi Health adds a consumer wellness layer on top of the existing system without challenging the system's architecture. The 2,000 waitlist signups since March 18 suggest demand among the target demographic, but demand for a premium product among the already-privileged isn't the same as making an invisible tax visible to people who can't afford to see it.

The bull case deserves its due: if Omi Health captures longitudinal biomarker data on hundreds of thousands of dogs, those breed-specific health baselines become genuinely proprietary. Sindu's operational capability is real — she's done DTC health subscription scaling at the highest level, and that muscle memory matters in execution. The membership model with two annual tests creates natural retention and longitudinal switching costs. And the convergence of LLM-powered vet chat, normalized biomarker testing from Function Health's success, and generational pet spending shifts creates a plausible "why now." For this to become a Long Journey bet, I'd need to see the company evolve from selling diagnostics to becoming the health infrastructure layer for veterinary care — the platform vets and pet insurers build on, not a DTC brand competing with Embark for consumer attention. At 200+ early access users and 7 App Store ratings, that infrastructure pivot is aspirational, not underway.

Omi Health is a competent team executing a known playbook in a consensus-attractive market. The two dimensions that lock this as a pass: founder biographical forge — Sindu's Ritual experience is career capital applied to a school project, not a life story that made this problem unavoidable — and contrarian conviction, where the space is so well-funded and trend-aligned that my investment would be consensus-following, not conviction. I wish Sindu and Jakob well; they have the operational chops to build a real business here. But this isn't a bet that requires someone willing to be the first check when others won't write one. Plenty of investors are eager for this deal, and that's exactly why it isn't mine.
