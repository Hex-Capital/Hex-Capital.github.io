# Care GP -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Care GP is a solo-founder governance story stapled to a credentialed-outsider founder story, and those two facts about Melvin Chen ? Oxford Engineering valedictorian, Goldman Sachs / Terra Firma / Samena Capital, Forbes 30 Under 30 Finance ? tell me more about whether I write a check than the 150-clinic traction does. Care GP is exactly the deck shape I trained myself to flinch at: a finance pedigree pointed at a domain the founder has no biographical claim to. Care GP's product is real, the testimonials from Sonya at Yarrawonga and Josh Casey at Green Square are concrete, and the company is profitable ? and none of that moves me, because I am never playing the game I seem to be playing.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is the high rung for the founder dimension because Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before he pointed himself at defense ? the biography preceded the pitch. Care GP's Melvin Chen arrives from Terra Firma and Goldman Sachs PE, co-founded Avigo and StarLake Group in finance-adjacent contexts, and the dossier explicitly notes "no publicly disclosed prior healthcare-domain experience." That is the analytical-not-autobiographical pattern. A GP receptionist who built Samantha after watching her own clinic drown in pathology faxes would be a different conversation; Care GP's founder is a sharp capital allocator who identified Australian GP admin as a market, and "identified the market" is the failure mode of the Pass Example, not the success pattern of Anduril-Luckey or Niantic-Hanke.

HQ Trivia (2017) is the low rung I cite most often because Yusupov and Kroll could not name a tiebreaker and the company imploded ? and Care GP is the clean inverse of that failure. The YC page lists Melvin Chen as solo founder operating a 10-person team. No co-CEO ambiguity, no 50/50 equity tiebreaker problem, no telegraphed CEO-succession language from existing investors. Solo-founder execution risk at a 7,000-clinic stated expansion is real, but it is a *capacity* risk, not the *governance* risk that ate HQ Trivia and GameCrush. Care GP scores high here because the failure mode I weight is structurally absent, even if the substitute failure mode (one person carrying a 10-person org into a market with a $138M-funded competitor) is not.

The "magically weird" dimension is where Care GP collapses on mechanism analysis: an AI agent for Australian GP admin in the S26 batch is the precise opposite of Crusoe burning North Dakota flares in 2018. Heidi Health has already raised ~$138M at a ~$700M valuation with claimed 50% GP share; Lyrebird is bundled free into Best Practice Premier; Best Practice itself shipped a native admin agent (Medical Republic, 2026). Over 18 months this dimension compounds in the wrong direction ? agentic admin is the most-pitched YC vertical of 2025-2026, and the round will be priced as if the outcome is contested rather than discovered. Care GP is not "before consensus," it is mid-consensus arriving late.

Care GP fails my frontier-hardware / unsexy-regulated test as a counterfactual: it would have to look like Vigil Labs or Mindbloom on the regulatory-moat axis for the score to flip a tier. Healthcare-adjacent and ISO27001-certified is not the same as compounding regulatory complexity ? Australian clinical document routing is sensitive (pathology misallocation has patient-safety consequences, per the dossier) but it is software the incumbent PMS vendor can replicate, and Best Practice has signaled intent to do exactly that. The counterfactual that would move Care GP up here is a hardware artifact in the clinic, a payor-side regulated workflow, or something a software-only competitor cannot ship in a quarter; none of that is present.

Care GP on physical-world verifiability is a single-anchor parallel to Postmates (2011) inverted: I bet on Postmates because the delivery happened on my street, and I cannot stand in a Yarrawonga reception and watch Samantha file a pathology PDF. Sonya's "6 hours in under 30 minutes" testimonial is the closest tactile artifact in the Care GP dossier, and it is mediated through a customer quote rather than something I can install or photograph. I am in San Francisco; Care GP is a B2B Australian SaaS whose value lives inside practice management software I cannot access. The dimension defaults below the midpoint because the product is not in the wild for me.

My 36-month forecast for Care GP: acquired by Best Practice Software or by Heidi Health for under AUD $50M, or alternatively eaten by Best Practice's native admin agent and surviving as a sub-AUD $10M ARR regional tool. The mechanism is channel conflict ? Care GP is a listed partner of the platform that has now shipped the competing product, which is the worst possible position in a vertical SaaS supply chain, and Heidi at a ~$700M valuation has the capital to acqui-hire the team or starve the category.

The bull case stings: 150 clinics with zero churn and 140% MoM usage growth at pre-seed is genuinely remarkable, and HandUp (~2014) is my precedent for writing checks into commercially modest outcomes when something else compels me. The Care GP bull argues Melvin Chen is the rare polymath finance-to-ops operator, that profitability at pre-seed is the actual moat, and that the Heidi/Lyrebird scribe focus leaves admin genuinely open. The bear wins because HandUp's override was personal mission alignment with my own homelessness origin and DeepMind's override was Hassabis's chess-prodigy-plus-neuroscience polymath compounding ? Care GP's Melvin Chen has neither lived the GP-admin problem nor compounded across non-finance domains, so neither Known Exception triggers, and the consensus-AI-agent category is the exact lane the Pass Example was built to capture. The verdict flips to Invest if a credible Best Practice acquisition LOI materializes at terms that mark up the seed materially, or if Care GP demonstrates a non-Australian clinic deployment with a named partner ? until then, Care GP is the kind of competent S26 deck I let someone else fund.
