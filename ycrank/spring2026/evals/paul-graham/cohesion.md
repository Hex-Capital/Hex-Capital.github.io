# Cohesion -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Devon Krapcho spent five years at Long Path Partners covering software, doing the exact thing Cohesion now automates -- tracking earnings, podcasts, and X for investing signals -- and that organic-pain origin is the strongest thing in this dossier. The weakest is that nowhere in the founder section is there a GitHub repo, a Lisp prize, an open-source contribution, or any of the tribal hacker markers I over-index on. Cohesion is an analyst-built tool by a finance-native team, not a hacker-built tool by a code-native team, and that pairing tells you almost everything about what kind of company this is and what its likely exit looks like.

The Dropbox (2007) origin story is the rung I keep returning to on organic ideas -- Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and built the thing he himself needed. Cohesion has a version of this that is more credible than most YC dossiers I read: Krapcho, per the YC page and his LinkedIn, was a buy-side analyst literally spending hours per week tracking the inputs Cohesion now monitors, and Matt Munns previously built AI for investors at T. Rowe Price. The mechanism that made Dropbox work -- founder is the prototype user, so the product gets the small things right that the spec would never capture -- is genuinely present here. Where Cohesion diverges from the Dropbox rung is that Houston's pain produced a horizontal consumer product; Krapcho's pain produces a narrow workflow tool whose ceiling is bounded by the number of fundamental equity analysts on Earth, which is not many. The organic-idea signal is real; the ceiling thinking from "Frighteningly Ambitious Startup Ideas" (March 2012) is missing.

The MBA pass example I wrote earlier -- two-MBA team, glossy deck, "we'll hire engineers post-funding" -- failed my hacker-genealogy filter because there was no shipped artifact and no GitHub trail. Cohesion does not fail that test as badly: Matthew McBrien is a real Amazon/AWS engineer with an MS EE from Georgia Tech, and the product evidently works enough to be in front of ten funds. But the dossier explicitly notes "No public repos found" for all three founders, no Twitter for any of them, no open-source footprint, no Hacker News presence. The Collison brothers had Patrick's Young Scientist Lisp prize in 2005 before Stripe (2010); Aaron Swartz had the RSS spec before Reddit (2005). Cohesion has an Amazon engineer and two finance people. The pattern from my Pass Example partly applies -- not catastrophically, but the technical-taste signal that flipped Dropbox (2007) for me on first read is absent.

On animal resourcefulness, the dossier names a real pre-funding artifact: 10+ long/short and long-only fundamental equity fund customers managing $10Bn+ combined AUM, achieved while still inside YC Spring 2026. That is not nothing -- selling agentic software to ten hedge funds before demo day is harder than building a B2C waitlist, and it required Krapcho to convert his Long Path analyst network into paying logos. The 18-month mechanism, though, is that hedge-fund sales compound through reference accounts only if signal quality holds; if the agents miss an earnings surprise that a customer trades into a loss, that account churns and references reverse. Cohesion has bought itself one cycle of credibility, not a moat.

For the schlep dimension, the counterfactual is clean: for Cohesion to score where Coinbase (2012) scored, the dossier would need to name SOC2 certification work, a compliance build for MNPI handling, signed data-licensing deals with podcast platforms, or some other regulatory grind that AlphaSense's $500M ARR machine and Rogo's $300M+ war chest do not want to redo. Instead the dossier explicitly says "no public certification or backtest data found" and flags X API TOS as a dependency rather than something Cohesion has neutralized. The schlep is latent, not embraced. Brian Armstrong went toward the regulatory pain at Coinbase (2012); Cohesion's dossier reads like founders who haven't hit it yet.

The cofounder-configuration parallel is to the Airbnb (2009) trio: Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk had roomed together and worked on prior projects -- the dossier here says only "No public data on co-founder history" for Krapcho, McBrien, and Munns. Three founders is the right number per "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010), and the role coverage (analyst CEO, AWS engineer, prior fintech AI builder) is structurally complementary. But absent the dorm-roommate or prior-failed-startup glue, the configuration is the default 5/10, not the Airbnb 9/10.

Thirty-six months out, I expect Cohesion to be acquired by AlphaSense or Hebbia for $40-80M as a content-coverage bolt-on, or to have pivoted into compliance/IR monitoring where the buyer set is broader -- not standalone at $50M+ ARR, and not dead. The mechanism producing that forecast is the dimensional signature: organic founder-pain gets Cohesion to ten logos and a real product, but the absent hacker genealogy and the narrow analyst-tool ceiling mean Rogo's Series D capital or AlphaSense's distribution eats the workflow before Cohesion compounds into a defining company.

The strongest bull case against that forecast is the Loopt (2005) exception -- I funded Sam Altman before mobile location was usable infrastructure on pure force-of-personality, accepting weak product-market timing because the animal test overrode the idea critique. One could argue Krapcho selling to 10+ hedge funds while still inside YC Spring 2026 is exactly that animal signal -- buy-side sales cycles are notoriously slow, and pulling ten logos from inside the batch is a stunt of a kind. The bear retort is that Loopt's signal was Altman the person, not Loopt the traction number, and the dossier here does not give me a personal-force read on Krapcho the way Altman walked into the room. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: if within twelve months Cohesion lands a fund with $50B+ AUM as a named anchor customer at six-figure ACV, or if the dossier reveals that Krapcho personally shipped the v1 product without McBrien -- either would push the animal score from 18 to 28 and the verdict to Invest. Absent that, Cohesion is a credible Neutral that I would watch but not write a check into today.
