## The Idea

- **Problem:** Group 1 suicide / FPV attack drones (under 20 lbs, 10–100m engagement) threaten military vehicles, bases, and dismounted troops, and existing kinetic and RF-based counter-UAS systems are large, expensive, and power-hungry (9mothers.com, 2026).
- **Approach:** EDDA is a 23 kg / 50 lb AI point-defense turret with passive acoustic + visual detection (zero RF signature), 5° slew in <15 ms, and 1.5 arcminute tracking accuracy, engaging drones autonomously at close range (9mothers.com, 2026).
- **Differentiation:** Versus Anduril Lattice/fire-control (large fixed-site, software-defined) and Epirus Leonidas (high-power microwave area-effect, $595M raised — Tracxn/Contrary Research, 2026) and Allen Control Systems' Bullfrog (autonomous robotic weapon station retrofitting existing guns — BusinessWire, Mar 2025), EDDA is positioned as the smallest/lightest portable autonomous kinetic turret with zero-RF passive sensing (9mothers.com, 2026).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: Direct hardware sales to US Government and prime contractors; company states it is "currently selling to US Government and prime contractors" (9mothers.com, 2026); no public unit pricing for EDDA, Vor, or Atla; Gleipnir counter-attack drone targets <$10K cost-per-shot (9mothers.com, 2026).
- **TAM/SAM:** Counter-UAS market estimated at USD 2.6B–10.63B in 2026 with projections to USD 19–69B by 2034–2035 (Precedence Research; MarketsandMarkets; Fortune Business Insights, 2026); global government C-UAS contracts exceeded $29B announced in Q1 2026 (Defense Security Monitor, Nov 2025).
- **GTM / Distribution:** Direct DoD/prime-contractor sales path supported by ITAR registration, CAGE code 11NF2D, and DUNS 14-483-9928 (9mothers.com, 2026); [Inferred]: prime-contractor integration channel implied by stated customer set.

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** ITAR registration and active CAGE/DUNS codes lower the regulatory barrier to DoD contracting (9mothers.com, 2026); shipping a fielded product (EDDA) ahead of three not-yet-released systems (Vor, Atla, Gleipnir) creates a roadmap lead (9mothers.com, 2026).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Data flywheel from deployed turret engagement telemetry could train better autonomous targeting models; unproven now because no deployment counts have been disclosed.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Small SWaP (size/weight/power) form factor at vehicle/dismounted scale is a different engineering target than incumbents like Epirus' high-power microwave platforms or Anduril's fire-control infrastructure (Contrary Research; Breaking Defense, Oct 2025), reducing direct product-line substitution from larger defense primes.
- **Commoditization risk:** Counter-UAS funding tracked only 18 unique disclosed equity rounds in 24 months (New Market Pitch, 2026), but Allen Control Systems ($30M Series A, Craft Ventures lead, Mar 2025) directly targets the autonomous-turret kinetic-engagement niche (BusinessWire, Mar 2025), indicating low entrant-uniqueness on the kinetic-AI-turret thesis.

## Key Risks

- **Technical feasibility risk:** Real-time autonomous kinetic engagement of fast Group 1 FPV drones at 10–100m with 1.5 arcminute accuracy under field conditions is unproven at scale; 9mothers.com lists specs but no third-party test or live-fire results (9mothers.com, 2026).
- **Direct competition risk:** Allen Control Systems is also Austin-based, also building autonomous kinetic turrets for Group 1 drones, has $30M Series A, Craft Ventures backing, and a December 2025 US Army combat-vehicle integration selection (BusinessWire, Mar 2025; Defense Post, Dec 2025) — overlapping product thesis with more capital and earlier Army traction.
- **Founder domain risk:** All three founders' documented experience is in QA SaaS / data science at Rainforest QA (getprog.ai; LinkedIn, 2026); no public record of weapons engineering, robotics hardware, or DoD program-of-record execution; no military or defense advisors publicly listed.
- **Regulatory / ethics risk:** Autonomous lethal weapons face evolving DoD Directive 3000.09 and international policy constraints; over 100 companies are building "killer robots" per industry tracking (LinkedIn aggregation, 2025); export licensing (ITAR registration confirmed) limits non-US revenue.
- **Roadmap concentration:** Three of four announced products (Vor, Atla, Gleipnir) are slated for 2027 (9mothers.com, 2026), making near-term revenue dependent solely on EDDA execution.
