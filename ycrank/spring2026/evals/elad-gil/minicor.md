# Minicor -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Minicor is what you get when a real market catalyst (computer-use models crossing the accuracy threshold in 2024-2025) meets a founder pair with prior legacy-integration shipping (Faizaan Chishtie and Saheed Akinbile's Laminar Run) but with the one thing that should be loud in the AI cycle conspicuously quiet: no disclosed revenue curve, only "25,000 patients/day in production" as a usage proxy that may or may not carry over from the Laminar pivot. The high/low pairing here -- catalyzed market plus single-miracle architecture *up*, revenue velocity *down* -- describes a workflow-infrastructure attempt in an open category whose silence on dollars in 2026 is itself the signal.

**Anduril (2022)** is the comparable on market re-opening: defense looked dead, Ukraine catalyzed the window inside 24 months, peers misread it. Minicor sits on the same template structurally -- Anthropic claude-computer-use and OpenAI Operator made desktop self-healing tractable in 2024-2025, and Gartner is now projecting 40% of enterprise apps embed task-specific agents by 2026 versus <5% in 2025. The shared mechanism is a step-function that retired the prior incumbent's competence premise (Pentagon software for Anduril; UiPath's script-fragility for Minicor). Where Minicor diverges from the Anduril rung is that the same catalyst that opened Minicor's window -- foundation-model computer-use -- walks in the door behind them as Anthropic's and OpenAI's direct offerings, plus Skyvern's $2.7M open-source play. Anduril's catalyst (Pentagon dysfunction) didn't simultaneously hand Lockheed an autonomy stack; Minicor's catalyst is doing exactly that to UiPath's roadmap. Real re-opening, contested entry.

Harvey (2023) is the canonical "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth, something's fundamentally broken" failure mode when inverted -- I sized Harvey up at $3B → $5B → $8B in months because the curve was disclosed and the logos were named. The Harvey failure pattern, applied as a test, asks: does Minicor's dossier disclose revenue velocity? No pricing page on minicor.com, no MoM growth, no named customer logos extracted, no ARR figure, and the "25,000 patients/day" load cannot be cleanly attributed to Minicor versus inherited Laminar deployments per the pivot-continuity risk in the dossier. In the AI cycle this is not a stage-default absence -- pre-seed YC companies with real velocity disclose it because that *is* the cold-inbound weapon. The silence reads as "not yet just working" rather than "shy."

Faizaan Chishtie and Saheed Akinbile have shipped: Laminar Run (low-code integration), Homeboard (mid-five-figure ARR), and Akinbile's Morgan Stanley Java work cited at $30M/year in fee savings. The 18-month mechanism on this dimension compounds favorably for Minicor -- the founder pair has been pointed at "legacy-system integration" since 2019 (UX class at U Ottawa), built Laminar against the exact same problem space, and the Minicor pivot is a *re-aim* inside their domain rather than a leap. What's missing from the **Perplexity (2022)** Aravind pattern is evidence of between-meeting artifact velocity -- the dossier gives me prior products but no "showed up next week with the thing we discussed built" rhythm. Solid founder-market fit, unverified shipping cadence.

Stripe (2010) is the index-position anchor: every e-commerce entrant has to touch the payments rail. The counterfactual that would flip Minicor's index score a tier: if Minicor became the default "API for Windows desktop systems of record" that AI vendors (Decagon, Abridge, Harvey-style verticals) integrate against when selling into healthcare/automotive/logistics, then Minicor sits in the workflow path of the entire AI-into-legacy distribution curve -- the Stripe-shape. The dossier doesn't show that yet. It shows a desktop automation product competing on a 93-96% versus 80-85% click-accuracy delta against UiPath, Automation Anywhere, Power Automate, Skyvern, Browserbase, and TinyFish. That's a feature fight inside a contested category, not a rail position. Flip condition would be two or more named AI vendors publicly routing their healthcare deployments through Minicor's MCP endpoints.

Color Genomics (2015) is the single-anchor parallel for Minicor's miracle architecture: I co-founded Color explicitly as workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing rather than betting on the chemistry. Minicor's structure is the same shape -- deterministic Python automation plus AI reflection on top of commodity computer-use models, exposed as an API. The miracle is one: "self-healing UI recovery reliable enough that AI vendors stop building bespoke integrations." That's a single, validated-or-not capability with the rest being GTM and product execution. This is the strongest dimension for Minicor structurally, and it's why the company isn't a pass outright.

Thirty-six month forecast: Minicor lands at $3-8M ARR in healthcare-and-Citrix-bound desktop automation and is acqui-hired by Automation Anywhere or UiPath in the $40-120M range as a self-healing module, because the catalyst-driven window closes faster than Minicor can convert "25,000 patients/day" into an index position before the incumbent agentic-RPA features and the Anthropic/OpenAI computer-use APIs collapse the accuracy gap. The mechanism is the catalyst-and-commoditization symmetry -- the same model improvements that made Minicor's premise tractable in 2024-2025 will narrow its 93-96% versus 80-85% delta into noise by 2028.

The strongest opposing case is the Abridge (2023) analogy I funded: workflow on top of a commodity model (Whisper-class transcription) into a heavily regulated vertical (healthcare) with HIPAA as a real switching-cost moat, where the "boring ops layer" -- video replay, scaling, error logging, SOC 2 Type II -- compounds into a defensible enterprise product even as the underlying model commoditizes. Minicor's dossier supports that read: SOC 2 Type II and HIPAA already in place at pre-seed is unusual, on-prem/Citrix deployment options matter to exactly the buyers AI vendors can't reach themselves, and the Forward Deployed Engineer hire ($125K-$200K) signals an Abridge-style implementation motion. The dismantling fact is that Abridge by the comparable stage had named health-system logos and a disclosed enterprise pull -- Minicor's dossier names no customer logos and discloses no revenue, in a market where the "just working" tell is loud. Verifiable flip condition: Minicor discloses $5M+ ARR with 20%+ MoM growth and two named Fortune 500 healthcare or financial-services logos within the next two quarters, at which point this is an Abridge-shape bet and I'm in.
