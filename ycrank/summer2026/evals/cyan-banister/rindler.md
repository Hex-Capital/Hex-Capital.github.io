# Rindler -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Michael Serrano and Arthur De Los Santos both graduated MIT in 2025 and are now building MCP middleware for websites without APIs — Rindler is the analytical high point (a named CEO in Serrano, a named CTO in De Los Santos, clean role separation) paired with an analytical low point (a pure developer abstraction I cannot install, photograph, or hand to a friend). That pairing tells me exactly what Rindler is: a pedigreed CSAIL duo executing the direct center of YC Summer 2026's "Software for Agents" thesis (Forbes, Feb 2026), which is the shape of the consensus AI-agent deck I already have a pass example for.

Anduril-Luckey (2017) is the HIGH rung on founder-lived-experience for me — Palmer shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before defense, so when he walked into the room he had already *touched* the problem. Rindler's Serrano lists "infrastructure of the future web" as his MIT MEng focus and De Los Santos was an Autonomous Robotics intern at Telexistence — that is academic proximity to browser automation, not autobiographical contact with the "operators whose teams live inside portals" the YC page names as the customer. Neither founder's dossier surfaces a moment where they *were* that operator, and in the absence of that moment I read the "infrastructure of the future web" phrasing as a memo-origin, not a garage-origin. That is the difference between Luckey and the pass I already wrote.

HQ Trivia (2017) is the LOW rung on CEO authority — Yusupov and Kroll never named the tiebreaker and the feud contributed to the 2020-21 implosion. Rindler does not repeat that pattern on the surface: Serrano is titled CEO and De Los Santos is titled CTO on the YC page, and they were classmates before they were co-founders, which usually predicts smoother role fluidity than the HQ Trivia acquire-a-partner setup. The dossier is silent on equity split, board composition, and the operating agreement — which is the "they haven't had the conversation yet" default I always score at the midpoint. That is why Rindler earns a MID here rather than the SpaceX-Musk HIGH.

Magically-weird: the category compounds in the wrong direction over 18 months. Browserbase is at a $300M valuation with $40M Series B closed June 2025 and 1,000+ customers per Built In SF; Composio has $79.6M raised, $1M+ ARR, and 100K+ developers per PRNewswire July 2025; Anon has $13M in for the exact auth/2FA/CAPTCHA sub-wedge Rindler wants to bundle. Rindler is not laughed at the way Crusoe (~2018) burning flare gas was — Rindler is applauded in the same YC S26 batch memo as the "Dynamic Software Interfaces" thesis. Consensus is the deselection signal, and MCP as an open standard (Anthropic/OpenAI/Google/Microsoft, 2024-25) means the gateway players MintMCP, TrueFoundry, Obot, Bifrost can extend into per-site middleware without asking permission.

Counterfactual for the capital-intensity dimension: for Rindler to flip a tier here, one of two things would need to be true — either Serrano would disclose a specific regulated vertical (title insurance, prior-authorization portals, court-filing systems) where the ToS/CFAA exposure is the moat because incumbents refuse the legal risk, or Rindler would publish a consent-and-audit posture that reads like Anon's user-permissioned auth framework (TechCrunch, Apr 2024). Neither is present. What *is* present is the site-maintenance treadmill the YC page itself flags — "robust maintenance when sites change" for a two-person team against moving DOMs, auth flows, and bot detection. That is real unsexy work, but it is unsexy without the compounding regulatory moat that made Vigil Labs (August 2025) interesting.

Single-anchor parallel on physical-world verifiability: Flock Safety (~2018) I sourced from a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons café — I could see the artifact in the room. Rindler's product surface is `extract_content`, `dispatch_action`, `synthesize_schema`, `start_session`, and OAuth 2.0 PKCE session handling exposed as an MCP server. I cannot ride it, scan it, install it on my phone, or notice it in a hotel lobby. The @RindlerAI account exists on X with unknown followers and there is no Product Hunt, no Chrome Web Store, no waitlist number, no logo wall. This is the abstraction end of my ledger — the exact shape I wrote the pass example against.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Rindler is acqui-hired by Browserbase or Composio for $10-30M by mid-2029, or shut down — Serrano and De Los Santos are the kind of MIT CSAIL talent those incumbents buy, but the standalone Rindler ARR will not clear $2M against a $300M-valuation Browserbase distributing Stagehand under MIT license and a Composio already at $1M+ ARR with 100K developers. The mechanism is straightforward: MCP is an open standard, the wedge is a feature, and the two-person site-maintenance treadmill compounds against Rindler as coverage grows.

The bull case that would sting: Anthropic ships MCP as the default agent surface, "sites without APIs" turns out to be a genuinely different technical problem than "sites with APIs" (Composio's home turf) or "generic browser scripting" (Browserbase's home turf), and the Serrano/De Los Santos CSAIL depth lets Rindler ship a schema-synthesis primitive that the incumbents cannot copy in a quarter — plus DeepMind (~2013) is my Known Exception showing pedigreed polymaths can override the anti-credential bias when they compound across domains. I dismantle it on autobiography: Hassabis had chess-prodigy, neuroscience, and game-design compounding *before* DeepMind, and Rindler's founders' compounding is one domain (MIT CSAIL infrastructure research) applied to one adjacent domain (browser automation), which is not polymath breadth — that is a competent thesis project. One verifiable condition flips me: if Rindler names a signed LOI with a specific regulated-portal operator whose category incumbents refuse on ToS grounds, and Serrano can articulate the autobiographical moment he watched that operator's team suffer, I re-open the meeting.
