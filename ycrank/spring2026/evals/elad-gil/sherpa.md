# Sherpa -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Sherpa is a clean single-miracle execution bet ? Norbu Sonam at Lindy tripling paywall conversion across 50+ experiments is exactly the operating muscle the product automates ? sitting in a CRO category where the incumbents just consolidated into a $100M+ Everstone platform (TechCrunch, Jan 2026) that can layer the same LLM-variant feature onto an installed base. That high/low pairing ? architecturally clean, structurally contested ? is Sherpa's signature.

The dimension where Sherpa scores cleanest is single-miracle architecture, and the right reference rung is **Color Genomics** (2015), which I co-founded specifically to be workflow infrastructure on top of commodity sequencing rather than a chemistry bet ? one validated capability below, execution above. Sherpa runs the same architectural pattern: the underlying miracle is LLMs generating on-brand HTML/CSS variants reliably enough for production traffic, which the AgentA/B paper (arXiv 2504.09723, 2025) confirms crossed feasibility within the last twelve months, and everything above is GTM and product. Where Sherpa diverges from the Color template is that Color owned an operational layer ? the lab, the report, the GP workflow ? that competitors couldn't clone with a feature flag. Sherpa's "miracle wrapper" is a JS snippet, and VWO already ships GPT-3.5 copy generation (VWO blog, 2025). One miracle, clean ? but a thin wrapper around it.

Index position is where Sherpa is weakest, and the failure mechanism I keep coming back to is the **Lyft** Series C pass ? not because Lyft and Sherpa look alike, but because in both cases the dossier didn't show why the company captures the category's compounding rather than fighting for share inside it. **Stripe** (2010) is an index on e-commerce because every new merchant has to touch the rails; Sherpa is not an index on conversion optimization because the rails are the customer's own website, and VWO, AB Tasty (now Everstone-combined), Optimizely, and Kameleoon all sit in the same path with seat-based contracts already signed. The dossier's defensibility section gestures at a "cross-customer experiment dataset" as a future moat, but at 7.3M sessions Sherpa is below the data scale where that flywheel bites, and Kameleoon alone reported $22.1M revenue 2024 (GetLatka) ? Sherpa has to win share against incumbents, not ride a category.

Catalyst. The dossier names LLM variant generation crossing feasibility in 2025 as the "why now," which is a real step-function, but it's the generic transformer catalyst that opened forty other YC AI-wrapper categories simultaneously. Compare to my **Anduril** (2022) entry, where the catalyst was Ukraine ? specific, dateable, asymmetric to one category. The CRO market itself has been steady-state at ~11% CAGR (Future Market Insights, 2025) for a decade; Sherpa's catalyst is "LLMs got good," which is the same catalyst every Spring 2026 batch company is riding, and over eighteen months that compounds into commoditization pressure, not category re-opening ? VWO will ship autonomous variants because Sherpa proved the demand, not because Sherpa held the moat.

Revenue velocity. What would have to be true for Sherpa to flip from neutral to invest on this axis is a **Harvey** (2023)-style disclosure ? a real ARR number compounding visibly month over month with named enterprise logos signed in the last 90 days outside the founders' Lindy network. The dossier gives me 7,338,896 sessions analyzed, a +30% conversion lift at Lindy, 2.5x demo bookings at Gauge, and three logos (Lindy, Insforge, Gauge) ? all YC-adjacent, all founder-network. No MRR. No ARR. No non-network customer. In the AI cycle, silence on dollars when the curve is the easiest thing to disclose is itself the signal: "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863). I'll grant the absent-evidence default rather than penalize the stage, but this is not a Harvey-shaped curve and the dossier doesn't pretend otherwise.

Founder shipping rhythm is the dimension where Sherpa most reminds me of **Perplexity** (2022) on a single axis ? Aravind would show up a week later with the thing built. Norbu Sonam shipped Copilot image generation and Microsoft Designer features inside Microsoft, then ran 50+ growth experiments and tripled paywall conversion at Lindy; Ethan Kinnan was Lindy's first forward-deployed engineer and taught 10,000+ users to build agents. These are named artifacts, not pedigree claims, and founder-market fit is unusually tight because Norbu literally was the human Sherpa replaces. This is the strongest signal in the dossier and the reason Sherpa doesn't pencil as a pure pass.

Forecast, 36 months out: Sherpa lands at $2?6M ARR serving YC-batch and SMB growth teams, then is acqui-hired by Optimizely or the Everstone/VWO/AB Tasty platform for the team and dataset, not the standalone business ? the mechanism being incumbent feature-parity arriving before Sherpa accumulates enough cross-customer session data to make the dataset moat real. That outcome is consistent with the signature: a clean single-miracle execution team in a non-index position gets bought by the index holder.

The strongest case against this forecast is the **Decagon** (2023) parallel: Zendesk and Salesforce could have layered AI agents on top of their installed bases and crushed Decagon, except that seat-based pricing creates channel conflict that prevents incumbents from fully autonomizing ? and Decagon walked through that gap into the workflow position. VWO and AB Tasty sell per-seat experimentation suites to in-house designers and analysts; a fully autonomous agent cannibalizes their revenue model the same way, which is exactly the structural opening the dossier's market-structure inference identifies. That argument stings. What dismantles it: Decagon disclosed revenue velocity early and Sherpa has not, and Decagon was selling into the customer-support buyer ? a budget center hungry for headcount displacement ? whereas Sherpa is selling into growth/marketing teams whose budgets are smaller and whose buyers are the people the product replaces. One condition flips my verdict: Sherpa discloses $1M+ ARR by Q4 2026 with at least three non-YC-network logos signed in the prior 90 days. Until then, neutral.
