# Async -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Async is a services engagement wearing an AI-agents wrapper — the highest-scoring dimension is pivot-room across three verticals, the lowest is the leverage architecture itself, and that pairing tells me Ben Smith and Connor Park have built a labor-leverage consultancy dressed up in permissionless-code vocabulary. The website's own engagement language — "partner & audit → scope & deploy → prove & expand → maintain & compound" — is the giveaway. That is a McKinsey sequence, not a Notion signup flow.

Twitter (2007) is the leverage-architecture rung I hold every code-or-media bet against, and the mechanism was zero-marginal-cost replication with anyone-can-post distribution — Odeo didn't need to audit each new user's workflow. Async runs the inverse mechanism: withasync.com describes per-firm audits, custom agent builds, and maintenance retainers, and the corporate email is still founders@useskope.com because the legal entity is Skope Technologies Inc. Custom agents built per Clifton Law PLLC engagement do not compound the way a tweet does. This is capital and labor leverage — both of which "require permission" (nav.al/product-media) — with a foundation-model API in the middle. Async does not lose the leverage check because the tech is weak; it loses because the go-to-market is one firm at a time.

The pivot-room dimension is the one place Async scores above my absent-evidence default, and I want to walk through the mechanism rather than a ladder. The dossier shows three pivots inside twelve months: nonprofit fundraising agents → Skope outcome-based billing → Skope for law firms → Async across law, healthcare, and real estate. Read one way that is thrashing; read the way Venture Hacks taught me — "the first idea never works, so you need room to maneuver" — three regulated service verticals is legitimate pivot terrain, wider than most YC S25 companies get to work with. The trap is that HIPAA scope in healthcare is a category shift from SOC 2 Type I, and Async is two people. Room to maneuver is not the same as bandwidth to occupy the room.

Counterfactual on specific knowledge: for Async's founder-market-fit score to flip a tier, either Ben Smith would need documented years operating inside a law firm or health system, or Connor Park's GitHub would need to show sustained legal-tech or clinical-tooling artifacts predating YC. Neither exists in the dossier — Ben Smith's background is University of Michigan dropout, prior at Merit, retail commerce; Connor Park is @connorpark24 with 18 followers, 11 repos, 72 total stars, top pin being his personal site. Aravind Srinivas showed up to Perplexity with a Berkeley ML PhD in retrieval; Atwood and Spolsky shipped Stack Overflow as practicing developers writing for developers. Async's founders are shipping into three regulated verticals none of them has lived in.

Stack Overflow is the single anchor for the network-effect dimension, and the comparison isolates why Async's economics do not compound. Stack Overflow's Nth developer answer made the (N-1) previous questions permanently more valuable — the corpus itself was the moat. Async's Nth customer engagement makes the (N-1) previous customers zero percent more valuable, because each deployment is a bespoke agent bound to that firm's Word/Excel/SharePoint/CRM stack. The dossier hints at "cross-vertical library of agent templates" as future moat, but that library — if it materializes — is an internal knowledge base, not a user-value-compounding network. Harvey at $11B post-money and Legora at $5.55B are the incumbents already shipping Word add-ins into the same law firms Async is auditing; Async's Word add-in is the ninetieth Word add-in for legal, not the first.

The Buffett trio reads clean-but-thin on Async. No red flag on integrity — no contested exits, no unprompted honesty-signaling, no litigation. Also no positive signal: no public writing from Ben Smith, no long-form artifacts from Connor Park, no reputation trail I can hold against a decade. Integrity default holds, but I cannot upgrade above it. Energy is present — three pivots inside one batch, Launch HN with 55 points on the Skope iteration — Async's team ships. Intelligence I have no basis to grade. The 10-year-partner question at angel size lands at "I don't know these people yet, and nothing in the dossier makes me want to."

Thirty-six months out I expect Async either quietly shutters or gets absorbed by a mid-tier legal-AI shop for sub-$10M as an acquihire, most likely by Spellbook or a Series-A vertical player looking for a deployment team; a headline outcome requires Ben Smith and Connor Park to abandon the services engagement model and rebuild Async as a self-serve product before Harvey extends downmarket, and I do not see the founder-specific-knowledge reserve that pivot demands. The mechanism producing this forecast is the leverage architecture itself — labor-leverage service businesses in categories with $1B+-raised incumbents get squeezed between the incumbents' downmarket motion and cheaper offshore AI consultancies, and Async's SOC 2 Type I is table stakes, not moat.

The bull case that stings: Perplexity is my own Known Exception where a single-sided app in a hot category cleared three of five gates emphatically and I wrote the check anyway. Someone will argue Async is the same shape — pre-consensus vertical-AI agents, code-native, and founder-execution velocity carrying the specific-knowledge gap. I dismantle it on the leverage leg. Perplexity distributes through a browser tab and an API — permissionless, code-leverage-native. Async distributes through a "Book a demo" cal.com link and a partner-and-audit engagement. Aravind Srinivas also cleared the specific-knowledge gate at top quartile; Ben Smith and Connor Park clear it at bottom quartile with no shipped domain artifact. Three-of-five gates do not clear here — one gate clears (pivot room), and the other four either sit at default or below. The verdict flips only if Async ships a self-serve product with organic signup traction inside twelve months and drops the audit-and-deploy language from the site — until then, this is Pass.
