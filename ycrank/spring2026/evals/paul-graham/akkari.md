# Akkari -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Jeffrey Byun and Henry Lee built **OrderAhead** together (YC, acquired by Square 2016), then **BLADE** together ($1.2B+ monthly volume), and are now building Akkari together -- fourteen years of co-founding the same partner. That is the strongest fact in the Akkari dossier, and it sits next to the weakest one: nowhere in the Akkari dossier is there a shipped artifact, a stunt, or a single line about what Byun, Lee, or Moore did before the YC check arrived. Akkari reads as a credentialed serial-operator team executing a market thesis, not a Drew-Houston-with-a-demo-video moment.

The cereal boxes are what I think about first when I read the Akkari dossier, because they are conspicuously absent. **Airbnb** (2009)'s $40 Obama O's box was the moment Chesky, Gebbia, and Blecharczyk flipped my idea-level skepticism by doing something nobody told them to do. The Akkari dossier names Fidji Simo, Tony Xu, Eric Wu, and Othman Laraki on the cap table; it does not name a single unscalable thing Byun did to earn his way in. Byun's @jeffbyun account has 341 followers since 2008. Lee's LinkedIn reads "Building something new." Moore is so faint the dossier itself flags a name-disambiguation risk. Animals leave tracks. The Akkari tracks I see are investor logos, which is the opposite tell -- it suggests the round was raised on the OrderAhead-exit and BLADE-volume résumé, which is exactly the founder-credential pattern I learned to discount after watching too many post-exit operators chase the next wave.

Akkari's strongest dimension is the co-founder configuration, and it genuinely is strong. The **Stripe** (2010) Collison brothers analogy is the right reference: a pair who had already hacked commerce together at **Auctomatic** (2007) before Stripe -- the long pre-existing working relationship is what carried the conviction. Byun and Lee have the same shape on the time axis (two prior companies together, one Square exit, one $1.2B-volume operation) even if not the Lisp-prize shape on the hacker axis. Moore overlapping at OrderAhead with thirty-person GTM and 2,500-contractor responsibility means the Akkari trio has actually run the customer-ops machine they are now trying to automate. This is the part of Akkari I cannot dismiss.

Idea-organicity at Akkari is partial. Moore literally ran a 30-person GTM team chasing commitments across channels at OrderAhead -- that is closer to the **Dropbox** (2007) USB-stick origin than to the Pass-Example AI-wrapper team, but the Akkari dossier never quotes the founders saying "we lost this specific deal because we missed this specific Slack thread." It is operator-adjacent pain, not diary-entry pain. The organic-idea filter from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) asks whether the founders themselves wanted the thing; the Akkari product page reads like the founders themselves managed the thing manually, which is one notch weaker.

Schlep-zone analysis at Akkari is where the competitive landscape eats the thesis. **Coinbase** (2012)'s moat was that nobody else wanted to file the BitLicense paperwork. Akkari's schlep is integrating Slack, email, Discord, GitHub, and Linear into one autonomous loop -- but **Decagon** (raised $361M+, $4.5B valuation Jan 2026) and **Sierra** ($950M Series C at $15.8B May 2026) are running directly at the same integration surface from the inbound side, and **Pylon** ($31M Series B Aug 2025, 780+ customers) is already the unified inbox. The Akkari "outbound execution" wedge is real positioning but it is not schlep-blindness; it is the obvious next adjacency in a crowded space. The schlep is shared.

Hacker genealogy at Akkari is the dimension that would have to flip for me to lean in. Byun's bio leads MIT MBA, Morgan Stanley, BofA Merrill Lynch fixed income -- this is the credentialing path I learned at **Viaweb** (1995) to distrust. Lee was CTO of BLADE, which is real engineering, but the Akkari dossier finds no GitHub handle for either founder. Hackers leave GitHub trails, and "no verified handle found" applied to a CTO in 2026 is a softer answer than I want. If Lee had a thousand-star repo from 2014, my Akkari score moves materially. Counterfactual: a single named open-source contribution from Lee would push this dimension from a 5 to a 10 and probably pull the total into Invest range. The dossier does not provide it.

My 36-month forecast for Akkari: $3-8M ARR built on warm-network mid-market customer-ops buyers, then squeezed as Decagon and Sierra extend from inbound deflection into outbound execution loops, ending in a sub-$100M acquihire by HubSpot, Salesforce, or one of the larger AI-CS incumbents. The mechanism is the signature: a credentialed repeat team without the animal-moment that defines the winners can execute competently into an obvious adjacency, but cannot out-compound an incumbent with ten times the capital and integration footprint. This is a **Kiko** (2005)-shaped outcome dressed in better fundraising.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the **Twitch** (2007) precedent: Kan and Shear came back from **Kiko** (2005) and built a $970M outcome, and re-funding repeat YC founders is a pattern I have publicly endorsed. Byun and Lee are not coming back from failure -- they are coming back from a Square exit and a $1.2B-volume crypto derivatives platform, with Tony Xu (DoorDash) personally on the cap table. The bear answer is that **Twitch** worked because Kan and Shear were hungry hackers chasing a problem they were obsessed with after a humiliating eBay sale; Byun and Lee read on this Akkari dossier like serial operators picking a 2026 market, not like animals chasing a personal itch. The verifiable flip-condition: if Byun or Lee publishes a working Akkari demo on Hacker News in the next six months that the Akkari dossier currently does not mention, or if the Akkari team posts a named LOI with a customer-ops buyer Tony Xu introduced, I revisit. Until then, pass.
