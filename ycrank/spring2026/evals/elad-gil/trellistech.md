# Trellis -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Trellis claims "software is being commoditized" and that its AI agents reached feature parity with competitors in two weeks. That claim is simultaneously Trellis's thesis and its most dangerous vulnerability. If two founders can replicate Guesty's ($409M raised, $900M valuation) or Hostaway's ($543M raised, $1B valuation) core feature set in fourteen days, what stops those incumbents -- with hundreds of engineers and tens of thousands of existing customers -- from building an equivalent AI agent orchestration layer in a quarter? Trellis is making the structural argument that speed of software creation has collapsed, but that same argument applies symmetrically to every competitor in the space. When I invested in Stripe, the infrastructure moat was the API itself -- the integration surface area that thousands of companies built on top of. Trellis integrates with 200+ tools, but it sits on top of Airbnb and VRBO APIs. It depends on Guesty and Hostaway's data layers. That's an application on a contested infrastructure layer, not the infrastructure layer itself.

Trellis enters a market where AI for short-term rentals is fully consensus. Conduit (YC W24, $3.1M raised) already operates AI guest messaging across 77,000+ properties. Boom ($12.7M raised) launched its "BAM" agentic AI manager and operates in 20 countries. Guesty and Hostaway are both actively adding AI features to their platforms. The structural shift enabling Trellis -- LLMs reaching production-grade reliability for multi-step agentic workflows -- is real, but it's the most obvious application of the current AI wave. Every investor I talk to has seen five pitches for "AI agents for X vertical operation" this month. Compare this to when I backed Coinbase in 2012: crypto infrastructure was actively dismissed by mainstream investors, and the structural shift in digital asset rails was invisible to consensus. Or Harvey in early 2022: legal AI had been a graveyard for a decade, and the GPT capability curve crossing a precision threshold was a nonobvious window. STR AI is not a graveyard -- it's a gold rush with nearly $1 billion in competitor funding already deployed. Trellis scores poorly on the dimension I weight most heavily because the market timing is structurally correct but thoroughly consensus.

Lodovico Benvenuti's profile is the strongest element in Trellis's dossier. Scaling a property management company to 150+ listings and ~$2M revenue gives him firsthand operator pain. Building AI agents at Conduit (YC W24) gives him the technical capability to address that pain with production AI systems. Jan Sahagun's 116% of sales target at Besty AI and founding of STR Tech Report provide a genuine distribution channel into the target buyer persona. The founder-market fit is real -- these are not tourists in short-term rentals. The bull case writes itself: Lodovico has both the operator scar tissue and the AI engineering chops, Jan has the commercial relationships and a media property that reaches the exact buyer, and rebuilding a PMS from scratch around agents avoids the innovator's dilemma that constrains Guesty and Hostaway. If Trellis's cross-operational data network effect materializes -- "every workflow that works, every SOP that survives contact with guests, every resolution pattern" feeding back into the system -- early data accumulation could compound into a durable moat. That's the strongest version of the argument. But it requires at least two independent breakthroughs: winning distribution against competitors with $950M+ in combined funding AND accumulating enough cross-operational data before those competitors replicate the same network effect on larger customer bases. Two miracles, compounding odds.

Trellis's competitor provenance risk deserves direct attention. Lodovico previously built AI agents at Conduit, a direct competitor. Jan previously worked at Boom, another AI-first STR competitor. Both left competitors to start Trellis in the same market. I've seen this pattern work when founders take genuine technical insight from one context and apply it to a fundamentally different architecture -- but Trellis is building in the same vertical, targeting the same buyers, with the same AI-agent-for-STR positioning. The non-compete and IP contamination risks are non-trivial, and the relationship friction with former employers operating in the same YC ecosystem adds a layer of execution risk that doesn't appear in most pre-seed evaluations.

Trellis's margin architecture is unproven but structurally plausible. Per-listing SaaS pricing (the industry standard used by Guesty, Hostaway, and Boom) scales with portfolio size, and if the claimed 84.5% AI automation rate holds at production scale, the gross margin profile should be software-like. But Trellis is currently operating one property management company's operations -- the gap between a single design partner and a retention curve is too wide to draw margin conclusions. The $8.6B vacation rental software TAM is real but not large enough to generate the 100x outcomes I require when I'm also discounting for the competitive intensity in the space. One verifiable condition that would flip me toward Invest: if Trellis demonstrated that its cross-operational data network effect produces measurably better outcomes (higher guest ratings, fewer maintenance escalations, lower homeowner churn) for its tenth customer than its first -- evidence that the data flywheel compounds in a way that single-tenant competitors cannot replicate. Without that, Trellis is a well-executed application in a crowded, obvious market.

The two dimensions most decisive for my verdict are Nonobvious Market Timing and Infrastructure-Layer Positioning. Trellis enters a market that is structurally real but thoroughly consensus -- Conduit, Boom, Guesty, and Hostaway are all building AI agent capabilities for STR operations, and nearly $1B in competitor funding has already been deployed. Trellis sits on top of other platforms' APIs rather than becoming the infrastructure layer itself, which means it faces platform risk without compounding switching costs. Strong founder-market fit from Lodovico's operator-plus-AI background and Jan's commercial network cannot overcome a market structure that works against Trellis on the two criteria I weight most heavily.
