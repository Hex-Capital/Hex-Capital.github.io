# Vestris -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Vestris is a schlep-shaped problem attached to an invisible team -- a title-and-closing automation product aimed at replacing manual searchers pulling Schedule A/B and settlement statements out of DC and Montgomery County recording systems, wrapped around a founders@vestris.ai mailbox and a YC company page with Team Size = 0. The problem is real Coinbase-flavored grind. The founders are a black hole. That pairing is the whole evaluation.

The high rung on the schlep axis is Coinbase (2012), where Armstrong walked toward bitcoin compliance work every other applicant in the batch was unconsciously routing around, and the grind itself became the moat. Vestris sits closer to that rung than to the mid rung of DoorDash's last-mile ops, because title work carries RESPA exposure, underwriter liability on Schedule A/B, and per-county browser-agent buildouts against jurisdictions like Montgomery County, MD that no software-only YC team wants to spend a quarter on. The dossier's own risk list -- customer-labor cannibalization at agencies whose P&L includes searcher fees, county-by-county integration cost capping expansion, no disclosed E&O coverage -- is exactly the shape of unpleasantness "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) told me to lean into. Vestris diverges from the Coinbase analogy only in that Armstrong personally shipped an Android wallet to prove he'd do the grind; Vestris has shipped app.vestris.ai and two live counties, which is a real artifact, just not one with a face attached to it.

The low rung on founder resourcefulness is my Pass Example: the two-MBA AI-wrapper team with a glossy deck and no shipped artifact. Vestris is not that -- the product is live in two jurisdictions, which is more than most decks I reject -- but the failure mechanism I care about is founder invisibility, and Vestris has it. Animals leave tracks. The YC company page renders no founder names, vestris.ai lists no team, the only contact is a shared alias, and a web search returns nothing attributable. I've never funded a founder I couldn't name, and the absence isn't a research gap -- it's the founders' choice about what to publish, which is itself the signal. The Airbnb (2009) cereal-box story works because Chesky told it in the interview; there is no equivalent artifact here I can point at.

Idea organicity -- Vestris might be a title-industry insider building the tool they wished existed while grinding through commitments at a settlement company, which would be the Drew Houston USB-stick pattern applied to Schedule A/B. Or Vestris might be two generalist AI engineers who read that title agencies pay searchers hourly and wrote a browser agent. The dossier cannot tell me which, and over 18 months these two origin stories diverge violently: the insider founder ships the seventh county because they know which clerk to call in Fairfax; the generalist founder stalls at county three when the recording portal changes its login flow. No public data found on founder-market fit is not a neutral fact; it is the fact that determines whether Vestris becomes a Qualia-competitor or a Qualia-feature.

Hacker genealogy would flip a full tier if a single Vestris founder surfaced with a Browser Use-adjacent GitHub trail or a real-estate-adjacent hack shipped before the company -- Aaron Swartz co-authored the RSS specification before Reddit (2005), and that lineage was the whole bet. Vestris shows me no GitHub, no HN handle, no prior artifact, in a category where the technical primitive (browser agents) is already commoditized by 50k-star open-source projects listed in the same YC batch page. If the underlying layer is free, the moat has to be either the schlep or the hackers, and I can only see one of the two.

The Stripe (2010) parallel on co-founder configuration is instructive and unhelpful -- the Collisons were brothers who had shipped Auctomatic together for years before Stripe. Vestris reports Team Size = 0 with a founders@ plural email, which technically implies a pair but discloses nothing about how long they've built together, or whether one of them has ever balanced a settlement statement in production. My default for opacity is neutral because the YC interview surfaces chemistry; my default here bends negative because everything else about the founder disclosure is also empty, and the pattern is consistent rather than random.

My 36-month forecast for Vestris: acquired by Qualia or SoftPro (via Fidelity National Financial) for under $50M as a browser-agent bolt-on to the incumbent workflow suite, or dead in the county-integration treadmill by month 30, having reached six-to-eight jurisdictions and $1-3M ARR before Qualia's own browser-agent module ships and compresses pricing. The mechanism is that the schlep is real but the founders' invisibility prevents the schlep from compounding into a Coinbase-style moat -- Armstrong's public face was itself distribution, and Vestris has chosen the opposite.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass is that Vestris is exactly the Airbnb (2009) known exception in disguise: an idea I'm dismissing (title-industry back office, unglamorous, cannibalizes customers' labor) run by founders whose animal quality I simply cannot see from outside because they haven't done the cereal-box stunt yet. Fair. The Airbnb override required a specific pre-funding artifact I could point at -- 1,000 boxes at $40 each is not a vibe, it's a receipt. Vestris has shipped two live counties, which is a real artifact, but two counties is what a competent engineer produces in a quarter; it is not evidence of animal-grade determination against the specific unpleasantness of RESPA liability and clerk-by-clerk relationship building. One verifiable condition flips me to Invest: a named founder surfaces with either (a) prior title-industry operator experience -- worked at First American, Fidelity, or a mid-sized agency -- or (b) a shipped pre-Vestris technical artifact against county records systems. Either would convert the schlep from thesis into moat. Until then, Vestris is a PG-shaped problem I cannot underwrite because the founders are not on the page.
