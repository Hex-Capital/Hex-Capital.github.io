# Autostep -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Autostep is the 2026 YC AI-agent pitch rendered with one unusually long-tenured solo operator inside it — Aidan Pratt brings six years of automation work across Kleiner, 8VC, Neo, Keel, and Opto, and he is selling that conviction into the single most consensus enterprise category in the Spring 2026 batch (task-mining-plus-agent-generation against UiPath, Celonis, Skan, Mimica). The signature is high founder-domain depth, low category-sleeve fit, and that pairing tells you exactly what Autostep is: a competent operator running the standard YC AI-agent playbook into a category where four incumbents already have the distribution he doesn't.

Solana at $0.05 is what a high mark on Round Mis-Pricing looks like — a category most generalist funds had quit, priced where the math worked even if the thesis failed. Autostep is the inverse on the same axis: David Lieb partnering, Neo co-signing, YC S26 stamp, and a launch frame ("the bottleneck isn't tooling anymore, it's discovery") that could have been written by any of the AI-ops seeds in the same batch. The dossier shows no published price, no named tier-1 pass, no structural ambiguity — and the Pass Example I wrote for myself ("AI agent for [enterprise vertical], four tier-1 VCs already in") basically describes Autostep with the names redacted. I'm not paying YC-AI-agent-2026 pricing for a category Fortune Business Insights is forecasting at 34.4% CAGR; that growth rate is exactly why every late-stage incumbent is already there.

The Pass Example founder had no public writing on why agents fail in their vertical, and that's the failure mechanism for Founder Conviction — pattern-matching to opportunity rather than pursuing a non-obvious belief. Autostep partially escapes that pattern: Pratt's GitHub @Adawg4 shows years of side-projects (openapi-autospec, Hitsnag, Pinpoint.city), his six-year arc across Keel and Opto reads as a builder who lived inside the "what to automate" gap, and the YC Launch thesis at least picks a side ("discovery, not tooling"). What's missing is the artifact I actually underwrite — a long-form essay on why UiPath Task Mining will lose this fight, or a public dataset on agent-recommendation accuracy. The conviction is real but unpublished, which means I can't verify it before the meeting and the founder-as-thinker filter from the Antoine Buteau frame ("Writing is thinking") doesn't fire.

Capital structure compounds the problem over 18 months. Autostep is a per-seat desktop SaaS with no published pricing, no revenue disclosure, and a solo team selling org-wide installs that require SOC 2 and HIPAA review — that's a cap table that needs four to five more rounds before default-alive, with each tack getting harder once UiPath ships the same loop natively to its 10,000+ customers. The "compounding context" moat language reads like every other task-mining deck since 2021; absent disclosed retention or a structural-instrument twist (revenue share against automated savings, for instance — which would actually fit the "$100K+ uncovered with 10 people" claim), this is a standard SAFE on a standard SaaS curve.

Founder-as-Brand is where Autostep most resembles John Fish's inverse. Pratt's @aidan__pratt sits at 1,384 followers since 2019, the Discord member count isn't even retrievable from the autostep.ai page, and there's no Product Hunt, no press, no newsletter — the founder distribution channel that the $60M Slow Creator Fund formalized as a thesis is simply absent. That's not a disqualifier for a B2B ops product, but it removes the one lever that lets me underwrite a solo-founder enterprise sale against incumbents with billion-dollar field motions.

Category placement is the dimension I weight hardest against Autostep, and it's where the Joe Lonsdale podcast frame lands cleanest: "AI is just more internet." Counterfactual — if Autostep were a crypto protocol observing on-chain agent behavior, or a Teamshares-style rollup of small ops-consulting shops, or a Mogilko-shaped instrument written against a creator running an automation newsletter, I'd score this dimension at the top of the band. Instead Autostep is positioned squarely in the sleeve where I've publicly said startups can't generate alpha, against incumbents (UiPath FY2025 >$1.5B revenue, Celonis ~$13B last valuation) that have both the data and the distribution. The category itself is the deselection.

36-month forecast: Autostep reaches $400K–$1.2M ARR on a handful of mid-market design partners, fails to break into a Fortune 500 logo, and either gets acqui-hired by Mimica or a ServiceNow-adjacent vendor for the team, or pivots away from cross-org task discovery into pure agent-building-as-a-service for ops teams — the discovery layer collapses into UiPath's bundle before Autostep's "compounding context" dataset reaches a benchmarkable size.

The bull case that stings: Pratt is a single founder who has already shipped SOC 2 and HIPAA compliance, kept the team at one, and earned a Neo + YC dual stamp — that's unusual execution density, and the dossier's "$100K+ uncovered with 10 people" claim, if it holds at 50-person deployments, is the kind of dollar-denominated ROI pitch that compresses enterprise sales cycles in a way UiPath's task-mining bundle never has, because UiPath sells a platform and Autostep sells a number. Sublime Security is the Known Exception that could apply — I bought into an enterprise security category before the consolidation wave, and Autostep could be the same shape in ops-intelligence. It doesn't win the argument: Sublime had a non-obvious thesis on detection-as-code that the incumbents structurally couldn't ship, and Autostep's "observe + auto-generate agent" loop is exactly what every task-mining incumbent is already roadmapping. Distribution beats product cleverness in this category, and Pratt has neither distribution nor a structural reason incumbents can't copy the loop. Flip condition: a signed pilot LOI with a named Fortune 500 at six-figure ACV before the next round, or a round priced visibly below the S26 AI-agent median with a tier-1 pass on file — either one and I re-open the file.
