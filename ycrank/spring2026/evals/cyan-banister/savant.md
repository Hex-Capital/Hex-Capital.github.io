# Savant -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Anthony Kim (Yale EECS) and Kevin Yu (Cornell CS) walked into Y Combinator Spring 2026 with a "company brain every AI-native enterprise runs on," and Savant is, to my eye, the cleanest specimen of my Pass Example I've read this batch -- two CS-credentialed founders pitching a knowledge-memory layer that sits directly on top of Glean's $300M ARR run rate and Dust's $40M Sequoia-led Series B. The dimensional signature on Savant is unambiguous: the one rung where Savant stands upright is co-founder governance clarity (Anthony Kim is named CEO, Kevin Yu is named CTO -- not 50/50 cosplay), and the rung where Savant lies flat is "magically weird" non-consensus. A well-governed software wrapper in the most-crowded lane of the 2026 batch is, structurally, a replaceable operator team executing on a memo.

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before Anduril, John Hanke ran Keyhole and Google Earth for a decade before Niantic -- the lived-experience rung is owned by founders whose biography already touched the problem before the company existed. Savant's dossier offers no such intersection: Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu are listed with CS degrees from Yale and Cornell, no prior enterprise software employer, no knowledge-management operating history, no GitHub repos surfaced in search, no Twitter accounts found. The "capture undocumented procedures, exceptions, judgment calls" framing on heysavant.com is the language of someone who read about the problem in a Glean teardown, not someone who got burned by a missing runbook at 2am during an oncall rotation. Savant fails the Anduril-Luckey / Niantic-Hanke template at the most basic check.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling from my Pass Example -- two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, $200B TAM slide -- is Savant minus the FAANG line on the resume. Savant cites the AI in Knowledge Management market at >$5B in 2026 and broader KM Software at $15.92B, the exact TAM-deck move I disengage from, and the competitive set named in the dossier (Glean at $7.2B valuation, Dust at 3,000+ orgs and 300K+ agents deployed, Guru at ~$63M revenue, Credal, Microsoft 365 Copilot Agents, Atlassian Rovo, Notion Custom Agents) is the longest "10-VC-bidding category" list I've seen this week. Savant is not a contrarian secret; Savant is the seventeenth team to point at the same enterprise integration surface (Slack, Salesforce, Google Workspace, Office 365, Zendesk, Intercom) Glean already indexes.

On governance, Savant earns its only structural positive. The HQ Trivia 2017 post-mortem -- Yusupov and Kroll with no tiebreaker -- is what I'm screening against, and Savant's dossier lists Anthony Kim explicitly as "CEO & Co-Founder" and Kevin Yu as "Co-Founder & CTO." That's the named delineation. What's absent is the equity split, the operating agreement, and any history of these two working together before YC -- the dossier flags "no public data on co-founder history" -- so I'm crediting the title clarity but not extending it to "documented exit-conflict protocol."

Savant on the capital-intensity axis is pure software abstraction with a per-seat SaaS inference, no hardware, no regulated category, no rural-industrial domain, no fertility or defense or energy moat. Over 18 months the mechanism that compounds for SpaceX 2008 or Crusoe ~2018 -- technical or regulatory moat thickening with calendar time -- runs in reverse here: every quarter Microsoft, Notion, and Atlassian ship more bundled agent functionality into the same SaaS suites Savant integrates with, the marginal value of a standalone memory layer thins, and the integration list on heysavant.com becomes table stakes rather than differentiation.

Flock Safety I sourced from a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons -- a tactile artifact a normal human encounters in their physical environment. Savant offers me the opposite: a B2B knowledge layer that requires an enterprise procurement cycle, a Salesforce instance, and an admin's API keys before I can observe whether it works at all. The dossier flags "no customer logos, pricing page, or traction metrics; no press coverage, Product Hunt listing, or social-media presence" -- I cannot install Savant, photograph Savant, hand Savant to a friend at dinner, or feel the viral pull the way I felt HQ Trivia. The HQ Trivia exception does not rescue Savant because the override there was explosive tactile virality I could literally play; Savant has no such artifact.

Forecast, 36 months out: Savant is either dead or acquihired below $15M into Glean, Notion, or a mid-tier enterprise wiki vendor by mid-2029, with ARR never crossing $3M; the mechanism is platform-bundling absorption -- Microsoft 365 Copilot Agents and Notion Custom Agents ship the "memory layer" as a default feature inside the suites Savant integrates with, and Savant's 2-person team cannot out-distribute Glean's $600M+ war chest into the same buyer.

The strongest case against my pass is the DeepMind ~2013 exception -- pedigreed CS founders compounding across domains can override the outsider-trajectory bias if the polymath signal is real. Yale EECS plus Cornell CS is not nothing, and an AI-native enterprise knowledge layer is a legitimate technical problem. But Demis Hassabis arrived as a chess prodigy, neuroscientist, and game designer before DeepMind -- three domains compounding -- and the Savant dossier surfaces zero cross-domain evidence for Anthony Kim or Kevin Yu beyond their undergraduate degrees. The HQ Trivia exception requires tactile viral product I can feel; Savant has no shipped artifact. None of my three known exceptions trigger. The verdict flips if Savant produces a signed enterprise LOI with a named AI-native company (Anthropic, Perplexity, Harvey, Cursor) where the buyer articulates that Glean and Dust failed them on a specific procedural-knowledge axis -- absent that, Savant is a memo.
