On round-mispricing, the HIGH rung is Solana at $0.05/coin when generalists had quit crypto post-ICO crash; the MID rung is Sublime Security, where I bought into enterprise security before the next consolidation wave; the LOW rung is the canonical YC AI-agent pitch at $30M post with four tier-1s already in. Arlo Industries sits between MID and LOW and closer to LOW: YC's Summer 2026 RFS explicitly names drone defense, Anduril just secured a 10-year $20B-ceiling Army C-UAS framework in March 2026, and the dossier surfaces zero evidence of a price below batch median or any tier-1 visibly passing. Arlo Industries is being shown to me at the exact moment defense seed is most legible to every momentum allocator on Sand Hill — that is the textbook condition where my alpha goes to zero.

On founder-as-brand, the HIGH rung is John Fish / Bookshelved — owned YouTube distribution underwriting the entire deal; the MID rung is a founder with a niche newsletter and 10K free-acquired users; the LOW rung is a company depending entirely on paid acquisition or hired sales with no public footprint. Defense procurement doesn't reward creator distribution the way the Slow Creator Fund thesis does — but the absence of any public conviction artifact also kills the secondary version of this filter, where I'd at least want to read Deo Arlo's essays on why passive mesh beats active radar before the meeting.

On capital structure, the HIGH rung is Teamshares with its employee-ownership rollup instrument; the MID rung is Ro, my own Known Exception where regulatory schleps justified the capital intensity; the LOW rung is a vanilla SaaS cap table needing five more rounds to default-alive. Arlo Industries sits between MID and LOW: hardware-software defense build with ITAR/EAR overhead, multi-year procurement cycles, a solo named founder for a team of two, and zero evidence of a non-standard instrument — no royalty on sensor licensing, no holdco wrapping the IP, no employee-equity structure. The Ro exception almost rescues this — defense procurement *is* a venture moment in the Slow letter sense — but Ro had a clear path to prescription-rail dominance; Arlo Industries is selling sensors into a market where Anduril Lattice already exists as the integration substrate.

On asymmetric conviction, the HIGH rung is a founder publishing opinionated essays on the category by name with 3+ years of paid obsession; the MID rung is the domain operator without public artifacts; the LOW rung is sanitized strategic-consulting language. Deo Arlo's six years in Israeli defense plus the Technion robotics patent plus the self-reported Iron Dome / Shahed exposure puts Arlo Industries solidly at MID — there is real founder-market fit signal, but I cannot find the essay where Deo Arlo says the non-obvious thing about why active radar incumbents are structurally wrong, and "Writing is thinking" means I have to assume the thinking isn't there until I see it. On category sleeve, Sublime Security is my MID rung defense-adjacent precedent and Solana is the HIGH rung; Arlo Industries sits between MID and LOW because C-UAS in 2026 is the opposite of pre-consensus — Anduril's $20B framework and DroneShield's $13M R&D facility announcement put this squarely in "factory model" defense allocation territory, not the illegible sleeve where I make money.

Arlo Industries in 36 months: still pre-revenue or under $2M in pilot contracts, likely aqui-hired by Anduril, Shield AI, or a European prime for $15-40M as a sensor-fusion talent grab, with the passive-mesh IP folded into a Lattice-equivalent stack. The mechanism is straightforward — solo named founder for a hardware defense build cannot outrun a vertically integrated incumbent that already owns the customer relationship and the C2 layer, and Deo Arlo's frontline Ukraine demos convert to a logo, not a contract, before runway forces the conversation.

The bull case that stings: Ro is my own Known Exception precisely because regulatory complexity made capital intensity into a moat rather than a failure mode, and a passive-emission sensor mesh that genuinely scales accuracy non-linearly with node count is a sensing modality Anduril's Lattice does not natively cover — Deo Arlo's Israeli ecosystem network plus the Ukrainian frontline demo channel could compound into a sole-source contract that no software-trained allocator would underwrite at this price. That case loses against the dossier: the "exponential accuracy" claim has zero third-party benchmarks, the second team member is unnamed for a team of two, and Anduril's just-signed $20B framework gives the Army a default integrator before Arlo Industries can run a single field trial. The verifiable condition that flips me to invest: a signed pilot or sole-source contract with a named NATO MoD or U.S. service branch above $1M at a sub-$15M post — at that point the regatta has a real first tack and I can price the next leg.
