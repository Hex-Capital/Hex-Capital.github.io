# HeyClicky -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

HeyClicky is a Farza Majeed solo project that scores high on founder-learning-rate signal and almost zero on consequence magnitude -- which is exactly the inverse of the shape I underwrite. A native Swift app sitting next to the cursor on a Mac, MIT-licensed at github.com/farzaa/clicky with ~6K stars, talking to an LLM API on a "HeyClicky Agent" trigger, is a productivity overlay, not a redrawing of any economic mechanism. When I ask my entry-point question -- what world-state exists if HeyClicky hits maximum ambition -- the honest answer is "Mac users save 20 minutes a day in Notion, Gmail, and DaVinci Resolve." That is the **pass example** from my own framework verbatim.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence magnitude because the success-state was a civilizational redirection of cognition itself -- that is the only reason I wrote a $50M check into a no-revenue, unconventional-structure entity. HeyClicky's pitch on heyclicky.com names no incumbent regime that collapses, no labor-cost figure displaced, no $15T variable moved. The closest analogy in the dossier is "switches between ChatGPT and Claude desktop," which is friction-removal inside an already-mature consumer-AI stack. Mass cursor-side agents on Macs do not break an economic mechanism; they capture a slice of one Apple Intelligence is openly walking toward. The consequence delta between HeyClicky and the OpenAI rung is not a matter of degree; it is a matter of category.

A typical pre-seed "GPT-4 wrapper on sales calls" is the LOW rung on technical and capital risk because the failure mode is that any Tier-1 generalist can fund it and any product team at Salesforce ships the feature in twelve months. HeyClicky's dossier reproduces this failure mode with unusual fidelity: the codebase is MIT-licensed and the founder publicly invited forks via @FarzaTV status 2041691382008705321 -- "Go forth into the wild and build the future." No proprietary model, no on-device inference miracle, no hardware, no fleet, no plant. Apple Intelligence is the named platform substitution risk, and unlike **QuantumScape** (IPO 2020) or **Genesis AI** (2025) there is no physics here that the incumbent has to also discover. The "miracle count" required for HeyClicky to win is zero, which is the wrong number for my framework.

On founder defiance of expert consensus -- the dimension that pushed me into **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) when the fusion priesthood said two decades -- HeyClicky scores mid-low through a counterfactual lens: for this score to flip a tier, Farza Majeed would need to be on public record contradicting a load-bearing premise of consumer AI agents. He is not. He is an open-source-friendly builder with 108.6K followers @FarzaTV running the standard 2025 indie-AI playbook. That is neither expert continuity nor instigation; it is the median San Francisco consumer-AI posture.

The buildspace history is the only dimension where HeyClicky earns real points in my framework, and the mechanism it compounds through over 18 months is this: a founder who shut down a $10M-at-$100M-valuation company in August 2024 citing inability "to craft an updated direction that feels worth pursuing" (@FarzaTV 1827030900456169849) has a documented willingness to kill his own work rather than zombie-walk it -- a learning-rate signal closer to the **Slash** (2024) pattern than to founder rigidity. But the same fact reads as a follow-through risk: buildspace did not pivot, it dissolved. My fifteen points here split the difference because the dossier does not let me distinguish self-awareness from fade.

On required-capital profile, HeyClicky parallels nothing in my portfolio -- the closest single anchor is the inverse of **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) where the $115M Series A I led was a deployment scale no consensus VC would touch. HeyClicky's roadmap reads as a consumer Mac app with API costs, where ~$2-3M reaches a real user base. I am over-resourced for this deal, which is itself a signal: when the math says "any seed fund can do this," the deal is not mine.

My 36-month forecast on HeyClicky: shelved or quietly pivoted by Farza Majeed with under $1M ARR by mid-2029, no strategic acquirer, the farzaa/clicky repo still up at 8-12K stars as an indie-AI artifact. The mechanism: open-source commoditization plus Apple Intelligence substitution plus the founder's own documented willingness to close projects he cannot craft a direction for -- the buildspace pattern is the base rate.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash** (2024) precedent: YC-backed, second-time YC founder, documented prior pivot, $100M Series C check from me despite the consumer-business-banking thesis violating my consequence-magnitude prior. The bull would say HeyClicky satisfies three of those four override criteria already, and I should let founder-learning-rate carry the deal. It stings because the structural match is real. What dismantles it is that Slash had a *pivot* with a specific commercial result (the Kanye moment routed a real distribution channel into a real revenue line); HeyClicky has a *shutdown* with no commercial successor, and the new product is open-sourced into a moat-free pool. A signed enterprise distribution deal with a named Mac-fleet buyer -- Notion, Figma, or an MDM channel -- attached to a proprietary screen-context model HeyClicky owns end-to-end would flip my verdict. Absent that, the math is the math.
