# Zenbu -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Zenbu is Rob Pruzan, co-author of React Scan (22k stars, 800k weekly installs), force-fitting his second act into the single most consensus-priced category of YC Spring 2026 — "the extensible IDE for coding agents" — at exactly the moment Cursor is in talks at $50B and a same-batch YC company called Superset shipped "an IDE to orchestrate 100+ AI coding agents." That sentence is the entire deal. The asset here is the founder's distribution; the round shape is the canonical Pass Example I described word-for-word above.

The closest HIGH-rung analog I have for the founder-distribution axis is **John Fish / Bookshelved** — a creator with owned reach where the asset on the cap table is the audience, not the cap table. The mechanism that makes the analogy load-bearing: Rob Pruzan reaching 800k weekly developers through React Scan is a real, free, non-paid acquisition channel into exactly the persona Zenbu wants to sell to — which is structurally the same logic that made me underwrite Fish. Where Zenbu diverges from the John Fish rung is that React Scan's distribution is *tool-shaped*, not *person-shaped*: those 800k weekly installs follow the npm package, not @RobKnight__ (4,431 followers, no essay corpus on agent IDEs that I can find). Fish converts attention to whatever he points it at; Rob converts only to other developer-infra primitives, which is useful but not transferable.

The canonical LOW rung on round-pricing is the pitch I described as my standing pass: "AI agent for [vertical], YC seed, four tier-1s already in, $30M post." Zenbu doesn't quote a price in the dossier, but every signal around it triangulates to that exact deal — Cursor at $2B ARR (Feb 2026), Windsurf at $3B (OpenAI, late 2025), Cline at $32M Seed+A (Jul 2025), Superset in the same batch chasing the same thesis. This is the textbook consensus sleeve where I have no edge and Slow has no edge — "AI is just more internet" was my line on the Lonsdale pod, and Zenbu is literally an IDE shell around someone else's open-source agent (github.com/badlogic/pi-mono, maintained by an external author). The pattern that killed the canonical LOW deal — no price discount, no contrarian belief, no structural moat against the incumbent's next sprint — is fully present in Zenbu.

On Capital Structure (mechanism over 18 months): Zenbu is a solo founder, no revenue, no pricing page, an open-source framework (zenbu.js, 222 stars) plus a presumed freemium commercial IDE. That cap table compounds the wrong way — to compete against Cursor's $2B ARR and a Cognition-operated Windsurf, Zenbu needs to hire a 10-person team in 12 months and run paid distribution into a market where the customer's switching cost is *zero*. Each tack of the regatta makes the cap table worse, not better, because the financing instrument is a generic SAFE for a product that has no asymmetric cash-flow shape underneath it. There is no Teamshares-style instrument here, no royalty, no holdco — just standard venture drainage into an arms race I can't price.

On Founder Conviction (counterfactual): for Zenbu's score to flip a tier, Rob would need a public, opinionated long-form essay explaining why Cursor's architecture is structurally wrong and why a hackable plugin model wins — the kind of "writing is thinking" artifact I read before the meeting. The dossier shows none. What it does show is genuine builder-credibility (React Scan, ex-Vercel Next.js core) and a precursor framework (zenbu.js) — which is real but reads as "talented infra-builder picking the obvious next project," not as "missionary with a non-obvious belief other people will talk him out of." Closer to MID than HIGH, but not the John Fish or Drop.io archetype.

On Category Sleeve (single-anchor parallel): contrast Zenbu against **Sublime Security**, my recent enterprise-security entry. Sublime was pre-consensus in a category most security investors had abandoned for late-stage SOC SaaS — the sleeve fit was that nobody else was bidding at seed. Zenbu is the inverse: AI dev tools at the absolute peak of consensus, with the dominant incumbent at $2B ARR, an OpenAI-owned challenger, a $32M-funded open-source agent, and a same-batch YC twin. The sleeve isn't just unfavorable; it's the specific sleeve my "AI is just more internet" frame de-rates by construction.

My 36-month forecast on Zenbu: acqui-hire to Vercel (Rob's prior employer) or to one of Cursor/Cognition for sub-$30M total consideration, or a quiet pivot of zenbu.js into a plugin library inside the broader Pi/Cline ecosystem, with standalone ARR under $500K. The mechanism producing this is plain — solo founder, third-party agent dependency, zero price discipline in the round, incumbent feature-absorption inevitable within two product cycles. The founder lands fine; the equity doesn't.

The strongest case against my forecast is the Cursor-is-closed wedge: Cursor's $2B ARR was built on a closed, opinionated editor that genuinely cannot be hacked, and there is a real power-user developer segment — exactly Rob's React Scan audience — that hates this and will adopt an open, plugin-editable IDE on principle. The bottom-up open-source distribution playbook (Cline already at 5M installs without an IDE) is the bull mechanism, and Rob's 800k weekly React Scan reach is the lowest-cost top-of-funnel any IDE startup in this batch has. What dismantles it: Zenbu's wedge is "hackability," which Cursor can ship as a feature in a quarter, and the underlying agent isn't even Zenbu's — Pi is maintained by badlogic, meaning Zenbu's "core integration" can be replicated by any of the five other competitors I named. None of my Known Exceptions apply: no operator-network sourcing (Rob is ex-Vercel, not Facebook IPG or Kortina-adjacent), no Ro-style regulatory schlep, no Pinterest-style personal-network override. The verdict flips if Zenbu shows, within 6 months, either (a) a non-standard financing instrument tied to React Scan's user base, or (b) Rob publishing an opinionated essay on agent-IDE architecture that earns 50k+ developer reads — neither of which exist in the dossier today.
