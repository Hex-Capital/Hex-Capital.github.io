# Netter -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Netter is a polished European mid-market data platform whose strongest dimension — a faint pull on internal opacity through that "10% dormant receivables recovered" line on netter.ai — is also weaker than my mid-rung anchors, while its weakest dimension — contrarian conviction — places it squarely in the consensus territory I avoid. Samuel Ladroue and Amin Hamzaoui are clearly capable operators inside a 4-person Paris team, but Netter is not the kind of magically weird bet that has historically pulled my check from across the room.

Contrarian Conviction is where Netter sits lowest, and this is the dimension I weight hardest after the biographical question. My HIGH rung here is Anduril — defense tech when Silicon Valley wouldn't touch it, the kind of room that goes quiet when you say the name. My MID rung is RTFKT, weird enough to be genre-defying without being stigmatized. My LOW rung is the AI wrapper from my Pass Example: "consensus AI in a consensus market." Netter sits at the LOW rung. The dossier names Fivetran ($565M raised, ~$200M ARR), Airbyte (~$181M raised, 550+ connectors, open-source), Hex ($96M raised), Palantir Foundry ($2.8B revenue), and Workato ($200M+ raised) all swimming in the same water. No room of sophisticated VCs shifts uncomfortably when you describe Netter — they nod and ask about ACV. That is the opposite of the signal I look for.

Invisible System is where Netter ranks highest, and even there the placement is modest. My HIGH rung is Affirm — Max Levchin attacking credit card opacity, value extraction from people who couldn't read their own APR. My MID rung is Carta, where the opacity was real but the victim was sophisticated (founders, not ordinary people). My LOW rung is generic productivity SaaS where no hidden structure exists. Netter sits between MID and LOW. The nursing-home group "manually chasing receivables across disconnected tools" and the retail chain with "years of unused loyalty data" gesture at internal entropy that extracts from the business itself — but this is enterprise data hygiene, not an opaque cartel exploiting a teenager who doesn't know her rights. The "invisible system" Netter exposes is mid-market mess, not the medallion cartel Uber broke.

Founder's Biographical Forge is where I open every conversation, and Netter does not give me the story. My HIGH rung is James Proud at Substrate, a founder I described publicly as "forged in fire and came out unbroken" — I literally tattooed the company on my body. My MID rung is Levchin: credentialed, but the biography binds him to fintech in a way you can feel. My LOW rung is the Google PM from my Pass Example saying "I saw the opportunity." Netter sits between MID and LOW. Hamzaoui's Mines Paris + ENS Ulm pedigree is real technical pedigree, but pedigree is not forge. Ladroue is positioned as the commercial founder and his pre-Netter operator history is unverifiable in the public record — the dossier flags this directly. When I read "Building Netter" as the LinkedIn headline and no narrative beneath it, I do not feel a person who could not ignore this problem; I feel two smart people who picked a real market.

Infrastructure-Grade Defensibility places Netter near my LOW rung. My HIGH rung is Flock Safety — physical camera network that compounds with every install. My MID rung is Niantic's spatial data platform. My LOW rung is HQ Trivia and IRL, where attention or fabrication stood in for moat. Netter's claimed 120+ connectors sit against Airbyte's 550+ open-source library, which is a definitional commoditization risk. The ontology layer could become a moat, but the dossier offers no evidence yet that vertical schemas across nursing-home AR, logistics routing, and retail loyalty are reusable rather than bespoke. Cascading Real-World Impact is similarly thin against my Uber and Wonderschool anchors — Netter improves margins for Carrefour and Tesco; it does not create new economic participants the way Postmates created delivery workers or Serve Robotics extended that thread.

My 36-month forecast: Netter reaches €2–5M ARR concentrated in French and EU mid-market retail and logistics, then either gets acqui-hired by Fivetran, dbt Labs, or a French enterprise software roll-up at a modest multiple, or persists as a regional SaaS that never breaks into the US market. The mechanism is the dimensional signature — a competent team in a consensus space with a faint internal-opacity wedge produces a competent regional outcome, not a defining company. The Carrefour, Tesco, and Auchan logos are a real distribution signal for that outcome, not for a $10B trajectory.

The strongest case against my pass is the European-distribution exception: Carrefour, Tesco, and Auchan as named deployments at pre-seed is genuinely unusual, and the RTFKT exception in my framework allows that a narrow timing window with an identifiable acquirer landscape can substitute for an infrastructure moat. If those three logos are paid contracts rather than unpaid pilots, Netter has a GTM motion most YC S26 companies can only fantasize about, and the EU mid-market is genuinely underserved by Fivetran's enterprise pricing. That case stings — I have under-weighted European deals before. But the dossier explicitly flags that no public press release, case study, or contract value confirms paid usage versus pilot, and the homepage testimonial ("~10% dormant receivables recovered") is a customer outcome, not company revenue. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a confirmed signed paid contract with one of those three retailers at €100K+ ACV, plus a founder story from Ladroue that explains why this problem found him rather than the other way around.
