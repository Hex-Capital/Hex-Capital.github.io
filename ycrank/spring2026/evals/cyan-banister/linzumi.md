# Linzumi -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Sean Grove is the right founder attached to the wrong-shape company, and that pairing is Linzumi's whole story. The man literally co-authored the "deliberative alignment" vocabulary at OpenAI (the-decoder.com, 2025) and is now selling it as a Slack-shaped wrapper on coding agents inside the most crowded lane of YC Spring 2026 — sitting next to Cognition at $492M ARR, Factory at $1.5B post, Cursor, Claude Code, and the Windsurf carcass Cognition swallowed last July. I respect the founder. I do not respect the silhouette.

Anduril is the HIGH rung on lived experience: Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever touched a Predator drone, so when he pitched defense in 2017 the biography did the underwriting, not the deck. Linzumi's Sean Grove rhymes with that more than any other founder I've looked at this batch — OneGraph through YC W19, acquired by Netlify in 2021, then Principal Architect there, then OpenAI on model-spec and deliberative alignment, and now he's pointing that exact mechanism at engineering teams instead of language models. That is autobiographical, not analytical. Where Linzumi diverges from the Luckey trajectory is shape: Luckey's prior artifact (Oculus) was physical and the new artifact (Anduril hardware) was physical; Grove's prior artifact (OneGraph, alignment infra) was abstract and the new one is more abstract still. The biography is real. The atoms are not.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling I passed on — two ex-FAANG PMs, $20M oversubscribed, four-logo team page — is the LOW rung on capital-intensity-or-aesthetic-edge, and its failure mechanism is that the moat lives in the model rather than the founder, so a polymath generalist rebuilds it in a quarter. Linzumi has a better founder than that pass case, but the category is the same category. "Direct your AI engineering team from one team chat" is exactly the surface Cognition can ship as a Slack integration (and per their docs, already has), or Anthropic can extend from Claude Code, or Cursor can bolt on as a Teams feature. The Matrix-SV-Angel-Decibel-Pioneer-Axiom-YC syndicate stamped on linzumi.com is the literal pattern Long Journey was built to avoid — that's a consensus cap table.

The weirdness dimension compounds against Linzumi over the next 18 months, not for it. When Crusoe pitched gas-flare Bitcoin in ~2018, two or three credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds — that was the tell. Linzumi's seed page reads the opposite way: every name on it is a brand-correct YC-adjacent fund, and Garry Tan is the partner. The $100/month flat-rate-unlimited pricing against Cursor's $40/seat and Devin's $80/seat (buildbetter.ai, 2026) is not a weird business model; it's a land-grab discount that compresses ARPU exactly when compute scales with agent count. I keep waiting to find the thing about Linzumi that sounds laughable at a dinner party. I cannot find it.

For Linzumi to flip the governance score a tier, I need to see who the other two founders are and which one Sean Grove defers to when the spec layer and the chat layer disagree on roadmap. The dossier names exactly one of three — Linzumi's YC page lists only Grove, LinkedIn is "Thinker at Linzumi," and the two names that surfaced in search ("Assil Halimi," "Drew Walker") could not be confirmed and the prominent Halimi is an unrelated MIT nuclear engineer. That opacity is not a pre-seed footnote for me; it is the conversation HQ Trivia's Yusupov and Kroll never had in 2017 and the conversation GameCrush never had in ~2011. I default low and stay there until Linzumi tells me who breaks ties.

Flock Safety in ~2018 I sourced from a Four Seasons Wi-Fi name; I could see the cameras on the way out of the lobby. Linzumi is a waitlist landing page with 79 LinkedIn followers and an @linzumi_ai handle I cannot retrieve a follower count for, and the only artifact I can touch is Sean Grove's AI Engineer World's Fair keynote on YouTube. That is not nothing — the keynote is the artifact, and Grove's distribution as a developer voice is real — but it is a founder artifact, not a product artifact. I cannot install Linzumi, I cannot watch a teammate ship a PR from a phone, I cannot photograph the chat thread. Pre-launch is fine at pre-seed; what is not fine is that even the demo gif on linzumi.com is the artifact a polymath generalist could fake in an afternoon.

Thirty-six months out, my forecast is that Linzumi gets acqui-hired by Cognition, OpenAI, or Anthropic for the Grove team and the deliberative-alignment IP at a sub-$150M outcome, before crossing $5M ARR — because the chat surface gets commoditized by an incumbent integration in 2026 and the spec-compilation layer is more interesting as a feature inside Devin than as a standalone $100/month SKU. The mechanism is straightforward: Sean Grove is more valuable than Linzumi-the-product, and the acquirer pool already employs his collaborators.

HQ Trivia is the steel-man against me — I overrode unresolved co-founder governance there because the tactile virality of the product was so strong I could feel it in my hand before any metric existed, and the lesson I drew is that explosive product-led pull can override the governance heuristic. The bull case for Linzumi runs on the same exception: if the spec-compiles-team-decisions mechanism is real and a 50-person eng team feels the pull within a week of install the way I felt HQ at dinner, the consensus-lane objection collapses and the founder-mirror of OneGraph→Netlify→OpenAI→Linzumi reads like a polymath compounding the way Hassabis did into DeepMind. That case stings. It loses today because Linzumi is a waitlist, not a viral artifact — I cannot feel the pull, only read about the mechanism — and the HQ override required the feeling to come first. One verifiable condition flips me to invest: a public, embeddable demo where a real engineering team ships three merged PRs through Linzumi chat in one week, with the spec artifact visible, before any incumbent ships a comparable Slack-native multi-player surface. Until then it is a respectful pass and a note to watch Sean Grove's next move whether or not it happens inside Linzumi.
