# screenpipe -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Louis Beaumont has a secret, and it is the reason screenpipe is not the median "AI for X" YC pass ? but the secret is a positioning bet, not a monopoly bet. The dossier's load-bearing fact is that Meta acquired Rewind and disabled its desktop app on December 19, 2025, and Microsoft Recall has spent a year cycling through security-researcher disclosures. Screenpipe's thesis ? that always-on capture must be local-first and source-available or it will not survive enterprise privacy review ? is a real contrarian read on where cloud-first incumbents structurally cannot go. But the analytical signature of screenpipe is a company with a real named-incumbent failure mode married to a moat that Microsoft can commoditize in a single Windows update. That pairing decides everything else.

Stripe (2010) is the correct ladder rung to interrogate on the contrarian-secret axis, because Patrick Collison named a specific incumbent failure mode ? PayPal's checkout and legacy processor APIs were structurally hostile to developers ? and the market read that as a settled category. Beaumont's screenpipe names the analogous failure mode: Rewind's cloud architecture cannot survive its own acquirer's compliance stack, and Recall's Copilot+ tether cannot cross macOS or Linux. Where screenpipe diverges from Stripe is that Collison's secret compounded into an integration moat that took a decade for anyone to touch, while screenpipe's "local-first" reading is a positioning statement two competent engineers with Rust and a Whisper checkpoint can replicate ? the forks atzamis/screen-pipe and EvolvingSoftware/screen-pipe already exist on GitHub, which means the secret is public before the moat is built.

The pure-software SaaS pass pattern is where screenpipe's lowest dimension lives, and the failure mechanism is specific: undifferentiated software in a commoditizing market has no pricing-power story at year five because the marginal cost of a replicant approaches zero. Trace it into screenpipe: 19k GitHub stars is a distribution asset, but the source-available Rust codebase means every enterprise buyer's platform team can evaluate a fork before signing the $150/seat Enterprise tier. Microsoft Recall ships at $0 marginal cost on Copilot+ PCs. There is no atoms-heavy capex, no regulated certification depth, no government customer relationship of the sort Palantir (2003) built its 15-year moat on. Screenpipe is exactly the software-only-in-a-commoditizing-market profile the Founders Fund manifesto (2011) was written against, dressed in a privacy narrative.

On monopoly trajectory, the mechanism analysis over 18 months is not favorable to screenpipe. The moat hypothesis terminates at "we are local and they are cloud" ? but Microsoft can ship Recall to macOS or extend to Linux the moment the category matters, and open-source forks are already live on GitHub. Screenpipe's 250k user number and Microsoft/Google/NVIDIA logos are top-of-funnel signal, not switching-cost signal; nothing in the dossier indicates that a Datadog or Adobe user's accumulated screen memory produces integration lock-in a competitor cannot mirror in a quarter. The pricing tiers ($25 / $50 / $150) are published, but published pricing without disclosed enterprise contracts is a hypothesis, not a moat.

On the definite-plan axis, the counterfactual for screenpipe to move from indefinite to definite is narrow: Beaumont would need to name a 2-5 year technical commitment ? a specific on-device model architecture, a specific regulated-vertical certification path, a specific Pipes-library depth target ? that he will not pivot from. The dossier gives us 48+ integrations shipped, three pricing tiers, and a proprietary PII-scrubbing model, all of which read as iteration-mode breadth rather than definite-mode depth. This is the "let's ship integrations and see what sticks" posture Zero to One (2014) frames as indefinite optimism.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is the single-anchor parallel for the founder-lineage question, and screenpipe fails the analogy in the way that matters. Luckey had shipped Oculus to a $2B Facebook exit before he touched defense; the "ousted from Facebook" narrative was a Straussian override, not the base rate. Beaumont's prior Mediar rehearses the screen-capture-to-automation thesis directly, which is real founder-market fit, and Techstars '22 plus OrangeDAO F1 is credentialing at the margin ? but Mediar was not a shipped-to-scale exit, and screenpipe is a solo-founder listing on the YC page with a team of six spanning macOS, Windows, Linux, integrations, and on-device models. That is execution surface area without operator-pedigree coverage.

My 36-month forecast for screenpipe: acqui-hired or asset-sold to a mid-tier productivity or observability vendor (Notion, Atlassian, Datadog, or a Rippling-adjacent HRIS) for $40-120M as Microsoft Recall extends to macOS and enterprise buyers default to bundled platform capture, with ARR plateauing in the $3-12M range and no defense or regulated-vertical anchor customer signed. The mechanism is the commoditization-plus-platform-substitution pincer the dossier itself names as risks one and two, unmitigated by any structural moat screenpipe has publicly built.

The steelman against my pass is that Beaumont is running the Granola playbook one architectural layer deeper: Granola raised $192M at a $1.5B valuation in March 2026 on meetings-audio-only cloud, and screenpipe covers the full screen across macOS/Windows/Linux locally, so the category-leader-at-scale outcome is within reach if enterprise privacy review kills every cloud competitor simultaneously. The Asana (2010) Known Exception is the precedent ? pure SaaS with no atoms-heavy moat, overridden by founder-pedigree-plus-network-effect. But Moskovitz had shipped Facebook to hundreds of millions of users before Asana; Beaumont has shipped screenpipe to 250k users and a prior small automation company, which is not the same override. What would flip my verdict: a signed Fortune 500 enterprise contract at the $150/seat tier with a regulated-industry counterparty ? bank, hospital system, defense prime ? disclosed in the next 12 months, because that would convert the local-first thesis from a positioning statement into the beginning of a Palantir-shaped moat. Absent that, screenpipe is a pass.
