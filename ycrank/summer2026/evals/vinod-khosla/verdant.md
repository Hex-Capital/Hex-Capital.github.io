Aidan Ng is a Georgetown undergraduate Government major selling automated plan-review reports to municipal permit staff, and that single sentence is Verdant's analytical signature: a workflow-efficiency SaaS where the highest-scoring dimension is "consequence" only because permits gate U.S. housing supply, and the lowest is "technical/capital risk that repels consensus VCs" because LLM document parsing is precisely what does not repel them. Verdant is the inverse of what I underwrite -- it is an optimization play in a $4.8B market (Dataintelo 2025) with GovWell already at 130+ municipal customers across 34 states and a $25M Insight Partners Series A (PRNewswire May 2026) sitting two years ahead.

On consequence, the HIGH rung is **Impossible Foods** (2011) -- success-state collapses animal protein production, a named civilizational variable. Verdant's stated success-state is "AI reads applicant documents, plans, and diagrams, flags issues, and drafts the final review report for staff sign-off" (YC company description). But Verdant's own framing does not name that consequence. It names Accela and Tyler's "intake and track" gap. That is a procurement-displacement pitch, not a civilizational-displacement pitch, and the Outlook Business directive applies in reverse: Verdant is leading with the metric (faster reports) and assuming the consequence takes care of itself.

The LOW rung on technical/capital risk is the GPT-for-sales-calls pitch I pass on in thirty seconds, where the failure mechanism is that any Tier-1 generalist can fund it and I am over-resourced for the deal. Trace that pattern through Verdant's dossier: two founders, no disclosed proprietary dataset on verdantapp.com, no patents, foundation-model document parsing broadly available, and GovWell already shipping the identical "AI AutoCheck for staff" feature (PRNewswire May 2026). The capital required for Verdant to hit $1M ARR is the standard $5-10M YC-to-Series-A bridge -- exactly the deployment where I am the wrong investor. There is no fusion magnet, no catalyst-yield miracle, no fleet to deploy. The science risk is zero, and zero science risk in my framework is not a feature.
