# Arlo Industries -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Deo Arlo lived through Shahed-136 attacks near his apartment in Israel and spent six-plus years inside the Iron Dome ecosystem before building a passive sensing mesh for the exact munition class that scared him -- that is the dimensional signature of Arlo Industries: an Anduril-shaped founder fit arriving into a category that has stopped being lonely. The high rung is real and load-bearing; the low rung is that counter-drone is now the most consensus lane in the YC Spring 2026 defense sleeve, with Anduril past $2.5B raised, Echodyne sitting inside a $490M USAF program, and $1.8B in federal counter-drone money already appropriated.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is the right anchor for Arlo Industries on founder-lived-experience -- a founder who'd already shipped a hardware artifact to scale in an adjacent domain (Oculus) before pivoting into defense, with a personal aesthetic obsession that made the founder *of* the place, not *visiting* it. Deo Arlo passes the autobiographical test: living through Shahed strikes is not a memo, and the Technion robotics work with a patented end-effector says he can actually build. Where Arlo Industries diverges from the Luckey rung is the prior-shipped-product-at-scale signal -- ex-CTO of Makrverse with no public scale data is not Oculus, and the two other co-founders described only as "PhD in drones and autonomy and military experts" are not named, which means I am underwriting one biography, not three.

Crusoe (~2018) is the cautionary inverse on magical weirdness -- nobody wanted to talk about flaring gas to mine Bitcoin in North Dakota, which is precisely why the round priced sanely and the founder had room to pivot to AI infrastructure when commodity prices collapsed. Counter-UAS in 2026 is the opposite weather: MarketsandMarkets says 25.1% CAGR through 2030, Dedrone exited to Axon for $130M, Echodyne is in a $490M Air Force program, and every defense-curious LP is asking their managers about Shahed responses. Arlo Industries's only genuinely non-consensus move is architectural -- passive, non-emitting, mesh-distributed where the incumbents are active phased-array -- and that is a real technical bet, but the round itself will not be underpriced because the YC P26 batch will price defense the way YC W21 priced crypto.

On single CEO authority, Deo Arlo is named, but the dossier names exactly one of three team members -- mechanism over 18 months: if the unnamed "PhD in drones and autonomy" is the actual technical brain and the relationship cracks under frontline pressure (Kyiv hiring, Helsinki hiring, San Francisco HQ, Troy NY -- a four-city startup at team size three), the GameCrush (~2011) failure mode shows up before Series A. I'd ask the question before I sign: who decides when the Ukrainian customer wants something the Pentagon hates.

On capital intensity, Arlo Industries sits a step below my Anduril rung -- counterfactual: if Arlo Industries had a frontline-Ukraine contract dollar figure disclosed (even $250K), this dimension would flip from "credible hardware-defense bet" to "validated frontier-procurement company," and the score moves a tier. The product is sensors plus mesh software for ITAR-relevant end users with frontline EU/US distribution already running -- this is the lane that historically produces my outsized winners, and the lane no consensus YC investor wanted in 2017 when Anduril started, even if everyone wants it now.

On physical verifiability, Arlo Industries is closer to Niantic-Ingress than to Flock Safety -- I cannot drive to a Four Seasons café and read its Wi-Fi name, I cannot install Mentat sensors in my backyard, and the only humans who can observe the product in the wild are Ukrainian operators on a frontline I will not visit. The tactile-artifact criterion is partially satisfied by the fact that hardware exists and is being demoed, not just slideware, but the verifiability loop runs through founder testimony and the YC partner's diligence, not my own eyes.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Arlo Industries raises a $15-30M Series A from a tier-one defense-coded fund (Founders Fund, Lux, 8VC, a16z American Dynamism) at $80-150M post on the back of Ukrainian frontline pilot conversion to $1-4M of lumpy ARR, does not crack a U.S. program of record in the window, and ends month 36 as a credible acquisition target for Anduril, Shield AI, or BlueHalo at $200-400M -- the magically-weird-but-not-quite tax keeps Arlo Industries from becoming the category-defining outcome that Anduril already is.

The bull rebuttal that stings: passive sensing in an EW-saturated frontline is not a feature, it is the *only* architecture that survives Russian jamming at scale, and Deo Arlo is one of perhaps a dozen humans on earth with both the Iron Dome operational scar tissue and the Ukrainian frontline access to validate it under real fire -- the "consensus defense category" framing misreads the actual technical bifurcation. I take the point, and the passive-vs-active split is the part of Arlo Industries that almost moves me to write. What dismantles it for me today is the moat: passive coherent location is academically established, the dossier surfaces no proprietary signal-processing IP or patents, and if Anduril's Pulsar team absorbs the architectural insight, Arlo Industries becomes a feature inside someone else's bundle. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: a signed Ukrainian Ministry of Defense or USAI contract above $500K with named-counterparty disclosure within the next two quarters -- that turns the "frontline demos" line in the YC blurb into procurement reality, and I am in the next round.
