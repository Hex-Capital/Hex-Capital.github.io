## The Idea

**Problem:** Veterinary care is reactive — pet owners typically discover disease only after symptoms appear, at which point treatment is more costly and less effective. Current standard of care relies on annual vet bloodwork, which checks a limited panel and often misses early-stage conditions. Gen Z and Millennial pet parents, who control the U.S. pet spending market, increasingly want data-driven, preventative health approaches for their pets mirroring human wellness trends like Function Health and Superpower (a16z Speedrun portal).

**Approach:** Omi provides a longevity health testing system for dogs that analyzes 48+ biomarkers to detect early signs of hundreds of diseases — including cancer, kidney disease, metabolic disorders, and infections — before symptoms appear (joinomi.com). The product includes: (1) a vet-visit blood panel testing 48+ biomarkers, (2) an at-home saliva swab measuring biological age and genetic health risks, (3) an iOS app with personalized health plans and 24/7 AI-powered vet chat with veterinary oversight (App Store). The service is structured as an annual membership including two health tests and an ongoing personalized care plan (joinomi.com).

**Differentiation:** Unlike Embark Veterinary (DNA/breed identification and genetic disease screening) or Basepaws (cat-focused genetic testing, acquired by Zoetis in 2022), Omi combines biomarker blood panels with at-home saliva testing and ongoing AI-powered care management in a single membership. Loyal ($150M+ raised) focuses on pharmaceutical longevity drugs for dogs rather than diagnostics (BusinessWire, Jan 2026). Innovative Pet Lab offers at-home biomarker testing but lacks the integrated app-based care management and AI vet chat layer (petcareinnovation.net).

**Business Model:** No pricing is publicly listed on joinomi.com. The product is described as an "annual membership" (joinomi.com). The iOS app is free to download (App Store). The company claims 2,000 waitlist signups translate to "~$1M estimated ARR" (a16z Speedrun portal), implying an estimated price point of ~$500 per annual membership. [Inferred]: Most likely monetization is a subscription membership ($400–$600/year) bundling testing kits with app access and vet chat, potentially with upsells on supplements or additional tests.

**TAM/SAM:** U.S. pet industry expenditures reached $158 billion in 2025 and are projected at $165 billion in 2026 (APPA, 2026 State of the Industry Report). The global pet health products market is estimated at $14.5 billion in 2026, projected to reach $27.7 billion by 2033 (Coherent Market Insights, 2026). The company's own materials reference a "$94B market" controlled by Gen Z and Millennial pet parents (a16z Speedrun portal), though this specific figure was not independently verified. [Inferred]: The serviceable market is the subset of U.S. dog-owning households willing to pay for premium preventative diagnostics — likely a fraction of the ~$36B spent annually on U.S. vet care and products.

**GTM / Distribution:** The company operates a waitlist/early-access model via joinomi.com, with active social media on Instagram (@omipethealth), TikTok, YouTube, and Substack (joinomi.com). [Inferred]: Initial distribution is DTC via social media marketing to millennial/Gen Z pet parents, leveraging the same playbook as human wellness brands like Ritual and Function Health. The founder's experience scaling Ritual to $250M+ ARR is directly relevant to this channel.

## Defensibility

- **Data moat (potential):** Each test generates longitudinal biomarker data per pet. Over time, this could enable proprietary health benchmarks and predictive models by breed, age, and condition. This moat does not exist today at 200+ users but could compound with scale.
- **Switching costs:** Once a pet's health history and biomarker trends are tracked in the Omi app, switching to a competitor would mean losing longitudinal data and personalized baselines. [Inferred]: This is modest at early stage but grows with each annual test cycle.
- **AI model quality:** The 24/7 AI vet chat trained on aggregated pet health data could improve over time with more users, creating a feedback loop. [Inferred]: This is speculative at current scale.

**Market structure:** Incumbent veterinary chains (Banfield/Mars, VCA/Mars) are structured around episodic, in-clinic visits and treatment revenue. A preventative diagnostic membership that reduces emergency vet visits could cannibalize their core revenue model. [Inferred]: This creates a business model conflict that may slow incumbent adoption of a similar DTC preventative approach.

**Commoditization risk:** The biomarker testing itself can be replicated by any company with veterinary lab partnerships. The AI chat layer uses generally available LLM technology. [Inferred]: The primary barrier is brand trust among pet parents and the longitudinal data advantage, not the underlying technology.

## Key Risks

**Name confusion and discoverability:** "Omi" is shared by at least two other entities — Omi AI (open-source AI wearable, $2M seed, 5K+ Twitter followers) and Outcomes Matter Innovations (@OMI_Health on X). This creates brand confusion in search results and social media, complicating organic discovery and SEO.

**Regulatory and liability exposure:** The app provides AI-powered health recommendations with a disclaimer that it "does not provide veterinary medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment" (joinomi.com/terms). If pet owners act on AI recommendations that lead to adverse outcomes, legal liability risk exists despite the disclaimer. Veterinary practice regulations vary by state and could constrain the AI vet chat feature.

**Waitlist-to-revenue conversion uncertainty:** The company claims 2,000 waitlist signups translate to "~$1M estimated ARR" (a16z Speedrun portal), but this is a projection, not realized revenue. Actual conversion from free waitlist to paid membership is unverified. The app has only 7 ratings (App Store), suggesting limited paid adoption to date.

**Incumbent entry risk:** Zoetis ($90B+ market cap) already acquired Basepaws and could extend into dog biomarker testing. Embark ($91M+ raised) has an established brand in canine diagnostics and recently partnered with Innovative Pet Lab on a canine health study (PRNewswire, 2026). Either could launch a competing subscription product with existing distribution.

**Single-species concentration:** The product currently covers dogs only. Cat owners represent a large adjacent market but would require separate biomarker panels, AI models, and veterinary validation.
