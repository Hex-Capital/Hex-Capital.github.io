# Litmus -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Litmus is a two-undergrad B2B hiring-assessment SaaS at $60K ARR distributing through Ashby, Greenhouse, and Lever — clean-integrity founders on the exact leverage-architecture profile I built the framework to filter out. Shaivi Rau's origin story is her Series A-founder brother's hiring pain, and Elena Zhao's edge is a Meta internship plus incoming Two Sigma; the signature is a coherent memo-founder pair on a single-sided vertical SaaS built inside gatekeeper surfaces. Nothing in my ledger of 273 checks rewards that shape.

Karat is the low rung on leverage architecture — human-run interviews at $10K+ per role, permission-gated enterprise sales, labor-and-capital leverage, exactly what nav.al/product-media says I don't fund. Litmus sits one step above Karat, not five: the delta is that Litmus's assessment generation is software, but Litmus's *distribution* is Ashby/Greenhouse/Lever ATS integrations plus founder-led outbound to YC-network engineering leaders. That is the App-Store-curation / partnership-required pattern I mark down in the Red Flags list. The Litmus repo-context generation could have shipped as an open-source eval harness that developers pulled in themselves — the Stack Overflow (2008) developer-pull pattern — and the founders chose ATS embedding instead. The choice is not incidental; it's the whole leverage architecture.

Perplexity was my Known Exception on the network-compounding criterion because Aravind Srinivas cleared the specific-knowledge gate at top quartile and the AI-cycle timing was pre-consensus. Litmus is single-sided in the same way — each Litmus customer's assessments are economically independent of every other customer, and the 3,000+ evaluated assessments do not make customer N+1's grading better in a way customer N can feel. But Litmus does not clear the two other gates that let Perplexity through: Rau and Zhao are Columbia '26 undergrads without the Berkeley-ML-PhD-scale specific knowledge, and engineering hiring assessments is a mature category with HackerRank (G2 4.6, 553 reviews) and CodeSignal (~$87M raised) as consensus incumbents, not a pre-consensus wave. The Perplexity exception does not extend here.

Mechanism on specific knowledge over 18 months: Zhao's Meta plus incoming Two Sigma gives Litmus real hiring-funnel insider view, and Rau shipped a Python/LangChain pipeline at August Law — the founders are not empty pedigree, they can build. But the specific-knowledge test is "shipped a comparable product to scale" or "multi-year deep-domain immersion," and Litmus's domain is *the buyer's problem*, not the founders' lived problem. Rau discovered engineering hiring through her brother; that is the "responding to someone else's success" memo-founder pattern in the Red Flags list, differing only in that the someone-else is family. Eighteen months of building does not synthesize the decade of hiring-manager scar tissue that would clear this gate.

Counterfactual on market size: for Litmus's Big Market score to flip a tier, the dossier would need a documented adjacent pivot vector — Litmus becomes the AI-prompt-behavior dataset for developer-productivity benchmarks, or Litmus becomes a candidate-side portfolio product distributed through code and media. Neither is present in litmushiring.com or the YC page; Litmus is described purely as engineering hiring assessments sold to hiring teams via ATS integrations. Karat raised $150M+ inside this same vertical without escaping it — the market absorbs capital, not pivots. "Niche markets don't work because the first idea never works" (Venture Hacks, 2007-10), and Litmus's dossier gives no pivot-room to survive the second idea.

Single-anchor parallel on founder trio: Ivan Zhao at Notion (2013) was also young, also unproven at scale, also on a category that looked crowded. Litmus's Rau and Zhao read clean on integrity — no contested exits, no self-promotion about ethics, no 50/50 unresolved-CEO signaling (Rau is named CEO), and the Columbia Spectator (May 2026) piece has the tone of builders, not promoters. This is the one dimension where Litmus scores above the absent-evidence default. But Ivan Zhao had rebuilt personal productivity tools since adolescence; Litmus's founders discovered the domain last year. Clean does not mean deep.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Litmus dies or gets acqui-hired by Ashby or Greenhouse for under $20M as a bolt-on assessments feature, with the probability-weighted median outcome being a HackerRank or CodeSignal free-tier ship of repo-context assessment generation inside 18 months that compresses Litmus's wedge before the round runs out. The mechanism is directly downstream of the signature — a single-sided vertical SaaS distributed through gatekeeper ATS surfaces has no permissionless compounding to outrun incumbent replication, and the LLM-wrapper commoditization risk the dossier itself names has no structural answer.

The strongest bull case: engineering hiring in the AI-tool era is a genuine narrative shift, Zhao's Meta plus Two Sigma gives Litmus real access to elite-engineer employer buyers, and the per-customer grading calibration on 3,000+ submissions could compound into a proprietary AI-prompt-to-code trace dataset no incumbent has — the Clearview AI exception logic, where extreme specific data flexes the other gates. The dismantle: Clearview's specific knowledge was the facial-recognition model itself, not the customer relationships, and Litmus's calibration data lives inside customer accounts under contract, not in a portable model artifact. The Litmus dossier explicitly flags this: "no benchmark or model artifact is public." One verifiable condition flips my verdict: Litmus ships a public benchmark demonstrating their grading model outperforms a frontier-LLM-as-judge on 1,000+ real engineering submissions AND open-sources the eval harness. That would convert Litmus from ATS-embedded vendor to developer-pull permissionless-code leverage — the Stack Overflow pattern — and I'd write the check.
