# TrustAI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

TrustAI is the exact shape of the pitch I described in my Pass Example -- a two-founder MIT team building suggestion-layer automation on top of HubSpot, Salesforce, Slack, and Gmail -- and its dimensional signature is the one I flag hardest: highest on nothing, lowest on consequence magnitude. Hannah Chung's own site frames the product as "runs quietly alongside your tools" surfacing one-click suggestions against a competitive set that includes Bardeen ($25.3M raised, Dropbox and HubSpot Ventures on the cap table), Zapier, Magical, and Lindy. That is not a civilizational variable moving; that is a productivity feature.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence, and I wrote the $50M first VC check without a revenue model because the success-state was AGI displacing the "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" I keep returning to in Fortune (2026-03-06). TrustAI's success-state, per auto.trytrust.ai, is a knowledge worker at a HubSpot shop clicking "accept" on a suggested Slack-to-CRM handoff instead of copy-pasting it -- the incumbent regime that collapses in the maximum-ambition case is Zapier's rule-authoring UX, not a category. The mechanism that made OpenAI load-bearing for me was that no consensus VC could price the consequence; the mechanism that pins TrustAI to the Pass Example rung is that any HubSpot product manager can price this feature and probably ships it in twelve months. That gap is dispositive.

Bardeen is the LOW rung on technical/capital risk, and its failure mechanism is instructive: raised $25.3M with Insight Partners leading the Series A in June 2022, added Dropbox and HubSpot Ventures in August 2024, still ships a Chrome extension for cross-tool automation, still has no dominant position -- because the underlying problem is a distribution race between incumbents with browser context, not a science problem with a moat at the end of it. TrustAI's dossier repeats the pattern exactly: three people, browser observation, "no installation required" as the entire technical claim, no proprietary dataset cited, no patent, no capital-intensive roadmap. There is no miracle here that repels the consensus VC; there is a feature race Bardeen is already losing.

On founder-defies-consensus, the mechanism analysis matters more than a rung: Hannah Chung's LinkedIn shows an MIT Washington DC summer intern 2025 now running a YC S26 company, and Medha Venkatapathy is USA Physics Team '21 with an MIT Lincoln Laboratory stint. This is credential continuity, not the "earn the right by ignoring experts" pattern from my 2013 TechCrunch op-ed. Over 18 months this dimension compounds negatively for TrustAI because the founder profile shapes what the company defaults to building -- MIT-technical juniors default to shipping cleaner LLM plumbing on top of HubSpot's API, not to contradicting the workflow-automation orthodoxy that Zapier and Bardeen already embody.

Counterfactual on learning rate: for TrustAI to score into the Slash exception zone -- the $100M Series C I wrote after Ivan's publicly-told Kanye West pivot proved learning-rate override -- Hannah Chung and Medha Venkatapathy would need a documented prior thesis they abandoned, a shipped product they killed, or a company they sold. The dossier shows first company, no GitHub repos, 715 X followers on @hannah_chuu, no Product Hunt launch. That is not evidence of rigidity; it is evidence of insufficient history to observe rigidity or flexibility. My default of 7/15 is right, and I hold it.

Single-anchor parallel on required-capital: **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) is where I led a $115M Series A because the roadmap required capital depth no generalist could underwrite. TrustAI is the inverse -- a three-person pre-seed on browser context capture, where any Tier-1 generalist writing $500K is over-resourced and where the entire capital story disappears once Salesforce Flow ships a suggestion pane. I would be paying Khosla Ventures rent to be on this cap table.

My 36-month forecast: TrustAI is acquihired by HubSpot, Salesforce, or Bardeen for under $25M by mid-2029 with under $2M ARR at the point of exit, or it shuts down and Hannah Chung and Medha Venkatapathy start their real second company. The mechanism producing this outcome is the one Bardeen already demonstrated over four years and $25.3M -- browser-observation automation is a distribution race the incumbents win by shipping the same suggestion layer into a tool the customer already pays for, and TrustAI has neither the capital nor the moat to outlast that.

The strongest opposing case is the Slash exception -- YC-pedigreed, capital-light B2B, founder-learning-rate override on a consumer-adjacent workflow -- and it stings because I did quietly override my "70-80% of VCs add negative value" stance to write that $100M Series C into a YC company. But Slash had a publicly told pivot story that proved the learning rate before I wrote, and consumer business banking was at least a nameable category being reshaped. TrustAI has neither: no documented pivot from Hannah Chung, no reshaped category beyond "Zapier suggestions instead of Zapier rules." One verifiable condition flips my verdict: TrustAI publishes, within twelve months, a suggestion-quality benchmark against Bardeen and Zapier on real enterprise workflows where TrustAI wins by a factor Bardeen cannot close by shipping the same feature. Absent that, this is a pass in 30 seconds, as promised.
