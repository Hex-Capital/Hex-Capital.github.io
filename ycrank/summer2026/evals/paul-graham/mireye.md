# Mireye -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Mireye is the inverse of the pattern I keep funding: the founder résumé is operator-commercial (Ansh Chokshi's reported Poker Pit 0→$250K ARR exit, Shashwat Kapoor's six years of data-engineering at Tools for Humanity and Lyra Health) while the artifact is silent — no GitHub, no demo on Hacker News, no founder-pain anecdote about geospatial grounding. The dimension where Mireye places highest is animal resourcefulness on the strength of Chokshi's prior Poker Pit story; the dimension where it places lowest is hacker genealogy because neither founder leaves a public technical trail. A company built by operators who once sold something, aimed at a developer-tool surface (MCP), with no shipped hacker artifact in the dossier — that is a recognizable shape, and historically it is not the shape I write checks into.

Airbnb's cereal boxes are the high rung on resourcefulness because the stunt was dated, photographable, and produced revenue from strangers — Obama O's at $40 a box. Mireye's analogue is Chokshi's claim of scaling Poker Pit to $250K ARR in four months before an acquisition, which is the kind of fact that, if true and unassisted, would put him meaningfully above the MBA-team pass-pattern. The divergence from the Airbnb rung is that the Chokshi figure arrives via a YC blurb snippet rather than as a stunt I can see; there is no equivalent Mireye-era stunt — no pre-funding demo, no Cap'n McCain's, no published prototype that 200 developers have stress-tested. Resourcefulness in someone's last company is a weaker signal than resourcefulness this week, and Mireye gives me only the former.

The AI-wrapper MBA team from my pass example failed hacker genealogy because the team had CS credentials and zero shipped code — credentials-only, no GitHub trail. Mireye's dossier matches that failure mechanism almost line-for-line: "GitHub: No public repos found" appears for both Chokshi and Kapoor, neither founder surfaces in open-source geospatial work (no PostGIS, no GDAL contributions, no MCP server published before the YC batch), and the technical lineage of the pair is data-engineering-at-enterprise rather than hacker-community. Patrick Collison had won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp before Stripe; Aaron Swartz had co-authored RSS before Reddit. Mireye's founders have job titles where I want repo links.

On organic-idea fit, the mechanism that compounds over 18 months is that founders who hit the problem in their own workflow keep generating non-obvious feature ideas the competitor team cannot derive from PRDs — Drew Houston kept editing Dropbox because he kept losing files. Mireye's dossier describes Kapoor's prior work at Lyra Health (mental health) and Tools for Humanity (Worldcoin orb infrastructure), and Chokshi's ML at Seismic (sales enablement) — none of which involve querying federal terrain, land cover, or flood-risk data. The idea reads as "we noticed MCP launched in late 2024 and incumbents are wiring up geo-MCP, let's build a geo-MCP startup," which is the spotted-an-opportunity pattern, not the lost-USB-stick pattern.

Mireye scores higher than I expected on the schlep dimension because the counterfactual is genuinely interesting: if the team really does normalize USGS, NOAA, and Census layers into a single MCP-queryable schema with per-cell citations, that is unglamorous federal-data ETL that most YC AI applicants in Summer 2026 would not touch. To flip this dimension into Coinbase-grade schlep territory, Mireye would need to also take on the parts incumbents won't — international cadastral data, real-time risk feeds, the licensing grind with state and county GIS offices. The dossier flags US-only coverage versus xMap's 200-country footprint as a risk, which suggests Mireye has chosen the easier slice of the schlep, not the harder one.

Reddit's Huffman and Ohanian shared a dorm at UVA, and that two-year prior relationship is the high rung on co-founder configuration; Mireye gives me a pair with no described shared history — "No public data on co-founder history (prior shared employer or school not surfaced in WebSearch)." This is the place where I usually hold judgment and let the YC interview surface chemistry, so I apply the default and move on; the absence is not damning, but with two founders and no GitHub and no shared backstory in the dossier, the team is doing none of the work to overcome the default.

My 36-month forecast for Mireye is that Google Maps Platform's MCP grounding plus CARTO's MCP server plus Mapbox's MCP DevKit — three incumbents named in the dossier, all shipping in 2025-2026 — squeeze the US-federal-data wedge into a feature, and Mireye either pivots into a vertical wrapper (insurance underwriting, real-estate diligence, climate-risk for lenders) or winds down below $300K ARR and gets soft-landed into xMap or a Series-A geospatial player. The mechanism is straightforward: when the underlying data is public and the protocol is standardized, the moat has to come from indexing quality or distribution, and Mireye's dossier shows neither yet.

The strongest case against me is Airbnb. I rejected Airbnb in my own head before the cereal boxes flipped me, and Mireye's analogue would be that Chokshi's Poker Pit $250K ARR is the cereal-box stunt I am underweighting because it happened in a prior life rather than in front of me. The bull case adds that Kapoor's Tools for Humanity tenure puts him adjacent to Worldcoin and Sam Altman's orbit, which is hacker-adjacent even without a GitHub trail. What dismantles it: the Airbnb cereal-box stunt was contemporaneous with the company and physically demonstrated revenue-from-thin-air; Chokshi's Poker Pit number is a YC-page claim about a previous venture, and the Mireye-era artifact column is empty — no HN demo, no public MCP server fingerprint, no developer reaching out unsolicited. The condition that flips my verdict is concrete: post a Mireye MCP server to Hacker News answering twenty geospatial queries that Google Maps Platform's MCP grounding answers wrong or refuses to cite, with five named developer integrations within sixty days. Until then, Mireye is the clever-idea-soft-founders pattern, which is where I lose money.
