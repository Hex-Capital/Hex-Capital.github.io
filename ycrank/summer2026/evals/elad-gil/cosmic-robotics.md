# Cosmic Robotics -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Cosmic Robotics is a founder-execution story stapled onto a multi-miracle hardware bet -- James Emerick's Built Robotics pedigree and Lewis Jones's Relativity/SpaceX/JPL rap sheet are exactly the shipping-artifact profile I underwrite, but the underlying architecture (mobile field robot + terrain generalization + certified-operator GTM + a lunar side quest) is precisely the multi-axis dependency chain I was describing when I said I've "largely avoided science projects" on Tim Ferriss #863 (April 2026). The signature here is *high-shipping-founders-on-a-multi-miracle-substrate*, which is a very specific failure mode: the founders are good enough to keep the company alive long past the point where the math has stopped compounding.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on catalyst, and Cosmic Robotics almost fits the template -- IRA-era utility-scale solar is running at a 41 GWdc 2025 target (SEIA Q4 2025) with installer labor costs up 15% annually (IREC 2025 Census), and EPC overhead/margins up ~40%, which is a real unit-economics wound that automation can dress. But Anduril had a *sharp* re-opening -- Ukraine, 24 months, Pentagon software inadequacy exposed on video -- whereas Cosmic Robotics is riding a *slope*, not a step-function. The catalyst is directional, not binary. That's why Cosmic Robotics sits below the Anduril rung on this dimension: the window is open, but it's the kind of window Terabase has already walked $130M through (pv magazine, Apr 2026), which is 30x Cosmic's $4M pre-seed. Same market, worse position on the capital ladder.

The failure mode I want to lead with on the single-miracle axis is the one I've watched kill dozens of Khosla-style hardware bets: the company needs terrain generalization AND unit-economics parity with human crews AND channel adoption through EPCs AND -- per the YC page -- a NASA lunar contract that doesn't appear in any CLPS/LSII/SBIR listing I can find. Cosmic Robotics's dossier compounds those probabilities rather than isolating one. **Color Genomics** (2015) -- my own company -- was structured explicitly to *avoid* this: workflow layer on top of commodity sequencing, so the miracle was distribution, not chemistry. Cosmic Robotics inverted that: the hardware IS the bet, and Cosmic-1A's rate of "one panel every 30-40 seconds" (Electrek, Mar 2026) is at parity with the fastest human installers -- not a step-function, which means the ROI is a labor-arb spreadsheet, not a category re-opening.

*Revenue velocity* -- I look at the dossier and see zero disclosed dollars from Cosmic Robotics. "Tens of thousands of panel installs" (YC page) is a unit-count, not a revenue curve, and SunRobi becoming the "first certified operator" (Solar Power World, Feb 2026) is a channel milestone, not $3B ? $5B ? $8B in months. In the AI cycle I said silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose -- the same logic applies to robotics-as-a-service. If Cosmic Robotics had a compounding ARR curve from SunRobi's deployments, that number would be in the dossier and in TechCrunch. It isn't.

*Founder shipping* is where Cosmic Robotics actually earns its keep. Emerick was an early employee at Built Robotics -- that's shipped-autonomous-construction-equipment-to-real-jobsites experience, not a McKinsey-to-founder pivot. Jones helped launch Relativity's first 3D-printed rocket, which is aerospace-grade reliability muscle. This is the closest thing in the dossier to my **Perplexity** (2022) template -- founders whose prior artifacts predict their execution rhythm. If I were scoring only this dimension, Cosmic Robotics would be a check. The problem is I don't score on this dimension alone.

*Index position* is where Cosmic Robotics fails my Stripe test. **Stripe** (2010) is an index on e-commerce because every entrant in the category must touch the payments rail. Cosmic Robotics is not the rail -- it's *one of several* hardware OEMs (Terabase, AES Maximo, Sarcos/Blattner) competing to be integrated by EPCs that have a channel-conflict incentive to slow-walk any automation that cannibalizes their labor billings. Cosmic Robotics has to *win share* against Terabase's $130M and Maximo's 100 MW deployed base, not ride the category up.

**36-month forecast:** Cosmic Robotics gets acquired by a strategic -- most likely Terabase Energy, AES, or a Blattner-adjacent EPC -- in the $60-180M range as a hardware-and-team pickup, with SunRobi as the reference deployment. Not zero, not a Harvey outcome. The mechanism is exactly the multi-miracle failure mode: Cosmic Robotics executes well enough on terrain and throughput to be worth buying, but not well enough (against Terabase's capital and AES's captive pipeline) to become the independent OEM standard. The lunar contract quietly disappears from the pitch deck by month 18.

The strongest bull case is that this is an Anduril-shaped exception -- Emerick and Jones are the Trae-Stephens/Palmer-Luckey pair for construction robotics, the market re-opening is real (IRA + labor inflation), and the "single miracle" is actually software-on-existing-hardware just like Anduril. I take that objection seriously; it's exactly why I read the dossier twice. But Anduril's exception worked because I wrote a *growth-stage* check at $4.6B post, after revenue was compounding visibly and after the catalyst had already sharpened into procurement dollars. Cosmic Robotics is pre-seed at $4M with no revenue disclosed and a competitor with 30x the capital already in-market. The Anduril template says *when consensus pays* -- consensus here is Terabase, not Cosmic. **Flip condition:** if Cosmic Robotics discloses $10M+ ARR with three or more certified EPC operators beyond SunRobi within 12 months, I revisit at the Series A. Until then, pass with respect for the founders.
