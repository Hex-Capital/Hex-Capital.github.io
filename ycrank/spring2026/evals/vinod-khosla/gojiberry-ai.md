# Gojiberry AI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is a high-learning-rate team aimed at a low-consequence target -- three repeat founders with two prior exits (CoCo.ai, Edusign) selling $99/month seats into the AI SDR category, a market Research and Markets sized at $5.81B in 2026. That pairing is the entire signature. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant, Romàn Czerny, and Dylan Teixeira can execute -- $2.5M ARR in 10 months, profitable, 35% MoM, pivoted into the current product after a failed prior AI swing -- but the success-state of Gojiberry AI at maximum ambition is "more replies per LinkedIn outbound sequence." No incumbent regime collapses. No civilizational variable moves.

**OpenAI** (2019) is what consequence magnitude looks like when the dimension is doing real work: I wrote $50M into a no-revenue, unconventional-structure entity because the success-state was a rewrite of cognitive labor itself -- the "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" frame I gave Fortune (2026-03-06). Gojiberry AI sits two rungs below that. The dossier names the consequence with a number -- AI SDR market $17.58B by 2030 -- and that number is the indictment, not the pitch. A category that small does not collapse an incumbent regime; it redistributes commissions inside one workflow at one persona. Compare to the **Impossible Foods** (2011) test: animal protein production was the thing that had to die. For Gojiberry AI, what dies if it wins? The human SDR, partially, alongside Clay's SDR, Apollo's SDR, Artisan's SDR, and Outreach's SDR -- a shared, divided consequence, not a category collapse.

The pass-example pattern I described -- "we use GPT-4 to summarize sales calls and integrate with HubSpot" -- is the failure mode on capital risk, and Gojiberry AI's stack reads the same: LinkedIn scraping, 15+ third-party enrichment providers, LLM-written outreach, $500K raised per PitchBook, already profitable. There is no science risk that repels consensus VCs here; this is the exact deal any Tier-1 generalist or growth-stage SaaS fund would write. Clay closed $100M at a $3.1B valuation on $100M ARR in August 2025 with the same data-and-channel surface -- that is the consensus market, not my edge.

Founder defiance of expert consensus -- the dimension that pulled me into **Physical Intelligence** (2024) with Sergey Levine and Chelsea Finn -- is silent in Gojiberry AI's dossier. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant ran CoCo.ai (~€500K ARR, sold 2025), Dylan Teixeira ran Edusign (multi-million ARR, sold 2025), Romàn Czerny was Lallemant's co-founder at CoCo.ai. These are competent SaaS operators continuing the SaaS-operator playbook in an AI wrapper. None of them has publicly contradicted a field, defended an unfashionable thesis, or earned the "instigator" pattern from my TED Talk. Founder-market fit, yes. Founder-as-contrarian, no.

Learning rate is where Gojiberry AI loads the ladder. Two documented exits across three founders plus a documented pivot inside Gojiberry AI itself -- $33K in four months post-pivot, 100+ customers in 60 days (Superframeworks 2025) -- is the **Slash** (2024) shape that justified my $100M Series C into a YC-backed fintech on the strength of the Kanye West pivot story alone. If I am writing a check on Gojiberry AI, this is the only dimension doing the work. It is real and it is rare.

Capital profile is the quiet disqualifier. Gojiberry AI hit $2.5M ARR profitably on $500K raised; the roadmap as described needs another $10-20M, not the $50M-$200M deployments where my checks earn their seat -- **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) at $115M Series A, **Genesis AI** (2025) at $105M co-led seed, the $405M OpenAI SPV (Oct 2024). At $99/month SMB pricing with Clay and Apollo on the other side of the data moat, I am over-resourced for Gojiberry AI by an order of magnitude.

Thirty-six months out: Gojiberry AI reaches $8-15M ARR on continued LinkedIn-led growth, then gets acquired by Clay, Apollo, or HubSpot for $80-200M as the AI SDR category consolidates -- or LinkedIn ToS enforcement compresses the signal pipeline and the team pivots into a Clay-style workflow builder. Either way, this is a sub-$500M outcome that returns nothing meaningful against a $3.5B Khosla Ventures fund. The mechanism is straightforward: commodity stack on a platform dependency in a category with two $100M+ ARR incumbents already shipping agentic outbound.

The strongest case against the pass is the **Slash** (2024) exception itself: YC pedigree plus a documented pivot plus repeat-founder learning rate is exactly the override pattern I codified, and Gojiberry AI hits all three. The bull says Lallemant and Teixeira already proved they can build, sell, and exit B2B SaaS, and consensus dismissal of "another AI SDR" is precisely the expert-extrapolation error my TED Talk warned against. The dismantle is in the numbers Slash had that Gojiberry AI does not: Slash was a distinct distribution surface (consumer business banking) without two $3.1B-valuation incumbents already on the field. Clay at $100M ARR with $204M raised is the structural fact Gojiberry AI cannot out-engineer at $500K raised. Flip condition: a signed enterprise contract above $1M ACV with a named Fortune 500 logo within 12 months, demonstrating a moat beyond the SMB $99/month seat -- that would force me to re-evaluate the consequence ceiling and reopen the file.
