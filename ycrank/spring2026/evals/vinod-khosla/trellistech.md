# Trellis -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Trellis is a domain-competent operator team running a feature-parity race in a non-civilizational market -- Lodovico Benvenuti scaled his own property-management book to ~$2M revenue across 150 listings before this, and he is now selling AI agents back to the same operator profile he just left. That pairing -- decent founder-market fit on top, near-zero consequence magnitude on the bottom -- is the analytical signature, and it is the same signature as the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer I pass on in 30 seconds. The dossier's own framing of the problem ("operators stall between 10-50 listings") is an optimization play by definition. Nothing about the success-state of Trellis at maximum ambition rearranges a civilizational variable.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence -- $50M into a no-revenue entity because the success-state was a redirection of how cognitive labor gets done, my Fortune 2026-03-06 "$15 trillion of GDP" frame. Trellis at maximum ambition replaces a few seats of operations staff at vacation-rental property managers between Guesty's $900M PMS and Breezeway's $36.2M cleaning-ops layer. The shared mechanism a Khosla check is supposed to fund -- collapse of an incumbent regime -- is absent. Trellis does not collapse Guesty; at best it gets bought by Guesty. The TAM line in the dossier literally reads "No public TAM/SAM data found" and the benchmark offered is Hostaway at $925M. That is the ceiling, not the floor of consequence. My Outlook Business directive was "too important not to do." Vacation-rental operators going from 10 listings to 50 listings is important to those operators. It is not important to the world.

The **KiOR** (~2008) failure mode was misjudged capital adequacy on the path to a real scientific miracle -- catalyst yield, feedstock economics, refinery integration, three falsifiable miracles each. Apply that template to Trellis and look for the miracles. There are none. The CEO admits feature parity in two weeks via AI, which is not a confession he meant to make -- it is the entire problem. Trellis's "200+ integrations" is not a miracle, it is a maintenance burden that depends on Airbnb, VRBO, Guesty, and Hostaway not revoking API access the moment Trellis becomes a competitive threat. KiOR's risk repelled consensus VCs because the science was hard. Trellis's risk does not repel consensus VCs at all -- any Tier-1 generalist could fund this seed, and several already fund the adjacent ones. The fact that HostAI/Conduit (YC W24, $3.1M raised) is Benvenuti's prior employer doing the same thing tells you the moat width in months, not years.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus: the dossier reads as pure domain-expert continuity. Benvenuti did STR AI at the previous YC STR AI company. Sahagun ran the STR media platform. They are extending the same playbook with better models. My TED Talk cited UC Berkeley's 28,000-prediction study showing experts "only slightly more accurate than chance" -- the Trellis founders are extrapolating the past of their own careers, not defying it. There is no instigator move here, no "I left petroleum to attack petroleum" inversion like Pat Brown at **Impossible Foods** (2011).

Founder learning rate is where Trellis scores least badly -- in 18 months, the compounding question is whether Jan Sahagun's STR Tech Report distribution converts into a feedback loop where SOPs that "survive contact with guests" become cross-customer training data. That is the dossier's stated moat thesis and it is the only mechanism that could move Trellis off the optimization-play prior. But the dossier offers no customer count, no ARR, no retention curve -- only an "84.5% automation rate" and one "100+ units" testimonial. Without a pivot story and without a baseline, the default 7/15 holds.

The counterfactual on capital profile is short: Trellis would have to disclose a roadmap requiring >$50M to first commercial milestone -- a fleet of physical agents in homes, a proprietary foundation model trained on operator behavior, a hardware play in smart-home dispatch -- for the dimension to flip a tier. The dossier shows "Get started for free" pricing and a 6-person team. That is the **Slash** (2024) capital profile without the Kanye-pivot story to override the consequence-magnitude prior. I am over-resourced for this deal by an order of magnitude.

In 36 months Trellis is acquired by Guesty or Hostaway for $30-80M, or absorbed into a consolidation play with HostAI/Conduit -- a fine outcome for Benvenuti and Sahagun, an irrelevant outcome for a $3.5B Khosla Ventures fund. The mechanism producing this forecast is the founder's own admission of two-week feature parity colliding with $409M of Guesty's incumbent capital, which means the path to outsized outcome runs through being bought, not through collapsing the incumbent.

The strongest objection is the **DoorDash** (2013) precedent -- I funded capital-light consumer logistics that contradicted my science-risk rhetoric and it returned a $72B IPO, so why not Trellis. The mechanism in DoorDash was a genuine distribution miracle in a category that did not yet exist, plus founder ambition framing that mapped to reshaping consumer-commerce flow. Trellis is third or fourth into AI-native STR ops; the category exists and is contested; Sahagun's STR Tech Report is distribution to thousands of operators in a market where Guesty already owns the relationship and the channel-manager rails. DoorDash collapsed restaurant-delivery economics into a new layer; Trellis layers on top of Airbnb and Guesty and depends on both for survival. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: signed enterprise contracts with top-20 property managers at $100K+ ACV demonstrating active displacement of Guesty seats rather than coexistence with them. Absent that, this is a pass.
