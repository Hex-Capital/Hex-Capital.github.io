## The Idea

- **Problem:** In-house legal, compliance, and ESG teams at Fortune 500s, consultancies, and law firms manually track laws, bills, and grants across dozens of countries and U.S. states, missing developments on obscure local sites, draft bills, non-English sources, and informal channels (regbase.com, About).
- **Approach:** AI agents scan multi-jurisdictional sources across any country, state, or language to build a real-time regulatory database, with a natural-language query layer and automated email alerts tied to citations of official text (regbase.com).
- **Differentiation:** Company-stated comparison set is Legora, Harvey, ChatGPT, and local counsel; Regbase claims to catch state/local U.S. and non-English regulatory developments these "consistently miss" (regbase.com via search snippet).
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: B2B SaaS subscription priced for enterprise legal/compliance buyers — no pricing page is published on regbase.com (WebFetch, May 2026).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment; adjacent regulatory change management software pricing benchmark referenced by Changeflow (2026) but no sized market figure cited.
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Direct enterprise sales to legal/compliance buyers, leveraging founders' Paul, Weiss network — site references "Fortune 500 companies, global consultancies, and leading law firms" as customer base (regbase.com).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** [Inferred]: Proprietary multilingual crawling/indexing of obscure regulatory sources is the stated technical wedge (regbase.com); no public metrics on source coverage or accuracy benchmarks found.
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Cumulative regulatory database with citations across jurisdictions could create switching costs as customers build alerts, saved queries, and historical comparators on the platform — unproven at 3-person, Spring 2026 stage.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbents (FiscalNote, Bloomberg Government, LexisNexis, Thomson Reuters) are anchored on U.S. federal and English-language legislative data; rebuilding around multilingual sub-national/draft-bill ingestion is architecturally different from their current pipelines (CB Insights, FiscalNote alternatives page).
- **Commoditization risk:** Legal-AI peers (Harvey, Legora) and incumbent regtech vendors (Corlytics, FiscalNote) could extend into multilingual sub-national tracking; Legora reached a $5.6B valuation as of April 2026 (TechCrunch, Apr 2026) and has the capital to expand scope.
