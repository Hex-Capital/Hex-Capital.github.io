# Incandor -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Incandor is a two-engineer enterprise fraud-API play landing into an entrenched labeled-incumbent category — BioCatch sitting at $185M ARR with the Wells Fargo channel already locked — at what reads like standard YC SAFE pricing, with zero founder distribution and zero structural angle on the cap table. The high/low signature here: a mild lift on category sleeve (Slow has written into enterprise security via **Sublime Security**) paired with an effectively zero score on founder-as-brand (neither **Matthew Yekell** nor **Luc Rosenzweig** has a public writing footprint, a Discord, a newsletter, or a GitHub I can find). That pairing names the company precisely: Incandor is a competent commodity-shaped enterprise security seed that could have been written by any other YC S26 team in the same batch.

Founder-as-brand is the lowest rung and it deserves the dedicated paragraph. The HIGH rung here is **John Fish / Bookshelved** — a creator with owned literary distribution where the asset is the person before the company exists. The MID rung is **Marina Mogilko** — millions of YouTube subs, where I structured 5% of 30-year earnings because the asset was already legible. The LOW rung is the canonical YC team with no public writing and no community footprint that fails the "Writing is thinking" filter outright. Incandor sits at the LOW rung without ambiguity: Yekell's LinkedIn headline reads "Vista Equity Partners," Rosenzweig's reads "Stanford University," no GitHub repos surface for either, no podcast, no essay on why label-free behavioral clustering is the right frame. There is nothing on this cap table that compounds for free.

Mis-pricing is where the deal lives or dies for me and Incandor lives at the LOW-to-MID end. The HIGH rung is **Solana** at $0.05/coin when every generalist had quit crypto. MID is **Sublime Security**, a pre-consensus enterprise security entry before the next consolidation wave. LOW is the canonical "AI agent for [vertical], $30M post, four tier-1s already in" YC default I pass on before the meeting starts. Incandor's dossier is silent on round size, price, and who else is in — and silence on round dynamics is itself a deselection signal because price-discipline is the entire alpha. Worse, the tags read "Security, AI" with a self-described ">99% accuracy" line on the website that is exactly the YC-deck language that screams consensus pattern-match into the 2026 AI-for-fraud sub-batch. With BioCatch at $185M ARR and Sardine at $145M raised, this is a category Datos Insights has already mapped with 10+ vendors — the opposite of illegible.

Capital structure is venture-shaped but in the boring direction. The HIGH rung is **Teamshares** — an employee-ownership rollup no other fund can underwrite. MID is **Sublime Security** — a clean SaaS cap table where the venture math actually works. LOW is the generic AI SaaS needing five rounds to reach default-alive. Incandor sits firmly between MID and LOW: it is a standard developer-API SaaS that will need to clear enterprise bank procurement (2-3 year cycles) on multiple rounds of dilution before any tack toward a Series A starting line is even visible. No revenue-share, no holdco, no royalty — just a SAFE into a category where the incumbent was bought by Permira in May 2024 and the next tier is bundled inside Mastercard.

Conviction and sleeve round out the picture. The conviction HIGH rung is **Ben Silbermann** at Pinterest — missionary temperament sourced through personal network; MID is the Sublime founders with prior security operating depth; LOW is the sanitized consulting-language pitch. Yekell's Vista Equity / Cboe / Stanford Management Co background reads as finance career rather than missionary obsession with mule rings and coerced-transaction fraud, and Rosenzweig's NVIDIA/Apple/Epic stack is credible engineering but not a public stance on why unsupervised behavioral clustering beats supervised in production — Incandor sits below MID. On sleeve, **Sublime Security** is the precedent that gets Incandor onto the ladder at all, but BioCatch's Permira exit and Sardine's $70M Series C in February 2025 say this sub-segment is post-consensus, not pre-consensus — closer to the LOW end of the sleeve ladder than the **Solana** / **Allbirds** end.

My 36-month forecast: Incandor fails to displace BioCatch or Sardine in bank procurement, raises a difficult bridge in 2027 at flat or down, pivots to a narrower fintech-only horizontal (think Mercury / Brex tier) at $1–3M ARR, and gets acqui-hired by Sardine, Alloy, or Unit21 for low single-digit millions before mid-2029. The mechanism is straightforward: the >99% accuracy claim cannot survive contact with a labeled production benchmark at a bank that already pays BioCatch, and the "programmable map vs. risk score" abstraction is an analyst-workflow change that fraud-ops teams will not absorb without a design-partner case study Incandor does not have.

The strongest case against my pass is the cold-start argument: if Incandor's label-free unsupervised clustering genuinely works in production, it solves the new-platform/new-attack problem that **BioCatch**'s supervised stack structurally cannot, and the 26.95% CAGR in the fraud sub-segment plus Sardine's $145M total raised proves the category is fundable. Stanford CS + NVIDIA/Apple/Epic systems chops is real engineering credibility for the latency-and-signal-processing work this requires. But the dossier surfaces no published benchmark, no named design partner, no patent, no opinionated essay from either founder explaining why unsupervised wins where supervised loses — and absent that, I am underwriting a consensus-priced commodity-category seed against incumbents with decade-long bank channels. The verifiable condition that would flip me: a named tier-1 bank or fintech (Plaid, Mercury, Brex, Chime) signs a deployed POC with public cold-start metrics outperforming BioCatch on a labeled production dataset — at which point I'd revisit, ideally on a non-standard structure or a discounted insider round, not at this SAFE.
