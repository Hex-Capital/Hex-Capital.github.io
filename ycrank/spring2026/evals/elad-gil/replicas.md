# Replicas -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Replicas is a competent-builder-on-commoditizing-substrate company: Connor Loi shipped Helicone's AI Gateway at YC W23 -- which is literally the multi-vendor LLM proxy pattern Replicas is now re-applying to coding agents -- but the substrate underneath (Claude Code, Codex) is being natively extended by Anthropic itself via Claude Code Remote Tasks (Mar 20 2026 per Blink Blog). That high-founder-fit-on-vendor-substituting-platform pairing is Replicas's analytical signature, and in my portfolio it most resembles the wrapper-on-OpenAI deals I've consistently passed on rather than the **Decagon** (2023) or **Abridge** (2023) workflow-infra bets I funded.

**Perplexity** (2022) is my HIGH rung for founder ship-rhythm -- Aravind would "show up a week later with the thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and that compounding artifact rate, not pedigree, is what bought my check. Replicas's Loi has a real shipping trace: Helicone AI Gateway + Prompts in 8 months, a CalHacks 11.0 "Best Use of Vapi" win on LiveStory, a V1 launched roughly two months pre-dossier, and an active @connortbot launch tweet. The mechanism load-bearing here is *prior-shipped-LLM-infra-in-the-exact-stack* -- AI Gateway is the multi-vendor proxy abstraction Replicas needs internally. Where Replicas diverges from the Perplexity HIGH rung is that the dossier shows no cold-inbound "built between our calls" cadence to me specifically and no visible weekly artifact stream on @connortbot beyond the V1 launch tweet -- so I credit founder-market fit strongly, but I cannot credit the Perplexity-grade execution rhythm I write checks on.

**Lyft** Series C is my pass template here, and the failure mechanism was structural, not founder-quality: Lyft sat in a winner-take-all market where #2 captures a fraction of #1's economics (20VC interview), and no amount of execution inverts that. Replicas's index-position problem is the same shape with a different vendor: it is a BYO-credentials wrapper on Claude Code and Codex, trigger-agnostic across Slack/Linear/GitHub, while Anthropic shipped Claude Code Remote Tasks on Mar 20 2026 and Anysphere shipped Cursor Background Agents on Feb 24 2026 -- both natively in the workflow path Replicas occupies. Replicas does not become an "index on the coding-agent category"; it must beat Cursor ($2B ARR, talks at $50B pre-money per Next Web Apr 2026) and Cognition ($492M ARR, $26B post-money per TechCrunch May 27 2026) on integration breadth alone, and the model vendors themselves are the substitution risk. That is exactly the "#2 in a winner-take-all-but-not-by-you" pattern from my Lyft pass.

Replicas's market-catalyst score uses mechanism analysis: the autonomous-PR threshold did cross in 2026 (Cursor Background Agents Feb, Claude Code Remote Tasks Mar, Cognition's 13× ARR ramp by May), which is a real "skating for the puck" window. But the catalyst compounds *against* Replicas over 18 months because every quarter the underlying model vendors absorb more of the orchestration surface natively -- BYO-wrapper economics compress as Anthropic and OpenAI ship the Slack/Linear/GitHub triggers themselves. The catalyst is real; Replicas just isn't uniquely positioned for its window the way **Anduril** (2022) was for post-Ukraine defense.

Replicas's revenue-velocity score is set by counterfactual: to flip this dimension a tier, the dossier would need named monthly ARR with month-over-month growth like the **Harvey** (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" arc (TechCrunch Nov 2025). What Replicas discloses instead is "20+ YC startups," 12 logos (Mintlify, Composio, Helicone, Dart, Knowunity, Trellis, Chronicle Labs), a ">30% of PRs" company-stated claim, and no dollar figures. In an AI cycle where "if you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863), silence on $ at the moment Cognition reports $492M ARR is itself the signal -- real velocity is easy to disclose.

Replicas's single-miracle score uses the **Decagon** (2023) parallel: Decagon assumed LLM accuracy at customer-support tasks and treated everything downstream as GTM and product execution, which is why I funded it. Replicas inherits a similar single-miracle shape -- autonomous PR generation is empirically working at Cursor and Cognition, so Replicas's remaining work is orchestration plumbing and distribution, not new science. This is the dimension where Replicas earns its strongest mark, and it's why this isn't a Strong Pass.

My 36-month forecast on Replicas: acqui-hire by a coding-agent platform (most likely Cursor, Factory.ai, or Cognition) for under $50M in equity-and-talent value, or sunset after Claude Code Remote Tasks plus Cursor Background Agents plus a Factory.ai enterprise push compress the BYO-orchestration wedge to sub-$5M ARR. The mechanism is platform-vendor absorption of the orchestration layer Replicas resells, with Loi and Arora landing soft at one of the acquirers given their Helicone/Ramp pedigree.

The strongest bull case on Replicas is that Loi is doing to coding-agents what Helicone (YC W23) did to LLM observability: build the multi-vendor abstraction Anthropic and OpenAI structurally *won't* build because each only wants its own model in the loop -- and that the trigger-agnostic Slack/Linear/GitHub/API surface becomes the "Zapier of coding agents" that engineering orgs running both Claude Code and Codex consolidate on. Loi has shipped this exact abstraction shape before in production. The reason this bull case stings but doesn't win: Helicone itself is the falsification -- the multi-vendor LLM-infra abstraction has not, in the three years since Helicone's W23 batch, produced an index outcome at the scale that pencils against Cursor's $2B ARR or Cognition's $26B post-money. Multi-vendor wrappers in AI infra have repeatedly compressed to feature-sized exits. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: Replicas crosses $3M ARR with >25% MoM growth and at least three non-YC enterprise logos signed in a single quarter -- that would invalidate the customer-concentration-in-YC-network risk and prove "just working" rather than YC-network "false signal."
