# FinalDose -- Elad Gil Evaluation

FinalDose is three Oxford PhDs proposing a programmable DNA-sensing kill-switch that destroys "80% of cancers existing drugs cannot reach" -- and the dimensional signature is unambiguous: the highest rung the dossier earns is Jeff Liu's prior $15M-raised Vivid Dx, and the lowest is a multi-miracle dependency chain where efficacy, specificity, delivery modality, and ~$100M+ capitalization all have to land independently. FinalDose is the precise archetype I've spent a decade explaining I don't write checks into -- "capitalization issues and science risk and market risk" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) -- and the dossier doesn't carry the structural overrides that produced **Color Genomics** (2015) or **Anduril** (2022).

The single-miracle ladder is where FinalDose collapses fastest. **Decagon** (2023) sat at the top of that ladder for me because the LLM accuracy miracle had already happened and the remaining work was GTM into post-LLM customer support. **Anduril** (2022) sat mid-rung because software-on-defense distribution was the one unsettled variable; the autonomy stack existed. Fusion startups and novel-battery-chemistry pitches sit at the bottom because each miracle is independent and the probabilities compound the wrong way (NFX, *Unusual Signs of Billion-Dollar Companies*). FinalDose belongs on that bottom rung: the YC Launch page asserts a DNA-sensing protein "undergoes a sharp structural change on recognition" that the team has "hijacked into a kill switch," but the dossier confirms no in vivo efficacy data, no off-target benchmarks, and an undisclosed delivery modality (LNP, AAV, otherwise). That's three independent unknowns before you get to IND, and standard preclinical-to-IND timelines of 3-5+ years sit on top. This isn't a single-miracle bet; it's the textbook multi-miracle chain.

The market re-opening dimension is the one place a generous read might apply. **Anduril** (2022) is the canonical HIGH rung -- Ukraine inside a 24-month window catalyzed Pentagon software procurement, a specific re-opening peers misread as a steady-state graveyard. FinalDose's dossier offers, by contrast, only an [Inferred] "maturation of DNA-sensing protein structural biology and large-scale CRISPR screening pipelines" as the why-now. That is not a named catalyst inside a 24-month window; it is a category that has been compounding quietly across the post-Casgevy decade, with **Beam Therapeutics** and **CRISPR Therapeutics** already public and **Intellia** running in vivo trials. If the catalyst were real, FinalDose's dossier would name a 2024-2026 paper or regulatory clearance that opened the door -- it does not.

Revenue velocity compounds the problem mechanically. In an 18-month window, FinalDose has no revenue path: preclinical biology produces no MoM curve, no enterprise logos, no DAU. My **Harvey** (2023) framing -- "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" tracking real bookings (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) -- has no analog in therapeutics-stage biotech, which is exactly why I almost never write these checks. The dimension cannot compound for FinalDose for at least 36 months absent a major-pharma option deal, and the dossier names no Beam-style or Sanofi-Scribe-style partnership. "Silence on growth" against my own thesis is itself a negative signal, and biotech structure doesn't earn an exception -- it earns a reclassification into the science-project bucket I avoid.

The index-position dimension fails by counterfactual: the score flips a tier only if FinalDose is workflow infrastructure on top of a hard thing, not the hard thing itself. **Color Genomics** (2015) sits on the workflow-on-commodity-sequencing layer; **Applied Intuition** sits on software-on-autonomy. FinalDose, by the dossier's own framing, is the therapeutic asset -- a single molecule reprogrammed by guide sequence. It must *beat* Beam (~$1B+ raised), CRISPR Therapeutics (Casgevy approved with Vertex), and Intellia on clinical outcomes, not sit in the workflow path that each of them touches. To flip this tier, FinalDose would need to license its DNA-sensing scaffold to Beam and Intellia as a programmable kill-switch *layer* rather than running its own pipeline -- the dossier shows no such intent.

Founder-shipping is where FinalDose earns its only real points, via a single-anchor parallel to **Brex** (2017): Jeff Liu co-founded Vivid Dx and raised $15M for sepsis diagnostics -- that's an actual shipped venture-backed artifact, not pedigree-only. Li-Yao Huang built a Cas9 discovery-to-validation framework; Steven Lin built an ML coevolution method at Gutsee Biotech. These are credentialed academic shippers, not the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind Srinivas pattern of "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863). Academic papers and one prior sepsis diagnostics raise are real signal in life sciences, but they don't carry the dimension to its top rung, and three equal-equity Oxford co-founders without a publicly named final decision-maker quietly trips the NFX *Unusual Signs* equal-founder-equity flag.

My 36-month forecast on FinalDose: still preclinical, no IND filed, raised a $15-30M Series A from biotech specialists (a16z Bio, ARCH, Sofinnova type) rather than generalist tech capital, no clinical data, zero revenue -- alternatively, dead from inability to bridge to that round. The mechanism is the multi-miracle math: efficacy × specificity × delivery × capital, each independent, compounding against them inside the window where YC-stage capital runs out. FinalDose will not look like **Harvey** (2023) on any month-over-month curve in that window; it will look like a biotech preclinical asset, which is a different asset class than the one my framework underwrites.

The strongest objection is the **Color Genomics** (2015) exception -- I have backed Oxford-credentialed biology before, so why not FinalDose? Because Color was *operated*, not passively backed, and I architected it explicitly as workflow infrastructure on top of *commodity* sequencing -- the hard-tech layer was already de-risked by Illumina. FinalDose is the inverse: the hard-tech layer (programmable DNA-sensing therapeutic) *is* the bet, and I am being asked to underwrite the chemistry, not the workflow. The exception doesn't transfer. One verifiable condition would flip my verdict: a published in vivo specificity benchmark from FinalDose showing tumor-cell-selective elimination without healthy-cell apoptosis in a named murine model, paired with a Beam-or-Intellia-scale optioning deal inside 12 months -- at that point the single miracle has narrowed to delivery and GTM, and I would re-underwrite at Series A.
