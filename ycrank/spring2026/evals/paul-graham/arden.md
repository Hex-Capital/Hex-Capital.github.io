# Arden -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Arden is a schlep-zone bet placed by founders who haven't personally lived the schlep -- Aryaman Khanna's Databricks AI tenure and David Lomelin's MIT CSAIL LLM post-training work give them the agent-building chops, but neither has ever sat in a Big Four audit room watching a senior associate screenshot a ServiceNow ticket at 11 p.m. on quarter-end. SOX testing is exactly the kind of unpleasant, regulator-shadowed grind I praise in "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012); the trouble with Arden is that the schlep was identified by reading KPMG's $2.3M annual-spend statistic, not by suffering through it.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the right HIGH-rung comparison on the schlep axis -- Brian Armstrong picked bitcoin compliance because he wanted to live inside the regulatory grind, and the grind became the moat. Arden picks the same kind of terrain: PCAOB-acceptable workpapers, 20+ enterprise integrations across Workday/SAP/NetSuite/Okta, SOC 2 Type I in progress. That is real schlep surface, and it is the most thesis-aligned thing in the Arden dossier. Where Arden diverges from the Coinbase rung is the asymmetry of motivation -- Armstrong was already buying bitcoin and getting his accounts frozen; Khanna and Lomelin appear to have surveyed the market and chosen the ugliest-looking square on the board. The schlep is genuine; the appetite for it is unproven, and SOX schleps reveal themselves slowly -- the first Big Four audit partner who refuses to accept an Arden workpaper is when we'll learn whether these two stay or flinch.

The pass example in my own file is the polished MBA AI-wrapper team with a deck and no shipped artifact, and Arden does not look like that -- but Arden's organic-idea profile sits closer to that low rung than I would like. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and built **Dropbox** (2007) for himself; the Collisons had been hacking commerce since they were teenagers before **Stripe** (2010). Arden's dossier contains no analogous fact -- Khanna's prior work is at Databricks on AI infrastructure, Lomelin's prior company Blueprint was UGC marketing automation, and neither LinkedIn surfaces a CPA, an internal-audit rotation, or even a finance-adjacent internship. "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) said the best ideas are something the founders themselves want; Arden looks like something the founders identified as wanted by a stranger reading a KPMG survey.

Mechanism analysis on the hacker-genealogy axis: Khanna co-authored NeurIPS 2025 research on privacy-preserving federated learning (HypeFL), Lomelin came out of MIT CSAIL with LLM post-training research and ships under @jdl20515 -- that compounds over 18 months because computer-use agents navigating 20+ enterprise SaaS surfaces is a genuinely hard agent-orchestration problem, and these two can probably build it. It is not Patrick Collison winning Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp at 16, but it is real shipping lineage and I weight it above the default.

Counterfactual on animal resourcefulness: for Arden to flip from a mid-quartile score to top-quartile here, the dossier would need to show one of Khanna or Lomelin having walked into a public-company internal-audit department uninvited and run a free SOX test cycle by hand, or having built an evidence-pull bot for a Fortune 500 controller before raising a dollar. What the dossier shows instead is 20+ integrations listed on a website and an "approximately one hour" enterprise setup claim with zero named customers -- shipped product, not shipped stunt. That is the gap between Arden and the **Airbnb** (2009) cereal-box moment, which I keep re-extracting as the canonical animal test.

Single-anchor parallel on cofounder configuration: **Reddit** (2005) was Huffman and Ohanian as University of Virginia dorm roommates, two people who had already failed at MyMobileMenu together before pitching me. Arden is a Berkeley CS grad and an MIT undergraduate with no disclosed prior overlap -- the dossier explicitly notes "no public data on co-founder history." Two technical founders is the right count; the unknown is whether they fight well together when the first auditor rejects a workpaper, and the dossier does not tell me.

36-month forecast: Arden gets acquired by Optro or Workiva for $30-80M as an AI-features acqui-team within 24 months, after Petual's $20M a16z round (April 2026) wins the first three enterprise SOX deals and Arden's 2-person team cannot match the sales motion -- a Kiko-shaped outcome with better engineering. The signature drives this: a real schlep that the founders can technically attack but did not personally suffer typically produces a credible product that loses the channel war to either a better-resourced peer or an incumbent that bolted on AI.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Stripe** (2010) precedent in my own Known Exceptions list -- I funded the Collisons even though they bent the YC process, because the prior Auctomatic shipping outcome made the artifact override the domain question. Arden's Khanna-Lomelin pair has shipped real artifacts (Databricks AI, NeurIPS, Blueprint at 2,500+ businesses) and the 20+ integrations live today is more than most P26 companies have built; if the agent infrastructure they're putting down is genuinely Stripe-for-audit-evidence, the domain ignorance gets fixed by hiring one ex-Big-Four manager. The reason this objection does not flip me: the Collisons had Auctomatic *in the same domain* as Stripe -- commerce APIs preceded payments APIs. Arden has no audit-domain Auctomatic; the analogous prior artifact would be a tool one of them built while doing internal audit at Databricks, and it does not exist. The single condition that flips me to invest: a named Big Four firm or Fortune-500 CFO publicly accepting an Arden-generated workpaper in a real SOX cycle within six months -- that is the cereal-box equivalent for this category, and the dossier currently shows zero of it.
