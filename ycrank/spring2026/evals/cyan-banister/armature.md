# Armature -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Armature is a competent operator's bet planted in the single most-consensus YC AI category of Spring 2026 — Louis Scremin actually shipped MCP servers in production at Joko before this, which is the lived-experience signal I look for, but Armature is selling agent-testing infrastructure into a lane where Braintrust just closed an $80M Series B at $800M and LangSmith is sitting at a $1.25B valuation. The founder is *of* the problem; the category is *of* the herd. That mismatch is Armature's whole story.

Palmer Luckey at **Anduril** (2017) shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before pivoting to defense — the founder had built the comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain before pitching me, which is the trajectory I underwrite. Armature's Louis Scremin led "AI Agents & MCP servers" at Joko (LinkedIn, theorg.com), and the team's own copy says "we've deployed MCPs used by millions of customers and built observability products from the ground up" (armature.tech, May 2026). That is autobiographical, not analytical — he is solving for the version of himself who was paging at 2am when an MCP regression broke a Joko workflow. Where Armature diverges from the Anduril rung is the asymmetry inside the co-founder pair: Theodore Otzenberger spent ~5 years at Palantir, which is adjacent enterprise software but not the MCP-shipping seat Scremin occupied — half the founding team lived the problem, half observed it.

The Pass Example I keep on the wall is the consensus YC AI-agent darling — six top funds chasing, $200B TAM slide one, oversubscribed seed — and Armature sits closer to that profile than to any magically-weird company I have ever written into. Braintrust ($80M Series B at $800M, Axios Feb 2026), LangChain/LangSmith ($125M at $1.25B, Fortune Oct 2025), Arize ($70M Series C, TechCrunch Feb 2025), and Datadog LLM Observability are all in-market with overlapping product surface; Braintrust already ships an MCP server integration. I am supposed to find them *before* it becomes consensus, and Armature's competitive page reads like an arrival announcement at consensus. No credible investor has visibly passed on Armature for aesthetic or capital-intensity reasons — there is no whiff of the SpaceX "rockets are uninvestable" tell that signals the round is underpriced.

On founder governance — mechanism analysis — Armature is a two-person team with no named CEO in the dossier and both founders intersecting at the same prior employer (Joko, also an investor). The HQ Trivia (2017) and GameCrush (~2011) lessons compound over 18 months when product-market-fit pressure forces a decision neither co-founder has authority to overrule alone; the failure mode is not visible at the seed, it surfaces when a Series A board wants a pivot and the founding pair splits. Armature's dossier shows no tiebreaker protocol, no equity split, no vesting language — I apply the absent-evidence default and call it a near-term blow-up risk masquerading as harmony.

On capital-intensity — counterfactual — Armature would have to be selling not a $79-$199/mo SaaS dashboard but a regulated/hardware/energy adjacency for the score to flip. The pricing page (armature.tech, May 2026) tells me everything: Starter $79, Pro $159, Enterprise custom. This is the undifferentiated software lane I down-weight by default. If Armature were instead deploying agent-execution sandboxes into defense MCPs or FDA-cleared health-agent surfaces — domains where the regulatory moat compounds — I would be in a different conversation. It is not.

On physical-world verifiability — single-anchor parallel to **Flock Safety** (~2018) — Flock I could spot in the wild by reading a Wi-Fi name at a Four Seasons café; the camera was a tactile artifact bolted to a pole. Armature is an MCP endpoint plus a dashboard, and the value lives entirely in founder testimony about catching "the wrong tool picked, the off-script retry, the path that only fails on Opus 4.7" (armature.tech, May 2026). I cannot install Armature, I cannot photograph it, I cannot hand it to a friend and watch their face. That is the developer-tools abstraction lane where my pattern-recognition gifts go quiet.

My 36-month forecast for Armature is acqui-hire by Braintrust, Datadog, or LangChain for low-single-digit millions, *or* a flat ~$1-3M ARR existence as a niche MCP-specific tool that never escapes the eval/observability gravity well. The mechanism is competitive crowding plus a category whose defensibility lives in the integration breadth incumbents already have — Braintrust serves Notion, Replit, Cloudflare, Ramp, Dropbox (SiliconAngle, Feb 2026), and Armature is not going to out-distribute that with two founders and a YC stamp.

The strongest case against my pass is that Scremin genuinely shipped MCPs at Joko at scale and Palantir + Joko both put money in — these are not strangers, these are the people who watched him build the thing. If Armature's wedge — live multi-harness agent execution driving real workflows, not passive trace logging — is genuinely distinct enough that Braintrust would have to rebuild rather than extend, then founder-market fit plus a real product gap could compound into the agent-testing standard the way Crusoe (~2018) compounded into AI infrastructure. The bull case stings because Scremin is exactly the kind of operator I take meetings with. But founder fit alone is not my thesis — it combines with magically-weird OR capital-intensive lane, and Armature has neither. The flip condition is concrete: a signed enterprise contract with a named frontier-model lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, Cursor) at $250K+ ACV in the next two quarters would force me back to the table, because that converts the "consensus category" critique into "Armature is the chosen vendor inside consensus" — a different game entirely.
