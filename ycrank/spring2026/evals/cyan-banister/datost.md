# Datost -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Datost is a clearly-led but utterly consensus AI SaaS wrapper -- Maceo Cardinale Kwik is unambiguously the CEO and Jason Hy Wang is "Co-Founder" without title contest, which is the cleanest thing in the dossier, paired against the weakest possible reading on "magically weird" given Tracxn counts 47 active competitors and TextQL alone has raised $21.1M for the same text-to-SQL thesis. That high/low pairing is the signature: governance is fine, the lane is a stampede. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game here looks exactly like the game it seems to be -- "AI data analyst in Slack" is the YC Spring 2026 batch's median pitch, not its outlier.

The consensus YC AI-agent pass example I keep in my head -- two ex-FAANG PMs, Stanford CS, deck opens with TAM, founder discovered the problem in a memo -- is the rung Datost sits just above. The shared mechanism with that pass is that the moat lives in the model, not the founder: Datost's sole disclosed differentiator is 75.2% on BIRD-Interact versus 33% for Claude Opus 4.6, which means the edge is the prompt scaffold and semantic layer over a frontier LLM that improves every quarter underneath them. Where Datost diverges upward from the pass example is that Maceo actually was a full-stack engineer at Traba and felt the analyst-capacity wall in his own ticket queue, which is more lived contact than the memo-discovers-the-market profile -- but Traba is one job out of a 4.0 Virginia Tech CS degree, not a decade of John Hanke at Keyhole before Niantic. It's a rebuttal to my pass, not a refutation.

Crusoe is the rung that owns "magically weird" -- gas flares, North Dakota, Bitcoin, three uninvestable things stapled together, and almost no one chasing it. Datost is the inverse mechanism: Hex at $36/editor/mo is already there, Snowflake Cortex and Databricks Genie are already there, Athenic raised $4.28M in Aug 2023, Alkemi literally markets itself as "AI data teammate in Slack." The Long Journey thesis is to find founders before it becomes consensus, and Datost has been announced via a @ycombinator launch tweet into a market where the consensus has already congealed. If five credible BI incumbents and a Series-A-funded competitor are already shipping the same product surface, the round is going to be priced as if the outcome is decided -- which is when I leave the table.

Founder-surfaced-from-lived-experience compounds the wrong way over 18 months for Datost. Maceo's Traba exposure gave him the problem statement but not the scar tissue: Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus before Anduril, John Hanke ran Keyhole before Niantic, Chase Lochmiller was quant-adjacent to oilfield operators before Crusoe -- each had a prior product at scale that made the next one inevitable. Maceo's GitHub shows a top owned repo at 1 star and contributor status on OpenDSA; Jason Hy Wang has no public technical footprint at all per the dossier. That is a perfectly normal pre-seed posture, and it would not bother me if the lane were "magically weird," but compounded against 47 competitors it means in month 18 the question becomes "why these two and not the TextQL team that has $21M and a year head start?"

Capital-intensity would flip a tier if Datost were doing something I cannot install from a Slack app directory. The score moves from a 3 to an 11 only if the semantic-layer architecture required customer warehouse co-location, on-prem GPU inference for regulated finance, or a hardware appliance that sat between the BI tool and Snowflake -- any of those would put Datost into the Vigil Labs / Mindbloom / Anduril lane where the moat compounds because other investors won't touch the operational ugliness. Slack-native pure software with no governance, RBAC, or audit-trail story disclosed against autonomous SQL execution on production warehouses is the opposite trajectory: it's a feature surface that any incumbent ships in a quarter.

Flock Safety is the single-anchor parallel for physical verifiability, and Datost almost gets there. I sourced Flock from a Wi-Fi name at a Four Seasons because I could see the camera artifact and touch the deployment; Datost I can @mention in my own Slack and watch a 42-second answer post in-thread, which is more tactile than a developer-tools API but less than a camera on a pole. 18,402 queries answered in a measured week is the kind of usage signal HQ Trivia had when I overrode my co-founder-governance heuristic on tactile virality alone. Datost's number is real, but B2B Slack queries do not virally pull the way a trivia show at 9pm did, so the HQ Trivia exception does not transfer here.

Datost in 36 months is either shut down or acqui-hired by Hex, Snowflake, or Databricks for under $40M, with the most likely path being a quiet sub-$30M tuck-in into whichever BI platform needs a Slack-front-end checkbox -- and somewhere between now and then they will hit $1-3M ARR before incumbent native features compress the wedge. The mechanism producing that forecast is the high/low signature: clean CEO authority means Maceo will execute crisply and not implode the company, but a consensus lane with no frontier-hardware or regulated-category moat means the executed product gets commoditized by the people who own the warehouse layer underneath.

The bull case that stings: DeepMind was the exception where polymath cross-domain compounding overrode my outsider-trajectory bias, and Maceo's resume -- Summa Cum Laude CS, Top 0.1% IMC Prosperity Trading Challenge, Lighthouse Startups non-profit, ex-Traba engineer -- is a polymath-in-formation profile, and the BIRD-Interact 75.2%-vs-33% gap is a real technical artifact that suggests his semantic-layer architecture is non-obvious. If Maceo can convert per-customer semantic graphs into genuine switching cost before Snowflake ships Cortex's Slack integration, Datost compounds. I dismantle that with one fact from the dossier: there is no disclosed customer, no signed LOI, no named design partner -- 18,402 queries could be internal testing, friends-of-YC, or one enthusiastic pilot, and the dossier explicitly notes "no revenue, customer count, paid users." The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a named enterprise design partner -- a Fortune 500 logo or a regulated-industry customer running Datost against production data with an audit trail -- because that converts the BIRD-Interact benchmark into the kind of production trust I cannot dismiss as a model-layer artifact.
