# Rindler -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Rindler is picks-and-shovels middleware for the agent economy built by two MIT CSAIL grads from the class of 2025 -- Michael Serrano and Arthur De Los Santos -- and the analytical signature is a company whose highest dimension (consequence) is only borrowed from the agents it serves, while its lowest dimension (capital profile) tells me I am the wrong investor for a two-person MCP wrapper competing with Browserbase's $40M Series B at $300M and Composio's $79.6M raised. That pairing -- consequence-by-proxy plus over-resourced check size -- is what Rindler is. Not a bet on a world-state; a bet on a plumbing standard.

**OpenAI** (2019) is my consequence-first anchor -- the success-state was civilizational and I wrote $50M with no revenue and no clean corporate structure. Rindler shares the vector because agents operating the web without APIs is genuinely part of the "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" thesis I laid out in Fortune (2026-03-06). But Rindler is not the agent -- it is the adapter cable. The consequence of Rindler winning is that Composio's 1,000+ toolkits get extended to sites-without-APIs; the incumbent regime that collapses is "developers hand-writing Playwright scripts," which is not a civilizational variable. My Pass Example -- the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer -- fails on the same axis, and Rindler is a rung above it only because MCP is a genuine standardization moment, not because Rindler itself displaces a named labor market.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) is the low rung on capital-hostile technical risk for a reason -- the miracle stack was real, plant construction was $200M+, and no Tier-1 generalist would touch the science risk. Rindler's stack is a headless browser plus an LLM planner plus an MCP wrapper plus OAuth 2.0 PKCE session handling. The dossier itself concedes that "the core capability is being built by multiple funded competitors" -- Browserbase ships it, Anon ships the auth slice for $13M raised, Stagehand is MIT-licensed on GitHub. There is no falsifiable miracle here. When I asked "what economic mechanism breaks if this works," the honest answer is "developers save a week of scripting per site" -- that is a Tier-1 generalist deal, not a KV deal.

On founder-defies-expert-consensus, mechanism analysis: Serrano's LinkedIn phrase "infrastructure of the future web" and De Los Santos's Telexistence robotics internship are competent MIT CSAIL credentials -- they are the profile McKinsey's 1986 <1M cellphone forecast punished, not the profile that beat it. Neither founder has publicly staked a controversial claim about how agents will operate the web, no manifesto, no adversarial position against Browserbase's SDK-first approach. Over 18 months this compounds badly: the defensible middleware players will be the ones whose founders publicly committed to a wrong-seeming schema-synthesis architecture in 2025 and got vindicated in 2027. Rindler's dossier shows no such commitment.

Learning-rate counterfactual: for Rindler to reach the **Slash** (2024) rung where I overrode consequence-magnitude for a documented pivot, Serrano and De Los Santos would need a public "we were wrong about X" moment -- an abandoned first thesis, a shipped-then-killed product, a Kanye-shaped story. What the dossier shows instead is a linear post-graduation launch: MIT 2025 → YC S26 → rindler.ai. That is not rigidity; it is short company history. I apply my absent-evidence default rather than penalize aggressively, but the counterfactual required to flip this tier is a founder story that does not yet exist.

**Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) is my single-anchor parallel for the capital dimension -- I led $115M Series A because the roadmap required plant-scale deployment no consensus VC would fund. Rindler's roadmap requires a two-person team, a Vercel account, and API credits. YC pre-seed norms mean ~$500K in, and the natural next round is a $3-5M seed led by any generalist. I am over-resourced by an order of magnitude and my check would distort the cap table without buying me any information asymmetry. This is the mechanical reason Rindler does not fit, independent of consequence.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Rindler is acqui-hired by Browserbase or Composio for under $20M by mid-2029, or dead. The mechanism is the dossier's own concession -- MintMCP, TrueFoundry, Obot, and Bifrost are already building MCP gateway infrastructure, and the site-maintenance treadmill (auth flows, 2FA, bot detection, DOM drift) is a scaling constraint that eats a two-person team before it eats a Browserbase engineering org with 20K developers already integrated. The site-specific schema library Rindler accumulates is worth more inside Browserbase's distribution than as a standalone company.

The bull case that stings: MCP standardization is a genuine 2024-2025 distribution surface, YC S26's "Software for Agents" thesis is the real "why now," and the **DoorDash** (2013) Known Exception proves I will violate my science-risk rule when a founder's ambition frames a network-effect land grab. If Serrano is the Tony Xu of agent-web plumbing and MCP becomes the HTTP of agents, Rindler could be the CDN of that stack. My rebuttal is that DoorDash owned the two-sided network -- dashers and merchants -- while Rindler owns neither the agents nor the websites; the value accrues to Anthropic, OpenAI, and the site operators, and Rindler is the toll booth that gets bypassed the moment agents ship native browsers or sites ship native MCP endpoints. The one verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed enterprise contract at >$500K ACV with a named Fortune-500 operator whose internal portal workflow depends on Rindler within 12 months -- that would prove distribution moat rather than protocol tourism.
