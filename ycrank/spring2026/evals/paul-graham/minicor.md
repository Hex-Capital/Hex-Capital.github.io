# Minicor -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Minicor is the rare pre-seed where the schlep dimension scores highest and the organic-pain dimension scores lowest, and that pairing tells you almost everything: Faizaan Chishtie and Saheed Akinbile are running toward Windows desktop apps inside HIPAA-bound EHRs and Citrix-only health systems — territory no software-only YC applicant in P26 wanted — but they are building it as a vendor-to-vendors tool for "AI vendors selling into legacy industries," one layer removed from anyone who actually feels the pain. The "25,000 patients/day in production" line is the dossier specific that pulls Minicor up; the absence of any founder anecdote that begins "we kept hitting this problem ourselves" is what pulls it down.

On the schlep axis, the natural comparison is **Coinbase** (2012) — Brian Armstrong won that batch on his appetite for bitcoin compliance grind, and what made the compliance a moat was that nobody else in YC wanted to touch it. Minicor's equivalent is the Windows-VM, on-prem, Citrix, SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA stack the dossier lists as the deployment surface. Anthropic's computer-use API and Skyvern's open-source browser agent (YC, $2.7M raised) do not solve a Cerner instance running inside a Citrix session at a regional hospital; they solve Chrome. Where Minicor diverges from the **Coinbase** (2012) analogy is that Armstrong personally wanted to use bitcoin and built the wallet for himself, whereas Minicor's founders are selling pickaxes to other AI vendors rather than being the AI vendor themselves — the schlep is real, but the founders are positioned as a middleware company, and middleware companies historically get squeezed when the layer above them realizes the schlep is the product.

On the organic-pain axis, the pass case I keep in my head is the polished AI-wrapper pitched by founders who "spotted a market opportunity" — clever framing, no biographical contact with the problem. Minicor isn't that, but it isn't **Dropbox** (2007) either; Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and built the thing for himself. Faizaan and Saheed built Laminar Run, a low-code integration platform for legacy systems, and pivoted that into Minicor in 2023-2024 — same problem class, adjacent solution. So the dossier shows pattern-fit rather than personal-fit. They have integration scar tissue, which is more than the MBA pass case has, but neither founder has worked inside a hospital, an auto OEM, or a logistics dispatcher who was screaming at a Windows desktop. That gap is why "25,000 patients/day" reads to me as a customer's number, not the founders' number.

The animal-resourcefulness dimension compounds or fails on whether the Laminar-to-Minicor pivot was a real second-act hustle or a graceful rebrand — the **Kiko** (2005) → **Twitch** (2007) pattern says prior YC failure is a re-fund signal, but Laminar wasn't a YC failure, it was a side-door restructure, and the dossier notes laminar.run now redirects to Minicor branding. That makes the "25,000 patients/day" attribution genuinely ambiguous: carried-over Laminar deployment or net-new Minicor production? Over 18 months this resolves cleanly — either Minicor publishes named hospital logos and a third-party benchmark against the claimed 93-96% click accuracy, or the number stays a homepage line and degrades into a tell.

For hacker genealogy, the counterfactual question is what would have to be true to push Minicor up a tier: Saheed's GitHub @sakin070 shows 43 repos but no headline OSS project, and Faizaan's @FaizChishtie has no recognizable hacker-tribal marker the way Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize did before **Stripe** (2010). To flip this dimension I would need to see one of them shipping a well-starred open-source self-healing-agent SDK that the rest of YC P26 was forking — that would convert "two software engineers from Ottawa with a Morgan Stanley resume line" into "the people who wrote the canonical desktop-agent recovery library." Absent that, the technical credentials read as competent-by-credential.

Co-founder configuration is the cleanest comparison in the dossier: **Airbnb** (2009)'s three founders had years of shared building before the cereal boxes. Faizaan and Saheed met in a UX class at the University of Ottawa in 2019, built Software For Love during the pandemic, co-founded Laminar, and pivoted into Minicor — that is roughly seven years of paired work across two ventures. This is the rung where Minicor most cleanly resembles a portfolio anchor on a single axis.

My 36-month forecast: Minicor reaches $4-9M ARR concentrated in mid-market healthcare RPA, gets tucked into UiPath or Automation Anywhere for $40-90M as an agentic-RPA acquihire by 2029. The mechanism producing that forecast is the schlep-high / organic-low signature — real revenue in an unloved corner, but no founder-conviction flywheel of the kind that pushed Brian Armstrong through five years of compliance grind at **Coinbase** (2012). Middleware to AI vendors, sold into Citrix, by competent non-domain founders, lands at acquihire altitude.

The strongest case against that forecast is the **Coinbase** (2012) precedent itself: schlep blindness *is* the moat, and incumbents like UiPath built brittle script architectures they cannot cannibalize without destroying their existing contracts — exactly the conditions under which Minicor's self-healing wedge in healthcare could compound into a standalone outcome. "25,000 patients/day" plus SOC 2 Type II plus HIPAA at pre-seed is not nothing; it is more deployed surface than most P26 companies will have at Demo Day. What dismantles the bull case for me is that Minicor's accuracy edge — 93-96% vs. 80-85% — is self-reported and benchmarked against a baseline that Anthropic and OpenAI are improving monthly, so the moat compounds only if Faizaan and Saheed turn out to be animals on a dimension the dossier hasn't shown yet. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named Top-20 US health system signing a $1M+ ACV contract with Minicor directly (not through an AI-vendor reseller) within twelve months. That would tell me the founders are selling the schlep themselves, not waiting for AI vendors to drag them in.
