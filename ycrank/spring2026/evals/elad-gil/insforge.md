# InsForge -- Elad Gil Evaluation

InsForge is the inverse of the **Harvey** (2023) profile: clean single-miracle architecture on a recently-catalyzed category, with the canonical AI-cycle red flag that no MRR or curve is disclosed anywhere in the dossier. Hang Huang and Tony Chang have built an Apache-2.0 backend with 10.7k GitHub stars and 4,147 commits riding the MCP/Claude-Code interface gap, but the only growth disclosure is "2000+ databases hosted" and "5 enterprise teams" -- launch-partner language, not revenue customers. That high/low pairing -- crisp miracle isolation, invisible revenue curve -- is what makes InsForge legible to me.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on the catalyst dimension, and not for the obvious reason: both companies sit downstream of a specific 24-month interface re-opening (Ukraine for defense software; MCP protocol release plus Claude Code/Cursor for backend tooling), and in both cases the incumbent stack (Lockheed; Supabase) was structurally configured for the prior interface paradigm. Where InsForge diverges from the Anduril template is that defense procurement had a Pentagon buyer with budget urgency, whereas InsForge's catalyst -- agents provisioning backends autonomously -- still requires the agent-tooling ecosystem itself to mature past its <18-month-old state, per the dossier's own [Inferred] note. The catalyst is real but softer; I'd put InsForge between Anduril and **Decagon** (2023), closer to Decagon.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure mechanism I keep replaying on InsForge's index-position dimension: a #2 in a market where the #1 has already added the differentiating feature captures a fraction of the economics, not a contrarian premium. Supabase at $70M ARR August 2025, $5B valuation October 2025, and *already shipping an MCP server* per the sealos.io comparison is the exact "Harvey is the current leader" pattern from my *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) piece -- except InsForge is the new entrant claiming a 1.6x speed / 30% fewer tokens / 1.7x accuracy edge on self-published benchmarks against the incumbent that just added the same interface layer. That's not a distribution wedge I haven't seen; that's the Lyft setup with a benchmark deck.

On revenue velocity, the mechanism over 18 months is straightforward and unkind: open-source star velocity is uncorrelated with paid conversion in BaaS (Appwrite and Supabase both demonstrated this on the way up), and the dossier's silence on ARR, paying customers, or month-over-month anything is itself the signal -- if InsForge had a Harvey-style $0?$2M?$5M curve, founders@insforge.dev would be circulating it. The 198 Product Hunt upvotes and 1,036 Discord members are tourism metrics, not buying behavior. This is what "false signal vs. just working" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) looks like at pre-seed.

Counterfactual for InsForge's founder-ships dimension: to flip from MID to HIGH, the dossier would need to name a prior shipped product Tony Chang built independently before Databricks (the "5x Hackathon Winner" line is suggestive but unnamed), or evidence Hang Huang shipped a developer-facing artifact pre-InsForge beyond his Amazon PM tenure. What's actually there -- 44 releases, v2.1.9, four Show HN posts (IDs 44772898, 45291644, 45449787, 48181342) in roughly one cycle -- is the InsForge product *itself* compounding as the shipping artifact, which is closer to the **Perplexity** (2022) cadence than the typical Yale-MBA-plus-ex-Amazon-PM dossier suggests. I credit the cadence; I don't credit the prior-shipping line.

The single-miracle test is the cleanest part of InsForge: the company assumes MCP-style agent runtimes keep adopting structured backend context, and the rest is GTM and product execution -- one validated capability, no chemistry risk, no regulatory dependency, no two-sided marketplace. That's the same architecture as **Decagon** (2023) and structurally why I funded that one; InsForge passes the test the same way. The risk isn't multi-miracle, it's that the single miracle is shared with every other BaaS that ships an MCP server in the next two quarters.

**36-month forecast**: InsForge is acquired by Vercel, Cloudflare, or Render for $30-80M as a developer-tools tuck-in by Q2 2029, OR stalls below $2M ARR after Supabase's MCP layer reaches parity, with GitHub stars plateauing in the low 20k range. The mechanism is that interface-layer differentiation on top of commodity Postgres compresses fast when the $5B incumbent ships the same interface; open-source velocity buys an acquisition floor but not an independent path.

**The bull case that stings**: MCP is genuinely the API-first moment for the agent era, and InsForge's Apache-2.0 + Hugging Face/Replicate self-hostable posture is the **Stripe** (2010) developer-channel pattern -- Tony Chang's Databricks networking-infra background is exactly the systems chops that made early Supabase work, and 10.7k GitHub stars at pre-seed with 919 forks is not nothing. If agent-generated apps become the majority of new developer work by 2027, InsForge sits in the workflow path by default and the "index on a compounding category" math flips. I dismantle this with one fact: Supabase added an MCP server before InsForge had a pricing page, and a 1.6x self-published benchmark advantage against a $544M-raised incumbent with $70M ARR distribution is the *exact* margin-compression bet I called out in my pass example. **The verifiable condition that flips my verdict**: InsForge discloses $1M+ ARR with >20% MoM growth and at least three named enterprise logos signed in the prior 90 days -- absent that, this is consensus open-source-BaaS at consensus pre-seed prices, and the math doesn't pencil.
