## The Idea

**Problem:** Enterprise knowledge is fragmented across meetings, messages, emails, and documents. Decisions, commitments, and context disappear or remain siloed, forcing employees to manually reconstruct institutional knowledge (Sentra.app website; Yahoo Finance press release, Jan 2026). CEO Jae Gwan Park: "Every conversation, decision, and document adds to a growing ocean of context that disappears moments later or stays siloed" (Yahoo Finance, Jan 2026). Current alternatives include manual note-taking, standalone meeting transcription tools (Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai), and enterprise search platforms (Glean).

**Approach:** Sentra deploys a voice-enabled AI agent that joins meetings, Slack channels, Jira, calendars, and HR systems to create a persistent knowledge graph of organizational conversations (SiliconANGLE, Jan 2026). Features include: searchable meeting memory, automatic decision and action-item capture, risk detection (conflicting priorities, missed commitments), automated weekly status reports, pre-meeting briefs, and autonomous agent workflows (sentra.app website). The platform provides a "living company memory that learns, reasons, and reflects" rather than a static archive (Pulse2, Jan 2026).

**Differentiation:** Unlike point solutions (Otter.ai, Granola) focused on meeting transcription, Sentra positions as a cross-organizational memory layer connecting conversations, decisions, and context across teams and timelines (Yahoo Finance, Jan 2026). Unlike Glean's search-first approach across documents, Sentra captures real-time conversational context and builds a temporal decision graph linking "who, what, when, and why" (Yahoo Finance, Jan 2026). CTO Starenky: "Sentra acts as the brain of your business, allowing real-time, true understanding of what your teams do" (SiliconANGLE, Jan 2026).

**Business Model:** Team Plan: Free base + $16/user/month for 2–20 users, with 2,000 action credits per user/month; integrations include Slack, Gmail, Calendar, Outlook, GitHub, Linear. Enterprise Plan: Custom pricing for 20+ users with SSO/SAML, SOC 2, audit logs, API access, and custom data retention (sentra.app/pricing). [Inferred]: Per-seat SaaS with usage-based credits suggests hybrid consumption model targeting mid-market and enterprise.

## Defensibility

- **Data moat (potential):** As Sentra accumulates organizational memory — meetings, decisions, commitments over time — the knowledge graph becomes increasingly valuable and difficult to replicate (sentra.app website). [Inferred]: Switching costs increase with tenure as historical context accrues; however, this moat is nascent at the POC stage.
- **Security certifications:** SOC 2 and ISO 27001 compliance, plus on-premise/isolated VPC deployment options (sentra.app website) — these are table stakes for enterprise sales but represent an early investment for a seed-stage company.
- **Research foundation:** Co-founder Ashwin Gopinath co-authored Reflexion (NeurIPS 2023), a foundational paper on agent self-improvement with 3.1k GitHub stars (GitHub, noahshinn/reflexion).

**Market structure:** [Inferred]: Incumbents like Microsoft (Copilot) and Google (Gemini for Workspace) could build similar organizational memory features. However, their horizontal platform strategy may limit depth in cross-tool memory synthesis. Meeting-focused competitors (Otter.ai, Granola) would need to expand significantly beyond transcription into cross-platform decision tracking. No structural barrier identified at this stage beyond execution speed and data accumulation.

**Commoditization risk:** Meeting transcription and note-taking is commoditizing rapidly, with Otter.ai ($100M ARR, BusinessWire Dec 2025) and Granola ($1.5B valuation, TechCrunch Mar 2026) scaling aggressively. [Inferred]: The organizational memory and cross-tool knowledge-graph layer is less crowded than transcription, but well-funded competitors (Glean, Microsoft Copilot) could enter this space.

## Key Risks

**Name confusion with Sentra.io:** A separate, well-funded cloud data security company called Sentra (sentra.io) has raised $100M+ including a $50M Series B (BusinessWire, Apr 2025). This creates brand confusion in search results, investor conversations, and enterprise procurement processes.

**Heavily funded competitive convergence:** Glean ($610M raised), Granola ($192M raised), and Otter.ai ($100M ARR) are all expanding toward organizational knowledge and context layers (TechCrunch, Mar 2026; BusinessWire, Dec 2025). Microsoft Copilot and Google Gemini for Workspace also operate in adjacent territory. Sentra's $5M seed creates significant resource asymmetry.

**Platform dependency:** Core integrations (Slack, Google Calendar, Jira, GitHub) are controlled by large incumbents who could restrict API access or build competing features. Slack's acquisition by Salesforce and Microsoft Teams' dominance create concentration risk in the communication-layer dependency.

**Enterprise sales cycle vs. burn rate:** Paid POC with SoftBank is the only disclosed customer signal (SiliconANGLE, Jan 2026). Enterprise sales cycles for security-sensitive organizational data tools are typically 3–6+ months. [Inferred]: With a $5M seed and 12-person team, runway may be limited if enterprise conversion is slow.
