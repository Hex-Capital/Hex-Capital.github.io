# Standout -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Standout is the inverse signature of the bet I usually win on: the highest-scoring axis is Aftalion's animal resourcefulness from Zealy (1.5M MAU, $3M ARR in twelve months), and the lowest is whether the hiring-is-broken thesis is organic to the founders' own pain -- which the Standout dossier never claims it is. That pairing -- proven operator, borrowed problem -- is the shape of a company that will probably make some money and probably not be the No. 1 portfolio company that beats the next 199 combined.

The Airbnb rung on the animal-resourcefulness ladder is cereal boxes at $40 each, 1,000+ sold; the Dropbox rung is the Hacker News demo video before the seed check. Standout sits between those two: Aftalion already built Zealy to 1.5M MAU and $3M ARR before Standout, and the Standout v0 WhatsApp launch on Product Hunt in February 2026 with a reported 1M+ X views is a real pre-funding stunt, not a TAM slide. Where Standout diverges from the Airbnb rung is that the resourcefulness was spent on a *prior* company, not on Standout itself -- Aftalion is bringing in a track record, not a stunt I can point to from inside the current dossier. That is still meaningfully above the AI-wrapper-MBA bottom rung. Aftalion is an animal; the question is whether he is animal-on-this-problem.

The pass case I keep writing against is the two-MBA AI-wrapper team that opens with "hiring is broken on both sides" -- which is, almost word for word, how the Standout YC page describes itself. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and built Dropbox for himself; the Collisons had been hacking commerce since Auctomatic. Standout's dossier gives me de La Chapelle's Zarobky (a Polish job-intelligence product, 100K MAU) as the closest analog, which is genuine adjacency but not personal pain -- neither founder is on record saying they hated their last job search, or that they tried to hire a founding engineer at Zealy and the process broke them. The framing is market-shaped, not life-shaped, and that is the exact filter from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012) where Standout reads thin.

On schlep, Standout is software-on-software. Coinbase took the bitcoin compliance grind; DoorDash took last-mile delivery operations no other Spring batch applicant wanted; Standout takes two-sided agentic matching, which is the most consensus YC thesis of the last eighteen months. The compounding question over 18 months is whether agentic matching turns into a moat or commoditizes -- and with Mercor at $492M raised, $10B valuation, and Paraform at $65M raised through Series B in March 2026, the answer the market is already giving is that this is a capital-arms-race category, not a schlep category. There is no compliance clause, no manual operation, no recruiting overhead in the Standout dossier that other founders would route around.

Hacker genealogy in Standout is split. Counterfactual: if Aftalion himself had a GitHub trail or a Lisp-equivalent prize, Standout would clear into Invest tier on this axis -- but the dossier shows ESSEC, Centralesupélec, Alven (VC), Rocket Internet, vOx. That is a business-operator pedigree, not a hacker pedigree. De La Chapelle carries the technical lineage alone: CMU MS CS, Dropbox/Samsara/Chime, GitHub @wit221. One real hacker plus one strong commercial cofounder is the Stripe shape only if the commercial one is also technical, which Patrick Collison was; Aftalion is not Patrick.

Standout's cofounder configuration parallels the Stripe pair structurally -- two founders, complementary -- but without the brothers-since-childhood relational depth that made the Stripe bet trivial. The Standout dossier reports no public information on how Aftalion and de La Chapelle met, no shared prior employer, no described pre-startup working relationship; Côme is a first hire, not a third cofounder. Standout passes the no-solo-founder bar I set in "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010), and I default to neutral on team chemistry because the YC interview surfaces it -- but neutral is the ceiling without evidence.

My 36-month forecast for Standout: alive but squeezed -- $2-5M ARR by mid-2029, positioned as the founding-engineer-and-early-GTM niche between Mercor's contractor focus and Paraform's recruiter-in-the-loop, and most likely acquired by a mid-tier ATS or sourcing incumbent (Ashby, Gem, Greenhouse range) rather than going independent. The mechanism is straightforward: Aftalion's Zealy chops will get Standout to liquidity in one vertical, and the capital gap to Mercor and Paraform will close the rest.

The strongest objection is that I am underweighting Zealy. A founder who scaled a community marketplace to $3M ARR in twelve months is exactly the kind of repeat operator who, in the Airbnb pattern, makes the borrowed-idea problem irrelevant -- because animals find their pain on the way. Loopt is the Known Exception that comes closest: I funded Altman on force-of-personality before mobile location was usable infrastructure, overriding the weak product-market timing. Standout could be that. What dismantles the objection for me is that Loopt was batch one and I had no Mercor-shaped $10B competitor sitting on Altman's path; Aftalion does. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Standout reports a closed-hire conversion rate above 5% on the 100 intros from month one, with named full-time placements at YC-batch or Series A companies inside six months. Absent that, Standout is a competent pass.
