# Foaster -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Foaster is two unusually-credentialed outsiders -- Raphaël Dabadie, who joined Rafael Nadal's team as an agent at 18, and Alexandre Combes, who shipped 60,000+ AI customer calls at Répondia -- running a consulting playbook on top of commodity LLMs against McKinsey and BCG. The pairing that defines Foaster in my framework is high founder learning rate against near-zero technical or capital risk. That is the exact dimensional signature my Pass Example was written about, and the "3-5x cheaper than traditional consulting" pricing line on foaster.ai is precisely the optimization-play language I told Outlook Business I do not fund.

**Cognition** (Series B April 2024, $175M) sits at the high rung for stack-deep AI labor displacement; Foaster sits two rungs below it. Cognition justified my AI thesis because the underlying engineering of an autonomous software engineer touches the $15T labor displacement figure I gave Fortune (2026-03-06) directly -- the agent is the labor. Foaster does not displace the labor; Foaster displaces a slide deck about the labor. The consequence-at-maximum-success for Foaster is that mid-market firms with 50+ employees get a faster, cheaper transformation roadmap. No incumbent regime collapses -- McKinsey QuantumBlack and BCG X (parent BCG, ~$3.6B of $14.4B 2025 revenue already AI-related per Metaintro 2026) absorb the methodology and keep the C-suite relationships. That is the **Impossible Foods** (2011) inversion: alt-meat displaced animal protein production; "AI-native consulting" does not displace consulting, it competes inside consulting.

**KiOR** (~2008, bankrupt 2014) lost roughly $160M because the catalytic biomass-to-crude science required three falsifiable miracles -- catalyst yield, feedstock economics, refinery integration -- and consensus VCs would not touch the deployment scale. That failure mechanism is the inverse of what shows up in Foaster's dossier: no miracles, no capital depth, off-the-shelf LLMs running 30-45 minute interview scripts. The foaster.ai about page names "proprietary AI interview agents" but the dossier identifies zero proprietary model, zero data network effect, zero IP, and no SOC 2 or ISO certifications despite ingesting sensitive HR data on every employee. Any LLM-fluent services firm -- Accenture's $3B FY24 generative AI bookings makes them the obvious one -- replicates the workflow in a quarter. Foaster is fundable by any Tier-1 generalist; that means I am the wrong capital.

On founder defiance, Dabadie's career path -- 400,000-person tennis community at age 11, Nadal team at 18, 12M-view LLM benchmarks with Combes -- is unconventional enough that the dossier reads outsider, not the McKinsey-to-AI-consultant pattern my TED Talk warned about. Combes shipping Répondia into French restaurant groups is real product-shipping evidence. But neither founder has publicly contradicted a field -- there is no Levine/Finn-style published thesis breaking with robotics orthodoxy, no "instigator" position from my "12 Good Instigators" framing. Unconventional biography is not the same as defied expert consensus.

The learning-rate dimension is where Foaster genuinely earns points in my framework. Counterfactual: for this to flip a tier upward, the dossier would need a documented prior pivot inside Foaster itself -- a "we built X, customers told us Y, we rebuilt as Z" story like the **Slash** (2024) Kanye-pivot that justified my $100M Series C. What the dossier does show is Combes pivoting from Répondia (vertical AI calls) into Foaster (horizontal AI diagnostics) and Dabadie's serial reinvention from sports to LLM benchmarks to enterprise advisory. That is a real arc -- not a single linear plan -- and the absent-evidence default underweights it. The capital-profile dimension is the cleanest read of all: services unit economics, 2-person team, FDE delivery -- Foaster needs $1-3M to find product-market fit, which means a $50M Khosla check has nowhere to deploy.

My 36-month forecast on Foaster: $3-8M ARR as a European mid-market AI consulting boutique, acqui-hired by Capgemini, Accenture, or a Tier-2 SI for $15-40M, or pivoted into a vertical operations-diagnostics SaaS product spun out of the cross-company workflow dataset. The mechanism is straightforward -- the CTO is in Paris, Répondia's distribution roots are French restaurant chains, and the most efficient buyer for an AI-native services firm with that geography is a European incumbent rebuilding its AI practice. None of those outcomes return a $3.5B fund.

The bull case that stings: **Distyl AI** (Khosla Ventures led ~$32M Series A, 2024) is named directly in Foaster's dossier as an overlapping FDE-model competitor, and I wrote that check on essentially the services-to-product thesis -- enterprise AI deployment work that compounds into a proprietary playbook and dataset. Foaster could be the European Distyl. The reason the analogy fails is the founder shape: Distyl's team carried Palantir FDE pedigree and pre-existing Fortune 500 distribution, and Foaster's dossier discloses zero Fortune 500 logos, zero advisors, and a 6,315-follower CEO X account. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: three signed Fortune 500 or CAC 40 enterprise contracts at $500K+ ACV within 12 months of YC Demo Day, with named counterparties. Until then, Foaster scores roughly where my Pass Example scores -- a competent services firm in a real market that any generalist can fund.
