# Care GP -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Care GP is the inverse profile of what my framework usually rewards: the revenue velocity dimension lights up — 150+ clinics, zero churn, 140% average MoM usage growth, Samantha at 175% MoM for five months (Globe and Mail, Oct 2025; Startmate) — while the distribution and founder-shipping dimensions are weak in exactly the ways my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy flags. Melvin Chen has built something that is "just working" by the TechCrunch (Nov 2025) standard, inside a channel that is already turning against him.

**Harvey** (2023) is the load-bearing analogy on velocity. The shared mechanism is real buying behavior at a clip that resolves the "false signal vs. just working" question on its own — Harvey ran $3B → $5B → $8B in months because enterprise legal was paying, not piloting (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). Care GP has the curve shape: 150+ clinics, profitable, zero churn, and Sonya at Yarrawonga citing six hours of reception work done in thirty (caregp.com.au). But Harvey's velocity was denominated in *dollars* disclosed by later-stage marks; Care GP's is denominated in *clinics and usage* with no ARR figure anywhere in the dossier and a self-described "profitable" floor that in Australian primary care could mean $2M AUD ARR or $15M AUD ARR — that gap is the difference between a Harvey-template and a well-marketed Startmate company.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure mechanism on the distribution dimension, and it shows up in Care GP's dossier almost verbatim. The Lyft pass was about being structurally #2 in a market whose incumbent owned the rails (20VC interview). Care GP is a *listed partner* of Best Practice Software (bestpracticesoftware.com), and Best Practice has launched its own AI admin agent and integrates Lyrebird natively (Medical Republic, 2026). That is not a distribution moat — that is a tenant waiting for the landlord to evict. Compound this with Heidi Health at ~$138M raised, ~$700M valuation, and a claimed 50% GP share extending from scribe into admin (Medical Republic, 2026), and Care GP's "ISO27001 + onshore encryption" moat (caregp.com.au) becomes table stakes rather than defensibility. This is the *Lyft-shaped* hole in the deal.

Melvin Chen's founder-shipping signal — mechanism analysis over 18 months: the dossier names Oxford Engineering Valedictorian, Terra Firma, Samena Capital, Goldman Sachs, Forbes 30 Under 30 Finance, and co-founding Avigo and StarLake Group (LinkedIn; Startmate). What it does *not* name is a single shipped software artifact, no GitHub, no Twitter. My **Perplexity** (2022) filter is whether the founder shows up next week with the thing we discussed built (Tim Ferriss Show #863) — Chen's profile is the *operator-allocator* archetype, not the ships-between-meetings archetype. Over 18 months that compounds as slower product iteration against Heidi's 383-person engineering org and Best Practice's native development — pedigree without shipping rhythm doesn't out-execute capitalized competitors.

Care GP's catalyst — counterfactual frame: the dossier frames "why now" as agentic LLMs reaching reliability for OCR-plus-routing combined with the Australian GP admin shortage (Globe and Mail, Oct 2025). For this to score where **Anduril** (2022) scored on catalyst, there would need to be a specific 24-month regulatory or reimbursement shift in Australian primary care admin — an MBS code change, a RACGP mandate, something that *forced* clinic adoption rather than allowed it. The dossier names none. The catalyst as written is "GPT-4 got good enough" which is a 2023 generic catalyst, not a 2026 specific one, and it equally enables Heidi, Lyrebird, and Best Practice's native agent.

Single-miracle architecture — single-anchor parallel: **Decagon** (2023) isolated the miracle to "LLM accuracy at customer service tasks" and made everything else GTM. Care GP isolates similarly — the miracle is "LLM accuracy at clinical document routing into PMS taxonomies." That's clean. The complication is that the routing miracle has a *patient-safety* tail (pathology misallocation, referral misfiling) the dossier acknowledges with "human oversight for exceptions" but no third-party accuracy audit. That doesn't add miracles, but it adds a regulatory failure mode that doesn't exist for Decagon — one bad pathology misroute and the Australian narrative changes.

**Forecast (36 months):** Care GP gets acquired by Best Practice Software or MedicalDirector as a feature tuck-in at $30–80M AUD, or plateaus at $3–8M AUD ARR as Best Practice's native admin agent and Lyrebird's admin expansion compress the partner channel that produced the original curve. The mechanism is straightforward: velocity is real, but it is being borrowed from a distribution layer that is now competing with the borrower, and the founder profile doesn't predict the product-velocity needed to out-ship a capitalized incumbent inside its own rails.

The bull case that stings: Heidi did exactly this from Australia and reached ~$700M while everyone underestimated antipodean healthcare AI — Care GP's 140% MoM and zero churn are the leading indicators that look identical to Heidi's pre-breakout period, and the Australian GP admin labor crisis is a more acute pain than scribing. If Care GP's "profitable" floor is actually $8M+ AUD ARR and Chen quietly closes a non-Best-Practice PMS exclusive (MedicalDirector or Genie), the channel-conflict thesis inverts. I dismantle this with one fact: Heidi reached scale *before* Best Practice launched its native scribe and *before* Lyrebird got bundled — Care GP is launching its admin agent into a platform that has already declared its intent to own the layer, which is the opposite market structure. The verdict flips if Care GP discloses $5M+ AUD ARR independently or signs a non-Best-Practice PMS as primary channel within 12 months. Until then, this is a competent operator running a real product in a market with a landlord problem — Neutral, not Invest.
