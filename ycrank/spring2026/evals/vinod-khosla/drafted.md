# Drafted -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Nick Donahue shut down **Atmos** in March 2025 after five years and $20M of capital (some of it from my fund and Sam Altman's) and stood up Drafted within months — that is the only dimension on which Drafted clears my bar, and it is paired with the lowest possible reading on consequence magnitude. Drafted is a documented-pivot founder reentering the same residential-design problem at the wrong altitude: a freemium PDF/CAD exporter at $1.65M on a $35M post (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025), aimed at homeowners rather than at the architectural labor stack itself. That signature — high learning-rate, low consequence — is the **Slash** (2024) shape without the Slash justification.

**Impossible Foods** (2011) is the HIGH rung on consequence: the success-state retired animal-protein production as a category, a named civilizational variable. Drafted's success-state, on its own dossier framing — "Design your home instantly with AI," 325k floor plans generated last month, 85,000+ plans hosted — is that custom residential design gets cheaper and faster for a slice of U.S. homeowners. That is a workflow compression, not a category collapse. The $15T labor frame I cited in Fortune (2026-03-06) would require Drafted to displace the architectural and permitting labor pool itself, end-to-end through structural engineering and code review; the dossier says plans are "ready to be sent to a structural engineer," which is the giveaway — the human stays in the loop, the consequence is bounded, and Autodesk's seat model survives.

**KiOR** (~2008) is the LOW rung as a cautionary inversion on technical-capital risk: three falsifiable miracles, $160M of my capital, a $75M Mississippi state loan — risk other VCs would not touch was the entire moat. Drafted's "miracle" is a specialized multimodal model trained on permitted plans at 0.2¢ per generation vs 13¢ for general-purpose AI (TechCrunch, Dec 23 2025). That is a unit-economics story, not a physics story. Maket.ai has been shipping a comparable AI floor-plan generator since 2019 on $2.47M raised; any frontier lab can fine-tune on a plan corpus in a quarter. Drafted's capital plan — $1.65M at $35M post — confirms the risk profile: a Tier-1 generalist can fund this in their sleep, and that is the exact pattern my TechCrunch Disrupt 2013 critique was about.

Counterfactual on founder-defies-expert-consensus: Donahue would have to score a tier higher only if Drafted's dossier showed him publicly contradicting the residential-architecture orthodoxy — claiming, for instance, that AI-generated plans can clear permitting in a named jurisdiction without an architect of record. The dossier shows the opposite: Forbes 30 Under 30, NC State, "grew up in a homebuilding family," second venture at the same problem with four ex-Atmos employees following him in. That is domain continuity in the **Glean** Arvind-Jain shape — but Glean cleared my override on capital scale and Fortune-500 information asymmetry, neither of which Drafted offers. Donahue is an industry insider applying the playbook with a better model; my TED Talk was explicit that this is the profile experts confuse for signal.

Mechanism analysis on founder learning rate over 18 months: the Atmos post-mortem Donahue gave TechCrunch — "an extremely operational business, kind of like a glamorized architecture firm" that "never quite replaced the humans," collapsed when the Fed raised rates and clients defaulted on dream-home contracts — is the cleanest pivot articulation I have seen this batch. Drafted is the explicit thesis update: strip the services layer, ship pure software, monetize on exports. That is exactly the "learning rate above everything else" signal I told Entrepreneur magazine I weight. It compounds well if Donahue keeps moving — but pivot quality is a multiplier on consequence, and the multiplier is being applied to a small number.

**Cognition** (2024) is the single-anchor parallel on required-capital — a $175M round because foundation-model training for software engineers needs that depth. Drafted raised $1.65M on a $35M post and the YC-page-stated $17.5M cumulative figure is disputed by the TechCrunch reporting; either way, the company's roadmap (consumer self-serve, social and word-of-mouth distribution, freemium PDF/CAD exports) does not require Khosla check sizes. I am over-resourced for this deal. The investor list — Patrick Collison, Ben Silbermann, Josh Buckley, Jack Altman, Samsung, Charlie Songhurst — is a consumer-product angel syndicate, not a science-bet syndicate, and it is correctly calibrated for what Drafted actually is.

My 36-month forecast: Drafted reaches roughly $5-15M ARR on freemium-to-paid plan exports, gets acquired by Autodesk, Zillow, or a homebuilder-tools roll-up for $80-200M, and Donahue takes a third swing at the residential problem with the proceeds. Not dead, not a billion-dollar outcome — a respectable consumer-AI exit that DoorDash-era generalists will be glad they wrote the angel check on. The mechanism: monetization gravity from a 120k-user freemium top of funnel will produce real revenue, but the permitting-grade output bottleneck Donahue already hit at Atmos will cap end-to-end displacement, and Maket.ai plus an Autodesk fast-follow will compress pricing before Drafted can compound a data moat.

The strongest opposing case is the **Slash** (2024) override: I wrote a $100M Series C into a YC-backed consumer-adjacent company that violated my consequence-magnitude prior because the founder's Kanye-pivot demonstrated learning rate strong enough to dominate the criterion stack. Donahue's Atmos-to-Drafted pivot is structurally identical — a publicly told failure with a clearly stated lesson, executed inside a YC batch, with a credible angel roster. Why not the same override? Because Slash cleared my bar at Series C with traction and unit economics already proven; at pre-seed the override has not earned the right to fire, and the Slash exception was about extending an already-working consumer business, not seeding a workflow tool whose ceiling is bounded by structural-engineer review. The condition that flips my verdict: Drafted demonstrates AI-generated plans clearing permitting in a top-10 U.S. metro without an architect of record, at which point the labor-displacement frame from Fortune (2025-07-01) actually applies and the consequence-magnitude score moves a full tier.
