# Trellis -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Trellis is a domain-expert vertical SaaS wrapped around a structurally permissioned distribution architecture -- Lodovico Benvenuti scaled a 150-listing property-management book to ~$2M before building AI agents at HostAI/Conduit (YC W24), and that operator-plus-AI-builder pairing is exactly the specific knowledge I underwrite, but the 200+ integrations with Airbnb, VRBO, and the very PMS incumbents Trellis aims to displace -- Guesty ($409M raised, $900M valuation) and Hostaway ($543M raised, $925M valuation) -- mean Trellis's distribution surface is gated by the permission of its competitors. That high/low pairing is the whole bet.

Stack Overflow is the rung I want Trellis to be on for specific knowledge -- Atwood and Spolsky were practicing developers writing for developers, and the founder's own lived problem was the seed. Trellis clears that bar on this axis better than most YC pre-seeds I see: Benvenuti ran a 150-listing operation through the exact tool-chain hell the YC Launch page describes ("5 to 10 disconnected tools"), and Jan Sahagun built STR Tech Report and attended 20+ industry events before becoming a co-founder. Where Trellis diverges from the Stack Overflow rung is that Benvenuti's specific knowledge is operator-of-the-thing, not builder-of-the-substrate -- he learned the workflow pain, not the underlying AI orchestration substrate -- and his prior employer HostAI/Conduit is the closest AI-native competitor with $3.1M already raised. The specific knowledge is real; it is also reproducible by HostAI in a way Spolsky's was not by random Stanford clones.

Anchorage Digital is the failure mode I keep returning to on leverage architecture -- I made the exception because the OCC charter itself became the moat, and the dossier needs to show me something analogous in Trellis. It does not. Trellis's distribution depends on API access from Guesty, Hostaway, and Airbnb, which the dossier itself flags as a risk -- "API access could be restricted by these incumbents if Trellis becomes a competitive threat." That is the inverse of permissionless leverage; that is the textbook definition of capital-and-labor leverage requiring permission from a gatekeeper, and the gatekeeper in this case is the entity Trellis is trying to disintermediate. Sahagun's STR Tech Report media platform is the one genuine permissionless lever in the stack -- founder-owned, X-style organic-distribution shape -- but the product itself ships through integration-broker plumbing, not through media.

Trellis's network claim -- "every SOP that survives contact with guests feeds back into the network" -- is the kind of pre-revenue compounding story I have to evaluate by mechanism, not by aspiration. The compounding requires each new operator's workflow data to make the next operator's AI agents materially better, and at six people with no disclosed customer count, the feedback loop is asserted rather than instrumented. Over 18 months the dominant force is single-sided SaaS economics: every customer is economically independent of the next, the 84.5% automation rate either holds per-account or it doesn't, and the network claim resolves to a generic "we have data" moat that any AI-native competitor with the same integration list can mirror. This is the dimension Perplexity also failed -- I overrode there because three other gates cleared emphatically; Trellis does not get the same override.

Trellis's TAM would need to support at least two founder pivots to clear my Venture Hacks "first idea never works" bar -- and the adjacent pivot vectors here are thin. The honest pivot adjacencies from STR-ops-AI are: long-term residential property management (different operator psychology, different tool stack), hotel ops (incumbents have ten-figure budgets), and vacation-rental booking direct-to-consumer (different business entirely). None of them are platform-shaped the way developer tooling or consumer media are. Guesty at $900M and Hostaway at $925M tell me the STR PMS category itself can produce billion-dollar outcomes, but they also tell me the ceiling is bounded by what those incumbents already occupy. Pivot room is real but not generous.

Buffett's trio reads clean on Trellis. No litigation, no contested exits, no founder-honesty signaling of the kind I learned to distrust at Epinions. The one tension is that Benvenuti's prior employer HostAI/Conduit (YC W24) is now the closest AI-native competitor, and the dossier does not surface how that separation happened -- in a 10-year-partner test, that thread matters, but absent contrary evidence I score it neutral-positive. Sahagun's multi-year STR Tech Report ownership is the kind of long-term-game signal I want to see.

My 36-month forecast: Trellis reaches $2-6M ARR on per-listing pricing and is acquired by Guesty, Hostaway, or Breezeway in the $30-80M range as a tuck-in AI-agent layer -- a perfectly fine outcome for a YC seed but structurally bounded by the permissioned distribution architecture, which means the upside math caps at roughly 50-100x at YC-seed valuations rather than the 1,000-10,000x I underwrite for. The mechanism producing the cap is that Trellis cannot scale faster than its integration partners allow, and those partners have $952M of combined dry powder to build the same AI layer in-house or to acquire it once it's de-risked.

The strongest bull case is the Perplexity analogy: Benvenuti's "feature parity in two weeks" claim is exactly the pre-consensus AI-cycle signal I caught when Aravind Srinivas was rebuilding search before ChatGPT scale, and an AI-native rebuild of an entire vertical-ops stack at 10x lower cost is the kind of architectural inversion that turns Guesty's $409M raised from a moat into innovator's-dilemma ballast. If Trellis distributes primarily through Sahagun's STR Tech Report media platform with organic property-manager pull -- the Stack Overflow developer-pull pattern -- the leverage architecture reclassifies from permissioned to media-permissionless and the bear case dissolves. The dossier evidence that wins the argument today is the explicit integration-dependency risk flagged in the dossier itself plus the absence of any customer-count or organic-pull metric beyond a single testimonial. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Trellis demonstrating that more than half of new customer acquisition flows through Sahagun's media platform rather than through integration-list breadth, within the next 12 months. Until then, I pass and watch.
