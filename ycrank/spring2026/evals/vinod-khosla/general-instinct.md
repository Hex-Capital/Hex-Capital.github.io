# General Instinct -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

General Instinct is a credential-rich infrastructure plumbing play whose highest dimension is the founder pedigree of Guanming Wang (ex-DeepMind, Cambridge MPhil, Google DeepMind AI Masters Scholarship) and Bill Jiao (Siemens multimodal foundation model) -- and whose lowest dimension is consequence magnitude, because the success-state of "Instinct Edge takes a model + target device + latency budget as inputs and outputs an offline runtime" does not collapse any incumbent regime; it lubricates one. That pairing is the analytical signature: two top-program researchers building a compression toolchain in the gap between Modular ($380M raised, $1.6B valuation) and the first-party SDKs (NVIDIA TensorRT, Qualcomm AI Engine, Apple Core ML). The gap is real. The gap is not civilizational.

On consequence, the **OpenAI** (2019) rung is "what happens to cognitive labor if this works" -- a $15T variable. **DoorDash** (2013) is the mid rung: capital-light consumer logistics that reshaped a flow without collapsing a regime. General Instinct's tagline -- "Deploy AI into any edge device, fully offline, maximum performance" -- is infrastructure tooling for whoever is building the actual physical-AI consequence. The Jetson Orin NX production demo cites "111 ms cold start, 100% of decisions inside a 150 ms budget, zero cloud calls," which is an engineering specification, not a world-state. When I ask my own question -- what economic mechanism breaks if General Instinct works -- the answer is "cloud inference bills for robotics deployments get smaller." That is a feature inside the **Physical Intelligence** (2024) or **Genesis AI** (2025) thesis, not a thesis. General Instinct sits much closer to DoorDash than to OpenAI on this ladder, and that is being generous.

The lowest-dimension ladder is capital profile, and the failure mode of the typical YC pre-seed sales-call summarizer I pass on in 30 seconds is that the dossier is over-served by a $500K check and any Tier-1 generalist can fund it. General Instinct's PitchBook profile lists exactly that: $500K from Y Combinator, 2-person team, no roadmap requiring $115M like **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) or $105M like **Genesis AI** (2025). The StartupHub.ai figure of "$5M Raised" looks like a data error; the standard YC deal aligns. The whole runtime can be built by two researchers with a Jetson Orin NX on a desk, which is the inverse of the deployment-scale gate that made me lead Commonwealth Fusion when no consensus VC would touch fusion timelines. There is no capital chasm here for General Instinct to be the only one to cross.

Technical and capital risk -- the dimension that compounds. Eighteen months out, the General Instinct moat hypothesis depends on accumulated device/model compression profiles becoming a data moat. The compounding fails in two places: llama.cpp, ONNX Runtime, and TensorRT-LLM are improving in the open, and Qualcomm's March 10, 2025 acquisition of Edge Impulse ($52.8M raised) bundles a no-code workflow with Snapdragon NPUs -- one of General Instinct's four named target classes. The compression delta General Instinct produces over a tuned open-source pipeline must widen as frontier models grow, not narrow as silicon catches up. The dossier offers a single Jetson Orin NX reference. One data point is a claim, not a curve.

Counterfactual on founder-defies-expert-consensus: for General Instinct to flip a tier on this axis, Wang or Jiao would need to have published a contrarian position on edge inference that the field rejected -- something like "frontier-model compression is fundamentally a runtime-scheduling problem, not a quantization problem, and here is why TensorRT is architecturally wrong." Nothing in the dossier shows that. The Agno Global Agent Hackathon 2025 win ($5,000) is a competence signal, not a defiance signal. The Siemens multimodal foundation model line on Jiao's background reads as paradigmatic domain-expert continuity, the exact pattern my TED Talk flagged via the UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study. The Glean known exception does not rescue General Instinct here, because Glean's override was capital scale and Fortune-500 consequence magnitude -- General Instinct has neither.

Single-anchor parallel on learning rate: **Slash** (2024) earned its $100M Series C because the founder told a publicly documented Kanye West pivot story that proved the learning rate. General Instinct's dossier shows two researchers who "dreamed of building startups since high school" -- continuity, not pivot. Jiao's high-school tardigrade-protein patents are charming and signal early agency, but they are not a thesis update. I apply my mid default and do not penalize aggressively, because the company is weeks old and pivots have not had time to occur.

Forecast, 36 months: General Instinct is acqui-hired by Modular or a Tier-1 robotics OEM (Skydio, Figure, or Anduril) for under $30M in talent value, or it pivots into a vertical-specific runtime for one of those customers. The mechanism producing this is the signature itself -- credentialed researchers in a commodity-tooling gap that is being closed simultaneously from above by Modular's $380M war chest and from below by Qualcomm-owned Edge Impulse and open-source quantization stacks. There is no civilizational consequence to underwrite the 90% failure tolerance I extended to **KiOR** (~2008).

The strongest bull case is that physical AI deployment is the actual bottleneck for the robotics foundation-model wave I am already funding through **Physical Intelligence** (2024) and **Genesis AI** (2025), and a cross-silicon runtime that genuinely delivers "any frontier model, any edge device, within any latency budget" would be picks-and-shovels for the Khosla physical-AI thesis itself. If General Instinct compounds compression profiles into a real data moat, Modular's datacenter-first focus leaves the edge-only physical-AI niche wide open. The bear answer wins on dossier facts: Modular ($380M, $1.6B valuation) already ships MAX across edge and datacenter, Qualcomm already owns the no-code Snapdragon path via Edge Impulse, and General Instinct's one cited production reference covers one device out of the four target classes. The flip condition is precise: a signed multi-year deployment contract with a named Tier-1 robotics or drone OEM (Skydio, Anduril, Figure, Waymo, or one of my own Physical Intelligence / Genesis AI portfolio companies) showing the Instinct Edge runtime delivers latency or model-size performance the first-party SDK provably cannot. Absent that, General Instinct scores as a Pass on my framework -- not because the founders are weak, but because the consequence is not large enough to underwrite the probability of failure.
