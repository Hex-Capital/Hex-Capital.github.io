# Auxos -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Auxos is a credentialed infra-engineering team chasing a real catalyst in a category where the consensus winners already raised growth rounds — Aaru's $50M+ Series A at a $1B headline (TechCrunch, Dec 2025) and Simile's $100M Series A (ai2.work, 2026) landed inside the 18-month window Auxos is now entering at pre-seed. The dimensional signature here is *catalyst-present, velocity-absent*: the market re-opening is real but Auxos has no revenue curve, no named logo, and no benchmark to distinguish itself from the funded incumbents who are already several rounds in.

**Anduril** (2022) is the high rung on market re-opening I keep returning to — Ukraine was a specific, datable catalyst that re-opened defense procurement in a 24-month window, and the dossier here does contain a catalyst of the same shape: LLM persona fidelity crossing usability around 2024, evidenced by Aaru and Simile both clearing nine-figure rounds inside 18 months. Where Auxos diverges from the Anduril template is that Anduril sat in a market peers had written off entirely; Auxos sits in a market peers have *already declared on* with $150M+ in announced capital to two competitors. The catalyst exists, but Auxos is not skating to the puck — Auxos is skating to where Aaru and Simile already are, with a pre-seed check and three months of runway-equivalent brand. The catalyst is real; the window for a *new* entrant in it is the question, and the dossier offers no answer.

The Harvey (2023) failure mode in reverse — "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) — is what the revenue-velocity ladder bottoms out on, and Auxos's dossier produces exactly that silence. The "847 packaging tests, 2,104 ad creative A/B tests" on useauxos.com are labeled illustrative, not disclosed customer usage; no ARR, no pilots, no named logo, no waitlist count. I distinguish "false signal" from "stuff that is just working" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) — Auxos has neither, which is worse than a false signal because it removes the ability to even debate the curve. Evidenza is bootstrapping to "seven figures in revenue" (FishDog 2026) without YC, and Auxos's dossier shows no comparable revenue signal three months post-launch in the same category. In a pre-seed I forgive missing metrics; in a *pre-seed in a category where competitors are already at growth-stage revenue*, the absence is itself the signal.

Mechanism on the founders: Kerry Lu built ad-simulation infra at Amazon Ads "modeling 40% of US e-commerce traffic" and Jerry Wu built Instagram reels/ads infra credited with "$100M+ of incremental revenue per year." Those are real simulation-and-ads-infra credentials — exactly the technical substrate digital-twin fidelity needs. But over 18 months the founder-ships-between-meetings test compounds on *independent shipping artifacts*, not big-company scope. Auxos's dossier names no GitHub repos, no prior side projects, no shipped iterations of the twin product between YC office hours and now — only big-company titles. The infra credentials are load-bearing for the technical miracle; they're not load-bearing for the Aravind-pinged-me-on-LinkedIn-with-a-built-thing pattern (Tim Ferriss Show #863) that gates my cold-inbound bets.

Counterfactual on index position: for Auxos to tier up here, the dossier would need to show that every entrant in the AI-research category *must touch* an Auxos primitive — a recruitment graph, a fidelity benchmark, a twin-API standard. **Stripe** (2010) is "an index on e-commerce" because the rails were architectural; Auxos as described is a vertical app that must *beat* Aaru on synthetic fidelity and Synthetic Users on flexibility and Evidenza on enterprise distribution. The "one real person behind every digital twin" wedge could in principle become a proprietary recruitment-and-calibration dataset, but the dossier shows no scale, no retention, no longitudinal recalibration loop yet. Without that, Auxos is fighting for share in a category that already has $150M+ of declared capital aimed at it.

Single-anchor parallel on miracles: **Decagon** (2023) earned my check because the architecture isolated to one miracle — LLM accuracy at customer-support triage — with the rest GTM. Auxos compounds three: (1) twin responses must materially approximate real-respondent outcomes (the syntheticusers.com and MeasuringU posts both flag that the category lacks settled accuracy benchmarks), (2) per-twin recruitment economics must amortize across enough queries to undercut the panel cost Auxos itself names as broken, (3) enterprise research buyers must displace incumbent Qualtrics/Dynata workflows. Each is independent. Multiplying small probabilities is how science projects look from the outside, and this is the *Unusual Signs* (NFX) test Auxos fails most cleanly.

My 36-month forecast on Auxos: acquihired by Qualtrics, Dynata, or a CX-research roll-up for sub-$40M, or dead, with the modal outcome being the latter — Aaru and Simile consolidate the digital-twin category on capital advantage while Evidenza captures the bootstrapped-enterprise lane, leaving Auxos without a wedge that justifies the next round. The mechanism is straightforward: the catalyst is real, the category is crowded, and Auxos's only differentiator (real-person grounding) re-imports the recruitment cost it claims to eliminate, which means unit economics either undercut the wedge or undercut the value proposition.

The strongest bull case against my forecast runs through the Decagon/Abridge template — workflow infrastructure on a recently catalyzed category, exactly the *AI Market Clarity* "still wide open" lane (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025). On that read, Auxos's "real person per twin" is a proprietary-data moat the synthetic-only incumbents can't replicate, and the founder trio's simulation-infra pedigree (Amazon Ads, Meta) is the right substrate to win on fidelity. The bear-side answer is that the *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy I wrote treats market-research-via-twins as a sub-category where capital has already declared, not a wide-open lane, and Auxos's dossier provides no benchmark, no logo, and no curve to demonstrate the data wedge is anything more than a recruiting tax. I would flip to Invest on one verifiable condition: Auxos publishes a head-to-head fidelity benchmark vs. Aaru and Synthetic Users showing materially better predictive accuracy against held-out real-respondent outcomes, AND signs three named Fortune 500 logos at >$100K ACV within six months. Absent that, this is a Harvey-clone-shape bet in a category where Harvey-equivalents already exist.
