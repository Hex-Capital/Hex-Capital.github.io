# PLAN0 AI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Arash Barati ran $7B of CRE through PE, lending, and development before pointing a computer-vision model at architectural plans -- so PLAN0 AI's analytical signature is high founder-market fit bolted onto a category I keep saying is "just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024). The dossier reads like a vertical-AI SaaS pitch that any of four other YC Spring 2026 teams could have written -- except the CEO has actually underwritten the deals PLAN0 AI claims to accelerate. That tension is the whole evaluation: a missionary operator strapped to a mercenary cap table, in a sleeve I de-rate by default.

Sublime Security is the closest portfolio analogue on the conviction axis -- enterprise operator buying into a category before the next consolidation wave, on the strength of domain depth rather than a viral deck. PLAN0 AI's Arash Barati clears the "3+ years working on the problem" filter by roughly a decade -- 8 years CRE PE at $1.5B, 3 years lending at $4.5B, 3 years development at $1.2B (founder bio via search snippet) -- and brother Shervin Barati ran AirPods hearing-health features as an Apple engineering manager, which is a real ML/shipping spike. Where PLAN0 AI diverges from Sublime is the absence of any public artifact of contrarian belief: no essays, no opinionated threads, no published take on why CRE underwriting estimators have failed for fifteen years. The domain is in the resume, not on the page.

The pass example I keep returning to -- "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, four tier-1 VCs in" -- failed because the category was consensus and the cap table was generic SaaS. PLAN0 AI lands adjacent to that pattern rather than on top of it: $4M seed (StartupHub.ai, May 2026), no tier-1 visibly passing, no disclosed pricing, demo-only funnel, and a product description -- "AI powered construction cost estimation & analytics" -- that I could swap for three other Spring 2026 vertical-AI takeoff plays without anyone noticing. Six AI estimating platforms were already benchmarked publicly in February 2026 (Robotics & Automation News). The category is crowded, the round is silent on price, and silence on price is how I find out later I overpaid.

Mechanism on the cost-database moat: PLAN0 AI claims proprietary localized pricing via unnamed "industry partnerships," and within 18 months the question is whether that data asset compounds or commoditizes. Togal.AI has $17.2M raised and a convertible note from August 2025 (Crunchbase via search snippet) -- if Togal licenses RSMeans or Gordian feeds and bolts them onto a 98%-accuracy takeoff engine that already prices at $299/mo, PLAN0 AI's differentiation collapses into "we have Arash's Rolodex." That's a real asset, but it's a sales moat, not a data moat, and sales moats don't survive a competitor who out-raises you 4x.

Counterfactual on capital structure: for PLAN0 AI's cap table to flip from generic SaaS to venture-shaped, I'd need to see the $20B-of-projects claim attached to a revenue-share or transaction-fee instrument against the actual capital flowing through those underwriting decisions -- a basis-point take on closed loans, or an equity-for-data swap with the named lenders. None of that appears in the dossier. PLAN0 AI is taking a standard seed SAFE into a workflow that touches billions in transaction value, which is exactly the financing-structure mismatch I flag as founder-drainage in Criterion 2.

Founder-as-brand on PLAN0 AI is the John Fish / Bookshelved inversion. Arash Barati has a Twitter handle (@ash_barati) with follower count not retrievable, no public writing on CRE underwriting, no podcast, no newsletter, no Discord -- distribution is entirely "demo-gated funnel plus founder Rolodex." That's a legitimate enterprise GTM, but it's not the cap-table asset the Slow Creator Fund (February 2025) was built to underwrite, and it means PLAN0 AI's CAC depends on Arash personally showing up to every Tishman and Related pitch for the next three years.

Forecast: in 36 months PLAN0 AI is most likely a $4-9M ARR underwriting-acceleration tool acquired by a CRE data incumbent -- CoStar, Procore, or Yardi -- for $40-90M, a soft single for the seed and a non-event for Slow's fund math. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: Arash Barati's $7B network closes the first twenty enterprise logos, but the cost-database moat doesn't compound faster than Togal's $17.2M war chest can replicate it, and the company gets absorbed before reaching a Series B priced at venture-return math.

The bull case that stings is the Ro exception: regulatory and data-integration complexity in CRE underwriting (lender-specific stress tests, jurisdictional cost variance, draw-schedule logic) might constitute a genuine "venture moment" where only VC capital can clear the integration schleps, and Arash Barati's $7B of relationships is exactly the asymmetric channel a generalist competitor can't replicate. The problem is Ro had 50-state prescription rails -- a legal moat. PLAN0 AI has localized cost data, which RSMeans and Gordian already sell, and lender relationships, which Togal can recruit a CRE GM to clone in a quarter. The Ro analogy fails on the licensing schlep test. I flip to Invest if PLAN0 AI publishes (a) a named anchor customer like Blackstone or Related at six-figure ACV or (b) a financing instrument structured against transaction volume rather than seats -- absent either, PLAN0 AI is a competent acqui-hire candidate, not a Slow check.
