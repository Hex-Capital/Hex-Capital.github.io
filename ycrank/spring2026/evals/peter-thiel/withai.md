# WithAI -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

WithAI is a credentialed-insider pedigree bet wearing the costume of a contrarian thesis ? Ian McInnis ran Bridgewater's "how can we use LLMs to invest" workstream, Greg Jensen and Karen Karniol-Tambour (Bridgewater co-CIOs) are on the angel cap table, and the product is software-only middleware sitting on top of Anthropic in a hedge-fund client's cloud. The dimensional signature here is high founder lineage paired with a near-absent atoms-heavy moat ? which makes WithAI an **Asana**-shaped exception candidate rather than an **Anduril**-shaped definite-optimism check.

Founder lineage is where WithAI ranks highest, so start there. **Affirm** (2014) was the HIGH rung ? Max Levchin re-upping after PayPal, an operator who had shipped to scale and was running back into a category he understood structurally. The LOW rung is the standard pass: a current-batch Stanford CS pair with no shipped product and no proxy signal. WithAI sits between MID (**Stripe** ? Collison as credentialed-outsider Olympiad with technical signal but no shipped-to-scale exit) and HIGH ? McInnis is buy-side-Bridgewater not PayPal-Mafia, but the Jensen and Karniol-Tambour angel checks are the Bridgewater-equivalent revealed-sourcing channel, and Benjamin Finch's Sentient Labs ($85M raised) founding-researcher tenure plus the OML cryptographic paper (IACR ePrint 2024/1573) is a real technical signal, not LinkedIn cosplay. This is the strongest dimension in the dossier and the only one that would make me take the meeting.

Atoms-heavy moat is where WithAI ranks lowest, and the gap is severe. **SpaceX** (2008) and **Anduril** (2017) are the HIGH rung ? hardware capex plus defense-customer regulatory depth that pure-software competitors cannot replicate. **Asana** (2010) is the LOW rung that nevertheless got funded as a known exception, where pedigree overrode missing structural moat. WithAI sits adjacent to the **Asana** position but worse: hedge funds are not a regulated-customer base in the **Palantir** sense, client-cloud deployment is a security feature not a regulatory certification depth, and the "60+ integrations including Bloomberg, FactSet, Snowflake, Enfusion" stack is exactly the kind of workflow assembly a competent finance-ML team replicates in eighteen months. There is no atoms here and no regulatory cert moat. The Founders Fund manifesto (2011) ? "we wanted flying cars, instead we got 140 characters" ? was written against precisely this shape of bits-only middleware.

Contrarian secret: **Stripe** (2010) is the HIGH rung ? developer-first payments when PayPal had "won." The LOW rung is the pass-example "AI for [vertical X]" with no named incumbent failure mode. WithAI sits in the MID-to-LOW band: the founders do name a real architectural read ? finance is "non-verifiable knowledge work" where outputs cannot be cheaply checked, so a chatbot deliverable model (Rogo's IB memos, AlphaSense's search-over-content) misses the actual job, which is a persistent command center. That is closer to a secret than most Spring 2026 dossiers contain. But it sits inside the consensus AI-for-finance wave that Rogo's $75M Series C in January 2026 already validated to every herd VC, and the named-incumbent failure mode terminates at "Bloomberg and FactSet have channel conflict" rather than at a Lockheed-Raytheon-style structural impossibility.

Monopoly trajectory: **Palantir** (2003) is the HIGH rung ? integration-depth-plus-government-customer pricing power compounding for fifteen years. The LOW rung is the consensus AI-for-X with "we have a better model" as the entire moat hypothesis. WithAI sits between MID (**Lyft** 2010 ? duopoly override on power-law positioning) and LOW: client-cloud-plus-ontology switching costs are real once embedded, but the hedge-fund universe is fragmented, the addressable mid-size-asset-manager segment is a few thousand firms, and Rogo's $154M total raised plus AlphaSense's content-library distribution mean WithAI is at best one of three credible vendors ? competition framed as the market, not as the failure mode. Definite plan: **SpaceX** (2008) is the HIGH rung with a fifteen-year reusability roadmap; the LOW rung is iteration-to-PMF. WithAI sits MID-to-LOW ? "Multiplier" plus white-glove consultative implementation is a GTM motion, not a multi-year technical commitment the founders refuse to pivot from.

Thirty-six-month forecast: WithAI reaches $4-10M ARR across 12-25 mid-size asset managers sold through the Bridgewater-Jensen-Karniol-Tambour network, then either gets acquired by Bloomberg or FactSet at a defensive $80-150M tag to neutralize the seat-cannibalization risk on the terminal, or stalls at sub-$15M ARR as Rogo's $154M war chest expands from IB deliverables into the hedge-fund persistent-workspace category and compresses WithAI into a Bridgewater-network niche. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: strong-pedigree-software-only plays in fragmented financial-services verticals end as feature acquisitions or as also-rans, not as monopolies, because the moat that gets you to $10M ARR (firm-specific integration depth) is not the moat that gets you to $1B (network density or regulatory cert).

The strongest opposing case rests on the **Asana** (2010) exception ? Moskovitz had shipped **Facebook** to scale, the workflow-tools category was consensus, the moat was missing, and the check worked because operator-pedigree weight overrode the structural gaps. Apply that template here and the bull says: Bridgewater co-CIOs as angels is the Bridgewater-Mafia equivalent, McInnis ran the in-house AI-investing R&D that every fund will need to copy, Finch is a real frontier-LLM researcher, and the non-verifiable-knowledge-work secret is genuinely contrarian against Rogo and AlphaSense. The objection stings on lineage. It breaks on shipped-product asymmetry: Moskovitz had shipped Facebook to a billion users before Asana; McInnis was an Investment Associate ? a research role, not a builder-to-scale role ? and Asana also had a viral self-serve growth loop that compounded the lineage advantage, which WithAI's consultative-enterprise-sale motion explicitly does not have. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: WithAI reaches eight named hedge-fund logos with disclosed six-figure ACVs within twelve months of YC Demo Day, sourced beyond the Bridgewater angel network ? at which point the Asana-exception override becomes live and I would take the next round.
