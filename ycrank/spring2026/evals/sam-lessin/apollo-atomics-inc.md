# Apollo Atomics, Inc. -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Assil Halimi did a 2021-2025 MIT PhD under Koroush Shirvan on exactly the compact iPWR steam-generator architecture Apollo Atomics is now commercializing -- that's the highest-loading fact in the dossier, and it pairs with the lowest-loading fact, which is that Apollo Atomics is a capital-intensive nuclear hardware company sitting in zero of my documented sleeves (no crypto, no DTC, no prescription rails, no creator). The signature here is a missionary technical founder on the wrong cap-table shape for me: a 2028 NRC construction-permit target, a 2028 10MWe pilot, and competitors who have already raised $127M (Aalo) and $700M+ (X-energy) tell me Apollo Atomics is venture-funded only if you have a half-billion dollars of patient capital behind the SAFE -- which Genesis Fund, Intuition, Paperjet, and NSF do not constitute.

On founder conviction, the load-bearing analogy is **Marina Mogilko** -- not because Apollo Atomics is a creator deal, but because Mogilko had spent a decade obsessing over language-learning before I ever wrote the check, and that pre-pitch obsession is the only filter that survives across categories. Halimi's MIT NSE bio, the 2023 MIT News profile, and the Shirvan-advised PhD on compact PWR thermal hydraulics is exactly the same shape of unpaid 4-year obsession -- Apollo Atomics' technical co-founder has been building this artifact since before there was a company. Where Apollo Atomics diverges from the Mogilko rung is the absence of any public writing or owned channel: no Twitter, no GitHub, no opinionated essays on why every other SMR architecture is wrong. "Writing is thinking" is my filter, and Halimi's thinking is locked inside a dissertation I cannot read on the way to a meeting.

On founder-as-brand, the failure mechanism that kills the **enterprise SaaS with no community** rung is that distribution has to be bought with every dollar of the next round, which compounds against a long licensing runway. Apollo Atomics has @radioactivedrew on Instagram and a LinkedIn page tagged "(YC P26)" with unretrievable follower count -- that is the dossier evidence, and it tracks the failure mode exactly. Nuclear customers (utilities, hyperscalers, industrial heat buyers) are not bought through community anyway, so Apollo Atomics doesn't lose points relative to its category -- but it also gets none of the founder-brand alpha that John Fish or Mogilko brought to the cap table. Nothing here for the Slow Creator Fund frame to grab.

On capital structure -- the dimension I read before founder or market -- Apollo Atomics is mechanically incompatible with the regatta model over an 18-month window. The pre-Series-A tacks for a compact PWR are: experimental two-phase flow validation at MIT, an NRC pre-application engagement, a construction-permit filing in 2028, fuel qualification, and a 10MWe demonstration -- each of those tacks is a $20-100M financing event, and none of them produces revenue. Aalo raised $100M in August 2025 to co-locate a microreactor with a data center; X-energy took $500M+ from Amazon. Apollo Atomics' disclosed backers (Genesis, Intuition, New Era, Paperjet, NSF) are seed-shaped checks behind a Series D-shaped capital plan. The **Ro** exception is the only thing keeping this from a single-digit score -- Ro burned through rounds because 50-state Rx licensing was the moat -- but Ro had revenue from month one, and Apollo Atomics has a 2028+ revenue date.

The counterfactual on round mispricing: for Apollo Atomics to flip from a Pass to a real consideration on Criterion 1, I'd need to see this round priced at a 60-70% discount to where Aalo or Oklo's seeds cleared, with at least one tier-1 nuclear specialist (DCVC, Breakthrough Energy) having visibly passed for a stated reason I disagree with. The dossier instead shows a clean seed-stage investor list and no disclosed round size -- which under my absent-evidence rule defaults to "consensus pricing in a category the hyperscaler narrative just made hot." Apollo Atomics is riding the same data-center power-demand thesis that Aalo's $100M raise advertised; that's the opposite of an illegible asset.

The single-anchor parallel on sleeve fit is **Sublime Security**, which I entered as a pre-consensus enterprise security bet before the next consolidation wave -- the entry rationale was a category sleeve I was already convicted on. Apollo Atomics is the inverse: a category I have on-record dismissed as adjacent to "AI is just more internet" -- nuclear-for-data-centers is consensus narrative as of 2026, and the SMR sleeve is being underwritten by hard-tech specialists (Founders Fund, DCVC, Breakthrough Energy) who are better positioned than I am to ride the regatta. I don't have an edge here.

**Forecast (36 months):** Apollo Atomics burns through its seed and a $20-40M Series A from hard-tech specialists, misses the 2028 NRC construction-permit application by 12-18 months, generates $0 product revenue, and either gets absorbed into a strategic (Westinghouse, GE-Hitachi, or an NuScale-style merger) at a modest markup OR raises a $150M+ Series B that dilutes my seed position into irrelevance -- in both cases I do not make venture-shaped money from the entry round, because the regatta has too many tacks left and each one costs more than the last.

**Strongest objection:** The bull case leans on the Ro exception -- regulatory complexity as moat -- combined with the Aalo precedent that hyperscalers will pay to co-locate compact reactors with data centers, meaning Apollo Atomics could ride the same Amazon/Microsoft narrative tailwind to a $500M+ Series B at a 5-10x markup on seed. Halimi's MIT pedigree and the Shirvan lineage give Apollo Atomics a credible shot at being the technically-superior compact PWR, and PWR fuel is commercially available where Aalo's sodium fuel is not -- so Apollo Atomics could legitimately leapfrog. That bull case stings because it's structurally identical to how Ro worked. What dismantles it: Ro shipped telehealth prescriptions in year one with real revenue funding the regulatory schlep; Apollo Atomics has a 2028 pilot date and no commercial bridge, which means every tack between now and then is dilutive equity, not customer cash. **The condition that flips my verdict:** Apollo Atomics signs a named data-center offtake LOI (Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Oracle) for the A-10 or A-50 before the Series A closes -- that converts this from a "nuclear startup hoping for hyperscaler pull" into a Mogilko-shaped illegible asset I'd structure around.
