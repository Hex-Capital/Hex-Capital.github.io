# Lattice Health -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Lattice Health is a structurally correct orchestration thesis sitting in a slot already occupied by Microsoft/Nuance and Bayer/Blackford, fronted by a solo founder — Christine Park — who is concurrently a UW neurosurgery PGY-3 resident and a Stanford CS PhD candidate, with zero disclosed revenue, zero named LOIs, and no accessible product surface. The dimensional signature is unmistakable: the *index position* is the highest-loading dimension here, and *revenue velocity* is the lowest. That pairing — right slot, no curve, contested incumbents — is the analytical fingerprint of a company that has a real catalyst behind it but is being run with research-lab cadence rather than enterprise-sales cadence.

**Stripe** (2010) is the canonical HIGH rung for index-like distribution — payments rails that compounded with every new e-commerce entrant, "an index on e-commerce" (Tim Ferriss Show #863). Lattice Health is *attempting* the same shape: an orchestration layer that compounds as the FDA-cleared imaging AI population grows past 700 devices (MarketsandMarkets, 2025), so every hospital deploying a third Aidoc/Gleamer/Rad AI combination has to touch the Lattice Health layer. The mechanism that made Stripe load-bearing was that the developer channel was *unoccupied* when Patrick and John shipped the API. Lattice Health diverges precisely there — Blackford (Bayer 2023) and Nuance Precision Imaging Network (Microsoft) already sit inside PowerScribe reporting workflow as the default orchestration marketplace, so Lattice Health is not arriving early to an empty rail; it is arriving late to a rail two strategic acquirers already paved. The index logic is correct; the chair is taken.

Pre-revenue AI companies in the dossier-silent posture are exactly the profile that triggers my "something's fundamentally broken" rule (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) — the failure mechanism is that when a real growth curve exists in this cycle, founders disclose it, because disclosure itself is the wedge into capital and customers. Lattice Health's dossier shows no revenue, no customer count, no signed LOI, no partnerships, no job postings, no web traffic, and no public product website at the YC entity — and the founder is split across a residency and a PhD. That is not a stage-default gap; that is the canonical "false signal vs. just working" failure where there isn't even false signal yet (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). Lattice Health gets the absent-evidence default on this dimension, full stop.

The catalyst dimension is where Lattice Health earns its real points. The >700 FDA-cleared radiology AI devices figure (MarketsandMarkets 2025) is a genuine 24-month integration-burden re-opening — the kind I respect because it mirrors the *Stripe* template (merchant-account fragmentation generated the rail opportunity). Over 18 months this compounds in Lattice Health's favor mechanically: every new FDA clearance adds an integration that some hospital somewhere has to wire into PACS/EHR, and the marginal cost of in-house glue code rises monotonically. The dimension fails Lattice Health only if Microsoft or Bayer compresses the orchestration price to zero as a loss-leader for PowerScribe or Calantic — which, given Nuance's embedded position, is the base-rate outcome rather than the tail.

For founder-ships, the score flips a tier only if Lattice Health discloses a working product shipped to a named academic medical center within 90 days of YC Demo Day — Park's arXiv 2511.18208 (2025) multimodal brain MRI paper and the medRxiv POC MRI midline-shift work prove she ships *research*, not enterprise infrastructure, and the Duke MEng-AI plus Stanford CS PhD plus UW residency stack is a credential ladder, not a shipping ladder. The counterfactual is concrete: one paid pilot at UCSF or Stanford Health, structured as a Lattice Health orchestration deployment rather than a Park-authored model, would move this from a 7 to a 12 on this dimension. Nothing in the current dossier authorizes that move.

**Color Genomics** (2015) is the single-anchor parallel for single-miracle architecture — I co-founded it precisely because the workflow layer on commodity sequencing isolated the bet to GTM and product execution rather than chemistry. Lattice Health is structurally trying the same move: workflow on commodity FDA-cleared models, not a new model. The problem is that Lattice Health's miracle is not actually singular — it requires (a) hospital enterprise sales success as a solo founder, (b) heterogeneous PACS/EHR/HL7/DICOM integration depth across health systems, and (c) displacing Nuance and Blackford from a slot they already monetize. That is two-and-a-half miracles compounded, not the clean one-miracle isolation Color achieved by owning the lab.

Forecast (36 months): Lattice Health does not reach $3M ARR, fails to displace Nuance/Blackford in any IDN of >500 beds, and either pivots to a neurosurgery-specific imaging workflow leveraging Park's domain expertise (the Color-style narrowing) or gets acqui-hired by a PACS vendor or imaging-AI marketplace for sub-$30M — with the modal outcome being quiet death after the Stanford PhD reabsorbs Park's bandwidth. The mechanism is bandwidth math, not thesis math: solo founders with two concurrent terminal training programs cannot run enterprise health-system sales cycles of 6-18 months at the velocity the catalyst window demands.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass is the **Color Genomics** (2015) precedent itself — I funded (and operated) exactly this archetype: clinician-engineer hybrid building workflow infrastructure on top of a commoditizing hard-tech layer, in a market with regulatory tailwinds. Park's MD/MEng-AI/CS-PhD combination is genuinely rare founder-market fit for clinical imaging AI orchestration, and the >700 FDA-cleared device catalyst is real. The bear case stings because the archetype is in my portfolio. It loses on one dossier fact: Color had me as co-founder running the operating machine while the scientific co-founders focused on the technical layer, and Lattice Health has *one* person doing both jobs while finishing a residency and a PhD. Verifiable flip condition: a non-clinician co-founder with prior enterprise health-system GTM experience joins Lattice Health full-time within 90 days, AND one paid pilot is signed with a named academic medical center. Until then, this is a pass that I'd happily revisit at Series A if the curve materializes.
