# Codag -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Codag is the inverse of the company I look for: the single-miracle architecture is unusually clean — Michael Zhou is wrapping a known-good algorithm (Drain3) in Rust and shipping it through MCP into Claude Code and Codex — but the revenue velocity line in the dossier is blank, and "AI company with no growth curve disclosed" is the exact phrase from Tim Ferriss Show #863 (April 2026) where I said "something's fundamentally broken." That pairing — tightest miracle, loosest velocity — is what Codag is.

**Anduril** (2022) is the right ladder rung for Codag's market-catalyst dimension because the analogy is mechanical, not vibes: both companies are betting that a specific 24-month consumption shift (Ukraine revealing Pentagon software inadequacy for Anduril; Claude Code and Codex routinely ingesting multi-megabyte log windows for Codag) opened a workflow window that incumbents weren't built around. Where Codag diverges from the Anduril template is that the Anduril catalyst was named on the front page of every defense outlet by Q2 2022, whereas Codag's catalyst — "within two years, agents will be the primary consumer of logs" — is the founder's own thesis statement on codag.ai, not an externally validated step-function. That's the difference between "skating where the puck is going" and "asserting where the puck is going" — I score this between my Decagon (2023) and Anduril rungs, closer to Decagon.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure mode that haunts Codag's revenue-velocity dimension, but not for the network-effect reason — it's for the "the dossier doesn't show what I need to see" reason. With Lyft I needed share inversion math and didn't get it; with Codag I need month-over-month token-throughput or paid-conversion numbers and the dossier returns "no public revenue, customer count, press coverage, or Product Hunt launch found." A free 50 MB/month tier with $19 Hobby and $199 Team pricing is a price list, not a growth curve. The Codag homepage demo compressing 26.6M tokens to 3,317 tokens is a benchmark, not buying behavior. In the AI cycle, founders who are growing publish the number — silence here is the false-signal pattern from my November 2025 TechCrunch interview, not modesty.

Michael Zhou clears the founder-shipping bar by the **Perplexity** (2022) template — Aravind Srinivas got the check because he "showed up a week later with the thing we discussed built," and Codag's founder has two repos on GitHub (`michaelzixizhou/codag` at 346 stars, `codag-megalith/codag-drain` at 42 stars), a LogHub-2.0 benchmark with blind-judge evaluation, live MCP integrations with two named agent runtimes, and a backstory ("bootstrapped Codag until his bank account hit zero, pivoted 5 times, did door-to-door sales") that reads as shipper, not promoter. Mechanism: this dimension compounds for Codag because the OSS repo *is* the resume — every commit moves the founder-graph signal even if revenue doesn't. The 18-month risk isn't that Michael Zhou stops shipping; it's that solo-founder bandwidth caps the surface area he can ship across once GTM enters the picture (YC page shows 0 open roles).

Counterfactual on index position: for Codag to flip from a 6-of-15 to a 12-of-15 on the Stripe-style "index on e-commerce" dimension, the dossier would need to show that MCP integration into Claude Code and Codex is exclusive, contractual, or technically deep enough that a Datadog-native log compression feature couldn't substitute. It does not. ClickHouse already blogged "improve logs compression with log clustering," Drain3 is the open-source antecedent, and `codag-megalith/codag-drain` is itself MIT-licensed Rust. Codag wants to sit in the workflow path every agent touches; what it actually has today is a thin wrapper above a public algorithm, distributed through an OSS CLI — the opposite of how Stripe (2010) made itself unsubstitutable through deep API surface area.

Single-anchor parallel for the single-miracle dimension: **Decagon** (2023) is the closest portfolio template — one validated capability (LLMs work for customer support) plus GTM and product execution, no chemistry or regulatory dependencies. Codag is structurally cleaner than most pre-seeds I see this batch: the miracle ("agents consume so many log tokens that compression is worth paying for") is already validated by Claude Code's own context-window economics, and the remaining work is distribution, pricing, and template-grouping accuracy. This is why Codag scores highest on this dimension — there is no second miracle hiding in the dossier.

Forecast, 36 months: Codag is acquihired by Datadog, Grafana, or Anthropic for under $50M as a feature integration by mid-2029, or pivots into agent observability (the Langfuse/Arize lane) once log-compression gets bundled natively by an MCP host. The mechanism is the high/low signature: a clean-miracle, no-velocity infrastructure bet against incumbents who can ship the same compression as a checkbox feature gets bundled before it gets monetized — same fate as most "feature-not-a-company" dev tools where the founder is excellent but the wedge is shallow.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-bear-case is that Codag is doing the **Color Genomics** (2015) move — workflow infrastructure on top of a commodity layer (sequencing for Color; Drain3 for Codag) — and the workflow layer is where the durable margin sits even when the underlying tech is public. The objection stings because Michael Zhou's pivot history and shipping velocity are real, and "selling units of cognition" (per my *AI Market Clarity* essay, Nov 2025) implies every token saved on agent context is metered value. What dismantles it: Color owned the patient-data and clinician-workflow surface area, which compounded into switching costs; Codag's "workflow surface" is a CLI and two MCP plugins that any competitor — including the agent runtimes themselves — can replicate in a sprint. The verifiable condition that flips my pass: Codag posts $50K+ MRR with three named enterprise logos by Q4 2026, or signs a non-trivial revenue-share with Anthropic or OpenAI specifically for Claude Code / Codex context compression. Absent that, the consensus-pays framing doesn't apply because there's no consensus on the curve yet.
