# StableBrowse -- Elad Gil Evaluation

StableBrowse is a category-right, traction-silent pre-seed: the agent-browser-infrastructure thesis is real, but four co-founders -- Sarthak Awasthi, Jay Mehta, Deepit Shah, Somansh Shah -- are pitching into a window where Browserbase has already raised $67.5M and booked ~$3M in revenue. The signature here is a high market-catalyst score paired with a near-empty revenue-velocity column, which is the exact pairing my Tim Ferriss #863 framing calls "fundamentally broken" for an AI company that's been visible long enough to disclose a curve. StableBrowse's dossier names performance percentages ("70-80% fewer tokens, 98% success rate") but not a single dollar of revenue, not a paying logo, not a usage compounding rate.

**Decagon** (2023) is the load-bearing analogy on market catalyst -- workflow infrastructure in a post-LLM open sub-market, exactly the *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) "still wide open" template. The shared mechanism is real: LLM agent adoption crossed a usability threshold in 2024-2025, and the dossier correctly cites Browserbase's Series B, Kernel's $22M Series A (Accel), and TinyFish's $47M Series A (ICONIQ) inside a twelve-month window as the catalyst proof. Where StableBrowse diverges from the Decagon rung is that Decagon entered a sub-market without a $300M-valuation incumbent already sitting on the developer-API layer; StableBrowse is launching against Browserbase's 20,000 developer signups and 50M browser sessions (Upstarts Media, 2025), so the catalyst is genuine but the *uncontested* part of the window has narrowed.

The "AI company with no growth curve disclosed" red flag from Tim Ferriss #863 is the failure mechanism I'd anchor on -- when a dossier substitutes self-reported benchmarks ("3-4x faster execution") for revenue or DAU, my prior is that real growth is easy to disclose and silence is its own signal. Tracing the pattern in StableBrowse: no pricing page exposed at stablebrowse.com, no paying-customer count, no MRR, no Product Hunt launch, no Discord, only an engineer-affiliation list (Meta, Google, Amazon, Citi, EY, Purdue, Stanford, UCSD) which is a logo-name-drop, not a buying-behavior signal. StableBrowse is YC Spring 2026, so the absence is partially stage-default, but the four-founder team has been building long enough to put one numbered usage line in front of investors and chose not to.

Founder shipping rhythm -- the **Perplexity** (2022) test where Aravind "showed up a week later with a thing we discussed built" -- compounds over 18 months as the only legible signal in pre-seed: founders who ship between conversations widen distance from incumbents by quarter, founders who don't get absorbed. StableBrowse's dossier names Jay Mehta's distributed-systems background and Deepit Shah's prior Amazon role plus an RBF numerical-methods paper, but no GitHub repos, no prior shipped product, no side-project artifact, no founder Twitter. That's pedigree, not shipping evidence, and it's the disqualifier my Hustle Commons framework explicitly calls out.

For StableBrowse to clear the index-position bar that would flip this to a 65+, one counterfactual has to hold: the persistent semantic graph would need to compound cross-customer such that the second insurance carrier StableBrowse maps is materially cheaper than the first, and the tenth EHR is near-free. That's the only architecture under which StableBrowse becomes "the layer Browserbase eventually integrates against" rather than a feature Browserbase ships in a quarter. The dossier asserts the moat ("persistent site memory," "execution patterns") but discloses zero graph-coverage data, zero customer count, zero cross-customer reuse metric -- so the index claim is structural, not demonstrated.

**Color Genomics** (2015) is the single-miracle parallel: I structured Color as workflow infrastructure on commodity sequencing precisely to isolate the bet to one capability rather than chaining science + regulation + behavior. StableBrowse's miracle stack does not isolate cleanly -- semantic-graph reliability across heterogeneous sites is one miracle, HIPAA-grade compliance for healthcare prior-auth is another (and SOC 2 is *in progress*, not complete, per the dossier), and beating Browserbase/Kernel/TinyFish on developer adoption is a third. That's a three-miracle dependency chain in a category that's already past pioneer-stage, which is the inverse of the Decagon setup.

My 36-month forecast: StableBrowse either gets acqui-hired by Browserbase, Kernel, or TinyFish for under $20M as a semantic-graph feature bolt-on, or pivots to a single vertical RPA wedge (insurance quote binding or prior authorization) and lands sub-$2M ARR. The mechanism is the dimensional signature -- correct category, no curve, three-miracle stack, $67M-funded incumbent owning the developer-API slot -- which historically produces absorption, not breakout. Same shape as my **Lyft** Series C pass on winner-take-all dynamics, except here it's an oligopoly already pricing in the consolidation.

The strongest opposing case is the Decagon-pattern bull read: agent-browser-infra is in the *AI Market Clarity* "wide open" column, Browserbase at only $3M revenue against $67M raised proves the category is real but unwon, and a persistent execution-graph could be a genuinely differentiated single moat that headless-browser session-vendors structurally can't bolt on without rebuilding their data model. That stings -- it's the same shape as backing Decagon in 2023. What dismantles it is the revenue-velocity silence in StableBrowse's dossier: Decagon had named enterprise pilots compounding inside 90 days, and StableBrowse has zero. The condition that flips my verdict: StableBrowse shows >$50K MRR with two named regulated-vertical logos (one insurance carrier, one health system) inside six months, and I'll re-underwrite at a markup rather than pass.
