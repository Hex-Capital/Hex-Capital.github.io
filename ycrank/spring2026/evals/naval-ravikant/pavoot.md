# Pavoot -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Pavoot is two ETH computer scientists — Gohar Tamrazyan and Ana Yoon Faria de Lima — pointing genuine ML skill at a B2B problem neither has lived inside, and the asymmetry between extreme team-density and near-zero specific knowledge IS Pavoot's signature. The dossier shows a scope migration mid-batch from "AI media hub distributing photos at START Summit" to "AI agent that turns events into pipeline with CRM sync." That sentence tells me almost everything I need to know.

Specific knowledge is where I always start, and Pavoot sits on the low rung. Perplexity sits at the top of this ladder — Aravind Srinivas spent years at Google Brain and OpenAI building the exact substrate of LLM search before he ever called it a company. The bottom rung is the YC archetype I described in my pass example: two operators identifying that "enterprise needs AI summaries" and wrapping an API. Pavoot is closer to that bottom rung than to Notion's middle. Tamrazyan is a six-time Swiss Chess Champion and an ETH CS teaching award winner; Faria de Lima has 20+ Scientific Olympiad medals and a KOF Swiss Economic Institute background. None of that is RevOps knowledge, demand-gen knowledge, or event-marketing knowledge. The company was founded after a course at the ETH AI Center — not after years of obsessing over why marketers can't attribute pipeline to a $50K booth. If Tamrazyan and Faria de Lima disappeared, two other ETH CS graduates could rebuild Pavoot in a quarter. The product is not an extension of their non-replicable expertise; it is an extension of their availability.

Access democratization is where Pavoot sits even lower. AngelList and CoinList are the high rung — both removed structural gatekeepers (accredited-investor networks, securities lawyers) for millions of people. Polymarket sits in the middle. The low rung is any enterprise SaaS that sells a cleaner version of a workflow already served by Cvent and Bizzabo. Pavoot is selling to CMOs and demand-gen leaders at companies that already spend "millions on events" — there is no gatekeeper being removed, no previously excluded population getting access. The buyer is the same Salesforce-administering RevOps director who already has a Vendelux contract. No democratization mechanism is even claimed in the dossier.

Permissionless leverage gets Pavoot partial credit. Twitter is the high rung — zero marginal cost public communication scaling to hundreds of millions. Wish is the cautionary middle — marketplace leverage without underlying creator utility. Pavoot is an AI agent layered on top of OpenAI/Anthropic APIs and Salesforce/HubSpot SDKs, which is renting someone else's leverage rather than owning it. Software was eaten by AI, yes — but in this case the AI Pavoot rents is the same AI Cvent and HubSpot Breeze can rent natively, with distribution Pavoot does not have. Code leverage exists; structural leverage does not.

Principal-agent diagnostic: Sahil Lavingia at Gumroad continued shipping after his Series A failed because he could not stop building it — that is the high rung. The low rung is the founder who chose the idea because it was fundable. Pavoot's mid-batch pivot from "media hub doing photo tagging at START Summit" to "pipeline agent with CRM sync" reads as agent behavior, not principal behavior. Principals do not reposition the entire company in three months; they discover the deeper version of the same problem they were already obsessed with. Team-to-value is Pavoot's one strong dimension — Midjourney with 163 people doing $500M is the high rung, and Pavoot at two technical founders with an AI-first stack genuinely qualifies as the kind of shrinking-company architecture I keep predicting. That's real, but the leverage is pointed at a problem the founders did not earn the right to solve.

In 36 months, Pavoot reaches roughly $1–3M ARR on a handful of mid-market logos, then gets absorbed by Cvent, HubSpot, or Vendelux for under $30M — or quietly dies when Salesforce Einstein ships native event-attribution. The mechanism is the one I name in every AI-wrapper pass: all the value flows to the customer and ultimately to the foundation model and the CRM incumbent. Innovation without a moat is consumer surplus.

The strongest bull case against my pass: Notion was also a small technical team building a productivity tool in a crowded category, and the team-density plus agentic-AI moment plus a credible START Global reference customer could compound into something Vendelux cannot match because Vendelux's $14M Series A makes them an agent of their own roadmap. Fair. But Notion's founders had been obsessing over productivity tools for years — they rebuilt the product from scratch when it didn't work because they could not do anything else. Pavoot's founders pivoted the product category mid-batch. That is the difference between principals and agents, and no AI moment papers over it. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: five enterprise logos at six-figure ACVs within twelve months, sold by Tamrazyan and Faria de Lima themselves, with a roadmap that does not require hiring an enterprise sales leader. Until then, I pass.
