## The Idea

- **Problem:** Early-stage companies face regulatory tracking, policymaker lobbying, and government-contract pursuit but rely on traditional lobbying/GA firms whose services are cost-prohibitive to startups (company website, 2026).
- **Approach:** Combines "elite lobbyists with AI agents" — AI systems monitor policy, draft briefs, map stakeholders, and flag opportunities, paired with human political judgment (search snippet, company site, 2026).
- **Differentiation:** Versus FiscalNote/Quorum/POLITICO Pro (software-only policy intelligence platforms with human analyst add-ons per Parsagon blog 2026), Justinian bundles AI monitoring with a delivered lobbying service; versus traditional GA firms, positioning is "fraction of the cost" (company site, 2026).
- **Business Model:** No public pricing page; [Inferred]: Services-plus-software retainer model given firm framing and hiring of policy staff on equity/salary bands (YC job listings, 2026).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment; adjacent reference: Anthropic and OpenAI each spent ~$3M on federal lobbying in 2025 (Forbes, Feb 2026).
- **GTM / Distribution:** [Inferred]: Founder-led direct sales to early-stage/YC-network companies, evidenced by demo booking flow on founder's personal Cal.com link (justinianai.com, 2026).

## Key Risks

- **Name collision / market confusion:** Vulcan Technologies (YC 2026) publicly markets an AI agent named "Justinian" at vulcan.ai/agents/justinian for law/government use cases (vulcan.ai, 2026), and HAQQ operates a "Justinian Legal AI Engine" (haqq.ai/justinian, 2026); brand and SEO ambiguity plus potential trademark friction.
- **Services-margin trap:** Government-affairs firms are labor-intensive; scaling "elite lobbyists" alongside AI software creates hybrid unit economics that may not compress toward SaaS margins, per FiscalNote's own analyst-services blend (fiscalnote.com blog, 2026).
- **Regulatory/lobbying registration risk:** Delivering lobbying (not just software) triggers Lobbying Disclosure Act registration and state-level lobbyist registration obligations; [Inferred]: heavier compliance overhead than pure-software peers (no direct citation on company's registration status).
- **Concentrated founder dependency:** Only one founder publicly identified with domain background (LinkedIn, 2026); second team member unnamed, and no technical co-founder is publicly disclosed while the company is actively recruiting a Technical Staff role (YC page, 2026).
- **Distribution unproven:** No cited customers, press, revenue, or waitlist figures found; founder X account has 38 followers (X.com, 2026), limiting organic top-of-funnel.
