# Alchemize -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Alchemize is the right schlep attacked by founders I can't yet see as animals. Samuel Fu and Robert Hou have picked a problem that genuinely sits where mainstream YC applicants flinch -- US customs brokerage, sub-15% broker license exam pass rate, $3T of imports flowing through 1990s software -- but the dossier hands me a Columbia bio, an unretrievable LinkedIn, and zero shipped artifact. The category is **Coinbase** (2012)-shaped. The founders, on the evidence in front of me, are not.

The schlep dimension is where Alchemize ranks highest, so start there. The HIGH rung is **Coinbase** (2012): bitcoin compliance grind nobody else in the batch wanted, and Brian Armstrong's appetite for that grind became the moat. The MID rung is **DoorDash** (2013): last-mile delivery ops, real schlep, but a schlep many competitors eventually accepted. The LOW rung is the polished AI-wrapper team from my pass example -- pure software, no offline grind, nothing unpleasant. Alchemize sits between Coinbase and DoorDash on the category map: filing entries with CBP requires a licensed broker (sub-15% pass rate, Mordor Intelligence 2026), HTS classification is the kind of tedious work LLMs were built to grind on, and Flexport's October 2025 AI Customs Suite landing at a claimed 0.2% error rate confirms the schlep is real enough to mobilize incumbents. What pins Alchemize below Coinbase rather than at it: the dossier does not name which founder holds the broker license, and without that license Alchemize cannot file a single entry. The schlep is named; the founder's appetite for personally doing it is not.

Animal resourcefulness is where Alchemize ranks lowest, and this is the dimension I weight at 35. The HIGH rung is **Airbnb** (2009) -- the Obama O's cereal boxes at $40 each, over 1,000 sold to fund the company when no investor would. The MID rung is **Coinbase** (2012) -- Armstrong rebuilding his Android Bitcoin wallet to overturn a written YC rejection. The LOW rung is the two-MBA AI-wrapper team from my pass example -- no shipped artifact, no stunt, just a deck. Alchemize lands at the LOW rung, not between. The dossier gives me no Product Hunt, no Hacker News Launch, no Chrome extension, no app store presence, no pilot importer named, no signed LOI, no GitHub repos for either founder, and no Twitter accounts. The single retrievable founder fact is Samuel Fu's ~4K LinkedIn followers. Animals leave tracks. I am reading a dossier with no tracks.

Organic-idea fit ladders the same way and I'm forced to apply the absent-evidence default with a downward lean. **Dropbox** (2007) is the HIGH rung -- Drew Houston forgetting his USB stick at MIT until he built the thing to stop forgetting it. The LOW rung is, again, the market-opportunity MBA team. Alchemize gives me Samuel Fu's headline "Building AI-enabled customs brokerage" and a possible Netflix tenure inferred from mutual connections -- neither of those is a customs-domain pain story. No prior freight role, no licensed-broker history, no trade-compliance employer surfaced for either founder. This reads like a market spotted, not a problem suffered.

Hacker genealogy lands between Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize (HIGH rung, preceding **Stripe** (2010)) and the credential-only non-hacker (LOW rung). Alchemize has no GitHub trail, no open-source contribution, no HN presence pre-company, no Lisp or competitive-programming marker for either Fu or Hou. The Cognition LinkedIn snippet for a "Robert Hou" is unresolved disambiguation, not evidence. I lean down from the 6/15 default. Cofounder configuration is the cleanest of the weak dimensions: two founders, which beats solo per "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) and clears the **Twitch** (2007) trio bar from the wrong direction; but the dossier offers no shared-school overlap, no prior working relationship, no dorm-roommate-pattern of **Reddit** (2005). I sit at the default.

My 36-month forecast: Alchemize is acquihired by a mid-tier freight forwarder or freight-tech roll-up for under $10M, or pivots into selling AI workflow tools to existing licensed brokers -- the **Amari AI** lane -- with ARR under $2M. The mechanism is the signature: a real schlep without an animal to grind it produces a company that gets out-executed by Flexport's in-house AI Suite on the importer-direct lane and by KlearNow.AI's $69M war chest on the platform lane, leaving Alchemize the picked-over middle where acquihires happen.

The bull case that should sting: the schlep is genuinely there, the tariff-policy chaos catalyst is genuinely there, the labor scarcity from a 43+ average broker age is genuinely there, and a hungry two-person YC team with a regulatory wedge has won this kind of fight before -- Armstrong at **Coinbase** (2012) didn't have customs-domain pedigree either, he had appetite. Fair. But Armstrong rebuilt the Android wallet after rejection; that was the artifact that flipped the call. Alchemize's dossier contains no equivalent -- no demo video, no pilot importer, no entry filed, no stunt revenue. The **Airbnb** (2009) Known Exception requires a cereal-box moment; Alchemize has not shown me one. One verifiable condition flips this: either Fu or Hou is a licensed customs broker who has personally filed entries, OR Alchemize ships a public demo clearing a real entry for a named importer. Either one and I re-read. Until then, pass.
