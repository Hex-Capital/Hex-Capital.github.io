# Madrone -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Madrone is a hardware thermodynamics bet riding a genuine AI-compute catalyst with zero validation and a multi-miracle dependency chain — the analytical signature of a company whose highest dimension (market re-opening, real Texas hyperscale heat constraint) is structurally undermined by its lowest (single-miracle architecture, because Akshay Trikha and Erik Meike need the thermodynamic process to work at hyperscale, *and* humid Texas wet-bulb to cooperate, *and* hyperscaler procurement to adopt against Johnson Controls). This is the "science project" archetype I've publicly said I largely avoid, dressed in the clothing of an AI-infrastructure deal.

On the market re-opening ladder, **Anduril** (2022) sits at the top — a 24-month Ukraine catalyst that re-opened defense — and **Lyft** sits at the bottom, a steady-state rideshare market with no recent catalyst. Madrone lands between **Decagon** (2023) and Anduril: AI compute buildout in arid Texas at 31.5% CAGR for liquid cooling (Globe Newswire, May 2026) is a real recent catalyst, and "up to 40% of data center power diverted to cooling" (madrone.cool) is the specific window. It's not Anduril-grade — the catalyst is downstream of the AI step-function, not the step-function itself — but it's nothing like Lyft's steady-state. The catalyst is the strongest thing Madrone has going.

On the revenue velocity ladder, **Harvey** (2023) at "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) is the high rung and any pre-revenue ML pioneer pre-2022 is the low. Madrone sits at the low rung, hard: no revenue, no pilots, no LOIs, no named hyperscaler counterparty in the dossier. NVIDIA Inception is a non-equity partner program, not a commercial commitment. In an AI-adjacent category where "if you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863), the absence here is not pre-seed noise — facility cooling startups can disclose signed pilots even before revenue, and Madrone hasn't.

On the founder-ships ladder, **Perplexity**'s Aravind (2022) shipping between meetings is the top rung and a credentialed-but-no-artifacts pedigree founder is the bottom. Erik Meike at Apple iPhone wireless power is a real hardware-shipping artifact, and Trikha's QuantumScape ML and Berkeley scaling-laws work is legitimate research, but Trikha's GitHub at 18 followers / 25 repos / top repo 1 star (dossier) is a junior shipping footprint, and neither founder has shipped *in HVAC, refrigeration, or facility engineering*. Madrone lands between low and mid — Apple hardware shipping isn't Color Genomics-equivalent domain depth, and the absence of an ex-Trane/Carrier/JCI operator is the gap that would have justified the **Color Genomics** (2015) exception I made for myself.

On index position, **Stripe** (2010) — "an index on e-commerce" — is the top rung and **Lyft** as #2 in a winner-take-all is the bottom. Madrone is closer to Lyft than to Stripe: dew-point/evaporative cooling is a known thermodynamic principle, the dossier flags commoditization risk, and Madrone has to *beat* Johnson Controls, Carrier, and Trane head-on — those incumbents have direct cannibalization incentive against chiller-less approaches, which is why JCI bought Alloy Enterprises in May 2026 rather than building. Madrone isn't in the workflow path every entrant must touch; it's fighting for share. On single-miracle, **Decagon** (2023) — one validated LLM capability, rest is GTM — is the top rung and fusion/novel battery chem is the bottom. Madrone requires the thermodynamic process to clear hyperscaler-grade efficiency *in humid Texas summers* (wet-bulb cap is real), hyperscaler facility-team adoption against incumbents, and modular manufacturing economics — three independent miracles. That's the multi-miracle compound I pass on.

Forecast at 36 months: Madrone runs one pilot at a mid-tier Texas colocation operator, fails to clear hyperscaler-grade procurement, and gets acqui-hired by Johnson Controls, Carrier, or Flex in the $15-60M range — the Alloy Enterprises / JetCool template — well below the $4.6B-style outcomes my portfolio is built on. The mechanism is exactly the high/low signature: a real catalyst pulls in a pilot, but multi-miracle architecture without facility domain expertise caps the upside at strategic acquisition rather than category index position.

The strongest bull case: Madrone is the **Color Genomics** (2015) template — workflow/equipment infrastructure on top of a hard-tech layer (cooling thermodynamics) rather than a chemistry bet, with NVIDIA Inception unlocking GPU-customer warm intros, and JCI's Alloy acquisition proving the M&A floor exists. If Madrone signs one Texas hyperscaler pilot at >5MW with measured 30% efficiency, the strategic value compounds fast. The dismantling: Color worked because *I operated it* and architected away the science risk, and neither Trikha nor Meike has the HVAC/facility credibility a passive check would require — the Anduril exception was "Trae Stephens ex-Palantir + Palmer Luckey ex-Oculus had shipped at scale in the adjacent stack," which is precisely what Madrone lacks. One condition flips me: a signed pilot with a named hyperscaler (Crusoe, Stargate, Meta, Oracle) at >5MW load with third-party-measured 30% power/water reduction in real Texas climate envelopes — at that point the single miracle is isolated and I revisit.
