# TrustAI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

TrustAI is a technically-credentialed team pointed at a non-schlep problem -- Medha Venkatapathy made the USA Physics Team in 2021, and TrustAI's product is a "suggestion layer" sitting on top of HubSpot, Salesforce, and Gmail with Bardeen's $25.3M and Zapier's ~$300M ARR already occupying the room. That pairing -- hacker horsepower on one axis, consensus-comfortable problem on the other -- is what TrustAI actually is, and it is the inverse of the shape I look for.

**Airbnb** (2009) is the rung I keep as the ceiling on animal resourcefulness -- Chesky and Gebbia sold over 1,000 cereal boxes at $40 each because no one would fund them, and that stunt, not the deck, is what flipped my rejection. TrustAI's dossier has no analogous artifact. Hannah Chung's public surface is 715 X followers and an MIT Washington DC intern posting from 2025; the company's demand funnel is a Calendly demo booker with no pricing page, no waitlist number, no Product Hunt launch, no Chrome Web Store listing. The "no installation required" claim on auto.trytrust.ai is the one line in the dossier that could have been the demo -- a working artifact doing the thing everyone assumes requires an extension -- but the mechanism is not documented and no third party has verified it. Animals leave tracks; TrustAI's tracks in the dossier are a Calendly link.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the rung I use for the schlep test in reverse -- Brian Armstrong wanted the bitcoin compliance grind that everyone in his batch flinched from, and the grind became the moat. TrustAI is running the opposite play. Cross-tool automation suggestions on HubSpot and Salesforce is where Bardeen ($25.3M raised, Dropbox and HubSpot Ventures on the cap table), Zapier (8,000+ integrations), Magical (~$60M+), Lindy, and n8n are all camped. That is not schlep blindness; that is where the light is brightest. TrustAI's dossier does not name a single unpleasant thing the founders have committed to doing that the competition will not -- no manual onboarding grind, no regulatory clause, no offline ops. The problem is pure software with a suggestion-generation twist, and suggestion generation is exactly the layer Salesforce Flow and Bardeen can bolt on with their existing distribution.

Dimension four -- hacker genealogy -- is the one place TrustAI actually pushes upward, via counterfactual. Medha's USA Physics Team 2021 credential and MIT Lincoln Laboratory time are the kind of raw-signal markers that got me interested in Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010). But there is no public GitHub for either founder, no open-source contribution, no hack demo, no Hacker News post. For a company whose entire promise rests on cross-tool context capture without installation, the absence of any shipped artifact from the technical cofounder is louder than the physics olympiad. To flip this dimension from mid-low to high, I would need one of them to have shipped something -- a browser-agent side project, a repo, anything with commits.

Founder-pain fit is the dimension I read via 18-month mechanism. TrustAI's product suggests automations to knowledge workers on HubSpot and Salesforce; Hannah Chung's most recent professional context is a summer DC internship in 2025, and Medha's is Lincoln Lab and MIT coursework. Neither has sat inside a sales org watching reps copy-paste between Salesforce and Slack -- the **Dropbox** (2007) USB-stick pattern requires the founder to have personally suffered the workflow. Without that lived tissue, the suggestion engine will optimize for what the founders imagine sales reps do rather than what they actually do, and every subsequent product decision compounds that gap. Eighteen months of that compounding is what separates an organic product from a demo that impresses the YC batch and then flatlines.

Team configuration I judge against a single anchor. **Stripe** (2010) was two brothers who had built **Auctomatic** (2007) together -- long working relationship, complementary technical coverage, mutual trust priced in. TrustAI is Hannah and Medha, both MIT-affiliated, and the dossier surfaces no shared prior project, no roommate history, no co-authored artifact. Team size is listed as 3 but only two founders are named. Neutral placement -- the YC interview would surface whether they actually work like the Collisons or like two people who paired up for the application.

TrustAI's 36-month forecast: acquihired by Bardeen or HubSpot Ventures' portfolio for under $15M inside 30 months, or dead by month 24. The signature drives it -- technically credentialed founders on a non-schlep problem with named competitors already funded and distributed hit the same ceiling **Kiko** (2005) hit, minus the eBay auction. The Medha physics-team horsepower is exactly the kind of credential that makes a strategic like HubSpot write a small check to bring the team in-house rather than compete for their attention.

The strongest case against my pass is the "no installation required" claim. If TrustAI has actually built a browser-side context capture layer that works across HubSpot, Salesforce, Gmail, Slack, and Google Docs without an extension or per-app OAuth, that is a genuine technical artifact and would make Medha's dossier retroactively look like Aaron Swartz's RSS work at **Reddit** (2005) -- a hacker who shipped something specifically hard. The bull mechanism is that Bardeen's $25.3M is a Chrome extension, and if TrustAI has genuinely routed around the extension model, incumbent substitution goes the other direction. Dossier evidence that wins: the "mechanism not documented publicly" line, the absent GitHub, the Calendly-only funnel. Founders who have shipped a novel browser-agent architecture put a demo video on Hacker News; TrustAI has not. The condition that flips my verdict: a working public demo of the no-install cross-tool capture, plus three named paying design partners inside six months. Pass, revisitable.
