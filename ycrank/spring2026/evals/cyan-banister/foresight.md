# Foresight -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Eytan Rozenblum and Antoine Bertrand are running their second pivot together -- PrompTessor in 2023, then Fluentive AI, now Foresight as "on-demand consumer insights" -- and the dossier cannot point to a single hour either of them spent inside a Nestle insights team or behind a Caudalie packaging review. That is the signature of Foresight in my book: a generalist AI-tooling duo wrapping a synthetic-consumer SaaS around a category that already has Simile at $100M Series A (Feb 2026, Index Ventures) and Aaru at a $1B headline (Dec 2025, Redpoint) sucking the oxygen out. The product is a B2B abstraction sold into a buyer Foresight has never been, in a market that is the loudest room in AI right now. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game on offer here is "follow Karpathy and Fei-Fei Li into the obvious lane."

Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus out of his parents' garage before Anduril -- the founder *was* the headset before he was the company. Eytan Rozenblum's prior artifact is PrompTessor, a reverse-prompt tool, and his own description of 2023 as "likely the worst year of my life" with a barely-raised pivot is the tell. That is grit, and grit is real, but it is not the same as having stood in a Sephora endcap meeting watching a brand manager kill a launch over a 3-cent COGS surprise. Foresight's pitch sells conjoint analysis and price elasticity simulation -- two of the most epistemically loaded outputs in CPG research -- and neither founder has, in any retrievable public record, run a single real conjoint study against a real panel. Antoine Bertrand's LinkedIn lists "Co-Founder @ FluentiveAI" and nothing else legible. The biography that should put Foresight in front of this problem first is missing.

The Pass Example I keep in my back pocket is the consensus YC AI-agent darling -- two ex-FAANG PMs, $200B TAM slide, six funds chasing, moat is the model -- and Foresight rhymes with it on the dimension that matters most to me. The category isn't unsexy. The category is the *most* sexy AI vertical of Q1 2026: Simile raised $100M four months ago, Aaru raised a $1B headline two months before that, and the Ditto market map names eight active entrants in the same synthetic-research lane. That is the inverse of "find them before it becomes consensus." Foresight is YC P26 launching into a room where the institutional money has already picked its horses and the pricing in the next round will reflect it.

Foresight's CPG-vertical wedge -- copy, conjoint, packaging, price elasticity packaged as test types -- is the one thing that could compound differently than Simile's horizontal digital-twin platform over 18 months. Quantilope at $40M raised serves 300+ CPG enterprise clients including Nestle and Estee Lauder, so the buyer exists and the budget exists. The mechanism that would matter is calibration data: every Caudalie test result that comes back from a real launch is a row in a feedback loop nobody else has. But Foresight has one named customer running 15 tests/week, and Simile/Aaru can hire a CPG vertical lead with $5M of their war chest tomorrow. The wedge only compounds if Foresight gets to five named enterprise logos before Q4 2026, and the dossier shows no signed pipeline.

Two founders, no documented tiebreaker, two prior pivots together -- Foresight isn't HQ Trivia's Yusupov/Kroll powderkeg, but it isn't a single-CEO SpaceX either. What would have to be true to flip this to an Invest tier on dimension three is one line in the dossier I do not see: a named CEO with majority operating authority. The Fluentive AI -> Foresight pivot suggests the partnership survives stress, which I weight as a soft positive, but soft positives don't earn rebuttal weight against a category this consensus.

The HandUp exception is the only one I check seriously here -- did Eytan's "worst year of my life" 2023 pivot pattern-match to my own Tucson-to-IronPort arc strongly enough to override commercial defensibility? It doesn't. HandUp was personal mission alignment with homelessness; Foresight is two founders who tried a prompt tool, tried Fluentive, and are trying synthetic consumers because the synthetic-consumer category is what raised in late 2025. That is the iteration loop of a generalist AI team, not the gravitational pull of a lived problem.

My 36-month forecast on Foresight: dead or acqui-hired below $15M by Quantilope or YouGov (which already bought Yabble), never crosses $3M ARR, outspent by Simile's $100M and Aaru's $50M into enterprise CPG accounts where Quantilope already owns the relationship. The mechanism is straightforward -- the technical question of synthetic-prediction validity at conjoint and price-elasticity granularity is unresolved for the *whole category*, and the entrant with the smallest validation budget loses that resolution race.

The bull case stings, so I will name it. HQ Trivia overrode my co-founder governance rule because the tactile pull of the product was undeniable when I played it -- and Caudalie's 15 tests/week at "25x lower cost per insight" is the closest thing in this dossier to a workflow-addiction signal. If that number is real and replicable, Foresight's vertical packaging could become the Figma-of-CPG-insights while Simile and Aaru chase horizontal digital-twin glamour. But Caudalie is one logo, the 25x claim has no published accuracy benchmark behind it, and the moat narrative depends on calibration data Foresight cannot accumulate faster than a $100M-funded competitor running parallel pilots. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: five named non-beauty CPG enterprise logos under contract by Q1 2027 with a published validation study against real-panel outcomes. Absent that, Foresight is the synthetic-consumer entrant whose name collides with five other "Foresights" in search results, and that is not a moat. Pass, with one foot in love for the grit of a second pivot.
