# Instance -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Instance is a five-month-old pivot wearing a YC S26 badge: Lucy Cai and Claire Mao were publicly "building Instance, AI for databases" on LinkedIn in January 2026 and are now selling a "physics-aware quality layer" for synthetic video in June 2026, and the analytical signature that produces is a team with the highest possible academic credential density (MIT MEng AI, MIT Lincoln Lab 2D-to-3D point clouds, MIT Math+CS) paired with the lowest possible owned-distribution surface (no Twitter for either founder, no GitHub, no published essays, no Product Hunt, a landing page that says "working on world models"). That high-low pairing is what Instance actually *is*: a research-credentialed academic wrapper on top of four already-open arxiv benchmarks — T2VPhysBench, WorldModelBench, VideoPhy-2, LikePhys — with no community, no conviction artifact, and no structural reason the round in front of me is anything other than the modal AI-data S26 SAFE.

Solana at $0.05 is the high rung for round mis-pricing because every generalist had quit crypto in 2018-2019 and the entry price compensated for the illegibility; Instance lives nowhere near that rung. The dossier discloses no round size, no valuation, no lead, and no pass-list — and silence is exactly what my Value Seed essay tells me to punish, because at a Summer 2026 YC batch the absence of "Sequoia and a16z passed" almost always resolves to "Sequoia and a16z are quietly bidding." The category — synthetic video evaluation for world-model training — sits inside the single hottest AI-data sub-sleeve of 2026 (NVIDIA Cosmos January 2025 launch, Encord's $60M Series C, Surge raising at $15B), which is the structural opposite of the abandoned-category discount that made the Solana check work. There is no dossier evidence Instance is the bid no one else wants; the prior is the bid everyone wants at the price I won't pay.

The pass example in my own file — "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, four tier-1 VCs already in" — is the low rung for founder pre-pitch conviction, and Instance fails the same filter through a slightly different door: not a sanitized agent pitch, but a five-month-old pivot with zero public writing by either founder on world models, physics violations, or synthetic data. The "Writing is thinking" test (Antoine Buteau, ~2023) is binary for me — I read the founder's stuff before the meeting, and there is no stuff. Lucy Cai's LinkedIn headline still says "AI for databases" as of the dossier fetch; that is not a missionary on a long arc, that is a team that ran a text-to-SQL experiment, watched it fail to land, and reached for the most fundable adjacent narrative the partners would underwrite. The MIT Lincoln Lab point-cloud work is genuine domain adjacency, but adjacency is not obsession, and a four-month-old thesis pivot is not "three-plus years on the problem before the round."

Founder-as-brand: counterfactual. For Instance to flip from a 3/15 to anything that registers on the Slow Creator Fund frame, I would need to see Lucy Cai running a 10K+ subscriber Substack on world-model evaluation methodology, or Claire Mao building a Discord of physical-AI data engineers, or either of them shipping an open-source eval harness that the NVIDIA Cosmos team cites. None of those exist. The dossier confirms LinkedIn and Twitter handles are registered with un-retrievable follower counts, which in 2026 is the polite way of saying "below the threshold worth disclosing." There is no community pre-existing the company by two years; there is no founder media channel; there is no organic acquisition surface. This dimension is not load-bearing for Instance, and the company will have to be sold to the NVIDIA / Genie / Veo procurement org through cold outbound, which is the most expensive distribution money buys.

Capital structure compounds the problem over the next 18 months in a specific way: Instance is a usage-based enterprise-data-infra play with a buyer set the dossier itself describes as "a narrow group of frontier video-world-model labs," meaning the company needs Scale-AI-shaped GTM (named-account enterprise sales) on a YC-shaped balance sheet (two non-commercial cofounders, no sales hire). That trajectory requires four-to-five additional rounds before default-alive, each priced against Encord's $110M raised and Surge's $15B mark — comps Instance cannot grow into without consuming the cap table. There is no creative instrument on the table: no revenue share against model-lab evaluation contracts, no royalty on physics-violation datasets, nothing that would let me underwrite Instance the way I underwrote Teamshares or Mogilko. It is a standard SAFE on a standard SaaS shape that needs venture-Series-B-class capital to clear the regatta.

The Joe Lonsdale podcast line — "AI is just more internet, it's not disruptive, crypto is fundamentally disruptive" — is precisely the sleeve-allocation rule that puts Instance in the de-rate bucket, and the dossier compounds it: four academic benchmarks already publish what Instance is trying to sell, Meta-owned Scale AI has the workforce to add a physics module in a quarter, and Encord is already inside Toyota Woven and Skydio with broader product surface. Instance is not pre-consensus crypto with on-chain economic activity; it is the third commercial layer on top of a commoditizing academic substrate inside the most consensus AI capex story of the cycle.

My 36-month forecast: Instance is acqui-hired by Encord, Surge, or a frontier model lab (NVIDIA, Google DeepMind, or a Cosmos-adjacent team) for sub-$25M in talent-and-IP terms before reaching $2M ARR, or pivots a third time into a different AI-data niche by Q4 2027. The mechanism is the high-low signature: MIT-grade credentials are exactly the asset acquirers buy at the seed-acqui-hire price band, and the absence of owned distribution plus the pivot velocity makes standalone scale unlikely once Encord ships its physics module and the open-source benchmarks tighten.

The strongest case against me is that Lucy Cai's Lincoln Lab work on ML-driven 2D-to-3D vision is the rarest founder-market fit on the YC S26 list for this specific problem, and if NVIDIA Cosmos or a Genie/Veo successor formally adopts a third-party physics-eval standard the way ML adopted MLPerf, Instance becomes the de-facto benchmark and the academic papers become the citation, not the substitute. That bull case stings because it's how Sublime Security looked at seed before the security category re-rated. It loses to two dossier facts: (1) model labs almost always build evaluation in-house when the eval is also their training signal, because eval-as-data is too valuable to outsource, and (2) Instance pivoted off text-to-SQL in January 2026 and the YC narrative dropped onto the world-model thesis without a single piece of founder long-form writing defending why physics-violation detection at commercial reliability is solvable when LikePhys (October 2025) frames it as open research. I flip to invest if Lucy Cai publishes a credible physics-eval benchmark paper or open-source harness that NVIDIA Cosmos or a comparable lab cites by name within twelve months — that single artifact converts the pivot into conviction. Until then, pass.
