# Netter -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Netter is a horizontal data-pipes-and-dashboards tool for mid-market European retailers and nursing homes — its highest-percentage dimension is the affordability of data engineering for companies that can't hire data engineers, and its lowest is consequence magnitude. That pairing tells me what I need to know: if Netter fully works, a Carrefour regional manager builds a dashboard in four hours instead of waiting six weeks for a ticket. That is real, and it is not the kind of outcome I write checks for.

Consequence magnitude is where Netter loses me before any other analysis. Put OpenAI at the top of this ladder — a $50M check that required an apology letter to LPs because the consequence of AGI is civilizational. Put DoorDash in the middle — no technical secret, but transformed food access for hundreds of millions, which is why Keith led that round. At the bottom is the YC pattern I keep passing on: AI copilots for accountants and recruiters that automate 40% of a workflow and produce a moderately profitable SaaS. Netter sits one rung above that floor, pinned there by the dossier's own examples — a nursing home group recovering "~10% dormant receivables," a logistics operator reducing empty trucks, a retailer activating loyalty data. These are efficiency gains for the income statement of mid-market businesses across France, Spain, and the UK. Useful. Substitutable. Not the world I get up in the morning to fund.

Rocket Lab anchors my non-linear ladder — the Rutherford engine, electric turbopumps, 3D-printed combustion chambers, a step-function in $/kg-to-orbit. DoorDash is the mid rung, a Known Exception where I overrode the technical-novelty requirement because Tony Xu had planetary consequence on the table. The bottom rung is the LLM wrapper. Netter is a wrapper with packaging — 120+ connectors, natural-language-to-pipeline, an "ontology" layer — sitting in a market where Airbyte already advertises 550+ connectors as open source and Fivetran took $565M to dominate managed ELT. Netter's CTO Amin Hamzaoui has the Mines Paris + ENS Ulm pedigree to build this competently, but competent integration of commodity LLM-to-SQL, commodity connectors, and a chat layer is not a Rutherford engine. It is a 2x improvement in time-to-dashboard, not a 10x discontinuity.

Mach Industries' 19-year-old MIT-dropout founder anchors my learning-machine ladder — the naive outsider taking on Lockheed Martin because he didn't know what couldn't be done. Sun's early team is the mid rung, technically credible but inside the paradigm. The low rung is the founder reading from a deck unchanged from six months ago. Netter's CTO Hamzaoui has elite French academic pedigree, which is signal of raw horsepower, but the dossier surfaces zero evidence of pivots, surprises, or rate-of-change over the YC batch — and Samuel Ladroue's pre-Netter commercial track record could not be verified, with conflicting "Almanax" snippets I would not underwrite on. Defaulting toward the floor of the absent-evidence band.

Hermeus' Mach 5 passenger aircraft anchors improbable ambition — a goal that provokes incredulity from every aerospace expert in the room. Impossible Foods is the mid rung — improbable but conceivable. The low rung is "AI for vertical X." Netter's tagline is "Data Science Autopilot for the real economy," which is a competent product description, not a future that makes industry experts laugh. No one says fewer empty trucks at a French logistics operator can't be done. On affordability, Curai Health anchors the high rung — AI primary care at near-zero marginal cost — and the low rung is enterprise SaaS capturing value through premium positioning. Netter has a real but bounded affordability claim: data engineering capability accessible to mid-market companies that cannot pay $300K for a data team. Genuine, but it is the affordability of an operating expense for businesses, not the affordability of healthcare or energy for billions.

Forecast for Netter over 36 months: it survives, reaches roughly $3M–$15M ARR by 2029 selling to French and Iberian retailers and logistics operators, and is acquired by Snowflake, Databricks, or Fivetran for under $300M as a mid-market wedge into the EU. The mechanism is direct: the consequence ceiling caps the outcome at "useful integration product," and the connector-plus-LLM category is consolidating fast enough that a 4-person Paris team with no novel technical moat is more valuable as a talent-and-logos tuck-in than as an independent path to $1B revenue.

The opposing case worth taking seriously is the DoorDash override — execution-heavy mid-market plays that I almost passed on but which compounded because the founder was a learning machine attacking a planetary problem with high velocity. Netter does have Carrefour, Tesco, and Auchan on the homepage, and EU enterprise distribution at pre-seed is rare. If Ladroue is the Tony Xu of European mid-market data and these are paid six-figure contracts rather than logo-grade pilots, the bear case weakens. The dossier kills the override for me on two facts: there is no evidence the named logos are paid versus POC, and the consequence ceiling does not approach DoorDash's — automating a regional retailer's loyalty analytics is not transforming food access for half a billion people. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed, disclosed annual contract above $500K ACV with one of the named logos, paired with evidence the team has narrowed from five verticals to one ontology-deep wedge. Until then, this is the wrong shape of bet for my fund and my own money.
