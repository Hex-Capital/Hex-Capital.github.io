# screenpipe -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Louis Beaumont shipped screenpipe to 19,000 GitHub stars and a claimed 250,000 users in Rust, source-available, cross-OS, before writing YC's application — and he did it alone. That pairing is screenpipe's signature: high on the ship-before-you-pitch axis and the schlep axis, low on the co-founder axis. The company reads as a lone hacker doing unpleasant work at a moment when the cloud incumbents just embarrassed themselves. It is the profile I have historically funded and then quietly worried about in bed the same night.

Drew Houston's Hacker News demo for **Dropbox** (2007) is the reference shape here, and screenpipe belongs on the same axis — an artifact on the internet, in the wild, that got users to arrive before the check. Houston's demo was a video; Beaumont's demo is 19k stars, a Rust codebase, and enterprise logos (Microsoft, NVIDIA, Shopify, Datadog, Stanford) listed on screenpipe.com. The divergence from the HIGH rung is that Houston's artifact was ambient — sync-your-files, universally legible; screenpipe's 250k-user claim comes from a LinkedIn snippet, not a paid-conversion cohort, and the recent license flip from open-source to source-available (Screenpipe license-update blog) tells me part of that top-of-funnel is being deliberately taxed to protect the business. Beaumont is animal enough — the prior Mediar company doing SAP screen-automation is not a hobby project, it's a pattern — but the dossier does not contain a single cereal-box moment I can point at.

The failure mode I associate with solo founders is not that they can't ship — Beaumont just did — it's that they run out of the specific kind of stubbornness a co-founder replenishes. The **Pass Example** in my head is the two-MBA AI-wrapper team, but screenpipe's opposite risk is more instructive: a lone hacker shipping a product that spans macOS, Windows, Linux, 48+ integrations, an on-device PII model, and a $150/seat enterprise motion with SSO/SAML and MDM. Matt Diakonov was Beaumont's Mediar co-founder in 2024 and is not on screenpipe's team of 6 per the YC page. That is the datum I keep circling. Whatever happened between Mediar and screenpipe subtracted a co-founder. "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) was direct on this and I do not get to pretend I wasn't.

Louis Beaumont's prior company Mediar (Founders Inc portfolio) built automated data entry for legacy software via screen input — screenpipe is Mediar's thesis rewritten as a horizontal memory-and-automation layer. That is organic in the strict sense from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012): he did not spot a market, he stayed in the same problem two companies in a row. The **Stripe** (2010) parallel is fair — the Collisons hacked commerce at **Auctomatic** (2007) and then hacked commerce again — but Auctomatic sold; Mediar's status is unclear from the dossier, and the founder returning to the same problem after an unresolved first attempt is a slightly different animal than the Collisons returning after a $5M exit.

Screenpipe's schlep surface compounds well over 18 months in a way I have seen before. Always-on screen and audio capture with on-device PII scrubbing, sold into Fortune 500s that require SSO/SAML, MDM, and legal review at the $150/seat tier — that is the **Coinbase** (2012) compliance-as-moat mechanism transposed onto workplace surveillance. Microsoft Recall's repeated security-researcher disclosures one year post-launch (GeekWire, 2026) and Meta disabling the Rewind desktop app on December 19, 2025 (GeekWire, 2026) are exactly the kind of incumbent flinch that widens a schlep moat. The 48+ integrations screenpipe ships — Slack, Gmail, Linear, Zoom, Salesforce, HubSpot — are individually boring and collectively the moat. Nobody who wanted a fun problem picked this.

For screenpipe to flip a tier on hacker genealogy, the dossier would need one of two things: a named pre-screenpipe open-source project of Beaumont's with independent traction, or a technical co-founder with a shipped compiler / kernel / infra artifact. The 479 GitHub followers on @louis030195 and the Rust codebase get screenpipe most of the way, but the hacker-tribal markers I over-index on — Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp win before **Stripe** (2010), Aaron Swartz's RSS spec before **Reddit** (2005) — are absent from the Beaumont file. What is present is a person who wrote a lot of Rust that a lot of people starred, which is not nothing and is not Swartz.

Thirty-six-month forecast: screenpipe reaches $4-10M ARR on the enterprise SSO tier, gets an acquisition offer from Notion, Atlassian, or Datadog in the $80-200M range, and Beaumont takes it — because the solo-founder ceiling meets a Microsoft Recall that is by then either fixed or federally mandated, and because the source-available license flip was already a signal that Beaumont is optimizing for the business, not the movement. This is a $50M-good outcome, not a **Stripe** (2010) outcome, and the mechanism producing that ceiling is the missing co-founder, not the market.

The bull case that stings: **Airbnb** (2009) is my standing precedent that founder determination overrides my idea-level and configuration-level skepticism, and Beaumont has already done the animal thing — 19k stars, 250k users, Rust, prior Mediar, and a license change that suggests he watched Rewind die on December 19, 2025 and decided he would not. If enterprise privacy anxiety compounds and screenpipe becomes the default "AI computer memory" layer that GPT-native workflows plug into, the Pipes marketplace is a real network effect and the $150/seat tier scales into nine figures. The dossier evidence that keeps me from fully underwriting that bull case is the absence of a named enterprise contract — the Fortune 500 logos are listed as "users," not customers — and the co-founder gap. The verifiable condition that would flip me from Invest-low to Strong Invest: Beaumont recruits a technical co-founder with a shipped systems artifact within six months and screenpipe closes a named Fortune 500 enterprise contract at the $150/seat tier before the end of S26.

I write the check, small, and I tell Louis in the first meeting to find his second person.
