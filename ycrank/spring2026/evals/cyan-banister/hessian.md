# Hessian -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Hessian is a clean-governance, deeply-credentialed engineering team -- Bao Nguyen as CEO, James Smith as COO, Tom Watson as CTO, all Durham CS with Cambridge scholarships and Bloomberg/GoCardless/DRW infra ship records -- pointing all three barrels at the single most consensus YC category of Spring 2026. That pairing is the signature: highest dimension is the structural one (one unambiguous CEO with two titled lieutenants), lowest is "magically weird," because forward-deployed AI agents for back-office ops is exactly the lane Distyl is running at $1.8B and Sierra at $10B. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Hessian seems to be playing is the one everyone on Sand Hill is already playing too.

On the founder-lived-experience axis, the high rung in my book is Palmer Luckey shipping Oculus from a garage before pivoting Anduril into defense -- the biography put him on the well pad, so to speak, before the company existed. Hessian's founders shipped real infrastructure -- Tom Watson migrated GoCardless's core payments DB with zero downtime on a system processing over £1B on peak days, James Smith spent five years on Bloomberg's billion-observation data platform, Bao co-founded Zettafleet for decentralized LLM training out of stealth in February 2026 -- and that is not nothing; that is more lived-infrastructure credibility than most pre-seed AI agent decks I see. But none of the Hessian three has ever *run* the mid-market invoice reconciliation or month-end close that the company now proposes to forward-deploy into. They built the rails; they did not sit at the deli counter. That is the gap between Luckey and a very capable Bloomberg SWE.

HQ Trivia's low rung on governance is the productive failure I keep tattooed -- Yusupov and Kroll could not name the tiebreaker, and the feud was already audible at the cap-table conversation. Hessian inverts that pattern cleanly: Bao Nguyen is explicitly titled Co-Founder & CEO, James is COO, Tom is CTO, and the three share a Durham undergraduate cohort plus overlapping Bloomberg time. That is a co-founder set that has already negotiated who answers the phone at 2 a.m. without me having to prosecute it across the table. This is the one dimension where Hessian outscores most pre-seed I see.

Mechanism on "magically weird": the AI-agent-platform category has compounded from contrarian in 2023 to crowded in 2024 to capitalized-to-the-gills in 2025. Decagon is at $35M ARR with $250M Series D, Sierra is at $104M ARR and $10B, Cresta is $52M ARR, Distyl AI is running the exact forward-deployed-engineers-plus-runtime pattern Hessian describes -- with $207M raised. Garry Tan is the YC partner. Twelve months from now this dimension does not get *more* weird; the moat compounds for whoever already has Palantir-FDE-scale customers, not for the three-person team launching into the same wedge.

Counterfactual on capital-intensity/unsexy: for Hessian to flip from a 3-5 rung to a Crusoe-tier rung on this axis, the dossier would need to show a regulated vertical that nobody else will touch -- claims adjudication in workers' comp, FDA-regulated lab ops, defense procurement reconciliation, something where the moat *is* the regulatory pain. Hessian's stated scope -- "sales proposals, invoice reconciliation, month-end close, internal-system sync" -- is the soft middle of horizontal SaaS. The single named customer is Fazeshift, a fellow YC company. Software-only, horizontal, YC-network distribution; the absence of a frontier vertical is itself the signal.

Niantic is the single-anchor parallel I want to draw on physical verifiability and it does not hold. I sourced Niantic because I was already playing Ingress in 2012; I sourced Flock from a Four Seasons Wi-Fi name; I sourced Postmates by watching the courier walk up. Hessian's product surface is a Google Calendar demo link and a dashboard widget claiming 15 workflow runs in the last 24 hours at 7% failure rate and 14.2s P95 latency. A normal human cannot install Hessian, photograph Hessian, or hand Hessian to a friend at the dinner table. The dashboard telemetry is founder-controlled and unverifiable.

My 36-month forecast for Hessian: acqui-hired into Distyl, Glean, or a Series-B AI-platform consolidator for $20-60M, or quietly wound down at sub-$3M ARR by mid-2028 -- not a venture outcome at this competitive cap stack, regardless of how well Tom Watson can migrate a payments database. The mechanism is straightforward: Distyl has a two-year head start and 400x the capital on the identical FDE-plus-platform thesis, Sierra and Decagon own the self-serve flank with $10B and $1.5B war chests, and a three-person London-cohort team cannot out-distribute four of them simultaneously inside the YC network.

The strongest objection to that forecast is the DeepMind exception: Hassabis was pedigreed too, and the polymath cross-domain compounding overrode my anti-credential prior. Could Bao's Zettafleet ML-systems trajectory plus Tom's payments-grade reliability engineering plus James's entity-disambiguation pipelines compose into a polymath agent-runtime that the spreadsheet-SaaS incumbents structurally cannot ship? It stings because the founder substrate is real -- this is not two ex-FAANG PMs with a Figma deck. But the DeepMind override required cross-*discipline* compounding -- neuroscience, chess, game design, RL -- not three flavors of data infrastructure pointed at the same wedge. Hessian's polymath claim collapses to "three good infra engineers," and good infra engineers are not a moat in the agent layer. The condition that would flip my verdict: two named non-YC Fortune 500 logos disclosed at six-figure ACV within twelve months of YC Demo Day. Until then, this is a pass, with one foot in love for the founders.
