# Foresight -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Foresight is a twice-pivoted two-person technical duo selling synthetic-consumer simulations into CPG at the exact moment Simile just took $100M from Index and Aaru took ~$50M at a $1B headline from Redpoint — which means Foresight's strongest signal (Eytan Rozenblum's persistence across PrompTessor → Fluentive AI → Foresight, the "turns of the crank" pattern I respect) is paired with its weakest (no owned distribution, no public writing, no founder-brand to substitute for a price I cannot get on this round). That high/low pairing tells me what kind of company Foresight is: a competent execution team running a third tack into a category that the market has already declared consensus and priced four-to-five orders of magnitude above where I can underwrite.

Solana at $0.05 in 2018 is the high rung of my price-discipline ladder — I bought a category most generalists had quit, and the alpha came entirely from being the only credible bid. Foresight sits at the opposite end of that ladder, much closer to the canonical 2026 YC AI-agent pass I described in the pitch template: Aaru's December 2025 Series A at a $1B headline (TechCrunch) and Simile's February 2026 $100M (AI2.work) didn't just validate the category — they consensus-priced it, and every YC seed in synthetic-consumers now sells into a buyer pool that has already been pitched by two well-capitalized direct competitors and Quantilope's 300+ enterprise CPG accounts. The dossier discloses no price for Foresight's round, no tier-1 pass list, no structural ambiguity — silence on round dynamics in a category this hot is the version of consensus I punish hardest.

The lowest rung of my founder-brand ladder is the pass-example founder with zero pre-funding public writing and no community footprint, and Foresight lands on that rung cleanly: no verified Twitter for Eytan Rozenblum, no Twitter for Antoine Bertrand, LinkedIn follower counts unretrievable, no GitHub stars, no podcast, no newsletter, no Discord, no opinionated long-form on synthetic research that I can read before the meeting. The failure mechanism this dimension exposes for Foresight is not vanity — it's that in a category where Simile can outspend you 200:1 on enterprise sales motion, the only durable counter is owned distribution to the insights buyer that doesn't cost CAC, and Foresight has Caudalie-as-testimonial instead of a founder who CPG insights leads already follow.

Capital structure compounds against Foresight over the next 18 months on a mechanism basis: a standard YC SAFE into a category where the median Series A is now $50-100M means Foresight will need to raise three more tacks just to reach competitive parity on sales headcount with Simile, and each tack is underwritten against the same Caudalie case study unless enterprise logos compound fast. The Slow "venture moment" question — can this be default-alive in 24 months — answers itself: no, because the competitive set has already decided this is a capital-intensive land grab. Foresight's only escape is a structure other funds won't write (revenue share against test volume, or a closed proprietary panel partnership), and nothing in the dossier suggests Foresight is shopping that instrument.

Eytan Rozenblum's prior YC cycle — solo-founder PrompTessor in 2023, self-described as "likely the worst year of my life," then Fluentive AI with Antoine Bertrand, now Foresight — is the dossier's most interesting fact and the one that prevents me from going lower on conviction. The counterfactual that would flip founder-conviction a full tier is opinionated public writing by Eytan on why synthetic consumer prediction is non-obvious at the conjoint/price-elasticity granularity — the technical question the entire category has not answered — and a 3+ year prior obsession with consumer insights specifically rather than three sequential AI wrappers. The dossier shows persistence across pivots but not category obsession; Eytan's missionary arc, if it exists, is for AI tooling broadly, not for the consumer-insights buyer Foresight now serves.

Foresight's category placement is the cleanest read in the file: synthetic consumer research is not pre-consensus crypto, not DTC consumer with brand margin, not a Wizard-of-Oz services-to-software pivot, not employee-equity rollup, not creator-economy infrastructure. It is LLM-on-top-of-vertical-SaaS dressed as market research — the exact "AI is just more internet" exposure I told Joe Lonsdale I would not underwrite at seed pricing. Against my Solana / Aleo / Lightning Labs sleeve, Foresight is the inverse trade: consensus category, commodity primitive, well-funded competitors, no protocol-level economic activity to underwrite.

My 36-month forecast for Foresight: by Q2 2029, Foresight is either dead, pivoted away from synthetic consumer research into a horizontal AI-research-tooling adjacency, or acqui-hired by Quantilope, YouGov, or Toluna at sub-$15M with $0-2M ARR, because the high/low signature — pivot-persistent founders without owned distribution in a consensus-priced AI category — produces exactly this outcome when the category leader (Simile or Aaru) takes the enterprise share that Caudalie-class references cannot defend.

The strongest bull case against my pass is Ro: I overrode capital-efficiency on Ro because 50-state prescription rails were a genuine venture moment where only VC capital could clear the licensing schleps, and someone could argue Foresight's CPG-vertical packaging (copy, conjoint, price elasticity, brand lift as productized test types versus Simile's horizontal twin platform) is the same kind of regulatory-adjacent specialization that justifies capital intensity. That case stings — vertical packaging into CPG insights teams is a real wedge, and Caudalie at 15 tests/week is the kind of repeat-usage signal pre-seed rarely produces. But the dismantling is in the dossier: Ro's moat was statutory and unforgeable, Foresight's moat is prompt engineering plus a single named logo, and Quantilope already sits inside Estee Lauder, Nestle, and Kraft Heinz with a hybrid AI layer. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Foresight discloses a non-standard financing structure (revenue share against customer test volume, or a closed proprietary outcome-validation dataset partnership with 3+ named CPG enterprises) and prices the round below YC P26 batch median — then I'll take the meeting.
