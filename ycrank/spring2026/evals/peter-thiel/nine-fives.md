# Nine Fives -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Noah Levy is the signature. Nine Fives is a credentialed-operator re-up from a Founders Fund portfolio company — Levy was the responsible engineer for the Falcon 9 Radar Altimeter, the Dragon 2 S-Band Transceiver, and Starship GPS/S-Band radios — pointed at a $3.99B fragmented market where the top five incumbents hold only ~55% combined and no one clears 18%. The dimensional signature is a SpaceX-pedigree founder with a specific named incumbent blind spot ("January 1997 revision of the USB Human Interface Device specification") attacking a category whose hardware layer is visibly commoditizing under him. That is the analytical shape: high on lineage and on the named secret, low on monopoly trajectory.

**Affirm** (2014) is the right ladder anchor on founder lineage — Levchin was the PayPal Mafia re-up, the operator Thiel had already underwritten once. Nine Fives is the same mechanism transposed: SpaceX is a 2008 Founders Fund position, and Levy spent 2016–2023 there shipping flight-qualified RF radios at production scale, with six "kickass awards" as a revealed-preference signal from inside the company. Where Nine Fives diverges from Affirm is that Levchin had already founded and exited PayPal; Levy is a Senior IC, not a prior founder. That is the credentialed-outsider variant — closer to Palmer Luckey's profile than Levchin's — and it is the strongest single signal in the dossier. The Andrew Kurtz addition (MIT Aerospace, Aurora Flight Sciences staff ME) is competent but not load-bearing; Levy is the bet.

**Lyft** (2010) is the cautionary anchor on monopoly trajectory, and Nine Fives sits below it. Lyft was a duopoly with stable driver-side network effects in a category that would clearly produce two winners taking everything; the override was power-law positioning in a winner-takes-most mobility wave. Nine Fives has neither the duopoly structure nor the network effects. DS Instruments already ships a 6 GHz 90 dB programmable attenuator at $649–$749 with USB/Ethernet control; Mini-Circuits has more than 400 attenuator SKUs reaching 67 GHz. The hardware is replicable, and Keysight, Rohde & Schwarz, and Emerson-owned NI collectively hold ~55% of revenue plus the TestStand and Connection Expert software layers that Nine Fives explicitly targets for compatibility. There is no named regulated-customer moat — these are commercial cellphone, drone, and satellite manufacturers, not government. The pricing-power hypothesis terminates at "our software is better," which is a feature, not a ten-year moat.

The contrarian secret is more specific than most Spring 2026 YC dossiers I have read. Nine Fives names the architectural failure mode at the level of the bytes — instruments built around a 1997 USB HID spec, drivers shipped as bundled enterprise suites that the incumbents cannot unship without cannibalizing their own software and services revenue. That is the disruption-from-below shape: Keysight and Emerson/NI cannot ship driverless REST-native instruments without detonating the Connection Expert and TestStand revenue lines that justify their multiples. The Straussian read of the dossier is that the real secret is not "driverless RF" but "the LLM skill.md as the new test-automation primitive" — version-controlled rack diagrams compiled into test code by a model. That secret has 18 months of compounding before the incumbents copy the surface; the question is whether Nine Fives uses that window to lock in the API as the default.

On definite plan, Nine Fives compounds over 18 months only if the four shipped SKUs (PD8X, POE-ATTEN-6G, POE-SWITCH-6G, POE-PMETER-6G) extend into a rack-system architecture rather than a SKU catalog. The PoE-plus-REST-plus-SCPI-plus-skill.md commitment is a definite architectural bet, not iteration-mode language; the dossier contains no "pivot to product-market fit" framing. But the roadmap depth visible is one product generation, not the SpaceX-style ten-year arc. This is definite-optimism at the architecture level and indefinite at the company level.

For atoms-heavy moat, the counterfactual that would flip the score: if Nine Fives owned a fab-level or RFIC-level capex barrier — proprietary mmWave silicon, a custom synthesizer, a calibration process requiring a multimillion-dollar anechoic chamber — this would clear 8/10. It does not. The instruments are assembled from commercial parts; DS Instruments demonstrates the capex floor is low. The moat has to be the software layer, which means atoms-heavy is the wrong frame and the bet is a software bet in hardware clothing.

The 36-month forecast: Nine Fives reaches $3–8M ARR selling primarily into the SpaceX-adjacent aerospace and drone hardware network, then is acquired by Keysight or Emerson/NI for $30–80M as a strategic team-and-API tuck-in once the driverless-REST workflow proves itself in design wins. Not a fund-returner; not a failure. The mechanism is the high/low signature directly — the Levy pedigree opens the design-in conversations at SpaceX-network manufacturers, the contrarian secret produces real wedge revenue, and the commoditizable hardware plus incumbent software retaliation caps the terminal value before category dominance.

The bull case that stings: the skill.md pattern is the real prize, not the instruments. If LLM-driven test automation becomes the default workflow for AI-era hardware development — and the timing argument here is genuine, since PoE instruments, REST tooling, and code-generating models all crossed viability in the last 12–24 months — then Nine Fives is positioning to be the GitHub of RF test, with the rack-configuration web app as the lock-in surface. That is a category-defining outcome, not an acqui-hire. The bear response: Keysight and Emerson own the customer relationships and the existing automation frameworks; eighteen months after Nine Fives proves the wedge, Connection Expert ships a REST mode and TestStand ships an LLM adapter, and the lock-in surface never compounds because the incumbents are not structurally locked out of shipping it — they are revenue-locked out, which is a softer constraint than architectural lock-out. Anduril worked because Lockheed cost-plus contracting structurally cannot ship iteratively; Keysight has no such structural prohibition on shipping a driverless SKU. The verdict flips to Invest if Nine Fives lands a named design-in at a top-five drone or satellite manufacturer within twelve months with the NineVue/skill.md layer cited as the reason — that is the verifiable condition.
