# General Instinct -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

General Instinct is a deep specific-knowledge bet — Guanming Wang ex-DeepMind, Bill Jiao on Siemens' first multimodal foundation model — built on a permissioned distribution architecture. That pairing is the signature. The technical edge is real (111ms cold start, 100% of decisions inside a 150ms budget on Jetson Orin NX), the founder backstory passes the gate Aravind Srinivas passed at **Perplexity** (2022), and the distribution path runs straight through NVIDIA TensorRT, Qualcomm AI Engine, and Apple Core ML — three first-party SDKs whose business is to make General Instinct's runtime unnecessary on their own silicon. Smart team, wrong leverage shape for the check I write.

The leverage check fails first, which is where I always start. **Twitter** (2007) was permissionless because anyone could post and the marginal cost of replication was zero — media-as-protocol, no gatekeeper. General Instinct's Instinct Edge is code, but the code is sold per-deployment to physical-AI teams: robotics, drones, manufacturing, named explicitly on the YC page. There is no GitHub repo cited, no open-source pull, no developer community already running the runtime in the wild. The dossier names $500K from YC (PitchBook; the $5M on StartupHub.ai looks like a scrape error) and a "founders@" email as the GTM surface. That is enterprise sales motion to robotics customers gated by chipmaker certification — labor leverage on top of permissioned silicon channels. The Qualcomm acquisition of Edge Impulse on March 10, 2025 makes the Snapdragon target a direct overlap with an incumbent that owns the silicon. This is the inverse of the **Stack Overflow** (2008) developer-pull pattern.

The network dimension is where the **Perplexity** (2022) exception precedent matters, because that is the only frame under which I write the check. Perplexity is single-sided too — each user's value does not compound the next — and I waived criterion #2 because criterion #1 (API distribution), criterion #3 (Srinivas's Berkeley retrieval PhD), and pre-consensus AI timing all cleared at top quartile. General Instinct passes criterion #3 emphatically and probably has a real category-timing tailwind in physical AI. It does not pass criterion #1. The Instinct Edge runtime ships through per-device licensing, not through an API a developer integrates without asking. Two of the three gates that earned Perplexity its exception do not clear here.

Wang and Jiao are the strongest thing in the dossier. Wang's Google DeepMind AI Masters Scholarship at Cambridge plus the Agno Global Agent Hackathon 2025 win ($5K cash) plus a 30K Rednote following is real energy paired with real specific knowledge — exactly the founder-product-market-fit gate from my 2018 post. Jiao filing two tardigrade-protein patents as a high school junior and then working on Siemens' first multimodal foundation model is non-trainable depth in the precise domain (frontier-model compression) that the product requires. Compared to **Notion** (2013) — Ivan Zhao rebuilding personal productivity tools since adolescence — General Instinct's founders have the depth without the shipped public artifact. No GitHub repo for either founder shows up in the dossier. The depth is real; the proof-by-shipping is asserted, not visible.

Pivot room for General Instinct is medium-narrow over an 18-month runway. The market spans robotics, drones, manufacturing, and edge inference broadly, which sounds platform-shaped, but the engineering surface is "model + device + latency budget → runtime" — every pivot still requires deep silicon-specific compression work per target chip. **Uber** (2010) could pivot from black-car to UberX because the marketplace primitive was preserved; General Instinct cannot pivot to a consumer or media surface without throwing away the technical moat. If the runtime business hits the chipmaker-channel wall, the realistic adjacent move is to a vertical robotics-platform product or a Jetson-specific dev-tool — both narrower than the current pitch.

The Buffett trio reads clean. No partner litigation, no contested prior exits, no honesty-signaling tells of the kind I learned to flag at **Epinions** (1999–2005). Wang and Jiao are described as international students who dreamed of building startups since high school — quiet ambition, not promotional ethics talk. Integrity reads neutral-to-positive because nothing in the dossier indicates otherwise; I prefer false-negative to punishing quiet founders.

Thirty-six month forecast: General Instinct gets acqui-hired by NVIDIA, Qualcomm, or a robotics platform (Anduril, Figure, Skydio) for $30M–$80M as a compression-team tuck-in inside 30 months. The mechanism is the channel conflict resolving the way it always does — the cross-silicon runtime that promises "any frontier model, any edge device" becomes, in practice, a Jetson-first tool that NVIDIA absorbs before the Qualcomm-Edge-Impulse equivalent can be sold into Snapdragon customers. The specific-knowledge depth in Wang and Jiao is what makes the acquihire likely; the leverage architecture is what makes it cap there.

The strongest bull case is the **Anchorage Digital** exception. Anchorage violated criterion #1 because regulated custody is the structural opposite of permissionless distribution, and I wrote it anyway because pre-consensus category conviction since **MetaStable** (2014) outweighed the architecture rule and the regulatory moat itself flipped into permission-as-moat structural leverage. Physical AI in 2026 looks structurally similar — frontier models crossing the on-device feasibility threshold, Jetson AGX Orin at 275 TOPS, a category I have not yet anchored into. The Anchorage exception requires a pre-existing accumulation pattern (years of MetaStable conviction) plus a moat that flips from permissionless to permission-as-moat. General Instinct has neither: I have no analog of the 2014 crypto accumulation in physical-AI infra, and the dossier shows no certification, no patent, no exclusive silicon-vendor relationship that would make the permission requirement *into* the moat rather than the wound. Without those two conditions, Anchorage does not precedent this check. **Pass.** I would flip to invest if Wang and Jiao open-source the Instinct Edge runtime with documented organic adoption from 1,000+ robotics developers inside six months — that single condition would convert the permissioned enterprise-sales motion into the **Stack Overflow** (2008) / **Replit** (April 2023) permissionless-code pattern, and the specific-knowledge gate already passes.
