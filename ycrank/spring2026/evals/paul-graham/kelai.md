# KelAI -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Jeremie Cohen spent eight years as a portfolio manager at WorldQuant and led the ML team at Millennium before starting KelAI, and that is the only sentence in the KelAI dossier that makes me lean forward. Everything else — the solo-founder line, the missing GitHub, the absent cereal-box equivalent, the "Generative AI / Swarm AI" tag salad — pushes the other direction. So KelAI's analytical signature is a domain insider with an organic idea, attacking it alone. That pairing has a name in my notebook: it is the shape Sam Altman had at **Loopt** (2005), minus Altman.

The **Stripe** (2010) Collisons are the high rung on organic-idea fit because they had been hacking commerce since **Auctomatic** (2007) and built the payments API they themselves needed. KelAI's Cohen is closer to that rung than to the Dropbox-USB-stick midpoint, because a quant PM at WorldQuant automating his own systematic-research loop — literature review, signal formulation, backtesting, monitoring — is literally writing the tool he used to have analysts build for him by hand. The divergence from Stripe is that the Collisons shipped a self-serve developer artifact a stranger could touch in five minutes; KelAI's kelaitech.com exposes only a careers form per the May 2026 WebFetch, and the only public product signal is the YC page's claim that "signals began running in production" in October 2025. Organic, yes. Demonstrable to me, no.

The AI-wrapper team from my standing pass example fails on resourcefulness because the deck arrives before the artifact, and KelAI's dossier shows the same gap from the opposite direction — there is an artifact (production signals since October 2025) but no stunt, no unscalable manual operation, no anecdote about Cohen running the loop by hand for a friend's fund at 2am. **Airbnb** (2009)'s Brian Chesky sold $40 cereal boxes to fund the company; **Coinbase** (2012)'s Brian Armstrong shipped an Android wallet to overturn a rejection. KelAI has none of that on the page. Animals leave tracks, and the only track I see is a LinkedIn title change to "Founder at KelAI | YC P26."

On schlep, the mechanism that matters compounds over 18 months: hedge-fund procurement demands SOC2, model validation, and data lineage that AlphaSense ($1.4B raised) and Hebbia ($159M raised) have already absorbed, and the second KelAI's first fund customer asks for a model-risk-management package, Cohen — alone — has to choose between writing it himself or hiring someone who has. That schlep is real and it is the kind I usually reward, but the KelAI dossier does not say Cohen has embraced it; it says "no published compliance posture." The schlep is in front of him, not behind him. Coinbase's Armstrong embraced his schlep visibly in the application itself.

Counterfactually, KelAI's hacker-genealogy score flips a tier only if Cohen turns out to have a GitHub I missed or shipped a non-trivial open quant library under a handle the dossier did not catch. The page says no GitHub found, no Twitter found, and an École Polytechnique EE degree from 2007–2011 that is credential, not lineage. Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp before **Stripe** (2010); Aaron Swartz co-authored RSS before **Reddit** (2005). A Millennium ML-lead title is a job, not a hack. I lean down.

KelAI is a solo founder. The YC page lists Cohen as the only named founder of a three-person team, and "Hiring senior ML engineers and quant engineers" on kelaitech.com/careers tells me the other two seats are employees, not co-founders. My single most consistent portfolio pattern — Collisons, Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk, Huffman/Ohanian, Kan/Shear/Vogt — is two-or-three founders who built together before the company existed. KelAI is the configuration I most often regret funding. The override case is **Loopt** (2005)'s Altman, and Altman was a force-of-personality animal; the KelAI dossier does not give me evidence Cohen is.

My 36-month forecast for KelAI: acqui-hired by a data vendor in the FactSet/S&P-Kensho cluster for under $40M, or quietly wound down with Cohen returning to a PM seat at Millennium or WorldQuant, ARR under $3M either way, no Series A led by a top-tier firm. The mechanism is straightforward — a solo domain insider can sell two or three hedge funds via his network (this is the realistic upside of the WorldQuant Rolodex), hit a procurement wall around SOC2 and model validation, and discover that out-shipping Hebbia and Rogo while also running enterprise sales alone is the schlep that breaks him.

The strongest case against my forecast is the bull mechanism a real WorldQuant alum could run: Cohen calls eight ex-colleagues now seeded across Citadel, Point72, Balyasny, and the smaller pods, lands three pilots at $300K each on relationship alone, and is at $3M ARR in twelve months without ever doing outbound. That is exactly how AlphaSense's earliest revenue came in. The reason it does not flip me is that the KelAI dossier names zero customers, zero LOIs, zero advisor names from that network as of May 2026 — the network is theoretical until a logo appears, and **Airbnb** (2009)'s lesson is that the artifact-or-stunt must already exist on the table during the interview, not be implied by the resume. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: KelAI names a technical co-founder of Millennium/WorldQuant seniority within six months, or publishes one paying hedge-fund customer by name. Either one and I take another meeting.
