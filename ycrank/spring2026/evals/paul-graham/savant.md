# Savant -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Savant is the shape of company my Pass Example was almost literally describing -- two CS-credentialed cofounders, Anthony Kim out of Yale EECS and Kevin Yu out of Cornell CS, pitching a B2B memory layer for "AI-native enterprises" against Glean at $300M ARR, with no shipped artifact, no stunt revenue, no GitHub trail, and no founder-pain origin story anywhere in the dossier. The highest-scoring axis is the schlep dimension, because integrating Salesforce, Zendesk, Intercom, Slack, Office 365, and Google Workspace is genuinely unpleasant plumbing; the lowest is animal resourcefulness, because the dossier is silent in the exact place the **Airbnb** (2009) cereal-box story or the **Dropbox** (2007) Hacker News demo would have landed.

The **Airbnb** (2009) cereal-box test is the rung I keep going back to on resourcefulness -- those founders sold over 1,000 boxes at $40 to fund a company nobody would back, and the stunt was the artifact. Savant's dossier on this axis is conspicuously empty: no Product Hunt listing, no Twitter or X account for either Anthony Kim or Kevin Yu, no GitHub repos, no public demo, no design-partner LOI, no customer logo, no pricing page on heysavant.com. Animals leave tracks, as I keep saying, and the entire visible track for Savant is a YC company page and a `contact@heysavant.com` mailto. When I read a dossier this quiet on pre-funding hustle, the prior is not "they're heads-down shipping"; the prior is that nothing remarkable happened yet, and the absence of a single named stunt against five well-resourced incumbents is the data.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the schlep parallel worth running on Savant, because Brian Armstrong's moat turned out to be his appetite for the bitcoin compliance grind nobody else in the batch wanted. Savant's claimed wedge -- capturing "procedures, exceptions, judgment calls, and unwritten rules" via integrations into Salesforce, Zendesk, Intercom, Slack, Office 365, Google Workspace and custom databases -- has real schlep texture; that integration matrix is exactly the kind of unglamorous surface Glean is grinding through. The trouble is that capturing *undocumented* procedural knowledge is not a schlep, it is an open AI research problem dressed as a schlep, and the dossier offers no benchmark, no case study, no design partner who has lived with the capture mechanism. Savant gets credit for picking up part of the **Coinbase** (2012) shape and partial credit only, because the load-bearing piece of the pitch is the magic, not the grind.

On organic-idea origin, Drew Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and the lost-stick was the company; Patrick and John Collison had been hacking commerce flows since **Auctomatic** (2007) before **Stripe** (2010). For Savant the dossier gives me Yale EECS and Cornell CS and no prior enterprise software, no knowledge-management operating history, no story of either Anthony Kim or Kevin Yu personally drowning in scattered Salesforce fields and undocumented Zendesk macros at a previous job. The "AI-native enterprises lack a single source of operational context" framing reads as market observation, not lived pain. This is precisely the inversion of my organic-idea filter from "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012), and the default penalty applies.

The hacker-genealogy dimension compounds badly over the next eighteen months if Savant doesn't fix it. Aaron Swartz had the RSS specification before **Reddit** (2005); Patrick Collison had the Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010); my unstated pattern is that hackers leave artifacts before they leave companies. Savant's founders are CS-credentialed but credential-only in the dossier -- no GitHub for Anthony Kim, no GitHub for Kevin Yu, no Twitter presence, no open-source contribution, no hacker-community footprint that predates the company. In an enterprise category where the moat will be technical taste against Glean's engineering org, credential-only founders without a visible technical signature are the wrong starting position, and absence here is itself signal.

The cofounder configuration would flip a tier if the dossier disclosed a multi-year prior relationship between Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu -- the **Reddit** (2005) dorm-roommate pattern, or the Collison-brothers pattern. The dossier discloses neither; it confirms two founders and nothing about how they met, which is the YC-interview-surfaces-it case where I default to neutral. A 2-person team beats solo, and that is the floor I'm willing to give Savant without further evidence.

My 36-month forecast on Savant: dead or quietly acqui-hired into Glean, Notion, or Atlassian at under $3M ARR, having never landed a named enterprise logo that survived contract renewal. The mechanism is the Pass Example mechanism -- credential founders, AI-wrapper positioning over a commoditizing integration surface, no shipped artifact at the moment of fundraising -- compounded by Microsoft 365 Copilot Agents and Notion Custom Agents bundling the same functionality into suites the customer is already paying for. Savant's category is the one Glean is currently mowing at $300M ARR and Dust is mowing at 3,000 organizations, and being the third memory-layer entrant without a hacker artifact is a hard place to start.

The steel-man case for Savant runs through Harj Taggar's partnership and the genuine fact that enterprise agent memory is a real gap -- Dust's $40M Series B from Sequoia in May 2026 is not a fake market signal, and an AI-native enterprise *does* need a context store distinct from a wiki. The known exception that would rescue this is the **Loopt** (2005) override: Sam Altman without product-market-fit, where the animal test on the founder overrode the idea. That exception requires a founder who walks into the interview and visibly is the animal, and the dossier on Anthony Kim and Kevin Yu does not give me that read -- no public writing, no Hacker News presence, no stunt, no prior shipping, no claimed schlep already swallowed. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a working demo on Hacker News from Anthony Kim showing live procedural-knowledge capture from a real customer's Salesforce and Zendesk instance, with the customer named. Until that ships, pass.
