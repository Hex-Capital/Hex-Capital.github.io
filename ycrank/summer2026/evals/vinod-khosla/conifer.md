# Conifer -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Conifer is a systems-engineering optimization play dressed as an AI infrastructure bet -- Michael Jeffords and two unnamed teammates have hand-written Metal and CUDA kernels to run open models on-device and route to cloud "only when a local model cannot meet the quality bar," and the headline claim on conifer.build is "~80% AI bill reduction." That pairing -- respectable kernel work at the top, margin arbitrage at the bottom -- is exactly the signature that fails my entry point. My **Sun Microsystems** (1982) question was not "can we shave workstation cost by 80%," it was "what happens if every engineer has a networked workstation." Conifer's success-state is a lower invoice from OpenRouter.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the HIGH rung on consequence -- I wrote the $50M first VC check because the success-state redrew what human cognition costs. Conifer shares one thin mechanism with that check -- both move token economics -- but Conifer moves them the wrong direction for my framework: it captures savings inside an existing regime rather than collapsing the regime. The dossier names the incumbent Conifer is fighting -- OpenRouter's $113M Series B from Google and NVIDIA (TechTimes, May 2026), Portkey's Palo Alto Networks acquisition intent, Ollama's 52M Q1 2026 monthly downloads -- and every one of them is a system Conifer *optimizes against*, not one it *ends*. Compare to **Impossible Foods** (2011), whose success-state was animal protein production disappearing; Conifer's success-state is Anthropic's per-token API line item shrinking 80%. That is a P&L event, not a civilizational one, and my Outlook Business framing -- "too important not to do" -- does not fire on invoice reduction.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) is the LOW rung I keep in front of me when a dossier understates required burn -- the failure mode was capital adequacy misjudged on the way to plant closure in 2011. Conifer inverts that failure mode: the capital plan is not understated because there is no capital-hungry science underneath it. Hand-written fused matmul, RMSNorm, RoPE, and flash-style attention kernels are hard engineering, but they are engineering any well-funded gateway can replicate on a 12-month sprint, and OpenRouter now has Google and NVIDIA cash to do exactly that. Conifer does not resemble the **Range Fuels** failure -- it resembles the **KiOR** anti-pattern only in vocabulary. No thermodynamic wall, no physics miracle, no fund-clock-defying timeline. The `sage` repo at v1.2.14 with 303 stars in July 2026 is a competent Rust project, not the capital-hostile science that repels consensus VCs and creates my edge.

Michael Jeffords compounds the third dimension the wrong way over 18 months. My TED Talk cited the UC Berkeley study -- 28,000 expert predictions, "only slightly more accurate than chance" -- to argue that expert credentials are a soft negative, but the dossier reports no verified LinkedIn, no confirmed GitHub, no prior company for Jeffords, and two unnamed teammates. This is not the anti-expert profile that produced my **DoorDash** (2013) check; that was a founder ambition signal, not an information vacuum. Absent public thinking, absent a defied-orthodoxy moment, absent a "too important not to do" framing anywhere in Conifer's own copy, the dimension collapses to my absent-evidence default and then a hair below because the sole founder cannot be found. Eighteen months from now, either Jeffords surfaces as a serious systems engineer with a specific opinion the field rejects, or Conifer looks like every other YC infra team.

Counterfactually, Conifer's learning-rate score flips a tier only if the dossier produces a documented pivot -- Jeffords having sold a prior inference startup, or a public "we were wrong about pure-local, here is why we added cloud fallback" note. The **Slash** (2024) $100M Series C hinged on the publicly told Kanye West pivot story; that was the override signal that let me write into a YC-backed consumer-fintech consensus bet. Conifer has no such artifact. The `sage` release cadence up to v1.2.14 by June 2026 shows shipping discipline, not thesis update. Default holds.

Conifer versus **Cognition** (Series B April 2024, $175M) on the required-capital axis: Cognition needed my check because training a foundation model for software engineering is a capital-depth problem where the roadmap does not close under $200M. Conifer's roadmap -- Rust desktop app, kernel work, marketplace "Coming soon," bottom-up GitHub distribution via 303 stars and 57 org followers -- closes under $10M. I am over-resourced for this deal, which is my persona's exact anti-pattern for check-size fit. Any Tier-1 generalist funds Conifer; that is the tell.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Conifer is acquired for under $75M by OpenRouter, Portkey (post-Palo Alto Networks), or a hyperscaler wanting an on-device inference feature, OR shuts down as LiteLLM and Ollama absorb the wedge and the routing layer commoditizes. Zero probability, in my framework, of an independent $1B+ outcome -- the success-state is a feature, and features get bought or buried. The mechanism is straightforward: OpenRouter's $113M from Google and NVIDIA is precisely the balance sheet needed to ship local-first routing before Conifer builds distribution beyond 303 GitHub stars.

The strongest bull case: my own Fortune (2025-07-01) thesis that AI will do 80% of economically valuable work by 2030 requires inference costs to collapse, and local-first is one path there; enterprises citing "financials, user data" privacy constraints is a real regulatory tailwind; Conifer's live routing dataset could compound into a proprietary prompt-to-cheapest-model corpus that OpenRouter's cloud-only telemetry cannot replicate. That case stings because the privacy wedge is genuine -- I would not send Khosla Ventures LP data to Anthropic either. But it dies on the dossier: the "live intelligence index" is a reference, not a disclosed dataset; the marketplace is "Coming soon"; there is no signed LOI with a named F500 counterparty; and Ollama's 52M monthly downloads means the substrate Conifer's paid layer sits on is already free and improving faster than any three-person team can differentiate against. One verifiable condition flips me: a signed on-prem deployment contract with a named financial-services or healthcare buyer above $1M ACV, disclosed publicly, before the Series A. Absent that, Conifer is the 30-second pass from my Pass Example -- competent engineering, no consequence.
