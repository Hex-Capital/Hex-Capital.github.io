# FinalDose -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

FinalDose is a frontier-biotech moonshot I cannot touch -- the Oxford-DPhil trio claims a "programmable DNA drug destroying all cancers," and the strongest dimension (capital-intensive frontier swing) is structurally fighting with the weakest (physical-world verifiability) in a way that maps to neither the **SpaceX** template nor the **Flock Safety** template I usually look for. Jeff Liu is the named CEO with a real prior in Vivid Dx ($5M seed + $10M Pre-Series A, John Sperzel installed as external CEO July 2025), and that is genuinely useful, but FinalDose is asking me to underwrite a molecule whose specificity at single-base-pair resolution is unvalidated in any public preclinical data the dossier could surface.

On the capital-intensive frontier dimension -- FinalDose's strongest -- the ladder runs from **SpaceX** (2008, the first-ever angel check into rockets when peers called them uninvestable) at the top, through **Crusoe** (~2018, energy-infrastructure-as-arbitrage where I embedded Jonathan for six months through the 2020 collapse) in the middle, down to the consensus YC AI-agent SaaS wrapper at the bottom. FinalDose sits between **SpaceX** and **Crusoe**: cancer therapeutics with IND-enabling tox and a peer set (Intellia public, Caribou $300M+ IPO, Mammoth $375M raised) that just executed 27% and 32% layoffs in 2025 is exactly the "too hard or too unsexy" lane, and the peer layoffs are themselves a deselection signal that creates room for non-consensus entries. The reason FinalDose sits below **SpaceX** rather than alongside is that the pre-seed-into-biotech capital math is brutal -- a YC seed does not fund IND-enabling work for a programmable kill-switch with no FDA precedent class.

On physical-world verifiability -- FinalDose's weakest -- the ladder runs from **Flock Safety** (~2018, sourced from a Four Seasons Wi-Fi network name and a camera I could photograph) at the top, through **Niantic** (~2015, which I played as Ingress obsessively from 2012) in the middle, down to the consensus YC AI-agent darling (pure software abstraction, no public app, founder testimony only) at the bottom. FinalDose sits between the **Niantic** floor and the AI-darling basement: a preclinical molecule is not installable, not photographable, not rideable, not deployable in a friend's house in 48 hours, and the only artifact in the dossier I can actually click is a ScienceDaily article from April 2026 whose attribution to FinalDose the researcher could not confirm. I am gifted with the ability to make these connections, and the connections I make are visual and physical -- a sequence of base pairs is not a connection I can make.

The founder-origin dimension is where FinalDose nearly trips a Red Flag without quite tripping it. The ladder here runs from **Anduril**-Luckey (2017, who shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before defense) at the top, through **DeepMind**-Hassabis (~2013, my Known Exception where polymath compounding overrode the pedigree-as-thesis bias) in the middle, down to the consensus AI darling where ex-FAANG PMs discovered the problem in a memo. FinalDose sits between the **DeepMind** rung and the LOW rung: Jeff Liu's Vivid Dx prior is the closest thing to a Luckey-style "shipped a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain by a specific prior year" pattern, but no founder describes a specific physical moment they FELT cancer -- no family member, no clinical exposure, no autobiographical contact. I am scoring this as the **DeepMind** polymath override partially holding, not failing.

On the magically-weird dimension, FinalDose passes the **Crusoe** (~2018) "sounds ridiculous on paper" test -- "one molecule, new guide, R&D cost collapses to near zero" is the sort of claim that gets laughed off in a partner meeting -- but the YC Spring 2026 stamp and the Garry Tan partnership create the inverse risk: this is exactly the batch darling that will have 10 VCs circling by Demo Day, which moves FinalDose toward my Pass Example pattern and away from the **Crusoe** "find them before it becomes consensus" pattern. On the single-CEO dimension, Jeff Liu is named CEO, which is structurally cleaner than the **HQ Trivia** (2017) Yusupov/Kroll partnership that imploded, but his ongoing Vivid Dx association (Sperzel CEO installed July 2025 does not mean Jeff exited) sits between the clean **SpaceX**-Musk rung and the **HQ Trivia** failure rung -- it's a divided-attention risk rather than a tiebreaker-authority risk.

My 36-month forecast for FinalDose: alive but quiet, no IND filed, the "destroys all cancers" platform pitch narrowed to a single lead oncology indication (most likely BRCA1-deficient given Li-Yao Huang's MRC Weatherall postdoc work on PARP-inhibitor resistance), raising a bridge round flat-to-down from existing YC-adjacent investors because the $300M peer raises set a capital bar pre-IND data cannot clear. The mechanism producing that outcome is the gap between platform pitch and asset reality: pharma licenses assets, not platforms with no in-vivo specificity data, and the FDA has no precedent class for a programmable DNA kill switch, which extends timelines beyond the runway a YC seed can buy.

The strongest case against my forecast is the **DeepMind** Known Exception writ large: three Oxford DPhils compounding across oncology, biochemistry, and computational biology is exactly the "age of the polymath" thesis I have been writing about, and Jeff Liu's Vivid Dx track record (diagnostic time 5 days to 30 minutes, $15M raised, Oxford Science Enterprises-backed) is verified prior-product trajectory in an adjacent domain -- the **Anduril**-Luckey template for biotech. The bear-case mechanism that wins against this bull case is the absence of the autobiographical-contact pattern: Hassabis was a chess prodigy and neuroscientist compounding into AGI; Luckey was a VR obsessive shipping Oculus from a garage; FinalDose's founders are credentialed academics translating literature, not patients, caregivers, or oncologists who watched the undruggable-target failure mode happen in front of them. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a peer-reviewed preprint showing single-base-pair in-vivo specificity in a mouse tumor model with off-target editing below 0.1% -- that is the tactile artifact I cannot install but can read, and it would move FinalDose from Neutral to Invest before the next round prices.
