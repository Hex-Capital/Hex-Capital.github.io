# Thomas -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

**Human Thomas built game bots from age 13, presented at NeurIPS at 18, and scaled a freelance practice to $40K/month before automating himself out of it** — and then he walked that résumé into the most consensus category of the YC W26 batch and packaged it as "the first AI founder." That high/low pairing is Thomas's whole signature: a genuinely missionary Wizard-of-Oz operator pointed at AI agents, which is the single sleeve where I have publicly said "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale #113, 2024). The interesting question is not whether Thomas is a good builder — he obviously is — but whether the persona wrapper around the agent makes this a creator-shaped asset I can structure into, or a vanilla SaaS-on-LLM cap table dressed up in a costume.

**Fin, 2015 — Kortina and me running a "completely manual process" before we ever shipped automation** — is the load-bearing analogy for Thomas's founder dimension, and it's why I read past the absent public writing on madebythomas.ai. Human Thomas already lived the Wizard-of-Oz arc on his own freelance book: he ran the manual $40K/month operation, learned what was actually automatable from inside the work, then built the agent on top of demonstrated unit economics. That's the temperament I look for, and it's rarer in this batch than the AI-agent framing suggests. Where Thomas diverges from the Fin template is that there is no published essay, no opinionated thread, no public artifact of what he believes about why every other agent company is wrong — and "Writing is thinking" (Antoine Buteau, ~2023) is a filter I apply before the meeting, not after. Strong missionary signal on operating history, thin missionary signal on category conviction.

**The Pass Example I literally wrote into the persona file — "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, $30M post, four tier-1 VCs already in" — is what Thomas's category looks like to me in 2026**, and the dossier gives me no round size, no price, no lead investor to argue with. The mechanism here is straightforward: 18 months from now, every YC batch will have shipped twenty agent startups, frontier labs will have shipped first-party computer-use products at zero margin, and the rounds that priced like Series Bs in W26 will be marked down or quietly recapped. Thomas does not name the price he is raising at, which under my default rule for silence on round dynamics means I assume consensus pricing and punish it — because the entire alpha at seed comes from being the bid at a number no one else will write, and an AI-agent persona in spring 2026 is precisely where the consensus bid is loudest.

**Marina Mogilko's $1.7M for 5% of 30-year earnings plus 5% of IP** is the right counterfactual to run on Thomas's capital structure — because if I am honest, "an AI persona that runs its own companies" is structurally a holdco wrapped around a piece of operated revenue, not a software company. For Thomas's capital-structure score to flip a tier, the dossier would need to show me a non-SAFE instrument: a royalty on the revenue of Thomas-operated entities, an equity stake in the persona-IP separable from the operating LLC, or a holdco share that explicitly captures the brand equity of @madebythomasai independent of any single product. None of that is disclosed. The asset is illegible in the way I like — that's the half of the Mogilko trade I would take — but the instrument is presumably a vanilla YC SAFE, which is the half that destroys the trade.

**@madebythomasai on X and /in/madebythomasai on LinkedIn exist but counts are "not retrievable"** in the dossier (X returned 402, LinkedIn returned 999), and that single line is the entire founder-as-brand question. John Fish had a visible YouTube audience before Bookshelved; Marina had millions of language-learning subscribers before LinguaTrip; the Slow Creator Fund formalized the rule that I underwrite the audience, not the intent to build one. Thomas has the intent — the persona-first GTM is correctly identified as the distribution wedge — but no public number proves the audience exists yet, and persona-driven companies without measurable owned distribution are exactly the lifestyle-vlogger trap the Creator Fund explicitly avoids (TechCrunch, October 12 2023). The persona strategy is the right answer to a question I have not yet been shown evidence Thomas can answer.

**My 36-month forecast on Thomas**: either he pivots into a small Wizard-of-Oz holdco where the AI persona becomes a media property and three or four agent-operated micro-businesses aggregate to $1-3M ARR with the persona itself as the durable asset, or he's quietly dead by mid-2028 because frontier-lab computer-use products commoditize the operator layer before @madebythomasai accumulates the audience needed to make the persona the moat. The mechanism producing either outcome is the same: the tech bet (sustained autonomous operation of revenue-generating companies) and the distribution bet (persona-audience growth) are both unresolved, and the company resolves whichever one breaks first.

**The strongest bull case on Thomas is that I am pattern-matching him into the AI-agent sleeve when he actually belongs in the creator-economy sleeve** — the same dismissal mistake other VCs made on Mogilko when they read "YouTuber" and stopped reading. Read correctly, Thomas is a persona-IP holdco using AI as the operating layer rather than an agent SaaS using a persona as marketing skin, and the right Slow check is a Creator Fund-shaped royalty on persona revenue, not a SAFE. That argument stings, and it would be the right argument — if @madebythomasai had a retrievable follower count north of 50K and a Thomas-operated company with disclosed MRR. Neither exists in the dossier. Mogilko had millions of subscribers and a profitable LingauTrip on entry; Thomas has a tagline and two social handles whose engagement I cannot measure. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: @madebythomasai showing 100K+ engaged followers within 12 months or one Thomas-operated entity disclosing $50K+ MRR — at which point I'd come back with a royalty instrument, not equity. Until then, pass, but the founder is worth a follow-up coffee.
