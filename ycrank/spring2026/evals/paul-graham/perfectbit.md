# PerfectBit -- Paul Graham Evaluation

PerfectBit is the inverted **Airbnb** (2009): the founders lived the problem so intimately that the idea is almost overdetermined, but nothing in the dossier shows me they are animals. Peter Vajda ran Emu and Movie Gen at Meta for eleven years; Seiji Yamamoto sat inside Core Llama post-training for nine. They know what training data the frontier labs are starving for because they were the ones starving. That puts PerfectBit's organic-idea signal near the top of my ladder. What's missing is the cereal-box moment -- the stunt, the demo on Hacker News, the unscalable hustle that flips conviction. The dossier reads like a Meta org chart, not a hacker's GitHub.

**Founder pain ladder**: HIGH is Drew Houston forgetting his USB stick at MIT and shipping a demo before **Dropbox** (2007) raised; MID is the Huffman/Ohanian dorm hack that became **Reddit** (2005); LOW is the two-MBA AI wrapper from my Pass Example, where no founder had personally suffered the problem. PerfectBit sits just below the HIGH rung. Yamamoto post-trained Llama; Vajda generated media at frontier scale. When they describe "web-scraped text that produces hallucinations on tasks humans find trivial," that is not a deck phrase, that is the thing they were debugging on Tuesdays. Verifier-grounded data was their own wish-list. This is the same shape as the Collisons hacking commerce at **Auctomatic** (2007) before they hacked it at **Stripe** (2010) -- the company is downstream of the founders' actual workdays.

**Animal resourcefulness ladder**: HIGH is the Obama O's cereal boxes at **Airbnb** (2009); MID is Drew Houston's pre-seed Hacker News demo video for **Dropbox** (2007); LOW is the polished two-MBA team with a TAM slide and no shipped artifact. PerfectBit sits between MID and LOW, closer to LOW. There is no demo URL on perfectbit.ai -- the site is at v0.4 with no public artifact, no Product Hunt, no Twitter, no GitHub for either founder. Vajda's Crunchbase shows IEEE papers; Yamamoto's shows PNAS. These are credentials. Brian Armstrong rebuilt his **Coinbase** (2012) application as an Android wallet after I rejected him. What did Vajda or Yamamoto build between leaving Meta and walking into Garry's office? The dossier doesn't say, and animals leave tracks.

**Schlep ladder**: HIGH is Armstrong's bitcoin compliance grind at **Coinbase** (2012); MID is **DoorDash** (2013) last-mile ops; LOW is the software-only AI wrapper. PerfectBit sits in the MID band -- assembling physics simulators, formal proof systems, and oracle scientific databases across heterogeneous domains is a real engineering schlep, the kind Alexandr Wang at **Scale AI** would recognize as the gap incumbents are routing around. But it's a software schlep, not a regulatory or physical-world one. **Surge AI** at ~$1.2B 2024 revenue and **Mercor** at $450M+ ARR built moats by recruiting humans; PerfectBit's moat thesis is that verifier engineering across domains is harder than it looks. No GitHub surfaced for either founder. No open-source release. No Hacker News footprint. My unstated pattern around competitive-programming and Lisp-coded founders does not light up here. The team is technical, but technical-by-credential, which is exactly the texture I learned to discount after **Viaweb** (1995).

**Cofounder ladder**: HIGH is the Collison brothers; MID is Huffman/Ohanian roommates at **Reddit** (2005); LOW is solo or hackathon-met. PerfectBit sits between MID and HIGH -- two founders, overlapping Meta years on adjacent foundation-model teams, complementary CS/Physics coverage. Not brothers, not roommates, but a multi-year working proximity that the YC interview will easily verify. This dimension does not save or sink the bet.

My 36-month forecast for PerfectBit: $5-15M ARR on 2-4 frontier-lab contracts (one of OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI; possibly Meta out of relationship loyalty), then either acquihired by a frontier lab at sub-$300M or starved as labs insource verifier pipelines they already partially run (per the llm-stats.com 2026 snippet). Not a **Stripe** (2010), not a **Coinbase** (2012). The mechanism is the dimensional signature: organic-idea fit gets PerfectBit the first contract, but the absence of animal evidence means I cannot predict they will out-grind **Surge AI** or **Scale AI** when those incumbents extend into RLVR data.

The bull case stings, so I will name it. **Surge AI** went from nothing to ~$1.2B in 2024 revenue selling preference data to frontier labs, and Edwin Chen did not start with cereal boxes either -- he had Google Brain credentials and a thesis. If RLVR is the post-training paradigm of 2026-2028 (and the Foundation Capital and llm-stats sources suggest it is), then a credentialed-insider team with direct Llama and Emu relationships could ride that wave to a $1B outcome on relationship velocity alone. The bear rebuttal is that **Surge** and **Scale** were founded into an empty category; PerfectBit is launching into a category where the incumbents already have the buyer relationships and the capital to extend. Alexandr Wang publicly cited my "Schlep Blindness" essay as his trigger; nothing in PerfectBit's dossier suggests Vajda or Yamamoto have a similar contrarian fact about themselves I can underwrite. One verifiable condition flips me: a signed and disclosable contract with a frontier lab outside Meta, or a public verifier-stack release that hits Hacker News with users on it. Until then, the founders are smart and the idea is right and the cereal boxes are nowhere.
