# PerfectBit -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

PerfectBit is two ex-Meta foundation-model leaders -- Peter Vajda (11 years, Director of Media Generation, Emu/Movie Gen) and Seiji Yamamoto (9 years, Senior Staff RSM, Core Llama / AGI Foundations) -- selling RLVR-grounded training data into the most consensus YC category of Spring 2026, with no named CEO and no tactile artifact. The signature is striking: highest possible founder-buyer proximity coupled with the lowest possible "magically weird" score. Two people who were literally inside the buyer's org last quarter, walking into a market where Scale AI sits at $14B and Surge AI is in $15-25B fundraise discussions. That pairing tells me what this is: a credentialed-insider acquihire candidate dressed up as a frontier infrastructure bet.

Founder-lived-experience is where PerfectBit earns its ladder placement. The HIGH rung is Palmer Luckey at **Anduril** (2017), who built Oculus in his parents' garage before turning to defense -- a builder *of* the domain. The LOW rung is the consensus YC AI-agent founders from my Pass Example, ex-FAANG PMs who discovered the problem in a memo. PerfectBit sits between them, closer to the polymath **DeepMind** mid-rung than to Anduril: Yamamoto's PNAS and Phys Rev Letters publications plus Vajda's Stanford Visiting Asst Prof history is Hassabis-shaped pedigree, but the lived-problem signal is real -- these two were the *buyer* of training data at Meta last year. They know exactly which Llama post-training gaps Surge couldn't fill. That is autobiographical in the operator sense, even if the dossier reads "Stanford CS, PhD Physics" on slide one.

Magically weird is where PerfectBit collapses. The HIGH rung is **Crusoe** (~2018), burning North Dakota gas flares to mine Bitcoin -- three uninvestable things stapled together that I underwrote anyway because the founder was *of* the oilfield. The MID rung is **Niantic** (~2015), where John Hanke's location-data thesis was unfashionable but defensible. The LOW rung is the consensus YC AI-agent darling I pass on before slide three. PerfectBit pins to the LOW rung: Scale AI at $14B raised, Surge AI at ~$1.2B 2024 revenue, Mercor at $450M+ ARR, Micro1 at $500M, plus AfterQuery and Snorkel already in the verifier-adjacent lane -- and an arXiv paper from January 2026 (EigenData, 2601.22607) describing self-evolving synthetic data with executable per-instance checkers. The thesis isn't laughed at; it's the most-funded sub-category of AI infra. Find them before it becomes consensus -- this *is* the consensus.

Single CEO with tiebreaker authority is the structural risk that turns a maybe into a no. The HIGH rung is Luckey at **Anduril**, unambiguous from day one. The LOW rung is **HQ Trivia** (2017), Yusupov and Kroll, the partnership feud that contributed to the 2020-21 implosion -- a productive failure I cite often. PerfectBit pins toward the LOW rung: two co-founders, both ex-Meta senior, both with overlapping Stanford-adjacent academic ties, and the YC page lists no CEO. When two equally-credentialed PhDs split media generation and Llama post-training between them, who decides the verifier stack roadmap when physics simulators and proof systems both demand engineering bandwidth? The dossier is silent. That silence is the **GameCrush** (~2011) silence and I have learned to read it.

Capital-intensive-frontier-or-unsexy is where PerfectBit sits between the MID rung -- **Crusoe**'s energy-arbitrage software-on-hardware -- and the LOW rung consensus SaaS wrapper. PerfectBit's verifier stacks (physics simulators, formal proof systems, oracle scientific DBs) have technical depth, but the delivery is software, NDA-bound, and a 2-person team with in-person SF hiring is throughput-gated. It is adjacent to frontier infra without being frontier infra. Physical-world verifiability is pure LOW rung: not **Flock Safety** (~2018) where I read the Wi-Fi name in a Four Seasons cafe, not **Niantic** where I played Ingress for three years. PerfectBit ships datasets behind NDAs to maybe ten labs. I cannot install it, photograph it, or hand it to a friend. The website is at v0.4 and there is no Product Hunt, no public Twitter, no observable artifact.

PerfectBit, 36-month forecast: acquihired into Scale AI, Snorkel, or Meta in the $40-90M range by mid-2028, with no Series B priced as a venture outcome. The mechanism is buyer concentration in ~10-20 frontier labs combined with the ex-Meta network's natural pull back into Llama or into Scale's verifier-product expansion. Vajda and Yamamoto are the asset; the verifier IP is replicable from open RLVR literature.

The strongest bull case sits in my own **DeepMind** Known Exception: pedigreed founders with polymath cross-domain compounding can override the credentialism red flag, and Yamamoto-physics-plus-Vajda-media is a real cross-domain stack that maps cleanly onto verifier engineering across heterogeneous scientific domains. Add Garry Tan as the YC group partner and direct founder relationships at OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, and xAI from the Llama and Movie Gen years, and PerfectBit could plausibly land two NDA-bound contracts at $3-5M each within twelve months -- the Mercor 0-to-$450M-ARR speed is recent precedent. That stings. What dismantles it: Mercor's growth came from a contractor-marketplace flywheel; PerfectBit's bespoke verifier-stack engagements have no flywheel and the buyers are the world's best ML engineers who can insource. The DeepMind exception held because Hassabis was a polymath building toward AGI itself, not selling picks-and-shovels into AGI builders -- the second position is structurally an acquihire. The verdict flips if Vajda and Yamamoto designate one CEO publicly AND PerfectBit announces a named frontier-lab contract (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI) above $5M before the YC demo day -- both, not either.
