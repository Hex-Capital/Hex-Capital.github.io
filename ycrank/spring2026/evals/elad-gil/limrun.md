# Limrun -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Muvaffak Onuş, the Crossplane emeritus maintainer behind Limrun, is the dimensional signature here: highest on founder-shipping (11.7k-star Crossplane, 457-star Upjet, KubeCon EU 2022) and lowest on revenue velocity (no ARR disclosed, dashboard literally meters "0 minutes streamed in the last week" on the marketing site). Limrun is the workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-a-hard-thing bet — XCode/Simulator wrapped as an agent-callable API — where the founder profile is the *Color Genomics* (2015) archetype but the curve I need to see is absent in the dossier.

The **Perplexity** (2022) rung on founder execution rhythm is the right ladder reference for Limrun. Aravind pinged me cold and "showed up a week later with a thing we discussed built" — the mechanism was demonstrated shipping cadence outside any employer's scaffolding. Onuş is not Aravind on charisma, but he is unusually close on the shipping artifact axis: Crossplane is a CNCF incubating project, Upjet is the code-gen layer that "cloudifies" provider APIs behind a controller surface, and that is *literally the same engineering pattern* Limrun is applying to XCode and the iOS Simulator. Where Limrun diverges from the Perplexity rung is that I have no evidence of week-over-week artifact velocity post-YC-acceptance — seven integrated platforms (Replit, Rork, Momentic, Minitap, Droidrun, Vibecode, x1.new) is a shipping outcome, but the dossier doesn't show me what landed last week vs. last month.

**Harvey** (2023) is the failure mechanism I have to flag on revenue velocity: at the angel stage I sized Harvey up because $3B → $5B → $8B "in months" was visible compounding, and the test I apply is "false signal vs. just working" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). Limrun's dossier gives me a logo wall — Coconote (Quizlet), SorceJobs (YC W25), seven coding-agent platforms — but zero MRR, zero minutes-streamed, zero named contract value, and a Twitter account at ~22 followers four months in. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose, and Limrun's pricing page does not exist publicly. That is the single biggest gap between Limrun and an *Invest* verdict today.

Counterfactual on market re-opening: for Limrun's catalyst to compound into the **Anduril** (2022) template, the 2024-2026 emergence of Replit Agent, Cursor agents, and Devin-class systems has to keep routing mobile workflows through cloud sandboxes rather than back to local laptops, and Apple's macOS SLA has to stay permissive enough to keep MacStadium and AWS EC2 Mac from collapsing the layer Limrun sits on. The catalyst is specific and recent — coding-agent sandboxes lack XCode, full stop — which puts Limrun closer to the high rung than the low rung. What would flip this to a top-quartile score is a named enterprise (not another agent startup) signing Limrun in the next two quarters, because right now the catalyst is real but the buyer composition is concentric to the same wave.

Mechanism analysis on Limrun's index position: Limrun is trying to be the **Stripe** (2010)-of-mobile-CI for agents — the workflow path every coding-agent platform must touch when it ships an iOS or Android build. The compounding works if Replit, Cursor, Vercel v0, and whatever ships next all integrate Limrun's simulator-control + log-streaming + 60fps recording surface before MacStadium ($68.1M raised) or AWS EC2 Mac add an agent-oriented API. Eighteen months out, this either compounds into the default abstraction (Limrun-inside every agent that touches mobile) or compresses into a thin wrapper that Apple's XCode Cloud subsumes when Apple decides agents are a real buyer. Limrun's seven integrations are real but they are all in the agent-platform tier — none of them are the durable enterprise buyer I'd want as an index anchor.

Single-anchor parallel to **Color Genomics** (2015) on the single-miracle test: Limrun isolates the miracle to "operate Apple Silicon fleets at low-enough latency for agent loops with good build caching" — that is execution, not new physics. Onuş's Crossplane/Upjet history is the single most credible CV I could ask for on that specific miracle. The reason Limrun doesn't get full marks here is that Apple's licensing posture is an exogenous second miracle: if Apple tightens macOS virtualization terms (no specific incident named in the dossier, but the SLA is restrictive today), Limrun's unit economics move independent of execution. That is the "in your in-laws" risk — somebody else can change your board dynamics.

Forecast: in 36 months Limrun is either acquired by Replit, Vercel, or MacStadium in the $40-120M range as the mobile-agent build layer, or it stalls at $2-6M ARR as picks-and-shovels to the coding-agent cohort and gets squeezed by an Apple XCode Cloud agent-API release — I do not see the path to a standalone $1B outcome because the index position is one tier removed from the durable enterprise buyer. The forecast is consistent with the signature: a founder I'd back on shipping artifacts, in a category whose catalyst is real, but with no revenue curve to justify paying up the way I did on Harvey (2023).

Bear case I have to take seriously: Limrun is the *Brex*-of-mobile-CI in the sense that the operationally-disciplined infrastructure founder (Onuş, ex-Upbound staff, Crossplane maintainer) is exactly the *High Growth Handbook* archetype, and the customer concentration in the agent-platform tier is a feature in year one because it gives Limrun a routing-layer position before MacStadium notices. The bear-case counter is sharper: solo founder, YC hiring flag False, no co-founder, no advisor list, and the *Lyft* Series C lesson — when Apple's XCode Cloud or AWS EC2 Mac decides to ship an agent API, Limrun is the #2 in a winner-take-most infrastructure market without a network-effect moat. What flips me to Invest: $1M ARR within nine months with at least three enterprise logos *outside* the coding-agent cohort (an Airbnb, a Robinhood, a Block engineering team running their mobile CI through Limrun). Until that exists, this is a watch — not a check from my own money.
