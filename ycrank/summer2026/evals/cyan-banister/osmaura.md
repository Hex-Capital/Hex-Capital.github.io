# Osmaura -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Osmaura is two MIT CS graduates -- Kali Abeje and Jity Woldemichael -- selling AI matter-origination to law firm partners, which means the highest dimension on my ladder is "unsexy-regulated swing" (legal BD is genuinely a sleepy conservative buyer) and the lowest is "physical-world verifiability" (a partner-facing CRM overlay competing with Nexl and Introhive is not something I can scan at a Four Seasons café). That pairing tells me what Osmaura is: a credentialed, analytically-derived enterprise SaaS bet into a category where the incumbent just raised a $23M Series B in October 2025, and where the founders' relationship to the problem is "100+ partner interviews," not a lived practice-development scar.

The HIGH rung on lived experience is Palmer Luckey at Anduril in 2017 -- he had already shipped Oculus to consumers before he ever pitched a defense drone, so the biography pre-dated the company by years. Osmaura does not load on that mechanism. Kali Abeje's law-adjacent history, per the dossier, is mock trial, debate, and MIT GOV/LAB NLP research on argument extraction -- that is intellectual interest in law, which is the opposite of being the partner who got passed over on origination credit and felt it in the comp committee. Jity Woldemichael's resume is Meta, Jane Street, DRW -- elite SWE pedigree, no law firm. The 100+ partner-interview discovery process is the exact "discovered the problem in a memo" pattern from my Pass Example; Luckey did not interview 100 marines before building Anduril, he had already built the thing he wanted to sell.

The LOW rung on physical verifiability is the consensus YC AI-agent darling from my Pass Example -- pure software abstraction I cannot install, photograph, or hand to a friend. Osmaura is squarely that pattern. It is a partner-facing opportunity-surfacing tool that scans web, news, and a firm's InterAction or Nexl CRM and routes alerts; a normal human cannot ride it the way I rode Uber in 2010, cannot scan it the way I scanned Flock's Wi-Fi SSID, cannot play it the way I played Ingress before Niantic. The dossier lists zero public press, zero Product Hunt presence, and no Osmaura LinkedIn follower data -- the artifact does not exist in my environment. That failure mechanism -- "value can only be evaluated via founder testimony" -- is exactly what the Flock and Niantic sourcing method was built to avoid.

Mechanism analysis on Magically Weird: legaltech BD tooling in 2026 is the opposite of Crusoe in 2018. The dossier names Nexl ($29.6M raised, 150+ law-firm customers), Introhive ($99M raised), Litera Matter Management, LexisNexis InterAction, and Darrow as live competitors -- and over 18 months any one of them can bolt LLM-driven web/news signal extraction onto an existing CRM footprint, which is the exact commoditization risk the dossier itself flags. Osmaura would need to be ignored or laughed at right now to compound into asymmetry; instead the category is funded, named, and crowded.

Counterfactual on Single Unambiguous CEO: the dossier names two co-founders and zero CEO. To flip from default 6/16 toward the Anduril rung I would need to see a vesting agreement naming Abeje or Woldemichael as tiebreaker, or a clean equity split documented somewhere public. Neither exists. Given my GameCrush (~2011) and HQ Trivia (2017) scars, I read the silence as "they have not had the conversation yet" -- it is a near-term blow-up risk, not a neutral data gap, and selling into AmLaw partnerships will surface that disagreement faster than most categories because procurement cycles are 9-18 months.

Single-anchor parallel on Capital-Intensive / Unsexy-Regulated: Vigil Labs (August 2025) sits in regulated finance with a moat that compounds because the founders authentically live in the compliance domain. Osmaura is adjacent to that pattern -- law firms are conservative buyers with slow procurement, which is unsexy in the right way -- but the dossier shows zero domain operator on the founding team, no AmLaw partner advisor, no named design partner. The "unsexy" half of the criterion is there; the "founder authentically lives in the domain" half that makes Vigil work is missing.

My 36-month forecast on Osmaura: dead or acqui-hired by Nexl, Introhive, or Litera for under $20M with sub-$2M ARR, because Nexl's freshly-closed $23M Series B in October 2025 will be deployed precisely into the AI growth-platform features Osmaura is building, and two MIT CS grads with zero law firm relationships will not outrun a vendor sitting inside 150+ firms already. The mechanism is straightforward: in legaltech the distribution moat is incumbent-owned, and Osmaura's only path to escape velocity is a founder-distribution edge it does not have.

The strongest case against my pass: Abeje's MIT GOV/LAB NLP research on argument extraction is an unusually specific technical lineage for this exact problem, and the polymath override I used on DeepMind in 2013 -- pedigreed founders compounding across disciplines -- could apply here if Abeje's law-and-NLP intersection is more lived than the dossier surfaces. That would sting if Abeje had a partner-level design partner already paying, or a working prototype routing real opportunities into a named AmLaw firm. The dossier shows neither -- "0 open jobs, no revenue signal, no LOI counterparty, no integrations disclosed." The DeepMind override required a Hassabis-class cross-domain trifecta visible on Day 1; Osmaura shows two strong CS resumes pointed at a market they researched, not one they bled in. The verifiable condition that would flip me: a signed pilot with a named AmLaw 100 firm and a partner advisor with origination credit -- absent that, this is a rebuttal that does not become a yes.
