# Result -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Result is a hacker-pair company in a consensus-occupied space — Savio Martin's GitHub trail (codehouse 961 stars, slickr 588, gradientking 347, ~2K followers) is the strongest signal in the dossier, and the schlep-zone score is the weakest because incorporation, banking, and payments are already crowded by Doola, Mercury, Stripe Atlas, Firstbase, and Clerky. That pairing — real hacker genealogy on the founder side, no contrarian schlep on the problem side — is what Result is.

Savio Martin's hacker trail is the part that loads my framework. Patrick Collison won Ireland's 41st Young Scientist for Lisp work years before **Stripe** (2010), and the lesson I keep extracting is that shipped technical artifacts pre-date the company that matters. Savio shipped Codehouse, Slickr, and a product that hit #5 in the App Store inside a month, and he won Product Hunt's Maker of the Year Golden Kitty in 2021 at sixteen. The mechanism — a teenager with public artifacts and an audience built before the pitch — is the same. Where Result diverges from Patrick is the second seat: Aaryan Kushwah's dossier shows a TreeHacks 2026 "Best Overall AI Agent" win at Stanford but "No public repos found." Half of a Stripe-style hacker pair is half a signal.

The schlep ladder is where Result loses me, and the failure mechanism I keep pattern-matching is the **Pass Example** AI-wrapper: a polished founder-tools layer with no offline grind the founders themselves must do. **Coinbase** (2012) worked because Brian Armstrong wanted the bitcoin compliance grind; Result's pitch is the inverse — the AI agents do the operational work, and the dossier explicitly notes "no public partner disclosed" for the banking and payments layer. That's not Result running toward a schlep. That's Result hoping someone else takes it (likely a BaaS partner) while Result sits on top with agents. Doola raised $12M, Mercury raised $700M at $5.2B and serves 300K customers — they own the schlep. Result is competing on the layer above it, which is exactly the layer that compounds slowest when incumbents add AI features as a quarterly release.

The organic-idea dimension compounds in Result's favor over the next 18 months only if Savio and Aaryan keep being their own customer. Savio skipped college to build Result; he is the ICP — a 19-year-old internet founder stitching Stripe Atlas, Mercury, and hosting together. The dossier doesn't say "we built this because we needed it," but the founder biography reads like the **Reddit** (2005) pattern of building the link-aggregator the founders wanted to read. If Result ships its own incorporation through Result and runs 90nine.in's operations on Result's agents, the organic-idea score is load-bearing. If Result hires a head of product who designs for a hypothetical user, this dimension decays fast.

For the animal-resourcefulness score to flip a tier, Result would need a named pre-funding stunt — the **Airbnb** (2009) cereal-box equivalent — and the dossier names none for Result specifically. What it does name is adjacent: a #5 App Store ranking from one of Savio's prior products, a Product Hunt Maker of the Year, a TreeHacks Best Overall AI Agent for Aaryan. That's animal behavior on prior products, transferred to Result by reputation, not animal behavior on Result. The Brian Armstrong post-rejection prototype counterfactual would require Result to ship something the morning after a YC rejection. They got accepted, so the test never ran.

The cofounder configuration parallels **Twitch** (2007)'s Kan/Shear pairing on one axis only: both are young technical founders. Where it breaks is the prior relationship — Kan and Shear had built **Kiko** (2005) together and failed together; Result's dossier shows "no public data on co-founder history" for Savio and Aaryan, two India-origin founders matched into a YC batch. Five out of ten is where the dial sits when the YC interview hasn't told me yet whether they actually like each other.

Result in 36 months reaches $1-3M ARR as a Gen-Z indie-founder tools layer riding Savio's 50K-follower distribution, then either pivots into a pure AI-agent-for-creators product when Mercury or Doola ships an "operational agents" tab, or gets acqui-hired by Firstbase or Doola for the founder talent. The mechanism: hacker genealogy carries the build, but the absence of a named schlep means there's no compounding moat against incumbents who already own the regulated layer underneath.

The bull case I have to take seriously is **Coinbase** (2012) — a crowded, regulated, incumbent-heavy space where the right founder appetite still won. Brian Armstrong's appetite for compliance was the wedge. The bear-side reply is that Result's product description explicitly offloads the schlep onto agents and partners; the founders aren't volunteering to do the regulatory grind, they're routing around it. That's the **Pass Example** pattern, not the **Coinbase** (2012) pattern. The verdict flips to invest if Savio or Aaryan publicly ships Result's own money-transmitter or BSA-compliance posture in-house rather than via a BaaS partner, because that would be the named schlep my framework requires.
