# Thomas -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

The dossier line that pulls me in is the single tagline ? Thomas is "a virtual human who runs his own companies" ? and the dimension that makes it interesting is exactly the dimension where the dossier is weakest: this is a maximum-consequence pitch riding on a minimum-capital execution profile, which is the Slash (2024) shape, not the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) shape. If "Human Thomas" actually instantiates an AI agent that founds and operates revenue-generating companies, then the incumbent regime that collapses is human entrepreneurship as a labor category ? a clean fit to the $15T labor-displacement frame I gave Fortune (2026-03-06). That is the entry point. Everything else gets evaluated against whether the dossier earns that consequence claim.

On consequence magnitude, my high rung is **OpenAI** (2019), where the success-state was civilizational redirection of cognition itself, and I wrote the $50M check before the moat thesis existed. Thomas shares the load-bearing mechanism ? if an AI persona can found, capitalize, and run real companies on the open internet, the "founder" itself becomes a commodity and the consequence is the labor-cost collapse I keep pointing at in Fortune (2025-07-01). Thomas diverges from the **OpenAI** (2019) rung on specificity: the dossier names no operated company, no revenue from any operated company, no list of legal entities Thomas-the-agent has incorporated, and madebythomas.ai per the WebFetch returned only a headline. The consequence frame is real; the consequence evidence is a tagline.

On required-capital profile, the low rung is the "GPT-4 summarizes sales calls and integrates with HubSpot" pass pattern, where the failure mechanism is that the company is fundable by any Tier-1 generalist with a $3M seed and I am structurally over-resourced for the deal. That same pattern walks straight through Thomas's dossier ? no proprietary model, explicit dependency on Anthropic and OpenAI computer-use APIs, two-person team (where one of the two is the AI persona itself), and zero capital deployment plan that would absorb a **Commonwealth Fusion**-style $115M Series A. Thomas does not need my money the way **Genesis AI** (2025) needed the $105M co-led seed; Thomas needs a YC check and an audience.

On technical and capital risk that repels consensus VCs, the compounding question over 18 months is whether agent reliability for sustained multi-week operation of a real business crosses a usable threshold ? and whether Thomas controls any part of that stack. The dossier is explicit: model and platform dependency on third-party LLMs, no proprietary training, no fine-tuned vertical agent disclosed. That means Thomas is downstream of capability gains made by Anthropic and OpenAI ? exactly the wrapper failure mode I flagged in my red flags. **Cognition** (2024) earned my thesis because it built stack-deep against software-engineering agents; Thomas, on the evidence in the dossier, is a persona layer on top of someone else's computer-use API.

On founder defies expert consensus, the counterfactual for a tier flip is straightforward: if "Human Thomas" had publicly contradicted a named position in the agent or autonomy research community ? a benchmark dispute, a published "we were wrong about X" post, a thesis-update thread ? Thomas would jump a tier. What the dossier actually offers is biographically interesting but consensus-shaped: game bots at 13, NeurIPS at 18, $40k/month freelance scaled and then automated. The freelancer-automates-himself move is the most thesis-relevant fact in the entire dossier because it shows Thomas the human cannibalizing Thomas the human's own income ? a learning-rate signal more than a defied-expert signal. I score the defied-expert dimension at the bio-only floor and let the cannibalization land in learning rate.

On founder learning rate, the single-anchor parallel is **Slash** (2024), where I wrote a $100M Series C into a YC fintech because the documented pivot (the Kanye-West origin story) proved a founder who updates publicly under pressure. Thomas has a smaller version of the same shape ? a founder who built a $40k/month freelance practice and then deliberately destroyed it by automating the role. That is a real pivot, on the record, with the right directional sign (toward harder problems, away from cash flow). It does not yet rise to **Slash** because there is no second pivot, no public thesis update on the agent stack, and no evidence of Thomas-the-human revising the "AI founder" framing in response to a real-world failure. The signal exists; it is not yet compounding.

My 36-month forecast: Thomas does not operate a real autonomously-run company at meaningful scale by May 2029. The most probable path is a quiet pivot into selling "the AI founder" as a SaaS product to solo operators and indie hackers ? a Synthesia-adjacent persona-as-a-service play landing somewhere in $500K-$2M ARR with a soft acqui-hire by an agent platform (Replit-class, an Anthropic agent surface, or a Manus successor) before the next YC winter batch. The mechanism producing that forecast is the dimensional signature: maximum-consequence framing colliding with minimum-capital, wrapper-shaped execution produces brand-equity outcomes, not labor-displacement outcomes, and brand equity in agents without a proprietary stack gets absorbed.

The strongest case against my forecast is the **Slash** (2024) exception in reverse. **Slash** violated my consequence-magnitude prior ? consumer business banking is not civilizational ? and I still wrote the $100M check because the documented founder pivot proved a learning rate that overrode the consequence shortfall. Thomas inverts that: the consequence frame is enormous (entrepreneurship-as-labor collapsing) and the founder has one documented pivot (the freelancer auto-cannibalization). A disciplined application of the **Slash** override would say: write a small check, let the founder learning rate compound, and accept that the dossier silence on traction is pre-seed noise. The reason that argument loses on the current evidence: **Slash** had revenue and a working consumer product when I wrote the check, and Thomas has neither a retrievable follower count on @madebythomasai nor a single named operated company on madebythomas.ai. The condition that flips my verdict is concrete and verifiable: Thomas publishes a named, legally incorporated company with six consecutive months of agent-operated revenue above $50K/month, audited by anyone credible. Until then this is a tagline with a NeurIPS bio attached, and I pass.
