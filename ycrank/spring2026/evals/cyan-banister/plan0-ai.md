# PLAN0 AI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

PLAN0 AI is the structurally interesting shape of an "invisible system" thesis — a Bloomberg of construction attacking the opacity of localized trade costs — built by founders whose biographies look like finance-career polish rather than crucible. Arash Barati's resume runs through Drake Real Estate Partners and Explora Ventures; the dossier registers no homelessness, no failed prior company, no survival story. That mismatch — right thesis shape, wrong founder forge, in a category where Provision already has $7M USD — is the entire evaluation.

PLAN0 AI's lowest dimension is the Founder's Biographical Forge, and the ladder is unforgiving. James Proud at Substrate sits at the top — I literally tattooed that company on my spine because Proud was "forged in fire and came out unbroken," a founder whose biography left him no choice but to fight ASML. The bottom rung is the founder I described in my Pass Example: ex-Google, "I saw the opportunity," credentialed but uncrucible. Arash Barati of PLAN0 AI sits closer to the bottom rung than the top — his trajectory through a real estate PE shop and an Ibanez family office is exactly the credentialed-bystander pattern, just dressed in real-estate quant rather than Big Tech. And Shervin Barati, the CTO of PLAN0 AI, has no verifiable LinkedIn, no GitHub, no public domain footprint at all per WebSearch May 2026 — the shared surname suggests a sibling co-founder, which is fine, but I cannot evaluate a forge I cannot see. When I ask "tell me your story, not the business," PLAN0 AI's dossier returns a CV.

PLAN0 AI's highest dimension is Invisible System Made Visible, and even here the placement is mid-ladder, not high. Affirm sits at the top of this rung — Max Levchin attacking credit card opacity that extracts value from ordinary consumers who cannot read a billing cycle. Generic enterprise productivity SaaS sits at the bottom — no hidden tax, no asymmetry. PLAN0 AI lands above the middle because preconstruction estimating is genuinely opaque: 2+ week manual turnarounds, fragmented localized cost data, and the "Bloomberg of construction" framing per the YC page maps directly onto my pattern. But the people on the other side of PLAN0 AI's transparency are real estate developers, general contractors, and investors — already-resourced professional counterparties, not the homeless teenager I once was navigating systems she could not see. The opacity is real; the moral charge that pulls me into Ownwell or Affirm is not.

Contrarian Conviction in Hard Spaces is where PLAN0 AI fails the room-of-VCs test. Anduril sits at the top of this ladder — defense tech so politically toxic in 2017 SV that writing the check required social courage. The bottom rung is the consensus AI wrapper I described in my Pass Example. PLAN0 AI sits closer to that bottom rung: Robotics & Automation News benchmarked six AI estimating tools in February 2026, Provision raised $7M USD for the identical "AI co-pilot for pre-construction estimates" wedge per BetaKit, and Togal, Beam, and Kreo are already in market with public pricing. PLAN0 AI is not entering a stigmatized space — it is entering a contested one with less capital than the leader.

Infrastructure-Grade Defensibility at Inflection places PLAN0 AI between HQ Trivia at the bottom — entertainment with no compounding moat — and Flock Safety mid-ladder, where each new camera makes the network harder to displace. PLAN0 AI asserts a CV training set on "tens of thousands of examples" plus a proprietary localized cost database per the YC page, and a flywheel could plausibly emerge as estimates refine the database. But CoStar already owns the underlying real-estate data feed, Autodesk owns the plan files, and the dossier flags zero IP, zero switching-cost evidence, zero named pilot. Cascading Real-World Impact sits near the bottom — Uber expanded transportation to people who could never hail a yellow cab, while PLAN0 AI sells faster cost intelligence to developers and investors. There is a thin second-order story about housing supply if preconstruction friction drops, but it is a stretch I would not narrate to an LP with a straight face.

The 36-month forecast for PLAN0 AI is a $20-50M strategic acqui-hire by Procore, Trimble, or CoStar somewhere in 2028, or a quiet drift to $500K-$1.5M ARR while Provision and Togal consolidate the category. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: PLAN0 AI's thesis shape is good enough to attract a strategic, but Arash Barati's biography lacks the forge to outlast a multi-year capital and incumbent battle. Founders without crucible exit early — that is not a moral judgment, it is what I have watched happen across HQ Trivia, GameCrush, and a dozen others where the founders were smart but not forged.

The strongest case against my pass is the Substrate exception. James Proud was a finance-adjacent operator before Substrate; my conviction came not from his prior crucible alone but from the infrastructural ambition at a CHIPS-Act inflection. A bull on PLAN0 AI argues the same shape applies: Arash Barati's quant-finance background is genuinely rare in construction tech, the "quant finance inspired DNN" forecasting layer is differentiated from Togal's pure CV takeoff, the preconstruction market grows from $2.38B to $5.65B by 2032 per GII Research, and Provision's $7M USD is a head start measured in months, not years. That case stings — the Bloomberg-of-construction framing is the kind of thesis I have written checks on. But Substrate's tattoo went on my spine because Proud had been broken and rebuilt; PLAN0 AI's dossier offers no such evidence on either Barati, the CTO is unverifiable, and the category was already consensus-benchmarked by trade press in February 2026. The condition that flips my verdict: signed LOIs with three or more named developers or GCs showing real product pull, plus one founder interview where Arash Barati tells me a story that earns the forge — until then, PLAN0 AI is a pass.
