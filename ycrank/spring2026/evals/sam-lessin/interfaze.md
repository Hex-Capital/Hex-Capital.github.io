# Interfaze -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Interfaze published its entire architecture in a peer-reviewed paper — arXiv:2602.04101, accepted at IEEE CAI 2026 — which means every engineering team at Google, Amazon, and Microsoft can read exactly how the hybrid DNN/CNN routing system works over lunch. I've seen founders do a lot of things that made me scratch my head, but handing your differentiation to your largest competitors in an academic journal is a special kind of unforced error. Google already sells Document AI. Amazon already sells Textract. Azure already has Document Intelligence. These are billion-dollar cloud divisions with enterprise distribution Interfaze cannot replicate, and now they have Interfaze's architectural blueprint for free. The custom web scraping engine for bot-protected sites is the one piece that stays proprietary, but Firecrawl raised $16.2M to do exactly that one thing, and Apify pulled $13.3M in 2024 revenue doing it at scale. Interfaze is bringing a unified API to a knife fight where every opponent has a bigger knife for their specific task.

YC tagged Interfaze with "Deep Learning, Developer Tools, Generative AI" — and I challenge anyone to name three hotter consensus categories in Spring 2026. This is not a mispriced bet. This is the most consensus-priced category in venture right now. I've been saying for two years that AI seed investing has been a washout, and Interfaze is the archetype of why. The bull case is "we bundled OCR, scraping, STT, and classification behind one OpenAI-compatible API at $1.50 per million input tokens." You know what that sounds like? A feature. Every cloud provider on earth is looking at that value proposition and thinking "we should add a unified endpoint." When I backed Solana at four cents, the entire market had dismissed alt-L1s — the risk was in the price and the upside was uncapped. Interfaze is the opposite: everybody loves AI developer tools, which means the price already reflects the upside and the downside is hiding.

Yoeven Khemlani brings legitimate operational chops — Imperial College London, senior technical lead at UOB, and a prior prop-tech startup (Stayr) that claimed $1M ARR in year one with a possible acquisition. Harsha Vardhan Khurdula adds ML research depth from Purdue with publications in CV and NLP. These are competent, credentialed people. That's exactly the problem. Interfaze is a sensible idea built by sensible people — "let's unify fragmented AI perception APIs into one model." Nobody hears that pitch and thinks "that's fucking crazy." Everybody hears it and thinks "yeah, that makes sense." When I write checks, I want a very reasonable person pitching me something that makes me uncomfortable. Interfaze makes me comfortable, which makes me bored. And the co-founders have no visible pre-existing relationship — no shared employer, no shared university, no multi-cycle observation window. Yoeven was in Singapore building prop-tech; Harsha was at Purdue doing NLP research. I've learned that my best returns come from founders I've watched through three companies, not from cofounders who found each other through the startup ecosystem.

Interfaze fails the AI kill test more cleanly than almost anything I've looked at this batch. Pure software. API-only delivery. No physical assets, no protocol-level network effects, no regulatory moat, no creator audience. Compare this to Metropolis, which I seeded and is now worth roughly $5B — they manage actual parking infrastructure that no LLM can replicate. Interfaze manages API calls to specialized models, which is something any well-funded DevOps team can assemble from open-source components: Surya for OCR, Whisper for STT, Firecrawl for scraping. The 98-99% structured output accuracy claim on interfaze.ai lacks independent verification or disclosed methodology. The architectural moat is documented in a public paper. Software companies that had moats are learning they aren't moats anymore — and Interfaze is starting without one.

The strongest argument for Interfaze is the Stripe analogy: Stripe unified fragmented payment APIs into one developer-friendly layer, and the bundling itself became the moat through developer lock-in and network effects. An advocate would point to the Groq partnership, the Vercel AI Accelerator participation, and the OpenAI-compatible API as early signs of ecosystem embedding. The $1.5M raised pre-YC ($500K from Antler, $1M from Ada Ventures) shows reasonable capital discipline for a team of five. And the IEEE-published architecture does represent genuine technical sophistication — this isn't a GPT wrapper. But Stripe's moat was financial regulation, money transmission licenses, and the operational complexity of moving actual money across borders. Interfaze's "bundling" has no equivalent structural barrier. If Interfaze showed me $500K ARR with 150%+ net revenue retention from enterprise customers who've built their document pipelines on the API and can't switch — that's when I'd reconsider. Lock-in you can measure, not architectural elegance you can publish.

Capital discipline is the one dimension where Interfaze doesn't actively concern me. $1.5M raised, team of five, consumption-based pricing — that's lean enough. But capital discipline on a melting ice cube doesn't save you; it just means you melt slower. The two dimensions that kill this deal are consensus mispricing and structural durability. Interfaze is a consensus-hot AI developer tool in a category I've publicly called a washout, competing against cloud incumbents who can replicate the value proposition as a product feature, with an architecture published in a paper anyone can read. Pass.
