# Petrarch -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Petrarch is a labor-and-capital brokerage business wearing marketplace clothing, in a genuinely large adjacent market (Scale AI at $1.5B ARR proves the AI-data demand is real), run by three undergraduates with no data-brokerage specific knowledge. That pairing — the highest dimension is pivot-room-in-a-big-market, the lowest is leverage architecture — is the diagnostic signature. Petrarch is trying to arbitrage bankruptcy trustees and legacy wind-downs into AI-lab buyers, which is a business where every unit of supply requires a phone call, a lawyer, and an anonymization pipeline. That is not the architecture I fund.

**Twitter (2007)** is my anchor for permissionless-media leverage — anyone posts, the platform charges nothing, marginal cost of replication is zero. Petrarch is the structural inverse. To move one dataset, the Petrarch team must reach a bankruptcy trustee (permission), execute an asset-purchase agreement (permission), diligence chain-of-title against GDPR/CCPA (permission), anonymize (labor), then outbound-sell to a Scale-AI-buyer segment (labor). The dossier's own inferred GTM says "direct outbound to AI labs on the buy-side and to bankruptcy trustees on the sell-side" — that is capital-and-labor leverage, requiring permission at every node. **Anchorage Digital** is the only case in my ledger where I let permission-heavy architecture through, and that override was underwritten by seven years of pre-consensus crypto conviction since **MetaStable** (2014). I have no such accumulation into "distressed dataset acquisition." The exception does not transfer to Petrarch.

**Perplexity (2022)** is my anchor for a single-sided product I funded anyway — the marketplace-compounding criterion waived because criterion #1 and criterion #3 both cleared top-quartile. Petrarch fails that override test: the supply side is *episodic and non-recurring* by construction — a startup dies once, sells its dataset once, disappears. There is no Nth-user-makes-N-1-user-more-valuable loop; there is a catalog of one-shot orphan assets. Ian Lee's construction background from CBRE and DPR sourcing Google/Apple projects — the strongest operational credential in the Petrarch dossier — is the pattern of one-off asset acquisition, not compounding network mechanics. That maps to episodic brokerage, not to the **Uber** or **OpenSea** compounding curve.

Sudhish Swain's ISEF 2023 grand award for computational modeling of chemotherapy metabolism is impressive science; it is not data-brokerage specific knowledge. Samuel Hahn is a Harvard undergraduate in Economics and History. Ian Lee's operational depth is in construction procurement, not AI data-licensing. Compare this to **Stack Overflow** (2008), where Atwood and Spolsky were shipping developer tools for developers they *were*, or **Replit** (2023), where Amjad Masad had been building Repl.it since 2011 — that is what I mean by non-trainable specific knowledge in a domain. Petrarch has three intelligent generalists who identified a market opportunity, which is the archetype of the "ex-FAANG PM sees TAM" pattern I flag as a red flag under different vocabulary.

The market is not the problem. AI labs paying for proprietary training data is a real and growing wallet — the $4.9B labeling number is a floor, not a ceiling, and IP-licensing settlements through 2024-2025 confirm the buyer intent. Petrarch has pivot-room here: from distressed-dataset brokerage into general data-licensing agency, into synthetic-data augmentation, into bankruptcy-asset advisory more broadly. That is the one dimension where the dossier reads well — the market can absorb 2-3 founder pivots without refinancing.

Founder trio reads clean but thin. No integrity red flags, no self-promotion about ethics, no contested prior ventures — Petrarch just has no signal either direction because two of the three are still undergraduates. Default is neutral; I hold there.

**Forecast, 36 months:** Petrarch is either wound down under $500K in lifetime revenue, pivoted into a general data-licensing consultancy that looks nothing like the current pitch, or absorbed by an existing bankruptcy-asset firm (Hilco, Sherwood Partners) that added an AI-data vertical. The mechanism: episodic supply plus labor-heavy diligence plus undergraduate execution against a legally hairy multi-sided market where the incumbent buyer (Scale AI) can vertically integrate any successful supply channel Petrarch surfaces.

The bull case that would sting: if the AI-lab demand curve is as steep as Scale AI's 97% YoY suggests, and if one wound-down mid-tier AI company has a genuinely proprietary training corpus — call it 100K rows of human-graded reasoning traces from a failed Anthropic competitor — then Petrarch as the sole broker on that transaction could clear a $5M+ take-rate on one deal and return the pre-seed check ten times over. The **Anchorage Digital** precedent whispers here: permission-heavy architecture is not automatically disqualifying when a category timing call is strong enough. That bull case dies on two facts in the Petrarch dossier: no named acquired dataset, no named AI-lab counterparty, no exclusive-licensing framework disclosed, and no founder with prior experience closing that specific transaction type. **Anchorage** had John Bruzas and Diogo Monica with regulated-infrastructure specific knowledge; Petrarch has undergraduates. The flip condition is falsifiable and narrow: one signed exclusive dataset acquisition >100K rows with clean chain-of-title paired with a named AI-lab buyer LOI, before the seed converts. Absent that, this is a pass on architecture, not on ambition.
