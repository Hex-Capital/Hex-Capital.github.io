# Netter -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Netter is a sane French team building a sane French idea in a category every other LP at demo day will also be funding — Hamzaoui's Mines Paris and ENS Ulm pedigree is the strongest asset, and Netter's "120+ connectors plus chat-driven workflows for mid-market" is the weakest, because that sentence describes roughly forty seed-stage companies in 2026. The signature here is sane founder, sane idea — which is exactly the profile I find boring. I want sane person, *insane* idea. Netter is sane person, sensible idea, and sensible ideas are commodities.

On consensus mispricing, my ladder runs Solana at four cents (HIGH — every smart crypto person told me Ethereum had won; the asymmetry was embarrassing) down through Slack (MID — hot deal, but the primitive override saved it) to the YC AI-wrapper batch (LOW — Adobe will add that feature next quarter). Netter sits one rung above the AI-wrapper floor. The dossier itself flags "DataSci.Pro YC X25" pursuing the same wedge, plus Hex at $96M raised, Fivetran at $565M+, Palantir Foundry doing $2.8B in revenue with the actual ontology pitch, and Workato at ~$100M ARR. This is not a category mainstream VCs have written off — this is a category mainstream VCs are *crowding into*. The risk is not baked into the price; the upside is. What if they're right? They're not contrarian. There's no "they."

On primitive creation, the ladder runs Venmo (HIGH — created a verb, peer-to-peer mobile payments did not exist before) to Patreon (MID — extended patronage into a software primitive) to "better CRM" (LOW — execution play in an existing market). Netter pins between MID and LOW, leaning LOW. Carrefour, Tesco, and Auchan plugging their TMS, ERP, and loyalty data into a chat-driven ontology layer is not a new behavior — it is Palantir Foundry priced for the mid-market, which is a legitimate GTM wedge but definitionally not a primitive. Nobody will say "Netter me the dashboard" the way they say "Venmo me." If your category description requires the words "data autopilot for the real economy" to differentiate, you are competing on framing, not behavior.

On the AI kill test, my ladder runs Metropolis (HIGH — physical parking infrastructure, software amplifies but cannot replace) to Patreon (MID — creator relationships have some moat) to the Adobe-for-AI wrapper (LOW — incumbent adds feature, you die). Netter is closer to LOW than I am comfortable with. Airbyte already advertises 550+ connectors versus Netter's 120+. Snowflake, Databricks, and Fivetran have every incentive and every distribution channel to bolt a chat-driven ontology layer onto what they already sell to the same Carrefour CIO. Software companies that had moats are learning they aren't moats anymore — and Netter does not even claim a moat yet. The "living ontology" is a marketing phrase, not a defensible artifact.

On the sanity-insanity calibration, the ladder runs Raj at Solana (HIGH — engineer I'd watched through three companies, calm, pursuing something most smart people called insane) through Butterfield at Slack (MID — proven founder, sensible idea) to incubator-matched co-founders (LOW). Hamzaoui from Mines Paris + ENS Ulm gives Netter genuine technical sanity — that pedigree is real, it produced Mistral and Datadog founders. But Ladroue's pre-Netter operator track record is unverifiable in public sources, and the *idea* is not insane. There is no "fucking crazy" here. Sane plus sane is a B-grade outcome at best. On capital discipline, the ladder runs Gusto (HIGH) to Slack (MID, raised aggressively but earned it) to Allbirds (LOW — factory-farm DTC). Four-person team, zero open jobs, founder-led demo flow off Ladroue's calendar — Netter looks lean enough to clear the default bar. This is the only dimension where Netter is unambiguously not embarrassing.

My 36-month forecast: Netter reaches €1–3M ARR converting two or three of its EU mid-market pilots (most likely the Carrefour and Auchan logos), fails to raise a competitive Series A against the Hex/Airbyte/Foundry pricing pressure, and gets acqui-hired by a French strategic — Dassault Systèmes, Cegid, or a Doctolib-adjacent vertical SaaS — for €15–40M. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: sane founders execute sane plans into sane outcomes, and the EU mid-market data integration category supports a respectable acqui-hire but not a fund-returner because the moat thesis ("living ontology") cannot survive the incumbent kill test.

The bull case stings, and I'll steel-man it: Hamzaoui is from the same talent pool that produced Mistral's Mensch, EU mid-market is genuinely underserved by Palantir's enterprise pricing and Fivetran's no-app-layer wedge, and *if* Carrefour and Tesco are paid deployments rather than POCs, then Netter has secured top-10-global-retailer distribution at pre-seed on a YC check — that is real. The vertical ontology, if it accumulates schemas for nursing-home AR and logistics route-load over five years, becomes the actual moat the way Foundry's did. The Slack precedent matters: a sane founder, hot category, eventual primitive. The dossier itself wins the argument against the bull case, though — there is no public press release confirming any of the three logos is paid, no funding announcement, no founder I've watched across three companies, and the founder commercial track record is opaque enough that I cannot underwrite GTM execution. The flip condition is verifiable and specific: a signed seven-figure ARR contract with Carrefour or Tesco, with switching-cost evidence that the ontology layer creates real lock-in. Until that lands, Netter is a competent EU acqui-hire candidate, and competent acqui-hire candidates do not return funds. Pass.
