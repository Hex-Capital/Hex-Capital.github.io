# Amulet -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Amulet's signature is a wrapper on commodity CRM APIs staffed by two ex-Meta ads engineers -- highest relative placement on capital-fit (they need almost nothing, which is the wrong kind of match to me), lowest placement on technical risk that repels consensus VCs. Nithik Bala came out of Meta Core Ads Growth and Varun Puru came off Facebook product; the dossier gives me a team size of 2 building agents against a $3.61B TAM that Research and Markets says grows to $8.3B by 2030. That is the shape of a company any Tier-1 generalist can fund, and the shape I am structurally over-resourced for.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the consequence-magnitude rung I keep in my head when a dossier lands, and Amulet is nowhere near it -- Amulet is closer to my Pass Example of the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer than to any anchor I hold. The mechanism that made me write the $50M first check into OpenAI was a success-state where an entire category of cognitive labor gets restructured; Amulet's stated success-state is that CSMs at mid-market SaaS companies get their churn-risk flags drafted for them. Even if Amulet wins completely, the Gainsight/ChurnZero/Vitally regime does not collapse -- it consolidates and re-badges, exactly as Gainsight did when it acquired Staircase AI in July 2024. I quoted Fortune (2026-03-06) that $15T of U.S. GDP is labor; Amulet is not pricing that displacement, it is pricing a Gainsight seat.

**Range Fuels** (~2007) failed on capital adequacy misjudgment, and the failure mode I remember is not that the science was wrong but that the deployment scale was so extreme that consensus VCs would not follow -- exactly the shape that made it mine. Amulet inverts that pattern in the opposite direction: the dossier describes an agent connecting to CRM, support, product-usage, and communication APIs, which is a build any competent engineer -- and Nithik Bala and Varun Puru clearly qualify -- ships in a quarter. No physics miracle, no plant, no fleet, no foundation-model training run. Staircase AI raised only $5M before Gainsight tucked it in; that is the price band Amulet is quietly playing in, and it is a band my $3.5B fund (TechCrunch, 2025-02-24) cannot deploy into meaningfully.

Founder defiance: over 18 months, this dimension compounds through founders publicly picking a fight with the incumbent orthodoxy of their category, and I do not see the ingredients in Amulet's dossier. Nithik Bala's public footprint is a LinkedIn as "Nithik B." SWE @ Meta and Georgia Tech; Varun Puru does not even disambiguate on LinkedIn. Neither founder has published a thesis about why the Gainsight architecture is wrong, why human CSMs are the wrong unit of account, or why post-sales should be re-drawn -- the kinds of instigator claims my TED Talk framework rewards. Ex-Meta Core Ads is a competent engineering profile; it is not a defied-expert profile, and Amulet's pitch as summarized in the YC entry does not name a heresy.

Learning rate: to flip Amulet's placement here to the **Slash** (2024) tier, the dossier would need a documented pivot, a public "we were wrong about X" moment, or a prior sold company from Bala or Puru. It contains none of these -- the pair are first-time founders roughly 2 years out of undergrad-to-Meta pipelines, with launch year 2026 and no pivot to reason about. I do not aggressively penalize young founders for absence of pivot evidence, but I also cannot grant the Slash exception without the Kanye-moment analog, and Amulet has not supplied one.

Counterfactual on capital: for Amulet's capital-profile score to jump a tier, the roadmap would need to name a >$50M line item -- a proprietary post-sales foundation model trained on cross-customer signal, a fleet of embedded agents with hardware, something that forces a Khosla-sized check. Instead the dossier notes the amulet.so landing page returns HTTP 403, hiring is False, and the go-to-market is unspecified. Two engineers plus commodity LLM API calls plus CRM integrations equals a company that reaches $1M ARR on $5-10M, and at that scale I add nothing and any seed generalist adds the same.

Forecast, 36 months: Amulet is acquihired by Gainsight or Vitally for under $25M in an all-stock or mixed transaction that looks structurally identical to the Staircase AI (July 2024) tuck-in, or Amulet is dead and Bala and Puru are back at a Meta-tier employer or building their next YC company. The mechanism is exactly the incumbent tuck-in substitution the dossier itself flags: Gainsight already ran this play once at $5M of Staircase seed capital, and Vitally has the $30M Series B (March 2023) balance sheet to run it again against a two-person team with no distribution.

The strongest case against my pass rides on my own **Slash** (2024) and **Glean** exceptions -- YC pedigree plus Diana Hu as partner plus my Fortune (2025-07-01) claim that AI does 80% of economically valuable work by 2030 says post-sales CSM labor is exactly the $15T bucket I keep pointing at, and Amulet is a wedge into it. That case stings because I have written the Slash-shaped check before. It loses on the specifics: Slash had a documented Kanye pivot demonstrating learning rate, Glean had Fortune-500 capital scale and Arvind Jain's Google-search domain depth, and DoorDash had a founder-ambition frame in the dossier that Amulet's YC entry does not carry. Amulet gives me none of the three overrides -- no pivot, no capital scale forcing my check size, no consequence framing beyond "flag churn." I flip to Invest if Bala and Puru surface a signed LOI with a named Fortune 500 CRO committing to replace 20+ human CSMs with Amulet agents at a defined seat-price, because that is the labor-displacement anchor I have been asking the AI-agent category to produce; absent that, this is the 30-second pass from my Pass Example.
