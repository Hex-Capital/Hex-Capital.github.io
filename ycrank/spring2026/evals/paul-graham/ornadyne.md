# Ornadyne -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Ornadyne is what you get when two best-friends-since-middle-school aerospace engineers point themselves at the ugliest procurement maze in the country and the dumbest-sounding product line in the batch -- "robot birds for surveillance" -- with the CEO, Geourg Kivijian, having literally written his Cal Poly MS thesis on variable-flapping ornithopters before the company existed. The signature is high-schlep, high-genealogy, low-stunt: serious aerospace credentials and a long pre-company obsession, but no cereal-box moment, no Hacker News demo video, no Brian-Armstrong-rebuilds-the-wallet stunt to prove animalhood under pressure.

The HIGH rung on the schlep axis is **Coinbase** (2012) -- bitcoin compliance was a regulatory grind nobody else in the batch wanted to touch, and the unpleasantness itself became the moat. Ornadyne's schlep is structurally the same shape: ITAR export controls, multi-year DoD qualification cycles, SBIR paperwork, flight certification, and a customer set that takes years to close its first program office. AeroVironment, the public incumbent at ~$717M FY24 revenue that built DARPA's Nano Hummingbird, has been routing around flapping-wing for a decade because rotor and fixed-wing are easier to ship and sell. Where Ornadyne diverges from the Coinbase analogy is that Armstrong had a working wallet running on Android before his rejection appeal; Ornadyne's dossier has 2+ years of AIAA/IEEE publications and a thesis but no public flight-hour data, no fielded unit, no signed pilot, just $1M raised and a Launch post. The schlep is real and the founders are pointed at it. Whether they'll grind through the first program office the way Armstrong ground through FinCEN is the open question.

The LOW rung on animal resourcefulness is the polished MBA team I pass on every batch -- the deck-first, demo-never, "we'll hire engineers post-funding" pattern from my pass example. Ornadyne does not match that failure shape, but it does not match Airbnb's cereal-box shape either. Geourg Kivijian published flapping-wing papers at AIAA and IEEE over 2+ years and shipped a 130-page MS thesis on variable-flapping ornithopters; Armen Arakelyan helped machine parts for a USC rocket that hit 144 km altitude and then went to Starship. Those are real shipped artifacts in the academic-and-hardware register. What's missing is the unscalable revenue stunt or the post-rejection rebuild -- the specific pattern that flips conviction. Their pre-funding behavior reads like competent engineers doing competent engineer things, which is closer to Dropbox's Drew Houston (demo on HN) than to Airbnb's Chesky (sold 1,000 cereal boxes at $40 each), but without the public demo-video moment that flipped my read on Houston.

Mechanism analysis on the organic-idea axis: Kivijian has been building flapping-wing things for years -- not because he was a soldier who needed close-range ISR, but because he found the physics beautiful. That's a different organic than Drew Houston's USB-stick organic at Dropbox (2007); it's an engineer's obsession rather than a user's pain. Over 18 months this compounds well on the technical side -- he will out-iterate any team that started caring about ornithopters in 2026 -- but compounds poorly on the customer-fit side, because Ornadyne's actual buyers are program officers in Crystal City who don't care about the elegance of variable-flapping kinematics. The 18-month tell will be whether Kivijian learns to talk to a JPO the way he talks to a thesis committee.

Counterfactual on hacker genealogy: Ornadyne would jump a tier on this axis if either founder had a public GitHub trail or an open-source flight controller they'd shipped pre-company. The dossier says GitHub: not found for both. What Ornadyne has instead is aerospace-hacker lineage -- NASA JPL Mars Sample Return, Astrolab, SpaceX Starship, USC RPL's 144 km record, a Cal Poly thesis defended in public. My essays over-index on Lisp and software hacker markers because that's the **Viaweb** (1995) tribe I came from, but the underlying signal is "did this person ship something hard before anyone paid them," and Kivijian and Arakelyan ship hard things. I am willing to extend genealogy credit to aerospace shipping. Not as much as I'd extend to a public RSS spec author, but credit.

Single-anchor parallel on cofounder configuration: **Reddit** (2005) -- Huffman and Ohanian were dorm roommates at UVA, and the long prior relationship was load-bearing for the company surviving its early identity crises. Ornadyne's Kivijian and Arakelyan have been best friends since middle school. That is more years of working trust than Huffman/Ohanian had when I funded them, in a domain (hardware) where co-founder conflict is more expensive than in software because every bad week costs a physical prototype. Complementary coverage too -- aero/systems on one side, manufacturing/hardware on the other. This is the strongest single signal in the dossier.

My 36-month forecast for Ornadyne: alive, $3-8M in SBIR Phase II and program-of-record contracts, one or two fielded demonstration units with a forward-deployed unit somewhere uncomfortable, a Series A in the $8-15M range led by a defense-adjacent fund (a16z American Dynamism or Lux), but not yet at commercial scale -- the kind of company that looks like it's working in 2029 without yet being obviously a Stripe-shaped outcome. The mechanism producing this forecast is exactly the schlep-as-moat dynamic from Coinbase (2012): defense procurement is slow, but once Ornadyne is on a program, the switching cost protects them from the inevitable second flapping-wing startup in 2027.

The bear case that stings: Ornadyne's founders are credentialed, not feral. JPL and SpaceX are prestigious shops, but they're shops -- the credential pattern is closer to my Red Flag of "MBA program or consulting firm" than I'd like to admit, just transposed into aerospace. The dossier contains no pre-funding stunt, no rejection-and-rebuild moment, no instance of Kivijian or Arakelyan doing something a normal aerospace engineer would not do to keep Ornadyne alive. My "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012) thesis says the unpleasantness is the moat -- but only if the founders run *toward* the unpleasantness, and a polished JPL/SpaceX resume is the resume of someone who has been selected by institutions that route around unpleasantness. None of the Known Exceptions clearly apply: this isn't Altman-grade force-of-personality, it isn't a re-funded YC alum, and the cereal-box stunt is absent. The verifiable condition that would flip me from Invest to Pass: if by month 18 Ornadyne has zero signed DoD contracts and Kivijian is still publishing AIAA papers instead of cold-emailing program officers, then he's still a researcher, and the bear case won. The condition that confirms Invest: one signed Phase II or program-office LOI before the Series A, naming a specific JPO, by end of 2027.
