# Archal -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Archal is a right-schlep, wrong-hackers company. The highest dimension on my ladder is the schlep zone -- spinning up stateful clones of Stripe, GitHub, Slack, Google Workspace, Linear, and Supabase, drift-tested against the live APIs, is exactly the kind of tedious reverse-engineering work most YC applicants in Summer 2026 are unconsciously routing around. The lowest dimension is hacker genealogy -- Noah Song and Aidan Tiruvan have no public GitHub repos surfaced anywhere in the dossier, which is conspicuous for two people claiming to maintain six high-fidelity SaaS clones. That pairing -- real schlep, invisible hackers -- is Archal's analytical signature, and it is the wrong half of the **Coinbase** (2012) template.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the right HIGH-rung comparison for Archal on the schlep axis, and the mechanism is the same: incumbents won't touch the unpleasant work because it conflicts with their existing SKU. Braintrust just raised $80M at an $800M post in February 2026 to score traces; building stateful Stripe clones is engineering work that pulls them off their roadmap. Archal lists six integrations on archal.ai (Jun 2026), each requiring continuous drift-tracking against a live third-party API and each carrying ToS exposure -- this is the kind of grind I wrote about in "Schlep Blindness" (January 2012), and it is the asset, not the risk. Where Archal diverges from Coinbase is the moment Brian Armstrong rebuilt his Android Bitcoin wallet to overturn his YC rejection -- Archal's dossier names no equivalent artifact, no pre-funding stunt, nothing that would let me describe either founder as an animal.

The MBA-team pass example from my own playbook is what the hacker-genealogy axis surfaces here, and Archal sits closer to it than I want. The failure mechanism in that pass is that the founders are technical-by-credential rather than technical-by-shipping -- a CS degree without a GitHub trail, a market thesis without a demo video on Hacker News. Tiruvan's Google Scholar profile (randomized algorithms, convex optimization, per scholar.google.com user weutgQUAAAAJ) reads as academic, not hacker; Song's prior role teaching grade-school programming and robotics at Illinois Tech 2017?2020 is not a substitute for a shipped open-source artifact. **Reddit** (2005) had Aaron Swartz co-authoring the RSS specification before the first batch; **Stripe** (2010) had Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize. Archal has two LinkedIn pages and a website. The pattern I avoid is not active here -- there are no MBAs -- but the affirmative hacker tribal markers I over-index on are missing.

**Mechanism analysis -- animal resourcefulness over 18 months:** The compounding question on this dimension is whether Archal ships something Hacker News notices before the seed runway burns. Two founders, no disclosed revenue, no Product Hunt launch, no user count, no signed design partner named in the dossier (archal.ai, WebSearch, Jun 2026). The way this fails over 18 months is mundane: LangSmith Sandboxes (shipped March 2026, per Latitude) eats the obvious wedge, and Archal's clones never reach enough fidelity across six SaaS APIs to convert a paying CI customer before Braintrust's $80M war chest funds a competing SKU. Animals leave tracks; the dossier surfaces none. Default 11/35.

**Counterfactual -- what would flip organic-pain to a higher tier:** Tiruvan's listed tenure as ML Research Engineer & Intern at Scale AI (ZoomInfo, Jun 2026) is the only thread connecting either Archal founder to the agent-eval problem from the inside. To flip this dimension into the **Dropbox** (2007)-tier "I forgot my USB stick" territory, the dossier would need a specific story: an Archal-built internal eval harness at Scale that failed when an agent wrote to a production database, a forked open-source mock library Tiruvan maintained, anything that says "I personally lost a day to this and got angry." Absent that, Archal reads as market-spotted, not pain-organic. Default 10/25.

**Single-anchor parallel -- cofounder configuration vs. Reddit:** Huffman and Ohanian were University of Virginia dorm roommates before **Reddit** (2005); Archal's two founders are an Illinois Tech B.S. graduate (Song) and a current CU Boulder CS+Math student (Tiruvan), with no public data on how they met or how long they've worked together (WebSearch, Jun 2026). Two founders is the right count -- it is not the solo-founder red flag from "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) -- but the relationship is dossier-silent, and the geographic split (Illinois, Colorado) gives me no roommate prior. 5/10.

**The 36-month forecast for Archal:** acqui-hired by LangSmith or Braintrust for under $20M, or quietly dead, because the two-person Archal team cannot maintain stateful clones of Stripe, GitHub, Slack, Google Workspace, Linear, and Supabase against drift faster than Braintrust's $80M Series B can fund a competing sandbox SKU layered on top of trace ingestion they already own. The mechanism producing this is not idea quality -- the wedge is real -- it is the absence of the animal signal that would tell me Archal can out-grind the incumbent's product roadmap.

The bull case I have to dismantle is the one Alexandr Wang's **Scale AI** trajectory makes me take seriously: Archal is sitting on a Schlep Blindness essay in motion, the work is unpleasant enough that LangSmith and Braintrust will route around it the way the rest of the industry routed around data ops in 2016, and Tiruvan came directly from the company that proved that thesis. If Archal is the next Scale-style schlep bet, passing on it because of the missing GitHub trail looks like the same mistake other investors made on Wang. The dossier evidence that wins against this case: Wang dropped out of MIT and shipped the early Scale data-ops operation by hand -- there is a pre-funding artifact, a personally-run grind, an animal moment. The Archal dossier surfaces none of these for either Song or Tiruvan. The Coinbase template requires both the schlep AND the wallet prototype; Archal has the schlep alone. **One verifiable condition flips my verdict:** a public shipped artifact from either Archal founder before the next YC Demo Day -- an open-source SaaS-clone fixture library on GitHub with non-trivial stars, or a named design partner with a Stripe/GitHub-clone scenario running in their CI -- would move this from Pass to Invest in a single line.
