# Standard Signal -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Standard Signal is a founder learning-rate story strapped to a consequence ceiling I cannot pretend not to see -- Michael Royzen built **Phind** (YC S22) into 150M+ searches and is now pivoting an entire identity from AI search into an AI-run hedge fund, which is a real cross-domain jump, but the success-state at the top of that ambition is "another quant fund with a Sharpe 2.0+ target and a $100,000 LP minimum." That is not a category collapsing. That is capital reallocating inside an existing one.

**OpenAI** (2019) is the high rung on consequence because the success-state at maximum ambition was a civilizational rewrite -- that is why I wrote the $50M first VC check without a moat thesis. Standard Signal sits two rungs below: the maximum-ambition state is Standard Signal taking share from Renaissance, Two Sigma, and Numerai (which itself reported 25%+ net returns in 2024 and $500M of JPMorgan capacity per Ainvest). When I ask my own question -- what economic mechanism BREAKS if this works -- the answer is: a handful of discretionary portfolio managers lose their seat. Compare that to the **Impossible Foods** (2011) frame, where animal protein production breaks. Standard Signal's dossier names no incumbent regime that disappears; the closest it gets is "discretionary and traditional quant hedge funds cannot process the breadth." That is a workflow complaint, not a $15T labor-displacement consequence in the Fortune (2026-03-06) framing.

The **KiOR** (~2008) failure mechanism I defended in my 2014 CBS letter was capital-hostile science where the miracles were falsifiable -- catalyst yield, feedstock economics, refinery integration. Standard Signal's dossier has the opposite shape: zero physics, zero biology, zero hardware, two open job listings (ML Research Engineer, Quantitative Researcher), and a solo founder. The capital required to reach the first commercial milestone is whatever it costs to fine-tune a financial LLM and rent execution infrastructure -- which is precisely the SaaS-economics profile my "Technical Risk That Repels Consensus VCs" dimension is designed to filter out. No consensus VC declines Standard Signal because the science is too hard; they decline because a hedge fund is the wrong vehicle for venture equity.

Royzen's profile against my "defies documented expert consensus" lens is mixed in a way that does not help him -- he is defying expert consensus, but inadvertently. He has no Goldman, Citadel, Renaissance, or Two Sigma lineage in the dossier. That is the inverse of the **Glean** Known Exception, where Arvind Jain's ex-Google search credential was the override. Standard Signal cannot run the Glean play because Royzen has no asset-management credential to override with; he is simply a builder walking into a regulated capital allocation business without the labor-cost displacement framing or the named-PI research depth of **Physical Intelligence** (2024). Eighteen months in, this dimension compounds badly: the SEC/CFTC registration, qualified custodian, and compliance hires the dossier flags as missing are exactly the surface where domain ignorance becomes operational drag.

Royzen's prior pivot from **Phind** (YC S22) into Standard Signal is the strongest signal in the dossier and the reason I do not write this off at the 30-second mark. **Slash** (2024) earned my $100M Series C check on the back of a documented Kanye-pivot story; Royzen has a quieter version of the same shape -- he shipped an LLM-trained product at scale (150M+ searches, AWS case study, Paul Graham backing per Humanloop) and chose to leave it for a harder, more capital-rich domain. That is learning-rate behavior. It would matter more if the destination were a category where his AI execution advantage compounded into a civilizational consequence rather than into LP returns.

The required-capital profile is where Standard Signal and my checkbook structurally mismatch -- an LP hedge-fund vehicle at $100K minimums is not the **OpenAI** $50M equity shape, the **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) $115M Series A shape, or the **Genesis AI** (2025) $105M co-led seed shape. I would be funding GP economics, not a company. Counterfactual: if Standard Signal were instead "the LLM-trading infrastructure layer Renaissance and Two Sigma rent" priced as a software business raising equity, this dimension would flip a tier on its own. The dossier does not describe that company.

My 36-month forecast: Standard Signal does not reach $100M AUM as a fund, and Royzen pivots within 24 months into selling LLM trading infrastructure or research agents to existing quant shops -- the picks-and-shovels adjacency that fits his Phind execution muscle and bypasses the SEC/CFTC overhead the dossier admits no progress on. The mechanism is structural: the alpha thesis is unverified, the regulatory burden eats a solo founder, and Royzen's revealed preference is shipping AI products fast, not running a compliance-heavy LP vehicle for seven years.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is Numerai's reported $500M JPMorgan capacity (Ainvest 2026) -- institutional appetite for AI-run strategies is empirically real, YC issued an AI-Native Hedge Funds RFS for Spring 2026, and Royzen's Phind throughput suggests he can iterate models faster than incumbents. If LLM chain-of-thought trading produces even a modest verified Sharpe, GP economics on a single billion of AUM is a 9-figure outcome and the **Slash** learning-rate override applies. That case stings because Royzen's pivot signal is real. It loses because Numerai's returns came from ensembled crowdsourced models -- a fundamentally different alpha-generation mechanism than a centralized proprietary LLM -- and because **Quantopian** shut in 2020 over weak returns despite better operational scaffolding than Standard Signal currently shows. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a published, third-party-audited live track record at minimum 18 months with Sharpe >1.5 net of fees, on real capital, before Spring 2027. Absent that, I pass.
