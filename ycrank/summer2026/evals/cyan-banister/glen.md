# Glen -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Nikos Dritsakos is a solo founder with a clear CEO seat and a prior acqui-exit (SalesBop into SellWell/FliteHouse), and he is building an MCP-based shared memory layer that competes head-on with Mem0's $24M-raised, 48K-star open-source baseline. That pairing -- maximum governance clarity, minimum non-consensus weirdness -- is Glen's signature. This is the dev-infrastructure version of the company I pass on most often: a competent operator pointing a small software wedge at a category where three funded incumbents already own the developer mindshare. The dossier reads as deliberate, MCP-savvy, and on-trend; "on-trend" is the wrong vibration for me at pre-seed.

Glen's highest dimension is governance: a single named CEO, no 50/50 partnership to litigate, no Yusupov-and-Kroll tiebreaker problem from **HQ Trivia** (2017) waiting to surface in year two. That is real -- I score it almost the full sixteen because Nikos Dritsakos is the unambiguous decision-maker per the YC page (team size 1). But the **Anduril** (2017) template is not just "one CEO"; it is one CEO with a co-built team carrying delivery weight. Glen has a single founder shipping connectors to code, PRs, issues, docs, and meetings, plus an MCP server, plus per-observation access control with OAuth 2.1 and argon2id, plus GTM. That is platform scope on one pair of hands, which converts my governance positive into an execution risk before Series A. Clear CEO authority is necessary, not sufficient, and Glen sits at the upper rung on authority while the workload behind that authority is what worries me.

The **consensus YC AI-agent darling** I pass on is defined by an analytical, not autobiographical, origin -- the founders read the memo, they didn't live the problem. Glen partly inverts that pattern: Nikos Dritsakos actually built SalesBop, an AI sales-coaching B2B SaaS, sold it into FliteHouse, and ran VP Tech & Product, which is real shipped-and-exited muscle in adjacent multi-agent B2B workflows. That is meaningfully better than the four-logo team page I throw in the pile. What it is not is the **Niantic**-Hanke ~2015 pattern of a decade inside location data before the company existed, or the **Anduril**-Luckey 2017 pattern of an Oculus-shaped prior artifact. Nikos has operator credibility for B2B SaaS, not lived contact with the org-wide-agent-context problem specifically -- the SalesBop work was sales coaching, not memory infrastructure. He has shipped before; he has not lived this.

Mechanism analysis on "magically weird": agent memory in mid-2026 is the opposite of the **Crusoe** (~2018) gas-flare moment. Mem0 has $24M and 186M API calls per quarter as the AWS Agent SDK provider; Letta closed $10M at ~$70M post; Zep beats Mem0 on LongMemEval 63.8% to 49.0%; LangMem ships natively in LangChain. Glen's wrinkle -- org-wide reconciled context with access control applied per observation -- is a genuine architectural choice, but it is a wrinkle inside the most-funded YC-adjacent category of the year. The "find them before it becomes consensus" filter (Yahoo Finance, March 2025) inverts here. No credible investor has visibly passed on aesthetic grounds; the category is already priced.

Counterfactual on capital intensity and unsexy-regulated swing: for Glen to flip a tier on this dimension, Nikos would need to be selling into a domain where the moat compounds with regulatory or physical-world friction -- the **Vigil Labs** (August 2025) or **Anduril** (2017) lane. Glen is pure software abstraction on top of MCP, gated behind a work-email waitlist. No SOC 2 in the dossier, no enterprise-fertility-defense-energy adjacency, no hardware. This is the cleanest, most aesthetically acceptable software category Y Combinator currently produces. I default low here by design; nothing in Glen's dossier lifts it.

Single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability: **Flock Safety** (~2018) I sourced by reading a Wi-Fi network name at a Four Seasons -- the product was tactile before I ever met the founder. Glen is the inverse artifact: an MCP server I cannot install in 48 hours because onboarding happens "in waves" via waitlist, behind work-email gating (tryglen.com, 2026). I cannot ride it like Uber (2010), I cannot play it like Ingress, I cannot scan it like a Flock camera. The only humans who can observe Glen in the wild today are developers already inside Claude Code, Cursor, or Codex sessions. Dev-tools abstraction is where this dimension lives at its weakest, and Glen sits there.

Forecast, 36 months: Glen is acqui-hired into Mem0, LangChain, or Anthropic itself for under $25M by mid-2029, with Nikos taking a director-of-engineering title; the alternative is quiet shutdown after the waitlist never converts into paid logos because Mem0's AWS-distributed memory provider becomes the default org-wide context plane. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: clear CEO authority on a single pair of hands, attacking a category where three funded peers compound mindshare faster than a solo founder can ship connectors-plus-MCP-plus-access-control. Governance clarity is what makes the acqui-hire clean; the absence of magical weirdness is what makes the acqui-hire necessary.

The bull case I have to take seriously: solo founders with prior B2B SaaS exits are exactly the outsider-trajectory archetype I have backed before (Brock University, not Stanford; FliteHouse, not Stripe), and the **DeepMind** (~2013) exception establishes that I will override the "lived experience" criterion when a founder is compounding across domains during a platform-shift window. MCP in 2026 is arguably that window. Nikos shipped and exited an AI SaaS company while still in his early career -- there is a "professional daydreamer" texture there I do not want to dismiss. The reason that bull case does not win: the DeepMind override required polymath compounding across neuroscience, chess, and game design. Nikos is a focused B2B SaaS operator who has stayed in adjacent lanes, which is a real skill but not the polymath thesis. The verdict flips if Nikos either lands two named enterprise design partners (Stripe, Anthropic, Shopify, anyone with a logo) by Q2 2027, or pulls a technical co-founder with infrastructure-scale chops before the seed closes -- absent one of those, Glen is a pass.
