# Enjamb Labs -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Rayan Mubarak published cancer immunotherapy biomarker work at the University of Florida College of Medicine before Enjamb Labs existed, which is the most interesting fact in this dossier and also not enough. Enjamb Labs is a credible-domain founder wrapped in the single most consensus YC Spring 2026 packaging imaginable -- "Full stack AI Agents accelerating Biopharma R&D" -- and that high/low pairing is the whole story. The man has touched the problem. The product category is what every other P26 deck is pitching.

Anduril's Palmer Luckey is my high rung on founder origin because he shipped Oculus from a garage and then arrived at defense already knowing how to ship hardware -- biography first, company second. Enjamb Labs's Rayan Mubarak is closer to the DeepMind-Hassabis middle rung than the Luckey high one: a published biomedical ML researcher with genomic biomarker work, which is real lived contact with clinical data, but not a prior product at scale and not the autobiographical "I sat in the regulatory submission room at 2am redrafting an IND" moment I'm looking for. Maadhav Deekshitha's Broadcom / Dell / FIRST / Stemiverse path per RocketReach reads as engineering generalist, not domain-of-pain. I want the founder who got burned by a CRO's $2M SDTM invoice in a prior life; Enjamb Labs gives me a researcher who studied adjacent science, which is a real ingredient and an incomplete one.

Crusoe burning flare gas in North Dakota is the high rung on magical weirdness because two or three credible VCs had already passed on aesthetic grounds before I wrote the check -- that pattern is exactly inverted in Enjamb Labs. McKinsey labeling this "the agentic era" for biopharma, SignalFire writing the "end of outsourced drug development" thesis, Medable and Saama and Veeva all bolting on agents per PharmaVoice, Formation Bio raising $370M+ in the adjacent lane -- Enjamb Labs is sitting inside the most-discussed category of the batch. The $650K from Y Combinator and Founders, Inc. is a modest entry price, fine, but the category itself is consensus, and "find them before it becomes consensus" cuts the other way here. I am being asked to bet that two pre-seed founders out-execute Veeva's product org on Veeva's home turf.

Single CEO authority -- mechanism analysis. Rayan is named CEO on the YC page and Maadhav is co-founder with no title disclosed, which on its surface clears my HQ Trivia tripwire, but the eighteen-month compounding question is whether one biomedical-ML founder and one generalist engineer can carry GxP and 21 CFR Part 11 validation without a third compliance hire that forces a governance renegotiation. The Enjamb Labs dossier shows no operating agreement, no documented founder vesting, no quality/compliance officer; the two-person structure is what it is at pre-seed, but the moment they hire a regulatory lead the question of who owns the submission-liability decision becomes load-bearing. I score this as silence, not as a green light.

Capital-intensive and unsexy-regulated -- counterfactual. Enjamb Labs is software-only, "Free to use. Forever" per the homepage, which on the surface fails my Crusoe-Anduril-Vigil hardware-or-regulated swing test. To flip this dimension a tier, Enjamb Labs would need to disclose a signed enterprise pilot with a named sponsor or CRO operating under a 21 CFR Part 11-validated workspace agreement -- that single fact would convert this from "AI wrapper" to "regulated infrastructure" in my framework, the same way Vigil Labs's financial-regulation posture qualifies. The dossier as written does not contain that fact. The FDA-audited document workflow gives modest lift over a pure SaaS API, but it is not the moat-compounding swing.

Physical-world verifiability -- single-anchor parallel to my pass example, the consensus YC AI-agent darling. Enjamb Labs is a B2B workspace I cannot ride like an Uber, scan like a Flock camera, or play like an Ingress portal. The "Free to use. Forever" homepage does technically let me sign up and poke at the agent, which is more than zero -- I could open it tomorrow -- but a regulatory-document drafting workspace is not the kind of artifact I encounter in my own life the way Postmates showed up at my door. The 250M papers / 136 databases / 700 journal templates integration count on enjamb.ai is the kind of metric a deck produces, not the kind of artifact I touch.

My 36-month forecast for Enjamb Labs is acqui-hire by Veeva, Saama, or Medidata in the $15-40M range for the team and the template library, or alternatively a narrow pivot to oncology-trial stats programming reaching $1-3M ARR before stalling against incumbent procurement. The mechanism is straightforward: Rayan Mubarak's domain credibility is real enough that an incumbent will rather buy the team than watch a credible biomedical-ML researcher build customer relationships, and the "Free to use. Forever" pricing creates exactly the kind of unmonetized usage graph an acquirer values more than the founders will.

The bull case that stings: Formation Bio raised over $370M structuring Sanofi and Lilly asset deals, which proves biopharma will write nine-figure checks to AI-native pharma operators, and Enjamb Labs at $650K pre-seed is the asymmetric early-stage version of that bet -- if the CRO channel-conflict thesis is real, an independent agent-native workspace is the natural beneficiary. I would dismantle this with the dossier itself: Formation Bio owns drug assets, which is the moat; Enjamb Labs owns a workspace, which is software Veeva can replicate. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a named biopharma sponsor disclosed as a paying customer running an IND or NDA workflow end-to-end through Enjamb Labs's workspace -- one Pfizer-tier logo with a paid contract converts this from "consensus AI wrapper with a credible founder" to "regulated infrastructure with embedded distribution," and I would revisit. Until then, Enjamb Labs is the pass-example shape with a slightly better founder.
