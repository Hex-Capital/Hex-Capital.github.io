# HeyClicky -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Farza Majeed shipped HeyClicky in roughly four weeks, open-sourced the Swift codebase under MIT, and pushed it to 5.8K?6.1K GitHub stars on top of a 108.6K-follower X distribution channel -- that's a founder-shipping-rhythm score that genuinely activates my Perplexity (2022) template. But HeyClicky also sits in the worst possible index position I evaluate: a cursor-side consumer agent that Apple Intelligence is structurally positioned to absorb, built on a codebase the founder publicly invited the internet to fork. The signature here is *Perplexity-style founder velocity attached to a Lyft-style structural position* -- and in my book the structural position dominates the founder when the moat is this thin.

Perplexity (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for HeyClicky on founder-shipping rhythm: Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built, and Farza's pattern of going from idea to live Mac app + open-source repo + 5.8K stars in ~4 weeks is the same artifact-density I score on. Where HeyClicky diverges from Perplexity is the asterisk in the dossier: buildspace raised $10M at a $100M valuation from a16z and YC and Farza voluntarily wound it down in August 2024 citing inability "to craft an updated direction that feels worth pursuing." Aravind didn't have a documented voluntary-closure pattern on a nine-figure-valued prior company. That's not disqualifying -- second-time YC founders with this kind of audience are rare -- but it means I underwrite HeyClicky's shipping rhythm at a discount to the Perplexity comp.

Lyft Series C is the failure mechanism I keep returning to on HeyClicky's index dimension: I passed on Lyft because #2 in a winner-take-all network-effect market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and the dossier didn't show a path to share inversion. HeyClicky's version is worse than #2 -- Granola is already at a $1.5B valuation on $192M raised (Bloomberg, Mar 2026), Cluely has $20.3M including a16z's $15M Series A (TechCrunch, Jun 2025), and the substrate underneath HeyClicky is Apple Intelligence, which ships screen and app context at the OS layer. The MIT license on farzaa/clicky and Farza's explicit "go forth into the wild and build" post mean any competitor can clone the codebase without R&D cost. HeyClicky isn't fighting for share in an oligopoly; it's a thin app on a substrate the platform owner controls.

On the market re-opening catalyst, the mechanism that matters over 18 months is whether Anthropic's computer-use API and OpenAI Operator (the [Inferred] "why now" in the dossier) compound into a defensible product layer or into commodity glue. Computer-use reliability improving is a tailwind for HeyClicky, but it's also a tailwind for every Mac-shell competitor, and the dossier doesn't name a single catalyst HeyClicky uniquely captures -- no exclusive model access, no proprietary screen-context dataset, no enterprise distribution wedge. That's a real catalyst with no uniquely-positioned-team story attached.

On revenue velocity, the counterfactual is sharp: HeyClicky would need to disclose paid MRR compounding >20% MoM within six months -- the Harvey (2023) "$3B ? $5B ? $8B in months" signature -- to flip my placement up a tier. The dossier shows no pricing on heyclicky.com, no paid users, no LOIs, and a Windows version stuck on a Tally waitlist. GitHub stars and Twitter followers are not revenue -- I learned to distinguish "false signal" from "just working" specifically by watching audience-driven launches that never converted (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). HeyClicky is currently audience signal, not buying behavior.

The single-miracle test for HeyClicky is the one place the company actually screens cleanly: one miracle (reliable computer-use agents across third-party Mac apps), plus GTM execution. Compare to Decagon (2023), where the miracle was LLM accuracy on support tickets and the rest was workflow + sales -- I funded that on exactly this architecture. HeyClicky's miracle is similarly isolated, which is why this dimension scores higher than the index dimension. The catch is that the same single miracle is available to every fork of farzaa/clicky and to Apple itself, so isolating the miracle doesn't isolate the *winner*.

My 36-month forecast on HeyClicky: dead or sub-$30M acqui-hire by a mid-tier consumer AI app (think Raycast, Arc successor, or a Granola adjacency), with Apple Intelligence having absorbed cursor-side screen-aware agent functionality at the OS layer by WWDC 2027. The mechanism is the compound of three dossier facts -- MIT-licensed code inviting forks, Apple platform substitution risk, and Farza's documented pattern of voluntarily shutting down a $10M-funded company when the direction stopped feeling worth pursuing. None of those are individually fatal; together they make the modal outcome a quiet wind-down or talent-acquisition, not a venture-scale return.

The bull case that stings: Farza is running the exact Perplexity (2022) cold-inbound pattern -- 108.6K X reach, 5.8K stars in four weeks, open-sourced to drive adoption flywheel, second-time YC founder with prior OpenAI sponsorship relationships from buildspace. Consumer AI agents sit squarely in my *AI Market Clarity* "wide open" sub-category, and HeyClicky's voice + screen + cross-app-action surface is a plausible workflow layer no incumbent currently owns at the consumer tier. If Farza converts even 1% of the 108.6K following into $20/mo paid users, HeyClicky is at $260K MRR within months. What dismantles the bull case is the open-source-by-design choice plus Apple at the platform layer -- Perplexity had a defensible index and zero MIT-licensed clones; HeyClicky has neither. **The condition that flips my verdict**: HeyClicky discloses paid MRR >$100K/mo with >20% MoM growth within six months AND ships at least one proprietary integration (exclusive API, embedded partnership) that a forker cannot replicate. Absent both, this is a pass.
