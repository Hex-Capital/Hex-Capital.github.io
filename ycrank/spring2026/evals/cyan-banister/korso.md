# Korso -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Korso is a software-abstraction agent layer whose single strongest signal is Martin Pan's stint as an automations engineer at General Motors, and whose single weakest is that the korsoai.com sample dashboard cites two fictional companies -- "Northbridge Metals" and "Blue Harbor Components" -- in place of any named pilot. That pairing tells me what kind of bet Korso is: a credentialed-engineering team writing toward a real industrial problem from one step removed, in a YC batch (P26) where a sibling -- Lumari -- is already running the same play. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Korso is playing on paper -- "AI agents for manufacturing ERP/CRM" -- is the consensus 2025-2026 lane, not the contrarian one.

Palmer Luckey and Anduril (2017) is the high rung of founder-from-lived-experience: a teenager who built Oculus in his parents' garage, sold it, and then *chose* to walk into defense procurement because he wanted to. The mechanism is autobiography preceding pitch -- the founder was already *of* the problem when they showed up. Korso has one founder whose biography brushes the manufacturing floor: Martin Pan's automations-engineer role at GM. That is real and I want to weight it honestly. But Pan was inside a Tier-1 OEM with an internal IT stack, not inside a 200-person job shop running SYSPRO or Plex, which is exactly the mid-market customer Korso's seven-ERP integration list targets. Daichi Hiraoka and Pan together on Penn Electric Racing is a formula-team friendship, not a manufacturing-operator origin. The Korso dossier reads "engineers who worked near a factory" rather than "operators who lived inside one" -- which is the Anduril test stripped of the autobiography.

The pass example I keep in my head -- two ex-FAANG PMs, $200B TAM slide, four-logo team page, oversubscribed seed -- fails the verifiability filter because a normal human cannot install it, photograph it, or hand it to a friend. Korso fails the same filter for a quieter reason: the korsoai.com product page references long-running checkpointed workflows across SAP, NetSuite, Dynamics, Sage, SYSPRO, Plex, and Acumatica, but the only artifact a normal human can observe is a screenshot dashboard populated with invented company names. Flock Safety I could touch -- the camera bolted to a pole. Niantic I could play. Postmates I could order from. Korso, I cannot ride. The "$2.4M pipeline, 47 active quotes, 89% close rate" line on the homepage is mock data, and mock data on a homepage at YC launch is the tell that the tactile artifact has not yet shipped to a named customer.

On magically weird and consensus, the mechanism over 18 months is what concerns me about Korso. Lumari is in the *same* YC company directory chasing RFQ extraction and PO tracking with $500K raised; Turian has already taken $3.78M from Cherry Ventures, Everywhere Ventures, and Visionaries Club out of Berlin doing the identical RFQ/PO/sales-order agent for mid-market manufacturers. AWS Bedrock AgentCore is shipping procurement agents natively. Eighteen months from now, the agent layer over an ERP either becomes a feature owned by SAP and Microsoft, or it consolidates into the best-funded independent -- and Korso, at three engineers with no disclosed round size and no disclosed customers, is not the best-funded independent. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus; Korso *is* the consensus.

On single unambiguous CEO, the counterfactual to flip Korso from a 5 to a 10 in this dimension is simple and absent: the dossier lists Daichi Hiraoka, Alex Liu, and Martin Pan as co-founders with no named CEO, no tiebreaker, and no documented operating agreement. HQ Trivia (2017) is the cautionary tale I cite in my sleep -- Yusupov and Kroll, no resolution mechanism, eventual feud, 2020-21 implosion. If the Korso YC page tomorrow named one of the three as CEO with majority operating authority, this dimension would re-rate immediately. Until then, three engineers with formula-team friendship and no governance disclosure is exactly the structural shape I screen against before signing docs.

On capital-intensive or unsexy-regulated, my single-anchor parallel is Vigil Labs (August 2025) in regulated financial software, where the moat compounds in a domain other investors refuse to enter because of compliance and audit complexity. Korso *gestures* at this with "verification layers, dry-run validation, and audit trails" on korsoai.com -- manufacturing ERP has real audit and ITAR exposure -- but the product itself is a software wrapper, no hardware, no regulated license, no capital-intensive infrastructure. Mid-market manufacturing is mildly unsexy in the way that procurement is mildly unsexy; it is not Mindbloom-ketamine unsexy or Anduril-defense unsexy. The moat compounds slower here than the seven listed ERP vendors' own agentic roadmaps.

Thirty-six months out, my forecast for Korso is acqui-hire or wind-down with ARR under $1M, with Turian or an ERP incumbent (most likely Microsoft via Dynamics 365 Copilot) absorbing the team or the workflow patterns. The mechanism is that the wedge -- multi-week checkpointed workflows -- is a reliability problem that the better-capitalized European competitor and the natively-distributed ERP incumbents will solve in parallel, while Korso's three-person team burns its YC check on enterprise sales cycles into manufacturers who require implementation-partner blessing before they sign.

The bull case that stings: Pan's GM automations role is the closest thing in this YC batch to a founder who has actually configured a manufacturing-execution system in production, and "long-running checkpointed workflows that span weeks" is a genuinely hard reliability problem that Lumari's RFQ-extraction framing does not even attempt -- if Korso ships that primitive first, the moat is real and the YC sibling becomes a feature comparison rather than a competitor. Mid-market SYSPRO and Plex shops are exactly the unsexy underserved customer I claim to like, and HandUp (~2014) is my reminder that I will fund a modest-ceiling company when the founder mirrors the customer. What dismantles it: HandUp had founder-customer biographical mirroring; Korso has founder-*adjacent* biographical brushing. Pan was at GM, not running a 50-person job shop. The fictional dashboard on launch day is the artifact that wins -- if a real factory were piloting, the screenshot would say so. One verifiable condition would flip my verdict to Neutral: a named mid-market manufacturer (Plex or SYSPRO customer, ideally) running Korso's Atlas agents on a real multi-week PO-expediting workflow with documented checkpointing, disclosed publicly. Until that artifact exists, Korso is the consensus lane I pass on, with bubbe warmth toward Pan for going back to the floor he came from.
