# Meridian -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Meridian is the platonic ideal of the deal I pass on. "The AI operating system for consulting firms" -- I can hear the pitch deck building itself. Every smart investor I talk to right now is drowning in vertical AI applications for knowledge work. Glean has $610M+ in funding and $200M ARR doing enterprise AI search. Microsoft Copilot already plugs into SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams, and Outlook -- literally the same integration list on Meridian's website. There is no mispricing here. Zero. The market has not dismissed AI for enterprise knowledge management -- the market is so in love with it that Glean got a $7.2B valuation. When I say "find cool shit that others do not believe," I mean find the thing the market has actively rejected. Meridian is the thing the market is actively bidding up. The upside is already in the price across the entire category.

The primitive test kills this for me. Meridian doesn't create a new behavior -- it automates existing ones. Consulting firms already search for documents, already generate decks from templates, already track client relationships in CRMs. Meridian does these things faster with RAG and LLMs. That's a feature enhancement, not a primitive. Venmo created peer-to-peer mobile payments as a behavior that didn't exist. Patreon created direct creator monetization. Meridian creates... faster document retrieval? "Smart Folders"? These are UX improvements on SharePoint, not a new category of human interaction. When I ask "will people use Meridian's name as a verb?", the answer is obviously no. Nobody's going to say "Meridian me that proposal."

The founders -- Kashyap Nathan, Chris Farrington, Shanay Champaneri -- have solid intern-level credentials at real companies. Palantir FDE, OpenAI, Sandia Labs, Robinhood. For college students and recent dropouts, that's a strong resume. But here's my problem: the idea is exactly as sane as the founders. "AI for consulting knowledge management" is precisely what you'd expect three sharp CS students with consulting-adjacent internships to build. There's nothing insane about it. No managing partner is going to hear this pitch and say "you're out of your mind." They're going to say "yeah, we've been looking at Glean and Copilot for that." Sane people with a sane idea is a sane investment -- and sane investments don't produce asymmetric returns.

The structural durability concern is where Meridian is most exposed. The entire stack -- RAG over document stores, LLM-based generation, template-based output -- is reproducible with off-the-shelf components. The dossier itself acknowledges "the core technology stack is reproducible with off-the-shelf components." The founders frame their moat as "behavioral signal on how consulting work actually gets done. You can't download it." Every AI wrapper makes this claim. Every single one. "Use us for two months and we become irreplaceable." That's not a moat, that's a retention marketing slide. Kenley, a YC S24 company, is building the exact same product. When your direct competitor got funded by YC a year before you -- and you got into YC and then left the batch for undisclosed reasons -- the competitive dynamic is moving against you, not for you.

The bull case deserves honest consideration. The claimed ~$700K in contracted revenue four months in, with 3x month-over-month growth, would be genuinely impressive traction if independently verified. Competitive bake-off wins against Databricks and Palantir, if real, suggest the product works and consulting buyers prefer the vertical solution. The consulting industry is a $300B+ market with specific workflows that horizontal tools serve poorly. If Meridian genuinely accumulates irreplaceable institutional knowledge per-client and the switching costs compound engagement by engagement, they could become the Salesforce of consulting -- deeply embedded vertical infrastructure. But every one of these signals is self-reported from the a16z Speedrun portal with zero independent verification. No press coverage, no customer testimonials, no third-party validation. And even if the traction is real, the question isn't whether Meridian can sell to consulting firms today -- it's whether Glean adds a consulting vertical template next quarter and makes this a feature of a $7.2B platform.

Meridian sits at the precise intersection of my two strongest pass signals: consensus-hot category with no mispricing, and an AI application layer that incumbents will commoditize. The comparison to Solana is instructive by inversion -- Solana was a new protocol primitive in a category investors had dismissed, built by a founder I'd watched across three companies. Meridian is an application wrapper in the hottest category in venture, built by talented students I have no relationship history with. The dimensions that produced a 2,000x return are all pointing the wrong direction here.
