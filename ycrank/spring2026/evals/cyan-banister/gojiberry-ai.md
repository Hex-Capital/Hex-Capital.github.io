# Gojiberry AI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and his two co-founders run a clean operating playbook -- $2.5M ARR in ten months, 35% MoM, profitable -- inside the most consensus lane of the entire Spring 2026 YC batch, which is what Gojiberry AI is: a competent repeat-SaaS team executing the textbook AI-SDR motion in a $5.81B market that every other founder in this cohort is also chasing. The signature on Gojiberry AI is high CEO clarity paired with near-zero "magically weird," and that pairing tells me almost everything before I open a pricing page.

Anduril (2017) is the high rung on founder-as-CEO because Palmer Luckey walked in with one unmistakable name on the org chart and a defense thesis that nobody on his board would later try to top-grade -- that single-spine clarity is half of why I tattooed the conviction. Gojiberry AI sits one notch below: Pierre-Eliott is unambiguously CEO, Rom?n Czerny owns CMO, Dylan Teixeira owns CTO, and the trio already shipped CoCo.ai to ~?500K ARR and an exit together before this. That is the inverse of the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll problem -- nobody here is fighting for the steering wheel. Where Gojiberry AI diverges from the Anduril rung is the company they are steering: a $99/mo LinkedIn outreach tool, not a sovereign-capability bet, so the governance strength compounds into operational discipline rather than tattoo-grade conviction.

The pass example I keep on file -- two ex-FAANG PMs, consensus YC AI-agent darling, $200B TAM slide, six funds chasing -- is the rung Gojiberry AI sits closest to on "magically weird." The dossier is unambiguous: AI SDR market $5.81B in 2026, 31.9% CAGR, Clay just closed $100M at $3.1B valuation, Apollo cleared $100M ARR years ago, Artisan and Outreach and Instantly and Smartlead are all in the same room. Gojiberry AI's own blog is structured as "alternative to Clay / alternative to Apollo / alternative to Instantly" -- founder-led SEO inside a category whose taxonomy is already set by the incumbents. I am supposed to find them before it becomes consensus; this *is* the consensus, and the round being a likely standard YC $500K (PitchBook) only tells me they took the default, not that anybody was wrong about the price.

Founder-market fit on the dossier is genuine but analytical, not autobiographical. Pierre-Eliott and Rom?n built CoCo.ai (WhatsApp marketing SaaS, ~?500K ARR exit) and Dylan built Edusign (multi-million ARR exit); the lived experience is "we have sold SaaS to SMBs before," which is closer to the DeepMind polymath-override pattern than the Anduril-Luckey lived-it pattern -- except these are not polymaths compounding across chess, neuroscience, and game design, they are SaaS operators compounding across two SaaS exits. Over 18 months this dimension compounds as faster execution and better retention math (churn already down 30%, per Superframeworks), but it does not compound into the unfair social license that lets Chase Lochmiller walk a North Dakota well pad. They sell to people like themselves -- useful, repeatable, not magic.

Counterfactual on the capital-intensive / unsexy axis: for Gojiberry AI to flip a tier here, the LinkedIn dependency would need to be reframed as a regulatory moat -- e.g., the team becomes the licensed counterparty for compliant outbound under a future SEC/FTC enforcement regime against cold outreach, the way Vigil Labs (August 2025) is positioned in regulated finance. Nothing in the dossier hints at that posture. What the dossier *does* show is the opposite: LinkedIn ToS exposure with no mitigation noted, $99/mo SMB pricing, and a SF-plus-Lisbon distributed team running a pure-software wrapper around a third-party social graph. The category is the inverse of "too hard or too unsexy" -- it is easy and crowded.

Physical-world verifiability is the one place Gojiberry AI is better than the pass-example template without quite earning the Postmates (2011) rung. I can sign up for the Pro plan at $99/mo, point two LinkedIn senders at a campaign, and watch reply rates inside 48 hours -- that is more tactile than a dev-tools API. But the artifact I would observe is a sequence of LinkedIn DMs and emails that look indistinguishable from the Clay-plus-Smartlead stack I could assemble in an afternoon; I cannot photograph it the way I photographed a Flock Safety camera on a pole or the way I felt the viral pull of HQ Trivia (2017) on my own phone. Tactile, but not transmissible.

Thirty-six-month forecast: Gojiberry AI is acquired for $30-80M by Clay, Apollo, or Outreach in the 2027-2028 window after plateauing in the $5-10M ARR band, as Clay ships native agentic outbound on its $3.1B-valuation balance sheet and Gojiberry AI's LinkedIn-signal advantage commoditizes; a LinkedIn ToS enforcement event accelerates the timeline. The mechanism is straightforward -- repeat operators with a clean exit history rarely die, they sell early, and the buyers with the most strategic need are the same incumbents whose names are already in Gojiberry AI's SEO blog.

The bull case worth steel-manning: $2.5M ARR in ten months from zero, profitable, 35% MoM, 2,000+ customers, churn down 30%, and a founding team that has already exited two SaaS companies together -- on raw execution metrics this is the strongest-traction company in any pre-seed dossier I will read this batch. The HQ Trivia (2017) override is the relevant Known Exception: tactile product-led traction can outrun governance and consensus concerns. The reason I do not let it override here is that HQ Trivia's pull was consumer virality I could feel on my own phone; Gojiberry AI's 35% MoM is B2B SMB churn-and-replace inside a category Clay will absorb with one product release. The condition that flips my verdict is concrete and verifiable: a named Fortune 500 logo signed at $500K+ ACV that demonstrably displaced Clay or Apollo, not another founder-LinkedIn post about demos booked.
