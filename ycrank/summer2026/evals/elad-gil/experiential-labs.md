# Experiential Labs -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Experiential Labs is a credentialed-research pre-seed without a demand pulse: Silen Naihin's AutoGPT (150K+ stars) and Kion Fallah's Waabi staff-research tenure put founder-shipping near the top of my ladder, while the GitHub flagship `world-model-harness` sitting at 21 stars and a 5-follower org with no disclosed revenue puts the curve dimension near the bottom. That high/low pairing -- shipping pedigree without buying behavior -- is the dimensional signature, and it's the exact profile where "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863) is most diagnostic.

Naihin is the highest rung on my founder-ships ladder for this deal -- the same axis that made me lean into **Perplexity** (2022) when Aravind kept showing up a week later with the thing built. The shared mechanism is *legible artifacts shipped independently*, not pedigree -- and Naihin's 150K-star AutoGPT contribution plus the NeurIPS paper (arXiv:2311.10538) on agent benchmarks is the real article, not McKinsey-deck founder theater. Where Experiential Labs diverges from the **Perplexity** rung is concurrency: Naihin's LinkedIn still lists him as Co-founder/CEO of Endflow (YC W24) alongside Experiential Labs, and Aravind was singular-focused when he was pinging me. Split-attention at pre-seed in a category with $1B+ adjacent entrants is a real ding, not a footnote.

The low rung on revenue velocity is every AI company I've passed on where the dossier went silent on the curve -- not because growth was bad, but because there was no growth to report, which in this cycle is the same thing. Experiential Labs lives on that rung: `world-model-harness` at 21 stars updated June 2026, `openclaas` at 54 stars and 4 forks, `autoresearch-distillation` at 8 stars, no press, no Product Hunt, no LOI counterparties named. This is not "false signal" -- I haven't even seen false signal. **Harvey** (2023) was visibly compounding before the markup; Experiential Labs has not yet generated the equivalent of a single named enterprise pilot in public, and the absent-evidence default is doing most of the scoring work here, which is itself the signal.

Mechanism analysis on the market-catalyst dimension: Fortune (May 2026) flagging world models as where "AI field's biggest names are betting billions" is a real catalyst, but it compounds the *wrong way* for Experiential Labs over 18 months. Catalysts that pull in $1.23B (World Labs) and $1.03B (AMI Labs) seed rounds don't open a window for a 2-person pre-seed -- they close it, because the talent and customer attention concentrate on the funded labs. The defensible read is that the catalyst here is "agent eval pain post-LLM," not "world models" -- and on the eval axis the catalyst is 24+ months old and already has named leaders.

Index-position counterfactual: for Experiential Labs to flip a tier on distribution, the wedge would have to be that every agent developer must route telemetry through their simulator the way every e-commerce flow eventually touched **Stripe** (2010). That requires LangSmith, Arize, and Braintrust to *not* add a simulation backend -- which is exactly the move Braintrust telegraphs in its 2026 observability piece. Experiential Labs sits in a workflow path that incumbents already own and adjacent giants can extend into; that's the inverse of **Stripe**'s 2010 position.

Single-anchor parallel on the miracle test: **Color Genomics** (2015) is the right comp because I structured it as workflow on top of commodity sequencing rather than a chemistry bet. Experiential Labs is trying the same template -- workflow on top of world-model simulation -- but the cited 99.7% reconstruction fidelity is a Waabi-domain figure, and generalizing simulation fidelity from driving scenes to arbitrary agent environments is the second miracle hiding inside the dossier. Color worked because commodity sequencing was actually commoditized; world-model fidelity outside narrow domains is not.

Forecast, 36 months: Experiential Labs is acqui-hired by Braintrust, LangSmith, or a world-model lab for sub-$50M, or pivots into RL-environment generation for agent training where the simulation fidelity is the product rather than the eval wrapper. Sub-$1M ARR by month 36. The mechanism is the high/low signature -- shipping-credentialed founders attract acqui-hire interest precisely because no curve materialized to justify a venture-scale outcome.

The bull case that stings: Naihin shipped AutoGPT to 150K stars as a founding engineer, which is the strongest "founder ships between meetings" signal in this batch, and Fallah's Waabi tenure is exactly the talent-concentration moat I called out as a structural advantage -- self-driving-grade simulation expertise *is* scarce. If Experiential Labs lands two named frontier-lab pilots in the next two quarters while Braintrust is still shipping CI-eval features, this is **Decagon** (2023) for agent observability and I'm wrong to pass at consensus pre-seed. The dossier evidence that wins the argument is the silence: AutoGPT pedigree without a single named design-partner in the materials, in a category where pilot logos are the cheapest possible disclosure. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is one $100K+ signed contract with a named AI lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, a Harvey, a Decagon) inside the next 90 days -- exactly the kind of disclosure that real velocity makes trivial.
