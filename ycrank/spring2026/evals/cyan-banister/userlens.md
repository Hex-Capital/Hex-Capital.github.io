# Userlens -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

$660K in 2025 revenue on a pre-seed cap table would normally make me sit up straight. But the dossier flags that this figure may include revenue from Wudpecker, the AI meeting assistant that Userlens pivoted away from — and the Wudpecker domain still hosts Userlens's blog content. That ambiguity is the most analytically important signal in this entire memo, because if Userlens's standalone traction is a fraction of that number, then what remains is a recently pivoted team with impressive-looking customer logos (Linear, Deel, Calendly, McDonald's) but no independently verified paid engagement depth. Before I write a check, I need to know what the Userlens-specific number actually is. When I invested in Uber, I didn't need verified revenue — I needed a driver so excited about the product he was handing out business cards. The product had escaped the building. Userlens's logos look like they might still be inside the building, possibly on free trials attached to a YC offer for three months free.

Ankur Dahama and Hai Ta co-founded Wudpecker at Tampere University in 2022, grew it to 25K users and 1,000+ customers, then pivoted to Userlens when they realized they had no visibility into which of those customers were about to leave. That's a legitimate "problem found the founder" story — they lived inside the churn problem as operators. But when I ask my opening question — "tell me your story, not the business" — what I hear is "we ran a SaaS company and experienced a common SaaS company problem." Ankur's prior experience at Fortum, Mercedes, and Haiilo is corporate. Hai's background is in sales and UX design. There is no biographical inevitability here — no moment where the problem chose them because of who they are, only a moment where they noticed a gap while running a different product. Compare that to Max Levchin building Affirm after watching consumers get destroyed by credit card opacity, or the Crusoe founders who'd stood in North Dakota oil fields watching energy literally burn away. Userlens's founding story is professional, not forged.

Gainsight at ~$200M revenue, ChurnZero at $180.8M, Vitally with $40.2M raised — customer success is one of the most heavily funded, consensus-attractive categories in B2B SaaS. Nobody shifts uncomfortably in their chair when you pitch "AI for churn prediction." There's no regulatory frontier, no political toxicity, no stigma. Lee Jacobs and I have been explicit about this: we're not looking for "some B.S. SaaS company or AI wrapper." Userlens connects to Mixpanel, Amplitude, PostHog, Salesforce, and HubSpot — all existing data sources — then applies LLMs to generate churn scores and QBR decks. The dossier itself states the core capability is "technically reproducible." Gainsight has already announced AI features. Amplitude and PostHog could add account-level health scoring as a native feature tomorrow. When I ran my SpaceX check, nobody could replicate what SpaceX was doing — the physics, the team, the regulatory capture were the moat. Userlens's moat is API integrations and per-deployment ML training, which creates switching costs but not structural irreplaceability.

The bull case an advocate would make is real: Userlens occupies a genuine gap between product analytics tools (built for PMs) and heavyweight CS platforms (built for enterprise), offering an AI-native middle path at $500/month that auto-generates deliverables rather than just dashboards. The Quartr case study — 60% reduction in prep time, 31% increase in accounts per CSM, 152% increase in upsell pipeline — shows concrete ROI that could create rapid word-of-mouth adoption among CS teams. The Smartly testimonial about predicting 23 six-figure churn deals is the kind of "holy shit" moment that sells itself. And the timing is sharp: the 2023-2024 SaaS downturn shifted every company from growth to retention, creating urgent demand. If Userlens can lock in enough deployments before incumbents add AI features, the per-account trained models become genuinely sticky. I checked my known exceptions — could this be an RTFKT-style cultural timing bet? No, because there's no narrow window closing; AI features are being added by everyone simultaneously. Could it be Superplastic-style category-boundary weirdness? No — churn prediction is the opposite of weird. The bull case is "good company, real market," but that's not the same as "Long Journey bet."

Userlens doesn't attack an invisible, extractive system — it adds analysis to data that already exists in transparent tools. B2B SaaS churn isn't an opaque structure exploiting vulnerable people; it's a business operations problem for well-funded companies. When I backed Ownwell, property tax systems were extracting value from homeowners who didn't understand the appeals process. When I backed Carta, equity compensation was a black box that disadvantaged employees. Userlens helps Calendly and Deel retain their enterprise customers more efficiently. That's useful. It's not structural revelation. The cascading real-world impact is similarly constrained — Userlens makes CSMs more productive ($30K average value added per FTE per the Quartr study), but it doesn't expand economic access or create new participants in a previously gatekept market. It optimizes an already-functioning system for already-successful companies.

One verifiable condition would flip me to Invest: if Ankur or Hai carried a biographical connection to systemic opacity that I haven't seen — the kind of story where the problem of hidden, extractive information asymmetry was personal before it was professional — AND if Userlens-specific (post-Wudpecker-pivot) contracted ARR were independently confirmed above $400K with at least three named logos on paid annual plans. That would shift both the forge score and the defensibility calculus meaningfully. Without those signals, Userlens is a competent team executing well in a consensus category that doesn't need me. The two dimensions that locked this verdict are Founder's Biographical Forge — professional problem discovery without biographical inevitability — and Contrarian Conviction — a crowded, well-funded market where Gainsight, ChurnZero, and the analytics platforms themselves are all racing toward the same AI-native features Userlens is building.
