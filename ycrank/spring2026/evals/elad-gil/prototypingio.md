# Prototyping.io -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Prototyping.io is a two-person AI-CAM bet (Revanth Bodepudi, ex-Schlumberger via UT Austin aerospace; Prerit Oberai, ex-NASA mission-ops tooling via CU Boulder) attacking a category where the catalyst they're riding -- LLM/vision parsing of CAD geometry -- has already been monetized by **Xometry** ($687M FY2025 revenue, NASDAQ:XMTR) and re-claimed by **Factorem**, which already markets itself as "AI-native full-stack." The signature is a credible founder pair plus a real but already-absorbed catalyst, paired with zero revenue disclosed behind an early-access waitlist. That's the **Lyft** Series C shape, not the **Anduril** (2022) shape -- a #2 entering a market the obvious bet has already mapped, not a market peers misread as dead.

On market re-opening, the load-bearing analogy is **Anduril** (2022): Ukraine was a named 24-month catalyst that re-priced US defense software, and incumbents (Lockheed, Raytheon) structurally could not absorb it because their quoting/procurement stacks predated the shift. Prototyping.io's catalyst -- transformers crossing the CAD-parsing threshold -- is real, but the dossier itself flags that **Xometry's** AI-powered instant quoting is already in production (RapidDirect 2025) and **Factorem** launched AI-native. The divergence from the Anduril template is exactly the part that mattered for Anduril: in 2022 the incumbents *couldn't* execute the catalyst; in 2026 the incumbents *are already* executing it. That's not market first; that's market third.

**Lyft** Series C is the right low-rung pattern for the index-position dimension -- a #2 in a category where #1 sits in the workflow path with structural advantages (network of riders/drivers, in Lyft's case; 250-300+ vendor partners and a public-company balance sheet in **Xometry's** case). Prototyping.io's pitch is that automating machine-program generation cannibalizes the human-estimator unit economics that anchor **Xometry** and **Fictiv** ($200M raised, joining MISUMI). That's the right theoretical wedge -- channel conflict for incumbents is real -- but the dossier shows zero signed capacity, no factory partners, no owned fleet, and two people in Sunnyvale. A wedge without distribution is not an index position; it's a thesis.

Revenue velocity is where Prototyping.io fails my **Harvey** (2023) test most loudly: "$3B ? $5B ? $8B in months" was the curve that earned the consensus price. Prototyping.io discloses no MRR, no logos, no waitlist count, no pilot revenue. In AI, silence on growth is itself negative -- real growth is easy to disclose. The waitlist gate at prototyping.io in May 2026 is the founder-narrative-without-curve I reject by default, and the dossier offers no offsetting signal (no third-party validation of the ?0.0002" tolerance claim, no benchmark, no demo).

The single-miracle test is where Prototyping.io most clearly diverges from my **Color Genomics** (2015) override. Color worked because it was workflow-infrastructure on top of *already-commodity* sequencing -- one miracle (consumer/clinician workflow), one validated capability beneath it (Illumina chemistry). Prototyping.io's claimed surface area is CNC + sheet metal + 3D printing + injection molding + extrusion + die casting with 40+ materials. Auto-CAM across that geometry-process matrix is not a solved problem in public literature -- that's the dossier's own language. Stack on top: fulfillment-network miracle (no disclosed partners), DTC-engineer acquisition miracle against **Xometry's** ad budget, and working-capital miracle against **Fictiv's** $200M. That compounds to four independent miracles, and per the NFX framework, compounding small odds is how you lose money in pre-seed.

Founder execution rhythm is the one dimension where Prototyping.io doesn't actively repel me. Bodepudi shipped NSF-funded nanowire/aerogel research at UT Austin and then operating-engineered at Schlumberger; Oberai shipped embedded systems, Flutter mobile at POSSIBLE, and satellite mission-ops tooling. That's a real mechanical+software pair, not a McKinsey-MBA promoter. But there's no shipped artifact in the manufacturing automation domain itself -- no demo, no Product Hunt launch, no GitHub project on toolpath generation, no "thing built between meetings" in the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind sense. The pair clears the disqualifier; they don't clear the bar.

My 36-month forecast: Prototyping.io is acqui-hired by **Xometry** or **Fictiv** for under $30M as an AI-DFM/auto-CAM feature team, or pivots to a SaaS DFM layer sold *into* existing shops rather than competing with marketplaces -- the second outcome is the better one and the one I'd expect Nicolas Dessaigne to nudge them toward inside YC. The mechanism is straightforward: the catalyst Prototyping.io is riding has been priced in by funded incumbents with distribution, and a 2-person pre-seed cannot out-capitalize **Fictiv's** $200M or out-distribute **Xometry's** public-company marketplace -- but the auto-CAM artifact, if it exists, has clear acquihire value.

Bull case worth steel-manning: Factorem's positioning proves the "AI-native full-stack" wedge is investable, and if Prototyping.io's automated machine-program generation actually works across processes, the channel conflict against **Xometry's** human-estimator unit economics is genuinely existential for incumbents -- this is the workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-hard-thing pattern that produced **Anduril**. The dismantling: Anduril had Trae Stephens' Palantir forward-deployed muscle and Palmer Luckey's Oculus shipping history pre-loaded against a market with $700B+ of annual procurement; Prototyping.io has two strong engineers, no shipped manufacturing-domain artifact, no disclosed capacity, and a waitlist in a market where the public-comp leader already does AI quoting. The condition that flips my verdict: Prototyping.io publishes a live demo generating valid 5-axis toolpaths from arbitrary CAD with third-party-verified tolerance results, and discloses >$50K MRR or three named enterprise pilots within 90 days. Until then, this is a pass under the same logic as the **Lyft** Series C -- not a contrarian bet, just a worse version of the obvious one.
