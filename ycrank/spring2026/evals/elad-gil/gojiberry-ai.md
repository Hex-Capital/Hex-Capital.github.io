# Gojiberry AI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is a revenue-velocity outlier sitting in an oligopoly position — $0→$2.5M ARR in ten months at 35% MoM, profitable, against Clay at $100M ARR and Apollo at $150M ARR. That high/low pairing is the entire story: when I see profitable seven-figure ARR at pre-seed I lean in on the curve, and when I see #5 in a category where the #1 just raised $100M at a $3.1B post (Clay Series C, CapitalG, June 2025) I run the Lyft test. Pierre-Eliott Lallemant and Romàn Czerny built CoCo AI to ~€500K ARR and sold it in 2025, so this is not a first rodeo — but the question is whether Gojiberry AI is "just working" in the Harvey sense or a $99/month SMB tool ceiling-locked under Clay and Apollo's eventual agent layers.

**Harvey (2023)** is the revenue-velocity comp that loads here — what "just working" looks like in actual dollars rather than founder narrative (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). The shared mechanism is the curve doing the talking: Harvey ran $3B→$5B→$8B in months because real enterprise legal was *buying*, not piloting. Gojiberry AI's $0→$1.4M ARR in 9 months (Dylan Teixeira LinkedIn, Nov 2025) compounding to $2.5M at month 10 with 2,000+ paying customers and *positive cash flow at pre-seed* is the same shape of curve, and that is precisely the signal I refuse to discount when consensus pays. Where Gojiberry AI diverges from the Harvey template, and where I have to mark it down: Harvey's ACVs landed at AmLaw 100 firms with six-figure contracts; Gojiberry AI's G2 cohort is 11 reviews, 5.0/5, *every reviewer under 50 employees*, on a $99/month Pro plan. That is the difference between enterprise buying behavior and SMB SaaS churn risk, and the dossier offers zero evidence of a Series B+ logo on the customer list.

**Lyft Series C** failed my framework because rideshare was a winner-take-all market and Lyft was #2 — the dossier offered no credible path to share inversion (20VC). The same failure mechanism applies here, and the Gojiberry AI dossier confirms it: Clay owns data orchestration across 50+ sources at ~$100M ARR, Apollo owns the 250M-contact database at $150M ARR and 34% YoY, and the YC page positions Gojiberry AI as "intent-first" — a feature, not a category. G2 reviewers themselves flag that the product "depends heavily on social and public signals" any competitor can scrape. Apollo can ship an agent layer on its existing distribution in a quarter; Gojiberry AI cannot ship Apollo's database in three years. That is the Lyft pattern in B2B GTM, and *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) puts AI SDR/sales agents on the "leaders emerging" side of the taxonomy, not the wide-open side.

The catalyst Gojiberry AI is riding — LLM personalization quality crossing a threshold in 2024-2025 plus the 11x.ai implosion (claimed $14M ARR vs ~$3M actual, 70-80% churn per gtmlens.com Q2 2026) — is real but it is a *competitor stumble*, not a Ukraine-grade market re-opening. Over 18 months that catalyst compounds two ways: either AI SDR consolidates into 2-3 winners and Gojiberry AI must be one of them, or the category fragments by vertical and the $99 SMB wedge becomes a sticky base. The 11x.ai churn opening is a 12-month tailwind, not a 5-year re-opening, which is why I cannot pay Anduril-style premiums on this catalyst.

For the score on **Founder Ships Iteratively** to flip from where I have it to a top-quartile read, Gojiberry AI would need to show what is actually already half-shown: Pierre-Eliott and Romàn pivoted from a v1 with a 2,000-person waitlist and zero conversions to a paying product in 60 days (wearefounders.uk case study, 2026), and Dylan Teixeira separately built and sold Edusign in 2025. Three founders, three shipped artifacts, two exits. This is the **Perplexity (2022)** founder-rhythm signal in B2B clothing — Aravind shipped between meetings, this team pivoted between quarters. I would weight this higher if there were a public GitHub trail or product cadence telemetry; there is not, so I'm anchoring on the exits.

On the single-miracle test, Gojiberry AI is clean: the miracle is LLM-driven personalization at outbound scale, already validated. **Decagon (2023)** is the one-anchor comparable — workflow infrastructure assuming the LLM works, with all remaining risk in GTM and product execution. Gojiberry AI inherits the same architecture: no new physics, no regulatory unlock required, no proprietary data partnership pending. The risk surface is entirely competitive and distributional, which is exactly where Clay and Apollo are strongest.

My 36-month forecast: Gojiberry AI reaches $8-15M ARR by mid-2028, fails to cross the SMB→mid-market chasm against Clay's agent rollout, and gets acqui-hired by Clay, Apollo, or HubSpot in the $50-150M range — a 3-5x outcome on a $500K check, not a portfolio-defining position. The mechanism producing this: the revenue velocity is real (Harvey signature) but the ACV ceiling is structural ($99/month SMB cohort, LinkedIn-signal dependency), and the index position is backwards (must beat #1 and #2, not ride them), so the asymptote is acquisition rather than IPO.

The bull case that stings: Gojiberry AI is profitable at $2.5M ARR with $500K raised, which means founder dilution stays low and the team can compound for years without the 11x.ai burn trap — and the CoCo AI + Edusign exits prove this team finishes things. If the agentic SDR category fragments by vertical the way email tools did, a profitable bootstrapper-mindset team can absolutely take a vertical slice to $50M ARR and stay independent. The dossier evidence that wins for me anyway: every G2 reviewer is sub-50 employees and the product is gated on public LinkedIn engagement, which is the textbook "false signal" cohort (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) — pilot tourism by founders pitching founders, not durable enterprise adoption. **The verifiable condition that flips my verdict**: a signed $100K+ ACV contract with a named Series B+ company disclosed within 6 months, demonstrating Gojiberry AI clears the chasm Clay and Apollo own. Until then, small check on the velocity, eyes on the cohort mix.
