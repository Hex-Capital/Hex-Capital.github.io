## The Idea

- **Problem:** B2B teams running outbound waste effort on cold lists, SDRs, and sequencers and "guess what works"; founders cite this as the "most painful part" they faced operating their prior SaaS (wearefounders.uk case study, 2026).
- **Approach:** Gojiberry tracks 30+ intent signals (competitor engagement, funding rounds, job changes, LinkedIn post interactions), then enriches, drafts, sends, follows up, and books meetings as one AI agent (SyncGTM review, 2026; salesforge.ai review, 2026).
- **Differentiation:** Versus Apollo (volume + price on 250M-contact DB) and Clay (data orchestration across 50+ sources) — Gojiberry is positioned as an intent-first agent rather than data tooling; vs 11x.ai (AI SDR "Alice") it claims "2-5x higher reply rates" through signal targeting (YC page; the-ai-corner.com, 2026).
- **Business Model:** Pro plan $99/month with 30+ intent signals, 2 LinkedIn senders, unlimited campaigns; Custom plan for enterprise (SyncGTM, April 2026).
- **TAM/SAM:** No public TAM/SAM data found for this specific segment.
- **GTM / Distribution:** Founder-led LinkedIn content (Romàn publishes growth/sales playbooks), Product Hunt launch March 2, 2026, G2 listing, and inbound from blog/case studies (producthunt.com; g2.com; LinkedIn posts by Romàn Czerny).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Compounding signal/outcome data — company states "Every campaign makes Gojiberry smarter… learns which buyers convert, which signals matter, which messages get replies" (YC page, 2026); no proprietary data partnerships disclosed.
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Cross-customer learning loop on which intent signals/messages convert could become a data moat if scale advantage materializes; unproven now because cohort is 2,000 SMB customers (G2 reviews note all reviewers are <50-employee firms).
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: No structural barrier identified at this stage — incumbents Clay ($100M ARR), Apollo ($150M ARR) and 11x.ai are building overlapping agentic outbound with greater capital and data assets (Sacra; TechCrunch, June 2025).
- **Commoditization risk:** Signal aggregation depends on public LinkedIn/web signals that any competitor can scrape; G2 reviewers note the tool "depends heavily on social and public signals" (g2.com, 2026).

## Key Risks

- **Pivot recency and SMB-only customer base:** Current product was rebuilt after the original "AI sales assistant" v1 had a 2,000-waitlist but zero conversions (wearefounders.uk, 2026); all 11 G2 reviews come from <50-employee businesses (g2.com, 2026), so enterprise durability is unproven.
- **Well-capitalized incumbents in overlapping space:** Clay ($100M Series C at $3.1B, CapitalG) and Apollo ($251M raised, $150M ARR) are building AI agent layers on top of larger data/distribution assets (TechCrunch, June 2025; Sacra, 2025), creating direct substitution risk.
- **Public-signal dependency:** G2 reviewers explicitly flag that "if your target audience is not active on LinkedIn or does not publicly engage with posts, you may get fewer intent signals" (g2.com, 2026), capping TAM to LinkedIn-active buyer segments and exposing the product to LinkedIn platform/policy risk.
- **Category-level customer-success risk:** 11x.ai's $14M claimed vs $3M actual ARR and 70-80% churn (gtmlens.com Q2 2026) suggests AI SDR outputs can fail in production at scale; Product Hunt sentiment for Gojiberry is mixed (~30% negative per syncgtm.com, 2026), an early signal worth monitoring.
- **Reported-metric verification:** ARR ($2.5M), growth (35% MoM), and customer count (2,000+) are self-reported on the YC page and founder LinkedIn posts (Nov 2025) and not independently audited.
