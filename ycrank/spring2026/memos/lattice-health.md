## The Idea

- **Problem:** [Inferred]: Hospitals and radiology groups deploying multiple FDA-cleared clinical AI imaging tools (e.g., Aidoc, Gleamer, Rad AI) face fragmented integration, monitoring, and workflow orchestration across PACS/EHR systems; current alternative is point-vendor integrations or in-house glue code.
- **Approach:** [Inferred]: An "operating system" layer that unifies deployment, orchestration, and monitoring of third-party and proprietary clinical AI models for imaging across a health system (YC tagline, "operating system for clinical AI in imaging," YC page).
- **Differentiation:** [Inferred]: Differs from single-application AI vendors (Aidoc — acute triage; Gleamer — fracture X-ray; Rad AI — reporting workflow) by acting as a horizontal platform rather than a single diagnostic model (competitor descriptions, Docus.ai 2026; CB Insights).
- **Business Model:** No public pricing data found; [Inferred]: enterprise SaaS sold to hospital systems / radiology groups given B2B Infrastructure tag (YC page).
- **TAM/SAM:** Radiology AI market estimated by MarketsandMarkets in its "Radiology AI Market Report 2025-2030" (MarketsandMarkets, 2025); specific dollar figure not retrieved in search snippet.
- **GTM / Distribution:** No public GTM data found; [Inferred]: direct enterprise sales to health systems and radiology groups, consistent with B2B Infrastructure positioning on YC page.

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** No defensibility signals found in public sources.
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Integration depth (PACS/EHR/HL7/DICOM connections) and accumulated model-performance/monitoring data across deployed AI tools could create switching costs; unproven now as the company is solo-founder, pre-launch on public web (YC page, Spring 2026 batch).
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Single-application AI imaging vendors (Aidoc, Gleamer, Rad AI) would face channel conflict commoditizing their own models if they built a neutral orchestration layer hosting competitors' models (competitor positioning, Docus.ai 2026).
- **Commoditization risk:** [Inferred]: PACS incumbents (GE Healthcare, Philips, Sectra) and AI marketplaces (Blackford, Nuance Precision Imaging Network, Sirona/Nvidia MONAI) could replicate orchestration features; cloud players (Microsoft, AWS HealthLake Imaging) also have building blocks.
