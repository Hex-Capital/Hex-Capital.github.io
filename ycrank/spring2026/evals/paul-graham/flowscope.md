# flowscope -- Paul Graham Evaluation

flowscope is the inverse of the company I keep funding: the work is unpleasant in the right way, but the founders haven't yet done the one unpleasant thing that matters before the check. Javier Leguina and Samuel Mirpuri want to shadow employees for two weeks and ship automations on existing systems — that's a real schlep, the kind of physical-world enterprise grind RPA boutiques have done badly for a decade. What flowscope's dossier doesn't contain is the cereal-box moment: no shipped demo on Hacker News, no paying pilot with a named CFO, no Armstrong-style prototype built to overturn a rejection. The schlep is named; the animal is not.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the load-bearing analogy on the schlep axis because Brian Armstrong wanted to do the bitcoin compliance work the rest of his batch was unconsciously routing around, and that appetite became the moat. flowscope's "2-week shadow then ship to production" is a schlep of the same family — going into a mid-market CFO's office, watching humans reconcile invoices, then deploying code into their actual SAP instance is exactly the offline grind UiPath ($PATH) and Adept ($415M raised, talent acqui-hired to Amazon) both flinched away from in favor of horizontal platforms. Where flowscope diverges from Armstrong is that Armstrong shipped the Android wallet before YC said yes; flowscope's dossier lists zero customer logos, zero pilot dollars, and no Product Hunt launch as of May 2026. The schlep is correctly identified. Whether Leguina and Mirpuri will personally run into the schlep at 2am, or hire it out, is the open question.

The Pass Example I wrote — two MBAs, glossy deck, "we'll hire engineers post-funding" — is the failure mechanism to test flowscope against, and the test comes back uncomfortably warm. Samuel Mirpuri is Harvard MBA plus QuantumBlack/McKinsey, which is the exact resume that triggered the Pass Example's red flag. Leguina rescues this from being a pure mismatch — he was Founding Engineer at Model ML (YC W24) and ML Eng at Encord (YC W21), which means he is the technical half of a real pair, not a credential. But flowscope's dossier offers no pre-funding artifact, no public GitHub project with stars (jleguina has 8 repos, none notable), no shipped revenue stunt. McKinsey lineage plus a competent engineer is not the **Dropbox** (2007) HN-demo pattern; it's the consulting-firm-in-startup-clothing pattern, and I have to score it that way until evidence overrides.

On organic-idea fit, the mechanism over 18 months either compounds or it doesn't: if Mirpuri spent QuantumBlack engagements watching CFOs do the exact reconciliation work flowscope now maps, then every customer call is muscle memory and pricing intuition; if he was building decks one layer removed from the workflow, flowscope discovers the actual texture of finance ops in production and pivots hard. The dossier hedges with "[Inferred]: Mirpuri's QuantumBlack/McKinsey background maps to the enterprise-consulting buyer" — that "[Inferred]" is doing heavy lifting. **Dropbox** (2007) didn't need an inferred founder-pain claim; Drew Houston forgot his USB stick, period.

On hacker genealogy, the counterfactual that would flip flowscope a tier is concrete: if Leguina had been a meaningful open-source contributor to an agent framework — a LangChain core committer, a public computer-use harness with thousands of stars — flowscope would read as a Collison-shaped bet where the technical half is recognized in the hacker tribe. Eight unstarred repos and a UCL MSc plus an Imperial aero degree is a working engineer's footprint, not Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010). Competent. Not tribally marked.

On co-founder configuration, **Reddit** (2005)'s Huffman and Ohanian were dorm roommates at UVA before the company existed; that two-year prior relationship is the anchor for why I trust 2-person teams to survive the YC pressure cooker. flowscope's dossier states plainly: "No public data on co-founder history." Mirpuri and Leguina may have met three months before applying or three years before; the dossier can't tell me, and the absence in a Spring 2026 batch profile is itself mild evidence the prior relationship isn't a story they wanted to tell.

My 36-month forecast for flowscope: $2-6M in services revenue, indistinguishable from a boutique AI-consulting shop, acquired by Accenture or a tier-2 systems integrator for $15-40M as a delivery-capability tuck-in, or wound down quietly when Mirpuri returns to a partner-track role. This is what the high-on-schlep, low-on-animal signature produces: real work, real revenue, no power law. flowscope doesn't have the cereal-box stunt that lets me imagine the Airbnb outcome.

The strongest bear-case-against-pass: **Airbnb** (2009) is a Known Exception precisely because I rejected the idea silently before the cereal boxes flipped me, and flowscope's Mirpuri-plus-Leguina pairing could surprise me the same way in person — a McKinsey operator who has watched 200 CFOs and an engineer who shipped at two prior YC companies is, on paper, a perfectly reasonable bet. The dismantling: Chesky's cereal boxes were a stunt nobody asked him to do, generated from desperation, that revealed something credentials cannot reveal. flowscope's dossier offers credentials where I need a stunt. Mirpuri's Harvard MBA and Leguina's Encord tenure are exactly what these founders would write on a slide; they are not what passes the animal test. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict before demo day: a named Fortune 1000 paid pilot — not LOI, paid — with a CFO or COO Mirpuri personally closed for $50K+, demonstrating that flowscope can convert McKinsey's network into revenue without McKinsey's invoice. Until then, pass.
