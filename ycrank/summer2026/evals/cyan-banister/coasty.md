# Coasty -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Coasty's signature is a founder with a genuinely felt origin story dropped into the single most consensus YC category of 2026 -- Nitish Kovuru watched an AI agent delete his production database on his laptop, and the company he built from that bruise is now competing on a public benchmark (OSWorld, 82%) against Anthropic, OpenAI, Browserbase ($67.5M raised), Browser Use (~$17M), Kernel ($22M Series A), and Anchor ($6M). The lived-experience rung is the only place I light up. Everything else fights my thesis.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril is the HIGH rung for founder-surfaced-from-lived-experience -- he shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever pitched defense, so the biography pre-dated the company by years. Kovuru's "AI agent wrecked my prod DB" story is the right *shape* of origin -- autobiographical, tactile, the sandboxed-VM architecture flows directly from the wound -- but a single incident is not a decade adjacent to the problem the way Hanke had with Keyhole before Niantic. Where Coasty diverges from the Luckey template is that the felt moment produced a *reliability* insight (sandbox the agent) rather than a *product* insight that only this founder could see; the dev.to post is the founder telling me he learned a generalizable lesson, not that he is uniquely positioned to solve it. Jannu's Microsoft/Purdue/TruGen ML resume is competent-credentialed but pattern-matches closer to "ex-FAANG PM, Stanford CS" than to the outsider trajectory I write checks for.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling from my Pass Example is the LOW rung for magically-weird, and Coasty sits on top of it. The failure mechanism there is structural: six top funds chasing, deck priced as if the outcome is already certain, moat that lives in the model rather than the founder. Trace it in Coasty's dossier -- Browserbase $67.5M, Kernel $22M Series A, Browser Use $17M, Anchor $6M, plus Anthropic Computer Use API (Oct 2024) and OpenAI Operator (Jan 2025) shipping the same primitive natively. Coasty is not the misfit nobody will fund; Coasty is the eleventh entrant in a category Firecrawl already wrote a "11 Best Browser Agents 2026" listicle about. The thesis is to find them *before it becomes consensus*, and the consensus has already arrived with a benchmark and a press circuit.

Single-CEO authority on Coasty is a counterfactual exercise: the dossier names two co-founders, no CEO designation, no equity split, no operating agreement language. For this score to flip a tier upward, I would need to see Jannu or Kovuru identified publicly as CEO with documented tiebreaker authority -- a LinkedIn title, a press quote, anything resolving the HQ Trivia / GameCrush risk I have been burned by before. Two-founder ML teams without a public CEO declaration in month one tend to relitigate it in month eighteen.

Capital-intensity for Coasty compounds the wrong direction over 18 months. This is a pure software wrapper over frontier vision-language models -- no hardware moat, no regulatory permission asset, no spectrum, no permit, no fertility clinic, no flared-gas wellhead. The mechanism that worried me about Crusoe in 2018 was commodity exposure I could underwrite; the mechanism that worries me about Coasty is that every Anthropic and OpenAI model release directly compresses the 82-vs-72 OSWorld delta, and Coasty has no countervailing asset that compounds on the same clock. The $19/$50/$99 PLG pricing is honest about what this is -- a dev-tools subscription, not a frontier swing.

Physical verifiability for Coasty is the one place a Niantic-style parallel partly loads -- I can install Coasty, type plain English at `/v1/predict`, watch a sandboxed VM execute, and feel whether the agent self-corrects. The open-source `coasty-ai/open-computer-use` repo means I can read the trajectory data shape directly. That is real, and better than a B2B API I would have to take on faith. It is not the Ingress moment, though; with Niantic I felt a *category* coming alive in my hand. With Coasty I feel a *demo* working, which is a different signal at a different magnitude.

My 36-month forecast on Coasty: acqui-hired by Browserbase, Kernel, or a frontier lab for under $30M as the OSWorld delta closes with the next two Anthropic/OpenAI model releases, or quietly winds down with the open-source repo left as the artifact. The mechanism is the consensus-category compression I just described -- when the moat is a benchmark and the benchmark is public, the half-life is one model cycle, and Kovuru's reliability framing is more likely to be absorbed as a feature inside a larger browser-infra play than to compound into a standalone outcome.

The strongest bear-against-my-forecast case is the HandUp exception -- I have written checks before where the founder's autobiographical wound (Kovuru's prod-DB incident mirrors my own "agent damaged something I cared about" reflex) overrode commercial-defensibility weakness, and Kovuru's origin story is exactly that shape. The reason it does not win here: HandUp's override was *mission alignment with my own homelessness origin*, which is a much narrower trigger than "founder had a bad day with an AI agent," and the HandUp lesson was explicitly that I accepted the modest ceiling. Coasty asks me to accept a modest ceiling *and* fund into the most crowded category on YC's S26 list. The condition that would flip my verdict: a named Fortune 500 deploying Coasty into a production internal-systems workflow with a signed multi-year contract, because that resolves the enterprise-trust gap the founder himself flagged in the Humans in the Loop interview and gives Coasty a moat-asset (the customer relationship) the model labs structurally cannot replicate.
