# Touchmark -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Touchmark is a single-sided B2B billing primitive in a platform-shaped market -- the dimensional signature is "huge category, no network compounding," which is the exact shape of company I keep telling Nivi to skip at the angel-check stage. Ilia Bolgov spent 6y9m as a Product person at Revolut and Roman Yanushevskyi did two Citadel QR internships, and they are shipping a TypeScript SDK that re-prices each LLM call after an LLM-as-judge eval. The leverage check fires first, before anything else, and Touchmark fails it.

Twitter (2007) is the canonical permissionless-media-leverage bet -- anyone posts, the platform charges nothing, marginal replication cost is zero, and the 400x outcome compounded on the architecture, not on a sales motion. Touchmark's @touchmark/sdk is code, technically, but the distribution it implies is *embedded billing infrastructure sold to AI platform decision-makers* -- the SDK only runs after a vendor's CFO and head-of-platform sign off on letting a third party adjust their invoices downward. That is labor-and-capital leverage wearing a code-leverage costume. No GitHub repos for either founder, no open-source posture on touchmark.ai, no Product Hunt launch, no organic community contribution predating the round -- every signal I look for in the Stack Overflow (2008) or Replit (2023) pattern is absent. The Twitter analogy loads only on surface technology; it breaks on distribution geometry.

Perplexity (2022) is the failure-mode-that-I-funded-anyway on the marketplace dimension -- single-sided, no Nth-user compounding -- and I waived criterion #2 only because criterion #1 (permissionless API distribution), criterion #3 (Aravind's Berkeley retrieval PhD), and pre-consensus AI-cycle timing all cleared top quartile emphatically. Touchmark exhibits the same single-sidedness without any of the three offsetting clearances: distribution is B2B-sales-gated, the founders' specific knowledge is credentialed but not domain-shipped, and the category (AI billing) had its consensus moment when Stripe paid $1B for Metronome in December 2025. The "cross-customer dataset of output-quality distributions" the dossier inferred as future moat is exactly the kind of *eventual* network argument that vanishes when incumbents (Orb's Vercel/Replit/Pinecone/Perplexity install base per AIBilling.dev) ship the same eval wrapper next quarter. The Perplexity exception does not extend here.

Specific knowledge: Bolgov's 6y9m at Revolut and Yanushevskyi's Citadel QR work are real credentials, but credentials are not the gate. Aravind shipped retrieval research; Ivan Zhao rebuilt productivity tools from adolescence; Amjad Masad obsessed over Repl.it since 2011. Touchmark's founders have product and quant resumes adjacent to the problem, not a multi-year shipped artifact in usage billing or LLM evals. The Yanushevskyi-at-Lovable line is the closest thing to a domain artifact and it is an internship. This reads closer to "ex-fintech PM and ex-quant intern identified market opportunity in $1B Metronome-acquisition TAM" than to founder-product-market-fit at the depth I underwrite.

Market and pivot-room is the highest dimension and it is where Touchmark actually earns its score. Billing infrastructure is platform-shaped -- Metronome's $1B acquisition by Stripe (Dec 2025), Orb's ~$44M raised, Lago's $34M from FirstMark, Solvimon's $9.6M seed -- so the seed runway can absorb a pivot from quality-adjusted billing into AI observability, evals-as-a-service, or LLM monitoring without re-fundraising. This is the one dimension where Touchmark would not get strangled by criterion-#4's "first idea never works" axiom.

Forecast, 36 months: Touchmark either acqui-hires into Orb or Metronome-inside-Stripe for under $30M, or pivots off billing entirely into LLM evaluation/observability where the eval library has standalone value -- the forecast follows directly from a platform-shaped market plus a single-sided architecture, because pivot-room without compounding user value is the *exact* exit-by-acqui-hire geometry. The "average to zero" revenue-neutrality clause in Touchmark's own copy is the mechanism that produces this -- AI vendors do not pay take-rate to a vendor whose value prop is reducing their invoices, so the business has to pivot away from refund-mechanics or sell into a billing primitive owner.

The strongest case against my pass goes: Bolgov spent seven years inside a usage-priced fintech that bills millions of users on metered consumption, Yanushevskyi's Citadel QR experience is *literally* quantitative-output evaluation, the Bessemer 2026 AI pricing playbook reports per-seat SaaS fell from 21% to 15% in twelve months, and 2026 is the first AI renewal cycle where outcome-based pricing has explicit buyer pull. That bull case stings -- founder-market fit on the eval-mechanics side is genuinely better than my paragraph above gave credit for. It dismantles on the leverage architecture: even if the eval is technically correct, the distribution requires incumbent billing platforms (Stripe-Metronome, Orb) to either partner or be displaced, and switching costs in billing primitives are documented to be high -- the dossier itself names this. The verdict flips to Invest if Touchmark ships an open-source eval-scoring library that gets organic GitHub adoption from AI platforms *before* the priced round closes -- the Stack Overflow (2008) and Replit (2023) pattern, leverage architecture flipping from permissioned to permissionless. Until then, this is a competent team building a billing wrapper in a category that just had its $1B consensus moment, and the asymmetric upside math does not punch through.
