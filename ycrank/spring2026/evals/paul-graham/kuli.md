# Kuli -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Kuli is the kind of company where the cofounder bond is the strongest single signal in the dossier and the hacker lineage is the weakest -- a Hodara/Hassan pair who have been "building side hustles together for about ten years" (Hassan, LinkedIn 2026) selling AI to enterprise marketing buyers, with no GitHub trail and no Lisp-prize-equivalent under either name. That pairing -- long-bonded commercial operators, no hacker spine -- is what I have to evaluate, because it tells me the ceiling. This is not Patrick and John Collison rebuilding commerce from the API up. This is a competent two-person go-to-market team that has landed Disney, Nivea, and Havas as named accounts in five months.

The Airbnb cereal-box rung is what I reach for on animal resourcefulness, because the question I ask about Kuli is not "did they raise capital" but "did they do something unscalable to get the first dollars in." Brian Chesky and Joe Gebbia sold over 1,000 boxes at $40 each because no investor would write the check. Kuli's animal evidence is different in shape: in five months of operating they got a Fortune 100 entertainment brand to publicly say they "ship 3x more campaigns without adding headcount," and they got Disney/Nivea/Havas onto the customer slide before the YC launch. That is real enterprise distribution at pre-seed -- most marketing-AI YC companies cannot fake that in a year, let alone five months. But Kuli's dossier does not name the stunt. There is no equivalent of the cereal boxes, no Armstrong-wallet-prototype moment after a rejection. The logos may be the stunt, or they may be Hassan's KPMG rolodex converting warm intros, and the dossier does not let me tell which.

The Pass Example from my own file -- the MBA team with the glossy deck, the $50B TAM slide, the "we'll hire engineers post-funding" plan -- is the failure mechanism I have to test Kuli against on hacker genealogy, because Hassan's six years of KPMG M&A advisory is exactly the credential profile that flipped that example to a pass. Kuli does not fully repeat the pattern: Hodara has an EPFL MSc in Robotics and ran AI systems allocating $20M of paid-ads spend at Aircall, which is more shipped technical work than the MBA team had. But the dossier finds "no public repos" for Hodara, no open-source contributions, no hacker-community presence, and the third teammate's name is not even surfaced. The "watches every video" claim in Kuli's positioning is the kind of pipeline a Collison-shaped team would have a benchmark for; Kuli has no published benchmark and no proprietary dataset disclosed. Hackers leave tracks. Kuli's tracks are enterprise logos, not commits.

Organic-pain fit compounds or fails over eighteen months depending on whether the founders keep choosing the product over the next adjacent revenue. Hodara was the buyer-analog -- he ran AI for marketing spend at Aircall and Logitech -- so he has been near the pain, but the dossier never says he personally suffered the creator-discovery schlep that Kuli sells against. Hassan advised on M&A, which is even further from the workflow. Drew Houston forgot his USB stick; the Collisons had been hacking commerce since Auctomatic. Kuli's founders did not need Kuli for themselves before they built it, and that is the kind of distance that produces a roadmap drifting toward whatever the Disney account manager asks for next, rather than the obstinate product instinct that built Dropbox.

Counterfactually, for Kuli's schlep score to flip up a tier, the dossier would need to name the unpleasant work the founders are personally absorbing -- the platform TOS negotiation with TikTok, the manual creator-rights paperwork, the per-brand fine-tuning labor. "Watches every video on socials" sounds like a schlep, and at compute cost it is one, but foundation-model APIs are eating the multimodal-video moat in real time. Kuli's dossier explicitly flags this: "video-LLM capabilities are increasingly available via foundation-model APIs." Coinbase's compliance grind got worse for Armstrong every year, which is what made it a moat. Kuli's video-watching grind gets cheaper for every entrant every quarter, which is the inverse.

Stripe is the single anchor I use on cofounder configuration, because Patrick and John had the brother-bond, and Hodara and Hassan have ten years of side-hustle-building together. That decade of prior collaboration is the part of Kuli's dossier that most resembles a portfolio anchor. The break from Stripe is that the Collisons were both hackers; Kuli is hacker-plus-M&A-advisor, which is a sales-coverage pair, not a product-coverage pair. The third teammate could close that gap, but the dossier does not name them.

My 36-month forecast for Kuli is acquisition by CreatorIQ or Sprout Social at $30M-$80M for $3M-$8M of ARR, as a workflow add-on to a seat-priced incumbent suite -- a Kiko-shaped outcome, not a Twitch one. The mechanism is straightforward: Kuli's wedge is workflow ownership at enterprise consumer-brand marketing teams, the underlying video-understanding capability is commoditizing under them, and incumbents holding $35K-$200K/yr CreatorIQ contracts have both the distribution and the motive to either bundle or buy. Hassan's KPMG M&A instinct will recognize the offer when it lands.

The bull case against this forecast is that Disney, Nivea, and Havas in five months is not a normal pre-seed traction profile -- it is the kind of enterprise pull that, if it compounds, produces a $50M-ARR standalone company before the incumbents finish their AI roadmaps. The "3x campaigns without adding headcount" testimonial from a Fortune 100 brand is the seat-compression story that justifies replacing CreatorIQ entirely, not augmenting it. I take that case seriously. What dismantles it for me is that the dossier shows no proprietary data asset and no model benchmark to defend the wedge once GPT-class video understanding is a $0.01-per-minute API call; the moat has to be workflow lock-in, and workflow lock-in at enterprise marketing buyers is exactly what incumbents already have. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Kuli ships either a proprietary creator-outcomes dataset that incumbents demonstrably cannot replicate, or hits $5M ARR within eighteen months of the May 2026 launch -- either one tells me the founders are running toward the schlep, not the logo slide.
