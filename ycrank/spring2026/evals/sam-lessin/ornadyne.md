# Ornadyne -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Ornadyne is the kind of bet I find structurally interesting and almost always pass on: two genuinely credentialed deep-tech engineers — Geourg Kivijian with a Cal Poly MS thesis (#3083) on the exact variable-amplitude flapping mechanism they're commercializing, and Armen Arakelyan ex-SpaceX Starship — building a better form factor in a category that already exists. The signature is high on sanity-insanity calibration and low on primitive creation. Robot birds for surveillance sounds insane at the dinner table, but inside the defense ISR market it's a stealth-optimized sUAS competing against Teledyne FLIR Black Hornet 4 and Skydio X10D. That's not a new behavior. That's a form factor.

The sanity-insanity ladder runs from Raj at Solana — engineer who builds things that work, pitching a Layer 1 in the face of "Ethereum won" consensus — through Stewart Butterfield at Slack, sane proven founder but the idea wasn't crazy, down to the venture-studio idea-guy with synthetic commitment. Ornadyne sits closer to the Raj rung than I'd expect for a YC P26 hardware deal. Geourg Kivijian won AIAA Region VI 1st place for "Aerodynamic Force Characterization of a Novel Variable Amplitude Flapping Wing Robot," then went to JPL on Mars Sample Return, then to Astrolab for lunar rovers. That's not a pitch deck — that's a person whose entire CV is the company's mechanism. Arakelyan ran the USC Rocket Propulsion Lab machining team that set student/amateur solid-motor world records. These are missionary hard-tech engineers, and what they're building sounds fucking crazy until you read the thesis. Pinning evidence: AIAA 1st place + Cal Poly thesis #3083 on the *specific* drive mechanism. This is the rare case where sanity-insanity scores genuinely high.

Primitive creation runs from Venmo — created peer-to-peer mobile payments as a verb — through Slack, which replaced email, down to "fine-tuned GPT-4 for industry X." Ornadyne sits between the bottom and middle rungs and closer to the bottom. The dossier itself concedes "Flapping-wing aircraft have existed for years." Flygildi raised a $2.2M Seed in September 2023 for the same form factor. Northwestern Polytechnical University already demonstrated a 4+ hour eagle-mimetic flapping drone (CGTN, March 2026). TU Delft's DelFly lineage produced Flapper Drones a decade ago. Aerium's RoBird has been bird-mimicking commercially for years. Ornithopter-as-ISR is a known mechanism with academic literature going back to the 2000s. Ornadyne might execute it better, but nobody will say "the Venmo of drones" about a stealth quad replacement. That's category-share capture, not category creation.

Consensus mispricing is split. The HIGH rung is Solana at four cents; LOW is the AI-copilot YC pitch. Defense tech writ large is consensus-hot post-Anduril — premium pricing, ten VCs bidding — but ornithopters specifically sit in a corner most generalists don't touch, and Flygildi's modest $2.2M round signals the sub-segment isn't priced for perfection. Ornadyne lands in the middle: not Solana-cheap, not AI-wrapper-overheated. Mid-teens out of 30. Structural durability is the dimension I'd over-pay for if I were paying — Metropolis is the HIGH rung, AI copilots the LOW rung, and Ornadyne lands closer to Metropolis: physical hardware, mechanical IP, ITAR/EAR encumbrance, defense procurement lock-in. AI cannot commoditize a flapping-wing actuator. Capital discipline is where I get nervous. Allbirds is the LOW rung — physical product that still needed factory-farm capital to scale. Defense hardware is more capital-intensive than DTC sneakers. A two-person team with hiring flag False on the YC profile means they haven't started the burn, but they will, and Ornadyne will need flight-test infrastructure, manufacturing, and BD hires that no $500K YC check survives. No evidence Geourg or Armen have articulated what happens if this is the last money they ever raise.

My 36-month forecast: Ornadyne wins one or two SBIR Phase II / OTA awards in the $3-10M range on the strength of the Cal Poly mechanism plus the SpaceX manufacturing pedigree, fails to displace Teledyne FLIR's Black Hornet 4 in the Soldier-Borne Sensor program of record, and gets acqui-hired by Teledyne, Anduril, or Red Cat for $40-80M by 2029. That's a fine outcome for the founders. It's a 2-3x for an angel check at YC valuation, not a fund-returner. The mechanism: defense procurement timelines are 5-7 years, the SRR Tranche 2 and SBS slots are already filled by Skydio X10D and Black Widow, and the strategic value of the flapping mechanism is higher to an incumbent with a contract vehicle than to a standalone two-person company. Ornadyne is a feature waiting to be acquired, not a category waiting to be born.

The bull case stings, so let me steelman it. Anduril looked exactly like this in 2017 — weird hardware, defense procurement skeptics, "DoD won't buy from a startup" consensus — and it's now worth north of $30B. Counter-UAS detection is genuinely degrading conventional sUAS in contested airspace (the Ukraine war has made this a procurement crisis), and biomimetic stealth could be the platform answer. Geourg's variable-amplitude flapping mechanism, if it actually delivers endurance and payload that Northwestern Polytechnical hasn't matched, IS a primitive — a new ISR platform category, not a form-factor swap. Ornadyne could be a Metropolis-style physical moat where the mechanical IP compounds for a decade. Here's why I still pass: Anduril had Palmer Luckey's distribution and Trae Stephens' DC network from day one. Ornadyne has two brilliant engineers and no defense BD signal in the dossier — no advisors, no named DoD relationships, no SBIR history. The Red Flag that lands hardest is regulatory dependency: the entire profitability path runs through ITAR-cleared U.S. defense buyers and a procurement cycle the company has zero stated leverage over. That's the scooter-companies pattern — unit economics conditional on a permission structure outside founder control. The condition that flips my verdict: Ornadyne signs a named DoD prime or SOCOM evaluation contract within 12 months of YC demo day, or recruits a defense BD operator with prior program-of-record wins. Until then, this is a mechanism, not a company.
