# Chert -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Chert is a smart-generalist team -- Gary Gao (Penn CS/Wharton) and Ian Fong (NYU Math, 8x hackathon winner) -- building an API wrapper on a protocol Apple controls. The signature is brutal in my framework: the dimension Chert places highest on is the founder-trio gate, and even there only at the mid-rung; the dimension it places lowest on is leverage architecture, because every single dollar of Chert's revenue exists at the sufferance of one company in Cupertino that has never sanctioned the integration. This is a permission-stack business wearing a Twilio costume.

The leverage check is where Chert dies fastest in my model. **Anchorage Digital** is the only company in my portfolio that knowingly violated the permissionless-leverage rule -- it required an OCC Treasury charter (January 2021) and I funded it anyway, because the category-timing call from my **MetaStable** (2014) crypto thesis was strong enough to flip "permissioned" into "permission-as-moat." Chert does not have that override. The Chert dossier names Sendblue (5+ years in market), LoopMessage, Blooio ($39/mo flat-rate), and Linq -- four funded competitors already operating on the same unofficial iMessage techniques, all subject to the same Apple kill-switch. No regulatory moat accrues to Chert from getting there first because there is no regulator; there is only Apple, which could ship an official business-iMessage API in a WWDC keynote and zero the entire category. The "Twilio for iMessage" framing on the Launch HN post is exactly the framing that fails my opening question: code leverage, yes, but distribution requires gatekeeper permission, so it is not the leverage I underwrite.

Sendblue is the load-bearing comparison on category structure, and it tells me the asymmetric-payoff math doesn't work. Sendblue has been live 5+ years with the broadest feature set per its own comparison content, and PitchBook reports $500K of funding -- meaning even the category leader has not unlocked a venture-scale outcome after half a decade in the same protocol-gated swamp. My "downside one X, upside 10,000 X" rule (Knowledge Project #18) collapses when the category ceiling is itself bounded by an Apple policy choice. Chert's claimed 65% response rates and 90% action rates are unverified company numbers without a named case study, and even if true, they are a feature that Sendblue or HubSpot can replicate inside a quarter.

Founder specific knowledge is where Chert places relatively highest, but "relatively" is doing the work. Ivan Zhao at **Notion** (2013) had been rebuilding personal productivity tools since adolescence; Aravind Srinivas at **Perplexity** (2022) brought a Berkeley ML PhD in retrieval specifically. Chert's founders spent "the last six months building projects on iMessage for leasing companies, DTC startups, and home service agencies" per the Product Hunt launch -- that is six months of applied iteration, not the multi-year obsession that produces non-trainable edge. Gary's prior year on neuromorphic chips is impressive and irrelevant; Ian's Courant algorithms work is impressive and irrelevant. This is the "smart generalists picked a market" profile, one degree softer than the FAANG-PM red flag but architecturally the same shape.

Network compounding -- mechanism analysis over 18 months: Chert is a single-sided B2B API where customer N+1's onboarding produces zero value for customer N. There is no marketplace, no creator graph, no developer community contributing back upstream. The line-health-check data could in principle compound across customers into a deliverability advantage, but every CPaaS in history has claimed that and Twilio still competes on price. Eighteen months of single-sided ARR accretion does not produce the user-added-value compounding that underwrote **Uber** (2010) or **Stack Overflow** (2008); it produces a CPaaS reseller with a CRM-integration list (Salesforce, HubSpot, Attio, GoHighLevel, Close, Vapi) that any of those CRMs can build natively the moment Chert's GMV becomes interesting.

Market pivot room -- counterfactual: for Chert's market score to flip a tier, the dossier would need to name a credible adjacent expansion vector outside Apple's protocol, and it does not. SMS/RCS fallback is mentioned, but pivoting into SMS/RCS is pivoting into Twilio's home court with a 2-person team and $500K. The CPaaS TAM at $21.27B (Mordor, 2026) is real, but Chert occupies the iMessage-shaped slice of it that exists by Apple's tolerance. The "first idea never works" axiom (Venture Hacks 2007-10) requires room to maneuver; Chert's room is bounded by a protocol it does not own.

Founder trio -- the integrity leg gets the closest look. No contested exits, clean reads on both founders, and the hackathon-pair-since-2025 origin is the "play long-term games with long-term people" signal I look for. But the dossier flags that Chert's site lists LinkedIn and Grafana as "backers" alongside Y Combinator/Z Fellows/Betafund with no public announcement confirming direct investment from either. That is either careless or loose-with-truth, and "if someone is going around and talking about how honest they are, they're probably dishonest" (Knowledge Project #18) has an adjacent rule: founders who advertise backer logos they have not earned fail the same screen in a quieter register. Not disqualifying. Noted.

Forecast at 36 months: Chert is acquihired into Sendblue, HubSpot, or Close for $5-15M total consideration -- returning roughly 1x to YC's $500K -- after failing to break out of the iMessage-API commodity tier, or alternatively gets zeroed by an Apple policy change at WWDC 2027 or 2028. The mechanism is straightforward: Sendblue has a 5-year head start and $500K of funding, Blooio is racing to the bottom at $39/mo, and Apple is the cap on the entire category's outcome geometry. Smart founders, wrong leverage stack.

The bull case I owe steel-manning: AI-agent conversational quality crossed a threshold in 2025-26 such that 65% response rates on outbound iMessage are now technically real, and the channel itself -- blue-bubble, end-to-end-encrypted, with tapbacks and typing indicators -- is the highest-fidelity messaging surface that exists outside WhatsApp. If Chert's line-health technology is genuinely better at avoiding Apple's anti-spam bans than Sendblue's, that becomes the only thing customers care about, and the company could compound to $20-40M ARR before Apple notices. Hackathon-grade execution speed against a Sendblue that took $500K in 5 years is not nothing. The dismantle: my Anchorage exception required pre-consensus category timing from a thesis I had been accumulating into for years (**MetaStable** 2014). I have not been quietly accumulating into iMessage infrastructure. There is no pre-consensus category here -- four funded competitors is post-consensus -- and the override condition therefore does not trigger. **One verifiable condition flips my verdict**: if Chert publishes a signed enterprise contract above $500K ARR with a named Fortune 500 logo within 12 months AND demonstrates Apple has tacitly sanctioned the category (e.g., a public Apple partnership announcement or a formalized business iMessage API in which Chert is a reference integration), then the permission-gating risk has materially abated and the leverage check re-opens.
