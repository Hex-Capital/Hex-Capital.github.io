# TrustAI -- Elad Gil Evaluation

**TrustAI** is a workflow-suggestion overlay entering the already-crowded cross-tool automation category with Hannah Chung (CEO, MIT '25 DC intern) and Medha Venkatapathy (CTO, MIT CS+Physics, Lincoln Lab) -- and its analytical signature is the pairing of a plausibly single-miracle architecture with an entirely absent revenue curve. That pairing describes a specific archetype: technically competent MIT-lineage team building an AI-native re-do of Bardeen ($25.3M raised, Dropbox + HubSpot Ventures backed per TechCrunch Aug 2024) without a named 24-month catalyst, a shipped artifact history, or a distribution wedge that separates TrustAI from Zapier, Magical, Lindy, or n8n.

**Anduril** (2022) is my catalyst-ladder HIGH rung -- Ukraine created a specific, dateable Pentagon-software re-opening in a 24-month window, and Trae Stephens sat exactly in that window's execution path. TrustAI's dossier gives me no comparable catalyst: "2024-2026 improvements in browser-agent context capture" is tagged `[Inferred]` and describes a steady-state capability drift, not a step-function like ChatGPT-November-2022 or Ukraine-February-2022. Bardeen has been in-market since 2020 and Zapier since 2011; the "why now" for TrustAI reads as "LLMs got better" -- which is a margin-compression bet, not a market re-opening, and margin-compression bets are exactly what I passed on when the "OpenAI-for-X" wave came through. My *First Round Review* line applies: market first, and this market's window opened five years ago.

**Harvey** (2023) at "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch Nov 2025) is what "just working" looks like on my revenue-velocity axis; TrustAI shows the opposite silhouette. The dossier is explicit: no pricing page, demo-only Calendly funnel, no revenue disclosed, no waitlist, no Product Hunt launch, no Chrome Web Store listing, Hannah Chung's @hannah_chuu at 715 followers. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself the signal -- "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). TrustAI isn't hiding a real curve; it's a Summer 2026 batch that hasn't shipped a curve yet, which is stage-normal but leaves the dimension empty rather than positive.

Founder-shipping compounds over 18 months in a predictable way: the founders who ship between meetings compound into product-market fit even in crowded categories, and the ones who don't compound into acqui-hire outcomes. TrustAI's dossier gives me Hannah Chung as an MIT DC summer intern in 2025 and Medha Venkatapathy with USA Physics Team '21 and Lincoln Lab -- credential signals, not shipping signals. No GitHub repos found for either founder. No prior product artifacts named. This is the profile I described in my Hustle Commons framework as the disqualifier -- "has this founder actually shipped products before, not just at a big company, but independently?" -- and the dossier answers no. Compare **Perplexity** (2022): Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built. TrustAI's team may well be that, but the dossier surface doesn't show it.

For TrustAI's index-distribution score to flip a tier upward, one of two counterfactuals would need to hold: either the "background layer with no installation required" claim (company website, Jul 2026) resolves to a genuinely novel context-capture mechanism that becomes the default rail every AI-workflow entrant integrates against, or TrustAI wedges into a specific vertical (RevOps for HubSpot mid-market, say) where the workflow path is narrow enough to defend. Neither is evidenced. The dossier's own risk section flags that "no installation required" typically requires an extension, agent, or OAuth per app -- the mechanism isn't documented. Without either counterfactual, TrustAI competes for share against Bardeen and Zapier on features, which is the **Lyft**-Series-C posture I passed on: a #2 in a market with entrenched #1s and no share-inversion path.

**Decagon** (2023) is my single-anchor parallel on miracle-architecture -- I funded it because the LLM-accuracy miracle was validated and the remaining work was GTM and product execution in an open post-LLM customer-support market (per *AI Market Clarity*). TrustAI is structurally similar on paper: assume LLM tool-use reliability, execute GTM and product. But TrustAI adds a second miracle Decagon didn't need -- browser-side cross-tool context observation "with no installation" across HubSpot, Salesforce, Slack, Gmail, and Google Workspace, dependent on DOM stability and MV3 policy. That's one-and-a-half miracles, not one, and the second half is exactly the platform-dependency risk the dossier flags.

**36-month forecast**: TrustAI is dead or acqui-hired into HubSpot, Zapier, or Bardeen for sub-$10M within 36 months, with a ~20% chance of a pivot into a narrow RevOps-agent wedge (HubSpot-only, sub-$1M ARR at month 36). The mechanism is straightforward: no catalyst window means the incumbents' distribution advantage compounds faster than TrustAI's product advantage, and the "no-install" promise either breaks against platform policy or resolves into an extension that puts TrustAI back on Bardeen's exact playing field.

The bull case that would sting: Bardeen has been in-market since 2020 and hasn't broken out despite $25.3M and strategic backing from Dropbox and HubSpot Ventures -- which could mean the category is genuinely open for an AI-native re-do by a technically stronger team, and MIT CS + Lincoln Lab is not a weak technical profile. If TrustAI's "observed top-performer behavior" dataset compounds into personalized suggestions Bardeen structurally can't replicate, the *Decagon* template holds. I dismantle this with the dossier: Bardeen's non-breakout is evidence the *category* isn't a re-opening, not evidence a better team wins it -- "great teams crushed by terrible markets" is precisely why I run market-first. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: TrustAI showing >$50K MRR with three named enterprise logos signed within 90 days of YC Demo Day, month-over-month growth disclosed. Absent that curve, this is a pass in the mold of every Harvey-clone I've declined since November 2025.
