# Humwork -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Humwork is two Berkeley friends shipping an MCP server that lets Claude Code tap a human when it loops on a bug — clear CEO in Yash Goenka, clean co-founder split with Rohan Datta, sixteen-year friendship as the operating substrate, and almost nothing frontier or transgressive about the surface area. That's the signature: structurally well-governed software-marketplace wrapper in the most-fished pond of Spring 2026. Humwork has the *governance* of a company I'd back and the *category* of one I'd pass on, and those two facts have to fight each other in the same paragraph for the rest of this memo.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) shipped VR from a garage before pivoting to defense — the problem ownership was autobiographical, the kid had been gluing headsets together since he was fourteen. Humwork's founders are not that. Yash Goenka has shipped Qualify.bot at YC S25 (AI phone agents for commercial lenders) and Rohan Datta ran an AI voice platform clocking over one million call minutes — they have built agents, watched agents fail, and that is real. But the *lived* origin of the Humwork problem ("agents get 80% of the way there") is not biography, it's developer-tool ergonomics observed from inside the same agentic stack everyone else is observing. It's adjacent-domain operator credibility, not Palmer-Luckey lived-the-thing. Humwork sits between the MID rung and the LOW rung here — capable builders, not the founder whose childhood put them in front of this problem.

Six top funds chasing the same agentic AI darling — that's the LOW rung's failure mechanism: consensus crushes price and the founder is the model, not the moat. Does that pattern appear in Humwork? Partially. The launch press in TestingCatalog, Analytics Drift, The Agent Times, blockchain.news, and an @ycombinator quote-tweet of @theyashgoenka on April 15, 2026 is the exact YC-blessed agent-tooling consensus wrapper I've been deselecting on. Humwork's competitive frame — HumanLayer ($660K revenue per Getlatka), Skills.sh (Vercel-backed), Agensi — is itself a tell that several smart funds and operators are circling the *same conceptual neighborhood*. The A2P framing has a sliver of magical-weirdness ("AI agents will pay you to chat") but it's the kind of weird that gets a TestingCatalog headline, not the kind that gets credible investors passing on aesthetic grounds. Crusoe was uninvestable to most VCs at investment; Humwork is *eminently* investable to most VCs at investment.

Mechanism on CEO clarity over 18 months: Yash Goenka is the named CEO, Rohan Datta the CTO, sixteen years of friendship from Berkeley, no public 50/50 ambiguity, and the corporate vehicle (Orange AI Inc.) is Yash's prior shell — that's the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov/Kroll feud I've cited as the cautionary tale. The compounding here is real: clear authority means decisions get made fast when the agent platforms (Anthropic, Cursor) shift terms underneath them. Humwork has the governance hygiene I demanded after GameCrush (~2011). One asterisk: Yash is publicly listed as Co-founder & CEO of Qualify.bot (YC S25) *and* Humwork (YC P26) simultaneously, which is the founder-bandwidth red flag I've named explicitly — single CEO of two companies is a different failure mode than two CEOs of one, but it's still a leadership-attention defect.

Counterfactual on capital-intensity to flip this dimension a tier: Humwork would need to own something physical or regulated — for instance, an indemnity/insurance product for expert advice ingested by automated agent workflows, or a credentialing/licensure layer for legal and medical experts that competitors couldn't replicate without two years of regulatory work. The dossier explicitly notes "no public data found on insurance/indemnity structure" — that's the lever they haven't pulled. Today Humwork is an MCP server plus a credit-billing system plus a 3,000-expert Rolodex; any of Upwork ($760M FY24 revenue), Toptal, or GLG could ship an MCP endpoint on top of an existing freelancer pool tomorrow. The Mindbloom / Vigil Labs / Superpower lane is closed to Humwork unless they go take on liability the incumbents won't touch.

Single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability: Niantic's Ingress was the canonical case — I'd been playing since 2012, I'd felt the pull in my own thumbs before I sat outside John Hanke's door. Humwork's product is technically installable ("plug and play in 60 seconds" into Claude Code, Cursor, Lovable, Replit, Cline, OpenClaw), so a developer-me could pip-install it and feel the handoff. But I'm not running production agentic workflows that stall on legal nuance — the tactile artifact lives inside someone else's workflow, not mine. Humwork is more touchable than a B2B API but less touchable than a scooter ride. It clears the bar I set for HandUp but not the bar I set for Niantic.

Thirty-six months from now Humwork is either acquired by an agent platform — Anthropic or Cursor absorbing the "ask a human" provider into the first-party MCP surface for $20M to $60M — or it has pivoted hard into a single vertical (legal-expert routing, or software-engineering-only) after horizontal cold-start economics collapsed under that 3,000-experts-to-2,858-beta-questions supply/demand ratio. Standalone $50M+ ARR outcome is the tail; the mode is acqui-hire because Humwork's wedge sits inside a platform-owned distribution surface, and the platforms will either build it or buy it.

The bull case stings: Yash Goenka has *already* gone through YC once with Qualify.bot, Rohan has *already* shipped a million-minute voice system, the 87% resolution rate on 2,858 beta questions is real liquidity not vapor, 130% NRR in the Software Engineering domain is the only number on the page that suggests genuine pull, and if MCP becomes the dominant agent protocol and Anthropic doesn't ship a first-party "ask a human" provider, Humwork is the default A2P primitive for a generation of agents — the same way Twilio became the default SMS primitive while everyone underestimated how much developers hate building telecom plumbing. The HQ Trivia exception teaches me that tactile virality can override governance concerns; the inverse holds, which is that clean governance plus a real liquidity flywheel could override my category aversion. Humwork's dismantle: the 3,000 experts versus 2,858 cumulative questions is roughly 0.95 questions per expert across the entire beta — that's not a flywheel, that's a Rolodex stapled to an MCP server, and the cold-start collapses on the supply side the second expert payouts compress. Yash running two YC companies at once is the founder-bandwidth defect I treat as a near-term blow-up risk. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Humwork hits consistent expert utilization above five resolved consultations per expert per month for six straight months, *or* Anthropic names Humwork a reference MCP partner — either signal converts this from agent-tooling consensus into infrastructure with platform endorsement, and I'd take the call.
