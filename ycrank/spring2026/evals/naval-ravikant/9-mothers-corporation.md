# 9 Mothers -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

9 Mothers is a clean-integrity QA-SaaS team building an ITAR-registered 50-lb autonomous lethal turret called EDDA -- the analytical signature is a top-quartile founder-trio read paired with the most permissioned distribution architecture I have evaluated this batch, and that pairing is structurally backwards for the kind of asymmetric-payoff math that funds my ledger. Russell Smith ran Rainforest QA cleanly, the three co-founders shipped together for eight years, and they have now pointed that trust into CAGE code 11NF2D and DUNS 14-483-9928, which is the inverse of "Code and media are permissionless leverage" (Naval on X, May 2018).

**Twitter** (2007) is the HIGH rung of my permissionless-leverage ladder because anyone could post, the protocol charged nothing, and the marginal cost of replication was zero -- that is the architecture I keep telling Nivi we should be funding. 9 Mothers ships the exact opposite: a 23 kg kinetic weapon system whose distribution requires ITAR registration, a DoD CAGE code, and prime-contractor relationships, where every unit sold needs export-license review and every customer is the US Government or a prime (9mothers.com, 2026). The Twitter mechanism -- a media object replicating itself through the network at zero cost -- has no analog in EDDA, where each sale is a bespoke gated procurement gated by Directive 3000.09 and ITAR. 9 Mothers is closer to **Anchorage Digital** on the leverage spectrum than to anything else I own, but Anchorage was a known exception built on twelve years of pre-consensus crypto accumulation since **MetaStable** (2014); I have no analogous accumulating thesis in defense hardware, so the exception does not transfer.

**Perplexity** (2022) is the LOW rung on the network-compounding ladder I waived once, because Aravind Srinivas's retrieval PhD plus pre-consensus AI timing cleared three of five gates emphatically -- the lesson was that single-sided products earn the waiver only when criterion #1 and criterion #3 both pass at top quartile. 9 Mothers fails the same single-sidedness test (each turret sold is economically independent of the previous turret) but cannot earn the Perplexity waiver: criterion #1 collapses on ITAR-permissioned distribution, and criterion #3 collapses on a Rainforest QA founding team with zero documented weapons, robotics-hardware, or DoD program-of-record experience. The inferred "data flywheel from deployed turret engagement telemetry" in the dossier is the kind of theoretical compounding I discount when the deployment count is undisclosed.

Specific knowledge in 9 Mothers is bifurcated in a way that matters: Roman Khomenko's eight years of computer-vision and ML pipeline work at Rainforest QA, plus a Kharkiv MSc and DataRobot tenure, is real specific knowledge in the *vision-based autonomous targeting* leg of EDDA -- it would have been load-bearing if 9 Mothers were selling a perception SDK. It is not load-bearing for the *weapon system* leg, where 9 Mothers needs ballistics, servo control, MIL-STD ruggedization, and program-of-record contracting muscle that the Rainforest QA backstory does not produce and the dossier does not name a single advisor or board member to supplement. Compare to **Stack Overflow** (2008): Atwood and Spolsky *were* the user, writing for the developer they had been for fifteen years. 9 Mothers's founders are not the user -- none served in a kinetic role, none built weapons before, and the Police of Ukraine line on Khomenko's resume is the closest thing to a defense signal in the entire team section.

Counter-UAS as a market category passes the TAM gate -- $2.6B-$10.6B in 2026 with $29B in publicly announced government contracts in Q1 2026 alone -- but fails the pivot-room test that "niche markets don't work because the first idea never works" (Venture Hacks, 2007-10) was designed to surface. 9 Mothers cannot pivot EDDA to consumer, to creator tools, to developer infrastructure, or to any of the adjacent vectors my pivot-room rule was written for; ITAR registration and the autonomous-lethal product category are a one-way door. The dossier confirms the concentration: three of four announced products (Vor, Atla, Gleipnir) are 2027 deliverables, so 9 Mothers's near-term revenue depends on a single SKU competing directly with Allen Control Systems, which is also Austin-based, has $30M Series A from Craft Ventures, and was selected by the US Army Applications Lab in December 2025 for combat-vehicle integration.

The founder trio test is where 9 Mothers scores its highest -- Russell Smith left Rainforest QA without litigation, the three co-founders chose to bet together again after eight years, and nothing in the dossier signals self-promotional-honesty performance or contested prior exits. The integrity leg is clean by my **Epinions** (1999-2005) scar-tissue standard. The wrinkle is the Clearview-shaped one: at angel check size, autonomous lethal weapons is a category where the reputational tail is asymmetric in the wrong direction for a generalist seed angel without a defense thesis, and Clearview was an exception I underwrote because the specific knowledge was extreme. 9 Mothers's specific knowledge is not extreme enough to repeat the Clearview override.

Forecast: in 36 months 9 Mothers is either acquired by a tier-two defense integrator (L3Harris, Kratos, or Anduril itself) in the $40-120M range as an acqui-hire of the Khomenko CV team, or it has shut down after Allen Control Systems wins the dominant Group-1 kinetic-turret program-of-record slot it has already begun to occupy. The mechanism is that EDDA is competing as the smaller, later-funded entrant against a directly overlapping Austin company with more capital and earlier Army traction, and defense procurement is a winner-take-most game where the second-place autonomous turret does not generate the revenue trajectory required to clear a Series B at venture-acceptable terms.

The strongest opposing case is that the YC Spring 2026 cohort is the defense cohort -- Garry Tan partnered, the Summer 2026 RFS explicitly named counter-swarm, and $29B in Q1 2026 contracts means the market will support multiple winners on Group-1 kinetic, not just Allen Control Systems. The bull argues that EDDA shipping today with ITAR cleared is a 12-month head start on the next twenty entrants, and that the Anchorage exception applies because the regulatory moat *is* the leverage in defense. The bull case stings until I run my own rule on it: Anchorage required me to have been accumulating crypto conviction since 2014, and the defense category in 2026 has no equivalent accumulating thesis on my ledger -- I am not a defense investor, the founder team is not a defense team, and "permission-as-moat" only works as an override when the category-timing call is mine to make, which here it is not. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a signed multi-year program-of-record with a named US service branch above $20M annual value before EOY 2026; absent that, 9 Mothers is a pass that I would re-evaluate only at the Series A if EDDA revenue materializes.
