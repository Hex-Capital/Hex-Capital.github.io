# Aster -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Emmett Bicker has been studying machine learning since age 15, shipped post-training work on long-context coding LLMs at Magic, and is first author on arXiv 2602.07040 -- and he is doing it alone. That pairing is Aster's signature: top-quartile specific knowledge sitting on top of a single-sided agent product with no compounding-user-value mechanic. This is the same shape as the Perplexity exception in my own ledger -- builder-tool, single-sided, deep founder -- which is the only template by which Aster gets a check.

Stack Overflow in 2008 distributed because Atwood and Spolsky were developers writing for developers, and the code itself was the marketing channel; that is the high rung for the leverage check. Aster is running the 2026 version of the same play -- the arXiv preprint, the PR #217 into KellerJordan/modded-nanogpt, the NanoGPT Speedrun world record at 95.2s, the METR citation on April 21 -- this is media leverage on a technical artifact, distributed to the exact buyer (frontier-lab researchers) with zero ad spend and zero gatekeeper. Where Aster diverges from the Stack Overflow rung is that the artifact ships as benchmark numbers, not as a product anyone is yet logging into, so the pull is on Bicker's credibility rather than on a self-serve product surface; the architecture is right, but one rung of the distribution staircase -- the open product -- has not been built yet.

Perplexity is the only company in my ledger that got a check despite failing this dimension, and Aster fails it for the same reason: a single-sided model-app where user N does not make user N-1 more valuable. The dossier offers an inferred future moat -- "a proprietary corpus of agent trajectories from customer research runs could compound into a data moat" -- but customer count and run volume are undisclosed, which means today there is no network. The Perplexity waiver required three of the other gates to clear emphatically; Aster only clears two emphatically (leverage, specific knowledge), with category timing actively against it given Sakana at $2.65B and Autoscience's $14M General Catalyst seed already inside the same square. So the exception precedent applies, but more thinly than I would like.

Specific knowledge is where Aster registers loudest. Bicker did post-training research on long-context coding LLMs at Magic -- that is the exact substrate of an agent that iteratively rewrites programs against an evaluation script. Add the ORCID, the Google Scholar profile, the first-author arXiv paper, and the GitHub PR that landed in a public ML training repository, and the founder is the product in a way an ex-McKinsey market-mapper never could be. This is the gate I cannot replace with capital or with hiring -- and at angel stage it is the gate I overweight against everything else, because it is what compounds over the 18-month window where Sakana and Autoscience will outspend Aster on infrastructure but cannot outspend Aster on Bicker's depth.

Big market with pivot room: the counterfactual for a tier flip is whether Aster can credibly pivot off "autonomous ML research lab" if Autoscience eats that wedge. The dossier gives two adjacent vectors -- GPU kernel optimization as a service (the 1.114ms TriMul result alone is sellable to any ML infra team) and broader scientific computing (the Erdős Minimum Overlap SOTA is the genre-bending signal). That is pivot-room, but not yet the platform-shaped surface Twitter had when it pivoted off Odeo's podcast carcass; the market is real and large, but the adjacency map is two vectors, not five.

Solo founder is where the Buffett trio gets uncomfortable, and the Notion comparison clarifies why -- Ivan Zhao survived multiple Notion rebuilds because the integrity and energy reads were so durable that the team rebuilt around him. Aster has no co-founder dossier to evaluate; the 14-point dimension defaults to neutral on integrity (Bicker volunteers nothing, which is the read I want) but solo against a multi-domain claim -- math, GPU kernels, biology, neuroscience, ML training, all from one engineer -- is operational risk, not character risk. Picking a co-founder is the most important decision Bicker has not yet made.

Thirty-six month forecast: Aster gets acqui-hired by Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google DeepMind for $40M-$150M inside 24 months, with Bicker going in as a research lead and the agent rolling into an internal ML-experimentation pipeline. The mechanism is Bicker's specific knowledge plus the public benchmark portfolio making him the cheapest way for a frontier lab to acquire a working program-search agent before Autoscience or Sakana absorbs the category; the same mechanism rules out an independent $100M ARR outcome because the moat is the founder, not the product surface.

The opposing case is the bear: Autoscience already raised $14M from General Catalyst in March 2026 explicitly to "build the world's first automated AI research lab" -- a phrase that targets Aster's positioning more precisely than Sakana's broader paper-generation play -- and Aster is one engineer with a GitHub account created on May 7, 2026, going against that. The red flag I would normally fire on is "responding to a competitor's success rather than to a personally-discovered problem," but Bicker's arXiv paper predates the Autoscience announcement, so the origin is genuine; the harder objection is that pre-consensus timing -- the Perplexity exception's third gate -- has already closed in this category, which is why the upside math compresses from 10,000x to maybe 50-100x. The verdict flips to pass if Bicker does not add a co-founder or hire a research engineer of equal depth before demo day; the solo-founder-against-multi-domain-scope condition is the single verifiable signal that would invert this check.
