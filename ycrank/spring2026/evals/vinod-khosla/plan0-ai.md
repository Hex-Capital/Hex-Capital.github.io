# PLAN0 AI -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

PLAN0 AI is a vertical AI copilot dressed up as a Bloomberg-of-construction data play, and that mismatch is the entire story. The dimensional signature is a relatively highest mark on Path to Radical Affordability — they do compress a two-week estimator workflow to 30 minutes — paired with a near-floor reading on Improbable Ambition, because the YC page lists six already-shipping AI estimating peers and Provision has $7M in to do the exact same wedge. PLAN0 AI is the kind of company that gets built when smart finance people see a 13.11% CAGR preconstruction software market and reverse-engineer a product into it.

On Consequence Magnitude, the ladder I anchor against runs from OpenAI at the top — a $50M check I had to apologize to LPs for, because civilizational consequences override every other criterion — through DoorDash in the middle, where Tony Xu's logistics build reshaped food access for hundreds of millions, down to the YC vertical-AI-copilot pass bucket I see weekly: "AI for accountants, AI for recruiters." PLAN0 AI sits one rung above that floor. Replacing a $2.38B preconstruction software market with a faster version of itself is not a consequence explosion; it's a workflow compression for general contractors and developers. The dossier offers no path by which PLAN0 AI's success makes housing cheaper, energy use lower, or buildings safer at scale — it makes bid windows shorter. That is not what gets me out of bed.

On Founder as Learning Machine — my second-most-discriminating filter — the ladder runs from the 19-year-old MIT dropout at Mach Industries who took on Lockheed without knowing what couldn't be done, through Tony Xu's outsider-immigrant execution velocity, down to the credentialed-but-scripted founder who validates their approach by citing expert endorsement. PLAN0 AI's Arash Barati was an Economist at Drake Real Estate Partners and a Director at Explora Ventures family office; his CTO and brother Shervin Barati has no verifiable public footprint at all, per the dossier's WebSearch May 2026. That is the inverse of my preferred profile. Arash is a domain expert in real estate finance pattern-matching toward "quant finance inspired deep neural networks" — exactly the linear extrapolation I distrust. There is no evidence in this dossier of a pivot, a surprise, or a learning curve over three months at YC. PLAN0 AI sits at the mid-low rung; the absent CTO footprint pulls it down further.

On Non-Linear Technical Approach, Rocket Lab is my high rung — the Rutherford engine with electric turbopumps and 3D-printed combustion chambers was a genuine cost-curve break. PLAN0 AI's claim of forecasting trade-level costs "up to 24 months out with high accuracy" against a non-stationary labor and commodity market is bold on paper but unverified — no published methodology, no third-party benchmark in the dossier. CV-parsing of architectural plans is exactly what Togal.AI ($299/user/month, claimed 98% accuracy), Beam, and Kreo ($35/month) already ship. PLAN0 AI is layering a forecasting wrapper on a now-commoditized takeoff capability. That is incremental, not discontinuous. On Improbable Ambition, against Commonwealth Fusion Systems at the top and the YC-vertical-copilot floor, PLAN0 AI's "CoStar or Bloomberg of construction" is a serious-sounding ambition that on inspection competes with CoStar — which already owns the data feeds — for an existing budget. That is not what I mean by improbable.

On Path to Radical Affordability — Impossible Foods at the high rung, where eventual sub-beef pricing changes how billions eat — PLAN0 AI does collapse a specific labor cost. Preconstruction estimating goes from two weeks of human takeoff to 30 minutes of model inference. That is a real 50-100x labor compression, and it is the strongest thesis-fit signal in this dossier. But PLAN0 AI is compressing the cost of a B2B service consumed by maybe 50,000 GCs and developers, not a basic human necessity. It is an Oscar Health-shaped argument without the Oscar Health-scale consequence. PLAN0 AI lands mid-low here too.

My 36-month forecast: PLAN0 AI reaches $1-3M ARR with mid-market general contractors by May 2029 and is acqui-hired by Procore, Autodesk, or Trimble for $15-40M, or alternatively shuts down after Provision's $7M head start and an Autodesk/CoStar in-house product compress its window. The mechanism is straightforward — Arash Barati's developer-and-investor network from Drake REP and Explora gives PLAN0 AI enough warm intros to land early logos, but the absent technical moat, the unverified 24-month forecast claim, and the data-owning incumbents (CoStar, Autodesk, Procore, Trimble) close the gap before PLAN0 AI builds a flywheel. This is the median outcome of a competent vertical AI play in a crowded category with no improbable ambition.

The strongest case against this forecast invokes my DoorDash exception: when consequence magnitude is large enough and the founder's learning velocity is real, I have overridden the technical-novelty requirement before. Bull-case PLAN0 AI: Arash Barati is an immigrant-shaped outsider with documented quant-finance domain depth, the cost-data flywheel compounds with every estimate generated, and the "Bloomberg of construction" thesis — if PLAN0 AI becomes the default cost-truth feed — produces a $10B+ data business analogous to CoStar's $30B market cap. That case stings because the founder-market fit is genuine and the affordability compression is real. It loses on the dossier evidence: DoorDash had a hundred-million-user consumer surface; PLAN0 AI is selling to a 50,000-buyer enterprise segment where Provision is already $7M ahead and where there is no named customer, no signed LOI, no pricing page, and a CTO whose public footprint cannot be verified. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is signed annual contracts above $500K each with two or more top-10 ENR general contractors within twelve months — that, plus a published forecast-accuracy benchmark beating BLS labor-cost forecasts by 30%+, would tell me the data flywheel is real and the moat is forming. Absent that, I pass.
