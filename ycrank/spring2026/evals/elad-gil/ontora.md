# Ontora -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Ontora is a clean single-miracle LLM application sitting inside a $50K-per-engagement consulting-replacement wrapper, which is the dimensional signature that should worry me most: the miracle works, but Vertiv as the lone named logo and zero disclosed MoM tells me Ontora is selling units of consulting, not compounding an index. The product the founders shipped -- 100 parallel ~20-minute voice agents synthesizing process maps -- is real and the LLM-step-function tailwind is real. The business model behind it is not. That tension is what the rest of the evaluation has to resolve.

Harvey (2023) is the ladder rung I want Ontora to live near on revenue velocity, because Harvey is what "just working" looks like in dollars -- $3B → $5B → $8B in months (TechCrunch, Nov 2025), with named enterprise logos pulling the curve. Ontora has Julien Kang at Vertiv saying "months of interviews ... delivered in a single afternoon" and that's it -- one testimonial, no second logo, no engagement count, no ARR. In the AI cycle, silence on growth *is* the growth signal, and Ontora's Launch YC page from April 17 2026 is now ~6 weeks old with nothing harder than a customer quote to show. That's not the Harvey pattern; it's the pattern I flagged in *AI Market Clarity* as a false signal -- enterprise-pilot tourism dressed as adoption. Until Ontora discloses a second $50K engagement closed in the last 90 days, I have to assume the curve isn't there to disclose.

Lyft Series C is the failure mechanism I want to test against on index position: I passed because #2 in a network-effect market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and the dossier didn't show share-inversion. Ontora isn't quite that pattern, but it sits in a more pedestrian failure mode -- a $50K project sale against McKinsey/BCG's $500K engagement, where the buyer relationship belongs to the consultancy and Celonis already runs an AI-enabled Process Intelligence Graph across 2,000+ enterprise implementations at €1.3B ARR (Tracxn, 2026). Ontora doesn't sit in the workflow path the way Stripe does for payments; it sits in the *project* path, which means every quarter Ontora has to re-sell. Celonis bolting an interview-agent on top of PI Graph is one PM-quarter of work, not a strategic re-platform, and they ship to the same C-suite buyer Ontora's cal.com/ontora/30min funnel is trying to reach.

The catalyst question is where Ontora's pitch has to live or die under my Entry Point lens -- what specifically opened this market in the last 24 months, and why are Leon Iwanowitsch, Maximilian Arnold, and David Korn uniquely positioned for the window? The dossier's own answer is [Inferred]: voice-LLM agents crossed usability in 2024-25. That's true, but it's the same catalyst that opens 200 other Spring 2026 voice-agent pitches, and "tacit knowledge capture" is not a market peers misread as dead the way defense was pre-Anduril (2022) or payments were pre-Stripe (2010). Ontora's HSG-and-Porsche team is competent, but they're not uniquely positioned for a window -- they're competently positioned for an open lane where Celonis, KYP.ai, Skan AI, and ProcessMaker are already running. Over 18 months this dimension compounds against Ontora: the catalyst gets more obvious to incumbents faster than Ontora gets distribution.

Counterfactual for founder shipping rhythm: to flip this from a default to an Invest tier, Ontora would need David Korn or Max Arnold to have a public artifact -- a GitHub, a side project, a prior shipped product -- that demonstrates the Perplexity (2022) pattern where Aravind showed up a week later with the thing we discussed built. The dossier explicitly notes "GitHub: No public account found" for all three Ontora founders. Max led process optimization at Porsche and Leon built AI automations at consultancies -- these are credentialed roles, not legibly-shipped artifacts. That's not a disqualifier at pre-seed, but it means I can't underwrite Ontora on execution rhythm; I'd have to underwrite on market, and the market doesn't carry the weight.

Color Genomics (2015) is the single-anchor parallel for Ontora's single-miracle architecture, and it's the one place Ontora actually scores well -- Color was structured as workflow on commodity sequencing, not a sequencing-chemistry bet, and Ontora is structured as a workflow/synthesis layer on commodity voice-LLM stacks, not a model bet. The miracle Ontora needs is one validated capability: voice agents conducting structured 20-minute interviews at adequate quality. That capability mostly exists. The remaining work is GTM and works-council/GDPR navigation, which Ontora's European founder base (HSG, Porsche, CODE) at least understands. This is the dimension where Ontora is cleanest -- one miracle, not three.

My 36-month forecast for Ontora: stalled below $3M ARR with the engagement model failing to compound into recurring revenue, leading to either a talent-acquisition exit to Celonis or a consulting firm (McKinsey/BCG) in the $10-30M range, or a pivot away from per-engagement pricing toward a continuous-monitoring SaaS that puts Ontora head-to-head with Celonis on Celonis's own field. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: clean single-miracle execution without revenue velocity and without an index position means Ontora has to keep selling $50K projects one at a time, and project businesses do not compound the way Harvey (2023) or Decagon (2023) compound.

The bull case I owe Ontora: Vertiv (NYSE: VRT) is a real Fortune 1000 industrial logo, the testimonial language is operationally specific ("months of interviews ... single afternoon") rather than founder-romantic, and the $50K-vs-$500K price gap against McKinsey is exactly the kind of 10× wedge I funded Decagon (2023) and Abridge (2023) into. If Ontora's engagement actually generates a "proprietary corpus of structured employee-interview transcripts" that customers feed into their own AI stacks, that's the workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-hard-thing pattern from my Unstated Patterns -- the same shape as Color Genomics on sequencing. The bear-case dismantle is the one fact that wins: Ontora's only public customer evidence after a six-week-old YC launch is a single quoted Strategy Manager. In April 2026 AI pre-seed, a single logo with no second-logo announcement is not the Decagon pattern -- it's the pattern I called out in Tim Ferriss Show #863 as "something's fundamentally broken." The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Ontora discloses three named Fortune 1000 customers with engagement counts >1 each by Q4 2026, demonstrating engagement repeatability rather than one-shot consulting tourism.
