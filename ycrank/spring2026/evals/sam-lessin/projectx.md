# ProjectX -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Rounak Adhikary is 23, self-funded ProjectX through his prior Woldix consulting shop, and is now pitching a per-app GPU virtualization stack against AWS WorkSpaces and a DigitalOcean that just paid $111M cash for Paperspace — the dimensional signature on ProjectX is a modestly conviction-tinted founder story bolted to the single worst category-sleeve fit I underwrite, with no owned distribution to cushion the legibility translation later. That pairing tells me ProjectX is a capital-intensive infrastructure bet dressed as an "OS for agents" narrative, which is the exact 2026 YC shape my framework is built to deselect.

Solana at $0.05/coin in 2018-2019 is my HIGH rung on the non-consensus dimension because the entire crypto category had been abandoned post-ICO crash and the price reflected that abandonment — the mechanism was buying a real protocol when the consensus crowd had quit the room. ProjectX diverges from that rung on the most important variable: the category is not abandoned, it is the loudest narrative in the Spring 2026 batch ("when half the users of a computer are not people," per the Inc42 description), and while the $704K cumulative raise (Tracxn) and unnamed Seed lead suggest no tier-1 has stamped this yet, the absence of named passes also means I cannot price the non-consensus angle — silence on round dynamics defaults low because in 2026 an "agent-native OS" pitch is consensus framing until proven otherwise.

The generic SaaS YC pitch needing five rounds to reach default-alive is my LOW rung on capital structure, and ProjectX sits squarely on that rung for a more expensive reason than SaaS: "each app in its own independent computer ? own GPU" (YC page) is a GPU-COGS business that will need to subsidize per-session compute below the AWS/Azure curve for years before unit economics work, and no benchmarks, density numbers, or pricing have been published. Teamshares could absorb capital because the employee-equity rollup structure WAS the moat; ProjectX is asking me to fund GPU arbitrage against the people who own the GPUs. There is no exotic instrument available here — this is a standard SAFE into a capex-shaped business, which is the worst of both ends of the regatta.

On founder conviction, Rounak Adhikary funding ProjectX out of his own Woldix consulting revenue at age 19-22 is the only real missionary signal in this dossier — that is not nothing, that is exactly the "Type 2 fun" temperament that compounds over non-linear time. The mechanism that worries me over 18 months: the original 2022 co-founding team (Adhikary, Ronit Banerjee, Suvrakamal Das) is not the YC P26 team (Adhikary, Karmakar, Majumder), and unexplained co-founder turnover before the batch is the most reliable signal I have for the "manic at the ups" failure mode, which compounds badly when a single CEO is the only continuity thread through a multi-tack regatta against hyperscalers.

For founder-as-brand, the counterfactual that would flip ProjectX a full tier is straightforward and absent: Bishal Karmakar publishing technical writing on per-app GPU virtualization, a working GitHub with the orchestration layer open-sourced, or an X account with a meaningful systems-engineering audience. Instead the dossier returns @ProjectXcloud with 14 followers since October 2022, no founder Twitter accounts found, no GitHub repos found, no Product Hunt, no Discord. The Slow Creator Fund thesis (Axios, February 12 2025) exists precisely because owned distribution is the cap-table asset I am willing to overpay for; ProjectX has the inverse — a pure paid-acquisition future against incumbents with infinite distribution.

Category sleeve is where I am hardest, and the single-anchor parallel is unkind: Aleo and Lightning Labs are the seeds I underwrote in the crypto sleeve because "crypto is fundamentally disruptive" while "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113, 2024), and ProjectX is "more internet" with extra GPU bills — it is cloud DaaS plus an agent narrative wrapper, attacking a $10.5B DaaS TAM (Gartner 2026) that hyperscalers already serve at scale. There is no Known Exception that rescues this; Ro got the override because 50-state prescription rails were a real regulatory schlep, and per-app GPU virtualization is a technical schlep that any hyperscaler can re-architect billing for in a quarter.

My 36-month forecast on ProjectX: fails to raise a priced Series A on agent-OS framing, pivots to an agent-runtime sandbox API for one of the AI-lab computer-use efforts (Anthropic, OpenAI, Cognition), and either lands a sub-$2M ARR contract book or gets acqui-hired into Neverinstall, Kasm, or a hyperscaler workspace team — the mechanism is GPU economics colliding with the hyperscaler cost curve while the founders have no community or non-standard structure to keep them alive through the tacks.

The bull case that stings: AI agents genuinely need isolated sandboxed compute, and if ProjectX becomes the picks-and-shovels execution environment for autonomous coding agents, the per-app VM architecture is actually the right primitive — Bishal Karmakar's systems/streaming/cloud-infra background plus Rounak Adhikary's self-funding history could compound into the Browserbase-for-desktops winner. The dossier evidence that wins for the bear case anyway: no named AI-lab counterparty in the LOI column, no published cold-start benchmarks, and the Paperspace-to-DigitalOcean $111M cash exit (TechCrunch, July 2023) tells me the incumbent acquirers have already bought their seat at this table. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: a signed deployment with Anthropic, OpenAI, or a named foundation-model lab for agent runtime — at which point ProjectX is no longer "OS for agents" narrative, it is enterprise infrastructure with a counterparty I can story-broker into a Series A.
