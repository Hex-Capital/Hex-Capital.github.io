# InLoop Robotics -- Elad Gil Evaluation

InLoop Robotics is the dossier-pattern I see most often in P26 robotics: a real catalyst (foundation-model manipulation crossing a generalization threshold over the last 12-18 months) paired with the worst possible miracle structure to ride it -- bimanual general manipulation, RaaS unit economics, and head-to-head distribution against Dexterity's $291M war chest. The highest-scoring dimension for InLoop Robotics is the catalyst itself; the lowest is single-miracle architecture, and that pairing is the entire story.

**Anduril** (2022) is the right HIGH rung for the catalyst dimension because Ukraine collapsed a 24-month decision window in Pentagon procurement and Trae Stephens was uniquely positioned to harvest it. InLoop Robotics is claiming the same template -- Isaac/IsaacLab and the manipulation foundation-model step-function -- but the load-bearing question is whether that catalyst is *opening* or *already 8 years in*. Dexterity has raised $291M at $1.65B (TechCrunch Mar 2025), Pickle has $87M (Globe Newswire Nov 2024), and Covariant got acqui-hired by Amazon at $380M *below* its prior $625M mark (Bloomberg 2024). That is not Ukraine-2022; that is rideshare-2014. InLoop Robotics is showing up to a catalyst that consensus capital already discounted, which is the inverse of why I led Anduril.

**Covariant**'s $380M acqui-hire from $625M is the LOW rung's failure mechanism: generalized AI manipulation is a science-and-economics miracle that ate eight years and $222M before the founders sold the team for parts. The pattern Covariant exposed is that "one policy across many SKUs" is not a single miracle -- it stacks (a) manipulation accuracy at production reliability, (b) hardware cost curve that supports flat-monthly RaaS, (c) teleop fallback economics that don't eat the margin, and (d) GTM against incumbents who already own the buyer. InLoop Robotics's own dossier names the Safety Module teleoperator fallback as the mitigation -- which is the tell. If the policy worked, you wouldn't need a 24/7 human in the loop bundled into the monthly price. That is exactly the multi-miracle compound I pass on per NFX (*Unusual Signs*), and the Covariant exit is the in-cycle comp.

On revenue velocity (mechanism analysis): InLoop Robotics discloses "300+ picks/hour per station" and "paid pilots" without an MRR figure, a logo, or a multi-station expansion. Pick rate is a tech metric, not a revenue curve. In an AI cycle where Harvey ran $3B->$5B->$8B in months (TechCrunch Nov 2025) because the curve was obvious enough to disclose, silence on dollars and named warehouse counterparties for InLoop Robotics compounds the wrong way over 18 months -- pilots that don't convert become reference customers competitors call to learn what didn't work. "Something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss #863) is the default reading when an AI/robotics company at YC launch can quote pick rate but not signed expansion.

On index position (counterfactual): for InLoop Robotics to clear the index test, the RaaS station would need to become the *layer Dexterity and Symbotic integrate against*, not a competing endpoint. That would require a developer/API surface for third-party manipulation policies, or a teleop network other robotics companies plug into. Nothing in the dossier suggests that architecture -- InLoop Robotics is selling a station, not a rail. To flip from neutral to invest on this axis I would need the founders to reposition as the teleop-and-failure-data infrastructure that Pickle, Dexterity, and the next twenty entrants all pay to use. Today InLoop Robotics is one of N station vendors fighting for the same fulfillment buyer Amazon Robotics is taking captive.

On founder shipping rhythm (single-anchor parallel to **Perplexity** 2022): Aravind Srinivas cold-pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing built. InLoop Robotics's three founders -- Zakariea Sharfeddine (KIT 2024 grad, ex-Bosch/BMW ML), Stepan Feduniak (robot-learning from age 18), Pasha Rizali ("Computer Science Student" per LinkedIn) -- have RoboTUM and student-robotics credentials but no named prior shipped artifact, no GitHub trace, and no Twitter footprint. That is not disqualifying at pre-seed, but it does not earn the Anduril Known Exception override either -- Stephens and Luckey had Palantir and Oculus on the board before I underwrote the hardware risk. InLoop Robotics is asking me to take Covariant-grade technical risk with a Perplexity-grade-unknown shipping signal, and the math doesn't compound.

My 36-month forecast for InLoop Robotics: under $2M ARR, acqui-hired by Dexterity or a Tier-2 systems integrator for sub-$40M, or pivoted into a teleop-services layer reselling human-in-the-loop hours to better-funded peers. The mechanism is the high/low signature -- a real but crowded catalyst cannot carry a multi-miracle hardware/RaaS/AI/distribution stack against $291M-funded incumbents, and the Covariant outcome at $380M is the *ceiling*, not the floor, because InLoop Robotics is starting eight years later with $500K.

The strongest bull case against my pass: manipulation foundation models genuinely did cross a step-function in 2024-2025, the RaaS plug-in motion is structurally different from Symbotic's $22.5B-backlog DC builds, and Anduril is the Known Exception precedent for "software-and-workflow on top of a hard physical layer" with a single-miracle isolation. If InLoop Robotics's policy actually generalizes across hundreds of SKUs at 300+ picks/hour without the teleop crutch, the per-station economics could compound the way Decagon's seat-based agents did in customer support. The Covariant acqui-hire might be Web 1.0 Pets.com, not the category epitaph. The reason this bull case doesn't win: the dossier itself lists the Safety Module teleoperator as a feature -- that's InLoop Robotics admitting the miracle isn't isolated yet. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named multi-station expansion at a logo like GXO, FedEx, or a Shopify 3PL with disclosed monthly revenue per station and a teleop-intervention rate trending below 5% within the next two quarters. Until then, InLoop Robotics is a worse-funded version of the bet Amazon already settled.
