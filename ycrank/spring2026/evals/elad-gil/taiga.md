# Taiga -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Taiga is a clean **single-miracle** bet (LLM accuracy on structured medical coding) sitting on top of an **empty growth curve** — one named customer, Cheskis Family Therapy, against Candid Health at ~250% YoY and Adonis at 4x in 2025. That high/low pairing is the analytical signature: the architecture is right, the proof is absent, and the well-capitalized version of this same bet is already compounding in someone else's hands. Nanda Guntupalli and Adam Wax have the founder-market story (both physicians' kids), but founder-market fit without a velocity curve is the **Harvey** template missing its only load-bearing piece.

On revenue velocity, **Harvey** (2023) is the HIGH rung — "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025), the canonical "just working" signal where I paid up at consensus because the curve was real. The shared mechanism is "AI-native services priced on outcomes in a category with frozen incumbent margins" — and on paper Taiga sits in exactly that architecture, with the same inability-of-billing-firms-to-cannibalize-labor-margin dynamic Harvey exploited in law. Where Taiga diverges from Harvey is the part that mattered most for my **Harvey** check: there is no curve. The dossier discloses one named customer, no revenue, no pricing page, and a self-reported 98% touchless rate with no denominator. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) — and at YC Spring 2026 stage Adonis already booked 4x in 2025 and Candid added 200+ orgs (TechCrunch, Feb 2025), so the velocity bar in this category is no longer "any traction"; it's "traction relative to a market that is already compounding without you."

**Lyft** Series C failed my screen because the company was a #2 in a network-effect market where #1's economics did not flow to #2 (20VC interview). The failure mechanism was structural: distribution leadership in a winner-leaning category is path-dependent and downstream entrants pay the tax forever. Tracing that pattern into Taiga's dossier: RCM is not network-effect, so this isn't a literal Lyft analog — but the distribution-position failure mode shows up differently. Candid Health, Adonis, and Akasa have raised $99.5M, $95M+, and $205M respectively and already occupy the "AI-native RCM" shelf in buyers' minds; Taiga's wedge is "full-stack service for independent practices" with two-week onboarding, which is a real segmentation but not an index position — it's a sub-segment Candid will downmarket into once multi-site saturates. Taiga doesn't sit in the workflow path the way **Stripe** (2010) sat in payments rails; it sits next to Candid in a buying conversation independent practices have once.

(*Mechanism analysis — Market Re-Opening*): The catalyst Taiga rides — GenAI accuracy on structured medical codes crossing the threshold for touchless submission — is real but it is the *consensus* AI-in-RCM catalyst that funded Candid's GenAI Series C messaging in Feb 2025 (HIT Consultant) and Adonis's $40M Series C in March 2026 (PRNewswire). Over 18 months this catalyst compounds *against* Taiga, not for it: every quarter of LLM-coding-accuracy improvement lifts incumbents' moat-via-customer-data faster than it lifts Taiga's onboarding pitch, because Candid's 200+ orgs generate denial-feedback data Taiga structurally cannot match yet. This is the inverse of the **Anduril** (2022) catalyst — Ukraine opened the window *and* incumbents (Lockheed, Raytheon) could not pivot software-first, so the catalyst protected the new entrant. Here the catalyst is shared and the incumbents are AI-native already.

(*Counterfactual — Founder Ships*): For Taiga's founder-shipping score to flip a tier, I would need to see named prior artifacts from Nanda Guntupalli or Adam Wax — a Penn class project that became something, a GitHub with deployed code, a side product with users. The dossier shows neither: no GitHub for either founder, no Twitter, and Guntupalli is Class of 2028 from Penn Engineering's Student Activities Council. The Perplexity (2022) test — "every time we talk, he'd show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863) — has no input data here to run. Friends-since-3rd-grade and physician-parent context gives founder-market fit but not founder-shipping rhythm; those are different signals and I weight the second more at pre-seed because it's the only legible one.

(*Single-anchor parallel — Single Miracle*): Versus **Decagon** (2023), the architecture maps cleanly: assume LLM accuracy at the structured task (coding, denial-appeal generation), the remaining work is integrations and GTM. Taiga has already named 10 EHR integrations and 50+ payers, which means the integration miracle is treated as execution, not research. This is where Taiga's dossier is genuinely strong — there's no chemistry, no regulatory approval gate, no consumer behavior change required. The miracle is one: LLM coding accuracy at parity with human billers, sustained under payer-specific drift. That maps to my Color Genomics (2015) discipline of building workflow on top of commodity capability rather than betting on the capability itself.

Taiga's 36-month outcome: sub-$3M ARR by mid-2029, acqui-hired by Tebra or athenahealth (named EHRs in the dossier that already offer billing modules and have every reason to absorb an AI-native team rather than let one compete) for a single-digit million-dollar outcome, OR pivoted into mental-health-vertical RCM where the Cheskis Family Therapy logo is the actual wedge. The mechanism producing this forecast is the high/low signature: clean single-miracle architecture means Taiga *will* build a working product, but absent velocity against Candid's 200+ orgs and Adonis's 4x curve, the path to durable independent company narrows to vertical-niche or absorption.

The strongest bull case stings: independent-practice RCM is structurally underserved because Candid Health is explicitly multi-site SaaS and Akasa serves 650+ hospitals — neither sales motion fits a solo dermatologist, and the "incumbent billing firms can't cannibalize labor margins" pattern is exactly the kind of frozen-incumbent dynamic I funded in **Decagon** (2023) and **Abridge** (2023). Both founders grew up inside the customer's pain (physician parents losing revenue to billing) — that's the **Brex** (2017) operational-discipline-from-lived-context archetype I have funded repeatedly. The bear dismantle: the independent-practice segment is a small slice of a $60B TAM where CAC is brutal (direct outbound to individual doctors), ASPs sit at percentage-of-collections on small practices, and Adonis at $40M Series C has every incentive to downmarket once it saturates mid-market. Taiga's wedge is real but it's a niche, not an index — and undergraduates with no shipped artifacts attacking a category where Candid and Adonis are already compounding is the wrong side of "consensus pays." The verifiable condition that flips me to Invest: 20+ independent-practice logos signed within 6 months at disclosed percentage-of-collections economics, or a named integration partnership with athenahealth or Tebra that turns EHR-dependency-risk into a distribution channel.
