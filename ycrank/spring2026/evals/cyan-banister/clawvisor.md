# Clawvisor -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Eric Levine is the cleanest single-founder profile I have read in this batch -- one named CEO, zero tiebreaker ambiguity, a prior $70M exit at Berbix, and Trust & Safety engineering at Airbnb from 2012 to 2017 -- and Clawvisor is also, exactly, the consensus YC AI-agent darling I wrote my Pass Example about. That is the signature: governance and founder hygiene at the ceiling, "magically weird" at the floor. Clawvisor is a competently-built, Garry-Tan-partnered, three-funded-competitor "authorization layer for AI agents" pitch in May 2026, and I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing -- the game here looks like agent infrastructure, but the game I'm actually being asked to play is "back the consensus pick at consensus pricing."

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is the high rung on lived experience -- shipped Oculus to scale, then pivoted into defense because he was *of* the hardware-and-policy world he was entering. Clawvisor's Levine carries a real version of that mechanism: he ran identity verification at Berbix through to a Socure acquisition in June 2023, and Airbnb T&S is where you learn what credential misuse actually costs at scale. The divergence from the Luckey rung is one of magnitude, not kind -- Berbix-to-Clawvisor is a continuation inside identity, not a transgressive jump into a category other people thought was uninvestable. I read Levine as authentically problem-adjacent, not memo-adjacent; this is the strongest single fact in the Clawvisor dossier and the reason I cannot file this as a clean pass.

The Pass Example I wrote -- two ex-FAANG PMs, $20M oversubscribed seed, six funds chasing, $200B TAM slide -- failed on magical-weirdness because the round itself signaled consensus. Clawvisor is in that failure mode by category, not by founder. Arcade.dev closed $12M in March 2025, Composio raised $29M with $1M+ ARR and 200+ paying customers by July 2025, and Permit.io has $14M from Scale Venture Partners. Three credible incumbents are already shipping overlapping functionality, and Clawvisor's stated differentiator -- "purpose-based verification" -- is a feature any of them can table-stakes into their roadmap inside two quarters. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus; Clawvisor *is* the consensus.

Solo-founder, single-CEO clarity at Clawvisor is the structural inverse of the HQ Trivia (2017) governance failure I have cited -- there is no Yusupov-and-Kroll feud waiting in the cap table because there is no co-founder. Over 18 months this compounds positively: every product call, every pricing decision, every pivot Eric Levine wants to make happens at the speed of one person's conviction. The risk shape inverts too -- Clawvisor's failure mode is solo-founder bandwidth against a 25+ headcount Composio, not boardroom paralysis.

Capital-intensity and unsexy-regulated swing is where Clawvisor would have to look genuinely different for the score to flip a tier -- the counterfactual is "what if Clawvisor were the SOC2/HIPAA agent-audit gateway selling into regulated health and financial services with signed enterprise LOIs?" Then the moat compounds in the lane that produced Vigil Labs (August 2025) and the regulatory artifacts become the switching cost. The Clawvisor dossier instead lists Gmail, Slack, Notion, Linear, and Stripe adapters -- pure horizontal SaaS, the lane where Composio's $1M+ ARR is already eating.

Physical-world verifiability for Clawvisor sits where Affirm sat for me -- you can install the OSS repo (202 stars, 27 forks on github.com/clawvisor as of May 2026), route a Gmail call through it, and observe the policy engine working. That is more tactile than a pure dev-tools abstraction, less tactile than a Flock Safety camera bolted to a pole. The 202-star count is real but modest; it tells me developers have noticed Clawvisor, not that they are deploying it.

My 36-month forecast on Clawvisor: tuck-in acquisition by Okta, Auth0/Okta, or one of Arcade/Composio in the $30-120M range -- a Berbix-shaped outcome, not a Long Journey-shaped outcome -- with low probability of independent venture-scale escape because the category will consolidate around the player with the deepest integration library and the most capital, and Composio already has both. The mechanism producing this forecast is exactly the high/low signature -- a strong solo operator with prior acquirer-relationships, executing competently inside a category that does not reward "magically weird" because every buyer in the category is rational and well-advised.

The strongest bear-case challenge to my forecast is the DeepMind exception: pedigreed founders compounding across adjacent domains can override the bias against consensus profiles. Levine at Clawvisor is a thinner version of that -- identity-to-authorization is one hop, not a polymath leap -- and the Berbix→Socure pattern is precisely the pattern that suggests Levine optimizes for acquisition rather than the durable independent outcome Long Journey IV's $181.8M needs. I would flip to Invest on one verifiable condition: a named Fortune 500 or regulated-industry design partner (bank, hospital network, defense contractor) that explicitly chose Clawvisor over Composio and Arcade for the purpose-based enforcement primitive, before the seed closes -- that would convert Clawvisor from "consensus AI infrastructure" into "regulated authorization wedge a polymath couldn't rebuild in a quarter."
