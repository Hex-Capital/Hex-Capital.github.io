# RealPact -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Ranvir Deshmukh and Erik Peterson are Dartmouth undergrads who shipped CourseMe to 90% of their campus and then went looking for a harder problem to attack — RealPact is what schlep-hunting looks like when it's done from the outside-in rather than from inside one's own pain. The signature here is high on the schlep axis, low on organic-idea fit: county-registry parsing across non-standardized jurisdictions is real work nobody wants to do, but Deshmukh and Peterson never sat at a brokerage closing table cursing the re-keying of a deed. They picked the schlep; the schlep didn't pick them.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the load-bearing comparison for the dimension where RealPact actually scores well. Armstrong's edge was an appetite for bitcoin compliance — the grind nobody else in his batch would touch — and RealPact's edge, to the extent it has one, is the same shape: each new state forces re-mapping county deed/tax/permit data and state-specific contract forms, which is exactly the kind of work Dotloop won't unbundle from its e-signature ARPU and SkySlope won't rebuild from scratch to serve 400,000+ professionals already on compliance-first workflows. Where RealPact diverges from the Coinbase rung is that Armstrong walked into the grind because he had worked fraud/risk at Airbnb and personally believed in bitcoin; Deshmukh's GitHub has 22 followers and his disclosed background is a Dartmouth course-recommender. The schlep is genuine; the founder's hunger for *this specific* schlep is unproven.

**Dropbox** (2007) is the rung RealPact most conspicuously fails to reach on the organic-idea axis — Houston forgot his USB stick at MIT and built the thing for himself before anyone asked. RealPact's founders did not forget a deed at a closing. They are Dartmouth CS students who, per the dossier, have "no disclosed real estate or brokerage operating background," and chose real-estate paperwork the way an MBA team chooses a TAM slide. That's the failure mechanism I've watched eat companies: when the founder hasn't lived the problem, the third unpleasant surprise — a county clerk who won't return calls, a state-form revision that breaks the parser, a brokerage that wants the audit trail formatted differently for E&O insurance — is the one they flinch from, because the problem was never theirs to begin with.

On animal resourcefulness, the dossier actually has tracks: CourseMe shipped, got acquired, hit 4,000+ MAU; the $20K Magnuson Competition win in May 2026; and Four Seasons Sotheby's International Realty plus three other NH brokerages signed before the YC check cleared. That last fact is the most interesting one on the page — closing a Sotheby's affiliate as undergrads is not nothing. It's not cereal boxes, but it's evidence the two of them can walk into a room of 50-year-old brokers in Hanover and leave with a signed contract, which compounds over 18 months because brokerage sales is a referral business and Four Seasons Sotheby's introductions to other Sotheby's affiliates are how this becomes a multi-state company rather than a New Hampshire curiosity.

The hacker-genealogy read on RealPact is shallower than it looks at first glance. CourseMe is a shipped artifact and an acquisition, which is more than most YC applicants have, but Deshmukh's 22 GitHub followers and absence of any notable open-source contribution put RealPact closer to "competent CS undergrads who ship" than to the Collison-brothers-at-17-with-Lisp-prizes lineage I over-index on. The dossier shows no competitive-programming wins, no recognized hacker-community presence, no Hacker News footprint predating the company. Voice-AI agents reading scanned tax assessments is also not a hard CS problem in 2026 — it's an LLM + OCR + integration stack any competent team can build, which is why Dotloop, sitting inside Zillow Group, is the substitution risk the dossier flags correctly.

On co-founder configuration, Deshmukh and Peterson built and shipped CourseMe together at Dartmouth across multiple years — that's the **Reddit** (2005) Huffman/Ohanian dorm-roommate pattern, not the hackathon-pair-from-last-month pattern I flag as a red signal. The third name, Veer B Patel, is tagged "RealPact (YC S26)" on LinkedIn with role unconfirmed in the dossier, which is the kind of ambiguity the YC interview resolves cleanly. Trio with a strong inner pair and an unclear third is fine — it's not Airbnb's three-roommate density, but it's not solo and it's not strangers.

My 36-month forecast for RealPact: reaches roughly $1-3M ARR concentrated in New England brokerages, gets squeezed when Dotloop ships native AI auto-fill on top of its ~50% transaction-management share, and exits sub-$30M to a Lone Wolf or SkySlope rather than becoming the OS for brokerages. The mechanism producing that ceiling is the organic-idea gap: Deshmukh and Peterson can grind the county-data integrations, but the second-order schleps — E&O insurance language, state-by-state contract form revisions, broker workflow politics — get discovered rather than anticipated, and Dotloop's distribution closes the gap before RealPact's data pipelines compound into switching cost.

The bull case that stings: Armstrong wasn't a banker either, and **Coinbase** (2012) was funded over my initial skepticism precisely because the schlep appetite mattered more than the resume. If RealPact's Four Seasons Sotheby's signature converts into 30+ brokerages across three states within nine months, the "they didn't live the problem" critique collapses — at that point they will have lived it, harder than any incumbent PM at Dotloop has. The honest answer is that I cannot dismiss RealPact the way I dismissed the MBA AI-wrapper team in my pass example, because the artifact exists, the customers exist, and the schlep is real. But the dossier gives me a competent shipping pair attacking a vertical they chose rather than suffered, and "Black Swan Farming" (September 2012) does not reward picking schleps from the outside — it rewards founders who couldn't help themselves. The verifiable flip condition: $50K+ MRR across three states by month nine, with at least one customer outside the Dartmouth/NH referral graph. Until then, RealPact is a neutral.
