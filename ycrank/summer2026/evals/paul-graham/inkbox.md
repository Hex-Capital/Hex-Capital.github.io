# Inkbox -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Inkbox is a schlep-heavy infrastructure bet with no animal moment on the record -- Ray Liao, Dima Vremenko, and Alex Wilcox are trying to become the identity layer for agents at the exact seam where 10DLC compliance, iMessage bridges, and PSTN provisioning meet, and that seam is real work, but the dossier gives me nothing like the cereal-box story that made me write the **Airbnb** (2009) check. That high-schlep, low-animal pairing is the whole read on Inkbox: it is a company whose *problem* would earn a check and whose *founders*, on this dossier, have not yet.

The dimension where Inkbox rhymes hardest with the portfolio is schlep. **Coinbase** (2012) is the load-bearing analogy: Brian Armstrong volunteered for a regulatory grind (bitcoin compliance, KYC, banking rails) that every other applicant in his batch was routing around, and the grind itself became the moat. Inkbox is doing the equivalent thing on the communications side -- provisioning PSTN numbers to autonomous agents at $0.03/SMS with $20/mo 10DLC campaigns, running dedicated iMessage infrastructure at a moment when Apple killed Beeper Mini in 2023-2024, and eating TCPA exposure specific to agent-originated messaging. Twilio can bundle SendGrid, SMS, and WhatsApp under one API, as its 2026 "infrastructure layer for every conversation in the agentic era" press release makes clear, but Twilio has spent fifteen years *not* productizing iMessage or agent-native 10DLC because those grinds don't fit its enterprise sales motion. Where Inkbox diverges from Coinbase is that Armstrong personally walked into the compliance grind with a shipped Android wallet in hand; Inkbox's dossier shows a $10/mo Hobbyist tier and 18 GitHub stars, not a founder who personally rebuilt an iMessage bridge to prove they'd survive Apple's next enforcement wave.

The MBA-team AI-wrapper I described passing on in my Pass Example fails on animal resourcefulness the same way Inkbox does here: no shipped stunt, no unscalable manual operation, no revenue improvisation named in the record. Inkbox's dossier gives me Liao's MIT CS PhD (2021, medical imaging under Polina Golland), Vremenko's Founders Inc. network, and Wilcox as an unverified identity -- credentials, not tracks in the snow. The one flicker is Vremenko's referenced "PG rejection story," which is the shape of an Armstrong-style comeback if it exists, but the dossier doesn't tell me what he built to answer the rejection. 533 commits and a third-party VectorlyApp SDK is the wrong scale of evidence: Drew Houston at **Dropbox** (2007) posted a demo video to Hacker News that flipped my head; Inkbox has an MIT-licensed repo with 2 forks. Absence of tracks is itself the signal.

On organic-idea fit, the mechanism to watch over 18 months is whether Inkbox's founders are *themselves* the customer. Coding agents need email, phones, and iMessage the way Houston needed his USB stick, and Liao/Vremenko/Wilcox are ostensibly agent-builders who would hit this pain first -- but the dossier does not name a prior project where they wired an agent to a Twilio number and cursed the seams. Liao's biography is medical imaging; Tandem was product/ops, not CPaaS. If the founders lived inside this pain, the dossier should read like the Collisons at **Auctomatic** (2007) hacking commerce for two years before **Stripe** (2010) -- and it does not.

For hacker genealogy, the counterfactual is what would flip Inkbox up a tier: a named open-source artifact predating the company, a competitive-programming result, a Show HN with real traffic. Patrick Collison won Ireland's Young Scientist for Lisp in 2005 before I ever met him; that kind of pre-startup marker is what I over-index on. Inkbox's GitHub org has 533 commits on the main repo but Liao's public code is @RayRuizhiLiao's medical imaging work, Vremenko's public code is the VectorlyApp org's Epstein-files scraper post, and Wilcox is publicly unverifiable. That is technical-by-credential, not hacker-by-lineage.

On co-founder configuration, Inkbox parallels **Airbnb** (2009) in shape -- three founders -- but not in chemistry. Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk had years of RISD and shared apartment history before they sold Obama O's; Inkbox's three list Boston area residence and Founders Inc. adjacency, and one of the three (Wilcox) I cannot confirm exists from public sources. A trio whose bonds aren't legible on the dossier isn't a red flag at the pre-seed stage, but it isn't a bonus either.

The 36-month forecast that follows from a high-schlep, low-animal Inkbox is acquihire by Twilio or Sinch at sub-$40M within 24-30 months, with ARR in the $1-3M range at the time of sale -- the schlep is real enough to build a small book of developer customers on Claude Code and Cursor plugins, but not deep enough to survive Twilio shipping an agent-identity SKU on top of its existing SendGrid + SMS + voice stack. Inkbox's Enterprise-tier iMessage line is the pivot-or-die axis: if Apple tightens the bridge, Inkbox loses its most differentiated channel and becomes a Twilio wrapper priced at $25/mo.

The bull case that stings: Alexandr Wang publicly named "Schlep Blindness" as the seminal essay for **Scale AI** because he ran *toward* data labeling when every other founder ran away, and Inkbox is doing the same thing at agent-native 10DLC and iMessage -- the exact grinds Twilio's enterprise motion is structurally unable to eat. If Vremenko's PG rejection story turns out to be a real Armstrong-shaped comeback with a shipped artifact behind it, and if the three founders are the population most acutely feeling this pain because they build with Claude Code themselves, then Inkbox is Coinbase-in-2012 shape and I should be leaning in on ceiling. The dossier evidence that wins against this: Coinbase-in-2012 shipped a wallet Armstrong personally coded to overturn a *written* rejection, and Inkbox's dossier gives me 18 GitHub stars, no revenue disclosed, no launch, no named design partner, and Liao's academic lineage in medical imaging rather than communications. Verdict: pass at this dossier. The single verifiable condition that flips me: a shipped, cited artifact -- Show HN post with meaningful traffic, a named Fortune 500 design partner on 10DLC-registered agent SMS, or a documented Vremenko comeback demo -- surfacing before the S26 Demo Day, in which case I write the check on the schlep alone.
