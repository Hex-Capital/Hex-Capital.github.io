# Scope -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Anand-Arnaud Pajaniradjane is a solo founder who spent his last job as an ML engineer in GEO/AEO and is now building the instrumentation he wished he had had — that is the dimensional signature of Scope: high on organic-pain origin, low on the cofounder configuration I've spent two decades telling people not to skip. The dossier shows team size 1 on the YC page and a founder whose prior employer category (GEO/AEO) is the exact category his product points a flashlight at. It is the right kind of founder solving the wrong kind of way: alone.

Drew Houston's lost USB stick at MIT is the canonical organic-idea story I keep retelling, because the founder didn't have to imagine the user — he was the user, and he shipped the demo video before he raised. Scope sits a step below that rung but on the same ladder: Anand was inside an AEO/GEO ML team watching agents pick or skip products, and the dossier says he then went and built the tool to measure exactly that. The divergence from the Dropbox rung is that Scope's first artifact is a cal.com/anand-scope/demo link — gated, sales-led, no Hacker News post, no public repo, no shipped binary I can poke at. Houston put the demo on the internet; Anand put a calendar booker on the internet. The pain is organic; the artifact is hidden.

The MBA-team AI wrapper I described in my own pass example fails because the founders never personally suffered the problem and never shipped anything before pitching. Trace that pattern through Scope and only half of it sticks. Anand did suffer the problem — Princeton interpretability research plus GEO/AEO ML engineering is not a TAM slide, it is a biography. But the pre-funding artifact column is conspicuously thin: no GitHub handle found, a HuggingFace profile at anand94ap with no notable models cited, no Product Hunt launch, no Twitter follower count retrievable, no customer logos, no LOIs. Animals leave tracks. I see one set of tracks (the resume) and not the other (the stunt). That is the half-pass that lands Scope in the default-penalty zone of my animal-resourcefulness criterion rather than the Armstrong-built-an-Android-wallet zone.

On schlep: multi-surface instrumentation across Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, plus customer APIs, CLIs, and MCP servers is integration grunt work the way Coinbase compliance was compliance grunt work — unpleasant enough that a funded incumbent like Profound, with $155M and a Series C announced February 2026, would rather extend "Agent runs" on top of its existing brand-mention pipeline than rebuild the protocol-level plumbing Scope is choosing. Over 18 months that schlep compounds if Anand does it himself and erodes if he tries to hire it. Scope is in a real schlep zone; I just don't yet see who is shoveling.

The hacker-genealogy column would flip a tier if the dossier surfaced one shipped open-source project, one MCP server Anand wrote, or one interpretability paper with his name on it. Princeton interpretability work is the right shape of credential, but credentials are not artifacts. The HuggingFace handle exists and the GitHub does not, which inverts the trail I expect from a Collison or a Swartz. Without that artifact, the founder reads as a strong applied ML engineer rather than as a hacker in the Reddit-batch sense, and the dimension scores accordingly.

Scope is solo. That is the dimension I am most consistent about across twenty years of essays, and the one that the YC interview is most likely to surface as a problem rather than fix. The single anchor here is Stripe — two brothers who had already shipped Auctomatic together, the longest-relationship pre-startup team in my portfolio anchors. Scope has the inverse: one person, no named cofounder pipeline in the dossier, not hiring per the company data. Anand's domain fit cannot substitute for a second brain on a product that has to integrate against three frontier-lab agent surfaces and N customer-side protocols simultaneously.

My 36-month forecast: Scope reaches roughly $300K–$1.5M ARR with a handful of API/infra design partners, fails to outrun Profound's extension into agent-run analytics, and exits via an acquihire into Profound, LangSmith, or a developer-observability buyer for under $25M — the Kiko outcome, not the Twitch outcome. The mechanism is the signature: a domain-correct solo founder, doing integration-heavy schlep work alone, while a $1B-valuation incumbent ships the adjacent feature on a bigger distribution engine.

The strongest case against that forecast is the Coinbase exception. Brian Armstrong was effectively a single operator when he hand-built the Android wallet to overturn his rejection, and Alexandr Wang publicly cited my "Schlep Blindness" essay as the trigger for Scale AI — both arrived close to solo, both attacked schleps incumbents avoided, and both compounded. If Anand is that founder, the cereal-box equivalent is sitting somewhere I cannot see through the demo gate, and Profound's brand-mention pipeline is the wrong shape to absorb protocol-level agent instrumentation. The dismantling is that the dossier gives me no pre-funding stunt to point at, no public artifact, and no second founder, where Armstrong gave me a working wallet and Wang gave me a labeling operation already at revenue. The verifiable condition that would flip Scope from Neutral to Invest: a named technical cofounder plus three publicly-disclosed paying API/infra/MCP customers within 90 days of Demo Day. Until then, this is a pass on solo, not on idea.
