# Loops AI -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Hakan Baş co-founded Peak Games, which Zynga acquired for $1.8 billion in 2020. That's a real exit — one of the biggest out of Turkey's tech ecosystem. So you've got a genuinely accomplished serial founder attached to Loops AI, and my instinct is always to pay attention when someone with that track record shows up. But here's the problem: Baş is a co-founder, not the CEO, and his $1.8B exit was in mobile gaming, not commerce AI. The CEO is Ari Nazir, whose background is finance and crypto — White House NEC, HSBC, a tokenized fund-of-funds. That's an interesting resume, but it's not the "I lived inside this problem for a decade" signal I look for. And the co-founders met in Bali, which is not exactly the "watched you through three companies" relationship pattern that gives me conviction. Baş is the star credential, but I can't tell from the dossier how operationally involved he is versus lending his name and network. That ambiguity matters.

The mispricing test here is brutal, and it's the whole story. AI commerce agents are the single most consensus application category in AI right now. Every demo day I've attended in the last eighteen months has three of these. OpenAI launched native agentic checkout in ChatGPT. Google launched agentic commerce in Search and Gemini. Gorgias has $80M+ ARR and is shipping AI Agent 2.0. Phia just raised $35M at a $185M valuation. Rep AI has 500+ merchants. When I say "AI seed investing has been a washout," this is what I mean — not that the technology doesn't work, but that the value accrues to the platform, not the wrapper. Loops AI is building at the application layer in a category where every major incumbent is already moving. The market hasn't dismissed this category — the market is throwing money at it. There's no asymmetry. The upside is already in the price, and then some.

The primitive test is equally clear. Loops AI combines semantic search, virtual try-on, AI sales agents, and product enrichment into one platform. That's a bundle, not a primitive. None of these capabilities are new behaviors — chatbots for e-commerce have existed for years, visual try-on has existed for years, semantic product search has existed for years. Combining them is execution, not category creation. Nobody is going to say "the Loops AI of X" because the behaviors already have names. Compare this to Venmo, which created a verb, or Patreon, which created a behavior that literally didn't exist. Loops AI is competing in existing markets on execution quality and geographic positioning, which is a fine business strategy but not the kind of bet I make.

The $500K ARR across 50+ brands is real traction if verified — that's roughly $10K ACV, which makes sense for mid-market e-commerce. The 2x growth in 45 days and zero churn claims are self-reported with no third-party verification, and zero churn across 50+ enterprise customers would be extraordinary. The geographic concentration in Turkey, MENA, and Europe is actually the most interesting angle: maybe Loops AI has found a market where Gorgias and Rep AI aren't competing as hard, and the local network effects from Baş's Turkish ecosystem created a real distribution advantage. If I squint at the bull case, it's this — build dominance in an underserved geography, compound the data advantage from 50+ brand integrations, and expand into the U.S. later with a more proven product. But that bull case requires surviving the platform kill. When OpenAI and Google are building agentic commerce at the infrastructure level, a twelve-person startup in the application layer doesn't have structural defenses. The dossier itself flags the core capabilities as "technically reproducible."

On capital discipline, I'll give partial credit: $400K pre-seed, twelve people, $500K ARR, zero claimed ad spend. If those numbers hold, the revenue-to-burn ratio isn't terrible. That's more discipline than most AI startups show. But twelve people on a $400K raise suggests either they raised additional undisclosed capital or they're burning fast — and the trajectory of the business requires raising significantly more to compete with Gorgias ($114M raised) and Rep AI ($11.2M) in the U.S. market. The plan implicitly depends on future rounds.

The strongest bull case would require three things to simultaneously be true: (1) Baş is deeply operational and brings the same intensity that built a $1.8B gaming company, (2) the Turkish/MENA geography is a genuine beachhead that creates compounding data advantages Gorgias can't replicate, and (3) the multi-modal product integration creates switching costs deep enough to survive platform-level competition from OpenAI and Google. Even if all three are true, I'm left with a well-executed application-layer AI company in the most consensus category in tech. That's not my bet. I invest in protocols, not apps. I invest where the market is wrong, not where the market is euphoric. Loops AI might be a perfectly good business — but the risk-reward asymmetry runs the wrong direction for how I deploy capital. Pass.
