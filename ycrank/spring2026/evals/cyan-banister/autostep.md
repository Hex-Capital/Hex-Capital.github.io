# Autostep -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Aidan Pratt is the only person on the cap table, the only person on the YC page, and the only person who can answer the phone when a Skan or a UiPath enterprise buyer asks about SOC 2 audit reports -- and Autostep is a desktop-monitoring app that has to be installed across an organization. The signature here is a credentialed solo operator chasing the most consensus lane of Spring 2026 (AI agents that find work for AI agents to do), with one structural strength: there is exactly one CEO, because there is exactly one human. I am never playing the game I seem to be playing, so let me look at who Aidan is before the deck.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is the high rung on founder-lived-experience -- he had already shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever pitched a defense-tech anything, and the problem was *in* his hands by the time the company existed. Autostep's Aidan Pratt has a Georgia Tech ML/CS degree and a stacked fellowship resume -- Kleiner Perkins, 8VC, Neo, Keel, Opto Investments -- plus "6 years working on automation-based products at seed-Series A startups." That is analytical adjacency, not autobiographical contact. He noticed the "what to automate" gap from the inside of other people's startups, which is closer to the consensus-YC AI-agent-darling pattern from my pass example than to Palmer in his parents' garage. Pratt has not run an operations team, has not lived inside a back office, has not felt the keystroke tax himself -- he is the fellowship-trajectory founder I screen against, not the outsider who limped into the problem.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling from my own pass example is the low rung on magically-weird, and Autostep is sitting on that rung. "Uncover repetitive tasks and automate them" is the median Spring 2026 pitch -- task mining plus agent generation is the lane every YC Operations-tagged company is crowding into, and the dossier names four well-capitalized incumbents (UiPath FY2025 revenue >$1.5B, Skan $54M raised, Mimica $32.2M, Celonis ~$13B last valued) who already do the discovery half and can extend into the generation half on a quarterly product roadmap. Crusoe was laughed at; Autostep is being recommended. When a category has both a $13B incumbent and a 34.4% CAGR projection from Fortune Business Insights, the round is priced as if the outcome is certain, which is the opposite of what I want.

Single-CEO authority is the one dimension where Autostep clears the high bar by accident -- Team Size: 1, no co-founder feud possible, no Yusupov/Kroll HQ Trivia 2017 governance bomb to defuse. But the same fact that resolves the governance question creates the enterprise execution question: a solo founder selling a desktop-monitored, org-wide install with SOC 2 + HIPAA review cycles, IT procurement gates, and works-council privacy resistance is the exact load profile that has historically required a GTM co-founder. The dimension scores high; the company is not safer for it.

SpaceX (2008) was the first angel check I ever wrote, and the reason was that rockets are uninvestable -- capital-intensive, technically risky, aesthetically transgressive to the 2008 VC consensus. Mechanism check on Autostep: SOC 2 and HIPAA compliance is table stakes for any desktop monitoring product, not a regulated moat in the Mindbloom or Vigil Labs sense. There is no hardware, no energy arbitrage, no fertility or defense clearance. Over 18 months, "compounding context" only compounds if Autostep wins enterprise logos faster than UiPath bolts a generation layer onto its existing task-mining product -- and the unit of competition is distribution, which UiPath has and Autostep does not.

Counterfactual on physical-world verifiability: Flock Safety's cameras I could photograph at a Four Seasons; Autostep's product is invisible monitoring software gated behind a Calendly demo and a no-pricing-page sales motion. To flip this dimension a tier, Autostep would need a public self-serve installer, a visible Chrome-or-desktop artifact a normal ops manager could deploy on their own machine in 48 hours, and a Product Hunt or community footprint better than a Discord with undisclosed member count and 1,384 Twitter followers on @aidan__pratt. None of that exists today.

Forecast, 36 months: Autostep gets acquihired by UiPath, Celonis, or ServiceNow for sub-$25M, or quietly sunsets after ~$2-3M burn when enterprise procurement cycles exceed solo-founder bandwidth -- the same way UltimateSuite ended up inside ServiceNow in 2024. The mechanism is incumbent absorption: the discovery-plus-generation loop is too obvious a roadmap item for UiPath's $1.5B-revenue platform team to leave alone, and Aidan Pratt's fellowship pedigree is exactly the profile that acquirers buy as a talent line item.

The strongest bull case rides DeepMind as the polymath exception -- Hassabis arrived with credentials, not lived outsider trajectory, and the override was cross-domain compounding (chess prodigy + neuroscientist + game designer). The argument would be that Pratt's six-startup operating tour plus the Kleiner/8VC/Neo/Keel/Opto rotation is its own form of polymath compounding for the automation domain, and the marketing claim of "$100K+ uncovered with 10 people" implies real customer contact. The exception does not apply: Hassabis stacked three orthogonal disciplines; Pratt stacked five venture fellowships, which is one discipline (institutional pattern matching) repeated five times. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict: three named Fortune 500 LOIs published in the next six months, *and* a credible enterprise-GTM co-founder added with majority operating authority delegated by Pratt. Absent both, Autostep is a pass -- the founder did not live the problem, the category is consensus, and the moat lives in someone else's distribution.
