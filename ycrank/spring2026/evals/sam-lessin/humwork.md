# Humwork -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Tendem — backed by Bezos Expeditions with $72M and 10,000+ experts already running MCP-based human escalation for AI agents — exists. That's the whole ballgame for Humwork's mispricing thesis. When I look at a deal, I'm not asking "is this a good company?" I'm asking "is this company undervalued because the market is wrong?" The market is emphatically not wrong about AI agents needing human fallback. Everybody and their mother is investing in this exact thesis right now. The Spring 2026 YC batch was "dominated by autonomous agent pitches" according to Analytics Drift. Invisible Technologies pulled $134M in revenue in 2024 doing adjacent work. Surge AI is seeking a $15B valuation. The human-in-the-loop AI market is projected at $16.4B by 2030 at 24.9% CAGR. There is no discomfort here, no category that smart investors have dismissed, no narrative gap between market pricing and reality. Humwork is a consensus bet priced like a consensus bet — the upside is already in the price.

Humwork's pitch — "connect AI agents to human experts in 30 seconds" — is a speed improvement on existing behavior, not a new primitive. Nobody is going to "Humwork me." Posting on Stack Overflow, Slacking a colleague, hiring a freelancer on Upwork — getting expert help when you're stuck is the oldest behavior in professional work. Humwork wraps it in an MCP server and automates the routing, which is clever engineering, but it's the kind of cleverness that makes something faster, not the kind that creates a category that didn't exist before. When I backed Venmo, peer-to-peer mobile payments literally didn't exist as a behavior. When I invested in Patreon, direct creator monetization wasn't a thing people did. Humwork is "Upwork with a 30-second SLA for AI agents" — and I've never gotten excited about a company whose pitch is a faster version of something that already works.

Yash Goenka is clearly smart — repeat YC founder (S25 and P26), UC Berkeley CS, building LLM products since 2021, patent holder for graphene supercapacitor manufacturing. Rohan Datta brings substance too: five years at Microgrid Labs, built an AI voice platform handling 1M+ call minutes, UC Berkeley BS/MS. Their 16-year friendship and roommate history is the kind of co-founder bond I take seriously. But here's where Humwork breaks my framework: the sanity-insanity ratio is inverted. These are sane founders pitching a sane idea. "AI agents get stuck and need human help" — every single person at demo day nods along. Nothing about Humwork makes you think "that's fucking crazy, but what if they're right?" It makes you think "yeah, obviously, someone should build that." And when everyone agrees, the price already reflects the thesis. What worries me more is the pivot velocity — Yash went from Qualify.bot (YC S25, launched July 2025) to Humwork (YC P26, launched April 2026) in roughly nine months, both under Orange AI Inc. I backed Raj Dittmar across three companies because I watched him grind through each one. Pivoting in nine months isn't grinding — it's searching.

Humwork fails the AI kill test in two directions simultaneously, which is rare and worth noting. Direction one: the MCP integration layer is technically replicable — the dossier explicitly states "any company with an expert network could build an MCP server. Tendem has already done so." Humwork's 1,000+ experts face Tendem's 10,000+ with existing Anthropic and Shopify relationships. Direction two — and this is the one nobody talks about — as AI agents improve, they get stuck less. Every new Claude release, every Gemini upgrade, every Cursor improvement reduces the frequency of the exact failures Humwork monetizes. Humwork is building a business whose TAM potentially shrinks with every foundation model improvement. That's the opposite of Metropolis, where parking demand doesn't disappear because software gets better. Physical infrastructure compounds; dependency on AI failure rates decays.

Humwork's 87% resolution rate and 130% net retention in software engineering are real metrics, and an advocate would argue they demonstrate product-market fit that Tendem's broader positioning can't match. The advocate would say Humwork's narrow focus on real-time agent debugging creates a wedge that a generalist like Tendem can't serve as well, the way Solana's speed-optimized architecture created a wedge against Ethereum's generalist positioning. I take the argument seriously. But the structural parallel breaks down: Solana was a new Layer 1 consensus mechanism — definitionally a protocol-level primitive with network effects that compound over time. Humwork is an application-layer marketplace where the integration channel (MCP) is owned by Anthropic and the expert supply is rented, not owned. There is no protocol-level lock-in. There are no physical assets. The 130% net retention is on an undisclosed revenue base, which could mean three customers expanding to four. The condition that would flip me to Invest: if Humwork showed me $50K+ MRR with 70%+ gross margins and evidence that Anthropic is partnering with them rather than building competing first-party expert escalation — that would demonstrate the market has underpriced their execution advantage despite Tendem's head start.

Humwork's two-person team and $30K-$50K GTM hire signal lean operations, which I respect — that's the right instinct at pre-seed. But capital discipline alone doesn't save a deal where the consensus mispricing score is this low and the structural durability is this weak. The decisive dimensions here are mispricing and durability: Humwork is a consensus bet in the hottest category in venture (AI agent infrastructure), building a technically replicable software layer on top of someone else's protocol (MCP), competing against a rival with 10x the expert supply and 70x the funding, in a market whose TAM might shrink as the underlying technology improves. Pass.
