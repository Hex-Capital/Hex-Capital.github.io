# PerfectBit -- Elad Gil Evaluation

PerfectBit is a real-catalyst arbitrage dressed as workflow infrastructure, but the dossier exposes a multi-miracle architecture underneath -- Vajda and Yamamoto correctly named the 2025 window (Scale AI losing lab customers post-Meta's $14.3B investment, plus FOVER/RLVR papers establishing programmatic verifiers), yet PerfectBit's pitch quietly assumes both that formal verifiers extend beyond code/math/physics *and* that ~5-10 frontier labs buy externally rather than build in-house. That high-catalyst, low-single-miracle pairing is the signature.

PerfectBit's strongest dimension is market re-opening, and the right ladder rung is **Anduril** (2022). The shared mechanism is precise: a named incumbent failure mode -- Pentagon software pre-Ukraine for Anduril, Scale AI losing OpenAI/Anthropic/Google work after Meta's June 2025 acquihire-by-investment for PerfectBit (TechCrunch, Oct 2025) -- combined with a technological step-function (autonomy stacks for Anduril, verifier-grounded RL papers like arXiv 2505.15960 for PerfectBit) that re-opened a category peers had already written off as commoditized labeling. Where PerfectBit diverges from Anduril is the *durability* of the catalyst: defense modernization is a decade-long procurement re-platforming, while the Scale-displacement window is a 12-18 month scramble that ends the moment Anthropic stands up an internal verifier team -- and given Yamamoto worked on Core Llama post-training at Meta Superintelligence Labs, he knows better than anyone how fast in-house teams can replicate this.

The lowest-dimension ladder is single-miracle, and the failure mechanism I keep returning to is the **fusion/novel-battery archetype** I've stayed out of -- "capitalization issues and science risk and market risk" stacked (Tim Ferriss #863). PerfectBit isn't fusion, but trace the dependency chain: miracle one is that formal proof systems and simulators extend from code/math/physics into the open-ended language tasks where labs actually need data (arXiv 2506.15522 and 2507.16331 explicitly flag this as unsolved); miracle two is that frontier labs, who employ the authors of those papers, prefer external vendor data over in-house generation; miracle three is that PerfectBit wins that vendor slot against Snorkel's $100M Series D expert-data platform and Surge AI's $1B+ revenue. Color Genomics worked because I isolated the miracle to workflow on top of commodity sequencing -- PerfectBit hasn't isolated; it's stacked.

PerfectBit's revenue velocity is the dimension where the silence is itself the signal. The dossier returns "No public revenue data found" four separate times, no Product Hunt launch, no press coverage, no disclosed pilots -- and in the post-2022 AI cycle, real verifier-data revenue would be the easiest thing in the world to leak. Compounded over 18 months: if PerfectBit lands one frontier lab in 2026, monopsony pricing means that single customer is 40-60% of ARR and dictates renewal terms; if it lands zero, Vajda and Yamamoto are an acqui-hire by Q3 2027. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" -- PerfectBit is too early to fail that test, but the absence of a single named pilot in the dossier at YC application time, given the founders' Meta Superintelligence Labs rolodex, is the kind of thing I notice.

Founder execution rhythm at PerfectBit is the counterfactual dimension worth flipping. Vajda ran Director of Media Generation at Meta Superintelligence Lab on text-to-image/video, and Yamamoto was Senior Staff on Core Llama pre-training -- this is the inverse of the **Perplexity** template, where Aravind cold-pinged me and shipped between meetings. To flip PerfectBit from a 10/20 to a 16/20 on this axis, I'd need to see independent shipped artifacts: a public verifier library on GitHub, a HuggingFace dataset with download counts, a demo with named domain coverage. The dossier returns "No public repos found" for both founders, which for two ex-Meta research leads building a data infrastructure company in 2026 is not a pre-seed-norm absence -- it's a posture choice.

PerfectBit's index position is where the Stripe parallel breaks cleanly. Stripe (2010) was an index on e-commerce because every new merchant had to touch the payments rail and the buyer pool was unbounded. PerfectBit's "training data for frontier AI labs" sits in a workflow path, yes -- but the path has 5-10 endpoints, all of whom employ the researchers writing the verifier papers PerfectBit is commercializing. That's monopsony exposure, not index exposure. Mercor at $10B post and $450-500M ARR (TechCrunch, Oct 2025) is the proof that the buyer pool can sustain a winner, but it's also the proof that the slot may already be taken.

My 36-month forecast for PerfectBit: acqui-hired by Anthropic, Google DeepMind, or Meta at sub-$200M for the founder pair and a narrow verified-code/math dataset library, with no standalone ARR above $15M -- the mechanism is that the catalyst is real but the durability is 12-18 months, and Vajda/Yamamoto's value to a frontier lab as in-house verifier-data leads exceeds their value as an external vendor selling into a 5-buyer market with Snorkel and Mercor already entrenched.

The strongest bull case stings: Scale AI was a 2-person team in 2016, and PerfectBit's ex-Meta Superintelligence Labs network is the exact seller-side moat that turned **Decagon** (2023) and **Abridge** (2023) into early winners in workflow infrastructure for catalyzed categories. If Yamamoto's Core Llama relationships convert one OpenAI or Anthropic contract at >$5M ACV in the next 12 months, the index-position critique inverts -- training data becomes the rail, PerfectBit becomes the rail's vendor, and the verifier library compounds into proprietary domain coverage Snorkel can't replicate from a heuristic-labeling starting point. The dismantle: Scale launched before any programmatic alternative existed; PerfectBit launches into Snorkel's $100M Series D, Mercor's $10B Series C, Surge's reported $15B raise, *and* a buyer pool of research labs who publish the underlying techniques themselves. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict is a named frontier-lab contract (OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, or xAI) at >$5M ACV publicly disclosed within 12 months -- absent that, PerfectBit is a Neutral pre-seed bet on the team optionality, not a thesis-fit check from my own money.
