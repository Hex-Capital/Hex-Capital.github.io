# Tasklet -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Andrew Lee carved Tasklet out of Shortwave because Shortwave's own users asked for the agent without the email client, and that one sentence is the entire reason Tasklet is interesting on a dimension I care about while being almost uninvestable on another. The signature is a founder with twelve years of cloud-runtime shipping muscle -- Firebase to Google 2014, then Firestore at Google, then Shortwave's agent stack -- landing in the single most consensus YC category of Spring 2026 at a $175M post on a $20M Series A led by USV and Lightspeed with Jeff Dean and the Collison brothers on the cap table. The founder is unusually well-fit. The round is unusually crowded. Tasklet is the rare deal where I respect the operator and recoil from the price in the same breath.

Palmer Luckey at Anduril (2017) is the rung I keep wanting to compare Tasklet to on lived experience, because the mechanism that mattered there was the same one that matters here: a founder who already shipped a hard runtime at consumer scale in an adjacent domain before this company existed. Andrew Lee did ship Firebase, and Firebase was a cloud runtime that ate the developer market the way Tasklet is trying to eat the knowledge-worker market. The divergence from the Anduril rung is that Palmer's contact with defense was autobiographical -- he wanted to build the thing himself, not because Oculus users asked. Tasklet's origin is closer to "we noticed the agent we built inside Shortwave was the actual product," which is real product instinct but still feels more like a carve-out than a calling. That difference is why Tasklet ladders below Anduril and above the pedigree-only AI-agent pass example, not at the Anduril rung itself.

The consensus AI-agent darling I wrote up as my pass template -- two ex-FAANG PMs, oversubscribed, six funds chasing, $20M seed -- failed my filter because the round itself was the deselection signal. Tasklet's $20M Series A at $175M led by USV plus Lightspeed plus Jeff Dean plus Patrick Collison plus John Collison plus YC is not just consensus, it is the most decorated cap table a Spring 2026 batch company could assemble. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus. Tasklet is consensus. The competitive set -- Lindy at $53.6M raised, Gumloop at $20M, Relay at $8.2M, plus Zapier Agents and OpenAI's native agent surface -- is exactly the "ten VCs already bidding" pattern that inverts the signal for me. There is no aesthetic objection, no regulatory hair, no capital-intensity scare keeping anyone out.

Crusoe (~2018) is the single anchor I hold up against Tasklet on capital-intensity, and the comparison is brutal in the wrong direction. Crusoe had roughnecks, well pads, generators, a North Dakota physical footprint, and a moat that compounded with every gas-flare contract. Tasklet has a chat box, a cloud sandbox, and "thousands of integrations" that any of Lindy, Gumloop, Zapier, OpenAI, or Anthropic can replicate in a quarter. Andrew Lee himself said on Cognitive Revolution that frontier models will generate UIs on the fly and that this logic is why he killed the Shortwave email client -- the same logic eats the horizontal agent UI Tasklet is currently selling. That is a founder publicly narrating his own commoditization risk, which I respect for the honesty and discount for the implication.

Tasklet's CEO question is the easy one: Andrew Lee is CEO, Jonny Dimond is CTO, they have worked together since Firebase circa 2012-2014, survived the Google acquisition, ran Shortwave together, and carved Tasklet out together. That is twelve years of partnership with a clear functional split. None of the HQ Trivia (2017) Yusupov-Kroll co-CEO ambiguity, none of the GameCrush (~2011) tiebreaker absence. If I were writing this check, this dimension is not where Tasklet would blow up.

Physical-world verifiability for Tasklet is the awkward middle. The Pro tier at $35/month and the free tier mean I can sign up tonight and try it, the Product Hunt rating of 4.8 across 144 reviews suggests something tactile is shipping, and 32 new integrations in a single release including Microsoft apps means the product is observable. It is not Pokemon Go on a street corner and it is not a Flock Safety camera bolted to a pole, but it is closer to the Uber (2010) "I rode it" test than to the developer-API abstraction I screen against. Tasklet earns mid-table on this dimension because the artifact exists, it just is not the kind of artifact a non-knowledge-worker can feel in their environment.

My 36-month forecast for Tasklet: acquired by Google or Microsoft as an agent-infrastructure tuck-in for $400M-$900M, with $30M-$70M ARR at the time of deal, after the model providers ship native agent surfaces that compress horizontal-agent pricing and Andrew Lee runs the same play he ran with Firebase. The mechanism producing this forecast is the dimensional signature: a high-pedigree shipping founder selling cloud infrastructure into a category that commoditizes before it consolidates, with a cap table -- USV, Lightspeed, Jeff Dean -- engineered for a clean strategic exit rather than a generational standalone outcome. Tasklet is Firebase, not SpaceX.

The bull case that should sting is the Firebase echo itself. Andrew Lee already executed this exact playbook -- cloud runtime, developer-shaped buyer, integration sprawl, acquired by a hyperscaler -- and returned capital. The $5M ARR with >1,200% growth since January 1, 2026 is genuinely fast for a product that launched out of beta in October 2025, and the carve-out logic is sound: users pulled the agent out of the email client, not the founders. If I am wrong, it is because I let the consensus-round signal override a founder who has literally already won this game once. The flip condition I will accept: if Tasklet crosses $25M ARR within 12 months with more than 60% of revenue from triggered, persistent, multi-step agents -- not one-shot chat runs -- the moat is compounding faster than model providers can erode it, and the Firebase analogy bends from "acquihire-shaped" to "Stripe-of-agents-shaped." Until then Tasklet is a pass for my own check at $175M, with the door open to follow if the trigger-revenue mix shows up in the next disclosure.
