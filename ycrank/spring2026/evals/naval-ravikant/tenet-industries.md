# Tenet Industries -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Hugo Frisk is shipping a 3,000 SEK kamikaze quadcopter from Stockholm to Swedish defense procurement officers and Ukrainian battlefield buyers, which means Tenet Industries is the structural inverse of every check I write — capital-and-labor leverage selling through ISP export licensing into multi-year government procurement cycles, the exact permissioned architecture I spent twenty years moving away from. The dimensional signature on Tenet Industries is *manufacturing-cost-curve play with zero distribution leverage*: highest on founder-domain fit through Emil Falk's Falks Metall AB exposure, lowest on the leverage architecture that opens every one of my checks. That pairing names what Tenet Industries actually is — a metals-and-molding shop wearing a software-shaped YC wrapper — and it tells me the answer before I get to founder vibes.

**Twitter** (2007) sits at the top of my permissionless-leverage ladder because Jack Dorsey's marginal cost of replication was zero and the platform required no gatekeeper to add the Nth user; Tenet Industries sits near the bottom of that same ladder because every Tenet drone shipped requires Swedish ISP export approval, a signed contract with a sovereign defense buyer, and a physical injection-molded chassis that costs real money per unit. The HX-2 from Helsing's €695M round and Anduril's >$6B war chest are already inside the Swedish and Ukrainian procurement officer's calendar; Tenet Industries is competing for the same calendar slot with $125K of YC stage-one capital. "Code and media are permissionless leverage" (May 2018) is the line I keep returning to, and a 14-part plastic-cast airframe sold one at a time to ministries of defense is the categorical opposite — capital-leverage requiring permission at every node.

Naval-passed-on **Pinterest** because I confused "I don't get it" with framework, and the lesson there is the inverse of what's happening with Tenet Industries: Pinterest *was* permissionless media with compounding user-added value and I missed it on taste; Tenet Industries is none of those things and I am rejecting it on framework, not taste. A single Tenet drone sold to the Swedish armed forces does not make the next drone more valuable to the next buyer — there is no Nth-user effect, no marketplace, no protocol. This is the exact single-sided architecture my Knowledge Project #18 anchor explicitly avoids because the upside math collapses from 10,000x to maybe 30x even if everything goes right; you cannot get a Twitter outcome out of a unit-sales hardware business no matter how cheap the unit gets.

Emil Falk's prior roles at Falks Metall AB and Hugo Frisk's "Mass production" LinkedIn headline are the only specific-knowledge signals in the dossier, and they are real but thin — this is **not** the **Replit** (April 2023) Amjad Masad profile of an obsessive who has been building the actual product since 2011, nor the **Stack Overflow** (2008) Atwood/Spolsky profile of practicing operators writing for their own tribe. Three KTH and SSE students, founded ~5 months before the April 2026 article, with a metals-family background contributing the manufacturing lens — over 18 months this dimension compounds well if Falk has shop-floor instinct that Helsing's €830M-funded engineering org cannot replicate, and collapses if "DFM from first principles" turns out to be undergraduate-thesis territory the moment a fielded military airframe needs vibration tolerance, EW resilience, and supply-chain redundancy.

The market dimension would flip from Pass to Neutral if Tenet Industries could credibly pivot from sub-$300 strike drones into adjacent sovereign-EU defense manufacturing categories — counter-drone, ISR airframes, missile components — without re-clearing ISP licensing each time, but the dossier shows zero evidence of that pivot path and the "first idea never works" axiom (Venture Hacks 2007-10) lands hard on a 4-person team with a single product wedge in the most permission-gated industry that exists. The Buffett trio on Tenet Industries reads as a defaultable neutral: three students, no integrity flags, no contested exits, but also no decade-long evidence of how Hugo Frisk, Fabian Andersson, and Emil Falk behave when the Swedish Inspectorate of Strategic Products denies the Ukraine export license and the runway is six months — which is the test that actually matters here.

My 36-month forecast on Tenet Industries: acqui-hired by Helsing or a Saab subsidiary for the DFM team at a sub-$30M outcome by mid-2029, with the founders absorbed as a manufacturing pod inside the acquirer's strike-drone line, never reaching $5M ARR as a standalone. The mechanism is the dimensional signature — manufacturing-cost knowledge has real value but zero distribution leverage, so the value accrues to whoever already owns the procurement relationship, not to the cost-curve innovator. Helsing's €695M (June 2024) buys them the patience to wait Tenet Industries out and the optionality to hire the team at a discount.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Anchorage Digital** exception — regulated custody violated my permissionless-leverage rule and I invested anyway because the regulatory moat itself became permission-as-moat in institutional crypto, and one could argue Sweden's ISP licensing plus EU sovereign-supply preference creates exactly that structural carve-out for Tenet Industries against US-component drones blocked by ITAR. The override condition for Anchorage was that I had been dollar-cost-averaging into crypto since **MetaStable** (2014) co-founding, and the category-timing call I had earned the right to make outweighed the architecture rule. I have made no equivalent pre-consensus accumulation in European defense; this is not a category I have been quietly building positions in for a decade, so the exception does not transfer. The condition that would flip my verdict: a signed and ISP-approved contract with a named Swedish or Ukrainian buyer at production volume above 10,000 units — at that point Tenet Industries has converted permission into moat the way Anchorage did, and the leverage architecture flips. Until that contract is named, the pass holds.
