# Gojiberry AI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Gojiberry AI is a clean-governance, well-executed team -- Pierre-Eliott Lallemant as CEO, Roman Czerny as CMO, Dylan Teixeira as CTO, three repeat founders with 2025 exits under their belts -- pointed at the single most consensus category of YC Spring 2026: AI SDR for small sales teams, $99/mo, competing into Apollo ($150M ARR) and Clay ($3.1B valuation). The signature is "clean operators, crowded lane." I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, so the question is whether anything in this dossier rebuts the lane.

Anduril-Luckey is the high rung on lived founder origin: Palmer shipped Oculus from his parents' garage before pivoting to defense, the biography preceded the company. The mechanism is that the founder was *of* the problem, not visiting it. Gojiberry AI diverges sharply -- Lallemant and Czerny sold CoCo AI, a ~€500K ARR WhatsApp marketing app for Shopify ecom, and Teixeira sold Edusign, paperless student attendance software at 600K+ users. Both prior companies are SaaS exits, both are commendable, neither put the founders inside the cold-outbound problem they're now selling against. They are operators who picked a hot category and are executing it well, which is closer to the ex-FAANG-PM-discovers-market template than to Luckey. The dossier is silent on whether any of the three ever ran an SDR seat or owned a sales pipeline as the user, and silence here reads as absence.

HQ Trivia is the low rung on magical weirdness for an instructive reason: it was a consumer artifact you could literally feel pull on you, which is why it overrode my co-founder governance rule. Gojiberry AI inverts this -- the dossier names Apollo, Clay, Amplemarket, Regie.ai, 11x, Smartlead, Outreach, Salesloft, Lemlist on a single page as the "consolidated AI-SDR field." That is not magically weird. That is the most legible category in the batch, with $186M+ raised at Clay alone and Regie.ai growing 300% YoY on $30M Series B. Long Journey's stated thesis is "find them before it becomes consensus," and Gojiberry AI is being evaluated *because* the consensus has already formed around it. No sophisticated investor has passed on this category on aesthetic grounds -- they have all funded a horse in it.

On single-CEO authority, Gojiberry AI scores well by absence of red flag: Lallemant is named CEO, Czerny is CMO, Teixeira is CTO, no shared title, no 50/50 language in the dossier. Three co-founders is the danger zone where I usually want documented tiebreaker authority, and the dossier offers none -- but the role separation is the cleanest signal available pre-seed, and the CoCo AI partnership between Lallemant and Czerny survived an exit, which is its own stress test. Counterfactually, what would flip this dimension lower is if either Czerny or Teixeira held co-CEO billing on LinkedIn; the dossier doesn't show that. Apply the default toward the upper half.

Gojiberry AI on capital-intensity is the opposite of the SpaceX 2008 swing. Pure-software AI-SDR at $99/mo with LinkedIn scraping as the primary moat is the lane that compounds the slowest defensively -- the dossier names LinkedIn platform-policy risk explicitly, citing past enforcement against Dux-Soup and Phantombuster. There is no hardware, no regulatory cordon, no rural-industrial license-to-operate. Eighteen months from now, if Clay or Apollo ship native intent + sequencer, Gojiberry AI's $1.4M ARR is a feature in a roadmap, not a company. The moat compounds in domains other investors refuse to enter; this is the domain every investor is entering.

Physical verifiability parallels Flock Safety in only the weakest sense -- you can install Gojiberry AI, you can run a campaign, the product ships and 1,000+ paying customers (or 500+, the dossier flags the inconsistency itself) are using it. Unlike Flock, I cannot photograph it in the wild or hand it to a friend over dinner. It is a B2B dashboard whose value resolves to founder-reported reply-rate lift ("3-5x more replies" per Product Hunt) that no third party has benchmarked.

Forecast, 36 months: Gojiberry AI reaches $4-8M ARR riding the 44% MoM curve into deceleration, then gets acquired by Apollo, Clay, or Outreach for $25-60M as a feature acquisition, or quietly aqui-hired into Salesforge. The mechanism is that clean execution in a commoditizing lane produces a respectable strategic exit, not a defining company. Lallemant and Czerny already proved they can sell -- they sold CoCo AI in 2025 -- and Teixeira sold Edusign the same year. Gojiberry AI is on the same trajectory.

The bull case that stings: HQ Trivia is my own Known Exception -- explosive product-led traction in a tactile category overrode my structural concerns, and Gojiberry AI's $112K MRR in 9 months with 44% MoM is the closest thing to explosive traction in this dossier. Maybe the compounding-data-loop claim is real, maybe the intent-signal index becomes proprietary at scale, maybe Lallemant is a Kalanick-at-The-Lobby quiet operator I haven't observed under non-pitch conditions. The dossier doesn't let me run that test -- no public founder demeanor data, no podcast moment that reveals "I don't know what he's going to do, but I'm going to watch him." What dismantles the bull case is the competitive set: HQ Trivia had no funded incumbent shipping the same game; Gojiberry AI has nine named competitors with collectively over $500M in capital. Tactile virality can override co-founder governance risk, but it cannot override category commoditization with this much funded competition. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Gojiberry AI hits $5M ARR by month 18 with sub-3% logo churn AND ships a non-LinkedIn signal source that Apollo cannot replicate from public data. Absent that, I pass and watch from the next round.
