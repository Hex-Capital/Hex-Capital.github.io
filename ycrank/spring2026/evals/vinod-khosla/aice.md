# AICE -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

AICE is what happens when a recent pivot meets a market that consensus VCs have already flooded -- which makes AICE's strongest dimension (Greg Chomette and Rémi Bouteiller pivoting from "sensors to cut energy bill by 30%" to subsea defense drones ~2 weeks before YC research) collide with its weakest (Anduril at $60B, Helsing at $18B, and Saronic at $9.25B already shipping into the exact category AICE just entered). The dossier hands me a documented learning-rate moment and, in the same breath, tells me this is no longer capital-hostile science -- it is a capital-saturated land grab.

**Slash** (2024) is the load-bearing analogy for AICE on founder learning rate, because the Slash check at $100M Series C was justified by a publicly documented pivot that demonstrated the founders updated their thesis under real-world signal, not by the consequence magnitude of consumer business banking. AICE has the pivot artifact -- Pivot Archive logged the energy-sensor-to-submarine-drone reorientation in May 2026 -- but the divergence from Slash is brutal: Slash's pivot was forced by a viral distribution event and resolved into a category with consumer compounding, while AICE's pivot is two weeks old, into a category with 24-month procurement cycles and ITAR gating, with no announced LOIs, no Marine Nationale partnership, no DGA contact. A pivot is evidence of learning rate only when the new vector compounds; AICE's new vector decays unless a defense buyer signs.

**Commonwealth Fusion** (2019) is where my framework places capital-hostile science: I led the $115M Series A precisely because consensus VCs would not touch fusion timelines, and that aversion was the moat. AICE's category does the opposite -- Saronic just closed $1.75B at $9.25B in March 2026 led by Kleiner Perkins, Anduril took $4B at $60B from a16z and Thrive the same month, Helsing is raising $1.2B at $18B as of May 2026 led by Dragoneer. The dossier does not describe a capital-hostile science problem; it describes a capital-saturated procurement race where AICE enters with two people and YC pre-seed against three multi-billion-dollar incumbents already shipping into the use case (Dive-LD at 6,000m, SG-1 Fathom at 90-day endurance). When consensus VCs are writing nine-figure checks into the same category, my edge disappears.

Consequence magnitude -- the dimension I evaluate first -- runs the mechanism test: what economic mechanism breaks if AICE wins? Subsea cable and pipeline sabotage is a real grey-zone variable, the Defense News (May 2024) French underwater-acoustics AI program is a real procurement signal, and the MarketsandMarkets AUV forecast of $3.13B → $4.64B by 2030 is a real number -- but none of these collapse an incumbent regime the way **Impossible Foods** (2011) collapses animal protein or **OpenAI** (2019) collapses the cognitive-labor input cost curve. AICE protects existing assets; it does not redraw a $15T variable.

Counterfactual on founder-defies-expert-consensus: what would have to be true for Chomette and Bouteiller to clear the **Impossible Foods** (2011) bar rather than the Pass Example bar? Chomette would need a publicly staked contrarian position on subsea autonomy that the Naval Postgraduate School or the AUV research community had rejected -- the MIT AeroAstro 2021 thesis is on aerospace, the NASA Asteroid Risk Assessment work (2019-2021) is orbital mechanics, and neither establishes a defied-expert moment in marine systems. The dossier gives me outsiders to the domain, which is not the same as defiers of the domain.

Required-capital profile maps to a single anchor: **Genesis AI** (2025), where I co-led $105M because the foundation-model-plus-robotics stack genuinely required nine-figure capital depth to reach a first commercial milestone. AICE's roadmap, by contrast, plausibly reaches a first deployable swarm prototype on $20-40M -- meaningful but below the threshold where my check size is the marginal capital that unlocks the deal. AICE could be funded by any defense-curious Tier-1 generalist, which means I am not the differentiated buyer.

My 36-month forecast: AICE is acqui-hired by Anduril or Helsing for under $30M (talent plus modular-swarm IP) by mid-2029, or shuttered, with no path to $5M ARR as an independent -- because the Dive-LD and SG-1 Fathom incumbency plus Saronic's $9.25B war chest closes the procurement window before AICE's two-person team clears ITAR qualification and a first navy contract. The mechanism is straightforward: in a defense category where Anduril acquired Dive Technologies in February 2022 for inorganic capability build, the small-form-factor swarm thesis becomes an acquisition target before it becomes a company.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Slash** (2024) exception applied straight: YC pedigree, documented pivot, and Nicolas Dessaigne's France ties plus Paris manufacturing could open the DGA and Marine Nationale procurement channel that the U.S.-centric incumbents (Anduril, Saronic) cannot easily own, with Helsing as the only real European competitor -- and France's May 2024 underwater-acoustics AI program is a named procurement signal, not an inferred one. That bull case stings, because the European-sovereign-defense thesis is real and I do back YC pivots. It loses on one fact: Slash's pivot landed in a category with consumer compounding mechanics, while AICE's pivot landed in a category where the buyer signs on a 24-month qualification cycle and the incumbent (Helsing, now acquiring Australian AUV firm Blue Ocean as of October 2025) is already inside the European procurement perimeter. The verdict flips if AICE announces a signed contract or LOI above $5M with the DGA, Marine Nationale, or a NATO navy within 12 months -- absent that, this is a category bet I would make through Helsing or Anduril, not through AICE.
