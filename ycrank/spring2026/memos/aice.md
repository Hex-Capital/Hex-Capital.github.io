## The Idea

- **Problem:** Naval assets and coastal/subsea critical infrastructure (cables, pipelines, ports) face evolving underwater threats, treated as "prime targets in grey-zone conflict" requiring covert mine countermeasures, seabed mapping and infrastructure surveillance (MarketsandMarkets AUV report, 2025).
- **Approach:** Swarms of autonomous modular subsea drones rated to 600m depth providing surveillance, detection and protection of underwater assets (aicepower.com).
- **Differentiation:** Anduril's Dive-LD is a single large-diameter AUV with 10-day endurance and 6,000m depth (anduril.com); Saronic builds surface vessels not submersibles (Bloomberg, Apr 2025); Helsing's SG-1 Fathom is a single 60kg glider for 90-day surveillance (DroneXL, Jun 2025) — [Inferred]: AICE's differentiator is modular swarm architecture at the small-form-factor end vs. monolithic large AUVs.
- **Business Model:** No public pricing data found; [Inferred]: defense procurement contracts with navies and critical-infrastructure operators, given product positioning on aicepower.com and YC description.
- **TAM/SAM:** Global AUV market USD 3.13B in 2025 forecast to USD 4.64B by 2030 (MarketsandMarkets, 2025); defense/homeland security captured 45.06% share in 2026 (Cognitive Market Research); broader UUV market USD 5.58B in 2025 to USD 25.9B by 2035 at 16.6% CAGR (Market Research Future).
- **GTM / Distribution:** No public GTM data found; [Inferred]: Direct sales to defense ministries, likely starting in France given hardware manufacturing in Paris (aicepower.com) and YC Group Partner Nicolas Dessaigne's France ties.

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** No defensibility signals found in public sources beyond founder domain credentials (Greg Chomette MIT AeroAstro MSc thesis, dspace.mit.edu; NASA Asteroid Risk Assessment Engineer 2019-2021, per LinkedIn).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Swarm coordination software, ITAR/defense export certifications, and classified contracts could create switching costs; unproven now because company is 2 weeks past pivot from energy sensors (Pivot Archive, May 2026) with no announced contracts.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: Defense procurement cycles and security clearance requirements create barriers for non-defense entrants; however incumbents Anduril, Helsing and Saronic are well-capitalized defense-native firms (TechCrunch, May 2026; CNBC, Mar 2026), so this barrier protects incumbents more than AICE.
- **Commoditization risk:** Multiple well-funded competitors already ship subsea AUVs (Anduril Dive-LD, Helsing SG-1 Fathom, Riptide UUVs per CB Insights), and Anduril acquired Dive Technologies in Feb 2022 for inorganic capability build (anduril.com), indicating the hardware category is replicable by acquirers.

## Key Risks

- **Recent pivot risk:** Company pivoted from energy sensors to submarine defense drones approximately 2 weeks before 2026-05-16 (Pivot Archive, May 2026), meaning all prior-product traction and learnings are largely non-transferable and the current product is effectively pre-MVP within the YC Spring 2026 batch.
- **Founder-domain gap:** Neither founder shows public naval, undersea, or defense-systems experience (per MIT AeroAstro thesis, NASA CERES paper, LinkedIn snippets); Chomette's domain was aerospace/asteroid risk and Bouteiller's was building energy sensors, raising execution risk in a domain (subsea acoustics, marine hardening, ITAR) materially different from prior work.
- **Well-capitalized incumbents:** Anduril ($60B valuation, Dive-LD shipping), Helsing ($18B valuation, SG-1 Fathom shipping with 90-day endurance), and Saronic ($9.25B valuation) have raised multi-billion-dollar rounds in the last 14 months (CNBC Mar 2026, TechCrunch May 2026, PRNewswire Mar 2026) and are already executing on subsea/maritime autonomy contracts; AICE enters with team of 2 and YC pre-seed capital.
- **Defense procurement cycle risk:** [Inferred]: Naval procurement typically requires multi-year qualification, ITAR compliance, and security clearances; a 2-person team 2 weeks post-pivot has no announced contracts, LOIs, or government partners (no public data found).
- **Name disambiguation risk:** "AICE" returns unrelated results including AICE Marine Science (Cambridge exam), AICE Inc. (David Chu CEO), and Aice ice cream brand (Instagram) — operational risk for SEO, trademark, and brand recognition in defense procurement contexts.
