# Zomma -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Varun Arumugam is an ex-JPMorgan founder with a prior YC exit doing $10-12M in sales, and he's building the exact company from my Pass Example — "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], YC S26, Vercel accelerator, Anthropic-and-Hermes on the developer page, /call demo CTA." Zomma's highest dimension is founder conviction via legitimate domain fit; its lowest is category sleeve, because "Computer Use Agents for back offices in finance" is the platonic instance of what I mean when I say "AI is just more internet." That pairing tells you what Zomma is: a competent finance-ops team shipping into the one 2026 sleeve I have explicitly re-weighted away from, at the exact moment the foundation model vendor announced it was building the same product itself.

The high-rung comparison for founder-market fit is my own **Drop.io** arc — bootstrapping a workflow primitive against free incumbents on the strength of having lived the file-sharing pain personally. The mechanism that makes the analogy load-bearing is that Arumugam did sit inside a JPMorgan back office and Cashel Fitzgerald shipped SWE at **Voyant** financial planning software, so Zomma's founders have touched the reconciliation and KYC workflows they're now selling into — that is not nothing. Where Zomma diverges from the Drop.io rung is missionary artifact: no essays, no threads, no GitHub, no public writing at all, just LinkedIn one-liners. "Writing is thinking. If you can't write, you can't think." I can't find Zomma's founders thinking in public about why vision-agents beat UiPath's DOM scripts on a specific custodian portal, which is the exact non-obvious belief a missionary founder in this category would already have published by demo day.

**Anthropic Finance Agents** — 10 pre-built templates for KYC and month-end close, plus a $1.5B JV with Blackstone, Goldman, and H&F in May 2026 — is the failure mechanism for the category-sleeve dimension: the foundation model vendor collapses the workflow layer before the seed-stage entrant can capture switching costs. That pattern is stamped into Zomma's dossier directly. Zomma's site names Claude as a supported agent, meaning Zomma is a customer of the same vendor now shipping competitive KYC and close templates directly into Zomma's ICP with Jamie Dimon–adjacent distribution attached. This is the "AI is just more internet" thesis playing out in real time in the dossier itself, not a hypothetical.

**Round pricing** — no evidence disclosed, applying my absent-evidence default and then pushing down. YC S26 + Vercel AI Accelerator + "finance AI agents" is the most consensus-priced sub-category in the batch; silence on round dynamics in a category this hot means I should assume the SAFE cap is bid up, not that it's cheap. My "VERY price sensitive" filter deselects Zomma before the meeting for the same reason my Pass Example deselects itself.

**Capital structure** compounds badly over 18 months. Zomma's "every action stops for human approval before anything submits" is a marketing feature and an ROI ceiling — labor-savings math on gated agents is capped, so the per-seat contract has to carry the whole story, and SEC-grade vendor risk review at hedge funds means the sales cycle burns 12-18 months per logo. That's a five-more-rounds SaaS cap table dressed in "Agent Trust Framework" language, not a **Teamshares**-style structure I can underwrite as venture-shaped. To flip Zomma's structure score one tier, I'd need a disclosed AUM-priced or outcome-priced contract — Qurrent's SLA-backed BPO shape — not per-seat, not demo-CTA.

**Founder-as-brand** compared against the **John Fish / Bookshelved** anchor: zero. No Twitter accounts found for any of the three founders, no GitHub, one un-disambiguated Jihyun Kim, no newsletter, no podcast, no owned community, and the primary distribution channel is a "/call" button and the Vercel accelerator cohort. This is the Slow Creator Fund filter applied in reverse — Zomma has none of the pre-existing distribution that makes the founder-as-brand line item score above the default.

Zomma's 36-month path is acquisition by **UiPath**, **SS&C**, or **iCapital** for under $50M as a talent-and-audit-trail tuck-in, or a squeeze-death against Anthropic's finance templates before crossing $3M ARR — the vision-agent thin layer between the model vendor and the fund-admin incumbent is where value gets sandwiched, not captured. The mechanism is straightforward: Anthropic already owns the model and now owns the workflow templates through the Blackstone-Goldman JV, while SS&C owns the buyer relationship — Zomma's audit-trail middleware has to justify itself in the shrinking gap between those two.

The strongest bull case invokes **Sublime Security** as Known Exception: pre-consensus enterprise security seed in a category incumbents dismissed, and the finance-ops portal-automation problem is arguably illegible to Anthropic's horizontal templates because scoped identity per custodian portal is a schlep the model vendor won't do. Zomma's ex-JPMorgan + ex-Voyant founder pair could be the team that translates SEC-audit-trail requirements into a moat Anthropic can't ship generically. The reason this doesn't win: Sublime entered before the consolidation wave broke; Zomma is entering after Anthropic's May 2026 announcement, meaning the consolidation wave is arriving on top of the seed round, not two years after it. One condition flips my verdict: a disclosed signed pilot with a named top-20 hedge fund on an AUM-priced or outcome-priced contract — that would prove Zomma is selling an illegible operational asset rather than a per-seat AI SaaS Anthropic will overwrite by year-end.
