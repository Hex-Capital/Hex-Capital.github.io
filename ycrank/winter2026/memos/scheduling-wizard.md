## The Idea

**Problem:** Hospitals and academic medical centers still rely on manual workflows — spreadsheets, phone calls, and ad hoc processes — to create physician schedules across rotations, call shifts, clinic assignments, and attending coverage. Residency program directors and chief residents spend hundreds of hours annually building these schedules while balancing ACGME duty-hour regulations, contractual obligations, vacation requests, and fairness constraints. The YC page cites $760B in annual inefficiency from manual hospital operations (YC company page). The primary customer segment is residency/fellowship program directors, chief residents, and attending physicians at academic medical centers.

**Approach:** Scheduling Wizard outsources the entire physician scheduling process from hospital departments. Rather than selling self-serve software, the company takes in scheduling constraints (rotation requirements, vacation requests, duty-hour rules) and returns completed schedules. This is powered by a proprietary "Scheduling Programming Language" and AI-driven workflows (YC company page). The product covers four schedule types: block/rotation schedules, call schedules, clinic schedules, and attending schedules (company website). The company claims integrations with Epic, Cerner, PACS, and billing systems, and compliance with HIPAA, GDPR, HITECH, SOC 2, and ISO 27001 (HealthAIDB listing, via search snippet).

**Differentiation:** Existing solutions fall into two categories: (1) legacy self-serve software like Amion ($449/year, 200,000+ providers) and Lightning Bolt (PerfectServe), which require departments to learn and operate the tool themselves; and (2) enterprise platforms like QGenda ($76.3M revenue, acquired by Hearst for $2.5B in August 2024), which target large health systems with broad workforce management suites. Scheduling Wizard differentiates by offering a managed service — departments outsource scheduling entirely rather than operating software. The proprietary Scheduling Programming Language is a technical differentiator versus rule-based or template-driven competitors. YouShift (YC W25), backed by Bain Capital Ventures, is pursuing a similar AI-powered approach but as self-serve software targeting the "Rippling for healthcare" vision (YC company page, via search snippet).

## Defensibility

The proprietary Scheduling Programming Language represents a technical moat — a domain-specific language purpose-built for healthcare scheduling constraints is non-trivial to replicate and accumulates institutional knowledge with each department onboarded. Each new client engagement encodes department-specific rules, preferences, and constraints, creating a growing library of scheduling logic. Switching costs are moderate: once a department has outsourced scheduling and encoded its constraints, reverting to manual scheduling or migrating to a new provider requires re-encoding all rules.

[Inferred]: A data moat could develop over time as the company accumulates scheduling patterns across hundreds of departments, enabling better default templates and optimization for new clients. However, this moat is nascent at 19 departments.

**Market structure:** QGenda's acquisition by Hearst for $2.5B (August 2024) (Crunchbase/CB Insights, via search snippet) validates the market but also signals consolidation at the enterprise tier. Lightning Bolt (PerfectServe) is focused on self-serve optimization for large systems. [Inferred]: Incumbents sell software tools, not managed services. Offering a managed scheduling service would cannibalize their per-seat software revenue model and require building an operations team — a fundamentally different business structure. This business model mismatch is the primary structural barrier.

**Commoditization risk:** The managed-service component is labor-intensive and could be replicated by consulting firms or staffing agencies. The AI/optimization layer could be built by well-funded competitors — QGenda (694 employees, $76.3M revenue) has the resources to develop similar capabilities. YouShift (YC W25) is pursuing an overlapping AI-powered approach. The Scheduling Programming Language is a differentiator, but its defensibility depends on how much domain-specific complexity it encodes versus general constraint-satisfaction techniques.

## Key Risks

**Managed-service scalability:** The current model requires the team to actively produce schedules for client departments rather than selling self-serve software. With 19 departments across 14 hospitals and a 3-person team, scaling to hundreds of departments may require either significant hiring or a transition to a more automated/self-serve model. The expansion into patient triaging and provider management systems adds operational complexity.

**Direct YC-batch competitor:** YouShift (YC W25), backed by Bain Capital Ventures, is building AI-powered shift scheduling for healthcare with a self-serve "Rippling for healthcare" vision (YC company page, via search snippet). As a fellow YC company one batch ahead, YouShift may compete for the same hospital customers and investor attention.

**Incumbent response risk:** QGenda's $2.5B acquisition by Hearst (August 2024) gives it significant resources to expand into the academic medical center / residency scheduling segment. Lightning Bolt (PerfectServe) already holds the #1 KLAS rating for physician scheduling for five years. Both incumbents could add managed-service offerings or enhanced AI features to their existing platforms with large installed bases.

**Customer concentration in academic medicine:** The current customer base (Johns Hopkins, Mass General, UT Southwestern, HCA, UCSF) is concentrated in academic medical centers and residency/fellowship programs. This is a specific niche within the broader hospital market; expanding beyond it into community hospitals, outpatient settings, or non-physician scheduling represents a different sales motion and product requirement set.

**Brand disambiguation:** "Scheduling Wizard" is a generic term that appears in multiple unrelated contexts (Aptora field service software, FindMySoft downloads, general scheduling features). This may create SEO and brand-recognition challenges as the company scales.
