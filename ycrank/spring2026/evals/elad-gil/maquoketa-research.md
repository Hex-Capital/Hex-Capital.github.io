# Maquoketa Research -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Maquoketa Research is what happens when a real catalyst arrives at a category I've already declared a winner in: the Pentagon's Drone Dominance program is genuinely opening a 30,000-unit OWA procurement window at ~$5,000/unit (Defense One, Jan 2026), but Maquoketa Research is pitching me a hardware-first attritable drone in the same trench where **Anduril** (2022) — a company I led the Series D on at $4.6B post — already owns the software-autonomy index. The dimensional signature here is high catalyst, low single-miracle: the window is real, and Maquoketa Research needs three independent miracles to walk through it.

On the catalyst dimension, the template is **Anduril** (2022) — Ukraine + Pentagon software inadequacy compressed a 24-month re-opening that peers misread as a graveyard market, and Maquoketa Research is fishing in the exact same waters with Drone Dominance and the March 2026 "drone swarm crucible" exercise (DefenseScoop). The mechanism that made Anduril load-bearing was that the catalyst opened *distribution* for a software layer, not procurement for another airframe SKU. Maquoketa Research diverges precisely there: the YC page frames the deliverable as "vertically integrated drone stack with in-house computer vision, RF/mixed-signal electronics, airframes, and ground control software" — meaning Maquoketa Research is trying to capture the catalyst by being a *unit*, while Anduril already captured it by being a *layer*. Same wave, wrong board.

The lowest rung on the single-miracle ladder is the one I cite in my pass example — "OpenAI for X" pitches whose only miracle is "the LLM gets cheaper," which is not a single miracle but a margin-compression bet. Maquoketa Research presents a four-miracle stack: (1) autonomous terminal-guidance CV that matches Ukraine field data at a $5K BOM, (2) US-only vertically integrated hardware manufacturing from a five-person team in Elk Grove Village, (3) DoD procurement entry without being named in the Replicator selectees alongside AeroVironment, Swarm Aero, and Performance Drone Works (Defense One, Jan 2026), and (4) survival against Anduril at $30.5B and Shield AI at $12.7B (TechCrunch, Mar 2026). Compound those probabilities and the NFX *Unusual Signs* math gives you a number you do not write a check against.

On revenue velocity, the **Harvey** (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" curve (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) is the reference for what "just working" looks like in dollars, and Maquoketa Research shows zero — no revenue, no contracts, no named pilot, only a company claim that autonomy "can quadruple mission success rates" based on Ukraine data (YC page). At pre-seed I don't penalize the absence of MRR, but in defense the legible early signal is a named program-of-record entry or a DoD prototype contract, and Maquoketa Research is conspicuously not on the Replicator list while three of its direct competitors are. That is the "false signal vs. just working" distinction running the wrong direction.

On founder shipping rhythm, the counterfactual is sharp: if Alejandro Hernandez's Berkeley Wireless Research Center work on 140 GHz MIMO phased arrays had translated into a shipped seeker module, a public flight-test video, or even a GitHub trail of RF tooling, Maquoketa Research jumps a tier. What's in the dossier is a researcher's CV plus a CEO (Evan Yeager) whose prior shipping was derivatives-trading software at SpiderRock — competent, but not the **Perplexity** (2022) Aravind pattern of "showed up a week later with a thing we discussed built." The dossier lists no GitHub for any founder and no flight-test artifact, which in hardware pre-seed is the legible signal that should be cheap to produce.

On index position, compare to **Coinbase** (2013): I bought it because it was "effectively a basket of every cryptocurrency" — Coinbase's value compounded automatically as the category grew, regardless of which token won. Maquoketa Research is the inverse — every dollar of Drone Dominance procurement that goes to AeroVironment's Switchblade 600 or to a Replicator-named peer is a dollar Maquoketa Research has to *take back* on the next contract cycle. Per my *AI Market Clarity* taxonomy, defense autonomy is a "winners declared" sub-market with Anduril at the top, and this is the same red flag I flag for Harvey-clones at consensus seed prices.

My 36-month forecast: Maquoketa Research is acquihired by Anduril, Shield AI, or AeroVironment for under $40M as a CV/RF talent pickup, or it fails to win a Replicator-adjacent contract by Q4 2027 and winds down — either way it does not reach $10M ARR as a standalone unit-economics business at $5K/unit against incumbents holding orders-of-magnitude more capital and procurement scar tissue.

The strongest opposing case rides on my own Known Exception: I overrode "science project" aversion for **Anduril** (2022) because the catalyst, the shipped founders, and the single-miracle architecture all lined up. A bull on Maquoketa Research argues the same template applies one stage earlier — Hernandez's mmWave phased-array depth is the rare seeker-relevant IP, the Drone Dominance window is genuinely open, and being first-mover on autonomous OWA at the $5K price point is the workflow position. The case stings because defense pre-seed is exactly where the catalyst pays. It loses to two facts in the dossier: Trae Stephens and Palmer Luckey had shipped Palantir and Oculus at scale before I led Anduril's Series D, while Maquoketa Research's founders have shipped research papers and trading software; and Anduril's *position* was software-on-defense, while Maquoketa Research's is hardware-versus-Anduril. I flip to invest on one verifiable condition: a named DoD prototype contract or Replicator follow-on selection with a disclosed dollar figure before the end of YC Demo Day.
