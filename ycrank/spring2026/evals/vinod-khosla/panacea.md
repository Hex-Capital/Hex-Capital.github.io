# Panacea -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Panacea is what my Pass Example was built to filter out, dressed in a higher-status vertical: two founders from Anterior using LLMs to compress the billable hours of RQM+ and ProPharma, selling a fixed-price consulting service to medtech buyers who currently pay $26K in 510(k) user fees and $250K-$5M when submissions fail. The dimensional signature is unambiguous -- Consequence Magnitude is the highest-placed axis and even there Panacea sits below **DoorDash** (2013), while Capital Profile and Technical Risk are at the floor. That pairing names the company: a services-arbitrage play on a real but bounded category, not a regime collapse.

On consequence, the ladder runs **OpenAI** (2019) at the top -- a civilizational redirection of cognitive labor -- through **DoorDash** (2013) in the middle as a consumer-commerce reshape, down to the GPT-4-sales-summarizer pattern I pass on in 30 seconds. Panacea sits between **DoorDash** and that pass example, closer to the pass: Haris Javed-Akhtar's pitch on withpanacea.com collapses the *cost* of FDA submissions by 12x, but the FDA itself does not disappear, the pathway does not disappear, and biotech timelines do not bend by a decade. The shared mechanism with **DoorDash** -- intermediating a fragmented professional services market -- is real, but Panacea's success-state at maximum ambition is "regulatory consulting becomes 80% cheaper," which is exactly the "30% better than the incumbent" anti-pattern in my Red Flags, just with a higher percentage. The $15T labor-displacement frame I quoted in Fortune (2026-03-06) does not load when the labor being displaced is several thousand FDA consultants billing hourly at RQM+ ($301M raised, PitchBook).

On technical and capital risk, the failure mechanism at the LOW rung -- the GPT-4 sales-summarizer profile where any Tier-1 generalist can fund and the science risk is zero -- is the pattern Panacea reproduces almost line-for-line. The withpanacea.com site describes a "proprietary AI platform" across IND, NDA, BLA, ODD, Pre-Subs, 510(k), De Novo, PMA, IDE, and BDD pathways, but there is no model training disclosed, no proprietary submissions corpus, no hardware, no clinical data partnership -- the dossier explicitly notes "no defensibility signals found in public sources." Compare to **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), where the $115M Series A I led was justified by SPARC's HTS-magnet physics requiring miracles consensus VCs refused to underwrite; Panacea requires zero miracles, which is precisely why Weave Bio's $20M USVP Series A (BusinessWire Oct 2025) and Innolitics's 3-month guarantee already crowd the space. Software wrapper on commodity foundation models with no proprietary stack -- that is the exact Red Flag from my framework.

Founder-defies-expert-consensus is where Panacea most quietly resembles the profile I publicly distrust. Haris Javed-Akhtar (MBBS Imperial College, ex-Partnerships at Anterior) and Yaman Ziadeh (ex-Bloomberg, ex-AI Platform at Anterior) are doing the Anterior playbook applied to a regulatory adjacency -- not the **Physical Intelligence** (2024) Sergey-Levine-defying-his-field pattern, but its inverse. Over 18 months this dimension compounds against Panacea: as Weave Bio's Parexel partnership (Parexel newsroom Oct 2025) signs more pharma logos, Panacea's pitch reduces to "we are the YC-pedigreed boutique version," which is a credential argument, not a defiance argument. My TED Talk citation of the UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study cuts both ways -- it tells me to discount expert validation, and Panacea offers no anti-expert position to validate in the first place.

Learning rate would flip from default to High only if Yaman Ziadeh's prior Baqi co-founder role were documented as a real pivot with stated reasoning, the way the Slash team's publicly-told Kanye-pivot story justified my $100M Series C in 2024. The dossier gives Baqi as a line on LinkedIn with no outcome, no thesis change, no "we were wrong about X" moment -- this is the absent-evidence case, and I apply the 7/15 default rather than penalize aggressively for a short company history. Counterfactual: if the YC partner Aaron Epstein surfaces a documented pivot from Baqi or a contrarian thesis update from Anterior, this score moves to 11-12/15 and partially rescues the founder dimension, but it does not rescue consequence magnitude.

Required-capital is the cleanest read in the dossier. Panacea is a 2-person YC pre-seed pre-deal at roughly $500K, and the roadmap is consultants-plus-AI -- it will reach $1-3M ARR on $3-5M total raised, the exact "Khosla is over-resourced" profile. Single-anchor parallel: this is the opposite shape of **Genesis AI** (July 2025), where Eclipse and I co-led $105M because robot foundation models required pretraining compute and a robotics-data flywheel that smaller checks could not buy. Panacea has no equivalent capital sink. My $50M into **OpenAI** (2019) and the $405M SPV (Oct 2024) were concentrated bets because the deployment of capital was load-bearing for the thesis; here the thesis does not need my capital.

Forecast, 36 months: Panacea reaches $4-9M ARR as a boutique AI-augmented FDA consultancy, raises a Series A from a healthcare-vertical fund (Bessemer Healthcare, Andreessen Bio, or similar -- not Khosla), and either gets acquired by Weave Bio or Parexel for $30-60M or settles into a profitable lifestyle services business under the shadow of Weave Bio's platform. The mechanism is straightforward: AI compresses billable hours, Panacea captures real margin against RQM+'s hourly model, the fixed-price wedge works, but the absence of a proprietary submissions dataset and the absence of named ex-FDA reviewers on the public team means Weave Bio's $30M war chest and Parexel distribution outscale Panacea before it builds a moat.

The strongest opposing case invokes the **Slash** (2024) Known Exception: YC pedigree plus a documented learning-rate signal can override a weak consequence-magnitude score, and Panacea has the YC pedigree. The bull would argue that FDA regulatory bottlenecks gate trillions in healthcare value, that Anterior-trained founders are the right ex-operator profile for medical AI, and that fixed-milestone pricing in an hourly-billing category is structurally cannibalistic to incumbents -- a real moat-by-business-model. That case stings because the cannibalization point is correct: RQM+ cannot match Panacea's pricing without destroying their own P&L. But Slash had a publicly-told pivot story that demonstrated learning rate; Panacea's dossier offers Anterior-continuity, not defiance. And the Slash check was Series C with traction; this is pre-seed with "no customer count, revenue, or named client disclosed" and a competitor sitting on a Parexel partnership. The verdict flips if by month 18 Panacea discloses 25+ completed FDA submissions, names 3+ ex-FDA reviewers on staff, and demonstrates a proprietary predicate-and-reviewer-response dataset that Weave Bio cannot replicate -- short of that, Panacea is a fine YC company and not a Khosla company.
