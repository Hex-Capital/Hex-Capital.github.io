# Thomas -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Thomas is a polymath self-automator wrapped around a commodity model substrate -- the highest-fit dimension is that Human Thomas built game bots at 13, presented at NeurIPS at 18, and scaled his own freelance work to $40k/month before automating himself out of that role, and the lowest is that the entire stack rides Anthropic's and OpenAI's computer-use APIs with no hardware, no regulation, no physical artifact beyond a one-line headline on madebythomas.ai. That pairing -- biographical authenticity stapled to a commoditizable substrate -- is what I'm actually pricing here.

Hassabis at DeepMind is the rung Thomas is reaching for on the lived-experience axis, and the analogy actually loads: a kid compounding across game-bot tinkering, academic ML, and freelance ops is the same polymath silhouette I described as "the age of the polymath" when I wrote into DeepMind in 2013. Where Thomas diverges from the Hassabis rung is that Hassabis carried chess prodigy + neuroscience PhD + game studio into a research lab whose moat was the lab itself, while Human Thomas carries his autobiography into a wrapper that any other polymath with an Anthropic API key could rebuild in a quarter -- the founder lived the problem, but the artifact he built does not yet require him.

The pass example I keep on file -- the consensus YC AI-agent darling with two ex-FAANG PMs, $20M oversubscribed, and a deck that opens with $200B TAM -- failed the capital-intensity axis because it was pure software abstraction whose moat was the model, not the founders. Thomas exhibits the same failure pattern in dossier form: no hardware, no regulatory wedge, no fertility or defense or energy moat that compounds with calendar time, just an autonomous-operator persona layered on third-party LLMs. The dossier explicitly notes "no proprietary model disclosed" and "LLM platform dependency" -- that is the **Vigil Labs** / **Crusoe** lane in negative.

The magically-weird dimension compounds or fails on whether the round is being chased or avoided. Eighteen months from now, "an AI that runs its own companies" either reads as the most-passed-on pitch in W26 because tier-1s found it gimmicky -- which is exactly when I write the check -- or it reads as the most-bid pitch because every agent fund pattern-matched it as the consumer face of computer-use. Dossier gives me no signal either way: no named co-investors, no TechCrunch W26 mention, no Product Hunt presence. The silence reads to me as "not yet consensus," which is why I score this above its midpoint rather than below.

CEO authority is the dimension where Thomas is structurally clean by accident of being a solo human founder; the "team size 2" line where the second member is the AI persona itself removes the HQ Trivia Yusupov/Kroll failure mode by definition. I don't have to ask who the tiebreaker is when one of the founders is a software process the other founder owns. This is the inverse of the GameCrush collapse -- no activist co-founder can strip product DNA when there is no human co-founder to align against.

Physical verifiability is where Thomas tests my Niantic-Ingress heuristic and partially fails it. I can follow @madebythomasai on X and observe the persona in the wild, which is more tactile than a pure dev-tools API; that is the **HQ Trivia** override pattern in miniature -- you can feel the artifact before you can measure it. But the dossier says madebythomas.ai shows "only a headline" via WebFetch, and there's no operated-company list, no MRR, no demo. The artifact I can touch is a tweet thread, not a product, and that is below where Postmates was when I bet on it because the delivery happened in front of me.

Thirty-six months out my forecast is that Thomas either gets acquihired by a foundation-model lab for $30-80M as a persona-and-distribution play, or pivots into a specific vertical agent (likely solo-operator marketing or e-commerce automation) running at $1-4M ARR -- the binary outcome the lived-experience-plus-commoditizable-substrate signature predicts. The persona equity compounds enough to be worth absorbing; the underlying tech does not compound enough to be worth scaling independently.

The strongest objection is the Hassabis exception itself: a true polymath who automated his own income IS the autobiographical founder I claim to fund, and the "moat from model not founder" red flag misreads who Thomas is -- the moat is the founder's ability to keep being the most legible AI-founder character on the internet, which compounds with audience the way Palmer Luckey's persona compounded with Anduril's recruiting. The dismantling is that Luckey shipped Oculus to scale before Anduril; Human Thomas shipped a $40k/month freelance practice, which is real but is not Keyhole-to-Niantic or Oculus-to-Anduril in magnitude, and the artifact under the persona is still a one-line headline. What flips my verdict: if within six months Thomas publishes a named operated company with verifiable third-party revenue above $25k/month attributable to the agent, I write the second check at the next round. Until then this is a watch, not a bet.
