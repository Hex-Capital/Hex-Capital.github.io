# Cova -- Peter Thiel Evaluation

Ansh Tandon's Cova is what happens when a YC team picks a regulated business model and mistakes the license for the secret. The pitch — "the entire operation runs on AI agents" inside a Michigan-licensed home care agency paying "the highest base rate in the state" off a $12.25/hour Medicaid Home Help reimbursement — is regulatory arbitrage dressed as a thesis. Cova's signature in my portfolio is the inversion of **Anduril** (2017): the most-present dimension is a regulated customer, and the most-absent dimension is a contrarian secret. That ordering is the wrong way around. Anduril had the secret first and the regulation second; Cova has the regulation first and is hoping a secret emerges from operating leverage.

**Stripe** (2010) is the load-bearing analogy on the contrarian-secret axis, and Cova does not load-bear against it. Patrick Collison's secret was that PayPal had declared the payments category closed while every developer trying to take a card hated the experience — a named incumbent and a named architectural blind spot the incumbent could not fix because its UX was its compliance perimeter. Cova's dossier names no analogous incumbent failure mode. Visiting Angels and Comfort Keepers are listed as competitors, but the dossier offers no hypothesis about why those franchises *structurally cannot* deploy the same AI back-office — the cited "cannibalization disincentive" is marked [Inferred] and is not the load-bearing barrier Lockheed cost-plus contracting was for Anduril. Worse, Sage Care and MochaCare (YC W26) are already selling the back-office AI into the same agencies, which means Cova's wedge ("we operate, they sell software") is a business-model preference, not a secret hidden from the outside world.

The indefinite-optimism pattern is what sinks Cova on the definite-plan dimension. The Founders Fund manifesto attacked "iterate-to-PMF" because it is the modern failure mode of capital allocation, and Cova's dossier surfaces no multi-year named milestone — no roadmap for which states unlock next, no caregiver-throughput target, no AI-agent accuracy benchmark on Medicaid prior-auth, no commitment to an architectural decision Tandon refuses to pivot from. Compare against **SpaceX** (2008), where Musk committed publicly to reusability on a 15-year clock; Cova's equivalent commitment surface is empty. The dossier offers a phone number, an email, and a claim about base rates. That is operating mode, not plan mode.

Monopoly trajectory compounds the wrong way over 18 months for Cova. The Michigan home care license is a regulatory asset, but it is the kind every licensed Michigan operator already owns — replication cost is filing paperwork in 49 other states, which is exactly the schlep software competitors Sage Care and MochaCare do not have to do. Pricing power is structurally capped: Cova bills against a state-administered $12.25/hour Medicaid rate and promises caregivers the highest base rate in the state, which means margin lives entirely inside AI-driven overhead reduction. That is not Palantir-grade integration depth; it is a thin operating-margin bet on agent reliability inside a price-controlled reimbursement system. Pricing power five years out terminates at whatever Michigan Medicaid decides the rate is in 2031.

The score on founder lineage flips one tier if Jordan Ibe turns out to have shipped a Medicaid-billing or eligibility product to scale at a prior company — that is the counterfactual. As the dossier stands, Ibe's identity is unconfirmed (the Cornell CS+Stats LinkedIn match is flagged as "not confirmed"), and Tandon's background is IC AI work at Apple and vehicle software at Tesla — competent technical pedigree, no shipped-to-scale product, no Medicaid-operations exposure. Against **Affirm** (2014) and the Levchin PayPal-Mafia re-up pattern that is my actual revealed sourcing channel, Cova has no signal. Against my **Asana** (2010) exception, where Moskovitz's Facebook-shipped pedigree carried a pure-software workflow bet, Cova has no override to spend.

On the atoms-heavy axis, parallel to **Affirm** (2014): Affirm sat inside a regulated payments rail where Levchin's compliance pedigree was itself the moat. Cova sits inside Medicaid Home Help, but the regulatory perimeter is thinner — a state agency license is not a banking charter, and Michigan-only concentration is what the dossier explicitly flags as the geographic-risk vector. The regulated-customer column is half-checked, not load-bearing.

My 36-month forecast on Cova: stuck under $3M ARR as a Michigan-only Medicaid Home Help agency, or pivoted to selling its AI billing-and-intake stack to other agencies — which means becoming Sage Care or MochaCare from the opposite direction. The mechanism is that the operating-leverage thesis cannot beat the price ceiling fast enough to fund multi-state license acquisition, and the software-vendor pivot is the gravity well every operator-with-tooling falls into when expansion capex outpaces reimbursement margin.

The strongest bull case against Cova's pass is **Honor**, which raised ~$625M to a $1.25B valuation by treating home care as a real operating business and not a software wrapper, and one could argue Tandon's AI-agent stack is the wedge Honor lacked. Cova-as-Honor-with-better-margins is the steel-man. The dossier dismantles it: Honor's path required hundreds of millions of capex to assemble agency-partner networks across geographies, which is the opposite shape of a power-law angel check — it is a private-equity rollup with venture cosplay. **Lyft** (2010) is the only nearby exception in my portfolio where I overrode monopoly purism, and Lyft had genuine driver-side network effects compounding in real time; Cova has no analogous compounding loop in the dossier, only a wage-premium claim. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Cova signing a Medicaid managed-care payer contract in a second state within 18 months of YC Demo Day, paired with a published AI-agent claims-accuracy benchmark at or above licensed-biller baseline. Absent that, this is the indefinite-optimism pattern Zero to One was written against. Pass.
