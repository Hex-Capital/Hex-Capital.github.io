# Kuli -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Kuli is a workflow-efficiency tool wearing an AI-agent costume, and the highest-scoring dimension in my framework -- the ten-year cofounder partnership between Michael Hodara and Jonathan Hassan with operational AI-marketing depth at Aircall ($20M paid-ads allocation) -- still cannot rescue the lowest dimension, which is that Kuli's success-state at maximum ambition is "marketing teams ship 3x more influencer campaigns." That is the Pass Example from my own framework almost verbatim. The Fortune 100 entertainment customer testimonial is not a civilizational variable moving; it is HubSpot's 2027 roadmap.

The HIGH rung on consequence magnitude is **OpenAI** (2019) -- $50M into a no-revenue entity because the success-state redrew what cognitive labor means against my "$15 trillion of U.S. GDP is labor" frame (Fortune 2026-03-06). The shared mechanism a Kuli bull would invoke is labor displacement -- the "8+ hours/week per marketer" figure on the YC Launch page is literally a labor-cost claim. But Kuli diverges from the OpenAI pattern precisely where it matters: OpenAI's success-state collapsed the category of cognitive work itself; Kuli's success-state collapses a Slack channel inside one persona at consumer-brand marketing teams. CreatorIQ at $35K-$200K/yr is the incumbent regime Kuli claims to disrupt, and the total influencer-marketing seat economy is a rounding error on the labor-displacement thesis I actually fund.

The LOW rung on technical/capital risk is the GPT-4 sales-call summarizer I pass on in 30 seconds -- the failure mechanism is that the underlying capability lives in a foundation-model API and the wrapper captures no durable economics. That pattern is in Kuli's dossier in plain text: the Key Risks section concedes "multimodal video analysis (the core 'watches every video' claim) is increasingly available via general-purpose model APIs," with no proprietary dataset or model benchmarks disclosed as a mitigation. Hodara's EPFL Robotics MSc does not change this; the "watch every video" pipeline is a prompt-engineering and pipeline-orchestration moat, not a Commonwealth Fusion ($115M Series A 2019) physics moat. Consensus VCs will fund Kuli precisely because the risk profile is software-only, which means I am the wrong investor by my own definition.

Hodara's profile -- EPFL MSc Robotics 2020, AI/data-science lead at Aircall running $20M in paid ads, Logitech voice/visual analysis on a $25M marketing budget -- and Hassan's six years at KPMG advising consumer brands on M&A is paradigmatic domain-expert continuity. My TED Talk cited the UC Berkeley study of 28,000 expert predictions performing only slightly better than chance for a reason; the founder pattern that earns the right with me has publicly contradicted their own field (the Impossible Foods Pat Brown pattern), and nothing in the dossier shows Hodara or Hassan staking a contrarian position against the influencer-marketing orthodoxy they are now selling into. They are selling the same buyer they used to work for. That is the inverse of what triggers my check.

Counterfactual on founder learning rate: the "side hustles together for about ten years" line on Hassan's LinkedIn (activity-7335991264493887491) is signal -- it is the only sentence in Kuli's dossier that scores above the absent-evidence default of 7/15 on this dimension. To flip this dimension a tier I would need a documented prior pivot -- the **Slash** (2024, $100M Series C) Kanye-West-pivot pattern that justified my override on a similarly non-civilizational consumer-business-banking thesis. Kuli does not have that documented inflection. A decade of side projects shipping together is durable partnership evidence, not a thesis-update event.

Required-capital profile compounds the mismatch over 18 months: Kuli's go-to-market is founder-led enterprise sales through Hodara's Calendly link with a free-trial funnel, and the implied burn to first commercial milestone is the standard $3-5M pre-seed-to-seed SaaS ladder. My recent solo checks -- $50M into **OpenAI** (2019), $115M lead Series A in **Commonwealth Fusion** (2019), $105M co-led seed for **Genesis AI** (2025) -- exist because the roadmap could not be funded at smaller scale. Kuli's roadmap can be funded by any Tier-1 generalist or by HubSpot's corp dev team writing a check from petty cash. I am over-resourced for this deal, which by my framework is itself a pass signal.

My 36-month forecast: Kuli reaches $3-8M ARR by Q2 2029, gets acquired by CreatorIQ, Sprout Social, or HubSpot in the $25-80M range as the AI-agent layer their incumbent seats need, and never operates as an independent compounder past a Series A. The mechanism is the Key Risks section's own admission -- foundation-model video understanding commoditizes the "watches every video" wedge while CreatorIQ's $35K-$200K enterprise contracts give incumbents the distribution to bundle Kuli's surface features into existing seats before Kuli locks workflow ownership.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass is the **DoorDash** (2013) exception -- capital-light consumer logistics that contradicted my science-risk rhetoric and returned a $72B IPO 2020. The argument: Kuli sits at the throat of a creator-brand transactional layer that is "doubling in 2026" per the YC Launch page, with Disney, Nivea, and Havas already cited as users on kuli.one; the consequence is rewiring how $30B+ of global influencer spend gets allocated, and Hodara's $20M Aircall paid-ads operational chops make him a credible Tony-Xu-shaped founder for marketing-spend allocation. The dossier evidence that wins against this: DoorDash's override was consequence magnitude in reshaping consumer-commerce flow at the scale of food itself -- a $700B+ category -- whereas the influencer-marketing economy at full saturation is roughly two orders of magnitude smaller and Disney/Nivea/Havas are listed as users, not as named contract counterparties with disclosed ACVs. One verifiable condition flips my verdict: Kuli posts $10M+ ARR within 18 months with at least three multi-year enterprise contracts above $500K ACV showing that the workflow-ownership lock-in actually compresses CreatorIQ's seats rather than supplementing them. Absent that, this is a pass and the math is not close.
