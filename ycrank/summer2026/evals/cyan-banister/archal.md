# Archal -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Archal is the eval platform for autonomous software pitched by two co-founders — Noah Song, who taught grade-school robotics at Illinois Tech from 2017–2020, and Aidan Tiruvan, a current CU Boulder CS+math undergrad with a Scale AI internship — chasing the most heavily capitalized lane of the 2026 YC cycle, where Braintrust closed $80M at an $800M post in February 2026 and LangSmith shipped a Sandboxes feature in March 2026. The signature here is a credential-light, software-only team running at the consensus eval-infra wedge with no lived-problem origin and no tactile artifact a normal human can touch. That is the opposite of the lane I write checks into.

Archal's founders did not surface from running an agent that wrote to a production Stripe account and got burned — they surfaced from a CS undergraduate trajectory and an ML-research internship into the hottest infra category of the batch. Tiruvan's Scale AI exposure is real adjacent context, not lived pain; nothing in the archal.ai landing copy or the LinkedIn footprints says "we shipped autonomous agents at scale, watched them irreversibly write to production, and built the test harness we wished we had." This is closer to the consensus YC AI-darling pass-example than to the Luckey template, and the dossier's absence of a personal "moment" of contact is itself the signal.

Crusoe ~2018 is the low rung on magical weirdness for the inverse reason this dimension matters: every credible VC had passed on aesthetic grounds, which was the tell. Archal sits inside the most contested 2026 category — Braintrust just printed at $800M post, LangSmith has Sequoia/Benchmark/IVP behind Sandboxes, Langfuse got acquired by ClickHouse in January 2026, and Modal raised >$80M for the adjacent sandbox lane. Archal is not the company nobody is chasing; Archal is the company that exists *because* everyone is chasing this. The thesis is to find them before it becomes consensus, and consensus arrived eighteen months before Archal's batch.

Over an 18-month window the frontier-hardware dimension does not compound for Archal because the surface is pure software — six SaaS clones today against an incumbent expansion vector where LangSmith Sandboxes already lives inside the LangChain SDK distribution that Archal's customers also use. Capital-intensity here runs the wrong direction: Archal pays the engineering tax of reverse-engineering Stripe, Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub, Supabase, and Linear behavior continuously while a Braintrust with $124.3M raised can fund the same clone library as a free SKU bundled with trace ingestion. This is the "AI moat is the model, not the founders" failure mode from the pass example — except here the moat is a fixture library, and a polymath generalist with the Braintrust war chest can rebuild it in a quarter.

A 2-person team with one current undergraduate and no documented prior partnership is the configuration I have been burned by, not the configuration that earns the override.

HQ Trivia 2017 is the single-anchor parallel on physical verifiability: the override there was that I could literally open the app, feel the viral pull on a Wednesday night, and watch the lobby count climb. Archal has no pricing page on archal.ai, no Product Hunt launch, no published demo I can install in 48 hours, no customer logo I can call. The product is a B2B developer abstraction whose value lives entirely in founder testimony and a markdown-scenario syntax I cannot photograph. That is the pass-example shape, not the HQ Trivia override shape.

My 36-month forecast for Archal: acqui-hire into LangSmith, Braintrust, or Modal by mid-2028 at sub-$500K ARR with the clone-fixture IP folded into the acquirer's sandbox SKU, after the maintenance treadmill on six-plus SaaS APIs outruns a 2-person team's engineering bandwidth and Stripe or GitHub sends a ToS letter that forces a rewrite. The mechanism is the dimensional signature: a non-lived founder origin in a consensus category with no tactile artifact resolves into a feature acquisition, not a defining company.

The strongest bull case for Archal is that stateful SaaS-API clones are genuinely technically distinct from both trace-and-score observability (Braintrust, LangSmith, Arize) and generic code-execution sandboxes (E2B, Modal, Blaxel), and Tiruvan's Scale AI ML-infra exposure plus Song's hands-on engineering instructor background could let two people ship a higher-fidelity Stripe and GitHub clone faster than a $124.3M-raised incumbent re-orients its roadmap — and if the clone library becomes the de-facto CI fixture the way Faker became the default test-data library, the switching costs compound. That sting is real, and the Known Exceptions list does include DeepMind, where pedigreed-but-polymath founders overrode my lived-experience screen. The dossier evidence that wins against the bull case is the third-party-API ToS exposure and the LangSmith Sandboxes March 2026 launch inside the LangChain distribution Archal's own SDK already integrates with — Archal is building on rented land while the landlord is shipping the same product. The verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is a public partnership announcement from Stripe, GitHub, or Google Workspace naming Archal as an official sandbox/testing partner, because that converts ToS exposure into a moat overnight. Until then, pass.
