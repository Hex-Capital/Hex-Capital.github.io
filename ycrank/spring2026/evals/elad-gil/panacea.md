# Panacea -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Panacea is the kind of deal that looks AI-native in the headline and reveals itself as a billable-hour consultancy with an LLM bolted on once you read past the homepage -- two ex-Anterior founders, Haris Javed-Akhtar (MBBS, Imperial) and Yaman Ziadeh (ex-Bloomberg, AI Platform), pitching fixed-milestone FDA regulatory work in a market where 510(k) user fees jumped 20% year-over-year and the FDA cleared 295 AI/ML device submissions in 2025 (Innolitics, Cruxi). The catalyst is real. The architecture is wrong. That is Panacea's signature: highest on Market Re-Opening, lowest on Index Position, and the gap between those two is exactly the gap between an infrastructure bet and a services bet.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for Panacea on the catalyst dimension -- in both cases the market peers had quietly written off (defense pre-Ukraine; FDA reg consulting as a hourly-billed legacy trade with RQM+/ProPharma/MDI Consultants) got re-opened by a step-function the incumbents structurally can't absorb. For Anduril the unlock was Pentagon software inadequacy revealed in a 24-month window; for Panacea the unlock is LLM tooling hitting production usability against a submission cost curve where 510(k) remediation runs $250K-$5M (Cruxi, 2025) and AI/ML 510(k) volume hit 295 clearances in 2025. Where Panacea diverges from the Anduril rung is the *magnitude* of the catalyst -- Ukraine forced a procurement reset; a 20% fee hike and rising AI/ML submission volume is more like a gentle tailwind than a re-opening. It's a catalyst, just not a Stripe-2010 / Coinbase-2013 / Anduril-2022 one.

**Stripe** (2010) is the index-position template and Panacea fails it cleanly. Stripe sits in the workflow path of every e-commerce entrant -- they don't beat Shopify on storefronts, they get integrated against. The failure mechanism for a non-index company is that growth requires winning each engagement individually rather than compounding as the category grows, and Panacea's dossier shows exactly this: "Book my free consultation" via cal.com/haris-panacea/call, fixed-price project fees scaled to pathway complexity, no published price tiers, no SaaS surface. Weave Bio raised $20M Series A from USVP in October 2025 and partnered with Parexel precisely because the *platform* layer is where index economics live (BusinessWire, Oct 16 2025); Panacea is selling consultant-time-plus-AI, which is the legacy RQM+ model with better margins, not a structural position. Panacea does not become more valuable as 510(k) volume compounds -- they have to staff up to capture it.

Revenue velocity: the dossier discloses "client work 12x faster with error-free outputs" as an internal claim and zero customers, zero ARR, zero named logos. In the AI cycle, silence on growth is itself a signal -- Harvey ran $3B → $5B → $8B in months precisely because the curve was disclosable (TechCrunch, Nov 2025), and over 18 months a services business with fixed-price exposure to FDA cycle-time variance either accumulates a submission dataset that becomes a proprietary moat or gets caught in margin compression when a 510(k) bounces. With two founders and no named ex-FDA bench on the website, Panacea is at the wrong end of that compounding curve.

Founder shipping: counterfactual to flip a tier here would require Yaman Ziadeh's Baqi to be a named, shipped, in-market product with users, plus public GitHub artifacts from the Anterior AI Platform work, plus a named ex-FDA reviewer on the team page. None of that is in the dossier -- Haris's Partnerships Lead role at Anterior is not a shipping role, and Yaman's CTO/co-founder stint at Baqi has no product surface I can verify. Compare to the Perplexity 2022 origin -- Aravind pinged on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with a built thing. Panacea's founders have credible healthcare-AI proximity through Anterior, but proximity is not shipping rhythm.

Single-miracle test on Panacea: the company requires *two* independent miracles to compound -- LLM accuracy at FDA submission drafting (assumed, plausibly validated) *and* consulting-supply economics where ex-FDA reviewers can be hired into a fixed-price model whose margin survives FDA review variance. **Color Genomics** (2015) succeeded as a workflow layer on commodity sequencing because there was one miracle (workflow), and the chemistry was already commoditized. Panacea is closer to that template than to a science project, but the consulting-bench dependency is a second miracle the dossier doesn't isolate -- no named ex-FDA hires, no disclosed engagement count, no published bench size. That second miracle is what makes me read this as a services company rather than infrastructure.

36-month forecast: Panacea reaches $3-8M in services revenue as a boutique YC-branded FDA reg consultancy, gets out-distributed by Weave Bio's $30M war chest plus the Parexel partnership pipeline, and either acqui-hires into a CRO (Parexel, IQVIA, or RQM+ itself) or pivots into a SaaS product layer that sits underneath the consulting work -- which is the only path where Panacea becomes the *index* rather than the labor.

The bull case stings on one specific axis: Anterior alumni network plus the MBBS+AI-platform founder pairing is genuinely scarce, and the fixed-price wedge against hourly incumbents like RQM+ ($301M raised, Linden/SLR-backed) is a real cannibalization trap incumbents can't easily counter -- Color Genomics 2015 is the Known Exception precedent for a workflow-on-hard-thing healthcare bet I'd back. The dismantling is that Color was operated, not passively invested, and architected from day one as data infrastructure on top of sequencing -- Panacea's dossier shows the opposite shape, a consulting business with AI tooling, not a data-accumulating workflow layer. Verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Panacea discloses 10+ named device or biotech clients within 12 months with month-over-month revenue growth above 20% *and* names a credentialed ex-FDA reviewer publicly. Until then, this is the right team in the right catalyst with the wrong structure -- a pass for me, but a watch-list pass.
