# RASPIRE -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Kareem Selim and Hassan Mostafa spent years inside Egyptian banks and fintechs fixing the exact post-compilation gap RASPIRE now sells against — that is the part that loads in my hand. The part that doesn't is the artifact itself: RASPIRE is invisible runtime protection inside someone else's mobile binary, in a G2 category already populated by Appdome, Zimperium, Guardsquare, Promon, and Approov. The signature is domain-authentic founders building an unobservable B2B layer in a commoditized lane — the inverse of how I usually find these.

Anduril is the high rung on lived-experience: Palmer Luckey built Oculus first, then walked into defense because he had already shipped hardware that worked. RASPIRE's founders sit one rung below that — Selim and Mostafa fixed bank and fintech apps for years, Mostafa at Cyshield, and the protection-gap-after-shipping is the precise pain they touched, not a memo they read. They have not shipped a comparable product to scale in an adjacent domain the way Luckey had Oculus, but they have something better than the ex-FAANG-PM template I pass on reflexively: a biography that put them in front of this problem before they wrote a deck. The 20M+ end-user footprint cited in the FwdStart coverage is not the kind of number I trust without seeing the contracts, but the founders' shape is right.

HQ Trivia is the low-rung shadow on physical verifiability — I overrode my co-founder rule because I could open the app and feel the viral pull in my own hand. RASPIRE inverts that exception entirely. I cannot install RASPIRE. I cannot scan for it at a Four Seasons café the way I found Flock Safety. The only way to "observe" RASPIRE is to take a banking app, decompile it, and look for the instrumentation — and that is not how I source. Twenty million end users are using protected apps somewhere in MENA, and I cannot point at one of them. The Niantic-Ingress mechanism — me playing the product for three years before writing the check — has no analog here. The artifact is real; my method cannot reach it.

On magically-weird, the round dynamics are wrong in a specific way: RASPIRE's "no-code + AI" positioning is the exact wrapper Appdome already prints on its homepage, and Appdome has $39.6M in the bank to defend it. The non-consensus angle, if there is one, is MENA-first GTM through Sadq and Vision 2030 procurement — that *is* a lane Western VCs won't touch, and it's the only place in the dossier where I see something other investors would skip on aesthetic grounds. Eighteen months of compounding here looks like either a regional moat that US incumbents don't bother penetrating, or a slow grind against Zimperium's $60.1M war chest on global accounts.

On CEO authority, the counterfactual to flip a tier would be evidence the Selim/Mostafa split is 50/50 with no operating agreement — the GameCrush failure mode. The dossier shows Selim as CEO, Mostafa as CTO, clean role delineation, no shared title, no public friction. That is enough to clear the heuristic, not enough to celebrate. I would still want to hear the tiebreaker conversation out loud before signing.

On capital-intensity-and-unsexy-regulated, the single anchor is Vigil Labs — regulated financial software, no hardware, founder lives in the domain. RASPIRE sits on that exact axis: banking, insurance, government as named buyers, no physical asset, regulatory tailwind from Saudi Vision 2030. This is the dimension where RASPIRE genuinely resembles a check I have written before, and it is also the dimension where the moat is thinnest — Vigil's defensibility is the regulatory native language, and I have not yet heard RASPIRE's MENA-compliance native language articulated as the wedge.

The 36-month forecast: RASPIRE reaches $3-8M ARR concentrated in Saudi and Egyptian banking customers off the Sadq channel, never breaks into US tier-1 banks where Appdome and Zimperium are entrenched, and gets acquired by Appdome or a Gulf-region cybersecurity holding company for $40-90M — a respectable outcome for the founders, not the venture-scale return I write checks for. The mechanism producing it is the collision between authentic founder-market fit and a category where the technical primitives are catalogued commodity features.

The bull case that stings: Crusoe sounded like three uninvestable things stapled together, and the override was that Chase was *of* the oil patch, not visiting it. Selim and Mostafa are *of* MENA fintech security, not visiting it, and Saudi Vision 2030 procurement is a wall most American security vendors structurally cannot climb because they cannot pass the local data-residency and Arabic-language compliance bar. If the 20M+ end-user figure converts into disclosed seven-figure ARR from named Saudi banks before Demo Day, the regional-moat thesis is live and I am underpricing this. The condition that would flip me to Invest: a signed contract with a named GCC tier-1 bank, with ARR disclosed, before the YC batch ends — proof that the Sadq partnership is a channel and not a logo slide.
