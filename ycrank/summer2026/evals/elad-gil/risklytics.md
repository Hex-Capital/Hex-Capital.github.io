# Risklytics -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Risklytics is a "right catalyst, wrong distribution slot" company: CA DOI's May 2025 approval of forward-looking wildfire cat models is a genuine 24-month re-opening, but the three names on the approved list are Verisk, KCC, and Moody's -- not Risklytics -- and Samuel Gold and Alexander Risio are competing into a market where ZestyAI's Z-FIRE already sits inside carriers writing 40% of California homeowners and CAPE Analytics distributes through Moody's post its January 2025 acquisition. That signature -- highest on catalyst, lowest on index position -- is what I'm scoring around.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analogy on catalyst. Same template: a market peers had written off (defense pre-Ukraine, California homeowners insurance post-2.8M non-renewals), a specific regulatory or geopolitical event that changed the math (Pentagon software procurement post-2022; CA DOI release 052-2025 letting insurers file rates using forward-looking models for the first time), and a workflow-infrastructure-on-top-of-hard-thing thesis rather than a science bet. Where Risklytics diverges from the Anduril template is founder positioning against the catalyst -- Trae Stephens had built Palantir's forward-deployed muscle before Anduril was Anduril, whereas the Risklytics dossier surfaces zero prior insurance, actuarial, or catastrophe-modeling roles for either Gold (Harvard CS/Econ, one year, undergrad finance club) or Risio (Harvard AB/SM CS). The catalyst is real; the founders arrived at the window without the domain scar tissue that made the Anduril bet obvious at $4.6B post.

**Lyft** Series C is the failure pattern on distribution, and it's the pattern I have to test Risklytics against. I passed on Lyft because #2 in a network-effect market captures a fraction of #1's economics, and Risklytics is walking into a similar structural pin: ZestyAI has $76.97M raised, 200B+ datapoints, and is already deployed inside 40% of the California carrier base, while CAPE now ships through Moody's rails. The dossier's own moat claim -- 131,929 CAL FIRE damage inspections -- is drawn from a public dataset, so the underlying corpus is not proprietary in the way the API page implies. Risklytics's retrofit-path-to-insurability packaging is a real product wedge, but it's a *feature* wedge in a market where the distribution wedge is a Guidewire integration or a Moody's SKU, and Risklytics has neither.

On revenue velocity (mechanism analysis): in the AI cycle, silence on ARR is itself the signal, and Risklytics discloses 13,685,446 California homes scored but zero dollars, zero named carriers, zero LOIs -- the pricing page is gated behind an API-key application form. The mechanism that compounds over 18 months in this category is *underwriter contract velocity*, because one top-15 CA carrier signature drags in three more via consultant channels; the mechanism that fails is *pilot-tourism-without-conversion*, where insurers score portfolios for free and never move to production. Nothing in the Risklytics dossier distinguishes which side of that mechanism they're on, which is why I'm defaulting rather than crediting the 13.6M-homes-scored number.

On founder shipping (counterfactual): Risklytics has actually shipped a scored artifact -- 30,469 homes in the January 2025 LA fires with 91% discrimination between burned and unburned is a real technical output, not a deck slide. For this score to flip to the **Perplexity** (2022) tier, I'd need to see Gold or Risio's prior shipped products named -- a Harvard side project that got users, a github repo with pull traffic, an earlier ML system deployed somewhere. The dossier surfaces neither; Risio's personal site "lists a resume and contact links only" and Gold has no public GitHub. So Risklytics gets credit for the Ember artifact but not for the Aravind-pattern of shipping between meetings, because there's no *prior* shipping rhythm on record.

On single-miracle architecture (single-anchor parallel): **Color Genomics** (2015) is the right comparison because we deliberately structured Color as workflow-on-top-of-commodity-sequencing to isolate the miracle to distribution rather than chemistry. Risklytics tries the same structural move -- ML on top of public CAL FIRE data -- but the miracle chain doesn't collapse cleanly: they need model accuracy (arguably done at 91%), *plus* CA DOI filed-rate approval to enter the actual pricing workflow, *plus* carrier underwriting-rule acceptance of retrofit recommendations, *plus* geographic expansion beyond California to escape the concentration risk. The dossier explicitly notes the filed-rate approval barrier gates them to non-filed use cases. That's not one miracle; that's three, compounded.

My 36-month forecast: Risklytics ends 2029 either at sub-$2M ARR selling non-filed eligibility screening to E&S/surplus-lines writers, or acqui-hired for talent-plus-dataset by a second-tier cat modeler (Karen Clark, or a Guidewire-adjacent analytics buyer) -- not Moody's, because Moody's already has CAPE. The mechanism producing this: the catalyst opened the window, but the three CA-DOI-approved names captured the filed-rate lane, so Risklytics is fighting for the underwriting-decision lane against a better-funded and better-distributed ZestyAI. That is a share fight, not an index position, and share fights at pre-seed against $76M-raised incumbents rarely produce the durability I'm underwriting for.

The strongest opposing case: the California FAIR Plan grew from 126K policies to 450K+ with $650B exposure in seven years, and carriers are non-renewing at scale -- so the *screening* market (who do I bind, who do I decline, what retrofits move a decline to a bind) is arguably wide open because Verisk/KCC/Moody's are optimized for filed rates, not eligibility APIs. Risklytics's retrofit-path packaging *is* a genuinely different API primitive from Z-FIRE's risk score. That case stings because it maps to my *AI Market Clarity* "still wide open" sub-market pattern -- workflow infrastructure below the incumbents' altitude. What defeats it in the dossier: the founder domain gap. Gold and Risio don't have the underwriter-network to run the "in-your-in-laws" GTM this requires, and no advisor, actuary, or ex-carrier operator is disclosed. Verifiable flip: a signed pilot with a top-15 CA admitted carrier or FAIR Plan itself in the next six months, disclosed publicly with a named counterparty -- that would move Risklytics from the ZestyAI-shadow lane to the **Decagon**-style workflow-infrastructure lane and I'd revisit.
