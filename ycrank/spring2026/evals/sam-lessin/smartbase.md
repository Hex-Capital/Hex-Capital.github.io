# Smartbase -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Sam Goldman and Taira Fujioka **slept at a factory** to source the wedge, and that one detail in the YC dossier is the entire reason Smartbase is not an immediate trash-can pass — it's the Wizard-of-Oz signal I institutionalized from my own **Fin** life. But the dimensional signature here is a classic vertical-AI-SaaS mismatch with my sheet: the HIGHEST rung Smartbase climbs is founder conviction via manual-process embedding, and the LOWEST is founder-as-brand (zero Twitter, zero GitHub, zero public writing surfaced in the dossier). That pairing tells me exactly what Smartbase is — missionary operators running a five-figure-ACV document-extraction tool into metal finishing shops, where the business is the asset and the founders are not, which means I have to underwrite this as a software cap table at whatever the standard YC P26 SAFE prices in, and that is a price I have rarely been able to make money at.

The HIGH rung on conviction is **Drop.io**-style operator obsession — I built a file-sharing primitive against free incumbents because I lived the problem, and Smartbase's founders sleeping at a metal-finishing plant to watch clerks rekey faxed POs into Epicor is the same shaped artifact. Where Smartbase diverges from that rung is timeline: my Drop arc was a 3-year missionary stretch before Facebook; Smartbase is a pivot off **Anytrace** (YC S25), a different AI product Sam and Taira were running just one batch ago, which means the conviction on metal finishing POs is six months old, not three years. The sleeping-at-the-factory line reads great in a YC application — I just can't tell yet whether it's missionary temperament or rapid ICP-shopping after Anytrace didn't compound.

The LOW rung on founder-as-brand failure is the canonical YC AI-agent team with no public footprint that the **Slow Creator Fund** explicitly exists *not* to fund — and Smartbase tracks that pattern exactly. Sam Goldman: no Twitter found, no GitHub surfaced, LinkedIn count not retrievable. Taira Fujioka: same. The **John Fish / Bookshelved** thesis was that owned distribution is itself the asset; Smartbase has the inverse profile — pure direct-outbound enterprise sales into shops the founders have to physically visit, which is fine as a GTM but means there is no community on the cap table, no distribution surplus to securitize later, and no founder-brand optionality that lets me story-broker this into anything other than what it literally is.

On round pricing (mechanism analysis), Smartbase's category is the most consensus-priced sleeve of P26: vertical AI agents on top of LLMs, exactly what **Joe Lonsdale podcast #113** flagged as "just more internet." The dossier discloses no round size, no valuation, no investor list — which under my absent-evidence default means I assume consensus YC SAFE pricing, and over an 18-month window Smartbase will need to clear at least two more financing tacks (seed-extension, A) against Leverage AI ($14.9M raised), Turian ($3.78M), Evolinq, and the latent threat of Epicor/NetSuite bundling PO extraction natively. At consensus entry pricing, the regatta math doesn't clear.

On capital structure (counterfactual), Smartbase would flip a tier if the metal-finishing ICP came with a non-standard instrument — say, a revenue-share against the **$81K-per-shop annual savings** claim on the smartbase.so ROI calculator, structured as a royalty on labor-cost reduction rather than equity. That would be a **Teamshares**-shaped underwrite I could actually be the only bid on. Instead Smartbase is "five-figure annual contracts" on a presumably vanilla SAFE — a software cap table indistinguishable from fifty other P26 B2B AI teams, which is the failure mode Criterion 2 is built to catch.

On category sleeve (single-anchor parallel to **Sublime Security**), Sublime worked for me because enterprise security at that moment had tier-1 funds clustered at SOC late-stage SaaS while the pre-consolidation wave at the perimeter was un-bid — I bought the un-bid edge. Smartbase has a candidate version of that argument: ITAR/CMMC/DFARS-compliant U.S. enclave for defense-supply-chain metal finishing is a regulatory wrinkle Leverage AI and Berlin-based Turian explicitly do not advertise. That's the one piece of dossier evidence that pulls Smartbase off the floor on category — but unlike Sublime's perimeter thesis, document-extraction itself is commoditized at the API layer, and the ITAR enclave is a deployment artifact, not a moat.

36-month forecast: Smartbase reaches $2-5M ARR across roughly 30-60 defense-adjacent metal-finishing and aerospace job shops, then gets acquired by a tier-2 ERP (Epicor, Infor, or Acumatica) or rolled into **Leverage AI** as the niche-vertical consolidation play, at a price that returns the YC SAFE and not much more. The mechanism is that the ITAR enclave plus 9-ERP integration set is exactly the asset an ERP incumbent buys to check a compliance box, and the founders' AWS-SageMaker-inference depth makes the technical due diligence trivial — but the document-extraction layer commoditizes before Smartbase clears the third tack of the regatta.

The strongest bear-case-against-my-pass is that Smartbase fits the **Ro** exception almost cleanly: regulatory complexity (ITAR/CMMC/DFARS across the defense supply chain) is the kind of "venture moment" where only VC capital clears the licensing schleps, the founders are doing the unsexy in-person work tier-1s dismiss as services-business, and metal finishing is precisely the "lifestyle / small market" sleeve where Slow has historically been the only bid. The case stings because Sublime started here too. What dismantles it is the pivot velocity — Anytrace to Smartbase inside one YC cycle is not the Ro-style 3-year regulatory moat-build, it's category-shopping — and the dossier surfaces zero defense-industry advisor, zero named ITAR-certified design partner, zero LOI with a Tier-1 DoD prime. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Smartbase ships a signed multi-year contract with a named DoD-prime supplier (Lockheed, RTX, or one of their Tier-1 machining vendors) at six figures ACV with a CMMC Level 2 attestation in hand — that converts the enclave from a marketing artifact into the actual moat, and at that point I want to talk about a structured deal, not the SAFE.
