# Klarify -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Klarify is a competent B2B SaaS workflow tool ? a 4-person team led by Moody Abdul shipping AI scribe + claims + supervision automation to therapists at $16?$71/month per seat, with the founder profile and distribution muscle to reach $5M ARR and the ambition ceiling of every other YC company building "AI-native [vertical] OS." Its highest dimension is founder/distribution fit; its lowest is consequence magnitude. That pairing is the analytical signature: a workmanlike operator team executing a known playbook in a market where four named competitors ? Upheal, Mentalyc, Blueprint, Heidi ? are already shipping the same wedge. This is exactly the profile I described in my Pass Example: AI copilots for a single profession, automating ~40% of the workflow, with the consequence of success being "a moderately profitable SaaS company."

On Consequence Magnitude, the rungs are OpenAI at the top (civilizational AGI ? worth an apology letter to LPs), DoorDash in the middle (transforming food access for hundreds of millions ? planetary scale was the override that justified violating my technical-novelty preference), and the generic "AI copilot for accountants" Pass Example at the bottom. Klarify sits one rung above the Pass Example and well below DoorDash: the dossier's most concrete impact metric is "31,696 hours returned to care" ? Klarify is recovering admin time for the 5,200 therapists already practicing, not creating a new tier of mental healthcare access for the people locked out of it. The mental health crisis is real and the unmet need is enormous, but Klarify is making the existing supply 15?20% more productive, not collapsing the cost of access to therapy by 10x. The consequence of full success is "therapists do less paperwork."

On Non-Linear Technical Approach ? my second-largest dimension and Klarify's lowest ? the rungs are Rocket Lab at the top (Rutherford engine, electric turbopumps, 3D-printed combustion chambers ? a genuinely new mechanical architecture), Impossible Foods in the middle (heme synthesis as a real biology lift), and at the bottom the wrapper companies I pass on every batch. Klarify sits at the bottom rung. Klara generates 20+ note formats (SOAP, DAP, BIRP, CBT, EMDR) using GPT-class transcription and reasoning ? the same primitives Upheal raised $14.35M on and Mentalyc, Blueprint, and Heidi are shipping. There is no proprietary model, no novel architecture, no data moat beyond 309,121 generated notes that any well-funded competitor will surpass within a quarter. The dossier explicitly flags commoditization risk and names a better-funded direct competitor that closed a $10M Series A led by Headline in September 2025. This is not a technology bet ? it's a distribution-and-execution bet dressed up as one.

Founder as Learning Machine is Klarify's strongest dimension and the reason I'm not at a Strong Pass. The rungs are the 19-year-old MIT dropout at Mach Industries (improbable learning rate, took on Lockheed naively), Tony Xu at DoorDash in the middle (immigrant operator velocity that overrode my technical-novelty filter), and at the bottom the resume-driven domain experts with scripted answers. Moody Abdul sits between the middle and bottom rungs: he took CircleBack to $50K ARR in 4 months ? real 0?1 muscle ? and he owns a "Future of Therapy" podcast/newsletter reaching 27,000+ therapists, which is a genuine distribution asset, not a vanity number. Alexander Bergholm's ex-Workday ML-ops background pairs cleanly with the sales/distribution side. But neither founder has clinical mental-health experience, neither has a documented improbable-ambition moment, and the team profile is "well-matched B2B SaaS founders," not "outsiders attempting what couldn't be done."

Improbable Ambition Level pulls the same direction: the high rung is Hermeus building Mach 5 passenger aircraft, the middle is Impossible Foods replacing animal agriculture, and the low rung is the generic SaaS playbook. Klarify's stated mission ? "AI-native OS for mental health practices" ? sits at the bottom rung. The dossier names four direct competitors already executing the same thesis. No expert in this field is saying "that can't be done"; they're saying "everyone is doing it." On Path to Radical Affordability, the rungs are Curai Health at the top (AI primary care at near-zero marginal cost), Guardant Health in the middle (liquid biopsy replacing expensive surgery), and at the bottom premium SaaS that captures value without collapsing cost. Klarify sits at the bottom: $16?$71/month per therapist is value-capture pricing that reduces therapist back-office cost; it does not make therapy itself 10x cheaper for the patient who can't afford $200/hour. If Klarify were building an AI therapist delivering clinical care at $10/session, this dimension would score very differently.

Forecast, 36 months out: Klarify reaches $3?8M ARR on the back of the podcast distribution channel and the Canadian counselling-association beachhead, then gets acquired by SimplePractice, Jane, or TherapyNotes for sub-$75M as the incumbent EHRs ship comparable AI modules and Upheal's better-funded full-EHR play squeezes from the other side. The mechanism is straightforward: this is a feature, not a company, in a market where the data flywheel sits inside the EHR rather than the scribe. That outcome is fine for the founders and a complete miss for the option-pricing framework I bring to angel checks ? I want to either lose my money or make 50x.

The bull case I have to take seriously is the DoorDash exception. DoorDash failed my non-linearity test ? it was operations and marketplaces, no novel science ? and I funded it because the consequence magnitude was planetary and Tony Xu was an immigrant operator with absurd learning velocity. Could Moody Abdul's owned audience of 27,000 therapists plus 5,200 active users compound into a category-defining therapist platform the way DoorDash compounded into food infrastructure? The honest answer is no: DoorDash's consequence was expanding food access to hundreds of millions of consumers, while Klarify's consequence is making existing therapists 20% more productive. Mental health access expansion would require Klarify to actually deliver care, not assist providers ? that's the Curai trajectory, not the Klarify trajectory as currently scoped. The verifiable condition that would flip me: Klarify ships a product where Klara conducts a clinically-validated therapy session unsupervised at <$20/session and a credentialing body or peer-reviewed RCT validates outcome equivalence. Until then, this is the Pass Example written in mental health.
