# Voquill -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Michael Gibson spent five years at Techcyte shipping AI pathology to Mayo Clinic and rose to VP of Engineering managing 55 engineers -- and that founder-execution signal is the highest-loading dimension in Voquill's dossier, while a single F500 pilot with 25 pathologists and zero disclosed revenue is the lowest. What kind of company is that pairing? Voquill is the pathology-specific instantiation of the **Abridge** (2023) post-Whisper scribe template, run by people who have actually integrated AI into pathology workflows at scale, but stalled at the "is this an enterprise-pilot tourism or is it just working?" stage that I explicitly flagged in TechCrunch (Nov 2025) as the line that separates real AI companies from broken ones.

On founder shipping rhythm, the rung I keep returning to is **Perplexity** (2022) -- Aravind would ping me on LinkedIn and show up a week later with the thing built (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026). Voquill's Gibson and Josiah Saunders both shipped production digital-pathology AI at Techcyte to reference labs including Mayo, and Saunders was the founding engineer at Relay (YC S22). That is independent, repeated, narrow-domain shipping -- not a McKinsey deck. Where Voquill diverges from the Perplexity rung is that the dossier shows no shipping-between-meetings artifact, no GitHub velocity on Saunders's account beyond a UI-widgets repo called "dough," and Henry Habib's profile is an Udemy course business, not a co-shipper. So two of three founders clear the shipping-history bar that gates most of my checks, which is enough -- I don't need all three to be Aravind, I need the technical builder lane to be real, and Gibson-plus-Saunders is real.

On revenue velocity, the failure mechanism I learned from passing on weak AI pitches is the one I named in Tim Ferriss Show #863: in this cycle, silence on growth is itself a negative signal because real growth is easy to disclose. Voquill's dossier shows 25 pathologists onboarded at one F500 lab with 20% per-pathologist daily time savings, "No public data found" on revenue, no second customer, no signed LOI. That is the "false signal" pattern, not the **Harvey** (2023) $3B→$5B→$8B-in-months pattern (TechCrunch, Nov 2025). One pilot in healthcare can sit for 18 months before anyone writes a real PO -- usage time-savings at a single account doesn't compound the way Decagon's logo expansion compounded.

On market re-opening catalyst, the dossier marks "medical-grade ASR plus LLM extraction crossed accuracy thresholds in 2024-2025" as [Inferred] with no analyst statement tying these to pathology specifically, which is exactly the kind of derivative catalyst that compounds weakly over 18 months -- if the catalyst is just "ambient-scribe tech improved," then DAX, Abridge, and DeepScribe (each raised >$100M per the dossier) can walk into pathology any quarter they choose, and Voquill's window narrows rather than widens. A catalyst that strengthens over time looks like Ukraine for **Anduril** (2022); a catalyst that weakens looks like "the underlying model got better and now bigger scribes can enter." Voquill is the second kind.

For Voquill's index position, the counterfactual is what it would take to flip this dimension up a tier. Today the dossier says Voquill "sits on top of existing LIS/IMS without migration" while PathAI ($255M raised), Paige ($241M raised), and Proscia ($34M raised) own the slide/diagnostic-AI surface. That is an integration layer, not an index position -- Voquill is not the rail every pathology AI entrant must touch the way **Stripe** (2010) became the rail every e-commerce entrant touched. To flip to index-tier, Voquill would need the CPT-coding-optimization data loop to become the reimbursement-truth layer that the diagnostic platforms route through -- possible, not demonstrated, and the dossier names no API partnership with PathAI or Proscia that would suggest the rails posture.

On the single-miracle test (NFX, *Unusual Signs*), Voquill compares structurally to **Color Genomics** (2015) -- workflow infrastructure on top of a hard layer, in my own portfolio. But Color isolated the miracle to commodity-sequencing-plus-consumer-GTM, where I was operating. Voquill stacks two independent miracles: medical-grade ASR accurate enough for pathology sign-out (no WER benchmark in the dossier) and CPT-code suggestion accurate enough not to trigger payer audits on the "$8B+ left on the table" pitch. Either miracle alone is plausible; compounded, the probability drops, and the dossier offers no evidence Voquill has solved either to a defensible threshold.

**36-month forecast**: Voquill reaches $3-8M ARR across 5-15 mid-size pathology labs and gets tuck-in acquired by Proscia or PathAI for $40-120M, or pivots into broader pathology workflow software once Abridge/DAX extend ambient-scribe into the niche. The mechanism: founder-market fit is real enough to land paying labs, but the catalyst is derivative and the index position is integration-layer, so the natural exit is bundling into a platform that already owns the slide/diagnostic surface -- not category-defining.

The strongest bull case stings: **Abridge** (2023) is exactly this template in clinic-visit scribing and is reportedly past $5B -- I funded it, and at the equivalent stage Abridge also had one health-system pilot and no second logo. Domain-deep founders applying post-Whisper ASR to a narrow medical workflow is precisely the *AI Market Clarity* "open market" sub-category (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) where I've written checks. The counter is that Abridge attacked ~1M US clinicians while Voquill attacks ~12K US pathologists, and that the CPT-optimization wedge -- the part of Voquill's pitch that does the financial heavy lifting -- is the part most exposed to payer audit and human-in-loop dilution, which Abridge's ambient-clinic value prop never depended on. **Flip condition**: a second named pathology lab signing a paid contract within 90 days plus a published WER benchmark on pathology terminology would move Voquill from neutral to invest -- without one of those two, I am at the edge of my "wait for the curve" discipline.
