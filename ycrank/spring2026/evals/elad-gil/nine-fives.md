# Nine Fives -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Noah Levy spent 2016–2023 as the responsible engineer for the Falcon 9 Radar Altimeter, the Dragon 2 S-Band Transceiver, and Starship GPS/S-Band radios -- and Nine Fives is selling driverless RF test instruments into a $3.99B market growing 8.74% CAGR with no named 24-month catalyst. That pairing is the entire diagnosis: Nine Fives is a credentialed-hardware-founder bet against a steady-state industrial category, the exact inverse of my Stripe/Coinbase/Anduril template where the founder might be unknown but the *market* had visibly re-opened. The SpaceX shipping rhythm is real; the catalyst is not.

Anduril (2022) is the ladder rung Nine Fives is reaching for on market re-opening, and the mechanism that made Anduril work was specific: Ukraine, in a 24-month window, exposed Pentagon software inadequacy in a way that re-priced an entire procurement category. Nine Fives gestures at a similar story -- PoE plus REST plus LLM-generated test code "viable in the last 12–24 months" (dossier, [Inferred]) -- but the dossier flags that as inference, and Precedence Research pegs the underlying RF test equipment market at a placid 8.74% CAGR with top-five concentration of ~55%. That's not a re-opening; that's an oligopoly humming along. The "January 1997 USB HID specification" line in Nine Fives' YC Launch is good copy, but Keysight and Mini-Circuits aren't dying of legacy -- they're shipping. I need a named catalyst (a regulatory shift, an incumbent failure mode, a step-function in customer behavior), and Nine Fives' dossier doesn't name one.

Harvey (2023) is the rung on revenue velocity -- "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" -- and the discipline I've earned from that deal is the inverse one: when the curve is real, disclose it; when it's silent, the silence is itself information. Nine Fives launched on YC ~7 days before research date with 4 SKUs (PD8X, POE-ATTEN-6G, POE-SWITCH-6G, POE-PMETER-6G), max storefront price filter at $1,500, no public revenue, no named customer logos, no Product Hunt launch. For a hardware pre-seed at day 7 that's not damning -- but it means I have zero "just working" signal. The bar is not "show me ARR" at this stage; the bar is "show me one named cellphone, drone, or satellite OEM cycling units repeatedly." Nine Fives' dossier shows none.

Counterfactual on founder shipping: if Noah Levy were a Stanford CS grad with a Notion deck and no shipped RF hardware, Nine Fives would be a hard pass on dimension three. Instead Noah was the responsible engineer for production radios that fly on Falcon 9 and Starship, with 6× SpaceX "kickass award" and a co-founder, Andrew Kurtz, who ran Falcon 9 First Stage test automation and Fairing 2.0 integration before Aurora Flight Sciences. The four SKUs already publicly listed are themselves shipped artifacts -- this is not a promoter pitching a roadmap. Nine Fives clears the founder-shipping bar that disqualifies most of the deals I see; the question is whether Nine Fives ships *between meetings* on the software/API side (NineVue, skill.md) the way Aravind Srinivas did at Perplexity, which the dossier can't tell me yet.

Mechanism on index position: over 18 months, a hardware-sales company in RF test compounds linearly with unit volume and gets re-rated as a "specialty instruments" multiple -- it does *not* compound as an index on cellphone, drone, or satellite manufacturing the way Stripe compounds on every new internet business. Nine Fives' compatibility with NI TestStand and Keysight Connection Expert is a drop-in replacement positioning, not a rails position. The NineVue rack-config app plus the LLM skill.md layer is the only path to an index-like outcome -- if every RF lab's test rack ends up version-controlled against Nine Fives' API the way every payments integration ended up against Stripe's, the math changes. The dossier shows the surface area but no customer adoption of it.

Decagon (2023) is the single-anchor comparable on the miracle test, because Decagon assumed one validated capability (LLM accuracy at customer support tasks) and made everything else GTM and product execution -- and I funded it. Nine Fives' implicit miracle is similar in shape: assume incumbents (Keysight, R&S, NI/Emerson) cannot or will not ship a credible driverless/REST replacement inside 24 months, and the rest is hardware execution plus enterprise sales. That's actually a clean single-miracle structure, closer to my Color Genomics "workflow infrastructure on top of a hard thing" template than to the multi-miracle science projects I avoid. The dossier even notes the disruption-from-below dynamic explicitly: incumbents have software/services revenue and channel partners they'd cannibalize. The miracle is GTM-shaped, not physics-shaped.

Forecast (36 months): Nine Fives reaches $3–8M in annualized hardware revenue selling to a handful of cellphone, drone, and small-sat test labs, then gets tuck-in acquired by Keysight, NI/Emerson, or Rohde & Schwarz in the $40–120M range -- a respectable outcome for the founders and a non-event for an angel check sized for power-law math. The mechanism: the 8.74% CAGR market doesn't generate a breakout window, DS Instruments and Mini-Circuits keep the bottom of the market commoditized, and the incumbents -- once they see Nine Fives' API/skill.md layer working -- ship a credible REST-and-driverless story themselves, at which point Nine Fives' rational exit is to *be* that layer for an incumbent. This is the "operationally excellent specialty hardware company" outcome, not the index-on-a-category outcome.

Bull case worth steel-manning: Nine Fives is Anduril-shaped at the seed -- unsexy industrial category, hardware-with-software-layer, ex-SpaceX founders who shipped at the most demanding hardware org on Earth, and a software lock-in surface (NineVue + skill.md) that could become the GitHub-for-test-racks if RF testing genuinely modernizes. My Color Genomics override was precisely this pattern: workflow infrastructure on top of a hard-tech layer beats betting on the hard-tech layer. The bear answer is that Anduril had Ukraine and Color had a sequencing-cost curve; Nine Fives has 8.74% CAGR and an [Inferred] catalyst. The one verifiable condition that flips my verdict: within 6 months, Nine Fives discloses month-over-month unit-volume growth >20% with at least two named tier-1 customers (e.g., Skydio, Anduril, a named cellphone OEM RF lab) repeat-purchasing across multiple SKUs. Absent that, this is a pass at consensus seed terms and a maybe at a friend-price.
