# Drafted -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Drafted is a deep-domain repeat-founder bet wrapped around a single-sided generative consumer tool — Nick Donahue spent five years and $20M of Khosla/Sam Altman capital at Atmos designing $200M of custom homes before shutting it down in March 2025, and he is back at the same problem with a code-leverage answer instead of a labor-leverage one. The signature is high founder-product-market fit colliding with low user-value compounding, which is exactly the shape of a company my framework calls "interesting but not 10,000x."

Stack Overflow is the ladder rung I want Drafted to climb toward on leverage architecture — Jeff Atwood and Joel Spolsky shipped a free product that distributed through the open web at near-zero marginal cost and compounded its own corpus into a permissionless distribution moat. Drafted has the first half of that pattern: 120k users in a single month, 325k+ plans generated, the homepage offering "Free AI House Plans with PDF & CAD Downloads," and the @DraftedAI account growing to 9,399 followers from a November 2025 cold start without paid acquisition. Where Drafted diverges from the Stack Overflow rung is the artifact itself — a house plan is a private deliverable a user takes away, not a public document the next user searches; the leverage is permissionless on the input side but bounded on the output side, which is why I price this in the high-teens-to-low-twenties of the dimension rather than at the Twitter/Stack Overflow ceiling.

Perplexity is the rung I keep returning to on the network-compounding axis, and Perplexity is in my book as a Known Exception precisely because it is single-sided and I let it pass anyway when criteria #1 and #3 cleared emphatically. Drafted shows the same failure mechanism — the Nth Drafted user generating their dream home in Austin does not make the (N-1)th user in Denver's plan better, and the 85,000-plan gallery is a marketing artifact rather than a compounding graph the way Stack Overflow's Q&A index was. The Perplexity exception worked because Aravind's specific-knowledge gate was extreme and the AI-cycle timing was pre-consensus; Drafted has the founder gate but not the timing — Maket.ai has been shipping the comparable product since 2019, so the "right first" premium I paid on Perplexity is not available here.

Donahue's specific knowledge is the dimension that almost saves the check on its own — this is a founder who grew up in a homebuilding family, made Forbes 30 Under 30, raised $20M from Khosla and Sam Altman at Atmos, employed 40 people, hit $7M of revenue, and watched the Fed rate cycle destroy his backlog when "clients who'd spent months designing their dream homes couldn't afford them anymore." The 18-month mechanism here is unusually clean: Donahue already knows which parts of custom residential design are operationally radioactive, four of his early Drafted employees came over from Atmos meaning he is not re-paying the team-formation tax, and the trained model on real permitted plans at 0.2¢/generation versus 13¢ for general-purpose AI is the artifact of someone who has sat across from permit-office bureaucracy long enough to know what "structured CAD files... grouped and sized to common standards" actually has to mean. This is not an ex-FAANG PM identifying a TAM — this is scar-tissue specific knowledge, and it pins Drafted's dim-3 placement near the Stack Overflow rung.

Drafted's market would need TAM evidence I cannot find in the dossier to flip a tier on dim 4 — the counterfactual is straightforward: if the dossier showed Drafted exporting to homebuilders (the Lennar/D.R. Horton segment), to real-estate listings (Cubi.casa's adjacency), or to interior renovation (a category 10x the size of custom new-build), I would size this up. Today the dossier shows free PDF downloads to consumers in a category that Atmos itself proved is macro-rate-sensitive, and the same Federal Reserve dynamic that killed Atmos is still live in 2026. The pivot room exists structurally — residential design touches construction, permitting, real estate, and interiors — but the dossier names no current movement toward any of them, so I default below midpoint.

Notion is my single-anchor parallel on the founder trio — Ivan Zhao spent years rebuilding personal productivity tools, survived two ground-up product rewrites, and treated his prior teams cleanly enough that capital came back for the next round. Donahue is rhyming with that pattern in the most load-bearing way an angel can read: Patrick Collison, Ben Silbermann, Jack Altman, Alex Blania, and Kevin Mahaffey are not tourist names, and they are repeat-backing a founder whose prior company wound down rather than exited. That is the integrity leg of the Buffett trio in its quietest form — capital that watched the closing and came back is a stronger signal than any public testimonial.

Drafted's 36-month forecast: I expect Drafted to plateau at $3–8M of consumer ARR by late 2027 and then pivot to a B2B sale into production homebuilders or real-estate listings, with an acqui-hire by Autodesk or Zillow as the modal exit; the mechanism is that Donahue already knows from Atmos that consumer custom-home demand is the wrong substrate to compound on, and the trained-model + CAD-export asset is more valuable to a Lennar-shaped buyer than to a 1,000-DAU consumer funnel.

The bull case stings: the dataset of 325k generated plans per month at 0.2¢/inference is compounding into a proprietary corpus no general-purpose model will replicate without similar permit-data access, Donahue's $200M-of-homes operator rolodex is exactly the distribution wedge into builders that Snaptrude has never had, and the funding-figure discrepancy ($1.65M at $35M post in TechCrunch versus $17.5M cumulative in the YC description) suggests follow-on capital is already underwriting a B2B pivot the press has not caught. My counter: the dossier's free-PDF homepage is the tell — a founder pivoting toward builders does not lead with "Free AI House Plans with PDF & CAD Downloads," he leads with a builder logo. Drafted today is still selling the Atmos dream in a thinner wrapper, and the network-compounding gap means the upside math caps below the 10,000x ceiling my portfolio needs. I would flip to Invest on one verifiable condition: a signed pilot with a top-20 US homebuilder or a paid integration with an MLS-tier real-estate platform within the next two quarters.
