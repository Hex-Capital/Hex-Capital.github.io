# HeyClicky -- Elad Gil Evaluation

HeyClicky is a founder-brand consumer overlay where the strongest signal — Farza Majeed's demonstrable shipping rhythm and 89.3K-follower distribution — is attached to the weakest possible structural position: a free, open-source Mac menu-bar app sitting directly in the feature path of OpenAI's "Work with Apps" and Anthropic's "computer use." That high/low pairing is the whole story. Farza is exactly the kind of founder who pings me on LinkedIn and shows up next week with the thing built; HeyClicky is exactly the kind of company that ships a great demo and then gets compressed between an incumbent's OS-native feature and its own forked Windows clone (emreyilmaz46/clicky_windows).

On founder execution rhythm, where HeyClicky scores highest, the HIGH rung is **Perplexity** (2022) — Aravind pinged me cold and built between conversations — and the LOW rung is the pedigreed promoter who lists "ex-FAANG" and ships nothing. HeyClicky sits closer to the HIGH rung than the MID rung (**Brex**, 2017, operationally disciplined but not viral): Farza ran buildspace from 2019-2024, raised $10M from a16z, and despite the August 2024 wind-down he's now publicly shipping farzaa/clicky to ~5.8k GitHub stars, a ~2.9M-view launch demo, and a 4.9 Product Hunt rating across 23 reviews. The buildspace shutdown is a real scar — execution at zero-headcount on consumer infra is a single-point-of-failure — but the public artifact trail is unambiguous. This is a builder, not a deck-jockey.

On index-like distribution, where HeyClicky scores lowest, the HIGH rung is **Stripe** (2010), "an index on e-commerce" — every entrant in the category had to touch the rails — and the LOW rung is **Lyft** Series C, my regret-pass on a #2 in a winner-take-all network market. HeyClicky sits closer to Lyft than to **Decagon** (2023) in the middle: Decagon at least sits in the workflow path of every post-LLM CS deployment, whereas HeyClicky must *beat* OpenAI ChatGPT desktop's "Work with Apps" and Anthropic's Claude "computer use" on the very same Mac (dottie.ai, 2026), with the same Claude SSE + AssemblyAI + ElevenLabs stack its own README documents. There is no rail here — there is a character overlay on top of someone else's rail, and the character itself is forkable (jasonkneen/openclicky already exists).

On market re-opening, the HIGH rung is **Anduril** (2022) — Ukraine, a named 24-month Pentagon-software catalyst — and the LOW rung is any "AI for X" pitch where the only catalyst is "the LLM got cheaper." HeyClicky sits one notch above LOW: the "why now" is real but inferred (streaming Claude SSE, ElevenLabs latency, Cluely's June 2025 viral validation per TechCrunch), and none of those is a market re-opening of a *dead* market — they are infrastructure improvements inside a market that consensus already discovered. This is not Stripe-after-PayPal; this is Cluely-after-Cluely.

On revenue velocity, the HIGH rung is **Harvey** (2023) — "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" — and the LOW rung is any AI company "silent on growth," which I treat as "something's fundamentally broken." HeyClicky has no pricing page, no ARR, no enterprise logos, no MRR — just 5,034 @heyclicky X followers, a Tally waitlist for Windows, and a free OSS binary. The 2.9M-view demo is a usage signal but, in my own *false signal vs. just working* taxonomy (TechCrunch, Nov 2025), this is unmonetized attention, not buying behavior. On single-miracle architecture HeyClicky is actually clean — LLM accuracy is validated, the rest is GTM — but the miracle is already so validated that the README itself enables clones, so the score is the default-ish range rather than the **Decagon**/**Abridge** workflow-infrastructure HIGH rung.

My 36-month forecast: HeyClicky stays a beloved indie OSS product, never crosses $2M ARR on its own, and either gets acquihired by a creator-tools company (Figma, Notion, ElevenLabs) for the Farza brand and the audience, or Farza pivots HeyClicky into a paid education-vertical SaaS where his buildspace-alumni audience converts — the OS-native overlay layer itself gets eaten by OpenAI and Anthropic shipping the same UX one Mac update later. The mechanism is the index-position failure compounding with the lack of switching costs the dossier itself flags ("None identifiable today").

The strongest bull case against my pass is the **Granola** template — TechCrunch March 2026, $125M Series C at $1.5B, started as a "just a notetaker" overlay that incumbents could obviously replicate, scaled anyway on brand and UX taste. If you believe Farza's 89.3K-follower distribution + character-driven UX is a true consumer brand moat that OpenAI structurally won't replicate because it cannibalizes their general-purpose chat positioning, HeyClicky rhymes with Granola more than with Lyft. That objection stings. What dismantles it: Granola's wedge was post-meeting *artifacts* — durable user data the user came back for — and HeyClicky has no analogous data layer; the dossier explicitly notes "switching costs / data: None identifiable today." The condition that flips my verdict is concrete: HeyClicky discloses >20% MoM paid revenue growth on a published pricing tier with named consumer or education-segment retention curves within 6 months — the **Harvey** "just working" signature, not the Cluely "viral demo" signature.
