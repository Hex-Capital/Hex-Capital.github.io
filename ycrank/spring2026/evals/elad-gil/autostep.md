# Autostep -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Autostep is a solo-founder desktop-observation app in Spring 2026 YC selling task discovery into the same enterprise buyer that UiPath ($1.5B+ FY2025 revenue), Celonis (~$13B last mark), Skan ($54M raised), and Mimica ($32.2M raised) already cover with bundled task-mining modules — which makes its analytical signature a founder-ships-iteratively story bolted onto an index-position and single-miracle profile that I cannot make pencil. Aidan Pratt has shipped six named side projects (openapi-autospec, Hitsnag, Pinpoint.city, TweetWidget, Ramble, Pitstop.to) plus six years at Keel/Opto/Neo-adjacent automation startups, which is the highest-conviction dimension here; the revenue curve and the distribution position are the lowest, and that pairing is the entire deal.

Perplexity (2022) is the load-bearing analogy for the founder-shipping axis — Aravind pinged me on LinkedIn and showed up a week later with the thing we'd discussed built, which is the only reason I leaned into a cold-inbound at consensus prices. Autostep's Pratt clears a meaningful share of that bar on artifacts alone: a GitHub trail of independently-shipped products, six years building automation tooling at seed-Series A, and a working desktop app already SOC 2 and HIPAA-claimed at team size one. Where Autostep diverges from Perplexity is the *between-meetings* tempo — the dossier shows shipped *prior* artifacts, not the iterating-in-real-time rhythm I actually filter on. I can give Pratt credit for the shipping history without believing the Perplexity-grade execution loop has been demonstrated.

Lyft Series C is the failure mechanism I keep returning to on the index-position axis — a #2 in a market where the #1 captures structurally better economics, which is exactly the math I refused to pretend was contrarian (20VC interview). Autostep's positioning is worse than Lyft's because Lyft at least had a real consumer brand; Autostep is selling "observe + recommend agent" into the same Fortune 500 IT buyer that UiPath already invoices, against a UltimateSuite product ServiceNow already absorbed in 2024. The dossier names no distribution wedge — no SI partnership, no vertical concentration, no pricing-disruption angle — which means Autostep has to beat task-mining incumbents on features, the exact configuration I called out in *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025) as the wrong side of a winner-already-declared category.

The revenue-velocity dimension compounds badly over the next eighteen months for Autostep. "If you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026) — and the only number in Autostep's dossier is a marketing claim that "the smallest team we've worked with uncovered $100K+ in operational waste with 10 people," with customer count undisclosed, no pricing page, demo-only Calendly, and 0 open jobs on the YC page. In an AI cycle where Harvey ran $3B→$5B→$8B in months precisely because the curve was undeniable, Autostep's silence on growth is itself the signal. Eighteen months of solo-founder enterprise sales with a six-week SOC 2 review per logo does not produce a Harvey curve; it produces a ten-customer wedge that incumbents undercut.

Counterfactual on the catalyst dimension: for Autostep's market-re-opening score to flip a tier, the dossier would need to show that task-mining-plus-agent-generation is a *post-2024* category opened by a specific step-function — say, a Pentagon-equivalent buyer mandate, a regulatory shift on AI deployment governance, or an incumbent failure mode that nobody is currently filling. What the dossier actually shows is Pratt's founder thesis that "the bottleneck isn't tooling anymore, it's discovery," which is a real observation but describes a *live* category that UiPath, Celonis, Skan, and Mimica are already iterating on — not a re-opened market like defense pre-Anduril (2022) or payments pre-Stripe (2010). The catalyst is real; the window is shared.

Single-miracle test on Autostep, the way I ran it on Color Genomics (2015): Color isolated the miracle to *workflow on commodity sequencing*, not the chemistry. Autostep does the opposite — it stacks at least three independent miracles. Miracle one: desktop observation at org-wide scale without works-council revolt and without keylogger-grade privacy blowback. Miracle two: LLM-generated agents reliable enough across arbitrary enterprise workflows that the auto-recommendation isn't a toy. Miracle three: solo-founder enterprise procurement against UiPath's incumbent IT relationships. Compound those probabilities and the math does what NFX's *Unusual Signs* piece said it would.

Thirty-six-month forecast for Autostep: quiet acqui-hire into a process-mining incumbent (Celonis, UiPath, or ServiceNow extending UltimateSuite) for low-eight-figures, or dead with Pratt onto his next thing — sub-$2M ARR, no Series A from a top-tier fund, no enterprise-logo announcements above mid-market. The mechanism is the signature: real founder shipping ability colliding with a non-index position in a live-incumbent category produces a competent product that gets out-distributed before the curve compounds.

Strongest bear-case-against-my-pass — Decagon (2023) was a workflow-infrastructure-on-a-recently-catalyzed-category play I funded against louder customer-support incumbents, and Autostep claims the same shape: agent-era operational intelligence on a post-LLM catalyst with a shipping solo founder. It stings because the structural analogy *is* there. What dismantles it is the index test — Decagon was building into a sub-category (*AI Market Clarity*: still-open) where no incumbent had bundled the agent layer; Autostep is building into task mining where UiPath, Skan, Mimica, and ServiceNow-via-UltimateSuite have already shipped or acquired the discovery layer and will extend into agent generation as a defensive feature, not a category. The single condition that would flip my verdict: Autostep names three Fortune-500 customers paying six-figure ACVs by Q4 2026, with month-over-month seat expansion — that's the Harvey-pattern "just working" disclosure, and absent it I am applying my AI-without-explosive-growth heuristic and passing.
