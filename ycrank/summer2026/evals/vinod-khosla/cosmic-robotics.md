# Cosmic Robotics -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Cosmic Robotics is a consequence-adjacent hard-tech bet let down by an expert-continuity founding team -- Emerick out of Built Robotics with a four-generation construction family, Jones out of Relativity/SpaceX/JPL -- executing the next obvious step in solar installation rather than defying anyone's orthodoxy. That pairing is Cosmic Robotics's signature: a real physical-world problem attached to a labor pool of 178,713 US solar installers (IREC 2025 Census), but a founder profile that reads like the McKinsey 1986 cellphone forecast -- extrapolating the past into the future.

**Impossible Foods** (2011) is the ladder rung I want Cosmic Robotics to reach on consequence. Pat Brown said animal agriculture disappears; the success-state was a named incumbent regime collapsing. Cosmic Robotics gestures at that shape -- utility solar at 41 GWdc 2025 buildout (SEIA Q4 2025), EPC margins up ~40%, labor cost up 15% annually -- but the success-state is "install panels 2x faster," not "the utility-scale EPC labor model disappears." One panel every 30-40 seconds (Electrek, Mar 2026) is a productivity multiplier, not a category collapse. The lunar construction line on the YC page is where consequence could escalate -- but the dossier concedes no NASA award ID surfaces in CLPS, LSII, or SBIR public listings, which is where **ICON**'s $57.2M award is verifiable. Cosmic Robotics diverges from the Impossible Foods rung precisely there: the civilizational framing is asserted but not underwritten by a falsifiable incumbent-death claim.

**Glean** is the ladder rung Cosmic Robotics sits closest to on founder-defies-expert-consensus, and that is not a compliment in my framework -- I made Glean a Known Exception, not a template. Arvind Jain was ex-Google search building enterprise search; Emerick is ex-Built Robotics building construction robotics; Jones is ex-SpaceX building aerospace-grade hardware. The pattern the TED Talk warns against -- experts extrapolating the past -- is exactly this shape. Nothing in the dossier shows either founder publicly contradicting field orthodoxy, taking a "too important not to do" position, or pivoting from an adjacent expertise into something dismissed. Glean earned the override on capital scale and Fortune-500 consequence; Cosmic Robotics has neither the $100M+ check size nor the labor-displacement scale to trigger the same exception.

Consequence-adjacent hard tech -- mechanism analysis on the technical/capital risk axis: over 18 months Cosmic Robotics either closes a Series A north of $30M and starts absorbing fleet-manufacturing capex the way **QuantumScape** absorbed pilot-line capex, or it stays capital-starved against **Terabase Energy**'s $130M war chest and gets outrun on deployment volume. The $4M pre-seed (TechCrunch, Apr 16 2025) with Giant Ventures, HCVC, MaC and Azeem Azhar is a validation ticket, not a scale ticket. Terabase's Terafab V2 field-factory just cleared field testing (pv magazine, Apr 2026) -- Cosmic Robotics is 30x behind on capital in a market where hardware iteration cycles are measured in field deployments per quarter. This axis is a real hardware risk that could repel consensus VCs, but the risk is being run at pre-seed scale, which is the wrong scale for the game.

Counterfactual on learning rate: for Cosmic Robotics to jump a tier here, Emerick would need a documented "we were wrong" moment -- a public pivot away from initial thesis, or a prior sold company, or a written thesis update on why mobile field robots beat tent-based prefab after the team looked at Terabase's approach. None of that is in the dossier. Absent evidence, I default to 7/15 -- not a penalty for being early, a recognition that Cosmic Robotics has not yet given me the **Slash**-style pivot evidence that let me write that $100M Series C in 2024.

Single-anchor parallel on capital profile: **Genesis AI** co-led at $105M seed in July 2025 tells you what a Khosla-shaped check into full-stack physical AI looks like. Cosmic Robotics at $4M pre-seed is priced for a different investor -- Giant Ventures and HCVC are the right room for this round. The DOE American-Made Solar Prize and JLL Foundation non-dilutive grants (Samson Rose, 2025) are exactly the capital-lite validation that suggests Cosmic Robotics may not need Khosla scale until a Series B, which is precisely when the Terabase capital gap forces the question.

36-month forecast: Cosmic Robotics closes a $25-40M Series A in 2026-2027 led by a strategic (Blattner, an EPC, or a solar-adjacent industrial), quietly deprioritizes the lunar construction narrative when the NASA contract fails to materialize as a public award, and gets acquired by 2029 by either **Terabase Energy** or a large EPC at a $150-300M outcome -- respectable but sub-fund-returning at Khosla scale. The mechanism is capital gravity: SunRobi as sole certified operator (Solar Power World, Feb 2026) is the wedge, not the moat, and hardware-only defensibility is unlikely to survive Terabase's field-factory economics at scale.

The bull case that could sting: Cosmic Robotics is the literal embodiment of my Fortune 2026-03-06 "$15 trillion of GDP is labor" thesis applied to the 178,713 US solar installer workforce, and Emerick's Built Robotics lineage is the physical-AI-operator analog of the Sergey Levine research-PI pattern I paid for at Physical Intelligence (2024). The bear rebuttal wins on two dossier facts: Terabase's $130M raise is 30x Cosmic Robotics's capital, and the Sarcos/Blattner and AES Maximo (100 MW deployed) competitors mean hardware capability is not scarce in this category. The one condition that flips me to invest: a Series A above $50M led by a Khosla-adjacent hard-tech investor with a verifiable NASA lunar surface construction award ID and a signed multi-year framework agreement with a top-5 US EPC covering more than 5 GWdc of committed pipeline. Absent those, Cosmic Robotics is a company I would be proud to fund at a Tier-1 generalist and pass on at Khosla.
