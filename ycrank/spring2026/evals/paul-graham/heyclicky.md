# HeyClicky -- Paul Graham Evaluation

HeyClicky is what you get when a real hacker with a 89.3K-follower distribution channel ships a viral artifact in a commodity zone -- Farza Majeed turned a screenshot-plus-Claude-plus-ElevenLabs overlay into 2.9M views and 5.8k GitHub stars while sitting alone in San Francisco with a "Team Size: 0" YC page. The signature is animal resourcefulness on top, solo configuration plus anti-schlep on the bottom. That pairing -- viral-shipper, no co-founder, pure-software architecture publicly documented in the README -- is the analytical fingerprint here, and it's the same fingerprint that produced **buildspace** (2019), which raised $10M from a16z in November 2022 and then shut down in August 2024.

HeyClicky's strongest dimension is animal resourcefulness, and the ladder is clear. The HIGH rung is **Airbnb** (2009) -- the cereal boxes at $40 a box, 1,000 sold, financed the company because no one else would. The LOW rung is the MBA-deck team from my standard pass example -- no shipped artifact, glossy slides. The MID rung is **Dropbox** (2007) -- Houston's Hacker News demo video, technical proof shipped before the seed check. HeyClicky sits between Dropbox and Airbnb: Farza shipped a working macOS menu-bar app, drove ~2.9M views on the launch demo, then open-sourced farzaa/clicky in April 2026 (~5.8k stars), and earned an XDA Developers feature calling it "the most useful thing I've tried in months." That is animal behavior. Open-sourcing the thing that's supposed to be your moat is also the stunt move -- it's the cereal-box pattern's cousin, generating top-of-funnel by giving away what other founders would hoard.

HeyClicky's weakest dimension is schlep -- this is anti-schlep. The HIGH rung is **Coinbase** (2012), the bitcoin compliance grind nobody else in the batch wanted. The MID rung is **DoorDash** (2013), last-mile delivery ops where the software-only YC applicants flinched. The LOW rung is the AI-wrapper pass case, a polished consumer overlay with no offline grind. HeyClicky sits at the LOW rung adjacent to a Pluely or Cluely fork: the architecture (screenshot + transcript → LLM → TTS) is publicly documented in the README, Windows clones already exist (emreyilmaz46/clicky_windows, jasonkneen/openclicky), and OpenAI's "Work with Apps" plus Anthropic's "computer use" ship screen-aware Mac functionality natively. There is no compliance grind, no manual ops, no recruiting overhead Farza personally has to absorb. A schlep is something OpenAI won't ship next quarter; HeyClicky is something OpenAI *did* ship.

HeyClicky's organic-idea score sits between **Stripe** (2010) -- Collisons hacking commerce flows since Auctomatic -- and the MBA pass-example team that "spotted a market opportunity." Farza ran buildspace, an education community for builders, and explicitly frames HeyClicky as "the future of education." That's adjacent founder-pain, not the Drew-Houston-lost-his-USB-stick variety. Farza didn't tell us he was personally stuck inside DaVinci Resolve tutorials; he's building a tutor because he ran an education company. Closer to mid-rung than top.

HeyClicky's hacker genealogy is real. **Stripe** (2010) sits at the HIGH rung (Patrick's 41st Young Scientist Lisp prize); a credentialed CS-degree-only founder sits at the LOW rung. HeyClicky lands at mid-to-high: Farza started a company at 13, worked on self-driving cars in academia, and farzaa/clicky has ~5.8k stars on a repo he wrote. He leaves GitHub trails. On co-founder configuration -- the ladder runs from **Airbnb** (2009)'s Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk trio at the top to the explicit solo-founder red flag at the bottom -- HeyClicky is the LOW rung. Solo. The YC page lists zero teammates. That's not a default-neutral; that's the configuration my October 2010 essay explicitly avoided.

My 36-month forecast: HeyClicky reaches $0-$2M ARR, gets acquihired by a larger consumer-AI or education platform for under $30M, or dies as a free OSS tool while Farza pivots into his third consumer-AI experiment. The mechanism is the bottom of HeyClicky's signature: commodity architecture + solo execution + free pricing means the company is downstream of whatever OpenAI ships in its next desktop update, and viral distribution alone -- even Farza's 89.3K X followers and 2.9M-view launch -- doesn't compound into defensibility when the README itself is the build guide.

The strongest case against my pass is the **Kiko** (2005) → **Twitch** (2007) re-fund pattern. Farza already did YC with buildspace and it wound down -- exactly the profile where I have historically leaned in hardest, because prior failure compounds hustle rather than disqualifying. Buildspace also raised $10M from a16z at $100M, which is a real institutional signal that Farza can convene capital and audience. If you squint, this is the Kan/Shear refund profile. The reason I don't squint: Kan and Shear came back as a *pair* and then added Vogt -- HeyClicky is solo, and my explicit avoidance from "What We Look for in Founders" (October 2010) is the solo-founder configuration. It also isn't the **Loopt** (2005) Altman exception, because Altman's override was force-of-personality on a product fighting timing; HeyClicky has no timing problem (the demo works today) and no Altman -- it has a real-but-not-singular hacker-influencer with a shut-down prior company in the same vertical. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a technical co-founder with prior shipping history joins HeyClicky and Farza converts even 1% of the 2.9M launch viewers into paying subscribers by month 12. Until then, this is a Wufoo-without-the-Wufoo-margins bet, and Wufoo's margins were the only reason that bet worked.
