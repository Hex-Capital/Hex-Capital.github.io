# Pango -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Pango is a schlep-shaped operations bundle built by competent European operators who do not read like hackers in the **Viaweb** (1995) sense -- highest on the schlep test, lowest on hacker genealogy, and that pairing is the whole story. Steve Rahimi and Lukasz Reszczynski are running at 100+ carrier integrations, EU AI Act compliance, and money-moving returns workflows from a 5-person Stockholm team, which is exactly the kind of work other S26 applicants are routing around. But neither founder has a GitHub trail, a Lisp prize, or any of the tribal markers I over-index on, and that asymmetry is what Pango actually is.

**Coinbase** (2012) is the right HIGH-rung comparison on the schlep dimension because Brian Armstrong's appetite for the bitcoin compliance grind -- the unpleasant regulatory work nobody else in the batch wanted -- *was* the moat, not a tax on the moat. Pango's "100+ carrier integrations" claim plus ISO 27001 in progress plus refund-decision agents on a 5-person team is the same shape of work: carrier-API drift is a known killer (Parcel Perform's entire value prop, per clickpost.ai, is event-normalization across 1,100+ carriers), and Pango is volunteering for it. Where Pango diverges from the **Coinbase** (2012) rung is that Armstrong personally shipped a working Android wallet to overturn a rejection; Pango's dossier names the schlep but does not name the equivalent personal grind artifact -- the Vinnova grant and TINC Fall 2025 placement are institutional validations, not the kind of unscalable thing Armstrong did alone in a room.

The pass-example MBA-deck team I described in my own essay -- two MBAs, glossy deck, no GitHub -- is the LOW rung on hacker genealogy, and the failure mechanism is that hackers leave tracks and the absence of tracks is itself a signal. Pango is not that team -- Reszczynski's Toptal resume with patents and a prior SaaS that quoted "$1.5 billion in products" is real shipping capability, and Rahimi at Shopstory means at least one founder has touched Swedish e-commerce tooling. But the dossier explicitly returns "No public account found" for Rahimi's GitHub and "No public handle found" for Reszczynski's, and neither has the open-source or competitive-programming footprint that pre-dated **Stripe** (2010) or **Reddit** (2005). The Toptal patent line is the strongest hacker-adjacent signal Pango produces, and it is a credential, not a hack.

On animal resourcefulness, the mechanism compounds this way: Pango shipped 6 named brand customers (Opus Mushrooms, Sundaze, Kallpressen, BAMBAM, Brighteq, Understatement Underwear) inside two months of launching, won a Vinnova grant, got into TINC Fall 2025 for U.S. scaling, and got Steve Rahimi profiled in Dagens Industri as an unaccompanied-refugee-turned-AI-founder -- that is six independent third-party signals, none of them a cereal-box stunt but together they pin Pango above the absent-evidence default. What Pango does *not* have is the single unscalable trick I retell at parties; the resourcefulness is institutional, not theatrical.

On organic-to-founder pain, the counterfactual is sharp: if Rahimi or Reszczynski had run a D2C brand and personally lost weekends to AfterShip + Returnly + a TMS spreadsheet, Pango's organic-idea score flips a tier upward. Instead Rahimi's nearest exposure is Shopstory (e-commerce content tooling, not post-purchase ops) and Reszczynski's is a generic full-stack/SaaS career; neither lived inside the "~50% of operational team time" pain that pango.ai cites as the wedge. This is "spotted a real market" rather than "Drew Houston forgot his USB stick again."

On cofounder configuration, the single-anchor parallel is **Stripe** (2010) -- two founders, complementary technical/operational split, which is the configuration that has worked across the portfolio. Pango's Rahimi/Reszczynski pair maps cleanly onto the operator/builder split, but the dossier produces no public data on how long they have worked together, which is precisely the relationship-history signal I weight; Pango Solutions AB at SSE Business Lab is the only anchor and it dates to the company, not before it.

My 36-month forecast: Pango reaches $2-5M ARR powering Nordic and EU mid-market D2C brands, then gets squeezed when AfterShip ships agentic features on top of its existing $85M ARR base and 1,200-carrier footprint, and Pango exits to AfterShip or Parcel Perform in the $20-50M range -- a respectable outcome that is not a YC top-decile bet. The mechanism is straightforward: Pango's bundling thesis structurally cannibalizes incumbents' per-module pricing, which buys it 12-18 months of differentiation, but the integration-maintenance burden on 5 people plus Stockholm-to-U.S. GTM friction means Pango runs out of differentiation runway before reaching escape velocity.

The strongest objection is the **Airbnb** (2009) Known Exception: I funded a team whose idea I had mentally rejected because the cereal-box stunt overrode my skepticism, and Pango has six paying brand customers two months after launch plus a Dagens Industri profile of a refugee-founder building under hard constraints -- that is the *shape* of an animal signal, and if I am consistent I should weight it the way I weighted Chesky's cereal boxes. The dossier evidence that wins against this is the asymmetry: Chesky's stunt was *theatrical and unscalable* in the specific way that proved he would do whatever it took; Pango's six brands plus Vinnova grant plus TINC placement is *institutional and scalable*, which is closer to the **Loopt** (2005) profile than the **Airbnb** (2009) one, and **Loopt** (2005) is not a return I underwrite again. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: if Rahimi or Reszczynski personally walks 20 Nordic D2C founders through onboarding in the next quarter and Pango lands a signed annual contract above $50K ACV from a brand outside the founders' Stockholm network, that is the unscalable artifact I am missing and I would write the check.
