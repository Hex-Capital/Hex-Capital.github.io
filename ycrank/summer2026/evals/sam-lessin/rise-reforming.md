# Rise Reforming -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Rise Reforming is a capex-heavy industrial hardware bet at a genuinely non-consensus pre-seed price with essentially zero founder-brand or owned distribution on the cap table — the highest-scoring dimension is the round itself (LongJump + UChicago Booth at $650K, total $1.06M raised), the lowest is the founder-as-brand axis, where three UChicago Molecular Engineering undergrads have no X presence, no GitHub, and no public writing on the reforming category they intend to define. That signature — cheap non-consensus round, zero legibility asset, real capex thesis — is the diagnostic. Rise Reforming is a Ro-shaped ambition in a category where Emvolon has already raised $10.5M and is closing a $30M Series A.

**Solana at $0.05/coin** is the HIGH rung on price discipline, and the shared mechanism with Rise Reforming is real: consensus VC has quit industrial hardware climate the way it quit crypto in 2018, so a $650K pre-seed with a Chicago university-adjacent syndicate and no tier-1 name on the SAFE is exactly the deal shape I go looking for. Where Rise Reforming diverges from the Solana analogy is what sits on the other side of the low entry price — Solana had an emerging on-chain economic activity curve nobody was pricing; Rise Reforming has 1,800 hours of syngas POC and a "conditional" $5M DME offtake, which is credible for a hardware pre-seed but doesn't produce the same convexity because the next tack requires $8–15M of Series A capex, not a token launch. Cheap round, yes. Solana-shaped optionality, no.

**The canonical YC AI-agent pitch** — $25K MRR, $30M post, four tier-1s in — is the LOW rung on founder-as-brand for the opposite reason Rise Reforming is: that founder has no owned distribution because they don't need one, since VCs are bidding on the paper. Rise Reforming has no owned distribution because industrial B2B doesn't reward it, and that's the pattern I need to sit with. George Rose is a 776 Climate Fellow with a DOE EnergyTech UP $50K prize, but there's no newsletter, no essay corpus, no engaged Discord — the Slow Creator Fund thesis doesn't apply here because the customer is a wastewater plant operator, not a follower. This isn't the founder-as-brand game, so I score it low but I don't punish it as the same failure as the AI-agent pitch — it's a different asset entirely.

Capital structure is where Rise Reforming most resembles **Ro** as a Known Exception candidate — high burn, multi-round trajectory, capital intensity as the moat rather than the failure mode. The mechanism I need to compound over 18 months: shipping-container-sized on-site reforming units at biogas producers require per-site capex on the order of $1–3M, and every site added is a fresh capital ask that Emvolon can outbid because they're already $40M ahead in the regatta. Ro cleared 50-state prescription rails where no direct competitor had scale; Rise Reforming's Chicagoland wastewater pilot is one of at least four modular biogas-to-liquid-fuels players (Emvolon, Modular Plant Solutions, Advanced Energy Materials, Topsoe SynCOR) already tacking. The Ro override doesn't quite fire because Ro was first through the regulatory door, and Rise Reforming is second through a technical door.

Founder conviction: the counterfactual for a tier flip is straightforward — if George Rose or Lucas Zubillaga Maharg had 18 months of public writing on why containerized reforming beats Emvolon's repurposed-engine architecture on catalyst deactivation economics, this criterion moves from a 7 to a 14. What the dossier actually shows is a UChicago undergrad cohort with Gagliardi Group and Argonne provenance, prior product traction on plastic-to-DME that later pivoted to biogas-to-DME/methanol — a real pivot the dossier flags as a "prior-product vs. current-product traction conflation" risk. That's not conviction; that's opportunity-following, and the missionary temperament filter (Antoine Buteau, ~2023) requires I see the belief, not infer it.

Category sleeve, on a single-anchor parallel: this is not **Aleo** or **Klaytn** — pre-consensus crypto with on-chain economic activity — and it's not **Allbirds** DTC either. Rise Reforming is industrial climate hardware, a sleeve I've been publicly cool on because "AI is just more internet" and, by the same logic, "modular reforming is just more chemicals engineering" — no fundamental disruption, just distributed unit economics against incumbents like Topsoe and Thyssenkrupp. The $35bn/y TAM is company-cited and unvalidated by third parties. The ARR discrepancy — PitchBook's $14M vs. YC's $92M on the same 7 supply agreements — is the exact kind of pipeline arithmetic I've seen inflate hardware pre-seed decks and evaporate at Series A due diligence.

Forecast: in 36 months Rise Reforming is alive but sub-scale, with 1–2 deployed containerized units at wastewater or agricultural sites, sub-$3M realized revenue, and a $10–15M Series A raised at a 3–4x markup behind Emvolon's category consolidation — or, in the tail, they license their patent-pending reforming process to a larger modular player (Emvolon, Topsoe) and pivot to catalyst IP. The mechanism producing this: Emvolon's $10.5M + Montauk JV + Pioneer Energy partnership is a two-year head start in exactly the tack Rise Reforming needs to complete, and modular biogas-to-methanol is a consolidation game, not a network-effect game.

The strongest bull case sings a Ro-analog: regulatory tailwinds from IMO 2024 marine methanol rules plus federal RNG incentives create a genuine venture moment, the $92M ARR pipeline (even discounted to PitchBook's $14M) implies real biogas producer demand, and 776 Foundation's involvement is a founder-quality signal Alexis Ohanian doesn't give lightly — Rise Reforming could be the on-site distributed play where capital intensity IS the moat and Emvolon's engine-based approach hits catalyst deactivation faster. That case stings because Ro is a real Known Exception in my portfolio. Where I dismantle it: Ro was the first-mover through a regulatory door no incumbent had cleared; Rise Reforming is entering a door Emvolon has already walked through with $40M of committed capital and a Montauk JV. The verdict flips to Invest if one condition becomes verifiable in the next six months — a binding (not MOU, not LOI, not "conditional") offtake or supply agreement at ≥$10M with a named counterparty that Emvolon competed for and lost. Absent that, this is a pass I make politely and revisit at the Series A if the tack resolves.
