# HeyClicky -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Farza Majeed shipped Clicky as an MIT-licensed Swift app with a YouTube demo, a 5.8K-star GitHub repo, and a 108.6K-follower X megaphone before a YC partner ever signed the SAFE — that is the signature. HeyClicky is a code-and-media-leveraged single-user agent where the founder *is* the distribution and the product distributes itself, but every user remains economically independent of the next user. The high dimension and the low dimension on HeyClicky are doing exactly opposite work: permissionless leverage at the top, zero network compounding at the bottom. That pairing tells me what kind of company this is before I read another line.

Twitter (2007) is the load-bearing analogy on HeyClicky's leverage architecture — a media product where the founder's own reach was the launch vector and the marginal cost of replication was zero. HeyClicky compresses that same architecture into a Mac app: Farza open-sourced farzaa/clicky under MIT and pushed it through his personal channel, the Tally Windows waitlist propagates by founder retweet, no paid acquisition appears anywhere in the dossier. Where HeyClicky diverges from the Twitter rung is the direction the leverage points — MIT-licensed code is permissionless leverage *for the ecosystem*, not for the cap table. Twitter's protocol nature locked value into the platform because the social graph was the moat; HeyClicky explicitly invites forks ("Go forth into the wild and build the future of education and the future of AI interfaces"), which means the same code-leverage that gets HeyClicky to 5.8K stars also gets the next team there with R&D cost approaching zero. The architecture passes my filter. The accrual of that architecture to *this* company is what I would push Farza on directly.

Perplexity (2022) is the rung where I waived the network criterion because criteria #1 and #3 cleared emphatically and the category-timing was pre-consensus — and the failure mechanism I have to be honest about is that HeyClicky is the same single-sided shape without the same offsets. The Nth HeyClicky user does not make user N-1 more valuable; there is no marketplace, no UGC layer, no social graph in the dossier. Aravind Srinivas brought a Berkeley retrieval PhD; HeyClicky brings a buildspace operator with consumer-distribution instincts, which is a different and lighter knowledge gate. And the AI-agents-on-Mac category in Spring 2026 is post-Cluely's $15M a16z round and post-Granola's $1.5B mark — narrative has arrived, the pre-consensus discount is gone. The same exception math that carried Perplexity does not carry HeyClicky.

On specific knowledge — Farza is a second-time YC founder who built buildspace to $10M raised at a $100M valuation with OpenAI sponsorship before voluntarily closing it in August 2024, and the README frames Clicky as an "AI teacher." That is *exactly* the education-distribution muscle the prior company built, redirected into the cursor. This is not a Stanford CS clone executing a memo. It is a founder who knows how to ship consumer products to learners and has the receipts. The knowledge is non-trainable in the way Naval defines it — you cannot bootcamp your way into a 108.6K-follower audience that trusts you to teach them.

The market dimension compounds or fails over the next 18 months on a single question: does Apple Intelligence ship third-party app actions before HeyClicky finds its pivot. The dossier names Notion, Gmail, Calendar, DaVinci Resolve, Figma, and After Effects as integration surfaces — that is a consumer-productivity platform shape with at least three adjacent pivot vectors (education agent, creator-tool copilot, voice-first OS layer). HeyClicky has the pivot room my framework demands. What HeyClicky does not have is structural insulation from the OS vendor, and on a 36-month clock the platform-substitution risk is the variable that dominates the geometry.

Stack Overflow (2008) is the single-anchor parallel on the founder trio — Atwood and Spolsky were not advertising integrity, they were shipping in public for years and letting the work speak. Farza's voluntary shutdown of buildspace ("I cannot craft an updated direction that feels worth pursuing") is the inverse of the zombie-company pattern that fails my integrity leg; he gave the money back rather than coasting on $10M. That reads cleaner to me than most founders with two YC stamps. Intelligence and energy are both visible in the artifact — a four-week-old native Swift app with a demo and an open-source release is a high-output signature. The 10-year partner test passes.

My 36-month forecast on HeyClicky: $1–3M ARR ceiling under a freemium model, an Apple Intelligence WWDC announcement compresses the wedge, and the company either pivots into an "AI tutor" consumer-education product (the buildspace lineage reasserting) or gets acqui-hired by a productivity incumbent — Notion, Linear, or Granola itself — for the team rather than the IP, because the IP is MIT. The mechanism producing that forecast is the high/low signature: permissionless leverage gets HeyClicky into the conversation, the absence of network compounding means there is no asymmetric upside math that survives Apple's platform move.

The strongest bull case against my forecast is the unstated-pattern argument: HeyClicky is *exactly* the builder-tool-by-a-builder-with-media-leverage shape that I have been quietly funding since AngelList (2010) and Airchat (2024), and at angel check size the downside is one X while the upside if HeyClicky becomes the default agent-runner for 100M Mac users is the 1,000x outcome that punishes timidity — the Twitter regret in literal form. That case stings because the pattern match is real and Farza's distribution is real. What dismantles it is the MIT-license decision and the post-consensus AI-agent timing: the leverage architecture works *for the founder's reputation* and *for the ecosystem*, but the accrual mechanism to the company is missing in a way it was not missing for Twitter (network), Notion (proprietary doc graph), or Replit (compute + hosting). The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: HeyClicky relicenses to source-available or BUSL, ships a per-user agent-memory graph that creates switching cost, and the next round prices in a Notion-style productivity acqui-hire floor. Until one of those three lands, the asymmetric math does not punish me for sitting it out — I would rather see Farza's *next* check, not this one.
