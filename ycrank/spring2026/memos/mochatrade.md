## The Idea

- **Problem:** Non-US retail traders (140M+ Indian demat holders) lack compliant, leveraged access to liquid US equities like TSLA/NVDA/AAPL through domestic channels (YC company description; founder quote, Indian Startup News, 2026).
- **Approach:** Self-custodial perpetual futures contracts referencing 50+ US stocks with up to 20x leverage, INR on-ramp via UPI with 24/7 real-time settlement and no US brokerage account required (Whalesbook/Entrackr, 2026).
- **Differentiation:** Vs. Indian brokers like INDmoney/Vested/Stockal which route to cash US stock accounts with FX remittance, Mocha offers leverage and perp structure with no foreign remittance; vs. Polymarket/Kalshi which launched crypto perpetuals in April 2026 (CNBC, 2026-04-21), Mocha focuses on single-name US equity underlyings rather than crypto/prediction markets.
- **Business Model:** [Inferred]: Trading fees and/or funding-rate spread typical for perpetual futures venues; no public pricing page found.
- **TAM/SAM:** Company-stated "$100B+ market opportunity" for leveraged US equity access to eligible global traders (Indian Startup News, 2026); India context: ~21 crore (210M) demat accounts by end-2025 (Business Standard/HDFC Sky, 2025).
- **GTM / Distribution:** India-first launch targeting 140M+ demat holders, INR/UPI rails, then expansion to other markets (YC company description; Indian Startup News, 2026).

## Defensibility

- **Moat today:** Regulatory/compliance groundwork is a stated use of pre-seed funds, with founders explicitly building "compliant pathways" for non-US traders (Indian Startup News, 2026; TechGraph, 2026).
- **Future moat:** [Inferred]: Liquidity network effects on the perp order book plus jurisdictional licensing across emerging markets could compound if Mocha achieves first-mover status in equity-referenced perps for retail.
- **Market structure:** [Inferred]: US-regulated incumbents (Robinhood, Coinbase) face channel conflict offering 20x leveraged US equity perps to US persons under CFTC/SEC rules; Mocha's non-US-person scope sidesteps a constraint binding on US incumbents (CNBC reporting, 2026-04-27, notes incumbents have not launched open-ended equity perps in US).
- **Commoditization risk:** Kalshi (CFTC DCM) and Polymarket entered crypto perps in April 2026 and could extend to equity underlyings; Hyperliquid and Ostium already host synthetic equity perps on-chain (Finance Magnates, 2026; CNBC, 2026-04-21).

## Key Risks

- **Regulatory risk across target jurisdictions:** India's FEMA/LRS framework restricts leveraged derivatives on foreign equities for retail residents, and SEBI/RBI scrutiny of crypto-adjacent offshore platforms has intensified; founders explicitly cite compliance buildout as a primary use of pre-seed funds (Indian Startup News, 2026), but no licenses or regulatory approvals are disclosed publicly.
- **Competitive substitution from US-regulated venues:** Kalshi (CFTC DCM, $11B valuation) and Polymarket ($9B) entered perpetual futures in April 2026 (CNBC, 2026-04-21), and Coinbase's $2.9B Deribit acquisition (August 2025) signals incumbents pursuing perps; an expansion by any of these into equity underlyings for non-US users would erode Mocha's window.
- **Underlying price-feed / counterparty risk:** Perpetual futures on US equities require continuous oracle pricing and a solvent counterparty/market maker absent the underlying cash market being open 24/7; no public detail found on Mocha's price-feed methodology or insurance/liquidation backstop.
- **Retail leverage and consumer-harm exposure:** 20x leverage on volatile single-name equities to retail users in emerging markets creates concentrated drawdown risk; Whalesbook (2026) explicitly flags "leverage can amplify losses for retail traders" and notes derivatives regulation risk.
- **Name disambiguation:** "Mocha" appears in multiple unrelated fintech and crypto product names; all citations in this memo verified against founder names and YC Spring 2026 batch.
