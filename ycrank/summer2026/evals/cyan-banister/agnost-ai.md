# Agnost AI -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Agnost AI is the shape of company where the co-founder titles are cleanly delineated but the category is the loudest room at YC S26 — Shubham Palriwala as CEO and Parth Ajmera as CTO on one side, and LangSmith at $70M, Braintrust at an $800M valuation, Arize at $70M Series C, plus Helicone rolled into Mintlify and Langfuse into Clickhouse on the other. That pairing — governance clarity paired with a fully consensus category — is the analytical signature. I'm never playing the game I seem to be playing, and the game Agnost AI seems to be playing is "win AI observability against five funded incumbents," which is a game I don't want to be at the table for.

Anduril is my HIGH rung on lived-experience founder origin — Palmer Luckey shipped Oculus from a garage before touching defense, so when he walked into the DoD conversation he had already made the artifact. The mechanism that makes Anduril load-bearing here is that the founder's autobiography *put him in front of* the problem before any memo did. Agnost AI diverges on this axis in a specific way: Palriwala did five years of OpenTelemetry-adjacent OSS at Kyverno, OpenMined, and GSoC/OWASP Juice Shop, and was a founding engineer at Formbricks — that is real domain adjacency to observability, not zero. But nothing in the dossier says Palriwala or Ajmera shipped a production AI agent, watched it fail silently, and got paged at 3am because a user got stuck in a tool-call loop. The lived moment is missing. The Microsoft and Cisco lines on Ajmera's LinkedIn are exactly the resume shape I flag as "discovered the problem in a memo," and no dossier evidence overrides that read.

The consensus YC AI-agent darling from my Pass Example — two ex-FAANG PMs, oversubscribed seed, six tier-one funds circling — is the failure pattern I want to trace onto Agnost AI. That pattern's mechanism is: category heat converges the round price, incumbents with $70M–$80M war chests ship the differentiating feature in a quarter, and the founder's edge evaporates before Series A. Agnost AI is not *quite* that darling — Palriwala and Ajmera don't have the Stanford-Goldman stamp, they came through Entrepreneurs First with a $250K IC to Ajmera, and Palriwala's LinkedIn shows OSS grinding rather than polished-PM cosplay. Good. But the *category* is the darling, and that is worse. Braintrust closed $80M in February 2026 with Iconiq and a16z; those partners will not let auto-PR generation be Agnost AI's moat for eighteen months.

On magically-weird, the counterfactual to move Agnost AI up a tier is narrow and specific: I would need to see two credible investors publicly pass on aesthetic or capital-intensity grounds and Palriwala able to articulate the misread. Agnost AI is the inverse — the WorkOS blog feature from November 10, 2025 and two consecutive Context podcast slots at the MCP Developers Summit are exactly the "hot deal" temperature that inverts the signal. The Astute Analytica line that AI agent observability grows from $0.4B in 2025 to $7.1B by 2035 at 33.3% CAGR is TAM-slide-one bait; it does not describe an ignored category, it describes a category the entire batch is chasing.

Governance is where Agnost AI actually scores best in my framework, and the mechanism to watch over eighteen months is whether the Palriwala-CEO / Ajmera-CTO title split holds under the first real disagreement. The dossier gives me delineated titles, which is more than HQ Trivia's Yusupov/Kroll partnership ever had before it feuded itself into the ground, but no operating agreement, no documented tiebreaker protocol, no vesting-cliff mechanic on the record. Two EF-cohort friends becoming co-founders is precisely the situation where the tiebreaker conversation hasn't happened yet.

Niantic is my single anchor on physical-world verifiability — I invested because I had been playing Ingress obsessively since 2012, so the product was in my pocket before the pitch was in my inbox. Agnost AI's "2-minute setup" OpenTelemetry SDK is installable, and a developer friend could show me a dashboard with 6,026 tool invocations and a 97.34% success rate as WorkOS demoed. But the artifact only exists inside another team's engineering console; I cannot ride it, scan it, or hand it to a friend at a Four Seasons café. Google as a logo on the landing page is not the same as Google-the-paying-customer, and the dossier is explicit that paid-vs-free breakdown is undisclosed.

My 36-month forecast for Agnost AI: acquihire into Braintrust, Arize, or Datadog at a sub-$50M sticker by the end of 2028, with the auto-PR primitive absorbed into an incumbent's eval product. The mechanism is the roll-up already visible in the dossier — Helicone into Mintlify in March 2026, Langfuse into Clickhouse in January 2026 — compressing the standalone-vendor exit window until Palriwala and Ajmera take the strategic offer to protect the team.

The strongest bear-case-dismantling I owe Agnost AI is this: Palriwala's OpenTelemetry-native OSS work at Kyverno and OpenMined is more domain-lived than the "found the problem in a memo" caricature deserves, MCP-first coverage is a genuine wedge because incumbents are LangChain-native, and the Odysser testimonial claiming 1,247 surfaced feature requests plus Lopus AI merging 16 of 18 autonomous PRs is real product love — not vanity-metric noise. If Agnost AI is actually the OpenTelemetry-of-agents standard and MCP's 97M+ downloads route through Palriwala's SDK, this is a Postmates-shaped bet hiding as boring analytics. The dossier evidence that wins the argument is the incumbent balance sheet: Braintrust ships auto-PR on top of $80M in the bank before Agnost AI's Series A. The single verifiable condition that would flip my verdict is Palriwala walking me through a specific 3am incident he lived through running a production agent — the autobiographical moment the dossier is missing — and one publicly named investor passing on aesthetic grounds. Absent that, this is the Pass Example, and I write the check into the Vigil Labs or Superpower class of company instead.
