# Sherpa -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Sherpa is the canonical Pass Example from my own deck walking into my inbox -- Ethan Kinnan and Norbu Sonam are real ex-Lindy operators with actual growth-experiment scar tissue (Norbu "tripled paywall conversion," ran 50+ experiments), and Sherpa is still a YC X26 AI-agent SaaS pointed at the most consensus-priced category in the 2026 batch. The signature here is operator-quality founders wrapped around commodity AI-agent exposure -- the highest dimension is founder conviction earned at Lindy, the lowest is a category sleeve ("AI is just more internet," Joe Lonsdale Ep 113) I have explicitly re-weighted away from. That pairing tells me everything: this is a competent team running the exact factory-model SaaS pitch I built the regatta deck to argue against.

On founder asymmetric conviction, the load-bearing analogy is Drop.io -- I bootstrapped a file-sharing primitive against a wall of free incumbents because I had a non-obvious belief about the asset, not because I was responding to opportunity. Sherpa's founders pass the operating-scar-tissue half of that filter: Norbu shipped Copilot image generation at Microsoft and then ran 50+ growth experiments at Lindy, which means Sherpa is the literal automation of a workflow he hated. Where Sherpa diverges from the Drop.io rung is the missing artifact -- I cannot find a single piece of public writing by Ethan Kinnan or Norbu Sonam arguing why VWO's GPT-3.5 copy-gen feature is wrong, why Optimizely's variant generator misses the point, or why autonomous CRO is anything other than "we wired an LLM to a JS snippet." "Writing is thinking" -- and on the most contested question in their category, Sherpa's founders have published nothing.

On category sleeve, the relevant failure pattern is my own Pass Example almost verbatim: "AI agent for [enterprise vertical], $25K MRR, three months out of YC, $30M post-money, four tier-1 VCs already in -- I'm out before the meeting starts." Sherpa is "AI agent for conversion rate optimization, three named logos all from the Lindy/YC network, Spring 2026 batch." The pattern appears in the dossier with almost no friction: VWO already ships GPT-based copy generation (VWO blog, 2025), Kameleoon markets "AI for A/B Testing," Optimizely added AI variant generation, and Everstone just rolled Wingify and AB Tasty into a $100M+ platform in January 2026 -- meaning Sherpa's core capability is being shipped by every distribution-advantaged incumbent in the category simultaneously. This is not pre-consensus crypto at $0.05/coin; this is consensus AI marketing-tech at whatever the YC SAFE cap is, and the LLM-generated-variant capability is not exclusive to Sherpa.

Round dynamics: the dossier is silent on price, which under my absent-evidence default means I assume Sherpa is taking the YC standard SAFE at a batch-median cap, and silence on round dynamics in a hot AI SaaS category is itself the signal -- price-discipline alpha lives in the rounds where the category is cold, not where every Spring 2026 batch deck names an AI agent for some vertical. The mechanism that compounds against Sherpa over 18 months is that VWO+AB Tasty under Everstone will price-match Sherpa's autonomous-variant feature inside an existing enterprise contract before Sherpa can clear its second financing tack, and the SMB segment Sherpa is winging on (Lindy, Insforge, Gauge) is exactly where consolidated incumbents will undercut on bundled pricing.

Capital structure is the other dimension where Sherpa fails my framework cleanly -- the counterfactual is sharp: if Sherpa's pricing were structured as a percentage of incremental conversion lift Sherpa generates for each customer (revenue-share against measured uplift), I would re-rate this from a generic SaaS cap table into a structure-first deal, because the asset would then be a portfolio of conversion royalties across hundreds of websites rather than a seat-priced subscription competing with VWO. Nothing in the withsherpa.ai dossier suggests Sherpa has considered that instrument; the [Inferred] business model is "SaaS subscription tied to monthly traffic volume," which is the cap table that needs five more rounds to reach default-alive.

Founder-as-brand parallel -- compare Sherpa to John Fish / Bookshelved on the single axis of owned distribution before the round. John Fish brought a measurable seven-figure YouTube audience to a literary product where the creator's distribution WAS the moat. Sherpa's closest analog is Ethan Kinnan having "taught 10,000+ people to build custom agents" at Lindy, which is real operator distribution -- but it is Lindy's distribution, not Ethan's, and it does not appear in Sherpa's go-to-market as an owned channel; the three named logos (Lindy, Insforge, Gauge) are the Lindy alumni network monetizing once, not a community Sherpa can re-pull at zero CAC. No newsletter, no podcast, no Discord, no public writing -- this fails the Slow Creator Fund filter in both directions.

My 36-month forecast on Sherpa: dead or acqui-hired into the Everstone/Wingify/AB Tasty platform for under $30M in stock at a flat-to-down outcome by Q2 2029, with $2-6M ARR ceiling before VWO's GPT variant generation feature reaches parity and bundled pricing collapses Sherpa's SMB land motion. The mechanism is straightforward -- Sherpa is a feature wearing a company costume in a category where the four largest incumbents just consolidated R&D budgets, and the founders' operating skill at Lindy does not change the asset-class mispricing on the round.

Steel-manning the bull case: Sublime Security was a pre-consensus enterprise security seed I bought when most security investors stayed in late-stage SOC SaaS, and Sherpa could be argued as the Sublime parallel for CRO -- buy into the category before the next consolidation wave, ride Everstone's M&A budget as exit liquidity, treat Norbu's Microsoft + Lindy experiment-running depth as the missionary-temperament moat. The bull case stings because Norbu genuinely is the exact founder for this workload and the 7,338,896-session dataset is a real cold-start advantage other YC teams won't replicate. What dismantles it: Sublime sat in a category where the incumbents (legacy email security gateways) had no LLM-native capability and were a generation behind on architecture, whereas VWO is shipping GPT copy generation today and Kameleoon is actively marketing AI A/B testing -- Sherpa is not pre-consensus, it is mid-consensus arriving late. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: Sherpa announces a non-SaaS instrument (revenue-share against measured conversion lift, or a holdco rolling up several SMB CRO agencies) at a sub-batch-median cap with at least one tier-1 VC visibly passing -- absent that, this is a pass.
