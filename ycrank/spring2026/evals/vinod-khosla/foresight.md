# Foresight -- Vinod Khosla Evaluation

Foresight is a documented-pivot founder on a commodity-consequence thesis -- Eytan Rozenblum calling 2023 "likely the worst year of my life" and exiting PrompTessor/Fluentive into synthetic consumer panels is the highest-loaded dimension in this dossier, and the consequence-at-maximum-success is the lowest. That pairing tells me exactly what kind of company this is: a Slash-shaped founder profile pointed at a market-research workflow, not a Slash-shaped founder profile pointed at anything that breaks an economic mechanism. Foresight wants to replace Quantilope panels at Caudalie; Foresight does not want to break the $15 trillion labor stack I described to Fortune (2026-03-06).

OpenAI (2019) is what a HIGH rung on Consequence Magnitude looks like -- a no-revenue check where the success-state was civilizational redirection of cognition itself, and I wrote $50M against it because the world-state at maximum ambition was unrecognizable. Foresight's success-state is that CPG insights teams at Estee Lauder run "15 tests per week at 25x lower cost per insight" instead of waiting six weeks for a Toluna panel. That is a procurement-line-item replacement, not a regime collapse. The $1.8B-to-$8.2B synthetic data TAM cited from the Ditto 2026 map is exactly the "slice of an existing market" framing my Outlook Business essay warned against. Nothing in the foresight.tt landing page names an incumbent system that disappears -- Quantilope keeps its 300+ enterprise clients, YouGov keeps Yabble, the panel industry just gets a cheaper input.

Range Fuels (~2007) failed the technical-miracle test in the direction of too many miracles; Foresight fails it in the inverse direction -- there are no miracles required at all. The Range Fuels failure mechanism was capital-adequacy misjudged against three coupled scientific risks; the Foresight risk pattern is the opposite anti-pattern from my Red Flags list: a wrapper on commodity foundation models with no proprietary stack, no panel data asset, no calibration corpus, no patents, one named customer. Simile raised $100M (Feb 2026, Index) and Aaru raised ~$50M at a $1B headline (Dec 2025, Redpoint) building the same primitive -- if the technical question were hard, those rounds would be smaller and the entrant count would be lower. Foresight sits on the wrong side of my Cognition (2024) comparison: Cognition was stack-deep on agent infrastructure; Foresight is API-deep on prompts that simulate a shopper.

Founder-defiance compounds over 18 months only if the founder has publicly committed to a contrarian position the field will be forced to confront -- the Levine/Finn pattern at Physical Intelligence (2024) where the academic record is the moat. Eytan Rozenblum's public record is a pivoted PrompTessor and a rebranded Fluentive; Antoine Bertrand's public record is a LinkedIn entry. Neither has staked a position the synthetic-consumer field would have to argue with. The UC Berkeley 28,000-prediction study I cite at TED is the relevant prior -- absent a defiance signal, I assume Foresight will execute whatever Quantilope's product roadmap concludes is correct.

Foresight's learning-rate score would only flip a tier if the pivot from PrompTessor through Fluentive into synthetic consumers were accompanied by a written thesis-update artifact explaining what the founders learned and why CPG was the corrected target -- which the dossier does not contain. Slash (2024) cleared my $100M Series C bar because the Kanye pivot story was a publicly told learning narrative with a concrete demand signal behind it; Foresight has the pivot shape but not the narrative artifact, and "barely raised" in 2023 is closer to capitulation than to thesis update. I score the dimension as real signal but un-multiplied.

Foresight against Slash (2024) on capital profile is the single-anchor comparison that decides the check-size question: Slash needed Series C dollars because consumer business banking required deposit float, compliance overhead, and a card-network integration that compounded with capital. Foresight needs a seat license and a prompt library. The roadmap implied by foresight.tt does not require $50M to a first commercial milestone -- it requires the two founders to keep shipping test types. I am over-resourced for this deal in the exact pattern my framework flags as a soft pass.

36-month forecast: Foresight is acqui-hired by Quantilope, YouGov, or Toluna for under $25M by mid-2029, or dies into the gap between Simile's $100M war chest and Aaru's $1B headline -- with acqui-hire the more likely outcome because Eytan's second-time-YC founder profile is worth more to a panel incumbent than the product is. The mechanism is straightforward: hybrid incumbents already at the CPG buyer table layer synthetic on top of real panels (HarmonAIze, Yabble), customers prefer one vendor with both, and the pre-seed two-person team cannot outspend Simile's engineering or Quantilope's sales relationships into Nestle and Kraft Heinz.

The strongest opposing case is that Foresight is the Slash-shaped exception: YC pedigree, documented founder pivot, Diana Hu as partner, and a category where Aaru hit a $1B headline at Series A -- meaning the market is rewarding the shape regardless of underlying consequence, and a Khosla check at YC stage gets the same exception-treatment my $100M Series C into Slash got. That bull case stings because the Aaru and Simile rounds are real and the YC+pivot profile is exactly what triggered Slash. It loses on two facts the dossier makes unavoidable: Slash had a publicly told pivot narrative tied to consumer-banking distribution Kanye gave them, and Foresight has a pivot with no equivalent demand-side proof beyond Caudalie. The verdict flips if Foresight publishes a peer-reviewed accuracy benchmark on price-elasticity or conjoint that beats real panels at p<0.05 across three categories -- that would be the falsifiable miracle that makes the synthetic thesis non-commodity, and I would re-open the file.
