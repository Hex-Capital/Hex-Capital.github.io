# HEVN, inc -- Elad Gil Evaluation

HEVN, inc is a shipped-team-in-waitlist-phase pre-seed: Peter Volnov and the three other HOT Labs co-founders have demonstrably built a 15M-user / $100M-TVL wallet together over four years, but HEVN, inc itself has no revenue, no pricing page on gethevn.com (May 2026), and a 1,056-follower X account against a waitlist for EU/US/Swiss/UAE accounts. That pairing — high execution rhythm, zero revenue curve — is the analytical signature, and it's the exact profile that "just working" vs "false signal" (*TechCrunch*, Nov 2025) is meant to adjudicate. The honest answer is that I can't adjudicate yet.

**Anduril** (2022) is the load-bearing analog on the catalyst dimension, and HEVN, inc sits a rung below it for a specific reason. Anduril's catalyst was Ukraine inside a 24-month window — a single, dateable event that re-opened a market peers had written off. HEVN, inc has a *stack* of smaller catalysts: YC itself funding Spring 2026 batches in USDC (*TechCrunch*, Feb 2026), Mercury/Brex/Ramp structurally requiring a US EIN (Slash blog, 2026), and Mercury hitting $5.2B / $650M ARR (CNBC, May 2026) without yet extending to non-US internet companies. That's a real opening — non-US YC founders today genuinely run Mercury + Wise + Deel + Stripe as four separate stacks — but it's a *gap in incumbent coverage* rather than a market peers misread as dead. HEVN, inc is playing the "Stripe-after-PayPal" template (workflow primitive that incumbents won't extend to) not the "defense-after-Ukraine" template.

The **Lyft** Series C pass is the failure mechanism on revenue velocity: a real market, a real team, a dossier that couldn't show me a curve that distinguished the bet from the obvious bet. HEVN, inc's dossier is worse on this axis — there is no curve at all. "<0.5% FX fees on USD card" is a marketing claim, not a metric. The +264.1% week-over-week on @hevn_finance is off a base of ~290 followers, which is launch noise, not the **Harvey** (2023) "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" signal I size up on. And in an AI-adjacent fintech cycle where YC is *literally wiring batch funding in stablecoins*, the absence of even a single named design partner or LOI is the kind of silence that "something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863) is meant to flag. I won't flag it that hard at pre-seed waitlist stage — but I score it.

On founder shipping rhythm, HEVN, inc is the strongest dimension and earns it the **Perplexity** (2022) comparison cautiously: Volnov shipped HOT Wallet to #1 on DappRadar for 8 months, Andrei Zhevlakov took CTO at 15-year-old-first-SWE pedigree and pulled a $250K Stellar grant (Chainwire, May 2025), and the four co-founders have shared a cap table for four years. That's the rare pre-seed where "have they shipped independently" answers itself. The compounding mechanism over 18 months: a team that already operationalized stablecoin UX for 15M consumers will iterate faster on the API/CLI surface than a banking-pedigree team would, and the prior product gave them KYC/compliance scar tissue across UAE/BVI/HK that a Mercury-clone team starts from zero on.

Counterfactual on index position: HEVN, inc scores a tier higher if the dossier showed that the 82-country local-bank partnership rail was *proprietary* — exclusive corridors, settled banking integrations HEVN owns rather than rents. It doesn't. **BVNK** already powers Deel, Worldpay, and Flywire (BVNK blog, 2025) and **Fasset** just raised $51M for stablecoin-neobank in emerging markets (CoinDesk, May 2026). HEVN, inc sits at the *customer-account* layer on top of rails that are commoditizing underneath it, which is the inverse of the **Stripe** (2010) "index on e-commerce" position. They're an app on the rails, not the rails.

**Color Genomics** (2015) is the single-miracle anchor and the one I co-founded specifically because it isolated workflow execution from chemistry risk. HEVN, inc does not isolate cleanly. Run the list: stablecoin regulatory posture across EU/US/Swiss/UAE simultaneously (Traders Union, 2026), local bank partnerships in 82+ countries that have to actually settle, MTL/banking-charter strategy as a Delaware C-corp with SF ops and UAE/BVI/HK founder history, and a GTM bet that non-US internet companies prefer a unified API over the existing Mercury+Wise+Deel stack. That's three independent miracles minimum — regulatory, distribution, displacement. The **Anduril** known exception doesn't apply here because Anduril's "single miracle" was software-on-existing-hardware-distribution; HEVN, inc's regulatory perimeter is the miracle and it isn't single.

36-month forecast: HEVN, inc reaches $3–12M ARR serving non-US YC and Bridge/Stripe-adjacent internet companies, then gets acquired by Mercury, Deel, or Wise for $40–150M as a non-US-entity wedge once Mercury's own non-US product roadmap stalls on charter constraints. The mechanism is the high/low signature: the team will ship — HOT Labs proves that — but the absence of a velocity curve at YC-stage, in a cycle where stablecoin neobanking is being commoditized by BVNK and Fasset underneath them, means HEVN, inc captures the wedge but not the category.

The bull objection that stings: Mercury at $5.2B / $650M ARR (CNBC, May 2026) *cannot* legally extend to non-US entities without a multi-year charter restructure, and Volnov's 463.2K X following plus the four-year shipping team gives HEVN, inc the same "founder-with-distribution-into-an-incumbent-gap" structure that **Brex** (2017) used to wedge Amex on US startups. If HEVN, inc is "Brex for non-US internet companies" and the YC USDC-funding catalyst pulls the entire Spring 2026 batch onto the product as design partners, the curve appears in six months and I'm wrong on revenue velocity. What dismantles this for now is the dossier itself: not one named design partner, not one signed LOI with a YC batch peer, no Product Hunt, no press on HEVN-the-product — only on Volnov-the-founder. The verifiable flip condition: if HEVN, inc shows 20+ paying YC Spring/Summer 2026 batch companies as accounts within 90 days of waitlist opening, I write the check at consensus seed prices. Until then, I pass and watch.
