# Smartbase -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Smartbase is Sam Goldman and Taira Fujioka building a clean single-miracle workflow layer -- LLM document extraction plus nine ERP integrations -- on top of a narrow ICP (metal finishing shops) that lacks index economics. That high/low pairing -- architecturally disciplined, distributionally narrow -- is the analytical signature: this is the kind of company that gets to $5M ARR servicing a real pain at five-figure ACVs and then has to argue its way out of the wedge, not the kind that becomes "an index on X."

**Color Genomics** (2015) is the load-bearing analogue on the dimension where Smartbase scores highest -- single-miracle architecture. The shared mechanism is *workflow infrastructure on top of a hard thing, not the hard thing itself*: Color was workflow on commodity sequencing, Smartbase is workflow on commodity multimodal extraction. Neither needed new physics. The Smartbase miracle is "extract structured POs from paper/fax/handwritten/email accurately enough that a metal-finishing shop will fire 1.4 headcount" -- and that miracle has been mostly resolved at the model layer by 2024-25, leaving GTM and ERP-integration execution as the remaining work. Where Smartbase diverges from the Color template is that Color was built as the data-and-workflow layer for an entire consumer-genomics category Othman Laraki and I architected end-to-end; Smartbase is a narrower bet on one vertical where the workflow rules don't obviously generalize across job shops -- the Acumatica/Epicor/SYSPRO integration breadth (nine ERPs) is the right defensive instinct but doesn't substitute for category position.

**Lyft Series C** is the lowest-rung reference on Smartbase's weakest dimension -- index position. The Lyft failure mechanism wasn't team or market size; it was structural #2-in-an-oligopoly economics where you fight share against a named leader and don't compound with the category. Smartbase's dossier shows that same fight pattern: Turian ($3.78M raised, Berlin), Leverage AI ($14.9M raised, NYC, Mark Cuban backed), Evolinq, plus Yooz and Netfira on the OCR floor, plus Epicor and SAP able to bundle natively as a services-attached upsell. Smartbase's response is "metal finishing shops with ITAR/CMMC/DFARS enclave" -- a real wedge, but a wedge into a narrow ICP, not an index position on a compounding category. Per *AI Market Clarity* (blog.eladgil.com, Nov 2025), PO automation is not a "winners declared" category yet -- but it is a *crowded* one, and Smartbase's distinguishing artifact (US-hosted compliance enclave) is a deployment configuration, not a distribution moat.

Smartbase on market re-opening: the catalyst is real but shared. Multimodal LLMs crossed the threshold in 2024-25 for heterogeneous-document extraction that prior OCR/RPA pipelines failed on -- that's a genuine 24-month window, not a steady-state market. The problem for Smartbase is that this is the *same* catalyst Turian, Leverage AI, and Evolinq are all riding into 2026, so the catalyst doesn't differentiate. The more interesting and quieter catalyst in the Smartbase dossier is defense-supply-chain re-shoring -- the ITAR/CMMC/DFARS-compliant US enclave maps to a Pentagon-procurement window that none of the named competitors advertise. Over 18 months, that compliance position either compounds (a defense prime mandates Smartbase to its finishing-shop suppliers and the moat becomes channel + procurement-required) or it stays a checkbox feature; the dossier currently shows zero defense primes named, so the compounding scenario is unproven.

Revenue velocity counterfactual: to flip Smartbase from a Pass-tier velocity score to an Invest-tier one, the dossier would need to show ACVs progressing from "five-figure" to high-five or low-six-figure across the same customer base within two quarters, or a count of 20+ shops live within 12 months of launch with stated logo retention. Today the dossier says "first customers" on five-figure annual contracts -- that's pre-seed normal, but it is not the **Harvey** (2023) $3B→$5B→$8B-in-months curve that makes me pay up at consensus. The ROI calculator claims $81K savings and 1.4 headcount per shop, which is a believable price ceiling but also tells me ACVs probably stabilize in the $20-40K band -- meaning Smartbase needs hundreds of shops to clear $10M ARR, which is a sales-velocity question their two-person team hasn't answered yet.

Founders -- single anchor on **Perplexity** (2022). Aravind would show up the week after a conversation with the thing built; what's the Smartbase equivalent? Taira Fujioka was SDE 2 on AWS SageMaker Inference and co-founded Anytrace in YC S25 -- which means the team *did* ship a prior product one batch ago and then pivoted into Smartbase for P26. That's a shipping rhythm signal, not a pedigree one. Sam Goldman's prior DevOps platform raised $2M but the dossier doesn't name the outcome, which is a yellow flag -- a $2M raise without a named exit or scale milestone reads as a one-cycle attempt, not a Brex-archetype operator. "Slept at a factory" is the right founder behavior for a vertical-workflow bet but it's also the kind of line that gets written into every YC profile; I'd want to see the actual customer-onboarding artifacts before crediting it as **Perplexity**-style shipping cadence.

Forecast (36 months): Smartbase reaches $3-8M ARR with 40-90 metal-finishing shops, gets acquired by an ERP-channel buyer -- Acumatica, Epicor, or a private-equity manufacturing-software roll-up like Roper or Constellation -- for a strategic-but-modest multiple, OR pivots to broader job-shop verticals (machining, plating, fabrication) to escape the metal-finishing ICP ceiling within 18 months. The mechanism producing this is the high/low signature: clean single-miracle architecture gets Smartbase to a real business, but the absence of index position caps the outcome at "useful ERP-adjacent tool" rather than category-defining infrastructure.

The strongest bull case against my pass goes like this: Smartbase's ITAR/CMMC/DFARS-compliant US enclave is precisely the **Anduril**-adjacent catalyst I keep missing -- defense supply-chain re-shoring is a real 24-month window driven by DoD policy, Berlin-based Turian structurally can't serve it, Leverage AI doesn't market the compliance angle, and metal-finishing shops sit downstream of every defense prime that needs a domestic supplier paper trail. In this reading the "narrow ICP" is the wedge, not the ceiling -- Smartbase becomes the compliance-blessed PO layer for defense-aligned job shops, and that *is* index economics on US industrial policy. The bull case stings because it maps onto a template I've used before. It loses on the evidence: the Smartbase dossier shows zero named defense primes as customers, zero named primes mandating the tool to suppliers, and no public traction artifact distinguishing the compliance enclave from a checkbox. The single verifiable condition that flips me from Neutral to Invest within 12 months is a named tier-1 defense prime (Lockheed, RTX, GD, or Anduril itself) requiring or recommending Smartbase to its finishing-shop suppliers -- that turns metal finishing from a narrow ICP into a procurement-mandated channel, and at that point I'm leading the bridge.
