# General Aviation -- Elad Gil Evaluation

General Aviation is a high-catalyst, multi-miracle company -- the rare pre-seed where the *why now* is genuinely named (DOT announcing a new ATC system in May 2025, FAA trialing Starlink at 17 sites, Verizon's $2.4B FENS contract publicly criticized) but where the path from Ben Frank's solo desk to a certified safety-critical separation service requires more independent breakthroughs than my framework tolerates. The signature here is *Anduril's market window without Anduril's distribution wedge or its founder bench.*

On **Market Re-Opening**, the HIGH rung is **Anduril** (2022) -- Ukraine collapsed the Pentagon-software status quo in a 24-month window and I led the Series D off that single sentence. The LOW rung is **Lyft** Series C, where rideshare was a steady-state oligopoly with no fresh catalyst (20VC interview). General Aviation sits *closer to Anduril than to Lyft*: the May 8, 2025 CNN piece on DOT's planned ATC rebuild, the avitrader.com March 5, 2025 confirmation that FAA is actively running Starlink at non-safety-critical sites, and the Iridium/Aireon $366.7M consolidation in May 2026 (FlightGlobal) together describe a category that just got re-opened. The catalyst is real and dated. This is the same template as Stripe-after-PayPal and defense-post-Ukraine, and General Aviation deserves credit for naming it rather than waving at TAM math.

On **Single-Miracle Architecture**, the HIGH rung is **Decagon** (2023) -- one validated capability (LLMs handle Tier-1 support) plus GTM execution, which is why I funded it. The LOW rung is any fusion or novel-battery seed where capitalization, science, and market all need breakthroughs at once (Tim Ferriss Show #863). General Aviation sits closer to the fusion end. Run the list: (1) FAA/ICAO certification of a new ATC architecture against DO-178/DO-254 safety-critical assurance -- NextGen took 20+ years per the CNN May 2025 piece; (2) displacement or co-option of Peraton, FAA's prime integrator across 52 vendors (FedScoop 2025); (3) two-way safety-critical satellite comms that even Starlink only does at "non-safety-critical sites"; (4) building or licensing constellation capacity against Iridium and SpaceX. That is not one miracle and a GTM problem. That is four miracles compounded, which is exactly the math NFX's *Unusual Signs* paper says kills companies. General Aviation does not isolate the miracle; the dossier names no specific technical wedge that gates the other three.

**Revenue Velocity** -- HIGH rung **Harvey** (2023) at $3B → $5B → $8B in months (TechCrunch Nov 2025), LOW rung the generic AI seed with founder narrative and no curve. General Aviation has "No public data found" across revenue, LOIs, pilots, and partnerships, and Hiring is listed as False with team size zero. Pre-seed allowance applies, but a regulated B2G infrastructure play with no signed ANSP or operator pilot at YC Demo Day stage is closer to my Lyft-pass quietness than to Harvey's curve.

**Founder Ships Iteratively** -- HIGH rung **Perplexity** (2022), where Aravind built a thing between every meeting (Tim Ferriss Show #863); LOW rung the FAANG-promoter founder with no shipped artifacts. Ben Frank sits in the upper-middle: MIT aero '14, co-founded Rotabull (still operating), Seer Aerospace (claimed acquired by Aviation Week per YC page, though Tracxn flags deadpooled -- worth verifying), CCO at Rotor Technologies running the R550X program with 200 helicopter operator conversations (MIT News, Feb 9, 2024), and he is a pilot and aircraft mechanic. That is a real founder-market-fit profile and meets my "has this founder actually shipped products before" test. He also satisfies the "final decision maker" unstated pattern by being solo. But there is no "between meetings" shipping evidence and no public GitHub.

**Index Position** -- HIGH rung **Stripe** (2010) as an index on e-commerce and **Coinbase** (2013) as a basket of every cryptocurrency (Tim Ferriss Show #863); LOW rung Lyft fighting Uber for share in a winner-take-all network. General Aviation aspires to be the protocol layer for "every aircraft, drone, rocket, and ground vehicle" -- that is the right framing -- but the dossier shows them sitting *between* Aireon (which Iridium just bought for $366.7M to own the space-based ADS-B layer) and Starlink (already in FAA trials). General Aviation is closer to Color Genomics' workflow-on-commodity-infrastructure pattern than to Stripe's index. The question is whether Iridium and SpaceX let them sit on top, or whether they capture the layer themselves. The May 2026 Aireon consolidation suggests the latter is already happening.

**36-month forecast**: General Aviation pivots within 18 months from "satellite ATC replacement" to a narrower UAS / drone-traffic-management wedge where certification bars are lower and Aireon's surveillance-only product does not compete, raises a $3-6M seed extension off that pivot, and by Q2 2029 either gets acqui-hired by Iridium or a Part-23 avionics OEM for sub-$50M or is dormant. The mechanism: the catalyst is real but General Aviation's miracle stack is incompatible with solo-founder pre-seed velocity, and the constellation owners will absorb the application layer before General Aviation can certify against DO-178.

The strongest case against me is the **Anduril Known Exception**: defense was supposedly uninvestable, Trae Stephens and Palmer Luckey had shipping history, and I made the bet by reframing the "miracle" as software-on-existing-hardware rather than new physics. Ben Frank has more aviation shipping rhythm than most pre-seed founders I see, and one could reframe General Aviation's miracle as *workflow on top of Starlink/Iridium constellations* -- my exact unstated pattern of workflow-infrastructure-on-hard-thing. That reframing stings. But the dismantling: Anduril was a Series D at $4.6B with contracts already compounding, not a solo-founder zero-team seed; Stephens had built Palantir's forward-deployed muscle which is the specific muscle ATC procurement needs; and Aireon's $366.7M Iridium acquisition is the constellation owner pre-empting the workflow layer, not waiting to be partnered with. **One verifiable condition flips me**: a signed FAA OTA, NAV CANADA, or named operator pilot with a dollar figure attached within nine months -- at that point this becomes Aireon-without-the-incumbency and I will look at it again, ideally co-investing with someone running the regulatory playbook for him.
