# Nebula Security -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

Nebula Security is a research-lab-with-real-bugs whose highest dimension -- specific knowledge, if the CVE-2026-6307 Chrome V8 sandbox escape and CVE-2026-5865 V8 RCE were shipped by Eten Zou himself -- collides with its lowest dimension, a founder trio so illegible I cannot find Eten Zou on Twitter, LinkedIn, or GitHub, and the second co-founder is not even named on the YC page. That pairing is Nebula Security's analytical signature: potentially extreme offensive-research chops wrapped in a founder-legibility gap I have no way to close before the batch closes.

Stack Overflow (2008) is the load-bearing analogy for permissionless leverage in a builder-tool -- Atwood and Spolsky wrote a public blog for years, the developer community pulled them into the product, distribution cost approached zero. Nebula Security has the shape of this at 20% resolution: the nebusec.ai/research writeup on CVE-2026-6307 is media-leverage, and CVE credits are the offensive-security industry's version of open-source commits. But Nebula Security's core distribution is a premium "Audited by Nebula Security" audit brand -- that is labor leverage, human-hours priced by the engagement, and labor leverage requires permission from every buyer's procurement office. The VEGA scanner could route around this if it distributed like Semgrep's OSS core, but Nebula Security's dossier shows no OSS repo, no free tier, no public pricing, no Product Hunt launch -- just an X handle @nebusecurity joined February 2026 and 87 LinkedIn followers, half of which point to a colliding "stealth" LA entity founded 2022. The research blog is real media leverage. Everything downstream of it is not.

Perplexity (2022) is the Known Exception I keep returning to for single-sided products: I overrode criterion #2 because Aravind Srinivas's Berkeley ML retrieval PhD was extreme, verifiable, and pre-consensus. Nebula Security is trying to run the same play -- the YC description credits the founders with "the first nginx RCE" and the "first Android 17 browser-to-kernel exploit chain," and if that were verifiable the Clearview-style override at angel scale would be defensible: one X downside, category-defining upside if the offensive-research founder becomes the trusted third-party audit brand. The dossier itself blows this up in the Key Risks section -- the "first nginx RCE" search surfaces CVE-2026-42945 credited to "depthfirst," not to Nebula Security or Eten Zou. That is not a small thing. Specific knowledge that cannot be tied to a named human on a public artifact is not specific knowledge; it is a marketing claim on a YC page. The Clearview override worked because Hoan Ton-That's model actually functioned; here the underlying artifact may function, but the attribution chain to Nebula Security's founders is severed at the person.

Uber's 4,000x came because supply added demand and demand added supply -- the mechanism compounds every marginal user. Nebula Security has no such loop: each customer of the VEGA scan is economically independent, and the "Audited by Nebula Security" mark only compounds if the mark itself becomes a trust standard, which requires years and a reputation-holder the market can name. At 87 LinkedIn followers with a colliding name against nebulasecurity.io and nebulaixdr.com, Nebula Security has not started the trust-standard clock. Absent-evidence default on criterion #2 lands below midpoint and I see no dossier fact that argues up from it.

For criterion #4 the counterfactual is simple: if Nebula Security's founders can pivot from "AI SAST + human audit" to "offensive-research-as-a-service for foundation-model labs" -- Anthropic, OpenAI, Google red-teams paying seven figures for V8-class findings -- the market becomes platform-shaped rather than a Snyk-alternative knife fight. Snyk raised $1.32B at $8.5B, XBOW raised $237M at >$1B, Semgrep raised $196M -- Nebula Security's $500K YC check does not survive frontal combat in that lane. Pivot-room exists inside AppSec because AI-generated code volume is real, but Nebula Security has to demonstrate the pivot vector its dossier does not name.

Notion (2013) is the single-anchor parallel for founder trio: Ivan Zhao had a decade of legible artifacts before I wrote the seed -- personal essays, prior product iterations, a public identity I could 10-year-partner-check. Nebula Security's Eten Zou has none of this in the dossier: no Twitter individual, no LinkedIn, no GitHub, no conference talks, no CVE credits I can independently trace. The second co-founder does not appear by name. The Buffett trio is not neutrally silent here -- it is actively illegible, and my Epinions scar tissue on partner integrity says illegibility at this depth is a below-default score, not a default score.

Forecast, 36 months: Nebula Security either gets acqui-hired by Snyk or Semgrep in the $20-60M range as a CVE-portfolio-plus-talent tuck-in, or it dies against XBOW's $237M war chest and a Semgrep OSS core that swallows the low end -- I do not see the ARR-scaling path that lets Nebula Security stay independent past Series A, because the audit-brand mechanic needs a legible founder identity Nebula Security has not built.

The strongest bull case runs through my own Clearview exception: at angel check size, extreme specific knowledge tolerates reputation and legibility gaps a brand-name fund cannot underwrite -- if Eten Zou did discover CVE-2026-6307 in V8 and CVE-2026-23274 in the Linux kernel at $10,500 bounty, that is genuinely rare human capital, and one X downside against a foundation-model-red-team pivot upside is the exact asymmetric math I invented the framework for. The dossier answers this with the "depthfirst" attribution on the nginx claim -- when the YC description and the CVE registry disagree on who found the bug, the framework cannot flex. Verifiable condition that flips me to Invest: Eten Zou's name appearing in the NVD acknowledgments for CVE-2026-6307 with a corresponding technical writeup published under his personal handle, plus the second co-founder named with equivalent bona fides. That is a two-hour verification for the founders and it has not been done in the dossier they gave YC. Until then, pass.
