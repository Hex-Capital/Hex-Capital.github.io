# HEVN, inc -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Peter Volnov shipped HOT Labs to 15M users and $100M TVL before HEVN, inc existed, and that single fact is the load-bearing element of this entire dossier. Strip that away and HEVN, inc is a four-person stablecoin-neobank waitlist with no pricing page chasing Mercury's $5.2B shadow. With it, HEVN, inc is a team that already proved it can drag a fintech-adjacent consumer product to scale and is now doing the unsexy B2B version. The signature is: highest on schlep-zone fit and animal resourcefulness, lowest on organic-idea purity, because Volnov and his three co-founders pivoted from crypto consumer to B2B treasury -- which is not the same as scratching your own itch.

Brian Armstrong's hand-built Android wallet rescued his rejected **Coinbase** (2012) application, and the mechanism that made me bend was that he was already doing the regulatory grind nobody else in the batch wanted to touch. HEVN, inc inherits this same animal-resourcefulness frame from a different angle: Volnov did not build a prototype to overturn a rejection, he ran HOT Labs to 15M users and $100M TVL and won a $250K Stellar Foundation grant with Andrei Zhevlakov as CTO, then walked away from that to "rebuild fiat banking" per the dossier. That is animal behavior on a longer time horizon than Armstrong's. Where HEVN, inc diverges from the Armstrong rung is that the pre-HEVN artifact is not the HEVN product -- the wallet was the wallet, the USD account is a new build still in waitlist. The shipped HOT Labs scale earns the score; the absence of a HEVN-specific stunt is what keeps it below the rung.

The MBA-wrapper team I described in my Pass Example fails because their narrative is "spotted a market opportunity" with no founder biography touching the problem. HEVN, inc has the inverse problem on a milder scale: the dossier says the team "operated companies across SF, UAE, BVI, and HK and felt the pain of global payments automation firsthand" -- which is a real organic claim, but it is not Drew Houston's USB stick at **Dropbox** (2007). It is closer to "we ran an international crypto company and noticed payouts were ugly." That is genuine pain, but the product HEVN, inc is building -- a USD account with API/CLI payouts in 82+ countries -- is a step removed from what HOT Labs needed for itself. The pivot from consumer crypto to B2B treasury is exactly where organic ideas usually thin out.

The schlep dimension compounds well here. HEVN, inc is courting EU, US, Swiss, and UAE banking jurisdictions simultaneously while sitting on USDC treasury and partnering with local banks across 82+ countries -- this is the kind of multi-jurisdiction compliance overhead that BVNK is willing to sell as infra precisely because nobody wants to do it themselves. Over 18 months the schlep either becomes the moat (HEVN, inc accumulates licenses and bank relationships that take years to replicate) or it eats the company (one regulator in one jurisdiction freezes the USDC treasury flow). The Coinbase pattern says the former is possible, but Brian Armstrong had a four-year head start on a single regulatory regime, not four at once.

What would have to be true to push HEVN, inc's hacker-genealogy score up a tier: I would need to see Volnov's @pvolnov GitHub star count or an open-source project of consequence, and similarly for Zhevlakov beyond the ITMO CS line and "first SWE role at 15." The dossier gives me Python/Rust on Volnov, ITMO and a Stellar grant on Zhevlakov, and silence on the other two for GitHub. That is enough hacker lineage to clear my default, not enough to anchor it next to Patrick Collison's Young Scientist Lisp prize before **Stripe** (2010). The Stellar grant is the most concrete technical credential and it is on the right person -- the CTO.

The cofounder configuration is where HEVN, inc most resembles **Airbnb** (2009)'s Chesky/Gebbia/Blecharczyk: four people who worked together at HOT Labs starting 2022-2024, which is the multi-year shared-foxhole pattern I want. Four is one more than I prefer, but the dossier shows clear functional coverage -- CEO/CPO/CTO/CMO -- and the CMO Pavel Mitsevich scaled HOT Labs' YouTube to 1.2M followers, which means HEVN, inc has a distribution operator who has already executed at scale. This is not a hackathon-paired team.

My 36-month forecast: HEVN, inc reaches $8-20M ARR serving non-US internet companies that cannot get Mercury or Brex accounts, then gets acquired by Stripe or Mercury for $120-300M as those incumbents extend non-US entity coverage and decide buying is faster than building the 82-country bank partnership stack. Not a fund-returner. The mechanism is that the schlep does become a real asset, but the asset is acquirable rather than compounding -- Mercury at $650M ARR has the capital to write that check, and Brex just demonstrated Capital One will pay $5.15B for distribution into the same buyer.

The bull case that stings: Volnov's 463.2K X following plus HOT Labs' distribution playbook is a real channel into exactly the audience that needs a non-US USD account, and the YC Spring 2026 stablecoin-funding signal means HEVN, inc is positioned for the moment when stablecoin banking stops being weird. If Volnov is actually the animal his HOT Labs run suggests, this is the **Stripe** (2010) pattern -- developer-first API for a payments primitive nobody bundled before -- and I am underwriting acquisition because I'm pattern-matching on incumbent size rather than founder ceiling. What would flip my verdict toward a higher conviction Strong Invest: HEVN, inc converting its waitlist to 200+ paying non-US YC-batch companies within 12 months and Volnov shipping a public CLI/API artifact that hits Hacker News the way Houston's demo did. Absent that, I write a check but I size it as a base hit, not a Black Swan.
