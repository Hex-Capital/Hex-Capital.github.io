# smol machines -- Naval Ravikant Evaluation

BinBin He worked at AWS in the container space with Firecracker — the microVM technology powering Lambda and Fargate — and then left to build smol machines, a desktop-native VM tool that does something AWS structurally cannot do with Firecracker: run on macOS. This is the principal-agent dynamic I evaluate obsessively. AWS's Firecracker team possesses the deepest microVM knowledge on the planet, but they're agents of a cloud business model — extending Firecracker to developer desktops on Apple Silicon would serve individual developers at the expense of AWS's cloud lock-in strategy. BinBin He, operating as a principal with no cloud revenue to protect, can move where those agents cannot. The structural parallel to Perplexity versus Google is real: insider leaves incumbent, builds the thing the incumbent's business model prohibits. When I backed Srinivas, the principal-agent gap between him and Google's search team was the moat. smol machines has a version of this gap against AWS. But a version is not the thing itself.

The specific knowledge question requires precision. "Worked at AWS in the container space with Firecracker" is not "built Firecracker." The dossier doesn't specify whether BinBin He was a core contributor, a user of internal APIs, or an adjacent team member. His GitHub confirms sustained Rust/systems output — ERA at 303 stars, envmap at 125 stars, 476 commits on smolvm — genuine builder-founder credentials, not a manager who hired engineers. But smol machines is built on libkrun, a Red Hat library incorporating Firecracker code, not on Firecracker directly. He has specific knowledge of the problem space, and the product — a Rust VMM CLI with sub-200ms cold starts across four platform targets — demonstrates real systems-level expertise. But the core technology layer belongs to Red Hat's containers organization. This is the difference between Aravind Srinivas building Perplexity's search stack from his years at Google Brain and OpenAI, and someone wrapping a foundation model API. smol machines' specific knowledge is above the median for a pre-seed YC company but falls short of the irreplaceability threshold I need to see for my highest conviction.

The leverage audit reveals a structural tension at the heart of smol machines. On one side: pure code leverage. A Rust CLI distributed via `curl | bash`, 82.8% of the codebase in a single compiled language, scaling to unlimited developers at zero marginal cost. 2,500 GitHub stars and 97 forks with a listed team size of one — that's output-per-person leverage that validates the shrinking company thesis I've been articulating since 2012 and that Midjourney and Cursor have since proved. On the other side: smol machines depends on libkrun, an upstream dependency maintained by Red Hat. The VMM, the custom kernel libkrunfw, the virtio balloon memory management — all controlled by a third party. Building permissionless leverage on top of someone else's permissionless leverage is fragile. If Red Hat ships a better CLI wrapper, or Docker integrates libkrun into its $393M-funded ecosystem, smol machines' differentiation compresses to UX polish and the `.smolmachine` packaging format. Both are replicable with sufficient investment. I backed Notion because the leverage was self-contained — a small team owned every layer of the product. smol machines doesn't own its foundational layer.

Docker's container images compound — every Dockerfile written reinforces the ecosystem, every Docker Hub pull deepens the network effect. smol machines' `.smolmachine` format could theoretically create similar compounding, but at 2,500 stars and v0.5.19 there's no registry, no distribution network, no evidence of artifact sharing between developers. Apache-2.0 open-source with no pricing page, no commercial tier, no stated monetization path. smol machines creates value today but has no mechanism to capture it — and open-source infrastructure companies that defer monetization often discover that the community that loves the free product resists any commercial layer introduced after the fact. HashiCorp learned this when they relicensed; Docker nearly died before finding enterprise revenue. The access democratization angle exists — smol machines gives individual developers VM-level isolation previously requiring QEMU expertise or enterprise VMware licenses — but it's incremental improvement in developer tooling, not the structural expansion of access to a previously restricted resource. AngelList gave millions of people access to startup investing. CoinList opened token launches to retail. smol machines gives developers a faster VM. Different magnitude.

Fu Qiao's complete absence from public records is a signal worth weighting. smol machines lists two founders but YC reports team size as one. No education, no employment history, no GitHub repos, no retrievable LinkedIn headline for Fu Qiao. No shared employer or university overlap with BinBin He is identifiable from available data. I watch specifically for co-founder relationships without pre-existing trust — co-founders who haven't built together before are agents to each other before they're principals together. Combined with BinBin He's clear builder credentials — 476 commits, prior Rust projects, AWS lineage — the asymmetry raises a question smol machines needs to answer: is this a genuine partnership or a nominal co-founder arrangement? Either way, the principal authenticity signal from BinBin He himself is positive. He left AWS. He built 476 commits worth of hypervisor-level code. He ships open-source systems tools for fun. This is builder behavior, not agent behavior. But it's not the level of obsessive, identity-fused commitment I saw in founders like Sahil Lavingia, who kept building Gumroad alone after every investor walked away.

The strongest bull case: AI coding agents — Claude Code, Cursor, Codex — need sandboxed execution for untrusted generated code, and smol machines' sub-200ms boot times with hypervisor-level isolation are exactly what this use case demands. E2B raised $21M for cloud-hosted AI sandboxes serving 88% of the Fortune 100; smol machines offers the local-first alternative at a moment when developers increasingly distrust cloud-only execution. If every AI-assisted developer needs a local VM, smol machines could become default infrastructure. I checked my Known Exceptions — the Varda precedent, where physics-based leverage overrides team-density concerns — but smol machines' moat isn't physics. It's a well-packaged CLI wrapping Red Hat's open-source library. The AI sandboxing timing is real, but the moat isn't structural enough to trigger an exception. What would flip me: evidence that BinBin He was a core Firecracker contributor with non-replicable architectural knowledge, a working `.smolmachine` registry showing organic artifact sharing, and any commercial signal — a single enterprise LOI or paid pilot. smol machines has genuine specific knowledge, real code leverage, and a principal who builds. But Specific Knowledge Productization lands below top quartile because the founder's exact Firecracker role is ambiguous and the core VMM belongs to Red Hat. Permissionless Leverage Architecture is structurally capped by the libkrun dependency that places smol machines' foundational technology under someone else's control. Those two dimensions hold the score.
