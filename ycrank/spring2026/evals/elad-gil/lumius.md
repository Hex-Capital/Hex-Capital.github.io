# Lumius -- Elad Gil Evaluation

Lumius is a three-Duke-PhD research-output team where the highest dimension is technical credentialing tied to a Jan 2025 *Nature Communications* paper on diffractive acoustic tomography (PubMed 39880853), and the lowest is the dossier's complete silence on revenue, pilots, or LOIs against Butterfly Network's $91–95M 2025 revenue run-rate. That pairing tells me exactly what kind of company Lumius is: a science-adjacent device play whose founder-market fit is on the *research* axis, not the *shipping* axis — and in my book that is the inverse of the Perplexity (2022) template, not a version of it.

On founder execution rhythm, the rung I want to load against Lumius is **Perplexity** (2022) — Aravind would "show up a week later with a thing we discussed built" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and that cadence is what justified a cold-inbound check. Lumius founders Luca Menozzi and Tri Vu have indisputable depth — first-author *Nature Communications* (Jan 2025), PAUSAT in *Photoacoustics* (Feb 2023), Duke Photoacoustic Imaging Lab heritage — but every shipped artifact in the Lumius dossier is a *paper*, not a *product*. No GitHub repos, no prior commercial device, no FDA submission timeline disclosed, no design-partner hospital named. Menozzi ships peer-review revisions on Duke's clock; Aravind shipped a working answer engine. Different muscle. Same domain depth does not buy you the same execution rhythm, and Lumius does not get Perplexity's score on this axis.

Lyft Series C is the rung I keep returning to on Lumius's distribution position — I passed because #2 in a winner-take-all market captures a fraction of #1's economics (20VC interview), and the failure mechanism is that you spend the round fighting for share rather than riding the category. Lumius is not literally #2 in a network-effect market, but the structural shape rhymes: Butterfly Network ($350M+ raised, $91–95M 2025 revenue, MedTech Dive), Exo Imaging (~$100M raised with stated 3D ambitions on pMUT), and the GE/Philips/Siemens incumbent triumvirate already own the workflow path Lumius needs to enter. Lumius does not sit *in* the workflow path of point-of-care ultrasound the way Stripe sits in e-commerce — Lumius has to *take* the path from capitalized operators who have spent a decade laying it. That is fighting for share, not indexing on a category.

On the catalyst question — counterfactual mode — the dossier offers two candidates: the Menozzi/Vu *Nature Communications* paper (Jan 2025) and the American Society of Echocardiography vascular cannulation guidelines (Mordor Intelligence, 2025). Neither is a step-function on the order of post-Ukraine defense or post-ChatGPT legal. For Lumius's score on this axis to flip a tier, the dossier would need to name a CMS reimbursement code shift, a Joint Commission mandate on 3D guidance, or an FDA pathway change in the last 24 months — none of those appear. A faster reconstruction algorithm and a guideline update is a *capability* catalyst, not a *market* catalyst, and the difference is what separates Lumius from the Anduril (2022) template I keep getting asked to apply.

Revenue velocity is the dimension where Lumius's silence does the work — "if you're an AI company and you're not seeing explosive growth quickly, something's fundamentally broken" (Tim Ferriss Show #863, April 2026), and while Lumius is a device company rather than pure AI, the principle compounds the same way at pre-seed: silence on traction *is* the signal. Harvey (2023) ran "$3B → $5B → $8B in months" (TechCrunch, Nov 2025); Lumius shows a Book-a-Demo CTA and zero named hospital design partners, zero LOIs, zero pilots. Over 18 months, this axis does not improve on its own — Lumius has to clear FDA 510(k) before it can compound anything, and the website itself disclaims any current medical use. That converts the next 18 months from "compound revenue" to "burn capital pre-revenue," which is the science-project shape I avoid.

The single-miracle test is where Lumius fails most cleanly when I line it up against **Color Genomics** (2015) — the closest Known Exception I could try to apply. Color was workflow infrastructure on *commodity* sequencing where I architected around chemistry risk; Lumius claims a similar shape ("off-the-shelf linear-array transducer," per PubMed 39880853), and a sympathetic reader could call this the Color template. It is not. Lumius needs (1) lab reconstruction to translate to real-time clinical robustness on central-line procedures, (2) FDA 510(k) clearance with no disclosed timeline, (3) hospital capital-equipment procurement against Butterfly's installed base, and (4) unit-cost engineering to undercut the ~10× incumbent 3D premium named in the YC description. Compound those four independent probabilities and the math does not pencil. Color worked because I was operating it and I isolated the miracle to workflow. Lumius is asking an angel to underwrite four miracles from a Durham seed.

My 36-month forecast on Lumius: still pre-FDA-clearance at month 36, raises a seed extension of $2–4M to bridge the 510(k) gap, and either gets acqui-hired by Butterfly Network or Exo Imaging for the diffractive acoustic tomography IP at a sub-$40M outcome, or quietly returns to Duke as a licensed-IP research program. The mechanism producing that outcome is the four-miracle dependency chain colliding with a hospital procurement cycle that does not reward unproven device entrants, regardless of how clean the *Nature Communications* paper is.

The strongest objection to that forecast is the Color (2015) override: workflow infrastructure on commodity hardware in a market with a real recent capability shift, founded by domain-native PhDs with peer-reviewed IP. A bull would argue Lumius is a 2026 Color — Menozzi's diffractive acoustic tomography is the commodity-hardware-plus-clever-reconstruction analog to commodity sequencing, and Duke + Columbia academic relationships are the design-partner runway. That objection stings because the structural rhyme is real. What dismantles it: I *operated* Color and structured around the miracle deliberately; Lumius is a passive angel check into a team whose strongest credentials are research artifacts, not commercial shipping artifacts, and the dossier shows zero signed hospital LOIs. The verdict flips if Lumius announces a Duke Health or Columbia Presbyterian paid pilot with central-line procedure volume disclosed within 90 days, *and* an FDA 510(k) submission accepted for review — that combination would convert Lumius from a four-miracle science bet into a one-miracle execution bet, and I would re-underwrite at that point.
