# Oddpool -- Paul Graham Evaluation

Avi Arora and Ritesh Malpani are technically credible builders who found a hot adjacency rather than lived inside a problem ? Oddpool is an institutional data broker for prediction markets built by two engineers whose strongest signal is that they pivoted to it from Benchspan inside the same P26 batch. That pairing ? high learning rate, low organic discovery ? is Oddpool's signature, and it's the signature of a competent acqui-hire candidate, not a Stripe.

Oddpool's strongest dimension is founder learning rate. On my ladder, the high rung is Sam Altman at Loopt ? 19 years old, no business experience, but the slope was vertical. The low rung is the kind of team that grinds the same idea for two years without visibly iterating. Mid-rung is Patrick Collison shipping Auctomatic before Stripe ? prior reps, visible learning. Oddpool sits between mid and high: pivoting from Benchspan (AI prompt-injection) to a prediction-markets data API inside one YC batch is, by definition, visible iteration. The dossier pins the placement ? both founders' LinkedIn headlines still read "Co-founder @ Benchspan (YC P26)" while Oddpool ships a 750-dashboard product and a published PredictionMarketBench paper. That's responsiveness to evidence. But the same fact restrains the upper rung: a pivot within months can be running-back flexibility or it can be flinching.

Oddpool's weakest dimension is organic problem discovery, which is the one I weight hardest after resourcefulness. The high rung is Drew Houston forgetting his USB drive and building Dropbox; the mid rung is the Collisons feeling payments friction as developers; the low rung is the McKinsey duo who "identified an opportunity in B2B workflow." Oddpool sits between low and mid. Avi was a Microsoft ML engineer on Prompt Shields; Ritesh built trading infrastructure at Bloomberg. Neither was a quant trader at a macro desk who couldn't get cross-venue Kalshi/Polymarket data and built the tool out of frustration. The 352-star Awesome-Prediction-Market-Tools repo is real domain immersion, but a curated link list is closer to noticing a market than living inside it. The dossier offers no founding moment of personal pain ? only an opportunity that opened up when Polymarket bought Dome.

The repels-before-it-attracts dimension is similarly weak. Coinbase in 2012 sits at the high rung ? everyone serious dismissed crypto. Replit at the mid rung ? online IDEs sounded dubious to people who liked their local toolchains. The low rung is any AI-agent startup whose pitch every VC nods along to. Oddpool sits squarely between low and mid: "Bloomberg for prediction markets" pattern-matches instantly for any analytics investor, the $44B 2025 volume figure makes the deck write itself, and the Polymarket-acquires-Dome story is now consensus narrative. Nobody hears Oddpool and says "that will never work."

Founder resourcefulness lands mid-ish. The high rung is the Airbnb cereal-box story; the low rung is founders who treat fundraising as the answer to every blocker; mid is Stripe's Collisons quietly slogging through PCI compliance. Oddpool's dossier evidence ? Avi's 352-star repo as an inbound channel, the forward-only orderbook archive they started accumulating in March 2026, the pivot itself ? shows builders who do things, but no single anecdote at cereal-box altitude. Earnest builder identity lands similarly mid: the published PredictionMarketBench paper and the awesome-list curation read as hackers-building-for-hackers, my favored unstated pattern, but the pivot recency makes it hard to know whether Oddpool is the obsession or the opportunity.

My 36-month forecast: Oddpool gets acqui-hired by Kalshi or a CoinAPI-style aggregator for under $20M before reaching $5M ARR independently, mirroring Dome's exit to Polymarket. The mechanism is the same one the dossier already exposes ? venues are structurally incentivized to internalize neutral aggregators rather than coexist with them, and a two-venue API with no quant-fund logos disclosed has neither the coverage breadth nor the customer concentration to resist that gravity.

The bull case stings on one point: Dome's acquisition is itself the precedent that proves venues will pay, and the running-back pivot from Benchspan is exactly the flexibility I praise in "The Right Kind of Stubborn." A $15M acqui-hire on a pre-seed check is a real return for a real angel. But that's a consolation outcome, and I write checks for black swans, not consolations. Dome's founders were ex-Alchemy with "50+ developers building" at launch; Oddpool's dossier discloses zero paying customers and a product that's a few months old. The Benchspan-to-Oddpool jump inside one batch isn't the Reddit pivot ? Alexis and Steve had me at the dinner table. Oddpool I'd want to watch. The verifiable condition that flips me to invest: $50K MRR within six months from named quant funds or prop shops, not retail Pro tiers ? that would prove institutional pull rather than developer-tier curiosity.
