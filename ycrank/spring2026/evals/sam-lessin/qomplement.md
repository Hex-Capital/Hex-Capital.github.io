# qomplement -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

qomplement is the Spring 2026 archetype my Pass Example was written about — "Agentic ERP" pitched by Kerim Taray and Andres Garza, two Tec de Monterrey CS grads with real RPA scar tissue, getting force-fit into the most consensus YC category of the year. The dimensional signature here is brutal and clean: the highest rung qomplement reaches is founder-market fit via prior RPA consulting work in Mexican enterprises, and the lowest is category sleeve — "AI agents on top of legacy ERPs for document processing" is what every fifth deck in S26 says, and "AI is just more internet" (Joe Lonsdale podcast #113) is exactly how I priced this category two years ago.

Start with the pass example I already wrote in my own head. The canonical 2026 YC seed pitch — "AI agent for enterprise vertical, four tier-1 VCs already in" — is qomplement with a different logo. Tom Blomfield is the YC partner, which is the hottest possible partner draw and a near-guarantee of consensus heat downstream. The dossier gives no round size, no price, no investor list beyond AWS listed on Crunchbase, and silence on round dynamics is exactly what I punish — because in this category in this batch, silence almost always means "$25M+ post on a SAFE, oversubscribed." Where qomplement diverges from the pass-example baseline at all is the 10,000 docs/day across 20+ companies, which is more substrate than the typical S26 demo-day deck — but that's a tack-five concern, not a tack-one price concern, and the price discipline gate fires first.

The low rung on category sleeve fit is my own "AI agent shell" anti-pattern, and qomplement's dossier lands on it directly: "Deploys AI agents that connect to existing enterprise tools without ERP migration." That failure mechanism is twofold — first, SAP's own "Supply Chain Trends for 2026" roadmap (cited in the dossier) signals native agentic overlays inside the ERP qomplement integrates with, which collapses the integration wedge from underneath. Second, Pivot in Paris is already at $70M raised and €2.5B annual invoice volume (SiliconANGLE, May 2026) — a horizontal player with the capital to enter grocery and trucking verticals before qomplement establishes any lock-in. The pattern is well-funded horizontal absorbs narrow vertical, and qomplement has no structural answer to either side of the squeeze.

On founder conviction (mechanism analysis): Kerim and Andres ran RPA implementations for Mexican enterprises before qomplement, which is genuine missionary substrate — they've lived inside the spreadsheet-and-email document hell they now sell against. Over 18 months that compounds into deeper vertical templates per customer, which is the only moat that could matter. But the dossier shows zero opinionated public writing on the category from either founder, GitHub blank, Twitter follower counts so low they're not even retrievable, and a May 2025 Product Hunt page for a prior product ("AI agent that controls your personal computer") that suggests opportunity-surfing rather than a category they've been missionary about for three years. Conviction signal: present but not artifact-backed.

On capital structure (counterfactual): qomplement is a generic per-seat or per-document enterprise SaaS contract — no royalty, no holdco, no employee-equity twist, nothing in the dossier suggests a non-standard instrument. For Criterion 2 to flip a tier into venture-shaped territory, qomplement would need to be either (a) bootstrappable from grocery-chain ACVs without a Series A — possible at 20+ customers, but no revenue is disclosed so I cannot underwrite this — or (b) structured as a vertical holdco buying up Mexican freight-doc services businesses, which would be a Teamshares-shaped bet I'd actually look at. Neither is the deal on offer.

On founder-as-brand (single-anchor parallel): compare qomplement directly to John Fish / Bookshelved. Bookshelved had a YouTuber with owned distribution before the check; the Slow Creator Fund thesis was that the audience was the asset. qomplement's distribution is Kerim's personal Cal.com link and three Mexican-language press hits in Expansión, Milenio, and Startups Latam. That is founder-led enterprise sales, not owned community — which is fine as a GTM but is not a cap-table-relevant distribution wedge. The "founder is the asset" frame does not load here.

36-month forecast: qomplement reaches $3-6M ARR in retail-grocery and trucking document operations by mid-2029 and gets acqui-hired by Pivot or a procurement-adjacent ERP player (Coupa, NetSuite, or a SAP partner) for $25-60M total consideration. The mechanism is the category-sleeve low rung: native ERP overlays from SAP and Pivot's European expansion eat the integration wedge before qomplement raises a Series B, and the founders' RPA-services DNA makes them a comfortable services-acquisition target rather than a standalone scaler.

Strongest opposing case: the bull read is that 10,000 documents per day across 20+ enterprise customers at pre-seed is genuinely uncommon — that level of integration depth before a YC demo day suggests qomplement has crossed a threshold most agentic-ERP pitches haven't, and the vertical narrowness (grocery + trucking) is sharper than Pivot's horizontal European procurement OS, which is the Allbirds-shaped "only fund in a category" setup I claim to look for. The dismantle: Allbirds had margin, brand attachment, and zero competitor with $70M of dry powder targeting the same verticals — qomplement has none of those. Customer-specific document templates accumulate switching cost only until SAP ships a native agent that reads the same POs inside the system of record, which the SAP 2026 roadmap explicitly telegraphs. One verifiable condition flips this: if qomplement is raising at sub-$10M post-money with $2M+ ARR and one named top-five US grocery chain on the customer list, the price-discipline gate reopens and I take a meeting. Absent that, this is the pass example.
