# Expanse -- Sam Lessin Evaluation

Ismaeel Bashir built a multimodal HPC resource predictor at EPCC that the YC blurb claims "beat every published baseline" -- that's the high end of Expanse's dimensional signature, and the inverse pole is that I cannot find a Twitter account, a Product Hunt launch, a blog post, or a retrievable follower count for any of the four founders. Expanse is the archetypal academic-origin enterprise infrastructure play: deep technical operator with a real research artifact, zero distribution surface, getting force-fit into a venture cap table to sell on-prem software to national supercomputing centres and quant funds. That pairing -- conviction high on the technology, dead-flat on owned audience -- tells me exactly what kind of regatta this is before I look at price.

Pinterest's Ben Silbermann is the missionary-temperament rung I keep returning to when a founder has real category obsession but no public artifacts of it -- the conviction was operational and personal, not published, and the deal arrived through operator-friend channel rather than competitive process. Expanse's Bashir is partway up that rung: the EPCC research is multi-year evidence of genuine domain depth on HPC resource prediction, and the COO/CPO mix (Yafet Melake out of G-Research, Eren Mendi from Imperial) is consistent with the "ran workloads at the largest quant funds" claim on the YC page. Where Expanse diverges from the Silbermann analogy is that Pinterest came to me through Kortina-adjacent network; Expanse is arriving as a YC-batched cold dossier with no operator-friend bridge into the Slow ops graph, and the "beat every published baseline" line is unverifiable without the paper -- I'd want to read Bashir's EPCC publications before underwriting it as missionary conviction rather than a research-claim-shaped pitch.

A canonical pre-seed deselection in my book is the founder with zero pre-funding public writing and no community footprint -- the "Writing is thinking" filter fails on the same dossier where the founder-as-brand filter fails, and the two compounding absences mean the company will be paying for every customer it ever acquires. Expanse hits that pattern almost cleanly: no founder Twitter found, LinkedIn presences confirmed but follower counts unretrievable, no Product Hunt launch, no press, and the only owned-distribution surface is a free OS Wastage Scanner at wastage.expanse.sh. Selling to national supercomputing centres and quant funds doesn't require a creator audience -- I'll grant that -- but it does require an inbound channel I'd recognize as a tack, and Expanse's only visible motion is book.getexpanse.io demo outbound. That's a sales-led enterprise grind, not a regatta with cheap tacks between here and Series A.

The financing-structure read is where Expanse loses me hardest, and this is mechanism analysis, not analogy: on-prem deployment to HPC centres and quant funds means 9-18 month procurement cycles, pilot-to-license conversion measured in quarters, and per-customer ARR that's lumpy until you've stacked ten logos. Compound that over 18 months and Expanse needs at least two more priced rounds before any honest default-alive conversation -- a seed, a bridge, and a Series A funded on logo count rather than revenue velocity, which is exactly the factory-model cap table I rate as founder-drainage when the underlying business cannot bootstrap. The Slurm-adjacent open-source competitive surface and Run:ai's post-NVIDIA-acquisition bundling means each of those rounds gets harder to clear, not easier.

Counterfactual on the round-pricing dimension: for Expanse to flip from a 10 to a 20 on Criterion 1, I'd need to see the dossier name two tier-1 funds that visibly passed on the round, OR a sub-batch-median price that signals the founders chose discipline over heat. Neither appears. What does appear is GPU optimization, which sits in the AI-infrastructure adjacency I explicitly de-rate -- "AI is just more internet" applies even to the picks-and-shovels layer when the picks are sold into a market the hyperscalers will bundle into their own stack within 24 months. NVIDIA Run:ai post-acquisition has every incentive to ship pre-submission prediction as a feature; Expanse's wedge is on-prem data-sovereignty for customers who won't deploy Run:ai, which is a real but small SAM.

Sublime Security is the single-anchor I'd use on the sleeve dimension -- I bought into enterprise security before the next consolidation wave, and the analogy would load if GPU optimization were genuinely pre-consensus the way SOC tooling was when I wrote that check. It isn't. GPU efficiency tooling is mid-cycle consensus in 2026: Dell'Oro's "from scale to optimization" framing of GTC 2026 is the exact headline that tells me a dozen other YC batches and a dozen seed funds are pattern-matching to the same thesis Expanse is pitching. Expanse's $6.59B 2026-2030 TAM cite from Technavio is the kind of report number that shows up in every GPU-orchestration deck this batch.

Forecast over 36 months: Expanse either gets acqui-hired by NVIDIA, HPE, or Run:ai for $20-60M as feature-absorption once Run:ai ships native pre-submission prediction, or grinds to roughly $2-4M ARR across 5-10 HPC and quant-fund logos and cannot clear a priced Series A as the bundle competition closes. The mechanism producing both branches is the same: Expanse's wedge is a prediction model that the incumbent scheduler vendors will either replicate or buy, and the customer set is too procurement-slow to compound ARR fast enough to outrun that clock.

The bull case that would sting: Bashir's EPCC predictor is genuinely state-of-the-art, generalizes across heterogeneous fleets, and the on-prem data-sovereignty requirement at national labs and quant funds is a permanent wall that NVIDIA Run:ai's hyperscaler-adjacent posture cannot cross -- in which case Expanse becomes a capital-efficient $30-50M ARR business that doesn't need many more rounds, the Ro override applies (regulatory/sovereignty complexity as the moat itself), and I missed a venture-shaped infrastructure pick at a sub-consensus price by reflexively de-rating AI adjacency. What dismantles that bull case in the actual dossier: the customer profile (national labs, quant funds) is precisely the slow-procurement segment that prevents the Ro override from kicking in -- Ro had 50-state prescription rails and millions of consumer touchpoints to scale into; Expanse has a buyer count probably under 200 globally. I'd flip the verdict if Expanse showed me three named, paying HPC or quant-fund customers on signed contracts within 9 months at a combined ARR above $750K -- absent that specific signal, the regatta tack count is too high for the price I'd be quoted today.
