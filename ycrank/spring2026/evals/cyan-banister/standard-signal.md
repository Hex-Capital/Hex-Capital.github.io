# Standard Signal -- Cyan Banister Evaluation

Michael Royzen is a solo founder with crisp tiebreaker authority by definition — there is no second name on the cap table to feud with — swinging at "AI-native hedge funds," which is the exact category YC published as a Spring 2026 Request for Startups. That pairing is the signature: Standard Signal's governance is the cleanest dimension in the dossier, and its non-consensus posture is the weakest, because the YC partner desk literally telegraphed this batch's hot lane in advance. I am never playing the game I seem to be playing — here the game looks like "back a frontier AI fund," and the actual game is "back the seventeenth team that read the same RFS as everyone else."

Anduril's Palmer Luckey is the high rung on lived-experience: he shipped Oculus from a garage before he ever touched defense, so the second act inherited the first act's calluses. Standard Signal's Michael Royzen has the LLM-shipping calluses — Phind (YC S22), 150M+ searches, prior ML at Lyft, Cloudflare, Microsoft Research at seventeen — which is real. The divergence is that the calluses are on the wrong hand. The dossier finds no public hedge-fund, trading desk, prop-shop, or risk-management chapter in Royzen's biography; his autobiographical relationship is to LLM productization, not to portfolio construction or to the LPs he now wants to take $100,000 minimums from. Luckey lived in the headset before he lived in the drone; Standard Signal's founder is shipping headsets and pitching drones in the same breath.

The pass example in my own file is the consensus YC AI-agent darling — six funds chasing, $200B TAM slide, two ex-FAANG PMs who discovered the problem in a memo — and the failure mechanism is that the category itself was the deck. Trace that pattern through Standard Signal. YC's RFS named "AI-Native Hedge Funds" explicitly. Alinea raised $22.5M in February 2026 in adjacent AI-finance space. Numerai is already sitting on $500M of JPMorgan capacity. Standard Signal is not arriving early to a category investors find aesthetically uninvestable the way Crusoe arrived at flared gas in 2018; Standard Signal is arriving on time to a category YC partners are actively underwriting. "Find them before it becomes consensus" — the founder published the launch tweet *after* the RFS dropped, not before.

On governance — solo founder, no co-founder, single-name LinkedIn line "Founder & CEO at Standard Signal (YC P26)." The HQ Trivia / GameCrush failure mode is structurally absent on day one. The eighteen-month compounding question is whether Royzen hires a CIO or head of trading and whether that hire gets equity and title parity, because the dossier already lists open roles for "ML Research Engineer" and "Quantitative Researcher" — both senior, neither named, neither described as a partner. Solo-founder cleanliness today can become an unresolved tiebreaker the moment a real quant walks in expecting a co-CEO badge to run a portfolio Royzen has never run.

On capital-intensity and unsexy-regulated, the counterfactual is what would flip this from mid to high. Vigil Labs sits in regulated finance and earned the swing because the founder's biography was *of* the regulated domain. For Standard Signal to score the way Vigil Labs scored, the dossier would need an SEC/CFTC registration in progress, a qualified custodian arrangement, a named compliance officer, and a founder chapter that touched portfolio management directly. None of those are present — the public site lists ML and quant roles, not legal or compliance, and "autonomous execution within risk constraints" is asserted, not registered. The regulatory moat *could* compound if Standard Signal builds it; today it is a regulatory liability dressed as a moat.

Numerai is the single-anchor parallel on physical-world verifiability, and it sharpens the problem rather than rescuing it. Numerai is also unobservable to a normal human — you cannot ride it, scan it, or photograph it — but Numerai compensates with a public tournament leaderboard, a published model methodology, and a credible third-party datapoint (the $500M JPMorgan capacity). Standard Signal offers "2,847+ asset pairs," "12ms latency on signal detection," and "87.3%-94.2% confidence levels" on its own marketing site, with no audited returns, no backtest methodology, no AUM, and no LP I can call. A $100,000 LP minimum into a vehicle whose only artifact is a marketing page is the developer-tools-abstraction failure mode, just with a hedge-fund wrapper instead of an API.

Standard Signal in 36 months: the LP fund doesn't clear escape velocity at $100K-minimums without a track record, and Royzen — to his credit, because he has shipped before — pivots Standard Signal into a B2B AI-research-and-execution tool sold *to* existing hedge funds (the Phind-for-portfolio-managers shape), landing somewhere between $1-4M ARR by Spring 2029 or quietly winding down and returning the YC money. The mechanism is the high/low pairing: solo-CEO governance lets him pivot decisively, but the consensus-YC-category posture means no aesthetic moat protects the original thesis when the LP raise stalls.

The bull case that stings: Royzen actually trained "top coding LLMs (2023-2024)" through Phind, which Paul Graham personally backed, and the polymath exception in my own file is DeepMind — Hassabis was credentialed, not outsider, and I wrote the check anyway because cross-domain compounding can override the biography filter. If Royzen is the financial-LLM version of Hassabis, the lived-experience dimension is the wrong instrument to measure him with. The dossier evidence that wins the argument: Hassabis had shipped chess-engine work, neuroscience PhD research, and Lionhead game design *before* DeepMind — three domains compounding. Royzen has shipped one domain (LLM productization at Phind) and is now claiming a second (autonomous portfolio management) without a prior artifact in it. One domain plus ambition is not the polymath exception; it is the pedigree pitch with a transformer attached. The verifiable condition that flips my verdict: a named, equity-significant co-founder from a Renaissance / Two Sigma / Citadel trading desk joining Standard Signal in the next six months, with SEC registration in motion. Absent that, I pass — and I would rather route the check to a Vigil Labs-shaped founder who lives in the regulated domain rather than visits it.
